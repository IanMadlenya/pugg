
        Monroe K. Spears, a critic, teacher and poet, died on May 23 at the Southern Tennessee Medical Center in Winchester, Tenn. He was 82 and lived in Sewanee, Tenn.
        Mr. Spears, a prolific critic, wrote regularly for many periodicals, including The New York Review of Books. He was considered an authority on modern British and American poets and was the author of ''The Poetry of W. H. Auden: The Disenchanted Island,'' ''The Literary Works of Matthew Prior,'' ''Dionysus and the City,'' ''American Ambitions'' and ''One Writer's Reality.''
        He taught at the University of Wisconsin, Vanderbilt University in Nashville and the University of the South in Sewanee, where he edited The Sewanee Review from 1952 to 1961. In 1964 he became an English professor at Rice University in Houston, where he taught until his retirement in 1986. He was a member of the Fellowship of Southern Writers.
        Mr. Spears is survived by his wife, Betty.

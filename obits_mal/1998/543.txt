
        Willis Coburn Armstrong, a former Assistant Secretary of State for economic affairs, died on Sunday at his home in Arlington, Va. He was 86.
        The cause was cancer, the Arlington Funeral Home said.
        Mr. Armstrong, who was also an educator, was an Assistant Secretary in Washington from 1972 to 1974. He had previously had positions dealing with economic affairs and international trade.
        He graduated from the Phillips Academy in Andover, Mass., in 1929 and Swarthmore College in 1933. A year later he earned a master's at Columbia University and started postgraduate studies in Russian history and language.
        He taught at Columbia from 1934 to 1939, when he joined the American Embassy in Moscow as an interpreter and translator. In World War II he worked for the Lend-Lease Administration in Washington.
        Mr. Armstrong was assistant dean of the Columbia School of International Affairs from 1967 to 1969 and later a senior lecturer in diplomacy,  until 1971. He also taught as an adjunct professor at Georgetown University in the 70's and as a lecturer on Soviet affairs at the School of Advanced International Studies in Washington from 1946 to 1958.
        Surviving are his wife, Louise Schaffner Armstrong, and a son, Ian, of Washington.

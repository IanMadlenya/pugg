
        J. Scott Howard, a risk management expert who was a vice president of J. P. Morgan & Company in New York, died on Jan. 14 at Mount Sinai Hospital after a long hospitalization for cancer. He was 53 years old and lived in Hightstown, N.J.
        Mr. Howard spent 27 years with J. P. Morgan. His company called him a pioneer in its Riskmetrics service, a data-based system for weighing investment risks, for which Mr. Howard designed a demonstration and training model.
        He earned a bachelor's degree in economics and a master's degree in international finance from New York University. He served in Vietnam as an Army sergeant.
        Mr. Howard is survived by his wife, B. Sue Mesner Howard; his parents, Mary Rowan and John H. Howard of Newport, R.I.; a brother, Jay, of Seattle, and a sister, Rowan Morse of Portland, Me.


        George S. Leonard, a former Washington trial lawyer and Justice Department official during the Eisenhower Administration, died on Feb. 16 at Alexandria Hospital in Alexandria, Va. He was 86 and lived in Alexandria.
        Mr. Leonard died of congestive heart failure, his daughter Jennifer Leonard said.
        Although he spent much of his career in private practice, Mr. Leonard served from 1953 until 1961 in the Justice Department as deputy to Warren Burger, an Assistant Attorney General. In his capacity as first assistant in the civil division he supervised foreign litigation involving the Federal Government.
        A Washington native whose parents were both lawyers, Mr. Leonard earned his bachelor's and law degrees at Columbia University, and in 1941 joined the law firm of Cravath, Swaine & Moore.
        During World War II, Mr. Leonard was the naval officer in charge of the air, ground and target intelligence organization for the Pacific at the Joint Intelligence Center at Pearl Harbor. He later retired as a captain in the Naval Reserve. After the war he returned to Cravath.
        Following his Government experience, Mr. Leonard founded two Washington law firms.
        Mr. Leonard's wife, Claire Dickinson Leonard, died in 1991.
        He is survived by a brother, Sidney Snider of Garden City, N.Y.; a son, George S. Leonard Jr. of Spotswood, N.J.; two daughters, Emily C. Leonard of Tegucigalpa, Honduras, and Jennifer Leonard of Rochester, and four grandchildren.

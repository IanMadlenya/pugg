
        Edward George Lowry Jr., a former chairman and president of the General Reinsurance Corporation, died on Aug. 11 at Sarasota Memorial Hospital in Sarasota, Fla. He was 95.
        Mr. Lowry was named president of General Reinsurance and its fire insurance affiliate, North Star Reinsurance, in 1946, and chairman four years later. After he stepped aside as chairman in 1960, Mr. Lowry was a director and chairman of the company's executive and finance committees. He retired in 1964.
        Mr. Lowry, a Rhodes scholar, received a bachelor's degree from Harvard University in 1925 and graduated with honors from Balliol College of Oxford University in 1928. He got a law degree from Columbia University in 1929. His legal career included positions as an associate at Hale & Dorr, in the law division of the Reconstruction Finance Corporation, and general counsel of Maryland Casualty. He was the treasurer of E. R. Squibb from 1943 to 1946, before joining General Reinsurance.
        He is survived by his wife, Ruth, of Sarasota; a daughter, Ruth Arnold, of Novato, Calif.; a son, Edward George 3d, of New Castle, Wash.; six grandchildren and six great-grandchildren.

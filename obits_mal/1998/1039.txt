
        Neil T. Shayne, a founder and senior partner of the law firm of Shayne, Dachs, Stanisci, Corker & Sauer in Mineola, N.Y., died Sept. 24 at Long Island Jewish Medical Center in Queens. He was 66 years old.
        Mr. Shayne, who lived in North Shore Towers in Floral Park, N.Y., died of pneumonia after a long battle with lymphoma, said a colleague, Russell Corker, of Muttontown, N.Y.
        Mr. Shayne was born in New York City and attended New York University. He received his law degree from Brooklyn Law School.
        Besides his law practice, Mr. Shayne was a faculty member and lecturer at the Practicing Law Institute and the New School for Social Research, and he wrote a column on economics and the law for the New York Law Journal. He also made an unsuccessful attempt to start a New York franchise in the World Hockey Association.
        He is survived by two sons, Jeffrey, of Hampton Bays, N.Y., and Mark, of Great Neck, N.Y.

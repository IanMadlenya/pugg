
        Robert Lyman Popper, a retired business executive who was long active in civic affairs and philanthropy, died Tuesday at White Plains Hospital. He was 90 and lived in Armonk, N.Y., and Manhattan.
        Mr. Popper was chairman of the New York State Office for the Aging in the Rockefeller, Carey and Cuomo administrations. Over the years he sat on the boards of the National Council on the Aging, Blue Cross-Blue Shield, the Health and Hospital Planning Council for Southern New York and the Federation of Protestant Welfare Agencies, among many others.
        He was born in New York City, a grandson of Lyman Gustav Bloomingdale, a founder of Bloomingdale's. A 1930 graduate of Yale University, he worked as a buyer for the department store and left in 1938 after it became part of Federated Department Stores. He had a business career at Dellwood Dairy but retired in 1962 to devote his time to the civil rights movement and his other civic interests.
        Mr. Popper's survivors include his wife of 30 years, Ellen Goldberg Popper, a founder of the Animal Welfare League. His first wife, Hermine Isaacs Popper, died in 1968.


        John J. Ryan, a former investment banker and founder of John J. Ryan & Company, died on June 23 at River Extended Care Residence in Red Bank, N.J. A resident of Palm Beach, Fla., and Red Bank, he was 86.
        He began his financial career in 1928 when he was 18, as a runner in the Wall Street firm of Lehman Brothers.
        He worked in the municipal bond department there until 1939, when he left to head the municipal bond unit of J. B. Hanauer, an investment firm based in Newark.
        In 1946, Mr. Ryan started John J. Ryan & Company in Newark. The firm specialized in municipal bond trading until 1963, when it started a division to trade in commercial bank stock.
        Mr. Ryan retired in 1969. In 1981, the company's name was changed to Ryan, Beck & Company.
        He is survived by his wife, Doris;  two daughters, Mary Jane Ryan of Moore Park, Australia, and Barbara Messinger of Red Bank; two sons, John L., of Colts Neck, N.J., and Kevin W., of Little Silver, N.J.; a sister, Mary M. Ryan of Red Bank; eight grandchildren, and one great-grandson.


        Martin H. Schrader, a former publisher at three Hearst magazines, died on Thursday at Mount Sinai Medical Center in Manhattan. Mr. Schrader, a resident of Croton-on-Hudson, N.Y., was 73.
        He died of a lung disease, his wife, Ceil, said.
        Mr. Schrader joined House Beautiful, one of 16 magazines published by Hearst Magazines, a division of the Hearst Corporation, in 1960. He worked in several marketing positions, and headed the advertising department for special publications at House Beautiful, before he was named publisher in 1966.
        Mr. Schrader was also the publisher at Town & Country, from 1969 to 1977, and then at Harper's Bazaar. He retired in 1991.
        Before joining Hearst, Mr. Schrader was a copywriter for three magazines at Esquire Inc., and worked at Alfred Auerbach Associates, an advertising agency, from 1950 to 1960, where he rose to vice president and creative director.
        In addition to his wife, he is survived by two sons, Howard of Scarborough, N.Y., and Daniel of Mountainview, Calif.; a daughter, Esther, of Newport Beach, Calif., and two grandchildren.

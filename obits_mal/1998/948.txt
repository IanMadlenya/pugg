
        Brendan M. Jones, who was a former reporter for The New York Times, died Sept. 18 at Western Queens Community Hospital. Mr. Jones, a resident of Jackson Heights, was 85.
        The cause of death was pneumonia, said his wife, Anne Virginia McCormick Jones.
        Mr. Jones joined The New York Times in 1947, after working at several newspapers, including The Clearwater (Fla.) Sun, The New York Mirror and The Washington Times. He concentrated on covering advertising, the retail industry and foreign trade for the Business section during his career with The New York Times. He retired in 1983.
        In addition to his wife, he is survived by a daughter, Maura Seger of Stamford, Conn., and two grandchildren.


        Harold L. Stewart, a pathologist for 32 years at the National Institutes of Health and a clinical professor at Georgetown University, died on May 30. He was 98 and lived in Rockville, Md.
        Dr. Stewart, a native of Houtzdale, Pa., retired in 1969 as chief of pathology at the National Cancer Institute but continued as a research scientist emeritus at the national institutes until retiring altogether in 1996.
        He was a past president of the American Association for Cancer Research and had previously taught at Jefferson Medical School in Philadelphia and Harvard Medical School.
        Dr. Stewart's wife, Eleanor, died in 1987. He is survived by a son, Robert, of Westfield, N.J.; a daughter, Janet S. Rowan of Bethesda, Md.; seven grandchildren, and six great-grandchildren.


        Anthony Salvatore, a Grammy Award-winning recording engineer, died on Nov. 18 at Hackensack University Medical Center in Hackensack, N.J. He was 77 and lived in Hasbrouck Heights, N.J.
        Mr. Salvatore worked with RCA Records from 1955 to 1993, then retired to work independently. He won Grammys for recordings of ''Lohengrin'' (1966), ''Sweeney Todd'' (1979) and ''The Heifetz Collection'' (1995), and was credited with having supervised more than 1,000 recordings.
        Among the artists he worked with were the conductors Fritz Reiner, Sir Thomas Beecham, Leopold Stokowski, Sir Georg Solti and James Levine; the instrumentalists Artur Rubinstein, Vladimir Horowitz, Van Cliburn, Jascha Heifetz and the Juilliard Quartet, and the vocalists Leontyne Price, Birgit Nilsson, Renata Tebaldi, Robert Merrill and Placido Domingo.
        He also worked on recordings by the Chicago Symphony, the Boston Symphony Orchestra, the Royal Philharmonic, the New York Philharmonic and the Philadelphia Orchestra.
        He is survived by his wife, Mary Jo; a daughter, Penny Salvatore Shepos of Somerset, N.J.; a son, Jack, of Los Angeles; two brothers, Gerald and Alfred, both of Parlin, N.J.; a sister, Palma Salvatore Mallory of Brooklyn, and four grandchildren.

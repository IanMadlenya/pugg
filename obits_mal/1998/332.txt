
        Michael N. Chetkovich, a prominent accounting expert and former managing partner at Haskins & Sells, a precursor of the Big Six accounting firm Deloitte & Touche, died on April 21 at Stanford Medical Center in Stanford, Calif. Mr. Chetkovich, a resident of Atherton, Calif., was 81.
        He joined McLaren, Goode, West & Company, a San Francisco accounting firm, in 1940. He rose to the position of partner in 1950, and, when McLaren merged with Haskins & Sells two years later, he became the head of the San Francisco office.
        In 1967, Mr. Chetkovich transferred to the Haskins headquarters in New York and was named managing partner in 1970. He retired in 1978, the year the company was renamed Deloitte Haskins & Sells to adopt one name for the firm internationally. It merged with Touche Ross in 1989 to form Deloitte & Touche.
        In 1976 and 1977, Mr. Chetkovich was chairman of the American Institute of Certified Public Accountants. He served on several national tax and accounting advisory boards and as a professor and a consultant at the University of California School of Business in Berkeley.
        Mr. Chetkovich graduated from Berkeley in 1939 and received a master's degree from the business school in 1940. In World War II, he was an intelligence officer in the Navy.
        He is survived by his wife, Alice, of Atherton; two daughters, Kathy, of Santa Cruz, Calif., and Carol, of Boston; two sons, Mark, of Santa Cruz, and John, of Los Angeles, and a grandchild.

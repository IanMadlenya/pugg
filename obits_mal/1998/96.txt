
        Robert Friend, a poet, translator and educator, died on Jan. 12 in Jerusalem, where he lived. He was 84.
        Mr. Friend's first book of verse, ''Shadow on the Sun,'' was published in 1941. His work appeared in many publications, including The New Yorker, The Atlantic, Partisan Review and Commentary. Among his many books are ''Salt Gifts,'' ''Selected Poems,'' ''Dancing With a Tiger,'' ''Cats and Other People'' and ''The Next Room.''
        Born in Brooklyn, Mr. Friend studied at Brooklyn College, Harvard and Cambridge, and taught English literature and writing in the United States, Panama, France, England and Germany. He moved to Israel in 1950 and taught English and American literature at Hebrew University.

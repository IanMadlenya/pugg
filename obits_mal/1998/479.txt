
        Thomas A. Williams, the president of the Depository Trust Company in Manhattan, died April 25 at the United Hospital Medical Center in Port Chester, N.Y.
        He was 61 years old and died shortly after suffering a heart attack while sailing on Long Island Sound, according to William F. Jaenike, chairman and chief executive of Depository Trust.
        Mr. Williams, a lifelong resident of Rye, N.Y., graduated from the Hotchkiss School in 1955, from Yale University in 1959 and Yale Law School in 1962.
        After graduation he joined the Manhattan law firm of Milbank, Tweed, Hadley & McCloy, becoming a partner in 1971.
        In his 32 years at the firm he was involved in its regulatory and corporate work for such banking organizations as the Federal Reserve, the Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation and the Banking Department of the State of New York.
        Mr. Williams retired from Milbank, Tweed in 1994 to become the president of the Depository Trust, the securities clearinghouse in New York, and he held that position at his death.
        Mr. Williams is survived by his wife, Lammie; a daughter, Laura Williams Jodice of Corvallis, Ore.; three sons, Thomas A. Williams Jr. of Perrysburg, Ohio; J. Carter Williams of St. Louis, and David D. Williams of Ithaca, N.Y., and a brother, Howard H. Williams 3d of Washington.

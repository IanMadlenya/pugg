
        George L. Fichtenbaum, a retired travel industry official, died on Monday at Calvary Hospital in the Bronx. He was 66 years old and lived in Little Neck, Queens.
        He died of complications from cancer, his family said.
        Mr. Fichtenbaum was the executive director of the American Society of Travel Agents from 1967 to 1974 and wrote the history of the organization, "Passport to the World."
        He left the society to become president of American Sightseeing International, a network of tour companies, for 10 years. He then moved to Washington as executive vice president of the Travel Industry Association of America for three years, retiring in 1987.
        Before working in the travel industry, he was at the Better Business Bureau in New York City for 19 years, where he became vice president.
        He was born in Brooklyn, graduated from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology and served as a lieutenant in the Navy.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Gloria Ackerman; three daughters, Ronna Gotthainer of Cleveland, Helene Simon of Little Neck, and Janet Fichtenbaum of Bayside, Queens; a sister, Ruth Goodgold of Manhattan, and two grandsons.


        James Brennand, a former principal bassist with the New York City Opera Orchestra and a member of the New York City Ballet Orchestra, died on Saturday at Benedictine Hospital in Kingston, N.Y. He was 69 years old and lived in Shady, N.Y.
        He died of an aneurysm, said Susan Woelzl, director of publicity for the New York City Opera.
        Mr. Brennand was born in Newark. He attended the Juilliard School of Music and played with the NBC Symphony under Arturo Toscanini and later with the Symphony of the Air. He was also principal bassist of the American Ballet Theater orchestra in the 1940's and 50's. He retired from the City Opera and City Ballet in 1989.
        He is survived by his wife, Nancy; a son, Joseph, of New York City, and two sisters, Mary Kozicki of Jacksonville, Fla., and Margaret Faulhaber of Whiting, N.J.


        Arthur Vare, a former senior partner of the Wall Street firm of Kalb, Voorhis & Company, died on Monday at the DeWitt Nursing Home in Manhattan. He was 86 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Vare had suffered from Parkinson's disease and had suffered a series of strokes in recent years, said his son, Robert Vare.
        A native of Odessa, Russia, Mr. Vare immigrated to New York City with his family at the age of 7. He began his career on Wall Street at Lehman Brothers after graduating from New York University in 1927. Mr. Vare joined Kalb, Voorhis in 1955 and retired in 1982.
        He was a member of the Harmonie Club of Manhattan, a director of Brookdale Medical Center, in Brookdale, L.I., and executive vice president of the Weitzman Institute of America.
        Mr. Vare's second wife, Dr. Minnie Gallay Vare, died in 1976.
        In addition to his son, who lives in Manhattan, Mr. Vare is survived by his third wife, Beatrice.


        Albert Tavares, a stage and casting director, died on July 28 in Taunton, Mass. He was 39 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        He died of AIDS, said Les Ribaudo, a friend.
        Mr. Tavares garnered more than 50 production credits in film and theater. He was the casting director for Walt Disney's Oscar-winning animated feature "Beauty and the Beast," and had recently completed casting Disney's next animated film, "Aladdin."
        Mr. Tavares was the assistant director and casting director for the Marvin Hamlisch-Howard Ashman musical "Smile" on Broadway, and cast the original Off Broadway production of "Little Shop of Horrors."
        He also directed a number of Off and Off Off Broadway plays, and taught acting at his own studio.
        He is survived by his parents, Albert and Madeline Tavares of Taunton; two sisters, Debby Dunham of Taunton and Tracy, of New York City, and two brothers, Paul, of Taunton, and George, of Derry, N.H..

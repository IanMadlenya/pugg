
        James J. Patterson, a former vice president and assistant managing editor of The Daily News in New York, died yesterday in Sibley Memorial Hospital in Washington. He was 69 years old and lived in Longboat Key, Fla.
        He died of complications from diabetes, his family said.
        Mr. Patterson was the son of Capt. Joseph M. Patterson, who with his cousin, Col. Robert R. McCormick, founded The News in 1919.
        A 1944 graduate of West Point, Mr. Patterson rose to the rank of Army captain during service in Europe and Japan. He joined The News in 1949 as a reporter in the Washington bureau and worked in various positions before being named assistant managing editor in charge of The Sunday News in 1958.  Mr. Patterson is survived by his wife of 48 years, the former Dorothy Clarke.

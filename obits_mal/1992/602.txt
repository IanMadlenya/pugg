
        Earl Morris, former president of the American Bar Association, died today at the Charleston Area Medical Center, where he was being treated for a fractured vertebra after a car accident near here last Friday. He was 83 years old.
        The cause of death was a heart attack, the hospital said.
        Mr. Morris, who lived in Bexley, Ohio, was born in Springfield, Ohio, and was a Harvard Law School graduate. He was a founding partner in the Columbus law firm of Porter, Wright, Morris & Arthur. He served at various times as president of the Columbus and Ohio bar associations, and headed the A.B.A. in 1967 and 1968.
        Mr. Morris is survived by his wife, Jean; a daughter, Anne Morris Sward of Livingston, Mont., and a grandchild.

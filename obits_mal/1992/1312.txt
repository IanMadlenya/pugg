
        H. Gardner Ingraham, a lawyer whose career in New York City spanned nearly four decades, died on Tuesday at his home in Northport, L.I. He was 80 years old.
        Mr. Ingraham died of heart failure, said a son, Robert E., of Northport.
        Mr. Ingraham, who was a native of Brooklyn, attended Polytechnic Preparatory School and earned a bachelor's degree at Wesleyan University in 1933 and a law degree from Yale Law School in 1936. He was a clerk for Judge Thomas W. Swan of the United States Court of Appeals in Philadelphia and he was later a lawyer for the National Labor Relations Board, the Office of Solicitor General and the State Department. He interrupted his Federal career to serve as a lieutenant in the Navy in the South Pacific during World War II.
        In 1947 Mr. Ingraham was a co-founder of the Manhattan law firm of McLanahan, Merritt & Ingraham, which became Windels, Merritt & Ingraham in 1974. Mr. Ingraham retired from practice in 1975 and then opened a private practice in Northport until 1986.
        Mr. Ingraham is survived by his wife of 50 years, Barbara; four sons, Michael G., of Logan, Utah, Henry A., of Barnet, Vt., Stevens L., of Rochester, and Robert E., of Northport; a daughter, Polly Merritt, of New Haven; two sisters, Mary Bunting Smith of Hanover, N.H., and Winifred Warner, of Laurel Hollow, N.Y., and six grandchildren.


        John F. Luchsinger Sr., a Suffolk County civic leader and businessman, died on May 18 at his home in Patchogue, L.I. He was 71 years old.
        He died of natural causes, a son, John F. Jr., said.
        Mr. Luchsinger, who had recently retired as vice president of the Swezey's department-store chain, was a member of numerous civic and business organizations. He was a former president of the Patchogue Chamber of Commerce, the Patchogue Rotary Club and the New York Council of Retail Merchants. He was also a former chairman of the National Retail Merchants Association, the Brookhaven Town Planning Board and the Suffolk County Planning Commission, among other activities.
        He was born in Solvay, N.Y., and graduated from Syracuse University in 1942 with a bachelor's degree in business administration. During World War II he served in the Navy as a pilot and was discharged with the rank of lieutenant commander.
        He joined Swezey's shortly after returning from the war.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Marion Rex; two sons, John F. Jr., of Fayetteville, N.Y., and David, of Yaphank, L.I., and five grandchildren.


        Edward Feigen, the head of three furniture store chains, died on Wednesday at his home in Manhattan. He was 61 years old.
        He died of cancer, a family spokesman said.
        Mr. Feigen was the president and chief operating officer of Maurice Villency, which runs five luxury furniture stores under that name and eight popularly priced stores under the name Preferred Seating, all in the New York region. He was also the president of a related company that operates three Roche Bobois luxury stores in Philadelphia, Seattle and Scottsdale.
        Born in Manhattan, he grew up in Forest Hills, Queens. He graduated from Queens College and served in the United States Marine Corps.
        After working as the media director of an advertising agency, he became president and chief operating officer of Henshaw of Nassau, a furniture and carpet store in Levittown, L.I. He joined Maurice Villency in 1976 and became its president in 1988.
        Mr. Feigen was the board president of his cooperative apartment house and supported programs assisting homeless children.
        He was married and divorced.
        He is survived by a son, Edward, of Washington; a daughter, Nancy, of Florida, and a brother, Michael, of Harlingen, Tex.

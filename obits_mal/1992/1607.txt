
        Martin S. Thaler, a partner in the New York-based law firm of Weil, Gotshal & Manges, died on Monday at the Washington Hospital Center. He was 60 years old and lived in Clarksville, Md.
        He died of complications from abdominal surgery, said his wife, Mary O'Brien Thaler.
        In 1990 in Budapest, Mr. Thaler opened the firm's first office in Central Europe. He was among the first American lawyers to go to Eastern Europe as the cold war ended.
        Mr. Thaler was also an adjunct professor at the Georgetown University Law Center and received the center's Charles Fahi Distinguished Adjunct Professor Award for 1990-91.
        He was a native of Brooklyn and graduated from City College of New York and Yale University Law School. He was admitted to the Washington bar in 1958 and the Supreme Court bar in 1960.
        His first marriage, to Barbara Friedman Mishkin, ended in divorce. Besides his wife, he is survived by their daughter, Megan, of Clarksville; four children by his first marriage, Diane, of Bethesda, Md.; Amy, of Los Angeles; Paul, of Silver Spring, Md., and David, of Oxford, England; a sister, Rita Minsky of Potomac, Md., and a granddaughter.

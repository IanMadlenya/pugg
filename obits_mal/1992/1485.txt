
        Nicholas J. Miranda, a former Long Island newspaper executive and columnist, and a founder of the Queens Baseball Alliance, died on Tuesday at his home in Millbrook, N.Y. He was 84 years old.
        Mr. Miranda died of heart failure, said his son, Richard.
        Mr. Miranda also lived in the Jamaica Estates section of Queens. He was a newspaper production expert for the Newhouse group and the mechanical superintendent of The Long Island Press and The Long Island Star-Journal. He retired in 1977.
        Mr. Miranda wrote a Sunday column on baseball for the Long Island papers.  He helped to organize the Queens Baseball Alliance, which promotes sandlot and semiprofessional baseball teams in Queens and on Long Island.
        In addition to his son, Richard, of Island Park, L.I., Mr. Miranda is survived by his wife of 63 years, the former Georgiana Osmond; a daughter, Georgiana Bjornlund; eight grandchildren, and seven great-grandchildren.


        Dr. Jack Praver, a psychiatrist and former official of the Queens Children's Psychiatric Center, died on Sunday at Memorial Sloan-Kettering Cancer Center in Manhattan. He was 62 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        He died of cancer, said a daughter, Leslie.
        From 1962 to 1965, Dr. Praver was on the staff of the Creedmoor Psychiatric Center in Queens. In 1964, he was appointed director of outpatient and in-patient programs at the Queens Children's Psychiatric Center and served in that position until retiring in 1988. Dr. Praver also had a private psychiatric practice in Manhattan and, after his retirement from the Queens center, was a psychiatric consultant to Blue Cross and Blue Shield.
        In addition to his daughter, Leslie, of Milan, Dr. Praver is survived by his wife of 33 years, Lucetta; a daughter, Simone, of Charlotte, N.C.; two brothers, Albert and Paul, both of Queens, and three grandchildren.

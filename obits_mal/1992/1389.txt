
        Joseph H. McCann Jr., a retired lawyer for the International Business Machines Corporation, died on Saturday at his home in Stamford, Conn. He was 70 years old.
        He died after a long illness, his family said.
        Mr. McCann was a 1943 graduate of Holy Cross College who earned a law degree at the Boston University School of Law. He served with the United States Marine Corps in the Pacific in World War II and in the Korean War, reaching the rank of captain.
        He joined I.B.M. in 1955. He retired in 1987 after 32 years as a member of the I.B.M. law department, where he held various positions, including managing attorney.
        Mr. McCann is survived by his wife, Mary Darling McCann; five children, Joseph H. 3d of Greenwich, Conn., Robin Holland of Seattle, Lisa Frey of Vancouver, Wash., Karen Keating of Stamford, and Jamey Murphy of Darien, Conn.; his sister, Doris McCann Brick of Manhattan, and 11 grandchildren.

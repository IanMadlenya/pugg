
        Harry Lyon Morris, a Liberian delegate at sessions of the United Nations General Assembly from 1959 to 1980, died on Thursday at New York University Medical Center. He was 77 years old and had lived in Manhattan full time since 1980.
        He died of lung cancer, said his wife, Wilma.
        In 1967, Mr. Morris was elected chairman of the General Assembly's Fifth Committee, which deals with finances, for the Assembly's 22d session, and he was on the committee for 20 other sessions. In the late 1970's he also served as Ambassador at Large for Liberia.
        Mr. Morris was born in Monrovia, Liberia, the son of John Lewis Morris, who was Secretary of the Interior and of Public Works in Liberia in the 1920's. Harry Morris attended Methodist missionary schools in Liberia and also went to high school in the Bronx while living with his uncle, Walter F. Walker, then Liberia's Consul General in New York.
        Mr. Morris, who was also a grandson of Dr. Ernest Lyon, a onetime United States Minister to Liberia, lived in the United States from 1929 to 1934, when he returned to Liberia and worked in the rubber industry. He later become chief executive of Morris Rubber, a family company that cultivated rubber trees and processed rubber. In 1980 he turned the management of the company over to his son.
        In addition to his wife, the former Wilma Holland, whom Mr. Morris married in 1946, he is survived by two daughters, Constance Hope of Washington and Judith Morris de Celis of Manhattan; a son, William Lyon Morris of Kakata, Liberia; two sisters, Maude Conte of Chicago and Ellen Warah of Philadelphia; two brothers, Richelieu Morris of Valencia, Spain, and Edwin Morris of Kakata; and six grandchildren.

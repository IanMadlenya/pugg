
        Thomas W. Kelly, a retired Manhattan lawyer who was the longtime general counsel for the national Girl Scouts and for several industry associations, died on Saturday at his home in Larchmont, N.Y. He was 74 years old.
        Mr. Kelly died of complications of lung cancer, his family said.
        He specialized in securities and antitrust law and also represented the Girl Scouts for more than 25 years.
        After serving as a captain in the Army during World War II, he returned to Breed, Abbott & Morgan, where he had been a law clerk, and was promoted to partner in 1958 and later became a senior partner. He retired four years ago.
        Surviving are his wife of 47 years, the former Patricia Morris; three sons, Robert, of Milford, Conn., and Craig and Douglas, both of Manhattan; two daughters, Gail Hiler of Manhattan and Tracy Kelly of Milford, Conn.; a brother, Charles, of Red Bank, N.J., and three grandchildren.

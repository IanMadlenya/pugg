
        Ralph W. Pritchard, a retired national leader in the real estate business, died Jan. 16 at his home in La Grange, Ill.
        He was 72 years old and died of a heart attack, his family said.
        Mr. Pritchard was a past president of the La Grange Board of Realtors, Illinois Association of Realtors, the National Association of Realtors and the International Real Estate Federation.
        He was born in Chicago and graduated from the Stevens Institute of Technology in 1941. He served three years in the Navy, reaching the rank of lieutenant.
        Starting in real estate in 1949, he became president of Thorsen Realtors in La Grange and later chairman of Coldwell Banker-Thorsen and president of Nationwide, a relocation company in suburban Chicago. He retired two years ago after a decade as chairman of Pritchard Enterprises, a residential and commercial real estate investment concern.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Grace Ruth Marsh; two daughters, Cindy Ashby of Indianapolis and Melanie Awe of Dallas; a son, Kevin of La Grange, and nine grandchildren.


        John J. Gallagher Jr., a retired Wall Street executive, died on Monday at his home in Plandome Manor, L.I. He was 72 years old.
        He died of a heart attack, relatives said.
        Mr. Gallagher was an executive at the stock brokerage company of Merrill Lynch for 32 years and a former member of the New York Stock Exchange's Board of Governors.
        He was born in Brooklyn, was a graduate of Columbia University and served in the Merchant Marine during World War II.
        At Merrill Lynch he began as a trainee in 1948 and eventually rose to become a senior vice president and a director of the company from 1967 to 1972 and its head floor broker in the stock exchange from 1973 to 1980, when he retired.
        As a member of the stock exchange's board, he was chairman of the selection committee.
        His wife, Rosemary, died in 1990. He is survived by three daughters, Bridget of Cambridge, Mass., Patricia of Everett, Mass., and Deirdre of Plandome Manor; two brothers, Edward of Middletown, N.J., and Joseph of Upper Saddle River, N.J., and two sisters, Mary Ryder and Therese Brown, both of Brooklyn.


        Chandler H. Kibbee, a retired Philip Morris Companies executive, died on May 8 at the Sheridan County Memorial Hospital in Sheridan, Wyo. He was 84 years old and lived in Sheridan.
        He died of a stroke, his family said.
        Mr. Kibbee worked at Philip Morris in Manhattan from 1949 until his retirement in 1973. His posts included senior vice president for strategic planning, executive vice president for finance, chief financial officer, secretary, treasurer and board member.
        He also served on the advisory board of Chemical Bank, was a director of the Christiania General Insurance Company and was a trustee of the Museum of the American Indian of New York and the Thacher School of Ojai, Calif.
        Mr. Kibbee was born in Minneapolis. After graduating from Yale University in 1930, he went to work for the Salomon Brothers investment company. In World War II he served as a lieutenant in the United States Navy.
        Surviving are his wife of 45 years, the former Mercedes K. Kellough; a son, Peter V. of McLean, Va.; a daughter, Sharon Kibbee de Lobo of Manhattan, and a granddaughter.

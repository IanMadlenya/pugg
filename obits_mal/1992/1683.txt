
        Arthur A. Herman, a retired administrative law judge for the National Labor Relations Board, died on Monday at his home in Long Beach, L.I. He was 73 years old and also had a home in Pompano Beach, Fla.
        He died after suffering a heart attack, his family said.
        Mr. Herman worked for the Federal labor agency in Brooklyn and Manhattan from about 1960 until he retired in 1986. He started as a staff lawyer and became a supervising attorney and then a judge.
        Born in the Bronx, Mr. Herman grew up there and in Brooklyn. After graduating from City College, he earned a law degree at New York University.
        Surviving are his wife of 51 years, the former Ruth Lippel; three daughters, Susan Mesibov of Potsdam, N.Y., Ali Edelstein of Briarcliff Manor, N.Y., and Cookie Woltz of Chappaqua, N.Y.; a brother, Irving, of Falls Church, Va., and six granddaughters.

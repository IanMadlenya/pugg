
        Sidney R. Diamond, a real-estate developer and philanthropist who contributed to the restoration of Bethesda Fountain in Central Park, died on Thursday at New York Hospital-Cornell Medical Center. He was 91 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        He died of complications from congestive heart failure, said his son, William J., the regional administrator of the United States General Services Administration.
        Born in the Bronx, Mr. Diamond graduated from Columbia College in 1920 and received a law degree from Columbia Law School in 1922.
        With his brother, Arthur, he developed properties in Manhattan, the Bronx and Queens in the 1940's, including apartment buildings at 170 East 77th Street, 177 East 77th Street and 160 East 89th Street.
        He was president of the Young Men's and Young Women's Hebrew Association of the Bronx from 1949 to 1953. Through the Diamond Brothers Foundation, he contributed to Columbia College and Law School, Lenox Hill Hospital, United Jewish Appeal and the Jerusalem Foundation, as well as the fountain restoration in 1988.
        Mr. Diamond is survived by his wife, Marie Helene; his son, William, of Manhattan; a daughter, Joan Steinberg, of Manhattan and Oyster Bay, L.I.; his brother, Arthur, of Manhattan; sisters Esther D. Bookman of the Bronx and Ethel Schonwald of Pompano Beach, Fla.; six grandchildren, and two great-grandchildren.

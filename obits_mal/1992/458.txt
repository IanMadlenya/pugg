
        Philip E. Burnham, a retired teacher, headmaster and adviser to the College Entrance Examination Board, died Thursday at his home in Hopkinton, N.H. He was 79 years old and died of cancer, his family said.
        Mr. Burnham worked at St. Paul's School in Concord, N.H., from 1946 to 1978 as a teacher, head of the English department, summer school dean and assistant headmaster. He also was headmaster of the Fessenden School in West Newton, Mass., from 1981 to 1983.
        For 20 years he worked part time for the college examination board, rising to chief reader in charge of 200 teachers who graded the English compositions and also chairman of the English Examiners Committee, which prepared the test. He also worked full time for the board for a year.
        Mr. Burnham was born in Corona, Queens. He graduated from Bowdoin College in 1934 and earned a master's degree from Middlebury College in 1941. In addition to his years at St. Paul's and Fessenden, he taught at Harvard University, Phillips Exeter Academy, New England College and schools in Florida, New York and Vermont.
        He wrote numerous articles for professional journals and several books on grammar and rhetoric. He also edited books on literature and developed an English course for WGBH, the public television station in Boston.
        Surviving are his wife of 56 years, the former Nella Barber; two sons, Philip Jr. of Newton Centre, Mass., and Jonathan of Sutton, N.H.; a sister, Beryl Webber of Kennebunk, Me.; a brother, Allen,of Manhasset, L.I., and five grandchildren.

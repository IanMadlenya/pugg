
        Stanley Weintraub, a businessman and philanthropist, died on Saturday at St. Vincent's Hospital in Manhattan. He was 87 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        He died of cancer, said his wife, Livia.
        Mr. Weintraub was chairman of the Major Realty Corporation, a real estate syndication company, which he founded in the 1950's and has been involved in many big real estate developments.
        He was also president of the Lombardy Hotel in Manhattan, and a founder and principal of Puritan Beef Company, a leading international food purveyor.
        A native of Manhattan, Mr. Weintraub earned bachelor's and law degrees at New York University.
        Mr. Weintraub was a major supporter of cancer research at Memorial Sloan-Kettering Hospital, Columbia Presbyterian Medical Center and other hospitals. He established the Z. R. Weintraub Foundation to finance cancer research in honor of his first wife, Zelda, who died from cancer in 1957.
        Mr. Weintraub is survived by his wife of 32 years, Livia; a stepdaughter, Ariane Grossman of Manhattan; a sister, Elynor Cohn of Manhattan, and three stepgrandchildren.

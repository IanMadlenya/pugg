
        Harold Potter, a film archivist and director of operations of 20th Century Fox's Movietone News division, died on Tuesday at his home in North Woodmere, L.I. He was 60 years old.
        He died of a heart attack, said his wife, Joyce.
        For the last 15 years, Mr. Potter maintained the newsreel collection of 20th Century Fox, which dates to 1919. He contributed film and research to many projects, including the films "Zelig," "Shining Through" and "This Is Elvis," and the Broadway production "The Will Rogers Follies." His television credits include "Life Goes to the Movies," "That's Hollywood," "A Current Affair" and "CBS This Morning."
        Mr. Potter was born in Brooklyn and graduated from the University of Arizona and New York University's School of Business.
        In addition to his wife, he is survived by three sons: Seth, of New York City; Robert, of Los Angeles, and Warren, of Tucson, Ariz., and a brother, Max, of Fort Lauderdale.

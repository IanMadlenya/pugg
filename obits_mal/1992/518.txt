
        Daniel Cowin, an investment banker and real-estate executive, died on Tuesday at his home in Manhattan. He was 70 years old.
        He died of cancer, his family said.
        Mr. Cowin served on the boards of 26 corporations. He was a founding director of Continental Telephone, which merged into G.T.E. He was an early director of Berkshire Hathaway, where he became associated with Warren Buffett, the investor.
        From 1958 to 1970, Mr. Cowin was a general partner at Burnham & Company. He was a director of Drexel Burnham Lambert until 1988. He was also the board chairman and chief executive of the United National Corporation from 1972 to 1985, and later board chairman of Goldome National Corporation.
        At his death, he was the managing director of Dome Capital Corporation, a trustee of New Plan Realty Trust and a director of Christiana Companies and Cantel Industries. Most of his companies invested in real estate.
        Mr. Cowin was a noted collector of Art Deco, American folk art and 1930's photography and was a trustee of the Museum of American Folk Art and the International Center of Photography.
        Surviving are his wife of 39 years, the former Joyce Josephs Berger; a daughter, Dana, of Manhattan; two sons, Kenneth, of Manhattan, and Andrew, of Washington; a brother, David of Manhasset, L.I., and a sister, Sarinda Dranow of Manhattan.

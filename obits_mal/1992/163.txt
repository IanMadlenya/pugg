
        Benjamin Newman, a real-estate lawyer in Manhattan, died on Monday at White Plains Hospital. He was 70 years old and lived in Scarsdale, N.Y.
        He died of a cerebral hemorrhage, his family said.
        Mr. Newman was born in the Bronx. He was a graduate of City College and the New York University Law School. During World War II he served five years in the South Pacific as a lieutenant in the Navy.
        Since 1952 he was a partner in the Manhattan law firm of Bachner, Tally & Mantell and its successor, Bachner, Tally, Polevoy and Misher. For 25 years he was also an arbiter in New York City's Small Claims Court.
        He served on the board of directors of the Beth Abraham Hospital in the Bronx for more than 30 years.
        Surviving are his wife, Anita; two sons, Ira and Jonathan, both of Manhattan; a daughter, Amy of Scarsdale; a brother, Herman of Queens; a sister, Dorothy Weiss of Queens, and a grandson.

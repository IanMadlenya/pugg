
        The Rev. Robert T. Taylor, a former general secretary of the American Bible Society, died on Sunday at Westminster Gardens Health Center in Duarte, Calif. He was 87 years old and lived in Duarte.
        Mr. Taylor worked at the society's national headquarters in Manhattan from 1949 until he retired in 1969. He was in charge of fund raising, administration and promotion, including a project that sent 10 million Bibles to Japan after World War II.
        He wrote "Wings for the Word," a history of the society, an interdenominational organization that translates the Bible into many languages and publishes and distributes copies at cost and free of charge for people unable to pay. He also wrote "Bible Baseball," a book of quizzes.
        A major program in his tenure was Today's English Version, also known as Good News for Modern Man or the Good News Bible.
        The son of a Presbyterian minister, Mr. Taylor was born in Sullivan, Ill. He graduated from Millikin University in 1926 and from Princeton Theological Seminary in 1929. In 1931 he became the minister of the First Reformed Church in Tarrytown, also known as the Old Dutch Church of Sleepy Hollow that Washington Irving wrote about. In 1944 Mr. Taylor joined the Bible Society's Chicago office.
        His wife of more than 60 years, the former Helen Hutchison, died in 1990.
        He is survived by his daughter, Sarah Taylor Dawson of Riverside, Calif., and a granddaughter.

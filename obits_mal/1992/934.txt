
        Mitchell May Jr., a retired insurance executive, died Tuesday at Lawrence Hospital in Bronxville, N.Y. A resident of Bronxville, he was 89 years old.
        He died of pneumonia, his family said.
        A 1923 graduate of Dartmouth College, Mr. May founded Mitchell May Jr. Inc., insurance brokers with offices in New York and Los Angeles. He was its president from 1926 to 1965, insuring movie and television studios and Hollywood actors.
        The firm then merged with another, Bayly, Martin and Fay, and Mr. May was its chairman until 1978. Later, until his retirement in 1983, he was a consultant to Alexander & Alexander Inc., an international insurance brokerage based in Manhattan.
        Mr. May is survived by his wife, the former Dorothy Levin; a daughter, Patricia Fitzgibbons of Amherst, Mass.; a son, Mitchell May 3d of Tuxedo Park, N.Y., and six grandchildren.


        William O. Beers, who began as a part-time cheese maker and became president and board chairman of Kraft, one of the world's largest food companies, died on Sunday at Scottsdale Memorial Hospital North in Scottsdale, Ariz. He was 77 years old and lived in Wilmette, Ill. He also had a winter home in Carefree, Ariz.
        He died of respiratory illness, his family said.
        Mr. Beers started at Kraft in 1929 and in 1969 became the president and chief operating officer. In 1972 he was named chairman and chief executive. He retired from full-time work in 1979, remaining as a director until 1985.
        He also served as the vice chairman of the Conference Board, a business-sponsored research group, and as chairman of the Grocery Manufacturers of America and of the Food Drug Law Institute. In addition, he was a member of the Business Roundtable and the Joint Commission on Accreditation of Health Care Organizations.
        Mr. Beers was a former board member of Sears, American Airlines, United States Steel, Manufacturers Hanover Trust and First National Bank of Chicago. He had also been chairman and president of the Lyric Opera of Chicago, vice chairman at Evanston Hospital and on the boards of the Chicago Orchestral Association, the Art Institute of Chicago and the Tuskegee Institute.
        Born in Lena, Ill., he grew up in Monroe and Platteville, Wis., and graduated from the University of Wisconsin in 1937.
        His first wife, Mary Elizabeth Holmes, died. He is survived by his wife of 38 years, the former Frances Lemaux Miller; three daughters, Marila Miller Beatty of Northfield, Ill., Mary Elizabeth Beers Sumner of Simi, Calif., and Barbara Ann Beers Guzzardo of Hinsdale, Ill.; two sons, Richard W. Beers of Barrington, Ill., and Duncan R. Miller of Cairo, Egypt, and nine grandchildren.


        C. Harold Taylor, who was a lawyer in Manhattan for more than 40 years, died May 3 at the Cottage Hospital in Nantucket, Mass. He was 84 years old and lived in Nantucket.
        He died of a stroke, his family said.
        Mr. Taylor started practicing law with the firm of Sullivan & Cromwell in 1932. In World War II, he enlisted with the United States Army, served in France and Germany and reached the rank of captain. He was a judge advocate in the war crimes trials.
        After the war, he resumed his legal career at the firm of Hall, Haywood, Patterson & Taylor, where he became a senior partner. He was also a director of the Duriron Company, a manufacturer. He retired in 1973.
        Mr. Taylor was born in Falls River, Wis., and grew up in Oklahoma. He graduated from Yale University in 1929 and from its law school in 1932.
        In retirement, Mr. Taylor became a member of Nantucket's Land Council, Arts Council and Civic League and also was a trustee of the Nantucket Ornithological Association.
        He is survived by his wife of 56 years, the former Juliet Baldwin; a daughter, Juliet, and two sons, Harold and Willard, all of Manhattan; a brother, Russell of La Jolla, Calif., and six grandchildren.


        Israel M. Labovitz, a retired Federal budget official and expert on aid to state and local governments, died on Jan. 1 at George Washington University Hospital. He was 84 years old and lived in Mitchellville, Md.
        He died of heart failure, his family said.
        Serving in the Bureau of the Budget from 1941 to 1956, he helped devise the accounting system used for intergovernmental aid, was chief of the Social Welfare/Federal-State Relations Branch from 1946 to 1951 and assistant to the chief of the Labor and Welfare Division from 1951 to 1956.
        He spent the next 17 years as a senior Congressional research specialist in social welfare for the Library of Congress. He also worked for the Federal Reserve System.
        He was born in Duluth, Minn., and graduated from the University of Minnesota. He was an instructor at the University of Chicago and an economist at the Illinois State Tax Commission before joining the Federal budget office.
        Surviving are his wife, Margaret; three sons, David and John of Washington, and Peter of Alexandria, Va.; four grandchildren and a brother, E. Maurice of La Jolla, Calif.

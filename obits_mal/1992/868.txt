
        Angus C. Littlejohn, for many years a leading foreign investor in Brazil, died on Thursday at North Shore University Hospital in Glen Cove, L.I. He was 75 years old and lived in Locust Valley, L.I.
        He died of complications of emphysema, his family said.
        Mr. Littlejohn, stationed as a naval attache in Rio de Janeiro in World War II, returned to Brazil in 1946 to co-found Deltec Securities, which became a major Latin American business bank. He became a local representative and adviser for foreign companies, including Pittsburgh Plate Glass Corporation, W. R. Grace & Company, Quaker Oats Company and Guggenheim Brothers.
        He also co-founded several insurance, finance and manufacturing enterprises and served on the boards of 16 manufacturing companies. He was the director of the American Chamber of Commerce for Brazil of Sao Paulo.
        Returning to the United States in 1964, he was an executive and consultant for the U.S. Trust Company of New York, serving five years as president of its international division. For three years he was deputy chairman of the Carbomin Group.
        Mr. Littlejohn was born in Marshall, Tex. He graduated from Dartmouth College and the University of Virginia Law School and rose to the rank of lieutenant in the Navy.
        He is survived by his wife of 46 years, the former Mercedes Daly Riggs; a daughter, Mercedes Dorson of Manhattan; two sons, Angus Jr. of New Canaan, Conn., and Duncan of Sao Paulo; a stepdaughter, Elizabeth Hansen, of Newport News, Va.; a stepson, Edward G. Riggs 3d of Miami; two sisters, Jean Aaberg of Pacific Palisades, Calif., and Margaret Reeves, of Manhattan, and seven grandchildren.

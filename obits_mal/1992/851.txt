
        Dr. George C. Sawyer Sr., a chemical engineer and drug developer who became an executive, professor and proponent of corporate social responsibility, died July 2 at the Butterfield Hospital in Cold Spring, N.Y. He was 66 years old and lived in Garrison, N.Y.
        He died of a lung infection, his family said.
        Dr. Sawyer, a professor of management at City College in Manhattan since 1980, wrote six books and numerous articles on management, mainly on the need for companies to act responsibly and address social issues.
        Dr. Sawyer worked on drug research and production for Lederle Laboratories of American Cyanamid from 1950 to 1968. He was among the scientists named in the patent for Streptokinase, a cardiac medication.

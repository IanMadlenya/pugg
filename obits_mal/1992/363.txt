
        Robert Volkening, a retired Wall Street broker and financial analyst, died on Sunday in Princeton Hospital in Princeton, N.J. He was 87 years old and lived in Meadow Lakes, a retirement community in Hightstown, N.J., where he moved in 1988 after 50 years as a resident of Summit, N.J.
        The cause of death was pneumonia, his family said.
        A graduate of Cornell University with an M.B.A. from Harvard, Mr. Volkening was a partner for 25 years in Burton, Dana & Company. He moved to Shearson Lehman Brothers in 1965 and retired from the company as a financial consultant four years ago.
        Mr. Volkening is survived by his wife, Rosalie Cox Volkening; a daughter, Josephine White of Portola Valley, Calif.; a son, Lindsay of Philadelphia; three stepchildren, Virginia Tulloch of Summit, Winston H. Cox of New York City, and Charles C. Cox of Little Silver, N.J.; four grandchildren and six stepgrandchildren.

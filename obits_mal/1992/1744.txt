
        William H. Avery, a lawyer and civic leader, died on Saturday at his home in Winnetka, Ill. He was 87.
        His family said he died after a long illness.
        He had been a partner and chairman of Sidley & Austin, one of the country's largest law firms, which he joined in 1930. He retired as chairman of the firm in 1975.
        He was prominent in civic, charitable, educational and religious affairs in Chicago, and played a leading role in efforts to make legal services available to the poor. He was a graduate of Princeton University and Harvard Law School.
        He is survived by his wife of 58 years, Jean; a daughter, Nancy Pressier of Houston; two sons, Cameron Avery of Winnetka and the Rev. Richard Avery of Spokane, Wash.; eight grandchildren and six great-grandchildren.

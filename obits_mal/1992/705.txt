
        Sterling A. Stoudemire, a scholar of Spanish, died Tuesday at the University of North Carolina Hospital in Chapel Hill. He was 89 years old and lived in Chapel Hill.
        Dr. Stoudemire died of heart failure, a university spokeswoman said.
        Dr. Stoudemire enrolled at the University of North Carolina in 1919 and remained as a student, teacher and administrator until his retirement 54 years later. He graduated in 1923, earned a master's degree in Spanish in 1924 and a doctorate in 1930. He began teaching in 1923 and was chairman of the Romance language department from 1949 to 1964.
        Dr. Stoudemire specialized in literature of 16th-century explorers. He wrote Spanish textbooks and translated many works into English.
        His work was honored by the Mexican Government. He was also a past president of the South Atlantic Modern Language Association and an editor of the Modern Language Journal.
        His first wife is deceased. His survivors include his wife of 46 years, the former Mary Arthur Billups; a son, Cranford of Manhattan; a daughter, Marian Hawkins of Rockville, Md.; a sister, Mae Armstrong of Chapel Hill, and five grandchildren.

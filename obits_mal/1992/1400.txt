
        Sidney Gaines, a retired lawyer and insurance company executive, died on Tuesday at the Mary Manning Walsh nursing home in Manhattan. He was 76 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        He died of complications of multiple sclerosis, his family said.
        Mr. Gaines was a co-founder and senior partner of Friedlander, Gaines, Cohen & Rosenberg, a Manhattan law firm. It specializes in insurance, welfare and pension funds and labor law and also represented the taxicab industry.
        He was also a founder, director and chief counsel of the Eastern World Insurance Company, an international company.
        He served on the board of Ben Gurion University of the Negev since 1985 and was on the New Rochelle Human Rights Commission from 1965-73.
        Mr. Gaines, who was born in Manhattan, grew up there and in the Bronx. He earned his bachelor's and law degrees at New York University. During World War II he served as a sergeant in the Army in Europe and the Pacific.
        After the war he returned to law practice and served six years as a senior attorney in the New York State Insurance Department.
        His first marriage lasted 18 years until his wife, the former Rita Kalcheim, died.
        He is survived by his wife of 26 years, the former Lis Dreifuss; four sons, Robert Gaines of New Rochelle, Irwin Gaines of Naperville, Ill., Steven Green of New Rochelle and Kenneth Green of Israel; two daughters, Frances Gaines Haxton of White Plains and Vivian Green of Demarest, N.J., and 12 grandchildren.


        Benjamin Davis Gilbert, a private investor who was involved in widely divergent enterprises, died suddenly on Jan. 15 at his home in Darien, Conn. He was 84 years old.
        He died of circulatory problems in his sleep, his family said.
        Mr. Gilbert, a native of Rochester, graduated from Phillips Andover Academy and Williams College. He worked on a series of projects for the military in World War II, after which he turned to private business. His ventures included oil exploration, perfume manufacturing, development of pollution-control systems and a cattle and coconut plantation in Jamaica. In the 1970's, he was a principal in the corporation that then owned the Mutual Broadcasting System.
        An avid sailor, Mr. Gilbert was a past commodore of the Stamford Yacht Club.
        He is survived by two daughters, A. Mary Gilbert of Manhattan, and Sue Gilbert of Hidden Hills, Calif.; four sons, Racey, of Furlong, Pa., Lee, of Woolrich, Pa., John H., of Huntington Beach, Calif., and Dan, of Arlington, Va., and nine grandchildren.

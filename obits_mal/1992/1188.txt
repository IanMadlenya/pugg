
        William L. Dill, a lawyer and a former mayor of Montclair, N.J., died on Monday at his home in Upper Montclair, N.J. He was 89 years old.
        No cause of death was given.
        Mr. Dill served two terms as Mayor of Montclair from 1953 to 1960. He was previously chairman of the town's Board of Adjustment, chairman of its planning board and president of the town redevelopment agency.
        Mr. Dill, who was born in Paterson, N.J., graduated from Princeton University in 1925 and Harvard Law School in 1928. He was chairman of the New Jersey State Bar Judicial and County Prosecutor Appointments Committee from 1972 to 1982.
        Mr. Dill's daughter, Bonnie, died in 1985.
        He is survived by his wife of 63 years, the former Dorothy Monro; a son, William L. 3d, of Old Lyme, Conn.; three sisters, Barbara Barber, of Columbia, S.C., Carolyn, of Fort Myers, Fla., and Jane Carter, of Naples, Fla.; a brother, James, of New Suffolk, L.I.,; four grandchildren, and two great-grandchildren.


        Rafe Blasi, a writer, an editor and a motion picture executive, died on Sunday at the Terence Cardinal Cooke Health Care Center in Manhattan. He was 55 years old and lived in Los Angeles.
        He died of pneumonia, his family said.
        Mr. Blasi was the head of marketing at Alive Films, an independent production and distribution company. Among the films he handled were Alan Rudolph's "Moderns" and Jean-Jacques Beineix's "Betty Blue," which was nominated for a foreign-language Oscar in 1986. He also served as a unit press agent for "48 Hours," "The Godfather Part III" and other movies. Earlier in his career he was the East Coast story editor for M-G-M.
        Mr. Blasi was the author of four novels, including "Nothing to It" and "The Hit Man." Before moving to Los Angeles, he was an editor at The New York Film Bulletin and at Cahiers du Cinema in English, and edited The Independent Film Journal.
        He is survived by his mother, Sadie, and a sister, Mary Lamiroult, both of Middle Village, Queens, and another sister, Rose, of Erie, Pa.

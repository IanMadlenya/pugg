
        H. Kendell Nash, a Boston broadcaster, died on Sunday at Spaulding Hospital in Boston. He was 54 years old.
        His family said he died after a long illness.
        Mr. Nash, who lived in Weston, Mass., was a native of Brooklyn and earlier worked in New York City as a marketing and management adviser for small-business owners.
        Since 1980 he owned and operated radio station WILD in Boston. The station's format, directed mainly at black listeners, features urban-contemporary music.
        Mr. Nash is survived by his wife, the former Geraldine Foster, and his mother, Marguerite Tazewell Shepard of Brooklyn.

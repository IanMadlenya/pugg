
        R. Stewart Kilbourne, a business and civic leader who served in the cabinet of Nelson A. Rockefeller when he was Governor of New York, died on Sunday in Brunswick, Ga. He was 87 and lived in Sea Island, Ga.
        Mr. Kilbourne died at the Southeast Georgia Regional Medical Center, said his son, George B. Kilbourne of Palm Beach, Fla.
        Governor Rockefeller named Mr. Kilbourne, who did not use his given first name of Robert, as his State Conservation Commissioner in 1966. He served as commisisoner until 1970, when he became a special assistant to the Governor on conservation affairs.
        Mr. Kilbourne was a native of Manhattan. He graduated from the Groton School and attended Yale from 1923 to 1925. That year he joined the family textile firm of William Skinner & Sons and served as its president from 1947 to 1961, when he retired.
        He was a trustee of the Equitable Life Assurance Society of the United States from 1946 to 1977. In the 1960's and 1970's he was a member of many conservation associations, including the Hudson River Valley Commission and the Bedford Historic Review Commission.
        He was a member of the Society of Mayflower Descendants, the New England Society of New York and the Sea Island Club.
        Mr. Kilbourne's first wife, the former Barbara Briggs, died in 1968. A son, R. Stewart 3d, died in 1966.
        In addition to his son, Mr. Kilbourne is survived by his wife, the former Jane Houk; a daughter, Belle Taylor, of Hillsborough, Calif.; a brother, William S., of Hightstown, N.J.; a sister, Mrs. William Hudnut, of Rochester; seven grandchildren, and six great-grandchildren.

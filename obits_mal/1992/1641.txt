
        Dr. Claude E. Forkner Sr., a former professor at Cornell University Medical School and an internist, died on Dec. 20 at his home in DeLand, Fla. He was 92.
        Dr. Forkner died in his sleep. He had suffered from diabetes, said his wife of 65 years, Marion Sturges DuBois Forkner.
        For 30 years, from the mid-1930's to the mid-1960's, Dr. Forkner was on the faculty of Cornell University Medical Center and an attending physician at New York Hospital. His patients included Senator Robert A. Taft, the Ohio Republican, the royal families of Iran, Saudi Arabia and Nepal, and film stars, including Fredric March and Gertrude Lawrence.
        A native of Stevensville, Mont., Dr. Forkner served with the Army medical corps during World War I, then earned bachelor's and master's degrees from the University of California at Berkeley.
        Dr. Forkner graduated from Harvard Medical School in 1926 and did further study at the Rockefeller Institute for Medical Research in New York City, the University of Freiburg in Germany, the National Hospital in London and Harvard Medical School.
        He joined the Cornell medical faculty in 1937.
        He was president of the New York Cancer Society from 1960 to 1961. In addition to his wife, Dr. Forkner is survived by two daughters, Helen Haskell, of Darien, Conn., and Lucy Greene, of Greenwich, Conn.; 11 grandchildren, and four great-grandchildren.
        Correction:  December 31, 1992, Thursday
        An obituary on Tuesday about Claude E. Forkner, an internist and former professor at Cornell University Medical School, omitted a survivor. He is a son, Dr. Claude E. Forkner Jr. of West Cornwall, Conn., and Scottsdale, Ariz.

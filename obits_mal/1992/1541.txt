
        Robert Lindsay, a specialist in financial management who taught economics and finance at New York University for the last 28 years, died on Saturday at his home in Ridgewood, N.J. He was 67 years old.
        He died of cancer, said his son John.
        Until his death, Professor Lindsay was chairman of the program in markets, law and ethics at the N.Y.U. Graduate School of Business.
        From 1971 to 1975, he was executive director of the New York State Council of Economic Advisers.
        Born in Greenville, S.C., he graduated from the University of North Carolina, then received an M.A. and Ph.D. in economics from Harvard. He was a senior economist and chief of domestic research at the Federal Reserve Bank of New York from 1954 to 1964.
        Besides his son John, who lives in San Francisco, he is survived by his wife, the former Helen Poland; two other sons, Robert of Portland, Me., and David of New York City; his mother, Helen Lindsay, and a sister, Jane Hinds, both of Atlanta; a brother, the Rev. Arthur M. Lindsay of Hampton, S.C., and one grandchild.

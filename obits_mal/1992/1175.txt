
        Edmund H. Kerr, a senior partner in the international law firm of Cleary, Gottlieb, Steen & Hamilton, died yesterday at his home in Greenwich, Conn. He was 67 years old.
        He died of cancer, his family said.
        Mr. Kerr, a native of Pittsburgh, saw wartime service with the Army in North Africa and Europe before graduating from Stanford University in 1949. He earned his law degree at Stanford Law School, where he was managing editor of the Law Review and a member of the Order of the Coif.
        Upon finishing law school in 1951, Mr. Kerr joined Cleary, Gottlieb. He became a partner in 1962. He specialized in corporate and securities law and wrote extensively on the Investment Company Act of 1940. His Manhattan firm has branches in Europe and Asia.
        Mr. Kerr is survived by his wife, the former Claire Winkler; four daughters, Susan K. Dregone and Jill K. Kinnear of Darien, Conn., Susan K. Durrell of Fairfield, Conn., and Jennifer M. Kerr of Greenwich; two sons, Richard M. of Hilton Head, S.C., and Daniel R. of Lakeville, Conn.; a stepdaughter, Wendi Winkler of Greenwich, and seven grandchildren.

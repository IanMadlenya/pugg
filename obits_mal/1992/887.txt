
        Norman Farquhar, a Washington investment banker and former president of the Chevy Chase Savings Bank, died on Tuesday at Georgetown University Hospital. He was 71 years old and lived in Washington.
        He died of complications from prostate cancer, his family said.
        Mr. Farquhar, who held a law degree from National University in Washington, retired from the Maryland bank in 1991 after three years as its president. He was a partner in the investment banking firm Alex Brown & Sons and was manager of its Washington branch from 1958 to 1979.
        Mr. Farquhar's first wife, the former Ann Randolph Jennings, died in 1980. In 1981, he married Elinor K. Brown, who survives. Also surviving are his daughter, Katherine Farquhar of Sandy Spring, Md.; four sons, Douglas B., of Baltimore, Edward P., of Montpelier, Vt., William T. N., of Bethesda, Md., and Witt J., of Washington; his mother, Helen N. Farquhar, also of Sandy Spring; his brother, Gordon N. of Weekapaug, R.I.; two sisters, Margaret F. Adelfio of Chevy Chase and Ann F. Forbush of Baltimore; three step-daughters, and nine grandchildren.

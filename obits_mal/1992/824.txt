
        Thomas Raymond Hauck, an architect and architectural historian, died on Wednesday at Roosevelt Hospital in Manhattan. He was 47 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        He died of cancer, said a friend, Nicholas Hubby.
        Mr. Hauck was the architect for the Long Island University campus system from 1979 to 1986, and collaborated with Albert Bush-Brown, then the college's chancellor, on many articles on design. He also wrote and illustrated several works, among them "Bellville: Defining an Architectural Identity in Ohio," and collaborated on a walking guide to Roslyn, L.I.
        He was resident architect for the Waterfront Historic Area League in New Bedford, Mass., and supervised the restoration of the seaport district there. He was also the resident architect of Planting Fields Arboretum in Oyster Bay, L.I. Most recently, he was an architect with the Eggers Group in New York City.
        He is survived by his mother, Rita Hauck of Toledo, Ohio; two sisters, Gloria Horne of Grosse Ile, Mich., and Margie, of Atlanta; four brothers, Michael, of Oregon, Ohio; Tim, of Mansfield, Ohio; Charles A., of New Bedford, and Paul, of Toledo, and a grandmother, Cora Hauck of Toledo.


        Ray Danton, an actor and director whose career embraced film, theater and television, died on Tuesday at Cedars-Sinai Medical Center in Los Angeles. He was 60 years old.
        He died of complications from a kidney ailment, said Jill Danton, a daughter-in-law.
        Mr. Danton began acting at age 12 on the radio show "Let's Pretend." In New York City in the early days of television, he appeared in dramatic roles on shows including "Studio One" and "Playhouse 90."
        In the 1950's, he went to Hollywood to work for Universal Studios, where he married Julie Adams, an actress. They were divorced in 1974.
        Among his film credits are "I'll Cry Tomorrow" (1956) with Susan Hayward, "The Rise and Fall of Legs Diamond" (1960), in which he played the title role, and "A Majority of One" (1962). He also starred in "The George Raft Story" (1962). Working in Italy and Spain
        His stage work included "Tiger at the Gates," with Robert Ryan, "Carnival" and "110 in the Shade."
        From the mid-1960's to the mid-70's, he lived and worked abroad, directing films in Italy and Spain. After returning to the United States, he began directing television shows for Universal including episodes of "Cagney and Lacy," "Quincy," "Fame," "Mike Hammer," "Dallas" and "Dynasty."
        His companion was Jeannie Austin.
        He is survived by two sons, Steve and Mitchell Danton, both of Los Angeles.

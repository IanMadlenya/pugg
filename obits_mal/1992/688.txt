
        John J. Flaherty, an executive in the home appliance industry, died on May 29 in Chicago, where he was attending the annual Consumer Electronics Show. He was 64 years old and lived in West Hartford, Conn., and Manhattan.
        He died of a heart attack, his family said.
        He helped introduce many new products, including popcorn poppers, hamburger makers and hand-held, battery-operated sewing devices.
        Mr. Flaherty was the president of the Singer Sewing Company and regional vice president of Singer North America.
        Before joining Singer in 1988, he was an executive at the Scovill Manufacturing Company for 23 years. His posts there included general manager of its Puritron division and president of its Hamilton Beach division. Earlier he worked 10 years at General Electric.
        Mr. Flaherty was born in Boston. After graduating from Boston College, he worked for the Department of Defense and the Hoover Company.
        Surviving are his wife of 39 years, the former Phyliss Fitzgerald; three sons, John 3d, of Hackettstown, N.J., Richard, of Cincinnati, and Michael, of Valhalla, N.Y.; three daughters, Nancy Tehan of Auburn, N.Y., and Deborah Olmsted and Michele McCarthy, both of West Falmouth, Mass.; a brother, Francis X., of Foxboro, Mass.; three sisters, Theresa Woodsome of Vero Beach, Fla., Sister Mary Concilla of Catskill, N.Y., and Evelyn Bectal of Bow, N.H., and 14 grandchildren.

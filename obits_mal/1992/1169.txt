
        Louis M. Fribourg, a former New York City judge and author on real-estate law, died on Thursday at Lenox Hill Hospital in Manhattan. He was 84 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        He died of congestive heart failure, his family said.
        In 1973 Mr. Fribourg was appointed a Civil Court judge and served in the Housing Court.
        Later he became the head of the hearings unit at the city's Housing Authority and a hearing officer in the Department of Housing Preservation and Development and in the Transit Adjudication Bureau. He was also a special master in State Supreme Court.
        Mr. Fribourg specialized in real-estate law. For many years he was a columnist in the Real Estate Forum, the monthly magazine of the Real Estate Board of New York. He also wrote two reference books for lawyers.
        Mr. Fribourg was born in Brooklyn. After graduating from Columbia University in 1929 and its law school in 1931, he formed a law firm in Manhattan with his brother, Albert.
        He was married for more than 20 years to the former Julia Heilbroner. After she died, he remarried.
        He is survived by his wife of 25 years, the former Ruth Goldstein; a daughter, Anne Fribourg of Manhattan, and a sister, Eugenie M. Fribourg of Brooklyn.

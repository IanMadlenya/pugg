
        James Milton Stuart, an investment banker and venture capitalist, died on Friday at St. Vincent's Hospital in Manhattan. He was 59 years old and lived in Purchase, N.Y.
        Mr. Stuart's family reported his death yesterday and said the cause was a heart attack.
        Mr. Stuart was president of the Rockhill Capital Corporation, an investment banking and venture capital firm, and of Integrated Technologies Inc., a computer software company. He was also president of Stuarco Oil Company, an oil and gas exploration and production concern.
        Mr. Stuart, with his father and brother, was a founder of Stuart Brothers, a Wall Street securities firm that operated from the mid-1960's to the early 1980's. He was a director of GAM Funds Inc., Warner Insurance Services Inc., and Celadon Group Inc.
        A native of New York City, Mr. Stuart graduated from Brown University in 1954 and from the Columbia Business School in 1958.
        Mr. Stuart's first wife, Ellen Menke Stuart, died.
        In addition to his brother, Alan of Harrison, N.Y., Mr. Stuart is survived by his wife of 15 years, Eve Rosenbloom Stuart; two sons, James Jr. and John, and two daughters, Mary and Nina all of Purchase; a stepson, Eddie Stern, and two stepdaughters, Kara Stern and Amanda Stern, all of Purchase and New York City; another stepdaughter, Kathy Kaufmann of Manhattan; his stepfather, Morton Stone of Cleveland, and two stepgrandchildren.


        Morris Ratner, a retired real-estate executive and a philanthropist, died on Wednesday at the Englewood Hospital in Englewood, N.J. He was 86 years old and lived in Tenafly, N.J., and Hollywood, Fla.
        He died of heart failure, his family said.
        Born on the Lower East Side of Manhattan, Mr. Ratner worked his way through high school and college with a variety of jobs.
        After graduating from City College, he became a certified public accountant and specialized in real estate. He founded the Ratner Management Corporation, a property investment and management company based in Manhattan. He retired in 1990.
        Mr. Ratner served on the boards of many organizations, including the Jewish Theological Seminary, Temple Emanu El of Englewood, N.J., the Jewish Home and Rehabilitation Center of Bergen and Hudson Counties, the United Jewish Community of Bergen and North Hudson, the American Friends of Hebrew University, the Jewish Community Center on the Palisades and the Jewish Federation of Broward County, Fla.
        He is survived by his wife of 60 years, the former Rosalind Miller; a son, Victor of Chevy Chase, Md.; a daughter, Linda Zimmerman of Fort Lee, N.J.; a brother, Herman of New Rochelle, N.Y.; two sisters, Gerry Prosnitz and Bess Platzker, both of Manhattan, and four grandchildren.

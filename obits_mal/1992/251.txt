
        Daniel J. Moore, a Federal bankruptcy judge in Newark since 1987, died at his home in Short Hills, N.J., on Thursday. He was 63 years old.
        He died of a heart attack, according to the Jacob A. Holle Funeral Home in Maplewood.
        Judge Moore, who was born in East Orange, earned his law degree at Seton Hall Law School in 1957 and was in private practice in Newark from then until his appointment to the bench. He was also a certified public accountant, and taught a course in accounting for lawyers at Seton Hall.
        A recreation commissioner in Millburn, N.J., for many years, he founded the Millburn Junior Football League. He was elected to the National Football Foundation Hall of Fame in 1985 for his contributions to amateur football.
        Judge Moore is survived by his wife, the former Florence McMahon; three sons, John M. of Martinsville, N.J., Brian T. of Randolph, N.J., and Owen D. of Key West, Fla.; two daughters, Kathleen A. Moore of Alexandria, Va., and Eileen M. Moore of Warrenton, Va.; a sister, Betty McCloskey of Westfield, N.J.; and four grandchildren.

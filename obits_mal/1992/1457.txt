
        Bernard M. Baruch Jr. a former member of the New York Stock Exchange, died on Thursday at the Mount Sinai Medical Center. He was 90 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Baruch was born in Manhattan to Barnard M. Baruch, the financier, who died in 1965, and his wife, Annie Griffen Baruch. He grew up in Manhattan, attended Milton Academy in Milton, Mass., and graduated from Harvard College in 1923.
        He was a member of the stock exchange from 1928 to 1936. He was commissioned in the Naval Reserve in 1937 and served in World War II, in Naval Intelligence, rising to the rank of commander. He was in the Naval Reserve until 1962.
        Mr. Baruch's World War II decorations included the Order of the British Empire.
        His 1932 marriage to Winifred Beatrice Mann ended in divorce in 1945.
        He is survived by his sister, Renee Baruch Samstag, of Manhattan.

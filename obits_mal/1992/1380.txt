
        Philip B. Hinerfeld, a retired advertising executive for Pepsi-Cola and the Chase Manhattan Bank, died Nov. 11 in Boca Raton, Fla. He was 85 years old and lived in Boca Raton.
        A native of Scranton, Pa., who graduated in 1929 from the Wharton School of the University of Pennsylvania, Mr. Hinerfeld joined Pepsi-Cola in 1959 as director of advertising after working in New York advertising agencies like Kenyon & Eckhardt Inc.
        He was soon promoted to vice president of advertising and directed the company's "Pepsi Generation" and "For Those Who Think Young" campaigns in conjunction with the advertising agency Batten, Barton, Durstine & Osborn.
        Retiring from Pepsi-Cola in the late 1960's, Mr. Hinerfeld became vice president of advertising for the credit card division of the Chase Manhattan Bank.
        Mr. Hinerfeld retired from business in the mid-1970's and moved to Boca Raton. He also supported research into the causes of Alzheimer's disease. His wife, Sally, died of Alzheimer's disease in 1990.
        Mr. Hinerfeld is survived by two sons, Joseph B. Hinerfeld, of Atlanta, and Stewart N. Hinerfeld, of Ames, Iowa; a daughter, June Restler, of Atlanta; a brother, Joseph B. Hinerfeld, of Pound Ridge, N.Y., and nine grandchildren.

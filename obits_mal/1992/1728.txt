
        Frank X. Zambetti, a retired construction engineer for the Federal Housing Administration and a civic leader in Riverdale, Calif., died on Thursday at his son John's home in Santa Monica. He was 77.
        He died of cancer, his family said.
        Mr. Zambetti worked at the F.H.A. for more than 30 years. After retiring from the agency, he worked as a construction consultant. He was also a member of John Zambetti Inc., a residential construction company founded by his father.
        A native of the Bronx, Mr. Zambetti helped found the Riverdale post of the American Legion, served on the boards of the Riverdale Yacht Club and the Loyola School in Manhattan, and was president of the Riverdale Sanitation Corporation, which operates the community's sewer lines.
        During World War II he flew 50 missions in Europe as a B-17 navigator for the Army Air Corps. He reached the rank of lieutenant and was awarded the Distinguished Flying Cross and Purple Heart.
        Besides his son John, Mr. Zambetti's survivors include his wife of 44 years, the former Gloria Martino; two sons, Francis, of Princeton, Mass., and Tedd,y of Santa Monica; a daughter, Christina Davis of Manhattan; four brothers, Emil, of Forked River, N.J., Edward, of Stuart, Fla., and John and George, both of the Bronx,; three sisters, Mary Brimo of Bricktown, N.J., Catherine Tancredi of the Bronx, and Joan Kruger of Queens, and five grandchildren.

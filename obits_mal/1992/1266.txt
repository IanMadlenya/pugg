
        Frank A. Weg, a lawyer who was an expert on insurance cases, died on Tuesday at his home in Manhattan. He was 64 years old.
        His family said he died of heart failure.
        Mr. Weg was the founding senior partner of Weg & Myers, a Manhattan law firm. He specialized in insurance law, especially property casualty cases, and represented clients who sued insurance companies. He also testified at legislative hearings on insurance laws.
        For many years he lived in Westchester County and was active in the Democratic party there. He was a candidate for the party's nomination for State Assembly and County Executive and was the state finance chairman for Hubert H. Humphrey's candidacy for President in 1968.
        Mr. Weg was born in Manhattan and grew up in the Bronx. He studied at Long Island University and earned his law degree at New York Law School.
        He also founded the Westchester Men's Diet Club.
        He is survived by his wife of 40 years, the former Anna Williams; a son, William of Rockaway, N.J., and two daughters, Sylvia and Tressa Weg, both of Manhattan.

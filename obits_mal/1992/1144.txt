
        Timothy J. Adams, a retired journalist, died Sunday at his home in the Chevy Chase section of Washington. He was 64 years old.
        He died of cancer, his family said.
        In 1968 Mr. Adams was named managing editor of The Washington Monthly at its founding by Charles G. Peters. Seven years later he became deputy editor of The Wilson Quarterly as it was being started by the Woodrow Wilson International Center for Scholars.
        He joined Cox Newspapers in 1977 as a reporter and retired from journalism in 1984.
        Mr. Adams is survived by his wife of 42 years, the former Judith Walker Clark; three sons, Bailey Adams of Silver Spring, Md., Peter Adams of Yarmouth, Me., and Jason Adams of Los Angeles; two daughters, Diana Hatchett of Kensington, Md., and Liza Thompson of Baltimore; a brother, Anthony Adams, of Danbury, Conn., and seven grandchildren.

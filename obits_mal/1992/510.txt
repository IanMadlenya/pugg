
        Frank C. Brown, a retired management consultant, died on Tuesday at Stamford Hospital in Stamford, Conn. He was 85 years old and lived in Stamford.
        He died of heart disease, his wife said.
        As head of the Frank C. Brown & Company consulting firm, he specialized in critical evaluations, legally known as "due diligence" reports, on companies seeking to borrow money from investment bankers. His firm also advised on policies and techniques for reducing costs, expanding operations, plant relocations, mergers and acquisitions.
        A native of Boston, he graduated in 1930 from the University of Maine, where he later became a trustee.
        He began working in management for New England Telephone. In World War II he was a Pentagon consultant expediting military production. After the war he worked briefly at two companies before founding his firm in 1947 in Rockefeller Center.
        His first marriage ended in divorce.
        He is survived by his wife of 22 years, the former Frances Kelly.

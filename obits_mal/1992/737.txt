
        Louis Biancolli, an author and a music critic, died on Saturday at the New Milford Hospital in New Milford, Conn. He was 85 years old and lived in New Milford.
        He died of complications after a hip fracture, said his daughter Amy Ringwald of Albany.
        Mr. Biancolli wrote 13 books, including "The Book of Great Conversations"; "The Beethoven Companion," with Thomas Scherman; "The Analytical Concert Guide"; "Forbidden Childhood," with Ruth Slenczynska, and "Mary Garden's Story," with Mary Garden. He spoke many languages and translated Dante's "Divine Comedy" in rhythmic blank verse.
        Mr. Biancolli was a music critic for The New York World-Telegram and Sun from 1928 to 1966 and the annotator for the New York Philharmonic Society from 1941 to 1949.
        He earned a master's degree from New York University in 1936 and did graduate work in Russian at Columbia University from 1936 to 1938. He was a member of the American Council of Learned Societies and Phi Beta Kappa.
        He is survived by his wife, Jeanne Mitchell Biancolli; another daughter, Margaret Weissbach of Roslyn Estates, L.I., and four granddaughters.

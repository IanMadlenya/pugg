
        Harry F. Reiss Jr., a retired leader in the accounting profession, died Monday at his home in Manhasset, L.I. He was 75 years old.
        He died of emphysema, his family said.
        Mr. Reiss was a former partner in the accounting firm of Ernst & Young in Manhattan. He was also a past president of the New York State Society of Certified Public Accountants, the founding chairman of the New York State Board of Accountancy and a former treasurer and director of the American Institute of Certified Public Accountants.
        In his 46-year career at Ernst, Mr. Reiss was promoted to a partner in 1955, appointed to head the New York office in 1957 and later directed technical auditing and accounting and Securities and Exchange Commission matters for the eastern region. He retired in 1978.
        He was the treasurer and a director of the New York City Council of Churches for 17 years and a past president of the Manhasset school board.
        He was born in Brooklyn and graduated from Bernard M. Baruch College.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Louise Weaver; a son, Paul, of Manhasset; two daughters, Susan R. Carroll of Mount Sinai, L.I., and Catherine M. of Manhattan, and a granddaughter.


        Haskell R. Coplin, Professor Emeritus of Psychology at Amherst College and a scholar of human sexuality, died Sunday at St. Mary's Hospice in Tucson, Ariz. He was 70 years old and lived in Amherst, Mass.
        He died of cancer, said his wife, Jo Ann Shinaberger Coplin.
        Dr. Coplin was a member of the Amherst College faculty for 42 years before retiring last June. He was also a staff consultant to the University of London and a visiting professor at several other colleges and universities.
        He directed the Amherst Summer Institutes for Sex Educators and Therapists from 1977 to 1983. Earlier, he and P. N. Sarrell of Yale University conducted a series of seminars on human sexuality at New England colleges and universities that was broadcast over National Public Radio.
        Besides his wife, he is survived by two daughters, Marilyn Coplin-Newberry of Huntington, Mass., and Joan Coplin Olson of Acton, Mass.; two sons, Robert B. Coplin of Sun Valley, Idaho, and S. Kent Coplin of Tucson; a brother, E. M. Coplin of Moreno Valley, Calif., and a grandson.

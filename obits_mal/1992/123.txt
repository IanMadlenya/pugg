
        Leonard L. Osten, an advertising salesman and executive for national magazines for more than five decades, died on Friday at North Shore University Hospital in Manhasset, L.I. He was 87 years old and was a resident of Great Neck.
        He died of natural causes after a brief hospitalization, relatives said.
        Mr. Osten was born in Chicago, where he started in advertising at the Ziff-Davis Publishing Company. Moving to New York in 1945, he became the advertising manager of Hi-Fi Stereo Buyers Guide and also worked for Esquire and Cue magazines. He retired in the mid-1980's.
        He was a past president of the Great Neck Community Concert Association and was active in the Boy Scouts.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Charlotte Silverstone; a daughter, Margery Loewenwarter of Manahttan; a son, Donald, of Nashville, and two grandchildren.


        Russell J. Cooper, the founder, owner and president of the Consolidated Metals Corporation in Dover, N.J., died on Tuesday at his home in Essex Fells, N.J. He was 74 years old.
        He died of cancer, his family said.
        A native of Manhattan, Mr. Cooper graduated from Rutgers University in 1948. He founded his company, a maker of stainless steel pipe, in 1970, and was still its head at the time of his death.
        Mr. Cooper is survived by his wife, Martha; a son, R. John Cooper 3d of Short Hills, N.J.; two daughters, Sharilen Cooper Gregg of Essex Fells, and Christienne Cooper Daher of Lafayette, N.J.,; a stepson, William C. W. Lamb of Essex Fells, and four grandchildren.


        John D. Baker, a former vice chairman and director of the investment firm of Dean Witter Reynolds, died yesterday at his home in Bush Hill Falls, Pa. He was 89 years old.
        He died of heart failure, his family said.
        Mr. Baker worked for several investment firms during a career that spanned more than 50 years on Wall Street. In the 1930's he joined the firm of A. M. Kidder, a predecessor to the firm of Kidder Peabody, where he became managing partner.
        In 1951 he joined Reynolds & Company as managing partner and in 1958 became its senior partner. When Reynolds and Dean Witter merged in 1978, Mr. Baker became vice chairman and a director of the combined company. He retired in 1981.
        During his career Mr. Baker was a Governor and Treasurer of the Association of Stock Exchange Firms and a member of the nominating committees of the New York and American stock exchanges. He was also a trustee of Bennett College, the Fifth Avenue Presbyterian Church and a member of the North Carolina Society.
        He was born in Jacksonville, Fla., and graduated from Davidson College in North Carolina.
        Mr. Baker is survived by his wife, the former Alice Yorke; three daughters, Joan Baker of Santa Fe, N.M., Barbara Mallory of Tuxedo Park, N.Y., and Alice Cullen of Devon, Pa.; a sister, Margaret Hicks, and a brother, Thompson S. Baker, both of Jacksonville; six grandchildren, and two great-grandchildren.

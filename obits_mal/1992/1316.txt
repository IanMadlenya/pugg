
        Richard Grossman, a retired corporate lawyer, died on Sunday at Highland Park Hospital in Chicago. He was 82 years old.
        He died of complications from pneumonia, his family said.
        Mr. Grossman, who lived in Lake Forest, Ill., worked for 40 years for Mayer, Brown & Platt in Chicago and retired in 1978 as a partner.
        He represented a coalition of domestic airlines in negotiations and contracts with the City of Chicago for the development of O'Hare International Airport. He also represented a consortium of United States and foreign steel producers.
        As counsel to Continental Bank, he developed bank financing innovations and also worked on acquisitions, takeovers and financing.
        Mr. Grossman, who was born in Chicago, graduated from the University of Chicago in 1930. He received a law degree from Harvard in 1933 and started at Mayer, Brown & Pratt that year.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Jean Weaver; two sons, Richard W. Grossman of Auckland, New Zealand, and Alan W. Grossman of St. Simons Island, Ga.; and a daughter, Jill W. Grossman of Middletown, Conn.

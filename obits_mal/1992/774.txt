
        Ernest E. Norris, a former executive vice president and director of the BBDO New York advertising agency, died at the Princeton Club in Manhattan on Monday. He was 69 years old and lived in John's Island, Fla.
        Mr. Norris died of a heart attack, said Philippe Krakowsky, a spokesman for BBDO.
        He joined BBDO in 1959, after working for Joseph E. Seagram & Sons, the beverage and distilling company, and two other advertising agencies, Young & Rubicam and Benton & Bowles. At BBDO, Mr. Norris's projects included the introduction of Campbell's Chunky Soups line, and of Wisk laundry detergent for the Lever Brothers Company.
        Mr. Norris retired in 1980 and moved to Florida.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Alice Wagner; a son, Ernest E. 3d, of Sea Cliff, N.Y.; two stepsons, Philip Sunkel 3d, and Sean Sunkel, both of Ridgefield, Conn.; a granddaughter, and two stepgrandchildren.

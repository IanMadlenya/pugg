
        Samuel Mindel, a developer who was active in many philanthropic organizations, died on Saturday at North Shore University Hospital in Manhasset, L.I. He was 64 years old and lived in Great Neck.
        Mr. Mindel died of cardiopulmonary arrest after a brief illness, said his daughter, Marlene.
        Mr. Mindel was born in Poland and during World War II spent several years in a Nazi concentration camp. He emigrated to the United States in 1950.
        After serving in the United States Army, Mr. Mindel came to New York City and in 1952 founded the Hydraulic Plumbing and Heating Corporation, which became one of the largest contracting companies in the metropolitan area. He also built and renovated numerous residential and office buildings.
        Mr. Mindel was a member of both the National Council and the North Shore cabinet of the United Jewish Appeal, a trustee of Brookdale Hospital, and a founder and a past president of Temple Torah, in Westbury, L.I. He was also a founding trustee of the Holocaust Resource and Archives Center, which is now at Queens College.
        Mr. Mindel is survived by his wife, Mary; a daughter, Marlene, of Great Neck; two sons, Joseph, of Manhattan, and Alan, of Great Neck; a brother, Norbert, of Chicago; and four grandchildren.


        John I. White, who sang cowboy songs on NBC Radio in the 1920's and 30's, died on Thursday at the Ward Homestead in Maplewood, N.J., where he lived. He was 90 years old.
        He died of pneumonia, said Rich Bradley of the William A. Bradley & Son Funeral Home in Chatham, N.J.
        Mr. White furnished the voice of the Lonesome Cowboy, who sang between acts of "Death Valley Days" on NBC Radio. He also wrote articles about cowboys, for American Heritage and other magazines, and books about writers of cowboy songs.
        He was born in Washington and graduated from the University of Maryland in 1924. He later worked for 38 years for a map-making company in Convent Sation, N.J.
        Mr. White is survived by his wife, Augusta; a son, Jonathan White of Naples, Fla.; a daughter, Jennifer Fischer of Chatham; a sister, Grace Hammond of Tucson, Ariz.; a brother, H. A. White of Wickenburg, Ariz.; six grandchildren, and two great-grandchildren.

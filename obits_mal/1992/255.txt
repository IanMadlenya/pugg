
        Dr. Gray Huntington Twombly, a professor of gynecology at New York University School of Medicine for 20 years until he retired in 1973, died on Tuesday at Hartford Hospital in Connecticut. He was 86 years old and lived at the Doncaster life-care community in Bloomfield, Conn.
        He died after a brief illness, his family said.
        Dr. Twombly, a former resident of Hartford, received a medical degree from Harvard Medical School in 1929, graduating second in his class. Before his appointment at N.Y.U., he was an assistant professor of cancer research and assistant professor of clinical gynecology and obstetrics at Columbia University College of Physicians and Surgeons. He specialized in radical pelvic surgery and the treatment of cervical cancer.
        He was a past member of the board of deacons of Riverside Church.
        Dr. Twombly was predeceased by his first wife of 54 years, the former Mary Forester Field. He is survived by his second wife, the former Margaret Olmstead Ford; a son, Robert G. Twombly of Austin, Tex.; two daughters, Helen Watkins of St. Paul and Edith Eddy of Palo Alto, Calif.; nine grandchildren and one great-grandchild.

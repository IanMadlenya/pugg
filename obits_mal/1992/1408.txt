
        Eric Burroughs, a stage and radio actor from the 1930's to the 50's, died on Nov. 12 at the Bronx Veterans Administration Hospital. He was 81 years old and lived in Morrisania, the Bronx.
        Mr. Burroughs was Manhattan trained as an actor in Berlin. He performed in many stage and screen productions there before returning to the United States in 1930. Orson Welles, in his 1935 production of "Macbeth," for the Federal Negro Theater Project, cast Mr. Burroughs as Hecate after he enlarged the role and changed the character to that of a man.
        Mr. Burroughs had parts in many stage productions, including "The Petrified Forest." He was also seen in the film "Odds Against Tomorrow."
        He is survived by his wife, Melissa Broome Burroughs; a daughter, Carola, of Brooklyn, and a son, Norris, of Manhattan.

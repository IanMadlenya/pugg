
        Bernard S. Moskow, a noted periodontist and dentistry educator, died on Thursday at Columbia Presbyterian Medical Center in Manhattan. He was 62 years old and lived in Engelwood, N.J.
        He died of lymphoma, his family said.
        Dr. Moskow was a clinical professor of dentistry at the Columbia University School of Dental and Oral Surgeryand a research scientist and also maintained a periodontal practice in Ho-Ho-Kus, N.J.
        His specialty was histopathology, the microscopic study of disease progression, wound healing and response to various treatments. He lectured internationally for 25 years, wrote more than 150 scientific papers, contributed to several books and was honored by the Northeastern Society of Periodontology.
        He assembled a book of artworks portraying dentistry over the ages and was also an accomplished amateur photographer.
        Dr. Moskow was born in Philadelphia. He graduated from Temple University and also earned his dental degree there. He did postgraduate work in periodontology for another degree at the University of Pennsylvania.
        He served as a captain in the Air Force.
        He is survived by his wife of 19 years, Marion; a son, Anthony of Englewood; a sister, Harriet Cohen of Havertown, Pa., and his mother, Esther Moskow of Philadelphia.

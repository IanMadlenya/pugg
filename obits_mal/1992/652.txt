
        David H. Sears, a writer on modern dance, died on Wednesday at the New York University Medical Center. He was 44 years old and lived in Jersey City.
        Mr. Sears died of cancer, his brother, Richard, said.
        Mr. Sears was a graduate of New York University, where he received a teaching degree. He wrote about dance in Dance News and Ballet Review. At the time of his death, he was at work on a book on Martha Graham and her use of Greek themes in her choreography.
        Mr. Sears also acted in summer stock and performed with the Niccolo Marionettes in the mid-1970's.
        His companion was Antonio Sanchez.
        Mr. Sears is survived by his mother, Claudia Bryant, of Falmouth, Mass., and his brother, of Cohasset, Mass.

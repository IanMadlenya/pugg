
        William T. Woodhull, a former newspaper advertising executive, died Nov. 3 at Charlton Memorial Hospital in Fall River, Mass. He was 80 years old and lived in Little Compton, R.I.
        Mr. Woodhull died of congestive heart failure, said his wife, Harriet.
        Mr. Woodhull was president of William T. Woodhull Company, a New York City-based company that helped companies place advertisements in newspapers. He was a past president of the Association of Publishers Representatives of New York City.
        In addition to his wife, Mr. Woodhull is survived by a daughter, Elizabeth A. Bersing, of Virginia Beach, Va.; a son, William T. Jr., of Bronxville, N.Y.; eight grandchildren, and five great-grandchildren.


        Francis A. McAnaney, a real-estate lawyer in New York City, died on Tuesday at United Hospital in Port Chester, N.Y. He was 85 years old and lived in Rye, N.Y.
        He died of heart failure, said a son, Edward G. McAnaney 2d of Manhattan.
        A native of Yonkers, Mr. McAnaney graduated from the University of Notre Dame in 1929 and from Harvard Law School in 1932.
        After World War II, Mr. McAnaney became counsel to the development firm of Webb & Knapp and helped William Zeckendorf to assemble the property for the United Nations complex. As counsel to the Galbreath-Ruffin Corporation (now the Galbreath Company) in the 1950's, he helped to develop office buildings, including the Mobil Building on East 42d Street.
        At the time of his death he was president of the Peter B. and Adeline W. Ruffin Foundation, which supports secondary and higher education.
        Mr. McAnaney's wife, the former Katherine McClatchy, died in 1979. He is survived by five daughters, Mary Elizabeth Barth, of Bedford, N.Y., Sheila Katherine Kostanecki, of Darien, Conn., Kathleen Glaser, of San Diego, Maureen M. Mokrzycki, of Rye, N.Y., and Joan Fay, of Summit, N.J.; three sons in addition to Edward, Francis A. Jr., of Manhattan, Brian T., of Darien, Conn., and Kevin G., of Bethesda, Md., and 25 grandchildren.


        Dr. David W. Molander, an internist and expert on cancer who practiced in New York City for 36 years, died yesterday during a stay at his summer home in Chatham, Mass. He was 69 years old and also lived in Naples, Fla.
        He died after being taken to Cape Cod Hospital in Hyannis, said a representative of the Nickerson Funeral Home in Chatham, who did not know the cause of death.
        A native of Iron Junction, Minn., Dr. Molander received his medical degree from the University of Michigan Medical School. He was affiliated with New York Hospital and Memorial Sloan-Kettering Cancer Center and was clinical professor of medicine at Cornell University. Dr. Molander edited three texts on Hodgkin's disease and diseases of the lymphatic system.
        Dr. Molander retired in 1987.
        His first wife, the former Nancy Price, is dead. Dr. Molander is survived by his second wife, Marian Jordan Molander; a daughter, Susan Piatek of New Fairfield, Conn.; a son, John, of Chatham; four stepsons, David Jordan, of Atlanta, Bruce Jordan, of Scarsdale, N.Y., Jeff Jordan, of Tempe, Ariz., and Dean Jordan of Alexandria, Va., and two grandsons.


        Leonard Dalsemer, a retired president of the International Paper Company and a philanthropist, died on Wednesday at his home in Montecito, Calif. He was 85 years old and also lived in Manhattan.
        He died of heart failure after suffering a stroke, his family said.
        Mr. Dalsemer's family owned Lord Baltimore Press, a carton manufacturer. When the company was purchased by International Paper, he became the Lord Baltimore division president and a vice president of the parent company and later its president. He also served on the boards of Bessemer Securities, Clevepak Corporation, Henley Company, Northeast Airlines and Bahamas Air.
        For 15 years he was chairman of the John A. Hartford Foundation, a medical research and care philanthropy, during which its funds grew from $12 million to $300 million. He also headed the executive committee of the Community Arts Association in Santa Barbara, Calif.
        Mr. Dalsemer was born in Baltimore and was a graduate of Johns Hopkins. He was divorced from his first wife.  His second wife, the former Emily Dulaney, died in 1980.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Cecelia Andrew Neville; a daughter, Rhodena Dalsemer of Manhattan; two sons, Richard of Carmel Valley, Calif., and Patrick of Winter Haven, Fla.; two stepdaughters, Cecelia Neville Lord of Santa Barbara and Susan Dulaney of Clinton, Iowa; two stepsons, Maurice Neville of Santa Barbara and David Dulaney of Clinton, and 11 grandchildren.


        Hal Schaffel, a television and film producer, died on Wednesday in Los Angeles. He was 78.
        The cause of death was complications after a heart attack and bypass surgery in December, said his son Robert, of Jersey City.
        Mr. Schaffel began his career as the producer of the radio show "Author Meets Critics" for WMCA in New York City. He began his career in television in the 1950's as the producer of the NBC children's show "Howdy Doody," and he was also the producer of "The Patti Page Show." He later became the production supervisor of the CBS series "The Defenders."
        Among the films he supervised are "Midnight Cowboy," "The Angel Levine" and "The Main Event."
        Since 1986, Mr. Schaffel was a production executive with the Completion Bond Company.
        In addition to his son Robert, he is survived by his wife, Lynn; another son, Kenneth Morse of Los Angeles; a sister, Juliette Klugman of Philadelphia, and two stepchildren, Susan Minoret and Richard Uchitel, both of Florida.

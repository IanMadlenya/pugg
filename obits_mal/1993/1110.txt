
        Donald H. McCampbell, a retired property management executive, died Aug. 9 at Sloan Kettering hospital in New York. He was 78 years old and lived in Manhattan and in Quogue, Long Island, and Manalapan, Fla.
        The cause was cancer, his family said in announcing his death on Friday.
        During 36 years with Allied Maintenance Corporation of New York, Mr. McCampbell rose to president and vice chairman. The company became one of the leading airport and commercial building services companies in the country.
        Born in Kansas City, Mo., Mr. McCampbell graduated from the University of Kansas City and the University of Missouri Law School.
        He is survived by his wife, Catherine Evans McCampbell of Manalapan, Fla.; a daughter, Laura of Manhattan; two sons, Gerry of Idyllwild, Calif. and Brian of Manchester, Vt., and three grandchildren.

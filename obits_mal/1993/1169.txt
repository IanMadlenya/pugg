
        Jerome Farmer, the former managing partner of a major accounting company and a retired business executive, died on Thursday at the Sloan-Kettering Cancer Center in Manhattan. He was 73 and lived in Manhattan, Stonington, Conn., and Tucson, Ariz.
        For more than 20 years Mr. Farmer worked for J. K. Lasser & Company, a leading accounting firm and publisher of a popular annual income-tax guidebook. He served as managing partner and managing director of its international division. Lasser eventually merged with Deloitte & Touche.
        After leaving Lasser, in the early 1970's he joined the American Recreation Group, a manufacturer of bicycles and camping equipment. He served as chairman and chief executive officer.
        He was also vice chairman of the Superior Farming Company, a California grape producer, and on the boards of Berkey Photo, Advanced Polymer Systems and the Syntex Corporation. Received Bronze Star
        Mr. Farmer was born in Manhattan. He was a graduate of City College and earned a master's degree in business at New York University.
        In World War II he served on the China-Burma-India theater in the 14th Air Force as an engineer officer. He reached the rank of major and was decorated with a bronze star.
        A sailor, Mr. Farmer competed in several Pacific and Bermuda races and cruised on the Baltic Sea. He was a former commodore of the Knickerbocker Yacht Club in Port Washington, L.I.
        Survivors include his wife, the former Harriet Eiger; a son, Richard of Manhattan; two daughters, Randye Farmer of Manhattan and Lisa Paulen of Pleasantville, N.Y., and six grandchildren.

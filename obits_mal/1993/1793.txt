
        Harmon Duncombe, a partner in general practice at the Manhattan law firm of Fulton, Duncombe & Rowe for the last 47 years, died on Saturday at Lenox Hill Hospital. He was 84 and a Manhattan resident.
        The cause of death was cancer, the firm said.
        Mr. Duncombe was born in Bogota, N.J., and graduated from Harvard University in 1931. He also received his law degree from Harvard, where he was an editor of The Law Review. He served as a special assistant to the Chief of the Military Intelligence Service in World War II, rising to the rank of lieutenant colonel, and was awarded the Legion of Merit.
        He was admitted to practice before the United States Supreme Court. He was president and a director of the Ambrose Monell Foundation, whose charities include hospitals, scientific research, museums and the performing arts. He was also vice president and director of the G. Unger Vetlesen Foundation, which supports scientific research, and secretary and director of the van Ameringen Foundation, which gives money to mental-health and social-welfare programs. All are based in New York City.
        He is survived by his wife, Virginia; a daughter, Kathleen, of Nantucket, Mass.; a son, Steven, of Syracuse; a sister, Lois Lovett of Fair Haven, N.J.; a brother, Alfred, of Brockton, Mass.; five stepchildren, Jed Day, Mary D. Laird, Katherine D. Moyer, Scott Day and George Day, and a grandson.

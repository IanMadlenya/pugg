
        Dr. Roy N. Barnett, a pathologist associated with Norwalk Hospital, Yale University School of Medicine and, most recently, Mount Sinai Medical Center, died on Friday at the Carolton Convalescent Home in Fairfield, Conn. He was 79 and lived in Westport.
        He had a long illness after a stroke a year ago, his family said.
        Dr. Barnett was born in Woodmere, L.I. He earned his medical degree at Yale in 1938 and taught at the medical school there from 1946 to 1982. He served as a pathologist and director of laboratories at Norwalk Hospital from 1947 to 1982. The hospital recognized his work in standardizing clinical pathology in 1986 with the dedication of its Roy N. Barnett Laboratory Information System.
        His association with Mount Sinai from 1982 until last year was as a consultant to the Pathology Department and professorial lecturer at the School of Medicine.
        Dr. Barnett contributed more than 100 articles to medical journals. His work also appeared in the McGraw-Hill Encyclopedia of Science and Technology.
        He was a founding member and longtime president of the Westport Nature Center for Environmental Activities. He was the founding chairman of the Westport Conservation Commission.
        He is survived by his wife of 52 years, Margaret Davidson Barnett; a daughter, Judge Edith Barnett of Washington; two sons, John R., of Norwalk, Conn., and Dr. Ted D., of Rush, N.Y.; two sisters, Natalie Konheim of Longboat Key, Fla., and Carol Kidder of Plymouth, N.H., and four grandchildren.

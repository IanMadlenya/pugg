
        Robert Arthur Schlichting, a retired investment banker in New York, died on Monday at Morristown (N.J.) Memorial Hospital. He was 67 and lived in Madison, N.J., and Sarasota, Fla.
        He had suffered a stroke, according to Wm. A. Bradley & Sons, a funeral home in Chatham, N.J.
        Mr. Schlichting retired in 1986 as a vice president and the managing director of the municipal bond department in the brokerage firm of Kidder Peabody & Company in Manhattan. He had worked for Kidder Peabody since 1950.
        He was born in Orange, N.J. In World War II, he served with the 80th Infantry Division of the Third Army (the Blue Ridge Division) in Europe, winning a Bronze Star. He was a 1947 graduate of Dartmouth College, majoring in business administration.
        He was a past president of the Municipal Bond Club of New York and a member of its board. He was active on the board of the Public Securities Association and formerly headed the municipal committee of the National Association of Securities Dealers.
        Mr. Schlichting's first wife, Elizabeth Millwood Schlichting, died in 1977.  In 1979 he married Barbara Beatty Schlichting, who survives. Also surviving are his son, William R., of Berkeley Heights, N.J., a sister, Jean Gunzenhauser of Sarasota, and four grandchildren.

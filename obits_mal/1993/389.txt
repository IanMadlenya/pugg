
        William J. Calise, a New York lawyer for 60 years and a neighborhood advocate on the Lower East Side, died on Saturday at the Cleveland Clinic Foundation in Cleveland. He was 88.
        He lived in Hudson, Ohio, and had previously resided in Point Lookout, L.I.
        He died after a brief illness, his family said.
        Mr. Calise served on the board of directors of the Henry Street Settlement for more than 40 years and was a founder of LENA, the Lower East Side Neighborhood Association.
        He was Referee of the Appellate Division of the Supreme Court of New York, First Department, and co-ordinator of a special census taken in New York City in 1957. He was also a past assistant secretary of the New York County Democratic Committee and headed a voter-registration drive on behalf of John F. Kennedy in 1960.
        He is survived by his wife of 57 years, the former Adeline Rota, two sons, William J. Jr. of Allendale, N.J., and Nicholas J. of Hudson, Ohio, and four grandchildren.


        Robert Tompkins Handville, a freelance artist who designed postage stamps and covers for Sports Illustrated and other magazines, died on Monday at Barnstable County Hospital in Pocasset, Mass. He was 68 and lived in North Eastham, Mass.
        He had Parkinson's disease, his family said.
        Mr. Handville was born and raised in East Orange, N.J., and graduated from Pratt Institute in Brooklyn. He served in the armed forces during World War II and the Korean War.
        In addition to his magazine work, he designed a stamp for the Postal Service honoring the National Parks system. He was a member of several professional organizations, including the American Watercolor Society.
        He is survived by his wife, Marylee Pollock Handville; two sons, Robert C., of South Salem, N.Y., and David C., of Peekskill, N.Y., and two granddaughters.

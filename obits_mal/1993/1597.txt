
        Dr. D. Martin Carter, a research dermatologist and cell biologist at Rockefeller University, died on Sunday at his home in Manhattan. He was 57.
        The cause of death was a ruptured aortic aneurysm, said Dr. Jules Hirsch of the Rockefeller University Hospital.
        Dr. Carter was the Carl J. Herzog Professor of Clinical Investigation and director of the laboratory for investigative dermatology at the university.
        He was also a professor of dermatology and co-head of the department at the New York Hospital-Cornell Medical Center. He was an expert on the use of new drugs on a wide variety of wounds and skin diseases.
        Dr. Carter was born in Doniphan, Mo. He was a graduate of Dartmouth College and received his M.D. from Harvard and a Ph.D. from Yale.
        He joined Rockefeller University in 1981 after serving as a professor of dermatology at the Yale University School of Medicine.
        Dr. Carter is survived by his wife, Anne Babson Carter; two daughters, Anna Florence of Blairstown, N.J., and Elizabeth Rooney of Virginia Beach, Va.; a son, Christopherof Boston; his father, Joseph M., of Cape Girardeau, Mo.; a brother, Dr. James L., of St. Paul, and one grandson.

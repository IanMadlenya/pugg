
        Kurt Steiner, the voice of the New York Road Runners Club and a former marathon runner, died yesterday. He was 71.
        Fred Lebow, the president of the club, said the cause of death was a heart attack.
        Mr. Steiner ran in 99 marathons, including the 1972 Boston Marathon at the age of 51, finishing in 2 hours 58 minutes.
        In addition to his work as a claims adjuster for the New York State Insurance Bureau, he was an announcer at many Road Runners races, a race official and a member of the club's board of directors.
        There are no known survivors.

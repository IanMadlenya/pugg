
        Benjamin B. Rosenberg, an accountant who worked for District Attorney Frank S. Hogan of Manhattan and later for the New York State Harness Racing Commission, died on Friday at his home in Deerfield Beach, Fla. He was 79.
        Mr. Rosenberg had suffered from a heart condition and diabetes, said his son, Alan, of Mount Vernon, N.Y.
        Mr. Rosenberg, who was born in Mount Vernon, received a degree in accounting from St. John's University in 1936. He was with Mr. Hogan's office from 1946 to 1954 and worked on many of Mr. Hogan's criminal cases.
        In 1954, he became deputy chief accountant for the State Harness Racing Commission, a job he held until his retirement in 1973.
        In addition to his son Alan, Mr. Rosenberg is survived by his wife, the former Miriam Nierenberg, and another son, Leonard J. Rosenberg of Cliffside Park, N.J.
        Correction:  February 27, 1993, Saturday
        An obituary yesterday about Benjamin B. Rosenberg, an accountant, misstated the day of his death. It was Thursday.


        Bogdan Raditsa, a Croatian writer, former Yugoslav diplomat and professor emeritus at Fairleigh Dickinson University, died on Sunday in St. Luke's-Roosevelt Hospital Center in Manhattan. He was 89.
        For 15 years starting in 1928 Mr. Raditsa was an official of the Yugoslav foreign service in Geneva, Washington and New York. He settled here in 1943 working as a freelance writer, and in 1950 became a lecturer in European history at Fairleigh Dickinson. He retired as a full professor of history in 1975.
        Mr. Raditsa is survived by a son, Leo, and a daughter, Bosiljka, both of Manhattan.

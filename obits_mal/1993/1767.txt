
        James M. Franey, a former movie executive in Manhattan, died on Monday at St. Joseph Villa, a retirement home in Flourtown, Pa. He was 92.
        From 1946 to 1971, Mr. Franey served first as the head of United World Films, a subsidiary of Universal Pictures, and later as the head of Universal Education & Visual Arts, a division of M.C.A. Inc.
        Mr. Franey was born in Philadelphia. He was an Army colonel in World War II in Europe.

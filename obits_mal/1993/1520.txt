
        Dr. Walther F. Goebel, an immunologist who was a professor emeritus of biochemistry at Rockefeller University in Manhattan, died on Nov. 1 at his home in Essex, Conn. He was 93.
        Dr. Goebel joined the university in 1924 and during his 46-year career there conducted research aimed at a greater understanding of the body's chemical defenses against infection.
        After his retirement from the university in 1970, Dr. Goebel worked as a volunteer at the Walker Laboratory of the Sloan-Kettering Institute for Cancer Research in Rye.
        He was born in Palo Alto, Calif. His undergraduate and advanced degrees were from the University of Illinois. He was a member of the National Academy of Sciences and received honorary degrees from Rockefeller University in 1978 and Middlebury College in 1959.
        Dr. Goebel is survived by two daughters, Cornelia Branson of Fairfield, Conn., and Anne Barkman of West Mount, Quebec; six grandchildren, and three great-grandchildren.

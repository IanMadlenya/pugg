
        Emlen Etting, a painter, sculptor and illustrator, died on Tuesday at his home in Philadelphia. He was 88.
        The cause was Parkinson's disease, said his wife, Gloria Braggiotti Etting.
        Mr. Etting was born in Philadelphia and attended schools in Lausanne, Switzerland, and Newport, R.I. After graduating from Harvard in 1928, he studied with Andre Lhote in Paris.
        During World War II, he served in the psychological warfare division of the Office of War Information. His sketches of the liberation of Paris appeared in Fortune magazine.
        Mr. Etting taught at the Temple Tyler School in Philadelphia and the Philadelphia College of Art. His work is in a number of museums.
        He is survived by his wife.

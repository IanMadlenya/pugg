
        Waclaw Jedrzejewicz, a Polish emigre and historian, died Nov. 30 at the Cheshire Convalescent Center in Cheshire, Conn. He was 100.
        Mr. Jedrzejewicz was a prolific author of books and articles on Polish history between the two World Wars. During that time he served in a series of military and diplomatic posts in the Polish Government.
        He arrived in the United States in 1941 and two years later helped found the Josef Pilsudski Institute for Research in the Modern History of Poland which is now at 180 Second Avenue at 12th Street in Manhattan.
        He is survived by a daughter, Ewa Beck of Cheshire, and a son, Tomasz, of Cupertino, Calif.

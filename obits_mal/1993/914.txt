
        Harcourt Adderley Tynes Jr., a former journalist and community volunteer in Westchester, died on Saturday at White Plains Hospital. He was 62 and lived in Elmsford, N.Y.
        The cause of death was cancer, his family said.
        During a 30-year journalism career, Mr. Tynes worked on The Amsterdam News, The Scarsdale Inquirer, Currents magazine and The Record in Hackensack, N.J., before joining Gannett Westchester Newspapers in 1978 as a reporter. He also served as city editor for The Mount Vernon Daily Argus and associate editor for The White Plains Reporter Dispatch.
        Mr. Tynes was a graduate of Syracuse University. He is survived by his mother, Helen, of Hartsdale; a daughter, Antoinette Clark, of New York, and a son, Harcourt 3d, of Elmsford.

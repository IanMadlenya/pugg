
        Joseph Gillis, a mathematician and educator at the Weizmann Institute of Science in Rehovot, Israel, died there on Thursday. He was 82.
        From 1948 until recently, Dr. Gillis taught mathematics at the institute and helped develop mathematics education in Israel. He became director of the institute's science research and teaching programs, and later a professor emeritus.
        Dr. Gillis also worked at the Institute for Advanced Study at Princeton, N.J., and at the Institute for Space Studies.
        He received a doctorate from the University of Cambridge, England. As an intelligence officer in World War II, he helped break German codes.
        Dr. Gillis is survived by his wife, Olga, and two daughters, Ada and Michal, all of Israel.

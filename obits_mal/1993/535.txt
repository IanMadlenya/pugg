
        Samuel London, a lawyer who worked for New York City, New York State and the United States Justice Department, died on Friday at Albany Medical Center Hospital. He was 79 and lived in Watervliet, N.Y., and New York City.
        Mr. London retired in 1981 after 20 years as a senior trial attorney in the antitrust division of the Justice Department's New York office, where he supervised a staff of attorneys, economists and investigators.
        A native of Erie, Pa., Mr. London was admitted to the New York bar in 1938 and spent several years trying tort cases in New York State Supreme Court. He later filled various legal positions in the New York City Department of Investigation and the city Law Department, the New York Department of State and the New York office of the Treasury Department.
        Mr. London is survived by his wife, the former Margaret Kelly; a son, Michael; a daughter, Susan London; a sister, Jeanne Corben, and a brother, Sol, all of New York City.


        Peter A. Spina, vice president of public affairs for the Mobil Corporation at its headquarters in Fairfax, Va., died Wednesday at Georgetown University Hospital in Washington. He was 63 and lived in Arlington, Va., and Bellmore, L.I.
        He died of cancer, the company said.
        Mr. Spina was responsible for Mobil's worldwide dealings with both governments and the public. His projects included public television programs underwritten by Mobil, including "Masterpiece Theater" and "Mystery!"
        He also helped shape educational projects aimed at bringing recent "Masterpiece" presentations of "A Tale of Two Cities" and "Henry V" into the nation's classrooms.
        Born in New York City, Mr. Spina graduated from Brooklyn College and Brooklyn Law School. He practiced law and campaigned for New York politicians, including Gov. Nelson A. Rockefeller. He was executive secretary of the New York State Petroleum Council before joining Mobil in 1968.
        He was named manager of public affairs for Mobil's worldwide refining and marketing division in 1979. He became general manager of public and government relations for the corporation in 1988 and was elected a vice president two years ago.
        Mr. Spina is survived by his wife, Astrid; three daughters, Janet Spina of White Plains, Karen Spina of Greystone, N.Y., and Lauren Hampton of Charlotte, N.C.; two brothers, Gaspar, of Boca Raton, Fla., and Robert, of Wayne, N.J., and two sisters, Jeanne Orlando of Staten Island and Tina Spina of Wayne, N.J.

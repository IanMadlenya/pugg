
        Francis M. Ellis, a former managing partner of the Manhattan-based law firm of Carter, Ledyard & Milburn who did legal work for the American Express Company, died on Thursday at his home in Manhattan. He was 70.
        The cause was a heart attack, said his wife, the former Marilyn Martin.
        Mr. Ellis retired from Carter, Ledyard as a senior partner in 1991 and remained active in the firm in an advisory capacity.
        At his death he was on the boards of directors of two American Express Company subsidiaries, American Express Centurion Bank, headquartered in Wilmington, Del., which issues the Optima credit card, and IDS Life Insurance Company of New York, headquartered in Albany.
        Mr. Ellis became a partner in Carter, Ledyard in 1959 and was its managing partner for several years in the 1970's and 1980's.
        He specialized in corporate law and was an expert in law on abandoned property.
        Born in India to American missionary parents, Mr. Ellis grew up there and in Connecticut, was an Army officer in World War II, graduated from Princeton and Columbia Law School and joined Carter, Ledyard in 1950.
        His marriage to Sarah Baker Ames ended in divorce.
        In addition to his wife, to whom he was married in 1983, he is survived by a daughter, Carolyn Terry Ellis of Brooklyn; two sons, Francis Lyman Ellis of Katonah, N.Y., and Nathan Ames Ellis of Manhattan; three grandchildren, and a sister, Dr. Grace D. Ellis of Niantic, Conn.

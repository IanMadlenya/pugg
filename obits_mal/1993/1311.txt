
        William H. Osborn Jr., a retired investment banker and consultant with Lehman Brothers, died yesterday at his home in Locust Valley, N.Y. He was 72.
        His family said the cause was pancreatic cancer.
        A yachtsman, pilot, skier and photographer, he graduated from Princeton in 1942 and enlisted in the Army Air Forces. He left military service in 1945 as a captain.
        He joined Lehman Brothers in 1948 as an associate in the industrial department, was admitted as a partner in 1960 and was managing director until his retirement in December 1989.
        He is survived by his widow, the former Barry Martin of New York City and Locust Valley, and three children by a previous marriage, Pamela Osborn McVeigh of London, Katherine Osborn Roberts of New York City and William H. Osborn 3d of Morristown, N.J.


        Maurice Morrissey Class, a civil and military engineer and land surveyor, died on June 23 at Beverly Manor Hospital in Monterey, Calif. He was 94 and lived in Santa Barbara, Calif.
        His son, Maurice 2d, said the cause was congestion and heart failure.
        Mr. Class graduated in 1919 from the United States Army Engineer School in Chaumont, France, and saw action with the American Expeditionary Force in France. In World War II he was a staff engineer with the Ninth Air Force, commanding the combat engineer units constructing airfields during the invasion of Normandy. He rose to the rank of colonel, was wounded and received the Bronze Star Medal and the Purple Heart.
        He served on General Douglas MacArthur's occupation staff from 1947 to 1953, as director of production and utilities and chief of industry for Japan. He also saw combat in the Korean War.
        His final tours of duty were as a Foreign Service officer.
        Besides his son, Maurice, he is survived by a daughter, Shelmerdene M. Leo, both of Monterey; 14 grandchildren and six great-grandchildren.

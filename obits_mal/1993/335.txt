
        Jerome I. Meyers, a lawyer who specialized in personal bankruptcy cases, died March 15 at his home in Norwich, Vt. He was 64.
        He died after a heart attack, his family said.
        An advocate of debtors' rights, Mr. Meyers wrote the book "Wipe Out Your Debts and Make a Fresh Start" (Chancellor, 1974) and produced a videotape of advice on bankruptcy.
        He practiced law for 20 years in Manhattan, where he specialized in entertainment clients, then moved to Vermont in 1974, practicing in Springfield and, later, White River Junction.
        In New York he was active in Democratic campaigns and helped organize inaugural festivities for President John F. Kennedy.
        He was born in Brooklyn. After graduating from the University of Oregon, he earned his law degree at Syracuse University. He served in the Navy in World War II and the Korean War.
        He was previously married and divorced.
        Surviving are his wife of 17 years, the former Sarah Brobston; three daughters, Katherine Meyers of Norwich, Nettie Wedden of Brooklyn and Laurie Cass of Ascutney, Vt.; two sisters, Lucille Brandes of Del Ray Beach, Fla., and Evelyn Horoff of Roslyn, L. I., and a granddaughter.

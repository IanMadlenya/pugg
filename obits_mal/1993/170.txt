
        Gordon Bradford, a banker who specialized in helping African governments with their economies, died on Wednesday in Kampala, Uganda. He was 68 and lived in Manhattan and Saugerties, N.Y.
        He had a heart attack while on a consulting assignment for the State Department, said John Charlton, a managing director at Chase Manhattan bank.
        Mr. Bradford was born in Honolulu and served in the Army during World War II. After attending George Washington University, he took a job with the Bankers Trust Company in 1948 and later worked for Philip Morris, American Express Bank, BHF Bank and Chase Manhattan. He traveled widely and was posted to London, Zurich and Caracas, Venezuela.
        He retired from Chase Manhattan in 1991 and then worked for Harvey & Company, where he advised the governments of Benin, Bolivia, Cameroon, Guinea, Kenya, Senegal, Togo and Zambia.
        Mr. Bradford is survived by his wife, Mary; a brother, Sidney Bradford of Mill Valley, Calif., and a sister, Anne Wetmore, of Mill Valley.


        Theodore Reade Nathan, a retired New York hotel and advertising executive, died on Monday at Roosevelt Hospital in Manhattan. He was 82.
        He had had a brief illness, his family said.
        Mr. Nathan wrote a number of books on hotel marketing and management. His latest, "Hotelmanship" (Prentice Hall, 1981), remains in print. Until his retirement about 10 years ago, he operated his own agency, Theodore Reade Nathan Advertising.
        A native of Rouses Point, N.Y., he attended New York University and Columbia University. He became an executive for several hotels in New York and Washington and founded the Hotel Sales Management Asscociation. He was an executive assistant for the showman Billy Rose and worked as a broadcaster in World War II. He later served as an assistant to the president of the New York City Board of Education.
        Over the years he also taught at Fairleigh Dickinson University and Wagner College and prepared a course in hotel sales, promotion and advertising for Cornell University. He was a past co-chairman of the National Conference of Christians and Jews.
        Mr. Nathan is survived by his wife of nearly 60 years, Elizabeth Angelier Nathan; two daughters, Joy Elizabeth Stern of Washington, and Gay Theodora Nathan of Santa Fe, N.M., and two grandsons.

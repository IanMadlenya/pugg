
        Albert P. Molinaro Jr., an advertising executive who marketed health-care products, died on Monday at Hunterdon Hospital in Flemington, N.J. He was 65.
        The cause was prostate cancer, his family said.
        For nearly 20 years, Mr. Molinaro worked for Klemtner Advertising Inc., a subsidiary of Saatchi & Saatchi North America. The company is now called Klemtner/Healthcom. Mr. Molinaro joined the company as president and chief operating officer in 1971 and was appointed chief executive officer and chairman in 1979.
        He started his career in advertising in the antibiotics division of Charles Pfizer, Inc., the predecessor of Pfizer Laboratories. He then worked in senior management positions for pharmaceutical companies such as Ortho Pharmaceuticals and Mead Johnson and Company. After moving to advertising companies, he was an executive at several, including Sudler & Hennessey Inc., before joining Klemtner.
        Mr. Molinaro was also a director and member of the executive committee of the Epilepsy Foundation of America.
        He is survived by his wife of 43 years, Louise; two daughters, Louise Ballaj of Montclair, N.J., and Jessica Frutchey of Caldwell, N.J.; a son, Philip, of Fanwood, N.J.; his mother, Rose, of Hackettstown, N.J., and two grandchildren.

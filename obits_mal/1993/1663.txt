
        C. Corey Mills, an architect and former State Senator from Brooklyn, died on Monday at Northern Westchester Hospital in Mount Kisco, N.Y. He was 84 and lived in Bedford, N.Y.
        He had had a long illness, his wife said.
        A native of Brooklyn, Mr. Mills graduated from Poly Prep Country Day School in Bay Ridge and the Beaux Arts Institute of Design in New York City. He also attended New York University and Columbia University. A Republican, he represented the 13th Senate District in Brooklyn from 1946 through 1948.
        Mr. Mills worked for the architectural firm of Harrison & Abramowitz, retiring in the mid-1970's. He was a past president of the New York chapter of the Construction Specifications Institute, a board member of the Council of Churches of the City of New York, and a member of the advisory committee of the New York Society of Architects.
        A resident of Bedford since 1958, he was a past director of the Bedford Players, a member of the Bedford Republican Town Committee and chairman of the Town of Bedford Zoning Board of Appeals.
        Mr. Mills is survived by his wife, Helen Beebe Mills.

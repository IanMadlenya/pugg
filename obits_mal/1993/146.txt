
        Herbert A. Rothman, a lawyer specializing in health law, died on Friday at the Winthrop Memorial Hospital in Mineola, L.I. He was 64 years old and lived in Old Westbury, L.I.
        He died after suffering a heart attack at home, said his son Jeffrey.
        Mr. Rothman was a partner in the Manhattan-based law firm of Seligson, Rothman & Rothman, which he founded in 1955. He was a past president of the Southern New York Residential Health Care Facilities Association.
        At his death, he was president of the Israel Histadrut Foundation, which provides money for social and welfare activities in Israel, and a trustee of the Morris J. and Betty Kaplun Foundation, which provides money for Jewish education in the United States.
        In the early 1970's, he was a member of the Rockville Centre, L.I., school board.
        He was born in Brooklyn and graduated from City College and the New York University law school.
        His first wife, the former Sylvia Shapiro, died. His second marriage, to the former Marilyn Cohen, ended in divorce.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Patricia Sedutto; two sons, Jeffrey, of Manhattan, and Stewart, of Brooklyn; two daughters, Ariana, of Islip, and Melinda, of Old Westbury; his mother, Evelyn, of Miami Beach; a brother, Martin, of Manhattan, and three grandchildren.

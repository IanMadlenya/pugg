
        Gerard P. Meyer, a poet, author, editor, teacher and book collector, died on Tuesday at the Glengariff nursing home in Glen Cove, L.I. He was 83 and lived in Greenvale, L.I.
        For many years, Mr. Meyer wrote scripts for educational radio and television programs for students on subjects like Shakespeare and the United Nations.
        He also wrote and edited books and articles. His "Pioneers of the Press" (Macmillan, 1961) was reprinted in several foreign languages. He reviewed books for the Saturday Review of Literature and other periodicals.
        Mr. Meyer published two volumes of poetry. His poems also appeared in anthologies, The New York Times and periodicals. He served on the board of the Poetry Society of America.
        He taught English, literature and drama in the New York City public schools and at Queens College, Columbia, Adelphi, Hofstra and Brooklyn Polytechnic.
        Over the years he put together a large collection of rare and out-of-print books, and in 1986 he gave 14,600 volumes to Georgetown University.
        Born in Manhattan, Mr. Meyer grew up in Mount Vernon, N.Y. After graduating from Columbia in 1930, he earned a master's degree in English there in 1931.
        Surviving are his wife of 52 years, the former Emma Lampert Meyer; a son, Eugene L., of Washington; a daughter, Deborah Meyer DeWan of Bearsville, N.Y., and three grandchildren.


        William C. McNeil, chairman of the history department at Barnard College and an associate professor, died on Sunday at Northern Westchester Hospital Center in Mount Kisco. He was 46 and lived at Mohegan Lake, N.Y.
        The cause was a heart attack, said the Westchester County Medical Examiner's office.
        A specialist in European international relations, he joined the Barnard faculty in 1981, after receiving his Ph.D. from the University of California. He was also a member of the faculty of the School of International and Public Affairs at Columbia University.
        He is the author of "American Money and the Weimar Republic" (Columbia University Press, 1986) and was working on a book on the breakdown of the Bretton Woods international financial system.
        Surviving are his wife, Victoria; a daughter, Emily; a son, Nathan; his parents, Edna and Charles McNeil of Pullman, Wash.; a brother, Earle, of Olympia, Wash., and a sister, Ellen, of New Guinea.


        John Gutheil, a senior partner of the New York law firm of Parker Chapin Flattau & Klimpl, died on Wednesday at Phelps Memorial Hospital Center in North Tarrytown, N.Y. He was 48 and lived in Ossining, N.Y.
        He died of cardiac arrest, the firm said.
        Mr. Gutheil joined Parker Chapin in 1984 and was chairman of the real-estate department. He developed the legal concepts and documentation for Citibank's Equity Source Account, a variation on the traditional second mortgage.
        He also represented Citibank in connection with the construction and financing of several projects, including the South Street Seaport and Riverwalk in New Orleans. Other clients included Kings Plaza Shopping Center and Marina in Brooklyn and Chase Manhattan Bank, N.A.
        Mr. Gutheil was a native New Yorker. He graduated magna cum laude from Columbia University and received his Juris Doctorate from Harvard Law School in 1968.
        He began his legal career at the New York law firm of Trubin, Sillcocks, Edelman & Knapp in 1969 and was a partner there for nine years until the firm's dissolution in 1984.
        He was also a trustee of the Postgraduate Center for Mental Health in Manhattan and a member of the advisory board of First American Title Insurance Company of New York.
        Survivors include his wife, the former Irene L. Anderman; two sons, David and Robert, and a brother, Dr. Thomas Gutheil of Boston.

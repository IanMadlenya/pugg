
        Morris Hamburg, a retired professor of educational administration at Brooklyn College, died on Wednesday at his home in San Diego. He was 80. The cause of death was a brain tumor, according to his wife, Marian Hamburg.
        Mr. Hamburg taught at Brooklyn College from 1964 to 1979. He also served as an an umpire with the United States Tennis Association from 1954 to 1980.
        In addition to his wife, he is survived by two daughters, Jean Hamburg of Marblehead, Mass., and Jacalyn Hamburg of Mountain View, Calif., and two granddaughters.

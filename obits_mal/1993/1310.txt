
        Jules Ritholz, a nationally known tax lawyer, died yesterday at Memorial Sloan-Kettering Cancer Center in Manhattan. He was 68.
        The cause was lung cancer.
        Mr. Ritholz was a senior partner in the firm of Kostelanetz, Ritholz, Tigue & Fink. His clients included major corporations, theater and sports celebrities and politicians. He represented Mayor David N. Dinkins in a case involving late filing of income-tax returns when Mr. Dinkins was Manhattan Borough President.
        A resident of New York City, Mr. Ritholz was an adjunct professor of law at New York University for more than 20 years. He was also an elected fellow of both the American College of Trial Lawyers and the American Council of Tax Lawyers. His articles on tax fraud appeared regularly in the Journal of Taxation and Taxes magazine.
        He was born in Brooklyn and graduated from Brooklyn College. He was a graduate of Harvard Law School. He was president of the Brooklyn College Foundation, the school's alumni association.
        He is survived by his wife, Chen Ing Chang; a son, Adam; a daughter, Emily Reusing of Lancaster, Pa., and a brother, Kenneth, of Hallandale, Fla.

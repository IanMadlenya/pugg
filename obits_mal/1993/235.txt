
        John E. Berry, a former executive director of the New York State Bar Association, died on Jan. 25 at the Shrewsbury Nursing Home in Shrewsbury, Mass. He was 78.
        His family said he died of heart failure.
        Mr. Berry worked at the bar association's headquarters in Albany for 30 years. He started as an executive assistant in 1948 and was promoted to chief administrator in 1954 with the title of executive secretary, which was changed to executive director in 1959. He retired in 1978.
        He was a founder and past president of the National Association of Bar Executives. He also served for 10 years as the editor of the New York State Bar Bulletin and nine years as secretary of the New York Bar Foundation.
        He was born in Bridgeport, Conn., and received a bachelor's degree from Syracuse University in 1936 and a law degree from Cornell University in 1939. He practiced law in Binghamton, Syracuse and Marcellus, N.Y.
        In World War II he served in the Army, reaching the rank of first lieutenant.
        Mr. Barry's wife, the former Susan Hickox, died in 1979. He is survived by a son, John E. Berry Jr. of Sheldon, Vt.; a daughter, Jill Priester of Shrewsbury, and five grandchildren.


        Lawrence Eckenfelder, a financial-services analyst working in the San Francisco office of Prudential Securities, died on Saturday at his home in Oakland. He was 41 years old.
        The cause was a heart attack, the company said in New York.
        Mr. Eckenfelder, a native of Teaneck, N.J., graduated from Vanderbilt University and earned an M.B.A. at the University of Texas. A chartered financial analyst, he worked for the firm of Dean Witter Reynolds Inc. for four and a half years before joining Prudential Securities in 1986.
        Institutional Investor named him to its All-America Research Team 10 times, ranking him No. 1 since 1987.
        Mr. Eckenfelder's survivors include his wife, Dora, and three children, Kelsia, Adam and Lauren.

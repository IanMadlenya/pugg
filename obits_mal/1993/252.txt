
        Philip J. Levens, a retired vice president of the American Broadcasting Companies, died on Tuesday at Massapequa (L.I.) General Hospital. A Massapequa resident, he was 74.
        His family said the cause was heart failure.
        A native of New York City, Mr. Levens received an engineering degree from New York University. He spent a 45-year television career at ABC, retiring in 1987 as vice president of broadcast operations and engineering. In 1968, he was assigned to the network's Olympic coverage, starting with the games in Mexico City.
        He became the engineer in charge at the Olympic Games in Munich in 1972, Innsbruck and Montreal in 1976, Lake Placid in 1980 and Sarajevo in 1984.
        Mr. Levens is survived by his wife, Joyce; three sons, Richard, of Massapequa, Scott Kramer, of Island Park, L.I., and David, of Baldwin, L.I.; two daughters, Beth Brobst, of Lawrenceville, N.J., and Gail Kramer, of Valley Stream, L.I., and four grandchildren.


        Arnold E. Beermann, a retired advertising executive for The New York Times, died on Monday at St. Francis Hospital in Roslyn, L.I. He was 68 and lived in Queens.
        The cause of death was non-Hodgkins lymphoma, his family said.
        Mr. Beermann joined The New York Times in 1946 and rose to become manager of classified advertising and direct marketing for The Times. He retired in 1988.
        Mr. Beermann was born in Brooklyn. During World War II he served in the Navy in the South Pacific.
        Surviving are his wife of 38 years, the former Joanne Gilligan; two sons, Christopher M., of Queens and Arnold E. Jr., also known as Barney, of Manhattan; two bothers, Frank and James, both of Manhattan; and two grandsons.
        Correction:  February 26, 1993, Friday
        Because of an editing error, an obituary yesterday about Arnold E. Beermann, a retired advertising executive for The New York Times, omitted his nickname. He was known as Barney, as was his son Arnold E. Jr.

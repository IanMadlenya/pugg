
        John P. O'Byrne, a longtime principal of the Bay Terrace School, Public School 53, on Staten Island, who also taught education at two colleges, died on Friday at Monmouth Medical Center in Long Branch, N.J. He was 66 and lived in West Long Branch, N.J.
        The cause was pancreatic cancer, said his son, Charles J. O'Byrne, S.J., a Jesuit scholastic who is the assistant to the president of St. Peter's College in Jersey City, N.J.
        John O'Byrne was named an Educator of the Year in 1984 by the Association of Teachers of New York. He became the acting principal of the Bay Terrace School, an elementary school in Staten Island's Bay Terrace section, in 1969 and was its principal from 1971 until his retirement in 1986.
        He also taught education part time at Notre Dame College and in the graduate school of Wagner College. Both colleges are on Staten Island.
        Mr. O'Byrne was born in Manhattan, grew up in Flatbush, Brooklyn, and graduated from Regis High School in Manhattan. After serving in the Navy in World War II, he earned a B.A. degree in 1949 from St. John's University, where he was valedictorian of his class, and an M.S. in education from St. John's in 1951.
        From 1950 to 1979 he was a teacher and administrator in various public schools in Manhattan and on Staten Island.
        Besides his son, of Manhattan, Mr. O'Byrne is survived by his wife of 41 years, the former Margaret M. O'Farrell; three daughters, Dr. Margaret G. of Cleveland and Deidre G. and Patricia G., both of Manhattan; a granddaughter, and a brother, William F., of San Anselmo, Calif.

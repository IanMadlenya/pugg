
        Bradford C. Williams, a puppeteer and teacher, died on Sunday at Butterworth Hospital in Grand Rapids, Mich., from injuries suffered in an automobile accident. He was 42 and lived in Holland, Mich.
        Mr. Williams, who was born in White Plains, attended Hope College in Holland. He received his master's degree in puppetry from the University of Connecticut.
        Mr. Williams acted as consultant, instructor and artist in residence at several institutions around the country, including Ithaca College, The National Institute for the Deaf at Rochester Institute of Technology, Indiana Repertory Theater and the Institute of Professional Puppetry Arts at the Eugene O'Neill Theater Center in Waterford, Conn. In 1987 he received a certificate of appreciation from the City of New York for his volunteer work in the pediatrics ward at Memorial Sloane-Kettering Cancer Center.
        He is survived by his parents, Robert and Patricia Williams, and his sister, Lorma, all of Holland.

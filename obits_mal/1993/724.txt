
        Robert Dingilian, a movie executive, died on Monday at his home in Los Angeles. He was 60.
        The cause was heart failure, his family said.
        At his death he was Metro-Goldwyn-Mayer's executive vice president for marketing. From 1988 to 1991 he was president of International Creative Associates, which marketed "My Left Foot," "The Grifters" and "Mr. and Mrs. Bridge."
        As Columbia Studios' executive vice president for marketing from 1984 to 1988, he promoted "Jagged Edge," "La Bamba, "White Nights" and "The Last Emperor."
        Surviving are his wife, the former Martha Kaufman, known as Muffett; two sons, Tim of Arlington, Va., and Christopher of Los Angeles; two daughters, Susan Cocoltchos of Fairfax, Va., and Erin Dingilian of Los Angeles, and a brother, Norman, of Los Angeles.

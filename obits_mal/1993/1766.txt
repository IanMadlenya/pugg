
        Dr. John H. Menkart, a retired senior vice president of Clairol Inc., died on Thursday at his home in Greenwich, Conn. He was 71.
        The cause was amyotrophic lateral sclerosis, also known as Lou Gehrig's disease, said his wife, Edith Menkart.
        From 1971 to 1987 Dr. Menkart was with Clairol, the Stamford, Conn., company that primarily makes hair-care products, where he directed its technology division for a decade. From 1987 until his death he was the president of Shagbark Research of Greenwich.
        Dr. Menkart was born in Czechoslovakia and graduated from Leeds University in England with a doctorate in textile chemistry. He was the author of 50 technical papers and held 10 patents.
        Besides his wife, he is survived by a son, Andrew J. of Haddonfield, N.J., and a daughter, Deborah J. Menkart of Washington.

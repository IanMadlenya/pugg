
        Alvaro Obregon Tapia, the former Governor of Sonora, Mexico, died on Monday in a hospital in Tucson, Arizona. He was 76.
        Mr. Obregon headed the northern border state from 1955 to 1961. He was born on Dec. 18, 1916, to Gen. Alvaro Obregon Salido, a Mexican revolutionary leader who helped pacify Mexico and create the modern Mexican state.
        The general was President from 1920 to 1924. He was assassinated in 1928 shortly after re-election.
        His son, as Governor of Sonora, emphasized building schools and highways. He was responsible for starting a program for night classes for adults and the construction of 139 schools, mainly in rural areas.
        He is survived by his wife, Fernanda; four sons; two brothers, and three sisters.

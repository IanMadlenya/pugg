
        Dan Rodgers, a drug industry executive who was also a former president of Revlon Inc., died on Friday at New York Hospital-Cornell Medical Center. He was 73 and lived for the last two years in Palm Beach, Fla.
        His daughter Elly Yates said that Mr. Rodgers, who previously lived in Pomona in Rockland County, N.Y., died of heart failure and kidney cancer.
        At his death he was president and chief executive of the Thompson Medical Company in West Palm Beach, Fla., which manufactures over-the-counter pharmaceutical products, and which he joined in 1986. He was also a director of the Nonprescription Drug Manufacturers Association. Advertising Agency Posts
        Mr. Rodgers was president of Revlon from 1966 to 1969. He also held posts at various advertising agencies, including Ted Bates & Company, of which he was a senior vice president and a director from 1956 to 1963.
        He was a vice president of American Home Products Corporation from 1963 to 1966 and later was president of that concern's house advertising agency, John F. Murray Advertising.
        Mr. Rodgers was born in Manhattan. He was married in 1943 to Ruth Steigerwald, who died in 1964. His 1965 marriage to Doris Kanner Lazare ended in divorce. He is survived by his wife, Irma Oestreicher, to whom he was married in 1992; three daughters, Sanda Rodgers of Ottawa, Canada; Cathy Rodgers of Guttenberg, N.J., and Terry Yates of La Belle, Fla.; and two grandchildren.

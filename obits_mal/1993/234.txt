
        Dr. Frederick H. Amendola, a retired chief of surgery at Roosevelt Hospital, died at his home in Manhattan on Friday. He was 89.
        Dr. Amendola, a Phi Beta Kappa graduate of the City University of New York, received his medical degree from Cornell University Medical College and joined the Roosevelt staff in 1927.
        He was appointed an assistant attending surgeon in 1932 and rose through the ranks to become chief of surgery in 1953. He served as president of the hospital's medical board in 1956 and 1957. He retired as chief of surgery in 1968.
        He is survived by his wife, Britta, who was a nurse at Roosevelt when they first met, and a sister, Susanne A. Kohut of Manhattan.

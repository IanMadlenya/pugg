
        Arnold Schneidman, an expert in the design and application of computer software for accountants, died on Sunday at a hospital in New Haven. He was 69 and lived in Greenwich, Conn.
        No cause of death was provided.
        Mr. Schneidman developed a computerized tax-preparation program called Software 1040, sold through a company of his that in 1982 was acquired by Prentice-Hall. At that time, more than 800 C.P.A. firms used the software package.
        For many years after 1982, Mr. Schneidman was a lecturer and consultant on computer-related accounting issues for the International Business Machines Corporation.
        A C.P.A. as well, Mr. Schneidman was a founder with his brother, Seymour, of the New York firm of Seymour Schneidman and Associates. His brother predeceased him, and, at the time of his death, Arnold Schneidman was a managing partner in charge of the firm's information-services group.
        Born in the Bronx, Mr. Schneidman was a member of the first graduating class of the Bronx High School of Science, the class of 1941. He received his B.B.A. degree in accounting from the City College of New York and an M.B.A. from New York University. From 1944 to 1946, he served in the Navy.
        Mr. Schneidman is survived by his wife of 47 years, Hilda; a daughter, Jill Cohen; two sons, Steven and Gary; a sister, Ruth Alpern, and six grandchildren.

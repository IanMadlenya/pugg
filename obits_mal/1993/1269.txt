
        Henry T. Mortimer, a former New York stockbroker who had been living in retirement in Palm Beach, Fla., died on Monday at his son's home in Southampton, L.I. He was 77.
        The cause of death was emphysema, said John Jay Mortimer, a brother.
        Mr. Mortimer retired early this year as an associate director of Bear, Stearns & Company in Manhattan. Before his service there he had been managing director of Clark, Dodge & Company, a New York investment firm.
        He graduated from Harvard College in the class of 1939. He served in the Army during World War II, becoming a lieutenant colonel. He received the Bronze Star for bravery during the Battle of the Bulge.
        He is survived by his wife, Linda Metcalfe Mortimer of Palm Beach; three sons, Henry T. Jr., of Southampton, Alexander, of Palm Beach, and John, of Palm Beach; a daughter, Victoria de Navacelle of Paris; three brothers, Stanley G., of Harriman, N.Y., and Richard and John Jay, both of Southampton; two sisters, Eve Ledyard of Unionville, Pa., and Katharine Blaine of Southampton, and five grandchildren.

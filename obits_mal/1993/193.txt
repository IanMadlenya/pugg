
        Gordon de Lima Hamilton, a coach and teacher, died on Feb. 16 at Montefiore Medical Center in the Bronx. He was 28 and lived in Manhattan.
        His mother, Jean Hamilton, said the cause was Hodgkin's disease.
        Mr. Hamilton was the lacrosse coach at the Dalton School from March 1991 until he became ill last fall. He was also a student-teacher at Public School 87 in 1990-91.
        He began his career teaching outdoor skills to underprivileged children in the South Bronx with the Dreamers, a nonprofit organization.
        Mr. Hamilton worked with Outward Bound and the National Outdoor Leadership School, which teach wilderness skills.
        He was born in New York City and attended the Dalton School. He graduated from Northwestern University and earned a master's degree in education with Honors from Columbia Teacher's College in 1991.
        Besides his mother, he is survived by his father, Richard Hamilton; his companion, Eva Karelsen; a sister, Elizabeth Hamilton Abou Negm, all of Manhattan, and a brother, Minard Hamilton, of Los Angeles.

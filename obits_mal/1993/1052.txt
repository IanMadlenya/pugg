
        A. Garth Hamby, retired executive vice president of the Coca-Cola Company, died on Tuesday at St. Joseph's Hospital in Atlanta. He was 55 and lived in Atlanta.
        The cause was emphysema and lung cancer, said a company spokesman.
        Mr. Hamby joined Coca-Cola's headquarters in Atlanta in 1967 as staff representative in public relations. In 1978, he was elected a vice president, managing corporate external affairs.
        In 1979 he became a senior vice president and in 1980 executive vice president of administration, managing staff functions at corporate headquarters. He retired last April.
        A native of Birmingham, Mr. Hamby graduated from the University of Alabama in 1959 with a degree in journalism. He began his career as a reporter for The Ledger-Enquirer in Columbus, Ga.
        He is survived by his mother, Dora Blackmon Hamby; a brother, Ralph E. Hamby, and a nephew, Cary B., all of Altoona, Ala.


        Ralph S. Gallagher, a publishing executive, died on Wednesday at a hospital in Mount Kisco, N.Y. He was 67 and lived in Chappaqua. The cause of death was heart disease, his family said.
        Mr. Gallagher was a vice president of Enquirer/Star Group Inc., publishers of The National Enquirer and The Star. He joined The Enquirer in 1976 as its advertising director. Earlier, he worked for 23 years at Time-Life as a sales executive, for a time as Life magazine's marketing manager. He also spent two years as Signature magazine's sales development director.
        Mr. Gallagher was born in Chicago and graduated from Georgetown University. In World War II, he served as a sergeant in the United States Army in Italy. He was married for 18 years to the former Nancy Goldsborough, who died in 1968.
        His survivors include his wife of 23 years, the former Mary Norris Shiland; four daughters, Catherine Carroll of Somers, N.Y., Eileen Stubblefield of Woodstock, N.Y., and Mary Annette and Helen Gallagher, both of Fairfield, Iowa; three sons, John, of Kona, Hawaii, Ralph Jr., of Maui, Hawaii, and Charles, of Tucson, Ariz.; four stepchildren, Alicia Rounds of Boston, Harriet McDonough of Manhattan, Elizabeth Shoemaker of Mountain View, Calif., and Andrew Shiland of Standish, Me., and 14 grandchildren.

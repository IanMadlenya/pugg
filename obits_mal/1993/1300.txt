
        Ronald Parham, a Bloomingdale's executive who was assistant to the department store's former chairman, Marvin Traub, from 1978 to 1993, died on Friday at Memorial Sloan-Kettering Cancer Center. He was 48.
        The center said the cause was acute lymphoma.
        Mr. Parham, who lived in Mount Vernon, N.Y., previously served as an assistant to Keith Barash, a film producer. Before that, he was an executive at Dimitri, a men's custom tailor.
        He also was a member of the advisory council of Safe Space, a haven for homeless children at 133 West 46th Street.

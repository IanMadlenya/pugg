
        Harry Bakalian, science editorial director of the school division at Prentice Hall publishers, died on Saturday at his home in Manhattan. He was 44.
        The cause of his death was a heart attack, a family spokesman said.
        Mr. Bakalian spent 10 years at Prentice Hall with responsibility for science publications at the company's offices in Englewood Cliffs, N.J., and Needham, Mass.
        Earlier, he was an editor at Harcourt Brace Jovanovich and a science teacher in New Jersey high schools. In addition, he wrote science fiction for youngsters.
        A graduate of Rutgers College, he received a master's degree in science education at Columbia University in 1972.
        Mr. Bakalian is survived by his wife, the former Elaine Bettinger; his parents, Harry and Lucy Bakalian of Rutherford, N.J.; a brother, Richard, of Rutherford, and a sister, Anne Clark, of Rutherford.

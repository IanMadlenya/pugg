
        Herbert L. Abrons, a lawyer and businessman whose philanthropy centered on settlement houses like the one that had helped members of his family, died on Jan. 15 in Naples, Fla. He was 92 and had been a longtime resident of the Riverdale section of the Bronx.
        His death was announced by his family.
        Mr. Abrons was a lawyer who led various family businesses founded by his father, Louis Abrons. As a trustee of the Louis and Anne Abrons Foundation, he helped finance the Abrons Arts Center at the Henry Street Settlement, where his widowed grandmother once found work as a seamstress. A former director of the boys' club there, Gov. Herbert H. Lehman, helped his father pay for college. His parents met at a dance there in 1903, and he was named for the governor. Mr. Abrons was also a benefactor of the Riverdale Neighborhood House and a board member of United Neighborhood Houses, an association of New York settlement houses and community centers.
        Mr. Abrons was a board member from 1981 to 1997 of Wave Hill, the horticultural preserve and educational center in the Bronx, where 10 acres of forests and meadows are known as the Herbert and Hyonja Abrons Woodland.
        Herbert Lehman Abrons was born in Rutland, Vt., on May 4, 1912, and received degrees from Yale and Yale Law School. After serving as a gunnery officer in the Navy during World War II, he was counsel to the General Aniline & Film Corporation. From 1976 on, he managed various family enterprises, including Overlook Groves, a citrus orchard in Ona, Fla.
        Mr. Abrons's first two marriages ended in divorce. He is survived by his wife, Hyonja, ; a son, Henry, of Oakland, Calif.; two daughters, Alix and Anne, both of Manhattan; a brother, Richard, of Manhattan; a sister, Rita Aranow of Scarsdale, N.Y.; three stepchildren, Jai Chang of San Francisco, Alyxandria Chang of Los Angeles and Jason Chang of Manhattan; four grandchildren; and a great-grandchild.

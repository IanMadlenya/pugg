
        Perry Lafferty, a retired network television executive who helped produce a string of popular shows, including ''All in the Family,'' ''M*A*S*H,'' ''The Mary Tyler Moore Show'' and ''Maude,'' died on Aug. 25 at his home in Century City, Calif. He was 87.
        The cause was cancer, his family told The Los Angeles Times.
        From 1965 to 1976, Mr. Lafferty was head of the West Coast programming department at CBS. He was later a senior vice president at NBC, in charge of the network's movies and mini-series.
        At NBC, Mr. Lafferty developed and produced the critically praised movie ''An Early Frost'' (1985), one of the first television dramas to deal with AIDS. It told the story of a young man (Aidan Quinn) who must tell his parents (Gena Rowlands and Ben Gazzara) that he is dying of the disease. The film won a Peabody Award.
        Perry Francis Lafferty was born on Oct. 3, 1917, in Davenport, Iowa. Trained as a pianist, he earned a degree from the Yale School of Music and afterward worked in New York as a radio director and producer. In 1943, he married Frances Carden, a radio actress. She died in 1999.
        Among the other shows with which Mr. Lafferty was associated were ''Studio One,'' ''Robert Montgomery Presents,'' ''The Danny Kaye Show'' and ''The Waltons.''
        Mr. Lafferty is survived by a daughter, Marcy, of Lexington, Ky.; a son, Steven, of Pacific Palisades, Calif.; and two grandchildren.

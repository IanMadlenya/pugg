
        Joseph J. Kovach, a former deputy managing editor of The Daily News, died on Sunday at Lenox Hill Hospital in Manhattan. He was 78 and lived in East Hampton, N.Y.
        The cause was congestive heart failure, said his brother, Bill Kovach.
        Mr. Kovach worked for The News from 1963 until 1988, when he retired as the managing editor in charge of the paper's night news operations.
        Joseph James Kovach was born in Chester, Pa., and grew up in Johnson City, Tenn. Mr. Kovach's marriage to Marjorie Pugh ended in divorce, as did his marriage to Susan Wolfson of Manhattan. In addition to his brother, a resident of Chevy Chase, Md., Mr. Kovach is survived by two daughters from his first marriage, Jill Draper of Alva, Fla., and Pauline Winegar of Johnson City; and a sister, Eleanor Weekly of Tomball, Tex.

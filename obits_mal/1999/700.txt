
        William Riva, a scenic designer who worked in theater and television, died on July 3 in Bern. He was 79.
        Mr. Riva's credits included the sets and lighting for a 1950 production of Gian Carlo Menotti's operas ''The Telephone'' and ''The Medium,'' presented in the round at the Arena Theater in the Hotel Edison in New York. He also did the settings for an opera production of Leonard Bernstein's ''Trouble in Tahiti,'' broadcast on NBC in 1952.
        He is survived by his wife, Maria, the daughter of Marlene Dietrich; four sons, J. Michael, John Paul and J. David, all of California, and Peter of New York, and seven grandchildren.

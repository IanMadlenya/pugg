
        William James Fadiman, the Hollywood producer, script editor and book reviewer, died on June 30 at his  home in Bel Air, Calif. He was 90.
        Mr. Fadiman was born in Brooklyn. He attended the Sorbonne and graduated Phi Beta Kappa from the University of Wisconsin. While a student he created a game called ''Poetic Posers'' that was published weekly in The New York Herald.
        He began his career as a literary and film agent in New York. He contributed book reviews to The New York Times Book Review, The New York Herald Tribune, Saturday Review and The Nation and was a regular literary critic for The Los Angeles Times. From the 1940's to the 70's he supervised script development at  MGM, RKO and Columbia. He produced ''Bad for Each Other'' in 1953, ''Jubal'' in 1956, ''The Last Frontier'' in 1956 and ''Rampage'' in 1963.
        He was a brother of Clifton Fadiman, the essayist, critic, editor and  anthologist who helped to establish the Book-of-the-Month Club, and who died at 95 in June.
        Mr. Fadiman was also a screenwriting teacher and lectured at the University of California at Los Angeles and the American Film Institute. He wrote the books ''Hollywood Now,'' ''Shivering in the Sun'' and ''The Clay Oscar.''
        Survivors include his wife, Regina, and two sons, Jeffrey and James.

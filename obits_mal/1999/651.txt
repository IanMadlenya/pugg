
        Charles F. Mansfield, a former president and chief executive officer of the Marine Midland Bank in Manhattan, died Monday at a hospital in Rockville Centre, N.Y. He was 77 years old.
        According to a statement released by his family, Mr. Mansfield died of cardiac arrest following a brief illness.
        Mr. Mansfield, who lived in Garden City, N.Y., and Stuart, Fla., also served as the chief executive officer and chairman of Continental Copper and Steel Industries of Charlotte, N.C., which is now called CCX Inc., when it was based in Cranford, N.J., and Manhattan.
        Mr. Mansfield was born Oct. 26, 1921, in Manhattan and graduated from Fordham University. He served in the United States Army Air Corps during World War II.
        He was hired in 1951 by Marine Midland and rose through the ranks, becoming president and chief executive officer in 1974. He left the company shortly after Marine Midland and nine other banks merged in 1976. Marine Midland is now part of HSBC of London.
        He then spent 10 years at the helm of Continental Copper and Steel Industries before retiring in 1987.
        Mr. Mansfield was also a governor of the American Stock Exchange.
        He is survived by his wife, Mary, two sons, Charles Mansfield Jr. of Westhampton Beach, N.Y., and Michael Mansfield of Blooming Grove, N.Y.; four daughters, Patricia Phelan of Freeport, N.Y., Peggy Mansfield of Ventura, Calif., Elisabeth Mansfield of Goshen, N.Y., and Kate Mansfield of New Haven, Vt.; a sister, Alice Mary Lissemore of Scotch Plains, N.J.; four grandchildren, and four great-grandchildren.

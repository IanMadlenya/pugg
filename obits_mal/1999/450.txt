
        Dane N. Bath, a former picture editor of The New York Times and The Chicago Tribune, died on Wednesday at Lenox Hill Hospital in Manhattan. He was 58 and lived in Parsippany, N.J.
        The causes were cardiac arrest and complications related to diabetes, his wife, Patricia, said.
        He attended the Universities of Maryland and Wisconsin and worked for newspapers in Wisconsin and Iowa. He was with The Tribune from 1965 to 1973, rising from reporter to picture editor and then graphics editor. He was picture editor of The Times from 1973 to 1981. Later, he founded dnb Media, a public relations and political consulting firm.
        Besides his wife, he is survived by a son, Dane 3d, of Queens, and a brother, Michael, of Franklin, Wis.

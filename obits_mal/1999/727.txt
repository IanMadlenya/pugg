
        Dr. Seymour Tilles, a vice president and founding partner of the Boston Consulting Group who also helped found the Technion Institute of Management in Israel, died June 29 at a hospital in Boston. He was 74 years old. He died of leukemia, his wife, Gloria, said.
        Before joining Boston Consulting, a management consulting firm, in 1964, Dr. Tilles was a special assistant to the Assistant Secretary of the Navy during the Kennedy Administration. He was also a consultant to the United Nations in Israel and Brazil, and a production engineer for the Timken Axel Company.
        While with the Boston Consulting Group, Dr. Tilles was based in Boston, Milan and London. After retiring in 1986, he taught at Northeastern University and in the executive education program at Oxford University in England.
        Dr. Tilles, who lived in Belmont, Mass., was born in the Bronx on June 3, 1925. He earned a bachelor's degree from the City College of New York, a master's degree from Purdue University and a doctorate from the Harvard Business School.
        His first wife, Marcella, died in 1988. Besides his second wife, Gloria, he is survived by a daughter, Sabrina Tilles-Cahn of Redmond, Wash.; two sons, Ron of Clearwater, Fla., and Alexander of Seattle; a stepdaughter, Jane Lappin of West Roxbury, Mass.; four grandchildren, and one step-grandchild.

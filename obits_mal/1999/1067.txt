
        John J. Barrett, a Manhattan lawyer and former chairman of the New York Urban League, died on Friday  at Mount Sinai Hospital. He was 95 and lived on the Upper East Side of Manhattan.
        Mr. Barrett, a specialist in trusts and estates law, practiced in Manhattan for 71 years, most recently with Kirkpatrick & Lockhart. Previously, he was a partner in Lord, Day & Lord, Barrett Smith, which was dissolved in 1994.
        He was chairman of the New York Urban League from 1988 to 1990.
        He was an active member of the agency's board of directors for much of the time from 1972 until his death. He was also general counsel from 1972 to 1982 and senior vice president from 1982 to 1985. All his work for the agency was as an unpaid volunteer.
        In 1994, Mr. Barrett received the agency's highest award, the Frederick Douglass Medallion, for ''his incomparable contribution, commitment and dedication.''
        He also volunteered for what became Morningside House, a charitable retirement home, in the Bronx.
        Born in the Bronx, he received his law degree in 1928 from Fordham and was a founding partner of the firm of Barrett Smith Schapiro Simon & Armstrong, which merged with Lord Day & Lord, producing Lord Day & Lord, Barrett Smith.
        His wife, Josephine, died in 1992.
        He is survived by a brother, Oscar, of Bronxville, N.Y.

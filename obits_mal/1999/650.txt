
        Thomas L. Kimball, former chief executive of the National Wildlife Federation, died on July 20 at Manor Care Convalescent Home in Citrus Heights, Calif. He was 81 and lived in Roseville, Calif.
        The cause was a stroke, said his wife, Arvella Allen Kimball.
        As chief executive, Mr. Kimball was the highest-ranking paid officer of the wildlife federation from 1973 until he retired in 1981. From 1960 until his promotion to chief executive, he was executive director.
        Under Mr. Kimball, the foundation expanded its educational and advocacy efforts and constructed two buildings, Mark Van Putten, the group's president, said.
        Mr. Kimball served on various committees dealing with state and national environmental policy and was a delegate to meetings of the International Whaling Commission. The honors he received included the Conservation Service Award from the Interior Department.
        He was born in Los Angeles, grew up in Phoenix and received a bachelor's degree from Brigham Young University. He was director of the Colorado Game and Fish Department from 1952 to 1960.
        In addition to his wife of 59 years, he is survived by two sons, Thomas S., of Oakdale, Calif., and Allen K., of Seattle; four daughters, Patricia Clark of Boise, Idaho, Pamela Hunt of Escondido, Calif., Kay Lafleur of Herndon, Va., and Andrea Zimmerman of Granite Bay, Calif.; two brothers, Horace, of Elkton, Md., and Howard, of Phoenix; a sister, Nina Finley of Salt Lake City, 25 grandchildren and 12 great-grandchildren.

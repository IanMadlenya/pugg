
        Mason Walsh, a former publisher of The Arizona Republic and The Phoenix Gazette, died on Saturday of complications from pneumonia. He was 86 and lived in Scottsdale, Ariz.
        In 1966, he became general manager of the Arizona papers and in 1975 was given the additional job of assistant publisher. He was named publisher of the papers in 1978, was made publisher emeritus in 1980 and retired in 1981. Mr. Walsh was a past president of the Associated Press Managing Editors Association.
        He is survived by his wife, Anne; a daughter, Peggy, and two sons, Timothy and Kevin.

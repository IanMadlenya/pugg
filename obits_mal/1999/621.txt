
        Frederick L. Sachs, a physician, hospital administrator and educator long associated with Yale University and Yale-New Haven Hospital, died on Sunday at his home in Woodbridge, Conn. He was 61.
        The cause was prostate cancer, said his wife, Ruth.
        Dr. Sachs, a pulmonologist, or expert in lung diseases, was associate chief of staff and associate chief of medicine at Yale-New Haven Hospital in New Haven. He had been affiliated with the hospital since 1963.
        He was also a clinical professor at the Yale University School of Medicine from 1970 until earlier this year.
        Dr. Sachs was born in Brooklyn on Feb. 28, 1938. He received his bachelor's degree from Princeton University in 1959 and his medical degree from Columbia University in 1963.
        He was a past president of the New England chapter of the American College of Chest Physicians.
        Besides his wife, he is survived by three daughters, Lori Sachs-Ceriello of Norwalk, Conn., Jennifer Sachs of Chicago and Alison Cohen of Manhattan.

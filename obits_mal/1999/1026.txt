
        Harold Clarence Passer, an economic forecaster for business and the Government, including the Nixon White House, died on Oct. 8 at his home in Portsmouth, N.H., where he retired in 1983. He was 77.
        Dr. Passer served as Assistant Secretary of Commerce for Economic Affairs from 1969 to 1973. At the time, he sat on the President's Council of Economic Advisers and regularly appeared at news conferences about the country's economic health and prospects.
        Before and after his years in Washington, he worked as an economic analyst for the Eastman Kodak Company in Rochester and was the company's assistant treasurer.
        Harold Passer was born in Lakefield, Minn. He graduated summa cum laude in 1943 from Harvard University, where he earned an M.A. in economics in 1948 and a Ph.D. in the subject in 1950. He taught economics at Harvard before joining Eastman Kodak in 1952.
        Dr. Passer also lectured at the University of Rochester and Cornell University. He served as chairman of the New York State Council of Economic Education in the 1970's and earlier as a consultant to the Federal Reserve Board.
        He wrote many articles and a book, ''The Electrical Manufacturers'' (1953).
        Dr. Passer is survived by his wife of 32 years, Astrid Louise Anderson Passer; a stepdaughter, Christine Thurber Streeter of Portland, Me.; a stepson, Dr. David C. Thurber Jr. of Raleigh, N.C.; and a sister, Carolyn Passer of Faribault, Minn.

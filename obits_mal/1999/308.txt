
        Roy M. Fisher, a former editor of The Chicago Daily News and former dean of the University of Missouri School of Journalism, died on Thursday at a hospice in Evanston, Ill. He was 80 and lived in Wilmette, Ill.
        Mr. Fisher was dean of Missouri's journalism school from 1971 to 1982. In 1981, the Associated Press Managing Editors Association rated it the country's top journalism school.
        Under Mr. Fisher, the school began an exchange program for Chinese journalists and improved its radio and television broadcasting facilities. After stepping down as dean he remained there as a professor until 1989, overseeing the university's graduate reporting program in Washington.
        From 1966 to 1971, Mr. Fisher was the editor of The Chicago Daily News, which ceased publication in 1978. Earlier he held lesser posts at the newspaper and was an executive with World Book.
        Born in Stockton, Kan., he graduated in 1940 from Kansas State University and was a Naval officer in the Pacific in World War II.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Anne Fallon; four daughters, Leslie Muir of Glencoe, Ill., Patricia Fisher of Palo Alto, Calif., Mary Fisher of Rockford, Ill., and Sarah Fisher of Littleton, Colo.; 11 grandchildren; two brothers, Paul C. of Boulder City, Nev., and Robert H. of Lake Forest, Ill., and a sister, Ruth Hall of Davis, Calif.

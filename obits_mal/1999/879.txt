
        Dr. John Laurence McHugh, a former professor of marine resources at the State University of New York at Stony Brook, died on Aug. 20 at a hospital in Fairfax, Va. He was 87.
        Dr. McHugh, who lived in McLean, Va., was born on Nov. 24, 1911, in Vancouver, British Columbia. He received bachelor's and master's degrees from the University of British Columbia and a doctorate from Scripps Institute of Oceanography. He served in World War II with the Canadian Scottish Regiment and the Queen's Cameron Highlanders.
        From 1970 to 1984, he was a professor at the marine sciences center at SUNY. From 1959 to 1970, he was chief of biological research at the Federal Bureau of Commercial Fisheries. Before that, he was director of the Virginia Fisheries Laboratory in Gloucester Point, Va., and a professor of marine biology at the College of William and Mary. He was also a member of the International Whaling Commission.
        His second wife, Sophie Kleban McHugh, died in 1980. He is survived by a son, Peter McHugh of Annandale, Va., and two daughters, Heather McHugh of Seattle and Jan McHugh of London, who are his children by his first wife, Eileen Smallwood McHugh of Arlington, Va., and by two stepsons, Edward Kleban of Orleans, Mass., and Donald Kleban of Manhattan.

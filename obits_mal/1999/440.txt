
        George McKinley, a New York lawyer who was active in the Citizens Union and other civic groups, died April 15 at St. Luke's Hospital in Newburgh, N.Y. He was 95 and lived in New Windsor, N.Y.
        Mr. McKinley, who was born in New York City, retired in 1978 as a partner in the office of Hawkins, Delafield & Wood in Manhattan.
        He was a president of the Bronx County Bar Association. He was also a director of the Citizens Union of New York from 1954 to 1979 and served as vice chairman, chairman and head of its Committee of Local Candidates. He appeared on the union's television program ''Searchlight'' for more than 15 years.
        Mr. McKinley is survived by two daughters, Linda Miller of Newburgh, and Nancy Hawkins of New Windsor; a son, Barton, of Newburgh, and six grandchildren.

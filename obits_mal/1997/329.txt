
        Wilfred C. Bain, former dean of the Indiana University School of Music, died on March 7 at his home in Bloomington, Ill. He was 89.
        Mr. Bain was dean of the music school from 1947 to 1973 and led its transformation from a regional institution to one of international stature. During his tenure, enrollment rose from 225 to 1,700, and the faculty expanded to 150 from 25.
        Over the years, artists like Menahem Pressler, Janos Starker and Josef Gingold have taught at the school.
        Mr. Bain also presided over the founding of the school's Opera Theater, which opened in 1948, and over the construction of the Musical Arts Center in 1972.
        He was born in Shawville, Quebec, and came to the United States in 1918. He received a master's degree and a Ph.D. in music education from New York University.
        He is survived by his wife, Elisabeth; a stepson, Thomas Perkins Myers of Lincoln, Neb.; a sister, Doris Bain Thompson of Houston, and a step-grandson.


        William M. LeFevre, a stock market analyst who frequently discussed financial trends in newspapers and magazines and on radio and television, including CNN and the Public Broadcasting System, died Dec. 4 at his home in the Murray Hill section of Manhattan. He was 69.
        He had been treated for lung cancer, his daughter, Marie Keidel, said.
        Mr. LeFevre was a senior analyst at Ehrenkrantz King Nussbaum, a New York stock brokerage firm. He had worked in the Wall Street financial district since 1949 and began writing a weekly markets newsletter in 1973. He was born in Muskegon, Mich., and attended Phillips Exeter Academy in Exeter, N.H., and the University of Michigan. He had a weekend home in Old Lyme, Conn.
        Survivors include his wife, Mathilda Bock LeFevre; three daughters, Ms. Keidel, of New York; Jeanne Van Vlandren, of Alexandria, Va., and Suzanne Goldman, of Newark, Del.; a son, William 3d, of Philadelphia, and two grandchildren.

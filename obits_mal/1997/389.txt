
        John Kremer Armstrong, a New York lawyer specializing in health and intellectual-property law, died on Saturday at his home in Bronxville, N.Y. He was 62.
        The cause was a heart attack, his family said.
        Mr. Armstrong started with the law firm of Davis, Hardy & Schenck in 1960, the year he graduated from Yale Law School, then spent several years in private practice before joining the firm of Cole & Dietz. Since 1985, he had been a partner in the Wall Street law firm of Carter, Ledyard & Milburn.
        He was a past board chairman of the Brooklyn Botanic Garden, director of the Netherlands-American Amity Trust and a Regent of Long Island Hospital. He was also a director of the Aitken Neuroscience Institute and was active with the New York State Bar Association.
        He was a native of Washington and a 1956 graduate of Haverford College.
        Mr. Armstrong is survived by his wife, Mieke van Haermsa Buma Armstrong; a daughter, Marca; a son, Jeb, and a brother, Alan.

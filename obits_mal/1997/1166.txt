
        Howard Schulman, a labor lawyer and a commissioner of the Port Authority of New York and New Jersey for two terms, died Oct. 23 at Sharon Hospital in Sharon, Conn. He was 79 and lived in Millbrook, N.Y., and Boca Raton, Fla. The cause was congestive heart failure, his family said.
        A founder of the New York firm of Schulman & Abarbanel, Mr. Schulman specialized in labor-related cases. He was chief counsel to the Seafarers International Union from the 1950's into the 1980's.
        He is survived by his wife of 56 years, Paula Berrick Schulman; three daughters, Ann Milbank of Merrick, N.Y., Judith Lederer of Manhattan, and Janet Dicker of Livingston, N.J., and six grandchildren.


        Frederick Werle, a composer and former dean of the Mannes College of Music, died on May 15 at his home in Southbury, Conn. He was 87.
        Mr. Werle studied composition with Nadia Boulanger in Paris, with Rosario Scalero in Italy and at the Curtis Institute in Philadelphia. He also had degrees from the Cinncinnati Conservatory and the Eastman School of Music in Rochester.
        He taught theory and composition at Mannes and was dean from 1972 to 1976. He also was director of the extension division from 1973 to 1976 and created the department's diploma program. Among his published works are a Toccata and a Sonata Brevis for piano and many pedagogical works for young musicians.
        No immediate family members survive.

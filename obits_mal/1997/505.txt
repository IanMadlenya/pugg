
        Paul Lambert, a character actor in movies and on television, died on April 27 at St. John's Medical Center in Santa Monica, Calif., where he lived. He was 74.
        In a diverse career, Mr. Lambert played cowboys, gangsters, ministers and executives and the national editor of The Washington Post in ''All the President's Men.'' He made his movie debut in 1960 in ''Spartacus'' and was also in ''The Russians Are Coming! The Russians Are Coming!'' ''Planet of the Apes,'' ''Play It as It Lays'' and ''Death Wish II.''
        On television, he acted in 300 shows, appearing 14 times on Playhouse 90. His credits include ''The Missiles of October,'' ''The Execution of Private Slovik'' and ''The Sam Shepard Murder Case,'' as well as such series as ''L.A. Law,'' ''Hogan's Heroes,'' ''McCloud,'' ''Cannon'' and ''Gunsmoke.''
        He was born in El Paso, Tex., and grew up in Kansas City. During World War II he was a lieutenant in the Army Air Forces. He trained at the Actors Lab in Los Angeles and then moved to New York, where he worked Off Broadway. He acted with Rod Steiger on Broadway in a revival of Clifford Odets's ''Night Music.'' In 1955, he returned to Hollywood.
        He is survived by a daughter, Dorte, and a sister, Dorothy Blass.

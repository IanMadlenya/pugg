
        Winston Ross, a supporting actor and singer who appeared on Broadway and with touring companies throughout the country, died on Nov. 26 in a nursing home near Montrose, Pa. He was 84 and lived in Rush Township, Pa.
        Born in Manhattan into a musical-theatrical family, John Winston Ross was educated on three continents but made his debut in this country. He appeared in 25 Broadway productions, including ''Idiot's Delight,'' ''Billy Budd,'' ''Julius Caesar'' and ''Twelfth Night.'' He performed at Radio City Music Hall and toured with Alfred Lunt, Lynn Fontanne and Noel Coward, among others.
        Mr. Ross is survived by his wife, Marley Stevens Ross; a son, Stephen, of Grand Rapids, Mich.; a daughter, Ilona Ross of Manhattan; four grandchildren and two great-granddaughters.

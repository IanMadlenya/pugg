
        Paul Denis, a former editor of Billboard and an early writer on television, died on Wednesday at Montefiore Hospital in the Bronx. He was 86 and lived in Riverdale, the Bronx.
        Mr. Denis worked for Billboard from 1931 to 1943, first as an associate editor, then as managing editor, before moving to The New York Post, where he became assistant to the columnist Earl Wilson. He also wrote for magazines and was the author of six books, including ''Inside the Soaps.'' Mr. Denis founded Daytime TV magazine in 1969.
        He is survived by his wife, Helen; two sons, Michael, of Riverdale, and Christopher, of Woodstock, N.Y., and a sister, Helen Denis, of Westwood, N.J.


        Chester Baylis Jr., who spent 44 years as an executive of the Bankers Trust New York Corporation, died of cancer on Nov. 25 at his home in Locust, N.J. He was 89.
        Mr. Baylis, who joined the bank after graduating from Princeton University in 1929, was chairman of Bankers Trust's executive committee, one of the firm's highest positions, when he retired in 1974. In the 1960's he served as head of the bank's international department. Later, he headed banking in both the United States and overseas.
        Mr. Baylis is survived by his wife, Dorothy; his son, Robert M. Baylis of Darien, Conn.; three grandsons, and a great-granddaughter.

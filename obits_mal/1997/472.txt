
        Thurston B. Macauley, a journalist whose assignments included seven years as a London correspondent for The New York Times, died on Saturday during a visit to Fort Lauderdale, Fla. He was 95.
        A resident of Maidenhead, England, and Villefranche-sur-Mer, France, he began his career as a reporter in 1922, when he was hired by The Yonkers Statesman. He joined The Times in 1928 as a reporter and was assigned to the London bureau from 1929 to 1936.
        He later joined the staff of Newsweek magazine and was assistant foreign editor there from 1938 to 1942. He became a correspondent for the International News Service during World War II, and covered the war crimes trials in Nuremburg, Germany, that followed the Allied victory.
        Mr. Macauley is survived by his second wife, Doris, of Hollywood, Fla.; a daughter, Carol Jones, of Columbia, Mo.; two sons, Ian, of New York City, and Ted, of Brive, France; 10 grandchildren and a great-grandaughter.

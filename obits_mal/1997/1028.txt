
        Jules L. Green, a television producer, died on Sept. 15 at New York Hospital-Cornell Medical Center in Manhattan. He was 84 and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Green began his career as a film booker in Pittsburgh. After moving to Los Angeles, he worked as Bob Hope's agent before becoming Steve Allen's personal manager. He developed the first ''Tonight'' show for NBC and was the show's executive producer. In the late 1950's, he moved Mr. Allen to prime time, becoming executive producer of ''The Steve Allen Show.'' He went on to become executive producer of ''Bell System Family Theater,'' producing television specials for Vladimir Horowitz and Jascha Heifetz.
        He is survived by a son, Steven, of Pembroke Pines, Fla; a stepson, Tony Stern of Los Angeles; a stepdaughter, Susan Cookson of Hastings-on-Hudson, N.Y., and nine grandchildren.

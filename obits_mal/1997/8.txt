
        Joseph F. Murphy, who was Commissioner of Insurance in the Cabinet of Gov. Thomas H. Kean of New Jersey from 1982 to 1984 and was part of an effort to bring down the high auto insurance rates in the state, died on Jan. 19 at the Garden Terrace nursing home in Chatham, N.J. He was 81.
        Mr. Murphy was a commander in the Navy's Air Combat Intelligence division in World War II and was awarded a Bronze Star.
        After the war, he worked as a lawyer for the Kemper Insurance Group. He was Deputy Superintendent of Insurance in New York State from 1952 to 1955. He later became executive vice president and general counsel of the Continental Corporation of New York.
        He is survived by his wife of 55 years, the former Marion L. Barrett;  a daughter, Dr. Elizabeth Whelan of Manhattan; two sons, Kevin, of Pottersville, N.J., and Brian, of Chatham, and five grandchildren.

        Daniel W. Fox, an inventor who was the retired manager of chemical development for General Electric Company's plastics division, died of cancer Thursday at his home in Pittsfield, Mass. He was 65 years old.
        In 1953, Dr. Fox invented Lexan, a transparent plastic used in manufacturing compact discs, baby bottles and construction materials. Dr. Fox, who retired last year, held 44 patents on inventions developed in his 36 years with General Electric.
        Born in Jamestown, Pa., Dr. Fox earned a bachelor's degree from Lebanon Valley College and master's and doctoral degrees in chemistry from the University of Oklahoma.
        Surviving are his wife, Joyce; a daughter, Barbara, of Philadelphia; a son, Daniel, of Pittsfield; two sisters, Sheila Clark of San Diego and Mildred Koons of Annandale, Va., and one grandson.

        William Draper Carter, a former director of the Department of International Exchanges at Unesco, died of kidney failure and pneumonia Friday at Huntington Hospital on Long Island. He was 80 years old and had lived in Huntington since 1965.
        From 1947 to 1965, Mr. Carter headed Unesco's Paris division, which sends educators and advisers to developing countries to help improve their agriculture and schools.
        In 1965, he returned to his native New York and worked as a consultant at the United Nations. From 1973 until his death, Mr. Carter was the president of the Feris Foundation of America, which administers the Albert Gallatin Fellowship in International Affairs, a grant for doctoral candidates.
        He graduated from Harvard with a bachelor's degree in 1931 and studied international affairs in Geneva on a Gallatin Fellowship until 1933. During World War II, Mr. Carter directed psychological warfare operations in Southeast Asia for the Office of War Information.
        He is survived by his wife, Gilberte; a son, Paul, of Manhattan, and a brother, John, of Fairhope, Ala.

        William S. Rose, vice president and general counsel of Avnet Inc., an electronics company, died of cancer on Tuesday at his home in Weston, Conn. He was 60 years old.
        Mr. Rose, a native New Yorker, joined Avnet, based in New York City, in 1979. In 1980 he was named a vice president and general counsel. He had earlier been vice president of Beaunit Corporation and vice president and general counsel of Joseph Bancroft & Sons.
        Mr. Rose was a graduate of Emerson College and Fordham University School of Law.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Natalie Seidner; a son, Jacob H., of Manhattan; three daughters, Jennifer, of Boston, Elizabeth, of Bridgeport, Conn., and Rachel Ann Fredericks, also of Bridgeport, and two grandchildren.

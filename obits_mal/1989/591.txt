        John Eastman Jr., an arts administrator, died of heart failure Wednesday on the island of St. Martin in the West Indies. He was 73 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Eastman, who was born in Framingham, Mass., was a vice president of the American Arbitration Association from 1947 to 1963. He was a trustee and later vice president of the Skowhegan School of Painting and Sculpture, an art colony in Skowhegan, Me., from 1962 to 1981, and also served as chairman of the advisory council of Colby Council Museum of Art. He had recently been working as a consultant on special events to the World Monuments Fund.
        He is survived by his brother, Curtiss, of Winter Park, Fla., and a sister, Sally Blackham of Maine.

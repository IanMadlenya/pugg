        Sidney Dobish, a retired cameraman for ABC News and special projects, died Friday of cancer at the Nathan Adelson Hospice in Las Vegas, where he lived. He was 68 years old.
        Mr. Dobish shared in Emmy awards for his work in a 1965 documentary, ''I Leonardo da Vinci,'' and in a 1981 documentary, ''American Held Hostage.'' He also received awards from the Robert F. Kennedy Foundation, the National Press Photographers Association and the University of Oklahoma.
        Mr. Dobish, a native of Manhattan, was a gunnery sergeant in the Army in World War II, serving in North Africa and Italy.
        Surviving are his wife, Nancy; a son, Douglas, of the Bensonhurst section of Brooklyn; a daughter, Diane, of Stamford, Conn.; two brothers, Jack, of Flushing, Queens, and Robert, of Miami, and two grandchildren.

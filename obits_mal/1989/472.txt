        Dr. Morris A. Lipton, professor emeritus of psychiatry, biochemistry and nutrition at the University of North Carolina School of Medicine, died of cancer March 15 at his home in Chapel Hill, N.C. He was 73 years old.
        Dr. Lipton studied drugs that influence the mind and behavior. He was the founder and director of the Biological Sciences Research Center at the University from 1965 to 1986, when he retired.
        Since 1980, he had been deputy editor of the American Journal of Psychiatry. He was a past president of the American College of Neuropsychopharmacology. He was a member of President Lyndon B. Johnson's Commission on Obscenity and Pornography.
        Born in Manhattan, Dr. Lipton graduated from City College of New York. He earned master's and doctorate degrees from the University of Wisconsin and a medical degree from the University of Chicago. He joined the University of North Carolina medical school in 1959 and led the department of psychiatry from 1970 to 1973.
        Surviving are his wife, Barbara; a son, David, of Raleigh, N.C.; two daughters, Judy, of Seattle and Susie, of San Antonio; a brother, Gary, of North Hollywood, Calif., and four grandchildren.

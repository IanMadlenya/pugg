        Yoshiro Araki, chairman of the Fuji Bank and Trust Company, died of pneumonia on Saturday at the Keio Hospital in Tokyo. He was 67 years old and lived in Yokohama.
        Mr. Araki joined the bank in 1945 and became president in 1981. He had been chairman since 1987. Born in Tokyo, Mr. Araki graduated from Kyoto University.
        He was a former chairman of the Federation of Bankers' Associations of Japan and a former vice chairman of the Japan Association of Corporate Executives.
        Surviving are his wife, Kimiko; a son and a daughter.
        Correction:  June 10, 1989, Saturday, Late Edition - Final
        An obituary yesterday about Yoshiro Araki, a Japanese banker, described his position incorrectly. He was chairman of the Fuji Bank Ltd., the parent company of the Fuji Bank and Trust Company.

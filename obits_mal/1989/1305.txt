        Robert J. Mullens, a retired municipal-bond specialist and a community leader on Staten Island, died of a brain tumor on Monday at the St. Vincent's Medical Center on Staten Island. He was 66 years old.
        Mr. Mullens was a member of the original New York Knickerbockers basketball team in 1946-47. He later joined the brokerage concern of J. J. Kenny Company in Manhattan. Mr. Mullens was executive vice president and head of trading, retiring in 1985.
        Mr. Mullens was a trustee of St. Vincent's and president of the Staten Island Mental Health Society.
        He graduated from Fordham University in 1944.
        Surviving are three sons, Thomas, of Huntington, L.I., Charles, of Staten Island, and Kevin, of Silver Spring, Md.; two daughters, Jane, of Staten Island, and Nancy Reiling, of Budd Lake, N.J.; five sisters, Margaret Higgins of Vernon, Conn., Mary Guttman of Pittsfield, Mass., Helen, of Darien, Conn., Jean, of Manhattan, and Annamae McGann of Alexandria, Va., and a granddaughter.
        Correction:  July 28, 1989, Friday, Late Edition - Final
        An obituary on Wednesday of Robert J. Mullens, a retired municipal bond specialist and community leader on Staten Island, listed his survivors incompletely. They include his widow, the former Geraldine M. Lynch.

        Milton Pikarsky, director of the City University's Institute for Transportation Systems at City College and a former transportation official in Chicago, died, apparently of a heart attack, Wednesday at the Mayflower Hotel in Washington, where he was attending a meeting of the American Society of Civil Engineers. He was 65 years old and lived in Yonkers.
        Mr. Pikarsky, who graduated from the Illinois Institute of Technology with a degree in civil engineering, was a professor at City College and had headed the institute since it was created in 1985. Previously, he was chairman of the Chicago Transit Authority and the Regional Transportation Authority in Chicago. He had also served as Commissioner of the Chicago Department of Public Works.
        He is survived by his wife, Christine Johnson; a son, Joel, of Fort Collins, Colo.; a daughter, Amy, of Los Angeles, and a sister, Ruth Weinstein of Yonkers.

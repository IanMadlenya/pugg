        Joseph G. Kvasnicka, an international economist and former vice president of the Federal Reserve Bank of Chicago, died of cancer Tuesday at the Northwestern Memorial Hospice in Chicago. He was 59 years old and lived in Chicago.
        Mr. Kvasnicka, who emigrated here from Czechoslovakia in 1951, first to Canada and then to the United States, was a graduate of Wayne State University. He joined the Federal Reserve Bank of Chicago in 1964 and retired in 1987. He remained as a consultant and was a teacher of finance at DePaul University.
        In 1974 and 1975 he served as senior economist to the President's Council of Economic Advisers.
        He is survived by his wife, Melissa; a son, Joseph, of Huntley, Ill.; three daughters, Anna and Monica, of Irvine, Calif., and Susan, of Windsor, Ontario; and three grandchildren.

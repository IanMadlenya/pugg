        Herman Epstein, an electrical engineer who developed high-speed electrostatic printing techniques for computers, died of leukemia Monday at Yale-New Haven Hospital. He was 62 years old and lived in Fairfield, Conn.
        After earning a Ph.D. in engineering from Yale University in 1950, Dr. Epstein worked for several companies before forming his own, Omnitronics Inc., in 1960. He sold it six years later, joined the General Electric Company for two years and then became president of the Instrumentation Division of the Singer Company.
        In 1963 he received the Edward Longstreth Medal from the Franklin Institute for his achievements.
        Dr. Epstein is survived by his wife, Bernice; three daughters, Cynthia, of Manhattan; Vivian, of Ridgefield, Conn., and Beth, of Old Bridge, N.J.; two sons, Larrie, of Radnor Township, Pa., and Saul, of Gladwyne, Pa., and six grandchildren.

        Rostislav Hlopoff, an art conservator and a professor emeritus of art conservation at the State University College at Oneonta, N.Y., died of bone cancer on Friday at the Tolstoy Foundation Nursing Home in Valley Cottage, N.Y. He was 86 years old.  Mr. Hlopoff restored 18th-century French furniture for such museums as the Frick Collection, the Toledo Museum of Art and the J. Paul Getty Museum in Malibu, Calif., as well as for private clients. He was born in Russia, and worked as a sculptor in France before beginning his career as a restorer of porcelains, sculptures and furniture in the United States in the late 1930's.
        Mr. Hlopoff is survived by his son, Nick Hlopoff, of Palm Beach, Fla.

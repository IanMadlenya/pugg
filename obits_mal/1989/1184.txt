        Edward J. McShane, a mathematician who taught at the University of Virginia for 39 years until his retirement in 1974, died of congestive heart failure Thursday at the University of Virginia Hospital in Charlottesville. He was 85 years old and lived in Charlottesville.
        Dr. McShane was a former president of the American Mathematical Society and a member of the National Academy of Sciences. He was a graduate of Tulane University and received his doctorate from the University of Chicago.
        In World War II he served as a mathematician at the Ballistic Research Laboratory of the Aberdeen Proving Ground in Maryland.
        Dr. McShane is survived by his wife, the former Virginia Haun; two daughters, Jennifer Ward of Charlottesville and Virginia Warfield of Seattle; a sister, Edith Engel of New Orleans, and six grandchildren.

        Thomas A. Stanley, a surgeon in New Jersey, died from complications of a stroke Sunday at St. Mary's Hospital in Orange, N.J. He suffered the stroke three weeks earlier. He was 71 years old and lived in Basking Ridge, N.J.
        Dr. Stanley was president of the medical staff at St. Mary's in 1968 and 1969, a member of the board of trustees from 1974 to 1980 and chief of surgery from 1976 to 1980, when he retired.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Dorothea Dowd; five sons, Thomas A., of Millington, N.J., Paul J., of Naperville, Ill., John C., of Annandale, N.J., Peter M., of Far Hills, and Jerome B., of Basking Ridge; three daughters, Barbara Fritts, Suzanne McCarthy and Rosemary Carrara, all of Basking Ridge; a brother, Charles P., of Mendham, N.J., and 26 grandchildren.

        Bart Winer, the associate editor of ''The Collected Works of Samuel Taylor Coleridge,'' died after a heart attack Sunday at Lenox Hill Hospital. He was 70 years old and had homes in Manhattan and London.
        For the past 30 years, Mr. Winer was associate editor of the Coleridge series, of which 18 volumes have been completed so far. This series, originally sponsored by the Bollingen Foundation, is published by the Princeton and Cambridge University Presses.
        Mr. Winer wrote a children's book, ''Life in the Ancient World.'' Among other works he edited are ''The Art of French Cooking'' and, with Wallace Brockway, ''A Second Treasury of the World's Great Letters'' and ''Homespun America: A Collection of Writings Designed to Capture the Essential Quality of the Free United States.''
        He is survived by his wife, Shirley, and a brother, Melvin.

        Hanan C. Selvin, an educator, sociologist and statistician, died of lung cancer Thursday at his home in Fair Lawn, N.J. He was 67 years old.
        Dr. Selvin, who earned a Ph.D. in sociology from Columbia University in 1956, taught sociology at the State University of New York at Stony Brook from 1967 to 1985 and was chairman of the department from 1967 to 1970. Previously, he held a similar post at the University of Rochester and had taught at the University of California at Berkeley.
        After his retirement in 1985, he lectured occasionally at Ramapo College of New Jersey. Although he became blind from a hereditary eye disease in mid-career, Dr. Selvin continued to teach. In 1987, he received the Joseph J. Larkin Award for leadership from Helen Keller Services for the Blind.
        He is survived by his wife, Elaine; a daughter, Barbara, of Sunnyside, Queens; a son, Albert, of St. Louis, and one grandson.

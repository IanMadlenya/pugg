        James Patterson, a Republican who represented Connecticut's Fifth Congressional District in the House of Representatives from 1947 to 1959, died Tuesday at Our Lady of Lourdes Hospital here. He was 80 years old and lived in Bethlehem, Conn.
        After serving six terms he was defeated for re-election in 1958 by John Monagan, a Democrat and the former Mayor of Waterbury. He lost a 1960 rematch to Mr. Monagan.
        Mr. Patterson, who was a lawyer, owned the P&S Plumbing Supply Company and Builders Supplies of Waterbury.
        He is survived by his wife, Jeanne; two sons, Dr. James, of Fort Lauderdale, Fla., and Sam, of Texas, and two daughters, Dr. Ann Hayden Patterson of Texas and Dr. Jeanne Marie Cohen of Philadelphia.

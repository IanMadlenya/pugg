        William J. Cassell, the comptroller and senior civilian official in the Defense Department's Defense Logistics Agency in Washington, died of heart failure on Saturday at his home in Annandale, Va. He was 58 years old.
        A native of Brooklyn, Mr. Cassell was known for his role in furthering the careers of women and minorities in his agency. He had held his post since 1973.
        He had served as the first comptroller of the Defense Mapping Agency and as deputy director for operations in the Office of the Assistant Secretary of Defense (Comptroller).
        He graduated from St. Bonaventure University in 1952 and served in the Air Force until 1956.
        He received Presidential Meritorious Executive awards in 1982 and 1989 for his work in the agency.
        Mr. Cassell is survived by his wife, Betsy; three daughters, Teresa Crane, of McLean, Va., Suzanne Albert, of Falls Church, Va., and Eileen Fulk, of Springfield, Va.; a son, Robert Cassell, of Richmond; his mother, Mae Cassell of Manassas, Va., and a grandson.

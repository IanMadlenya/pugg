        Morris Furman, a real-estate developer and philanthropist, died Sunday of complications of a stroke in Fort Lauderdale, Fla. He was 80 years old and lived in Fort Lauderdale.
        As founder of the Furman-Wolfeson Company and R. D. Management, Mr. Furman built office buildings in midtown Manhattan and shopping centers around the nation.
        He was a supporter of Maimonides Hospital in Brooklyn and the Albert Einstein College of Medicine of Yeshiva University.
        He is survived by his wife, Rose; a daughter and son by a previous marriage, Barbara Murray of Hewlett Bay Park, L.I., and Jay, of Manhattan; two sisters, Dorothy Denmark of Fort Lauderdale and Fay Whitlin of Brooklyn; two brothers, Max and Gershen, both of Fort Lauderdale, and five grandchildren.

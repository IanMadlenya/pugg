        William Winder Laird, a financial adviser, philanthropist and a former director of E. I. du Pont de Nemours & Company, died Oct. 20 at his home in Wilmington, Del. He was 79 years old.
        A 1933 graduate of the Massachusetts Institute of Technology, Mr. Laird was elected a director of du Pont in 1965, after starting his own financial advising firm. He was a philanthropist, contributing to the arts, education and to the Young Men's Christian Association.
        He was a founder of the Wilmington Opera Society, now known as Opera Delaware, and also donated land for construction of the University of Delaware's north campus.
        He is survived by his wife, Winnifred; a son, James, of Gloucester, Va.; a daughter, Mary Silver, of Falls Church, Va.; three sisters, Alletta Downs, Wilhelmina Craven and Rosa Hayward McDonald, all of Wilmington, and three granddaughters.

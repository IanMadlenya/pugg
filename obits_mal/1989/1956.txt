        Robert T. Ashmore, a former member of the House of Representatives from South Carolina, died Wednesday at St. Francis Hospital in Greenville, S.C., where he lived. He had undergone open heart surgery last summer and was suffering from pneumonia and kidney failure. He was 85 years old.
        Mr. Ashmore, a Democrat, served in the House from 1953, when he was elected to fill a vacancy, until his retirement in 1968. He was chairman of the House elections subcommittee that dealt with issues like election reform and disputed elections to the House.
        Previously he had served as Greenville County Solicitor.
        He is survived by his wife, Willie; a daughter, Nancy, of Columbia, S.C.; and a sister, Lucille A. Eskew, of Greenville.

        Vic Perrin, an actor and announcer on many radio and television shows, died of cancer Tuesday at Cedars-Sinai Medical Center. He was 73 years old.
        Mr. Perrin was known to television viewers in the 1960's as the other-worldly voice that introduced each episode of ''The Outer Limits,'' a popular science-fiction series.
        Early in his career, he worked as a radio announcer for both ABC and NBC and was a regular on such shows as the radio version of ''Gunsmoke,'' ''One Man's Family,'' ''Fort Laramie'' and ''Yours Truly Johnny Dollar.''
        In television, Mr. Perrin appeared regularly on shows like ''Dragnet,'' ''Mission Impossible,'' ''Gunsmoke,'' ''Star Trek,'' ''Big Valley'' and ''Maverick.''
        He is survived by his wife, Rita, a son and a stepson.

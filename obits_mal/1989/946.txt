        Ralph Winsor, chairman of Curtice-Burns Inc., a food processing company based in Rochester, died, apparently of a heart attack, on Thursday at his dairy farm in Harpursville, N.Y. He was 53 years old.
        Mr. Winsor, a native of Binghamton, N.Y., joined the company as a director in 1973 and was named chairman in 1980. He had owned and operated the dairy farm since 1958 and continued to do so after joining Curtice-Burns.
        He was also a director of Pro-Fac Cooperative, a farm cooperative that is a companion organization to Curtice-Burns, and had been a member of the Agway Inc. board of directors since October 1966.
        He graduated from Cornell University in 1957, with a bachelor's degree in agriculture.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Susanne Swalm; a daughter, Carolyn, and two sons, Alan and Glenn, all of Harpursville.

        Russell C. Stanhope, the founder and president of R. C. Stanhope, a manufacturer of steel pilings, died Thursday at his home in Darien, Conn. He was 90 years old and also lived in Delray Beach, Fla.
        Mr. Stanhope served in the Navy during World War I and later attended St. Louis University's school of engineering.
        He founded R. C. Stanhope in 1929 and later started the Darien Corporation, which refurbished parts for locomotives and other heavy equipment. He retired in 1985.
        A 50-year resident of Darien, Mr. Stanhope was a member of the Wee Burn Country Club, the New York Athletic Club and the Delray Beach Club.
        He is survived by his wife, Jean; two daughters, Michelle Stanhope Sparks of New Canaan, Conn., and Celeste Stanhope Wiley of Syracuse, and five granddaughters.

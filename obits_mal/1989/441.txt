        Theodore W. Oppel, an internist at New York Hospital-Cornell Medical Center for almost 50 years, died of pneumonia Tuesday at the Sarah Newman Nursing Home in Mamaroneck, N.Y. He was 85 years old and lived in New Rochelle, N.Y.
        Dr. Oppel, a native of Madison, Wis., joined New York Hospital in 1932 and retired in 1979. He was also on the teaching staff of Cornell University Medical College.
        He is survived by a sister, Catherine Crocker of Madison; a son, John W., of Brisbane, Australia; three daughters, Margaret Kwan of Los Angeles, Mabel Morgan of Anchorage, Theodora Kroll of Boulder, Colo., and seven grandchildren.

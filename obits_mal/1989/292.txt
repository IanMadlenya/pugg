        The Rev. Dr. Fredrik Axel Schiotz, a retired president of the American Lutheran Church, died of the effects of a stroke yesterday at Martin Luther Manor in Minneapolis, where he lived. He was 87 years old. In 1954, he was elected president of the church, now known as the Evangelical Lutheran Church in America, based in Chicago. Dr. Schiotz was president until 1970, when he retired. Since then he had lectured widely on interchurch activities.
        Born in Chicago, he graduated from St. Olaf College in Northfield, Minn., and earned a master's degree at Luther Theological Seminary in St. Paul. His first pastorate was in Duluth and, from 1945 to 1948, he was pastor of Trinity Norwegian Lutheran Church in Brooklyn. He served as director of a program to rescue missions that had become isolated during World War II.
        He was a former president of the Lutheran World Federation and the central committee of World Council of Churches from 1961 to 1971.
        Surviving are his wife, Dagny; two sons, Paul, of Black Duck, Minn., and Nathan, of Minneapolis; a daughter, Lois Eid, of Minneapolis; two brothers, Harold, of Ladysmith, Wis., and Eiler, of Camarillo, Calif., and eight grandchildren.

        Jerome J. Ossip, a restaurateur, died of colon cancer Wednesday at his home in Harrison, N.Y. He was 69 years old.
        Mr. Ossip was an owner and a co-founder of the Assembly Steak House and three other restaurants in Rockefeller Center. Born in New York City, he graduated from the University of Illinois and received a master's degree from New York University. During World War II, he was a major in the United States Army.
        Surviving are his wife, Audrey; a daughter, Dale Battaglia of Eastchester, N.Y.; two sons, Brad, of Manhattan, and Michael, of Boca Raton, Fla.; two sisters, Sylvia Finn of Indianapolis, and Rosa Zimbler of Chicago; a brother, Kenneth, of San Diego, and two grandchildren.

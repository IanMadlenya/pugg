        Herschel Lewis Roman, a geneticist whose work led to the widespread use of yeast in genetic research, died of a stroke Sunday at Swedish Hospital in Seattle. He was 74 years old.
        Dr. Roman, a native of Poland, earned a Ph.D. in genetics from the University of Missouri in 1942. He joined the faculty of the University of Washington and taught botany for many years. Dr. Roman was the founder and chairman of the university's department of genetics. He retired in 1980.
        He was a member of the National Academy of Sciences and the Academy of Arts and Sciences and was president of the Genetics Society of America.
        Dr. Roman, who lived in Seattle, is survived by his wife, Caryl; two daughters, Linda Roman, of Oakland, Calif., and Ann Roman Weiner, of Indianapolis; a brother, Arnold, of Houston; a sister, Ruth Levin, of St. Louis, and a grandson.

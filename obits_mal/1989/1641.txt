        Giovanni Migliuolo, Italy's representative at the United Nations since last September, died of brain cancer yesterday at Memorial Sloan-Kettering Cancer Center. He was 62 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Migliuolo, a career diplomat, came to the United Nations after postings as Ambassador to Egypt for three years and before that Ambassador to the Soviet Union for four years.
        While stationed in Cairo, he was instrumental in obtaining the release of passengers hijacked aboard the cruise ship Achille Lauro in 1985.
        Mr. Migliuolo, born in Naples, joined the Foreign Ministry in 1948 and saw service in Prague, Paris and Moscow before his first assignment to the United Nations mission from 1969 to 1975.
        In Rome, he held positions in the Secretariat for Cultural Relations as well as in departments dealing with economic relations with the Soviet bloc and with international cooperation in economic and technological matters.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Dagmar Zlatohlavkova; three sons, Stefano, of Needham, Mass., Andrea, of Rome and Michele, of Pittsburgh, and a daughter, Anna, of Manhattan.

        Robert A. Greenberg, professor of English at Queens College, who specialized in Victorian literature, died of leukemia Monday at his home in Manhattan. He was 59 years old.
        Dr. Greenberg was the author or editor of five books on poetry and was an authority on Jonathan Swift, the text of whose ''Gulliver's Travels'' he annotated. In his 23 years at Queens College he also taught courses on Algernon Charles Swinburne, Robert Browning and George Eliot.
        Dr. Greenberg, a native of the Bronx, was a graduate of Brooklyn College. He received his doctorate in English from New York University.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Dolores Breitman; three sons, Michael, of Mineola, L.I., Ivan, of San Francisco, and Roger, of Manhattan; his parents, Perle and Harry, of North Bergen, N.J.; a brother, Irwin, of Cincinnati, and a grandson.

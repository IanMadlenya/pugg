        William M. Benkert, a retired Coast Guard rear admiral who was a specialist in marine environment and maritime safety, died of lung and liver cancer on Dec. 14 in McLean, Va., where he lived. He was 66 years old.
        Admiral Benkert, a native of Chicago who graduated from the United States Coast Guard Academy, headed the Coast Guard's Office of Marine Environment and Systems from 1971 to 1974 and its Office of Merchant Marine Safety from 1974 until his retirement in 1978.
        From 1978 to 1984 he was president of the American Institute of Merchant Shipping, after which he became president of Petroferm Marine Inc., a biotechnology company.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Dorothy Greig; a daughter, Eileen, of Alexandria, Va., and a son, Alan, of Salem, Conn.

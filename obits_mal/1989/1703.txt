        Augie Lio, a member of the College Football Hall of Fame and a pro kicker with four teams in the 1940's, died Sunday at his home here. He was 71 years old.
        After retiring from football in 1947, Mr. Lio spent the next 37 years at The Herald & News of Passaic as a sports editor and writer covering the Giants.
        Mr. Lio, who was inducted into the College Football Hall of Fame in 1979, went to Georgetown, playing in the Hoyas' 14-7 Orange Bowl loss in 1941 to Mississippi State.
        The Detroit Lions drafted Mr. Lio in 1941 and he played for three years. He also played for three other teams.
        Mr. Lio is survived by his wife, Helen, and two sons, Richard and Mark. Services are scheduled for Thursday.

        Francis Speight, a landscape artist, died of cancer on Tuesday at his home in Greenville, N.C. He was 93 years old.
        Mr. Speight made his reputation as a painter in the 1920's and 30's and taught at the Pennsylvania Academy of Fine Arts and East Carolina University. His awards included the gold medal of the Pennsylvania Academy of Fine Arts. In 1981, the National Academy of Design awarded him its Carnegie Prize.
        Surviving are his wife, Sarah Blakeslee Speight; a son, Thomas Blakeslee, of Albany, and a daughter, Elisabeth Sharrock Speight, of Philadelphia.

        George Sawyer Pettee, a research analyst, educator and author who wrote extensively on national security issues, died of Parkinson's disease on Nov. 17 at the Kensington Gardens Nursing Home in Maryland. He was 85 years old and lived in Bethesda, Md.
        Dr. Pettee worked for 20 years with the Operations Research Office, later known as the Research Analysis Corporation, at Johns Hopkins University. He retired in 1969 as assistant director.  He taught at Harvard, where he received a Ph.D. in 1931, and Amherst College. He wrote many books and articles, including ''The Future of American Secret Intelligence'' published by Infantry Journal Press in 1946.
        He is survived by his wife, Margaret; a daughter, Susan, of Washington; a brother, Charles, of New Fairfield, Conn.; a sister, Virginia Conklin, of Reeders, Pa.; and two grandchildren.

        Gordon H. Strom, an aeronautical engineer and educator, died Friday at his home in Tenafly, N.J. He was 75 years old.
        Dr. Strom, who received his undergraduate and graduate education at the University of Minnesota, joined the aeronautical engineering faculty at New York University's College of Engineering in 1940. He continued teaching there after its merger in 1973 with the Polytechnic University of New York. He retired in 1982.  Dr. Strom worked on early versions of wind and smoke tunnels to study air pollution and smoke and fume dispersal from industrial smokestacks. Previously, he had worked as a stress analyst for the Glen L. Martin Company of Baltimore.
        He is survived by his wife, Eleanor; three sons, Richard, of Beilin, the Netherlands, William, of Lake Tahoe, Nev., and Kenneth, of Kearney, Neb.; a daughter, Janet, of Tenafly, and four grandchildren.

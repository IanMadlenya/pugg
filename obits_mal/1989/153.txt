        Dr. Thomas Holmes, a psychiatrist who helped create a stress scale of such events as marriage and work troubles, died Dec. 24 of a stroke. He was 70 years old.
        Dr . Holmes and Richard Rahe, a Navy scientist, introduced the Social Readjustment Rating Scale, also known as the Holmes-Rahe stress scale, in 1967. The scale consists of 43 events, ranging from the death of a spouse to minor violations of the law, that can indicate a susceptibility to some illnesses.
        Dr. Holmes joined the University of Washington psychiatry department in 1949 and retired in 1984.

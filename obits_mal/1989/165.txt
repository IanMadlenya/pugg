
       Clement A. Smith, professor emeritus of pediatrics at Harvard Medical School and a past president of the American Pediatric Society, died Saturday at his home in Cambridge, Mass. He was 87 years old. Dr. Smith conducted studies on the prevention of deaths of babies. He was a recipient of the Howland Award, the highest honor of the pediatric society, and a chair in pediatrics at the medical school was named for him.
        Clement A. Smith, professor emeritus of pediatrics at Harvard Medical School and a past president of the American Pediatric Society, died Saturday at his home in Cambridge, Mass. He was 87 years old. Dr. Smith conducted studies on the prevention of deaths of babies. He was a recipient of the Howland Award, the highest honor of the pediatric society, and a chair in pediatrics at the medical school was named for him.
        Dr. Smith's first wife, the former Margaret Earhart, died in 1960. He is survived by his second wife, the former Mary Bunting; three daughters, Pamela, of Harvard, Mass., Margaret Abigail Biggar of Concord, Mass., and Hilary, of Peacham, Vt.; a son, Reynolds, of Pembroke, Mass.; a sister, Constance Qua of Cleveland; five grandchildren, and two great-grandchildren.

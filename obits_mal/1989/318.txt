        Michael I. Hirsch, an advocate for services to people afflicted with AIDS, died of complications of the disease on Friday at St. Vincent's Hospital. He was 34 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        In 1987 Mr. Hirsch founded Body Positive of New York, an AIDS support group. He had earlier been active with New York AIDS Action and was the first executive director of the People With AIDS Coalition.
        Mr. Hirsch was born in Long Beach, L.I., and was a graduate of the State University of New York at New Paltz.
        He is survived by his mother, Claudia, of Long Beach; a brother, Jonathan, of Huntington, L.I., and two sisters, Wendy Goldberg, of Bayside, Queens, and Nancy Hirsch, of Manhattan.

        Frank G. Opton, a lawyer who specialized in international business, died of cerebral thrombosis Tuesday at a retirement home in Hightstown, N.J. He was 83 years old. Mr. Opton, a graduate of the University of Cologne, practiced law in Germany before coming to the United States in the early 1930's. He was admitted to the New York Bar in 1939 and was a founding partner in the Manhattan firm of Opton Handler Gottlieb Feiler Landau & Hirsch.
        He was the author of several books and was active with the Unitarian Universalist District of Metropolitan New York and the American Civil Liberties Union.
        He is survived by his wife, Eva; four daughters, Ilse Tebbetts and Dorothy Scott, both of Yellow Springs, Ohio, Dr. Barbara Pizer of Cambridge, Mass., and Margaret Morin of Tinmouth, Vt., and 11 grandchildren.

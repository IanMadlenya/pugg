        Samuel Weiss, an investment banker and a member of the New York Stock Exchange, died of cardiac arrest at St. Luke's-Roosevelt Hospital Tuesday night after collapsing at the Metropolitan Opera House. He was 72 years old and lived in Manhattan and Warwick, N.Y.
        In 1966, Mr. Weiss formed Samuel Weiss & Company and became a member of the stock exchange, where he created the system of leasing seats. Previously, he had been a vice president and national sales manager of another stock exchange member, Golkin, Bomback & Company, which became part of the First Hanover Corporation.
        A graduate of the City College of New York, he taught at the Baruch School of Business at City College for more than 20 years. He had also been travel editor at The New York Post.
        He is survived by his wife, Renee, and two sisters, Sara Weiss of Warwick and Sylvia Press of Manhattan.

        William J. Hampton, a longtime aide to the late Representative Adam Clayton Powell Jr. and a housing developer, died of cancer of the colon and liver Sunday at Calvary Hospital in the Bronx. He was 70 years old and lived in Corona, Queens.
        From 1948 until 1966, Mr. Hampton was an adviser to Mr. Powell. He was also a consultant to the House Education and Labor Committee, of which the Democratic Congressman was chairman.
        As a principal of Housing Developers Unlimited, Mr. Hampton was associated with the construction of 4,500 dwelling units in New York and Hoboken, N.J., including the Espalanade Gardens and Riverbend middle-income developments in Harlem.
        Mr. Hampton, who was born in Madison, Fla., held a bachelor's degree from City College.
        He is survived by his wife, Dorothy, and two daughters, Kail McKeller of Queens Village, Queens, and Glenda Davis of Brooklyn.
        Correction:  May 27, 1989, Saturday, Late City Final Edition
        An obituary on Wednesday about William J. Hampton, an aide to the late Representative Adam Clayton Powell Jr., misidentified a surviving daughter. She is Gail McKeller.

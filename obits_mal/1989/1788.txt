        Thomas Cox Allen, one of the first black pilots to fly across the United States, died at Presbyterian Hospital on Monday after a long illness. He was 82 years old and lived in Oklahoma City.
        In 1932 Mr. Allen teamed with J. Herman Banning for an 18-day flight from Los Angeles to New York. Both were acclaimed as aviation pioneers in a Smithsonian Institution exhibition in 1982.
        Mr. Allen worked for Douglas Aircraft in California as a mechanic and later was a lecturer and guide at the Oklahoma Air Space Museum.

        Douglas Ensminger, a sociologist who directed programs for the Ford Foundation in Nepal, India and Pakistan for nearly two decades, died of acute respiratory distress syndrome on May 25 at the University of Missouri Hospital in Columbia, Mo. He was 79 years old and lived in Columbia.
        In his service overseas from 1951 to 1970 Dr. Ensminger, a specialist in rural sociology, supervised the spending of $110 million in grants for projects like family planning centers, rural development and the introduction of high-yield wheat to farmers.
        Dr. Ensminger received bachelor's and master's degrees from the University of Missouri and a doctoral degree from Cornell. From 1939 until 1950 he was with the Department of Agriculture's land-grant college program.
        In 1970 Dr. Ensminger returned to the University of Missouri, where he was a professor of sociology until his retirement in 1980.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Mary Morgan; two sons, W. Douglas of Houston and Morgan of New York City; three daughters, Mary Ellen Atwood of Akron, Ohio, Peggy Moe of Lincoln, Neb., and Jeanie Francis of Columbia; two brothers; two sisters; 10 grandchildren, and 8 great-grandchildren.

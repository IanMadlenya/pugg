        Carl M. Spero, retired president of the Spero-Whitelaw Company, Manhattan insurance brokers, died of pneumonia on Monday at New York Hospital. He was 86 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Spero, a native of Manhattan, was co-founder of the company in 1928 and remained with it until he retired in 1983 after having been stricken with Alzheimer's disease. In 1951 he was elected president of the Chartered Life Underwriters, a national professional organization.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Mildred Wolfe; a son, Michael, of Manhattan, and two grandsons.

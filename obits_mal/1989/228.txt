        Elliot Kahn, a building contractor who was a past president of the Subcontractors Trade Association of New York, died of stomach cancer Wednesday at his home in New Rochelle, N.Y. He was 68 years old.  Mr. Kahn was founder and president of the Arrow Louver and Damper Corporation, a heating, ventilation and air-conditioning contracting concern. He was born in Scranton, Pa., and served in the Army Air Corps during World War II.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Marcia Leben; two sons, Paul, of Cranston, R.I., and George, of Santa Monica, Calif.; a daughter, Linda, of Yorktown Heights, N.Y.; two brothers, Herbert, of Leominster, Mass., and Melvin, of Hicksville, L.I., and three grandchildren.

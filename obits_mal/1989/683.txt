        David B. Findlay, an art dealer for more than 50 years, died Tuesday at Greenwich Hospital in Connecticut after suffering from cancer, diabetes and kidney failure. He was 84 years old and lived in New Canaan, Conn.
        The first Findlay art gallery was established in Kansas in 1870. In 1938, David B. Findlay openeed a gallery in New York, and his brother Walstein (Wally) opened one in Chicago. David Findlay first specialized in the American Western artists Frederic Remington and Charles Russell. Later he represented French Impressionist and Post-Impressionist painters.
        He is survived by his wife, Harriet, of New Canaan; a daughter, Lindsay, and two sons, David Jr. and Peter, all of New York; a sister, Helen, of Chicago, and two brothers, William, of Isla Morada, Fla., and Walstein, of Palm Beach.

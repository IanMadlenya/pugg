        Robert Edward Anderson, a retired advertising executive who was a vice president of Batten, Barton, Durstine & Osborne, died of cancer Monday at Seton Medical Center in Austin, Tex. He was 77 years old and lived in Hico, Tex.
        Mr. Anderson, a 1932 alumnus of the University of Pennsylvania, joined Batten, Barton in 1938 in its Buffalo office. He became vice president and general manager of the Detroit office in 1949 and retired in 1966. He later taught at the University of Texas Graduate School of Business.
        Correction:  August 25, 1989, Friday, Late Edition - Final
        An obituary yesterday about Robert Edward Anderson, a retired advertising executive, omitted the list of his survivors. They are his wife, Zelda, of Hico, Tex.; two daughters, Molly Garcia of San Antonio and Joan Anderson of Austin, Tex.; two sisters, Mary Gardner of Mulberry, Fla., and Ann Tracy of Lake Worth, Fla.; three brothers, Ernest, of Palm Beach Shores, Fla.; Norman, of Port Charlotte, Fla., and Joseph, of Clinton, N.Y., and 11 grandchildren.

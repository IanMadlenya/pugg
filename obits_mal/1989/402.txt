        Dr. Walter Henry Mais, a retired dean of faculty at Brooklyn College, died of cancer Saturday at his home in Longboat Key, Fla. He was 82 years old.
        A graduate of City College, Dr. Mais earned his advanced degrees at Columbia University, including a Ph.D. in physics in 1934. He was with Brooklyn College for more than 50 years as a tutor, professor, physics department chairman and, from 1957 until his retirement in 1970, dean of faculty.
        During World War II he was with the Navy's Office of Scientific Research and Development and received the Ordnance Development Award for his work on a ramjet engine. He also was a co-author of several college textbooks.
        Dr. Mais is survived by his wife, Mildred; two sons, James, of Cleveland, and Walter, of Wilmington, Del.; a brother Paul, of Whiting, N.J., and one grandson.

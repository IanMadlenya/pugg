        George J. Couri, the chairman of Couristan Inc., an importer of floor coverings, died of lung cancer on Tuesday at his home in the Dyker Heights section of Brooklyn. He was 77 years old.
        Mr. Couri was born in Brooklyn and attended New York University. He joined two older brothers at Couristan a few years after they founded the importing and manufacturing company in 1926. In 1970 he started a division that makes carpeting for hotels.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Eleanor Shehab; four sons, George, of Manhattan; Ronald, of Demarest, N.J.; Robert, of Cliffside Park, N.J., and Steven, of Brooklyn, and two sisters, Tillie Boutross of Brooklyn, and Victoria Meena of Columbus, Ohio.

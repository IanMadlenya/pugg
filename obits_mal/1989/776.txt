        William Payette, former general sales manager of United Press International and a former president of Sigma Delta Chi, died of cancer yesterday at his home in Dallas. He was 75 years old.
        Mr. Payette joined The United Press in Los Angeles in 1936. He became Los Angeles bureau manager in 1944 and five years later went to South America in 1949 as a manager.
        He moved to New York in 1951 to organize a television service for United Press and was appointed general sales manager in the early 1960's. He left the agency, by then known as United Press International, in the early 1970's to join United Features Syndicate as president. He retired as chairman in the early 1980's.
        In 1972, he was elected president of Sigma Delta Chi, the professional journalism society.
        He is survived by his wife, Virginia; a daughter, Susan McDougal of Austin, Tex., a son, Bruce of San Jose, Calif., and six grandchildren.

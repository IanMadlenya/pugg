        Sotiros N. Cachules, a former president of the Atlantic Bank of New York, died of complications Sunday after surgery at Memorial Sloan-Kettering Cancer Center. He was 75 years old and lived in Valley Stream, L.I.
        Mr. Cachules, who used the name Sam, joined the Hellenic Bank and Trust Company, a predecessor of Atlantic, in 1936 as a messenger. He was appointed assistant treasurer in 1949, was elected a director in 1969, was appointed president in 1973 and was named vice chairman in 1984. He retired in 1985.
        Mr. Cachules, who was a native of Chicago, attended the American Institute of Banking and New York University. He served in the Army in World War II.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Julie Loukakos; two daughters, Joan Leondis and Ellen Leondis, both of Rockville Centre, L.I.; a sister, Cresanthe Pallas of Stony Brook, L.I.; a brother, Chris, of Northport, L.I., and five grandchildren.

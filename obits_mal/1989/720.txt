        Leal F. Norton, an investor who headed Norton Venture Partners of Palo Alto, Calif., died after a heart attack in Boston last Thursday while on a business trip. He was 58 years old and lived in Hillsborough, Calif.
        Mr. Norton founded Arscott, Norton & Associates, now Norton Venture Partners, in 1979. Through that firm he started several high-technology companies, including Telco Systems, Lam Research, RF Monolithics and Sequoia Systems, and provided venture capital for public companies like Dallas Semiconductor, Iomega, Archive and Businessland.
        He was a graduate of the University of Santa Clara and worked for Dean Witter Reynolds, White Weld and Merrill Lynch before starting his own business.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Imogene Rosa; three daughters, Christina Moya of Oakland, Calif., Elizabeth, of Burlingame, Calif., and Katharine, of Hillsborough; two sons, Michael, of Montara, Calif., and Leal Patrick, of Burlingame, and a sister, Jean Neuer of Seattle.

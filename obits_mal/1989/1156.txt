        Vasili Romanov, the last prince born in Russia before the Bolshevik revolution, died Saturday at his home here. He was 81 years old.
        Mr. Romanov's mother was Grand Duchess Xenia, sister of the last Czar, Nicholas II. His father was Grand Duke Alexander, the Czar's cousin.
        Rescued by a British battleship after the Czar, his wife and their five children were slain, he later worked as a cabin boy, shipyard worker, stockbroker, winemaker and chicken farmer.
        Mr. Romanov is survived by a daughter, Marina Beadleston of Aspen, Colo., and four grandchildren.

        Jens Norgaard, who played on the University of Iowa's 1939 Ironmen football team and was a decorated pilot in World War II, died Sunday in Burlington, Wis., after a short illness. He was 72 years old.
        He played end for the Ironmen team, so named because Coach Eddie Anderson used most of his players on both offense and defense. The 1939 Iowa team finished with a 6-1-1 record and achieved fame by beating Notre Dame, 7-6.
        Mr. Norgaard became a lieutenant colonel in the Army Air Corps during World War II and won the French Croix de Guerre for bravery.
        He is survived by his wife, Josephine, two sons and three daughters.

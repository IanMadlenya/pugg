        John Max Lentz, a retired Army major general, died Friday at a nursing home in El Paso, Tex. He was 93 years old and lived in El Paso.
        He was in field artillery service in World War I. In World War II he was assistant chief of staff for the Army Ground Forces, responsible for supervising and training the bulk of the Army's ground troops. In 1944 as artillery officer, he joined the XII Corps, which became part of the Third Army under Gen. George C. Patton.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Elizabeth Heuisler-Hebner, and five stepchildren, Kirby Atterbury of Bethel Island, Calif.; George Hebner of Southport, Conn.; Anne Dussol of Minneapolis; Elizabeth Kemsley of Lafayette, Calif., and J. Stanley Hebner of Richmond.

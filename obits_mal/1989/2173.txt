        Kenneth E. Beer, founder in 1944 of the Manhattan Publishing Company, which specialized in publishing international documents, died of a stroke Monday at Phelps Memorial Hospital in North Tarrytown, N.Y.
        He was 80 years old and lived in Croton-on-Hudson, N.Y.
        Mr. Beer, who came to the United States from Vienna in 1938, published the working papers of the 1945 organizational meetings of the United Nations in San Francisco. He continued to publish for the United Nations in New York and also published work for the European Community in Strasbourg, France; the Council of Europe, also in Strasbourg, and the Carnegie Foundation for International Peace.
        Mr. Beer published economic yearbooks for the German, Dutch and Belgian Chambers of Commerce and the official guidebooks for the United States Pavillion at Worlds Fairs from 1957, in Brussels, to 1971, in Osaka, Japan. He was the author and publisher in 1961 of ''The U.S.A. Answers Questions,'' which was translated into 27 languages by the United States Information Agency.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Anita Gerstel; a daughter, Nancy Johnson of Croton-on-Hudson, and two grandsons.

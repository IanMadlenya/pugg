        Edwin A. Elbert, a retired import-export executive, died of a heart attack Wednesday at Daniel Freeman Hospital in Marina Del Rey, Calif. He was 85 years old and lived in Marina Del Rey.
        A 1924 graduate of the Wharton School at the University of Pennsylvania, Mr. Elbert was engaged in foreign trade with Elbert & Company, a family business in New York, from 1930 to 1956. He then became assistant executive vice president of the National Council of American Importers in New York, which was later known as the American Importers Association and still later as the American Association of Exporters and Importers. He retired in 1982.
        Mr. Elbert, a longtime Westchester County resident, was also a former secretary of the New York Produce Exchange and was assistant director of the export-import division of the Federal Office of Price Stabilization.
        He is survived by his wife, Muriel; a daughter, Jaret, of San Francisco, and a son, Edwin Jr., of Santa Monica, Calif.

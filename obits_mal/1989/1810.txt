        Dr. R. Gordon Douglas Sr., a former chief of obstetrics and gynecology at the New York Hospital-Cornell Medical Center, died of Parkinson's disease on Sept. 9 at a nursing home in Bristol, R.I. He was 93 years old and lived in Little Compton, R.I.
        Dr. Douglas joined the medical center staff in 1930. He was chief of obstetrics and gynecology from 1949 to 1965, when he retired. At the same time, he was director of the center's Lying-In Hospital for the care of pregnant women.
        Dr. Douglas was born in Winnipeg, Manitoba, and was educated at the University of Alberta and McGill University, from which he received his medical degree. He served in the Royal Canadian Field Artillery in France during World War I. He is survived by his wife, the former Alice Lewis; three children, Dr. R. Gordon Jr., of Manhattan; Lynne Hargrove of Nashville, Tenn., and Jane Thompson of St. Joseph, Mo.; nine grandchildren, and one great-grandchild.

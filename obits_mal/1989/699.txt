        John M. Joyce, retired chairman of Joyce Beverages Inc. and a prominent Roman Catholic layman and philanthropist, died yesterday at his home in New Rochelle, N.Y. He was 80 years old.
        Mr. Joyce headed the beverage concern, based in New Rochelle, from 1973 until his retirement in 1984. It was an outgrowth of a family soft-drink bottling company and included 21 brands with eight plants and 39 distribution centers in seven states.
        Mr. Joyce was born in Grand Rapids, Mich., and worked for Travelers Life Insurance Company before joining the family business in 1937. He later served as a trustee or director of several banks, including the Emigrant Savings Bank in New York and Lincoln First in Rochester.
        For the last 46 years, Mr. Joyce was a governor of the New Rochelle Hospital Medical Center, serving as president for three years and as chairman for six years. He was a former president of the New Rochelle Civic and Commerce Association.
        Mr. Joyce headed the John M. and Mary A. Joyce Foundation, which contributed several million dollars to hospitals, churches and educational institutions. He was a trustee of Catholic Charities, the Alfred E. Smith Memorial Foundation, St. Patrick's Cathedral and the New York Medical College in Valhalla, N.Y., and was a lay trustee of Iona College.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Catherine Peet; three sons, John 3d of New Rochelle; Thomas, of Winnetka, Ill., and Timothy, of Bedford, N.Y.; four daughters, Patricia Figge of Washington; Mary Catherine McCooey and Cathleen Egan of Rye, N.Y., and Elizabeth Grace of Harrison, N.Y.; a brother, Robert, of Wilmette, Ill., 33 grandchildren and 15 great-grandchildren.

        Russell Joffree Barrnett, a cell biologist at the Yale University School of Medicine, died of a heart attack Aug. 21 at his home in Mashpee, Mass. He was 69 years old.
        Dr. Barrnett, a native of Boston, joined the Yale faculty in 1959. He was chairman of the department of anatomy from 1967 to 1978. He was director of graduate studies from 1967 to 1974 and again from 1978 to 1984. He was also chairman of the cytology section of the department of cell biology from 1974 to 1978. Most recently he had a joint appointment with the Yale School of Medicine and the Yale Institution of Social and Policy Studies.
        From 1949 to 1959, Dr. Barrnett was an assistant professor at Harvard Medical School. He was a graduate of Indiana University in Bloomington and Yale Medical School.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Gail Voelker; two sons, Dr. Russell J. Jr., of Nashua, N.H., and William Thomas of North Granby, Conn.; a daughter, Elisa Barrnett of New Haven; three stepchildren, Katia Spiegelman of Brooklyn, Maia Spiegelman-Hunter of Skillman, N.J., and Eric Spiegelman of Oakdale, N.J., and four grandchildren.

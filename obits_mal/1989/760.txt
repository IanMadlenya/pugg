        William Lee Phyfe, a former director of shareholder relations for A.T. & T., died Monday at Indian River Memorial Hospital in Vero Beach, Fla., after a brief illness. He was 78 years old and lived in Vero Beach.
        Mr. Phyfe, a 1933 graduate of Princeton University, retired in 1976 after 30 years with A.T. & T. He handled correspondence with the company's three million shareholders and developed its dividend reinvestment plan.
        Mr. Phyfe was president of the Gifford Chapter of the Telephone Pioneers of America. He was a retired Army Reserve colonel and received two Bronze Stars as an artillery officer in the Phillipines during World War II.
        He is survived by his wife, Lillian; a daughter, Barbara Bryant of Ridgewood, N.J.; a brother, Duncan, of Old Saybrook, Conn., and three grandchildren.

        Harrison Merrill Davis Jr., headmaster of several preparatory schools during his long career as an educator, died of complications of Parkinson's Disease Monday at the Montgomery (N.Y.) Nursing Home. He was 80 years old and lived in Cornwall-on-Hudson, N.Y.
        A graduate of Bowdoin College and Harvard University, Mr. Davis was director of admissions at New York Military Academy from 1961 until his retirement in 1971. He had earlier been headmaster of the Storm King Preparatory School in Cornwall-on-Hudson, the Hackley School in Tarrytown, Derby Academy in Hingham, Mass., and the Evans School in Tucson. He was also former president of Nasson College for Women in Springvale, Me.
        He is survived by his wife, Lydia; a son, Harrison M. 3d, of Brooklyn; two daughters, Lydia, of Dobbs Ferry, N.Y. and Margaret Paul Cavallier of St.-Cloud, France, and seven grandchildren.

        Shigeharu Matsumoto, a founder of the International House of Japan, died of a stroke last Tuesday in Tokyo, where he lived. He was 89 years old.
        In 1952, Mr. Matsumoto became the first managing director of International House, a Tokyo-based center that teaches foreigners about Japanese culture. He became chairman of the board of directors in 1965 and held that post at the time of death.
        Born in Osaka, he graduated from Tokyo University.
        He was the widower of Hanako Matsutaka. Surviving are two sons, Hiroshi and Ken, and a daughter, Misao Maki, all of Tokyo.

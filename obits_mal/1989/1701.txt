        Gordon F. Miller, a former teacher, coach and athletic director who was known as Moose to generations of students at the Horace Mann School in the Bronx, died of a heart attack Monday at his home in Boynton Beach, Fla. He was 82 years old.
        Mr. Miller, a 1933 graduate of Springfield College, taught English and mathematics at Horace Mann. He also coached the baseball and basketball teams and was athletic director at the school from 1937 to 1968.
        He was a founder of the Ivy Prep School Athletic League and for 51 years was involved with Camp Moosilauke, a boys' camp in Orford, N.H.
        He is survived by his wife, Janet; two sons, Gary, of Riverdale, Conn., and Gordon, of Mount Kisco, N.Y.; four brothers, Berk, of Naples, Fla., Dudley, of New Haven, Spencer, of Hamden, Conn., and Donald, of Centerville, Mass.; a sister, Constance, of Middletown, Conn.,; seven grandchildren and two great-grandchildren.

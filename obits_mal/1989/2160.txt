        Richard J. Hayes, president of Xerox Venture Capital, a subsidiary of the Xerox Corporation, died of a heart attack Tuesday while on a business trip in Raleigh, N.C. He was 57 years old and lived in New Canaan, Conn.
        Dr. Hayes was in charge of supplying capital to start businesses that offered promise in technical fields that were of strategic interest to Xerox, which he joined in 1971.
        Dr. Hayes, a native of Medford, Mass., held bachelor's and master's degrees in engineering from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology, a master's in business administration from George Washington University and a doctorate in computer science from American University. He served in the Air Force as a test pilot.
        In the 1960's, Dr. Hayes was director of systems engineering for the National Aeronautics and Space Administration's Apollo space project. Later he was director of engineering for the Polaroid Corporation.
        Dr. Hayes is survived by his wife, the former Ellin Donoghue; his parents, Joseph and Katherine Hayes of Medford; two daughters, Priscilla Luckow of South Salem, N.Y., and Victoria, of Manhattan; a son, Richard Jr., of Boston; three sisters, Barbara Martin of Bethesda, Md., Joan Gustavson of Boise, Idaho, and Rena Walker of Melrose, Mass., and a grandson.

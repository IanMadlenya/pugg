        Richard Perrin Graves, a retired real estate executive who was a former official of the League of California Cities, died Tuesday at his home in Pebble Beach, Calif., after a long illness. He was 82 years old.
        Mr. Graves, a graduate of the University of California at Berkeley, was vice president of the Tishman Realty and Construction Company in Los Angeles from 1964 until his retirement in 1983. Previously, he was vice president of the Philadelphia Industrial Development Corporation and from 1933 to 1953 executive director of the League of California Cities.
        In 1954, he was the unsuccessful Democratic candidate for Governor of California, losing to Gov. Goodwin J. Knight.
        He is survived by his wife, Helen; a son, Stephen, of Yardley, Pa.; a daughter, Perrin MacEachern, of Virginia Beach, Va.; a sister, Katheryn Wicks, of Fremont, Calif.; nine grandchildren, and one great-grandchild.

        Walter T. McLaughlin, the athletic director at St. John's University for 39 years until he retired in 1973, died on Tuesday at his home in Largo, Fla., after a long illness. He was 83 years old.
        Mr. McLaughlin, who was born in Brooklyn, graduated from St. John's in 1928. In addition to his athletic director's duties, he coached baseball and golf and served on numerous Eastern College Athletic Conference and National Collegiate Athletic Association committees. He was also chairman of the Metropolitan Intercollegiate Basketball Association, the sponsoring group for the National Invitation Tournament, the annual basketball event at Madison Square Garden.
        He is survived by his wife, Anne; a daughter, Patricia McLaughlin Mahoney, and two sons, Edward and Eugene.

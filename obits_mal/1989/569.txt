        Irving N. Wax, former chairman of the Wasko Gold Products Corporation, died of cancer Monday in the Good Samaritan Hospital in Palm Beach, Fla. He was 61 years old and lived in Woodmere, L.I., and Boynton Beach, Fla.
        Mr. Wax, who was born in Brooklyn, joined his father's jewelry manufacturing business in 1960. He became chief executive, a job he held until 1978, when the business was sold. He retired as a director of the company in 1981.
        He was a former president of the Manufacturing Jewelers and Silversmiths Association.
        Surviving are his wife, Barbara; three sons, Martin, of Philadelphia; Andrew, of Syosset, L.I., and Mitchell, of Hartsdale, N.Y.; a sister, Sylvia Glasser of Boca Raton, Fla., and four grandchildren.

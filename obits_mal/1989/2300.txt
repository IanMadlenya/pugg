        Harold Ellis Klue, a principal at the Riverdale Country School for Boys for 29 years, died on Monday at the Columbia-Presbyterian Medical Center. He was 84 years old and lived in the Riverdale section of the Bronx.
        Mr. Klue was a graduate of Hobart College and Columbia University. He taught social studies at the Riverdale school from 1927 until 1943, when he was appointed principal of the boys' elementary section. After retiring in 1972, he served as a school consultant until 1981.
        For about a decade, Mr. Klue was the director of the school's summer camp at Long Lake in the Adirondacks.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Albina Labutis, and several cousins.

        Albert G. Schmerge Jr., a real-estate investor and former insurance agent, died of liver cancer Tuesday at Greenwich (Conn.) Hospital. He was 71 years old and lived in Greenwich.
        Mr. Schmerge was a founder in 1970 of AGS Properties in Manhattan. AGS has holdings across the country. He had been a general agent for the Mutual Benefit Life Insurance Company for 20 years and was a life member of the Million Dollar Round Table.
        Mr. Schmerge was an alumnus of Xavier University in Cincinnati.
        Surviving are his wife, Margaret; five sons, Albert, of Wilton, Conn., Peter, of Rye, N.Y., Stephen, of Philadelphia, Anthony, of New Rochelle, N.Y., and Joseph, of Clearwater, Fla.; six daughters, Martha Zerega and Mary Archer, both of Mamaroneck, N.Y., Frances Rinder of Exeter, N.H., Ann Noonan of Philadelphia, and Rita, of New Haven; a sister, Martha Meuhlenkamp of Cincinnati; 6 stepchildren, 29 grandchildren and 2 great-grandchildren.

        George Harold Dennis, retired president of the International Telephone and Telegraph Company in Madrid and former representative of Radio Liberty to the Spanish Government, died on Thursday at the White Plains Medical Center after a long illness. He was 91 years old and lived in White Plains.
        Mr. Dennis joined I.T.T. in 1927 and remained with the company, principally in Spain, for 33 years, taking early retirement in 1960. He was president of the company in Spain from 1953 to 1958 and executive vice president in Argentina from 1958 to 1960. For the next 25 years he was the representative of Radio Liberty to Spain, retiring from that post in 1985.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Amarie Whitters; a son, Dr. Ward Dennis of Pleasantville, N.Y., dean of the School of General Studies at Columbia University, and three grandchildren.

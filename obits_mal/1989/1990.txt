        Judge Norman F. Smith, an administrative law judge who was a leading advocate of abortion rights, died of cancer Friday in Crouse Irving Memorial Hospital in Syracuse. He was 64 years old and lived in Syracuse.
        At his death, Judge Smith was an administrative law judge with the New York State Department of Labor. Previously, he was a lawyer in private practice in Syracuse. Born in New York City, he graduated from the Syracuse University College of Law.
        Since the late 1960's, Judge Smith had been an outspoken advocate of abortion rights. He was a member of the board of the Greater Syracuse chapter of the National Organization for Women.
        He is survived by his sister, Florence Engelson of Flushing, Queens.

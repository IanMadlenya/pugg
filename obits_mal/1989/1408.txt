        Frank Mullen, a retired chairman of the Greater New York Savings Bank, died of heart failure Sunday in West Palm Beach, Fla., where he lived in retirement. He was 90 years old and had lived in Bellerose, Queens, and Syosset, L.I.
        Mr. Mullen was a native of Brooklyn. He began his banking career in 1914 at the age of 15 as a junior clerk at Greater New York when it had one office in Brooklyn. When he retired as chairman and chief executive 60 years later, the bank had 16 offices, 275,000 depositors and $1.8 billion in deposits.
        While working at the bank he earned a bachelor's degree from St. John's University and did graduate work at New York University.
        Mr. Mullen is survived by a son, Rolf, of South Egremont, Mass.; two sisters, Helen, of Ridge, N.Y., and Margaret Cortese of Shirley, L.I., and two granddaughters.

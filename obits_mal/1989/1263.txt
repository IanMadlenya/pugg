        Fred W. Lyon, a former vice president of news pictures for United Press International, died of pneumonia Monday after brain surgery at Northern Westchester Hospital Center in Mount Kisco, N.Y. He was 60 years old and lived in Montrose, N.Y.
        Mr. Lyon, who joined The Associated Press in 1983 as an editor in its photo department after nearly 40 years with U.P.I., began for both news services new systems using computers and digital image transmission to deliver photographs to newspapers and television broadcasters throughout the world.
        He is survived by his wife, Deborah Lane; two sons, David, of Montrose, and William Scott, of Freehold, N.J., and a daughter, Anne, of Lawrenceville, N.J.

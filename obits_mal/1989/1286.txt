        Woodrow T. Lewis, a New York State Assemblyman from Brooklyn for a decade, died of congestive heart failure Tuesday at Interfaith Medical Center in Brooklyn. He was 72 years old and lived in Brooklyn.
        Mr. Lewis was a Brooklyn-born lawyer who had worked for the New York City Department of Welfare, the Parking Violations Bureau and the New York State Division of Parole.
        He was first elected from the 53d Assembly District in 1972. He was a member of several committees, including the codes committee, in which he sponsored legislation for stricter gun control and vigorously opposed the death penalty. He retired in 1982.
        Mr. Lewis was a graduate of Alexander Hamilton High School and Brooklyn College, and had a degree from Brooklyn Law School.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Ruth Hemmings; and two sons, Woodrow Jr. of Los Angeles, and Franklin, of Brooklyn.

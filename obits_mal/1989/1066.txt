        Rabbi Yitzhak Yaakov Weiss, the spiritual leader of the rigorously Orthodox Ashkenazim in Jerusalem, died on Wednesday. He was 88 years old.
        Rabbi Weiss died of a heart attack at Bikur Holim hospital, the national news service Itim reported.
        He was respected as a scholar and was the chief of the rigorously Orthodox rabbinical court in Jerusalem. He was considered an expert on Jewish religious law.
        Rabbi Weiss was a well-known figure among anti-Zionists in Israel.
        Rabbi Weiss was born in 1905 in Dolina, in the Ukraine. He moved to Hungary and survived the Nazi genocide by escaping to Rumania.
        After the war, he was appointed head of the rabbinical court in Manchester, England. He became head of the Orthodox court in Jerusalem in 1970.
        The police estimated that 30,000 people turned out for his funeral on Wednesday.
        He is survived by a son, Berish, of Manchester; five grandchildren and several great-grandchildren.

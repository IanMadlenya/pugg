        Wilbur W. McEachern, a banker in the Southeast for more than half a century, died of pulmonary and heart disease Thursday at St. Francis Hospital in Greenville, S.C. He was 84 years old and lived in Greenville.
        Mr. McEachern held executive posts at banks in Florida, Virginia and South Carolina. In 1931, at the age of 27, he became president of the Union Trust Company in St. Petersburg, Fla. From 1946 to 1950 he was executive vice president of the Bank of Virginia.
        He was named president of the First National Bank of Greenville in 1950 and seven years later, when the bank merged with the South Carolina National Bank, he was made president of that institution. He became chairman and chief executive in 1966 and retired in 1970.
        Mr. McEachern (pronounced Mc-EACH-n) was president of the Florida Bankers Association in 1941 and the South Carolna Bankers Association in 1958-59.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Mildred Walsh; two daughters by his previous marriage to the former Bonnie Ehler, who died in 1963, Jo Ann Leitner of St. Petersburg and Mary Lou Trevillian of Clemson, S.C.; two sisters, Allie Mims of Hazelhurst, Ga., and Mary Alice Tucker of Jacksonville, Fla.; four grandchldren, and six great-grandchildren.

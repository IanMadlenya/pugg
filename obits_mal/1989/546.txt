        Edward L. Ballard, a former director of inventory control for Saks Fifth Avenue, died Wednesday at Columbia Presbyterian Medical Center in Manhattan after suffering complications from a heart transplant performed on Jan. 1. He was 47 years old and lived in Baldwin, L.I.
        Mr. Ballard joined the store in 1977 and was director of inventory control when illness forced him to go on disability leave in 1987.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Karen Schatz; a daughter, Lisa; a stepdaughter, Emily Davidson; a stepson, Eric Davidson, all of Baldwin; his mother, Margaret; a brother, Antony, and a sister, Deidre, all of Queens.

        Edwin N. Altman, an executive in the airline and maritime industries for nearly 50 years, died Sunday at Overlake Hospital in Bellevue, Wash., after a long illness. He was 71 years old.
        Mr. Altman, who was senior vice president of Republic Airlines when he retired in 1984, began his airline career in 1962 as a vice president of West Coast Airlines in Seattle. In 1967 he was appointed executive vice president of Air West Inc.
        In 1970 Air West was bought by Howard Hughes and renamed Hughes Airwest. Mr. Altman was elected the new carrier's vice president of operations and a director, positions he held for 10 years. Hughes Aircraft was acquired by Republic in 1980, with Mr. Altman named president of Republic West, a subsidiary.
        Earlier in his career, Mr. Altman was a lobbyist for the American Maritime Association in Washington and a member of the 1960 campaign staff of John F. Kennedy. From 1945 to 1957 he was associated with a union, the Marine Engineers Beneficial Association, where he rose to become president.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Marion Gelfer; four daughters, Regina Austin and Cynthia Maten, both of Bellevue, Diane Altman of Seattle and Karon Altman of Sacramento, Calif.; a son, Edwin Jr. of Seattle; a brother, Harold of Delray Beach, Fla.; 11 grandchildren and two great-grandchildren.

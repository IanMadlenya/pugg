        Leo Rosen, a retired lawyer, died yesterday at the Columbia Presbyterian Medical Center after a stroke. He was 82 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        For 50 years, Mr. Rosen was a partner in the former Manhattan firm of Greenbaum, Wolff & Ernst. He retired five years ago.
        He was born in Kaunas, Lithuania, and graduated from Washington University and the Yale Law School.
        He was a former member of the board of the Ethical Culture Schools, Riverdale Neighborhood House, Brotherhood in Action and the Encampment for Citizenship, and was a founder and the first president of the Riverdale-Yonkers Society for Ethical Culture. He was a former trustee of the Hudson Guild, a Manhattan settlement house.
        Surviving are his wife, Lucille Gruliow Rosen; a daughter, Elizabeth Reiniger of Vancouver, British Columbia; two sons, Michael, of Somerville, Mass., and Charles, of Manhattan, and four grandchildren.

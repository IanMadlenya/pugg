        Henry A. Simons, former vice president and creative director of the Newspaper Advertising Bureau in Manhattan, died of a heart attack on Monday at White Plains Hospital. He was 64 years old and lived in Scarsdale, N.Y.
        Mr. Simons joined the bureau in 1959 as a copywriter and was creative manager and copy chief before becoming creative director in 1971. He retired in 1988. He was instrumental in the creation of the newspaper industry's annual Athena Awards for creative excellence in advertising.
        He was born in Nashville, Tenn., and graduated from Syracuse University in 1949. He also studied at Duke University and the Sorbonne. In 1943 he joined the Navy and served as a radioman with the Pacific Fleet.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Setsuko Miura; a son, Christopher, and a daughter, Barbara, both of Scarsdale.

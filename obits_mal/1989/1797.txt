        Lorenz Graham, a prize-winning author of children's books, died of cancer Monday in the Hospice of the East San Gabriel Valley in West Covina, Calif. He was 87 years old and lived in Claremont, Calif.
        Mr. Graham received the Follett Award for his novel ''South Town,'' and the Child Study Association's 1958 award for the best book dealing realistically with problems confronting young people. The Thomas Alva Edison Foundation honored him for his adaptation of the Ten Commandments for Classics Illustrated.
        Among his other books are ''Tales of Momulu,'' ''North Town,'' ''A Road Down to the Sea'' and ''How God Fix Jonah.''
        Mr. Graham, whose father was the president of Monrovia College in Liberia, had been a Methodist missionary in Africa and set many of his 18 books on that continent. He taught English and Bible studies in several African countries and English at California State Polytechnic University in Pomona, Calif. Mr. Graham's sister, Shirley, was also an author.
        Mr. Graham was a former president of the Los Angeles chapter of PEN, the worldwide writers organization.
        Surviving are his wife, Ruth; two sons, Lorenz Jr. and Charles M.; two daughters, Joyce Johnson of Sacramento, Calif., and Dr. Ruth May Siegrist of Huntington Beach, Calif., 15 grandchildren, and 12 great-grandchildren.

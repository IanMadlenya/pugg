        Philip L. Boyd, the first Mayor of Palm Springs, died at Eisenhower Mecical Center in Rancho Mirage Saturday after suffering a fall. He was 88 years old.
        Mr. Boyd. a banker and land developer, helped incorporate Palm Springs in 1938 and served as Mayor until 1942.
        He served in the California Assembly from 1945 to 1949 and in 1950 was named chairman of the Republican State Central Committee. Seven years later he was appointed a regent of the University of California.
        Survivors include his wife, Dorothy, two sons and a daughter.

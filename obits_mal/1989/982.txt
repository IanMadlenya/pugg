        Alan Douglas Ferguson, an educational administrator and former assistant dean at Yale University, died of a pulmonary embolism Wednesday at the Lahey Clinic Medical Center in Burlington, Mass. He was 75 years old and lived in Lexington, Mass.
        A 1954 Ph.D. graduate of Yale, Dr. Ferguson was with the university from 1948 to 1961. He was director of veteran affairs, a director of graduate admissions, a history instructor and assistant dean.
        He later served with the Woodrow Wilson Fellowship Foundation, the United States Department of Education, the Fund for Advancement of Education and, most recently, the New England Board of Higher Education.
        He is survived by his wife, Vera; a son, James, of San Francisco; two daughters, Letitia Lewis of Weston, Conn., and Faith Taylor of Brookline, Mass; three sisters, Patricia Seeger of Seattle, Nancy Longstreth of Athens, Ga., and Barbara Ferguson of Buffalo, and one grandson.

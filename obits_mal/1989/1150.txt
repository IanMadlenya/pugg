        Bernard Brownstein, a metal distributor and philanthropist, died of a heart attack Monday at Mount Sinai Hospital. He was 78 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Brownstein was founder and president of metal exporters in Manhattan, including the Berlang Company, the Dale Metal Company, the Browning Export Corporation and the Berlang Steel Corporation.
        He also supported philanthropic causes, including the Federation of Jewish Philanthropies, the Hebrew Immigrant Aid Society, the Anti-Defamation League of B'nai B'rith, Yeshiva University and the United Jewish Appeal Federation.
        He was an alumnus of the Brooklyn Law School.
        Surviving are his wife, Harriet; two daughters, Lynn Halbfinger and June Gottlieb, both of Manhattan; a sister, Edna Bein of Brooklyn; a brother, Alex, of Manhattan, and two grandchildren.

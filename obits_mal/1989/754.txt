        Thomas J. Brogan, founder of an automobile dealership and a former chairman of the New Jersey Racing Commission, died Sunday at St. Francis Hospital in Miami Beach. He was 93 years old and had homes in Bal Harbor, Fla. and in Ridgewood and Spring Lake, N.J.
        Mr. Brogan was chairman of the Brogan Cadillac Oldsmobile Company, with five showrooms in New Jersey.
        He was on the Racing Commission for 19 years, 17 as chairman. He was also a former chairman of the North Jersey District Water Supply Commission, a former Police Commissioner in Paterson, a trustee of St. Joseph's and Valley Hospitals and a director of several banks.
        Surviving are his wife, Mildred; a daughter, Patricia Ely of Arlington, Va.; two sons, Thomas Jr., of Manhattan, and J. Peter, of Ho-Ho-Kus, N.J.; 13 grandchildren, and 16 great-grandchildren.

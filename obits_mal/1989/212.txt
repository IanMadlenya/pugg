        Richard D. Lombard, a co-founder and former chairman of Lombard, Vitalis & Paganucci Inc., a securities firm, died of lung cancer Friday at Memorial Sloan-Kettering Cancer Center in Manhattan. He was 58 and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Lombard founded the firm, which is no longer in business, in 1961 and retired in 1973, when he became an advisor to private investors. He graduated from Dartmouth College and its Amos Tuck School of Business Administration.
        He was a former member of the board of Dartmouth, a trustee and former vice-chairman of the board of Memorial Sloan-Kettering Cancer Center and chairman of Affiliated Artists of New York, which helps performers obtain jobs.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Jane Kettering; a son, Richard J., of Manhattan; a daughter, Sarah, of Bedford, Vt., and a brother, John, of Dayton, Ohio.

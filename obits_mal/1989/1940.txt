        Milton M. Rosenbloom, a copyright lawyer, died of a heart attack Monday at the Bellevue Hospital Center in Manhattan. He was 82 years old and lived in Teaneck, N.J.
        Mr. Rosenbloom, an alumnus of the New York University Law School, was a partner in Raftery, Grainger, Rosenbloom & Drew, a Manhattan law firm that represented many celebrities over the years, including Irving Berlin, George M. Cohan, Charlie Chaplin and Mary Pickford.
        He is survived by his wife, Mabel; a son, David, of Washington; a daughter, Judy Gondeiro, of Fairbanks, Alaska; a stepdaughter, Charlotte, of Manhattan; a sister, Helen Irwin, of Manhattan, and six grandchildren.

        John Eugene Unterecker, a poet, biographer and educator, died after heart surgery Monday at Straub Hospital in Honolulu. He was 66 years old and lived in Hawaii.
        Mr. Unterecker was the author of ''Voyager: A Life of Hart Crane,'' which was nominated for the National Book Award in 1970, and ''A Reader's Guide to W. B. Yeats.'' He was an editor of the Columbia University Press American Poets series. His own poetry appeared in The New Yorker, The Nation and Poetry magazine.
        After serving on the faculties of the College of the City of New York from 1946 to 1958, and Columbia University from 1958 to 1974, he became Citizens' Professor in English at the University of Hawaii, a position he held until his death.
        He is survived by a sister, Helen Fiske, of Rochester.

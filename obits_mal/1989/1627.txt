        James H. Sheils, a former New York City Commissioner of Investigation and a retired president of the Siboney Corporation, died of complications from lymphoma Monday at his home in Somers, N.Y. He was 76 years old.
        Mr. Sheils, a 1939 graduate of Fordham Law School, was deputy commissioner of investigation from 1943 to 1950. As the Commissioner from 1950 to 1954, he led investigations into shoddy sewer construction in Queens, payments to police officers to overlook illegal gambling and the sale of promotions in the Fire Department.
        He was in private law practice before and after his city service and was a managing partner in Gregory & Sons, a stock brokerage firm, from 1960 to 1969. In 1969, he became president of Siboney, an oil and gas concern that also makes educational products and computer software. He retired in 1985.
        He is survived by his wife, Margaret; seven daughters, Mary, of Harrison, N.Y., Clare, of Santa Cruz, Calif., Ann Koepper, of Somers, Catherine Bestall, of Rock Hill, S.C., Elizabeth Arnold, of Longhorne, Pa., Ellen V. Duggins, of London, and Patricia O'Donnell, of Lowell, Mass., and two sons, William, of West Bloomfield, Mich., and Paul, of Yardley, Pa.
        He is also survived by five brothers, Dr. William, of New Rochelle, N.Y., Robert, of Ardsley, N.Y., Thomas, of Canyon Lake, Calif., and Richard, of Elizabeth, N.J.; three sisters, Elizabeth Ricker, of Yarmouth Port, Mass., Ann McCormick, of Greenwich, Conn., and Patricia Loughman, of Morro Bay, Calif., and 15 grandchildren.

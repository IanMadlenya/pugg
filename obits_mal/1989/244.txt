        Aertsen P. Keasbey Jr., an engineer and a leader in the insulation industry, died of cancer Sunday at the Northern Westchester Medical Center in Mount Kisco, N.Y. He was 72 years old and lived in Mount Kisco.
        Mr. Keasbey was chief executive of the Robert A. Keasbey Company of Manhattan, an insulation contractor.
        He graduated from Cornell University in 1939. He served with the Army Engineers in World War II and was awarded the Bronze Star.
        Surviving are his wife, Mary; a son, Keith, of Granite Springs, N.Y.; a daughter, Parry, of Manhattan; three stepsons, Dr. Charles J. Lamb of Providence, R.I., John A. Lamb Jr. of Southborough, Mass., and Christopher H. Lamb of Los Angeles; a brother, Thomas, of Patterson, N.Y., and four grandchildren.

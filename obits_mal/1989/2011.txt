        Thomas J. Spenker, retired president of the J. C. Penney Insurance Companies, died of heart failure Sunday at his home in Riverside, Conn. He was 74 years old.
        In 1967 Mr. Spenker joined Penney to set up an insurance subsidiary. He had previously been executive vice president in charge of life and health insurance for Allstate, a subsidiary of Sears, Roebuck. He was president of the Penney Insurance Companies from 1970 until his retirement in 1976. He then headed his own international consulting firm on insurance management.
        Mr. Spenker was born in San Mateo, Calif. He received a law degree from the University of Montana and was a lieutenant in naval intelligence in World War II.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Charlotte Randall; two daughters, Elizabeth Harris and Charlotte Ball, and four grandchildren, all of Greenwich, Conn.

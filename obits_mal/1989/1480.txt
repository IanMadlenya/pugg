        Nathan Susskind, a New Jersey lawyer and philanthropist, died of cancer Friday at Bayonne (N.J.) Hospital. He was 91 years old and lived in Bayonne.
        Mr. Susskind, an expert in tax law, was one of the oldest practicing lawyers in New Jersey. He had lived in Bayonne from 1903, when his family emigrated from Austria.
        He graduated from the New York University School of Business in 1921 and Rutgers University Law School in 1926.
        A veteran of both world wars, Mr. Susskind contributed money and legal advice that helped dozens of Jewish families escape Nazi Germany in World War II.
        He was active in many organizations, including the Zionist Organization of America, Israel Bonds, B'nai B'rith, Jewish National Funds and the National Conference of Christians and Jews.
        Surviving are his wife, Lillian; two daughters, Betty S. Troum of Florida, and Henriette Weiss of North Woodmere, L.I.; a son, M. Stanley, of Bayonne; four sisters, Yetta Honig Cowan and Ann Tempkin, both of Florida, and Viola Steinbaum and Esther Shapiro, both of Bayonne; seven grandchildren, and one great-granddaughter.

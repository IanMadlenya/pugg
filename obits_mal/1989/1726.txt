        Hugh P. Fox, an architectural engineer and former commissioner of the Board of Standards and Appeals for New York City, died of heart disease yesterday at the Medical Center at Princeton, N.J. He was 84 years old and lived in Rossmoor, N.J.
        Mr. Fox, who was born in Jersey City, entered city service as a clerk in 1920. He transferred to the board in 1926 and worked there for 47 years. He retired in 1970.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Marjorie Peters; a daughter, Pamela Henderson of North Charleston, S.C.; three grandchildren, and two great-grandchildren.

        Walter William Friend Jr., senior vice president of Paine Webber, the Wall Street investment company, died of a heart attack Tuesday at his home in Norwalk, Conn. He was 69 years old.
        With Paine Webber, Mr. Friend specialized in hospital and college financing and was investment adviser to the health and education departments of New York, Connecticut, Michigan and Massachusetts.
        He was also a director of the Greater New York Savings Bank since 1954.
        Mr. Friend graduated from Dartmouth College and attended Yale Law School. He was born in Brooklyn.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Doris Schwanhausser; a son, Walter W. 3d, of East Dennis, Mass.; a daughter, Eleanor Hickok of Atlanta; a sister, Edith Wearin of Greenwich, Conn., and three grandchildren.

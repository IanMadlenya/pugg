        Dr. Peter C. Freudenthal, former director of air- and land-use programs and environmental affairs for Consolidated Edison, died Wednesday at his home in Allendale, N.J., after a long illness. He was 55 years old.
        Dr. Freudenthal joined Con Edison in 1970 and organized its first section for air quality analysis and control. He was also active in the environmental division of the Air Pollution Control Association, the Electric Power Research Institute, the Utility Air Regulatory Group and the air subcommittee of the New York Power Pool.
        He was a graduate of New York University, where he also earned master's and doctoral degrees. He was an adjunct associate professor at Long Island University's Graduate Department of Marine Science from 1966 to 1975.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Ruth Ephrain, and two sons, Eric, of Manhattan, and David, of Allendale.

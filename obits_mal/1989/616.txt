        Bertram Greenglass, a former official of the National Aeronautics and Space Administration and a business executive, died of leukemia Friday at George Washington University Hospital in Washington. He was 56 years old and lived in Lake Ridge, Va.
        Mr. Greenglass, a graduate of New York University, was president of the Consolidated Bailing Machine Company, of Jacksonville, Fla. at his death.
        He had previously been director of planning, program control and administration for the Housing and Urban Development Administration, chief of launching operations and of the Apollo program control at the Kennedy Space Center, and director of alcohol fuels at the Department of Energy.
        He is survived by his wife, Polly; three daughters, Gwen Bleustein, of Baltimore, Felice Latzko, of Vienna, Va., and Leslie Titcomb, of Wheaton, Md.; a brother, Gerald, of Oradell, N.J., and two grandchildren.

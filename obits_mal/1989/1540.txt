        John G. Piper, a former vice president of the Pepperell textile company, died of bone cancer Friday at the Connecticut Hospice in Branford. He was 74 years old and lived in Westport, Conn.
        Mr. Piper, who retired in 1970 from the Pepperell Manufacturing Company, now West Point-Pepperell Inc., led an industry group that devised flammability standards for bed sheets and blankets in 1967.
        He was a native of Winnipeg, Manitoba, and a graduate of Harvard. He served as a lieutenant in the Navy in World War II.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Mary Hamill; a son, John Jr., of Kansas City, Kans.; two daughters, Heather P. Cole of Pease Air Force Base, N.H., and Bonnie, of Washington; a brother, William, of Los Altos Hills, Calif,, and three grandchildren.

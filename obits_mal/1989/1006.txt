        Robert Thomases, an educator and former director of a camp for disturbed children, died Thursday at his home in Rhinebeck, N.Y. He was 71 years old.
        Mr. Thomases taught for 27 years at Sewanhaka (L.I.) High School and was director emeritus of the Ramapo Anchorage Camp in Rhinebeck. Mr. Thomases became camp director in 1952. He used innovations to help emotionally disturbed or learning-disabled children, including recruiting high school and college students to work with them one-to-one.
        Mr. Thomases was an alumnus of the University of Illinois and Columbia University.
        Surviving are his wife, Kay; a son, Thomas, of Kingston, N.Y.; a daughter, Linda Lafourcade of Bordeaux, France, and three grandchildren.

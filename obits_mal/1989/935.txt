        James Arthur Campbell, an educator, chemist and former dean of faculty at Harvey Mudd College in Claremont, Calif., died of Lou Gehrig's disease Monday at his home in Claremont, Calif. He was 72 years old.
        Dr. Campbell, who received a Ph.D. from the University of California at Berkeley in 1942, worked on the atomic bomb project before joining the chemistry faculty at Oberlin College in 1945.
        He left in 1957 to become chairman of the chemistry department at Harvey Mudd College, where he became dean of faculty in 1974. He retired in 1987.
        In the 1960's he directed the Chemical Education Material Study, a project that revised the chemistry curriculum for high schools across the country.
        He is survived by his wife, Dorothy; two daughters, Kathleen Fischer, of Del Mar, Calif., and Christine North, of Olivenhain, Calif.; five granddaughters and one grandson.

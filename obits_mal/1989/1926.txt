        Joseph William Allen, an electrical engineer, inventor and pioneer in the field of aviation engineering, died Monday at the Morristown (N.J.) Medical Center of injuries sustained in a recent fall at his home in Whippany, N.J. He was 96 years old.
        A 1915 graduate of Bucknell University, Mr. Allen was a pilot-observer in the Aviation Division of the United States Army during World War I. He was later chief electrical engineer with Bendix Aviation for over 20 years. He held numerous patents on designs of electrical equipment. He retired from Bendix in 1961.
        He is survived by his son, William, of Washington, and a brother, Alexander, of Great Neck, L.I.

        Henry Evans Griffith, a plastics industry executive, died Tuesday at the Rivercrest Nursing Home in Concord, Mass. He was 74 years old and lived in Concord.
        Mr. Griffith joined the Monsanto Chemical Company in 1937, shortly after graduating from Princeton University. He was with the Plax Corporation from 1941 to 1949 as vice president for marketing and later became vice president of the tube division of the American Can Company.
        In 1958 he founded Plastic Tube and Bottle Inc., which later became part of Dart Industries, where he was vice president for research. He had been with Plastic Packaging Associates, of Waccabuc, N.Y., since 1975.
        He is survived by four daughters, Christine Heyworth of Riverside, Conn., Constance Dix of Avon, Conn., Suzanne Griffith of Dodgeville, Wis., and Pamela Simonds, of Concord; a stepdaughter, Julie Cabanas, of LeGrange, N.Y., and 12 grandchildren.

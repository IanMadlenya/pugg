        Harry A. Logan Jr., who was chairman and president of the United Refining Company from 1957 until he retired in 1981, died of heart failure on Wednesday at his home in Warren, Pa. He was 64 years old.
        Mr. Logan, a native of Warren, was a graduate of Princeton. He served in the United States Army in the Pacific in and spent his entire business career with United, an independent oil refiner based in Warren that was sold to Coral Petroleum Inc., in 1981.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Kay Hardesty; a son, Thomas, and a sister, Marian Wendell, both of Warren.

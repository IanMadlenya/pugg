        Mair J. Benardete, professor emeritus of Spanish at Brooklyn College, died of a heart attack Friday at his home in Somerville, Mass. He was 93 years old.
        Professor Benardete, who was born in Turkey, graduated from the University of Cincinnati and earned a doctorate at Columbia University.
        He retired in 1965 after teaching for 35 years at Brooklyn College. He also taught at Hunter College and was a member of the Royal Academy of the Spanish Language.
        He is survived by three sons, Jose, of Syracuse, Seth, of Manhattan, and Diego, of Somerville; two stepdaughters, Elektra Vise and Penelope Vise, both of Brooklyn; two brothers, Sabbetay and Albert, both of Istanbul; a sister, Corinne Ajiman of Istanbul, and four grandchildren.

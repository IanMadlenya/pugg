        Michael O'R. Nash, who was photo and communications chief in Paris during a long career in The Associated Press foreign service, died in Paris today after a long battle with cancer, his family said. He was 69.
        Mr. Nash, former assistant chief of bureau, photo editor and chief of communications in Paris, retired in 1985 after 44 years with A.P.'s foreign service.
        He restored A.P. news photo services in France after World War II and oversaw major changes over the next four decades in the Paris A.P. bureau's photo and communications operations.

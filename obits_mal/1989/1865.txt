        John Archer Gifford, a lawyer, died Sunday at Falmouth (Mass.) Hospital after a brief illness. He was 88 years old and lived in Manhattan and Woods Hole, Mass.
        Mr. Gifford, who specialized in trusts and estates, was a senior partner in White & Case, the Manhattan law firm, until his retirement in 1972. He joined White & Case in 1929, becoming a partner in 1937.
        He had been a trustee of Presbyterian Hospital and was one of the founders of the Associates of the Woods Hole Oceanographic Institution.
        He was born in Newark and graduated from Yale College in 1922 and Harvard Law School in 1925. He served in the Navy during World War II as a special assistant to the Assistant Secretary of the Navy, with the rank of lieutenant commander.
        He is survived by a son, Prosser, of Washington; three grandchildren and a great-grandson.

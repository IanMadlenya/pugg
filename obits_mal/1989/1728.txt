        Charles Raymond Mackland, a retired director of the photographic division at the United States Information Agency and former photo editor at Life magazine, died of cancer Monday at Georgetown University Hospital in Washington. He was 78 years old and lived in Washington.
        Ray Mackland, as he was known professionally, was a graduate of the University of Nebraska. He was a reporter for The Omaha World Herald from 1928 to 1941, when he left to join Life. From 1942 to 1944 he was Life's chief Washington correspondent and then went to New York as an assistant picture editor. He was picture editor from 1950 to 1961.
        He then joined U.S.I.A. and retired in 1978.
        He is survived by his wife, Catherine, and a daughter, Mary Joan, of Forest Hills, Queens.

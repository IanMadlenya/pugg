        Dr. Elliott F. Osserman, the American Cancer Society Professor of Medicine at the College of Physicians and Surgeons of Columbia University, died of Parkinson's disease yesterday at his home in Closter, N.J. He was 64 years old.
        Dr. Osserman's major work was in the diseases of multiple myeloma and amyloidosis, fatal forms of cancer. He joined the medical school in 1953 and became a full professor in 1968.
        Among his honors was the Ehrlich Medal of the International Society of Hemotology. Dr. Osserman was a Fellow of the American Association for the Advancement of Science.
        He was born in New York and was an alumnus of the Horace Mann School, Columbia College and the College of Physicians and Surgeons.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Nancy Kringel; a sister, Marian Bauer of Manhattan; two daughters, Pamela Bank of Tel Aviv and Deborah Ray Leith of Washington, and three grandchildren.

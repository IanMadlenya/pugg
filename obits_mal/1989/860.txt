        Maurice H. Belkin, an engineer and construction executive, died of a heart attack on May 8 at his home in Lawrence, L.I. He was 86 years old.
        Mr. Belkin was president of the MacArthur Construction Corporation of Manhattan from its founding in 1934 until his death. The company has worked on major apartment and office buildings, including the Phoenix House at 160 East 65th Street.
        Mr. Belkin began his career as an owner's representative for the construction of an office building at 16 Court Street in downtown Brooklyn and of Chatsworth Gardens, an apartment complex in Larchmont, N.Y.
        He graduated from Brooklyn Polytechnic Institute in 1926.
        Surviving are two sons, Eugene, of New Rochelle, N.Y., and Lewis, of Spotswood, N.J.; a brother, Ben, of Plainfield, N.J., a sister, Alice Feldman of Brooklyn, and four grandchildren.

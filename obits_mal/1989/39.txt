        Louis Hausman, a broadcasting executive, died of pneumonia Wednesday at George Washington Hospital in Washington. He was 82 years old and lived in Washington.
        From 1940 to 1959, Mr. Hausman was a vice president of CBS Inc. From 1962 to 1965, when he retired, he was a vice president of NBC. He previously served as director of the Television Information Office, a public-information service for the broadcasting industry.
        Mr. Hausman took a series of public-service jobs after he retired to Washington. He was an assistant for the United States Commission of Education, director of planning for the National Endowment for the Humanities, vice president of the Washington Better Business Bureau, a director on the United States Committee for UNICEF and director of planning for the National Council on Aging.
        He is survived by his wife, Theodora.

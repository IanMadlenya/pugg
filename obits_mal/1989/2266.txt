        Roy Bennett, an adviser to three Presidents on economics and foreign policy, died of a heart attack Wednesday at New York Hospital, where he was recovering from surgery. He was 77 years old and lived in Teaneck, N.J.
        During his career Mr. Bennett was an executive of several companies, including Project Development International, which specializes in metals and advanced technology and has offices in Manhattan, London, Brussels and Moscow. He was also involved in reconstruction programs in the Soviet Union.
        A Democrat, Mr. Bennett was an adviser on foreign and economic policies to Presidents John F. Kennedy, Lyndon B. Johnson and Jimmy Carter. He was also a campaign adviser to Senator Edward M. Kennedy of Massachusetts and Representative Robert Torricelli of Bergen County, N.J.
        Mr. Bennett was a board member of the National Committee for a Sane Nuclear Policy and Americans for Democratic Action, a national member of the American Committee on East-West Accord and a president of Open Party Democrats of Teaneck. He was associate editor of Social Policy, a quarterly, United Nations correspondent for The London Tribune and a contributing writer to magazines.
        Mr. Bennett was born in Rockaway Beach, Queens, and attended Ohio State University.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Pearl Weslock.

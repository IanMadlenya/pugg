        Frank Day Tuttle, a theatrical and radio producer and director, died in his sleep Monday at the Actors Fund Home in Englewood, N.J. He was 86 years old.
        In 1939 Mr. Tuttle, Richard Skinner and Cheryl Crawford produced ''Family Portrait,'' starring Judith Anderson. He and Mr. Skinner also founded the Westchester Playhouse in Mount Kisco in the early 1930's and produced a Broadway revival of ''Charley's Aunt,'' starring Jose Ferrer, in 1940.
        On radio Mr. Tuttle directed ''The March of Time,'' ''Henry Aldrich'' and the serial ''Bright Horizon.''
        From 1947 to 1957, he taught in the Smith College drama department. Returning to New York, he directed an Off Broadway production of George Bernard Shaw's ''In Good King Charles's Golden Days.'' He also converted the Bohemian Club into the East 74th Street Theater.
        He is survived by two daughters, Nancy Tuttle Sackson of Austerlitz, N.Y., and Mary Elizabeth Tuttle of Paris; a son, Anthony Marchant Tuttle of New York, and three grandsons.

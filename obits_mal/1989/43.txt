        George Cassidy, the starter at New York Racing Association tracks for 53 years, died in his sleep early yesterday at his home in Brookville, L.I. He was 82 years old and had been ill with liver cancer.
        In addition to starting races in New York, Mr. Cassidy served as starter at Hialeah and Churchill Downs. He retired in 1975.
        He is survived by his wife, Jeanne; three sons, George Jr., Noel and Brian, and several grandchildren.
        Correction:  January 27, 1989, Friday, Late City Final Edition
        An obituary on Sunday about George Cassidy, the official starter at New York Racing Association tracks, omitted a survivor. She is a daughter, Sheila Cassidy Outcault.

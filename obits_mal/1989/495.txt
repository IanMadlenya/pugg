        Hanina (Hank) Zinder, who took part in establishing the Tennessee Valley Authority and the Federal Power Commission, where he was head of the rates division, died Saturday after suffering a heart attack at his home in Longboat Key, Fla. He was 85 years old.
        After leaving Government service in 1946, Mr. Zinder was president of the consulting engineering company, H. Zinder & Associates, and helped set up many of the nation's major natural gas pipelines.
        Mr. Zinder was a native of Pittsburgh and a graduate of what is now the Carnegie-Mellon University and Northwestern University.
        Surviving are his wife, Helen; two daughters, Ruth Anne Rubinstein, of Great Neck, L.I., and Miriam Cutler, of Washington, D.C.; two brothers, Ruben, of Tucson, Ariz., and Harry, of Jerusalem; two sisters, Leah Chemerow, of Kenosha, Wis., and Shami Lubin, of Tel Aviv; nine grandchildren and two great-grandchildren.

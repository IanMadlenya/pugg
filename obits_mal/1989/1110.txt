        Dr. Stanley L. Wallace, a specialist in studying and treating gout and a professor of medicine at the state's Health Science Center at Brooklyn, died of lymphoma Tuesday at his home in Brooklyn. He was 65 years old.
        Dr. Wallace joined the faculty of the school, formerly the Downstate Medical Center, in 1951. At his death, he was chief of the internal medicine division. He was also director of medicine at the State University Hospital and acting chief of the rheumatology division. Dr. Wallace studied the pharmacology of gout and wrote on rheumatic and arthritic diseases.
        Dr. Wallace was born in Jersey City. He was a Phi Beta Kappa graduate of Duke University and received his medical degree from the Bowman Gray School of Medicine. He served as a captain in the Air Force medical corps. Dr. Wallace was a past president of the state chapter of the American College of Physicians and the New York Rheumatism Association, Surviving are his wife, the former Eleanor Zucker; three sons, Jonathan and Richard, of Brooklyn, and Joseph, of Pleasantville, N.Y.; a sister, Roslyn Schechtman of Brightwater, N.Y., and a grandson.

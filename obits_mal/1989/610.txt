        Walter S. Fried, a member of the board of the New York City Housing Authority from 1970 to 1986, died Thursday at his home in Manhattan. He was 80 years old.  Mr. Fried, a lawyer, was formerly commissioner in charge of the office of housing and facilities production in the Housing and Development Administration. As such, he supervised the city's Mitchell-Lama middle-income housing program.
        Mr. Fried entered Federal Government service in 1946, working with veterans housing. From 1956 to 1960 he was regional administrator of the Housing and Home Finance Agency, the predecessor of the Department of Housing and Urban Development.
        A native New Yorker, Mr. Fried was a graduate of the City College of New York and Columbia University Law School.
        He is survived by a daughter, Laura, of Brooklyn.

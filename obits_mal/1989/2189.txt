        James K. Polk, a former Manhattan tax lawyer, died of congestive heart failure yesterday at his home in Jacksonville, Fla. He was 91 years old.
        Mr. Polk, a 1926 alumnus of Georgetown University's law school, served with the Internal Revenue Service before joining Whitman & Ransom in 1935 as head of its tax department. During his 53-year career with the law firm he represented Consolidated Edison and the Western Pacific Railroad and served on tax committees of the American Bar Association.
        He is survived by his wife, Ethel; a daughter, Jeanne Murray of Lady Lake, Fla.; two sons, James, of Whittier, Calif., and William, of Paris; 13 grandchildren and six great-grandchildren.

        William F. Redfield, a retired grocery executive and former president of the Hills Brothers Company, died Monday at his home in Montclair, N.J., after a long illness. He was 94 years old.
        Mr. Redfield, a 1917 graduate of the United States Military Academy at West Point, was with Hills Brothers, a manufacturer of cake mixes and other foods, from 1921 until his retirement in 1957. The company is now a division of RJR-Nabisco.
        Mr. Redfield was a former director of the Grocery Manufacturers Association and of the Grocery Store Products Company.
        He is survived by his wife, Louise; two daughters, Aubin Sander of New Canaan, Conn., and Jane Fosberg of Westwood, Mass.; a son, William, of Princeton, N.J.; a brother, Alden, of Chapel Hill, N.C., eight grandchildren and five great-grandchildren.

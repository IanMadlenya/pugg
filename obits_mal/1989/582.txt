        Richard R. O'Connor, a lawyer and former New Jersey judge, died Monday in Mountainside Hospital in Montclair, N.J., after a brief illness. He was 70 years old and lived in Elizabeth, N.J.
        Mr. O'Connor served for seven years as a state judge in the former Juvenile and Domestic Relations Court early in his career. He also was a commissioner of the New Jersey Highway Authority and, as such, had an active part in developing and building the Garden State Arts Center in Holmdel.
        He was a graduate of Georgetown University and of the university's law school. He also graduated from the Harvard University Graduate School of Business Administration.
        He is survived by two sons, Richard Jr. and Martin, and a sister, Edith Bruning, all of Elizabeth.

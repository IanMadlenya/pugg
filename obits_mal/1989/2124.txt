        Sidney Krakauer, an inventor of biomedical systems and a retired corporate executive, died Monday at Boca Raton (Fla.) Hospital after a brief illness. He was 69 years old and lived in Highland Beach, Fla.
        Mr. Krakauer joined the Pall Corporation, a manufacturer of disposable filters and fluid control equipment in Glen Cove, L.I., in 1952. He received more than 25 patents, including several for filters for anesthesia, blood-transfusion, intravenous-fluid and breathing systems. As a result of his work, Pall formed the Pall Biomedical Products Corporation in 1975, with Mr. Krakauer as its president. The same year he was named senior vice president of the parent corporation.
        He was elected a director of the parent company in 1984 and retired in 1987.
        A native New Yorker, Mr. Krakauer was a graduate of the City College of New York.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Vera Osman, eight children and seven grandchildren.

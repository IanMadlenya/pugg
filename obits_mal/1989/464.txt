        G. Bennett Larson, a former vice president of WPIX-TV in New York and a former communications industry consultant, died of pneumonia Saturday at St. John's Hospital in Santa Monica, Calif. He was 79 years old and lived in Brentwood, Calif.
        Mr. Larson produced radio shows before joining WPIX in 1950. After leaving the station in 1953, he bought and sold stations for Blackburn & Company and later formed his own consulting company. In the 1970's, he was the owner of WOKR-TV in Rochester.
        Surviving are his wife, Doris, and two sons, Christian, of Pacific Palisades, Calif., and Eric, of San Clemente, Calif.

        Donald S. Gilpatric , a retired Foreign Service officer, died of emphysema Jan. 30 in St. Augustine, Fla. He was 79 years old.  Mr. Gilpatric, a Brooklyn native, retired from the Foreign Service in 1981 after nearly 20 years as director of the Office of Foreign Commercial Services in the Department of Commerce. In World War II he was director of the China Relief Mission and economic adviser to Allied Forces Headquarters in Algeria.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Marjorie Dudley; a son, Kenneth, of Washington; a daughter, Virginia, of Manhattan; and a brother, Roswell, also of Manhattan.

        Gerald J. Weber, a retired United States District Court judge who was the chief Federal judge in western Pennsylvania from 1976 to 1982, died of cancer on Monday. He was 75 years old and lived in Erie.
        Judge Weber, a former Erie city solicitor, was named a Federal judge in 1964. He retired last Dec. 31.
        He was best known for a 1981 order consolidating several suburban Pittsburgh school districts to end a racial discrimination lawsuit.
        He is survived by his wife, Berta; two sons, Thomas of Erie and William of Boston, and a daughter, Mary, of New York City.

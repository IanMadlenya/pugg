
       Lieut. Gen. Sarwo Edhie, retired, who led army troops in suppressing a Communist coup attempt in 1965, died on Thursday of a stroke. He was 62 years old. He joined the army in 1943 when he was 16 to fight for independence from the Netherlands. He also was a member of Parliament, from which he resigned in 1988.

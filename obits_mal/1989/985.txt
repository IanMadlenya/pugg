        Forrest Comer Lattner, an investment banker, philanthropist and oil executive , died of a stroke April 28 at Bethesda Memorial Hospital in Boynton Beach, Fla. He was 87 years old and lived in Delray Beach, Fla.
        Mr. Lattner became the largest shareholder of the Central Louisiana Electric Company and chairman of its executive committee. In 1961, he founded the Delray Oil Company in San Antonio. Mr. Lattner also founded a foundation that bears his name and contributed to many groups, including the Boy Scouts and the Leukemia Foundation.
        He graduated from the University of Texas in 1924. He went to work for the Guaranty Trust Company in Manhattan and later became a managing partner of Lazard Freres & Company.
        Surviving are his wife, Frances; two daughters, Martha Connelly, of Wichita, Kan., and Susan Hollenbeck, of St. Louis; a sister, Estelle L. Brown, of Denton, Tex., and six grandchildren.

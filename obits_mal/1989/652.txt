        The Rev. Frederic C. Lawrence, the retired Suffragan Bishop of the Episcopal Diocese of Massachusetts, died Sunday at his home in Brookline, Mass., after a long illness. He was 89 years old.
        Bishop Lawrence, who became a priest in 1925 after graduating from the Episcopal Theological School in Cambridge, Mass., was rector of St. Peter's Church there and St. Paul's Church in Brookline.
        He was elected Suffragan Bishop in 1956 and retired in 1968. He was active with the Episcopal chaplaincy at Harvard and was a trustee of Milton Academy and of the American University in Beirut.
        He is survived by his wife, Katharine; three daughters, Katharine, of Arlington, Mass., Margaret Drinker, of Belmont, Mass., and Marian, of Lubec, Me.; two sons, Frederic C. Lawrence Jr., of Cambridge, and Peter, of Portland, Me.; eight grandchildren and one great-grandchild.

        Herbert B. Loper, a retired Army major general and former chairman of the Military Liaison Committee of the Atomic Energy Commission, died of cardiac arrest on Friday at the Palm Bay Care Center in Florida. He was 92 years old and had lived in Bozman, Md., before entering the nursing home last year.
        General Loper, a 1918 graduate of the United States Military Academy, at West Point, was commissioned in the Corps of Engineers. In World War II he was in an engineering intelligence unit and later helped plan operations for the Marianas and Okinawa campaigns. He retired from active duty in 1953 after serving with the Joint Chiefs of Staff. From 1954 to 1961 he was chairman of the Military Liaison Committee.
        He is survived by two sons, Herbert and Thomas, both of Satellite Beach, Fla.; six grandchildren, and six great-grandchildren.

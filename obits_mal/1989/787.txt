        Dr. Mack Lipkin, clinical professor of medicine at the University of North Carolina's Chapel Hill School of Medicine, died of a brain stem infarction Tuesday at his home at Chapel Hill. He was 82 years old and also maintained a residence in New York City.
        When he was in private practice, from 1934 to 1972, Dr. Lipkin served as an attending physician at New York, Mount Sinai, University, Metropolitan and Goldwater Hospitals in New York City. When he left private practice, he became professor of medicine at the University of Oregon and later held visiting professorships at Dartmouth and the University of California at San Diego.
        Dr. Lipkin joined the Chapel Hill School of Medicine in 1980 as distinguished research scholar and became clinical professor in 1985.
        A native of New York City, he graduated from City College in 1926 and Cornell University Medical College in 1930.
        He is survived by his wife, Carol Fraenkel Lipkin; two sons, John of Perry Point, Md., and Mack Jr. of New York City; a daughter, Deborah Lipkin Goldsmith of New York City, and two grandchildren.

        Marvin S. Corwin, a retired New York advertising executive, died Tuesday at his home in Beverly Hills, Calif., after a long illness. He was 75 years old.
        After graduating from journalism school in 1935, Mr. Corwin was hired by John H. Dunham, a small Chicago agency that handled the O'Henry candy bar account and some of Sears, Roebuck & Company's advertising.
        In 1937 he moved to New York to take a job in copy research with Benton & Bowles. He became copy chief for Grey Advertising Agency in 1941, leaving the following year to join the Army.
        After the war he returned to Grey in an administrative job and two years later returned to Benton & Bowles as an account supervisor. In 1954 he joined the staff of Doyle Dane Bernbach as senior vice president. In 1965 he beame president and chief operating officer of Erwin Wasey Inc., a subsidiary of Interpublic International. In the 1970's he became senior vice president at Interpublic and president of McCann-Erickson Europe, another of the Interpublic Group. He was based in London and retired in 1979.
        He is survived by a son, Kip, of Manhattan.

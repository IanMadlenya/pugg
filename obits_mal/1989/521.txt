        Henry Gerard Bruns, a retired investment broker and railroad executive, died Wednesday at his home in New London, N.H. He was 84 years old and had lived in Montclair, N.J., before moving to New Hampshire in 1977.
        Mr. Bruns, a native of Manhattan, was for many years a senior partner in the Wall Street brokerage firm of T. L. Watson. He was also the last chairman of the Norfolk Southern Railroad Company. The company merged with Southern Railway on Jan. 1, 1974, the year Mr. Bruns retired.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Mildred Scott; two daughters, Elizabeth Johnson of Greenwich, Conn., and Priscilla Glander of San Rafael, Calif., and four grandchildren.

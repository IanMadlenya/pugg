        Robert J. Eliasberg, a retired Manhattan lawyer, died of cancer and emphysema on Sunday at his home in Santa Monica, Calif. He was 83 years old.
        Mr. Eliasberg's clients included the New York City Patrolmen's Benevolent Association. He defended officers who were indicted on charges stemming from the 1971 Knapp Commission investigation, which uncovered widespread corruption in the Police Department.
        Mr. Eliasberg was born in Newark. , He graduated from the City College of New York and New York University Law School.
        Surviving are his wife, Katherine; two sons, Kenneth, of Canyon Country, Calif., and Michael, of Beverly Hills, Calif., and three grandchildren.

        Leslie C. Overlock, an early marketing administrator in the photocopier business, died Monday at his home in Sun City, Ariz., where he had moved in 1985 from Greenwich, Conn. He was 69 years old.
        Mr. Overlock first worked as a sales administrator at the A. M. Photocopy Company in Chicago after his discharge from the Army Air Corps in 1946. He later worked for Smith Corona Marchant as vice president of marketing, a post he held from 1959 to 1966. At his retirement in 1982, he was president of Roneo Vicker, manufacturers of office equipment.
        He was born in Chicago and graduated from Dartmouth College in 1941.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Fern Wilson; a son, Leslie, of San Francisco; two daughters, Starr Nelson of Napa Valley, Calif., and Joelle Baker of Albany; a brother; four sisters, and six grandchildren.

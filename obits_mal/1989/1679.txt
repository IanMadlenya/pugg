        Asa Paul Ruskin, director of rehabilitation medicine at the Kingsbrook Jewish Medical Center in Brooklyn, died of a ruptured abdominal aneurysm yesterday, his 60th birthday, at Mt. Sinai Medical Center. He lived in Manhattan.
        Dr. Ruskin was also an associate clinical professor of rehabilitation medicine at the Albert Einstein College of Medicine in the Bronx and lectured widely in this country and abroad. At Kingsbrook he supervised a staff of more than 100 professionals in rehabilitation medicine. He was also a prominent advocate for the disabled.
        He was born in Manhattan and was a graduate of the Bronx High School of Science, Emory University and the University of Paris, where he received his medical degree. Before joining Kingsbrook in 1961 he was chief resident in neurology at Montefiore Hospital in the Bronx.
        Dr. Ruskin was a fellow of the New York Academy of Medicine, the American College of Physicians and the American Academy of Physical Medicine and Rehabilitation. He was president of the New York Society of Physical Medicine and Rehabilitation in 1980, and secretary of the American Congress of Physical Medicine and Rehabilitation from 1983 to 1987.
        Dr. Ruskin is survived by his wife, the former Francine Klein; two sons, Mark and Philippe, both of Manhattan, and a daughter, Adina, of Paris.

        David Goodman, a lawyer who practiced real-estate law in Manhattan for more than 60 years, died of heart failure Wednesday at New York Hospital-Cornell Medical Center. He was 91 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Goodman, a graduate of Brooklyn Law School, was a founding partner of the Goodman & Mabel law firm, which no longer exists. He served as an ensign in the Navy during World War I. He is survived by his wife, Theresa; a daughter, Margot Hentoff of Manhattan; two sisters, Esther Schiff of Brooklyn and Hannah Schiff of Woodmere, L.I.; four grandchildren, and three great-grandchildren.

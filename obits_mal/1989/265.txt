        Joseph R. Marro, a retired justice of the New York State Supreme Court, died of heart failure yesterday at his home in Manhattan. He was 81 years old.
        Justice Marro was a native of Manhattan. He graduated from St. John's College and St. John's Law School. After practicing law he was elected on the Democratic ticket in 1952 to represent the Lower East Side in the Stater Senate. In 1964 he won a seat on the New York City Civil Court, and in 1974 he was elected to the State Supreme Court. He retired in 1977.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Anita Aloi; a daughter, Linda Donato of Smithtown, L.I.; a son, Joseph P., of Manhattan; a sister, Marie Schiaffino of Manhattan, and two grandchildren.

        Norman Abner Adler, a lawyer and former CBS executive, died of complications of emphysema Saturday at Mission Bay Hospital near his home in La Jolla, Calif. He was 80 years old.
        A 1933 alumnus of Yale Law School, Mr. Adler was with CBS, which was formerly called the Columbia Broadcasting System, for 23 years. He joined Columbia Records in 1948 after serving with the anti-trust division of the Justice Department. He helped organize the Columbia Record Club and was named executive vice president of CBS Records in 1960. He later managed the educational services division of CBS and in 1967 joined the corporate staff as a vice president and general executive. He retired in 1974. He is survived by his wife, Leona; a son, Jonathan of Los Angeles; a daughter, Louise Adler Burton of Albany, Calif.; a brother Walter, of Manhattan, and two grandchildren.

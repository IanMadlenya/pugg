        Charles Impaglia, director of program scheduling for the Public Broadcasting Service, died of AIDS on Sunday at the Hospice of Washington. He was 39 years old.
        Mr. Impaglia spent his entire career in public broadcasting, working as a press agent and writer for stations WNET in New York, WGBH in Boston, KQED in San Francisco and KCET in Los Angeles. He became director of broadcasting at the Los Angeles station in 1984 and was named director of program scheduling for PBS last year.

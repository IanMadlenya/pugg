        John S. Chapman Jr., a lawyer for 69 years in Manhattan, died of cardiopulmunary arrest Monday at his home in Tuckahoe, N.Y. He was 91 years old.
        Mr. Chapman was a longtime partner in Duer & Taylor. He became of counsel to Reynolds, Richards, LaVenture, Hadley & Davis in 1984.
        He was a former justice and trustee of Tuckahoe and a former arbitrator with the American Arbitration Association. Mr. Chapman was born in Charlottesville, Va., and was an alumnus of the University of Virginia and its Law School.
        Surviving are two daughters, Betty Cirrincione of Tuckahoe and Sallie Anderson of Portland, Ore.; nine grandchildren, and eight great-grandchildren.

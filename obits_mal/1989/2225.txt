        John M. Alden, an engineer and the founder of Alden Electronics, died of cancer Wednesday at his home in Needham, Mass. He was 66 years old.
        Mr. Alden was a leader in the transition from analog weather chart recorders to computer-based weather data terminals. He held many patents in facsimile and imaging technology.
        He is survived by his wife, Joan Keller; two daughters, Elizabeth, of Wellesley, Mass., and Katherine, of Spring Mills, Pa.; a son, Peter, of North Easton, Mass.; three sisters, Louise Sands of Marshfield, Mass., Elizabeth Morrall of Lexington, Mass., and Priscilla Cross of West Bridgewater, Mass.; a brother, William, of Westborough, Mass., and eight grandchildren.

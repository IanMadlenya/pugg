        Georg von Lilienfeld, a German foreign service officer and diplomat in the years before and after World War II, died on Dec. 11 in Munich, where he lived. He was 77 years old.
        Mr. von Lilienfeld was born in Riga, Latvia. He served as a censor of radio programs in Germany's Foreign Office before the Nazi defeat in World War II.
        After the war he came to the United States to testify in trials of Americans accused of treason. He served later as head of the United States division of the West German Foreign Office and as West Germany's Ambassador to Iran from 1968 to 1974 and to Spain from 1974 to 1980.
        Survivors include his wife, the former Margit Cable von Ternes-Hattburg, and a daughter, Charlotte, of Manhattan.

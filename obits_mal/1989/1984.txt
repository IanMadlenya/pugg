        Louis Levine, a retired manufacturer of women's coats and suits, died of colon and bladder cancer on Monday at the Memorial Sloan-Kettering Cancer Center. He was 78 years old and lived in Great Neck, L.I.
        Mr. Levine was president of Morris Levine & Son, a family concern in Manhattan established in 1937. He became president in 1956, after his father's death. A division, Kiera Leather Fashions, was added in 1974. In 1977, Mr. Levine was elected president of the Fur Buyers Association of the Cloak and Suit Industry. He retired in 1980.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Ida Forer; a son, Barry, of Chevy Chase, Md.; two daughters, Rita, of Bayfield, Colo., and Barbara Rackman, of Manhattan; a brother, Julius, of Brooklyn, and three grandchildren.

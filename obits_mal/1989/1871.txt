        Henry Bowen Burnett Jr., a specialist in direct-mail marketing, died Friday at his home in Santa Barbara, Calif., after a long illness. He was 59 years old.
        After working in Boston and New York, he moved to California in 1959. He was then creative director for Benson, Stagg & Associates, a New York City agency that is now defunct.
        Later, in Santa Barbara, he became a partner in Forum Communications, where he was instrumental in developing Cuisine and Politics Today magazines. He also created mailing packages for nonprofit environmental agencies.
        Mr. Burnett was born in Summit, N.J., and graduated from Harvard College in 1952.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Gretchen Fall; his mother, Alicia Burnett of Warwick, R.I.; a son and two daughters from a previous marriage, W. Nicholas Burnett of Portland, Me., Whiteley Wheeler of Weston, Mass., and Alicia Burnett of Santa Barbara; two brothers, Bruce of Attleboro, Mass., and David of Warwick, R.I., and two grandchildren.

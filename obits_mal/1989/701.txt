        Carel Goldschmidt, former president of the American Sumatra Tobacco Corporation, died of a heart attack Saturday at New York Hospital. He was 84 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Goldschmidt was also a director of the corporation and a former member of the managing board and the executive committee of the Dali Maatschappij, Amsterdam, a corporation with interests in tobacco.
        Mr. Goldschmidt came to the United States in 1940 and was instrumental in arranging for the shipment of the last available wrapper tobacco from Sumatra to the United States before the Japanese invasion of Indonesia. He retired from the Dali Maatschappij in 1968.
        A widower, he is survived by two sons, Dolf L. Goldsmith of San Antonio, Tex., and Smith E. Goldsmith of Manhattan, and six grandchildren.

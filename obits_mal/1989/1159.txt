        James F. Sweeney, a retired steel company executive, died Tuesday at his home in Titusville, Pa., after a long illness. He was 88 years old.
        Born in Clarendon, Pa., he served in the Navy in World War I. He was vice chairman of Struthers Wells Corporation, a steel forging plant, chairman of the Manion Steel Barrel Company and president of Energy Associates Inc.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Frances E. Dillon; two daughters, Katherine Brinkley of West Palm Beach, Fla., and Ann Tonetti of Snedens Landing, N.Y.; a son, John of Torrance, Calif., and three grandchildren.

        Milton Frome, a comic actor who appeared in more than 50 movies and was Milton Berle's straight man on television, died of heart failure Tuesday at the Motion Picture and Television Hospital in Woodland Hills, Calif. He was 78 years old. Mr. Frome, whose career began in vaudeville and burlesque, was a regular on the Berle show during the 1950's and played an autocratic studio boss on ''The Beverly Hillbillies'' from 1964 to 1967.
        Among his films were ''Ride 'em Cowboy!,'' ''Bye Bye Birdie'' and ''The Nutty Professor.'' His stage credits included ''Follow the Girls,'' ''The Student Prince'' and ''Top Banana.'' He also toured with Mickey Rooney and Ann Miller in ''Sugar Babies.''
        He is survived by his wife, Marjorie, a son, Michael, and a brother.

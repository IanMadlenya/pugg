        Charles A. O'Malley, former chairman of Fred S. James & Company, an insurance brokerage firm, died of leukemia on Saturday at Johns Hopkins Hospital in Baltimore. He was 71 years old and lived in Stuart, Fla.
        Mr. O'Malley, a former resident of Manhattan and Glen Cove, L.I., was a native of Brooklyn. He was a graduate of Rutgers University. He served as a first lieutenant in the Army Air Corps in World War II and entered the insurance business with Lethbridge, Owens & Phillips, becoming president of the firm in 1955.
        Eight years later he merged Lethbridge with the James firm and become chairman of James, which was acquired by the Transamerica Corporation in 1982. Mr. O'Malley retired in 1985 but remained a director of Transamerica until last year.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Nancy Bertelsen; two daughters, Brooke Booker of Flora Vista, N.M., and Barbara Morrison of Nashville; three sons, Charles 3d, of Avon, Conn., Arthur, of Los Angeles, and Michael, of Boston; two sisters, Edna, of Easton, Md., and Florence McCreery of Brooklyn; a brother, Joseph, of Willows, Calif., and seven grandchildren.

        William Carl Woodward, a former executive with the Aluminum Company of America, died of respiratory arrest following surgery yesterday at Georgetown University Hospital in Washington. He was 76 years old and lived in Pittsburgh and Washington.
        Mr. Woodward was associated with Alcoa for more than 40 years. He retired in 1977 as vice president of research and development. In 1971 he was president of the National Alliance of Businessmen, a nonprofit organization created by President Lyndon B. Johnson in 1968 to find jobs for the hard-core unemployed.
        Mr. Woodward, a native of Monessen, Pa., was a graduate of Allegheny College and Harvard Business School.
        A widower, he is survived by a daughter, Cynthia Woodward of Manhattan, and a son, William Carl Jr., of Atlanta.

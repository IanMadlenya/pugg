        Raymond Jean Picard, who helped establish subsidiaries of major French chemical companies here and in Canada and Mexico, died of cancer Monday at Doctors Hospital. He was 74 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Picard, through licensing agreements, founded subsidiaries of Rhone-Poulenc S.A., a large European chemical producer. He was president of Rhodia Inc. in New York from 1945 until his retirement in 1979. Mr. Picard was also president of the French Chamber of Commerce in the United States and a director of the New York Board of Trade.
        Mr. Picard was born and educated in Paris. He became an American citizen in 1954.
        Surviving are his wife, Lucette; two sons, Patrick, of Annapolis, Md., and Bertrand, of Queens, and six granddaughters.

        Dr. Richard E. Wilson, a medical educator and researcher who specialized in cancer surgery, died of a brain tumor Tuesday at the Brigham and Women's Hospital in Boston. He was 60 years old and lived in Wellesley, Mass.
        Dr. Wilson, a 1951 graduate of the Medical Center at the State University of New York at Syracuse, was an innovator in the use of chemotherapy for breast cancer and also helped develop new methods for matching transplant donors and recipients.
        He was professor of surgery at the Harvard Medical School and was chief of cancer surgery at the Dana Farber Cancer Institute there. He was also served as a surgeon with the Army in Korea.
        He is survived by his wife, Myrna; a son Keith, of Cleveland Heights, Ohio; a daughter, Andrea, of Boston; his mother, Lillian, of Hollywood, Fla.; and a sister, Edith Kutscher, of Manhattan.

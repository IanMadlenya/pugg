        James M. Fulton, a former executive at Merck & Company in Rahway, N.J., died of a heart attack on Monday at his home in Martha's Vineyard, Mass. He was 75 years old.
        Mr. Fulton joined the pharmaceutical company in 1951 and rose to become vice president, general counsel and secretary. He retired in 1977. Before joining Merck & Company, he was in private law practice in Manhattan.
        After his retirement, Mr. Fulton became chairman of Tivoli Industries, a small manufacturing company in Santa Ana, Calif., and chairman of the finance committee of Martha's Vineyard Hospital.
        He was born in Cincinnati and graduated from Yale in 1935 and from Yale Law School in 1938. He served in the Navy from 1940 to 1945 and was discharged with the rank of commander.
        Mr. Fulton is survived by his wife, the former Anne O'Connor; two sons, Pattison, of Owings Mills, Md., and James M. Jr., of Los Angeles; three daughters, Judith Higby, of Wilton, Conn., Millicent Dunfey, of Marlborough, Mass., and Joan Villa, of Geneva; a sister, Jean George, of Santa Ana, and six grandchildren.

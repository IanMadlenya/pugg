        David A. Teichman, a former vice president of Arthur Murray Inc., died Monday at the Mount Sinai Medical Center in Manhattan. He was 90 years old and lived in Manhattan and Truro, Mass.
        Mr. Teichman joined Arthur Murray, the dance-studio company founded by his brother, in the mid-1940's and retired in 1962, when the family sold the business. From 1973 to 1987, he worked for a securities concern, the First Manhattan Company.
        He was born in Manhattan and graduated from New York University.
        He was a co-founder of the Living Arts Foundation, established to advance and exhibit work of contemporary artists, and was a former board member of the Provincetown (Mass.) Art Association.
        Besides his brother, Arthur Murray of Honolulu, he is survived by his daughter, Wendy Levine of Manhattan; another brother, Ira Murray of Miami Beach; four grandchildren, and six great-grandchildren.

        The Rev. Dr. Theodore A. Rath, retired president of Bloomfield College and a Presbyterian minister, died Friday after being hit by an automobile outside his home in Lakeland, Fla. He was 85 years old and a former resident of Sussex, N.J.
        He was president of Bloomfield from 1960 to 1969, when he retired. Previously, he had served in several churches in New York and New Jersey and was executive for the Synod of New Jersey Presbyterian Churches in the 1950's. He was born in Baltimore and graduated from Wesleyan University and Union Theological Seminary.
        Surviving are two sons, Robert, of Montague, N.J. and Donald, of Manasquan, N.J.; two daughters, Janet Garrison of Modesto, Calif. and Lois King of Plano, Tex.; three brothers, Alfred, of Lakeland, Wilbert, of Webster Groves, Mo. and Walter, of St. Albans, Vt.; two sisters, Miriam Koster of Kenosha, Wis. and Dorothy Rath of Ashville, N.C.; 15 grandchildren, and 12 great-grandchildren.
        Correction:  November 20, 1989, Monday, Late Edition - Final
        An obituary yesterday about Theodore A. Rath, a retired president of Bloomfield College and a Presbyterian minister, listed his survivors incompletely. They include his wife, Lottie Mahoney Rath.

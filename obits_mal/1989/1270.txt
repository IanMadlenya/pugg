        Elliot W. Dann, a New York real-estate developer, died of amyotrophic lateral sclerosis, commonly known as Lou Gehrig's disease, on Wednesday at his home in Manhattan. He was 57 years old.
        Mr. Dann had been head of his own Manhattan real-estate brokerage, Elliot W. Dann Company, since 1969. Earlier he had been a partner in Abrams & Dann Inc., a real-estate company, and before that a partner in a family-owned construction concern, Asher Dann and Sons, which built high-rise apartments. In the early 1980's, he was chairman of the sales brokers committee of the Real Estate Board of New York.
        Mr. Dann was born in Brooklyn and graduated from Princeton University in 1953.
        He is survived by a son, Geoffrey, and two daughters, Deborah and Jennifer, all of Manhattan; and by two brothers, Asher and Arthur, both of Manhattan.

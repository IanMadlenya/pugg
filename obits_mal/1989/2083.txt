        Dr. Richard Wesley TeLinde, a former chairman of the gynecology department at the Johns Hopkins School of Medicine and a former chief of gynecology at Johns Hopkins Hospital, died on Nov. 16 at his home in Baltimore after a long illness. He was 95 years old.
        Dr. TeLinde was named to both Johns Hopkins posts in 1939, when he became a full professor. Although he retired in 1960, he continued a practice in gynecology and urology until 1972.
        He was a former president of the American Gynecological Society and the American Gynecological Club, and the author of the textbook ''Operative Gynecology,'' which was first published in 1946. He was a graduate of the University of Wisconsin and the Johns Hopkins School of Medicine.
        Dr. TeLinde was predeceased by his wife, Catherine. He is survived by two nephews, Edwin Bayley of Carmel, Calif., and Robert Bayley of Punta Gorda, Fla., and a niece, Lois Mathews of Aukland, New Zealand.

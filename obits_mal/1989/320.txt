        Fernando Namora, one of Portugal's best-known novelists, died today. He was 69 years old.
        Mr. Namora was born in the central Portuguese town of Condeixa in 1919 and studied medicine in Lisbon. He became a doctor serving remote mountain locations, writing about his experiences in ''Mountain Doctor.'' Mr. Namora wrote more than 30 books and several volumes of poetry.
        Last year, President Mario Soares awarded Mr. Namora Portugal's highest civilian honor, the Order of Henry the Navigator.

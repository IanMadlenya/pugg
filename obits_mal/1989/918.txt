        Eugene T. Ferraro, an educator, executive and a former deputy undersecretary of the Air Force, died Sunday at the Boulevard Manor Nursing Home in Boynton Beach, Fla., after a long illness. He was 76 years old and lived in Delray Beach, Fla.
        Dr. Ferraro was a graduate of the New School for Social Research with a doctorate in government and economics. He left a teaching job in Little Falls, N.J., to join the military industry in 1943.
        He served as deputy undersecretary for manpower with the Air Force during the Johnson Administration. When he retired in 1978, he was a vice president with the Singer Company.
        He is survived by his wife, Victoria; two sons, Philip, of Kingston, Nova Scotia, and Michael, of Alexandria, Va.; a daughter, Eugenia Lewis of McLean, Va., and five grandchildren.

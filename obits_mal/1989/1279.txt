        Frank David Campion, a former executive with the American Medical Association, died of cancer of the larynx Thursday at his home in Winnetka, Ill. He was 67 years old. Mr. Campion, a 1943 alumnus of Yale University, wrote for Life magazine, serving as bureau chief in Chicago and Los Angeles, before joining Young & Rubicam Inc., the advertising agency, as a writer and executive.
        In 1970 he became director of communications for the American Medical Association and in 1985 he became director of the association's consumer book program. He retired in 1988.
        He is survived by his wife, Georgene, two daughters, Kate, of Winnetka, and Barry Evans, of Venice, Calif; two sons, Geoffrey, of Winnetka, and Frank Jr., of Winston-Salem, N.C., and a brother, Thomas, of Hanover, N.H.

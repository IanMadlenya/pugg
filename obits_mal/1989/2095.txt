        Clyde F. Kohn, a geographer and author, died of multiple myeloma on Wednesday at Holy Cross Hospital in Fort Lauderdale, Fla., where he lived in retirement. He was 78 years old.
        Dr. Kohn was chairman of the geography department at the University of Iowa when he retired in 1984. He had previously taught at Harvard, Mississippi State College for Women and Northwestern University.
        Dr. Kohn was a graduate of Northern Michigan University, where he earned a master's degree, and he earned a a doctorate at the University of Michigan. He served with the Office of Naval Research and was a consultant to the State Department.
        He was a past president of the Association of American Geographers and of the National Council for Geogrphic Education, and was the author of 25 books.
        Dr. Kohn is survived by his wife, the former Doris Merker; a daughter, Susan Eddins of Glen Ellyn, Ill.; a son, the Rev. George F. Kohn of Salinas, Calif; a brother, Russel, of Mandan, N.D.; three sisters, Laura Luhr of Sun City, Ariz., Gertrude Kohn of Chapel Hill, N.C., and Patricia Malenfant of Thunder Bay, Ontario, and four grandchildren.

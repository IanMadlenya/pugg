        Edward E. Benedict, former chairman of Norda Inc., a privately owned flavor and fragrance company until its acquisition by Unilever United States in 1985, died Tuesday at Harkness Pavilion, Columbia Presbyterian Hospital, after a brief illness. He was 78 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Benedict, a native of Boston, spent his entire career with Norda. He became chairman in 1971, a post he retained until he retired in 1980.
        Mr. Benedict graduated from New York University in 1939. He was bred and raced throughbreds and was founding president of the New York Thoroughbred Breeders' Association.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Elena Duke; six daughters, Elise Brown and Patricia Benedict, both of Greenwich, Conn., Diane, of Elbert, Colo., Elena Benedict-Smith of Longboat Key, Fla., Celeste Pinelli of Manhattan and Verna Neilson of Kinderhook, N.Y.; two sisters, Ermeline Polastri of Montclair, N.J., and Vera Bell of Clifton, N.J., and 14 grandchildren.

        Dr. Oscar J. Falnes, an educator and specialist in Scandinavian intellectual and cultural history, died on Friday at his home in Manhattan. He was 91 years old.
        Dr. Falnes was assistant chairman of the history department at New York University from 1957 to 1960. He retired in 1966. In World War II, he was chief of the Scandinavian-Baltic unit of the American intelligence agency that became the Office of Strategic Services.
        Dr. Falnes earned his Ph.D. at Columbia University in 1933. He joined the N.Y.U. faculty in 1927, after having taught at Stanford University and Bethany College.
        Surviving are his wife, Augusta, and a sister, Evelyn Mathieson, of Milwaukie, Ore.

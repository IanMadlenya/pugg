        Charles I. Rostov, retired president and chief executive officer of the Trans-Ocean Import Company, a home furnishings importer in Manhattan, died of a heart attack Friday in Budapest, where he was traveling. He was 74 years old and lived in Greenwich, Conn.
        He joined the company in 1946 and retired in 1986. He had been involved in trading with China since 1972 and was former chairman of the American Importers Association's China Trade Committee. He was a former vice chairman of the National Council for United States-China Trade.
        Mr. Rostov, who was born in Baltimore, graduated from Johns Hopkins University. He was on the advisory council of the Johns Hopkins Nanjing Center for International Studies. He was a former director of the American Association of Exporters and Importers and a former president of the Mental Health Association of Westchester County.
        He is survived by his wife, Dorothy; two daughters, Jan Kutler of Kentfield, Calif., and Terry, also of Kentfield; a son, Gene, of New York City; a brother, Louis, of Norfolk, Va., and one grandson.

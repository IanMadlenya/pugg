        Lawrence Goossen, a co-producer of plays and a company manager, died of AIDS on Monday at his home in Greenwich Village. He was 46 years old.
        Mr. Goossen was a co-producer of Off Broadway and Broadway productions, including the first David Mamet play presented in New York, ''Sexual Perversity in Chicago''; ''Real Estate,'' by Louise Page, and a play by the actress Shelley Winters, ''One Night Stands of a Noisy Passenger.''
        He was company manager for Broadway productions of ''Bent,'' ''Sugar Babies'' and a revival of ''Hello, Dolly!'' and general manager for such shows as ''Aren't We All,'' ''Blithe Spirit,'' ''Strange Interlude'' and ''Eastern Standard.''
        Surviving are his mother, Ruth Singer of Tuscaloosa, Ala.; his father, Frederick, of Marco Island, Fla., and a brother, Richard, of Littleton, Colo.

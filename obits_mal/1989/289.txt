        Robert Crimmins, chairman of the Thomas Crimmins Contracting Company, died of cancer on Wednesday at his home in Darien, Conn. He was 73 years old.
        Mr. Crimmins's family founded the contracting company in 1848. It specializes in excavation work and has dug foundations for several skyscrapers in New York City.
        He was born in Manhattan and was a graduate of Harvard. He also held a civil engineering degree from Columbia. He joined the family business in the mid-1930's and returned to it after service in the Army during World War II.
        Mr. Crimmins is survived by his wife, the former Violet Rothwell; three daughters, Sally Thorne of Des Moines, Anne, of Arvada, Colo., and Susan, of Waterbury, Conn.; a sister, Sheila Parsons of Darien, and four grandchildren.

        Paul W. Bradford, a retired executive of American Express, died on Friday at his home in Ponte Vedra Beach, Fla., after a brief illness. He was 89 years old.
        Mr. Bradford joined American Express in 1921 as a management trainee. A short time later, he was posted to Yokohama, Japan, and over the next 20 years worked for the company in Asia.
        After the war, he oversaw the rebuilding of American Express offices in the United Kingdom and Ireland. In 1950, Mr. Bradford was transferred to the New York headquarters, and from 1955 until his retirement in 1961, he was a member of the American Express board.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Elizabeth Lambert; four daughters, Pamela Gaffney of Atlanta, Patricia Ockene of Akron, Alicia Bregman and Marcia Bradford, both of Delhi, N.Y., and a stepdaughter, Florrie Berggren of Stockholm, Sweden.

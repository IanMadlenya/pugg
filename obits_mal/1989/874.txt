        James M. Campbell, a lawyer for the New York City Commission on Human Rights, died of complications from AIDS Monday at his home in Brooklyn. He was 37 years old.  Mr. Campbell joined the commission in 1986 in the law-enforcement bureau and helped to redraft the Human Rights Law and rules of practice. He worked on the AIDS panels of the State Bar Association, the National Lawyers Guild and the Greater New York Hospital Association.
        He was born in Denver. Mr. Campbell graduated from Dartmouth College and the University of Oregon Law School.
        Surviving are his parents, Margaret and David 3d, and three brothers, David 4th, Craig and Bruce, all of Denver.

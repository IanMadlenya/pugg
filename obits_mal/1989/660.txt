        William Peter Howard, a film reproduction executive, died of a heart attack Friday at Sand Lake Hospital in Orlando, Fla. He was 61 years old and lived in New Vernon, N.J.
        Mr. Howard, who was a graduate of St. Paul's School in Concord, N.H., and Yale University, was president and owner of Image Services Inc., a film and tape reproduction company in Manhattan. He had also been president of Cineffect's, the laboratory division of National Showmanship, and a vice president of the T.V.C. Laboratories. He was involved with amateur hockey and was a founder of the Winter Club Hockey League, an amateur men's hockey organization.
        He is survived by his wife, Kim; a son, William Jr., of Orlando; a daughter, Kristi Miller of Far Hills, N.J., and two grandchildren.

        Dr. G. Alx Galvin, a doctor who was the first black to become president of the New York State Academy of General Practice, died April 16 while visiting a sister in Baltimore. He was 83 years old and lived in Ithaca, N.Y.
        Dr. Galvin served as president of the academy, now known as the Academy of Family Physicians, in 1958 and 1959.
        Dr. Galvin, a native of Newport News, Va., was a graduate of the Howard University Pharmaceutical School. He received his medical degree from Meharry Medical College. In 1937 he became the first black doctor to establish a practice in Ithaca.
        Dr. Galvin's wife, the former Corinne Brown, died last August. Dr. Galvin is survived by a daughter, Jane Lewis of Brooklyn, and two sisters, Susie Mansfield of Franklin, Va., and Elizabeth Patterson of Baltimore.

        A. William Salomone, professor emeritus of history at the University of Rochester, died of cancer Tuesday at Lankenau Hospital in Philadelphia. He was 73 years old and lived in Ardmore, Pa.
        Born in Guardiagrele, Italy, he came to the United States 60 years ago. He graduated from La Salle College and received master's and doctoral degrees from the University of Pennsylvania.
        A specialist in modern Italian history, Professor Salomone taught at New York University for 15 years before going to Rochester, where he taught for 20 years. He retired in 1982.
        He was the author of several books, including ''Italian Democracy in the Making,'' published in 1960 by the University of Pennsylvania Press.
        Surviving are his wife, Lina; a daughter, Ilia Salomone Smith of San Francisco; three brothers, James, Albert and Italo, all of Philadelphia; two sisters, Clementina Garrity and Sera Fascella, both of Philadelphia, and two grandchildren.

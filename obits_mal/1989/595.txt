        Edward H. Mahla, a lawyer specializing in maritime law, died of cancer Wednesday at Calvary Hospital. He was 74 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Mahla was a senior partner with the law firm of Haight, Gardner, Poor & Havens in Manhattan. He was counsel to banks and insurers and was involved, after World War II, with fleets controlled by shipowners like Stavros Niarchos and Aristotle Onassis.
        Mr. Mahla joined the firm after graduating from the Columbia University Law School in 1939, and he retired in 1986. Mr. Mahla served in the Navy in World War II.
        Surviving are his wife, Betty; two daughters, Leicia Marlow and Holly Askling, both of Manhattan; a brother, Elbert, of Seabrook Island, S.C., and two grandchildren.

        William H. Bambrick, an advertising executive, died of kidney failure Tuesday at Lenox Hill Hospital in Manhattan. He was 68 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        From 1973 to 1986, when he retired, Mr. Bambrick was a senior vice president at Rumrill Hoyt, where he supervised advertising campaigns for products like Actifed, Sudafed and Bacardi rum. He began his advertising career in 1947 and worked for several agencies.
        Mr. Bambrick was born in Weirton, W. Va., graduated from West Virginia University and had a master's degree from Columbia University.
        Surviving are his brother, Lewis, of Trenton, and a nephew, William, of Mountain View, Calif.

        Abdullah Jassim al-Shaker, Bahrain's United Nations representative and its Consul General in New York, died of heart failure yesterday at his home in the Elmhurst, Queens. He was 60 years old.
        Mr. Shaker had been Consul General in New York since 1971, when the emirate won independence from Britain.
        Born in Muharraq, Bahrain, Mr. Shaker had degrees from the University of Cairo and the University of Karachi. At the time of his death, he was president of the Society of Foreign Consuls.
        Surviving are his wife, Jeanne; a daughter, Pamela, of Woodside, Queens; two sisters, Amina of Muharraq, and Fatima Kanoo of Manama, Bahrain, and a brother, Mohammed, of Manama.

        Dr. Frank W. Foote Jr., an educator and pioneer in tumor pathology and classification, died of pneumonia Saturday at Sarasota (Fla.) Memorial Hospital. He was 78 years old and had moved to Sarasota from Manhattan in 1972.
        Dr. Foote, a graduate of the University of Virginia Medical School in 1935, was chairman of the pathology department at the Memorial Sloan-Kettering Cancer Center from 1959 to 1972. He joined the department in 1939, after teaching at the University of Virginia. He developed the diagnostic criteria for cancerous tumors of the breast, salivary glands and thyroid glands.
        He is survived by two daughters, Emilie Thomson of Surrey, England, and Carolin Colby of Richmond, Va.; a son, Francis, of Arlington, Va., and five grandchildren.

        Charles W. Dow, a former president of the Equitable Life Insurance Society of the United States and a former chairman of the finance committee of the CIT Financial Corporation, died of pneumonia Tuesday at the Hilaire Farm Nursing Home in Huntington, L.I., where he had been a patient for several years. He was 82 years old and lived in Northport, L.I.
        Mr. Dow was born in Marion, Iowa, and graduated from Iowa State University. He joined Equitable in 1935 and rose to the presidency in 1956. He resigned the following year and took the CIT post, which he held until he retired in 1971.
        Mr. Dow is survived by his wife, the former Katherine Ryley; two sons, Michael, of Huntington and John, of Thousand Oaks, Calif.; two daughters, Melinda Hyatt of Nashville and Susan Clark of Titusville, Fla.; a sister, Jean Creamer of South Bend, Ind.; a brother, James, of North Bend, Ore.; eight grandchildren and one great-grandchild.

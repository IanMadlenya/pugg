        Harry J. Donnelly a retired acting justice of the New York Supreme Court and a former New York City Highway Commissioner, died of cancer yesterday at St. Vincent's Hospital in Manhattan. He was 66 years old and lived in Breezy Point, Queens.
        Mr. Donnelly was appointed by Mayor John V. Lindsay to the New York City Criminal Court in 1968 and served there until being named an acting Supreme Court justice in 1980. He retired from the bench in 1987.
        Mr. Donnelly was born in Brooklyn and was a graduate of St. John's College and St. John's University School of Law. He served in the Coast Guard during World War II.
        A Republican, he was appointed an assistant state Attorney General in 1951. Five years later he was elected to the Assembly. He served two terms.
        In 1960 Mr. Donnelly was named legislative counsel to Lieut. Gov. Malcolm Wilson. Three years later he was elected Councilman-at-Large from Brooklyn. He resigned the Council seat in 1966 to become Highway Commissioner in the Lindsay administration, a post he held until his appointment to the Criminal Court bench.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Mildred Squires; three daughters, Barbara Dehler, of Garden City, L.I., Bonnie Fenton, of Ridgefield, Conn., and Denise McGuire, of Fairfax Station, Va.; two sons, Robert and Richard, both of Queens, and eight grandchildren.

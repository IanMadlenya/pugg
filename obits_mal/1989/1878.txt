        Edward O. Johnson, a scientist and inventor with RCA for 34 years, died of cancer on Oct. 11 at his home in Princeton, N.J. He was 69 years old.
        Mr. Johnson, an alumnus of Pratt Institute, joined RCA Princeton Laboratories shortly after graduation in 1948. From 1959 to 1972, he was manager of the RCA Semiconductor and Tube Divisions and was director of RCA's Research Laboratories in Tokyo from 1975 to 1982.
        He remained as a director after the laboratories were bought by Corning Glass Works in 1982. He retired in 1984.
        He held patents on about 30 inventions and was the author of about 35 technical papers.
        He is survived by his wife, Margaret, and two daughters, Lonni Sue, of Manhattan, and Aline, of Princeton.

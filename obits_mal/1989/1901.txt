        Arthur J. Keeffe, a law professor and columnist on legal matters, died of congestive heart failure Monday while recuperating from surgery at Georgetown University Hospital in Washington. He was 86 years old and lived in Washington.
        From 1952 through 1983 he wrote a monthly columnn reviewing current legal literature in The American Bar Association Journal. He also wrote widely in law reviews on subjects like Federal procedure and constitutional law.
        Mr. Keeffe, a native New Yorker, received bachelor's and law degrees from Cornell University. After a dozen years with the New York firm of Milbank, Tweed, Hope & Hadley, he returned to Cornell as a law professor.
        At the end of World War II he headed a board formed by the Navy to review court-martial sentences and to recommend changes in military justice.
        In 1955 he was named to a professorship at the Columbus School of Law at Catholic University, where he taught until 1972.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Mary Catherine Daly; two sons, John, of Eastchester, N.Y., and Stephen, of Washington; a daughter, Mary Ellen Sawyer of Arlington, Va.; nine grandchildren and two great-grandchildren.

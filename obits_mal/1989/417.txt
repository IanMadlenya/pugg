        Jerome A. Koch, a former president of the Karl Koch Erecting Company, which built the World Trade Center and Giants Stadium, died of cancer Sunday at his home in North Palm Beach, Fla. He was 68 years old and also lived in Englewood, N.J.
        Mr. Koch was born in Jamaica, Queens, and graduated from Manhattan College. He joined his family's construction company in Carteret, N.J., in 1945 and was its president from 1975 to 1983. At his death Mr. Koch was chairman of the Action Bolt and Tool Company, in Lake Park, Fla.
        Surviving are his wife, Mary Lynn; four daughters, Marianne Bartlett of East Lyme, Conn; Karolyn Hutto of Thiells, N.Y., Juliann Cook of North Palm Beach, Fla., and Kathryn O'Brien of Hartford; a son, Jerome Jr., of Fair Haven, N.J.; two brothers, Robert, of North Palm Beach, and Daniel, of Maple, Fla.; five sisters, Julia Chisholm of North Palm Beach; Gladys Brown of Queens, Delores Palmer of Port St. Lucie, Fla., Cornelia Landers of Port St. Lucie and Patricia Bracken of Scarsdale, N.Y., and 11 grandchildren;

        Winslow Shelby Coates, a Long Island lawyer for six decades who specialized in conservation and zoning legislation, died in his sleep Wednesday at his home in Bayville. He was 97 years old.
        In the 20's, Mr. Coates wrote legislation to preserve open space on the North Shore of Long Island. He started his own practice in Locust Valley in 1923 and was an organizer of the Matinecock Bank, then the only one in the community and now a branch of Norstar Bank.
        Mr. Coates, a native of East Orange, N.J., was a graduate of Phillips-Andover, Yale and the New York Law School. He served in the Navy in World War I. His wife of 64 years, the former Jane Brush, died in 1987.
        He is survived by three daughters, Jane Halaby and Virginia Chafee, both of McLean, Va., and Elise Alsop of Lloyd Harbor, L.I.; a son, W. Shelby Jr., of Locust Valley, L.I.; two sisters, Mary Grace Miller-Blood and Jane Driggs, both of Lansdale, Pa.; 18 grandchildren, and 11 great-grandchildren.

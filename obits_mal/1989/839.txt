        Alan V. MacDonald, an F.B.I. agent who specialized in investigating the sexual abuse of children, died of an aortic aneurism May 8 while visiting relatives in Inverness, Scotland. He was 44 years old and lived in Swampscott, Mass.
        Mr. MacDonald was a special agent in the bureau's Manhattan office from 1975 until 1987, when he transferred to the Boston office. In New York, he helped break up several rings of pedophiles while working on a task force with the New York City Police Department.
        Mr. MacDonald, a native of Swampscott, received bachelor's degrees from St. John's Seminary and Salem State College. He earned a law degree at Boston College, a master's degree in criminal law at New York University and a master's in sociology at Fordham University.
        Surviving are a brother, Richard B., and his mother, Eva, both of Swampscott.

        Raymond Woodrow, a research scientist and former director of the office of research administration at Princeton University, died of a chronic obstructive lung disease Wednesday at the Medical Center at Princeton, N.J. He was 76 years old and lived in Princeton.
        He was the university research board assistant for special studies at his retirement in the mid-1980's.
        Mr. Woodrow, a graduate of Williams College and the Massachusetts Institute of Technology, was with the Federal Office of Scientifc Research and Development in World War II. He was also associated with the M.I.T. Radiation Laboratory, where he worked on the airborne early-warning project.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Isabelle Noble; three sons from a former marriage, Peter J., of Amsterdam, Richard H. B., of Menlo Park, Calif., and William K., of Portola Valley, Calif.; a daughter, Judith Johnson of Northridge, Calif.; a stepson, David H. Rhodes of Monroe, Conn.; 12 grandchildren, and two great-grandchildren.

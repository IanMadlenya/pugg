        Albert A. Lawrence, a retired Coast Guard captain who was a professor and dean for 23 years at the United States Coast Guard Academy, in New London, Conn., died Saturday at Florida Keys Memorial Hospital in Key West, Fla. He was 84 years old.
        Captain Lawrence, a graduate of Dartmouth College, taught humanities, politics and economics at the academy, where he retired in 1960 as dean of the faculty. Captain Lawrence served in the Coast Guard from 1937 to 1961. After retiring from the Coast Guard Academy he served as dean of the New York State Maritime College in the Bronx from 1960 to 1966.
        He is survived by his wife, Jean; two daughters, Mrs. Margaret Traver of Gorham, Me., and Lenorda Chambers of Cayucos, Calif.; a son, Albert Jr. of Gorham; two brothers, Morgan, of Tulsa, Okla., and Philip, of Tucson, Ariz., 15 grandchildren and 11 great-grandchildren.

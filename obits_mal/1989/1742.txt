        Charles H. Lee Jr., a lawyer and an investment banker, died of cancer yesterday at his home in Washington. He was 62 years old.
        A longtime resident of New York City, Mr. Lee worked at the Manhattan law firm of Dewey, Ballantine, Bushby, Palmer & Wood before becoming an investment banker with the First Boston Corporation in 1967. He worked at the Bank of New York from 1980 to 1983, when he became a consultant in Washington.
        Surviving are two sons, Peter and Matthew, and a daughter, Kathryn all of Washington.

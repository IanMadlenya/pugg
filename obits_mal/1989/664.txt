        Henry C. Howells, who was active in the popularization of French wines in the United States, died on Monday at his home in Palm Beach, Fla. He was 88 years old and also lived in Stonington, Conn.
        In 1946 Mr. Howells, then a Washington resident, joined with the French Ambassador at the time, Henri Bonnet, to establish the Washington Sous-Commanderie of the Burgundian Wine Society, which he headed for 24 years.
        Mr. Howells was a native New Yorker and a graduate of Princeton.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Alice Post; a son, Henry 6th of Lorton, Va.; a daughter, Sallie Mitchell of Beauford, S.C., and five grandchildren.

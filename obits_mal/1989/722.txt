        Stanley Laurie Sloane, an investor and philanthropist who held a number of leadership positions in the United Jewish Appeal, died of cancer Tuesday night at his home in Manhattan. He was 65 years old.
        Mr. Sloane, who began his career in retailing, later specialized in franchising, mostly in the fast-food business. He was among the first to operate McDonald's restaurants in the New York City area.
        He was an honorary vice chairman of the national United Jewish Appeal, past treasurer of its board of trustees, a member of the board of governors of the Jewish Agency, which oversees the absorption of refugees in Israel, and a member of the executive committee of Ben Gurion University in Israel. He was also active in the United Jewish Appeal of New York.
        Mr. Sloane was born in Philadelphia and was a graduate of the Wharton School of Finance of the University of Pennsylvania. He served in the Army in World War II.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Harriet Goldstein; a son, Robert, of Manhattan; a daughter, Ann, of Rockville, Md., and a brother, Charles, of Philadelphia.

        John C. Giordano, a lawyer and retired Superior Court judge in Freehold, N.J., died Wednesday at the Monmouth Medical Center in Long Branch, N.J. He was 93 years old and lived in Long Branch.
        Judge Giordano, a 1921 graduate of New York University Law School, was a Superior Court judge from 1948 to 1961, when he retired and became associated with his son's law firm, Giordano, Halloran & Ciesla. Previously, he had served on the lower courts in Monmouth and Ocean Counties and as an assistant Monmouth County prosecutor.
        He is survived by his wife, Ruth; a son, John, of Middletown, N.J.; two daughters, Gloria Henneberry of Tinton Falls, N.J., and Joy Conhagen of Red Bank, N.J.; a brother, Edward, of Ocean Township, N.J.; a sister, Esther Hurley of Boca Raton, Fla.; eight grandchildren, and eight great-grandchildren.

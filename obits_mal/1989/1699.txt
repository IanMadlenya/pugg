        David Knox Rowe, a retired management consultant, died of heart failure yesterday at New York Hospital in Manhattan. He was 65 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Among Mr. Rowe's clients were General Dynamics and the Southland Corporation. He had also been a professor at Towson State College in Maryland and a reporter for Radio Liberty, now Radio Free Europe.
        Mr. Rowe was the author of ''Industrial Relations Management for Profit and Growth,'' a book published by the American Management Association in 1971.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Susan Bell; three children from a former marriage, Elizabeth Gilbert, of Palo Alto, Calif., Frederick, of Falls Church, Va., and Alexander, of Manhattan; his mother, Florence, and a brother, William, both of San Francisco.

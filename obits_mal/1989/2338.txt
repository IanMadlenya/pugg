        Eugene Newton Greenblatt, a pharmacologist who specialized in anti-depressant drugs, died of heart disease Saturday at Good Samaritan Hospital in Suffern, N.Y. He was 65 years old and lived in Chestnut Ridge, N.Y.
        Dr. Greenblatt, who earned his bachelor's degree from Long Island University in 1952 and a Ph.D. in pharmacology from the University of Connecticut in 1957, was head of ENG Scientific Consultants at the time of his death. He had been with Lederle Laboratories of the American Cyanamid Company for 28 years. He wrote many scientific articles and held six patents for anti-depressants.
        Dr. Greenblatt was also the chairman of the Ramapo Zoning Board of Appeals.
        He is survived by his wife, Barbara; three daughters, Jill Pearlman of New Windsor, N.Y., Kathi Honor of Coconut Creek, Fla., and Lyn Greenblatt of Chestnut Ridge; a sister, Elaine Rivkind of Woodbury, L.I.; and one grandson.

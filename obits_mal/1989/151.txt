        Dr. John B. Newman, a retired professor of speech at Queens College and an expert in semantics, died of heart failure Monday at his home in Whitestone, Queens. He was 71 years old.
        Dr. Newman, who earned a Ph.D. in education at New York University in 1950, joined the faculty of Queens College in 1946. He retired in 1982 as a professor in the communications, arts and sciences department. He returned and taught voice in the college's drama department until 1986.
        He is survived by a daughter, Jane Albert of Great Neck, L.I.; a son, John, of Manhattan, and two grandchildren.

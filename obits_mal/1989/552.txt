        James W. Gaynor, a former New York State Commissioner of Housing and Urban Renewal, died of congestive heart failure Thursday at the Palm Beach Gardens (Fla.) Medical Center. He was 79 years old and lived in Tecuesta, Fla.
        Mr. Gaynor was appointed to the state housing post in 1959 by Gov. Nelson A. Rockefeller and served for 10 years.
        At his death Mr. Gaynor was chairman of HMG Courtland Properties, a Flordia real-estate concern traded on the American Stock Exchange.
        Mr. Gaynor was born in Rensselaer, N.Y. and attended the Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute, where he received a masters degree in business administration and a bachelor of science degree in management engineering.
        After graduation, he served in many state, Federal and New York City government positions.
        From 1936 to 1940, he was state director of the Works Progress Administration, then served as a lieutenant colonel in the Army during World War II.
        After the war he joined the New York City Housing Authority as director of management and became its executive director in 1950.
        Soon after the Korean war began, in 1950, he was called back to military service.
        After leaving the state housing post, he continued to serve in government posts, including as a trustee for the Mental Hygiene Facilities Improvement Fund, an agency that oversaw construction of mental hospitals and schools for the retarded.
        Mr. Gaynor was the husband of the late Marion Patterson. Survivors include his children James, Magdalen and Anne and a sister, Anne G. Lane.

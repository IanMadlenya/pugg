        Irving Newman, a retired hat manufacturer who served as a neighborhood aide to three New York City mayors, died of a heart attack Thursday at Edward White Hospital in St. Petersburg, Fla. He was 74 years old and lived in Tampa, Fla.
        Mr. Newman was the head of Emme, a Manhattan hat manufacturer for several years before he retired in 1979. He had also been head of Rovita, another hat manufacturing company.
        A Queens resident for many years, Mr. Newman became the first mayor of the ''Little City Hall'' in Queens, which was established by Mayor John V. Lindsay. He went on to act as a liaison to tenant groups and organizations for the elderly for Mayor Abraham D. Beame and Mayor Edward I. Koch.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Beatrice Britt; two children from a former marriage, a son, Steven, of Queens, and a daughter, Robin Stone of Raleigh, N.C.; a stepson, Frederick Britt of Tampa, and eight grandchildren.
        Correction:  October 17, 1989, Tuesday, Late Edition - Final
        An obituary on Oct. 7 about Irving Newman, a former neighborhood aide to three New York City mayors, misstated one aspect of his career.
        He was an employee of Emme, a hat manufacturer; he was not its head.

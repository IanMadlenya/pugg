        Julian Miller, a retired actuary who became an environmentalist, died of cancer Thursday at Holy Name Hospital in Teaneck, N.J. He was 85 years old and lived in Teaneck.
        Mr. Miller, a native of Coatsville, Pa., was an actuary with the New York Life Insurance Company in Manhattan for nearly 35 years. He retired in 1966 and became active as a hiker and an environmentalist.
        He was secretary for the New York-New Jersey Trail Conference, an umbrella organization for hiking groups, and a founder of the New Jersey environmental lobby. He was conservation chairman for the New York-North Jersey chapter of the Appalachian Mountain Club and was a member of the Palisades Interstate Park Commission Advisory Board.
        There are no immediate survivors.

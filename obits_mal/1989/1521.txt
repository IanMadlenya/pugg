        Walter A. Davidson, who served as cantor of Temple Beth Emeth in the Flatbush section of Brooklyn for half a century, died of multiple myeloma Sunday at Maimonides Hospital. He was 86 years old and lived in Brooklyn.
        Mr. Davidson was a founder of the Board of American Hazzan Ministers, the American Conference of Cantors and the School of Sacred Music at Hebrew Union College-Jewish Institute of Religion.
        Mr. Davidson, who was born in Prashka, a town on the Polish-German border, came to the United States as a teen-ager after World War I. He joined Temple Beth Emeth in 1928 and formally retired in 1978 but continued his cantorial work until his final illness.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Sophie Rubin.

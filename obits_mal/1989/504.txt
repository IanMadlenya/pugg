        Anthony Riolo, an administrator for several arts organizations including the Joffrey Ballet and the New York City Ballet, died of complications from AIDS yesterday at the University of New Mexico Hospital in Albuquerque. He was 46 years old. Mr. Riolo, who was also a lawyer, was the executive director of the Santa Fe Opera at the time of his death. From 1977 to 1979, he was the director of finance and development for the Joffrey Ballet. He was the director of development for the New York City Ballet from 1979 to 1984, when he joined the Sante Fe Opera. Mr. Riolo was the resident counsel at the Ford Foundation from 1973 to 1977, and associate counsel at the law firm of Debevoise, Plimpton, Lyons & Gates from 1968 to 1973. He was also a consultant to the National Arts Stabilization Fund.
        He is survived by his father, Anthony P. Riolo Sr., and a sister, Julie, both of Los Angeles.

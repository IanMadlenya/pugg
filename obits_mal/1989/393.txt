        Dolph C. Simons, chairman of the board of The Journal-World, a daily newspaper in Lawrence, Kan., and of the World Company, its parent company, died in his sleep yesterday morning at his winter home in Scottsdale, Ariz. He was 84 years old and lived in Lawrence.
        Mr. Simons, whose father, W. C. Simons, founded the paper in 1891, was publisher from 1944 to 1962. He was editor from 1950 to 1979. He had been a director of The Associated Press.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Marie Nelson; two sons, Dolph C. Simons Jr., editor and publisher of The Journal-World, and Dr. John Simons of Scottsdale, Ariz.; two sisters, Dorothea Johnson of Wymore, Neb., and Blanche Maloney of Tulsa, Okla.; eight grandchildren and two great grandchildren.

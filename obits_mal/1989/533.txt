        Dr. Morris Orgel, a retired dentist, died of cancer Monday at his home in Plantation, Fla. He was 78 years old.
        Dr. Orgel was born in Brooklyn and graduated from the University of Pennsylvania and its School of Dental Medicine.
        He practiced in Ridgewood, Queens, from 1936 to 1973 when he retired. He was the dentist for the Brooklyn Dodgers from 1948 to 1955.
        Dr. Orgel was a member of the board of the Dental Society of the State of New York from 1957 to 1973. He was a former president of the second district Dental Society of the State of New York and a former member of the American College of Dentistry.
        Surviving are his wife, Shirley; a son, Henry, of Croton-on-Hudson, N.Y.; a daughter, Lynne, of Davie, Fla.; a brother, Samuel, of Queens, and a granddaughter.

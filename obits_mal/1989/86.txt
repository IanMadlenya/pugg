        Chauncey Devereux Stillman, an investor and philanthropist, died Tuesday at his apartment in Manhattan. He was 81 years old.
        Mr. Stillman was a trustee of the New York Zoological Society for almost 30 years and was a director of the National Audubon Society and the New York Botanical Garden. He was also an annual patron of the Animal Kingdom Fund.
        In the 1960's, Mr. Stillman was commodore of the New York Yacht Club and recently founded the Wethersfield Institute, a Manhattan group that sponsors religious seminars.
        He was an alumnus of Harvard College and the Columbia University School of Architecture. In World War II, Mr. Stillman was a squadron air combat intelligence officer and served on the Enterprise and Lexington.
        Surviving are two daughters, Elizabeth Shafer of Manhattan and Theodora Budnik of Poughkeepsie, N.Y., and five grandchildren.

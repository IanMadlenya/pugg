        Arthur C. Austin, a former conductor and radio producer, died of cancer Tuesday in Roosevelt Hospital. He was 77 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Austin, retired director of special events for the National Council of Churches, led music ensembles in New York City during the 1930's under the auspices of the Works Project Administration. He also directed the Princeton University Band.
        Working with NBC, he produced radio programs featuring conductors like Serge Koussevitzky, Fritz Reiner, Leopold Stokowski, Arturo Toscanini and Arthur Fiedler. He also edited ''The Family Book of Hymns,'' published in 1950 by Funk & Wagnalls.
        He is survived by his wife, Charlotta; a brother, Carrol, of Grover City, Calif.; a sister, Merlene Paul, of Dallas, two nieces and a nephew.

        Dr. Pier F. Paci, a pathologist at Brigham and Women's Hospital in Boston who taught at Harvard Medical School, died of pancreatic cancer on Feb. 15 at his home in Brookline, Mass. He was 58 years old.  Dr. Paci, who was born in Assisi, Italy, and received his medical degree from the University of Florence, specialized in the diagnosis of breast cancer and other tumors. In 1964, after six years of residency at Boston hospitals, he returned to Italy to teach at Bari University and at the Regina Elena Cancer Institute.
        In 1968 Dr. Paci returned to Boston and served at Beth Israel and Cambridge hospitals before joining the staff of Brigham and Women's Hospital in 1974.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Mary Revelle; two sons, Christopher and Stefano Paci, both of Manhattan; a daughter, Myra, also of Manhattan; his parents, Dr. Cleante Paci and Jole Paci of Assisi, and three sisters, Adriana Comparato of Perugia, Italy, Emma Paci of Assisi and Giuseppina Liakakos of Athens.

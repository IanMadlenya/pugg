        Charles Ernest Goshen, psychiatrist and professor emeritus of Vanderbilt University, in Nashville, died of a heart attack Aug. 29 at his home in Glenwood, Md. He was 72 years old.
        Dr. Goshen was director of the American Pyschiatric Association's General Practitioner Education Project and taught at George Washington University Medical School and the Schools of Engineering and Medicine at Vanderbilt. He retired in 1981.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Patricia McGuire; a son, Charles Robert of Glenelg, Md.; a daughter, Anne Goshen of Leucadia, Calif.; a sister, Vivian Marsden of Tempe, Ariz., and three grandchildren.

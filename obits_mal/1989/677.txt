        Randolph Mauldin, conductor and music administrator for the Washington (D.C.) Opera and a conductor of Broadway musicals, died Sunday at his home in Falls Church, Va. He was 37 years old.
        Mr. Mauldin died of acute kidney failure, said Suzanne Stephens, the public-relations manager of the Washington Opera.
        Since 1985, Mr. Mauldin was the opera's principal coach and conductor in residence. He held similar positions with the New York Lyric Opera, Los Angeles Light Opera and Michigan Opera Theater. Three years ago, he conducted the Australian premiere of ''Porgy and Bess'' at the Festival of Three Worlds in Melbourne. In 1983, he was the conductor for the Broadway revival of ''Zorba the Greek,'' with Anthony Quinn. He also conducted touring companies of ''Sweeney Todd'' and ''Evita.''
        He is survived by his parents, Ruth and George Mauldin, of Jekyll Island, Ga., and his sister, L. Cedelia Gregorsky, of Atlanta.

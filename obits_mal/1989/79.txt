        Dr. Arthur Ralph Gold, a professor of English at Wellesley College in Massachusetts, died of cancer Saturday at Cambridge Hospital. He was 53 years old and lived in Cambridge, Mass.
        Dr. Gold, the William R. Kenan Professor of English at Wellesley, had taught at the college for 26 years. He received his doctorate at Harvard Unviersity.
        He is survived by his wife, Marie-Elene, and a daughter, Anna, both of Cambridge; his mother, Bernice Gold, of East Orange, N.J.; and two sisters, Barbara Zingman, of Louisville, Ky., and Betty Rosen, of Livingston, N.J.

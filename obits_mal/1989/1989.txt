        Col. C. Emil Nelson, the former national chief secretary of the Salvation Army, died of complications of heart disease Thursday at Jersey Shore Medical Center in Asbury Park, N.J. He was 83 years old and lived in Asbury Park.
        Colonel Nelson became a Salvation Army officer in 1925 and held posts including divisional commander for eastern New York and principal of the school for officers' training. In 1962, he was appointed field secretary and in 1970, was sent to the Salvation Army national office as national chief secretary, the second-highest rank in the oragnization. He retired in 1972.
        Survivors include his wife, the former Elsie C. Lock; two daughters, Alice Fahey of Portland, Maine, and Elsie Wibarg of Rochester; a son, Karl, of Huntington Station, L.I.; 10 grandchildren, and five great-grandchildren.

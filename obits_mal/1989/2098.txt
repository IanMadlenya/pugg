        Lieut. Gen. Alva R. Fitch, a survivor of the Bataan ''death march'' in the Philippines in 1942 who later became a deputy director of the Defense Intelligence Agency, died of a kidney ailment on Friday at Walter Reed Army Medical Center. He was 82 years old and lived in Williamsburg, Va.
        General Fitch, then a major, commanded artillery units in the early days of World War II. He was awarded a Distinguished Service Cross and a Silver Star. After the Allied surrender at Bataan he was taken prisoner.
        In the Korean War he was an artillery commander. He commanded the Third Armored Division in West Germany and served as chief of staff of Army intelligence before being named to the Defense Intelligence Agency post in 1964. He retired in 1966 and for the next 11 years was military editor of the Kiplinger Newsletter.
        General Fitch was a graduate of the United States Military Academy.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Carolyn Shaw; a daughter, Carolyn Weiskirch of Alexandria, Va.; a son, John, of Charlottesville, Va.; three brothers, Thomas, of North Platte, Neb., Eugene, of Montclair, Calif., and Theodore, of Fillmore, Calif.; three grandchildren and two great-grandchildren.

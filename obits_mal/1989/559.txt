        Dr. Paul Margolin, a specialist in molecular genetics and professor of biology at City College, died after a heart attack Sunday at the New York University Medical Center. He was 65 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Dr. Margolin was director of the Center of Cellular and Molecular Bases of Development at City College, a project financed by the National Institutes of Health.
        He published more than 45 scholarly articles.
        Dr. Margolin was born in New York and received a bachelor's from New York University after serving in Europe in World War II. He received a doctorate from Indiana University and did postdoctoral work at the California Institute of Technology, Edinburgh University and the Brookhaven (L.I.) National Laboratory.
        From 1958 to 1967, Dr. Margolin was on the staff of the Cold Spring Harbor Laboratories, where he worked on bacterial genes. He joined the Public Health Research Institute of the City of New York in 1967 as chairman of its genetics department. In 1985, he joined City College.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Jean Spielberg, and a sister, Vera Fiddler.

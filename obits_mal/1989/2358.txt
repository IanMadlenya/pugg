        Seymour Levine, a publisher and freelance writer, died of melanoma, a skin cancer, yesterday at his home in Manhattan. He was 66 years old.
        Mr. Levine had worked for other publishers and had his own textbook company, Olcott Forward, in White Plains in the 1970's.
        He wrote several travel books and training programs. He retired in 1987.
        He was born in Newark, graduated from Bucknell University and received a master's degree from the University of Michigan.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Joan Burns; two sons, Ned, of Providence, R.I., and Wm., of Cambridge, Mass.; a sister, Ann Berryman of Manhattan; and two grandsons.

        Anthony J. Posillipo, the former supervisor of the Town of Rye in Westchester County, died of pulmonary edema Saturday at United Hospital in Port Chester, N.Y. He was 81 years old and lived in Port Chester.
        Mr. Posillipo, a Democrat in a largely Republican area, was first elected to office in 1941 as a trustee of Port Chester and later became mayor there. He was supervisor of Rye from 1954 to 1984.
        He was instrumental in attracting developers to Rye and paved the way for construction of the Rye Town Hilton and offices for companies that included General Foods, MCI International and Citicorp.
        He is survived by his wife, Rose; a daughter, Ann Rose Rubino; a son, Thomas; a sister, Mamie Bertino; three grandchildren, and two great-grandchildren, all of Port Chester.

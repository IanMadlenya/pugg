        Max Gewirtz, a retired assistant superintendent of New York City schools, died of pneumonia yesterday at Mount Sinai Medical Center. He was 98 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Dr. Gewirtz was a native New Yorker. He graduated from the City College of New York in 1913, and earned a master's degree from Teachers College at Columbia University and a doctorate from New York University.
        He began teaching in 1916, served as an assistant principal and principal, and in 1930 was appointed assistant superintendent of a district in Queens. He held similar posts until his retirement in 1961.
        After his retirement, he was head of a religious school at Temple Israel in Lawrence, L.I.
        A widower, Dr. Gewirtz is survived by his daughter, Geraldine Friedman, and a son, Stanley, both of Manhattan; four grandchildren, and five great-grandchildren.

        Otis Douglas, who began his National Football League playing career at 35, died Tuesday after a long illness. He was 77 years old.  Douglas had never seen a football game before playing tackle at William and Mary from 1929 to 1930.
        He was head coach at Arkansas for three years in the early 1950's, and in between began a four-year playing career with the Philadelphia Eagles in 1946.
        He is survived by his wife, Eleanor; a son, Otis 3d of Richmond; a daughter, Dr. E. Carter Douglas of Fredericksburg, Va., three sisters and a brother.

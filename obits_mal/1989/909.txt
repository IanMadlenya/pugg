        J. Keith Harwood, an executive with R. H. Macy's & Company in Britain for almost 40 years, died of a heart attack Sunday at his home in Beaconsfield, England. He was 62 years old.
        Mr. Harwood, who was educated in schools in England and India, was vice president and managing director of Macy's buyers' office in London at the time of his retirement two years ago. He had joined Macy's in 1948 after service with the Black Watch Regiment of the British Army in World War II. He was awarded the Order of the British Empire in 1985 for his success in increasing exports from the British Isles.
        He is survived by his wife, Lady Felicity Harwood, the daughter of former Prime Minister Clement Attlee of Britain; four daughters, Nicola Messer, of Middletown, N.Y., Penelope Harwood, of London, Joanna Roundell Greene, of Beaconsfield, and Sally Camps, of Sussex, England; a son, Richard, of London; and six grandchildren.

        Michael John Danaher, a senior vice president of Prudential-Bache Capital Funding, died Wednesday at his home in Cold Spring, N.Y. He was 41 years old.
        The cause of death was lymphoma, his family said.
        Mr. Danaher, a 1970 graduate of Villanova University, started on Wall Street in 1983 and helped develop zero-coupon bonds. Previously, he was active in academia and had headed the Hester School in Philadelphia.
        He is survived by his mother, Catherine, of Brooklyn, and two brothers, John, of Albany, and Brian, of Brooklyn.

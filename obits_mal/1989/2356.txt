        John Hill Tyner, a retired antiques dealer, took his own life Monday at his home in Chatham, Mass. He was 69 years old and had suffered from depression for 20 years, a family member said.
        Before retiring to Cape Cod in 1976, Mr. Tyner was president of Edwin Jackson Antiques of New York City, which he joined in 1967. Earlier, he had been the advertising director of the Bristol-Myers Company and of the National Biscuit Company.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Joan Kettering Martin; a son, Christopher, of Los Angeles; two daughters, Victoria Kilburn and Sandra Heath, both of Greenwich, Conn.; two sisters, June Gile of Chatham and Nancy Gagarin of Greenwich, and a granddaughter.

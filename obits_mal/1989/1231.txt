        Francis B. Mooney, a career naval aviator who later taught mathematics in public schools on Long Island, died of lung cancer Wednesday at his home in Southold, L.I. He was 72 years old.
        Mr. Mooney, a native of Fall River, Mass., and a graduate of Holy Cross College, was a Navy squadron commander in the Pacific during World War II. He received a Silver Star for leading a group of carrier-based torpedo-bombers that sank an enemy heavy cruiser near the naval base at Kobe, Japan, on July 28, 1945.
        After his retirement as a lieutenant commander in 1962, Mr. Mooney taught mathematics in the Greenport, Riverhead and Connetquot School Districts until 1979.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Caroline Puvogel; four daughters, Mary, of Manhattan; Cathleen Kane of Rochester; Carol Mooney-Hevesi of Kew Gardens Hills, Queens, and Dana Aubin of Newburyport, Mass.; a son, Paul, of Southold, and four grandchildren.

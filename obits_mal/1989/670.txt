
       Bill Brown, an assistant football coach at Hofstra University who played briefly in the American Football League, died of cancer Tuesday night. He was 52 years old.  Mr. Brown was a 1958 graduate of Syracuse University and captain of the 1957 team, which played in the Cotton Bowl. He was the coach of the offensive line for Hofstra since 1986.
        Bill Brown, an assistant football coach at Hofstra University who played briefly in the American Football League, died of cancer Tuesday night. He was 52 years old.  Mr. Brown was a 1958 graduate of Syracuse University and captain of the 1957 team, which played in the Cotton Bowl. He was the coach of the offensive line for Hofstra since 1986. He played linebacker with the Boston Patriots in 1960.
        Mr. Brown was a high school coach before joining the staffs at C. W. Post and Hofstra. His Berner High School teams in Massapequa, L.I., finished undefeated four times.

        Thomas R. Mulroy, a lawyer who was a former president of the Chicago Crime Commission, died Monday at Lakeland Hospital in Elkhorn, Wis., after suffering a stroke on Saturday. He was 84 years old and lived in Lake Geneva, Wis.
        Mr. Mulroy was senior counsel of the law firm of Hopkins & Sutter. In 1966, when racial violence flared in Chicago, he was named chairman of the Citizens' Committee to Study Police-Community Relations, which published a 300-page study known as the Mulroy Report.
        He received bachelor's and law degrees from the University of Chicago. He served as president of the Executives' Club of Chicago, president of the Bar Association of the Seventh Federal Circuit and chairman of the Evanston Hospital Corporation.
        Mr. Mulroy is survived by his wife, the former Dorothy Reiner; two daughters, Dee King of Evanston, Ill., and Joan Lifvendahl of Orlando, Fla.; a son, Thomas Jr. of Kenilworth, Ill., and 11 grandchildren.

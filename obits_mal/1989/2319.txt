        Abraham Neuwirth, the superintendent of the Bellmore, L.I., school district from 1967 to 1982, when he retired, died Saturday in a Great Neck, L.I., nursing center. He was 73 years old and had homes in Great Neck and in Alford, Mass.
        After his retirement, Mr. Neuwirth was an associate professor of education at Pace University, a consultant on special education for New York City and a hearing officer for the Long Island school system.
        Mr. Neuwirth, a Brooklynite, was credited with batting a baseball over an elevated transit track. He was a graduate of Brooklyn College and City College. In World War II, he was a flight instructor in the Air Force.
        He served in the New York City school system for 26 years, as a biology teacher, assistant science director and school principal. He also taught at Brooklyn College.
        Surviving are his wife, Myra; two daughters, Louise Magun of Portland, Ore., and Jean Vogel of Worcester, Mass.; three sons, Dr. Michael, of Manhattan, Dr. Jerry, of West Hartford, Conn., and Dean, of Denver; a sister, Helen Genberg of Long Beach, L.I., and six grandchildren.

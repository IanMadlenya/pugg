        Lyn Murray, a composer and arranger for radio, television, theater and film for more than 50 years, died of cancer May 20 in Cedars-Sinai Medical Center in Los Angeles. He was 79 years old and lived in Pacific Palisades, Calif.
        Mr. Murray won an Emmy award in 1986 for composing and conducting a National Geographic television special, ''Miraculous Machines.'' His radio credits included the ''Radio Reader's Digest,'' ''The March of Time,'' ''Twenty-Six by Norman Corwin'' and, a program that used his mixed choir, the Lyn Murray Singers, ''Your All-Time Hit Parade.'' He also composed choral music for the Broadway musicals ''Panama Hattie'' and ''Finian's Rainbow.''
        Mr. Murray composed the score for the film ''To Catch a Thief'' and for ''Incident at Vichy'' and ''Requiem for a Nun'' on public television.

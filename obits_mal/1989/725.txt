        Dr. Arthur J. Mannix Jr., a surgeon who founded the largest doctor-owned company writing malpractice insurance, died of cardiac arrest on Wednesday at New Rochelle (N.Y.) Hospital, where he had practiced for four decades. He was 66 years old and lived in New Rochelle.
        Dr. Mannix was president of the Medical Liability Mutual Insurance Company of New York. The company was started in 1975 in response to difficulties surgeons and other medical practitioners were experiencing in obtaining malpractice insurance.
        Dr. Mannix became active in malpractice issues on behalf of the New York State Medical Society in 1955 and often testified before legislative panels.
        Dr. Mannix was born in Chelsea, Mass. He was an alumnus of Fordham University and received his medical degree from Long Island College.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Mary Lou Feuerbach; 8 daughters, Mary Regina of Teaneck, N.J., Catherine, of Andover, Mass., Sara, of Lexington, Mass., Theresa, of Seattle, Margaret, of College, Alaska, Martha, of San Francisco, Rosemary, of Corvallis, Ore., and Shelagh, of Henniker, N.H.; 3 sons, Arthur 3d and Christopher, both of Talkeetna, Alaska, and J. Matthew, of Larchmont, N.Y.; a brother, Richard, of Larchmont, and 14 grandchildren.

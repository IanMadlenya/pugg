        Lionel Malamed, a stockbroker who starred in basketball for City College in the late 1940's, died of a heart attack while playing tennis Sunday at the East River Tennis Club in Long Island City, Queens. He was 65 years old and lived in Forest Hills, Queens.
        Mr. Malamed was co-captain of the City College team in the 1947-48 season. He later played for several professional basketball teams before pursuing a career in Wall Street, where he became a vice president of Herbert Young & Company, a securities concern.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Shirley Diamond; two sons, Michael and Jonathan, both of Forest Hills; a brother, Paul, and a sister, Adele, both of Brooklyn.

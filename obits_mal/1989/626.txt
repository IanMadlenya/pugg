        Francis H. Griswold, a retired Air Force lieutenant general who had been a commandant of the National War College and a vice commander of the Strategic Air Command, died of heart failure Tuesday at Saddleback Community Hospital in Laguna Hills, Calif. He was 84 years old.
        General Griswold, who lived in Laguna Hills, was a native of Erie, Pa., and a graduate of Ohio State University. He enlisted in the Army Air Corps in 1928 and was a fighter pilot in World War II, rising to brigadier general in 1944 and serving as chief of staff of the Eighth Fighter Command in England. In 1945 he became chief of operations of the Eighth Air Force in Okinawa.
        After the war General Griswold was named deputy chief of operations for the Air Force. From 1954 to 1961 he was vice commander of the Strategic Air Command in Omaha and deputy to Gen. Curtis LeMay. General Griswold's final assignment was as commandant of the National War College in Washington, a post he held until his retirement in 1964.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Jeff Sutherland; two daughters, Mary Smith of Rockville, Md., and Claire Pollack of Pacific Palisades, Calif., and six grandchildren.

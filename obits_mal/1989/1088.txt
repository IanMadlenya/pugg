        Dr. Arnold G. Blumberg, a founding physician of North Shore University Hospital, died of cancer on Friday at his home in Lake Success, L.I. He was 69 years old.
        Dr. Blumberg, who specialized in internal medicine, was also on the staff of Long Island Jewish Medical Center from its inception in 1953, the same year that North Shore, which is now North Shore University Hospital-Cornell University Medical College, was founded.
        He was born in Brooklyn and graduated from City College. He earned a master's degree from the University of Pennsylvania and a medical degree from New York University.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Barbara Salmanson; a son, Martin, of Catawissa, Pa.; two daughters, Flori Schwartz of Marblehead, Mass., and Dr. Emily Blumberg of Merion, Pa., and five grandchildren.

        Jack K. Lippert, a retired editor in chief and vice chairman of Scholastic Inc., an educational publishing company, died of cancer Thursday at Norwalk (Conn.) Hospital. He was 86 years old and lived in Norwalk.
        Mr. Lippert joined the company in 1931 and retired in 1973, when he became a consultant. Previously, he was a reporter and editor at The New York World, The Pittsburgh Press and The Pittsburgh Post Gazette. He was born in Pittsburgh and graduated from the University of Pittsburgh and the Columbia University School of Journalism.
        Surviving are a stepson, Dwight Tracy of Havre, Montana, and two grandchildren.

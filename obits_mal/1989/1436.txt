        William K. Beckers, an investment banker who was a partner in Spencer Trask & Company for four decades and later was a vice president of Shearson, Lehman, Hutton, died of a brain tumor on Aug. 10 at his home in East Hampton, L.I. He was 89 years old.
        Mr. Beckers was a governor of the New York Stock Exchange from 1938 to 1942 and from 1944 to 1950, and was the exchange's vice chairman from 1947 to 1950.
        He was a native of Elberfeld, Germany, and was taken to the United States as an infant. He was a graduate of Yale University and did postgraduate work in economics at Columbia University before joining Spencer Trask in 1925.
        Four years later he became a partner, remaining with the firm when it merged with Hornblower & Weeks; Loeb, Rhoads and finally Shearson Lehman Hutton.
        He is survived by two daughters, Antoinette B. McNamara, of Weston, Mass., and Annadel B. Timson, of Fairfield, Conn; a sister, Elsa B. Schreiber, of Ridgefield, Conn.; seven grandchildren and two great-grandchildren.

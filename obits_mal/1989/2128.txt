        John Courtney Ivey, a retired lawyer, died yesterday at Bethesda Memorial Hospital in Boynton Beach, Fla. He was 86 years old and lived in Delray Beach, Fla.
        A 1929 graduate of Yale Law School, Mr. Ivey was admitted to the bar in 1932 and was in private practice before becoming a senior partner with the New York firm of Brown, Wood, Fuller, Caldwell & Ivey in 1942.
        He was the lawyer for Merrill Lynch, Pierce, Fenner & Smith in its dealings with Howard Hughes. In 1972, he helped track down Mr. Hughes in Managua, Nicaragua, to confirm a $140 million underwriting for the Hughes Tool Company. He had earlier dealt with Mr. Hughes in the underwriting of Trans World Airlines. Mr. Ivey retired in 1982.
        He is survived by his wife, Dorsey; three daughters, E. Dorsey Smith of Bronxville, N.Y., Stinceon Dennen and Courtney Combe, both of Greenwich, Conn., and seven grandchildren.

        Robert Grimshaw Eoff, one of the last survivors of the Lafayette Escadrille, died of pneumonia Friday at his home here. He was 93 years old.
        The escadrille consisted of American volunteers who joined the French Army as pilots before the United States entered World War I in 1917.
        James J. Parks of Denver, an honorary member of the escadrille, said the only known remaining survivors were Henry Forster, 100, of New York City and Reginald Sinclaire, 96, of Colorado Springs.
        Mr. Eoff, a retired businessman, is survived by a sister, Harriett Eoff Castle of Rochester, N.Y.

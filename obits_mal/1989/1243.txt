        Jay Lasker, a recording-industry executive who was president of ABC-Dunhill and Motown Records, died of cancer Sunday at his home in Encino, Calif. He was 65 years old.
        Mr. Lasker worked for Decca Records in 1945 after serving in the army during World War II. After getting a law degree, he became the Detroit branch manager for Decca in 1951, and in 1956 became the company's vice president for sales. Five years later, he became vice president of Reprise Records, and then worked for Vee-Jay Records. In 1965, he became a partner in and president of Dunhill Records; when ABC bought the company in 1966, he became president of ABC-Dunhill.
        In 1975, Mr. Lasker became a partner in the short-lived Ariola America Records, and in 1980 he was appointed president of Motown Records, where he revitalized the company by reissuing many catalogue recordings and working closely with such performers as Lionel Richie and Smokey Robinson. He retired due to illness in 1987. Mr. Lasker is survived by his wife, Harriet; a daughter, Marcy; a son, Scott, and two grandchildren.

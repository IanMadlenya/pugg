        Harry Schnell, a retired surveyor whose projects included Lincoln Center and Madison Square Garden, died of pneumonia Tuesday at Parkway Hospital in Forest Hills, Queens. He was 85 years old and lived in Forest Hills.
        Mr. Schnell was born in Brooklyn. A surveyor for more than 50 years, he retired in 1975 as president of Earl B. Lovell & S. P. Belcher Inc., the Manhattan-based surveying firm.
        He was a former president of the City Surveyors Association of Greater New York.
        Surviving are his wife, Lillian; a daughter, Jean Muff of East Meadow, L.I.; two grandchildren, and three great-grandchildren.

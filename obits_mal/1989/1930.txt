        Arnold Ehrlich, deputy editor of Town & Country magazine and a former editor in chief of Publishers Weekly, died of lung cancer Tuesday at Calvary Hospital in the Bronx. He was 66 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Before joining Town & Country in 1984, he was executive editor of Arbor House, a unit of the Hearst Corporation, for three years and editor in chief of Publishers Weekly for seven years.
        Mr. Ehrlich had also held senior editorial posts with Holiday, Show, Venture and Atlantic Monthly magazines.
        A graduate of Johns Hopkins University, he was an Army infantryman in Europe during World War II. After the war he was a publications officer for the United States Information Agency in the Far East.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Michele Doolan; two sons, Christopher, of Los Angeles, and Michael, of Manhattan, and a daughter, Elizabeth, also of Manhattan.

        Clifford Hoitt, founder and president of Mapeco Products Inc., a marine engineering concern with headquarters in Glen Head, L.I., died Tuesday at his home in Deerfield Beach, Fla., after a long illness. He was 76 years old and also had homes in Port Washington, L.I., and Manomet, Mass.
        Mr. Hoitt founded the Marine Products and Engineering Company in 1946, and it was incorporated into Mapeco Products in 1973 with Mr. Hoitt as president. He was also president of Walz & Krenzer Inc. in Rochester for many years. Both companies supply specially engineered products to the marine field.
        He was a graduate of the Webb Institute of Naval Architecture in Glen Cove, L.I. He received the school's Owen Award in 1976 as outstanding alumnus and the Golden Award of the Society of Naval Architecture in 1987.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Flora Quinn; two sons, Clifford Jr., of Long Beach, Calif., and Christopher, of Glen Cove; a daughter, Marianna Ybarra of Topeka, Kan.; nine grandchildren, and two great-grandchildren.

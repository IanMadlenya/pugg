        Frank G. Taormina, a food importer and manufacturer who was chairman of Progresso Quality Foods when he retired in 1969, died yesterday at Morristown (N.J.) Memorial Hospital. He was 89 years old and lived in Peapack, N.J.
        Mr. Taormina was born in Montevago, Sicily, and came to the United States after World War I. The family started Progresso in New Orleans and later moved to Jersey City. Progresso is now based in Vineland, N.J., and is a subsidiary of IC Industries, Inc.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Rose Uddo; two sons, John, of Manhattan and Joseph, of Rivervale, N.J.; two daughters, Carol Laraja of Greenwich, Conn., and Eleanor Loew of Convent Station, N.J.; a sister, Eleanora Ciro of Partanna, Sicily; nine grandchildren and four great-grandchildren.

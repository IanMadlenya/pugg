        Heinrich W. Brinkmann, a retired chairman of Swarthmore College's mathematics department, died of diabetes and old age Friday at his home in Swarthmore, Pa. He was 90 years old.
        Dr. Brinkmann was chairman of the committee that formulated the Educational Testing Service's advance placement test for calculus. He received his B.A. degree from Stanford University and his M.A. and Ph.D. degrees from Harvard.
        His wife, Betty, died last year. Surviving is a brother, Gerhard, of Riverside, Cal.

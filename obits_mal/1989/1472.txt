        Lewis Conn, a former newspaper publisher and labor union leader, died after life support systems were removed from him at the request of the family Tuesday at the Anne Arundel Medical Center in Annapolis, Md.
        Mr. Conn, who was 73 years old and lived in Annapolis, had been in a coma for a month as a result of accidental poisoning. He was executive director of the Association of Area Business Publications, an organization of business-oriented publications.
        He had been the publisher of The Kentucky Business Ledger and The Jefferson Reporter, a weekly. He had also been an official of the Textile Workers Union of America.
        He is survived by his wife, Irene; two sons, Peter and Paul; a daughter, Deborah Cincotta; two brothers, Richard and Harry, and three grandchildren.

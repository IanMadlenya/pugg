        John William Bainton, an engineering executive, died of melanoma Thursday at Jefferson University Hospital in Philadelphia. He was 63 years old and had homes in Lyford Cay, the Bahamas, and in Amagansett, L.I.
        Mr. Bainton, a 1947 graduate of the Columbia University School of Engineering, was vice chairman of the board of Viatech Inc., an international engineering and packaging concern based in Syosett, L.I. and chairman of its executive committee. Previously, he had been vice president of Chemtex Inc. in Manhattan and was general manager for South American operations for the Pennwalt Company.
        He is survived by his wife, Aileena son, Kenneth, and a daughter, Cressida, both of Manhattan; his mother, Mildred, and a sister, Patricia B. Healey, both of Larchmont; two brothers, Robert, of Rumson, N.J., and Donald, of Greenwich, Conn., and two grandchildren.

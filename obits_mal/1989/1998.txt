        Richard F. Uhlmann, a grain dealer who served as president of the Chicago Board of Trade in 1948, died at Highland Park (Ill.) Hospital on Monday after a brief illness. He was 91 years old.
        Mr. Uhlmann was considered one of the principal traders in the grain business. For years he headed the Uhlmann Grain Company in Chicago, serving often as an industry spokesman. In 1964 the company was acquired by H. Hentz & Company.
        He is survived by his wife, Catherine; a son, Frederick; two daughters, Janis Weil and Audrey Rosenheim, 13 grandchildren and 11 great-grandchildren.

        Sheldon A. Vogel, a former partner in the Manhattan law firm of Thacher Proffitt & Wood, died of respiratory failure on Wednesday at Doctors Hospital in Manhattan. He was 68 years old and lived in Manhattan and Coronado, Calif.
        Mr. Vogel, a 1947 graduate of Yale Law School, specialized in admiralty and maritime law. He was with the firm of Bigham Englar Jones & Houston for 35 years before joining Thacher Proffitt & Wood in 1982. He retired in 1988 but continued as counsel to the firm.
        He was a former member of the Maritime Transportation Research Board of the National Research Council and also of the permanent advisory board of the Tulane University Admiralty Institute. He taught admiralty law at Brooklyn Law School from 1954 to 1960 and served as counsel to the American Hull Insurance Syndicate and the Water Quality Insurance Syndicate, associations of insurance companies.
        He is survived by two sons, Story, of Coronado, and Kent, of Manhattan; a daughter, Margaret G. Vogel of Southport, Conn., and a brother, Ralph, of Atlanta.

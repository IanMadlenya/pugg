        Charles Greenfield, inventor of a dehydration process used in waste disposal, died of pneumonia Friday at Overlook Hospital in Summit, N.J. He was 75 years old and lived in New Providence, N.J.
        Mr. Greenfield was a developer of the Carver-Greenfield Process, a way to dehydrate municipal and industrial wastes without creating odors or pollution. He was the founder and chief executive officer of Dehydro-Tech Corporation, which modifies waste-disposal plants to use the process.
        Born in Brooklyn, he graduated from the Cooper Union for the Advancement of Science. He was a fellow of the American Chemical Society.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Jacqueline Coblens; a son, David, of New Providence; a daughter, Joan Maxwell of Orinda, Calif; two sisters, Estelle Wiener of Queens and Florence Cohen of Plainview, L.I., and two grandchildren.

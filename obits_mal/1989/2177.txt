        The Rev. Dr. Gene E. Bartlett, president emeritus of Colgate Rochester Divinity School, died of a heart attack on Nov. 3 at Genesee Hospital in Rochester, where he lived. He was 79 years old.
        Dr. Bartlett, who was president of the divinity school from 1960 to 1970, oversaw the merging of several schools during his tenure. The Baptist Missionary Training School was incorporated into the divinity school, providing for women preparing for the ministry. Bexley Hall, an Episcopal seminary, and Crozer Theological Seminary, another Baptist school, also became part of the divinity school.
        Dr. Bartlett was born in Parkersburg, W. Va., and graduated from Denison University and Colgate Rochester Divinity School. After his tenure as divinity school president, he was pastor of the First Baptist Church of Newton Centre, Mass.
        The author of several religious books, he was a guest preacher in many churches and had a radio ministry from 1935 to 1960. He was a former president of the Massachusetts Council of Churches and the American Baptist Churches in the U.S.A.
        Surviving are his wife, Jean; three sons, David of Richmond, Va., Randall of Northampton, Mass., and Stephen of Rochester; two daughters, Marion Bartlett VanArsdell of Northampton, Mass., and Margaret Bartlett of Boston, and nine grandchildren.

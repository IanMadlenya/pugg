        James B. Ramsey Jr., a financial consultant to the Small Business Administration in New York, died of a respiratory illness Saturday at the Carteret General Hospital in Morehead City, N.C., near his vacation home. He was 66 years and also lived in Wyckoff, N.J.
        Mr. Ramsey was a former president of Blair & Company, brokers of Manhattan, and a former senior vice president of Thomson McKinnon Securities Inc., also of Manhattan. He had held many posts in Wyckoff, including Mayor and Finance Commissioner. In World War II, he served in the Marine Corps in the Pacific.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Anna De Lucia; two daughters, Richarda Randall of Kinston, N.C., and McClain Sokoloff of Dingmans Ferry, Pa.; his mother, Villita Sisk Ramsey of Wyckoff; a sister, Julia Wright of Nashville, and two grandchildren.

        Charles Punia, a New York real-estate developer for more than 60 years, died Wednesday at his home in Brooklyn after a long illness. He was 86 years old.
        Mr. Punia came to the United States from Poland as a child. He began his career as a mortgage broker and went on to develop real estate in Manhattan, Brooklyn and Queens. He was active in the United Jewish Appeal Federation and other philanthropies.
        A widower, he is survived by three sons, Leonard, of Princeton, N.J., Herbert, of Livingston, N.J., and Jerome, of Brooklyn; four grandchildren, and six great-grandchildren.

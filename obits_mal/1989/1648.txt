        Vladimir S. Littauer, a horseback riding instructor and the author of several books on the subject, died Thursday at his home in Syosset, L.I. He was 96 years old.
        Mr. Littauer was a former captain in the Russian Imperial Cavalry. He later became an instructor at the Boots and Saddles School in Manhattan and taught private pupils at his home in Syosset. He retired in the late 1970's.
        Mr. Littauer wrote several books, including: ''Common Sense Horsemanship,'' ''Horseman's Progress,'' and his memoirs of the cavalry, ''Russian Hussar.''
        He is survived by his wife, the former Mary Aiken Graver, and a son, Andrew A. Littauer of Hopewell, N.J.

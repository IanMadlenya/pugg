        Charles D. Morrissey, a civil engineer, died of a heart attack Tuesday in Nairn, Scotland. He was 63 years old and lived in Briarcliff Manor, N.Y.
        Mr. Morrissey was a partner in Basil-Morrissey P.C., a consulting engineering concern in Manhattan. He had been president of Basil Engineering Inc. in Washington. He was born in the Bronx, graduated from Manhattan College and received a master's degree from New York University.
        He was a fellow of the American Society of Civil Engineers and the American Consulting Engineers Council and was a member of the American Arbitration Association.
        Surviving are his wife, Monica; two sons, David, of Lansing, Mich., and James, of Daytona, Fla.; two daughters, Karen Topp of Somers, N.Y., and Christine Morrissey Duncan of Manhattan; a sister, Sister Margaret Morrissey of Tarrytown, N.Y., and two granddaughters.

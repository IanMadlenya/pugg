        Murry Dickson, a pitcher who compiled a 172-181 record and a 3.66 earned run average with 5 teams in 18 major league seasons, died from complications of emphysema today at Bethany Medical Center. He was 73 years old and lived near Leavenworth, Kan.
        His career began in 1939 and ended in 1959. His best seasons were with the Cardinals in 1946, when he was 15-6 with a 2.88 earned run average, and with the Pirates in 1951, when he was 21-16. He lost 20 games in a season twice.
        He is survived by a son, Steven, of Tucson, Ariz., and a daughter, Donna Kersberger, of Prairie Village, Kan.

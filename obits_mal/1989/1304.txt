        John M. Driscoll, former chief mechanical engineer of the Consolidated Edison Company of New York, died of cardiac arrest Monday at his home in Oradell, N.J. He was 85 years old.
        Mr. Driscoll joined Con Edison in 1925, following his graduation from Brown University. He retired in 1969 after 44 years with the utility. He held numerous patents, including one for an innovation in the design of a steam turbine.
        He is survived by his wife, Margaret; two sons, John, of Oradell, and Peter, of Manhasset, L.I.; a daughter, Sheila D. Rosenthal, of Dix Hills, L.I., and 17 grandchildren.

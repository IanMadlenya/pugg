        George E. Bartol 3d, former chairman and chief executive of the Hunt Manufacturing Company, Philadelphia, manufacturers of art and office supplies, died of a brain tumor yesterday at his apartment in Manhattan. He was 68 years old and also had a home in Sonoita, Ariz.
        Mr. Bartol, a native of Philadelphia, joined the company, which was then family-owned, in 1946. The company went public in 1971. He was chief executive from 1957 to his retirement in 1981. He was chairman from 1969 to 1987 and a director until his death.
        He was also a director of the Federal Reserve Bank Board of Philadelphia, chairman of the Franklin Institute and a trustee of the Academy of Natural Sciences.
        He graduated from Princeton in 1943 and served with the Navy in World War II as a lieutenant junior grade.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Mary Farr; four daughters, Blair Mac Innes of Morristown, N.J., Mary Rush Wolfzon of St. Paul, Minn., Victoria Vallely of Boston, Mass., Katherine Lunt of Greenfield, Mass., and nine grandchildren.

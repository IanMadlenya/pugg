        Terry A. Francois, a civil rights lawyer who was the first black to sit on the San Francisco Board of Supervisors, died of cancer Friday at his home here. He was 67 years old.
        A Democrat, Mr. Francois served on the board for 14 years before resigning in 1978 to return to private practice.
        He is survived by his wife, Marion; four sons, a daughter, two sisters and six grandchildren.

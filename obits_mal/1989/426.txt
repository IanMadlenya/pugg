        Elmer Lee Fingar, a former partner in McCarthy, Fingar, Donovan, Drazen & Smith, a law firm in White Plains, died Tuesday at Martin Memorial Hospital in Stuart, Fla. He was 84 years old and lived in New Canaan, Conn.
        From 1964 to 1969 Mr. Fingar was a member of the New York State Commission on Estates. He had been chief clerk of the Westchester County Surrogate's Court and senior vice president of the National Bank of Westchester. He joined the law firm in 1965. Mr. Fingar graduated from Cornell University and Yale University Law School. He was a former town attorney of New Castle, N.Y.
        Surviving are two daughters, Carol Maloney of La Grange, Ill., and Jane Branigan of Darien, Conn.; a son, Lee, of Darien, and seven grandchildren.

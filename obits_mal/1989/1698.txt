
       Benjamin Busch, a lawyer and a law professor, died on Sept. 15 at his home in Lakeville, Conn., after a long illness. He was 77 years old. Mr. Busch specialized in international law. He was a partner in the Manhattan firm of Katz & Sommerich from 1946 to 1976. He then became of counsel to Hamburger, Weinschenk, Molnar & Busch in Manhattan.
        Benjamin Busch, a lawyer and a law professor, died on Sept. 15 at his home in Lakeville, Conn., after a long illness. He was 77 years old. Mr. Busch specialized in international law. He was a partner in the Manhattan firm of Katz & Sommerich from 1946 to 1976. He then became of counsel to Hamburger, Weinschenk, Molnar & Busch in Manhattan. He was also an adjunct professor of international law at the New York Law School. He graduated from the St. Lawrence University law school in 1933.
        Mr. Busch is survived by his wife, Phyllis; two sons, Frederick, of Sherburne, N.Y., and Eric, of Philadelphia, and two grandchildren.

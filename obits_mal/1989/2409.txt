        Lionel E. Rubinstein, a founder of The Rome Daily American, an English-language newspaper published in Italy for 31 years, died Friday at Central Suffolk Hospital in Riverhead, L.I. He was 81 years old and lived in Westhampton Beach, L.I.
        Born in Rumania in 1908, Mr. Rubinstein was with British intelligence and later American intelligence in Palestine and Egypt during World War II. He co-founded The Daily American in 1946. It ceased operations in 1977. He was also with Radio Free Europe and Radio Liberty. In the late 1960's he operated the Grassmere Hotel in Westhampton Beach. He retired in 1979.
        He is survived by his wife, Georgette.

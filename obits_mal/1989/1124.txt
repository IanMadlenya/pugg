        Dr. Walter B. Hoover Sr., who headed the ear, nose and throat department at the Lahey Clinic in Boston for three decades, died June 17 at Universal Hospital in Plantation, Fla., after a long illness. He was 91 years old.
        Dr. Hoover, a 1922 graduate of the Washington University School of Medicine in St. Louis, started the ontological and laryngological department at the Lahey Clinic in 1928 and was chief until his retirement in 1963. He then served several tours aboard the hospital ship Hope, off South America and West Africa.
        Dr. Hoover, who lived in Palm Beach, Fla., is survived by his wife, Cecelia; two daughters, Patricia Petot, of Pikesville, Ky., and Lorna Hoover, of Chelsea, Mass.; two sons, Dr. Walter Jr., of Palm Beach, and C. Jackson, of Duxbury, Mass.; 13 grandchildren and 9 great-grandchildren.

        Wellington Powell, a retired vice president of the American Telephone and Telegraph Company, died of cancer Thursday at the Washington Hospice. He was 89 years old and had moved to Washington from Manhattan three years ago.
        In 1921, following his graduation from Colgate University, Mr. Powell joined the New York Telephone Company, a subsidary of A.T.& T. He was named vice president and general manager for the Albany area in 1946. In 1955, was named to head the company's operations in Manhattan, the Bronx and Westchester. In 1960, he became vice president of operations. He retired three years later.
        Mr. Powell was also a Colgate trustee for many years.
        He is survived by his wife, Roma; two sons, John, of Phoenix, and Donald, of Greensboro, N.C.; a stepson, Harrington Drake of Carpenteria, Calif., and five grandchildren.

        Rudolf Uhlenhaut, an automotive engineer who designed and engineered Mercedes-Benz racing and passenger cars, died of heart disease on May 8 at his home in Stuttgart, West Germany. He was 82 years old.  Mr. Uhlenhaut began his 41-year career with Mercedes-Benz in 1931 as a test engineer. In 1936 he became head of the company's racing-car division and guided production of the Silver Arrow cars that dominated automobile racing in the late 1930's.
        He later concentrated on the development and testing of production cars. He retired in 1972 as head of passenger-car development.

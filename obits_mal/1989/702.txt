        Aaron M. Diamond, a senior partner in the New York law firm of Diamond & Golomb, died of brain cancer yesterday at Beth Israel Medical Center. He was 71 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Diamond was born in Far Rockaway, Queens. He was a graduate of Brooklyn Law School and a former chairman of the tax committee of the New York County Lawyers Association.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Elinor Ross; two sons, Mitchell, of Towson, Md., and Stuart, of Manhattan; a stepson, Ross Lewis of Manhattan, and a granddaughter.
        Correction:  April 27, 1989, Thursday, Late City Final Edition
        An obituary on Tuesday about Aaron M. Diamond, a New York lawyer, misidentified his widow. She is the former Elinor Lewis; her stage name as a soprano is Elinor Ross.

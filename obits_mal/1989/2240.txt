        Bobby Capo, a pop composer, died Monday at Beekman Downtown Medical Center after suffering a heart attack in his office at the State Department of Probation in Manhattan, where he worked. He was 68 years old.
        Mr. Capo, whose given name was Felix Rodriguez, was born in Coamo, Puerto Rico, in 1921. He composed more than 2,000 songs, many of which were romantic ballads that were recorded by well-known Latin American singers.

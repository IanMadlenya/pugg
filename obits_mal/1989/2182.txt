        Charles Blake, a retired teacher in New Rochelle, N.Y., and a founder of the Academy of Lifelong Learning, a continuing education program for the elderly in Wilmington, Del., died of congestive heart failure Thursday at Christiana Hospital in Newark, Del. He was 78 years old and lived in Wilmington.
        In New Rochelle, he was supervisor of foreign language instruction for the school system until 1978, when he retired to Wilmington. He had been a winner of the Distinguished Teachers Award from the New York State Association of Foreign Language Teachers.
        Born in New York City, Mr. Blake graduated from Wesleyan University and earned a master's degree from Columbia University. He was a social worker and probation officer in Westchester County from 1934 to 1943.
        Surviving are his wife, Anne; a son, Charles H., of Ithaca, N.Y., and two grandsons.

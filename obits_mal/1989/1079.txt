        Everett Melosh, who won four Emmy awards as a lighting director for ABC-TV, died on Thursday at Valley Hospital in Ridgewood, N.J., after a heart attack. He was 62 years old and lived in Woodcliff Lake, N.J.
        Mr. Melosh, who joined the network in its infancy in 1949, won awards for his work at the 1984 Summer Olympics in Los Angeles and the 1988 Winter Olympics in Calgary, Alberta. He also won for his lighting on the television soap operas ''One Life to Live'' and ''All My Children.''
        As a senior lighting director, Mr. Melosh was in charge of lighting or designed the lighting for several of the network's news programs, including ''Nightline,'' ''World News Tonight,'' ''20/20'' and most of the election coverage and political conventions for the last 30 years.
        He was born in Jersey City and was a technical sergeant in the Army during World War II.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Maria Scognamiglio; a son, Robert, of Woodcliff Lake; and a brother, Richard, of Montvale, N.J.

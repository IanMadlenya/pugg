        Frederick Hutchinson Kingsbury Jr., a general partner in the private banking firm of Brown Brothers Harriman & Company, died Monday at his home in Manhattan. He was 82 years old.
        A 1929 alumnus of Princeton University, Mr. Kingsbury joined Brown Brothers shortly after graduation. In 1931, the firm merged with W. A. Harriman & Company and Harriman Brothers & Company to form the present firm. He was named a general partner in 1949 in charge of international banking. He also served on the boards of many business and charitable organizations, including the Prudential Insurance Company and the Montclair Art Museum.
        He is survived by his wife, Eleanor; a son, Frederick 3d, of Manhattan; a daughter, Hope Costikyan of New Canaan, Conn.; a stepson, William O'Donnell 3d, of Kemah, Tex.; a stepdaughter, Ellen Page of Marblehead, Mass.; six grandchildren, and four step-grandchildren.

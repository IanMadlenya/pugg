        Frederick R. Moseley Jr., former executive vice president of the Morgan Guaranty Trust Company, died of bone cancer yesterday at Huntington (L.I.) Hospital. He was 75 years old and lived in Jericho, L.I.
        Mr. Moseley became associated with J. P. Morgan & Company Inc., now Morgan Guaranty Trust Company, after his graduation from Harvard in 1936. He became vice president in 1953, senior vice president in 1965 and executive vice president in 1966. He retired in 1979.
        He was president of the Harvard Club of New York from 1979 to 1981. While at Harvard he was captain of both the football and hockey teams. He was elected to the United States Hockey Hall of Fame in 1975.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Lilias Kent Knott; three sons, Christopher L., of Unionville, Del., Daniel G., of Ronkonkoma, L.I., and Frederick R. 3d, of Los Angeles; two brothers, Thomas, of Naples, Fla., and William, of Philadelphia,; a sister, Ardele Fullerton of Dedham, Mass.; eight grandchildren, and three great-grandchildren.

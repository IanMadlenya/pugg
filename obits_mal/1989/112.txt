        Joseph A. Adorno, a retired Superior Court judge and former state treasurer, was killed Friday in a traffic accident on Route 66 here. He was 76 years old and lived in Middletown.
        Mr. Adorno's wife, Mary, and two others were seriously injured in the crash at about 11:30 A.M. Friday, when a pickup truck crossed the center line and struck the Adornos' car head-on, the police said.
        Mrs. Adorno was listed in serious but stable condition at Hartford Hospital, where she was taken by helicopter.
        Mr. Adorno was appointed to the state's old Circuit Court in 1966. He became a Superior Court judge in 1978 when the court system was reorganized.
        He became a state trial referee in 1982 after he reached the mandatory retirement age of 70.
        Mr. Adorno, a Republican, was elected state treasurer in 1947 and served eight years under four governors.
        He also served as deputy attorney general from 1955 until 1959. He ran unsuccessfully for lieutenant governor in 1962.
        Mr. Adorno graduated from Fordham University in 1933 and received his law degree from Boston University School of Law in 1936.
        He and his wife had two daughters.

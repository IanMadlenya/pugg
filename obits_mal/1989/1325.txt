        Henry Tannenbaum, a restaurateur, died Wednesday at Beth Israel Hospital in Manhattan. He was 93 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Tannenbaum was a part owner of Bill's Gay 90's, a restaurant and nightclub that opened on East 54th Street in 1930 and in the 1950's of Charles' French Restaurant on the Avenue of the Americas in Greenwich Village. He retired in 1965.
        He is survived by two daughters, Carolyn Kaplan, of West Palm Beach, Fla., and Lawrence, L.I., and Doris Glick, of Beverly Hills, Calif; a son, Sheldon J. Tannen, of Manhattan; seven grandchildren, and eight great-grandchildren.

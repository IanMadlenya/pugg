        Cyril Simon, a theater director, died of a heart attack on Monday at New York Hospital-Cornell Medical Center. He was 68 years old.
        Among the Off Broadway productions Mr. Simon directed were Lewis John Carlino's ''Telemachus'' and revivals of ''Medea'' with Gloria Foster and ''The Petrified Forest'' with Jason Robards. He also directed plays on television for the Armstrong Circle Theater.
        He is survived by two brothers, Robert, of Vineland, N.J., and Sidney, of Amherst, Mass., and a sister, Dorothy Golomb of Boynton Beach, Fla.

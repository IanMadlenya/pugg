        Guillermo Belt, a former Cuban Amb-assador to the United States and a signer of the Charter of the United Nations, died Sunday at Arlington Hospital of complications after surgery for an intestinal blockage. He was 83 years old and lived in Bethesda, Md.
        Mr. Belt, a 1926 graduate of the University of Havana Law School, was Ambassador to the United States from 1944 to 1949. He was concurrently representative to the Organization of American States, and from 1945 to 1949 was also the Cuban delegate to the United Nations.
        He was chief of the Cuban delegation to the 1945 meeting in San Francisco that resulted in the setting up of the United Nations, then later served on various United Nations commissions and was a governor of several international banking organizations.
        From 1956 to 1959, he was professor of international law at the University of Santo Tomas de Villanueva in Havana and in 1961 came to live in the United States after Fidel Castro's rise to power.
        He is survived by his wife, Elisa; a daughter, Marilys, of Manhattan; three sons, Guillermo, Noel and Juan, all of Washington; 11 grandchildren and 5 great-grandchildren.

        Alfred M. Wilson, a retired executive vice president of the Minneapolis-Honeywell Company, died after a heart attack yesterday at Vineyard Haven Hospital on Martha's Vineyard, Mass. He was 85 years old and lived on the island.
        Mr. Wilson was captain of Yale University's unbeaten varsity crew, which won the gold medal in the 1924 Olympics in Paris.
        A native of Minneapolis, he graduated from Phillips Andover Academy before going to Yale. After several years with the New York investment banking firm of J. & W. Seligman and Company, he joined Minneapolis-Honeywell in 1941. Mr. Wilson became a vice president in 1943 and was in charge of the company's aeronautical division, which manufactured bombsights in World War II. He retired in 1968. Surviving are his wife, the former Josefa Watjen; a daughter, Josefa Wrangham, and a son, Timothy, both of Somerville, Mass.; three stepsons, Winfield Shiras of Winnetka, Ill., Peter Shiras of Lincoln, Mass., and David Shiras of Los Angeles; 13 grandchildren, and two great-grandchildren.

        J. Emerson Thors, a retired investment banker, died of cancer Tuesday at his home in Water Mill, L.I. He was 87 years old.
        Mr. Thors, a native New Yorker, was a partner in the investment banking house of Kuhn, Loeb & Company. He joined the company in 1917 and in 1941 became manager of the syndicate department. He was named a partner in the early 1940's and became a limited partner in the 1960's, when he retired. Mr. Thors remained a financial consultant to the firm, which is now part of Shearson Lehman Hutton Inc.
        Mr. Thors was a governor of the Association of Stock Exchange Firms and of the Bond Club of New York. He was a special financial adviser to the Jamaican Government and a board member of the Sheraton-Kingston Hotel Corporation. He was a member of the national committee of the Boy Scouts of America and was co-chairman of the Windmill Restoration Fund of Water Mill.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Jane Urquhart; two sons from a former marriage, Jay, of Manhasset, L.I., and Barry, of Wilton, Conn.; 10 grandchildren, and two great-grandchildren.

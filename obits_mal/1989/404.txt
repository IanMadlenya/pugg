        George Katz, a founding partner of Wachtel, Lipton, Rosen & Katz, a law firm in New York City, died of a heart attack yesterday at his home in Manhattan. He was 57 years old.
        Mr. Katz helped establish the firm in 1965. His practice involved mergers and acquisitions and corporate counseling and litigation. Earlier he had been associated with the firms of Seligson & Morris and Becker, Ross & Stone in Manhattan.
        He was an alumnus of City College and the New York University School of Law.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Frances Schechter; his mother, Bella, of Brooklyn; three daughters, Deborah, Jodi and Amy, all of Manhattan; a brother, Jerome, of Great Neck, L.I., and a sister, Lauretta Roth of Port Washington, L.I.

        James B. Rosenwald, a securities analyst who specialized in Japanese insurance companies and banks, died Sunday at his home in Kings Bay Estates, Tobago, West Indies, after a series of strokes. He was 83 years old and also lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Rosenwald served as a financial consultant to Nikko Securities Ltd., for 30 years. Five years ago he joined Daiwa Securities America Inc., where he worked until his death.
        In Tobago, where he lived for many years, he was active in civic causes. He contributed land for a water-treatment plant serving a substantial portion of Tobago and donated to Trinidad and Tobago a heliport he had built.
        Mr. Rosenwald was born in New York and was a graduate of Williams College and the Columbia University Graduate School of Business.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Dorothy Bleyer; a daughter, Patricia Heller of Manhattan and East Hampton, L.I.; a son, James Jr., of Ossining, N.Y.; 10 grandchildren, and three great-grandchildren.

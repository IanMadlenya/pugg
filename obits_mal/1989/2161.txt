        Richard J. Vorisek, who received an Academy Award nomination for sound direction for the 1981 film ''Reds,'' died at Stamford Hospital on Tuesday after a long illness. He was 71 years old and lived in Stamford.
        Mr. Vorisek was an internationally known sound director whose credits include ''Kramer vs. Kramer,'' ''The Untouchables'' and ''All That Jazz.'' In the 1950's he worked on the CBS television series ''The Honeymooners,'' starring Jackie Gleason and Art Carney.
        Mr. Vorisek, who was born in New York City, worked in the film business for 50 years. He had been a principal in TransAudio Inc. of New York since 1973 and had also taught in the film division of the Graduate School of the Arts at Columbia University.
        Surviving are his wife, Margaret; a son, Richard, of Morristown, N.J.; two daughters, Mary Jane Ford of Charlottesville, Va., and Barbara Heymann of Fairfield, Conn.; a brother, Jack, of New York City, and four grandsons.
        A funeral Mass is to be said at 10 A.M. on Friday at St. Gabriel's Church, 948 Newfield Avenue, in Stamford.

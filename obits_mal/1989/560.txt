        Emory S. Basford, a retired chairman of the English department at Phillips Academy, Andover, Mass., died Wednesday at Asbury Village, a retirement community in Gaithersburg, Md., his residence since 1970. He was 90 years old.
        Mr. Basford taught at Andover from 1928 until retirement in 1964. He also was a founder of St. Stephen's School in Rome, where he taught for five years before moving to Gaithersburg.
        He is survived by a sister, Mabelle B. Smith, of Towson, Md., and a brother, Carroll, of Bohannon, Va.

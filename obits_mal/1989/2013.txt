        Francis Charles Miller, a retired advertising executive, died yesterday at the Moorings Park Health Center in Naples, Fla. He was 88 years old and lived in Naples.
        Mr. Miller founded Leading National Advertisers in 1945 in Manhattan. The company analyzes advertising data for manufacturers. He stepped down as president in 1972 and retired in 1977.
        Mr. Miller sold magazine advertising before 1945. He graduated from the University of Wisconsin in 1922.
        Surviving are his wife, Esther; a son, C. Scott, of Darien, Conn., and three grandchildren.

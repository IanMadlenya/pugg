        David Miller, the Mayor of Tonawanda, N.Y., and an official of the Western New York Economic Development Corporation, suffered a heart attack and died on his way to work Monday at the development corporation's office in Buffalo. He was 52 years old.
        Mr. Miller, a Democrat, was serving his fourth two-year term as Mayor. He had been city clerk of Tonawanda and was an assistant project manager for the development corporation.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Leatrice Henningham; two daughters, Lori O'Neill and Lisa Miller, both of Tonawanda; a son, Jeffrey, of Grand Island, N.Y., and a grandson.

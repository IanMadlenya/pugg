        David Victor, a producer of numerous television series including ''Dr. Kildare'' and ''Marcus Welby, M.D.,'' died after a heart attack on Oct. 18 while he and his wife, Florence, were vacationing in Williamsburg, Va. He was 79 years old and lived in Los Angeles.
        When he was 13, Mr. Victor and his widowed mother emigrated from Russia to the United States. He began writing radio scripts during the Depression. By the 1960's he had moved into television, where he worked as a writer and producer.
        His productions included the series ''The Man From U.N.C.L.E.,'' ''The Girl From U.N.C.L.E.,'' ''Owen Marshall, Counselor at Law'' and ''Women in White.'' His films for television and his mini-series included ''Little Women,'' ''The Woman I Love'' and ''Portrait: The Man Whose Name Was John,'' starring Raymond Burr as Pope John XXIII.
        Mr. Victor is survived by his wife; a daughter, Fern Seizer of Beverly Hills; a son, Dr. Michael Victor of Tyler, Tex., and four grandchildren.

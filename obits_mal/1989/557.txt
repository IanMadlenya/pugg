        Clayton Huff, former chief financial officer of the advertising concern of Batten, Barton, Durstine & Osborn Inc. in Manhattan, died of pneumonia Wednesday at Sarasota (Fla.) Memorial Hospital. He was 74 years old and lived in Longboat Key, Fla.
        Mr. Huff, a 1937 graduate of the Harvard Business School, joined the company in 1956 and was treasurer and executive vice president when he retired in 1975. He was also an assistant treasurer for Compton Advertising Inc. and was with J. Walter Thompson, J. Stirling Getchell Inc. and the Chrysler Corporation. During World War II he was a lieutenant in the Navy.
        He is survived by three sons, Joseph, of Scarsdale, N.Y.; William, of Boston, and Robert, of White Plains; a daughter, Patricia O'Dwyer of Manila; a brother, James, of Sarasota, and five grandchildren.

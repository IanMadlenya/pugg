        Terence F. Green, a managing director at the Chase Manhattan Bank, died Sunday at Memorial Sloan-Kettering Cancer Center. He was 42 years old and lived in Manhattan and Southold, L. I. His family said Mr. Green died of pneumonia.
        Mr. Green, a native of Evansville, Ind., was a graduate of the Gunnery School and Yale. He received a master's degree in divinity from the General Theological Seminary and a master's in business administration from Columbia University.
        He joined Chase Manhattan in 1978, became a vice president in 1983 and became a managing director in the corporate finance division for North America in 1987.
        He is survived by his parents, Quentin and Elizabeth Green of Grayville, Ill., and a sister, Elizabeth Green of Manhattan.

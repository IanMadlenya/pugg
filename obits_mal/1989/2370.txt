        Benjamin J. Lucarelli, a contractor whose company built some of New Jersey's best-known apartment and hotel complexes, died yesterday at his home in Spring Lake, N.J. He was 82 years old and had lived in Jersey City and Sea Girt, N.J.
        In 1937 he founded B. J. Lucarelli & Company, an industrial, commercial and institutional contractor based in Newark. He served as its president until 1967, when he retired.
        Among the company's New Jersey projects were the Versailles apartments along the Palisades in West New York, the Mayfair apartments in East Orange and Carlton House, a hotel in Asbury Park. He worked on the United States Military Academy at West Point, Rutgers University's New Brunswick campus, and McGuire Air Force Base and Fort Monmouth in central New Jersey.
        He was born in Jersey City and attended the Stevens Institute in Hoboken and Columbia University. Stevens awarded him an honorary degree in 1954. He was a former president of the Building Contractors Association of New Jeresey.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Jean R. Iorio; a son, Joseph T., of Rumson, N.J.; a daughter, Rosanne Kemm of Rumson; a sister, Ida Sabatino of Bricktown, N.J.; 11 grandchildren, and one great-grandchild.

        Albert J. Lima, a California leader of the Communist Party, died of cancer June 3. He was 82 years old and lived in Oakland.
        In 1952 he and 13 others were convicted of conspiring to overthrow the United States Government, but the jury verdict was set aside in 1958 by the United States Supreme Court.
        In 1963 he spoke at the University of California at Berkeley, ending the school's 13-year ban on Communist speakers. He ran unsuccessfully twice as a Communist candidate for the House of Representatives.
        Survivors include his wife, Helen; two daughters and three grandchildren.

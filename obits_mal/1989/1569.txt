        Dr. Kurt Lange, a professor emeritus of pediatrics and medicine at the New York Medical College and a consulting physician at Lenox Hill Hospital, died of heart disease Saturday at his home in Manhattan. He was 82 years old.
        Dr. Lange was born in Berlin and received an M.D. degree at the University of Berlin, where he was successively an intern, a resident in pathology and an instructor and practitioner in internal medicine. He fled Germany in 1938 and began teaching and practicing in New York City the following year.
        Dr. Lange was a former director of renal service of the New York Medical College and the Metropolitan Hospital Center. He was the American editor of Clinical Nephrology, a medical-research journal, and wrote or co-wrote 227 papers on his specialties.
        He won many honors, including the Lester Hoenig award from the Kidney Foundation of New York in 1977. He was the chief of the Army's renal service and laboratory from 1941 to 1948.
        His wife, Helen, died in 1985. Surviving are a son, Peter, of Durham, N.C.; a daughter, Monica Lange of Rocky Hill, N.J.; a brother, Werner Lange of Los Angeles, and four grandchildren.

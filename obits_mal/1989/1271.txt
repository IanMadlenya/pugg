        Dr. F. Howard Westcott, a physician in Manhattan for 36 years, died of cancer Wednesday at his home in San Diego. He was 86 years old.
        Following his graduation from Syracuse University Medical School in 1927, Dr. Westcott was affiliated with New York Postgraduate Hospital and St. Luke's Hospital until his retirement in 1966. During this time, he was also medical director of the P. Lorillard Company and J.P Stevens. On three occasions, he was an attending physician on the hospital ship, Project Hope, off the coasts of Peru, Vietnam and Nicaragua. A pilot, he was also a member of the Flying Physicians, a group that provides medical supplies and assistance at disaster sites.
        He is survived by three daughters, Ruth Barnhardt of Asheville, N.C., and Martha Hawley and Julianna Westcott, both of San Diego; eleven grandchildren and eight great-grandchildren.

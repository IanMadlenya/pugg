        Frederick Blake Payne, a retired senior partner of Tucker, Anthony & R. L. Day, the stockbrokerage house, died on Monday at his home in Clogheen, Ireland. He was 88 years old and had been suffering from Parkinson's disease.
        Mr. Payne was with Tucker, Anthony for more than 50 years. In 1956 and 1957, he was director of the Office of Economic Affairs for the United States mission to the North Atlantic Treaty Organization and later was a member of the United States delegation to the United Nations.
        Mr. Payne was also chairman of the board of the American University of Cairo and was a former director of the Greenfield Tap and Die Corporation; West Virginia Coal and Coke, and Black, Starr & Gorham.
        He graduated in 1924 from Princeton University.
        Surviving are a daughter, Pamela Patterson of Stonington, Conn.; a sister, Caroline Barnes of Manhattan; three grandchildren, and two great-grandchildren.

        David Glaser, a former principal of the Elisabeth Irwin High School in Manhattan, died of a heart attack Thursday at his home in Hyde Park, N.Y. He was 64 years old.
        Mr. Glaser began teaching at the private school in 1961, was appointed principal in 1971 and retired in 1976. He was also director of Camp Trywoodie, a coeducational camp in Hyde Park, for 30 years, and a board member of the American Camping Association.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Rima Rothman; a son, Jed, and a sister, Mildred Levy, both of Brooklyn, and three grandsons.

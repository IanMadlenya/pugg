        Dr. Harold S. Leopold, a psychiatrist and former director of group psychotherapy at the Bronx Psychiatric Center, died of emphysema yesterday at the New Rochelle Medical Center. He was 88 years old and lived in Mamaroneck, N.Y.
        He held the Bronx Psychiatric Center post for about 10 years before retiring in the mid-1970's. From 1950 to last year, Dr. Leopold had a private psychiatric practice in Manhattan and Mamaroneck.
        Dr. Leopold, who was born in Barchfeld, East Germany, graduated from the University of Wurzburg and came to this country in 1936. He was a graduate of the Postgraduate Center for Psychotherapy in New York.
        He taught group therapy at the Postgraduate Center and was an associate clinical professor at the Albert Einstein College of Medicine from 1957 to the mid-1970's.
        Surviving are his wife, Edith; two daughters, Marion, of Montreal, and Ingrid Blanco of Mamaroneck, and two granddaughters.

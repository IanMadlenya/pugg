        Liet. Gen. Donald Harry Cowles, retired, an Army deputy chief of staff in the mid-1970's, died Saturday at Alexandria Hospital. He was 71 years old.
        General Cowles was a native of Westfield, Mass. He served in the Army in World War II in Europe, and also served in West Germany in the Berlin crisis in 1961. He held staff posts in Vietnam from 1970 to 1972, and as headquarters chief of staff of the Military Assistance Command helped direct the withdrawal of United States forces from Vietnam.
        He was promoted to lieutenant general in 1972 and was named an Army deputy chief of staff. He retired in 1975 as deputy chief of staff for operations and plans.
        After his retirement he joined the BDM Corporation, now a division of Ford Motor Company, in McLean. He retired as a company vice president in 1984.

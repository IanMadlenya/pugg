        Jacob M. Schein, who headed a manufacturer and distributor of generic drugs and supplies for the medical, dental and veterinary professions, died of cancer Saturday at his home in Pound Ridge, N.Y. He was 49 years old.
        He was chairman of Henry Schein Inc. and of Schein Pharmaceutical Inc.
        Mr. Schein, a graduate of Alfred University and Fordham Law School, had served as vice president for legal affairs of the Commodity Exchange and as a vice president of the Wall Street firms of Reynolds Securities, now Dean Witter Reynolds, and du Pont Glore Forgan before joining the Schein family business in 1979.
        He was a director of the Orphan Drug Foundation, which develops and provides pharmaceuticals for people suffering from rare diseases, and of the Generic Pharmaceutical Industry Association.
        He is survived by his wife, Pamela Joseph; his mother, Esther Schein of Jamaica, Queens; a daughter, Pamela, of Manhattan; two stepsons, Charles H. Welles 5th and M. Gaetano Welles of Pound Ridge, and a brother, Marvin, of Oyster Bay, L.I.

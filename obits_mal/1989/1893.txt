        William F. Poten, a retired ship broker in Manhattan who specialized in chartering oil tankers, died Tuesday at his home in St. Simons Island, Ga., after a long illness. He was 79 years old.
        Shortly after graduating from the Woodrow Wilson School at Princeton University in 1931, Mr. Poten became a ship broker with D. B. Dearborn in Manhattan. In World War II he was general manager of War Emergency Tankers Inc. and supplied oil to naval vessels in the Atlantic, the Pacific and the Mediterranean. He was later founder and president of Poten & Partners Inc. He retired in 1970. He is survived by his wife, Clara; two daughters, Constance, of Missoula, Mont., and Ann Martin of Atlanta; and two sons, Peter, of Elizabeth Bay, Australia, and William Jr., of Bergenfield, N.J.; a sister, Carol Minelli of Cleveland, and 10 grandchildren.

        Ross Reid, a lawyer and business executive, died on Monday at New York Hospital after a long illness. He was 72 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Reid was a former vice president and general counsel of Beech-Nut Life Savers, which merged with the Squibb Corporation in 1968. He later became a senior vice president, general counsel and secretary of Squibb. He also served on the company's board, retiring in 1984.
        He was a member of the board of Allegheny Power System Inc. and a trustee of the Emigrant Savings Bank and of Whitman College. He was a past board chairman for the American Heart Association and received its Gold Heart Award. He was also a fellow of the American Bar Foundation.
        He was a graduate of Whitman College and received his law degree from Northwestern University in 1942. After serving in the Army Air Corps, he joined the law firm of Root, Clark, Buckner & Ballantine, now Dewey Ballantine, Bushby, Palmer & Wood, in Manhattan.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Marney Meeker, and two grandchildren.

        J. Harold Merrick, a lawyer and former corporate executive, died of congestive heart failure Monday at Danbury (Conn.) Hospital. He was 90 years old and lived in Ridgefield, Conn.
        Mr. Merrick, a 1925 graduate of Harvard Law School, was the retired vice president and general counsel of the Rheem Manufacturing Company of New York and San Francisco. Previously he was an official with International Telephone and Telegraph of South America, Remington Rand, Schick Shavers, and the Postal Telegraph Company. During World War II he was counsel to the British Purchasing Commission.
        After his retirement in 1948, Mr. Merrick practiced law in Ridgefield. He was also a charter member of the New York Young Republican Club.
        He is survived by his wife, Helene; two sons, N. Munro, of Del Mar, Calif., and David, of Seattle, and five grandchildren.

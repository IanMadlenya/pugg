        John C. Franklin, a retired executive with the General Dynamics Corporation and a number of other corporations, died Monday at his home in Winter Park, Fla., after a brief illness. He was 84 years old.
        Mr. Franklin, a native of Palo Alto, Calif., joined General Dynamics in 1967 and retired in 1970 as vice president of corporate development. Before joining General Dynamics he was a general partner in Lazard Freres, the investment banking firm, Earlier he was vice president of Trans World Airlines, manager of the Oak Ridge operation of the United States Atomic Energy Commission in 1948 and 1949, vice president of the General Aniline and Film Corporation and assistant group executive of the Bendix Aviation Corporation.
        He was a graduate of Stanford University and received a master's from the Graduate School of Business Administration at Harvard University in 1929.
        Mr. Franklin is survived by his wife, the former Helen Firstbrook; two sons from his first marriage, to Francis Snyder, Dr. Peter Scott Franklin of Parkman, Ohio, and Edward Curtis Franklin of Rockland, Me., and two grandchildren. His second wife was Ruth Wagreen.

        Dr. Michael D. Williams, a cardiologist who practiced in Harlem for more than 40 years, died of complications of heart disease yesterday at the St. Luke's-Roosevelt Hospital Medical Center. He was 74 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Dr. Williams was director of the medical clinics at Harlem Hospital when he retired in 1986. He was also a clinical professor at the Columbia University College of Physicians and Surgeons.
        Dr. Williams received his medical degree from Howard University in 1942 and did his internship and residency at Harlem Hospital. He was a captain in the Medical Corps in World War II, assigned to a hospital in Tuskegee, Ala.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Dolores Clarke; a daughter, Saundra Williams-Cornwell of Brooklyn, a son, M. Steven, of Manhattan, and a grandchild.

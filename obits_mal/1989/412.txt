        James G. O'Hara, a Democrat who represented a district from suburban Detroit in the House of Representatives from 1959 through 1976, died of complications of cancer Monday at George Washington University Medical Center. He was 63 years old.
        At his death Mr. O'Hara was a lobbyist and lawyer with the firm of Patton, Boggs & Blow. He joined the firm in Washington in 1977 after losing a Senate primary election to Donald Riegle, who still holds the Senate seat.
        The first Democrat to represent the Thumb region north of Detroit in Congress since 1895, he generally supported liberal, pro-labor causes, including the Great Society programs of the Johnson Administration.
        He was chairman of the House subcommittee on postsecondary education and served in 1972 as parliamentarian at the Democratic National Convention.
        Born in Washington, he moved to Detroit with his family when he was 13. He earned bachelor's and law degrees from the University of Michigan after serving as a combat paratrooper in World War II. He practiced law in Detroit before entering Congress.
        Survivors include his wife, Susan, five sons and two daughters.

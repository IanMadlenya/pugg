        Frederick A. Bush 3d, an oil executive and consultant, died Monday of leukemia at the Greenwich Woods Health Care Center in Greenwich, Conn. He was 85 years old and lived in Greenwich.
        Mr. Bush, who was born in Silver City, N.M., graduated from Stanford University. He spent more than 40 years with the Sinclair Oil Corporation, now part of ARCO, and retired in 1968 as chairman of the Sinclair International Oil Company, a subsidiary of Sinclair Oil.
        Mr. Bush, a conservationist, was a former member of the executive committee and board of advisers of the paleontology section of the American Museum of Natural History. He was a member of the Explorers Club, the New York Academy of Science and Societe Geologique de France.
        Surviving are his wife, Margaret; a daughter, Margaret E. Brooks of Rye, N.Y., and two grandchildren.

        The Rev. Dr. Charles T. Fritsch, a biblical scholar and former professor of Old Testament literature at Princeton Theological Seminary, died Monday at his home in Jamesburg, N.J., after a long illness. He was 76 years old.
        A 1935 graduate of Princeton Theological Seminary, Dr. Fritsch earned his Ph.D. at Princeton University in 1940. He spent his entire teaching career at the seminary, joining the staff as an instructor in 1937 and retiring as the William Henry Green Professor of Old Testament Literature in 1979.
        Dr. Fritsch led many expeditions to the Holy Land and was an archeologist with a 1960 group that studied artifacts found in the ancient harbor of Caesarea.
        He is survived by his wife, Eleanor; a son, C. Theodore, of Doylestown, Pa.; a daughter, Susan Faber of Middlebury, Conn.; a sister, Dorothy Bortz of Palmerton, Pa., and three grandchildren.

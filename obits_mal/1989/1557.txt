        Charles E. Robbins, a co-founder and first president of the Atomic Industrial Forum, an industry association seeking peaceful uses of atomic energy, died Wednesday at a retirement center in Sarasota, Fla., after a brief illness. He was 83 years old and had recently moved to Sarasota from Westport, Conn.
        Mr. Robbins, who helped found the forum in the late 1940's, was a former member of the national advertising staff of The New York Times and had been with Bozell & Jacobs, a New York advertising firm.
        He is survived by his wife, Pauline Frederick, former United Nations correspondent for NBC; a son, Charles E. Jr., of Haddonfield, N.J.; a sister, Helen L'Fallotte of Oklahoma City, and two grandsons.

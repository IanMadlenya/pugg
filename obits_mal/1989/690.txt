        Alfred Rice, a prominent Manhattan lawyer who numbered Ernest Hemingway and E. E. Cummings among his clients, died of kidney failure Wednesday at Long Island Jewish Hospital in Queens. He was 81 years old and lived in Roslyn, L.I.
        Mr. Rice was a graduate of Fordham University Law School. He specialized in copyright law and worked closely with many writers. He handled all of Hemingway's literary, radio and television and motion picture properties. He began representing Hemingway in 1944, often traveling to Cuba to meet with his client.
        Mr. Rice was a trustee of the Hospital for Joint Diseases Orthopaedic Institute in Manhattan for 16 years and was president for eight years.
        He is survived by his wife, Pearl, and a son, Philip, of Jamaica Estates, Queens.

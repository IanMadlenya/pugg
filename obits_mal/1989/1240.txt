        Dr. Victor Hugo Raisman, an orthopedic surgeon in Brooklyn and Queens for 50 years, died of respiratory failure on May 29 at a convalescent home in West Palm Beach, Fla. He was 84 years old and lived in Kew Gardens and West Palm Beach.
        Dr. Raisman had been on the staffs of the Hospital for Joint Diseases and Brooklyn Jewish and Trinity Hospitals. He had also been acting director at the Queens Hospital Center and had been on the staffs of Jamaica, Kew Gardens and Parkway Hospitals.
        He was an alumnus of the New York University-Bellevue Hospital Medical School.
        Surviving are his wife, Mira; a daughter, Marjorie Feder of Berkeley, Calif.; a son, Dr. Arthur, of San Rafael, Calif., and a granddaughter.

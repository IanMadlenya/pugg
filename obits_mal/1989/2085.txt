        William Randall Crawford Jr., a lawyer in New York City, died of cancer on Wednesday at his home in New Canaan, Conn. He was 90 years old.
        Mr. Crawford was a senior partner at Crawford & Reed from 1937 to 1968. He was of counsel to the firms of Kramer, Marx, Greenlee & Backus from 1968 to 1974, and Windels, Marx, Davies & Ives from 1974 to 1981.
        At the time of his death, he was chairman of the board of Somat Corporation of Pomeroy, Pa., which makes waste-handling equipment.
        Born in Chicago on Aug. 22, 1899, Mr. Crawford graduated from the University of Washington in 1923. In 1922, he was selected All-Pacific Coast basketball player.
        He is survived by his wife, Elizabeth Broward Crawford of New Canaan; three daughters, Dorcas Crawford Casey of New Canaan, Catherine Crawford Pike of New Canaan and Wilmington, Del., and Caroline Crawford Maryan of Jakarta, Indonesia; a son, William Randall Crawford 3d of Long Beach, Calif., and nine grandchildren.

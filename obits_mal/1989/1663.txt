        Robert M. Gray, an advertising executive who devised the ''Tiger in Your Tank'' gasoline campaign for Exxon in the mid-1960's, died Friday at the Twelve Oaks Hospital in Houston. He was 85 years old and lived in Houston.
        Mr. Gray, a native of Indianapolis, was advertising sales manager of the Humble Oil and Refining Company, Exxon's forerunner, from 1961 to 1967. Earlier, he was manager of advertising and sales promotion for the Esso Standard Oil Company for 16 years.
        He was a former chairman of the Advertising Federation of America and a past president of the Advertising Club of New York.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Marjorie Alspaugh; two daughters, Gay Roane of Houston and Ann Alspaugh of Oklahoma City; six grandchildren, and two great-grandchildren.

        Charles M. Shapp, a retired New York City school superintendent, died of a heart attack Tuesday in Marbella, Spain, where he lived. He was 82 years old.
        Mr. Shapp was born in New York City and graduated from the City College of New York. He worked in the city's school system for 40 years, as a teacher, principal and a superintendent in districts in Harlem and the Bronx. He retired in 1972.
        He was co-author of 42 children's books in the ''Let's Find Out About'' series, published by Franklin Watts Inc.
        Surviving are his wife, Martha; a son, Mark, of Chicago; a stepson, William Glauber of Salt Point, N.Y., and a brother, David, of Margate, Fla.

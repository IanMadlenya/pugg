        Edmond P. Bartnett, a New York reporter and editor for more than 51 years, died Saturday at the New Rochelle Hospital Medical Center after a long illness. He was 98 years old and lived in New Rochelle.
        Mr. Bartnett was an assistant departmental editor of The New York World-Telegram & Sun when he retired in 1965. A native New Yorker, he became night editor of The New York Sun in 1918 and city editor in 1920, at age 28. In 1950, The Sun was bought by The New York World-Telegram.
        He is survived by a daughter, Doris White of New Rochelle, two grandchildren and two great-grandchildren.

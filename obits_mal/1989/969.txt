        Walter H. Blucher, an authority on city planning who was a former director of the American Society of Planning Officials, died of bone cancer April 20 at his home in Napa, Calif. He was 88 years old.
        Mr. Blucher, a graduate of the University of Wisconsin who earned a law degree from the Detroit College of Law, was a member of the Detroit City Plan Commission from 1925 to 1934. He was director of the American Society of Planning Officials and of the Association of State Planning and Development Agencies.
        An expert on hospital and school planning, he lectured at many universities and was a planning consultant to communities around the nation.
        He is survived by his wife, Phyllis; two sons, Mark, of Danby, Vt., and Stephen, of Colorado Springs; and two grandsons.

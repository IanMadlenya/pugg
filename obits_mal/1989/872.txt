        Harold Mager, a United States Treasury official in World War II and a State Department planner in the postwar period, died of kidney disease Tuesday at Roosevelt Hospital. He was 81 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Mager, a graduate of the University of North Carolina who earned a law degree at New York University, was an assistant to Treasury Secretary Henry Morgenthau in the war finance program.
        After the war he was director of public relations for the Federation of Jewish Philanthropies before returning to Government service in the early 50's on the planning staff of the Point Four foreign-aid program.
        He planned the first statewide program for the aging as a special assistant to Gov. W. Averell Harriman of New York in 1955 and later was assistant to the president of the Colonial Penn Insurance Company.
        Mr. Mager is survived by his wife, the former Naomi Levinson; a son, Ezra, of Manhattan; a brother, Murray, of Long Beach, L.I.; a sister, Rose Ettinger of Southbury, Conn., and three grandchildren.

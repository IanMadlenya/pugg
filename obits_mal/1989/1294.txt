        Dr. Charles A. Weymuller, a pediatrician and professor at the former Long Island College of Medicine for several decades, died in his sleep Saturday at his home in Albuquerque, N.M., where he lived in retirement. He was 92 years old.
        Dr. Weymuller lived and practiced in Brooklyn Heights from 1924 to 1970 and was director of pediatrics at Long Island College Hospital. He wrote research papers on children's diseases and was a founding member of the Pediatric Research Society.
        After retiring from the medical school, Dr. Weymuller moved to Albuquerque, where he taught pediatrics at the University of New Mexico Medical School.
        He was born in Omaha. Dr. Weymuller received bachelor's and medical degrees from the University of Nebraska and did research at Harvard and Cornell.
        Surviving are his wife, Caroline; two daughters, Margaret Menger of Ramona, Calif., and Carol Wellman of Wilton, Conn.; a son, Frederick, of Rochester; a brother, Ernest, of Manhasset, L.I.; a sister, Helen Kroehler; seven grandchildren, and three great-grandchildren.

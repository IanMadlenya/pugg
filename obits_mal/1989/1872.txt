        R. Nelson Rose, an engineer whose knowledge of rust inhibitors helped preserve equipment salvaged from the French liner Normandie after she sank in New York Harbor Feb. 9, 1942, died of a pulmonary embolism Sunday at Stamford (Conn.) Hospital. He was 87 years old and lived in Darien, Conn.
        Mr. Rose, a graduate of Columbia University, worked for the Colonial Beacon Oil Company, a subsidary of Standard Oil of New Jersey, at the time of salvage operations. He retired in 1967 as marketing coordinator for the international division of Esso, now known as Exxon.
        He is survived by his wife, Jane; two sons, Robert, of Singapore, and Donald, of Richmond, Va.; a sister, Kathryn Kerr of Hackettstown, N.J., and two stepdaughters, Hilary Gripekovem of Portland, Ore., and Mercer Field of Westport, Conn.

        George Pope MacNichol Jr., a former president of Libbey-Owens-Ford, the glass company, died of pneumonia Tuesday at his home in Perrysburg, Ohio. He was 90 years old.
        Mr. MacNichol attended the University of Michigan and Yale University before joining the Edward Ford Plate Glass Company in 1920 as an assistant treasurer.
        He became treasurer and later sales manager of the company. After it merged with the Libbey-Owens Glass Company in 1930 he was named a vice president. He became president of Libbey-Owens-Ford in 1953 and was chairman of the executive committee when he retired in 1965. He was a director of many companies, including Goodyear Tire and Rubber and Aramco Steel.
        He is survived by two sons, George 3d, of Perrysburg, and Roland, of Old Lyme, Conn.; a daughter, Carol Orser, of Perrysburg; a sister, Laura Webb, of Darien, Conn.; nine grandchildren, and nine great-grandchildren.

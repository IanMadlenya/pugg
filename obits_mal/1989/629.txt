        Jacob Goodstein, a lawyer and certified public accountant, died of complications following a hernia operation Thursday at Overlook Hospital in Summit, N.J. He was 84 years old and lived in Millburn, N.J.
        Mr. Goodstein, a graduate of New York University and the New Jersey Law School, was associated for more than 45 years with the international accounting concern of S. D. Leidesdorf & Company of Manhattan, now known as Ernst & Whinney. He retired in 1974.
        He had been president of the New Jersey YM/YWHA Camps and the Jewish Community Foundation of MetroWest. He was also a former vice president of the National Jewish Welfare Board and a member of the American Jewish Committee.
        He is survived by his wife, Evelyn; a son, Daniel, and a daughter, Claire Gering, both of Los Angeles; two sisters, Anna Johnes of Margate, Fla., and Ruth Markowitz of Beechhurst, L.I.; 10 grandchildren, and four great-grandchildren.

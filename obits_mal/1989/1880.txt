        John A. McLaughlin, chairman emeritus of the biology department at Fordham University, died Tuesday at Southside Hospital in Bay Shore, L.I., after a long illness. He was 65 years old and lived in Oakdale, L.I.
        Dr. McLaughlin, a microbiologist, joined the faculty as department chairman in 1964. He was the director of a study of ecological conditions in the Bering Sea conducted by the United States and the Soviet Union in 1977. He retired in 1984.
        Dr. McLaughlin was founding director of the Louis Calder Conservation and Ecology Study Center, affiliated with Fordham, in Armonk, N.Y., where he lived until August. The center is devoted to ecological and conservation studies.
        He was born in Queens and graduated from St. Francis College in 1950. He received a master's degree from St. John's University and a doctorate from New York University.
        Dr. McLaughlin is survived by his wife, the former Margaret Smith; four sons, John Jr., of Sparks, Nev., Mark and Joseph, both of Armonk, and Paul, of Dunedin, Fla.; two daughters, Patricia Velucci of Queens and Therese, of Jersey City; two brothers, and two grandchildren.

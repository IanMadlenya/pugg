        Lester C. Hopton, a former president of the Ingersoll-Rand Company, manufacturers of heavy machinery and industrial equipment, died Monday in Boca Raton, Fla. He was 87 years old and lived in Boca Raton.
        Mr. Hopton joined the accounting department of Ingersoll-Rand, which has its headquarters in Woodcliff Lake, N.J., in 1926, while an undergraduate at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology. He was promoted to comptroller and became president in 1955. He retired in 1967 but remained on the company's board until 1974.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Phylis Pack; two children, Lester C. Jr., of Moorestown, N.J., and Elsie MacKethan of Grosse Pointe Farms, Mich.; four grandchildren, and two great-grandchildren.

        Dana Converse Backus, a retired lawyer, died of Alzheimer's disease Sunday at St. Francis Hospital in Roslyn, L.I. He was 82 years old and lived in Manhattan and Sands Point, L.I.
        Mr. Backus was a partner in the Manhattan firm of Windels, Marx, Davies and Ives. He retired in 1981.
        He was born in Bayonne, N.J., and graduated from Harvard College and Harvard Law School.
        He worked as a lawyer for the Secretariat at the founding conference of the United Nations in San Francisco in 1945, and was a former vice president of the Citizens Union.
        Surviving are four daughters, Mary Louise Rankin of Washington, Janet Stason of Colebrook, Conn., Elizabeth Girard of Germantown, Md., and Harriet Todd of Lincoln, Mass., and seven grandchildren.

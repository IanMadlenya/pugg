        Abraham P. Conan, a lawyer who specialized in labor and immigration matters and helped European refugees after World War II, died of pneumonia on July 26 following a stroke at his home in Manhattan. He was 91 years old.
        Mr. Conan helped refugees in postwar Germany as part of the United States Displaced Persons Commission. He held Federal Government posts dealing with immigration and displaced persons from 1939 to 1953. He later served as educational and political director for Local 66 of the International Ladies Garment Workers Union.
        Mr. Conan is survived by two daughters, Laura, of Manhattan and Julie Freestone of Berkeley, Calif; a stepson, David Geliebter of Philadelphia and step-daughter Amy Cameron of Brooklyn; a grandson and five step-grandchildren.

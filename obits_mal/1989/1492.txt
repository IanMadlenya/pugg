        Otto Heller, founder and chairman of Lace of France, a Manhattan-based lace manufacturing and importing company, died last Thursday at United Hospital in Port Chester, N.Y., after a long illness. He was 76 years old and lived in Rye Brook, N.Y.
        Mr. Heller, a native of Vienna, came to the United States in 1938 and founded Lace of France in 1953. He was also active in the United Jewish Appeal.
        Mr. Heller is survived by his wife, the former Rita Tschaikowsky; two sons, Dr. Jakob, of Fort Lee, N.J., and Martin, of Manhattan; a daughter, Cynthia Knoll of New Rochelle, N.Y., and two grandsons.

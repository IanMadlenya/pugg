        Henry L. Ughetta 2d, a former partner in the law firm of Wilkie Farr & Gallagher, died Tuesday at his home in Scarsdale, N.Y. He was 63 years old and had suffered from Alzheimer's disease for 11 years.
        Mr. Ughetta, who specialized in trusts and estates, was associated with the Manhattan-based firm for 26 years. He became a partner in 1961 and retired in 1978. He had been associate counsel with the Long Island Railroad Commission, and the New York State Special Legislative Committee on Integrity and Ethical Standards in Government.
        Mr. Ughetta graduated from Princeton University and received a law degree from Columbia Law School in 1949.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Mary Alice Andre; his mother, Casper B. Ughetta of Rye, N.Y.; five sons, Henry L. 3d, of Scarsdale, Charles, of Chappaqua, N.Y., James, of Lawrenceville, N.J., John, of Houston, and Richard, of Los Angeles; four daughters, Mary of Manhattan, Alice Bussiere of Wilmington, Del., Nancy Thomas of Scarsdale, and Anne Lyons of Lawrenceville; a brother, William C., of Corning, N.Y.; a sister, Marrianne Brew of Mount Kisco, N.Y., and seven grandchildren.

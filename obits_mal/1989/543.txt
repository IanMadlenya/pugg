        Charles L. Mendelson, a certified public accountant who was active in New York civic affairs, died Thursday at his home in Brooklyn. He was 67 years old.
        Mr. Mendelson, a native of Brooklyn, joined the New York City Department of Housing, Preservation and Development in the 1950's and helped establish the state's Mitchell-Lama housing program. He retired in the 1970's and worked to promote legislation for low- and moderate-income housing and for equity in the tax system.
        He is survived by a daughter, Jill, of the Bronx; a son, Dan, of Baltimore, and three grandchildren.

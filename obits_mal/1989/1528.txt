        Arthur Rutzen, a Federal Bureau of Investigation agent who later became a Commerce Department official, died of an aneurism Thursday at the Boca Raton Community Hospital in Florida. He was 79 years old and lived in Delray Beach, Fla.
        Mr. Rutzen, a longtime resident of Manhasset Hills, L.I., graduated from the Fordham Law School and the bureau Academy in 1935.
        He was a special agent-in-charge of the offices in Miami, Milwaukee, Springfield, Ill., New Orleans and St. Paul from 1935 to 1942, and was an instructor at the National Police Academy in Quantico, Va.
        In World War II he was involved in the bureau's counterespionage effort.
        Mr. Rutzen later entered private business and was director of international operations for the Wurlitzer Company for 13 years. In 1962 he was named director of the New York regional office of the Department of Commerce, a post he held for 21 years.
        Mr. Rutzen is survived by his wife, the former Helen Doyle; a son, Arthur Jr. of Piedmont, Calif.; a stepson, Barry Knox of New Canaan, Conn., and four grandchildren.

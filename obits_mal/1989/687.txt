        Hurst R. Anderson, president of American University from 1952 to 1968, died of bone cancer Wednesday at the Edward White Hospital in St. Petersburg, Fla. He was 84 years old and lived in St. Petersburg.
        Mr. Anderson served as president of Centenary Junior College and of Hamline University in St. Paul, Minn., before assuming the presidency of American University in Washington, D.C.
        He was born in Cleveland and was a graduate of Ohio Wesleyan University and received a law degree from the University of Michigan and master's degrees from Northwestern and the University of Chicago. He was on the faculty of Allegheny College from 1928 to 1943.
        Dr. Anderson is survived by his wife, the former Marian Powell, whom he married in 1932; a son, Powell, of Rochester; a daughter, Kathleen, of Takoma Park, Md; a brother, Paul, of Philadelphia; five grandchildren and four great-grandchildren.

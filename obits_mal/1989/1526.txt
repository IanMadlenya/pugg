        Walter W. Belson, retired assistant to the president and director of public relations for the American Trucking Associations in Washington, died of a stroke Tuesday at his home in Bethesda, Md. He was 87 years old.
        Mr. Belson, a native of Hartford, Wis., was active in the trucking industry for more than 30 years. From 1933 to 1940 he was managing director of the Wisconsin Motor Carriers Association. In 1934, he was elected charter secretary of the newly formed American Trucking Associations and later served with the Office of Defense Transportation in Chicago.
        In 1943 he rejoined the American Trucking Associations in Washington as director of public relations. He remained with the organization until retiring in 1967.
        He was a former national president of the Public Relations Society of America.
        A widower, he is survived by a son, Judge James A. Belson of the District of Columbia Court of Appeals; two daughters, Elizabeth Flynn of New Canaan, Conn., and Barbara Sullivan of Potomac, Md., and 20 grandchildren.

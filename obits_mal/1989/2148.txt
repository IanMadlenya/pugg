        Vivian Mercier, an Irish literary critic and teacher whose published works included a major study on the work of Samuel Beckett, died Saturday in London, The Irish Times reported today. He was 70 years old. The cause of death was not announced.
        Mr. Mercier taught at New York University, the University of Colorado at Boulder and the University of California at Santa Barbara. He was the author of ''The Irish Comic Tradition,'' ''A Reader's Guide to the French New Novel'' and ''Samuel Beckett,'' among other works.
        Mr. Mercier was also co-editor of an anthology, ''A Thousand Years of Irish Prose,'' and was at work on a ''A New Critical History of Anglo-Irish Literature.'' He was a frequent contributor to The New York Times Book Review, among other publications.
        He is survived by his wife, the novelist Eilis Dillon, and three children from his first marriage.
        Correction:  November 17, 1989, Friday, Late Edition - Final
        An obituary by The Associated Press on Nov. 7 about Vivian Mercier, an Irish literary critic and teacher, misidentified an American institution where he taught. It was the City University of New York, not New York University.

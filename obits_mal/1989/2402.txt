        Osmund A. Leahy, a retired Army major general, died of cancer Saturday at a nursing home in Bethesda, Md. He was 74 years old and lived in Chevy Chase, Md.
        A 1940 graduate of the United States Military Academy, General Leahy served with the 82d Airborne Division in World War II as a battalion commander in North Africa and Europe.
        His later assignments included command of a regiment of the 82d Airborne and chief of personnel services in Washington. By 1964 he had risen to brigadier general and commanded a military training mission to Saudi Arabia. In 1967 he was named a major general and took command of the II Army Corps at Fort Wadsworth, Staten Island. He was deputy commanding general of the 6th Army at the Presidio in San Francisco from 1970 to 1972 and retired in 1977.
        He is survived by his wife, Patricia; two sons, James and Thomas, both of San Francisco; two daughters, Elizabeth of San Francisco and Margaret Vastola of Brooklyn; a sister, Marie, and a brother, Eugene, both of Fort Lauderdale, Fla., and four grandchildren.

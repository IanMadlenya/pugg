        Harold William Conroy, a lawyer specializing in corporate law, died of a disease of the cartilage on Saturday at his home in Southhold, L.I. He was 82 years old.
        Mr. Conroy was a partner in the law firm of Cadwalader, Wickersham & Taft in Manhattan before retiring in 1972. He remained of counsel.
        He had been a trustee of Manhattan Eye, Ear and Throat Hospital and Eastern Long Island Hospital. He was also secretary and trustee of the John LaPorte and Irene Heinz Given Foundation and a director of the Y.M.C.A. in Port Chester, N.Y.
        He graduated from the Fordham University Law School in 1932.
        Surviving are his wife, Ruth; a daughter, Virginia Tabor, of Orient, L.I.; a son, William, of Southhold; four grandchildren, and two great-grandchildren.

        Pardo Frederick DelliQuadri, who was the dean of several schools of social work, died of congestive heart failure Thursday at his home in Los Angeles. He was 74 years old.
        Mr. DelliQuadri, a 1941 graduate of the University of Nebraska with a master's degree in social work, was dean of the University of Alabama School of Social Work from 1972 until his retirement in 1981. Before that he was dean of the schools of social work at the Universities of Hawaii and Wisconsin.
        From 1961 to 1967, he was dean of the New York School for Social Work at Columbia University. Before that, he was director of the division for children and youth at the Wisconsin State Department of Public Welfare for 10 years and headed government welfare agencies in Wyoming and Illinois.
        He was the United States representative to the United Nations Children's Fund (UNICEF) under President John F. Kennedy and chief of the children's bureau of the Department of Health, Education and Welfare under President Lyndon B. Johnson.
        He is survived by his wife, Velma; three daughters, Toni and Geri, both of Los Angeles, and Lyn of Chicago, and four grandchildren.

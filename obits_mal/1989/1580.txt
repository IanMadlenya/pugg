        Dr. Robert S. Hotchkiss, a retired New York urologist, died of pneumonia Monday at Danbury (Conn.) Hospital. He was 86 years old and lived in New Fairfield, Conn.
        Dr. Hotchkiss was professor and chairman of the Department of Urology at New York University Post-Graduate Medical School, a unit of the Bellevue Medical Center, from 1949 until his retirement in 1972. As such he was in charge of all urological research, teaching and patient care at the center. He was among the first to treat male infertility as a specialty and, in 1950, he established the city's first artificial kidney unit, a precursor to dialysis.
        From 1932 to 1936, Dr. Hotchkiss was a member of the N.Y.U. faculty. He left to become associate professor of Surgery at Cornell University Medical College.
        He served as an officer in the Navy from 1942 to 1946.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Ella Parr; a daughter, Erica Smith of Sonora, Calif.; a son, Sherman, of Greenwich, Conn., and two grandchildren.

        Thomas Jackson Oakley Rhinelander, a real-estate broker and a member of a prominent family in New York, died of complications of Parkinson's disease on Saturday at the Oakwood Healthcare Center in Newport, R.I. He was 69 years old and lived in Newport.
        Mr. Rhinelander negotiated the purchase of the six-story Dodge Mansion at Fifth Avenue and 61st Street, a coveted parcel, in 1975. He had also been a director of the Rhinelander Real Estate Company, a family organization that was sold in 1962.
        Mr. Rhinelander joined the real-estate concern of Douglas L. Elliman & Company after World War II and was named a vice president in 1956. He was one of the company's youngest directors. When Douglas Elliman was sold in 1964, Mr. Rhinelander was associated with the Arthur Hardgrove Company and continued commercial brokerage until retiring in 1972.
        He attended Princeton University and left after his sophomore year to join the Army Air Forces. He was a captain in Burma in World War II, Surviving are his wife, Tatiana; two daughters, Jeannine Schoeffer of West Stockbridge, Mass., and Serena, of Manhattan; a brother, LeBrun R. McKnight of Manhattan, and three grandchildren.

        Allan Meltzer, a press agent who began his career representing the stage productions of Orson Welles and John Houseman for the Federal Theater Project, died of heart disease on Saturday in Key Biscayne, Fla. He was 77 years old.
        Born in Worcester, Mass., Mr. Meltzer came to New York in 1935. In addition to promoting Federal Theater productions, he also worked for the CBS Artists Bureau. During World War II he worked in Washington, where he publicized a Government-sponsored network radio show, ''This Is War.'' After the war, he worked for Warner Brothers before beginning his own public relations agency.
        He is survived by his wife, Helen; a daughter, Pat Tourk Lee; two brothers, Milton and Marshall, and two grandchildren.

        Alan Bowne, a playwright and author, died of AIDS on Sunday at his home outside Petaluma, Calif. He was 44 years old.
        William Alan Bowne wrote several plays that were produced off Broadway, including ''Forty-Deuce,'' ''The Beany and Cecil Show,'' ''Sharon and Billy'' and ''Beirut.'' He adapted ''Forty-Deuce,'' about the subculture of 42d Street, as a film and also wrote ''Mixed Blood'' and ''Spike of Bensonhurst'' for the movies. Recently, he completed a novel, ''Wally Wonderstruck.''
        Mr. Bowne is survived by his parents, William and Virginia Bowne, of Santa Ana, Calif., and his sister, Sharon Von Eckern, of Fountain Valley, Calif.
        A memorial service is to be held on Dec. 8 by New Dramatists, of which Mr. Bowne was a member, and the Manhattan Class Company at the Nat Horne Theater, 442 West 42d Street, at 1 P.M.
        Correction:  December 8, 1989, Friday, Late Edition - Final
        An obituary on Saturday about Alan Bowne, a playwright and author, misstated the date of his death. It was Nov. 24.

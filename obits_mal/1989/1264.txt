        Herman D. Bragin, a lawyer for 49 years and a supporter of Jewish causes, died Tuesday at the Parker Geriatrics Institute in Queens. He was 83 years old and lived in Bayside, Queens.
        Mr. Bragin practiced in Manhattan and Rockville Centre, L.I. He was a founder of the Oakland Jewish Center in Bayside and was active in Zionist organizations.
        Mr. Bragin graduated from the Brooklyn Law School in 1930.
        Surviving are a son, David, of Brooklyn; a daughter, Joy Baker of Manhattan; two sisters, Edna Bayer of Delray Beach, Fla., and Cele Gerlich of Manhattan, and five grandchildren.

        Jan V. Mladek, an official of the International Monetary Fund for nearly 30 years, died of bone cancer Monday at George Washington University Hospital in Washington. He was 77 years old and lived in Georgetown.
        Mr. Mladek, a native of Bochnia, Poland, came to the United States in the late 1940's. He joined the I.M.F. in 1948, becoming a director of the European office from 1953 to 1959 and director of the newly established African Department in 1961. In 1964 he became head of the fund's Central Banking Service. He retired in 1977.
        With his wife, Mr. Mladek established a foundation to sponsor works of Central European artists and organized the show ''Expressiv: Central European Art since 1960'' at the Hirshhorn Museum of the Smithsonian Institution in 1987.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Meda Sokol.

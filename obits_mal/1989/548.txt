        Lewis Ketcham Sillcox, a retired mechanical engineer and an authority on transportation, died of pneumonia yesterday at the Samaritan-Keep Home in Watertown, N.Y. He was 102 years old and lived in Watertown.
        Mr. Sillcox began his career in railroading in 1903 and worked as a mechanical engineer for railroads in the United States and Canada before becoming vice president of the New York Air Brake Company in Watertown in 1927. He retired in 1959 and became director of the newly formed New York State Office of Transportation, a post he left in 1960. He was president of the American Society of Mechanical Engineers and received many awards and honorary degrees.
        He is survived by a son, Robert, of King, Ontario; four daughters, Elsie Bridge of Laguna Hills, Calif.; Esther Whittemore of Steamboat Springs, Colo.; Enid Brown of Manhattan and Edith Reiman of Stamford, Conn.; 21 grandchildren, and 22 great-grandchildren.

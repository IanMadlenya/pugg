        Walter D. Floersheimer, an investment banker and philanthropist, died of cancer Monday at his home in Zurich. He was 89 years old and also lived in Palm Beach, Fla.
        Mr. Floersheimer was a senior partner in the Wall Street firm of Sutro Brothers & Company before his retirement in 1962. He was a major benefactor of the American Friends of the Israel Museum in Jerusalem, where the Floersheimer Pavilion for Impressionist and Post-Impressionist art is named for him.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Charlotte Solomon; a son, Stephen, of Zurich; three grandchildren, and one great-grandchild.

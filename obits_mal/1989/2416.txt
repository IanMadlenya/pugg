        Peter Amzi Berla, an advertising and marketing executive, died of cancer yesterday at Stamford (Conn.) Hospital. He was 59 years old and lived in Darien, Conn.
        Mr. Berla, a 1952 alumnus of Cornell University, had been vice president for marketing services for Saab-Scania of America in Orange, Conn., since 1979. Previously, he was an account supervisor at Scali, McCabe & Sloves Inc. in Manhattan, handling the Volvo of America and BMW accounts. From 1973 to 1975 he was with Keenan & McLaughlin Inc. as account supervisor for Alfa Romeo and media director for all accounts.
        He started in advertising with Benton & Bowles after serving in the Air Force, then moved to Ogilvy & Mather before joining Carl Ally Inc., where he worked from 1962 to 1972.
        He is survived by his wife, Harriet; his parents, Harold and Dorothy, of Longboat Key, Fla., and a sister, Susan Perry of Washington.

        Alphonse Riesenfeld, professor emeritus of anthropology at Hunter College, died Tuesday at Mount Sinai Hospital after a series of strokes. He was 77 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Dr. Riesenfeld was born in Germany, graduated from the University of Berlin and received his doctorate from the University of Vienna. After coming to the United States, he was associated with the Wenner-Gren Foundation for Anthropological Research and from 1949 to 1956 was on the staff of the American Museum of Natural History.
        He taught at Hunter from 1951 until his retirement in 1976. He was a member of the City University of New York's doctoral faculty and a Fellow of the American Association of Physical Anthropologists.
        He is survived by a daughter, Vivian McRae of Burbank, Calif., and a grandson.

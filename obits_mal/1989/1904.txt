        Frank F. Blaisdell, a former mayor of Middletown Township in New Jersey and longtime business and community leader in Red Bank, N.J., died at his home Thursday after a long illness. He was 76 years old.
        Mr. Blaisdell was a retired president of his family's business, the Blaisdell Lumber Company. A Republican, he was the mayor of Middletown Township in the 1950's. He also served on the New Jersey Board of Institutional Trustees, which oversees state hospitals and prisons. He served in the Navy during World War II and was a graduate of the Wharton School at the University of Pennsylvania.
        Survivors include his wife, Marie; a son, Bruce F. Blaisdell of Rumson; two daughters, Deane B. Wolfe of Locust and Susan B. Coe of Middletown; a sister, Frances B. Williams of Palo Alto, Calif., and seven grandchildren.

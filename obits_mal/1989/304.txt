        John Patierno, who pioneered development of the B2 Stealth bomber, died Saturday of cancer at Hoag Memorial Hospital in Newport Beach, said a Northrop spokesman, officials of the Northrop Corporation said today. He was 54 and lived in Newport Beach.
        Thomas V. Jones, chairman and chief executive of Northrop, said Mr. Jones, the former corporate vice president and general manager of Northrop's B2 division, spent 17 years developing the designs and technology meant to enable the B2 to fly through enemy radar without being detected.

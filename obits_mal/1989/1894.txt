        Alfred C. Bruce, a retired civil engineer and business executive, died of heart disease Thursday at a nursing home in East Haven, Conn. He was 88 years old and had lived for many years in Southport, Conn.
        Mr. Bruce, a native of Yonkers and a graduate of the United States Naval Academy, supervised the construction of several hydroelectric projects on the East Coast, including the effort to harness the tides of Passamaquoddy Bay between Maine and New Brunswick and flood-control projects on the Susquehanna and Chenango Rivers in upstate New York.
        Mr. Bruce was a former chairman of Pervel Industries, a subsidiary of the Bemis Company, a manufacturer of packaging, and a former vice president of the William E. Nichols Company.
        He is survived by a son, Alfred Jr., of Guilford, Conn.; two daughters, Adele Riepe of Kanigswinter, West Germany, and Margaret Sealander of Wilson, Wyo.; four grandchildren, and one great-grandchild.

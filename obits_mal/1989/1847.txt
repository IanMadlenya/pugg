        James Benton Grant, a lawyer in Manhattan for 40 years and an avid yachtsman, died of leukemia Thursday at Greenwich (Conn.) Hospital. He was 71 years old and lived in Greenwich.
        After serving in the Navy Submarine Service in World War II as a lieutenant commander, Mr. Grant graduated from the Harvard Law School in 1947. He was a partner in the firm of Hale, Grant, Meyerson, O'Brien and McCormick until he retired in 1987. He then served as legal counsel to the firm of Commings and Lockwood in Greenwich.
        He was a trustee of the New York Yacht Club, president of the Yacht Racing Association of Long Island, commodore of the Indian Harbor and Belle Haven yacht clubs in Greenwich, and chairman of the 1986 Newport-Bermuda Race Committee.
        He is survived by his wife, Irina; two daughters, Nanette Burrows of Brussels and Alexandra Bingham of Hawaii; a son, James, of Greenwich, and five grandchildren.

        John Francis Hennessy, a consulting engineer who supervised the design of major buildings in New York City and Washington, died of a heart attack Monday. He was 60 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Hennessy, a graduate of the Massachusetts Institute of Technology, was chairman and chief executive of the consulting firm Syska & Hennessy Inc. He supervised the engineering of such buildings as the Lincoln Center for the Performing Arts, the Jacob K. Javits Convention Center and the John F. Kennedy Center for the Performing Arts in Washington.
        He is survived by a daughter, Kathleen, of Manhattan; five sons, John 3d, James and Peter, all of Manhattan, Kevin, of Walnut Creek, Calif., and David, of Norwalk, Conn., and a brother, Paul, of New Rochelle, N.Y.

        Warren H. Reed, a specialist in management training and a retired vice president of Paine Webber, died of AIDS on Sunday at University Hospital in Stony Brook, L.I. He was 62 years old and lived in Cherry Grove, L.I.
        Mr. Reed, who was born in Brooklyn, received bachelor's and master's degrees from New York University. He served in the Navy in World War II.
        In 1971, after serving as training manager for E. F. Hutton, Mr. Reed joined Paine Webber as director of its national training center, a post he held until 1982. He later was a consultant to Kidder, Peabody.
        For several years Mr. Reed was an adjunct professor at the N.Y.U. School of Business. He was the author of ''Positive Listening,'' published by Franklin Watts in 1985.
        Mr. Reed is survived by a sister, Marge Christian of Seaford, L.I. His longtime companion was Stephen Wells of Cherry Grove.

        Francis J. Purcell, a lawyer who served as New York regional administrator of the Securities and Exchange Commission in the mid-1950's, died of heart failure Thursday at his summer home in Fairfield, Conn. He was 77 years old and lived in Stratford, Conn.
        Mr. Purcell headed the Federal agency's office from December 1953 to July 1955. He had previously been a partner in the New York law firm of Murphy & Purcell, and he resumed practice with the New York firm of Townsend & Lewis.
        He is survived by a son, Francis Jr. of New Milford, Conn.; two daughters, Penny Takacs and Pamela Miller, both of Stratford, and eight granddaughters.

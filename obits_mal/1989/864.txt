        Dr. Robert O'Clair, a poetry editor and a professor of English at Manhattanville College for 22 years, died of a stroke on Monday at Memorial Hospital in Winsted, Conn. He was 66 years old and lived in Sandisfield, Mass.
        Dr. O'Clair was co-editor with Richard Ellmann, the James Joyce scholar, of ''The Norton Anthology of Modern Poetry'' and of ''Modern Poems.'' Last year the Robert O'Clair Prize for Poetry was established in his honor at Manhattanville.
        Dr. O'Clair, who also taught at Harvard University, Lehman College and the State University College at Purchase, N.Y., served as chairman of Manhattanville's English department from 1968 to 1970 and from 1972 to 1974, and was director of the college library from 1978 to 1982. He retired in 1983.
        He is survived by his wife, Margaret, of Sandisfield; a daughter, Hanna, of Pound Ridge, N.Y., and a son, Michael, of Seattle.

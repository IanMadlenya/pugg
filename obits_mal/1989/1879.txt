        Stanley S. Joseloff, a radio and advertising executive, died of leukemia Sunday in Yale-New Haven Hospital in New Haven. He was 82 years old and lived in Branford, Conn.
        Mr. Joseloff, who was a lawyer, had a multi-faceted career. He was a legal adviser to the theatrical producers Lee and J. J. Shubert. He also created ''Be My Guest,'' a travel service that later became part of the American Express Company.
        Mr. Joseloff produced two popular radio programs, ''The Eddie Cantor NBC Radio Hour'' and ''The Life of Riley.'' His Storecast Corporation sent FM broadcasts of music and commercials into hundreds of supermarkets.
        He worked for two advertising firms, heading the radio department at the Brow agency and directing programs and talent for Young & Rubicam.
        He also wrote a song, ''Dear Arabella,'' that was recorded by the Glenn Miller orchestra.
        Mr. Joseloff is survived by his wife, Pearl; two sons, Gordon, of Westport, Conn., and Michael, of Manhattan; a daughter, Jill, of Manhattan, and two sisters, Jenny Beinfield of Fairfield, Conn., and Minnie Malkin of Manhattan.

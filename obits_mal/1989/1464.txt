        Dr. Michael A. Diana, a Manhattan surgeon who served for many years as a volunteer in hospitals in Afghanistan, India and Nicaragua, died of prostate cancer on Sunday at the Medical Center Hospital of Vermont in Burlington. He was 80 years old and lived in Ferrisburg, Vt.
        Dr. Diana, who was born in Manhattan, was a graduate of New York University. He received his medical degree from the New York Medical College at the age of 22, and was a surgeon in private practice for 45 years.
        During World War II, he served as a major in the Army medical corps. While serving as a deacon at Riverside Church in the early 1960's, he became interested in medical missionary work. After serving in Asia and South America, he went to Africa to work with Dr. Albert Schweitzer in Lambarene, Gabon. After Dr. Schweitzer's death, Dr. Diana served on several occasions at the Lambarene hospital and at Schweitzer hospitals in Haiti and Peru.
        At his death, Dr. Diana was vice president of the Albert Schweitzer Fellowship in the United States and a member of the international directorate of Schweitzer associations.
        Dr. Diana is survived by his wife, the former Gretchen Keelan; three sons, Michael, of Manhattan, Stewart, of Baltimore, and Peter, of Boston; a daughter, Gretchen Hernandez of Warren, Vt.; a sister, Eleanor Manfredi of Milford, Conn., and five grandchildren.

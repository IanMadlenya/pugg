        Jack Smith, an independent film maker and theater artist, died of AIDS on Monday at Beth Israel Hospital in Manhattan. He was 57 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        From 1958 to 1964, Mr. Smith's films and performance art were familiar elements of the Pop Art movement. His best-known film was ''Flaming Creatures,'' a 1963 movie about transvestites.
        He is survived by a sister, Mary Sue Slater, of Austin, Tex.
        A memorial service is to be held at Performance Space 122, at 150 First Avenue, at Ninth Street, at 8 P.M. on Oct. 16.

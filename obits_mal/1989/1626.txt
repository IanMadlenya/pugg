        Peter T. Jones, a lawyer, businessman and educator who specialized in international trade, died in a horseback riding accident Tuesday in Aspen, Colo. He was 59 years old and lived in San Francisco.
        At the time of his death Mr. Jones was an adjunct professor at the Walter A. Haas School of Business at the University of California at Berkeley.
        Mr. Jones, a 1957 graduate of Harvard Law School, was Deputy Assistant Secretary of Commerce under Presidents John F. Kennedy and Lyndon B. Johnson and was also an executive in Latin America with the International Telephone and Telegraph Company and W. R. Grace & Company. He later joined Montgomery Ward as general counsel and in 1974 became senior vice president at Levi Strauss & Co. He retired in 1985.
        He is survived by his wife, Elizabeth; a son, Paul, of Putney, Vt., and two daughters, Katherine, of Anchorage, and Sara.

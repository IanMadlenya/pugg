        The Rev. Dr. Hugh Douglas McCandless, rector emeritus of the Episcopal Church of the Epiphany at York Avenue and 74th Street in Manhattan, died of cancer Sunday at his home in Hamden, Conn. He was 81 years old.
        Dr. McCandless was rector from 1945 to 1972, when he retired. Previously, he was rector of Christ Church in Suffern, N.Y., and St. Simons-in-the-Cove on Staten Island. He founded the East Midtown Hospital Chaplaincy and was a trustee of many groups, including the Seamen's Church Institute, St. Barnabas Hospital, the Blind Players Lodge, the Cathedral of St. John the Divine and the Corporation for the Relief of Widows and Orphans.
        He was an alumnus of of Yale University and the Virginia Theological Seminary.
        Surviving are his wife, Dorothy; a daughter, Martha Durkee of Santa Ana Heights, Calif.; a son, Hugh, of Manhattan; five grandchildren, and three great-grandchildren.

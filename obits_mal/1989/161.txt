        Harold H. Goldberg Jr., a realtor who headed the American Jewish Committee in New Jersey, died yesterday at his home in Short Hills, N.J., after a long illness. He was 62 years old.
        Mr. Goldberg was the founder of the Harold H. Goldberg Company, a realty firm based in Irvington, N.J.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Judith Stern; a son, James Leslie of Manhattan; four daughters, JoAnn Wood of Manhattan, Susan Abramowitz of Wilkes Barre, Pa., Karen Beno of Maplewood, N.J., and Kathy Pandorf of Denver; a sister, Jane Grad of Verona, N.J., and 10 grandchildren.

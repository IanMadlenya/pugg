
        Winslow Meston Lovejoy Jr., a lawyer in Manhattan for 35 years, died on Thursday at his home in Southampton, L.I. He was 63 years old and also had a home in Manhattan.
        He died of cancer, a family spokesman said.
        Mr. Lovejoy was of counsel with Curtis, Mallet, Prevost, Colt and Mosely, a firm with which he had been associated for 35 years. He was a 1950 graduate of Yale University and 1954 graduate of Harvard Law School, He was a trustee of Lenox Hill Hospital for more than 30 years. He was also a past president of the Association of the Lighthouse for the Blind, president and treasurer of the Robert Sterling Clark Foundation and a trustee of the Parrish Art Museum.
        He is survived by his wife, Faith; a son, Christopher, of Manhattan; a daughter, Barbara Spring of Concord, Mass; five brothers, John F. Horn and L. Stoddard Horn, both of Manhattan, William Lovejoy of Montclair, N.J., Robert Lovejoy of Novelty, Ohio, and Thomas Lovejoy of Manhattan, and three grandchildren.

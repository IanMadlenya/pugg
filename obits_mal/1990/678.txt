        John G. Harlan Jr., a retired executive vice president and director of the Engelhard Minerals and Chemical Corporation of Edison, N.J., and former Federal official, died of congestive heart failure on Monday at his home in Gloucester, Va. He was 71 years old.
        Mr. Harlan was also former president and senior executive officer of Engelhard Industries Division. He joined the corporation in 1969 and retired in 1975.
        Before joining Engelhard Mr. Harlan retired as Commissioner of the General Services Administration in Washington. He was a Federal employee for more than 32 years.
        He had also been a member of a task force appointed by the White House to set up the Marshall Plan in Europe after World War II. He also went to Korea to develop plans for the transfer of United States activities from Army to civilian control.
        He was born in Pittsburgh and attended George Washington Univesity.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Margaret Wetmore, and his father, John G. Harlan of Gloucester.

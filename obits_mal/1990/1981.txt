        George R. Ince Jr., a first vice president of Bankers Trust Company in Manhattan for more than 40 years, died on Thursday at Winthrop University Hospital in Mineola, L.I. He was 81 years old and lived in Garden City, L.I.
        Mr. Ince died of lymphatic cancer, his son, George Jr. of Garden City, L.I., said.
        He retired from Bankers Trust in 1974. At his death he was a director of the Long Term Credit Bank Trust Company, a subsidiary of the Long Term Credit Bank in Japan.
        Mr. Ince was a graduate of Harvard University in 1932 and of the advance management program at the Harvard Business School in 1956.
        Besides his son, he is survived by his wife, the former Isabel Sorzano; three daughters, Judith Craven of Newtown, Conn., Catherine Stevens of Denver, and Carlie Wille of Greenville, N.C.; anotherson, John F., of Sausalito, Calif.; two brothers, Robert and John, and a sister, Joan Graves, all of Bellport, L.I., and 15 grandchildren.

        Myron Matlaw, emeritus professor of English at Queens College, died of complications resulting from amyloidosis, a blood disease, Friday at Beth Israel Hospital. He was 65 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Dr. Matlaw, who was born in Berlin and lived in Palestine as a child, came to the United States in 1937.
        He graduated from Hofstra College and held master's and doctoral degrees from the University of Chicago. He taught drama and theater at Hunter College from 1957 to 1960.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Julia Moody of Atlanta; a daughter, Laura Murphy of Atlanta; a son, John, of Manhattan; a brother, Ralph, of Chicago, and a grandaughter.

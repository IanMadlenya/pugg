
        Allan Shaw, president of Shaw & Todd Inc., a Manhattan advertising agency, died on Saturday at Danbury Hospital in Connecticut. He was 63 years old and lived in Bayside, Queens.
        Mr. Shaw died of a heart attack, his son-in-law, Jonathan Siskind, said.
        An art director, Mr. Shaw was also creative director for the agency, which opened in 1980 and specializes in industrial advertising. He spent his entire career in industrial advertising and had been associated with several agencies, including Young & Rubicam and McCann-Ericson.
        Mr. Shaw was born in Brooklyn and attended Cooper Union for the Advancement of Science and Art.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Roberta Goldstein; two daughters, Marlyn Siskind of Oyster Bay, L.I., and Deborah Parks of Syosset, L.I., and three grandchildren.


        Harold L. Luxemburg, a lawyer who represented several labor unions in the hotel and restaurant field, died on Sunday at his home in Manhattan at the age of 78.
        He died in his sleep, his son, Mark, said.
        Mr. Luxemburg, who practiced law until his death, was counsel for the Hotel and Restaurant Employees International Union, A.F.L., in the 1940's and 1950's and for several local unions of restaurant, bar and engineering workers. From 1957 to 1962 he was counsel to the Town of Putnam Valley, N.Y.
        He was born in Brooklyn, held a bachelor's degree from Columbia College and graduated summa cum laude from Brooklyn Law School.
        In addition to his son, who lives in Manhattan, Mr. Luxemburg is survived by his wife, the former Phyllis Baron Jones; a daughter, Leslie Rosenthal of McLean, Va., and four grandchildren.

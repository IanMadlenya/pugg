        Harry Bennett, a consulting chemist and the author or chief editor of more than 50 reference books, including the 28-volume ''Chemical Formulary,'' the ''Chemical and Technical Dictionary'' and the Encyclopedia of Chemical Trademarks and Synonyms,'' died on Friday at Mount Sinai Hospital in Miami Beach, where he lived.
        His death, at the age of 95, resulted from heart failure, a family spokesman said.
        Mr. Bennett was the director of the B.R. Laboratory in Miami Beach and founder of the Chemical Publishing Company of New York and the Glyco Products Company of Williamsport, Pa. He was a native of Manhattan and a graduate of New York University.
        His wife of 62 years, the former Rose Michaels, died in 1983. Surviving are two daughters, Helene Bennett of Washington and Lynn Ziegler of Englewood Cliffs, N.J.; five grandchildren, and a great-grandson.

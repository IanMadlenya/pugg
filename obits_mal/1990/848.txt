        Edward C. Oelsner, retired president of the United States Navigation Company, which represented steamship companies from around the world, died of leukemia on Monday at his home in North Palm Beach, Fla. He was 75 years old and also lived in Arlington, Vt.
        Mr. Oelsner was associated with the company, which was founded by his father in 1911, from 1937 until he retired in 1979.
        In World War II he served in naval port operations in the United States, England and France. He left the service as a lieutenant commander.
        In 1976 he was decorated with the Officers Cross by the West German Government for helping to re-establish shipping between the United States and West Germany after the war.
        All-America Soccer Player
        Mr. Oelsner was a former governor of the New York Shipping Association, a former president of the American Shipping Society and a member of the board of the German-American Chamber of Commerce. He had been president and a governor of the Whitehall Club, a luncheon group of shipping-industry executives.
        He was born in Brooklyn and graduated in 1937 from Princeton University, where he was an all-America soccer player. He was New York State squash champion in 1947.
        He is survived by his wife of 50 years, the former Hope Bryan; two sons, Edward C. 3d, of Manhattan, and James L., of Denver; two daughters, Glenny Glenn of Bayville, L.I., and Lesley Oelsner of Manhattan, and six grandchildren.

        Norman W. Patterson, an architect who designed the Walter Reed Army Medical Center in Washington, died March 28 after suffering a heart attack at his home. He was 73 years old.
        Nationally known for his hospital work, Mr. Patterson designed a number of hospitals in Califoria, including the Pacific Presbyterian Medical Center in San Francisco, El Camino Hospital in Mountain View, Peninsula Hospital in Burlingame, Valley Medical Center and Alexian Brothers Hospital in San Jose and Letterman Army Medical Center in the Presidio, the military base in San Francisco.
        He was an architecture graduate of University of California at Berkeley and founded a firm of architects in San Francisco in 1942 that became Stone, Marraccini & Patterson. He was president and board chairman until retiring in 1982.
        Mr. Patterson is survived by his wife, the former Lois Churchman; three daughters, Carol Pearcy of Oakland, Janet Patterson of Cocoa Beach, Fla., and Sarah Weber of Merritt Island, Fla.; a son, Garen Patterson of Sonoma, Calif., and 13 grandchildren.

        George E. Hamilton Jr., a Washington lawyer who practiced for nearly seven decades and who was a past president of the Corcoran Gallery of Art, died Thursday at his home in Washington. Mr. Hamilton was 95 years old.
        His family said he died after a stroke.
        Mr. Hamilton, a fifth-generation Washingtonian, joined his family's law firm, Hamilton & Hamilton, in 1920 after receiving bachelor's and law degrees from Georgetown University and serving in the Army in World War I. When he retired five years ago, he had been a senior partner for 40 years.
        He was president of the Corcoran Gallery from 1953 to 1974 and was a life trustee there.
        Mr. Hamilton's wife, Marian, whom he married in 1922, died in 1984. Surviving are two daughters, Louise Matthai of Bethesda, Md., and Betty Mae Madden of Lakeville, Conn.; two sons, George 3d of Chevy Chase, Md., and William, of Annapolis, Md.; 19 grandchildren and 19 great-grandchildren.

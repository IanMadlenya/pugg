
       Jay Lloyd Block, a chemist and designer of ceramic plates, porcelain and crystal, died on Wednesday while jogging near his home in Briarcliff Manor, N.Y. He was 62 years old.
        Mr. Block was president of the Block China Corporation, a Manhattan-based designer and manufacturer of contemporary dinnerware patterns. He was the former vice president of the Portugal-United States Chamber of Commerce and was given an award for excellence in promoting the ceramic industry in Portugal. He was a chemist with the Naval Ordnance Laboratory before founding Block China in 1961.
        He is survived by his wife, Elizabeth, three sons, Joseph, of Boston, Michael, of Vail, Colo., and Frederick, of Briarcliff Manor; and a daughter, Sarah, also of Briarcliff Manor.

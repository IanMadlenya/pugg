        Robert H. Messing, a business executive and author, died of cancer on Thursday at his home in West Palm Beach, Fla. He was 52 years old and had also lived in Milford, Conn.
        Mr. Messing was owner and chief executive officer of Mitchell-Bradford Inc., a specialty chemical company in Milford. He was also a director of the Power and Systems Institute in Boston and of Humanics Publishing Group in Atlanta.
        In 1979, he founded the Delata Training Institute for business managers in Atlanta and was its president until 1984. Previously, he was president of a precious metals subsidairy of Occidental Petroleum.
        In 1989 he wrote a book on management titled ''The Tao of Management,'' published by Humanics.
        He is survived by his wife, Joan Wenzel Messing; three daughters, Robin and Bonnie, both of Hoboken, N.J., and Susan, of Chicago; a stepdaughter, Kim Wenzel of West Palm Beach; two brothers, Andrew, of Lake Worth, Fla., and Gilbert, of Palm Beach, Fla.; and a sister, Madeline Levy of Greenwich, Conn.


        Charles W. Tiernan, a former owner of Wallace & Tiernan, Inc., maker of water-treatment equipment, died on Nov. 22 in Delray Beach, Fla., where he had a retirement home. He was 72 years old and also lived in Ho-Ho-Kus, N.J., and in Shelter Island, L.I.
        He died of a cerebral hemorrhage, his family said.
        Mr. Tiernan was born in Jamaica, Queens, and attended Princeton. He spent his working career in the family business, which is based in Belleville, N.J. It was sold in the late 1960's, when Mr. Tiernan retired.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Diana Van Vlaanderen; three sons, Charles Jr. and Richard, both of Manhattan, and Timothy, of Shelter Island; three daughters, Carroll, of West Scarborough, Me., Christine Muller of Santa Fe. N.M., and Susan Madrigan of Orange, Mass.; a brother, Martin, of Essex Fells, N.J.; a sister, Nancy Swenson of Dallas, and seven grandchildren.

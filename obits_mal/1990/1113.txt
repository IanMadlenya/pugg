        Herbert R. McCarthy, a retired restaurateur, died of a stroke on Thursday at Southampton (L.I.) Hospital. He was 79 years old and lived in Southampton.
        Mr. McCarthy founded the Bowden Square Restaurant in Southampton in 1936. Among the restaurant's more famous patrons were the Duke and Duchess of Windsor, Ginger Rogers, Gary Cooper, Henry Ford 2d and Irwin Shaw.
        In 1986, Mr. McCarthy sold the restaurant to Michael O'Keefe, owner of the Water Club in Manhattan and the River Cafe in Brooklyn, and retired.
        Mr. McCarthy, a native New Yorker, graduated from Fordham College and attended Brooklyn Law School.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Lori Dieterle, and a son from an earlier marriage, Michael, of Southampton.

        The Rev. Dr. Wellman Joel Warner, a retired social psychologist and an educator, died on Thursday at Griffin Hospital in Derby, Conn. He was 93 years old and lived in Shelton, Conn.
        Dr. Warner died of pneumonia, a spokesman for the family said.
        Dr. Warner headed the departments of sociology and anthropology at New York University from 1946 to 1959, and from 1959 to 1963 taught at the university's Graduate School of Arts and Sciences. In 1963, he became the school's chairman emeritus.
        Dr. Warner graduated from La Salle College and George Washington University. He was ordained as a Methodist minister and began his career as a director of field work at Yale University in 1922. From 1930 to 1938 he was a professor of social ethics at Ohio Wesleyan University and from 1938 to 1946 he headed the department of social sciences at Dickinson College.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Miriam Saint; a son, Joel, of Union City, N.J.; a daughter, Linda, of San Francisco, and three grandchildren.

        John P. E. Brown, a retired vice president of the American Arbitration Association, died on Thursday at his home in Manhattan. He was 78 years old.
        Mr. Brown was born in Corinth, N.Y., and was a graduate of Ithaca College. He was executive secretary of the New York Kiwanis Club from 1940 to 1947, when he joined Schenley International as assistant to the president. He moved to the arbitration group five years later to direct its public relations, serving until 1977.
        He was a former youth director of the Democratic National Committee and a former president of the New York City Young Democrats.
        Long active in the Roman Catholic Archdiocese of New York, in retirement he served as financial consultant to St. Patrick's Cathedral, conducting its annual appeal. He was a Knight of the Holy Sepulcher.
        He leaves no immediate survivors.

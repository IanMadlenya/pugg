        Harold G. Warner, who rose from a job installing parts at the General Motors Corporation to become a member of the automaker's board, died of cancer Friday. He was 81 years old.
        Mr. Warner retired in 1973 after a 46-year career with the company. He went to work for the automaker in 1927 and held a number of positions. He later attended the General Motors Institute in Flint, Mich., where he earned an engineering degree. In 1968, Mr. Warner was named executive vice president in charge of the operations staff and elected to the board.
        Survivors include his wife, Elizabeth, and a daughter.

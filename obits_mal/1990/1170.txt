        David Yohalem, a specialist in the developing nations of Africa, died of lymphoma yesterday at his home in Northampton, Mass. He was 46 years old.
        Born in Queens, Mr. Yohalem graduated from Queens College in 1965 and served in the Peace Corps in Togo. From 1969 to 1976 he taught political science at Boston University and later directed Peace Corps volunteers in Sierra Leone.
        From 1980 until his death he was an international consultant, involved with Wash, a program financed by the United States Government that deals with rural water, sanitation and health projects throughout Africa. He was also a consultant to the Agency for International Development.
        He is survived by his wife, Harma Stahl; three children, and a brother, Alan, of South Orange, N.J.

        Charles R. Kaufman, a founder of the Chicago law firm Vedder, Price, Kaufman & Kramholz, died Wednesday at Northwestern Memorial Hospital in Chicago. He was 81 years old and lived in Winnetka, Ill.
        A spokesman for the law firm said he died after a stroke.
        Mr. Kaufman, who was born in Chicago, was a magna cum laude graduate of the University of Michigan and was first in his class at Harvard Law School.
        He served with the Securities and Exchange Commission from its establishment in 1934 until 1937, when he went into private practice. He was one of seven founding partners of Vedder, Price and continued in active practice until his death.
        Mr. Kaufman is survived by a daughter, Constance Dickinson of Winnetka; a son, Christopher, of Woodside, Calif.; a sister, Ruth Hyman of Lufkin, Tex., and three grandchildren.

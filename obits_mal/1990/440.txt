        John A. Reuling, a retired missionary and executive with the overseas mission of the United Church of Christ, died of complications from Alzheimer's disease on Monday at a nursing home in Port Huron, Mich. He was 83 years old and had lived in Tenafly, N.J.
        Mr. Reuling was the executive head of the division of mission of the United Church Board for World Ministries from 1962 through 1971. From 1946 to 1962 he was regional secretary for Africa with the American Board of Commissioners for Foreign Missions, now part of the World Board. He retired in 1971.
        A widower, he is survived by two daughters, Edna Wade of Port Huron, Mich., and Anna Lois Pratt of Framingham, Mass.; two sons, John Adam of Salem. Ore., and Walter of Canton, Mo.; a sister, Nellie McComb of Sarasota, Fla.; 11 grandchildren, and six great-grandchildren.

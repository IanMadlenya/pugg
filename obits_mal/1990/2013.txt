
       K. Blair Benson, a televison engineer who helped develop a broadcast-quality videotape machine, died on Tuesday while traveling to an engineers' conference in Washington. He was 71 years old and lived in Norwalk, Conn. He died of a heart attack, his wife, Dolly, said.
        He died of a heart attack, his wife, Dolly, said.
        Mr. Benson was at CBS Television, working with the Ampex Corporation, when he helped develop a videotape machine that used rotating heads. The first broadcast of a program recorded on the machine was on Nov. 30, 1956. Mr. Benson and Charles Ginsburg of Ampex won an Emmy for engineering.
        Mr. Benson worked for CBS for 25 years. In 1961, as manager of audio and video systems, helped design the CBS Broadcast Center on West 57th Street. He was later a vice president at Goldmark Communications and the Video Corporation of America.
        He graduated from the Worcester Polytechnic Institute, Mr. Benson was a Fellow of the Society of Motion Picture and Television Engineers and edited engineering handbooks.
        Also surviving are three daughters, Carole Benson of Paoli, Pa., Candi LaClair of South Norwalk, Conn., and Kathi, of East Norwalk, Conn.; a son, Ken, of Redding, Conn., and four grandchildren.


       Jenkin R. Hockert, a judge who served for many years in Queens before he retired in 1964, died on Friday at his home in Boca Raton, Fla. He would have celebrated his 96th birthday today. His family said he died of heart failure.
        His family said he died of heart failure.
        Judge Hockert, a longtime resident of Jamaica, Queens, was successively Queens County Clerk, a New York City Magistrate, a Municipal Court judge and City Court judge and an acting justice of the State Supreme Court.
        He was native of Chicago and a graduate of Columbia Law School. In World War I he was an aviator, serving on the Italian front under the command of Fiorello H. LaGuardia, who later, as Mayor of New York City, appointed him to the bench.
        Judge Hockert is survived by a daughter, Joan Donnelly of Manhattan; a son, Lorance, of the Bronx, and two grandchildren.

        John T. Stiefel, a chemical engineer and former vice president of the Westinghouse Corporation of Pittsburgh, died of a brain tumor on Sunday at his home in Scarsdale, N.Y. He was 69 years old and also lived in Sarasota, Fla.
        Mr. Stiefel joined Westinghouse in 1949 and left as a vice president in 1972. From 1972 to 1974, he was a senior vice president of the Fluor Corporation, a Los Angeles engineering and construction concern, where he started a nuclear power division. In 1974 he founded and became president of Stiefel Associates, which helped public utilities design and operate nuclear plants.
        Mr. Stiefel commanded an Army chemical warfare unit in Europe in World War II. He was born in Pittsburgh and was a graduate of the University of Illinois.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Joan Lico; a son, Reid, of Houston; a sister, Carol Swift of Pittsburgh, and two grandchildren.

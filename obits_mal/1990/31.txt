        James Averell Clark Jr., a World War II fighter pilot who led a varied career in business after the war, died of a stroke on Thursday at his home in Manhattan. He was 69 years old.
        Mr. Clark, who was credited with destroying 16 German planes during the war, worked as a stockbroker with Clark Dodge Inc. in Manhattan from 1946 to 1950. He then returned to the Air Force as the commander of a jet fighter training group.
        In 1953, he became president of the Lamson Aircraft Company, which serviced crop dusting planes. In 1959, he returned to Wall Street with Joseph Walker and Son, and in 1963 became president of the Condor Mining Company, which operated the Tipuani Gold Mine in the Bolivian Andes. In 1973, he was with Pegasus Air Freight, a company that transported race horses and cattle worldwide. He retired in 1980.
        He is survived by his wife, Armeme; a daughter, Carey, of Manhattan; a son Christopher, of Norfolk, England, and two grandchildren.

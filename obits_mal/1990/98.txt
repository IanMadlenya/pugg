        Clifford V. Coons, retired president of the Rheem Manufacturing Company, died on Monday at Boca Raton (Fla.) Community Hospital. He was 78 years old and had homes in Boca Raton and Highlands, N.C.
        Mr. Coons was president of Rheem, a Manhattan manufacturer of heating and air-conditioning equipment, from 1967 to 1972, when he retired. He joined the company in 1934 after attending the University of California at Berkeley.
        He progressed steadily through the ranks of management, becoming general manager of sales in 1945 and vice president in charge of sales in 1948. In 1950 he was elected to the board of directors and in 1956 was named executive vice president and vice chairman of the executive committee.
        Mr. Coons is survived by his wife, Delma; a son, Dr. Robert, of Houston; a brother, Clinton, of St. Joseph, Mo.; two sisters, Mabel Simpson of St. Joseph and Mildred Stanford of Leavenworth, Kan., and four grandchildren.

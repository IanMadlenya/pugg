        Hugo Gladstone Koehler, an investment banker, died on Tuesday at a friend's home in Manhattan. He was 60 years old and lived in Manhattan and in Cos Cob, Conn.
        He died of a heart attack, said a spokesman for his half-brother, Senator Claiborne Pell of Rhode Island.
        Mr. Koehler was a graduate of Brown University. He was affiliated with J. M. Hartwell & Company in Manhattan and was a director at Hartwell Growth and Hartwell Emerging Growth Funds. He had also been a manager at Train Smith Counsel, Inc. since 1966.
        In addition to Senator Pell, he is survived by his wife, Polly, of Greenwich, Conn.; a daughter, Ellen, of Manhattan; and a son, William, of Newtown, Conn.

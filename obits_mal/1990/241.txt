        Alan Handley, a television director and producer, died of a heart attack on Friday at his home in Los Angeles. He was 77 years old.
        Mr. Handley's three-decade careeer in television began in the medium's early days, when he produced and directed programs that starred performers including Dinah Shore and Danny Thomas. He also staged the Academy Award shows from 1957 through 1959, as well as Ringling Brothers circus broadcasts. He won an Emmy Award for directing a 1965 Julie Andrews special.
        In the 1930's Mr. Handley was an actor, performing with stock and touring companies and in a few Broadway plays. He served with the Army Engineers in World War II.
        Surviving are two sons, James, of Alta Loma, Calif., and Paul, of Torrance, Calif.

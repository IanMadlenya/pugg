        Steven A. Martindale, a Washington lawyer and lobbyist known for his access to socialites and for the parties he gave, died of complications from AIDS on Wednesday at George Washington University Hospital. He was 46 years old.
        A native of Pocatello, Idaho, and a graduate of Stanford University, he was active in Republican politics. He came to Washington and served on the staff of Senator Charles Goodell, Republican of New York, He entered public relations after Mr. Goodell left the Senate in 1971.
        Recently he had been associated with Adnan Khashoggi, the Saudi entrepreneur. He wrote a book about Mr. Khashoggi, ''By Hook or by Crook,'' that was published in Britain last year.
        Survivors include his parents, Clarissa and Addington Martindale, of Pocatello.

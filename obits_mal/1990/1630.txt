        Bolton Sullivan, the founder and retired president of the Skil Corporation of Chicago, died Thursday at his home in Lake Forest, Ill. He was 94 years old.
        He died in his sleep, said his grandson, Bolton Carroll.
        A graduate of the United States Naval Academy and of the University of Minnesota, Mr. Sullivan founded the company, which developed the Skilsaw, a portable electric saw, and other portable electric tools, in 1924. He retired in 1965. The Skil Corporation is now a subsidiary of the Emerson Electric.
        He was a former director of the Missouri Pacific Railroad and received honorary degrees from Loyola University and Georgetown University. He was named a Knight of St. Gregory by Pope Pius XII.
        He is survived by his wife, Constance; a son, John of West Palm Beach, Fla,; a daughter, Gieriet Carroll of Winnetka, Ill; 14 grandchildren, and one great-grandchild.

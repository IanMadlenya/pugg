        John J. Fowler Jr., former president and director of the Sterling National Bank and Trust Company of New York, died of congestive heart failure on Sunday at the Mount Sinai Medical Center in Manhattan. He was 68 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Fowler, a native New Yorker, had been with the bank, the principal subsidiary of Sterling Bancorp, as a senior vice president since 1971. He was named president and director in 1978. He retired in 1986 at the age of 65 because of ill health. Earlier he had been president of the Irving Trust Company in Manhattan.
        He graduated from New York University and studied banking at Rutgers University in New Brunswick, N.J.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Muriel Passarelli, and a son, Robert, of Manhattan.

        Rabbi Nathan N. Rosen, a chaplain at Brown University, Providence, R.I., for 25 years, died of cancer yesterday at Long Island Jewish Hospital. He was 85 years old and lived in Jamaica Estates, Queens.
        Rabbi Rosen came to Brown in 1947 as a chaplain and became the founder and first director of the Brown chapter of Hillel, the social service organization for Jewish students. He retired in 1972.
        He was a graduate of Yeshiva University and Columbia College. He was an Army chaplain in World War II.
        Rabbi Rosen is survived by his wife, the former Rose Eisenberg; a son, David of Queens; and a sister, Rebecca Posner of Brooklyn.

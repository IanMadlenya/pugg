        A memorial concert for John Henry Faulk, the Southern humorist and radio personality, is to be held at 5:30 P.M. on Monday at the Public Theater, 425 Lafayette Street. In 1962, Mr. Faulk, who had a show on CBS radio, won a libel suit against Aware Inc., which was devoted to alerting broadcasters and advertisers about those in the entertainment industry it felt had ties to the Communist Party. He became a champion of First Amendment rights. Mr. Faulk died of cancer on April 9 at the age of 76. The program is to include readings from his stories and essays, and performances by Pete Seeger, Odetta and others. The gathering is to be free and open to the public.

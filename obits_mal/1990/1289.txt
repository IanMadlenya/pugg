        James A. Moss, University Professor of Social Science Emeritus at Adelphi University on Long Island, died on June 20 at New York Hospital. He was 70 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Dr. Moss died of a heart attack, his son, Jay, said.
        Dr. Moss, who was also director of the university's African-American Studies Program, had joined the faculty in 1976. He retired in 1988. He was a former vice president and deputy president of Medgar Evers College of the City University of New York and former acting dean for international studies at the State University of New York at Buffalo. He had also been chairman of the Middletown (N.Y.) Housing Authority.
        Dr. Moss lectured in the West Indies, Europe and Africa. He was editor of and a contributor to ''The Black Man in America, Integration and Separation,'' published by Dell in 1971.
        He was born in Newark, and was a graduate of Fordham University and the New School for Social Research. He received an M.A. and a Ph.D. from Columbia University.
        Besides his son Jay, of San Francisco, he is survived by a daughter, Alison Wright Moss of Ureka, Calif.


       James R. Eiszner, the chief executive of CPC International, one of the nation's largest food companies, died yesterday at Columbia-Presbyterian Medical Center in Manhattan. He was 63 years old and lived in Cresskill, N.J.
        Mr. Eiszner had been chairman of the company since 1987 and chief executive from 1984 to August of this year. Before that he had been president of CPC International since 1979 and a director since 1975.
        A 1952 graduate of the University of Chicago with a Ph.D. in organic chemistry, Mr. Eiszner began his career as a research chemist with the Standard Oil Company of Indiana.
        He was a member of various organizations, including the Americas Society, the Committee for Economic Development and the Advisory Council of the International Executive Service Corps.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Joyce Holland; two sons, James, of Ridgewood, N.J., and Timothy, of Conyers, Ga., and five grandchildren.

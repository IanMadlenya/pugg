        Comdr. Bradwell Turner, who led a daring rescue of 299 Allied seamen held prisoner on a German ship in 1940, died on March 21 at the age of 92. The cause of death was not disclosed. Commander Turner led a boarding party from the British destroyer Cossack, which freed the prisoners from the German supply ship Altmark in a Norwegian fjord in February 1940.
        Commander Turner received a Distinguished Service Order in 1940 for his part in the action. He received the Order of the British Empire in 1951.
        He was a naval attache in Oslo from 1954 to 1957 and worked for the Marconi electronics company from 1957 to 1972.

        William E. Davies, a retired official of the United States Geological Survey, died on Wednesday at his home in Falls Church, Va. He was 72 years old.
        Mr. Davies died of a heart attack, his wife said.
        Mr. Davies, a native of Cleveland, came to Washington in World War II, when he was an officer with the Army Map Service.
        He stayed on there as a civilian until 1949, when he joined the Geological Survey. There, his assignments included surveying caves in Pennsylvania and West Virginia and research on permafrost.
        In the mid-1950's he took part in a Geological Survey expedition to Antarctica. The Davies Escarpment in Antarctica was named for him. He retired in the mid-1980's.
        Mr. Davies was a graduate of the Massachusetts Institute of Technology. He received a master's degree in geology at Michigan State University.
        He is survived by his wife, Geraldine; a son, William H. of Alexandria, Va.; a daughter, Pamela G. of Atlanta, and a brother, John A. of Fairfield, Ohio.

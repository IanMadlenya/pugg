        Gerard A. Fulham, former chairman of the Pneumo Corporation, a concern with holdings in the aerospace and military industries and food and drug distribution, died Sunday at Massachusetts General Hospital in Boston. He was 70 years old and lived in Oyster Harbors, Mass.
        His family said he died of complications from diabetes.
        Mr. Fulham was associated with Estabrook & Company, a stockbrokerage, and with his family's business, Fulham Brothers, before becoming chief financial officer of Cleveland Pneumatic Tool in 1959. A decade later he was elected chairman and chief executive of the tool company's successor, Pneumo, from which he retired in 1985. The corporation is now the Pneumo Abex Holdings Company, based in Boston.
        He was born in Winthrop, Mass., was a graduate of the Roxbury Latin School and Harvard College and served in the Navy in World War II.
        Surviving are his wife of 47 years, the former Barbara McGoldrick; two sons, John of Centerville, Mass., and Gerard Jr. of Needham, Mass.; four daughters, Gerarda and Barbara Ann, both of Oyster Harbors, Trudy Sullivan of Centerville and Maura Edmonds of Elberon, Va.; a sister, Margaret Mullin of Osterville, Mass.; two brothers, Thomas and John, both of Wellesley, Mass., and 11 grandchildren.

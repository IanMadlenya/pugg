        Souther (Chick) Whittelsey, a naval architect and yachtsman, died on Wednesday at Greenwich (Conn.) Hospital. He was 79 years old and lived in Greenwich. He died of a brain tumor, his wife, Harriet, said.
        Mr. Whittelsey was president of the naval architectural and marine engineering firm of H. Newton Whittelsey of Manhattan for almost 40 years. It designed ships for the Navy in both world wars as well as New York City fireboats. He was vice chairman of the New York metropolitan section of the Society of Naval Architects and Marine Engineers.
        An avid yachtsman, Mr. Whittelsey raced in regattas in the United States and England for more than 50 years, often winning with his sloop Interlude. He had also been active in the Greenwich Town Meeting since 1943.
        Also surviving are two sons, Henry, of Huntington, L.I., and Neil, of North Ferrisberg, Vt.; a daughter, Gale Sherman of Pocatello, Idaho; three sisters, Lorna Hibberd of Rye, N.Y., Myrtle Campbell of Boynton Beach, Fla., and Cecelia Hunter of Lake Forest, Ill., and five grandchildren.

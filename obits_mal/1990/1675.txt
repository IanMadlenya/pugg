        George Napack, a chemical engineer and a retired vice president of the Hatco Chemical Corporation, died on Tuesday at Overlook Hospital in Summit, N.J. He was 65 years old and lived in Westfield, N.J.
        The cause of death was Hodgkin's disease, a spokesman for the family said.
        Mr. Napack graduated from the Columbia University of Engineering School in 1950 and worked for several chemical companies before joining W. R. Grace & Company in 1963. He worked at the Hatco Group of Grace in Fords, N.J., until 1978, when it was sold. He was a senior vice president with Hatco Chemical until he retired in 1986.
        Surviving are his wife, Marilyn; two sons, Robert, of Fremont, Calif., and Steven, of Corvalis, Ore.; a daughter, Susan, of Brooklyn; a sister, Rosalie Reinhardt of Manhattan, and two grandchildren.

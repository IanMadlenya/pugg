        Emanuel Lazzaro, a retired trucking company executive, died on Saturday at his son's home in Brooklyn. He was 90 years old and lived in Brooklyn.
        Mr. Lazzaro, who immigrated to the United States from Italy in 1902, was president of the Glory Transportation Company and the Sunshine Truck Rental Company in Brooklyn until he retired 20 years ago. He was a longtime member of the Italian Welfare League and shipped tons of relief materials to Italy after World War II. He also aided victims of earthquakes in southern Italy in recent years.
        He is survived by his wife, Olga; a son, Dr. E. Clifford, of Brooklyn; five grandchildren and two great-grandchildren.

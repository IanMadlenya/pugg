        Daniel J. Conroy, retired managing director of William Sword & Company, investment bankers, in Princeton, N.J., died Tuesday at his home in a retirement community in Jamesburg, N.J., after a long illness. He was 73 years old.
        Mr. Conroy retired as managing director last year. For 29 years earlier, until he retired for the first time in 1976, he was a vice president of Morgan Stanley & Company, the Manhattan investment banking firm.
        He attended Pace Institute from 1936 to 1939 and graduated from Fordham University in 1946. He graduated from John Marshall Law School in 1948.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Leonora Burtt; two sons, Daniel J. 3d., of Lake Hopatcong, N.J., and Capt. John F., of Prattville, Ala.,; three daughters, Catherine Lockard of Fairbanks, Alaska, Helen Conroy of Philadelphia and Eileen Conroy of Morristown, N.J., and three grandchildren.

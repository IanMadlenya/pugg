        Randall Walrath Hoffmann, a professor of English who had been an administrator at several colleges, died on Tuesday at St. Luke's Hospital in Tryon, N.C. He was 77 years old and had recently moved to Tryon from Croton-on-Hudson, N.Y. He died of heart failure, his wife said. Mr. Hoffmann was the dean of students at Hofstra University in Hempstead, L.I., from 1951 to 1966. At the time of his retirement in 1974, he was director of the Human Relations Center at Queensborough Community College in Bayside, Queens.
        He had also been the director of Worcester Junior College in Worcester, Mass., and taught English at Northeastern University in Boston and at Tilton Preparatory School in Tilton, N.H.
        Mr. Hoffman was born on Staten Island, graduated from Middlebury College in Vermont and received his master's degree in English from Boston University.
        He is survived by his wife, Bettye; a daughter, Martha Rucker of East Rochester, N.Y., and six grandchildren.

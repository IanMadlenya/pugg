        John C. Carroll, retired executive vice president of the Communications Workers of America, died Saturday at the Washington Hospital Center. He was 68 years old and lived in Forestville, Md.
        A union spokesman said Mr. Carroll died of congestive heart failure after open-heart surgery.
        Mr. Carroll, who served in union posts for 50 years before he retired last year, was a principal negotiator in bargaining with the American Telephone & Telegraph Company in the 1970's and 1980's.
        He is survived by his wife, Mary Mays-Carroll; two daughters, Nancy Blanning of Denver and Ginger Golz of Akron, Ohio; two sisters, Catherine Auld of Pasadena, Calif., and Sister Bernadette Mary Carroll of Orange County, Calif.; a brother, Joe, of Lake San Marcos, Calif., and six grandchildren.

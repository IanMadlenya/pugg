        Fremont C. Peck, publisher of The Brooklyn Daily Times and The Brooklyn Times-Union in the 1920's and 1930's, died on Tuesday at his home in Locust Valley, L.I. He was 92 years old.
        Mr. Peck, a Brooklyn native and a 1920 graduate of Princeton University, was the son of an executive of the F.W. Woolworth Company and inherited considerable wealth. In 1921 he bought The Brooklyn Daily Times, an evening newspaper founded in 1848, and in 1932 he acquired a rival evening paper, The Standard-Union, which had been published since 1863. The merged paper was sold to The Brooklyn Eagle in 1937.
        Mr. Peck was a director of Woolworth for more than 35 years and was a trustee of Carson C. Peck Hospital in Brooklyn before its merger with Methodist Hospital. In World war II he served in the Army Air Forces, rising to lieutenant colonel.
        His first wife, the former Isabel Foster, whom he married in 1921, died in 1969. He married the former Elizabeth Gleason Moffett in 1970, and she died last year. Surviving are a son, Fremont Jr., of Slingerlands, N.Y.; a daughter, Marybel, of Guilderland Center, N.Y.; nine grandchildren and three great-grandchilldren.

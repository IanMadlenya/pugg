
        Joseph W. Cullen, director of a Denver-based cancer research center, died Saturday at St. Luke's Hospital in San Francisco. He was 53 years old and lived in Engelwood, Colo.
        He died of a malignant brain tumor, a family spokesman said.
        Dr. Cullen had been director of the AMC Cancer Research Center, formerly the American Medical Center, in Denver since July 1989.
        From 1982 to 1989 he was deputy director of the division of cancer prevention and control of the National Cancer Institute in Washington. He had been deputy director of the Jonsson Comprehensive Cancer Center at the University of California at Los Angeles and had also been with the Pavlovian Research Laboratory in Perry Point, Md.
        Dr. Cullen received his doctorate at Florida State University in 1968. He was on the editorial boards of five scientific journals.
        He is survived by his wife, Katherine; a son, Neal, of State College, Pa.; a daughter, Jennifer, of Berkeley, Calif.; a brother, Richard, of Northville, N.Y.; and a sister, Joan Degnan, of Long Island.

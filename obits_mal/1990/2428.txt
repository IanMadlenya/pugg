
        Dr. William H. Lewis Jr., a former New York internist who played a role in the early years of nuclear medicine, died on Saturday at Sibley Memorial Hospital in Washington. He was 88 years old and had lived in Washington for the last two years.
        Dr. Lewis died of a cerebral hemorhage, his son Ogden said.
        As a specialist in cardiology, Dr. Lewis was a research collaborator in the medical department at Brookhaven National Laboratory in Upton, L.I., from 1958 to 1962. He was a member of the house of delegates of the New York State Medical Society from 1953 to 1960.
        He had a private practice in Manhattan for many years and had long associations with Doctors Hospital and Memorial Sloan-Kettering Cancer Center, where he was an assistant attending physician in the medical department from 1940 to 1967.
        Dr. Lewis received the Order of Merit of the Federal Republic of Germany in 1963 for his work in nuclear medicine.
        He was born in Denora, Pa., and was a graduate of Harvard College and Harvard Medical School. From 1938 to 1946 he taught at Cornell University Medical College.
        Dr. Lewis is survived by two sons, William Hall 3d and Ogden Northrop Lewis, both of Manhattan; a sister, Helen Holley of Washington, and two grandsons.

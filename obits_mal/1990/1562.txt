        F. K. Henri Henrion, an international graphic designer and educator, died on Thursday at his home in London. He was 76 years old.
        Mr. Henrion died of cancer, said Kathleen Row, a friend and colleague.
        He was active in design education through the International Design Student Organization and Icograda, the international congress of graphic design associations. He was also a lecturer and visiting professor at institutions around the world, including the Munich Typographic Society in Germany, the Bangkok Design Council, Carnegie Mellon University in Pittsburgh, Bezalel Academy in Jerusalem, the Moscow Institute of Industrial Design and Pratt Institute and Cooper Union in Manhattan.
        He was president of the Alliance Graphique Internationale from 1963 to 1966, president of the Society of Designers and Artists from 1960 to 1962 and president of Icograda from 1968 to 1970. He was also a vice president of the Royal Society of Arts.
        Francis Kay Henri Henrion was born in Hamburg, Germany, and was trained as a textile designer in Paris and at the Ecole Paul Colin. He was a designer of two pavilions for architecture and natural science in 1949 and 1951.
        He is survived by two sons and a daughter and two grandchildren. His companion for many years was Marion Wesel.


        Douglas A. Anello, a communications lawyer, died Thursday at Melbourne Regional Medical Center in Melbourne, Fla. He was 78 years old.
        After serving in the Coast Guard in World War II, Mr. Anello joined the Federal Communications Commission in 1946. He served as chief of the legal and enforcement division of the commission's safety and special services bureau.
        In 1957 he joined the National Association of Broadcasters. He was the association's general counsel from 1961 to 1969, when he established a private practice in communications law.
        He is survived by a son, Michael, of La Jolla, Calif.; a daughter, Diane Anello, of Alexandria, Va., two sisters, Leila DeTemple of Falls Church, Va., and Yolanda DeVivo of Morehead City, N.C., and three grandchildren.

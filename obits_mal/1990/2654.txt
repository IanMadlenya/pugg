
        Robert Lee Bonaparte, a retired vice president of the Boise Cascade Corporation, died Saturday at his home in Portland, Ore. He was 67 years old.
        He died of heart failure, his family said.
        Mr. Bonaparte, a specialist in marketing newsprint, joined Boise Cascade in 1963 and retired three years ago. He had previously been with McKinsey & Company, a management consulting firm, and with United States Plywood. He also was a co-founder of Schramsberg Vineyards in Calistoga, Calif., producer of sparkling wine.
        Mr. Bonaparte, a native of Old Greenwich, Conn., was a graduate of Columbia College. He served for three years as an Air Force navigator based in Italy in World War II and flew 34 combat missions.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Ruth Power; three daughters, Anne, of San Francisco, Renee Erickson of Washington and Ruth Mohanram of Sunnyvale, Calif.; a son, Robert, of Portland; a brother, Roy, of Vero Beach, Fla., and three grandhildren.

        Dr. David E. Shoch, an ophthalmologist and educator, died of cancer on Tuesday at Northwestern Memorial Hospital in Chicago. He was 71 years old and lived in Glencoe, Ill.
        Dr. Shoch, a native of New York City, joined the ophthalmology department at Northwestern Medical School in 1953, after receiving doctoral and medical degrees at the university. He headed the department from 1966 to 1983.
        He was also a practicing ophthalmolgist at Northwestern Hospital, Children's Memorial and the Veterans Administration Lakeside Medical Center. He was editor of abstracts for the American Journal of Ophthalmology for more than 20 years.
        He is survived by his wife, Gertrude; two sons, James of Somerville, Mass., and John of Palo Alto, Calif.; and a sister, Florence Kerson of Flushing, Queens.

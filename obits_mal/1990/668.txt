        David Teitelbaum, a producer of news and documentary programs for NBC, died of cancer of the prostate on Tuesday at Beth Israel Medical Center in Manhattan. He was 69 years old and lived in Englewood Cliffs, N.J.
        Mr. Teitelbaum joined NBC News in 1954 and was a news editor and writer for ''The Huntley-Brinkley Report,'' NBC's evening news program. In the early 1970's, he won Emmy Awards as the producer of documentaries on the Bangladeshi capital, Dhaka, and on the welfare system. As director of NBC News operations in the Mediterranean from 1972 to 1977, he oversaw the network's coverage of the 1973 Arab-Israeli war and the Portuguese revolution.
        When he retired in 1986, Mr. Teitelbaum was executive producer of ''A-News,'' a news service provided by NBC News to its affiliated stations.
        Mr. Teitelbaum is survived by his wife, Sylvia; three sons, Ned, of Englewood Cliffs, Henry, of Brooklyn, and Richard, of Manhattan, and a brother, Charles, of Jamaica Estates, Queens.

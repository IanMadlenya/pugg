        Prescott A. Tolman, a retired vice president of the E. F. MacDonald Incentive Company and a sport fisherman, died on Friday at St. Luke's Hospital in Bethlehem, Pa. He was 80 years old and lived in Swiftwater, Pa. He died of leukemia, his family said. Mr. Tolman joined MacDonald, the Plaid Stamp company that is now part of the Carlson Marketing Group, in 1952 and was vice president for international relations when he retired in 1982. He had previously been a regional sales manager for Eastern Air Lines.
        Mr. Tolman was a founder of the Federation of Fly Fishermen and of the American Museum of Fly Fishing in Manchester, Vt.
        He was born in Manhattan, had a bachelor's degree from Harvard College and was a major in the Marines in World War II.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Katherine Jackson Carpenter; a son, Prescott, of Dunbarton, N.H., and a grandson.

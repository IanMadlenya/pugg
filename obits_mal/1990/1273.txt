        Irwin Panken, a lawyer in Manhattan for more than 50 years, died on Sunday at his home in Plainfield, N.J. He was 82 years old.
        He died of cancer, his wife, Helen, said.
        Mr. Panken was admitted to the bar in 1933 after graduating from New York University School of Law. He was in private practice for most of his career and was the village attorney for Croton-on-Hudson, N.Y., for several years. He was also a member of the American Arbitration Association and served on the Committee on Legislation for the New York State Conference of Mayors.
        Besides his wife, he is survived by a son, Jonathan, of Warwick, N.Y.; a daughter, Elizabeth, of Portland, Ore., and two grandchildren.

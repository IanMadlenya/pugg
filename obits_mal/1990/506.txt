        Stuart MacClintock, a professor of philosophy who became an intelligence analyst for the Federal Government, died of cancer on Monday at his home in Silver Spring, Md. He was 70 years old.
        Mr. MacClintock, who received his doctorate at Columbia University, was an assistant professor of philosophy at Indiana University from 1952 to 1960, a period in which he also taught at Columbia and the University of Virginia.
        He specialized in ancient and medieval philosophy and in 1956 wrote a book, ''Perversity and Error.''
        In 1960 he joined the Defense Department as an intelligence analyst. He worked for the National Security Agency, and retired in 1985.
        In World War II and the Korean War he was a Navy intelligence officer. He retired from the Naval Reserve in 1979 with the rank of captain.
        He is survived by his wife, Nancy; a son, Alec of Laurel, Md.; a daughter, Lucy of Cambridge, Mass.; and a sister, Joyce Nicolait of San Diego.

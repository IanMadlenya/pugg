        Leonard Rosenblatt, former president and chief executive of Ausimont Industries, a multinational chemical company with headquarters in Waltham, Mass., died yesterday at Dana Farber Institute in Boston. He was 60 years old and lived in Boston.
        Mr. Rosenblatt died of cancer, said a spokesman for the family.
        In 1981 Mr. Rosenblatt joined Compo Industries, makers of shoe machinery and materials, as president, chief executive and a board member. In 1984 he negotiated a merger with Ausimont and was elected its president, chief executive and a member of the board. He retired in July 1989.
        He previously worked 18 years with W. R. Grace & Company, where he had been executive vice president of the construction products division.
        Mr. Rosenblatt was a member of director of several organizations, including Outward Bound and the Suffolk University School of Management.
        A native New Yorker, he graduated from City College in 1952.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Miriam Brautman; a son, Barton of Boston; a daughter, Helane Weiner, also of Boston, and two grandchildren. Two brothers and two sisters also survive.

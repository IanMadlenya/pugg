        O. Cedric Rowntree, a former chairman of the R. T. French Company, maker of French's mustard and other food products, died yesterday at his home in Rochester. He was 78 years old.
        His family said he died of heart failure.
        Mr. Rowntree, who was born in Scarborough, England, was a graduate of Queens College, Oxford, and studied at the Sorbonne, Bonn University and the Center for Historical Studies in Madrid.
        In 1935 he joined Reckitt & Colman Ltd., a company associated with R. T. French, working with affiliates in France, Belgium and the Netherlands. He served as a major in British Army intelligence in World War II and was awarded the French Croix De Guerre.
        Mr. Rowntree moved to Rochester in 1948 as a director of French and became chairman in 1972. He retired in 1977.
        He was a trustee of the University of Rochester from 1965 until last year.
        His first wife, the former Brenda Myers, died in 1981. He is survived by his second wife, the former Catherine Lindsay; two daughters, Prudence Miller of Forks, Wash., and Victoria, of Salt Lake City, and two sons, Peter, of Scarsdale, N.Y., and Nicholas, of Chobham, England.

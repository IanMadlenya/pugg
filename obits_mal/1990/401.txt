        Frederick S. Leinbach, former president of the Riegel Paper Corporation, died of a heart attack on Friday at his home in Ocean City, N.J. He was 79 years old.
        Mr. Leinbach joined Riegel as a development engineer in 1934. He was named president in 1959 and was chief executive from 1962 to 1968.
        In World War II, Mr. Leinbach was assistant director of the containers division of the War Production Board. He received a bachelor's in chemical engineering at Lafayette College and taught science and mathematics before joining Riegel.
        Mr. Leinbach had headed groups like the American Paper Institute and the Packaging Institute. He was also a member of the governing committee of the United States Philips Trust and a director of the Rexham Corporation and the North American Philips Corporation. He was also president of the board of the Ocean City Arts Center.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Polly Dottorer; a daughter, Penny Howell of Atherton, Calif.; a son, Michael, of Downey, Calif., and two granddaughters.

        Wolcott Andrews, a retired New York City landscape architect, died of a heart attack on Tuesday at Miles Memorial Hospital in Damariscotta, Me. He was 87 years old and lived in Wiscasset, Me.
        Mr. Andrews was the senior landscape architect for the New York City Housing Authority for more than 20 years, retiring in 1966. Before that he was with the city's Parks Department, where he worked with Frederick Law Olmsted Jr. in designing and constructing Fort Tryon Park in upper Manhattan, the site of the Cloisters.
        He was past president of the New York chapter of the American Society of Landscape Architects and of the American Federation of Fine Arts of New York City. He was a trustee of the Municipal Arts Society.
        Mr. Andrews graduated from Bowdoin College in 1927 and received a master's degree in landscape architecture from the Harvard School of Design in 1930.
        He is survived by his wife, the former, Anne Kirkham Lord; two sons, Martin, of Hoboken, N.J., and Christopher, of Noank, Conn.; a daughter, Penelope Sprague of Port Washington, L.I., and six grandchildren.

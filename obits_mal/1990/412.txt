        Dr. Archibald Ross Lewis, an author, educator and historian, died of a heart attack Sunday at his home in Tucson, Ariz. He was 75 years old and had homes in Amherst, Mass., and Marblehead, Mass.
        At his death Dr. Lewis was editor of American Neptune, a journal of maritime history. He was a professor of history at the University of Massachusetts from 1969 to 1985 and a former chairman of the department.
        Before that, he taught at the University of Texas at Austin for 16 years and was head of the history department there. From 1959 to 1961 he was co-director of the Munson-Maritime Institute in Mystic, Conn.
        Educated at Princeton University, Dr. Lewis was the author of a dozen books and numerous articles. His books included ''Knights and Samurai: Feudalism in Northern France and Japan,'' ''Nomads and Crusaders'' and ''The Islamic World and the West.''
        He is survived by his wife, the former Elizabeth Z. Cutler; and one granddaughter. A son and a daughter predeceased him.

        Irving Jacobson, chairman of the Jacobson and Quails chains of men's clothing stores in New York, New Jersey and Pennsylvania, died of liver disease yesterday at the Somerset Medical Center in Somerville, N.J. He was 65 years old and lived in Bridgewater, N.J.
        Mr. Jacobson joined Archie Jacobson Inc., a family business founded by his father on Staten Island, in 1948. In 1960 he became president and opened branches in New Jersey. In 1968 he founded the current chain of Quails stores. The two chains operate a total of 18 stores.
        Mr. Jacobson was a graduate of the New York University College of Engineering.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Cynthia Pines; four sons, Peter, of Middletown, N.J.; David, of Chicago; Kenneth, of Frederick, Md., and Clifford, of Piscataway, N.J., and three grandchildren.

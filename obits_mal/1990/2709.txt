
        Darwood Gillespie Myers, a retired stockbroker, died on Monday at the Riverview Hospital Extended Care Residence in Red Bank, N.J. He was 90 years old and lived in Middletown, N.J.
        He died of complications of a stroke, a family spokesman said.
        Mr. Myers, a Princeton graduate, entered the securities industry after graduation and spent almost 50 years with the Manhattan-based securities firm of Foster & Adams, which later became part of Tucker Anthony Inc. He was named a partner in the firm in 1929 and retired in 1970. He served with the Army Air Corps in World War II.
        He is survived by his wife, Elizabeth; a son, Howard, of Lawrenceburg, Ky.; two daughters, the Rev. Elizabeth Williams Myers of Milford, Pa., and Margaret Brown Myers of New Haven; a sister, Constance Anderson of Kennett Square, Pa., and one granddaughter.

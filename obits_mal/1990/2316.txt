        Mr. Moses, an outfielder, hit .325 as a rookie with the Philadelphia Athletics in 1935. He hit .471 in the 1946 World Series for the Boston Red Sox in his only appearance in the post-season event. In his professional career, Mr. Moses, who also played for Chicago, played in 2,012 games, had 2,138 hits, drove in 695 runs and hit 89 home runs.
        Mr. Moses served as a batting coach for 13 years with the A's, the Philadelphia Phillies, the Cincinnati Reds, the Yankees and the Detroit Tigers. A native of Uvalda, Ga., he was inducted into the Georgia Sports Hall of Fame in 1988. Survivors include his wife, Billie Moses; a daughter, Judy Lathem of Washington, a grandson, two brothers and a sister.

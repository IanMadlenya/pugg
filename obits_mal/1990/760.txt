        John B. Wick, a retired Superior Court judge in New Jersey, died of complications of a stroke on Saturday at Underwood Memorial Hospital in Woodbury, N.J. He was 82 years old and had lived in Woodbury.
        Judge Wick was on the bench from 1948 to 1972. He was first a judge of Gloucester County Court. Judge Wick was appointed to Superior Court in Camden County in 1957.
        In 1965 he ruled that Congressional Districts in New Jersey were unconstitutional in that they did not comply with the one-man one-vote principle and ordered that new lines be drawn. He retired in 1972.  Judge Wick, who graduated in 1931 from the George Washington University Law School, was a clerk for a lawyer in Woodbury before becoming a partner with former United States Senator Robert C. Hendrickson. From 1935 to 1947, he was an assistant prosecutor in Gloucester County. He had also been solicitor in several municipalities.
        Surviving are a daughter, Carol Ericksen of Chester Springs, Pa.; a son, John, of Parkesburg, Pa; a stepdaughter, Jeanne Budd of West Deptford, N.J., and seven grandchildren.

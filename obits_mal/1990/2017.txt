
       John W. O'Beirne, a retired special agent of the Federal Bureau of Investigation who was once a special assistant to the bureau's Director, died on Wednesday at Holy Cross Hospital in Washington. He was 72 years old and lived in Silver Spring, Md. His family said he died after a heart attack.

        Mr. O'Beirne, a native of Elmhurst, Queens, served in the bureau's Washington field office, at the F.B.I. Academy as a firearms instructor and on the bureau's inspection staff. He retired in 1972 as a special assistant to the Director.
        Survivors include his wife, the former Peggy Donnelly; two sons, John and Michael, and a daughter, Kelly, all from a former marriage and all of Chevy Chase, Md.


        Richard M. Bleier, retired chairman of the I. B. Kleinert Rubber Company of Manhattan, died on Monday at his home in Chappaqua, N.Y. He was 86 years old.
        Mr. Bleier died of a heart attack, a spokesman for the family said.
        He joined the company in 1945 as plant engineer and became president in 1962. He was chairman from 1967 to 1969.
        He also ran the New Castle Water Company from 1945 to 1976, when it was sold to the town of New Castle.
        Mr. Bleier was a vice president of the United Jewish Appeal-Federation of Jewish Philanthropies.
        He graduated from Cornell University in 1935 and served as a captain in the Army Coast Artillery Corps in World War II. He was awarded an Army certificate of achievement for developing a new anti-aircraft training device.
        Mr. Bleier is survived by his wife, the former Jeanette Guinzburg; three sons, Richard, of Montrose, Colo., Steven, of Chappaqua, and Ralph, of Alamo, Calif., and five grandchildren.

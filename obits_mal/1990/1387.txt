        Marvin Cohn, a professor of reading and head of the reading clinic at the Adelphi University School of Education, died on Sunday at Glen Cove (L.I.) Hospital. He was 66 years old and lived in Plainview, L.I. He died of cancer, a university spokeswoman said.
        Dr. Cohn, who received his Ph.D. from New York University in 1967, was with Adelphi for 24 years and helped more than 1,000 local schoolchildren read better. He also taught clinical reading courses at Adelphi and supervised graduate students. He was the author of ''Helping Your Teen-Age Student.''
        He is survived by his wife, Helen; a son, Joel, of Honolulu; a daughter, Carol, of Cambridge, Mass., and a brother, Irving Cohen of Los Angeles.

        Robert Francis Pulleyn, a corporate lawyer in Manhattan for 40 years, died yesterday at his home in Oldwick, N.J. He was 88 years old. Mr. Pulleyn joined the law firm of Simpson Thacher & Bartlett in 1926 after graduating from the Columbia University Law School, where he was editor of The Law Review. In his undergraduate days at Columbia College, he played varsity baseball, football and basketball.
        He was an associate with Simpson Thacher until 1951, then went into private practice and retired in 1973.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Peggy Wattles; a daughter, Pamela Machold of Princeton, and three grandchildren.

        Vernon C. Myers, a magazine executive who was publisher and president of Look in the 1950's and 1960's, died on Thursday at St. Joseph Medical Center in Stamford, Conn. He was 78 years old and lived in Norwalk, Conn.
        With the exception of the year following his graduation from college Mr. Myers spent his business career with the Cowles organization, publisher of Look.
        A native of St. Louis and a 1932 graduate of the University of Missouri School of Journalism, he joined the Cowles-owned Des Moines Register and Tribune promotion staff in 1933 and was made promotion manager two years later.
        In 1938 Mr. Myers became director of visual research for Look, and rose through advertising and promotion posts to become publisher of the magazine in 1952. In 1967 he was made president of the Look division of Cowles Communciations.
        In World War II he was with the Army Air Corps in the Pacific and was awarded the Bronze Star.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Mary Jane Martin; a son, Manning, of Chalfont, Pa.; a daughter, Melinda Beagle, of Orinda, Calif., and five grandchildren.

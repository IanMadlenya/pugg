        Murray S. Kaplan, a lawyer and textile executive, died of cancer Tuesday at his home in Palm Beach, Fla. He was 82 years old.
        Mr. Kaplan was a partner in the Palm Beach law firm of Stierer, Amendola & Kaplan, where he specialized in estate planning and probate litigation.
        Before moving to Florida in 1978, he was associated with his family's textile concern, Samuel Kaplan & Sons, in Manhattan. He was named president in the 1960's.
        A native New Yorker, Mr. Kaplan was a graduate of the University of Pennsylvania and of the Columbia University Law School. He became chief legal officer at Hickam Air Depot in Oahu, Hawaii, in World War II and was awarded a Bronze Star.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Muriel Sheerr; two daughters, Dr. Janet Belsky of Manhattan and Amy Eckman of Bedford, N.H.; two sons, S. Jerrold, of San Francisco, and James S., of Manhattan, and six grandchildren.

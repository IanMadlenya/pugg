        Raymond C. McCron, former senior vice president and treasurer of the Equitable Life Assurance Society, died of lung cancer on Saturday at his home in Scarsdale, N.Y. He was 69 years old.
        Mr. McCron joined the insurance company in 1971. He was appointed a vice president and treasurer in 1972 and became a senior vice president in 1978. He retired in 1986. Earlier in his career he had been treasurer of the New York Central Companies.
        He received a bachelor's degree in corporate finance from the Wharton School of the University of Pennsylvania in 1943.
        In World War II, he served with the Army in Europe and was recalled to active duty during the Korean War as a finance officer.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Corinne Mathews; two sons, James, of Brookfield, Conn., and John, of Madison, Wis.; two daughters, Linda Stover of Hamilton, Mont., and Trudy Cox of Litchfield, Conn., and three grandchildren.

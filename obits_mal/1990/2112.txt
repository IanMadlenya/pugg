        Charles S. Garland Jr., managing director of the Baltimore securities firm of Alex. Brown & Sons, died Saturday at his home in Baltimore. He was 63 years old. His family said he died of cancer.
        Mr. Garland, who joined Alex. Brown in 1952, became a general partner in 1964. He was born in New York City and was a graduate of Yale University. He was chairman and a former president of the Baltimore Opera Company.
        He is survived by his wife, Joan; two daughters, Margaret Whitman of Lawrenceville, N.J., and Elizabeth Wilmerding of Glyndon, Md.; a son, Charles 3d of New York City; his mother, Aurelia, of Baltimore; two sisters, Courtney Iglehart and Aurelia Bolton, both of Brooklandville, Md., and two granddaughters.

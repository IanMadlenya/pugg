        Richard P. Martel Jr., a senior vice president and associate creative director of BBDO New York, the largest operating division of the advertising agency BBDO Worldwide Inc., died on Tuesday at St. Luke's-Roosevelt Hospital Center. He was 35 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Martel died of complications from AIDS, a company spokesman said.
        Mr. Martel was a native of Brockton, Mass., and graduated from Bowdoin College. He joined BBDO in 1977 and won awards for his work on television advertising. He became a senior vice president last year.
        He is survived by his parents, Richard Sr. and Elena, of Brockton; three brothers, William and Michael, of Brockton, and David, of Boston, and a sister, Lisa, of Boston.

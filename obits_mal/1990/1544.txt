        William J. Kenney, a former senior vice president of the Bank of New York, died on Sunday at the Jersey Shore Medical Center in Neptune. He was 67 years old and lived in Manhattan and Spring Lake, N.J.
        He died of a heart attack, his wife, Jean, said.
        Mr. Kenney, who worked for the bank from 1961 to 1971, organized a group that created and installed a computerized trust and investment accounting system.
        He began his career at the International Business Machines Corporation. In later years, he was associated with Lester B. Knight and Mead Data Central. He retired in 1982.
        Also surviving are a son, William Jr., of Richmond; a daughter, Mara Peterson of Ridgewood, N.J., and three grandchildren.

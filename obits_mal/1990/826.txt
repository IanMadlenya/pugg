        The Rev. Leslie John Alden Lang, an Episcopal priest and an honorary assistant at St. Thomas Church on Fifth Avenue in Manhattan, died yesterday at his home in Manhattan. He was 81 years old.
        A graduate of General Theological Seminary, Mr. Lang had been an assistant priest at St. Thomas since 1974. Previously, he was the rector of St. Peter's Church at Westchester Square in the Bronx for 20 years. He had also served as rector of St. Paul's Church in Brooklyn and St. Edward the Martyr in Manhattan. Mr. Lang, a direct descendant of John Alden, the pilgrim who helped to settle the Plymouth Colony and one of the subjects of the story, ''The Courtship of Miles Standish,'' was ordained in 1934.
        He was chairman of the board of St. Hilda's and St. Hugh's School in Morningside Heights and had been a trustee of the House of the Holy Comforter in the Bronx since 1955. He was also chaplain to the Order of St. John of Jerusalem.
        He is survived by his sister, Virginia Witham of Lynn, Mass.

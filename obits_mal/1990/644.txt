        Thomas J. Livornese, a lawyer and jazz pianist, died of cancer on Friday at his home in Manhattan. He was 65 years old.
        A 1952 graduate of Brooklyn Law School, Mr. Livornese had a private practice in Manhattan and was also affiliated with Mount Carmel Cemeteries, serving as executive director from 1964 to 1974. He was a hospital corpsman in World War II. As a jazz musician, he entertained at private parties and sometimes in nightclubs.
        Mr. Livornese is survived by his mother, Mary Cavaliere of Lynbrook, L.I., and a sister, Maria Fitzgibbon of Aspen, Colo.

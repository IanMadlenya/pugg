
        George L. McKay Jr., an educator and a printer, died on Wednesday at his home in Hoffman Estates, Ill.
        He was 65 years old and died of Alzheimer's disease, his son, Douglas, of Queens, said.
        For 29 years, Mr. McKay was owner and president of the George McKay Press, a printing company in Manhattan. In 1967 he established a printing and graphic design course at the Dalton School in Manhattan and taught the course until 1975. Earlier in his career he founded The Morningsider, a newspaper published every two weeks for the Morningside Heights section of Manhattan. He was a 1948 graduate of Columbia College and a longtime resident of Queens. He retired in 1985.
        In addition to his son, he is survived by his wife, Patricia; two daughters, Meredith Sonda of Winnetka, Ill., and Eugenie Kolivosky of Lansing, Mich., and six grandchildren.

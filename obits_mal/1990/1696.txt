        Joe Lombardo, an award-winning graphics designer, died on Aug. 15 at St. Luke's-Roosevelt Hospital Center. He was 45 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        He died of pneumonia, said Barry Zaid, a friend.
        At the time of his death, he headed his own firm, Joe Lombardo, which he founded in Manhattan in 1970. He had won several Clio awards for packaging design, including one for Gourmet vinegars. He also did packaging design for Good & Plenty candies, Arrow shirts and Wisk detergent. Earlier in his career, he worked for Allan Bernie, a design firm in Manhattan; Gerstman & Meyers and other packaging firms.
        He is survived by his parents, Rose and James J. Lombardo of Queens; a brother, Peter, of Rancho Cucamonga, Calif., and a sister, Betty Ann Drago of East Patchogue, L.I.

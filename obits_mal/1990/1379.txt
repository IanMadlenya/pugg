        Nathaniel M. McKitterick, a retired Government consultant on international development, trade and finance, died Friday at his vacation home in Woodville, Va. Mr. McKitterick, who was 64 years old, also had a home in Alexandria, Va. The cause was a heart attack, said Allan Eustis, a son-in-law.
        Mr. McKitterick was a policy adviser to Eugene R. Black, the president of the World Bank, from 1956 to 1961, and was also a special adviser to President Lyndon B. Johnson on Southeast Asian economic affairs. He was a native of Hingham, Mass., and a graduate of Phillips Exeter Academy and Yale. In the late 1940's, he was an ensign in the Navy.
        Surviving are his wife, Katherine; two daughters, Molly of Washington and Sarah of Portland, Me.; a son, John of Columbia, Md.; his mother, Mary, of Hamden, Conn.; a sister, Gertrude of Figerolles, France, and three grandchildren.

        Jack L. Ashby, an investment banker and a retired president of the Kaiser Steel Corporation, died Jan. 7 at Children's Hospital in San Francisco, where he had been taken after a short illness. He was 78 years old.
        Mr. Ashby, who was born in Berkeley, Calif., was a graduate of Stanford University. He was with E. F. Hutton and other investment firms before joining Kaiser Steel, a subsididary of Kaiser Steel Resources Inc., at its founding in 1941. By 1959 he had become president of the company; a decade later he was named vice chairman.
        Mr. Ashby was instrumental in bringing international attention to ore discoveries in Canada, Australia and other countries. He was a founder of the the United International Shipping Corporation, which moved materials produced in Africa, Australia, Japan and the United States.
        Mr. Ashby, who lived in San Francisco, is survived by his wife, the former Patricia Robbins; two sons, Peter, of Novato, Calif., and Richard, of Mission Viejo, Calif., and two daughters, Sarah, of Darien, Conn., and Charlotte Flanagan of San Francisco.

        Walter John Lee, the owner and publisher of The Westfield (N.J.) Leader for 62 years, died on Wednesday at his home in Westfield after a long illness. He was 83 years old. Mr. Lee took over The Westfield Leader, a weekly newspaper, from his father in 1928, shortly after graduating from Lehigh University. He was still publishing the 100-year-old newspaper at the time of his death.
        He was a past president of the New Jersey Press Association.
        He is survived by his wife, E. Harriet, and a son, Walter James, of Keswick, Va.

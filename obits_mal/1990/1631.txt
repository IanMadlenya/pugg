        Dr. Edward Mahon Kline, an educator, cardiologist and retired medical director of the General Electric Company, died on Monday at his home in Southport, Conn. He was 81 years old.
        He died of a heart attack, his wife, Dr. Susan Anderson Kline, said.
        Dr. Kline joined the medical faculty at Case Western Reserve University in Cleveland in 1939 after receiving his medical degree there in 1934 and completing his medical training. He was on the faculty until 1966 and maintained a private practice until 1963. He was also a consulting physician for the Lamp Division of General Electric in Cleveland from 1949 to 1966 and was then appointed corporate medical director for G.E. in New York. He joined the faculty of Cornell University Medical College in 1967 and retired from G.E. and Cornell in 1974.
        In addition to his wife, he is survived by two daughters, Connie Perry of Deerfield, Ill., and Katherine Briggs of Fairfield, Conn.; and five grandchildren.

        Herman S. Bloch, a retired chemist and inventor and a former chairman of the American Chemical Society, died on Saturday at Northwestern Memorial Hospital in Chicago. He was 78 years old and lived in Skokie, Ill.
        His son said Mr. Bloch died after suffering a heart attack.
        Mr. Bloch was born in Chicago and received a B.A. and a Ph.D. from the University of Chicago. He began his career in 1936 with Universal Oil Products, a subsidiary of Allied-Signal Inc. He retired in 1977 as deputy director of research.
        He was elected to the National Academy of Sciences in 1975.
        In the 1950's he was an inventor of the catalytic converter, a device that removes pollutants from automobile exhaust. He held 200 patents either alone or with others.
        He was the first chairman of the Skokie Human Relations Commission and for many years was chairman of the Cook County Housing Authority.
        Mr. Bloch is survived by his wife, the former E. Judith Kahn; a son, Aaron of Bridgewater, N.J.; two daughters, Janet Martin of Miami and Merry Jones of Gladwyne, Pa.; a sister, Miriam Lovins of Santa Fe, N.M., and five grandchildren.

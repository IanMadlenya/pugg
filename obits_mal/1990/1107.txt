        Alan H. Shure, co-founder, chairman and chief executive of the International Components Corporation, an electronics manufacturer based in Chicago, died Wednesday at Northwestern Memorial Hospital in Chicago, after a brief illness. He was 59 years old and lived in Chicago and Nederland, Colo.
        In 1967 Mr. Shure, with James Gaza, founded International Components, specializing in making tiny motors for electric toothbruhes, toy trucks, hair dryers and the like, with plants in Japan, Korea, Taiwan and China.
        Mr. Shure was also active in real estate development in Chicago and Nederland.
        He was a graduate of the United States Naval Academy in Annapolis in 1953 and served as a captain in the Marine Corps until 1959.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Michal Colby; a brother, Myron of Chicago; two daughters, Janet of Chicago and Carol Ann of Carbondale, Colo.; a son, Edward of Nederland; two stepdaughters, Elizabeth and Katherine Laidlaw, both of Chicago, and four grandchildren.

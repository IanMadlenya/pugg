        Theodore Birnbaum, a New York business executive, died on Wednesday at St. Clare's Hospital in Manhattan. He was 73 years old and lived in Ossining, N.Y.
        Mr. Birnbaum died of a heart attack in his office, said his wife, Janet Farnham.
        At his death, Mr. Birnbaum was chief financial officer of Cardinal Type Service, which he joined in 1988. He had been chairman of Automatech Graphics Corporation in Manhattan and president and chief operating officer of the Venus Pen and Pencil Company.
        He was born in Washington and was a graduate of the Wharton School of Finance. He enlisted in the Army during World War II and was later commissioned a lieutenant.
        In addition to his wife, he is survived by three daughters, Cristin and Meg, both of Boston and Lyuba, of Annapolis, Md.

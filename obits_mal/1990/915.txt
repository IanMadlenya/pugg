        Adm. Petar Simic, a Politburo member and the political chief of the armed forces, died of a brain hemorrhage in Belgrade today, the official Tanyug press agency reported. He was 57 years old.
        Admiral Simic had advocated strong army and Communist Party influence in Yugoslav politics.
        His hard-line stands were often criticized by news organizations and officials in Slovenia and Croatia, the liberal Yugoslav republics that have rejected the Communist Party monopoly.
        Tanyug made no mention of survivors.

        John B. Griffin, a lawyer in Manhattan for more than 50 years, died of a heart attack on Thursday at his home in Larchmont, N.Y. He was 84 years old.
        Mr. Griffin's clients included the Bristol-Myers Company, the Du Pont Company, the American Telephone and Telegraph Company and the National Manufacturers Association.
        In 1950 his client, Wallboard Machinery Company, sued a Soviet trading company that had canceled contracts for equipment for plants in the Soviet Union. Mr. Griffin won $158,701 for his client.
        Mr. Griffin received his law degree from the New York University School of Law in 1932 and opened a practice on East 42d Street. He moved his office to Larchmont in 1985.
        Surviving are two daughters, Dr. Anne Griffin, of Manhattan, and Cornelia Griffin Farmer, of Pittsburgh; two sisters, Evelyn B. Griffin and Rosella Griffin McCann, of Woodridge, N.J., and four grandchildren.

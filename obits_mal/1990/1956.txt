        Robert M. Egan, a former senior vice president and management supervisor at Saatchi & Saatchi Advertising, died on Monday at the Memorial Sloan-Kettering Cancer Center. He was 55 years old and lived in Garden City, L.I.
        He died of pancreatic cancer, his family said.
        Mr. Egan joined the Dancer Fitzgerald Sample agency, now part of Saatchi & Saatchi, in 1961. During his tenure, which ended with his retirement this year, he supervised the Life Savers account, which grew in billings from $2 million to $80 million a year while he was there.
        He was born in St. Albans, Queens, and was a graduate of Georgetown University and the Wharton School.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Betty Picinich; three sons, Leo, of Manhattan, and Kenneth and Michael, both of Garden City; a daughter, Nancy, also of Garden City; his mother, Rita, of Denver; a brother, Peter, of Manhattan, and a sister, Virginia, of Denver.

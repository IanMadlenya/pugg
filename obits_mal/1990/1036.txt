        Mr. Affandi, an Expressionist painter whose works were exhibited worldwide, died Wednesday after a long illness. He was 83 years old.
        The artist died in the river-front home he designed 400 miles southeast of Jakarta, on Indonesia's main island, Java. He had been in a coma for several months.
        Mr. Affandi, considered Indonesia's foremost Expressionist painter, was born in western Java and began professional work in 1938.
        His works were exhibited in the United States, London, Paris, Rome, Rio de Janeiro and the Hague.
        Survivors include four children and several grandchildren.

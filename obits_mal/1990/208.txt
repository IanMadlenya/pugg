        Theodore I. Felner, a retired executive of Burlington Industries and of Macy's New York, died of an embolism on Wednesday at his winter home in Pompano Beach, Fla. He was 81 years old and also had a residence in Manhattan.
        Mr. Felner, who was born in Philadelphia and brought up in Brooklyn, was a 1930 graduate of New York University. He joined Macy's as a trainee that year and rose to become a merchandise vice president before he left to join Burlington in 1950.
        After serving as president of a subsidiary, the Burlington Retail Fabrics Company, he was made a vice president of the parent concern in 1953. He retired in 1973 but continued to serve as a textile marketing consultant until his death.
        For many years, Mr. Felner was chairman of the Carmel (N.Y.) Republican Committee. He was also a member of the Taconic State Park and Recreation Commission.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Sylvia Leopold; a son, Richard, of Mamaroneck, N.Y., and three grandchildren.

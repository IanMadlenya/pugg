
       W. Craig Bennett, a vice president with Citicorp and a New York City official in the Lindsay administration, died on Monday at the Memorial Sloan-Kettering Cancer Center in Manhattan. He was 45 years old and lived in Glen Ridge, N.J.
        At Citicorp, Mr. Bennett was a vice president in the United States investment management division in Manhattan. Before he joined Citicorp, Mr. Bennett was deputy director of human resources under Mayor John V. Lindsay from 1972 to 1975. In 1970 and 1971 he was a consultant in public administration services for Booz, Allen & Hamilton Company.
        Mr. Bennett was born in Montclair and graduated cum laude from Harvard in 1967. He received a master's degree in public administration from the John F. Kennedy School of Government at Harvard in 1972.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Ondina de Zayas; two daughters, Lourdes and Lilian, and a son, Bryan, all of Glen Ridge; and his parents, William F. and Marjorie Bennett of Nutley, N.J.

        Herbert Whyman, a Manhattan lawyer for more than half a century, died of bladder cancer on Wednesday at his home in Roslyn Heights, L.I. He was 76 years old.
        Mr. Wyman, who specialized in real-estate law, was a graduate of City College and the Fordham Law School. He became a partner in the Manhattan firm of King, Frank & Whyman in 1939 after serving for three years as a staff attorney to the board that drafted a new administrative code for New York City.
        The firm, later Frank & Whyman, was dissolved in 1980 and Mr. Whyman became counsel to Moroze, Sherman, Gordon & Gordon, where he remained until his death.
        In the late 1970's Mr. Whyman was counsel to the Bedford-Stuyvesant Restoration Corporation, an antipoverty organization in Brooklyn. Earlier, when he lived on the West Side of Manhattan, he was a director of the Councl for Community Action. After moving to Roslyn in 1949, he was a director of the Roslyn Economic Opportunity Council, which sponsored urban renewal.
        Mr. Whyman is survived by his wife, the former Betty Price; two sons, Dr. Andrew, of San Francisco, and Dr. John, of Wellesley, Mass; a brother, Martin, of Hollywood, Fla., and five grandchildren.

        Harry W. Faeber, a designer of printing presses and a consultant to printing concerns around the world, died at Brookhaven (L.I.) Hospital on Thursday. He was 95 years old and lived on Shelter Island. His family said he died of pneumonia.
        Mr. Faeber, who ended his formal education in the eighth grade, held more than 100 patents on printing equipment.
        He was chief engineer for Time, Inc., from 1946 to 1961 and installed the first high-speed color press for the publisher's magazines. Earlier he had been general manager of Babcock Printing of Philadelphia and a design engineer for the R.R. Donnelly Company of Chicago. He was born in New York City.
        Mr. Faeber is survived by two daughters, Marion Gleason and Phyllis Wallace of Shelter Island; eight grandchildren, and nine great-grandchildren.

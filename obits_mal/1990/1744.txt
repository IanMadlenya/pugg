        Harris Levin, a lawyer who specialized in bankruptcy law, died yesterday at Franklin Medical Center in Valley Stream, L.I. He was 83 years old and lived in Woodmere, L.I.
        He died of congestive heart failure, his son, Dr. Stephen Levin of Manhattan, said.
        Mr. Levin was a founder and partner in the Manhattan law firm of Levin & Weintraub, which he and Benjamin Weintraub opened in 1933. The firm merged this year with Kaye, Scholer, Fierman, Hays & Handler of Manhattan. He retired in 1983.
        With Mr. Weintraub, Mr. Levin was co-author of a number of handbooks on bankruptcy reoganization and creditors' rights.
        Besides his son, he is survived by his wife, the former Camille Sormani; two daughters, Ruth Edenbaum of Princeton Junction, N.J., and Judith Jamieson of North Bennington, Vt.; a sister, Rebecca Karp of Lake Worth, Fla., and seven grandchildren.

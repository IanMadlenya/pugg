        Earl E. Welch, a textbook publisher and education consultant, died on Saturday at the King James Care Center in Chatham, N.J. He was 88 years old and lived in Madison, N.J.
        Mr. Welch was president of the Silver Burdett Publishing Company, which he joined as an editor in 1937. He became editor in chief in 1941 and created several series of elementary-school texts, including ''Making Sure of Arithmetic'' and ''Man's Ways and Times.'' Many of his books are still in use.
        He was named president in 1960 and retired in 1968, becoming a consultant.
        Mr. Welch was a graduate of the University of Iowa and held two master's degrees from the University of Wisconsin. He was a high school principal and superintendent in Wisconsin and Minnesota before entering publishing.
        Mr. Welch was married to the former Kathryn Johnston for 63 years until her death last November. He is survived by two daughters, Barbara Valk of Madison and Cynthia Weaver of Chatham, and four grandchildren.

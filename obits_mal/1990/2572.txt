
        Francisco Gabilondo Soler, a writer of children's songs, has died of a heart attack, the independent news service Excelsior reported today. He was 83 years old.
        Mr. Gabilondo Soler, who wrote hundreds of songs, died on Friday at his home. No cause of death was mentioned in the report.
        Mr. Gabilondo Soler was born in the Gulf Coast state of Veracruz, and moved to Mexico City in 1925. He was on the air for 27 years, and his nickname, Cri-Cri, a child's expression for cricket in Spanish, became a household word.

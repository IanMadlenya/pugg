
        Samuel J. Moss, a venture-capital specialist in the field of new technologies, died on Dec. 8 in Tiburon, Calif., where he lived. He was 54 years old.
        He died of leukemia, his family said.
        Mr. Moss was a general partner in Wolfensohn Partners, which co-manages a venture-capital fund and is associated with the Manhattan investment banking firm of James D. Wolfensohn Inc. He had been a vice president and director of the information systems group at Time Inc., from 1980 to 1983, and from 1973 to 1978 he was vice president for systems and communication at American Express.
        He was born in Milwaukee, graduated from Ripon College and held master's and doctoral degrees in physics from the University of Wisconsin.
        After serving in the Army and doing research work, he turned to business as a specialist in systems and communications.
        Mr. Moss is survived by a daughter, Ruth, of Manhattan; a son, Alan, of Boston, and two sisters, Nancy Denz of Tiburon and Elizabeth Slutsky of Stamford, Conn. His fiancee and longtime companion was Alice Rohrbacher.

        S. Zachary Swidler, a tax law specialist and a founder of Swidler & Swidler law firm, died of heart disease on Friday at New York Hospital. He was 72 years old and moved last year from Manhattan to Boynton Beach, Fla.
        Mr. Swidler graduated from New York University and the New York Law School, while he worked in accounting during the day. He served in the Army in World War II. He and a son, Steven, founded Swidler & Swidler in Manhattan in 1978.
        Besides Steven, of Ardsley, N.Y., he is survived by his wife, Bea; two other sons, Dr. Sanford, of Stamford, Conn., and Robert, assistant counsel to Gov. Mario M. Cuomo, of East Greenbush, N.Y.; a sister, Marsha Hazelkorn of Boynton Beach, and five grandchildren.

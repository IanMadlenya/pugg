        James Scheuer Wallerstein, a biochemist and the president of the Sarah S. Ollesheimer Fund, a college scholarship foundation in Mount Kisco, N.Y., died on Thursday at his home in Mount Kisco. He was 79 years old.
        Mr. Wallerstein joined the foundation, which was established in 1924 in honor of his aunt, in the early 1940's.
        In the 50's, he was a brewmaster for the Wallerstein Company Laboratories, which processed malts and hops for beer making. The company was sold in the 50's.
        Mr. Wallerstein also wrote children's plays and books for young adults.
        Surviving are two sons, Ronald, of Stamford, Conn., and Thomas, of Carlisle, Mass.; a sister, Elizabeth Schappert of Hollywood, and four grandchildren.

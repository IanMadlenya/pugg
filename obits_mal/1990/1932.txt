        Kendall W. Goodwyn, retired managing editor of Popular Science magazine, died on Sept. 15 at the Rosary Hill Nursing Home in Hawthorne, N.Y. He was 79 years old and lived in North Tarrytown, N.Y.
        His family said he died of lung cancer.
        He was with Popular Science for a quarter century, starting in 1951 as a reporter and rising in 1962 to managing editor, a post he held until he retired in 1976. Before joining Popular Science he was editor in chief of Adventure magazine.
        He was born in Springfield, Mass., and attended New York University. In World War II he served in the Army Signal Corps in Europe and the Pacific.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Helen Zeman, and two daughters, Pamela Goodwyn-Wolk and Camilla, both of Ossining, N.Y.

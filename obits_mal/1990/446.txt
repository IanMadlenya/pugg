        A. F. Jacobson, retired president of the Northwestern Bell Telephone Company, died at Clarkson Hospital in Omaha on Wednesday after a brief illness. He was 84 years old.
        Mr. Jacobson had worked for the telephone company in Omaha, Minneapolis, Duluth, Minn., and Sioux Falls, S.D. In 1953 he was named assistant vice president of operations for the American Telephone and Telegraph Company in in New York.
        In 1955 he became president of Northwestern Bell, retiring in 1970. He then worked as a consultant to an Italian telephone company.
        Mr. Jacobson was a former president of the Telephone Pioneers of America. He was a former chairman of the board of Creighton University.
        Survivors include his wife, Ruth, and a son, Allen, of St. Paul.

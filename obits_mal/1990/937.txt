        Irving Kobrin, a retired business executive, died of pneumonia yesterday at Lenox Hill Hospital. He was 87 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Kobrin, a Latvian immigrant who came to New York as a boy, started his business career with Kobrin Brothers, jewelry retailers in Manhattan. He later became president of the Lanella Corporation, a Manhattan textile importing concern, and in 1964, he became a partner and executive vice president of Mechanical Mirror Works, a mirror manufacturer in Washington Heights. He retired in 1984.
        Mr. Kobrin is survived by his wife, the former Hortense Freezer; three sons, Lawrence A. and Richard E., both of Manhattan, and Jay A., of Stamford, Conn., a brother, Abraham, of Hillsboro Beach, Fla., and two sisters, Bertha Steinfeld of Laguna Hills, Calif., and Rose Bush of Manhattan, and five grandchildren.


        Howard S. Spingarn, a retired stockbroker, died on Saturday at his home in Manhasset, L.I. He was 82 years old.
        Mr. Spingarn, who had cancer, died of pnuemonia, his wife, the former Georgette Alexander, said.
        Mr. Spingarn was a founding partner in Spingarn, Heine & Company in Manhattan in 1952. The company took over the management of Mutual Shares, a small mutual fund which grew in size and value and is now managed by Heine Securities, the successor to Spingarn, Heine & Company. Mr. Spingarn retired in 1970.
        He was born in Manhattan and graduated from Columbia University in 1927.
        Besides his wife, he is survived by a son, James, of Glen Head, L.I.; a daughter, Ellen Shapiro of Manhattan; and five grandchildren.

        William A. Grier, a retired lawyer in Manhattan for the General Motors Corporation, died last Friday at a nursing home in Vero Beach, Fla. He was 78 years old and lived in Vero Beach.
        Mr. Grier died of throat cancer, his daughter Judith said.
        He joined General Motors in 1936 and retired in 1977.
        He was born in Brooklyn in 1912 and graduated from Princeton University and the Columbia University Law School. He was on the New Castle, N.Y., Town Board. Later he was a town commissioner in Highland, Fla. In World War II, he served in the Third Army in Europe.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Marjorie English; two daughters, Judith Davidson of Clinton, Conn., and Susan Knoll of Houston, and six grandchildren.

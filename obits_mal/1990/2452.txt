
        Burton S. Goldberg, a computer-information technolgy consultant and a retired executive of I.B.M., died on Sunday while swimming at the White Plains Y.M.C.A. He was 62 years old and lived in Rye Brook, N.Y.
        He died of a heart attack, his son Brad said.
        Mr. Goldberg was assistant general manager for the International Business Machines Corporation's entry systems in Europe when he retired in 1984. He then formed his own consulting concern. He was an adviser to New Enterprise Associates and to the Mayfield Fund and was on the board of several computer software companies.
        A graduate of the Colorado School of Mines, he joined I.B.M. in 1958 and worked at the company's offices in Denver, Houston and New York. He was involved in developing product designs for several I.B.M. systems and was a vice president for systems development.
        In addition to Brad, of Dallas, he is survived by his wife, Lenisse; a son, Paul, of Brookfield, Conn.; a daughter, Nancy Grass of Somers, N.Y.; a sister, Mildred Feldman of Denver, and seven grandchildren.

        Franklin Waldheim, a former vice president of Walt Disney Productions who was a copyright lawyer for the firm for more than 50 years, died of congenital heart failure on Sunday at a nursing home in Boca Raton, Fla. He was 93 years old.
        Mr. Waldheim, a graduate of City College of New York and New York Unviersity Law School, joined the Disney organization in 1929 to handle a legal problem involving cartoon distribution. He remained with the company until he retired at the age of 87.
        He taught public school while studying law and later lectured and wrote articles on copyright matters.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Eleanor Sheehan; three grandchildren, and two great-grandchildren.

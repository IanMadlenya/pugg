        John A. Ludlow, a New York broker and former governor of the American Stock Exchange, died on Wednesday at a nursing home in Cedar Grove, N.J. He was 95 years old and lived in Upper Montclair, N.J.
        He died of a heart attack, a spokesman for the family said.
        He was the founder of J. A. Ludlow & Company, brokers, and served as its president for more than 40 years. He retired in 1975. He was also the Mayor of Saltaire, on Fire Island, from 1950 to 1958, and was a town trustee.
        Mr. Ludlow, who was born in East Orange, N.J., served as an Army sergeant in World War I. He is survived by his wife, the former Louise Schlachter; a daughter, Betty Jean Vautier of Little Falls, N.J.; a son, John A. Jr., of Kansas City, Mo.; 10 grandchildren, and nine great-grandchildren.

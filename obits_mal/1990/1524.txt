        Lloyd Walter Hazleton, retired president of Hazleton Laboratories Inc., a biotechnology research company, died on Monday at his home in McLean, Va. He was 79 years old.  Dr. Hazleton and his wife, Harriet, founded the company in 1946 on the grounds of an old country schoolhouse in Fairfax, Va. It became one of the world's largest biological testing services, dealing with new chemicals and chemical compounds.
        In 1968 he retired and sold the business, which included laboratories and offices in California, Switzerland and Jamaica, and a cancer drug-screening laboratory in Reston, Va.. The company is now the Hazleton Laboratories Corporation, with headquarters in Herndon, Va.
        Dr. Hazleton was a graduate of the University of Washington, where he received a master's degree and a doctorate in pharmacology.
        His first wife, Harriet Rosenzweig, died in 1986. He is survived by his wife, the former Grace Murphy; a son, William of Atlanta, two daughters, Elaine Bolton of Griffin, Ga., and Mary Wills of Alexandria, Va.; a sister, June Cole of Vancouver, Wash., and six grandchildren.

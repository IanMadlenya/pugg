
        Walter L. Kidd, retired director of taxes for A.T.& T., died on Thursday at a nursing home in White Plains. He was 82 years old and lived in Scarsdale, N.Y.
        Mr. Kidd died of congestive heart failure, his wife, the former Vivian Ericson, said.
        He joined the Bell System in 1930 and rose to become director of taxes for the comptroller's office of A.T.& T. He retired in 1970.
        In World War II, he was a lieutenant colonel in the Tactical Air Command of the Army Air Force.
        He was born in Columbus, Ohio, and graduated from Ohio State University in 1930.
        Besides his wife, he is survived by two daughters, Virginia Pasternak of Bedford, N.Y., and Marylin Lidz of Calabasas, Calif., and four grandchildren.

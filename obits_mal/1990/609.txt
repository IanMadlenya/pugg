        Donald Davidson, a special assistant with the Houston Astros whose baseball career spanned more than 40 years, died today after having suffered from cancer. He was 64 years old.
        Mr. Davidson joined the Astros in 1976 as public relations director and had served the club as traveling secretary and as administrative assistant to the president. Mr. Davidson's affiliation with baseball began in 1939 when he was a batboy for the Boston Braves and the Red Sox. His administrative career began in 1948 with the Braves' public relations department.
        Mr. Davidson is survived by his wife, Patti; his son, Deehan, and two daughters, Patrika and Margo.

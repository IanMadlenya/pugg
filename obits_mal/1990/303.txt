        Mortimer Edelstein, a lawyer who was assistant general counsel to the Port Authority of New York and New Jersey before he became a partner in the law firm of Paul, Weiss, Wharton & Garrison, died on Thursday at his home in Longboat Key, Fla., where he had retired in 1977. He was 85 years old.
        Mr. Edelstein was born in Easton, Pa., and was a graduate of the Wharton School at the University of Pennsylvania and of the Cornell Law School. He joined the port agency, then the Port of New York Authority, in 1933. A decade later he became a partner in Paul, Weiss, moving later to his own firm, Young, Kaplan & Edelstein.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Rosabelle Winer; a daughter, Jane Rosenbloom of Manhattan, and two grandchildren.

        William Cahalan, a judge who was a former Michigan Racing Commissioner and Wayne County prosecutor, died of cancer on Wednesday at his home. He was 64 years old.
        Mr. Cahalan was Wayne County prosecutor for 16 years before quitting in 1983 to enter private practice. In 1985 Gov. James Blanchard appointed him Racing Commissioner. He was appointed to the Wayne County Circuit Court bench in 1989.
        He is survived by his wife, Mary; four daughters, three sons, two brothers, a sister and a grandchild.

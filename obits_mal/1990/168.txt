        John P. Devine, a municipal-bond trader, died of leukemia on Tuesday at Overlook Hospital in Summit, N.J. He was 52 years old and lived in Short Hills, N.J.
        Mr. Devine joined the Chase Manhattan Bank in 1955, after attending New York University. He became a senior vice president in charge of trading in government and municipal bonds. In 1982, he joined Manufacturers Hanover, leaving after less than a year to trade his own account.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Wilan Ryan: a son, Christopher, and a daughter, Deborah, both of Short Hills; four sisters, Mary Kane Kenny and Ginger Roll, both of Morristown, N.J., Bonnie Finn of Manhasset, L.I., and Susan Camille of Paris, and three brothers, Christopher Jr., of Manhattan, Gerald, of Rye, N.Y., and Robert, of Clifton, N.J.

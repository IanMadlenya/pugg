        George L. Hubbell Jr., a retired partner in the New York law firm of Cullen & Dykman, died on Thursday at his home in Garden City, L.I., at the age of 96. He died of pneumonia, a spokesman for the law firm said.
        Mr. Hubbell, a lifelong resident of Garden City, was a graduate of Williams College and earned a law degree at Columbia after serving in World War I as an infantry major.
        He entered law practice in 1919 and was a member of several Wall Street firms, dealing principally in corporate and trust matters. He was elected to the New York State Board of Regents in 1955 and served in that post for 10 years.
        Mr. Hubbell is survived by a brother, Sherwood, of Garden City; two sons, George 3d of Stuart, Fla., and William of Alexandria, Va.; three daughters, Elizabeth Parsons of Spokane, Wash., Barbara Fields of Lake Placid, Fla., and Susan Wohlers of Basking Ridge, N.J.; 19 grandchildren, and 20 great-grandchildren.

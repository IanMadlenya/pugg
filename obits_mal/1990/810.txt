        Bernard J. Miller, an advertising and public-relations executive who was founding publisher of Gentlemen's Quarterly magazine, died of liver cancer yesterday at his home in Manhattan. He was 76 years old.
        Mr. Miller helped start Gentlemen's Quarterly, which also calls itself GQ, in 1957, and became a vice president of Esquire Inc. in 1962. He started the Bernard J. Miller Organization, a public-relations and licensing concern, in 1973.
        His career began in 1946, when he joined Esquire Inc. as a space salesman for Apparel Arts magazine. He became publisher of Apparel Arts in 1948. Mr. Miller was born in New York.  He was on the executive committee of the National Conference of Christians and Jews and on the board of the United Jewish Appeal. Mr. Miller was in the Army from 1941 to 1945.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Frances Linder; a daughter, Susan Barocas of Manhattan, and two grandsons.

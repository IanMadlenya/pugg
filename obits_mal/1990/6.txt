        Stephen H. Mayer, chairman of the MEM Company, a toiletries concern with headquarters in Northvale, N.J., died of a stroke on Wednesday at Tucson (Ariz.) Medical Center. He was 74 years old and had homes in Tucson and Denver.
        In 1936, Mr. Mayer joined the perfume and soap company, which was founded in 1883 by his grandfather in Austria. From 1943 to 1946, he was a member of the Army Air Corps and the National Guard. He became president of the company in 1947 and chairman in 1973. Under his leadership, MEM developed the English Leather line of men's fragrances.
        Mr. Mayer was also director of the Cosmetics, Toiletry and Fragrance Association.
        Born in Vienna, he was a graduate of the University of Vienna and the Diplomatic Academy of Austria. He emigrated to the United States in 1939.
        Mr. Mayer is survived by his wife, the former Elizabeth C. Hieffer; a daughter, Laurie Beach of Philadelphia; a son, Gay, of Closter, N.J., and four grandchildren.

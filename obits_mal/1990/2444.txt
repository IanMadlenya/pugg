
        W, Barron Rockwell, a retired investment banker, died on Friday at the Rochester (Minn.) Methodist Hospital. He was 84 years old and had lived for the last 15 years in Denver and Estes Park, Colo., where he moved from Manhattan.
        He died of pneumonia, his family said.
        Mr. Rockwell, a native of Chicago, spent his entire career with the investment banking firm of Halsey, Stuart & Company, which he joined on his graduation from Princeton in 1928. He retired in 1971.
        He served in the Navy in World War II and was a past president of the Municipal Bond Club.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Georgia Rendle Sweet; a stepson, William E. Sweet 3d of Denver, and a stepdaughter, Diana Rumsey of Aspen, Colo.

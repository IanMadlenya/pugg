        John C. Lackas, a retired Army colonel and a professor emeritus of business at Queensborough Community College, died on Friday at the Monroe Health Care Center, in Monroe, N.J., where he lived. A family spokesman said he died of natural causes. He was 85 years old.
        Colonel Lackas, who served from 1942 to 1960, was a liaison officer to the British Air Force, commandant of the Army's Finance School and assistant comptroller for international affairs with the General Staff.
        At Queensborough College, where Dr. Lackas taught from 1960 to 1974, he was also dean of administration and acting president. He lived in Syosset, L.I. for 28 years.
        He was the director of the School of Business at Seton Hall University from 1938 to 1942, when he joined the Army and served in wartime Africa and Europe. His honors included the Legion of Merit.
        Survivors include his wife, the former Genevieve Meekins; and a sister, Marion James of Metuchen, N.J.

        The Rev. Paul J. Murphy, a Jesuit priest who was a former president of Morality in Media, the national anti-pornography organization, died Monday at Massachusetts General Hospital in Boston. He was 82 years old and lived in Weston, Mass. A spokeswoman for Morality in Media said he died of pneumonia.
        Father Murphy was ordained in 1938 after graduating from Weston College. He taught at St. George's College in Kingston, Jamaica, at Holy Cross College in Worcester, Mass., and at Boston College. He was a Navy chaplain in World War II and for 16 years was director of St. Joseph's Retreat League for working men in the Charlestown section of Boston. He was president of Morality in Media from 1985 to 1988 and was then its vice chairman.
        He is survived by a brother, Richard of South Dennis, Mass., and a sister, Kathleen Prendiville of Millbury, Mass.


        Wing N. Toy, an electrical engineer who was with A. T. & T. Bell Laboratories for 37 years and held 28 patents in communications applications, died on Sunday at Evanston Hospital in Illinois. He was 64 years old and lived in Glen Ellyn, Ill.
        He died of liver cancer, his family said.
        Mr.Toy was born in Canton, China, and came to the United States when he was 13 years old. He held bachelor's and master's degrees from the University of Illinois and a doctorate from the University of Pennsylvania, all in electrical engineering.
        He joined Bell Labs in Murray Hill, N.J., in 1953, later working in Holmdel, N.J., and Napierville, Ill. As a supervisor in broad-band communications development, he played an important role in devising equipment for A. T. & T.'s digital switching network.
        He was a Fellow of the Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers and wrote three textbooks on computers.
        Mr. Toy is survived by his wife, the former Ramayne Louis; his father, Share Young Toy of Skokie, Ill., and two sisters, Pearl Lee and Agnes Hwe, also of Skokie.

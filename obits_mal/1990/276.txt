        Casimir J. F. Patrick, a retired bank executive and a lawyer in Manhattan for 40 years, died of congestive heart failure on Tuesday at his home in Port Washington, L.I. He was 83 years old.
        Mr. Patrick retired in 1971 as executive vice president and general counsel of the New York Bank for Savings, where he worked for 26 years.
        He began his law career in Manhattan with Larkin, Rathbone & Perry shortly after graduating from the Fordham Law School in 1930. He was later associated with a firm headed by Joseph V. McKee and then with White & Case.
        He is survived by his wife, Florence; two sons, Casimir, of Port Washington, and Edward, of Knoxville, Tenn.; two daughters, Carol Breslin of Port Washington and Martia Patrick of Manhattan; two brothers, Edward, of Port Washington, and Louis, of Salem, Va.; a sister, Mary Patrick of White Plains, and eight grandchildren.

        Rear Adm. Paul Foley Jr., former head of the Command and Staff Department of the Naval War College in Newport, R.I., and a retired Hofstra University official, died on Saturday at his home in Manhasset, N.Y. He was 81 years old and died of pnuemonia and congestive heart failure, his wife, Cornelia, said.
        Admiral Foley was head of the Command and Staff Department at the War College in 1953. In 1954 he became commander of the carrier Bennington and, in 1958, chief of staff of the Naval Post Graduate School in Monterey, Calif.
        He retired from the Navy in 1969 and moved to Manhasset to join Hofstra University, where he supervised construction of many campus buildings. He retired in 1971. Admiral Foley was born in Annapolis, Md., and graduated from the Naval Academy in 1929.
        Besides his wife, he is survived by a daughter, Jean, of Severna Park, Md.; two sons, John, of Columbia, Md., and Mark, of Cooperstown, N.Y.; a brother, Francis D., of Annapolis, and two grandsons.

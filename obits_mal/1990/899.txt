        Dr. James G. E. Smith, a social anthropologist and curator at the Museum of the American Indian-Heye Foundation, died of brain cancer on April 2 at St. Vincent's Hospital. He was 63 years old and lived in the Riverdale section of the Bronx.
        Dr. Smith, who joined the museum staff in 1978, was an authority on the native people of the central Canadian sub-arctic. For the last eight years, he had also advised the Lubicon Lake Indian nation.
        He was born in Chicago and received bachelor's, master's and doctoral degrees from the University of Chicago. He taught at several universities, including the University of Indiana, Moorhead State in Minnesota and McMaster University in Ontario. He was also an ethnologist at the National Museum of Canada and a research associate of the Royal Ontario Museum.
        In 1968, he was a co-founder of the Algonkian Conference, an annual gathering of scholars of the North American Indian linguistic group's culture.
        There are no immediate survivors.

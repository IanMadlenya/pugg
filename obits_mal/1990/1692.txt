        Robert W. Trombino, chief of stadium and marina operations for the New York City Department of Parks and Recreation, died on Sunday at his home in East Elmhurst, Queens. He was 39 years old.
        A department spokesman said he died after a heart attack.
        Mr. Trombino oversaw the major city-owned stadiums, including Shea Stadium, Yankee Stadium and the National Tennis Center in Flushing, Queens.
        He was born in Manhattan, attended Queens College and served in the Army for five years.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Nina Maviglia, three sons, Robert, Anthony and Joseph; his mother, Bella Mandracchia of Rego Park, Queens, and two brothers, Joseph Riccitelli of Bellerose, Queens, and Ronald Riccitelli, of Flushing, Queens.

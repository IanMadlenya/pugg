        Stephen Bander, president of the National Systems Corporation, died of a brain tumor yesterday at the Memorial Sloan-Kettering Cancer Center in Manhattan. He was 43 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Bander joined National Systems, a Manhattan electronics data exchange software manufacturer, in 1983.
        A 1972 graduate of Union College in Schenectady, N.Y. with a master's degree in operations research, he was vice president from 1974 to 1983 of T.M.I. Systems and its successor company, the SEI Corporation, both automated financial services concerns in Manhattan. Previously he had worked for General Electric in Schenectady.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Wendy McKinley; his mother, Anna, of Floral Park, Queens, and a sister, Anna Freier of Chestnut Ridge, N.Y.

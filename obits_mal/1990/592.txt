        Charles R. Jones, retired vice president for external affairs for A.T.& T.'s eastern region, died on Saturday at his home in Westfield, N.J., after a long illness. He was 61 years old.
        Mr. Jones joined the New York Telephone Company as an installer in 1955. He became vice president for service costs in 1970. In 1976 he moved to A.T.& T. as assistant vice president of state regulatory matters and was named vice president for external affairs in 1983. He retired in 1988.
        Mr. Jones was born in Beacon, N.Y., and graduated from Union College in 1951. He joined the Navy in 1951, serving as operations officer on destroyer duty in Korea.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Mary Psarakis; two daughters, Bethany Sicilia of Eastonia, N.C., and Hope Cottle of Richmond, Va.; two sons, Charles H., of Mamaroneck, N.Y., and Thomas C., of Westfield, and two grandchildren.

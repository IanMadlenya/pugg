        Jule Gordon, a retired executive who founded the National Barber and Beauty Manufacturers Association, died of cancer on Monday at his home in Manhattan. He was 87 years old.
        Mr. Gordon, a former president of the association, represented the industry before the War Production Board in World War II. He later developed a program to protect manufacturers from product piracy.
        After the war he joined the Kreml Company, a manufacturer of men's hair products, as vice president of sales and advertising. In 1952, he left Kreml to form companies in Manhattan, the Gordon/Evans Sales Company, which has been dissolved, and the Gambine Company, manufactuer of cosmetics and hair products. It was sold in 1986, when Mr. Gordon retired.
        Mr. Gordon was born in Chicago. He attended Northwestern University.
        Surviving are two daughters, Joyce Gordon Grant of Manhattan and Jill, of Westport, Conn.; two brothers, Neal, of Delray, Fla., and Gilbert, of Chicago; two sisters, Lenore Brown of North Miami Beach, Fla., and Geraldine DeFant of Marquette, Mich., and two grandchildren.

        Dr. Harry Louis Segal, a gastroenterologist and educator, died of pneumonia on Monday at Strong Memorial Hospital in Rochester. He was 90 years old and lived in Brighton, N.Y., a suburb of Rochester.
        He was physician in chief at Genesee Hospital in Rochester from 1955 to 1967 and had been on the staff since 1930. He also directed the gastroenterology training program at Strong Memorial Hospital in Rochester from 1959 to 1967.
        In 1970, Dr. Segal was named professor emeritus of medicine at the University of Rochester Medical School, where he had taught since 1932. He was also coordinator of medical education at Rochester General Hospital and helped establish a medical school in Lagos, Nigeria. He was in private practice in Rochester from 1929 until his retirement in 1972.
        Dr Segal was a 1924 graduate of Syracuse University Medical School. He was the co-developer of the gastroscopic color photographic system and received many honors, including the Schindler award of the American Gastroscopic Society in 1959.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Evelyn Edith Buff; a sister, Fannie Garber of Rochester, and several nieces and nephews.

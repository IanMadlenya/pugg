        J. Edward Skeath, chairman of the mathematics department at Swarthmore College, died of cancer on Sunday at his home in Swarthmore, Pa. He was 53 years old.
        Dr. Skeath, a Swarthmore graduate who received his doctorate at the University of Illinois, joined the faculty at Swarthmore in 1965 after teaching at Cornell University for two years.
        He was Buffington Professor of Mathematics at Swarthmore and had been chairman of the mathematics department since 1981. He also served as dean of male students in the 1970's.
        Dr. Skeath spent a year as a Fulbright Scholar at the University of Liverpool and on sabbatical leaves was a visiting professor at the University of Warwick, in Coventry, England, and at the Institute for Theoretical Physics in Trieste.
        His research interests included complex analysis and symbolic integration.
        He is survived by his wife, Ann Wertz Skeath; a daughter, Susan of Wellesley, Mass.; a son, James of Madison, Wis., and his stepmother, Frances K. Skeath of Montoursville, Pa.

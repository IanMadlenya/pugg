        Earl Wilson, a Republican who represented Indiana in the House of Representatives for 22 years, died on Friday. He was 84 years old and lived in Bedford.
        Mr. Wilson served in the House from 1941 until he was defeated in the 1958 election. He regained his seat in 1960 and served until 1965. He was a member of the Appropriations Committee and was a frequent critic of Federal spending. He was an Indiana State Senator from 1969 to 1976.
        His survivors include a son, Philip E.; a daughter, Linda Corman, a brother and a sister.

        Samuel G. Domash, a retired businessman and philanthropist, died of complications of diabetes on Tuesday at Brookdale Hospital in Brooklyn. He was 89 years old and lived in the Manhattan Beach section of Brooklyn.
        Mr. Domash was a founder and principal stockholder of Conway Import Company, a Brooklyn manufacturer and wholesaler of condiments, mayonnaise, salad dressings and edible oils.
        He graduated from the City College of New York and the New York University Law School in the 1920's.
        He was active in Jewish philanthropic causes and was a founder and president of Temple Beth El in Manhattan Beach. He also had served as treasurer of Brooklyn Jewish Hospital.
        He is survived by his wife, Esther; a son, Norman, of Great Neck, L.I.; two daughters, Muriel Pace of Merrick, L.I., and Florence Gittelman of Manhattan; a sister, Rose Siroty of Brooklyn; a brother, Joseph Drew, of Oklahoma; seven grandchildren, and four great-grandchildren.

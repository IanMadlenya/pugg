
        Daniel E. O'Sullivan, an insurance industry executive, died on Sunday at Fairfax Hospital in Fairfax, Va. He was 61 years old and lived in McLean, Va.
        He died of a heart attack, a family spokesman said.
        Mr. O'Sullivan was president andchief executive of Ullico Inc., the holding company in Washington for several insurance companies, including the Union Labor Life Insurance Company.
        Mr. O'Sullivan was a 1948 graduate of Fordham University, and he joined Union Labor Life in 1950. He later served two years in the Army and in 1958 moved to San Francisco, where he managed Union Labor Life's western division. He returned to New York in 1964 as vice president and assistant to the president and was named a senior vice president in 1969. He became executive vice president in 1970, president in 1979 and chief executive in 1984.
        Mr. O'Sullivan was also a board member of the American Council of Life Insurance and the Health Insurance Association of America.
        He is survived by his wife, Rosemary; two daughters, Nora Elizabeth, of Washington, and Julie Rose, of Manhattan; a son, Daniel, of Manhattan; a sister, Eileen Bergen of Pearl River, N.Y., and a brother, Edward, of Gaithersburg, Md.

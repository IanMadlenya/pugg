        Richard Louie, an associate director of the Freer Gallery of Art and the Arthur M. Sackler Gallery of the Smithsonian Institution system, died on Sunday in the Washington Hospital Center after being struck by a car on Saturday in Arlington, Va. He was 51 years old and lived in Arlington. Mr. Louie became assistant director of the Freer in 1978 and participated in the planning and financing of the Sackler in the 1980's. Both museums are noted for their collections of Asian art.
        Mr. Louie, who grew up in the Cleveland area, received a bachelor's degree from Princeton University in 1960 and master's degrees in Far East studies and economics from the University of Washington in Seattle.
        Before joining the Smithsonian, he was an economist for the Institute for Defense Analyses in Alexandria; the Private Investment Company for Asia in Tokyo, and the United States Postal Service. Mr. Louie had also been president of Asian Development Business Services and vice president of May Lee Industries, both in New York City.
        He is survived by his wife, Katherine Preciado; his mother, Gam B. Louie of St. Louis, and two sisters, Sue Fay King of St. Louis and Maie Lin Carrell of Evansville, Ind.

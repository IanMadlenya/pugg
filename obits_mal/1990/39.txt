        Jack L. DiGiovanni, financial director of The New York Times circulation department, died yesterday at the Columbia-Presbyterian Medical Center after a short illness. He was 54 years old and lived in Oradell, N.J.
        Before joining The Times in 1987, Mr. DiGiovanni was a financial executive with The Daily News for 20 years. Before that he was with Dow Jones & Company.
        He was a native of Manhattan and a graduate of City College and held a master's degree in business administration from Pace University. From 1985 to 1988 he was a Councilman of the Borough of Oradell and at his death was chairman of the Oradell Envionmental Committee.
        Mr. DiGiovanni is survived by his wife, the former Patrina Barone; two daughters, Diane and Patricia, both of Oradell; his parents, Maria and Sebastian DiGiovanni of Elmont, L.I.; a brother, Michael, of Manhattan, and a sister, Catherine Freeman of Queens Village.

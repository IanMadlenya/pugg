        Philip Nichols Jr., a senior judge of the United States Court of Appeals and a former Commissioner of Customs, died of a heart attack Friday at Georgetown University Hospital in Washington. He was 82 years old and lived in Washington.
        Judge Nichols served on the Court of Claims for 16 years. When the court was reorganized in 1982, he remained as a judge of the Court of Appeals for the Federal Circuit. He assumed senior status in 1983. Before that he had served for two years on the United States Customs Court and as United States Commissioner of Customs from 1961 to 1964.
        A graduate of Harvard University Law School, he practiced in Boston for six years before moving to Washington in 1938.
        He is survived by his wife, Dorothy; two sons, Donald of Vienna, Va., and Christopher of the Azores; a daughter, Patricia Nichols of Berkeley, Calif.; a sister, Katharine Winslow of Boston, and five grandchildren.

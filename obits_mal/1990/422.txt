        Grosvenor Dana, a retired shipping executive, died of injuries sustained last Thursday when he was struck by a hit-and-run driver near his home in Mount Vernon, N.Y. He died on Sunday at Mount Vernon Hospital at the age of 88.
        Mr. Dana was with the Furness Bermuda Shipping Line from 1923 until he retired as head of the management department in 1967. In World War II he took a leave of absence to serve as personnel officer for North America in the British Ministry of War Transport.
        Mr. Dana was born in Woodstock, Vt. He was an alumnus of Dartmouth College. In 1950 he was made an officer of the Order of the British Empire for his wartime service.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Frances Spear; a son, Loomis, of Pownal, Vt.; three grandchildren, and a great-granddaughter.

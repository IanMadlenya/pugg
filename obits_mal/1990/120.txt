        Douglas Reid Brash, a retired investment banker, died of cardiac arrest on Wednesday at his home in Essex, Conn. He was 66 years old and lived in Essex and Point O'Woods, L.I.
        Mr. Brash was a general partner and a vice president of the Manhattan investment firm of F. Eberstadt & Company, which he joined in 1964. He retired in 1975. Before that, he was with Smith Barney & Company, with the International Bank for Reconstruction and Development, and the Paribas Corporation.
        He was a 1950 graduate of the Harvard School of Business Administration.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Nancy Ludington; a daughter, Diana, of Philadelphia; a son, Alexander, of Manhattan, and a sister, Mary Reid of Manhattan.

        Siro Vazquez, a retired senior vice president and director of the Exxon Corporation, died of congestive heart failure on Monday at his home in Manhattan. He was 80 years old.
        Dr. Vazquez was named a director of Exxon in 1965 and a senior vice president in 1970. He was the first national of a Latin American country to be elected to the board of what was then the Standard Oil Company (New Jersey).
        Dr. Vazquez was born in Caracas, Venezuela, and joined the Creole Petroleum Corporation, an affiliate of Exxon, in 1937. He worked in petroleum engineering and production management jobs before being elected to the Creole board in 1953. He went to Exxon's headquarters in Manhattan in 1958 as production coordinator. As a director, his main responsibilities included worldwide exploration and production.
        Dr. Vazquez graduated from the Central University of Venezuela in 1930 with a degree in civil engineering and studied petroleum engineering at the University of Tulsa in 1933.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Claire Duff; two daughters, Claire Otaola of Caracas and Wesley Hogan of Locust Valley, L.I., and 10 great-grandchildren.


        Dr. David L. Carter, a scientist for A.T. & T. Bell Laboratories in Murray Hill, N.J., died on Dec. 5, at Overlook Hospital in Summit, N.J. He was 57 years old and lived in Plainfield, N.J.
        He died of cancer of the esophagus, a spokeswoman for the family said.
        At his death Dr. Carter supervised Bell Laboratories' device technology group in the Electronic Technology Planning Center. Before joining Bell Laboratories in 1980, he was a staff scientist at Texas Instruments, manager of the photo research group at the Xerox Corporation, and director of corporate technology groups for the Singer Corporation.
        He was born in Cleveland and graduated from Ohio State University in 1956. He received a Ph.D. in physics from Columbia University in 1962. He studied for two more years at the University of Pennsylvania and was a visiting professor at the Ecole Normale Superieure in Paris.
        Dr. Carter is survived by his wife, the former Karen Craig; a daughter, Pamela, of Manhattan; a son, Jeremy, of Brookline, Mass., and a brother, Martin, of Macon, Ga.

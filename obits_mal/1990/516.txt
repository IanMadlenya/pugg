        William J. Simmer, public-affairs director of the Regional Plan Association, died on Wednesday at Beth Israel Hospital. He was 39 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Simmer died of complications of pneumonia, said Mary Rivers, an associate at the association.
        Mr. Simmer was also a director and board member of the Drawing Legion, a theater company, and wrote nine plays performed Off Off Broadway.
        He graduated from Princeton University in 1973 and received a master's degree in theater from New York University in 1977.
        Surviving are his father, Joseph, of Naranja, Fla.; two sisters, Jackie Brake of Keller, Tex., and Virginia Finger of Redford, Mich., and three brothers, Thomas, James and David, all of Dearborn, Mich.

        Dr. Donald Leroy Sloan Jr., a biochemist and chairman of the chemistry department at City College of the City University of New York, died yesterday at St. Luke's-Roosevelt Hospital Center. He was 45 years old.
        His sister, Sheree Sloan, said that the exact cause of death had not been determined but that he had been suffering from pneumonia.
        Dr. Sloan graduated from the University of Utah in 1972 with a Ph.D. in biochemistry. He joined the chemistry faculty of City College a short while later and was named chairman in 1987.
        Besides his sister Sheree, of Denver, he is survived by his father, Donald Sr., of Keenesburg, Colo., and another sister, Nancy Musselman of Oklahoma City.

        Harvey J. Nuland, a certified public accountant and a nationally recognized specialist in the accounting management of union health plans, died on Thursday at his home in New City, N.Y. He was 63 years old.  He died of intestinal cancer, said his brother, Dr. Sherwin B. Nuland of Hamden, Conn.
        A graduate of New York University, Mr. Nuland was a managing partner in the Manhattan accounting firm of Buchbinder, Tunick and Company, which he joined in 1951. He helped initiate health plans for the United Federation of Teachers and for District Council 37 of the American Federation of State, County and Municipal Employees, and was managing accountant of those plans.
        Besides his brother, he is survived by his wife, the former Laretta Kornhaber; a son, Seth, and a daughter, Sarah, all of New City.

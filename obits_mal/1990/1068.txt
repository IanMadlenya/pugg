        Gerson T. Hirsch, a retired New York architect, died of pneumonia on Thursday at a nursing home in Walnut Creek, Calif. He was 84 years old.
        He studied architecture at Harvard and New York University. Except for wartime service with the Army Corps of Engineers, he had his own office in Manhattan from 1934 to 1952, when he moved it to Pleasantville, N.Y.
        A past president of the Westchester chapter of the American Institute of Architects, Mr. Hirsch designed private homes, along with some public buildings in Westchester and New York City. He moved from Westchester to Walnut Creek about 20 years ago.
        Mr. Hirsch is survived by his wife, the former Carolyn Swartz; a daughter, Anne Sehulster of Piedmont, Calif.; a son, David, of Brooklyn; a brother, Leonard, of Manhattan; five grandchildren, and two great-grandsons.

        Peter I. Feinberg, a real-estate developer, died of cancer of the pancreas on Tuesday at his home in Manhattan. He was 79 years old.
        Mr. Feinberg was president of Feinberg Realty and Construction in Manhattan, a company of real-estate investors and developers he founded in 1933. At one time, he owned the Drake Hotel and office buildings at 250 Park Avenue and at 260 and 261 Madison Avenue.
        Mr. Feinberg was vice chairman of the board of the Jewish Theological Seminary in Manhattan and the first chairman of its development committee. He was on the board of the Hebrew Home for the Aged in Riverdale and of the Jewish Museum in Manhattan.
        He was born in Warsaw and came to the United States in 1913 at the age of 3. He was a graduate of New York University and its law school.
        Mr. Feinberg is survived by his wife, the former Ceil Spitz; two sons, David, of Manhattan and Michael, of Hollywood, Fla., and five grandchildren.

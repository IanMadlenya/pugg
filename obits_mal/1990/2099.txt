        Dr. Harold M. Fisher, a former president of the New York State Optometric Association and of the American Academy of Optometry, died on Friday at the Southwestern Vermont Medical Center in Bennington. He was 85 years old and lived in Grafton, Vt.
        He died of complications from pneumonia, his family said.
        Dr. Fisher was a specialist in aniseikonia, a condition of the eyes in which an image is seen in different sizes by the left and the right eye. He practiced in Manhattan from 1950 until he retired in 1978 and also practiced in Mt. Kisco, N.Y., for several years.
        Dr. Fisher was born in Katonah, N.Y., and was a graduate of Middlebury College. He received a bachelor's degree in optometry from Columbia University and a doctorate from the Pennsylvania State College of Optometry.
        He was elected president of the state optometric association in 1952 and served as chairman of the New York State Board of Examiners in Optometry from 1955 to 1957. He was president of the National Board of Optometry from 1958 to 1960 and was for many years a director of the Better Vision Institute.
        Dr. Fisher is survived by his wife, the former Mildred Lewless; two daughters, Mabel Dean of San Carlos, Calif., and Barbara Parke-Wolff of San Francisco; a sister, Helen Sarles of Bedford Hills, N.Y., and two grandchildren.

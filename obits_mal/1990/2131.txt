        Dr. Leon Olinger, a psychiatrist who was a former assistant director of Brooklyn State Hospital, died yesterday at his home in Wilmette, Ill,, where he had lived in retirement for the last four years. He was 82 years old.
        Dr. Olinger was born in Podwoloczyska, then a village in Poland and now in the Soviet Union, and attended the University of Lvov before receiving his medical degree from the University of Padua in Italy.
        He came to the United States during World War II and became a resident at Brooklyn State before joining the hospital's staff. He practiced in Brooklyn and was an assistant professor of psychiatry at Downstate Medical Center.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Stella Rosenberg; a son, Dr. Edward Olinger of Glencoe, Ill., and two grandchildren.

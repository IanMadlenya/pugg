        Charles F. White, retired president of M & M Mars in Hackettstown, N.J., died on Saturday at Hershey Medical Center in Hershey, Pa. He was 86 years old and lived in Lewisburg, Pa.
        Mr. White died of complications resulting from open-heart surgery, his wife said.
        Mr. White went to work for M & M Ltd. of Newark in 1941, where he was sales manager and vice president in charge of sales and advertising. He became president in 1957. He continued as president after the merger of Mars and M & M in the 1950's. The company relocated to Hackettstown in 1958, and he retired in December 1965.
        He was a member of the New York Cocoa Exchange, the New York Coffee and Sugar Exchange and the National Confectioners Association, for which he was policy committee chairman.
        Mr. White was born in Blakely, Pa., and graduated from Bucknell University.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Eleanor Tobin Hull; a daughter, Miranda McGinnis of Lewisburg; a sister, Jeannette Hemsley of Hummelstown, Pa., and four grandchildren.

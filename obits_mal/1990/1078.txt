        Henry A. Keller, who helped pioneer the use of prefabricated aluminum products in the home-building industry, died at his home on Sunday after a long illness. He was 70 years old.
        In 1950, in the midst of South Florida's postwar housing boom, he founded Air Control Products, which was renamed Keller Industries in 1968.
        When he retired in 1983, 4,000 workers at factories and warehouses across the country were producing a range of home-building products, from window frames to stepladders.
        He is survived by his wife, Joan; two sons, three daughters and five grandchildren,

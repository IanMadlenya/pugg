        William Kelly Beard, a retired business publications executive, died on Thursday at White Plains Hospital. He was 91 years old and lived in Scarsdale, N.Y.
        Mr. Beard, who retired in 1964, was a former corporate vice president of promotion and research for McGraw-Hill. He had been associated with the publishing company for 26 years. Before that, he was publisher of Electrical World and of Electrical Contracting magazines.
        Mr. Beard was a 1918 graduate of the Wharton School at the University of Pennsylvania, and was a member and former president of the Association of Business Publishers. He also served as a director of the Advertising Federation of America and the National Industrial Advertising Association. In 1985, he was included in the Publishing Hall of Fame.
        He is survived by his wife, Emily; two children from his marriage to Florence Wynkoop, Helen Jordhal of Lutherville, Md., and William Jr., of Hagerstown, Md.; a stepson, Thomas Livingston of Carmel, Calif., and seven grandchildren.

        Irving N. Hinnau, a retired executive of the International Business Machines Corporation, died on Tuesday at his home in Stamford, Conn. He was 66 years old. His family said he died of melanoma. Mr. Hinnau graduated from the University of Cincinnati and held a master's degree in business administration from Columbia. He served in World War II and the Korean War as an Air Force first lieutenant. He joined I.B.M. in 1949 and was director of Asia-Pacific area marketing before retiring as a vice president of I.B.M. World Trade in 1976. He then bought the Greenwich Hardware Company and ran its Greenwich store until 1985.
        He is survived by his wife, Shirley; two sons, Thomas, of Simsbury, Conn., and Mark, of Huntington, Conn.; a daughter, Sarah Ransom of Villa Hills, Ky., and two grandchildren.

        Irving Rosenthal, a retired executive vice president of the Guardian Life Insurance Company of America, died on Monday at Mount Sinai Hospital. He was 82 years old and lived in the Bronx.
        Mr. Rosenthal died of cancer, a spokesman for the family said.
        Mr. Rosenthal joined Guardian Life in 1930 as an actuarial clerk. He was named executive vice president in 1970 and retired in 1974. He was a director of the company-sponsored mutual funds until 1986.
        He was born in the Bronx and graduated magna cum laude from the City College of New York in 1930.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Anne Bernstein; two daughters, Dr. Barbara Almond of Palo Alto, Calif., and Alice Rosenthal of San Francisco; a brother, Saul, of New Rochelle, N.Y.; a sister, Madeleine Greenberg of Manhattan, and three grandchildren.

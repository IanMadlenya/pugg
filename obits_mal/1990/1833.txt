        William R. Moseley, retired chief executive of Day & Zimmermann Inc., a major Government munitions supplier, died last Sunday at a nursing home in Springfield Township, Pa. He was 75 years old and lived in Philadelphia.
        His brother, Thomas, said the cause of death was complications from a stroke.
        Mr. Moseley spent almost 40 years with Day & Zimmermann, a Philadelphia-based company that is the country's eighth-largest supplier of ordnance for the Army. He joined the company in 1941 as a special assistant to engineering and construction managers and retired in 1980 as chairman.
        After World War II, during which he served in the Navy, Mr. Moseley was named manager of the company's New York office. From there he moved on to become assistant general manager of its Lone Star ordnance division in Texarkana, Tex., and later president of the operations division, which among other jobs managed the Army Ammunition Plant in Parsons, Kan., and the Defense Logistics Agency's equipment center in Atchison, Kan.
        He became the company's chairman in 1976, the year the company was awarded a contract to run the Hawthorne Army Ammunition Plant near Reno.
        Mr. Moseley was a 1936 graduate of Williams College and a member of the American Defense Preparedness Associaition and the Society of American Military Engineers.
        In addition to his brother, who lives in Woods Hole, Mass., he is survived by his wife,, Anne; a daughter, Mary Schwartz, of Philadelphia; a son, William Jr., of Boiling Springs, Pa.; a sister, Ardelle Fullerton, of Dover, Mass., and five grandchildren.

        Alfred M. Vinton, a retired senior vice president of Citibank, died on Thursday at United Hospital in Port Chester, N.Y. He was 84 years old and lived in Rye, N.Y.
        He died of kidney and liver failure, his son John said.
        Mr. Vinton spent his entire career with the overseas division of First National City Bank of New York, as it was known at the time. He was made a vice president in 1956, supervising the bank's business in Argentina, and became a senior vice president in 1961, in charge of eastern Caribbean branches. He retired in 1967.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Jean R. Guiterman; two daughters, Helen D. Lally of Manhattan and Margaret J. Vinton of Caldwell, N.J.; two sons, John, of Wellesley, Mass; and Alfred Jr., of London, and 10 grandchildren.

        Alfred H. Greenberg, the editor in chief of Skiing magazine for more than a decade, died of leukemia on Saturday at Mount Sinai Medical Center. He was 65 years old and lived in Larchmont, N.Y.
        Mr. Greenberg, who became an accomplished skier in his late 30's, pursued the sport around the world, including advanced slopes in the Soviet Union, Japan and Argentina. He edited the magazine for 20 years until 1987, when he became a columnist and editor at large.
        He also was an editor for McCall's magazine and Women's Wear Daily and wrote political articles for The Nation and The New Republic.
        Mr. Greenberg, a native of Manhattan, graduated from DeWitt Clinton High School and Columbia College, and pursued graduate studies at the Sorbonne and Yeshiva University. In World War II, he served in the Army Corps of Engineers in Europe, assigned to a demolition unit.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Adele Rodbard; three daughters, Danielle Klebanoff of Cross River, N.Y., Michele, of Brooklyn, and Suzanne, of Olympia, Wash.; a brother, Robert, of Columbus, Ohio; a sister, Miriam Muskin, of Cleveland, and a grandson, Benjamin.

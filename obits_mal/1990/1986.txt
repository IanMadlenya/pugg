        Dr. Thomas A. Flinn, a professor of political science at Cleveland State University for 20 years, died Wednesday at Lakeside Hospital of University Hospitals in Cleveland. He was 65 years old and lived in Cleveland.
        Professor Flinn died of heart failure after a stroke, a spokesman for the university said.
        Before coming to Cleveland State, Dr. Flinn, a Ph.D. graduate of the University of Minnesota, taught at Ohio State University for three years and at Oberlin College for 12 years. He had also been an instructor at the University of Minnesota and held a management position at the United States Department of the Interior. He served as chairman of the Oberlin City Council in 1966 and was a member of several professional societies.
        He is survived by his wife, Barbara; two daughters, Jennifer Marks of Kent, Ohio, and Carey Howells of Lyndhurst, Ohio; a brother, James of Vergas, Minn, and two grandchildren.

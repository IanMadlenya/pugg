        Walter W. Niebling, a New York financier, died on Sunday at his home in South Hero, Vt. He was 72 years old.
        Mr. Niebling died of cancer, a spokesman for the family said.
        He had a 40-year career in municipal finance on Wall Street, mostly at Merrill Lynch, where he was instrumental in developing the municipal bond department. At his retirement in 1979, he was a vice president and director of Merrill Lynch capital markets.
        He was past president of the Municipal Bond Club of New York and the Municipal Forum of New York.
        Mr. Niebling was a native of Newark and a graduate of the University of Pennsylvania's Wharton School of Finance and Commerce. He began his career with Wood, Struthers & Winthrop and after World War II moved to Moody's Investors Service and Shields & Company.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Shirley Bradford; a son, F. Bradford, of Piedmont, Calif.; four daughters, Sarah Krebs of South Hero; Ann Bartie of Skillman, N.J.; Mary, of Plainfield, Vt., and Kathleen Waskin of Cantonment, Fla.; two brothers, Robert, of Mystic, Conn., and Richard, of Exeter, N.H., and nine grandchildren.


        Rudolph E. Ludt, a specialist in international trade and finance, died Wednesday at Penrose Hospital in Colorado Springs. He was 76 years old and lived in Colorado Springs.
        He died of cardiac arrest, his wife, Patricia, said.
        Mr. Ludt was currently chairman of the Symetrix Corporation, and had been chief executive officer of the Micromirror Corporation. From 1969 to 1987, he was head of the Intercontinental Energy Corporation, an oil and mineral exploration company he founded. A graduate of Columbia University, he had also been an executive with the Celanese Corporation, the Hoover Company and Caltex Oil.
        In addition to his wife, Mr. Ludt is survived by his mother, Marguerite of Colorado Springs; three sons, Steven of Tupelo, Miss., David of Shrewsbury, Mass., and Richard of Sacramento, Calif.; a daughter, Michelle Ludt of Denver; a brother, William of Princeton, N.J., and seven grandchildren.

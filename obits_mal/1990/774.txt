        John H. Reurs, chairman of the International Committee of Passenger Liners, a trade organization for cruise ships, died of prostate cancer on April 12 at the Columbia Presbyterian Medical Center. He was 66 years old and lived in Rowayton, Conn.
        Mr. Reurs, a graduate of Harvard College, joined the organization as chairman in 1975 and his contract had recently been renewed for three years. He kept the industry apprised of regulatory developments and changes affecting it throughout the world. From 1972 to 1975, he was resident director of the Norwegian American Lines.
        Earlier he had been general manager for the United States, Canada and Mexico for the Holland-America Line, which he joined in 1952. After various assignments in New York, Boston and Cleveland, he was named passenger traffic manager in 1962. He was appointed assistant general manager in 1964, deputy general manager in 1967 and general manager in 1968.
        His marriage to the former Mary Ann Hall ended in divorce. He is survived by two daughters from that marriage, Catherine Anne, of Ottawa and Jill W., of Boston, and two sisters, Erna Gray of Twyford, England, and Catherine McDermott, who lives in Ontario.
        His companion was Jacqueline Loeb of Rowayton.

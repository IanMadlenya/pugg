        Walter J. Petry Sr., a member of the board of the Harlem Y.M.C.A. for 50 years, died on Wednesday at his home in Manhattan. He was 86 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        He died of intestinal cancer, said his son, Walter Jr., of Fairfield, Conn.
        Mr. Petry, who worked as a supervisor for the United States Postal Service, was a founder in the 1920's of the Catholic Laymen's Union of New York, a forerunner of the Catholic Interracial Council. Last year he was awarded the Pierre Toussaint medallion at a Mass celebrated at St. Patrick's Cathedral by John Cardinal O'Connor. The medallion is bestowed annually on member of the black community who demonstrates an active commitment to freedom, human rights and spiritual values.
        Besides his son, he is survived by a brother, George, of Old Saybrook, Conn.

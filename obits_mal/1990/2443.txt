
        Philip A. Fulvi, a professor of foreign languages at Pace University, died on Sunday at Cabrini Medical Center in Manhattan. He was 58 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Dr. Fulvi died of cancer, a spokesman for the family said.
        He was chairman of the department of foreign languages at Pace's Dyson College of Arts and Sciences from 1979 to 1988. He had joined the university as an adjunct professor of French and Itlian in 1964. Dr. Fulvi began the Italian program at Pace and was an authority on the author and playwright Luigi Pirandello.
        He was born in Dedham, Mass., and graduated from Boston University. He received a master's degree from Boston University and a Ph.D. from New York University.
        Dr. Fulvi is survived by his wife, the former Caroline Marshall, and a brother, Anthony, of Boston.

        James Alan Roberts, a Canadian diplomat who was Canada's first Deputy Secretary General to NATO, died on Monday at a nursing home in Lymington, England. He was 84 years old.
        He had suffered from Alzheimer's disease.
        Mr. Roberts was Canada's Deputy Minister of Trade and Commerce under both the Conservatives and the Liberals in the 1950's and 60's, and helped lay the foundation for Canada's large wheat sales to Communist countries.
        After a four-year stint with the North Atlantic Treaty Organization, he was Canada's Ambassador to Switzerland and Algeria.

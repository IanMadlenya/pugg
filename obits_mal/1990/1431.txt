        Robert Ginell, professor emeritus of chemistry at Brooklyn College, died on July 10 at St. Joseph's Hospital in Baltimore. He was 78 years old and lived in Baldwin, L.I.
        Dr. Ginell, who had been visiting relatives in Baltimore, died of a heart attack, a spokesman for the college said.
        Dr. Ginell, whose specialty was theoretical physical chemistry, joined Brooklyn College in 1946 and taught there until his retirement in 1978. In World War II, he was associated with the University of North Carolina, where he worked in the field of high explosives.
        He was a graduate of the Polytechnic Institute of New York, where he received a Ph.D. in 1943.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Margot Mayer; a son, Stephan, of Philadelphia; a daughter, Heidi Mauricio of Eugene, Ore., and three grandchildren.

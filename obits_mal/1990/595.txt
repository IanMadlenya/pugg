        Hobart C. Carr, professor of banking at Adelphi University and professor emeritus of banking at New York University, died on Sunday at University Hospital in Mineola, L.I., several days after suffering a heart attack. He was 78 years old and lived in Garden City, L.I.
        Professor Carr taught at Adelphi as a visiting professor until his death. From 1956 to 1977 he taught banking at N.Y.U. and from 1956 to 1970 he was chairman of the banking and finance department of N.Y.U.'s School of Commerce.
        From 1942 to 1956, he was a division chief for the Federal Reserve Bank of New York.
        In the 1960's Professor Carr was a consultant to the Federal Home Loan Bank Board in the Kennedy Administration and a director of the Federal Home Loan Bank of New York. In 1969 he taught at the Vargas Institute in Rio de Janiero and Sao Paulo, Brazil.
        He graduated from the University of Iowa and received his master's degree there. In 1941 he received a Ph.D. from the University of Illinois. He was a trustee of the Village of Garden City, L.I.
        Professor Carr is survived by his wife, the former Virginia Eichler; a son, Gerald, of Newark, Del.; a daughter, Valerie Larrea of Ridgewood, N.J., and two granddaughters.

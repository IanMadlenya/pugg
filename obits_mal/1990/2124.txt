        Maurice R. Chambers, a retired shoe manufacturing executive, died Sunday at Bethesda-Dilworth Memorial Hall in St. Louis. He was 74 years old and lived in St. Louis.
        A spokesman for the family said Mr. Chambers died of complications of a stroke.
        Mr. Chambers, the former chairman and chief executive officer of Interco Inc., a diversified apparel and footwear company, had been in the shoe industry since 1931 when he started as a factory hand.
        His first employer was the Tweedle Footwear Corporation of Jefferson City, Mo. He became a salesman and the operator of four shoe stores, merchandise manager for shoes at Montgomery Ward and joined the International Shoe Company, Interco's predecessor, in 1949 as a sales manager.
        He rose to vice president in 1956, became a director in 1957 and president and chief executive in 1962. Three years later, he was named chairman of the board, a position he held until 1976. When he retired in 1981 he was designated chairman emeritus.
        Mr. Chambers, was active on many corporate boards, including the Angelica Corporation, Anheuser-Busch Companies Inc., Dillard Department Stores, Emerson Electric Company, Mercantile Bancorporation and Southwestern Bell Corporation. He was also active in civic affairs, including the United Way of Greater St. Louis, the Arts and Education Council, Barnes Hospital and the St. Louis Symphony Society.
        He is survived by his wife, Mildred; a daughter, Cynthia Berg of St. Louis; two brothers, Thomas, of Albuquerque, N.M., and John, of Cleveland; a sister, Kathryn Evans of St. Louis and two grandchildren.

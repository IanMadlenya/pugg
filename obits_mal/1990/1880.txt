        Francis Leo Hogan Jr., a retired underwriter with the John Hancock Insurance Company, died on Sunday at Montefiore Hospital in the Bronx. He was 77 years old and lived in the Riverdale section.
        He died of congestive heart failure, his daughter Leslie said.
        Mr. Hogan was with the company for 35 years, retiring in 1975. A union officer, he helped negotiate the health care coverage plans for John Hancock insurance agents.
        He was born in Manhattan and graduated from Manhattan College in 1935. He attended Fordham Law School before joining John Hancock.
        Besides his daughter Leslie, of the Bronx, he is survived by his wife, the former Traute Weigert; two sons, Brian, of Fontainbleau, France, and Francis L. 3d, of Sydney, Australia; two other daughters, Marietta, of Iowa City, and Laurie Elizabeth, also of the Bronx; two sisters, Lillian Foss of the Bronx and Loretta McEntegart of New Rochelle, N.Y., and three grandchildren.

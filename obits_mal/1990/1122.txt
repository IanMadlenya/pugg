        Donald MacArthur, a former treasurer of Sears, Roebuck & Company, died Thursday of heart failure at the Lansing Holiday Inn, outside Chicago, while traveling to Wisconsin. He was 88 years old and a resident of Sarasota, Fla., and Antigo, Wis.
        Mr. MacArthur spent 20 years at the Sears headquarters in Chicago, also holding executive positions with the Allstate Insurance Company, a Sears subsidiary, as well as the Sears, Roebuck Acceptance Corporation and the Sears Bank and Trust Company.
        He was also elected a director of Helene Curtis Industries of Chicago. After retiring from Sears, he became a member of several brokerage firms in Chicago.
        He is survived by his wife, Margaret, of Sarasota; seven sons, Donald Jr. of Deerfield, Ill., David of Winnetka, Ill., Will and Robert, of Tucson, Ariz., Gordon of Sarasota, Bruce of Milwaukee and Andrew of San Diego; two daughters, Mary of Washington, and Carol of Sacramento, Calif., and 10 grandchildren.

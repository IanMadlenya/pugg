        Herbert Spencer Duckworth, a retired vice admiral who commanded the aircraft carrier Cowpens in World War II, died at St. Luke's Hospital on Tuesday. He was 90 years old.
        He was air officer on the aircraft carrier Lexington at the battle of Coral Sea in 1942. In 1947 and 1948 he commanded the Jacksonville Naval Air Station.
        After retiring to Jacksonville in 1952 he served as president of the Jacksonville Art Museum.
        His survivors include his wife, Myrtle, two sons and two daughters.

        John C. Clark, who was an official of the United States Export-Import Bank after a career on Wall Street and in banking, died of prostate cancer on Friday at his home on Hilton Head Island in South Carolina. He was 77 years old.
        Mr. Clark, a native New Yorker, spent 43 years in investments and international banking. He began his career as a bond trader at the Wall Street firm of Shields & Company. After serving in the Army in World War II and rising to the rank of lieutenant colonel, he joined the bond department of the Chase Manhattan Bank. Later he was senior vice president of the Wachovia Bank and Trust Company in Winston-Salem, N.C., and manager of its bond department.
        In 1969, he was appointed by President Richard M. Nixon as a director of the Export-Import Bank and held the post until 1976. He was in charge of financing for bank operations and was responsible for all loans in Asia and the Middle East.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Lillian Fischer; a son, Rogers, of Fort Myers, Fla., and three grandchildren.

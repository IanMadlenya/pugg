
       Dr. Robert D. Leeper, a specialist in the treatment of thyroid cancer, died yesterday at the Penobscot Bay Medical Center in Rockport, Me. He was 65 years old and lived in Scarsdale, N.Y., and Camden, Me.
        His family said he died after a stroke.
        Dr. Leeper was an attending physician in endocrinology and nuclear medicine at Memorial Sloan-Kettering Cancer Center in Manhattan. He was associated with the center for more than 30 years.
        A native of Lewiston, Idaho, he was a graduate of the University of Idaho and received his medical degree from the College of Physicians and Surgeons of Columbia University. In World War II he was an Army sergeant in the European theater.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Virginia Knapp; four sons, Dwight, of Fanwood, N.J., Paul, of Searsmont, Me., James, of Brooklyn, and Thomas, of Washington; a brother, Donald, of Walnut Creek, Calif., and three grandchildren.

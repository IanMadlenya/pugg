
       Edward A. Montgomery, a retired Marine Corps brigadier general, died Saturday at St. Joseph Hospital in Augusta, Ga. He was 82 years old and lived in Augusta.
        He died of a heart attack, a funeral home representative said.
        In World War II, he was operations officer for aircraft in the Pacific and later held command positions with the Fleet Marine Force. A graduate of the National War college, General Montgomery served as head of the plans development section in the office of the Chief of Naval Operations.
        In the Korean War, he was chief of staff of the First Marine Aircraft Wing in the Far East and later became commanding officer of the Cherry Point Marine Corps Station in North Carolina. He also served as Commander in Chief for Pacific cold war planning.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Margaret Mary O'Connor; two daughters, Margaret Gibson of Evanston, Ill., and Blair McKelvey of Williamsport, Pa.; a sister, Anna Merrill of Salem, Mass.; 15 grandchildren and 23 great-grandchildren.

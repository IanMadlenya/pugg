
        Earl Torgeson, who played for five major league baseball teams in 18 years and served four years as a Snohomish County commissioner, died of leukemia at his home on Friday. He was 66 years old.
        Mr. Torgeson, a first baseman, batted .265 with 149 home runs and 740 runs batted in during a career extending from 1947 to 1962, mostly with the Boston Braves. In 1950, Mr. Torgeson batted .290 with 23 homers and led the National League in runs scored with 120. He batted .389 for the Braves in the 1948 World World Series.
        Survivors include his wife, Molly; three daughters, Christine Shaw of Anna Maria, Fla., and Holly Taylor and Tina Torgeson, both of Everett; a son, Bradley Torgeson of Everett, and three grandchildren.

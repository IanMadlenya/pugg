        Maurice A. Pollak, president of the Maurice Pollak Investment Office in West Long Branch, N.J., died on Sunday at his home in West Long Branch, N.J. He was 94 years old.
        Mr. Pollak had been in declining health since he underwent surgery in May, his son, Henry, said.
        He had been president of Henry Pollak Inc., a family business in Manhattan that imported raw materials for hats and knitwear, from 1928 to 1958, when he opened the investment office.
        Mr. Pollak was a life trustee of Monmouth College and honorary trustee of Monmouth Medical Center, where he founded the Pollak Mental Health Center.
        He was president of the Monmouth Park Charity Fund and was the first to receive the Helen Herrman Humanitarian award of C.P.C. Mental Health Services, formerly the Children's Psychiatric Center in Eatontown, N.J., in 1985.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Lucille Ross; a daughter, Lois Broder of West Long Branch; a son, Henry Pollak 2d of White Plains; eight grandchildren, and 15 great-grandchildren.

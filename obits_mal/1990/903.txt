        Wendell G. Earle, professor emeritus of marketing at Cornell University, died of cancer Tuesday at Tompkins Community Hospital in Ithaca, N.Y. He was 66 years old and lived in Ithaca.
        Dr. Earle joined the Cornell faculty in 1950 and developed a program in food industry management for the Department of Agricultural Economics. He retired in 1978.  He was born in Hardwick, Vt., and earned bachelor's, master's and doctoral degrees from the University of Vermont.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Francelia Connor; three sons, Brian, of Freeville, N.Y.; Bruce, of Devon, Pa., and Terry, of Bernardsville, N.J.; two daughters, Wendy Brayshaw of Lake Clear, N.Y., and Shelley Mitchell of Sapulpa, Okla.; his mother, Blanche Earle of Hardwick; two brothers, Ronald, of Hardwick, and Walton, of St. Johnsbury, Vt., and seven grandchildren.

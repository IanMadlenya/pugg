
        Ernst Behrendt, an editor who specialized in writing about automobile safety, died on Tuesday at the Northern Westchester Hospital in Mount Kisco, N.Y. He was 80 years old and lived in Pound Ridge, N.Y.
        Mr. Behrendt was the United States editor of Automobil Revue, a Swiss motoring weekly, and a consultant to the German National Safety Council. A native of Berlin and a graduate of the University of Berlin, he came to the United States in 1938 and was with the United States Information Agency in World War II.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Ilse Vandsburger; a daughter, Barbara Cerva of Harvard, Mass.; a son, Dr. Andrew E. Behrendt of Philadelphia, and a grandson.

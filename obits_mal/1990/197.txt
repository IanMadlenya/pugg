        Bruce Jay Heim, an investment banker, died of complications of AIDS on Thursday at his home in Bernardsville, N.J. He was 37 years old.
        Mr. Heim retired as executive vice president of Peter B. Cannell & Company in Manhattan in 1988 because of poor health. He had earlier been a vice president of F. Eberstadt & Company in Manhattan and continued as an officer when the company merged with Robert Fleming & Company in 1976.
        He had begun his career with the Chase Manhattan Bank shortly after graduating from the Amos Tuck School of Business Administration at Dartmouth in 1974.
        Mr. Heim was on the boards of the Chemed Corporation, RotoRooter, Omnicare and the National Sanitary Supply Corporation. Several years ago he created a charitable foundation bearing his name.
        He is survived by his mother, Nettie Heim of North Miami; a grandmother, Rose Heim of Miami Beach; two sisters, Eva Freeman of Fort Myers, Fla., and Barbara Pailet of Metairie, La., and a brother, Stephen, of Coral Gables, Fla.
        His companion was Daniel Mullay of Bernardsville.

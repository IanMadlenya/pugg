        Charles G. Daley, a lawyer who served under Thomas E. Dewey in racketeering investigations in the 1930's, died on Sunday at Lawrence Hospital in Bronxville, N.Y. He was 80 years old and lived in Bronxville. He died of heart failure, his family said.
        Mr. Daley went to work for Mr. Dewey, then the Manhattan District Attorney, in 1937 and later was with the District Attorney's Appeals Bureau.
        From 1940 to 1960, Mr. Daley was a lawyer for International Business Machines and later was on the legal staffs of Tenneco Chemicals and Macmillan, the publishing company, where he was corporate secretary. In 1974 he established a law practice in Bronxville.
        For 50 years he wrote a legal column, ''What's Your Answer?'' for American Banker.
        He was born in Brooklyn, was a graduate of Columbia College and held bachelor's and master's degrees in law from New York University. In World War II he was an Army captain.
        Mr. Daley is survived by his wife, Marcia; a daughter, Kathy Bohnen of Bronxville; a son, Charles Jr., of Hingham, Mass., and four grandchildren.

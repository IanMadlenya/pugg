        Thomas Watson Armitage, a retired insurance executive, died of pneumonia on Friday at a nursing home in Fairfield, Conn. He was 83 years old and lived in Fairfield.
        Mr. Armitage began his career with Dominick & Dominick Inc. in Manhattan. In 1932 he joined the Fairfield Land and Title Company and in 1938 moved to Slasson & Company. He served with the Army Air Forces in World War II.
        He joined Griswold & Company in Manhattan in 1947 and in 1951 was recalled to active duty by the Air Force during the Korean War. He was separated in 1953, but remained active as a reservist and retired a lieutenant colonel in 1966.
        In 1954 Mr. Armitage rejoined Griswold & Company, which was bought in 1962 by Marsh & McLennan Inc. He retired in 1974 but continued to work with H. Jackson Sterns, the former Fairfield Land and Title Company.
        Mr. Armitage graduated from Princeton University in 1929.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Emily Goodwin; a son, Robert G., of Fairfield, and a daughter, Alice Neff of Ranch Palos Verdes, Calif.; another son and daughter from an earlier marriage, Arthur L., of Phoenicia, N.Y., and Frances Thorington of Annapolis, Md.; eight grandchildren, and three great-grandchildren.

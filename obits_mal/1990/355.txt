        Dr. Edgar H. Bates, a retired professor of surgery at the New York University Medical School, died of a stroke on Feb. 14 at North Shore University Hospital in Manhasset, L.I. He was 84 years old and lived in Douglaston, Queens.
        Dr. Bates was on the faculty of the medical school from 1934 to 1987. He also had a private practice, from which he retired in 1972.
        He was a Navy commander in the Pacific in World War II and was on one of the first evaluation teams at Hiroshima after the atomic bomb had been dropped.
        After the war, Dr. Bates became medical director for the National Biscuit Company. He was also on the state's Workers' Compensation Board of Arbitration.
        He was born in Rochester and was an alumnus of the University of Rochester and the N.Y.U. Medical School.
        Surviving are three sons, Roger, of San Francisco, Richard, of Jericho, L.I., and Jeffrey, of Huntington, L.I.; a daughter, Constance, of Anchorage, Alaska, and three grandchildren.


        Benson B. Sloan Jr., a retired stockbroker, died on Monday at Park View Hospital in Nashville. He was 75 years old and lived in Tequesta, Fla.
        He died of heart failure, his son, Benson B. 3d, of Nashville, said.
        Mr. Sloan was a graduate of the Groton School and a 1937 graduate of Princeton University. He was a navigator in the Army Air Forces in the South Pacific in World War II and later joined Harris, Upham & Company. He retired as a partner in the company, now known as Smith, Barney, Harris, Upham & Company, in 1965.
        In addition to his son, he is survived by his wife, Virginia; a daughter, Stephanie Whitley of Winter Park, Fla.; two brothers, William, of Providence, R.I., and Samuel, of Jupiter, Fla., and six grandchildren.

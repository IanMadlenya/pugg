        Jeff Alexander, a composer who worked in radio, television and motion pictures for more than 50 years, died of cancer on Dec. 23 at his home on Whidbey Island in Washington. He was 79 years old.
        Mr. Alexander, a founder of the Screen Composers of America, wrote music for more than 40 feature films, starting with ''Shall We Dance?'' Among his other scores were those for the films ''The Mating Game,'' ''The Tender Trap,'' ''All the Fine Young Cannibals'' and ''Jailhouse Rock.'' He also collaborated on several M-G-M musicals, including ''Gigi,'' ''Singin' in the Rain'' and ''Kismet.''
        In radio he arranged music for ''The Hit Parade,'' ''The Camel Hour'' and ''The Lucky Strike Show.'' He was music director for ''Amos 'n' Andy'' and ''Hollywood Star Playhouse.''
        Mr. Alexander's credits in television included composing for ''Julia,'' ''Columbo,'' ''Wagon Train'' and ''The Twilight Zone.''
        Surviving is a daughter, Jill, of San Francisco.

        Thomas Lee Higginson, a partner in the Manhattan law firm of Shearman & Sterling who specialized in corporate and securities law, died yesterday at his home in Cold Spring Harbor, L.I., after a long illness. He was 70 years old and also lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Higginson began his career with the firm after graduating from Harvard Law School in 1949. He became a partner in 1957.
        He was a director of the Fiduciary Trust Company International, a trustee of the Greenvale School in Greenvale, L.I., and of the Frick Collection and a director of the Winthrop-University Hospital in Mineola, L.I. In World War II, he served overseas with the Army's 65th Infantry Division and was promoted to the rank of major. He served on Gen. Joseph T. McNarney's staff in Germany and was awarded the Bronze Star.
        Mr. Higginson is survived by his wife, the former Shirley A. Foerderer; two sons and a daughter from an earlier marriage, Thomas L. Jr., of Rectortown, Va., Robert W., of Santa Fe, New Mexico, and Elizabeth Rideman of Cambridge, Mass., and five grandchildren.

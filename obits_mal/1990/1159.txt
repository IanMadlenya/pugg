        Endre Bohem, a film and television producer, died on Saturday at his home here. He was 89 years old.
        Mr. Bohem left Hungary for the United States shortly after World War I. He became a scenarist in Hollywood, then began producing films, among them ''Alias Nick Beal'' and ''The Night Has a Thousand Eyes.'' He was the screenwriter for ''The House of a Thousand Candles.''
        Mr. Bohem also produced ''Rawhide,'' the television western with Clint Eastwood. It ran from 1959 to 1966.
        Mr. Bohem returned to Hungary in 1968 and was a producer with the national film company for ''The Boys of Paul Street,'' which was nominated for an Academy Award as best foreign picture.
        He is survived by his wife, Hilda; a son; a daughter; five grandchildren, and two great-grandchildren.

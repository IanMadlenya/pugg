        Ralph McKenzie Freeman, a senior Federal district judge, died Thursday after undergoing surgery for a ruptured aorta at William Beaumont Hospital in Royal Oak, Mich. He was 87 years old.
        Judge Freeman, a 1927 graduate of the University of Michigan Law School, was the prosecutor for Genesee County, Michigan, from 1930 from 1932 and was a member of the Flint Board of Education from 1935 to 1949.
        He was appointed to the Federal bench for the Eastern District of Michigan by President Dwight D. Eisenhower in 1954 and was chief judge from 1967 until 1972. He became a senior judge in 1973 but continued to hear cases.
        He is survived by his wife, Emmalyn, and a brother.

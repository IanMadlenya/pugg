        Joel A. Reiss, a lawyer and founding partner of the law firm of Reiss & Rosen, died of a heart attack while jogging on Sunday in Riverside Park in Manhattan. He was pronounced dead at St. Luke's-Roosevelt Hospital Center. He was 50 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Before founding his own firm in Manhattan, Mr. Reiss was a partner in Fine Tofel Saxl Berelson & Barandes in Manhattan. He was admitted to the New York Bar in 1964 after graduating from New York University Law School. He was also an arbitrator in Small Claims Court and was a member of the American Judges Association.
        He is survived by his wife, Elaine; a son, Joshua, a daughter, Naomi, and his mother, Carrie, all of Manhattan, and a brother, Charles, of Brooklyn.

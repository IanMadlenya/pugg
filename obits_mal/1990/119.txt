        Charles Goldstein, former operations director of the New York branch of the Postal Service, died of bone cancer on Tuesday at his home in Bloomfield, N.J. He was 83 years old.
        Mr. Goldstein started in the post office as a clerk. In a career of nearly 50 years, he rose to the highest Civil Service rank in the busiest station in the country before retiring in 1973.
        He was secretary for 10 years of the New York branch of the National Association of Postal Supervisors and later a regional vice president of the union.
        Mr. Goldstein was born in Manhattan. He was a flight engineer in the Army Air Forces in World War II.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Juanita Taffet, and three sisters, Sally Schiffman of North Bergen, N.J., and Betty Greene and Lena Mercado, both of Fort Lauderdale, Fla.

        Ettore Maserati, a founder and guiding force of the Maserati racing-car company, died today, the ANSA news agency reported. He was 96 years old.
        With one of his five brothers, Alfieri, Mr. Maserti produced his first racing car in 1926. Maseratis went on to become a power in racing with victories in the Indianapolis 500 in 1939 and 1940, and in the 12 hours of Sebring in 1933.
        In 1937, the company ws bought by the Orsi group in Midena. Mr. Maserati worked for Orsi for 10 years as technical advisor.
        After leaving Orsi, he and his brothers founded the Osca Maserati car-manufacturing company.
        Mr. Maserati retired in 1966 as the age of 72, the last surviving brother.
        The Maserati company, based in Modena, is now controlled by the Argentine-born Alejandro de Tomaso.

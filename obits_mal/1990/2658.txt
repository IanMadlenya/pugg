
        David White, an actor in theater, films and television, died of a heart attack on Tuesday. He was 74 years old.
        Mr. White, a graduate of Los Angeles City College, started out at the Pasadena (Calif.) Playhouse and the Cleveland Playhouse. After serving in the Marine Corps in World War II, he made his Broadway debut in 1949 in "Leaf and Bough," a play by Joseph Hayes. He also appeared in "Anniversary Waltz" and "The Happy Time."
        He also appeared in films and television shows, and was best known to television viewers as Larry Tate, the white-haired advertising executive on "Bewitched," which ran on ABC from 1964 to 1972. He was also in the series "Cagney and Lacey," "Remington Steele," "The Love Boat" and "The Mary Tyler Moore Show."
        His film credits included "The Apartment" and "Sunrise at Campobello."

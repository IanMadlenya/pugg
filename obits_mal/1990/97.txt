        Brother Cornelius Luke, an educator, missionary and former overseer of educational training centers for the Brothers of the Christian Schools, died of complications of diabetes on Monday at De La Salle Hall in Lincroft, N.J. He was 95 years old and had retired to De La Salle in 1983.
        Born in New York City as John Joseph Pryor, he joined the Roman Catholic teaching order in 1910.
        Brother Luke studied and taught in Belgium before earning a master's degree from New York University in 1927 and a Ph.D. in English from the Catholic University of America in Washington. He taught at several high schools and at Manhattan College.
        In 1938 he was named visitor general of the order and for 24 years was in charge of educational training centers in the United States, England, Ireland and Australia. He was then named director of an educational center in Santa Fe, N.M., and later was a missionary in Malaya, Sri Lanka and Manila.
        He is survived by a sister, Mary Miorin of Harrisburg, Pa.


        Jonathan L. Alper, an artistic associate of the Manhattan Theater Club, died on Monday at Lenox Hill Hospital.  He was 40 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        He died of AIDS-related causes, the Manhattan Theater Club said.
        In the last decade, Mr. Alper was instrumental in developing and presenting new plays by British and American writers, including New York premieres of works by Brian Friel, Terrence McNally and Beth Henley.
        Mr. Alper was born in Chevy Chase, Md., and graduated from Amherst College. He also studied at the Weber/Douglas Academy of Dramatic Arts in London.
        While literary manager for the Shakespeare Theater at the Folger in Washington, he directed several productions. After moving to New York, he directed a production of "Safe House" at the Manhattan Theater Club, winning an Obie award in 1978. He became the literary manager of the Manhattan Theater Club in 1980 and its artistic associate in 1984.
        Mr. Alper's companion was Kip Said. He is survived by his father, Jerome, and his stepmother, Muriel, both of Chevy Chase, and two brothers, Andrew, of Manhattan, and Alan, of Silver Spring, Md.

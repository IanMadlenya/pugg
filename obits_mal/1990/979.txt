        James M. Wallace, retired executive vice president of N. W. Ayer Inc., the advertising agency, died in his sleep Tuesday at his home in Pennswood Village, Newton, Pa. He was 86 years old.
        Mr. Wallace joined N. W. Ayer's Philadelphia office in 1936 and devoted his career largely to the company, from which he retired in the early 1970's. He established Ayer's plans-merchandising department, which analyzes market data and formulates marketing plans, and was its director for many years.
        Mr. Wallace was born in Philadelphia and attended Temple University.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Christine Klesius; two daughters, Antonia Hamilton of Iowa City, Iowa, and Douglass Newbold of Malvern, Pa.; and four grandchildren.

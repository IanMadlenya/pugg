        Walter C. Reisinger, an executive of the Anheuser-Busch Companies, died of lung cancer on Friday at Jewish Hospital in St. Louis. He was 66 years old and lived in St. Louis, where the brewery is based.
        Mr. Reisinger was born in New York City, a great-grandson of the company's founder, Adolphus Busch. He joined the company in 1946 after wartime service in the Army Air Corps.
        He was a member of the Anheuser-Busch board for the last 26 years and also was on the board of a subsidiary, the St. Louis Cardinals baseball team. He spent most of his career in marketing and advertising and was special representative for customer relations for the last 14 years.
        Mr. Reisinger's survivors include his wife, Vivian Uyeda Reisinger; four sons, Walter C. Jr., Edward and Walter B., all of St. Louis, and Ronald of North Redington Beach, Fla.; two stepdaughters, Donna T. Simmons of St. Louis and Melodie T. Hunt of Palm Desert, Calif.; a stepson, Thomas A. Tucker of St. Louis, and 10 grandchildren.

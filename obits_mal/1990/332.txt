        H. Clinton Pollack Jr., a stockbroker and an advertising executive, died on Sunday at the Mount Sinai Medical Center in Manhattan. He was 48 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Pollack was the president of Sentinel Securities, a discount brokerage house he founded, and president of Pace Securities, a member of the New York and American Stock Exchanges. He was also managing director of the European American Technology Corporation and president of the Solutions Group, an advertising concern he founded in 1970. From 1976 to 1979, before he founded Pace Securities, he was chairman of the Wall Street Discount Corporation.
        A 1963 graduate of Brown University, Mr. Pollack had been in advertising with the J. Walter Thompson agency in New York and Societa Edison S.P.A. and McCann Erickson Italia in Milan, Italy.
        He is survived by his wife, Jessie McKay Pollack; two sons, H. Clinton 3d and Hugh; a daughter, Alexa; his parents, Henry and Sylvia Pollack of Newton, Mass., and a brother, Steven, of Poughkeepsie, N.Y.

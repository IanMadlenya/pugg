        Willliam McCarthy, a former First Deputy Commissioner of the New York Police Department, died on Thursday at the hospice of the Colonial Palms Nursing Home in Pompano Beach, Fla. He was 73 years old and lived in Pompano Beach.
        He died of leukemia, his family said.
        As a uniformed inspector in 1960, Mr. McCarthy organized the Mobile Reserve Force, predecessor of the Tactical Patrol Force. He later commanded the department's firearms instruction program, its Traffic Division and its Emergency Service Division.
        He retired in 1964 as a deputy chief inspector but was called out of retirement in 1971 to become First Deputy Commissioner and directed efforts to combat narcotics traffic and organized crime. He retired again in 1974.
        As an Army Air Force pilot in World War II, he flew 35 bombing missions over Germany, and in the Korean War he served in the Strategic Air Command, rising to lieutenant colonel.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Anne McIntyre; a son, William J., of Brandon, Miss.; a brother, John, of Tallahassee, Fla.; a sister, Margaret Dahl of Clifton Park, N.Y., and two grandsons.

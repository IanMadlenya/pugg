        Peter J. Wolfe, a senior vice president of the Chase Manhattan Bank, died of a heart attack on Thursday at United Hospital in Port Chester, N.Y. He was 53 years old and lived in Pompton Plains, N.J.
        Mr. Wolfe joined Chase Manhattan in 1988 as operations and systems executive of Chase InfoServ International, Chase's information and transaction division. Before that, he had worked for Citibank for 32 years.
        He was a graduate of the Massachusetts Institute of Technology Sloan School of Management and became chief of Citibank's retail operations in Britain in 1975. He became a division head in charge of financial institutions services in 1978.
        He left Citibank to become senior vice president of the Home Insurance Company, and returned to Citibank in 1986 as a senior executive in charge of global electronic markets.
        He is survived by his wife, Jane; a daughter, Megan, of York, Pa.; three sons, Peter, of Nashua, N.H., Kevin, of Quantico, Va., and Edward, of Hoboken, N.J.; two brothers, John, of Staten Island, and Robert, of Bow, N.H.; two sisters, Kathy Krebbs of Kendall Park, N.J., and Roberta Cherego of East Brunswick, N.J.; his stepmother, Eleanor, two half brothers, Sean and Patrick, and a half sister, Patricia, all of Chatham, N.J.

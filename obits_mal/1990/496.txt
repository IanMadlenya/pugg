        Sidney Shenkman, chairman of Extebank, the United States subsidiary of Banco Exterior Group of Spain, died of a heart attack on Monday at his home in Manhattan. He was 69 years old.
        Mr. Shenkman began his banking career in 1965 with the Century National Bank & Trust Company, specializing in financial services to the garment industry. He became president of Century in 1978. Two years later, after Century was acquired by the Spanish group, he was named president of Extebank and became chairman in 1982. He was to retire at the end of this year.
        Mr. Shenkman was born in Manhattan and was a graduate of City College. He served in the Army in World War II. He is survived by his wife, the former Phyllis Weiss; his mother, Ethel, of Sunnyside, Queens; a daughter, Randi, of Amherst, Mass., and a son, Richard, of Seattle.

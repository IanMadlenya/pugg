        Wallace Herndon Smith, an artist who worked mostly in oils and watercolors, died on Saturday at his home in Ladue, Mo. He was 88 years old.
        Mr. Smith was born in St. Louis. He graduated from Princeton University and later studied at the Ecole des Beaux-Arts in Paris. He gave up architecture to become a full-time painter in the 1930's and spent 10 years in New York City before moving back to St. Louis in 1942.
        He is survived by his wife, Kelsey; a son, Jay, of London; a brother, Robert, and a sister, Katharine Halls, both of St. Louis.

        John B. Goodman, retired executive vice president for the Downtown Lower Manhattan Association, a civic group, died on Tuesday at his home in Bellingham, Wash. He was 78 years old and had lived in Darien, Conn., for many years before moving to Bellingham.
        Mr. Goodman died of Parkinson's disease, a spokeswoman for the family said.
        Mr. Goodman retired from the association in 1975. He had been with the organization since the 1960's. Earlier he had worked in public relations and advertising for United Airlines, the American Oil Company and the Chamber of Commerce in New York City.
        A graduate of the University of Denver, he was a Navy commander in the Pacific in World War II.
        A widower, he is survived by a son, John B. Jr., of Bellingham, and a sister, Dorothea Fox of Snowmass, Colo.

        Harry J. Halperin, a prominent New York lawyer for 66 years, died on Friday night at Lenox Hill Hospital in Manhattan. He was 90 years old.
        He died of congestive heart failure, his son Theodore said.
        Mr. Halperin graduated from St. Lawrence University Law School in 1921. He was the senior partner in the law firm of Halperin, Shivitz, Scholer & Steingut until he retired in 1987.
        In the 1940's, as counsel to the Joint Rent Action Committee, a group representing 31 trade associations, Mr. Halperin was instrumental in the adoption of commercial rent control for New York City. He had also served as New York City treasurer for the National Democratic Committee.
        Besides his son Theodore, who lives in Manhattan, Mr. Halperin is survived by his wife, the former Clara Herman; another son, Eliot, of Washington; a sister, Ethel Shrage of Delray Beach, Fla.; four grandchildren and one great-grandchild.

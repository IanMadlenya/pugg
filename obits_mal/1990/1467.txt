        Robert O. Shepler, president of the General Reassurance Corporation for 20 years before retiring in 1987, died on Wednesday at Greenwich (Conn.) Hospital. He was 66 years old and lived in Greenwich.
        A spokesman for the company said he died of pancreatic cancer.
        Mr. Shepler, who was born in Springfield, Ohio, received business and law degrees from the University of Michigan. He served in the Army Air Forces in World War II.
        He practiced law privately and was on the legal staff of the Maccabees, a life-insurance company in Detroit, before entering the reinsurance field as a regional vice president of North American Reinsurance in Chicago.
        In 1967 he joined General Reassurance, the life-insurance subsidiary of General Reinsurance. In 1981 he became a vice president of the parent company. General Reassurance was sold to the Life Reassurance Corporation of America after his retirement.
        Mr. Shepler became president of the United Way of Greenwich in 1988.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Christine Blair; two sons, Robert, of Manhattan, and John, of Santa Fe, N.M.; a daughter, Julie, also of Santa Fe; his parents, Dallas and Martha, of Rochester, Minn., and a sister, Sally Struck of Birmingham, Mich.


        Osgood Endecott Perry, executive vice president of the Monarch Brass and Copper Corporation of New Rochelle, N.Y., died on Monday at his home in Wilton, Conn. He was 61 years old.
        He died of a heart attack, a family spokesman said.
        Mr. Perry was president of two Monarch subsidiaries, the New Haven Copper Company and Monarch of New England Associated.
        He was born in Washington, Conn., and was a 1953 graduate of Williams College. He received an M.A. from Syracuse University in 1963.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Patricia Ives; four sons, Scott, of Torrance, Calif., Michael, of Norwalk, Conn., Brian, of Menlo Park, Calif., and Christopher, of Wilton, and a sister, Marie Masland of Carlisle, Pa.

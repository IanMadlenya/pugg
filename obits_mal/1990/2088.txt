        N. Mitchel Jason, a physician and former chief of urology at Huntington Hospital on Long Island, died on Tuesday at Good Samaritan Hospital in West Palm Beach, Fla. He was 68 years old and lived in West Palm Beach. He died of cardiac arrest, his wife, Dorothy, said.  Dr. Jason joined the staff of Huntington Hospital in 1954 after completing his residency at Brooklyn Jewish Hospital. He retired as chief of urology in 1982 after suffering a stroke.
        He graduated from the City College of New York in 1946 and the University of Rochester Medical School in 1951. In World War II, he was a B-26 bomber pilot in Europe and Morocco.
        Besides his wife, he is survived by a sister, Evelyn Brown of Wilkes-Barre, Pa.; and a brother, Bernard, of New Rochelle, N.Y.

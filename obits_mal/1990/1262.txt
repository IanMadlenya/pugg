        Arthur J. Crockett, a writer and crime magazine editor, died on Friday at Jamaica Hospital. He was 69 years old and lived in Richmond Hill, Queens.
        He died of a heart attack, his stepson said.
        Mr. Crockett worked for Reese Communications, the publishing company, for many years, and was editor in chief of True Detective and other crime magazines for seven years.
        During World War II, he was a radioman with the 100th Infantry Division and was wounded during the Battle of the Bulge. He received the Bronze Star and the Purple Heart. He also wrote for Stars and Stripes.
        Mr. Crockett is survived by a son, Eugene Crockett, and a stepson, James Donnelly, both of Richmond Hill.

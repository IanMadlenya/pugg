        Wesley Lefferts Bailey, a retired magazine executive, died June 13 in Tampa, Fla. He was 80 years old and had moved to Tampa last year from Sea Cliff, L.I.
        He had been suffering from Parkinson's disease, his daughter Beverly said.
        Mr. Bailey studied journalism at New York University and joined Time Magazine in 1937. He was a special assistant to Henry R. Luce, the publisher, until 1947. He then joined Good Housekeeping magazine, where he was creative director. He left in 1960 to form his own public relations firm.
        He is survived by his wife, Eileen; three daughters, Carol, of Sea Cliff, Leslie McHale of Tampa and Beverly Shields of Seattle; a sister, Charlotte Downey of Glen Rock, N.J.; four grandchildren, and one great-grandchild.

        John Kung-Fu Liu, an inventor and business executive, died of heart failure Monday at the Medical College of Pennsylvania in Philadelphia. He was 59 years old and lived in Valley Forge, Pa.
        Mr. Liu, a graduate of the University of Pennsylvania and the Illinois Institute of Technology, was an expert on large power systems used to propel conveyors, ships, catapults and power plants.
        He invented a hydroviscous drive system and also received patents for the extraction of coal, the development of robotics for the Navy and a hydropower system to harness the energy of the Schuylkill River.
        Mr. Liu was a former vice president of the Philadelphia Gear Corporation and was president of Global Engineering, in Norristown, Pa., at his death.
        He was a member of the American Society of Inventors and was its president from 1976 to 1978.
        Mr. Liu is survived by his wife, the former Penny Wu; a son, Kristopher, of Valley Forge; his mother, Mary Liu, of Manhattan; two sisters, Elizabeth Bainbridge, of Chadds Ford, Pa., and Margaret Liu, of Arlington, Va.; and two brothers, David, of Manhattan and Robert, of Los Angeles.

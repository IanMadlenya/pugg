        Joseph V. Giffuni, a New York City builder and real-estate developer, died on Friday at his home on the Upper East Side. He was 77 years old.
        Mr. Giffuni was a second-generation developer who worked in Manhattan, Brooklyn and Queens for more than 50 years. He built extensively on the East Side before putting up the 26-story Harkness apartment building on Broadway a block from Lincoln Center in 1981.
        Mr. Giffuni, a former director of Casa Italiana at Columbia University, was active in Italian-American artistic and cultural affairs.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Flora Baldini; two daughters, JoAnn G. Sher and Cathe Giffuni; a son, Vincent J., and a brother, Andrew L., all of Manhattan, and two sisters, Caroline Marcellino and Viola Baker.

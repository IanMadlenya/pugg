        Haskell Barst, a lawyer who specialized in immigration, died of a heart attack yesterday at his home in Pompano Beach, Fla. He was 82 years old.
        Mr. Barst was a founder and former president of the Association of Immigration and Nationality Lawyers. Mr. Barst, a 1929 graduate of St. John's University Law School, helped found the firm of Barst & Mukamal in Manhattan in 1942. He retired in 1985.
        He is survived by his wife, Rose; two daughters, Shelly Davis and Nancy Straus, both of Manhattan; a sister, Dorothy Polstein of East Rockaway, L.I., and seven grandchildren.

        The Rev. Dr. Ralph M. Holdeman, a former official with the National Council of Churches who worked in the civil rights movement, died July 31 at Elkhart General Hospital in Elkhart, Ind.. He was 84 years old.  Dr. Holdeman, who lived in Elkhart, died of a stroke, said his wife, Mildred.
        A graduate of the Evangelical Theological Seminary in Naperville, Ill., Dr. Holdeman was a leader with the United Methodist Church and the United Brethren Church before joining the National Council of Churches as director of evangelism. He worked with the Rev. Dr. Martin Luther King Jr. in Montgomery, Ala..
        Besides his wife, he is survived by a daughter, Margaret Rose Holdeman of Elkhart.

        Nathan (Nate) Monaster, a writer for television and film, died of heart disease on May 12 at Cedars-Sinai Medical Center in Los Angeles. He was 74 years old and lived in Los Angeles.
        Among Mr. Monaster's more than 100 credits are the television series ''Duffy's Tavern,'' ''The Burns and Allen Show'' and ''The Milton Berle Show.'' His screen credits include ''That Touch of Mink,'' starring Cary Grant and Doris Day, for which he received an Academy Award nomination; ''Call Me Bwana,'' with Bob Hope, and ''A Very Special Favor'' with Rock Hudson and Leslie Caron.
        Mr. Monaster was president of the Writers Guild of America West from 1963 to 1965.
        He is survived by his wife, Gladys; two daughters, Susan McGarr and Pamela Karps; a son, Matthew, and a granddaughter, all of Los Angeles.

        Wilson Mothershead, retired chairman of the Indiana National Bank and its parent organization, the Indiana National Corporation, died Tuesday at Methodist Hospital here. He was 84 years old and lived in Indianapolis.
        Mr. Mothershead joined the bank in 1939 as a vice president and director. In 1964 he was named chairman. He was also the first chairman of the parent company, which was formed in 1969. He retired in 1970.
        During his term as chairman the bank built the 37-floor Indiana National Bank Tower and joined with five other American banks to form a bank in London.
        Survivors include his wife, the former Katharine Brown, a son and a daughter.

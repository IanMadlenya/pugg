        Joseph H. Braverman, a retired vice president of the Tishman Realty and Construction Company, died of lymphoma on Saturday at University Hospital. He was 72 years old and lived on Staten Island.
        Mr. Braverman worked for Tishman from 1955 to 1977. He supervised the design and installation of mechanical and electrical equipment at all projects, including the World Trade Center, the John Hancock Building in Chicago and Penn Plaza in Philadelphia.
        Mr. Braverman had been a design engineer for the Armo Air Conditioning Company; chief engineer for Kerby Saunders Inc., a mechanical contractor, and senior design engineer of atomic energy facilities for the Vitro Corporation.
        He born on Staten Island. Mr. Braverman had a bachelor's in mechanical engineering from City College and an M.S. from the Stevens Institute.
        Survivors include his wife, the former Charlotte Aidus; two daughters, Carol Brill of Great Neck, L.I., and Betty, of Manhattan; a son, Mark, of Manhattan, and two grandchildren.

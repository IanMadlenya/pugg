        Edward C. Embury, an architect who designed the Children's Zoo in Central Park among other recreational, educational and residential projects, died on Wednesday of emphysema at the Winchester (Va.) Medical Center. He was 84 years old and lived in Berryville, Va.
        Mr. Embury, who had his own architectural firm in Manhattan before he retired in 1970, was a longtime resident of Greenwich, Conn.
        He was born in Englewood, N.J., and was a graduate of Lawrenceville and Princeton. He joined his father, Aymar Embury 2d, in architectural practice in 1928. From 1938 to 1940 he supervised construction of the American Embassy in the Dominican Republic.
        In World War II Mr. Embury served with the Air Transport Command in North Africa and Europe, rising to the rank of lieutenant colonel.
        He is survived by two sons, Phillip, of Millwood, Va., and Edward Jr. of Denver, and two grandsons.

        Robert Lawrence Finley, a lawyer in Manhattan and a retired Government official, died of cancer on Sunday at Huggins Hospital in Wolfeboro, N.H. He was 90 years old and lived in Tamworth, N.H.
        Mr. Finley graduated from Harvard Law School in 1925 and entered private practice in Manhattan. He was a founding partner of Parker, Finley & Benjamin in 1932, and he left the firm in 1941 to serve in the Army as a legal officer in France and Germany.
        From 1946 to 1949, he was a partner with Spence, Hotchiss, Parker & Duryee and then began a career in Government. He was a special assistant to the Under Secretary of the Army from 1949 to 1952, served as special assistant to the administrator of the Defense Production Agency and later served as assistant deputy director of the Office of Defense Mobilization. He also served as acting treasurer of New Jersey. In 1963 he became deputy assistant director of the Federal Arms Control and Disarmament Agency and retired in 1966.
        He was the son of Dr. John H. Finley, who was president of City College of New York from 1903 to 1913 and headed the editorial page of The New York Times in 1937 and 1938.
        Mr. Finley is survived by a son, Alexander, of Chestnut Hill, Mass.

        Arthur Mead Irwin Jr., the owner of Mead Irwin Associates, publishers' representatives, died of lung cancer on Tuesday at his home in New Rochelle, N.Y. He was 72 years old.
        Mr. Irwin founded the firm in 1957 and operated it until his death, at first from Manhattan and since 1974 from Larchmont, N.Y. In 1963 and 1964 he was president of the National Association of Publishers' Representatives. Mr. Irwin is survived by his wife, the former Marie Louise O'Donnell; two sons. Arthur 3d, of Boston and Michael, of Long Beach, Calif.; a sister, Marion Bru of Los Altos, Calif., and two brothers, Charles, of Ojai, Calif., and Samuel, of Largo, Fla.

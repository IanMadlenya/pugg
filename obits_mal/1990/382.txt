        John R. Slonaker, an investment banker, died of cancer on Friday at his home in Summit, N.J. He was 42 years old.
        Mr. Slonaker had been with Keefe, Bruyette & Woods, a Manhattan investment firm, since 1981. Previously he had been a vice president of Fidelity Bank Corporation in Newark, a vice president of Chemical Bank in Manhattan and a member of the aeronautical division of Johnson & Higgins insurance company in Manhattan.
        He was born in Philadelphia, and graduated from Princeton University with a degree in aeronautical engineering in 1969. He then went to work for Grumman Aircraft Corporation in Bethpage, L.I.
        Mr. Slonaker was a former board member of the Boys Club of Newark and past president of the United Way of Chatham, N.J.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Margery Krieger; three daughters, Sarah, Kathryn and Leigh, all of Summit, and a sister, Linnea Flint, of Westford, Mass.

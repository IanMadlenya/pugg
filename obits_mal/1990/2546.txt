
        Bernard Seiderman, the Superintendent of Schools in Lynbrook L.I., died on Thursday at South Nassau Community Hospital in Oceanside, L.I. He was 63 years old and lived in Roosevelt, L.I.
        He died of lung cancer, a family spokeswoman said.
        Dr. Seiderman, a graduate of Harvard, had been head of the Lynbrook schools since 1981. Before that he had been an assistant superintendent of the Great Neck, L.I., public schools from 1971 to 1980 and a teacher in junior and senior high schools in Pennsylvania, New Jersey and New York.
        He is survived by his wife, Goldie, two daughters, Cara, of Cambridge, Mass., and Pamela, of Hewlett, L.I.; a son, Ian, of Roosevelt, and two sisters, Celia Greitzer of Miami and Marcia Marko of Philadelphia.

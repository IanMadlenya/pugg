        Robert B. Davies, a certified public accountant in Manhattan for 35 years, died on Wednesday. He was 72 years old and lived in White Plains.
        Mr. Davies suffered a heart attack on a commuter train and died at New Rochelle Hospital, his son, Robert, said.
        Mr. Davies became a managing partner at Davies & Davies, an accounting firm established by his grandfather. He was a senior partner at his death.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Majorie Real; his son, Robert, of Rowayton, Conn., two other sons, Sanders, of Darien, Conn., and Allan, of Boston; three stepchildren, Stephen Real of South Salem, N.Y., Pamela Real of Northampton, Mass., and William Real of Pittsburgh, and six grandchildren.
        Correction:  July 21, 1990, Saturday, Late Edition - Final
        An obituary on July 13 about a certifed public accountant in Manhattan rendered his full name incorrectly.
        He was Robert S. Davies.


       Dr. John J. Dorsey, a plastic surgeon who practiced in Yonkers for many years, died on Tuesday at the Manhattanville Nursing Center in the Bronx. He was 78 years old and lived in Yonkers. He died of a stroke, his family said. Dr. Dorsey served as a plastic surgeon in the Army in World War II, taking part in the Normandy invasion and the Battle of the Bulge.
        Dr. John J. Dorsey, a plastic surgeon who practiced in Yonkers for many years, died on Tuesday at the Manhattanville Nursing Center in the Bronx. He was 78 years old and lived in Yonkers. He died of a stroke, his family said. Dr. Dorsey served as a plastic surgeon in the Army in World War II, taking part in the Normandy invasion and the Battle of the Bulge.
        A Brig. Gen. in the Army Reserve, he was commander of the 102d Medical Battalion of the 42d Infantry (Rainbow) Division and commanding general of the 818th Hospital Center.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Anne Petty; four sons, Lieut. Col. John J. Jr., who is in the Army in Missoula, Mont., Edward, of Carmel, N.Y., Francis, of Manhattan, and Capt. Robert, who is also in the Army; four daughters, Maureen and Meg, both of Manhattan, and Mary Ann Smith and Susie Harrington, both of Yonkers; 10 grandchildren and four great-grandchildren.

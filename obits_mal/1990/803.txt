        Johnny Beazley, who won two games in the 1942 World Series as a rookie to help the St. Louis Cardinals beat the Yankees, died Saturday after having suffered from cancer. He was 71 years old.
        He had a 21-6 record in 1942, his only outstanding season in the major leagues. He pitched two complete games against the Yankees in the 1942 Series and his victory in Game 5 gave St. Louis the championship.
        He never regained his form after suffering an injury during Army service and had an overall record of 31-12, finishing his career in 1949 with the Boston Braves.
        He owned a beer distributorship in Nashville and served on the Metro Nashville Council from 1974-76.

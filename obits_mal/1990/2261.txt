        Dr. John Rensselaer Whittier, an expert on Huntington's disease and illnesses affecting the elderly, died on Tuesday at Eastern Long Island Hospital in Greenport. He was 71 years old and lived in Orient, L.I.
        He died of a heart attack, his wife, Margaret, said.
        A 1943 graduate of Columbia University College of Physicians and Surgeons, Dr. Whittier did his internship at Gorgas Hospital in the Panama Canal Zone and served in the Army on a hospital ship as medical transport officer in both the European and Pacific theaters.
        He did a residency in neurology at the Neurological Institute of New York and a psychiatry residency at the Veterans Administration Hospital in the Bronx. He was in private practice in New Jersey before being appointed principal research scientist in psychiatry at Creedmoor Institute of Psychobiological Studies at Creedmoor State Hospital in Queens.
        Dr. Whittier was director of medical information for the Committee to Combat Huntington's Disease, a national organization seeking a cure for the brain disorder. After the institute was closed in 1976, he became director of a psychobiology unit at the Long Island Research Center in Stony Brook. and later was clinical psychiatrist for Catholic Charities Service in Rockville Centre, L.I.. He retired in 1979.
        In addition to his wife, he is survived by two sons, Coty, of Washington, and Brian, of Hollywood; a stepbrother, Raymond, of Little Rock, Ark., and four grandchildren.

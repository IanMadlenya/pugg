        David Campbell, who retired in 1972 as president of the United States operations of the William Collins Ltd. publishing house, died Thursday of a heart ailment at St. Francis Hospital in Jersey City. He was 95 years old and lived in Jersey City.
        Mr. Campbell, a native of Scotland, joined the St. Andrew's Society of New York, a fraternal organization, when he arrived in the United States in 1924, and played the bagpipes for many years at dinners of the Robert Burns Society. He was also a member of the American Lawn Bowls Association.
        A native of Pollokshaws, a suburb of Glasgow, Mr. Campbell began working for the Collins house in his teens. He served in a Scottish regiment of the British Army and with the Royal Flying Corps in World War I. Sent by Collins to its New York offices in 1924, he rose to head the American publishing activities of the company, which later merged with World Publishing Company.
        Mr. Campbell's wife and daughter predeceased him. He is survived by a nephew, William Robertson of St. Petersburg, Fla., and a niece, Margaret Robertson of Colts Neck, N.J.

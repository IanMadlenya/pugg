        Dr. Ebenezer Thompson Breed, a physician on Long Island and surgeon for the Garden City police and fire departments for more than 35 years, died on Sunday at the health center at Vicar's Landing, a retirement community in Ponte Vedra Beach, Fla. He was 90 years old.
        He was an internist at Nassau Hospital, now Winthrop University Hospital, where he worked until his retirement in 1972. He was also in private practice and was on the staff of Meadowbrook Hospital, now called Nassau County Medical Center. He was a trustee of Hofstra University and was a member of many medical societies.
        Dr. Breed, a 1928 graduate of Cornell University Medical School, was for many years medical adviser to Planned Parenthood, Nassau County and to the Selective Service System in Nassau and Suffolk Counties. He was an Army veteran of both World Wars.
        He is survived by his son, Eben, of Garden City.

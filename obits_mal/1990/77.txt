        Alfred Van Santvoord Olcott, a financial planner and founder of a maritime historical society, died of complications of heart surgery at the University of Pennsylvania Hospital in Philadelphia.
        He was 70 years old and lived in Princeton, N.J.
        Mr. Olcott was a financial planner for Investors Diversified Services of New Jersey until his retirement in 1986.
        Mr. Olcott, who grew up in the Riverdale section of the Bronx, graduated from Princeton University in 1944 and served with the United States Navy in the South Pacific.
        He was a founder of the Hudson River Maritime Center in Kingston, N.Y.
        He is survived by his wife, Diana Morgan Olcott, of Princeton; two sons, C. Townsend Olcott 2d, of Hopewell, N.J., and Richard M. Olcott, of Manhattan; a daughter, Leslie Olcott, of East Arlington, Vt., and one grandson.

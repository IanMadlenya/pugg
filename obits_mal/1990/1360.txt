        Myron A. Wick Jr., former chairman and chief executive officer of Dominick & Dominick, the investment banking house, died on Wednesday at his home in Hobe Sound, Fla. He was 75 years old.
        Mr. Wick had been with Dominick & Dominick for 16 years before he resigned in 1972. He was also president of Plastics Manufacturers Inc. of Stamford, Conn., for many years.
        He was an alumnus of the St. Marks School, Harvard College and the Harvard Graduate School of Business Administration. He was a Navy lieutenant in World War II.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Elizabeth Dillingham; four children, Wendy Chase of Greenwich, Conn., Penelope DeYoung of Winnetka, Ill., Myron 3d, of San Francisco, and Walter, of Manhattan; a sister, Barbara Hunnewell of Wellesley, Mass., and 10 grandchildren.

        Jerome Schnur, an award-winning director and producer of television shows, died of melanoma on Monday at Mt. Sinai Hospital. He was 66 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Schnur staged ''Beat the Clock,'' ''I've Got a Secret,'' ''Two for the Money'' and ''What's My Line?'' He also produced ''Michelangelo's Pauline Chapel'' at the Vatican; ''Tomorrow Was Yesterday,'' a CBS science special; ''Light in the Wilderness,'' a Dave Brubeck oratorio, and ''L'Enfance du Christ,'' which ran for eight consecutive Christmases on CBS in the 1960's. Many of Mr. Schnur's productions were packaged by Schnur-Chastain Productions, which he founded in 1963.
        A former board member of WPBT in Miami, Mr. Schnur helped plan its studios and its programs. At WNET in New York, he worked with many choreographers, including Agnes de Mille, Antony Tudor and Alvin Ailey, to develop the ''Dance in America'' series. He directed the Joffrey Ballet in the series premiere. He received several Peabody and Emmy Awards.
        He is survived by his mother, Frances Buchsbaum Schnur of Miami Beach, and a sister, Celeste Stone of Houston.

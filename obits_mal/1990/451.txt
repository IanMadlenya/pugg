        Nat S. Rogers, co-founder of a company that became bi>Univar, one of North America's largest chemical distributors, died Tuesday at a nursing home in Seattle, his home city.He was 91 years old.
        Mr. Rogers was co-founder of Van Waters & Rogers Inc., in 1924. Over the years the Seattle-based company grew to become the Univar Corporation, with annual sales in excess of $1.4 billion.
        Mr. Rogers was a director and chairman of the executive committee of Univar. He was also a director of Pacific Northwest Bell, the United Pacific Corporation, Pacific Fisheries Inc. and the Olympic Steamship Company. He retired in 1970.
        Mr. Rogers, born in Portland, Ore., interrupted his studies to enlist in the Navy in World War I. After the war he graduated from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology.
        Mr. Rogers is survived by his wife, the former Marian Wurzbacher; two sons and a daughter: Robert S. of Hunts Point, Wash., and N. Stewart of Mercer Island, Wash.; Ann Wiborg of Tacoma, Wash.; and 11 grandchildren and 23 great-grandchildren.

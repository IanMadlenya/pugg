        Joseph H. Magruder, a retired businessman, died of lung cancer on Saturday at his home in Venice, Fla. He was 76 years old and had lived for many years in Fairfield, Conn.
        Mr. Magruder, a native of Savannah, Ga., was a graduate of the Kent School and Princeton. When the United States entered World War II he was one of a number of executives at the Sperry Gyroscope Company, where he was assistant treasurer, who were sent to England to form the staff nucleus of the Army's Eighth Air Force. He rose to the rank of colonel.
        After the war he worked for the Dixie Cup Company, the Oxford Paper Company and Automatic Data Processing, for which he was special projects manager when he retired in 1983.
        Mr. Magruder is survived by his wife, the former Marjorie Saunders; a daughter, Catherine Armour of Minnetonka, Minn.; two sons, Joseph Jr., of Concord, N.H., and Andrew, of Newport, R.I.; a brother, L. Burns Magruder Jr. of Barnstable, Mass., and five grandchildren.

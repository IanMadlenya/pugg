        Edwin Fortune Russell, a retired partner in the law firm of Cullen & Dykman, died on Thursday at Lawrence Hospital in Bronxville, N.Y. He was 80 years old and lived in Bronxville.
        He died of a heart attack, a spokesman for the firm said.
        Mr. Russell joined Cullen & Dykman in 1938 after graduating from New York University Law School. He spent his entire career with the firm, except for military service. He was in the Army Ordnance Department in 1942 and then served in the Navy until 1952, retiring as a lieutenant commander.
        He became a partner in Cullen & Dykman in 1952 and specialized in public utilities. He represented Brooklyn Union Gas Company and other utilities. In 1983 he was named a partner emeritus.
        He served as president of the New York State Bar Association from 1976 to 1977 and of the Federal Energy Bar Association, then known as the Federal Power Bar Association, from 1955 to 56.
        He was mayor of the Village of Bronxville from 1965 to 1967 and was a village justice from 1984 to 1988.
        He is survived by a son, Edwin, of Essex, Conn.; a daughter, Sarah Etchart of Fort Worth; a sister, Ann Kelly of Tucson, Ariz., and two grandchildren.

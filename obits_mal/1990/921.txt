        Osborne K. Taylor, a retired assistant deputy treasurer of the Standard Oil Company of New Jersey, died of heart disease on Monday in a nursing home in Montclair, N.J. He was 88 years old and lived in Caldwell, N.J.
        Mr. Taylor began in 1917 as an office boy with the company, which is now Exxon. He became a division manager in 1933 and retired as assistant deputy treasurer in 1959.
        After retiring, Mr. Taylor was a consultant to the Ford Foundation on its investment portfolio and to the Bankers Trust Company on pension problems of expatriate employees.
        He was a member of the Boy Scouts' national committee and a member of the board of Bloomfield College.
        There are no immediate survivors.

        Antonio Cipollone, whose lawsuit against three cigarette makers resulted in the first jury award against the industry involving the death of a smoker, has himself died, his wife said today. He was 66 years old.
        Mr. Cipollone was admitted to Community Memorial Hospital in Toms River Jan. 4 and died Jan. 10 of pneumonia and heart failure, his wife, Dorothy Cipollone, said. .
        On Jan. 5 the United States Court of Appeals for the Third Circuit overturned a $400,000 judgment against the Liggett Group Inc. But the court ordered a new trial and left the door open for other issues.
        The award to Mr. Cipollone in June 1988 was was the first granted by a jury in a lawsuit brought by a smoker or relatives of a smoker against the tobacco industry. Mr. Cipollone had continued a suit brought by him and his first wife Rose, who died at 58 of lung cancer in 1984. The suit named Liggett, Lorillard Inc. and Philip Morris Inc., which made the cigarettes Mrs. Cipollone smoked.
        Mr. Cipollone's attorney, Marc Z. Edell, said the family was still considering whether to continue the case.
        Mr. Cipollone was born in Italy and came to the United States at the age of 15, settling down in New York City. He worked in construction and as a cable splicer for United States Steel and in a business of his own. He retired in 1986.
        He and Rose Cipollone lived in Little Ferry, and he spent the last years of his life in Lakehurst.

        Frank Hohner, the former president of the American branch of H. Hohner Inc., the world's largest producer of harmonicas, died on Wednesday at a nursing home in Huntington, L.I. He was 81 years old and lived in Oyster Bay, L.I.
        He died of heart failure, his wife, Margarita, said.
        Mr. Hohner was president of Hohner U.S.A., Hohner's North American subsidary in Hicksville, L.I., from 1960 until his retirement in 1980. In 1983, the company moved its headquarters to Richmond. Before that, he was manager of the company's South American branch in Buenos Aires. The headquarters of the parent company is in Trossingen, Germany.
        Besides his wife, he is survived by three daughters, Erica Babcock of Cold Spring Harbor, L.I., Inge Mahon of Lawrenceville, Ga., and Karen Keith of Boston, and five grandchildren.

        Harold Field Kellogg Jr., an architect who restored or altered 200 town houses in Manhattan, died of a heart ailment Jan. 1 at Cottage Hospital in Nantucket, Mass. He was 72 years old and lived on Nantucket island.
        Mr. Kellogg shaped changes on the Harvard campus and designed many American homes, libraries at Princeton and Cornell, a Japanese teahouse at the Brooklyn Botanical Gardens and hotels in Jamaica. He was an advocate of solar energy.
        A native of Plymouth, Mass., he graduated from Brown and Yale universities. In World War II, he was a pilot in the Navy Air Corps.
        Surviving are two daughters, Alice, of Surrey, England, and Deryn, of Lynn, Mass.; two sons, Thomas, of St. Paul, Minn., and H. Field 3d, of Jacksonville, N.C.; a brother, Charles, of Woodbridge, England, and a grandson.

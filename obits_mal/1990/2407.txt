
        William H. Lynn, an adverting executive and yachtsman with international standing in the sport, died Tuesday near his home at Scotland Cay in the Bahamas. He was 59 years old.
        His wife, Gay Stirling Lynn, said from the Bahamas yesterday that the death occurred by accidental drowning while the couple was snorkeling.
        Mr. Lynn was vice president and corporate media director for the Coca-Cola Company in Atlanta, where he moved in 1983, while still maintaining a home in Rye, N.Y.
        From 1986 to 1988, he was president of the United States Yacht Racing Union, and vice president from 1979-1985. This year, he had become chairman of the International Classes Committee of the London-based International Yacht Racing Union, the sport's governing authority.
        Mr. Lynn competed in Olympic trial races series for the Star Class over the years, ending with the 1968 Olympic trials.
        Besides his wife, he is survived by his mother, Helen Martin Lynn of New Rochelle; a son, William Harcourt Lynn 3d of Noroton, Conn.; a daughter, Margaret Stirling Lynn of San Francisco, and a sister, Eileen Lynn Walker of Richmond, Va.

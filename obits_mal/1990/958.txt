        J. Francis Canny, an executive recruiter, died of lung cancer on Sunday at his home in Manhattan. He was 77 years old.
        In 1954 Mr. Canny helped start Hoff, Canny, Bowen & Associates, a Manhattan employment agency that recruited executives and that later became Canny, Bowen Inc. He later sold his interest in the company, and in 1984 he helped found Seiden Associates, another executive employment organization. He was with Seiden at the time of his death.
        He also was a founder of the Association of Executive Recruiting Consultants, of which he was president, and he was a director of the Federation of Protestant Welfare Agencies.
        In World War II Mr. Canny served as a personnel officer for the Seventh Amphibious Force and as commanding officer of LST 575. After the war he was a veterans' counselor at Columbia University.
        Mr. Canny was born in Norwich, N.Y., and graduated from the University of Rochester in 1935. He received a master's degree from Teachers College, Columbia University, in 1946.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Helen J. Stofer; a son, Dr. Christopher R. Canny of New Haven, and two grandchildren.

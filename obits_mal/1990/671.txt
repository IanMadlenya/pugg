        Dr. Ernest E. McMahon, an author, educator and a former dean at Rutgers University, died of cancer last Thursday at the William Hill Health Center in Easton, Md. He was 79 years old and lived in St. Michaels, Md.
        Dr. McMahon was associated with Rutgers for more than 40 years after receiving a bachelor's degree there in 1930. He later earned a Ph.D. from Columbia University.
        He first worked for Rutgers's public relations department and later as secretary of the university and director of alumni relations. In 1951, he was appointed dean of University College and of the University Extension Division. He became director of the Institute of Management and Labor Relations in 1959 and a professor of adult education in 1962. He retired in 1970.
        Dr. McMahon served with many civic and educational organizations and was mayor of Metuchen, N.J., in 1960 and 1961. He also wrote several books, including ''The Emerging Evening College,'' published in 1960.
        He is survived by his wife, Warranita; a daughter, Audrey Stahl of Staten Island, and two grandsons.

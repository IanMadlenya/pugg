        John R. Bond, publisher of Road & Track magazine from 1949 to 1972, died Friday at his home here. He was 77 years old. He died of emphysema, family members said.  Mr. Bond bought Road & Track, an automobile magazine, with his second wife, Elaine, said his son, John Jr. As publisher, Mr. Bond instituted staples of automotive journalism like the technical road test. In 1954 he wrote ''Sports Cars in Action.''
        He was trained as an automotive engineer and once designed motorcycles for Harley Davidson, his son said.
        In addition to his son, Mr. Bond is survived by his wife, Mercedes; a daughter, Marilee Nudo; a sister, Virginia Bundy, and a brother, William Bond.

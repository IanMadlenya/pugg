        Clarence (Hap) Day, who led the Toronto Maple Leafs to six Stanley Cup championships as a player and a coach in the 1930's and 1940's, died today at his home. He was 88 years old.
        Mr. Day was inducted into the Hockey Hall of Fame in 1961. He was a defenseman and team captain when the Maple Leafs captured the Stanley Cup in 1932.
        As a coach he led the Maple Leafs to five Stanley Cup championships, the first in 1942 and the second in 1945. Under his leadership, the team was the first in the National Hockey League to take the Stanley Cup in three consecutive years, from 1947 to 1949. Mr. Day resigned from the Maple Leafs in 1957, when he went into private business.

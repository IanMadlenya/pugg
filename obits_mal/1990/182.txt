        Peter Paul Muller, an architect, died of cancer on Sunday at Southampton (L.I.) Hospital after a long illness. He was 72 years old and lived in Bridgehampton, L.I.
        He was the head of Peter Paul Muller Architects in Southampton, which he founded in 1975. Among his clients were Market Square in Water Mill, L.I., and the Mansard, a Victorian office building in Southampton that was rennovated and restored. Earlier, he had been a partner in the architectural firm of Muller & Ash in Manhattan.
        Mr. Muller, who was born in Flushing, Queens, graduated from Bard College and from the Columbia School of Architecture.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Helen Bates; two daughters, Margaret Carr of Germantown, Md., and Katheryn Noble of Boyds, Md.; a son, Peter Paul Jr., of Sag Harbor, L.I., and five grandchildren.

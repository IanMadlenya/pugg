        Henry Salko, the president of the New York and New England district of B'nai B'rith, died of prostate cancer yesterday at the Stamford (Conn.) Hospital. He was 65 years old and lived in Stamford.
        Mr. Salko was chosen last year to lead the district of the Jewish service organization.
        He was the vice president of the Max Salko Corporation from 1950 until his death. The textile concern, at 17 West 31st Street in Manhattan, was founded by his parents, Max and Louise Salko. His mother, who survives him, is the president of the corporation. Mr. Salko lost an eye in the explosion. In addition to his mother, Mr. Salko is survived by his wife, the former Myrna Schacht; three children, Richard, of Manhattan, and Amy and Karen, both of Los Angeles; two stepsons, Michael, of Washington, and Evan, of Los Angeles; a brother, Alan, of White Plains, and two grandsons.

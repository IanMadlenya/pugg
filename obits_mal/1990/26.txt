        Dr. W. Rhys Jones, a former president of the medical staff and director of obstetrics and gynecology at Mountainside Hospital in Montclair, N.J., died of a heart ailment on Jan. 2 at Suburban Hospital in Bethesda, Md. He was 95 years old and lived in Chevy Chase, Md.
        Dr. Jones, also a former chief of obstetrics at Montclair Community Hospital, was a native of Scranton, Pa., who earned degrees at the University of Pennsylvania and its medical school and the Sloane Hospital for Women at Columbia University. He had a lifelong interest in music and history and gave his collection of Civil War memorabilia to Washington and Lee University.
        His wife, Ruth, of 63 years, died in 1987. Surviving are two daughters, Margaret Steuart of Chevy Chase, and Elizabeth Glaeser of Philadelphia; a son, Griffith H., of Montclair; eight grandchildren, and two great-grandchildren.

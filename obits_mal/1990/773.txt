        John D. McElroy, retired vice president and general counsel for the liquor division of the former National Distillers and Chemical Corporation, which is now the Quantum Chemical Corporation, in Manhattan, died of cancer on Friday at Westchester Medical Center in Valhalla, N.Y. He was 67 years old and lived in Bronxville.
        Mr. McElroy joined the corporation in 1961 and retired in 1987. Earlier, he had been assistant legal counsel for the Distilled Spirits Institute, a Washington trade association, and assistant general counsel to the National Association of Manufacturers.
        He was born in Altus, Okla., and graduated from the University of Oklahoma and from George Washington University Law School.
        Mr. McElroy is survived by his wife, the former Mary Dwyer; a daughter, Olivia Deleo of Wilton, Conn.; a brother, Fred, of Purcell, Okla.; a sister, Juanita Dorsett of Altus, and a granddaughter.

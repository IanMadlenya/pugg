        Gordon S. Braislin, a former chairman of the Dime Savings Bank and a retired real-estate broker, died on Tuesday at Greenwich (Conn.) Hospital. He was 89 years old and had homes in Manhattan and Pawling, N.Y.
        Mr. Braislin graduated from Cornell University in 1923 and joined New York Investors Inc. in the mortgage and real-estate departments. He later became president of two of the company's real-estate subsidiaries. In 1938, he founded his own real-estate company, later known as Braislin, Porter & Wheelock Inc. He was president of the company until 1965, when he was named chairman and chief executive officer of the Dime Savings Bank of Brooklyn, now known as the Dime Savings Bank of New York. He had served on the board of the bank since 1946, and he retired in 1976.
        Mr. Braislin was also a director and chairman of the Savings Bank Life Insurance Fund and a director of the Savings Bank Trust Company. He served as president of the New York Eye and Ear Infirmary for 15 years and helped raise money for construction of two hospital buildings.
        Mr. Braislin was a founding member of the New York Blood Center and a director of the American Bible Society. He was also a director of the Brooklyn Institute of Arts and Sciences and a member of the board of managers of the Epsicopal Diocese of New York.
        He is survived by two daughters, Elizabeth Powers of Winter Park, Fla., and Alice Bahrenburg of Rye, N.Y.; two sons, Gordon Jr. and William, both of Sherman, Conn., and 13 grandchildren.

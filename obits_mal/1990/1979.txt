        Alvin Broido, a teacher and principal in the New York City public school system for about 45 years, died on Thursday at Scripps Memorial Hospital in Encinitas, Calif. He was 85 years old and had lived in Rancho La Costa, Calif., since retiring in 1970.
        He died of heart failure, his son, Jeffrey, of La Jolla, Calif., said.
        Dr. Broido, joined the school system shortly after graduating from City College in 1924. He taught economics at Lafayette High School in Brooklyn and was an assistant principal at Lincoln Junior High School before being appointed principal of Public School 115 in the Canarsie section of Brooklyn in 1952. He held that post for 18 years until he retired.
        He also served as president of the New York City Elementary Schools Principals Association and president of the New York State Elementary Schools Principals Association.
        He was also a founder of the Canarsie Mental Health Clinic before moving to California where he was a founder of the La Costa Hospice.
        Besides his son, Jeffrey, of La Jolla, Calif., he is survived by his wife, June; a daughter, Valerie Kaufman of Hillside, L.I., and four grandchildren.

        Dr. Nahum J. Winer, a cardiologist, died on Thursday at Lenox Hill Hospital. He was 82 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Dr. Winer was an adjunct clinical professor of medicine at the New York Medical College in Valhalla, N.Y. He was a fellow of the New York Academy of Medicine.
        In addition to his private practice in New York, Dr. Winer researched ballistocardiography, a diagnostic technique used in the early evaluation of cardiac disease. In 1985, he received the H. C. Burger Memorial Award for his achievments from the 13th European Congress of Noninvasive Cardiovascular Dynamics in Brescia, Italy.
        Dr. Winer was born in Manhattan. He graduated from Harvard College and the Long Island College of Medicine.
        In World War II, Dr. Winer served with the 117th Evacuation Hospital in Europe, becoming a major. He was awarded the Bronze Star.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Toba Brill; two daughters, Deborah and Jessica, both of Manhattan, and a sister, Rosabelle Adelstein of Longboat Key, Fla.

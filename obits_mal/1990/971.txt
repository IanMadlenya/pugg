        Jose Lefebre, the Cuban-born artistic director of the Royal Ballet of Wallonie, has died after a long illness, the company said today. He was 54 years old.
        Mr. Lefebre became artistic director of the company in December 1980. ''Carmina Burana,'' ''Le Sacre du Printemps'' and ''Carmen'' are among his most successful productions.
        He was born into a Cuban-French family in Santiago de Cuba. In the 1950's he studied dance in New York with Martha Graham and others, and in 1959 he went to Europe, where he joined Maurice Bejart's Ballet of the 20th Century in Brussels. His first work for the Royal Ballet of Wallonie was ''Invitation to Jazz.''

        James F. Whelan, a Manhattan trucking company executive, died Sunday at Valley Hospital in Ridgewood, N.J. He was 86 years old and lived in Ho-Ho-Kus, N.J.
        Mr. Whelan died of heart failure, his son Michael said.
        Mr. Whelan was president for 25 years of the trucking company founded by his father, Joseph F. Whelan. He was also a trustee of the New York City Trucking Authority and of the health and pension fund of Locals 282 and 807 of the International Brotherhood of Teamsters.
        Mr. Whelan was a 1926 graduate of Notre Dame. In his sophomore year he was a second-string varsity guard on Knute Rockne's team, the last one to feature the ''Four Horsemen.''
        Besides his son Michael, of Ho-Ho-Kus, Mr. Whelan is survived by another son, James, of South Portland, Me.; two daughters, Mariellen, of Rosemont, Pa., and Julie Considine of South Bend, Ind.; a brother, Vincent, of Rumson, N.J.; two sisters, Irene Watters and Bernadette Watters, both of Brielle, N.J., and Scotsdale, Ariz.; nine grandchildren and one great-grandchild.

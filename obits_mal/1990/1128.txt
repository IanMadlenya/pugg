        Bernard S. Rogin, a retired vice president of the real estate firm of Wm. A. White & Sons, died of arthritis Friday at Tisch Hospital. He was 83 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Rogin, who was born in New Britain, Conn., and attended Boston University, was in charge of international dealings for White & Sons, with which he was associated for two decades before his retirement in 1970. He earlier ran his own firm in Connecticut.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Bette Chiaet Brown; two sisters, Dorothy Asher and Edith Pemsteen, both of Worcester, Mass., and a brother, Edward, of West Hartford, Conn.

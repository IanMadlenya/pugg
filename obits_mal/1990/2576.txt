
        Berkeley D. Johnson, a former vice chairman of the United States Trust Company of New York, died on Sunday at Palm Beach Gardens Hospital in North Palm Beach, Fla. He was 84 years old and lived in North Palm Beach and in Sharon, Conn.
        He died of a stroke, his wife, Helen, said.
        Mr. Johnson, an officer of United States Trust for 43 years, was vice chairman and a trustee from 1962 to 1972, when he retired. He had also been a director of many companies, including the Celanese Corporation, the Cunard Line, the General Portland Cement Company and the Skandia America Corporation.
        Among his civic posts were treasurer of the American Heart Association, chairman of the Westside Y.M.C.A. in Manhattan and an executive board member of the Salvation Army. He was a native of Hopedale, Mass., and a graduate and trustee of Wesleyan University in Middletown, Conn.
        Surviving, besides his wife, are two sons, Berkeley Jr., of New Canaan, Conn., and Craig, of North Palm Beach; two daughters, Lee Corbett and Gay Rogers, both of Bronxville, N.Y.; a sister, Eleanor Bates of Rancho Palos Verdes, Calif.; 13 grandchildren, and a great-granddaughter.

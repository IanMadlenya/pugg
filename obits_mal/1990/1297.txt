        Dr. Richard A. Atkins, a former first deputy commissioner of local government in New York, died on Sunday at the Albany Medical Center Hospital. He was 82 years old and lived in Delmar, N.Y.
        Dr. Atkins worked in the Office for Local Government from 1961 to 1974 and was first deputy commissioner from 1969 to 1972. In 1975, he joined the State Senate Task Force on Critical Problems, from which he retired in 1981 to become a consultant. He had previously been director of the Syracuse Governmental Research Bureau and executive director of the Boston Municipal Research Bureau.
        In 1973, the American Society for Public Administration gave Dr. Atkins the Gov. Alfred E. Smith Award for service in public administration.
        He was born in Detroit and graduated from Hamilton College in 1929. He received an M.S. from Syracuse University and a Ph.D. from Harvard University.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Helen Fiske; three daughters, Elizabeth, of Toronto, Virginia Warnes of Rye Brook, N.Y., and Carolyn Lathey of Oneonta, N.Y., and a granddaughter.

        Mikolaj W. Balicki, a former judge in Poland and a founder of a childrens' hospital in Cracow, died of heart failure on Wednesday at his daughter's home in Montclair, N.J. He was 97 years old.
        Mr. Balicki became a judge after graduating from the University of Cracow in 1921. In 1939 he was arrested by Russian troops and sent to Siberia. Released in 1942, he joined the Polish Army in Russia and served as a judge until 1945. He moved to London and in 1955 came to the United States. In the early 1960's, he was part of group that helped secure financing to build a 300-bed children's hospital in Cracow. The hospital, the Pediatric Institute of the Medical University of Cracow, now has 750 beds and is one of the largest children's hospitals in Europe.
        He is survived by his daughter, Zofia Biernacki-Poray of Montclair, three grandchildren and one great-grandchild.

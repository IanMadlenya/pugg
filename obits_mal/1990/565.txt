        The Rev. Winfield Blair Sutphin, a nationally known television preacher in the 1950's, died of pulmonary failure on March 12 at his home in Pompano Beach, Fla. He was 71 years old.
        In the 1940's and 1950's he made about 200 radio and television appearances on national radio and television networks, on programs like ''Lamp Unto My Feet'' and ''Midday Chapel.''
        His books of sermons included ''Thine the Glory,'' published by E. P. Dutton in 1962 and ''No Greater Love,'' published in 1965 by John Knox Press.
        A graduate of the New Brunswick Theological Seminary, Mr. Sutphin was ordained a minister in the Reformed Church in America in 1944.
        He had been senior minister of St. John's Christian Church (Disciples of Christ) in Pompano Beach since 1969. He was pastor of the Lighthouse Point First Presbyterian Church in Pompano Beach from 1956 to 1965 and pastor of the Park Hill Reformed Church in Yonkers, N.Y., from 1946 to 1955.
        He is survived by his wife, Julie, and a sister, Charlotte Payton of Boca Raton, Fla.

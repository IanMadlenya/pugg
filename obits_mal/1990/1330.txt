        Roger Allan Moore, a lawyer who served as general counsel to the Republican National Committee from 1981 until last year, died of cancer of the esophagus yesterday at the Dana-Farber Cancer Institute in Boston. He was 58 years old and lived in Boston.
        Mr. Moore, a partner in the law firm Ropes & Gray, was chairman of National Review Inc., publisher of National Review. since 1960. In the Presidential campaign of 1956 he was executive director for Massachusetts of Citizens for Eisenhower, and he was general counsel in the campaigns of Barry Goldwater and Ronald Reagan.
        Born in Framingham, Mass., Mr. Moore graduated with honors from Harvard College and received his law degree from Harvard Law School. As an undergraduate he was an official of the Young Republican National Federation. In 1987 he headed a delegation sent by President Reagan to observe the first Presidential election in Haiti in three decades.
        Mr. Moore is survived by his wife, the former Barbara Wildman; three sons, Marshall of North Andover, Mass., Taft of Concord, N.H., and Allan of Boston; a daughter, Elizabeth of Alexandria, Va., and five grandchildren.

        Philip M. Neufeld, second vice president and financial consultant with Shearson Lehman Hutton, died of congestive heart failure on Tuesday at the New York University Medical Center. He was 84 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Neufeld was an executive with Carl M. Loeb Rhodes earlier in his career. It later became part of Shearson Lehman Hutton. In the late 1950's and early 1960's, he also headed his own firm with seats on the New York and American Stock Exchanges.
        A book lover and stamp collector, Mr. Neufeld was a member of the board of Yale Library Associates for 31 years. He graduated from Stuyvesant High School in 1922 and attended New York University.
        A widower, he is survived by a son, Mace, of Beverly Hills, Calif.; a daughter, Carol Heller of White Plains; a brother, Ben, of Tamarac, Fla.; two sisters, Sarah Selis of Manhattan and Laura Robbins of Yonkers, and five grandchildren and two great-grandchildren.

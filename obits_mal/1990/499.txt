        George Robert Lyon, an advertising executive, died of a heart attack on Wednesday at Presbyterian Hospital in San Francisco. He was 67 years old and lived in Scarsdale, N.Y.
        Mr. Lyon was the former chairman of Grey, Conahay & Lyon, a Manhattan-based advertising concern that handled the accounts of International Business Machines and the Borden Company. Most recently, he was head of G. R. L. Development, a marketing and advertising consultant company he founded in Scarsdale.
        He began his career as a salesman for Sterling Drug in 1939 and in 1947 joined Fuller & Smith & Ross, eventually becoming vice president and general manager. In 1965, he bought the Robert Conahay agency and became founder and president of Conahay & Lyon. In 1980, Conahay merged with Grey/2 to create Grey, Conahay & Lyon.
        He is survived by his wife, Jacqueline; three daughters, Maureen, of Brooklyn, Patrice, of Manhattan and Dana, of San Francisco, and two grandchildren.

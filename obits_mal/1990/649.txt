        Gene L. Harmon, retired vice president of corporate public affairs for Sears, Roebuck and Company, died of a heart attack Saturday while traveling from Palm Springs, Calif., to his home in Albuquerque, N.M. He was 62 years old.
        Mr. Harmon joined Sears as a trainee in Lexington, Ky., and held a number of management posts before being named personnel director for its Midwestern territory in 1965.
        In 1973 he became personnel director for Sears headquarters in Chicago. He was elected vice president in 1981 and retired in 1982, after heart surgery.
        Mr. Harmon was a director of the Illinois State Chamber of Commerce and of the Public Affairs Council. He was also on the board of the Chicago Association of Commerce and Industry.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Heba Fowler; three daughters, Connie Presenza of Dundee, Ill., Cathy Thonn of Albuquerque and Christy Hale of Western Springs, Ill.; his mother, Chloe Harmon Spencer of Whitley City, Ky.; a sister, Karen Hewitt of Savannah, Ga.; a brother, Virgil of Cincinnati, Ohio ; and six grandchildren.

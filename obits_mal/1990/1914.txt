        George D. Bjurman, a retired investment portfolio manager, died Monday at the Medical Center of the University of California at Los Angeles. He was 84 years old and lived in Los Angeles.
        He died of leukemia and heart failure, a spokeswoman for the family said.
        Mr. Bjurman, who retired last year, was a former chairman and chief executive of George D. Bjurman & Associates, a Los Angeles money management firm he founded in 1970. From 1946 to 1970 he was investment manager for the Occidental Insurance Company, a unit of the Transamerica Corporation. He started his career with Wells Fargo Bank in 1930.
        He is survived by his wife, Dorothy, a daughter, Susan Bjurman, and a son, Andrew Bjurman, all of Los Angeles, three brothers and a sister.

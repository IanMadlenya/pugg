        Charles C. Ellis 2d, a corporate financial officer who held posts with the RCA Corporation, Irving Trust and the Philco Finance Corporation, died last Wednesday at Sibley Memorial Hospital in Washington. He was 71 years old and lived in Washington.
        He died of liver cancer, his family said.
        Mr. Ellis retired from RCA in 1982 as executive vice president for financial planning after a decade with the communications company. From 1965 to 1973 he was executive vice president and controller of Irving Trust, and before that he served for three years as controller of the consumer products division of Philco, a unit of the Ford Motor Company based in Philadelphia.
        Earlier he had been with the Kordite Corporation in Macedon, N.Y., with Ford in Dearborn, Mich., and with Armstrong Cork in Lancaster, Pa.
        Mr. Ellis was born in Baltimore, graduated from Juniata College and held a master's degree in business administration from Harvard University. In World War II he served as a Navy officer in ordnance shipping.
        He was chairman of the board of Juniata at his death and was a former chairman of the Pro Musicis Foundation, which sponsors artists who perform in prisons and nursing homes.
        Mr. Ellis is survived by his wife, the former Jean Good; two sons, Charles 3d of Vienna, Va., and Richard, of Narberth, Pa.; two daughters, Pauline, of Manhattan, and Nancy, of Tiburon, Calif.; a sister, Ruth Hoddinott of St. Petersburg, Fla., and four grandchildren.

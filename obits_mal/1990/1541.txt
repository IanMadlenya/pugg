        John B. Inglis, a former chairman and senior partner in the accounting firm of Price Waterhouse, died on Saturday at a nursing home in Lake Forest, Ill. He was 89 years old and had lived in Northbrook, Ill.
        Mr. Inglis became a partner in the firm in Manhattan in 1939, a member of its executive committee in 1944 and chairman and senior partner in 1954. He retired in 1961. He was a member of the American Institute of Certified Public Accountants and of the New York and New Jersey State Societies of Certified Public Accountants. He was a member of the National Association of Accountants and was its president from 1958 to 1959.
        In 1962 and 1963 he was an adjunct professor of business at the Columbia Graduate School of Business.
        He was born in Coromandel, New Zealand, and received his training as a public accountant there. He came to the United States in 1924, when he joined Price Waterhouse.
        Surviving are a daughter, Susan Chapman of Winnetka, Ill.; seven grandchildren, and two great-grandchildren.

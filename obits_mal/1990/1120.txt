        Benjamin Ellis Tenzer, a metallurgical engineer who was involved in reform Democratic politics in Brooklyn, died of amyotrophic lateral sclerosis at his home in Brooklyn last Wednesday. He was 54 years old.
        Mr. Tenzer, a graduate of the Massachusetts Institute of Technology, was a vice president of corporate quality programs at Ebasco Services Inc. in New York City.
        In 1982, along with Democratic colleagues, he successfully challenged the City Council in Federal court on the principle of one person one vote. The suit resulted in a special redistricting of the City Council.
        Mr. Tenzer is survived by his wife, Florence Bady Tenzer; two daughters, Emily Louise Tenzer of Brooklyn and Adrianne Diane Horowitz of Chicago, and two grandchildren.

        Dr. Edward Massie, a St. Louis cardiologist for 50 years, died Monday at a convalescent hospital in St. Louis County, Mo. He was 79 years old and lived in Clayton, Mo.
        The cause of death was systemic amyloidosis, a rare disease caused by an abnormal accumulation of the amyloid protein, Dr. Massie began practicing and teaching in St. Louis in 1938 after graduating from Washington University and completing his medical training at Peter Bent Brigham Hospital in Boston.
        Pioneer in Computer Method
        In 1941 he became director of the heart station at Barnes Hospital in St. Louis and developed the heart station at Jewish Hospital. He helped to pioneer the use of computers in the interpretation of electrocardiograms.
        Dr. Massie was a clinical professor of medicine at Washington University Medical Center in St. Louis. He was the author, with Dr. Bernard Lipman, in 1951 of ''Clinical Electrocardiography,'' a standard text now in its eighth edition. He retired in 1988.
        He was a fellow of the American College of Physicians and the American College of Cardiology. He was treasurer and a director of the American College of Cardiology. An Edward Massie Distinguished Lectureship and an Edward Massie Endowment Fund were established in his name at Washington University.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Felice Ozerovich; two sons, Dr. Henry Massie of Berkeley, Calif., and Dr. Barry Massie of Mill Valley, Calif.; a brother, Joel of St. Louis; and three grandchildren.

        Patrick V. Peppe, an associate professor of political science at Lehman College of the City University, died yesterday at New York Hospital after heart surgery. He was 49 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Dr. Peppe, who specialized in Latin American studies, had taught at Lehman since 1970. A native of Manhattan, he was a graduate of Harvard College and held a Ph.D. from Columbia. He was a board member of the City University's research foundation.
        Blind since the age of 15, Dr. Peppe was an advocate for the blind and was a director of The Lighthouse, formerly the New York Association for the Blind.
        He is survived by his wife, Ann Kinney.

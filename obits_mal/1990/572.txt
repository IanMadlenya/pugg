        Ronald T. Lyman Jr., who retired in 1970 as a general partner of Scudder Stevens & Clark after 40 years with the Boston investment firm, died of a stroke on Monday at Roper Hospital in Charleston, S.C. He was 84 years old and lived in Boston and Charleston.
        Mr. Lyman, who was born in Waltham, Mass., was a graduate of Harvard College, where he was elected to Phi Beta Kappa.
        He joined the investment firm two years after leaving Harvard and was president of the Scudder Stevens & Clark Balanced Fund, the first mutual fund in this country, and of the Scudder Common Stock Fund.
        In World War II, as a naval officer, he commanded an airfield on Saipan Island in the Pacific.
        Mr. Lyman is survived by his wife, the former Susan Storey; a son, Ronald 3d of Boston; three daughters, Jennifer Littlefield of Washington, Mabel Whiteside of Milton, Mass., and Elizabeth Lyman of Carrara, Italy; a stepson, S. Parkman Shaw Jr. of Boston; a stepdaughter, Jane Bizzi of Belmont, Mass.; a sister, Charlotte Clark of Pound Ridge, N.Y., and five grandchildren.

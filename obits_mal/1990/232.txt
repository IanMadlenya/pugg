        Robert W. Graham, a former personal representative of Defense Secretary Caspar W. Weinberger at the Vienna arms talks, died Friday of respiratory failure. He was 74 years old.
        Mr. Graham, a former Seattle lawyer, was appointed by President Lyndon B. Johnson to the National Administrative Conference, which analyzes Federal bureaucracy.
        In 1970, he was named to a State Department task force examining embassy operations in the Far East.
        Beginning in 1986, Mr. Graham served as the personal representative of Mr. Weinberger during negotiations in Vienna among NATO and Warsaw Pact countries on reductions of conventional arms in Europe.

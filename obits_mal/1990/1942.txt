
       Paul J. McCluskey, a vice president of Harcourt Brace Jovanovich, who headed the book publisher's school department, died on Thursday at his home in Orlando, Fla., at the age of 48.
        Mr. McCluskey, who joined the publishing concern in 1968, did early work in electronic publishing in San Diego. In Orlando, he directed video operations before heading the school department.
        He was born in Sioux City, Iowa, and raised in Lyons, Neb. A graduate of Wayne State College in Nebraska, he received a master's degree in fine arts from the University of Notre Dame.
        He is survived by his mother, Marian, of Fremont, Neb.; a son, Peter, of Fayetteville, Ark.; two brothers, David, of Tempe, Ariz., and Patrick, of Richmond, Calif., and a sister, Mary Hendrickson of Fremont.

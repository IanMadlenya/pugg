        Butler T. Henderson, an official of the NAACP Legal Defense and Educational Fund for the last 18 years, died at St. Luke's Hospital on Monday. He was 71 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        He died of cardiac arrest, his family said.
        Mr. Henderson joined the fund in 1972 as director of the Earl Warren Legal Training Program.
        He was born in Knoxville, Tenn., and received degrees from Morehouse College and New York University. He taught at the University of Arkansas before becoming assistant to the president of Morehouse in 1960, a post he held until 1968, when he became associate director of the United Board for College Development. From 1970 to 1972 he was with the United Negro College Fund.
        Mr. Henderson is survived by his wife, the former Charlie Lee Scott, and two brothers, Hovey and John, both of Hot Springs, Ark.

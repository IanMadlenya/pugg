        Anthony F. Barry, the principal of Public School 42 in Chinatown for the last 15 years, died of cancer on Sunday at his home on Staten Island. He was 59 years old.
        Mr. Barry was a graduate of the City College of New York and began his career in education as a teacher at Junior High School 112 in Brooklyn. After five years, he was appointed assistant principal of P.S. 2 in Manhattan and remained there for 10 years. At P.S. 42, he helped develop bilingual programs for Chinese-speaking students and was recently honored as the district's supervisor of the year.
        He is survived by his wife, Rose; three sons, Anthony, James and Edward, all of Staten Island; two sisters, Catherine Grable of Long Beach, N.J., and Margaret Klauder of Edison, N.J., and a brother, John, of Atlanta.


       Dr. Eugene Moskowitz, a specialist in physical medicine and rehabilitation, died on Saturday at Sharon Hospital in Connecticut. He was 78 years old and lived in Salisbury, Conn.
        His wife, Virginia, said her husband died of colon cancer.
        Dr. Moskowitz was director of the department of physical medicine and rehabilitation at the Westchester County Medical Center in Valhalla, N.Y., from the late 40's to 1981. He was a former director of medicine and rehabilitation at Lawrence Hospital in Bronxville, N.Y., and an attending physician at Metropolitan Hospital in Manhattan. He was director of rehabilitation and medicine at Sharon Hospital from 1981 until retiring last year.
        Dr. Moskowitz was born in Manhattan and graduated from City College in 1932 and the University of Basel Medical School in 1937.
        In World War II he was in the Army Medical Corps from 1941 to 1945, leaving as a major. He won the Soldier's Medal.
        Also surviving are three sons, William, of Saratoga Springs, N.Y., Paul, of Brookfield, Conn., and John, of Eastchester, N.Y., and nine grandchildren.

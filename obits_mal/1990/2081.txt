        Eli A. Freydberg, a former executive with Consolidated Foods, died on Thursday at White Plains Hospital. He was 87 years old and lived in Fort Lauderdale, Fla.
        He died after an aneurysm ruptured in his stomach, his son, Anthony, of Chappaqua, N.Y., said.
        Mr. Freydberg was with Consolidated Foods as a group vice president from 1966 until he retired in 1970. Before that, he was president of Consolidated Trimming Corporation, a Manhattan manufacturer of drapery and upholstery trimmings, later known as Conso Products Inc. He was a graduate of Pomona College.
        Besides his son, he is survived by his wife, the former Peggy Finke; three daughters, Lynn Warshow and Susan Wolff, both of Manhattan, and Nancy Verner of Westport, Conn., and six grandchildren.

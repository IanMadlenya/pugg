        Dr. Elmer Louis Grimes, a surgeon and former chairman of the Medical Inter-Insurance Exchange of New Jersey, died last Thursday at Our Lady of Lourdes Medical Center in Camden, N.J. He was 76 years old and lived in Haddonfield, N.J. He died of a brain tumor, his family said.
        Dr. Grimes was an attending physician at Our Lady of Lourdes from 1951 until 1988, when he retired. He was the center's chief of surgery from 1972 to 1982. In 1976, he helped found the Medical Inter-Insurance Exchange, one of the first medical insurance groups in the country organized by physicians.
        He was a native of Winthrop, Mass., and a graduate of Harvard College and Tufts Medical School. In World War II, he served in the Navy Seabees in the South Pacific.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Juie Gambal; a daughter, Ann, of Washington; three sons, Peter, of Reading, Pa., and Paul and Thomas, both of Manhattan, and two granddaughters.

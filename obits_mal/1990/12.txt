        Frederick Hurd, a retired investment counselor and a trustee of the Children's Aid Society, died of a heart attack on Wednesday at St. Luke's-Roosevelt Hospital Center in Manhattan. He was 86 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Hurd had been a partner in the investment counseling firm of Brundage Story & Rose from 1960 to 1980, when he retired. He joined Brundage in 1941. Before then he worked for several Manhattan investment firms and for Standard Brands in Buffalo.
        He is survived by his wife, Katherine; four stepchildren, Nancy C. Berresford of Philadelphia, Susan V. Berresford and Geoffrey C. Berresford, both of Manhattan, and John W. Berresford of Washington; a brother, Christopher, of Cambridge, Mass., and three step-grandchildren.

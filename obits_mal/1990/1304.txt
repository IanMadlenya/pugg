        Newton Felch McKeon, a retired professor of English and former director of the Robert Frost Library at Amherst College, died Tuesday at his home in Lexington, Mass. He was 85 years old. The cause of death was not disclosed.  Mr. McKeon joined the faculty at Amherst in 1931 and, except for a year at Cambridge University in England on a research fellowship, spent his career there. He retired as professor emeritus in 1970.
        A 1926 graduate of Amherst, Mr. McKeon became assistant to the director of the library there in 1935 and was director from 1939 to 1970.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Mary Maury Fitzgerald; a daughter, Edith King Abbott of Brookline, Mass.; three sons, Maury of Simsbury, Conn., Lewis Herndon of Lebanon, N.H., and Seamus of Weston, Conn., and nine grandchildren.

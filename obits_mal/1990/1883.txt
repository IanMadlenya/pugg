        John B. Creegan, a retired New York lawyer, died on Sunday at Columbia-Presbyterian Medical Center. He was 80 years old and lived in the Riverdale section of the Bronx.
        He died from complications of a stroke he suffered two weeks before, said his wife, the former Jane Wright.
        Mr. Creegan, a native New Yorker, was managing attorney with the law firm of Shearman & Sterling when he retired in 1978. Earlier, he worked as an assistant United States attorney in Manhattan and was a law assistant in the Appellate Division of the State Supreme Court.
        He was a graduate of Fordham College and of Fordham Law School.
        Besides his wife, he is survived by two daughters, Marylyn Flynn of Wilton, Conn., and Joan Elicker of New Canaan, Conn., and five grandchildren.

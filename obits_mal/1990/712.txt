        John W. Larsen, a former president and vice chairman of the Bowery Savings Bank, died on Sunday at his home in West New York, N.J., after suffering a stroke. He was 75 years old.
        Mr. Larsen was with the Bowery for 39 years, becoming president in 1966 and vice chairman in 1978, before retiring in 1979. In the years he was president the Bowery was the largest savings bank in the city.
        He was a leader of the Citizens Budget Commission, a watchdog group in New York, for many years and was president of the group from 1968 to 1971 and 1979 to 1981. He was also honorary chairman.
        Mr. Larsen was born in New York. He had a bachelor's degree from New York University and attended the Rutgers Graduate School of Banking and the N.Y.U. Graduate School of Business Administration. He served in the Navy in World War II.
        Before joining the Bowery in 1949, Mr. Larsen was with the North River Savings Bank for 16 years.
        He was also a trustee of N.Y.U., the Museum of the City of New York, the Inner-City Scholarship Fund of the Roman Catholic Archdiocese of New York, the Jewish Guild for the Blind and the National Jewish Hospital in Denver.
        Mr. Larsen was a former chairman of the Haworth, N.J., planning board.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Eleanor Waterhouse; two sons, Dr. John Jr., of Washington, and Grergory, of Cambridge, Mass; two daughters, Nancy Farrell of Manhattan and Dr. Melissa Larsen of Burke, Va.; a sister, Elsie Burrows of Tenafly, N.J., and five grandchildren.

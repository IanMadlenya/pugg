        Alfred L. Ferguson Jr., an investment executive with the Wall Street securities firm of Dominick & Dominick before his retirement in 1965, died of congestive heart failure on Wednesday at his home in Atlantic Highlands, N.J. He was 85 years old. Mr. Ferguson, who was born in Manhattan, was a graduate of Yale and the Yale Law School. He was with the securities firm of Estabrook & Company before serving in the Army Air Forces in World War II and rising to the rank of lieutenant colonel.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Janet Kinney; a son, Alfred 3d, of Fair Haven, N.J.; a daughter, Joan Ellis of Middletown, N.J.; a brother, Howard, of Avon, Conn.; two sisters, Carol Chapman of Mount Kisco, N.Y., and Ruth Woolfe of Camden, S.C.; six grandchildren, and seven great-grandchildren.

        Paul W. Pinkerton, a partner in Haskins & Sells accounting firm, died on Friday at Memorial Sloan-Kettering Cancer Center. He was 74 years old and lived in Wayne, N.J.
        Mr. Pinkerton died of leukemia, his daughter, Judy, said.
        Mr. Pinkerton, a certified public accountant, had worked for Haskins & Sells, now Deloitte & Touche, in Manhattan for 40 years before retiring in 1976.
        He was born in Chicago and graduated from the University of Michigan in 1937. He received a master's degree in business administration from the university in 1938.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Marian Treybal; four daughters, Judy, of Manhattan, Christine Speiser of Red Bank, N.J., Helen Rydell of Santa Barbara, Calif., and Jeanne Homes of Irvine, Calif.; a brother, David, of Lynchburg, Va.,; a sister, Estelle Black of Whiting, N.J., and eight grandchildren.

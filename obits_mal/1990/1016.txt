        Garrison R. Corwin, an insurance broker and a transportation executive for more than 50 years, died of heart failure on Sunday at his home in Scarsdale, N.Y. He was 81 years old.
        Mr. Corwin founded the Scarsdale Bus Company in 1932 and later the White Plains Bus Company and Chappaqua Transportation. He was a founding member of the New York School Bus Contractors' Association and was an early proponent of interdistrict school-transportation contracts.
        He was also an insurance broker in Manhattan with Dunn & Fowler and later with Frank B. Hall. Mr. Corwin served in the Army in World War II.
        Surviving are his wife, Rosamond; three sons, Garrison Jr., of Yorktown Heights, N.Y., Seth, of Hornell, N.Y., and Nathaniel, of Cornwall, Conn.; nine grandchildren, and three great-grandchildren.

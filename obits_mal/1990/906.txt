        David M. Robb, an art historian and a teacher, died on Monday at his home in Scituate, Mass. He was 86 years old.
        Mr. Robb, a Guggenheim fellow and Fulbright scholar, taught at Colgate University, the University of Minnesota and, for 35 years, at the University of Pennsylvania. He retired in 1974 as professor emeritus.
        He published ''The Harper History of Painting: The Occidental Tradition'' and ''The Art of the Illuminated Manuscript.'' He was a co-author, with J. J. Garrison, of ''Art in the Western World.''
        Surviving are his wife, Jane Howard Robb; two daughters, Martha Spiegel of Pelham, N.Y., and Sara Yorke of Scituate; a son, David M. Jr., of Huntsville, Ala., and three grandchildren.


       James D. Seamen, a clinical psychologist who was an official of the Community Research Initiative at St. Luke's-Roosevelt Hospital, died on Wednesday at the hospital. He was 46 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        He died of lymphoma, a spokesman for the hospital said.
        Since 1987 Dr. Seamen had been co-director of research for the Community Research Inititative, which conducts clinical studies of experimental drugs to expedite government approval of new treatments for AIDS.
        From 1984 to 1987 he was senior psychologist and coordinator of psychological services at Seaview Hospital on Staten Island.
        He is survived by his mother, Ann, of Lake Grove, L.I.; a brother, Edward, of Cliffside, Park, N.J., and a sister, Carol Millwater of Middle Island, L.I.

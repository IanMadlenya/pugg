        Alfred H. Lovell, a retired consular official and former executive at the Book-of-the-Month Club, died Aug. 1 at his home in Ann Arbor, Mich. He was 74 years old.
        He died of leukemia, said his daughter Stephanie Lovell, of West Hartford, Conn.
        Mr. Lovell joined the Foreign Service after graduating from the Fletcher School of International Law and Diplomacy in Medford, Mass. in 1939. He served in Toronto; Bogota, Colombia; London, Warsaw and Paris. He retired from the State Department in 1960 and joined the Book-of-the-Month Club as executive director and editor. He retired in 1980.
        Besides his daughter Stephanie, he is survived by his wife, Krystyna; two other daughters, Katherine Lovell of East Longmeadow, Mass., and Hope Lovell of Greenwich, Conn.; two sons, Andrew Lovell of Washougal, Wash., and Douglas Lovell of Goffstown, N.H.; a brother, Robert Lovell of Ann Arbor; and six grandchildren.

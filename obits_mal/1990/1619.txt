        Robert Alan Roth, a New York lawyer, died on Monday at his home in Manhattan. He was 40 years old.
        Mr. Roth died of Hodgkin's disease, a family spokesman said.
        He maintained a private practice in Manhattan and handled estate law and landlord-tenant cases, among other matters. Among Mr. Roth's more well-known cases was Chin vs. London Management, in which he won a judgment in New York City's Landlord and Tenants Court ruled that unmarried tenants shared survivors' rights to an apartment.
        He was born in Englewood, N.J., and graduated from the Horace Mann School in 1967, from Cornell University in 1971 and from Fordham Law School in 1975.
        He is survived by his parents, Fred and June Roth of Teaneck, N.J., and a sister, Nancy Bjorkman of Nashville, Tenn.
        Correction:  July 27, 1990, Friday, Late Edition - Final
        An obituary on July 12 about Robert A. Roth, a New York lawyer, described his background incompletely.
        He was active in representing gay and lesbian clients on issues like landlord-tenant disputes, often without charge, and was a founder of the Cornell University Gay and Lesbian Alumni Association.

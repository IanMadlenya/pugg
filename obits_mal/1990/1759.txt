        Herbert C. Johnson, retired chairman and president of the Consolidated Natural Gas Company, died on Wednesday at St. Vincent's Hospital on Staten Island. He was 81 years old and lived in Dongan Hills, S.I.
        Mr. Johnson died of congestive heart failure, said his son, Keith.
        Mr. Johnson had been with Consolidated Natural Gas since 1943, when that company was formed to own and operate the natural gas utility subsidiaries divested by Standard Oil of New Jersey, now Exxon. He retired in 1974, after serving as treasurer, financial vice president, executive vice president, president and chairman.
        From 1972 to 1983, he was a director of Brooklyn Union Gas and a trustee of the Staten Island Savings Bank. He had been a director and treasurer of the American Gas Association and a member of the National Petroleum Council.
        He is survived by his wife, the former May Welzin; two sons, Keith E., of Staten Island, and Douglas W., of Appleton, Wis.; two sisters, Ethel Lambert, also of Staten Island, and Betty Lentz of Timonium, Md., and three grandchildren.

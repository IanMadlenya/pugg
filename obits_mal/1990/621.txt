        Henry W. Happel, a retired senior partner in the Manhattan law firm of Brown & Wood, died on Sunday at Riverhead Hospital on Long Island after a long illness. He was 78 years old and lived in Quogue, L.I.
        Mr. Happel, a 1936 graduate of Harvard Law School, where he was editor of the Law Review, joined the predecessor firm of Brown, Wood, Fuller, Caldwell & Ivey and was named a partner in 1943.
        He served in the Office of General Counsel in the Navy Department from 1944 to 1945 and returned to the law firm, where he remained until his retirement in 1976. He served as secretary to the Charles E. Merrill Trust and was a trustee of the Nichols Foundation and the Yorkshire Fund.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Alice Hicks; and a son, H. William 3d, of Los Angeles.


        Theodore C. Marrs, a retired Air Force brigadier general and aide to President Gerald R. Ford, died Monday at his home in Albuquerque. He was 72 years old.
        The cause of death was not disclosed.
        General Marrs, a doctor who graduated from the University of Tennessee medical school, was named a special assistant for human resources by President Ford in 1974.
        From 1970 to 1974 he was Deputy Assistant Secretary of Defense for Reserve Affairs under President Richard M. Nixon. Previously he was a special assistant to the Air Force surgeon general.
        He is survived by his wife, Annette; two daughters, five sons and nine grandchildren.

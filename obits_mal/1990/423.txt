        Bodo Ludwig Otto Richter, a retired professor of romance languages, died on Friday at his home in West Southport, Me., after a long illness. He was 74 years old.
        Dr. Richter was professor of French, Spanish and Italian at the State University of New York at Buffalo from 1965 until his retirement 20 years later at the age of 70. Before joining the state university he taught for 14 years at the University of Pennsylvania and earlier at Brown University, Phillips Exeter Academy and Lafayette College.
        Born in Leverkusen, Germany, Dr. Richter received a master's degree from Harvard University in 1941 and a Ph.D from the University of Pennsylvania in 1951. He contributed articles to many scholarly journals and was a member of various professional organizations, including the Modern Language Association of America and the Dante Society of America.
        He is survived by his wife, Gail; two sons, Andrew and Claude, and a daughter, Michelle, all of West Southport; a brother, Bruno, of South Berwick, Me., and Arno, of Lancaster, Pa.

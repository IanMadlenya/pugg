        Joe Fox, the track and field coach at Fordham Prep for 30 years before his retirement in 1976, died Wednesday at New Rochelle Hospital after a brief illness. He was 79 years old.
        Mr. Fox, who was born in Carbondale, Pa., and lived in the Riverdale section of the Bronx, was a leading figure in New York City high school track and field for more than four decades. In 1956, he started the Eastern States Outdoor High School Track and Field Championships and conducted the meet for many years. He also founded the New York Relays meet and served as chairman of the Catholic High Schools Athletic Association track and field section for more than 10 years.
        There are no immediate survivors.

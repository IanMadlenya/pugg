        Robert J. Wood, former chairman and chief executive of the public relations agency of Carl Byoir & Associates, died of amyotrophic lateral sclerosis, also known as Lou Gehrig's disease, on Wednesday at his home in Scarsdale, N.Y. He was 71 years old.
        When Mr. Wood retired in 1984, Carl Byoir was one of the largest agencies in the field. He started as an account executive in Byoir's Chicago office in 1947 and moved to New York as executive vice president in 1961. He was elected president in 1965, became chief executive in 1977 and was named chairman in 1981.
        Among Mr. Wood's corporate clients were A & P, American Can, Eastman Kodak, B. F. Goodrich, Hallmark, Howard Johnson, Hughes Aircraft and F. W. Woolworth.
        Reporter in Syracuse
        A native of Syracuse, Mr. Wood received a bachelor's degree from Syracuse University and a master's degree from the Columbia Graduate School of Business. He was a reporter for The Syracuse Herald-Journal and a correspondent for The Associated Press in central New York State before serving as a public relations officer in the Army Air Forces in World War II.
        Mr. Wood's account of his days in the business, ''Confessions of a PR Man,'' was published in 1988 by New American Library.
        He is survived by a son, Robert Jr., of Chicago; two daughters, Judy Humbrecht of McLean, Va., and Maureen Hoberman of Scarsdale; a brother, the Rev. John M. Wood of Rome, N.Y., and three grandchildren.

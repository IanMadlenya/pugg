        John J. Manning, security chief for the 1964-65 New York World's Fair, died of a heart attack on Monday at his home in North Palm Beach, Fla. He was 82 years old.
        Mr. Manning established and managed a police force of 1,000, a three-company fire department and a variety of other services for the fair in Flushing Meadows-Corona Park in Queens.
        He had previously been a special agent of the Federal Bureau of Investigation for 20 years, working in Atlantic City, Philadelphia and New York.
        He was born in Worcester, Mass., and graduated from St. John's University in 1930.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Edith Hoffman; two sons, John, of Huntington, L.I., and Kenneth, of Milwaukee; a daughter, Dr. Stephanie Maroney of Philadelphia, and 13 grandchildren.

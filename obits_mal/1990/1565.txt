
       Jean Dabry, navigator for Jean Mermoz on several of the French pilot's pioneering flights in the 1930's, died on Wednesday. He was 89 years old. The cause of death was not disclosed. On May 12, 1930, Mr. Dabry and Mr. Mermoz completed the first nonstop flight between Africa and South America, crossing the South Atlantic from Senegal to Brazil in 21 hours and 14 minutes.
        Jean Dabry, navigator for Jean Mermoz on several of the French pilot's pioneering flights in the 1930's, died on Wednesday. He was 89 years old. The cause of death was not disclosed. On May 12, 1930, Mr. Dabry and Mr. Mermoz completed the first nonstop flight between Africa and South America, crossing the South Atlantic from Senegal to Brazil in 21 hours and 14 minutes. Four years later, they completed the first commercial flight between Paris and Buenos Aires.

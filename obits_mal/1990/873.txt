        Harold Leland Stults, a retired partner in the law firm of Curtis, Morris & Safford in New York, died of cancer last Saturday at his home in Short Hills, N.J. He was 84 years old.
        Mr. Stults, a patent lawyer, worked his entire career at the firm, which he joined in 1936. He retired in 1982.
        He was born in Lebanon, Ind. Mr. Stults graduated from Purdue University in 1930 with a degree in electrical engineering and the George Washington University Law School in 1934.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Evelyn Rose Lynch; two sons, Harold Jr., of Manhattan, and Robert, of Palo Alto, Calif.; a brother, Walter, of Arroyo Grande, Calif.; a sister, Elsie Wiedenhoeft of Columbia City, Ind., and four grandchildren.

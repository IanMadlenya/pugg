        Sidney Brill, a civil engineer and the owner of a public-works construction company, died on Monday at Good Samaritan Hospital in Bay Shore, L.I. He was 64 years old and lived in Manhattan. He died of lung cancer, his family said.
        He founded the Sarcon Construction Company of Astoria, Queens, in 1964. Among its projects were reconstruction of Harlem River bridges and rehabilitation of Metro-North stations. He received a bachelor's degree from Virginia Polytechnic Institute and an engineering degree from Cornell after Army service in World War II.
        He is survived by his wife, Marlyn; a daughter, Jane, and two sons, James and Peter, all of Manhattan.

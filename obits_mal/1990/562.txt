        Robert A. Sander, president and chief operating officer of Golden Valley Microwave Foods Inc., in Edina, Minn., died of cancer on Monday at University Hospital in Ann Arbor, Mich., where he was undergoing treatment.
        Mr. Sander was 65 years old and lived in New Canaan, Conn., Minneapolis and Longboat Key, Fla.
        He joined Golden Valley as chief operating officer in 1988 and was named president in 1989. From 1985 to 1988 he was president of Robert A. Sander Inc., a sales and marketing consulting concern in New Canaan, and from 1972 to 1985 he was a senior vice president of Pepsico Inc., in Purchase, N.Y.
        Mr. Sander graduated from the Wharton School of the University of Pennsylvania. He began as a salesman with the Scott Paper Company and rose to vice president in charge of sales and marketing for Scott's consumer package products before joining Pepsi.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Aubin Redfield; two daughters, Margaretta, of Schwenksville, Pa., and Nancy, of Los Angeles; a son, Robert, of Mission Viejo, Calif., and two stepsons, James Bryan Jr. of Greensboro, N.C., and William Bryan of New Canaan, and 10 grandchildren.

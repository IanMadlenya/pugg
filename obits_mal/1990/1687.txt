        John Fox, a novelist and short-story writer, died of AIDS on Aug. 14 at his home in Manhattan, a family spokesman said. He was 38 years old.
        His most successful novel, ''The Boys on the Rock'' (1984), recounted a young athlete's initial denial and later acknowledgement of his homosexuality.
        Mr. Fox, a native of the Pelham Bay section of the Bronx, was a graduate of Cardinal Hayes High School and Lehman College.
        Surviving are his parents, John Sr. and Joan, of the Bronx; a brother, James, of Danbury, Conn., and a sister, Dorothy Schmidt of Malvern, Pa.

        Vladimir B. Adzemovic, a banker and president of the lay committee of the Serbian Orthodox Cathedral of St. Sava on West 26th Street in Manhattan, died on Monday at his home on Staten Island. He was 63 years old.
        He died of cancer, said his son, Radivoje, of Charlotte, N.C.
        Mr. Adzemovic, a graduate of the University of Paris, was a vice president of the French-American Banking Corporation. He was born in Washington, where his father was in the Yugoslav diplomatic service, and he had lived in the United States since 1958.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Vogislava Gavrilovic; a daughter, Kosara, of Staten Island; his mother, Aspasija, of Alexandria, Va.; two sisters, Danica Adzemovic of Alexandria and Mirosandra Amber of Amherst, Va.; and three grandchildren.

        David S. Parker, a retired Army major general who was a former Governor of the Panama Canal Zone, died of congestive heart failure on Wednesday at Marin General Hospital at Greenbrae, Calif. He was 71 years old and lived in Belvedere, Calif.
        General Parker was chairman of a special review channel in the office of the Army Chief of Staff in March 1971 when he was appointed Governor of the Canal Zone, a post he held until he retired in 1975.
        He served in the Canal Zone from 1952 to 1954 as military assistant to the Governor and returned in 1963 as lieutenant governor. He was Acting Governor in 1964 when Panamanian students demonstrated against flying the American flag in the zone. #24 Deaths, Relations Severed He called on United States troops to restore order, an action that resulted in the deaths of 20 Panamanians and 4 American soldiers. Relations between the United States and Panama were severed for four months.
        The son of an Army officer, David Parker was born at Fort Huachuca, Ariz. He was graduated from the United States Military Academy in 1940.
        In World War II, he served on the staff of Adm. Chester Nimitz and on Gen. Douglas MacArthur's staff in Tokyo as chief of construction. In 1948, he returned to the United States, where he received a master's degree in civil engineering from the University of California at Berkeley.
        General Parker's awards included the Distinguished Service Medal and the Legion of Merit.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Betty Augur; three sons, David and Bruce of Washington, and Steven of Fairbanks, Alaska; a daughter, Ann Diggory of Saratoga Springs, N.Y.; a brother, Richard B. of Washington, and four grandchildren.

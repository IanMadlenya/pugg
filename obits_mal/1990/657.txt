        Vincent J. Commisa, Chief Judge of the United States Bankruptcy Court for the District of New Jersey, died of a heart attack on Sunday at his home in West Orange, N.J. He was 68 years old.
        Judge Commisa had been a referee in bankruptcy court since 1967. In 1984 when the court was set up, he became its first Chief Judge, sitting in Newark. Two years later he was appointed to a new 14-year term.
        Judge Commisa, a native of Newark, was a graduate of Notre Dame. After service in the Army in World War II and a brief career as a professional football player and coach, he received a law degree from Rutgers.
        In 1961 he joined the United States Attorney's office in Newark as chief of the civil division.
        Judge Commisa is survived by his wife, the former Ann Conrey, and a sister, Nicolina Smyth of Glen Ridge, N.J.

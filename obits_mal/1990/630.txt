        Frank L. Haynes Jr., vice president and assistant general counsel at the Prudential Insurance Company in Newark, died on Monday at the Columbia-Presbyterian Medical Center in Manhattan. He was 45 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        His mother, Carrie E. Haynes, said he died of a collapsed lung and breathing difficulties.
        Mr. Haynes, who grew up in Harlem, graduated from Yale Law School in 1969. He was involved in antipoverty law in Los Angeles before joining Millbank Tweed Hadley & McCoy in Manhattan. He joined the legal staff at Prudential in 1976. He was general counsel of the Prudential's health maintenance organization, Pru-Care.
        He was also associated with the Dance Theater of Harlem and Ballet magazine.
        In addition to his mother, he is survived by his father, Frank Sr., of Manhattan; three sisters, Pat Rodriguez of Manhattan, Juliette, of Philadelphia and Alice, of the Bronx, and four brothers, Jonathan, of the Bronx, Jerry, of Newark, Allan, of Manhattan, and Bryan, of Brooklyn.

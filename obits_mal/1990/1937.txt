        Joseph Leavitt, executive vice president and general manager of the Philharmonic Orchestra of Florida, died Friday at a nursing home in Boca Raton, Fla. He was 74 years old and lived in Boca Raton.
        Mr. Leavitt died of complications from a stroke he suffered on Aug. 13, a spokesman for the family said.
        Mr. Leavitt, who moved to Florida in 1984, merged the Fort Lauderdale Symphony Orchestra with the Boca Raton Symphony to create the Philharmonic Orchestra of Florida, which performs in Dade, Broward and Palm Beach counties.
        From 1973 to 1984, he was executive director of the Baltimore Symphony Orchestra. He had previously been executive director and producer of the Wolf Trap Foundation for the Performing Arts in Vienna, Va., from 1970 to 1973.
        Mr. Leavitt was born in Chelsea, Mass. He graduated from the New England Conservatory of Music in 1940 and received a bachelor's degree in radio and television management from American University in 1954. He also studied at Boston University, the Manhattan School of Music and Harvard Business School.
        He is survived by his wife, Sally a son, Howard, of Manhattan; a daughter, Joan, of Buena Park, Calif.; and two brothers, Herbert and Irving, both of Boston.

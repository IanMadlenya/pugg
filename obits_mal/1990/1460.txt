        The Rev. Dr. Nelson Thayer, a professor of psychology and religion at Drew University, died yesterday at his home in Madison, N.J. He was 53 years old.
        He died of kidney cancer, his son, Nelson Jr., said.
        Dr. Thayer, an ordained Episcopal minister, had been at the university for 20 years, teaching mainly graduate students.
        He was a psychologist and marriage counselor, with a private practice in Chatham, N.J. He was also an associate minister at Grace Episcopal Church in Madison and was a founding member of the Grace Counseling Center, where he served as a trustee.
        Dr. Thayer was the author of ''Spirituality and Pastoral Care,'' published in 1985 by Fortress Press.
        He was born in Bryn Mawr, Pa., and graduated from Yale College. He received graduate degrees in divinity from the Union Theological Seminary in Manhattan and a doctorate in religion from the University of Chicago.
        Besides his son, he is survived by his wife, Mai Leung; a daughter, Mai-Ning, of Hong Kong; his mother, Eliza T., of Newtown Square, Pa.; a sister, Marian M. Toland of Malvern, Pa., and a brother, Harry E. T., of Washington.

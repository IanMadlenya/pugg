        Gerhard Wagner, chairman of the Osterreichische Landerbank in Vienna, which ranks 164th among world banks, died Thursday while cycling in a province of lower Austria, where he was on vacation. He was 56 years old and lived in Vienna.
        Mr. Wagner died of a heart attack, a spokesman for the bank said.
        Mr. Wagner had been chairman of the bank since 1984 and a managing board member since 1981. He was also chairman of the Vienna Stock Exchange, a post he had held since 1986, and served on the boards of various Austrial financial and industrial concerns.
        He is survived by his wife, Ingrid, and a daughter, Michaela, both of Vienna.

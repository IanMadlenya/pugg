        Marion Weatherford, an agricultural leader, died Feb. 27 at a hotel in The Dalles, Ore., after a long illness. He was 83 years old and lived in Pendleton.
        In 1947 Mr. Weatherford was one of three wheat growers who led in the formation of the Oregon Wheat Commission, the first such state organization in the nation. He was its chairman from 1953 to 1957. He also helped to form the National Association of Wheat Growers and was a former president of the Oregon Wheat Growers League.
        Mr. Weatherford was a former trustee of the Oregon State University Foundation and received the university's distinguished service award in 1979.
        Survivors include his wife, Leona.

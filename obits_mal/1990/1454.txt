        Edward J. Williams, senior managing director in the government securities department of Bear, Stearns & Company, the investment banking and brokerage firm, was pronounced dead on Wednesday at St. Francis Hospital in Roslyn, L.I. He was 42 years old and lived in Lloyd Harbor, L.I.
        Mr. Williams died while playing tennis, apparently of a heart attack, a spokesman for the family said.
        Mr. Williams joined the company in 1981. From 1973 to 1981, he had been associated with The First Boston Corporation, a Manhattan investment banking and brokerage firm. He began his career as an accountant with Price Waterhouse.
        He was born in Queens and graduated from Fairfield University in 1969.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Margaret Kaminski; two daughters, Christine and Mary,, his mother, Helen Jerge of Delray Beach, Fla. and a sister, Joanne Williams of Bogota, N.J.

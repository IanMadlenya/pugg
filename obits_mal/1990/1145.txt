        J. Walter Yeagley, a former official of the Justice Department and a retired judge of the District of Columbia Court of Appeals, died Saturday at the Hospice of Palm Beach County in West Palm Beach, Fla. He was 81 years old and had lived in Riviera Beach, Fla., since retiring in 1984.
        In 1959 Mr. Yeagley was named Assistant Attorney General and director of the internal security division, which investigated organizations and individuals suspected of Communist affiliations, Mr. Yeagley, a graduate of the University of Michigan, practiced law in South Bend, Ind., before becoming an agent of the Federal Bureau of Investigation. Later he was an administrative aide to its director, J. Edgar Hoover.
        He worked on cases that led to the indictments of Communist leaders on charges of advocating the overthrow of the government. He left the bureau in 1948 and joined the Justice Department in 1953. He became a judge in 1970.
        He is survived by his wife, Gail; three sons, John of McLean, Va., William of Salem, Va., and Richard of Annandale, Va., and two grandchildren.

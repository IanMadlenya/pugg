        Fred W. Lessing, a retired executive and philanthropist, died of a heart attack on Sunday at his home in Scarsdale, N.Y. He was 75 years old.
        Mr. Lessing was the former president and chief executive of the Stewart Stamping Corporation of Yonkers, a subsidiary of the International Silver Company, which he joined after graduating from New York University.
        Mr. Lessing was born in Bamberg, Germany. He went to England in the 30's to study at the London School of Economics and came here in 1942. He worked with the Friends of Tel Aviv University, the Wiener Library at the Institute of Contemporary History in London, the Leo Baeck Institute and the American Federation of Jews from Central Europe in Manhattan.
        Surviving are his wife, Helen; two daughters, Joan, of Manhattan and Jerusalem, and Pamela Friedman of Boulder, Colo., and two grandchildren.

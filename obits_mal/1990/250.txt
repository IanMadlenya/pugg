        Arthur J. O'Brien, former president of the Associated Dry Goods Corporation, died of a heart attack Monday at his home in Jupiter, Fla. He was 80 years old.
        Mr. O'Brien, a native of Quincy, Mass., began his career as a stockboy at R. H. White's in Boston. He gained national recognition among retailers when he led the development of one of the first suburban branches of the Baltimore department store Steward & Company, of which he was president.
        From 1958 to 1967, he was president of Sibley's department store in Rochester, where he established one of the first free-standing discount department stores allied with Associated Dry Goods. He was president of Associated Dry Goods from 1967 to 1972 and took the leadership in promoting the development of branches like Lord & Taylor in New York and Robinsons of California. He retired in 1972.
        Mr. O'Brien is survived by his wife, the former Lya Doucette; a brother, Joseph, and a sister, Phyllis, of Washington; two sons. Marc, of Cambridge, Mass., and Thomas, of Charlotte, Vt.; two daughters, Anne, of Penzance, England, and Margaret, of Washington; two stepsons, Joseph Doucette of Mexico City and Daniel Doucette of Bourne, Mass., and four grandchildren.

        Dr. Gilbert Sherman, a retired dentist and a former dental director of the Nassau County Departments of Health and of Social Welfare, died on Saturday at Morristown Memorial Hospital in Morristown, N.J. He was 83 years old and lived in Madison, N.J., and in Pompano Beach, Fla.
        He died of complications from lymphoma, his family said.
        Dr. Sherman, who was born in Nashwauk, Minn., was a graduate of the McGill University College of Dentistry. His service with the Nassau County agencies began in 1953 and ended with his retirement in 1976.
        He was an early campaigner for fluoridation of water supplies and later worked for adoption of laws barring smoking in public places.
        Dr. Sherman is survived by a daughter, Roberta Thornton of Madison, and a granddaughter.

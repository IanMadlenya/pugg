        Robert Synes, a producer and writer of television game shows, died Sunday at Cedars-Sinai Hospital in Los Angeles. He was 58 years old and lived in Los Angeles.
        Mimi Bookstaver, his sister, said he died of melanoma, a form of skin cancer.
        Mr. Synes, a graduate of Syracuse University, was executive producer of ''Fun House,'' a children's game show. He joined Lorimar Television productions in 1984, supervised ''The All New Let's Make a Deal'' and was co-creator and executive producer of ''The $1,000,000 Chance of a Lifetime.'' Before that, he had been affiliated with a number of audience-participation shows.
        In addition to his sister Mimi, who lives in Haworth, N.J., he is survived by his mother, Anne Synes of Hackensack, N.J., and another sister, Janice Weissman of Manhattan.

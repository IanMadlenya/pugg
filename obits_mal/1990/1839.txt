
       Robert W. Cleveland, a road builder and heavy-equipment supplier, died on Thursday at the United Hospital in Port Chester, N.Y. He was 80 years old and lived in Rye, N.Y. His family said he died of lymphoma. The company that bears his name was the contractor for road work at several major projects in the 1930's and 1940's, including the New York World's Fair of 1939-40 and the Pentagon in World War II.
        Robert W. Cleveland, a road builder and heavy-equipment supplier, died on Thursday at the United Hospital in Port Chester, N.Y. He was 80 years old and lived in Rye, N.Y. His family said he died of lymphoma. The company that bears his name was the contractor for road work at several major projects in the 1930's and 1940's, including the New York World's Fair of 1939-40 and the Pentagon in World War II. Since 1948, Mr. Cleveland has been a dealer for Caterpillar tractors and other equipment, first in Harrisburg, Pa., and later in Armonk, N.Y.
        Mr. Cleveland is survived by his wife, the former Anita Harding; two sons, Robert W. Cleveland Jr. of Rye and C. E. Thomas Cleveland of Briarcliff Manor, N.Y.; a sister, Margaret Barton of West Orange, N.J.; eight grandchildren, and a great-grandson.

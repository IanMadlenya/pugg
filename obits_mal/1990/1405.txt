        William M. Weil, a retired manufacturer of specialized lighting, died on Monday at Lenox Hill Hospital. He was 89 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        He died of renal failure, his wife, the former Marion Lissauer, said.
        Mr. Weil retired in 1960 after 21 years as head of the Radiant Lamp Company in Newark. The company made lighting for stadiums and theaters. Mr. Weil had worked in real estate and foreign bonds. He was a former vice president of the Central Synogogue in Manhattan.
        Also surviving are two sons, Robert, of Pittsburgh, and Richard, of St. Paul; a daughter, Suzanne Ruttenberg of Manhattan; a sister, Rose Weil of Claremont, Calif., and four grandchildren.

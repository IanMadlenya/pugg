        Gary M. Young, the executive director of the Connecticut Commission on the Arts, died Saturday while jogging in Manhattan. He was 48 years old and lived in Hamden, Conn.
        The cause of death has not been announced. Noel Tomas, a spokesman for the Commission on the Arts, said an autopsy was performed, but the results have not been released.
        In the nine years that Mr. Young headed the state arts organization, he helped establish three state-operated galleries, a savings fund for arts organizations and a slide bank for Connecticut artists. He had also been chairman of the National Assembly of State Arts Agencies. He served as treasurer for the New England Foundation for the Arts and on many committees of the National Endowment for the Arts. Mr. Young was executive director of the Oregon Arts Foundation from 1975 to 1981, and he wrote many articles on arts management.
        Mr. Young is survived by his mother, Wanda Lumpkin of Rangley, Colo.; a daughter, Heather E. Young of Eugene, Ore., and two sisters, Janice Karren of Dinosaur, Colo., and Starr Greenwall of Vernal, Utah.

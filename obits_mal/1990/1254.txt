        Robert H. Lurie, a part-owner of two professional sports teams in Chicago, died of cancer Wednesday at Northwest Memorial Hospital, hospital officials said today. He was 48 years old.
        Mr. Lurie, who with his partner Samuel Zell created a real estate and investment empire, owned parts of the Chicago White Sox baseball team and the Chicago Bulls basketball team.
        In 1981, Mr. Lurie and Mr. Zell joined a partnership that took over the White Sox and the Bulls. Mr. Lurie sat on the board of directors of both teams.
        He is survived by his wife, Ann; three sons, Jesse, Andrew and Benjamin; three daughters, Abigail, Sara and Elizabeth; his mother, Sylvia, and two brothers and a sister.

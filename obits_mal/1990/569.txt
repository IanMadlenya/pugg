        George Cooper, deputy chairman of the department of biology at City College of the City University of New York, died of cancer on Thursday at Columbia-Presbyterian Medical Center. He was 61 years old and lived in Orangeburg, N.Y.
        Professor Cooper, whose area of expertise was hematology, joined the biology department in 1962. Besides teaching, he did research on the mechanisms of blood clotting. He became deputy chairman of the department in 1982.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Barbara Dreishpiel; a daughter, Cynthia Gorlick of Forest Hills, Queens; a son, John, of Orangeburg; his father, Henry, of Palisades, N.Y., and a brother, Stanley, of Rye Ridge, N.Y.

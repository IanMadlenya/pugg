        Mel Hosansky, editor in chief of Successful Meetings magazine, died of liver cancer on Wednesday at the Cabrini Hospice in Manhattan. He was 64 years old and lived in Jamaica, Queens.
        Mr. Hosansky joined the magazine in 1987, after 21 years as founding editor and later editor in chief of Meetings and Conventions magazine. He was also a consultant on meetings and a director of the Greater New York chapter of Meeting Planners International.
        Mr. Hosansky was born in Queens. He attended Queens College and received a master's in playwriting at the University of South Carolina.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Anne Lichtman; a daughter, Tamar, of Shirley, Mass., and a son, David, of Jacksonville, Fla.

        Martin Shwartz, a theater press agent for more than 40 years, died of heart failure on Sunday at his home in Los Angeles. He was 66 years old.
        Mr. Shwartz represented more than 90 Broadway shows, including ''My Fair Lady,'' ''Camelot,'' ''Funny Girl,'' ''Member of the Wedding,'' ''The Great White Hope,'' ''Kismet'' and ''I Am a Camera.'' He moved to Los Angeles in 1972, and served as the director of publicity for the Los Angeles Civic Light Opera.
        He is survived by a sister, Norma Sherman, of Newton Highlands, Mass.

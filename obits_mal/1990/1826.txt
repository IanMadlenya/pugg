        The Rev. Dr. Donald R. Heiges, a former president of two Lutheran seminaries, died yesterday at the Lutheran Retirement Village in Gettysburg, Pa. He was 80 years old. A funeral home spokesman said Dr. Heiges died of cardiomyopathy, a disease of the muscular tissue of the heart.
        Dr. Heiges was president of the Lutheran Theological Seminary in Gettysburg from 1962 until his retirement in 1976, and concurrently, from 1964 to 1970, of the Lutheran Theological Seminary in Philadelphia.
        He was ordained in 1935 after graduating from the Gettysburg seminary. He served as a chaplain and assistant professor at Gettysburg College from 1935 to 1944, when he was named campus pastor for the Lutheran Church at colleges and universities in New York.
        Dr. Heiges was the denomination's executive secretary for campus ministry from 1950 to 1958, and concurrently held a similar position with the National Council of Churches. For four years he was dean of the Chicago Lutheran Theological Seminary.
        He is survived by his wife, Mary; two daughters, Carol Sue Reinhardt of Sycamore, Ill., and Joan Blythe of Lexington, Ky., and two grandchildren.

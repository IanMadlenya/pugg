        Herbert E. Baldwin, an apple grower, legislator and first selectman of Westport, Conn., for 10 years, died on Monday in the Carolton Convalescent Hospital in Fairfield, Conn. He was 96 years old and lived in Westport.
        Mr. Baldwin died of respiratory failure, a son-in-law, W. F. Young, said.
        Mr. Baldwin, a Republican, was elected first selectman five times, from 1957 to 1967, when he retired. He was instrumental in acquiring the Longshore Country Club for the town.
        Mr. Baldwin began his orchard in 1919. In 1935, he was elected to the Connecticut House of Representatives, where he served three terms, followed by two terms in the Senate, ending in 1945. He was a founder of the Westport Arts Center in 1969 and was on its board for many years and was its president in 1971 and 1972. He was born in Norwalk, Conn.  Surviving are his wife, the former May Smith; a son, Herbert Jr., of Frankfort, Ind.; two daughters, Martha Ordeman of Dayton, Ohio, and Phyllis Young of Larchmont, N.Y.; nine grandchildren, and eight great-grandchildren.

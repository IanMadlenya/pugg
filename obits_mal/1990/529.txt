        William B. Platt Jr., a Southampton, L.I., Village Attorney for more than 20 years, of heart failure on Friday at Southampton Hospital. He was 84 years old.
        Mr. Platt, a lifelong resident of Southampton, was appointed Village Attorney, the chief counsel for the village, in 1938 and remained in the position until 1961, when he returned to private law practice. He was a member of the Southampton Board of Zoning Appeals from 1937 to 1950, and headedthe board from 1950 to 1953. He drafted Southampton's 1957 zoning ordinance, which became the blueprint for planning in the village, said a son, Jonathan J. Platt.
        From 1940 to 1947, Mr. Platt was the Government appeals agent for the Southampton Draft Board. He was a director of the Southampton Hospital Association from 1948 to 1954 and helped establish its school of nursing, which has since closed.
        In the early 1960's, he donated land for the construction of dormitory space for Southampton College of Long Island University. He taught business law at the college.
        In addition to Jonathan, of Southampton, Mr. Platt is survived by three other sons, William 3d and Harold, also of Southampton, and Richard, of Marlborough, Mass.; three daughters, Meredith P. Joyce of Southampton, Emalie P. Feerick of Mount Kisco, N.Y., and Elinor P. Hoar of Broken Arrow, Okla., and 21 grandchildren.

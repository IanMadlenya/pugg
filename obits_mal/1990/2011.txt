
       Howard Barkell, who retired as president of the Phelps Dodge Copper Refining Company in 1978, died on Aug. 28 at his home in Englewood, Fla. He was 76 years old and had lived in Merrick and Shelter Island, L.I., until a decade ago. His family said he died of heart failure.
        His family said he died of heart failure.
        Mr. Barkell, who was born in Lake Linden, Mich., joined Phelps Dodge in 1936, two years after he received a degree in metallurgical engineering from Michigan Technical College. He became a plant manager for Phelps Dodge in 1948, vice president in 1952 and president in 1967.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Olive Benson; a son, Gordon, of Babylon, L.I.; a daughter, Linda Dunstan of Albuquerque, N.M.; four grandchildren, and two great-grandchildren.

        Joe McDonald Ingraham, a Federal judge for 36 years, died of complications from heart disease on Sunday at Memorial Southwest Hospital. He was 86 years old and lived in Houston.
        Mr. Ingraham was a Federal judge for the Southern District of Texas from 1954 to 1969 and joined the United States Court of Appeals for the Fifth Circuit in 1969.
        In 1967 he presided at the draft-evasion trial of Mohammed Ali, the heavyweight boxing champion then also known as Cassius Clay. Mr. Ali was convicted and sentenced to jail, but the conviction was overturned by the United States Supreme Court.
        Judge Ingraham graduated from the National University Law School in Washington in 1927 and practiced law in Stroud, Okla., Fort Worth and Houston. In World War II he served in the Army Air Corps.
        In the 1950's, he was elected Republican Party chairman for Harris County, which includes Houston.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Laura Munson.

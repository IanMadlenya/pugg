        Dr. William Peter McEwen, an educator and former provost of Hofstra University, died of cancer on Wednesday at the Berkshire (Mass.) Medical Center. He was 77 years old and lived in Becket, Mass.
        Dr. McEwen, who helped build Hofstra into a nationally recognized university, served as provost and dean of the faculty, the second-highest position at the university, from 1966 until 1974.
        He joined the faculty in 1946 after teaching at Boston University and serving as a director of the American Red Cross in the Middle East and North Africa. He was chairman of the departments of philosophy and social sciences, and was later named dean of the faculty at Hofstra. He retired in 1978.
        He is survived by his wife, Marjorie; two daughters, Susan, of Becket, and Priscilla, of Lynn, Mass.; two sons, Peter, of Boston, and William, of Peru, Mass.; a brother, John, of Indianapolis, and three grandchildren.

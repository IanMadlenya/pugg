        J. Ernest Wharton, a lawyer and a former Republican Congressman from upstate New York for eight terms, died of a stroke on Friday at his home in Summit, N.Y. He was 90 years old.
        Mr. Wharton was elected to the House in 1950. The district included Columbia, Greene, Schoharie and Ulster Counties. In 1960, he beat Gore Vidal, the playwright. Mr. Wharton lost his race for re-election in 1966.
        Mr. Wharton was Schoharie County District Attorney from 1932 to 1941 and was later a county judge, also for nine years.
        He graduated in 1919 from the Albany Law School. Mr. Wharton then worked for the Travelers Insurance Company and was in private practice.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Marion Turner; a daughter, Beverly Radez of Summit; a sister, Lucy Addis of Cobleskill, N.Y.; two grandchildren, and seven great-grandchildren.

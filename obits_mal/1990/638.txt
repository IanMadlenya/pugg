        Charles Harman Foster, a former vice chancellor of the State University of New York, died of leukemia on Sunday at St. Peter's Hospice in Albany. He was 77 years old and lived in Delmar, N.Y.
        Mr. Foster joined the State University at its inception in 1949 and was one of the founders of the Research Foundation there, which dispenses grants to colleges in the state university system. He served as vice chancellor for business affairs from 1964 until his retirement in 1971.
        Mr. Foster had also worked for New York State's Division of the Budget. He had been an official of the American Red Cross and president of the Civil Service Employees Association Retirees.
        He is survived by his wife, Margaret; three daughters, Cynthia Koss, of State College, Pa., Deborah Deasy, of Avon Lake, Ohio, and Jonatha Carroll, of Delmar; three sons, James and Charles, both of Saratoga Springs, N.Y., and Mark, of Delmar; two brothers, Ralph, of Dallas, and Francis, of Pittsford, N.Y.; a sister, Florence Carpenter, of Canandaigua, N.Y., and 12 grandchildren.

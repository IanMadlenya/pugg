        Monroe Oppenheimer, a lawyer in the New Deal Administration of Franklin D. Roosevelt, died Tuesday at George Washington University Hospital in Washington. He was 86 years old and lived in Washington.
        His daughter, Judith, said he died of cancer.
        Mr. Oppenheimer graduated from Yale University Law School and was in private practice in St. Louis before joining the Roosevelt Administration in 1933. He served as chief attorney for the Agricultural Adjustment Administration, as general counsel of the Resettlement Administration, as head attorney for the Agricultural Department's solicitor's office, and as general counsel of the Board of Economic Warfare.
        He is survived by his wife, Elena; his daughter, Judith Loth, and a son, Mark, all of Washington.

        Clyde H. Allison Jr., a former special counsel to the Congressional subcommittee investigating allegations of mismanagement at the Department of Housing and Urban Development during the Reagan Administration, died on Wednesday at his home in Manhattan. He was 31 years old. Mr. Allision died in his sleep, apparently of a stroke, said Martin London, a partner in the Manhattan firm of Paul, Weiss, Rifkind, Wharton & Garrison, where Mr. Allison was an associate.
        Last November Mr. Allison took a five-month leave from the firm to work on the H.U.D. investigation in Washington.
        Mr. Allison was born in Neshanic Station, N.J., and studied for the ministry at the Bible Baptist College East Seminary. He was a graduate of Rutgers College and Harvard Law School.
        At his death, Mr. Allison was engaged to be married to Lauren Silverman in September.
        He is survived by his parents, Clyde H. and Shirley Allison of Neshanic Station, and two brothers, Douglas, of Plainfield, N.J., and Kenneth, of Dunellen, N.J.

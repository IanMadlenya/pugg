        Oscar J. Gass, a retired economic consultant, died of respiratory and circulatory collapse Monday at Suburban Hospital in Bethesda, Md. He was 76 years old and lived in Washington.
        Mr. Gass moved to Washington in 1933 from Portland, Ore., his birthplace, to work as an economist for the Treasury Department. He joined the War Production Board in 1942, serving until 1945.
        He went on to become an independent economic consultant and, in 1948, became economic adviser to the new state of Israel, a post he held until 1955.
        Mr. Gass was also a consultant to the Governments of Indonesia and Poland and to various Federal departments and Congressional committees. He had been a director for the Bank Leumi and was a trustee of Reed College. He retired in 1987.
        He is survived by a brother, David, of Chicago, and two sisters, Lillian Menashe, of Chicago, and Frieda Cohen, of Portland.

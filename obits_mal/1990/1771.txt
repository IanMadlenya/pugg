        Dr. John Honan Keating Jr., a retired specialist in internal medicine and cardiology, died on Thursday at the Riverview Medical Center in Red Bank, N.J. He was 70 years old and lived in Locust, N.J.
        He died of lung cancer, his stepson John White, said.
        Dr. Keating and his father, Dr. John H. Keating, who died in 1963, had a private practice in Manhattan in the late 1940's. The younger Dr. Keating was an attending physician at St. Luke's-Roosevelt Medical Center and an assistant clinical professor of medicine at the College of Physicians and Surgeons at Columbia University until he retired in 1978.
        Dr. Keating graduated from Columbia University in 1941 and from the College of Physicians and Surgeons in 1943.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Margaret White; three daughters and a son from a former marriage, Joan Tarsy of Colts Neck, N.J., Barbra Braun of Houston, Sarah Homann of North Lauderdale, Fla., and John H. 3d, of Little Silver, N.J.; two stepsons, Dr. Richard White Jr., of Arlington, Va., and John White of Boston; two brothers, Dr. Richard, of North Truro, Mass., and Dr. Paul, of Upper Saddle River, N.J.; 14 grandchildren, and one great-grandchild.

        Louis Freddie Kohlman, a jazz drummer, died of cancer on Saturday at his home in New Orleans. He was 75 years old.
        Mr. Kohlman began playing professionally as a teen-ager when he appeared with such musicians as Oscar (Papa) Celestin and Joseph Robichaux.
        A native of New Orleans, he moved to Chicago in 1956, returning to his hometown 11 years later to perform, among other places, at the New Orleans Jazz and Heritage Festival.
        As a member of the Preservation Hall Jazz Band, Mr. Kohlman traveled widely in the United States and overseas.
        He appeared in several movies, including ''Pete Kelly's Blues,'' ''Pretty Baby'' and ''Angel Heart.''

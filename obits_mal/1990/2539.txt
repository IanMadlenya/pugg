
        Earl A. Verpillot, a former executive vice president of the Wigton-Abbott Corporation, an architectural, engineering and construction concern. died on Saturday at his home in the Rossmoor retirement community in Jamesburg, N.J. He was 90 years old.
        Mr. Verpillot died of cancer, the A. S. Cole Funeral Home said.
        He was a resident of Staten Island for many years before he moved to Katonah, N.Y., in 1962 and to Rossmoor in 1973.
        He was a native New Yorker and a graduate of the Pratt Institute. Mr. Verpillot, who was commissioned a lieutenant in the Navy's Civil Engineer Corps in 1933, was in charge of construction of the naval shipyard at Bayonne, N.J., from 1940 to 1946.
        He later joined New York City's Department of Marine and Aviation where he was responsible for developing the Staten Island Ferry Terminal and Pier 57 on the Hudson River. He was deputy chief engineer of marine and aviation when he retired in 1952 and joined Wigton-Abbott.
        Mr. Verpillot's first wife, the former Marjorie Whitney, died in 1969. He is survived by his second wife, the former Marion Hey; a son, Paul, of Marietta, Ga.; a stepdaughter, Constance Norton of Bishop, Calif., and a stepson, Donald Hey of San Pedro, Calif., and two granddaughters.
        Correction:  November 14, 1990, Tuesday, Late Edition - Final
        An obituary yesterday about a former New York City engineering executive rendered his first name incorrectly. He was Emil A. Verpillot, not Earl.

        Perry Kimball Sellon, a business editor for U.S. News and World Report for 21 years, died of congestive heart failure on Monday at his home in Chevy Chase, Md. He was 78 years old.
        Mr. Sellon, a 1934 graduate of Princeton University, was with the research department of the Federal Reserve Bank of New York before moving to Washington, where he edited a magazine published by the War Production Board in World War II.
        He worked for the Foreign Economic Administration and the State Department before joining the news magazine, then called World Report, in 1946. Until 1967 he was the editor of its ''Business Around the World'' column. He worked for the World Bank before retiring in 1969.
        He is survived by his wife, Louise; a son, Charles, of Potomac, Md.; a daughter, Louise, of Manhattan, and three grandchildren.

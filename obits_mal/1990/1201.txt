        John Edison Sloane, an investment counselor and a grandson of Thomas A. Edison, died on Thursday at his home in Summit, N.J., at the age of 72. He died of multiple strokes, the Brough Funeral Home said.
        Mr. Sloane had worked for Thomas A. Edison Inc. before joining the investment-counseling business of Trainer, Wortham & Company in New York in 1958. He later became a partner in the counseling company of John B. Braine Inc., before forming his own firm, John Edison Sloane Inc., from which he retired in 1985.
        He was a board member and later executive director of the Edison Birthplace Association in Milan, Ohio. In World War II he served with the 102d Cavalry in England, Africa and Italy, leaving as a captain.
        Mr. Sloane was born in West Orange, N.J. He was an alumnus of Yale.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Jule Day; a daughter, Madeleine, of Nicasio, Calif.; two sons, J. Haywood, of Rose Valley, Pa., and C. Barry, of Mount Royal, N.J.; two brothers, Thomas, of Branford, Conn., and Peter, of Mashpee, Mass., and a granddaughter.

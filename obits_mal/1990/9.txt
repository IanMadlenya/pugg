        Dr. Jerome I. Sashin, a psychiatrist who was chairman of the research committee of the Psychoanalytic Institute of New England East in Needham, Mass., died of lymphoma on Jan. 5 at Massachusetts General Hospital in Boston. He was 49 years old and lived in Boston.
        Dr. Sashin taught at Harvard and Tufts Medical Schools and was on the faculty of the Psychoanalytic Institute since its inception in 1975. From 1983 to 1985 he was a visiting professor at the Institut des Hautes Etudes Scientifiques in Paris.
        Born in Manhattan, Dr. Sashin was a graduate of the Massachusetts Institute of Technology and the New York University School of Medicine.
        He is survivied by his wife, the former Bonnie Wolinsky; a daughter, Daphne, of Boston; his mother, Pearl, of Manhattan; a brother, Donald, of Pittsburgh, and a sister, Helene, of Manhattan.

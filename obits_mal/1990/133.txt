        Morris M. Mintz, a lawyer and former New York State Assemblyman, died of Alzheimer's disease Wednesday at the Liberty Hill Nursing Home in Delray Beach, Fla. He was 81 years old and lived in Delray Beach.
        A 1933 graduate of Brooklyn Law School, Mr. Mintz served in the Legislature in 1941 and 1942 as a Democratic Assemblyman from Manhattan. Early in his career, he was a director and counsel to the Ninth Street Day Nursery and Orphan's Home in Manhattan. After his legislative service, he was in private law practice in Manhattan until 1977, when he retired.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Ruth Klinghoffer; a daughter, Rayna Ragonetti of Manhattan; a son, Peter, of Santa Barbara, Calif.; a brother, Elias, of Lauderhill, Fla., and three grandchildren.

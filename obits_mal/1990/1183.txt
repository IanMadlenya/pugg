        Harold C. Roser Jr., retired manager of community development programs for the Exxon Corporation, died on Wednesday at New York Hospital. He was 69 years old and lived in Manhattan and Southampton, L.I.
        Mr. Roser died of heart failure, his wife, said.
        Mr. Roser joined Exxon in 1953 and was vice president of Exxon Enterprises, an affiliate, from 1967 to 1971. In 1971 he joined the corporation's public affairs department where he was influential in developing a community relations program that included philanthropy. He resigned in 1982. Earlier he had spent 10 years in the United States Foreign Service in Europe and Asia.
        Besides his wife, the former Cecelia Jue, Mr. Roser is survived by a son, Lorin, of Manhattan.

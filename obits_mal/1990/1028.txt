        J. Jacob Hahn, a retired bankruptcy lawyer, died of congestive heart failure on Tuesday at Good Samaritan Hospital in Palm Beach, Fla. He was 87 years old and lived in Manhattan and in Palm Beach.
        Mr. Hahn was the founding partner of Hahn & Abeson in Manhattan, now Hahn & Hessen, where he specialized in bankruptcy law. He was a founder of the Association of Commercial Finance Attorneys, and a founder and president from 1965 to 1967 of the Lawyers Association of the Textile Industry. He retired in 1980.
        Mr. Hahn came to New York City from Germany as a child, attended City College and graduated from New York Law School in 1924.
        He is survived by his wife, Dorothy Weidner; two sons, George, of Scarsdale, N.Y., and James, of Manhattan; two daughters, Babette Rakosky of Dallas, and Norma Weiner of Clearwater, Fla.; nine grandchildren and six great-grandchildren.

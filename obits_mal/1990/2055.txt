        Frank David Kitchin, the director of the department of Occupational Health at New York Hospital, died on Monday at his home in Manhattan. He was 66 years old.
        He died of a heart attack, his former wife, Dr. Gail Arnold, said.
        Dr. Kitchin, a native of Nottingham, England, received his undergraduate and medical training at the University of Liverpool. In 1963 he joined Rockefeller University as an associate professor in the department of human genetics.
        In 1967 he became an associate professor in clinical medicine at Columbia University's College of Physicians and Surgeons. He joined Cornell University Medical College and the New York Hospital in 1979.
        Dr. Kitchin is survived by a daughter, Jennifer, of Manhattan; a son, Jonathan, of Greenwich, Conn.; a sister, Margaret Sutton of Birkenhead, England, and a brother, Richard, of Dereham, England.

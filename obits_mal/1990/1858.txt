        Stanley R. Kay, an authority on schizophrenia, died on Monday in Kyoto, Japan where he was attending a medical conference. He was 44 years old and lived in Mahopac, N.Y.
        He died of a heart attack, his wife, Theresa, said.
        A 1980 Ph.D. graduate of the State University of New York at Stony Brook, L.I., Dr. Kay was chief of schizophrenia research in the department of psychiatry at the Albert Einstein College of Medicine-Montefiore Medical Center in the Bronx. He had joined the staff there in 1980 as a clinical instructor. Since May 1987, he had also been director of psychological research at the Bronx Psychiatric Center.
        Dr. Kay was known for his research into the symptoms of schizophrenia. He also helped develop a diagnostic test to differentiate schizophrenia from other disorders and created a scale to delineate the types of schizophrenia.
        Besides his wife, he is survived by two daughters, Lisa and Stacy; his father, Leslie Kay of Natanya, Israel, and a brother, Kenneth, also of Natanya.

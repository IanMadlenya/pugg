        John J. Snodgrass, founding director of the Division of Radiology at North Shore University Hospital in Manhasset, L.I., died at the hospital on Monday. He was 70 years old and lived in Manhasset.
        Dr. Snodgrass suffered a stroke on Aug. 12, his wife, Lette, said.
        He was associated with North Shore from its beginning in 1953 until 1968 and was chairman of its medical board from 1962 to 1963 and from 1965 to 1966. He was in private practice from 1968 until his retirement in 1987.  From 1949 to 1953, he was a resident and attending radiologist at New York Hospital. He was also a fellow of the American College of Radiology.
        Dr. Snodgrass was born in Ceylon, now Sri Lanka, where his father was a missionary doctor. He was a graduate of Colgate University in 1942 and Cornell Medical College in 1945.
        In World War II he was a lieutenant in the Naval Reserve Medical Corps.
        Besides his his wife, the former Lette McGreer; he is survived by a daughter, Jamie Higgins of Medford, Mass.; three sons, John, of Port Washington, L.I., Jim, of Los Angeles, and Joel, of Plandome, L.I.; a sister, Barbara, of Bronxville, N.Y., and five grandchildren.

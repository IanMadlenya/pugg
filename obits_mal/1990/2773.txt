
        Laurence B. Henriques, a former stockbroker, hospital official and sales executive, died on Tuesday at the Pomperaug Woods Nursing Center in Southbury, Conn. He was 84 years old and had lived in Southbury and Larchmont, N.Y.
        He died of bone cancer, a spokeswoman for the family said.
        Mr. Henriques joined Kidder, Peabody & Co. in 1921 and became a registered broker at Kidder in 1925 and an assistant branch manager in 1932.
        In 1944 he became assistant secretary and treasurer at Columbia Presbyterian Hospital in Manhattan. In 1954 he became vice president and secretary of J. B. Henriques Inc., a sales company that was the American distributor for the plastics division of Imperial Chemical Industries Ltd., the British-based chemical company. He retired in 1971.
        Mr. Henriques's wife, the former Carlota Emerson, died in 1988. He is survived by two daughters, Noel H. Brakenhoff of Jamestown, R.I., and Carlota H. Welty, of Wyckoff, N.J.; a son, Laurence Jr., of Hoboken, N.J; two brothers, J. B. Henriques of Delray Beach, Fla., and Harry P. Jr., of New Canaan, Conn.; five grandchildren and one great-grandchild.

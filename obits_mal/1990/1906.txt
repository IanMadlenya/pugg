        Peter Oldenburg, a book and graphic designer long associated with the Metropolitan Museum of Art, died on Wednesday at his home in Ossining, N.Y. He was 74 years old. He died of heart failure, his family said. Since 1959, Mr. Oldenburg designed the museum's annual engagement calendar. He also designed hundreds of books and catalogues, and the Metropolitan Museum of Art Bulletin and its Journal.
        Among his book designs were ''Prints and People: A Social History of Printed Pictures'' and ''A Walk Through the Cloisters.'' In 1985, the museum held a retrospective exhibit of his book and graphic designs.
        Mr. Oldenburg, a native of Hamburg, Germany, came to the United States by way of England and served in the United States Navy in World War II.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Hilde Kantorowicz; a son, Philip, of Manhattan; a daughter, Julia, of Toronto; two sisters, Rugh Tiranoff of Clinton, Md., and Nan Black of Mill Valley, Calif., and two grandchildren.

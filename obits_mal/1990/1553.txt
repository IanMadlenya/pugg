        Hans-Rudolf Kurz, a Swiss military historian who headed an international inquiry into the World War II record of President Kurt Waldheim of Austria, died on Friday. He was 75 years old. The cause of death was not disclosed. Mr. Kurz headed a commission of five historians that the Austrian Government named in 1987 to investigate accusations that Mr. Waldheim had links to Nazi war atrocities.
        The panel's report found no evidence that Mr. Waldheim was directly involved in war crimes, though Mr. Kurz publicly called the findings ''not encouraging'' for the Austrian President.
        Mr. Kurz was an adviser to five Defense Ministers over a 40-year Government career and was a colonel in the Swiss Army.

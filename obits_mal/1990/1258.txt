        Frank Hinchman Platt 3d, a retired lawyer and conservationist died at Greenwich (Conn.) Hospital on Wednesday. He died after a brief illness, his wife, Audrey said. He was 77 years old.
        Mr. Platt was a former partner in the New York City law firm of Bleakley & Platt. A native of New York City, Mr. Platt earned his bachelor's degree from Yale in 1935 and his law degree from the University of Virginia in 1939.
        A resident of Greenwich since 1946, Mr. Platt served as president of the Greenwich Library board in 1968 and 1969 and as president of the Greenwich Hospital board between 1971 and 1973.
        He was chairman of the Little Cumberland Island Association in Georgia, which has maintained a sea turtle breeding ground for 27 years.
        Besides his wife, Mr. Platt is survived by a daughter, Deborah Sterling of Greenwich; two sons, Richard, of Grosse Pointe, Mich., and Jeremy, of Palo Alto, Calif.; two brothers, William, of Hobe Sound, Fla., and Livingston, of Essex, Conn.; a sister, Priscilla Hooke of Rochester, and seven grandchildren.

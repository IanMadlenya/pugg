        Henry L. Duncombe Jr., a retired vice president of General Motors, died on Aug. 18 at Baptist Memorial Hospital in Miami. He was 76 years old and had homes in Key Biscayne, Fla., and Southampton, L.I.
        He died of a heart attack, a General Motors spokesman said.
        Dr. Duncombe, a 1948 graduate of Northwestern University with a Ph.D. in economics, was an assistant dean and a professor at the Amos Tuck School of Business Administration at Dartmouth College before joining General Motors in 1957.
        He was named director of economic studies at G.M. in 1968, and in 1972 was named chief economist, retiring in 1979. He served as chairman of the economic research committees of both the Motor Vehicle Manufacturers Association and of the Business Roundtable and was a trustee of the International Chamber of Commerce.
        He is survived by his wife, Lee; four daughters, Patricia, of Bayside, Queens, Nancy Eder of Glen Head, L.I., Sue Kehl of Sparta, N.J., and Beth Nehls of Bellerose Village, L.I., and seven grandchildren.

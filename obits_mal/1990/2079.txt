        William Henry Harrison, a five-term Republican Congressman who was a descendant of two Presidents, died of heart failure Monday at Palms of Pasadena Hospital in St. Petersburg, Fla. He was 94 years old.  Mr. Harrison was first elected to the House of Representatives in 1950. He served there through 1954, when he lost a race for the Senate, and again from 1957 to 1961 and 1963 to 1965.
        He was a grandson of President Benjamin Harrison and a great-great grandson of President William Henry Harrison.
        Survivors included his second wife, Dorothy; a daughter, Maribeth Rose of Scottsbluff, Neb., and a son, William Jr. of Sheridan.

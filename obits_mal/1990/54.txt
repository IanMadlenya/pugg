        Mel Most, a former foreign correspondent and public-relations agent, died of a heart attack on Jan. 6 at Cabrini Hospital. He was 75 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Most was a correspondent in Vichy, France, in World War II and was interned by the Germans at Baden-Baden in 1944 before being repatriated.
        Later he was an associate editor of Barron's, the financial journal, and a reporter for The Record in New Jersey. He worked in public relations for the Puerto Rican rum industry and for the Government of Puerto Rico.
        Mr. Most was born in Manhattan and attended Columbia University.
        Surviving are a daughter, Mary Salerno of Manhattan; a brother, Amicus, of Los Angeles, and a sister, Linda Schneider of Washington.

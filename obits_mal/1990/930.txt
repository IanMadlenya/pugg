        Dr. Armond Vincent Mascia, a physician who specialized in children's allergies, died of cancer on Saturday at his home in Tarrytown, N.Y. He was 69 years old.
        After graduating from the New York University College of Medicine in 1945, he took additional training as a pediatric allergist and was an attending physician at Phelps Memorial Hospital in Tarrytown, New York Hospital, Blythedale Children's Hospital in Valhalla and the Westchester County Medical Center, also in Valhalla.
        He was a director of the Westchester County Medical Society and had been president of the Westchester Allergy Society and the Association for the Care of Asthma. He taught pediatrics at Cornell University, Columbia University College of Physicians and Surgeons, and New York Medical College.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Elizabeth Scarborough; five daughters, Dr. Mary Ella Pierpont of St. Paul, Melissa Verdi of North Tarrytown, and Margaret Mascia, Etta Biloon and Sara Mascia, all of Tarrytown; two sons, Alfred and Nicolas, both of Tarrytown; a sister, Yola Melchiorre of Hartsdale, N.Y., and seven grandchildren.

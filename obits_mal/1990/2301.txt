        Arnold Olsen, a former Congressman who was one of Montana's most durable Democratic politicians, died at a nursing home in Helena, Mont., on Tuesday after a long illness. He had been comatose since July. He was 73 years old.
        In a political career that spanned more than 40 years, Mr. Olsen served two terms as Montana Attorney General, five terms in the House of Representatives and 15 years as a district judge in Butte. He was also a candidate for governor and for the Montana Supreme Court.
        He is survived by three daughters and four grandchildren.

        LeRoy Makepeace, a career Foreign Service officer, died Friday at Martha's Vineyard Hospital in Massachusetts. He was 75 years old and lived in Washington.
        He died of heart failure after an asthma attack, a spokeswoman for the family said.
        Mr. Makepeace joined the State Department in 1942 after graduating from Yale University and earning advanced degrees from the University of California at Berkeley and Harvard University. He served in Spain, South America, West Germany, Pakistan and India before retiring in 1973.
        He then became an adviser on south Asian affairs at the Smithsonian Institution, a post he held until 1983.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Lindsay Harper; a daughter, Anne-Lindsay of Manhattan; two sons, Timothy of Washington and Peter of San Francisco; a brother, John, of Waterbury, Conn, and two sisters, Melicent Makepeace of Woodbury, Conn., and Evelyn Cochrane of Darien, Conn.

        John B. Goodwin Sr., an executive recruiter for financial institutions, died yesterday at Stamford Hospital in Connecticut. He was 85 years old and lived in Darien, Conn.
        He died of a stroke, his wife, the former Mary Ward Ford, said.
        Mr. Goodwin, a graduate of Princeton, retired in 1983 as a partner in the recruiting concern of Korn Ferry International. He was a former vice president with Russell Reynolds Associates and a vice president at J. P. Morgan and at the Chase Manhattan Bank. He had also been a director of the Development Credit Corporation, a subsidiary of the American Motors Corporation.
        Besides his wife, he is survived by a daughter, Lynne G. Langmaid of Vail, Colo.; a son, John Jr., of Darien, and five grandchildren.

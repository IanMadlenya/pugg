        Abraham Hershson, a retired manufacturing company executive and a philanthropist, died of a heart attack on Thursday at Good Samaritan Hospital in Palm Beach, Fla. He was 89 years old and lived in Jamaica Estates, Queens.
        Mr. Hershson was the founder and former chairman of Hygiene Industries, a manufacturer of shower curtains and bath accessories with factories in five states. He was born in Krasnik, Poland, in 1900 and came to the United States when he was 19. He founded his company in 1933.
        His philanthropic actvities included establishing a community center in Tel Aviv in 1945 and creating a fund to help Polish emigres in Israel. He supported Brookdale Hospital Medical Center in Brooklyn, the Hillcrest Jewish Center in Queens, the United Jewish Appeal and many other charities.
        He is survived by his wife, Evelyn; two daughters, Harriette Levine of Manhattan and Patti Gerber of Lake Forest, Ill; a son, Stephen, of Cambridge, Vt., and four grandchildren.

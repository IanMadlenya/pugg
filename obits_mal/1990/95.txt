        Alan K. Magary, a former executive of Seth Thomas, the clockmaker, died on Saturday at his home in Woodbury, Conn., after a long illness. He was 78 years old.
        Mr. Magary worked for the Elgin Watch Company as European representative and director of sales research from 1933 to 1957. In World War II he served in the Marine Corps in France, and won a Bronze Star.
        After leaving Elgin, he joined the General Time Corporation, working at its Seth Thomas subsidiary as head of the watch division and then as general sales manager.
        Mr. Magary is survived by his wife, Joan G.; two sons, Alan B., of Wayzata, Minn., and Douglas K., of Dallas; two sisters, Virginia Benson of Pompano Beach, Fla., and Louise Magary of Harwich, Mass.; two stepsons, R. David Weigand of Redmond, Wash., and Peter W. Weigand of Granada Hills, Calif., and seven grandchildren.

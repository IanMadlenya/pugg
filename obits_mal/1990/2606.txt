
        Karl Bargen, a freelance violist who played with the Orchestra of St. Luke's and the Brooklyn Philharmonic, died on Dec. 8 at Cabrini Medical Center. He was 38 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        He died of AIDS, said his mother, Anne Bargen.
        Mr. Bargen performed with many local orchestras and chamber groups. He also played in the Orpheus Chamber Orchestra, and he was the principal violist with the Riverside Symphony and a co-principal of the Philharmonia Virtuosi of New York. He played solo viola for several Broadway productions, including "Jerome Robbins's Broadway" and Stephen Sondheim's "Into the Woods."
        A frequent chamber performer, Mr. Bargen was a member of several ensembles, including the Composers String Quartet, Parnassus, and Steve Reich and Musicians.
        Born in Liberal, Kan., Mr. Bargen graduated from Drake University in Iowa in 1974. Before moving to New York City, he was principal violist of the Des Moines Symphony, the Toledo Symphony in Ohio and the Hong Kong Philharmonic.
        Besides his mother, he is survived by a brother, Mark, of Boston.

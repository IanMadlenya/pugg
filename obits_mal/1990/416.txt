        Adm. Eugenio Henke, who led the Italian secret service in the late 1960's when its agents were linked to right-wing terrorism and coup plots, died of a heart attack on Sunday. He was 81 years old.
        Admiral Henke led the secret service from 1966 to 1970 and served as military Chief of Staff from 1972 to 1975. He was called as a witness in a number of parliamentary and judicial investigations and always denied knowledge of any illegal activity by his agents.

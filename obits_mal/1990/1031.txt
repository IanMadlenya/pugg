        Mark Lewis, a lawyer and a founder of the Liberal Party, died of congestive heart failure on Tuesday at Holy Cross Hospital in Silver Spring, Md. He was 93 years old and lived in Silver Spring.
        Mr. Lewis began his career as a Socialist and later became a conservative. He was a tax collector in New York City under Mayor Fiorello H. La Guardia in the 30's.
        He was a founder of the Liberal Party in 1943. In the 40's he was executive secretary and treasurer of the United Hatters Cap and Millinery Union in Manhattan, from which he retired in the early 50's. Mr. Lewis was born in Brooklyn.  Surviving are two sons, Glenn, of Macon, Ga., and Lionel, of Silver Spring; five grandchildren, and nine great-grandchildren.
        Correction:  May 25, 1990, Friday, Late Edition - Final
        An obituary yesterday about a lawyer who was a founder of the New York Liberal Party rendered his name incorrectly. He was Marx Lewis.

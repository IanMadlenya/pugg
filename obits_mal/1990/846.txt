        Daniel L. Dyer, a sugar broker, died of a heart attack on Sunday while playing paddle tennis in Bronxville, N.Y. He was 73 years old and lived in Bronxville.
        Mr. Dyer was senior general partner of B. W. Dyer & Company, his family's sugar and sweetener brokerage concern in Manhattan.
        A 1939 graduate of Dartmouth College, Mr. Dyer joined the company as a salesman and later became manager of the export sugar department and then a partner. He had been a member of the New York Coffee and Sugar Exchange and of the Sugar Club of New York and the president of the National Sugar Brokers Association.
        He is survived by his wife, Yvonne; two daughters, Suzanne, of Manhattan, and Diana Watson of Larchmont, N.Y.; a son, Daniel, of Chatham, N.J., and four grandchildren.

        Rossell Hope Robbins, retired International Professor of English at the State University of New York at Albany, died of a heart attack on Monday at his home in Saugerties, N.Y. He was 77 years old.
        Professor Robbins joined SUNY in 1969. He also taught as a visiting professor at a number of schools in the United States and Canada. He retired in 1982.
        In 1987, he presented his library of medieval literature to the University of Rochester.
        Professor Robbins was born in Wallasey, Merseyside, England. He was a graduate of the University of Liverpool. In 1937, he received a Ph.D. from Cambridge University, after which he won a Commonwealth Fellowship enabling him to come to the United States to research medieval English.
        He and Dr. Carleton Brown, an American medievalist, wrote ''The Index of Middle English Verse,'' ( Columbia University Press, 1943). He also wrote ''The Encyclopedia of Witchcraft and Demonology'' (Crown Publishers, 1959), as well as other works.
        He served in the army in World War II and became a United States citizen in 1944.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Helen Ann Mins.

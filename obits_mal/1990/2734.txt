
        Jacques Schurre, a retired trial lawyer for the Federal Government and a dealer in Early American prints, died on Wednesday at Beth Israel Hospital in Manhattan. He was 88 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        He died of a stroke, his family said.
        Mr. Schurre, who was born in Lithuania and raised in Paris and New York, was a graduate of Brooklyn Law School. After practicing law for several years in Queens, he joined the legal staff of the United States Labor Department. He also served with the Office of Price Administration in Alaska and the National Labor Relations Board in New York. He retired in 1967.
        He was the author of "The Revised Currier & Ives Prints Checklist," a sourcebook for museum libraries.
        Mr. Schurre is survived by his wife, the former Gila Gordon, and a sister, Mary Kavitt of Brooklyn.

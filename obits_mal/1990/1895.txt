        Ivan Hill, an advertising executive and founder of the Ethics Resource Center in Washington, died Tuesday at the Northern Illinois Medical Center in McHenry, Ill. He was 83 years old and lived in McLean, Va. A spokesman for the center said he died of cancer.
        A graduate of the University of California at Los Angeles, Mr. Hill worked in advertising and television in Chicago.
        He is survived by two daughters, Lisa Fenning of Los Angeles and Nina Hill of Manhattan; two sons, Barrington of London and C. Theodore of Crystal Lake, Ill., and 10 grandchildren.

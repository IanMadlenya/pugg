
        The Rev. Walter J. Benedict, a retired Methodist minister, died on June 15 at his home in Venice, Fla. He was 88 years old.
        He died of cancer, a spokesman for the family said.
        He began his career as an associate minister of the Madison Avenue Methodist Church, now Christ Church, Methodist, in Manhattan in 1929 and remained there until 1933. He then held positions at churches in New York State and in 1962 became superintendent of Brooklyn South and Long Island West District of the New York Conference of the United Methodist Church, a post he held until 1968 when he became pastor of First United Methodist Church of Mount Vernon, N.Y. He retired in 1971.
        He graduated from the New York State College of Forestry at Syracuse University and Drew Theological Seminary in Madison, N.J. He was a trustee of Green Mountain College in Putney, Vt., and of Syracuse University. He was also a member of the board of the Methodist Hospital in Brooklyn.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Margaret Speare; a son, David, of Kinnelon, N.J.; two daughters, Dr. Barbara Bunker of Buffalo and Patricia Hawkins of Denver; a sister, Florence Frette of East Hartford, Conn.; two brothers, Frank, of Colorado Springs and Mark, of Winter Park, Fla., and four grandchildren.

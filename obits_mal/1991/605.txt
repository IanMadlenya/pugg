
        Jacob Myer Geist, a chemical engineer who was an international authority on the liquefaction and separation of gases, died Friday at his home in Allentown, Pa. He was 70 years old.
        He died of cancer, said his wife, the former Sandra Levy.
        Since 1982, Mr. Geist was president of Gest Tec, an engineering consulting company that specialized in safety, cryogenics, membrane technology, separation processes and energy efficiency.
        Before that he was chief engineer with Air Products and Chemicals Inc., in Allentown. He joined the company in 1955 after teaching at universities in the United States and Israel.
        He was a lieutenant in the Army's Chemical Warfare Service in World War II. He received his Ph.D. in 1951 from the University of Michigan.
        In addition to his wife, he is survived by two sons, Eric of Arhus, Denmark, and David of Friday Harbor, Wash.; a daughter, Ellen Geist of Brooklyn; a stepson, David Weinstein of Allentown; two stepdaughters, Lisa Simmons of Allentown and Beth Levy of Philadelphia; four grandchildren, and six great-grandchildren.


        Malcolm S. Mackay Jr., a former executive with Northwest Airlines, died in Red Lodge on Wednesday after a series of strokes. He was 82 years old.
        Mr. Mackay lived in Long Lake, Minn. His family had maintained a ranch in Montana for many years.
        Mr. Mackay became director of Northwest Airlines in 1948 and executive vice president in 1951. He continued in management until 1961 and served on the airline's board into the 1970's.
        Before World War II, Mr. Mackay was a managing partner of Mackay & Company, a Wall Street investment banking firm that later merged with Laidlaw & Company.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Gray Grebenstein; two sons, two daughters, a brother, a sister, eight grandchildren and four great-grandchildren.

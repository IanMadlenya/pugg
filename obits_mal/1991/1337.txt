
        Kenneth Andrew Roe, a mechanical engineer and chairman of Burns & Roe Enterprises, a New Jersey-based engineering and consulting firm, died on Monday at Broward County Medical Center in Fort Lauderdale, Fla. He was 75 years old and had homes in Palm Beach, Fla., Greenwich, Conn., and Nantucket, Mass.
        He died of heart failure, a family spokeswoman said.
        In 1941, Mr. Roe, who held a degree in chemical engineering from Massachusetts Institute of Technnolgy and one in mechanical engineering from the University of Pennsylvania, joined Burns & Roe, which his father founded.
        He led the company into many advanced technological projects like the Project Mercury and Gemini space programs and the construction of nuclear-generating plants at Oyster Creek, N.J., and at Clinch River in Oak Ridge, Tenn. He was named president in 1963 and chairman in 1971.  Active in Professional Societies
        He was active in many professional societies and was a president of the American Society of Mechanical Engineers and a founding member of the American Association of Engineering Societies.
        He was on the boards of Stevens Institute of Technology, Columbia University's Engineering Council, Manhattan College's Council on Engineering Affairs and the University of Pennsylvania's School of Engineering.
        He is survived by his wife, Hazel; four sons, Ralph and Keith, both of Greenwich, Bart, of Maplewood, N.J., and Randall, of Potomac, Md.; a daughter, Hollace Lindsey Mejer of Manhattan, and eight grandchildren.


        Donald Bogart Kipp, a retired New Jersey lawyer, died on Thursday at the Good Samaritan Medical Center in West Palm Beach, Fla. He was 86 years old and lived aboard his yacht at Hobe Sound, Fla., and Northeast Harbor, Mount Desert, Me.
        A 1928 graduate of Princeton University, Mr. Kipp received his law degree from Harvard and was a partner in the firm of Pitney, Hardin, Kipp & Szuchok in Newark and Morristown. He also was a past trustee of Morristown Memorial Hospital, Mystic Seaport and Princeton University, as well as commodore of the New York Yacht Club.
        Mr. Kipp is survived by his wife, the former Wilhelmina Jessup, whom he married in 1986 after the death of his first wife, the former Beatrice V. Behr. Also surviving are a daughter, Beatrice Kipp Nelson of Gloucester, Mass.; three sons, Peter and Donald, of Cummington, Mass., and Eric, of Edgartown, Mass.; a sister, Margaret Bushnell of Berwyn, Pa., a brother, Jack, of Cold Spring Harbor, L.I., and four stepchildren.

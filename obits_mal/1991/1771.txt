
        Jesse R. Fillman, a retired Boston lawyer who was an expert on tax law, died on Sunday at his home in Cambridge, Mass. He was 86 years old.
        He died of cancer, said his wife, the former Mary Otis Stevens.
        Mr. Fillman was a lawyer for almost 50 years. He joined the Justice Department shortly after graduating from Columbia University Law School in 1931. In 1935 he joined the Manhattan law firm of Milbank, Tweed & Hope and in 1943 moved to Philadelphia to work with Morgan, Lewis & Brockius. From 1946 to 1956, he was a partner in the Manhattan firm of Carter, Ledyard & Milburn and then moved to Boston with Choate, Hall & Stewart. He retired in 1980.
        He taught at New York University and at Boston University. For 22 years, he was general counsel to the American Heart Association and had served as chairman of the Massachusetts Heart Association. He was also active in civic and civil rights causes.
        In addition to his wife, he is survived by two daughters, the Rev. Marthe Dyner of Nashua, N.H., and Katrina Lannen of Tucson, Ariz.; three stepsons, Christopher McNulty of Terryville, Conn., Steven McNulty of Boston, and James McNulty of Blackstone, Mass.; four grandchildren, and one great-grandchild.

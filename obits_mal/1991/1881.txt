
        John L. Jerro, a retired shoe manufacturer, died on Tuesday at St. Vincent's Hospital on Staten Island. He was 89 years old and lived in Brooklyn.
        Mr. Jerro, a native of Aleppo, Syria, came to this country in 1921 and was the co-founder, with his three brothers, of Jerro Bros. Inc. Jerro women's shoes and high-heel bedroom slippers were sold nationally in department stores and boutiques, and Mr. Jerro served as president until the company was sold in 1963.
        Mr. Jerro was active in Catholic Charities and the Cardinal's Committee of the Laity for more than 40 years.
        He is survived by a daughter, Joan Jerro of Todt Hill, S.I.; two sons, Ronald, of McLean, Va., and John R., of Browns Mills, N.J.; two sisters, Angele Kassar and Jeannette Sayour, both of Brooklyn; seven grandchildren, and one great-granddaughter.

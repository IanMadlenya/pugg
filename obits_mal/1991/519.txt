
        E. Sherman Adams, a retired economist and senior banking officer at several institutions, died on Friday at Florida Hospital North in Altamonte Springs. He was 80 years old and lived in Mount Dora, Fla., and Preston, Conn.
        He died after a heart attack while hospitalized for a fractured hip, his daughter, Nancy Bartlett, said.
        Before retiring in 1975, Mr. Adams was an assistant vice president of the Central Hanover Bank and Trust Company, a senior vice president of the First National City Bank and a senior vice president and economist for the Fidelity Bank in Philadelphia.
        In 1952 he became the deputy manager of the American Bankers Association, directing its departments of monetary policy and economics and research in eight years there.
        He also was assistant vice chancellor for development at New York University, where he had lectured in economics and finance. And he was director of the Stonier Graduate School of Banking at Rutgers University.
        Surviving are his wife, Alice, and three daughters, Ms. Bartlett of Ridgefield, Conn., Cynthia Adams of Santa Cruz, Calif., and Priscilla Adams of Bolinas, Calif.


        Harold Hazelton, an advertising writer and genealogist, died on Monday at his home in Manhattan. He was 87 years old.
        He died of heart failure, his family said.
        Mr. Hazelton was born on Sept. 20, 1904, in Marysville, Mich. He attended the Chicago Music College.
        During his career in advertising, Mr. Hazelton worked for such agencies as Ferry Hanly, Kaastor Farrell, Grey Advertising and the Don Rowe Agency.
        In 1965, he became library director of the New York Genealogical and Biographical Society, a position he held until 1976. He was also associate editor of the society's quarterly publication, "The New York Genealogical and Biographical Record," from 1963 to 1965.  Mr. Hazelton was a genealogist of many patriotic societies, including the Huguenot Society of America, the National Society of Colonial Dames, and the Society of Daughters of Holland Dames.
        He is survived by his wife, Nika Standen Hazelton, a cookbook writer, and two stepsons, Anthony and Julian Standen.

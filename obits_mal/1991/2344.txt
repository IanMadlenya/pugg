
        Roger Hackney, a retired executive vice president at the Johns Manville Corporation, died on Tuesday at his home in New Canaan, Conn. He was 89 years old.
        He died of complications from strokes, his family said.
        He worked at the company, a maker of industrial and business products now know as the Manville Corporation, from 1942 until his retirement in 1967. Mr. Hackney, who was born in Wellington, Kan., graduated from the University of Kansas with an engineering degree in 1924. After working in construction, he joined Eastman Dillon, a Wall Street company, in 1928. In 1934 he went to another investment company, Foster Marvin. He was also a director of the Polymer Corporation, Advocate Mines Ltd., the Coalinga Asbestos Company, the Halcyon Fund, Bush Universal, Universal Airlines and Kislidjan H.F.
        He is survived by his wife of 61 years, the former Margaret Flaherty; two daughters, Cynthia Matthews of Stamford, Conn., and Ann Webb of New Canaan; a sister, Lela Bell of Maryville, Mo., and seven grandchildren.


        Richard E. (Dick) Bailey, a pioneer in independent sports-television in the 1950's, died on Oct. 25 at Bayshore Community Hospital in Holmdel, N.J. He was 80 years old.
        In 1956, Dick Bailey created Sports Network Incorporated to fill the voids in sports coverage by the Big Three networks. After 12 years, the network was sold to Howard Hughes and became the Hughes Television Network, with Mr. Bailey as president.
        Mr. Bailey's first wife, Erna, died. He is survived by his second wife, Patricia K.; his daughters, Rosemarie E. Martin of Gladstone, N.J.; Denise Bohan of Tinton Falls, N.J.; and his sons, Richard E. of Morristown, N.J.; Roger of Boonton, N.J.; Linwood T. of Cedar Grove, N.J.; Gene C. of Tinton Falls; Stephen A. of Vienna, Va.; and Geoffrey T., of Colts Neck; a brother, J. Edward Bailey of WaWa, Pa., a sister, Katherine Schroeder of Smithtown, L.I., and 22 grandchildren and 4 great-grandchildren.


        Irwin N. Alberts, a publishing executive, died on Thursday at his home in East Hampton, L.I. He was 57 years old.
        Mr. Alberts died of multiple myeloma, or cancer of the bone marrow, said a spokeswoman for the family.
        He was a past president and chief executive officer of Panini USA, a publisher of children's magazines, based in Manhattan, and a senior vice president of Macmillan Publishing Company. Panini and Macmillan are affiliates of the Maxwell Communications Company.
        Mr. Alberts, a native of Boston, was a graduate of the University of Massachusetts and Boston College Law School. After service in the Army as a lieutenant he worked as a lawyer for Federal agencies before joining Crowell, Collier & Macmillan as industrial relations manager. He then became an executive of G. Schirmer, music publishers, and joined Panini in 1989 as a president and chief executive officer.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Helen Daniel, and a stepson, Clifford L. Ennis 3d of Jacksonville, Fla.

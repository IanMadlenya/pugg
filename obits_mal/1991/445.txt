
        Carl B. Spaeth, dean of the Stanford Law School from 1946 to 1962, died of pneumonia Sunday at a convalescent home. He was 83 years old.
        Mr. Spaeth, a native of Cleveland, graduated from Dartmouth College in 1929 and continued his legal studies at Yale University. He then taught at Temple University, Northwestern University and Yale.
        He served in the State Department from 1940 until 1946, specializing in Latin American affairs under Nelson A. Rockefeller. He left to become dean at Stanford Law School.
        In his tenure at Stanford Mr. Spaeth enlarged the faculty and course offering and founded the first of the school's three law reviews.
        He stepped down as dean in 1962 to devote his time to foreign studies, but remained at Stanfod for the next 10 years teaching international law.
        Survivors include his wife, Sheila; a son, Carl of Palo Alto, and a daughter, Laurin, of Stanford.

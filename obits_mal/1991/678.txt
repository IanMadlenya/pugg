
        Dr. Michael T. Modny, an internationally known orthopedic surgeon who held seven United States patents for inventions of surgical pins and nails, died on Sunday at Mountainside Hospital in Glen Ridge, N.J. He was 74 years old and lived in Montclair, N.J.
        He died of pneumonia, a funeral home representative said.
        Dr. Modny was a practicing orthopedic surgeon for more than 40 years. A 1942 graduate of Long Island College of Medicine, he served his internship at Morrisania Hospital in the Bronx and did his residency at Mountainside Hospital. He was on the staffs of Mountainside Hospital, Community Hospital in Montclair and Children's Hospital in Newark. He was also a clinical professor of orthopedics at New York Medical College. He lectured internationally on orthopedics and was writing a book about the specialty.
        He is survived by his wife, Mary; two daughters, Dr. Cynthia J. Modny of Verona, N.J., and Marilyn, of Manhattan; a son, Allan, of Charlottesville, Va., and two grandchildren.


        David Goodman, a government administrator and former city manager of Peekskill, N.Y., died on Friday at the Country House, a retirement home in Yorktown Heights, N.Y. He was 79 years old.
        He died of heart failure, a family spoksman said.
        Mr. Goodman became the first city manager of Peekskill in 1968, developing a master plan for the parks, carrying out public improvements of more than $200 million and computerizing management functions. After retiring in 1975, he began a career as a consultant, advising many municipalities in Westchester County. In 1967, he served as a consultant to the Minister of Interior of Iran, advising him on the reorganization of local governments. From 1958 to 1967, he was village manager of Mount Kisco, N.Y., where he centralized administrative operations.
        Mr. Goodman, a graduate of Pennsylvania State University, had been chief administrator of cities and civil affairs with the military government in Italy during World War II and afterward was town manager of Abingdon, Va.
        He is survived by two sisters, Edith Maximon of Tarpon Springs, Fla., and Nellie Hall of Fort Worth, and a brother, Martin, of Hollidaysburg, Pa.

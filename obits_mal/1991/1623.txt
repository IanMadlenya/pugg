
        John M. Gerstenmaier, retired vice chairman of Goodyear, died Monday at his home in Menlo Park, Calif. He was 74 years old and also lived in Naples, Fla.
        He died of heart failure, his family said.
        Mr. Gerstenmaier joined Goodyear in 1938 as a laboratory engineer. He became manager of industrial products development in 1957, vice president in charge of United States manufacuring in 1967 and executive vice president in 1971. He was elected president in 1974 and vice chairman and chief operating officer in 1978.
        He received a bachelor's degree from the University of Minnesota and a master's degree in industrial management from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology.
        He is survived by his wife, Patti; and three children from a previous marriage, a son, John, of Akron, and two daughters, Jan Panhuis of Chagrin Falls, Ohio, and Jo Ellen Ally of Manhattan.

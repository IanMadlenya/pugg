
        Dr. William Alden Gardner, an internist in Manhattan for more than 50 years, died on Sunday at New York Hospital. He was 88 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        He died of kidney failure, his wife, Gloria, said.
        Dr. Gardner, who was born in Brooklyn, was a graduate of Dartmouth and received his medical degree from the College of Physicians and Surgeons of Columbia University.
        In addition to his wife, he is survived by two daughters, Susan Surine of St. Paul and Janet Weinberg of Belfast, Me.; a son, Dr. James P., of Pocatello, Idaho; a sister, Lois Kiefer of Ithaca, N.Y.; five grandchildren, and a great-granddaughter.

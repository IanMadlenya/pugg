
        Eric R. Bachmann, founder and retired president of the Eric R. Bachmann Company, a machine-tool importer, died on Saturday at his home in Manhattan. He was 83 years old.
        He died of cancer, his son, Peter, said.
        Mr. Bachmann started the business in Long Island City in 1939, specializing in precision metal-working machine tools. In 1955, he founded Ferroquip, an affiliate corporation in Long Island City, to import large industrial equipment.
        Mr. Bachmann was born in Mannheim, Germany, and graduated as a mechanical engineer from the Technical University in Berlin in 1932. He came to the United States in 1939.
        Besides his son, Peter, of Brooklyn, he is survived by his wife, the former Marion Strueken; twin daughters, Mona Bachmann of Hamilton, Mont., and Nicky Fronson of West Hartford, Conn., and a granddaughter.


        Roy W. Jastram, professor emeritus of business administration at the University of California at Berkeley, died on Friday at a nursing home in Glendora, Calif. He was 76 years old and had lived in Carmel Valley, Calif., for many years.
        He died of a respiratory ailment, the university said.
        Dr. Jastram, an authority on the gold standard, joined the Berkeley faculty in 1946 and became a full professor in 1954. He was associate dean of Berkeley's School of Business Administration from 1951 through 1954 and retired from teaching in 1982.
        He wrote "The Gold Constant," published in 1977, and "Silver: The restless Metal" (1981).
        Dr. Jastram was born in Fresno, Calif., and received bachelor's and doctoral degrees from Stanford University. He was with the National Resources Planning Board and the National Bureau of Economic Research before serving with the Navy in World War II.
        He is survived by two sons, Jeffrey, of Upland, Calif., and William, of Tualatin, Ore.; a daughter, Judy, of San Francisco; three grandchildren and one great-grandchild.

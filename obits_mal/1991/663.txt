
        Jerome Lipper, an international lawyer and banking executive, died yesterday at the Dana Farber Cancer Institute in Boston. He was 54 years old and lived in Scarsdale, N.Y.
        He died of multiple myeloma, his family said.
        Mr. Lipper was a senior partner in the Tenzer Greenblatt Fallon & Kaplan law firm in Manhattan and was chairman of the Ansbacher Bank, a European commercial and trade bank, in Dublin.
        He was a specialist in international river disputes and at various times served as legal counsel to the State Department and the Governments of Germany, Pakistan and Bangladesh. He was chairman of the Profession Committee of the City Bar Association in New York.
        Mr. Lipper, who was born in the Bronx, was a graduate of Long Island University and of the New York University School of Law.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Binnette Blatt; two sons, David and Allen, both of Scarsdale; his parents, Sally and George, of the Bronx, and a brother, Kenneth, of Manhattan.

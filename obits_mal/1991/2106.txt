
        Clement J. Wyle, a magazine and broadcast writer and public relations expert, died on Thursday at University Hospital in Manhattan. He was 88 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        He died of cancer, his family said.
        Mr. Wyle and his wife, Helen Price, wrote for Esquire, Good Housekeeping and other magazines. They helped develop programs for the "Big Story" radio and television series on CBS and were also consultants for the television series "Treasury Men in Action."
        They also collaborated in a public relations business he started in 1946. Their clients included Avnet Inc., the Bristol-Myers Company, Carter-Wallace Inc., the Phillips-Van Heusen Corporation, the U.S. Life Corporation and the Zayre Corporation. He also helped create the Institute of Public Relations at the University of Bridgeport.
        Mr. Wyle is survived by his wife; a daughter, Beth Thrope of Barrington, Ill., and two grandchildren.


        Neill Alexander McAllister, a retired executive of McAllister Brothers, a marine towing and transportation company founded by his great grandfather, died on Tuesday at Memorial Sloan-Kettering Cancer Center in Manhattan. He was 65 years old and lived in Belle Terre, L.I., and Tequesta, Fla.
        He died of complications from esophageal cancer, said his daughter, Suzanne McAllister of Manhattan.
        The company, one of the world's largest barge and towing companies, has been a major force in the shipping industry in New York Harbor for more than a century. Mr. McAllister spent his entire business career with McAllister Brothers, of which he became co-owner and executive vice president.
        Mr. McAllister was active throughout his career in the Downtown Athletic Club of New York, where he served during the 1970's as governor, chairman of the Heisman Trophy Committee, vice president and president.
        Mr. McAllister attended Union College and received a mechanical engineering degree from Brown University. After retiring, he received a master's degree in English Literature from Adelphi University.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Barbara Rice; three sons, Neill Jr. of New City, N.Y., Patrick of Manhattan, and Dennis of Darien, Conn.; five daughters, Mary Barbara Fader of Sudbury, Mass., Alexandra Woodfield of Palm Beach, Fla., Elizabeth Pope of Jenkintown, Pa., and Suzanne and Nancy, both of Manhattan; his mother, Helen, a brother, James P., and a sister, Ellen Perrott, all of Stamford, Conn., and six grandchildren.

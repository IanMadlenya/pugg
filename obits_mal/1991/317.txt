
        George W. Arnett, a banker and former president of what is now the Trenton Savings Bank, died on Feb. 15 at a nursing home in Mill Valley, Calif. He was 87 years old and lived in Mill Valley.
        He died of heart failure, said his son Cornelius, of Belvedere, Calif.
        In 1933, Mr. Arnett joined what was then the Trenton Saving Fund Society and by 1936 he was a vice president. He was became president in 1956 and held that post until he retired in 1968. He continued to served as chairman until he moved to California in 1984.
        He was a past president of the Savings Banks Association of New Jersey and was a member of the National Association of Mutual Savings Banks. He also served as president of the Standard Fire Insurance Company of New Jersey and, after it merged into the Reliance Insurance Company, became a director there. He was on the board of trustees of the First Presbyterian Church of Trenton for many years.
        He is also survived by two daughters, Lee Young of Louisville, Ky., and Janice Palmer of Santa Cruz, Calif.; another son, Dixon, of Arlington, Va.; eight grandchildren, and three great-grandchildren.


        Noel M. P. Reilly, an economic official for the British Government who retired in 1965 and then became active in politics in New Hampshire, died April 22 at the Lakes Region General Hospital in Laconia, N.H. He was 88 years old and lived in North Sandwich, N.H.
        Mr. Reilly was a financial counselor at the British Embassy in Washington from 1960 until his retirement and also was an alternate executive director with the World Bank at the time. From 1953 to 1959 he was Britain's economic counselor for the Persian Gulf, stationed in Kuwait.
        He was born in Pinner, England, and received a master's degree from Cambridge University and bachelor's and doctoral degrees from London University. He taught at a private school in Boston in 1924 and was in business in New Zealand and in England before World War II. He also served in the British Treasury in London.
        After he retired he became an American citizen, was active in the Democratic Party in New Hampshire and was a trustee of the Northern Energy Corporation.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Dorry Rainsford; a son and a daughter from a previous marriage, Wyn Reilly of Macclesfield, England, and June Watt of Toronto, and five grandchildren.


        Milton Greenstein, a former vice president and assistant secretary of the New Yorker magazine, died on Monday in New York Hospital. He was 79 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        He died of respiratory failure, said his daughter Ruth, of Washington.
        Mr. Greenstein, a lawyer, joined the magazine in 1945, checking articles for possible libel. He then headed the legal department and in 1962 became vice president. He was named assistant secretary in 1976. After retiring in 1976, he served as a consultant to the magazine.
        Besides his daughter Ruth, a vice president of the Institute for Defense Analyses, he is survived by his wife, Beatrice; a daughter, Naomi, who is on the faculty of the University of California at Santa Barbara, and a brother, Samuel, of Brooklyn, a State Supreme Court justice.

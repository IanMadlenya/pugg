
        John P. Tembeck, who was active in New York's Armenian community, died last Tuesday at his home in Yonkers. He was 75 years old.
        Mr. Tembeck died of cancer, a family spokeswoman said.
        Mr. Tembeck, a salesman for Quality House of Graphics in Long Island City, was a member of St. Vartan's Armenian Cathedral in Manhattan. He was chairman of the consecration committee of the cathedral in 1967 and also served as a diocesan delegate. He was chairman of the board of trustees of Holy Cross Armenian Apostolic Church in Manhattan.
        Mr. Tembeck was also a past president of the Photoengravers Board of Trade in Manhattan.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Lucy Vakassian; a son, John P. Jr., of Harrison, N.Y.; two daughters, Susan Warner of Old Greenwich, Conn., and Bonnie Paster of Manhasset, L.I., and seven grandchildren.

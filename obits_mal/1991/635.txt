
        Joseph De Grazia, a former partner in B. Brown Associates, direct-mail advertising specialists, died on Monday at his home in Ft. Lauderdale, Fla. He was 70 years old.
        He died of a heart attack, his family said.
        Mr. De Grazia, a Manhattan native and a graduate of New York University, began work as a messenger at B. Brown and eventually became its president and co-owner before the agency was sold in 1971. He served in the Army in World War II.
        He leaves a sister, Carmela Baldan, and two nieces, Lisa De Guzman and Isabella Baldan, all of Manhattan.

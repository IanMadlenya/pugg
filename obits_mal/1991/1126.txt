
        Kermit K. Schooler, a psychologist and former dean of the school of social work at Syracuse University, died on Tuesday at his home in Manlius, N.Y. He was 67 years old.
        He died of amyotrophic lateral sclerosis, also known as Lou Gehrig's disease, said his daughter Marianne.
        Dr. Manlius joined the faculty at Syracuse in 1970 and was dean from 1972 until retiring in 1989. He had previously taught at Brandeis University for 18 years and worked on the research staff of the Arthur D. Little Company in Cambridge, Mass. He received a Ph.D. in psychology in 1956 from the University of Michigan.
        He was a member of the American Psychological Association, the American Public Health Association and the Council on Social Work Education.
        He was a fellow of the Gerontological Society of America and former secretary of the Association for Gerontology in Higher Education.
        He is survived by his wife, Ellen; a son, Aaron, of Manlius; two daughters, Esther, of Ann Arbor, Mich., and Marianne, of Boston; a brother, Lawrence, of Elmira, N.Y., and two grandchildren.

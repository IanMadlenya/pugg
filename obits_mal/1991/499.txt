
        John de Braganca, a former vice president and treasurer of the Rhinelander Real Estate Company and later an investment banker, died on Tuesday in Palm Beach, Fla. He was 79 years old and lived in Winston-Salem, N.C.
        Mr. de Braganca, a great-grandson of King Miguel of Portugal, who ruled from 1828 to 1834, was born in Sussex, England, and graduated from Harvard. He served as a commander in the United States Navy in World War II.
        He retired as vice president of the Manhattan investment banking firm of Winslow, Cohu & Stetson.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Katharine Bahnson; a son, Miguel, of Boston; a brother, Miguel, of Palm Beach, and three grandchildren.

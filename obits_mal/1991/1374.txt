
        Anson Burlingame, the director of Columbia University's Office of Projects and Grants from 1962 to 1973, died on Saturday at his home in New Canaan, Conn. He was 83 years old.
        He died of a heart ailment, his son, Edward, said.
        Before taking the university post, he was an executive with the Singer Company for 25 years, rising to regional director of operations in Europe for the sewing machine company. From 1974 to 1976, after retiring from Columbia, he headed the New York office of the International College in Beirut, a regional secondary school with ties to the American University there.
        Mr. Burlingame, a native of Manhattan, graduated from Phillips Exeter Academy and Harvard College. During World War II, he was an Air Force captain working in intelligence in London for the Office of Strategic Services.
        Besides his son, who lives in Purdys, N.Y., he is survived by his wife of 57 years, Elizabeth; a daughter, Susan Weidlich of Darien, Conn., and six grandchildren.


        Spencer Dumaresq Moseley, a transportation executive who was a football star at Yale and a former member of the Yale Corporation, the university's governing body, died on Wednesday at a nursing home in Chicago. He was 72 years old and lived in Chicago.
        The family declined to give the cause of death.
        Mr. Moseley was a former chairman and chief executive of R.E.A. Express, formerly the Railway Express Agency, in Manhattan, from which he retired in the mid-1980's. He had joined the organization in 1968.
        Mr. Moseley was captain and center of the 1942 Yale football team, and a member of the All-East team. He was ranked as one of the top 20 players in Yale football history by a committee formed in commemoration of the 100th anniversary of the Harvard-Yale game. He broke his jaw in his second season but played every game wearing a specially designed harness. He graduated with honors from Yale in 1943.
        His father, the late George Moseley, was an All-American end at Yale, class of 1917.
        In World War II Mr. Moseley was a pilot in the Marine Corps with the rank of captain. He was awarded three Air Medals. He also served in the Korean War, receiving the Distinguished Flying Cross.
        He was a graduate of the Hill School in Pottstown, Pa., and Cheshire Academy in Connecticut. In 1943 he joined the General American Transportation Corporation in Chicago, now GATX, and was its president from 1961 to 1967. He was briefly president of the Mangood Corporation before joining Railway Express. He was a former director of the Container Corporation of America.
        His marriage to the former Virginia Gillette Kleitz ended in divorce.
        He is survived by three sons, Bill of Los Angeles, Dan of Denton, Tex., and Bruce of Billings, Mont., and a granddaughter.

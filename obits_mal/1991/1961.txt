
        Sidney Schwartz, a frozen-food industry pioneer, died on Oct. 16 in Padua, Italy, while vacationing with his wife. He was 70 years old and lived in Long Beach.
        He died in an accidental fall, said a daughter, Eileen Lieberman.
        In 1946, Mr. Schwartz helped found Temple Frosted Foods, based in Brooklyn, which was an early maker of frozen Chinese food. He was active in the company until he sold his interest in 1957 and was later a food broker and trade organization executive.
        He is survived by his wife Bess; two daughters, Ms. Lieberman, of Brunswick, N.J., and Debra Schwartz of Chevy Chase, Md.; a brother, Harry Schwartz of Redondo Beach, Calif.; two sisters, Gertrude Korn of Brooklyn ahd Shirley Findley of Garden Grove, Calif., and six grandchildren.

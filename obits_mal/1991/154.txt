
        Walter A. (Hoot) Evers, a former outfielder with the Detroit Tigers and a long-time executive with the baseball club, died Friday at St. Luke's Hospital in Houston after a short illness. He was 69 years old and had recently suffered a heart attack.
        Mr. Evers played six full seasons and parts of three others with Detroit. He also played with the Boston Red Sox, the New York Giants, the Baltimore Orioles and the Cleveland Indians.
        In 1971, he joined the Tigers as director of player development. In 1978, he became a special assignment scout with headquarters in Houston.

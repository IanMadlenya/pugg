
        Durwood Alton Blaisdell, president of the U.S. Baird Corporation, machine-tool builders of Stratford, Conn., died on Monday at the Connecticut Hospice in Branford. He was 68 years old and lived in Orange, Conn., and Fort Lauderdale, Fla.
        He died of heart attack, his daughter said.
        He joined the U.S. Baird Corporation in 1955 and became its president in 1961. Under his leadership, it acquired the U.S. Tool Corporation, the Globe Machine Company, the A.H. Nilson Corporation, the Clay Engineering Company and Finzer Incorporated.
        He was a former director of the City Trust Corporation of Bridgeport, Conn.
        Mr. Blaisdell was born in Skowhegan, Me., and graduated from Becker College in Worcester, Mass.
        His first wife, the former Patricia Jane Warner, died in 1979.
        Besides his daughter from the first marriage, Deborah Ann of Milford, Conn., he is survived by his wife, the former Marilynn Beyer; two stepchildren, Andrea Lynn Doyle and James Doyle, both of Orange, Conn., and two sisters, Elaine Cannell of Skowhegan and Reta Wheater of Pascoag, R.I.

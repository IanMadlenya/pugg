
        Charles M. Bliss, a retired chairman of the Bank of New York, died on Friday at his home in Southport, Me. He was 87 years old.
        He died of bone cancer, said a funeral home spokesman.
        Mr. Bliss, who retired in 1969, joined the Bank of New York in 1931 as a member of a newly formed investment counseling group. He had previously been with the Equitable Trust Company and, after a merger, the National City Bank.
        At the Bank of New York, he became a vice president in 1942, executive vice president in 1957 and chairman in 1963. He was a member of the executive council of the American Bankers Association and served as chairman of the trust division of the New York State Bankers Association.
        He was a former chairman of the board of trustees of St. Barnabas Hospital.
        Mr. Bliss is survived by his wife of 65 years, the former Ella Flanders; a son, Richard, of Manhattan; a daughter, Marjorie Garling of Bel Air, Md.; a sister, Ethel Anderson of Los Angeles; nine grandchildren, and eight great-grandchildren.

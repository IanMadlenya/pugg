
        J. William Rosenbluth, a lawyer in Manhattan for more than 60 years, died on Sunday at Boca Raton Hospital in Florida. He was 82 years old and lived in Manhattan and in Boca Raton.
        He died of pneumonia, his family said.
        Mr. Rosenbluth was a co-founder of the League of Voluntary Hospitals and Homes, an organization of nonprofit institutions. He participated in writing the National Labor Relations Act, known as the Wagner Act, as a lawyer in the 1930's and was later active in real-estate law.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Claire Kaplan; a daughter, Susan Sherman of Manhattan; a son, Lawrence, of Manhattan; two sisters, Gertrude Zwerling of Palm Beach, Fla., and Selma Sendroff of Fort Lauderdale, Fla.; a brother, Kalman, of Fort Lauderdale, and four grandchildren.

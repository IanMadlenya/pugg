
        Msgr. Robert G. Rappleyea, pastor emeritus of the Roman Catholic Church of the Holy Cross at 42d Street near Eighth Avenue in Manhattan, died on Thursday at St. Francis Hospital in Poughkeepsie, N.Y.
        He was 66 years old and died of leukemia, a funeral home spokesman said.
        Monsignor Rappleyea was born in Poughkeepsie and graduated from Poughkeepsie High School in 1942 and Cathedral College in 1944. He graduated from St. Joseph's Seminary in Yonkers in 1950 and received a master's degree in psychology from Iona College.
        He was ordained in 1950 and that year was assigned year to be associate pastor of St. Mary's Church in Wappinger Falls, N.Y. In 1973, he moved to Holy Cross Church as pastor. He was named a Domestic Prelate in 1986, making him an honorary member of the Papal household.
        An an outspoken critic of the sexually oriented businesses that multiplied in the Times Square area during the 1970's, Monsignor Rappleyea was also the chaplain of the Port Authority Police from 1975 to 1985 and a member of several civic organizations and committees.
        He is survived by two brothers, Guy J. Rappleyea of Bend, Ore., and Allan E. Rappleyea of Poughkeepsie, and three sisters, Kathleen O'Leary of Hyde Park, N.Y., Mary Jean King of Rhinebeck, N.Y., and Nancy Rappleyea of Poughkeepsie.

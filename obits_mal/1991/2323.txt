
        Brian Jay Dunbar, a Broadway company manager whose shows included Neil Simon's "Lost in Yonkers," died last Tuesday at Cabrini Medical Center in Manhattan. He was 33 years old.
        He died of pneumonia, said Glen Gardali, his companion.
        Mr. Dunbar began his career in the Broadway management office of Soloway & Francis, co-producers of Michael Cristofer's "Shadow Box" and general managers of the 1976 revival of Edward Albee's "Who's Afraid of Virginia Woolf?" with Colleen Dewhurst. He was assistant house manager at the Morosco and Helen Hayes Theaters.
        Mr. Dunbar was born in Big Pond, Pa., and graduated from the University of Pennsylvania. Since 1983 he had worked for the producer Emanuel Azenberg, managing shows like "Sunday in the Park With George," "Jerome Robbins's Broadway" and "Biloxi Blues."
        He is survived by his parents, Dilmon and Lola Dunbar, and a brother, Dale, all of Big Pond; another brother, Gene, of Los Angeles, and a sister, Karen, of Buffalo.

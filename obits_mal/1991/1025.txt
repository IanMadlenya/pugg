
        Thomas M. Biallo, an executive in the perfume industry, died on Thursday at New York Hospital. He was 66 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Biallo died as a result of a stroke, said his daughter, Suzanne Andree Biallot of Manhattan.
        Mr. Biallo was president of Camilli, Albert & Laloue, a perfume supplier in Manhattan, from 1949 to 1962. He then became a director of International Flavors and Fragrances Inc., perfume suppliers. In the mid-1960's, he founded his own company named after his daughter, Andree Biallot Ltd., a natural extracts and raw materials concern in Manhattan, of which he was president. He retired in 1987, after an earlier stroke.
        He was born in Brussels and came to the United States in 1946. He was a graduate of Cambridge University.
        Besides his daughter, he is survived by his wife, the former Marion Hutchinson.

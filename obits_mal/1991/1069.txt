
        Thomas Robeson Morse Jr., a retired chief justice of the Massachusetts Superior Court, died of Alzheimer's disease on Wednesday at the Veterans Administration Hospital. He was 63.
        Justice Morse, a former trial lawyer, was named a Superior Court judge in 1973 by Gov. Francis W. Sargent and served as the court's chief justice for five years before retirement in 1988.
        He is survived by his wife, two daughters, one son, a sister, a brother and two granddaughters.


        Francis X. McDermott, a retired lawyer and New York City police officer, died on Sunday at New Rochelle Hospital. He was 74 years old and lived in Bronxville, N.Y.
        Mr. McDermott died of a heart attack, a spokeswoman for the family said.
        He joined the New York City Police Department in 1941 and became head of its legal bureau at the Manhattan headquarters. He retired in 1961 with the rank of lieutenant. He then became a partner in the Manhattan law firm of Byrne & McDermott and retired for the second time in the late 1960's.
        Mr. McDermott was born in Manhattan and graduated from Fordham College and Fordham Law School.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Margaret Graham; two daughters, Eileen, of Pacific Palisades, Calif., and Kathleen, of Manhattan; three sons, Gerard, of Boca Raton, Fla., Daniel, of Pearl River, N.Y., and James, of Tampa, Fla., and nine grandchildren.

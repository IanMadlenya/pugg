
        Thomas A. Carlin, a stage, film and television actor, died Monday at his home in New Rochelle, N.Y. He was 62 years old.
        He died of heart failure, said his wife, the actress Frances Sternhagen.
        During the 1950's and 60's, Mr. Carlin appeared in nine Broadway plays, including "Time Limit," "A Thousand Clowns" and "The Deputy."
        On television, he played starring roles on "Kraft Television Theater," "Alcoa-Goodyear Playhouse" and "Studio One." He portrayed Fred Metcalf on the daytime drama "Search for Tomorrow" and recently appeared on "The Guiding Light."
        In the 1960's and 70's, Mr. Carlin taught and directed at Pace University in Pleasantville, N.Y., and at Rye High School.
        He returned to Broadway in 1976 in "Players." He later appeared Off Broadway in Hugh Leonard's "Summer" and in "Pigeons on the Walk."
        His film credits include "Ragtime," "Fort Apache," "Caddyshack," "The Pope of Greenwich Village," "Matewan," "Family Business" and the recently released "One Good Cop."
        Besides his wife, he is survived by six children: Paul, Amanda, Tony, Peter and John, all of New York, and Sarah Morse of Columbus, Ohio; a brother, Philip M., of Chicago, and two grandchildren.

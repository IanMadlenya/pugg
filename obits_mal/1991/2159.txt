
        Concetto di Mauro, a retired business executive, died on Wednesday at St. Francis Hospital in Hartford. He was 61 years old and lived in West Hartford and South Dartmouth, Mass.
        He died of liver cancer, his family said.
        Mr. di Mauro, known as Chet, retired in June as a vice president of United Technologies International in Hartford after 16 years in which he helped develop the company's overseas business. He was previously employed for 10 years at McGraw-Hill International in London, where he became vice president for operations. He was in charge of joint ventures and was the managing director of several affiliated companies.
        In the 1960's he was a reporter and editor at United Press International in New York City, holding posts including United Nations correspondent, World's Fair bureau chief, overnight city editor, day city editor and general news editor.
        Mr. Di Mauro, a native of the Bronx, graduated from Emerson College in Boston and was a Ford Foundation Fellow in international studies at Columbia University.
        He is survived by his wife, Elizabeth Ann; two sons, Geoffrey and Alexander, both of West Hartford; a sister, Ida Daidone of New York City, and two brothers, Paul and Louis, both of Chicago.


        The Rev. John J. Gorman, a Roman Catholic priest in the Brooklyn Dioscese for 54 years who served as director of the Vatican Pavilion at the New York World's Fair in 1964-65, died on Wednesday at Lutheran Medical Center in Brooklyn. He was 79 years old and lived at the Monastery of the Precious Blood in Brooklyn.
        He died of a heart attack, his family said.
        Father Gorman, a native of Brooklyn, was a graduate of the College and Seminary of the Immaculate Conception in Huntington, L.I., where he was ordained in 1937. He served at several parishes in Brooklyn and was pastor of St. Luke's in Whitestone, Queens, for several years. In 1983 he was named director of th Confraternity of the Precious Blood.
        He is survived by a sister, Mildred Kheel of Brooklyn.

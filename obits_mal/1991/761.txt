
        Joseph M. Cosenza, the chief architect for the Port Authority of New York and New Jersey, died on April 10 at St. Francis Hospital in Roslyn, L.I. He was 60 years old and lived in Bayside, Queens.
        He died of cancer, a family spokesman said.
        Mr. Cosenza joined the Port Authority's aviation division in 1967 and played a major role in the redevelopment of Newark International Airport. In 1982 he moved to the engineering department as assistant chief architect. He returned to the aviation division in 1987 as manager of a new redevelopment phase for Newark Airport, known as Newark 2000.
        In April 1989 he was appointed chief architect of the Port Authority.
        He was a graduate of Columbia University.
        He leaves his wife, Beatrice; two sons, Joseph Jr., of Jersey City, and John, of Hicksville, L.I.; two daughters, Carol Pane of New Hyde Park, L.I., and Andrea Ryan of Floral Park, L.I.; a brother, Henry, of Fort Washington, L.I., and three grandchildren.

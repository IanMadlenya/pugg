
        William Bache Fretter, a physicist who was vice president of the University of California from 1978 to 1983, died Sunday. He was 74 years old.
        Before becoming vice president, Dr. Fretter taught physics at the Berkeley campus for 32 years and conducted research into high-energy particle physics.
        He received two Fulbright scholarships and a Guggenheim fellowship. In 1964 he was named a Chevalier of the French Legion of Honor.
        After retiring as vice president, he was associated with a World Bank project to revitalize Chinese universities devastated by the Cultural Revolution of the 1960's.
        He is survived by his wife, Grace; a daughter, two sons and six grandchildren.

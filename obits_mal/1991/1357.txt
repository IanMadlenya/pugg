
        Dr. Antonio Rottino, director of laboratories and chief pathologist at St. Vincent's Hospital for more than three decades, died on Tuesday at the hospital. He was 88 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        He died of a heart attack, his family said.
        Dr. Rottino, who was born in Hoboken, N.J., was a graduate of New York University, from which he also held doctoral degrees in medicine and biology. He joined the medical staff of St. Vincent's in 1934 and was head of the hospital's Hodgkin's Disease Research Foundation. He formally retired in 1968 but contunued to conduct research at the hospital until five years ago.
        His wife, the former Rose Zarcone, died in 1977. He is survived by a daughter, Mary Ann Bednar of Camarillo, Calif.; two sons, William, of Tenafly, N.J., and Antonio, of Racine, Wis.; four brothers, Alfred, of Bayside, Queens, Joseph, of Hampton Bays, L.I., Ronald, of Forest Hills, Queens, and John, of Hialeah, Fla.; a sister, Betty, of Lynbrook, L.I.; 14 grandchildren, and six great-grandchildren.

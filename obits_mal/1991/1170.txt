
        Paul Mittler, a former lawyer for the United Restititution Organization, which seeks restitution for victims of Nazi atrocities, died on May 2 at the Robert Woods Johnson University Hospital in New Brunswick, N.J. He was 77 years old and lived in Highland Park. N.J.
        He died of a stroke, said his son Uri.
        Mr. Mittler, who was born in Czechoslovakia, received a law degree from Komensky University in Bratislava, Czechoslovakia, in 1938 and moved to Palestine in 1939. He served in a Czechoslovak unit of the British Army in World War II and returned to Palestine in 1948. He came to the United States in 1953 and was with the United Restitution Organization for 28 years before retiring in 1982.
        He is survived by his wife, Melitta; two sons, Dan, of Ithaca, N.Y., and Uri, of Highland Park, N.J.; a sister, Trude Stern of Sun City, Ariz., and four grandchildren.

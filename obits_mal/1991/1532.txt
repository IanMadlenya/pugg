
        James Harper Jr., a retired vice president of Marine Midland Bank, died on Wednesday at Greenwich Hospital in Connecticut. He was 72 years old and lived in Greenwich.
        He died of leukemia, said his sister.
        Before he retired in 1981, Mr. Harper was vice president of trust and estate administration for the Eastern region of Marine Midland. He was a graduate of Columbia College and Columbia Law School and in the 1950's joined Grace National Bank, which later became part of Marine Midland.
        His great-great-grandfather, James Harper, was Mayor of New York City in 1844 and 1845 and a founder of Harper and Brothers, publishers.
        Mr. Harper is survived by his wife of 47 years, the former Barbara Haskell; a daughter, Denise Bennett of Montclair, N.J.; three sons, John, of Lake Forest, Ill., Michael, of Lawrenceville, Ga., and Kevin, of Towson, Md.; a sister, Helen Leale Harper of Pelham, N.Y., and six grandchildren.

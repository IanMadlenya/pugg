
        Richard W. Freund, a retired vice president of Citibank, died Feb. 3 at his home in Clearwater, Fla. He was 79 years old.
        He died of complications of prostate cancer, his daughter, Susan, said.
        Mr. Freund joined the First National City Bank, as it was then known, in 1927 as a mail clerk. He had been with the bank's automation planning unit when he was appointed vice president in 1961. He assumed supervision of the bank's research and development department. He retired in 1976 and moved to Clearwater.
        Mr. Freund was born in Queens and was a graduate of New York University.
        During World War II, he served with the Army in the Philippines.
        His wife, the former Alice Lowney, died in 1975.
        He is survived by his daughter, Susan, of Manhattan.

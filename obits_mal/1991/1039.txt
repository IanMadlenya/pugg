
        George Pampel, a retired advertising excutive, died on Saturday at Greenwich Hospital in Connecticut. He was 86 years old and had lived in Greenwich since 1946.
        Mr. Pampel, who suffered from Parkinson's disease, died of pneumonia, a spokesman for the family said.
        He began his career managing a small chemical company in Seattle. Later he handled the Monsanto account for Gardner Advertising in St. Louis and New York City.
        He was executive vice president of Ben Sackheim Inc., a Manhattan advertising agency, and president of its successor, Pampel & Associates. He retired in 1971.
        Mr. Pampel was born in Livingston, Mont., and graduated from the University of Washington in 1926.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Marianne Slipper; a son, John, of Greenwich; a sister, Dorothy Murphy of Sedona, Ariz., and a granddaughter.

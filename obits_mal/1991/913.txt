
        Nicholas G. Marotta, retired president of the National Starch and Chemical Corporation, died on Monday at his home in Watchung, N.J. He was 61 years old.
        He died of cancer, a spokesman for the family said.
        He joined the company in 1959 as a food chemist after several years with Nabisco and Standard Brands. He was named president in 1984 and chief executive in 1985. He retired last year.
        Mr. Marotta also was a director of Unilever United States, an overseer of the foundation of the New Jersey Institute of Technology and a director of National Westminster Bank in Jersey City.
        He was a graduate of Long Island University, a director and vice chairman of the National Association of Manufacturers and belonged to several professional groups, among them the Chemical Manufacturers Association and the Institute of Food Technologists.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Antonia Scariano; four sons, Eugene C., of Weston, Conn., Dr. Charles J., of Chatham, N.J., Nicholas Jr., of Middlesex, N.J., and Gregory G., of Basking Ridge, N.J.; a daughter, Constance M. Spagnola of Watchung, and five grandchildren.


        William W. Parsons, an educator who was an Assistant Secretary of the Treasury under Presidents Harry S. Truman and Dwight D. Eisenhower, died Saturday at St. John's Hospital in Santa Monica, Calif. He was 79 years old and lived in Los Angeles.
        Mr. Parsons died of a stroke, his wife said.
        He held a number of government and education posts. Besides serving as Assistant Secretary of the Treasury from 1950 to 1959, he was also a vice president of the University of Hawaii, a vice president of the System Development Corporation, a not-for-profit organization working with Federal agencies, and an assistant regional manager of the United States General Accounting Office in San Francisco and Los Angeles.
        For the last 12 years he was a professor at the University of Southern California's School of Public Administration in Los Angeles.
        Mr. Parsons graduated from the University of Southern California and earned a master's degree in public administration from Syracuse University.
        He is survived by his wife, Janette Rainwater; three sons, Roger P. of Mammoth Lakes, Calif., Richard W. of Ventura, Calif., and Arthur S. of Northampton, Mass.; a stepson, Michael V. Rainwater of Redmond, Wash.; three brothers, Paul G. of San Marino, Calif., Robert H. of La Canada, Calif., and Edgar E. of Monroe, Ore.; a sister, Barbara M. Merrill, of San Marino, and two grandsons.

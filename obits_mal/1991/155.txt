
        William E. Little Sr., a manager of consumer goods trade shows, died yesterday at New York Hospital in Manhattan. He was 81 years old and had homes in Manhattan and in Redding, Conn.
        He died of cardiac arrest, a spokeswoman for the family said.
        Mr. Little was head of George Little Management, which was founded by his father in 1924. Among the major shows that he helped start were the National Stationery Show, the Atlantic City China and Glass Show, and the New York Lamp and Home Furnishing Show. He was also managing director of the Chicago Gift Show from 1957 to 1982. He was a trustee of the China Institute of America and was a member of the Japan Society, Friends of Asian Art at the Metropolitan Museum of Art and Friends of the Hood Museum at his alma mater, Dartmouth College.
        He is survived by his wife, Gladys; a son, William Jr.; a daughter, Sally Swanson of Honesdale, Pa.; a brother, Donald, of Glen Head, L.I., and six grandchildren.

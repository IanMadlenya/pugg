
        Lowell Hoskins, the president of Arbor Acres Farm, a poultry genetics company that supplies breeding stock to the poultry industry, died on Dec. 30 at his home in Glastonbury, Conn. He was 63 years old.
        He died of a heart attack, a family spokesman said.
        He had worked for Anderson Clayton & Company for 22 years in Brazil, Mexico and Peru before joining Arbor Acres in 1973. He was a 1948 graduate of the University of Texas.
        He is survived by his wife, Margaret; four daughters, Bonnie Hoskins-Llamas of Manhattan, Melissa Schreff of New Canaan, Conn., Laurel Hoskins of Ridgefield, Conn., and Jill Levine of Stratford, Conn; a son, David, of New Canaan; a brother, Hays, of Kerens, Tex.; and three grandchildren.

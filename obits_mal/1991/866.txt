
        Frank Gustine, an All-Star infielder for the Pittsburgh Pirates in 1946, 1947 and 1948, died of a heart attack on Monday in Davenport, Iowa. He was 71 years old.
        Mr. Gustine was in Iowa for the first voyage of the President, a casino riverboat owned by John E. Connelly, his partner in ownership of the Sheraton Inn at Station Square in Pittsburgh.
        Mr. Gustine played for the Pirates from 1939 until 1948 and then played one season for the Chicago Cubs and part of another for the St. Louis Browns. He had a career batting average of .265 with 38 home runs and 480 runs batted in.

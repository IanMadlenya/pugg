
        Robert Sansone, former president of Mattel USA, a toy company based in El Segundo, Calif., died Monday at his home in Marina del Rey, Calif. He was 49 years old.
        Mr. Sansone died of cancer, said a spokesman for the family.
        Before joining Mattel in 1987, Mr. Sansone had been associated with General Foods in the New York area for 24 years. He was named vice president of General Foods and president of its Pet Foods Division in 1979, and was group vice president of customer support services when he left the company in 1987.
        In 1969 Mr. Sansone served as a White House Fellow and special assistant to Secretary of Commerce Maurice Stans, working on minority enterprise development.
        A native New Yorker, Mr. Sansone was a graduate of Stuyvesant High School and Columbia University. He received a bachelor's degree in 1961 and an M.B.A. from the Columbia Graduate School of Business in 1963.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Cynthia Sokol; three children from a former marriage: a son, Robert B. of Marina Del Rey; a daughter, Susan of Ossining, N.Y.; a brother, Paul of Manhattan; and two sisters, Joan , also of Manhattan, and Carol of Arlington, Va.

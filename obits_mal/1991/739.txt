
        Dr. Irwin Hanover, a podiatrist who founded and published the Journal of Current Podiatric Medicine, died on Sunday at the Orlando Regional Medical Center in Florida. He was 75 years old and lived in Orlando.
        He died of heart failure, his family said.
        Dr. Hanover was born in Brooklyn and lived there until he retired in 1976. He was a graduate of the First Institute of Podiatry and practiced in New York for more than three decades. He founded the Journal of Current Podiatric Medicine in 1950.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Sylvia Reisman; two sons, Douglas, of Manhattan, and Roy, of Englishtown, N.J.; a daugther, Michelle Winnick of Rosewell, Ga., and six grandchildren.

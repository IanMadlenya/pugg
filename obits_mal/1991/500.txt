
        Joseph H. Snyder, a management consultant, died on Tuesday at his home in Manhattan. He was 70 years old.
        He died of a brain tumor, his family said.
        Mr. Snyder, who in 1959 founded the Color Corporation of America in Tampa, Fla., a color photographic laboratory, held a number of patents in photography.
        In 1972 he founded Young Entrepreneurs of America to encourage leadership among young adults and in 1989 he founded the Foundation for Leadership Quality and Ethics Practice to foster development of future leaders in education and business.
        He was born in Brownsville, Pa., attended the University of Pittsburgh and was a captain in the Army in World War II.
        He is survived by two daughters, Sally Brasher of Mascotte, Fla., and Vicki Toye of Mascotte, Fla.

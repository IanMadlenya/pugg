
        Rudolph Rella, the general foreman of the pressroom of The New York Times, died on Friday at his home in Sayreville, N.J. He was 62 years old.
        Mr. Rella died of pancreatic cancer, said the director of the pressroom, James Fitzhenry.
        He began work in the pressroom in 1965, became a foreman in 1980, was promoted to assistant general foreman in 1986 and general foreman of the pressroom in 1990.
        He was a member of the Printing Pressman's Union, Local No. 2, since 1948.
        Surviving are his wife, Dorothy; two sons, Rudy, of Freehold, N.J., and Steven, of Sayreville; two daughters, Marie Carstens of Lopatcong, N.J., and Diana, of Sayreville; three brothers, Vito, of Hempstead, L.I., Alfred, of Seaford, L.I., and Mauro, of Newbern, N.C.; two sisters, Fin Fiorentino of Port St. Lucie, Fla., and Grace Rella of Poughkeepsie, N.Y., and three grandchildren.

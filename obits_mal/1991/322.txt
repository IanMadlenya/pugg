
        Dr. M. William Voss, a Navy officer who served as a White House physician, died Wednesday at his home in Bethesda, Md. He was 67 years old.
        He died of cancer, said his wife, Helen.
        Dr. Voss was a Navy fighter pilot in World War II before graduating from the University of Colorado and the University of Buffalo Medical School.
        He re-entered the Navy and served at hospitals in Bethesda, in San Diego and in Nice, France. From 1966 until he retired from the Navy as a captain in the mid-1970's he was a physician in the White House.
        From then until his death, he taught at the University of Maryland Medical School.
        In addition to his wife, he is survived by two daughters, Dr. Susan Voss of Bethesda, Md., and Dr. Sally Krieger of San Francisco; a son, Dr. Stephen Voss of Alexandria, Va., and one grandchild.

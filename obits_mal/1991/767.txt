
        H. Hunter Williams, a retired New York Telephone executive, died on Friday at United Hospital in Port Chester, N.Y. He was 82 years old and lived in Rye, N.Y.
        He died of a heart attack, his family said.
        Mr. Williams was born in Plainfield, N.J., and was a graduate of the Hotchkiss School and Yale University. He joined New York Telephone in 1930 and rose to assistant controller and assistant vice president before he retired in 1971. He had also worked with Michigan Bell and A.T.& T.
        He is survived by two sons, Howard H. Williams III of Washington and Thomas, of Rye; a brother, C. Dickerman Williams of New Haven, and seven grandchildren.


        James R. Bartsch, an inventor and legal consultant, died on Friday in Manchester, Mass. He was 58 years old and lived in Gloucester, Mass.
        He died of injuries suffered in an automobile accident, said his sister, Jean Higgs of Cherry Hill, N.J.
        In 1967, Mr. Bartsch received a patent for a solid molded golf ball. He sold the patent to the Princeton Chemical Research Company.
        He was a graduate of the Massachusetts Institute of Technology and the Nova University Law Center, and he operated a sports shop on Nantucket from 1968 to 1972. More recently, he was a legal consultant.
        Besides his sister, he is survived by his wife, Brenda; five children from a previous marriage, Cary, of Grenoble, France, Philip, of New Haven, Vt., David, of Seattle, Adam, of Chicago, and Jessica Hartigan of Ocala, Fla.; a brother, Robert, of Seminole, Fla., and two grandchildren.

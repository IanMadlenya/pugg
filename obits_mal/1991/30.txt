
        Frederick E. Croxton, a statistician, educator and author, died on Thursday at the Lakeland Health Care Nursing Center in Florida. He was 91 years old and moved to Lakeland in 1987 from Leonia, N.J.
        He died of complications of a stroke, said his son, Fred, of Mulberry, Fla.
        Dr. Croxton taught statistics at Columbia University for 38 years and was named professsor emeritus in 1964. He joined the faculty in 1926 after graduating from Columbia with a Ph.D. and was named a full professor in 1944, a post he held for 20 years. He also served in a number of administrative posts, including interim dean of admissions in the early 1950's.
        He was the co-author of several widely used college textbooks on statistics, including "Practical Business Statistics," and "Applied General Statistics," both published by Prentice-Hall.
        Dr. Croxton was a member of several professional organizations, including the American Association for the Advancement of Science.
        Besides his son, he is survived by a brother, Frank, of Columbus, Ohio, and two granddaughters.


        Thomas W. Gerber, editor of The Concord Monitor for 16 years, died Friday at his home in Concord, N.H. He was 69 years old.
        He died of prostate cancer, his daughter Cheryl said.
        Mr. Gerber was editor in chief of the Concord newspaper from 1967 to 1983.
        He served in the Army Air Force in World War II, earning an Air Medal for his service as a bomber navigator. He graduated from Dartmouth College in 1948 and was editor in chief of the student paper, The Dartmouth.
        In 1954, he was awarded the American Newspaper Guild's Heywood Broun Award for articles on Boston charity rackets.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Electra Bilmazes; two daughters, Cheryl, of Brooklyn, and Linda, of Kingston, N.Y., and one grandchild.


        John G. Winchester, a retired New York banker and investment adviser, died on Wednesday in Clark House, a nursing home in Westwood, Mass. He was 89 years old and was a longtime resident of Juno Beach, Fla.
        He died of complications from pneumonia, said his lawyer, Christopher C. Angell in Manhattan.
        Mr. Winchester, who was graduated from Harvard College, was with the Fiduciary Trust Company of New York from 1940 until 1966, when he retired as a senior vice president. Over the years he also worked with the W. R. Compton Company, Gammack & Company and Tucker, Anthony and R. L. Day.
        He is survived by his wife, Julia; three sons, Charles A., Gordon and John F.; two stepchildren, Barbara Coolidge and Chester Bowles Jr.; 10 grandchildren and six great-grandchildren.


        Benjamin Franklin Baer, chairman of the United States Parole Commission, died April 9 at Washington Hospital Center in Washington. He was 73 years old and lived in Rockville, Md.
        He died after surgery for a heart ailment, a spokeswoman for the family said.
        Mr. Baer joined the commission in 1982. It is an independent agency within the Justice Department that approves or denies parole applications. He was also a member of the United States Sentencing Commission and had served on President John F. Kennedy's Juvenile Delinquency Commission.
        He began his career in 1942 as director of a Los Angeles County probation camp. From 1954 to 1960 he was associate warden of San Quentin prison. He joined the Parole Commission in California in 1974 and moved to Washington in 1982.
        Mr. Baer was a member of the advisory board of the National Institute of Corrections.
        A native of Peoria, Ill., he was a graduate of San Diego State University and received anM.A. in social work from the University of Southern California.
        He is survived by his wife, Frances; two sons, Dr. Marc of Holland, Mich., and Bartley B. of San Francisco; a daughter, Meridith Jan Baer of Los Angeles, and four grandchildren.


        Justin V. McCarthy, a former Long Island businessman who was active in Brookhaven and Islip Townships, died on Wednesday at his home in Stuart, Fla. He was 72 years old.
        Mr. McCarthy died of cancer, his son, Christopher, said.
        A resident of Bayport, L.I., for many years, he was the owner of a shellfish company, J.& M. Shellfish Inc., operating out of Long Island and Chincoteague, Va., in the 1970's.
        A staunch Republican, he was industrial commissioner for both Brookhaven and Islip Townships in the late 1950's and 60's, and was Islip's commissioner of marine resources in the 1970's.
        In the early 1980's, he was senior aide to the Suffolk County Legislature. He retired in 1983.
        Mr. McCarthy enlisted in the Army in 1940 and served throughout World War II until 1946. He received several awards, among them the Bronze Star.
        A widower, heis survived by two daughters, Marie Lynch of Sharpton, Md., and Ruth Ann Frucht of Northport, L.I.; seven sons, Justin Jr. of Virginia Beach, Va., Malcolm of Islip, L.I., John of Brookside, N.J., Peter of West Islip, L.I., Christopher of Amityville, L.I., Mark of Sayville, L.I., and Matthew, also of Amityville; and 20 grandchildren.


        Harold Klein, a retired psychiatrist, died on Wednesday at Mount Sinai Medical Center. He was 80 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        He died of a heart attack, said his wife, the former Marguerite Perrin.
        Dr. Klein, who was also a pediatrician, specialized in child and adolescent care. He joined the staff of the Jewish Hospital in Brooklyn in the 1960's and later headed its adolescent clinic. He was a life fellow of the American Psychiatric Association and a member of the Child and Adolescent Psychiatric Association. He retired in 1987.
        Dr. Klein was born in Manhattan, graduated from New York University and received a medical degree from the Royal College of Physicians and Surgeons in Edinburgh.
        Besides his wife, he is survived by a son, Dr. Michael L. Klein, a professor of English at Queens University of Belfast in Northern Ireland; a sister, Rose Schnitzer of Manhattan, and a grandson.

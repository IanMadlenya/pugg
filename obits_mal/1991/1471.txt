
        Ralph Friedman, founder of Magno Sound and Video in Manhattan, a concern that performed technical services for the film industry, died on Tuesday at the Memorial Sloan-Kettering Cancer Center. He was 66 years old and lived in Rye, N.Y.
        He died of complications from cancer of the colon, his son Robert said.
        Mr. Friedman founded his business in the early 1960's after starting in the entertainment industry as a sound engineer for Alan Funt's "Candid Camera" television series. His company did recording, editing and mixing functions for the film industry.
        Mr. Friedman was a graduate of Long Island University and received a law degree from the New York Law School. He was a corporal in the Army infantry in World War II.
        Besides his son Robert, who lives in Manhattan, he leaves his wife, the former Sonya Dash; another son, David, of Manhattan; two daughters, Barbara, of Manhattan, and Carol, of Sunnyvale, Calif., and a sister, Pearl Silvers of Palm Beach, Fla.


        George Sim Johnston, president and chairman of Scudder, Stevens & Clark, a New York investment management concern, died on Tuesday at Columbia Presbyterian Medical Center. He was 66 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        He died of cancer, a spokesman for the family said.
        He joined the investment concern in 1948, became a partner in 1957 and was elected president in 1970. He was named chairman in 1988. His career in investment management spanned four decades, during which he was a public director of the New York Stock Exchange.
        Mr. Johnston, an advocate of investing in other nations' securities, helped establish in 1953 the first international mutual fund in the United States registered with the Securities and Exchange Commission -- the Scudder Fund of Canada, now known as the Scudder International Fund.  Brotherhood Award
        He represented the United States on the United Nations investments committee and was a member of the Council on Foreign Relations.
        He received the Brotherhood Award of the National Conference of Christians and Jews in 1989 and the Man of Vision Award of the Eye-Bank for Sight Restoration in 1990.
        Among his various charitable activities, he was a board member of the New York Society for the Prevention of Cruelty to Children for 23 years, and was a member of the finance committee of the Southampton Fresh Air Home for Crippled Children. He was also a board member of the Visiting Nurse Service of New York, the Boys Club of New York and the Southampton Hospital.
        Mr. Johnston, a native of Memphis, graduated in 1948 from Yale College. He served in the Army during both World War II and the Korean War.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Cynthia Cogswell; four sons, Sim, William, Scott and Bart, all of Manhattan, and five grandchildren.

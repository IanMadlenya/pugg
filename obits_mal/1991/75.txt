
        Dr. Arthur J. Harriman, who was director of research for Thomas J. Lipton Inc., the food company, for 25 years before he retired in 1971, died on Sunday in Freehold, N.J. He was 84 years old and lived in Bay Head, N.J.
        He died of heart disease, said a spokesman for the Neary Funeral Home in Manasquan, N.J.
        Dr. Harriman, who had been a longtime resident of Glen Ridge, N.J., was born in Washington and held a degree in chemical engineering and master's and doctoral degrees in chemistry, all from Catholic University.
        From 1931 to 1942 he taught at Catholic University, establishing its Division of Biochemistry and holding the posts of registrar and secretary of the faculty. In World War II he was a Navy commander and served on the War Production Board.
        He joined Lipton in 1946 and headed its research activities until his retirement. The company, based in Englewood Cliffs, N.J., makes soup, salad dressing and other food products.
        Dr. Harriman is survived by two sons, Arthur, of Holmdel, N.J., and James, of Atlanta; two daughters, Patricia Foley of Bay Head and Ann Hill of Nashville; 17 grandchildren, and nine great-grandchldren.

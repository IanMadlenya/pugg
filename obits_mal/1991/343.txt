
        J. Franklin Ray Jr., who served in several United Nations and State Department posts after World War II, died on Friday at his home in Santa Fe, N.M. He was 85 years old.
        He died of heart failure, his family said.
        Mr. Ray was director of the Far East office of the United Nations Relief and Rehabilitation Administration from 1945 to 1947, supervising welfare operations in Korea, the Philippines, India, Australia, New Zealand and China.
        In 1948 and 1949 he was a member of the American delegation to the Far Eastern Commission and from 1951 to 1953 he served in United States and United Nations posts dealing with Korean economic development.
        Mr. Ray is survived by three sons, Dr. Jefferson F. Ray 3d, of Marshfield, Wis., Louis, of Alexandria, Va., and Lester, of Princeton, N.J.; two daughters, Gerda, of San Francisco, and Anne, of Santa Fe; a brother, Hermon, of Honolulu, and 10 grandchildren.

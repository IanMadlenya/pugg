
        Paul D. Catalano, a specialist in computer data storage, died on Jan. 13 at North Shore Hospital in Manhasset, L.I. He was 60 years old and lived in Manhasset.
        He died of cancer, his family said.
        Mr. Catalano, who was born in Manhattan, graduated from Principia College in Elsah, Ill.
        He was chief executive officer of Vital Records, of Flagtown, N.J., and Vital Archives, of Elizabeth, N.J. In 1986, he opened Dataport, a million-cubic-foot storage center beneath the World Trade Center. With his wife, Hope, Mr. Catalano also published North Shore Magazine on Long Island.
        In addition to his wife, the former Hope Giordano, he is survived by theee daughters, Dawn Hale of Clinton, N.J., Joy Hepburn of Denville, N.J., and Deirdre Catalano of Manhasset, and four grandchildren.

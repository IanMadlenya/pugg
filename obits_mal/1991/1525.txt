
        Theodore Wilson, a television and movie actor who appears in the new Mel Brooks film "Life Stinks," died on Sunday at Cedars-Sinai Medical Center here. He was 47 years old.
        His sister-in-law, Naomi Pringle, said he had suffered a stroke.
        Mr. Wilson played Earl the postman in the situation comedy "That's My Mama" and appeared in many other television shows, including "Crazy Like a Fox," "Good Times," "The Redd Foxx Show," "Roll Out," "You Can't Take it With You," "Quantum Leap," "L.A. Law" and "Gabriel's Fire."
        In addition to "Life Stinks," his film credits include "That's My Life," "A Fine Mess," "The River Niger," "Carny" and "The Hunter."
        He was a member of the Negro Ensemble Company in New York City.


        E. L. McNeely, the former president and chairman of Wickes Companies, a diversified retail concern, died Sunday at the Green Hospital Cancer Clinic-Scripps Clinic in La Jolla, Calif. He was 72 years old and lived in La Jolla.
        He died of cancer, said a son, Kevin.
        Mr. McNeely attended colleges in Missouri and graduated from Northeast Missouri State University in 1940. After serving in the Navy in World War II, he worked for Montgomery Ward & Company until 1964 as a merchandise manager.
        He then joined Wickes Corporation, based in California, which manufactures and distributes automotive equipment and formerly specialized in lumber and building supplies. In 1966 he rose from director of marketing to senior vice president for retailing. He became a director in 1965 and was president and chief executive officer from 1969 to 1974, when he was named chairman. He retired in 1982. He later served as chairman of Oak Industries.
        He is survived by his wife, Alice, a daughter, Sandra Gressl of Wichita, Kan.; three sons, Gregory of Milwaukee, Mark of Seattle and Kevin of Dallas, and five grandchildren.

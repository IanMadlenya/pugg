
        Robert Strunsky, a former director of corporate affairs for CBS Inc., died on Wednesday at his home in Kingston, N.J. He was 84 years old.
        He died of cancer, a son-in-law said.
        Mr. Strunsky joined CBS in 1944 as an advertising copywriter. He was later a corporate spokesman for the company, director of press information and director of corporate affairs. He was also a speechwriter for several top CBS executives, including the company's chairman, William S. Paley.
        During the 1950's Mr. Strunsky worked for a short time as director of advertising for Simon & Schuster and was with the Ford Foundation and the Institute for Advanced Study in Princeton. He later returned to CBS. After graduating from Amherst College in 1927, he worked for several newspapers, including The New York Sun.
        He is survived by his wife, Martha; three daughters, Mary Wisnovsky of Princeton, Martha Ilic of Paris and Jane Wiseman of Arlington, Mass.; three grandchildren and one great-grandchild. His daughters are from his marriage to the former Louise Culver, who died in 1974.

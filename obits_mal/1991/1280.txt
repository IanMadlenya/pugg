
        Reginald T. Blauvelt Jr., longtime chief executive officer of the Lincoln Mayflower Moving and Storage Company of Orange and Madison, N.J., died on Saturday at the Medical Center in Princeton, N.J. He was 85 years old and lived in Hightstown, N.J., and Lake Wales, Fla.
        He died of advanced cancer, a family member said.
        Mr. Blauvelt was president of the family-owned moving company from 1943 to 1985, when he became its board chairman. He also founded Reginald T. Blauvelt Jr. Appraisals Inc., which appraised American antiques, and he was a founding member of the Appraisers Association of America.
        A native of East Orange, N.J., he graduated from the Newark Academy. He was a trustee of both the academy and the Beard School, a director of East Orange General Hospital and a member of the acquisitions committee of the Newark Museum.
        Surviving are his wife of 56 years, the former Brita Neilson; a son, Reginald T. 3d, of Chatham, N.J.; a daughter, Lisa Blauvelt-Weil of Tours, France, and four grandchildren.

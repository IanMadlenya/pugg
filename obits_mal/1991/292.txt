
        Walter Joyce, a magazine publishing consultant, died on Saturday at the Hackensack Medical Center in New Jersey. He was 58 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        He died of lung cancer, his family said.
        At his death, Mr. Joyce was president of Walter Joyce Inc., a consulting firm he founded in 1984, and was a columnist for Folio magazine. He had previously been senior vice president for promotion for Playboy and promotion director of Fortune, Psychology Today, Careers Today and The Saturday Evening Post.
        He was born in Flushing, Queens, and was a graduate of Rutgers.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Edith Locke, and two daughters, Susan, of Boston and Cathleen, of Providence, R.I.


        Norbert A. Bogdan, an investment banking executive, died on Thursday at the Berkshire Medical Center in Pittsfield, Mass. He was 87 years old and lived in Manhattan and in Tyringham, Mass.
        He died of lung disease, his family said.
        Mr. Bogdan, who was born in Abattsia, Italy, and was raised in Vienna, studied at the London School of Economics before coming to the United States in 1923, where he joined the International Acceptance Bank. From 1937 to 1948 he was a vice president of the J. Henry Schroder Banking Corporation in charge of business in continental Europe, South America and Asia.
        From 1948 to 1953 he was vice president and treasurer of Ford International, after which he was with General Dynamics before becoming president of his own investment banking firm, N. A. Bogdan & Company, in Manhattan.
        He served in World War II as an Army Air Forces officer and was with the Allied Control Council for Germany and later an adviser to the American Military Government in Japan. He also served in several advisory posts with NATO.
        An accomplished musician, Mr. Bogdan was a leader of the Musicians Emergency Fund.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Barbara Cates; two stepdaughters, Julia Marks of Buffalo and Barbara Washburn of Santa Monica, Calif.; a stepson, Louis Wilkin of Berkeley, Calif., nine grandchildren, and one great-grandchild.

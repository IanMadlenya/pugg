
        Harry Lancaster Towe, a former Congressman and deputy attorney general of New Jersey, died on Friday at his home in Lakewood, N.J. He was 92 years old.
        Mr. Towe, a Republican, was first elected to the House of Representatives from the Ninth District in New Jersey in 1942. A member of the Armed Services Committee, he served in four succeeding Congresses until he resigned in September 1951 to become special deputy attorney general for Bergen County, a prosecutorial post. He held that position until 1953, when he resigned to enter private law practice.
        Mr. Towe devoted much of his life to public affairs, starting out as a United States commissioner in 1930 and serving as assistant prosecutor of Bergen County in 1931.
        He was also executive director of the Associated Railroads of New Jersey and secretary and general counsel of the publishing firm of Medical Economics Inc. in Oradel, N.J.
        Born in Jersey City, Mr. Towe attended the United States Naval Academy and graduated from the New Jersey Law School in 1925.
        He is survived by one daughter, Virginia T. Beck of Mantoloking, N.J.; two sons, Harry B. of New Orleans and Jerome D. of Hockessin, Del.; a brother, Jerome D. and a sister Isabel T. Bates, both of Southbury, Conn.; nine grandchildren, and 15 great-grandchildren.

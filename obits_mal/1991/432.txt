
        Raymond Paul Wallace, an engineer and registered patent agent, died on Thursday at Community Hospital in Montclair, N.J. He was 81 years old and lived in Montclair.
        Mr. Wallace, who had graduate degrees from the University of California, the Metallic Engineering Institute and Kensington University, had still been active as a consultant.
        In 1945, he received an atomic energy award from the War Department. After working as a nuclear engineer, he became chief patent engineer with the Mycalex Corporation of America. He joined the Curtiss-Wright Corporation in 1957, where he worked until he retired in 1975 to go into private practice.
        There are no known survivors.


        Joseph Paul Young, retired senior vice president and director of circulation for Conde Nast Publications, died on Monday at his home in White Plains. He was 86 years old.
        He died of Parkinson's disease, a spokesman for the family said.
        Mr. Young was with the publishing company from 1956 until 1983, when he retired. Earlier, he had been assistant director of circulation for Time Inc. for 20 years. He also served 10 years on the magazine directors advisory committee of the Audit Bureau of Circulation.
        He was born in Providence, R.I., and graduated from Presbyterian College in Clinton, S.C., in 1926.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Frances Lally; two daughters, Margaret Perkins of Tampa, Fla., and Anne Fitzpatrick of Weston, Conn.; two sons, Douglas, of Syracuse, and Robert, of Rahway, N.J.; nine grandchildren and four great-grandchildren.

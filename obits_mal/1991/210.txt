
        John G. Brookhuis, a chemical industry executive, died Jan. 2 at Massachusetts General Hospital in Boston. He was 69 years old and lived in Boston.
        He died of a bleeding ulcer, a family spokeswoman said.
        Mr. Brookhuis, a former resident of Mendham, N,J., was president of the American Hoechst Corporation from 1970 to 1982 and then was chairman until he retired in 1987.
        Born in the Netherlands, he was educated in that country at a textile school in Enschede. After World War II he joined his family's textile company and later became an agent of Farbwerke Hoechst, now Hoechst AG, a major German chemical manufacturer. In 1960 he was named manager of Hoechst's Netherlands operations and in 1970 he became president of the American Hoechst Corporation in Somerville, N.J.
        He is survived by two daughters, Jenneken Mechti of Abeldoorn, the Netherlands, and Annemarie Corver of Lasne-Chapelle-St. Lambert, Belgium; a brother, Karel, of Enschede, and six grandchildren.


        David Janvier Scott, a marketing consultant and former advertising executive, died on Thursday at the University of Pennsylvania Hospital. He was 68 years old and lived in Manhattan and in New Hope, Pa.
        Mr. Scott died following heart surgery, a spokesman for the family said.
        He had been an executive vice president at Ogilvy & Mather Inc., a Manhattan advertising agency, for more than 10 years and was a member of the company's board of directors. He retired in 1986.
        Mr. Scott began his advertising career at R. H. Macy & Company in San Francisco. He also worked for Scott Paper Company and several small agencies before moving to New York to join Ted Bates & Company as an executive vice president and creative director.
        He left Princeton University at the beginning of World War II to enlist in the Army, serving with the 36th Texas Infantry Division in North Africa. He attained the rank of captain and received the Bronze Star. He later served for 18 months during the Korean War.
        Mr. Scott was born in Fort Huachuca, Ariz., and graduated from Princeton in 1947.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Blanid Ennis; three daughters, Sheila, Bridgett , and Nora of Manhattan; and two sons, S. Reynolds and Peter, also of Manhattan.

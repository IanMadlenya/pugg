
        Granger Tripp, a retired advertising executive, died on Wednesday at Northern Westchester Hospital Center in Mount Kisco, N.Y. He was 68 years old and lived in Mount Kisco.
        He died of colon cancer, his wife said.
        Mr. Tripp joined J. Walter Thompson in 1953 as a copywriter and spent his entire career at the advertising agency. He retired as a senior vice president and creative director in 1984 and became a consultant to the agency and to the Ford Motor Company.
        Mr. Tripp was a graduate of Union College and also studied at Yale University. He served in the Army in Europe during World War II.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Kathleen Martin; four daughters, Kathleen, of Manhattan, Rosemary, of Paris, Valerie, of Silver Spring, Md., and Suzanne, of Seattle; a son, Granger, of San Francisco, and six grandchildren.

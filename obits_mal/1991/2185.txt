
        Leon M. Nad, a national expert on taxes and a retired partner of the Price Waterhouse accounting firm in New York City, died on Thursday at St. Barnabas Medical Center in Livingston, N.J. A resident of West Orange, N.J., Mr. Nad was 63 years old.
        He died of complications of cancer and an aneurysm, his family said.
        Mr. Nad often testified before Congress on tax policy issues, and his views were widely quoted.
        Born in Houston, he was a graduate of Rice University. He received a master's degree in business administration from the University of Pennsylvania and taught there for three years. In 1951, he joined the accounting firm of Ernst & Whinney.
        In 1956, he moved to Price Waterhouse and remained with the firm until his retirement in 1988. He became a partner in 1964, was appointed national director of technical tax services in 1978, and became associate vice chairman for tax consulting in 1985.
        He is survived by his wife, Marcia; a daughter, Charlotte, of Manhattan; his father, Irving of Houston, and a brother, Abraham of Wesport, Conn.

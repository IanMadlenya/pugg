
        Robert Kazmayer, a former minister who became a business analyst and publisher, died at his home in Mariposa, Calif., on Aug. 4.
        He was 82 years old and died of lung cancer, his son, Robert, said.
        Mr. Kazmayer, a longtime resident of Rochester, founded Kazmayer Seminar Tours, published Things to Watch and Watch For, a newsletter for business executives, and lectured widely.
        He was born in Rush, N.Y., attended the University of Rochester and Colgate Rochester Divinity School and was ordained as a Methodist minister.
        In the 1930's, he was pastor of the Lewiston Avenue Methodist Episcopal Church, now Wesley United Methodist Church and at the Monroe Avenue Methodist Episcopal Church, both in Rochester.
        Besides his son, who lives in Huntington, L.I., he is survived by his third wife, Doris.

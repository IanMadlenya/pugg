
        John Underhill Crandell, a former publisher of Money magazine, died yesterday at his home in Manchester, Vt. He was 74 years old.
        He died of cancer, a family spokeswoman said.
        Mr. Crandell, a 1940 graduate of Dartmouth, joined Time Inc. in 1954 as an advertising salesman in New York for Time magazine. In 1961 he became sales manager. In 1963, he was named advertising sales manager at Life and was assistant publisher before being named advertising sales director in 1966.
        He was named associate publisher of Sports Illustrated in 1969, returned to Life as associate publisher in 1972 and a year later became national sales manager at Money. He served as publisher of Money from 1977 to 1982.
        He is survived by his wife, Betty; two sons, John Jr., of Ponte Vedra, Fla. and Jeffrey, of Brentwood, Calif.; a daughter, Cathy Long of Summit, N.J., and a brother, Edwin of Rye, N.Y.

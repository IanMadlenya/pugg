
        Dr. Gerald J. Lustig, the former director of cardiothoracic surgery at Staten Island University Hospital, died there Monday. He was 70 years old and lived in Todt Hill, S.I.
        He died of cancer, his wife said.
        Dr. Lustig, a former president of the New York Society of Surgeons, was on the New York State Board of Regents. He was a 1948 graduate of the University of Colorado Medical School and did his internship at Colorado General Hospital in Denver and residencies at hospitals in Philadelphia and New York.
        He began in private practice on Staten Island in 1958 and from 1975 to 1980 was director of surgery at Staten Island University Hospital. He was also an assistant professor at Downstate Medical Center in Brooklyn and past president of the Richmond County Medical Society and of the New York State Federation of Physicians and Surgeons. He retired last year.
        He is survived by his wife, Phyllis; a son, Gerald Jr., of Staten Island; two stepsons, Ross Weinberg of Clifton, Va., and Mitchell Weinberg of Staten Island; a brother, William, of Springfield, Ohio; and a sister, Jean Reed of Boynton Beach, Fla.


        Henry Charles Bayer, managing partner and co-founder of Mercury Securities, a Manhattan investment firm, died on Monday at Southampton (L.I.) Hospital. He was 48 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        He died of a heart attack, his family said.
        Mr. Bayer graduated from Cornell University and had an M.B.A. from Pace University. He started his career in 1964 at L. F. Rothschild & Company, a Wall Street investment concern, where he became a partner at the age of 27. He helped establish Mercury in 1979.
        Mr. Bayer is survived by his wife, the former Janice Bridell; two children, Bradley and Hilary; his parents, Molly and Merwin Bayer of Manhattan, and a sister, Virginia Slifka, also of Manhattan.

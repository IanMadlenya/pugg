
        Edward J. Geng, a former Federal Reserve official and later president of Fundamental Brokers Inc., died on Sunday at North Shore University Hospital in Manhasset, L.I. He was 59 years old and lived in North Merrick, L.I.
        He died of lung cancer, his family said.
        Mr. Geng was a native of Queens and a graduate of St. John's University with a master's degree in business administration from New York University. He joined the Federal Reserve Bank of New York in 1957. Two years later he went to Washington as a special assistant to the Secretary of the Treasury.
        In 1971 he left Government service to work for Paine Webber and later for Hilliard Farber & Company and Baer American Banking. In 1982 he rejoined the Federal Reserve Bank as senior vice president in charge of government securities surveillance.
        Mr. Geng returned to the private sector in 1986 as president of Fundamental Brokers, which deals in Treasury securities.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Arlene Fuchs; three sons, Alan, of Staten Island, Glenn, of Long Beach, L.I., and Scott, of Red Hook, N.Y.; a sister, Joan Brown of Park Ridge, N.J., and two grandchildren.

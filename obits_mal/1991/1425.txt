
        Lucius Root Eastmen 4th, a venture capitalist who was president from 1961 to 1988 of the Lucius and Eva Eastman Fund, a philanthropy founded by his parents, died on Saturday at his home in Manhattan. He was 78 years old.
        He died of emphysema, a family member said.
        Mr. Eastman invested in emerging high-technologyj companies and was a former chairman and longtime trustee of the World Monuments Fund, a non-profit group that restores historic sites around the world.
        A native of Upper Montclair, N.J., he was a graduate of Deerfield Academy and Amherst College. In World War II, he served in the Office of Strategic Services in North Africa and Europe and was awarded a Bronze Star.
        He is survived by a son, Lucius 5th, of Cupertino, Calif.; three daughters, Beatrice, of the Bronx, Jennifer, of Westwood,Mass., and Susan, of Manhattan, and three grandchildren.

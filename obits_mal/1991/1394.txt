
        Saul Fromkes, a New York lawyer who was founder and president of the City Title Insurance Company, died on Saturday at his home in Manhattan. He was 83 years old.
        He died of cancer, a spokesman for the family said.
        With his brother, Otto, Mr. Fromkes founded the company in Manhattan in 1928. He was president of the company at his death.
        He was a member of the board of the New York Medical College; a member of the Insurance Board of New York State; a director of the Empire Mutual Insurance Company in Manhattan and vice chairman of the board of St. John's University. The St. John's University law school building, Fromkes Hall, is named in his honor.
        He was a member of the New York State Supreme Court Committee on character and fitness for the bar. He was a founding trustee of the Inner City Scholarship Fund of the Roman Catholic Archdiocese of New York.
        Mr. Fromkes received the Order of Merit of the Republic of Italy in 1982 for strengthening relations between the two countries.
        He was a graduate of St. John's Law School in 1928 and was admitted to the New York State Bar in 1930.
        His wife, the former Amelia Blanch, died in 1975.
        He is survived by his brother, Otto, of North Plainfield, N.J.

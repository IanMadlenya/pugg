
        Dr. H. Norman Runsdorf, a doctor in Brooklyn for more than 50 years, died on Wednesday at his home in the Midwood section.
        He was 82 years old and died of a heart attack, said a daughter.
        Dr. Runsdorf received his medical training at the University of Edinburgh. He was affiliated with Kings County Hospital, Kingsborough Psychiatric Center and Brooklyn-Caledonian Hospital, where in the 1960's he was president of the medical board. He also taught for many years at Downstate Hospital Medical School.
        During World War II, he was a major in the Army Medical Corps in North Africa and Italy.
        He is survived by his wife, Elizabeth; four daughters, Blithe, of Pittsburgh, Merry Mendelson of Rockville, Md., Lynn Adams of Tarpon Springs, Fla., and Helen Runsdorf of Manhattan; a son, Joseph, of Manhattan, and four grandchildren.

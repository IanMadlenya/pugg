
        Howard Lane Rubendall, a clergyman who was the retired president of Dickinson College in Carlisle, Pa., died Saturday at his home in Naples, Fla. He was 80 years old.
        He died of respiratory failure resulting from cancer, a spokesman for the college said.
        Dr. Rubendall was president of Dickinson from 1961 to 1975, when enrollment rose by 60 percent and 20 buildings were added to the campus. He also started a governance system for the institution that is still in use.
        A graduate of Dickinson and Union Theological Seminary, Dr. Rubendall was chairman of the religion department at The Hill School in Pottstown, Pa., and was later pastor of the First Presbyterian Church in Albany. For next 17 years before heading Dickinson he was president of Northfield Schools, a corporation operating the Mount Hermon School for Boys and the Northfield School for Girls in Massachusetts.
        He is survived by his wife, Carolyn; two daughters, Linda Fletcher of Mobile, Ala., and Suzanne Faudon of Lyons, France; a brother, Robert of Halifax, Pa.; a sister, Evelyn Barnhart of Sarasota, Fla.; six grandchildren and three great-grandchildren.

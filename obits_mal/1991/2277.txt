
        William A. W. Krebs, a retired vice president of Arthur D. Little Inc., died Nov. 26 at Massachussetts General Hospital in Boston. He was 75 years old and lived in West Newton, Mass.
        He died of colon cancer, his family said.
        Mr. Krebs, an expert on economic development, was with Little, the Cambridge, Mass., consulting concern, from 1956 to 1986. He went on missions to dozens of countries as a consultant to governments on the management of science and technology and in other roles.
        In a 1963 interview, he said he liked to think of Little "as a bridge between American technology and societies overseas wishing to become industrialized."
        He also performed tasks for the State Department and other organs of the United States Government.
        Mr. Krebs was born in Ebensburg, Pa., studied at Yale College and Yale Law School and was in Naval intelligence in World War II.
        He was married in 1938 to Annette Claire Boucher. She died in 1989.
        He is survived by a sister, Betty Payne of Tulsa, Okla.; two daughters, Dr. Stephanie Krebs of Bangkok, Thailand, and Allison Krebs-Khalil of Cairo; two sons, Christopher of Newton and Anthony of Seattle; and three grandchildren.

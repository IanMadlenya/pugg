
        William Francis Shea, who was budget director for New York City in the early 1960's, died on Monday at Valley Hospital in Ridgewood, N.J. He was 88 years old and lived in Delray Beach, Fla.
        He died of an intestinal infection, his daughter, Therese McDonald, said.
        Mr. Shea studied accounting at City College. He joined the city government in 1924 as an accountant in the Department of Finance. In 1929 he transferred to the office of the Commissioner of Accounts, now known as the Department of Investigations. In 1934 he joined the Budget Bureau and in 1945 he was named chief budget examiner. In 1952, he became assistant budget director and in 1962 moved up to become budget director for the duration of Mayor Robert F. Wagner's third term in office. He retired in 1965.
        Besides his daughter, of Wyckoff, N.J., Mr. Shea is survived by three grandchildren and one great-grandchild.


        Dr. Jesse Robert Solomon, a dentist, died on Monday at the State University of New York Health Science Center in Brooklyn. He was 83 years old and lived in the East New York section of Brooklyn.
        He died of bone cancer, a spokeswoman for the family said.
        Dr. Solomon had a private practice on Pennsylvania Avenue in East New York. He was president of Temple Sinai there from 1970 to 1985.
        He was born in East New York and graduated from Cooper Union with a degree in architecture in 1931. He received a B.A. from New York University in 1936 and graduated from dental school there in 1940. He retired in 1987.
        He served in the Army as a captain in the medical corps fom 1941 to 1946.
        Dr. Solomon is survived by his wife, the former Ruth Antis; a son, Marshall, of Brooklyn; a brother, Morton, of Glenwood, N.J.; and four grandchildren.

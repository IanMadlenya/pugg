
        Sidney Michael Kantoff, a retired textile executive, died on Tuesday at Holy Cross Hospital in Fort Lauderdale, Fla. He was 75 years old and lived in Lauderhill, Fla., and Forest Hills, Queens.
        He died of a heart attack, his son said.
        He was a vice president of QST Industries in Manhattan for 20 years. The Chicago-based company makes Ban-Rol for creating waistbands in trousers and other textile components. Earlier he had been an executive with Crown Textiles in Manhattan.
        He held bachelor's and master's degrees from the City College of New York.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Liselotte Kraus; a son, Dr. Philip Kantoff of Boston; a daughter, Gwen Moss of Old Westbury, L.I.; a sister, Evelyn Korelitz of Newton, Mass., and five grandchildren.

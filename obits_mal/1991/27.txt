
        Christopher J. O'Brien, chief operating officer of Multitasking Systems of New York, died on Jan. 8 at Roosevelt Hospital. He was 30 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        He died of cardiac arrest, his family said.
        Before joining Multitasking, an employment program for workers disabled by AIDS and HIV infection, Mr. O'Brien was with the National Artists Management Company. He performed in Off Broadway productions and was a member of the New York City Opera acting ensemble.
        Mr. O'Brien was born in the Bronx and was a graduate of New York University.
        He is survived by his mother, Barbara, and two brothers, Kevin and John, all of West Nyack, N.Y.; his grandmother, Helen Rich of White Plains, and a sister, Marypat Moller of Alexandria, Va.

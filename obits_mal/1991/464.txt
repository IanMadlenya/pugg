
        J. Read Smith, a lawyer for more than 50 years, died on Friday at Winthrop University Hospital in Mineola, L.I. He was 80 years old and lived in Garden City, L.I.
        He died of a heart attack, a family spokesman said.
        He worked for the law firm of Cullen & Dykman in Brooklyn. He had worked for Smith & Reiher, a Brooklyn law firm founded by his grandfather, which dissolved in 1980.
        Mr. Smith, a 1935 graduate of St. John's University Law School, served as administrator of the Sullivan Drydock and Repair Corporation of Brooklyn and during the Korean War was regional counsel for the United States National Production Authority. He had also served on the board of the Anchor Savings Bank.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Joan Bernice McAllister; a daughter, Janet Daddario of Rockville, Md.; three sons, J. Read Jr., of Kings Park, L.I.; Eric, of Annapolis, Md., and Gerard, of Manhattan; two brothers, Edward, of Norwalk, Conn., and Robert, of Manhattan; a sister, Grace Curtayne of Brooklyn; five grandchildren, and three great-grandchildren.

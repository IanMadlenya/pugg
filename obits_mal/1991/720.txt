
        Dr. Harold Marcus, an attending psychiatrist at Mount Sinai Hospital in Manhattan, died on Saturday at his home in New Rochelle, N.Y. He was 68 years old.
        He died of heart failure, a family spokeswoman said.
        Dr. Marcus graduated from Baruch College before serving as a captain in the Army Air Forces in World War II. He graduated from the University of Chicago Medical School in 1951. He did his residency in psychiatry at the Columbia Psychoanalytic Clinic and joined Mount Sinai Hospital and the Mount Sinai Medical School in 1957. He was also in private practice in Manhattan and Westchester.
        He is survived by his wife, Liz Atwood-Marcus; a son, Richard, of Sacramento, Calif.; two daughters, Cynthia, of Austin, Tex., and Elizabeth, of Denver; two stepchildren, Jane Metz of Hartford and Richard Metz of Manhattan; his mother, Anna, of Rego Park, Queens; two sisters, Mildred Cassell of Port Washington, L.I., and Selma Marmurek, of Rego Park, and two grandchildren.

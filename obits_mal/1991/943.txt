
        Richard A. McDonough, a former tire retailer, died on Sunday at Holy Cross Hospital in Fort Lauderdale, Fla. He was 71 years old and lived in Sea Girt, N.J., and South Orange, N.J.
        He died of a respiratory ailment, his son Richard 3d said.
        In 1960, he helped found Air Treads Inc., a retreader and retailer of commercial and military aircraft tires with seven plants in the United States and Canada. It is now a subsidiary of Goodyear. He was the former president of R. A. McDonough & Company, a leading Goodyear tire retailer.
        He retired in 1980 and founded Wine Products, of Sea Girt, of which he was president at his death.
        He was born in Newark, graduated from Holy Cross College and served with the Navy in World War II.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Ruth Marie Everett; three sons, Richard, of New Canaan, Conn., William, of New Providence, N.J., and David, of Washington, and five daughters, Joan Marie Benjamin of Columbia, Md., Diane O'Connor of Summit, N.J., Nancy Hall of Mesa, Ariz., Colleen of Hoboken, N.J., and Catherine of Waltham, Mass.
        He is also survived by two brothers, John C., of Essex Fells, N.J., and Robert E., of Fort Lauderdale; three sisters, Jane Reynolds of Brielle, N.J., Elizabeth Lyons of Mantoloking, N.J., and Marie Drury of Fort Lauderdale, and 22 grandchildren.

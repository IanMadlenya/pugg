
        John W. Wheeler, a Manhattan lawyer who was a retired senior partner in Thacher Proffitt & Wood, died on Tuesday at St. Francis Hospital in Port Washington, L.I. He was 75 years old and lived in Port Washington and in Boca Raton, Fla.
        He died of heart failure, a family spokesman said.
        Mr. Wheeler, a graduate of Columbia College and theColumbia University Law School, was an officer in the Columbia Alumni Federation for six years and served as counsel to the university from 1969 to 1979.
        With the exception of service with the Naval Air Corps in World War II, he was with Thacher Proffitt & Wood for his entire 45-year law career, specializing in banking and real-estate law. He retired in 1984.
        He was a founding member and a past president of the National Association of College and University Attorneys. He was also a former director of the Downtown Lower Manhattan Association.
        He is survived by his wife, Muriel; two sons, Richard, of Laguna Beach, Calif., and Robert, of Manhattan, and three grandchildren.

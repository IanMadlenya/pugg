
        Dr. John F. Summa, a physician who was director of cardiology at Waterbury Hospital in Connecticut, died on Tuesday at the Hospice Center in Branford, Conn. He was 59 years old and lived in Southbury, Conn.
        He died of cancer, a spokeswoman for the hospital said.
        Dr. Summa spent his entire medical career at Waterbury Hospital. He had served his internship and residency there after graduating from New York Medical College in 1958. In addition to having a private practice, Dr. Summa had been a staff physician at Waterbury since 1963.
        In high school he was an outstanding athlete and turned down a contract with the Brooklyn Dodgers, choosing instead to study at Holy Cross College.
        Dr. Summa was an adjunct professor at the Yale University School of Medicine and a member and former president of the Northwest chapter of the American Heart Association. He was also a past commissioner on the Waterbury Board of Aging.
        He is survived by his wife, Medelise; two daughters, Patricia Summa of Somerville, Mass., and Medelise Reissteck of Saunderstown, R.I.; four sons, John of New Milford, Conn., Geoffrey of Chebeague Island, Me., Robert of Southbury, and Mark of West Haven, Conn.; a sister, Roseanne Benson of Greenwich, Conn., and two grandchildren.


        Dr. Thomas Jefferson Parks, a New York obstetrician and gynecologist, died on Tuesday at his home in Oyster Bay, L.I. He was 87 years old.
        He died of lung cancer, a spokesman for the family said.
        Dr. Parks was on the staff of the Columbia College of Physicians and Surgeons from his early training until he retired in 1969. He had a private practice in Manhattan for 50 years.
        He was born in Brooksville, Miss., and was a graduate of the University of Mississippi and of Tulane University Medical School. He was a member of the New York Obstetrical Society, the American College of Obstetricians and Gynecologists, and a Fellow of the American College of Surgeons.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Blanton Sanders; a daughter, Melinda Conrad of Wilton, Conn.; two sons, Thomas W., of San Jose Calif., and Nicholas R., of Laurel Hollow, N.Y., and eight grandchildren.


        Kirby Peake, a former chairman of Carter-Wallace, Inc,, the drug and toiletries manufacturer, died on Tuesday at his home in Irvington, Va. He was 76 years old.
        He died of heart failure, his family said.
        Mr. Peake joined Carter-Wallace in 1959 as administrative vice president and became president in 1961 and chairman in 1972. He had previously been president of the the Vick Chemical division of Richardson-Merrell, Inc.
        A native of Detroit and a graduate of Colgate University, he was a reporter for The Binghamton Press in upstate New York before entering the drug business. He served as chairman of the New York State Commission for the Catskills from 1972 to 1975.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Hanna Mayer; two sons, Jonathan, of Simsbury, Conn., and Robert, of Pleasantville, N.Y.; and a daughter, Sarah, of Carmel, N.Y.

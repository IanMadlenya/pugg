
        Irving Cohen, who was a boxing manager and promoter for 35 years and handled the career of the onetime middleweight champion Rocky Graziano, died Tuesday night at Scottsdale Memorial Hospital in Arizona. He was 87 years old. Mr. Cohen had been taken to the hospital on Saturday after he suffered a heart attack.
        Before he retired in 1965, Mr. Cohen spent four years as a promoter at St. Nicholas Arena in Manhattan and Sunnyside Gardens in Queens.
        He is survived by his wife, Jeanette; a brother, Edward; a son, Herbert; four grandchildren, and six great-grandchildren.

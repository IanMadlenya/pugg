
        George H. Haley Jr., founder of Haley Associates Inc., an executive recruitment concern, died on Monday at his home in Manhattan. He was 76 years old.
        He died of a stroke, his family said.
        Mr. Haley founded Haley Associates in 1964, and it became a leading company in recruiting senior management for large companies. When Haley Associates was sold in 1989, he returned to Ward Howell International Inc., an executive search company that he helped found in 1952.
        He graduated from Yale University in 1939 and received a law degree from the University of Virginia in 1942.
        Surviving are three sons, George H. 3d and Stephen P. of New York City and Edward C. of Rye, N.Y.; a daughter, Anne P. of New York City, and six grandchildren.

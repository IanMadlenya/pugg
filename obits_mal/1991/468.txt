
        Robert C. Wilson, a lawyer, corporate official and retired major general in the Army Reserve, died on Friday at the King Street Nursing Home in Rye Brook, N.Y. He was 79 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        He died of Alzheimer's disease, his family said.
        Mr. Wilson, a graduate of Culver Military Academy, Princeton and the Yale Law School, was with the Allied Chemical Corporation from 1946 until he retired in 1976 as corporate secretary. Earlier he had been a lawyer with Donovan Leisure specialing in anti-trust matters.
        In World War II, he served with the 78th Infantry Division through several campaigns in the European theater, and he became deputy commander of the 301st Logistical Command. In the Berlin Airlift of the early 1960's, he served as a major general with the 82d Airborne Division. He retired from the Army in 1965 after 32 years of active and reserve service.
        He was a past commander of the New York chapter of the Military Order of the World Wars.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Bessie Larkin; a son, Clifford Wilson 3d of Manhattan; a daughter, Elizabeth Tallaferro of Rye, N.Y.; a brother, Charles, who lives in Florida, and two granddaughters.

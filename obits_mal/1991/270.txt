
        Sante Uberto Barbieri, a bishop of the United Methodist Church who was an early president of the World Council of Churches, died on Wednesday in Buenos Aires, where he lived. He was 90 years old.
        Bishop Barbieri, a native of Italy, held bachelor's, master's and divinity degrees from Southern Methodist University and a master's degree from Emory University. After serving in Brazil and Argentina, he was elected a bishop in 1949 by the church's Latin American Central Conference to supervise Methodist work in Argentina, Bolivia and Uruguay. In 1954, he was one of six presidents elected to seven-year terms by the World Council of Churches.
        In 1924 he married Odette de Oliveira, who died in 1983. They had four children.


        William J. Marschalk, an executive vice president of the Great Western Financial Corporation in Los Angeles, died Friday at City of Hope Hospital in Los Angeles. He was 46 years old and lived in Calabasas Park, Calif.
        He was being treated for Hodgkin's disease, said a spokesman for the company.
        Mr. Marschalk joined Great Western 12 years ago and was named executive vice president for administrative services in 1986. Earlier, he was a trial lawyer for the Federal Trade Commission in Washington and was the House and Senate liaison for the Department of Housing and Urban Development.
        Born in Passaic, N.J., he was a graduate of the University of Bridgeport and Rutgers University Law School.
        Mr. Marschalk is survived by his wife, Jeanne; two children, Cory and Heather; his mother, Irene Marschalk of Princeton, N.J., a sister and two brothers.

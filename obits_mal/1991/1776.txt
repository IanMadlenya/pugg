
        Murray Heilweil, a retired vice president of Hasbro Industries, died on Sunday at Greenwich (Conn.) Hospital. He was 80 years old and lived in Greenwich.
        He died of cardiac arrest, his wife, the former Charlotte Freeman, said.
        He was an Army lieutenant in World War II and graduated from St. John's University Law School. He worked in sales and marketing, first with Hearst Publications and later as merchandising director with the National Broadcasting Company and as national sales manager for Revlon's wholesale division. He joined Hasbro in the mid-1960's and set up its first direct sales force. He retired in 1979 as vice president for sales in the Eastern region.
        In addition to his wife, he is survived by two daughters, Samantha, of Norwalk, Conn., and Maggi, of Manhattan; a sister, Betty Schwartz of Manhattan, and two grandchildren.
        Correction:  August 10, 1991, Saturday
        An obituary on Thursday about Murray Heilweil, a retired vice president of Hasbro Industries, omitted a survivor. He is a son, Marc Heilweil of Atlanta.

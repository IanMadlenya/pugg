
        John Pauker, a former official of the United States Information Agency who was also a poet, died on Tuesday at his home in Washington. He was 70 years old.
        He died of congestive heart failure, his famiy said.
        A native of Hungary, Mr. Pauker came to the United States as a child and was reared in New York City. He was a graduate of the Fieldston School and Yale.
        He began his government career in World War II with the Office of War Information, broadcasting to the Axis countries from Algiers. After service in the Army he became a commentator and analyst for the Voice of America.
        In 1956 he was sent by the Voice of America to cover the Hungarian revolt. He later wrote speeches for the White House and in 1970 served with the United States Embassy in India. He retired from the information agency in 1975 as chief policy guidance officer.
        Mr. Pauker published several volumes of poetry and was editor of the literary quarterly Furioso for seven years. His work appeared in The New Republic, The Paris Review, The Kenyon Review and other publications.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Pam Pierce, and, from a previous marriage, a daughter, Molly, of Washington, and a son, Jethro, of Los Angeles.

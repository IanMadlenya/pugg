
        George H. Dunlap, the first general chairman and chief executive officer of Nationwide Insurance, died Wednesday at Riverside Hospital in Columbus. He was 85 years old.
        Mr. Dunlap was elected general chairman of Nationwide Insurance in 1969 and served until 1972, when he retired. For five years before he became general chairman he was president of Nationwide Corporation, a financial services holding company.
        He served as a Nationwide Insurance board member from 1939 to 1975 and was chairman of Nationwide Mutual, the flagship company in the Nationwide group, from 1941 to 1972.
        Mr. Dunlap and his wife, Gladys, owned and operated farms in Ohio. He held several posts in the Ohio Farm Bureau Federation and was an incorporator of Landmark Inc., a cooperative that became the largest farm supply group in Ohio. It is now Countrymark Inc.
        Besides his wife, he is survived by a sister.

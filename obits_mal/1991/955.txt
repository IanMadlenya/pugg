
        Courtlandt Otis, retired executive vice president of Johnson & Higgins, Manhattan insurance brokers, died on Saturday at the Mount Sinai Medical Center in Manhattan. He was 91 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        He died of natural causes, his son, John, said.
        For 15 years, he was president of Otis, Jones & Company, insurance brokers. When the concern merged with Johnson & Higgins in 1940 he became executive vice president. He held that post until he retired in 1964.
        Earlier in his career he had been national president of the United States Junior Chamber of Commerce and a director of the New York Heart Association and of the executive committee of Beekman Downtown Hospital. He was also a member of the advisory committee for the New York Public Library.
        Mr. Otis was born in Lansing, Mich., and graduated from Princeton University in 1920.
        Besides his son, John, of Manhattan, he is survived by a daughter, Helen Pope of Ocean Isle Beach, N.C.; two grandchildren and four great-grandchildren.

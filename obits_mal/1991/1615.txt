
        Otto Marx Jr., an investment banker and president of Marx & Company, a Manhattan investment banking firm, died on Friday at a hospital in Nice, France. He was 81 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        He became ill aboard his yacht and died after emergency surgery, his lawyer, Roger Harris, said.
        Mr. Marx was a 1932 graduate of the University of Virginia and was commander of the destroyer escort Reybold during World War II.
        He was a partner in the firm of Ladenburg Thalmann & Company before founding his own firm in 1961. From 1965 to 1969, he was president and later chairman of the Paribas Corporation. He then resumed the presidency of Marx & Company.
        He was also a director of the Ohio Hotel Corporation, the Westover Travel Corporation, the Otmar Real Estate Corporation, the Birmingham Corporation, the Lehman Capital fund and Bremar Systems Inc. He was a trustee of the New York Zoological Society and vice chairman of the national council for the Johns Hopkins School of Medicine.
        He is survived by his wife, Page; a stepson, John V. Summerlin of Fort Lauderdale, Fla., and a sister, Audrey Skirball Kenis of Los Angeles.


        Fred Thormann, retired vice president of Conde Nast Publications, died on Monday at Stamford Hospital in Connecticut. He was 81 years old and lived in Stamford.
        He died of heart failure, a family spokeswoman said.
        Mr. Thormann joined Conde Nast in 1936 as an accountant, became comptroller in 1957, treasurer in 1969, a vice president in 1981 and retired in 1988.
        He was active in the United Way and Stamford Hospital.
        A native New Yorker, he was a graduate of Stuyvesant High School and attended Columbia University and New York University.
        A widower, he is survived by a daughter, Peggy Lawler of New Haven; a sister, Eleanor Kuhl of Cobbleskill, N.Y.; four grandchildren, and a great-grandson.


        Isidore M. Cohen, the retired president of Joseph H. Cohen & Sons, a leading manufacturer of men's clothing, died on Saturday in New York Hospital. He was 93 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        He died of heart failure, a family spokesman said.
        Mr. Cohen was born in Watertown, N.Y., and grew up in Manhattan. He graduated from Townsend Harris High School and then joined his father's manufacturing company as a salesman. He served as president from 1961 to 1975, when he retired. He was also the founder and president of Reiss-Cohen Inc., a Manhattan graphics gallery.
        Mr. Cohen was a trustee of Beth Israel Medical Center and a former board member of the Park Avenue Synagogue, where he donated a library. He was a fund-raiser and benefactor for many philanthropies, including the United Jewish Appeal, the American-Israel Cultural Foundation, the Jewish Theological Seminary of America and the Israel Museum of Jerusalem. He served in the Navy in World War I and advised the Government on uniform procurement in World War II.
        Surviving are a daughter, Jody Robbins, and a stepdaughter, Jill Joseloff, both of Manhattan; two stepsons, Michael Joseloff of Manhattan and Gordon Joseloff of Westport, Conn.; two brothers, Wilfred, of Port Washington, L.I., and George, of Manhattan; a sister, Mildred Fink of Croton-on-Hudson, N.Y.; six grandchildren, and four great-grandchildren.


        John S. Hilson, an investment banker with the Wertheim firm for 41 years, died yesterday at his Manhattan home. He was 66 years old and also lived in Greenwich, Conn.
        He died of cancer, a family member said.
        Mr. Hilson joined Wertheim & Company in 1950; became a general partner in 1957; was vice chairman from 1971 until 1986, when it merged with the British merchant bank Schroders, and since then had been a managing director of Wertheim Schroder & Company.
        He was a native of Manhattan, where he attended Collegiate School, and graduated from Deerfield Academy and Princeton University. He was a former trustee of Collegiate and Deerfield and was a trustee of the Guggenheim Museum and the Solomon R. Guggenheim Foundation, as well as a director of the American Cancer Society.
        Mr. Hilson was a partner of a sports syndicate headed by Bill Veeck which once owned the St. Louis Browns and the Chicago White Sox. In World War II, Mr. Hilson served with the Army in the Burma campaign, and in his later years, he was a fund raiser for the Republican Party.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Gail Healy; his mother, Mildred Stern Hilson of Manhattan; two sons, William, of Manhattan, and Dwight, of Cos Cob, Conn.; a daughter, Melissa Parker of Palm Beach, Fla.; four stepsons, Lee Davis of Tucson, Ariz.; Andrew Davis of Sydney, Australia; Allan Maca of Cambridge, Mass., and Michael Healy of Manhattan; a stepdaughter, Michele Healy of Manhattan, and three grandchildren.

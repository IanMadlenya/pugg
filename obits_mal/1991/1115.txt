
        Franklin Walter Mohney, a management consultant and former president of the General Battery Corporation, died on Thursday at Lenox Hill Hospital in Manhattan. He was 64 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        He died of prostate cancer, said his wife, Patricia Main.
        Mr. Mohney was with the Singer Manufacturing Company in Manhattan from 1949 to 1967 and served as general manager of its power tool division. He then became vice president of Black & Decker and from 1972 to 1980 was vice president of the Chicago Pneumatic Tool Company. He then became president and chief operating officer of the General Battery Corporation and in 1984 formed his own consulting firm, the Venture Components Corporation, in Manhattan. He was also affiliated with Gilbert C. Osnos and Associates, management consultants.
        In addition to his wife, he is survived by two daughters, Geneve Lynn Coddington and Deborah Susan Hoyes, both of Reading, Pa.; three stepdaughters, Lisa Nicole Kelly of Seattle, Susan Jennifer Singer of Reading and Jacqueline Eve Singer of Manhattan, and his father and stepmother, Paul and Agnes Mohney of Boca Raton, Fla.


        Nicholas Martini, a former Mayor of Passaic who was active in Passaic County and state Republican politics, died on Sunday at the General Hospital Center in Passaic. He was 86 years old.
        He suffered a stroke on Thursday while working in his Passaic law office.
        He was elected a Passaic City Commissioner in 1935, serving 20 years. He was elected mayor in 1943, the same year he was appointed Passaic County Counsel. He retired as counsel in 1961.
        He also active in Italian-American charities, including Boys' Towns of Italy and organizations in Paterson.
        His wife, Theresa, died in 1990.
        He is survived by a brother, Anthony, of Passaic.

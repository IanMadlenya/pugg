
        Henry M. Stern, a men's clothing manufacturer, died on Sunday at New York Hospital in Manhattan. He was 83 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        He died of heart failure, said his wife, the former, Marie Lou Freedman.
        Mr. Stern graduated from Colgate College and in 1938 became president of the Manhattan office of Michael Stern & Company, a men's clothing manufacturer founded by his great-grandfather in 1849. He was president until 1978, when the company was sold, and then became a vice president at Genesco & Company in Manhattan. He retired in 1983.
        Besides his wife, he is survived by two children from a previous marriage, Anthony Morley Stern, an advertising executive, of Los Angeles, and Susan Cookson, a psychotherapist, of Manhattan; a sister, Harriett Strongin of Amagansett, L.I.; and six grandchildren.

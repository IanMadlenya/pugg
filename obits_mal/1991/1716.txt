
        Lionel Fitzgerald Payne, a retired New York City employee and a civic leader in the Bedford-Stuyvesant section of Brooklyn, died on July 20 at Long Island College Hospital in Brooklyn. He was 80 years old and lived in Brooklyn.
        He died of cancer, said his son Richard.
        Mr. Payne, a graduate of Brooklyn College, worked in city government for 31 years as a supervisor of housing inspectors for the Buildings Department, as deputy director of the Model Cities program in Bedford-Stuyvesant and as coordinator of the city's real-estate property records. He retired in 1983. He also served with many community organizations, including the Central Brooklyn Coordinating Council and the Kings County Hospital Center Advisory Board.
        He is survived by his wife, Edith; four sons, John, Richard, Donald and David, all of Brooklyn; two daughters, Frances Payne of Brooklyn and Janette Squires of Baldwin, L.I.; a stepson, Daniel, also of Brooklyn; 11 grandchildren, and 10 great-grandchildren.


        Dr. Walter Nudelman, an internist, died on Tuesday at Saint Barnabas Medical Center in Livingston, N.J. He was 66 years old and lived in Harding Township, N.J.
        He died of lymphoma, a family spokesman said.
        Dr. Nudelman was a former head of the cardiac clinic at Newark Beth Israel Medical Center, with which he was associated in the 1950's and 1960's. He was appointed to the St. Barnabas staff in 1964 and was clinical chairman of the department of medicine at Saint Barnabas from 1989 to 1990.
        He was also a clinical instructor at the University of Medicine and Dentistry of New Jersey. He became a Fellow of the American College of Chest Physicians in 1966.
        He served in the Navy in World War II and as a doctor in the Air Force in the Korean War. He graduated from New York University in 1944 and New York University College of Medicine in 1947.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Norma Berman; two sons, Karl H., of Bloomfield, N.J., and Dr. Robert W., of Greensboro, N.C.; a daughter, Judith E., of Boston; a sister, Ruth Mittleman of Somerville, N.J., and two granddaughters.

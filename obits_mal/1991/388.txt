
        Monroe Arnold, an actor, died Saturday at his home in Queens. He was 64 years old.
        He died after a heart attack, said his former wife, Judi Arnold.
        Mr. Arnold acted in the theater, on television and in films. Off Broadway, he performed 900 times as Coach in the musical "Leave It to Jane." He made his Broadway debut in 1963 in "Enter Laughing."
        Mr. Arnold, one of Lee Strasberg's early Method acting students, performed on television in "Naked City," "The Kaiser Aluminum Hour," "The Armstrong Circle Theater" and "Car 54, Where Are You?" In films, he was seen in "Alice's Restaurant," "Goodbye Columbus," "Air Power" and "Killer's Rage."
        He is survived by his brother, Ralph Leindorf of San Marcos, Calif.


        Irwin Leibowitz, a lawyer and former administrative law judge, died on Sunday at Long Island College Hospital in Brooklyn. He was 84 years old and lived in Brooklyn.
        He died of complications from diabetes, said his wife, Ruth.
        Mr. Leibowitz graduated from St. John's University Law School and was admitted to the bar in 1931. For more than 40 years he was affiliated with the law firm of Powers, Kaplan & Berger and from 1968 to 1978 was an assistant attorney general for New York State. He then served as an administrative law judge for several years and was in private practice before retiring in the mid-1980's. He had also been active in Democratic Party politics in Brooklyn.
        In addition to his wife, he is survived by a brother, Samuel, of Boston.

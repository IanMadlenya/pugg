
        Dr. Paul B. Johnson, a pediatrician who practiced in Manhattan for more than 60 years, died on March 12 at St. Mary's Hospital in Waterbury, Conn. He was 99 years old and lived in Cheshire, Conn., and Saranac Lake, N.Y. Before retirement in 1981 he had lived in the Bronx for many years.
        He died of heart failure, his family said.
        Dr. Johnson, a graduate of the College of Physicians and Surgeons of Columbia University, served with the Army Medical Corps in World War I. He was an attending physician at Flower-Fifth Avenue Hospital and taught pediatrics at several medical schools.
        Dr. Johnson's wife, the former Helen Eldred, died in 1958. He is survived by a son, John, of Cheshire; a daughter, Barbara Parnass of Saranac Lake, seven grandchildren and six great-grandchildren.

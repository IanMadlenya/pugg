
        Morris Ettenberg, an educator and professor emeritus of electrical engineering at City College, died on Friday at St. Luke's-Roosevelt Hospital Center in Manhattan. He was 75 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        He died of a ruptured aorta, a family spokesman said.
        In 1963, Dr. Ettenberg, a Ph.D. graduate of New York University, became a professor of electrical engineering at the Graduate School of the City University and later served as chairman of the department. He retired in 1985. He had previously taught at City College and at Columbia University while working for the Sperry Gyroscope Company.
        He had also taught at the Polytechnic Institute of Brooklyn and had been a Fulbright Professor at Technion, Israel Institute of Technology in Haifa. In World War II, he was a radar engineer and taught in the Naval Radar School in Brooklyn. Afterward, he went to Israel and was commander of the radar squadron of the Israeli Air Force.
        He is survived by his wife, Sylvia; a daughter, Isa Aron of Los Angeles; a son, David, of Manhattan; a brother, Emanuel, and a sister, Ethel Judis, both of Lauderdale Lakes, Fla., and two grandchildren.

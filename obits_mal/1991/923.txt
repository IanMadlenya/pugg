
        Frederick A. Crane, who taught history at Bard College from 1949 to 1978, died on Monday at Northern Dutchess Hospital in Rhinebeck, N.Y. He was 80 years old and lived in Rhinebeck.
        He died of respiratory arrest, a funeral home spokesman said.
        Professor Crane, a native of Elizabeth, N.J., was a graduate of the College of Wooster in Wooster, Ohio. He worked as a newspaper reporter in Morristown, N.J., before becoming dean and later president of Morris Junior College in Morristown. After teaching naval aviation cadets at Wesleyan University in World War II, he received a Ph.D. in history at Yale.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Harriet Brown; a son, Arthur, of Rhinebeck; a daughter, Pamela Ellison of Washington; two sisters, Frances Bourns of Jamesburg, N.J., and Christine Fowle of Harwich Port, Mass., and four grandchildren.

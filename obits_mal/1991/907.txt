
        Franklin R. Hall, who directed operations abroad for Exxon, died on March 31 at the Arbors nursing home in Shelburne, Vt., where he lived. He was 74 years old and had lived in Bronxville, N.Y., and New Milford, Conn.
        He suffered from Alzheimer's disease and died of pneumonia, his family said.
        Mr. Hall was a graduate of the Lawrenceville School and Princeton. He was with the First National City Bank in New York and in Brazil before serving in the Air Force in World War II.
        After the war he directed Exxon's operations in Latin America and Asia. He retired in 1972 and established a consulting group, Area Consultants, specializing in corporate relocation.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Phyllis Carey; a daughter, Anne Brodginski of Ridgefield, Conn.; a son, Carey, of Chappaqua, N.Y., and two grandchildren.

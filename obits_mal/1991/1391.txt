
        Monroe D. Donsker, a professor of mathematics at New York University, died on Saturday at the Columbia-Presbyterian Medical Center in Manhattan. He was 66 years old and lived in Fort Lee. N.J.
        He died of cancer, a spokeswoman for the family said.
        Dr. Donsker, who was known for his work in mathematical probabilities, became a professor at N.Y.U.'s Courant Institute of Mathematical Science in 1962. Before joining N.Y.U., he taught at Cornell and the University of Minnesota.
        He was a former chairman of the Board of Foreign Scholarships, which oversees Government-sponsored foreign-exchange programs. He was appointed to the board by President Gerald R. Ford in 1975 and reappointed by President Jimmy Carter in 1977.
        Professor Donsker was born in Burlington, Iowa, graduated from the University of Minnesota in 1944 and received a Ph.D. there in 1948.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Mary Davis, and a brother, Stanley, of St. Paul, Minn.

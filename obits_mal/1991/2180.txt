
        William C. Kroeger, a private research analyst and vice chairman of the state Workers' Compensation Board, died on Friday. He was 56 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        His family said he had suffered a heart attack on a street near his home.
        A native of San Antonio, Tex., and a graduate of the University of Mississippi, Mr. Kroeger had been a member of the state board since 1975. He was a former city administrator and executive director of the Lower Eastside Neighborhoods Association, as well as past chairman of the New York County Liberal Party and a member of the party's state policy committee.
        Mr. Kroeger is survived by his wife, the former Sharon Lee Daniel; a daughter, Andrea; a son, William, and a brother, Donald, of Knoxville, Tenn.


        Richard Freedman, an educator and film critic, died on Friday at St. Luke's-Roosevelt Hospital Center in Manhattan. He was 59 years old and lived in Hoboken, N.J.
        He died of cancer, a family spokesman said.
        Mr. Freedman was film critic for The Star-Ledger in Newark, and his column was syndicated throughout the Newhouse Newspapers group. He also wrote book reviews for a number of publications, including The New York Times, The Washington Post, The National Observer and Life. A graduate of the Columbia University Graduate School of Journalism and a Ph.D. graduate of Cornell, Mr. Freedman had also been a professor of English at Simmons College in Boston and was the author of "The Novel," a college text often used in English courses.
        He is survived by his wife, Helen; a son, David, of Hoboken, and a daughter, Pamela Skrinski of Rockaway, N.J.


        Dr. Constantin V. Teodoru, a doctor who specialized in geriatric medicine for 30 years, died on Friday at New York Hospital in Manhattan. He was 76 years old and lived in Jackson Heights, Queens.
        He died of heart failure, a son said.
        Dr. Teodoru was born in Romania and received his medical training at the University of Bucharest in 1939. In World War II, he was head of a medical unit on the Russian front and afterward headed medical departments for the American Red Cross, National Catholic Welfare and the Romanian Red Cross.
        He came to the United States in 1952 and was affiliated with Mount Sinai Hospital and the Albert Research Institute in Brooklyn. He became chief of long-term medical care at Kingsbrook Medical Center in 1964, and later was a medical director at Goldwater Memorial Hospital. He was also in private practice and taught at Downstate Medical Center and at New York University Medical School.
        He is survived by his wife, Mirela; three sons; Nicolas, of Spring Valley, N.Y., Daniel, of Bergen, N.J., and Michael, of Jackson Heights, and four grandchildren.


        Dr. Frank Henderson Mayfield, a Cincinnati neurosurgeon who was a past president of the American Academy of Neurological Surgery, died Wednesday at Christ Hospital in Cincinnati. He was 82 years old and lived in Cincinnati.
        He died of cancer, said his son R. Glen Mayfield of Cincinnati.
        A 1931 graduate of the Medical College of Virginia, Dr. Mayfield served in the Army Medical Corps in World War II and afterward taught at the University of Cincinnati College of Medicine, where he developed a graduate program in neurosurgery. He also established neurosurgery departments at Christ Hospital and at Good Samaritan Hospital.
        In addition to his son Glen, he is survived by his wife, Belle; two daughters, Sara Schreiner of Glenview, Ill., and Victoria Hall of Cincinnati; another son, Frank Jr., also of Cincinnati; a brother, Boyd of Norlina, N.C.; two sisters, Lucy Brown of Roanoke Rapids, N.C., and Catherine Short of Pompton Lakes, N.J.; 11 grandchildren, and three great-grandchildren.

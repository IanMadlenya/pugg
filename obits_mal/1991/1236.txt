
        Philip Ernst, a marketing consultant and former marketing executive, died on Monday at his home in Darien, Conn. He was 60 years old.
        He died of cardiac arrest, a funeral home representative said.
        At his death, he was associated with the Tidewater Group, a career management concern in Stamford, Conn. Before that, he was chief operating officer of Laura Lupton Inc., a cosmetics company in Rowayton, Conn., and owned Crusty's Pizza in Bridgeport, Conn.
        In 1973, he was named director of marketing for the Reuben H. Donnelley division of Dun & Bradstreet and was later general manager for home products for the Union Carbide Corporation. From 1976 to 1986, he was managing partner of Lisher & Company, a Manhattan marketing consulting firm.
        He is survived by his wife, Anne; a son, Thomas, of Durham, N.C.; a daughter, Elizabeth, of Boston, and a sister, Carol Hopkins of Rochester.

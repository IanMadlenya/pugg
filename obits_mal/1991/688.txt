
        Vanderpoel Adriance, a former stockbroker, investment consultant and governor of the American Stock Exchange, died on Feb. 18 at his home in Shelly Bay, Bermuda. He was 81 years old.
        He died of a heart attack, a son said.
        Mr. Adriance became a stockbroker in 1933 after graduating from Williams College. He bought a seat on the American Stock Exchange and was head of his own firm, Adriance & Finn in Manhattan. He served on the Board of Governors of the American Stock Exchange from 1958 to 1963. He was a member of the exchange's executive committee and was chairman of the finance committee.
        He left Wall Street for Bermuda in 1969. There, he became an investment consultant with the bank of N. T. Butterfield & Son, Ltd., in Hamilton, Bermuda. In 1980, he joined the Beneficial International Insurance Company, also in Hamilton, as manager of its investment portfolio. He retired in 1985.
        He is survived by three sons, H. Benson, and Edwin, both of North Sandwich, N.H., and Vanderpoel 3d, of Center Sandwich, N.H., and three granddaughters.

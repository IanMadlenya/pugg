
        V. Henry Rothschild 2d, a lawyer in Manhattan since 1934, died on Monday at his home in Manhattan. He was 82 years old and also had a home in Truro, Mass.
        Mr. Rothschild was a senior partner in the law firm of Rothschild & Salwen in Manhattan from 1955 to 1981. He was then named of counsel with Lord Day & Lord, Barrett, Smith.
        A graduate of Cornell University and Yale University Law School, Mr. Rothschild was an associate with Root, Clark, Buckner & Ballantine (now Dewey, Ballantine, Bushby, Palmer & Wood) from 1932 to 1940.
        He was chief counsel to the Salary Stabilization Board and lectured at the Practising Law Institute in Manhattan and at the New York University Institute on Federal Taxation. He was an authority on executive compensation.
        He is survived by his wife, Jacqueline; two sons, Thomas, of Newton, Mass., and Henry 3d, of Santa Fe. N.M.; three stepsons, Michel Roy of Hillsboro, N.C., Serge Roy of Duxbury, Mass., and Christian Roy of Manhattan, and nine grandchildren.

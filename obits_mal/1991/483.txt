
        Elmo Pope Brown, retired assistant vice president of the United States Trust Company of New York, died on March 7 at his home in Columbus, N.Y. He was 85 years old and had lived in East Islip, L.I., for 50 years before moving to Columbus two years ago.
        He died after a stroke, his daughter, Alison Lewis of Philadelphia, said.
        Mr. Brown was a charter member and president of the Municipal Forum of New York, a member of the Municipal Finance Officers Association of the United States and Canada and a governor of the Money Marketeers of New York University. He retired in 1969.
        He was a graduate of the University of Michigan and studied finance on the graduate level at New York University.
        Besides his daughter, he is survived by his wife, the former Helen Spurr; another daughter, Dr. Beverley Bryant of Monte Sereno, Calif., and two grandchildren.

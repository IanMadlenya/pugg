
        Charles Blayney Colmore Jr., a retired executive of the Procter & Gamble Company and a former chair umpire for the United States Lawn Tennis Association, died on May 12 at his home in Baltimore. He was 78.
        He died of cancer, said his sister-in-law, Mary Virginia Perrine Colmore.
        Mr. Colmore, who was born in Havana, joined Procter & Gamble after graduating from Princeton University in 1935. After serving as a district manager in the Northeast, he became a sales manager in the Philippines in 1952 and retired in 1960.
        As a chair umpire, he officiated Davis Cup tournaments in the 1970's.
        He is survived by his wife, Margaretta Erikson Davis; three children, the Rev. C. Blayney Colmore of La Jolla, Calif., Silvia de Murias of Somerville, Mass., and Jean Perrine Catlin of Andover, Mass.; two sisters, Sarah C. Adams of Lexington, Ky., and Margaret C. Dunn of Providence, R.I., and 13 grandchildren.

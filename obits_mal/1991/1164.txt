
        Francisco Soldati, an Argentine businessman and former director of the Central Bank of Argentina, died Monday at the Diagnostic Institute in Buenos Aires. He was 51 years old.
        He died as a result of injuries suffered in a polo accident on Sunday in San Miguel del Monte, reports from Argentina said.
        Mr. Soldati, who was a prominent figure in Argentine economic circles, was head of the Sociedad Comercial del Plata, a holding company involved in oil, fertilizers, insurance, engineering, communications, railroads, television and agriculture. He played an active role in the privitization of Argentine industry. He served as Under Secretary of Finance in the Argentine Government and from 1976 to 1980 he was director of the Central Bank.
        He is survived by his wife, Ariane; three daughters, Sabrina, Victoria and Yasmina; a son, Francisco; two brothers, Alejandro and Santiago; and two sisters, Maria Pando and Veronica Montalenbert.

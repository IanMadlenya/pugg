
        Charles C. Kujawa, who was associated with the Young Men's Christian Association for 44 years, died on Thursday at Cabrini Medical Center in New York City. He was 70 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        He died of a heart attack, a daughter said.
        When he retired in 1986, Mr. Kujawa was director of the Management Resource Center of the Y.M.C.A. of Greater New York.
        Mr. Kujawa had been national director of Y.M.C.A. camping services, past president of the American Camping Association and a board member of Trailblazers of New York City.
        Mr. Kujawa was also on the boards of the Episcopal Camp and Conference Center in Ivoryton, Conn., the Geneva Point Center, an ecumenical conference center at Wolfboro, N.H., and the National Council of Churches.
        He was born in Winona, Minn., graduated from Carleton College in Minnesota and received a master's degree from the University of Maryland.
        He is survived by his wife, Nina Preston; two daughters, Susan Preston Nowelsky of Windsor, Calif., and Laurel Preston Nette of Woodinville, Wash.; a son, Todd, of Yardley, Pa. and four grandchildren.

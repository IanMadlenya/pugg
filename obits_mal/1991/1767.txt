
        Benjamin Garcia Jr., an executive vice president of the Social Service Employees Union, died on Saturday at Memorial Sloan-Kettering Cancer Center in Manhattan. He was 50 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        He died of cancer, said his daughter Yvette.
        Mr. Garcia, who began working for New York City in the mid-1960's, was a supervising human resource specialist with the Community Development Agency. He began working with Local 371 of the Social Service Employees Union in 1979 as an organizer and since 1985 has been coordinating the union's national political action committee campaign. He became an executive vice president of the union in 1989.
        He is survived by his wife, Angela; two daughters, Yvonne and Yvette, both of Manhattan; a son, Benjamin 3d; three sisters, Vicky Garcia of Bayamon, P.R., and Lydia Guzman and Abbie Colon, both of Albuquerque, N.M.; two brothers, Jaime, of Allentown, Pa., and Jose, of Manhattan, and one grandson.


        Charles Webster Carleton, retired president of South American operations for Nabisco Brands, now R.J.R. Nabisco, died on Friday at a family home in New Canaan, Conn. He was 71 years old and lived in Naples, Fla.
        He died of prostate cancer, a spokeswoman for the family said.
        He joined Nabisco in 1968 and became president of South American operations in 1982. He retired in 1984.
        From 1946 to 1968 he was director of European operations for the Pillsbury Company.
        Mr. Carleton was born in Minneapolis. He was a graduate of Dartmouth College and of the Amos Tuck School of Business Administration there.
        He served in the Navy during World War II.
        Mr. Carleton is survived by a daughter, Sarah Wechsler of Dallas; a son, Charles Jr., of Salisbury, Conn.; a sister, Mary F. Carleton of Minneapolis, and a grandson.

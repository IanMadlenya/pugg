
        Matthew Rapf, a film and television producer, died last Wednesday at his home in Malibu, Calif. He was 71 years old.
        He died in his sleep after being ill with the flu, his family said.
        Mr. Rapf produced a number of films, many of them for Metro-Goldwyn-Mayer, including "The Sellout," "Desperate Search," "Big Leaguer" and "Half a Hero." His television credits include the Loretta Young show, "The Great Gildersleeve," "Kojak" and "Ben Casey."
        He was a graduate of Dartmouth College and served in the Navy during World War II. Early in his career, he wrote the scripts for several motion pictures, including "Rampage" and "The Hijackers."
        He is survived by his wife, Carol; four sons: James and Matthew William, both of Malibu; Steven, of Portland, Ore., and Harry John, of San Francisco; two stepsons, David Weil of Malibu and Timothy Weil of Maryland; a brother, Maurice, of New York City, and eight grandchildren.


        Carl Edward Rogers, an advertising executive and publisher of the old Madison Avenue Magazine, died on Sunday at Lenox Hill Hospital. He was 70 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        He founded the magazine in 1960. It ceased publication for a while and was reborn in 1971 with the help of an advertising agency, Warner, Bicking & Fenwick. Mr. Rogers continued as editor and publisher until 1975, when he became a vice president for public relations at Citibank. He was later with Arthur Young & Company and retired in 1988.
        Mr. Rogers graduated in 1944 from the University of Texas. In 1946, he became an advertising manager with the Fred W. Amend Company. Mr. Rogers became an account executive with Foote, Cone & Belding in 1948. From 1953 to 1956, he was product manager with Lever Brothers and later an account supervisor with Donahue & Coe.
        Surviving are his wife, Maria; two daughters, Judy, of Albuquerque, N.M., and Elizabeth Sobczak of Duluth, Minn.; a sister, Katheryn, of Chicago; a brother, William, of Alameda, Calif., and a grandchild.

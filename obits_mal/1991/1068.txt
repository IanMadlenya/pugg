
        Peter J. Hazou, a retired United Nations official, died on May 19 at New York Hospital. He was 75 years old and lived in Hastings-on-Hudson, N.Y.
        He died after a heart attack, said his daughter, Linda, who is political officer for the United Nations Office of Humanitarian Affairs in Southeast Asia.
        Born in Jerusalem in 1916, Mr. Hazou moved to the United States in 1947 and went to work for the United Nations, then at Lake Success, L.I.
        In more than 30 years with the organization, he served with many official missions, including the administration during decolonization in Eritrea, the Disarmament Commission in London, the peacekeeping operation in the Belgian Congo; the Conference on Trade and Development in Geneva, and special sessions of the Security Council in Panama in 1973.
        When he retired in 1978, he was the executive officer of the United Nations Department of Conference Services, which coordinates United Nations conferences worldwide.
        In addition to his daughter, Mr. Hazou is survived by his wife, Winnie, an author and professor of sociology at Mercy College in Dobbs Ferry; a son, Peter Jr., a vice president of Manufacturers Hanover Trust Company in London; a brother, Marcos, of Jackson Heights, Queens, and a sister, Mary, of Bethlehem, in the Israeli-occupied West Bank.

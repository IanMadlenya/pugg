
        Benjamin Lichtenberg, a professor of philosophy at Caldwell College in Caldwell, N.J., and chairman of its philosophy department, died on Wednesday at Mountainside Hospital in Montclair, N.J. He was 45 years old and lived in Verona, N.J.
        He was being treated for heart disease, said his sister.
        Dr. Lichtenberg, a native of West New York, N.J., received a bachelor's degree at the New School for Social Research and his doctorate from Fordham University. He taught at Caldwell College for 22 years.
        Dr. Lichtenberg specialized in bioethics and futurism. He lectured extensively in the United States and Europe, wrote for Forbes magazine and was the author of "Insights of an Outsider," a collection of aphorisms (Jaico Press; 1968.)
        He is survived by two sons, Noah and Illya, of Verona; his mother, Olga Lichtenberg, of West New York, and a sister Eva Lee Lichtenberg, a lawyer, of Leonia, N.J.


        Donald W. Smith, a retired Foreign Service officer who held consular posts in London, Bonn and Sydney, Australia, died on Monday at Venice Hospital in Florida. He was 85 years old and lived in Venice.
        He died of pneumonia, his family said.
        In 1938 he joined the State Department and served in Stockholm before returning to Washington as chief of personnel for the Foreign Service. He was appointed Consul General in Sydney in 1950, then took the same position in Bonn in 1955 and in London in 1957. He retired in 1962.
        Mr. Smith is survived by his wife, Virginia; two sons by previous marriages, David, a Foreign Service officer stationed in Athens, and Richard, of Brussels; six grandchildren, and a great-grandchild.


        James Maxime DuPont, founder and chairman of Thermoplastics Inc., a producer of plastic materials for automotive, textile and other products, died on Monday at his home in Watchung, N.J. He was 79 years old.
        He died of cardiac arrest, his family said.
        Mr. DuPont was born in Edinburgh and came to the United States as an infant. He founded Thermoplastics, in Warren, N.J., in 1948.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Violetta Pollara; three sons, James, Paul and Joseph, all of New Jersey; two daughters, Marietta Sansone and Claudette Ginsberg, both of New Jersey; two sisters, Jeanette Stewart of Howell, N.J., and Helen Fisher of Scotch Plains, N.J.; 14 grandchildren, and seven great-grandchildren.


        Albert E. French, a retired technical educator, died April 25 at Strong Memorial Hospital in Rochester. He was 81 years old and lived in Rochester.
        He died of a ruptured aortic aneurysm, his wife, Marjorie, said.
        Mr. French had been president of the Canton College of Technology in Canton, N.Y., from 1948 to 1972, when he retired. In 1950, he was one of the college presidents called to Albany by Gov. Nelson A. Rockefeller to found the State University of New York.
        He was a consultant in technical education to the Ford Foundation and various United Nations agencies.
        Mr. French earned a degree in electrical engineering from Clarkson University in Potsdam, N.Y., in 1934, and went to work for the Eastman Kodak Company. In 1937, he joined the faculty of the Alfred Agricultural and Technical College in Alfred, N.Y. He then became director of industrial arts and vocational education in the Elmira, N.Y., school system.
        In 1943 he joined the Navy, training civilian workers in the Brooklyn Navy Yard. After the war, he founded the Veterans School for Technical Training in Elmira, and in 1948 he moved to Canton.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Marjorie Stryker; two sons, Stephen, of Rochester, and Thomas, of Cleveland; a daughter, Margal Colegrove of Fulton, N.Y.; 11 grandchildren, and three great-grandchildren.

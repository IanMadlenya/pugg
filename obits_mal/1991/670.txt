
        Alexander E. Fox, a lawyer and a former New York State insurance official, died on Friday at the Cliffside Nursing Home in Flushing, Queens. He was 84 years old and had been a longtime resident of the Bronx.
        Mr. Fox, a native of Manhattan, was a graduate of St. John's University and its law school. He was in private law practice until 1942, when he joined the legal staff of the Association of Casualty and Surety Companies, now the American Insurance Association. In 1965 he was named deputy superintendent of insurance for New York State. In the 1970's he was with the Manhattan law firm of Abrams & Martin.
        Mr. Fox is survived by a son, Daniel, of Manhattan; a daughter, Laurel, of Santa Cruz, Calif., and six grandchildren.

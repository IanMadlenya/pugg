
        Albert A. Cuneo, a building contractor in New York for several decades and a past president of the General Society of Mechanics and Tradesmen, died yesterday at his home in Scarsdale, N.Y. He was 89 years old.
        Mr. Cuneo was born in Genoa, Italy, and came to the United States as an infant. He attended the Chicago Technical College.
        He headed a family business, the Arrow Contracting Company, until his death. He was a longtime leader, and president in 1962, of the General Society of Mechanics and Tradesmen, which was founded in 1785 and has maintained a library and instructional program for the building trades in Manhattan since 1820.
        Mr. Cuneo is survived by his wife, the former Florence Clay, whom he married in 1927; three daughters, Violet, of Scarsdale, Dorothy Croft of Sarasota, Fla., Barbara Olsen of Wilton, Conn., and Florence Frascati of Tarrytown, N.Y.; two sisters, Faustina Cuneo and Gemma Casazza, both of the Bronx; 11 grandchldren and two great-grandchldren.

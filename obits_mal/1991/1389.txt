
        Kennard C. Baker, retired chairman of Baker-Benjes, a New York shoe importer and distributor, died on May 31 at a convalescent home in Hobe Sound, Fla. He was 81 years old.
        He died of heart failure, a spokeswoman for the family said.
        Under Mr. Baker's guidance, Baker-Benjes Inc., which he founded in 1965, established a niche in the American footwear market for elegant, handmade shoes imported from Britain and Italy.
        In 1981 he formed Ralph Lauren Footwear, a separate company under license from the Polo Ralph Lauren Corporation. Mr. Baker was chairman of that company and Baker-Benjes until he retired in 1985.
        A native of Waterloo, N.Y., he was educated at the Universty of Virginia. He served as a captain in the Army during World War II.
        Hs is survived by his wife, the former Catherine Cross; three sons, R. Kennard, of Redding Ridge, Conn., William C., of Manhattan and Bruce A., of Darien, Conn., and three grandchildren.

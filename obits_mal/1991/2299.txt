
        William H. Droeger, the retired president of two major clothing companies, died Thursday at Beth Israel Medical Center in Manhattan. He was 65 years old and lived in Manhattan and also had a home in Cutchogue, L.I.
        He died of lung cancer, his family said.
        Mr. Droeger spent his entire career at Cluett, Peabody & Company of New York, a large group of apparel companies. Starting as a sales trainee in Chicago, he worked his way up to become president of two of Cluett's biggest amd best-known subsidiaries, Gold Toe Socks and the Arrow Shirt Company, a leading manufacturer of men's dress shirts.
        He was born in Oak Park, Ill., grew up in Chicago and graduated from Northwestern University in 1949. He worked for Cluett from 1950 until his retirement last year. Among his projects were Gold Toe's expansion into women's hosiery and Arrow's opening of manufacturing in Asia.
        His survivors include his wife, the former Charlotte Kresse; two sons, William H. Jr. of Eden Prairie, Minn., and Jeffrey of Steamboat Springs, Colo.; and a sister, Dolores Jarvis of Riverside, Calif.

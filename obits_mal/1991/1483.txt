
        Lutcher Slade Brown, a theatrical producer, musician and businessman, died on Saturday. He was 68 years old.
        Mr. Brown's most successful Broadway production was the 1960 musical "Bye Bye, Birdie." He also produced a number of other Broadway shows, including "Entertaining Mr. Sloane," "All American," "A Joyful Noise" and "The Rink."
        Mr. Brown was born in Orange, Tex., and studied piano, organ and voice. In addition to his theatrical interests, he was president of Brown Paper Industries and Higman Towing Company, a tugboat and barge fleet in the Gulf of Mexico.
        He is survived by two daughters, Robbin and Barbara; a son, Michael; a brother, John, and six grandchildren.


        Dr. Robert A. Herfort, a surgeon who practiced in White Plains for 35 years, died on Thursday at St. Joseph's Hospital in Bangor, Me. He was 72 years old and lived in West Penobscot, Me.
        He died of a heart attack, his family said.
        Dr. Herfort was president of the medical staff at St. Agnes Hospital in White Plains from 1982 to 1984 and director of surgery there from 1983 to 1985. He retired in 1986.
        In World War II he received the Silver Star and was wounded in action as a battalion surgeon with the 36th Armored Infantry Regiment, Third Armored Division in Europe.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Jane Ann Punzelt; three sons, John, of Manhattan; Stephen, of West Orange, N.J., and Robert Jr., of Conway, S.C., and a daughter, Deborah, of Westport, Conn.

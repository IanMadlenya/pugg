
        Gerald J. Dunworth, a lawyer in Manhattan for 35 years, died on Tuesday at his home in Waldwick, N.J. He was 79 years old.
        He died of complications of heart disease, a daughter said.
        Mr. Dunworth attended Fordham University and graduated from Brooklyn Law School. He did counter-intelligence work in Europe as an Army lieutenant during World War II and afterward returned to the law firm of Coudert Brothers in Manhattan.
        He retired in 1981 and was counsel to the firm of Lyon & Byer in Ridgewood, N.J. He was a former member of the Guild of Catholic Lawyers in Manhattan and in 1988 the Vatican named him an affiliate member of the Congregation of Marist Brothers for his years of service to the order.
        He is survived by his wife, Katharine; two daughters, Alice D. Cantara of Saco, Me., and Katherine D. Shirley of Mount Pleasant, Mich.; three sons, Gerald Jr., and Donald, both of Montclair, N.J., and John, of Glen Rock. N.J.; two sisters, Kathleen Williams of Prescott, Ariz., and Ellen Murphy of Huntington, L.I.; two brothers, Thomas, of Shaker Heights, Ohio, and Joseph, of Ridgefield, Conn., and eight grandchildren.

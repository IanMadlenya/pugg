
        Humberto Hernandez, a minister in the Seventh-Day Adventist Church and its director of Hispanic Ministries in the New York City area, died on Thursday while visiting his daughter in Alma, Mich. He was 59 years old and lived in Mamaroneck, N.Y.
        He died of pancreatic cancer, a church spokeswoman said.
        Mr. Hernandez, a graduate of the University of Havana, taught in the public schools in Cuba and directed a private school there before leaving in 1962. He then spent 16 years in teaching and administrative posts with the Adventist Church in Central America and the next seven years as dean of Antillian College in Puerto Rico.
        During his career he was also director of the Metropolitan Adventist Academy in San Juan, director of the Vocational Institute of Venezuela and academic dean of the Instituto Colombo-Venezolano in Medellin.
        He came to the United States in 1979 and was pastor of the Intervale Hispanic Church in the Bronx before being named director of Hispanic Ministries, coordinating the 35 Adventist Hispanic churches in the Greater New York Conference.
        He is survived by his wife, Gladys; a son, Humberto Jr,. of Loma Linda, Calif; two daughters, Ruth Sarli of Livingston, N.Y., and Dinah Hernandez of Alma, Mich.; five brothers, a sister and two granddaughters.

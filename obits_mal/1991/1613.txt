
        Edward W. Murray, a retired judge of the New York State Court of Claims, died on Sunday at the Lahey Clinic in Burlington, Mass. He was 75 years old and lived in Troy, N.Y.
        Judge Murray died of cancer, a spokesman for the family said.
        In 1977 he was appointed judge of the Court of Claims in Troy, where he remained until he retired in 1985. Before joining the court, he had been in private law practice with Bartle, McGrane, Duffy & Murray, now Bartle, McGrane, Duffy & Jones in Troy.
        He was born in Troy and graduated from Williams College in Williamstown, Mass., and the Albany Law School.
        He served three years in the Army infantry during World War II, participating in the New Guinea campaign in the Pacific and as part of the army of occupation in Japan in 1945. He received the Distinguished Service Cross and the Bronze Star.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Dorothy Reardon; four daughters, Louise Hoffman of Morristown, N.J., Elaine Tagliareni of Morrisville, Pa., Georgia Murray of Boston and Pamela Murray of Westport, Conn.; a brother, Thomas J., of Troy, and six grandchildren.

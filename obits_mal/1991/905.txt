
        Arthur Paul Wales, a retired vice president of the Smith Corona Marchant Corporation in Manhattan, died March 27 at a nursing home in Lantana, Fla. He was 73 years old and lived in Lantana.
        He died of prostate cancer, his son William said.
        He joined Smith Corona in 1936 as a student at Syracuse University and worked for it for 47 years. He began in a factory and was promoted to the accounting department. After World War II he joined the sales department and rose to become vice president of typewriter sales in 1970. He retired in 1984.
        He was born in Troy, N.Y., and served as a flight officer in World War II in the China-Burma-India theater of operations. He was awarded a medal for meritorious achievement.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Catheryn Smith; five children from a previous marriage, Katharine Anne, of Rowayton, Conn., Michael A., of Riverside, Conn., Dr. John H., of Delmar, N.Y., William F. and David C., both of Stamford; two stepsons, Raymond A. Smith and John Smith, both of Chestertown, N.Y.; and four grandchildren.

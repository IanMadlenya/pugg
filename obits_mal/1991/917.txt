
        Otto C. Neumann, a commissioner of the Connecticut Department of Public Utility Control and former state representative, died at Hartford Hospital on Monday. He was 55 years old.
        He died after developing complications from surgery.
        Mr. Neumann, a Republican, was first appointed in February 1987 by Gov. William A. O'Neill to fill a vacancy on the five-member board.
        To take the job with the state, Mr. Neumann resigned from the General Assembly, where he had represented the 62d House District since 1979 and served as chairman of the Appropriations Committee. He was first selectman of Granby from 1969 to 1977.
        He was born in Hartford and earned a bachelor's degree from Wesleyan University and a master's degree from Syracuse University.
        He is survived by his wife, Diane, and four children.

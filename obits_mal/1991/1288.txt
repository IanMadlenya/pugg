
        David Hare Harshaw, former president of the John B. Stetson Company, the hat manufacturer, died Saturday at the Dowden Nursing Home in Newtown Square, Pa. He was 87 years old and lived in Drexel Hill, Pa.
        He died of a stroke, said his wife, June.
        Mr. Harshaw was with the Stetson Company, headquartered in Philadelphia, for 50 years. A graduate of the University of Pennsylvania, he joined the company in 1935 after working as a management specialist.
        He became secretary-treasurer of Stetson in 1941, financial vice president in 1945 and in 1947 was named president, a position he held until 1966. He was vice chairman from 1966 to 1984 and was a director from 1947 until he retired in 1986.
        He was a past president of the Hat Institute of New York.
        In addition to his wife, he is survived by a son, Dr. David Harshaw of Austin, Tex.; a daughter, Adele Smith of Radnor, Pa.; a stepson, William G. French Jr. of Maplewood, N.J.; two brothers, James of Lancaster, Pa., and John of Wawa, Pa; six grandchildren; two stepgrandchildren, and one great-granddaughter.

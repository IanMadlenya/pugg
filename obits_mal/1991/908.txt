
        Lawrence K. Miller, former editor and publisher of The Berkshire Eagle, died of pneumonia on Sunday. He was 83 years old.
        Under Mr. Miller, the paper, published in Pittsfield, won a Pulitzer Prize in 1973 for editorial writing.
        He became publisher of The Eagle in 1972 after the death of his brother, Donald. In 1985 he retired and turned over operation of The Eagle and the remainder of the family publishing business to his three sons.
        The family had also acquried The Bennington Banner and The Brattleboro Reformer in Vermont and The Torrington Register Citizen and The Winsted Evening Citizen in Connecticut.
        Mr. Miller is survived by his wife, Amy Bess; four children, a sister and four grandchildren.

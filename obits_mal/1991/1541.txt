
        Oliver Gintel, a furrier in New York for more than 60 years, died on Tuesday at his home in North Miami Beach, Fla. He was 88 years old. Mr. Gintel died of congestive heart failure, his son, Robert, said.
        Mr. Gintel owned the House of Black Diamond in Manhattan, a manufacturer of mink coats that he bought in the early 70's and for which he was also designer. He sold the business in the early 80's and retired in 1989.
        Mr. Gitel was a director and president emeritus of the Associated Fur Manufacturers Inc. and a board member of the Fur Trade Foundation.
        He was born in Cracow and immigrated to the United States at the age of 2. He entered the fur trade after graduating from Commerce High School.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Frieda Heitler; his son, Robert, of Greenwich, Conn.; three grandchildren, and four great-granddughters.

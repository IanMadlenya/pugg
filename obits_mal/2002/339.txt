
        George M. Szabad, a lawyer, businessman and former member of the board of governors of the American Jewish Committee, died on March 30 at his home in Haverford, Pa. He was 85.
        Mr. Szabad, a longtime resident of Scarsdale, N.Y., was also a former president of the committee's Westchester County chapter. He received the group's national distinguished leadership award in 1999.
        George Michael Szabad was born in Russia and brought up in Warsaw. He came to the United States in 1934, attended college and graduated from Columbia Law School.
        In 1952, he became president of the TelAutograph Corporation, a communications equipment manufacturer. In 1954, he joined the Burndy Corporation, a maker of electrical connectors, where he was a senior vice president and director.
        Mr. Szabad's first wife, the former Shirley Meyers, died in 1992. He is survived by his second wife, the former Janet Fulton Omholt, and the two children from his first marriage, Peter, of Madison, Va., and Ellen Ljung of Geneva, Ill.; six grandchildren; and five great-grandchildren.


        Stanley Saplin, a sports historian and statistician in New York for more than a half-century, died Friday night at New York University Medical Center. He was 88.
        From the 1950's to the 1990's, Mr. Saplin was the field-events announcer at the Millrose Games. He was the historical editor of the monthly Track and Field News and contributed retrospectives to the sports pages of The New York Times.
        He was the New York correspondent for the Chronicle of Higher Education for many years and, at various times, worked in public relations for the Rangers, New York University, Manhattanville College, Hebrew Union College and Baruch College.
        Mr. Saplin, who was born in New York, graduated from N.Y.U.'s School of Commerce in 1934 with a degree in accounting and finance. He served in the Navy from 1942 to 1945.
        His first wife, the former Pearl Brunner, died in 1988. He is survived by his second wife, the former Gail Cooper-Hecht; two daughters, Anne Saplin of Jamestown, R.I., and Elizabeth Saplin Morcillo of New York; and a stepdaughter, Allison Hecht of New York.


        Walter Fuld Gips Jr., a retired electronics industry executive and former treasurer of the American Jewish Committee, died Sunday at his home in Princeton, N.J. He was 81.
        The cause was complications from colon cancer and diabetes, according to his family.
        Mr. Gips retired in 1985 as president and chief executive of Gulton Industries in Princeton, an electronics concern.
        A native of Scarsdale, N.Y., Mr. Gips graduated from Yale University in 1941 and from the Harvard Business School two years later. He rose to the rank of captain in the Army Quartermaster Corps in World War II.
        Mr. Gips is survived by his wife of 54 years, Ann Arenberg Gips; three sons, Walter F. III, of Minneapolis, Robert L., of Cape Elizabeth, Me., and Donald H., of Boulder, Colo.; a daughter, Ellen C. Nee, of Ballyconneely, Ireland; two sisters, Kay Ross, of Hamden, Conn., and Mary Jane Pelz, of Purchase, N.Y.; and eight grandchildren.

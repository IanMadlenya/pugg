
        Berl Senofsky, a prize-winning violinist who became a respected music teacher, died on June 21 at his home in Baltimore. He was 77. The cause was complications from heart and lung disease, his family said. Born in Philadelphia to Russian immigrant parents, Mr. Senofsky studied at the Juilliard School and won the Walter W. Naumburg Competition in 1946. From 1951 to 1955 he was the assistant concertmaster of the Cleveland Orchestra, playing under George Szell. In 1955 he took first prize at the prestigious Queen Elisabeth Competition, held in Brussels. In 1965 Mr. Senofsky joined the Peabody Conservatory in Baltimore, and remained there for 31 years.

        Arthur O. Wellman, former president and chairman of the board of Wellman Inc. of Boston, and Wellman Industries Inc. of Johnsonville, S.C., textile concerns, died of leukemia Wednesday at his home in Palm Beach, Fla. He was 92 years old.
        Mr. Wellman was a former senior partner in Nichols & Company, Boston and New York commodities brokers with whom he started his career as a salesman. The firm was eventually reorganized as Wellman Inc., to which Wellman Industries was added. Mr. Wellman retired in 1975.
        In 1981 he pledged $15 million to Massachusetts General Hospital to build the Arthur Ogden and Gullan M. Wellman Research Building.
        Mr. Wellman was born in Brookline, Mass. His older brother, was William Wellman, the Oscar-winning Hollywood director.
        Arthur Wellman is survived by his wife, the former Gullan Karlsson; two sons, Arthur O. Wellman Jr., of Boston, and John, of Mount Gilead, S.C.; a daughter, Marjorie Bullock of Jupiter, Fla.; 10 grandchildren and 10 great-grandchildren.

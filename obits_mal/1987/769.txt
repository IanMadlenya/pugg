        Rudolph L. Lagai, professor emeritus of management at the New York University School of Business, died Saturday at the Park Manor Nursing home in Middletown, Conn., after a long illness. He was 79 years old and lived in Middletown.
        Professor Lagai joined the staff of the business school in 1944. He was promoted to full professor in 1959 and retired in 1966. From 1967 to 1980, he was chairman of the business division of Orange County Community College in Middletown.
        Mr. Lagai, who was born in Newark, graduated from New York University in 1948. He also earned a master's degree from the Stevens Institute of Technology.
        His wife, the former Helen Pond, died in 1968.
        He is survived by a daughter, Sandra King of Middletown; a son, Rudolph C., of Hartford, and five grandchildren.

        Samuel Y. Gibbon, a former investment banker and medical equipment manufacturer, died of a heart attack Monday while aboard the Royal Viking Sea on a trans-Atlantic voyage to Southampton, England. A resident of Longboat Key, Fla., he was 82 years old.
        Mr. Gibbon was a partner in W. H. Newbold's Son & Company Inc., Philadelphia investment bankers, from 1933 to 1941. After service as an intelligence officer with the Eighth Air Force in World War II, he founded Air Shields, Inc., a medical equipment manufacturing concern that developed the Isolette, an incubator for premature infants, and other medical equipment. In 1960 he sold Air Shields to Narco
        Inc. and served on the Narco board for five years. After he retired in 1966, he entered local politics and was the Longboat Key Mayor from 1969 to 1974.
        He leaves his wife, Virginia; a son, Samuel Jr. of Engelwood Cliffs, N.J.; a daughter, Virginia Nyhart of Brookline, Mass.; a sister, Marjorie Battles of Philadelphia, five grandchildren and one great-grandson.

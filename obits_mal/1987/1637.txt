        Jack Roth, a former reporter for The New York Times, died in his sleep Sunday at his home in Manhattan. He was 63 years old.
        Mr. Roth was a City College correspondent for The Times from 1944 to 1945, when he was appointed to the city staff. He began his career on the radio broadcast desk and was later transferred to the general news staff. In 1967 he left the paper to become a senior vice president of the Sydney S. Baron Corporation, a public relations and management consultant concern.
        He was born in Manhattan and graduated from City College in 1945.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Lorraine Kennedy; a daughter, Doria Sklar of Mystic, Conn., and three grandchildren.
        A service will be held at 2:30 P.M. today at the Riverside Memorial Chapel, Amsterdam Avenue at 76th Street.

        Dr. William L. Donn, a researcher in climatology, geology and atmospheric physics, died Tuesday at his home in Grand View-on-Hudson, N.Y. He was 69 years old.
        Dr. Donn was on the staff of the Lamont-Doherty Geological Observatory of Columbia University in Palisades, N.Y., from 1953 until his retirement in 1983. He remained active as a special research scientist until his death. He also continued to work as a professor of geology at Brooklyn College and at City College.
        In recent years, he refined a computer-based climate model used to predict temperature deviations across North America a month in advance.
        Dr. Donn served as deputy mayor of Grand View-on-Hudson from 1974 until this spring.
        He is survived by his wife, Renee; a son, Mathew; a daughter, Tara, both of Grand View-on-Hudson; a brother, Bertram, of Greenbelt, Md., and a sister, Marion, of Pasadena, Calif.

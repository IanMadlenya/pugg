        Robert Lee Henry, a tax lawyer and founder of the National Hemophilia Foundation, died Saturday of Alzheimer's disease at Wellington Regional Medical Center in West Palm Beach, Fla. He was 80 years old and lived in Wellington, Fla., and Locust Valley, L.I.
        Mr. Henry was also a certified public accountant and was a founder of the United States Equestrian Team, a director of the National Horse Show and a horse show judge and steward.
        He and his wife, the former Betty J. Ferguson, established the Hemophilia Foundation, which provides money for treatment and guidance to those afflicted, and also organized Hemophilia Research Inc., which raises money to study the blood disease. Their only child, Lee Ferguson Henry, was a victim of the disease who died in 1963 at the age of 21.
        In addition to his wife, he is survived by a sister, Maxine Gingold of Jamesburg, N.J.

        Dr. Samuel Engle Burr Jr., a former professor of education at the American University in Washington, died Monday at the Princeton (N.J.) Medical Center. He was 89 years old and lived in Hightstown, N.J.
        Dr. Burr, who was associated with the American University from 1947 to 1968, was a founder of the Aaron Burr Association and a sixth-generation descendant of Burr, who was the nation's third Vice President and killed Alexander Hamilton in a duel in 1804.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Ella Mount; a son, Samuel E. Burr 3d of Arlington Heights, Ill., and a sister, Anna T. Burr of Medford, N.J.

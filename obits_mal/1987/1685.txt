        Herbert A. Steinberg, a portrait painter and muralist, died of a heart attack Sunday in Exeter, N.H. He was 59 years old and lived in Roosevelt, N.J.
        A student of the painter Moses Sawyer, Mr. Steinberg was also a commercial artist who worked in film, theater and television. His 30-foot mural depicting crafts people in the visual arts was commissioned in 1974 by the International Brotherhood of Painting and Allied Trades, A.F.L.-C.I.O., and hangs in the United Unions building in Washington.
        Mr. Steinberg is survived by two sons, Stephen, of Atlantic City, and David, of Roosevelt; two brothers, Walter, of Huntington Station, L.I., and Joel, of San Diego, and a sister, Sonia Rothschild, of Washington.
        Correction:  October 10, 1987, Saturday, Late City Final Edition

        Dr. George H. Vineyard, a former director of the Brookhaven National Laboratory in Upton, L.I., died of cancer Saturday at University Hospital in Stony brook, L.I. He was 66 years old and lived in Bellport, L.I.
        Dr. Vineyard, a specialist in solid-state physics, joined Brookhaven in 1954 after teaching physics at the University Of Missouri. He was Brookhaven's director from 1973 to 1981, when he stepped down to return to full-time research at the laboratory.
        He was president-elect of the 37,000-member American Physical Society and was to have taken office in January 1988.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Phyllis Smith; a daughter, Missy, of Northamphon, Mass.; a son, John H., of Ithaca, N.Y.; two brothers, Benjamin of St. Joseph, and Barkley, of Sanibel Island, Fla., and four grandchildren.

        Francis Keil Decker, a lawyer active in Westchester County civic affairs, died of leukemia Monday at his home in Somers, N.Y. He was 84 years old.
        Mr. Decker specialized in trusts and estates and practiced, most recently, with the Manhattan firm of Decker Hubbard & Welden. He served as president of the Chappaqua Board of Education and had been a town councilman.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Helen Wills; a son, Francis Jr., of Brooklyn; a daughter, Ann Erda of Guilford, Conn.; a sister, Annette Kynaston of New Canaan, Conn., and six grandchildren.

        Donald H. Putnam, chairman of the Conrac Corporation, died of lung cancer Friday at his home in Greenwich, Conn. He was 62 years old.
        Mr. Putnam joined Conrac, an electronics company then known as the Giannini Controls Corporation, in 1951. He was elected a vice president in 1953, a director in 1956, president in 1958 and chairman in 1984.
        He was a director of the Western Union Corporation, the National Aviation and Technology Corporation and the National Telecommunications and Technology Fund.
        Mr. Putnam was born in Washington and served in the Naval Air Corps in World War II. He graduated from Princeton University with high honors in 1947, and a year later earned a master's degree in mechanical engineering at the university.
        He is survived by his wife, Eileen; two sons, Donald Jr., of Manhattan Beach, Calif., and Charles, of Redding, Conn,; a daughter, Betts, of Tucson, Ariz.; a brother, Borden R. Putnam Jr. of Franklin Lakes, N.J.; a sister, Mary Follette of Metuchen, N.J., and four grandchildren.
        A memorial service will be held at 3:30 P.M. tomorrow in Christ Church in Greenwich.

        H. Spencer Martin, a real-estate developer, died of liver disease and heart failure Sunday at his home in Manhattan. He was 68 years old.
        He worked for 35 years for the N. K. Winston Corporation, serving as vice president for shopping center development. For the last two years he had worked for Joshua A. Muss Associates, a land-development and real-estate concern in Washington.
        He received a Bachelor of Arts degree from Wesleyan College and served in the Air Corps during World War II.
        He is survived by his wife, Buff Cobb Martin of Manhattan, and two brothers, Stuart, of Hobe Sound, Fla., and John, of Los Angeles.

        Anthony R. DiPaola, a former first deputy commissioner of the New York City Department of Water Supply, Gas and Electricity in the 1950's, died of a liver ailment last Wednesday at New York University Medical Center. He was 78 years old and lived in Queens.
        Mr. DiPaola, who was born in New York City, was with the Water Department from 1951 to 1953. He then became a trial commissioner for the City Department of Environmental Protection until he retired in 1979, adjudicating employee grievance cases.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Phyllis Esposito: three sons, Robert of Manhattan, Richard of Fairfield, Conn., and Thomas of Hicksville, L.I.; a daughter, Mary Moran of Plainview, L.I.; 15 grandchildren, and one great-grandchild.

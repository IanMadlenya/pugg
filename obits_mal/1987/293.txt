        Gillis Purcell, a Canadian journalist who spent 41 years with The Canadian Press, 24 of them as general manager, died Monday in Toronto. He was 82 years old and had a stroke last summer.
        A native of Brandon, Manitoba, Mr. Purcell worked for local papers before he joined the news gathering cooperative in 1928. He became general manager in 1945, after having been a press officer for the Canadian Overseas Corps in World War II.
        Mr. Purcell is survived by his wife, Mary, and five children from his first marriage to Charlotte Fleming, who died in 1968.

        Arthur S. Lowell, a New York lawyer who was active in New York politics as a member of the City Fusion Party in the 1940's, died last Wednesday in Englewood (N.J.) Hospital. He was 69 years old and was a resident of Tenafly, N.J.
        Mr. Lowell graduated from Harvard Law School in 1941. While with the City Fusion party in the 1940's he headed the youth division and later became New York County chairman of the party, which was a political reform movement. He participated in the 1941 re-election campaign of Mayor Fiorello H. LaGuardia and, in 1949, in the unsuccessful mayoral campaign of Newbold Morris.
        He is survived by his wife, Anne; two daughters, Melinda Faivus of Engelwood and Nancy-Jo Segerman of Manhattan; two sisters, Hanna M. Lyttle and Vivian Lowe of Manhattan; a nephew, Lawrence A. Lyttle of Santa Monica, Calif., and two grandchildren.

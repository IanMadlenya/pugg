        Roland F. Marston, who started as an insurance salesman and worked his way up to chairman of the State Farm Mutual Automobile Insurance Company, has died. He was 63 years old.
        He died of liver cancer Tuesday at the St. Francis Medical Center, a company spokeswoman, Sharon Tallon, said today. He had been under treatment for several months, she said.
        Mr. Marston was elected board chairman of the nation's largest automobile insurance company in September 1985, she said. He started his career in 1956 as a State Farm agent in Miami.

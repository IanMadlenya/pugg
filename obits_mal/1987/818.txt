        Paul E. Gay, a realtor in Chelsea and Greenwich Village, died of a stroke Wednesday at St. Vincent's Hospital. He was 48 years old and lived in Chelsea.
        Mr. Gay was chairman and chief executive officer of James N. Wells' Sons and president of Wells & Gay, real estate concerns based in Chelsea. He was also a former vice president of the real-estate concerns of L. B. Kaye Associates and the William B. May Company.
        One of the projects undertaken by Wells & Gay was the development of Fitzroy Place, a row of houses built in the 1850's on West 22d Street, west of Ninth Avenue, in Chelsea.
        Mr. Gay was born in Charleston, W. Va., and was a graduate of the University of South Carolina.
        He was a founder and trustee of the Chelsea Community Church, a director from 1978 to 1984 of the McBurney branch of the Y.M.C.A. At his death he was a director of the Washington Square Music Festival, a trustee of the Hudson Guild in Chelsea and a member of the Council of Overseers of the Eugene Lang College of the New School for Social Research.
        He is survived by his parents, Nadia and Nathan Gay, of Sarasota, Fla. His longtime companion was Hunter Tillman.

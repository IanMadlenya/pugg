        Robert S. Danforth, former head of investment research at Goldman, Sachs & Company, died Saturday at New York Hospital after a brief illness. He was 60 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        He joined Goldman, Sachs in 1948 and became a partner of the investment banking and brokerage firm in 1962. He was the partner in charge of its investment research department until 1976, when he became a limited partner, remaining active as a consultant and adviser to the firm.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Joan Lewis; five children from his previous marriage, to the late Joanne Krashaar - William, of Manhattan; Patricia, of Los Angeles; Sally, of Dallas, and Jill Sauer and Elizabeth, both of Bozeman, Mont. - and a grandson.

        Gen. Fernando Pamplona, the Brazilian Army Chief of Staff, died Sunday when he jumped from a plane and his parachute failed to open, the army said today. He was 64 years old.
        General Pamplona died in Foz do Iguacu on the Brazilian-Paraguayan border, where he was attending the world sky-diving championship.
        The army press office said that he jumped from a height of 13,000 feet and that his main parachute failed to open. His reserve chute opened too late. The general died on the way to the hospital from multiple fractures, the press office said.
        General Pamplona was formerly an instructor at the army's parachutist instruction center in Rio.

        James A. Dwyer, a former president of Dwyer Lighterage Inc., a tugboat and barge business in New York Harbor, died Tuesday at the Albany Avenue Nursing Home in Kingston, N.Y. He was 92 years old.
        Mr. Dwyer headed the company, which has ceased operation, starting in the 1940's. The company built and operated barges and carried dry cargo, grain and cement. It was also a ship chandler.
        Surviving are his wife, Mary; four daughters, Elizabeth Ryan, Anne Dwyer and Rosemary Gruner, all of Kingston, and Margaret Abernethy of High Falls, N.Y.; two sons, Peter, of Kingston, and James, of Willsboro, N.Y.; two brothers, John, of Manhattan, and Thomas, of Pelham, N.Y.; 22 grandchildren, and four great-grandchildren.

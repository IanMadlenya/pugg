        Godfrey Bloch, a former carpet manufacturer, died at Lenox Hill Hospital on Wednesday. He was 86 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Bloch was the inventor and patentee of a number of textile processes, some of which were licensed to E. I. duPont de Nemours & Company. He also was an official with the National Recovery Administration in the 1930's and the Small Business Administration in the 1960's.
        He is survived by his wife, Elsie; a daughter, Anne Snee, and a son, Godfrey C., both of Manhattan.

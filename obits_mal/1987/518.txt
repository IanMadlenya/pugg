        James H. Stubblebine, an art historian at Rutgers University, died of a heart attack Tuesday at St. Vincent's Hospital in Manhattan. He was 66 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Dr. Stubblebine's area of expertise was Italian paintings of the early Renaissance, spanning the late 13th and 14th centuries. He joined New Jersey's state university in 1957 and in 1977 achieved the highest rank on the Rutgers faculty, that of Distinguished Professor II. During his 30 years at Rutgers he helped found the graduate department of art history.
        He was born in Manhattan and graduated from Harvard in 1942.
        He is survived by two brothers, Robert, of Concord, Mass., and Richard, of Belmont, Mass.

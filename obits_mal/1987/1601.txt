        Dr. George R. Meneely, a founder of the Louisiana State University Medical Center, died Sept. 6 at his home in Shreveport, La. He was 75 years old.
        Dr. Meneely headed the physiology and biophysics department at Louisiana State from 1975, when the medical school opened, until his retirement in 1982.
        He specialized in research on hypertension, pulmonary function and nuclear medicine, and was a founding member of the American Federation for Clinical Research.
        Dr. Meneely, who earned his medical degree from Cornell University in 1937, was associated from 1943 to 1962 with Vanderbilt University, where he began his research in nuclear medicine.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Leslie Stewart; two daughters, Judith Underwood Meneely of Newport, R.I., and Eleanor Meneely Chapnick of Westport, Conn.; a son, George R. Meneely 3d of San Diego, and six grandchildren.

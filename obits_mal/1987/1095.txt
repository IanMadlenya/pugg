        Dudley Holbrook Mills, former president and chairman of the Discount Corporation, a dealer in United States Government securities, died Saturday at the Presbyterian Home at Meadow Lakes in Hightstown, N.J., where he lived. He was 93 years old.
        Mr. Mills, a native New Yorker, joined the Discount Corporation in Manhattan after World War I. He was president from 1934 to 1944 and chairman from 1944 until he retired in 1959.
        He graduated from Princeton University in 1915 and from the Harvard Graduate School of Business Administration in 1917.
        He is survived by two sisters, Louise Cunningham of Bedford, N.Y., and H. Lee Petty of Washington; two sons, Gordon, of Greenwich, Conn., and Bradford, of Princeton, N.J.; 13 grandchildren and 4 great-grandchildren.

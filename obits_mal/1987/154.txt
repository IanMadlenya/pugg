        Thomas W. Eglin, the dean of students at the Lawrenceville School, died of a brain tumor Wednesday at his home in Lawrenceville, N.J. He was 55 years old.
        Mr. Eglin, who was born in Philadelphia, was a 1950 graduate of the Haverford School in Haverford, Pa. He graduated from Princeton University in 1954 and received his master's degree in education from Harvard in 1959.
        Mr. Eglin served as assistant director of admissions at Princeton University for several years before joining the Lawrenceville faculty in 1962. In addition to his teaching duties at Lawrenceville, a private schools for boys, he served as a track coach and, in 1970, was named dean of students.
        Mr. Eglin is survived by his wife, the former Edith Baird; two sons, Thomas Jr. of Manhattan, and Edward of Groton, Mass.; two daughters, Cintra Eglin Willcox of Dallas and Linda H. Eglin of Manhattan; his mother, Mrs. Archibald C. Eglin of Gladwyne, Pa., and two sisters, Mrs. David W. Maxey of Gladwyne, and Ann E. Allen of Washington.

        Leonor F. Loree 2d, a retired vice chairman and director of the Chase Manhattan Bank, died Thursday at the Morristown (N.J.) Memorial Hospital from complications of pneumonia. He was 73 years old and lived in Millington, N.J.
        Mr. Loree retired in 1975 as the bank's vice chairman for worldwide banking, although he remained on the board of directors for another year.
        He was born in West Orange, N.J., and was a graduate of Yale University. Mr. Loree joined Chase in 1944 after six years with the American Trust Company of San Francisco. He served on the boards of directors of a number of corporations.
        He is survived by his wife, Elizabeth; three sons, Leonor 3d, of Richmond, Va., Peter, of Westfield, N.J., and Michael, of Gillette, N.J.; three daughters, Ghislaine Kirby of Houston, Margot Olsen of Manhattan and Mary Fishmann of Beverly Hills, Calif.; a brother, Robert Jr., of Rye, N.Y.; two sisters, Margaret Tubman of Otego, N.Y., and Alix Tubman of Sun City, Ariz., and 13 grandchildren.

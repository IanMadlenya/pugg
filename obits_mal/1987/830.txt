        William Morton, a star quarterback for Dartmouth in the early 1930's and a member of the College Football Hall of Fame, died Saturday. He was 77 years old.
        Mr. Morton died at Mary Hitchcock Memorial Hospital two days after he had participated in a ceremony naming a Dartmouth dormitory in his honor. After graduating from Dartmouth in 1932, Mr. Morton became an investment banker with Chase Manhattan, then founded his own firm, which was acquired by American Express in 1966. Mr. Morton was named American Express president in 1968 and served in the post for six years.

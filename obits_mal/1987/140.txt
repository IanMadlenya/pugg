        Ansel Edward McLaurine Talbert, an aviation writer and editor for 50 years, died Oct. 7 at his home in Bridgeport, Conn. He was 75 years old.
        Before World War II, Talbert's interviews with Jimmy Doolittle, Charles Lindbergh, Howard Hughes and Billy Mitchell helped call attention to the importance of military aircraft.
        He was aviation editor for The New York Herald Tribune from 1953 until the paper closed in 1966. He later wrote about aviation for two trade publications, Travel Agent Magazine andAir Cargo News.

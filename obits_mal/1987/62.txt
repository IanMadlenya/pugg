        Reinhard A. Hohaus, a former insurance executive and an adviser to governmental bodies, died Monday at his home in Westport, Conn. He was 90 years old.
        Mr. Hohaus was a retired executive vice president and director of the Metropolitan Life Insurance Company. He had been the company's chief actuary.
        In 1937 he was a member of the United States Senate advisory committee on Social Security.
        As a specialist on group and social insurance and their relation to the economic problems of old age, unemployment and health, Mr. Hohaus also served with New York State agencies. They included the Commission on Pensions, the Civil Service Commission's pension advisory council, the Comptroller's Advisory Council for the state employee retirement system, and the Workmen's Compensation Board's committee on disability benefits.
        Mr. Hohaus joined Metropolitan Life's actuarial division in 1921. He was appointed senior vice president and chief actuary in 1960 and was elected to the board of directors in 1962. In 1963 he was appointed executive vice president and retired that year, remaining a director until 1969.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Agnes Hyatt; two daughters, Gretchen Siebel of Manhattan and Joanna Kostraba of Fairfield, Conn., and five grandchildren.

        Isaac Pacht, a prison reformer and former judge who helped revise the California Penal Code, died Saturday. He was 96 years old.
        Mr. Pacht was appointed to the bench in Los Angeles Superior Court in 1931. In 1940 he was appointed to the State Board of Prison Directors. As chairman that year, he appointed the renown penologist Clinton Duffy as warden of San Quentin.

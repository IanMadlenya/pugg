        Jess Larson, the first Administrator of the General Services Administration and former chairman and president of the Air Force Association, died Wednesday. He was 82 years old.
        Mr. Larson served as general counsel to the War Assets Admininstration in 1946 and became Administrator of the agency a year later. In 1949, President Truman nominated Mr. Larson to head the Federal Works Agency. Less than a month later, he became Administrator of the General Services Administration, which was created that year, and served in that post until 1953.
        Mr. Larson, a retired major general in the Air Force Reserve, served as chairman and president of the Air Force Association from 1964 to 1971.
        Mr. Larson, who was born in Mill Creek, Okla., was counsel to Stoels, Rives, Boley, Fraser & Wyse, a Washington law firm, at the time of his death.
        He is survived by his wife, Zdenka Sokitch, and two brothers.

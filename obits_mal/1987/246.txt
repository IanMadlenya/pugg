        Harold Goldberg, associate executive vice chairman of the United Israel Appeal, died of cancer on Monday at New York Hospital. He was 50 years old and lived in Nanuet, N.Y.
        Mr. Goldberg became fiscal manager of the organization in 1973. Previously, he held similar positions with New York University, I.B.M., CBS, U.S. Printing Inc. and the Twentieth Century Fund. He also taught banking and finance at Fairleigh Dickinson University.
        He was born in Jersey City and was a graduate of New York University's School of Commerce and the Graduate School of Business.
        Mr. Goldberg is survived by his wife, the former Rosalind Rovner; his mother, Rose Goldberg of Miami; a son, Andrew, and a daughter, Dana, both of Nanuet.

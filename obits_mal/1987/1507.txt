        H. Bourke Weigel, a New York architect for nearly half a century, who served as coordinator on the design of the United Nations headquarters, died of liver cancer Sunday at Phelps Memorial Hospital in Tarrytown, N.Y. He was 75 years old and lived in Scarborough-on-Hudson, N.Y.
        A native of New York City and a graduate of the Cornell University College of Architecture, Mr. Weigel did extensive architectural and planning work overseas.
        As a partner in the firm of Damaz & Weigel, he developed the master plan and designed several buildings at the State University of New York campus in Stony Brook, L.I. At his death he was vice president of Urbahn Associates, specializing in design and construction of government centers.
        Mr. Weigel is survived by his wife, the former Mary Laura Sullivan; three daughters, Alison Hain of Oyster Bay Cove, L.I., Lucia Hrinyak of Hastings, Minn., and Andrea Clark of Ridgeland, S.C., and five grandchldren.

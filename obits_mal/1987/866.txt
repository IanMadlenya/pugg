        Irving N. Epstein, an apparel industry executive in Manhattan for more than 50 years, died of cancer Wednesday at New York Hospital. He was 82 years old and lived in Manhattan and Sarasota, Fla.
        From 1935 to 1968, Mr. Epstein was executive vice president of the women's intimate-apparel division of the M. C. Shrank Company, which was purchased in 1968 by the Van Raalte division of Cluett Peabody & Company Inc. From 1970 to 1985, Mr. Epstein was a sales executive with the intimate-apparel division of Wilker Brothers Company in Manhattan.
        As an expert on textile flammability, Mr. Epstein was a consultant to government agencies and insurance companies.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Pearl Osborn; a daughter, Arleen Robinowitz of Sarasota; two sons, Matthew, of Manhattan, and Andrew Terriss, of Scarsdale, N.Y., and five grandchildren.

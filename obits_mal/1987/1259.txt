        Javier A. White, the managing partner of Stevens, Hinds & White, one of the most influential law firms in Harlem, died of a heart attack Wednesday at Lincoln Hospital in the Bronx. He was 42 years old and lived in Washington Heights.
        Stevens, Hinds & White was founded by the late Hope L. Stevens, who also founded several black-owned businesses. The firm counted several Caribbean and African nations among its clients.
        A native of Costa Rica, Mr. White moved to the United States with his mother when he was 11 years old, settling in the Bronx. He was a graduate of Princeton University and the Columbia University Law School.
        He began his legal career with a Wall Street law firm but a year later became a staff attorney with the Bedford-Stuyvesant Restoration Corporation in Brooklyn. He became its director of property management before leaving in 1979 to join Stevens, Hinds & White.
        He is survived by his wife, Renee; a daughter, Naomi; a son, Javier; his mother, Clara White; his stepfather, Richard White, and a sister, Renee White. All live in Manhattan.

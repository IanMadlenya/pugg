        Benjamin J. Kotsher, president of Communication Channels Inc., an Atlanta-based business magazine publishing company, died of pulmonary fibrosis Tuesday at St. Joseph's Hospital in Atlanta. He was 64 years old and lived in Atlanta.
        Mr. Kotsher, who was born in New York City, received a bachelor's degree from New York University's school of journalism in 1943. He served as an Army intelligence officer in World War II.
        He was working for the Palmerton Publishing Company in New York City when it was acquired by Communications Channels in 1973. The company moved to Atlanta in 1977, and Mr. Kotsher became president in 1981.
        Communication Channels publishes magazines such as Business Atlanta, National Real Estate Investor, American City and County, Robotics World and Adhesives Age and various business directories.
        Mr. Kotsher is survived by his wife, Helen; a son, Paul, of Washington; a daughter, Carol Marden, of Raleigh, N.C.; a sister, Esther Ohayon of Bronxville, N.Y., and a grandchild.

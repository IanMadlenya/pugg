        Former United States Representative Howard W. Robison, who in his 17 years in Congress eventually became dean of New York State's Republican Congressional delegation, died of a heart ailment Saturday at his home in Rehoboth Beach, Del. He was 71 years old.
        Mr. Robison was born in Oswego, N.Y., and graduated from Cornell University, where he also earned a law degree. He was first elected to the House in a special election in 1958 from a district that included Binghamton, Elmira and Ithaca.
        Mr. Robison served on the House Appropriations Committee and was the ranking Republican on its Post Office and General Government Subcommittee.
        Mr. Robison is survived by his wife, Trudy; two sons, Howard W. Jr., of Columbia, Md., and Douglas E., of St. Petersburg, Fla.; a sister, Elinor Washburn of Arcadia, Fla., and four grandchildren.

        Donald C. Ringwald, a historian and writer on steamboat navigation, died of cancer Friday at his home in Loudonville, N.Y. He was 70 years old.
        Mr. Ringwald, whose first job was selling tickets on a Hudson River steamboat, wrote dozens of articles and three books on maritime subjects, including ''Hudson River Day Line.''
        He was born in Kingston, N.Y., and served in the Army in the Pacific in World War II, leaving with the rank of captain. After the war, he held financial jobs in several Veterans Administration hospitals and retired as financial manager of the veterans' hospital in Albany in 1974.

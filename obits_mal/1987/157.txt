        Harry Liman, a retired New York dress manufacturer, died of complications after surgery Thursday at New York University Medical Center. He was 89 years old and lived in Lawrence, L.I.
        Mr. Liman was the founder in 1920 of Liman & Son, where he worked until he retired in 1972.
        He was born in Manhattan and graduated from City College in 1918.
        He is survived by a daughter, Gladys Phillips of Oceanside, L.I.; a son, Arthur L. Liman, the Manhattan lawyer who was chief counsel of the Senate Iran-contra Committee, and five grandchildren.

        The Rev. Dr. Earle H. Ballou, for many years a Congregational missionary in China, died Sunday at a nursing home in Townshend, Vt. He was 93 years old and lived in Brookline, Vt.
        Dr. Ballou served under the American Board of Commissioners for Missions in China from 1917 until World War II. During the war he and wife were interned by the Japanese in the Philippines for 21 months.
        He also had been general secretary of the Congregational Church in China and secretary of the National Christian Council of China.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Thelma Havens; four sons, Hubbard, of Chester, Vt.; Laurence, of Brookline, Vt.; Christopher, also of Brookline, and Robert, of Charlo, Mont., 13 grandchildren and 12 great-grandchildren.

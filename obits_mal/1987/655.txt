        Woolf Colvin, a retired New York City high school principal, died Tuesday at a nursing home in Port Chester, N.Y. He was 90 years old and lived in Scarsdale, N.Y.
        Dr. Colvin was a member of the public school system from 1925 to 1960 and taught history, social studies and economics. In 1946, he was appointed principal of the Seward Park High School on the Lower East Side. When he retired in 1960, Dr. Colvin was principal of the George W. Wingate High School in Brooklyn.
        After his retirement, Dr. Colvin was in charge of a juvenile delinquency program for the former Federal Department of Health, Education and Welfare, now the Department of Health and Human Services, and then he taught economics at Queens College and York College, both of which are units of the City University of New York.
        He is survived by his wife, Helen Beckerman; a daughter, Naomi Gellman, of Manhattan; a son, Alex, of Oak Park, Mich.; a stepson, Dr. Barry Beckerman, of Chappaqua, N.Y., six grandchildren and two step-grandchildren.

        Forrest E. Kendall, a professor emeritus of biochemistry at Columbia University, died of a heart attack yesterday in North Shore University Hospital, in Manhasset, L.I. He was 88 years old and lived in Douglaston, Queens.
        Professor Kendall, who retired in 1972, was born in Victoria, Ill. He earned bachelor's and master's degrees and his doctorate, all in biochemistry, at the University of Illinois.
        He taught at Lafayette College, in Easton, Pa., before joining the Columbia faculty in 1927. At Columbia he became a biochemist in the university's College of Physicians and Surgeons and worked for many years at Goldwater Memorial Hospital on Roosevelt Island.
        He was a former president of the council of atherosclerosis of the American Heart Association.
        He is survived by his wife, Lydia; a daughter, Susan Winfield of Coram, L.I.; three brothers, Russell of Omaha, Albert of Alexandria, Va., and Donald of Akron, Ohio; two sisters, Florence Kendall and Eleanor Kendall of Akron, and one grandchild.

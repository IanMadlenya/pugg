        Henry W. Wenning, a former executive vice president of the Martin E. Segal Company, employee benefits, compensation and actuarial consultants, died of emphysema Tuesday at his home in Greenwich, Conn. He was 76 years old.
        In the 1930's and 1940's he was the national secretary treasurer of State, County and Municipal Workers of America. He joined the Segal company in 1944 and retired in 1971, when he was made a consultant.
        He was also the owner for a number of years of a book dealership in New Haven, called Modern Rare Books.
        He is survived by his wife, Adele; two daughters, Katherine, of Manhattan and Jessica, of Boston; two stepsons, Frederick Adler of Berkeley, Calif., William Adler of Los Angeles, and a grandson.

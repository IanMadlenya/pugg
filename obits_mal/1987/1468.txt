        W. Mason Smith, a counsel and former partner of the Manhattan law firm of Barrett Smith Schapiro Simon & Armstrong, died of complications from Parkinson's disease Sunday at his Manhattan home. He was 76 years old.
        Mr. Smith was a native of Staten Island and a graduate of St. Paul's School, Yale College and Harvard Law School. He was an assistant Corporation Counsel of New York City from 1938 to 1941 and a former director of the New York State Communities Aid Association, the Y.W.C.A. of New York City and the Brearley School.
        He served in the Navy in World War II in both the Atlantic and Pacific regions, rising to lieutenant commander.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Jane Prouty; a son, W. Mason 3d, of Cambridge, Mass.; a daughter, Olivia Leale, of Seattle; a sister, Eleanor Gaud, of Washington, and six grandchildren.

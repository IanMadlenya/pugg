        Bernard J. Hughes, an architect and artist, died of a heart attack Monday at his home in Manhattan. He was 92 years old.
        Mr. Hughes had his own practice from the 1930's to the early 1960's. Among his buildings were a group erected along the Hudson River from 52d Street to 72d Street for the New York Central Railroad and 13 buildings for the World's Fair in 1939.
        Mr. Hughes was also a painter whose medium was oils.
        He was born in Manhattan and had been a student at the Art Students League.
        There are no immediate survivors.

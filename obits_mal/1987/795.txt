        Albert H. Newman, a war correspondent, magazine editor and author, died of pneumonia Wednesday at Grand Strand Hospital in Myrtle Beach, S.C. He was 74 years old and lived in Manhattan and Myrtle Beach.
        Mr. Newman joined Newsweek magazine in the late 1930's and was its chief correspondent in World War II.
        From 1949 until 1957, he was managing editor of The Reporter magazine. Later he was the author of ''The Assassination of John F. Kennedy: The Reasons Why,'' published in 1970.
        Mr. Newman was born in Detroit and graduated from the University of Michigan.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Helen Fry; a son, John, of Minneapolis; a brother, Dr. Philip E. Newman of Paris, and four grandchildren.

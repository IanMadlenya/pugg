        Robert O. Driver, retired chairman and chief executive officer of the former Wilbur B. Driver Company of Newark, a manufacturer of metal alloy products, died Thursday in his home in West Orange, N.J. Mr. Driver, who also maintained a residence in Palm Beach, Fla., was 80 years old.
        Mr. Driver joined the company, which had been founded by his father, after graduating from the University of Pennsylvania's Wharton School in 1931. He became president of the company in 1946.
        In 1967, the company was merged into the Sylvania division of GTE Corporation, and Mr. Driver retired.
        He is survived by his wife, Gertrude; two daughters, Claire Redmond of West Orange and Ann Fitts of Charleston, S.C.; 10 grandchildren, and four great-grandchildren.

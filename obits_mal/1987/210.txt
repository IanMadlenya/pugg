        Liang Shih-chiu, a popular Chinese writer who devoted about 30 years to the translation of all of Shakespeare's works into Chinese, died Tuesday in a hospital here after suffering a heart attack, family members said today. He was 86 years old.
        Mr. Liang also was known for his studies of classical and contemporary English literature. He wrote a history of English literature in Chinese and compiled a Chinese-English dictionary.
        He began translating Shakespeare's plays in the 1930's and completed his project in 1967. He also wrote many essays.
        Mr. Liang, who was born in Beijing, was professor of English literature at Beijing University from 1934 to 1937. He came to Taiwan after the civil war on the China mainland in 1949, and taught at Taiwan's Normal University from 1949 to 1966. He studied at Harvard and Columbia Universities in 1924 and 1925.
        He is survived by his wife, Ching Ching, a son and a daughter.

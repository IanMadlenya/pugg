        John Henry Grady, a former vice president of I.B.M., died of cancer Sunday at Greenwich (Conn.) Hospital. He was 59 years old and lived in Greenwich.
        Mr. Grady joined I.B.M. in San Francisco in 1952 and served in a number of branch-office, sales and managerial positions. He was promoted to director of stockholder relations in 1963 and elected secretary of the corporation in 1970. He became a vice president in 1985, and retired in June 1986.
        Mr. Grady was born in San Mateo, Calif., and graduated from the University of California at Berkeley in 1951.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Alexandra Diepenbrock; four children, Kathleen Skelly of Boston, John H. Grady Jr. of Washington, Carolyn Grady of Boston and Alexander James Grady of Greenwich, and two sisters, Jacqueline Ray of San Mateo, Calif., and Virginia Cosgrove of San Francisco.

        Benjamin Grund, a former director and partner in Seidman & Seidman, an international accounting firm based in New York City, died of a heart attack last Friday at his home in Manhattan. He was 83 years old.  In 1920, Mr. Grund was hired as a file clerk by a founding partner of the firm. Two years later, he was made acting head of the New York office's tax department. In 1960, Mr. Grund - then the firm's first national director of taxation - was named one of the firm's first general partners, a position he held until his retirement in 1975.
        Mr. Grund was also president of the New York State Society of Certified Public Accountants from 1959 to 1960.
        Mr. Grund studied accounting at New York and Columbia universities and received a law degree from St. John's University.
        He is survived by his wife, Ruth; a son, Bruce; a daughter, Betty Ann; a sister, Augusta Rickarby, all of Manhattan, and five grandchildren.

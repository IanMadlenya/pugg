        Harav Moshe Steinberg, the rabbi who was the head of the Beth Din, the religious court of the Rabbinical Council of America, and a leading Talmudic scholar, died of a heart attack July 20 at Mount Sinai Hospital. He was 86 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Rabbi Steinberg was responsible for establishing and directing the largest Beth Din in the nation, serving Orthodox Jews on religious issues.
        Rabbi Steinberg was born in Poland. His father and grandfather were rabbis.
        Rabbi Steinberg became chief rabbi of Brody in Poland at the age of 33 and, after World War II, was named chief rabbi of Cracow. He came here in 1947.
        Surviving is his wife, the former Rachel Halpern.

        Oliver H. Renninger, a survivor of the first reported airplane crash, died Sunday of kidney failure at the Lebanon Veteran's Administration Medical Center. He was 103 years old.
        A native of Lancaster County, Mr. Renninger served in the Army from 1903 to 1909. In 1908, while a member of the Signal Corps he was assigned to Fort Myer, Va., to help the Wright brothers demonstrate their first plane to Army officials.
        Mr. Renninger was aboard when the plane crashed in September 1908, causing the death of Lieut. Thomas E. Selfridge. It was the first plane crash in the history of flight, Darrell Collins, park manager of the Wright Brothers Memorial in Kitty Hawk, N.C., said Monday.
        Correction:  February 19, 1987, Thursday, Late City Final Edition

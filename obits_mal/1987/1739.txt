        Dr. Hans W. Gatzke, a professor emeritus of history at Yale University, died of cancer Sunday in the Yale infirmary. He was 71 years old and lived in Weston, Conn.
        Dr. Gatzke, an authority on German foreign policy since World War I, was an associate professor and a full professor of history at the Johns Hopkins University from 1947 to 1964. He went to Yale in 1965 and became a professor emeritus last year.
        A former Guggenheim fellow, Dr. Gatzke wrote scholarly works. His most recent, ''Germany and the United States, a 'Special Relationship?' '' was published by the Harvard University Press in 1980.
        Dr. Gatzke was born in 1915 in Dulken, Germany. He attended the University of Bonn and the University of Munich before graduating from Williams College in 1938. He received a Ph.D. from Harvard in 1947.
        He served in the Army from 1944 to 1946, leaving as a second lieutenant.
        Surviving is a brother, Werner, of Krefeld, West Germany. Dr. Gatzke's companion of 30 years was David Gregory of Weston.

        George W. Kennedy, retired president and chairman of the Kelsey-Hayes Company in Romulus, Mich., an early manufacturer of wheels and brake components for automobiles, died Sunday at his home in Del Ray Beach, Fla. He was 96 years old.
        Mr. Kennedy joined the Kelsey Wheel Company in Detroit in 1919. The company merged with the Hayes Wheel Company, also of Detroit, in 1927 and Mr. Kennedy became president of the new corporation. He became chairman in the late 1950's. He retired in 1973, when Kelsey-Hayes was bought by the Fruehauf Corporation of Detroit.
        He was born in Cecil County, Md. and was a graduate of the Wharton School of the University of Pennsyvania.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Victoria Henry.

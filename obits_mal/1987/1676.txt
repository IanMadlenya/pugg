        Dr. Henry I. Fineberg, a retired pediatrician and former deputy commissioner of hospitals for New York City, died of a stroke Sunday at Huntington (L.I.) Hospital. He was 83 years old and lived in Dix Hills, L.I.
        Dr. Fineberg served as superintendent of Queens General Hospital in Jamaica, Queens, from 1942 to 1957. He was then appointed a deputy commissioner by Mayor Robert F. Wagner. , He held the post until 1961 then was executive vice president of the Medical Society of New York State until 1982.
        Dr. Fineberg was born in Cleveland, and received his undergraduate degree from Columbia University in 1924. He received his medical degree from Yale University School of Medicine in 1927.
        He is survived by his wife, Grace.; a son, Coleman, of East Northport, L.I.; a daughter, Karen Fineberg, of Dix Hills, and three grandchildren.

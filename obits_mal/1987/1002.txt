        Patrick J. Corbett, a former president of the New York State Sheriff's Association, died Tuesday. He had suffered a heart attack while playing golf in Syracuse. He was 79 years old and lived in Syracuse.
        Mr. Corbett headed the association in 1972 and 1973. From 1963 to 1978, he was the Onondaga County Sheriff, the first Democrat elected to the post.
        Mr. Corbett was a state police investigator 19 years and was also the chief investigator for the United States Office of Price Stabilization and the Onondaga District Attorney.
        He attended University College of Syracuse University and Cornell University.
        Surviving are his wife, Margaret; three daughters, Carol Comerford of Manhattan, and Kathleen Conroy and Conole Corbett, both of Syracuse, and a son, Gary, also of Syracuse.

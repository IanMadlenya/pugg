        Maj. Gen. William M. Creasy, former chief chemical weapons officer for the Army, died Sunday. He was 81 years old.
        He graduated from the United States Military Academy in 1926 and joined the Chemical Service in 1929.
        He became the officer in charge of technical design and acceptance in construction of the Pine Bluff Arsenal in 1941, and served as chief chemical officer for general supplies in the China-Burma-India theater of World War II.
        General Creasy retired from the Army in 1958 and worked for a contracting and engineering concern in New York until 1966.

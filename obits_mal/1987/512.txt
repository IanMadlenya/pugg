        Edmund Pendleton, organist at the American Church in Paris for 40 years and a composer and choirmaster, died Friday at his Paris home, his family said today. He was 86.
        Mr. Pendleton graduated from Columbia University before settling in Paris in 1925. He studied the organ with Marcel Dupre, composition with Paul Dukas and conducting with Charles Munch.
        He composed choral works, mainly on religious themes, for the American Church and was choirmaster of the Philharmonic Choir of Paris for 25 years.
        Mr. Pendleton was music critic of The New York Herald Tribune's Paris edition for 20 years and taught music at the American College in Paris.

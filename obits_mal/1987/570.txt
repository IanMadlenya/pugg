        J. Raymond Ylitalo, former United States Ambassador to Paraguay, died of cancer on Feb. 10 at Sibley Memorial Hospital in Washington. He was 70 years old and lived in Washington.
        Mr. Ylitalo was a career Foreign Service officer who joined the State Department in 1946. He served in Helsinki, Washington, Munich, Manila and Mexico City before being named Ambassador to Paraguay in 1969. From 1973 until he retired in 1976, he was Consul General in Toronto.
        He graduated magna cum laude from St. Olaf College in Northfield, Minn., and earned a master's degree in business administration from Northwestern University in 1938.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Jean Sarchet; a son, John, of Burtonsville, Md.; three daughters, Georgianne Farness of Flagstaff, Ariz.; Mary Katherine Ylitalo of Calgary, Alberta, and Sara Lisa Ylitalo of San Jose, Costa Rica, and eight grandchildren.

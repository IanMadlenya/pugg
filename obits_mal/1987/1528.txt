        Edward W. Whittemore, the former chairman and chief executive officer of American Brands Inc., died Saturday at Stamford (Conn.) Hospital. He was 64 years old and lived in Stamford.
        Mr. Whittemore, who was born in New York City and graduated from Columbia College in 1947, started his business career in 1947 as a sales trainee for the Wilson Jones Company, a manufacturer of office supplies in Chicago. In the 1960's the company was acquired by Swingline Inc., and Mr. Whittemore was subsequently named to Wilson Jones's top executive post.
        In 1970, Swingline was acquired by American Brands, an international consumer products and financial services company in Old Greenwich, Conn., and Mr. Whittemore became president of Swingline in 1975.
        He was elected a director of American Brands in 1977, vice president of subsidiary administration in 1978 and executive vice president for operations in 1979. He served as chairman and chief executive officer from Jan. 1, 1981, until June 15, 1987.
        Mr. Whittemore also was chairman of the National Office Products Association's research committee and head of its manufacturing division.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Jeanne McConnochie; a son, Edward Jr., of Fort Lee, N.J.; a daughter, Jeannette, of San Francisco; a sister, Florence W. Traviesas of Atlantis, Fla., and a brother, Harold C., of Cincinnati.

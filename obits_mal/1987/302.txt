        Joel Gordon, a retired business executive and former civil servant, died of a heart attack Sunday in Silver Spring, Md., while on his way to Florida. He was 78 years old and lived in Rosbury, Conn.
        Mr. Gordon retired in 1979 as executive vice president of Cars Overseas, a Manhattan concern that imported and leased cars. He joined the company in the late 1950's. A 1930 honors graduate of Harvard, Mr. Gordon worked for the Works Projects Administration and the United Nations Relief and Rehabilitation Administration in Washington before joining the staff of the United Nations Secretariat in New York.
        Mr. Gordon is survived by his wife, Anne; three daughters, Dr. Julie Cohen of Scarsdale, N.Y., Kathie Gordon Morin of Manhattan, and Dr. Jean Gordon of Palo Alto, Calif.; a son, Lewis, of Anchorage; six sisters, Ida Karp of Staten Island, Adele Rose and Evelyn Blum, both of Brighton, Mass., Ruth Brackman of Stoughton, Mass., Florence Herman of Washington, and Lenore Kergin of Greenville, S.C., and four grandchildren.

        Enno W. Ercklentz, a retired banker and industrialist, died Friday at Lenox Hill Hospital. He was 83 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Ercklentz, who was born in Breslau, Germany, came here in 1926 and became the New York representative of the Commerzbank of Berlin.
        In 1951, he became president and chief executive officer of the Kearny (N.J.) Manufacturing Company, which made chemicals for the textile industry. He retired in 1968, and the company was sold.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Hildegard Schlubach; a daughter, Hildegard E. Mahoney of Manhattan; two sons, Enno Jr., of Manhattan, and Alexander, of Greenwich, Conn., and nine grandchildren.

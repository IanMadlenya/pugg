        Ernest D. Ricca, former Eastern executive secretary of the Directors Guild of America, died Saturday at Mount Sinai Hospital, where he had been admitted with a heart condition. He was 69 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Ricca, who was born in Detroit, was a graduate of Wayne University, now Wayne State University, and Columbia University, where he received a master's degree in communications. In the 1940's and 1950's he directed such radio shows as ''The Romance of Helen Trent,'' ''Stella Dallas'' and ''Valiant Lady.'' He was the executive producer of the television shows ''Love of Life,'' ''The Secret Storm,'' and ''Hotel Cosmopolitan.''
        He was the Guild's Eastern executive secretary from 1962 to 1983, when he became administrator of the Guild's producers training program.
        He is survived by his wife, Jeanne; two sons, John, of Walnut Creek, Calif., and Gregory, of Ridgewood, N.J.; a daughter, Susan Broad of Naperville, Ill., and six grandchildren.

        Josiah Willard, a retired partner in the law firm of White & Case, died after a long illness last Thursday at his home in Manhattan. He was 84 years old.
        Mr. Willard, a specialist in taxation, joined White & Case upon his graduation from Harvard Law School in 1927 and remained with the firm until his retirement in 1978, except for a period in 1933 and 1934 when he served in the office of the Treasury Department's general counsel in Washington. He became a partner at White & Case in 1946.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Eugenia Bovey; a brother, Charles B., of Somers, N. Y.; a son, Charles A., of Manhattan, and a daughter, Margaret W. Bailey of Tampa, Fla.

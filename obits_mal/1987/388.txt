        Robert Elston, an actor on Broadway and television, died of an AIDS-related pulmonary embolism in the Amsterdam Medical Center in the Netherlands on Dec. 10, his lawyer, Roy Barnitt of New York City, has announced. Mr. Elston was 53 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Elston made his Broadway debut in 1958 and played leading roles in the Broadway productions of ''Spoon River Anthology,'' ''You Know I Can't Hear You When the Water's Running,'' and ''Vivat! Vivat! Regina.'' He also acted in many national tours.
        He had continuing roles in the daytime television serials ''Love of Life'' and ''As the World Turns,'' and appeared on many other shows. He also made more than 100 commercials.
        Mr. Elston was born in Manhattan. A graduate of City College, he earned a master's degree in English from New York University and also attended the Sorbonne in Paris.
        He was a member of the H/B Studio workshop company, and in 1975 formed his own company, the American Renaissance Theater. In its 12 years he produced 35 plays, among them Sylvia Plath's ''Difficult Borning.''
        Mr. Elston was the co-author with Murray Grand of a musical, ''Murder at the Gaiety,'' and wrote ''After Many a Summer,'' based on Chekhov's works. He also wrote two one-man shows, ''Portrait of a Man'' and ''Notes,'' which he co-produced with his longtime companion, John Bacher.
        Mr. Elston was a teacher of acting for 25 years. Bette Midler and Gretchen Cryer are among those who studied with him.

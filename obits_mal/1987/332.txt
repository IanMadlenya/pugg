        Joseph Marion Haggar, who founded and developed the Haggar Apparel Company, a major producer of men's slacks, sports coats and suits, died of heart failure Tuesday. He was 94 years old.
        Mr. Haggar founded the company in 1926, bringing assembly-line techniques to the manufacture of clothing. The company now has 16 plants in 14 cities and employs 7,000 people.

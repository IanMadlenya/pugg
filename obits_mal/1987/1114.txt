        Dr. Charles C. Wolcott, a retired ear, nose and throat specialist and a pioneer in the field of bronchoscopy - the use of a tubular instrument to examine and treat the inside of the windpipe -died Monday at Lawrence Hospital in Bronxville, N.Y. He was 94 years old and lived in Bronxville.
        A graduate of Kansas State University, he received his medical degree from the University of Michigan in 1917 and served as a Navy surgeon in World War I. In 1932 he helped establish the bronchoscopic department of Grasslands Hospital in Westchester. He was also a bronchoscopist at Bellevue Hospital and a consultant and lecturer on bronchoscopy at other hospitals. He was a past president of the New York Bronchoscopic Society.
        He is survived by his wife, Gertrude; two stepdaughters, Mary Van Riper of Newtown, Conn., and Elizabeth Hufnagel of Winthrop, Me., and a stepson, William H. Heep of New Rochelle.

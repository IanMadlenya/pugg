        Dr. Louis M. D'Esopo, a Tarrytown, N.Y., physician who practiced internal medicine and cardiology for more than 50 years, died Sunday at Middlesex Memorial Hospital in Middletown, Conn., after suffering a cardiac problem. A resident of Tarrytown, he was 86 years old.
        In the 1950's, Dr. D'Esopo helped two local hospitals, in Tarrytown and Ossining, to form a new hospital, Phelps Memorial in North Tarrytown.
        He is survived by his wife, Helen; a daughter, Ann Phillips of Tarrytown; three sisters, Grace Cassell of Stuart, Fla., and Carmella and Teresa D'Esopo, both of West Hartford, Conn.; three brothers, Dr. Anthony, of Needham, Mass., Dr. Joseph, of Hamden, Conn., and Dr. Nicholas, of Woodbridge, Conn., three grandchildren and one great grandchild.

        Harry Greenwald, who invented the coin meters on coin-laundry washing machines and dryers, died of a stroke Friday at Flushing Hospital in Queens. He was 75 years old and lived in Whitestone, Queens.
        Mr. Greenwald was a co-founder and retired president of Greenwald Industries, which sold the devices to coin laundries throughout the world.
        He is survived by his wife, Dorothy; a daughter, Janet Brown of Jericho, L.I.; a son, Kenneth, of Colorado Springs, Colo., and three sisters, Helen Kinkel, Carolyn Wellner and Laura Shirley, all of Arizona.

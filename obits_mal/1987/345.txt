        Fletcher Doughitt Richards Sr., a retired advertising executive, died Friday at the Albany Medical Center Hospital after a brief illness. He was 95 years old and lived in Clifton Park, N.Y., where he had moved from Greenwich, Conn.
        Mr. Richards was president of Campbell-Ewald of New York Inc. from from 1935 to 1948, after which the company took his name, handling accounts such as United States Rubber and Eastern Air Lines. He retired in 1964.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Elsa Schoenburg; a daughter-in-law, Lucille Chapman Edwards; six grandchildren, and four great-grandchildren.

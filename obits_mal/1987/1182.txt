        The Rev. Dr. Jitsuo Morikawa, a former official of the American Baptist Churches, died of stomach cancer Monday at the University of Michigan Hospital. He was 75 years old and lived in Ann Arbor, Mich.
        From 1944 to 1956 he was pastor of the First Baptist Church in Chicago. After that he served the American Baptist denomination at its Valley Forge, Pa., headquarters for 19 years, many of them as associate executive secretary.
        He was named interim senior minister of Riverside Church in Manhattan in 1976 and afterward served at Baptist churches in Ridgewood, N.J., and Ann Arbor.
        He was a vice president of the American Baptist Churches in 1984 and 1985.
        At the start of World War II, Dr. Morikawa, who was born in Canada, was interned for 18 months in a relocation center for Americans of Japanese ancestry.
        He is survived by his wife, Hazel, and two sons, Andrew, of Blacksburg, Va., and Dennis, of Philadelphia.

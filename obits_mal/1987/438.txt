        Thomas E. Powell Jr. of Burlington, N.C., the founder of two biological supply companies, died Thursday at Alamance County Hospital, after a three-month illness. He was 88 years old. Dr. Powell, who was born in Warrenton, N.C., and had a doctorate in biology, founded the Carolina Biological Supply Company in 1927 and headed its operation from 1936 until his death. In 1945, he founded Waubun Laboratories Inc. in Schriever, La., and, in 1970, Rana Laboratories in Brownsville, Tex., an operating division of Waubun Laboratories.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Annabelle Council; six sons, William, Samuel, Thomas 3d, John and James, all of Burlington, and Joseph, of Sanger, Tex.; two daughters, Dr. Annabelle Guy of Burlington, and Sophia Wolfe of Odenton, Md.; a sister, Caroline Powell of Elon College, N.C., 15 grandchildren and 2 great-grandchildren.

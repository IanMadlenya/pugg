        Albert C. Winters, president of Manhattan Store Interiors, Inc., a Brooklyn company that makes and sells store fixtures, died of a heart attack last Sunday at his home in Mill Neck, L.I. He was 66 years old.
        Mr. Winters co-founded a small store fixture business after World War II that became Manhattan Store Interiors, a leading company in the field with about 250 employees, counting those of its subsidiaries.
        Mr. Winters designed fixtures and helped design store interiors for department stores and other retail outlets. He held a patent on an adjustable glass cube display fixture that is widely used.
        Mr. Winters, who served in the Army Signal Intelligence Corps in World War II, was a graduate of New York University and Washington University in St. Louis.
        He is survived by his wife, Sandra; two sons, Preston, of White Plains, and Jordan, of Mill Neck; a daughter, Tracy, of Mill Neck; a sister, Florence Feinstein of Fort Lee, N.J., and a grandson.

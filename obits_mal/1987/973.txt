        Harvey Abramson, a consultant in the biomedical field who helped design life-support systems, died of a brain tumor Sunday night at St. Rose's Home in Manhattan. He was 56 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Abramson was once a designer of packaging. In the medical field, he designed an intrauterine birth-control device and a method of disposing of hypodermic syringes safely.
        For the last 10 years he was a consultant on research and development for Becton, Dickinson & Company of Paramus, N.J., a health-care products manufacturer. During this time he and his family lived on a boat at the 79th Street marina.
        He is survived by his wife, Suzanne; a daughter, Illana; a son, Ari; his parents, Joseph and Molly Abramson, all of Manhattan, and a brother, Boris, of Fort Pierce, Fla.

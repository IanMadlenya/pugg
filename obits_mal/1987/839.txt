        William H. Stoneman, former chief European correspondent for the Chicago Daily News, died Saturday in Paris. He was 83 years old and lived in a suburb of Paris since his retirement in 1969.
        Mr. Stoneman was born May 15, 1904, in Grand Rapids, Mich., and grew up in Ann Arbor, where he graduated from the Universty of Michigan in 1925.
        During his senior year at the universtiy, he served as sports editor of the Michigan Daily.
        Upon graduation, he went to work for the Chicago Daily News. Over the next four decades, he was assigned to various foreign posts, including Rome, Moscow, Paris and London.
        During World War II, he was with the British forces in France and was evacuated along with British troops at Dunkirk. In 1942, while covering the fighting in Tunisia, he was wounded. After his recovery, he returned to London and covered the invasion of Normandy and the subsequent war in Europe.
        From 1946 to 1949, he took a leave of absence from the Chicago Daily News to serve as special adviser to Secretary General Trygve Lie of the United Nations. The two had become acquainted when Mr. Lie was Foreign Minister of Norway and Mr. Stoneman was a London correspondent.
        Mr. Stoneman is survived by his wife, Ingrid, and a daughter, Florence, of the island of Elba in Italy.

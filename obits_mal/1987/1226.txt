        Joseph R. Strayer, a medievalist and former chairman of the history department at Princeton University, died Thursday at the Princeton (N.J.) Medical Center after an intestinal infection. He was 82 years old and lived in Princeton.
        Professor Strayer taught 12th- and 13th-century history and English constitutional history. He was the author, editor or co-author of more than 10 books and was the editor in chief of the 13-volume Dictionary of the Middle Ages, published by Charles Scribner's Sons.
        His last book, ''The Reign of Philip the Fair,'' received the Haskins Medal from the Medieval Academy of America in April.
        Professor Strayer was born in Baltimore. He attended the Horace Mann School and graduated from Princeton in 1925. He earned his master's degree and his doctorate from Harvard.
        He Strayer became an assistant professor in 1936, associate professor in 1940 and professor in 1942, the year he was also appointed to the Henry C. Lea Chair in History. He was named acting chairman of the history department in 1942 and chairman a short time later.
        Professor Strayer was president of the American Historical Association in 1971 and president of the Medieval Academy of America from 1966 to 1969.
        He is survived by his wife, Dr. Sylvia L. Thrupp; a daughter, Elizabeth Strayer Corson, of Fort Washington, Pa.; a brother, George D. Strayer Jr., of Bellevue, Wash.; 13 grandchildren, and three great-grandchildren.

        William V. Shannon, senior vice president of Gannett National Newspaper Sales, died Friday at the Memorial Sloan-Kettering Cancer Center. He was 57 years old and lived in Darien, Conn.
        He had been a senior advertising executive in New York for the Gannett Company since 1976.
        Mr. Shannon was born in Racine, Wis., and raised in Illinois. He was a graduate of Lake Forest College. He began his newspaper advertising career with his father's firm, Shannon & Associates, publishers representatives in Chicago.
        He was a member of the board of the International Newspaper Advertising Marketing Association and had been president of the Newspaper Advertising Sales Association.
        Mr. Shannon is survived by his wife, the former Molly Boylan; three sons, William Jr., of Stamford, Conn., and James and John, both of Darien; his mother, Ann Shannon of Tucson, Ariz.; a brother, George, of Phoenix, Ariz., and a sister, Jean Kettering of Denver.

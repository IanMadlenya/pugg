        Dr. Henry Young, a physician who practiced in Manhattan for more than 50 years and was an assistant professor of medicine at New York Medical College, died of a heart attack Monday at his home in Bloomingburg, N.Y. He was 74 years old.
        Dr. Young, an internist and chest specialist, had lived and had his office in Manhattan until early this year. He was a provisional medical officer of the city's Fire Department. At one time he worked for the United States Public Health Service.
        A 1936 graduate of New York University College of Medicine, he was for years affiliated with Lincoln and Lenox Hill Hospitals. He joined the New York Medical College faculty in 1979.
        He is survived by his wife, Sylvia; a son, Jerome, of Cincinnati, and a daughter, Judith, of Manhattan.

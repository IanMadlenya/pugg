        Rudolf F. Kallir, the former vice president of the American Mannex Corporation and a noted antiquarian specializing in musical manuscripts, died Sunday at his home in Manhattan. He was 91 years old.  Mr. Kallir's career began in Vienna, where he studied law, and continued in the steel industry in Prague, London and New York.
        At the same time he amassed a collection of musical autographs and manuscripts, published articles on the subjects of his collection, and served as a consultant on musical acquisitions to many institutions, including the Library of Congress, the Pierpont Morgan Library in New York and the Harvard University library.
        He is survived by a daughter, the pianist Lilian Kallir Frank, four grandchildren and one great-grandchild.

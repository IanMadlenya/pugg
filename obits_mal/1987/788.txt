        David W. Payne, who became a lawyer after working for years as a Broadway theater manager and in other positions related to the performing arts, died of lung cancer on March 27 at Howard County General Hospital in Columbia, Md. He was 48 years old and lived in San Francisco.
        Mr. Payne, who was deputy director of the performing arts programs of the New York State Council on the Arts in the 1970's, was, at his death, an associate of the San Francisco law firm of Heller, Ehrman, White & McAuliffe.
        Mr. Payne had a long association with the Broadway theater as a member and officer of the Association of Theatrical Press Agents and Managers, a union. He also had been a Broadway theater manager and managed touring companies of Broadway shows.
        He was born in Washington and was a graduate of Yale University. Last year, he received a degree from the University of California School of Law in Berkeley.
        He is survived by his mother, Frances C. Payne, of Columbia; a sister, Neenah C. Payne, of Brooklyn, and a brother, John C. Payne, of Ellicott City, Md.

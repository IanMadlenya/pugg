        William B. Beam, a retired executive vice president of the American Express International Banking Corporation, died of kidney failure Tuesday at Baylor Hospital in Dallas. He was 62 years old and lived in Dallas and Swiftwater, Pa. Until 1985, he also lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Beam was born and raised in Ohio and attended Purdue University before graduating from Harvard Law School in 1949. He became chief prosecutor of Mansfield, Ohio, before coming to New York to work in the banking industry.
        He worked initially for Chemical Bank, then joined American Express International Banking, a subsidiary of the American Express Company, where he spent the next 18 years. He became executive vice president and chief of staff to the board chairman before retiring in 1982.
        He is survived by his wife, Carol; three daughters by a previous marriage, Burgandy Nilles of Westby, Wis., Bradley Beam of Mexico City and Casandra Beam of the Bronx, and five grandchildren.

        Dr. William B. Terhune, a psychiatrist and founding professor of the psychiatric department at Yale University, died Friday of natural causes at Anniston (Ala.) Regional Medical Center. He was 93 years old and lived in Anniston.
        Dr. Terhune entered the field of psychiatry in 1916. He later advocated that psychiatric patients not be ostracized and he urged general hospitals to accept them as they would any other patients.
        Dr. Terhune retired from the Yale faculty in 1958.
        He was the author of three books, ''The Integration of Psychiatry and Medicine,'' ''Emotional Problems and What You Can Do About Them'' and ''The Safe Way to Drink.''
        He is survived by his wife, Evelyn, of Anniston; a son, William B. Jr., of Tiburon, Calif.; two daughters, Jane Hall of Little Compton, R.I., and Constance Anne Lancaster of Old Greenwich, Conn.; two stepsons, John P. Wheeler, of Anniston, and Dr. Porter K. Wheeler, of Bethesda, Md.; seven grandchildren, and nine great-grandchildren.

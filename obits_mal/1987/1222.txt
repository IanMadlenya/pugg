        John A. Mayer, former chairman of the board and chief executive officer of the Mellon Bank Corporation, died of a cerebral hemorrhage Wednesday at Latrobe Area Hospital in Latrobe , Pa. He was 77 years old and lived in Ligonier, Pa.
        Mr. Mayer joined the bank as vice president in 1951. He was named executive vice president in 1957, president and director in 1959 and chief executive officer in 1963. He was elected chairman of the board in 1967. He was active as a director, chairman of the board and chief executive officer of the bank and corporation from 1972 until 1974, when he retired. That year the Mellon National Bank and Trust Company was reorganized as a one-bank holding company and became the Mellon National Corporation. Mr. Mayer was given the honorary title of chairman emeritus in 1982.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Effie Flemming Disston; a sister, Hermine M. Haslem of Terre Haute, Ind.; three sons, John A. Jr., of Greenwich, Conn., Christopher, of Manhattan, Mark D., of Chatham, N.J., and five grandchildren.

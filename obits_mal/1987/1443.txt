        Dr. Gerald F. Jacobson, a pioneer in crisis intervention psychotherapy, died Saturday at St. John of God Nursing Home in Los Angeles, where he had been living for several months since suffering a heart attack. He was 65 years old.
        Dr. Jacobson was an authority on the effects of divorce and the author of a book called, ''The Multiple Crises of Marital Separation and Divorce.'' He also published numerous articles in professional journals based on divorce research, which was was an outgrowth of his work in crisis intervention therapy, a mode of short-term treatment for people confronted by life crises.
        In 1961, Dr. Jacobson founded the Didi Hirsch Community Mental Health Center, a community outpatient counseling center in Culver City, Calif. He was also on the clinical faculty at the University of Southern California.
        Born in Berlin, Dr. Jacobson fled the Nazis in 1938 and came to the United States. He earned a bachelor's degree in economics from the University of California at Berkeley in 1941, a master's in economics in 1942, and his medical degree from the University of Southern California in 1948.
        He is survived by his wife, Doris, of Los Angeles; a son, Eric, of Los Angeles; two daughters, Lisa of Oakland, and Julia of Santa Cruz, Calif.; a sister, Hannah Fox of Van Nuys, Calif. Private services are scheduled.

        Robert Warren, a song writer and music arranger, died of pancreatic cancer Monday at his home in Manhattan. He was 71 years old.
        Mr. Warren began his music career soon after graduation from Dartmouth College in 1936. Among his songs were ''Someone to Love,'' and ''Number Ten Lullaby Lane.'' As a music arranger, he worked on the ''Colgate Comedy Hour'' in the 1950's and was an arranger for the orchestra leaders Al Goodman and Raymond Paige.
        Mr. Warren wrote, recorded and published an album of song parodies titled ''Insanity!'' He also wrote several books, including ''The Facts of Wife,'' ''Nixon Made Perfectly Clear'' and ''Goldwater Made Simple.''
        He is survived by a sister, Anne W. Wolfson of Watertown, Mass., and a brother, Henry, of Rockville, Md.

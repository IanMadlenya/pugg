        Ulfert Wilke, a painter and calligrapher who was founding director of the University of Iowa Art Museum, died of complications from a stroke at his home in Anahola, Kauai, Hawaii, on Dec. 7. He was 80 years old.
        Mr. Wilke was born in Bad Tolz in what is now West Germany. He moved to the United States in 1938 and taught painting before becoming museum director museum in 1968.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Dorothy Kehl; two sons, Christopher, of Tucson, Ariz., and Nicholas, of Kauai, and a daughter, Karen, of Manhattan.

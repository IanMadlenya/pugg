        Carl C. Lella, an artist whose murals appeared in several Brooklyn high schools, died Thursday at Lenox Hill Hospital, after a long illness. He was 88 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Lella, who was born in Bari, Italy, did murals for Thomas Jefferson and Bushwick High Schools in Brooklyn, among others.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Louise Sabato; a brother, Donald of Colonia, N.J.; a son, Carl Jr., of Manhattan and Sag Harbor, L.I.; two daughters, Alba O'Connor of Manhattan and Gloria D'Tomaso of Naples, Fla., seven grandchildren and three great-grandchildren.

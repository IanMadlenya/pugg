        E. H. (Pat) Burgess, a World War II British bomber pilot who served for three years as director of sales for the Concorde supersonic airliner, died yesterday at his home in Cornwall, England. He was 63 years old.
        He was widely considered one of the world's foremost experts on airline economics.
        From 1975 until his retirement in 1985, Mr. Burgess worked in the New York area for Rolls-Royce Inc., the American subsidiary of the British aircraft-engine manufacturer. At his retirement he was senior vice president for marketing.
        Prior to joining Rolls-Royce, he was assistant secretary of Britain's Department of Industry, participating in international talks on environmental issues.
        While in the United States, he lived first in Greenwich, Conn., and then Stamford.
        He is survived by his wife, Hotch, and by two daughters, Deborah and Sarah.

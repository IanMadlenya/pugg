        Bohdan Nagorski, a port and shipping specialist, died Jan. 27 at Cabrini Medical Center. He was 96 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Nagorski served the United Nations as a technical assistance expert from 1966 to 1974 in several developing countries, mainly in the Middle East. He also worked in Greece, Egypt and Saudi Arabia.
        He was director of the port city of Gdansk, Poland, before World War II under a joint German-Polish directorship.
        Mr. Nagorski was born in Warsaw and graduated from Warsaw Polytechnic. He came to this country in 1941.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Zosia Krzyzanowska; two daughters, Barbara Wierzbianski and Kristina Nagorski, both of Manhattan; a sister, Walentyna Rudzka of Queens, and one grandson.

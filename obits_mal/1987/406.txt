        Emil Levin, a former commissioner of the New York State Human Rights Appeals Board, died Dec. 22 at his home in Queens after a long illness. He was 82 years old.
        He was born in Manhattan and graduated from Columbia College and New York University Law School. Mr. Levin practiced law in Manhattan and in Queens.
        In 1962 he ran unsuccessfully for the House of Representatives. He was a Human Rights Appeals Board commissioner from 1969 to 1978.
        He is survived by his wife, Vivian; a son, Victor, of Roslyn, L.I., and a daughter, Susan Silverman of Wilton, Conn.

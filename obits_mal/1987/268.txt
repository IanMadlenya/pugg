        Joseph H. McMahon, Hollis Professor of Romance Languages and Literatures at Wesleyan University, died Thursday at Middlesex Memorial Hospital in Middletown, Conn., where he was hospitalized after a heart attack on Oct. 21. He was 57 years old and lived in Middletown.
        He was born in New York City and was a graduate of Manhattan College and Stanford University, where in 1960 he received a doctorate in French and the humanities.
        He taught at Yale University from 1960 to 1968 before joining the Wesleyan faculty. In 1968 and 1969 he was dean of the college at Wesleyan.
        A specialist in French literature and cultural thought, he was the author of two books, ''The Imagination of Jean Genet'' and ''Humans Being: The World of Jean-Paul Sartre.''
        He is survived by a sister, Katherine Gardner of Philadelphia, and a brother, Thomas, of New York City.

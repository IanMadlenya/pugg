        Simon C. Kregar, an architect, died of an aneurism last Thursday at New York Hospital in Manhattan. He was 74 years old and lived in Queens.
        He was an architect with the Manhattan firm of Lockwood Greene Engineers, where he worked on such buildings as the American Airlines terminal at Kennedy International Airport.
        Mr. Kregar was born in Ljubljana, Yugoslavia, and was active in Slovenian affairs.
        He is survived by his wife, Sonja; two sons, Andrew, of Ardsley, N.Y. and Simon, of Tucson, Ariz., and three grandchildren.

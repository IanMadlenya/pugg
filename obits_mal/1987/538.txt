        Davidson Dunton, a former journalist and retired president of Carleton University, died in a hospital here Saturday. He was 74 years old.
        Mr. Dunton was chairman of the Ontario Press Council, an organization set up to promote the confidence of readers in newspapers, from 1972 to 1982. He was an editor at The Montreal Star in the 1930's and was named chairman of the Canadian Broadcasting Corporation Board of Governors in 1945.
        He was president of Carleton University here from 1958 to 1972, and was later a director.
        Mr. Dunton never earned a college diploma but attended universities in Canada and abroad, including Cambridge University and the University of Munich. He received seven honorary degrees.
        He is survived by his wife, Kathleen, and two daughters, Darcy and Deborah.

        Lionel Meyers Harrison, a New York lawyer and businessman, died of cancer Tuesday at the Winthrop University Hospital, Mineola, L.I. He was 72 years old and lived in Westbury, L.I.
        After graduating from law school, Mr. Harrison, who specialized in criminal law, joined the private practice established by his father, David Harrison, in Manhattan. He was also president of the Harrison Camera Corporation, a family concern in Westbury, from 1970 to 1985.
        He was born in Waterbury, Conn., and graduated from New York University in 1936 and from its law school in 1938.
        Mr. Harrison is survived by his wife, the former Lillian Teitelbaum; four sons, Marc, of Morganville, N.J.; Wayne, of Westbury; Glenn, of Matawan, N.J., and David, also of Westbury; two daughters, Karen, of Manhattan and Joan, of Los Angeles; a sister, Audrey Abrams of the Bronx, and a grandson.
        A funeral service will be held at 1 P.M. today at the I.J. Morris Funeral Home, 46 Greenwich Street, Hempstead, L.I.

        Maurice D. Hopkins, a former assistant superintendent of schools in New York City, with responsibility for high schools, died of a heart attack Sunday at his home in Morris, N.Y. He was 82 years old and also lived in Fort Lauderdale, Fla.
        Mr. Hopkins retired to Morris, his upstate birthplace, in the late 1960's after more than 40 years in the school system.
        After graduating from Columbia University, where he received his bachelor's degree in 1925 and master's degree in 1926, he taught economics at Richmond Hill High School in Queens.
        He was principal of Jamaica High School in Queens from 1946 to 1955 and of Martin Van Buren High School in Queens from 1955 to 1956.
        In 1956, he was put in charge of all Queens high schools. Later, as assistant superintendent, he was in charge of all high schools in the city.
        He is survived by his wife, Anita.

        Dr. Conrad George Lattes, director of the renal transplant unit of St. Luke's-Roosevelt Hospital Center, died of a heart attack Oct. 4, at St. Luke's Hospital. He was 50 years old and lived in Palisades, N.Y.
        Dr. Lattes had been associated with St. Luke's Hospital since 1970, when he joined the staff as an assistant attending physician in surgery. He became an attending physician in 1982.
        He was born in Torino, Italy and was a graduate of Swarthmore College in 1958 and the Columbia University College of Physicians and Surgeons in 1963.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Jane Abby Flax, and four children, Lisa of Portland, Me., and Conrad, Jane and Abigail, all of Palisades.

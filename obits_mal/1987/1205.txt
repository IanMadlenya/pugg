        George A. McDermott, a retired medical publisher, died of a heart attack last Wednesday at St. Francis Hospital in Roslyn, L.I. He was 85 years old and lived in Port Washington, L.I.
        Mr. McDermott, a native of Dorchester, Mass., joined the publishing concern of D. Appleton & Company in 1927 as a salesman. He became editor-in-chief of the medical department in 1946 and, in 1951, was elected secretary of the company, which is now known as Appleton-Century-Crofts. He retired in 1966.
        He was a graduate of the Boston Latin School and received a bachelor of science degree from Harvard University in 1924.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Virginia Boswell Smith; two sons, Richard of Danbury, Conn., and George Jr. of Philadelphia; a daughter, Emily Sewall of Waban, Mass.; and eight grandchildren.
        Correction:  July 30, 1987, Thursday, Late City Final Edition

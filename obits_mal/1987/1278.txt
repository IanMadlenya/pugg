        Harry J. Feeney, director of press information for CBS from 1969 until his retirement early last year, died Saturday at his home in Forest Hills, Queens, after heart surgery. He was 69 years old.
        Mr. Feeney was a native of Brooklyn and a graduate of St. John's University, and he joined CBS in 1942. He served in the Army beginning in 1943, and until the end of World War II he was in in Europe, fighting in the Battle of the Bulge and in combat on the Rhine and in the Ruhr Valley. He then edited the magazine edition of Stars & Stripes in Paris.
        He returned to CBS in 1946 as a public relations and publicity specialist for its radio network. In 1956, he transferred to the television network, and stayed on as head of press information when his department became part of CBS Entertainment in 1977.
        Mr. Feeney is survived by his wife, Eileen; two sons, Dennis and Brian,and two grandchildren.

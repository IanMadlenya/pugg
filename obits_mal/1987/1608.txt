        Lars-Erik Sjoberg, a former National Hockey League player and the chief European scout for the New York Rangers, has died in Sweden of cancer, the Rangers announced yesterday. He was 43 years old.
        One of the first European players to compete professionally in North America, Mr. Sjoberg was the captain of the Winnipeg Jets for six seasons. He scouted exclusively for the Rangers in Europe for the last eight years, and was instrumental in bringing to the league such players as Tomas Sandstrom, Jan Erixon, Kjell Samuelsson, Ulf Dahlen, and Raimo Helminen.

        Jerome H. Schloss, former secretary of the New York Foundation, a philanthropic organization that provides funds for worthy organizations, died Saturday at his home in Manhattan. He was 103 years old.
        Mr. Schloss, a native of Manhattan, was associated with the foundation from 1937 to 1955, when he retired. He continued to work for the organization as a volunteer until 1970. He was the author of a privately printed book tracing the decline of currencies throughout history, beginning in the 18th-century B.C., with the time of King Hammurabi of Babylon.
        His is survived by his wife, the former Evelyn Gomprecht; a daughter, Marjorie Isaac of Bronxville, N.Y.; a son, Walter, of Manhattan; five grandchildren, and three great-grandchildren.

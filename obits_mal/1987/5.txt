        Milton Adler, for many years a criminal trial lawyer on the staff of the Legal Aid Society in Manhattan, collapsed and died Monday on a subway platform in lower Manhattan. He was 76 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Adler, who was to have retired yesterday, had gone to his office Monday to work on his last trial.
        He joined the Legal Aid Society in 1945. From 1969 to 1971 he was attorney in charge of the Legal Aid Society's criminal defense division.
        In 1977, he received the society's Orison Martin Award, named for a lawyer and a former chairman of the society.
        Mr. Adler was born in Stamford, Conn., and was a graduate of the New York University School of Law.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Miriam Josephs.

        Dr. Natale Colosi, a bacteriologist and former chairman of the Interstate Sanitation Commission, died of cardiac arrest Thursday at his home in Yonkers. He was 85 years old.
        Dr. Colosi was a member of the commission, an air and water pollution control agency of New York, New Jersey and Connecticut, for 40 years, half that time as its chairman. He resigned in 1985.
        He was the director of education at the Cabrini Medical Center in Manhattan and a co-founder and dean of the Institute of Continuing Biomedical Education, which prepares foreign medical graduates to become doctors here.
        Dr. Colosi was born in Sicily. He was a graduate of St. Francis College in Brooklyn and New York University, where he received a Ph.D. in microbiology in 1935.
        He was a former dean of the New York Polyclinic Medical School and Hospital and a chairman of the department of bacteriology and public health at Wagner College.
        There are no immediate survivors.

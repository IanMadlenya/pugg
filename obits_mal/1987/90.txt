        Charles B. Ferris, an architect and engineer and a retired brigadier general in the Army Reserve, died Sunday at the Riverview Medical Center in Red Bank, N.J. He was 82 years old and lived in Shrewsbury, N.J.
        For 30 years, Mr. Ferris was head of the architectural and engineering firm of Charles B. Ferris Associates in Manhattan, which he sold two years ago. The firm designed and engineered schools, hospitals and industrial buildings in the United States and Europe.
        In World War II, Mr. Ferris was an Army colonel with the 27th Infantry Division. He later served as assistant chief of staff at the headquarters of the South Pacific Base Command.
        As a brigadier general in the Reserve, he commanded the 410th Engineer Brigade, which comprised all active engineer reserve units in New York State.
        Mr. Ferris was born in Manhattan, grew up in Yonkers and was a graduate of New York University.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Laura Soper, and a sister, Helen F. Heep of Bedford Hills, N.Y.

        Frank Kleinholz, a painter known for his realistic depictions of city life and city dwellers, died Saturday in Miami Beach. He was 86 years old.
        Born in Brooklyn, Mr. Kleinholz gave up a succesful law practice at the age of 40 to paint. He taught at Hofstra University in the 1950's and exhibited his work on Long Island, where he lived, and elsewhere, until this year.
        Mr. Kleinholz is survived by a son, Mark Klein, of Miami; two daughters, Lisa Kleinholz Kaplan, of New York, and Anna Kleinholz Ludmilla, of Miami Beach; and four grandchildren.

        Paul L. Sitton, the first Administrator of the Urban Mass Transportation Administration and a senior official at the National Academy of Sciences, died Wednesday of a liver ailment. He was 60 years old.
        In 1968, Mr. Sitton was named to head the Transporation Department's Urban Mass Transportation Administration, which oversees Federal transit programs. He helped establish the department a year earlier after he was appointed by President Johnson as Deputy Under Secretary for Transportation.
        Mr. Sitton joined the National Academy of Sciences in 1969 and served as the assistant to the academy's president and executive officer.
        In 1975, Austria awarded Mr. Sitton the Great Silver Insignia for Meritorious Service for his role in creating the International Institute of Applied Systems Analysis, an international center, near Vienna. that examines environmental problems.
        The Georgia-born Mr. Sutton moved to Washington in 1951 and worked for the Social Security Administration and the Interior Department. From 1956 to 1964, he was an examiner of Federal transportation programs for the Bureau of the Budget, now the Office of Management and Budget. Before he joined the Transportation Department, Mr. Sitton also worked in the Commerce and State departments.
        He was born in Atlanta, reared in Conyers, Ga., and graduated from Emory University in 1948. In 1950 he received a master's degree in public administration from the University of Pennsylvania.
        He was divorced from his wife, the former Judith Louchheim. They had no children.
        Mr. Sitton is survived by his mother, Pauline F. Sitton of Atlanta, and a brother, Claude F. Sitton of Raleigh, N.C.

        Lester A. Burcham, former chairman of the board and chief executive officer of the F. W. Woolworth Company, died Saturday at the North Carolina Baptist Hospital in Winston-Salem, N.C. He was 73 years old and lived in Delray Beach, Fla.
        Mr. Burcham, a native of Lancaster, Ohio, began his career in 1931 as a management trainee in the company's regional office, then based in Cleveland. He became a district manager in 1945.
        He was transferred to Woolworth's New York headquarters in 1955 as executive office representative and two years later was promoted to regional vice president of the mid-Atlantic region, with headquarters in Philadelphia.
        In 1961, having returned to New York, he became a member of Woolworth's board of directors and executive committee. The next year he was elected executive vice president. He was named president in 1965 and elected chairman and chief executive officer in 1970. He retired in 1977.
        Mr. Burcham is survived by his wife, Zora, and a son, James E. Burcham of Asheville, N.C.

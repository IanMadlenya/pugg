        Clyde Edd Hood Jr., who was was sentenced to prison with former Gov. Ray Blanton in a scheme to peddle liquor licenses to Mr. Blanton's political allies, died Thursday of head injuries from a tractor accident. He was 35 years old.
        Mr. Blanton, James M. Allen, the former Governor's 1974 campaign manager, and Mr. Hood, a former special assistant, were convicted of mail fraud and conspiracy to influence the state Alcoholic Beverage Commission.
        Mr. Hood completed an eight-month sentence in 1984.

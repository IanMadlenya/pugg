        Arthur Osgood Choate Jr., a retired general partner and executive committee chairman of Clark Dodge & Company, investment bankers, died Sunday at Jupiter Hospital in Jupiter, Fla. He was 75 years old and lived in Hobe Sound, Fla., and Manhattan.
        Mr. Choate was a native New Yorker and a graduate of St. Mark's School and Harvard College. He had been a longtime chairman of the board of trustees of the Joslin Diabetes Center in Boston and a president of the Lenox Hill Neighborhood Association in Manhattan.
        In World War II, Mr. Choate led a Navy frogman demolition team that prepared for five major invasions in the Pacific. He rose to the rank of lieutenant commander and was awarded a Bronze Star.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Eloise Weld; a son, Arthur, of Coral Gables, Fla.; a stepson, Dr. William Elkins of Villanova, Pa.; a stepdaughter, Carol Elkins of Manhattan; a sister, Anne Hyde Patterson of Bedford, N.Y.; a brother, Thomas, of Brookville, L.I., and two grandchildren.

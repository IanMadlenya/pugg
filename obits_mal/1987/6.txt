        Samuel Sloan, executive secretary of the American Friends of Wingate, a physical-education school in Israel, died of a heart attack yesterday at his home in Brooklyn. He was 73 years old.
        Mr. Sloan was executive secretary of the New York Olympic Committee from 1969 until his retirement in 1979. He had been executive secretary of the United States Committee for Sports in Israel from 1963 to 1979. From 1946 to 1952, he was one of the organizers of and particpants in the Haganah, the Jewish underground that worked for the establishment of the state of Israel.
        Mr. Sloan was born in Brooklyn. He graduated from Brooklyn College in 1934 and received a master's degree from New York University in 1936. He also received a degree in Hebrew literature from the Jewish Theological Seminary of America.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Doris Shamblott; a son, Allan, of Maplewood, N.J.; two daughters, Sara, of Brooklyn, and Vivian Honig of Oak Park, Mich., and four grandchildren.

        William Fifield, a writer of both fiction and nonfiction, died Monday at his home in Rancho Palos Verdes, Calif. He was 71 years old.  Mr. Fifield, who won an O. Henry short-story prize in 1942, was the author of ''In Search of Genius,'' a collection of interviews with people he considered geniuses, including Picasso, Cocteau, Miro, Dali and Robert Graves. He also wrote a biography of Modigliani and collaborated on ''Alexis Lichine's Encyclopedia of Wines and Spirits,'' which appeared in 1965.
        He was born in Chicago, a son of Dr. Lawrence Wendell Fifield, minister of the Plymouth Church of the Pilgrims in Brooklyn Heights during the 1940's and 1950's.
        Mr. Fifield is survived by twins, Donnali Fifield of San Francisco, and Brian Robert Shor of Washington, D.C.; and a daughter, Edwina Verhille of Paris.

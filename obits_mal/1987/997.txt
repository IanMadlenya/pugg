        Walter Aloysius Slowinski Jr., a leading international tax lawyer and law lecturer, died of cardiac arrest Monday at Georgetown University Hospital in Washington. He was 66 years old.
        A 1948 graduate of Catholic University's School of Law in Washington, Mr. Slowinski joined the international law firm of Baker & McKenzie in 1957, establishing a Washington office for them. At his death, he was the firm's most senior partner.
        Mr. Slowinski was an adjunct professor at Georgetown University's Graduate Law School from 1956 to 1970. He lectured on international taxation. Until his death, he also lectured at the law schools of Columbia University and the University of Virginia.
        Born in Newark, N.J., in 1920, Mr. Slowinski graduated magna cum laude from St. Vincent College in Latrobe, Pa., in 1941. The college awarded him an honorary doctorate in law in 1975.
        He interrupted his law studies to serve with the Navy in the Pacific in World War II, achieving the rank of lieutenant commander. He also served as a naval reserve officer in the Korean War.
        Specializing in South American and Latin American tax systems, he served as general counsel to the Council of the Americas in Washington, among other organizations.
        He is survived by his wife, Annette Carter Roberts Slowinski; eight children; six granchildren, and a brother and a sister.

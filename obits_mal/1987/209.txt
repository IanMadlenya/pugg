        David L. Goldring, a stockbroker, died Tuesday at his home in Litchfield, Conn. He was 44 years old and also lived in Manhattan. His wife, Nancy, said the cause of death was undetermined.
        Mr. Goldring was a founding partner of Dritz, Goldring & Wohlreich, a trading firm on the American Stock Exchange.
        At the time of his death, he was a partner of Silberberg, Rosenthal & Company, a New York brokerage firm.
        Born in Norfolk, Va., he obtained a bachelor's degree from Tulane University in 1965, majoring in English.
        In addition to his wife, he is survived by two daughters, Nancy Eden and Allison Rae, both of New York City.
        A service will be held today at 11:30 A.M. at the Frank E. Campbell Funeral Chapel, Madison Avenue at 81st Street.

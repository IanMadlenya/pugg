        Arthur A. Collins, a pioneer in radio communications, avionics and telecommunications who was founder of the Collins Radio Company, died Wednesday at Gaston Episcopal Hospital in Dallas, the city where he had lived for the last 14 years. He was 77 years old.
        Mr. Collins helped develop advanced data communications and control concepts, techniques used in digital switching for telecommunications.
        His company, which he founded in 1931, was acquired 40 years later by Rockwell International.
        Surviving are his wife, Mary; three sons, Michael Collins of California, and David and Alan Collins of Dallas; a daughter, Susan Collins of Cedar Rapids, and three grandchildren.

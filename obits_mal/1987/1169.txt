        Anthony D. Truglia, the assistant majority leader of the Connecticut State Senate, died at Hartford Hospital yesterday of complications stemming from injuries suffered in an automobile accident last week, a hospital spokesman said. He was 60 years old and lived in Stamford.
        The spokesman, James Battaglio, said the senator died at 1:25 P.M. He gave no further details.
        Mr. Truglia, a Democrat, had been in the hospital's intensive care unit. He was injured last Friday when his car collided with a truck on Route 3 in Glastonbury, according to the police there.
        Mr. Truglia, a former teacher, was elected to the state House of Representatives in 1971. In 1984 he won the 27th Senate District seat vacated by Thom Serrani when he was elected Mayor of Stamford.
        He was chairman of the Housing Committee and a ranking member of the Transportation Committee. He was a member of the Stamford Board of Representatives for 18 years.
        Mr. Truglia was a graduate of the Juilliard School of Music and the University of Bridgeport.
        He is survived by his wife, Christel; two daughters, Penny and Sallyanne; and a son, Anthony Jr. Funeral arrangements were not immediately disclosed.

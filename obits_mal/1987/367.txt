        George Armstrong Kelly, a professor and authority on the works of Hegel, died of a heart attack Wednesday in Manhattan. He was 55 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Professor Kelly was the author of a wide range of scholarly works, but was best known for his two books on Hegel, ''Idealism, Politics and History,'' and ''Hegel's Retreat from Eleusis.'' His other books included ''Lost Soldiers,'' a study of the French army during the Algerian crisis, ''Victims, Authority and Terror,'' ''Mortal Politics in 18th Century France'' and ''Politics and Religious Consciousness in America.''
        At his death, he was visiting Professor of Humanities at the Johns Hopkins University. He had previously taught at Harvard and Brandeis Universities and had been a visiting professor at the Institute for Advanced Studies in Princeton, N.J.
        He was born in Pittsburgh, and graduated from St. Paul's School in 1949. He received a bachelor's degree from Harvard College in 1953, a master's degree from Stanford University and a doctorate in political science from Harvard University.
        He is survived by his wife, Joanne Lyman; two sons, Charles Walton Kelly of Hollywood, Calif., and George Armstrong Kelly of Paris; a daughter, Eleanor Park Coughran of Canton, Mass.; a stepson, Christopher L.B. Finney of Plymouth, Mass., and a stepdaughter, Suzanne Finney-Sawits of Washington.
        Funeral services will be held at 11:30 A.M. Dec. 28 at the Church of the Resurrection, 115 East 74th Street in Manhattan.

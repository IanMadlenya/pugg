        Edward D. Kuekes, whose editorial cartoon on the irony of young men going to war before being old enough to vote won him a Pulitzer Prize in 1953, died Tuesday in a retirement home in Oklahoma City. He was 85 years old.
        Mr. Kuekes won the prize while at The Cleveland Plain Dealer, where he worked for 44 years until his retirement in 1966.

        Benjamin S. Hornstein, a philanthropist and retired retail executive, died Friday at St. Mary's Hospital in Palm Beach, Fla. He was 96 years old and lived in Palm Beach.
        Mr. Hornstein, a former resident of Manhattan, was the president of Charles Stores, a Manhattan-based chain of small department stores in the South. He sold the company in the late 1960's.
        He was a founder and former member of the Board of Governors, of the Albert Einstein College of Medicine at Yeshiva University. He was also a founder and former president of the Jewish Culture Foundation of New York University, a former overseer of the Jewish Theological Foundation and, at his death, an elector of the Spanish and Portuguese Synagogue in Manhattan.
        He has no immediate survivors.

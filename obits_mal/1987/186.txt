        Edward C. Maxwell, a realty and economic development executive, died of prostate cancer Sunday at Beth Israel Hospital in Manhattan. He was 77 years old and lived in Brooklyn.
        Before forming the Woodville Realty Corporation, Mr. Maxwell was vice president of the Brooklyn Local Economic Development Corporation. He was also vice chairman of the Brooklyn Navy Yard Development Corporation.
        He was born in Savannah, Ga., and was a career officer in the United States Postal Service until he retired in 1967. At his retirement, he was Superintendent of Registry and branch president of the National Association of Postal Supervisors.
        Mr. Maxwell was a life member of the N.A.A.C.P. and a member of the Brooklyn Rotary Club, the Brooklyn Chamber of Commerce and the Veterans of Foreign Wars.
        He is survived by his wife, Dr. Marcella Maxwell, the New York City Commissioner of Human Rights; a stepdaughter, Debra Young, and three sisters, Margaret Lawrence, Rachael Howell and Edwina Singleton.

        G. Wallace Bates, who was the first president of the Business Roundtable, an influential lobbying group, died of heart failure Sunday at his home in Greenwich, Conn. He was 79 years old.
        Mr. Bates had a 30-year career with the Bell Telephone group of companies, beginning in 1943 as a lawyer with the American Telephone and Telegraph Company. In 1957, he was named vice president, general counsel and director of the New York Telephone Company.
        In 1966, Mr. Bates was elected chairman of the committee on law reform of the New York Chamber of Commerce, and later became president.
        Mr. Bates also served as chairman of the administrative law committee of the New York State Bar Association. He was a member of the American Bar Association, the American Law Institute and the New York Law Institute.
        Mr. Bates retired in 1973 and almost immediately became president of the fledgling lobbying group, the Business Roundtable, which was designed to bring the collective influence of major corporate figures to bear upon Washington politics.
        Mr. Bates was born in Battle Lake, Minn., and graduated from the University of Minnesota in 1928. He went to Columbia University, where he received his LL.B. and LL.M. degrees.
        He is survived by two daughters, Elizabeth Zenowich of Princeton, N.J., and Anne Wilson Bates of Greenwich; four grandchildren and three great-grandchildren.

        Robert M. Dore, a New York advertising representative, died of colon cancer on Monday at his home in Westport, Conn. He was 78 years old.
        Mr. Dore, who was born in Brooklyn, began his career practicing law in Manhattan in 1937 and from 1941 to 1946 served as an agent of the Federal Bureau of Investigation.
        He then worked with several advertising agencies, including Carl Reimers Inc. and Weiss & Geller. In 1955, he formed Bob Dore Associates, radio station representatives, and served as its president until his retirement in 1976.
        He was a graduate of New York University and Brooklyn Law School.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Anita Wilkes; a brother, Irving, of Coral Gables, Fla.; two daughters, Marjorie Allen of Washington and Elizabeth Dore of Middlebury, Vt., and four grandchildren.

        Martin Dwyer Jr., the chairman of JWP Inc. of Lake Success, L.I., a technical industrial services company, died of cancer yesterday at his home in Jupiter Island, Fla. He was 65 years old and also lived in Mantoloking, N.J.
        JWP is also the parent company of the Jamaica Water Supply Company. JWP, formerly Jamaica Water and Utilities Inc., was established by Mr. Dwyer in 1966.
        He was born in Newark and was a graduate of Yale University and the New York Law School.
        In World War II, Mr. Dwyer was a first lieutenant in the Army Air Corps and was a prisoner of war in Germany. He received the Distinguished Flying Cross, the Purple Heart and the Air Medal with four Oak Leaf clusters.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Mary Noyes Thompson; three daughters, Diana Brooks of Manhattan, Elizabeth Fielding of Lake Tahoe, Calif., and Elinor McKenna of Southport, Conn.; two sons, Andrew, of Bedford, N.Y., and Henry, of Lake Tahoe; a sister, Evelyn Van Sciver of Bay Head, N.J.; a brother, Thomas, of Delray Beach, Fla., and seven grandchildren.

        Dennis A. Volman, a reporter for The Christian Science Monitor, died of cancer Monday at the Mount Sinai Medical Center. He was 37 years old and lived in Washington.
        Before joining The Monitor, Mr. Volman worked for the Trilateral Commission and the State Department. He was assigned to the embassies in Beirut, Lebanon, and Managua, Nicaragua. For The Monitor, he covered the invasion of Grenanda in late 1983 and was later bureau chief in Mexico City.
        Surviving are his parents, Sacha and Gusta, of New York City and Santo Domingo, Dominican Republic.

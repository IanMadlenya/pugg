        Charles C. MacLean, a senior partner with the Manhattan law firm Dewey, Ballantine, Bushby, Palmer & Wood, died of lung cancer Sunday at his home in Lloyd Neck, L.I. He was 80 years old.
        He was a graduate of Yale College and Harvard Law School, where he was an editor of the Harvard Law Review.
        Mr. MacLean wrote frequently on tax law and served as chairman of both the Section of Taxation of the American Bar Association and the Committee of Taxation of the Association of the Bar of the City of New York. He worked for 54 years at Dewey, Ballantine.
        Mr. MacLean is survived by his wife, the former Lee Selden Howe; three sons, Charles C. MacLean 3d of Phoenix, William A. H. MacLean of Birmingham, Ala., and Babcock MacLean of Manhattan; a daughter, Elizabeth O'Neill, also of Manhattan, and three grandchildren.

        Francis M. Gaffney, a partner in Peat, Marwick, Main & Company, an international accounting firm, died Friday after a brief illness at Columbia Presbyterian Medical Center. He was 58 years old and lived in Stamford, Conn.
        Mr. Gaffney, a certified public accountant, joined the firm in 1979 as a tax partner when it was known as KMG-Main Hurdman Company. It merged with Peat, Marwick, Main & Company in April of this year.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Helen Phillipi; a daughter, Kathryn, of Manhattan; four sons, Joseph, of Metuchen, N.J., Francis, of Washington, Michael, of Manhattan, and Robert, of Easton, Pa.; his mother, Mary Gaffney of Babylon, L.I.; four sisters, Kathryn Ayres of Babylon Village, L.I., Mary Gaffney of Babylon, L.I., Regina Keenan of Woodland Hills, Calif., and Therese Pinto of Dix Hills, L.I., and a grandson.

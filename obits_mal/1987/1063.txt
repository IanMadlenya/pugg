        Wilbur Evans, a singer and actor who starred opposite Mary Martin in the London production of ''South Pacific,'' died Sunday at his home in Mullica Hill, N.J. He was 81 years old.
        Mr. Evans was born in Philadelphia and attended the Curtis Institute of Music. He won the 1927 Atwater Kent Foundation's national radio-singing contest and went on to sing professionally on the air and to perform in operas and operettas around the country as well as on Broadway. He appeared at Carnegie Hall in ''The Merry Widow'' and ''The New Moon'' and in such other New York productions as ''Up in Central Park,'' ''Mexican Hayride,'' ''Desert Song'' and ''By the Beautiful Sea.''
        In 1951 Mr. Evans co-starred with Mary Martin in the British production of ''South Pacific'' at London's Drury Lane Theater. He also appeared with Jose Ferrer in ''Man of La Mancha'' at the Mastbaum Theater in Philadelphia in 1966.
        Mr. Evans, who was married four times, is survived by his fourth wife, the former Masako Ogura, and by his son, Michael, of Pawling, N.Y.

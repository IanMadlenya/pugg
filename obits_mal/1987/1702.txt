        William Grant Whitehead, a former editor in chief of E. P. Dutton, died of complications arising from acquired immune deficiency syndrome, yesterday at his home in Manhattan. He was 44 years old.
        Mr. Whitehead, born in Wilmington, N.C., graduated from Princeton University in 1965. He was a senior editor at Doubleday when he joined Dutton in 1974. There he developed the Obelisk line of trade paperbacks, which brought back into print a number of books by prominent authors. In 1985 he won the Carey-Thomas award, which is given annually for distinguished publishing.
        Mr. Whitehead joined Macmillan in November 1985 and remained until last December, when he became ill.
        He is survived by his parents, Allen and Alice Whitehead of New Orleans; a brother, Allen Jr., of Yorktown, Va., and his longtime companion, Anthony Blum.
        Funeral services will be private.

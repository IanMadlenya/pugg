        E. Howard Molisani, a garment-union official in New York City for 40 years, died of a heart attack Friday at his home in Port Washington, L.I. He was 76 years old.
        Mr. Molisani was elected a vice president of the International Ladies Garment Workers Union in 1956. He was first vice president from 1968 until he retired in 1978. He was secretary-treasurer of the state A.F.L.-C.I.O. from 1976 to 1984 and a vice president until his death.
        Mr. Molisani, who graduated from Fordham University in 1934 and the Brooklyn Law School in 1937, was elected executive secretary of Local 48 of the Italian Cloakmakers Union in 1947 and its manager in 1955. His father had been a president of the local, which was affiliated with the I.L.G.W.U.
        After earning his law degree, Mr. Molisani was a secretary to a municipal court judge and a lawyer for the Securities and Exchange Commission and the labor division of the War Production Board. During World War II, he served as a destroyer-escort officer in the Atlantic and become a lieutenant commander.
        Mr. Molisani was secretary of the Bronx Liberal Party in the 1960's and ran unsuccessfully as a candidate for City Councilman-at-Large in 1963.
        Surviving are his wife, Marianne, and two sons, Rex, of Tinton Falls, N.J., and Mark, of Brooklyn.

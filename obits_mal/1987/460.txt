        Morrill Cody, a former deputy director of the United States Information Agency, died Nov. 23 at the Manor Care nursing home in Wheaton, Md., after a long illness. He was 86 years old.
        Mr. Cody, who had been in the Foreign Service for more than two decades, was assistant director of the U.S.I.A. under Edward R. Murrow from 1961 to 1963, when he retired. From 1965 to 1976 he managed the Paris bureau of Radio Liberty/Radio Free Europe, retiring for a second time in 1976 because of failing eyesight. He was the author of several books.
        Mr. Cody, who lived in Front Royal, Va., leaves his wife, the former Jane Hoster; a son, Peter Malcolm Cody of Washington; two daughters, Judith Alden Cody of Silver Spring, Md., and Gabrielle Hamilton Cody of New Haven; seven grandchildren and five great-grandchildren.

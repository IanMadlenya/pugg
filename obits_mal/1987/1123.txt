        John Davenport, an author and freelance writer who was a former assistant managing editor of Fortune magazine, died Monday at Riverview Hospital in Red Bank, N.J. He was 82 years old and lived in Middletown, N.J.
        Mr. Davenport, who was born in Philadelphia, graduated from Yale University in 1926. He was an editor of Barron's, the weekly financial publication, from 1949 to 1954, and assistant managing editor of Fortune from 1954 to 1969, when he retired to become a freelance writer. He was the author of ''The Lives of Winston Churchill,'' written with Charles J. V. Murphy, and ''The U. S. Economy.''
        He is survived by his wife, Marie; six daughters, Ann Mavroleon and Susan Brooke of London, Glorianna Davenport of Cambridge, Mass., Amy Durrance of Montpelier, Vt., Sharon Davenport of Oakland, Calif., and Caroline Johnson of Brooklyn; a son, John, of New Brunswick, N.J., and five grandchildren.

        Frank Broglio, a former Long Island restaurant owner and a breeder of standardbreds, died Wednesday at Glen Cove Community Hospital. He was 86 years old and lived in Brookville, L.I., and Delray Beach, Fla.
        Many years ago, he owned the Plandome Gardens Restaurant in Manhasset, L.I., and later the Swan Club Restaurant of Roslyn Harbor, L.I. He retired in 1964 from the restaurant business and began breeding horses. He was a former vice president and director of the Standardbred Owners Association.
        He is survived by his wife, Lillian; a son, Lee, of West Palm Beach, Fla.; a daughter, Evelyn Diesendorf of Brookville; five grandchildren and 15 great grandchildren.

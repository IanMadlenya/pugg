        Edward Staley, former chairman of the W. T. Grant Company, died of cardiac arrest Friday at his home in Palm Beach, Fla. He was 84 years old.
        From 1966 until he retired in 1973, Mr. Staley was chairman of W.T. Grant, a nationwide variety-store chain that went bankrupt in 1976. He was the company's president from 1952 to 1959 and vice chairman from 1959 to 1966.
        Mr. Staley joined the Grant company in 1926 as a trainee and floorman. By 1933, when he left to join Montgomery Ward & Company as an assistant merchandising manager, he had risen to store manager. He rejoined Grant in 1940 as director of merchandising.
        He is survived by his wife, Frances Kennedy Staley; two sons, Paul, of Wayne, Pa., and Alan, of Westbury, L.I., and a daughter, Lynn Sternick, of San Francisco.

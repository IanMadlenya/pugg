
       James Stewart McDonald, president of J. S. McDonald Consultants of Darien, Conn., died of a heart attack Saturday at his home in New Canaan, Conn. He was 59 years old. Mr. McDonald was previously a district manager for I.B.M. and a vice president of the Computer Usage Corporation.
        James Stewart McDonald, president of J. S. McDonald Consultants of Darien, Conn., died of a heart attack Saturday at his home in New Canaan, Conn. He was 59 years old. Mr. McDonald was previously a district manager for I.B.M. and a vice president of the Computer Usage Corporation.
        He is survived by his wife, Barbara; three daughters, Catherine Rechlin of Dennisport, Mass., Laura Claire of Ferndale, Mich., and Elizabeth, of Boston; a sister, Eleanor, of Scarsdale, N.Y.; a brother, Henry, of Fairfield, Conn., and two grandchildren.

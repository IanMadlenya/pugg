        Joseph R. Orgel, a writer and educator who worked in the New York City public school system for almost 30 years, died of pneumonia and heart disease Sunday at Mount Sinai Hospital in Miami Beach. He was 84 years old and had lived in Pembroke Pines, Fla., for 14 years.
        Mr. Orgel, a native of Manhattan, graduated from City College in 1924. He joined the school system that year and taught while earning a master's degree in comparative literature from Columbia University and a Ph.D. in English literature from New York University.
        In 1932, Mr. Orgel joined the faculty of the newly opened Abraham Lincoln High School in Coney Island, became dean of boys and served in that post until his retirement in 1953.
        In his earlier years as a teacher, Mr. Orgel contributed articles on education to several newspapers. He also wrote textbooks on grammar and vocabulary.
        Mr. Orgel is survived by his wife, the former Ella Goldstein; a son, Dr. Shelley Orgel of Manhattan; a daughter, Phyllis Koestenbaum of San Jose, Calif., and seven grandchildren.

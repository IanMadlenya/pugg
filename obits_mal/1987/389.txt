        Stephen Gordon Glatzer, founder and president of two corporations that design, manufacture and sell aviation search-and-rescue equipment, died Saturday at the hospital of the Albert Einstein College of Medicine in the Bronx after a long illness. He was 51 years old and lived in New Rochelle.
        In 1968, Mr. Glatzer, a native New Yorker, founded Emergency Beacon Corporation in New Rochelle, followed by Glatzer Industries Corporation in 1975.
        He is survived by his wife, Joan Goodman; his mother, Dr. Henriette Glatzer of Manhattan, and a brother, John, of Oakland, Calif.

        Max J. Etra, a lawyer and philanthropist, died Wednesday at his home in Manhattan. He was 83 years old.
        Mr. Etra was associated for many years with prominent Jewish religious, educational, health and welfare organizations.
        From 1953 to 1977, he was chairman of the board of trustees of Yeshiva University. He was also a former president of the Union of Orthodox Jewish Congregations in America.
        At the time of his death, he was senior partner for the Manhattan law firm Etra & Etra.
        He was a graduate of City College of the City University of New York and Fordham University School of Law.
        He leaves no immediate survivors.

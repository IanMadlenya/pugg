        George O'Day, a sailboat designer and racer who captured an Olympic gold medal in yachting, died of colon cancer today. He was 64 years old.
        Mr. O'Day, who had raced sailboats competitively since high school, sailed the 5.5-Meter yacht Minotaur to victory in the 1960 Olympic competition off Naples, Italy. He served as assistant helmsman on the victorious United States yachts in America's Cup races in 1962 and 1967.
        A native of Brookline, Mass., Mr. O'Day designed 32 classes of sailboats, both for recreation and racing. He wrote three books on recreational boating for beginners.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Miriam Williamson; three sisters, a brother and a half-brother.

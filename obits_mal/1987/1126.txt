        Mark Olf, a composer and singer of Yiddish and Hebrew songs, died of leukemia Sunday at Booth Memorial Hospital in Queens. He was 82 years old and lived in Queens.
        Mr. Olf, a native of Russia, began his career as a teacher at the Henry Street Settlement on the Lower East Side of Manhattan, while developing skills as a violinist, guitarist and folk singer. He was later on the faculties of Queens College and the University of Rhode Island.
        His albums - three in Yiddish and one in Hebrew - feature traditional folksongs.
        Mr. Olf is survived by his wife, the former Rubia Goldman; a brother, George, of Queens; two sons, Julian, of Amherst, Mass., and Jonathan, of Queens, and a granddaughter.

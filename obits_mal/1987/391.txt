        Aaron Rubin, a former executive vice president and chief financial officer of NBC, died of a heart attack Dec. 23 at his home in Lake Worth, Fla. He was 72 years old, and also had a home in Brookville, L.I.
        Mr. Rubin was born in Brooklyn, and joined NBC in 1937, after attending City College. He later worked for ABC and served in the Army Air Forces in World War II, rejoining NBC in 1954 and retiring from the network in 1977.
        He is survived by his wife, Mary; a son, Stephen; a daughter, Mary, and two grandchildren.

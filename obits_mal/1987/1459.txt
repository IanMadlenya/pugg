        Charles J. Mascioli, a builder and developer on Long Island, died of cancer of the lymph glands Sept. 19 at his home in Great Neck, L.I.. He was 58 years old.
        With his father, Joseph, and his brother, Frank, Mr. Mascioli built hundreds of homes, apartment complexes, shopping centers and office buildings in Nassau and Suffolk Counties.
        A philanthropist, he was a director of Cooley's Anemia Blood and Research Foundation for Children in Garden City and a supporter of North Shore University Hospital in Manhasset and St. Francis Hospital in Roslyn, all on Long Island.
        Mr. Mascioli graduated from Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute of Troy, N.Y., as an architectural engineer and served with the Army Corps of Engineers during the Korean War.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Margaret A. Pfeiffer; a son, Dr. Charles J. Jr., of Great Neck; two daughters, Peggy R. Giuntini of Floral Park, L.I., and Diane Bonagura of Rockville Centre, L.I.; his parents, Joseph and Rose, of Manhasset; a brother, Frank, of Port Washington, L.I., and five grandchildren.

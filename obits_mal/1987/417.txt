        Victor Keppler, a commercial photographer, died Tuesday at his home in Manhattan. He was 83 years old.
        Born in Manhattan, he graduated from Stuyvesant High School and City College. He also attended New York University Law School.
        He received many awards for his commercial photographs and was the founder of the Famous Photographers School in Westport, Conn., where he also lived.
        Mr. Keppler was author of ''The Eighth Art, a Life of Color Photography,'' and ''Victor Keppler: Man and Camera.'' He was a past president of the Photographic Administrators Inc., and the Society of Photographic Illustrators.
        He is survived by his wife, Josephine Windmann Keppler; a son, Herbert; a daughter, Victoria Merl; two sisters, Lee Byck and Beatrice Zangwill, all of Manhattan, and four grandchildren.
        A memorial service is to be held at 10 A.M. today at Walter B. Cooke Inc., 1505 Third Avenue at 85th Street.

        Sir John Burgess, chairman of Reuters from 1959 to 1969, died Tuesday after a short illness. He was 74 years old.
        Sir John had spent his life involved with the press, much of it in his native northern England, where he was chairman of the Carlisle-based Cumbrian Newspapers group.
        From 1950 to 1957 he was also a director of Britain's national news agency, the Press Association, and from 1960 to 1981 he was chairman of Border Television, a private network serving the Scottish-English border.
        Sir John served in the 1939-45 war as an officer in the Border Regiment in France, the Middle East, Burma and India.

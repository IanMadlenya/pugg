        George William Holt 3d, a theater producer and director, died yesterday in Montclair, N.J. He was 46 years old.
        The cause of death was complications from pneumonia, according to Shirley Herz, Mr. Holt's press representative.
        Mr. Holt was born in San Francisco and graduated from the University of Oregon at Eugene. His first job in the theater was as assistant stage manager for the Mineola (L.I.) Playhouse. He was then hired by Hal Prince as assistant stage manager for the national company of ''Cabaret.''
        Mr. Holt, who was known as Fritz, later served as stage manager for the Broadway production of ''Indians'' and as production stage manager for the Hal Prince productions of ''Company'' and ''Follies.'' With Barry Brown, his partner for the last 16 years, Mr. Holt presented a revival of ''Gypsy'' in London, starring Angela Lansbury; a revival of ''The Royal Family''; ''Clams on the Half Shell,'' and the original ''Perfectly Frank.''
        Mr. Holt was executive producer of the Broadway production of ''La Cage aux Folles.'' He also produced and directed numerous benefits. With Mr. Brown, he staged an AIDS benefit, ''Best of the Best,'' at the Metropolitan Opera House in 1985. He directed the Actors' Fund of America benefit salute to George Abbott's 100th birthday at the Palace Theater last month.
        He is survived by his parents, Mr. and Mrs. George Holt of Lake Tahoe, Calif., and a sister, Patricia Holt, of San Francisco.

        Sheldon Claar, an investment manager, died Sunday of amyotrophic lateral sclerosis, also known as Lou Gehrig's disease, at his Manhattan apartment. He was 53 years old.
        Mr. Claar, a graduate of Colgate University and the Harvard Business School, was a founder and president of the S. Squared Technology Corporation, an investment managing concern. He recently advised the actress Raquel Welch in her portrayal of a victim of Lou Gehrig's disease for a television film, according to family and friends.
        He is survived by his mother, Barbara Gallay of Tamarac, Fla.; two daughters, Courtney and Jamie, of Greenwich, Conn., and a sister, Alice Claar of Manhattan.

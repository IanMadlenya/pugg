        Alexander Leitch, secretary of Princeton University for 32 years and a senior administrative officer for another decade, died of cancer Sunday at his home in Pennington, N.J. He was 86 years old.
        Mr. Leitch, a native of Paterson, N.J., was associated with the university from his graduation in 1924 until his retirement in 1966. He worked under three university presidents and one acting president.
        His duties included the general correspondence of the university, oversight of publications and the job of chief of protocol, handling commencement exercises and planning dozens of special events.
        He is survived by two children from his marrige to the late Mary Elizabeth Lancaster, Margaret Copeland of Pennington and Colin Leitch, of Jacksonville, Fla.; a sister, Emily Trottier of Troy, N.Y., and two grandchildren.
        A memorial service is scheduled for 11 A.M. Saturday at the Nassau Presbyterian Church in Princeton.

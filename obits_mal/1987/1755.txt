        Robert D'Arista, a painter, died of an aneurysm Oct. 11 at his home in Ashland, Mass. He was 58 years old.
        Mr. D'Arista, who taught for 26 years at the American University and Boston University, did large, richly colored, figurative still life and abstract paintings, as well as prints. His works are in the collections of the Guggenheim and other museums. Mr. D'Arista received a Fulbright fellowship in 1956 and an award from the National Institute of Arts and Letters in 1967.
        He is survived by his wife, Jane Webb D'Arista of Ashland; two daughters, Carla, of New York, and Antonia, of Ashland; two sons, Umberto, of Silver Spring, Md., and Thomas, of Ashland; two brothers, Frank, of Ridgefield, Conn., and Vincent, of Naples, and a sister, Angela Solli of Naples.
        A memorial service will be held Thursday at 4 P.M. at All Saints Church in Chevy Chase, Md.

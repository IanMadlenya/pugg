
       Henry Cotton, the most distinguished British golfer of his generation, died Tuesday. He was 80 years old.  Cotton, who turned professional in 1924, won the British Open in 1934, ending a long run of American victories and again in 1937 and 1948. He played many times in the United States and was captain of the Ryder Cup team in 1939, 1947 and 1953.

        Dr. Hugh Roberts Williams, director of the Talbot County Mental Health Clinic in Easton, Md., from 1977 to 1985 and the in-patient psychiatrist at Franklin Square Hospital in Baltimore from 1985 until early this year, died Wednesday of prostatic cancer at his home in Royal Oak, Md. He was 65 years old.
        Dr. Williams had been an assistant professor of psychiatry at the State University of New York at Buffalo and at the University of Colorado Medical Center. He also was an associate psychiatrist at the Mary Imogene Bassett Hospital in Cooperstown, N.Y., and at the Geisinger Medical Center in Danville, Pa.
        He was born in Orange, N.J., and was an alumnus of Haverford College and the Yale University Medical School. He served his internship at New York Hospital, and was a resident at the International Grenfell Association in Newfoundland. He also had a private practice in Highland Park, N.J.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Irene Sieminski; four sons, Hugh Jr., of Wilmington, N.C., David, of Easton, Md., Mark, of Highland Park, and Paul, of Clinton, N.J.; a daughter, Margaret Karl, of Albany; a sister, Anne Sullivan, of Glen Ridge, N.J., and four grandchildren.

        Emil H. Rice, former chief mechanical engineer of the North Atlantic division of the Army Corps of Engineers, died Wednesday at the North Shore University Hospital in Manhasset, L.I. He was 75 years old and lived in Great Neck, L.I.
        Mr. Rice worked for the Corps of Engineers in Manhattan from 1940 until his retirement in 1972. He joined the corps after working as an engineer for the Consolidated Edison Company of New York from 1933 to 1940.
        He is survived by his wife, Beatrice; two daughters, Toby Goldberg of Huntington, L.I., and Louise Rochkind of Woodland Hills, Calif.; three sisters, Gilda Feldman of Mount Vernon, N.Y, and Jeanette Kaplan and Ella Gabrilove, both of Palm Beach, Fla., and four grandchildren.

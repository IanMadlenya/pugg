        Graham D. Mattison, a retired investment banker, died Nov. 3 at his home in Rio de Janeiro after a long illness. He was 81 years old.
        Mr. Mattison, who was also a lawyer, was a partner of Dominick & Dominick, a New York investment house, from 1948 to 1964, when he retired. Earlier he had been a partner of White & Case, a New York law firm.
        He was born in Trenton and graduated from Princeton University in 1926 and from Harvard Law School in 1929.
        He was a 1922 graduate of the Kent School in Connecticut and later served as a trustee.
        Mr. Mattison was stationed in Europe in Army Air Corps intelligence from 1942 to 1945, reaching the rank of colonel.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Perla de Lucena; two sons, Mark, of New Canaan, Conn; Henry, of Woodstock, Vt.; four daughters, Pamela Hastings of Woodstock, Victoria Farrell of Wayland, Mass., Mary Dale Allen of Briarcliff Manor, N.Y., Margaret Mattison of Burlington, Vt., and 11 grandchildren.

        John C. Griswold, a retired executive vice president of W. R. Grace & Company, died Tuesday at his home in Manhattan. He was 85 years old.
        Mr. Griswold also had been an insurance broker and an investment banker. In the 1940's, he was head of Griswold & Company, an insurance brokerage that was later included in Grace's general business group, and, in 1962, merged with the Marsh & McLennan Companies.
        After Mr. Griswold retired from Grace in 1964, he was admitted as a general partner in the New York investment banking firm of Eastman Dillon, Union Securities & Company. He retired in 1972.  He was a trustee of Millikin University in Decatur, Ill., which he attended. He was also a founder and former governor of the New York College of Osteopathic Medicine of the New York Institute of Technology in Old Westbury, L.I., as well as a former trustee of Lenox Hill Hospital.
        He is survived by a daughter, Jacqueline Moore, of Cincinnati; a son, David, of Irvine, Calif., nine grandchildren and eleven great-grandchildren.

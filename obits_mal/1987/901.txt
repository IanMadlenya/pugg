        The Rev. Dr. Lowell Russell Ditzen, a clergyman, Presbyterian Church official and author, died Sunday from a stroke at the Georgetown University Hospital in Washington. He was 74 years old.
        Dr. Ditzen, who lived in Washington, was named director of the National Presbyterian Center in that city in 1963, a post he held until his retirement in 1974. From 1950 to 1962 he was senior pastor of the Reformed Church of Bronxville, N.Y.
        He was the author of 14 books, including ''Jesus and Our Human Needs,'' published in 1963, and ''The Minister's Desk Book, published in 1968.
        Dr. Ditzen, who was born in Kansas City, Kan., was graduated from the McCormick Theological Seminary in Chicago in 1936. He served as a minister in Chicago, New York City and Utica before moving to Bronxville.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Eleanor Davies Tydings, the widow of former Senator Millard E. Tydings of Maryland and daughter of Joseph E. Davies, the former United States Ambassador to the Soviet Union; two children from an earlier marriage, Lowell Stuart Ditzen of Philadelphia and Deborah Chiorino of Jaffrey, N.H., and three grandchildren.

        Clarence E. Unterberg, an investment banker, died Saturday at his home in Tinton Falls, N.J. He was 86 years old.
        In 1932, Mr. Unterberg founded C. E. Unterberg & Company, a small investment banking and brokerage firm that became L. F. Rothschild, Unterberg, Towbin Inc. after it merged with the Rothschild banking and brokerage concern in 1977, the year that Mr. Unterberg retired.
        He was president of the New York Securities Dealers Association in 1947 and a member of the National Association of Securities Dealers in the 1950's.
        Mr. Unterberg, who became noted as a philanthropist, served on the boards of the New York Cancer Society and the Monmouth County chapter of the American Red Cross and was active in a number of Jewish associations.
        Born in Manhattan, he attended the Horace Mann School and graduated from the Philadelphia College of Textile Engineering and the Columbia University School of Business Administration.
        He is survived by his wife, Marjorie; a son, Thomas I., of Manhattan; a daughter, Mary DeBare of Manhattan; a sister, Lillian Derector, also of Manhattan, and five grandchildren.
        A funeral service will be held Tuesday at 11 A.M. at Congregatation Shearith Israel, the synagogue at 8 West 70th Street where Mr. Unterberg was a board member for 50 years.

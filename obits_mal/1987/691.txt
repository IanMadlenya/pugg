        Marvin A. Asnes, a vice chairman of the Bowery Savings Bank, died of cancer yesterday at his home in Manhattan. He was 58 years old.
        Mr. Asnes joined the Bowery in 1985. He had been president and chief operating officer of Becton Dickinson & Company of Paramus, N.J., a manufacturer of medical and hospital supplies and industrial safety products. He resigned in 1983 to pursue other interests.
        He began his business career in 1954 with Clay Adams Laboratory Systems and was its vice president when the company was acquired by Becton Dickinson in 1964. He was elected chief operating officer of Becton Dickinson in 1974 and became president in 1980.
        At his death, Mr. Asnes was vice chairman and a trustee of the Mount Sinai Medical Center; treasurer and a director of the New York City Ballet, and treasurer and a director of the Bank Street College of Education.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Norma Ketay; three sons, Anthony, Andrew and James, all of Manhattan; his mother, Esther, of North Miami, Fla.; his father, Louis, of Hallandale, Fla., and a sister, Mrs. Sheldon Rothbart, of Hartford.
        A funeral service was scheduled for 11 A.M. Monday at Temple Emanu-El at Fifth Avenue and 65th Street.

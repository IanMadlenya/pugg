        Sidney Blaxill, a retired partner and managing director of Morgan Stanley & Company Inc., died of cancer Tuesday at Williamson County Hospital, Franklin, Tenn., after a long illness. He was 58 years old and lived in Princeton, N.J.
        Mr. Blaxill joined the firm of investment bankers in 1968. He became a partner in 1969, managing director in 1970 and retired as an advisory director in 1982. From 1953 to 1968 he was associated with J. P. Morgan & Company of New York in the bank's investment department.
        He was born in Cambridge, Mass., and graduated from Princeton University in 1949.
        Mr. Blaxill is survived by his wife, the former Marjorie Gibson; a brother, Peter of Manhattan; three sons, David Charles and Michael Arnold, both of Princeton, and Mark Francis of Boston, and a daughter, Susan Deal of Fort Pierce, Fla.

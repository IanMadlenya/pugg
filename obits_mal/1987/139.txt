        Amos Comay, a Pittsburgh businessman who was a member of several Jewish cultural and educational associations, died Nov. 12 in an automobile accident. He was 72 years old. He was president of the National Foundation for Jewish Culture from 1978 to 1982, and was a board member of the Council of Jewish Federations, the Jewish Education Services of North America and the American Jewish Joint Distribution Committee.
        Mr. Comay is survived by his wife, Ethel, a daughter, Deborah, and a son, Sholom, all of Pittsburgh; a brother, Eli, of Toronto, and a sister, Ann, of New York City.

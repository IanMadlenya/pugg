        Jerome M. Lane, a deputy attorney general of New Jersey from 1969 to 1978, died of complications from surgery Sunday at Mount Sinai Hospital in Manhattan. He was 55 years old and lived in Fair Lawn, N.J.
        Mr. Lane, who practiced law in Hackensack, was an expert on rent control. He had been a longtime counsel to the Fair Lawn Rent Leveling Board, town representative to the Bergen County Democratic Committee and a former president of the Fair Lawn United Democratic Club.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Phyllis Bressler; his father, Samuel, of Manhattan and Woodland Hills, Calif.; two sons, Jeffrey, of Paterson, N.J., and David, of Manhattan, and a daughter, Tracey Schwartz, of Livingston, N.J.

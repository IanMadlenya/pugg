        Humphrey Davis, a character actor whose stage, radio and television career spanned more than 50 years, died of cancer May 23 at Columbia Presbyterian Medical Center. He was 74 years old and lived in Edgewater, N.J.
        Mr. Davis, a native of Meriden, Conn., studied acting at Yale and made his stage debut in Connecticut at the age of 21. He made his last radio commercial days before he died and can still be seen in network television commercials and reruns.
        Mr. Davis last appeared on the New York stage in 1983 revival of John Osborne's ''Entertainer,'' in which he played the father of Nicol Williamson's Archie Rice. He also continued to tour campuses with his own one-man show, portraying the poet Longfellow.
        His Broadway credits included ''Othello'' and Peter Ustinov's ''Romanoff and Juliet.'' On television he appeared on ''Sergeant Bilko'' and the Alfred Hitchcock show, among others.
        Mr. Davis is survived by his wife, the former Mildred Anderson; two daughters, Lee Watters of Oxford, Conn., and Lenore Carnahan of Edgewater; a son, Robin Edward Davis of Greenwich, Conn.; a sister, Dorothy Konney of Windsor, Conn., and four grandchildren.

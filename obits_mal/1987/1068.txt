        Henry F. Argento, an executive of the electronics industry for many years, died May 23 at his home in Far Hills, N.J. He was 77 years old.
        He was a former vice president of Raytheon and Philco. For the last 20 years he was a director of Sanders Associates, a manufacturer of military electronics and computer graphics.
        Mr. Argento joined Raytheon in 1932 after studying at the Harvard Business School and remained with the company until 1956. He then joined Philco, heading its government and industrial division until 1962.
        He is survived by his wife, Iris; a son, Gerrit, of McLean, Va.; a daughter, Elizabeth Bennett, of Blawenburg, N.J.; two stepsons, Army Capt. Peter B. Zwack, of Washington, and John Zwack, of Far Hills; three step-daughters, Gioia and Alexa Zwack, both of Manhattan, and Iris Zwack of Rome, Italy; a sister, Elizabeth Frazer of Sacramento, Calif., and nine grandchildren.

        John W. Humes, a professor in the graduate division of management, Baruch College, the City University of New York, died of cancer of the colon Tuesday at Riverview Hospital in Red Bank, N.J. He was 51 years old and lived in Little Silver, N.J.
        Dr. Humes taught at Baruch College for 14 years, until he became too ill to teach. From 1975 to 1976 he was president of Cumberland County College in Vineland. Earlier he had taught at Moravian College and at Lehigh and Fairleigh Dickinson Universities.
        He was a partner in Humes, Stern Associates, a management consulting firm in Richboro, Pa., with which he had been associated for the last 17 years.
        Dr. Humes was born in Philadelphia and was a graduate of Lehigh University in 1957, where he later earned both a master's degree and a doctorate. He was a registered professional engineer.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Deborah Scott; two daughters, Connie Maltbie of Moorestown, N.J., and Alyson Humes of West Chester, Pa.; two stepsons, Stephen Redden of Manhattan and Scott Redden of Little Silver, N.J.; his mother, Eleanor Humes of Philadelphia, Pa.; two sisters, Eleanor Bartholomew of Cadillac, Mich., and Linda Kemp of Pottstown, Pa., and two grandsons.

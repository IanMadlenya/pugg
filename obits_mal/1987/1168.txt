        Walter O. Heinze, former president of the International Latex Corporation and past chairman of the board of the STP Corporation, died of cancer Monday at his home in Green Valley, Ariz. He was 83 years old. Mr. Heinze was chairman of the board of Control Fluidics Inc., a research and development company and industrial-filter company, at the time of his death.
        After serving more than 20 years as president of International Latex, manufacturers of Playtex brand consumer products, Mr. Heinze served concurrently as chairman of the board of STP and the Benrus Corporation.
        Mr. Heinze began his career as a distributor of microscopes and other scientific instruments. He was also an investment counselor.
        During World War II, President Franklin D. Roosevelt appointed Mr. Heinze director of the Smaller War Plants Corporation, which mobilized small businesses in the war effort. Before his appointment, he served on the war production board.
        Mr. Heinze, who enjoyed archaeology, was also a founder and active participant in the Deya Majorca Archaeological Museum and Research Center, in Majorca, Spain.
        He also served as president and a director of the Child Welfare League of America for many years.
        Mr. Heinze is survived by his wife, the former Louise Mendius; a son, Don R., of Largo, Fla.; two daughters, Carol Phillios of Melbourne, Fla., and Dorothy West of Bethesda, Md.; 11 grandchildren, and four great-grandchildren.

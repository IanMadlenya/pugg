        Robert T. Elson, a writer and former editor and executive for Time Inc., died Wednesday at Southampton (L.I.) Hospital of complications following a stroke. He was 80 years old and lived in East Hampton, L.I.
        Mr. Elson, a native of Lakewood, Ohio, joined Time Inc. in 1943 after working two decades as a Canadian newspaper reporter. He held various editorial and executive positions at Time, Fortune and Life magazines until he retired in 1969.
        He was the author of two volumes of a three-volume company history and of ''Prelude to War,'' which is part of a Time-Life series.
        He is survived by two sons, John, of Manhattan and Robert, of Chevy Chase, Md.; three daughters, Katherine Zadravec of Chevy Chase, Md., Elizabeth Elson of Manhattan and Bridget Elson of Toronto; a sister, Marjorie Lindsey of Bradenton, Fla., and 10 grandchildren.

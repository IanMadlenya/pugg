        Frank E. Schwartz, a book publisher and marketing innovator, died of a heart attack Wednesday while on vacation in Palm Beach Gardens, Fla. He was 40 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Until recently, Mr. Schwartz was president of Simon & Schuster's general reference group, which includes Prentice-Hall trade books, the J. K. Lasser Tax Guides, Webster's New World Dictionaries and the Frommer and Baedecker Travel Guides. He was instrumental in recent months in helping Simon & Schuster acquire publishing rights to both the Mobil Travel Guides and to General Mills's ''Betty Crocker Cookbook.''
        Mr. Schwartz joined Simon & Schuster as president of it's electronic publishing division, where he supervised the company's entry into computer software and helped create such products as the thesaurus software from Webster's New World Dictionary, Mr. Schwartz, born in Scranton, Pa., graduated from Lafayette College and earned a master's degree in business administration from the Harvard Business School. After working for two advertising agencies, he became a senior vice president at Wells Rich Greene Inc., where he was in charge of the Atari account.
        Mr. Schwartz is survived by his wife, Suzanne Jacobs Schwartz, a Broadway producer, and by his parents, Sidney and Hannah Schwartz of Palm Beach Gardens.
        The funeral service will be at noon Monday at the Frank E. Campbell Funeral Chapel, 1076 Madison Avenue, at 81st Street.

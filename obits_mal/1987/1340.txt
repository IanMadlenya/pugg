        Joseph Sawyer, a retired retailing executive and financial and marketing consultant, died of emphysema Wednesday at his home in the Riverdale section of the Bronx. He was 77 years old.
        Mr. Sawyer retired in 1975 as an executive of the Associated Merchandising Corporation, a buying service owned by major department stores. He joined the organization in 1941.
        Born in Boston, he was a graduate of the Boston Latin School, Harvard College and Harvard's Business School, where he received a master's degree in business administration in 1934.
        He was a former director of the Metropolitan Retail Financial Executives Association and a member of, among others, the National Retail Merchants Association.
        He is survived by his wife, Sara; a son, Marc, of Manhattan, and a sister, Betty Pendler of Manhattan.

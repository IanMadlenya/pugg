        Gerard L. Oestreicher, a real-estate developer and Broadway producer, died Sunday at New York Hospital after a long illness. He was 70 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Oestreicher was founder and chairman of the Oestreicher Realty Companies. Among his holdings were the Newsweek Building at 444 Madison Avenue and buildings at 708 Third Avenue, 430 Park Avenue and 160 Water Street.
        With James Nederlander, he built and operated the 1,940-seat Uris Theater at 221 West 51st Street. The theater, which opened in November 1972, is now known as the Gershwin.
        Mr. Oestreicher was probably best known on Broadway as the producer of ''Milk and Honey,'' a 1961 romantic musical set in modern Israel that was written by the composer Jerry Herman and the playwright Don Appell. He also produced ''A Girl Could Get Lucky'' (1964), ''The Royal Hunt of the Sun'' (1965) and ''But, Seriously. . .'' (1969). Received Bronze Star
        He graduated from Columbia College in 1937. In World War II, he received a Bronze Star for his actions in the Battle of the Bulge. He was the New York State finance chairman for the 1956 Presidential campaign of Adlai E. Stevenson.
        Mr. Oestreicher was the former chairman of the real-estate division of the Federation of Jewish Philanthropies, the former president of the Jewish Campus Life Fund at Columbia University and a former member of the board of the Educational Alliance.
        He is survived by his wife, Irma; a son, Lee, of Washington; two daughters, Laurie Lowenstein, of Lincroft, N.J., and Lynn Jeffrey, of Manhattan; two stepsons, Robert Shapiro, of Manhattan, and Daniel Shapiro, of Houston, and six grandchildren.
        A service is scheduled for 9:30 A.M. Wednesday at Temple Shaaray Tefila at 250 East 79th Street.

        Tommy Davis, a record-setting kicker with the San Francisco 49ers, died of lung cancer Thursday at his home in San Bruno, Calif., his relatives here reported today. He was 52 years old.
        Davis was a place-kicker and punter on the top-ranked Louisiana State University team in 1958. As a professional, he established the record for most consecutive extra points, 234, while making 348 of his 350 extra-point attempts before he retired in 1970. He also compiled a career punting average of 44.7 yards, second to Sammy Baugh's 45.1 in National Football League history.

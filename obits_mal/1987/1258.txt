        Israel Packel, a lawyer, educator and former state attorney general who served on Pennsylvania's Superior and Supreme Court benches, died Wednesday. He was 79 years old.
        Mr. Packel received a bachelor's and law degree from the University of Pennsylvania. In 1933, he joined President Franklin D. Roosevelt's National Recovery Administration.
        Mr. Packel was appointed to the Superior Court bench in 1972 and served as state attorney general from 1973 to 1975. In 1977, he was given an interim appointment to the Supreme Court, which he filled until reaching the mandatory retirement age of 70.

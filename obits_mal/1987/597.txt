        William M. Masland, a former pilot who in 1943 commanded the first round-the-world flight by a commercial aircraft, died of a heart attack Friday at his home in Manhasset, L.I. He was 79 years old.
        Mr. Masland joined Pan American Airlines in 1935. He was the junior officer on the airline's first flight to Hong Kong in 1936 and the navigator on its trans-Atlantic survey flights in 1937.
        In World War II, when Pan American's commercial planes were taken over by the military, Mr. Masland was in command of the round-the-world flight, from New York to Perth, Australia, and back. He retired in 1966.
        Mr. Masland was born in Philadelphia and graduated from Haverford College in 1929.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Sara Edgerton; three daughters from his marriage to the late Anne Comfort, Mary Adams of Arlington, Va., Dr. Susan Masland of Milwaukee and Rebecca Masland of Boston; two brothers, James G. of Newport, R.I., and Dr. Richard L. Masland of Englewood, N.J., and six grandchildren.

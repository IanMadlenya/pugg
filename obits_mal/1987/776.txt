        Frederick A. Wyckoff, a retired New York City real-estate executive, died of a heart attack Monday in Lakewood, N.J. He was 79 years old and lived in Lakehurst, N.J.
        Mr. Wyckoff, who retired about 20 years ago, specialized in cooperative conversions. He was a former president of L. J. Phillips-Wood, Dolson Company, which at one time managed thousands of middle-income co-op apartments in New York.
        From 1958 to 1960, Mr. Wyckoff was president of the Broadway Association, a civic group. He was also a former chairman of the city planning committee of the Real Estate Board of New York.
        Surviving are his wife, Helen; two sons, Robert, of Huntington, L.I., and Peter, of St. Paul; a sister, Ruth Redmond of Lakehurst, and seven grandchildren.

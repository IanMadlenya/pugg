        Sir David Roberts, British Ambassador to Lebanon from 1981 to 1983, died in Dartford, England, on Sunday, his family reported Monday. He was 62 years old.
        Born in Wales, he attended Oxford University and served in the army during World War II. He joined the Foreign Service in 1947, holding many posts. In 1983 he was in charge of British interests during a four-month Israeli bombardment of Beirut. Queen Elizabeth II made him a Knight Commander of the British Empire.

        Harris J. Klein, a former member of the New York City Transit and Human Rights Commissions, died of Alzheimer's disease Thursday at Maimonides Hospital in Brooklyn. He was 80 years old and lived in Brooklyn.
        Mr. Klein, a lawyer who specialized in transportation, served the Transit Commission from 1953 to 1959. In 1974, he was appointed to the Human Rights Commission, serving two years. He was also active in Brooklyn Democratic politics.
        Mr. Klein was a former chairman of Ward LaFrance of Taunton, Mass., a company that manufactured firefighting equipment. He headed the group of investors that purchased the company in 1959.
        Born in Manhattan, Mr. Klein was a graduate of the Brooklyn Law School.
        He is survived by his wife, Celia Reitman; three daughters, Carol Hall, of Raleigh, N.C., and Joan Tauscher and Barbara Goldfeder, both of Rockville Centre, L.I.; a son, Mitchell, of Nashua, N.H.; a brother, Philip, of Brooklyn, and seven grandchildren.

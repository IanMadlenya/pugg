        Walter T. Skallerup Jr., the general counsel of the Navy Department and a former deputy assistant secretary of defense, died of cancer Wednesday at the Georgetown University Hospital in Washington. He was 67 years old and lived in McLean, Va.
        Mr. Skallerup, who had been the Navy's general counsel since 1981, was in private law practice in Washington for many years in between periods of government service.
        He served as deputy assistant secretary of defense for security policy from 1962 to 1967 and was a member of the Atomic Safety and Licensing Board in 1970 and 1971 and a consultant to the Joint Congressional Committee on Atomic Energy in 1973 and 1974.
        He served as the general counsel and treasurer of the late Senator Henry M. Jackson's unsuccessful campaigns for the Democratic Presidential nomination in 1972 and 1976.
        Mr. Skallerup was born in Chicago and was a graduate of Swarthmore College and Yale Law School. He served in the Navy in World War II.
        His first wife, Nancy, died in 1981. He is survived by his second wife, Margaret; three children, Paula Osborn of McLean, Nancy Skallerup of Boston, and Walter 3d of Manhattan; three stepchildren, Catherine Fleming, Edward Hull and Dorothy Hull, all of Washington; his mother, Ruth, of Washington; a sister, Barbara Weiss of Chevy Chase, Md., and two grandchildren.

        John L. F. Sipp, chairman and chief executive officer of the Staten Island Savings Bank, died Monday in Bayley Seton Hospital, Staten Island. He was 68 years old.
        Mr. Sipp, who lived in Staten Island, was an Army pilot instructor from 1942 to 1946 and later a production engineer. He was admitted to the bar in 1949 after graduating from Brooklyn Law School and became a partner in the firm of Fach, Sipp & Hall.
        Mr. Sipp was named chairman of the bank in 1974. From 1975 to 1977 he served on the Temporary Commission on New York City Finances, to which he was named by former Mayor Abraham D. Beame.
        He is survived by his wife, Virginia; two sons, Jeffrey of Staten Island and Gregory of Boston, and a brother, William B. Sipp of Staten Island.

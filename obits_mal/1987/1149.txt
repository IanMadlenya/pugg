        Severin Peter Langhoff Jr., a former advertising executive and an international lottery and sports betting consultant, died Monday at Suburban Hospital in Bethesda, Md., after a brief illness. He was 77 years old and lived in Manhattan and Bethesda.
        He was a former vice president of Young & Rubicam, the New York advertising agency. He joined the agency in 1946 as director of research and became a vice president in 1948 and a senior vice president in 1964.
        In 1966, he left Young & Rubicam to become head of the American Research Bureau, which has since become Arbitron, the television rating service, with its headquarters in Beltsville, Md. In 1971, he served as a consultant to the automated wagering division of the Control Data Corporation. He founded his own company, Game Plan International, in Bethesda in 1975.
        Mr. Langhoff, who was born in Peoria, Ill., graduated from the University of Illinois in 1931 and received a doctorate in economics from the university in 1935.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Barbara Brumbaugh; three sons, Peter, of Oakland, Calif., and Christopher and Andrew, both of Manhattan, and a sister, Margaret Jacobs of Peoria.

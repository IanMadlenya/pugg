
        Allen Farish Maulsby, a New York lawyer specializing in commercial law and complex antitrust litigation who was associated with the firm Cravath, Swaine & Moore for 57 years, died on Nov. 26 at a hospital in Greenwich, Conn. He was 82 and lived in Old Greenwich.
        The cause was cancer, his family said.
        Mr. Maulsby, a native of Baltimore, graduated from Williams College in 1944. He received his law degree in 1946 from the University of Virginia, where he was editor in chief of the law review, and joined Cravath the next year.
        He became a partner in 1958 and a senior counsel to the firm in 1995. He represented major corporate clients in negotiations with federal and state agencies regarding mergers and acquisitions. After 1995, he was active as an arbitrator and mediator and a special master of the Appellate Division of State Supreme Court in New York, First Department.
        Mr. Maulsby is survived by his wife, Nancy Sands Maulsby; a daughter, Monica M. Beard; two sons, Allen F. Jr. and Tyler E.K.; two stepdaughters, Helen P. Curry and Tyler G. Duer; and four stepsons, William S. Post, Charles M. Post, Bradley B. Geist Jr. and Nicholas V.V. Geist.

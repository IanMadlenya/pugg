
        Anthony Ripley, a former national correspondent for The New York Times and a founding editor of the quarterly Discovery YMCA, died on Friday in Boulder, Colo. He was 75 and lived in nearby Lyons. The cause was a stroke, his family said.
        Mr. Ripley was a reporter for The Detroit News from 1956 to 1965 and for The Times from 1967 to 1975. He was an associate editor of The Rocky Mountain News from 1976 to 1978, then was a communications executive for the United Way of America.
        He is survived by his wife, Ann; six daughters, Sarah G. Ripley, Mary R. Ripley, Catherine Habsburg-Lothringen, Emily Ripley, Jane Ripley and Martha Gray; two sisters, Maud Granzow and Virginia McFarland; two granddaughters; and his stepfather, John A. Cooper.

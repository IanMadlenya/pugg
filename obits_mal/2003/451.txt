
        George H. Heyman Jr., a philanthropist and financier who was president of the Federation of Jewish Philanthropies of New York from 1969 to 1971 and a trustee of New York University and its Medical Center in the 1980's and 1990's, died on Sunday at his home in Manhattan. He was 86.
        Mr. Heyman was a member of Lehman Brothers Kuhn Loeb Inc.'s executive committee and held other senior posts at that firm in the late 1970's and early 1980's, after being president of the Wall Street concern Abraham & Company.
        Besides being active in fund-raising for the federation and N.Y.U., he was chairman of N.Y.U.'s trustee development committee, which oversaw the university's fund-raising, from 1978 to 2001.
        A native of Manhattan, George Harrison Heyman Jr. graduated from Townsend Harris High School in Queens and City College of New York and received a master's degree in business administration from New York University.
        He served in the Army in Iceland, France and Germany during World War II and was a captain when his active duty ended.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Edythe Forman, whom he married in 1946; two sons, William, of Manhattan, and John, of West Palm Beach, Fla.; and two grandchildren.
        Correction:  June 7, 2003, Saturday  An obituary on Wednesday about George H. Heyman Jr., a philanthropist and financier, misstated the location of Townsend Harris High School when he graduated. It was then in Manhattan. (It is now in Queens.)

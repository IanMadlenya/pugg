
        Edward Porter Alexander, an authority on museums, died on July 31 at a rehabilitation center in Chevy Chase, Md. He was 96.
        He was president of the American Association of Museums and the founder and first director of the University of Delaware's Museum Studies Program.
        His writings include the books ''Museums in Motion: An Introduction to the History and Functions of Museums,'' ''Museum Masters: Their Museums and Their Influence'' and ''The Museum in America: Innovators and Pioneers.''
        Born in Keokuk, Iowa, he held a bachelor's degree from Drake University, where he was elected to Phi Beta Kappa, and a doctorate from Columbia.
        His wife, the former Alice Bolton Wagner, died in 1999.
        Surviving are a son, John T., of Lawrence, Kan.; a daughter, Mary S. Alexander of Washington; five grandchildren; and a great-grandson.

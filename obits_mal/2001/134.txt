
        Robert E. Garis, a writer on dance, literature, music and film, died on Jan. 26 at his home in Cambridge, Mass. He was 75.
        Mr. Garis was best known in recent years for his ballet criticism, published in Ballet Review, Partisan Review and other journals. He also wrote ''Following Balanchine'' (Yale University Press, 1995).
        Born in Hawley, Pa., Mr. Garis was a Fulbright Scholar in England. He wrote for publications including The Nation, The Hudson Review and  Commentary.
        He began teaching at Wellesley College in 1951 and in his 43 years there was the chairman of the English department and dean of the college. He was named the Katherine Lee Bates Professor of English emeritus after his retirement.
        There are no immediate survivors.

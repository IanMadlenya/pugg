
        Dr. Seymour L. Wolfbein, a federal economist and authority on employment, died on Aug. 7 in a nursing home in Potomac, Md. He was 85 and lived in Chevy Chase, Md.
        Dr. Wolfbein joined the Department of Labor in Washington in 1942 and left it in 1967 for teaching. For years he was the department's senior specialist on manpower and spoke for the department on manpower trends.
        He was chief of manpower and employment in the department's Bureau of Labor Statistics and later director of the department's Office of Manpower, Automation and Training. He was appointed to that post in 1962.
        After leaving the Labor Department, he was dean of the Temple University business school and taught there. He held educational posts in Japan and Austria and lectured widely. He was also a consultant to business executives in the United States.
        His many writings include the book ''Manpower Policy: Perspectives and Prospects'' (1973).
        Born in Brooklyn, he graduated from Brooklyn College and received a doctorate from Columbia University.
        Surviving are his wife of 60 years, Mae; two daughters, Susan Morris of Washington and Deeva Garel of Bethesda, Md.; and four grandsons.

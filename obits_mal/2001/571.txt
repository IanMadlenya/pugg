
        William L. Rafsky, a manager in Philadelphia government for four decades, died on June 11 at his home in Philadelphia. He was 81.
        Mr. Rafsky, a Democrat who had worked for the War Production Board in Washington in World War II, was hired by Mayor Joseph S. Clark Jr., after his election in 1951.
        Mr. Rafsky, who had a particular interest in housing, went on to become development coordinator under Mayor Clark and his first two successors.
        He was then a member of the city planning commission under Mayor Frank L. Rizzo, deputy finance director under Mayor Bill Green and deputy managing director under Mayor W. Wilson Goode. He retired in 1992.
        Born in Lodz, Poland, he was brought to the United States by his parents in 1919, grew up in the Bronx and received a bachelor's degree from what was then City College of New York. He moved to Philadelphia in 1945.
        He is survived by his wife of 60 years, Selma Chafets Rafsky; a son, Lawrence C., of Livingston, N.J.; and a granddaughter.

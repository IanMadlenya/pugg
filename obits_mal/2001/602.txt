
        Rabbi Benjamin M. Kahn, executive vice president of B'nai B'rith International from 1971 to 1976, died on July 4 at a hospital in Silver Spring, Md. He was 87 and lived in Silver Spring.
        Rabbi Kahn retired from B'nai B'rith in 1976 and was then elected honorary executive vice president for life.
        From 1959 to 1971, he was international director of B'nai B'rith Hillel Foundations, which operate centers for Jewish student life on campuses in the United States and elsewhere.
        Rabbi Kahn was born in Lowell, Mass., graduated from Harvard College and was ordained at the Jewish Theological Seminary in New York.
        He married Rosalind Aronson in 1938. She died in 1968.
        Rabbi Kahn is survived by his companion, Lillian T. Shevitz; a daughter, Jenette Kahn of Manhattan; a son, Si, of Charlotte, N.C.; and three grandsons.

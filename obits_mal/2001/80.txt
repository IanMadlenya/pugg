
        Thomas J. Mirabile, a retired justice of the New York State Supreme Court and a past member of the New York City Council, died Wednesday at a hospital in Mineola, N.Y., near his home. He was 92.
        He was born in Brooklyn on March 22, 1908 and received a degree in business administration and accounting from New York University in 1927.
        A graduate of Brooklyn Law School, Justice Mirabile was a City Council member from 1947 to 1960.
        He served as a Civil Court judge, and in 1972 was elevated to the State Supreme Court. He retired in 1984.
        He is survived by his wife, Lucy; four daughters, Serena Bogucki, Eloise Bellard and Lisa DiChiara, all of Garden City, N.Y., and Paula Baker of Danbury, Conn.; a son, Dr. Thomas J. Jr., of Fort Lauderdale, Fla.; and eight grandchildren.

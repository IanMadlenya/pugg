
        Artemas Holmes Crimmins, former chairman and president of Thomas Crimmins Contracting, once a prominent builder in the New York City area, died on April 21 at a hospital in Boulder, Colo. He was 92 and had homes in Longmont, Colo., and Boca Grande, Fla.
        He retired in 1974 after 42 years with the family company, which began in 1847 and worked on Shea Stadium and the Pan Am Building..
        Born in Manhattan, he graduated in 1932 from Harvard and was an Army captain in the Pacific theater in World War II.
        He was predeceased by his wives, Doris Bates Crimmins and Virginia Ransom Smith Crimmins, and his sons, Artemas Holmes Crimmins Jr. and Kevin Bates Crimmins. He is survived by a daughter, Doris Crimmins of Steamboat Springs, Colo.; 9 grandchildren; and 13 great-grandchildren.

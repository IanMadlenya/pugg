
        Louis Edmonds, an actor who had a long career on the New York stage but was best known for his roles on the television shows ''Dark Shadows'' and ''All My Children,'' died on  March 3 at John T. Mather Memorial Hospital in Port Jefferson, N.Y. He was 77 and lived in Setauket, N.Y.
        Mr. Edmonds worked on Broadway, off Broadway and in regional theater. He played leading roles in works by Chekhov at David Ross's Fourth Street Theater, acted in plays by Shakespeare, Congreve and Ionesco, and was Algernon in the Off Broadway musical ''Ernest in Love.''
        He made his Broadway debut in 1956 in the musical ''Candide.''
        On ''Dark Shadows,'' he was Roger Collins and on ''All My Children,'' Langley Wallingford.
        He was born in Baton Rouge, La., and studied at Louisiana State University and the Carnegie Institute of Technology. During World War II he served in the Navy.
        He is survived by a brother, Walter Jr., and a sister, Alma Edmonds Fritchie, both of Baton Rouge.

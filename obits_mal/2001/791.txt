
        George I. Reynolds, a pioneer in cable television, died on Aug. 22 at his home in Cooperstown, N.Y. He was 85 and also had a home in West Palm Beach, Fla.
        Mr. Reynolds became interested in the potential of cable television in the 1960's, when cable was a technology that was largely confined to transmitting television signals to remote places.
        In 1969, he acquired Cable Vision in Lewiston, Me. In 1970, he founded Mid-Hudson Cablevision in Hudson, N.Y., and in 1971, Valley Cable Vision in Seymour, Conn. He was president of all three companies.
        Cable Vision was sold in 1974, and Valley Cable in 1990.
        Mr. Reynolds was born May 27, 1916, in Oneonta, N.Y. He graduated from Wesleyan College in 1938, was a lieutenant in the aviation wing of the Navy during World War II, and received an M.B.A. from Harvard in 1947.
        Mr. Reynolds is survived by his wife, Nancy Sullivan Reynolds; his brother, John M., of McLean, Va.; five sons, James, of Great Barrington, Mass.; Robert, of Sharon, Conn.; Frederick M., of Leeds, N.Y.; G. Jeffrey, of Vero Beach, Fla.; and David F. Wilber III, of Boca Grande, Fla.; a daughter, Janet L. Reynolds, of Washington; 23 grandchildren; and 5 great-grandchildren.

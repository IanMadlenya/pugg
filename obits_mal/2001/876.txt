
        Murray Schwartz, a former New York State senator from Queens, died on Oct. 5 at his summer home in Roslyn, N.Y. He was 82 and also had a home in Lake Worth, Fla.
        He was a Democrat in the State Senate from 1965 to 1973 and later was New York City commissioner of commerce and industry.
        He was on the Interstate Commerce Commission from 1960 to 1964. After 1978, he was a leader of the Honest Ballot Association, a watchdog association for elections.
        Mr. Schwartz is survived by his wife, Jeanette; a brother, the composer Jack Lawrence of West Redding, Conn.; a son, Fred, of Boca Raton, Fla.; a daughter, Joanne Lebow of Marion, Mont.; five grandchildren and two great grandchildren.

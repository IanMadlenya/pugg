
        Richardson Pratt Jr., a former president of the Pratt Institute in Brooklyn and chairman of Charles Pratt & Company, which was established in 1891 to oversee the Pratt family interests, died Tuesday at his home in Cold Spring Harbor, N.Y. He was 78.
        The cause was pancreatic cancer, said his wife, Mary.
        Mr. Pratt, known as Jerry, was a great-grandson of the Pratt Institute's founder, Charles Pratt, who was a partner of John D. Rockefeller in the Standard Oil Company. Mr. Pratt was president of the institute  from 1972 to 1990.
        Born on March 25, 1923, Mr. Pratt grew up in Brooklyn and Glen Cove, N.Y. He graduated from the Choate School and received a bachelor's degree from Williams College in 1946 and a master's degree from the Harvard Business School in 1948.
        During World War II, Mr. Pratt served on a Navy minesweeper. In 1948 he joined Standard Oil (now Exxon Mobil), where he held various positions in marketing, planning and economics until 1971, when he joined Charles Pratt & Company. He retired in 2000.
        In addition to his wife, Mary Offutt Pratt, Mr. Pratt is survived by their three children, Laura P. Gregg of Bryn Mawr, Pa., Thomas R. Pratt of Coconut Grove, Fla., and David O. Pratt of Chestnut Hill, Pa.; eight grandchildren; and one great-grandson.

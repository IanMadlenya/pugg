
        John B. Johnson, the editor and publisher of The Watertown Daily Times for the last 52 years, died yesterday at his home in Watertown, in northern New York. He was 84.
        The Daily Times, which covers Jefferson, St. Lawrence and Lewis Counties near the Canadian border, has a daily circulation of 32,608, and has maintained a one-man Washington bureau for decades. The paper is part of the family-owned Johnson Newspaper Corporation, a chain of seven daily and seven weekly papers. Mr. Johnson was its chairman.
        He was a member since 1950 of the American Society of Newspaper Editors, and he received the New York Newspaper Publishers Association's John Peter Zenger Award in 1993 for his contributions to the newspaper industry in New York State.
        John Brayton Johnson was born in Watertown in 1916 and graduated from Phillips Exeter Academy in Exeter, N.H., and from Princeton University. He was an officer in Army counterintelligence in World War II.
        He is survived by his wife, Catherine; two sons, John B. Jr., of Watertown, managing editor of The Daily Times, and Harold B., also of Watertown, vice president of the corporation; two daughters, the Rev. Deborah J. Johnson of King George, Va., and Ann J. Kaiser of Ossining, N.Y.; and eight grandchildren.

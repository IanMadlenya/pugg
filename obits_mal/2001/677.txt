
        S. Leslie Misrock, a New York patent lawyer who was an authority on biotechnology patent law, died on Tuesday at Memorial Sloan-Kettering Cancer Center in Manhattan. He was 73 and lived in Chappaqua, N.Y.
        He had prostate cancer for 27 years, his family said.
        Mr. Misrock was a senior partner in the Manhattan-based law firm of Pennie & Edmonds and represented many biotechnology companies.
        He was born in Jersey City, and he held a bachelor's degree in chemistry from the Massachussetts Institute of Technology, a master's degree in chemistry from Columbia University and a law degree from Fordham Law School.
        Surviving are his wife, Barbara; two daughters, Kathryn Misrock Goldstein and Victoria Misrock, both of Manhattan; and a grandson.

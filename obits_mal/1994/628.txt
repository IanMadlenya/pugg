
        Joseph F. Czechlewski, a retired New York lawyer who was active in Polish-American political and cultural affairs, died on Wedneday at Cabrini Medical Center. He was 85 and had homes in Manhattan and Wainscott, L.I.
        The cause was prostate cancer, his family said.
        A native of New York City, Mr. Czechlewski graduated from St. John's University Law School in 1931. He served as an assistant district attorney in Manhattan from 1939 to 1948.
        In World War II, he was a liaison between the Office of Strategic Services and the exiled Polish Government in London. In 1950 he helped organize the Polish National Fund to support that government and was the fund's treasurer for three decades.
        He was a past president of the Polish Singers Alliance of America, a cultural organization active in the United States and Canada. At his death he was a national director of the Polish American Congress. He also was actively involved with the Pulaski Day Parade in New York City and the Paderewski Foundation.
        Mr. Czechlewski is survived by his wife of 58 years, Sophie Wagner Czechlewski; a daughter, Joanne Wallace of Greenwich, Conn., and two grandchildren.


        Dan Bailey, a special education teacher in Manhattan public schools and a founding director of the Gay Men's Health Crisis, died yesterday at New York University Medical Center. He was 51.
        The cause was AIDS, said Joanna Halteman, his sister.
        Mr. Bailey worked as a teacher for the New York City Board of Education for 25 years. He was both a vice president and a board member of the G.M.H.C. Mr. Bailey was a graduate of Murray State University and New York University.
        He is survived by his parents, Mr. and Mrs. Donald Bailey of Richmond, Ind.; a brother, James, of Peru, Ind., and two sisters, Margaret Bailey of San Diego and Mrs. Halteman of Dallas.


        Dr. Paul A. Kerschner, an authority on aging, died on Monday at George Washington University Hospital in Washington. He was 52 and lived in Washington.
        He died of cancer, said Ed Howard, a friend.
        For the last two years, Dr. Kerschner was the director of the Gerontological Society of America. He directed the National Foundation for Long-Term Health Care from 1985 to 1989 and was senior vice president of the National Council on the Aging from 1989 to 1992.
        He graduated from California State University and received a doctorate in public administration from the University of Southern California.
        He is survived by his wife, Dr. Helen Kerschner, and a daughter, Alexis, both of Albuquerque, N.M.; a brother, Lee Kerschner of Sacramento, Calif., and a sister, Lois Carl of Los Angeles.

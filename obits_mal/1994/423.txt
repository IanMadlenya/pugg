
        Samuel Blutter, who retired in 1984 as president of Dolly Martin Inc., a maker of women's sportswear, died on Feb. 9, at a Manhattan nursing home. He was 83 and lived in Manhattan.
        He had had a long illness, his family said.
        Mr. Blutter, a self-made man and Manhattan native, went to work as a youth to support his family and spent more than five decades in the garment industry. He was a founder and past president of the Have-A-Heart Foundation, which the industry formed to assist garment workers and their families in times of need.
        As head of his manufacturing company, he served as chief collective bargaining agent for the sportswear industry for many years. He also was a past president of the National Association of Blouse Manufactuers and a director of the Atlantic Apparel Association.
        His wife, Dolly, died in 1984.
        He is survived by a daughter, Arlene Wolff of Manhattan; a son, Ted, of Sedona, Ariz., and one granddaughter.


        John W. Bristol, an investment counselor to schools and colleges, died on Thursday at the Woods Edge Care Center in Bridgewater, N.J. Mr. Bristol, who lived in Peapack, N.J., was 81.
        The cause was complications of Parkinson's disease, said his daughter, Barbara Bristol.
        His firm, John W. Bristol & Company in New York, pioneered the use of equity investment for endowment funds. Several dozen public and private institutions were clients of the firm, including Princeton and Howard Universities and Swarthmore and Colby Colleges.
        Mr. Bristol was a 1935 graduate of Williams College and served as an officer in the Army Air Force during World War II. In 1948 he began his career in advising schools on the means of investing their endowment funds; he retired in 1986.
        He is survived by his wife, Louise; a sister, Elizabeth Bell of Essex Fells, N.J., and two daughters, Barbara of Manhattan, and Nancy Homer of Southwest Harbor, Me.

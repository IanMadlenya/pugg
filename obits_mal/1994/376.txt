
        John Koshel, a retired Manhattan lawyer who specialized in copyright and trademark law, died on March 2 at Lenox Hill Hospital. He was 64.
        The cause was cancer, said his wife, Marysia.
        From 1986 to 1989 Mr. Koshel was counsel to Sesac Inc., a music performing-rights licensing group in Manhattan. From 1976 to 1985 he was the general counsel and secretary of Prentice-Hall in Englewood Cliffs, N.J. Before that he had been an associate with the Manhattan law firm of Lord, Day & Lord.
        He was a graduate of Long Island University and the Brooklyn Law School.
        After his retirement he raised goats on a farm in Shandalee, N.Y.
        Mr. Koshel is survived by his wife; two sons, John J. of Mount Vernon and Jeremy of Upper Nyack; a daughter, Ann van Buren of Baltimore, and four grandchildren.

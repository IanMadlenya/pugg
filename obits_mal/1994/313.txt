
        Milford A. Vieser, a retired mortgage and real-estate finance executive who lived in Short Hills, N.J., died yesterday at St. Barnabas Medical Center in Livingston, N.J. He was 91.
        The cause was pneumonia, said his wife, Vera.
        Mr. Vieser joined the Mutual Benefit Life Insurance Company of Newark in 1942 and rose to become executive vice president in charge of financial operations and chairman of the company's finance committee. He retired in 1970.
        He was a director of the First Fidelity Bank of Newark and Triangle Industries of New Brunswick, N.J. He was a president of the Greater Newark Chamber of Commerce and a director of the Regional Plan Association.
        He is survived by his wife; a son, William, of Seattle, and three grandchildren.

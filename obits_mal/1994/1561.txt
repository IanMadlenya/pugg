
        E. W. Swackhamer, who directed popular television series and movies, died on Dec. 5 in Berlin, where he was on location for a new film. He was 67 and lived in Encino, Calif.
        The cause was a ruptured aortic aneurysm, said his wife, Bridget Hanley.
        Mr. Swackhamer's credits included "M*A*S*H," "L.A. Law," "Murder, She Wrote," "Jake and the Fatman," "Bewitched," "The Partridge Family" and "The Flying Nun." He was nominated for an Emmy Award for directing the six-hour mini-series "The Dain Curse" in the 1977-78 season. Of the 27 pilots for television series directed by Mr. Swackhamer, 18 went into regular production, including "Eight Is Enough," "Quincy," "S.W.A.T" and "Nancy Drew."
        He went to Hollywood in 1961 after working on and off Broadway and for national companies as an actor, stage manager and director. He was the stage manager for the original Broadway production of "Cat on a Hot Tin Roof."
        In addition to his wife, he is survived by three daughters, Bronwyn, Megan and Elizabeth Swackhamer Pike, all of Los Angeles; a son, Ten Eyck, of Topanga, Calif.; a brother, John, of Orinda, Calif., and a grandson.

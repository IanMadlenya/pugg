
        Elliot H. Goodwin, a Republican Party leader in Manhattan in the 1950's, died on Saturday at his home in Glen Cove, L.I. He was 77.
        The cause was emphysema, said his wife, Hope.
        Mr. Goodwin served as president of the Ninth Assembly District Republican Club starting in 1955. In 1958 he lost a primary fight with John V. Lindsay in the race for their party's nomination for the seat in the 17th Congressional District. Mr. Lindsay won the seat in the general election.
        Mr. Goodwin graduated from Harvard University and Harvard Law School. He commanded a torpedo boat in the South Pacific during World War II and was discharged from the Navy with the rank of lieutenant.
        He was a former partner in the Manhattan law firm of Hall, McNichol, Hamilton, Clark & Murray. He retired in 1989.
        Mr. Goodwin is survived by his wife; a son, Charles, of Manhattan, and a daughter, Ellen Schmitz, also of Manhattan.

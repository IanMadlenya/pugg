
        Paul A. Murtaugh, a writer and marketing consultant in New York, died on Monday at Calvary Hospital in the Bronx. Mr. Murtaugh, who lived in Manhattan and Amagansett, L.I., was 72.
        The cause was cancer, said his wife, Suzanne.
        Mr. Murtaugh was the marketing and creative director of the Research Institute of America for about 20 years. He retired in 1982. He also was the publisher of "Art Market Guide and Forecaster."
        He was a graduate of the City College of New York and was an Army veteran of World War II.
        He is survived by his wife; two daughters, Margot and Barbara, both of San Francisco; a son, Paul, of Corvallis, Ore.; his mother, Margaret Murtaugh, and a sister, Margaret Marone, both of the Bronx, and three brothers, Charles, of Fort Myers, Fla., James, of Tuckahoe, and Joseph, of Putnam Valley, N.Y.


        Fred W. Wilson, a partner in the Wall Street investment banking concern Lazard Freres & Company, died on Wednesday at his home in Manhattan. He was 82.
        Mr. Wilson joined Lazard Freres in 1945, became a general partner in 1961 and a limited partner in 1971. Before joining Lazard, Mr. Wilson was a financial analyst with the General American Investment Company.
        Mr. Wilson was a 1933 graduate of Columbia College.
        He is survived by his wife of 47 years, Andrea Wilson of Manhattan; a son, David of Wayne, N.J.; a daughter, Pamela Wilson of Chicago, and two grandchildren.

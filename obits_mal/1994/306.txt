
        Nathan H. Mitchell, a former deputy state tax commissioner, died on Saturday at St. Luke's Hospital. He was 90 and a resident of Manhattan.
        He suffered a heart attack, his family said.
        A native of New York City, he graduated from New York University with a degree in accounting. In the 1930's he worked as an investigator for the State Department of Taxation and Finance and advanced to become deputy commissioner and head of special investigations in the 1940's. He retired from the Civil Service in 1968.
        Mr. Mitchell is survived by his wife of 67 years, Ida Blum Mitchell; a daughter, Carole Morris of Rye Brook, N.Y.; a brother, Samuel, of Dade County, Fla.; two grandchildren, and four great-grandchildren.


        John H. Stimpson, who spent 36 years with the New York Life Insurance Company and then retired to become a professional cook, died on Sunday at his home in Irvington, N.Y. He was 67.
        The cause was throat cancer, said his wife, Valerie Stimpson.
        Mr. Stimpson started with New York Life in Portland, Me., in 1951, retiring in 1987 as an executive vice president in Manhattan. He then attended the Culinary Institute of America for two years and became a cook for the Hudson House of Nyack.
        Mr. Stimpson was a graduate of the University of Maine and received an M.B.A. from the University of Pennsylvania.
        He is survived by his wife of 40 years; a son, Kevin of Darien, Conn.; two daughters, Karen Sweene of Peekskill and Kimberly Fisher of Oklahoma City, and a sister, Janet Hill of Cape Elizabeth, Me.

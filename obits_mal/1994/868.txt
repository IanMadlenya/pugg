
        Robert T. Orr, an expert on animal biology and behavior, died on June 23 at his home in Larkspur, Calif. He was 86.
        He had a long illness, The San Francisco Chronicle reported.
        Dr. Orr spent two decades with the California Academy of Sciences and was affiliated with the University of California at Berkeley, Stanford University and the University of San Francisco. His research included studies of seals, sea lions and whales along the California coast.
        His many writings include the books "Mammals of Lake Tahoe" (California Academy of Sciences, 1949) and "Marine Mammals of California" (University of California, 1972).
        A native of San Francisco, he studied at the University of San Francisco and the University of California at Berkeley.
        He is survived by his wife of 20 years, Margaret Cunningham Orr; a daughter, Nancy Davis of Spokane, Wash.; two granddaughters and three great-granddaughters.

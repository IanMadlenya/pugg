
        Enos Curtin, a retired financial consultant and investor who also helped direct advocacy groups for the blind, died on Tuesday at New York Hospital. A resident of the Upper East Side, he was 99.
        The cause was pneumonia, his family said.
        Apart from his consulting business, Mr. Curtin was a director of several corporations, including the Madison Square Garden Corporation, the American and Foreign Power Company and the Paramount Corporation.
        He also served as an officer of the American Field Service, the Lighthouse of New York and the American Foundation for the Blind, among other groups.
        In World War I, he was an ambulance driver for the American Field Service in France and subsequently, an artillery lieutenant in the United States Army. In World War II, he was on the civilian staff of Gen. Dwight D. Eisenhower in England, then rejoined the Army as an officer in charge of troop morale in England and North Africa.
        Born in Buenos Aires, Mr. Curtin was brought to this country as a boy. He studied at Columbia University and the Massachusetts Institute of Technology, where he majored in engineering.
        He is survived by a grandson, Curt Gross of Las Vegas, two great-grandsons and three great-great-grandchildren.

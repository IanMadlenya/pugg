
        Sheldon I. Diesenhouse, a lawyer who practiced in Middletown, N.Y., for 21 years, died yesterday in Horton Memorial Hospital there. He was 51 and a resident of Middletown.
        The cause of death was cancer, his family said.
        Mr. Diesenhouse was a partner in the firm of Meyer & Diesenhouse. He was a native of Brooklyn and graduated from City College and the University of Tennessee Law School.
        He served as an assistant district attorney both in Brooklyn and Orange County. He formerly was a staff attorney for the Orange County Legal Aid Society and acting City Court judge in Port Jervis, N.Y., from 1985 to 1990.
        He is survived by his wife, Arlene Pasternack Diesenhouse; a daughter, Samantha B., and a son, David B., both of Middletown; his mother, Sylvia Diesenhouse of Pembroke Pines, Fla.; and two sisters, Jacalyn D. Stewart of Woodbridge, Conn., and Susan Diesenhouse of Cambridge, Mass.

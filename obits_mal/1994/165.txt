
        Michael Wolff, a New York real-estate broker, died on Friday at his home in Larchmont, N.Y. He was 54.
        The cause was cancer, his family said.
        Mr. Wolff was executive managing director of Newmark Real Estate Services, a management concern. He joined Newmark in January 1993, in a merger with his own realty company, G. W. Michaels Inc.
        He was born in Brooklyn and graduated from Duquesne University. In the 1960's, he and Michael Godt formed G. W. Michaels. Mr. Godt later died.
        Mr. Wolff was a member of the Board of Overseers of the Albert Einstein College of Medicine of Yeshiva University. He was also an adjunct professor of commercial real estate at New York University.
        He is survived by his wife, Susan, and their two children, Russell and Joanna, all of Larchmont, and his mother, Lillian, of North Bergen, N.J.

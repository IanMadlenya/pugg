
        Philip H. Strubing 2d, a lawyer who served as president of the United States Golf Association in 1970 and 1971, died Sunday at a nursing home in Memphis. He was 86.
        His family said he died after a series of strokes.
        Before his retirement in 1972, Mr. Strubing, a trial lawyer who began his U.S.G.A. service as general counsel from 1959 to 1962, was chairman of the law firm of Pepper, Hamilton & Scheetz in Philadelphia.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Elizabeth Hanes; two daughters, Leigh Josey of Atlanta and Stephanie Flinn of Greenwich, Conn.; a son, Philip 3d of Memphis; eight grandchildren and three great-grandchildren.

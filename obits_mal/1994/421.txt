
        Roger A. Chaufournier, a former regional vice president of the World Bank in Washington, died on Tuesday at his home in Potomac, Md. He was 70.
        He died of cancer, said his daughter, Stephanie Chaufournier of Manhattan.
        Mr. Chaufournier retired 10 years ago from the bank, whose official name is the International Bank for Reconstruction and Development. He was an economist and administrator with the bank for 32 years. He held a Doctor of Laws degree from the University of Paris and a doctorate in economics from the University of Illinois.
        Besides his daughter, he is survived by his wife, Edna; a son, Roger, of Bethesda, Md., and a sister, Suzanne Berger of Chambery, France.

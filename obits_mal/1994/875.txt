
        Jeremy Brooks, a British novelist and playwright, died on June 27 at his home in Llanfrothen, Wales. He was 67.
        Mr. Brooks was born in Southampton, England. He studied at Oxford, served in the British Navy in World War II, and wrote the well-received novel "Jampot Smith" and other fiction in the 1960's.
        He was the literary manager of the Royal Shakespeare Company from 1962 to 1969 and adapted plays by Maxim Gorky and other Russian playwrights.
        His fiction included a 1986 volume of short stories, "Doing the Voices" (Viking Penguin), three stage plays, television and radio plays, three film scripts and numerous poems.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Eleanor Nevile; a son, William, and three daughters, Josephine, Margaret and Polly.


        John F. Hanley, who founded an executive recruiting firm in New York, died on March 31 in the St. Paul Health Care Center in San Diego, Calif. Mr. Hanley, who lived in San Diego, was 87.
        The cause was prostate cancer, said Philip Hanley, a nephew.
        Mr. Hanley founded Hanley Associates in 1951, retiring in 1970. The firm specialized in recruiting executives for financial companies.
        Mr. Hanley is survived by his wife, Florine Hanley of San Diego, and a brother, W. J. Hanley of Scarsdale, N.Y.

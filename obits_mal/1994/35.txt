
        Alvin James Ferro, retired president and chief executive of the Paddington Corporation, a liquor importer and marketer, died on Saturday at Palm Beach Cardena Hospital in West Palm Beach, Fla., at the age of 65. A former resident of Larchmont and Rye, N.Y., he lived in Tequesta, Fla.
        The cause was congestive heart failure, his family said.
        A native of the Bronx, Mr. Ferro graduated from the State University of New York at Plattsburgh and attended Harvard Business School. He started with the Minute Maid division of Coca-Cola in 1952 and held senior sales and marketing positions.
        In 1966 he was named vice president of marketing and sales for the Holland House division of the National Distillers and Chemical Corporation. He joined Paddington in 1973 as a vice president and was president and chief executive from 1978 until his retirement nine years ago.
        Mr. Ferro is survived by his wife, Helen Adams Ferro; four daughters, Marianne Smith of Utica, N.Y., Kathleen Martinez of Homestead, Fla., and Peggy Ferro and Cynthia Marie Boss, both of Tampa, Fla.; three sons, Daniel J., of Annapolis, Md., Robert J., of Rye, N.Y., and Michael J., of Greenwich, Conn.; three stepdaughters, Sharon Heede of Manhattan, Michele Heede of Boston and Kristin Murphy of Weston, Conn., and 12 grandchildren.

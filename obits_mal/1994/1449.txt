
        Charles J. Umlauf, a sculptor, died on Nov. 19 at a hospital in Austin, Tex. He was 83.
        He died after a lengthy illness, The Houston Post said.
        Mr. Umlauf studied at the Art Institute of Chicago and was among many artists who produced work for the Federal Art Project during the 1930's. He became a professor at the University of Texas in Austin in 1941. He retired as art professor emeritus in 1981.
        His works are in the collections of the Smithsonian Institution in Washington and the Metropolitan Museum of Art in New York City. Among his better-known sculptures is "Spirit of Flight" at Love Field in Dallas.
        He is survived by his wife, Angie; four sons, Karl, Louis, Arthur and Tom, and two daughters, Lynn and Madelon.

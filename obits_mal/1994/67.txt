
        Dr. Sherman Strong Hayden, an expert on American diplomatic history, international law and international relations, died on Monday at his home in Holden, Mass. He was 85.
        Dr. Hayden joined the faculty of Clark University in Worcester, Mass., in 1946, rising to Professor of International Relations and, in 1962, chairman of the department of history, government and international relations. He retired in 1973.
        A native of Cleveland, he graduated from Harvard University, in 1930, and from Harvard Law School. He received a Ph.D. at Columbia University, where he taught government from 1938 to 1942. He then spent two years as assistant to the president of the Foreign Policy Association.
        In World War II he served as a lieutenant in the Navy, assigned to the Office of Naval History in Washington.
        He is survived by his wife of 61 years, Marjorie Mallett Hayden; a son, Christopher M., of Madison, Wis.; two daughters, Clare H. White of Holden, and Lorna H. Power of New York City; seven grandchildren, and two great-grandchildren.


        Dr. Martin Bodian, a retired eye surgeon who practiced in Brooklyn for more than 40 years, died on Saturday at Mediplex of Southbury, a nursing and rehabilitation center in Southbury, Conn. Dr. Bodian, who lived in Southbury, was 82.
        The cause was a head injury sustained in a fall, said his wife, Claire Bodian.
        Dr. Bodian joined the staff of the Brooklyn Eye and Ear Hospital in 1946, becoming chief of its opthalmic plastic surgery clinic in 1953. He also was the director of opthalmic plastic surgery at Brookdale Hospital and was a professor of opthalmic plastic surgery at the Downstate Medical School in Brooklyn. He retired in 1990.
        Dr. Bodian graduated from Columbia College in 1932 and the New York University School of Medicine in 1936. He served as a captain in the Army Medical Corps in the South Pacific during World War II. .
        He is survived by his wife; two daughters, Marcelle Mazor of Suffern, N.Y., and Lisette Turner of Berryville, Va.; a brother, Dr. Eugene Bodian of Great Neck, L.I.; two sisters, Anna Koeppel of Lawrence, L.I., and Claire Rubin of Rochester, and five grandchildren.

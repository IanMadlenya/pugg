
        Paul V. Ames, a retired New York real-estate broker, died on Monday at the Veterans Administration Medical Center in East Orange, N.J. Mr. Ames, who lived in Woodcliff Lake, N.J., was 71.
        The cause was pneumonia, said his son, Paul V. Ames Jr.
        Starting in 1970, Mr. Ames was a commercial real-estate broker for the New York firm of Cushman & Wakefield, from which he retired in 1985. He previously had been a marketing executive for hotel and chemical companies.
        He graduated from the University of Notre Dame and in 1949 the Harvard Graduate School of Business. He served as a first lieutenant in the Army Air Force in World War II, was shot down over Romania and was a prisoner of war.
        He is survived by his wife of 43 years, Ruthmarie Ames; his sons, Paul Jr., of Manhattan, and Hugh, of Chatham, N.J.; two daughters, Elizabeth Ames and Allison Ames, both of Manhattan; a brother, Morgan, of Stamford, Conn., and a sister, Margaret Auer of South Orange, N.J.


        Dr. Harry G. Albaum, a research biologist who was a retired professor and vice president of Brooklyn College, died on May 23 in Brattleboro, Vt., where he recently moved from Rockville Centre, L.I. He was 84.
        He suffered a brief illness, the college said.
        A Brooklyn College graduate, Dr. Albaum received a master's degree from New York University and a Ph. D. from Columbia University. He joined the Brooklyn College Department of Biology in 1939 as an instructor and advanced to full professor in 1953. He served as department chairman in the early 1960's.
        He then held administrative positions at City University of New York and Brooklyn College, from which he retired as vice president for program research and administrative affairs in 1976.
        Dr. Albaum is survived by his daughter, Judith Gorman of Richmond, N.H., and two grandchildren.

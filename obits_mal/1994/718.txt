
        Leslie R. Severinghaus, former headmaster of Haverford School in Pennsylvania, died on Wednesday at his home in Coconut Grove, Fla. He was 94.
        The cause was heart failure, said a daughter, Elizabeth Warner of Osterville, Mass.
        Mr. Severinghaus taught at the school from 1929 to 1942, when he became the headmaster. He retired in 1965.
        Over the years, he was president of the Middle States Association of Colleges and Secondary Schools and the Country Day Schools Headmasters Association, as well as vice president of the Headmasters Association of the United States. He was a trustee of several educational institutions and organizations.
        Born in Nashville, he received a bachelor's degree from Cornell University in 1921 and then taught English in Beijing for several years. He earned a master's degree in English literature at Columbia University in 1927 and was later awarded four honorary doctorates. .
        In visits to Taiwan from 1966 to 1974, he established an English language laboratory for students.
        His writings included the book "Religions in History" (Vantage, 1986).
        He married Emmavail Luce, a sister of Henry R. Luce, co-founder of Time magazine, in 1924. She died in 1985.
        In addition to Mrs. Warner, Mr. Severinghaus is survived by a son, Dr. Sheldon Severinghaus of Ulan Bator, Mongolia; another daughter, Margaret Boles of Osterville; eight grandchildren, and 12 great-grandchildren.


        Stan Carter, a former foreign correspondent for The Associated Press and a retired Washington correspondent for The Daily Newsin New York, died on Wednesday at St. Vincent's Hospital. He was 69.
        The cause was complications from hepatitis, said his wife, Patricia Peters Carter.
        Mr. Carter was a correspondent for A.P. in Asia, Europe and the Middle East. After 12 years with the news agency, he joined The Daily News in 1961. He became its diplomatic correspondent in Washington in 1965.
        He retired from The News in 1985, became the editor and publisher of The Mill Valley (Calif.) Record, and returned to New York in 1991.
        He is survived by his wife, of Manhattan; a son, James S. Jr. of Baltimore, and a sister, Margaret Hodges of Palo Alto, Calif.

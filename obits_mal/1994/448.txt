
        Peter B. Seaman, a real-estate sales executive, died on Saturday in Greenwich Hospital in Connecticut. Mr. Seaman, who lived in Greenwich, was 42.
        The cause was cancer, said his wife, Nancy.
        Mr. Seaman was the chairman of the Houlihan/Lawrence Company, a real-estate business with more than 400 employees that specialized in residential properties in Westchester, Putnam and Dutchess Counties.
        He graduated from Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute and received a master's degree in business administration from Harvard University.
        He is survived by his wife; a daughter, Callie, and a son, James, both of Greenwich, and two brothers, David of Chicago and Richard of Boston.

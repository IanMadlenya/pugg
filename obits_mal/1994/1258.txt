
        Paul R. Picard, a television producer and executive, died on Oct. 3 at his farm in Tuscany, Italy. He was 63.
        The cause was pancreatic cancer, said a statement from Warner Brothers Television.
        Mr. Picard began his career as an actor before turning to production. He is best known for creating the television series "The Dukes of Hazzard," for which he also served as executive producer for eight seasons.
        He also produced a number of made-for-television movies and several mini-series, including "Scruples," "California Fever" and "The Pirate."
        In the 1960's, Mr. Picard was the director of daytime programs for ABC and then moved on to oversee "The Lawrence Welk Show," "The Hollywood Palace" and the Emmy and Academy Awards broadcasts. In the 1970's, he worked at MGM, American International Pictures and Warner Brothers.
        He is survived by his wife, Juliette; a daughter, Stephanie; his mother, Irene, and two sisters, Gert and Claire.


        Peter W. Allport, former president of the Association of National Advertisers, died on Oct. 3 at the Royal South Hants Hospital in Southampton, England. He was 74 and lived in Bronxville, N.Y.
        He died after having a heart attack on the Queen Elizabeth 2 while on his way to France, said his wife, Margaret Nichols Allport.
        Mr. Allport retired in 1984 after 24 years as president of the association, which is based in Manhattan and represents the interests of corporations that advertise regionally and nationally. Its members now have a total of more than 2,000 subsidiaries, divisions and other operating units.
        He joined the association's staff in 1945 as editor of its news publications and rose through its ranks. Over the years, he was chairman of the Commerce Department's Advertising Advisory Committee and a director of the Advertising Council, the Advertising Research Foundation and the American Advertising Federation.
        He was born in Vienna, where his father worked at the United States Embassy, and grew up abroad and in Bronxville. He graduated from Brown University and was a Navy officer in World War II. He was a copywriter with the Erwin Wasey advertising agency in Manhattan before joining the association.
        Besides his wife of 48 years, he is survived by a son, George, of Peapack, N.J.; a brother, Alexander, of Manhattan, and two grandchildren.

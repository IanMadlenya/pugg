
        Dr. Charles J. McPeak, who practiced surgery in Manhattan for 40 years, died on Friday at Lenox Hill Hospital. Dr. McPeak, who lived in Manhattan, was 75.
        The cause was cardiac arrest after surgery for a broken hip, said his wife, Maida.
        During his career Dr. McPeak was an attending surgeon on the staffs of James Ewing Hospital, the Memorial-Sloan Kettering Cancer Center, the Manhattan Eye, Ear, Nose and Throat Hospital and Lenox Hill Hospital. He was also on the faculty of the Cornell University Medical School.
        Dr. McPeak graduated from Temple University, where he also received a medical degree in 1944. He was a surgeon in the Navy during World War II and in the Korean War, serving in the Pacific, the Mediterranean and at the Naval Medical Center in Bethesda, Md.
        Dr. McPeak conducted research into and lectured on several types of cancer, including melanoma and soft-tissue sarcomas.
        He is survived by his wife of Manhattan and a sister, Eileen Linden of Manassas, Va.

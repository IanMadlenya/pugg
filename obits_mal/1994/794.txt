
        Douglas W. Bryant, a director of Harvard University's libraries, died on Sunday at Massachusetts General Hospital in Boston. Mr. Bryant, who lived in Lexington, Mass., was 80.
        He died of cancer, his wife, Rene, said.
        Mr. Bryant joined the Harvard library staff in 1952, becoming university librarian in 1965 and director of the library system in 1972, when he was also named a professor of bibliography. He retired in 1979.
        Mr. Bryant was instrumental in creating a computer-based catalogue for scholars, which evolved into a national consortium known as the Research Libraries Group.
        He received a bachelor's degree from Stanford University and a master's degree from the University of Michigan. In World War II he was a lieutenant commander in the Navy.
        Besides his wife he is survived by a daughter, Heather Jordan of Concord, Mass., and two grandchildren.

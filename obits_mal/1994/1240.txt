
        Burton M. Abrams, a well-known New York lawyer who specialized in corporate and securities law, died on Sept. 23 at Memorial Sloan-Kettering Cancer Center. Mr. Abrams, who lived in Brick Town, N.J., was 70.
        The cause of death was cancer, a nephew, Paul Lieberman of Middleton, N.J., said.
        For more than 40 years, as a member of various law firms in New York City, Mr. Abrams represented many major corporations. He was general counsel, director and an officer of the Struthers Wells Corporation, Harvard Industries and the New York Law Journal Publishing Company.
        Active in charities devoted to children, he founded the Peggy and Burton M. Abrams Philanthropic Fund for the benefit of hospitals and helped establish the Peggy and Burton M. Abrams Pediatric Center. The 15-patient facility is in the Brick Hospital Division of the Medical Center of Ocean County, in Point Pleasant, N.J. He bequeathed a substantial portion of his estate to the Memorial Sloan-Kettering Cancer Center.
        Mr. Abrams grew up in Brooklyn and graduated from City College, where he studied accounting and finance.
        During World War II, he served as an Army intelligence officer, studying Japanese and developing expertise in deciphering coded Japanese communications.
        In 1948, Mr. Burton graduated magna cum laude from Harvard Law School with a juris doctor degree after becoming an editor of the Harvard Law Review.
        Among other organizations, he was a member of the Association of the Bar of the City of New York, the American Bar Association, the New York State Bar Association, the New York County Lawyers Association and the Grand Street Boys Association.
        Mr. Abrams is survived by his wife, the former Marguerite Clower, and a stepdaughter, Jean Lapi of Manhattan.


        Harry Weltchek, an authority in probate law and a former president of the bar association in Union County, N.J., died on Feb. 5 at his winter home in Palm Springs, Calif. He was 92.
        The cause was congestive heart failure, said his nephew, James Rosenfield.
        Mr. Weltchek lived in Jamesburg, N.J. He was a former counsel for the Township of Clark, N.J., and for the Amalgamated Clothing and Textile Workers and the International Ladies' Garment Workers' Union.
        He was past president of the Elizabeth, N.J., Jewish Council and of Temple B'nai Israel there and was active in several Jewish civic organizations.
        He is survived by his wife, Elma, of Jamesburg; a daughter, Abby Wells of Woodbury, Conn.; a son, Roy of Watchung, N.J., and a grandson.

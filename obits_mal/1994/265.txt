
        John A. Snyder, a retired senior vice president of J. P. Morgan & Company, died on Feb. 6 at Stamford Hospital in Stamford, Conn. He was 69.
        The cause was prostate cancer and hemophagocytosis, a blood disorder, the company said.
        Mr. Snyder joined the investment bank in 1960 as a trainee and was assigned to the domestic banking group. He rose through the ranks and in 1971 was appointed senior vice preident in charge of the New York offices. He retired from the bank in 1987.
        A graduate of Harvard University and Harvard Business School, he was also a member of the Board of Finance of the Town of Darien, Conn., and a director of the National Schools Committee for Economic Education, a nonprofit group promoting entrepreneurship.
        Mr. Snyder's first wife, Sue Wilds Davis, died in 1976.
        He is survived by his wife, Mary Kniffin Huntington of Darien; two daughters, Sue S. Stebbins of Darien, and Pamela S. Borman of Rowayton, Conn., and four stepchildren, Tracy H. Hager of Cooperstown, N.Y., Hilary H. Huntington of Rhinebeck, N.Y., Robin S. Huntington of New York City, and R. Ford Huntington of Salt Lake City.

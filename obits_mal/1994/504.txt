
        Charles Elihu Winer, a television writer and arts administrator, died on Monday at New York University Hospital in Manhattan. He was 79 and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Winer had Alzheimer's disease for several years, said his son, Stephen, of Manhattan.
        Mr. Winer was born in Chattanooga, Tenn., attended Vanderbilt University and received a bachelor's degree from Yale University. After a stint as a drama reporter for The New York Herald Tribune, he worked as a story editor at Universal Studios. In World War II, he wrote training material for the Army. After the war, he was a story editor for Warner Brothers, Universal and Samuel Goldwyn.
        In the 1950's, Mr. Winer wrote for all three television networks. His scripts included "The Girl in Chapter One," starring Joanne Woodward, for "The Alcoa Hour," and "The Bounty Court Martial," starring Ronald Reagan, for "Omnibus." He also adapted Robert Traver's "Anatomy of a Murder" for the theater. His play "Chekhov on the Lawn" was produced in New York City at Theater East in 1981.
        From 1964 to 1967, Mr. Winer was the executive director of the National Council on the Arts, and from 1972 to 1984, he was the deputy executive director of the Writers Guild of America East, which he helped found.
        His first wife, Carol Kemmler, died in 1970.
        In addition to his son, he is survived by his second wife, Duvie; a stepson, Comdr. Jeff Kuhnreich of Springfield, Va.; a brother, Norman, and two sisters, Miriam Brown and Hadassah Gold, all of Manhattan.

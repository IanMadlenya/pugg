
        Donald R. Cohodes, the administrator of Federal programs for the Chicago-based Blue Cross and Blue Shield Association, died on Wednesday at Evanston Hospital in Evanston, Ill. He was 44 and lived in Wilmette, Ill.
        He died of cancer, said the national association of independent Blue Cross and Blue Shield plans.
        Dr. Cohodes supervised the association's Medicare operations and shaped policy on doctor-payment reform, health plans, rates and other regulatory matters. He joined the association in 1982 and was a vice president.
        He was born in Grand Rapids, Mich., and received a bachelor's degree in economics from the University of Michigan. He also had a master's degree in public affairs from the University of Minnesota and received a Ph.D. from Harvard University in 1980.
        From 1984 to 1990, Dr. Cohodes was the editor of Inquiry, an international journal on the organization and financing of health care. He was a senior program consultant for the Robert Wood Johnson Foundation and the author of more than 40 articles and book chapters on health care.
        He is survived by his wife, Dale Canchester Cohodes; three children, David, Sarah and Nina; his parents, Leona and Archie Freedman of Grand Rapids, and two brothers, Jay, of Miami, and Joel, of Houston.


        Kenneth J. Ludwig, an insurance executive, died on Friday at Brunswick Hospital in Amityville, L.I. He was 72.
        The cause was pulmonary fibrosis, a progressive degeneration of the lungs, said his son Christopher, of Massapequa, L.I.
        Mr. Ludwig began at Travelers Life in 1952 as a field supervisor and then worked at several companies before becoming a director at the United States Life Insurance Company in 1970. Four years later, he became its president.
        In 1983, after four years as executive vice president at the National Health and Welfare Mutual Life Assurance Association, he helped to found and became president of the John Adams Life Insurance Company and the Universal Holding Corporation, both of New York. He retired in 1991.
        He is survived by his wife, Bette; three other sons, Kenneth, of Golden, Colo., Mark, of Queens, and Daniel, of Lindenhurst, L.I.; five daughters, Susan Ambrosio of Hauppauge, L.I., and Elizabeth Bailey and Mary Diehlmann, both of Copiague, L.I., Barbara, of Seaford, L.I. and Amy, of North Massapequa, L.I.; a brother, Robert, of Amagansett, L.I., and eight grandchildren.

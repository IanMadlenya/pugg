
        Francis X. Murtagh, a retired lawyer and Assistant District Attorney in New York, died on Thursday at his home in Venice, Fla. He was 75.
        The cause was cancer, said his son, Francis X. Murtagh Jr.
        Mr. Murtagh became an Assistant District Attorney in Manhattan in 1947, and later was an Assistant United States Attorney in Manhattan. In 1950 he organized Murtagh & Cohen, a law firm in Garden City, L.I., that concentrated in antitrust and construction law. He retired in 1990.
        He was a graduate of New York University and earned a law degree at Harvard University in 1947.
        In addition to his son, of Long Beach, L.I., Mr. Murtagh is survived by his wife of 51 years, Lorayne Murtagh, and a daughter, Patricia Byrne of Rockville Centre, L.I.

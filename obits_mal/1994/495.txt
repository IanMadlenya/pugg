
        Lieut. Gen. John A. Heintges, who was deputy commander of the Military Assistance Command in Vietnam in the 1960's, died on Thursday at the Veterans Affairs Medical Center in Denver. He was 81 and lived in Colorado Springs.
        General Heintges died of complications from Parkinson's disease, the Army said.
        As a colonel in World War II, he commanded the Seventh Infantry when the regiment captured Hitler's villa at Berchtesgaden in May 1945. He was a 1936 graduate of West Point, and retired in 1971.
        General Heintges is survived by his wife, Marianne; two sons, Robert A., of Manhattan, and John A. Jr., of Dallas, and a daughter, Karen A. Gaucher of Darien, Conn.


        Rear Adm. Edwin Boardman Dexter, a retired executive director of the Pan American Coffee Bureau and the New York Coffee and Sugar Exchange, died on Oct. 19 at his retirement home in Seminole, Fla. A former resident of Manhattan, he was 88.
        He joined the bureau in 1953 and became executive director, representing the interests of 15 coffee-growing countries. From 1965 until his retirement in 1972, he was the executive director of the Coffee and Sugar Exchange.
        He is survived by a brother, William M., of Knoxville, Tenn.; and a sister, Louise D. Bell of St. Petersburg, Fla.

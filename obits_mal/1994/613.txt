
        Dr. Charles Botstein, the retired chief of radiotherapy at Montefiore Medical Center in the Bronx, died on Monday at his home in Riverdale. He was 83.
        The cause was kidney cancer, said his daughter, Dr. Eva Griepp.
        Dr. Botstein was director of the division of radiotherapy at Montefiore for more than 25 years. He also was a professor of radiology at the Albert Einstein College of Medicine. He retired in 1982.
        He was a pioneer in the use of radiotherapy in the treatment of uterine cancer. He was the author of more than 80 papers published in professional journals in the United States and abroad.
        He was born in Poland. He received a medical degree from the University of Zurich and immigrated to the United States in 1949.
        Dr. Botstein is survived by his wife, Dr. Anne Botstein of Riverdale; two sons, Dr. Leon Botstein of Annandale-on-Hudson, the president of Bard College, and Dr. David Botstein of Belmont, Calif., the chairman of the genetics department at Stanford University Medical Center, and his daughter, a cardiologist at Bellevue Hospital who lives in Manhattan.

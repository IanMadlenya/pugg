
        Edison Dick, a retired Chicago industrialist, died on Wednesday at his home in Lake Forest, Ill. He was 93.
        Mr. Dick was chairman of the executive board of the A. B. Dick Company of Niles, Ill. The company, founded by his father, Albert B. Dick, originally made mimeograph machines but kept updating its product lines over the decades. It is now a subsidiary of the General Electric Company and manufactures copying and electronic printing equipment, among other things.
        Mr. Dick was also a co-owner of several successful Chicago cafes and restaurants in the 1950's and 1960's, including the Cafe de Paris, Maison Lafite and Biggs Restaurant. The partners sold their holdings in 1969.
        Mr. Dick is survived by his wife, Jane Warner Dick; a daughter, Titia Ellis; a son, Edison W.; nine grandchildren, and five great-grandchildren.


        Stan Marx, a collector and seller of rare books, died on Wednesday at his home in Roslyn, L.I. He was 74.
        The cause was an aneurysm, his daughter, Rabbi Jo David, said.
        Mr. Marx and his son, Evan Marx, were the owners of BookMarx in Roslyn Heights, and the elder Mr. Marx was a founder of the Long Island Book Collectors Association. He also held seminars on book collecting at the Bryant Library in Roslyn.
        Mr. Marx was a founder and the first president of the Lewis Carroll Society of North America, and was the editor of a series of pamphlets on the works of Carroll. In 1981 Mr. Marx retired as a sales promotion manager of Zotos International, a cosmetics company in Darien, Conn.
        Mr. Marx is survived by his wife of 49 years, Diana Marx of Roslyn; a daughter, Rabbi David of Manhattan; a son, Evan Marx of Roslyn Heights, and a brother, Morty Marx of East Meadow, L.I.

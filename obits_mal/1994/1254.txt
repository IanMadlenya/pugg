
        Frederick Gordon Smith, a specialist in advanced computer applications, died on Tuesday at Holy Name Hospital in Teaneck, N.J. The cause of death was congestive heart failure, said his wife, Ann. A resident of Sneden's Landing, N.Y., he was 75.
        The son of missionary parents, Mr. Smith was born in Chefoo, China, and moved to the United States at age 10. He graduated from Swarthmore College in 1940 and began a computer marketing career that year at the International Business Machines Corporation.
        He remained at I.B.M. for 18 years, becoming an executive in its corporate advertising and public relations department. In 1958, he joined Sperry Rand International as director of marketing, with responsibilities in Europe and the United States. He became a vice president for marketing at Memorex and director of international marketing for Seagate Technology in Britain. He retired in 1990.
        Mr. Smith was the first United States delegate to the United Nations Computer Automation Conference, in 1978, and lectured at the Academy of Sciences of the U.S.S.R. He was also a guest lecturer at the Stanford Graduate School of Business in the 1980's. He was an author of the book "Marketing in Emerging Companies" in 1982.
        Besides his wife, Mr. Smith is survived by a son, Rick, of New York; a daughter, Sarah Kerr, of Dallas; a sister, Grace Lewis, of Sneden's Landing, and 14 grandchildren.

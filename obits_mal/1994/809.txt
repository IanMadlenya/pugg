
        Thomas Ryder Rudel, a leader in the machine-tool industry, died last Friday at St. Francis Hospital in Poughkeepsie, N.Y. He was 89 and lived in West Park, N.Y.
        The cause was a ruptured aneurysm, according to the hospital's death certificate.
        Born in Montreal, Mr. Rudel graduated from the Lawrenceville School in New Jersey and Princeton University. He worked for the Eberhard Faber Pencil Company from 1932 to 1952, serving as president and board chairman for the final three years. In 1938 he also joined Rudel Machinery Company of Montreal, founded by his father, and was its chairman from 1944 to 1960.
        He was founder, president and chairman of Rudel Machinery Company, Inc. of New York from 1941 to 1977 when he sold the business. He was chairman until 1985 of American S. I. P. Corporation, which he founded in 1960. He served as a member and director of the New York State Pension Fund.
        Mr. Rudel is survived by his second wife, Margaret Murchison Rudel; a daughter, Barbara R. Wendt of Key Largo, Fla.; a brother, John, of Long Boat Key, Fla.; a sister, Elizabeth Smith Gatov of Kentfield, Calif., and three grandchildren.

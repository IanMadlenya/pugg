
        Harvey Matofsky, a film producer and former publicity agent, died on Jan. 3 in Las Vegas, Nev. He was 60.
        The cause was heart failure, said his daughter Karen.
        Among the films Mr. Matofsky produced were "Billy" (1972), "Zandy's Bride" (1975), with Liv Ullmann and Gene Hackman, and the 1987 television movie "Poker Alice," which starred Elizabeth Taylor and George Hamilton.
        Mr. Matofsky was born in Brooklyn, N.Y., and was a graduate of New York University. Before becoming a producer, he was manager of publicity for United Artists and vice president of publicity for Embassy Pictures in Rome.
        In addition to his daughter Karen, he is survived by another daughter, Suzanne; his mother, Anna Matofsky, of Fort Lauderdale, Fla., and a brother, Alvin, also of Fort Lauderdale.

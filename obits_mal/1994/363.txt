
        Dr. Donald H. Swinney, a retired drama professor at Hofstra University, died on Thursday at Winthrop Hospital in Mineola, L.I. Dr. Swinney, who lived in Uniondale, L.I., was 75.
        The cause was a heart attack, said his son, David.
        Dr. Swinney was a member of Hofstra's drama department for over 40 years, during which he was also director of the John Cranford Adams Playhouse at Hofstra. He also served at various times as the designer, stage manager and general production manager of the Caramoor Festival of the Arts in Katonah, N.Y. He retired two years ago.
        Dr. Swinney is survived by his wife, Mary; a son, David, of San Diego, and two daughters, Barbara Lasher of Bargerville, Ind., and Suzannah Perosi of Hempstead, L.I.


        Brian Gormley, executive director of the Silvermine Guild Arts Center in New Canaan, Conn., died on Thursday at Norwalk Hospital in Connecticut. Mr. Gormley, who lived in Norwalk, was 49.
        The cause was cancer, said his brother, Bruce.
        Mr. Gormley headed the arts center for two years. He had previously been an executive of arts groups in Los Angeles, Valencia, Calif., and Lake Placid, N.Y. From 1973 to 1976 he was dean of students at the Visual Arts Center in Manhattan. He was a graduate of Boston College and received a master's degree in education from Fairfield University.
        Besides his brother, Mr. Gormley is survived by his wife, Marlitt, a daughter, Elona, a son, Keegan, all of Norwalk, and a sister, Maureen Bligh of Monroe, Conn.

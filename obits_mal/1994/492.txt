
        Dr. Arthur Berken, a physician who specialized in oncology, died on Saturday at his home in Huntington, L.I. He was 62.
        At his death he was a senior partner in the Mid-Island Internal Medical Consultants group. He had also been chief of staff and director of the department of medicine at Mid-Island Hospital in Bethpage, L.I.
        He had been clinical professor of medicine at the State University of New York at Stony Brook, L.I., since 1980.
        Dr. Berken, an advocate of health-care reform, wrote "America's Med-Life Crisis: Urgent Problem, Revolutionary Solutions," published by Quon in 1993.
        He graduated magna cum laude from the University of Miami in 1953. In 1957, he graduated from the Washington University School of Medicine in St. Louis.
        From 1958 to 1960 he was a captain and flight surgeon with the Air Force, stationed in Japan. He spent the following year at Tel Hasomer Hospital in Israel as a research fellow.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Roberta Menken, of Huntington; three daughters, Sharon of Cold Spring, N.Y.; Miriam and Betsy, both of Brooklyn; two brothers, Herbert, of Denver, and Gilbert, of Hollywood, Fla., and three grandchildren.

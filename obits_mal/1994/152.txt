
        B. W. Honeycutt, art director for Details magazine, died on Thursday at his home in Manhattan. He was 40.
        His family said he had been ill for a long time.
        Mr. Honeycutt began his magazine design career at GQ. He then worked for Vanity Fair and later became art director of Spy magazine. He taught editorial design at the School of Visual Arts in New York City.
        He received many awards for his art design, including recognition from the Society of Publication Designers, the American Institute of Graphic Arts and the Art Directors Club.
        He also wrote articles for How and Baseball magazines.
        Mr. Honeycutt was born in Franklinton, N.C. He attended the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill.
        He is survived by his mother, Miriam Gonella Honeycutt of Franklinton; a brother, John Thomas, of Wake Forest, N.C., and two nieces.

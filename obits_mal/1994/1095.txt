
        Karl Donch, a bass-baritone who sang at the Metropolitan Opera, died on Sept. 16 at a hospital in Vienna, where he lived. He was 80.
        He died after a long illness, his family said.
        Mr. Donch made his Metropolitan debut in 1959 as Beckmesser in "Die Meistersinger von Nurnberg," a role he sang in the house 45 times. He also appeared as the Witch in "Hansel and Gretel," Faninal in "Der Rosenkavalier" and the Doctor in "Wozzeck."
        Mr. Donch, who was born in Hagen, Germany, joined the Vienna State Opera in 1947. He was the general manager of the Vienne Volksoper from 1973 to 1987. He also sang at La Scala, the Paris Opera, Deutsche Oper Berlin and with other companies around the world.
        He is survived by his wife, Sonja; two sons, five daughters, a brother and a sister.

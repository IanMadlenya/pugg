
        Richard J. Walsh, a television producer, died on Tuesday at Brandywine Nursing Home in Briarcliff, N.Y. He was 68.
        Mr. Walsh served as a cryptographer in the Army Air Force in World War II and graduated from Fordham University in 1949. After working for the Young & Rubicam advertising agency, he joined the National Council of Catholic Men in 1953 and eventually became director of its film, television and radio division.
        He produced a wide range of programming on contemporary Catholicism for the national networks, including "The Catholic Hour," "Directions" and "Look Up and Live".
        Mr. Walsh is survived by his wife, Mary Farrell Walsh; five sons, Kevin and Chris of Irvington, N.Y., Richard of Tarrytown, N.Y., John of Dobbs Ferry, N.Y., and Mark of San Jose, Calif.; a daughter, Mary of New York; two brothers, Thomas and John; two sisters, Anne and Evelyn, and two granddaughters.


        Edward J. Donovan Jr., a publisher and public relations agent in New York for more than 25 years, died on Friday at a friend's apartment in Manhattan. He was 65.
        The cause was cancer, said his son, Edward J. Donovan 3d.
        From 1984 to 1992 Mr. Donovan was assistant director of public relations at Metro-North Commuter Railroad. He had previously been in public relations with the Sperry & Hutchinson Company in New York, and from 1973 to 1981 was the editor and publisher of the weekly newspaper Manhattan East.
        He graduated from Columbia College in 1950.
        Mr. Donovan is survived by his son, of Manhattan, and a daughter, Theresa Pope of Roseville, Calif.

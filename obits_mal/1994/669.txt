
        Norman Brad Blumenthal, a member of the review board of the Federal Communications Commission, died on Wednesday at Suburban Hospital in Bethesda, Md. He was 51 and lived in Chevy Chase, Md.
        The cause was a heart attack, his family said.
        Mr. Blumenthal was born and reared in Philadelphia. He was a graduate of New York University and the Dickinson School of Law in Carlisle, Pa.
        From 1968 to 1972 he was a member of the office of the general counsel of the F.C.C., and from 1972 to 1977 he was the legal assistant to agency's Commissioner, Benjamin L. Hooks. Mr. Blumenthal was an associate general counsel of the F.C.C. from 1977 to 1981 and since then had been a review board member.
        He is survived by his wife, Sara Kister Blumenthal; two daughters, Gery, of Chevy Chase, and Amanda, of Philadelphia; his father, Samuel Blumenthal of Boca Raton, Fla., and two brothers, Harold, of Boca Raton, and Mitchell, of East Hills, L.I.


        Winston May, who acted in Off-Broadway productions, television commercials and films in a 35-year career, died on April 29 at the New York University Medical Center. He was 57 and lived in Manhattan.
        The cause was AIDS, said his manager, Roxy Horen.
        Mr. May was an original member of the Roundabout Theater company in 1967. He played leading roles in its productions of "Candida," "Uncle Vanya," "King Lear," "The Play's the Thing" and "Trumpets and Drums." He last appeared with the Roundabout Theater Conservatory's Ensemble Company in "Fanny's First Play" in the 1990-91 season. He also appeared in supporting roles in daytime dramas and films, and he appeared in more than 300 commercials and industrial films.
        He is survived by a brother, Randy, of Mammoth Springs, Ark.

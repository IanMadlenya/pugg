
        Albert L. Kraus, editor emeritus of The Journal of Commerce and a former editor and financial reporter at The New York Times, died yesterday at his home  in Westfield, N.J., his wife, Patricia, said.
        Mr. Kraus, 75, suffered from Parkinson's disease.
        Mr. Kraus joined The Times in 1956 as a banking reporter. As assistant financial editor, he edited the Sunday financial section. For a time, he wrote a weekly economics column.
        In 1972, Mr. Kraus left The Times to become editor of all publications of the Bond Buyer Inc., including The Daily Bond Buyer, The Weekly Bond Buyer and The Money Manager.
        Mr. Kraus joined The Journal of Commerce in 1978 as chief editorial writer. He was named editor in 1981 and retired in 1987.
        In addition to his wife, Mr. Kraus is survived by three daughters, Carla Kraus Reichek of Berkeley, Calif., Linda P. Kraus of Lakewood, N.J., and Laura Kraus Bento of Assonet, Mass.; one son, Peter A. Kraus of Chicago, and a granddaughter.

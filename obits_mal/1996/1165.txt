
        Michael K. McCutcheon, executive director of labor relations at The New York Times, died yesterday at his parents' home in Manhattan. Mr. McCutcheon, who was 40, committed suicide, the police said.
        Mr. McCutcheon, who lived in Manhattan, joined The Times in April 1990 as confidential assistant director of labor relations. He became director of labor relations the following November and executive director in 1994. Before joining The Times, he held various posts at the Metropolitan Transportation Authority.
        Mr. McCutcheon is survived by his parents, William and Virginia; his wife, Collette; a son, Michael; a daughter, Loren; a brother, William, of Riverdale in the Bronx, and two sisters, Jeanne McAlary and Carolyn Phillips, both of Spring Lake, N.J.

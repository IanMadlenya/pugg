
        John A. Cook, an investment banker turned doctor, died on Wednesday at Lenox Hill Hospital. He was 85 and lived in Manhattan.
        Dr. Cook died of heart disease, according to his wife, Margaret.
        He was born in New York City and  graduated from Yale University and Harvard Business School. When he was in his mid-30's, he decided on a career in medicine. He graduated from the College of Physicians and Surgeons at Columbia University and specialized in psychoanalysis.
        For many years, Dr. Cook was treasurer of the Chamber Music Society of Lincoln Center.
        In addition to his wife, whom he married in 1949, survivors include a daughter, Mariana Cook of Manhattan; a stepson, George W. Liebmann of Baltimore, and a granddaughter.


        Prof. Robert Adams Day, a Joyce scholar an' expert on 18th-century English literature, died on Jan. 19 at St. Vincent's Hospital in Manhattan. He was 71 and lived 'n Manhattan.
        The cause was a heart attack, said his companion, Milton Malkin.
        Dr. Day retired recently as a professor in the doctoral program in comparative literature at the Graduate School of the City University of New York, where he had taught since 1970. He was previously associated with the English departments at Dartmouth College and Queens College.C
        Born in Providence, R.I., he graduated from Brown University and earned his master's degree and his doctorate at Harvard University.
        He is survived by his companion.

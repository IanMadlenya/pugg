
        William Larsen, a veteran character actor on Broadway, Off Broadway and in regional theater, died on Jan. 21 at his home in Friendswood, Tex. He was 68.
        The cause was a heart attack, said his wife, June.
        In the 1960's, Mr. Larsen was a founding member of the APA repertory in New  York, and in the 1980's he was a member of the Dallas Theater Center's acting company. He acted in Broadway musicals and in Shakespeare. At the American Shakespeare Theater in Stratford, Conn., he portrayed Gloucester to Morris Carnovsky's King Lear. He was also in the original Off Broadway cast of "The Fantasticks." In Texas, he was known for playing the Sheriff in "The Best Little Whorehouse in Texas."
        He was born in Lake Charles, La., grew up in Houston and graduated from the University of Texas. At the start of his career, he was a resident actor at Nina Vance's Alley Theater in Houston. His Broadway appearances included "Half a Sixpence," "Funny Girl" and the 1974 revival of "Cat on a Hot Tin Roof," in which he succeeded Fred Gwynne as Big Daddy.
        In addition to his wife, he is survived by a brother, Richard, of Katy, Tex., and his stepfather, A. Mabry Jones of Nachitoches, La.

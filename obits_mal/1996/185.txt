
        William Hogan, literary editor of The San Francisco Chronicle for 27 years, died on Wednesday at Kaiser Hospital in San Rafael, Calif. He was 82.
        Among the writers whose talents Mr. Hogan recognized and encouraged early in  their careers were Kurt Vonnegut Jr., Alex Haley, William Saroyan, Robinson Jeffers, Jessamyn West, Wallace Stegner and Herbert Gold.
        Mr. Hogan was born in Piedmont, Calif., and attended the University of California at Berkeley. After working for The Oakland Tribune and the International News Service, he joined The Chronicle in 1938 and remained with the paper until his retirement in 1982.
        Mr. Hogan was a co-author of the original screen version of "Up Front" by the cartoonist Bill Mauldin, with whom he had served in Italy during World War II. In addition to daily and Sunday book reviews, he also wrote and edited film criticism for The Chronicle.
        He is survived by his wife, Phyllis, of Mill Valley, Calif.; a son, Dennis, of Laytonville, Calif.; two daughters, Michele Liapes, of San Francisco, and Mary Ann, of Washington, and three grandchildren.

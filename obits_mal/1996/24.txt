
        David Weadon, an organist, conductor and teacher who specialized in liturgical music, died on Saturday at the Robert Wood Johnson Hospital in New Brunswick, N.J. He was 39 and had homes in Manhattan and Freehold, N.J.
        Carol Belles, his secretary, said the cause was respiratory failure.
        Mr. Weadon, who was organist and director of music at the Princeton Theological Seminary, performed frequently in Manhattan, both as a guest organist at Riverside Church and as organist and choirmaster at the Brick Church from 1987 to 1993. He was university organist and assistant director of the glee club and chapel choir at Princeton University from 1983 to 1987.
        He was born in Burlington, N.C., and studied music at the Westminster Choir College and Drew University. During his student years, he was assistant organist at the Fifth Avenue Presbyterian Church, and from 1980 to 1982 he was associate  chapel organist and assistant director of choral activities at Duke University.
        He was also on the editorial board of Yale University Press and wrote articles for the Dictionary of Religious Education, the Princeton Seminary Bulletin and American Organist.
        He is survived by his companion, David MacPeek; his father, Bernard Weadon, of Chapel Hill, N.C., and a brother, Mark, of Cary, N.C.

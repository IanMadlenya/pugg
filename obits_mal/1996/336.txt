
        Dr. Howard L. Steinbach, a San Francisco radiologist and educator, died last  Monday at the Jewish Home for the Aged in San Francisco. He was 77 and lived in  suburban Hillsborough.
        He had suffered a long illness, his family said.
        Dr. Steinbach was a retired professor of radiology, medicine and pediatrics  at the University of California at San Francisco School of Medicine. He also was a clinical professor emeritus of radiology at the Stanford University Medical Center and, from 1969 to 1983, chief of radiology at French Hospital in San Francisco.
        Dr. Steinbach, who was born in Pittsburgh, graduated from the University of California at Los Angeles and the St. Louis University Medical School. He trained in radiology at the University of California at San Francisco, where he  taught for 35 years.
        He is survived by his wife of 45 years, Ilse Rosengarten Steinbach; three daughters, Dr. Lynne Steinbach of Tiburon, Calif., Lisa Martin of Lake Oswego, Ore., and Caryn Steinbach of Redwood City, Calif.; a brother, Dr. Owen Steinbach of Fresno, Calif.; a sister, Phyllis Stone of Los Angeles, and a grandson.


        Joseph Mindell, an early proponent of using monetary indicators like interest rates and the money supply to make stock market decisions, died on Feb. 4 at St. Vincent's Hospital in New York. Mr. Mindell, a resident of Manhattan, was 86.
        Mr. Mindell, author of a 1948 textbook, "The Stock Market, Basic Guide for Investors," worked at Lawrence, O'Donnell, Marcus & Company for 59 years.
        He joined Marcus & Company, one of several specialist firms responsible for  trading specific stocks on the floor of the New York Stock Exchange, as a securities analyst in 1937. He became a general partner in 1939 and a partner when the company merged with Lawrence, O'Donnell in 1988. Mr. Mindell, who was also a member of the firm's management committee, continued working through the  end of January.
        Before joining Marcus & Company, Mr. Mindell was an analyst at the Argus Research Corporation for two years. He graduated from New York University in 1930 with a B.A. in philosophy. He studied for two years at the Columbia Law School and did graduate work at the School of Business at Columbia University.
        He is survived by his wife, Geraldine, of Manhattan.

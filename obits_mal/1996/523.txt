
        Robert Alexander (Bones) Hamilton, a standout running back at Stanford from 1933-35, when the university won 25 of 31 games and made three straight Rose Bowl appearances, died at his home in Palm Springs on Monday, Stanford announced today. He was 83.
        Hamilton, played defense and returned kicks as well as running with the ball, but it was his blocking ability that earned him the nickname Bones. He was named captain of the Stanford team as a senior in 1935 and was elected to the National Football Foundation College Hall of Fame in 1972.
        He is survived by his only son, Robert Hamilton Jr.

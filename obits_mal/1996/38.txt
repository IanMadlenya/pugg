
        Dr. Harold Edward MacMahon, emeritus professor of pathology at Tufts University, died on Jan. 13 at Emerson Hospital in Concord, Mass. He was 95 and  lived in Bedford, Mass.
        The cause was congestive heart failure, his family said.
        Dr. MacMahon joined the faculty at Tufts in 1930 and served as director of the pathology department of its School of Medicine and School of Dental Medicine for four decades, retiring in 1971. The author of some 100 scientific papers, he continued research on the pathology of the heart, lungs, liver, kidneys and blood vessels until 1987.
        Born in Aylmer, Ontario, he studied bacteriology and pathology and received his medical degree from the University of Western Ontario. He completed his training at Montreal General Hospital, Boston City Hospital and the University of Hamburg before accepting an appointment at Tufts.
        Dr. MacMahon is survived by a daughter, Elizabeth Jochnick of West Hartford,  Conn.; three sons, Hugh, of Falmouth, Me., D'Arcy, of Lincoln, Mass., and James, of Palo Alto, Calif.; a brother, M. D'Arcy, of Toronto; 12 grandchildren and two great-grandsons.

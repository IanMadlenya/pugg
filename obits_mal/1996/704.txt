
        Walter W. Morrison, a former speechwriter and policy adviser at the National  Association for the Advancement of Colored People, died on May 17 at Good Samaritan Hospital in Baltimore.
        A resident of Baltimore, he was 59.
        The cause was complications of diabetes and kidney failure.
        Mr. Morrison framed many of the civil rights organization's responses to Reagan and Bush Administration policies between 1986 and 1992, when he served as a top aide to the group's executive director, Benjamin Hooks.
        After Robert Bork was nominated for the Supreme Court, Mr. Morrison wrote a speech given by Mr. Hooks, which included a widely quoted vow to oppose the appointment: "We'll fight until hell freezes over, and then skate over the top."
        He urged the organization to reject the notion of black separatism and to keep its distance from Louis Farrakhan, leader of the Nation of Islam.
        Mr. Morrison was a former member of the editorial board at The Milwaukee Journal and a reporter in Chicago and Dayton, Ohio.
        The son of a janitor and homemaker, he was born in Bluefield, W.Va. Hegraduated from Miami University in Oxford, Ohio, in 1960.
        He is survived by a brother, James, of North Bethesda, Md., and his mother, Winnie, of Takoma, Md.

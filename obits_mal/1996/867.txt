
        Harold J. Ruvoldt Sr., a lawyer who practiced in Jersey City for more than 50 years, died on Monday at his home in Deal, N.J. He was 85.
        Mr. Ruvoldt was born in Jersey City and graduated in 1940 from John Marshall  College of Law, now part of Seton Hall University.
        He founded what became the firm of Ruvoldt & Ruvoldt, which was merged last  year into the Manhattan firm of Fischbein Badillo Wagner Harding.
        He was a former assistant Hudson County prosecutor and assistant county counsel. A founder of the Hudson County Bar Foundation, he supported its scholarship fund for law students, along with scholarship funds in other civic organizations.
        For many years, he headed the advisory board of St. Ann's Home for the Aged in Jersey City.
        Mr. Ruvoldt is survived by his wife, Margaret Darcy Ruvoldt; a daughter, Arlene Chiapetta of Hackettstown, N.J.; a son, Harold J. Jr. of Interlaken, N.J., the former Hudson County Prosecutor; four grandchildren, and two great-grandchildren.


        Vaclav Nelhybel, a composer born in Czechoslovakia who was a musical director of Radio Free Europe during the 1950's and composer in residence at the University of Scranton, died on Friday at a hospital here. He was 76 and lived in Fleetville, Pa.
        A university news release said the cause of death was not yet determined.
        Mr. Nelhybel, a native of Polanka, Czechoslovakia, was a co-founder of the World Premiere Composition Series at the university in 1984. He studied at Prague University, the University of Fribourg in Switzerland and the Prague Conservatory and began his career in 1939 as a conductor at Radio Prague and at the City Theater of Prague. After the Second World War, he was named conductor and composer in residence at Swiss Radio and a lecturer at the University of Fribourg.
        From 1950 to 1957, he was the musical director at Radio Free Europe in Munich, West Germany.
        Mr. Nelhybel came to the United States in 1957 and became a citizen in 1962.  He published more than 400 works, especially for wind instruments, including operas and orchestral works, and he conducted widely.
        His survivors include his wife of 39 years, Dorothea, a native of Frankfurt,  Germany; a daughter, Jana, of Washington, and two sons, Christopher, of White Haven, Pa., and Pavel, of Prague.

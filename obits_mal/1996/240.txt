
        Manolo Fabregas, an actor, producer and director who was a popular figure in  theater, films and television in Mexico, died on Sunday at his home here. He was 75.
        The cause was a heart attack, his family said.
        A third-ganeration member of a well-known theatrical family, Mr. Fabregas appeared in more than 60 films, including "Aventurera," "Arrabalera" and "La Mujer Legitima." He began his career in 1948 by founding his own acting company.
        Mr. Fabregas, whose original name was Manuel Sanchez Navarro, adopted the surname of his grandmother, Virginia Fabregas, a famous actress in Mexico and Spain.


        Henry G. (Hank) Walker, a former staff photographer for Life magazine, died on Oct. 13 in Miami. He was 75. The cause was heart failure, according to a statement from Life.
        Mr. Walker started at Life in 1948 as a contract photographer and joined the staff three years later. In 1951, he was assigned to Washington as a White House photographer. One of his photographs shows the silhouetted figures of John F. Kennedy and his brother Robert as they conferred in a hotel room on the eve of the 1960 Democratic convention.
        He is survived by his wife, Alma; a son, Henry G. Walker 3d of Hawaii; two daughters, Cynthia Walker Ziegler of Miami and Catherine Walker Eure of California, and four grandchildren.

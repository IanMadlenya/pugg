
        Joseph F. Miller, a lawyer, wartime Government counsel and former president of the National Electrical Manufacturing Association, died on Monday at his home in Deal, N.J. He was 89.
        Mr. Miller was born in Manhattan and graduated from Harvard Law School. In World War II, he was enlisted by the Reconstruction Finance Corporation as a lawyer for its Defense Plant Corporation.
        Mr. Miller is survived by his wife of 66 years, Marjorie Kohn Miller; two sons, Kenneth, of San Diego, and Keith, of Long Branch, N.J.; a daughter, Katharine S. Burger of Manhattan, and seven grandchildren.


        George B. Oliver, professor emeritus of dramatic literature at Nassau Community College and a former actor on Broadway, died on Jan. 14 at North Penn  Hospital in Lansdale, Pa. He was 76 and lived in Willow Grove, Pa.
        Mr. Oliver attended West Virginia State College on a tennis scholarship. While there, he participated in a Government-sponsored flight training program and was among the first black students to receive a pilot's license. He joined the Air Force and became one of the Tuskegee Airmen, the first squadron of black fighter pilots in World War II.
        After the war he moved to New York City to pursue an acting career. He auditioned for the 1945 Broadway production of Lillian Smith's "Strange Fruit,"  directed by Jose Ferrer, and became a member of the cast. The following year he  appeared on Broadway with Mr. Ferrer in "Cyrano de Bergerac."
        Mr. Oliver soon gave up acting for academe, earning a master's degree in theater arts at New York University and a Ph.D. in dramatic literature at Pennsylvania State University. He taught at several schools in the State University of New York system before settling at Nassau Community College, where he taught for more than 20 years.
        He is survived by his wife, Marjorie, and a daughter, Denise, of Astoria, Queens.

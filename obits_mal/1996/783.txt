
        R. Thornton Wilson Jr., a former Manhattan restaurateur and patron of the arts, died on Wednesday at his home in Newport, R.I. He was 73.
        The cause was congestive heart failure, his family said.
        Mr. Wilson, a descendant of John Jacob Astor, was born in Manhattan. He was an owner of the Ambassador Hotel and, later, of the Ermitage Restaurant, both in Manhattan.
        He was a benefactor of the Metropolitan Museum of Art and a trustee of the Browning School, the Juilliard School, Manhattan School of Music and the Seaman's Church Institute.
        Mr. Wilson's first wife, Carmel Snow Wilson, died in 1983. He is survived by  his wife, Sonia Lowis Wilson; a daughter, Carmel W. Fromson of Manhattan; a son, Richard T. of Portsmouth, R.I.; and a grandson.

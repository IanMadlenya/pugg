
        Jan Behr, who conducted nearly 150 performances at the Metropolitan Opera, died on Nov. 21 at his home in Lakewood, N.J. He was 85, and also had a home in Manhattan.
        Mr. Behr joined the Metropolitan Opera in 1952 as assistant conductor and was made associate conductor in 1964. He conducted performances of ''La Boheme,'' and ''La Traviata,'' among other works.
        He was born in Prague and first studied music with his father, a singer and teacher. He then studied law at the University of Prague, where he also enrolled in classes for piano and conducting being given by George Szell. After graduation, he served as Szell's assistant at the German Opera House in Prague.
        He survived imprisonment in several concentration camps during World War II.
        Before coming to the United States, he was guest conductor with the Orchestre National de Belgique, in Brussels.
        He is survived by his wife, Elizabeth, and a daughter, Evelyn, of Manhattan.

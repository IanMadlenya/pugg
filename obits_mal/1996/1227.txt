
        Edward Stewart, a novelist, died on Oct. 12 at St. Vincent's Hospital in Manhattan. He was 58 and lived in Manhattan. The cause was a liver disorder, said Lois Wallace, his friend and agent.
        Mr. Stewart was the author of 12 published novels. His first, ''Orpheus on Top'' (1966) concerned the picaresque adventures of a young university graduate whose life is a series of comic disasters. His other books include ''They've Shot the President's Daughter'' (1973), a political thriller; ''Ballerina'' (1979), a love story set in the world of dance, and ''Privileged Lives'' (1988), a murder mystery. His last book, ''Jury Double,'' a crime novel, is to be published in January by Dell.
        Mr. Stewart was a graduate of Exeter and Harvard University and studied music in Paris with Nadia Boulanger.
        He is survived by his parents, Helen Tucker of Butte, Mont., and Edward S. Stewart of Garden City, N.Y.; two sisters, Francesca Cohn of Garden City and Helen Burke of Aurora, Colo.; a brother, Tony, of New York City, and his companion, Gary Combs of Manhattan.

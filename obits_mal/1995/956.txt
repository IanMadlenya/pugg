
        Lawrence Berk, a composer who founded the Berklee College of Music here, died on Friday. He was 87.
        The college had three students when it opened in 1945. Now, with 2,700 students from 70 countries, it is one of the world's most famous jazz schools.
        Mr. Berk was born in Boston and began working in nightclubs as a professional musician at 13. He was later a staff arranger at NBC and CBS studios in New York.


        Harold L. Schiff, a lawyer whose clients included many prominent show-business figures, died on Sunday at his home in Pound Ridge, N.Y. He was 76.
        Mr. Schiff was born in New York City and was a graduate of Columbia University and Columbia Law School. A senior partner in the firm of Gottlieb, Schiff, Bergman & Sendroff, he represented such stars as Rex Harrison, Bette Davis, Claudette Colbert, Jim Dale and Glynis Johns.
        In the mid-1950's, with his partner Donald Wolin, he operated the Theater by the Sea, a summer playhouse in Matunuck, R.I. Among those who performed there were Tallulah Bankhead, Beatrice Lillie and Groucho Marx. He was also one of the producers of the 1979 Off Broadway play "The Brass Ring."
        No immediate family members survive.

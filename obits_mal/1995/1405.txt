
        Diego R. M. Del Vayo, a stockbroker in New York City, died on Monday at Lenox Hill Hospital. He was 65 and lived in Manhattan.
        The cause was complications from diabetes, according to his physician, Dr. Robert Abrams.
        Mr. Del Vayo was associated with the Manhattan brokerage house Ladenberg Thalmann & Company Inc. He served with the Army Corps of Engineers in France in the early 1950's.
        He was born in Madrid and came to New York at age 9 when his family was expelled by the Franco regime after the Spanish Civil War. He graduated from Hamilton College, and the Sorbonne.
        Mr. Del Vayo is survived by a brother, Juan Alvarez Del Vayo of Munich.

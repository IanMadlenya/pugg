
        Laurence R. Goodyear, a Buffalo lawyer who was active in civic affairs, died last Thursday at his home in Buffalo. He was 82.
        He suffered a short illness, his family said.
        Mr. Goodyear, a graduate of Harvard Law School, was a founding partner in the firm of Hodgson, Russ, Andrews, Woods & Goodyear, specializing in labor law and, later, in trusts and estates. He retired in 1981 and remained of counsel to the firm.
        He was a past chairman of WNED-TV, the Buffalo public broadcasting station, and served on the boards of Buffalo Children's Hospital and the University of Buffalo Foundation, among others.
        He is survived by his wife, Judy Benjamin Goodyear; two sons, Laurence Jr., of Bethesda, Md., and Daniel M., of Robesonia, Pa.; a daughter, Wendy G. Griswold of Glyndon, Md.; a brother, Austin, of Brooklin, Me; 12 grandchildren, two great-grandchildren and three stepchildren. His first wife, Ruth Millett Goodyear, died in 1976.


        Walter Burges Smith 2d, a retired Foreign Service officer who specialized in Eastern European and Middle East affairs, died on March 1 at Sibley Memorial Hospital in Washington. He was 65 and lived in Chevy Chase, Md.
        The cause was lung cancer, his family said.
        As a member of the Foreign Service from 1958 to 1987, Mr. Smith's postings included Moscow, Frankfurt, Warsaw, East Berlin and Tel Aviv. He also served in Washington as senior fellow in the Defense Department's Strategic Concepts Development Center and as deputy director of the Center for the Study of Foreign Affairs at the Foreign Service Institute.
        At his death, he was a legislative aide in the Washington law firm of Cleary, Gottlieb, Steen & Hamilton.
        Mr. Smith is survived by his wife, Nancy Clark Smith; three daughters, Lydia W. S. Smith of Chevy Chase, Katrina V. DeRamus of Templeton, Calif., and Marian G. P. Smith of Washington; two sons, Walter S. B. and Matthew W. H., both of St. Helena, Calif.; a sister, Prudence C. S. Peck of Matunuck, R.I.; and two grandchildren. A previous marriage, to Marian S. Achilles, ended in divorce.

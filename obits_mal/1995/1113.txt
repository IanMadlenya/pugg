
        Albert Hessberg 2d, a prominent Albany lawyer who served on many civic boards, died on Tuesday at the Albany Medical Center. He was 78 and a lifelong resident of Albany.
        The cause was cancer, said his daughter, Caroline Smedvig of Boston.
        A retired senior partner in the firm of Hiscock & Barclay, where he specialized in trusts and estate planning, he served as a referee with the New York State Commission on Judicial Conduct and was president of the Albany County Bar Association.
        Mr. Hessberg had been a member of the board of the Albany Medical Center since 1967 and was its chairman from 1985 to 1988. He was also a member of the board of the College of St. Rose. He was a member of the Albany Institute of History and Art.
        A Yale University graduate, he received a law degree in 1941 from Yale Law School.
        His wife of 46 years, Elisabeth Fitzsimons Goold, died in 1991. Besides his daughter, he is survived by two sons, Albert 3d, of Albany and Philip, of Atlanta, and three grandchildren.

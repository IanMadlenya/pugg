
        Stanley B. Thomas Jr., president of the Sega Channel of video games, died on April 15 at his home in Tenafly, N.J. He was 52.
        The cause was non-Hodgkin's lymphoma, his family said.
        Mr. Thomas was named to head and organize the Sega Channel in 1993 when it was launched as a joint venture of Time Warner Entertainment, Sega of America Inc., and Telecommunications Inc. The channel began its programming in December and is now carried by some 100 cable systems nationwide.
        Before heading Sega, he was a senior vice president at Time Warner. Early in his career he worked for I.T.T. and Home Box Office. From 1969 to 1976 he was an executive with the human development services division of the Department of Health, Education and Welfare.
        Mr. Thomas is survived by his wife, the former Lydia Lipincott Biddle, and four children, Kimberly, Kelly, Elizabeth and Stanley 3d.

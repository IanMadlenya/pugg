
        Solomon Seidman, a prominent official in tournament bridge, died on March 5 at St. Barnabas Hospital in Livingston, N.J. He was 85.
        The cause of death was pneumonia, his sister, Gertrude White of Springfield, N.J., said.
        Mr. Seidman represented New York City on the board of the American Contract Bridge League from 1972 to 1984. In that period, he was the chairman of the appeals committee at all national championships, arbitrating problems of law and ethics. He was vice chairman of the Appeals Committee at the world championships in Biarritz, France, in 1982.
        On his retirement in 1984, he was named Honorary Member of the American Contract Bridge League.
        Mr. Seidman was closely associated with the Greater New York Bridge Association from 1952, holding every office, including president. As a player, he won many major titles in the New York area, including the Reisinger Knockout Teams, the Goldman Open Pairs and the Von Zedtwitz Double Knockout.
        Mr. Seidman, who was born in Brooklyn, graduated from New York University in 1932 and received a master's degree from N.Y.U. He taught economics and American history.
        In addition to Mrs. White, he is survived by two sisters, Pat Kimmel of Plantation, Fla., and Mary Gellar of Springfield, Mass.


        William M. Perry Jr., who headed several fashion companies during a career of more than 40 years, died last Saturday at Valley Hospital in Ridgewood, N.J. He was 69 and lived in Ridgewood.
        The cause was an aneurysm, said a son, Timothy W. Perry of Ridgewood.
        At his retirement in 1989, Mr. Perry was chairman and chief executive of Booth Bay Ltd., now known as Aris Industries. Earlier, he was president of Marlene Industries. He also founded and was president of the apparel firm Cos Cob.
        Born in Manhattan, he graduated from University of Notre Dame.
        Besides his son Timothy, he is survived by his wife, Neilla; two other sons, William M. Perry 3d of Seneca, S.C., and C. Christopher Perry of Summit, N.J.; a brother, Todd, of Frankfurt; a sister, Katherine Perry of Manhattan, and eight grandchildren.

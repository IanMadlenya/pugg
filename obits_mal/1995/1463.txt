
        Arthur David Schulte, a retired New York investment banker and philanthropist, died last Wednesday at the Hospice Center in Palm Beach, Fla. He was 88 and had homes in Manalapan, Fla., Greenwich, Conn., and Manhattan.
        The cause was congestive heart failure, his family said.
        Mr. Schulte was a trustee of the Federation of Jewish Philanthropies and the Hospital for Special Surgery. He was also a past board member of the Hudson Institute, a research institute in Indianapolis.
        A native of Manhattan, he graduated from Phillips Andover Academy and Yale University.
        He was a former president of D. A. Schulte Inc. and the Park & Tilford Distillers Corporation, a large distilling and importing house, and a general partner at the Lehman Brothers investment firm until 1969. Over the years, he was a director of Schenley Industries and Helena Rubinstein Inc., among other corporations, as well as a board member of International Investors Inc.
        His first wife, Luise Meyer Schulte, died in 1954.
        He is survived by his wife of nearly 40 years, Vivian Sternfield Schulte; a daughter, Patricia Levinson, a son, Anthony, and a brother, David, all of Manhattan, five grandchildren and three great-grandchildren.

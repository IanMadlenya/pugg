
        Frank Massi, a former president and chief executive of the Hearst Corporation, died on Monday at his home in Garden City, L.I. He was 85.  He also had a home in Boca Raton, Fla. At his death, he was a director of Hearst.
        Mr. Massi joined Hearst in 1934, when the company bought The Pictorial Review, a magazine for which he was chief accountant. In 1953, Mr. Massi became an assistant treasurer and a director. He was elected executive vice president in 1968 and named president and chief executive in 1973. He retired from those posts in 1975, but continued as a director.
        Mr. Massi was also a director of the Hearst Foundations and a trustee of the trust established under the will of William Randolph Hearst, the founder of the company. In addition, Mr. Massi was director of Southwest Forest Industries, the Charter Indemnity Company and the Newspaper Advertising Bureau. He was on the board of the Manufacturers Hanover Trust Company.
        Mr. Massi was born in New York City. He attended New York University and City College. He began his career in 1927 as an accountant at McGraw-Hill Publishing.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Madeline D'Angelo; two daughters, Lucille Cazzetto of Hempstead, L.I., and Martha Polemeni of Montclair, N.J., 11 grandchildren and eight great-grandchildren.

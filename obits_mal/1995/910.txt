
        Csaba Elthes, who served five times as coach of the United States Olympic fencing team, died of a stroke on last Wednesday. He was 83.
        His death was announced on Monday by Jeno Kamuti, an Olympic silver medalist  in 1968.
        Mr. Elthes, an international fencing champion as well as a lawyer, left Hungary during the 1956 uprising. After coming to the United States, he became a coach and fencing master at the New York Athletic Club and the New York Fencers  Club in 1959. He served as coach of the United States Olympic teams in 1964, 1968, 1972, 1976 and 1984.
        Mr. Elthes is survived by his wife, Maria, and two daughters, Ezther and Adrienne.


        Jean Bony, a French-born art historian who taught at the University of California, Berkeley, from 1962 to 1980, died on July 7 in Australia. He was 86.
        His death was reported on Saturday in Le Monde.
        Mr. Bony's books included "The Resistance at Chartres" (1958), "The English Decorated Style" (1979) and "French Gothic Architecture of the 12th and 13th Centuries" (1983).
        Mr. Bony was born in Le Mans, France, and studied at the Sorbonne and at Cambridge University. He also taught at Eton, Cambridge and Southern Methodist University before joining the faculty at Berkeley.
        He was married twice and had one daughter.

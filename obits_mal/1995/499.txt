
        Harold Lamberg, a philanthropist and retired manufacturer, died on Friday at Mount Sinai Hospital. A resident of the Upper East Side of Manhattan, he was 87.
        At his death he was the president of the Anne & Harry J. Reicher Foundation, a charity that provides grants for health-care and human-service institutions in the New York metropolitan area. Among his personal beneficiaries were the Hospital for Joint Diseases' Orthopedic Institute and the hospital's research and development foundation.
        Mr. Lamberg was born in Brooklyn and was a business graduate of the University of Pennsylvania. He retired in 1983 as president of the White Metal Rolling and Stamping Corporation of Greenpoint, Brooklyn, started 60 years earlier by his father, Alfred, an emigrant from Russia.
        He is survived by his wife of 59 years, Pearl Rosenberg Lamberg; two daughters, Ann Zeff of Cincinnati and Carol Lamberg of Manhattan; a sister, Ethel Rosow of Manhattan; four grandchildren, and two great-granddaughters.

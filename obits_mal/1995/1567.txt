
        Benjamin Zdatny, a food industry executive and philanthropist, died on Friday at his home in Huntington, L.I. He was 68 and also had a home in Boca Raton, Fla.
        He had suffered from heart disease for some time, said his son, Ian Zdatny.
        Until the sale of the billion-dollar conglomerate, the Di Giorgio Corporation, in 1989, Mr. Zdatny was vice chairman and chief operating officer. He had been with the corporation since 1965, starting out as general manager of the White Rose food division. He was president of White Rose from 1974 to 1985.
        Long active as a philanthropist, he worked with the American Cancer Society, the Brookdale Hospital Center in Brooklyn, where he served on the board of trustees for nearly 25 years, and the United Jewish Appeal.
        Besides his son, Ian, who lives in Bayside, Queens, he is survived by his wife of 48 years, Rita; a daughter, Meryll, of Rome; two brothers, Jay, of Boynton Beach, Fla., and Harold, of Toms River, N.J., and a grandson.

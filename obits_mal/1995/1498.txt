
        Dr. Carlo E. Grossi, a former director of surgery at La Guardia Hospital in Queens and a professor of surgery at New York Medical College, died on March 8 at New York University Medical Center. He was 67 and lived in Manhattan.
        The cause was cancer, said his wife, Dr. Margaret T. Grossi.
        An attending surgeon at St. Vincent's Hospital and Medical Center of New York in Manhattan, he also was an associate professor of clinical surgery at New York University.
        Dr. Grossi was the author or co-author of more than 150 journal articles on various aspects of cancer, including blood clotting, surgical procedures and the training of physicians.
        He had been a Governor of the American College of Surgeons since 1990. He also was a past president of the New York Surgical Society, the New York Cancer Society and the New York State Cancer Program Associations.
        He was born in Mount Vernon, N.Y., and he received an undergraduate degree from Columbia College in 1945 and a medical degree from Long Island College of Medicine in 1949.
        Besides his wife, he is survived by a son, Dr. Eugene Grossi; three daughters, Dr. Mary Grossi, Elizabeth Grossi and Sarah Grossi, all of Manhattan, and two grandchildren.

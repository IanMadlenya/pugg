
        Farris A. Flint, the founder and honorary chairman of the Americas Foundation, died on Oct. 13 at his home in Scarsdale, N.Y. He was 95.
        Mr. Flint founded the organization, aimed at promoting cooperation and mutual understanding in the Americas, in 1942, and remained active in it until his death. The foundation sponsors activities like leadership seminars for students  that are held in Washington and New York.
        He was a founding member, also in 1942, of the Inter-American Press Association based in Miami, and helped to write its constitution.
        Mr. Flint, who grew up in Indiana, graduated from Northwestern University and worked as a newspaperman in Cleveland before moving to New York in 1931.
        He is survived by a son, Dan, of Chappaqua, N.Y.; three daughters, Nancy Flint of Placitas, N.M., Betsy F. Koester of Palo Alto, Calif., and Susan F. Lindsay of Dubuque, Iowa, 11 grandchildren and two great-grandchildren.


        John J. Farina, who founded radio stations in New York, Connecticut and New Jersey, died on April 6 at the Marcella Nursing and Rehabilitation Center in Burlington, N.J. A resident of Eastampton Townshiip, N.J., he was 69.
        The cause was a stroke, said Carmine J. Berardinelli, the manager of the Berardinelli Forest Hill Memorial, a funeral home in Newark.
        Mr. Farina joined the National Broadcasting Company in 1946, specializing in programming, advertising and planning for 15 years.
        In 1961, he left to become owner and general manager of WJJZ in Burlington. In 1968, he became general manager of WWLE, which served the Cornwall-Newburgh area of New York. He went on to found stations in Mechanicville, N.Y., and Bridgeport, Conn., and in 1981, founded WWJZ-AM in Mount Holly, N.J., which he continued to serve as owner and general manager until his death.
        He was born in Newark and graduated from Rutgers University in 1948.
        He is survived by a brother, Fred, of Roseland, N.J., and three sisters, Alice Michals of Flemington, N.J., Marjorie Strucke of Freehold, N.J., and Carol DeLorenzo of West Caldwell, N.J.


        Larry Maxwell, an actor in theater and films, died on Thursday at his home in Laurelville, Ohio. He was 43.
        The cause was leukemia, said his manager, T. C. Rice.
        He appeared in a number of Off Broadway productions. As a member of Charles  Ludlam's Ridiculous Theatrical Company, he created roles in the comedies "Le Bourgeois Avant-Garde" and "How to Write a Play." He appeared at the Theater for a New City in "Act and the Actor," "Aboriginal Sin" and other works. His theater credits also included "No Damn Good" (1987) and "Dark Shadows" (1988), an adaptation of the gothic television series.
        Mr. Maxwell was featured in the films "Poison" and "Public Access," which won prizes at the Sundance Film Festival. He had roles in more than 60 films, including "Big" and "When Harry Met Sally . . . "
        He is survived by his companion, Janie Marr Werum, of Laurelville; his mother, Harriet, of Champaign, Ill., and a brother, James, of Winterhaven, Fla.

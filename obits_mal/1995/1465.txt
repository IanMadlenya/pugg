
        Charles Scott Chetham, a former director of the Smith College Museum of Art in Northampton, Mass., died on Saturday at his home in Provincetown, Mass. He was 65.
        He died after a brief illness, his family said.
        Mr. Chetham was born in Medford, Mass., and was a graduate of Tufts University and the School of the Museum of Fine Arts in Boston. He received his doctorate in art history from Harvard University in 1960. His catalogue of the modern-art collection of Joseph and Louise Pulitzer Jr. was published in three volumes: in 1956, 1957 and 1972. He became the assistant director of the University of Michigan Museum of Art and assistant professor of art history at the university in 1961. He was named the director of the Smith College Museum of Art in 1962. He retired in 1988.
        He is survived by his wife, Mary, of Northampton; three daughters: Deirdre, Rebecca and Celia, all of Cambridge, Mass., and two brothers, John, of Sudbury, Mass., and Richard, of Austin, Tex.

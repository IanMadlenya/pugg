
        Hugo Munsterberg, an Oriental art scholar, writer and collector, died on Feb. 16 at his home in New Paltz, N.Y. He was 78.
        The cause was congestive heart failure, said his wife, Marjorie Bowen Munsterberg.
        He was the author of several books, including "The Folk Arts of Japan," "Chinese Buddhist Bronzes," "Symbolism in Ancient Chinese Art" and "The Japanese Print."
        In addition to his wife, he is survived by a daughter, Marjorie Gregor of Manhattan, and three grandchildren.

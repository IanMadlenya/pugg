
        Charles E. Adkins, former president of Briarcliff College in Briarcliff Manor, N.Y., died on Sunday at St. Luke's Hospital in Utica, N.Y. He was 85 and lived in Syracuse.
        Mr. Adkins became president of Briarcliff in 1960, when it was a two-year women's college. It adopted a full four-year program in 1965 and awarded its first B.A. degrees in 1967, while he was president. He stepped down in 1968. The school closed in 1977.
        He is survived by four daughters, Jameson Baxter of Barrington, Ill.. Allison VanTassel, also of Barrington, Terry Adkins of Manhattan and Leslie Gunnels of Fairfax, Va., and six grandchildren and a step-grandson. His marriages to Virginia Durning and Jean B. Gilmartin ended in divorce.

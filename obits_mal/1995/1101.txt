
        Hans P. Nordheimer, a retired international trade executive, died on Thursday at the Redwood Terrace convalescent center in Escondido, Calif. Mr. Nordheimer, who was 88, lived in San Diego County.
        He was a longtime vice president of H. A. Astlett & Company of New York and later was president of Fastnet Inc., a former American subsidiary of the leading gold producer Consolidated Goldfields Ltd. of London.
        A native of Germany, he was a graduate of the University of Cologne and immigrated to the United States in 1927.
        He is survived by two sons, Ronald of Wilmington, Del., and Jon of Island Heights, N.J., a reporter for The New York Times; six grandchildren and one great-grandson. He is also survived by a sister, Eva Stolle of Tucson, Ariz.

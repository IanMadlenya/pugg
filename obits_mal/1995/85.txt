
        Jack Alta Applebaum, a former executive of the Federation of Jewish Philanthropies, precursor of the UJA-Federation, died on Wednesday at the Westchester County Medical Center. He was 75.
        Mr. Applebaum joined the Federation of Jewish Philanthropies in 1949. During his time there, he served as budget director and, later, comptroller, a post from which he retired in 1982.
        Born in Poland, Mr. Applebaum emigrated to New York City with his family when he was 10. He graduated from the City College of New York in 1940.
        Mr. Applebaum is survived by his wife, Anne; three sons, Stuart, of New York City, and Edward and Irwyn of Golden's Bridge, N.Y., and a granddaughter.

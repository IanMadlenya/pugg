
        Charles A. McManus Jr., a former president of Americans for Constitutional Action, died on Wednesday after a stroke. He was 68 and lived in Bowie.
        Mr. McManus retired in 1991 after more than 35 years in public affairs, serving in three Republican administrations. He spent 16 years with Americans for Constitutional Action, which promotes and assists conservative candidates for Congress. He also served in the Departments of Agriculture, Energy and Interior.
        He had also been a political consultant, worked for the Republican National  Committee and helped to start the Off The Record Club in 1966 to arrange meetings among journalists and Republican leaders.
        He is survived by his wife, Catherine, of Bowie, seven children and seven grandchildren.


        Peter S. O'Brien, a founder and former president of the Council of Supervisors and Administrators, a New York school union, died on Wednesday at his home in Glen Rock, N.J. He was 70.
        The cause was cancer, his family said.
        A native of Brooklyn and a graduate of St. John's University, Mr. O'Brien spent 27 years in the Marine Corps and its reserve, retiring as a lieutenant colonel.
        He served in the Korean War and was a specialist in flight control.
        Mr. O'Brien worked 34 years in the New York City public schools as a teacher, an assistant principal, a principal and the superintendent of Community School District 30 in Queens. He retired in 1988.
        As president of the council, which was founded in 1966, he supported a strike by the United Federation of Teachers in 1975.
        He also helped found a national group, the American Federation of School Administrators, and served as its president.
        Mr. O'Brien is survived by his wife of 49 years, Bernadette Castronuovo O'Brien; four sons, Peter, of Glen Rock, Michael, of Vernon, Conn., Christopher, of Oakland, N.J., and Stephen, of Manhattan; a daughter, Bernadette O'Brien of Brooklyn; two sisters, Eileen Burke of Clawson, Mich., and Sister Ann Michael of Syracuse; a brother, Shawn, of Saratoga, N.Y.; and three grandchildren.

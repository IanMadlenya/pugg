
        W. Page Thompson, an executive in the publishing industry, died on Sunday in New York. He was 73. The cause of death was cancer, said his son Page.
        Mr. Thompson became publisher of American Home magazine in 1972. Two years later, he became publisher and president of Girl Talk, which he owned with a partner, Bernice Kennedy. After Girl Talk ceased publication in 1980, Mr. Thompson spent three years at Teen Magazine, a Scholastic publication, as its advertising director. He started a media consulting business when he left Scholastic in 1983.
        Mr. Thompson began his publishing career as an advertising director at Ladies Home Journal in 1952.
        He is survived by his wife, Pat, of Manhattan, three sons and a daughter from a previous marriage and five grandchildren. In addition to his son Page, of Weston, Conn., his children are Steve, of Barrington, R.I.; Charles, of Carmel, N.Y.; and Mary Embree of Winnetka, Ill.
        Correction:  September 19, 2000, Tuesday  An obituary on Friday about W. Page Thompson, a publishing executive, misidentified a survivor, Mary Embree. She is his sister, not his daughter.

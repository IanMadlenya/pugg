
        Warren J. Wolfe, a former advertising director at The New York Times, died on Sept. 1 in Westhampton Beach, N.Y. He was 83.
        Mr. Wolfe was born in Great Neck, N.Y. After serving as a first lieutenant in the Army in World War II, he worked in advertising sales at The World Telegram in New York before moving to The Times in 1949 as a retail advertising salesman. By 1973, he had worked his way up to advertising director, with responsibility for all advertising published in the paper. He held that position until he retired in 1975.
        No immediate family members survive.

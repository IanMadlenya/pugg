
        Julius L. Katz, a deputy United States trade representative who was a chief negotiator of the North American Free Trade Agreement, died on Jan. 27 at a hospital in Washington. He was 74 and lived in Bethesda, Md.
        The cause of death was stomach cancer, said his daughter, Linda Harrison of Houston.
        Mr. Katz was a trade representative from 1989 to 1993 and was also involved in the Uruguay Round of trade negotiations of the General Agreement on Tariffs and Trade.
        He was the lead negotiator in a trade pact between the United States and the Soviet Union that President George Bush signed in 1990.
        Mr. Katz served in the Army in World War II and later graduated from George Washington University.
        In 1950, he began work at the State Department and served as a foreign economic policy specialist.
        After he left public service, Mr. Katz worked for Donaldson, Lufkin & Jenrette, the brokerage firm, as head of its futures division.
        At the time of his death, he was president of Hills & Company, a consulting firm founded by Carla A. Hills, the chief trade representative under President Bush.
        Besides his daughter, Mr. Katz is survived by his wife, Charlotte; another daughter, Barbara Spieth, of Wheeling, W. Va.; a son, Lawrence Katz, of Boston; a sister, Ann Einstein, of San Mateo, Calif.; and two grandchildren.

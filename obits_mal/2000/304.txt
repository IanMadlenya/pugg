
        Harry McNally, a construction engineer with the Pennsylvania Railroad who was in charge of the demolition of Pennsylvania Station in 1963, died on March 11 at a hospital in Bradenton, Fla. He was 85.
        As the railroad's chief engineer for New York improvements from 1963 to 1967, Mr. McNally expanded the Madison Square Garden complex and the Penn Plaza office towers above the railroad's tracks.
        Mr. McNally, who was born in Philadelphia, began work with the Pennsylvania Railroad after graduation from Lehigh University. After he retired in 1979, he worked as a consultant for several years.
        His wife, Jane, died in 1988. He is survived by a son, Harry McNally Jr. of Fanwood, N.J.; a daughter, Sara Jane Eusebi of Newtown Square, Pa.; four grandchilden; and six great-grandchildren.


        Daniel Thursz, a former executive vice president of B'nai B'rith International, died on Tuesday at his home in Bethesda, Md. He was 71.
        The cause was a heart ailment, the organization said.
        At his death, Dr. Thursz was the Cardinal O'Boyle professor of social work at Catholic University of America, where he earned a master's degree in 1955 and a doctorate in 1960.
        He is survived by his wife, Hadassah Neulander Thursz; three daughters, Deborah T. Bleiweis of Cortlandt Manor, N.Y., and Deena T. Klopman and Tamar T. Truland of North Potomac, Md.; a son, David, of Baltimore; and eight grandchildren.

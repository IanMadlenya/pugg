
        David Bretherton, an Oscar-winning film editor, died on May 11 in Los Angeles. He was 76.
        Mr. Bretherton, whose father was the director Howard Bretherton, edited some 50 films. He won an Academy Award for his work on the 1972 film adaptation of ''Cabaret,'' starring Liza Minnelli and Joel Grey.
        A Los Angeles native, Mr. Bretherton began his editing career with ''An Affair to Remember'' (1957) and went on to edit films like ''The Diary of Anne Frank'' (1959), ''The Sandpiper'' (1965), ''Cannery Row'' (1982), ''The Pickup Artist'' (1987) and ''Sea of Love'' (1989).


        John B. Hurford, an investment adviser, philanthropist and foreign affairs expert, died on April 3 at a hospital in Manhattan. He was 62.
        The cause was a pulmonary embolism, said a colleague, Lynn Barry.
        Mr. Hurford, who lived in Manhattan, was a managing director of Credit Suisse Asset Management and Warburg Pincus.
        He began his career at Lazard Freres and in 1969 was a founder of BEA Associates, a predecessor of Credit Suisse Asset Management.
        Mr. Hurford won two Fulbright fellowships for study in India and was chairman of the J. William Fulbright Prize for International Understanding Committee.
        He was also a governor of the Foreign Policy Association, a director of the East-West Institute and a life member of the Council on Foreign Relations.
        Mr. Hurford also was chairman of the board of managers at Haverford College and was the founder and president of the Hurford Foundation, an organization that supports institutions and individuals addressing major foreign policy issues.
        John Boyce Hurford was born in Bryn Mawr, Pa., on Feb. 28, 1938. He graduated from Haverford College and the Harvard Business School.
        He is survived by his wife, Hildegard; and a daughter, Jennifer, of Manhattan.

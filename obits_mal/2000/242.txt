
        Lyndon K. Allin, who worked in the White House under three Republican presidents, died on Monday in a hospital in Washington. He was 59.
        The cause was chronic lymphocytic leukemia, said his daughter, Stephanie Allin Clemson of Evanston, Ill.
        Mr. Allin was named in 1967 as the national director of Youth for Nixon. After the 1968 election, he served on President Richard M. Nixon's staff, where he was a special assistant to the president. He continued under President Gerald R. Ford.
        From 1978 to 1981, Mr. Allin was the assistant information officer at the American Embassy in Nigeria.
        In 1981, he joined Ronald Reagan's staff as the deputy press secretary for foreign affairs and national security.
        In 1984, he became the press and cultural counselor at the American consulate in Leningrad. He retired in 1991.
        Lyndon King Allin was born in Detroit on Feb. 7, 1941.
        Besides his daughter, he is survived by his wife, Mary Ann; a son, Lyndon King 2nd, of Washington; his mother, Ruth Emory Allin of Minneapolis; a sister, Nancy Allin Nelson of St. Paul; and a brother, Robin Nail Allin of Hoffman Estates, Ill.

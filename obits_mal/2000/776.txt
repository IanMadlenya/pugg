
        William Harris, a dance writer and advocate for new dance artists, died on Thursday in Manhattan. He was 49 and lived in Manhattan.
        The cause was a heart attack, said his brother, John, of Berkeley, Calif.
        Mr. Harris wrote about dance for SoHo Weekly News, Dance Ink and The Village Voice and was a managing editor for Theater Crafts Magazine. He was also a frequent contributor to The New York Times on dance and theater. As a financial writer, he contributed to Forbes Magazine, Institutional Investor and The Daily Deal.
        Mr. Harris was an informal adviser to dance producers and helped to establish the careers of experimental dance artists and groups including David Dorfman, Tere O'Connor and the Belgian performance art company Plan K.
        In addition to his brother, Mr. Harris is survived by his mother, Vivienne, of Pittsburgh, and a sister, Ellen, of Denver.

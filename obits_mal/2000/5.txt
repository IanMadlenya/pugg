
        Deane McGowen, a sportswriter for The New York Times for more than three decades, died Saturday at North Country Hospital in Newport, Vt. Mr. McGowen, who lived in Newport, was 83.
        The cause was complications of emphysema, his family said.
        Mr. McGowen covered a wide range of sports, most notably boxing and college athletics, and was a recipient in 1997 of the Boxing Writers Association of America's A. J. Liebling Award for outstanding coverage.
        A native of Rock Island, Ill., he attended Dartmouth College, served in the Army during World War II and became a reporter for The Times in 1949. His father, Roscoe McGowen, covered the Brooklyn Dodgers for The Times for almost 30 years.
        Mr. McGowen is survived by his wife, Anita; a son, Richard Deane, of River Vale, N.J.; a daughter, Sharon Stoll, of Montvale, N.J., from his marriage to his first wife, Irene, who died in 1970; five stepchildren, Allan Andrews of Chandler, Ariz., Douglas Andrews of Kalispell, Mont., Stephen Andrews of Stockholm, N.J., Susan Olmsted of Middleton, Mass., and Andrea Downard of Minerva, Ohio; 13 grandchildren; and three great-grandchildren.

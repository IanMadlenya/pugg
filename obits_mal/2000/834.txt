
        Robert Edward Thibault, a staff editor on the national desk of The New York Times for 31 years, died Saturday in Manhattan. He was 69 and lived on the East Side of Manhattan.
        His death resulted from complications of a stroke, his family said.
        Born in Claremont, N.H., on Oct. 2, 1930, Mr. Thibault graduated from the University of New Hampshire in 1956 after service as an Army infantry officer. He began his newspaper career the next year at The Keene Evening Sentinel in New Hampshire.
        In the 1960's, he held reporting and editing positions at The Providence Journal in Rhode Island. He joined The Times in August 1969.
        Mr. Thibault is survived by his wife, Selma; two sisters, Eunice R. Denis and Roberta E. Rivard; and several nieces and nephews.

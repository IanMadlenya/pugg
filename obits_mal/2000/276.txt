
        Henry S. Middendorf Jr., a prominent New York lawyer who also was active in conservative politics, serving as vice chairman of the Conservative Party in New York State, died Thursday at Lenox Hill Hospital in Manhattan. He was 77.
        The cause of death was a heart attack, said Robert Speller, a friend.
        Henry Stump Middendorf Jr. was born in Baltimore on Feb. 23, 1923. He graduated from Harvard University and was in the Army in 1945 and 1946.
        He received a law degree from Harvard University in 1952 and another law degree from New York University in 1957.
        Mr. Middendorf worked for the law firm of Milbank, Tweed, Hope & Hadley in Manhattan from 1952 to 1956 and at Gilbert & Segall, also in Manhattan, from 1956 to 1959.
        He was in private practice since 1959. He was also editor of Law Today magazine from 1963 to 1969.
        Mr. Middendorf was vice chairman of the Conservative Party in New York from 1970 to 1974 and also worked on the presidential campaigns of Barry Goldwater in 1964 and Richard M. Nixon in 1968 as well as the Senate campaign of James L. Buckley in 1970.
        In 1965 and in 1974, Mr. Middendorf ran unsuccessfully for a seat on the State Court of Appeals and in 1975 he lost a bid for a seat on the State Supreme Court.
        He is survived by a brother, J. William Middendorf II of Little Compton, R.I., and a sister, Sarah Lofving of Portland, Me.

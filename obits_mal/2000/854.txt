
        Peter John Repetti, a retired law partner of the international firm of Fulbright & Jaworski, died Thursday at a hospital in Neptune, N.J. He was 82 and had homes in Spring Lake and Short Hills, N.J.
        He suffered from a long illness, his family said.
        Mr. Repetti, a corporate lawyer, worked in Manhattan at the firm and a predecessor, Reavis & McGrath, for about 50 years. He was a past director of several corporations, among them the Block Drug Company of Jersey City.
        Born in Brooklyn, Mr. Repetti graduated from Notre Dame University in 1939 and received his law degree in 1942 at Harvard. He served in the Army in World War II.
        Mr. Repetti is survived by his wife of 49 years, Joan Campbell Repetti; three daughters, Grace Mailman, Joan Repetti and Christina Long; a son, Charles; a sister, Edith Klein; and a brother, George.

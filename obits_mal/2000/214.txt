
        Frank V. Fowlkes, a novelist, journalist and editorial consultant, died on Feb. 16 at his home in Chevy Chase, Md. He was 58.
        The cause was lung cancer, the family said.
        Mr. Fowlkes wrote two suspense novels, ''The Peruvian Contracts'' (G.P. Putnam's Sons, 1976) and ''Majendie's Cat'' (Harcourt Brace Jovanovich, 1986).
        He was a reporter for several publications, including Congressional Quarterly and National Journal; a speechwriter for secretaries of commerce in the Ford and Carter administrations, and vice president for communications of the Pharmaceutical Manufacturers of America.
        For the last decade and a half, he was an independent consultant for interest groups ranging from bankers to university professors.
        He was born Sept. 9, 1941, in Richmond, Va., was reared in New Haven and graduated from Yale University.
        He is survived by his wife, Florence Bryan Fowlkes of Chevy Chase; four sons, Dudley, of Bangor, Pa., Paschal and V. W., of New York, and Davis, of San Antonio; a stepson, Bryan Wisner of Chevy Chase; a sister, Rives Carroll of Washington, and his mother and stepfather, Elizabeth and the Rev. Dr. Randolph Miller of New Haven.

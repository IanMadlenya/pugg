
        Arthur E. Goldschmidt, a United Nations economist who became an ambassador at the United Nations, died on Thursday at a retirement community in Haverford, Pa. He was 90.
        Mr. Goldschmidt was the United States representative at the United Nations Economic and Social Council from 1967 to 1969 with the rank of ambassador.
        Mr. Goldschmidt was born in San Antonio, grew up there and received a bachelor's degree from Columbia University. He held government posts in Washington and was chief of the Interior Department's power division before he going to work for the United Nations in 1950.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Elizabeth Wickenden ; a daughter, Ann Richardson of London; a son, Arthur, Jr., of University Park, Pa.; a brother, Walter, of Los Angeles; four grandchildren and a great-grandchild.

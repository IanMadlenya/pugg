        Fridolf Johnson, an author, illustrator and the editor of American Artists magazine until he retired in the 1970's, died after a heart attack last Tuesday in Benedictine Hospital in Kingston, N.Y. He was 83 years old and lived in Woodstock, N.Y.
        Mr. Johnson produced several books. He edited ''Rockwell Kent: An Anthology of His Work'' and illustrated ''The New Mother Goose Rhymes,'' by Ben Aronin. He was a calligrapher, a private printer specializing in art printing and a collector of bookplates.
        He is survived by his wife, Heidi Lenssen Johnson.

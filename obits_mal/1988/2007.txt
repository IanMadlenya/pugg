        Myron L. Boardman, a former book publisher and former executive director of the Foundation for Christian Living, died of bronchial pneumonia on Monday at the Bryn Mawr Hospital in Bryn Mawr, Pa., after a long illness. He was 74 years old and a resident of Gladwyne, Pa.
        Born in Kinston, R.I., Mr. Boardman joined Prentice-Hall Inc. as a proofreader after graduating from Swarthmore College in 1935. When he took early retirement from the publishing house in 1963, he was president of its trade books division.
        While at Prentice-Hall, Mr. Boardman published ''The Power of Positive Thinking'' by Dr. Norman Vincent Peale, which became a huge best seller. In 1964, Mr. Boardman became executive director of the Foundation for Christian Living, in Pawling, N.Y., which was founded by Dr. Peale. There, Mr. Boardman oversaw the publication and distribution of Dr. Peale's printed sermons and booklets. Mr. Boardman retired from the foundation in 1984.
        Mr. Boardman is survived by a son, Thomas J. Boardman, of Fort Collins, Colo.; a daughter, Jean A. Boardman of Drexel Hill, Pa.; two brothers, Robert Boardman of Salem, Oregon, and Philip Boardman of Asker, Norway, as well as four grandchildren.

        Robert S. Kerr, the seventh Episcopal Bishop of Vermont, died of a heart attack Friday at Medical Center Hospital in Burlington, Vt. He was 71 years old and lived in Vermont.
        A graduate of Trinity College and the General Theological Seminary, Bishop Kerr worked first at the Cathedral of St. John the Divine and the Cathedral School in New York City.
        He began his ministry in Vermont in 1946, in Barre. Before becoming the diocesan bishop in 1974 he was dean of St. Paul's Cathedral. He retired in 1986. He was a trustee of the General Theological Seminary.
        Surviving are his wife, Carolyn; a son, Phillips, of Burlington; a daughter, Elizabeth, of Shelburne, Vt. and two grandchildren.

        Irving Goldsmith, former president and chairman emeritus of Bromley Coats Inc., a manufacturer of women's apparel, died Monday at South Nassau Community Hospital in Oceanside, L.I. He was 85 years old and lived in Rockville Centre, L.I.
        Mr. Goldsmith started in the coat business as a salesman when he was 15. In 1928, he founded Bromley and was president and chairman until 1980, when he became chairman emeritus.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Anne Simon; two sons, Howard and Theodore, both of Port Washington, L.I.; a daughter, Ellen Danziger of Great Neck, L.I.; a brother, William, of Queens; three sisters, Etta Koshar of Queens, Fay Hecht of Englewood, N.J., and Beatrice Jaffe of Huntington, L.I., and six grandchildren.

        Jack Leonard, a singer who recorded many songs with the Tommy Dorsey Orchestra, died Friday. He was 73 years old.
        Mr. Leonard joined the Dorsey group in 1937 and recorded more than 200 songs with the orchestra, including such popular hits as ''Marie'' and ''Once in a While.'' He left the band for military service at the beginning of World War II and was succeeded by Frank Sinatra. Mr. Leonard subsequently headed a military band at Fort Dix, N.J.

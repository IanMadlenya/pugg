        Dr. Joseph Mollo, a pharmacist and chiropractor, died from complications after a leg amputation Sunday at Lutheran Medical Center in Brooklyn. He was 79 years old and lived in the Bay Ridge section.
        Mr. Mollo, who was born in Naples, graduated from Ryder College and the Columbia Institute of Chiropractic. He founded the Lynter Pharmical Company, retiring in 1960 to practice chiropractic in Bay Ridge, which he did for 25 years.
        He is survived by his wife, Sally; a son, Peter, of Staten Island; a daughter, Carolyn McGuire of Bay Ridge; two brothers, Robert, of Clifton, N.J., and Walter, of Deerfield Beach, Fla.; a sister, Mary Montalbano of San Diego, and five grandchildren.

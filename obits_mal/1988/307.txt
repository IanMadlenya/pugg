        William (Bill) Narduzzi, the defensive coordinator and linebacker coach of the Columbia University football team for the last two seasons, died of cancer on Feb. 4 at Columbia-Presbyterian Medical Center. He was 51 years old.
        Before going to Columbia, Mr. Narduzzi spent 11 years as head coach at Youngstown (Ohio) State. After his 1979 Youngstown team was 11-1, he was voted the National College Division I-AA Football Coach of the Year.
        Mr. Narduzzi, who lived in Teaneck, N.J., is survived by his wife, Angeline, and six children.

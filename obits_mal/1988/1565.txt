        Dr. Joseph E. Sokal, a researcher in cancer treatment, died Thursday at Duke Hospital in Durham, N.C., after a short illness. He was 71 years old and lived in Durham.
        Dr. Sokal was a Scholar in Residence at Duke University since 1979, when he retired as chief cancer research clinician at Roswell Park Memorial Institute in Buffalo. Earlier, he had been an assistant professor and scholar at Yale University.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Nancy Branan; two sons, Dr. David C., of Durham, and Dr. Paul J., of Dallas; a sister, Ada Booth, of Santa Clara, Calif.; a brother, Nathan, of Lexington, Mass., and four grandsons.

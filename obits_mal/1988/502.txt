        John L. Cullen, a retired investment banker, died of cardiac arrest Saturday at his home in Princeton, N.J. He was 86 years old.
        Born in Staffordshire, England, Mr. Cullen came to New York in 1908.
        He worked on Wall Street for 60 years, including 40 years with the investment banking firm of R.W. Pressprich and Company, where he was a partner. Before he retired in 1976, he was a vice president and consultant at Shearson, Hammill & Company, the predecessor of Shearson Lehman Brothers.
        Mr. Cullen was the founding president of the Corporate Bond Traders Club of New York.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Marion Buckelew; a daughter, Elizabeth Bischoff of New York; two sisters, Faith Paine of Sarasota, Fla., and Essex, N.Y., and Ellen Schiller of West Hartford, Conn.

        William Frederick Bramstedt, a retired executive with the Standard Oil Company of California, died Thursday after a long illness at his home in San Francisco. He was 83 years old.
        Mr. Bramstedt joined the company in 1927 and retired in 1970 as vice president of Standard Oil, now the Chevron Corporation.
        He leaves his wife, Ruthmarie Bramstedt of San Francisco; two sons, Eric, of Tiburon, Calif., and Harry Porter, Jr. of Portland, Ore; a daughter, Sally Richards of Boise, Idaho, and seven grandchildren.

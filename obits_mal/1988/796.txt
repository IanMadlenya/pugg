        Paul Bigelow, a theater consultant and an editor, died of cancer on May 18 at his home in Ocean City, N.J. He was 83 years old.
        Mr. Bigelow assisted the producer and director Cheryl Crawford in the Broadway production of Tennessee Williams's ''Rose Tattoo'' in 1951. He also edited several of Mr. Williams's works, including the novel ''The Roman Spring of Mrs. Stone,'' for New Directions Publishers.
        Mr. Bigelow, who was born in Maine and educated at Cambridge, had been associated with the New York Theater Guild since the early 1950's.
        There were no immediate survivors.

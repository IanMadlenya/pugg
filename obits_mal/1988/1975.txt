        Richard Weil Dammann, a lawyer and former director of Philip Morris Inc., died of gastric cancer yesterday at his house in Rye, N.Y. He was 77 years old.
        Mr. Dammann was a trustee of the Federation of Jewish Philanthropies, a director of Montefiore Hospital and chairman and a director of United Hospital in Port Chester, N.Y.
        He was born in Manhattan. Mr. Dammann was an alumnus of Princeton University and the Harvard University Law School. He was a partner in the law firm of Dammann & Engel in Manhattan and had been a director of the American Safety Razor Corporation.
        Mr. Dammann retired from the Philip Morris board in 1981, after having been a member more than 25 years.
        His wife, the former Marjorie Spiegel, died in 1986. Surviving are a sister, Margaret Dammann Eisner of Manhattan; three daughters, Debora D. Kramer of Washington, Pamela D. Adams of Lexington, Mass., and Penelope D. Johnston of Riverside, Conn., and six grandchildren.

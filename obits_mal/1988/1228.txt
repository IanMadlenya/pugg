        Arthur Klein, an educator who worked in the New York City public-school system for 50 years, died of cancer Friday night in a Pittsburgh nursing home. He was 90 years old.
        Mr. Klein was a lifelong Manhattan resident, but after colon cancer was diagnosed in February, he moved to Pittsburgh, where his son, Roger, lives.
        A Phi Beta Kappa graduate of the City College of New York in 1919, he began his teaching career at Public School 50 in Manhattan. He taught Spanish at several schools. In 1941 he became principal of Morris High School in the Bronx.
        In 1946 he was appointed to the Board of Examiners, which establishes eligibility requirements for teachers and principals and designs tests for applicants. He was chairman of the board twice and a member until his retirement in 1969.
        Mr. Klein married an elementary-school teacher and principal, Thea S. Silverstein, in 1933. Mrs. Klein died in 1982.
        Besides his son, Roger, Mr. Klein is surived by two grandsons, Joshua and Andrew.

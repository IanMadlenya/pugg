        Matthew Silverman, a labor lawyer, died after a long illness Thursday at his home in Manhattan. He was 80 years old.
        Born in Brooklyn, he graduated from Amherst College, where he was elected to Phi Beta Kappa, and had master's and law degrees from Harvard University. He was counsel for the Emergency Relief Bureau of New York.
        He represented Local 1 of the Amalgamated Lithographers of America, the United Furniture Workers of America and other unions.
        Surviving are his wife, Phyllis; a daughter, Peggy Sarno, and a son, Steven, both of Manhattan, and a grandson.

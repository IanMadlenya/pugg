        Dr. James Liebmann Leland, a former clinical professor of medicine at the Columbia University College of Physicians and Surgeons, died of sepsis, a blood disease, on Wednesday on a visit to California. He was 80 years old and lived in the Riverdale section of the Bronx.
        Dr. Leland received his medical degree in 1933 from Columbia and joined the faculty, retiring in 1978. He also had a private practice and did clinical teaching at Bellevue, Harlem, Roosevelt and Presbyterian Hospitals.
        Dr. Leland was born in New York. His father, Julius Liebmann, was president of Liebmann Breweries, producer of Rheingold beer. The brewery founder, Samuel Liebmann, was Dr. Leland's great-grandfather.
        Dr. Leland's wife, Dr. Winifred Bronson, died in 1986. Surviving are his daughter, Dr. Anne Leland Benham, and three grandchildren, all of San Mateo, Calif.

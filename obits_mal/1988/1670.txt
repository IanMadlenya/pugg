        Harry R. Mancher, the president of the Federation of Jewish Philanthropies from 1977 to 1980 and a longtime treasurer of the United Jewish Appeal-Federation, died of a brain tumor yesterday at Mount Sinai Hospital. He was 70 years old and lived in Scarsdale, N.Y.
        He had been the chief executive officer and senior managing partner of the accounting firm of S. D. Leidesdorf & Company for many years and, after a merger of the two firms, was vice chairman and regional partner of the accounting firm of Ernst & Whinney from 1978 until he retired in 1982.
        Mr. Mancher had been the associate treasurer and a trustee of Mount Sinai Medical Center. He was a native New Yorker and a graduate of City College.
        He is survived by his wife, Leona; a daughter, Lisa, of Scarsdale, and two brothers, Lee, of White Plains, and Joseph, of Queens.

        John H. Mitchell, a former television executive, died of a heart attack in Los Angeles Tuesday. He was 66 years old.
        Mr. Mitchell, who was born in New York and graduated from the University of Michigan, began his broadcasting career with the radio department of a New York advertising agency and the Mutual Broadcasting System. He helped develop radio and television stations in Rochester before becoming United Artists' director of television in the late 1940's.
        Mr. Mitchell joined Screen Gems, the predecessor of Columbia Pictures Television, in 1952 as vice president of sales and was an executive with Columbia Pictures for 25 years. He was president of the television division from 1968 to 1977 and oversaw the production of more than 100 television series - including ''Father Knows Best,'' ''Bewitched,'' ''The Flintstones'' and ''Police Story'' - and 50 mini-series or made-for-television movies, such as ''Brian's Song'' and ''QB VII.''
        In the early 1980's, he was president of the Academy of Television Arts and Sciences and founded the Television Academy Hall of Fame. He also operated a Los Angeles communications consulting firm and was president of the Hollywood Radio and Television Society.
        Mr. Mitchell is survived by his wife, Pat, of Los Angeles; a daughter, Jan Fletcher, of Portsmouth, R.I.; a brother, Ralph, of Miami; two step-children, Josephine Kotliar, of Toronto, and David Reader, of Sao Paulo, Brazil, and three grandchildren.

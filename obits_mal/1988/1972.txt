        Jerome Prince, dean of the Brooklyn Law School from 1953 to 1971, died Saturday in his apartment in Greenwich Village. He was 81 years old.
        Mr. Prince graduated from City College and went to the law school, where he earned two degrees. He joined the faculty and became vice dean and assistant dean before becoming dean. After retiring, Mr. Prince remained on the faculty, continuing to teach the law of evidence, his specialty, until this year. Mr. Prince was born in Manhattan.  Surviving are his wife, the former Elaine Lederman; two brothers, Dr. Harold Prince of Manhattan and Michael Prince of Los Angeles; two daughters from a prior marriage, Karen Gerstl of Manalapan, N.J., and Elayne Prince of Yonkers; a stepdaughter, Elizabeth Russomanno of Mineola, L.I.; a stepson, Stephen Katzberg of Manhattan; a grandson, and two stepgrandchildren.

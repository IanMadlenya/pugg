
       Israel Nathan Herstein, a leading mathematician and educator at the University of Chicago, died of cancer Monday night at his home in Chicago. He was 64 years old. Dr. Herstein was the author of the widely used textbook ''Topics in Algebra.'' His own work included pioneering advances in the theory of rings, an algebraic system.
        Israel Nathan Herstein, a leading mathematician and educator at the University of Chicago, died of cancer Monday night at his home in Chicago. He was 64 years old. Dr. Herstein was the author of the widely used textbook ''Topics in Algebra.'' His own work included pioneering advances in the theory of rings, an algebraic system.
        Born in Lublin, Poland, Dr. Herstein joined the University of Chicago in 1962.
        Survivors include his wife, Barbara Cortzen, a mathematician at De Paul University in Chicago.

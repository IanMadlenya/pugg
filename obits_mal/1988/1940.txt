        Emanuel Fritz, a forestry and conservation expert who helped to create California's state forest program, died last Thursday. He was 102 years old.
        Mr. Fritz, a co-founder of the Regional Parks Association, the forerunner of the East Bay Regional Park system near San Francisco Bay, was a professor of forestry emeritus at the University of California at Berkeley. He joined the faculty in 1919, retiring in 1954.

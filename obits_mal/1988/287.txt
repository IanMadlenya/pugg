        Dr. Edward S. Tauber, a psychiatrist and author, died yesterday in White Plains Hospital Medical Center from complications of a stroke he suffered two weeks ago. He was 79 years old and lived in Harrison, N.Y.
        Dr. Tauber, an expert in psychoanalytic theory and practice and the neurophysiology of sleep, did pioneering work in the field of dreams in animals.
        He was the author of numerous papers and two books, ''Prelogical Experience, An Inquiry Into Dreams and Other Creative Processes'' with Dr. Maurice R. Green in 1959, and ''In The Name Of Life: Essays in Honor of Erich Fromm'' with Dr. Bernard Landis in 1971.
        He is survived by a son, Carl, of East Greenwich, R.I., and a sister, Ethel Herzog of Fort Lauderdale, Fla.
        Correction:  February 9, 1988, Tuesday, Late City Final Edition

        James P. Farrell, a lawyer who served for many years as counsel to the Cities Service Company, died of Parkinson's disease Wednesday at his home in Montclair, N.J. He was 85 years old.
        Mr. Farrell, a 1926 graduate of Fordham Law School, spent his legal career with Frueauff, Farrell, Sullivan & Bryan of Manhattan, and was the senior partner when he retired in 1976. He was a Knight of Malta and was active in community and charitable affairs, serving for many years as president of the Charles A. Frueauff Foundation and the W. Alton Jones Foundation. He was a director of the Madison Square Boys Club and the Catholic Youth Organization, both in Manhattan.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Kathryn Fisher; a son, James Jr., of Rumson, N.J.; three daughters, Patricia Russell of Palm Desert, Calif., Kathryn Noumair of Queens and Hazel Murray of Short Hills, N.J.; 12 grandchildren, and one great-grandchild.

        Joseph Smolens, professor emeritus of immunology and microbiology at Temple University Medical School, died of liver cancer Thursday at his home in Wynnewood, Pa. He was 77 years old.
        Professor Smolens's research on blood protein components and ways of removing toxic products from blood led to the production of plasma protein products used to treat rabies and staphylococcus infections.
        He earned a bachelor's degree from Drexel University and began research at the University of Pennsylvania School of Medicine. He headed the department of immunology and immunochemistry at the Wyeth Institute of Biochemistry in Philadelphia. He was director of the serum exchange division at Children's Hospital in Philadelphia.
        Surviving are his wife, Hilda; a daughter, Nancy Melcher of Manhattan, and a son, Peter, of San Antonio.

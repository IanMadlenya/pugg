        Henri Frenay, a leader of the French Resistance in World War II, has died, family members said today. He was 82 years old.
        The family declined to give the date or cause of death.
        During the Nazi occupation of France, Mr. Frenay founded the Resistance group Combat and the underground newspaper of the same name.
        He was an army captain when the Germans invaded France in May 1940. He was captured on June 25, 1940, but escaped and returned to Lyons, his hometown, where he began organizing the Resistance. He served in the French government in exile in Algiers and after the liberation in the French provisional governments of 1944 and 1945. After the war, he managed a toy company.
        Mr. Frenay was author of ''The Night Will End,'' ''Volunteers of the Night'' and ''The Jean Moulin Riddle.''

        John Reilly, who served for two decades as Chicago's director of special events, died Sunday in Fort Lauderdale, Fla. He was 89 years old.
        Mr. Reilly was in charge of special events for the Century of Progress exposition here in 1933 and 1934, and served in the same capacity for the 1939 World's Fair in New York. In his Chicago post he staged five inaugural ceremonies for Mayor Richard J. Daley, arranged for the visit of Queen Elizabeth II of Britain to the city in 1959 and orchestrated city tours for celebrities.

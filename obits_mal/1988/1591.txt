        Sidney Reitman, a labor lawyer who was active in the civil rights movement in the 1960's, died of thyroid cancer at St. Peter's Medical Center in New Brunswick, N.J., on Wednesday. He was 73 years old and lived in West Orange, N.J.
        Mr. Reitman served as chairman of the New Jersey Commission on Civil Rights from 1961 to 1965 and helped draft the New Jersey Civil Rights Act.
        He is survived by his wife, Marian; three sons, Leslie W., of Denver, Jonathan W., of Brunswick, Me., and Marc W., of Port Washington, L.I.; two brothers, Alan, of Millburn, N.J., and Norman, of Highland Park, N.J., and four grandchildren.

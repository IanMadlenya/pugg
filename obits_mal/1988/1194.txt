        Dr. Stanley Block Tunick, a lawyer, accountant and educator, died of heart failure on Tuesday at South Nassau Communities Hospital in Oceanside, L.I. He was 88 years old and lived in Palm Beach, Fla.
        Dr. Tunick, who graduated from City College, held a Ph.D. from New York University and a law degree from St. Lawrence University. He was the former chairman of the accounting department at the Baruch School of Business and Public Administration, now Baruch College, and was named professor emeritus in 1961.
        He was a co-author of a leading college textbook on accounting, ''Fundamental Accounting,'' and was a senior partner in the Manhattan accounting firm of Tunick & Platkin until his retirement in 1971. He also practiced estate and tax law.
        He is survived by his wife, Mildred, of Palm Beach; two sons, Andrew, of Manhattan, and Richard, of Scarsdale, and five grandchildren.

        Jack Goldberger, a labor leader in the San Francisco Bay area for more than four decades, died Tuesday. He was 84 years old.
        Starting in 1937 as a founder and business agent for a local of the Newspaper and Periodical Drivers, he rose to become president of the San Francisco Labor Council, the Bay Area Council of Teamsters and the Western Conference of Teamsters. Mr. Goldberger, a negotiator, arbitrator and representative in labor negotiations, said that during his career he never called a strike.

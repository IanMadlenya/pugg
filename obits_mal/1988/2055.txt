        James R. Neely, a physiologist and researcher, died of a heart attack Tuesday at Geisinger Medical Center in Danville, Pa. He was 52 years old and lived in Danville.
        A senior research scientist at the Geisinger Foundation's Sigfried and Janet Weis Center, Dr. Neely, at the time of death, was studying the biochemical causes of cell death after heart attacks. He recently won a Merit Award from the National Heart, Lung and Bood Institute. He was the author of more than 100 research articles, many of which were widely cited by other scientists.
        He earned bachelor's and master's degrees at East Tennessee Polytechnical College and a doctorate from Vanderbilt University.
        Surviving are his wife, Dr. Janet Robishaw; a son, Brent, of Elizabethtown, Pa.; four daughters, Charmaine and Kay Neely, Margie Davis and Sandra Khoshnevissan, all of Elizabethtown; a brother, Denton, of Nashville, Tenn.; two sisters, Treva Ashburn and Noble Todd, both of Nashville, and five grandchildren.

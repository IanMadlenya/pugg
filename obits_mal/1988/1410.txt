        Morris Kaplan, a retired reporter for The New York Times, died of complications resulting from diabetes yesterday at Beth Israel Medical Center. He was 79 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Kaplan, a native of Portland, Me., began his career in 1927 on the Poughkeepsie (N.Y.) Eagle-News, now defunct. In 1939, he left to write a radio program for Ed Fitzgerald on WOR. He joined The Times in 1945, primarily reporting on the courts and writing general-assignment articles. He retired in 1978.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Margaret Stern; a daughter, Maryan Jaross of Acton, Mass., and two grandchildren.

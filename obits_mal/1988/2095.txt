        Harry Heller, owner of a taxicab fleet, died of cancer Sunday at the Columbia-Presbyterian Medical Center in Manhattan. He was 72 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Heller, who was born in New York City, had been a member of the Metropolitan Taxicab Board of Trade since 1946 and a member of its board of directors for the last 12 years. He was an owner and president of the Rego-Stan Maintenance Corporation, which operates about 100 medallion taxicabs.
        He is survived by his wife, Lillian; a daughter, Sheila Ross of Wilton, Conn.; two sons, Philip, of Lenox, Mass., and Frederic, of Manhattan; a sister, Anne, of Manhattan, a brother, Irving, of Miami, and five grandchildren.

        Thomas V. Sinclair Jr., a retired judge of the New York City Civil Court, died Tuesday at Columbia-Presbyterian Medical Center after a stroke. He was 73 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Judge Sinclair was elected to the court in 1976 from the 10th District in central Harlem and retired in 1985. Earlier, he had been a Housing Court judge, had served in Small Claims Court and had been the rent administrator for upper Manhattan.
        A native of New York, Judge Sinclair was a graduate of City College and received his law degree from St. John's University. He served in the United States Navy during World War II.
        Before he went on the bench, Judge Sinclair had been active in community affairs for many years, serving as chairman of Community Board 10, Harlem Teams for Self-Help and the Harlem Neighborhood Association. He also helped organize community support for construction of the State Office Building on 125th Street.
        He was the president for two terms of the Harlem Lawyers Association and regional director of the National Bar Association.
        Judge Sinclair is survived by his wife, the former Marion Keene; two sons, Robert and Thomas A., both of Manhattan; a sister, Eileen Braithwaite of Melbourne, Fla., and a grandson.

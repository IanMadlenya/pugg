        Thomas B. Dorsey, former director of the Los Angeles Times-Washington Post News Service, died of a heart atttack Wednesday at the Cedars-Sinai Medical Center in Los Angeles. He was 60 years old.
        Mr. Dorsey, who lived in Los Angeles, was a former associate editor of the Chicago Tribune-New York News Syndicate and was director of the Newsday Specials Syndicate from 1964 to 1969. Since 1977, he had headed Dorsey Communications Inc., consultants.
        He is survived by by his wife, Helen; a son, Blinn, of Alaska; and a daughter, Diana Dorsey of Manhattan.

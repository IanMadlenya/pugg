        Raymond Stross, a movie producer and director, died of heart disease Sunday at his home in Beverly Hills. He was 72 years old.
        Mr. Stross was born in Leeds, England, and owned a chain of theaters before becoming a producer. Among his films were ''A Question of Adultery,'' ''The Leather Boys'' with Rita Tushingham, and two that starred Anne Heywood, his wife of 28 years, ''I Want What I Want'' and ''The Fox,'' based on a story by D. H. Lawrence.
        In addition to his wife, Mr. Stross is survived by a son, Mark, and a daughter, Laraine, all of Los Angeles.

        Soloman Freedman, a lawyer for the Securities and Exchange Commission for more than 30 years, died of cancer Thursday at his home in Bethesda, Md. He was 78 years old.  Among the posts Mr. Freeman held at the S.E.C. was that of director of the division of corporate regulation. At his death he was associated with a number of investment companies managed by Neuberger Berman Management Inc.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Gladys Schwartz; three children, Jerrold Freedman of Santa Barbara, Calif., David Freedman of Alburquerque, N.M., and Sara Freedman of Somerville, Mass., and five grandchildren.

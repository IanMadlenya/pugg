        William B. Volet, a former government official and Democratic Party leader, died Tuesday when he was struck by a bus as he crossed 57th Street at Madison Avenue in Manhattan. He was 85 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Volet attended New York University and was confidential secretary to Judge Franklin Taylor of Kings County Court from 1926 to 1949. He was administrative assistant to the chairman of the New York City Board of Transportation from 1943 to 1953 and executive assistant to State Comptroller Arthur Levitt from 1955 to 1979.
        He is survived by a son, Andrew, of Manhattan.

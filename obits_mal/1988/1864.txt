        Dr. Harry Friedman, a cardiologist and former associate clinical professor at New York Medical College, died of heart disease Saturday at the John F. Kennedy Hospital in Lake Worth, Fla. He was 81 years old and lived in Palm Beach, Fla.
        Dr. Friedman, a graduate of New York University and New York Medical College, was in private practice in the Bronx for more than 30 years and was head of the electrocardiogram unit at New York Medical College. He retired in 1975.
        He is survived by his wife, Mae; three sons, Richard and Dr. Michael, of Stamford, Conn., and Dr. Laurence, of Lucas Valley, Calif., and four grandchildren.

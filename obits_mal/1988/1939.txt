        Dr. Isadore E. Gerber, a physician and clinical professor of medicine, died Monday at his home in Manhattan. He was 85 years old.
        Dr. Gerber was associated with the Mount Sinai Medical Center for more than 56 years. He had specialties in pediatrics, infectious diseases, internal medicine and pathology. Dr. Gerber was professor emeritus of clinical medicine at the Mount Sinai School of Medicine and was the former director of the pathology department at the Jewish Home and Hospital.
        He graduated from the University of Maryland Medical School in 1926, Surviving are his wife, Miriam; a daughter, Toni Hope of Manhattan; a son, William, of Thousand Oaks, Calif.; a stepson, Dr. David Alberts of Potomac, Md.; two stepdaughters, Dr. Rosanne Alberts of Manhattan and Ellen Bigman of Fair Lawn, N.J., and eight grandchildren.

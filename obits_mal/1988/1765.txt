        Roderick M. MacDougall, treasurer of Harvard University, died of cancer of the colon on Wednesday at Stillman Infirmary at the university in Cambridge, Mass. He was 62 years old and lived in Weston, Mass.
        Mr. MacDougall was a banker for more than three decades before he retired in 1984 as chairman of the Bank of New England Corporation and became Harvard's treasurer. Earlier in his career, he was a vice president of the Morgan Guaranty Trust Company in New York City and president of the Marine Midland Bank in Rochester.
        At the university, he was a member of both the Harvard Corporation and the Board of Overseers. His duties included the chairmanships of the Harvard Management Company and the Corporation Committee on Shareholder Responsibility. Degree from Harvard
        Mr. MacDougall was born in New York City and served in the United States Navy in World War II. He received a bachelor's degree from Harvard in 1951.
        Upon graduation he joined Morgan Guaranty and remained with the bank until 1967, when he was named executive vice president of Marine Midland, He became president the next year.
        In 1974 he went to the Bank of New England as president and two years later became chief executive officer. He moved up to chairman in 1978. At his death, Mr. MacDougall was a director of several corporations, including the New England Mutual Life Insurance Company, the Controlled Risk Insurance Company, the Constitution Capital Management Company and the Bank of New England.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Barbara Park; two sons, Douglas, of Conway, Mass., and Gordon, of Arlington, Va.; two daughters, Susan, of Boston, and Harriet Quirk of Stowe, Mass., and three grandchildren.

        Robert S. Potter, a lawyer, died of cancer Saturday at his home in Manhattan. He was 68 years old.
        Mr. Potter was for 30 years general counsel and for 20 years a director of the Dow Jones & Company Inc., which publishes The Wall Street Journal. He was a partner at Patterson Belknap Webb & Tyler until he retired last year. He was a graduate of Harvard College and the University of Virginia Law School.
        Mr. Potter was former chairman of the Brearley School; former chancellor of the Episcopal Diocese of New York; former trustee of the Institute for Policy Studies; the Center for Constitutional Rights, and the New York Theological Seminary.
        Surviving are his wife, Isabel; four daughters, Louise Ross of Philadelphia, Isabel Aldrich and Lorna Walker, both of Manhattan, and Linda Potter-Shriver of New Haven, Conn.; a son, Robert Jr., of Manhattan; a sister, Dorothy Boardman of Manchester, Mass., and six grandchildren.
        A memorial service is to be held Wednesday at 11 A.M. at St. James Episcopal Church on Madison Avenue at 71st Street.

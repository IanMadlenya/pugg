        John M. Duggan, president of Washburn University in Topeka, died of pancreatic cancer Tuesday in a Topeka hospital. He was 60 years old.
        Earlier this year, Dr. Duggan was named president of Washburn, a liberal arts school with an enrollment of 6,800 students.
        In 1975 he began 10 years of service as president of Saint Mary's College, a women's institution in South Bend, Ind. He left there to head the Independent College Funds of America, an organization that coordinates fund-raising among private colleges and universities.
        Dr. Duggan received a doctorate in psychology from Yale University.
        He is survived by his wife, Joan, and five sons: Paul, Timothy and John, of South Bend; Michael, of Minneapolis, and Christopher, of Los Angeles.

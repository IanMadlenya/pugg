        Dr. Irving Goldaber, a Miami sociologist died, apparently of a heart attack, Saturday in Monterey, Calif., where he was conducting a labor-management program. He was 62 years old.
        A native of New York and former associate professor at Brooklyn College, Dr. Goldaber was known nationally for techniques he developed for hostage negotiations for the police.
        He is survived by his wife, Marylin Marinoff Goldaber; two sons, Dr. Kenneth Goldaber of Dallas, and Richard Goldaber of New York, and a brother, Stanley Goldaber, also of New York.

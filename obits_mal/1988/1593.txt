        Theodore Freedman, director of the European office of the Anti-Defamation League of B'nai B'rith, died yesterday of cancer at a hospice in Houston. He was 66 years old.
        He joined the league in 1949 and worked in New York as director of the intergroup relations division. He became director of the European office, which is in Rome, in 1986.
        Surviving are his wife, Reba; three daughters, Phyllis Freedman of Washington, and Renee Stern and Elise Simons, both of Houston; a son, Theodore of Austin, Tex.; a sister, Ruth Ozoroff of Houston, and two grandchildren.

        Kenji Fukunaga, a veteran politician of the governing party and former speaker of Japan's lower house, died of respiratory failure at a hospital Tuesday, a hospital official said. He was 77 years old.
        Beginning in 1949, he won election 15 consecutive times to the House of Representatives. He served as lower house speaker for two years up to 1985. He was chairman of the Japan Amateur Sports Association at the time of his death.
        He also served as minister for labor, transport, and health and welfare between 1961 and 1977.

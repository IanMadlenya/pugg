        Elting Arnold, the first general counsel to the Inter-American Development Bank and a former assistant general counsel in the United States Treasury Department, died of cancer Wednesday at Sibley Memorial Hospital in Washington. He was 76 and lived in Chevy Chase, Md.
        Mr. Arnold was born in Staatsburg, N.Y., and graduated from Williams College and Columbia University School of Law.
        He is survived by his wife, Hannah; two daughters, Sarah, of Baltimore, and Patricia, of Hollister, Calif., and three grandchildren.

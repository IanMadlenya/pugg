        Theodore S. Amussen, an editor with book publishers for many years and later an editor in chief for the National Gallery of Art, died of a brain tumor on Wednesday at the Collingwood Nursing Home in Rockville, Md. He was 73 years old and lived in Washington.
        Mr. Amussen, who was born in Salt Lake City, joined Rinehart & Company in 1938 after studying at the University of Paris and at Harvard. He later was with Reynal, Hitchcock and with Harcourt, Brace before becoming editor in chief at Henry Holt & Company in 1950.
        He edited special publications for the National Geographic Society and was chief of the books division of the National Park Service before assuming his post at the National Gallery. He retired in 1983.
        Mr. Amussen is survived by a daughter, Deborah Jenin of Flushing, Queens; three sons, Daniel, of Boonville, N.Y., David, of Silver Spring, Md., and Timothy, of Reston, Va.; a brother, Robert, of Vancouver, British Columbia, and 11 grandchildren.

        Dr. Frederick L. Scarf, an American scientist who was an authority on space plasma physics, died of undetermined causes Sunday at Burdenko Hospital in Moscow. He was part of an international team of scientists conducting experiments associated with two Russian space ventures. He was 57 years old and lived in Sherman Oaks, Calif.
        Dr. Scarf, who received a Ph.D. in 1955 from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology, had been a research scientist with TRW Defense and Space Systems Group of Redondo Beach, Calif., since 1962. He was in Moscow working on the Phobos spacecrafts' instruments for detecting plasma waves.
        In 1981 and 1986 he was a recipient of Exceptional Scientific Achievement Medals from NASA.
        He is survived by his wife, Mimi Levin Scarf; a daughter, Elizabeth; a son, Daniel, all of Sherman Oaks, Calif., and another son, Robert, of Venice, Calif.

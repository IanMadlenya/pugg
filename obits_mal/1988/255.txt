        Donald S. Macdonald, a real-estate executive, died of emphysema Thursday at Sarasota (Fla.) Memorial Hospital. He was 82 years old and lived in Siesta Key, Fla.
        Mr. Macdonald, who was born in Brooklyn, graduated from Cornell University. He was president of Donald Macdonald Inc., a real-estate brokerage firm, from 1934 to 1946, when he joined Douglas Gibbons & Company, a real-estate firm, as vice president. From 1957 to 1968, he was president of Douglas Gibbons-Hollyday & Ives Inc., and its vice chairman from 1968 to 1974, when he retired.
        He is a former president of the Real Estate Board of New York.
        He is survived by his wife, Jean; a daughter, Nancy Cudlipp of Morristown, N.J.; a son, Robert, of Manitou Springs, Colo.; a stepson, Frederick Yardley of Phoenix, Md.; six grandchildren; three step-grandchildren, and four great-grandchildren.

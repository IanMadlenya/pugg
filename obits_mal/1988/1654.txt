        Leo Schloss, an educator and accountant, died Wednesday at University Hospital in Stony Brook, L.I., after a long illness. He was 74 years old and lived in Brooklyn.
        Mr. Schloss, a graduate of St. John's University and a certified public accountant, started the accounting department at the Long Island University School of Business in 1944 and was its head until 1958. He was named associate dean of the Business School in 1954. He retired in 1980 but continued to teach an accounting course at the school until 1985.
        He is survived by two daughters, Raela Bloom of East Rockaway, L.I., and Joan Stein of Stony Brook; two sisters, Hilda Blumberg of Queens, and Helen Goldberg of Brooklyn, and three grandchildren.

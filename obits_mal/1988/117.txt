        Harold M. Lane, former chairman of the Lerner Stores Corporation, died of a stroke Friday at his country home in Byram, Conn. He was 96 years and lived in Manhattan.
        In 1919 Mr. Lane and Samuel Lerner, a New York manufacturer, founded the Lerner Shops, which became a chain of more than 450 popular-price clothing stores.
        Mr. Lane became chairman and chief executive in the 1940's. He retired in 1976.
        He is survived by his wife, Helene; a son, Richard, and a daughter, Doris L. Frank, both of Manhattan; eight grandchildren, and two great-grandchildren.

        Rabbi Edward Ellenbogen, who was once the ranking Jewish chaplain in the United States armed forces, died Sunday after a long illness. He was 76 years old.
        Mr. Ellenbogen, who lived on Mercer Island, retired as a colonel in the Air Force in 1965. He served as deputy command chaplain of the Strategic Air Command in Omaha from 1961 to 1964 and as a base chaplain at McChord Air Force Base in Tacoma, Wash. He served in northern Africa in World War II and later in Europe and Southeast Asia.

        Winston E. Forrest Jr., a fund-raiser who was vice president for resources of the Planned Parenthood Federation of America, died of a viral infection at Lenox Hill Hospital Saturday. He was 51 years old and lived in Scarsdale, N.Y.
        Since Mr. Forrest joined Planned Parenthood in 1976, he was credited with raising $120 million, most of which went to the organization's education programs. He also served as a consultant to several other nonprofit organizations, including the National Resources Defense Council, Amnesty International and the American Civil Liberties Union.
        Mr. Forrest was born in Temple, Tex., and was a graduate of Washington University in St. Louis, where he also served as an administrator. From 1972 to 1975 he was president of the American Alumni Council, now the Council for the Advancement and Support of Education.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Donne Hansen; his parents, Winston and Irene Forrest of Arlington, Tex.; two brothers, Terry, of Pittsfield, Mass., and Raymond, of Oldsmar, Fla.; a sister, Anne McKendrick of Arlington; two sons, Peter, of Astoria, Queens, and Winston, of Aschaffenburg, West Germany, and a grandson.

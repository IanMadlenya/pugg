        George H. Oestreich Jr., a retired publishing executive and a former mayor of Baxter Estates, L.I., died of lung cancer Monday at his home in Baxter Estates. He was 69 years old.
        Mr. Oestreich, a graduate of Syracuse University, joined the Knapp Communications Corporation in 1973 and retired in 1985 as a vice president and publisher. Previously, he was publisher of Architectural Digest and advertising director of Bon Appetit. He began his career with the Curtis Publishing Company and later joined American Heritage Publishing and the United Nations Association.
        He served as an official, including mayor, of Baxter Estates for more than 30 years.
        He is survived by his wife, Nancy; three daughters, Tayler, Jean and Leslie Schechter, all of Port Washington, L.I.; a son, George, of Manhattan; a sister, Grace Lubbers of Manhassett, L.I., and three grandchildren.
        A memorial service will be held on Monday at 11 A.M. at St. Stephen Episcopal Church in Port Washington.

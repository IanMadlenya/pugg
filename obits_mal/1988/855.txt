        Chester Schwimmer, a former leader of the Queens Republican Party, died of amyotrophic lateral sclerosis, or Lou Gehrig's disease, today at Deepdale Hospital in Queens. He was 75 years old and lived in Freeport, L.I.
        Mr. Schwimmer was founder and chairman of the Fumex Termite Control Company in New Hyde Park, L.I. He also founded the John Foster Dulles Republican Club in Jamaica, Queens, and served as the club's president. He was a member of the Interstate Sanitation Commission in the administration of Gov. Nelson A. Rockefeller. Mr. Schwimmer was born in Brooklyn.
        Surviving are his wife, Evelyn; a daughter, Carole Frieder of Bellmore, L.I.; a son, Steve, of Syosset, L.I.; two brothers, Rubin, of Queens, and Edward, of Brooklyn, and eight grandchildren.

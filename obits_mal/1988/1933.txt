        Leonard J. Fliedner, an educator who was principal of Stuyvesant High School for 17 years until his retirement in 1973, died of complications from pneumonia Saturday at La Guardia Hospital in Queens. He was 88 years old and lived in Flushing, Queens.
        Dr. Fliedner, whose teaching career spanned more than half a century, received a bachelor's degree from City College, a master's degree from Columbia University and a doctorate in chemistry from Fordham University. He began teaching in 1922 and in 1950 became principal of George Washington High School before going to Stuyvesant in 1954.
        He is survived by a daughter, Patricia Murray of Bayside, L.I.; a son, Dr. Leonard J., of Flushing, and two granddaughters.

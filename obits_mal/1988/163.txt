        Dr. Adam Munz, chief psychologist at the St. Luke's-Roosevelt Hospital Center since 1962, died of cancer Monday at the Memorial Sloan-Kettering Cancer Center. He was 60 years old and lived in Leonia, N.J.
        Dr. Munz, who was an associate professor of clinical psychology at the College of Physicians and Surgeons at Columbia University, was born in Poland and came to the United States as a war refugee in 1944. He received a bachelor's degree from Hunter College and a doctorate in psychology from New York University.
        A past president of the New York State Psychological Association's clinical division and of the New York Society of Clinical Psychologists, Dr. Munz received the society's Distinguished Psychologist Award.
        Dr. Munz was an accomplished watercolor painter whose work has been exhibited in England, Canada and several American cities.
        Surviving are his wife, Dr. Letty Pogul Munz; a daughter, Robin; his father, Paul, of Queens; and a brother, Leo, of Queens.

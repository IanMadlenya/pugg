        Sir David Clive Crosbie Trench, a former Governor of Hong Kong, died Sunday at his home in Shillingstone, England. He was 73 years old. The cause of death was not announced.
        Sir David was Governor of Hong Kong from 1964 to 1971. Previously, he served as Britain's High Commissioner for the Western Pacific and as Deputy Colonial Secretary. He was awarded the British Military Cross and the United States Legion of Merit for his exploits behind enemy lines in the Solomon Islands in World War II.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Margaret Gould, of Shillingstone, and a daughter, Katherine Elizabeth, of London.

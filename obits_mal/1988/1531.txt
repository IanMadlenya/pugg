        Harold Eugene Davis, a Latin American scholar and professor emeritus at American University, died of cancer Tuesday at his home in this Washington suburb. He was 85 years old Mr. Davis served on the American University faculty from 1947 until he retired in 1973. He continued to teach part time until the fall of 1986.
        Mr. Davis had been chairman of the history and government department, director of the university's language center, dean of the College of Arts and Sciences and a professor in the schools of international studies and government and public administration.
        He traveled extensively in Latin America and spent sabbaticals there. Among his books were ''Americas in History,'' ''Latin American Social Thought'' and ''Latin American Thought: A Historical Introduction.''
        Born in Girard, Ohio, Mr. Davis grew up in Niles, Ohio, and graduated from Hiram College. He earned a master's degree at the University of Chicago and a doctorate in history at what is now Case Western Reserve University.
        Survivors include his wife, Audrey; a daughter, Barbara Lee Owen of Bethesda, Md.; a sister, Marjorie Pearce of Dunedin, Fla.; a brother, L. Gordon Davis of St. Louis; three grandchildren, and two great-grandchildren.
        Correction:  September 25, 1988, Sunday, Late City Final Edition
        A brief obituary by The Associated Press last Sunday about Harold Eugene Davis, a Latin American scholar and professor at American University, misstated the cause of death. His widow, Audrey, says it was pneumonia, not cancer.

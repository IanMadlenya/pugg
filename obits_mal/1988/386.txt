        Frederick Joseph (Bun) Cook, who starred in the National Hockey League with his brother Bill for the New York Rangers in the 1920's and 1930's, died Saturday at Providence Manor, a retirement home. He was 84 years old.
        Mr. Cook joined the Rangers in 1926, their first N.H.L. season, and played with them until 1936, when he was traded to the Boston Bruins. He and his older brother, who died in 1986, teamed with Frank Boucher to help New York win the Stanley Cup in 1928 and 1933.

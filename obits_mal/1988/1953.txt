        Anthony R. Hamilton, chairman of Avnet, Inc., died of liver cancer on Thursday at St. John's Hospital in Santa Monica, Calif. He was 64 years old and lived in Los Angeles.
        Mr. Hamilton served as chief executive officer of the distributor of semiconductors and other electronic components since 1980.
        In 1957 he formed Hamilton Electro Sales, which became the nation's first semiconductor distributor. Hamilton, based in Los Angeles, was acquired by Avnet, now based in Great Neck, L.I., in 1968. Two years later, when Avnet's electronics division was combined with Hamilton Electro, he became executive vice president of the parent concern, later moving up to vice chairman and chairman.
        He was born in North Bergen, N.J., and attended Columbia College.
        He is survived by his wife, Terry; two sons, Anthony Jr., of Orange County, Calif., and Richard, of Sunnyvale, Calif.; three daughters, Deborah Sword of Los Angeles, Terry Beaton of Delmar, Calif., and Sandra Hamilton of Eugene, Ore., and five grandchildren.

        Malcolm Reybold, an author and retired advertising executive, died Jan. 7 in Glen Cove Community Hospital on Long Island. He was 77 years old and lived in Locust Valley, L.I.
        Mr. Reybold, who was born in Atlanta, was a graduate of Georgia Institute of Technology. He was director of marketing for Foote Cone & Belding and associate director of marketing at J. Walter Thompson Company and McCann-Erickson.
        In 1962 he joined F. W. Dodge, a division of McGraw-Hill, from which he retired as publisher in 1965. In 1975, Saturday Review Press published his novel, ''The Inspector's Opinion: The Chappaquiddick Incident,'' which The New York Times Book Review described as ''a nice piece of camp.''
        Mr. Reybold is survived by three daughters, Jyl Reybold of Oyster Bay, L.I., Nyna Weiss of White Plains and Patricia Reybold of Manhattan.

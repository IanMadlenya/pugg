        William H. Van Rensselaer, executive director of the National Association of State Boards of Accountancy, died of lung cancer Sunday at his home in Darien, Conn. He was 51 years old.
        Mr. Van Rensselaer had held his post for 16 years. Previously, he worked at the American Institute of Certified Public Accountants. He was born in Manhattan and graduated from Hobart College.
        He is survived by his wife, Pamela; two daughters, Schuyler and Kate; his mother, Constance, of East Norwalk, Conn; a brother, Alexander, of Westport, Conn., and a sister, Anne Nelson of South Weymouth, Mass.
        Correction:  May 4, 1988, Wednesday, Late City Final Edition

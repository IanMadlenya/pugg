        Melvin H. Worth Sr., retired president and chairman of Cosmopolitan Mutual Insurance Company, died of cancer of the colon Thursday in Staten Island Hospital. He was 84 years old and lived in Deerfield Beach, Fla.
        Mr. Worth, who was born in Manhattan, worked as an insurance executive with the Manhattan-based company until he retired in 1970.
        He was on the board of Long Island College Hospital in Brooklyn and the First National Bank in Bay Harbor, Fla.
        He is survived by his son, Melvin Jr., of Staten Island, and five grandchildren.

        Collier Platt, a retired lawyer, died of a heart attack Thursday in the Syosset (L.I.) Community Hospital. He was 90 years old and lived in Laurel Hollow, L.I.
        Mr. Platt was a partner in the Manhattan law firm of Bleakley Platt Schmidt Hart & Fritz from 1923 to 1968, when he retired. He was mayor of Laurel Hollow from 1945 to 1949.
        Mr. Platt was born in Manhattan and graduated from Yale University and the Columbia University School of Law.
        Surviving are his wife, Louise; three sons, Henry, of Manhattan, Thomas, of Huntington, L.I. and Graham, of Cold Spring Harbor, L.I.; seven grandchildren, and eight great-grandchildren.

        Douglas E. Graves, who handled development of several major weapon systems in 43 years with the Boeing Company, died of cancer Thursday. He was 70 years old.
        Mr. Graves, who retired in 1983 as vice president and deputy manager of strategic planning and acquisitions for Boeing Aerospace, oversaw development of the air-launched cruise missile, the short-range attack missile, the airborne warning and control system, the E-4 advanced airborne command post and military uses of the 747 jumbo jet.
        He also was assistant program manager for the Minuteman intercontinental nuclear missile.

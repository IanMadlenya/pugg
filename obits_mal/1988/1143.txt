        John Hopkins Heires, an international banker, died of a heart attack Saturday at Suburban Hospital in Bethesda, Md. He was 69 years old and lived in Chevy Chase, Md.
        Mr. Heires spent the decade before his retirement in 1986 as international affairs adviser for the New York Federal Reserve Bank. Earlier he had been a vice president of Marine Midland Bank.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Alice Rea Chamberlin; three sons, John Jr., of Arlington, Va., David Chamberlin of Manhattan, Gregory North of Litchfield, Conn., and a sister, Virginia Tierney of Carroll, Iowa.

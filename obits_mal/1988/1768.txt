        F. Woodbridge Constant, former chairman of the physics department at Trinity College in Hartford died of cancer Wednesday at his home in Essex, Conn. He was 84 years old.
        Dr. Constant joined the Trinity faculty in 1946 as chairman of the physics department, a post he held until 1970. He retired in 1972.  He graduated from Princeton and earned a Ph.D. from Yale.
        He is survived by his wife, Elizabeth Bass; two daughters, Janice Neill of Kailua, Hawaii, and Anne Cohen of Paso Robles, Calif., and a son, Frederic W., of Nicasio, Calif.

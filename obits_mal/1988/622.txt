        Bruce Mair, a retired newspaper executive and editor, died of Alzheimer's disease April 13 at his home in Davis, Calif. He was 68 years old.
        Mr. Mair was the last president of The Newark Evening News, which ended an 88-year existence in 1972 amid heavy losses after an 11-month strike. Earlier, as a reporter and editor in Michigan and Wisconsin, Mr. Mair took part in local politics and was press director in Gerald R. Ford's first, successful campaign for Congress in 1948.
        He is survived by his wife, Adelaide; two stepsons, Mark and Karl Braunlich of Davis; a daughter, Susan Holden of Rye Brook, N.Y.; and three grandchildren.

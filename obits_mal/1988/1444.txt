        William Harmon Brown, a former manager of the New York State office building in Harlem, died of cardiopulmonary arrest on Friday at University Hospital in Washington. He was 72 years old and lived in Washington.
        Mr. Brown, who was a graduate of Howard University, was manager of the Adam Clayton Powell Jr. State Office Building in the mid-1970's. He had also managed the Hotel Theresa, a Harlem landmark.
        He is survived by two sons, Ronald and William, both of Washington; a daughter, Leslie Franklin of Chicago; nine sisters, Ola Jones, of Middletown, Pa.; Elizabeth Keys, Dorothy Winfield and Kaye Jackson of Steelton, Pa.; Rebecca Freeman and Gwendolyn Pettis of Bressler, Pa.; Helen James of Goldsboro, N.C., and Nancy Smith and Charlotte Stewart of Harrisburg, Pa.; a brother, James, of Harrisburg, and two grandchildren.

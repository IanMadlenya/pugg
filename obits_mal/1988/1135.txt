        Peter I. B. Lavan, retired senior partner of the Manhattan law firm of Stroock & Stroock & Lavan, died of heart failure yesterday at his home in Great Barrington, Mass. He was 93 years old.
        Mr. Lavan, who was born in Brooklyn, graduated from Columbia University Law School. He joined the law firm upon graduation.
        In 1956, he was appointed by President Eisenhower as chairman of the United States Committee for the United Nations.
        He was a board member of Columbia Law School, the Berkshire Theater Festival and Simon's Rock of Bard College, where an annual award is presented in his name. He founded the Pidly Fund, which supports halfway houses for the mentally disabled. He also established the Lavan Educational Center for the Performing Arts at the Berkshire Theater Festival and is a former board member of the Unitarian Service Committee.
        Survivors include his wife, Fay; two sons, Spencer, of Harpswell, Me., and Lawrence, of Palm Beach, Fla.; a brother, Morton, of Mahopac, N.Y., and eight grandchildren.

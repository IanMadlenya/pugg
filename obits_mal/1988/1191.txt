        Hubert P. Luckett, retired vice president and editorial director of Times Mirror Magazines and a former editor-in-chief of Popular Science, died of a brain tumor Sunday at his home in Dobbs Ferry, N. Y., He was 71 years old.
        Mr. Luckett was born in Cameron, Tex., and came to New York in 1945, when he was hired by Popular Science as a photographer. He held a succession of editorial posts at the magazine until 1971, when he was named editor-in-chief. Under his direction the century-old magazine, then faltering, was modernized. In 1980 he took over editorial direction of all four Times Mirror periodicals - Popular Science, Outdoor Life, Ski and Golf. He retired in 1984.
        A former president of Union Free School District 3 in Dobbs Ferry, Mr. Luckett also was a village trustee and, at his death, he was a member of the zoning board.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Nancy Bomar Frazer; two sons, James, of Dorchester, Mass., and Daniel, of Yorktown Heights, N.Y.; a daughter, Arleigh, of Gravenhurst, Ont.; a stepson, Terence Dyke of Austin, Tex.; a stepdaughter, Elizabeth Carlson of Croton-on-Hudson, N.Y., and five grandchildren.

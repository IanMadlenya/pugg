        Enzo Fano, an internationally known water-resources specialist at the United Nations, died of a heart attack Thursday at New York Hospital in Manhattan. He was 57 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Dr. Fano was deputy director of natural resources and energy at the United Nations and chief of the water-resources branch. He was in charge of water projects in about 50 developing countries, primarily in Africa. He held a Ph.D. in agricultural sciences from Perugia University in Italy.
        He is survived by his wife, Monah; a daughter, Emily, of Manhattan, and his mother, Yolanda, of Rome, Italy.

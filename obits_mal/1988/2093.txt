        Harold (Jerry) Hopper, who directed films and episodes of the TV programs ''Gunsmoke'' and ''Leave It To Beaver,'' died Saturday of heart failure. He was 81 years old.
        Mr. Hopper worked as a radio script writer and film editor before directing movies in the early 1950's. He began directing television programs in the 1960's.
        He directed 15 films, including ''The Private War of Major Benson,'' ''Pony Express,'' ''The Atomic City,'' ''Never Say Goodbye,'' ''The Toy Tiger'' and ''Naked Alibi.''

        James S. Mooney, a retired New York City Police Inspector, died after a heart attack Tuesday at North Shore Hospital on Long Island. He was 66 years old and lived in Bayside, Queens.
        Inspector Mooney was born in Manhattan and was a graduate of St. John's College and St. John's Law School. He served in the Marine Corps in World War II, rising to the rank of captain. On the police force, he was a detective commander and a specialist in racketeering and organized crime. He retired in 1983.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Mary Lynch; three sons, James 2d, of New Rochelle, William J., of Glendale, L.I., and Brian J. Mooney of Bayside; two sisters, Helen McNamee of Plainview, L.I., and Marianne, of Bayside, and four grandchildren.

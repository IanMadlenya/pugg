        Harry W. Raybin, a pioneer in the development of poison control centers throughout the country, died of pneumonia Friday at his home in Manhattan. He was 87 years old.
        Mr. Raybin had been the technical director of the poison control center of the New York City Health Department from its inception in the late 1950's until he retired in 1975.
        He was born in Bridgeport, Conn., and graduated from Yale University in 1923 with a degree in chemistry. He worked as a chemist for the Federal Bureau of Standards in Washington and in 1929 joined the city Health Department as a chemist.
        He is survived by two daughters, Dr. Peggy Lewin of Jerusalem and Carol, of Manhattan, and three grandchildren.

        Arthur E. Duram, president of the advertising firm of Fuller & Smith & Ross from 1966 until 1975, when he retired, died of bone cancer Tuesday in Norwalk (Conn.) Hospital. He was 76 years old and lived in New Canaan, Conn.
        Mr. Duram held posts in the sales division of the CBS television network from 1946 to 1952, when he joined Fuller & Smith & Ross. The agency, under his leadership, carried out the 1968 media campaign that helped elect Richard M. Nixon to his first term as President.
        Surviving are Mr. Duram's wife, the former Marjorie Barnett, and a son, Michael, of Hadley, Mass.

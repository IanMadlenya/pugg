        Harold Brayman, an author and retired director of public relations at the Du Pont Company, died of heart failure Sunday at his home in Wilmington, Del. He was 87 years old.
        Mr. Brayman was born in Middleburgh, N.Y., and graduated from Cornell University.
        He worked as a syndicated columnist and a Washington correspondent for several newspapers and was president of the National Press Club and of the Gridiron Club. He joined Du Pont in 1942 and retired in 1965.
        He wrote four books, ''Corporation Management in a World of Politics,'' ''Developing a Philosophy for Business Action,'' ''A History of the Lincoln Club of Delaware'' and ''The President Speaks Off the Record.''
        He is survived by his wife, Martha; two sons, Harold, of Washington, and Walter, of Shawnee Mission, Kan., and five grandchildren.

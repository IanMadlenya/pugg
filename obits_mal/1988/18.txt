        Joseph Conlon, a former general manager of the dyestuff chemical division of the GAF Corporation in New York City, died Jan. 11 at the Hospital of the University of Pennsylvania in Philadelphia after a brief illness. He was 76 years old and had lived in West Chester, Pa., since his retirement in 1970.
        Mr. Conlon is survived by his wife, the former Margaret Moriarty; a son, William, of Manhattan; two daughters, Andrea Gersen of Unionville, Pa., and Jane, of Manhattan; a brother, Robert, of Binghamton, N.Y.; five sisters, Frances McGowan of Albany and Teresa Feheley, Florence Day, Virginia Byrnes and Eileen Gallagher, all of Binghamton, and four grandchildren.

        Sam W. Reynolds, a businessman who was appointed a United States Senator and served for seven months, died at home of pneumonia Sunday. He was 97 years old.
        He owned and operated the Reynolds-Updike Coal Company for 76 years until retiring in 1984.
        In 1954, Gov. Bob Crosby appointed Mr. Reynolds, a Republican, to the Senate after the death of Senator Hugh Butler. Mr. Reynolds did not seek election to a full term.
        Mr. Reynolds leaves a brother, three daughters, six grandchildren and three great-grandchildren.

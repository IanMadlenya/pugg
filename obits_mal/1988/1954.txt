        E. C. Kip Finch, a lawyer who served in the Connecticut House of Representatives and who held several posts with Time Inc., died on Thursday at Norwalk (Conn.) Hospital after a long illness. He was 77 years old and lived in New Canaan.
        Mr. Finch, a graduate of Columbia College who received his law degree from Harvard University, joined Time in 1936 and later became general manager of its international editions and assistant to Henry Luce, the editor in chief.
        In 1954, he entered the private practice of law and at his death was a member of the firm of Finch, Makepeace, Van Dyk & Clark, of which he was a founder. In 1959 he was elected to a term in the Connecticut House of Representatives.
        Mr. Finch was a longtime board member of the Save the Children Federation and from 1958 to 1972 was its chairman. He served as chairman of the Community Development Foundation from 1959 to 1973.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Ruth Woodward; three sons, James, of Lakeside, Conn., Peter, of Rowayton, Conn., and Earle, of Gainesville, Fla; three sisters, Margaret Laird of Old Greenwich, Conn., Pauline Warner of Chatham, Mass., and Ruth Simons of Ridgefield, Conn., and nine grandchildren.

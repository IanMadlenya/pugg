        Robert James Lewis, a retired investment banker, died Tuesday at his home in Manhattan after a series of strokes. He was 89 years old.
        Before he retired in 1969, Mr. Lewis was a managing partner of Estabrook & Company. He began his career at the Bankers Trust Company after graduating from Yale College.
        He was a former president of the Bond Club of New York and a former governor of the New York Stock Exchange.
        Surviving are his wife, Helen; a son, Robert E., of Manhattan; a daughter, Ellen Lewis Parisot of Wilton, Conn., and eight grandchildren.

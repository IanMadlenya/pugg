        Dr. Albert H. Holland Jr., former medical director of the Food and Drug Administration, died of cancer Monday at his home in Manhattan. He was 69 years old.
        Dr. Holland, who graduated from the New York University School of Medicine in 1944, was medical director of the F.D.A. from 1954 to 1959. Before that, he worked as medical director of Armour Laboratories in Chicago, and from 1946 to 1950 was director of the Office of Research and Medicine for the Oak Ridge, Tenn., operations of the Atomic Energy Commission.
        He served as a vice president of the American Home Products Corporation from 1960 until his retirement in 1974.
        He is survived by his daughter, Nancy Holland Hawkins of Red Bank, N.J., and three brothers, John, of Vinton, Va., James, of Scarsdale, N.Y., and Thomas, of Basking Ridge, N.J.

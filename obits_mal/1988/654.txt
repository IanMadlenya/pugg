        James S. Berg, who broadcast for the Voice of America for a quarter of a century, died of emphysema Monday at Roosevelt Hospital. He was 78 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Berg, a native of Austria, was a political satirist performing in cabarets before he came to the United States in 1938. He joined the Office of War Information during World War II and began broadcasting to Austria, continuing with the Voice of America when it was established.
        He is survived by his wife, Gertrude.

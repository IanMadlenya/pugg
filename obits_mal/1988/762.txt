        Johannes Lichtenstein, a wine consultant and importer, died after a heart attack Thursday at New York Hospital. He was 62 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Lichtenstein, a native of Vienna and a graduate of the Swiss Hotel School in Lucerne, managed his own hotel, Vila Amanda, on the Italian island of Capri before going to work for Schenley Industries in 1964.
        He became a vice president of the company's wine-importing branch, Dreyfus Ashby Inc. He later became managing director of Laurent-Perrier Inc.-U.S. in New York, in charge of its North American market.
        Mr. Lichtenstein is survived by his wife, Avery; a daughter by a previous marriage, Karin Augusta of Piscataway, N.J., and his mother, Paula, and a sister, Charlotte Huppert, both of Vienna.
        A memorial service is to be held at 2 P.M. June 1 at St. Thomas More Roman Catholic Church, 65 East 89th Street in Manhattan.

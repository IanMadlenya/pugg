        Merrill Pollack, a sculptor, writer and former editor of The Saturday Evening Post, died Sunday at St. Joseph's Hospital in Parkersburg, W.Va., after a short illness. He was 63 years old and lived in Grantsville. His family did not disclose the cause of death.
        Mr. Pollack was also an executive editor for Viking Press and an editor for Simon & Schuster.
        In 1977, he moved to Grantsville, where he made sculptures and wood carvings. From 1981-86, he was editor of The Calhoun County Chronicle, and he was on the art faculty at West Virginia University.
        He is survived by his wife, Jeanne, five children, and a brother.

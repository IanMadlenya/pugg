        Charles W. Millard Jr., retired chairman of the Manufacturers and Traders Trust Company and a former New York State Regent, died Wednesday at Glens Falls (N.Y.) Hospital. He was 83 years old and lived in Schroon Lake.
        Mr. Millard became ill at his home on Tuesday and was taken to the hospital, where he died the following morning. The cause of his death had not been determined yesterday.
        A native of the Bronx, Mr. Millard, was a graduate of Blair Academy and of Princeton. He joined Manufacturers and Traders in 1938, two years after finishing college and rose to become chairman and chief executive officer, spending most of his career in Buffalo. He retired in 1969. In 1956 Mr. Millard was elected to the Board of Regents, the highest educational governing body in the state, serving until 1974, when he reached the mandatory retirement age.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Agnes Tennyson, and a son, Charles 3d, of Chapel Hill, N.C., from his his first marriage to the former Constance Keppler, who died in 1977.

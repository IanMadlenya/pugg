        Dr. Robert L. Yeager, a specialist in the treatment of pulmonary diseases and the head of the health center bearing his name in Pomona, N.Y., died of spine and bone cancer at Summit Park Hospital in Pomona. He was 81 years old and lived in Pomona.
        Dr. Yeager, who was born in Mineral Wells, Tex., was a graduate of the Virginia Military Institute and the University of Pennsylvania Medical School. After contracting tuberculosis, he decided to focus his career on pulmonary diseases.
        In 1942 he became medical director of Summit Park, which specializes in tuberculosis treatment and is now part of the Robert L. Yeager Health Center. Dr. Yeager, a former president of the American Thoracic Society, retired in 1980.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Winifred Doscher; four sons, Robert 3d, of Dallas, Brooks, of Washington, John, of Bridgeport, Conn., and Fabian, of South Pomona, Calif., and six grandchildren.

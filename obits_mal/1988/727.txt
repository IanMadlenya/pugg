        Dr. Robert S. Mendelsohn, a physician, author and critic of the medical establishment, died after a brief illness at his home here April 5. He was 61 years old.
        Among books he wrote criticizing organized medicine was ''Confessions of a Medical Heretic,'' published in 1979. He also wrote a syndicated newspaper column.
        A teacher of pediatrics at the University of Illinois and Northwestern University, he was forced to resign as director of the medical consultation service for the preschool Head Start program after criticizing public schools before a House committee in 1969.

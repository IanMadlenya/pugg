        Adolph E. Meyer, professor emeritus of educational history at New York University, died of pnuemonia Sept. 30 at his home in Champaign, Ill. He was 90 years old.
        Professor Meyer, a native of Manhattan, joined the N.Y.U. staff as an instructor in 1922 and retired in 1963. He wrote about American and European educational history and was the author of several books, including ''An Educational History of the American People'' (McGraw-Hill, 1957) and ''Voltaire: Man of Justice'' (Howell, Soskin, 1945).
        He is survived by his wife, the former Jessie Bryan.

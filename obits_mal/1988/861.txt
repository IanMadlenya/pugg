        Dr. Gabriel Kirschenbaum, a retired Army colonel, died Tuesday of congestive heart failure resulting from leukemia at New York Hospital-Cornell Medical Center in Manhattan. He was 87 years old and lived in Brooklyn.
        Dr. Kirschenbaum was the medical director of the Selective Service in New York City for more than 30 years and a practicing physician in Brooklyn for more than 64 years.
        Surviving are his wife, Zelda Celia Rosenbaum Kirschenbaum; a daughter, Deby Salama of Manhattan; a son, Dr. J. Michael Kirschenbaum of Bernardsville, N.J., and six grandchildren.

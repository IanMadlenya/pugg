        David Stech, a former associate art director of Life magazine, died of a heart attack Wednesday at his home in Cape May Point, N.J. He was 73 years old and also had a home in Armonk, N.Y.
        He worked for Life for 18 years before leaving in 1969 to become art director of On The Sound magazine, which was started by a group of former Time Inc. editors and writers.
        Surviving are his wife, Virginia; three sons, David A., of Fairbanks, Alaska; Harlan W., of Duluth, Minn., and Charles R., of Oak Ridge, N.J.; two sisters, Harriet Sloan of Raton, N.M., and Marion Haisley of Havelock, N.C., and six grandchildren.


       Charles Raper Jonas, a North Carolina Republican who served in the House of Representatives from 1952 to 1972, died of congestive heart failure Friday at his home in Lincolnton, Md. He was 83 years old.  Mr. Jonas, a member of the House Appropriations Committee, was active in efforts to curb growth in government and to achieve a balanced Federal budget.
        Charles Raper Jonas, a North Carolina Republican who served in the House of Representatives from 1952 to 1972, died of congestive heart failure Friday at his home in Lincolnton, Md. He was 83 years old.  Mr. Jonas, a member of the House Appropriations Committee, was active in efforts to curb growth in government and to achieve a balanced Federal budget. He was a graduate of the University of North Carolina, where he also earned a law degree.
        A widower, he is survived by a son, Richard E. Of Denver, N.C.; a sister, Celeste Gibson of Gibson, N.C., ; and six grandchildren.

        Nizar Jwaideh, director of public affairs for the Washington mission of the Arab League and editor of the magazine Arab Perspectives, died Wednesday of pancreatic cancer at George Washington University Hospital in Washington, D.C. He was 55 years old.
        A native of Iraq, Mr. Jwaideh was a foreign correspondent for The Associated Press from 1953 until 1960, then foreign and national editor for The Chicago Sun-Times and diplomatic editor for U.S. News and World Report. He took the Arab League post in 1981.
        He is survived by two sisters, Naila Harper of Madison, Wis., and Olga O'Grady of London.

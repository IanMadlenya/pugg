        R. Bayly Winder, director of the Hagop Kevorkian Center for Near Eastern Studies and former dean of faculty of Arts and Science at New York University, died of cancer Saturday at Princeton Hospital in New Jersey. He was 67 years old and lived in Princeton.
        Dr. Winder was a distinguished Arabist and member of the N.Y.U. faculty for more than 20 years. He joined the university in 1966 and created the Department of Near Eastern Languages and Literatures. In 1967, he established the Middle-East Language and Area Center, now known as the Hagop Kevorkian Center.
        He was an adviser to the Department of Health, Education and Welfare and from 1960 to 1961, he was a consultant on education to the Ford Foundation.
        He was born in Greensboro, N.C., and graduated from Haverford College in 1946. He earned an M.A. and Ph.D. from Princeton University in 1947 and 1950 respectively.
        Dr. Winder is survived by his wife, the former Viola Hitti, and a son, Bayly, of London.
        A funeral service will be held at 11 A.M. Thursday at Trinity Episcopal Church, 33 Mercer Street, Princeton.

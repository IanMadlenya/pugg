        Kenneth B. Willson, former president of the National Better Business Bureau, died of heart failure Monday at Boca Raton Community Hospital in Boca Raton, Fla. He was 90 years old.
        Mr. Willson, who was born in Manhattan and graduated from Columbia University, joined the business bureau in 1925. He became president in 1952 and retired in 1968. He later was an adviser to the Council of Better Business Bureaus in Washington.
        Surviving are his wife, Florence, of Boca Raton; a daughter, Elizabeth Auerbacher, also of Boca Raton, three grandchildren and five great-grandchildren.

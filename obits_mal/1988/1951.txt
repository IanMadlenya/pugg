        Joseph D. Ardleigh, former president of the Research Institute of America, died of spinal cancer at his home in Charlottesville, Va., on Wednesday. He was 74 years old and had been a resident of Greenwich, Conn., for many years.
        Mr. Ardleigh joined the Research Institute in 1937 and was president of the organization, which specialized in tax matters, from 1967 to 1979. At his death he was managing director of Ardleigh Associates of Charlottesville, a consulting firm in graphic design.
        A native of Scranton, Pa., he grew up in Brooklyn and was a graduate of the City College of New York.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Susan Seward; three sons, Paul, of Tiburon, Calif., Carl, of Delray Beach, Fla., and Hugh, of Charlottesville; a daughter, Teri Swift of Charlottesville; a sister, Violet Vogele of Shelburne, Vt., and seven grandchildren.

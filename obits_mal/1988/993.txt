        Peter J. O'Connor, a professor at Fordham University School of Law, died of colon cancer Thursday at his home in Manhattan. He was 59 years old.
        Professor O'Connor, who was an expert in criminal and civil procedure, became associated with the university in 1974. Earlier he had been an assistant district attorney and chief of appeals in Queens County, from 1967 to 1970, and an assistant district attorney in Manhattan under District Attorney Frank Hogan, from 1957 to 1962.
        He was the author of numerous articles for legal publications and was responsible for the revised edition of ''New York Practice and Procedure.'' At his death, Professor O'Connor was working on a new edition of ''New York Motion Practice'' for Little, Brown & Company.
        He graduated from Fordham in 1951 and from its law school in 1956. He earned a master's degree in law from Harvard Law School in 1957.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Agnes Shaker, and a brother, Patrick, of New Orleans.

        Patrick Dolan, a retired international advertising executive, died Dec. 31 as a result of a stroke at St. Joseph's Hospital in Baltimore, Md. He was 76 years old and had residences in Manhattan and Much Hadham, England.
        In 1946 he opened the London advertising office of Foote, Cone & Belding and in 1948 began his own advertising and public relations firms there: Dolan, Ducker, Whitcomb & Stewart and Patrick Dolan Associates, later bought by Batten Barton Durstine & Osborne Inc. Mr. Dolan remained as president of B.B.D.O. International, retiring in 1967.
        Mr. Dolan was born on Waterford Island, Ireland. As a young man he came to Chicago and became a United States citizen. He was an advertising executive at The Chicago Tribune and Columbia Records before serving in World War II. A major in the United States Office of Strategic Services, he was in charge of allied radio stations broadcasting in German from Nazi-occupied territories.
        He received the Silver Star from the United States and the Croix de Guerre from France for his role in the capture of Cherbourg after the invasion of Normandy.
        His wife, the former Britta Salen, died in 1986.
        He is survived by a son, Patrick of Powson, Md., and a daughter, Christina Shiach of London.

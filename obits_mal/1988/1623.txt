        Irving Elias, a businessman and philanthropist, died of pneumonia Saturday at Roosevelt Hospital. He was 88 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Elias founded what is now the Allstate Paper Box Company of Newark in the 1930's. He was an active fund-raiser for a number of Jewish groups and was a founder of Yeshiva University's Sephardic Studies Program.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Anne Negrin; a daughter, Estelle Cooper of Cliffside Park, N.J.; two sons, David, also of Cliffside Park, and Leonard, of Emerson, N.J.; eight grandchildren, and three great-grandchildren.

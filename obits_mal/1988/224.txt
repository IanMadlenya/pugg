        William J. Dempsey, former counsel of the Federal Communications Commission and a communications lawyer, died of heart failure Tuesday at Georgetown University Hospital in Washington. He was 81 years old and lived in Washington.
        Mr. Dempsey, who was born in Brooklyn, received bachelor's, master's and law degrees from Georgetown. He was the son of Representative John J. Dempsey, Democrat of New Mexico, who was also governor of that state.
        In 1937, Mr. Dempsey became assistant general counsel of the Federal Power Commission and later joined the Federal Communications Commission. He joined with William C. Koplovitz to form Dempsey & Koplovitz, a firm specializing in communications law.
        His wife, Mildred, and two sons, William Jr. and John Joseph 3d, are deceased.
        Mr. Dempsey is survived by two daughters, Mary Catherine of Washington and Joanna Walsh of Bethesda, Md.; a sister, Muriel McNeill of Santa Fe, N.M., and six grandchildren.

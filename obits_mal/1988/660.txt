        Richard Goldsmith, a New York philanthropist, died of cancer of the esophagus yesterday at his home in Manhattan. He was 94 years old.
        He served as treasurer of the United Jewish Appeal-Federation of Jewish Philanthropies from 1948 to 1987 and was honorary treasurer at the time of his death. He was a trustee emeritus and former vice president of the Mount Sinai Medical Center. He was a former director of the Horace Mann School, the Big Brothers Association of Philadelphia, the Aircraft Industries Association of America and the Institute of Aeronautical Sciences.
        Mr. Goldsmith, who was born in Manhattan, was president of the family business, the BG Corporation, which manufactured spark plugs for airplanes. He retired in 1964. He is survived by his wife, Jean.  A service is to be held on Sunday at 11 A.M. at the Frank E. Campbell Funeral Chapel, at 1076 Madison Avenue, near 81st Street.

        Bernard Krauss, former president of Esquire Inc., an educational and music publisher, died of cancer Jan. 4 at Memorial Sloan-Kettering Cancer Center in Manhattan. He was 58 years old and lived in Rye, N.Y.
        Mr. Krauss, a native of Chicago, had bachelor's and master's degrees from the University of Chicago. He was president and chief executive officer of Esquire from 1977 to 1984, when he retired.
        He is survived by his wife, Liliane; two daughters, Alexandra and Beatrice, and a son, Andrew, all of Rye, and a sister, Elizabeth Gerber of Skokie, Ill.

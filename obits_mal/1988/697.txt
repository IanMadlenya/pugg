        Edward G. McDonnell, president of a structural steel erecting company that helped build many prominent buildings in New York City, died of heart failure Sunday at New York Hospital. He was 79 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. McDonnell's company, Ermco Erectors of Flushing, Queens, erected the steel for the Jacob K. Javits Convention Center, Lincoln Hospital in the Bronx, One Exchange Place and many office buildings.
        Survivors include his wife, Florence,and two sons, Edward, of Lido Beach, L.I., and the Rev. Richard, of Aiken, S.C.

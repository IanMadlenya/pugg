        Dr. Alexander Gerber, an internist who specialized in cardiology and who did early work in the use of steroids in chemotherapy and organ transplants, died of a heart attack at Central Vermont Hospital in Berlin, Vt., while on vacation. He was 76 years old and lived in Atlantis, Fla.
        Dr. Gerber was chief of staff at Brooklyn Jewish Hospital from 1965 to 1970. He moved to Florida and was on the staff of Good Samaritan and St. Mary's Hospitals in West Palm Beach until his retirement two years ago.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Beatrice Kalish; a son, William, of Manhattan; a daughter, Miriam Kaplan of Germantown, Tenn.; two sisters, Rose Shapiro of Forest Hills, Queens, and Anna Holland of Long Beach, L.I., and three grandchildren.

        John A. B. Faggi, an assistant professor and administrator at Columbia University for more than 30 years, died of emphysema Saturday in St. Luke's Hospital. He was 73 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Dr. Faggi was director of admissions, assistant dean and director of pre-professional studies at Columbia's School of General Studies and director of the university's Foreign Student Center and the Casa Italiana. He was also an assistant professor who taught European history.
        As the first director of the Foreign Student Center in 1961, he helped 2,000 foreign students annually through their years at the university, acting as their principal counselor, supervising their instruction in English as a second language and arranging visits with faculty families during the holidays.
        Dr. Faggi was born in Chicago. He graduated from Princeton University in 1939. He earned an M.A. in 1949, and a Ph.D. in 1954 from Columbia after being named a history instructor at the school in 1952. He also spent a year as a Fulbright Scholar in Italy.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Virginia Xanthos; two daughters, Christina White and Alexandra Faggi, both of Manhattan; two children from a previous marriage, John Faggi Jr. of Oakland, Calif., and Cynthia Krebs of Branford, Mass., and three grandchildren.

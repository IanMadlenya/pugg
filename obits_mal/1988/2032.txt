        Edward M. Benton, a lawyer and counsel to the Hebrew Immigrant Aid Society, died Wednesday at Mount Sinai Medical Center in Manhattan after a long illness. He was 82 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        A 1931 graduate of Harvard Law School, Mr. Benton was for many years a member of the law firm of Finkelstein, Benton & Soll and more recently was with Benton & Benton. He was associated with the Hebrew Immigrant Aid Society for more than 50 years.
        He is survived by a daughter, Alice Chico of Syracuse; a son, Jeremy, of Manhattan; a sister, Florence Freedman of Marple Township, Pa., and three grandchildren.

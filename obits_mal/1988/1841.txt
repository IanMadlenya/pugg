        Dr. Harold B. Hermann, the chief of urology at Maimonides Hospital in Brooklyn from 1945 to 1970, died of a stroke Wednesday at his home in Woodstock, Vt. He was 88 years old.
        Dr. Hermann was a graduate of Cornell University and New York University Medical School. In World War II he commanded the 381st Station Army Hospital in Okinawa.
        His wife, Stella, died in 1986. Surviving are a son, Dr. Hugh P., of Woodstock, seven grandchildren and a great-grandson.

        Hal Lehrman, former president of the Overseas Press Club, died yesterday, apparently of a heart attack, at his Manhattan home. He was 77 years old.
        Mr. Lehrman was a winner of the George Polk Award for Outstanding Foreign Reporting in 1957. He began his career as a reporter for The Associated Press in 1934 and later became a war correspondent.
        He worked at Newsweek, The Daily News and The Herald Tribune. He also worked as a free-lance writer and book reviewer for The New York Times, The New York Post, The Wall Street Journal and Newsday and was a contributor to Life magazine, Commentary, Harper's and the Reader's Digest. He was a founding member of the Overseas Press Club in 1939 and was president from 1967 to 1970.
        He retired in the 1970's but continued to be active with the Council on Foreign Relations of the Rockefeller Foundation, of which he was a longtime member.
        Mr. Lehrman was born in the Bronx and graduated Phi Beta Kappa from Cornell University in 1932.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Annie Sayagh; two sons from a former marriage to the late Freda Jacobsohn, Frederick J., of Seattle, and Hal Jr., of Brooklyn, and four grandchildren.

        Paul M. Hirschland, an investment banker, died of lung cancer Friday at his home in Great Neck, L.I. He was 74 years old.
        He was a vice president of New York Hanseatic Corporation, investment bankers, until 1984, when he joined Strasbourger Pearson Tulcin Wolff, Inc., a securities firm.
        He had been on the boards of the National Cultural Center, the Great Neck Board of Education and the Archaeological Institute of America.
        Surviving are his wife, Ellen; a daughter, Dr. Nancy Ramage, of Ithaca, N.Y.; two sons, Roger, of Washington, D.C. and Edward, of Chicago, and two grandchildren.

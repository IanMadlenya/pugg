        Abraham Zeitz, a fund-raiser and former director of development at Yeshiva University, died Saturday of renal failure at John F. Kennedy Medical Center in West Palm Beach, Fla. He was 76 years old and lived in South Palm Beach.
        Mr. Zeitz, a graduate of City College and Brooklyn Law School, was a fund-raiser for the United Jewish Appeal and the Federation of Jewish Philanthropies before joining Yeshiva. During his tenure at Yeshiva, the university raised money to build its graduate schools of education and social work, the Benjamin N. Cardozo School of Law and the Albert Einstein College of Medicine.
        He is survived by his wife, Ann Kaplan Zeitz; a daughter, Diedre Snow White of Reston, Va.; a son, Carl Zeitz, of Bordentown, N.J.; a sister, Nina Neumark of Brooklyn, and four grandchildren.

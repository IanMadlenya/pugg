        Andrew J. Dempsey, the president and chief executive officer of Times Mirror Magazines, died of lung cancer yesterday at his home in Manhattan. He was 46 years old.  Late last year, Mr. Dempsey was instrumental in negotiating Times Mirror's $167.5 million acquisition of four magazines - Field & Stream, Skiing, Home Mechanix and Yachting - from Diamandis Communications Inc. Times Mirror Magazines also publishes Golf, Popular Science, Ski and Outdoor Life.
        A native of Philadelphia, Mr. Dempsey graduated from Villanova University.
        He moved to New York City in 1965 to join the McCall Publishing Company, and in 1973 became an executive of Norton Simon Industries. He was vice president in charge of finance and administration of the company's Halston Division when he rejoined McCall in 1980 as vice president of circulation.
        Mr. Dempsey joined Times Mirror Magazines as president and chief executive officer in August 1985.
        He was a member of the board of directors of the Magazine Publishers Association.
        Mr. Dempsey is survived by his wife, the former Sylvia Hashagen; a daughter, Elizabeth; and two sisters, Elizabeth White of York, Pa., and Eileen Valdez of Littleton, Colo.
        A funeral mass will be offered at 10:30 A.M. Friday at the chapel of Villanova University.

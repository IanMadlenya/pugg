        W. R. Higginbotham, a former correspondent and news executive for United Press, died Wednesday at District of Columbia General Hospital. He was 74 years old and lived in Fort Worth.
        After working in Europe, Mr. Higginbotham joined the Movietone news-film operation of United Press during World War II and became a vice president of 20th Century-Fox. He was an editor of King Features Syndicate and a consultant for The New York Times News Service.
        Surviving are two sons, James, of Cambridge, Mass., and Thomas, of Fort Worth.

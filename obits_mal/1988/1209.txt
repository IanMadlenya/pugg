        Charles J. Meyers, a former dean of the Stanford University Law School, died yesterday at his home in Denver. He was 62 years old and had been suffering from throat cancer.
        Mr. Meyers, a native of Dallas, was dean of the law school from 1976 to 1981 and had been a professor there since 1962. In 1982, he was named to the President's Intelligence Oversight Board by President Reagan.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Pamela Adams; a daughter, Katherine Garon of Phoenix; a son, George, of Mountain View, Calif.; a brother, James, and his parents, Mr. and Mrs. P.A. Meyers of Houston.

        Paul F. Douglass, a former president of the American University in Washington, died Sunday at the Rutland Regional Medical Center, Rutland, Vt., after a brief illness. He was 83 years old and lived in South Granville, N.Y.
        Dr. Douglass was president of the university from 1941 to 1952. He was also an adviser to Syngman Rhee, President of South Korea, from 1952 to 1956 and a member of the Vermont Legislature from 1933 to 1941.
        He was a graduate of Wesleyan University and received masters and doctoral degrees from the University of Cincinnati.
        He is survived by two sisters, Dr. Ruth Douglass and Anna Haldeman, both of South Granville.

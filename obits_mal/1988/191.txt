        Irving Hochstadter, a chemical engineer who founded Hochstadter Laboratories Inc., died Jan. 8 at the Valley Nursing Home in Westwood, N.J., where he lived. He was 100 years old.
        Mr. Hochstadter founded the company in 1910 and was its president and technical director until 1943, when the laboratories were absorbed by another company. Later, he was a consultant to the Army, Navy and Air Force.
        He is survived by two daughters, Jane Levin of Manhattan and Doris Haas of Solana Beach, Calif., and nine grandchildren.

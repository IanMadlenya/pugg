        Louis Effrat, a sports reporter for The New York Times for nearly 50 years, died yesterday at the Royal Manor Nursing home near his home in Royal Palm Beach, Fla.. He was 78 years old and had recently suffered a heart attack.
        Born on Feb. 21, 1910, on the Lower East Side of Manhattan, Mr. Effrat began work at The Times in 1927 as a city desk office boy.
        He soon moved into sports, writing about baseball, football and basketball. He retired in 1976.  He is survived by his wife, Alice; his son, Albert of Plantation, Fla.; a daughter, Linda Zolet of Commack, L.I., and four grandchildren.

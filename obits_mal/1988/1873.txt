        Thomas Hageman, a noted tenor, voice teacher and flutist, died of complications arising from AIDS on Oct. 15 at his home in Manhattan, according to Gregory Mowery, a longtime friend. Mr. Hageman was 55 years old.
        Mr. Hageman, who graduated from the Curtis Institute, sang with many regional opera companies and symphony orchestras. He was a sales executive for several music companies before devoting himself full-time to teaching voice to Broadway performers, including Raul Julia.
        He is survived by his daughter, Gigi Teeley, of Manhattan; his mother, Janecie Hageman, and a brother, Jere, both of Lafayette, Calif.

        Felix Eyle, a former concertmaster and orchestra manager of the Metropolitan Opera, died of heart failure on July 5 at his home in Hamilton, N.Y. He was 89 years old.
        Mr. Eyle was born in Lvov, in what is now the Soviet Union, and was a violinist with the Vienna Philharmonic Orchestra while studying at the Vienna Academy of Music. He moved to the United States in 1928, and was associate concertmaster of the Cleveland Orchestra before joining the Met as concertmaster in 1945. Mr. Eyle became the company's orchestra manager in 1957, and served in this post until his retirement in 1970. He also taught violin at Colgate University, in Hamilton, from 1973 until 1986.
        Mr. Eyle is survived by his wife, Elizabeth, of Hamilton, and a son, Nicolas, of Syracuse, N.Y.

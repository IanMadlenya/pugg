        Walter H. Framer, a producer of television and radio game shows, died of heart failure Tuesday at his home in Miami Beach. He was 80 years old.
        Mr. Framer began his career as a radio broadcaster in Pittsburgh before coming to New York in the 1930's to write for radio shows. The first game show that he produced, ''Strike It Rich,'' was on radio and television in the 1940's and 1950's.
        Among the other television quiz shows presented by his company, Walt Framer Productions, in the 1950's were ''The Big Payoff,'' ''Exchange for Happiness'' and ''For Love or Money.'' Mr. Framer also produced several Off Broadway plays.
        He is survived by his wife, Evelyn, of Miami Beach; three daughters, Jill Cohen of New York, June Moss of Boca Raton, Fla., and Meri, of Miami Beach; four sons, Ladd and Mory, of Los Angeles, and Gary and Benn, of Miami; a brother, Alan, of Atlanta; a sister, Ethel Finigan, of Pittsburgh; four grandchildren, and three great-grandchildren.

        Alexander K. Reddin, a retired executive-recruiting consultant, died of cancer April 14 at his home in Norwalk, Conn. He was 72 years old.
        He was executive vice president of Balfour Williamson & Company, a merchant banking firm in Manhattan, before establishing his own company, Alex Reddin Associates, in 1980. He retired in 1987.
        Survivors are his wife, Elaine; three daughters, Eve Lennon of Issaquah, Wash., Gillean Nelson of Wilton, Conn. and Dorothy Teplica of Summerville, S.C.; a son, Ian, of Norwalk; two brothers, Jack, of Adelaide, Australia, and Donald, of Lower Templestowe, Australia, and nine grandchildren.

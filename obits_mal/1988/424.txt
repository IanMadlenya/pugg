        Francis E. P. McCarter, senior partner of the law firm of McCarter & English, died of cancer on March 17 at his home in Rumson, N.J. He was 70 years old.
        The McCarter family has been associated with the firm since its founding in 1845 by Thomas N. McCarter. The firm is based in Newark and has offices in Cherry Hill, N.J.; Wilmington, Del.; Boca Raton, Fla., and Manhattan.
        Mr. McCarter, who graduated from Princeton University in 1939 and from Harvard Law School in 1947, joined the firm in 1948. He became a partner in 1950 and retired last September.
        During World War II, he was a lieutenant colonel in the field artillery and served in Italy and Germany. After the war he received the Medalia Merito di Guerra from the Italian Government.
        He was a councilman in Rumson from 1963 to 1980, serving the last five years as the Council president.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Marna Kuelthau; two sons, George, of Little Silver, N.J., and Robert, of Shrewsbury, N.J.; a daughter, Lisa, of Nashville, and three grandchildren.

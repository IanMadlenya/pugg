        Sydney Charles Rappaport, chairman of the department of dentistry at New York Medical College in Valhalla, N.Y., died Saturday at Lenox Hill Hospital after a brief illness. He was 69 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Dr. Rappaport, who retired from private practice in 1988, remained at the college as chairman and professor, a post he had assumed in 1972. As chairman, he was chief of service at Metropolitan Hospital; chief of service at Bird S. Coler Hospital; director of the dental department at Westchester County Medical Center in Valhalla since 1979, and chairman and attending dentist at Lincoln Hospital in Manhattan.
        He was born in Manhattan and was a graduate of City College and the University of Pennsylvania School of Dentistry.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Rosalind Appel; two daughters, Elizabeth A. and Suzanne R. Boydstun, both of Manhattan, and two grandchildren.
        Correction:  December 21, 1988, Wednesday, Late City Final Edition
        The headline yesterday on an obituary of Sydney Rappaport, a professor of dentistry, gave his age incorrectly.
        He was 69 years old, not 63.

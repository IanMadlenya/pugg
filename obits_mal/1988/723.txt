        Paolo Milano, a professor and literary critic, died of Parkinson's disease yesterday in Rome. He was 83 years old and lived in Rome.
        Mr. Milano, a native of Rome, moved to New York after graduating from the University of Rome. He was a professor of Romance languages and comparative literature at Queens College in the 1940's and contributed regularly to The New York Times Book Review. He later returned to Italy, where he was chief literary critic for L'Espresso.
        In 1948, his book, ''Henry James -The Voluntary Exile'' was published in Milan by A. Mondadori. Mr. Milano also edited a book on the Italian author Dante Aligheri, ''The Portable Dante,'' published in 1947 by the Viking Press.
        He is survived by his wife, Rachel, of Manhattan; a son, Andrew, of Scarsdale, N.Y., and three grandchildren.

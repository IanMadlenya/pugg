        Morris Enouch Kaufman, co-founder and chairman of American Mortgage Banking Ltd., died of heart failure March 5 at his home in Floral Park, Queens. He was 50 years old.
        Mr. Kaufman was born in the Bronx and earned a bachelor's degree at New York University. He founded OPOC Computing Inc., a data processing concern. Later, he formed Acker Retail Audits and was co-founder of Hart Systems, which does inventory auditing and data processing for department stores.
        In 1983, he co-founded American Mortgage Banking, which finances residential mortgages, in Westbury, L.I.
        Mr. Kaufman was a former president of the Hebrew Academy of Suffolk County.
        He is survived by his wife, Anita; his mother and father, Ruth and Irving Kaufman of the Bronx; two daughters, Stephanie Kaufman-Maza of Oceanside, L.I., and Marcia, of Floral Park; two sons, Ivan, of Bayside, Queens, and Andrew, of Floral Park; a brother, Stanley, of Coral Gables, Fla., and four grandchildren.

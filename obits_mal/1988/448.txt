        Jerome Katz, a lawyer who specialized in international cases, died of heart failure Monday at his home in Briarcliff Manor, N.Y. He was 75 years old.
        Mr. Katz, a native New Yorker, received a certificate in law from Balliol College at Oxford and worked for the London law firm of Theodore Goddard in the 1930's, returning to New York as the firm's correspondent. In 1936, he worked the divorce of Wallis Warfield Simpson, then the companion of King Edward VIII, whom she later married to become the Duchess of Windsor.
        Mr. Katz is survived by his wife, the former Judeal Gettinger; a sister, Sylvia Parker of Queens; a daughter, Joyce Zern of Ossining, N.Y., and four grandchildren.

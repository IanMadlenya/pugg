        Emanuel London, an advertising executive and an innovator in the field, died of anemia Tuesday at his home in South Orange, N.J. He was 95 years old.
        In the late 1920's, he helped pioneer the use of radio for advertising and was also an early advocate of real-estate advertising in newspapers. He also helped develop the insertion of advertising supplements in newspapers.
        Mr. London, who was born in Newark, began his advertising career in 1919 after serving in the Army Air Corps. He became president of the United Service Agency and the United Advertising Agency of New York and Newark and later was vice chairman of Williams & London Advertising. He retired in 1978 as chairman emeritus of Williams, London, Marchin, Weltman & Associates.
        He is survived by a son, Donald, of South Orange; five grandchildren and seven great-grandchildren.

        Dr. Carl Frederic Schmidt, a renowned pharmacologist and professor emeritus of the University of Pennsylvania's School of Medicine, died of heart and pulmonary failure last Thursday at his son's home in Radnor, Pa. He was 94 years old and lived in Radnor.
        Dr. Schmidt, who received his medical degree in 1918 from the University of Pennsylvania, developed a means for measuring the blood flow of the brain. He also did extensive work in respiratory pharmacology, aerospace medicine and drug addiction.

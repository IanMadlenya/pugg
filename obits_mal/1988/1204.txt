
       Dr. Wiktor Weintraub, an educator and author who specialized in Slavic studies, died of cancer on Thursday at his home in Cambridge, Mass. He was 80 years old. Dr. Weintraub held the Alfred Jurzykowski Chair of Slavic Studies at Harvard University from 1971 until his retirement in 1978. He was a prolific writer in Polish and English, with more than 400 books, articles and essays.
        Dr. Wiktor Weintraub, an educator and author who specialized in Slavic studies, died of cancer on Thursday at his home in Cambridge, Mass. He was 80 years old. Dr. Weintraub held the Alfred Jurzykowski Chair of Slavic Studies at Harvard University from 1971 until his retirement in 1978. He was a prolific writer in Polish and English, with more than 400 books, articles and essays. His most recent book was ''The Poet and the Prophet,'' published in 1982.
        He is survived by his wife, Maria Evelina Zoltowska Weintraub, of Cambridge.

        Donald K. deNeuf, retired president of Press Wireless Inc., the international telecommunications carrier and subsidiary of ITT, died of a heart attack July 24 at St. Mary's Hospital in Waterbury, Conn. He was 81 years old and lived in Southbury, Conn.
        Mr. deNeuf joined Press Wireless in 1930 as a vice president and retired in 1969. The organization was formed in 1929 by The New York Times and other newspapers and news agencies and was acquired by ITT in 1965. Mr. deNeuf subsequently became general manager of the Northeast Radio Corporation, rejoining Press Wireless in 1957 as executive vice president. He became president in 1963. Mr. deNeuf was a member of the Industry Advisory Committee of the Federal Communications Commission.
        In 1960, he was appointed a member of the original Ad Hoc Satellite Communications Committee, which led to the establishment of Comsat.
        Mr. de Neuf was born in Seattle. He attended Columbia University.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Ruth Mohrey; two daughters, Jeanne Lindstrom of Woodbury, Conn., and Carol Moseman of Holland Patent, N.Y.; 11 grandchildren, and a great-grandchild.

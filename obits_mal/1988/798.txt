        Franklin Lane Kauffmann, a novelist whose works include the 1964 best seller ''An Honorable Estate,'' died of emphysema in Glen Arm, Md. He was 66 years old.
        Mr. Kauffmann, the grandson of Franklin K. Lane, a Secretary of the Interior under President Woodrow Wilson, was born in Washington. He graduated from the Choate School and attended Princeton University.
        He wrote under the pen name Lane Kauffmann and published nine books, eight of them novels. Mr. Kauffmann's first book, the mystery novel ''The Perfectionist,'' won an Edgar Allen Poe Award in 1956 as the best mystery by a new writer. Besides ''An Honorable Estate,'' his works included ''Another Helen,'' published in 1968, and ''A Lesser Lion,'' which was adapted for the stage.
        Mr. Kauffmann, who resided in Glen Arm, is survived by his wife, Faith Lilien Kauffmann; by a son, Christopher Lane Kauffmann of Salt Lake City; and by a daughter, Jill Suzanne Kauffmann of East Stroudsburg, Pa. No funeral service was planned.
        The family requested that donations be made to the American Lung Association.

        Harold Lax, a Long Island manufacturer who was active in Zionist causes, died of chronic heart disease while awaiting a heart transplant Monday at the Texas Heart Institute in Houston. He was 64 years old and lived in Old Westbury, L.I.
        Mr. Lax, who was born in Harlem, founded Autronic Plastics Inc. of Westbury, a plastics manufacturing company, in 1953.
        He was a member of the national board of directors of the American Jewish Congress.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Charlotte Hershkovitz; two sons, Steven, of Woodbury, L.I., and Michael, of Oyster Bay Cove, L.I.; a daughter, Michele Lax-Garber of Baltimore, and five grandchildren.

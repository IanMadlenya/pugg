        Richard Storrs Childs, former deputy administrator for planning and administration for the New York City Department of Transportation, died Saturday at his home in Norfolk, Conn., after a brief illness. He was 78 years old.
        Mr. Childs was named deputy administrator in 1966 and remained in the post until 1969. Since then he was associated with the S. W. Childs Management Corporation, financial management consultants in Manhattan.
        Earlier in his career, Mr. Childs was active in publishing. In 1937 he and Samuel W. Craig founded Modern Age Books, one of the first companies to publish paperbacks. The company was dissolved during World War II. He graduated from Yale in 1932. Mr. Childs was chairman of the Jane Coffin Childs Memorial Fund for Medical Research in New Haven, and chairman of the Berkshire Construction Company in Falls Village, Conn.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Dorothy Strauss; four sons, Russell, of Key West, Fla., John, of Brookline, Mass., Richard Jr., of Goshen, Conn., and James, of Baltimore; two daughters, Alice Anderson of Richmond, Va., and Jenny Preston of Cambridge, Mass.; a brother, Edward, of Norfolk; a sister, Barbara Lawrence of Manhattan, and nine grandchildren.

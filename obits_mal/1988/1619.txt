        Alfred J. Marston, president of the Manhattan Downtown Community Council and a former chairman of Community Board 3 on the Lower East Side, died of a heart attack Saturday at his home in Manhattan. He was 64 years old.
        Mr. Marston was an economist with the Port Authority of New York and New Jersey from 1956 to 1979. He was born in Silesia, Poland, grew up in Paris and received a Ph.D. from the University of Paris in 1950. Mr. Marston was also a trustee of the New York Infirmary-Beekman Downtown Hospital and president and a director of the Chatham Towers cooperative apartments.
        Surviving is his wife, the former Vilma Mercaldi.

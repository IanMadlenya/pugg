        William Edwin Hill, an international management consultant, died yesterday at his home in Manhattan after a long illness. He was 78 years old.
        Mr. Hill, a 1932 graduate of Yale University, was the founder in 1958 of William E. Hill & Company, a management consulting concern that eventually became Hayes-Hill, international management consultants. The company is today a subsidiary of the management consulting concern of Towers, Perrin & Company. Mr. Hill was also a member of the board of advisers at the founding of the School of Organization and Management at Yale.
        He received distinguished service awards from the Catholic Youth Organization, the Boys Clubs of America and the Children's Aid Society.
        He is survived by his wife, Jane, of Manhattan; two daughters, Sarah, of Manhattan, and Susan McLaughlin of Cleveland, and three grandchildren.

        James McGlincy, a journalist who worked for several newspapers and for CBS News, died of cancer of the lymph glands on Tuesday at New York University Medical Center. He was 70 years old.
        Mr. McGlincy was a war correspondent for United Press from 1940-49 and later worked for The London Daily Express, The New York Mirror, The New York Post and The New York Daily News, where he received a George Polk Memorial Award in 1954 for metropolitan reporting. In 1950-51, he was Chicago bureau manager for Newsweek. Later, he worked for CBS News and Fairchild Publications until his retirement two years ago.
        He is survived by a son, Hugh, a student, and his former wife, Phyllis McGlincy, both of Manhattan.

        James H. Street, professor emeritus of economics at Rutgers University, died of prostate cancer Monday at John F. Kennedy Medical Center in Edison, N.J. He was 72 years old and lived in Metuchen, N.J.
        Professor Street taught at Rutgers from 1952 until his retirement in 1986, and had served for a time as chairman of its economics department.
        He held bachelor's and master's degrees from the University of Texas and a doctorate from the University of Pennsylvania. He was a Fulbright Scholar in Latin America.
        Professor Street was the author of ''The New Revolution in the Cotton Economy'' and co-author of ''Technological Progress in Latin America'' and ''Latin America's Economic Development.''
        He is survived by his wife, Mabel; a son, John W., of Vienna, Ohio; a daughter, Janet Street Ordonez of the Bronx; a brother, John R., of Richmond, Va.; a sister, Kathryn Hoffman of Euless, Tex., and three grandchildren.

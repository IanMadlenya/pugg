        Thomas P. Hackett Jr., chief of psychiatry at Massachusetts General Hospital, died of a heart attack Saturday while horseback riding. He was 59 years old and lived in Newton, Mass.
        Dr. Hackett headed the hospital's psychiatric consultation service from 1967 to 1976, when he was named chief of psychiatry. In 1977 he was appointed Eben S. Draper Professor of Psychiatry at Harvard Medical School.
        A child prodigy, he became an instructor of physiology at the University of Cincinnati when he was 13 and still in high school. He graduated from the University of Cincinnati and its medical school by the age of 23, and then served as a medical officer at several prisons, including Alcatraz.
        He leaves his wife, Eleanor, and four children: Melissa, Laura, Shelagh and Thomas.

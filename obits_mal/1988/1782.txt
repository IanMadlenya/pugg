        Dr. Robert Hamilton Ball, a former chairman of the English Department at Queens College of the City University of New York and an author, died of a stroke November 10 at Overlook Hospital in Summit, N.J. He was 86 years old and lived in Summit.
        Dr. Ball, who graduated from Princeton University, was a specialist in English drama and theater history, particularly Shakespeare and the Elizabethans. He taught at Princeton from 1929 to 1939 and at Queens College from 1939 until his retirement in 1970. In 1955, he established the chair of American Literature at the University of Ankara, Turkey.
        He wrote several books, including ''Shakespeare on Silent Film,'' published in England in 1968 by George Allen & Unwin Ltd.
        He is survived by his wife, Esther Marshall Smith Ball, of Summit; a daughter, Marcia Weatherstone, of Summit, and two grandchildren.

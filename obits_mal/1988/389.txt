        Dr. Daniel Woolf Jr. died of a heart attack on March 14 as he was driving near Springfield, N.J. He was 77 years old and lived in Summit, N.J.
        Dr. Woolf, a graduate of the Columbia University School of Optometry, taught at the State University of New York's College of Optometry and at Columbia and was a co-founder of the SUNY Optometric Center of New York.
        He is survived by his wife, Sylvia; two daughters, Renee Laster of Livingston, N.J., and Lynn Lamberg of Union, N.J.; two brothers, Robert, of Red Bank, N.J., and Irwin, of Cherry Hill, N.J.; a sister, Carol Dickstein of Manhattan, and four grandchildren.

        John A. Livingston, a naval architect and marine engineer, died of heart failure Tuesday at the Maine Medical Center in Portland. He was 85 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Livingston was born in Manhattan. He was president of the Goldschmidt Chemical Company from 1943 to 1983.
        Mr. Livingston designed the rudders used on Liberty ships during World War II. He was honorary vice president of the Society of Naval Architects and Marine Engineers.
        Surviving are his wife, Georgette, and his stepdaughter, Georgetta Lordi of Manhattan.

        Albert J. Rosen, a retired shirt manufacturer, died of heart failure Monday at his home in Hewlett Bay Park, L.I. He was 86 years old.
        He was president of the Quaker Shirt and Yale Shirt Corporations, both businesses of his family. He left the companies 30 years ago and became a vice president of Gimbels. He was an arbitrator in union disputes in the garment industry for the American Arbitration Association.
        Surviving are his wife, Millicent; two daughters, Patricia Kaplan of Branford, Conn., and Anne Rosen Noran of Manhattan, and three grandchildren.

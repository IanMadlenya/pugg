        Lewis B. Cuyler, retired senior vice president and director of personnel relations for the First National City Bank, now Citibank, died of congestive heart failure and kidney failure on Wednesday at his home in Princeton, N.J. He was 85 years old.
        Mr. Cuyler joined the bank's credit department in 1928, and was made vice president in charge of personnel after he returned from serving in the Army Air Force in World War II. In 1958 his duties were broadened to include staff relations and employee morale. He retired in 1965.
        In 1962 Mr. Cuyler was elected president of the Foundation for Child Development, formerly the Association for the Aid of Crippled Children. He served for five years as the agency's full-time chief executive.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Margery P. Merrill; two sons, Lewis C., of North Adams, Mass., and G. Grenville, of Charlestown, Mass.; two daughters, Juliana McIntyre of Princeton and Margery Perkins of West Norwalk, Conn., and five grandchildren.

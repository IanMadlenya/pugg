        Horace A. Hildreth, whose career as lawyer, broadcast executive, college president and two-term governor of Maine was capped with four years' service as an ambassador, died today. He was 85 years old. He suffered a heart attack while at Maine Medical Center undergoing an examination for a blood pressure problem, said his son, Horace Hildreth Jr. In 1953, President Dwight D. Eisenhower named Mr. Hildreth ambassador to Pakistan. At that time, Mr. Hildreth had been president of Bucknell University for four years.
        He had been governor from 1945-49. In 1948, he ran for the Senate, finishing second to Margaret Chase Smith in a four-way Republican primary contest.
        Mr. Hildreth, a Gardiner native, was a graduate of Bowdoin College and Harvard Law School. He practiced law in Portland and began his Hildreth Network of Maine radio and TV stations before entering politics.
        Mr. Hildreth is survived,in addition to his son, by his wife, Katherine, and three daughters.

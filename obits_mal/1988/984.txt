        Dr. Andrew Schildhaus, director of orthopedic surgery at Bronx Lebanon Hospital Center since 1968, died May 29 at Mary Fletcher Hospital in Burlington, Vt., where he had been moved after suffering a heart attack and lapsing into a coma in Boca Raton, Fla., on May 14. He was 73 years old and had moved to Boca Raton last winter.
        Dr. Schildhaus, who lived for many years in Roslyn, L.I., received his bachelor's and medical degrees from the University of Vermont and he did graduate work at the University of Pennsylvania. He served in the Army Medical Corps in World War II.
        Dr. Schildhaus also set up residency programs in orthopedics at several New York hospitals before going to Bronx Lebanon. He also was an associate professor at the Albert Einstein College of Medicine.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Ethel Pearl; two daughters, Sandra Burt of Orangeburg, S.C., and Rhonda Kost of Burlington, and a granddaughter.

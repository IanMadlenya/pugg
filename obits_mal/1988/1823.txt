        Dr. Ozzie G. Simmons, a sociologist and anthropologist who lectured and conducted research at several educational institutions, died of lung cancer Saturday at Pascack Valley Hospital in Westwood, N.J. He was 69 years old and lived in River Vale, N.J.
        Dr. Simmons was most recently affiliated with Fordham University's Hispanic Research Center, which he joined in 1981. Earlier he had worked in the United States and abroad for the Smithsonian Institution, Harvard University, the University of Colorado, Brandeis University and the Ford Foundation.
        Dr. Simmons was born to American parents in Winnipeg, Manitoba. He graduated from Northwestern University and received master's and doctoral degrees from Harvard. He served in the Army Air Forces for four years during World War II.
        In 1962, Dr. Simmons received the Hofheimer Prize from the American Psychiatric Association for ''outstanding creative research in human behavior.'' Among his several books and monographs was ''Perspectives on Development and Population Change in the Third World,'' just issued by the Plenum Publishing Company.
        Dr. Simmons is survived by his wife, the former Charlotte Sonenklar; three daughters, Gregor, of Manhattan; Paula, of Rochester, and Lauren White of North Reading, Mass., and two grandchildren.

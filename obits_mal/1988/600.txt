        Frank A. Tomaino, the founder of Rhinelander Florist who sold flowers to the Rockefellers, Astors and Whitneys, died of emphysema and cancer Thursday at the St. Francis Gardens Nursing Home in Albuquerque, N.M. He was 85 years old. Mr. Tomaino, who was born in Deal, N.J., spent his childhood in Calabria, returning to the United States as a gardener for Huntington Hartford. He opened Rhinelander Florist in the Rhinelander Mansion, at 72d Street and Madison Avenue in Manhattan, in 1936.
        He is survived by his wife, Ida, of La Jolla, Calif.; a son, Frank, of Manhattan; a daughter, Peggy Hayden of Albuquerque, and five grandchildren.

        Dr. Sidney Harold Ingbar, an authority on the physiology of the thyroid gland and its clinical diseases who taught at the Harvard Medical School for most of his career, died of lung cancer on Thursday at Beth Israel Hospital in Boston. He was 63 years old and lived in Brookline, Mass.
        Dr. Ingbar was a native of Denver and a graduate of the Harvard Medical School. A specialist in endocrinology, he practiced in Boston before joining the Harvard faculty in 1955, eventually becoming the William B. Castle Professor of Medicine as well as chief of endocrinology at Boston City and Beth Inrael Hospitals.
        He was the author of more than 350 scientific articles and other publications, on topics ranging from public health to basic physiology and molecular biology. Most of his laboratory work dealt with control of thyroid cell growth and the way the thyroid hormone is used in the body.
        He was a former president of the American Thyroid Association and of the Endocrine Society, and received the society's Ernest Oppenheimer Award.
        Dr. Ingbar is surivived by his wife, the former Mary Gimbel Mack; three sons, Dr. David Ingbar of Branford, Conn., Eric Ingbar of Laramie, Wyo., and Dr. Jonathan Ingbar of Dallas; a sister, Daveen Stein of Los Angeles, and a grandson.

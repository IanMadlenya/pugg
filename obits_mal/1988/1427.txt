        Milton Waltcher, director of administrative services for Automated Data Processing Inc., died of skin cancer yesterday at St. Clare's Hospital in Denville, N.J. He was 69 years old and lived in Madison, N.J.
        Mr. Waltcher was born in Newport, R.I., and held an engineering degree from the University of Rhode Island. After service in the Army during World War II and in the United States Military Government in Germany, he retired as a lieutenant colonel.
        In 1949, he joined General Analine and Film as an industrial engineer. He later held executive posts with Fairchild Camera and Instrument and the Hamilton-Standard division of United Aircraft. From 1970 to 1974, he was management services director of The New York Times. He joined Automated Data Processing the next year.
        Mr. Waltcher is survived by his wife, the former Edith Hoier; two sons from a previous marriage, Stephen, of Marquette, Mich., and Craig, of Los Angeles; a sister, Lillian Matthews of Royal Palm Beach, Fla., and a brother, Irving, of Jamaica, Queens.

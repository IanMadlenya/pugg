        Lucien Obed Hooper, a retired securities analyst who was known as the dean of Wall Street market-letter writer, died of heart failure Monday at the Valley Nursing Home in Westwood, N.J. He was 92 years old and lived in Ho-Ho-Kus, N.J.
        Mr. Hooper, who wrote a newsletter for almost 63 years, was a founder and former president of the Security Analysts Federation and past president of the New York Society of Security Analysts.
        He was director of research, senior analyst and vice president at W. E. Hutton & Company from 1941 to 1974. Mr. Hooper wrote a column for Forbes Magazine for almost 30 years.
        He was also mayor of Westwood, from 1934 to 1937.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Bernadine Oeffner; a daughter, Lois Gould of Paramus, N.J.; two sisters, Christine Boissivan of Portland, Me., and Alysan Hooper of Biddeford, Me.; a granddaughter, and a great-grandson.

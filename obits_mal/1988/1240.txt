        Nils Gosta Sahlin, former president of Quinnipiac College in Hamden, Conn., died of heart disease Thursday at the Sound View Specialized Care Center in West Haven, Conn. He was 88 years old and lived in New Haven.
        Born in Stockholm, he earned a bachelor's degree and a Ph.D. in Germanic languages and literature from Yale University. He was a professor of German at Russell Sage College in Troy, N.Y., from 1939 to 1943. He was president of Quinnipiac from 1956 to 1968.
        He is survived by his wife, Johanna; two sons, Nils Jr., of Sydney, Australia, and Clifford, of New Haven; a daughter, Nicki, of Providence, R.I., and a sister, Olga Cronberger of Karlshamn, Sweden.

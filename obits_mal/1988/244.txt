        Dr. Jonathan T. Lanman, a pediatrician who served in a high research post at the National Institutes of Health, died of a lung ailment Monday at his home in Bethesda, Md. He was 70 years old.
        Dr. Lanman directed the Center for Research for Mothers and Children, a part of the National Institute of Child Health and Human Development, from 1976 to 1978. He had previously been associate director of the biomedical division of the Population Council for four years.
        He was a native of Columbus, Ohio, and a graduate of Hotchkiss, Yale and the Yale Medical School. After serving as a medical officer in the Navy in China during World War II he was a United States Public Health Service fellow at the University of California.
        He taught at New York University while practicing at University and Bellevue Hospitals and in 1960 became chairman of the Department of Pediatrics at the Downstate Medical Center in Brooklyn. In 1972 he returned to N.Y.U. as professor of pediatrics.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Janet Edgerton; a daughter, Jacqueline Sheehan of Washington; a son, Jonathan Jr., of Manhattan; a sister, Harriet L. Fulton of Columbus, and two brothers, William, of Tequesta, Fla., and Henry, of Vero Beach, Fla.

        Albert S. Roe, a professor emeritus of art history at Cornell University, died of a brain hemorrhage on Dec. 19 at Tomkins Community Hospital in Ithaca, N.Y. He was 74 years old and lived in Ithaca.
        Born in Manhattan, Professor Roe graduated from Princeton University and earned a doctorate from Harvard University. He taught at Bowdoin College, Swarthmore College, the University of Pennsylvania and the State University of New York before joining Cornell as chairman of the Department of Fine Arts in 1961. He retired in 1984.
        Surviving are his wife, Prof. Daphne Roe of Cornell; a daughter, Laura, of London; two sons, David, of Portland, Ore., and Adrian, of Washington, and one grandson.

        Lou Boda, a sports broadcaster on radio and television for more than 35 years before he retired last July, died Friday night after a heart attack. He was 60 years old.
        Mr. Boda, who began his career in Dover, Ohio, worked for NBC and ABC in New York and helped found WFAN, the all-sports radio station, in 1987. In 1983, he received an Eclipse Award for outstanding achievement in thoroughbred racing broadcasting.
        He is survived by three sons, Mark of Mount Vernon, N.Y.; Craig of Flagler Beach, Fla., and Scott of Tampa, Fla.; four grandchildren; three brothers, and a sister.

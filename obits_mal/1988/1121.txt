        Samuel Huntington, a lawyer and electric utility executive, was killed by lightning Tuesday in Aspen, Colo., while hiking. He was 49 years old and lived in Newton, Mass.
        Mr. Huntington, who had been president and chief executive of New England Electric System since 1984, was attending the annual energy policy forum of the Aspen Institute for Humanistic Studies.
        He was a graduate of the Columbia University School of Law and a former associate at the Manhattan law firm of Hughes, Hubbard & Reed. He was formerly an assistant to the United States Solicitor General and an assistant professor at Boston University School of Law.
        He joined the New England Electric System in 1976 as assistant general counsel and was a vice president from 1979 to 1984.
        His father, Prescott B. Huntington, a lawyer who was a Republican New York State Assemblyman, died March 24 of this year. He was 82 and lived in St. James, L.I. The elder Mr. Huntington was a partner in the law firm of Choate, Reynolds, Huntington & Hollister in Manhattan and served as an Assemblyman from Suffolk County from 1957 to 1970.
        Mr. Huntington is survived by his wife, Jennifer; two children, Henry and Clare, all of Newton; his mother, Sarah Powell Huntington, of St. James, L.I.; two brothers, Francis and Lawrence, both of Manhattan, and two sisters, Sarah H. Fletcher of Locust Valley, L.I., and Susan H. Fisher of Greenwich, Conn.

        Major Robinson, a journalist and public relations figure who helped originate Jet magazine, the Johnson Publishing Company's pocket-sized weekly, died Tuesday at Veterans Hospital in Manhattan. He was 70 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Robinson wrote news stories and gossip columns for major black newspapers in the East in a career that spanned four decades. He originated Jet magazine's ''New York Scene'' column. As a public relations man he represented Atlantic Records, Brook Benton, Roberta Flack, Aretha Franklin, Clyde McPhatter, Jackie Wilson, Sarah Vaughan and Muhammad Ali.
        Mr. Robinson is survived by a son, Major Jr.; a daughter, Debra; a sister, Rosanna Robinson, and four grandchildren. Funeral services will be held today at 10 A.M. at Convent Avenue Baptist Church, on Convent Avenue at 145th Street.

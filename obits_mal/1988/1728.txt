        Thaddeus J. Dulski, an accountant and a former eight-term Congressman from the Buffalo area, died of leukemia Tuesday at the Roswell Park Institute in Buffalo. He was 73 years old and lived in Buffalo.
        Mr. Dulski, a Democrat, attended the University of Buffalo and was a local Councilman before winning a seat in the House of Representatives in 1958. He served as a member, and later chairman, of the Post Office and Civil Service Committee of the House.
        He did not seek re-election in 1974 and was a special assistant to Governor Hugh L. Carey, lobbying Congress for funds for New York State. He retired in 1983 but remained active in local politics.
        He is survived by his wife, Elizabeth; three daughters, Suzanne, Christine and Diane Tyler; two sons, Anthony and Leon; and six grandchildren, all of Buffalo.

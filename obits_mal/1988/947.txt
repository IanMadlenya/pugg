        George R. Brown, a longtime director of news at WOR Radio, died of pancreatic cancer June 14 at his home in Rockville Centre, N.Y. He was 79 years old.
        Mr. Brown joined WOR in 1944, serving as a writer and then editor; he was director of news from 1956 until his retirement in 1974. He was also, at various times, news director of WOR Television, vice president and director of programming for WOR Radio and news director of WOR's parent organization, RKO General Broadcasting.
        He covered the Eichmann trial in 1961, among other events, and won an Emmy award in 1968 for a television documentary on air traffic jams.
        Mr. Brown taught journalism at New York University and at the New School for Social Research. In 1982, he was inducted into the Museum of Broadcasting Hall of Fame.
        He is survived by his wife, Emily; a sister, Ethel, of New York; a son, Nicholas, of Hoboken, N.J.; a daughter, Susan B. Getting of Port Washington, N.Y.; a sister, Ethel, of New York, and three grandchildren.

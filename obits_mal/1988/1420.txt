        William H. Shaw, a retired economist for E.I. du Pont de Nemours & Company who was an Assistant Secretary of Commerce for Economic Affairs in the Johnson Administration, died of cancer Sept. 10. He was 79 years old and lived in Annapolis, Md..
        Mr. Shaw joined Du Pont in 1953 after spending 13 years with the Commerce Department in Washington. In 1966 he was appointed to the sub-Cabinet post. He returned to Du Pont in 1968 and served a term as president of the American Statistical Association before his retirement in the early 1970's.
        A native of Brooklyn, he received his bachelor's, master's and doctoral degrees from Columbia University.
        Mr. Shaw is survived by his wife, the former Rose Durkin; two sons, Peter, of Stockholm and Kevin, of Upper Marlboro, Md.; two daughters, Moira Dehn of Davidsonville, Md., and Elizabeth Shaw of Oakland, Md., and six grandchildren.

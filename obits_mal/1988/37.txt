        Cary Elliott Odell, a movie art director and three-time Academy Award nominee, died of cancer Tuesday. He was 77 years old.
        Mr. Odell's career began as a sketch artist in 1935 at Columbia Pictures. He was promoted to art director and worked on Frank Capra's 1937 film classic, ''Lost Horizon.''
        Mr. Odell was nominated for Academy Awards as art director for ''Cover Girl'' in 1944, ''Bell, Book and Candle'' in 1958 and ''Seven Days in May'' in 1964.

        Lawrence M. Orton, a planner and former member of the New York City Planning Commission, died of Alzheimer's disease Saturday at the Hartwyck West Nursing Home in Cedar Grove, N.J. He was 89 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Orton, a 1923 graduate of Cornell University, was an original member of the commission when it was formed in 1938 and its vice chairman at his retirement in 1969. He was also a member of the Committee on the Regional Plan of New York and its Environs and general director of the Regional Plan Association.
        In June, the New York Metropolitan Chapter of the American Planning Association created an award in his honor.
        He is survived by his wife, Anne; a daughter, Janet Fontaine, of Denver; a son, Lawrence F., of Binghamton, N.Y.; a brother, Douglas, of Tequesta, Fla; six grandchildren, and three great-grandchildren.

        David Muss, a real-estate developer who specialized in enclosed shopping malls, died of heart failure Saturday at New York Hospital in Manhattan. He was 74 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Muss was born in Cape Town and immigrated to the United States as an infant. He graduated from New York University and its law school. After some years as a lawyer, he founded Winston & Muss, a residential and commercial real-estate firm.
        From 1961 to 1968, the firm built several malls, including the Walt Whitman Shopping Center in Huntington, L.I.; the Moorestown Mall in Moorestown, N.J.; the Smithaven Mall in Smithtown, L.I., and the Livingston Mall in Livingston, N.J. After he retired from Winston & Muss in 1968, Mr. Muss developed the Sunrise Mall in Massapequa, L.I.
        Surviving are his wife, Joan; a son, Joshua, of Washington; two daughters, Helene Harpman of Oklahoma City and Alison Blumenfeld of Manhattan; two brothers, Charles and Hyman, both of Queens; three sisters, Lillian November and Bessie Markofsky of Palm Beach, Fla., and Ida Bobrow of Queens, and seven grandchildren.

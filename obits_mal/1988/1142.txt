        Rabbi Joel S. Geffen, a staff member at the Jewish Theological Seminary of America for more than four decades, died Saturday at Roosevelt Hospital after a short illness. He was 85 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Rabbi Geffen joined the seminary staff in 1944 as associate director of field activities and became the department's director a few years later. He retired three years ago.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Sylvia Mintz; two daughters, Lisa Schlesinger of Roslyn Estates, L.I., and Dr. Rela Monson of Philadelphia; four sisters, Lottie Simon of Atlanta, Annette Raskas of St. Louis, Mo., Helen Ziff of Minneapolis and Bessie Wilensky of New Orleans; three brothers, Louis, of Atlanta, Rabbi Samuel, of Forest Hills, Queens, and Dr. Abraham Geffen of New Rochelle, N.Y.; five grandchildren, and five great-grandchildren.

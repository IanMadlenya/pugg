        A. Hawley Peterson, a retired New York investment counselor and stockbroker, died Monday at his home in Naples, Fla. He was 83 years old.
        He was a founding partner of the predecessors of the brokerage firm of Roberts, Rutter & Company, now defunct. While a student at Yale University, he was a member of the United States heavyweight crew that won a gold medal in the 1924 Olympics in Paris.
        He is survived by his wife, Ninette; a son, Aubrey, of East Hampton, L.I.; a daughter, Karen Howard Earle of Manhattan; a sister, Millicent McCann of Pottstown, Pa., and five grandchildren.

        Malcolm Monroe, president of the Jay R. Monroe Memorial Foundation, a philanthropic organization with headquarters in Millburn, N.J., died of intestinal cancer Nov. 21 at Overlook Hospital in Summit, N.J. He was 71 years old and lived in Short Hills, N.J.
        Mr. Monroe was vice president for export of the Monroe Calculating Machine Company, founded by his father, Jay Rudolph Monroe, with headquarters in Orange. When the business was acquired by Litton Industries Inc. in 1958, Mr. Monroe became president of the family-financed foundation and directed its investment program.
        Mr. Monroe was born in Maplewood, N.J.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Dorothy Wheeler; a sister, Marjorie D'Aprix of Montclair, N.J.; a son, Jay, of Watchung, N.J.; a daughter, Martha Morrow, of Greenwich, Conn., and four grandchildren.

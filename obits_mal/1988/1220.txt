        Frank Gullino, a violinist, died of bladder cancer yesterday at his home in Lido Beach, L.I. He was 73 years old.
        Mr. Gullino was a violinist with the New York Philharmonic for more than 37 years, and often performed as a soloist. He gave his first recital at age 9, led a chamber-music group at 14, and became concertmaster of the New York Civic Symphony at 16. At 17, he was accepted by the Metropolitan Opera Orchestra after an audition.
        Mr. Gullino joined the Philharmonic in 1942. He was named assistant concertmaster in 1962 and associate concertmaster in 1971. He also served as concertmaster of the New Orleans Philharmonic from 1981 to 1986.
        Mr. Gullino is survived by his wife, Candis Hall Gullino; a sister, Adrianne Klun of Brookhaven, L.I.; 7 step-children; 18 grandchildren, and a great-grandchild.

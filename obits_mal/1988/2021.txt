        William Dunbar Griffith, a noted ballet teacher, died on Nov. 12 at the Cleveland Clinic. He was 60 years old.
        Mr. Griffith died of pneumonia, said the executor of his estate, Timothy C. Kelly.
        He was born in Brooklyn, and trained as a concert pianist before switching to ballet at the age of 21. In the 1960's and 70's, Mr. Griffith taught company classes for American Ballet Theater, the Joffrey Ballet and for Ballets USA, founded by Jerome Robbins. In this decade, he taught classes at the Dance Theater of Harlem.
        Mr. Griffith also served as a guest teacher at companies around the world, among them the Royal Ballet in London, the Ballet Academy of Stockholm, the Atlanta Ballet, Ballets San Juan and the Compagnie de Danse in Montreal. He also served as the coach to the principal dancers in the Herbert Ross film ''Nijinsky.'' At the time of his death, he was the master teacher for the Cleveland Ballet.

        John W. Heim, who developed a biological handbook while employed at a medical research laboratory at Wright-Patterson Air Force Base, died here Wednesday. He was 85 years old. No cause of death was given.
        Mr. Heim, seeing the need for a handbook to categorize and classify all biological data, persuaded the National Research Council of the National Academy of Sciences in 1950 to oversee the project. The book, the Handbook of Biological Data, was published in 1958.

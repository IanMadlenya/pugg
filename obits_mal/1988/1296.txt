        Aloysius Mazewski, president of the Polish American Congress since 1967, died of a heart attack on Wednesday at his summer home in Portage, Wis. He was 72 years old and lived in Northbrook, Ill., a Chicago suburb.
        Mr. Mazewski had also been president of the Polish National Alliance, a mutual benefit society and fraternal insurance group with more than 300,000 members, since 1966.
        As head of the Polish American Congress Mr. Mazewski was a frequent spokesman on Polish-American concerns, strongly supporting the Solidarty union movement and Pope John Paul II's efforts to ease tensions within Poland.
        Mr. Mazewski is survived by his wife, the former Florence Heider; a daughter, Marilyn; a son, Aoysius Jr., and two grandchildren, all of Northbrook.

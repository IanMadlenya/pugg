        Isaac Rabinowitz, professor emeritus of biblical and Hebrew studies at Cornell University and an authority on the Dead Sea Scrolls, died Sept. 11 at Tompkins Community Hospital in Ithaca, N.Y., after a brief illness. He was 79 years old and lived in Ithaca.
        Dr. Rabinowitz was the author of over 40 articles, including works on the Dead Sea Scrolls, ancient Semitic inscriptions and medieval and Arabic texts. He joined the Cornell faculty in 1957 and retired in 1975, but continued to teach after his retirement.
        He was executive director of the East New York Y.M.-Y.W.H.A. Associations in Brooklyn from 1946 to 1955. Earlier he directed B'nai B'rith Hillel Foundations at the University of Michigan, University of Pennsylvania and Brooklyn College.
        He was born in Brooklyn and graduated from the University of California at Berkeley. He received a doctorate from Yale University.
        He is survived by a son, Joel, of Ithaca; two daughters, Susannah Rubenstein of Brooklyn and Abigail Geman of Amherst, Mass.; a brother, Wilson G., of Berkeley, Calif.; a sister, Hannah Weiser of Houston, and seven grandchildren.

        Nick Pietrosante, a former running back at Notre Dame and with the Detroit Lions, died today of cancer. He was 50 years old.
        Mr. Pietrosante was the Lions' No. 1 draft choice in 1959 and was the National Football League Rookie of the Year that season. He played seven seasons with Detroit and two with the Cleveland Browns before retiring to private business in the Detroit suburbs.
        Mr. Pietrosante's best season was 1960, when he rushed for 872 yards.
        Mr. Pietrosante is survived by his wife, Geraldine; two daughters; a son; his parents, and a brother.

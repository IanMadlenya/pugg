        Walter G. Stackler, a Long Island builder, died Friday at a nursing home in Delray Beach, Fla., after a long illness. He was 83 years old and maintained residences in Gulf Stream, Fla., and Roslyn Harbor, L.I.
        In 1933 Mr. Stackler went into the home-building business with his stepbrother, Leonard L. Frank. Their partnership, Stackler & Frank, put up a small number of single-family houses in Springfield Gardens, Queens, as its first major project. Over the year, the firm has built more than 8,000 houses in Queens, Nassau and Suffolk Counties.
        The partners also built and owned one of Long Island's largest regional shopping centers, the Mid-Island Shopping Plaza in Hicksville. It was sold to the Delco Realty Corporation in 1986, according to Mr. Stackler's eldest son, Robert W. Stackler.
        The father was president of the Long Island Home Builders Institute in 1953 and 1954 and of the New York State Builders Association in 1970, and he was a life director of the National Association of Home Builders In addition to his son Robert, of Hicksville, he is survived by his wife, the former Ida M. Eberlin; another son, Walter L., of Centre Island, L.I., five grandchildren and three great-grandchildren.

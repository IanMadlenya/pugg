        Edwin W. Olson, a director of construction at Columbia University, died Thursday of liver cancer at Cabrini Hospice in Manhattan. He was 67 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        From 1979 to 1985, he oversaw construction of several Columbia buildings and the rebuilding of the McKim, Mead & White plaza and steps leading to Low Memorial Library. He joined Columbia in 1970 after 25 years in the construction industry.
        Surviving are his wife, Sona; two sons, Edwin Jr. of Mitchell, S.D., and Jeffrey, of Burbank, Calif.; a daughter, Deborah Imus of Mitchell, S.D.; a sister, Dorothy, of Staten Island; two stepsons, Julio and Marcos Caro of Manhattan, and four grandchildren.

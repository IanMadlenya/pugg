        R. Gerald McMurtry, a history professor who assembled a collection of Abraham Lincoln's possessions, letters and manuscripts while directing the Louis A. Warren Lincoln Library and Museum here, died Saturday. He was 82 years old.
        Mr. McMurtry was appointed director of the library and museum in 1956, and held the position for 17 years. The center was established in 1928 by the Lincoln National Life Insurance Company. Mr. McMurtry wrote more than 200 books, pamphlets and magazine articles about Lincoln.

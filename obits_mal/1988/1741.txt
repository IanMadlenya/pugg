        Thomas F. Power Jr., a career diplomat with the United Nations and director of the United Nations Development Program for 26 years, died of lung cancer Thursday at the Northern Westchester Hospital Center in Mount Kisco, N.Y.. He was 72 years old and lived in Somers, N.Y.
        Mr. Power was present at the founding of the United Nations in San Francisco in 1945 as a staff aide to Eleanor Roosevelt and later became Deputy Secretary General to the United States Mission. In 1950 he became director of the development program and held the position until his retirement in 1976. He also served as member of the Council on Foreign Relations, and as a consultant after his retirement.
        Mr. Power is survived by his wife, the former Julia Minds; a daughter, Meg, of McLean, Va.; three stepchildren, and three grandchildren.

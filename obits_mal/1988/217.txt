        Benjamin H. Brinton, a lawyer and a former treasurer of the New York Stock Exchange, died Wednesday at his home in Tucson, Ariz., after a long illness. He was 95 years old.
        Mr. Brinton founded Brinton & Company, a securities concern, and became a member of the stock exchange in 1927. He became treasurer in 1935 and retired in 1940.
        He is survived by his wife, Lillian; two sons, Benjamin, of Richmond, Va., and William, of San Francisco; a daughter, Cordelia Anne, of Rockville, Md.; seven grandchildren, and 12 great-grandchildren.

        R. George LeVind, a retired vice president of Blyth & Company, a Wall Street investment firm, died of a heart ailment on Thursday at his home in Short Hills, N.J. He was 84 years old.
        Mr. LeVind joined Blyth, now part of Paine Webber, in 1933 and rose to national manager of the firm's municipal bond department in 1960, serving in that capacity until his retirement in 1968. He was president of the Municipal Bond Club of New York in 1962 and 1963.
        He is survivedby his wife, the former Ada May Jones; a son, John, of Short Hills, and a granddaughter.

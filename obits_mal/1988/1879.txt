        James V. Altieri, a New York lawyer and labor arbitrator, died of lung cancer Monday at his home in Yonkers. He was 79 years old.
        Mr. Altieri devoted most of his time in recent years to labor arbitration. He took part in resolving disputes involving the New York City police and other public employees. At various times he was a member of the American Airlines Panel, the General Electric Panel and the Newspaper Publishers Panel. He was also a member of the National Academy of Arbitrators and the American Arbitration Association.
        He graduated from Fordham University in 1929 and Columbia Law School in 1932.
        From 1951 to 1953, he was chief of the enforcement and litigation branch of the New York regional office of the Wage Stabilization Board. He entered private practice in 1953. The next year he was a founding partner of Altieri, Kushner, Miuccio & Frind, a Manhattan law firm specializing in labor and construction law.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Helen Marinucci; three daughters, Eileen Steinbugler of Fayetteville, N.Y., Caroline Altieri-Bernal of Shrewsbury. Vt., and Helen, of Manhattan; a son, James, of Carmel, N.Y., and three grandchildren.

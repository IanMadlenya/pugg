        Maj. Gen. Lewis A. Mologne, commander of the Walter Reed Army Medical Center in Washington, died yesterday of cancer at the center. He was 56 years old and lived in Washington.
        General Mologne, a physician, commanded the center from June 1983 until his retirement on Aug. 1. He began his career in the Corps of Engineers after graduating in 1954 from the United States Military Academy at West Point. He was a company commander in Korea.
        He resigned from the Army in 1957 to attend medical school at the University of Pittsburgh. After his graduation, he was commissioned in the Army Medical Corps.
        From 1980 to 1981, he was head of the Army Medical Command in South Korea.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Rose Marie Gallardi, of Washington; three sons, Lewis Alan of Colorado, Lieut. Timothy Scott of San Diego, Calif., and Ens. Michael John of Port Hueneme, Calif.; two daughters, Mary Kathryn and Amy Louise, both of Washington; his mother, Kathryn Mologne of Smithton, Pa., and two sisters, Ester Bucci, of Scottdale, Pa., and Roberta Kubil of Greensburg, Pa.

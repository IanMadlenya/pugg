        Robin Kinkead, a New York Times correspondent in Moscow in the early 1930's and later the first bureau chief in the Soviet capital for Reuters, died of emphysema on Tuesday at his home in Sacramento, Calif. He was 82 years old.
        Mr. Kinkead, who was born in Manhattan, was a graduate of Stanford University and studied French literature at the University of Paris. After his service with The Times, he joined Reuters in 1933. He later worked in public-relations capacities for Pan American World Airways before retiring in 1968.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Angela McCann; a daughter, Melanie, of Sacramento; a son, Robin Jr., of Boston; a brother, David, of Foresthill, Calif., and a grandson.

        Marcus G. Christ, former presiding justice of the Appellate Division of the New York Supreme Court's Second Judicial Department, died yesteday at his home in New Hyde Park, L.I. He was 87 years old.
        Mr. Christ served 26 years on major courts, beginning in 1950 with his appointment to the Nassau County Court. Three years later he was elected a State Supreme Court justice and in 1959 he was appointed associate justice of the Appellate Division for the department that covers Brooklyn, Queens, Long Island, Staten Island, Westchester, Rockland and three other nearby counties. In 1970 Governor Nelson A. Rockefeller named him presiding justice of the division, a post he held until 1970, when he was again named associate justice. He retired in 1976. Before serving on the bench, Mr. Christ was a recognized authority on municipal law. He gained prominence as trial counsel in a case in which Nassau and Suffolk Counties and their municipal subdivisions were able to prevent New York City from sinking additional water-supply wells on Long Island.
        Mr. Christ's father, the late Philip J. Christ, was Supervisior of the Town of North Hempstead from 1907 to 1917 and chairman of the Nassau County Board of Supervisors for the last seven years of that period.
        Mr. Christ is survived by his wife, the former Marion C. Hallsted; a sister, Charlotte C. Hughes of New London, N.H.; three sons, Marcus Jr., of Oyster Bay, L.I., Philip, of Apton, Calif., and Donald, of Mill Neck, L.I.; 12 grandchildren and two great-grandchildren.
        A funeral will be held Tuesday at 10 A.M. at Fairchild Sons Funeral Home in Garden City, L.I.

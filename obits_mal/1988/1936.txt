        Warren J. Donovan, chief investigator for the New York State special prosecutor for nursing homes, health and social services, died of cancer Monday at Calvary Hospital in the Bronx. He was 63 years old and lived in Syosset, L.I.
        He had been chief investigator in the special prosecutor's office, a part of the Deputy Attorney General's Medicaid Fraud Control Department, since its inception in 1975, heading a staff that investigated health-care crimes.
        Mr. Donovan, who was born in Somerville, Mass., graduated from Niagara University. He was a special agent with the Federal Bureau of Investigation for 23 years.
        He is survived by his wife, Eileen; five sons, Patrick, of Huntington, L.I., John, of Syosset, Michael, of Manhattan, Thomas, of Boston, and Timothy, of Garrison, N.Y; three daughters, Anne Shapiro of West Caldwell, N.J., Kathleen Cessaro of Cherry Hill, N.J., and Margaret Donovan of Manhattan, and eight grandchildren.

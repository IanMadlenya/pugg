        Monroe D. North, former president of Fruit of the Loom Inc., died Tuesday at the Whitehall Boca Raton Nursing Home in Florida. He was 77 years old and had lived in Atlantis, Fla.
        Mr. North was a graduate of City College and Brooklyn Law School. He was a co-founder in 1946 of D. B. Fuller & Company, a textile manufacturing concern that was merged into J. P. Stevens in 1955. He left Stevens in 1961 to become president of Fruit of the Loom, the underwear manufacturer, a job he held until his retirement in 1975.
        His wife of 47 years, the former Viola Utstein, died in 1979.
        Surviving are his second wife, the former Evelyn Leader Goldfarb; a daughter, Julie Schwarz of Pound Ridge, N.Y.; a son, Charles, of Manhattan; a brother, Jerry, of Lake Worth, Fla.; two sisters, Helen Newman of West Palm Beach, Fla., and Pearl Pochoda of Cranbury, N.J., and four grandchildren.

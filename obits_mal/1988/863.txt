        Dennis M. Horan, a lawyer who represented individuals and groups fighting abortion and who wrote extensively on the legal aspects of euthanasia, died of a heart attack at his home in Chicago on Sunday. He was 56 years old.
        Mr. Horan was the managing partner of Hinshaw, Culbertson, Moelman, Hoban & Fuller, one of Chicago's largest law firms. Since 1977 he headed the legal defense fund of Americans United for Life, an anti-abortion group. He was a founder and past president of the Illinois Right-to-Life Committee and a legal adviser to the United States Catholic Conference.
        Among his books are ''Death, Dying and Euthanasia,'' published in 1976; ''Abortion and Social Justice,'' published in 1977, and ''Infanticide and the Handicapped Newborn,'' published in 1983. He also wrote a number of legal texts on medical malpractice and similar subjects. He taught at the University of Chicago for 10 years and at its business school for four years.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Dolores Volini; a daughter, Cecelia Anne Horan; a son, Matthew Dennis Horan; two brothers, Joseph Horan of Franklin Park, Ill., and Thomas Horan of Addison, Ill., and four sisters, Sheila Pembrooke of Ireland, Theresa Cortina of Springfield, Va., Margaret Hildebrand of Addison and Anne Marie Page of Frankln Park.

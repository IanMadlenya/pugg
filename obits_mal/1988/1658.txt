        Harvey R. Wellman, a United States diplomat and member of the Foreign Service for 32 years, died of a heart attack Sept. 27 at Middlesex Memorial Hospital in Middletown, Conn. He was 71 years old and lived in Old Saybrook, Conn.
        Mr. Wellman served in Latin America and Europe. In 1970, he was appointed special assistant to the Secretary of State for narcotics matters in Washington.
        After his retirement from the Foreign Service in 1974, he became deputy executive director to the United Nations Fund for Drug Abuse Control in Geneva and Vienna, a post he held until 1978, when he retired to Old Saybrook.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Helen Tewell; a brother, Donald of East Aurora, N.Y.; a son, Clarke, of Los Angeles; four daughters, Karen Fresco of Champaign, Ill., Judith Post of Front Royal, Va., Christine Wellman of Monroe, Mich., Charlotte Wellman of Washington, and a granddaughter.

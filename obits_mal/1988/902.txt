        Sidney M. Davis, a Manhattan lawyer and counsel to investigating committees in Washington and Albany, died of heart disease Sunday at New York Hospital. He was 69 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Davis, who received his undergraduate and law degrees from the University of Chicago, served as counsel to several United States Senate committees in the 1950's and 1960's, participating in the investigation of organized crime by Senator Estes Kefauver, as well as the Dixon-Yates antimonopoly investigation. He was also special counsel to the Moreland Act Commission of New York State, which investigated nursing-home fraud.
        Mr. Davis taught at the William Alanson White Institute, the New York University Law School and the Woodrow Wilson Institute of Public and International Affairs at Princeton University. At the time of his death he was a member of the law firm Shanks, Davis & Remer.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Tilly Laycock; two sons, Lincoln of Manhattan and Michael of Brooklyn; a daughter, Leonie Davis of Stony Brook, L.I., and two grandchildren.

        Seymour Goeld, who gave Caesars Palace its name and ran promotions for the Las Vegas resort and casino, died of heart failure Monday at the Miami Heart Institute. He was 71.
        ''He wanted a name that connoted great excitement in a very decadent theme,'' said his son, Paul, explaining how his father chose the casino name.
        Mr. Goeld, who also created the logo and invitations for the opening of the resort more than 25 years ago, won awards from the Hotel Sales Managers Association and other professional groups.

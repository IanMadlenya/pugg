        Joseph B. Terbell, a retired steel company executive and a former deputy housing commissioner for New York State, died of cardiac arrest Monday at his home in Fairfield, Conn. He was 82 years old.
        He was president of Jollette Steel Ltd., of Montreal, a subsidary of the American Brake Shoe Company, retiring in 1958, and was later a member of the Republican National Committee.
        He is survived by his wife, Mary, of Fairfield; two sons, Joseph Jr., of Westport, Conn., and Lawrence, of Hayward, Calif.; a daughter, Susan T. Hershey of San Francisco; 10 grandchildren, and six great-grandchildren.

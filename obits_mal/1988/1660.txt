        Kenneth J. Thornhill, a financial consultant and former member of the Data General Corporation's board of directors, died Saturday at his home in Boca Raton, Fla. His wife said he probably died of a heart attack. He was 61 years old.
        Mr. Thornhill moved to Boca Raton more than a year ago from Babylon, L.I., where he was a longtime resident. When he died, he was a director of 14 of the American Capitol Mutual Funds, which are based in Houston. He was a director of a number of corporations, including Loehmann's, a clothing concern, and the GCA Corporation, a manufacturer of integrated circuits.
        He had been a general partner in the Long Island Company and the Island Company, high-technology growth companies of the 1960's.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Joan Lawrence; two sons, Richard, of Queens, and David, of Manhattan; four daughters, Kathleen Thornhill of Manhattan, Patricia Adkinson of Fairfield, Conn., Susan McKenna of Flemington, N.J., and Debra Barra of Nutley, N.J., and six grandchildren.

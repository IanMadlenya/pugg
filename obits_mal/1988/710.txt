        Winfield W. Heckert, a chemist who worked on the development of nylon, died April 4 in a nursing home in Wilmington, Del., after a long illness. He was 85 years old and lived in Wilmington.
        Mr. Heckert joined E. I. du Pont de Nemours & Company as a research chemist in 1928 and was assigned to nylon research in 1935. After several advances, he was appointed assistant general director of the technical divisions in charge of pioneering research in 1959. He retired in 1967.
        He was a graduate of Miami Univerity in Oxford, Ohio, and earned a Ph.D in physical chemistry from the Ohio State University in 1928.
        Mr. Heckert is survived by his wife, the former Clarice Upson; a son, Clark W., of Wilmington; two daughters, Frances Pane of East Windsor, N.J., and Rowena, of Cheyenne, Wyo.; a brother, Richard E., of Kennett Square, Pa., and nine grandchildren.

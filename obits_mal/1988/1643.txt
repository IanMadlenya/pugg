        Marcus H. Ray, a retired Army colonel who served as a civilian aide to Secretary of War Robert P. Patterson, died of a stroke Wednesday at his home in Silver Spring, Md. He was 84 years old.
        Mr. Ray, a native of Chicago who was educated at the University of Illinois, commanded an artillery batallion in Italy in World War II. From 1945 to 1947 he was a civilian adviser to Secretary Patterson on matters dealing with black soldiers. He returned to military service in 1947 and was the chief adviser on black soldiers in the European command of Gen. Lucius Clay.
        After he retired in 1958, he worked in the office of the Army Adjutant General in Chicago until 1966.
        He is survived by his wife, Adine DeLuc Ray, of Silver Spring; a daughter, Adine Usher, of Mt. Vernon, N.Y.; a son, Marcus Jr., of Phliadelphia; and two grandchildren.

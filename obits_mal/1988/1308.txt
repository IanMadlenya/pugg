        George A. Palmer, a former superintendent of the Statue of Liberty and the first superintendent of the Home of Franklin D. Roosevelt National Historic Site at Hyde Park, died of a heart attack Tuesday at the Cooper Hospital University Medical Center in Camden, N.J. He was 81 years old and lived in Collingswood, N.J.
        Mr. Palmer worked for the National Park Service from 1933 until he retired in 1975. For the last 20 years of his tenure, he was associate regional director in Philadelphia.
        From 1934 to 1935 and again from 1937 to 1945, he was superintendent of the Statue of Liberty. At Hyde Park, where he worked until 1955, Mr. Palmer was archivist.
        Mr. Palmer initiated an oral history project by interviewing workers at Hyde Park, where Roosevelt lived, vacationed and, in April 1945, was buried.
        Mr. Palmer had been borough historian of Collingswood since 1983.
        Surviving are two sons, William, of Collingswood, and Frank, of Warwick, R.I.; a brother, Lewis, of Indiana, and three grandchildren.

        Irving Paparo, a retired clothing manufacturer, died of cancer of the liver Saturday at his home in Mahopac Falls, N.Y. He was 64 years old.
        Mr. Paparo, who was born in Manhattan, was the former owner of Baldwin Casuals, a manufacturer of women's slacks, with three plants on Long Island and a showroom in Manhattan. He retired from Baldwin in 1977 and bought a liquor store near his home. He was a member of the Baldwin Jewish Center for 25 years before moving to Mahopac.
        He is survived by his wife, Bella Cohen Paparo; a son, Michael, of Manhattan; two daughters, Sandie Rogers, of South Orange, N.J., and Eileen Kyranakis, of Levittown, L.I.; his parents, Meyer and Sophie Paparo, of Miami Beach; two sisters, Mildred Elie, of Plainview, L.I., and Rita Elias, of Emerson, N.J., and six grandchildren.

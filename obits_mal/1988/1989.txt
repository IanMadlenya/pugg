        John Upton Terrell, the author of more than 40 books and a historian of the Old West, died Thursday of a heart attack. He was 87 years old. A Chicago native, Mr. Terrell spent time as a youth on his uncle's ranch in Montana. When he was a teen-ager, he ran away from home to become a cowboy, and got work as a ranch hand. He later wrote for The San Francisco Chronicle and United Press and covered World War II for Newsweek.
        His books about the West included ''Life Among the Apaches,' ''The Arrow and the Cross: A History of the American Indian and the Missionaries,'' ''Furs by Astor'' and ''Faint the Trumpet Sounds'' with George Walton.

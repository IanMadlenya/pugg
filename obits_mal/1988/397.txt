        Kevin Patterson, a playwright and theater press representative, died of complications from AIDS Friday at his home in New York. He was 32 years old.
        Mr. Patterson was the author of ''A Most Secret War,'' a dramatization of the life of Alan Turing, which was presented at the Harold Clurman Theater in December 1987. Mr. Patterson's other work included the plays ''A Safe Harbor'' and ''Fascination Cha-Cha,'' which had been presented in regional theaters.
        For the past three years, Mr. Patterson was a press representative on the staff of the New York Shakespeare Festival, and had represented many Broadway and Off Broadway shows before that. He was a graduate of Duke University and recently earned a Master of Fine Arts degree from Rutgers University.
        He is survived by his mother, Louise, of Rockaway, N.J.; a sister, Kathleen Straussberger of Mount Freedom, N.J., and a brother, Robert, of Dover, N.J.
        A memorial service will be held at 6 P.M. on April 18 at the Public Theater, 425 Lafayette Street.

        Lewis Goodgold, a former New York City deputy comptroller, died of a heart attack Sunday at Beth Israel Hospital. He was 94 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Goodgold, who joined the comptroller's office in 1933, later established the Bureau of Excise Taxes and oversaw the start of the city sales tax.
        Mr. Goodgold, who was born in Manhattan, received law degrees from Columbia University and accounting degrees from New York University.
        Surviving are a son, Maurice, of Manhattan; a brother, David, of Brooklyn, and two grandchildren.

        Benjamin Moore Belcher, retired chief executive of the Benjamin Moore Paint Company, died of a stroke Sunday at the Sharon (Conn.) Hospital. He was 76 years old and lived in Lakeville, Conn.
        Mr. Belcher, who attended the University of Virginia, was the grandson of Benjamin Moore, who founded the company in 1883. Mr. Belcher joined the company in 1934 and helped it become one of the leading paint manufacturing companies in the United States.
        He served with the War Production Board in World War II and was a director of Sharon Hospital and of the Salisbury Association in Connecticut.
        He is survived by his wife, Nancy; three sons, Benjamin, of Stamford, Conn.; Dr. Jonathan, of Los Gatos, Calif., and Ward, of Ridgewood, N.J.; two daughters, Sara O'Connor, of Lakeville, and Jane Phinney, of Ripton, Vt., and 11 grandchildren.

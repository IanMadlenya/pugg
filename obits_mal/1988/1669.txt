        Frederick R. Lack, vice president in charge of radio and specialty products for Western Electric before his retirement in 1958, died of a heart attack Tuesday at the Southport Manor Nursing Home in Southport, Conn. He was 93 years old.
        Mr. Lack, who was born in England, went to work in 1911 at the age of 16 as an assembler for Western Electric, the manufacturing arm of the Bell telephone system. A decade later, he left for Harvard, where he earned a Bachelor of Science degree magna cum laude.
        After graduation, he joined Bell Telephone Laboratories, a sister company in the telephone system, and spent 13 years doing research in radio waves and vacuum tubes. He returned to Western Electric in 1938 to manage its radio and specialty products divisions, taking time out during World War II to direct the Army and Navy Electronics Production Agency in Washington.
        Mr. Lack was president of the American Standards Association in 1947.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Ethel Sturges; a daughter, Abbe Sawabini of Shelburne, Vt.; a sister, Eleanor White of Washington; a brother, Charles, of St. Petersburg, Fla.; four grandchildren and three great-grandchildren.

        Harold G. Rosson, the cinematographer who filmed ''The Wizard of Oz'' and ''The Asphalt Jungle'' during a 52-year Hollywood career, died Tuesday at his home in Palm Beach, Fla. He was 93 years old.
        Mr. Rosson made headlines in 1933 when he eloped with the movie actress Jean Harlow. The marriage lasted eight months.
        Mr. Rosson and W. Howard Greene shared an honorary Academy Award in 1936 for pioneering color photography in ''The Garden of Allah.'' Mr. Rosson received Academy Award nominations for ''Boom Town'' (1940), ''Thirty Seconds Over Tokyo'' (1944), ''The Asphalt Jungle'' (1950) and ''The Bad Seed'' (1956).
        He made ''The Wizard of Oz'' (1939) for Metro-Goldwyn-Mayer where he worked in the 1930's and 40's.
        Mr. Rossen, a native of New York City, left New York in 1914; his first Hollywood film, the silent feature ''David Harum,'' was released in 1915. He made his last picture, ''El Dorado,'' in 1967. Among his other films were ''The Docks of New York'' (1928), ''Gentlemen Prefer Blondes'' (1928), ''Treasure Island'' (1934), ''Duel in the Sun'' (1947) and ''Singin' in the Rain'' (1952).

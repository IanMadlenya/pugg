        Joseph P. Alduino, a former controller of The New York Times, died of pneumonia early Sunday at Beth Israel Hospital in Boston. He was 81 years old and lived in Boston.
        Mr. Alduino was born in Brooklyn and quit Bushwick High School at 14 to help support his family. He joined the newspaper in 1923 as an office boy in the auditing department and rose to become assistant auditor in 1946 and auditor in 1954. Two years later, he was named controller, putting him in charge of all financial affairs at the newspaper. He retired from that position in 1968.
        He is survived by his wife, Pauline; a son by a previous marriage, Joseph Jr., of Milford, N.H.; another son, K. Peter, of Boston; a daughter, Paula M. Suterland, of Natick, Mass.; a brother, Anthony, and a sister, Ann, both of Woodside, N.Y., and three grandchildren.

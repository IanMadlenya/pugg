        Morton Kline, a New York City lawyer and real-estate developer, died Friday at Mount Sinai Hospital. He was 85 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        After serving for many years as vice president of the National House and Farms Association, Mr. Kline became president of the development concern in 1953 and remained as the head of the company until retiring seven years ago.
        Under his leadership, the association developed properties throughout New Jersey, including the Lake Hiawatha cottages near Caldwell, N.J., and the White Meadow Lake and Country Club community near Dover, N.J.
        In 1957, Mr. Kline was elected president of the New Jersey Association of Real Estate Boards.
        He was a member of the United Jewish Appeal-Federation of Jewish Philanthropies of New York and was a founding member of the New York chapter of the Juvenile Diabetes Foundation.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Jessica Perlman; a daughter, Ellen Furman; a sister, Helen Goldfarb, and two grandchildren.

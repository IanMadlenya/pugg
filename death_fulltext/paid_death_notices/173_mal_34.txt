
        PETERS-Arthur King. Age 81, died peacefully at home in Bronxville on June 2, 2001. Beloved husband of Sarah Whitaker Peters, devoted father of Robert Bruce, Margaret Allen, and Michael Whitaker, loving grandfather of Philip, Elizabeth, Sarah, Griffin, Mark, and Kate. A 1940 graduate of Cornell University, he went on to serve during World War II as an officer in Army Counter-Intelligence. He later received a Ph.D. in French literature from Columbia University. During the 1970s, while running his own importing business, A.K. Peters Co., he taught French at Hunter College in his lunch hours. He was a translator, author and critic. Five of his books were published, including ''Jean Cocteau and His World,'' and ''Seven Trails West.'' He served as President and Chairman of the French American Foundation in New York from 1977-83. He was decorated with the Chevalier de l'Ordre des Arts et des Lettres by the French government in 1978, and received the Chevalier de la Legion d'Honneur in 1984. A mountaineer, he was a member of the American Alpine Club and the Alpine Club in London. There will be a celebration of life service at the Reformed Church of Bronxville, NY, on Thursday, June 7, at 3 PM. In lieu of flowers, contributions may be sent to the Jansen Memorial Hospice, 69 Main Street, Tuckahoe, NY 10707.


        COMBER-Thomas E., a member of the Paulist Fathers who was active in the field of publishing and inaugurated a nation-wide prison ministry in 1994, died on September 25, 1999, in New York City. Raised in Elkins Park, PA, he joined the Navy in 1943 and served until 1946. After attending LaSalle College in Philadelphia, he joined the Paulist Fathers and was ordained a priest in 1955. From 1962 until 1978, Fr. Comber directed marketing at Paulist Press, and served both as Chairman and member of the Board of Directors of the Catholic Press Association. From 1978 to 1991, he served as pastor of churches in Minneapolis, Houston and Tucson. Since 1991, he devoted himself to ministry at the Paulist National Catholic Evangelization Association, during which time he began PNCEA Prison Ministry services, providing a newsletter and religious material in English and Spanish to over 900 prison chaplains. He will be waked at the Paulist Fathers' Rectory, 415 W. 59th St., New York, Tuesday from 2:00 PM to 8:00 PM. His funeral Mass will be celebrated at St. Paul the Apostle Church, New York, Wednesday at 10:00, with burial at Oak Ridge, NJ. Donations may be made to PNCEA, 3015 Fourth St., NE, Washington, DC 20017 or The Paulist Fathers, 997 Macarthur Boulevard, Mahwah, New Jersey, 07430.

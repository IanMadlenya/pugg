
        CARVER--John A.H., 86, formerly of New York City, NY. He was born September 19, 1920 in New York, New York a son of Clifford Nickels and Helena Phiae (Maxwell) Carver. Mr. Carver graduated from St. Paul's School in Concord, NH in 1938 and graduated from Princeton University in 1943. He served as a commissioned Lieutenant in the Field Artillery Division during World War Two. Mr. Carver worked in the shipping business from 1946 -1953 and served as a President of Baker, Carver and Morrell, Inc. Ship Chandlers in New York City from 1953 -1957. He then was involved in various oil and mining ventures, before joining Loomis, Sayles & Company in New York as an investment counselor where he worked from 1965 -1978 serving as Partner and VicePresident up until his retirement. He was a member and Past-President of the New England Society of the City of New York. Mr. Carver was a member of The Sons of the Revolution, Society of Colonial Wars, Holland Lodge A.F. & A.M. as well as the Union Club, Leash Club and India House. He is survived by his wife of 55 years Cecile (Parker) Carver of Scarborough, ME, three children: George B. Carver of California; Philae Carver of California and Leslie C. Whitney of Pennsylvania and four grandchildren. A memorial service will be held at 1:00 PM on Thursday, November 2, 2006 at The Cathedral Church of St. Luke, 143 State Street, Portland ME with The Very Reverend Benjamin A. Shambaugh officiating. Private interment will be held at a later day in Searsport. For information: Jones, Rich & Hutchins Funeral Home, Portland ME.

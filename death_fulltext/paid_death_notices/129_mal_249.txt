
        GARZON-Julio M., 92, newspaper editor and USIA official, died on October 19. Born in Bogota, from 1925 to 1958 Mr. Garzon was Sports Editor & Managing Editor of the Spanish daily ''La Prensa'' of New York. From 1958 to 1971 he was Director of the Latin American Branch of the Press and Publications Service at the United States Information Agency. He was a founder of the InterAmerican Press Association. He retired to Florida in 1971 and was active in inter-American activities. Mr. Garzon was awarded the Maria Moors Cabot Gold Medal of the Columbia University School of Journalism, and was named a life member of the American Society of Newspaper Editors. Mr. Garzon is survived by his wife of sixtythree years, Manuela; two daughters, Maria Garzon of Los Angeles and Laura Wyman of Washington, D.C; three grandchildren and two great-grandchildren.

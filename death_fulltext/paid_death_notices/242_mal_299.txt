
        HEIM--Richard M. President of Richard M. Heim Advertising, died on March 26th, at the age of 81, and worked until the last day of his life. Richard lived a full and vibrant personal and professional life and was loved by all who knew him for his kindness and generosity. He served in the U.S. Army Air Corps in WWII and was a tail gunner on a B-24. His plane was shot at and he was forced to parachute over Yugoslavia. He was missing in action for two months and received a Purple Heart. Richard then went on to graduate from Penn State and did graduate work in history at NYU. He belonged to the Pi Lambda Phi Fraternity and continued a life-long dedication to it, holding several positions on its various councils. As a trustee of the Educational Foundation, he raised substantial monies to support its scholarship programs. Richard loved young people and tried to encourage them to reach their potential and counseled them on alcohol abuse. Richard was passionate about golf. He belonged to the Whippoorwill Club in Armonk and served on many committees at the club, including being a board member for nine years. Richard was a devoted husband to his wife, Rose Tripp Heim. He is also survived by his brother, Robert, his nephew, Gregory, and his niece, Carolyn, her husband, John, and their two sons, Luke and Evan. A memorial service will be held at Frank E. Campbell on Madison Ave. and 81st St. on Wednesday, April 4th, at 3:30pm.


        FEIG-Werner M. 65, of White Plains, NY, on Tuesday, December 23. Born June 29, 1932, in Breslau, Germany. He grew up in the Hongkew Jewish ghetto of Shanghai, China, from 1939 to 1946. Moved to Pittsburgh, PA, where he attended Westinghouse and Schenley High Schools. BA, University of Pittsburgh, 1955. Master's in Education, Harvard, 1958. Master's in Political Science, University of Chicago, 1964. Revered teacher at Scarsdale High School, 1968-1991, and Nightingale-Bamford, 1991-1997. Memorial service at Nightingale-Bamford, 20 E. 92nd St. on Saturday, January 31, at 2 PM. Survived by daughter Rebecca Feig and son Jonathan Feig. Donations to American Society for Yad Vashem or Prep for Prep.
        FEIG-Werner. The Trustees, faculty, staff and students of The Nightingale - Bamford School mourn the loss of our beloved colleague, teacher and friend. Mr. Feig was a brilliant history teacher who inspired countless Nightinggale students with his passion for history, his gift for making the past come alive in his classroom, and his deep affection for his students. We extend our sincere condolences to his children, Jonathan and Rebecca. Grant F. Winthrop, President, Board of Trustees Dorothy A. Hutcheson, Head of School


        BELL-Victor Charles. 96, lifelong Manhattan resident with distinguished naval and civilian careers, died March 5 at the home of his daughter Mrs. Wendy Walsh of Hackensack. Investment banker, industrialist and combat veteran of WWI and WWII, serving in the Mediterranean and on Guadalcanal. Business partner of Walter Chrysler, Jr., in Sonora Radio, Moto-Meter Gauge and Equipment Company, and Electric Auto-Lite. President and Chairman of General Aviation Equipment Company, manufacturer of WWII bomber controls and the support spar for Howard Hughes' Flying Boat, ''Spruce Goose.'' Drafted portion of the original FDIC Charter. Former member of Metropolitan and Wings Clubs. Survived by six children, four grandchildren and two great-grandchildren.

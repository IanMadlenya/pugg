
        GOTTLIEB-Janet Laib. A fashion writer and publicist, died Friday, November 26 from cancer. She was 73. Mrs. Gottlieb left college and moved to New York after publishing her first freelance article, ''Must College Girls Wear Diapers?'' about the coddling of co-eds. Though intending a life on the stage, she instead found a career in magazine publishing and freelance writing. She worked at Seventeen and the Tobe Fashion Report, and her byline appeared in Vogue, Ladies Home Journal, The New York Times and other publications. She formed Janet Laib Public Relations, with a small stable of clients including Xerox, Hathaway Shirts and William Greenberg Jr. Desserts. In 1981, she became president of Edward Gottlieb Inc., a communications firm, and subsequently married her partner, Mr. Gottlieb. They worked on communications strategies for merger and acquisitions and crisis management for clients including Volkswagen US, Bear Stearns and Fleet Bank. Mrs. Gottlieb kept her passion for theater, and was one of the first backers of the play ''Wit.'' She belonged to The Fashion Group, the Overseas Press Club and The Financial Women's Association, as well as the Public Relations Society of America, the American Society of Journalists and Authors, and the Players Club. Born in Detroit, she attended the George School in Bucks County, PA and the University of Michigan, receiving her B.A. from the New School for Social Research in New York. She leaves a daughter, Margo Amgott. Her husband predeceased her. Burial is private. A memorial service will be held at 4 PM, Saturday, December 11 at Frank E. Campbell, 1076 Madison Ave. at 81 St. Contributions may be made to the Visiting Nurse Service of New York, Development Office, 107 E. 70 St., NY, NY 10021 or to Memorial Sloan-Kettering Cancer Center, Development Office, 1275 York Ave., NY, NY 10021.

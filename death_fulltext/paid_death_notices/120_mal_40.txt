
        HARRINGTON - Charles James, a former Vice President of DuPont, died suddenly on January 1st at Bethesda Hospital in Boynton Beach, Florida. He was 85 years old. Mr. Harrington was born in DuPont, Washington and has been a resident of Greenville, Delaware for 82 years. He graduated from Hotchkiss School in Lakeville, Connecticut, Princeton University - class of 1933, and received his PhD in chemical engineering at MIT in 1937. He joined the DuPont Company in 1937 and retired in February 1976. During World War II he worked in Hanford, Washington on the Manhattan Project, which produced the atomic bomb. He was the DuPont representative in London from 1947 to 1952 and the following two years was a negotiator for the company in Japan. He was general manager of DuPont's Elastimer Chemicals division from 1964 to 1967. In 1972 he became Vice President. He served on the Wilmington Trust Board of Directors. He was the chairman of the executive committee of the Medical Center of Delaware and subsequently chairman of the Board of Trustees. He was a recognized philanthropist in the Wilmington community. Mr. Harrington was active in state and national Republican politics, a member of the Heritage Foundation, and on the national council of the World Wildlife Fund. He was a member of the Wilmington Club, the Wilmington Country Club, Greenville Country Club and the Vic Mead Club. In Florida he was a member of the Gulfstream Golf Club and the Gulf-stream Bath and Tennis Club. Mr. Harrington was married to Mary Gertrude Sproat in 1941. She died in 1979. In 1983 he married Elinore Hoelzel Hoopes. In addition to his wife, he is survived by two brothers, Willis and George, four stepchildren and seven step-grandchildren. A memorial service will be held at Christ Church Christiana in Greenville, Delaware at 11 AM, Tuesday January 7th.

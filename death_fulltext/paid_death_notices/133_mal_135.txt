
        GOLDSMITH-James A., Jr. Died suddenly at home in Hillsborough, Ca. on Febuary 7, 1998. He was 91 years old. He is survived by his wife, Irene T. Goldsmith, his children, MaryAnne Schwalbe of N.Y.C., James A. Goldsmith III of Briarcliff Manor N.Y., Jeanne Taylor and Marc Ducharme of Atlanta, Ga., Denise Omundson of Portland Or., sixteen grandchildren, six great grandchildren and two sisters, Katherine Lowenstein of N.Y.C. and Bertha Bernstein of Los Angeles, Ca. Born in WilkesBarre, Pa., he was raised in N.Y.C., attended the Horace Mann School and received a diploma from the Taft School in Watertown, Ct. He retired as President of Hess, Goldsmith, which was sold to Burlington Industries. He then owned and operated Barnum Travel in Westport, Ct. After selling the travel agency, he became President of the Westport National Bank. He was active in local and national Republican politics; he was a Past President and Director of the Jewish Guild for the Blind in N.Y. He moved to California in 1966 and became the Branch Manager of the H.Liebes Stores. In the 1970's, he brought the dream of the San Mateo Garden Center to a reality. He served for many years on the Board of Directors of the San Mateo County Fair. Donations in his name can be made to the San Mateo Garden Center, 605 Parkside Way, San Mateo, Ca. 94402 or the the American Heart Association.
        GOLDSMITH-James A., Jr. The Officers, Directors and staff of The Jewish Guild for the Blind note with deep sorrow the passing of James A. Goldsmith, Jr., a longtime Board member and past Chairman of The Guild. His dedication to improving the lives of blind and visually impaired persons will long be remembered, and he will be sorely missed by all of us who knew and worked with him. Our most sincere condolences to his family. James M. Dubin, Chairman Alan R. Morse, President John F. Heimerdinger, President Emeritus


        BRESSLER--Bernard. Died peacefully on Saturday morning, September 17. Bernie was the beloved husband of Teresa, father of Lisa Berg and Jeanette Bressler, grandfather of Adam and Joshua Berg, father-in-law of Michael Berg, future father-inlaw of Steven W. Perlsweig, and brother of Martin. He was 77 years old. He was a veteran of the US Navy in World War II and graduated from Rutgers University in 1948, and in 1952 from Harvard Law School, magna cum laude, where he was a member of the Law Review. Bernie was a founding senior partner in the New York, New Jersey and Florida law firm of Bressler, Amory & Ross and has written and co-written a number of legal works including the New York Lawyers Manual and the Tax Annotations for Nichols Encyclopedia. At the time of his death Bernie was the Director and Chairman of the Board of Trustees of the New Jersey Public Interest Law Center, which successfully litigated the issue of racial profiling by the New Jersey State Police. Over the years he was active in a number of charitable activities, including Area Chairman for the United Jewish Appeals and for the United Negro College Fund. He also served as a Trustee of the Summit Civic Foundation, the South Street Theater Company and the Community Theater of Morristown. Bernie was a member of the Lotos Club in New York and the Park Avenue Club in Morristown. Funeral services will be at Temple Sinai, 208 Summit Ave., Summit, NJ, on Tuesday, September 20, at 11 am, with interment at the Workman's Circle Cemetery, Route 33, Freehold, NJ. Donations may be made to the Bernard Bressler Fund at the Morristown Memorial Hospital in honor of Dr. Steven Papish.

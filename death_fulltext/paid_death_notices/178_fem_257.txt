
        WEINTRAUB-Bronka. Widow of Jacob Weintraub, Harry Rabin and Abraham Stawski, passed away on November 23, 2001, and brought to her eternal rest on Sunday, November 25, 2001. A vivacious and dedicated leader in philanthropy and the very noblest of causes on behalf of Israel, the Jewish people and Jewish education. She will be sorely missed by all who knew her, admired her and loved her. May her memory be a blessing.
        WEINTRAUB-Bronka. Albert Einstein College of Medicine of Yeshiva University records with profound sorrow the passing of our dear friend, Benefactor, and devoted member of our National Women's Division, who with her late husband, Jacob, generously supported medical research and education programs at our institution. To her nephew, Carmi Schwartz, and to all who knew and loved her, we offer our heartfelt condolences. Yeshiva University Dr. Norman Lamm, President Albert Einstein College of Medicine Robert A. Belfer, Chairperson Board of Overseers Dominick P. Purpura, M.D. The Marilyn and Stanley M. Katz Dean
        WEINTRAUB-Bronka. The American Friends of Assaf Harofeh Medical Center and Assaf Harofeh Medical Center, Israel mourn the passing of our dear friend and board member who, together with her husband, Jacob, devotedly supported our cause over the years. The Jacob and Bronka Weintraub Sculpture Garden at Assaf Harofeh will forever remain a monument of their benevolence to the hospital. Kenneth Kronen National Chairman

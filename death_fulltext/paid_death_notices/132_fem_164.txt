
        KOHN-Sally. On January 17, 1998, in Washington, D.C. With singular success, this brilliant and charming woman devoted her life to making other peoples' lives healthier and happier. The glow of her warmth and laughter lit her world and ours. Former Director of the New York AIDS consortium and a leading figure, in her quiet way, in the fight against AIDS and for child health, since 1990 Ms. Kohn was an assistant clinical professor at Albert Einstein College and was the recipient in 1990 of the U.S. Public Health Service Award for Outstanding Contributions to the HRSA AIDS Service Demonstration Program. At the time of her death, Ms. Kohn was the Deputy Director of Opening Doors and a consultant to the New York Community Trust and the Public Health Service. Ms. Kohn is survived by her husband, Sam Eskenazi, a daughter, Becca Zerkin of Washington, DC, parents, Ruth Kohn and Max Kohn, of Elkins Park, PA. She has two sisters, Susan Gleeksman of Cranbury, NJ and Jean Gray of Hilltown, PA, a brother Andrew Kohn of Cleveland, OH, and eight nieces and nephews. In lieu of flowers, donations may be made to the Jewish Fund for Justice, 260 Fifth Avenue, Suite 701, New York, N.Y. 10001. Family will be observing Shiva today through Thursday evening and Sunday at the residence.


        SCHWARZ-Erwin, 93. Of Tucson, AZ. Formerly of New York City and of Scarsdale, NY, died in Gaithersburg, MD, on February 1, 1998. He was married to the late Louise Lasker Schwarz for over 40 years and leaves his wife of 23 years, Julia B. (Judy) Schwarz, three children, Arthur of Scarsdale, NY, Peggy Akin of Phoenix, AZ, and John, of Tucson, AZ, eight grandchildren and ten greatgrandchildren. He received B.S. and M.S. degrees in Chemistry from Columbia University and entered the family business, Princeton Worsted Mills, in 1925; he was President of that company from 1939 until 1956. At that time he started a second career in education; after earning an M.A. in Classics from New York University, he taught Latin, Greek, History and Mathematics at Brooklyn College, NYU and the Fieldston School in New York and at the Greenfields School in Tucson. He also served as President of Westchester Jewish Community Services and Treasurer of the Arizona Friends of Music in Tucson. A man of wideranging interests and talents, he devoted much of his life to learning and to teaching; he loved music, gardens, books and, most of all, young people. A memorial service will be held at the Harmonie Club, 4 East 60th Street, New York, New York, on Sunday, February 8, at 2:00 PM. Contributions may be made to the Ethical Culture Fieldston Schools, 33 Central Park West, New York, New York 10023.

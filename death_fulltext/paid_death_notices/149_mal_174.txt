
        MacDONALD - Robert George. Robert George MacDonald, 36, of New York City, NY, died Sunday (June 6, 1999). Born on April 4, 1963, Rob grew up in Simsbury and was a former resident of Storrs and Willimantic. Rob attended the University of Connecticut and City College of New York. Rob was a romantic, philosopher and social activist who was instrumental in winning several civil liberty cases in New York City, NY and Chicago, IL. While living in Willimantic he founded and managed the Populist Pudding, an art gallery and night club. He is survived by his parents, Robert and Nancy MacDonald of Stamford, CT, a sister, Judith MacDonald-Lobato of Atlanta, GA, a brother, Douglas MacDonald of Vallejo, CA, and his maternal grandmother, Mrs. George Bustard of Chatham, NJ. A memorial service for family was held at Talcott Mountain State Park in Simsbury, CT Saturday, June 12. A memorial for friends and the public will be held at C.B. Gallery, 313 Bowery, New York City, NY on Thursday, June 17, from 7-10 pm. Donations may be made to Kids in Crisis, 1 Salem Street, Cos Cob, CT 06807.

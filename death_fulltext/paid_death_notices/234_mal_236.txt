
        KESSELER--Howard J., M.D., former Attending Surgeon at Lenox Hill Hospital in New York City, died of leukemia Wednesday at his home in Palm Beach, Florida. He was 83. Howard Joseph Kesseler was born on February 12, 1923 in Germany and grew up in Mahattan. He graduated from Amherst College in 1944 and from Cornell University Medical College in 1946, and also served in the United States Air Force at the end of World War II. He was associated with Lenox Hill Hospital from his internship in 1946 until his retirement in 1993. Dr. Kesseler was also Attending Physician at Bellvue Hospital, and Clinical Professor of Surgery at New York University and New York Medical College. In 1981, he was appointed to the New York State Public Health Council, on which he served until 1986. In addition to his wife, Sally, he is survived by his daughter, Heidi, his son, Howard J. Kesseler Jr. and daughter-inlaw, Jennifer, his grandson, Alexander Kesseler, and 4 nieces and one nephew. A Memorial Mass will be held in Palm Beach, FL, on Saturday, July 22, 2006. Contributions may be made to the Hospice of Palm Beach County, Inc., 5300 East Ave, W. Palm Beach, FL 33407.


        QUINN--John D. Former New York State Lottery Director, John D. Quinn of Saratoga Springs, NY, died November 16, 2005 at the age of 77. Born in New York City to Irish immigrants, Mr. Quinn was a career Army officer who retired as a Colonel in 1970 with the Bronze Star and Legion of Merit awards. From 1976 to 1988, he served as Director of the New York State Lottery, introducing Lotto to the U.S. gaming industry, making New York highest in sales among U. S. state lotteries, and enlisting celebrities like Frank Sinatra, Dean Martin, and Bob Hope to participate in drawings. He was instrumental in the acquisition of the USS Intrepid for the floating Sea, Air, and Space Museum in New York City. He is survived by Dora, his wife of 55 years; his children Carol Loulis, John Quinn, Anthony Quinn, and Timothy Quinn; his siblings James Quinn, William Quinn, and Kathleen Viccora; eight grandchildren; and a large, close, extended family. Funeral services, including burial with full military honors in Arlington National Cemetery, will be conducted on January 19, 2006. Contact the Adams-Green Funeral Home in Herndon, VA for details. In lieu of flowers memorial contributions may be sent to Catholic Relief Services, 209 West Fayette St., Baltimore, MD 21201-3443; or the American Hospice Foundation, 2120 L Street NW, Suite 200, Washington, D.C. 20037.

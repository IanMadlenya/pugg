
        BERGER-Ruth Louise Krug. Died at home July 30, 2000, age 87. Much loved by her many friends. Graduate of Montclair State Teachers College in 1933, and then volunteer teacher in North Carolina for one year. She was Administrative Secretary at the Herald Tribune, Personnel Director at Fairchild Publications, and Textbook Editor at Random House. She was a contralto lieder and oratorio singer and a member of the Metropolitan Opera chorus; for several years a soloist on WNBC and WOR radio and the Bell Telephone Hour; had a 15-minute weekly song program on WNYC; chosen by Virgil Thomson for lead alto role in the premiere of ''The Mother of Us All'' in 1947; soloist at Marble Collegiate Church & other churches and synagogues; active member of Sigma Alpha Iota, the international music fraternity; member of the Golden Eaglet Order of Girl Scouts, USA. Widow of Herman Berger, WOR Audio Engineer. Survived by her sister Gladys Luckenbach of Bluff City, TN.

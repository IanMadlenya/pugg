
        GOHDES-Clarence L.F. Noted author, pioneer scholar of American Literature, and James B. Duke Professor of English emeritus at Duke University, died December 8th. He received his Ph.D. from Columbia. He joined the English Department at Duke University in 1930, retiring in 1971. An expert on Whitman and Poe, Dr. Gohdes was editor of ''American Literature'', the prestigious journal of the American Literature section of the Modern Language Association. His ''Bibliographical Guide to the Study of the Literature of the USA'' has been standard in the field. Surviving are his wife, Celestine B. Gohdes, and two daughters, Eleanor Gohdes-Baten and Dr. Dorothy Gohdes.

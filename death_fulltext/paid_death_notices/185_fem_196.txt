
        KESSLEROHEN - Estelle/d. Born in 1909, died in New York City on June 24, 2002. She married Louis M. Cohen, a lawyer, and textile manufacturer and founding member of the United Jewish Appeal. They had two daughters Hannah and Sandra. When Louis died Estelle continued to work closely with the UJA and served as a Board member of the American Jewish Committees Women's Campaign. She later married Henry H. Kessler, founder of the Kessler Institute for Rehabilitation in New Jersey. In later years, after Henry's death, she spent most of her time working for Rehabilitation International and the Kessler Institute and watching her five grandchildren grow, David, Brian, Kenneth, Nikolas and Alexandr. She also stayed close to her son-in-law Herbert Halberg and David's wife Frances. You are welcome to pay your respects on Thursday, June 27th, from 7 to 9 PM at the Frank E. Campbell Funeral Home, 81st St and Madison Ave. Services Friday, June 28, 2002, 12 noon at Temple Emanu-El, Fifth Ave at 65th St. It will be followed by a burial service at Mt. Carmel Cemetery.
        KESSLER - Estelle. The Boards of Trustees and staff of The Henry H. Kessler Foundation and Kessler Medical Rehabilitation Research and Education Corporation mourn the passing of Estelle Kessler, wife of the late Dr. Henry H. Kessler, founder of Kessler Institute for Rehabilitation. Estelle was a wonderful woman whose generosity and good deeds spread throughout the world. She served as a Trustee and Trustee Emeritus of The Henry H. Kessler Foundation. Her drive and determination to help people with disabilities live fuller lives was surpassed only by her grace and charm. Her warmth and caring will be missed by all who knew her. With heartfelt sympathy, Thomas G. Greig III Chairman, Board of Trustees Henry H. Kessler Foundation

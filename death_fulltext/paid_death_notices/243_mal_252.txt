
        SCHLYEN--Samuel, M.D., 84, on April 18, after a four year battle with multiple myeloma, which he bore with strength and dignity. Survived by Maxine, his devoted wife of 41 years, loving children James, Peter (Renee), Elisabeth Ashbes, Alison, Jenifer Salzberg (Mark), granddaughters Emilie and Eliana, and sisters Edith Berman and Janet Verner. Predeceased by his first wife, Muriel, his grandson Adrian Ashbes and his brother Henry. Born in Passaic, NJ, an Eagle Scout, enlisted in the Army in 1942. Through Army Specialized Training Program and the G.I. Bill, attended Princeton and received his medical degree from Cornell Medical College (1949). Recalled during the Korean War, served at the National Cancer Institute, completed his fellowship at Memorial Sloan Kettering, where he became an adjunct professor. Private Practice in Rutherford, NJ, until his retirement to Boynton Beach, FL, in 2003 at the age of 80. Patriot, scholar, gentleman, devoted to and loved by his family, friends, colleagues and patients.

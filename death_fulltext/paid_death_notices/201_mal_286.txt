
        YUSKA--Henry. November 7, 1914 to October 25, 2003. Professor Henry B. Yuska died October 25, 2003 after a prolonged illness. For the past two years he has resided at Attic Angel Place. In his 89 years Henry lived a full and happy life. He began his career as an organic polymer chemist and was responsible for seven patents. He rose in the industry to Research Manager of Interchemical Corporation and later as Technical Manager of Sun Chemical Corporation manufacturing paints, coatings and varnishes. In mid-career he switched to academia as Professor and deputy chairman of chemistry at Brooklyn College of the City University of NY. Many of his students have gone on to become medical doctors and scientists. He is preceded in death by his wife Lillian and survived by his son Dr. Kenneth H. Yuska and daughterin-law Ginny Yuska of Madison WI and son Reynold Yuska of Queens, NY, two grandsons and one great-granddaughter. Henry loved his family, friends, colleagues, students, and his work. At his retirement as Professor Emeritus in 1985 his students established the Henry Yuska Organic Chemistry Award Fund for Outstanding Students.

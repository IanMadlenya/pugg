
        RIZK-Khalil. With great sorrow, the Board of Directors, Artist Members and staff of The Chamber Music Society of Lincoln Center mourn the sudden loss of our friend and former Director Khalil Rizk. A man of generosity and consummate elegance, Khalil cherished fine art, adored beautiful music, and valued good friends. His subtle and skillful influence enhanced New York's cultural life immeasurably. He will be greatly missed. We extend our deepest sympathies to his family and friends for their tremendous loss. Donaldson C. Pillsbury, Chairman Peter Frelinghuysen, President Marit Gruson, Vice-President David Shifrin, Artistic Director Norma Hurlburt, Exec. Director
        RIZK-Khalil. With deep sorrow the Officers, Directors and Members of The National Antique & Art Dealers Association of America, note the suddden passing of their esteemed member, Khalil Rizk. Khalil established The Chinese Porcelain Company in 1984. his gracious presence will be missed by all who knew him. We extend our deepest sympathy to his family and friends. Andrew H. Chait, President Arlie Sulka, Secretary
        RIZK-Khalil. New York City Opera mourns the death of a caring friend, supportive in so many ways. We hope his beloved survivors can one day find consolation in his legacy of grace, intelligence, achievement, and generosity. Paul Kellogg, General Director

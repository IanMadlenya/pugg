
        McKIERNAN-Francis B., 77. Of Middleburgh, New York, died on Tuesday, February 23rd, 1999, at the home of his daughter, following a long illness. He was born in Brooklyn on April 15, 1921, a son of Hugh and Beatrice Gilmartin McKiernan. He was raised in Ireland. He graduated from New York University with a Bachelor of Science degree in Electrical Engineering. He retired in 1978 from the New York Times where he had been a security guard for thirty years. He was a United States Army veteran of W.W. II and the Korean War. He was a member of the Veterans of Foreign Wars. He also was a member of the Newspaper Guild, Sein Fein, Clan Na Gael and the National Association for Irish Freedom. He was a communicant of St. Catherine's Church of Middleburgh. Survivors include his wife of fifty-three years, Mary Turner McKiernan, two daughters, Mary Cook of Mineola, NY and Eileen LaSpaluto of Middleburgh, two sons, Francis McKiernan of Middleburgh and Brian McKiernan of Floral Park, NY, his stepmother, Florence McKiernan of California, a sister, Marie Jennsen of California, 12 grandchildren, and eight great-grandchildren. A Mass of Christian Burial was celebrated on Friday, February 26th, at St. Catherine's Church, Middleburgh. Father Thomas Holmes, pastor of St. Catherine's Church, and Father Sean McMahon of St. Mary's Church and Our Lady of Knock Shrine, East Durham, New York were celebrants. A Spring burial will be made in Middleburgh Cemetery. Memorial contributions may be made to St. Catherine's Church, Main Street, Middleburgh, New York 12122 or Catskill Area Hospice, 1 MacArthur Avenue, Cobleskill, New York 12043.

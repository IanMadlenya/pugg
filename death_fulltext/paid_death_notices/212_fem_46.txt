
        BLUMBERG--Esterita (Cissie), 76, of Liberty, New York, died peacefully with her family at her side on September 3, 2004. She was the mother of Paul (and Noelle), and Amy (and Michael) Goldbas, Simsbury, CT, second mother of Kris Ohlson, grandmother of Matt, Jamie, Joanna, Jessie and Jake, sister of Robert (and Harriet) Rosenberg. Predeceased by her husband and best friend Larry Blumberg, they owned and operated Green Acres Hotel. She authored the popular book ''Remember the Catskills-Tales of a Recovering Hotelkeeper'' and was a columnist for the Sullivan County Democrat. She was active in progressive politics and community affairs, following in her father's footsteps, who was one of the first Socialists elected to the New York State Assembly, as well as the executive vice president of the Ladies Garment Workers Union. Cissie ran for Congress, was on the Board of Education, and was appointed by the governor of New York State to serve on the Board of Visitors for Rockland Psychiatric Center. Funeral services will be held on Wednesday, September 8, 2004, at 1 PM in the Garlick Funeral Home, 388 Broadway, Monticello, NY. The family will receive on Wednesday, September 8, 2004 at 4-8 and Thursday, September 9, 2004, at 2-8, at her home in Liberty. In lieu of flowers, donations in her memory may be sent to any group working to unseat George Bush, or to the New Hope Community, Box 289, Loch Sheldrake, New York 12759.


        LIDDELL -- Jane Hawley Hawkes, age 97, died peacefully at home July 20, 2005 of complications of a stroke. She had remained passionate and involved with her family and her causes until last week. The widow of Donald M. Liddell, Jr., with whom she had celebrated 59 years of marriage, she is survived by her daughter, Jane Liddell Bass, and her son and daughter-in-law, D. Roger Brooke and Florence Liddell, of New York City, and four grandchildren, Steedman L. Bass of Cambridge, MA, Margot S. Bass of Takoma Park, MD, Alice Liddell Rooke of Bloomfield, CT and Torrey B. Liddell of New York City. She was predeceased by one granddaughter, Catherine H. Bass. Burial was in Baltimore on July 25. A Memorial Service will be held September 16 at noon at St. James' Episcopal Church, Madison Avenue at 71 St. In lieu of flowers, contributions may be sent to the Liddell Memorial Garden at National Society of Colonial Dames, 215 East 71 Street, NY, NY 10021.
        LIDDELL -- Jane Hawley Hawkes. The Board of Directors of Youth Foundation, Inc. notes with profound sorrow the passing of its distinguished Director Emerita, Jane Liddell on July 20, 2005. She was the Foundation's long serving Vice President in which capacity she gave unstintingly of her time and energy. Her selfless contributions and dedication to the Foundation gave added luster to the term ''volunteer'' while her graciousness gave added luster to the term ''lady''. We extend our heartfelt condolences to her family. Robert W. Radsch, President Pamela S. Fulweiler, Vice President Guy N. Robinson, Secretary S. Scott Nicholls, Jr., Treasurer

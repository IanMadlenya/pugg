
        PARSA--Mohamad H., M.D., Professor of Clinical Surgery, Columbia University, College of Physicians and Surgeons and Director of the Trauma Center, Harlem Hospital Center passed away February 9th at the age of 64. An avid teacher and writer, he dedicated the majority of his professional efforts to total parenteral nutrition and is credited with its implementation and usage in New York State in the late 1960s. Personal interests included philosophy, poetry, and religion although his loyalty and dedication rested with his profession. A departmental commendation awarded June 2003 reads, ''Mohamad will forever be remembered as a distinguished surgeon and extraordinary human being who deprived himself of sleep and comfort in order to serve humanity''. He is survived by his wife, Gretchen and his son, Cyrus who reside in Bronxville, NY. Visiting hours will be held at the Whalen & Ball Funeral Home, 168 Park Avenue, Yonkers, NY on Thursday evening, February 12 from 7-9PM.

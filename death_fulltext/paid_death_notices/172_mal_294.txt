
        SIMINOFF-Paul, 78, immunologist. Paul Siminoff, a leading immunologist who founded the Immunology Department at Bristol Myers, died May 21 after a long illness at his home in Syracuse, New York. Born in New York City on May 8, 1923, he received his doctoral degree from the University of Illinois in 1951 after having served during World War II in the Army Signal Corps where he took part in the Invasion of Normandy. At Bristol Myers he worked on antiviral drugs to treat cancer and in 1977 he created the Immunology Department to pursue pioneering work in interferon as an anti-cancer agent. He also developed drugs for the treatment of asthma. He was a passionate advocate for social justice and worked actively for numerous organizations, including the Union of Concerned Scientists and the United Way. Dr. Siminoff is survived by his wife, Mildred, his daughters, Laura, Faren and Julie Siminoff and two grandsons, Jason and Sasha.

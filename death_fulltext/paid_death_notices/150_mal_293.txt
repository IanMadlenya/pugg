
        KING-Robert Augustin, 88, passed away on Friday, July 9th, 1999 in Memorial SW Hospital in Houston. Bob King was born on September 3, 1910 in Marion, Indiana, the son of Roy Melvin King and Estella Bernice Sheron King. He moved to Oklahoma at age 18 and earned a BS in chemical engineering from the University of Oklahoma at Norman, class of '35. Bob was a 2nd Lieutenant in the Field Artillery Reserve. He joined Phillips Petroleum Company in Borger, Tex., in 1935 as a ditch-digger and worked his way up to chief chemist. He left Phillips in 1943 to join E.B. Badger & Sons as a senior process engineer, working in New York and London until 1953. During this time he was involved in the Lend-Lease program and the planning stages of the Marshall Plan. From 1954-1956, he was the district manager for Stone & Webster in New York. In 1957, he became the managing director for Badger Company in The Hague, Netherlands. He left in 1964. In 1965, he founded King-Wilkinson, Inc., and guided it as president until 1984. In 1985, he founded Robert A. King, Inc., and functioned as president until 1998, when he assumed the role of chairman. Bob King is survived by his wife, Johanna Akkerman King; his son, Robert Alexander King; daughters Mary Elizabeth, Sharon Johanna and Estella Regina; grandsons Robert Frederick and James Dudley Wesseldijk and James Bernard King; granddaughters Rebecca King O'Rian and Deborah King; great-grandson Aidan O'Rian. A memorial service will be held at 2 P.M., Thursday, July 22, 1999 in the chapel of Geo. H. Lewis & Sons, 1010 Bering Drive, Houston, Texas. The family requests that in lieu of flowers, donations be made to the Robert A. King Chemical Engineering Scholarship Fund, University of Oklahoma Foundation, 100 Timberdell, Norman, Oklahoma 73019.

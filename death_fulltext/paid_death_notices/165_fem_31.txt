
        PIZZURRO-Margaret Elizabeth Holtkamp, beloved wife of Joseph C. Pizzurro and mother of Joseph, Peter, Virginia, Mary (Esposito), Elizabeth (Ossoff) and Catherine (Hliboki) and grandmother of ten died September 30, 2000 following a short hospitalization. Her two sisters, Sr. Catherine Mary and Esther (Wenzel), and her brother Edward also survive her. Mrs. Pizzurro was born in Kalida, Ohio on December 21, 1924, the second daughter of Edward and Mary Holtkamp. Mrs. Pizzurro attended Mary Manse College of Toledo, Ohio and in 1946 was awarded a B.A. in Chemistry. She earned a M.S. degree from the University of Detroit in Biology in 1948, a notable achievement for a woman at that time. Mrs. Pizzurro is remembered by her friends and family as a woman of unfailing grace and kindness. She was extensively involved in church and charitable activities. She had a lifelong love of music, which she shared through the piano, her singing and work in amateur musicals. Her strength and inner beauty will be cherished by her family and all those whose lives she touched. She was an active parishioner at Our Lady of Perpetual Help in Ardsley for over forty years and most recently attended St. Augustine's parish in Ossining. Calling hours at The Fred H. McGrath & Son Funeral Home, Bronxville between the hours of 7 to 9PM Monday 2 to 4 and 7 to 9PM Tuesday. Mass of Christian Burial St. Joseph's Church, Bronxville on Wednesday, Oct. 4 at 10AM. In lieu of flowers donations may be made in her name for Alzheimer's disease research to the Mayo Foundation, 200 First Street SW, Rochester, Minnesota 55905.

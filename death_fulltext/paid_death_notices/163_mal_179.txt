
        WELLS-George, 88. Of Hayden Lakes, ID, died August 12, 2000 at Life Care Center of Coeur D'Alene following a lengthy illness. He was born March 19, 1912 in Colon, Panama where his father was one of four departmental heads on the construction of the Canal. Mr. Wells attended public school in Montclair, New Jersey and preparatory school in Massachusetts. He graduated from Yale University in 1933 and Harvard Business School in 1935. He then attended New York City Law School for one year. Following two years in the New York offices of Air Reduction Company, he joined the Union Carbide Corporation where he spent 35 years in various plant and general management capacities. He was president of Union Carbide's former international division from 1958 until 1963, traveling extensively around the world. In 1964, he became a general vice president of the corporation. He married Zazilia Grabner of Ebensee, Austria on August 16, 1968. He retired from the corporation in 1974. After his retirement, they moved to Incline Village, Nevada where they lived for 5 years. In 1979 they moved to Hayden Lake, Idaho. He is survived by his wife of 33 years, Zazilia Wells at home; brother, Thomas M. Wells of Montclair, NJ and by a prior marriage a son, Bradley H. Wells of San Francisco, CA and daughter, Sarah Robertson of Connecticut; three granddaughters; 2 stepchildren, Stuart of Spokane, WA and Virginia of South Australia; A funeral service will be held 4:00 P.M. Wednesday, August 16, 2000 at Yates Funeral Home, Hayden Chapel with Pastor Eric Ranum officiating. A reception will follow at the Yates Hayden Gathering Center. Interment will be at Pines Mausoleum in Spokane, Washington at a later date. Yates Funeral Home, Hayden Chapel is in charge of arrangements. . The family prefers memorials to North Idaho Cancer Center, 700 Ironwood, Coeur D'Alene, Idaho 83814.

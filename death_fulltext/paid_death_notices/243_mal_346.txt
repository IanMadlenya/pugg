
        WIENER--Ernest Guest, 91, died peacefully in his sleep, surrounded by his family, on April 10th, 2007. He was in hospice care for liver cancer and lived with his daughter, Ann, in Riverside, California for his last five months. Born in Pilzen, he left Nazi-occupied Czechoslovakia in February, 1940. In December, 1942 he joined the US Army Air Corps and became a US citizen a few months later. Sent to England with the 392nd Heavy Bombardment Group, he married Peggy Rhodes in December, 1944 and had four children. He worked as a diplomat for the US Department of State from 1948-1970. He served in Washington DC and Germany, (Berlin, Frankfurt and Bonn), Vienna, Geneva, New York, Moscow and Brasilia. He retired in Brazil and worked for Standard Electrica (ITT) in Rio de Janeiro for ten years until he retired again, to Key Biscayne, Florida.


        ASEDO-Adina Stitzel, at 93, of Manhattan, died on Wednesday, November 3, 1999, at home. Born in London, England, in 1906, she came to the U.S. in 1912. She lived on the Lower East Side where she taught Hebrew before sailing to Palestine. In Jerusalem she met and married Dr. Jeroham Asedo, who died in 1992, after 62 years of marriage. She was active in many Zionist organizations and founded Oron, a Hebrew braille magazine. She is survived by two daughters, Yael Freedman of New York, Tamar Sherman of Northport; a son, Ethan Asedo of Marlboro, New Jersey, and nine grandchildren. Graveside services will be held at 11 AM today at Beth Israel Cemetery in Woodbridge, New Jersey.

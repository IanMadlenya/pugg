
        FABRE-Richard E. A member of the piano faculty at the Juilliard School of Music, Pre-College Division since 1961, died March 6, 1999. He was 68. Born in Washington, D.C. on December 22, 1930 to Velmont J. Fabre and Lucy D. Fabre, he began his music education in Sarasota, Florida at the age of 15. He graduated with honors from the University of Miami in Coral Gables, then studied under a two year Fulbright Grant at the Conservatoire Nationale de Paris. As a scholarship student at the Juilliard School, he was a student of Rosina Lhevinne. He has appeared in recitals, as soloist with orchestra, and in chamber concerts in this country and Europe. His students have been prize winners in virtually every national competition open to pre-college age. Mr. Fabre is survived by a sister, Mary Alice Noel of Nooksack, Washington, and three nephews and one neice of Juneau, Alaska. Interment will be in Sarasota, Florida. Contributions may be made to a charity of your choice.

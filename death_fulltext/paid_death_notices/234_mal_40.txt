
        O'CONNOR--George E. Died at his home in Manhattan on June 24th, 2006 after a long illness. He was 89 years of age. After enlisting in the army during World War II he served in Belgium, France, Holland and Germany and advanced to the rank of Lieutenant Colonel. He was awarded the Silver Star, the Bronze Star, the Purple Heart, the Croix de Guerre and the Combat Infantry Mans Badge. His career was spent at New York Life Insurance Company from which he retired in 1982 as Executive Vice President. During his tenure he completed the AMP at Harvard University. From 1982 until 1989 he acted as consultant for NYLIC in Washington, DC. He also served on the Board of Aqua Chem. He is survived by his wife of 60 years Dorothy; three children Gail Bushey of Brookville, NY, Jane and Christopher of Manhattan; son-in-Law and daughter-in-law, three grand children, and three greatgrandchildren. A memorial service will be held July 18th at 10AM at St. Vincent Ferrer at 66th Street and Lexington Ave. In lieu of flowers memorial donations may be made to The Lenox Hill Hospital Auxiliary, 100 East 77th Street, New York, NY 10021.

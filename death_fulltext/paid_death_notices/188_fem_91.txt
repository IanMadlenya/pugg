
        MARICLE-Marijane. January 13, 1922-July 30, 2002. Singer-actress, beloved friend. Maricle's long, diverse career included Broadway/National Company/Off-Broadway productions of Bye Bye Birdie, Paint Your Wagon, Allegro, Butterflys are Free, Carousel and Hair. She appeared in many commercials and on the Jackie Gleason, Ed Sullivan and Arthur Godfrey television shows. In 1991, she was invited to perform a one-woman show recapping her career at the LBJ Library and Museum. Born in Wichita Falls and a graduate of the University of Texas, she kept her roots in her home state, even though she resided in Manhattan for over 50 years. Her many friends across the country will remember her extraordinary spirit, humor and graciousness.

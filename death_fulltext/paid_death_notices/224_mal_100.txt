
        HIVNOR--Robert H. A playwright and teacher, died at his home in New York City on September 8. Born in Zanesville, OH, in 1915, he was educated at the University of Akron and Yale University. From 1942 to 1945 he served in the US Army and with the British Government Code and Cipher School at Bletchley Park. He taught writing and literature at the University of Minnesota, Reed College and Bard College. His publications include the plays ''Too Many Thumbs,'' ''The Ticklish Acrobat,'' and ''The Assault Upon Charles Sumner.'' He will be dearly missed by his wife Mary, his three children, James Hivnor, Hank Hivnor and Maggie Hivnor LaBarbera, and grandchildren Kathleen and Claire LaBarbera. A memorial service will be held Friday, October 7, 2005, at 3:30 pm, at Frank E. Campbell Funeral Chapel, 1076 Madison Ave at 81st St, New York, NY 10028.


        LATSEY-Van Peter. A prominent gynecologist/obstetrician passed away on July 28, 1998. Dr. Latsey graduated from Medical School at the University of Athens and trained at Boston City Hospital and St. Luke's Hospital in New York City. He remained on staff at St. Luke's and served as Chief of the Clinic at Women's Hospital, until his retirement in 1980. He was a member of the teaching staff of Columbia University Medical School, Diplomate of the Obstetrics and Gynecology Board, and Member of the Medical Society of New York State. Dr. Latsey was a respected and active member of the Greek community and the Greek Orthodox Church, having the title Archon Aktouarios conferred upon him in 1979 by His Eminence Archbishop Iakovos. He served as Vice President of St. Michael's Home for the Aged, Member of the Board of Trustees of the Archdiocesan Cathedral of the Holy Trinity, and Member of the Order of Orthodox Crusaders of the All-Holy Tomb of Christ. He is remembered as a loving husband, a kind gentleman, and a mentor and generous friend to many. His wife, Angelika, and nephews and nieces, in Athens, Washington, Boston and New York survive him. Reposing at Frank E. Campbell Funeral Chapel, 81st and Madison Ave., New York on July 30th from 6PM-9PM. Funeral services will be held at the Greek Orthodox Archdiocesan Cathedral of the Holy Trinity, 319 East 74th Street, New York City, on July 31st at 10AM. Interment Pinelawn Memorial Park. In lieu of flowers, donations to St. Michael's Home for the Aged, 3 Lehman Terrace, Yonkers, NY 10705, in memory of Dr. Latsey, will be appreciated.

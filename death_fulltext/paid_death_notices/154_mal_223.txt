
        NOLAN-William J. Jr. Of Irvington-on-Hudson, New York and Avon, Connecticut, died on November 17. The cause of death was prostate cancer. Mr. Nolan was born in Cambridge, Massachusetts in 1919. He was a graduate of Milton Academy (1937), Yale University (1941) and Yale Law School (1943). He spent his career as a tax lawyer and retired in 1981 as Vice President and Director of Taxes of Amax, Inc. He had leadership roles in several organizations, including the American Mining Congress and the International Chamber of Commerce. In the 1960's, Mr. Nolan was a member of the team that won the New York State Curling Championship. Mr. Nolan leaves his devoted wife of 55 years, Alice Edwards Nolan, 4 children and 2 children-in-law, William J. Nolan, III and his wife Wendy of New York City, Sally Wells Nolan of West Hartford, Connecticut, Beth Nolan of Washington DC and Lucy Pritchard Nolan and her husband Douglas Eichar of West Hartford, and 5 grandchildren, William J. Nolan IV, Anina Nolan, Nick Eichar, Andy Eichar and Chris Eichar. A memorial service will be held at the Immaculate Conception Church in Irvington at 11am on Monday, November 22. In lieu of flowers, contributions may be made to the Irvington Volunteer Ambulance Corps, P.O. Box 101, Irvington, NY 10533,or Phelps Memorial Hospital, 701 N. Broadway, Sleepy Hollow, New York 10591.

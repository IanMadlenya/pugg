
        HASSENFELT -- Marjorie (Sally). Broadway stage manager and theater consultant, died on Friday after a long battle with MS, according to her companion, David Pleva. She was fifty-two. Ms. Hassenfelt, during her career, served on Broadway as a stage manager for ''42nd Street'', ''Annie'',and ''Fiddler On The Roof'' (at the State theater at Lincoln Center). She was the Executive Director of The New Amsterdam Theater Company, in New York, and recently served as a theater consultant. Ms. Hassenfelt was born in Atlanta, GA, lived in Southern Pines, N.C., and was a graduate of The N.C. School of the Arts, in Winston Salem, North Carolina. She worked initially in New York as resident stage manager for the drama division of The Juilliard School under John Houseman, at The Goodspeed Opera House in East Haddam, CT, and for The Pauline Koner Dance Consort (The American Dance Festival 1978). In addition to working with national tours, regional theater, offBroadway and summer stock, she was involved with the Berkshire Theater Festival, Shakespeare In The Park, and The Milwaukee Repertory Company. In addition to her companion, David Pleva, she is survived by two brothers and a sister, Stephen C. Hassenfelt, of Greensboro, NC, Harold E. Hassenfelt, of Wilmington, NC, and Maureen H. Clark, of Southern Pines, NC. A memorial service will be held on Thursday, from 3-5 P.M. at John Krtil Funeral Home, 1297 First Avenue at 70th Street, and a family burial service at Holy Trinity Episcopal Church, Glendale Springs, NC.

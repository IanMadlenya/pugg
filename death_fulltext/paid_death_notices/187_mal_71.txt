
        THERON-Jacobus J. (known as ''Johan''), on August 3, after a long illness. He was 78. Born and educated in South Africa, Theron came to the United States in 1952 as a member of his country's diplomatic mission. He resigned in 1956 because of South Africa's racial policies and joined the United Nations Secretariat. He was a senior officer in the budget division for a number of years and was then appointed Chief Editor of the United Nations & Deputy to the Under Secretary General for the Department of Conference Services. In retirement he translated from Dutch into English, including the study ''Extremes'' by A.J. Dunning, and was a passionate gardener. He is survived by his companion, John Ferrone.

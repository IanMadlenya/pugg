
        MARSH-Robert Edward, Jr. Died suddenly at age 83 at his home in Forest Hills, New York, on December 11, 1997. Born on June 6, 1914, the son of Robert Edward Marsh and Grace Sophronia Marsh, nee Rollinson. Served in the U.S. Army during World War II on the staff of General Douglas MacArthur. Following his military service, joined the Arabian American Oil Company (Aramco), serving that organization and its affiliate, the Trans Arabian Pipe Line Company (Tapline), for 30 years, primarily in Saudi Arabia and Lebanon. Following his retirement and until his death, was associated with The Olayan Group in Beirut, London, Athens, and, in recent years, New York. Reflecting his long experience and interest in the Middle East, was a devoted memeber of several non-profit oranizations based in Washington, DC and New York, among them: the American-Arab Anti-Discrimination Committee; the Arab American Institute; American Near East Refugee Aid, where he was a director from 1980 to 1986 and 1988 to 1992; Americans for Middle East Understanding, where he was a founding director from 1978 until his death; and The Middle East Institute, where he served on the Board of Governors from 1990 to 1996. Also a long-time member of the Union League Club of New York and the West Side Tennis Club of Forest Hills. Survived by a sister, Mrs. Roslyn Rollinson Marsh Steinbach, of La Jolla, CA. Beloved uncle of six nieces and nephews: Roslyn Steinbach Tate of Philadelphia, PA; Sandra Rollinson Freiburg of San Diego, CA; Henry Hof of Hackensack, NJ; Robert Marsh Hof of Forest Hills; Nancy Hof Georgia of Long Island, NY; and Philip Marsh Hof of Easton, PA.


        TRIMBLE--Henry Weeks, Jr., Esq. Longtime resident of New York City was born June 6, 1920 in Montclair, N.J. and died January 31, 2004 from complications following surgery. Henry graduated from Princeton University, Yale University Law School and Harvard Business School. He served in the U.S. Army as a 2nd Lieutenant. He first practiced law in New York City with the firm of Cravath, Swaine & Moore and then became General Counsel and Secretary of IBM. He later became an investment banker at Hayden Stone. After his retirement, Henry continued practicing estate law. He and his wife Jane often traveled to their various homes in French Provence, the Caribbean Island of Anguilla and their country home in the Hamptons. Henry was a gifted pianist, singer and a devoted sports enthusiast. Mr. Trimble was a governor of the Metropolitan Club of New York City and a member of the Quogue Field Club and the Quogue Beach Club. He is survived by his loving wife of 20 years, Jane Imbs Trimble of New York City and devoted children, Pamela Mariani (John), of Bermuda, Henry Weeks Trimble III (Joan) of Charleston, S.C. and Neill Trimble (Blair) of Locust Valley, N.Y., brother Jim Trimble (Peggy) of Jacksonville, FL; 8 grandchildren and one great grandchild. Henry was dearly loved by all who knew him and he will be greatly missed. Services will be held at the Frank E. Campbell Funeral Chapel, 1076 Madison Ave (81st St), NYC on Thursday, February 5, at 1:30PM.


        De GRAAFF-Dr. Frances. Professor Emeritus of Russian at Bryn Mawr College. Died October 22, 1997. She taught at Bryn Mawr and Haverford Colleges for 26 years where she expanded the Russian program into a full scale graduate and undergraduate department. Professor De Graaff was born in the Netherlands, received her Ph.D. at the University of Leyden in 1933 and from 1933-1936 worked in Moscow as a translator and continued her research on the Russian poet Sergey Esenin. After returning to Holland, she taught French and Russian and was active in the Anti-Fascist movement. She came to the United States in 1939 and taught at Reed, Black Mountain, and Wells Colleges before joining Bryn Mawr and Haverford Colleges in 1947. She served as Chair of the Russian department from 1964 until retirement in 1973 when she received the Lindback Award for Excellence in Teaching. She is survived by seven nieces and nephews. Her family and friends miss her.

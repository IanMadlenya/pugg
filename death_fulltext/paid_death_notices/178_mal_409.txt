
        GOTTESMAN-Harold Aaron, 75. Died peacefully at home in Old Lyme, Conn., on November 28. A longtime figure in the New York real estate community, Mr. Gottesman co-founded the Edison Parking Corporation, parent of ParkFast garages and lots, Manhattan Mini Storage and the Hippodrome office building. After serving more than four decades as president of the company, he retired in 1998 to establish Edison Investment Advisors, a familyowned fund. Loving companion of Madelaine Goldish, devoted father of Ellen Garber, Robin Gottesman Levine and Andrew, father-in-law of Lawrence, Richard and Christine, grandfather of Trudy, Kate, Molly, Amy, Peter and Zachary, and brother of Jerome, A. Edward and the late Arlene Reff. Mr. Gottesman earned B.S. and M.S. degrees in mechanical engineering at the Carnegie Institute of Technology in 1946. He graduated from the United States Military Academy at West Point in 1950 and became a bomber pilot in the U.S. Air Force. Mr. Gottesman was a board member of the U.S.S. Intrepid Sea, Air and Space Museum and the Avenue of the Americas Association. He also was a member of the group that founded the West Point Jewish Chapel and remained an active participant on the Board of Overseers. Services will be held at the West Point Jewish Chapel on Friday, November 30. Guests must leave from the William F. Hogan Funeral Home, 135 Main St. in Highland Falls, 845-446-2868, at 9:15 A.M. In lieu of flowers, donations may be made to the Harold A.

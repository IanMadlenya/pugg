
        RUDNICK-Rebecca S., 48. Was drowned on June 2 in Papua New Guinea when the boat on which she was a passenger capsized and sank. She was a Visiting Professor of Law at Boston College. The author of numerous papers in the taxation area, she was a ''floating'' professor whose 14 appointments in the nation's law schools and as Prof-in-Residence at the IRS totaled more than any other tax specialist. She also held a variety of overseas appointments, and was an Advisor to the IMF. She was raised in Bakersfield, CA, received her J.D. from the University of Texas, and her B.A. from Willamette University. She served as law clerk to the Hon. Charles Schwartz, Jr., U.S. District Judge for the E.D. of Louisiana. A member of the New York, California, Texas, and Louisiana bars, she practiced law in New York for six years at Winthrop, Stimson, Putnam & Roberts. She was an accomplished sportswoman, who made frequent diving, fly fishing, and skiing expeditions throughout the world. She is survived by her husband, Robert Anthoine, also a tax attorney who frequently taught with her, and 8 half-brothers and sisters. Services will be held at Temple Beth El, Bakersfield, CA, on June 18 at 11 AM. A memorial service will be scheduled in New York in September. In lieu of flowers, contributions may be made to the Rebecca Rudnick Memorial Scholarship, Bakersfield High School, 1241 G Street, Bakersfield, CA. 93301.

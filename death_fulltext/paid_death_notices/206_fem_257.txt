
        WILSON--June Parker, of New York City and Chilmark, MA, died on March 6 of Alzheimer's disease. She was 87 years old. Ms. Wilson translated 10 books from French to English, most in collaboration with Walter Benn Michaels, including the best-seller Papillon, by Henri Charriere. She was born in Oakland, CA, the daughter of Carleton H. Parker, a well-known labor expert, and Cornelia Stratton Parker, a writer best known for ''An American Idyll'', an account of her marriage to Dr. Parker. Ms. Wilson graduated from Bennington College in 1939, and served on its board. Her husband of 51 years, Kenneth Wilson, died in 1991. She is survived by daughters, Cornelia Higginson, Lee Badger, and Meredith Michaels, nine grandchildren, and seven great-grandchildren.

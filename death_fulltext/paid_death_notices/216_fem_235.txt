
        NEGLIA -- Josephine. Died January 21, at the age of 91. She was born in Oyster Bay, NY, to Concetta and Frank Neglia. As a St. Luke's Nursing School graduate, she served as a captain in the army nurse corps, WWII, with the second evacuation hospital in Normandy and Europe, and received four battle ribbons. She received her BS degree from Columbia University and also studied medical art at the College of Physicians and Surgeons, Columbia University. She was Chief Medical Illustrator with the VA Medical Center, in New York. Her medical illustrations have appeared in professional journals, books, national exhibits, motion pictures and on TV. Upon her retirement she began a second career as a Chinese watercolor artist and studied with prominent artists at the New York China Institute and traveled to the Far East. Her several exhibitions include displays at the Society of Illustrators, Columbia University and the China Institute. She was member of St. Bartholomew's Church, The Society of Illustrators, Association of Medical Illustrators and Biological Photographic Association. She was predeceased by her siblings, Jennie Mazza, Carmela Martoccia, Rose Martoccia and Louis Neglia. She was a loving aunt, survived by three generations of nieces and nephews.

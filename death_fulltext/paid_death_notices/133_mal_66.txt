
        SIMMONS-Harry. January 14, 1998 at the Waveny Care Center in New Canaan, Connecticut. He was 90 years old. Harry was a baseball historian, writer, schedule maker, and The Authority on the rules of the game. He worked for the International Baseball League from 1945 until 1966 first in New York then in Montreal (1953-59). He then worked in the Baseball Commissioner's office until his retirement in 1982. He developed the playing schedules for the Majors and various Minor leagues for over 20 years. Well known as a historian and writer he did much original research into 19th Century baseball. He developed and wrote the weekly feature ''So You Think You Know Baseball'' which ran in the Saturday Evening Post from 1949 to 1961. His book under the same name was a best seller. For many years he wrote the entry for baseball in the Encyclopedia Britannica. In 1951, Harry testified as an expert witness to the Congressional hearings on the history of the reserve clause. At the 1979 baseball winter meeting in Toronto, he was honored as ''King of Baseball.'' In 1990 his contributions to the game were recognized by receiving the SABR (Society for American BAseball Research) Salute. Surviving are three sons, Ivor Ernest Simmons of Huntington Station, N.Y., Samuel Zackery Simmons of New York City, and David Ernest Simmons of Toronto; two daughters, Ann Preston of Trumbull, Connecticut, and Sara Valentine of New Canaan, Ct. and a brother Sidney Simon of Long Boat Key, Fla. He was predeceased by his wife of 50 years, Margaret Curry Simmons; and two sisters Elizabeth Issen, and Lois Rohr. A graveside service was held on January 16, at Beth El Cemetery in Westwood, N.J.

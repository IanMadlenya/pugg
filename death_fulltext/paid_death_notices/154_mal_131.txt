
        GALIMIR-Felix. On November 10, 1999. Violinist and teacher. Beloved husband of the late Suzanne. Brother of the late Marguerite, Renee and Adrienne. Cherished uncle of Elsa Miller, Richard Hurtig, Naomi Krasner, Viola Morse & the late Vien Krasner. Services Friday, 10AM at ''The Riverside'', 76 Street and Amsterdam Avenue.
        GALIMIR-Felix. The Board and staff of Lincoln Center for the Performing Arts, Inc. and the Executive Committee of the Avery Fisher Artist Program mourn the passing of our dear longtime colleague, violinist Felix Galimir. As a member of the Juilliard School Faculty since 1962, and as a long standing member of the Executive Committee of the Avery Fisher Artist Program, he made an inestimable contribution to Lincoln Center as a mentor to young artists. We send our heartfelt condolences to his family. Beverly Sills, Chairman Nathan Leventhal, President Lincoln Center for the Performing Arts, Inc. Chairman, Avery Fisher Artist Program
        GALIMIR-Felix. The Juilliard School deeply mourns the death of longtime faculty member, violinist Felix Galimir. Since 1962, Julliard musicians have been privileged to share the insight, humor, passion, and humanity that made Mr. Galimir's presence in the world of music so remarkable. We send our heartfelt condolences to the family. The artistry and sense of history that were his gifts to Juilliard and the profession will be profoundly missed by us all. Mary Rodgers Guettel, Chairman Joseph W. Polisi, President
        GALIMIR-Felix. The faculty, students, and alumni of The Curtis Institute of Music are saddened by the death of Felix Galimir, longtime friend, head of our chamber music studies, and teacher. We will miss his humor, wit, and incomparable musical knowledge, which he imparted for so many years to our students. Gary Graffman President/Director

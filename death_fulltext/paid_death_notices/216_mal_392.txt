
        FEINGOLD--Joseph, born in Fleischman, New York, on December 12, 1912, to Charles Feingold & the former Bessie Feinstein. He was raised in Brooklyn, New York, and died in Phoenix, Arizona, on January 22, 2005. In recent years he resided in Grosse Ile, Michigan. Dr. Feingold attended the University of Michigan, graduating Phi Beta Kappa in 1933 and he graduated from the University of Michigan Medical School in 1937. He served in the U.S. Army Medical Corps' European Theater during World War II. Dr. Feingold practiced general and internal medicine for over forty years in Fort Edward, New York. He was the quintessential small-town doctor carrying a black bag and making house calls. He was predeceased by his wife, the former Ruth Bartlett. He is survived by their four daughters: Estelle (George) Bletsas of Grosse Ile, Michigan; Margaret (Charles) Owens of Grosse Ile, Michigan; Lois (Laurence) Feingold-Taff of Cabin John, Maryland; and Barbara (David) Chardavoyne of San Antonio, Texas. He is also survived by seven grandchildren and ten greatgrandchildren. Dr. Feingold was a strong patient advocate, a voracious reader, a lover of history, and a lifelong reader of the New York Times.


        TANENBAUM-Mary Mayer, for 59 years beloved wife of Charles J. Tanenbaum, and loving mother of Ann Tanenbaum King. Born in San Francisco, the greatgranddaughter of Marcus Reyman, a California pioneer, she won the City Girl's Tennis championship and continued her tennis career at Stanford where she was also a junior member of Phi Beta Kappa. Beginning as an assistant book reviewer with the San Francisco Chronicle, she continued as a freelance journalist for various publications, and was an active member of the Overseas Press Club. She was deeply interested in Chinese Art and Culture and wrote over 50 essays in this field for the Christian Science Monitor. She also assembled an important collection of Chinese paintings which will pass to the Stanford Museum of Art, where there will be a seminar room in her name. Services will be held at Frank E. Campbell, 1076 Madison Avenue (at 81 St), Sunday, October 12, at 1:00 P.M. Contributions may be made in her name to the Stanford Museum of Art, Stanford, CA. 94305-5060.
        TANENBAUM-Mary. The Officers and Board of Trustees of the Jewish Foundation for Education of Women note with sorrow the passing of Mary Tanenbaum, wife of Charles, our former President and Chairman, and esteemed Boardmember for nearly 50 years. Mary was a talented and charming woman. We extend heartfelt condolences to Charles and Annie. Alan Cohn, Chairman Susan Schatz, President
        TANENBAUM-Mary Mayer. Congregation Emanu-El of the City of New York records with deep sorrow the death of our cherished member, Mary Mayer Tanenbaum. To her family and loved ones we express our profound sympathy. May beautiful memory bring comfort to those who mourn her passing. David E. Marrus, President Herbert C. Bernard, Secretary

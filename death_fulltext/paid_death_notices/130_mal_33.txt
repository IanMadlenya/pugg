
        GRANVILLE-SMITH-Walter, III, age 63, of Mollusk, Virginia, died November 1, 1997. The son of the late Walter and Jean GranvilleSmith, he was born in New York City on October 5th, 1934. He is survived by his wife, Pamela, his four sons, Richard, Stephen, David and Peter of Rowayton, CT., and his sister, Beverly Bullwinkle of Westport, CT. Mr. Granville-Smith was a 1952 graduate of Choate in Wallingford, CT., and a 1956 graduate of Dartmouth in Hanover, N.H. He began his business career in advertising with Benton & Bowles in New York City. He later worked at Young & Rubicam as a Vice President, and Needham, Harper & Steers as a Senior Vice President. In 1987, he and his wife, Pamela, moved to Mollusk, Virginia, where they ran a bed and breakfast inn at Greenvale Manor. He was a member of the Northern Neck Board of Realtors. While living in New York and Connecticut, he was an avid sailor, which included one-design and offshore yacht racing. Throughout his life, he had a passion for boating and fishing. A memorial service will be held on Tuesday, November 4 at 11 AM at the Grace Episcopal Church in Kilmarnock, Virginia. The family requests that in lieu of flowers, contributions may be made to the Upper Lancaster Rescue Squad, P.O. Box 176, Lively, Virginia 22507 or the Upper Lancaster Fire Department, P.O. Box 205, Lively, VA. 22507.

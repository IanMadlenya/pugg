
        CALLAWAY-Trowbridge, Jr., retired sugar company executive died February 17, 1996 after a long illness at his home in Delray Beach, Florida. Mr. Callaway formerly lived in Quogue, Long Island, New York, and in NYC. Born in 1912 in New York City, Mr. Callaway was educated at the Gunnery School and Harvard. He received general brokerage and investment training at George H. Farrington and Co., New York City and was with Clark Dodge & Company until 1942, when he went to Brown Brother Harriman and Company. He was a director of North American Sugar Company for many years and was VicePresident and Treasurer of that firm until 1970 when he retired. He was active in the Church of Atonement in Quogue, Long Island, a member of the Harvard Club of New York, and the Quogue Field Club. Mr. Callaway is survived by his wife Diana Stokes Callaway; by three sons: Trowbridge Callaway III of Ocean Ridge, FL, Norman T. Callaway of Princeton, NJ, and Tyler S. Callaway of La Jolla, CA, five grandchildren, two great grandchildren and by a brother, Samuel R. Callaway of Southbury, CT. In lieu of flowers contributions may be sent to the Church of the Atonement, P.O. Box 928, Quogue, New York 11959. Funeral services will be private with a memorial service planned for the summer in Quogue, Long Island.

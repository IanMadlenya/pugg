
        McINERNY-Matthew R., 83, a retired construction engineer, died December 27, 1999 at home in Melbourne Beach, Florida. Mr. McInerny was born in South Bend, Indiana, and graduated with a B.S.C.E. degree from Notre Dame University in 1937. He worked for several construction companies during his career including Burly Construction Corp., Resonant Pile Corp., Raymond International, Merritt, Chapman & Scott, and Conduit and Foundation Corp. He was active in the heavy construction industry in Chicago, Chattanooga, New York City & Baltimore. A Marine Corps veteran of World War II, he was the recipient of the Purple Heart from action in Okinawa. Mr. McInerny was a member of Immaculate Conception Catholic Church in Melbourne Beach, American Society of Civil Engineers, The Moles, and the Notre Dame Club. Preceded in death by his beloved wife of 55 years, Elizabeth Anne, he is survived by 7 children, Denis McInerny of Melbourne Beach, Suzanne Phillips of Fairfax, VA, Kathy McInerny of Oxnard, CA, Mary McInerny of Long Beach, CA, Sally McInerny of Tuscaloosa, AL, Michael McInerny of Marshfield, MA, Peggy McInerny of Takoma Park, MD, and sister Nancy Wagner of Accokeek, MD. He is also survived by fifteen grandchildren and two great-grandchildren. No calling hours are scheduled. A memorial Mass will be held January 15, at 11:00 am at Immaculate Conception Catholic Church. The family requests no flowers. Donations may be made to Immaculate Conception Church, 3780 Highway A1A, Melbourne Beach, FL 32951, Notre Dame University, or the National Kidney Foundation.

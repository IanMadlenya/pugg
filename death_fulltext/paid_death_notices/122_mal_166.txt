
        BONNOT-Captain Emile L., on March 11, 1997, longtime resident of Teaneck, NJ. Beloved husband of the late Dorothy (nee Gray). Uncle of William Gray, cousin of Shirley Holland. Godfather of Lynn Kuuscla. Captain Bonnot was a combat veteran of six campaigns in the Pacific Theater during W.W. II. He served as Executive Officer of the USS Freemont of which he was a ''Plank Owner''. Funeral service at the Petrik Funeral Home, 140 Palisade Avenue, Bogota, NJ, Monday morning at 11. Interment George Washington Memorial Park, Paramus, NJ. The family will receive friends 7-9 Saturday and 2-4 & 7-9 Sunday. Memorial Contributions to The Naval Reserve Association, C/O Capt. Austin Volk, 139 Chestnut Street, Englewood, New Jersey 07631 would be appreciated.
        BONNOT-Emile L. On March 11. The N.Y. Council, Navy League of the United States, announces with deep sorrow the passing of its esteemed director. During W.W. II, he was Executive Officer, USS Fremont, flagship of Admirals Blandy and Fechtler. Viewing Saturday 7 -9 PM, Sunday 2-4 PM and 7-9 PM at Petrik Funeral Home, 140 Palisade Ave., Bogota, N.J. Services Monday 11 AM. Interment 11:30 AM, George Washington Memorial Park, Paramus, N.J. Daniel V. Foley, President NY Council Navy League of the Uunited States
        BONNOT-Emile L. The Fellow Officers of the New York Commandery Naval Order of the United States regrets the passing on March 11, 1997 of their esteemed companion Captain Emile L. Bonnot USNR. He was a former Commander of the New York Commandery and Historian General of the Naval Order of the United States. Commander Lawrence Clarke


        BANKS-Paul N. On May 10, 2000, at his home in New York, after a short illness. Pioneer in the field of book and library preservation, a founding member of the American Institute of Conservation and president 1978-80. Born in California, graduate of Carnegie-Mellon University. Book designer, The Viking Press, New York, before being invited to the Newberry Library, Chicago, as conservator (1964-81). One of an international team of experts called to Florence to salvage books and manuscripts after the 1966 flood. Founder and director of the conservation/preservation program at the School of Library Service, Columbia University, he transferred the program to the University of Texas, Austin, in 1991. After retirement in 1996, he was consultant to the Library of Congress and the national Archives. Devoted partner of the late Dan Warshaw. He will be greatly missed by colleagues, students and many loving friends in Austin and New York. Memorial service to be announced.


        BIRNBAUM-Henry, 82. Died suddenly and tragically July 21, 1999. Born in St. Gallen, Switzerland, he served in an army intelligence unit 1941-1946, and was an investigator at the Nuremberg War Crimes Trials. He was the Chief Librarian at Brooklyn College 1952-1961, when he became University Librarian at Pace University, where he served with distinction for thirtythree years. Prior to his retirement in 1994, Pace University paid him the ultimate tribute by naming their library in his honor. He is survived by his brother Sol, his nephews Philip, Irwin, and Jeffrey, his nieces Phyllis, Marilyn, and Arlene, plus ten great and nine great great nephews & nieces. Funeral services will be held at Riverside Chapel, W. 76 Street & Amsterdam Avenue in Manhattan on Monday, July 26 at 12:15 PM.

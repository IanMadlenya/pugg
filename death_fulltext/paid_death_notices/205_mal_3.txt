
        DAHL--Norman C., Sc.D. Age 85. Died of Alzheimer's Disease January 11, 2004. Professor of Mechanical Engineering at MIT, he organized in 1961, a consortium of nine American universities to assist the new Indian Institute of Technology in Kanpur, India and served there until 1964. He joined the Ford Foundation 1968-1973, serving as Deputy Representative in India and in NYC. He was an activist, consulting internationally on development and higher technical education, as well as being a prime mover in his loved Block Island (RI) to investigate water resources and to arrange a research program on Lyme Disease. His survivors include his wife Dorothy (Sweet), his daughter Sabra, both of Cambridge, MA and his son Christian of King Ferry, NY and three grandchildren.


        PACE-Dr. William. (nee Wulf Peisachowitz.) Passed away June 8, 2000. Born in Shavl, Lithuania in 1902, he was a very prominent physician in Prague. When Hitler arrived, he continued his practice in Shavl. He is a survivor of the Shavl ghetto and of Dachau Lager 10, during which time he is credited with saving numerous lives, repeatedly placing his own at risk. After liberation he helped the allies conquer the typhoid epidemic in Dachau and directed the Jewish Hospital in Munich. He continued his practice in New York, affiliated with Mount Sinai Hospital until his retirement. He is survived by his niece, Dr. Bella Pace of Scarsdale, NY and his nephew Dr. Ben Pace of East Hills, NY. Funeral services will be held Sunday, 2:15 at ''The Riverside'' Memorial Chapel at Amsterdam Ave. and 76th St.

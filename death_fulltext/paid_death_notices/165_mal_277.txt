
        IANNONE-Anthony M., M.D., 75, died peacefully in the home, of cancer on Thursday, October 12. As the founding Chairman of the Neurology Department at the Medical College of Ohio, he established the Neurology residency program and spearheaded research in a variety of specialized fields including gait disorders, Parkinson's, Clonus and stroke. He was a guest researcher at the National Insitute of Health, Bethesda, MD. Dr. Ianonne's thirty years at the Medical College of Ohio also included service as Chief-of-Staff, teaching Professor, and Professor Emeritus. He was board certified in Neurology and a member of the American Academy of Neurology, Society for Neurosciences, American Board of Psychiatry and Neurology, the American and Ohio State Medical Associations and the San Francisco Neurological Society, along with extensive publications. He attended Stuyvesant High School, Columbia University, and at the age of 23, graduated from the Columbia School of Medicine. He is survived by his children Antoinette (Roger) Smith, Mary Ann (Roger) Bell, Michael (Maggie), James (Terese) of Toledo, OH and Susan (William) Frakes and Anthony (Amy) of Ann Arbor, MI, and thirteen grandchildren. Along with two children Christine (John) Abrams, and Martha Hudson of Martha's Vineyard, MA; four grandchildren, and one great-grandchild from a previous marriage, brother James (Lillian) Iannone of W. Hartford, CT and sister, Lola (Pasquael) Sylvestri of Rosyln Heights, Long Island. He was preceded in death by loving wife, Mary (Flanagan) Iannone, and children Peter and Joseph. Services were held at the Coyle Funeral Home, Toledo, OH.

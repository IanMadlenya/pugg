
        BYRD-Thomas Hugh, 90, former Palisade Interstate Park Commissioner and Treasurer, died October 12, 1997 in Belleair, Florida. Mr. Byrd was born in Hartsville, S.C., but spent most of his life in New York City with his brokerage firm, Byrd Brothers, after his graduation from Harvard in 1930. He was a senior member of the Harvard Club of New York. During World War II he joined the U.S. Navy and was active in many areas, the most notable being the sinking of U Boat 613 on its way to destroy Mayport Naval Base and the surrounding Jacksonville area July 23, 1943. The Navy rewarded him for this by naming its building for Fleet training units, Byrd Hall. After the war Mr. Byrd returned to his Wall Street firm but continued in the U.S. Naval Reserve, Commander of the Third District. In 1969 he was made Rear Admiral of the New York State Navy Militia by Governor Rockefeller. Mr. Byrd is survived by his wife of 67 years, the former Eleanor Deland; two daughters, Caroline Dunjoy of Paris, and Beatrice Myler of Belleair; four grandchildren; and one great-grandchild.


        BLUM--Samuel. Born on July 15, 1908, Sam grew up in Leipzig, Germany. In 1932 he moved to Paris, where he met Simone Padawer. They married on June 19, 1938, and on April 12, 1940, they came to New York City, where they have lived ever since. He was the president of United Feather and Down until his retirement, a member and trustee of Park East Synagogue for more than 50 years, and a founder and board member of Alyn, a hospital for disabled children in Jerusalem. Sam played bridge every afternoon for decades at the Regency Whist Club. A successful businessman on two continents and fluent in several languages, he was known for his ever-present smile, eternal optimism and impeccable appearance. He is survived by his beloved wife of 67 years, Simone; his son, Bentley; his daughter, Micheline; his sonin-law, Michael; his granddaughters, Megan and Jessica; his grandson-in-law, Edward; and his great-grandchildren, Noah and Charlotte. Services will be held at Park East Synagogue, 163 East 67th Street, on Monday, September 19, at 12 pm. Family visiting hours will be at his home, 130 East 67th Street, following services, and Tuesday through Thursday after 3 pm. Donations can be made to Alyn, 51 East 42nd Street, NYC, or Park East Synagogue.

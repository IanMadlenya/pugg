
        MORRIS-Ben C., 67. Died Monday, January 5, 1998 at St. Joseph's Hospital in Savannah, Georgia. Mr. Morris, an artist and fashion designer, had taught at the Savannah College of Art and Design since 1983, where he was well known and respected by faculty, staff, and students alike. Born in Defuniak Springs, Florida, Mr. Morris earned both a Bachelor's Degree and a Master's Degree from the State University of New York at Stony Brook, having also studied at Florida State University, Syracuse University and in Pavel Tchelitchew in Rome, Italy. His teaching experience prior to the Savannah College of Art and Design included Parsons School of Design, the Art Students League of New York, and Florida State University. Mr. Morris began his career as design assistant to M. De Givenchy at Hubert De Givenchy in Paris, and was later with Lily Dache in New York City. He worked as a fashion illustrator with The Philadelphia Bulletin, Women's Wear Daily & The New York Times. He also produced his original wallpaper, which was sold to a major manufacturer and is still being printed. As a freelance illustrator, Mr. Morris' clients included Elizabeth Arden, Lord & Taylor, Bloomingdale's, and Christian Dior. Mr. Morris joined the Savannah College of Art and Design in 1983, where he served as chair of the foundation department, and later the fashion department, a program he helped to develop. Mr. Morris is survived by his longtime companion, Don Mondanaro of Savannah, and several cousins. Remembrances may be made to: The Savannah College of Art and Design, Professor Ben Morris Scholarship Fund, P.O. Box 3146, Savannah, Georgia 31402-3146.

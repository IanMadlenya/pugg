
        FISHER-Robert P. Of Scarsdale, NY and New Suffolk, NY died peacefully on January 22 at the age of 78 due to leukemia. He was for many years the President of George F. Fisher Inc, a silk yarn company, and was the President of two leading silk trade groups the American Silk Council and the International Silk Association (US). Born in Brooklyn, NY, he was educated at Dartmouth College, MIT and the Amos Tuck Business School. Active in alumni affairs at Dartmouth and its business school, he served as Chairman of the Tuck Annual Giving Campaign. During World War II he served as a First Lieutenant in the Army Air Corps in the Atlantic and Pacific theatres. In Scarsdale he was a member of the Town and Village Civic Club, the Rotary Club, the Scarsdale Historical Society and was a deacon and elder of the Hitchcock Presbyterian Church. In New Suffolk, he was a member of the New Suffolk Civic Association, the Cutchogue-New Suffolk and Southold Historical Societies, the North Fork Country Club and served as the West Creek Baykeeper. A dedicated lifelong sailor, he served on the Race Committees at the Larchmont Yacht Club, the Old Cove Yacht Club, the Storm Trysail Club and was a member of the New York Yacht Club. As a teenager, he and his brother unsuccessfully tried to teach Albert Einstein how to sail. He is survived by his wife of 48 years Greta, children Rip of Scarsdale, NY, Melinda Fisher Callaway of La Jolla, CA, James of Brookfield, CT and Susan Fisher Nannig of Kingstown, RI, seven grandchildren and his brother John of New Suffolk, NY. Donations may be made to the Memorial Fund-Hitchcock Presbyterian Church, 6 Greenacres Ave., Scarsdale, NY 10583 or the charity of donor's choice.


        BERGER-Adolph R., M.D. The faculty and staff of New York University School of Medicine mourn the passing of Adolph R. Berger, M.D., age 89, a longtime faculty member and alumnus. Dr. Berger served as the Director of Student Health Services at both the medical and dental schools from 1942 to 1949, and was an early participant in one of the first cardiac catheterization laboratories in the country. He retired as Emeritus Professor of Medicine from the full-time faculty in July 1997. Even after retirement, he maintained a strong interest in medical education and the relationship between physicians and their patients. Kenneth G. Langone Chairman, NYU School of Medicine Foundation Board of Trustees Robert Glickman, M.D. Dean, NYU School of Medicine
        BERGER-Adolph R., M.D. We mourn the death of Dr. Adolph Berger who was an alumnus of our School of Medicine and was Professor of Clinical Medicine. He served us for over 50 years as an Attending at Bellevue and Tisch Hospitals and as Chief of Medicine at Goldwater Memorial Hospital. He was a devoted teacher, an active researcher and a devoted practitioner. Many medical students and house staff benefited greatly from his mentoring. We express our sympathy to his family. Saul J. Farber, M.D. M.A.C.P. The Frederick H. King Professor and Chairman, Department of Medicine NYU School of Medicine Director Medical Servieds Bellevue and Tisch Hospitals

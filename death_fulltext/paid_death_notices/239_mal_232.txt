
        BLOOM--Samuel, Ph.D. The Mount Sinai Medical Center and its Board of Trustees mourn the passing of Samuel W. Bloom, Ph.D., Professor of Community and Preventive Medicine, and founding member of the faculty of Mount Sinai School of Medicine. In addition to his skills as a clinician and teacher, Dr. Bloom was a medical sociologist and an accomplished author. His landmark book, The World As A Scalpel, A History Of Medical Sociology, earned great critical acclaim, as well as the prestigious book prize of the American Sociological Association. We extend our sincerest sympathy to his family and friends. Peter W. May Chairman of the Boards of Trustees Kenneth L. Davis, M.D. President, CEO and Dean Dennis S. Charney, M.D. Dean for Academic and Scientific affairs
        $(6$)BLOOM--Samuel W. Ph.D. The Department of Community and Preventive Medicine mourns the passing of Samuel W. Bloom, Ph.D., 85, Professor of Community and Preventive Medicine, and a founding member of the faculty of Mount Sinai School of Medicine. Dr. Bloom joined the Mount Sinai faculty in 1968, and served as Director of the Division of Behavioral Sciences in the Department during his 38 year tenure at Mount Sinai. His research focused on the sociology of medical education and on the social psychology of the doctorpatient relationship. Dr. Bloom was a gifted scientist, teacher, and an accomplished author of the landmark book, The World As Scalpel, A History Of Medical Sociology. Philip J. Landrigan, MD Professor and Chair of the Department of Community and Preventive Medicine

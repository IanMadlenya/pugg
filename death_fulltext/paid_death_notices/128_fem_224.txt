
        LARRABEE-Eleanor Doermann. Architect and college trustee, died September 27 at Saint Vincent's Hospital in New York. She was 74 years old. She died of head injuries after being struck by a car on September 13th. Mrs. Larrabee began architecture practice in 1955 at the Office of Charles H. Warner, Jr., and in 1963 became Associate in the successor firm of WBTL Architects. She specialized in the design of college buildings, particularly libraries. She served as a Trustee of Barnard College from 1976 to 1986. Mrs. Larrabee is the widow of Eric Larrabee, former executive director of the New York State Council on the Arts (1970-77), who died in 1990. Eleanor Larrabee was born in New York City on October 7, 1922, and was a lifelong resident of New York City. She graduated from Radcliffe College in 1943, and received a Master of Architecture degree from Columbia University in 1949. She is survived by two brothers and three nieces. A family graveside service will be held later. Memorial donations may be sent to Barnard College.

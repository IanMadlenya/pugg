
        ESTRIN--Joseph, M.D. Of Brooklyn, NY died after a brief illness on February 23 at the age of 93. Beloved son of the late Max and Anna Estrin. Brother of the late Eva Kanof. Dr. Estrin received his undergraduate training at the City College of New York and received his M.D. degree from the Long Island College of Medicine in 1936. He practiced medicine and later general surgery in Brooklyn from 1936 until his retirement in 1985. His medical practice was interrupted by his service as a Captain in the Army Medical Corps stationed in the Pacific during World War II. He was on the faculty of the State University of New York College of Medicine at Downstate and was highly valued as an educator in the training of generations of medical students and surgical residents. He was affiliated with Maimonides Medical Center as an attending physician and surgeon for over 50 years and continued to be active serving on committees there until the time of his death. He was the author of ''An Uncommon Commitment'', a history of Maimonides Medical Center. In addition to having been a highly regarded surgeon, he was a talented artist, sculptor, lecturer on diverse subjects including word origins, and was a memorable raconteur. He will be deeply missed by his extended family, his many friends and colleagues. Graveside funeral services will be held at 2 P.M. on February 26 at Mount Judah Cemetery, Queens, NY.

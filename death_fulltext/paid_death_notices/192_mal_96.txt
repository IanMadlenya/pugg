        HARPEL-Peter. The Department of Medicine of the Mount Sinai School of Medicine mourns the passing of Peter C. Harpel, the previous Chief of the Division of Hematology at Mount Sinai. Dr. Harpel died in Cleveland, Ohio on January 4 from complications of open heart surgery. He was 67 years old. Dr. Harpel earned his M.D. degree from Harvard Medical School and received his training in Clinical Medicine and Hematology at the Cornell Division of Medicine of Bellevue Hospital. After serving on the faculty of Cornell University Medical College from 1966 to 1990, he was recruited to Mount Sinai to head the Division of Hematology of the Department of Medicine. Dr. Harpel was an eminent physician and a world renowned scientist who made numerous important contributions to medicine and hematology in a wide variety of areas including complement activation, fibrinolysis, thrombosis and atherosclerosis. His intellect, wisdom and wit will be sorely missed. Our sincere condolences to his wife Louise, his daughter Elizabeth and his sons John & Michael. Paul E. Klotman, M.D. Murray Rosenberg Professor Chairman, Dept. of Medicine Mt Sinai School of Medicine

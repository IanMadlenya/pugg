
        HOBERMAN--Henry D., 89, biochemist and physician, on January 10, 2004. Dr. Hoberman was professor emeritus of biochemistry at the Albert Einstein College of Medicine. He was noted for his studies of pathways of hydrogen atoms in intermediary metabolism. He received his B.A. and PhD from Columbia University and MD from Harvard Medical School. At Columbia he was a member of the group that pioneered the use of stable isotopes in biochemistry. At Harvard, he conducted war related research. In 1947 he joined the faculty of Yale School of Medicine. In 1953 he became one of the founding members of the Albert Einstein College of Medicine, where he remained until his retirement in 1993. Dr. Hoberman was an attending physician in the Medical Clinic of the Jacobi Hospital, an affiliate of the Einstein. He is survived by his wife, Hilda Carnicero, his children John, David, Ruth and Michael and his grandchildren Steven, Richard, Madeline, Della & Langston.

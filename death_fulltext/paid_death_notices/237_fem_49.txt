
        FICHMAN--Pearl Spiegel. Of Pittsburgh, PA passed away on Saturday October 7, 2006 at the age of 86, after a long illness. Beloved wife of the Late Yehudi David Fichman, mother of Mark Fichman of Pittsburgh, and Eytan Fichman of Somerville, MA grandmother of Michael Fichman and Jonathan Fichman and mother in law of Ruth Fauman Fichman of Pittsburgh. She was the daughter of the Late Markus and Pessie Spiegel. Born in Czernowitz, Romania, she emigrated to the United States in 1947, then to Israel in 1950. She returned to the United States in 1957. She was a graduate of Columbia University and a language teacher and translator in the New York Area. Until 1999 she was a resident of New York City. In 1999, she and her husband Yehudi moved to Pittsburgh, PA. She wrote a memoir of her youth and Holocaust experiences, ''Before Memories Fade''. She worked with scholars and historians such as John Felstiner, Sir Martin Gilbert, Jerry Glenn and Florence Heymann, providing recollections of her friend, Paul Celan and the Holocaust in Romania. A play about the poet Selma Meerbaum-Eisinger, entitled ''Selma'' by Fred Apke, first produced in Furth, Germany, used her voice in conversation as part of the sound track of the play.

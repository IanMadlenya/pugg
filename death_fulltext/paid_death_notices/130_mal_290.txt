
        JOSEPH-J.J., 83. Economist, political writer, and peace activist, died Sunday from complications of Parkinson Disease. He graduated from the University of Michigan with a B.A. and a Master's in Public Administration. He worked with the OSS and UNRRA but a promising career in public service was derailed by the political blacklist. At The Purdue Frederick Company he rose to the position Senior Vice President. Mr. Joseph campaigned against the Viet Nam War and the Nuclear Arms Race. ''J.J.J.'', as he was known to his friends, loved to travel, was an avid tennis player, and enjoyed classical music. He remained a Marxist his entire life. Mr. Joseph is survived by Leepee, his wife of 36 years; his siter Tuz; his four children and ten grandchildren.

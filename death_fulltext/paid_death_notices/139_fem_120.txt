
        MILHAUD - ARSLANIAN - Melvie. A writer and film director, died Thursday, August 13, 1998, at the age of 45, in New York City, following a long illness. She was born in Beirut, Lebanon, to Melancthon and Violette Arslanian, and raised in Antwerp, Belgium, where she had her primary education. She attended the University of Aix En Provence, France, where she received a degree in Philosophy and Letters. She continued her studies at New York University where she received a degree in cinematography. She has been a director and producer of several films including ''Styletto'' and ''Fading'', but her most acclaimed work was ''Above the Waves,'' which was a conversation with her grandmother, Beatrice, who was her role model in life. Ms. Arslanian-Milhaud is survived by her son Arsen; husband David; mother Violette Arslanian; and brothers Ara and Raffi Arslanian. The family will receive visitors on Monday, August 17, at Gannon Funeral Home, 152 E. 28th St, New York City, from 7-9 PM. Funeral service will take place at St. Illuminator's Armenian Cathedral, 221 E. 27th St, New York City, on Tuesday, August 18, at 11 AM. The family requests in lieu of flowers donations be made to the Armenian Prelacy, 138 E. 39th St, New York, NY 10016 or St. Illuminator's Armenian Day School, 69-23 47th Ave, Woodside, New York 11377.


        HOLMES--Frank E. Jr. Former Executive Vice President of Encyclopaedia Britannica, died December 20 in Vero Beach, FL. A Wharton School of Business graduate, he spent nearly 40 years with Britannica, where he pioneered the development of leads through third-party mail inserts. Known as ''Mr. Britannica'', he built and managed the New York division, the company's most lucrative, and under his leadership the 238-year-old company enjoyed its most profitable years. He retired in 1992. Frank resided in Bronxville, New York; Southampton, New York; and John's Island, Vero Beach, FL. Frank is survived by his wife, Elizabeth; his three children, Hunter, Trilby and Blair; and his six grandchildren.


        HATTENBACH--Mark Roger, age 62, of Norwalk, CT, was the owner of Vanguard Temporary Services, located in New York and Connecticut, Colonial Wines and Spirits in Norwalk, and Hattco Properties. He died on May 19th after a long battle with lung cancer at his home in Delray Beach, FL. Mark served as President of NYATS in New York City. He served on the Board of Directors of the Dysautonomia Foundation, and the Board of Directors of Temple Shalom in Norwalk. Last fall, he was recognized for his many contributions through Norwalk Community College, where he established several scholarships, and was also involved in the renovation of the West Campus of the college. Mark was an active member of Rolling Hills in Wilton and the Hamlet Country Club of Delray Beach, FL. He is survived by his wife, Ellen Sue Hattenbach; a son, Charles Matthew Hattenbach; daughter-in-law, Laime Vaitkus Hattenbach, and two grandsons, Alexander and Theodore Hattenbach, all of Norwalk; father, Monroe Hattenbach, and stepmother, Muriel Hattenbach. He is predeceased by his mother, Nadine Hattenbach; his son, Bernard Hattenbach, and brother, James Hattenbach. Funeral services will be held Sunday, May 23rd, at 3:00 p.m., at Temple Shalom, 259 Richards Avenue, Norwalk, CT. Memorial donations may be made to the Dysautonomia Foundation, 633 Third Avenue, 12th Floor, New York, NY 10017; the Norwalk Community College Alumni Scholarship Fund, 188 Richards Avenue, Norwalk, CT 06854; the Bennett Cancer Center at Stamford Hospital, 34 Shelburne Road, Stamford, CT 06902.
        HATTENBACH--Mark. Director, Benefactor and long-time friend. The Officers and Directors of the Dysautonomia Foundation sadly mourn the untimely passing of Mark Hattenbach, and extend sincerest condolences to his beloved family, Ellen Sue, Charles and Laime, Alexander and Theodore. David Brenner, President Lenore F. Roseman, Executive Director

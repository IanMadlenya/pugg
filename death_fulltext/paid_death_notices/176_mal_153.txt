
        MELLO-Christopher D. A victim of the American Airline Flight 11 catastrophe, Christopher D. Mello, 25, of Boston, Mass, formerly of Rye, New York, died September 11, 2001. Chris was born June 22, 1976 in Greenwich, CT to Douglas and Ellen Mello. Chris was an outstanding student and athlete at Rye High School graduating in 1994. Chris was Vice President of the senior class and a member of the National Honor Society. Chris was co-captain of the varsity football team in 1993 which went on to the New York State finals. He was voted the most valuable defensive player of the tournament. He was also presented with the Golden Dozen Scholar Athletic Award for both his on-field and academic achievements. He was also ConEdison's athlete of the week in 1993. Chris graduated from Princeton University in 1998, where he majored in Psychology and was Vice President of the University Cottage Club, and a member of the men's Rugby Club, Kappa Alpha Order Fraternity and the 21 Club. Chris, who enjoyed playing golf with his family, was a member of The Apawamis Club in Rye, New York and the Rye YMCA. He worked as a financial analyst at BT Alex Brown in Baltimore, Md, before moving to Boston, Mass, where he worked as an analyst for Alta Communications. In addition to his parents Douglas and Ellen Mello of Rye, NY, Chris is survived by his brother, John Douglas Mello, also a graduate of Princeton and an avid athlete, who resides in Manhattan. Chris is also survived by his paternal grandmother, Alice Mello, of Barefoot Bay, FL. In lieu of flowers the family would like donations made to the Rye YMCA at 21 Locust Street, Rye, NY 10580, in Chris' memory.

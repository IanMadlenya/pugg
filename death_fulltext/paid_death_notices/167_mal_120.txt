
        ANDERSON IMBERT-Enrique, distinguished Argentine author and scholar, died in Buenos Aires, on December 6, 2000. Born February 12, 1910 in Cordoba, Arg., he immigrated to the US in 1947 as a result of the Peron regime with his wife, Margarita (Margot), and their two children. Prolific author of many collections of short stories, several novels and numerous essays and scholarly papers, he led a life dedicated to art and culture. Anderson was also Professor of Latin American Literature for many years at the University of Michigan at Ann Arbor and the Victor Thomas Professor of Latin American Literature at Harvard University from 1965 until his retirement in 1980. He was a member of the Argentine Academy of Letters and the American Academy of Arts and Sciences, the recipient of many awards, most recently the honoree of the Dia de los Mayores Notables Argentinos on August 18, 2000. Anderson is survived by a daughter, Dr. Anabel Anderson Imbert of Oakland, CA, a son, Carlos Anderson of Meredith, NH, three grandchildren, Robert, Vanessa and Enrique, and two great-grandchildren, Alexandra and Christopher.

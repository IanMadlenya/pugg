
        BELINKIE-Milton H. Connecticut's Superior Court Judge Milton H. Belinkie, 89, died January 29. He worked until the end and was predeceased by his wife Helen Jiler Belinkie, award-winning cookbook author. A graduate of Yale and Columbia Law schools, he did secret duty in WWII and gained notoriety for capturing Tokyo Rose, but refused to testify after the war. Milton loved poetry, Shakespeare, music, and played the violin in a symphony. He won trophies in tennis, squash, handball and played semi-pro baseball with a batting average of .422. He is survived by his daughter, actress, Carrie Jiler Belinkie, one brother and four sisters. He was loved by many and will be missed by all.

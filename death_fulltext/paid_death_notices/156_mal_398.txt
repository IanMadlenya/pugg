
        BUDGE-Don. The Officers and Trustees of the New York Sports Museum and Hall of Fame regret the passing of a charter member of the New York Sports Hall of Fame on January 26, 2000 in Scranton, PA. Bill Shannon, President Jordan Sprechman, Secretary
        BUDGE-Donald J. The Board of Directors and Staff of the International Tennis Hall of Fame mourn the loss of one of the game's greatest player, our friend, 1964 Hall of Fame Enshrinee, Don Budge. The first player to win all four grand slam tournaments in the same year in 1938, his keen interest in the sport remained to the end. His courage and good humor will be missed by all who knew him. We extend our deepest sympathy to his wife, Loriel, and his family. Jan Leschly, Chairman John R. Reese, Chairman, Executive Committee Joseph F. Cullman, III, Chairman Emeritus Jane G. Brown, President Tony Trabert, Vice President

        BUDGE-Donald. The United States Tennis Association mourns the loss of a true champion and friend, Don Budge. As a Davis Cup competitor and the first winner of the Grand Slam of tennis, Budge became a tennis legend. We extend our sympathy to his wife, Loriel, and sons, David & Jeffrey.

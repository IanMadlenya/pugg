
        PAYNE-John, Jr., 92, retired vice president and director of AMAX, Inc., died on June 21, 2001 at his home in Tucson, AZ. He formerly resided in New York City and Old Lyme, CT. Born in Magdalena, NM on December 6, 1908, he graduated from the University of Texas at El Paso in 1931. He worked in the Phillipines 1931-40, returned to the U.S. in 1940 and joined the American Metal Company, Ltd. (which later merged with Climax Molybdenum Co. and became AMAX, Inc.). From 194446 he served in the U.S. Navy, to Lieutenant, then resumed his AMAX career. He was elected corporate vice president in 1955 and director in 1969, retiring in 1973. Surviving are his wife, Setsuko U. Payne, and stepson Michael Davis and wife, Nagako, of Tucson, AZ. His former wife, Marjorie (Chubb) Payne, predeceased him in 1985. Funeral services were private. Memorial donations may be made to The University of Texas at El Paso, Geological Sciences Dept., Institutional Advancement, 1100 North Stanton-Suite 201, El Paso, TX 79902.

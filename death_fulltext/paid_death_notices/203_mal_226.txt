
        LOUTSCH--Enrique H., M.D. of Manhattan, died on December 15, 2003, of pancreatic cancer. He is survived by his wife, Cynthia Curran, a son, Johannes of Cos Cob, CT, a daughter Karen Gironda of Harrison, NY, and two grandsons, Nicholas and Conrad Gironda. Certified by the American Board of Psychiatry and Neurology in 1969, Dr. Loutsch was a psychiatrist in private practice for over 30 years. He was a Fellow of the American Academy of Psychoanalysis and a member of the American Psychiatric Association. During the early years of his career he was a supervising analyst and lecturer at the Post Graduate Center for Mental Health. He was an accomplished marathon runner and a devotee of the arts. Dr. Loutsch will be remembered by all whose lives he touched, for his wisdom, intellect and wit. A memorial service will be held Friday, January 16, at 11:00AM, at St. James Episcopal Church on Madison Avenue at 71st Street, Manhattan. Donations in Dr. Loutsch's memory may be sent to Memorial Sloan-Kettering, Pancreatic Cancer Research Center, P.O. Box 27106, New York, NY 10087 or to The Central Park Conservancy, 14 East 60th Street, New York, NY 10022.

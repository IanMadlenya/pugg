
        STOLMAN--Dr. Cynthia Jean, 63, Assistant Professor of Pediatrics at the University of Medicine and Dentistry of New Jersey in Newark, NJ died at her home in Short Hills, NJ on April 1, 2005. She received a BA from Brooklyn College in 1964, a M. Ed. in 1975 and a Ph.D in 1981 from SUNY Buffalo. She was Director of Medical Ethics at United/The Children's Hospital of New Jersey from 1994-1997, Chairperson of the Pediatric Bioethics Committee of the Department of Pediatrics and Curriculum and Evaluation, Director of the Pediatric Residency Training Program at UMDNJ from 1997-2005. She was the author of many medical journal articles and publications related to ethical challenges and dilemmas. She was a charismatic and admired teacher of medical students and physicians. She will be missed by her many friends, colleagues, students, and most of all by her family. She is survived by her husband Dr. Lewis P. Stolman, their children Lara Stolman Watzky and her husband Michael of NY, Dr. Karen Stolman Shmookler and her husband Eric of Sandy, UT, their grandson Caden Shmookler, and her brothers Dr. Michael Fein of Westin, CT and Dr. Eric Fein of Bernardsville, NJ. Funeral services will be held at Temple B'nai Jeshurun, 1025 South Orange Ave, Short Hills, NJ on Sunday at 1:30 PM.

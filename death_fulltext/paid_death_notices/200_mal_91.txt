
        DEE--John T. Of McAllister Drive, Saratoga Springs, and East 69th Street, Manhattan, died Friday, September 5, 2003, at Saratoga Hospital. He was 65. Born on July 25, 1938, in St. Louis, MO, he was a son of the late John F. and Alice D. (Kelly) Dee. Mr. Dee was a graduate of Cleveland High School and of the University of Missouri and had completed the Program for Management Development at the Harvard Graduate School of Business. He served as chairman of the board of Centerplate (trade name for Volume Services America Holdings, Inc) since August, 1998, and as director of Service America since January, 1993. He has been chairman of the board of Service America since January, 1997, and from August, 1998, to April, 2003, served as chief executive officer. He also served as president and chief executive officer of Service America from January, 1993, to April, 2002, and as a consultant to Service America from November, 1992, to January, 1993. From 1989 to 1992, Mr. Dee was president of Top Food Services, Inc, and from 1980 to 1989, he was group president at ARAMARK Corp. From 1979 to 1980, he held senior positions, including president at Sportservice Corp and was responsible for concessions and merchandise operations at many venues. From 1968 to 1979, he held various positions at ARAMARK Corp including president of Leisure Services Group. Mr. Dee was a member of several country clubs including the Loxahatchee Country Club in Jupiter, FL, the Innis Arden Country Club in Old Greenwich, CT, and the Aronimink Country Club in New Town Square, PA. He was also a member of the Rover Boys of New York City and The Skeeters, which was founded by the late Ted Husing. He was an avid horse patron and owned several thoroughbred racehorses, two of which won at Saratoga on July 26, 2003, as well as serving on the board of the New York Racing Association Backstretch Employee's Pension Fund. He also was trustee for the Committee for Economic Development and served as director of the USO of Metropolitan New York and The National Committee on American Foreign Policy. He is survived by his wife of nearly 35 years, Susan (Brier) Dee of Saratoga Springs and Manhattan; three sons, Ralph Dee and his wife Michelle of South Carolina, Ken Dee and his wife Judy of Manhattan and John Nicholas Dee of Saratoga Springs; his brothers, Patrick Dee and James and his wife JoAnn Dee, all of St. Louis, MO; and a niece and nephew, Jeanne and James P.

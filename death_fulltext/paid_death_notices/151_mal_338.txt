
        LEWIS-Myron S. Of Weston, Ct., founder and President of Lewis Laboratories, International Ltd., died on August 19th in Norwalk Hospital, Connecticut. He was a Lieutenant in the U.S. Army Signal Intelligence during World War II at which time he was in command of electronic equipment to intercept German communications at the front. He was in the Normandy invasion, behind enemy lines during the Battle of the Bulge and one of the first to cross the Ramagen Bridge. He was awarded the Bronze Star. Mr. Lewis, son of Aquilla Cornelius Lewis, prominent lawyer who won the famous ''Herrin Massacre'' case in 1922, and Oma Lewis, author and artist, was born in Harrisburg, IL. on April 19, 1921. He was a direct descendant of Francis Lewis, one of the signers of the Declaration of Independence (on his father's side) and Ethan Allen (on his mother's side). His family members were among the first colonists to arrive in 1682 and settled in Pennsylvania. He was an ardent defender of freedom and liberty. He was a graduate of Amherst College, class of 1942. Mr. Lewis was V.P. of International Marketing at Richardson Merrill and Vicks International and Excecutive V.P. at Shulton Inc. His wife of 24 years, Diana Lewis, survives him. He is also survived by five children from a previous marriage, daughter, Cornelia Sgoutas, sons, Daniel, Timothy, John, and Tobias; his brother, Dr. Winston B. Lewis of Annapolis, Maryland, and his stepdaughter, Lena. He is also survived by 25 grandchildren. Contributions in his memory may be made to The American Cancer Society, The Free Congress Foundation, 717 2nd St. NE, Washington, DC 20002 and/or Boy's Town of Italy, 250 East 63 Street, NY, NY 10021.


        ALLEGA-Alfred S. Died Wednesday, May 3, 2000. Mr. Allega was born in East Douglas, Massachusetts on December 19, 1917, the son of the late Josephine (Lavella) and Salvatore Allega. A World War II veteran, he served in the United States Army Air Force. Alfred Allega is survived by his wife Nadia (Mandalakis) Allega of New York City and by his two sisters, Josephine Anderson and Lillian Epstein. Two brothers and six sisters predeceased him. Well known in the wholesale produce business for many years, Mr. Allega was President of American Banana Company located in the Bronx. Previously he was President of Yel-O-Ripe Banana Company, Inc. which he founded in the Bronx with his two brothers in 1945. When the Yel-O-Ripe and American Banana Companies merged in 1952, Mr. Allega was appointed President of the American Banana Company, Inc. A philanthropist and an honorable man whose word was his contract, Alfred Allega was admired and very well respected by associates in the business community in the United States and by his adopted Greek community here and abroad. Farewell to a rare individual who gave so much of himself to his fellow man. Viewing hours are 2-4 and 6-8 p.m. on Sunday at F. Ruggiero & Sons, Inc., 732 Yonkers Ave., Yonkers, NY, 914375-1400. Funeral service 11 a.m. Monday at the funeral home with interment following at Gate of Heaven Cemetery.


        CHESTER-Alexander Campbell. 84, former president and chairman of H. Muehlstein & Co. died near his home on Shelter Island, NY of a stroke on December 2, 1999. He began his career at Muehlstein as the traffic manager in 1938 and retired as chairman of the board in 1978. Soon afterwards Muehlstein was sold to Mobil, some years later to be sold back again to the management. After retiring he began a second career, working for Century 21 in Oradell, NJ. He was a member of the Gardiner's Bay Country Club and the Boca Raton Hotel Club, with homes in Shelter Island, NY, Fort Lee, NJ, and Boca Raton, Fla. He is survived by his beloved wife of 59 years, Gladys; a daughter, Gail of Berwyn, Pa.; two sons; Alexander C. III of Washington, D.C. and David of Newton Square, Pa., a sister, Lillian of Toms River, NJ and five grandchildren.

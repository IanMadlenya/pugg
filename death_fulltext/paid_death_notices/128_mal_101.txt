
        STEINMAN-Lewis. On September 6, 1997, peacefully at Hartford Hospital, surrounded by his loving family, adored and adoring wife, Joan of 43 years; beloved sons Jeffrey, Peter and Robert; daughter-in-law Jody; and dear friends Robyn and Todd. Also survived by his much adored grandson Carlo; sister and brother-in-law Natalie and Milton Lewin; nephews, nieces, grandnephews and grandnieces; and very special motherin-law, Gracye Weisbrot. Services today, Monday, 11 AM, at Congregation Rodeph Sholom, 7 W. 83rd St, NYC. Contributions may be made to Congregation Rodeph Sholom or a charity of choice.
        STEINMAN-Lewis. Congregation Rodeph Sholom notes with deep sorrow the untimely passing of our beloved past president, Lewis Steinman. Lew was extraordinarily devoted to his family, friends, Rudin Management, and Congregation. He had an abiding Jewish commitment and took special pleasure in watching children grow and mature. We will miss his leadership, ready humor and joy in helping others. Our deepest condolences to his loving wife and helpmate Joan, devoted children Jeffrey and Jody, Peter and Todd, Robert and Robin, grandson Carlo and all their dear ones. We all are diminished by his death. Robert N. Levine, Senior Rabbi Jack Levitt, President Janet Neuberger, Chairman of theBoard
        STEINMAN-Lewis. The Board, officers and staff of Square-Arch Realty Corp. extend their sympathy and condolences to our fellow director, Jeffrey Steinman, on the passing of his father. Lewis was a mentor and friend to many in the 2 Fifth Avenue community; his counsel and wisdom will be missed. Thomas I. Marcosson, President Square-Arch Realty Corp

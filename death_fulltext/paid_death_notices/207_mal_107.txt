
        BARCLAY--John Williams. Born in New Haven, CT, August 6, 1913, the son of Albert Hampton Barclay, an attorney, and Laura Williams Barclay, who was originally from Rochester, NY. Educated at Phillips Academy, Andover, and then Yale, class of 1936, he received his law degree from Harvard Law School in 1939. He was admitted to the Connecticut bar in that year, and to the New York bar in 1940 and then joined the law firm of Barclay and Barclay with his father and brother. He joined the Navy in 1941, and served in the Pacific from 1943 to 1946 as an air combat intelligence officer on the aircraft carrier Hornet. In 1957, Barclay and Barclay joined with Thompson, Weir and MacDonald form the law firm of Thompson, Weir and Barclay. Mr. Barclay continued to practice law into his eighties, after Thompson, Weir, and Barclay merged with Murtha, Cullina, Richter and Pinney, LLP of Hartford and New Haven. Mr. Barclay s first wife, the former Alice Sturges of Providence, RI, died in 1962. He was married for thirty-six years to Eleanor Paul Barclay, who died in 2000. He leaves his wife, Stratton Nicolson Barclay of Old Lyme, a daughter Louise Rice, of Cambridge MA, a son, John Whitney Barclay, of Winston Salem, NC, a daughter, Nina Barclay, of Norwich, CT, a step daughter, Elizabeth Bohlen of Cambridge, MA, seven grandchildren and a great granddaughter.

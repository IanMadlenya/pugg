
        LEWIS--Myrna Irene, 67. Died November 15, born in Fountain, Minnesota, she was an undergraduate at the University of Minnesota and received her Master's and Doctorate at the Columbia University School of Social Work. She was co-author of ''Aging and Mental Health,'' the first comprehensive textbook on the subject, as well as the popular ''Love and Sex After 60.'' She devoted her life to confrontations with injustice and to understanding both the adverse events and possibilities in the lives of women, especially in their middle and later years. She taught at the Mount Sinai School of Medicine and was an active alumna of the Columbia University School of Social Work. She is survived by her husband, Robert N. Butler, M.D., daughter Alexandra, stepdaughters Christine Butler, Carole Hall and Cynthia Butler, her sister Diane Barnhart, two brothers Donald and Virgil Eickhoff and six loving grandchildren. A memorial service will be held at Unitarian Church of All Souls at 80th St. and Lexington Ave. at 12 PM on Monday November 21st. In lieu of flowers, the family requests that contributions be sent to the Myrna I. Lewis Minority Students Scholarship Fund, Columbia University School of Social Work, 1255 Amsterdam Ave., Room 629, NY NY 10027. Checks are payable to ''The Trustees of Columbia University.''

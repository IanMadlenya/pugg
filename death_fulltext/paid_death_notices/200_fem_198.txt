
        FISCHER--Myna Strauss, 92, long-time Manhattan resident and a nationally recognized fashion designer during the fifties and sixties, died 30 August 2003. Born in Iowa, she began designing evening wraps in New York in the thirties. Her fashion career catupulted when one of her evening wraps appeared on the cover of Life Magazine. During WWII, she was employed by Lockheed in California where whe worked on specifications for military planes and a hospital ship. In the forties, she became a stylist in the Advertising Department of Macy's. Later, she designed coats and suits and interpreted designs of Parisian designer Jean Desses for the American market. She was predeceased by her husband, William, a commercial real estate executive. Contributions in her memory may be made to the New York Public Library.


        LICHTENBERG--Alan. The officers, Board of Directors, and staff of UJA-Federation of New York note with sadness the passing of Alan Lichtenberg, beloved brother of Herbert Lichtenberg, cherished friends of UJA-Federation. Alan was deeply committed to supporting Jewish and humanitarian causes, and his brother, Herbert, serves as vice president of UJA-Federation's Camp Hatikvah. We extend our deepest sympathies to the entire Lichtenberg family. Larry Zicklin, President Morris W. Offit, Chair of the Board John S. Ruskay, Exec. VP & CEO
        LICHTENBERG--Alan A. We mourn the loss of one of the original founders of the West Point Jewish Chapel Fund. Alan graduated in 1951 and was a decorated combat veteran of the Korean War and a gifted leader. He was our treasurer and the financial force in the construction of the Jewish Chapel at the United States Military Academy. His good works will survive him. West Point Jewish Chapel Fund Louis Gross, Chairman
        LICHTENBERG--Alan. The Officers and Board of United Cerebral Palsy of New York City note with deep sorrow the passing of our longtime friend and supporter, Alan Lichtenberg. We extend our sincere sympathy to his children, Richard and Peggy, Dee Dee, Michael, Susan, and Neil, and Barbara and Matthew. Leo Hausman, Chairman Martin Hausman, President UCP of NYC, Inc.

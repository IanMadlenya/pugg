
        CHALFEN-Leo, Ph.D., 78, January 10, 2001. Psychoanalytic psychotherapist, training analyst, National Psychological Association for Psychoanalysis; Director of Group Therapy and Process, Lincoln Institute for Psychotherapy; Consultant, Management Counselor Corporation; Adjunct Professor of Psychology, New York University; Private Practice. His specialty focused on issues of adult development and retirement, conducting training workshops for leaders in retirement programs. Author of numerous professional articles. Member of American Psychological Association. He leaves behind his beloved family. His wife, Dr. Iris SanGiuliano, son Lucian, sisterin-law Olga Menaker, sister Esther Freeman of Boston. Friends may call at Frank E. Campbell, 1076 Madison Ave. and 81 St., Thursday and Friday 6 to 9 PM. Service and interment private. In lieu of flowers, donations in his memory may be made to Michael J. Fox Foundation for Parkinson's Research, Grand Central Station, PO Box 4777, New York, NY 10163.

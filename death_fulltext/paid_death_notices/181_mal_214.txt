
        LOBDELL-Francis. Of Shelburne, Vermont, 80, Died on Feb. 16, 2002, of renal failure. He is survived by his wife, Adair, by his 3 children, Peter Tillotson Lobdell, of Amherst, MA., Katherine Lobdell Norris of Southborough, MA., and Michael Childs Lobdell of New Canaan, CT, his sister, Brenda Rudner of Dorset, VT., and his brother, Leighton Lobdell of Ponte Vedra, FL. Also surviving members of the family include five stepchildren, nine grandchildren and two great grandchildren. For 32 years, Francis worked at John Wiley and Sons, New York, NY, a leading college textbook publisher where he was Vice President, Chief Financial Officer and Director. He retired in 1978. He was President of the Board of Trustees of Rumson Country Day School, Rumson, NJ from 1962 to 1968. Board member of the Rumson Country Club, President and Trustee of Ekwanok Country Club, in Manchester, VT. US Seniors' Golf Assoc, Emeritus, Member of the New York Yacht Club. He was born in New York, NY on Aug. 24, 1921, the son of Ruth and Edward Dean Adams, a descendant of John Adams, second President of the United States. He was a member of the founding class of Rumson Country Day School, Rumson, NJ. He attended St. Mark's School in Southborough, MA., and he graduated from Yale University in 1944. Francis received an advanced management degree from Harvard University in 1956. He married Jane Childs (deceased) on August 8, 1944 of Monmouth County, NJ. He remarried Adair Gould Gans on Feb. 24, 1979. Francis served as second lieutenant in the US Armed Forces Air Corps flying a P-47 in World War II. Francis was a member of Trinity Church of Shelburne, and Landgrove Church, Landgrove, VT. Memorial Service will be held at 11:00 o'clock at the Trinity Church in Shelburne, VT. on Friday, Feb. 22, 2002. An additional service will be held during the summer in Landgrove, VT. In lieu of flowers, donations to Circle of Care, PO Box 477, Hincsburg, VT. 05461 or Hospice at Visiting Nurse Assoc, 1110 Prim Road, Colchester, VT. 05446 will be deeply appreciated.


        STONE-Robert W. (Robinson Stone). Actor/professor, age 81, resident of NYC, native of Oak Park, IL., passed away May 11, 2000. Son of Mr. and Mrs. S.O. Stone and brother of Virginia Stone Holland. Loved uncle of Nancy and Charles Arends, Gay and Charles Mead and Sandra and Stephen Nelson. Graduate of Northwestern and Yale. Broadway and other stage credits: Othello with Paul Robeson and Uta Hagen, Cyrano de Bergerac with Jose Ferrer, Macbeth with Michael Redgrave, 20th Century with Gloria Swanson and Mamba's Daughters with Ethel Waters. Best known as Joey, the mute, in the film Stalag 17 starring William Holden. Served as casting agent for Dave Garroway's Today Show, Jose Ferrer and David Merrick. Later years spent as tenured professor at Fordham. Taught English with a focus on playwrights. Mr. Stone's courses had long waiting lists and he was an inspiration to many students. He came out of theatrical retirement to play opposite student Denzel Washington in Fordham's production of Othello. Mr. Stone became a mentor to Mr. Washington and helped him obtain his first agent. Mr. Washington often credits Mr. Stone for launching his career. In lieu of flowers, send donations to West Suburban Hospice; 1000 Lake Street; Oak Park, IL. 60302.

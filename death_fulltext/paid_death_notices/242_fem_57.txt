
        GOLDBERGER--Blanche Rubin, died March 5, 2007, at the age of 93 years. She and her late husband, Emanuel Goldberger, M.D., were longtime residents of Scarsdale, New York. A graduate of Hunter College and Columbia University, she also attended the Jewish Theological Seminary and studied at the Sorbonne. She taught Hebrew and French and was a gifted sculptor/jeweler whose work received numerous awards. She is survived by her loving sons, Richard and Ary, grandsons, Zachary and Alexander, sister-in-law, Sylvia, and daughter-in-law, Ellen. A woman of valor, Blanche will be remembered always for her intellect and inspiration; her life and art will endure as a memory of joy. Services will be private. In lieu of flowers, donations may be made to the Margret and H.A. Rey Institute for Nonlinear Dynamics in Medicine, Beth Israel Deaconess Medical Center, c/o Development Office, 330 Brookline Ave. (BR), Boston, MA 02215.

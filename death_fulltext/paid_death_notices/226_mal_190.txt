
        QUIGLEY--Leonard V., 72. Died peacefully at home in Forest Hills Gardens on November 15, surrounded by his family. Born in Kansas City, Missouri, Len graduated from Holy Cross and Harvard Law School, and served in the Navy in the Korean War. A partner for over 35 years at the law firm of Paul, Weiss, where he founded the Canadian practice group, Len served for over 25 years as the General Counsel of the Archaeological Institute of America (AIA), and was a member of the Council on Foreign Relations and the Board of Directors of the Covenant House. Survived by Lynn, his wife of 41 years; four children, Matthew, Cannon, Daniel and Megan; 8 grandchildren; a sister, Rosemary McPhillips, and an extraordinarily close extended family. Predeceased by his brothers, Bernard and James, and four faithful German Shepherds, Major, Alberta, Petra and Sparta. Visiting hours Wednesday and Thursday evenings from 6:30-9 PM at Fox Funeral Home in Forest Hills and Mass of Christian Burial at Our Lady of Mercy Church in Forest Hills Friday at 10:30 AM. In lieu of flowers, donations to the AIA or the Covenant House of New York.
        $(6$)QUIGLEY--Leonard V. The partners, counsel, associates and staff of Paul, Weiss, Rifkind, Wharton & Garrison, LLP greatly mourn the loss of our friend and colleague, Leonard V. Quigley. Len was a partner and of counsel in the Corporate Department, the founder of the firm's Canadian Practice and a member of the Paul, Weiss family for more than 35 years. We extend our sincere condolences to his wife Lynn, his children, Leonard, Cannon, Daniel and Meagan and to all other members of the family.

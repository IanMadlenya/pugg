
        GEIS-Irving, molecular artist, writer and poet, passed away July 22, 1997 at the age of 88. The recipient of a Guggenheim Fellowship, he was world renowned as a pioneer in the field of biomolecular art. His innovative interpretations of molecular structures were a source of inspiration to scientists as well as elucidating through his art, the complexities of molecules to an entire generation of researchers and students of science. He is survived by his loving wife of sixty-one years, Miriam, his devoted and loving daughter, Sandy, as well as his brother, Ralph Geisberg, sister-in-law, Anne, and adoring nieces and nephews. He will be deeply and forever missed. Services Friday, July 25, at 1 PM at Plaza Memorial Chapel, 630 Amsterdam Ave. at 91st St. Tel. 769-4400


        EDELHERTZ-Herbert. On June 28, 1999. Former President of the Northwest Policy Studies Center in Kirkland, WA. A lawyer and expert on white-collar crime, he managed nationwide prosecutions for the Criminal Division of the U.S. Department of Justice, then established the Batelle Law and Justice Center in Seattle, WA. He created the National District Attorneys Association Economic Crime Project and was a Director of the National Center on White-Collar Crime. He is survived by his wife Ruth; a daughter, Jean; a son, Paul; a grandson, Benjamin; and a sister, Millicent.

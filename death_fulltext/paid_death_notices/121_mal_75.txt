
        FLEISCHMAN-Lawrence A. The Board of Trustees and Staff of The New York Public Library mourn the loss of Lawrence Fleischman, devoted husband of Trustee Barbara Fleischman, and esteemed friend of the Library. His deep personal affection for and commitment to The New York Public Library and to our Library for the Performimg Arts and its staff will be forever remembered. A passionate collector and scholar of art and antiquities, Mr. Fleischman's interest and generous philanthropy have had a profound and lasting impact on cultural institutions in New York, in the nation, and abroad. We will always be grateful for his wisdom and dedication to our Library. Our deepest sympathies to his wife, Barbara, and to his children, Rebecca, Arthur, and Martha. Elizabeth Rohatyn, Chairman Paul LeClerc, President Brooke Russell Astor, Honorary Chairman Marshall Rose and Andrew Heiskell, Chairmen Emeriti
        FLEISCHMAN-Lawrence A. The Board of Directors and staff of American Friends of the British Museum mourn the loss of our incomparable chairman. Lawrence Fleischman was a leader, a motivator, a scholar, and a teacher who changed the lives of many who knew him. His vitality and generosity were inspirational. We extend our deepest sympathies to Mr. Fleischman's wife, Barbara; his children, Rebecca, Arthur and Martha, and his granddaughter, Jennifer. Herbert L. Lucas Jr. Vice Chairman Adele S. Greene Executive Director
        FLEISCHMAN-Lawrence A. The death of Lawrence A. Fleischman deprives the American art world of one of its strongest and most effective voices. We remember him with gratitude. Friends and colleagues mourn his passing and offer deepest condolences to his family. John K. Howat, The Lawrence A. Fleischman Chairman of the Departments of American Art, The Metropolitan Museum of Art

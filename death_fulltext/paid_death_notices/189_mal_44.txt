
        HARVEY - Gerald Joseph. July 27, 1925-October 2, 2002. We mourn the loss of our loving husband, father, grandfather and brother. While we are deeply saddened, we are relieved that he no longer suffers. Jeff was a wonderful inspiration to those who loved him. His accomplishments include: Service in the U.S. Navy in WWII, a Bachelor's of Science Degree in Chemical Engineering from Tufts University, 50 years as Head of the Largest Testing Laboratory based in New York, Chairman of the American Society for Metals, L.I. Chapter, 1983-84, Chairman of the New York Chapter of the American Society for NonDestructive Testing. A world renowned disaster investigator he was also the author of numerous articles and holder of patents, including one of the first waste to energy systems ever developed. Gerald was the Chairman of two public companies which he launched, and was a pioneer in the environmental testing industry. He is survived by his wife Dorothy, children Karen, David and Beth, grandchildren Jessica, Halie, Julia, Mariel and Hunter, his sister and brother-in-law Betty and Robert Slegman of Shawnee Mission, KA, and brother and sister-in-law Roger and Ellen Harvey of Woodmere, NY. A memorial service will be held on Sunday, October 6, 2002, 9:45 A.M. at ''The Riverside'', 76th St and Amsterdam Ave.

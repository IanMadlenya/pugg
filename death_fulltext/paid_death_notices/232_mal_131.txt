
        GINSBERG--William R. Environmentalist, lawyer, activist, father, friend. On May 14, 2006, Bill Ginsberg died at home in his sleep at 75. He is survived by his sons Mark and Josh, their wives Darby and Cathy, his grandchildren Simon, Zachary & Madeleine, his sister Alane Cater, his love and companion for a decade, Arlene Sterne, and her children Mark, Philip, and Anna Bergman. Donations in lieu of flowers may be made to the Catskill Center, www.catskillcenter.org or Citizens Housing and Planning Council www.chpcny.org
        $(6$)GINSBERG--William. Hofstra University and its School of Law are deeply saddened by the death of Bill Ginsberg, Distinguished Professor Emeritus, and integral part of the Hofstra community since 1974. Prof. Ginsberg created the first course in environmental law taught at Hofstra and shared his passion for teaching and law with many students throughout his illustrious career. He will be remembered by the Hofstra community not only as an outstanding professor, but also as a great scholar, lawyer, activist and friend. We extend our deepest sympathy to his family. He will be greatly missed. John D. Miller, Chair, Board of Trustees Stuart Rabinowitz, President, Hofstra University Aaron Twerski, Dean, Hofstra Law School

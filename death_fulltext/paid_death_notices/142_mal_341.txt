
        KEHAYA-Ery W. Of Jupiter Island, Hobe Sound, FL, age 75, died November 23, 1998, at his home in Florida. He was born in New York City on October 17, 1923, the son of Ery Kehaya Sr. of Turkey and Grace Whitaker of Winston-Salem, NC. He was educated at the St. Paul's School in Concord, NH and at Yale University. He was honorably discharged from the U.S. Army-Air Force where he served during World War II. Mr. Kehaya was known for his charity and his strong support for education. Among his many educational beneficiaries were Duke University; The St. Paul's School, where he was honored as a Shattuck Fellow; The Asheville School and St. Andrew's College, both of which he served as a member of their boards of trustees. Mr. Kehaya's father founded Standard Commercial Corporation in 1910. When he joined the company in 1945 it was a small, closely held tobacco merchant company with only three employees. Ery started out unloading oriental tobacco on the docks in New York. By the time he retired as Chairman of the Corporation, Standard Commercial Corporation was a New York Stock Exchange - listed multinational company with over 9,000 full and part-time employees and a world leader in leaf tobacco and wool trading. Mr. Kehaya is survived by his wife, Helga; a daughter, Elizabeth W. Kehaya of Boulder, Colorado; two sons, Ery W. Kehaya, II and his wife Sharon, of Wilson, NC and Mark W. Kehaya and his wife Hannah of St. Petersburg, Russia; and five grandchildren, James, Julian, Robert, Alex and Mary Louise.


        MACK--John Duncan. Of Concord, MA. and Chatham, MA. died suddenly on September 27 while in England. He was born in Brooklyn, NY in 1924. He served in the infantry in WWII in the Pacific, receiving a Silver Star. He graduated from St. John's College, Annapolis '45 and Harvard Business School '50. As a marketing executive, he worked at Welch's, Procter & Gamble, Clairol and Gillette. From 1976-1992, he was President of Carter Products in New York City. John also served as President of Wave Hill; on the Township Committee of East Amwell, NJ and as Deputy Mayor; and as a Trustee of St. John's College. In Concord, he served as a Trustee of The Thoreau Society and Thoreau Farm Trust; and on the Historic District Commission. John is survived by his wife Lorna Carey Mack; his sisters, Anne Dean and Mary Hurst; his daughters, Pamela Mack, Sheila Mack, Carey Weber and Lorna Sheridan; and 7 grandchildren. Memorial Contributions may be made to Thoreau Farm Trust, Box 454, Concord, MA. 01742.

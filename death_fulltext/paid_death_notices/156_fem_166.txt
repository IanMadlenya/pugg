
        STARK-Ruth. Much loved and ever loving wife; confidante and life-long friend of Morton Stark, died peacefully Sunday, January 16 after valiantly fighting a variety of ailments for years. Cherished sister of Paula Rabinow. Fond aunt of and role model for Hannah Scherer, Andrew Scherer, Claudia Slovinsky, Sara Whipple & Laura Whipple. Doting great-aunt of Maya Rose Scherer and Eli Scherer. Co-creator, along with her dear friend Florence Valla, of Stark-Valla, their pioneering Greenwich Village shop. All are invited to share fond memories at a memorial service to be held at 3:00 P.M., Thursday, January 20 at ''The Riverside'', 76th Street and Amsterdam Avenue.
        STARK-Ruth. The Trustees and staff of the Jewish Board of Family and Children's Services mourn the passing of Ruth Stark, former director of the Mary S. Froelich Division of Volunteer Services. A consummate professional with grace, elegance & high standards, Ruth enriched the lives of many thousands of children and families through her creative design and implementation of agency volunteer programs. It was she who laid the foundation for JBFCS' current 2200 volunteers to serve thousands of New Yorkers every year. Our sincere condolences to her beloved husband, Morton, her treasured sister, Paula, and her nieces and nephews. Joseph S. Kaplan, President, JBFCS Seymour R. Askin, Jr., Chairman, JBFCS Gladys Wiesenthal, Chairwoman, Volunteer Services Divisional Committee Alan B. Siskind, Ph.D, Exec Vice President, JBFCS

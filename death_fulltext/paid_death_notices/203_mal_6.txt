
        AARONS--Morris, a New York City attorney who was elevated to the judiciary in 1969 died on December 1, 2003 at age 96. Judge Aarons graduated from Fordham University School of Pharmacy in 1927 and received a law degree from Saint John's University in 1931. He became trial counsel for Prudential Life Insurance. In 1960 was named general counsel to the National association of Pharmaceutical Manufacturers. He labored successfully to influence Congress and state legislatures to license generic drugs for public use. A lifelong Republican active in politics, Judge Aarons ran for the United States Congress in 1960. Judge Aarons served respectively as a judge in civil court, criminal term of the Supreme Court, Surrogate's Court of New York County and the Court of Claims. Subsequently he served as a judicial hearing officer until he was 92 years of age. He continued to practice law during that period as well. He is survived by his wife, Miriam, of Scarsdale, New York, their children, Marjorie, Paul, Stanley, Gail and Joseph, seven grandchildren and five great grandchildren. Funeral services will be held at Weinstein Memorial Chapel, 1652 Central Park Avenue, Yonkers, New York on Wednesday, December 3, 2003 at 12:00 P.M. Visitation will take place on Tuesday, December 2, 2003 from 7-9 PM.


        MALIS--Leonard, M.D. The Association of the Attending Staff of the Mount Sinai Hospital mourns the passing of an outstanding colleague, surgeon, and teacher. Dr. Malis devoted four decades of expertise and service to the institution. He served as Chairman of the Department of Neurosurgery for 20 years. In the 1960's, he performed the first micro-neurosurgical procedure and pioneered the first superficial temporal middle cerebral bypass procedure. He also taught the first micro - neurosurgical course in the United States. His historical clinical achievements will continue in the many lives he touched. Dr. Malis will be remembered for his brilliant talent and innovation in the field of Neurosurgery. We extend our sincere condolences to his family. Michael T. Harris, MD, President Vicki Lopachin, MD, Vice President Amy J. Weinstein, Administrator$(6$)
        MALIS--Leonard. The Mount Sinai Medical Center mourns the passing of Leonard Malis, Professor Emeritus and former Chairman of the Department of Neurosurgery. Renowned for his innovation and technical skills, Dr. Malis invented many neurosurgical instruments still in use around the world and introduced the use of the binocular microscope in the neurosurgical operating room. As Chairman of Neurosurgery for more than 20 years, Dr. Malis expanded upon the clinical, research and educational missions of the department, making it a national leader in the field. We extend our sincerest sympathy to his family and friends. Peter W. May, Chairman of the Boards of Trustees Kenneth L. Davis, M.D. President, CEO and Dean

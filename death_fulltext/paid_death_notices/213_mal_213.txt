
        SCHMIDT--Herman J., 87, passed away on October 18, 2004, at Greenwich Hospital in Greenwich, CT. Mr. Schmidt, retired Vice-Chairman of Mobil Oil Corporation, was born in Davenport, Iowa, on February 26, 1917. He was a graduate of The University of Iowa and Harvard Law School. Mr. Schmidt is survived by his wife, Eileen Schenken Schmidt, of Greenwich; a son, Paul David Schmidt, of Portland, OR; a daughter, Sarah Louise Schmidt, of Whidbey Island, WA; a brother, Stanley E. Schmidt of MacQuarie Hills, New South Wales, Australia; two step children, David E. Carpenter, of Iowa City, IA and Nancy Carpenter Leahy, of Idaho Falls, ID; and four grandchildren. Prior to joining Mobil in 1951, Mr. Schmidt was an attorney with Cravath, Swaine & Moore in New York City. During World War II, he served in the U.S. Army Military Intelligence in the Pentagon and in England. In addition to Mobil, Mr. Schmidt was a director of H.J. Heinz Company, HON Industries, Mapco Inc., C.I.T. Financial Corporation, Kaiser Cement Corporation, Macmillan Inc., National Bank of North America, N.L. Industries, Ryder System, Inc., Tri-Continental Corporation, and the Seligman Funds. For many years he was a director of the American Enterprise Institute and the University of Iowa Foundation, both of which he served a period as chairman. At Mr. Schmidt's request, no services will be held. Contributions may be sent to The University of Iowa Foundation, P.O. Box 4550, Iowa City, IA 52244-4550.

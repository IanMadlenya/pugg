
        GOLDRING-Benjamin, 88, died April 17, 2000. After Columbia University Law School he joined the Abraham Lincoln Brigade in the Spanish Civil War where, in the first lines of the front lines, he was severely wounded four times. Also a veteran of the U.S. Army in WW II. After the war as civil rights lawyer he participated in trials & appeals of Harry Bridges & appeals of Morton Sobell. He was instrumental in effecting release from a Franco prison of Leoncio Pena. He is survived by his wife of 49 years, Muriel Goldring & nieces Brett Stein & Dana Newman. A memorial service will be held Sunday June 4 at 12:30 PM. Gifts to Southern Poverty Law Center or ALBA, Abraham Lincoln Brigade Archives are worthy memorials.

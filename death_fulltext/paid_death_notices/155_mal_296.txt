
        SHANLEY-John. Died peacefully on December 14. Born June 17, 1914 in New York, he was the son of the late John Shanley and the late Mary Burke Shanley. A 1937 graduate of St. John's, he was the captain of the 1936/37 St. John's basketball team. During World War II he served in the Army Counter Intelligence Corps. In 1944 he was assigned to the Manhattan Project. He returned to the New York City Police Department in 1947. Over the course of twenty years, he served in all branches and ranks, and was appointed as Chief Inspector of the NYPD in June of 1965. He retired to Boca Raton, Florida in 1970. He was predeceased by his wife Anna O'Brien Shanley in 1965 and his wife, Ethel Muffatti Shanley in 1991. He is survived by his devoted brother Frank Shanley of Brooklyn, his brother Bob Shanley of Fort Lauderdale, Florida, his son, John Shanley of Dallas, Texas, daughters Peg Hickey of Stamford, Connecticut, & Dr. Catherine Shanley of White Plains, New York, 7 grandchildren and one great grandchild. There will be a memorial mass at St. Patrick's Church, Bay Ridge, Brooklyn at 11 AM Tuesday, December 28.

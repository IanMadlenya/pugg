
        HYTIER--Adrian Doris, formerly Lichtenstein Dale Professor of French at Vassar College, where she taught from 1959 to 1997, died suddenly at her home in Poughkeepsie on April 18, 2007. Professor Hytier also taught for a short period at Columbia University and at the University of California, Davis. She was born in Iran, where her father, Jean Hytier, a prominent specialist of twentieth - century French literature, was a university professor. She grew up in Algeria and Paris, graduated summa cum laude from Barnard College in 1952 and received the doctorate in History from Columbia University in 1958. Known primarily as a scholar of the eighteenthcentury, Adrienne Hytier also published books on the Vichy regime, the history of warfare, and the nineteenth-century diplomat Arthur de Gobineau. She served on the Board of the American Society of Eighteenth Century Studies from 1971 to 1974, and for many years was editor of the French section of The Eighteenth Century: a Current Bibliography. Adrienne Hytier will be remembered for her Voltairian sense of humor, her passion for great literature and her dazzling flower garden that had become a Poughkeepsie landmark.

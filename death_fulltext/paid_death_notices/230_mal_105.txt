
        MURRIE--Richard Wallace. Of New York City, died peacefully at age 88 on March 6 after a long illness. He is survived by his wife of 30 years Rita T. Murrie, his stepson Steven Finley and stepdaughter Elizabeth Vary, four beloved stepgrandchildren, and his nephew William M. Clevenger and niece Mimi Chitwood. Dick was born in Hershey, PA on March 23, 1917, the son of William F.R. Murrie, who served as the President of the Hershey Chocolate Corporation from its incorporation in 1908 until 1947, and Sarah Maitland Murrie. Dick graduated from Lawrenceville School in 1935, from Princeton University, Summa cum Laude, in 1939 and from Yale Law School in 1942. He was a Lieutenant in the U.S. Navy, serving in both the North Atlantic and the Pacific from 1942 to 1946. Dick was a Senior Vice President and Fiduciary Counsel of Bessemer Trust Company, from which he retired in 1983, and served as the chairman of the Board of Pacific Inland Navigation Company. His prior marriage, to the former Elizabeth Horgan, ended with her death in 1975. He was a member of the Quogue Field Club, the Quogue Beach Club, the Princeton Club of New York, the University Club of New York, the St. Andrew's Society of New York, the Sons of the Revolution and the Society of Colonial Wars, and served as President of the New York Numismatic Club. Dick was a kind and generous man, an historian, a raconteur with a sharp mind, a quick smile and a ready laugh. He was beloved by numerous friends he easily accumulated over a long and very full life. Friends may call at Frank E. Campbell, 1076 Madison Ave. at 81st Street on Thursday, March 9, from 2 to 4 PM and from 7 to 9 PM. Funeral services will be held at the Church of St. Vincent Ferrer, Lexington Avenue at 66th Street at 10 AM on Friday, March 10, 2006.

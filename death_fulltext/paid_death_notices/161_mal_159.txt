
        WILKERSON-James (Joe) Platt, died on June 8, at his home, Dover House, Chipping Camden, Gloucester, England. He was 85 years old. He died from complications caused by cancer of the spine. A graduate of Amherst College in 1937, he joined the Young & Rubicam advertising agency, where he advanced to head one of its largest clients, General Foods. In 1959, he became Senior Vice President and Director, Y&R Europe and Managing Director of the agency's London office. After leaving Young & Rubicam as an Executive Vice President in 1968, he served on the board of Marsteller Advertising and Burson-Marsteller, Public Relations and took an active role in its European advertising operation. He was also a sheep farmer and tree planter at his former home, Admington Hall in Warwickshire. He was President of the International Wine and Food Society. He is survived by his wife, Audrey (Potter); three daughters, Ann Olson, from Olive Hill, Kentucky, Cathy Wilkerson, from Brooklyn, NY, and Robin Wilkerson from Lincoln, Mass., and four grandchildren. Memorial donations to your local community hospice.

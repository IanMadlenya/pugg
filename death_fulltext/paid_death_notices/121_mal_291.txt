
        SPECTOR-Herbert, 81. Beloved husband of Sylvia (Schachner), died on February 19, 1997 in Sarasota, Florida. Born in New York City in 1915. He was a graduate of Fordham University and Fordham Law School and an attorney in NYC since 1938. Following his service in World War II, Mr. Spector became a court attache in The Bronx County Court House. 33 years later he retired as Executive Officer of The First Judicial Department of The State Supreme Court. He taught for 18 years at The Bernard Baruch School of The City College of New York in the area of real property law, a field in which he was an acknowledged expert. He was a Democratic District Leader in The Bronx, a member of the Executive Board of The NYC Anti-Defamation League of B'nai Brith and The National Commission of The ADL. He served as Special Master in NY Supreme Court, was a Family Court Hearing Officer, and a Mediator in The Supreme Court in Florida. He was a member of The Bronx County and NY County Bar Associations and The American Judges Association. He was a member of Temple Hillel in Valley Stream for more than 35 years. He is survived by his wife of 56 years, Sylvia. He is the father of Amy Applebaum, Stephen Spector and Cheryl Spector. All 3 children followed his lead and became attorneys. He also leaves 7 grandchildren, Seth and Jessica (Amy), Morgan and Michael with (Stephen and Jane), and Rachel, Daniel and Leah (Cheryl and Tom). An avid reader, golfer and bridge player he was deeply loved by his family and will be sorely missed. A memorial service will be held at 1pm on Sunday, February 23 at Tishman Memorial Chapel, Swift Road and Stickney Point, Sarasota. Cremation will follow.

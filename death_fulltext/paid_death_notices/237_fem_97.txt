
        RUANE--Joy M., 65, of New York City, widow of William J., died Thursday, October 12th, of lung cancer. A devoted wife, mother, she is survived by her daughter, Paige, her son, Thomas, her stepson, William, and her stepdaughter, Elizabeth, her brother, Stephen Mehrbach and her sister, Doreen Errickson, and four granddaughters. Visitation will be Saturday, October 14th, 3-6 PM, at Frank E. Campbell--The Funeral Chapel, 1076 Madison Ave. at 81st St. Burial will be private in Connecticut.
        $(6$)RUANE--Joy. The Columbia University TeenScreen Program is deeply saddened by the death of Joy Ruane. Mrs. Ruane was a longtime friend of the TeenScreen Program and a co-founder of our National Advisory Council. She was a committed and inspiring advocate for the early identification of mental illness in young people. We will greatly miss her partnership and her grace. Our deepest sympathies go out to her children, Paige, Tom, Lili and Billy, and to her extended family.
        $(6$)RUANE--Joy. Mannes College The New School for Music and The New School deeply mourn the passing of Joy Ruane. She was a very special human being--a true rarity in today's world. Just beneath her elegant demeanor lay a passionate commitment to numerous worthy causes all united by the common thread of helping those in need. Her devotion to educating young musicians and also supporting Mannes' Community Orchestra built on her ardor for music's power to inspire creativity. Her extraordinary generosity and her personal commitment will be sorely missed by all who knew her. Philip Scaturro, Chair, Board of Trustees Bob Kerrey, President, The New School Joel Lester, Dean, Mannes College The New School for Music John Beerbower, Chair, Mannes Board of Governors

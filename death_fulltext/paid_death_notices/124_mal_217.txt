
        FOX-James M. The 35,000 members of the alumni, students, faculty and staff of John Jay College of Criminal Justice mourn the loss of James M. Fox, an admired law enforcement leader. A strong advocate for the professional development of law enforcement personnel to higher education, his contributions to public safety and leadership in the criminal justice community have made the city and nation a better place. He will be sorely missed by all those who had the privilege of knowing and working with him. We offer our deepest sympathy to the members of the Fox family. Gerald W. Lynch, President John Jay College of Criminal Justice, The City University of New York
        FOX-Jim. The volunteers and staff of Guiding Eyes for the Blind mourn the passing of a good friend, Jim Fox. Through his generosity and commitment to the important mission of Guiding Eyes, thousands of visually impaired individuals have been, and continue to be helped through the use of professionally trained guide dogs. The students and family of Guiding Eyes will miss him.
        FOX-James M. It is with our deepest sorrow that the Directors, Officers and Employees of the Dime Savings Bank of Williamsburgh mourn the passing of our friend and Director, James M. Fox. We extend our sympathy to his wife, Elaine, and their entire family. We will miss him greatly. The Dime Savings Bank of Williamsburgh. Vincent F. Palagiano, Chairman
        FOX-James M. The Officers, Directors and Staff of the USO of Metropolitan New York extend deepest sympathy to the family of James Fox, a valued Board Member since 1995. Oliver M. Mendell, Chairman

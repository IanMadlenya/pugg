
        ELION-Gertrude B. The Board of Trustees, President David Chang, Chancellor George Bugliarello, the Faculty, Alumni, Staff and Students of Polytechnic University note with profound regret the passing of Gertrude Elion, 1989 Honorary Doctorate recipient from Polytechnic, former Polytechnic doctoral student and corecipient of the 1988 Nobel Prize in Medicine. Her uncompromising dedication to pharmaceutical research and her important contributions to combat such diseases as cancer and AIDS will long be recognized. The Polytechnic community is honored to have known such a brilliant and wonderful woman and expresses its heartfelt condolences to her family and friends.
        ELION-Gertrude B. The City University of New York deeply mourns the passing of Gertrude B. Elion, illustrious 1937 graduate of Hunter College and member of Hunter College Hall of Fame, who was awarded the Nobel Prize in Medicine in 1988. She was a pioneering research scientist who served as an inspiring role model and helped develop life saving drugs to combat leukemia, AIDS, septic shock, maleria and organ transplant rejection. Her contributions to science, knowledge and the advancement of women will always be remembered. Anne A. Paolucci, Chairwoman, Board of Trustees Christoph M. Kimmich, Interim Chancellor
        ELION-Gertrude. The American Committee for the Weizmann Institute of Science mourns the loss of a brilliant American scientist, pioneer in the development of innovative drugs, Nobel Laureate in Medicine, champion for women in research and respected friend of the Institute. Gershon Kekst, Chairman Weizmann Institute of Science Prof. Haim Harari, President Robert H. Asher, Chairman American Committee Albert Willner, M.D., President Martin Kraar, Exec. V.P.


        MOONEY--Robert Knapp. Former Vice Chairman of Allied Van Lines and Chairman of Hartford Despatch, has died. He was 85. A lifelong resident of Connecticut until his retirement, Mr. Mooney was chosen as the state's Small Businessman of the Year in 1987. Son of Edward G. and Grace K. Mooney, he succeeded his father in the family's moving and warehousing business. A graduate of Harvard University and the Harvard School of Business Administration, Mr. Mooney served with the Chinese First National Army in WWII for which he received the Bronze Star and China's Order of the Flying Cloud. Devoted to the city of Hartford, he was chairman of the Chamber of Commerce and a Regent of the University of Hartford. He served as director of the Hartford Institute for Criminal and Social Justice and the Hartford Foundation for Public Giving. He was a Trustee of the Wadsworth Atheneum and the New Britain Museum of American Art. He leaves his wife, Barbara Connell Mooney; five children, E. Chadwick Mooney of Rancho Santa Fe, CA; Christopher G. Mooney of Larchmont, NY; Francia K. Mooney of Jamestown, NC; Cameron H. Mooney of Redding, CA; Anson B. Mooney of West Hartford, CT; two sisters, Mrs. John Kelly of Wayland, MA and Mrs. Thomas Sutton of Duxbury, MA; and six grandchildren. Contributions may be made to the ''Robert Knapp Mooney Memorial Fund'', payable to the Wadsworth Atheneum Museum of Art, 600 Main St., Hartford, CT 06103. A memorial service will be held at 10 am, Saturday, November 5, 2005, at the Trinity College Chapel in Hartford. In Florida, a service will be held at 11am, Saturday, February 18, 2006, at St. Andrew's Episcopal Church, Boca Grande. Funeral services were private.

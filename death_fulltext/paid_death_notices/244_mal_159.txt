
        LIPNER--Leon I. of Hollis Hills, NY born March 19, 1920, in Brooklyn, NY to Max and Esther Lipner died May 15, 2007. Graduate of Brooklyn College, MBA from Adelphi University. Merchant Marine officer in World War II. Beloved husband of Alice (Fein) for 67 years. Devoted father of Chester and Eileen and Marshall and Susan. Much loved grandfather of Marci and Colin, Kenneth and Lisa, Jay and Carol and Stephanie and Scott. Proud great-grandfather of Nicole, Ethan, Dylan, Mya, Rachel, Sydney, Emma and Madison. Certified Public Accountant since 1947 and respected founding partner of Lipner, Sofferman & Co., LLP where he practiced accounting for over 50 years. Emeritus Professor of Accounting and Taxation at Adelphi University. Member of New York State Society of CPA's and AICPA, Arbitrator/ Mediator with American Arbitration Association and NASD Board of Arbitrators. He was an unforgettable, generous personality, who enjoyed traveling the world with his beloved wife Alice. An avid tennis player and reader, he loved the New York Times crossword puzzles. He will be loved and missed forever. Services will be held Friday, May 18, 2007 at 12:00 noon at Riverside Nassau North Chapel, 55 Station Plaza North, Great Neck, NY (opposite Great Neck train station). A., National Headquarters, 1811 R Street NW, Washington, D.C. 20009 or to the Hospice Care Unit of the Margaret Tietz Nursing & Rehabilitation Center, 16411 Chapin Parkway, Jamaica, NY, 11432.

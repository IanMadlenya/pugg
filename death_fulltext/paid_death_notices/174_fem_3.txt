
        HERZOG - Elizabeth Peterson. Mrs. Elizabeth Peterson Herzog, 93, died peacefully May 24, 2001 in Ithaca, New York. She had moved to New York City after marrying Paul M. Herzog in 1981 and continued to live there after his death in 1986. She was a Vassar graduate who did postgraduate work at Radcliffe and in Europe. She married Max Habicht in 1932 and brought up her children in Geneva, Switzerland where she served on the board of the International School and founded the Cercle International des Amis de la Musique and the International Forum. In 1962, she married Paul Shipman Andrews, who died in 1966. In 197074, she served in the U.S. Peace Corps in Tunisia and helped to develop the nascent tourist commission. She was a member of the Cosmopolitan Club of New York City, and past chair of its Foreign Visitors Committee. She is survived by her children, grandchildren and great-grandchildren, step children and their families, and an ''adopted'' daughter and family. Her family and friends are thankful for her sharp mind and the sunshine she brought to them throughout her life. A memorial service will be celebrated for her in Duxbury, Massachusetts at the Peterson family plot on August 11, 2001.

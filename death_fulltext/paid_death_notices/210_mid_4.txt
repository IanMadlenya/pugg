
        WERKSMAN--Harry. The officers, board and staff of the American Technion Society (ATS) mourn the loss of their dear and steadfast friend, Harry Werksman, of Rancho Mirage, Calif. A founder of the Women's Division/Desert Chapter along with his wife Mary-honorary vice chair of the Women's Division and current Desert Chapter chairMr. Werksman served as president of the Pittsburgh Chapter, as well as a national vice president and member of the national Board of Directors. Life trustees of the ATS Board of Regents, the Werksmans are Technion Guardians who have reached the highest level of commitment to the ATS, the Technion and Israel. They established the Harry Werksman Physics Building and the Harry Werksman Research Fund, and supported a variety of Women's Division Medical Engineering Projects and scholarships. An honorary life member of the Technion International Board of Governors, Mr. Werksman, a retired businessman, was also involved with a variety of institutions in the Palm Springs area. Mrs. Werksman, recognized for her active devotion with a Technion honorary doctorate in 1996, is also active in local organizations. We extend our condolences to her, sons Harry, William and David, and the entire Werksman family. The American Society for Technion-Israel Institute of Technology Stephen A. Laser, President Evelyn Berger, Chairman of the Board Melvyn H. Bloom, Executive Vice President Natalie B. Lyons, Women's Division President Gloria Neimark, Women's Leadership Dir.

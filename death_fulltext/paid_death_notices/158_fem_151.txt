
        ADELSON-Loretta. Mother of Beth & Stewart, widow of the late Henry, sister of Hy, Sara, and the late Bob and Bette. Was a cherished sister and daughter, stalwart support of four siblings and helpmate of immigrant parents; Organizer of neighborhood baseball team and award-winning amateur vaudeville performer with siblings; Night student at Hunter College, street-corner organizer for social justice and civil rights, volunteer nurse's aide in WW II; A most gracious, charming, dignified, yet unassuming lady, she found purpose and joy in helping others. Columbia U. PhD, Deputy Chair of Department of Speech at Brooklyn College, grant-winner and creator of college's program of recording for the blind called of ''Incalculable Value'' by chairman; Outstanding, creative, inspiring teacher, deeply interested in and loved by students; Cherished wife of over 50 years, inexhaustibly dedicated to husband's well-being and comfort during long illness; Above all else, most caring, affectionate and thoughtful mother possible, never missing an opportunity to fill children's lives with security, joy, curiosity and love. Cherished beyond any words. Donations may be made in memory to the following charities: Recording for the Blind and Dyslexic, 20 Roszel Road, Princeton, N.J. 08540; Memorial Sloan-Kettering Cancer Center, 1275 York Avenue, NY, NY 10021 or The National Audubon Society, 700 Broadway, NY, NY 10003.

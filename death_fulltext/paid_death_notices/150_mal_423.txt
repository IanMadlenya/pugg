
        FLESCHNER-Malcom King. died at home on Tuesday, July 27,1999 from complications arising from renal failure. Mr. Fleschner was born in New York City on March 25, 1918 to the late Abraham and Beatrice Fleschner. He was raised in New York and graduated from the McBurney School in 1935 and the Wharton School of Finance at the University of Pennsylvania in 1939. In 1941 he married Janice Hubshman, also of New York City. During World War II Mr. Fleschner served as a second lieutenant in the United States Marine Corps where he saw action in the South Pacific. In the business world Mr. Fleschner apprenticed as an executive at Macy's, worked as an investment banker at Wertheim & Co and later, with William Becker, founded a hedge fund, Fleschner Becker Associates. In 1970 he moved from Westport, Connecticut to Palm Beach. A devoted husband, father and grandfather who dearly loved his family. Mr. Fleschner will be remembered for his courage, determination, generosity and wit. He and his wife Janice traveled extensively, for many years summering in the Dordogne region of France. He gave generously to many charitable organizations, including the United Way, Planned Parenthood, the ACLU, the United Negro College Fund, the Wharton School and the Democratic Party. Mr. Fleschner is survived by his wife, Janice; son and daughter-in-law Andrew and Constance Fleschner; daughter and son-in-law Laura and Gerhard Gschwandtner; sister Rita Caine and six grandchildren. In lieu of flowers, donations may be sent to the United Way of Palm Beach, 44 Coconut Row, Palm Beach, Florida 33480.

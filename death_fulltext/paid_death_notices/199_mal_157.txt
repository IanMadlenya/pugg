
        GITTLESON--Allan Tod. Hofstra University is profoundly saddened by the death of Chairman Emeritus Allan Tod Gittleson. A generous and dedicated member of the Hofstra University Board of Trustees from 1967, he served as Chair from 1973 through 1975. He was named Chairman Emeritus in 1992. He was founder and CEO of the Hofstra Health Dome. Allan Tod Gittleson Hall, Hofstra's life science building was dedicated in 1979. Mr. Gittleson's concern for the needs of students was evident. He was the creator and a major benefactor of the University's Distinguished Academic Scholar Program, an active supporter of Hofstra's NOAH program for under-represented student populations and of the program for Higher Education of the Disabled. He also helped found the Hofstra School of Law. He is survived by his beloved wife Mildred; daughters June and Nancy, her husband Michael; 3 grandchildren and 3 great grandchildren. In tribute to the memory of Chairman Emeritus Gittleson, all Hofstra University flags have been lowered to half staff. A memorial celebration will be held at Hofstra University in September, 2003.

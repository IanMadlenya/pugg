
        PETERS - James J. The Trustees, Faculty and Staff of Mount Sinai School of Medicine and The Mount Sinai Medical Center mourn the passing of James J. Peters, the Executive Director of the Eastern Paralyzed Veterans Association. For the past eighteen years, the Association has generously supported clinical research on spinal cord damage at Mount Sinai. Under James Peters's leadership, the Association also made major contributions to the Wound Care Program, research fellowships, and other scientific and clinical programs to improve the lives of people with disabilities. James Peters's courage and commitment to health care were inspiring, and he will be very much missed. We extend our sincere condolences to his wife, Mary Ann, and to his many friends and colleagues. Peter W. May, Chairman of the Board The Mt Sinai Medical Center Kenneth I. Berns, MD, PhD President and CEO The Mt Sinai Medical Center Nathan G. Kase, MD, Interim Dean Mt Sinai School of Medicine Larry H. Hollier, MD, President, The Mount Sinai Hospital
        PETERS-James J. The Faculty and Staff of the Department of Rehabilitation Medicine of The Mount Sinai Medical Center are deeply saddened by the passing of James J. Peters, the Executive Director of the Eastern Paralyzed Veterans Association and very special friend. James was a generous and dedicated leader who shared our special commitment to improving the lives of people with disabilities. He was courageous and compassionate, and he and his colleagues at the Eastern Paralyzed Veterans Association inspired all of us with their vision and extraordinary support of many of our important scientific and clinical programs. James Peters will be deeply missed. We extend our heartfelt condolences to his wife, Mary Ann, to his colleagues at the Eastern Paralyzed Association, and to all those whose lives he touched. Kristjan T. Ragnarsson, MD Dr. Lucy C. Moses Professor, and Chairman, Dept. of Rehabilitation Medicine Mount Sinai Medical Center

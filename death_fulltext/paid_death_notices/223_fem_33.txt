
        SHAPIRO--Dr. Miriam Klein. Jewish educator and scholar, respected and loved, a leading professional in her field. Beloved wife of Saul. Selfless mother of Ephraim, Sara and Jonathan Shuter, Rachel and Barry Kirzner, Sim and Susan, and Rivke Dina. Adoring and adored grandmother of Avishai, Benjy, Yehudit and Elisheva, Yakira, Margalit and Dalia, Avigayil and Isaac. Cherished sister of Hannah Katz, Rivke and Jerry Berkowitz. She touched and enriched the lives of all who knew her. Services Sunday, August 7th, 10:30 a.m. at Young Israel of White Plains, 135 Old Mamaroneck Road.
        SHAPIRO--Dr. Miriam Klein. The entire Camp Ramah in the Berkshires mishpacha of campers, staff, alumni, board members and friends mourns the passing of our friend and mentor. Miriam was a legend in our camp as a teacher, rosh hinuch and board member. Always quick with a word of Torah, Miriam influenced two generations of Ramahnicks. Her wisdom, insight and vision will be sorely missed. Her name will live on in the Beitan Shapira named in honor of both Miriam and her beloved husband and honorary chair of camp, Saul, and through her family who are an integral part of the camp community. Hamakom yinacheym...To Saul, to the children, Ephraim, Sara, Rachel, Sim and Rivke Dina, and the grandchildren. Steve Goldstein, President Rabbi Paul Resnick, Director
        SHAPIRO--Dr. Miriam Klein. The New York Metropolitan Region of the United Synagogue of Conservative Judaism mourns the untimely passing of the wife of our past president Dr. Saul Shapiro. Our condolences to the entire family. May her memory be for a blessing. Jerry I. Klibanoff, Pres. Bruce F. Greenfield, Executive Director
        SHAPIRO--Miriam. Westchester Hebrew High School mourns the passing of Jewish educator, Dr. Miriam Shapiro. May the family be comforted amongst mourners of Zion and Jerusalem. Rabbi B. Majerowicz, Headmaster David Kalman, President


        DAVIS--O.C. ''Cliff'', 85, an innovator and leader in the natural gas industry, died on Sunday, April 23. As chairman of the American Gas Association, he guided the industry through price decontrol, earning him the Gas Man of the Year Award in 1983. Davis was also known for introducing natural gas storage fields to the industry. Davis was the architect of restructurings and mergers of several energy companies over his career. He served on the National Petroleum Council under President Nixon. In 1986, Davis became executive VP and a director of Occidental Petroleum where he remained until his retirement in 1991.

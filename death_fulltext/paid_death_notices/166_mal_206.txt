
        DAIN-Frank McLellan. A retired radiologist and resident of Chapel Hill, North Carolina, died November 11, 2000 at his home. He was 79. Dr. Dain was born December 27, 1920 in Jamaica, N.Y., the son of Marguerite Nason Dain and Frank McLellan Dain, II. He graduated from Dartmouth College in 1943 and received his accelerated medical degree during World War II from Columbia-Presbyterian Medical School in N.Y., N.Y. in 1945. He was an intern and resident at St. Luke's Hospital in New York City. He served in the U.S. Navy from 1942-1948, stationed in Bainbridge, MD. from 1945-1948. On May 27, 1944, he and Blossom Mary Huizer were married in Belleville, N.J. Dr. Dain began practicing at Englewood Hospital in Englewood, New Jersey in 1948, and later opened a radiology practice in Nyack, N.Y., which grew to 8 physicians. Before his retirement in 1982, Dr. Dain was the Chief of ultrasound at St. Luke's Hospital in New York City. He was recognized as one of the early specialists in the nascent field of ultrasound. He and his wife retired to N.C. in 1982 and lived in Tryon and New Bern before moving to Chapel Hill in 1995. Dr. Dain was predeceased by his sister, Grace Mary Stern, in 1998. In addition to his wife of 56 years, survivors include his three children, Joanne Dain Hoag of Dryden, N.Y., Susan Single-Dain of Toronto, Ontario, and Frank Westbrook Dain, of Chapel Hill, N.C., 7 grandchildren, and ten nieces and nephews.

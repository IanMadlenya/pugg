
        TOWER-John L. 81, a long time Ridgefield resident and retired International Paper Company executive died Wednesday at Laurelwood Care Center in Ridgefield. Mr. Tower of 3 Peaceable Hill Road, Ridgefield was the husband of Shirley Leake Tower. Born in Auburndale, Massachusetts on April 2, 1916. He was the son of C. Lothrop and Esther Day Tower. He attended Phillips Exeter Academy and received a Bachelor of Arts Degree from Dartmouth College in 1938. Mr. Tower began as a newspaperman in Nashville, Tennessee and later joined International Paper Company in New York City. During his 25-year career at International Paper, he rose to become Corporate Vice President of Public Relations and later President of the International Paper Company Foundation. He retired in 1971. In 1950, Mr. Tower moved to Ridgefield where he served for 15 years on the Ridgefield Library Board of Trustees. Along with other board members, he was integral in bringing professional librarians and a larger structure to the library. Mr. Tower was the former President of Silver Spring Country Club and served on its Board of Trustees from 1980 on. He also sat on the Board of Trustees at Cardigan Mountain School in Canaan, New Hampshire for 28 years. He led Cardigan's first capital fund campaign. Besides his wife, he is survived by three daughters, Ellen Mulvaney of Argyle, New York, Sherry Purdy of Scituate, Massachusetts and Judith Symon of Shoreham, Vermont, a brother Frederic Tower of Wilton, seven grandchildren and a great-grandson. Contributions in Mr. Tower's name may be made to The American Parkinson's Disease Association, 116 John Street, NY, NY 10034. There are no calling hours. Services will be held Tuesday, July 15 at 2PM at St. Stephen's Church in Ridgfield.

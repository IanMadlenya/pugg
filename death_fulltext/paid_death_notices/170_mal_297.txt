
        de GIVE-Paul de Barsy. In Newburyport, MA, of Newburyport, MA, formerly of NYC and Southampton. Retired senior vice president of Dominick & Dominick, Inc, where he was editor and publisher for 25 years of ''Technical Comments.'' Served as treasurer of St. David's School and Lenox Hill Neighborhood Association, NYC. Graduate of St. Paul's School '30, and Harvard '34. Mr. de Give became captain of the Harvard hockey team and was awarded the John Tudor Cup for hockey in 1933 and 1934. He was elected to the Harvard Varsity Hall of Fame for football, hockey and baseball, and selected to be goaltender for the 1936 US Olympic hockey team. He served as a lieutenant in the navy in World War II. Mr. de Give is survived by his wife of 59 years, the former Eleanor Hoguet, children David, Ghislaine, Paul, Philip and Dominique, seven grandchildren, two step-grandchildren, and brothers Lawrence and Louis. A Memorial Mass will be held Saturday, April 7, at 10 AM, at St. Louis DeGonzague Church in Newburyport, MA.


        WEYHER-Harry F. Jr. On March 27 in La Grange, North Carolina. He was 80. Born in Wilson, North Carolina, he attended the University of North Carolina where he was Phi Beta Kappa. He joined the War in Europe in 1943, becoming a First Sergeant in the Army and earning two battle stars. In 1949, he graduated magna cum laude from Harvard Law School, where he was an editor of the Harvard Law Review, following which he joined Cravath, Swaine & Moore. From 1951 to 1952, he served as Special Assistant Attorney General to the NY State Crime Commission. In 1954, he co-founded the law firm of Olwine, Connelly, Chase, O'Donnell & Weyher. He was also an Adjunct Associate Professor at New York University School of Law. He was later ''of counsel'' to the NY law firm of Hollyer, Brady, Smith, Troxell, Barret, Rockett, Hines & Mone. In the mid-1990's, he returned to North Carolina and continued to serve as President of the Pioneer Fund, a non-profit foundation. He was also a Member of the Society of the Cincinnati. He is survived by his wife, Michelle, a sister, Ella Green Weyher of Kinston, and 3 children by his marriage to the late Barbara Dore McCusker: Barbara Brandon Weyher of Raleigh, NC; Harry Frederick Weyher III of Wilton, CT; & Laura Weyher Hall, also of Wilton. He is also survived by 8 grandchildren. A memorial was held in Kinston, NC on March 30. Contributions may be made to the Laura C. Weyher Charitable Foundation, a foundation named after Mr. Weyher's mother, which supports charities in Eastern North Carolina (1108 West Vernon Avenue, Kinston, N.C. 28501.)


        SULLIVAN--Stephen, Brother J., F.S.C. The Board of Trustees and the entire Manhattan College community mourns the passing of Brother J. Stephen Sullivan, F.S.C., the College's 17th President. The Boston native was 86. Noted teacher, scholar, theologian and administrator, Brother Sullivan served Manhattan College tirelessly for more than a quarter century. Brother Sullivan was a member of the Brothers of the Christian Schools, a Roman Catholic teaching order he entered in 1938. He joined Manhattan College as Assistant Professor of Theology in 1959 and later became Academic Vice President, Executive Vice President and Provost, and in 1975 was named President, a position he held for 12 years. A champion of Catholic higher eduction, he was dedicated to establishing new programs, which enhanced the College's academic landscape. Programs for teaching the handicapped, graduate programs and courses in professional ethics, biotechnology and computer science were introduced. Brother Sullivan is credited with fully implementing the transformation of Manhattan College into a co-educational institution, insuring the integration of women into the entire College curriculum. During this time, the College experienced significant growth in enrollment and began transformation into a residence institution. He will be deeply missed. Visitationwill be on Friday, January 12th from 3 to 5 pm and 7 to 9 pm in The Rotunda of Memorial Hall at Manhattan College, 4513 Manhattan College Parkway, Riverdale, NY. Mass of Christian Burial will be held on Saturday, January 13th at 10 am in the Chapel of De La Salle and His Brothers at Manhattan College. Interment will follow immediately at Gate of Heaven Cemetery in Hawthorne, NY. Br. Thomas J. Scanlan, F.S.C., President and Members, Board of Trustees


        MOORE-William Joseph. Of Rye Brook, died November 4, 2001, after a brief illness. He was born September 18, 1918 in New York City. He married Justine Collins in 1945 who predeceased him. He graduated Fordham Prep 1935 and from Fordham University 1939. Mr. Moore graduated from New York University School of Law after he had completed his military service. Mr. Moore served as Dean of Admissions at Fordham Law School and retired in June of 2001 after more than thirty years of service. He previously served as General Counsel for a major life insurance company in Washington, D.C. He was a captain in the Army Corps of Engineers during W.W. II and took part in the DDay Invasion of Normandy. Thereafter he was reassigned to a select group of volunteers who were sent to the Far East for the planned invasion of Japan. He was a member of the Friendly Sons of St. Patrick and the New York State Bar Association. Mr. Moore was a recipient of the initial Gov. Malcolm Wilson Distinguished Alumnus Award in 2001 and was awarded the Fordham University Bene Merenti Award in 1990. He is survived by 5 children, Sheila Moore-McSpedon, William Moore Jr., Patricia Andersen, Michael Moore & Eileen Wooden. His brother George, and two sisters, Mary Carpenter and Regina Boyle, and eleven grandchildren also survive him.


        ALEXANDER-Fritz W., II. The NYU community grieves for the loss of its cherished friend, Fritz Alexander, an alumnus of our law school and a member of our Board of Trustees. A long-time member of the judiciary, the first African-American appointed to a full term on New York's highest court, a New York City Deputy Mayor and a teacher of the law, Judge Alexander epitomized our highest tradition of public service. We shall miss his wisdom and guidance, and we offer our deepest sympathies to his loved ones and many friends. L. Jay Oliva, President Martin Lipton, Chairman, Board of Trustees
        ALEXANDER-Fritz. The entire NYU Law School community mourns the death of our trustee, alumnus and friend, Fritz Alexander. A leader of the bench and bar, and a public servant of the first order, Fritz's life is a model of a career in law. Our thoughts and prayers are with his loving wife, Beverly, and his entire family. Lester Pollack, Chair, Board of Trustees John Sexton, Dean, New York University, School of Law
        ALEXANDER-Fritz W., II. The Justices of The New York State Supreme Court, Appellate Division, First Department, mark with sorrow the passing of their valued friend and former colleague. His spirit and scholarship will serve as his legacy to this court. Joseph P. Sullivan, Presiding Justice
        ALEXANDER-Fritz. Respected as a colleague and cherished as a friend. My sympathy and love to his beloved wife, Beverly. Sol Wachtler

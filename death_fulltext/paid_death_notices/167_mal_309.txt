
        ROTERT-Warren Herbert. Died after a long illness on December 19, 2000. Son of the late Herbert and Fannie (Wilson) Rotert, he grew up in Little Falls, NJ, and was a graduate from Passaic Valley High School (1947). He received his undergraduate degree in Civil Engineering from Lafayette College, Easton, PA (1952), his Juris Doctor from George Washington University Law School (1959), and served the United States Armed Forces from 1953-1955. Mr. Rotert was admitted to practice before the Virginia and New York Bars, the United States Supreme Court, the Federal Appellate and District Courts and was a skilled intellectual property trial lawyer. In addition to admission to the Order of the Coif and Phi Delta Phi, Warren was Patent Editor of the Law Review and a Patent Examiner before joining the New York City law firm of Morgan Finnegan in 1960. He was partner, senior partner, and served for 36 years before retiring in 1996. He was a member of the Ramsey Golf and Country Club, Ramsey, NJ, the Tuxedo Club in Tuxedo Park, New York, and Ford's Colony Country Club in Williamsburg, VA. Mr. Rotert leaves behind a family forever inspired by his show of strength, dignity and unending devotion: his loving wife Jean and their son Edward; daughter Lise Thayer and her husband Brian Thayer of Higganum, CT; son Kevin of Linwood, MA; son Todd and his wife Lauren of Ramsey; and brother David of Columbia, PA. His four grandchildren Alison, Jennifer, Ryan and Mary Beth will miss him. Warren was predeceased by his first wife, Mary Jane (Walton) Rotert in 1974.

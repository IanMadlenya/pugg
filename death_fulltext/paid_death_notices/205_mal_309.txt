
        GOLDBERG--Bernard. Age 79, passed away peacefully February 22. Beloved husband for 56 years to Carole. Devoted brother of Dotty Raines, and the late Gussie Camac. Dedicated father of Stewart (Gladys) and Avery (Leila) and adoring grandfather of Jonathan. Bernard was the nucleus and the very being of his family. A man of great intellect, cherished by friends and respected by his associates. Former President of Schenley Industries and Chairman of American Distilling Company, former President of the Hebrew Home for the Aged, former Chairman of Park East Synagogue and Honorary Chairman, founder of Park East Day Care and Minskoff Cultural Center. Services held Tuesday, February 24, 10 am, Park East Synagogue, 163 East 67th St.
        GOLDBERG--Bernard. Park East Synagogue mourns the death of Bernard Goldberg, honorary chairman of the Board of Trustees, a man of faith, who has been a pillar of strength to our congregation. His wise and generous leadership and friendship will surely be missed. To his beloved wife Carole, his children Avery and Leila, and Stewart and Gladys, his grandson Jonathan, his sister Dotty, our deepest sympathy. Funeral services Park East Synagogue, Main Sanctuary, 163 East 67th St, Tuesday, February 24, 2004, 10 am. Rabbi Arthur Schneier Michael Scharf, President Julius Gewirtz Chairman of the Board
        GOLDBERG--Bernard. Rabbi Arthur Schneier Park East Day School and Minskoff Cultural Center are saddened by the death of a guiding founder and benefactor. Committed to Jewish education, he has provided generous scholarship aid to many gifted students. Sincere condolences to the bereaved family.


        WILLIAMS-M. Dean. Died July 12, 1997, in Anchorage, AK. Born in Salt Lake City in 1913. He attended the University of Utah, graduating in 1941 with a Bachelor's Degree in geology and civil engineering. In 1947 he met June Maxwell in New York City where she was working as a registered nurse at New York Hospital. They were married in Lima, Peru, in December that year. Following college and during WWII, he served as a geologist for the U.S. Army Corps of Engineers in the Panama Canal where he became fluent in Spanish. After two years with the U.S. Geological Survey he began a 23 year career in oil exploration. Dean worked for various exploration subsidiaries of Exxon in Ecuador, Peru, Columbia, Venezuela and Pakistan. He was vice president of Exxon's exploration subsidiary in the Phili ppines at the time of his retirement. For his role in conducting the preliminary geological survey of the Upper Amazon Basin, he was listed in Who's Who of American Geologists. Surviving are his wife of 50 years, June Maxwell Williams, Yuma, AZ; daughter, Tamara, Palmer, AK; son and daughter-in-law, Benjamin and Paula, Draper, UT; and sister-in-law Miriam Maxell Kent of New York City. Burial took place at Mt. Olivet Cemetery, July 17, Salt Lake City.

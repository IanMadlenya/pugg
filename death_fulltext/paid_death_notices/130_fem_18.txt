
        SCHARMAN-Frida. Died peacefully Saturday, October 25 at her home after a long illness. Born in 1908, she was the daughter of August Charles Scharman and Lillie Rueger Scharman of Brooklyn. Miss Scharman was a lifetime resident of New York City and was a talented squash racquets player. She represented the United States against Great Britain in 1931. She also won innumerable tennis titles in doubles at The River Club in New York City, Piping Rock Club, Locust Valley, and The Heights Casino in Brooklyn. She was a graduate of Smith College, Northampton, MA., and of the American Academy of Dramatic Arts, New York. She joined the American Red Cross in 1942 and served in London during the Blitz. After the war, she was a volunteer at the United Nations. She leaves a niece, Ann Hester Huntoon, Charleston, SC, two grand-nieces, Susan Payson, Tucson, AZ, and Jill Kennedy, San Diego, CA., and a cousin, Mrs. John MacKay, Locust Valley. Memorial contributions may be made to the American Red Cross, POB 37243, Washington, D.C. 20013, marked for the Frida Scharman Memorial Fund.

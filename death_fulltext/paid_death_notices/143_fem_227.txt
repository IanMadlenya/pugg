
        TOMSON-Rose, of Bloomingburg, NY. A self employed playwright in NYC and formerly of Great Neck, NY, died at her home December 22, 1998. She was 86. The daughter of the late Israel and Celia Lynnchitz, she was born on April 13, 1912 in New York City. She was the widow of New York State Supreme Court Justice Bernard Tomson. Survivors include a son Louis Tomson of Albany; two daughters, Susan and Elizabeth Tomson both of Bloomingburg, New York. A sister Claire Barrens of San Diego, CA.; five grandchildren, Daniel Tomson, Anders Tomson, Sara Cordin, Alison Coleman, and Alexandra Tomson; one great granddaughter, Anika Tomson. She was predeceased by a son Steven Tomson and predeceased by two brothers, Jack Lynn and Sam Lynnchitz. Graveside service will be held on Thursday, December 24, 1998 at 11:30 A.M. at Beth Moses Cemetery in Pinelawn, New York.
        TOMSON-Rose. Temple Beth-El of Great Neck records with profound sorrow the passing of its long-time member. We extend heartfelt sympathy to her bereaved family. Shelley M. Limmer, President Jerome K. Davidson, Rabbi

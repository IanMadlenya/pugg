
        CLARK-Irwin, pioneer in the study of calcium and phosphoros metabolism in bone growth and regeneration, died on November 6 at his home in Plainfield, NJ after a lengthy illness. He was born on April 28, 1918, received his Bachelor's degree from Harvard University, and was awarded a Doctorate in Biochemistry from Columbia University. After working at Merck for many years he became a professor at the College of Physicians and Surgeons, Columbia Medical School. In 1971 he relocated to the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill for four years, after which he returned to the metropolitan area to conduct research at Rutgers University until retirement at the age of 71. He is survived by four children and nine grandchildren. Donations may be made to the Center for Hope, 176 Hussa Street, Linden, NJ 07036.

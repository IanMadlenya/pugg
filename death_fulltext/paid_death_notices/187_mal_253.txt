
        ROSENBERG - Dr. Louis. Longtime resident of Hastings-on-Hudson, NY passed away peacefully on the morning of July 5, 2002 at the age of 83 after a long battle with Parkinson's disease. Born in Harlem, educated at City College, NYU, and the William Alanson White Institute, Lou was in private practice in Manhattan as a psychotherapist for almost 40 years as well as a teacher at both NYU and Fordham. Husband to Miriam (Mitzi) Silver for over 50 years, he is survived by their three children, Steve Rosenberg of Oakland, CA., Eve Martin of Clinton, NY, and Carrie Crockett of Peapack-Gladstone, NJ, and five grandchildren, Louise, Laura, Jesse, Clarissa, and Lucas. He was an inspiration for his unwavering curiosity about all things, strength in adversity, and sense of wonder and acceptance. He relished the outdoors, his close friends, and a good meal. Hiker, tennis player, singer, gardener, devotee of mushrooms and Shake speare, as well as teacher of many aspiring psychotherapists. Gifts in Lou's memory may be sent to the: Senior Director of Individual Giving, American Museum of Natural History, Central Park West at 79th Street, New York, New York 10024-5192.

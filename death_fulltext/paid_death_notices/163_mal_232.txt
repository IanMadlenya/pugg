
        WILSON-C. Philip, M.D. Of Manhattan and Old Lyme, CT, on August 17, 2000 at age 79 of cardiac causes. Pioneer in the psychoanalysis of eating disorders, he is survived by his wife of 49 years, Christine (nee Bagaloff) Wilson, children, Cynthia, Marc and Scott, daughter-in-law, Anne Allan Wilson, grandson, Eric, and brothers, Donald and Stephen. A memorial service will be held at Frank E. Campbell, 1076 Madison Ave. at 81 St., NY NY on September 9th, 2000 at 11:30 AM. Contributions in his memory may be made to the Endowment for the Advancement of Psychotherapy, M.G.H. Development Office, 100 Charles River Plaza, Suite 600, Boston, MA 02114.
        WILSON-C. Philip, M.D. We mourn the loss of of our esteemed and dear friend and colleague, C. Philip Wilson. Dr. Wilson pioneered in the treatment of patients with severe psychosomatic disorders and wrote extensively about anorexia and bulimia. He was always eager to teach and to help, and was cheerful and optimistic in the face of adversity. Phil will always be remembered and respected as a clinician and researcher, but he will be mourned as a dear friend and companion. The NJ Psychoanalytic Society

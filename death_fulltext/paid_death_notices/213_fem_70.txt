
        HUDIS--Dr. Ann. Of Monroe Township, NJ, passed away on October 9, 2004, after a long illness. Beloved daughter of Wolf and Sara Wax, she was born at Hadassah hospital, Tel Aviv, Israel, on May 1, 1922. She immigrated to the United States at age three and lived in Ohio and Michigan. She served in The American Red Cross during World War II before marrying and settling in New York City in 1946. Returning to college after raising her family, Dr. Hudis received a B.A. from Sarah Lawrence College, and an M.P.H., M.Ed., and Ed.D. in health education and gerontology from Columbia University. She taught at Hunter College and retired as Professor from William Patterson University. During a 40 year academic career she was elected Fellow of the Gerontological Society of America and Sigma Phi Omega, served as President of the State Association of Gerontological Educators (NY), and was a charter member of the Society on Aging of New Jersey. In 1992 she was honored by Columbia University with the prestigious Columbia University Federation Medal, the highest honor for service by alumni to the university. She was a life member of Hadassah and on the Board of UJA, Middlesex County. Married to her beloved husband Morris Mac Hudis, DDS for 53 years, together they embodied the 20th century American dream. Family, education, travel and community service were their consuming passions. She is survived by her loving children, Penni, Iris, and Stephen, son-in-law Ira Fink, daughter-in-law Merrye Shavel, and adored grandchildren Loel and Suzanne. She will be sorely missed by family, friends, and all of those whose lives she touched. She was a true friend to all who knew her. Services and interment will be at 1:30 p.m. on Monday at Sharon Gardens, Valhalla, NY. For information contact Orland's Ewing Memorial Chapel, Trenton, NJ 609883-1400.

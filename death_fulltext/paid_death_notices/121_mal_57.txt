
        SEXAUER-James Monroe, of West Palm Beach, Florida, died on Friday, January 24, 1997, at his home, following an extended illness. Mr. Sexauer had been a resident of the Palm Beach area since 1991, coming here from Harrison, NY. Born July 21, 1896 in Belvidere, IL, he was one of seven children born to John and Lena Bleiler Sexauer. On July 15, 1935 he married Dorothy Bohan at the Church of the Transfiguration in NYC. Mr. Sexauer was a graduate of the University of Illinois and a member of Sigma Chi Fraternity. He joined the J.A. Sexauer Manufacturing Company in 1923, which was founded by his brother John A. Sexauer in 1921. He succeeded his brother as President and Chairman of the Board of Directors in 1971. In 1976 he sold the Company to the Dyson-Kissner Corporation. At the time of his death, Mr. Sexauer was President and Chairman of the Board of Trustees of the Sexauer Foundation. He was a member of St. John Episcopal Church in Larchmont, NY and was a member of the Winged Foot Golf Club in Mamaroneck, New York from 1929 until 1992. Survivors include his wife, Dorothy B. Sexauer of West Palm Beach, FL; a daughter, Nancy Sexauer Walsh of Palm Beach, FL and his grandson, James R. Walsh of NYC. A Memorial Service will be held at 2:00 PM on Thursday, February 6, 1997 at St. John Episcopal Church in Larchmont, New York, with the Reverend Thomas Nichols, Rector, officiating. Inurnment will follow in the Columbarium at St. John's Church. In lieu of flowers the family requests contributions to the Sexauer Foundation, c/o Mr. Wesley G. Cawley, Palm Beach National Bank & Trust Company, 125 Worth Avenue, Palm Beach, FL 33480. For information, Quattlebaum-Holleman-Burse Funeral Home, West Palm Beach, FL.


        SIPSER--Morris, December 26th, 2004 at age 84. Born in NYC November 9th, 1920, Mr. Sipser served in the U.S. Air Force during W.W. II. He reenlisted and served as an army intelligence officer in post-war Germany and was commissioned to apprehend prominent German scientists including Werner von Braun. Mr. Sipser graduated from Brooklyn College and married Rose Silverstone. He was a founding professional, City Manager and Executive Vice President of the worldwide Israel Bonds Organization and was instrumental in its development and transformation. He served as senior development consultant for the Holocaust Memorial Museum. Mr. Sipser had a lifelong commitment to civil rights, peace and human rights. In 2000, his wife Rose, a writer and book reviewer, died. He is survived by loving family.

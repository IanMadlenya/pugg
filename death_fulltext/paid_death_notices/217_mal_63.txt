
        HAVERSTICK--John Mitchell, 85, of Summit, NJ, formerly of NYC, died on Saturday, January 29, 2005 at Overlook Hospital in Summit. Mr. Haverstick was born in Carlisle, PA and lived most of his life in NYC. He moved to Summit in 2002. A graduate of Amherst College, Mr. Haverstick retired as the Editorial Secretary of the United Church of Christ based in Philadelphia. Previously he had been an executive editor at various publishing houses, including Holt, Rinehart & Winston, and religion editor for LIFE Magazine. A direct descendant of Colonel Michael Haverstick, head of the Pennsylvania Quartermaster Corps for Washington's Army, and Lieutenant William Haverstick, the noted Philadelphia silversmith, who was decorated for bravery at the Battle of Princeton, Mr. Haverstick served in World War II as a correspondent for YANK Magazine. Mr. Haverstick's interest in American religion led him to write numerous articles for the Saturday Review, The New York Times Book Review and LIFE Magazine. His pinnacle achievement was the publishing of his book, The Progress of the Protestant, in 1968. The book is still used in college curriculums. His marriage to the former Iola Wise Stetson, now deceased, ended in divorce. He is survived by his son and daughter-inlaw, Alec and Jessica Haverstick, of Short Hills, NJ and four grandchildren: Emily, Sam, Woodney and Genevieve.

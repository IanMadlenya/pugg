
        HOFF-Knut. Born December 28th, 1912 in Tonsberg, Norway. Died April 30th, 2000 in NYC. Joined Norwegian Merchant Marine in 1928. Spent 1932-34 in Antarctic on Anglo-Norwegian whaling expeditions, wintering on the island of South Georgia. Received degree in engineering from Ilmanau in Thuringen, 1937. Joined in Resistance at Narvik, Norway in June 1940. After internment by Nazis, walked to Sweden in 1941, then traveled through Russia, the Middle East and India to join the Royal Norwegian Air Force headquartered in Toronto, Canada. Sent to Britain in 1942 and stationed in London. Transferred to U.S. in 1943 for further training at Harvard and MIT. Met and married Maud Sanderson in Boston in 1943. Retired from Air Force in 1952 and settled in Los Angeles where he joined Lockheed Aircraft Corp. in the same year. From 1959 to 1977 worked in the Lockheed ''SkunkWorks'' on projects including the YF12A, SR71 (''Blackbird''), U2 and Orion P3. Vestry member and longtime Sr. Warden of St. John's Episcopal Church, Los Angeles. Active member, with his wife Maud, of St. James Episcopal Church in NYC since 1986. Survived by his son, Knut, Jr., two grandchildren, and nieces and nephews in the U.S. and Norway. Memorial service Monday, 1 PM, at St. James Episcopal Church, Madison and 71st St.

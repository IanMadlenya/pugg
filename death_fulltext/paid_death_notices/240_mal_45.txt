
        HAUPTFLEISCH--Louis A. Born and raised in Waterloo, Ill. July 17th 1918, passed away December 14th, 2006, of complications from Alzheimer's. His parents were Herman E. and Amanda (nee Koenigsmark) Hauptfleisch. Mr. Hauptfleisch retired in 1983 after 10 years as first VP of Municipal Finance at Smith Barney, NYC. Prior he had been employed by Halsey-Stuart and Goldman Sachs, NYC as VP of Municipal Finance. He received a Bachelor's degree in Finance from the University of Illinois in 1940. He was President of The Municipal Bond Club of New York in 1976, and a member of Summit Old Guard and duplicate Bridge Club, a former member of the University Club, NYC and Morris County Golf Club, Convent Station. A WWII Army veteran, he served as captain with the 82nd Airborne, 504 Regiment as a paratrooper from 1942 to 1945. He was in the Battle of the Bulge and received the Purple Heart, Bronze Star, and six Battle Stars. Mr. Hauptfleisch was predeceased by his first wife, Margaret, and his sister, Esther Coldwell. Surviving are his wife of 32 years, Pamela (nee Wilson), his son, David Hauptfleisch, of Ben Lomond, CA, his stepdaughters, Melinda Shumway of Concord, MA, Lauren Mitchell of Jaffrey, NH, Gayle Winn of Summit, NJ and Hillary Campbell of Stirling, NJ; a brother, Dick Hauptfleish of Wildomar, CA, and eight grandchildren.


        JAMES--Theodore Jr., died on October 7th, 2006 at a nursing home in Greenport, Long Island, near his house in Peconic where he had lived for many years with his friend and colleague, Harry Haralambou. Death resulted from a severe stroke he suffered in 2002. A 1957 graduate of Princeton University Mr. James' career began in advertising, followed by a regular column in ''W'' Magazine. He was a prolific writer on a variety of subjects ranging from social history to gardening, much of which was published by Harry N. Abrams, Inc. He contributed numerous travel articles for the NY Times and European Travel and Life Magazine. Mr. James had a colorful life. His career took him all over the world. He loved people, parties and telling stories. For his special interest in Belgium he was made a ''Chevalier de l'Ordre de la Coronne'' by the Belgian government. He was a major figure on the North Fork of Long Island, active in community affairs. Services will be private. Contributions may be made to the Princeton University Class of 1957 Classmates Fund, Princeton, NJ 08544.

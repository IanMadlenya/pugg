
        ROBIN--Franklin Ellis, who served in the OSS in World War II, died in Ridgewood, NJ, on Tuesday, November 19, 2003. During his 30 year career at Dun and Bradstreet, Mr. Robin maintained homes in Manhattan and Brick Town, NJ, until recently. He was 88. Born in Rutherford, NJ, on September 8, 1915, as a young man he played a leading role in a literary circle in that town that included William Carlos Williams, Kathleen Hoagland, and other writers and artists. He graduated from Dartmouth College Magna Cum Laude and Phi Beta Kappa in 1937. His longtime companion Howard Ross predeceased him. He is survived by his nieces Nancy Robin Jaicks of Ridgewood, NJ, and Diana Robin of Chicago, IL, four grandnieces and nephews, and five greatgrandnieces and nephews. Services will be held Monday, 12 noon, at Christ Episcopal Church in Ridgewood. In lieu of flowers, donations may be made to the New Jersey Audubon Society of the American Red Cross.

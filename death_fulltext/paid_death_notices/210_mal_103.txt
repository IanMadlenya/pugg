
        SHELTON--Marvin L., M.D. On July 7, 2004, at home of multiple myeloma. He was born in Pittsburgh, PA, July 25, 1931, and raised in Wilmington, NC. He practiced orthopedic surgery at the New York Presbyterian Hospital and served as the director of Orthopedics at Harlem Hospital Center. He is survived by his wife of 48 years, Arden; his daughters Yvonne, LeAnn and Marla and his son Marvin; three grandchildren; two brothers, Dr. Lee Shelton and Dr. Thomas Shelton, and numerous nieces, nephews and cousins. Funeral service, Wednesday, July 14, at 5 pm, at Christ Church, Riverdale, NY. In lieu of flowers, donations may be sent to the Orthopedic Department of New York Presbyterian Hospital for the Marvin L. Shelton Lectureship.
        SHELTON--Marvin L., M.D. New York-Presbyterian Hospital and Columbia University Medical Center mourn the loss of our dear friend and colleague Dr. Marvin Shelton. A leading orthopedic surgeon who pioneered advanced operative techniques and implants for the treatment of ankle fractures, Dr. Shelton was Attending Surgeon at New York-Presbyterian Hospital/Columbia University Medical Center for four decades. He was also Professor Emeritus and Special Lecturer at Columbia University College of Physicians & Surgeons, and served as Director of Orthopedic Surgery and Director of the Residency Training Program at Harlem Hospital Center, where he formalized the rotation of New York-Presbyterian/Columbia residents. Our deepest condolences are extended to the entire Shelton family. He will be much missed. The family requests that, in lieu of flowers, donations be sent to the Department of Orthopedic Surgery at NewYork-Presbyterian Hospital to support the Marvin L. Shelton Lecture Series in Orthopedic Trauma. Dr. Herbert Pardes President and CEO New York-Presbyterian Hospital Dr. Gerald D.

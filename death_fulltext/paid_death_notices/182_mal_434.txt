
        STEINER-Lee N. Died at his home in Scarsdale, NY, on March 28, in his 80th year. He leaves his wife Rosalind and three daughters, Terry and her husband Roger Smith, Catherine and her husband Fred Adair and Nancy and her husband Robert Fraiman Jr. He also leaves four grandchildren, Daniel and Lily Adair and Kate and Anne Fraiman. During WWII, he served in the Navy as the skipper of an LST in the Pacific theater and was cited by Admiral Halsey for valor in action. A partner in the law firm of Loeb and Loeb, he was one of the early and preeminent practitioners in the field of entertainment law. A past trustee of Carnegie Hall, he was also secretary and trustee of the Cousteau Society, secretary of the Counterpart Foundation and a former trustee of Lafayette College. He was a past president of Sunningdale Country Club. A memorial service will be held at Frank E. Campbell, 1076 Madison Avenue, at 81st St, on Wednesday, April 3, at 4 PM. In lieu of flowers, contributions may be made to the Parkinson's Disease Foundation, 640 West 168th Street, NYC 10032.

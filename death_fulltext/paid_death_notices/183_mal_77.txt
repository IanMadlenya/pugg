
        MACKEY-Leonard B. A resident of Rye, NY, Greenwich, CT, and Sarasota, FL, died on April 2, 2002, in Sarasota. He was 76 years old. Mr. Mackey had a long career as an intellectual property lawyer. Initially employed by the General Electric Corporation in Schenectady, he retired as Vice President and General Patent Counsel of ITT Corporation. He was active with the intellectual property bar, serving at various times as President of the American Intellectual Property Law Association, as well as the national and international units of the Licensing Executives Society, Vice President of the AIPPI (International Association for the Protection of Industrial Property) and President of the National Investors Hall of Fame Foundation Board. For years he was involved in Rye civic activities, serving on the Planning Commission and City Council. Mr. Mackey was born in Washington, DC, on August 31, 1925. He graduated from Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute with an electrical engineering degree and from George Washington University Law School. A lifelong avid sailor, Mr. Mackey was a member of the American Yacht Club and New York Yacht Club. He served as a Secretary of the American Yacht Club and was a member of its Race Committee for 31 years. Mr. Mackey is survived by his wife, Britta; his son, Leonard, of Riverside, Connecticut; his daughters, Cathleen Surface of Richmond, Virginia, and Wendy Arendt, of Mountain View, California; step-sons, Eckart Walther and Harald Walther, of Palo Alto, California, and eight grandchildren. A memorial service will be held at Rye Presbyterian Church, Rye, NY, on Saturday, April 13, at 10 AM. In lieu of flowers, friends may send donations to the United Hospital Medical Center, Port Chester, NY 10573.

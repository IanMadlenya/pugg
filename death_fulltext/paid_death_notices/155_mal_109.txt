
        SAFRA-Edmond J. Manhattan High School, Machon Yaakov, mourns the tragic passing of Edmond Safra, who perpetuated the memory of his father, Jacob Safra, z.l., by naming the school in his memory. He was an extraordinary philanthropist whose generosity, leadership and vision influenced the lives of Jewish people and causes throughout the world. We offer our heartfelt condolences to his wife and family. May they be comforted among the mourners of Zion and Jerusalem. Rebbetzin Ruthy Assaf, Principal Rabbi Chaskel Besser, Malcolm Hoenlein, George Klein, Directors.
        SAFRA-Edmond. Just One Life mourns the tragic passing of Edmond Safra, from whom we have all benefitted. He was unwavering in his support of Jewish children. He will be greatly missed. We extend our deepest condolences to the entire family; may they be comforted amongst the mourners of Zion. Joel Gold, President Jack Forgash, Chairman Hillel Weinberger, Chairman Steven Adelsberg, V.P. Rabbi Martin Katz, Executive V.P.
        SAFRA-Edmond J. The Officers and Board of the American Associates, Ben-Gurion University of the Negev, mourn the tragic passing of our firend and supporter, Edmond Safra. Mr. Safra was the founder of ISEF, a charitable foundation that generously supports many BGU programs. We extend our deepest sympathies to his wife Lily, and the entire Safra family. Jules I. Whitman, President Bernard C. Moscovitz, Exec V.P.

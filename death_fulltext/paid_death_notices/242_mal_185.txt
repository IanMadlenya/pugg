
        GILE--David Emery, died peacefully at home in Greenwich, CT on Thursday, March 15, 2007. Mr. Gile was born in Cambridge, MA on June 7, 1922 and was the youngest child of Clement and Anne Gile. He graduated from Phillips Andover Academy in 1940 and Yale University in 1944. At Yale he sang in the Whiffenpoofs and was a member of The Elihu Club. Mr. Gile was a Lt. J.G. during WWII in the Pacific and served as Commanding Officer of LSM 500. After the War, he graduated from the University of Pittsburgh Law School in 1949, earned an LL. B. and was elected class president. He married Patricia Schoen in 1949 and three children followed: Nancy (1950), David Jr. (1952) and Larry (1955). Mr. Gile was a banker, first as an assistant trust officer with the Union National Bank of Pittsburgh then as a commercial lending officer with Pittsburgh National Bank and then Marine Midland Bank. Following retirement from banking, he served as Vice President for the Council for Aid to Education. Mr. Gile felt strongly about serving his community and church and was active in many organizations. Among his noteworthy activities and accomplishments: awarded the Yale Medal, President of the Rye Presbyterian Church Board of Trustees, recipient of the Whiffenpoof Cup, honorary LL.D. from the Berkeley Divinity School at Yale, member of the Yale Club Council, Secretary of the Rye Rotary Club, member of the Rye, NY City Council (he was a lifelong Republican) and volunteer at The Osborn Home and United Hospital. He is survived by his wife of 57 years, Patricia Gile and sons David Jr. and Larry. A memorial service will be held at Rye Presbyterian Church on Saturday March 24, 2007 at 11:00 am with a private reception to follow. In lieu of flowers, contributions can be made to the Rye Presbyterian Church Mission Fund.

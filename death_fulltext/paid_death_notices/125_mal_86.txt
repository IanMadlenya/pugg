
        DURAND-Celestin A. III. Passed away peacefully June 3, 1997 at home with his usual faith, courage, tenacity and sense of humor. Celestin touched the lives of everyone he met. He was greatly loved and admired and his great joy and love of life will be sorely missed. A member of the New York Stock Exchange since 1963. He founded and managed Durand & Cullen a specialist firm in 1966. In 1972 they merged with Beachamp & Co. In 1989 while managing partner, the firm was sold to Merrill Lynch. He remained with Merrill Lynch as Vice President of their newly formed Specialist Division for three years until his retirement in 1992. A lifetime resident of Garden City, NY, he retired to Miami, Florida. There, he enjoyed the things in life which gave him his greatest pleasures, his dog Clancy, golf, travel and horses. He bred and raced Thoroughbred horses as a partner in Royal Lines Stable. He was a member of the Thoroughbred Owners and Breeders Assoc and the California Thoroughbred Owners and Breeders Assoc. Since 1993 he served on the Board of Directors of World Fuel Services, a listed Company based in Miami since 1994. He also served on the Board of Directors of The Jockey Club Condominium Assoc, Phase I. He was the beloved husband and best friend of Lin, the loving father of C.A., Michael and Christopher, doting grandfather of eight, the big brother of John, DeeDee and Joanne. Visiting at the Fairchild Chapel, Franklin Ave at 12th Street, Garden City, 3-5 and 7-9 P.M. Monday. Interment Tuesday, Holy Rood Cemetery, Westbury, NY. Family suggests donations to St. Rose of Lima Parish, 415 E. 105th St, Miami Shores, FL 33138 or St. Jude's Children's Research, P.O. Box 50, Memphis, TN 38101. His generosity of spirit and willingness to be kind to everyone, will be remembered by all who knew him. ''Time flies, suns rise and shadows fall let it pass by love regins forever overall.''


        WILSON-Vivian Deborah. A retired law professor, died suddenly and unexpectedly at her home in Mill Valley, CA. on June 13, 2001. Known as ''Debi'' to many of her friends, she taught law at Hastings College of Law from 1975 until her retirement in 1993. She was born in New York City on September 5, 1922, was a graduate of Hunter College and a member of Phi Beta Kappa. She earned a Master's degree in Journalism from Columbia University. After graduating from Hastings College of the Law in 1969, she served as a Public Defender until accepting a teaching position at Hastings in 1975 as a Law Professor. She also served as Dean of Students. She was a published author, writing numerous magazine articles and ''Watch Out for the Weather,'' a book about the effects of weather on human behavior. Recently, she had been a peer counselor. She is survived by her sister, Suzanne Barnett of New York, her children Andrew Wilson and Claudia Wilson and four grandchildren who reside in California. She was 78.

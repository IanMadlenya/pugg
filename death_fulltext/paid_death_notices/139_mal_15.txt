
        REBELL-Harry. The Officers, Board of Directors and staff of UJA-Federation of New York mourn the passing of Harry Rebell, a devoted philanthropist who showed a deep concern for the Jewish community and humanitarian causes. He was greatly admired for his generous spirit and wisdom, while serving as a member of our Accountants Division, an assembly member of our Overseas Affairs Division, and a board member of the 92nd Street YM-YWHA, a UJA-Federation beneficiary. We extend our sincerest condolences to his son Michael, and his wife, Sharon; his son Arthur, and his wife, Adele; his daughter Phyllis Osterman, and her husband, Thomas; and his grandchildren, Joshua, Barbara, Daniel, Laura, Deborah, Peter, Jonathan, Rachel and Sarah. He was a cherished friend, and inspiring leader who will be greatly missed. Judith Stern Peck, Board Chair James S. Tisch, President Stephen D. Solender, Exec. V.P.
        REBELL-Harry. The Board of Directors and Staff of the 92nd Street Y mourn the passing of Harry Rebell, a dedicated and long-time member of our Board and our dear friend. His years of commitment to the 92nd Street Y are deeply appreciated. Our hearts and prayers go out to Harry's sons and daughters-in-law, Arthur and Adele Rebell, Michael and Sharon Rebell, as well as Harry's daughter and son-in-law Phyllis and Thomas Osterman. Our hearts and prayers also go out to Harry's nine grandchildren. He will be missed by all who knew him. Philip Milstein, President Joseph Leff, Chairman Sol Adler, Executive Director
        REBELL-Harry. We mourn the loss of our dear friend. He lived life to the fullest and served as a mentor to all who had the privilege of knowing him. To his family we extend our heartfelt sympathy. We will always remember and miss his adventurous spirit for living. Gloria and Paul Asnes


        BANKS-J. Eugene. A limited partner of Brown Brothers Harriman & Co., died on December 16, 1999 at Hightstown, New Jersey. He was 91 years old. He maintained homes in St. James, New York and Hightstown, New Jersey. Mr. Banks had been with Brown Brothers Harriman & Co. from 1942 until his death. Shortly after he joined the firm, Mr. Banks went into service in the U.S. Coast Guard. At the time of his discharge in 1945, Mr. Banks was a Lieutenant Commander. Upon his return to Brown Brothers Harriman & Co., Mr. Banks worked as an economist until he was named head of its institutional investment department in 1950. He was appointed a manager of the firm in 1957, and became a general partner in 1962. A leading authority on investments, Mr. Banks was the author of the ''Guide to Stock Market Policy'' in 1949, ''Institutional Investment Guides'' in 1955, and ''Guides to Growth Stock Investing'' in 1959. Mr. Banks was born in Kirksville, Missouri on January 26, 1908. After his graduation from Washington University in 1930, Mr. Banks joined the Boatman's National Bank in St. Louis. From 1932 to 1938, he managed the funds of a St. Louis family. He worked as an analyst with Fenner & Beane, one of the forerunners of Merrill Lynch, from 1938 until he joined Brown Brothers in 1942. Long active in civic affairs, Mr. Banks served for many years as a warden of Trinity Church, as a trustee of the Trinity Church Association, and director of St. Margaret's House. He was also a trustee of Teachers College at Columbia University, a member of the American Economic Association, the Military Order of the World Wars, Theta Ki, the Church Club, the University Club and the Down Town Association. Mr. Banks is survived by his wife, the former Barbara Hall, his stepdaughter, Mrs. Gardner Mundy, and two stepgrandchildren, Philip Mundy and Catherine McKee. Services will be held at Trinity Church, Broadway and Wall Street, on Saturday, December 18 at 11:00 A.M. In lieu of flowers, donations to St. James Episcopal Church, 490 No. Country Road, St. James, N.Y. 11780, or Teachers College at Columbia University, 525 W. 120th Street, New York, New York 10027, would be appreciated.

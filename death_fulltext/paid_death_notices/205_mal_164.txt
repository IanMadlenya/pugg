
        ONYAN--Blaine Wellington, of Fort Lee, died on February 11, 2004, at the age of 92. Blaine, beloved husband of Edith Hufman Onyan, liked to call himself the ''an upstate Apple Knocker.'' He was born in Clinton, NY and raised in Oriskany Falls. He was especially proud of his service in the US Army during World War II, a draftee rising to Captain over his 52 months. For many years he was a sales engineer with the Cyclone Fence Division of US Steel. After retiring he was associated with Royal Guard Fence Company in Westbury and acted as consultant on fencing for New York City Parks Department. Besides his wife, he leaves nieces Susan Skogerson, Patricia Reske; nephews Kenneth and Howard Gould. Visiting will be Friday 3:00-5:00 and 7:009:00 p.m. at the Frank A. Patti and Kenneth Mikatarian Funeral Home, 327 Main Street ''opposite the Fort Lee Library.'' Religious service Saturday at 10:00 a.m. Burial in Pinelawn Cemetery, Farmingdale, NY at 12:30 p.m. In lieu of flowers, contributions may be made to the Children's Aid Society of New York or the charity of your choice.


        LEW--John Stephen, 72, of Ossining, NY, died September 14 after a long illness. He was born in New York on September 9, 1934, the son of Ann Light Lew and Edward A. Lew. He attended Mamaroneck High School and then Yale University, where he graduated in 1955. He earned a Ph.D. in Physics at Princeton University in 1960 and served in the U.S. Air Force in Albuquerque, NM, from 1960 to 1962. He taught applied mathematics at MIT and Brown University before taking a position in the Department of Applied Mathematics at the IBM Thomas J.Watson Research Center in Yorktown Heights, where he worked for twenty - four years. His avocations included literature and music. A long-time member of Mensa of Greater New York, he served for a time as the poetry editor of the Mensa Bulletin. For many years he wrote program notes for, and served on the board of Friends of Music of Ossining, NY. He is survived by his brother and sister-in-law Robert A. Lew and Elizabeth Wright and their children Carolina and Edward, of Lexington, MA; his daughter and son-in-law Jennifer Beulah Lew and Paul Nathan Foster and their sons Abram and Michael, of Baltimore, MD; and his son and son-in-law Nathaniel Geoffrey Lew and Jason Paymar Lorber and their son Maxwell, of Burlington, VT. A funeral will be held at 10 a.m. on Monday, September 18, at the John J. Fox Funeral Home, 2080 Boston Post Rd, Larchmont. Memorial donations in his name may be made to Friends of Music, 251 Croton Dam Road, Ossining, NY 10562.


        WASHTON--Nathan, 86, of Fairfield, CT and Manhasset, NY, on September 30, 2003 of lung cancer. Nathan retired as professor of education and coordinator of science education at Queens College, CUNY, in 1982. His publications include more than 30 scholarly articles, several textbooks, and a number of edited science series. During WWII, Nathan served his country as a captain in the 20th Army Air Force, piloting B-29s based in India and training pilots in San Antonio, TX. Nathan is survived by his loving wife of 58 years, Sylvia, his three daughters, Gale Dubrow, Ruth Washton-Brown and Laura Orr and their spouses, his five grandchildren and four great - grandchildren. Funeral services will be held at 10:30 AM on Thursday, October 2nd at Congregation B' nai Israel, 2710 Park Avenue, Bridgeport, CT. Interment will be at 1:00 PM at the family plot in Mt. Ararat Cemetery, Farmingdale, New York.

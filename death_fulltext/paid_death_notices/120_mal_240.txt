
        McVITY-Leonard Howard, died early Friday morning, January 17, 1997 at St. Vincent's Hospital in New York City. Born in Toronto, Canada on September 20, 1906, he graduated from the University of Toronto in June 1928. While still a student, he began his actuarial career as an Actuarial Clerk with Manufacturer's Life of Canada in June 1925. He remained there until January 1936 when he became Secretary and Actuary for the Canadian Life Insurance Association in Toronto. In April 1938, Mr. McVity joined the Business Men's Assurance (BMA) Company in Kansas City, Missouri and remained there until March 1943. He left BMA to become an Associate Actuary with The Equitable Life Assurance Society of the United States in New York City. In February 1964, he was appointed to the position of Vice President and Comptroller which he held until December 1969 when he left to become Vice President and Secretary of Equitable Life Mortgage and Realty Investors. In September 1972, after almost 30 years as an actuary, Mr. McVity retired from The Equitable. Subsequently, he was associated with Coopers & Lybrand and Tillinghast as an Actuarial Consultant. In September 1979, Mr. McVity joined the New York State Department of Insurance as a Supervising Actuary, a position he held until shortly before his death. In 1993, Mr. McVity celebrated a milestone-the 60th anniversary of attaining his Fellowship in the Actuarial Society of America, now called the Society of Actuaries. In addition to his wife, Frances, he is survived by a son, Richard L. McVity of Newton, MA, a daughter, Anne E. Miles of Chapel Hill, NC, ten grandchildren and 9 great-grandchildren. A Memorial Service is planned for Friday, January 31, 1997 at the Church of the Ascenscion, 12 West 11th Street, in New York City. In lieu of flowers, contributions may be made to a charity of choice.

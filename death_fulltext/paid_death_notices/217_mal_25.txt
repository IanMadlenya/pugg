
        BARNWELL -- William J., known to all as ''Jim,'' died on February 1 after a brief illness. He was 86 and had lived in Rye for the past 22 years. Jim retired in 1989 as a NY State Parole Board Commissioner. He graduated from Fordham University with a BA in 1940, later earning a Master's in Social Work. As an undergraduate he was the sports editor for the Fordham newspaper and later became a ''stringer'' for the New York Times, covering subjects from religious services to college sports. He served in the US Army during WWII. Jim was a kind, gentle man with the great Irish gift of wit. He enjoyed tennis into his early 80's. Mr. Barnwell was married to Elizabeth Griffin in 1953. She predeceased him as did his daughter Carolyn. He is survived by his children Elizabeth B. Boucher of Rye, NY and Nicholas J. Barnwell of Harrison, NY, as well as four grandchildren. Family will receive friends at the Graham Funeral Home in Rye from 24 and 7-9 on Friday February 4th. Funeral mass will be held at the Church of the Resurrection, Rye, NY at 10AM on Saturday, February 5th.


        ELLIOTT--Dolores Dengel, age 70, of Copake, NY, died peacefully at home June 12, 2004. Born in New York City, the daughter of Harry J. and Althea (Meeker) Dengel, Mrs. Elliott was a resident of White Plains, NY from 1937 to 1993. She received a B.S. in business administration from Good Counsel College in 1956 and was a medical transcriptionist in the Pathology Department of St. Agnes Hospital in White Plains from 1970 to 1986. Dolly owned and showed Pekingese and many other breeds of dogs, some of which were also used in advertising with various celebrities including Johnny Carson and Richard Avedon. Mrs. Elliott is survived by her husband of 48 years, Roger Palmer Elliott, and her daughter, Dr. Holly Elizabeth Elliott of Kerhonkson, NY. Her son, Roger Palmer Elliott, Jr., died in 1981. Arrangements are private. In lieu of flowers, friends may send memorial donations to Columbia Greene Humane Society, 125 Humane Society Rd., Hudson, New York 12534.


        YOUNG-Benjamin. On April 6, 1998. Lawyer and community leader of Riverdale, NY, died at age 94. The oldest practicing attorney of New York Law School (Class of '24), he served his profession and community until age 92, was Counsel for the National and New York Associations for Meat and Poultry Purveyors and for the Marketmen's Association of the Port of New York. He served his fellow men as a Government Appeal Agent for the Selective Service Board during WWII, President of the Bronx High School of Science Parents' Association, assisted by his devoted wife of 48 years, Bess, and as Trustee and Vice President of Riverdale Temple. His pride in his children, Ellen Rosenberg of Riverdale, NY and Laurence of Newton Centre, MA., his grandchildren Eliot, Leslie, and Robert Young, and Lauren and Elizabeth Rosenberg and great grandchildren Bess, Ella, Joshua and Evan, was matched by their admiration and love for him. He is survived by his wife Bernice, son-in-law Jerry Rosenberg, daughter-in-law Jody Young and sisters Celia and Molly. Services are on Wednesday, April 8 at 11:30 A.M., Riverdale Temple, West 246 Street and Independence Avenue, Riverdale, New York. Contributions in his memory may be made to Riverdale Temple.
        YOUNG-Benjamin. The Officers and Board of Directors of Women of Reform Judaism are saddened by the death of Benjamin Young, father of our Executive Director, Ellen Y. Rosenberg. A major force in Ellen's life, Benjamin Young guided her with wisdom and a foundation based in Jewish values, establishing a rich heritage for his children, Ellen and Laurence, grandchildren and great grandchildren. We extend our sympathy to Ellen and the family. May his memory be for a blessing.


        WEISSENSTEIN-Herbert F. Age 56, of New York City and Sharon, CT, died October 26, 2001, of cancer after a short illness. Beloved husband of Constance Shuman, cherished father of Kathryn and Michael, loving son of Ruth Popper, and the late Heinz Weissenstein. Dear brother of Peter, Robert, David, Stephan, Karin, Lenora, Evelyn and Thomas. Devoted son-in-law of Marjorie Shuman, caring brother-in-law of Peter, John and Laura. Services private. A memorial service will be announced at a later date. Contributions in his memory may be made to a charity of your choice.
        WEISSENSTEIN-Herbert F. The Board and staff of BAM mourn the passing of Herbert F. Weissenstein, a longtime friend & advisor. We thank him for all he did for BAM and extend our deepest sympathy to his family. Karen B. Hopkins, President Joseph V. Melillo, Exec. Producer Alan Fishman, Chair Campaign for BAM
        WEISSENSTEIN - Herbert. The Carnegie Hall family mourns the loss of our friend and colleague, Herbert Weissenstein, who was a key member of our staff from 1979 to 1984. His contributions to the broader cultural community have been many. We will miss his quick witted company and his incisive counsel. Our hearts go out to Connie and the family.
        WEISSENSTEIN-Herbert. Congregation Rodeph Sholom mourns the untimely death of our longtime member, Herbert Weissenstein. Our deepest sympathy is extended to his wife Constance, their children Michael & Kathryn and all their dear ones. Robert N. Levine, Sr. Rabbi Mark Biderman, President
        WEISSENSTEIN-Herbert. Manhattan Theatre Club mourns the loss of its beloved friend, colleague and trusted adviser, Herbert Weissenstein. We extend our heartfelt sympathies to Connie, Kathryn, Michael and his entire family.
        WEISSENSTEIN-Herb. The Folksbiene Yiddish Theatre is deeply saddened by the loss of our very wise and thoughtful colleague and friend. We send our condolences to his wife and family.

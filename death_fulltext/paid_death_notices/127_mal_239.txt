
        RYLE-Joseph D. A prominent public relations executive died July 25th at his home in New York. He was 86. A Memorial Mass will be held in Lacy Chapel, St. Patrick Cathedral, on September 26, at 10:30 A.M. Memorials may be made to East Side House, 337 Alexander Avenue, Bronx, New York. Mr. Ryle served on the East Side Board for 40 years and was a founder of its Winter Antique Show. His long career in public relations over six decades included such projects and creative efforts for such diverse clients as the New York Racing Association, CocaCola, the Irish Georgian Society, the chanteuse Hildegarde and the Metropolitan Museum of Art. Of equal significance were his volunteer efforts on behalf of such organizations as East Side House, Shaker Village in Massachusetts, the Isabel O'Neil Foundation, the Westchester Kennel Club and the Remsenberg Association in the village where he maintained a second home for many years, ''The Academy''. Other celebrations that involved Mr. Ryle included the 40th Anniversary of Time Magazine and the Centennial of the Metropolitan Museum of Art. During WWII he served in the US Air Force rising to the rank of Major. Following the war he was Director of Public Relations for American Overseas Airlines based in London.

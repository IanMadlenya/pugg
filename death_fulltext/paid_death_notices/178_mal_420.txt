
        SILVERSTEIN-Lawrence I. Of Clifton, NJ and Delray Beach, FL, passed away on Wednesday in Florida at age 76. Mr. Silverstein was born in the Bronx and lived in Spring Valley, Brooklyn and Passaic before moving to Clifton 30 years ago. Mr. Silverstein served in the U.S. Navy as a Signalman Third Class during WW II, and was stationed in Zamboyanga and Mindanao, the Philippines. He attended St. John's University and had a law degree from The NewYork Law School. He was employed by J.I. Kislak and Co., of Newark, and was a partner in Buchbinder, Silverstein and Solove, a Springfield, NJ based mortgage brokerage firm, before retiring in 1998. Mr. Silverstein, an avid sports fan, was a Board member of the Foundation of The General Hospital Center of Passaic, The Boys and Girls Club of Clifton, and The Clifton Optimist Club. He was also an active member of Temple Emmanuel of Passaic. Mr. Silverstein is survived by his wife, Hermine (nee Neuman), daughter, Lori Solomon, son-in-law Jerry of North Caldwell, NJ, granddaughter Hannah, sister Bernice Schoenberg, and many loving nieces and nephews. Services will be held 10:30 AM Friday at the GuttermanMusicant Funeral Home, corner Park and Passaic Streets, Hackensack, NJ. The family requests that in lieu of flowers, donations be made to The Children's Center for Therapy and Learning Capital Campaign, 15 Halko Drive, Cedar Knolls, New Jersey 07927.


        SCHREIBER-Aaron M. The Yeshiva University family mourns the passing of a cherished alumnus and revered leader of the Jewish communities of New York, Miami Beach, and Israel, whose good works on behalf of Jewish education and whose chesed activities were widely respected. He nurtured a magnificent Torah family with three sons, all rabbis ordained by the University's affiliated Rabbi Isaac Elchanan Theological Seminary who, with their wives, are RIETS Fellows. Through his family foundation, with his family, Aaron Schreiber was a YU Guardian. He also established the Blanche Schreiber Torah Tours Program at RIETS in memory of his deceased wife, and was a Founder of our Albert Einstein College of Medicine. We extend our heartfelt condolences to his wife, Esther Maidenbaum Schreiber; children: RIETS Trustee Rabbi Joel M. (and Judith) Schreiber, Rabbi Simeon (and Rose) Schreiber, and Rabbi David N. (and Hadassah, YUHS Board member) Schreiber; sister, Pearl Pincus; nine grandchildren; and twenty-one great-grandchildren. May the entire family be comforted among the mourners of Zion and Jerusalem. Dr. Norman Lamm, President Yeshiva University David S. Gottesman, Chairman YU Board of Trustees Judah Feinerman, Chairman RIETS Board of Trustees Elliot Gibber, Chairman YUHS Board
        SCHREIBER-Aaron M. We express sincerest condolences to our devoted friend, Esther Schreiber, on the loss of her beloved husband, Aaron. May they be comforted amongst the mourners of Zion and Jerusalem. American Committee for Shaare Zedek Medical Center in Jerusalem Erica Jesselson, Chair Menno Ratzker, President Lee Weinbach, President, National Women's Division Stuart Tauber, Executive Director
        SCHREIBER-Aaron M. During his more than 90 years, Mr. Schreiber devoted his life to Torah, the Jewish People and all worthy Jewish causes, and instilled these ideals in his children and grandchildren. Joel Schreiber has served as AFYBA President for more than ten years, and his son, Avram is CoChairman of AFYBA's Five Towns Committee, continuing their father's and grandfather's committment and dedication to Israel and Religious Zionism.

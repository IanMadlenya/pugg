
        HERZ-John W., a senior partner in the New York City law firm of Wolf, Haldenstein, Adler, Freeman & Herz, died October 19. He was 83 years old. Mr. Herz, who resided in Larchmont, New York, died after a prolonged illness. He was born in New York City and received both his B.A. and his law degree from Columbia University. He practiced corporate law with his present firm for over fifty years. He was the editor of the text Business Acquisitions and a faculty member for the Practising Law Institute. In addition to his law practice, Mr. Herz was a community leader and served as President of the Guidance Center of New Rochelle, a trustee of the Strang Cancer Prevention Center, President of Camp Madison-Felicia, a trustee of the Hudson Guild, Inc., Vice President of I Care and a Trustee of the Mamaroneck School District Community Education Program. Mr. Herz also served as a U.S. Army Captain during World War II. John Herz is survived by Elise, his wife of 57 years, his three sons: Andrew, James and Bill, his three daughters-in-law and eight grandchildren. A memorial service will be held at Ethical Cultural Society of Westchester in White Plains, New York, at 2 p.m. on Wednesday, November 11, 1998. Contributions can be made on his behalf to the Strang Cancer Prevention Center in New York City or the Guidance Center of New Rochelle.


        ROGERS--Sydney H. 85, passed away on Friday, February 4, 2005. Syd had lived in Stonington, CT, since 1987 and in Darien, CT, from 1952 to 1987. Syd was born on August 22, 1919, in Fall River, MA, the son of William Prescott and Gretchen Harwood Rogers. He was a graduate of The Middlesex School, Yale and Harvard Business School. During WW II, Syd served in the U.S. Navy on the destroyer U.S.S. Hart, in the 7th Fleet in the Western Pacific. He supported invasions in Tinian, Saipan, Iwo Jima, Okinawa, Borneo and Jinsen, and served as navigator for the last four months of duty. Syd was a passionate sailor. He made transatlantic passages, sailed 12 Bermuda Races, six Southern Ocean Racing Circuits, the 1967 Admiral's Cup, and served as chairman of the 1968 Bermuda Race. Syd was a founding member of the Stonington Harbor Yacht Club and was on the harbor commission. He was a member of the Wadawanuck Club, the Cruising Club of America, the New York Yacht Club, the Herreshoff Museum, Mystic Seaport Museum, and the New Bedford Whaling Museum. From 1963 to 1987, Syd worked for Ziff-Davis Publishing Co. as VP and Publisher of Boating and A+ magazines. Prior to Ziff-Davis, he was employed by Freeport Sulphur and Springs Mills. Syd is survived by his wife of 60 years, Harriet G. Rogers, four children and eight grandchildren. A memorial service will be held at 11 A.M. on Saturday, February 12, 2005 at the Calvary Episcopal Church in Stonington. In lieu of flowers, a donation can be made to the Sydney H. Rogers Community Sailing Program, an educational program dedicated in Syd's honor and designed to promote instruction in seamanship, sportsmanship and safety in boating activities in the Stonington community.

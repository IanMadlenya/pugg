
        LARRECQ--Anthony J., age 96, died 16 January 2005 at Pennswood Village, Newtown, PA. Born in Yorba Linda, California in 1908, he lived in Yardley, PA for more than forty years before moving to Pennswood Village. An entrepreneur, he founded Power Generators, Inc. in Trenton, New Jersey in 1945. He served as its president for more than fifty years during which time the company was a prime contractor for the U.S. Navy involved in engineering, design and technical services for aircraft carrier catapults and arresting gear. The Navy has cited him for his many contributions. Prior to forming Power Generators he worked as a Project Engineer for the Allison Div. of General Motors and before that served for a number of years as a test engineer for General Electric Company. He is the holder of 20 patents in the field of heat transfer equipment and rotating and reciprocating machinery. A registered professional engineer in the states of New Jersey and Pennsylvania, he was a lifetime member of the American Society of Mechanical Engineers. He graduated Cum Laude from the California Institute of Technology in 1929 where he received a BSME degree. He was elected to the honor society of Tau Beta Pi and in his senior year was elected as an associate member of Sigma XI. Husband of the late Leonia Elizabeth (aka Alice) Larrecq, he is survived by two daughters Leigh Larrecq Van den Heuvel of New York City and Linda Larrecq Yarnes of Fort Myers, Florida, as well as a brother Henry in California. Funeral services will be held on Sunday, January 23, 2005 at 2:00 P.M. at the J. Allen Hooper Funeral Chapel, 41 W. Trenton Ave., Morrisville, PA. Interment will be private and at the convenience of the family in St. Mary Cemetery in Westfield, Massachusetts. Relatives and friends may call on Saturday, January 22, 2005 from 6 to 8:00P.M. at the funeral chapel.

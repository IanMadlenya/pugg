
        SHEVLIN-Alexander. Of Atlantic City, NJ, died on October 20, 2001. Formerly of Philadelphia and Honolulu. Son of the late Isador and Celia Shavelenko and husband of the late Jean Korn Shevlin. He is survived by his sister Sally and her husband Abbe, and their two sons David Edward and Ian Seth, and numerous cousins. Dr. Shevlin was a true Renaissance man. He loved and was touched by people, the arts, flowers and animals. In Philadelphia, from 1961 to 1971, he hosted an educational TV adult literacy program called ''Operation Alphabet.'' He also edited a curriculum for the Philadelphia school system on teaching the Holocaust. Dr. Shevlin's body was willed to science and there will be no formal service for him. If you wish to visit his home in Atlantic City, his sister will be in residence on November 2, from 2 to 7 PM, November 3, from 10 AM to 7 PM, and November 4, from 10 AM to 8 PM. Dr. Shevlin will be remembered lovingly by family and friends for his cheerfulness, kindness and charm. The world will be a poorer place without him. Donations in his memory may be made to the American Diabetes Association, or any animal humane association of your choice.

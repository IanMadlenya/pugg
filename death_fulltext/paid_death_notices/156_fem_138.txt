
        EISELE-Carolyn. 98. Died on Saturday, January 15, 2000, after a prolonged illness. She was a renowned scholar for her work on Charles S. Peirce, the great American philosopher and mathematician. She taught at Hunter College of New York, retiring as Professor Emeritus in 1972 after serving for 50 years. She was beloved by her many friends and colleagues in different parts of the world, and will be greatly missed. Interment will be private. A memorial service to honor her memory will be announced at a later date. Contributions to her memory may be sent to: Peirce Edition Project, c/o Dr. Nathan Houser, Director, Indiana University, 425 University Blvd., Indianapolis, IN 46202-5140.

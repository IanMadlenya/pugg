
        SALTON-Albin. Died on September 28, 2001, at the age of 85. He was born in Budapest, Hungary, on August 27, 1916, and came to the United States in 1937. Educated at Queens College, Cambridge, England and at Harvard Business School, Mr. Salton was Managing Director of Lehman Brothers, Inc., prior to his retirement. He was a world traveler and connoisseur of arts and antiques. Mr. Salton's parents, Joseph Tivadar and Emmy of New York, and his sister, Thea Elek of Philadelphia, predeceased him. His wife, the former Anny de Rivera, whom he married in 1968, died in 1997. He leaves his niece, Maria Elek Roberts, her husband Larry, grandniece Teresa, grandnephews Gregory, Bruce and Eric Roberts, together with their families, including two great-grandnephews. He will be missed by his family and a host of friends in New York and throughout the world. A memorial service will be held at Frank E. Campbell, 1076 Madison Ave at 81 St, 11:30 AM, on October 6, 2001. In accordance with Mr. Salton's request, friends are encouraged to make donations to their favorite charity in lieu of flowers.

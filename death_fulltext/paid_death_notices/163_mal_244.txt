
        BARTON-Shelley J. Died August 16. Age 74. Born in Bensonhurst, NY December 21, 1925. WW II Veteran. BS Syracuse University, 1948. Survived and cherished by daughters, Lin and Antigone, wife Priscilla and companion Jean Gatehouse. Music lover and art collector. Veteran NYC publishing executive. Spent the bulk of his career at Fawcett Publications and retired from Petersen Publishings. He was a long time member of the Friar's Club, member of the New York State Veterans Boxing Association. President of the Slivovitz Institute and a regular fight announcer in the New York area. Shelley traveled extensively, was fluent in many languages and for many years conducted group walking tours in Dublin reciting the works of James Joyce. Considered by many to have one of the broadest and most varied circle of friends and interests of anyone in his profession. Shelley leaves behind in addition to his family large group of individuals who loved and admired him for his loyalty and humor. His quick fire NYC brand of wit was unique and memorable. Shelley's distinguished ''turn of the century'' look and style was very much his trademark. Shelley Barton never went out of style. Memorial service to be announced.


        SHILOWITZ-Stephen, architect, died on October 19 in New York City. He was 69 years old. He is survived by his wife, Carole Clarke, of Malvern, Pa, his daughter Yumi Shilowitz Ross of Chicago, Ill., his son George Shilowitz of New York City and his treasured grandchildren Ian and Camryn Ross. He received his architectural training at the University of Pennsylvania, MIT and the Ecole des Arts Americains in Fountainbleu, France. A two year stay in Tokyo, Japan, working for renowned architect Antonin Raymond, was a lifelong influence on his architectural philosophy. Recipient of a Ford Foundation grant for design of a mixed-use school and a Graham Foundation Grant for a traveling exhibition on Antonin Raymond, he taught archirectural concepts at the Phelps School in Malvern, Pa. He was a committed sailor, who was working on the design of a new catboat at the time of his death. Services will be held today at 9:45AM at The Riverside Memorial Chapel, 76th St and Amsterdam Avenue.


        RIVKIN-Lois Herman. The family of Lois Rivkin mourns the loss on April 27, of their beloved wife, mother, grandmother and sister, after a valiant fight against lymphoma. Her creation of Friends of the Public Schools exemplified her warmth, energy, optimism & love of New York City. A memorial service will be held Sunday, May 2, at 11 A.M. at Congregation Rodeph Sholom, 7 West 83rd St. Donations in lieu of flowers may be made to Congregation Rodeph Sholom, or to the Citizens Committee for New York City, 305 7th Avenue.
        RIVKIN - Lois. Congreation Rodeph Sholom mourns the death of our beloved, longtime member, Lois Rivkin. We will miss her indomitable spirit, incredible devotion to public service and Jewish Ideals, her passionate devotion to family and friends and courage under affliction. Our deepest sympathy is extended to her her husband, Donald, their children, David, Judith and Daniel and all their dear ones. Memorial service at Congregation Rodeph Sholom on Sunday, 11AM, 7 West 83 Street. Robert N. Levine, Senior Rabbi Mark Biderman, President
        RIVKIN-Lois. Sotheby's extends its condolences to the family of Lois Rivkin, who believed deeply in the importance of art in the lives of young people. Sotheby's was privileged to work with Lois in planning and hosting the Annual Exhibition of Art by the students of the public schools of New York City, where her commitment and enthusiasm were an inspiration. She will be greatly missed.

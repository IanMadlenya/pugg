
        RUCKENS-Irving. Age 88, died August 6, 2001 in Manhattan. Beloved husband of Sylvia. Ultimate New Yorker, loved traveling, history, politics, progressive causes and the performing and visual arts. He held an accounting degree from Columbia and before retirement was controller of The Juilliard School. Serving in the US Army in World War II, he performed intelligence work behind the German lines during the Normandy offensive. A long time resident of Lower Manhattan, he was involved in community affairs and arts. In the 1960s he founded Dynarts, a non-profit organization staging public music and theater events. In retirement, he and his wife remained active, attending concerts and plays. He was a strong supporter of Juilliard. He will be missed dearly.

        $(6$)SPICEHANDLER--Jonathan R., M.D. Infectious Disease Specialist, Of Short Hills, N.J. Former Chairman Schering Plough Research Institute. During his 24 years with Schering, Jonathan oversaw the development of Intron, Temodar, Claritan and Zetia, dedicated work that has made a difference in countless lives. His contributions to the broader pharmaceutical and scientific communities have been widely recognized. Dr. Spicehandler received his B.A. degree in biology from Union College, Schenectady, N.Y. in 1970 and his M.D., cum laude, in 1974 from St Louis University School of Medicine. Dr. Spicehandler has earned admiration for his contributions to numerous boards including Cancer Care Inc. of New Jersey, Kessler Research Foundation, Montclair State University, Kent Place School , Children's Specialized Hospital Foundation, Keryx Pharmaceuticals N.Y., XTL Biopharmaceuticals and Color By Pergament. He is survived by his mother Florence, his wife Debra, his four children, Daniel, Alexandra, Benjamin, and Rebecca, his sister Debra and brother David. His presence continues to be felt through the lives of his families and friends. In Jonathan's memory, donations can be made to The Preston Robert Tisch Brain Tumor Center at Duke DUMC Box 3624 Durham, N.C. 27710 or The Cancer Center at S.B.M.C. 94 Old Short Hills Road Livingston, N.J. 07039.

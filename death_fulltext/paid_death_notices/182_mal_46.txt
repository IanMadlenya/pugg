
        LEVINE-Irving J. Died March 4, 2002, at the age of 89 in Princeton, New Jersey. Beloved husband of the late Dorothy Thome Levine for 61 years, he is survived by his son Richard J. Levine, daughter Nancy L. Castro, sister Pauline L. Rosenblum; four grandchildren, Jonathan D. Levine, Stephanie L. Castro, Russell N. Levine and Wendy E. Castro; three greatgrandchildren, twins Emma S. Levine and Caroline B. Levine and Trevor S. Levine; and daughter-inlaw Neil Ann S. Levine and son-inlaw Peter E. Castro. Born in Harlem in 1912 and educated in New York City public schools, he graduated from Brooklyn College in 1933 and earned a Master's degree in history from Columbia University in 1935. A dedicated, life-long educator, he spent 38 years in the New York City school system. He taught history at Manual Training and Midwood High Schools as well as at the Ramaz School in Manhattan and Brooklyn College, served as chairman of the social studies department at Benjamin Franklin High School and was principal of John D. Wells Junior High School 50 in Williamsburg and later Isaac Bildersee Junior High School 68 in Canarsie. He retired in 1975 and lived in Southbury, CT and Deerfield Beach, FL. Graveside service at Mount Ararat Cemetery, Farmingdale, LI, Thursday, March 7, at 12:30PM.

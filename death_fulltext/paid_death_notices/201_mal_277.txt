
        ARMINGTON -- Nicholas Meister. Nicholas Meister Armington, a maker of documentary films, died October 18, 2003 while scuba diving in the waters off Anacapa Island, California. His death was an accident. He had turned 41 on October 15th, the previous Wednesday. Born in New York City, Mr. Armington and his wife Sari were currently living in San Rafael, California. Prior to moving to California, he lived and worked in New York City, spending his summers at the family home in Siasconset, Nantucket Island. Mr. Armington was an accomplished filmmaker who, with his wife, Sari, formed a film production company, Alchemedia, Ltd. He also had numerous corporate clients. At the time of his death, they had been in production on the public television documentary, ''The Funnies: Life in the Comics'' with KQED TV in San Francisco. The film is a celebration of the role that newspaper comics have played in American society. He also was an accomplished music composer and played the keyboards. Mr. Armington followed his late father, Hervey Foster Armington, into filmmaking. He attended Dartmouth College, where he earned a bachelor's degree in computer sciences in 1984. After summer positions with McKinsey & Company and American Express Company, he worked for many years for MeadWestvaco Corporation in New York producing numerous coporate films and videos. He left in 1996 to start Alchemedia, which is based in San Rafael, California. Mr. Armington is survived by his wife, Sari Armington of San Rafael, and by his mother, Dr. Irene Meister-Armington, of New York and Nantucket. A memorial service will take place on November 4, 2003 at 10 A.M. at the Church of Saint Thomas More, 65 East 89th Street, New York, NY 10128.

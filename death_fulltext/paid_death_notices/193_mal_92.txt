
        JEHLE - Faustin Francis. Born September 16, 1914 in New York. Son of Anna and Augustus Jehle, died at the age of 88 in Palm Beach, Florida on January 21, 2003, from complications of cancer. He is survived by his loving and devoted wife of 54 years, Rosemary, by his three adoring daughters Michele, Patricia and Diane, by his son-inlaw Victor Somogyi and by his brother Philip Jehle. All who knew him will miss his playfulness and his gentle and generous spirit. He graduated St. John's Collge of Law with an LLB in 1937 and a Doctorate of Law in 1940. He served as Lieutenant Colonel with the 91st Infantry Division from 1942 until 1946. Among other decorations and citations, he was awared the Bronze Star in 1944 for outstanding service during the Italian campaign by Major General William G. Livesay. He was Associate General Counsel for McGraw-Hill Publishing Company for 29 years, retiring in 1979 to private practice in New York City before moving to Florida with his wife. In ''retirement,'' he authored ''The Complete and Easy Guide to Social Security, Health Care Rights and Government Benefits,'' which sold over 1 million copies of his 13 editions. A funeral mass will be held in New York City at the Church of St. Ignatius Loyola, at 11am, on February 13, 2003. A private military burial will be held on February 13, 2003 at 2:30PM, at the Calverton Veteran's Cemetery on Long Island, New York. In lieu of flowers, the family requests donations are sent to the Hospice of Palm Beach County, which so lovingly cared for Mr. Jehle during his final days.


        BERNARD-Viola Wertheim, M.D. On March 21, 1998. Daughter of the late Jacob and Emma Wertheim. Beloved aunt of Joan Wofford and David Whittlesey. Interment private. Memorial service to be announced.
        BERNARD-Dr. Viola W. The Executive Committee, faculty, staff and candidates of the Columbia University Center for Psychoanalytic Training and Research, express our deepest sorrow over the death of one of the Center's founders and our esteemed colleague, Viola Bernard. Dr. Bernard was a vital part of the Columbia Psychoanalytic Center for over fifty years. Bringing psychoanalytic understanding to important issues in the fields of social and child psychiatry, her work brought insights and approaches to the amelioration of emotional suffering of children and adults. Robert A. Glick, M.D., Director
        BERNARD-Viola, M.D. The officers and members of the New York Psychoanalytic Society and Institute express their profound sorrow at the death of their respected and beloved colleague Dr. Viola Bernard. Our deepest sympathy is extended to her family. Lester Schwartz, M.D., President/Society Edward Hartmann, M.D., President/Institute
        BERNARD-Viola, M.D. Always the advocate for, and care giver to, those in need. Including victims of bigotry, poverty, abandonment and political oppression. She was also a renowned clinician, teacher and mentor and beloved friend. E. Gerald Dabbs, M.D.

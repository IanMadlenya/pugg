
        BAIRD-Robert B. II of Quogue, N.Y., passed away on Wednesday, July 22, while on vacation in Spain. He was born on January 1,1923 in Brooklyn, N.Y. and grew up in both Brooklyn and Quogue. His wife, Barbara Arnold Baird, survives him. He is also survived by 15 children, Margaret M. Baird, Barbara B. Zeman, Camille R. Baird, Robert L. Baird, Esther S. Baird, Mary E. Baird, Belinda B. Fremont, Cynthia Baird Goode, William T. Baird, David T. Baird, Catherine Bennett, Andrew H. Baird, Dr. Susan Arnold, Margaret Arnold and James Arnold. He also leaves behind 20 grandchildren and one great-grandchild. He is predeceased by his first wife, Esther S. Baird. Mr. Baird was owner and C.E.O. of Polyesther Corporation, which imports and exports specialty chemicals for the cosmetics industry, and Robert B. Baird Division, which imports crude rubber. Mr. Baird was a graduate of Polytechnic Preparatory Country Day School in Brooklyn where he enjoyed an illustrious wrestling and track career, winning three national championships, which he carried on to Lehigh University winning medals and trophies in virtually every sport he participated in. During W.W.II he served in the U.S. Army Air Corps. Mr. Baird was a member of The Shinnecock Hills Golf Club, the Quogue Field Club, Quogue Beach Club, the Shinnecock Yacht Club, Westhampton Yacht Squadron, the New York Yacht Club, the Princeton Club, India House, the Southampton Club, the V.F.W. in Quogue and the Circumnavigators Club. Mr. Baird was also very active in the community. He was a founder of the South Street Seaport Museum. He served on the Board of Directors of Good Samaritan Hospital in West Islip, Southampton Hospital and Central Suffolk Hospital in Riverhead and is a former Director of the Suffolk County Red Cross. He served on the Planning Board of the Village of Quogue, was a Village Trustee for 11 years and had terms as Quogue Fire Commissioner and Acting Police Commissioner. He was also instrumental in the establishment of the Quogue Wildlife Refuge Nature Center as well as being Director of the Southampton Wildfowl Association. Some of his other activities include serving on the Board of Directors at the Lincoln Savings Bank, serving on the Board of Directors of several schools, and being assistant director of the Parrish Art Museum in Southampton. A memorial service will be held at Saint Mark's Church in Westhampton Beach, New York, at 11 A.M. on Friday, July 31, with a reception afterwards at the Quogue Field Club. In lieu of flowers, please send contributions to Polytechnic Prep School, 92nd Street and 7th Ave., Brooklyn, N.Y. 11228 or to the Southampton Township Wildfowl Association, Box 492, Quogue, New York 11959.

        BAIRD-Robert B. The Trustees, Staff and Headmaster of Poly Prep Country Day School mourn the loss of Robert B. Baird. Former member of the Board, member of the Headmaster's Advisory Council, loyal and generous member of the class of '42. Bob remembered with gratitude the great teachers and coaches who trained and challenged him when he was a boy at Poly. We will miss him sorely.

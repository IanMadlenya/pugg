
        MATTLAGE-Louise. Multi-talented modern dance, artist, and poet died on March 9, 1999, in Boca Raton, FL. She was 92, and the cause was advanced Alzheimer's Disease. A sophisticated woman with a wide range of cultural interests, Ms. Mattlage's career took her from modern dance with Jose Limon, and Doris Humphrey, to director and choreographer of her own dance company, based in CT, called The Dancers of Faith. The Dance Company performed in countless churches and synagogues throughout the eastern United States. She taught modern dance at college and universities all around the world. She was the author of four books of poetry. Her numerous poems and essays were published in periodicals often concerning women's issues. Born and raised in Boston, MA, Ms. Mattlage lived for many years in Fairfield and Southport, CT. She moved to Key Biscayne, FL in 1971. She continued to spend summers in Fairfield, CT. She was a member of the Miami chapter of the Pen Women and she lectured and performed her dance and poetry well into her 80's. In 1989, she moved to Boca Raton, FL. She is survived by her son Jonathan Mattlage of Acworth, GA, her daughter Valerie Mattlage of Boca Raton, FL, and her two grandsons: Jason Ledyard, of New York, NY, and Jonathan Mattlage, Jr. of Kennesaw, GA. She was pre-deceased by her husband of 65 years, W.F.H. Mattlage. Memorial contributions may be made to either the National Organization for Women, or The Alzheimer's Association.

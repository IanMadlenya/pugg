
        GOLDSTEIN--Hope Perry of New York and Chicago on September 14th in New York at Memorial Sloan-Kettering Cancer Center after a long and courageous battle with cancer. Beloved wife of Alfred Goldstein, dedicated mother of Mark and Robert and daughter-in-law Stephanie (nee Levinson), adoring grandmother of Nicholas and Danielle and loving sister of Dr. Jerome Perry. A former executive of Sears, Roebuck and Co., and volunteer at the Costume Institute of The Metropolitan Museum of Art of New York, she was currently a founding member of The Jewish Women's Foundation of Metropolitan Chicago, member of the Women's Association of The Chicago Symphony Orchestra and a Trustee of The Museum of Contemporary Art of Chicago. Funeral Services will be held September 15th at 10 AM at Central Synagogue in New York at 55th Street and Lexington Ave. Memorial Observance (Shiva) will be held at her New York residence from 3PM to 6PM today. Gifts in her memory may be made to The Hope Perry Goldstein Fund at Memorial Sloan-Kettering Cancer Center, 633 3rd Avenue, NY, NY, 28th floor.
        GOLDSTEIN--Hope. Central Synagogue records with profound sorrow the passing of its member, Hope Goldstein. We extend heartfelt sympathy to her bereaved family. Peter J. Rubinstein, Rabbi Alfred Youngwood, President

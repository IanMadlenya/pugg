
        QUILLAN-Francis Seymour. Retired senior vice president of The Prudential Insurance Company of America died February 27 at his home in Palm City, FL. He was 86. Mr. Quillan was born on Block Island, RI on September 12, 1912. He was the son of Edward Thomas Quillan and the former Mabel Hayes. He graduated from Moses Brown School in 1929. In 1933 he graduated Summa cum Laude in mathematics from Brown University where he was elected to Phi Beta Kappa and admitted to Sigma Xi, an honorary scientific fraternity. Mr. Quillan joined the Prudential in 1933. In 1961 he was named senior vice president in charge of the General Office Administration, Claim, Debit Policy and Ordinary Policy Departments. In 1969 he also assumed responsibility for the Governmental Health Program Department. Mr. Quillan retired in 1973. For 37 years Mr. Quillan resided in Glen Ridge, NJ and was a member of the Board of Education there from 1951 until 1963. He served as president of the board from 1955 to 1963. Mr. Quillan had been vice president of the Associated Boards of Education of Essex County, treasurer and trustee of the United Community Corporation of Newark, chairman of the board of the Better Business Bureau of Greater Newark, a member of the Education Committee of the Chamber of Commerce of the United States, the national director of the Interracial Council for Business Opportunity, a director of the National Urban League, and the metro director of the National Alliance of Businessmen for Newark. Mr. Quillan was married on October 7, 1939 to the former Frances Lane of Nutley, NJ who died in 1993. He is survived by four children, Marjorie Wendell of Chatham, NJ, Joanne Cotterill of Madison, NJ, Dorothy Williams of Mystic, CT and George Quillan of Morristown, NJ; three grandsons and three granddaughters; a brother, Edward Hayes Quillan of East Greenwich, RI; two nieces and one grandnephew. Services will be private. Those who wish may contribute to Hospice of Martin & St. Lucie, P.O. Box 1168, Stuart, FL 34995-1168.

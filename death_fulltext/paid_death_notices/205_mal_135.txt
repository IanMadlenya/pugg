
        TABLER--William Benjamin. Age 89 of Upper Brookville, NY retired head of William B. Tabler Architects died Tuesday, February 3, 2004. Graduate of Harvard University, B.S. Cum Laude, 1936, M. Arch. 1939. Survived by his wife of 67 years, Phyllis Baker Tabler, his children, William B. Tabler, Jr. and Judith Tabler Cook, and three grandchildren, W. Benjamin Tabler, III, Rebecca Kelsey Roby, and Parker B. Kelsey. Mr. Tabler was the architect of over 400 hotels worldwide, including the Hiltons in New York and Washington, and more recently the Brooklyn Marriott. During WWII, Mr. Tabler served in the Navy and supervised the construction of the airstrips on Saipan and Tinian used for the B-29 bombers. He was the recipient of the Horatio Alger award, a Fellow of AIA, ASCE, Royal Institute of British Architects. He was a member of the Piping Rock Club and Royal and Ancient Golf Club of St Andrews. Interment private. Memorial service at a later date.

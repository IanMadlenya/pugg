
        GOLDSTEIN-Syd Rossman. In her 95th year. From 1958 to 1962, she was National President of Woman's League for Conservative Judaism. She was currently National Treasurer of the Torah Fund Campaign. She leaves a daughter and son-in-law. Arlene and Dr. Edgar Braunstein, daughter-in-law Barbara Rossman, brother and sister-in-law Harold and Leah Sher. Grandchildren Vicki Braunstein and Dr. Steven and Laurie Braunstein, Dr. Barry and Terri Rossman, Lauri and Paul Feldman and her beloved great - grandchildren, Melissa, Stephanie, Carly, Jake, Andrew and Michael. She was beloved by all who knew her. She was predeceased by her husbands, Dr. Herbert Rossman and Louis Goldstein and a son, Dr. Melvin Rossman. Funeral service on Thursday, 1:15 P.M., at ''The Riverside'', 76 Street and Amsterdam Avenue.
        GOLDSTEIN-Syd Rossman. Women's League for Conservative Judaism mourns with profound sadness the death of its revered past national president, Syd Rossman Goldstein. A woman of integrity, humility, wisdom, and kindness, Syd was a model for all who followed. Syd died peacefully on the eve of her 95th birthday, young in spirit, sharp of mind, beloved by all who knew her, leaving a legacy of unrivaled service to her community and to her people. May the memory of the righteous be for a blessing. Evelyn Seelig National President Bernice Balter Executive Director
        GOLDSTEIN-Syd Rossman. The Officers, Board of Directors, and staff of UJA-Federation of New York mourn the passing of Syd Goldstein, an outstanding leader and supporter of UJA-Federation. She was a chair of the Women's Campaign and a lifelong activist for many Jewish causes, including efforts to advance the role of women in Jewish life. We extend our sincerest condolences to her daughter, four grandchildren, and five great grandchildren. Judith Stern Peck, Board Chair Louise B. Greilsheimer, President Susan K. Stern, Chair, Women's Campaign Stephen D. Solender, Exec. V.P.

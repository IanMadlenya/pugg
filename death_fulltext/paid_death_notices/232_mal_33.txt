
        RUKEYSER--Louis Richard. Louis Rukeyser, the pioneering financial journalist and broadcaster, died May 2 in Greenwich, Connecticut, at age 73 of multiple myeloma. Born in New York City, Mr. Rukeyser graduated from Princeton in 1954. He served in the U.S. Army in Germany and spent 11 years with the Baltimore Sun papers as a political and foreign correspondent and eight years with ABC News as a foreign correspondent and network television's first Economic Editor. In 1970 he launched television's first and perennially most popular national financial program, Wall $treet Week With Louis Rukeyser, which ran for 32 years on public television and then, as Louis Rukeyser's Wall Street, for over a year on CNBC. Along the way he wrote bestselling books on economics and investing, was a syndicated newspaper columnist, edited two successful newsletters, and gave hundreds of speeches. His many awards included the Gerald Loeb Lifetime Achievement Award for Distinguished Business and Financial Journalism, the first Lifetime Achievement Emmy for Business and Financial Reporting, and nine honorary doctorates. Mr. Rukeyser is survived by his wife, Alexandra; three daughters, Beverley Bellisio, Susan Rukeyser and Stacy Rukeyser; two grandchildren, Samantha Bellisio and Jeremy Wyman; and his three brothers, Bud, William S. and Robert. Funeral services will be private. A memorial service will be held later in New York City. In lieu of flowers, contributions may be made to Princeton University (Louis R.


        VanderKloot-Nicholas J., 89 years old, of Palm Beach, FL, died at his home on February 9, 1997, after a three month battle with cancer. Born in Chicago, IL, on April 25, 1907, Nick joined R.R. Donnelley & Sons, the world's largest commercial printer, as a pre-apprentice, in 1924 at the age of 17. During his 48 years at Donnelley, Nick had a distinguished career as a leader in the printing industry and as a legend at Donnelley as Senior Vice President and a member of the Board of Directors. He also was a Director of the Lake Shore Club of Chicago and a member of the Chicago Athletic Club, the Union League Club, and the Calumet Country Club. In 1972, he retired from Donnelley to Palm Beach, where he was a member of the Board of Directors and Treasurer of the Beach Club until 1996. A veteran of WWII, Mr. VanderKloot served as a Lieutenant Colonel on the Army Air Force Air Command Staff at the Pentagon. He is survived by his wife of 48 years, Mary Louise; his step-son, Ron Klensch of Chicago, IL; and his nephew, Frederic VanderKloot of New York. Mr. VanderKloot was preceded in death by his daughter, Pamela, in 1981. A Memorial Service will be held at 2:00 P.M. on Saturday, February 22, 1997 at Quattlebaum-Holleman-Burse Funeral Home, 1201 South Olive Avenue, West Palm Beach, with the Rev. Ralph R. Warren, Jr., D.D. Rector of Bethesda-by-the-Sea Episcopal Church, Palm Beach, FL, officiating. In lieu of flowers, contributions may be made in his memory to Hospice of Palm Beach County, 5300 East Avenue, West Palm Beach, FL 33407.


        LEVINE-Irving. Age 84. Died on August 1, 1999, Albuquerque, N.M. Bronx born, architect & professor at Pratt. During World War II he drove a truck over the Burma Road and edited two Army newspapers. He felt his greatest accomplishment, in addition to his marriage and children, was his writing, including three books of poetry; he was also an avid fiddler. Survived by his wife, Peg Gerard; his children, Matthew, Elena and Michael; his stepchildren Sheila Mints and David McHaffey; four grandchildren, Daniel, Jack, Gem and Roxanne. Donations may be made to the National Yiddish Book Center, 1021 West Street, Amherst, MA. 01002.


        OBROW-D. Irving. Of Little Silver, NJ. Died on October 26, at the age of 81. He is survived by his wife of 55 years, Lucille, his daughters Joanne Bailey and Patti White, and five grandchildren, Jennie, Peter, Edward, Timothy and Trevor. Irving Obrow was a graduate of the Columbia University School of Business and had a long and distinguished career in corporate finance. In the early 1940's, he worked on The Manhattan Project and subsequently went to International Playtex Corp, VP Finance, and to Glen Alden Corp, VP and CEO. In 1978, he joined Johnston Industries in association with his longtime friend, Paul Johnston, succeeding him as President and CEO in 1985. He was a director of Plains Resources and Jupiter Industries. Throughout his career, his depth of understanding and professionalism drew the trust and respect of his colleagues. He had a great love of classical music, and served on the Board of the Orpheus Chamber Orchestra. Memorial contributions in his name may be made to the Orpheus Chamber Orchestra Inc., 490 Riverside Dr, New York, NY 10027. A memorial service will be held on November 4 in NJ. For details, please contact the family.


        WEI-Mark. Died at 84 years of age on April 30, 2002 in his home in Manhattan. Born in Hong Kong, China, Mr. Wei came to New York from China in April of 1941. Mr. Wei began his insurance career at Chubb & Son, Inc. in 1947 and retired after 35 years of service. At the time of his retirement, he was Senior Vice President and a member of the Board of Directors of Chubb & Son, Inc. Mr. Wei also served as a Director of the American Institution of Marine Underwriters, the National Cargo Bureau and the Security Bureau, Inc. He was a former Chairman and Director of the American Cargo War Risk Reinsurance Exchange and the Cargo Reinsurance Association. Mr. Wei received a B.A degree from St. John's University in Shanghai, China and a Master's Degree in Business Administration from Colorado College. He also attended law school at Indiana University. During World War II, he served as a lieutenant in the United States Marine Corps in the Pacific Theater and in the Far East. At the time of his death, Mark was a director and Principal in Kemark Financial Services, Inc. headquartered in Pearl River, NY. In lieu of flowers, donations may be made to Hofstra University, Mark Wei Athletic & Scholarship Fund, c/o Michael D'Amato, 101 Hofstra University, Hempstead, NY 11549.

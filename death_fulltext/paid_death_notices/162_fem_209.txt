
        POWERS-Ruth Pedersen. Died July 25, 2000 in Saint Louis, MO. at the age of 93. She had a long, effective career as a social worker in Buffalo with the Family Service, and in New York City at Travelers' Aid & Community Service Society. Ms. Powers was born in Brooklyn and moved to Stroudsburg, PA. during her high school years. She graduated from Cornell University, with a B.A. and from the New York School of Social Work with a MSW. At Travelers' Aid, she was in charge of the Port Department in the years after WWII, assisting the displaced persons arriving at the New York docks. She then worked at Community Service Society where she managed the Homemaker Service Program that offered temporary support to help clients weather a variety of domestic crises. After her retirement from CSS, she lived an active, independent life in Morningside Gardens where she enjoyed serving as surrogate grandma to several foreign students. For the past three years, she suffered from Alzheimer's Disease, finally succumbing, reluctantly to its complications. She was briefly married to Wollerton Powers and had two daughters, Elizabeth Harris of New York & Susan Lagunoff of Saint Louis, MO. She has five grandchildren, Nick and Sam Jordan, and Rachel, Liza and Michael Lagunoff, and one great granddaughter Sasha Lagunoff Eberhardt. An older sister, Laura Henninger lives in Seattle. Memorial contributions may be made to Habitat for Humanity, 334 Furman Street, Brooklyn, New York 11201.

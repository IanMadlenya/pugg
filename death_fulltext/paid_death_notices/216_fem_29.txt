
        SCHULMAN--Ann Weinfeld. Died peacefully at home on January 4, 2005, at the tooyoung age of 73, after a brief battle with lung cancer. The elder daughter of Judge Edward and Lillian Weinfeld, Ann grew up on the Lower East Side, was educated in New York's public schools and then Vassar College, married (and later divorced) Alvin Schulman, with whom she had their daughters Amy and Nina, and then at the age of 49 was graduated from the NYU Law School, going on to become a passionate and effective advocate for labor unions and their members. A marvelous cook, avid theater goer, delightful conversationalist, intrepid traveler and committed devotee of tennis and baseball, Ann befriended countless people from all walks of life. Her exceptional emotional generosity and warmth of spirit were special gifts that will be long treasured by those who knew her. Ann leaves behind Amy and her family, David Nachman, Ezra, Gideon and Rafael; Nina and her family, Hal Ruddick, Lydia and Saul; her sister Fern and her family, Daniel Cohen, Seth, Elizabeth and Josh and their children; and so many others whose love for Ann will endure. In lieu of flowers, the family requests that donations be made to the Jewish Fund for Justice, 330 Seventh Ave., NY, NY 10001, or Vassar College, 124 Raymond Avenue, Poughkeepsie, NY 12604. Funeral services will be held on Wednesday, January 5, 2005 at 11:45 AM at ''The Riverside'', 76th Street and Amsterdam Avenue.

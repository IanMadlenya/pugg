
        BALLENTINE-George Newton, age 87, an internationally known gynecologist died on January 15, 1997 in Muncy, PA. He was best known for his instrument and equipment designs in the field of gynecology, including the Ballentine Hysterectomy Clamp. A graduate of Bucknell University and Cornell University Medical College in New York City. He was a diplomat of the National Boards and the American Academy of Obstetrics and Gynecology and a Fellow of the American College of Surgeons. A diplomat of the World Medical Association, he traveled to world famous cancer research institutions and operated in many hospitals throughout the world. An American relative of Sir Isaac Newton, he was the son of Floyd George Ballentine and Grace Agnes Newton. He is survived by his son George Newton Ballentine, Jr. Richmond, VA.


        AMEN-Grover. On June 13, 1997 at his home in Brooklyn at the age of 64. A poet, a novelist, a writer for The New Yorker, a teacher, an independent spirit. His short stories, including ''The Prophet of Bourbon Street'' and poetry were published in The New Yorker, Grand Street and Parnassus. His long poem, ''F Train Ramble,'' was published by Reluctant Buddha Press. He is survived by his sister Frances Payne, his son John Amen and his loving friend Elizabeth Albrecht. Services 11:30 AM, Monday, June 16, First Unitarian Church, Pierrepont Street, between Monroe and Clinton Streets, Brooklyn Heights.

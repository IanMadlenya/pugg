
        MITCHELL-Philip Crawford. Died suddenly July 16, 1998 in his home town of Bronxille, N.Y. A veteran of WW II, Mr. Mitchell flew a P47 Fighter Plane in the European Theater. Among his many decorations were the Distinguished Flying Cross, the Air Medal and the Purple Heart. In May 1998, he received a Medal of Honor from the Federation of French War Veterans for his WWII service. Prior his retirement, Mr. Mitchell served as the Chief Executive Officer of James E. Mitchell & Son, a familyowned construction management firm established in New York City in 1888. One of his many projects included the complete restoration of Saint Patrick's Cathedral, for which he was later appointed ''Keeper of the Fabric''. He was honored by Long Island College Hospital as ''Man of the Year'' for a decade of service as Construction Manager during the Hospital's major expansion program. Mr. Mitchell was President of the Saint Andrew's Golf Club in Hastings-onHudson, N.Y. from 1970 to 1982, and a Deacon of the Reformed Church in Bronxville, N.Y. At the time of his death, he was the Project Director for Church Restoration and Chairman of the Building and Grounds Committee. He is survived by his wife Jane Kerr Mitchell of Bronxville, his daughter Nan Mitchell Harman of Palos Verdes, CA., sons Peter Mitchell of Locust Valley, N.Y., Steven Mitchell of Palos Verdes, CA., grandchildren Hayden and Spencer Harman, both of New York City, Christopher Mitchell, a student at the University of VT. and Meghan Mitchell, a student at Middlebury College, a brother James S. Mitchell of Bronxville, 4 nephews and two nieces. Services will be held at the Reformed Church, Pondfield Road & Midland Avenue, Bronxville, N.Y. Saturday, July 18th, 3PM. In lieu of flowers, contributions may be sent to the Reformed Church or Lawrence Hospital, Bronxville, N.Y.

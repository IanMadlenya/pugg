
        WOLGEL-Milton Irving, M.D., died on August 26, 1999, at age 84. Graduate of Downstate Medical School in 1935, Milton completed his residency in Internal Medicine & Gastro Enterology at Beth Israel in 1939. As a World War II vet, he served in Europe as captain of the medical unit for the Military Police Battalion. A respected physician, he cared for generations of families during his successful 30-year practice in Internal Medicine in Kew Gardens. He authored some of the original research articles on the treatment of peptic ulcer. Milton was one of the founders of Parkway Hospital in Forest Hills, New York, and a long-standing member of the New York State Medical Society. He is survived by his wife of 48 years, Jacqueline; children, Vicki and Claude, and daughter-in-law, Lisbeth; two granddaughters, Lindsay and Chelsea; brother, Harold, and sister, Marjorie. Services Sunday, 12 Noon, at Riverside-Nassau North Chapels, 55 North Station Plaza, Great Neck, New York.

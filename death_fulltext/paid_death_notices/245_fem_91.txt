
        KIMMEL--Laura, age 88 of Washington, D.C. died on Thursday, June 14, 2007 at Georgetown University Hospital of pneumonia. Born on August 11, 1918 in Lvov, Poland, Laura Kimmel survived the World War II Holocaust. She and her husband, Dr. Alfred Kimmel immigrated to New York City in 1949. She became a US citizen, graduated with honors from Long Island University and started graduate studies in psychology. Devoted wife and mother, she loved her adopted country and the equalities it was founded upon. She struggled valiantly against progressive disability her entire adult life, always seeking independence of body and spirit. Mrs. Kimmel was preceded in death by her husband, Dr. Alfred Kimmel; daughter, Karen Ann Kimmel-Slavin; parents, Lazar and Clara Spath. She is survived by her son, Dr. Paul L. Kimmel and his wife, Dr. Prudence Kline. Funeral Service Friday June 15 at 1pm at Agudas Achim Congregation, Alexandria, Virginia. Graveside services Sunday, June 17 at 1pm at Montefiore Cemetery in Queens, New York. Donations may be made to the Karen Ann Kimmel-Slavin Memorial Fund, Division of Hematology/Oncology, Department of Medicine, George Washington University, 2150 Pennsylvania Ave, NW Washington, D.C. 20037.

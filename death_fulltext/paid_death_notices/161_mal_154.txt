
        NOYES-Frederick. June 10, 2000, of heart failure. Born September 25, 1912, Colebrook, N.H. He graduated from MIT in architecture, 1936. Served in the U.S. Army as a Major, WWII. As a Project Designer at Skidmore, Owings & Merrill, he was instrumental in the design of Lever House, the city's first ''glass box'' office building. Later his established his own busy and varied practice. In retirement he was active in architectural preservation and in MIT alumni activities. He belonged to several NGOs at the U.N., the Shanghai Tiffin Club and Goodland Country Club. For years he enjoyed singing in his church choirs and was a member of the Canterbury Choral Society. His ''Central Park office'' was a favorite spot. An energetic, creative, and highly original man, he'll be fondly and vividly remembered by his many friends. He is survived by his brother and sister-in-law Irving and Katherine Noyes; his halfbrother and sister-in-law Bernerd and Hannah Burbank; his sons Jonathan, David and Peter and their wives; his grandchildren Jennifer, Jonathan, Lee, and Jonathan; his loving nieces and nephews and his friend Caroline Helmuth. Memorial service at St. James' Episcopal Church, 865 Madison Ave (71 St), June 24th at 10:00AM.

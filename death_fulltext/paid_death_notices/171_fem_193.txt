
        LEE-Sarah Tomerlin. Distinguished interior designer, fashion editor, advertising executive, beloved mother, grandmother, great grandmother. A memorial service will be held Wednesday, April 25, 2001 at 10 A.M. at Christ Church, 520 Park Ave at 60th St. Her family welcomes all colleagues & friends. In lieu of flowers, contributions in her memory may be made to either the New York School of Interior Design, 170 East 70 Street, NY, NY 10021, or Randolph-Macon Woman's College, 2500 Rivermont Avenue, Lynchburg, VA. 24503.
        LEE-Sarah Tomerlin. The President, The Board of Directors and the membership of The Decorators Club deeply mourn the loss of our beloved member, Sarah Tomerlin Lee. A member since 1968, Mrs. Lee was a devoted Club member and especially dedicated to our work for Historic Preservation and Student Scholarship programs. She was active on the Board of Directors and served as President from 1995-1997. In 1982 she was named Honorary Member and received our Club's distinguished Medal of Honor. Mrs. Lee delighted us, and she embraced and inspired us with her love of beauty. Our Club's deepest sympathy is given to her very dear family. President, Joanne De Palma
        LEE-Sarah Tomerlin. The Board and staff of the Landmarks Conservancy mourn the death of one of our founders and longest-serving Board members. She was a constant source of inspiration and support and contributed greatly to the preservation of the City she loved. Our deepest sympathy to her family. Stuart Siegel, Chairman Peg Breen, President
        LEE-Sarah. The ASID NY Metro Chapter and Education Legacy Fund mourn the passing of a distinguished chapter member of the Interior Design Industry. Condolences to Mrs. Lee's family.

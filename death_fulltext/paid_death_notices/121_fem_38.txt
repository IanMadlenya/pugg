
        HERZIG-Margot. The Board of Trustees, Administration and Medical Staff of the Hospital for Joint Diseases Orthopaedic Institute mourns the passing of Margot Herzig, a remarkable lady, distinguished Trustee, committed friend and loyal benefactor of the hospital. Margot leaves a profound void at HJD. Her lifetime of dedicated work, encourgement and philanthropic support of our major intiatives will always be remembered. To her children Jane Godsick, John Roten, Alan Herzig, and the late Donald Herzig, her grandchildren and great-grandchildren, we extend our heartfelt condolences. William A. Perlmuth, Chairman of the Board Victor H. Frankel, M.D., Ph.D., President John N. Kastanis, FACHE, Chief Executive Officer Joseph D. Zuckerman, M.D., Surgeon-in-Chief
        HERZIG-Margot. The Auxiliary of The Hospital for Joint Diseases Orthopaedic Institute records with deep sorrow the passing of Margot Herzig. Margot served as former Chairman of the Women's Division with dedication, wisdom and leadership. She was an inspiration to us all and will be sorely missed. We extend our deepest sympathy to her entire family. Ginny Samuels, Chairperson, Auxiliary
        HERZIG-Margot Roten. Congregation Emanu-El of the City of New York records with deep sorrow the death of our cherished member, Margot Roten Herzig. To her family and loved ones, we express our profound sympathy. May beautiful memory bring comfort to those who mourn her passing. David E. Marrus, President Herbert C. Bernard, Secretary
        HERZIG-Margot. The Officers, Board of Directors and staff of Ramapo Anchorage Camp sorrowfully mourn the passing of their beloved member, Margot Herzig, who, for more than forty years, was dedicated to this camp for troubled children. We extend our heartfelt sympathies to her family. Nina P. Freedman, Pres. Bernard Kosberg, Exec. Dir.

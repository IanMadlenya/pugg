
        SAMMONS-Robert Lee. Died peacefully on January 20, 2000, age 74, after a long illness. Survived by his sister, Lucille Schelich of Celina, OH, nephews Robert and Tom Schelich and niece Jackie Colburn. A resident of Manhattan for over fifty years, Bob was best known for his uncanny sense of humor and his love of music and the written word, the theater and the arts, and for travel. He was Travel Editor of Town and Country Magazine in the '60's and early '70's and later Senior Travel Editor for East/West Network. He was an active member of the New York Travel Writers Association, of which he served as President, and of the Society of American Travel Writers. A 1949 graduate of Ohio State Univ., he was a member of the U.S. Air Force from 1943-45 and flew 34 combat missions as a tail gunner and was honorably discharged as Staff Sergeant. Bob's family and friends will celebrate his life at a gathering in Manhattan in late spring/summer. Contributions in his name to the Central Park Conservancy are appreciated.

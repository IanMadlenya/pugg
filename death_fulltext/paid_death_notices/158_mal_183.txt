
        YANIS-David. Retired partner in Hewitt Associates, died on March 12, 2000 in Boca Raton, Florida. A graduate of The Bronx High School of Science, and The City College of New York, he became a partner at Hewitt in 1975. Mr. Yanis was a member of ADATH OR, a Jewish Renewal Temple in Fort Lauderdale, FL. A chess player and Bronze Life Master in bridge, Mr. Yanis was also a highly competitive tennis player who served as President of The Wildwood Tennis Club in Great Neck, NY. The author of two bridge pamphlets, Mr. Yanis also wrote a book ''From Yanoshevsky To Yanis'', describing his parents' immigration from Russia. He was a man of adventurous and independent spirit who always strove to realize his dreams. He is survived by his wife, Kathleen Leonard, his son, Steven, a daughter, Elaine Bennett, two stepchildren, David Leonard, and Elizabeth Leonard, four grandchildren, Michael and Jillian Bennett, and Sophia and Jared Yanis. He will also be missed by many friends worldwide. Funeral services will be held on Wednesday March 15, 2000 at 10 A.M. at Temple Kol Ami in White Plains, NY. Memorials may be made to The Dave Yanis Scholarship Fund, C/O Community Foundation, 324 Datura Street, Suite 340, West Palm Beach, Florida 33401, 561-659-6800.

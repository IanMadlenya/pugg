
        KOFFLER--Joseph H., Professor of Law, Emeritus at New York Law School. The trustees, dean, faculty, students, staff, and alumni of New York Law School mourn the death of Professor Joseph Koffler who passed away after a long battle with Parkinsons Disease on November 13. Professor Koffler, who was 85, taught at New York Law School for more than fifty years, until he became Emeritus Professor and retired fully in 2003. A 1934 graduate of the City College of New York, he earned his law degree from Harvard Law School in 1948. In 2001, he was honored with a Special Trustees Award from New York Law School, in recognition for his many years of teaching and scholarship. A funeral service is scheduled for 3:00 pm on Thursday, November 16th, at the Leo P. Gallagher & Son Funeral Home in Stamford, Connecticut. Burial will be at Congregation Agudath Shalom Cemetery in Stamford. He is survived by his wife, Monica, his daughter Lynne, and three sisters. Donations should be made to the Joseph H. Koffler Scholarship Fund at New York Law School or to the Parkinsons Disease Foundation. Arthur N. Abbey, Chair of the Board of Trustees, Richard A. Matasar, Dean and President

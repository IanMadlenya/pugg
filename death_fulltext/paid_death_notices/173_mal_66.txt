
        BOYCE-Gerard R. 46, died on June 4, 2001 at his home in Franklin Lakes, NJ. He is survived by his beloved wife, Anna, and three children, Lauren (10), Aidan (2), and Garrett (8 months), and two sisters, Peggy Louie Chaplin and Veronica Gunnerson. Admitted to the bar in 1983, Jerry was a partner at Brobeck, Phleger & Harrison, LLP, resident in the firm's Manhattan office, concentrating his practice in tax and corporate law. He was a columnist in corporate finance for the New York Law Journal and Lecturer in limited liability company law for the New York State Bar Association. Educated at Fairleigh Dickenson University (B.S., Cum Laude, 1976), Seton Hall University (J.D., Cum Laude, 1983) and New York University (LLM in Tax, 1987). He was a member of the Association of the Bar of the City of New York and the New York State and American Bar Associations. Jerry was a New Jersey Certified Public Accountant (1978), and a member of The American Institute of Certified Public Accountants. He will be remembered for his boyish innocence and love of life. He was a mentor, a true friend and a philosopher. He framed his family's motto, ''Do The Right Thing'', and lived his life by it. In Jerry's honor, donations may be made to The Gerard R. Boyce Memorial Racing Fund, in care of Mark Limardo, Esq., Jenkins, Gilchrist, Parker, Chapin LLP, 405 Lexington Avenue, New York, NY 10174.
        BOYCE-Gerard. The members, attorneys and staff of Brown Raysman Millstein Felder & Steiner LLP sadly record the untimely loss of our esteemed former partner, Gerard Boyce. Jerry's drive, can do attitude, unflagging good nature and talent as a practitioner contributed immeasurably to the growth of our firm. We extend heartfelt sympathy to his wife, our dear friend Anna, and their children, and wish them the brilliant future he intended to provide. Brown Raysman Millstein Felder & Steiner LLP

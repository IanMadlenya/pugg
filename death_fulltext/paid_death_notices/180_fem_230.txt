
        GOLDSTEIN-Sylvia. A Board member of the American Music Center and a former corporate vice president of Boosey & Hawkes, died at her home on January 21, 2002. She was 82. Goldstein's career included the support and nurturing of generations of composers from Copland to Reich, and the mentoring of many executives in the field. Composer/ author Ned Rorem devoted a chapter of his 1996 book ''Other Entertainment'' to her. Born in New York City on February 21, 1919 she received an undergraduate degree in economics from Long Island University in 1941. She began working for Ralph Hawkes of Boosey & Hawkes shortly after graduating. Encouraged by Hawkes, she graduated from Brooklyn Law School in 1944. After her retirement from Boosey & Hawkes in 1993 she held the title of corporate vice president for several years. She also maintained an active role on the Board of Directors of the American Music Center. In January 2002, AMC bestowed Sylvia Goldstein with its Letter of Distinction. The award was presented to her ''in recognition of her 50-plus years of remarkable service in support of composers and the field of new music''.

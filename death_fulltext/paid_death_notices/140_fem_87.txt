
        RAYMOND-Mrs. Henrietta Dana Skinner. Historian, teacher and author, died at the Dunroven Health Care Center in Cresskill, NJ. She was 84 years old. Mrs. Raymond, known as Penny, earned her Master of Arts and Master of Philosophy degrees at Columbia University. She later published her thesis on Sophia Willard Dana Ripley, co-founder of Brook Farm. After teaching at Hunter and Rollins Colleges and the University of Southern Maine, she volunteered in VISTA, teaching Native Americans in Utah. She also volunteered at Jobs for Youth, preparing young adults for the workplace and at Saint Matthew's Learning Center in Manhattan, tutoring children with learning difficulties. She received awards from the City of New York and the N.A.A.C.P. for her volunteer efforts. A former resident of York Harbor, Maine, she was also an active member of the Colonial Dames of America where she served on the book awards committee. Mrs. Raymond was the widow of Professor Irving Woodworth Raymond who taught medieval history at Columbia College of Arts and Sciences and Brooklyn College. She is survived by five daughters: Henrietta Dana Raymond of Boynton Beach, FL; Dr. Elizabeth Bailey of Haddonfield, NJ; Mary Carrington Salpukas of Tenafly, NJ; Margaret Hill Foudy of Dumont, NJ; and Marion Woodworth Bestani of Charlestown, MA; a sister, Eleanora Carroll Calderon of Alexandria, VA; 13 grandchildren and 13 greatgrandchildren. Visitation will be at Barrett Funeral Home, 148 Dean Drive, Tenafly, NJ on September 14 from 6-9 P.M. A funeral mass will take place on September 15 at 9:30 A.M. at St. Therese's Church, Madison Avenue, Cresskill, NJ. In lieu of flowers, donations may be made to Habitat for Humanity.

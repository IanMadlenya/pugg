
        WEISBERG-Howard Beck, 77. Died on Thursday, March 12, 1998. Born on November 21st, 1920, in Buffalo, New York. He was preceded in death by his wife of 44 years, Lillian Poltorak Weisberg, parents, Nathan and Cecilia Weisberg, brother Sheldon Weisberg, and sister, Sally Weisberg Alperin. He is survived by his 3 children, Caryl Ben Basat of Weston, FL., Barbara Weisberg Tennison of Austin, TX., Jonathan Howard Weisberg of Dallas, TX., grandson Avi Ben Basat of Weston, FL, and sister Barbara Whiting of New York, NY. Dr. Weisberg graduated from the Nichols School in Buffalo, N.Y. in 1937. After attending the University of Michigan for two years, he enlisted in the American Field Service in 1940 and served with the British Eighth Army under General Montgomery in North Africa until 1942. He then joined the U.S. Air Force and served as a gunnery drill instructor until the end of W.W. II. At the conclusion of the war, he returned to Buffalo, where he married Lillian Poltorak on Dec. 26, 1953. He attended the University of Buffalo, earning a Bachelor's and a Master's Degree in Physiology. In 1960, he earned a PhD. in Physiology from the University of Buffalo. He devoted his life to medical research and helping others. He worked first for the University of Chicago Medical School from 1960 until 1965, and then joined the American Heart Association at their national headquarters in New York. He moved to Dallas in 1975, when the Heart Association relocated its headquarters there. He served as Director of Research with the American Heart Association from 1975 until he retired in 1985. Following his retirement from the American Heart Association, he returned to the University of Chicago Medical School until 1986. He had a quick wit and a keen intelligence, and loved people, art and literature. He was a loving and devoted father, husband, brother and friend and will be deeply missed. A memorial service will be held at Temple Emanu-El in Dallas on Friday, March 20, 1998. Condolences can be sent to 7215 Royal Lane, Dallas, Texas 75230. Donations may be made to the American Heart Association.

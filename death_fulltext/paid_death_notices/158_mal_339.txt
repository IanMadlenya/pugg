
        ANDON-Ashod J., 75, on March 28, 2000 at his home in Vero Beach, Florida. Born in Pelham, New York on December 15, 1924, Mr. Andon grew up there and graduated from Pelham Memorial High School in 1943. He graduated with honors from Yale University in 1946. In 1949, at age 24, he founded TIC Gums Incorporated in New York City and became the world's leading authority on agricultural hydrocolloids. He served as an advisor to such nations as India, Pakistan, Spain, Sudan, and Turkey. Mr. Andon also founded and served as President of the Treasure Coast Jazz Society in Vero Beach. An avid yachtsman and off-shore fisherman, he was a member of the New York Yacht Club, Yale Club of New York, Moorings Club, Moorings Yacht Squadron, and The Yale Club of the Treasure Coast, of which he was a past President. He is survived by his wife of 53 years, the former Jean Hamilton Eisele, and three children: Christopher E. Andon of Bel Air, MD, Stephen A. Andon of Havre de Grace, MD, and Susan A. McKay of Wellesley, MA. Also by 10 grandchildren and a brother, Arma E. Andon of Westhampton Beach, NY and a sister, Anais A. Corsar of Pelham, NY. A memorial service will be held on Friday, March 31, 2000 at 11 AM in St. Augustine's Episcopal Church, 475 43rd Ave., Vero Beach. In lieu of flowers, memorial contributions may be made to the Treasure Coast Jazz Society, PO Box 4003, Vero Beach, FL. 32964.


        de ALESSANDRINI di NEUENSTEIN-Dr. Luigi Maria. Died on April 10th. He was 82 and lived in New York City. Born on August 3, 1918, in Genoa, Italy. He graduated from the University of Genoa's Medical School on June 18, 1943 and received his OBGYN specialty on November 16, 1949. He served as Lt. Col. Medical Doctor during World War II in the Italian Marines. He was Health Immigration Officer from 1948-49. His interest in exploring life and seeing the world brought him to Uruguay where he met, fell in love with and married Alfa Morales on April 26, 1950. They lived in Portofino, Italy, where he practiced medicine. He was Assistant Professor of OBGYN at the University of Genoa until 1957. On April 21, 1957, he came to the US and established a private medical practice in Virginia and was on the Boards of Norfolk General and De Paul Hospitals. During his career, he also worked at Mount Vernon, Riverside and Eastern State Hospitals, where he received a specialty in Psychiatry. On May 21, 1965, Luigi was made Vice Counsel of Italy for the State of Virginia by the Italian Ministry of Commerce and Foreign Affairs and confirmed by Dean Rusk, U.S. Secretary of State. After spending 1970-73 in Italy, he returned to Virginia in 1973. In 1978 he became Assistant Professor at the Eastern Virginia Medical School. During the 70s and 80s he assisted with clinical research in the field of OBGYN, contributing to the discovery and treatment for various venereal diseases. Between 1975-1988, he became Clinical Director of the Portsmouth Health Department and was part of the National Family Planning Board, sponsored by the White House. In recognition of his contribution to special medical education programs, he was made Fellow of The American Academy of Family Physicians in October 1983. He loved his wife dearly. They have been loving friends & companions for 51 years. Alfa survives him as do his sons, Paolo and Enrico, daughters-inlaw, Jackie and Ellen and grandchildren, Alexa and Giulio. In lieu of flowers, the family asks that donations be sent to Cabrini Hospice C/O Cabrini Medical Center, 227 East 19th Street, NY, NY, 10003.

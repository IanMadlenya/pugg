
        STABILE-Vincent A. Engineer and philanthropist, formerly of Verona, NJ, died at his home in Naples, FL on July 13. A funeral mass was held in Naples on July 17th. Burial will be August 7, 2002, at 1:30 P.M. in the St. Gertrude Cemetery, 53 Inman Avenue, Colonia, New Jersey.
        STABILE-Vincent. The faculty, staff, and officers of Columbia University Health Sciences extend our heartfelt condolences to the family and friends of Vincent Stabile of Naples, Florida, Columbia alumnus and benefactor of the Toni Stabile Osteoporosis Center. Named for his sister, the Center is dedicated to improving the care of patients with or at risk for osteoporosis through research, education, and clinical excellence. Mr. Stabile is survived by his beloved sister, Toni. Gerald Fischbach, M.D. Executive Vice President for Health and Biomedical Sciences and Dean of the Faculty of Medicine Ethel S. Siris, M.D. Madeline C. Stabile, Prof. of Clinical Medicine Director, Toni Stabile Osteoporosis Center
        STABILE-Vincent A. The Boards, Faculty, and Staff of NewYork-Presbyterian Hospital mourn the loss of our close friend and benefactor, Vincent A. Stabile. A tireless champion of health care and medical education, he, together with his sister, Toni Stabile, had a great impact on the field of osteoporosis treatment, prevention, and research. For countless people, he made possible better care, an improved prognosis, and an enhanced quality of life. He was a kind and generous man who earned the respect of all who had the privilege of knowing him. We extend our deepest sympathies to his sister, Toni. Herbert Pardes, M.D. President and CEO NewYork-Presbyterian Hospital

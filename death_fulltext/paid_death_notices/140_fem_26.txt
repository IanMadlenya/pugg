
        SPAETH-Eloise O'Mara. Of East Hampton, NY on September 4, 1998. Wife of the late Otto. Much beloved mother of Otto L. Spaeth, Jr.(Tony), Mary Louise Koon of St. Louis, and the late Marna Doherty and Deborah Shakespeare Green. Grandmother of sixteen. Great grandmother of 15. Mass of Christian Burial at Most Holy Trinity Church, Buell Lane, East Hampton on Friday, September 11 at 11 o'clock. In lieu of flowers, contributions may be sent to East End Hospice, PO Box 1048, Westhampton Beach, NY 11978 or to the Eloise Spaeth Collection Preservation Fund at Guild Hall in East Hampton.
        SPAETH-Eloise. The Board of Trustees of Guild Hall Museum mourn the loss of Eloise Spaeth, a singularly distinguished benefactor, honorary life trustee and Venerated Museum Committee member, who was largely responsible for the emergence of Guild Hall Museum as a force in 20th century American art. We extend our deepest sympathy to her family. Henry Korn, President Melville Straus, Chairman, Board of Trustees Michael Lynne, Chairman, Museum Committee
        SPAETH-Eloise O. The Board of Trustees of the Archives of American Art wishes to extend its sincere condolences to the family of Eloise O. Spaeth. As a fellow Board member and Chairman Emeritus of the Archives of American Art, she will be remembered as an empowering influence on the Archives and its mission. Her leadership will be missed and her friendship remembered. Frank Martucci, President Dr. Richard Wattenmaker, Dir. Archives of American Art

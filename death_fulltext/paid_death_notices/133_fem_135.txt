
        WILE-Grace Carlin. Of Princeton, NJ. Died suddenly in Sanibel, FL, on February 14, 1998. A graduate of Cornell University Class of 1929 with an M.A. from the Teachers College of Columbia University, Ms. Wile was a talented teacher of mathematics and later became an even more talented school administrator. She was the cherished wife of the late Louis D. Wile and is survived by her two devoted daughters, Lenore Wile May and Karen Wile Wright, and their husbands Kenneth and Richard. Devoted and adored grandmother of Diane and Shari May and Louis and Rebecca Wright, beloved sister of Rita Fremont, Estelle Kalstein and the late Benson Carlin, loving, caring aunt to her many nieces and nephews and still found time to be a docent at the Princeton Art Museum for 20 years and a tutor to many students at the International Center. She also supported a myriad of charities. Grace lived up to her nickname, ''Gracious,'' as attested to by her large circle of friends. She loved life and lived it to the hilt, loved to travel, read, play bridge, bird watch and just converse with any of her large circle of friends. She will be rememebered with great love and affection by all who knew her. Services Tuesday, February 17, 1998, at 1:30 pm, at the Princeton Jewish Center, Route 27, Princeton, NJ. Contributions in her name may be made to Princeton Public Library, Witherspoon St.
        WILE-Grace. Beth El Synagogue of New Rochelle records with sorrow the passing of the mother of our dear member, Lenore May, and mother-in-law of Honorary President, Kenneth May. Heartfelt condolences to the entire family. Melvin N. Sirner, Rabbi Dorothy Fox, President

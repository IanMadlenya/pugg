
        WALKER-Janet Hayes. Founder and producing director of the York Theatre Company, died February 20, 1997 after a long and valiant battle with cancer. Wife of Charles Dodsley Walker, mother of Peter Hayes Walker and Susan Walker Wyss, grandmother of Natalie Starr Wyss, Branford Hayes Walker and Camille Walker Wyss. Sister of Dr. E. Morris Hayes and Marian Hayes Miller. She was born in Shanghai, China in 1925 of missionary parents, and recieved a Master's degree in music from the New England Conservatory and a Master's degree in theatre from Hunter College. As a singer, she was an active recitalist, giving concerts in Europe and the U.S. Later she appeared as a singing actress, in a career which included roles in many Broadway shows and in regional theatres. In 1969 she founded the York Theatre Company, which became known particularly for its outstanding revivals of musicals, generating numerous awards both for the theatre and for Janet. Memorial services will be held at Trinity Episcopal Church, Southport, Connecticut at 1 P.M., on Tuesday, February 25, and at the Church of the Heavenly Rest, Fifth Avenue at 90th Street, at 1 PM on Thursday, February 27. A celebration of Janet's life in the arts will take place at a later date. In lieu of flowers, contributions may be made to the York Theatre Co., Inc., 619 Lexington Avenue, New York, New York 10019, where a memorial fund is being created in her honor.

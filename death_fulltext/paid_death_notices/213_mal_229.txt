
        ZIEGLER--George J. Former Chief Engineer and VP of the NYC Transit Authority for 34 yrs, died October 17, at 74. He was involved with planning, design and construction of the Billion Dollar New Routes Program including the 63rd St subway, East River tunnel, 2nd Ave subway, Jamaica's Archer Ave line, and rehab of the subway & bus systems. He received several awards including NYC's ''1987 Municipal Engineer of the Year'', NYC's Municipal Engineer's ''Alfred Brady Award-1982'', and ASCE's ''Government Civil Engineer of the Year-1982''. He loved to travel, golf & play the accordion. Survived by Janet, his wife of 48 years, three children and four grandchildren

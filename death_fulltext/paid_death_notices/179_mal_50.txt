
        CHERNEY-Paul, President and founder of Cinefot International, a photographic import-export management business until his retirement in 1987, died Decemer 3, 2001. He was 94. Born in Budapest, Hungary, he grew up in Vienna, and came to the United States in 1929. A major in the United States Air Force during World War II, he was stationed in London as a principal prisoner of war interrogator with the Combined Intelligence Objectives subcommittee of the Royal Air Force. President Emeritus of the National Association of Exporting Companies, in 1972 he received the ''E'' Award from the United States Department of Commerce for Excellence in promoting American exports. His first wife, Clare Rubin died in 1944. He is survived by his wife, Phyllis Brighten Cherney of Lenox, MA., and four daughters, Elizabeth Gordon of Sebastopol, CA; Sara Evans of New York City; Geraldine McElroy of Davidson, NC.; and Michelle Gillett of Stockbridge, MA; ten grandchildren, and three greatgrandchildren.

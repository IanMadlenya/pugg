
        GOTTESMAN-Esther G. Librarians and staff at Yeshiva University's Mendel Gottesman Library extend heartfelt condolences to children David S. and Ruth Gottesman and Milton Gottesman, grandchildren, great-grandchildren, brothers, sister, and the entire family upon the passing of a woman of dignity, creativity, and dedication to principle and community. May the education she so valued and the tradition she so cherished be transmitted to generations of young men and women through the Library which bears the Gottesman name. May the family be comforted among the mourners of Zion and Jerusalem. Pearl Berger Benjamin Gottesman Librarian Yeshiva University
        GOTTESMAN-Esther. The Jerusalem Foundation mourns the passing of one of the great leaders of The American Jewish community, an individual who worked brilliantly and tirelessly in behalf of Jewish people and the State of Israel. We offer our condolences to her family and many friends. May they be comforted among the mourners of Zion and Jerusalem. Teddy Kollek, Chairman International Council Alvin H. Einbender, Nat'l Chrmn. Michael Neiditch, Nat'l Pres. Mendel Kaplan, Int'l Chrmn. Ruth Cheshin, Int'l Pres.
        GOTTESMAN-Esther. The Abraham Joshua Heschel School community, its families and trustees mourn the loss of Esther G. Gottesman, who touched the lives of so many in the Jewish community. We extend our deepest sympathies to our trustee, Bob Gottesman, and his wife, Trudy, their children, and the entire Gottesman family on their loss. Terry Ann Krulwich, President Roanna Shorofsky, Director

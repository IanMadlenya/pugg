
        VOLZ-Hilda (Weber). On February 4 at the age of 95. Born in Mannheim, Germany, she immigrated to the U.S. in 1928, becoming a U.S. citizen in 1934. She was the widow of Hermann Volz, who reopened Chase's branches in Germany after the Second World War. Mr. Volz died in 1976. She was a community volunteer throughout her life. After moving from New York to Frankfurt, Germany with her husband in 1948, she became active in fostering German American relations. Later she became a member of the committee that awarded Fulbright scholarships to German graduate students and a Board member of the Steuben Schurz Gesellschaft. Returning to New York in the 1960s, she raised scholarship funds for the exchange of American and German graduate students. She is survived by her daughters, Anita Wien of New York, NY, and Janet Kirwan of Greenwich, CT; two grandchildren, Deborah Wild and Peter Kirwan, Jr., and two great grandchildren. Donations in her memory may be made to the Institute of International Education, Attention Quadrille Fund, Development Office, 809 UN Plaza, New York, NY 10017.


        THACKARA-James J. A retired economist and banker, died on June 24. He was 87 years old. He graduated from Harvard College in 1936 and entered banking in 1937. During World War II, he served with the US embassy in Brazil and then in the US Navy. In 1946, he was advisor to the Brazilian Delegation to the International Monetary Conference in Savannah, Georgia, and was later decorated by the Brazilian Government with the Order of the Southern Cross. He then joined the Latin American Division of the International Monetary Fund where he served as an economist until 1955. Later that year, he joined the Chase Manhattan Bank where he became Vice President in charge of the bank's branch in Paris, France. He was a member of the Board of Directors of the American Hospital in Neuilly, France. Returning to New York, he was appointed head of the bank's Middle Eastern Division. Between 1970 and 1979, he represented the Credit Lyonais, Paris, in North America. He was a member of the Board of Directors of the UBAF Arab American Bank. In 1975, he was decorated by the French Government with the National Order of Merit. He is survived by his wife, the former Shirley Ingalls of Pelham, NY, a daughter Lucy Kennedy, a son John, a son James, a brother Alexander, and four grandchildren. Services will be held at the Huguenot Memorial Church in Pelham, NY on July 7 at 11 AM. The family suggests that any memorial contributions be sent to the Carmel Valley Manor Building Fund.

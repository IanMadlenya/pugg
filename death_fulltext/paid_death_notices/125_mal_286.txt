
        MILGRAM-Morris (age 81). On June 22, 1997 at Attleboro Nursing and Rehabilitation Center in Langhorne, Pa., of a sudden stroke after 2 years of declining health. He was a pioneer developer of racially integrated housing, starting in 1952. He is survived by a daughter, Betty Milgram of Silver Spring, Md. and a son, Gene Milgram, of Lusby, Md. In keeping with his wishes for a celebration of life, a memorial service will be held on Sunday, August 17, 1 PM, at The First Unitarian Church, 2125 Chestnut St, Philadelphia, Pa. Memorial contributions may be made to the organization he founded with James Farmer in 1975, Fund for an Open Society, 311 South Juniper Street, Suite 400, Philadephia, Pa. 19107, (215) 735-6915, or to the group he headed from 1937 to 1947, The Workers Defense League, 275 7th Avenue, 15th Floor, New York, N.Y. 10001.


        CHERTKOV-Dr. Lisa. Of New York City, on May 23 at age 38. The winner of the AMES award and the Hoopes Prize, she was a 1985 magna cum laude graduate of Harvard University and a 1994 graduate of Columbia University College of Physicians and Surgeons. She completed a dual residency in Internal Medicine and Psychiatry at Columbia-Presbyterian Medical Center in 1999 and was just finishing a fellowship in Psychiatry at Memorial Sloan-Kettering Cancer Center. Her life was dedicated to ensuring that her patients received the highest quality care and she will be sorely missed-both as a care provider and advocate for change in our healthcare system. Parents Lynn and Boren, his wife Ann, brother Evan, his wife Cheryl and his daughter Taylor and sons James and Robert, grandmothers Frieda Schoonmaker and Ruth Chertkov, and many friends mourn a cherised women who lived life to its fullest and who deeply cared for all whose paths crossed hers. A light has passed from our lives and we will miss her spirit, her passion and her laughter. A memorial service will be held on Wednesday, May 30, 2001 at 12:45 PM at Riverside Memorial Chapel, 180 W. 76th St. (at Amsterdam Ave.) NYC. The interment will be private. In lieu of flowers, memorial contributions may be made to: Family Learning Solutions, 2403 Esther Court, Silver Spring, Maryland 20910, C/O Dr. Lore Melman.
        CHERTKOV-Lisa, M.D. The Department of Psychiatry & Behavioral Sciences at Memorial SloanKettering Cancer Center expresses its deep sense of grief over the untimely death of its clinical fellow Dr. Lisa Chertkov. We share with her family the loss of this highly accomplished internist and psychiatrist. She will be greatly missed. Her Friends and Colleagues in the Department of Psychiatry, Department of Pediatrics and the Bone Marrow Transplant Service


        SINGER-Herman. Editor and political analyst, died at his home in Manhattan on November 8, 2001, at the age of 86. He worked with Norman Thomas on behalf of the Socialist Party, serving as Editor of The Socialist Call from 1945 to 1958. He then joined the staff of East Europe magazine, published by Radio Free Europe (RFE), becoming Editor in the mid-1960's. He was also active in the New York Newspaper Guild, and was for several years elected the National Delegate for the RFE Unit. He was a contributing editor to Dissent magazine and a freelance contributor to other national journals. In later years, he became Editor of the MDA News, published by the Muscular Dystrophy Association. He was a writer and person of depth, charm, compassion and courage, and is greatly missed by his family and friends. He is survived by his sisters, Sylvia Singer and Frieda Singer-Shapiro, of New York; his daughter, Judith Singer Sam, of Arcadia, California; and his grandchildren, Michael and Carolyn.

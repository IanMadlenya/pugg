
        STRAUSS-Edward M. Jr. Of New York and Buck Hill Falls, Pa., died on June 21, 1997, at home in New York, after a long illness at age 79. Survived by his wife of fifty years, Dorothy R. Strauss; his son, Edward M. Strauss III, of New York his daughter, Cynthia L. Strauss, of Dallas, Texas, his brother, Willis A. Strauss, of Omaha, Ne., and his granddaughters Louisa Ryder Strauss and Eleanor Kirtland Strauss, of New York. Born in Omaha, Mr. Strauss was a 1940 engineering graduate of Iowa State University. After a forty year career with the Aluminium Company of America (Alcoa) in Baltimore, Fairfield, Connecticut, Pittsburgh, Philadelphia, and Lausanne, Switzerland, he was President of the Business Committee for the Arts, New York, from 1979 to 1982. He was President of the Swiss-American Chamber of Commerce in 1978-1979, and was a founder of the American Council for Jean Monnet Studies. Services are at All Saints Episcopal Church, 230 East 60th Street, on Friday, June 27 at 11 A.M. In lieu of flowers, contributions to All Saints Church would be appreciated.


        HIRSCH-Joseph Samuel of Manhattan. On April 23 at age 92. He left Germany in 1934 for what was the British mandate of Palestine, bringing his parents, niece and nephew to safety. A ships carpenter, he came to the U.S. in 1943 and saw action in World War II when his freighter was torpedoed during the Murmansk Run and bombed off Sicily. Retired in 1968, he remained vigorous until recently. He is survived by Belle, his wife of 55 years, sons Michael of Manhattan and Mark of Arlington, Va. and grandchildren Johanna, Matthew, and Emma. In lieu of flowers donations to Hebrew Tabernacle Congregation, 551 Ft. Washington Ave., NYC 10033, are appreciated.

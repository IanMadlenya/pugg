
        JOHNSON-Beverley Peck. The Juilliard School mourns the death of Beverley Peck Johnson, esteemed member of the faculty since 1964. As one of the most distinguished voice teachers of the 20th Century, with dignity, grace and remarkable focus, Beverley developed generations of singers who have had an enormously positive impact on the profession. Her loving and caring ways have endeared her to her many students and colleagues. She will be deeply missed by all members of the Juilliard community. A memorial tribute will take place on February 5, 2001, at 5 PM in the Juilliard Theatre. Mary Rodgers Guettel Chairman Joseph W. Polisi President
        JOHNSON-Beverley Peck. The Manhattan School of Music is saddened by the loss of former faculty member, Beverley Peck Johnson, a renowned member of our voice faculty from 1982 to 1989. Her intellect, energy and compassion enriched the lives of her many students. Beverley's invaluable warmth and giving will be sorely missed by all who knew her. David A. Rahm, Chairman, Board of Trustees Marta Istomin, President


        RICHLAND--W. Bernard. August 14, 2003. Born in Liverpool, England, March 25, 1909. Predeceased by his wife Pauline and seven brothers and sisters. Survived by his sister Gladys Sarony Frye, his beloved and adoring family, daughters Robin Silverton and Lisa Richland, grandchildren Seth Silverton, Alexandra Uhll and Rafael Maciejak, great-grandchildren A.J. and Emma Silverton, Hannah and Jack Richland Uhll and his beloved and devoted companion Barbara Carroll Weiler. Mr. Richland was associated with Judge Samuel Seabury from 1925-1943, and was admitted to the Bar in 1937. He was the Chief of the Opinions and Legislation Division of the New York City Law Department and Acting Corporation Counsel from 1943-1958, New York City's Corporation Counsel from 1975-1977. He was an expert in municipal government, legislation and regulations, and he lectured and litigated in these fields extensively. He drafted the New York City Charter, adopted in 1961, and was Commissioner, New York City Charter Revision Commission, 1973-1975. He was Special Master for Appeals (Agent Orange Administration) U.S. District Court Eastern District New York from 1988-1996. Mr. Richland was Adjunct Professor of local government law, New York Law School from 1978-1988. His book, ''You CAN Beat City Hall'' was published in 1980. In private practice he was partner in the law firm Baer Marks and counsel to the firm of O'Dwyer and Bernstein and to Botein Hays Sklar. Mr. Richland was mentor to many young lawyers, giving them his time, counsel and friendship with enthusiasm. He took pleasure and satisfaction from their subsequent success, and remembered each one fondly. His family and friends mourn his passing but celebrate his long, productive and happy life. A memorial service will be held at Frank E. Campbell, The Funeral Chapel, 1076 Madison Avenue, NY, on Monday, August 18, at 3:30 p.m. Burial private. In lieu of flowers donations may be made to the Metropolitan Hospice of Greater New York, 6323 Seventh Avenue, Brooklyn, NY 11220-4711.

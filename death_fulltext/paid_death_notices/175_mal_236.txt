
        HARRISON-John T. Jr., died of a stroke August 22nd at his home in Essex, Connecticut. He was 89 years old. Born in Corona, Long Island, he was a resident of New York City for most of his life. He attended the Allen Stevenson School, graduated from St. Paul's School in Concord, N.H., in 1931 and from Yale University in 1935. After training for two years with the Insurance Company of North America, he entered his father's firm of Flynn, Harrison and Conroy, Insurance Brokers, where he remained until he joined the Navy in 1942 as a commissioned officer rising to the rank of Lieutenant Commander. After the War, he resumed his career as an insurance broker, becoming a general partner of Flynn, Harrison and Conroy in 1952. He served as Director of the Insurance Brokers Association of the State of New York from 1955 to 1974 and was President of the Association from 1962 to 1964. He also was Treasurer of the National Association of Insurance Brokers from 1959 until 1966. Following the merger of his firm with Marsh and McLennan in 1966, he was appointed Vice President of that company where he remained until his retirement in 1974. He was a member of the vestry of the Church of the Heavenly Rest and President of the Heavenly Rest Day School. He was also a Trustee of St. John's College in Annapolis, Maryland, and Santa Fe, New Mexico. In 1980, he became a Trustee of the Bennington Museum in Bennington, Vermont, and he subsequently served as Chairman of the Museum's Board of Trustees from 1990-1993. He is survived by Patricia Wood, his wife of sixty-three years, and four children, Frederick Wood Harrison, Virginia Harrison Berstene, John T. Harrison, III, and Daniel Hall Harrison. He was a loving and kind husband and father, as well as a devoted grandfather of six. His funeral service will be held at St. James Church, Arlington, Vermont, on August 26th at 3 o'clock. In lieu of flowers his family encourages a contribution in his memory to the Bennington Museum.

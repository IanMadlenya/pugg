
        WORCESTOR-Dean Kirkham, Jr. Of New York City, July 15, 2000, age 77. Loving father of Charles L. of Medford, MA, Edward D. of Seattle, WA., and Amanda W. Swinchoski of Watertown, MA. Grandfather of James and Louisa Swinchoski. Also survived by sisters Shirley Braun of New York City and Sue Light of Suffield, CT. Former husband of Barbara Pennypacker Worcestor of New York City. Graduated from Brooks School, North Andover, MA, 1940. He served in North Africa and Italy from 1942-1945 in the Army Signal Corps. After receiving his BA from Yale in 1947, Dean attended Cambridge University, England (Peterhouse) where he received his Doctorate in History and was awarded one of the first Fullbright Scholarships. His Doctoral Thesis examined a diary of an English villager in the mid 1700's and was later basis for the book, ''The Diary of Thomas Turner'', edited by David Vaisey. Dean worked for Doubleday & Co. and Banker Trust; member of the Blue Hill Troupe and Canterbury Choral Society. He will be greatly missed by family and friends. Memorial service at the Church of the Heavenly Rest will be held at a later date. Memorial donations may be made to: New York Public Library, 5th Avenue and 40 St, NY, NY 10018.


        PAPE--Irving E. Passed away on April 30, 2005, in Hackensack, NJ, surrounded by his loving family. He died as he lived, with dignity, a fighter to the last. He leaves behind his cherished wife of 57 years Doris (Subin); three sons, Stuart and his wife Ginger of Washington, DC, Howard and his wife Karen of Earlysville, VA; Neil of Winter Park, FL; and four grandchildren, Jeremy, Evan, Samuel and Sarah. He was born on July 28, 1921, in Waterbury, CT, the son of Harry and Edith. Raised in East Orange, he graduated from Rutgers University in 1943. He served in the US Army in Europe, helped to liberate Paris, and was awarded four battle stars. During his long tenure as correspondent for the Class of 1943, he authored a poem in honor of his classmates who died in WWII. This poem, ''The Groves of Remembrance,'' is the centerpiece of a memorial on the Rutgers campus. In 1944, he was honored by being elected to the Loyal Sons of Rutgers. The family requests that donations in his memory be made to The Building Goodness Foundation, P.O. Box 2246, Charlottesville, VA 22902. Services will be held on Monday, at 1:30 pm.

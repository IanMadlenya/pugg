
        DECHTER-Martin. For many years a well known and respected leader of the children's wear industry and an active participant in Jewish and civic philanthropies, died Saturday, May 3, at Mt. Sinai Hospital in Miami Beach, FL. He was 92. Arriving in the United States at age 16 from Odessa, Russia, Mr. Dechter went to Columbia University at night while working during the day to help support his family. In 1936, Martin Dechter along with Henry Riegel, founded Riegel & Dechter which became a leading manufacturer of children's clothing in the United States. A pioneer in the children's wear industry for over three decades, Riegel and Dechter's Fashions were featured in every leading department store in America. Founder of the B'Nai Brith chapter in Borough Park, Brooklyn and its president for many years, an active member of the AntiDefamation League, he was also recognized by the American Jewish Committee for his work on their behalf industry-wide. Mr. Dechter is survived by his wife of 66 years, Helen, his brother and sister, a daughter and a son, three grandchildren and three great grandchildren. Memorial services will be held at The Riverside Memorial Chapel, 180 West 76th Street, on May 8th at 1:30 PM.


        CHILL-Emanuel S., Professor Emeritus of History, City College, NY, died on November 13 in West Hartford, CT of complications from Parkinson's Disease. He was 76. After graduating from Columbia University, he attended Oxford University and joined the history faculty of City College in 1962. His book ''Power, Property and History, Joseph Barnave's Introduction To The French Revolution,'' as well as other publications, are valuable contributions to American studies of The French Revolution. Professor Chill served with honor in The Armed Forces during World War II. He is survived by his wife Philippa Wehle, his son Paul Chill and wife Brigid Donohue, his daughters, Carol Adinolfi and Nina Chill, and grandchildren, Madeleine, Camille, and Declan.

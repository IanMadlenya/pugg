
        GREENFIELD -- Harriet Copelin. Formerly of Hartland, VT and New York City, died Friday, November 25, 2005 in Palm Beach Gardens, FL, after a long illness. She was 82 years old. She is survived by her five children; Juliet G. Six & Faith G. Lewis both of Washington, DC, Gordon K. Greenfield Jr. of Norwich, VT, Hope Greenfield of Oldwick, NJ, and James D. Greenfield of New York City; by a brother, Donald E. Copelin of Ketchum, ID; and by 8 grandchildren. Her husband of 60 years, Gordon K. Greenfield, died this past January. Harriet Greenfield was best known for her dedication to charitable causes, especially those involving children. In 1995 she received the Helenka Pantaleoni Award from the U.S. Committee for UNICEF for her work as Founder and CoChair of the New York City Chapter. She served on the Board of the Youth Counseling League and the World Adoption International Fund (WAIF). In the 1980's she was Chairman of the Women's Division of The New York Chapter of the Arthritis Foundation. She joined the staff of The National Red Cross in 1984 to develop their Jr. Committee where she earned The Steuben Apple Award. She also joined her husband as a patron of music in New York, as a key fundraiser for the Opera Orchestra Society. Additionally, she served as President of the Board of Immigration and Refugee Services of America (IRSA), and its subsidiary, The U.S. Committee for Refugees (USCR), from 1993-1997, and on its Board from 1989 to 2001. In Vermont, she served on the Boards of Historic Windsor and Lebanon College, Lebanon, NH. Harriet Greenfield, born in Philadelphia, was a graduate of the University of Pennsylvania, 1943. A burial service will be held in December in Hartland, VT. Please send donations to Hospice of Palm Beach County, Florida.

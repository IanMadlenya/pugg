
        ANDERSEN--William G. Jr., retired Executive Director of the Episcopal Church Foundation, died peacefully at home on October 20th. He encouraged innovation in leadership formation and resource development as ways of building new capacities among Episcopalians at a time of unprecedented change in religious life. Initiatives launched under his guidance are reshaping mission and ministry in spheres of influence that extend into the whole Anglican Communion. A graduate of Cornell University (BSc) and New York University (MPA), he was conferred with an honorary degree, Doctor of Humane Letters, from Berkeley Divinity School, Yale University in 2005 for his dedicated work and service. He is survived by his loving wife Lydia E. Andersen, his daughter Susan Andersen-Earley of Barrington, RI, his son John Eric Andersen of Califon, NJ, and his daughter, Kristin Chamorro of Maplewood, NJ, and his seven grandchildren. A memorial service will be held at St. George's Church, 550 Ridgewood Rd., Maplewood, NJ on Saturday, October 28th, 2006 at 3:00PM.

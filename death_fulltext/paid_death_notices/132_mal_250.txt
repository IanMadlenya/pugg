
        MURPHY-Joseph. The Trustees, Faculty and Staff of The Mount Sinai Medical Center are deeply saddened by the death of Joseph Murphy, former Chancellor of the City University of New York. A respected educator and humanitarian, Professor Murphy was an earnest supporter of Mount Sinai School of Medicine and its mission of expanding and disseminating medical knowledge, both during his tenure as Chancellor and in the years that followed. We extend our sincere sympathy to his wife, children, grandchildren, mother and to the entire family. Stephen M. Peck, Chairman John W. Rowe, M.D., President Arthur H. Rubenstein, MBBCh Dean Barry R. Freedman, Executive Vice-President Wendy Z. Goldstein, Director
        MURPHY-Joseph S. The Board of Directors of the YIVO Institute for Jewish Research and the faculty of YIVO's Max Weinreich Center for Advanced Jewish Studies mourn the tragic and untimely passing of our treasured Board member and esteemed academic colleague, Professor Joseph S. Murphy. He will be remembered for his lifelong commitment to the fundamental principles of Yiddishkayt: the furtherance of education and the tireless pursuit of social justice. All honor to his memory. Bruce Slovin, Chairman Dr. Allan Nadler, Dir. of Research YIVO Institute for Jewish Research
        MURPHY-Joseph S. The City University of New York deeply mourns the tragic and untimely death in Ethiopia of our esteemed former Chancellor, College President and University Professor Joseph S. Murphy. He was a leading educator, humanitarian, activist, and champion of access to education. We extend our deepest condolences to his family. He will be sorely missed. Anne A. Paolucci, Chairwoman, Board of Trustees Christoph M. Kimmich, Interim Chancellor

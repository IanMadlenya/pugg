
        MERRITT-Francis Sumner, 87. Died Dec. 27, 2000. An innovative educator and visual artist, who as founding director of the Haystack Mountain School of Crafts, Deer Isle, ME, from 1951-1977, created an international community of craftmakers. A painter and print maker, his work was exhibited at the Metropolitan Museum of Art, Museum of Modern Art and The Corcoran Gallery. Among his many honors he was a fellow of the American Craft Council and The Royal Society of Art. Survived by his wife Priscilla of Belfast, ME, his brother, The Reverend Richard A. Merritt of Nagoya, Japan, two sons, Dr. Alfred M. Merritt of Gainesville, FL, and Steven H. Merritt of St. Isadore, Ontario, Canada, six grandchildren and 7 great-grandchildren. A celebration of his life will take place at the Haystack in Deer Isle, ME, next summer. Memorial contributions may be sent to Haystack, P.O. Box 518, Deer Isle, ME. 04627.

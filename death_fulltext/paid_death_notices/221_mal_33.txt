
        DAVOL--Rector Thomson, M.D. Dr. Davol, 90, of Greenwich, CT died in Greenwich May 29, 2005. The cause of death was cardio-respiratory arrest. He was born in Brooklyn, NY on May 21, 1915 and moved to Stamford, CT in 1924. Dr. Davol was a graduate of Yale, Class of 1937 and received his M.D. from Columbia University College of Physicians & Surgeons in 1941. He interned at Bellevue and completed a residency in otolaryngology at New York Eye and Ear Infirmary. He was a Lieutenant in the Navy Medical Corps during WW II and served in the Pacific. After his discharge in 1946, he was an Instructor in Otology at NYU. He moved to Greenwich to practice otolaryngology. He was Director of the Department of Otolaryngology at Greenwich Hospital, a member of the AMA, President of the Greenwich Medical Society, and President of the Fairfield County Medical Society. He was a Diplomate of the American Board of Otolaryngology. Dr. Davol is predeceased by two sons, John Davol and Peter Davol. His widow Anne survives, as do two daughters, Gaynor Davol Casner of Concord, MA and Dr. Joan Davol Popkin of Ridgewood, NJ, and a son Ward Miller Davol of Greenwich, CT as well as fifteen grandchildren and nine great grandchildren. There will be a memorial service on Thursday, June 9 at 12 noon at the Round Hill Community Church in Greenwich. The family asks that donations be made to Yale Alumni Association, Class of 1937 or Greenwich Hospital.

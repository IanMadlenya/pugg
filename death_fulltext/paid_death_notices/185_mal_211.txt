
        MURPHY-John A. Philip Morris Companies, Inc. notes with sorrow the death of retired Philip Morris executive and director, John Murphy, who during his 30 year career played an enormous role in the growth and development of the company. Mr. Murphy served as a member of the Boards of Directors from 1971 until 1992. He retired in 1992 as vice chairman of the company, a post he had held since 1991. Mr. Murphy served as president of the company from 1984 to 1991. He also served as group executive vice president responsible for Miller Brewing Company and two other Philip Morris subsidiaries; group executive vice president of Philip Morris Incorporated; chairman and chief executive officer of Miller Brewing Company; executive vice president of Philip Morris Incorporated; and president and chief executive officer of Miller Brewing Company. He was elected a vice president of Philip Morris Incorporated in August 1967, and an executive vice president in August 1976. Before his election as president and chief executive officer of Miller, he was assistant to the president and executive vice president of Philip Morris International. Under his stewardship, Philip Morris became the largest American tobacco company in Europe, Africa and Latin America. Mr. Murphy joined Philip Morris in 1962 as assistant general counsel. The Philip Morris Board and employees pay warm tribute to his memory and extend their deepest sympathy to his wife, Carole; his children, John, Kevin, Robert, Timothy, Kellyann, and Kathleen; twenty grandchildren; and his extended family. A wake will be held Wednesday, June 19, 2002, from 6pm to 9pm at Whitting Funeral Home, 300 Glen Cove Ave, Glen Head, NY 11545. The funeral Mass will be Thursday, June 20, 2002 at 10am at St. Paul the Apostle R.C. Church, 2534 Cedar Swamp Rd (Rte 107), Brookville, NY 11545.


        HEMPHILL-Herbert Waide., Jr. We mourn the passing of a very special person, Herbert Waide Hemphill, Jr., beloved son of the late Emma Bryan Bradley Hemphill and the late Herbert Waide Hemphill, Sr. Born in Atlantic City, NJ, January 21, 1929, died in New York City, NY, May 8, 1998. Curator and collector of American Folk Art of the 18th, 19th and 20th centuries, his insights contributed greatly to the growing awareness of art ''of the people, by the people, for the people.'' A memorial tribute will be held at a future date.
        HEMPHILL-Herbert Waide, Jr. The Board of Trustees and professional staff of the Museum of American Folk Art record with profound sorrow the death of their friend & colleague, Herbert Waide Hemphill, Jr., a founder of the Museum in 1961, its first curator from 1964 to 1973, and Trustee Emeritus for many years. Bert will be remembered by us with respect and affection for his passion as a collector, his wide-ranging influence as a tastemaker and the significance of his generous contributions to the Museum's permanent collection. A person of quiet dignity and modesty, he has left a legacy of enduring importance to American art and culture. Ralph O. Esmerian, President Board of Trustees Gerard C. Wertkin, Director

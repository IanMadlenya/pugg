
        JOHNSTON -- Alistair. Of Riverside, CT, and Rancho Santa Fe, CA, died suddenly at his home in California on August 19. He was born in Glasgow, Scotland on June 7, 1937 and, after service in the Highland Light Infantry, began a career in Reinsurance at Lloyds of London. He moved to the U.S. in 1961 to work for General Reinsurance where he became a vice president as well as president of Herbert Clough, a General Re subsidiary until he retired in 1997. An avid golfer, fly fisherman, and bicyclist as well as wine enthusiast, he also served on the Board of Directors of the nonprofit agency FIRST and as secretary of a family foundation. He was a devoted father and thrilled to be the grandfather of six. Sadly, he was predeceased by a son, Cameron in March. He is survived by two other children, Andrew and Rosemary; his wife of twenty-one years, Penelope; his sister, Elspeth Clark; two step daughters, Sheila Dietz and Katherine Penna; and his former wife, Melissa MacKenzie. A memorial service will be held September 10, at 4 p.m. at The Recital Hall, The Performing Arts Center, Purchase College, 735 Anderson Hill Rd., Purchase, NY. Contributions may be made in his memory to The Alliance of Information and Referral Systems 211, 11240 Waples Mill Rd., Suite 200, Fairfax, VA 22030 or to Partnership For After School Education, 120 Bway, NY, NY 10271.

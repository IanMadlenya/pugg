
        BERRY--Loren C. Lawyer, died at his home in Hilton Head S.C. on May 3, 2005 at the age of 93. He was the son of Gordon Lockwood and Katharine Dwight Berry. He graduated from the Choate School, Yale University (AB cum laude 1934), and Columbia University Law School, 1938. He became a member of the Bar in Mississippi in 1937 and New York in 1939. He practiced estates and trusts law at Rogers & Wells from 1939-1941, and 1945-1990 and was of counsel at Clifford Chance Rogers & Wells at the time of his death. During WW II he served as a lieutenant commander in a US Navy Scouting Squadron. He served as a director of Church & Dwight Co., Inc from 1961 to 1984, Councilman of the Town of Huntington 1953 to 1957, vice president of the Association of Towns of the State of New York 1957 to 1960, and delegate from the second senatorial district to the New York State Constitutional Convention 1967. He was a member of the Suffolk County Republican Committee from 1952 to 1985. He served on the vestry of St. John's Church, Cold Spring Harbor NY. He was a member of the Elks, the Union Club, and the Tahawus Club. He is survived by his wife of 64 years, Florence Bateson Berry, his daughters, Rosina B. Dixon (Richard W. Dixon), Lucinda B. Berry and five grandchildren, Erica H. Dixon, Douglas R. Dixon, Andrew D. Dixon, Ryan W. Berry, Jason T. Berry, and his sisters, Helen McClure Bluhm and Louise Berry Caldwell. He was preceded in death by his son, Roger Wolcott Berry, and his sister, Marion Berry Scott. A memorial service will be held at St. John's Church, Cold Spring Harbor NY on May 21, 2005 at 1:00 P.M. In lieu of flowers, please contribute to The First Presbyterian Church Hilton Head, 540 William Hilton Pkwy, Hilton Head SC 29928, St. John's Church, PO Box 266, Cold Spring Harbor NY 11724, or the charity of your choice.


        MARION--James Preston, Jr., 88, of New Canaan, CT, died Wednesday, March 28, 2007, in Vero Beach, FL. He was born July 12, 1918 in Sumter, S.C. to Dr. James P. and Lillian Marion. He was a 1941 graduate of the U.S. Naval Academy and was a Golden Gloves boxer during his years at Annapolis. During World War II he served on the USS Perkins based at Pearl Harbor and as Lieutenant Commander on the submarine USS Cobia. He worked for Bloomsburg Mills Inc. from 1947 until 1992, serving as Chairman and CEO. He is survived by his wife of 63 years, Virginia Weber Marion of New Caanan; four children; Leslie M. Jarbeau, Meredith M. Huse, James P. Marion III, and Geoffrey E. Marion; one sister, Lyllian Tubbs of Greenwood, MS; seven grandchildren; and five greatgrandchildren. A memorial service will be held in New Caanan at the Congregational Church on Friday, April 27 at 2:00pm. In lieu of flowers, the family requests that donations be made to The Wisconsin Maritime Museum at Manitowoc, 75 Maritime Drive, Manitowoc, WI 54220, where the USS Cobia is now a war memorial.

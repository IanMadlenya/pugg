
        BERGER-Rita H. Died at home on November 16, 2001, after a brief illness. Born 1912, in Poland, she grew up and completed medical studies in Vienna. She came to the United States in 1941. In the U.S. she pursued medical research and served for many years as a volunteer woking with metally ill clients of The Bridge, Inc. She was a director of The Bridge, and its first residence, on Amsterdam and 83rd St. in Manhattan, is named Rita Berger House in her honor. Her husband, Jacob Berger, died in 1976, and her brother, Frederick Both, died in 1971. She is survived by her daughter, Vivian O. Berger, who is Nash Professor of Law Emerita at Columbia Law School. Services 11:30 AM, November 20, at The Riverside, 76th St and Amsterdam Ave.


        KIRSHENBAUM--Isidor. On September 24, 2006. Dr. Kirshenbaum was born on June 22, 1917 to Samuel and Kate Kirshenbaum. He obtained his PhD in Chemistry from Columbia University in 1942 and served on the Manhattan Project. After WW II, Dr. Kirshenbaum joined Esso Research and Engineering Company (currently Exxon) in Linden, New Jersey and retired in 1985 as a Senior Research Advisor and head of the Chemical Information Department. During his career, Dr. Kirshenbaum was awarded over 70 patents in the areas of nuclear research, petrochemicals and plastics, and authored a definitive work on the properties of heavy water (published by McGraw Hill in 1954) and over 35 scholarly articles. Dr. Kirshenbaum is survived by his beloved wife of 59 years, Lucy (nee Gutstein) of Scarsdale, N.Y., formerly of Westfield, N.J.; his four children and their spouses, Howard (Elaine), Steven (Nancy), Barbara (Mark Weliky) and Kenneth (Rena); his ten cherished grandchildren, and his brother Maurice (Carol). Funeral services will take place at Riverside Memorial Chapel in Mount Vernon, N.Y. September 26, 2006, 1:30 p.m.


        MURPHY--Charles E., Jr., died April 5th, 2007 at 12:55 am PDT. He was 83 years old. Born and raised in Brooklyn, New York he graduated from Dartmouth College and Columbia Law School. During World War II he served actively with the United States Marine Corp from 1942 to 1946. He separated from the Service as First Lieutenant. Following Law School, he served during the Korean War on active duty from 1950 to 1952. Following his Honorable Discharge as a Captain in 1952, he became a staff attorney for the Texas Company until 1954 when he joined the firm of Wandless, Havens, Stitt & Tighe. Becoming a partner in 1956, he rose to Senior Partner and merged his firm with Whitman and Ransom in 1976. He remained a Senior Partner of Whitman and Ransom until 1993 when he again merged the firm. He then became ''of counsel.'' In 2004, he retired from Satterlee Stephens Burke & Burke. Mr. Murphy spent the major part of his life in New Castle, New York where he served on the Town Board, the New Castle Zoning Board, and the Adoption Service of Northern Westchester, now Family Services. He also served on various Boards of Directors including Fleet/Norstar, The John A. Hartford Foundation of which he was a Director, Vice Chairman and Chairman of the Grants Committee and Chairman of the Finance Committee. He is survived by his wife Anne Hogate, two sons, Charles (Reva) of Berkley, California, Kenneth (Diane) of Boise, Idaho, ten grandchildren and three great-grandchildren, a brother Thomas Sawyer of Rye, New York and sister Elizabeth Orteig of Scarsdale, New York. There will be a memorial service at the Quaker Hill Country Club, 1013 Quaker Hill Road, Pawling, New York, 12564 on Friday, April 13, at 11:30 am. In lieu of flowers, donations to the Boise Chapter of Make-AWish Foundation, 4355 Emerald St, Suite 280, Boise, Idaho, 83706 would be appreciated.

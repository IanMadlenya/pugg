
        BOOGAERTS-John Joseph, Jr. Architect and urban planner for the US General Services Administration, resident of Greenwich, CT, died on March 28, 2002 in New York City. He was born in Alexandria, LA, graduated from Tulane University School of Architecture and Columbia University with a Masters in Urban Planning. He ran for Congress in 1974 and was active in the historic preservation of South Street Seaport and Carnegie Hill. He published maps of the Arabian Peninsula and its major cities in English and Arabic. He was a member of the Century Association. He leaves his wife of 41 years, Florence, daughters, Charrette Boyce and Yvonne, and sons Whitmore and Pieter and three granddaughters. A memorial service will be held at the Presbyterian Church of Old Greenwich, 38 West End Avenue, Old Greenwich, CT on Saturday, April 6 at 2 PM. Donations may be sent to Memorial Sloan-Kettering Center, Dr. Howard Scher, Cancer Research, 1275 York Avenue, New York, NY 10021 or Tres Dias of Fairfield County, 215 Sturges Ridge Road, Wilton, CT. 06897.

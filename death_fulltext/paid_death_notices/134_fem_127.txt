
        CLARK-Susanne Williams. Born July 21, 1935, in Kansas City, MO. Died peacefully after a courageous battle with esophageal cancer at Calvary Hospital on Friday, March 13. She graduated from Randolph-Macon Women's College in Lynchburg, VA in 1957. Since then she has lived in Manhattan where she had a career in advertising and airline sales. Following a laryngectomy operation in 1986, she devoted much of her life to the rehabilitation of those who had lost their voices. She was an active volunteer in Memorial Sloan-Kettering's patient-to-patient program and currently served as Secretary of the International Association of Laryngectomies. She was preceded in death by her loving husband, James G. Clark. She will be sorely missed by her family and her many devoted friends all over the country who were uplifted by her lively artistry on the piano and her warm and unique humor. She is survived by her brother, Dr. Tom Williams and his wife, Elizabeth, of Etowah, TN. Survived also by a niece, Alison Coulson and three nephews, Murray Williams, Stephen K. Williams and Tommy Williams. There will be a memorial service at the Frank E. Campbell Funeral Chapel, 1076 Madison Avenue at 81st Street, at 2 PM, on Thursday, March 19, with the Rev. John A. Calhoun officiating. Friends are welcome at Rita Bell's immediately following the service. Interment will be the following day at Moravian Cemetery on Staten Island.


        ROCKICIOLI-SILK-Catherine M. (nee Troccoli). On October 21, 2000. She had a distinguished career as one of the first women to have her own fashion business on 57th Street. Inaugural gowns she made for Eleanor Roosevelt are part of the First Ladies Collection at the Smithsonian Institute. She also made costumes for the Ziegfeld Follies, Lou Walters Latin Quarter Shows, Sonya Heinie, Theda Bara, and the Gish sisters. She served as President of the American Legion Auxiliary and was a charter member of the local chapter of the AARP. Her most cherished role was as a devoted mother, grandmother and greatgrandmother to her family. Beloved wife of the late Albert. Loving mother of Assunta and Manuel Carballeira, and Ralph and Alfonsa Rockicioli. Cherished grandmother of Catherine, Joseph and Katie, Andrew and Margaret, Lita and Daniel, Frances and Albert. Dear great-grandmother of Andrew, James, Brooke, Christopher, Matthew, Rose, William, Joseph, and Caroline. Loving sister of Thomas, Joseph, Dominick, and Esther. Also survived by many nieces and nephews. Reposing at the Frederick Funeral Home of Flushing, Northern Blvd at 193rd St. Visiting hours 2-5 and 7-10 PM, today. Funeral Mass Tuesday, St. Andrew Avellino RC Church at 10:15 AM. Interment Mt. St. Mary's Cemetery. In lieu of flowers, donations to Astor Home, Children's Fund, POB 5005, Rhinebeck, NY 12572.

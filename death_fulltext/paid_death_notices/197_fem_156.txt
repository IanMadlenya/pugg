
        CLARK--Olive Marion. 80, passed to eternal life June 21, 2003, in Lenox, MA. Daughter of Earle and Mildred Roberts, she was born and educated in New York City, where she also studied under George Balanchine at the School of American Ballet and sang in choirs, including the Collegiate Chorale with Robert Shaw and Arturo Toscanini. A graduate of Barnard College (B.A.) and Teachers College, Columbia University (M.A., Ed.D.), she served for 33 years as teacher and administrator in the New York City Public School system. Assistant Professor at Fordham University Graduate School of Education at Lincoln Center. She retired to the Berkshires in 1985. In addition to music and art, her lifelong passion was the care and protection of animals. Survived by her devoted husband Robert I. Clark Sr. of Lenox, her beloved son Robert Francks and his partner Laurence Gleason of New York City, and her cousin Dorothy Somers of Boca Raton, FL. Her beautiful spirit will continue to inspire and guide the many people whose lives she touched with such grace and love.

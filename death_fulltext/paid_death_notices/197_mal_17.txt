
        POWERS -- William Doyle. Retired attorney with the Bell System. A lifelong resident of Manhattan. Died on May 30, 2003, after an extended illness. He was 74. Mr. Powers was a 1950 graduate of Fordham University and received his bachelor's and master's degrees in law from New York University School of Law. From 1957 to 1990 he practiced law with several law firms and corporations, serving as general counsel for Nassau Recycling Corporation, an AT&T subsidiary headquartered in Columbia, SC. Mr. Powers was an independent equity trader from 1990 to 1993, when he retired. Surviving are his wife of 40 years, Marie Wallace Powers, two sons, Corbett and Christopher, and a brother, Richard of Baltimore, MD. A Knight of Malta since 1989, Mr. Powers was active in a number of charitable and civic organizations, including on the Boards of Casita Maria and Marymount College from 1961 to 1965. He was a cofounder of Pregnancy Help, Inc., and served on its Board of Directors and as Vice President since 1994. In 1993 Mr. Powers became a member of the Board of Directors of the Patrons of the Vatican Museum in Rome, Italy. An avid golfer, he and his family summered at Buck Hills Falls, PA, and was a member of its country club for many years. He recently joined the Lost Tree Golf and Country Club in Palm Beach, FL.

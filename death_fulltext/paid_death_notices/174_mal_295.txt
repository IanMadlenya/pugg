
        CLARKE-Laurence Breen. Of Manhattan, passed away suddenly on Friday, July 20, 2001 at New York Presbyterian Hospital after a brief but valiant struggle with leukemia. Born in New York City in 1924 of William J. Clarke and Grace Breen Clark. Educated at Collegiate School in Manhattan, Yale University, B.A. and Master's in International Relations '45W and Columbia Law School '49. He served honorably in the U.S. Navy during W.W. II, rising to the rank of Lieutenant, commanding a Sub Chaser in the Pacific Theater. He was a retired civil trial attorney and also enjoyed an operatic career. He was a member of the U.N. Association, Yale Club, University Glee Club, Council of the Americas, The Navy League, Amateur Comedy Club, Military of Foreign Wars and was a Commander in the Naval Order. Survived and very much loved by his son William J. Clarke, his daughter Lauren Clarke-Bennett and grandson Taylor Clarke Bennett. Memorial services to be on Thursday, August 2nd, 2001, at 10:30AM in the Chapel of Saint Bartholomew's Church, where he was a member. Following the service, he will be laid to rest at Woodlawn Cemetery. In lieu of flowers, donations in his memory to either the Leukemia and Lymphoma Society, 212 448-9206 or the Navy League Scholarship Fund 212-825-7333 will be appreciated.


        PROSHANSKY-Etta W. Age 74, of New York, NY, and Redding, CT, suddenly on February 12. Her 35 years of professional life were dedicated to early childhood education. She began as a classroom teacher, then became a staff developer and later Director of Early Childhood Education and Staff Development in District 3 of Manhattan. After retirement from the NYC Board of Education, she continued as advisor in the Educational Leadership Department at the Graduate School of the Bank Street College of Education. She was the wife of the late Harold M. Proshansky, former President of the Graduate Center of the City University of New York. Mother of Eric and Ellen, both of New York City, and grandmother of Kate and Lucas. Etta lived life to the fullest in the company of friends and family who loved her a lot. A memorial gathering will be held at her home on Saturday. In lieu of flowers, donations may be made in her name to the Benign Essential Blepharospasm Research Foundation, P.O. Box 12468, Beaumont, TX 77726-2468.
        PROSHANSKY-Etta. The Graduate Center of the City University of New York mourns the passing of Etta Proshansky, early childhood educator and wife of our former president, the late Harold Proshansky. Etta made many contributions to our institution and will be sorely missed. We extend our condolences to her children, Eric and Ellen and their families. Frances Degen Horowitz, Pres.
        PUSTILNIK-Jack. Poet, philosopher, utopian, died at 71, on February 9, 2000 in Philadelphia. Writer of the lines: Here today if we wish to unite/All of the earth into a single nation/Though there is no oppressing army/To struggle against and defeat/There still are victories/Of the mind and spirit possible. Beloved husband of Beverly, brother of Bernard Phillips and Seymour, father of Stephen, grandfather of Jeremy. He will be missed. Graveside services Tuesday, February 15, 11:30 AM, at Cedar Park Cemetery, Paramus, NJ.


        KAHN--Alex Bennett. 95, of Manhattan, died on March 8. The last survivor of six siblings, he leaves three generations of nieces and nephews. Alex served in the army in World War II as a speech writer and entertainment director. His productions led to a personal relationship with President and Mrs. Roosevelt. A prominent New York interior designer for over 50 years, his second career was as a cabaret artist, notably on the QE II and at the Algonquin's Oak Room, where Kahn was referred to as ''the Algonquin's very own Noel Coward.'' Burial in Pittsburgh was private.

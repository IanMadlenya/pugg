
        KWALWASSER--Marie Kane, a long-time resident of Jamaica Estates died Friday, January 26, after a long illness. Born in Dundee, Scotland, January 6, 1913, she served as the Goodwill Ambassador of the Scottish Development Council traveling to the United States from 1936-1939 promoting the Empire Exhibition held in Glasgow in 1939. Married the late Dr. Simon Kwalwasser in 1940. Devoted wife until his death in 1971. Active volunteer at Long Island Jewish Hospital for many years and a joyful member of the Institute for Retired Professionals at the New School. Survived by her son, Harold, of Washington, DC, her niece, Louise Greenberg of Hewlett, and her nephews, Dr. Barnett Geller of East Rockaway, Daniel, Martin, Alain, and Pascal Kahan of the United Kingdom. Services to be held at Sinai Chapels, 162-05 Horace Harding Expressway, Fresh Meadows, Queens, on Tuesday January 30, 2007, at 1:15 pm. In lieu of flowers, donations may be made to St. Andrews University, (c/o Development, University of St. Andrews, St. Salvator's College, St. Andrews, Fife, Scotland KY16 9AJ), in honor of Marie and Simon, a graduate of its then medical school.

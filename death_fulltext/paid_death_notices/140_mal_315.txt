
        FISHER-David. Devoted husband of the late Bettie Lou is survived by his children Barbara, Jane, David, Timothy, Padraic, Andrew; five grandchildren; and devoted companions Mitchell Grossman, Nora Knowles, and Ramzi Pio. He was 87. A well-respected attorney for over 65 years, he represented many famous clients including The Steinway Corporation, The New York Yankees, and many celebrities and served as general counsel for U.S. Surgical Corporation from 1968 to 1995. A voracious collector and accomplished bookman, his 35,000 volume library is among the most extensive private collections in the country. A prolific writer in his retirement, his works include ''The Human Relations Factor'' 1990, and the unpublished ''Journey to the Sun'' and his epic celebration of Native Americans, ''Red Eminence.'' In his professional career as a leading authority in securities law, the Fordham University graduate authored many of New York State's securities law statutes. Services will be held Tuesday, September 29, 1998 at 12 noon at the Waterbury & Kelly Funeral Home, Briarcliff Manor, NY. In lieu of flowers, contributions may be made in memoriam to the American Indian College Fund.

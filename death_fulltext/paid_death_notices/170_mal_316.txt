
        SUNDELL-Abner Jerome. Age 87, died Sunday, March 11, 2001. He was born in New York City on August 18, 1913, the son of Isadore and Molly Sundell. During World War II, he served in the U.S. Army in northern France. He wrote cowboy stories for pulp westerns and was involved in the early days of comic books as a writer and editor. He was a magazine editor, an artist, and founder and manager of Offset Color Separations. He grew up in Brooklyn, brought up his family in Freeport, Long Island, and then moved into Manhattan. After retiring, he spent summers and winters in San Miguel de Allende, Mexico, and worked full time on his multimedia canvasses, which were exhibited in one-man shows in New York, Mexico, and Hot Springs, Arkansas. He was preceded in death by his wife of 59 years, Thelma Gordon Sundell, & by his sister, Beatrice Irwin. He is survived by two sons, Eric Gordon Sundell and his wife, Milanne, of Monticello, Arkansas, and Jonathan Douglas Sundell of Winston-Salem, North Carolina; 1 brother, Sidney Sundell of Enosburg Falls, Vermont; two sisters, Zelda Simon and Marilyn Levinson of North Miami Beach, Florida; and three grandsons, Michael, Jack and Joe. Abby will be remembered by family and friends for his generous heart and adventurous spirit.

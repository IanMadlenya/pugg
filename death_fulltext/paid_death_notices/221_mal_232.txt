
        KLINE--Irving, a longtime Yonkers resident died Monday, June 27, 2005 at the Hebrew Home for the Aged in Riverdale, N.Y. He was 92. He was born on December 31, 1912 in New York City to Morris Kline and Rebecca Norr Kline. He was educated at Evander Childs High School (1930) in New York and graduated from City College in 1934. During the Second World War, he served in the U.S. Army 44th Cavalry Recon as a Staff Sergeant in the U.S., Britain, France and Germany. During W.W. II he served in a U.S. Army tank patrol that liberated two concentration camps in Germany. He gave testimony to the Survivors of the Shoah Visual History Foundation Project. He held memberships in the Jewish War Veterans, Yonkers Post No. 68; the Zionist Organization of America; the Crestwood B'nai B'rith; and the Simon Wiesenthal Center for Holocaust Studies in Vienna. He married Julia Hurwitz of Syracuse in 1946. She died in 1978. His wife Muriel Kline survives him. Irving and Muriel were honored for their commitment to world Jewry at the Midchester Jewish Center for the 1980 UJA-Federation Campaign. He was again honored on October 30, 1988 at the Minyanaires Breakfast at the Midchester Jewish Center. After WWII, Irving was first partner with his uncle, Ben Gold, and then President of the Jacobson Paint Co, Inc. in the Bronx. The funeral will be held in the Weinstein Memorial Chapel in Yonkers, N.Y. on Wednesday at 9:45AM. Burial will be in the Sharon Gardens Cemetery in Valhalla. The family requests that donations be made in his memory to Greenburgh Hebrew Center, 515 Broadway, Dobbs Ferry, N.Y. 10922.


        KNOPPERS--Antonie T. Died peacefully at his home in Manhattan on Tuesday, December 16 at age 88. Beloved husband of Ria, proud father of a son Bastiaan Knoppers of Amsterdam, Netherlands, and three daughters, Mieke Knoppers Cole of New York, NY, Anneke Woodward of Atlanta, GA, and Els Knoppers of Arlington, MA, and devoted grandfather of eight grandchildren. Dr. Knoppers lived life fully and passionately. He loved music intensely, especially opera. A native of the Netherlands, he received his medical degree in 1939 from the University of Amsterdam and a doctorate in pharmacology in 1941 from the University of Leiden. He was Professor of Pharmacology at the Free University of Amsterdam. Dr. Knoppers became a citizen of the United States in 1958. Dr. Knoppers joined Merck and Co. in 1952 and held various positions in the International Division of Merck Sharp and Dohme, becoming President of the Division in 1957, elected a member of the Board of Directors of Merck in 1970, and President and Chief Operating Officer of Merck in 1971 until his retirement. He served as Chairman of the International Chamber of Commerce, Chairman of the Salzburg Seminar, and as a Director on the Boards of Hewlett Packard, Scott Paper Co., Humana, Ahold, Centocor, Agouron, and the Salk Institute. Dr. Knoppers was a member of the Council on Foreign Relations, the Century Club, Baltusrol Club, and the Metropolitan Opera Club, proudly serving as President of the Opera Club 1987-1988. His excitement for life was contagious and will be missed. Contributions in his memory can be made to the Ricardo O'Gorman Garden and Center, 23 W 129 St, New York, NY 10027 or the Wright Center on Aging, Box 5907 GPO, New York, NY 10087.

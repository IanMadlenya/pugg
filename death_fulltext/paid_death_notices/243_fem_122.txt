
        LEYNSE--Judith L., who served as director of media relations for the Committee to Protect Journalists from 1996 through 1999, and as the longtime associate director of Columbia University's Office of Public Information, died peacefully in her sleep Sunday morning at her home in Manhattan. She was 68. Judith Light was born in Pontiac, IL and graduated from Cornell and the Columbia Graduate School of Journalism. She joined the Chicago Daily News as a cultural reporter and wrote for its arts magazine Panorama. She also edited the paper's TV magazine. Joining the Foreign Service in 1963, she worked for the United States Information Agency in Seoul, where she married Humphrey W. Leynse, another U.S.I.A. officer. Leaving the agency, Ms. Leynse and her husband made films about Asia from 1966 to 1970. Ms. Leynse taught journalism from 1971 to 1979 at Washington State University. Following Mr. Leynse's death in 1977, she served as Columbia's associate director of Public Information from 1979 to 1996. There she met her second husband, Frederick H. Knubel, the office's director, who died in 1998. Ms. Leynse is survived by her sons James A. Leynse, a photographer, of Leonia, NJ, and Andrew D. Leynse, artistic director of the theatrical company Primary Stages; her sister, Doris Tarachow, of Milwaukee, and two grandchildren. A memorial service will be held on April 30 at 2 p.m. at Temple Emanu-El (65th Street, E. of Fifth Ave.)


        TOWNSEND-M. Wilbur. Retired chairman and CEO of Handy and Harman in New York City, died on February 7, 2002 in Ormond Beach, Florida at the age of 90 due to heart complications. Born in Oyster Bay, New York on January 17, 1912, Bill graduated from Choate in 1930 and from Wesleyan University in 1934 where he earned a Phi Beta Kappa key. During World War II, Mr. Townsend worked in Washington, D.C. at the War Manpower Commission. In 1946 he joined Handy and Harman and in 1964 became chairman, president and CEO. Under his leadership the company was listed on the New York Stock Exchange on May 1, 1967. Mr. Townsend was active as a member of the boards of Home Life Insurance Company, Lazare Kaplan, the Jewelers Security Alliance, the Jewelers Vigilance committee, the Jewelers Industry Council and the 24 Karat Club of NYC, president in 1972 and then chairman for 3 years. Married for 62 years to Barbara Hayden, they lived most of their lives in Scarsdale, N.Y. They were members of the Greenville Community Church where Bill was a Deacon and an Elder. He was a member of the Union League Club and the Westchester Country Club. In addition to his wife, Barbara, Bill was predeceased by his sister, Sally. He is survived by his two sons, Barrett Townsend of Greenwich, CT., and Philip Townsend of Eastchester, N.Y. Also surviving are 3 grandchildren & 4 great grandchildren.

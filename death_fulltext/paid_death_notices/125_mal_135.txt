
        SCHEIDKER-Richard L., Sarasota, Fl. Former senior vice president of the American Association of Advertising Agencies, died here on June 9, 1997 at the age of 81. During World War II he was naval aide to the Director of the Office of Stragetic Services, Maj. Gen. William J. Donovan, and assisted him at the Nuremberg Trials. Mr. Scheidker joined the advertising agency association in 1946 and headed its public relations activities until his retirement in 1971. He helped to establish the National Advertising Review Board, also the Advertising Educational Foundation, and organized a program to train minority people for advertising jobs. He was born in Hannibal, Missouri, and was graduated from the School of Journalism of the University of Missouri in 1936. He was a radio writer before entering the advertising agency business. During World War II, after action in the Battle of Leyte Gulf, he was awarded the Bronze Star Medal. He is survived by a sister, Mrs. H. Norman Schnitzlein of Tampa, Florida. For more information, contact Toale Brothers Funeral Home, Gulf Gate Chapel.


        ASZLING-Richard A. Richard Anderson Aszling, a retired corporate communications executive, died of pneumonia on Wednesday in Middletown, Connecticut. He was 85. Mr. Aszling retired in 1979 as vice president/corporate relations for General Foods Corp. He graduated from Oberlin College in 1936 and began his career as a writer/editor for the Dayton, Ohio Daily News. In 1940, he entered public relations, serving as manager with the Ohio State Medical Association, the Borden Company (in Columbus, Ohio, and New York City), and General Foods. In the late Fifties and early Sixties, he was a partner at Earl Newsom & Co. and a vice president of the Interpublic Group. Returning to General Foods in 1964, he was made a vice president in 1968. At Borden and General Foods, he pioneered corporate strategies for promoting convenience foods and maintaining positive relations with consumer activists. Mr. Aszling was an elected member of the Representative Town Meeting in Greenwich, Conn, where he and his family lived for 43 years. In retirement, he served as chairman of the International Juvenile Diabetes Foundation. He is survived by his wife, Carolyn Gill Aszling, and by four children: Philip, Kristen Howard, Gretchen Leache, and Jill Aszling Shek. He has six grandchildren and one greatgrandson. Memorial donations to the International Juvenile Diabetes Foundation, 381 Park Ave, Suite 507, New York, New York 10016, will be appreciated.

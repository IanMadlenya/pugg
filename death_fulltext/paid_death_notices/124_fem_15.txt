
        CAPARN-STEEL-Rhys, age 87, Danbury, CT, a noted sculptress, died Tuesday in Danbury, CT. She was formerly of Newtown, CT., and was the widow of H. Johannes Steel. Mrs. Steel was born in Onteora Park, NY, July 28, 1909, daughter of Harold and Clara Jones Caparn. From 1927 to 1929 she attended Bryn Mawr College and from 1929 to 1930 studied at Ecole Artistique des Animaux in Paris, France and from 1931 to 1933 studied at Archipenko School of Art in New York City. She taught Sculpture at the Dalton School in New York for 21 years. Following her first exhibit in 1933, at the age of 24, Ms. Caparn received many awards, including the Medal of Honor for Sculpture from the National Assoc. of Women Artists, and a major prize for American Sculpture at the Metropolitan Museum. She was a life member of the International Institute of Arts and Letters and Italian Arts and Labor. Her work is in the permanent collection of the Whitney Museum, Corcoran Gallery of Art, Fogg Art Museum, St. Louis Art Museum, and the US State Dept. Collection for US Embassies around the world as well as numerous private collections. There are no immediate survivors. Memorials may be made to the Bryn Mawr Club of Fairfield County, c/o Grace Papp, 110 Dunn Ave., Stamford, CT 06905-1113. There are no services or calling hours. Cremation will be in Ferncliff Crematory, Hartsdale, NY. Hull Funeral Service, Danbury, CT is in charge.


        RAWLEY--Ruth Kolb Otis, of Baldwin, NY died April 24, 2007 in Cairo, Egypt after a short illness. She was visiting her son James W. Rawley, United Nations Resident Coordinator in Egypt, and his family. Born April 12, 1921, Mrs. Rawley was raised adjacent to the Holtsville Tuberculosis Sanatorium where her father Edwin Kolb, M.D. was the Medical Director. She was an accomplished Cellist and the first woman hired by the Syracuse Symphony. Mrs. Rawley volunteered with the League of Women Voters and the American Red Cross. She married George Otis III, who was killed in WWII, and William K. Rawley, who retired from British Airways and passed away four years ago. Surviving are her daughters, Ruth Otis Cudney of Pacific Palisades, CA and Dr. Ann Rawley of Berkeley, CA; son James W. Rawley of Cairo, Egypt; six grandchildren, and two greatgrandchildren. A family memorial will be held June 24, 2007. In lieu of flowers, donations to the American Red Cross or UNICEF would be appreciated.

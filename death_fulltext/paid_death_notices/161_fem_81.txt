
        SCHULMAN-Rosalind Blum, of New York City, age 90, passed away peacefully in her sleep on June 9, 2000. Beloved mother of Susan Linda Schulman and the late Donald M. Schulman, devoted grandmother of Robin Schulman Torriero, loving great-grandmother of Jenifer, Lisa and Jessica, and adored wife of the late Saul T. Schulman. A distinguished child psychologist, she established The Psychological Testing Center in New York City in 1941, and for more than 60 years found great joy in guiding parents and children to the best school for their needs and abilities. She received her B.A. and M.A. in Clinical Psychology from Teachers College, Columbia University, where she taught courses in tests and measurements for many years. She created the original admission program for Hunter College Elementary School and was consulting psychologist for many private schools, including The Professional Children's School, St. David's, and St. Hilda's and St. Hughes. She was extremely instrumental in the creation in the Rodeph Sholom Nursery School and later the Day School, and was the pro bono school psychologist for both schools for many years. She continued in private practice until her death. She was a member of the American Psychological Assoc., NY State Psychological Assoc., NY Society of Clinical Psychologists and the American Assoc. of Counseling and Development. She was a NY State Certified Psychologist and held a Diplomat in School Psychology. She is survived by her daughter, granddaughter, great-granddaughter, thousands of families she helped and many, many friends. Service Monday, June 12th, 11:15AM at ''The Riverside,'' 76 St. & Amsterdam Ave. interment at Mt. Hebron Cemetery.

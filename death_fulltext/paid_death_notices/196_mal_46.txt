
        STORK-Dr. Gerard J. A former radiologist who practiced medicine in Queens, New York for 43 years and was one of the first practitioners of radiation for the treatment of cancer, died on Friday, May 2, 2003, at the Memorial Hospital at Easton. He was 88. Born in Maspeth, New York in 1914, Dr. Stork graduated from St. John's University and Downstate Medical School of the State University of New York. He served as a major in the U.S. Army Medical Corps during WWII and opened offices in Fresh Meadows, Elmhurst and Ridgewood, New York after the war. A longtime member of the Queens Medical Society, his practice operated the first privately owned cobalt machine in New York City. After retiring from medicine, Dr. Stork moved to St. Michaels, Maryland in 1995. His survivors include his wife Mary, six sons and two daughters, 23 grandchildren and two great-grandchildren. Visiting hours will be at the Fairchild Funeral Home in Manhasset, New York on Tuesday, May 6, 2003, from 3 to 5 and 7 to 9 PM. A funeral Mass will be celebrated at 10 AM on Wednesday, May 7, 2003, at St. Mary's Catholic Church in Manhasset, New York. Burial will follow in St. John's Cemetery, Middle Village, Queens, New York. Memorial donations may be made to the St. Vincent de Paul Society and Food for the Poor, Deerfield Beach, FL.

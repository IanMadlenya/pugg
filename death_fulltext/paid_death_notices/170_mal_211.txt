
        WARD-Maurice John, Colonel, U.S.A.R. (Ret.). Died peacefully in his sleep early Saturday morning, March 17. Born in Donegal, Ireland. He came to this country as a young man. He served as an officer in the Corps of Engineers in the European theater in World War II, and was on active duty during the Korean War. He graduated from Command and General Staff College at Fort Leavenworth, Kansas. He was a professional engineer with a B.S. and M.S. degrees from Columbia School of Engineering. He was with NASA, private industry and later with the Small Business Administration. He is survived by his wife Evelyn. Beloved brother of Dr. Joseph, Patrick, and Maureen Micek, and thirteen nieces and nephews. His brother Raymond predeceased him. We will always remember his quiet strength and gentle manner. Viewing at Fred McGrath's in Bronxville, Monday, 7-9 PM. Funeral Mass at St. Joseph's Church, Tuesday, 11 AM, followed by interment at Gate of Heaven Cemetery. Contributions to a charity of your choice would be appreciated.

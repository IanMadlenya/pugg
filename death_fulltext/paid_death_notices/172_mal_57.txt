
        FINKEL-Edward. Son of Julius and Tamara Finkel, a pioneer in the development and manufacture of television antennas and electronic components, died Saturday morning at 4:45AM at the age of 81. Mr. Finkel, along with his father Julius and his six brothers, employed over one thousand people in building JFD Electronics into one of the world's largest manufacturers of indoor and outdoor antennas, including the famous ''rabbit ears'' antennas that came with virtually every TV set sold in the US in the 50's and 60's. Mr. Finkel is survived by his first wife, Johanna Gillman, an accomplished artist, his second wife, Elaine Land, owner of Ted Land Shoes of Palm Springs, his sons Todd and Glen, his daughter Randii and his seven grandchildren. Edward Finkel was loving, generous and supportive, especially to his family and many friends. Funeral services will be held at Riverside Memorial Chapel, 180 West 76th St. (corner of Amsterdam Avenue) on Monday, May 7, 2001 at 11:15AM.

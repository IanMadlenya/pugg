
        AHMAD-Hazrat Mirza Tahir Ahmad, 74. World Renowned Muslim Religious Leader. Hazrat Mirza Tahir Ahmad, head of the worldwide Ahmadiyya Muslim Community, born on December 18, 1928 in Qadian, India, passed away on April 19, 2003 in London, England. Mirza Tahir Ahmad was the fourth successor and grandson of Mirza Ghulam Ahmad, reformer of the age, and founder of this community established in 1889 in Qadian, India. Today this community comprises of over 170 million in membership, spread out in more than 170 countries. Here in the United States, it has been in existence since 1921. Among the most notable of Hazrat Tahir Ahmad's numerable achievements is the institution of Muslim Television Ahmadiyya, a first of a kind ever, 24 hour satellite station for Muslim programming. Also he oversaw the translation of the Holy Quran in excess of 50 languages. He himself was a prolific writer on various religious subjects. Remarkably, the entire membership of the Ahmadiyya Muslim Community had undeterred access to him personally.

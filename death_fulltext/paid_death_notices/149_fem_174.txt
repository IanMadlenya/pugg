
        BUZZELLI-Margaret Scripps, 77, of Sarasota, FL; died June 17, 1999 at her residence. Born on April 1, 1922 in Washington, D.C., she was the daughter of the late Robert P. Scripps and Margaret L. Culbertson. Mrs. Buzzelli was the Granddaughter of the Late E.W. Scripps, the founder of Scripps Newspapers, headquartered in Cincinnati, OH. Mrs. Buzzelli moved to Sarasota 24 years ago from Cold Spring Harbor, NY. Founder and President of Anabasis Rehabilitation Center in Sarasota, FL, was a graduate of Scripps College in Claremont, CA, and worked as a Journalist in the Pacific Theatre during WWII. Mrs. Buzzelli is survived by three daughters, Mary M. Peirce and Elizabeth A. Logan, both of Sarasota, FL, and Margaret E. McCabe of San Francisco, CA, Three Sons, Peter S. McCabe of Portland, OR, Charles K. McCabe of San Francisco, CA, and Robert A. Buzzelli of Sarasota, FL, One Sister, Nackey Scripps Loeb of Goffstown, NH, Three Brothers, Robert A. Scripps of Fredericksburg, TX, Charles E. Scripps of Cincinnati, OH and Samuel H. Scripps of Rhinebeck, NY, Seven Grandchildren and One GreatGrandchild. A Celebration of Her Life will be held on Tuesday, June 22, 1999 at 6:00pm at her residence. Memorial donations may be made to The Humane Society of Sarasota, Inc, 2331 15th St, Sarasota, FL 34237. For information concerning services call Toale Brothers Colonial Chapel, in Sarasota, FL

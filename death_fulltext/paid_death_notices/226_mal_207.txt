
        SOLOMON--Horace H. On November 16, 2005, after a long struggle with cancer. Thank you L.S./M.F.T. Born on February 11, 1929 in New York, he was the son of Lillian (Rubin) and Nat L. Solomon of New York, Englewood, NJ and Palm Beach, FL. Loving husband of Anita Grossman Solomon, author and quilter; father of devoted children John of Glendale, CA and Thomas of Los Angeles; brother of the late Muriel Ribner; loving cousin of many Taplinger and Solomon family members. Horace was educated at the Englewood School for Boys, graduating in 1946 and Yale College, Class of 1950. He joined Solo Products Corporation where he remained for 25 years, eventually serving as President and Director of various associated companies, including the family charitable foundation. Known throughout his life for his superb taste and keen sensibilities, he cofounded the Holly Solomon Gallery in Soho in 1975. In 1985, Horace joined The Brooklyn Museum as the Vice Director in charge of development. In 1988, he founded the Arts Video News Service, a subscription-based monthly video service featuring critical reviews, interviews, and up-to-date information on work featured in galleries and museums primarily in the New York area. He retired in 1992. He was a member of the Harmonie Club, the Metropolis Country Club and the City Athletic Club. Services will take place at Congregation Emanu-El, 5th Ave. at 65th St. on Friday at 10 a.m. Internment follows at Temple Israel Cemetery, Hastings-on-Hudson, NY.


        GREEN--William (''Bill''). RN, Lieutenant Colonel, USAF (Ret.) of West Islip, NY and formerly of Brooklyn, NY died on September 9, 2005 at the age of 90. He was born May 10, 1915, the fourth of five children, the only son of immigrant parents, Rose and Louis Green. A graduate of Brooklyn's Boys High School in 1932, he was a life member of its alumni association as well as a member of Mu Sigma fraternity which he served for many years, finally as national president. He graduated from the School of Nursing, Brooklyn State Hospital (class of 1939) and earned his B.S. degree at St. John's University (class of 1951). His distinguished military career of reserve and active duty service began as a private in the medical battalion of Brooklyn's 106th Infantry, NYNG and then as a hospital corpsman in the U.S. Navy and Marine Corps through world War II. Commissioned a first lieutenant in the Medical Service Corps, USAF, he served in the Korean War and in Occupied Japan. In Japan he started collecting antique Japanese woodblock prints known as ukiyo-e, assembling a notable collection eventually given as a gift to Amherst College. His extensive reference library on Japanese art was given to Amherst's Robert Frost Memorial Library. In 1973, he organized the Ukiyo-e Society of America serving as its first president and was eventually awarded honorary membership. Survived by nieces and nephews. Funeral and burial arrangements private. Interment at Montefiore Cemetery, Springfield Gardens, NY.

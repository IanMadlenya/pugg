
        PRESS--Arthur, of New York and Florida, died on January 9, 2007. He was 77 years old. Mr. Press or Artie as he was known to those that knew him was born and raised in Brooklyn, NY. He attended Lafayette High School and Western Maryland College where he was selected as a first team All American in basketball and was subsequently selected by the Baltimore Bullets as their first round draft pick. Mr. Press was a banker with Chemical Bank for over 35 years and retired as a Vice President. He served his country with distinction as a Lieutenant in the army during the Korean War. He spent his retirement in Pennsylvania and Florida. In keeping up the legacy begun by his father, he touched countless lives through his charitable endeavors and his community service. Funeral Services to be held 11 a.m. Sunday at Sherman's Flatbush Chapel, 1283 Coney Island Avenue in Brooklyn. He is survived by Peggy, his childhood sweetheart and wife of 55 years, his three children, Craig, Ivy and Robert and his six grandchildren Michael, Daniel, Morgan, Alexa, Ashley and Amanda. His generousity of spirit and optimistic disposition will be missed by all who knew him.

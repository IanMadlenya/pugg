
        LIEBERMAN -- James San ford, MD. We are deeply saddened by the passing of our dear friend and colleague, Dr. James S. Lieberman. Chairman and physiatrist-in-chief of the Department of Rehabilitation for New York-Presbyterian at both Columbia University Medical Center and Weill Cornell Medical Center, he held joint academic appointments as the H.K. Corning Professor of Rehabilitation Medicine Research, senior associate dean for clinical services and associate vice president for health sciences at Columbia University College of Physicians and Surgeons, and professor of rehabilitation medicine at Weill Cornell Medical College. A noted authority in neuromuscular disease and a leader in physical medicine and rehabilitation, Dr. Lieberman served as a consultant on disability issues for the Social Security Administration, as a member of the Institute of Medicine of the National Academy of Sciences and on the Board of Trustees for the Association of Academic Physiatrists. Board-certified in both physical medicine and rehabilitation and neurology, he was editor-in-chief of Archives of Physical Medicine and Rehabilitation from 1988 to 1994. A warm and brilliant man beloved by his patients, students, colleagues and staff, Dr. Lieberman will be missed by all. Our heartfelt condolences are extended to his wife, Carol, and the entire Lieberman family. Dr. Lee Goldman EVP and Dean Columbia University Medical Center Dr. Antonio M. Gotto Dean Weill Cornell Medical College Dr. Herbert Pardes President and CEO NY-Presbyterian Hospital


        KAUFMAN--George T. A Holocaust survivor and refugee from the 1956 Hungarian uprising, passed away from heart failure on Sunday, May 7, 2006. Mr. Kaufman is survived by his wife of 60 years, Mary Kaufman (nee Politzer), also a survivor of the concentration camps. Mr. Kaufman had a long and successful career with Colorite Plastics of Ridgefield, NJ. After starting in 1956 as a maintenance worker, he quickly rose to Vice President of Operations within 18 months of arriving in the United States. In addition to his wife, Mr. Kaufman is also survived by his twin sister Lili Horvath of Hungary, his son and daughter-in-law Michael and Sherryl Kaufman, both attorneys, of East Brunswick, NJ, his daughter Kathy Kaufman, a school psychologist with the New York City Department of Education and three grandchildren Alex, Amanda, and Andrea Kaufman. Donations in Mr. Kaufman's name can be made to: West Side Hatzoloh (volunteer ambulance company), C/O The Jewish Center, 131 W. 86th Street, New York, NY 10024

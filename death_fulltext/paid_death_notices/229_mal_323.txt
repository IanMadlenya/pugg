
        KOHLOFF--Roland L. 71. On February 24, 2006. Beloved husband of Janet (nee Unger), loving father to Jami (Mark) Grassi and Steven, grandfather of Cassandra and Gianna, brother of Cindi and George. For 32 years, he was principal timpanist in the New York Philharmonic, and 16 years prior as Principal timpanist San Franciso Symphony. A professor at Juilliard School, many of his students have become accomplished and famous timpanists throughout the musical community. Roland was a stong advocate for the mentally ill. Funeral 11 am, Thursday, Edwards-Dowdle Funeral Home, Dobbs Ferry (Saw Mill Exit 17). In lieu of flowers, memorials to National Alliance on Mental Illness, www.nami.org or The Amercian Cancer Society
        $(6$)KOHLOFF--Roland. The New York Philharmonic deeply mourns the passing, on February 24, of its esteemed former Principal Timpanist. Mr. Kohloff, who retired November 1, 2004, joined the Orchestra in 1972, succeeding his former teacher, Saul Goodman. Previously, he was principal timpanist of the San Francisco Symphony; he also taught for many years at The Juilliard School, where he had been a student. Philharmonic Associate Principal Percussionist Daniel Druckman said Mr. Kohloff played ''with precision, elegant phrasing, musical conviction and, always, a point of view.'' Our heartfelt condolences to his wife, Janet, his children, Jami Grassi and Steven, and family. Lorin Maazel, Music Director Members of the Orchestra Paul B. Guenther, Chairman Zarin Mehta, Pres/Exec.Dir. Members of the Staff

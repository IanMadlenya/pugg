
        REDMOND-Lida Schock, died at her home in Glen Cove, NY, on January 25, after a short illness. She was the wife of Geraldyn Livingston Redmond. Born in St. Louis, Missouri, in 1926, she attended the Community School and Mary Institute there and graduated from the Foxcroft School in Middleburg, Virginia. An accomplished tennis player and athlete, she was known for her verve on and off the court. Nationally ranked as a junior tennis player, she defeated Gloria Thompson, mother of tennis great Jimmy Connors, as a fourteen year old, and later won numerous club championships. She was previously married to David Wendover Riesmeyer and Anthony John Adrian Bryan. She is survived by her husband, Geraldyn Redmond; four children, Lida Higginson of White Post, Va., Carol Bryan of San Francisco, California, Pamela Bryan of Los Angeles, California, John Bryan of Los Angeles, Calif.; two stepchildren, John Redmond of Long Island, N.Y., and Joan Redmond of Pine Plains, New York, and seven grandchildren, Hadley Higginson of Burlington, Vt., Timothy McCandless, Michael McCandless and Lisa McCandless of San Francisco, Calif., Gwendolyn McCaw of Seattle, Wash., Sophie and Adrian deSelliers of Los Angeles, Calif. Funeral services will be held Thursday, January 28, at 11 AM, at the Brookville Reformed Church, Brookville, Long Island, NY. In lieu of flowers, contributions may be sent to the Foxcroft School, Middleburg, Va.

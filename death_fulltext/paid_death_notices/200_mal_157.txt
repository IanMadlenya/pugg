
        HABER--Louis. 93 of Brooklyn Heights, NY on September 8, of Parkinson's Disease. Violinist and composer, Lou was a leading studio musician from the early 1960's to the 1990's, playing on recording sessions of well known popular artists. Lou wrote the song, ''Thursday'', for Lew Leslie's Blackbirds of 1939. It was sung by Lena Horne and recorded by Count Basie. Lou studied composition with Darius Milhoud at the Paris Conservatory and wrote many trios, duets, and other chamber works. Those recorded were Trio for Violin, Flute and Piano and Six Miniatures for Flute and Violin. Lou was born on January 10, 1910. During WWII, he served in the U.S. Army Air Corp. He was a member of the American Federation of Musicians, ASCAP and the Bohemian Club. He is survived by his beloved wife of 52 years, Nelle, three brothers, Dr. William Haber of New York, Bernard and Milton Haber of Florida, a step brother Moe Sussell of Long Island, and many nieces, nephews, grand nieces and grand nephews.

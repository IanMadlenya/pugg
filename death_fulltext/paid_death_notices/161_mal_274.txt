
        CALAUTTI-William. 85, Died June 22 in Virginia. He was a musician and artisan who designed chandeliers for hotels, theaters and restaurants in New York and in the Unites States Capitol Building in Washington DC. He was born March 14, 1915 in New York and served in the U.S. Navy during World War II. After the war, he ran a chandelier business. He also started a band and played the saxophone, clarinet and trumpet under the stage name of Bill Caldwell. In his later years in Richmond, Virginia, he donated his time to perform at senior centers. He is survived by his children Lee, Lucy, William Jr., Kevin, John and Patricia; nine grandchildren and one great-grandchild. The family will receive Guests Mon. June 26, 3-5 and 7-9 P.M. at Fairchild's Funeral Home in Manhasset, NY. Funeral service at 10 A.M. on Tuesday, June 27, at St. John's Cemetery (The Cloisters) in Queens.

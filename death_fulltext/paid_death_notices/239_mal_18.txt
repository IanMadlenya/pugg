
        ANDRESEN--Malcolm, 89 died from natural causes on Monday, November 27, 2006 at New York Presbyterian Hospital surrounded by his wife and friends. Born in Medford, WI on July 26, 1917, he received his Law degree from the University of Wisconsin in 1941 and was admitted to the Bar (Wisconsin 1941, New York 1946). In 1941 he joined the Marine Corp and took part in the landing at Okinawa. He rejoined civilian life in 1946 decorated with a Bronze Star and retired from inactive service in 1958 with the rank of Lieutenant Colonel. After positions in tax law with several New York Firms, he became Senior Tax Counsel and Senior Government Relations Advisor to Mobil Oil Corp (1952-71). He maintained a working relationship with Mobil Oil for several decades thereafter. he also served as Director of Tax Legal Affairs with the National Foreign Trade Council (1970-73). Throughout the 1960's and 70's he was active in the International Fiscal Association, presiding over its 1971 Washington Congress which was attended by over 1,000 delegates from around the world. He continued to practice law with Whitman & Ransom NYC (1977-86) and in his own offices in Manhattan (1986-2006). In addition to his professional activities Malcolm Andresen was active in many social movements. In the 1960's he served as trustee, Senior Vice President, and Finance Chairman for six years of the National Urban League. He participated in the 1963 March On Washington. Shortly afterwards he served on the board of directors of the Symphony of the New World, an orchestra focused on providing employment for talented musicians of all races. In 1970, he visited Moscow as a member of the Fund for Peace. He was a member of the University Club since 1964, and in 1986 supported the admission of women members at this venerable institution. From 1979 to 1985 he was a trustee and treasurer of the Cathedral Church of St. John the Divine. A member of the Episcopalian Club and an ardent and lifelong Democrat, he is survived by his wife Nigi Sato and by children from his first marriage Anthony Andresen and Susan Bridges. His youngest son Abbott Andresen died in July 2006 . A memorial service will be held in St. James Chapel at the Cathedral Church of St. John the Divine, 1047 Amsterdam Ave at 112th St, New York City, on Saturday, December 9, 2006 at 2:00 p.m.

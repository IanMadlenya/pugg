
        BEATTIE-Edward J., M.D. The Boards of Overseers and Managers and Staff of Memorial Sloan-Kettering Cancer Center note with great sorrow the death of their dear colleague and friend, Dr. Edward J. Beattie. Dr. Beattie joined the staff of Memorial Hospital for Cancer and Allied Diseases as an Attending Surgeon & Chief of the Thoracic Service in 1965; he was appointed Chairman of the Department of Surgery in June of 1966. Later in 1966 Dr. Beattie was named Chief Medical Officer of Memorial Hospital. He held that position until his retirement in 1983. At the time of his death, Dr. Beattie was Chief Medical Officer Emeritus and member of the Board of Overseers Emeriti. As Chief Medical Officer, he pioneered a multi-disciplinary approach to the treatment of cancer, bringing surgeons, medical oncologists, and radiation oncologists together for patient care. Dr. Beattie also helped develop staging techniques which became the world's standard index of lung cancer. As a physician, he was a role model for generations of physicians. He will be greatly missed. We extend our heartfelt condolences to the entire Beattie family. Laurance S. Rockefeller, Honorary Co-Chairman Benno C. Schmidt, Honorary Co-Chairman James D. Robinson III, Chairman, Board of Overseers Douglas A. Warner III, Chairman, Board of Managers H. Virgil Sherrill, Vice Chairman Richard L. Gelb, Vice Chairman Paul A. Marks, M.D., President Memorial Sloan-Kettering Cancer Center

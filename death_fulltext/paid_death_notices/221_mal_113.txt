
        CHASE--Edward Tinsley. On June 9, at home in New York, at age eighty-six, following a long illness. He leaves his wife of fifty-six years, Ethelyn Atha Chase, two sons by a first marriage, Edward Thornton and Cornelius Crane Chase, two daughters, Cynthia Chase-Culler and Daphne Chase Rowe, and nine grandchildren. A service will be held on June 16 at St. Luke's Episcopal Church, East Hampton, for members of the immediate family. A memorial service will be announced. Mr. Chase had a long, distinguished career in publishing as an editor and writer. Following his graduation from Princeton University in 1941, he taught at Stanford University and served as a lieutenant in the Navy during World War II.

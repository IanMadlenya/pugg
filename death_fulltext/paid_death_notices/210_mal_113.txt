
        CAMPAZZI--Earl James, Sr., 82. Of Boca Raton, FL. Died at the Mayo Clinic in Rochester, MN, from leukemia on July 11, 2004. He is survived by his wife of 52 years, Betty (nee Carlson), and his son, Earl Jr., M.D., both of Boca Raton, FL. Mr. Campazzi joined the New York City Police Force on his twenty-first birthday and was its longest serving member, retiring on his 65th birthday. He was shot three times in the line of duty which resulted in his being named New York's Hero of the Month in March 1952. He later headed the Missing Persons Squad and its 50 detectives for ten years in the late 1970's and early 1980's. Those wishing to pay their respects are invited to Edward D. Lynch Funeral Home, Inc., 4307 Queens Blvd, Sunnyside, NY, Wednesday, July 14, from 5 pm until 8 pm. A Funeral Mass with police honors will be held at St. Joan of Arc R.C. Church, Jackson Heights, NY, on Thursday morning at 11 am. Memorial contributions are being accepted by the Dr. Alexander Schirger Special Projects Fund at the Mayo Clinic.

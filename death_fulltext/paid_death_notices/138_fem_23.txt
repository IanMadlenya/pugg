
        GRAVES-Marilyn Preston. Died on July 3, 1998 in Fairfield, CT after a long respiratory illness. Beloved daughter of the late Helen Mitchell Graves and the late Duncan Graves. A life-long New Yorker, Marilyn devoted 44 years of her life as a volunteer at The New York Hospital, and more than 20 years as a volunteer at Memorial Sloan-Kettering Cancer Center, first by helping patients in several clinics and then by dedicating her life to New York Hospital's Patients' Library, which was named for her late twin sister, Helen Mitchell Graves. She was honored for her outstanding volunteer accomplishments by New York City Mayors Lindsay and Beame, as well as, by the United Hospital Fund. She was an active volunteer for many years in the Junior League of New York and was a long-time member of the Colonial Dames in the State of New York and the Daughters of the Cincinnati. Her immediate survivors include her cousins, Augustus W. Kelley III and his children, Christina Gilmartin, Corrine Wagner and Marc W. Kelley, and by Reginald H. Fullerton, Jr., and Nan Fullerton Shupe. A memorial service will be held on Thursday, July 23, at 11 AM at St. Paul's Episcopal Church, 661 Old Post Rd., Fairfield, CT. For information and directions, call (203) 259-0824. In lieu of flowers, contributions may be made to the Pulmonary Function Dept. of Bridgeport Hospital, 267 Grant St., Bridgeport, CT 06610.

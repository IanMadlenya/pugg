
        TEICHER--Florence E. Died on February 5, 2005, in NYC, after a long illness. She was an accomplished sculptor having exhibited in the Hamptons and in NYC at the Weintraub Gallery. Florence Teicher was actively involved in philanthropic activities including Human Rights Watch, Amnesty International and the Anti Defamation League. She was also a principal in Epstein Philanthropies. She received a BA Degree from Hunter College and her Master's Degree from Wellesley College. She is survived by her husband Milton S. Teicher, a New York attorney, and by her niece Karin Kuhns and her nephew Hal Epstein. A memorial service is planned for a later date. Mr. Teicher will be home evenings this week from 5-8 pm, to receive callers.
        TEICHER--Florence. The officers, Board of Directors, and staff of UJA-Federation of New York mourn the passing of Florence Teicher, an accomplished artist, and cherished supporter of the New York Jewish community whose dedication to the arts and human rights left an indelible mark. We extend our deepest sympathies to her beloved husband Milton, and the entire family. Morris W. Offit, President Susan K. Stern, Chair of the Board John S. Ruskay, Exec. V.P. & C.E.O.

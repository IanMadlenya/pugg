
        KORSHIN-Carolyn R. (nee Relis) died peacefully at her home in Whitestone, NY on February 4, 2003, after a short illness in which she was attended by close friends and her granddaughter Farra Korshin. She was born in New York City on November 25, 1910, and grew up near Liberty, NY. She graduated from Hunter College in 1931 and began her teaching career the same year in a one-room schoolhouse in Bushville, NY at a salary of ten dollars a week. After the birth of her children, she resumed her teaching career in New York City, earned two M.A. degrees from Queens College and, after many years of teaching at Thomas Edison High School in Queens, retired in 1975. She had an active retirement, enjoying travel, gardening, and her piano, and will be greatly missed by her friends and family. Her husband Nathan Korshin, whom she married in 1937, predeceased her. She is survived by her sons, Jonathan of Prestonburg, KY., Oliver of Anchorage, Alaska, and Paul of Wyncote, PA., six grandchildren, and two greatgrandchildren. Funeral arrangements will be private. Donations in her name may be made to the Korshin Family Fund, St. John's College, Annapolis, MD 21404.


        TREMONT-T. Paul. Age 65. Of Fairfield, CT, died Thursday, March 4, 1999, of cancer. Paul was born in New York City and raised in Bridgeport, Connecticut. After graduating from Fairfield University, he received his law degree from Georgetown Law School where he served as editor of the Georgetown Law Review. After clerking for the U.S. Court of Military Appeals in Washington, DC, he returned to Bridgeport where he practiced law for over forty years. He was the founder and managing partner of Tremont and Sheldon, PC, a firm specializing in personal injury law. Throughout his professional career, Paul has served as President of the Connecticut Trial Lawyers Association, President of the Greater Bridgeport Bar Association, President of the Connecticut Chapter of the American Board of Trial Advocates, and Fellow of the American College of Trial Lawyers. In 1984, Paul received the Fairfield University Alumni Professional Achievement Award and, most recently, was awarded the Lifetime Achievement Award from the Connecticut Trial Lawyers Association for his prominence in the legal community. He is survived by his wife Sharon, his son Geoffrey Tremont of Rehoboth, Massachusetts, his son Jason Tremont of Fairfield, CT, grandchildren Madeline and Emma, daughters-in-law Felecia and Michelle Tremont, stepdaughters Kristen and Kimberly Stram, and several aunts, uncles, and cousins.


        HAFNER-Helen Karin. 86, died peacefully May 20 in San Dimas, CA. Born in Duluth, MN, June 8, 1913, she earned a Ph.D. from the Sorbonne, Paris, France, in 1935, and an MSW from the University of Michigan in 1952. She taught at Manchester College, NY, in the Department of Education, specializing in courses in mental health and behavioral problems. A longtime resident of Greenwich Village, she was a supporter of the arts in NYC and befriended countless struggling performers. She was active in numerous volunteer organizations, taught intrapersonal process and was a dedicated teacher of spiritual, artistic and psychological training and methods. Her generosity of spirit and love of life was felt by all who came in contact with her. She is survived by a sister, Eleanor of LaMarque, TX, several nieces and nephews, and many other relatives and close friends. Memorial service will be at Community Church of New York, 40 East 35th St., September 15, at 1 PM. In lieu of flowers, memorial donations may be made to the scholarship fund established in her memory (Brooklyn College Foundation c/o Gary Hafner, 54 Bette Circle, Vernon, CT 06066) or to Planned Parenthood.


        VAN HEUKULOM-Carel. Known as a pioneer in the investment company business, died after a long illness. He was an executive of Technology Fund, a management and distribution company, now part of the Kemper Group. Mr. Van Heukulom was known to have been responsible for the first introduction of a U.S. mutual fund on the continent of Europe in 1951. During WWII he took part in the re-conquest of Sicily and was later Military Commander of an area of that island. He next served on General Eisenhower's staff as a Finance and Liaison Officer during the liberation of the Netherlands. He was decorated with Military Order of Oranje Nassau for his dedicated service. Born in New York City in 1908, he is survived by two nephews, Jean Pierre and Michael Guepin of the Netherlands. Friends may call at Frank E. Campbell, 1076 Madison Ave at 81 St, Tuesday, October 28, 7-9 PM. Interment private. In lieu of flowers donations in his memory may be made to Blind Veterans Association, 477 H St, N.W., Washington, DC 20001.

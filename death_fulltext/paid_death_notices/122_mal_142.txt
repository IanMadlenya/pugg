
        PAYNTER-Grenville Howard. 65. On March 9, 1997 in Tucson, AZ. He is survived by his wife Sally Gooch Paynter and by his three children, Bradford Grenville, Cameron Clark, Nathaniel Collingwood and by his mother Mary Jane Howard Paynter and brother Richard Kates Paynter III. An executive of Chemical Bank for 35 years and a respected outplacement consultant for the past 8 years, Grenville was also an enthusiatic world traveler, and forever a Princeton man. A man who always showed the utmost consideration and courtesy to everyone, Grenville has been a warm, loving and humorous husband, father and friend. His services will be held at Trinity Church, Broadway and Wall Street on Friday, March 14 at 3PM. In lieu of flowers, memorial contributions may be sent to The School Volunteers, 352 Park Ave South, NY NY 10010 or Manhattan Eye, Ear and Throat Hospital, 210 East 64 Street, NY NY 10021.
        PAYNTER-Grenville H. The Board of Directors, Administration and Medical Staff of Manhattan Eye, Ear & Throat Hospital mourn the untimely passing of Grenville H. Paynter on March 9, 1997. Mr. Paynter was a devoted friend and Board member for over fifteen years. His tireless energy and financial acumen helped guide the hospital's Finance, Nominating, and Planning Committees, and his wisdom will be deeply missed. To his family and friends, we extend our sincere condolences. Lindsay C. Herkness, III President George A. Sarkar, Ph.D., J.D. Executive Director Jonathan Konovitch, M.D. President Board of Surgeon Directors
        PAYNTER-Grenville H. The Board of Trustees of The Nighingale-Bamford School notes with sorrow the death of Grenville H. Paynter on March 9, 1997. Mr. Paynter was a valued member of the School's Board from 1983 to 1989. We extend our heartfelt condolences to his wife Sally and their three children. Grant F. Winthrop, President Board of Trustees The Nightingale-Bamford School

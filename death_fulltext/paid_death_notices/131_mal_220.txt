
        SHAIO-Victor, 80. Died on December 13, 1997, at his home in New York after a long illness. He is survived by his wife, Anne, his son Alberto Shaio of Greenwich, CT., his three daughters, Flory Anzellini of Manalapan, FL., Diana Keevan of San Francisco, CA., and Joyce Mitrani of Bogota, Colombia, ten grandchildren, three great grandchildren and a stepdaughter, Elizabeth Uzielli of New York, NY. Also survived by his brother, Eduardo Shaio of Palma de Mallorca, Spain and his sister, Joyce Haim of Cannes, France. Mr. Shaio founded many industrial companies including Sedalana, Celanese Colombiana, and Petroquimica Colombiana, as well as Polifilm in Venezuela. In the United States, he was Chairman and Chief Executive Officer of New Energy Corporation of Indiana and past President of the Renewable Fuels Association. He was honored by all for his integrity, wisdom and courage. Mr. Shaio was a benefactor in Bogota, Colombia through the Shaio Clinic, a founder of the Universidad de los Andes and together with his father, built and donated the first Sefaradic temple in Bogota. Funeral service on Tuesday, December 16, 1 PM at Temple Emmanuel, Fifth Avenue and 65 Street, New York, NY. Interment Kensico Cemetery. In lieu flowers, contributions may be sent to Robert E. Barrett's Neurology Research Fund, Dept. of Neurology, Columbia University College of Physicians & Surgeons.
        SHAIO - Victor. Congregation Emanu-El of the City of New York records with deep sorrow the death of our cherished member, Victor Shaio. To his family and loved ones, we express our profound sympathy. May beautiful memory bring comfort to those who mourn his passing. David E. Marrus, President Herbert C. Bernard, Secretary

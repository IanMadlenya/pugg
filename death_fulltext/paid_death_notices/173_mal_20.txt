
        HANNAN-Joseph. An advertising executive, died at New York Hospital on May 30, 2001. He was 71. Mr. Hannan was senior vice president and member of the Board of Directors at Benton & Bowles, later DMB&B, where he was responsible for the Texaco account. He introduced Texaco to a long and successful association with the Bob Hope Specials in the 1970's and supervised many of the productions. Mr. Hannan was born and raised in New York City. He graduated from the United States Military Academy, West Point, in 1952 and served in the Regular Army Airborne Infantry in Korea. From 1955-56 he was assigned Aide-de-Camp to the Superintendent at West Point after which the Army sent him to study International Affairs at Princeton. In later years he served on the U.S.M.A. West Point Board of Visitors, the highest position afforded a civilian. Mr. Hannan was a longtime supporter of St. Jude Hospital in Memphis, Tennesee and served on the ALSAC-St. Jude Board of Directors and Governors from 1992 to the present. St. Jude Children's Research Hospital is widely recognized for providing treatment to children who suffer catastrophic illness. His daughter, Alexandra Scott, of New York City, survives him.


        SULLIVAN--Warren Jr. A publishing executive in strumental in establishing overseas markets for American Books, died in Englewood, New Jersey, on May 23rd. He was 85 years old. A 1944 graduate of Dartmouth College, Sullivan served with the U.S. Navy during World War II. In 1946, he joined publisher John Wiley & Sons, Inc., traveling widely in Europe, Latin America, and the Far East, to build foreign sales. In the 1960s, he was president of the Macmillan Company and then of Barnes and Noble booksellers, returning to Wiley in 1971. From 1983 to 1988, he served as Chairman of Basil Blackwell, Inc. He is survived by Madeleine (Terry), his wife of sixty years; a daughter, Kathleen; and triplet sons, Peter, William, and Jeffrey. Burial was private. A celebration of his life is planned for early fall. In lieu of flowers, contributions in his name may be sent to the Alumni Fund, Dartmouth College, Class of 1944.

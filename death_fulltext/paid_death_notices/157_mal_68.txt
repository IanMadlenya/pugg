
        STEIN-Irvin, Dr. Of Boca Raton, FL, formerly of Philadelphia, died on February 3, 2000 at Johns Hopkins Hospital, Baltimore, MD. He was 93. Dr. Stein was born in Fayetteville, NC and graduated from the University of North Carolina. He received his medical training at Jefferson Medical College and his Intern and Resident training at Johns Hopkins University Medical Center and the University of Pennsylvania. He was Chief of Orthopedic Surgery at Philadelphia General Hospital and at the Albert Einstein Medical Center. He was Professor of Orthopedic Surgery at the University of Pennsylvania Medical School. Dr. Stein served on the Board of the Phildelphia Orchestra and was the doctor to famous musicians including Ormandy, Serkin, Toscanini, and Van Cliburn. He received the Lily Award from the Easter Seal Society in the 1970's. He was a member of the Locust Club, the Philmont Country Club, and Rodeph Shalom Synagogue. His interests included golf and travel. Dr. Stein was married to Dorothy Bluthenthal for 51 years before her death in 1985. He is survived by his wife, Bunny Levy Hutzler, three daughters, Jane Finerman, Margery Schab and Kathy Sachs, 10 grandchildren and 10 great-grandchildren. Funeral services will be held on Sunday at 2 PM at Joseph Levine & Son, 4737 Street Road, Trevose, PA. Interment is at Roosevelt Memorial Park, Trevose, PA. Contributions in his memory may be made to the University of Pennsylvania.

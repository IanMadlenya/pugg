
        WHITEMAN-Dr. Harold Bartlett, Jr., 82. Died May 6, 2002 at his home in Nashville, TN. Dr. Whiteman was born on April 22, 1920, son of Emma Anderson Whiteman & Harold Barlett Whiteman. He attended the Taft School in Watertown, CT., and Yale College in New Haven, CT. where he was a member of Phi Beta Kappa and captain of the 1940 varsity football team. He served in the military during World War II in Africa. He received his M.A. in political science from Vanderbilt in 1950, and his Ph.D. in International Relations from Yale University in 1958. Dr. Whiteman was married in July of 1946 to the former Edith Uhler Davis of Nashville. He served as a mathematics instructor at Taft from 1946-1947 and a Teaching Fellow at Vanderbilt from 1947-1948. He served Yale University as Dean of the Freshman Year from 1948-1964. He was named as Assistant to the President of New York University in NYC in 1964 and he served there until 1971 as Vice Chancellor for Student Affairs. In 1971 Dr. Whiteman was named President of Sweet Briar College, a womens' college in Amherst County, Virginia. He served there until his retirement in 1983. Dr. Whiteman served on many committees and and boards during his notable career, including the board of the Berkeley Divinity School at Yale, the Virginia Foundation of Independent Colleges, and the English Speaking Union. Outside of his academic achievements, Dr. Whiteman spent many days sailing the waters of New England and Martha's Vineyard, where his family maintains a summer home. He is survived by his wife, Edith Uhler Davis Whiteman; three children, Harold Bartlett Whiteman III of Lookout Mountain, GA, Maclin Davis Whiteman of Nashville, and Priscilla Whiteman Kellert of New Haven, CT.; three grandchildren, three step grandchildren and one step-great grandchild.

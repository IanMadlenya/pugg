
        PFLUG-John Rueger (Johnny). One of the last players from the golden age of polo in New York, died September 1,2000 He was 90 years old and lived in Plandome. N.Y. A star athlete at St. Paul's School in Garden City, N.Y. Mr. Pflug was offered a baseball contract by the Brooklyn Dodgers, but chose instead to attend Colgate, where he was an end on the fammous 1932 Red Raiders Football Team, which was undefeated, untied and unscored upon, uninvited to The Rose Bowl that year. Winners of many regattas with his brother Carl in their Star Class sloop ''Wings'' in the early 1930's. The Pflugs were victorious in the trials for the 10th Olympiad in 1932, but decided to return to college, passing up the chance to represent the United States at the Los Angeles games. Polo, however, was John's passion. As a teenager, he and his brother helped win the Junior National Indoor Championship for the Brooklyn Riding and Driving Club and later won the National Indoor Polo Championship. He carried an 8 goal handicap and was one of the highest rated active players in the indoor game before the War. Mr. Pflug played for The New York Athletic Club and Squardon A Indoors and Laddie Sandford's Hurricanes among other teams outdoors. Born in Brooklyn on April 10, 1910, he was the youngest son of Dr. Charles J. and Emma R. Pflug. He served as an officer in the Coast Guard during World War II on a landing craft carrier in the Pacific theatre and worked for many years for the Lincoln Savings Bank. He is survived by his sister, Helen Pflug MacKay of Locust Valley, daughters Jane Pflug of Manhattan and Melissa Pflug of Burlington, VT, a son, John R. Pflug of Cold Spring Harbor, N.Y., and a grandson Jock Pflug. Any contributions may be made to Colgate University, Hamilton, N.Y. 13346.

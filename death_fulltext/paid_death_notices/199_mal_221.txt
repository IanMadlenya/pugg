
        EWEN--William H., 90, beloved husband of Cathleen E. (Ryan) Ewen, of Westerly, RI died at the Westerly Hospital, August 19, 2003. Mr. Ewen was born in Yonkers, NY. Son of the late William C. and Marjorie Ewen. He was raised in Hastings-on-Hudson, NY. He was a 1935 graduate of New York University. He was the corporate director of advertising for the Borden Company and managed Borden's first national radio program and its first TV advertising. In 1962, he revived Borden's famous trade character, Elsie the cow. He was Chairman of the audit bureau of circulations, 196567. He later helped established the National Advertising Review Board and was its first Executive Director. He retired to Westerly, RI in 1976. He was a life member of the Steamship Historical Society of America and held several offices, including President. He had been a founder and President of the Hastings-onHudson, NY Historical Society and President of the Westerly RI Historical Society. He was on the board and served as treasurer of Scenic Hudson Inc. He authored the book ''Days of the Steamboats'' and wrote numerous articles on maritime history, as well as advertising and marketing. He was also an experienced speaker and presenter. In addition to his wife, he is also survived by his son and daughter-in-law, William H. Ewen Jr. and Susan Ewen of Providence, RI. One daughter and son-in-law Barbara E. Ewen and Theodore Lyszczarz of Concord, MA. Two granddaughters, Chrystena Ewen of Derry, NH and Amy Ewen of Providence, RI. Funeral services will be held in the Gaffney-Dolan Funeral Home, 59 Spruce St, Westerly, RI, Saturday at 1:00PM. Interment will be private.

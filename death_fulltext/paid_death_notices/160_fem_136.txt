
        McDONALD-Kathryn Austin, on May 16, peacefully at Mount Sinai Hospital at age 82. Loving mother of K. Ann McDonald, dear sister of Malcolm Patrick Austin, and adored wife of the late Robert J. McDonald. A former attorney with the Legal Aid Society's Juvenile Rights Division, Family Court Judge and Administrator of the New York City Family Court, Kay McDonald devoted her professional life to helping poor children and their families. Friends may call at Frank E. Campbell, 1076 Madison Avenue at 81st St., on Friday, May 19, 2-5 PM, Saturday, May 20, 2-5 and 7-9 PM, and Monday, May 22, 7-9 PM. Mass of Christian Burial will be held at St. Vincent Ferrer Church, 66th St. and Lexington Ave., on Tuesday, May 23, 10 AM. Contributions in her memory may be made to the Legal Aid Society, Columbia Law School, or New Yorkers for Children.
        McDONALD-Kathryn A. The Board of Directors, the President's Council, the management and staff of The Legal Aid Society are saddened by the death of Kathryn A. McDonald, our beloved friend who championed the cause of quality representation for children as an attorney and supervisor at the Society and as a distinguished Family Court Judge. Her leadership, wise counsel, and insight were sources of inspiration to the Board and management for many years. Kay's lifetime of unceasing contributions to equal justice were recognized last year when she received the Society's Servant of Justice Award. Kay's intelligence, warmth, her sense of humor and great integrity held our respect and won our hearts. We will miss her greatly. We extend our sincere condolences to her daughter, Anne. D. Stuart Meiklejohn, Chair Daniel L. Greenberg, President and Attorney-in-Chief

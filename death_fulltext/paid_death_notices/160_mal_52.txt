
        SCULLY-Charles G. Age 74, died suddenly on April 27, 2000 at home in Yonkers, NY. Born in Waldwick, NJ, a veteran of WWII, a sergeant in the U.S. Army serving in India. After the war Charles went into the broadcast industry as general manager and program director at radio station WPAT in Paterson, NJ. He later went to work for radio station WAAT in Newark, NJ as manager and program director. In 1952 he joined SESAC in station relations/PR department and was an executive vice president and director of public relations when he retired in 1992. Charles served on the Board and was a lifetime member of CMA (Country Music Association) and GMA (Gospel Music Association). He also served on the Board of AWRT (American Women in Radio and Television), the National Academy of Popular Music (The Songwriter's Hall of Fame). He was a broadcast pioneer and a long time member of the Friars Club. A deeply caring, kind and compassionate human being, ''Charlie'' shall long be remembered and sorely missed by all those who knew him and were fortunate to have shared in his warmth, wit and loyalty. Survived by his sister, Norma Miller, Lancaster, PA, his brother, Jack Scully, Rio Rancho, NM, several nephews and a niece, and long time dear friend, Fernando Esnarriaga, Yonkers, NY. Donations in his memory may be made to the charity of your choice. Condolences may be sent to his family at 42 Inwood Street, Yonkers, NY 10704.

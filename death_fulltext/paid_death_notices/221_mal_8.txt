
        SCHLOSSMAN--Abraham M.D., age 87, died on May 30, 2005 from complications of an accident on May 13. He is survived by his loving and beloved wife of 53 years, Carolyn and his adored and adoring daughter, Nina. An internationally known ophthalmologist, his passions were his family, profession, teaching, art, music, history and science--a true renaissance man. He will always be remembered. Services Thursday at 11:00 A.M. at Temple Shaaray Tefila, East 79 Street and Second Avenue.
        SCHLOSSMAN--Abraham. The Greater New York Society for Pediatric Ophthalmology and Strabismus remembers with great fondness and respect one of our founding members and champions. Loved and admired by all who knew him. Survived by Caroline, his loving wife, and Nina, his daughter who was his pride and joy. Abe died Sunday, May 30, 2005. He was born May 21, 1918. The service will be held on Thursday, June 2, 11 AM, at Temple Shaaray Tefila, 250 East 79th Street (entrance corner of 79th Street & 2nd Avenue).
        SCHLOSSMAN -- Abraham, M.D. Temple Shaaray Tefila records with profound sorrow the death of our long-time, active and cherished member. Heartfelt condolences are extended to his wife, Carolyn, and his daughter Nina. May his memory be for blessing and may his loved ones be comforted amidst the mourners of our people in Zion and Jerusalem. Rabbi Jonathan A. Stein Michael H. Laufer, Pres.
        SCHLOSSMAN -- Abraham. The members of the Manhattan Ophthalmological Society mourn the loss of their honored member, Abraham Schlossman. A true giant in ophthalmology, Abe was a teacher, a mentor and a friend whose energy, commitment, wisdom and insights touched and inspired us all. Our sympathies are with Carolyn and Nina. Stephen Obstbaum, Pres. Paul Orloff, Secy

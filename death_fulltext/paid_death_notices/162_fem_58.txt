
        SILBERMAN-Rhoda Truax. Born in New York City on October 28, 1901, died June 29, 2000 in Colorado Springs, where she had lived since 1992. Prior to moving to Colorado she was a resident of Cambridge, MA for many decades. She was a graduate of the Horace Mann School and Barnard College in NY. She was the daughter of Justice Charles Henry Truax of the New York State Supreme Court and his wife, Caroline Sanders. Rhoda Truax wrote 12 novels & historical books. Her best known book, ''The Doctors Warren of Boston'', is in the collection of most libraries in this country. Rhoda was the sister of Carol Truax of NY, who was the author of 24 cookbooks. In 1924 she married Dr. Robert Henry Aldrich of Boston. In 1955 she married Henry R. Silberman, a businessman of Boston. She is survived by her daughter, Carolyn Aldrich Frost, and her son-in-law, Hunter S. Frost, both of Colorado Springs, her grandchildren, Daniel Hunter Frost of Irvine, CA, William Truax Frost of Santa Barbara, CA, and Juliana Frost of Colorado Springs, and three great grandchildren, and also by a stepson, James H. Silberman of NYC, and by a step daughter, Dorothy Silberman Altman of Cambridge, MA. Gifts in memory of Rhoda Truax Silberman may be made to the Southern Poverty Law Center, 400 Washington Ave., Montgomery, AL 36104 or to Planned Parenthood, POB 97166, Washington, DC 20077. A private memorial service will be held at a later date.

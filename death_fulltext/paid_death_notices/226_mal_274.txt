
        CRONIN--Michael Thomas Ignatius, M.D. Died on November 23, 2005 in Branford, CT after a long battle with Parkinson's disease. Born on February 1, 1924, he came to the United States in 1952, becoming a citizen in 1958. He received a Doctor of Veterinary Medicine degree from University College, Dublin in Ireland in 1945 and from Trinity College, Dublin in Ireland he earned a Master's of Science in Bacteriology in 1946 and a Ph.D. in Pathology and Bacteriology in 1948. He received his M.D. degree from Georgetown University in 1965 with postgraduate training at Yale University. His medical career spanned 30 years with Yale University, St. Raphael's Hospital, New Haven and Memorial Hospital, Meriden among others. From 1971 to 1989 he was a Consulting Editor for the ''American Scientist.'' A longtime resident of New Haven, Dr. Cronin moved in 1995 to Branford. He leaves his wife of 55 years, Carmel Sheridan Cronin, and eight children, Ingrid Cronin of Wilkes-Barre, PA, Agnes Cronin of San Francisco, Geraldine Burke of Rutland, VT, Charles Cronin of New York, Arthur Cronin of Hummelstown, PA, Carmel Cronin Gill of Lafayette, CO, Brenda Cronin of New York, and Michael Cronin of Littleton, CO. His son, Tom Cronin, died in 1975. His sister, Deirdre MacDonald of Ireland and his brother, David Cronin of France, also survive him. In addition, he has fifteen grandchildren. A Mass will be celebrated Saturday at 10AM at St. Mary's Church, Hillhouse Avenue, New Haven, CT.

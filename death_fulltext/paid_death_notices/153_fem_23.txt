
        FISHEL-Edith Rubin. On October 2, 1999, at age 76. Loving wife for 51 years of James Fishel. Beloved mother of Elizabeth and Anne Fishel. Devoted grandmother of Nathaniel and William Houghteling and Gabriel and Joseph Daly. Loyal sister of Carol Meyer and Harvey Rubin. Proud mother-inlaw of Robert Houghteling and Christopher Daly. Daughter of the late Dr. Isidore C. Rubin and Sylvia Rubin. Services are Tuesday, October 5, at 12 noon, at Riverside Memorial Chapel, 180 W. 76 St, NYC. In lieu of flowers, gifts may be sent to the 92nd Street Y, 1395 Lexington Ave, NYC 10128.
        FISHEL-Edith. The 92nd Street Y mourns the passing of Edith Fishel, wife of our longtime Board member, James Fishel. We are honored to have played a big part in Edith's career, one of the nation's foremost Judaica ceramist. Edith worked in our studios for more than 25 years. Her work was exhibited at leading museums including the Jewish Museum in Manhattan, the Skirball Museum in Los Angeles, the National Museum of American Jewish History of Philadelphia and the Diasporama Museum in Paris. Her art was featured in the New York Times, New York Magazine and many other publications. Our condolences go out to Jim, his daughters, Elizabeth and Anne, and their families. May her memory be a blessing. Philip Milstein, President Joseph Leff, Chairman Sol Adler, Executive Director
        FISHEL-Edith Rubin. Congregation Shearith Israel, the Spanish and Portuguese Synagogue, mourns the loss of its devoted member and extends its deepest sympathy to her family. Alvin Deutsch, Parnas


        SHATZ-Robert H., 79. Died at his home in Palo Alto, California, on August 4, 1998. Mr. Shatz had a productive and varied career in aeronautics and astronautics, energy resources and policy, and the design of high speed trains and boats. While at United Aircraft he led the design team for the Lunar Module Abort Guidance System which brought Apollo 13 back to earth following the emergency evacuation of the command module. He also worked for Lockheed, Cornell Aeronautical Laboratory, Hudson Institute, the Federal Energy Administration, & TRW. An avid skier and tennis player, he is survived by his wife Shanah and two children, Carla and Jonathan.

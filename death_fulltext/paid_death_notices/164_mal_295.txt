
        NABER-James H, 67, of Litchfield, Connecticut, formerly of Fairfield and Easton, died on Sunday, September 10. He was President and CEO of Glendinning Companies from 1975 to 1985, previously holding positions including Managing Director of International Operations and President of U.S. Operations, beginning the relationship as a founding member in 1963. He was widely recognized as an innovator, changing the way companies market their products globally. Prior to joining Glendinning, he worked in brand management at Procter & Gamble. His professional and personal interests included serving as a member of the People - to - People Presidential marketing delegation to China and as a lecturer at Oxford University and the University of Illinois Graduate School. He acted as President of the Citizens for Easton and was Chairman of the Board of Directors of the Nature Center in Westport. An avid fisherman, he was a seasonal resident of Great Harbour Cay, Bahamas and served as President of its Property Owners Association. He is survived by his wife Donna and his sons and their wives, J. Mark and Jodie Naber of Easton and Stuart Naber and Mary Kaeding of Minneapolis. He is also survived by his brother and sister-in-law, Dave & Pat Naber of Brownsville, TX. Services will be private. Memorial donations may be made to his alma mater Carlton College, Attention: Elisa Chaffee, One North College Street, Northfield, MN 55057.

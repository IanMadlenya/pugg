
        SEDA-Damaso, of Cortlandt Manor, NY, died April 25, 2000, age 62. Born NYC, April 17, 1938 to Damaso and Mercedes Ramos Seda. Graduated Empire State College, earned a B.S. degree in 1983. Served in US Navy, 1955-59. After starting out fueling buses and maintaining subway cars, he eventually succeeded in becoming President of the Transport Workers Union Local 100 and then the International Vice President, Transport Union NYC. Survived by his wife, Luz Ginorio Seda; three sons, Derek, Princeton, NJ; Keith, Rahway, NJ; and Kenneth, Cortlandt Manor; 2 sisters, Emma Sanchez and Aida Palermo, both of Puerto Rico. Visitation Thursday, 2-4 and 7-9PM, at Joseph F. Nardone Funeral Home, 414 Washington St., Peekskill, NY. Funeral Mass Friday, 10AM, St. Columbanus Church. Interment Assumption Cemetery.
        SEDA-Damaso. The Transport Workers Union of America, AFLCIO, is deeply saddened by the passing of Damaso Seda, former International Vice President and President of the Transport Workers Union of Greater New York, Local 100, which represents the 35,000 men and women who operate the New York City bus and subway systems. As the first Hispanic to serve as president of TWU Local 100, Brother Seda blazed an important trail for minority leadership in New York City's labor movement. His contributions on behalf of transit workers throughout New York are significant and longlasting. We will miss his dynamic personality and dedicated leadership. Our hearts to out to his wonderful family. Viewing April 27, 2-4PM and 7-9PM at the Nardone Funeral Home, Peekskill. Funeral April 28, 10 AM at St. Columbanus RC Church, Peekskill.

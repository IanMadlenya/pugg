
        COMNAS-George Demetrie. Of Bridgehampton, NY, passed away on November 1, 1997. Born in Vancouver, BC, on April 22, 1913. George was raised in Cuero, Texas, received his B.S. in Chemical Engineering from Texas A&M University ('35); from 1936 to 1942 he served to Captain in the U.S. Army and was awarded Best Drilled Trooper in the U.S. Cavalry. He received an M.B.A. from New York University and was a student of Advanced Management Practices at Harvard University. George Comnas had a highly distinguished career as an oil and shipping executive, beginning in 1935 with Humble Oil & Refining in Baytown, Texas, continuing with Standard Oil Co. of New Jersey (Esso), based in New York, Caripito, Venezuela, London, England, Rio de Janeiro, Brazil and Geneva, Switzerland. He joined C.M. Lemos & Co., Ltd., as Managing Director and Chairman of the Board between 1969-74, thereafter serving as an oil and shipping consultant to several governments until retiring to Bridgehampton in 1987. George Comnas served as a senior warden and vestryman in both the Episcopal and Greek Orthodox churches throughout the world. He was a member of Shinnecock Hills G.C., Oyster Harbor G.C., Royal St. Georges G.C., Royal Wimbledon G.C., Golf Club de Geneve, Larchmont Yacht Club, and was a Knights Templar (Shriner). In 1976 he was named Distinguished Alumnus of Texas A&M University. George is survived by his loving wife, Jo Ann (Booth), his devoted children Basil G. Comnas, Demetrie G. Comnas and Aspasia Comnas Neuman, and by five adoring grandchildren. Reposing at the J.M.

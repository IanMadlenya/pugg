
        TURNBULL-William. 94, a lifetime resident of Far Hills, NJ, died at home October 23, 2002. Born in New York City December 6, 1907, Mr. Turnbull attended the Buckley School, St. Mark's School, Princeton University (1930), the Ecole des Beaux Arts, and Yale University School of Architecture. He then went on to work with Eero Saarinen at Cranbrook Academy of Art. He subsequently joined the family brokerage firm, Post and Flagg, in New York City. In 1938 he enlisted in the U.S. Navy and served as an O.S.S. officer in World War II. Following the war he founded United Instrument Corporation with his brother, Arthur. After the sale of the company in the late 1950's, he pursued his lifelong love of agriculture with interests in farms and ranches in Georgia, Colorado, California and New Jersey. In the 1960's he became a partner in the Ferris Corporation, a commercial real estate firm. Throughout his life Mr. Turnbull was a leader in and supporter of many community organizations, among which were the Sheltering Arms Children's Service, the Victoria Foundation, the Peapack-Gladstone Bank, the Lamington Presbyterian Church, the Somerset Agriculture Development Board, and the Upper Raritan Watershed Association. He is survived by his wife of 68 years, Elizabeth Howe Turnbull; his son, Thomas R. Turnbull; his daughters, Ellen T. Edwards and Margaret T. Simon; his daughters-in-law, Rosamond P. Turnbull and Mary E. Griffin; 12 grandchildren and five greatgrandchildren. He was predeceased by his eldest son, architect William Turnbull Jr. in 1997.

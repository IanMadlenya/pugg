
        BIERER-Frederick Bentley. Died June 21, 1999, at the age of 82, in New York City after a long illness. He was raised in Furth, Germany and joined The British Army in 1940, serving in the Pioneer, Commando, Tanks and Intelligence Corps. He emigrated to America in 1946, and married Sally Erlanger in 1950, who filled his heart. He founded the Bentley Industrial Corporation, a machine tool importing company that engaged his exceptional aptitude for finding solutions to life's problems, mechanical and othewise. Fred is survived by a grateful and happy family including his wife, three children, all of whom became physicians, their spouses and, as of now, five grandchildren. He enjoyed rich and long - lasting friendships throughout his life and, until his lasts minutes, was able to inspire and amuse those who loved him. The service will be held at Frank E. Campbell Funeral Home, 1076 Madison Avenue (at 81st Street), on Thursday June 24, 1999, at 4 P.M. Family flowers only; donations may be made in his name to The Hebrew Immigrant Aid Society, 333 Seventh Avenue, NY, NY 10001, an organization that assisted his family's escape from Germany.

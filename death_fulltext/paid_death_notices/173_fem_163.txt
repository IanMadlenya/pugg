
        HACKLEY-Helena J. Mother of four children, Emilia Fanjul, Anthony May, Helena Martinez & Sherlock Hackley, Jr., grandmother of eight grandchildren and one great granddaughter, died Monday, June 18, 2001, at Memorial Sloan-Kettering Cancer Center. Mrs. Hackley was born in Paris, France, on September 1, 1922, the daughter of the late Dr. Theodore Johnson and Emilia Aguilera of Havana, Cuba. She graduated from The Ethel Walker School and Vassar College with a degree in chemistry. Mrs. Hackley lived most of her life in the city she loved best, New York City. Here she devoted her time as an active volunteer at Memorial Sloan-Kettering Cancer Center beginning in 1974. She served on the Administrative Board of The Society of Memorial Sloan-Kettering and most recently was a member of The Society's Advisory Council. During her years at MSKCC, she headed, initiated and participated in many programs to improve patient care, especially for children with cancer. Visiting hours for Mrs. Hackley will be held at Frank E. Campbell Funeral Home, 1076 Madison Ave., at 81st Street on Tuesday, June 19 from 6PM-9PM. Mass service will be held on Wednesday, June 20th at 11 AM at St. Vincent Ferrer Roman Catholic Church on 66th St. and Lexington Ave. The burial will take place at 11:00 A.M. on Thursday, June 21st at Hillcrest Memorial Cemetery in West Palm Beach, Florida. In lieu of flowers, memorial contributions for Helena Hackley to support ovarian cancer research may be sent to The Society of Memorial Sloan-Kettering Cancer Center, Box E, 1275 York Avenue, New York, New York 10021.
        HACKLEY-Helena J. The Society of Memorial Sloan-Kettering mourns the passing of devoted Board Member, Helena Hackley. We offer our sincere condolences to her daughter-in-law and Society Past President, Mrs. M. Anthony May and to her entire family. Mrs. William M. Carson, Pres.

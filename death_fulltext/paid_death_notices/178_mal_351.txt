
        REIT-Seymour Victory. On November 21, 2001. Dearly beloved husband of Edmee (nee Busch), devoted brother to Phyllis and Irwin Nelson and Guy Busch, loving uncle to his many nieces, nephews and their children. Born on Armistice Day, 1918, he graduated from New York University and served with distinction as a Captain in the Army Air Corps in World War II. He began his career as a cartoonist and animator and created the character of Casper the Friendly Ghost. He was the author of many children's books and two works of nonfiction. Friends may pay respect at Frank E. Campbell, Madison Ave. at 81 St. on Monday, November 26, from 6 to 9 PM. A memorial service will be held there on Tuesday, November 27, at 11:30 AM, with interment to follow at Fairview Cemetery, Westfield, NJ. Contributions in his memory may be made to the School of American Ballet, 70 Lincoln Center Plaza, New York, NY 10023, or the organization of your choice.
        REIT-Seymour V. Adored brother and brother-in-law of Phyllis Reit Nelson and Irwin Nelson. He was one of the best, kind, gentle, loving and caring. Beloved uncle and great-uncle of Mary Marks, Alan Nelson, David Marks and Nina Schlosser. The world is poorer for his loss. He shall be deeply mourned by all who had the privilege of knowing him. Phyllis and Irwin

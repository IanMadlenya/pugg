
        OSTROM--Dorothy (Dodie) Miller, a life long resident of Greenwich, CT died peacefully in her home after a 20 year struggle with Alzheimer's disease on October 17th, 2006. She was born February 11th, 1919 in New York City to the late Drs. John and Katherine Miller. She graduated from Ethel Walker School and Sarah Lawrence College. Dodie was a noted athlete: she was an avid Equestrian and won many blue ribbons competing in the New York Horse Show at Madison Square Garden. She enjoyed fox hunting and rode with the Greenwich Foxhounds. Dodie was also an accomplished Golfer and competed successfully with the Westchester Fairfield Women's Golf Association. As a member of Round Hill Club, she won the Woman's Club Championship and was several times runner up. She also had a great passion for sailing and won many races with her late husband, Stuart Roesler aboard their boat, ''Amber.'' She also had a great love of gardening and flower arranging and she served two terms as President of The Greenwich Garden Club in 1980 and 1981. In 1950, she was the President of the Round Hill Community Guild. Dodie's father, John Miller, was a doctor in Greenwich and served as Chief of Staff at Greenwich Hospital until his death in 1950. Her mother was the first woman doctor to graduate from Cornell Medical School. Dodie was predeceased by her son John T. Davol and her first husband, Rector T. Davol, MD of Greenwich. She was also predeceased by her late husbands, M. Stuart Roesler and Vincent F. Ostrom. She is survived by her daughter, Gaynor D. Casner of Concord, MA, and her son, Ward M. Davol of Greenwich, CT. She also has 10 grandchildren and nine great-grandchildren. A memorial service will be at 2 PM on Monday, October 23rd, 2006 at Round Hill Community Church, 395 Round Hill Road, Greenwich, CT. A private burial will precead the service. In lieu of flowers contributions may be made to Alzheimer's Association, 255 N. Michigan Avenue, Chicago, IL 60601.

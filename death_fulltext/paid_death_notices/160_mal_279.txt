
        GLICK-Ira S. Passed away on May 22, 2000. He was born in Chicago in 1922, attended The University of Chicago and served in The US Air Force 1942-45. After moving to New York, he founded and was President of Glick & Lorwin, a management and educational consultant firm, from 1955 to 1992, when he retired. He then founded Sound Remedy, to assist organizations and provide aids and services for people with impaired hearing. For many years he was on the Boards for The Washington Square Association, The Washington Square Music Festival, and The 59 West 12th Street Condominium, as well as Bayberry Close, East Hampton. Mr. Glick was a lover of art works and a collector. He is survived by his wife Paula and a brother Larry, in Chicago. He will be greatly missed.

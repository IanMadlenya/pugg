
        HANLON-R. Brendan. Died of cardiac arrest at his home in Marshfield, MA, on January 2, 1999, at the age of 62. Brendan was an actor, author, accountant, jazz enthusiast, a rare literary man, and an illustrious raconteur on practically everything from the Marx Brothers to poet Seamus Heaney. He appeared in a number of films shot in Boston and New York, including ''A Civil Action'', ''Good Will Hunting'', ''Meet Joe Black'', ''The Spanish Prisoner'', and the 1998 film ''Dirty Laundry'', starring Jay Thomas, where he was featured as a gynecologist. Brendan wrote a book entitled ''The New Tax Guide for Performers, Writers, Directors, Designers and Other Show Biz Folk'', that remains a definitive guideline for artists and their taxes. Brendan was born in Boston, graduated from Boston Latin High School, earned a bachelor's degree in theater and speech at Emerson College, and did graduate work in theatre and literature at Smith College. He lived and worked in New York City before moving to Marshfield. He leaves behind his wife, Marjorie J. Hanlon, and his daughter Nell Anna O'Dea Hanlon of Marshfield, and his sisters and brothers all of Massachusetts: Kathleen Fulton and Maureen Smith of Sandwich, Robert Hanlon of Pembroke, and George Hanlon, of Carver. He also leaves behind 23 nieces and nephews as well as many, many broken-hearted friends and admirers. Brendan will survive in the memory of all who had the pleasure of knowing him. A memorial service will be held at 2 p.m. Saturday, at the First Parish Unitarian Universalist Church in Duxbury, MA, where Brendan had founded and operated the Fine Performance Concert Series and was also an active member. Burial services will be private. Contributions in his memory may be made to the Social Responsibility Committee of the First Parish Unitarian Universalist Church, P.O. Box 1764, Duxbury, MA. 02331-1764.


        KRONGELB-Irving. Died August 29, 2000, in New York City, age 82. He had a long career as Professor of Psychology and Director of Student Counseling at Brooklyn College, retiring in 1981. He was a devoted father, son, brother, husband, companion and friend, and dedicated his life to helping others. Irving is survived by his beloved parter, Ethel Elkin; son and daughter-in-law, Malcolm and Judy Krongelb of Acton, MA; daughter, Dory Beatrice of La Jolla, CA; and four sisters, Beatrice, Adele, Paula and Roslyn. He is remembered dearly by scores of friends and family who knew him as a nurturing, giving, gentle person. It was a privilege to have him in our lives.


        BARNARD--Edward T., of North Branford, CT, died on February 19 at Yale-New Haven Hospital. Born on October 10, 1910 in New Rochelle, he graduated from Yale in 1933. He worked for United Press before enlisting in the US Army Air Corps, serving in England during World War II and retiring as a Lieutenant Colonel in Military Intelligence. Appointed to the CIA in 1947, he served until his retirement in 1970. He was a certified professional genealogist and a member of the Baronial Order of the Magna Charta and the Descendants of the Illegitimate Sons and Daughters of the Kings of Britain. Predeceased by the mother of his children, Charlotte Williams Barnard, and his second wife, Jeanette Poore Barnard, he leaves behind his daughters, Sally B. Edwards of New Jersey and Jeanette T. Barnard of Maine, his son, Lt. Col. David W. Barnard, also of Maine, three grandchildren and four great-grandchildren. A memorial service will be held at Evergreen Woods in North Branford, CT, at 11:00 on Saturday, February 24. In lieu of flowers, contributions may be made to the Wellesley Students Aid Society (781283-2292), 106 Central Street, Wellesley, MA 02481.


        LEVY-Robert I., M.D. The boards, faculty and staff of Columbia University Health Sciences, The New York-Presbyterian Hospital, and The Joan and Sanford I. Weill Medical College of Cornell University mourn the loss of our colleague, Dr. Robert I. Levy. Dr. Levy served as Vice President for Health Sciences at Columbia University from 1983 to 1984, and as Senior Assistant Vice President for Health Sciences from 1984 to 1987. During that time, Dr. Levy was a Professor of Medicine at Columbia University College of Physicians & Surgeons and continued as an Adjunct Professor until his death. Before coming to Columbia, Dr. Levy served as Vice President for Health Sciences, Professor of Medicine and Dean of the School of Medicine at Tufts University. Earlier, he spent six years as Director of the National Heart, Lung and Blood Institute of the National Institutes of Health. Dr. Levy was a respected physician, educator and researcher. He received several prestigious awards throughout his career, including the Albert Lasker Special Public Health Award for his work in promoting high blood pressure detection and control. We extend our heartfelt condolences to his family and friends.


        VINEIS-Norman Demetrius. Age 83, long-time advertising agency and art studio principal died on Tuesday, April 14, 1998. Mr. Vineis was a co-founder of Monogram Art Studios; founder of Norman Affiliates Ltd. and the Vineis-Mead Agencies. He specialized in the field of creative pharmaceutical advertising until his retirement in 1979. Born in Haledon, N.J., he graduated Paterson Central High School and attended The School of Commerce of New York University. Enlisted in the Army-Air Force, was appointed an aviation cadet and was commissioned in 1943. Posted to the United Kingdom he served as Fighter Control Officer during the invasion of France. Mr. Vineis was a long-time resident of Haworth and member of First Congregational United Church of Christ, Haworth. He served on the Board of Governors of North Jersey Country Club and later became a member of Hackensack Country Club. He is survived by two sons; Paul Jonathan Vineis of Columbus, Ohio and Robert Scott Vineis of Malvern, PA. Also survived by two grandchildren; Drew Jonathan and Reid Patrick Vineis both of Columbus, Ohio. Predeceased by his beloved wife Myrtle M. (nee Lanigan). Funeral Service Friday, April 17, 1:30 PM at First Congregational United Church of Christ, 276 Haworth Avenue, Haworth. Interment George Washington Memorial Park, Paramus. Visiting 12 Noon to 1:15 PM at Church. For information, Volk-Leber Funeral Home, Oradell.

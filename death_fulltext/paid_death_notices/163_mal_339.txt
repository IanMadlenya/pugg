
        MAULE-Quentin F. Dr. August 30, 1919-March 16, 2000. Of Maryland and Florida. Died in Sarasota Fla. from Lymphoma Chemo complications. Burial was in Sand Springs, Ok., his birthplace. He received a Doctors Degree in Classical Art History from Berkeley and was an authority on Etruscan Bronzes. He had taught at Universities of Arkansas, Illinois and Fordham. He also worked at Hearst and Time Publications. He retired from the Smithsonian in 1983. In 1942 while attending the University of Chicago he unknowingly worked on the first atomic chain reaction. In 1944 he enlisted in the American Field Service. He donated his art book collection to the University of Arkansas. His love and wisdom are deeply appreciated. Happy birthday David.


        GILL--Madeleine Henriette. Of Water Mill, NY, and Staten Island, NY, on August 7, 2004. Beloved wife of the late Austin Gill. Loving mother of Helene A. Demisay. Dear mother-in-law of Nicholas Demisay. Cherished grandmother of Michael, Peter and Bernadette Demisay and greatgrandmother of Michelle, Danielle and Nicole Demisay. Madeleine Gill was a native of France. She was educated at the Sorbonne in Paris and held degrees in English Literature and Art History. She remarried in Scotland where she taught French at the University of Edinburgh. She was General DeGaulle's Representative in Scotland during WWII. She lived in Paris from 1945-1955 where her husband was the Director of the British Council. They lived in Oxford, England where her husband was a Professor of French at Magdalen College and where she taught French language. In the 70's, where they lived in Glasgow, Scotland, she was a special guide in the Glasglow Museum and the Burrell Collection, specializing in the art of the Middle Ages and French impressionist painting. She was an avid world traveler. She visited the United States in the summer for 30 years and came to stay permanently with her daughter in 1993.

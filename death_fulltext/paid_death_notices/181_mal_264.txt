
        LEWIS-Bernard, a former newspaperman and publicist born May 15, 1912, died February 11, 2002, worked as a reporter for the Old Brooklyn Daily Eagle and then for the Associated Press. Later he worked as a Publicist for Paramount Pictures, 20th Century-Fox, Italian Films Export, Kingsley International Pictures, The Brooklyn Opera Society and The Arts at St. Ann's. In the 1950s Mr. Lewis was instrumental in publicizing and introducing foreign films to an American audience. He will be profoundly missed. He is survived by his daughter Judy Nash, her husband, Tony Onorato, his son Andy Lewis and his wife Cindy, by six granchildren, two granddaughters-in-law and a great-granddaughter. His wife Florence of 63 years died in April, 2002.


        ELLIOT-''Win'' Irwin. Age 83 of Westport and Weston, CT, died peacefully at Norwalk Hospital on Thursday, September 17, 1998. Born in Chelsea, MA, he was a graduate of Everett High School & the University of Michigan, '37. He served with the U.S. Maritime Service 1939-1941. During a 50 year career as a nationally recognized radio and television emcee, he broadcast virtually every major sporting event from boxing to New York Ranger Hockey. He is best known for his 18 year award winning broadcasts of Sports Central USA on the CBS Radio Network, horse racing's Triple Crown, Football's Superbowl & baseball's World Series. However when asked, he wished to be remembered as a ''Great Dad.'' He is survived by his wife of 47 years Rita Elliot of Weston, CT; four daughters, Sue Elliot-Bearce, Marilyn McDonough, Patti Elliot & Kathy Cobb; 5 sons, Richard Elliot, Peter Elliot, Michael Elliot, Robert Elliot and Douglas Elliot, one brother; Biff Elliot and seventeen grandchildren. He was predeceased by his youngest son, Chuck and brother Stanley Shalek. All he has touched are invited to call at Shaughnessey Funeral Home, 50 Reef Road, Fairfield, CT., on Sunday, September 20, 1998 from 48pm. A Memorial Service will be held at St. Luke's Church, Turkey Hill Road, Westport, CT on Monday at 10:00 AM. In lieu of flowers it would be appreciated if donations were made in his memory to: The Chuck Elliot Memorial Award at The Staples Tuition Grants Committee, 70 North Avenue, Westport, CT. 06880.

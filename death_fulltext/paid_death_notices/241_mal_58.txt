
        WIPPERN--Ronald F., Ph.D. died on January 31, 2007 at his home in Napa, California after a prolonged illness. He was 73. Mr. Wippern was a consultant who lectured widely on the topics of valuation, corporate finance, strategy, and derivatives. He was a tenured Professor of Finance at the Graduate School of Management, Yale University and served on the business school faculties at Harvard, IMEDE, and Dartmouth. He was an extraordinary teacher who impacted the lives of many. He was witty, intelligent and was always impeccably dressed. Ron Wippern was born in West Virginia in 1933 and grew up on the south side of Chicago. He worked his way through school bagging groceries, waiting tables, and playing drums and vibes in bands. He loved jazz and sang, played the guitar, piano and other instruments throughout his life. He received his B.S. from the University of Colorado in 1954, was an officer in the Navy, then received his Masters from the University of Colorado in 1960. He was a Sloane Fellow at Stanford University where he completed his Ph.D. in 1964 and then he taught for several years at the University of Minnesota. He is the beloved father of Chris Wippern, Mitch Wippern, Joscelyn Wippern, and Stacy Pak and he will be sorely missed. A memorial service will be held at 10:00 am on February 17th at the Tulocay Cemetery Association in Napa, California. In his memory, donations may be made to the American Cancer Society.


        THRUSH-Donald Bell. Born in Brooklyn, NY on November 19, 1918, he died in Vero Beach, FL. on September 15, 2002. He served in W.W. II as Captain of the 464th Anti-Aircraft Battalion in China-Burma-India. He owned Thrush Press 1946-1988 and served the printing industry through local and national associations, most notably as past chairman of the Printing Industries of America and Graphic Arts Mutual Insurance Co. He and his son Bill ran a marketing service firm, Thrush from 1989 through 1996. He is survived by his wife of 50 years, Jocelyn Lake Thrush; three children: Lauriston Thrush Avery; William Lake Thrush and Dana Thrush Foote; seven grandchildren and his sister, Eugenie Thrush of Westport, CT. Memorial Service will be held on Saturday, September 21st at Trinity Episcopal Church in Vero Beach. In lieu of flowers, the family requests that donations be made to Trinity's ''Growth for Ministry Fund'', Trinity Church, 2365 Pine Ave, Vero Beach, Florida 32960.

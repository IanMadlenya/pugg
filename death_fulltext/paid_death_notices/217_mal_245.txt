
        GIMON--Jean-Paul, 68, died on Wednesday, February 16, 2005. He will be remembered for his distinguished career in international finance, his love of the mountains, his Catholic faith and philanthropic actions, and, above all, for his devotion to his family. Born in France, in 1936, he received a PhD in statistics from Stanford University. There he met Eleanor Hewlett who became his wife and the mother of his children: Nathalie, Eric, Juliette and Marianne. In 1971, Mr. Gimon joined the Credit Lyonnais and from 1984 to 1996, he was executive vice president and general representative of the bank in North America. Mr. Gimon served on the board of Hewlett-Packard from 1993 to 2000. In later years he applied himself to diverse philanthropic efforts, including the IHES in Paris, the Gimon collection at Stanford University. He is held dearly in the memory of his family and friends for his humor, grace, intelligence and selfless involvement in the life of others. Funeral Mass on Monday at 11:00 AM at St. Catherine of Siena, Riverside, Connecticut.

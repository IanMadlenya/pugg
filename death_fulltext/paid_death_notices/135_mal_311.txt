
        TATE-William. A longtime Stamford resident passed away at the Courtland Gardens Health Care Center on Wednesday, April 22, 1998 due to stroke. He was 87. Born in Hackensack, New Jersey on November 6, 1910, he was the son of the late Thomas and Sarah Tate. Mr. Tate was employed at the New York Times for forty-seven years from 1929-1976. He served in classified, national and international advertising. He was named Assistant Advertising Manager in 1946, Director of Special Advertising Project in 1965, International Advertising Manager in 1968 and Advertising Manager with responsibility for the retailadvertising department in 1973. A World War II army veteran, Mr. Tate was a sergeant in the Second United States Calvary from 1943 to 1945. He served as an Armored Car Commander during World War II and was distinguished with The Silver Star, The Purple Heart and The Bronze Star. He was predeceased by his sister Doris Mitros in 1997. He is survived by his wife Mildred A. Hemmerich Tate of Stamford, Connecticut. His 3 sons: Bill Tate of Palm Harbor, FL, Brad Tate of Oceanside, CA., and Allan Tate of Bedford, MA; his two daughters Joyce Robinson of Tolland, CT., and Elaine Hjelte of Ridgefield, CT.; his sister Ethel Bullett of Fairlawn, New Jersey and eight grandchildren. Funeral services will be held at the Arlington National Cemetery in Arlington, Virginia on Friday, May 1, 1998 at 2 PM. There will be no calling hours. For further information, contact Bouton & Reynolds Funeral Home, 2900 Summer Street, Stamford, Connecticut. In lieu of flowers, donations can be made to The Alzheimer's Association, Fairfield County, Chapter 607, Main Avenue, Norwalk, CT 06851.

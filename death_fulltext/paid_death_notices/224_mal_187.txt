
        WASHBURN--Stanley Jr. October 6, 1908--September 11, 2005. Born in Minneapolis, Minnesota, he graduated from Yale in 1931. During WWII, he was a pilot with cargo-carrying Air Transport Command. Shortly after, he organized the American Airship Company and co-piloted the 120 foot long non-rigid airship. His corporate career followed as System Promotion Director for Pan Am World Airways. Stanley invented the ''bongo board'' and wrote countless published articles and books. He is remembered by his devoted wife AnnaBell, daughter Nancy, son Brodie, four grandchildren, one great grandchild and beloved nephews Tyler and Thomas Leinbach. A memorial service will be held on October 8 at 11 AM at Christ Church United Methodist, 520 Park Avenue at 60th Street.


        CHARNEY-Hanna Kurz. A longtime French and comparative literature professor at Hunter College and the CUNY Graduate Center, died on March 30, 2002. She was 71. Ms. Charney began teaching at Hunter College in 1952 after she earned a Masters degree in French literature from Smith College. She received her Bachelors degree in French from Hunter College in 1951 and was a Phi Beta Kappa member. In 1956, Ms. Charney received her Ph.D. from Columbia University in French literature. At Hunter College, she served as chairwoman of the Romance Languages Department, acting executive director of the Ph.D. program in French and director of the CUNY baccalaureate program. She is survived by her husband Maurice Charney, two sons Leopold and Paul, daughterin-law Carina Yervasi, and her sister Renee Wiener. Jennifer J. Raab, President Hunter College

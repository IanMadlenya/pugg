
        HUPFEL-John G. Born in 1900 in New York City, died March 8, 1997, in Gulf Stream, Florida. He was 96 years old. He administered the last of New York's big real estate businesses based on the holdings of the early German brewing families. He worked for the J. Chr. G. Hupfel Co., Inc. while it was still a brewery and later became President. He was also with Peter Doelger, Inc., a former brewery that developed luxury apartment buildings. The family owned 350 properties in New York City before they sold out in 1961. Mr. Hupfel attended Andover and Cornell, then lived in Biarritz, France, for 15 years. He was a member of the New York Yacht Club; the Southport and Essex Yacht Clubs in Connecticut; the Gulf Stream Golf Club and Gulf Stream Bath and Tennis Clubs in Gulf Stream, Florida; and The Maidstone Club in East Hampton, New York. He is survived by his wife, Margaret Lampert Hupfel; four nephews, Manuel Sanchez de Movellan (Marques de Movellan), Juan Sanchez de Movellan and Pedro Sanchez de Movellan of Comillas, Spain and Antonio Sanchez de Movellan of Lexington, Kentucky; 14 grand nieces and nephews and 10 great-grand nieces and nephews; two stepchildren, Matt Spence of Boca Raton, Florida and Laurie Reeves of Lyme, Connecticut; and two step-grandchildren, Beth and Ross Spence. A memorial will be held at St. Vincent Ferrer Catholic Church, Delray Beach, Florida, on Wednesday, March 12, 1997 at 11 AM. Graveside services in Woodlawn Cemetery, Bronx, New York will be private.

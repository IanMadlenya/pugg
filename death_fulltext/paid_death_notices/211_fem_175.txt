
        WHITE--Marjorie (nee Taggart), M.D. Psychologist and psychoanalyst, died in New York Hosptial on August 17, 2004 at the age of 88. She had suffered from Alzheimer's disease for many years. Dr. White was born in Upstate New York and attended the University of Rochester, where she earned her undergraduate and graduate degrees. A pioneer in the selfpsychology movement, she enthusiastically mentored many psychologists. A lifelong member of the American Psychological Association, Dr. White was a prolific author of books and professional journal articles. Dr. White had been pre-deceased by her husband, Llewelyn ''John'' White, who had been an editor of Newsweek magazine. A memorial service was held on Monday, August 23, 2004. Contributions for research may be sent to the Alzheimer's Association, 180 North LaSalle St., Chicago, IL. 60601.


        BONO-Ludwig P. August 9, 1906October 15, 1999. A notable New York architect with an excellent reputation for high quality architectural and interior design. He designed many churches, chapels, convents, mausoleums and other quality monumental designs for the Catholic Archdiocese of New York and many religious buildings for New York Protestant and Jewish congregations. Mr. Bono was born in Jersey City, New Jersey; spent his early years in Pittsburgh, and his childhood and early youth in Biella, Italy, where he received a degree from the Biella Polytechnic Institute in 1927. He returned to the United States in 1928, working in the respected office of McKim, Meade, & White, assisting in the design of significant public buildings in Washington, D.C. In 1945, he opened his own office in the Bronx, New York, completing many small and large projects. He was the architect for the Stella D'Oro Biscuit Company designing their main headquarters in the Bronx, and many factories, restaurants, high quality residences in Riverdale, N.Y., and other projects in New York City and throughout the country. His specialty church and mausoleum projects were considered the very highest examples of this architectural genre at the time. Mr. Bono was granted honorary knighthood in the Knights of the Holy Sepulchre by the Catholic Church. He was preceded in death by his wife, Lea, and is survived by two sons: his son Richard, an architect in York, Pennsylvania; and his son, Robert of Staten Island, New York, and by a granddaughter, Lea. Mr. Bono was buried beside his wife in the Gate of Heaven Cemetery in Westchester, N.Y. on Wednesday, October 20, 1999. Services were held in the beautiful stained glass St. Francis Chapel he designed.

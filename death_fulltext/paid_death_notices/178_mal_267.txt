
        RAUCH-R. Stewart. 87, at his home in Bryn Mawr, PA, November 16, 2001, after a brief illness. Born in Villanova, PA, on July 15, 1914, he attended the Montgomery Country Day School and graduated from St. Paul's School, Concord, NH. A member of the Class of 1936 at Princeton Unversity, he was elected to the Phi Beta Kappa Society and was a member of the Ivy Club. He held a LLB from the University of Pennsylvania and honorary degrees from Swarthmore College and Temple University. He was married to the late Frances Brewster Rauch on June 7, 1941, and is survived by a sister, Paton Rauch Roberts of South Natick, MA, a brother, Thomas M. Rauch of Easton, MD; five children, nine grandchildren and one great-grandchild. He was associated with the law firm of Barnes, Dechert, Price, Myers and Clark from 1941-1949, except for service as lieutenant with the U.S. Navy from 1942-1945. He joined the Philadelphia Saving Fund Society in 1949, and served as President from 1955-1971, and as Chairman from 1971-1979. He was a member of the Board of Directors of many corporate and civic organizations, including Bell Telephone of Pennsylvania, Delaware River Port Authority, General Accident Fire and Life Association, Girard Trust Bank, Penn Central Corp., Pennsylvania Railroad, The Philadelphia Contributionship, The Philadelphia Orchestra Association, the Urban Affairs Partnership. An early and fervent advocate of civil rights, he received the Philadelphia Award for the individual who advances ''the best and largest interest of the communty'' in 1978. Burial will be private; a memorial service will be held Monday, December 17, at 11:30 AM, at St. Christopher's Church, Gladwyne, PA. In lieu of flowers, contributions may be sent to The American Philosophical Society, f/b/o The Rauch Scholars Fund, 104 South Fifth Street, Philadelphia, PA 19106.


        LAVINE--Leroy S. A practicing orthopedic surgeon and researcher in NY for more than 30 years, died on July 25 in Chapel Hill, NC. He was 86. Born in Jersey City, NJ, he received his undergraduate and medical degrees from NYU. He was a Captain in the US Army in WW II. Dr. Lavine was Chief of Orthopedic Surgery at LIJ Hillside Medical Center (1956-81), Professor and Head of Orthopedic Surgery at SUNY Downstate Medical Center (1964-81), Associate Director of the Biophysics Research Lab at NYU (l967-81). After retiring as a surgeon, Dr. Lavine began a second career in Boston in 1983 that lasted nearly 20 years. He was Professor at the MGH Institute of Health Professions, Chief of Orthopedic Rehabilitation at Spaulding Hospital and Lecturer at Harvard Medical School. Dr. Lavine was active in many professional organizations. He was Chairman of the Orthopedic section of the NY Academy of Medicine, President of the Bioelectric Repair and Growth Society, advisor to the NIH, member of the Orthopedic Devices Panel of the FDA and Examiner for the American Board of Orthopedic Surgery. He was a member of Sigma Xi, the Cosmos Club, the American Orthopedic Association and a Fellow of the American Association for the Advancement of Science. A proponent of the arts, Dr. Lavine was a member of the MIT Council for the Arts and on the Advisory Board of MIT's List Visual Arts Center. Dr. Lavine's wit and humor won him many friendships. He kept his wry sense of humor to the end. He is survived by his wife of 59 years, the former Dorothy Kopp, his children, Shaughan of Tucson and Nancy Warwick of Chapel Hill, and his three grandchildren, Caila, Ryan and Zachary.

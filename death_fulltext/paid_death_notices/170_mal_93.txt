
        McALLISTER-Donald H. A former Partner, Managing Director and, most recently, an Advisory Director of Morgan Stanley Dean Witter, died on March 6, 2001, in Orlando, Florida. Mr. McAllister was born March 21, 1925 in New Haven, Connecticut. The son of Harold C. and Carolyn H. McAllister, he was raised in Manchester, New Hampshire. He graduated from Dartmouth College in 1946 and received an MBA from the Amos Tuck School of Business Administration in 1948. Mr. McAllister served in the U.S. Navy in the Pacific Theater during the Second World War. Following graduation from Amos Tuck, Mr. McAllister worked for R. P. Burroughs Co., an insurance broker, before returning to Asia with the Navy in 1951 and 1952, during the Korean Conflict. In 1953 Mr. McAllister joined Morgan Stanley & Co.'s corporate finance department, specializing in the consumer and oil service industries. Mr. McAllister was elected a Partner in 1963, a Managing Director in 1970 when the company incorporated and an Advisory Director in 1990. He played an important role in founding the firm's equity and research departments. Mr. McAllister lived in Greenwich, Connecticut and is survived by his wife, Katherine; his son Donald, Jr., his daughter-in-law, Ellen, and grandsons, Donald III and Edward McAllister; his daughter, Wendy; and his son, Malcolm and his daughter-in-law, Diddle. A memorial service will be held Tuesday, March 13, 2001, at 11:00AM at the Round Hill Community Church, Round Hill Road, Greenwich, Connecticut. In lieu of flowers, contributions may be sent to the Boys and Girls Club of Greenwich, Connecticut.

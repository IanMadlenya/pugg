
        FRAZER-William L. Died on January 19, 1999 at home after a long bout with lung disease. Born in Brooklyn on March 11, 1917 to Charles and Lela Frazer, Mr. Frazer grew up in Brooklyn Heights. After graduating from Pratt Institute in 1938, he went on to a very successful career as an artist, photographer and advertising art director, recently retired from McCann Erickson. Mr. Frazer served in WWII as US Army Sergeant and was a contributing editor of Yank Magazine. Along with his other accomplishments, he was a longtime member of the Society of Illustrators, official artist for the US Coast Guard, active member of the World Ship Society, NYC Sierra Club, National Audubon and was a thoroughbred horse owner. Mr. Frazer is survived by a niece Frederica King, R.N. and her husband Tim and grandnephew Christian Cerone. Friends may call at Frank E. Campbell, 1076 Madison Avenue at 81 Street, Thursday and Friday 2-5 & 7-9PM. Funeral Saturday, January 23, 10AM.
        FRAZER-William. The Officers and members of the Society of Illustrators deeply mourn the passing of beloved member Bill Frazer. He will be missed by all who knew him.

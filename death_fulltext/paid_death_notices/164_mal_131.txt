
        LANG-Martin. On September 4, 2000. Born January 28, 1915 in Brooklyn, NY. Martin was the devoted husband of Bertha Friedman Lang for 62 years, loving father to Judith Lang Zaimont, Doris Lang Kosloff and Robert David Lang and grandfather to Adam Lang Harris Kosloff, Daniel Max Lang Kosloff, Jordan Scott Berman Lang and Michael Elliot Lang Zamont. In his 40 year career of outstanding service to the City of NY, he served as Commissioner of the Departments of Water Resources, Sanitation, and Park and Recreation, as well as being the first Deputy Administrator of the Environmental Protection Administration. He was the President of the National Water Pollution Control Federation and was elected to the National Academy of Engineering. He became the President of the Management Consultant Division of Camp, Dresser & McKee, consulting worldwide on water quality management. Martin graduated from CCNY (BS) and NYU (MS). He served with distinction in WW II as 2nd Lt. in the Army Engineering Corps, and taught Chemistry at LIU and Engineering Management at CCNY. Among his many awards are CCNY's Townsend Harris Medal in 1973 and the David D. Steinman Medal in 1985. Memorial contributions may be made to the American Association of Kidney Patients, 100 South Ashley Drive, Suite 280, Tampa, Fla. 33602. Services today, September 5 at 12:30 at Riverside-Nassau North Chapels, 55 North Station Plaza, Great Neck, NY.

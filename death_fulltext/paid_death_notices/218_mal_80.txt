
        TRAFLET--Robert F., Sr., passed away on Monday, February 14, 2005 after a brief battle with cancer. Mr. Traflet founded Neumann Advisors, Inc, an equity management firm, where he worked until his recent illness. Mr. Traflet spent most of his career at Allied Signal Corporation. He joined the company in 1958 and continued there for more than thirty years, holding a variety of management positions including the office of president of three divisions: Plastics and Functional Chemicals, Fibers, and Fluorine Products. In 1989, he testified on behalf of Allied Signal before a US Senate Committee on the subject of ozone depletion. While at Allied Signal, Mr. Traflet was also appointed to serve as a member of New Jersey Governor Brendan T. Byrne's Capital Needs Commission. For many years, Mr. Traflet was active in St. Anne's Parish, Fair Lawn, New Jersey and had served as president of their Board of Education. He also served as President of the Board of Education of the Archdiocese of Newark. More recently, he was a member of the board of directors of the Picatinny Technical Innovation Center. Mr. Traflet held a Bachelor of Science degree in Chemical Engineering from Villanova University and a Master's of Business Administration from Fairleigh Dickinson University. The funeral was held at Mt. Carmel RC Church on February 18. In lieu of flowers, donations in Robert Traflet's memory may be sent to St. Vincent's Hospital, NY, NY

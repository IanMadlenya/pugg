
        YAMADA-Tadayoshi, one of Japan's leading internationalists, passed away on November 6, 1999. He was born on January 10, 1909 in Tottori Prefecture, Japan, received a Bachelor of Laws degree from Meiji University in 1933 and a Master of Arts degree in American history from Columbia University in 1935. He was Senior Managing Director at Yawata Iron and Steel Company until 1969 and then Senior Executive Adviser at Nippon Steel until 1981. A founding Director of Malayawata Steel Corporation and a Director of Honshu Rheem Corporation, he also served as Vice Chairman of the Japan-America Society and as President of the Canada-Japan Society. Among his many accomplishments, he negotiated the first loan from the World Bank to a Japanese company after World War II. Morever, Mr. Yamada helped found the World Trade Centers Association in 1970 and served as Vice President and Secretary from 1970 until 1987, when he was elected Chairman. Today, the Association has 330 member organizations in 98 countries. He also was Chairman of the World Trade Center in Tokyo from 1974 to 1999. In a long and productive career, he received numerous awards, including the Order of the Sacred Treasure from the Emperor of Japan and both Chevalier de l'Ordre de la Couronne and Officier de l'Ordre de la Couronne from the King of Belgium. Mr. Yamada's wife of 50 years, Takako Takami Yamada, died in 1985. He is survived by daughter Kazuko Nitta of Tokyo, Japan and sons Akiyoshi Yamada of Miami, FL., and Tadataka Yamada of Philadelphia, PA., and seven grandchildren.

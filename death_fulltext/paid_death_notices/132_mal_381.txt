
        TISON-Joseph Southwood. Of Wilton Road West in Ridgefield, CT., died at his home Tuesday, January 27, after a long battle with cancer. He was 54. Born June 23, 1943, in Savannah, Georgia, he was the son of the late Henry Lawton and Edna Williamson Tison of Savannah, GA. A graduate of the Savannah Country Day School, he received his Bachelor's Degree from the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill and his Master's Degree in Business from Old Dominion University. He served as an officer in the U.S. Navy. For the past fifteen years he worked in New York City as General Manager of the North American operations of Citrus Products, Inc. During this time he served as Chairman of the Membership Committee, Director, Treasurer, Secretary, and Vice President of the Brazilian American Chamber of Commerce in New York and as a member of the Board of Directors of Citrus Associates of the New York Cotton Exchange, Inc. An active member of St. Stephen's Episcopal Church in Ridgefield, CT., he is survived by his wife, Sarah, and four children: Kate, Joseph, Lawton, and Fletcher, and his brother Henry and sister Lucy, both of Savannah, GA. Services will be held at 11 A.M. on Saturday, January 31st, at St. Stephen's Church. Burial will be at the convenience of the family in Black Swamp Cemetery in Garnett, S.C. In lieu of flowers, contributions in his memory may be made to St. Stephen's Church, Ridgefield; the Visiting Nurse Association of Ridgefield; or for cancer research to the Cornell University Medical College, C/O Dr. David Wolf, 115 East 61 Street, NYC, NY 10021.

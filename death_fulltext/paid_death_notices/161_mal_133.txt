
        BENTON-John H. Educational film executive and a magazine publisher passed away at home in Los Angeles, California, on 16 February 2000 at the age of 57. Born in Chicago, Illinois, John was reared in Southport, Connecticut and Phoenix, Arizona. He was a graduate of Deerfield Academy, Yale University and King's College, Cambridge University. He worked with UNESCO in Paris prior to joining Encyclopaedia Britannica, of which his father, Senator William Benton was publisher. In 1973 he became Chairman of The Board of Britannica's Educational Film Subidiary. A race car participant and enthusiast, he also published Racecar Magazine. After relocating to California in the late 1970's, he deepened his lifelong relationship with Taliesin and served on The Board of The Frank Lloyd Wright Building Conservancy. He is survived by his children, William Worth Benton and Lillian Hemingway Benton as well as a sister, Helen B. Boley and a brother, Charles Benton. His sister, Louise H. Benton Wagner, predeceased him.

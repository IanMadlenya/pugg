
        GALIMIR-Felix. The Board of Trustees and Staff of Carnegie Hall mourn the passing of Felix Galimir. A musician's musician who appeared on the Carnegie Stage more than a dozen times, he had a central role in the training of generations of some of America's finest musicians. The entire Carnegie Hall family feels his loss. He inspired a legacy that will endure for generations. Isaac Stern, President Sanford I. Weill, Chairman Franz Xaver Ohnesorg, Executive and Artistic Director
        GALIMIR-Felix The Israel Philharmonic Orchestra and its American friends mourn the passing of Felix Galimir, a founding member of the Israel Philharmonic Orchestra, who performed as first violin with the Orchestra from it inaugural year in 1936 to 1938. We extend our deepest sympathies to the entire family. Zubin Mehta and Itzhak Perlman Co-Chairmen Herman S. Sandler, President Avi Shoshani, IPO, Secretary General
        GALIMIR-Felix. With the passing of Felix Galimir, we have lost not only a vital musician and gentle and beloved friend, but also an artist who was influenced by, and knew intimately, some of the seminal musical and creative minds of the twentieth century. All those who were touched by his knowledge and devotion to musical truth, should count themselves blessed and fortunate. Isaac Stern

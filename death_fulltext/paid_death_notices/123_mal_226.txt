
        HUDSON-John. Family and friends mourn John's passing on April 12, 1997, in Portland, Oregon. John was a labor union and human rights activist his entire life. Born February 26, 1949, in Danville, Illinois, he completed undergraduate studies in economics at the University of Illiniois, Urbana in 1971, and graduate studies in community and social psychology at Goddard College. He then lived and worked in New York City for over 20 years. His abstract oil paintings were included in a number of gallery exhibits in New York. He married Patricia Westwater in New York, in November 1995. They moved to Portland in January 1996. His parents, John and Helen, and his brother Joseph and his family live in Seattle. Disposition was by cremation. John requested that in lieu of flowers, contributions go to the Leukemia Society of America, 600 3rd Ave, New York, 10016. A memorial service will be held on May 10, at 1:00PM, Lafayette Avenue Presbyterian Church, 85 South Oxford Street, Brooklyn.
        HUDSON-John. The officers, staff and members of UNITE mourn the untimely passing of an heroic labor leader and human rights activist on April 12, 1997 in Portland, Oregon. John served as a leader of ACTWU and UNITE for 20 years. He worked extensively with South African unions and the anti apartheid movement and recently served as a consultant to American and South African unions. He was also an accomplished visual artist. John was ceaseless in his determination to live life to its fullest and to create a better life for others. He inspired all those whose lives he touched. Our condolences go out to his wife, Patricia Westwater, to his parents, John and Helen, and to his brother Joseph and family. UNITE Union of Needletrades, Industrial and Textile Employees

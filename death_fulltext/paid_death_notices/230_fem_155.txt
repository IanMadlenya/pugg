
        LINDEN--Patricia, 80, of New York City. On March 9th. She had been in failing health for several years. She leaves behind her beloved son and daughter-in-law, Peter and Joanne Linden of Houston and her grandson, Christopher Linden of Nashville. A highly regarded editor, journalist and author, she wrote a number of in-depth articles on a wide range of topics for a number of magazines. She served as Contributing Editor for Boston Magazine and Town & Country Magazine under Frank Zachary for whom she wrote primarily in the 1970s and 1980s. She also was a contributing writer for such publications as Avenue, Esquire, Harper's Bazaar, Reader's Digest, Travel & Leisure, Ultra and Veranda. Additionally, she briefly published I.D. Confidential, a newsletter for interior design professionals. In the early part of her career in Boston, she worked as a copywriter and became the owner of an advertising agency, Imagination, Inc. Later she became Editor of Epicure Magazine. Published under the aegis of S.S. Pierce Co., Epicure was one of the first glossy magazines devoted to fine cuisine. Ms. Linden was also the author of several books. Her titles included: Cooking with Bernard, Levin's Laws, Living in Style, The Woman's Selling Game and Women & Work. Funeral services will be private.

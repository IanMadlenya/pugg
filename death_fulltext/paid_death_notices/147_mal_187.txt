
        BLIT-Roman A. Died peacefully on April 23, 1999 at age 92. He left Warsaw after the German Occupation of Poland in 1939, and through Russia and Lithuania, came to New York in April 1941. He was an intelligent and educated man who worked closely with the Labor Movement. He was employed by the ILGWU for many years. He was devoted to his family. His passing is marked with great sorrow by Helen, his wife of 49 years, his daughter Barbara, his grandson Zachary, and all who knew him. Burial will be private.

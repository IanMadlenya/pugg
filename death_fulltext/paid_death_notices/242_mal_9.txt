
        MACKOWSKI--John J. Dies at 81. John J. Mackowski, insurance executive and proponent for corporate responsibility and ethical practice, died of lung cancer February 24, at his home in Ponte Vedra Beach, Florida. He was 81. Mr. Mackowski was the former Chairman and CEO of The Atlantic Companies, a mutual insurance company headquartered in New York, which he joined in 1951. He was a Director and Chair of the audit committee of Transatlantic Holdings, and was a former director and Chairman of the audit committee of F.W. Woolworth Co. through its transition to Venator Group, Inc. Additionally, he was a director of the Insurance Information Institute, The Seamen's Church Institute of New York and the American Bureau of Shipping. Mr. Mackowski, throughout his career, advanced issues of corporate fiscal responsibility, business ethics, and community service. In the 1980s, he was a member of New York's Trinity Center for Ethics and Corporate Responsibility as well as Director and Vice Chairman of the YMCA of Greater New York. In 2006 he endowed the John J. and Ruth W. Mackowski Professorship in Organizational Ethics at Duke University. Mr. Mackowski graduated from Duke University and Harvard Business School's Advanced Management Program. He served in the U.S. Marine Corps during World War II as a first Lieutenant. Mr. Mackowski was born in Westport, MA. He is survived by his wife Ruth Williams Mackowski and their four children, Marty Jones of Ponte Vedra Beach, FL, J.

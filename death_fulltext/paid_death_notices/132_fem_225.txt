
        GOLDSTEIN-Syd Rossman. In her 95th year. From 1958 to 1962, she was National President of Woman's League for Conservative Judaism. She was currently National Treasurer of the Torah Fund Campaign. She leaves a daughter and son-in-law. Arlene and Dr. Edgar Braunstein, daughter-in-law Barbara Rossman, brother and sister-in-law Harold and Leah Sher. Grandchildren Vicki Braunstein and Dr. Steven and Laurie Braunstein, Dr. Barry and Terri Rossman, Lauri and Paul Feldman and her beloved great - grandchildren, Melissa, Stephanie, Carly, Jake, Andrew and Michael. She was beloved by all who knew her. She was predeceased by her husbands, Dr. Herbert Rossman and Louis Goldstein and a son, Dr. Melvin Rossman. Funeral service on Thursday, 1:15 P.M., at ''The Riverside'', 76 Street and Amsterdam Avenue.
        GOLDSTEIN-Syd Rossman. The administration, faculty, students and board of the Jewish Theological Seminary record with sorrow the passing of Syd Rossman Goldstein, an honorary member of the JTS board and lifelong activist on behalf of the Seminary and Conservative Jewry. At 95, she served as the national treasurer of the annual Torah Fund campaign of Women's League for Conservative Judaism, marking more than 50 years of uninterrupted service. She broke new ground for women in the many leadership roles she held, most notably as the first woman member of the JTS board's executive committee. She is survived by a daughter, four grandchildren and five greatgrandchildren. May her memory be for a blessing. Ismar Schorsch, chancellor Gershon Kekst, board chairman
        GOLDSTEIN-Syd R. You were a woman of virtue and dignity. In your untiring gracious manner, you taught us all the meaning of family. We will miss your ever present smile and incomparable guidance. Lee Kahn

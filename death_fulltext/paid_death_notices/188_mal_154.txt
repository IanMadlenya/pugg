
        COX-William W. Lawyer for the United Nations and former resident of Manhattan, died August 18, 2002 at The Quadrangle in Haverford, PA. He was 88. A Phi Beta Kappa graduate of Ohio Wesleyan University, he earned a law degree from Columbia University in 1939, where he was an editor of the Law Review. After naval intelligence service in London during World War II, he worked for the United Nations until his retirement in 1974. His career spanned a broad range of service as UN lawyer in many of the world's hot spots during the late 1940's, 1950's and 1960's, including Vienna, Geneva, Bolivia, Jerusalem, Gaza, and the Congo. In later years, he served in a variety of deputy director positions at UN headquarters. He is survived by his wife of 56 years Nancy, whom he met in London during the war when they both worked in intelligence translating German documents. Other survivors include nieces Marguerite Bartlett and Barbara Prunty. No memorial service is planned. Memorial contributions may be made to Planned Parenthood, 810 Seventh Avenue, NY, NY 10019.


        MANTON--Sir Edwin A.G. The family of Sir Edwin A.G. Manton mourns his death of natural causes, at the age of 96, on October 1. Born in Essex, England, on January 22, 1909, Sir Edwin lived almost his entire adult life in New York City as a successful senior executive of American International Group, Inc., and as an active philanthropist and leader of his church. Married for 67 years to the former Florence Vivian Brewer, who died in 2003, he is survived by his daughter, Diana H. Morton, his two granddaughters, Julia Grenville Morton Krapf and Sandra Toby Morton Niles, and five great grandchildren, all of Massachusetts. He was knighted by Queen Elizabeth in 1994 for his services to charity. A memorial service will be announced at a later date. In lieu of flowers, the family requests that contributions be sent to The Church of The Ascension, 12 West 11th Street, New York, NY 10011, or to The American Fund for the Tate Gallery, C/O Sullivan & Cromwell LLP, 125 Broad Street, New York, New York 10004-2498.
        $(6$)MANTON--Sir Edwin A.G. The entire St. Luke's-Roosevelt Hospital Center (SLR) family joins with the leadership of its parent company, Continuum Health Partners, in acknowledging with sadness the passing of our dear friend and longtime colleague, Sir Edwin A.G. Manton, a former Trustee of the Continuum Board. Sir Manton also served for many years on the St. Luke's-Roosevelt Board of Trustees as a representative of the St. George Society, which helped to establish St. Luke's Hospital. He will forever be remembered as an outstanding business leader and a devoted champion of excellence in medicine. To his family, friends and loved ones, we extend heartfelt condolences. Morton P.


        BERNE-Robert M., M.D. Former professor and chair of the Department of Physiology at the University of Virginia, School of Medicine, died at his home in Charlottesville, Virginia, Thursday, October 4, 2001. An alumnus of the University of North Carolina and Harvard Medical School he authored over 200 scientific papers, and several textbooks. He co-developed the medication Adenocard, used in the treatment of cardiac arrhythmias, for which he received the American Heart Association's 1979 Research Achievement Award and Merit Award and the 1985 Gold Heart Award as well as the Lifetime Achievement Award of the Science Museum of Virginia in 1989. He was elected to the Institute of Medicine in 1979 and the National Academy of Sciences in 1988. Royalties from the Adenocard patent funded the Robert M. Berne Professorship in Cardiovascular Research, established in 1992. In addition to his scientific interests, Dr. Berne was an avid fisherman, hunter, tennis player, & guitarist. Bob Berne is survived by his wife of 57 years, Beth Goldberg; a sister, Peggy Leavitt; four children and their spouses, Julie & Phil Speasmaker, Amy and Steve Kaminshine, Gordon and Rosalyn Berne, and Michael and Mary Berne as well as 8 grandchildren. A memorial service will be held at the University of Virginia Chapel in Charlottesville on October 21, 2001, at 12PM. In lieu of flowers, memorial donations may be made to the Robert M. Berne Educational Fund, CVRC, Univ. of Virginia, 409 Lane Road, MR-4 Bldg., Room 6032, Charlottesville, VA. 22908.

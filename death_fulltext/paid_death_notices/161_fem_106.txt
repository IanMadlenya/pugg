
        WEEBER-Marion. New York industrial designer of silverware and jewelry, died June 12, 2000 of a heart attack at Bellvue Hospital at the age of 94. She was trained at The Arts Students League of New York, where she became a Life Member, Honorary Member of The American Federation of Arts, and President of her Manhattan design firm, Marion Weeber, Inc., for fifty years, contributing awardwinning design to leading American manufacturers and importers. Her inventions, designs, paintings and drawings are in museums and private collections. She is survived by two stepchildren and their families, and was the widow of the late Paul N. Welsh, advertising executive. Her father was the late Christian F. Weeber, pioneer automotive and aeronautic inventor, of Albany, NY. Visitation Wednesday 7-9 P.M. at Frank E. Campbell, The Funeral Chapel, 1076 Madison Avenue at 81st Street, then on Thursday 4-8 at The Frederick Funeral Home, 633 Central Avenue, Albany, NY. Services 11:30 Friday, with burial to follow. in Albany Rural Cemetery.

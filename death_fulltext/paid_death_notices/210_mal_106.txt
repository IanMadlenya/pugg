
        BENASULI--Alan. Of NYC and Shelter Island, NY, on July 10, 2004, peacefully, at his home on Shelter Island where he was a summer resident for the last 35 years. He was born on May 22, 1940, in Tangiers, Morocco. He is survived by his beloved wife Nancy Cooley, and their son James, as well as his son Alex and daughter Marina, children from a first marriage. Alan completed a joint business and law degree at Hautes Etudes Commerciales (HEC) in Paris in 1964. He went on to receive an MBA from Harvard Business School in 1966. Alan pursued a long and illustrious career on Wall Street, achieving a ranking as one of the street's leading areospace and defense analysts at Drexel Burnham Lambert, and subsequently as a member of the Executive Committee and Director of Global Research at Smith Barney, and until recently as a Managing Director and Partner at European Investors in New York. In lieu of flowers, the family requests that contributions be made to the New York Brain Tumor Project at Weill Cornell Medical Center, NYC, the East End Hospice, Westhampton, NY, and the St. Bernard's School, NYC.

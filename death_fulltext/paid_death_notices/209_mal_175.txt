
        BASCOM--Charles. Born in NYC on July 3, 1937, He served in the United States Navy from 1954-1958 and received an honorable discharge. In 1966 he went to work for the New York Times and worked there until the time of his death. Charles met Sylvia Yarrell of Brooklyn, NY and was married in 1965. He was a member of Unity of Christianity Church in Valley Stream, NY. Charles was affectionately called ''Chuck'' by his family and friends and was a lover of golf, travel and keeping up with world news. He leaves to mourn his passing, a loving wife of 39 years, Sylvia and two children, Todd and Nasya; one daughter-in-law, Andrea; one brother, Lionel, four sisters, Pat, Nancy, Barbara, all of Danbury, CT, Eleanor of Norwalk, CT, two brothers-in-law, Willie Knight of Danbury, CT and Leander Yarrell of Laurelton, NY; several cousins, nieces, nephews, other relatives and friends. Funeral services will be held at J. Foster Phillips Funeral Home, Inc. 179-24, Linden Boulevard, Jamaica, NY, Thursday, June 17 at 10 AM (718) 526-5656. Interment Calverton National Cemetery. Donations can bemade to the American Cancer Society or Columbia Presbyterian Hospital, Kidney Transplant Center in his name.
        BASCOM--Charles. The New York Times records with deep sorrow the passing of Charles Bascom, associated with The Times from June 1967.

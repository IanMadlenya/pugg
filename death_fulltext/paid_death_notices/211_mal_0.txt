
        EHRSAM--Theodore George, Ph.D. An accomplished author and Professor Emeritus of English at New York University, died of natural causes on July 30, 2004 at Hackensack Medical Center in Hackensack, NJ at the age of 94. Theodore George Ehrsam was born December 7, 1909 in New York, NY. He received his bachelor's degree from Lehigh University in 1931 and his master's degree in 1932. Prior to receiving his Ph.D. from New York University in 1948, Theodore taught as an instructor of English at Lehigh University, Hofstra College, and New York University. His tenure at NYU as Professor of English spanned 31 years. Dr. Ehrsam was the author of ''Major Byron,'' 1951, co-author of ''The Shelley Legend,'' 1945, and compiled the Bibliography of Joseph Conrad, 1969. In addition, Dr. Ehrsam was the Editor of the Handbook of Alpha Kappa Psi, 1993. Dr. Ehrsam was the President of the Board of Trustees for the River Vale Public Library from 1991-2003. He served as an honorary Commodore of the Lake Hopatcong Yacht Club since 1991. Dr. Ehrsam was a member of the Modern Language Association (MLA), the Thoreau Society, and the Lake Hopatcong Historical Society. He received the Distinguished Service Award from Alpha Kappa Psi in 1993. Dr. Ehrsam is survived by Marcia, his wife of 28 years, his daughter, Jean Sammons, from his first marriage to Wanda Budd (deceased 1975), and his daughters, Jaye Meyers and Susan Kaplan. His infectious love of life will live on through his family, particularly his six grandchildren and three greatgrandchildren.

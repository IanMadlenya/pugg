
        MYERS-Professor Charles A., an expert in labor economics, management, and education and development, died April 2, 2000, age 86, in Naples, Florida. Mr. Myers was a professor of economics and management at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology for 42 years and author of more than 85 books and articles. He was born in 1913 in State College, Pennsylvania and graduated from Penn State University. He earned a Ph.D in economics from the University of Chicago. From 1942 to 1945 he served on the War Production Board and the War Labor Board; from 1957 to 1959 on the Federal Advisory Council on Employment Security; from 1960 to 1962 on the Presidential Railroad Commission; and from 1969 to 1971 on the National Manpower Policy Task Force. He was a Fellow of the American Academy of Arts and Sciences, President of the Industrial Relations Research Association, and Charter Member of the National Academy of Arbitrators. His academic work included pioneering studies of U.S. labor markets; of comparitive labor relations in OECD and developing countries; and of the relationship between investment in education and the rate and nature of economic and social development. He leaves his wife of 63 years, Nancy (Nash); a son, Charles N. of Duxbury, MA; 2 daughters, Anne M. Forsyth of Midlothian, VA and Temson M. Ely of Amherst, MA; a sister Christine Ellenburger of Fairfield, CT; seven grandchildren and two great grandchildren.

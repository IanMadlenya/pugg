
        BUTLER-John F. Longtime enthusiast of thoroughbred racing and a director of the National Horse Show Association of America, died July 22 at age 90 in his Manhattan home after a three year illness following a stroke. Mr Butler, born in New York City on September 2, 1907, graduated Georgetown University, served as Commander U.S. Navy World War II, and in Naval Reserve, retired in 1963 with permanent rank of Commander. Survivors include his wife Marion Donovan, his nieces Mary and Amy Betancourt, step children Christine, Sharon, and Dr. James Donovan. Memorial service will be announced later.


        GORDON-Alvin J., M.D. Died on July 10, 2002 at age 87 after a short illness. He died at Mount Sinai Hospital where he worked as a cardiologist from 1938-2001, heading the Cardiac Catheterization Team for 25 years and serving as Clinical Professor of Medicine. He was also Chief of Cardiology at Doctor's Hospital, later Beth Israel North, and maintained a private practice in cardiology for over 50 years. During his lifetime, he published over 50 medical papers and was a pioneer in cardiac catheterization research. He also served as a Naval Medical Officer in the South Pacific during W.W. II. Dr. Gordon loved the outdoors and enjoyed tinkering in his workshop. He is survived by Elaine, his wife of 62 years, his children, Melissa (and Chris), Todd (and Susan), three grandchildren, Camilla, Sam and Basia, and his sister and brother-in-law, Charlotte and Paul Kirschner. Contributions in his memory may be made to the Cardiovascular Institute, 1 Gustave L. Levy Place, Box 1030, NY NY 10029. A memorial service will be announced shortly.
        GORDON-Alvin, M.D. The Staff of the Zena and Michael A. Wiener Cardiovascular Institute of the Mount Sinai Medical Center mourns the passing of our friend and colleague, Alvin Gordon, M.D., Clinical Professor Emeritus, Mount Sinai School of Medicine. Dr. Gordon started his long relationship at Mount Sinai in 1946 following distinguished service in the Navy during W.W.II. He established one of the first cardiac catheterization laboratories in the country, and conducted a pioneering research in hemodynamics and pacemakers. He provided meticulous and compassionate care to his patients. His warmth, skills and wit were appreciated widely and will be missed deeply. Our profound condolences to his wife Elaine, his daughter Melissa, his son Todd and his grandchildren Camilla, Sam and Basia. Valentin Fuster, M.D., Ph.D. Director, Cardiovascular Institute

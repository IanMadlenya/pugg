
        OSTERWEIS--Steven L, age 91, of Sarasota, FL died January 23, 2004. Born on May 16, 1912 in NYC and moved to Sarasota in the late 1980's. Retired with the rank of Lt. Commander from the Navy 1945. BA from City College of NY Phi Beta Kappa, MBA Harvard. Worked at the US Securities and Exchange Commission, Washington DC 1934-1942. 1948-1966, Gimbel Brothers, Inc, assistant to CEO and Vice President Administration & Personnel NY, CEO Gimbels, Pittsburgh. President Associated Merchandising Corp, 1966-1976. Director Institute of Retail Management NYU, 1978-1981. President, 92nd Street Y, 1981 - 1983, currently Director Emeritus of Phillips-Van Heusen Corp. Director, several Neuberger Berman mutual funds. Survivors include his wife of 63 years, Harriet Blatt of Atlantic City, NJ; two daughters, Dr. Marian of Washington DC and Laurie Stangos of Athens, Greece; a son, John of San Francisco, CA; four grandchildren and one great-granddaugher. Services will be in Sarasota. In lieu of flowers, memorial donations may be made to the Harriet and Steven Osterweis Charitable Fund, The Community Foundation of Sarasota County, Inc, PO Box 49587, Sarasota, FL 34230 or the 92nd Street Y, 1395 Lexington Avenue, NYC, NY 10128.
        OSTERWEIS--Steven L. The Board of Directors and Officers of Phillips Van Heusen Corporation mourn the passing of our longtime Director and friend, Steven Osterweis, a man of extraordinary integrity and wisdom. His advise and view of the future carried with it a unique combination of historical perspective and youthful optimism and energy. His sage advise and support will be missed more than words can express. Our sincere condolences to his beloved wife, Harriet, his children and grandchildren. We are all better for having known him. Bruce J. Klatsky, Chairman and CEO

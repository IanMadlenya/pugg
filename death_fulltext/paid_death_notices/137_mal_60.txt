
        KENESSEY-Zoltan. Died on June 4, 1998 at the age of 68 after a valiant struggle with brain cancer. A brilliant and highly cultivated intellectual, he was a leader in international statistics. His career spanned the globe, from the Hungarian Statistical Office in Budapest and the United Nations in New York to the Federal Reserve in Washington and, most recently, the International Statistical Institute in the Netherlands. He will be profoundly missed by all those who knew him. His loss is deeply mourned by his loving wife Stephanie, his children Stefania and Alec, his son-in-law Andrew, and his adoring grandchildren Dora and Jordan. Funeral services are Tuesday, June 9 at 10:00 AM at the National Presbyterian Church at 4101 Nebraska Avenue, NW, in Washington, DC.

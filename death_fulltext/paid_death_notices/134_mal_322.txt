
        BURMAN-Herman Joseph, M.D. 7/14/1898-3/30/1988. A graduate of Fordham University and Tufts Medical School, Dr. Burman was Director of the Department of Otolaryngology and Bronchoesophagology at Harlem Hospital and was a consultant at several New York City hospitals. He held several academic appointments at area medical schools including Columbia University, New York Medical College and Polyclinic Medical School. He was the author of ''Textbook of Diseases of the Nose and Throat,'' as well as numerous medical journal articles. A Fellow of the American College of Surgeons, a Diplomate of the American Board of Otolaryngology and a Fellow of the American Academy of Ophthalmology and Otolaryngology, he was also a member of many other professional organizations. ''A soft answer turneth away wrath.''

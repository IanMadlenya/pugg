
        DEISS-Joseph Jay. 1912-1999, of Wellfleet, MA., and Gainesville, FL died peacefully on April 10. Active in Farm Tenancy under the New Deal, Vice Director American Academy. Rome 1965-69, Writerin-Resident, Harvard, 1975, he was author of award-winning novels, biographies, and archeological works, including Herculaneum-Italy's Buried Treasure (J.P. Getty Museum). Mr. Deiss' work was translated into twenty-one languages. He was knighted by the Italian Government in 1971.

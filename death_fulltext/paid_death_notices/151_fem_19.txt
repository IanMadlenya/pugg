
        CROW-Ruth L. Ackerman, 81 years old, a longtime Palm Beacher, former Washingtonian and Manhattanite, died peacefully at home, Saturday, July 31, 1999 after an extended illness. During World War II, Mrs. Crow was the U.S. answer to ''Tokyo Rose,'' while assigned to the OSS in Honolulu. She graced the January cover of Town and Country Magazine in 1942. 1950 found Ruth proudly assisting Eleanor Lambert as a fashion show consultant. A sheer joy, here Ruth was at her happiest. From 1966 until 1984 the government agency founded by Roosevelt, EEOC, was Ruth's employer. Ruth is survived by her daughter, Lydia Reed Crow. A Scripture Service was held on Monday, August 2, 1999 at Quattlebaum-HollemanBurse Funeral Home, 1201 South Olive Avenue, West Palm Beach, Florida 33401. In lieu of flowers, memorial donations in Ruth's name may be made to Hospice By The Sea, 1531 West Palmetto Park Road, Boca Raton, Florida 33486 or Medway Plantation Trust, 300 Medway Road, Goose Creek, SC 29445. A Tuttie Miei Amici Grazie, Ruthie. To All My Friends, Thank You, Ruthie


        WOLF-Philip I. Of Singer Island, FL on June 15 at age 76 of cancer. He was the retired chairman of Pollux Corp. Over a long career in executive management at aerospace, life sciences and electronics firms, he commercialized the disposable syringe, interferon, CD, dental film processor and honeycomb cores. He was a decorated submarine veteran of WWII serving aboard ''Silverside'' and ''Kingfish''. He graduated from Harvard College in 1950 and was President of several Harvard Clubs. He was President of the Singer Island Civic Association. Survived by his wife, Merle, son Tony and wife Paula, daughter Lisa, cousins, nieces, nephews and four grandchildren. His ashes will be scattered at sea. The family asks that donations be made to the Nature Conservancy.

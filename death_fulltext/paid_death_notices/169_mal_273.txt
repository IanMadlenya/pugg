
        FORNARI-Dr. Harry D. Of 2 Rivers Drive, Great Neck, NY, passed away on the morning of February 24, 2001, after a two year illness with cancer. He was born in Rome, Italy, on May 21, 1919, and emigrated to the US in June, 1939, to escape the Fascist regime prior to World War II. He served in the US Army from 1941 to 1945 in North Africa and Europe in the War Information Office. He returned to the US in 1947 with his wife Maria and son James and embarked on a distinguished business career that spanned over 50 years. Notable among his accomplishments, Dr. Fornari served as Vice President for Bunge Corp. from 1952 to 1984, and afterwards as the Director for Risk Management for TIE Communications. He completed his Doctor of Letters degree from the University of Bologna, BS and MA degrees from Columbia University, and received a Ph.D. in History from the City University of New York. He was the author of several books on history and the commodities industry, including: ''Mussolini's Gadfly,'' ''Bread Upon the Waters,'' and ''A Story of Peace and Other War Stories.'' Dr. Fornari also served as Executive Director of the American Fats and Oils Association, and for the past 40 years as an arbitrator for the American Arbitration Association and the National Association of Securities Dealers. Dr. Fornari also served as an Officer and Director of the Graduate Faculties Alumni of Columbia University for 30 years. He was also the principal of his own consulting firm, Risk Management Counsel. He is survived by his loving wife Maria and his sons James D. Fornari and Daniel J. Fornari and their families. His life was filled with rich experiences and challenges that he always met with resolve, skill and determination. His spirit and ever present will to help others will remain with us forever. Funeral services will be private.

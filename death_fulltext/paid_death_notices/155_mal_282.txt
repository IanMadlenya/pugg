
        SIMON-John L., M.D., J.D. 86, born in Philadelphia January 18, 1913. Attended Jefferson Medical College of Thomas Jefferson University and served as a physician in the Abraham Lincoln Brigade during the Spanish Civil War. Practiced neurology and psychiatry in New York and Puerto Rico. Obtained law degree from the University of Puerto Rico. Known as an independent thinker with a wry wit and egalitarian political convictions. Dedicated member of the Abraham Lincoln Brigade. Survived by wife Myrtle D. Simon, former wife Ruth B. Simon, six children, two step-children, 14 grandchildren and his sister. Died December 20 after goodbyes to loving family.

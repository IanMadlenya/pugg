
        PRESTON--Stuart. Died in Paris on February 10th. He was born in 1915, educated at Yale and was an extraordinary presence in London during World War II prior to landing at Normandy in 1944. Author and former art editor for the New York Times, he lived in New York, London and Paris after the war. His friendships with John LeesMilne, Harold Nicolson, Stephen Spender, Jean Cocteau, Nancy Mitford were the stuff of legend (not to mention diaries, journals and novels). An era dies with him.

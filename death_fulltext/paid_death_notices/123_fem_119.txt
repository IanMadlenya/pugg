
        THURM-Sara. The Yeshiva University family mourns the passing of a dear friend who, with her beloved husband, Leo, was a YU Guardian and Founder of our Albert Einstein College of Medicine. They established two scholarships in memory of their parents for students graduating from Yeshiva College or Stern College for Women and entering AECOM-Ben jamin M. and Anna Thurm Memorial Scholarship and the Max and Fannie Tillem Memorial Scholarship. We extend our heartfelt condolences to children, Phyllis (and Michael) Hammer and Max (and Sandra) Thurm, grandchildren, and the entire family. May they be comforted among the mourners of Zion and Jerusalem. Dr. Norman Lamm, President Yeshiva University David S. Gottesman, Chairman Board of Trustees
        THURM-Sara. Migdal Ohr Institutions in Israel and American Friends of Migdal Ohr deeply mourn the passing of Sara Thurm, mother of Max Thurm, President of American Friends of Migdal Ohr. She was a woman of valor whose deeds were an inspiration to all. We express our condolences to husband, Leo, son, Max and daughter, Phyllis Hammer. May the A-lmighty comfort the family in their time of deep bereavement and may they be comforted amongst the mourners of Zion and Jerusalem. Rabbi Yitzchok Dovid Grossman Dean and Founder, Migdal Ohr Isaac Rokowsky, Chairman of the Board Shalom Siegfried, Executive Director Marion Zweiter, Associate Director
        THURM-Sara. Our condolences on the passing of Sara Thurm, wife of Leo Thurm. There is little in the way of words to comfort one on the loss of a wife, but we trust that the A-lmighty will grant you the strength to bear this loss. Rabbi Yitzchok Dovid Grossman Shalom Siegfried, American Friends of Migdal Ohr

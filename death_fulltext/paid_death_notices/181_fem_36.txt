
        BERNAL-Olga. Died in Cazoul Les Beziers, France, Thursday, January 24, 2002. Born in Czechoslovakia in 1929, deported to Auschwitz at the age of 14, she was the soul survivor of her family. After the war, she received her doctorate in French Literature at Columbia University and subsequently taught at Vassar College and SUNY Buffalo. She published several books with Gallimard in France. In 1980, she moved to the south of France and dedicated herself entirely to the creation of sculpture. Her radiant presence will continue to cast its light upon the lives of her friends and admirers.

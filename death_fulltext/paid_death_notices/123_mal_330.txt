
        KERST-Richard N. The Board of Trustees, administration and medical staff of The Presbyterian Hospital, Columbia-Presbyterian Medical Center mourn the loss of Richard Nevin Kerst, 83, former President of The Presbyterian Hospital/Columbia - Presbyterian Medical Center, who passed away April 22, 1997. Mr Kerst was a resident of Katonah, N.Y. A graduate of the University of Wisconsin, M. Kerst served in the Pacific during World War II as an officer on the U.S.S. Phoenix. In 1945 he began his career at The Presbyterian Hospital as Assistant Comptroller. Over the next 34 years, Mr Kerst rose to become the hospital's Comptroller, Assistant SecretaryTreasurer, Vice President of Administration, and in 1975, President. From 1977 to 1979, he was Vice Chairman of The Presbyterian Hospital's Board of Trustees. Allen I. Hyman, M.D., Chief of Staff and Medical Director of The Presbyterian Hospital said, '' For many years, Mr. Kerst was a faithful and valued member of the hospital's administration.'' Mr. Kerst was also a member of the Board of the Hecksher Foundation for Children and served on the Board of Directors of the Katonah Museum of Art. Beloved husband of Barbara Clark Kerst; father of R. Duncan Kerst of Portland, OR and Katrena K. Engstrom of Woodbridge, CT; brother of Kenneth Kerst of Heathville, VA; grandfather of Nicholas Engstrom, Cameron Kerst and Allison Kerst. Mr Kerst was pre-deceased by his first wife, Annie Lee Duncan. A memorial service will be held on Monday, June 2, 1997 at 11 AM at the South Salem Presbyterian Church, South Salem, NY. Committal will be private. Memorial contributions may be made to the Katonah Museum of Art, Route 22 at Jay St, Katonah, NY 10536. The Presbyterian Hospital Daniel B. Burke John J. Mack Co-Chairmen of the Board William T. Speck, M.D. President and C.E.O. Allen I. Hyman, M.D. Chief of Staff

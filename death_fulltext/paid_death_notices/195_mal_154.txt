
        SMEAL-Frank P., 84, died April 8, 2003 at his home, after a long illness. Mr. Smeal was born in Sykesville, PA. He lived in Jackson Heights, Queens, NY and New Shrewsbury before moving to Rumson, NJ in 1969. Mr. Smeal, a 1942 Phi Beta Kappa graduate in economics at Pennsylvania State University, went on to earn a Master's in Business Administration from Harvard University in 1947 and a law degree from New York University in 1952. A retired partner of Goldman Sachs, Inc., he spent his 38 year career on Wall Street as an expert in the municipal bond market. As Executive Vice President and Treasurer of Morgan Guaranty Trust Co., he was instrumental in counseling New York City through its 1975-76 fiscal crisis. He became a partner and member of the Senior Management Committee of Goldman Sachs in 1977 and retired in 1985. Frank was a past-president of the Municipal Bond Club of New York and former Chairman of the Government and Federal Agencies Securities Committee of Public Securities Association. He was treasurer of the United Chapters of Phi Beta Kappa and of the Phi Beta Kappa Foundation. Frank also served as Chairman of the Greater New York Councils of Boy Scouts of America, Chairman of the Penn State Development Councils, and Chairman of the Citizens Budget Commission, NYC, NY. He was also a member of the Board of the Mount Nittany Society. Frank was named a Distinguished Alumnus of Penn State University in 1974 and an Alumni Fellow of the College of the Liberal Arts in 1986. Penn State's Smeal College of Business Administration was named in Honor of Frank and his wife, Mary Jean. They were also recognized by the National Center for Disability Services, Albertson, Long Island, NY where the Frank P. and Mary Jean Smeal Learning Center was established. Mr. Smeal was predeceased by his wife of 55 years, Mary Jean (nee Popp); his brother, Ted; a sister, Pauline Alexander, and his parents Samuel and Mary Smeal. He is survived by his daughter, Mary Margaret ''Maggi'' Smeal, MD; his son, Henry ''Hank'' F. Smeal; two grandchildren; sisters Dorothy Schwabenbauer and Olga Stormer; and three nieces, Janice King, Josephine Caylor, and Judy Olsen.

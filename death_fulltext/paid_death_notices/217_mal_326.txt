
        BEARE--Gene Kerwin. Of Saint Louis, MO, died Tuesday, February 15, 2005. He was 89. Born in Chester, IL., he graduated from Washington University with a B.S. in Mechanical Engineering in 1937 and earned his Master's degree from the Harvard Graduate School of Business Administration in 1939. Following graduation, he joined Automatic Electric Company in Chicago, left to serve in the Navy as an officer, and returned to Automatic Electric. Climbing the corporate ladder, he eventually became president of international affiliated companies, vice president of operations and director of Automatic Electric International. Following a merger with General Telephone Company in 1959, Mr. Beare moved to Darien, CT., where he became president and a director of Sylvania International, and later, the president and director of Sylvania Electric Products, Inc. Retiring from GTE in 1972, he became executive vice president and director of General Dynamics Corporation, and relocated back to Saint Louis. After retiring in 1980, Mr. Beare served as a director on many boards including Asbestos Corp Ltd., American Research and Development Corp., Westvaco Corp., Emerson Electric Company, Nooney Realty Trust in Saint Louis, Saint Joe Minerals Corp. in New York, American Maize-Products Corp., Datapoint Corp. in San Antonio, and Middlesex Mutual Building Trust in Boston. He is survived by his third wife, Lena May Stetzinger Beare, and two daughters, Gail Kathryn Beare of Pittsfield, MA., and Joanne St. Vrain Beare Sandomenico of Mamaroneck, New York.

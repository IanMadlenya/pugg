
        WEINSTEIN-Edwin, M.D. It is with great sorrow that The Mount Sinai Alumni mourns the passing of our distinguished friend and colleague. Dr. Weinstein, a pillar in the Department of Neurology at Mount Sinai for almost forty years, was a world recognized authority on Aphasia & communications disorders and has served as President of the Academy of Aphasia. In 1988, the Mount Sinai Alumni presented its most coveted award, the Jacobi Medallion, to Dr. Weinstein in acknowledgment of his profound professional achievements and his extraordinary service to the Medical Center and the Alumni. Our sincere condolences are extended to his family. Wayne S. Fuchs, M.D., President Avi Barbasch, M.D., Vice Pres. Maria Padilla, M.D., Secretary William G. Stein, M.D., Treasurer
        WEINSTEIN-Edwin A. M.D. 89. On Monday, Sept. 7, 1998, of Bethesda, MD. Beloved husband of Anne Weinstein. Devoted father of Lucy Haagen and Robert Weinstein. Fond grandparent of Jonathan and Christopher Haagen. Interment private. A memorial service will be held at a later date.
        WEINSTEIN-Edwin, M.D. The Mount Sinai Association of the Attending Staff mourns the passing of our esteemed colleague, Professor Emeritis of Neurology and a member of our staff for forty years. He contributed significantly to the field of neurological communication disorders, developed special tests for mental status, authored a major treatise on Woodrow Wilson, and was honored with a Jacobi Medallion for his overall outstanding achievements at Mount Sinai. He will be remembered as a warm and dedicated gentleman-scholar. We extend our sympathy to his entire family. Peter H. Rubin, M.D., President Lynda Mandell, M.D., Secretary Amy J. Weinstein, Administrator

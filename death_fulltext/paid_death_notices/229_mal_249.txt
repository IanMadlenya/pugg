
        FURMAN--Seymour, M.D. On February 20, 2006. Beloved husband of the late Evelyn. Devoted father of Bruce, Gary and Neil. Surgeon at Montefiore Hospital for over 50 years. Services Thursday 11:45 AM at ''The Riverside'', 76th St. and Amsterdam Ave. $(6$)FURMAN -- Seymour, M.D. Died February 20, 2006 after a prolonged illness. He is widely known as the pacemaker pioneer who, on July 16, 1958, first showed that pacemakers could stimulate the heart through plastic - covered wires inserted through veins into the heart, avoiding the need for opening the chest to sew electrodes onto the surface of the heart. His associates were Doris J.W. Escher, M.D., and the late John B. Schwedel, M.D. He invented telephone monitoring of pacemaker function in cooperation with bioengineer Bryan Parker. He was the Founder of the Heart Rythm Society (HRS), a professional organization, and long-time editor of its journal, PACE. Dr. Furman rose to international fame, with over 1000 publications, leadership of many national and international conferences, and presidency of (HRS). Many details are available at the HRS website, www.hrsonline.org. Seymour Furman was born in the Bronx in 1931, he graduated from NYU, and SUNY Downstate College of Medicine. He trained in surgery at Montefiore Hospital in the Bronx, then served in the U.S. Navy Medical Corps. After training as a heart surgeon, he returned to Montefiore, with progressive emphasis on pacemakers, so that much of his career was spent with the cardiology division. He remained at Montefiore and the Albert Einstein College of Medicine where he became professor of both medicine and surgery. Dr. Furman was predeceased in 2002 by his wife, the former Evelyn Katz. He leaves behind three grown sons, Bruce, Neil, and Gary. He will be missed by many.

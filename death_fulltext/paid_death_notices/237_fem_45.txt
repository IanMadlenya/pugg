
        ZOLA--Dr. Judith Collier of Englewood Cliffs, NJ on October 7, 2006 peacefully at her home after a long illness. Loving daughter of Eleanor and the late Harry Collier of Cape May, NJ, devoted mother of her sons Erik and Adam Zola and elated grandmother of her grandson Brandon Zola. Dearest friend of Joan Brown and Rob Nunn her dear companions through the years. Born in Cape May, NJ on April 14, 1943, Dr. Zola graduated Cape May High School and attended Columbia University and Barnard College of New York from which she graduated Phi Beta Kappa and Magna Cum Laude. She achieved her Ph.D. in Microbiology at Columbia Medical School. A lover of teaching, she educated students in biology and the allied sciences at the collegiate level in New Jersey for many years. Continuing her devotion to research and education she formed her own medical writing firm which she lead until prevented by her illness. Always an avid lover of the outdoors, she spent many days with her family at the New Jersey shore and in the Rocky Mountains. Exercise and healthful eating always with the blessingof humor remained constants throughout her life. Chocolate was both an object of her passion and the focus of her connoisseurship. Services at Wien & Wien, 402 Park St, Hackensack, NJ, Monday, October 9 at 11:30 AM. Contributions may be made in Dr. Zola's memory to the Center for Parkinson's Disease, Columbia University Medical Center, Neurological Institute, 710 W. 168 St, 3rd Floor, NY 10032.

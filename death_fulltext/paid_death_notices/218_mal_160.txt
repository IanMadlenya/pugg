
        HINCKLEY--George R. 78. On March 12, 2005, in Manhattan. Born in Yorkville, July 29, 1926, lifelong New Yorker, graduate of Fordham College and St. John's Law School, practiced law with the Legal Aid Society and the firms Javits & Javits, Javits Hinckley Rabin & Engler, and Hinckley & Silbert. He was a champion of civil rights, and spent the summer of 1964 in Mississippi fighting for the rights of black voters. Loving husband of Kathleen (nee Kirland), beloved father of Kathy, George Jr., Valerie and Lizbeth, grandfather of James and Melissa. Survived by six sisters and two brothers, and numerous nieces and nephews. Friends may call at Frank E. Campbell Funeral Chapel, 1076 Madison Ave. at 81st St. on Monday and Tuesday, March 14-15, 2-4 pm, and 7-9 pm. Memorial service at the Church of the Holy Trinity, 316 E. 88th St., Wednesday, March 16, 10 am. George requested donations in his memory to the ACLU.
        HINCKLEY--George R. We mourn the loss of our cherished brother, friend and mentor. His guidance helped shape many of our lives. His Sisters: Dolores, Anne, Grace, Barbara Virginia, Clare and Brothers: Walter & Gerard

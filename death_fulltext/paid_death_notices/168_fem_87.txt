
        HABER-Jane M. Supervisor of the Blood Bank at the New York Hospital for forty-four years, she died December 30, 2000 in Boca Raton, FL. She was 84 years old. She received both her Bachelor's and Master's Degrees from the University of Michigan. In 1979 she received the Ivor Dunsford Memorial Award of the American Association of Blood Banks in recognition of her pioneering work spanning the first forty years of modern blood banking, during which time she established the blood bank supervisor as the mainstay of the hospital transfusion service and as a consultant to the practicing physician in the care of patients. In 1983 she received an award from The New York Blood Center for her pioneering role in American blood banking and support of the Blood Center. Her sister, Margaret H. Tinkler, a nephew and three nieces survive her. Contributions in her memory can be made to the New York Hospital or The New York Blood Center.

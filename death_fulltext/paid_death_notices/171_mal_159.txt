
        NICHOLSON-William Jamieson. April 9th, 2001, at age 70, of heart failure while running in Fair Lawn, NJ. He was Professor Emeritus of Community Medicine at Mount Sinai School of Medicine. Working with Dr. Irving J. Selikoff, he did extensive statistical studies of the excessive rates of cancer and deaths among exposed asbestos workers as well as the health hazards of vinyl chloride, PCBs in the Hudson River, dioxin and Agent Orange. He was a leader in countering the disinformation campaign of the asbestos industry. Raised in Kirkland, WA, he graduated from MIT in 1952. In the US Air Force, he studied ionizing radiation effects during atomic bomb testing in the Nevada desert. Receiving a PHD in physics from the University of Washington in 1960, he joined the staff of the IBM Watson Laboratory at Columbia University. In 1966 he ran for US Congress as a Peace Party candidate and was a delegate to the 1968 Democratic Party Convention in Chicago. An enthusiastic athlete, he ran the Boston and New York Marathons, completed the gold level of the Canadian Ski Marathon and at 67 was the oldest cyclist to finish the 154-mile RAMROD, a one day ride around Mount Rainier. Bill is survived by his companion, Valerie M. Josephson, two daughters, Jamie Nicholson Leener of New York City, Darcy Nicholson of Santa Fe, NM, a son David of Seattle, WA, one grandchild, and many cousins in the Shetland Islands and Scotland. His famiy and friends miss him dearly. Donations in his memory may be made to the Nature Conservancy, 4245 N. Fairfax Drive, Arlington, VA 22203.


        BLANCATO-Frank M., prominent attorney in Westchester County, New York, passed away at his home in Peekskill, New York, on March 3, 1999, after a long illness. He attended New York University and St. John's Law School. He served as a Company Commander in Korea at the Demilitarized Zone in 1962 and 1963. He was a retired Officer from the United States Army and received a Bronze Star. He practiced in Westchester, Rockland, and Dutchess Counties and in the Bronx, Manhattan, and Brooklyn. He had a distinguished career as a trial lawyer, with numerous criminal trials to his credit. Mr. Blancato was past-president of the Peekskill Board of Education, the Peekskill Bar Association, and the Salvation Army of Peekskill; served on the Peekskill Zoning Board, on the advisory board of HUD, and on the advisory board of the Peekskill Drug Abuse task force. Mr. Blancato was the original legal counsel for the Peekskill Police Federation and Aunt Bessie's Open Door. He also served as judge for moot court at his alma mater, St. John's Law School. In 1968, he was a Mayoral Candidate in Peekskill. He was born and raised in Peekskill, attended local schools before enrolling at New York University. He is survived by his wife, Diana, of Peekskill; a son, Philippe, of Wallingford, CT; a brother, Jerry, sister-in-law, Susanne, and two nephews, of Las Vegas, Nevada.

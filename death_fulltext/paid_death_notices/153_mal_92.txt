
        CAMERON-William Allan, of East Hampton, N.Y., and New York City, on October 5, 1999 at his home in East Hampton. He was 71 years old. Mr. Cameron died of lung cancer. Mr. Cameron was a graduate of Princeton University and the Harvard Law School. He spent his entire legal career at the firm of Carter, Ledyard & Milburn in New York City. During his years there he was Chairman of the Trusts and Estates Department and served on its Executive Committee. Mr. Cameron was Chairman of the South Fork-Shelter Island Chapter of The Nature Conservancy and also served on its New York State Board. He was a member of the Union Club, the Downtown Association and the St. Andrew's Society of New York City and The Maidstone Club and the Devon Yacht Club in East Hampton. He is survived by his wife of 23 years, the former Katharine Torrey; his daughters Jean Cameron-Smith of Croton-on-Hudson, N.Y., and Anne Cunningham of Villanova, Penn.; four grandchildren; and his stepchildren, Karen Forkner Wells of Houston, Tex., and Stanley Forkner of Darien, Conn. Also by his sister-in-law, Mrs. Alexander Cameron, her daughter, Laurie Cameron Carson, and his sister-in-law, Mrs. James Allan Cameron, and his cousin, Robert Cameron, all of Southampton, N.Y.; and his cousins, David Cameron of Woodbridge, Va., and J. Hamilton Allan of Stevenson, Md. There will be a private family service on Saturday, October 9, at the East Hampton Presbyterian Church, Main Street, East Hampton. A memorial service will be held at 11 AM Thursday, October 21, 1999 at the Central Presbyterian Church, 593 Park Avenue at 64th Street. In lieu of flowers, gifts may be made to The Nature Conservancy's South Fork-Shelter Island Chapter, PO Box 5125, East Hampton, NY 11937.


        ETHRIDGE--James Murdock III. Age 83. Died peacefully at home after a lengthly illness on January 5, 2004. He is survived by his beloved wife Ann, his children James M. Ethridge IV, Judy Goodale, Brooke Cort and step-daughter Sybil Hajek, six grandchildren and two great-grandchildren. He graduated from Andover in 1938 and Yale University in 1942 and served proudly as a captain in the U.S. Army during World War II. He was the President of Howe Furniture Corporation in New York City. He grew up in Greenwich, Ct., where he was a longtime member of the Greenwich Field Club. He was the national doubles squash racquets champion in 1956 and 1957. Upon retirement, he moved to Hobe Sound, Fla. where he was currently a member of the Loblolly Club. His love for children lead him to become a mentor for many years at local elementary schools, where he enjoyed spending and giving his time. He will be greatly missed by all of those who knew him and loved this gentle man. A memorial service will be held on Saturday, January 10 at 11:30AM at Christ Memorial Chapel on Jupiter Island. In lieu of flowers donations may be made in his name to the Michael J. Fox Foundation for Parkinson's Research, Box 4777, Grand Central Station, New York, N.Y. 10163.

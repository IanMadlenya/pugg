
        HOCH--Frank. Brown Brothers Harriman mourns the loss of our friend and Partner Frank W. Hoch. Frank joined the Firm in 1947 and became a Partner in 1960. He was importantly responsible for the growth of our international businesses. Frank combined his business acumen with his personal graciousness. We extend our condolences to his wife Lisina and his family. Michael W. McConnell for the Partners
        $(6$)HOCH--Frank W. Passed away peacefully Friday, April 13, at his home in Irvington New York at the age of 85. Partner of Brown Brothers Harriman & Co., he served on several corporate boards in the financial and pharmaceutical sectors. Beloved husband of Lisina and devoted father to Steven, Alix, Daphne and Roland and grandfather of eleven. Educated in Switzerland, he received his Doctor of Law from the University of Zurich (Switzerland) in 1947. During World War II he served as a Lieutenant of the Swiss Alpine Infantry. Passionate philanthropist and engaged community leader, he served on the boards of the Smithsonian Institution, Woods Hole Oceanographic Institution, Phelps Memorial Hospital, Exodus Transitional Community, Irvington Presbyterian Church, Polly Hill Arboretum, American-Swiss Association, American Council on Germany, and was an active member of the Council on Foreign Relations, the Explorer's Club of New York, and the US Council for International Business. A memorial service will be held at Irvington Presbyterian Church, Saturday, April 28 at 11:00am. In lieu of flowers, donations in his memory may be made to Exodus Transitional Community, New York, Phelps Memorial Hospital, Sleepy Hollow, NY or Woods Hole Oceanographic Institution, Woods Hole, MA.

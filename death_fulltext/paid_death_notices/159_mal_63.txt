
        MUNDHEIM-Marshall, MD. The Trustees, faculty and staff of New York University School of Medicine are deeply saddened by the passing of our beloved colleaque and friend, Dr. Marshall Mundheim, Clinical Associate Professor of Medicine. Marshall, a long time member of the NYU family, completed his internship and his residency, and fellowship in rheumatology in the NYU/Bellevue program. Marshall was an exemplary physician and educator; his dedication was to caring for his patients and to teaching his students to become the finest clinicians possible. He was a recipient of the AOA Outstanding Clinical Teacher award, the first for a NYU school of medicine faculty member. We extend our deepest condolences to his wife, Ingegerd and daughter, Marie Elizabeth. Funeral services will be held on Friday, April 7th. 11AM, at the St. James Church, 865 Madison Avenue, (at 71 St.). Robert M. Glickman, MD, Dean New York University School of Medicine
        MUNDHEIM-Marshall. We are deeply saddened by the loss of our associate, Marshall Mundheim and feel a true sense of tragedy at his untimely passing. Marshall was known for his dedication as a physician and his superb teaching skills. We will always remember his cheerful nature, his wit, his warmth and honesty. He touched many lives both professionally and personally and he will be missed. Our sincerest condolences to the members of his family. Friends and colleagues from the Department of Rheumatology, Hospital for Joint Diseases and the Division of Rheumatology, NYU School of Medicine

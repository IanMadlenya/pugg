
        LOCKTON-John Dudley. Retired Treasurer of General Electric. Died of natural causes September 12, 2000, in Greenwich, Connecticut. Mr. Lockton was born in Logansport, Indiana, in 1903. The son of an Episcopal clergyman, he was one of five brothers, all now deceased. He graduated from the University of Michigan, joined GE in 1926 and rose to Treasurer in 1948. During his long and varied career at GE, Mr. Lockton was responsible for many of the financial departments of GE, including the General Electric Foundation and being Chairman of the Board of GE Credit Corporation. While at GE Mr. Lockton developed the corporate cash management practices which are now widely copied by corporations around the world and was the subject of numerous articles including appearing on the cover of Business Week. Mr. Lockton was New York State Chairman of the U.S. Treasury Department's Savings Bond Division for many years, was a Director of numerous public corporations including Arkwright Mutual Insurance and Booth Computing, was a Trustee of Russell Sage College and Bennett College, served as a business director of the Metropolitan Opera and was a Vestryman of Christ Church, Greenwich. His charitable activities included serving the Boys and Girls Club of America in various Director and Finance positions. His club memberships included the Union and University Clubs, New york; the Round Hill and Field Clubs, Greenwich; Blind Brook Club, Purchase; Clove Valley Club, Millbrook. He married Alice Hanson, now deceased, in 1931 and Catherine Baker MacGregor, now deceased, in 1960. He is survived by a son, John D. Lockton, a daughter, Mrs. Judith Jones, a stepdaughter, Mrs. Robin Morton, three grandchildren and five great-grandchildren. A memorial service will be held at Christ Church, Greenwich at 2:00 p.m. September 16, 2000.

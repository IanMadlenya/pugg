
        WISNER-Charles H., 91. Of Manasota Key, Florida, died May 20 1998 in Englewood, Florida. He was born October 30, 1906, in New York City, was raised in Summit, New Jersey and later moved to Weston, Connecticut where he lived most of his life before coming to Manasota Key in 1971. Mr. Wisner graduated from Lawrenceville Preparatory School in 1924 & from Princeton University in 1928. He then was a Commander in the United States Navy during W.W. II and as National Skeet Champion he was recruited by the Navy prior to W.W. II to be a member of the Staff of Naval Air Command Training to head their gunnery program. Mr. Wisner was co-owner of Wisner & deClairville Stock Brokers in New York City which later sold to Spear, Leeds and Kellogg where he was a limited partner until his death. He was past President of Buttonwood Club (Noon luncheon group of the New York Stock Exchange), Founding member of Aspetuck Valley Country Club and the Weston Gun Club, Past President of Manasota Key Association, and member of Squadron ''A'' in New York City. Survivors include three daughters; Matilda J.W. Barrett and Susan A. Wisner both of Venice, Florida and Penelope J. Wisner of San Francisco, California and a granddaughter; Christopher Lee Barrett of Boca Raton, Florida. He was preceded in death by his wife of sixty years, Mary Jane Wisner, in 1996. A memorial Service for Mary Jane Wisner and Charles H. Wisner will be held at 11:00 A.M. on Tuesday, June 16th, 1998 at Emmanel Church, Lyons Plains Road, Weston, Connecticut. Memorial donations may be made to Hospice of Southwest Florida, 5955 Rand Boulevard, Sarasota, Florida 34238.

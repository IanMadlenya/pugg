
        WELLS-Gwendolyn. Long-time union activist and leader, died Friday, January 15, of pancreatic cancer. She was 53. At the time of her death, she was the Director of Education and Research for the Office and Professional Employees International Union (OPEIU), AFL-CIO, and the editor of the official union publication, White Collar. Wells also served as a Vice President of the Board of the Coalition of Labor Union Women (CLUW). Born and raised in Dallas, TX, she resided in New York City. Wells was a graduate of the University of Texas and Cornell University School of Labor Relations. She is survived by her mother, Rose Wells, her sister Mrs. Patty Warren, and brother-inlaw Henry Warren, all of San Antonio, TX. A memorial service will be held at a later date in New York. In lieu of flowers, a memorial fund has been established through the American Cancer Society, 19 West 56th St., New York, NY 10019.

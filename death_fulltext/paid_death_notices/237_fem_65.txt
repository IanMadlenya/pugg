
        CRAWFORD--Anne Norris Forrester (Mrs. Charles T.) died October 9, 2006 at age 65 in New York City following five years of treatment for ovarian cancer. Until shortly before her death, she enjoyed good quality of life. Born in 1940 in Kansas City, Missouri, Annes parents were Judge Bruce M. Forrester of the United States Tax Court and Anne Lee Broaddus Forrester. She was a graduate of Smith College, including a junior year at the University of Geneva. In New York, in1977, she joined Colgate Palmolive where for most of her career she was Director of Investor Relations. She retired in 2001. Until shortly before her death, she was president of the National Society of Colonial Dames in the State of New York, a 450-member organization. Earlier, she was a Board member of the Financial Womens Association of New York. Mrs. Crawford was also a member of the Colony Club and the New York Junior League. She is survived by her beloved husband of 22 years, Charles T. Crawford; a sister, Jean Forrester Cappello of New York City; a brother, Bruce M. Forrester, Jr., of Dayton, Ohio; and six nieces and nephews. Funeral will be at St. James's Church, 71st and Madison at 4:30 p.m. on Thursday October 12. There will be a viewing at the church from 5:00 to 7:00 p.m. on Wednesday October 11. Burial will be in Topeka, Kansas. In lieu of flowers, donations may be made to the National Society of Colonial Dames in the State of New York, 215 East 71st Street, New York, New York 10021.

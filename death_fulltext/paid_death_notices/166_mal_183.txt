
        SMITH-Philip Cleary, 78, retired Chairman and CEO of the former National Securities and Research Corporation, a New York mutual fund company, died yesterday in Rye, New York from complications following a stroke. A native of Brooklyn, New York, Mr. Smith graduated from Brooklyn Prep, and then attended Williams College before leaving to serve in the US Army during World War II. After the war, he graduated from Fordham University, then Fordham Law School. He joined the New York law firm of Satterlee, Warfield & Stevens and later joined National Securities and Research Corporation from which he retired in 1985. A former resident of Rye, Mr. Smith had moved to Tequesta, Florida where he was a member of Jupiter Hills Country Club. He also maintained a membership in Winged Foot Country Club in Mamaroneck, New York. Mr. Smith was married for 49 years to the former Carol Ann Simonson who died in 1995. He is survived by his five children, Kate McDonnell of Wayne, Pennsylvania, Carol Felch of Sebastopol, California, Cleary Simpson of New Canaan, Connecticut, Lauren Steers of Rye, New York, and Philip C. Smith, Jr. of Charlotte, North Carolina and 11 grandchildren. The family will receive visitors at the William H. Graham Funeral Home, in Rye, New York, 1036 Boston Post Road, Rye, on Friday November 17th 2-4pm and 7-9pm. A Mass of Christian Burial will be celebrated at the Church of the Resurrection, 910 Boston Post Road, Rye on Saturday, November 18th at 9:45am. In lieu of flowers, the family suggests donations designated ''for research'' to the Alzheimer's Association, 919 North Michigan Avenue, Suite 1100, Chicago, IL 60611.

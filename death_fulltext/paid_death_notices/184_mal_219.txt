
        BRYAN-Robert F. Of Delray Beach, Florida, formerly of Rye, NY. On May 16, at the age of 89, due to complications from Parkinson's Disease. A graduate of Oberlin College, Phi Beta Kappa, in 1934, he earned a Ph.D. in Economics from Yale University in 1939 having been an instructor at both Yale and Princeton Universities. He was an economist with Lionel D. Edie & Co. from 1939-1948 except during W.W. II, 1941-42, when he was with the OPA. In 1948 he joined J.H. Whitney & Co. becoming a partner in 1951. From 1959-69 he was a financial vice-president, treasurer and director of Whitney Communications Corp. and from 1967-69 a partner in Whitcom Investment Co. He was a member of the executive committee of the Yale Graduate School Council 1969-73 and a Trustee of Oberlin College 1960-70. He was predeceased by his wife, Dorothy Darr Bryan, in 1979 and his wife, Gertrude G. Bryan in 1999. His first wife, Elaine Norwood Bennett, who is also the mother of his two daughters, survives him as does his oldest daughter, Diane Bryan Lyon, of New York City. His younger daughter, Barbara Bryan Bardo, passed away in 1992. He is also survived by three granddaughters, Cecil Lyon Sanford and Wendy Lyon McCammon both of Miami, Florida, Michelle Bowen Kowalski of Berkeley, California and one grandson, Robert Bryan Bowen of Petaluma, CA. as well as four great-grandchildren. Burial will be in Greenwood-Union Cemetery, Rye, New York.

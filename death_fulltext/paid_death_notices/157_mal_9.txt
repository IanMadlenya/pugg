
        McMORRIS-LeRoy Sells. Durham, NC resident, age 74, died Tuesday evening, January 25, 2000 at his home. Mr. McMorris was a veteran of the U.S. Army and the U.S. Air Force, having served during both WWII and Korea. He was retired from the City of New York where he worked as an accessibility assessor for the Mayor's Office for the Handicapped. He was a member of Christ Church Bay Ridge Episcopal of Brooklyn. He served in numerous volunteer organizations including the DAV and the Amateur Astronomers Association, and was an officer in the Benevolent Society for Retarded Children. Mr. McMorris was the son of the late Daniel MacMorris, the noted Kansas City artist and the grandson of the late Elijah Watt Sells, co-founder of the accounting firm of Haskins and Sells. Surviving are his wife, Mrs. Marion Louis McMorris; a son, Louis McMorris of Brooklyn; a daughter, Ms. Mary McMorris and her husband, Leonard Santoro, of Chapel Hill, NC; a sister, Lee MacMorris of Laguna Niguel, CA; and grandsons, Evan McMorris-Santoro and Stefan McMorris-Santoro, of Chapel Hill, NC. A memorial service will be conducted Saturday, February 5, at 2:30 PM in Clements Funeral Chapel in Durham, NC, the Rev. Harold W. Morcombe officiating. Memorial contributions may be made to the Benevolent Society for Retarded Children, 1150 Forest Hill Road, Building L, Staten Island, New York 10314.

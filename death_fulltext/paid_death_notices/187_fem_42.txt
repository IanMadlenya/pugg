
        MUTRUX - Elizabeth ''Lynne'' (Lofting), 88. Died on August 4, 2002 in Glastonbury, CT. Born in New York City, she was the daughter of Flora Small and Hugh Lofting, author of the Dr. Dolitte books. She grew up in Connecticut and lived most of her adult life in Wilton. She studied art history in Vienna and was active in the New York fashion world, both as a photographer for Glamour Magazine and as a designer for Lilly Dache. She designed clothes for celebrities such as Helen Hayes and Rosalind Russell. In 1959 she joined Miss Thomas School for Girls in Rowayton where she taught art history and later became assistant head-mistress. In the 1970s she taught art history at Fairfield University, later donating to the University an extensive slide collection of art and architecture compiled during decades of world travel with her husband. (The collection is now known as the Mutrux Visual Resource Collection). She is the author of a children's book, ''Squawky and Bawky'', as well as several short stories for children. She will be remembered for her sense of humor, her love of animals, her creativity and her fabulous cooking. She is predeceased by her husband, Robert Henri Mutrux, as well as a brother, Colin Lofting of Pennsylvania. She is survived by a half brother, Christopher Lofting, as well as four daughters: Jennifer Lander of Boston, Massachusetts; Michele Witt of London, England; Brien Chelminski of Bourron Marlotte, France; and Robin Worley of Glastonbury, CT, also seven grandchildren and five great-grandchildren. There are no calling hours. In lieu of flowers, memorial contributions may be made to Protectors of Animals, P.O. Box 24, South Glastonbury, CT 06073.


        MERENDINO--Vincent J, M.D. died at Englewood Hospital on Sunday morning, April 8, 2007, following a courageous battle with numerous complications. Vincent was born on January 5, 1917, in New York City. He graduated from Columbia College in 1937, and received his M.D. from New York Medical College in 1942. He was a Captain in the Marine Corps during WWII, where he was Chief of Surgery for the 112th Evacuation Hospital. Vincent had a long and distinguished medical career. Among the positions he held were; Chief of Gynecology, Prospect Hospital, (1948-1976), Professor of Clinical Obstetrics and Gynecology, New York Medical College (1976-1988); he was Chief of Service, Obstetrics and Gynecology, Flower and Fifth Avenue Hospital (19751986); Lecturer in Obstetrics and Gynecology, Mt. Sinai School of Medicine (1976-1986); Attending Gynecologist, Metropolitan Hospital (1954-1988); Associate Attending Gynecologist, Lenox Hill Hospital (1976-1986). Vincent is survived by his wife, Rita, his daughter, Melissa, Rita's daughter, Debra Solomon and his grandchildren, Charles and Lesly Solomon. He is further survived by his innumerable friends and family who loved and respected him dearly. Funeral services for Vincent J. Merendino will be held at 11 am on Wednesday, April 11th, at Gutterman Musichant Funeral Home, in Hackensack, NJ. Contributions may be made to Englewood Hospital.


        KEMMERER - Mary Eli zabeth. A former board member and financial supporter of many national, state and community organizations in New Jersey and daughter of noted industrialist Ernest K. Halbach, died on Monday, July 29, 2002, at her home in Basking Ridge, NJ. She was 85 years old. Born in Haverford, PA, Mrs. Kemmerer lived most of her life in Short Hills, NJ, where she was a member of the board of directors of numerous organizations including New Jersey Roadside Council, Millburn Community Council, Millburn-Short Hills Neighborhood House, American Red Cross (the Millburn-Short Hills Chapter) and Short Hills Garden Club. She was active also in such organizations as Garden Club of America, Girl Scouts of America, Christ Church in Short Hills and Overlook Hospital, as well as a sustaining member of the Junior League of the Oranges and Short Hills. Mrs. Kemmerer was a generous and tireless supporter of other charitable organizations such as: Nature Conservancy, Paper Mill Playhouse and the Belize Zoo. Mrs. Kemmerer was the oldest daughter of Ernest K. Halbach, founder of the predecessor company to (GAF) General Analyne and Film Company and a member of the War Production Board during World War II. Wife of businessman John L. Kemmerer Jr., Chairman of Kemmerer Resources and its predecessor companies, Kemmerer Corporation and Kemmerer Coal Company, among numerous other energy and resource companies. She spent summers in Nantucket Island (Maine) for many years and was a parttime resident of Sun Valley, ID. Besides her husband, Mrs. Kemmerer is survived by the couple's three children, Constance A. Kemmerer, Elizabeth K. Gray and John L. Kemmerer III, and six grandchildren and her younger sister, Anne H. Bumsted. A memorial service is planned at Fellowship Village, Liberty Corner, NJ, on Friday, August 2, 2002. The family requests that donations in lieu of flowers be made to American Red Cross (Millburn-Short Hills Chapter).

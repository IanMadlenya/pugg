
        BROWNING - John. The House of Steinway & Sons notes with profound sorrow the untimely passing of its beloved friend, the eminent American pianist John Browning, on January 26, 2003 at the age of 69. A Steinway Artist for more than 46 years, Mr. Browning was an articulate master of the piano who captivated audiences wherever he played. He leaves a legacy rich with historic performances and award-winning recordings. John Browning earned the wide respect of his colleagues and a special place in the hearts of Steinway employees and dealer representatives everywhere. To his family we extend our heartfelt condolences. He will be deeply missed. Bruce A. Stevens, President Peter B. Goodrich, V.P. Worldwide Concert and Artist Activities
        BROWNING-John. With sadness, The Juilliard School mourns the death of John Browning, a distinguished artist, alumnus and piano faculty member from 1986-1996. His artistry and dedication remain as a legacy for future generations. Joseph W. Polisi, President
        BROWNING-John. Marilyn Horne and the Trustees and staff of the Marilyn Horne Foundation are deeply saddened by the loss of our friend, John. He was a consummate musician and will be remembered-& missed.
        BROWNING-John. I was honored to be his manager and friend for many years. Diane Saldick


        HOLMES--Dr. Douglas. The Board of Directors, Residents and Staff of The Hebrew Home for the Aged at Riverdale mourn with profound sorrow the loss of our longtime colleague and friend, Dr. Douglas Holmes. Dr. Holmes' influential research on issues affecting the elderly have been internationally recognized. He has left his mark on the Hebrew Home community and will be sorely missed. We extend our heartfelt condolences to his wife Marcia, his children Deborah, Pamela and Gregory and his four grandchildren. The Hebrew Home for the Aged at Riverdale Jeffrey S. Maurer, Chairman Daniel Reingold, Pres & CEO
        $(6$)HOLMES--Douglas, Ph.D. The staff of the Research Division of the Hebrew Home for the Aged at Riverdale mourns the loss of their leader, Dr. Douglas Holmes. Dr Holmes was an internationally known psychologist and gerontologist who published over 150 articles in the areas of health services research, computer applications in health care, and minority aging research. He was the principal investigator of over 50 federally-funded studies. His research had a major impact on programs such as Head Start, Meals on Wheels, and later on dementia care. He was a pioneer in computer science, and in 1958 was one of the first to develop software for statistical applications. Dr. Holmes was a member of the Columbia University Stroud Center and Faculty of Medicine. He will be remembered by all for his wit, compassion and intellect. We miss him dearly. Jeanne A. Teresi, Ed.D.,Ph.D., on behalf of the Research Division staff.

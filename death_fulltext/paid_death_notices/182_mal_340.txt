
        MANN-R. Mowry, of Hamburg Road, Lyme, CT, died Thursday, at Middlesex Hospital in Middletown, CT, at age 57. Mowry was born and grew up in Montclair, NJ. He was a graduate of the University of Wisconsin at Madison, and a member of Kappa Sigma fraternity. He worked in advertising with Marstellar Inc., where he created the award-winning crying Indian campaign for Keep America Beautiful. He later started his own advertising firm, Hoffman-Mann and shared his creativity with many clients. Mowry married Gretchen Pratt in Armonk, NY, in 1983. They lived in Essex, CT, before settling at Jollywood Farm in Lyme. Mowry founded Greenwich Publishing Group, Inc., in 1988. He was an active member of The Leash in New York City and a trustee and former President of the Connecticut River Museum in Essex, CT. In addition to his wife of nineteen years, Mowry is survived by a daughter Molly Bellemore Mann; a son and daughter-in-law, William Mowry Mann and Jennifer Mann; two sisters, Cynthia Treene of Montclair, NJ, and Marcia Butler of Palos Verdes, CA, and two young granddaughters Campbell and Hollis Mann. Mowry has been and remains a tremendous presence in the lives of many. A memorial service will be held on April 6, at the Connecticut River Museum in Essex, CT. For information contact Fulton-Theroux Funeral Home, Old Lyme CT


        KATZ-Susan. The Jewish Museum mourns the passing of Sue Katz, a devoted and passionate advocate for art and Jewish culture. As a founder of our Children's Hanukkah Program in 1995, Sue nurtured its growth through her enthusiasm, commitment, and energy. And as one of the first members of The Museum Council, she and her husband, Jerry, have been important supporters and dear friends for a decade. We extend sincerest condolences to Jerry, their sons, and the entire family. Susan Lytle Lipton, Chairman John L. Vogelstein, President Joan Rosenbaum, Helen Goldsmith Menschel Director
        KATZ-Susan Kohl. She was an alunma, friend and devoted supporter of The Ohio State University. We extend our deepest sympathy to Susan's husband Jerome, their children, grandchildren and family. Over the years, Susan and Jerome affirmed their friendship for the university in meaningful ways that have touched the lives of those that followed. She was an inspiration and a source of pride for us. We cherished her friendship as a woman of courage, valour and loyalty, and we will honor her memory. Karen A. Holbrook, Pres., The Ohio State University Columbus, Ohio
        KATZ-Susan K. The officers, Board of Directors, and staff of UJA-Federation of New York note with sadness the passing of Susan K. Katz, beloved wife of Jerome L.         KATZ-Susan K. The members of The Lotos Club mourn the loss of their distinguished colleague and longtime friend, Susan K. Katz, and send condolences to her family.

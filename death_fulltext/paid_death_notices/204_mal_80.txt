
        KADUSHIN--Phineas. Age 78. Died January 1, 2004 in Fort Lauderdale, FL., where he lived with his loving daughter, Janine. Also surviving are his other children with his late wife Susan, Tamar and Michael, and his brother Charles. A graduate of the Jewish Theological Seminary, he served as a Chaplain in the Air Force during the Korean War and, subequently, as a Hillel Director at Harvard and Brandeis, and later at Ohio University. He led two congregations, one in Plainview, L.I., and the other in Metuchen, N.J. His increasing interest in psychoanalysis led him to become a full time psychotherapist in New York City, an activity from which he retired ten years ago. He was the author of a book and several professional articles in psychology, and had a lifelong interest in the arts, especially photography, the cinema and the theater. A memorial service will be held in Fort Lauderdale, Sunday, January 11, 2004. Further information, 954-764-7782

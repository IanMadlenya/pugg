
        CAPUTO--Emily Stull, of Riverdale, NYC, and formerly of Bridgehampton, NY, died on March 22, 2007, at age 89. Devoted and loving wife of Dante S. Caputo, and mother of Patricia P. Anderson of Mystic, CT, Pengwynne P. Blevins, M.D. of Arlington, MA, Priscilla Potter (deceased 1957), Peter Potter of Gaithersburg, MD, Pamela Potter of Smyrna, GA, Paul Potter of Shelburne, VT; Kathleen M. Caputo of Washington, DC and Thomas A. Caputo of Greenwich, (children by marriage), eight grandchildren and two greatgrandchildren. She attended Smith College, graduated from the University of Kentucky and earned Master Degrees from New York University (in English Literature) and Columbia University (in Library Science). After completing her education at age 50, she was a tenured Reference Librarian and instructor at New York University Bobst Library. She was an avid golfer and tennis player. While in high school, she studied modern dance with Martha Graham. Emily was a devotee of the arts. She had a lifetime passion for reading and was a faithful member of the Shakespeare Club of Riverdale. She enjoyed dressing with style and entertained with lively wit and grace. In lieu of flowers donations may be sent to Christ Church, Riverdale, NYC, or to Wave Hill in Riverdale. A Memorial Service is planned for Saturday, April 21st, at 11:00am at Christ Church in Riverdale, NYC.

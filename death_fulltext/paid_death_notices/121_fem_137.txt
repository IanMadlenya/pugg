
        GOLD-Irene Leck Shembs, 89 of Asheville, North Carolina, died Thursday, February 20, 1997, in an Asheville hospital. Born in Newton, Iowa, she was a daughter of the late Thomas W. and Katherine Engel Goodhue and a niece of the late Wanda Stanard of Asheville. She retired to Asheville with her husband, William H. Leck, from New York City 30 years ago. A graduate of Iowa State University at Ames, she was a field director for the American Red Cross during World War II and a national home economist for Maytag Company, Newton, Iowa, and Leck Industries, NY. She was published in many magazines and made several guest appearances on the Today show. She was a member of Grace Covenant Presbyterian Church, The Children's Welfare League, Colonial Book Club, Chatmore Club, the Country Club of Asheville, and was a sustaining member of the Junior League. She is survived by a son, William J. Leck, and his wife, Maureen, of Rockville Centre, NY; daughter, Nancy Leck Galas, and her husband, Joseph, of Denver, CO; sister, Wanda Buchanan of Crystal Springs, MS; six grandchildren; 18 great-grandchildren; and nieces and nephews. A memorial service will be held at 11 AM, Monday, at Grace Covenant Presbyterian Church, Asheville, NC with the Reverend Genie Martin officiating. A reception will follow at the church. The family will receive friends from 3-5 PM, Sunday, at Morris Funeral Home, 304 Merrimon Ave, Asheville, NC. Memorials may be made to Grace Covenant Presbyterian Church, 789 Merrimon Ave, Asheville, NC 28804 or to the Owen Heart Center at Memorial Mission Hospital, 509 Biltmore Ave, Asheville, NC 28801.


        WYCKOFF--William S. The Directors, Officers, Staff, Members and Advisory Board of The Wyckoff House & Association wish to express their deep sorrow on the death of William F. Wyckoff and their deepest condolences to his children, grandchildren and other close family members on their loss. It was through Bill's unselfish devotion, tireless energy, and dogged determination over a 40 year period that the Pieter Claesen Wyckoff House originally built in 1652, was saved from destruction and designated as New York City's first landmark in1965 and restored and opened to the public in 1982 as a museum dedicated to the City's early Dutch history. 20 years later, Bill was instrumental in the Museum's redevelopment as the Wyckoff Farmhouse Museum and Education Center. Bill served as the Association's First Vice-President from 1957 to 1967 and then as President from 1967 to 1972. From that point, he served as Chairman of the Executive Committee until 1989. Bill was the eighth great-grandson of Pieter Claesen Wyckoff, who arrived in New Amsterdam on March 4, 1637. E. Lisk Wyckoff, Jr. Chairman Naj Wikoff, President Sean Sawyer, Executive Dir


        MARTIN--Francis Jr. Who helped take New York City broadcasting representative, John Blair & Co., public in the 1960s, died surrounded by his family on December 23, 2003 in Reno, NV, after a long battle with cancer. He was 83. Son of a distinguished Bronx County judge, Martin was a sales representative for the Dumont Co. before joining John Blair in the 1950s. In 1965, he was promoted to President and Chief Executive Officer. He retired after 15 years with the company. He was born in New York City on June 8, 1920 to Edith and Judge Francis Martin. Judge Martin was a presiding justice of the Appellate Division and the first District Attorney of Bronx County, NY, where a regional branch of The New York Public Library stands named in his honor. Martin attended Columbia University and Cornell Law School. He was a member of St. Anthony Hall fraternity, and the Union Club in Manhattan, and a former member of Maidstone Club in East Hampton and the Apawamis Club in Rye, NY. He was a lifetime social member of the La Quinta Country Club in La Quinta, CA, where he served for several years on the board of directors. He then moved to Merrakesh where he spent three wonderful months a year. He also served on the Board of Desert Hospital in Palm Springs for eight years. After retiring, he enjoyed playing golf, backgammon, bridge, fly fishing and traveling. He is survived by his wife of 40 years, Mimi Martin, his two children, Anne Rossman of Menlo Park, CA and Francis III of Portland, OR, his stepson, Scott Marshutz of Laguna Niguel, CA and two granddaughters, Eleanor and Grace of Portland, OR. He is missed by all. Friends are invited to attend a Celebration of Life service on Saturday, January 24, 2004 from 4-6 P.M. at the Hidden Valley Country Club, 3575 Hidden Valley Drive, Reno, NV 89502. In lieu of flowers, donation may be made to the American Cancer Society.

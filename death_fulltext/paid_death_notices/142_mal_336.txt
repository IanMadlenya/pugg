
        HECHT-George Anthony. Former bookseller. Died on November 25 in Rye Brook, NY. He was 88. He joined Doubleday & Co. in 1925 and became General Manager of Doubleday Book Shops in 1940. He bacame Vice President of Doubleday & Co. in 1948, President of the Old Corner Bookstore, Inc., in 1950, and President of Doubleday Book Shops in 1970, positions he held until his retirement in 1975. He was the Director of the Council on Books in Wartime and the Armed Services Editions from 1941 until 1946. He was the President of American Book Travellers in 1937 and President of the American Booksellers Association from 1946 until 1947; he was Board Chairman of the Association in 1948. He is survived by his wife, the former Dorothea Ackermann, and his daughters Karen Hecht and Dorothea Hecht, all of New York City; his son George Anthony Hecht, Jr., of San Leandro, CA; three grandchildren and two greatgrandchildren. Contributions in his memory may be made to the Salvation Army or the New York Public Library. Private interment at Nassau Knolls Cemetery in Port Washington, New York.

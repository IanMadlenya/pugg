
        BRIDGES-Harold V. Of Hampton Bays. A retired operations engineer at Columbia Broadcasting System in New York City died on July 6 at Southampton Hospital. He was 93 years old. Mr. Bridges' CBS assignments over 30 years included broadcasts of The New York Philharmonic, The Boston Symphony and The Cleveland Symphony. He served in the rostrum of every National Convention from 1948 to 1962 in the Call of States. He officiated at Cape Canaveral in the moon flight of 1969. He attended New York University for television engineering and was a member of the I.B.E.W. and The Audio Society of New York. He is survived by a niece, Mrs. Howard Johnson (nee) Rosemarie Crawley of Montclair, NJ and a cousin Mrs. Barbara Creaser of Torrington, CT and six grand nieces and nephews. He was predeceased by his sister Lillian Bridges Crawley and a nephew, John J. Crawley. For more information contact R.J. O'Shea Funeral Home, Inc., 94 East Montauk Highway, Hampton Bays, NY. A Mass of Christian Burial was held on July 9th from St. Rosalie's Church in Hampton Bays followed by an interment in Calvary Cemetery. Remembrances may be made to the Dominican Sisters, PO Box 1028, Hampton Bays, New York 11946-0404.

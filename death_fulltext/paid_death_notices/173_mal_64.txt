
        STANGER-Wesley A. Jr., died of natural causes on May 23 at 92. Mr. Stanger graduated from the University of Pennsylvania's Wharton School in 1929. He joined Dillon, Read & Co. and in 1933 was a founder of Riter & Co., an NYSE member. He served in the U.S. Navy Reserve as a lieutenant during WWII. In the late 1970's, he joined McKay, Shields & Co., pension fund investment manager. Mr. Stanger was a trustee of the University of Pennsylvania for twenty years, and chairman of the board emeritus of both the Madison Fund and American South African Investments, Ltd. He was a director emeritus of Gulf & Western, Talley Industries and numerous other corporations. Mr. Stanger was a member of Baltusrol Golf Club, Springfield, N.J. He is survived by his second wife, Natalie Williams Stanger. His first wife, Sarah McKee Stanger, died in 1975. He is also survived by his brother, Rhys L. Stanger; his sons, William M. Stanger and Robert A. Stanger; seven grandchildren; and seven great grandchildren. A memorial service will be held at 3:00 PM, June 16th, at the Brough Funeral Home, 535 Springfield Avenue, Summit, NJ 07901. No flowers please, but a donation to the University of Pennsylvania in his memory would be appreciated.


        PLAXEN-Norma, June 20, 1912September 20, 1999. Born in Brooklyn, she attended Far Rockaway High School. During WW2 she served with the American Red Cross in Germany and the Philippines. She established Diner's Card and American Express Card dining programs, was associated with the New York Convention & Visitors Bureau, Esquire Magazine and World Publishing and was an editorial assistant, account executive and tour guide with travel writer Richard Joseph, Town and Country Travel of Manhattan and Skinny-Dip Tours. She is survived by siblings Selma Weinberg of Palm Beach, FL, Anne Hamberger of Deerfield Beach, FL, Jesse Plaxen of Far Rockaway, New York, and three generations of loving nieces and nephews.

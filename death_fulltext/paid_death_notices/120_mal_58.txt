
        LANE - Burton, Composer. At his home in Manhattan on the evening of January 5. Survived by his wife Lynn, his daughter Diana, and stepdaughters Elizabeth, Peggy, and Hillary Kaye. Funeral services Wednesday, 11:15 A.M. at Riverside Chapel, 76th and Amsterdam. In lieu of flowers, contributions may be made to the Young Adult Institute, 460 W. 34 St, 11th floor, NY, NY 10001.
        LANE, Burton. The Board of Trustees of YAI/National Institute for People With Disabilities, is greatly saddened by the passing of Burton Lane. As a distinguished member of our Board, he was a tireless and passionate advocate on behalf of people with diabilities. We extend our heartfelt sympathy to his devoted wife Lynn, his daughter Diana, and to his family.  Marcella Fava, Chairman  Dr. Thomas-Robert Ames.  Vice Chairman  Board of Trustees  Dr. Joel M. Levy, CEO  Dr. Philip H. Levy, COO  YAI/National Institute for People  with Disabilities  460 W. 34 St. NY, NY 10001
        LANE - Burton. The American Society of Composers, Authors and Publishers joins music lovers around the world in mourning the passing of one of America's classic songwriters, Burton Lane, on January 5, 1997 in New York City. Burton, a former ASCAP Board member, joined the Society in 1933. His great success as composer for Tin Pan Alley, Broadway, and Hollywood was matched by his years of selfless service to music creators. Our sincere condolences to Lynn and the family.  Marilyn Bergman,  President and Chairman, ASCAP


        LINVILLE-Clarence Edwin. Of Rye, New York, died Friday, July 13, 2001, on the day of his 92nd birthday at the Osborn Pavilion. Ed was born in State College, PA, on July 13, 1909. He grew up in Elizabeth, New Jersey, graduated in 1930 from Princeton University and received a master's degree in history from Rutgers University. He spent most of his career as an educator and administrator in the New York City public school system. From 1963 through 1972 he served as the principal of the Taft High School in the Bronx. As with many of his generation, his career was interrupted by World War II. He served four years with the army, teaching military intelligence and attaining the rank of Major. Ed lived with his family for over 50 years in Scarsdale, New York. After retiring in 1972, he continued his active participation in community affairs, serving in leadership roles with the Greenburgh Nature Center, the Scarsdale Baptist Church, the Schepp Foundation, the Scarsdale Historical Society and the St. Christopher-Jennie Clarkson Home. Ed is survived by his wife of 58 years, Susanne Gay Linville; his sons, John Evans Linville of New York City and James Coker Linville of Rowayton, Connecticut; and four grandsons. Friends are welcome to attend a memorial service Tuesday, 4 PM, at the Scarsdale Community Baptist Church.

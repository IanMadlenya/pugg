
        GUTHRIE-Henry B., a prominent lawyer in NYC for many years, died Wednesday, February 12, at his home in Bedford, NY, at the age of 94. He was predeceased just one month ago by his devoted wife of 64 years, Elizabeth Archer Guthrie. A native New Yorker, Mr. Guthrie graduated from Hill School in 1920 and Phi Beta Kappa from Princeton University in 1924. After graduation from Harvard University Law School in 1927 he joined the law firm of Shearman & Sterling in NYC. He became a partner of the firm in 1941 and practiced there until his retirement. Avid sailors, ornithologists and salmon fishermen, Mr. and Mrs. Guthrie also maintained a summer home in Southwest Harbor, ME. Active in civic affairs in NYC for many years, Mr. Guthrie was a former Chairman of the Board of Big Brothers, Inc., in NY and former Vice President of Big Brothers of America. He served on the Board of the Camille and Henry Dreyfus Foundation from its founding in 1946 until his death. He served as Secretary and Treasurer of the Board initially and served as its Chairman for a number of years. Mr. Guthrie was a former trustee of the Mount Desert Island Hospital, a Director of the United Hospital Fund of NY and a former Chairman of St. Luke's Hospital. He was on the Board of Green-wood Cemetery in Brooklyn and served as its President in 1973. He was a Director of Doubleday & Co. Inc., where he was Chief Officer of the Board for 10 years and also served as Chairman in 1973. Mr. Guthrie is survived by a brother-in-law, E. Chandlee Archer of Hanover, NH, and a niece, Elizabeth F. Nixon, of Greenwich, CT, as well as many other loving cousins, nieces and nephews. A memorial service will be held May 31, 1997, at the family home in Bedford. Contributions may be made to the American Museum of Natural History, Mianus Gorge Preserve Inc., College of the Atlantic, Bar Harbor, ME, or the charity of your choice.


        WILKING--LEO F.J., M.D. Died following a stroke in Houston, Texas on October 13, 2006. Leo F.J. Wilking was born in Port Arthur, Texas, on April 1, 1919. He was educated in public schools in Texas and Casper, Wyoming, and received his undergraduate degree from Harvard in 1941 and his M.D. from Columbia College of Physicians and Surgeons in 1944. After postgraduate training at St. Luke's and serving in the Army he began private practice in pediatrics in Manhattan, from which he retired in 1979. From 1979 to 1981 he was Director of the Pediatric Service at St. Luke's. He held a clinical appointment in the Department of Pediatrics at Columbia for 25 years. He also served as school physician for The Brearley School, St. Bernard's School, and the St. Thomas Choir School. Dr. Wilking lived with his wife, Virginia Nichols Wilking, M.D., in New York, Delaware, and Nantucket, Massa chusetts. Virginia Wilking died in 2002. Dr. Wilking is survived by his sons, Spencer, Andrew, Leo, and Frank, his daughter Nora, and 12 grandchildren. Funeral services will be held at St. Thomas Church at 5th Avenue, 53rd Street at 3:00 P.M. on Thursday, December 28, 2006.

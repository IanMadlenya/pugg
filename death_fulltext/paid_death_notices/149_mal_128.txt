
        MAUER-Irving, MD. Practiced orthopedic surgery for fifty years in Queens and was on the staff of the Orthopedic Institute and Parkway Hospital. He organized physicians to volunteer their services at Hadassah Hospital in Israel from 1964 to 1973. Many of these physicians including Dr. Mauer came to Israel during the 1973 Yom Kippur War to operate on wounded Israeli soldiers. He also worked in Indonesia under the auspices of C.A.R.E. teaching orthopedic surgery to Indonesian doctors. He was a NYC police surgeon since 1968. He is survived by his wife, Annette, daughters, Eve Morrow and Judy Mauer, sons-in-law, Dr. Richard Morrow and Ilya Slovesnik, two grandchildren, Rebecca and Jack Morrow, brother, Leonard Mauer and sister, Constance Lazarus. Funeral will be Wednesday, June 9, at I.J. Morris, 46 Greenwich St., Hempstead, New York at 1:30PM.

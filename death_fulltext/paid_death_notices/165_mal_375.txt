
        CONKLIN-Aubrey Robert. A longtime Scarsdale, East Hampton and Vero Beach, FL, resident, died of a sudden heart attack early Saturday, October 28, while in New York City. He was 60 years old. Mr Conklin was born June 13, 1940, and raised in Goshen, NY. He was the son of Frances and Aubrey V. Conklin. He earned a Business degree from Pace University. In 1970, he co-founded Aubrey Thomas Personnel with offices in the tri-state area. Under Mr. Conklin's leadership, Aubrey Thomas, Inc., was later sold to the British firm Hestair. Mr. Conklin co-founded Accountec, Inc., in 1999, of which he was CEO. Aubrey served on the boards of Iona Preparatory School in New Rochelle, Bayberry Close in East Hampton and the John's Island Club in Vero Beach. As an active fund raiser, he was responsible for saving the McKee Botanical Gardens, a National Trust in Vero Beach. He was also a member of Our Lady of Fatima Church, the Scarsdale Golf Club and Windsor Club in Vero Beach. He is survived by his three sons, Thomas, Gregory and Paul; his daughters-in-law, Christine and Jennifer; a new grandson, Aubrey James; his fiancee, Linda Musilli and her son and daughter-in-law; and a sister, Patricia Eckerson of Goshen, NY. His wife of 30 years, Joan F. Conklin, died in January 1992. For information, contact Bennett Funeral Home in Scarsdale. In lieu of flowers, contributions in his loving memory may be made to the McKee Botanical Gardens in Vero Beach, FL.


        GWIRTZMAN - Burton. Died peacefully on April 14 from lung cancer at age 75. A true friend to literally thousands with whom he has maintained loving contact throughout his lifetime. A man of the highest integrity, loyalty and humanity. He served bravely as a medic in World War II, he worked with his beloved dad in the garment industry; opened his own brokerage firm as a successful and trusted investment counselor; ultimately became Vice President at Paine Webber. Chosen by the N.Y. Rangers to raise the 1994 Stanley Cup banner at Madison Square Garden after six decades of dedication to the team. Selected by the N.Y. Giants for induction into the NFL Fans Hall of Fame as the all-time premier Giant's fan. Had a passion for fresh water fishing. Adored his wife of 15 years, Sarina, who he proclaimed made his last 15 years his best. Also survived by his brother, Arthur, and the innumerable grateful friends and relatives whose lives he touched with a unique gentleness and wisdom, He made an indelible impact that will always be treasured. Services to be held Wednesday, April 18, at 1:30 PM at Temple Shaaray Tefila, 250 East 79 St. Contributions in his memory may be made to cancer research at NYU Medical Center Office of Development, Attn: Amit Sillman, 550 First Ave, NY, NY 10016 or to The Woodie Wheaton Land Trust, Forest City, Me. 04413

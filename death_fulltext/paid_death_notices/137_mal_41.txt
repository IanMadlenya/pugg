
        SMART-Louis Edwin, of counsel and a former partner at the law firm Hughes Hubbard & Reed LLP, died on Monday, June 1, at St. Luke's Hospital in New York City. He was 74. Mr. Smart was born in Columbus, OH on November 17, 1923, and served as a lieutenant in the U.S. Naval Reserve from 1943 to 1946. He graduated magna cum laude from Harvard University in 1947. In 1949, he received his J.D. magna cum laude from Harvard Law School, where he was an editor of the Law Review, and became associated with Hughes Hubbard & Reed (then Hughes, Hubbard & Ewing) the same year. He joined the firm's partnership in 1957. During his tenure with Hughes Hubbard, Mr. Smart handled major corporate transactions for Bendix Corporation. He left the firm to assume the position of President of Bendix International in 1964. In 1967, he moved to Trans World Airlines, Inc. (TWA), and over the next decade he held the positions of senior vice president of corporate affairs, vice chairman and chief executive officer. Mr. Smart served as chairman of the board at TWA from 1979 to 1985, and also served as chairman and CEO of Transworld Corp. from 1978 to 1987. He returned to Hughes Hubbard as of counsel in 1989. Mr. Smart has served as chairman of Hilton International Company (1978-86), as a member of the Board of Directors of the New York Stock Exchange (1982-88), and on the board of numerous major corporations, including Continental Corp., Sonat Inc. and the corporation formerly known as Flagstar Companies, Inc. In addition to his wife, Jeanie M. Smart, whom he married in 1964, Mr. Smart is survived by his sister, Esther Monteith, and her family; his three children, Cynthia S. Smart of San Antonio, Texas; Douglas E. Smart of Madison, CT; Dana G. Smart of Long Beach, CA; and five grandchildren. In lieu of service, a private reception for family, friends and colleagues has been scheduled for Monday, June 15, from 1PM to 3PM at the Marco Polo Club at the Waldorf-Astoria in New York City. Mr. Smart's family requests that expressions of sympathy be made in the form of memorial contributions to the L. Edwin Smart Lecture Series Fund at Ohio State University. Contributions should be directed to Chairperson, Department of Economics, Ohio State University, 410 Arps Hall, 1945 North High Street, Columbus, OH 43210.

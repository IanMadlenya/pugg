
        PARSONS--Evelyn Holmes. Died peacefully at The Connecticut Hospice in Branford CT on February 2, 2004. She was born on October 7, 1919 in Manchester, NH, the only child of James Robertson and Lena (Eckhardt). She attended Manchester public schools and The University of New Hampshire, before moving to Boston, where she worked as a legal secretary at Hale and Doerr. She cofounded and operated Academic Year Abroad with her first husband, the late Stetson Holmes. She lived variously in New York City, Paris, France, Beirut, Lebanon, and Connecticut, spending the last two decades in Madison, as the loving wife of Howard L. Parsons, who predeceased her. She is survived by her daughter, Shelley Holmes, her son, Brian Holmes (and his wife Eugenie Jenkins), step sons William Parsons (and his wife, Margo), and George Parsons (and his wife, Delores), three grandchildren, and three stepgrandchildren. She will be remembered by those who knew her as a good and cheer ful friend with a gift for words, a wonderful sense of humor, and a love of reading, The New York Times Crossword Puzzle, gardening, gourmet cooking, entertaining, and serving as Treasurer of The Windermere Beautification Committee. A Memorial Service will be held at 3 P.M. on Saturday, February 21, in The Chapel of The First Congregational Church of Madison, which is located at 26 Meeting House Lane, in Madison, CT. Donations in her memory may be made to: The Connecticut Hospice, 100 Double Beach Road, Branford, CT 06405.


        YOUNG--Stanley R., Dr. Of Southbury, Connecticut, formerly of Mamaroneck, New York, died on February 15, 2004 after a short illness. He was 80 years of age. Dr. Young was in private practice as an optometrist in Mamaroneck, N.Y. for 40 years before his retirement in 1990. He was past President of the New York State Optometric Society and past President of the New York State Board of Optometry. Dr. Young was born on December 1, 1923 in Bronx, N.Y. to Carl and Celia Ratner Young. He was educated in New York City where he received a B.S. and M.S. from Columbia University and a Doctor of Optometry degree from the Massachusetts College of Optometry. On December 22, 1946 he married Faith Arnold in New York City. Dr. Young lived in Southbury, Conn. for the past two years. Before that he lived in Armonk, N.Y. for nineteen years and Mamaroneck, N.Y. for 32 years. Dr. Young was a founding director of the S.U.N.Y. College of Optometry where he was a clinical professor for many years. He will be remembered by both students and colleagues as a warm and devoted doctor and friend. He will also be remembered for his love of traveling, photography and art which he shared with his wife Faith. Dr. Young is survived by his wife of 57 years Faith Young of Southbury, Conn., sons Dr. Harris Young (Linda), Santa Rosa, CA., Dr. Joseph Young (Lynn), Newtown, Conn., M. Scott Young (Linda), Madison, WI., grandchildren Matthew, Lindsey, Crystal, Chelsea and sister Mildred Spar, Lake Success, N.Y. In lieu of flowers, the family requests donations be sent to the Dr. Stanley R. Young Memorial Scholarship Fund, New York Optometric Center, 33 West 42nd Street, New York, New York.

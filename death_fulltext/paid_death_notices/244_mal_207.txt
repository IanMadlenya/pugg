
        STEVENS--John, 92, of North Branford and Madison, CT died peacefully May 15th after several months of declining health. A recipient of the Yale Medal in 2003, he was also Trustee Emeritus of Quinnipiac University, Past President of the UConn Foundation and a former Trustee of Kent School and The Foote School. He was Past President of Gaylord Hospital and Chairman of the Connecticut State Commission on Human Services (1986-1988). Born June 12, 1914, in Wallingford, CT, John was a graduate of Kent School and Yale College, Class of 1937. After starting out at J. Walter Thompson, he joined the International Silver Company in 1938 where, after wartime service in the Atlantic and Pacific as Lt. Commander in the U.S. Naval Reserve, he became President, then Chairman, and then Executive Vice President and a director of Insilco Corporation. John was co-founder and first President of The Farms Country Club, Wallingford, Conn. and a Past President of The Madison Beach Club and The 24Karat Club of New York City. Having survived Marcia, his wife of 63 years who died in January, he is survived by his four children, Jay Stevens of New Haven, CT, Mary Stevens of Seattle, WA, Philip Stevens of Guilford, Conn. and Catherine Wilson of Seattle, WA, nine grandchildren and a great-grandchild. A memorial service will be held on Friday, May 25 at 11am at First Congregational Church of Guilford, CT Donations (payable to Yale University) may be made to the John B. Stevens Scholarship Fund, c/ o Yale Alumni Office, PO Box 1890, New Haven, CT 06508. Guilford Funeral Home, 115 Church St, Guilford, CT in charge of funeral services.

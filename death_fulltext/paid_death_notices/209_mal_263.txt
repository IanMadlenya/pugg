
        SEAMAN--William. William Bernard Seaman, M.D., a leader among radiologists in New York and nationwide in the 1960s and 70s, passed away in his home in Tequesta, Florida on June 7, 2004. Dr. Seaman, who was 87 years old at the time of his death, was born in Iron Mountain, MI, the son of Dorothy and Benjamin Seaman. Dr. Seaman graduated from the University of Michigan and received his M.D. from Harvard in 1941. He completed a residency in Radiology at Yale in 1947 and by 1955 had reached the rank of full professor in the Department of Radiology at Washington University School of Medicine in St. Louis, MO. In 1956 Dr. Seaman accepted the position of Professor and Chairman of the Department of Radiology at the College of Physicians and Surgeons of Columbia University in New York City, where he served as chair and holder of the James Picker Professorship until 1982. He remained on the faculty at Columbia until his retirement in 1990. During his prominent career Dr. Seaman served as president of numerous organizations, including the Association of University Radiologists (195556), the NY Roentgen Society (1961-62), the Society of Chairmen of Academic Radiology Departments (1967-68), the American Roentgen Ray Society (1973-74), and the American College of Radiology (1980-81). Dr. Seaman also served as the Chairman of the Board of Chancellors of the American College of Radiology, which is the College's highest position, and on several advisory committees of the National Academy of Science. He published over 100 scientific articles, primarily in his field of gastrointestinal radiology, and presented 15 named lectureships. A worldrenowned lecturer, Dr. Seaman was inducted as an Honorary Fellow of the Royal College of Radiology in London, England, being one of only 10 Americans to hold that honor at the time. He received the W.B. Cannon medal of the Society of Gastrointestinal Radiologists and the gold medals of the Association of University Radiologists, the American Roentgen Ray Society and the American College of Radiology. Dr. Seaman is survived by his wife of 60 years, Veryl, three children & five grandchildren.

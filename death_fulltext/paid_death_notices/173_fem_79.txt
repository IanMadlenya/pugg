
        WATERMAN-Elise DaCosta. On June 7, 2001, at age 93. Beloved wife of the late Philip M. Waterman. Devoted and proud mother and mother-in-law of Philip M. Waterman Jr. and Jane, Marie W. Harris and Kenneth. Loved and admired grandmother of Philip M. Waterman III, Anne L. Waterman, Kenneth A. Harris Jr. and Ellen Harris Knoblock and Henry. Adored great-grandmother of Christopher H. Knoblock and Caroline D. Knoblock. Dear sister of Manette D. Kaufmann. Elise was a remarkable and elegant woman. She distinguished herself to family and friends by her wisdom, wit and zeal for life. Her special prescence will be deeply missed. Services will be private. Doantions may be made to the American Jewish Committee, 165 E. 56th St, NY, NY 10022.
        WATERMAN-Elise D. The Women's Campaign Board of the American Jewish Committee mourns with profound sadness the death of one of our Founders and past Chairs, Elise Waterman. A remarkably gifted leader with the ability to inspire generations of women toward a life of philanthropy and civic commitment, Elise served AJC in numerous capacities over more than a half century, including as a National Vice President. She also sat on AJC's Board of Governors, Board of Trustees and Interreligious Affairs Commission, and was actively involved with programs spanning the agency's broad-based agenda. We will sorely miss her friendship, wisdom and generosity of spirit. We send our heartfelt condolences to her entire family. Stephanie Saft, Chair Elaine Petschek, Honorary Chair
        WATERMAN-Elise D. Congregation Emanu-El of the City of New York records with deep sorrow the death of our cherished and devoted member, Elise D. Waterman. To her family and loved ones, we express our profound sympathy. May beautiful memory bring comfort to those who mourn her passing. Robert A. Bernhard, President Herbert C. Bernard, Secretary


        McHUGH-Janet, 63, born 6 October 1937, in Redding, Calif., passed away peacefully in the Bronx on 29 July 2001, following a long illness. She worked at Columbia University in the Projects and Grants Department. She attended Vassar College Class of 1959 and studied history and classics. Her student years ignited a lifelong passion for books and the study of antiquity. She devoted a quiet private life to research, study and travel, collecting for a study of the history of costume through the ages. Her mother, Alice, and her brothers, Patrick and Thomas McHugh, survive her. Her family cherishes her memory. A family memorial will be held in Fort Fairfield, Maine, and interment will be in 29 Palms, Calif.


        SLIFKA-Sylvia. Passed away Sunday, April 6th, 2003 after a long and lovely life. Adored wife of the late Joseph Slifka. Loving mother of Alan and Barbara Slifka. Grandmother of Michael, Randolph and David, and great grandmother of Grace and Joshua. Services private. Family receiving Tuesday and Wednesday evenings, 5-8PM at Mrs. Slifka's residence. Contributions in her name may be made to Temple Emanu - El, 5th Avenue at 65th Street, NY, NY 10021, or The Abraham Joshua Heschel School.
        SLIFKA-Sylvia. The officers, Board of Directors, and staff of UJA-Federation of New York mourn the passing of Sylvia Slifka, beloved mother of Alan B. and Barbara Slifka, cherished friends of UJA-Federation. Mrs. Slifka and her late husband, Joseph, left a legacy of caring to her children, including generous support of Jewish education, humanitarian causes, and promoting understanding among Israel and the world's people. We extend our deepest sympathies to Alan and Barbara, & to the entire Slifka family. Larry Zicklin, President Morris W. Offit, Chair of the Board John S. Ruskay, Exec. V.P. & C.E.O.
        SLIFKA-Sylvia. Congregation Emanu-El of the City of New York expresses to the family of our cherished and devoted member, Sylvia Slifka, our deepest sympathies on the occasion of her passing. It is our hope and prayer that beautiful memories will linger as a legacy to her life. May all who loved her be comforted. Rabbi David M. Posner Robert A. Bernhard, Pres. Herbert C. Bernard, Sec'y.
        SLIFKA-Sylvia. Phyllis Hattis and Bill Rubin express our deepest sympathy to Barbara and Alan Slifka and mourn the passing of a woman whom we have known for over thirty years (in Bill's case, sixty). Sylvia's humor brightened every day we spent with her. We loved her and shall miss her deeply.

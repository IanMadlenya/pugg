
        FINDLAY-Harriet Lindsay. Died unexpectedly in her Manhattan apartment on July 5th, 2001. Known as Lindsay, she was born on August 16, 1935, in Kansas City, MO, and grew up in New Canaan, CT. A graduate of Dana Hall School, Goucher College and the Harvard-Radcliffe Business Program, she initially worked for The Putnam Fund in Boston, MA. In 1958 she joined her father, David B. Findlay Sr., in his Manhattan art business, David Findlay Galleries. The original Findlay Galleries was founded in Kansas City, MO, 1870. Lindsay was a member of the fourth generation of the family to be involved with the gallery. In 1989 her father died, and Lindsay assumed control of the gallery. She is survived by her mother, Harriet Jonew Findlay, two brothers, David B. Findlay Jr. and H. Peter Findlay, and numerous nieces and nephews. Each of the brothers has his own eponymous art gallery in Manhattan. A memorial service will be held at the Congregational -United Church of Christ, 23 Park Street, New Canaan, CT, on Tuesday, July 17th at 11:00 AM. In lieu of flowers, contributions may be made to the Humane Society of Manhattan in memory of the deceased.
        FINDLAY-H. Lindsay. The members of the Art Dealers Association of America mourn the passing of their colleague, a member of a distinguished family of art dealers, and the President since 1989 of David Findlay Galleries. Our deepest sympathy and condolences to her mother and her two brothers, both members of ADAA. Richard Gray, President Gilbert S. Edelson, Admin. V.P.


        CAMARATA-''Monty''. Berkeley Heights, N.J. Monroe ''Monty'' Anthony Camarata died June 19, 2001 after a long illness. He died at Overlook Hospital in Summit, N.J., surrounded by his family. Monty was the son of Charles and Dorothy (Holtzman) Camarata. He was born and grew up in Newark, N.J. He graduated from Seton Hall College in South Orange, N.J. and Rutgers School of Law-Newark in Newark, N.J., and served in the U.S. Navy during WWII. He owned and operated clothing stores throughout Bergen County, N.J. Monty was a member of the Bergen County Chapter of the Optimists Club, & a frequent volunteer at the Betty Bacharach Rehab Hospital in Pomona, N.J. He was devoted to his wife and daughters, and loved music, especially opera. Throughout his life, Monty rescued and cared for homeless animals of all kinds. He appreciated flowers and gardens, and enjoyed the simple pleasures of everyday life with his family and friends. He is survived by his wife of fiftysix years, Jeannine (Honnegger) Camarata, and two daughters, Roberta Renard of Summit, N.J. and Jessica Camarata of Denver, CO. A private service was held on Thursday, June 21st for the family at Saint Rocco's Church in Newark. In Monty's memory, please make a donation to the charity of your choice.


        CHANDLER-Amy. New York, NY, age 97. On Wednesday, September 26, 2001. Member of the Civic Repertory Theater in the 1930s and the American Repertory Theater in the 1940s. Born in Philadelphia, PA, and came to New York in the 1920s with her friend Thelma Chandler to become a ballerina. Toured with Margaret Webster's company's Bus and Truck tours, which took the theater to towns and cities across the U.S., and the bus and truck tours of Golden Apple, Funny Girl, No Time for Sergeants and Teahouse of the August Moon. Companion to Jane Cowl. Lifelong Democrat. She was a lover and supporter of dogs and the arts, including the New York Metropolitan Opera, the New York City Ballet, as well as the theater and classical music. She was preceded in death by both her parents, her sister Joyce Neely, and her brother Jack Knorr. She is survived by her sister, Violet Brown of Bossier City, LA, and three nieces, Joyce Brown of Nottingham, England, Lois Ennis of Shreveport, LA, and Jill Knorr Smith of New Cumberland, PA. The family requests that donations in memory of Amy be made to the Actors Fund, 729 Seventh Avenue, New York, NY 10019.


        POWELL--Jane. Executive Director and cofounder of Shofar Synagogue in Los Angeles, passed away on Saturday, December 17, 2005 after a battle with pancreatic cancer. She had also served as Executive Director and was co-founder of the Synagogue for the Performing Arts. Born on February 28, 1937, to Samuel S. Burman and Lee Goldstein Burman, she received a journalism degree from New York University in 1958. Her husband Charles M. Powell, who passed away in 1991, was a long-time Governor and Officer of the Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences. She is also the co-author, with Lori G. Berthelsen, of the forthcoming book ''Now Playing: Original Movie Posters from the Silent Era through the 1940s.'' She is no relation to the actress of the same name. She is survived by one son, Mel Powell, a writer and representative of Pre-Paid Legal Services, Inc., and her beloved cats. Donations may be made to Shofar Synagogue, Los Angeles, California.


        SHEPLEY--John R. A leading translator from Italian to English and writer published in numerous scholarly and literary magazines, died on May 31, 2003. He was 78 years old. Cause of death was cancer of the bladder. Mr. Shepley was a graduate from Harvard in 1946. He lived in Florence, Italy, from 1955 to 1961. He was a member of P.E.N. American Center where he served for many years in the Translation Committee. In 1987 he was awarded the first Italo Calvino prize from Columbia University for his best Italian translation of Pierpaolo Pasolini book ''Roman Nights and Other Stories.'' Among his numerous translations are works by Oriana Fallaci, Beppe Fenoglio, Ferdinando Camon, Maria Messina and Enzo Siciliano. His own fiction appeared in such literary magazines as the Southern Review, Southern Humanities Review, Massachusetts Review, Salamagundi, Mondo Greco, Bitter Oleander and New Orleans Review. >>AD#

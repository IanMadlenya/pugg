
        DURSO--Joseph. A writer with the New York Times for 50 years, 1950-2000, died Friday night after a long battle with cancer. He was the author of 14 books, six of which were published in Japanese. He was the recipient of the J.G. Taylor Spink Award of the National Baseball Hall of Fame in 1995. His radio program, Sports of the Times with Joe Durso, was broadcast on WQXR for 27 years. His last book, ''Dimaggio,'' is an intimate biography of America's greatest sports hero. Durso was a friend of Dimaggio for 40 years, and one of the few men allowed in his inner circle. The book is written with a rich sense of history, from the perspective of a close friend. His assignments have taken him all over the world, to more than 25 World Series, 20 Kentucky Derbies and the Olympics. He has done extensive lecturing, radio and television work. He was a also a fighter pilot in the United States Air Force. Durso, a Phi Beta Kappa, was graduated magna cum laude from New York University with a Bachelor of Arts degree. He received a master of science degree and a Pulitzer Traveling Scholarship from the Columbia Graduate School of Journalism, where he also served as professor for 15 years. He also received an honorary Doctor of Letters from Dowling College on Long Island. He is survived by his wife Margaret, a former advertising executive, three children, Peter, Christopher and David, two step-children, Joseph and Elise, and nine grandchildren. He is also survived by his sister Teresa and brother Michael. His Funeral Mass will take place at St. James Church in Setauket, LI, Wednesday, January 5, 10:30 am, with a reception at his home immediately following.

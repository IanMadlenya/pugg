
        ARISON-Ted. The officers, board and staff of the American Technion Society (ATS) express profound regret at the loss of Ted Arison of Miami and Tel Aviv. Mr. Arison was a Technion Guardian who had reached the highest level of commitment to the Technion, the ATS and Israel. For his exemplary support of the Faculty of Computer Science, the Technion awarded him an Honorary Doctorate in 1999. We extend heartfelt condolences to his wife Lin and the entire Arison family. American Society for TechnionIsrael Institute of Technology Lawrence S. Jackier, President Irving A. Shepard, Chairman of the Board Melvyn H. Bloom Executive Vice President
        ARISON-Ted. On behalf of the Trustees, Staff, the thousands of young artists who benefitted from his vision, as well as the hundreds of donors and supporters who share that vision, the National Foundation for Advancement in the Arts sends its deepest condolences to the Arison family. Ted Arison's life was an example for all of us to emulate and continue in his love and support for the arts. Thanks to him thousands of young artists around the country will continue to experience the arts and receive the support needed to accomplish their dreams. As founder, friend and supporter, he will be sorely missed by all of us in the arts community across America.
        ARISON-Ted. Albert Einstein College of Medicine of Yeshiva University records with deep sorrow the passing of our good friend and respected member of our Society of Founders. To his wife, Lin; his children, Shari and Mickey; and his entire family, we extend our heartfelt condolences. Dr. Norman Lamm, President Yeshiva University Burton P.


        HUGHES-James M. Beloved husband of Catherine M. Hughes (nee; Enright.) Born in New York City, he was a graduate of City College of New York and St. John's University Law School. After admittance to the New York Bar he served as an Assistant Attorney General of the State of New York, assigned to the Stock Fraud Bureau. After his service in the Attorney General's office, he entered into the private practice of law in New York City. he served in the War Shipping Administration during World War II and thereafter joined the Wall Street law firm of Bigham, Englar, and Jones & Houston where he continued to work for 38 years retiring from there as a partner of said firm. During his law practice with the law firm he was admitted to practice in the Supreme Court of the United States as well as the U.S. District Courts for the Southern and Eastern Districts of New York in addition to the Courts of New York State. He was a fellow of the American College of Trial Lawyers. He was an honorary member of St. John's Law School Alumni. He was also a member of the American Bar Association, the Maritime Law Association of the United States, the Catholic Speakers Assembly, the Knights of Columbus and the Telistians. Mr. Hughes was predeceased by his two brothers, Robert and Thomas, both of who were residents of Fort Lee at the time of their deaths.

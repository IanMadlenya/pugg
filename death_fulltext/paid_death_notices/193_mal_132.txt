
        LAMBOS-Constantine Peter, 76, of Tampa, Florida, formerly of Crestwood, New York, died peacefully in his sleep at home on Saturday, February 8, 2003. Born August 4, 1926, in Manhattan, Peter served in the United States Army as a rifleman-scout with the 63rd Infantry Division in France and Germany during World War II. He graduated first in his class from New York University Law School, where he was the Editor-in-Chief of the Law Review. This year marked his fifty-fifth year as a lawyer. He was the senior partner of Lambos & Junge in New York City and served as chief management counsel to the East Coast longshore industry for the past forty years. Peter had served as the President of Leewood Golf Club in Eastchester, New York and the Downtown Athletic Club in New York City. For the past twenty years he was the managing partner of The Eagles Golf Club in Odessa, Florida. He is survived by his devoted wife of fifty years, Theodora, his loving son, William, his grandchildren, Elizabeth, Katherine and Peter, his sisters, Theresa Coutroulos, Mary Lambos, Helen Papantonis, and Aphrodite Danas, and his daughters-in-law, Carol and Diane. The funeral will be held on Thursday, February 13, 2003, at 10 A.M. at the Holy Trinity Greek Orthodox Church in New Rochelle, New York. Memorial donations may be made to the Peter and Andrew Lambos Foundation, 16101 Nine Eagles Drive, Odessa, Florida 33556.

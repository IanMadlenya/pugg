
        SILLECK--Robert Bosworth, 87, of Greenwich, Connecticut, died peacefully at Greenwich Hospital on April 10, 2005. Mr. Silleck was born on June 5, 1917 in Brooklyn, New York. He graduated from The Hill School in Pottstown, Pennsylvania and from Yale University in 1939. He attended OCS at Annapolis, and after his graduation was commissioned an officer in the United State Nave. He served as captain on destroyers throughout World War II, participating in many of the major naval battles in the Pacific, including the Battle of the Coral Sea, Midway and Okinawa. After returning from the war, he joined the First National Bank, and eventually became a Vice President of First National City Bank. Following his retirement from Citibank, he joined Bradford Corporation. Mr. Silleck remained an active sailor throughout his life. He was a member of the American Yacht Club, and a former member of the Bronxville Field Club and the Racquet & Tennis Club of New York. He met his wife, Patricia Phillips Silleck, in Hawaii shortly after the Pearl Harbor attack. She survives him as do his son, Robert Yadn Silleck, of Denver, Colorado and his daughter, Patricia Ranlet Conway, of Greenwich, as well as four grandchildren. He was predeceased by his son, John Howland Silleck, and his grandson, John Phillips Conway. A memorial service will be held this summer at Kimogener Point on Long Island. In lieu of flowers, donations may be made to The Hill School, 717 East High Street, Pottstown, Pennsylvania 19464.


        HALLIGAN--Theresa, Ph.D, died on January 19, 2007, at Calvary Hospital in the Bronx, New York, after suffering a stroke this past December. Dr. Halligan, 80, was a long-time resident of Ridgefield Park, New Jersey. A Requiem Mass will be held for Dr. Halligan on Monday, January 22, 2007 at 12pm at the Chapel at the Jeanne Jugen Residence, 2999 Schurz Avenue, Bronx, New York, 10465. The Mass will be offered by her brother, John J. Halligan, SJ. Dr. Halligan was born in the Bronx. After receiving a Bachelors of Arts degree from Mount St. Vincent College, Dr. Halligan received her masters and doctorate from Fordham University, where she served on the facility from 1953 until her retirement as an Associate Professor Emerita of English. Upon her retirement, Dr. Halligan continued her charitable works, including tutoring students at the St. Aloysius Catholic School in Harlem, as well as dedicating her time to Family Unity International, Incorporated, which supports the Working Boys Center, established by Fr. Halligan in 1964 to help working poor families in Quito Ecuador. In addition to her brother, Dr. Halligan is survived by her sisters, Patricia Walsh of Quincy, Massachusetts, and Sr. Kathleen Halligan, SND, and her 16 nieces and nephews. Dr. Halligan was predeceased by her brothers, Joseph Halligan and Fr. Peter Halligan, MM, and her sisters, Sr. Margaret Mary Halligan, SND, and Sr. Mary Rose Halligan, SND. In lieu of flowers please send donations to the Family Unity International, Incorporated, 12750 Stephen Place, Elm Grove, Wisconsin 53122.

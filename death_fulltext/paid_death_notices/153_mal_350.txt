
        HUET-Jean. Chairman and Chief Executive Officer, SG Americas. A memorial service will be held on Friday, October 29 at 11AM at Church of St. Ignatius Loyola, 980 Park Avenue at 84th St., New York City. Born in Barcelona, Spain, Mr. Huet received his degree from the Paris-based Institut d'Etudes Politiques. He began his career with Societe Generale in 1968 in the Inspection Generale, achieving the rank of Inspecteur Principal. In 1978 he joined the International Division as Area Manager of North America and Great Britain. He came to the United States five years later as Deputy General Manager of SG in the U.S. From 1988 until 1993, he served as General Manager of Societe Generale in London before returning to the U.S. as Chief Executive Officer. Mr. Huet was responsible for leading the significant growth in SG's activities in the United States, Canada and Latin America over the last several years. He was also active in the professional, academic and cultural affairs of New York and the U.S. Mr. Huet served as a member of the Board of the French-American Chamber of Commerce in the United States, Co-Chairman of the Business Committee of the Meotropolitan Museum of Art, Co-Chair of the Carnegie Hall Corporate Fund, and Chair of the Advisory Board of the Center for French Civilization and Culture of New York University, among others. His professional and civic accomplishments were recognized in 1997 when he was awarded the French Legion of Honor, one of the French Republic's highest civilian honors. He is survived by Veronique and Jean-Michel and their mother Michel Huet.


        TURI--Dr. Paul George. 87, on February 7, 2005. Winner of the 1934 Hungarian national mathematics prize, denied entry to medical school under fascist laws and confined to slave labor during World War II, he escaped to America in 1956. He worked for three decades as a Ph.D. research chemist in pharmaceutical sciences at Sandoz (Novartis). An optimist despite adversity, a passionate traveler with a far-ranging intellect, he was a scientist, Latin scholar, poet, chess player and philosopher. Beloved husband of Edith, adored father and father-in-law of Zoltan and Adele, cherished grandfather of Michael; loved and missed terribly. Services on Friday at Platt Memorial Chapel, Haddonfield-Berlin Road, Voorhees, New Jersey.

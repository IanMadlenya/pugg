
        SMITH-Datus C., Jr. Died in Princeton, New Jersey, on November 17, 1999 at the age of 92. He played a leading role in the development of American scholarly publishing, and in the promotion of book publishing in the Third World. During his later years, he was also active in international work, particularly with The J.D.R. 3rd Fund, The Asia Society, and The U.S. Committee For UNICEF. His publications include Land and People of Indonesia, and Guide To Book Publishing, each of which has been translated into seven or eight languages. His memory will be cherished by his daughters, Sandra Smith Opdycke, and Karen Houston Smith, his son-in-law, Leo Opdycke, three granddaughters, and one great granddaughter.


        PECKERMAN-Edward R. Jr., Washington, CT, 93, died Tuesday morning at Rose Haven in Litchfield after a short illness. Mr. Peckerman was born in Boston, June 3, 1905. His family moved to New York in 1914. He graduated from Horace Mann School, where he later served as chairman of the board of trustees. He graduated from Princeton University in 1925 and from Columbia Law School in 1928. In 1943 he volunteered to serve in the armed forces, he joined naval aviation and was onboard an aircraft carrier in the South Pacific; he was in the command that secured the island of Guam. He was discharged in 1946 with the rank of Commander. In 1971 he retired and he and his wife moved to Washington, CT, were he became involved in environmental organizations, among others, Steep Rock Association, Housatonic Valley Association, the Roxbury Land Trust and served on the zoning commission in Washington. He was a member of the University Club in New York and the Washington Club. He is survived by the love of his wife, Kristen. Contributions in his name may be made to the Steep Rock Association, P.O. Box 279, Washington Depot, CT. 06794.


        HADLEY--Dr. Jane B., died on March 18, 2004 at 74 years after a courageous battle with cancer. Jane was born in New York City, graduated from Music and Art High School in 1945, and from the University of Virginia in 1949 on a full scholarship, Junior Phi Beta Kappa and Summa Cum Laude. She also modeled, becoming Miss Chesterfield in the early 1950's. When her marriage began dissolving in the 1960's she entered Columbia Graduate School in psychology and did her analytical training at the Metropolitan Center for Mental Health. She interned at Queens College and went on to became a noted psychotherapist in Manhattan. In 1979, she married Arthur T. Hadley, who survives her. She is also survived by two daughters, Elisabeth Wheeler of Stockbridge, MA and Caroline Thies of Boston, along with four grandchildren, Alexandra, Angus, Benjamin and Austin. She was on the boards of Planned Parenthood of New York and the American Liver Foundation, Greater New York Chapter. She was a member of The Cosmopolitan Club and The Century. A memorial service will be held for her at The Century on Friday, April 2nd at 3:00 P.M.

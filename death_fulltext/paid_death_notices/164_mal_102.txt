
        SCHEIBER - Leopold, M.D. (1914-2000) Dr. Leopold Scheiber, a physician, specializing in surgery and urology, was a native of Budapest, Hungary. He had practiced medicine in Budapest and for decades in New York. He died in Budapest on August 7, 2000, a few days after his arrival there on a visit, where he was buried. Dr. Scheiber was from a famous family of rabbis, he was the devoted brother of the legendary scholar, Professor Alexander Scheiber, the former Director of the Jewish Theological Seminary of Hungary. He survived the Holocaust physically, but in his thoughts and feelings he remained a victim. He could never forget witnessing the murder of his mother and friends by Hungarian Nazis in the last days of the war in Budapest. Dr. Scheiber devoted his life, energy, talent and industry to the healing profession, he practiced as a full time physician until his stroke in January 2000. Dr. Scheiber was a philanthropist for many Jewish causes, and a lover of music and the arts. He is mourned by his niece, Doctor Maria Scheiber and her family in Budapest, Hungary, and by numerous friends, relatives and patients in New York, Israel and elsewhere.

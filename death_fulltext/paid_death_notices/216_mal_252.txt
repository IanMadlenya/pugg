
        CARTER -- Sidney, M.D. Members of the Department of Neurology at Columbia University and the Neurological Institute of New York mourn the death of Dr. Sidney Carter on January 16, 2005. He was the Dwight D. Eisenhower Professor of Neurology Emeritus and had been Director of Pediatric Neurology at Babies Hospital from 1951 to 1978. Dr. Carter was one of the founders of pediatric neurology. He led the development of this superspecialty of neurology, established guidelines, obtained financial support for pediatric neurology training programs, and designed the requirements for certification of child neurologists. He was a brilliant clinician and an outstanding teacher who made Columbia Presbyterian Medical Center the center of child neurology in the United States for over 25 years. An internationally recognized leader, he served as President of both the American Academy of Neurology and the American Neurological Association. He was beloved by all who knew him and the field of pediatric neurology is his lasting legacy. We extend our sympathy and admiration to Elizabeth (Buff), his wife for 60 years, his sons Jeffrey, Jonathan and Jeremy, and their families. Timothy A. Pedley, M.D. Henry and Lucy Moses Professor of Neurology Chairman, Department of Neurology & Lewis P. Rowland, M.D., Chairman Emeritus, Department of Neurology

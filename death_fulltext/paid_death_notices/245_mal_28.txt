
        KANE--Lester, died peacefully at age 84 on May 31, leaving his devoted wife of 61 years, Libby, and children Nancy, Robert, Richard and Claudia, sons-in-law Michael and Ross, daughter-in-law Susan, and four grandchildren, Micah, Logan, Diego and Alicia. He was a World War II veteran who served the Judge Advocate General in Saipan with distinction. Les was a court reporter for 39 years, during which time he founded the Association of Surrogate and Supreme Court Reporters Within the City of New York, serving as its president for 18 years. He was a recipient of the Louis Goldstein Memorial Award, and a Fellow of the National Court Reporters Association. He was also the award-winning editor of -SERVE,-the B'nai B'rith (Crestwood Chapter) newspaper. Continuing a life-long interest in art, after retirement Les attended and graduated from Sarah Lawrence College. As a second career, he curated exhibits in Westchester of contemporary New York area abstract expressionist artists. He was an avid tennis player and was watching the French Open on television the day he died. His family and his dog Penny will miss him very much. In lieu of flowers, donations may be made to your favorite charity.


        SOLMSSEN-Ulrich V., 92. Formerly of Essex Fells, NJ, died on Sunday, June 9, 2002 in Hightstown, NJ. Born in Berlin, Germany, in 1909, he attended MIT as an exchange student in 1932. After receiving his Ph.D in chemistry from the University of Zurich, he returned to the United States in 1938 to pursue his life's career in pharmaceutical research first at Hoffman-LaRoche and then at Warner Lambert. Prior to his retirement in 1972, he served as scientific director of the international division and president of the chemical division. A resident of Essex Fells, NJ for 36 years, he served on the boards of the Essex Fells Public Schools, Montclair-Kimberley Academy, Mountainside Hospital and the NJ Chamber Music Society. Admired and respected by many, he will be greatly missed by his wife of 66 years Tinka Solmssen of Meadow Lakes, Hightstown, NJ; his four daughters: Julie Steedman of York Harbor, ME; Lily Moureaux of New Rochelle, NY; Wendy Sommer of Washington, DC; and Teresa Shartar of Toronto, Canada; nine grandchildren, & one great-granddaughter. He is also survived by his brother, Harold of Muskeegan, MI. Memorial contributions may be made to Lekotek of Georgia, a non-profit organization dedicated to serving the needs of children with special needs and their families. Lekotek of Georgia, 1955 Cliff Valley Way, Suite 102, Atlanta, GA. 30329.

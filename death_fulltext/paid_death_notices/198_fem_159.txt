
        LAW--Alma H., of Scarsdale, NY died peacefully in her sleep on July 22, 2003. She was 76 years old. She was born on April 19, 1927 in Racine, Wisconsin. She was the daughter of Alma S. Hanson and Allen C. Hanson. She married Edward G. Law in 1946 who passed away in 1986. Alma was an important writer and scholar of Soviet theatre and an authority on the work of playwright Vsevolod Meyerhold. She conducted extensive research in the Soviet archives in the 1970's long before they were generally open to scholars in the West. She wrote several books and translations of Soviet playwrights and was a valued resource to scholars of Soviet theatre. Alma received her PhD in Slavic Studies from Columbia University, was a fellow at the City University of New York in Slavic Studies and a fellow at the Woodrow Wilson Center in Washington, DC. Alma is survived by two sons, Keith Law and his wife Ann of Hartsdale, NY and Eric Law and his wife Candace of Berkley, Michigan. She is also survived by her sisters May Allyn Hanson of Racine, Wisconsin and Karen H. Poling of Bellevue, Washington. Services to be held at the funeral home Friday 12 noon.


        CONCANNON--Thomas J., 61, died April 23, 2005, having survived five years with multiple myeloma. His love of life lives on in his wife, Jan Mardfin (''the Enchantress''); children, Peter and Leah Concannon; sisters, Regina, Constance, and Claire; brothersin-law, Joseph, Vincent, and Frank; 17 nieces and nephews; and countless friends and colleagues. For 33 years he devoted his life to defending and befriending the downtrodden and the defenseless, for the last 23 years as Attorney-In-Charge of the Eastern District Federal Defender Division of the Legal Aid Society. Calling hours will be Wednesday, April 27, 2005, from 6 PM to 9 PM, at the Riverside Memorial Chapel, 180 W. 76th St. at Amsterdam Ave., NYC. The funeral mass will be held on Thursday, April 28, 2005 at Holy Trinity Roman Catholic Church, 213 W. 82nd St., NYC, at 11 AM. In lieu of flowers, contributions may be made to Covenant House (www.covenanthouse.org), The Legal Aid Society (www.legal-aid.org), the International Myeloma Foundation (www.myeloma.org), and Memorial Sloan-Kettering Cancer Center (www.mskcc.org), or a charity of your choosing.
        CONCANNON--Thomas J. The New York Council of Defense Lawyers mourns the death of our longtime member and Director. Tom was a recipient of the NYCDL's Ostrow Award for his outstanding contribution to the defense of liberty and the preservation of individual rights. As the Attorney-in-Charge of the Federal Defender Office in the Eastern District of New York, Tom exemplified the highest and noblest aspirations of the legal profession. We extend our deepest condolences to his wife, Janet, and to his children, Peter and Leah. We have all lost a giant of humanity and integrity. Marjorie Peerce, President New York Council of Defense Lawyers

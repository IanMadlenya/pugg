
        ULLMAN-Emily, 87, o f Port Washington, NY, died peacefully in her sleep at home on October 25. Emily was a wonderful wife, mother, grandmother and greatgrandmother who led an extraordinary life. Her strength, intellect and compassion touched many. She devoted much of her life to charitable activities. She was a volunteer at the Human Resources School in Albertson, NY for 25 years and also served as a trustee of the Unitarian-Universalist Congregation at Shelter Rock, NY, of which she and her husband were members for 50 years. An accomplished pianist, she loved music. She also acted in many local theatre productions. Emily and her husband, Frank, to whom she was married for 64 years, survived the Holocaust in hiding in Amsterdam. They immigrated to the U.S. and settled in Port Washington, NY in 1947. Her family and friends will miss her very much. Her life and love will always be remembered. She is survived by her husband, Frank, her sons Henry and Leo, her daughters-in-law, Kathleen and Katharine, six grandchildren and five great-grandchildren. A service to celebrate her life will be held at The Unitarian-Universalist Congregation at Shelter Rock Rd., Manhasset , NY on Sunday, November 5 at 4 PM.


        TERIAN-Peter G. Of Manhattan and East Hampton, passed away at home at the age of 65 on October 2, 2002, after a courageous battle with prostate cancer. He is survived by his beloved wife, Juliana Curran Terian, an architect, and by his beloved and cherished daughter, Olivia. Peter immigrated to the West Side of Manhattan from Paris, France, at the age of nine. Ten years later he cofounded Rallye Motors, one of the nation's most prestigious car dealerships, and served as its president to this day. He was a renowned collector of art, who frequently loaned significant pieces to museums worldwide, including the Metropolitan Museum of Art, where he served as a William Cullen Bryant fellow. Peter was also on the board of the Dakota building, where he resided for many years. He will be deeply missed by all who knew and loved him. Friends may call at Frank E. Campbell, 1076 Madison Ave. at 81st St., Thursday, October 3, 5-9 PM. Service Friday, October 4, at 11 AM, at the City Church, 64th St. off Central Park West. Interment private. Donations in his memory may be made to the New York Foundling Hospital, a children's charity which was Peter's passion

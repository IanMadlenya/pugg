
        VERSFELT-William H. Jr. Of Greenwich, CT, died August 12, 2002, after a long illness. Mr. Versfelt was born in Brooklyn, NY, on November 11, 1925, the son of William H. Versfelt and Margaret Drown. He was educated in the Garden City, NY, public schools and received his B.S. degree from the Wharton School of the University of Pennsylvania in 1947. He served in the U.S. Army 99th Infantry Division during World War II and lost his right arm at the Battle of the Bulge in December, 1944. On August 29, 1946, he married Ruth Eileene Gerland, also of Garden City. Mr. Versfelt was associated with the St. Regis Paper Co. for 38 years, rising to the position of Vice President and General Manager of the Multi-wall Bag Division. He also served as President of the Paper Shipping Sack Manufacturing Association (PSSMA). As a resident of Irvington-on-Hudson, Mr. Versfelt was elected Trustee of the Village in 1960 and 1962. He was a member of the Ardsley Country Club, Ardsleyon-Hudson, for more than 30 years, as well as the Ardsley Curling Club. He taught and directed the Sunday School and served as an elder at the Irvington Presbyterian Church, where he was a member since 1956. He is survived by his wife of 56 years, Ruth G. Versfelt; by two daughters, Gail V. Hay of Arlington, TX, and Claudia H. Versfelt of Indianapolis, IN, as well as one son, David S. Versfelt of Greenwich, CT, plus seven grandchildren, a stepmother, Evelyn Lewis of Sun City, AZ and a stepsister, Suzanne Graven of San Diego, CA. Funeral services are private. In lieu of flowers, donations may be made to the Irvington Presbyterian Church, 25 N. Broadway, Irvington-on-Hudson, NY 10533.

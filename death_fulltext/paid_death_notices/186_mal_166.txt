
        GORDON-Alvin J., M.D. It is with profound sorrow that The Mount Sinai Alumni mourns the passing of our friend and colleague, Dr. Alvin Gordon, a highly esteemed physician whose remarkable career at Mount Sinai covered almost half a century. A pioneer in cardiac catheterization, Dr. Gordon headed the Cardiac Catheterization Team at Mount Sinai from 1946-1970. Devoted to medicine, Mount Sinai and its Alumni, Dr. Gordon served as President of the Association in 1966 and, in 1973. The Mount Sinai Alumni presented its most coveted award, the Jacobi Medallion, to Dr. Gordon in acknowledgment of his profound professional achievements and his extraordinary service to the Medical Center and The Alumni. He will be remembered for his exceptional skills, special warmth, and outstanding commitment to Mount Sinai. We extend our heartfelt condolences to his entire family. Jeffrey S. Freed, M.D., Pres. William G. Stein, M.D., VicePres. Maria L. Padilla, M.D., Secy George Raptis, M.D., Treas.
        GORDON-Alvin J., M.D. The Association of the Attending Staff of the Mount Sinai Hospital mourns the passing of a beloved colleague, teacher and friend. Dr. Gordon came to Mount Sinai as an intern in 1938 and received board certification in Internal Medicine and Cardiovascular Diseases. From 1943-1946, Dr. Gordon served as a lieutenant in the United States Navy. Upon his return to Mount Sinai, he became one of the early pioneers in Cardiac Catheterization. In addition to extraordinary contributions in private pratice and teaching, he coauthored a book, ''Hemodynamics of Aortic and Mitral Valve Diseases.'' He was a dedicated and outstanding member of the attending staff and was awarded the Jacobi Medallion in 1973. We extend our sincere condolences to his entire family. Anthony Squire, M.D., Pres. Vicki Lopachin, M.D., Secy Amy Weinstein, Administrator

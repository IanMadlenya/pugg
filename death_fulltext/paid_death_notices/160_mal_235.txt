
        SUITER-James Evans Butler. Born September 8, 1914, at Williamsport, PA, to Albert Ezra and Hannah Rickard Suiter. Died May 7, 2000, at Monroe County, Georgia Hospital after a brief illness. He received his Bachelor of Laws Degree from John Marshall College of the Rutgers University in 1938. He received his Master of Arts degree from Rutgers in 1939. He received his Ph.D. from New York University in 1959. He was appointed a Counselor at Law by the State of New Jersey in February of '43 and served in several positions as a State appointed judge and magistrate. He entered the teaching field in 1960 and taught at Western Illinois University at Macomb, Illinois, and retired as a professor of English literature from Kean University in Union, NJ. He was a member of the Modern Language Association, English Speaking Union, Beaux Arts Alliance, Navy League, World Ship Society. He is survived by his daughter, Margarita Defore of Smarr, GA, and sons, A. Nicholas Suiter of Avis, PA, and Galen Suiter, Sr., of Coosada, AL, four grandchildren and six great-grandchildren. Burial will be Monday in Lock Haven, PA. In lieu of flowers contributions may be made to the Rutgers Alumni Association, 732932-8808.


        TURNBULL--David, died peacefully at home in Cambridge, MA on April 28, 2007, at 92 years. Loving husband for 52 years of Carol, who died in 1999; father of Lowell, (Randi Greenwald) Murray, and Joyce McDonald (Michael); grandfather of Ian and Derek Turnbull and John and Scott McDonald. Gordon McKay Professor of Applied Physics Emeritus, Harvard University; thesis advisor to more than 40 Ph.D. students. Recipient of the Japan Prize, Franklin Medal, TMS Bruce Chalmers Award, ASM Sauveur Memorial Award, MRS von Hippel Award, Acta Metallurgica Gold Medal, APS International Prize for New Materials, AIME Hume-Rothery Award, honorary Sc.D.s from Case Western Reserve University, Universite Claude Bernard (Lyon, France) and Monmouth College, and other honors for his contributions to materials science. Coeditor, Solid State Physics series, 1954-1994. Member, National Academy of Sciences; Fellow, American Academy of Arts and Sciences. David Turnbull Lectureship, endowed by his friends, has been awarded annually by MRS since 1992. Memorial service at noon, September 17, Appleton Chapel, Harvard Memorial Church. Donations in his memory may be made to Materials Research Society, 506 Keystone Dr., Warrendale, PA 15086 Attn: John Balance f/b/o David Turnbull Memorial Fund.

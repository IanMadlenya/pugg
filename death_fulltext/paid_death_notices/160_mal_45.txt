
        SHERVINGTON-Walter W., MD. The National Medical Association lost its President, Walter W. Shervington, MD to cancer on Saturday, April 15, 2000. Dr. Shervington, a psychiatrist and hospital administrator in New Orleans, La., was a member of the NMA for over 30 years. He dedicated his career to serving the health care needs of African Americans, and in later years, became an influential national leader in mental health, particularly the mental health aspect of HIV/AIDS. Dr. Shervington is survived by his wife of 19 years Denese McFarlane Shervington, MD, their two daughters Shanga and Iman and two sisters Anne Shervington Davis and Carol Shervington Wright.

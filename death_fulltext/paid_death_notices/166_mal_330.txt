
        JONES-James S. Architect and sportsman, died at the age of 86 near his home in Gladstone, New Jersey. Jimmy Jones had his own architectural firm in Morristown, New Jersey for over forty years.He was a graduate of Yale University and the Yale School of Architecture. And he was a member of the American Institute of Architects. Jimmy Jones was involved in the hunting of hounds in New Jersey all his life. As a young man, he hunted with the Essex Fox Hounds, the Spring Valley Hounds and the Vernon Somerset Beagles. In 1950, he and a friend started the Tewksbury FootBassets which he then hunted for 35 years. He authored many books and articles on hounds and hunting and judged at numerous hound shows. Jimmy Jones was considered by many to be ''the dean of American foot hound hunters.'' Mr. Jones served in the Navy during World War II. Mr. Jones leaves a son, F. Macy Jones of Wenham, MA, and a daughter, Elizabeth J. Dennis of Santa Fe, NM. And he leaves four grandchildren, Patrick S. Jones, Jr. of Bernardsville, New Jersey, Mrs. Walker H. Jones and Mrs. Benjamin B. Haskell, both of Boston, MA and E. Briggs Jones of Wenham, MA and three great grandchildren. A memorial service will be held on Friday, December 8th at 11 A.M. in the St. Luke's Episcopal Church in Gladstone, New Jersey. In lieu of flowers, donations may be made to the Tewksbury Foot Bassets, P.O. Box 312, Far Hills, NJ 07931


        DALTON--Madeline Eileen, on April 16, educator and Olympic fencing athlete, died at the Franciscan Oaks Health Center in Denville, NJ. She was 93. Resident of Manhattan and Rainbow Lakes, NJ. Retired in 1979 as Director of Special Education Department City of NY. Graduate of Hunter College, she lectured internationally on Special Education. She was U.S. National Women's Fencing Champion in 1944 and named to the 1944 London Olympics, cancelled due to the war. Survived by nieces and nephews, Edith Dalton Marinucci, MD, Joan Dalton McDevitt, NY, Michael Dalton, WA; Catherine Sataniello NJ, Karen Lee Dalton, MN, Michael Crumrine, NJ, Kevin P. Crumrine, NJ and 16 greatgreat-nieces and nephews. Predeceased by her sister Catherine Dalton and brothers Col. Harry Dalton (USAF Ret), Leo A. Dalton and nephew, Leo Dalton. Visiting will be at St. Catherine's Church, Mt. Lakes, NJ on Saturday April 21 from 8:45 to 9:15 A.M. followed by a Mass of Christian Burial at 9:30 A.M. Interment at Gate of Heaven Cemetery, Hawthorne, NY. Services by Norman Dean Funeral Home. www.normandean.com. Memorial gifts may be made to The Hunter College Foundation, Inc., 695 Park Ave., NY, NY 10021 for the ''Madeline E. Dalton Special Education Scholarship'' for Hunter College graduate students.

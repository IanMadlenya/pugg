
        SCHWARTZ-Arnold M. Retired First Sergeant. Age 65, a retired Battalion Administrator, Department of the Army, died April 2, at his home in Conway, SC, due to an illness acquired during his participation in the Desert Storm Campaign. Mr. Schwartz was born in Brooklyn, NY, the son of the late Phillip Schwartz and the late Dorothy S. Schwartz. His careers included lengthy service in the New York City criminal justice system, as well as 30 years service in the United States military. Mr. Schwartz was a veteran of both the Korean War and the Persian Gulf War. His assignments also included Operation ''Wintex 83'' in the Federal Republic of Germany. He was a member of the Shomrim Society, New York Veteran Police Association, Veterans of Foreign Wars and the Disabled American Veterans. His awards and commendations include the Legion of Merit, the Bronze Star, the Meritorious Service Medal with oak leaf cluster, the Army Commendation Medal with four oak leaf clusters, the army Achievement Medal with three oak leaf clusters, the Army Reserve Component Overseas Training Ribbon, the Armed Forces Reserve Medal, the Army Service Ribbon, the Defense Service Medal with Bronze Service Star, the Army Component Achievement Medal with Hourglass, the Southwest Asia Service Medal with three bronze service stars, the Saudi Arabia Kuwait Liberation Medal and the Kuwait Liberation Medal. Mr. Schwartz is survived by his wife Janice M. Schwartz, of Conway, a son David P. Schwartz and wife Lisa DeAnne Schwartz, of Hamilton, GA, a daughter Janice E. Schwartz, of Myrtle Beach, two granddaughters and three sisters. Interment will be at Arlington National Cemetery, VA. The family requests that expressions of sympathy be made in Mr. Schwartz's memory to either St. Jude Children's Research Hospital, PO Box 50, Memphis, TN 38101; or Myrtle Beach Humane Society.

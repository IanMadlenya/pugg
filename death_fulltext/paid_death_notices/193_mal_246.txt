
        DILLON-Irwin T. Born 1906 in Toronto, Canada, passed away surrounded by his family January 13, 2003 at the age of 96. A graduate of University of Toronto and New York University. He became a leading tenor with New York City Opera in the '40's under Lazlo Halasz after years of touring Canada and U.S. with leading Oratorio societies and numerous radio performances. He sang with Columbia Concerts 1944 to 1945. At NYCO he sang Don Jose in Carmen, Turiddu in Cavalleria Rusticana and Pinkerton to Camilla Williams in Madama Butterfly. His many concert conductors included A. Dorati, E. Leinsdorf, H. Von Karajan, L. Stokowski, A. Toscanini and A. Rodzinski. In 1947 he sang three historic performances of Elektra with Marjorie Lawrence-her first performance standing after a six-year battle with polio. On April 3, 1948, he sang Beethoven's Symphony No. 9 with the NBC Symphony televised. His final performances of Wozzeck were in October 1952 at NYCO. He joined Irving Trust Co. in '52 and retired in '69. Lived in Coral Springs, FL, and last seven years in CA. Survived by daughters Patricia of Garden City, NY and Mary Jane of Dana Point, CA, three grandchildren and two great grandsons. Interment at Our Lady Queen of Heaven, Ft. Lauderdale, FL, February 1, 2003.

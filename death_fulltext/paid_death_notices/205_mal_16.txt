
        PERSONS--John Pat. Of Norfolk, CT and Vero Beach, FL, formerly of Scarsdale, NY, died on January 29, 2004 from complications of Parkinson's Disease. He was born on October 21, 1926 in Gastonia, NC and grew up in Atlanta, GA. He received his BA from the University of North Carolina Chapel Hill in 1947, attending as part of the Navy V-12 program. He received a JD from the University of Georgia Law School in 1950 and an LLM from Harvard Law School in 1951. His legal practice was begun in the Chief Counsel's office of the Internal Revenue Service in Washington, DC. In 1955 he joined the New York City law firm of Patterson, Belknap & Webb (now Patterson, Belknap, Webb & Tyler). He was made a partner in 1961, retiring in 1996 and practiced general corporate law with a specialty in tax. He served as a Trustee of the Shelburne Museum in Vermont from 1972-1996. In Scarsdale, NY he was President of the Scarsdale Friends of the Parks and a Board member of the Fox Meadow Tennis Club. Also he was a long-time member of the Harvard Club of New York City. He is survived by his wife, Florence Douglas Persons, his daughter, Sarah Persons, his son, Alexander Persons and two grandchildren. In lieu of flowers, contributions in his name may be sent to the Shelburne Museum, Shelburne, VT 05482. A memorial service is being planned for the Spring in Scarsdale, NY.

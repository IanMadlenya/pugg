
        MORTON--Jack, 94, of Vero Beach, Florida, died on June 28, 2004, at his home at John's Island after a long, full life. He was born June 6, 1910, in Newport, NC, and moved to Washington, DC, in 1931, where he lived before making Vero Beach his permanent home. Mr. Morton graduated from George Washington University in 1936 with a Bachelor's degree in psychology. In 1939, he founded what is now Jack Morton Worldwide, a leading experiential marketing and global brand communication agency. Survivors are his wife of 67 years, Anne; daughters Mary Lou Seilheimer of Orange, Virginia, and Diane Fentress of Washington, D.C.; son, William Morton of Greenwich, Connecticut; eight grandchildren; and four great-grandchildren. In lieu of flowers, memorial contributions may be made to the Anne Morton Children's Theater Endowment, 3280 Riverside Park Drive, Vero Beach, Fl. 32963. A memorial service will be at 10 AM on Wednesday, June 30, at the First Presbyterian Church at Vero Beach, Florida.
        MORTON--Jack. The 600 employees of Jack Morton Worldwide express our gratitude for the life and legacy of our company's founder, Jack Morton. We express our heartfelt best wishes to the Morton family, and we commit ourselves to ensuring that the values Jack Morton exemplified in our company-passion, agility and respect-continue to live on.

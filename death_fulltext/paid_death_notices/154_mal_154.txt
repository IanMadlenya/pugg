
        CONRON-Michael A. Aviator and Aviation Executive of Greenville, Delaware, Tolouse, France and Lyme, Connecticut, October 29, 1999. AB, Political Science, Brown University; MA, American History, University of Massachusetts, Amherst; MThil, American History, Columbia University. After Brown, he served as a US Naval Aviator and there after was on the faculty of The Institute of Aviation of the University of Illinois, Champaign. He was with Pan American World Airways for 22 years in various capacities as a pilot and executive in New York, Africa and Europe. In 1990, he joined Airbus Industrie where he was a member of the Flight Test Department, and most recently, Director of Flight Operations Development. He is survived by his wife, Shana Rimel Conron, and his daughter, Rachel Meyer Conron. A memorial service will be held at 4PM, Friday, December 10 at The Wings Club, 52 Vanderbilt Avenue, NY, NY 10017. Contributions may be made to the Michael A. Conron Fund at Brown University, established to further the understanding in the U.S. of the history of Southeast Asia and the U.S. involvement in Vietnam.

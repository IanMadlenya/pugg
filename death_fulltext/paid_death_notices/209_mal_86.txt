
        SHRIVER--Denton A., 80, died June 6, 2004, at Paoli Memorial Hospital from a sudden illness. Born on Staten Island, NY, son of Anna Fowler Shriver of Haverstraw, NY and Alfred Shriver, a founding partner of Morgan Stanley, Denton graduated from Yale University and Harvard Law School. From 1953 until 1967 Denton served as General Counsel and Executive Vice President of Vanadium Corporation of America. In 1968, Denton moved to the Philadelphia area to take a position as Vice President and Secretary of Safeguard Scientific where he worked until his retirement in 1982. He brought his family together at his first love, Merriewold, his summer home inthe Catskills. He was also an accomplished gardener, enjoyed writing short stories and visiting England. He was the devoted husband of Joan Grant Shriver, deceased, and is survived by his siblings: Nancy Shriver Hahn and Donald Shriver, children: Gifford Shriver, Anne Shriver, Susan Jankus, Kimberly Cameron, Alexander Shriver, and eight grandchildren. There will be a service at Mauger/Givnish Funeral Home, 24 Monument Ave., Malvern, PA on Wednesday, June 9, 2004 at 3 p.m. Memorial donations may be made to the Alzheimer's Association.

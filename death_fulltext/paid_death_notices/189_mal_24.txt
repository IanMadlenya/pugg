
        SNOWDON-Edward Woodruff. Age 91, died September 27, 2002 at his home in Manalapan, FL. with his family in attendance. Mr. Snowdon graduated from Western High School in Washington, D.C. in 1929 and from Wesleyan University in 1933. He was a Captain in the U.S. Army Signal Corps during World War II and participated in the D-Day invasion and the Battle of the Bulge. He was awarded the Bronze Star and two Croix de Guerre from France for his service in liberation. Mr. Snowdon worked for the Charles L. Wagner Opera Company and Young and Rubicam in New York City until his retirement in 1972, when he and his late wife Lee Hill Snowdon moved to Manalapan, FL. from their longtime residence in Plainfield, NJ. He is survived by two sons, Richard of Washington, D.C. and Ted of New York City; a daughter Margot of Wilson, WY; five grandchildren and one great grandson. A memorial service will be held at 10 AM on Friday, November 1, 2002 at Bethesda by the Sea Church in Palm Beach. In lieu of flowers, the family requests contributions to the Snowdon Fund at the Community Foundation for Palm Beach and Martin Counties, 700 South Dixie Hwy, West Palm Beach, FL. 33401. Babione - Stephenson Funeral Home, Lantana, FL.


        CROUCH-Archie R., devoted and loving husband, beloved father, writer and China scholar, died in Englewood, Colo., at the age of 90, on September 10. Born in Veblen, S.D., he was educated at Jamestown College, Princeton Theological Seminary, Cornell University, and received his Ph.D. from the University of Southern California. He was ordained by the Presbyterian Church and served as a missionary in China before and during World War II. The Rev. Dr. Crouch retired from COEMAR of the United Presbyterian Church, USA, as Secretary of Communications in 1971. He then began working with his wife on what would become a prize-winning scholarly work, ''Christianity in China: a Scholar's Guide to Resources in the Libraries and Archives of the United States,'' completed and published in 1989. Dr. Crouch is survived by his wife of 63 years, the lovely Ellen Elizabeth, four children, two half-sisters, one brother, and five grandchildren. Memorial contributions to honor the love and devotion of Archie's life may be made to the Presbyterian Church (USA), East Asia Pacific Office, 100 Witherspoon St., Louisville, KY 40202; or The Ricci Institute, University of San Francisco, 2130 Fulton St., San Francisco, CA 94117.


        GILBRIDE--John T., 90, the former Chairman and Chief Executive Officer of Todd Shipyards Corporation, a longtime resident of Greenwich, CT, and recently of Stamford, passed away at Stamford Hospital on March 17, 2007. Mr. Gilbride was elected as Director of Todd in 1951, President of the Corporation in 1958 and Chairman and Chief Executive Officer on June 30, 1975, which office he held until his retirement in 1986. A staunch advocate of American sea power and a leading spokesman on behalf of the reconstruction of naval forces and the American Merchant Marine. Mr. Gilbride was appointed by President Nixon, in January of 1971, to the Commission of American Shipbuilding, serving until December 1973. He was a Trustee of the Atlantic Mutual Insurance Company, Emigrant Savings Bank, Webb Institute of Naval Architecture, the United States Merchant Marine Academy, and the United States Research Council. He was a member of the Board of Managers of the American Bureau of Shipping, a member of the Executive Committee, and American Committee of Llyod's Register of Shipping, and a member of the Maritime Association of the Port of New York, and held numerous positions in organizations related to the maritime industry. He is survived by his beloved wife of 66 years, Rosemary Shelare Gilbride of Stamford, his son Frank J. and his wife, Carol R. of Greenwich, his son John T. Jr. and his wife, Victoria of Media, Pennsylvania, his son Gary G. and his wife, Angela of Old Greenwich, his former daughter-in-law Bari Taylor of Greenwich, seven grandchildren and five, soon to be six, great-grandchildren.

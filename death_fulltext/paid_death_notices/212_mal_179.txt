
        RUTTENBERG--Derald H. 88, was born in Lafayette, IN, on February 17, 1916, and died on September 19, 2004, in New York City. He graduated from the University of Wisconsin in 1937, Yale Law School in 1940, and Harvard University Graduate School of Business in 1942. In 1943, he entered the armed forces and served in the European Theater of Operations in the U.S. Strategic Bombing Survey; he was discharged as a Lieutenant in the U.S. Air Force in 1945. Following his honorable discharge, he owned and ran various closely-held foundry and manufacturing companies in the midwest. He later moved to New York City and became Chairman of ITE-Circuit Breaker Co. in 1967. In 1969 he became President and Chief Executive Officer of Studebaker-Worthington, Inc. and Chairman and Chief Executive Officer in 1971, in which capacities he served until 1980. He then returned to private investing through Tinicum Incorporated, his family owned investment firm. He is survived by his wife Janet of 53 years and their four children John, Eric, Kathy and Hattie, as well as eight grandchildren. A memorial service will be held at a later date to be announced. In lieu of flowers donations may be sent to either Yale Law School or the Derald H. Ruttenberg Cancer Center at the Mount Sinai School of Medicine in New York City.

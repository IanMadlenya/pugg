
        EVE-Dr. H. Janet. Henrietta Janet Eve, a practicing physician in New York for over 40 years, died at her home in Sarasota, Florida on November 16, 1998. Dr. Eve was born June 6, 1923 in Hot Springs, Arkansas. She was a graduate of Fisk University and the Howard University College of Medicine. An internship at Harlem Hospital in NYC was followed by a residency at Freedmen's Hospital, Washington, D.C. Dr. Eve returned to New York and joined the medical staff at the NY Board of Education, later becoming the first African American woman to serve as Medical Director. She was also a partner with the Yorkville Medical Group of the H.I.P. of New York. Upon her retirement in 1992, she had been medical director of the East Harlem Branch of the Yorkville Medical Center for a number of years. Dr. Eve was a most compassionate and generous person, giving tirelessly to her patients and community. She served on the Executive Committee of the community effort of the Harlem YWCA, and was member of the New York County and State Medical Society and the Alpha Kappa Alpha Sorority. She is survived by her sister and brother-inlaw, Della E. Hale and Dr. Edward H. Hale; many devoted family members and loving friends. A memorial service was held in Sarasota on Nov. 20. Memorial gifts may be made to the Howard University College of Medicine, 520 W. St, N.W., Washington, D.C. 20059.

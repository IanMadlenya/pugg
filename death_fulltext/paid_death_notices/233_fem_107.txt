
        KETELS--Antoinette (nee Staaterman) 88, of Washington, D.C., formerly of Montvale, NJ and Berkeley Township, NJ, died Thursday, June 15th at The National Lutheran Home in Rockville, Maryland. Mrs. Ketels, the daughter of Frederick Staaterman and Frieda Schoene Staaterman, was born in New York City. She grew up there and in Demarest and Cresskill, NJ. Her husband of 50 years, Henry Conrad Ketels, died in 1986. She was also predeceased by her son, Peter Conrad Ketels, in 1958 and her brother, Henry Staaterman who died in 1990. Her identical twin sister, Marion Staaterman, died in childhood. While beginning her family, Mrs. Ketels worked with her husband in their delicatessen businesses in New York City and later in Northvale, NJ. In 1950, Mrs. Ketels and her family moved to Montvale, where she was an active member of the community for 31 years. She was one of the original organizers of the Montvale Little League Women's Auxiliary. A member of St. Paul's Episcopal Church, Montvale and the Order of the Eastern Star, she was also active in the Montvale PTA/PTO. Before retiring with her husband to Berkeley Township, NJ in 1981 she enjoyed working as an Aid in the Montvale School System. she moved to the Washington, D.C. area in 1996 and in 2002 took up residence at The National Lutheran Home in Rockville, Maryland. Mrs. Ketels is survived by her daughter, Marion Ketels Ellis of Seattle, Washington, and her two sons, John Paul Ketels of Washington, D.C. and James Edward Ketels of Ocean Township, NJ. She is additionally survived by five grandchildren and 11 great grandchildren. There will be visitation at the Robert Spearing Funeral Inc., 155 Kinderkamack Road, Park Ridge, NJ from 7:00 to 9:00 PM on Wednesday, June 21st. A funeral service 11:30 AM Thursday, June 22nd, 2006 at St. Paul's Episcopal Church in Montvale. Interment in George Washington Memorial Park, Paramus, NJ at 1:00 PM. In lieu of flowers memorial contributions may be made to The National Lutheran Home, 9701 Veirs Drive, Rockville, MD 20850. A memorial service will be held at The National Lutheran Home.

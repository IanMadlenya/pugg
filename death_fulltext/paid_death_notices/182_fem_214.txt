
        KRASNER-Miriam. Passed away on March 15, 2002 in Palo Alto, CA at age 77 of complications related to Parkinson's disease. Born in NYC to Sarah and David Shostak on January 27, 1925, she was a much beloved wife, mother, grandmother, educator and friend. Married to Leonard Krasner for almost 55 years, she is also survived by her children Wendy (Richard Levine) of Bethesda, MD; David (Marsha) of Rutland, VT; Charlie (Lisa) of Stockton, CA; and Stefanie (Maureen Farran) of Los Angeles; grandchildren Robin, Michelle, Zachary, Henry and Harry; siblings Selma Rosenbaum (Lawrence, NY), Dorothy Moss (Las Vegas, NV) & Jerry Shostak (Staten Island); & scores of loving friends. Miriam graduated from NY City College and received a Master's in Education from Columbia. She taught elementary school at the Three Village School District in Stony Brook from the mid-60s to the mid-80s. She collaborated with her husband, Leonard Krasner, psychology professor emeritus, SUNY Stony Brook, on numerous articles, speeches and textbooks. She was active in Hadassah, the Sherlock Holmes Society, and several other longstanding book clubs. Gatherings to celebrate her life will be held on the West Coast and the East Coast at a future date. In lieu of flowers, her family requests donations be sent to Spiritual Care Services, Stanford University Hospital, 300 Pasteur Drive, Stanford, CA 94305, Attn: Jewish Chaplaincy, or the charity of your choice.

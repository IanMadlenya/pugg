
        BADGER-Mary Elizabeth ''Liz'' passed away unexpectedly on October 21, 2000, at the age of 68. Liz was born in New York City, the daughter of James and Alicia Owens Boeri. Liz was a graduate of Middlebury College and received her Doctorate in Educational Psychology at the University of London. After the death of her husband Sherwin in 1968, Liz and her three young sons moved to England. Liz worked as an Associate Professor at Central London Polytechnic and as a Senior Research officer for the National Foundation for Educational Research in England and Wales. When she returned to the United States in 1982, Liz worked first for the Massachusetts Department of Education as Assessment Director, and then for the College Board as Director of Instructional Assessment. Liz retired two years ago and spent time traveling, visiting with her family, enthusiastically improving her photography skills, and volunteering for Doctors Without Borders. Liz is survived by her sons, Campbell and Matthew Badger, her daughters-in-law Joanna Badger, Juliet HolmesSmith and Madonna Badger, her sister Alicia McGinely, and her grandchildren, Sam, Alice, Thomas and Mary Elizabeth. Predeceasing Liz was her oldest son Mark Badger. In lieu of flowers, donations may be made to the Mary Elizabeth Badger Fund, Doctors without Borders P.O. Box 2247, New York, NY 10116-2247.

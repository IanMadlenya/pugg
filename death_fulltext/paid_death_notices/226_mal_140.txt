
        BLOCK--Leonard N. (December 21, 1911-November 9, 2005). Survived by Adele Block, wife of 69 years, children, Peggy Danziger and Thomas Block; grandchildren, Michael and Katie Danziger and Jonathan and Alison Block; great-grandchildren, James, Robby, Charlie and Teddy Danziger and Jake and Jilly Horowitz. Services Sunday, November 13, 11AM at Temple Emanu-El, 5th Avenue at 65th Street, NYC.
        $(6$)BLOCK--Leonard. The Board and staff of Lincoln Center Theater are deeply saddened by the death of Leonard Block, who passed away peacefully on November 9. Beloved husband of founding Board member Adele Block, Leonard was a staunch and generous advocate of the theater, passionate in his determination to see the theater reestablished, artistically distinguished and organizationally sound. We extend our condolences and love to Peggy, Tom and the entire Block family. John B. Beinecke, Chairman John Chalsty, President Andre Bishop Artistic Director Bernard Gersten Executive Producer
        $(6$)BLOCK--Leonard. The Board of Directors and staff of the New York Service for the Handicap - Camp Oakhurst are deeply saddened at the death of Leonard Block. His extraordinary commitment to philanthropy's broadest reach, along with his wife Adele, found expression in six decades of support for the physically disabled children and adults who benefit from the programs at Camp Oakhurst. To his family go our heartfelt condolences. Marilyn Friedman, President Marvin A. Raps, Executive Director
        $(6$)BLOCK--Leonard. The Board and staff of the Associated Camps, Inc., mourn the loss of the founding benefactor of the Block and Hexter Vacation Center, Leonard Block. His generosity has provided much needed recreational and community services for thousands of elderly. Our thoughts and prayers are with his family at this time. Michael Loeb, President Donald Ginsberg, Vice President Laurie Epstein-Graves, Executive Director


        FRANK--Elizabeth Pope. 91, longtime women's magazine editor, died January 30, 2006 after a lengthy illness at Meadow Lakes in Hightstown, NJ. The daughter of the late Melville and Belle Oberfelder Welt, she was born December 9, 1914 in Detroit. Mrs. Frank earned her B.A. at Vassar College in 1936. She edited the school paper and was a well-known liberal activist. Her classmates named her ''Most Likely to Start a Revolution''. Her career began at Look Magazine in Des Moines, IA in 1936. In 1938, she married Vernon Pope, the magazine's founding editor. After the couple moved to New York, she contributed to McCall's, the Ladies Home Journal, Redbook, Reader's Digest and Good Housekeeping. Divorced from Mr. Pope in 1961, she married Morton Frank, the Publisher, President and CEO of the Sunday supplement Family Weekly, in 1963. They were married until his death in 1989. Mr. Pope died in 1995. Known as ''Betty'', Mrs. Frank served as features editor and articles editor of Good Housekeeping. After retiring in 1977, she served as an editorial consultant to Family Circle magazine. Mrs. Frank won numerous journalism awards, including the National Magazine Award for feature writing and the Penny-Missouri Magazine Award. Survivors include her daughter, Barbara Pope, her son John Pope. Also surviving: three stepchildren: Allan Dodds Frank, Michael Frank and Marilyn Dodds Frank; granddaughters Lindsey Jane Pope, Cara Hall and two step-grandchildren. Mrs. Frank's other child, Anne Elizabeth Pope, died of cancer in 1994. A Memorial service will be held March 10, 2006 at 12:15pm at the Cosmopolitan Club in NY. In her memory, the family requests contributions to Planned Parenthood.

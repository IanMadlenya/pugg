
        WHYTE-William H., Jr. With a critical eye and a lover's heart, he figured out the subtle alchemy of places and people. With evangelical zeal leavened with wit, he persuaded city fathers to redress destructive policies and redo depressing places. His books, his mesmerizing little movies, and his long days on the lecture circuit have done their work. All over the country, better places are being built because people have learned from Holly to take the time to look. The Municipal Art Society of New York


        WICKS-Lucy A. Born March 20, 1951; died September 9, 2000, from breast cancer. She was a skilled clinical psychologist and enthusiastic education activist, as well as an artist, spiritual seeker, devoted wife and mother and loving friend. Memorial donations may be made to the Friends of the Manhattan School for Children, 154 West 93rd St., New York, NY 10025. A memorial service is planned for Saturday, September 16, at 8 P.M., at Paul's Chapel on Columbus University Campus, 116th Street and Broadway.
        WICKS-Lucy. Broadway Democrats celebrates the life and mourns the passing of Lucy Wicks, activist, artist, psychologist, educator. The greatest tributes to Lucy Wicks are the kids of the Manhattan School for Children, which she helped found. We extend our deepest sympathies to her husband, Dan Arshack and their children, Adam and Eliza. Luis Roman, President Curtis Arluck Paula Diamond, District Leaders Ed Sullivan, Assemblyman
        WICKS-Lucy A., Ph.D. As Director of the HIV Mental Health Clinic at Columbia-Presbyterian, she was a compassionate therapist, superb teacher, respected mentor and true friend. A visionary, a pioneer and a powerhouse, she leaves the Clinic as a legacy to her devotion to all she did.
        WICKS-Lucy. The players and families of The Dynamite of the Manhattan Soccer Club are saddened by the passing of one of our devoted soccer moms. Her strength and spirit will always remain with us. Frank Lowe

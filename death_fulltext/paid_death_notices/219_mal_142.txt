
        OSTROW--Barnet. Formerly of Roslyn, L.I. Passed away April 12, 2005 after a long illness. Devoted husband of Annette Glassman Ostrow, loving father of son Ronald and daughter-in-law Beth and son Bono Yvertes and daughterin-law Nancy. He will be deeply missed by his cherished grandchildren Alonna Ostad and husband Michael, Sharone Gilbert and husband Richard, Jonathan Ostrow and Juliette Yvertes and great grandchildren Hannah, Alexandra, Ari and Benjamin. Barnet Ostrow was a creative and innovative chemical engineer who invented and developed many electroplating processes used in the electronics, industrial and decorative industries. He co-founded LeaRonal, Inc. which was started in a garage in Long Island City in 1953 and grew to become a major global supplier of specialty chemicals. He served as President until 1983 and as Chairman of the Board of Directors until 1995. The company, listed on the New York Stock Exchange was sold to Rohm & Haas in 1999. A kind and gentle man, Barnet was philanthropic to numerous Jewish organizations and was honored by the Solomon Schechter Day School of Nassau County before the onset of his illness.

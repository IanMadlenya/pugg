
        BROWN-John Terry, Jr. Of New York City died May 30th. at home of cancer. He was the son of Mr. & Mrs. John Terry Brown of Racine, Wisconsin. He was a graduate of The Choate School and Phoenix Jr. College and received a BA from NYU. He attended the Parsons School of Design and established an extraordinary reputation for detail and color. Mr. Brown was an interior decorator and designer with clients throughout the United States. In the 1960's, he coproduced plays, among them the off Broadway musical ''All In Love'' and Sean O'Casey's ''Red Roses for Me.'' He was a member of the Society of Colonial Wars in the State of New York. He is survived by his sisters Barbara Brown Lee and Elizabeth Brown Greeley of Milwaukee. A Memorial Service will be held Thursday, June 7th at 3pm at St. Bartholomew's Church on Park Ave betw 50th and 51st Streets. In lieu of flowers the family has requested contributions be made to the Arthur B. Ashe Foundation, Broadway Cares Equity Fights Aids at 165 West 46 St., Suite 1300, NY, NY 10036, and the Milwaukee Art Museum, 750 North Lincoln Memorial Drive, Milwaukee, WI 53202. He will be missed by a legion of devoted friends.

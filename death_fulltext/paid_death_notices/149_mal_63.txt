
        SOBEL-Dr. Robert. Hofstra University is profoundly saddened by the death of Dr. Robert Sobel, Professor Emeritus of Business History. Dr. Sobel taught at Hofstra for forty years holding the Lawrence Stessin Distinguished Professorship in Business History. Upon his retirement in 1998, he accepted the position of Senior Fellow at the Milken Institute in Santa Monica, California. Dr. Sobel, author of more than fifty books covering the histories of many corporations, was a graduate of C.C.N.Y. and received his M.A. and Ph.D. from New York University. A remarkable member of the Hofstra community, Dr. Sobel will be remembered for his dedication to the University and students he taught. He is survived by his wife of forty years, Carole; a daughter, Barbara; a son, David; a daughterin-law, Linda; two granddaughters, Lauren and Elizabeth; his mother, Blanche; and brother, Alan. A memorial service will be held at Hofstra University on Monday, September 13, 1999. Call 516-463-6818 for details and directions. The family has established the Robert Sobel Memorial Scholarship Fund. For information and donations, contact the Office of Development, 101 Hofstra University, Hempstead, N.Y. 11549. In tribute to the memory of Professor Sobel, all Hofstra University flags have been lowered to half staff. James M. Shuart, President Helene Fortunoff, Chairperson, Board of Trustees Hofstra University


        STAHL-Pearl, of Passaic, NJ, died on Sunday, April 14, 2002. A woman of valor, whose remarkable life we celebrate for its warmth, generosity and graciousness. For fifty years, her indefatigable lifetime commitment inspired people to work for the betterment of the State of Israel and the Jewish people. Beloved wife of the late A. Duey Stahl; cherished mother of Sharon Barash and Stanlee Stahl. Loving grandmother of Tracy Barash, Jeffrey Barash and David Ackerman. Mother-in-law of Brent Barash and George Ackerman and beloved sister of Bert Goldblum. She was National Director of the American Red Magen David for Israel, where she worked for more than 28 years. Upon her retirement at age 80, she volunteered at The Jewish Foundation for the Righteous. She was a member of Congregation Tifereth Israel in Passaic, NJ, for 64 years. Even as we mourn with great saddness her passing and remember her with great admiration and affection, we recognize and honor the real difference that she made. Donations can be made to The Jewish Foundation for the Righteous, 305 7th Ave., New York, NY 10001, or to ARMDI, 888 7th Ave., NY, NY 10106. Services will be at Jewish Memorial Chapel, 841 Allwood Rd., Clifton, NJ, on Tuesday, April 16th, at 10 AM. Interment will follow at Passaic Junction Cemetery in Saddle Brook, NJ.
        STAHL-Pearl. The National Board of Directors and Membership of ARMDI-American Red Magen David for Israel records with profound sorrow the passing of Pearl Stahl. She was a dedicated and devoted National Director of ARMDI for more than 30 years. We extend our heartfelt sympathy to all the members of her bereaved family. May the memory of her life be a blessing. Robert L. Sadoff, M.D. National Chairman Mark D. Lebow, Esq. National President Benjamin Saxe National Executive Vice President

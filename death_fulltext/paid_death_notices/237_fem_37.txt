
        WILLIAMS--Cheryl A; Ph.D. 51, passed away on September 30, 2006 in Summit, N.J. following a long illness. Dr. Williams received her BS in business from Fairleigh Dickinson University. After a successful marketing management career with AT&T, she entered New York University where she received her Master's in psychology. She took her graduate work at Albert Einstein College of Medicine of Yeshiva University and received her Doctorate in clinical psychology. Dr. Williams was an extern at the Bernard L. Pacella, MD Parent Child Center and began her career in psychology at the New York Psychoanalytic Institute. Dr. Williams specialized in working with children and adolescents. In 2001, she was the first recipient of the Erma Brenner Prize. Dr. Williams taught various psychology courses at John Jay College of Criminal Justice. She was preceded in death by her father, David C. Williams and is survived by her mother, Irene C. Williams of Lebanon, NH and a brother and sisterin-law, D. Craig Williams and Bernadette Williams of Philomath, OR.

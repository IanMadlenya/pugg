
        SZARKOWSKI--Jill Anson, architect and preservationist, died in her sleep in her New York apartment on the night of December 29th. Mrs. Szarkowski was born in New York in 1933 and educated at the Rose Haven School, The Brearley School, Abbot Academy, Radcliffe College, and Harvard University's Graduate School of Design. After receiving her architectural degree she worked for the firm Warner, Burns, Toan and Lunde, and later independently in association with Edmund Stevens, Jr. From 1986 to 1991 she worked for Christopher Gray at the Office for Metropolitan History. From 1994 to 2002 she was director of the tour programs of the Municipal Art Society and was co-author of that organization's A Juror's Guide to Lower Manhattan. Mrs. Szarkowski was the daughter of Nina Anson and the stepdaughter of the biochemist Mortimer L. Anson, of New York and London. Mrs. Szarkowski was a member of the Colony Club, the American Contract Bridge League, and the United States Croquet Association. She is survived byher husband, John Szarkowski, two daughters, Mrs. Adrian Holdship Jones and Mrs. Colton Noble Brown, and two grandchildren. A memorial service for her friends will be announced at a later date.

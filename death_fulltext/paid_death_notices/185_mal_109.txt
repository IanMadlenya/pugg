
        NICHOLS-Stuart E. Jr., M.D. 67, former President of the New York County District branch American Psychiatric Association, and founder of the Gay and Lesbian Psychiatrists of NY 1982, passed away on June 6, 2002, after a long bout with heart disease. He was much admired and respected for his courage, leadership and innovations in his struggle for humane treatment of gay and lesbian patients as well as for sufferers from substance abuse and HIV. He is survived by two sisters, Ann and Miriam of Richmond, VA, and by his devoted companion of 27 years, Pedro Garcia. He was loved by so many. In place of a funeral, a memorial service will be announced at a future date.
        NICHOLS-Stuart E. Jr., M.D. The entire Beth Israel Medical Center family joins with the leadership of its parent company, Continuum Health Partners, and with the physicians, nurses and staff of the hospital's Methadone Maintenance Treatment Program (MMTP) in acknowledging with profound sadness the passing of our dear friend and beloved former colleague, Stuart E. Nichols Jr., M.D., who served with great distinction for many years as chief of medical services for MMTP. Dr. Nichols forever will be remembered for his personal commitment to the needs of his colleagues, his fierce defense of those seeking addiction treatment, and the extraordinary care he provided to all who sought his help. He will be greatly missed. To his partner, Peter Garcia, his family and all his loved ones, we extend our most heartfelt condolences. Morton P.

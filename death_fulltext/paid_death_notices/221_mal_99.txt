
        LEWIS--John S., M.D. Otolaryngologist and Head and Neck Surgeon Invented Surgical Resection of Temporal Bone, an operation to treat a previously inoperable cancer, while at Memorial Sloan-Kettering Cancer Center. Born Medicine Hat, Alberta, Canada; Died Pelham, NY, June 4, 2005, age 85, where he lived for over 50 years. Director of Otolaryngology, Roosevelt Hospital (Roosevelt-St. Luke's Hospital after the merger) for 18 years. Associate Attending Surgeon on Head and Neck Service of Memorial SloanKettering Cancer Center for many years. Graduated University of Alberta, Edmonton Doctor of Medicine, 1943; Royal Canadian Army Medical Corps, Overseas E.T.O., 1943-1946; Graduate training, Surgery, University of Edinburgh, Scotland, 1946-1947; Pathology, University of Alberta, 1947; Residency, Otolaryngology, Hospital of the University of Pennsylvania, 1948-1950; Fellowship, Head and Neck Surgery, Memorial Sloan-Kettering Cancer Center, N.Y., 1950; Diplomate, American Board of Otolaryngology, 1951. Governor, American College of Surgeons 6 years. Past President of the following societies: American Society of Head and Neck Surgery, 1970-1, American Otolaryngological Society, 1983-4 New York Otological Society, New York Laryngological Society, Physician Scientific Society; Vice President, Eastern Section, Triological Society, 1979. Treasurer, American Society of Head and Neck Surgery, 1964-9; Past Secretary and Chairman, Section of Otolaryngology, N.Y. Academy of Medicine. Member, Society Surgical Oncology Head and Neck Surgeons, American Medical Association. University Appointments: Associate Clinical Professor of Otolaryngology, Columbia University School of Medicine. Author of 32 medical papers & 7 book chapters. Awards and Prizes: Newburn Medal for Surgery, University of Alberta; Newcomb Award, American Laryngological Society; Presidential Citation, Triological Society; Ogura Lecture, Triological Society; Distinguished Alumnus Award, University of Alberta; Guest of Honor, American Skull Base Society, American Society for Head and Neck Surgery, and American Laryngological Society. Survived by his wife of 55 years, Marion DeLong, two sons, Harry DeLong Lewis, J.D., of N.Y.C. & John Myers Lewis, M.D.C.M., F.R.C.S.(C), F.A.C.O.G. (wife Katharine), of Middlebury, CT, a daughter, Barbara Logan Buchman (husband John Andrew), of Bethesda, MD, Executive Director of International Lyme and Associated Diseases Society, and two grandchildren, John Lewis Buchman and Sarah Elizabeth Buchman.

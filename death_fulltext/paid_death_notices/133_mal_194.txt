
        MILLER-Theodore R. Pioneering cancer surgeon and educator, died peacefully at home at the age of 90 on February 4, 1998. He is survived by his wife of 64 years, Helen R. Miller; his sister, Ruth E. Miller and 3 brothers, Robert I., Ralph A. and Dr. William H. Born and raised in Philadelphia. Studied at Swarthmore, Yale and Stockholm U. MD from Temple Medical School in 1933. Diplomat of the American Board of Surgery. For more than 40 years he pioneered cancer surgery and was world renowned for developing new procedures. He began practice in Hackensack. Served in the 14th Evacuation Hospital in India during WWII, discharged as a Lt. Colonel. Dr. and Mrs. Miller spend a year in Stockholm and Copenhagen immediately after the war. He returned to NY to begin practice with the Pack Medical group and his life long association with memorial Sloan-Kettering where he became chief of the bone tumor service. Dr. Miller was clinical professor of surgery at Cornell Medical College and at Rutgers Medical College. He was past president of the American Radium Society and the James Ewing Society. Dr. Miller enjoyed music, playing the violin and the recorder; he was also an accomplished artist. He will be missed by all who knew him. A memorial service will be held at Monroe Village in Jamesburg, NJ on Saturday, February 21 at 11am. Donations may be made to the Presbyterian Homes of NJ.

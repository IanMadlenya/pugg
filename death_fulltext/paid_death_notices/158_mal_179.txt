
        RAINER-John D., M.D. On March 12, 2000, age 78. Beloved husband of 55 years of Barbara, revered brother of Lester, devoted father of Jeff and Peter, proud grandfather of Marta, Jeremy and Alexander, cherished father-in-law of Iwona, adored cousin of Naomi, honored brother-in-law of Judith and Jonathan, loving uncle of June, Garvin, Heather and Donna. Eminent psychiatrist, pioneer in medical genetics and treatment programs for the mentally ill deaf. 50 year professional association with Columbia University. Passionate admirer of Melville, Milton, Joyce, and Gilbert & Sullivan, scholar of philosophy and religion. His shining example will be an inspiration forever. Funeral service private, family will receive visitors at home, Thursday and Friday after 2 P.M., Saturday after noon. Contributions to the American Cancer Society or Calvary Hospital would be appreciated.
        RAINER-John, M.D. We are much saddened by the death of our esteemed colleague and friend, Dr. John Rainer, whose distinguished career at Psychiatric Institute span almost fifty years. From his psychiatric residency here through his many years as Director of Medical Genetics, where he made many contributions to studies of Schizophrenia and Manic-Depressive illness, to his pioneering studies in the treatment of the mentally ill deaf and his service as CoChairman of the Institutional Review Board, he was an inspiration to many. We will miss him. John D. Oldham, MD, Director NYS Psychaitric Institute
        RAINER-John, MD. The faculty, staff and candidates of the Columbia University Center for Psychoanalytic Training and Research mourn the passing of our esteemed colleague, John Rainer. As co-chair of the NYS Psychiatric Institute IRB and a Training and Supervising Analyst of the Psychoanalytic Center, he will be greatly missed. We extend our sympathy to his wife Barbara, his children, Jeff and Peter and his grandchildren. Robert A. Glick, M.D. Director


        NORTON--William F., Jr., died on January 6th at the Schervier Nursing Care Center in Riverdale, NY. He was 90. A former FBI agent, Mr. Norton was in charge of the investigation that culminated in the execution of atom bomb spies Julius and Ethel Rosenberg. He resigned from the FBI in 1953 to enter private practice in the admiralty field. Mr. Norton graduated from St. Francis Xavier High School, Fordham College and, in 1938, Fordham University School of Law. In 1941 he entered the FBI Academy at Quantico, VA. His work as a special agent included assignments in Latin America and, during World War II, counterespionage, counter sabotage and national defense investigations in New York. He was cited twice by FBI Director J. Edgar Hoover and also commended by the U.S. Attorney's office for his work in the Rosenberg case and the Soviet spy cases involving Harry Gold and his associates. Mr. Norton survived his wife of 56 years, Rita Elaine who died in 2001 and his sons William and Thomas. He is survived by his loving children Mary Elaine Morris, Margaret Clare, Jeanne Anne, Gerard, Michael and Joan Marie, his son-in-laws David M. Morris, Kenneth D. McGuinness and daughter-inlaws Jennifer Norton and Nancy Belley; adoring grandchildren Jennifer and Alexis Morris, Alexander, Kelly Anne, Patrick and Michel Kelly Neville Norton, as well as his former daughter-in-laws Carolyn Butler and Marie Khatami.

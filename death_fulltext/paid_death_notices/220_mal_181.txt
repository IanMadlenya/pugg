
        LIPPITT--Frederick, died on May 11, 2005 in Providence, RI at the age of 88. He was the son of United States Senator Henry F. Lippitt and Lucy Hayes Herron Lippitt. He is survived by his sister Mary Ann Lippitt. He graduated from St. Marks School, Yale University '39 and Yale Law School '46. He served in the Philippines and Italy in WWII and received a Bronze Star and a Purple Heart. During the Korean War he served two years in Germany. He retired as a Lt. Colonel and the commanding officer of the Rhode Island National Guard 103rd Field Artillery in 1963. He was a partner in the law firm of Edwards & Angell, Director of the Department of Administration for the State of Rhode Island and a member of the Rhode Island House of Representatives from 1961 to 1983, including ten years as House Minority Leader. He ran unsuccessfully for Mayor of Providence three times, losing in 1984 and 1990 by only a few hundred votes. For 50 years he devoted himself to charitable and civic causes including serving as senior Fellow of Brown University, Chairman of the Board of Rhode Island Hospital, Chairman of the Board of the Providence Plan, Head of the Board of Regents for Elementary and Secondary Education and Chairman of the Board of Trustees of St. Marks School. Brown awarded him an honorary degree in 1977 and the President's Medal in 2004. The Memorial Service will be held Wednesday, May 18th, 2005 at the First Unitarian Church in Providence. Donations may be sent to the Frederick Lippitt Endowment Fund, C/O The Rhode Island Foundation, 1 Union Station, Providence, Rhode Island 02903.


        LIBERT-Orion J. Colonel, USAF (Ret). On February 17 at St. Joseph's Retirement Community, Lauderdale Lakes, Florida. Born in Madison, WI., on April 8, 1901 Col. Libert was a 1928 graduate of Georgetown University's School of Foreign Service. Col. Libert served abroad with the 8th Air Force in London during World War II, and was an International Affairs Officer in the Office of the Secretary of Defense during the Korean War. He was also a Foreign Service Officer with the U.S. Department of State having served in various capacities in Washington, D.C., Santiago, London, Paris, Manila, Montevideo and Bogota. Prior to his retirement in 1962. Col. Libert served as First Secretary for Economic Affairs and Deputy Chief of Mission for the U.S. Embassy in Panama. He is survived by his wife Viola, three sons: Francis G. of Rockville, MD, John C. of Great Falls, VA, and Andre J. of Fairfield, CT; a daughter, Mary T. Simonette of Palmyra, VA; nine grandchildren and two great grandchildren.

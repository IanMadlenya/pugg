
        GRAHAM-David Tredway. Internist and educator, died in Newark, Delaware on Saturday, November 13, 1999 at age 82. He was a pioneer in the development of psychosomatic medicine whose research demonstrated a relationship between particular diseases and associated attitudes toward life events. His research has been published widely in scientific journals. Dr. Graham was born in Mason City, Iowa, son of Drs. Evarts A. and Helen T. Graham, distinguished faculty at Washington University's School of Medicine in St. Louis, and brother of Evarts A. Graham Jr., former Managing Editor of the St. Louis Post-Dispatch. He graduated from Princeton Univeristy (1938), received an M.A. degree in Psychology from Yale University (1941), and the M.D. degree from Washington University (1943). After serving as 1st Lieutenant and Captain in the Army Medical Corp in Africa and Germany during World War II he completed residency training at Washington University and was a research fellow with the late Dr. Harold G. Wolff at the Cornell Medical School in New York City from 1948 to 1951. Following six years on the faculty of the Department of Medicine of Washington University Medical School, Dr. Graham taught for 29 years at the Medical School of the University of Wisconsin-Madison as Associate Professor, Professor, and Chair (1971-1980) of the Department of Medicine. After retiring in 1986, he served as Adjunct Professor in the Department of Psychology of the University of Delaware. Dr. Graham was editor of Clinical Research Proceedings from 1954-1959, President of the Society for Psychophysiological Research in 1970, and President of the Psychosomatic Society in 1979. He was a Fellow of the American College of Physicians and a Diplomate of the American Board of Internal Medicine. Dr. Graham is survived by Frances Keesler Graham, his wife of 58 years; two daughters, Norma Graham of New York City and Mary Graham of Wilmington DE; a son, Andrew Graham of Midland, MI; and eight grandchildren.

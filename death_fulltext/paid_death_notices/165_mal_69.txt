
        RABER-Irving R. Loving husband and companion of Naomi of 54 years. Beloved father of Michael (Hila) and Patricia Max (Ken). Caring grandfather of Jesse and Maia Raber, Lindsey & Benjamin Max. Dear brother of Murray (Lila). He will live in our hearts forever. Services at Beth El Synagogue, Northfield Road, New Rochelle, Thursday at 11 AM. RABER-Irving. The Officers, Board of Directors, and staff of UJA-Federation of New York mourn the passing of Irving Raber, a longtime friend and generous supporter of our Westchester Campaign. We extend our sincere condolences to his beloved wife, Naomi; his children, Patty and Michael; his grandchildren; and the entire family. James S. Tisch, President Larry Zicklin, Board Chair & President-Elect Barry Effron, Chair, Westchester General Campaign John S. Ruskay, Exec VP & CEO RABER-Irving R. The Chinatown Community mourns the loss of Irving R. Raber, founder of the Irving R. Raber Company, Inc. on Canal Street. Mr. Raber has been developing the Chinatown area since 1940 and has helped improve the face of Chinatown. From the Bowery to Broadway, Mr. Raber has helped the Chinese Community to expand in immeasurable ways. He will be greatly missed by all of his associates and his employees at The Irving R. Raber Co. RABER-Irving. Beth El Synogogue of New Rochelle mourns the loss of our longtime devoted member an Honorary Trustee. Heartfelt condolences to the entire family. Melvin N. Sirner, Rabbi Russell Fayer, President


        LIPCHITZ -- Yulla. Passed away peacefully in her 92nd year on Sunday, July 20th after a short illness. A sculptor, photographer and painter, she was the widow of Jacques Lipchitz and is survived by her sons Hanno D. Mott of NYC and Frank L. (Susan) Mott of Columbus, OH, her daughter, Lolya R. Lipchitz (Harold Kasimow) of Grennell, IO, her sister, Hilda Ascher, of Zurich, Switzerland and her brother, Theodore N. Halton of Jerusalem, Israel. She will be sorely missed by her grandchildren Joshua A. Mott, Tamar E. Mott, Sophie Kasimow and Johanna Kasimow. The funeral will be in Jerusalem, Israel where her late husband is also buried. No flowers, please and any contributions may be made to the American Friends of the Israel Museum, 500 Fifth Avenue, NY, NY of which she was a Director and a close friend.
        LIPCHITZ--Yulla. The Board and staff of the American Friends of the Israel Museum and the Israel Museum, Jerusalem are deeply saddened by the death of AFIM's longtime Board member, and generous supporter Yulla Lipchitz. Our heartfelt condolences to AFIM Vice President, Hanno Mott and the entire family. Ronnie F. Heyman Pres., AFIM James S. Snyder Anne & Jerome Fisher Dir., IM

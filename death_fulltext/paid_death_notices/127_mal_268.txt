
        Von ELM-H. Walter. Age 82, died peacefully on Sunday August 17 at Calvary Hospital in The Bronx, New York after a long illness. Born in Brooklyn, New York on March 19, 1915, he was the son of the late Margaret and Henry Von Elm, who was former President and Chairman of the Board of The Manufacturers Trust Company. He was graduated from the Choate School and Princeton University, Class of 1937. During World War II he served in the South Pacific as a Captain in the Army Air Corp. He spent his career in banking and retired as a Vice President of the Manufacturers Hanover Trust Company. He was a member of The Everglades Club, The Cap and Gown Club at Princeton, The Princeton Club of New York and the University Club in New York, where he resided for many years. Mr Von Elm is survived by a sister Dorothy Putnam, of East Hampton, New York, a brother Charles Von Elm, of Forest Hills, New York, three nieces and two nephews. Services will be at Frank E. Campbell, 1076 Madison Avenue at 81st Street, Thursday, August 28 at 5:30 P.M. Interment will be private. In lieu of flowers, contributions in his memory may be made to Princeton University.

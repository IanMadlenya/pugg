
        HEARST-Randolph Apperson. On December 18, 2000 at the age of 85. Husband of Veronica, father of Catherine Millicent Hearst, Virginia Anne Hearst Randt and Patricia Campbell Hearst Shaw, Anne Randolph Hearst, and Victoria Veronica Hearst, and four grandchildren. Service private. In lieu of flowers a contribution may be made to Family Connection Literacy Program, C/O Dr. Jean Fuller, Superintendent, Bakersfield City Schools, 1300 Baker Street, Bakersfield, CA. 93305-4399.
        HEARST-Randolph A. It is with profound sorrow that the Officers, Board and members of United Cerebral Palsy of New York City acknowledge the passing of a devoted and most generous friend. Through his longstanding friendship with our founder, Jack Hausman, Randolph Hearst became a staunch supporter of the needs of children and adults with cerebral palsy-and their families-over many years. We extend our heartfelt condolences to his wife, Veronica, and to his five daughters. Leo Hausman, Chairman Martin Hausman, President Edward R. Matthews, Exec. Dir.
        HEARST - Randolph Apperson. The Board and staff of the American Foundation for AIDS Research (amfAR) mourn the loss of our valued friend, Randolph Apperson Hearst, who was a strong supporter of amfAR's research programs through the William Randolph Hearst Foundation. We extend our sincere condolences to his wife, Veronica, who has also been a long-time and generous supporter of amfAR's work. Mathilde Krim, Ph.D., Founding Chair and Chairman of the Board Jerome J. Radwin, Chief Executive Officer
        HEARST-Randolph A. The members of The Lotos Club mourn the loss of their distinguished colleague and longtime friend, Randolph A. Hearst, and send condolences to his family.

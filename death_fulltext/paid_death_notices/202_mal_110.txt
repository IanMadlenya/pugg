
        TOMPKINS--John, 79, died suddenly Saturday night, November 8th of a heart attack. John was a World War II veteran who enlisted at the age of 17. After the war, he graduated from the University of Michigan on the G.I. bill, and then moved to New York to work for Grace Line. The move to New York launched his distinguished career as a journalist working first for the Wall Street Journal. From there he moved to The New York Times, Business Week and finally Time Magazine which led to his appointment to the Price Commission in Washington, DC. He authored two non fiction books ''The Crime Confederation'' and ''The Weapons of World War III''. At the time of his death, John was writing a column for CoiningMoney.com an online investment newsletter. He loved to scrounge for obscure antiques and had an undending thirst and curiosity for information. He was a man of great faith and conviction. His passing is a loss for all who knew him. He is survived by his devoted wife Michele Stephenson and his daughters Claire Tompkins Timothy and Nancy Tompkins Heinemann. The memorial service will be at 5:30 PM Friday, November 14 at Frank E. Campbell Funeral Home at 81st Street and Madison Ave. Donations may be made to Hospice of New York, 45-18 Court Square, Long Island City, NY 11101.

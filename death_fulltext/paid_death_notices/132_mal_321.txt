
        SUTRO-Ogden Winne. Of Long Hill Farm in Guilford, CT died Wednesday evening January 14, 1998, at the age of 84. Mr. Sutro was a Connecticut businessman who devoted much of his time and interest to the Boy Scouts, Education and the development of industry in Southern Connecticut which included founding an electronics company, a bank and a consulting firm. In particular, he devoted his time and energy to The Foote School and Yale University, where he was Managing Director of the Yale Alumni Fund for several years, and to his class of 1936, ''Yale's greatest class'' of which he was class secretary until his health declined. He was a direct descendent of Christiaan Winne, one of the Dutch founders of New York City. He was a member of the St. Nicholas Society, the New Haven Lawn Club, and the Hartwood Club. He was born in New York City and graduated from the Pingry School prior to attending Yale University where he graduated in 1936 from the Sheffield School of Engineering. He played football and lacrosse for Yale. He was a Naval Officer in World War II in the South Pacific. He lived and worked in Connecticut. His first wife, Priscilla Alden Egleston, died in 1984. He is survived by his wife Susanna Small Sutro of Guilford, CT; three children, by his first wife, Ogden Hillyer Sutro of Stonington, CT, Virginia Sutro Morse of Winchester, MA, and Curtis DeWitt Sutro of Stratford, CT; two grandchildren, Andrew Hillyer Sutro and Alden David Morse; his brothers, Louis Lefevre Sutro and Fred C. Sutro. There will be a private interment followed by a memorial service on Saturday, February 7, 1998 at 12:00 noon at Trinity Church on The Green located at the corner of Chapel and Temple Street, New Haven, CT.

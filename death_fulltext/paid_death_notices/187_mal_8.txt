
        TAPPER-David, MD, 57, died July 23 after a courageous battle with kidney cancer. He joined Children's Hospital and Regional Medical Center in Seattle, WA, in 1983 as their first surgeon-in-chief and professor and vice-chairman of Surgery at the University of Washington Medical School. Dr. Tapper recently concluded his presidency of the American Pediatric Surgical Association. Dr. Tapper graduated Magna Cum Laude in 1970 from the University of Maryland Medical School and began his surgical training at the University of California in San Francisco. Dr. Tapper completed a research fellowship at Boston Children's Hospital, and after completing his surgical residency in San Francisco in 1977, he returned to Boston for training in Pediatric Surgery at Boston Children's. He stayed on the faculty of the Harvard Medical School and was an attending surgeon at Boston Children's. Devoted to his family, he is survived by his loving wife of 34 years, Susan; their four children, JoEllen, Erica, Jacalyn, and Aaron; mother, Sylvia; sister, Martha (Jerry) Benesch, and brother, Jeffrey (Barbara). He was preceded in death by his father, Herman. In lieu of flowers, his family requests contributions be made to: The David Tapper Residents Fund, C/O UW Foundation, 1325 Fourth Ave. Suite 2000, Seattle, WA. 98101.

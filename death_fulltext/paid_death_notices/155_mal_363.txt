
        BAKER-James Anderson. Born October 6, 1927 in Garland Texas, Died December 27, 1999 in Naples, FL. Graduated from Garland Texas Public Schools. Attended Texas A & M then on to study and graduate with a BSEE from Southern Methodist University in Dallas, TX. Later attended and graduated from the Harvard AMP Program. He served in the Merchant Marines as well as a 1st Lt. in the U.S. Army. He joined the General Electric Company in 1952 and worked in many areas such as Heating, Service Shops, and Industrial Controls. In 1973 he became Vice President of General Electric's Lamp Division at Nela Park, Ohio. He was named a Senior VP and moved to GE Headquarters in Fairfield, CT in 1979. Prior to retiring he coined the phrase, ''Automate, Emigrate or Evaporate.'' In 1986 he retired from GE as Sector Vice President. He was a lay minister in the United Methodist church, active in United Way and was acting 1986 United Way Chairperson for Tri-State (Connecticut, New York, New Jersey) United Way. He was an active patron of the arts. He is survived by his wife, Patsy Smith Baker of 50 years. Daughters and sons-in-Law Janet and Greg Henley of Clinton, SC; Carol and Chris LeSesne of Noblesville, IN; and Laura and John Flick of Naples, FL. Twelve grandchildren; one great grandson; two brothers, Paul Baker of Garland, TX and Donald Baker of Satanta, KS and many nieces and nephews.

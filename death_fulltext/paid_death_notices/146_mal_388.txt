
        ALBERGA-Glenn H.., 96. Accomplished industrial engineer, died on February 19 in Venice, Florida. Born Brooklyn, 1902, he graduated Erasmus High School and Cornell University (E.E., 1926). He helped develop first telephone dialing system in NYC (with Western Electric) and first wireless European connection (with Bell Labs). His professional accomplishments are recorded in Who's Who in America, Who's Who in New England, Who's Who in Engineering. Member of Mensa, AIIE, ASME, Cornell and Ivy League Clubs, and Lambda Chi Alpha. Surviving are his daughter, Sandra (Maine), his son, Robert (Florida, and his niece, Virginia (Florida). Donations to the American Cancer Society.


        ROSSMAN-Isadore, M.D. Dr. Rossman died Tuesday, June 3 at home. The cause of death was cancer. The nationally and internationally recognized clinician and scholar, Dr. Rossman made seminal and enduring contributions to the fields of social and geriatric medicine. Dr. Rossman was editor of the first widely read textbook of geriatrics, and later published ''Looking Forward,'' a guide for successful aging. As a member of the Council to the National Institute on Aging, and president of the American Geriatrics Society, he made significant contributions to public health policy. Dr. Rossman will be best remembered as an unusually astute, caring and compassionate physician, adored by family, friends, patients and colleagues alike. Contributions to a scholarship fund in his name-in social medicine-may be forwarded to: Albert Einstein College of Medicine, c/o Michael Reichgott, M.D., 1300 Morris Park Ave, Rm 210, Bronx, NY 10461. Memorial services will be held Monday, June 9 at 7pm at the New York Society for Ethical Culture, 2 W 64 St, NY, NY.
        ROSSMAN-Isadore, M.D. The Albert Einstein College of Medicine of Yeshiva University sadly notes the passing of Dr. Isadore Rossman, clinical professor emeritus of epidemiology and social medicine. A distinguished member of the Einstein faculty for more than three decades, he was an internationally recognized pioneer in the field of geriatric medicine. We extend our sincere condolences to his wife, Sylvia, his son, Dr. Paul Rossman, and the entire family. Dominick P. Purpura, M.D., Dean Albert Einstein College of Medicine

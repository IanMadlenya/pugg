
        LAVINSKY--Larry M., born October 23, 1929, in New York City, died July 8, 2004. He was a graduate of City College of New York and New York University Law School, a partner at Proskauer Rose LLP, a former chairman of the National Law Committee of the AntiDefamation League of B'nai B'rith, and a founding member of the Committee on Equal Education Opportunity. With unwavering dedication to human equality, he argued civil rights cases before the United States Court of Appeals and the United States Supreme Court. He was a passionate scholar of music, religion, and philosophy. He is survived by his wife, Dena, his three sons, Joshua, Avram, and Ezra, his daughterin-law, Priscilla, his sisters-inlaw, Deborah and Marcia, his nephews, Roger and Steven, and his niece, Barbara.

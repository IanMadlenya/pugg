
        BROWNSTEIN-Vivian, former Chief Economist of Fortune magazine, died on Monday at the age of 69. Vivian Brownstein worked at the Federal Reserve Board in Washington, D.C., served on the Commission on Money and Credit, and ended her career as Chief Economist at Fortune magazine, where her column, The Fortune Forecast, was read by the business community and Washington policy makers. Since her retirement in 1995, Vivian Brownstein has been active serving as the President of the New York chapter of the NYABE, an officer of the Downtown Economists, and an officer and member of various community organizations. Vivian Brownstein is survived by Edward, her husband of 33 years, daughters Katherine, Alison and Nancy, son Glenn, brother Leon Rosenson, mother-in-law Bess, and grandchildren Nina, Samuel and William. Funeral service will be held on Thursday, July 13, 2000 at 11 A.M., at John J. Fox Funeral Home in Larchmont, New York.

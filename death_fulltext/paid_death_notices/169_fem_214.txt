
        CLAY-Harriet McLean. Of East Hampton, New York, passed away at her home on February 13, 2001 after a long battle with cancer. Piddy, as she was known, was born in New York City on February 21, 1928, to Major Henry C. McLean, of Newburgh, N.Y., and Harriet Peckham McLean, of Albany, N.Y. Piddy will be remembered as an athletic, independent, fun-loving, and glamorous person loved by a strong circle of family and friends. She is survived by two children from her first marriage to Howland P. Rogers of Hobe Sound, Florida: Lisa Rogers Bornstein, of Oakland, CA, and Henry P. Rogers, of San Mateo, CA; five granddaughters, two nieces and two nephews. A private memorial celebration will be held in June. Donations in her memory may be made to the East End Hospice, P.O. Box 1048, Westhampton Beach, New York 11978-7048


        ROBINSON - Anne Grosvenor. Daughters Kip Greenthal and Grosvie Cooley honor their cherished mother who died suddenly March 11, 2001. Born August 5, 1919, she attended Miss. Chapin's, Foxcroft, and Vassar, graduating in 1940 at age twenty. She wrote for The New York Daily News, where she was ''the first female copy boy'' to cover Police Headquarters, and later Life Magazine. In 1952, she moved with her husband, Dwight Edwards Robinson (1914-1988) to Seattle, where she was an active member of numerous boards and organizations. She receieved an MSW from the University of Washington in 1971 with high honors. Survivors include her two daughters, five grandchildren and beloved companion, Robert B. Wilson.


        FONER-Samuel Newton. Of Temple Terrace, FL. (Tampa area) on Saturday, October 14, 2000. Born March 21, 1920 in New York City, died of cancer. Dr. Foner, an atomic and molecular physicist was a specialist in mass spectrometry and molecular beams. He worked on the Manhattan project during WWII and spent a long and distinguished career at the Johns Hopkins Applied Physics Laboratory from 1945 until his retirement in 1990 in Florida. He received numerous distinguished service awards for his studies of constituents of flame reactions. During his research at APL he served in several management positions including Vice Chair of the Milton S. Eisenhower Research Center 1975-1983 and Senior Scientist of Strategic Systems 1984-1990. His degrees, all at the Carnegie Institute of Technology, in Pittsburgh, a BS in Physics and in Mathematics in 1940, MS in 1941 and D.SC in 1945 in Physics. He was a Fellow of American Physical Society, Life Fellow of the American Association for Advancement of Science, Fellow of the Washington Academy of Sciences, member of the Philosophical Society of Washington, member of Cosmos Club, The Combustion Insititute, The American Vacuum Society, Phi Kappa Phi and Sigma Xi. He leaves siblings, Dr. Max Foner of Memphis, TN, Mary Foner Rosenthal of Pittsfield, MA, Rachel Foner Powell of Thorneville, OH, Dr. Simon Foner of Belmont, MA, and David N. Foner of N. Andover, MA.

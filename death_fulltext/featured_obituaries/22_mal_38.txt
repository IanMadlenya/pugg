        Robert Baylor Semple, former chairman of the board of BASF Wyandotte Corporation, a multinational manufacturer of chemicals and video tapes, died of myeloma yesterday at his home in Grosse Point, Mich. He was 78 years old.
        Mr. Semple joined Wyandotte in 1949 after 16 years with Monsanto Chemical Company. Born in St. Louis, he earned bachelor's and master's degrees from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology.
        An accomplished amateur clarinetist, he was former chairman of the board of the Detroit Symphony Orchestra. He was a life member of the M.I.T. Corporation, a former director of the Chrysler Corporation and former chairman of the executive committee of the Manufacturing Chemists Association, Surviving are his wife, Holly, and four sons and a daughter from a previous marriage to the late Isabelle Ashby Neer, Robert Jr. of Manhattan, Lloyd of Grosse Pointe, William and Nathaniel, both of Washington, and Elizabeth Hart of Manhattan, and 11 grandchildren.

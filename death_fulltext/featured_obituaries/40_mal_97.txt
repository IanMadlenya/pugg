        Thomas L. Boardman, a former editor for two Scripps Howard newspapers, died of leukemia on May 4 at his home in Cleveland. He was 70 years old.
        Mr. Boardman was editor of The Cleveland Press from 1966 until his retirement in 1979 and, previously, editor of The Indianapolis Times from 1962 until 1966.
        He joined The Press after graduating from Oberlin College in 1939 and returned there after Navy service in World War II. He was chief editorial writer when he was named editor of The Indianapolis Times in 1962.
        He was a trustee of Oberlin College and served on the academic advisory board of the United States Naval Academy at Annapolis.
        He is survived by his wife, Cynthia; a son, Dr. Thomas of San Francisco; a daughter Caroline Curtiss of Falls Church, Va., and four gandchildren.

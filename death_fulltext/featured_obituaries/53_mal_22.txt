
        Percy Marcus, a New York trucking industry executive, died on Sunday at Columbia-Presbyterian Medical Center. He was 78 years old and lived in Miami Beach and Englewood Cliffs, N.J.
        He died of cancer, a spokeswoman for the family said.
        Mr. Marcus joined the United States Trucking Company in 1929 and spent his career there. He was executive vice president, chief operating officer and a director when he retired in 1978.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Elaine Katz; a son, Alan, of Ridgewood, N.J.; two daughters, Paula, of West Hollywood, Calif., and Nan Blecker of Milltown, N.J.; a brother, Irving, of West Palm Beach, Fla.; two sisters, Bertha Leichter of Palisades Park, N.J., and Lillian Shilt of West Palm Beach, and three grandchildren.

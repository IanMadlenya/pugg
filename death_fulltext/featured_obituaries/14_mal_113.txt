        Lawrence Turcic, assistant curator in the drawings department of the Metropolitan Museum of Art, died Monday at Lenox Hill Hospital in New York. He was 38 years old.
        His family gave the cause of death as complications resulting from pneumonia.
        Mr. Turcic worked for the Metropolitan Museum for 11 years as a researcher and curator. He was co-author of museum catalogues on 15th- and 16th-century Italian drawings and 15th-to-18th-century French drawings with Jacob Bean, the department's curator. Mr. Turcic studied at Fordham University and New York University's Institute of Fine Arts and wrote for several art journals.
        Mr. Turcic is survived by his parents, John and Mary Turcic, of New York City; his brother, Thomas, of Poughquag, N.Y., and three sisters, Christina, Mary and Stefanie, all of New York City.


        Milton Weinick, founding partner of a New York accounting firm, chairman of a men's apparel maker and a philanthropist, died on Wednesday while vacationing with his wife and friends in Calgary, Alberta. He was 74 and lived in Manhattan.
        He suffered a stroke, his family said.
        Mr. Weinick was born in Brooklyn and graduated from Brooklyn College and St. John's University. In 1948, he co-founded the firm of Weinick, Sanders & Company in Manhattan and served as its managing partner for the last 45 years.
        He joined Nautica Enterprises Inc., based in Manhattan, as a director in 1971. The company, formerly known as State-O-Maine, designs and manufactures men's apparel and sportswear. Mr. Weinick became chairman in 1981.
        Gov. Mario M. Cuomo appointed him a board member of the New York State Medical Care Facilities Finance Agency in May 1989; his term was to expire at the end of this year. He also was a director of the Atalanta Sosnoff Capital Corporation of Manhattan.
        Mr. Weinick was board chairman of the Park East Synagogue of Manhattan and co-founded the Albert Einstein College of Medicine and the Rabbi Arthur Schneier Chair in International Relations at Yeshiva University. He was a trustee of the Young Men's Philanthropic League. Established a Fellowship
        He established the Rita and Milton Weinick Research Fellowship at New York Hospital-Cornell Medical Center, as well as scholarship funds at the Jewish Theological Seminary and Park East Synagogue. He served as the synagogue's board chairman for the last 15 years. He was a founder of the Park East Day School and of the Minskoff Cultural Center on East 68th Street.
        Mr. Weinick is survived by his wife, the former Rita Lohman Greenfield; three daughters, Faith Drucker of Manhattan, Jill Iscol of Pound Ridge, N.Y.; and Beth Sperber of Setauket, L.I.; two stepsons, Van and Bruce Greenfield, both of Manhattan, and 10 grandchildren.

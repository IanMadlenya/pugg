        Dr. Hubert Park Beck, a psychologist and retired professor of educational psychology, died of cardiac arrest Monday at St. Luke's-Roosevelt Hospital Center in Manhattan. He was 81 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Dr. Beck, who earned a Ph.D. in educational administration from Columbia University in 1945, joined the education department at City College of New York in 1947 and was promoted to full professor in 1969. He retired in 1977.
        He was a former trustee of the New Lincoln School and a former secretary of the New York City unit of the National Education Association.
        He is survived by his wife, Dorothy; a daughter, Brenda, of Toronto, and a brother, Robert, of West Chester, Pa.

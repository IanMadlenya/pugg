        Seymour Kleinman, a senior partner at the Manhattan law firm of Golenbock & Barell and a noted philanthropist, died of pneumonia yesterday at Long Island Jewish Hospital. He was 70 years old and lived in Valley Stream, L.I.
        Mr. Kleinman led numerous fund-raising drives for various charities, including the United Jewish Appeal, the Federation of Jewish Philanthropies, the Community Fund and the Association for the Help of Retarded Children.
        He served on the board of Hebrew University in Jerusalem and was a trustee and founder of the American Friends of Hebrew University.
        His legal career spanned more than 45 years. He specialized in corporate, securities and real-estate law.
        Surviving are his wife, Shirley; his mother, May, of Valley Stream, L.I.; a brother, Daniel, of Forest Hills, Queens; a sister, Edith Siegal of Valley Stream; two sons, David, of Great Neck, L.I., Louis, of Los Angeles, and two daughters, Dr. Dale Berman of Columbia, Md., and Devorah Berman Kleinman of Manhattan.

        Mabel Hampton, an advocate in the gay rights movement, died of pneumonia Thursday at St. Luke's-Roosevelt Hospital Center. She was 87 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Ms. Hampton, a native of Winston Salem, N.C., had been a dancer in earlier years. In 1974, with three other women, she founded the Lesbian Herstory Archives in Manhattan, a collection of lesbian books and artifacts including her personal library.
        She was an annual participant in the city's Gay and Lesbian Pride March and was its grand marshal in 1985.
        There are no immediate survivors.

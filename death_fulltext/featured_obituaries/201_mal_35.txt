
        Richard Talmage Perkin, a television executive and philanthropist who founded the VZV Research Foundation, which backs research and education about chickenpox, shingles and other infections caused by the varicella-zoster virus, died on Thursday in Manhattan. He was 72.
        He was chairman and chief executive of the VZV Research Foundation and chairman of the Perkin Fund, which provides support for the arts and scientific research. He was on the Rockefeller University Council and a trustee of the Juilliard School and of the Wildlife Conservation Society.
        He was born July 13, 1931, in Brooklyn. His father, Richard Scott Perkin, was a founder and for many years chairman of the Perkin-Elmer Corporation, manufacturers of scientific instruments in Norwalk, Conn.
        Richard Talmage Perkin graduated from Harvard College in 1954. Before creating the foundation, he worked for Citibank in Manhattan, as an executive at the Manhattan headquarters of the Burson-Marsteller advertising agency and as a Manhattan-based executive in the field of television syndication.
        His marriage to Marianne H. K. de Lidertejed ended in divorce.
        Surviving are his wife, Leslie Manning Bergesch; a daughter and a son from his first marriage, Kristina Davison and Nicolas; a daughter from his second marriage, Caroline S. Perkin; a granddaughter; a sister, Winifred Perkin Gray of Wenham, Mass.; and a brother, Robert S., of Darien, Conn.


        Kenneth C. Swenson, a partner with Campbell Advisors, a Manhattan-based investment firm, died on Friday in White Plains Hospital Center, a nursing supervisor, Kathleen Poli, said. He was 48 years old and lived in Scarsdale, N.Y.
        He died of an aneurysm that he suffered on Monday at his home, his wife, Catherine Blodgett Swenson, said.
        Mr. Swenson was born in Kenmare, N.D. He earned a bachelor's degree in 1965 and a master's degree in business administration in 1967, both from the University of Wisconsin at Madison, where he was a member of Phi Beta Kappa.
        He joined the Campbell firm as a portfolio manager and partner at its founding in 1970.
        Mr. Swenson is survived by his wife; a brother, Gary L., of Greenwich, Conn.; a sister, Gale Hankey of Darien, Conn.; a daughter, Jennifer, and a son, Craig.

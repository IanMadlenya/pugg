        William G. Erickson, a former vice president of Air Chemical Corporation in Carlstadt, N.J., died of a heart attack June 18 at Northern Dutchess Community Hospital in Rhinebeck, N.Y. He was 70 years old and lived in Athens, N.Y.
        Mr. Erickson, who retired in the mid-1970's, was a founder of Venture Inc. of Nanuet, an organization of parents of the mentally disabled residents of Venture, a group home. He had also been the group home's president, and for many years was a trustee of the village of Upper Nyack.
        Mr. Erickson was born in Hudson, N.Y., and graduated from Hamilton College in Clinton, N.Y., and received a master's degree from the Fletcher School of Law and Diplomacy in Medford, Mass. He was a captain in the Army Air Corps in World War II.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Jane VanLoan; a son, Mark, of Larchmont, N.Y.; a daughter, Eleanor Erickson of Nanuet; a sister, Joye McDonald of Philmont, N.Y.; his stepmother, Viola Erikson of Stottville, N.Y., and two granddaughters.


        Keith W. Vandivort, a lawyer who specialized in securities regulations and was an advocate for gay rights and people with AIDS, died on Sunday at a hospital in Washington. He was 40 and lived in Arlington, Va.
        He died of AIDS, his family said.
        Mr. Vandivort, who was born in Toledo, Ohio, and graduated from Northwestern University and Georgetown University Law Center, was a partner in the Washington law firm of Dechert, Price & Rhoads.
        He served on a committee of the Human Rights Campaign Fund, a gay rights organization, and on the board of the Northern Virginia AIDS Ministry.
        Surviving are his mother, Helen Collier-Scrimgeour of Reno; his father, William, of Toledo; a sister, Heidi Zalobowski of Henderson, Nev., and a brother, Daniel, of New Canaan, Conn.

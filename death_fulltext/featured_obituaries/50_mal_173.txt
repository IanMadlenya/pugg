
        Sherwood Noble, one of the men credited with founding Dairy Queen stores, died here on Sunday. He was 82 years old..
        Mr. Noble was the first franchisee of Dairy Queen, the soft ice cream chain that now includes more than 5,200 stores nationwide.
        Mr. Noble, who once owned as many as 35 stores in Illinois, had seven at the time of his death, said a spokeswoman for International Dairy Queen Company in Bloomington, Minn.
        After completing a dairy course at Iowa State College, Mr.Noble managed an ice cream store in his hometown of Clemons, Iowa. In 1931, he opened another store in Kankakee, where he and J. F. McCullough developed the soft-serve ice cream business in 1938.

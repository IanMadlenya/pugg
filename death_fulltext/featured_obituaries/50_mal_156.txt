
        Henry Waters Taft 2d, an executive and former president of Outward Bound, U.S.A., died yesterday at his home in Camden, Me. He was 64 years old.
        He died of pancreatic cancer, a sister said.
        Mr. Taft, a grandnephew of President William Howard Taft, graduated from Yale University and the Harvard University Graduate School of Business Administration.
        He was treasurer of the Doubleday Company from 1953 to 1959, and treasurer and vice president of the Brystol-Myers Company from 1962 to 1973. He then became president of Outward Bound, and in 1981 moved to Maine. He was a trustee of various institutions, including the Taft School and the Conservation Foundation.
        He is survived by his wife, Jan; a son, Henry W., of Annapolis, Md; two daughters, Charlotte, of Dallas and Laurie Zaccagnino of Norwalk, Conn; four stepsons, Joshua Porter of Camden, Terrence and Eric Parks, both of Mill Valley, Calif., and Anthony Parks of Newport, R.I.; two stepdaughters, Jennifer Porter of Camden, and Laurie Coleman of Petaluma, Calif; his mother, Elizabeth, of Watch Hill, R.I.; two sisters, Elizabeth Johnson of Watch Hill and Lucy Bard of Locust Valley, L.I., and six grandchildren.

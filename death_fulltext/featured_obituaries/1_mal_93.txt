        Prof. Jose Vazquez-Amaral, a critic of Spanish literature and a professor at Rutgers University, died Tuesday at Overlook Hospital in Summit, N.J. He had a heart condition. He was 73 years old and lived in Warren, N.J.
        Professor Vazquez-Amaral headed the Spanish and Portuguese department at Rutgers from 1947 to 1982, when he retired.
        He was born in Los Reyes, Jalisco, Mexico, and was an alumnus of La Universidad Nacional Autonoma de Mexico, from which he also received a law degree.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Mary Helen Madiraca; a son, Pedro, of New Orleans; four daughters, Lydia Watson and Marta Alicia Lopez, both of Mexico City, Maria Elena, of Warren, and Jacinta Rosa Amaral of Aurora, N.Y., and 10 grandchildren.

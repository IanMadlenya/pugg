        John McCarthy, a former senior vice president of the Manufacturers Hanover Trust Company, died yesterday in London, where he was undergoing treatment for cancer of the throat. He was 55 years old and had been living in Porto Ercole, Italy, since his retirement a year ago.
        Mr. McCarthy joined Manufacturers Hanover as a vice president of merchant banking in 1971. Before that he had been a vice president of the Crocker National Bank in San Francisco and London. He retired from Manufacturers Hanover in London, where he was the bank's senior representative in Europe.
        He is survived by his sister, Phyllis Oliver of Watsonville, Calif., and two aunts.

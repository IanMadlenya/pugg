        Leonard M. Horton, a former president and chairman of Aubrey G. Lanston & Company, a dealer in Treasury securities, died on Saturday in Delray Beach, Fla., where he lived in retirement. He was 83 years old and had lived for many years in Short Hills, N.J.
        Mr. Horton left the Chemical Bank and Trust Company, where he had been vice president in charge of the Wall Street division, in 1949 to become a founder of Lanston. He became president of the securities concern in 1959.
        Mr. Horton was an alumnus of Lehigh University, where he later was a trustee. He was also on the board of the American Bible Society.
        Surviving are his wife, Gladys; a daughter, the Rev. Nancy H. McCarthy of Boca Raton, Fla.; five grandchildren, and one great-grandchild.

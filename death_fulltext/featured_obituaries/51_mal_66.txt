
        Dr. Alfred L. Frechette, a former Massachusetts Health Commissioner, died Sunday at Framingham Union Hospital. He was 81 years old.
        The cause of death was not disclosed.
        Dr. Frechette was commissioner from 1959 to 1972 and from 1979 to 1983. In 1979, his order made Massachusetts the first state to ban urea formaldehyde foam insulation as a health hazard. The Environmental Protection Agency imposed a nationwide ban years later.
        He had been a senior associate director of the Sidney Farber Cancer Institute of Boston and a clinical professor at the Harvard School of Public Health.
        Dr. Frechette is survived by a son, Alfred L. of North Kingston, R.I., a sister, and three grandchildren.

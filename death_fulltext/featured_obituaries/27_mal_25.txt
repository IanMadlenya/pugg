        Roger Cortesi, an investment banker, died yesterday at Westerly (R.I.) Hospital. He was 87 years old and lived in Stonington, Conn.
        Mr. Cortesi was a founding partner in 1931 of Auchincloss, Parker & Redpath, which became Thomson & McKinnon, Auchincloss Inc. Mr. Cortesi was an executive vice president. He retired in 1974.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Mary Hathaway Williams; four children from a previous marriage, Roger, of Washington, Henry and Alexander, both of Manhattan, and Katharine Armstrong of Boston, and eight grandchildren.

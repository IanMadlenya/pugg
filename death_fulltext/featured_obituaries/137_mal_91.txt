
        Emanuel Levenson, who shared his love of opera with schoolchildren across the country, died on June 9 at the University of Michigan Hospital in Ann Arbor. He was 81 and lived in Ypsilanti, Mich.
        Mr. Levenson, a graduate of Columbia University, studied piano with Joseph Adler and taught piano and vocal techniques. He directed an opera workshop at the New School for Social Research, and he was music director of the After Dinner Opera Company.
        He founded the Pennybridge Opera Company in Brooklyn and the Berkshire Pro Musica in Pittsfield, Mass., and was a founder of the Becket Arts Center in Becket, Mass.
        He was the director of the music organization called Young Audiences when the group brought opera performers into schools.
        He is survived by his wife, Fay; a stepdaughter, Davi Napoleon; a sister, Judith Corin of Anacortes, Wash.; a brother, Mickey, of Castro Valley, Calif., and two grandchildren.

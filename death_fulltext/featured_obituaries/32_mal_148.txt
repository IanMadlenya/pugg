        Mathew Seshi Ogawa, who was to assume the presidency of Lions Club International next year, died Thursday at University of California-Los Angeles Medical Center after cancer surgery. He was 69 years old and lived in Tokyo.
        Mr Ogawa, a graduate of Kobe University, was executive director of the AVACO Christain Mass Communication Center in Tokyo and had also been an instructor at Interntional Christian University in Tokyo.
        He was a member of the Shibuya Lions Club in Tokyo and was the first vice president of the 1.4 million-member international service organization.
        He is survived by his wife, Ikuku, and five children.

        Jacob Martin Seiler, chairman of the former A. J. Armstrong Company, a finance company in Manhattan, died of a heart attack on Tuesday at Palm Beach (Fla.) Regional Hospital. He was 81 years old and lived in Palm Beach.
        Mr. Seiler and his family bought Armstrong in the 40's. He retired in 1981, when it was sold to the Security Pacific Corporation of San Diego.
        In the 1960's he was president and later chairman of the National Commercial Finance Association, a trade group. Mr. Seiler had also been a director of the Brooklyn Loan Corporation, the Sheffield House Furniture Company and the Association of Commercial Discount Companies; a partner in the City Industrial Company, and a vice president and director of the National Commercial Fianance Conference.
        He had lived in Mamaroneck, N.Y., and Greenwich, Conn. He was married twice.
        Surviving are a daughter, Elaine, of Great Barrington, Mass.; a son, Louis, of Belvedere, Calif.; a stepson, John Heffer of Chappaqua, N.Y.; a stepdaughter, Jane Galluzzo of Greenwich; 11 grandchildren, 2 brothers and a sister.

        Helen Walker Puner, the first female editor at Fortune magazine and the author of four books, died Friday at Westchester County Medical Center in Valhalla, N.Y. She was 74 years old and lived in Ossining, N.Y.
        Mrs. Puner joined Fortune in 1936 and later became the first woman to be an editor there. She wrote a biography of Sigmund Freud, ''Freud: His Life and His Mind,'' published in 1947, and three books for children.
        Mrs. Puner is survived by her husband, Samuel; a son, Nicholas, of Chappaqua, N.Y.; two daughters, Maggie Levin of Toronto and Polly Puner of Manhattan; a brother, Marty Walker of Manhattan, and four grandchildren.

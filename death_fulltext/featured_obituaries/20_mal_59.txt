        Lloyd Mortimer Hastings Francke, director of information sales and services at The New York Times, died of cancer of the bladder yesterday at the New York University Medical Center. He was 61 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Francke began his career at The Times in 1950, working as a temporary office clerk promoting a fashion show. He became a telephone advertising solicitor in 1951, telephone supervisor in 1956, national sales manager in 1968 and index manager in 1972. Eight years later, he became director of information sales and services, which includes the Times index and educational services.
        He was born in San Francisco in 1927 and was taken to China by his mother when he was 6 months old. After the Japanese attack on Pearl Harbor, he was interned at a prisoner of war camp. He returned to the United States after the war.
        He is survived by his wife, Louise Francke, group manager in marketing services at The Times.
        A memorial service will be held on Friday at 1 P.M. at the Community Church, at 4 East 35th Street in Manhattan.

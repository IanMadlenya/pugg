
        John Sylvester Woodbridge, former controller of Pan American World Airways, died on March 1 at his home in Tucson, Ariz. He was 94 years old.
        Mr. Woodbridge was associated with Pan American for nearly 40 years as an accountant, treasurer and, in the early 1950's, as controller. He retired in the early 1970's.
        He was born in Shanghai, where his parents were both Presbyterian missionaries, and came to the United States at the age of 14. He graduated from Princeton University and joined the French Army in World War I as an ambulance driver. He was twice decorated for gallantry in action.
        A widower, he is survived by three daughters, Marshall Barron of New Haven, Nancy Langstaff of Cambridge, Mass., and Jeanie Donohue of Upland, Calif.; a son, John S. 2d, of Los Angeles; nine grandchildren, and 12 great-grandchildren.

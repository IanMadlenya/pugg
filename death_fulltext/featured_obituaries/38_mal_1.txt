        Dr. Joseph Dalven, a doctor who practiced medicine for more than 60 years, died on Tuesday at Maimonides Medical Center in Brooklyn after a short illness. He was 90 years old and lived in Brooklyn.
        He was medical director of the Sephardic Home for the Aged in Brooklyn for 35 years until he retired in 1985. In December 1988, he gave up his private practice in Brooklyn. He was chairman of the Metropolitan Jewish Geriatric Center's utilization review committee from 1974, when it was formed, until he retired from the panel in 1989.
        Dr. Dalven, who was born in Greece, came to the United States in 1909 at the age of 10. He graduated from New York University's Washington Square College and earned a medical degree from N.Y.U.
        In World War II, he served in England as a captain in the Army Medical Corps.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Josephine Schwartz; a son from a former marriage, Richard, of Oakland, Calif.; and two sisters, Dr. Rachel Dalven of Manhattan and Sophie Hazan of Brooklyn.

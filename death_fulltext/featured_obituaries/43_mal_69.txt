        Edward F. Buxton, an author and advertising executive, died on Tuesday at New England Memorial Hospital in Boston. He was 73 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        He died of a heart attack, his wife, Susan, said.
        Mr. Buxton was creative director of of several advertising agencies before starting Ad Day, a newsletter on advertising issues. He sold it to Ad Week and began Ad Business Report, where he was co-editor and publisher. He wrote several books, including ''Promise Them Anything,'' published in 1972 by Stein & Day.
        In addition to his wife, he is survived by two daughters, Leslie Moreno and Gail Buxton, both of Manhattan; a sister, Jean Evans of Portland, Ore., and three brothers, Bewster, of Madison, Wis., Winslow, of Seattle and Robert, of Portland, Ore.

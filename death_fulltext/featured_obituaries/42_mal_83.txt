        Dr. George E. Wheeler, a professor of biology at Brooklyn College, died on Sunday at Lenox Hill Hospital. He was 75 years old and lived in Manhattan. He died of a cerebral hemorrhage, a stepdaughter, R. Khela Ransier, said.
        Dr. Wheeler researched plant cells at the college and the Marine Biological Laboratory in Woods Hole, Mass. He was also active in animal and wildlife organizations. He retired in 1980.
        Dr. Wheeler was also a gymnast, representing the United States in the 1936 Olympics. He won 25 championships and was the all-around Amateur Athletic Union champion for five years in a row after the Berlin Games.
        He was born in Pittsburgh. He graduated from the University of Pittsburgh, where he also received an M.S. His Ph.D. was from Columbia University.
        Also surviving are his wife, Rose Piper, and a stepson, Steven Piper, of Manhattan.

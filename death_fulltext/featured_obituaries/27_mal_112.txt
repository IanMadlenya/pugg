        Charles F. Bischoff, a management consultant and retired vice president of Allied-Signal, died of a heart attack Friday at his home in West Caldwell, N.J. He was 61 years old.
        Mr. Bischoff joined Allied in 1963 and held several management jobs before retiring in 1986. At the time of his death, he was a partner and president of Goodrich & Sherwood, a Manhattan-based management-consulting firm.
        Born in Brooklyn, Mr. Bischoff graduated from Rutgers University. He was a trustee and elder in the First Presbyterian Church in Caldwell.
        Surviving are his wife, Marguerite; a son, Charles 3d, of Chatham, N.J.; a daughter, Anne Brindisi of West Caldwell, and two brothers, Lawrence, of Caldwell, and Robert, of El Dorado, Kan.

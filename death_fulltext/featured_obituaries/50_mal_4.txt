
        George L. Ginsberg, a psychoanalyst who was president of the New York Psychiatric Association, died on Tuesday at Bellevue Hospital Center. He was 56 years old and lived in Bronxville, N.Y.
        He died of a heart attack, his family said.
        Dr. Ginsberg was a professor of clinical psychiatry and director of graduate education in psychiatry at New York University Medical Center. He was also chairman of the Area II Council of the American Psychiatric Association.
        He was born in Chicago, graduated from Brown, received his medical degree from the College of Physicians and Surgeons of Columbia University and graduated from the New York Psychoanalytic Institute.
        Surviving Dr. Ginsberg are his wife, the former Fritze Pedersen; a daughter, Malina, of Boston; three sons, Jarl, of New Haven, Erik, of Chicago, and Gorm, of Manhattan, and two brothers, Michael, of Naples, Fla., and Lawrance, of Andover, Mass.

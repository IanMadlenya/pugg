
        Hubert M. Blalock Jr., a sociologist at the University of Washington who was internationally known for his work on statistical research methods, died of cancer on Feb. 8 at a nursing center in Bellevue, Wash. He was 64 years old.
        Mr. Blalock joined the university as a professor of sociology in 1971 and retired in 1989. He was president of the American Sociological Association in 1978-79 and was a member of the National Academy of Sciences.
        His textbook "Social Statistics" is widely used by social science students.
        He is surived by his wife, Ann, who collaborated on several of his books; two daughters, a son and three grandchildren.

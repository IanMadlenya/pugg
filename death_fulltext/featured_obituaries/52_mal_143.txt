
        Robert Humphrey, a chemist, metallurgical engineer and founding chairman of Bayside Controls, an aerospace manufacturer, died Monday at Greenwich Hospital in Connecticut. He was 88 years old and lived in Pelham, N.Y.
        Mr. Humphrey died of congestive heart failure, his son-in-law, Konrad Matthaei, said.
        Mr. Humphrey founded the company in Whitestone, N.Y. in 1952 and sold his interest in it in 1984, when he retired.
        He was a past president of the American Society of Metallurgists.
        He was born in Mechanicville, N.Y., and graduated from Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute in Troy, N.Y. He received a master's degree in chemistry from Carnegie-Mellon University in Pittsburgh in 1926, and a Ph.D. from Columbia in 1928, where he also taught as an associate professor.
        Mr. Humphrey is survived by his wife of 61 years, the former Gladys Humphrey; a brother, Martin, of Amsterdam, N.Y.; a sister, Mildred, of Pelham; twin daughters, Gay Matthaei of Manhattan, and Jewel Grutman of Greenwich; and four grandchildren.

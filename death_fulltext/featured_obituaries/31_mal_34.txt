        Frank Gulden Jr., retired chairman and president of Gulden Mustard Company, Inc., a division of American Home Products Corporation, died Wednesday at Southside Hospital in Bay Shore, L.I., after a brief illness. He was 82 years old and lived in Bay Shore.
        Mr. Gulden, a native New Yorker, joined the company, which was established by his grandfather in 1862, in 1931 after attending Dartmouth. He retired in 1960, the year the company was sold to American Home Products. Mr. Gulden assumed the posts of president and chairman following the retirement of his father in the 1940's.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Louise Guastavino; two daughters, Barbara Black of Kensington, Md., and Louise Henriksen of Islip, L.I.; a son, Frank 3d, of Fairfield, Conn.; nine grandchildren, and one great-grandson.

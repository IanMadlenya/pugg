        Wilfred B. Doner, founder of W. .B. Doner & Company, an advertising agency based in Detroit, died Thursday, apparently of a cerebral hemorrhage, at his home in Birmingham, Mich. He was 75 years old.
        Mr. Doner, a 1936 graduate of the University of Wisconsin, established the agency in 1937. It grew from two employees to 550 today in nine offices in the United States, Canada and Britain.
        At his death Mr. Doner was chairman of the agency's executive committee.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Rolla Jacobs; three daughters, Judith Berne of West Bloomfield, Mich., Mary Baron of Manhattan and Beth Wexner of Chicago; three sons, Frederick, of Manhattan, Randolph Friedman of Farmington Hills, Mich., and Dean Friedman of Franklin, Mich.; a brother, Milton, and a sister, Alice Burnes, both of Southfield, Mich., and 13 grandchildren.

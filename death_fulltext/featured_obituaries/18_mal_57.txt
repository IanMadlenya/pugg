        Aaron Solomon, a New York lawyer who specialized in labor relations, died of a heart attack yesterday at New York University Hospital. He was 80 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Solomon was a former counsel for the Association of Private Hospitals, a longtime member of the American Arbitration Association, and a founder of Long Island Jewish Medical Center.
        He was past president and trustee of Jewish Community Services of Long Island, a member of the board of governors of the Anti-Defamation League, a member of the United Hospital Fund Manpower Commission, and a vice president and trustee of the Jewish Association for the Aged.
        A native of Newark, he attended Columbia University and received his LL.B. degree from Brooklyn Law School.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Eleanor Konigsberg; a son, Stephen, of Brooklyn; two daughters, Jane Zalkin of Martinsville, N.J., and Dina Ruth Goldman of Roslyn, L.I., and four grandchildren.

        Dr. John F. Grady, a retired surgeon, died of congestive heart failure Tuesday at his home in Greenwich, Conn. He was 87 years old. He practiced in Manhattan from 1933 to 1974, when he retired. He had been an attending surgeon at the French Hospital in Manhattan and a director of the French Polyclinic Hospital. Dr. Grady, who was born in Jersey City, graduated from Fordham University and the Columbia University College of Physicians and Surgeons.
        He is survived by his wife, Helen Marie; three daughters, Mary Keith of Groton, Conn., and Elizabeth McKay and Helen Marie Grady, both of Greenwich, and five grandchildren.

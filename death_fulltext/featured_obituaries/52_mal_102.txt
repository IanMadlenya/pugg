
        Kenneth Lothaire Isaacs, an investment banker and former chairman of Massachusetts Investors Trust, died yesterday at Massachusetts General Hospital in Boston. He was 86 years old and lived in Boston.
        He died of a stroke, said his son, Kenneth.
        Mr. Isaacs joined Massachusetts Investors Trust, one of the nation's oldest mutual funds, in 1936 after having been assistant to the controller of Cornell University and a private investor. He retired as chairman in 1969. He remained a limited partner in the advisory group, Massachusetts Financial Services, until it was sold in 1982.
        Mr. Isaacs, a graduate of Lehigh University and Harvard Business School, was also a former director of General Public Utilities Corporation, Phelps Dodge Corporation, Southern Pacific Company, the Exchange Fund of Boston and the Canada General Fund.
        He was a trustee of the Suffolk Franklin Savings Bank, The Children's Hospital in Boston and of Lehigh, which awarded him an honorary degree of doctor of laws in 1965.
        In addition to his son, Kenneth of Walpole, Mass., he is survived by his wife, Helen; a daughter, Anne Merwin of Baltimore, and five grandchildren.

        Samuel Reid Sutphin, a retired vice president of the Scott Paper Company, died of cancer Tuesday at Methodist Hospital in Indianapolis. He was 76 years old and maintained residences in Zionsville, Ind., and Manhattan.
        Mr. Sutphin, who was born in Indianapolis, received a bachelor's degree from Yale and studied at the Institute of Paper Chemistry in Appleton, Wis. He was associated with family paper businesses for many years before 1965, when they were acquired by Scott and he became an officer of that company, serving until 1969. He remained a Scott director until 1982.
        He served in the United States Navy for five years in World War II, emerging as a commander and winning the Legion of Merit for combat duty at Okinawa.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Lisa Spilman; a brother, Dudley, of Indianapolis; a son, Samuel 2d, of Indianapolis; a daughter, Agatha Barclay of Potomac, Md., and two grandchildren.

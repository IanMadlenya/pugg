        Earl L. Sydnor, an actor, died of lung cancer on Sunday. He was 81 years old and lived in New York City.
        Mr. Sydnor made his stage debut with the Works Progress Administration Federal Theater Project during the Depression. He appeared on Broadway in ''Cabin in the Sky,'' ''The Skin of Our Teeth,'' ''Detective Story,'' ''Emperor Jones'' and ''First Monday in October.''
        He appeared on television in ''The Defenders,'' among other shows, and was a familiar figure in commercials. His film credits include ''Funny Girl'' and ''Requiem for a Heavyweight.''
        He is survived by his wife, Lucille, and a sister, Joan Walker, of Teaneck, N.J.

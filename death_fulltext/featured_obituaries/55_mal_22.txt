
        Donald Day, an author and editor who wrote or compiled biographies of Will Rogers, three American Presidents and other historical figures, died on July 22 at the Mediplex Nursing Home in Westport, Conn. He was 92 years old.
        He died of complications from pneumonia and other illnesses, said his wife, Clara.
        Mr. Reed was born in Millreed, Tex., and began his literary career as an editor of The Southwest Review. He wrote frequently about Texas and its lore throughout his life.
        In the late 1940's, Mr. Day became West Coast editor for Readers Digest, and remained with the publication until the early 1960's. During those years, he wrote the biography "Will Rogers," and compiled three collections of Rogers's writings.
        Mr. Day also compiled books about Theodore Roosevelt, Woodrow Wilson, Franklin D. Roosevelt, Sam Houston and the 19th-century writer and humorist Josh Billings. He also published a history of Texas and a history of relations between the sexes.
        In addition to his wife, of Manhattan, he is survived by a daughter, Dorothy Ullom of Corpus Christi, Tex.; a stepson, Michael D. Feigin of Westport, Conn.; a sister, Flavia Swearangin of Durand, Okla., and several grandchildren.

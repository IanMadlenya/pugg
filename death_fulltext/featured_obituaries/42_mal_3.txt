        Milton Filker, retired president of Council 52 of the American Federation of State, County and Municipal Employees in Jersey City, died on Thursday at his home in Jersey City. He was 73 years old.
        Mr. Filker died of lung cancer, his daughter, Judith Carluccio, said.
        Mr. Filker was a native New Yorker. He was re-elected nine times after becoming the council president in 1973. He had been a regional director of the United Shoe Workers of America and an organizer for the Congress of Industrial Organizations. He retired in 1981.
        Mr. Filker is survived by his wife, Sophie; two daughters, Judith, of Toms River, and Elaine Sheffler of Tenafly; two sisters, Elsie Albertine of Belleville, and Estelle Greenberg of Passaic; a brother, David, of Atlanta, and four grandchildren.

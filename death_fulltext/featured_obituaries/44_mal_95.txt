        Larry Forde, a theatrical director and production stage manager, died on Aug. 23 at Lenox Hill Hospital. He was 57 years old and lived in Manhattan. His family said he had died of liver failure. Mr. Forde directed the Off Broadway musical ''Oil City Symphony.'' He directed Mark Connelly's ''Stitch in Time'' on Broadway and the national companies of ''Agnes of God.'' For regional, stock and dinner theaters, he directed shows including ''1776,'' ''Grease,'' ''The Diary of Anne Frank'' and ''Inherit the Wind.''
        Mr. Forde was the stage manager of more than 25 Broadway productions, among them ''The Rothschilds,'' ''1776,'' ''The Ritz'' and ''Grease.''
        He is survived by his father, Jack, of Bridgeport, Conn., and a sister, Jackie Lamonte of Copperas Cove, Tex.

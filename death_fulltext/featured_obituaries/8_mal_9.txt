        Dr. Henry D. Fearon, a retired cardiologist and former chief of medicine at Methodist Hospital in Brooklyn, died of cancer Sunday at New Milford (Conn.) Hospital. A resident of Washington, Conn., he was 83 years old.
        Dr. Fearon, a 1927 graduate of the Syracuse University Medical School, founded the first cardiac clinic at the hospital, where he was chief of medicine from 1953 to 1968. In 1968, he moved his practice from Brooklyn to Washington, Conn. He retired about 10 years ago.
        He is survived by his wife, Frances; three sons, the Rev. Dr. H. Dana Fearon 3d of Lawrenceville, N.J., Dr. Richard Fearon of New Haven and Dr. Douglas Fearon of Baltimore; two brothers, Robert, of Oneida, N.Y., and Charles, of Hilton Head, S.C., and seven grandchildren.

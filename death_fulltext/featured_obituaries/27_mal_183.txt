        Irving Usen, president of O'Donnell-Usen Fisheries, one of the country's largest independent seafood processors, died of kidney failure Sunday at Brigham and Women's Hospital in Boston. He was 92 years old and lived in Newton, Mass.
        Mr. Usen, a native of Boston, founded Usen Fisheries after his discharge from the Army in World War I. In 1930 it merged with the O'Donnell Fishery Company. In 1945 he founded the National Fisheries Institute in Washington and was its first president.In 1964 he was knighted by the King of Denmark for his contribution to that nation's fishing industry.
        Mr. Usen was also a founding member of Brandeis University, Waltham, Mass., and was the principal donor to the Marine Biology Laboratory at the University of Jacksonville in Florida. He was a trustee of the New England Aquarium, the Boston Museum of Fine Arts and the Boston Symphony Orchestra.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Edyth Sharaf; two sons, Robert of Brookline, Mass., and Richard of Lexington, Mass.; and four grandchildren. A sister, Tye Wolf, and two brothers, Hyman and George Trilling, all of Brookline, also survive.

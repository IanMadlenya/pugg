        John L. Summers, the squash and tennis coach at Massachusetts Institute of Technology from 1930 to 1957 and the first national professional squash champion, died Dec. 21 in Holliston, Mass. He was 95 years old.
        Mr. Summers, who was born in England, came to the United States in 1911. He first won the national professional squash championship in 1930, then won it in 1931, 1932 and 1934.
        Mr. Summers is survived by his wife, Wilhelmina; five sons, John, of Keene, N.H., William and David, of Holliston, Charles, of Boxborough, Mass., and Frank, of Scotts Valley, Calif.; 10 grandchildren, and 10 great-grandchildren.

        Charles J. Buesing, a retired sales executive and longtime trustee of the American Cancer Society, died of emphysema Saturday at Indian River Memorial Hospital in Indian River Shores, Fla., where he lived. He was 79.
        A New Jersey resident for 74 years, Mr. Buesing was a sales executive with the Mutual Life Insurance Company of New York before he retired in 1974. He served as president of the American Cancer Society's New Jersey division, and was a trustee for 40 years. For 27 years he was a national director of the American Cancer Society.
        Mr. Buesing had an honorary doctorate degree from Monmouth College.
        Surviving are his wife, Ann, of Vero Beach, Fla.; two sons, Guy, of Rye, N.H., and Gregory, of Wayland, Mass; a daughter, Virginia Kreuzberger of Woodland Hills, Calif.; 10 grandchildren, and 6 great-grandchildren.

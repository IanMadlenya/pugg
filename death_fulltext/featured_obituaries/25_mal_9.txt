        Dr. Eugene Alvah Staley, an educator and international economist, died of lymph cancer Tuesday at the Stanford University Hospital in Palo Alto, Calif. He was 82 years old and lived in Palo Alto.
        Dr. Staley, who received his Ph.D. in economics from the University of Chicago in 1928, was a professor at the International Development Education Center Stanford from 1965 until 1968. He was an economist with the Stanford Research Institute from 1950 to 1968.
        He is survived by his wife, Phyllis; a daughter, Pamela Herr, of Palo Alto; a son, Thomas, of Guilford, Me., and five grandchildren.

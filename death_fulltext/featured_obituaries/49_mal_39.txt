
        Dr. William C. Clyne, a former staff president of St. Joseph's Medical Center in Yonkers, died on Sunday at the center. He was 81 years old and lived in the Riverdale section of the Bronx.
        He died of cancer, the center said.
        Dr. Clyne, a specialist in internal medicine and chest diseases, headed St. Joseph's medical staff from 1972 to 1974. He maintained a private practice in the Kingsbridge section of the Bronx from 1944 to 1988.
        He was born in Clara in County Offaly, Ireland, and received his medical degree from University College in Dublin. He came to the United States in 1939 and for several years was a doctor at the Frances Schervier Home and Hospital in Riverdale.
        Dr. Clyne is survived by his wife, the former Kathryn Fitzgerald; two daughters, Kathryn, of Charlottesville, Va., and Mary Bayerle of Moretown, Vt.; two sons, William Jr. and John, both of Riverdale; a brother, Thomas, of Dublin, and two granddaughters.

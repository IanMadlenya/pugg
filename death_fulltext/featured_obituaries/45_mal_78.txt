        The Rev. Henry G. Bovenkerk, a Presbyterian minister and former missionary to Japan, died on Sunday at Muhlenberg Hospital in Plainfield, N.J. He was 85 years old and lived in Westfield, N.J.
        He died of renal failure, his wife, Helen, said.
        Mr. Bovenkerk graduated from Western Theological Seminary in 1930 and was sent to Japan by the Board of Foreign Missions of the Presbyterian Church. At the outbreak of World War II he was interned by the Japanese but was repatriated in 1942.
        In the 1950's he served on the board of the World Mission of the Reformed Church in America and later was director of finance of the Committee on World Literacy and Christian Literature in Asia. He retired in 1970 but later served on the staff of the Presbyterian Church in Westfield and as interim minister of the First Presbyterian Church in Elizabeth, N.J. He retired again in 1985.
        In addition to his wife, he is survived by two sons, Warren, of Ann Arbor, Mich., and Paul, of Los Angeles; two daughters, Ruth Davidson of Greenwich, Conn., and Adelle Hoh of York, Pa.; four stepchildren, Shirley Cloyes of Brooklyn, Gail Coles of Emmaus, Pa., Steven Cloyes of Wilton, Conn., and John Cloyes of Manhattan; two brothers, Edward, of Muskegon, Mich., and Harold, of Worthington, Ohio; two sisters, Elsie Braun of Southgate, Mich., and Anne Johnson, of Grosse Isle, Mich., and 12 grandchildren.

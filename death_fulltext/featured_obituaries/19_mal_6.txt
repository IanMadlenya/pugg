        Donald Erickson, a former editor in chief of Esquire magazine and more recently an editor with The New York Times Magazine, died of throat cancer yesterday at the Harkness Pavilion of Columbia-Presbyterian Medical Center. He was 56 years old and lived in Manhattan and Clinton Corners, N.Y.
        Mr. Erickson was on the Esquire staff from 1964 to 1979 and held a number of positions, including associate editor, managing editor, editor in chief and editorial director. Later he was the founding editor of The Dial, a magazine published by WNET (Channel 13).
        He joined The Times as a story editor of the magazine in 1984 and was on the staff at his death.
        Mr. Erickson, a native of Kansas City, Kan., was a 1953 graduate of Yale and was a Rhodes Scholar at Oxford from 1953 to 1955, receiving an M.A. in English language and literature.
        Before joining Esquire, Mr. Erickson was a writer and editor on Long Lines, the American Telephone & Telegraph Company employee publication, and the editor of the Cyanamid magazine, published by the American Cyanamid Company.
        Mr. Erickson is survived by his mother, Mildred Erickson of Overland Park, Kan.
        He will be buried at Old Quaker Cemetery in Clinton Corners, N.Y., where a graveside service will be conducted Saturday at 11 A.M. A memorial service will be held in New York City.

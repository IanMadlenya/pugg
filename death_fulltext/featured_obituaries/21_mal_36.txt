        Henry V. Leonard Jr., executive vice president of the General Motors Overseas Corporation and general manager of its Japanese branch, died of a heart attack Friday in Norwich, England, while on a business trip. He was 64 years old and lived in Tokyo.
        Mr. Leonard joined G.M. in 1947 and was with the company abroad for 36 years in 10 countries.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Doris Krentel; a daughter, Betsy Snedeker of Wilton, Conn.; a son, John, of Stamford, and two grandchildren.

        Abraham L. Zwerdling, a leading labor lawyer and civil rights advocate, died Sunday of leukemia. He was 73.
        A graduate of the University of Michigan Law School, Mr. Zwerdling was a key figure in the growth of public sector labor law. He was an early adviser to Walter P. Reuther, president of the United Automobile Workers, and served as general counsel to the American Federation of State, County, and Municipal Employees from 1971 to 1980.
        In 1980, Mr. Zwerdling established a labor law firm in Washington. He was special counsel to the New York Civil Service Employees Association, A.F.S.C.M.E. Local 1000, in 1986 and 1987.
        He also a member of the board of directors of the American Arbitration Association, a committee chairman in the Labor Relations Law section of the American Bar Association and an adjunct professor of labor law at the Georgetown University Law Center.
        Mr. Zwerdling was a lifetime member of the National Association for the Advancement of Colored People.
        In 1969, as president of the Detroit Board of Education, Mr. Zwerdling presided over a controversial racial integration plan. This plan, eventually upheld by the courts, led to his recall and removal from the board.
        Mr. Zwerdling is survived by his wife, Thelma; two children, and five grandchildren.

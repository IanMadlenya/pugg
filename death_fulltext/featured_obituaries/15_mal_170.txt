        Ike Gellis, sports editor of The New York Post for 30 years until his retirement in 1978, died yesterday at Nyack Hospital after a long illness. He was 80 years old.
        Mr. Gellis, who lived in Nyack, began his career at The Post in 1928, as a sports reporter covering soccer. He later wrote frequently about boxing before becoming sports editor.
        He is survived by a brother, Abner; a son, Harry, of Richmond, Va.; twin daughters, Dorothy Frishman and Nancy Goldsmith, both of Nyack, and six grandchildren.

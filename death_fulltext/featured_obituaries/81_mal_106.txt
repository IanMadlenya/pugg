
        Edward C. Perry, a financial adviser to the Lily Tulip Company and Encyclopedia Britanica, died on Thursday at his home in Manhattan. He was 84.
        The cause was esophageal cancer, said his companion, Adolpho Sardina, who is the fashion designer known as Adolpho.
        Mr. Perry was born and raised in Newark and received his bachelor's degree from Rutgers University. In the late 1940's, he worked as a comptroller for the Lily Tulip Company, a manufacturer of disposable paper cups and other paper products. He left the company in the early 1950's and worked for several years as a comptroller and financial adviser for Encyclopedia Britanica. After leaving, he worked as a financial adviser for Baptist Affiliated Charities in Manhattan, until he retired in 1974
        He is survived by Mr. Sardina and by his niece, Joy MacLennan, of Short Hills, N.J.
        Correction:  October 26, 1993, Tuesday
        An obituary on Saturday about Edward C. Perry, a financial adviser who died on Thursday in Manhattan, misspelled the names of a company for which he worked, and of his companion. The company is the Encyclopaedia Britannica. His companion is Adolfo Sardina, the fashion designer known as Adolfo.

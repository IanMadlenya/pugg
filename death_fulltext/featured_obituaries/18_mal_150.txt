        Francis C. Gannon, a retired ship-supply executive, died of a heart attack Tuesday at the Princeton (N.J.) Medical Center. He was 76 years old and lived in Jamesburg, N.J.
        Mr. Gannon was president of the New York Marine Distributing Company from its founding after World War II until his retirement in 1971. Earlier, he had been with Gibbs & Cox, American Export Lines and the Tankers Company. Mr. Gannon specialized in arranging supplies for merchant ships in ports around the world.
        He was born in Brooklyn. Mr. Gannon attended Pratt Institute.
        He spent two tours of duty abroad on missions to assist developing countries, in 1967 and 1978 in Turkey and in 1975 in Sierra Leone, under the auspices of the Agency for International Development.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Jane Upton; two daughters, Anne Farley of Freeport, L.I., and Mary Jane Milner of Norwich, N.Y.; a son, Francis, of Houston; two sisters, Anne Harker of New Hyde Park, L.I., and Ellen Modreska of Palm Bay, Fla.; two brothers, John, of Sun City, Ariz., and Richard, of Haubstadt, Ind., and 12 grandchildren.

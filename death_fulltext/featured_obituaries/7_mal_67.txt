        Arthur Schwarzschild, the chairman of the Physics Department of the Brookhaven National Laboratory in Upton, L.I., died of colon cancer Thursday at his home in Patchogue, L.I. He was 57 years old.
        Mr. Schwarzschild was an experimental scientist in the field of high-energy physics. He had been employed at the Brookhaven laboratories since 1958 and had been chairman of the Physics Department for the last six years.
        He was born in Brooklyn and received his doctorate in physics from Columbia University in 1957.
        He stayed on at Columbia for a year doing research before joining the laboratory.
        He is survived by his wife, Liliane; his mother, Helen, of Brooklyn; two sons, Marc of Highland Park, N.J., and Roger of Amherst, Mass.; a daughter, Karen Simon, of St. Louis, and three grandchildren.

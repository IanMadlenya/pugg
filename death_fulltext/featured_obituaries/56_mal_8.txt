
        Hendrick Christian Ahlers, a retired executive in the wholesale dairy and cooking-oil business, died on Thursday at Meridian-The Woodland Care Center in Plainfield, N.J. He was 73 years old and lived in Chatham, N.J.
        Mr. Ahlers, who was president of the New York Mercantile Exchange at the age of 27, was vice president and chief executive of Carl Ahlers Inc. of New York City, a family business founded in 1898. The company was a bulk distributor of food products. Mr. Ahlers retired when the company closed in 1975.
        He had been a Chatham borough councilman, and had served on the borough's Board of Education and Planning Board.
        Mr. Ahlers is survived by his wife, the former Dorothy Bailey; two sons, Hendrick C. Jr., and Peter B., both of Summit, N.J.; a brother, Harold, of Rumson, N.J., and four granddaughters.

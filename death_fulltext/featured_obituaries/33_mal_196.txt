        Murray Levy, a clothing manufacturer and philanthropist, died of cancer Sunday at his home in Rye, N.Y. He was 77 years old.
        Mr. Levy was active in the women's garment industry in Manhattan for more than 60 years. He founded Don Sophisticates, which he sold in 1974. Two years later he founded and became chairman of Kenar Enterprises.
        He supported the United Hebrew Geriatric Center and Mount Sinai Medical Center.
        He is survived by his wife, Sally; a daughter, Donna Feldman of Weston, Mass.; a brother, Albert, of Southern Pines, N.C.; two sisters, Ann Cohn of Hallandale, Fla., and Mathilda Levin of North Miami Beach, Fla., and two grandchildren.

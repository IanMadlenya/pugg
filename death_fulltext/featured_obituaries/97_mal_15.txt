
        Harrison R. Wesson, a retired surgeon and former president of the Mayo Clinic Alumni Association, died on Sunday at the Cherry Nursing Home in Montclair, N.J. Dr. Wesson, who lived in Montclair for 56 years, was 89.
        The cause of death was cardiac arrest, said his daughter Sally W. Cogan.
        Dr. Wesson, who practiced general surgery from 1939 until his retirement in 1979, was director of the department of surgery and the president of the staff at Mountainside Hospital in Montclair.
        He was born in Lawrenceville, Va., and graduated from the College of William and Mary in 1925. He received his medical degree from the University of Virginia Medical School in 1929.
        In 1932, he began a fellowship in surgery at the Mayo Clinic, serving as first assistant to William and Charles Mayo until 1937.
        Dr. Wesson was a member of the board of governors of the American College of Surgeons, president of the Associated Physicians of Montclair and Vicinity, president of the Clinical Society of the Oranges, director of the department of surgery for Community Hospital in Montclair and Essex County Overlook Hospital in Cedar Grove, N.J.
        In addition to Mrs. Cogan, also of Montclair, he is survived by his wife, the former Dorcas Freeman, and four other children: Donald H. Wesson of Longmeadow, Mass.; Wendy W. Benchley of Princeton, N.J.; Bruce F. Wesson of Summit, N.J.; Michael F. Wesson of Wyckoff, N.J.; a sister, Mildred Lewis of Stony Creek, Va., and 18 grandchildren.

        J. Henry Neale, a lawyer and banker, died of a stroke Monday at White Plains Hospital. He was 84 years old and lived in Scarsdale.
        A graduate of Amherst College and Harvard Law School, Mr. Neale was associated with the Scarsdale National Bank and Trust Company from 1946 to 1976, serving as president and later chairman. He was president of the State Bankers Association from 1959 to 1960 and of the Westchester County Bankers Association from 1949 to 1952.
        He served as general counsel to the Navy Department during World War II and was awarded the Legion of Merit. He was president of the New York State Bar Association from 1967 to 1968 and of the Westchester County Bar Association from 1953 to 1955. He was also on the boards of the Scarsdale Foundation, the Y.W.C.A. of White Plains and Central Westchester, the Legal Aid Society and White Plains Hospital.
        He is survived by his wife, Laura; two sons, Edwin, of Chappaqua, and J. Henry Jr., of Scarsdale; two brothers, Russell, of Nashville, and Edwin, of Philadelphia, and four grandchildren.

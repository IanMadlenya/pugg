
        Morris B. Rotman, a founder and former head of Harshe-Rotman & Druck, the large independent New York public relations firm, died on Sunday in Chicago. He was 85 and lived in Rancho Mirage, Calif., and Chicago.
        Mr. Rotman was chairman and chief executive of Harshe-Rotman & Druck from 1966 to 1981.
        It merged in the early 1980's with Ruder & Finn, forming the larger firm of Ruder Finn & Rotman, with headquarters in New York and Chicago. Mr. Rotman was its president until he resigned in 1988.
        He was an adjunct professor of communications at the College of the Desert in Palm Desert, Calif. His writings include the book ''Opportunities in Public Relations Careers'' (McGraw-Hill Trade, 1995).
        Mr. Rotman was born in Chicago and attended Northwestern University. He served in the Army Air Forces in the United States during World War II.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Sylvia Sugar; a daughter, Betty Ruth Rotman; two sons, Jesse and Richard Everett; and four grandchildren.


        Frank J. Graziano, a former chairman and chief executive of the Crompton & Knowles Corporation, died Oct. 27 at Memorial Sloan-Kettering Cancer Center in New York. He was 79.
        The cause of death was multiple myeloma, a form of bone-marrow cancer, a family spokesman said.
        Mr. Graziano lived primarily in Short Hills, N.J.
        In 1969, he was named president and chief executive of Crompton & Knowles, which he helped transform from a maker of textiles and machinery into a marketer of specialty chemicals. He led the company until his retirement in 1986.
        Earlier, he was vice president of the Monarch Machine Tool Company in Ohio; director of operations at the Continental Copper and Steel Company in New York and senior vice president at the American Can Company in Greenwich, Conn.
        Mr. Graziano served on the boards of the A.D.T. Corporation, the M & B Railroad and the New Jersey Chamber of Commerce. He was also board chairman of Webb Institute, a college of naval architecture and engineering in Glen Cove, N.Y.
        Mr. Graziano, who was born in Jersey City, graduated from the United States Naval Academy in 1940 with a bachelor's degree in electrical engineering. After service in the Navy during World War II, he earned a master's degree in mechanical engineering from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology.
        Mr. Graziano is survived by his wife, the former Anne D. Dysart, and by four children by his deceased first wife, Joanne D. Divisek: James of Princeton, N.J.; Joseph of Palos Verdes, Calif.; David of New York, and Gayle Sciorilli of Summit, N.J. He is also survived by a stepdaughter, Elisabeth Walkely of Nashville; his mother, Catherine Graziano, of Fair Lawn, N.J., three sisters, two brothers and four grandchildren.

        James V. Siena, a former Deputy Assistant Secretary of Defense and an executive at the American Institute of Architects, died of cancer last Sunday at Georgetown University Hospital in Washington. He was 55 years old and lived in Washington.
        Mr. Siena worked in the Defense Department in the Carter Administration from 1977 to 1981 with responsibility for European affairs. Before that, he was general counsel for Stanford University. From 1967 to 1969, he was Deputy Under Secretary of the Army for International Affairs. He served as counsel for the Office of Economic Opportunity from 1964 to 1965, in the development of President Johnson's anti-poverty program.
        When he left the Government, he practiced law in Washington before joining the American Association of Architects, of which he was general counsel and executive for government affairs.
        Born in Cleveland, Mr. Siena graduated from Case Western Reserve University and Stanford University Law School, where he was president of the Law Review.
        Mr. Siena was a member of the Stanford Law School Board of Visitors, the American Bar Association and the bars of the District of Columbia and California.
        He is survived by his wife, Jane Slate Siena; two daughters, Katherine J. S. Hubley of Silver Spring, Md., and Margaret J. Siena of Manhattan; a son, James of Manhattan; a sister, Virginia Bivona of Dallas, and two grandchildren.

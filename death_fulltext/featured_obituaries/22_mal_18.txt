        Thomas R. Hardart, former president and chairman of the Horn & Hardart Company, originators of the Automat, the coin-operated cafeterias, died of prostate cancer yesterday at his home in Bronxville. He was 70 years old.
        Mr. Hardart was a grandson of Frank Hardart Sr., who helped found what could be called the first fast-food chain. He became associated with the company in 1938 and was its president and chairman from 1967 to 1972. The company, based in Las Vegas, operates 200 restaurants nationwide - including one remaining Automat in Manhattan at Third Avenue and 42d Street - and owns Hanover House, the direct-marketing concern.
        Mr. Hardart was also a lawyer, serving as counsel to Smith, Ranscht, Pollock, Manos & Connors, a law firm in White Plains, N.Y.
        Mr. Hardart, who was born in Manhattan, graduated from the University of Notre Dame in 1938 and Columbia Law School in 1948.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Rosemary Dunne; two sons, Thomas and Paul, both of Manhattan; four daughters, Madeline, of Brookline, Mass., Rosemary Wylie of Wilmette, Ill., and Anne and Marcella, both of Manhattan; a brother, Dr. Frank, of Bronxville; a sister, Evelyn Murray of Greenwich, Conn., and three grandchildren.

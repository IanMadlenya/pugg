        Selden Jerome Dickinson, a pediatric surgeon, died of lung cancer yesterday at his home in Manhattan. He was 62 years old.
        Dr. Dickinson was affiliated with Lenox Hill Hospital in Manhattan, where he maintained a private practice. He was also affiliated with Brooklyn Hospital and the medical department of Blue Cross/Blue Shield in Manhattan.
        He was a fellow of the American College of Surgeons, a member and past president of the Canterbury Choral Society and a contributing editor of the Journal of Pediatric Surgery.
        Dr. Dickinson graduated from Harvard College, where he also received a master's degree, and received his medical degree from the Columbia University College of Physicians and Surgeons.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Jeanne D. Richmond; two sons, Selden, of Montclair, N.J., and Robert, of Nantucket, Mass.; two daughters, Anne, of Nantucket, and Juliet, of Manhattan; a sister, Mary Herbert of Grosse Point, Mich., and a grandson.

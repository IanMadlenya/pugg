
        Richard A. Brown, a sales executive at The New York Times, died on Wednesday at the Mount Sinai Medical Center in Manhattan. He was 54 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        He died of cancer, his family said.
        Mr. Brown, who started at the Times as a mail room clerk 33 years ago, was promoted this year to director of sales administration for the advertising and circulation departments.
        Before that he was the director of financial services, in charge of accounts payable, contracts, credit, advertising billing and circulation accounting.
        Mr. Brown was born in Brooklyn. He graduated from Brooklyn College in 1960 and earned an accounting degree at the New York Institute of Technology in 1978.
        He is survived by two sisters, Susan Brown of Wilton, Conn., and Arlene Brown of Freeport, L.I.

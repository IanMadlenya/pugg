
        John C. Bradford, a former art director in the recording and publishing business, died on Dec. 30 at St. Vincent's Hospital. He was 73 and had homes in Manhattan and East Hampton, N.Y.
        The cause of death was lung cancer, said his nephew, Bradford Guthrie.
        From 1970 until he retired in 1987, Mr. Bradford was vice president and art director of Family Circle magazine.
        From 1949 until 1959, he was a designer and later was associate director of design at Marshall Field & Company in Chicago. In 1960, Mr. Bradford moved to New York to become art director of Rockmore Advertising, and in 1962 he was named art director of CBS-Columbia Records. From 1965 until 1970, he was art director for Harlan Quist Books.
        Mr. Bradford was a graduate of the University of Illinois, where he received a degree in graphic design and architecture. He served in the Army in Europe during World War II and received the Bronze Star.
        In addition to his nephew, Mr. Guthrie, of Englewood, Colo., Mr. Bradford is survived by a niece, Paula J. Guthrie of Aurora, Colo.

        Dr. Harold Aaron, formerly a medical consultant to Consumers Union Inc., which publishes the monthly Consumer Reports magazine, died of Parkinson's disease yesterday at his summer home on Fire Island. He was 82 years old and lived in Tenafly, N.J.
        Dr. Aaron was with Consumers Union from its inception until 1972 when he founded The Medical Letter, a semimonthly medical publication for doctors. He retired in 1985.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Riva Stocker; two children, Jenny Granata, of Tenafly, and Paul, of Manhattan; three grandchildren, and two sisters, Sylvia Victor of Leonia, N.J., and Viola Taft of Milburn, N.J.

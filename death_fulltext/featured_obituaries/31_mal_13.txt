        John W. Guilfoyle, a retired executive of the International Telephone and Telegraph Corporation, died of cancer Sunday at the New York Hospital-Cornell Medical Center. He was 67 years old and had homes in Manhattan and Manchester, Vt.
        Mr. Guilfoyle, a native of Burbank, Calif., joined I.T.T. in 1951. In 35 years with the company, he served in a number of executive capacities. He was appointed executive vice president of I.T.T. and president and chief executive of the I.T.T. Telecommunications Corporation in 1983 and became a company director in 1984. He retired in 1986.
        He had been group executive in Latin America for 10 years, and from 1966 to 1968 he was in charge of the company's Far East and Pacific Operations.
        He served as president of I.T.T. Europe Inc., and in 1978 and 1979 was in charge of the corporation's operations in Africa and the Middle East. He was elected a senior vice president in 1979.
        Mr. Guilfoyle was chairman of the board of Molloy College in Rockville Centre, L.I.
        In World War II, he was a member of the Army Air Corps, retiring as a captain. He was awarded the Silver Star, the Distinguished Service Cross, the Distinguished Flying Cross and the Air Medal with 14 clusters.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Shirley Ann Lumpp; a daughter, Patricia Ann Goldstein of Millington, N.J., and three grandchildren.

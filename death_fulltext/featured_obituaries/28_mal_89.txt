        Douglas F. Young, retired justice of the State Supreme Court, died of complications from Parkinson's disease Thursday at St. Francis Hospital, Flower Hill, L.I. He was 77 years old and lived in Sands Point, L.I.
        Justice Young was a member of the court from 1976 to 1983, when he retired. From 1965 to 1975 he was a judge of the Nassau County Court; from 1962 to 1963, he was a judge of the Nassau County District Court, and from 1946 to 1962, he practiced law in Baldwin and Hempstead, L.I.
        He was born in Zion, Ill., and graduated from Rutgers University and St. John's Law School.
        He is survived by his wife, Elizabeth Mount; a daughter, Constance Young of Astoria, Queens, and a sister, Jean M. Dougherty of Garden City, L.I.

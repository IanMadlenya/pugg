
        Milton H. Stern, a nationally known tax lawyer and a founding partner in one of New Jersey's biggest law firms, died on Monday at his home in Livingston, N.J. He was 68 years old.
        He died of leukemia, said a spokeswoman for the firm, Nancy Lasersohn.
        Mr. Stern was a partner in Hannoch Weisman, formed in 1960. He was its managing partner from its founding until 1975. New Jersey's second-largest law firm in terms of revenues, it originated in Newark and is now based in Roseland, with offices in Trenton and Washington.
        A native of Newark, Mr. Stern was a 1943 graduate of Lafayette College, where he majored in chemistry. He served in the Army in Europe, reaching the rank of first sergeant and winning the Bronze Star and four battle stars.
        In 1948, he earned his law degree at Columbia University Law School. There, he was a Harlan Fiske Stone Scholar and an editor of the Columbia Law Review. Focus on Family Businesses
        Specializing in tax techniques for planning estates, Mr. Stern advised business enterprises and their principals. He focused on family businesses.
        He contributed articles on the subject and wrote "Inside the Family-Held Business" (Harcourt Brace Jovanovich, 1985), "Advising Family Enterprises" (Harcourt, 1985), and "Inside the Family-Held Insurance Agency" (Insurance Foundation, 1987).
        Mr. Stern is survived by his wife, Joan, a management consultant; two teen-age daughters, Patricia and Ellen; three sons by a previous marriage, Andrew of Washington, Kenneth of Denver, and Thomas of Durham, N.C.; his mother, Sabina Lieberman of West Palm Beach, Fla.; two sisters, Hetty Kruvant of South Orange, N.J., and Mayfair Gleaner of Boca Raton, Fla., and four grandchildren.

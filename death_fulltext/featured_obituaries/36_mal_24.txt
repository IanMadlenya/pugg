        Jay Wells, who founded Wells Television, which became the country's largest supplier of rental television sets to hotels and hospitals, died of a heart attack on Tuesday at his home in Scarsdale, N.Y. He was 73 years old.
        Mr. Wells was born in Cleveland and was a Phi Beta Kappa graduate of Western Reserve, now Case Western Reserve University. He founded Ski magazine in 1940 and sold it the following year. During World War II he published magazines distributed in military camps and bases.
        In 1946 Mr. Wells began renting coin-operated radio sets, and later television sets, to hotels and hospitals. He sold the company to the American Hospital Supply Corporation in 1973. Two years later he became semi-retired, pursuing charitable and personal-investment interests until his death.
        Mr. Wells was a commissioner of the Adirondack Park Agency in the late 1970's. He was a director of United Brands for the last 15 years.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Dotty Weiss; a son, Richard, of Chappaqua, N.Y.; two daughters, Katherine and Beth Elkin, both of Manhattan; a brother, Arnold, of Manhattan, and four grandchildren.

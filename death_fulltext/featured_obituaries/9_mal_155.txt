        Junius P. Wilson Jr., a lawyer who helped found the Hofstra University Law School, died of a heart attack Wednesday at his home in Manorville, L.I. He was 82 years old.
        Mr. Wilson, who practiced in Mineola, L.I., for many years before moving his office to Manorville, served on a special committee that worked to establish the law school, which opened in 1970. He was a former trustee of Hofstra.
        Mr. Wilson, a graduate of the University of Virginia and Yale Law School, worked for the Justice Department as a special prosecutor in the early 1930's. In World War II he served in the Office of Strategic Services.
        He is survived by his wife, Mona; a son, Junius 3d, of Stone Mountain, Ga.; a daughter, Mona T. Beard of Honolulu, and four grandchildren.

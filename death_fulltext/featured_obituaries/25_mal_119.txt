        John Braswell, a co-director of the E.T.C. Company and the director of the experimental theater program at Sarah Lawrence College, died of AIDS on Wednesday at his family's home in Champaign, Ill. He was 51 years old and lived in Bronxville, N.Y.
        The E.T.C. Company is a repertory company and part of La Mama E.T.C., the experimental theater company in Greenwich Village.
        Mr. Braswell wrote several plays that were performed at E.T.C. and at Sarah Lawrence.
        Mr. Braswell was born in Paxton, Ill., graduated from the University of Illinois and earned a master's degree at Sarah Lawrence. He joined the faculty in 1969 and headed the workshop theater program.
        He is survived by his mother, Pearl, of Champaign, and a brother, Daniel, of Palm Beach, Fla.

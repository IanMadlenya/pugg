
        The Rev. Hamlin G. Tobey, a retired religious educator, died July 1 at Hartford Hospital in Connecticut. He was 85 years old and lived in Lakeville, Conn.
        He died of kidney failure, his family said.
        Mr. Tobey served for many years with the National Board of Christian Education of the Presbyterian Church, U.S.A., and later directed Christian education for 288 congregations in the presbyteries of New York City, Long Island and the Hudson Valley. After he retired in 1971, Mr. Tobey was interim minister at Presbyerian and Congregational churches in Amenia, N.Y., and Cornwall, Sharon and Salisbury, Conn.
        He received bachelor's and master's degrees from Boston University and a divinity degree from the San Francisco Theological Seminary.
        Mr. Tobey is survived by his wife, the former Kathrene McLandress; a son, Robert, of East Longmeadow, Mass.; two sisters, Rachael Hill of Rockland, Me., and Lois Loughman of Skowhegan, Me., and four grandchildren.


        Dr. Daniel S. Giddings, an orthopedic surgeon for almost 50 years, died on Tuesday at his home in Lawrence, L.I. He was 73 years old.
        He died of heart failure, said his daughter-in-law, Sarajane Giddings.
        After graduating from the Long Island College of Medicine in 1942, Dr. Giddings did his internship at Mount Sinai Hospital and his residency at the Hospital for Joint Diseases in Manhattan, where he remained an attending physician.
        For the last 30 years, he was also with the department of orthopedic surgery at Peninsula Hospital in Rockaway Beach, Queens, and was affiliated with Long Island Jewish Hospital and the South Shore division of St. John's Episcopal Hospital, where he was chief of orthopedics. He was also a former director of orthopedics at Creedmore State Hospital and taught at the Downstate Medical Center.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Renee Ross Bloomgarden; a son, Geoffrey, of Lawrence; a daughter, Wendy Gold of Great Neck, L.I., and two sisters, Anita Fendrick of Lawrence and Zelda Schoengold of Woodsburgh, L.I.

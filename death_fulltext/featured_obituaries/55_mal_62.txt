
        Robert Monzon, an audiologist who had one of the largest hearing aid practices in New York City, died on Wednesday at his home in Upper Grand View, N.Y. He was 65.
        He died of cancer, a spokesman for the family said.
        Mr. Monzon was born in Cuba and after serving in the United States Army during World War II attended evening classes to earn a bachelor's degree from the City College of New York and a master's degree in audiology from Teachers College. He was the chief executive of the Audionics Corporation at 60 East 42d Street in Manhattan.
        He is survived by his wife, Anna; a son, Dr. Gary Monzon of Portland, Me.; and a brother, Mario, of Nanuet, N.Y.

        Clyde A. Holbrook, Danforth Professor of Religion emeritus at Oberlin College, Ohio, an authority on Jonathan Edwards, the American theologian and philosopher, died Thursday, apparently of a heart attack, at his home in Oberlin. He was 77 years old.
        Professor Holbrook joined the Oberlin faculty in 1951 and served as chairman of the religion department for 24 years. In 1966 he received the Danforth Foundation's E. Harris Harbison Award for distinguished teaching.
        After his retirement in 1977 Professor Holbrook returned to Oberlin as distinguished visiting professor of religion in 1980-81 and 1984-85. He was the author and editor of a number of books.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Dorothy B. Wheeler; a daughter, Deborah Harville of LaCrosse, Wis.; two sons, Richard C. of Chicago and Arthur W. of Ottawa, Ontario; two sisters, Madge Burke of Greenfield, Mass., and Ruth Farnum of Colorado Springs, and seven grandchildren.

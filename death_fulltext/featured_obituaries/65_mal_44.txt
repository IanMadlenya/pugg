
        Leonard V. Haber, a retired architect and industrial designer, died on June 4 at his home in Manhattan. He was 80 years old.
        Mr. Haber died of heart failure, said a stepson, Robert F. Murchison.
        A native of New York City, Mr. Haber attended schools in Manhattan and studied art and design in Paris and Florence. He also studied fine arts at Yale, from which he graduated in 1936.
        After his studies, Mr. Haber taught fine arts at the College of William and Mary. While there, he also worked on the restoration of Colonial Williamsburg and served as an adviser to the Metropolitan Museum of Art in New York on the selection of paintings.
        After the war, Mr. Haber was one of the first designers of television sets for the Magnavox Corporation, for which he also designed radios and stereophonic systems.
        Mr. Haber's wife, the late Jane Coleman Murchison Haber, died last year. He is survived by three stepsons, Clint W. Murchison 3d, Burk C. Murchison and Robert F. Murchison, all of Dallas; a stepdaughter, Coke Anne Murchison Sanders of New York City; two sisters, Ruth Haber of New York City and Vivian Nelson of Houston, and 13 stepgrandchildren.


        Arnold S. Greenspan, a suburban real-estate developer, died on Wednesday at his home in Briarcliff Manor, N.Y. He was 71 years old.
        He died of cancer, his family said.
        He was a past president of the Westchester-Putnam Home Builders Association. He started in real-estate management and later developed shopping centers and single-family homes.
        Mr. Greenspan, a horseman, had served as president of the Rombout Hunt Club and was a columnist for the New York Horse Council's newsletter. He was a past president of the Briarcliff Conservation Council and of the Jonathan Lodge of B'nai B'rith.
        He graduated from New York University. During World War II he served in the Air Corps in Europe and Africa.
        Surviving are his wife of 40 years, the former Dolores Goodman; two daughters, Betsey Aronsonof Ossining, N.Y., and Bryna Greenspan of Amherst, Mass.; a brother, Leonard of Boca Raton, Fla., and one grandson.

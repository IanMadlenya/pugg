        Frances T. Pennoyer, the last surviving child of J. P. Morgan, died yesterday after a stroke at her home in Locust Valley, L.I. She was 92 years old.
        Mrs. Pennoyer was the third of the financier's four children. Born in Manhattan, she married Paul G. Pennoyer, a partner in the Manhattan law firm of White & Case, who died in 1970.
        She was a former board member of the Glen Cove, L.I., Visiting Nurses Association, a fellow of the Pierpont Morgan Library and a founding member of Green Vale (L.I.) School, a private day school.
        Surviving are two sons, Paul Jr., of Locust Valley and Robert, of Katonah, N.Y.; four daughters, Virginia Livermore of San Rafael, Calif., Frances Schilling of Woodside, Calif., Katherine O'Donnell of Marlborough, Mass. and Jesse Snyder of Greenwich, Conn.; 28 grandchildren and 32 great-grandchildren.

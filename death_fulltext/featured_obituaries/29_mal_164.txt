        Sol Halpern, a partner in the Manhattan accounting firm of Irving Kaufman & Company who served for the last five years as president of the Brooklyn School for Special Children, died of colon cancer Tuesday at his home in Brooklyn. He was 69 years old.
        Mr. Halpern, who was born in the Bronx, received an accounting degree from City College and a law degree from Brooklyn Law School.
        In World War II he served as an infantry lieutenant in the United States Army. He joined the Kaufman firm 45 years ago and was made a full partner in 1956.
        He was associated for more than 25 years with the Brooklyn school, a nonprofit, nonsectarian agency that cares for mentally retarded and multiply handicapped children. The school is affiliated with St. Vincent's Hospital.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Janet Berner; a sister, Irene Cohen of Brooklyn, and a brother, Wilfred, of Woodmere, L.I.

        Margit Van Leight-Frank, a clinical psychologist who was a founding member of the C. G. Jung Institute of New York, died Wednesday at her home in Weston, Conn. She was 91 years old.
        Mrs. Van Leight-Frank, who opened a practice in Manhattan in 1954, followed by a second in Weston, remained active until June of last year when illness forced her to retire.
        She was born in Berlin and began her analytical training with Professor Carl Gustav Jung in Zurich. She attended medical school for three years in London until the outbreak of World War II. Later she received a master's degree in philosophy at McGill University in Montreal.
        She was a member of the New York and the International Associations for Analytical Psychology, and a founding member, training analyst and board member of the C. G. Jung Institute of New York.
        She is survived by a daughter, Anne Marie Roth of Weston; a sister, Illa Jones of Naples, Fla.; a grandson, and two great-grandchildren.

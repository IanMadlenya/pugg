        Stephen Csoka, a Hungarian-born realist painter who taught art in New York for four decades, died Thursday at his home in Kings Park, L.I. He was 92 years old.
        Mr. Csoka, who was educated in Hungary, moved to the United States in 1934 and taught at the National Academy of Design, Hunter College and the Fashion Institute of Technology. His work, a vigorous, romantic brand of realist art, is in the British Museum, the Brooklyn Museum and the Library of Congress.
        Mr. Csoka is survived by his wife, Margaret, and two children, Frank and Clare.

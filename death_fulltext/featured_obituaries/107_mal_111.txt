
        Loree (Rip) Collins, a former New Jersey Assemblyman and Union County Republican chairman, died on Dec. 14 at Memorial Sloan-Kettering Cancer Center in Manhattan. He was 68 and lived in Summit, N.J.
        The cause was cancer, said a family spokeswoman, Margo Feiden.
        At his death, he was a special assistant in the New Jersey Division of Consumer Affairs.
        Mr. Collins served the United States Secretary of the Navy as an adviser on training policies during the Reagan Administration. He was also a trustee of the Naval Academy Foundation and chairman of its Quarterdeck Committee, which he established in 1993 to create an academic chair of naval policy at the United States Naval Academy.
        He was a graduate of the Naval Academy, the New York University Graduate School of Business and Rutgers University Law School. He worked in journalism and public relations before establishing his own career-counseling business.
        Mr. Collins is survived by his wife, Edith Berke Collins; a son, Craig L., of Summit; two daughters, Sherry Collins-Zabel of Bergenfield, N.J., and Cynthia Lohrke-Lewis of South Orange, N.J.; two stepdaughters, Lynn Dubal and Phebe Tanners, both of Manhattan; a stepson, Robert Berkowitz of Anselmo, Calif.; a brother, David M. 2d, of Bonita Springs, Calif., and a sister, Jessie Kushell of Scottsdale, Ariz.

        Joseph R. Concilio, a certified public accountant and former executive with NBC, died on Thursday at Cabrini Hospice in Manhattan. He was 47 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        He died of complications of AIDS, a friend, Richard Feldman, said.
        Mr. Concilio graduated from Lehigh University in 1964 with a degree in business administration. He served in the Army for two and a half years, rising to the rank of captain.
        From 1969 to 1980, he was with the National Broadcasting Company where he was director of the finance and personnel departments. He then formed his own accounting concern. He was also active in gay and lesbian organizations.
        He is survived by a brother, Richard, of Marietta, Ga.; and three sisters, Teresa Goody of Irving, Tex., Anita Keire of Greenwich, Conn., and Marie Kittelberger of Columbia, Md.

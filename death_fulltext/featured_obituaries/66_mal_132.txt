
        Robert A. dePalma, vice chairman of the board of the Rockwell International Corporation died yesterday in Pittsburgh. He was 56 years old and lived in Mount Lebanon, Pa.
        Mr. dePalma died of cancer, said William B. Blanning, a company spokesman.
        Mr. dePalma was appointed vice chairman last year after holding several top executive positions with the aerospace and military company during a 22-year career there. He had been the company's treasurer, vice president of financial analysis and from 1976 to 1991 its chief financial officer. He had served on its board since 1979.
        Before joining Rockwell, Mr. dePalma had been a vice president at the Pittsburgh National Bank.  Basketball at Duquesne
        A native of East Orange, N.J., he graduated from Duquesne University with a bachelor's degree in 1958 and a master's degree in business administration in 1963. In college, he was a captain of Duquesne's basketball team, one of the leading basketball teams in the country at that time, and was named an All-American in 1957.
        He was an officer in the intelligence and security department of the Army from 1958 to 1960. He was recalled to active duty during the Berlin crisis in 1961 and was discharged a year later with the rank of captain.
        Mr. dePalma is survived by his wife, Maryann, and their three children, Robert, Diane Lange and Carolyn, and one grandchild, all of Pittsburgh.

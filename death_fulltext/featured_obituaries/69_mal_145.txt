
        Walter Butler Mahony Jr., a retired executive editor of Reader's Digest and a member of its board, died on Saturday at his home in Bedford, N.Y. He was 77 years old.
        His family said he died of cancer.
        A native of Scarborough, N.Y., Mr. Mahony spent 42 years at the Reader's Digest. He joined the editorial staff in 1937, became a senior editor in 1949, and managing editor as well as a vice president in 1965. He was executive editor from 1973 to 1976 and retired in 1979 with the title of chief text editor, Reader's Digest General Books.
        Mr. Mahony graduated from Amherst College in 1936. Commissioned into the Naval Reserve in 1941, he rose to lieutenant commander.
        He is survived by his wife of 51 years, the former Marguerite du Pont Lee; a daughter, Barbara Mahony Kent of Manhattan; two sons, Walter B. (Peter) Mahony 3d of Manhattan, and Robert Murray Mahony of Lafayette, La.; two sisters, Mary Murray Brown of Bedford, and Janet Wilson of Williamsburg, Va.; eight grandchildren, and one great-grandson.

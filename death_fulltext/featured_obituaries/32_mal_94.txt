        Charles H. Russell, a newspaper publisher who turned to politics and served as Governor and Congressman, died of a heart attack Wednesday at a hospital here. He was 85 years old.
        Mr. Russell suffered from emphysema, a lung illness he contracted from cigarette smoking, and had a cardiac arrest, said his son Todd.
        A Republican, he was Governor from 1951 to 1958, when he was defeated for re-election by Grant Sawyer, a Democrat.
        He served five terms in the Nevada Legislature and one term in the United States House of Representatives before winning the governorship. He also administered the Marshall Plan, which aided Western European nations after World War II, and foreign aid programs in Paraguay. From 1929 to 1946 he was publisher of The Ely Record.
        As Governor he pushed for creation of the sTate Gaming Control Board, the enforcement arm for the Nevada Gaming Commission, which oversees casinos.
        Mr. Russell is survived by his wife, Marjorie Anne, and five children, Clark, Craig, David and Todd, and Virginia Sakal.

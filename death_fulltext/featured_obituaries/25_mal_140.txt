        Thayer Cumings, an advertising executive who worked for Batten, Barton, Durstine & Osborne and a predecessor agency for more than 30 years, died Sunday after a long illness at his home in York Harbor, Me. He was 84 years old and also had a home in Manhatttan.
        In 1927 Mr. Cumings, a native of New York City and a 1926 graduate of Harvard College, joined the George Batten Company, which later became part of BBDO. He was named a vice president of the agency in 1947 and a director in 1951. He retired in 1963. In World War II he served in the Navy as a lieutenant commander in charge of the Navy Radio Office.
        After retirement Mr. Cumings was vice chairman of a fund drive that raised $58 million for Harvard Medical School and its teaching hospitals.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Virginia Richardson; two daughters, Gay Hackett of Manhattan and Julie Kenny of Mendham, N.J., and four grandchildren.

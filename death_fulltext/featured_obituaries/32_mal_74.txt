        Peter Goswyn Franck, an economist and former director of the Business Research Center at Syracuse University, died of a stroke Thursday at his home in Ruckersville, Va. He was 75 years old.
        Dr. Franck received law degrees from the University of Berlin and the University of Basel before receiving a Ph.D. in economics from the University of California at Berkeley. He joined the faculty at Syracuse in 1966 as a professor of international trade and investment. He had previously taught at Robert College in Istanbul.
        He retired from Syracuse University in 1980 and then taught at the American University in Beirut and the University of Sfax in Tunisia.
        He is survived by two daughters, Karen and Marianne, both of Manhattan; two sisters, Inge Hunzinger of Rahnsdorf, East Germany, and Gisela Bayer of Leipzig, East Germany, and a brother, Michael, of Weisbaden, West Germany.

        H. Kermit Green, a lawyer who was a former chairman of the North Jersey District Water Supply Commission, died of complications from surgery Saturday at Mercy Hospital in Miami. He was 86 years old and lived in Key Biscayne, Fla.
        Mr. Green was a founding partner of Green, Lasky & Cohen in Newark, and practiced law for more than 60 years. He was chairman of the water supply commission in the 1950's. He also headed a committee of lawyers supporting the candidacy of Gov. Robert Meyner in 1952 and again in 1956.
        He was a graduate of Cornell University in 1924 and the Yale University Law School in 1926.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Sarah Phillips; two sons, Frederic, of Livingston, N.J., and H. Kermit Jr., of Berkeley Heights, N.J., and four grandchildren.


        Francis J. Prout, a former management consultant, died on Saturday at the Memorial Sloan-Kettering Cancer Center in Manhattan. He was 76 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Prout died of a heart attack, said a son, Alexander.
        Mr. Prout, a native of Jersey City, graduated from Fordham University in 1939 and the John J. Marshall Law School in 1947. He interrupted his education to enlist in the Army Air Corps at the onset of World War II.
        After the war Mr. Prout worked for the State Department as a security agent for the United States Embassies in Greece and Turkey. In the early 1950's Mr. Prout was a general manager for Brazilian Airlines, then was employed by several New York City-based management consulting firms. He retired from Frank C. Brown & Company in the late 1970's.
        Mr. Prout is survived by his wife, Tomiyo; five children: John, of Paris; Frances Selby, of Manhattan; the Rev. Thomas, of Tokyo; Alexander, of Manhattan, and Catharine, of Manhattan, and one granddaughter.

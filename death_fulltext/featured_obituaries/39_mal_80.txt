        Dr. Allen D. Tanney, a physician who practiced as a consultant to manufacturers, hotels, a newspaper and several baseball teams, died of heart failure yesterday at Lenox Hill Hospital in Manhattan. He was 79 years old and lived in Harrison, N.Y.
        A 1938 graduate of the Loyola University School of Medicine, Dr. Tanney was in private practice in Manhattan and was affiliated with Doctors Hospital and Lenox Hill. He was also medical director for Philip Morris and in 1955 set up a health department at the company's Manhattan headquarters.
        Dr. Tanney was a medical consultant to the New York Yankees, the Chicago Cubs and Pittsburgh Pirates; The New York Daily Mirror, the Waldorf-Astoria and Commodore hotels, the Turner Construction Company and Chrysler Motor Company. He was also a consulting physician with the New York State Athletic Commission and the New York State Employees Retirement System.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Beatrice Lilienfeld, and two sisters, Lillian Ross of Delray Beach, Fla., and Dorothy Allen of Fort Lauderdale, Fla.

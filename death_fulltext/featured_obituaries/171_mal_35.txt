
        John W. Raber, a former chairman and president of the Green Point Savings Bank, died on March 29 at his home in Jacksonville, Fla. He was 89.
        Mr. Raber joined Green Point in Brooklyn in 1928 as a clerk. He became an officer in 1942 and was secretary and executive vice president before becoming president in 1967. He became chairman in 1974 and retired in 1981.
        He is survived by a daughter, Phyllis Collett, of Kfar Hanassi, Israel; a son, Douglas J., of Washington; five grandchildren; and four great-grandchildren.

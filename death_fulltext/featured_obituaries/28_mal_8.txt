        Gerald Wilkinson, executive director of the National Indian Youth Council, died last Thursday at Georgetown University Hospital in Washington after suffering a heart attack while on a business trip. He was 50 years old and lived in Albuquerque, N. M. Mr. Wilkinson, a Cherokee Catawba Indian, was born in Statesville, N. C., and attended Duke University and the Columbia University Law School.
        He lived in Paris, where he taught English, before returning to work with American Indian advocacy groups. In 1969 he became executive director of the Youth Council, based in Albquerque.
        He is survived by his mother, Virginia, of Statesville and a sister, Barbara Cornell of Raleigh, N.C.

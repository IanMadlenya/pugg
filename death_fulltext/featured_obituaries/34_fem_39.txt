        Johanna M. Hopkins, who was a schools superindendent in the northwest Bronx from 1942 to 1964 and the first woman to hold such a post in New York City, died Thursday in the Huntington (L.I.) Hospital. She was 94 years old and lived in Kings Park, L.I.
        Miss Hopkins was a native of Brooklyn, a graduate of Erasmus Hall High School and the Brooklyn Training School for Teachers and received bachelor's and master's degrees from New York University. She had been an assistant principal and principal in schools in Astoria and Jackson Heights, Queens.
        Her survivors include nephews, nieces, grandnephews and grandnieces.

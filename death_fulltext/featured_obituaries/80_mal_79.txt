
        Torkel Opsahl, a Norwegian human rights expert who was directing a United Nations commission on war crimes in the former Yugoslavia, died Thursday in his office in Geneva. He was 62 and had his permanent home in Oslo.
        He died of a heart attack, Reuters reported, quoting United Nations officials in Geneva.
        Dr. Opsahl was a professor of law at the University of Oslo and had been president of the Norwegian Institute of Human Rights since 1987.


        Richard J. Cattani, former editor of The Christian Science Monitor, died on Friday at his home in Wellesley, Mass. He was 63.
        His wife, Jacqueline, said his death followed a long illness.
        Mr. Cattani joined The Monitor in 1968 and in the next 29 years held every major reporting, editorial and administrative position. He was appointed editor in 1988 and remained in that position until August 1994, when he became editor at large. He retired in 1997.
        In addition to his work for The Monitor, Mr. Cattani was a director at the Roper Center for Public Opinion Research at the University of Connecticut at Storrs.
        Born on June 17, 1936, and reared in Detroit, Mr. Cattani earned a bachelor's degree in English at Harvard in 1958 and a master's degree from the Harvard Graduate School of Education in 1959.
        Before joining The Monitor, Mr. Cattani taught English and mathematics and was executive vice president and general manager of a Detroit public relations firm.
        In addition to his wife, Mr. Cattani is survived by two sons, Jeremy, of Boston, and Gabriel, of Wayland, Mass.; a daughter, Ruth Cattani Hope of Chester, England; two sisters, Geraldine Wilson of Lewes, Del., and Diana Cattani of St. Clair Shores, Mich., and two granddaughters.

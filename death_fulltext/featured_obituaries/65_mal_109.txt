
        Bernard D. Nossiter, an author and journalist, died yesterday at his home in Manhattan. He was 66 years old.
        He died of lung cancer, said his companion, Eleanor Hauser. Mr. Nossiter was chief of the New York Times bureau at the United Nations from 1979 to 1983 after reporting for The Washington Post for 24 years.
        His books were "Fat Years and Lean: The American Economy Since Roosevelt" (HarperCollins, 1990), "The Global Struggle for More" (Harper & Row, 1987), "Britain: A Future That Works" (Houghton Mifflin, 1978), "Soft State: A Newspaperman's Chronicle of India" (Harper & Row, 1970) and "The Mythmakers: An Essay on Power and Wealth" (Houghton Mifflin, 1964).
        Mr. Nossiter, who was born in Manhattan, was graduated from Dartmouth in 1947 and received a master's degree in economics from Harvard in 1948. He was married to Jacqueline Robinson in 1950. They separated in 1988.
        Besides his wife, of San Francisco, he is survived by his mother, Rose Jacobson of Manhattan; a brother, Paul of Wellfleet, Mass.; four sons, Daniel of Washington, Joshua of San Francisco, Adam of New Orleans and Jonathan of Manhattan; and four grandchildren.

        Robert H. Bethke, a former chairman and chief executive officer of the Discount Corporation of New York, died of cancer Thursday at the Columbia-Presbyterian Medical Center. He was 71 years old and lived in Armonk, N.Y.
        Mr. Bethke, who was born in Chicago, graduated from the University of Chicago in 1937. During World War II, he served, with the rank of lieutenant colonel, as assistant chief statistician of the United States Army Service Forces, for which he received the Legion of Merit.
        At the end of the war, he joined the Discount Corporation, a leading Government securities concern. He served as its chairman and chief executive from 1978 until he retired in 1981. He was president of the company from 1974 to 1978 and had been a director since 1967.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Patricia Davis; two sons, Robert, of Newark, Del., and William, of Basking Ridge, N.J.; a brother, Arthur, of Boston; a sister, Katherine Doak of Westfield, N.J., and four grandchildren.

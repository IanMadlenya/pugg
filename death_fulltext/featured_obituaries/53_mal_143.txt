
        Richard W. Couch, a retired communications executive with General Telephone and Electronics, died on Friday at Potsdam (N.Y.) Hospital, while visiting relatives. He was 73 years old and lived in Harwichport, Mass., and Sarasota, Fla.
        He died of complications from asthma, his family said.
        Mr. Couch retired from G.T.E. in 1982 as marketing vice president for communications products. He had served as president of G.T.E.-Iran Communications Systems from 1975 to 1979 and as program manager of the ground electronics system for the Minuteman missile from 1960 to 1966.
        Mr. Couch, a native of Charleston, W. Va., and a graduate of the United States Military Academy, held a master's degree in business administration from the Wharton School of the University of Pennsylvania. In 1955 he joined the Sylvania Corporation, which became a subsidiary of G.T.E.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Patricia Burnett; three sons, Richard Jr. of Hanover, N.H., Thomas, of Skokie, Ill., and John, of Jacksonville, Fla.; four daughters, Deborah Farrington of Kihei, Hawaii, Ann Pampel and Jane Katz, both of Greenwich, Conn., and Joan Workman of Weston, Conn.; a brother, James, of Charlottesville, Va., and 12 grandchildren.

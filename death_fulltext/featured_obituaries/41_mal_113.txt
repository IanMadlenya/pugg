        David M. Corcoran, president of the international division of Sterling Drug Inc., for 40 years before his retirement in 1973, died in his sleep on Thursday at his home in Waterville Valley, N.H., his family reported. He was 86 years old.
        Mr. Corcoran, a native of Pawtucket, R.I., began his business career in 1928 with General Motors, serving in the Far East, where he became manager of the concern's Tokyo office.
        In 1933 he was made president of the newly formed international division of Sterling Drug.
        In the late 1930's Mr. Corcoran organized China Defense Supplies to provide military equipment to the Chinese in their war with Japan. He also helped organize the American Volunteer Group, which became known as the Flying Tigers under Gen. Claire Chennault in Burma and China.
        Since 1965 Mr. Corcoran had been a director of the Waterville Company, which owns the Waterville Valley resort complex.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Joan Woltman; three sons, Tom of Waterville Valley, David Jr. of San Francisco, and Jon of Bristol, Vt.; two daughters, Joey of Seattle and Jennifer of Santa Fe, and eight grandchldren.

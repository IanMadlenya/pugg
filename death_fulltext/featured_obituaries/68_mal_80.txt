
        Hideo Gosha, a film director known for his samurai movies, died on Aug. 30 at a hospital in Kyoto, Japan. He was 63 years old.
        He had had dyspnea, a breathing disorder, said a spokesman for the World Artists Cinema Corporation, which released two of his films on home video.
        His samurai films used sword-fighting choreography. Among those shown in the United States were "Goyokin" (1969), "The Wolves" (1971), "Hunter in the Dark" (1979), "Onimassa" (1983) and "Four Days of Snow and Blood" (1990).
        Mr. Gosha was born in Tokyo and graduated from Meiji University before working for Nippon Broadcasting.
        He is survived by a daughter, Tomoe, of Japan.

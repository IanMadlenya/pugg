
        John Lewis Miller, who was superintendent of the Great Neck, N.Y., public schools from 1942 to 1970, died at his home in Great Neck on Dec. 22. He was 93.
        Mr. Miller's career in education spanned more than 30 years, and his policies in the Great Neck school system brought him national attention.
        One of the town's high schools was renamed in his honor when he retired in 1970.
        Dr. Miller was a trustee of North Shore University Hospital and a member of the boards of national organizations in his field.
        He was born in Quincy, Mass.,  graduated from Bates College in Maine and received his master's and doctorate degrees in education from Harvard University. He was Superintendent of Schools in Brockton, Mass., from 1937 to 1942.
        Mr. Miller is survived by his wife of 48 years, Grace Bryant Miller; two sons, John Jr., of Asbury Park, N.J., and Donald, of Buffalo; two daughters, Jean Remler of Fairfax, Va., and Melanie Miller of Hays, Kan.; seven grandchildren, and one great-grandchild.

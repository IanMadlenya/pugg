        Jay James Swift, a former official of the New York City Environmental Control Board and of the city's Addiction Services Agency, died of cancer Thursday at Roosevelt Hospital. He was 62 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Swift held several city positions under Mayors John V. Lindsay, Abraham D. Beame and Edward I. Koch. He was in charge of community development for the Addiction Services Agency from 1968 to 1970 and for the next three years was first deputy commissioner. After a brief term as a deputy city administrator, Mr. Swift was named executive director of the Environmental Control Board, a post he held from 1974 until he retired in 1983.
        After his retirement, Mr. Swift served as a special consultant to the Queens District Attorney and to the Ad Hoc Review Committee of the National Institute of Mental Health.
        Born in Manhattan, he was a graduate of Townsend Harris High School and of Yale.
        He is survived by two sons, Jay Joseph Swift of Manhattan and Gordon Rutledge of West Orange, N.J.; a daughter, Susan Leslie Swift of San Diego, and a granddaughter.


       Harry K. Weiss, a retired vice president of Helmsley-Spear Inc., the real-estate concern, died on Monday at his home in Scarsdale, N.Y. He was 83 years old and also lived in Delray Beach, Fla. His family said he died of lymphoma.

        Mr. Weiss, who was born in Manhattan and was a graduate of Cornell, was an insurance broker before he joined a predecessor of Helmsley-Spear in 1944. He retired in 1978. He is survived by his wife, the former Tess Cohen; a daughter, Marjory Lehan of Westfield, Mass.; a son, Dr. Richard Weiss of New Rochelle, N.Y.; a sister, Bertha Rubin of West Palm Beach, Fla., and six grandchildren.

        Philip Pratt, chief Federal judge for the Eastern District of Michigan, died of cancer Tuesday at his home in Bloomfield Hills, Mich. He was 64 years old.
        Judge Pratt, a former prosecutor, was appointed a state circuit judge in 1963. In 1970, President Nixon nominated him to the Federal District Court and in 1986 he was made chief judge for the Eastern District.
        He is survived by his wife, Mary; a son, two daughters, a grandchild and a sister.

        Andrea Busiri Vici, an architect who helped design the Italian Pavilion at the 1939 World's Fair in New York City, died of heart failure Tuesday at the European Hospital here. He was 86 years old.
        Mr. Busiri Vici, who also designed homes in the Rome area and published several books on art, was known for combining elements of traditional and modern architecture. He and his brother, Michele, worked on the Italian Pavilion at the 1939 exposition.

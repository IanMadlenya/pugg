        Benjamin Halpern, professor emeritus of Near Eastern and Judaic studies at Brandeis University, died May 5 at Beth Israel Hospital in Boston. He was 78 years old and lived in Brookline, Mass.
        Dr. Halpern taught at Brandeis from 1960 to 1980. He was the author of several books, including ''The American Jew, A Zionist Analysis,'' ''The Idea of a Jewish State,'' ''Jews and Blacks,'' and, most recently, ''A Clash of Heroes: Brandeis, Weizmann and American Zionism.''
        He was born in Boston and held bachelor's and doctoral degrees from Harvard and a second bachelor's degree from Hebrew Teachers College.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Gertrude Gumner; two sons, Elkan, of Newton, and Joseph, of Brookline, and a brother, Samuel, who lives in Israel.

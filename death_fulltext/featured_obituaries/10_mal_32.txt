        Bing Quon Chu, a businessman and leader of Chinese-American organizations, died of a heart attack Tuesday in Booth Memorial Hospital in Flushing, Queens. He was 65 years old and a resident of Flushing.
        Mr. Chu was president of New Modern Wet Wash, a company that services hand laundries and institutions in the New York area.
        Born in Xinhui village, Guangdong Province, China, in 1921, Mr. Chu came to the United States in 1939. During World War II, he was a captain in the 97th Signal Corps, serving as a liaison officer between the Chinese and American armies in Burma.
        At the time of his death, Mr. Chu was executive vice-president of the Loong Kong Tien Wee Association, an overseas Chinese mutual-help organization. He was a former commander of the American Legion's Lieut. Benjamin Kimlau post in Chinatown and was active in several community organizations.
        He is survived by his wife, Irene Jung; two sons, John, of Chinatown and Philip, of Bayside, Queens; five daughters, Susan Chu Lai of Bayside, Jean Chu Kline of Little Neck, L.I., Dr. Dianne Chu Leong and Eva Chu, both of Flushing, and Helen Chu of Berkeley, Calif.; his father, Tin Mei Chu of Xinhui village in China; his mother, Moon Hong Chu of Brooklyn; two sisters, Leong Chee Main and Ng Ngan Yee of Flushing, and nine grandchildren.

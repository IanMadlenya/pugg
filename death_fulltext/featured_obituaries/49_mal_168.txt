
        Raymond A. Young Jr., a pilot for Pan American World Airways who set up the line's route network in Latin America in the 1930's, died on Tuesday at Somerset Medical Center in Somerville, N.J. He was 80 years old and lived in Somerville.
        He died of bone cancer, his family said.
        Captain Young joined Pan Am in 1936 after graduating from the University of Alabama and taking flight training in the Army Air Corps.
        He served in the Air Transport Command in World War II. In 1945 he operated Pan Am's first postwar trans-Atlantic service from La Guardia Airport. He retired in 1970 and became a director of the Soaring Society of America.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Ceil Rawley; three sons, Raymond 3d, of Redding, Conn., Ralph, of Mahwah, N.J., and Brian, of Marshall's Creek, Pa., and six grandchildren.

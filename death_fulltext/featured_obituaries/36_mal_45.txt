        Alexis P. Fekula, an oil and gas consultant and choral conductor of Russian liturgical music, died of heart disease on Thursday in Boston. He was 58 years old and lived in Southport, Conn.
        Mr. Fekula, a native of Riga, Latvia, trained church choirs on Long Island and in Manhattan in Russian liturgical music. He was a former member of the Diocesan Council of the Russian Orthodox Church Outside of Russia.
        A graduate of the Wharton School of Business of the University of Pennsylvania, he was an independent oil and gas consultant in Syosset, L.I., for many years.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Cara Worthington; his mother, Maria Fekula of Sea Cliff, L.I.; a brother, Walter, of Manhattan; and five children, Peter, of Glen Cove, L.I., Andrew, of Boston, Alexander, Paul and Cristina, all of Southport.

        James W. Dye Jr., executive vice president of the Long Island Lighting Company, died of intestinal cancer Thursday at his home in Oyster Bay, L.I. He was 63 years old.
        Mr. Dye joined Lilco in 1949 and became executive vice president in 1984. He was to retire Jan. 1.  He is survived by his wife, Isabel Kelleher Dye; four sons, Thomas, of Coral Gables, Fla., Kevin, of Kyoto, Japan, and Christopher and Richard, both of Huntington Station, L.I.; three daughters, Ellen Sack of San Antonio, Patrice Gleason of Stony Brook, L.I., and Margaret, of Oyster Bay, and seven grandchildren.

        Carlyle R. Bennett, an archivist for the Federal Government and for New York City, died Monday at the Princeton (N.J.) Medical Center after a short illness. He was 73 years old and had homes in Twin Rivers, N.J., and in Margate, Fla.
        Mr. Bennett, a graduate of City College of New York, served with the Federal Archives and Records Center in New York from 1953 until 1973, supervising its accession and disposal branch. From 1973 until his retirement in 1975 he was director of New York City's municipal archives.
        He is survived by his wife, Estelle; a brother, Richard, of New York City; three sisters, Virginia Litt, Barbara Boren and Lillian Solomon, all of Ridgefield, N.J.; two sons, Bruce, of Dix Hills, L.I., and Roger, of El Toro, Calif., and four grandchildren.

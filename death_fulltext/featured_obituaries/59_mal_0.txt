
        Herbert J. Deitz, a New York City lawyer, died on Wednesday in the Jacob Perlow Hospice of Beth Israel Medical Center. He was 82 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        He died of cancer, his wife, Suzanne, said.
        Mr. Deitz was formerly a senior partner in the firm of Cole & Deitz, a predecessor firm of Winston & Strawn, which has offices in Manhattan, Washington and Chicago. At his death, he was of counsel to Winston & Strawn and was a trustee of Beth Israel Medical Center and Beth Israel Hospital North.
        A native New Yorker, Mr. Deitz graduated from the City College of New York and Harvard Law School. In 1955 he joined the firm that became Cole & Deitz, where he focused on litigation and corporate law.
        In addition to his wife, he is survived by a son, Roger M., of Larchmont, N.Y.; four daughters, Margaret, of Newton, Kan., Susan Milmoe of Scarsdale, N.Y., Laurie Deitz Maser of Chappaqua, and Duffy, of Seattle, and five grandchildren.


        Irving Passon Berelson, a retired New York lawyer and philanthropist, died last Sunday at his home on the Upper East Side of Manhattan. He was 83.
        Mr. Berelson retired in 1995 as a senior partner at the Manhattan law firm Parker Chapin Flattau & Klimpl, which he joined in 1943.
        A native of Brooklyn, he graduated from New York University in 1937 and from Harvard Law School in 1940.
        He had a long association with the Metropolitan Opera, and became an advisory director in 1993.
        He had also been president of the League for the Hard of Hearing, and backed the New York Philharmonic program of children's concerts.
        Mr. Berelson is survived by his wife of 60 years, Thelma Dorfman Berelson; a daughter, Ellen Berelson of Alexandria, Va.; and a brother, Elliot, of New Springville, Staten Island.

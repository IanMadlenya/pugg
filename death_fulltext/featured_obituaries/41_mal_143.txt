        Roy Ketner McDonald, publisher of The Chattanooga News-Free Press, died in his sleep yesterday at his home in Lookout Mountain, Tenn. He was 88 years old.
        Mr. McDonald was also chairman of the Chattanooga Publishing Company, a joint company that publishes the morning Chattanooga Times and the afternoon Chattanooga News-Free Press.
        He began The Free Press in 1933 as a door-to-door advertising circular for his chain of grocery stores. By 1936 he had converted it into a daily newspaper with a circulation that is now about 55,000.
        Mr. McDonald, known for his conservative views, opposed most civil rights bills and measures for environmental controls.
        He was chairman of Blue Cross and Blue Shield of Tennessee.
        He is survived by his wife, Elizabeth; four daughters, Helen Exum of Lookout Mountain, Betsy Anderson of Chattanooga, Dr. Martha McDonald of Memphis and Nancy Reinke of Arlington, Va.; a son, Frank of Lookout Mountain; three sisters, Margaret Raines of Winter Park, Fla., and Catherine McDonald and Sue Groves, both of Dayton, Tenn.; 15 grandchildren and 12 great-grandchildren.

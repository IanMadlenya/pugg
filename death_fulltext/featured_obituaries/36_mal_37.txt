        Charles E. Arch, founder and chairman of Blistex Inc., manufacturers of lip care products, died of heart disease Monday at Loyola University Medical Center in Maywood, Ill. He was 79 years old and lived in Oak Brook, Ill.
        He established Blistex in 1947, and in 1967 left Sheffield to devote full time to the company he had founded. He served as Blistex chairman from 1982 until his death.
        He is survived by his wife, Mildred; two daughters, a son, two brothers, a sister and nine grandchildren.

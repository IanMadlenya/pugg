
        Rudolph Stewart, for 29 years a  behind-the-scenes presence at Carnegie Hall, died in New York on  Wednesday. He as 74.
        A spokesman for Carnegie Hall said the cause was liver cancer.
        Mr. Stewart began at Carnegie Hall in 1970 as an usher, moving to the job of head usher and finally to that of associate house manager, the position he held most recently.
        Mr. Stewart is survived by two brothers, two sisters, a daughter and a grandson.

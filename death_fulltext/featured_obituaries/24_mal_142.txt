        Buckley M. Byers, the deputy chairman of the Republican National Finance Committee since 1976, died of a stroke Friday in Georgetown University Hospital in Washington, where he lived. He was 72 years old.
        A Pittsburgh native and Yale University graduate, he joined the family business, the A. M. Byers Company, a steel manufacturer that was acquired by the General Tire and Rubber Company.
        Active in Republican Party politics since 1952, Mr. Byers held a variety of positions and was a major fund-raiser for President Bush.
        Services will be at 3 P.M. today at the Church of the Immaculate Conception in Washington, with burial Tuesday in Pittsburgh.
        Surviving are his wife, Mary Helen; three sons, Buckley Jr., of Detroit, Joseph, of Cleveland, and Christopher, of Washington, and three grandchildren.

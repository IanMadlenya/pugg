        Max Nierman, a commodities trader and member of the Chicago Board of Trade for more than half a century, died Friday at the Desert Hospital in Palm Springs, Calif. He was 90 years old and lived in Palm Springs.
        When he joined the Board of Trade in 1920 he was its youngest member, and when he retired in 1973 he was one of its oldest. In 1925 he founded Max Nierman & Company.
        He is survived by his wife of 65 years, the former Ada Steinberg; three daughters, Marcia Wagner of Winnetka, Ill., Miriam Lazar of Lake Forest, Ill., and Joan Weiss of Los Angeles; six grandchildren, and two great-grandchildren.

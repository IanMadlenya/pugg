        John McClanahan Henske, former chairman of the Olin Corporation and of the Chemical Manufacturers Association, died on Tuesday at his home in Westport, Conn., after a long illness. He was 66 years old.
        Mr. Henske began his career with Olin in 1969 as vice president of the chemicals group. He was named president in 1973, chief executive officer in 1978 and chairman in 1980. He retired as chairman on July 1, 1988.
        Earlier he was associated for 21 years with the Dow Chemical Company, where he was a vice president and a director.
        Mr. Henske was born in Omaha, Neb. After spending two years at Yale, he joined the Army Corps of Engineers in 1943. He left the Army in 1946 and returned to Yale, graduating in 1948 with a degree in chemical engineering. He joined Dow that year as a research engineer.
        Director of Other Companies
        He was chairman of the Chemical Manufacturers Association from 1979 to 1980. Under his direction it changed its name from the Manufacturing Chemists Association and developed a greater advocacy role in representing the positions of industry. He also served as a director of the Sun Company, American Precision Industries and the Hydraulic Company.
        Mr. Henske was also active in civic affairs and was chairman of a number of organizations including the United Negro College Fund and United Way campaigns. He was a trustee of Stamford Hospital and Yale-New Haven Hospital.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Maryanne Decker; two sons, Robert C., of Medford, Mass., and John D., of Fairfield, Conn.; two daughters, Kathryn Sweeney, also of Fairfield, and Allison Collins of Piermont, N.Y.; a brother, Joseph, of Scottsdale, Ariz., and 11 grandchildren.

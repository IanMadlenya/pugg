        John L. Davenport, who directed the cancer research laboratories of Pfizer Inc. before his retirement in 1967, died of cancer Wednesday at Boca Raton (Fla.) Community Hospital. He was 82 years old and lived in Boca Raton. He had formerly lived in Westwood, N.J.
        Mr. Davenport joined Pfizer in 1929 as a chemical engineer and became superintendent of the Brooklyn plant in 1945. He was named executive vice president in 1949, and five years later, chairman of the executive committee.
        In 1957, he relinquished his corporate duties to head the John L. Smith Memorial for Cancer Research, then in Maywood, N.J., and now in Groton, Conn. The laboratories, operated in conjunction with the National Cancer Institute, developed drugs to treat some forms of cancer.
        Mr. Davenport was born in Hamilton, Ontario. He was an alumnus of the University of Toronto, Surviving are his wife, Edna; a son, Peter; a daughter, Linda Spire, and five grandchildren.

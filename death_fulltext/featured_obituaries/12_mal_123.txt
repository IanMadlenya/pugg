        Jean Mitry, a film historian and co-founder of the Cinematheque Francaise, died of cancer Monday. He was 83 years old.
        Mr. Mitry produced dozens of articles and books on films, including works on John Ford, Sergei Eisenstein, Charlie Chaplin and Rene Clair. He also compiled the ''Larousse Dictionary of the Cinema'' and the 35-volume ''History of Cinema.'' In 1936, he created the Cinematheque Francaise with Henri Langlois and Georges Franju.

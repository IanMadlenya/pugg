        Clyde H. Shaffer, retired president of the Breyer Ice Cream Company, died Friday in the Whitehall Nursing Home in Boca Raton, Fla. He was 90 years old and lived in Pompano Beach, Fla.
        A family member said Mr. Shaffer had died of cancer.
        Mr. Shaffer joined Breyer in Philadelphia as a timekeeper in 1925 after graduating from Penn State University, and advanced quickly, becoming president in 1941. He held that post until his retirement, in January 1966.
        He also is a former head of the Hydrox Ice Cream Company of New York and he served as a director of the National Dairy Products Corporation. He was a past president of the International Milk and Ice Cream Association.
        Mr. Shaffer served as Mayor of Hillsboro Beach, Fla., from 1972 to 1980.
        Mr. Shaffer was a native of Hooversville, Pa.
        He is survived by his wife, Elizabeth; a daughter, Diane S. Castor of Ambler, Pa.; four grandchildren, including two children of his son, the late Comdr. Clyde H. Shaffer, Jr., who was a United States Navy submarine captain.

        Malcolm Fooshee, a philanthropist and retired partner of the Manhattan law firm of Donovan Leisure Newton & Irvine, died of heart failure Friday while traveling from his Manhattan home to his country home in Pawling, N.Y. He was 90 years old.
        Mr. Fooshee, who practiced business and corporate law for more than half a century, was a native of Charleston, Tenn., and a graduate of the University of the South in Sewanee, Tenn., for which he endowed scholarships for 10 students and had been a trustee.
        He was a graduate of the Harvard Law School and earned a bachelor of civil law degree at Christ Church College at Oxford, where he was a Rhodes scholar. Over the years, he established a reading library at the University of the South and a library of American works at Christ Church College.
        Mr. Fooshee was a trustee of the American Rhodes scholarships, a director of the Huguenot Society of New York and a former chairman of corporate and business law of the American Bar Association.
        Survivors include his wife, the former Wynne Byard; a daughter, Clare Childres of Manhattan, and four grandchildren.


        Charles Lewis Whipple, a former editor of the editorial page of The Boston Globe and the newspaper's first ombudsman, died of complications after surgery on Sunday at Cooley-Dickinson Hospital in Northampton, Mass. He was 77 years old.
        Mr. Whipple retired as ombudsman in 1979 after 43 years at The Globe. From 1979 to 1982 he was an editor of China Daily, an English-language newspaper, in Beijing. He also worked for Xinhua, the official Chinese news agency.
        Mr. Whipple, a graduate of Harvard College, was a reporter before joining the editorial page, which he headed from 1964 to 1975.
        He is survived by his wife, Priscilla; two sons from a previous marriage, two stepsons, two sisters, a brother and two grandaughters.

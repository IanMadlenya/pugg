        Andre Blumenthal, a retired textile executive and civic leader, died of cancer Friday at his home in Norwalk, Conn. He was 85 years old.
        Mr. Blumenthal joined his father's textile-making firm, Sidney Blumenthal & Company, in 1925 after graduating from Yale University. He retired as chairman in 1958 when the firm was sold to Burlington Industries. Then he founded Norwalk Powdered Metals Inc., which compacted and powdered metal to produce parts. He retired in 1982.
        Mr. Blumenthal was born in Manhattan. He was a Navy commander in World War II.
        He was a former president of the Textile Research Institute. He was a member of the Connecticut State Board of Mental Health from 1958 to 1964. He was a former president of the Connecticut Hospital Association and a former chairman of the advisory board of the Connecticut Mental Health Center. He was a former president and board member of the Norwalk Hospital and a former president of the Connecticut Hospital Planning Commission. He was a founder of the Greater Norwalk Community Council.
        Surviving are his wife, Mildred; two sons, William, of Norwalk and Thomas, of Southington, Conn.; one daughter, Elizabeth, of Arlington, Mass.; two sisters, Doris Stein of New York City and Yvonne Pappenheim of Cambridge, Mass., and eight grandchildren.

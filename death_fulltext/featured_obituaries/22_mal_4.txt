        J. Howard Wood, former president and chairman of the board of the Tribune Company and former member of The Associated Press board, died Sunday at Lake Forest Hospital. He was 87 years old.
        As president and chief executive officer from 1960 to 1966, Mr. Wood initiated an ambitious acquisition program that helped build the company that publishes The Chicago Tribune into a diversified conglomerate with holdings in newspapers, radio, television and newsprint.
        Mr. Wood started as a reporter covering suburban news at The Tribune in 1925. In 1966, he was elected chairman of the board of the parent company, and he retired in 1970. He also served as publisher of The Chicago Tribune from 1960 to 1968.
        From 1968 to 1972, Mr. Wood served on the A.P. board of directors. He was chairman and president of the American Newspaper Publishers Association from 1966 to 1968.
        At age 16, while still in high school, Mr. Wood started work as an apprentice reporter on The Daily Ledger in Canton, Ill. His father was editor of the paper.
        After covering north suburban Chicago for The Tribune, Mr. Wood moved to the financial section in 1928. In 1942, he was named auditor and comptroller of the Tribune Company. Through the years, he served as treasurer, vice president, business manager and general manager.
        Mr. Wood is survived by his wife, Barbara, two daughters, two sons and six grandchildren.

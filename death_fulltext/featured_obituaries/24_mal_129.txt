        John D. Hickerson, a former Ambassador and chairman of the group that established NATO, died of cancer Wednesday in Maryland at Bethesda Retirement and Nursing Center. He was 91 years old and lived in Washington.
        A career Foreign Service officer, Mr. Hickerson was Ambassador to Finland from 1955 to 1959 and to the Philippines from 1959 to 1962, when he retired.
        Born in Crawford, Tex., he graduated from the University of Texas and joined the Foreign Service. He served in Latin America and Canada and was an adviser to the United States delegation at the Dunbarton Oaks conference in 1944 and at the United Nations organizing conference in San Francisco in 1945.
        Later, he served as chairman of the group of senior foreign ministry officials that drafted the treaty establishing the North Atlantic Treaty Organization. From 1949 to 1953, as an Assistant Secretary of State, he dealt with Asian matters, primarily the Korean War.
        Surviving are his son, John, of Chevy Chase, Md., three grandchildren and two great-grandchildren.

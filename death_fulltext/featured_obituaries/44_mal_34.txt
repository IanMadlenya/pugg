
       Wilson S. Johnson, the chief executive of the National Federation of Independent Business, died Aug. 30 at Mills Hospital in San Mateo, Calif. He was 76 years old and lived in San Mateo.
        A spokesman for the family said Mr. Johnson had died of cancer.
        A graduate of the University of Maryland, Mr. Johnson joined the small-business advocacy group in 1958 and became its president in 1969. He held the position until 1983, when he was elected chairman of the board.
        He was an outspoken advocate of small business and served on President Ronald Reagan's Private Sector Survey on Cost Control, headed by J. Peter Grace, and the National Bipartisan Commission on Central America, headed by Henry Kissinger.
        He was also a founding member of the International Small Business Congress and served on the National Industrial Energy Council.
        He is survived by his wife, Charlotte; a son, Donald of Brush Prairie, Wash.; two daughters, Helen Cain of Walla Walla, Wash., and Carol Tol of Eureka, Calif.; two brothers, Bennett of Dover, Del., and Ralph of Clayton, Del.; two sisters, Helen Roach and Essie Pepper, both of Denton, Md., and six grandchildren.

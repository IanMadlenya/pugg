        August H. Groeschel, a founder and the first president of the New York Blood Center, died of pneumonia Sunday at the Jersey Shore Medical Center in Neptune, N.J. He was 80 years old and lived in Sea Girt, N.J.
        Dr. Groeschel joined the staff of New York Hospital, now New York Hospital-Cornell Medical Center, as assistant director in 1947. He later became vice president of the medical center and an assistant professor of public health at the Cornell Medical College.
        In 1959 he was named president of the Community Blood Council of New York, which evolved into the New York Blood Center and opened in 1964. The New York Blood Center now operates the largest blood program in the United States.
        Dr. Groeschel was born in Jersey City, N.J. He graduated from Holy Cross College in 1927 and Columbia University College of Physicians & Surgeons in 1931. In 1947, he earned a master's degree at Columbia. He also served in the U.S. Army Reserve.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Eileen Bosquett; two sons, Peter, of Roanoke, Va., and Noel, of Grassmere, S.I.; three daughters, Moya and Catherine, of Manhattan and Margaret Anderson of Northport, L.I., and eight grandchildren.

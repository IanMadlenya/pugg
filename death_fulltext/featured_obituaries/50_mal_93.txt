
        H. S. Allan Kastrup, former head of The American Swedish News Exchange in Manhattan, an agency sponsored by the Swedish Government, died on Thursday at Lenox Hill Hospital. He was 84 years old and lived in Manhattan and in Sweden.
        Mr. Kastrup died of complications following surgery, a spokesman for the family said.
        He began his career as a journalist in Sweden. In 1943 he came to New York to work for The American Swedish News Exchange, which ceased publication in 1972, the year he retired.
        He was the author of "The Swedish Heritage in America," published by the Swedish Council of America in 1975. The book is a collection of Swedish contributions to American life as recorded by Mr. Kastrup.
        He is survived by three brothers: Sverker, Harald and Tryggve,and a sister, Greta Gothall, all of Sweden.

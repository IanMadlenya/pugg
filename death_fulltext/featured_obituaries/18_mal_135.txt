        Alfred W. Barth, an international banker and former executive vice president of Chase Manhattan Bank, died of a heart attack Monday at his home in Manhasset, L.I. He was 85 years old.
        Mr. Barth was born in Zurich and came to the United States in 1921.
        He received the French Legion of Honor for devising a way in 1950 for the French Government to borrow $225 million from banks in the United States.
        There are no survivors.

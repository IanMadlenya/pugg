
        Richard Irving, a television director and producer whose credits included "Columbo" and the "$6 Million Man," died on Sunday after heart surgery at an undisclosed San Diego hospital. He was 73 years old.
        Mr. Irving, a former actor, was a dialogue director at the RKO film studio before entering television. Later he helped establish Revue Productions, the MCA Universal television-production subsidiary.
        He oversaw the production of 36 television films during one six-month period in 1971. In 1949 and 1950, he produced 22 episodes of the 90-minute "Mystery Theater." His production credits included "Pepsi-Cola Playhouse," "Name of the Game," "Columbo" and "Wagon Train."
        He also directed the pilot film for ABC-TV's "$6 Million Man" and the NBC-TV mini-series "Seventh Avenue."
        Mr. Irving was executive vice president of Universal TV until 1979.
        Most recently, he directed the ABC series "The Insiders" and NBC's "Hell Town" and produced the television movie "The Last Days of Pompeii."
        He is survived by his wife, Joanne; a daughter, a son, three stepchildren and seven grandchildren.

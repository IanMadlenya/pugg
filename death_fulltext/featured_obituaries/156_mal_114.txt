
        George Dorsey Braden, a legal consultant on constitutional issues, died Jan. 3 at his home in Brooklyn. A former resident of Schenectady, N.Y., and Scarborough, Me., he was 85.
        A native of Indianapolis, he graduated Phi Beta Kappa from Swarthmore College in 1938 and graduated from Yale Law School in 1941. After serving in the Army in World War II, he taught law at Yale until 1951.
        He joined the General Electric Company in the late 1950's and worked on its legal staff in Schenectady and Manhattan for 17 years. He left to become a consultant on constitutional matters. He advised state constitutional conventions in New York, Illinois and Texas in the late 1960's and early 1970's.
        He was also a corporation counsel for the City of Schenectady. He was an assistant New York State attorney general in charge of legal opinions until his retirement in 1983.
        Mr. Braden's wife of 52 years, Josephine Clarke Braden, died in 1997. He is survived by two daughters, Ann B. Johnson of Brooklyn and Magdalen Braden Blanco White of Philadelphia; two sons, William of Austin, Tex., and Daniel of Manhattan; and two granddaughters.

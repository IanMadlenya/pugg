        Carlos Drummond de Andrade, a poet who was a leader of Brazil's modernist movement in literature, died Monday night following a heart attack. He was 85 years old.
        Among Mr. Drummond's works published in English were two volumes of poetry, ''Souvenir of the Ancient World,'' which was translated by the American poet Mark Strand, and ''Traveling in the Family: Selected Poems.''
        Mr. Drummond had been hospitalized since Saturday.

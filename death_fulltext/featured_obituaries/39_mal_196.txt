        Vincenzo Lamanna, a restaurateur, died of a pulmonary embolism on Wednesday at Mount Sinai Medical Center in Manhattan. He was 78 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Lamanna was the owner and president of the Isle of Capri, a restaurant he operated for the last 35 years at 61st Street and Third Avenue in Manhattan.
        He is survived by his wife, Maria Citriniti Lamanna; two daughters, Gloria Schiavetta and Jane D'Agostino, both of Jackson Heights, Queens, and three grandchildren.

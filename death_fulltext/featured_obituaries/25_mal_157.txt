        Joshua B. Powers, a publishers' representative, died at his home in Manhattan Tuesday after a short illness. He was 96 years old.
        Mr. Powers's company, which bore his name, represented newspapers throughout the world, including The New York Times abroad. He was a founding member of the International Advertising Association and the Inter-America Press Association. He won the Maria Moors Cabot award from Columbia University in 1950.
        Mr. Powers was a conservationist and a founder of the Caribbean Conservation Corporation, which helped establish a national park in Costa Rica.
        Surviving are two daughters, Carolien Maynard of Pelham, N.Y., and Susan Urstadt of New Canaan, Conn.; two sons, Joshua Jr., of Manhattan, and Thomas, of Norwich, Vt.; 12 grandchildren, and 2 great-grandchildren.

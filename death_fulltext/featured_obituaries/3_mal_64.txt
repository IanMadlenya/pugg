        Wayne Ellsworth Chapman, a tax lawyer and partner in the law firm of Cravath, Swaine & Moore, died of cancer Saturday at his home on Manhattan's Upper East Side. He was 54 years old.
        Mr. Chapman, a native of Chicago, graduated from the University of Michigan in 1954 and later from the Columbia Law School, where he was an editor of The Columbia Law Review and a Kent Scholar. He was admitted to the New York bar in 1957 and joined Cravath, Swaine as an associate.
        A partner in the firm since 1965, Mr. Chapman headed its tax department at the time of his death. Among his clients were Lazard Freres & Company, Salomon Brothers Inc., the First Boston Corporation, Dean Witter Reynolds Inc. and Paine Webber Inc.
        Mr. Chapman is survived by his wife, the former Helen M. Boyes; four children, Steven, Karen, Paul and Emily, all of Manhattan; a sister, Joan Lang of Sun City, Ariz.; and three brothers, John, of St. Petersburg, Fla., Stuart, of Macomb, Ill., and Roger, of New Providence, N.J.
        A memorial service is planned at 4 P.M. today in the Brick Presbyterian Church, Park Avenue and 91st Street.

        Dr. Aaron Janoff, an educator and research pathologist who won awards for his work on emphysema and smoking, died Sunday at his home in Setauket, L.I., after a long illness. He was 58 years old.
        Dr. Janoff, who received a Ph.D. in biology from New York University in 1959, was a professor of pathology at the State University of New York at Stony Brook. He was also a collaborating research scientist at Brookhaven National Laboratory in Upton, L.I. Before coming to Stony Brook in 1971, he taught at New York University.
        In 1987, he received awards from the National Institutes of Health and the American Thoracic Society.
        He is survived by his wife, Lenore Janoff, of Setauket; a son, Phillip, of Boston; two daughters, Diane, of Astoria, Queens, and Tami, of Yorktown Heights, N.Y., and one granddaughter.

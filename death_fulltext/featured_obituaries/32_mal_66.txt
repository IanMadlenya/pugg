        Richard M. Brett, business manager of the New York Public Library from 1947 until his retirement in 1953, died of a stroke Thursday at Hartford Hospital. He was 86 years old and lived in Bloomfield, Conn.
        Mr. Brett, a native of Darien, Conn., who spent most of his life in Woodstock, Vt., and Fairfield, Conn., was a graduate of the Taft School, Williams College and the Yale School of Forestry. He was treasurer and general manager of the Macmillan Company before World War II, during which he served in the Army Air Corps.
        In 1953 he moved to Vermont, where he set up an experimental woodlot - a tree farm with habitats for wildlife -at Hawk's Hill in East Barnard. He was a trustee of the Vermont Natural Resources Council, among other conservation organizations.
        Mr. Brett's first wife, the former Elizabeth Baldwin, died in 1984. Survivors include his second wife, the former Helen Shaw, and two daughters, Clare Smith of Farmington, Conn., and Betsy Carpenter of Los Altos, Calif.

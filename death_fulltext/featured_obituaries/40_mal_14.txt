        David G. Fernald, a former treasurer of Rockefeller Family and Associates, died of complications of leukemia on Monday at Mountainside Hospital in Montclair, N.J. He was 66 years old and lived in Montclair.
        Mr. Fernald was a 1947 graduate of Harvard University with a master's degree in business administration and a 1956 graduate of New York University with a doctorate in law.
        He joined Loomis, Suffern & Fernald, accountants, in 1947. The firm merged with Lybrand, Ross Bros. & Montgomery in 1958, and Mr. Fernald was a tax partner there until 1967.
        He then joined the Rockefeller interests, serving as treasurer of Rockefeller Family and Associates and of many of the Rockefeller corporations. He retired in 1988 but continued to serve as treasurer of the Rockefeller Brothers Fund, the Rockefeller Family Fund and the JDR 3rd Fund.
        He is survived by his wife, Judy; two sons, David Jr., of Stroudsburg, Pa., and John, of Montclair; a daughter, Julia, of Montclair; two brothers, Olaf, of Boca Raton, Fla., and Willard, of Columbus, Ohio, and two grandchildren.

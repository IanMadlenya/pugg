
        Mel Dinelli, a screenwriter and playwright who specialized in stories of suspense, died in Los Angeles on Nov. 28. He was 79 years old and lived in Los Angeles.
        He died of heart failure, said Bridget Aschenberg, his agent at International Creative Management in New York City.
        Mr. Dinelli wrote a number of radio and television scripts. His play "The Man," about a housewife held captive by a killer, was produced on Broadway in 1950 with Dorothy Gish.
        His screenwriting credits include "The Spiral Staircase" (1946), in which Dorothy McGuire played a mute menaced by a psychopath; "The Window" (1949), about a boy who is unable to convince anyone that he has witnessed a murder, and "Beware, My Lovely" (1952), with Ida Lupino as a widow trapped in a suburban house with a menacing stranger, played by Robert Mitchum.
        Mr. Dinelli was born in Albuquerque, N.M., and attended the University of Washington.
        He is survived by a nephew, Robert Matteucci of Albuquerque.

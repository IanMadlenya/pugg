        Joseph J. Lombardo, a New York State Supreme Court justice in Brooklyn, died of pneumonia on Tuesday at St. Vincent's Hospital in Manhattan. He was 75 years old and lived in Brooklyn.
        A 1938 graduate of St. John's University Law School, Justice Lombardo was in private practice before being elected to the bench in 1978. He was a former president of the Brooklyn Bar Association and a former national commander in chief of the Veterans of Foreign Wars.
        He is survived by three sons, Carmyn, of Washington; Francis, of Garden City, L.I., and Joseph, of Brooklyn; three sisters, Rose Fazzalare of Brooklyn, Angela Asher of Yorktown Heights, N.Y., and Natalie Lowen of West Palm Beach, Fla., and three grandchildren.

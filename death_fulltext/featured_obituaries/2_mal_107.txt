        Takeru Higuchi, a pharmaceutical chemist who developed pills that release medicine slowly into the bloodstream, died Tuesday at the University of Missouri-Columbia Hospital and Clinics. He was 69 years old.
        Mr. Higuchi, who held more than 100 patents in chemistry and pharmaceutics, died underwent cardiovascular bypass surgery March 18.
        Among his scientific accomplishments was the development of slow-release time pills, such as those used in nonprescription cold medicine and certain prescription drugs, including an antiglaucoma compound and a contraceptive used extensively in Europe.

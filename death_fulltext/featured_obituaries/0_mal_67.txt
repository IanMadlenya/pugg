        Tom Morgan, a former relief pitcher for the Yankees and one of the original members of the Los Angeles Angels in 1961, died today following complications from a stroke he suffered last week. He was 56 years old.
        Besides the Yankees and the Angels, he pitched for the Kansas City Athletics, the Detroit Tigers and the Washington Senators during his 12-year career. He had a 67-47 won-lost record for his career and a 3.61 earned run average. He appeared in three World Series while a member of the Yankees, in 1951, 1955 and 1956.

        Matthew E. Dann, former headmaster of the Trinity School in Manhattan and the Trinity-Pawling School in Pawling, N.Y., died Aug. 4 at Danbury (Conn.) Hospital of injuries from an automobile accident. He was 84 years old and lived in Pawling.
        Born in Delhi, N.Y., Mr. Dann earned a bachelor's degree and a master's degree from Columbia University.
        He joined the Trinity School as a mathematics teacher in 1927 and became headmaster in 1937. When Trinity bought the Pawling School in 1947, he became headmaster of that institution. In 1955, he resigned the Trinity post to devote his efforts to Trinity-Pawling. He retired as headmaster in 1971.

        Philip C. Rutledge, a retired partner in Mueser, Rutledge, Wentworth & Johnson, a consulting engineering firm, died Saturday at Indian River Estates Medical Center in Vero Beach, Fla. He was 84 years old and lived in Vero Beach.
        The family declined to reveal the cause of death.
        Mr. Rutledge, who joined the Manhattan firm in 1952, was known for his design of foundations for buildings, bridges, industrial plants and waterfront structures. He retired in 1977.
        In 1968 he was elected to the National Academy of Engineering. He was national director of the American Society of Civil Engineers from 1953 to 1955 and a member of the advisory committee on siting of a nuclear powered desalting plant for the Department of Interior. In 1949 he received the War Department certificate of appreciation for services in developing airfield pavements for heavy aircraft.
        He was an instructor at the Harvard Graduate School of Engineering from 1933 to 1947, a professor of civil engineering at Purdue University from 1937 to 1943 and a professor of civil engineering and department chairman at Northwestern University from 1943 to 1952.
        Philip Casteen Rutledge was born in Champaign, Ill., and graduated from Harvard in 1927. He received a master's in civil engineering from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology in 1933 and an Sc.D. from Harvard in 1939.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Catherine Porter; two children from a former marriage, John, of Boston, and Cecily Megrue of New Canaan, Conn., and three grandchildren.

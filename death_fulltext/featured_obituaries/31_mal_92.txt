        Joseph F. Hengemuhle, former assistant special agent for the Foreign Counterintelligence Division of the F.B.I.'s New York City office, died during open-heart surgery Monday at Columbia-Presbyterian Medical Center. He was 57 years old and lived in Westwood, N.J.
        Mr. Hengemuhle, who was born in Teaneck, N.J., graduated from Fordham University. He served with the Federal Bureau of Investigation for 35 years, in counterintelligence in New York and Washington, until he retired in 1987. He was an expert on espionage.
        He is survived by his wife, Agnes; four sons, Joseph, Daniel and John, all of Boulder, Colo., and Mark, of Union, N.J.; three daughters, Patricia Spirko of West Milford, N.J., Beth Hengemuhle of Mays Landing, N.J., and Jeanne Hengemuhle of Westwood; his mother, Nora, of Miami; two sisters, Ann Hoey of Miami and Mary Thomson of South Amboy, N.J., and a brother, William, of Ambler, Pa.

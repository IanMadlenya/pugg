
        George Watt, the retired administrator of the Community Mental Health Center at Maimonides Hospital in Brooklyn, died on Thursday at Columbia Presbyterian Hospital. Mr. Watt, who lived in Northport, L.I., was 80.
        The cause of death was cancer, said his son, Steven Watt.
        Mr. Watt joined the hospital in 1968 after graduating from Brooklyn College and receiving a master's degree in social work from New York University. He retired in 1982.
        Mr. Watt had previously been involved in Communist youth activities, and from 1937-39, he served as a political commissar in the Abraham Lincoln Brigade during the Spanish Civil War.
        Mr. Watt was a sergeant in the Army Air Force during World War II. A flight engineer, his B-17 was shot down over Belgium in 1943, and he made his way to his unit in England.
        After the war, Mr. Watt became an official of the American Communist Party and was one of a group of party officers convicted of sedition in 1953 and whose convictions were overturned on appeal. Mr. Watt broke with the Communist Party in 1958.
        He is survived by his wife, Margaret Wechsler Watt of Northport; two sons, Steven and Daniel, both of Cambridge, Mass.; four grandchildren, and four great-grandchildren. Mr. Watt's first wife was the late Ruth Rosenthal Watt.

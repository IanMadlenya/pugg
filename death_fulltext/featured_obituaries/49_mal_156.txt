
        W. R. Bruce Porter, founder of The Bruce Porter Company, which specialized in private fund raising for nonprofit groups, died yesterday at Memorial Sloan-Kettering Cancer Center. He was 43 years old and lived in Manhattan and Westport, Conn.
        He died of colon cancer, a spokeswoman for the family said.
        He founded the company in 1972. Its clients included Brandeis University, the Burden Center for the Aging, the Citizens Committee for New York City, the Citizens Union, the Floating Hospital, the Marine Biological Laboratory in Woods Hole, Mass., the Lenox Hill Neighborhood Association and the National Center for Health Education.
        Mr. Porter, a Republican, was involved in Connecticut politics and during President Gerald R. Ford's term of office handled press relations in the New York area for Betty Ford.
        He is survived by his mother, Marguerite F. Porter of Westport; two brothers, Briand, of St. Louis, and Brett, of Manhattan; a half-brother, Roger, of Miami, and a sister, Brook, also of Manhattan.

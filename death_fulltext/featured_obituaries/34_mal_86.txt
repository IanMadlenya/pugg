        Marshall D. Ketchum, professor emeritus of finance at the University of Chicago's Graduate School of Business, died of cancer on Sunday at his home in Chicago. He was 83 years old.
        Dr. Ketchum, who taught at the university from 1946 until 1971, was the author of more than 50 books and scholarly articles. For many years he conducted the business school's seminar for financial analysts.
        He was born in Buffalo and received bachelor's and master's degrees from Syracuse University and his doctorate from Chicago. Before joining the faculty there he taught at Duke University, Utah State and the University of Kentucky.
        He was a former president of the American Financial Association and was editor of its Journal of Finance from 1946 to 1955.
        Dr. Ketchum is survived by his wife, the former Clara Whitten; two sons, Marshall, of Chandler, Ariz., and Richard, of Las Cruces, N.M., and two grandsons.

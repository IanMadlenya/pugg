
       William Silent Hughes, an investment banker and former governor of stock exchanges on the East and West Coasts, died last Sunday at his home in San Marino, Calif. He was 84 years old.
        He died of degenerative heart disease, a family spokesman said.
        Mr. Hughes was president of Wagenseller & Durst, a Los Angeles investment banking concern, from 1948 to 1979 and was later vice chairman and a director. He was a governor of the New York Stock Exchange from 1966 to 1969 and the Pacific Stock Exchange from 1978 to 1984. He was also a member of the National Association of Security Dealers and of the Investment Bankers Association.
        He is survived by his wife, Marion; two daughters, Dorothy Matthiessen and Adele Bernwanger, both of Pasadena, Calif., and eight grandchildren.

        James Roy Smothers Jr., a college administrator and director of educational services for the United Negro College Fund, died of cardiac arrest Friday at New York Hospital-Cornell Medical Center in Manhattan. He was 68 years old and lived in Brooklyn.
        Mr. Smothers, who was educated at Fisk University in Nashville, spent his career developing educational opportunities for blacks in colleges and universities. He served with the fund from 1960 until his retirement in March 1986 and was also director of its national foundation support programs. He also served as registrar and director of admissions at Meharry Medical College in Nashville and at Fisk University.
        He is survived by a brother, Jerry, of Louisville, Ky.; two sons, James Roy 3d, of Nashville, and Dr. Kevin, of Stamford, Conn., and three grandchildren.

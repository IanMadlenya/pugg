        Nathan C. Belth, a writer and retired executive director of the Hebrew Free Loan Society, died of a heart attack yesterday at his home in Manhattan. He was 80 years old. Mr. Belth was director of the loan society from 1971 until his retirement in 1983. Previously, he was an official of the Anti-Defamation League of B'nai B'rith and a sportswriter with The Daily Mirror and The Brooklyn Eagle.
        His books included ''A Promise to Keep: A Narrative of the American Encounter with Anti-Semitism,'' published in 1979 by Times Books, and ''Barriers - Patterns of Discrimination Against Jews,'' published in 1958 by Friendly House.
        Mr. Belth was an alumnus of New York University.
        Surviving are a son, Don, and a daughter, Bernice Belth-Garbers, both of Manhattan; a sister, Frieda Springer of Brooklyn; a brother, Ira, of East Meadow, L.I., and seven grandchildren.

        Roger T. Briggs, who, as vice chairman of Esmark Inc. and later of the Beatrice Companies Inc., took part in the purchases and restructurings of dozens of companies, died of lung cancer Saturday in Chicago. He was 59 years old and lived in Wilmette, Ill.
        Mr. Briggs was for many years the top lieutenant of Esmark's chief executive, Donald P. Kelly. The two men were were admired for their dynamism and timing in juggling portfolios and buying, selling and sometimes buying back major companies in strategies that often pushed stock prices up.
        Mr. Briggs was, until last year, the executive vice president and chief financial officer of E-II Holdings Inc., a public company spun off from Beatrice. He was a native of Greenwich, Conn., where he attended public schools, and a graduate of the University of Miami in Florida.
        A Navy seaman in the 1950's, he was later a financial manager in Manhattan for Price Waterhouse Inc., the accounting concern, and for Sterling Drug Inc. He joined Esmark, which was then Swift & Company, a meat-packing house, in 1970.
        Surviving are his wife, Elizabeth; two daughters, Teresa Bridgman of Wilmette and Patricia Briggs of Chicago; a son, Roger T. Jr., of Manhattan; three brothers, Alfred, of North Palm Beach, Fla., George, of Greenwich, and Douglas, of North Miami Beach, Fla.; a sister, Nancy DeBolte, of Ormond Beach, Fla., and two grandchildren.

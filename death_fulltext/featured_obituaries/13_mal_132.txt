        Axel Axelsson Johnson, a Swedish industrialist and head of the Johnson Line, died of heart failure on Friday at his home in Naples, Fla. He was 77 years old.
        Mr. Johnson led the transformation of his family's shipping company into a diversified concern in oil, steel, agricultural research, computers, contracting, food wholesaling and stainless steel.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Antonia do Amaral Souza; a daughter, Antonia, and a brother, Bo of Stockholm; three sisters, Margaret von Bismarck of Geneva, Marie Claire Cronstedt of Vasteras, Sweden, and Mildren von Platen of Paris, and four grandchildren.

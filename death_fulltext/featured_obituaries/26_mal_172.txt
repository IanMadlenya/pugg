        William Jay Schieffelin 3d, the seventh generation of his family to head Schieffelin & Company, the Manhattan wine and spirit importer that was founded in 1794 as a pharmaceutical company, died yesterday at his home in Mount Kisco, N.Y. He was 67 years old.
        Mr. Schieffelin joined the company in 1948 after serving as an Army artillery officer in the China-Burma-India Theater of Operations. He was named vice president in charge of the pharmaceutical laboratories division in 1949 and became chairman and chief executive in 1962.
        He retired from active control of the company, later known as the Schieffelin & Somerset Company, in 1983, remaining as a consultant. Its parent is now LVMH Moet Hennessy-Louis Vuitton. He formally retired in 1986.
        Mr. Schiefflein was also chairman of Almay Inc., a cosmetics business, and the Simi Winery of Healdsburg, Calif.
        Mr. Schieffelin had also been a director of the New York Chamber of Commerce, chairman of the Commerce and Industry Association of New York, a director of the New York Board of Trade and a member of the French and British-American chambers of commerce.
        He was also a trustee of Tuskegee Institute, the Columbia College of Pharmacy and the Cisqua School in Mount Kisco, as well as an adviser to La Maison Francaise. This year, Mr. Schieffelin was an honorary trustee of the Northern Westchester Hospital Center in Mount Kisco.
        He graduated from Yale University in 1945.
        Surviving are his wife, Joy; a daughter, Hope Dooley of Mount Kisco; three sons, Timothy, of Darien, Conn., Andrew, of Westport, Conn., and Michael, of White Plains; his mother, Anette, and six grandchildren.

        Evelle Younger, a former Attorney General of California who as a district attorney oversaw the prosecutions of Charles Manson and Sirhan Sirhan, was found dead in his Hollywood home today, apparently of natural causes. He was 70 years old.
        In addition to his service as a prosecutor, Mr. Younger was a municipal and Superior Court judge. In 1978 he was the Republican nominee for Governor, losing to Gov. Edmund G. Brown Jr., after which he went into private practice.
        Born in Nebraska, Mr. Younger attended Northwestern Law School in Chicago. He was admitted to the California bar in 1946 and was a deputy city attorney in Los Angeles from 1946 to 1950. He became a Municipal Court judge in 1953 and was appointed to the Superior Court bench in 1958.
        In 1964 he became District Attorney in Los Angeles. In that position he oversaw the prosecution of Mr. Sirhan, who was convicted of assassinating Robert F. Kennedy in 1968, and Mr. Manson, convicted of masterminding the 1969 murders of the actress Sharon Tate and eight other people.
        He served as the California Attorney General from 1970 to 1978.
        Survivors include his wife, Mildred, and a son, Eric, a Superior Court judge.

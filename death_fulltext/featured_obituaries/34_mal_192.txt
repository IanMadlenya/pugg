        Nathan Edward Derecktor, a retired lawyer and real-estate developer, died on Saturday at Northern Westchester Hospital in Mount Kisco, N.Y. He was 95 years old and lived in Katonah, N.Y.
        Mr. Derecktor, who was called ''Red'' by friends and associates, was born in Meriden, Conn., and was an alumnus of Yale College.
        He had practiced law in Meriden after graduating from the Yale Law School. Then he worked on building and development and led management and development concerns in White Plains.
        He raised and bred Arabian horses as an avocation.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Lina Podoloff; a son, Robert, of Portsmouth, R.I.; a daughter, Tinka Topping of Sagaponack, L.I.; 10 grandchildren, and 7 great-grandchildren.

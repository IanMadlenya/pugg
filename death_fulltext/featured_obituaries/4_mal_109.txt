        Marcel Chandelier, a former head chef at Lutece restaurant in Manhattan and co-founder of Maison Germaine, a catering concern, died Saturday at his home in Long Island City, Queens. He was 53 years old.
        In addition to his wife, his mother, Georgette, and three brothers, Jacques, Georges and Jean Claude, survive.
        Mr. Chandelier was a member of the Maitres Cuisiniers de France, an order of French chefs. In Paris, he was head chef of Laperouse, a Left Bank landmark dating from the 19th century.


        Steven Alexander Kraft, a real-estate consultant and director of tennis programs, died on Saturday near Baltimore. He was 39 years old and lived in Baltimore.
        He drowned in a reservoir in Carroll County, Md., his brother Robert said.
        Mr. Kraft was a 1972 graduate of Harvard University and worked at Lipman, Frizzell & Mitchell, a real-estate firm in Luterville, Md.
        He had been the national coordinator of the United States Tennis Junior Development and director of the United States Open Junior Championship. He was a ranked junior tennis player as a teen-ager, wrote magazine articles and co-wrote two books about the sport.
        In addition to his brother Robert, of Los Angeles, he is survived by his parents, Lewis and Eve Kraft of Princeton, N.J. and another brother, Kenneth, of Haverford, Pa.

        Jospeh Crook Anderson, a retired Army colonel and former military attache, died of leukemia Monday at his home in Bethesda, Md. He was 75 years old.
        Colonel Anderson, who retired from the Army in 1962, was an account executive with Merrill Lynch, Pierce, Fenner & Smith in Washington until 1979. He was a 1935 graduate of the United States Military Academy and served with the Fifth Amphibious Force in the Pacific in World War II.
        His later positions included assistant military attache to Mexico, chief of the policy division for the Office of Security Affairs, commander of the 87th Infantry Regiment in Germany and assistant to the Joint Chiefs of Staff.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Eugenie Ward Riley; three sons, David, of Auburn, Me., and Alanson and Joseph, both of Bethesda; three daughters, Judith A. Vetter of Greenfield, Mass., Adelaide Anderson-Combellick, of Baltimore, and Eugenie R. R. Anderson, of Manhattan; two brothers, Seneca of Falmouth, Mass., and Harry of Sands Point, L.I.; a sister, Adelaide A. Cummings of Falmouth, Mass., and five grandchildren.

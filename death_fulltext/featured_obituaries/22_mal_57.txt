        Bill Hoest, an award-winning cartoonist whose work included ''The Lockhorns,'' died of lymphoma Monday at New York Medical Center. He was 62 years old and lived in Lloyd Neck, L.I.
        Mr. Hoest created ''The Lockhorns'' in 1968. Named best syndicated panel of 1976 and 1980 by the National Cartoonists Society, it appears in 500 newspapers and runs locally in The Daily News and New York Newsday.
        Mr. Hoest became cartoon editor of Parade magazine in 1979.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Bunny Mezz; his mother, Dorothea Whittinghill of Lloyd Neck; nine children and stepchildren.

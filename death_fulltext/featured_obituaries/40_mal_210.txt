        David Levine, a New York landscape architect, died of pneumonia on Tuesday at Los Gatos, Calif. He was 79 years old and moved to Los Gatos from the Bronx when he retired two years ago.
        Early in his career Mr. Levine was part of the New Deal public works program in the 1930's and continued to advise and assist various public projects until his retirement.
        In 1925 he was a founding partner and chief architect of John J. Kassner & Company in Manhattan. The company has since moved to Lake Success, L.I. Among his many projects for the company was the organization of the 1939 World's Fair in New York and the design of animal cages for the Bronx Zoo.
        Mr. Levine was a member of the American Society of Landscape Engineers and the New York Academy of Sciences. He was a member of the Art Commission of New York for two terms.
        A native New Yorker, Mr. Levine was a graduate of the New York University School of Architecture.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Sylvia Pargment; a son, Daniel, of Ann Arbor, Mich.; a daughter, Elanor Rusnak of Saratoga, Calif.; two sisters, Minnie Halkin of Jerusalem and Doris Fish of the Bronx; a brother, Irving, of Amherst, Mass.; five grandchildren, and two great-grandchildren.

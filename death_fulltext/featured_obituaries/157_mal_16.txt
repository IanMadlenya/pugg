
        Frank E. Morris, who was president of the Federal Reserve Bank of Boston from 1968 to 1988, died Monday at his home in Naples, Fla. He was 75.
        Earlier, Mr. Morris was an economist with the Federal Office of Price Stabilization and the Central Intelligence Agency, an assistant to the secretary of the Treasury in the Kennedy administration and a vice president of Loomis Sayles & Company, an investment firm.
        After leaving the Federal Reserve, Mr. Morris taught economics and finance for five years at Boston College.
        Frank Eugene Morris was born in Detroit on Dec. 30, 1924, graduated from Wayne State University and served in the Air Force during World War II. He earned a master's degree and doctorate in economics at the University of Michigan.
        He is survived by his wife, Geraldine; three daughters, Susan Carlson, of Acton, Mass.; Lisa Grobar, of La Crescenta, Calif.; and Betsy Rosen of Wellesley, Mass.; a sister, Dorothy Green, of Fort Lauderdale, Fla.; and six grandchildren.

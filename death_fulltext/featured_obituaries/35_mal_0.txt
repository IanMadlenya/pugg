        Francis J. McCaffrey, a lawyer and former Democratic State Senator from the Bronx, died of a stroke Wednesday at his home in the Riverdale section of the Bronx. He was 72 years old.
        He was an assistant district attorney in the Bronx from 1946 to 1948, when he became law secretary to Justice Edward R. Koch of State Supreme Court.
        He was elected to the State Senate in 1950 from the 29th District, which included Riverdale, and represented the district until 1956 when he was defeated for re-election.
        He then served as commissioner of records for the Bronx Surrogate Court and in 1961 went into private practice, specializing in admiralty law. He was with the Manhattan firm of Donovan, Maloof, Walsh & Kennedy until he retired in 1985.
        Mr. McCaffrey graduated from Cornell University Law School in 1942. He was a gunnery sergeant in the Army Air Force and flew 33 missions aboard a B-17 during World War II before being shot down and becoming a prisoner of war in 1944. He was freed at the end of the war.
        He is survived by his wife, Irene; two sons, Francis Jr., of Floral Park, L.I., and David, of the Bronx; two daughters, Joan Patton and Susan McCaffrey, both of Boston; two brothers, Joseph, of Lawrenceville, N.J., and Thomas, of Manhattan; a sister, Florence Mishaw of Ridgewood, N.J., and three grandchildren.

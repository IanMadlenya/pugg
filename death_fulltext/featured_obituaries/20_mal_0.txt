        Frederick Martin, a publishing executive, died of a stroke Friday at St. Francis Hospital in Jersey City. He was 51 years old and lived in Jersey City.
        Mr. Martin, who attended the University of Virgina, joined New Directions Publishers in 1967 as the managing editor. From 1975 to 1983 he served as vice president and managing director of the publishing house, which published works by Tennessee Williams, Thomas Merton, William Carlos Williams and Lawrence Ferlinghetti. Most recently he had been editing his own correspondence with many of them.
        He is survived by two sons, Alan and James, both of Snellville, Ga.

        Leonard Kudisch, an advertising research executive, died Thursday at Beth Israel Hospital in Boston after a long illness. He was 77 years old and lived in Brookline, Mass.
        Mr. Kudisch, a graduate of the State University of New York, was a co-founder and former president of Schwerin Research Corporation of New York, a national market research organization that pioneered studies of the impact of television commercials on consumers. He was a former trustee of the Westchester Reform Temple in Scarsdale, N.Y., and a former member of the Town Club of Scarsdale and the Overseas Press Club. In World War II, he was a captain in the Army Quartermaster Corps.
        He is survived by his wife, Doris; two daughters, Joanna Weinberg-Levine of Berkeley, Calif., and Diane, of San Francisco; a sister, Arlene Freedman, of Brookline, and two grandchildren.

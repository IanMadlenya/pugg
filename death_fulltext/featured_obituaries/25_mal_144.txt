        Dr. Cecil Mantell, a gastroenterologist and a founder of the Skylight Center, a new psychiatric rehabilitation center on Staten Island, died of complications from leukemia Monday at the Memorial Sloan-Kettering Cancer Center in Manhattan. He was 74 years old and lived in St. George, S.I.
        Dr. Mantell, who was born in Ste. Agathe, Quebec, earned his undergraduate and medical degrees from McGill University and did his graduate medical work at the University of Pennsylvania.
        From 1943 until his death, he was in private practice on Staten Island. He was on the staffs of St. Vincent's and Doctors Hospitals on Staten Island and was a former member of the board of directors of United Way of Staten Island and former president of the Jewish Foundation School of Staten Island.
        He is survived by his wife, Frances (Bobby); three daughters, Nancy Bokovza of Philadelphia, Helen Rodgen of Landing, N.J., and Barbara Mark of Sand Lake, N.Y.; a son, Stanley, of Staten Island; a brother, Nathan, of Pompano Beach, Fla.; three sisters, Esther Deitcher, Sonia Fink and Pearl Mantell, all of Montreal, and four grandchildren.

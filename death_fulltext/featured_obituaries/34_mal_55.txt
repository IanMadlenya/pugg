        Harold Greenstein, a former assistant New York attorney general, died of cancer Tuesday at New York Hospital. He was 83 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Greenstein was an assistant attorney general from the late 1920's to 1941. During World War II, he served as a lieutenant colonel on the Manhattan Project, which developed the atomic bomb. After the war, he went into private law practice and was also a real-estate developer in Westchester and in New York City.
        He was born in Manhattan and was a graduate of Brooklyn Law School in 1926.
        He is survived by two sons, David, of Manhattan, and Lewis, of Chevy Chase, Md.; a sister, Anna Gellen of Delray, Fla., and two granddaughters.

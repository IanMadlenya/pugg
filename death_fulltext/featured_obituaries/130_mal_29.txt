
        Adam Kennedy, a novelist, actor and artist, died on Oct. 16 at his home in Kent, Conn. He was 77.
        The cause was a heart attack, his family said.
        Mr. Kennedy was the author of 20 novels and 10 screenplays, including ''The Domino Principle'' (1975), which he also adapted for the screen. The 1977 film version, produced and directed by Stanley Kramer, starred Gene Hackman as a convict who becomes an assassin. His other novels included ''The Killing Season'' (1967), ''Maggie D.'' (1973), ''Just Like Humphrey Bogart'' (1978), ''The Fires of Summer'' (1987) and ''Somebody's Fool'' (1993).
        Mr. Kennedy also wrote a number of other screenplays, including ''Raise the Titanic,'' ''The Dove'' and ''Barlow's Kingdom.''
        Mr. Kennedy had a successful career as an actor, appearing onstage in ''Liliom,'' ''Period of Adjustment'' and ''Sabrina Fair,'' among others. His film credits included ''Act of Love,'' ''Men in War'' and ''Until They Sail.'' On television, he played the lead in two NBC series, ''The Californian'' and ''The Doctors.'' He appeared in more than 300 other shows, including ''Studio One,'' ''Playhouse 90'' and ''Gunsmoke.''
        Mr. Kennedy also painted. His oils and watercolors were shown in galleries and museums in the United States and Europe.
        Born on a farm near Otterbein, Ind., he was a graduate of DePauw University. He studied acting under Sanford Meisner at the Neighborhood Playhouse in Manhattan.
        He is survived by his wife, Susan Adams; two sons, Regan, of New York City, and Jack, of Santa Barbara, Calif.; a daughter, Anne Stromsted of Norway, and a grandson.

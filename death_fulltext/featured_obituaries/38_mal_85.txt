        Dr. Nicholas J. Pisacano, a physician who headed the American Board of Family Practice for 20 years, died on Sunday, apparently of a heart attack, at his home in Lexington, Ky. He was 65 years old.
        A 1951 graduate of Hahnemann Medical College, Dr. Pisacano was in private practice in Philadelphia before moving to Lexington. In 1969 he helped create the American Board of Family Practice, the certifying board for family practitioners. He resigned as executive director in January but remained as secretary.
        He taught at the University of Kentucky and was chairman of the hospital board there.
        He is survived by his wife, Virginia; four daughters, Toni Ann Myers of Fernandina, Fla., Nicki Rae Pass of Greenville, S. C., and Lori Sue Walls and Elizabeth Leigh Patterson, both of Lexington; two sons, Dean of Covington, Ky., and Don of Lexington; a brother, Frank of Wilmington, Del., and four grandchildren.

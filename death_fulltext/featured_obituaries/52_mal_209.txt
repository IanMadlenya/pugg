
        Foster Brand Whitlock, retired vice chairman of Johnson & Johnson and a leading figure in the pharmaceutical industry, died on Friday at Maine Coast Memorial Hospital in Ellsworth, Me. He was 76 years old and lived in Southwest Harbor, Me.
        He died of bone cancer, a family spokesman said.
        Mr. Whitlock retired from Johnson & Johnson in 1978 after 39 years with the company. He was appointed vice chairman of the board and president of Johnson & Johnson International in 1974.
        He joined the company in 1939 as a sales representative at Ortho Pharmaceutical Corporation, a subsidiary, in Raritan, N.J.
        During his career, he was also president of the American Foundation for Pharmaceutical Education, chairman of the Pharmaceutical Manufacturers Association and president of the International Federation of Pharmaceutical Manufacturers in Zurich.
        A native of Highland Park, N.J., Mr. Whitlock attended Rutgers University and graduated from the University of Wisconsin with a degree in electrical engineering.
        He was a trustee of Rutgers.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Edna Evans; three daughters, Charlane Mowery of McHenry, Ill., Frances Edmonson of Evanston, Ill., and Janet Wise of Sarasota, Fla.; a son, Foster Brand, of Pennington, N.J.; two brothers, Charles, of Gloucester, Mass., and Baird, of Belfast, Me.; a sister, Eloise Dunn of Franklin Park, N.J., and four grandchildren.

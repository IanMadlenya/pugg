        Sandra M. Schor, a poet, novelist and associate professor of English at Queens College, died on Monday at the Montefiore Medical Center in the Bronx. She was 58 years old and lived in Locust Valley, L.I., and in Manhattan. Her family said she died of cancer.
        A novel by Professor Schor, ''The Great Letter E,'' was published by North Point Press earlier this year. She was also the author of textbooks and scholarly articles on composition. Her poems and stories appeared in The Sewanee Review, Ploughshares and other literary journals.
        She was born in Richmond Hill, Queens, and received a bachelor's degree from Queens College and a master's from Columbia University. Before she joined the Queens College faculty in 1972 she taught at C. W. Post College.
        She is survived by her husband, Dr. Joseph M. Schor; a daughter, Esther, of Metuchen, N.J; two sons, Joshua, of Brookline, Mass., and Gideon, of Manhattan; two brothers, Jack Moshman of Bethesda, Md., and Bert Moshman of Kew Gardens, Queens, and three grandchildren.

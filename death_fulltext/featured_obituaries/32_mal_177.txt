        John T. Koehler, a former Assistant Secretary of the Navy and acting chairman of the Federal Maritime Board, died of cancer Saturday at his home in Chevy Chase, Md. He was 85 years old.
        Mr. Koehler joined the Treasury Department in 1933, a few years after graduating from Harvard University Law School. He was a commander in the Navy in World War II and joined the Navy Department as a lawyer in 1946. From 1949 to 1951 he was Assistant Secretary of the Navy and, in addition, acting chairman of the Federal Maritime Board. He returned to private practice in 1953 and retired in 1985.
        He is survived by his wife, Eleanor; a son, John, of Wendell, Mass.; two daughters, Mathilde Rothwell, of Brunswick, Me., and Elizabeth Peirce, of Arlington, Va.; two brothers, Richard and William Koehler, both of Pittsburgh; two sisters, Ruth Brown, of Victoria, British Columbia, and Dorothy Holl, of Johnstown, Pa.; and two grandchildren.

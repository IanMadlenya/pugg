        William W. Keeler, the former president and chief executive of the Phillips Petroleum Company and former chief of the Cherokee Nation of Oklahoma, died todayat the Heritage Manor Nursing Home here. He was 79 years old and had been in ill health for four years. .
        Mr. Keeler, born in Dalhart, Tex., was brought to Bartlesville as a child. He was a graduate of the high school here and the University of Kansas.
        Mr. Keeler was named principal chief of the Cherokees in 1949 and was reappointed until 1971, when the tribe won permission to elect a chief. He then was elected to a four-year term.
        He joined Phillips in 1924 as a part-time employee, gaining full-time status four years later. Mr. Keeler became executive vice president in 1956, chairman of the executive committee in 1962 and president and chief executive in 1968, serving until 1973.
        He is survived by his wife, Ruby, three sons and six grandchildren.

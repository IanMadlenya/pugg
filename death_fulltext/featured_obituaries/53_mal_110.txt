
        Dr. Milton Tarlau, a psychiatrist and neurologist with practices in Manhattan and eastern Pennsylvania, died on Wednesday at his home in Riegelsville, Pa. He was 81 years old.
        He died of bone cancer, his brother, Theodore, said.
        After graduating from the Long Island College of Medicine in 1934, Dr. Tarlau interned at City Hospital in New York and was a psychiatric resident at the State Hospital in Central Islip, L.I. He took post graduate training in nuerology at Kings County Hospital in Brooklyn.
        He was in private practice and held positions as a neurologist in Manhattan at Goldwater Memorial Hospital, Bellevue Hospital Center and the Veterans Administration Hospital and at Easton Hospital in Easton, Pa.
        In addition to his brother, Theodore, of Manhattan, he is survived by two daughters, Ann Crystal of Falls Church, Va., and Judith Claps of Hellertown, Pa., five grandchildren, and one great-grandchild.

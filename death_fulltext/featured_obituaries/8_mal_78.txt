        Henry A. Courtlandt, senior vice president of the New York Urban Coalition, died of cancer Saturday at New York University Hospital. He was 58 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Courtlandt had been on the boards of One Hundred Black Men, the Boys Choir of Harlem and United Neighborhood Houses. He worked for 21 years at the Consolidated Insurance Company, where he was a senior executive in the claims department.
        Mr. Courtlandt attended Cornell University, Columbia University and the St. John's Law School.
        Surviving are his wife, Dorothy; two daughters, Dr. Cheryl and Catherine, and a son, Anthony, all of New York.

        Daniel P. A. Sweeney, a retired lawyer and former chief of the New York City Police Department's legal bureau, died on Wednesday at his home in Breezy Point, Queens. He was 85 years old.
        Mr. Sweeney, a native of the Bronx, was a graduate of Fordham University in 1928 and Fordham University Law School in 1932. He joined the Police Department in 1926 and retired in 1948. He rose to captain and was chief of the legal bureau from 1940 to 1948.
        He was also counsel and legal adviser to Mayor Fiorello H. La Guardia and legal counsel to several police commissioners.
        He is survived by two daughters, Joan O'Connor of Brookfield, Conn., and Patricia Sheehy of Weston, Conn.; a son, Daniel J., of Washington; 13 grandchildren, and three great-grandchildren.


        Dr. Irving W. Kramer, a retired physician and hospital administrator, died on Sunday at his winter home in Bermuda Dunes, Calif. He was 82 and also lived in Floral Park, Queens.
        The cause of death was a stroke, his family said.
        Dr. Kramer practiced for 40 years as a family physician in Great Neck, L.I., starting in 1937 until his retirement. He also served as the first chief of general practice at North Shore University Hospital in Manhasset, L.I.
        Dr. Kramer, who was born in Brooklyn, graduated from Dartmouth College in 1932 and earned a medical degree at New York University in 1935. His internship and residency were at Kings County Hospital in Brooklyn.
        Surviving are his wife of 57 years, the former Ruth Eisenberg; a son, Kenneth, of Muttontown, L.I.; three daughters, Penny Carpenter of Sacramento, Calif., Dr. Karen Hein of Manhattan and Jacqueline Kupper of Scarsdale, N.Y.; a sister, Rose Gennes, who lives on Cape Cod in Massachusetts; nine grandchildren and two great-grandchildren.


        Stanley Jay Makowsky, a prominent figure in radio sports broadcasting under the name Stan Martyn for decades, died on March 1 at Temple University hospital in Philadelphia. He was 68.
        The cause of death was heart failure, according to his wife, Rose-Ila.
        Mr. Makowsky, a resident of Brooklyn, covered five Olympic Games, from the Mexico City Summer Games in 1968 through the 1992 Summer Games in Barcelona. In the late 1970's, he was also the host of a nationally broadcast morning sports show on NBC radio and of the radio network's National Football League pregame and halftime shows.
        Before joining NBC in 1978, Mr. Makowsky was a news and sports reporter for the ABC Entertainment Radio Network from 1968 through 1978. After he left NBC, he worked as sports director of WFAN, the New York radio station, from 1988 to 1992, and as a sports commentator for WBBR, another New York radio station, from 1993 to 1997.
        In addition to his wife, Mr. Makowsky is survived by two sons, Kenneth, of Minneapolis, and Monroe, of Chicago; a daughter, Hindy, of Queens, and a sister, Arline Lanin of East Brunswick, N.J.

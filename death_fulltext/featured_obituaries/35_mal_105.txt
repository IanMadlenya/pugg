        George Marion Fanelli, a retired New York State Supreme Court Justice and a former District Attorney of Westchester County, died of congestive heart failure yesterday at a nursing home in Lansdale, Pa. He was 92 years old and had lived in Lansdale for the last year after growing up and living in New Rochelle.
        Judge Fanelli, a graduate of Fordham Law School, was in private practice until 1936, when he became assistant district attorney and, in 1946, District Attorney of Westchester County.
        In 1953 he was appointed a Westchester County judge by Governor Thomas E. Dewey. In 1957 he was appointed a New York State Supreme Court Justice for the Ninth Judicial District by Gov. Nelson A. Rockefeller. He was later elected to the post. He retired in 1974.
        Judge Fanelli is survived by a son, George Jr., of Lansdale; a brother, Sullivan, of Manhattan; three sisters, Virginia McKenna and Josephine Fanelli, both of New Rochelle, and Regina McLaren, of Delmar, N.Y.; four grandchildren, and eight great-grandchildren.

        George Gentry Griffin, a voice coach who worked with many Hollywood stars in a long career, died on Wednesday in Century City, Calif. He was 80 years old.
        Mr. Griffin coached more than 100 film and stage personalities, including Jean Stapleton, Gloria Swanson, Hal Holbrook, Richard Chamberlain, Rock Hudson, Raquel Welch, Mary Tyler Moore and Marlo Thomas.
        He was born in Bryan, Tex., and had a singing career in operatic and musical-comedy roles, but eventually devoted all his time to teaching, which he continued until last year.
        Mr. Griffin is survived by a niece, Genevieve Meek of Dallas.

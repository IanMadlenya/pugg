        C. Robert Haines, a retired banker in Philadelphia and New York, died on Thursday at Shore Memorial Hospital in Somers Point, N.J. He was 79 years old and lived in Linwood, N.J. The cause of death was pneumonia, family members said.
        Mr. Haines was born in Philadelphia and was a graduate of Haverford College. He joined the Corn Exchange National Bank of Philadelphia and rose to vice president in charge of the foreign department.
        After a merger with the Girard Trust Bank, Mr. Haines became a senior vice president of Girard and organized the Girard International Bank of New York, of which he was president. He retired in 1975.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Mildred Tapper; two daughters, Jeanette Boothy of Beckenham, Kent, England, and Helen Thieler of Northfield, N.J.; a son, Rush of Haverford, Pa., and five grandchildren.

        Dr. Allan A. Gutstein, a dentist who was among the first to establish group dental-care centers in suburban shopping malls, died Tuesday at Lenox Hill Hospital. He was 43 years old, lived in Manhattan and suffered from hypertension.
        Dr. Gutstein operated the Universal Dental Centers on Long Island, the Dental Works near Allentown, Pa., and Omnidentics in the Boston area.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Blanche Labell, two sons, Chad and Drew, a daughter, Bret, all of Manhattan; his parents, Sarah and Philip, of Deerfield Beach, Fla.; a sister, Corinne Weinstein, of Valley Stream, L.I., and a brother, Dr. Harold Gutstein, of Manhattan.

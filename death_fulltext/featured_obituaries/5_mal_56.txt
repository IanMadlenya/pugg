        John M. Lyden, a former vice chairman of Ted Bates and Company, the advertising concern, died at his home in New Canaan, Conn. yesterday after a long illness. He was 81 years old.
        Mr. Lyden was an official of the Bates agency from 1942 to 1967. He also did volunteer work for the Advertising Council and raised money for Radio Free Europe.
        After leaving the advertising agency, he spent several years with the Tiderock Corporation, a think tank for corporations that he helped form.
        Mr. Lyden was born in Youngstown, Ohio, and graduated from Harvard College in 1928.
        He is survived by his wife, Ruth; a son, John, of Manhattan; a daughter, Lucy Anderson of New Canaan; a brother, Martin, of Climax, Pa., and five grandchildren.

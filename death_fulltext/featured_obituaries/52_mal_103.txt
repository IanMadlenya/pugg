
        John Gray Trezevant, a retired news executive, died Thursday at Marin County General Hospital in Greenbrae, Calif. He was 67 years old and lived in Tiburon, Calif.
        The cause of death was hepatitis, a spokeswoman for the family said.
        Mr. Trezevant began his career as a member of the editorial staff of The San Francisco Chronicle, where he worked from 1942 to 1955. After joining the Crowell-Collier Publishing Company in Manhattan, he became managing editor of Colliers magazine in 1956. He was named Sunday editor of The Chicago Sun-Times in 1958 and from 1962 to 1964 was the paper's managing editor. In 1964 he became executive vice president of The Sun-Times, a position he held until he retired in 1977.
        Among his other posts were those of executive committee member of Field Enterprises, chairman of the Field Newspaper Syndicate and board member of World Book-Childcraft International Inc. and of Pacific Forest Products Ltd.
        Mr. Trezevant was born in Alameda, Calif., and graduated from the University of California at Berkeley in 1943.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Dorothy Jean Heath; three sons, Robert, of Los Angeles; Richard, of San Francisco, and John, of Santa Rosa, Calif., and a granddaughter.

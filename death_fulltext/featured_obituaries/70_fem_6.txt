
        Sylvia Friedlander Robins, a theater and television producer, died on Saturday at her home in Manhattan. She was 74 years old.
        She died of natural causes, said her husband, Sy R. Robins.
        Mrs. Robins's credits include "Bathsheba," a 1947 play that featured the actor James Mason in his first Broadway appearance. From 1975 until 1989 she was the producer of the syndicated television talk show "All About TV."
        Mrs. Robins was born in New York. She attended Smith College and began her career in the 1940's working with the producer David Susskind on television projects including "The Kay Kayser Show" and "The Nash Airflite Theater."
        In addition to her husband, she is survived by two daughters, Claudia Whitman of Peaks Island, Me., and Debbie Robins of Los Angeles, and a brother, Norman Friedlander of Massapequa, L.I.

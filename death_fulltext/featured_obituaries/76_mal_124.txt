
        Jack Allen Kapland, an orthopedic surgeon in New York City for more than 45 years, died on Wednesday at St. Luke's-Roosevelt Hospital Center. He was 79 and lived in Manhattan and Palm Beach, Fla.
        The cause was pneumonia, said his daughter, Bonnie Postel.
        At his death, Dr. Kapland was attending physician in orthopedics at St. Luke's-Roosevelt Hospital Center. For many years, he was orthopedic surgeon at the former Polyclinic-French Hospital and professor of orthopedics at New Jersey College of Medicine.
        In 1990, Dr. Kapland established the Jack Allen Kapland lectureship and chair in orthopedic surgery at the University of Maryland Medical School. He was a fellow of the American Academy of Orthopedic Surgeons and the New York Academy of Medicine.
        He was born in New York City. A graduate of the University of Michigan, he received a medical degree from the University of Maryland in 1938.
        Besides his daughter, Bonnie, of Manhattan, survivors include his wife, Shirley; a son, Michael, of Los Angeles, and two grandsons.


        Charles Maurice Gourdon, a retired silk manufacturer died on Tuesday at his home in Southampton, L.I. He was 88 years old.
        He died of cancer, said his wife, Eunice.
        Mr. Gourdon, who was born in Lyons, France, was educated at the Ecole de Commerce de Lyons and represented his grandfather's concern in Canada and New York before founding his own company, C. M. Gourdon, Inc., in Manhattan in 1929. He became known for his high-fashion prints and brocades and won many awards for his fabrics and designs, including the American Fabrics Excellence Award. He retired in 1976.
        He is also survived by a son, Maurice Pierre, of Manhattan; a daughter, Suzanne Bovenizer of Reigate, England; and two grandchildren.

        Emmanuel Ellenis, a public relations executive, died of a heart attack Thursday at New York University Medical Center. He was 61 years old and lived in Dix Hills, L.I.
        Mr. Ellenis was the founder and for 28 years executive vice president of Development Counselors International, a Manhattan company active in economic development and tourism marketing. He had been director of public relations for the Government of Puerto Rico and plastics editor of the New York Journal of Commerce. He was a graduate of New York University.
        Mr. Ellenis is survived by his wife, the former Carol Van Der Mark; a daughter, Susan Ambrosio of East Northport, L.I.; a son, Steven, of Matawan, N.J.; a brother, Thomas, of Wayne, N.J.; a sister, Irene Ploss of Jacksonville, Fla.; four stepchildren, and two grandchildren.

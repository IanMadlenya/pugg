        Gregory Henderson, a former Foreign Service Officer and a specialist on Korea, died at Lawrence Memorial Hospital in Medford, Mass., on Sunday as a result of injuries suffered in a fall. He was 66 years old and lived in Medford.
        Mr. Henderson, who held graduate and undergraduate degrees from Harvard University, was a research associate at the John K. Fairbank Center for East Asian Research at Harvard University and had taught at the Fletcher School of Law and Diplomacy at Tufts University. He served with the United States Foreign Service at posts in Washington and in South Korea, Germany and Japan. He was the author of ''Korea: The Politics of the Vortex,'' published by Harvard University Press in 1968.
        He is survived by his wife, Maria von Magnus Henderson, of Medford; his mother, Lucy G. Henderson, of Cambridge, Mass., and a sister, Martha H. Coolidge, of Beverly, Mass.

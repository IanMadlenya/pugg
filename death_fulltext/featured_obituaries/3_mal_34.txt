        Ezekiel Schloss, an editor and political cartoonist, died after a long illness on March 30 at Roosevelt Hospital. He was 74 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        From 1942 to 1977, he was editor of World Over, a magazine for Jewish children published by the Board of Jewish Education in Manhattan. His political cartoons appeared in France Amerique, The New Republic and The Nation.
        Mr. Schloss, who was born in Latvia, came to the United States in 1940. He and his wife, Lillian, acquired a notable collection of Chinese tomb sculptures. In December 1984, they sold 50 major pieces from the collection for nearly $2 million at Sotheby's in New York.
        In addition to his wife, the former Lillian Sel, he is survived by a daughter, Simone Schloss of Manhattan.

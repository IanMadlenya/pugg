        The Rev. William W. Flanagan, an assistant professor of economics at Fordham University since 1960 and dean of the School of General Studies from 1969 to 1973, died of a heart attack Thursday at Loyola Hall, the Jesuit residence on the Bronx campus. He was 69 years old.
        Father Flanagan was a native of New York City and held a master's degree in economics from Fordham. He joined the Jesuit order in 1941 and was ordained in 1952, after which he taught at Canisius College in Buffalo.
        He is survived by a brother, James, of Sparta, N.J.


        Howard Wolfe Chalk, an advertising executive, died on Dec. 12 at Phelps Memorial Hospital Center in Sleepy Hollow, N.Y. Mr. Chalk, a resident of Cortlandt Manor, N.Y., was 76.
        The cause was lung cancer, his daughter, Robin Chalk Levy, said.
        Mr. Chalk's clients were mostly fashion accounts, and included Spring Mills, After Six, Pierre Cardin and British Knights. He started several advertising agencies, beginning with Chalk, Nissen, Hanft in 1968. He left that company when it was acquired in 1986 and started Chalk & Partners.
        In 1995, he closed Chalk & Partners and then started Aerie Marketing.
        Mr. Chalk attended New York University, and served in the Army in World War II. After the war, he joined Sterling Advertising.
        In 1955, he was hired by Altman Stoller as an account executive and eventually became a partner. He left what was then called Altman, Stoller, Chalk to start Chalk, Nissen, Hanft.
        Mr. Chalk is survived by his wife, Shirley, of Cortlandt Manor; a  daughter, Robin, of Chappaqua, N.Y.; a son, Russell, of Austin, Tex., and two grandchildren.

        Moses Moskowitz, secretary general of the Consultative Council of Jewish Organizations, died of pneumonia yesterday at Parkway Hospital in Forest Hills, Queens. He was 80 years old and lived in Flushing.
        Mr. Moskowitz, the author of four books on human rights and international law, had headed the council since its formation in 1947. The council is a nongovernmental organization accredited to the United Nations and the Council of Europe.
        Mr. Moskowitz was born in Stryj, in the Ukraine, and came to the United States as a teen-ager. He held a bachelor's degree from City College and did graduate work at Columbia.
        Among his books were ''Human Rights and World Order,'' published in 1958, and ''The Roots and Reaches of United Nations Actions and Decisions,'' published in 1980.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Leah Salvin; a son, Howard, of Hartsdale, N.Y.; a daughter, Judith Kunstadt of Ardsley, N.Y.; a brother, Ira, of Manhattan; a sister, Rita Stone of Cleveland, and four grandchildren.

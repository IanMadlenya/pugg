        Sir Roger Mynors, one of Britain's leading classical scholars, was killed Tuesday in a car crash. He was 86 years old.
        Sir Roger was educated at Eton and Balliol College, Oxford. A former professor of Latin at Cambridge and Oxford, he was an expert on ancient church manuscripts. In 1963, he was knighted by Queen Elizabeth II. Three years later, he served as president of the Classical Association.
        He published several books and received honorary degrees from a number of universities in Britain and at the University of Toronto. Sir Roger was also an honorary member of the American Academy of Arts and Sciences and the American Philosophical Society.


        Oliver DeGray Vanderbilt, a businessman and financier, died on Tuesday at Jupiter Medical Center in Jupiter, Fla. He was 85 and had lived in Hobe Sound, Fla., since 1983.
        Over the years, Mr. Vanderbilt was an executive of more than a dozen corporations, including Blair & Company, a brokerage firm, where he was executive vice president and then chairman for more than a decade.
        Mr. Vanderbilt was born in Manhattan, graduated from St. Paul's School in Concord, N. H., and received a bachelor's degree in 1937 from Princeton, where he was on the polo team.
        He served in the Army in Britain, North Africa and Europe during World War II, rose to major and was awarded the Bronze Star and the Croix de Guerre.
        In the early 1950's, Mr. Vanderbilt was president of the Taylor-Wharton Iron and Steel Company, which was based in a Cincinnati suburb. The company made railroad and industrial trackwork.
        Later he was a founder of the Seaboard Savings Bank in Stuart, Fla., and became its first chairman.
        Mr. Vanderbilt's survivors include his wife, the former Frances M. Philips; a son, Oliver DeGray Vanderbilt Jr. of Molokai, Hawaii; a daughter, Madelon V. Peck of Altadena, Calif.; eight grandchildren and eight great-grandchildren.

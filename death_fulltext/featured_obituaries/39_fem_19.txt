        Miriam Witkin, a soprano who sang on several New York stations in the early days of radio and who gave concerts at Aeolian and Chickering Halls, died in her sleep on Sunday at a family home in Bridgewater, Conn., after a brief illness. She was 97 years old.
        Mrs. Witkin was born in Helena, Ark., and moved to New York City in 1914. In the early 1930's, she used the name Mary Windsor in broadcasting a children's music series on station WOR.
        She is survived by her sons, Richard and William; a sister, Elinor; five grandchildren, and a great-granddaughter.

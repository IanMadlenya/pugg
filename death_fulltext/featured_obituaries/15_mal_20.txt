        Anthony Pelissier, an actor who later won fame as a director of plays and films, died Saturday in Eastbourne. He was 75 years old.
        Mr. Pelissier was the screenwriter and director of two widely shown films, ''The History of Mr. Polly'' based on an H. G. Wells novel, and ''The Rocking Horse Winner'' based on a D. H. Lawrence short story.
        He is survived by his fourth wife, the actress Ursula Howells, and by four children.

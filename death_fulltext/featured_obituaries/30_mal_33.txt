        Dr. D. Anthony D'Esopo, a retired surgeon and professor emeritus at the Columbia University College of Physicians and Surgeons, died of pneumonia Monday at Glover Memorial Hospital in Needham, Mass., where he lived. He was 91 years old and had lived in Tenafly, N.J., and Alpine, N.J.
        Dr. D'Esopo was an obstetrician and gynecologist in Manhattan. He taught at Columbia from 1928 to 1963.
        He was born in Hartford. Dr. D'Esopo graduated from Yale University and its medical school.
        Surviving are two sons, Donato Anthony Jr., of Palo Alto, Calif., and Peter, of Ramsey, N.J.; a daughter, Jane McKinnell of Newton, Mass.; three sisters, Carmella and Theresa D'Esopo and Grace Cassell, all of West Hartford, Conn.; two brothers, Joseph, of Hamden, Conn., and Nicholas, of Woodbridge, Conn.; eight grandchildren, and three great-grandchildren.

        Dr. David I. Arbuse, a psychiatrist and neurosurgeon, died of a heart attack yesterday at his home in Manhattan. He was 89 years old.
        Dr. Arbuse took his psychiatric residency at Mount Sinai Hospital and was a neuropsychiatrist for the Navy from 1942 to 1946. He was an attending neurologist at hospitals in the Bronx and Manhattan, most recently at the Montefiore Medical Center. He closed his practice in 1983. From 1948 to 1958, he was a clinical professor of neurology at New York Medical College.
        He served in the Naval Reserve Medical Corps for 25 years and retired as a captain.
        He graduated from the Long Island College of Medicine in 1923.
        Surviving are his wife, Joan; a son, Dr. Samuel Arbuse and a brother, Bernard, both of Palm Beach, Fla.

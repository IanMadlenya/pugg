
        S. Gene Odle, provost emeritus of Alfred (N.Y.) University, died yesterday at St. James Mercy Hospital in Hornell, N.Y., a university spokeswoman said. He was 64 years old and lived in Alfred.
        The cause of his death was not disclosed.
        Dr. Odle joined the nonsectarian university in 1970 as vice president for student affairs. He was named vice president for academic affairs in 1975 and provost in 1976. He retired in 1988.
        He was born in Caldwell, Idaho, and graduated from the College of Idaho there. He served in the infantry in World War II. He received a master's degree from Columbia in 1951 and a doctorate from Stanford in 1955. He later taught at the University of Minnesota.
        He is survived by a daughter, Andrea McConnell of Millis, Mass.; a son, Shawn, of Washington; and his father and stepmother, Glen and Lucille Odle of Whiteland, Ind.

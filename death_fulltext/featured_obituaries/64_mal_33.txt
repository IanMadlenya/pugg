
        William T. Collins 2d, a lawyer specializing in trusts and estates, died on Monday in Carleton House, a nursing home in Fairfield, Conn. He was 75 and lived in New Rochelle, N.Y.
        He died of cancer, his son Patrick said.
        Mr. Collins, the son of Cornelius F. Collins, a prominent New York State Supreme Court justice, earned a bachelor's degree at Holy Cross College in 1938 and and a law degree at Catholic University in 1941. He served with the Navy intelligence forces in the Pacific from 1941 to 1945.
        After the war, he joined the New York firm of Davis Polk & Wardwell and then formed his own firm, Perkins, Daniels, McCormack & Collins, with which he remained until his death. He was also an adjunct professor at New York Law School for many years beginning in the 1950's.
        Mr. Collins was named commander of the New York County Post of the American Legion for 1950-51, becoming the first World War II veteran to head a major post of the organization.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Rosemary Powers; five sons, William T. 3d of Stamford, Conn., Neil V. of Basking Ridge, N.J., Edward P. of Westwood, Mass., Michael F. X. of Atlanta, and Patrick M. of Larchmont, N.Y.; and four daughters, Catherine C. Emmanuel of Fairfield, Conn., Carolyn C. Kostiw of McLean, Va., Rosemary C. Weiss of Fairfield, Iowa, and Tracy M. of Waterford, Conn.; a sister, Patricia Fitzpatrick of Staten Island, and 20 grandchildren.

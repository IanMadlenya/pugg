
        Ward B. Gordon, an insurance executive, died on Tuesday at Mount Sinai Medical Center in Manhattan. He was 60 years old and lived in Weston, Conn., Manhattan, and Estes Park, Colo.
        He died of complications from colon cancer, his family said.
        In January, Mr. Gordon became president of the Brokers and Reinsurance Association, a national trade organization. From 1984 to 1987, he served as a governor of the New York Insurance Exchange.
        Born in Des Moines, he grew up there and in Akron, Ohio. He was a graduate of Kenyon College and the Wharton School of Finance. After serving in the Navy as a lieutenant commmander, he started in the insurance business in 1957 at Marsh & McLennan in Manhattan. In 1962, he joined Sterling Offices Ltd. and played a major role in its expansion into Intere Intermediaries Inc. He rose to president and chief operating officer of Intere in 1977, chief executive officer in 1979 and chairman in 1982.
        He is survived by his wife, Jane; a son, Alan, of Florida; a daughter, Lynn Gordon Sellon of Westport, Conn., and two grandsons.


        Alan Bush, a British composer, pianist and conductor, died on Oct. 31 at Watford, just northwest of London. He was 94 and lived in Radlett, near Watford.
        Mr. Bush was born in London, studied composition and piano at the Royal Academy of Music and then enrolled as a student of philosophy at Berlin University. On his return to England, he conducted many choral concerts and became involved in the Workers Music Association.
        His compositions include several symphonies, chorales and works for piano and strings. He wrote "Wat Tyler," an opera that was among four winners in a competition held for the Festival of Britain in 1951.
        He is survived by two daughters.

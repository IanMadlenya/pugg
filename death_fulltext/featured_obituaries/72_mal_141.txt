
        Dr. Wayne H. Decker, a retired obstetrician and gynecologist who specialized in the treatment of infertility, died on Monday at Phelps Memorial Hospital in North Tarrytown, N.Y. He was 70 and lived in Scarborough, N.Y.
        He died of heart failure, his family said.
        For many years Dr. Decker practiced in Manhattan and was also the executive director of the Fertility Research Foundation of New York. His father, Albert, was a founder of the foundation.
        Dr. Decker was one of the first to use cryogenics to create human sperm banks of frozen semen for artificial insemination. He also founded a journal, Infertility, and wrote a textbook, "Office Gynecology."
        He was an associate clinical professor of obstetrics and gynecology at New York University's School of Medicine and worked as an associate obstetrician and gynecologist at Lenox Hill Hospital.
        Dr. Decker was born in New York City. He earned a bachelor's degree at Cornell University and his medical degree at New York Medical College.
        Dr. Decker, whose first marriage ended in divorce, is survived by his wife of nearly 21 years, Terry Howard; a son, Wayne, of Cuddebackville, N.Y.; two daughters, Nancy Kirman of Boston and Doris Ann Decker of Florence, Italy, and three grandchildren.

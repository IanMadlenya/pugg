        Robert Merriman Young, director of economic services at the Primerica Corporation, died Thursday at St. Vincent's Hospital in Manhattan of complications from AIDS. He was 55 years old and lived in Manhattan and Sag Harbor, L.I.
        Mr. Young held economic posts for 20 years with the American Can Company, recently renamed Primerica. Previously he had been an economist with the Federal Reserve Bank in Atlanta and with McGraw-Hill Inc.
        He was a native of Utica, N.Y., and received a Bachelor of Arts degree from Maryville (Tenn.) College and a Master of Arts from Emory University. He served two years in the Army as an accountant.
        Surviving are two sons, Paul, of Boone, N.C., and David, of Jacksonville, Fla.; a daughter, Victoria, of Manhattan; his mother, Mary Tippett Young of Atlanta; his stepmother, Virginia Brady of Hamden, Conn., and a sister, Mary Elizabeth Young of Rochester.

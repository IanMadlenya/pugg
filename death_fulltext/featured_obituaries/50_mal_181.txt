
        The Rev. Marshal Logan Scott, former president of the McCormick Theological Seminary in Chicago, died March 13 at the Fairhaven retirement home in Whitewater, Wis. He was 81 years old and lived in Whitewater.
        Mr. Scott died of pneumonia, a spokeswoman for the family said.
        He was president of McCormick seminary from 1970 to 1975, when he retired. He came to the seminary after serving as dean of the Presbyterian Institute of Industrial Relations, a post he had held for 25 years, from its founding in New York in 1945.
        In 1962 he was elected moderator of the 174th General Assembly of the United Presbyterian Church. He was also pastor of Presbyterian churches in Prattsburg and Pultney, N.Y., and the First Presbyterian Church of Columbus, Ohio.
        Long active in civil and human rights, he was a member of the Commission on Human Relations of the City of Chicago.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Zoe Smith; three sons, Thomas of Minneapolis, Andrew of Chiba, Japan, and James 3d of Highland Park, Ill.; a daughter, Carolyn Futrelle of Newton, Mass.; two sisters, Mary Elizabeth Chur of Bradenton, Fla., and Jane Scott Clarke of Winona Lake, Ind., and eight grandchildren.

        John Heller, a retired candy manufacturer and writer, died of pnuemonia Monday at Phelps Memorial Hospital in Tarrytown, N.Y. He was 91 years old and lived in Scarborough, N.Y.
        Mr. Heller came to New York from Vienna in 1940 and re-established the family business, Heller Candy Inc., which he later sold. He was the author of several books, most recently ''Memoirs of a Reluctant Capitalist,'' published in 1983.
        He is survived by his wife, Dr. Edith Kepes; two sons, Dr. Mark, of Cooperstown, N.Y., and Peter, of Buffalo; six grandchildren, and two great-grandchildren.

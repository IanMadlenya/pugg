
        H. P. Baldwin Terry, a retired insurance broker, died on Thursday in Weston, Mass., where he lived. He was 78 years old and had lived for years in Laurel Hollow, N.Y.
        He died of head injuries suffered while felling a tree at his home, his family said.
        He retired in 1975 as a partner in Boston of the insurance firm of Alexander & Alexander. He entered the insurance business in 1935 with Terry & Company in Manhattan.
        Mr. Terry served in the Pacific in World War II.
        His first wife, the former Barbara Mason, died in 1986. Surviving are his second wife, the former Suzanne Merrill; a daughter, Sara Graves of West Bath, Me.; two sons, H.P. Baldwin Jr. of Canton, Conn., and W. Grey, of Washington; a brother, Walter, of Peapack, N.J., and seven grandchildren. An older brother, Wyllys Jr., of Kennett Square, Pa., also a retired partner in Alexander & Alexander, died on Tuesday.

        Dr. Lester Blum, a surgeon with Mount Sinai Medical Center for 50 years, died of heart failure Wednesday at Mount Sinai. He was 83 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Dr. Blum was an associate clinical professor emeritus of the Department of Surgery at Mount Sinai. In 1981 he was awarded the Jacobi Medallion for distinguished achievement for his work in vascular surgery. He retired in 1983.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Jeanne Kaufman; a stepdaughter, Ellen Trokel of Manhattan; two stepsons, Rael Gleitsman of Smithfield, R. I., and Martin Gleitsman of Portland, Me., and four grandchildren.

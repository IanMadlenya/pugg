        Robert W. Sparks, a retired executive vice president of the Bowery Savings Bank, died of pneumonia Tuesday at his home in Manhattan. He was 95 years old.
        Mr. Sparks, who was born in Queens, was a graduate of Columbia University, the Harvard School of Business Administration and the Graduate School of Banking at Rutgers. He served with a field artillery unit of the New York National Guard in France in World War I. He joined the Bowery in 1928 after brief careers as a newspaperman, advertising man and insurance executive. He became executive vice president in 1956. While with the Bowery he served for several years as president of the Financial Advertisers Association.
        He is survived by two sisters, Alida Clay of Deerfield Beach, Fla., and Florence, of Croton-on-Hudson, N.Y.


        John K. Boeing, the former president of the Recordak Corporation, a microfilm subsidiary of the Eastman Kodak Company, died on Saturday at his home in Manalapan, Fla. He was 96 years old.
        Mr. Boeing was with Kodak for 40 years, having joined the company in 1920. He held personnel, industrial relations and office-management positions until he was transferred to Recordak in 1928.
        Mr. Boeing was a graduate of the University of Wisconsin. He was named president of Recordak in 1952 and at his retirement in 1961 he was chairman.
        He is survived by a daughter, Janet B. Francis of Westport, Conn.; six grandchildren and three great-grandchildren.

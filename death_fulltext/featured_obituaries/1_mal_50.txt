        Karolos Koun, a Greek theater director whose lively staging of ancient Greek plays won him acclaim in Europe, died last Saturday after suffering a heart attack, according to friends in Athens. He was 78 years old.
        He had been praised throughout Europe for his bawdy, colorful stagings of Aristophanes' fifth-century B.C. political comedies.
        In 1942, during the Nazi occupation of Greece, he founded the experimental Art Theater. And he gave premieres in Athens of works by avant-garde European playwrights such as Bertolt Brecht and Luigi Pirandello.

        Phil Friedman, a Broadway stage manager, died, apparently of a heart attack, at his home in Manhattan on Monday, according to the police. He was 66 years old.
        Mr. Friedman served as production stage manager for more than 85 shows during a 40-year career that included both Broadway and West Coast productions. Among them were ''Kismet,'' ''How to Succeed in Business Without Really Trying,'' ''Little Me'' and ''The Boy Friend.'' He worked on 11 shows associated with Bob Fosse, including ''Pippin,'' ''Dancin' '' and the revival of ''Sweet Charity,'' and played the stage manager in Mr. Fosse's 1979 film ''All That Jazz.'' Mr. Friedman also taught stage management at Rutgers University.
        He is survived by two sisters, Annette Trubowitsch of San Francisco, and Sylvia Juvik of Gig Harbor, Wash.

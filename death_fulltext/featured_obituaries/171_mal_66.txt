
        Lawrence P. King, an authority on bankruptcy law who taught at the New York University School of Law, died on April 1 at Mount Sinai Hospital in Manhattan. He was 72 and lived in Manhattan and Saltaire, N.Y.
        He had cancer, his family said.
        At the time of his death, Professor King had been the law school's Charles Seligson professor of law for 22 years, and had been teaching there since 1959.
        For 21 years, he was a member of the Advisory Committee on Bankruptcy Rules of the Judicial Conference of the United States. He was also senior adviser to the National Bankruptcy Review Commission, which was established by Congress and remained in existence until 1997.
        For more than 25 years, he was editor in chief of successive editions of the multivolume treatise ''Collier on Bankruptcy'' (Bender). He was also co-author, with Michael L. Cook, of the book ''Creditors' Rights, Debtors' Protection and Bankruptcy'' (Bender, 1985).
        A former associate dean of the law school, he was also affiliated with the Manhattan law firm Wachtell, Lipton, Rosen & Katz. The honors he received included the N.Y.U. Law Alumni Achievement Award and the Distinguished Service Award of the American College of Bankruptcy.
        A native of Schenectady, N.Y., he graduated from City College and the N.Y.U. law school.
        He is survived by his wife, Dorothy; two daughters, Yvonne Braun of Yorktown Heights, N.Y., and Debbie King-Shaham of Israel; a son, David J. Kaufman of Manhattan; a sister, Dr. Anne King of Garden City, N.Y.; and two grandchildren.

        John Farley, a patent lawyer in Manhattan for 40 years, died of a cerebral hemorrhage on Saturday at his home in Westfield, N.J. He was 74 years old.
        Mr. Farley joined Fish & Neave law firm in Manhattan in 1946 after graduating from the University of Memphis Law School. He also received an engineering degree from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology.
        At his retirement in 1985, he was a senior partner with the firm and had handled patent matters for the Radio Corporation of America, Bell Laboratories and International Business Machines Corporation.
        He is survived by his wife Eileen; two daughters, Katherine, of Manhattan, and Stacey, of Brooklyn; six sons, John, of Las Vegas, Nev., Robert, of Walnut Creek, Calif., Joel, of Manhattan, Dr. Thomas, of New Orleans, Stephen, of Manhattan, and Calvin, of Chicago; a sister, Josephine Wall of Memphis, and three grandchildren.

        Ellsworth S. Howell, a publisher of books on dogs as well as a writer and dog show judge, died Thursday in Darien, Conn. He was 71 years old.
        Mr. Howell was an approved judge of the American Kennel Club from 1938 to 1986. Among his many assignments was judge of Best in Show at the 1985 Westminster Kennel Club show in Madison Square Garden. He was also the breeder and exhibitor of seven different breeds and bred 28 champion dogs.
        For the past 26 years, Mr. Howell was the chairman of Howell Book House Inc., a publisher of books devoted primarily to dogs as well as a few titles on cats, tropical fish and exotic birds. He was also a director and vice president of Grolier, specializing in mail-order sales.
        Mr. Howell is survived by his wife, Elizabeth; two daughters, Maureen Howell Mawicke and Jean Howell Salembier, and four grandchildren.
        Burial was private. A memorial service will be held at St. Thomas More Roman Catholic Church in Darien, Conn., tomorrow at 11 A.M..

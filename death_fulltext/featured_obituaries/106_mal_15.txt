
        Robin Hardy, a writer who founded the Publishing Triangle, an association of  lesbians and gay men in publishing, died on Saturday in Arizona. He was 43 and lived in Tucson.
        He died in a mountaineering accident in Tonto National Forest, according to Houghton Mifflin, for which he was working on his latest book, "The Landscape of Death: Gay Men, AIDS and the Crisis of Desire."
        He was also the author of about 20 paperback novels.
        He is survived by his parents, Bill and Jean Hardy of Ottawa; two sisters, Eloise Hardy of Toronto and Michele Hardy of Ottawa, and a brother, Charles, also of Ottawa.

        Roderick Firth, a professor of philosophy at Harvard University, died of pneumonia Tuesday at Symmes Hospital in Arlington, Mass. He was 70 years.
        Professor Firth was chairman of the philosophy department at Harvard from 1957 until 1963. He earned a doctorate at Harvard in 1943, joined the faculty in 1953 and became Alford Professor of Natural Religion, Moral Philosophy and Civil Polity in 1962.
        Professor Firth, who lived in Lexington, Mass., is survived by his wife, Maria; a son, Roderick Firth Jr., of Palm Beach Gardens, Fla., and a granddaughter.

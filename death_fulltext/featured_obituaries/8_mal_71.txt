        Graham Loving, a commodities investor and former investment banker, died of bone marrow cancer Sunday at his daughter's home in New York City. He was 62 years old and lived in Miami and Palm Beach, Fla.
        Mr. Loving went to New York in 1965 and founded a stock-research service, Equity Research Associates, and later opened an investment-banking service, Graham Loving & Company.
        He was born in Washington. After graduating from Georgetown University and the Georgetown Law School, Mr. Loving was counsel to the Internal Revenue Service until 1953, when he joined the law firm of Mostellers & Fellers in Oklahoma City.
        Surviving are two daughters, Jennifer and Candace, of New York and Aspen, Colo., and a son, Graham 3d, of Washington.

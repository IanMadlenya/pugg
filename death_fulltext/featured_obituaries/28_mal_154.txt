        Ralph A. Corbin Jr., a New Jersey banker for the last quarter-century, died of kidney failure at Mountainside Hospital in Glen Ridge, N.J., yesterday. He was 54 years old and lived in West Caldwell.
        Mr. Corbin was chairman of Midlantic National Bank/North in West Paterson, formerly the New Jersey Bank. He joined the bank in 1962.
        He was a native of Passaic and a graduate of Wheaton College. He served in the United States Army in the Korean War and began his banking career in Chicago before moving to New Jersey.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Janet Stover; two daughters, Jean Austin of Jacksonville, Fla., and Nancy Spaulding of Morristown, N.J.; two brothers, Raymond, of West Caldwell, and Richard, of Wilton, Me., and a grandson.

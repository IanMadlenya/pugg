        Michael S. Weisberg, executive director of Multitasking Systems of New York and a former assistant business editor of The Daily News, died of AIDS on Tuesday at Long Island Jewish Hospital. He was 36 years old and lived in Forest Hills, Queens.
        Mr. Weisberg was a founder of Multitasking Systems, a nonprofit organization established a year ago to do office work for people with acquired immune deficiency syndrome.
        He was a native of Brooklyn and a graduate of the State University of New York at Buffalo and of the University of Missouri School of Journalism.
        He is survived by his parents, Abby and Sidney, and a brother, Ira, all of West Palm Beach, Fla.
        Mr. Weisberg's companion was Frank Potash.

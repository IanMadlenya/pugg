
        Charles Cary Colt Jr., an administrator for the New York State Division of Housing and Community Renewal, died on Wednesday on Naushon Island, off Cape Cod, Mass., where he was on vacation. He was 66 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Colt died of a heart attack, his wife said.
        At his death he was a quality-control administrator for the state agency in the New York City area, having been appointed to the post in 1986. Earlier he had been with the marketing division of Time Inc. and Time-Life Inc.
        Mr. Colt was a past vice president of Family Advocates for the Mentally Ill, the Manhattan affiliate of the National Alliance for the Mentally Ill. More recently he became a member of the organization's state board.
        He was born in Boston and graduated from Harvard College and the Columbia Law School. He served in the Army infantry in World War II and received a Bronze Star for gallantry in action in Germany.
        Besides his wife, the former Clair Walthew, Mr. Colt is survived by four sons, Cary, Christopher, Michael and Nicholas, all of Manhattan; three daughters, Victoria, of Park City, Utah, Erika McCarthy of Salt Lake City and Susan Wilcox of Maynard, Mass.; his parents, Charles and Amy Lee Colt of Cambridge, Mass., three brothers, three sisters and five grandchildren.

        James F. Egan, a writer and a retired advertising executive, died Tuesday at St. Raphael's Hospital in New Haven as a result of complications after surgery. He was 78 years old and lived in Westport, Conn.
        Mr. Egan, who was born in Philadelphia, graduated from the University of Pennsylvania's Wharton School of Commerce and Finance. When he retired in 1962, he was senior vice president at Doherty, Clifford, Steers & Schenfield, an advertising agency in Manhattan.
        As a freelance writer, he contributed articles to The New York Times, McCall's and Town & Country.
        He is survived by his wife, Margaret; a son, Tony, of Waitsfield, Vt.; a stepdaughter, Deborah Guzzetti of Boston; a stepson, Thomas Fortson of Highland, N.C.; two grandchildren, and three step-grandchildren.

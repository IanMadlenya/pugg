        The Rev. F. Sanford Cutler, an Episcopal priest who was active in the peace and women's rights movements died on Monday at St. Luke's-Roosevelt Hospital Center in Manhattan. He was 68 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        He died of a heart attack, his son, Thomas, said.
        Mr. Cutler was the rector at the Church of the Redeemer in Morristown, N.J., for 15 years before retiring in 1985. Before that, he was Dean of the Episcopal Cathedral of San Juan Bautista in Puerto Rico. He was a 1961 graduate of the Church Divinity School of the Pacific, and was the former chairman of the New Jersey Religious Coalition for Abortion Rights.
        Besides his son, of Manhattan, he is survived by two daughters, Madeleine Thompson of Gaithersburg, Md., and Dr. Christine Halaburka of Monte Serrano, Calif., and six grandchildren.

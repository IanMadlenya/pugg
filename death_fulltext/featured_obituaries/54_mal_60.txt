
        Raymond Manning Brand, chairman and senior partner of Brand & Brand, a Garden City, L.I., law firm, died June 29 at the Westchester County Medical Center in Valhalla, N.Y. He was 69 years old and lived in Jericho, L.I.
        Mr. Brand died of cancer, a spokesman for the family said.
        Active in Nassau County politics in the 1950's and 60's, Mr. Brand, a Democrat, organized campaigns for judges and ran unsuccessfully for district attorney in 1963 and for the State Assembly in 1950 and 1954.
        He was born in Far Rockaway, Queens, and graduated from St. John's University and the St. John's Law School.
        Mr. Brand fought with the Army's Eighth Air Force in Europe in World War II and received the Air Medal with four Oak Leaf clusters.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Edythe Bernstein; two sons, David, a lawyer of Muttontown, L.I., and Clifford, of Manhattan; two daughters, Patrice Sheinbaum of Stamford, Conn., and Allison Bobley of Huntington, L.I., and four grandchildren.

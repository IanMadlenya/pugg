
        Richard D. Karfunkle, an economist, died on Monday at his home in Kennett Square, Pa. He was 59 years old.
        Mr. Karfunkle, who had leukemia, died of pneumonia, his son Jeffrey said.
        Mr. Karfunkle was president of Econoviews International Inc., an economics consulting firm he founded in Kennett Square in 1978. He was also a lecturer in economics at the University of Delaware and an economic adviser to former Gov. Pete DuPont of Delaware from 1977 to 1985.
        Mr. Karfunkle was active in organizations within the Union of American Hebrew Congregations, the umbrella organizations of the Reform movement in Judaism. He was the immediate past president of the National Federation of Temple Brotherhoods, which represents more than 300 men's clubs within the Reform movement throughout the United States and Canada.
        Mr. Karfunkle was also a past president and chancellor of the Jewish Chautauqua Society, an interfaith educational organization connected with the Reform movement.
        During the 1970's Mr. Karfunkle was a senior vice president and chief economist for three Wall Street investment firms: Laird, Bissell & Meeds; Abraham & Company, and Lehman Brothers Inc. In 1973 and 1975 he received an award for economic forecasting from the American Statistical Association. He was also a consultant to the Board of Governors of the Federal Reserve System, the United States Department of Labor and the Department of Commerce.
        In addition to his son, Mr. Karfunkle is survived by his wife, Shirley; a daughter, Sharon, of Wilmington, Del., and two grandchildren.

        David Heilweil, the former director of the drama school at the University of Connecticut and the co-founder of the Arena Theater, the first professional theater-in-the-round in New York City, died of pneumonia Wednesday at Windham Hospital in Willimantic, Conn. He was 71 years old and lived in Storrs, Conn.
        Mr. Heilweil, a graduate of Cornell University, retired this year as head of the drama school, where he had been for 18 years.
        Mr. Heilweil, with co-sponsor Derrick Lynn-Thomas, opened the Arena Theater in 1950 in the ballroom of the Hotel Edison on 47th Street, between Broadway and Eighth Avenue.

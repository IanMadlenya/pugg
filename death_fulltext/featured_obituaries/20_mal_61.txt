        Bradford Smith Jr., a retired chairman of the I.N.A. Corporation, one of the nation's largest insurance organizations, died of arteriosclerosis Tuesday at his home in Centerville, Md. He was 86 years old.
        From 1961 to 1969, Mr. Smith was also president and chairman of the Insurance Company of North America. I.N.A. was the holding company set up for the insurance company's diversification, and Mr. Smith presided over many acquisitions during his tenure.
        He retired in 1969. In 1982, I.N.A. merged with the Connecticut General Corporation to become Cigna.
        He spent his life in the insurance industry. After working for six years for other firms, he joined the Insurance Company of North America in 1929 as an agent. He worked his way up, becoming vice president in 1943, president in 1961 and chairman in 1964. Drexel University Trustee
        Mr. Smith, born in Cynwyd, Pa., attended Dartmouth College. He was a former member of the Board of Overseers of the Amos Tuck School of Business Administration at Dartmouth.
        He was a former board member of the Hospital of the University of Pennsylvania, Memorial Hospital in Easton, Md., and the governing committee of the Chamber of Commerce of Greater Philadelphia.
        Mr. Smith was the husband of the late Henrietta Dunne Smith. Surviving are a son, Bradford 3d, of St. Davids, Pa.; a daughter, Louisa Smith Hellman of Centerville, and four grandchildren.


        Thomas A. Crowley, a retired vice president of Johnson & Johnson, died on Wednesday at his home in Spring Lake, N.J. He was 70 years old.
        He died of cardiac arrest, his family said.
        Mr. Crowley was born in Jersey City and was a graduate of St. Peter's College. He served as a Navy officer in the Pacific in World War II. At Johnson & Johnson he was in charge of the pharmaceutical concern's hospital division before he retired in 1976.
        He was a former chairman of the Spring Lake Planning Board and a past chairman of the Jersey Shore Medical Center Foundation.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Catherine Reynolds; a son, Thomas Jr., of Montclair, N.J.; a daughter, Catherine R. Crowley of Spring Lake; a brother, Frank, of Jersey City; two sisters, Regina Graham of Jersey City and Marya Fitzgerald of Spring Lake, and two grandchildren.

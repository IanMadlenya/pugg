
        Benedict Jacob Bernstein, a psychoanalyst who practiced in New Jersey and New York, died last Friday at New York Hospital. He was 80 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        He died of complications after surgery, his wife said.
        Dr. Bernstein was in private practice in Maplewood, N.J., from 1953 to 1978 and in Manhattan from 1978 to the present. He taught at Mount Sinai Medical Center from 1947 to his death. He was also a faculty member at the Downstate Medical Center in Brooklyn.
        In 1970, he founded the Regional Council of Metropolitan Psychoanalytic Societies, with headquarters in Manhattan.
        He was born in Plainfield, N.J., graduated from City College and received his medical degree from the University of Munich in 1937. He served in the Army in World War II with the final rank of major.
        Besides his wife, Dr. Beth Anspach Bernstein, a psychoanalyst, he is survived by three children: Jonathan, of Manhattan, Carola Chase of the Riverdale section of the Bronx, and Michael, of Chicago, Ill.; a sister, Judge Libby Sachar of Plainfield, N.J.; two brothers, Gerald Bertin of Edison, N.J., a profesor of romance languages at Rutgers University, and Dr. Morton Bertin of Orlando, Fla., and four grandchildren.

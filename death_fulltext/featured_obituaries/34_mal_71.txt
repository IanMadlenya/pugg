        James S. Spain, executive director of the Association for the Integration of Management, died Nov. 2 at St. Luke's-Roosevelt Hospital Center in Manhattan. He was 56 years old and lived in Montclair, N.J.
        Mr. Spain died after a brief illness, his sister, Jacqueline Bennett, said.
        Mr. Spain graduated from Rutgers University and Fordham Law School. He joined the association, which is dedicated to advancing blacks in business management, 12 years ago.
        Surviving are his mother, Dorothy, of Montclair; two brothers, Wallace and Jesse, both of New York City; four sisters, Mrs. Bennett of Madison, N.J., Barbara Turner of Montclair, Jerrie Randall of Mt. Pocono, Pa., and Dorothy Jenkins of Silver Spring, Md.

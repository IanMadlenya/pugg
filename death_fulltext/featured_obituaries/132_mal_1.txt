
        Barrett G. Kreisberg, president of the Harkness Foundation for Dance and chairman of the New York State Board of Real Property Services, died on Monday at his home in Manhattan. He was 72.
        The cause was a heart attack, his family said.
        Mr. Kreisberg was named president of the Harkness Foundation in 1982. He was appointed head of the State Board of Acquisition and Assessment (now State Board of Real Property Services) in 1989 and held the post through 1996. Mr. Kreisberg earlier served as councilman of the Town of Greenburgh, N.Y., from 1972 to 1976 and chairman of the Greenburgh Housing Authority from 1970 to 1994.
        He was appointed by the Supreme Court to be arbitrator for Westchester County and served as special attorney for election frauds in the State of New York. He founded the state-wide law firm Kreisberg, Beebe, Grossman, Bergins and Mancuso in 1996.
        Mr. Kreisberg was a member of the National Law Committee and National Civil Rights Committee of the Anti-Defamation League of B'nai B'rith, chairman of the league's New York Public Policy Committee and former chairman of the league's Westchester Putnam Regional Board. He was also a trustee of Mercy College in Dobbs Ferry, N.Y, a founding board member of the Westchester Crime Victims Assistance Agency and a former treasurer and board member of Greenburgh Youth Service Organization.
        Born in New York City, Mr. Kreisberg received a bachelor's degree in civil engineering from George Washington University and a law degree from New York University.
        He is survived by his wife, Luisa, of Manhattan; a son, Joel, of Chatham, N.Y., and five grandchildren. A memorial service will be held tomorrow at Riverside Memorial Chapel, 180 West 76th Street at 10 A.M.

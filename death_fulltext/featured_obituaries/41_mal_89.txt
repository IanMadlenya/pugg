        Samuel Levine, a retired lawyer, died on Sunday at Yale-New Haven Medical Center. He was 79 years old and had lived in Guilford, Conn., for 14 years. He died of lung cancer, said his son Robert, of Guilford.
        Mr. Levine was a contracts lawyer with Levine & Wardlaw, a medical-research business in Guilford, for the last 14 years. He had worked for the Consolidated Edison Company for 50 years, beginning as an office boy in 1926 and retiring in 1976.
        He graduated from the St. John's University Law School in 1938.
        Also surviving are his wife, Sara; another son, Harvey, of Flushing, Queens; a brother, Abraham Levin of Manhattan; a sister, Ida Broder of Staten Island; nine grandchildren, and one great-grandchild.

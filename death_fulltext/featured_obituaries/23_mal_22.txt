        Joseph C. Nugent Sr., a retired New York stockbroker and emeritus member of the board of trustees of Marymount Manhattan College, died Saturday at New York Hospital after a brief illness. He was 85 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Nugent, a native of New York, had been a member of the college board of trustees for 27 years and was chairman of the board from 1967 to 1980. He also headed the college's President's Advisory Council at his death. He had been a senior and managing partner of Mabon, Nugent & Company, investment brokers, and a member of the New York State Banking Board, appointed by Gov. Nelson A. Rockefeller in 1967. He retired in 1986.
        A graduate of Columbia University in 1925, Mr. Nugent was associated with the brokerage concern of Whitney & Company from 1925 to 1938. In 1938 he joined the brokerage house of Mabon & Company and became a partner in 1941. In 1965 the concern's name was changed to Mabon, Nugent & Company.
        Mr. Nugent's first wife, the former Kathleen Dolan, died in 1978.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Helen Mara; a son, Joseph C. Jr., of Fairhaven, N.J.; two daughters, Barbara Bovers and Constance McQuade, both of Manhattan; two brothers, Paul, of Penn Yan, N.Y., and Thomas, of Northport, L.I.; 14 grandchildren and seven great-grandchildren.
        A funeral mass will be celebrated by John Cardinal O'Connor at 10 A.M. today in St. Patrick's Cathedral.

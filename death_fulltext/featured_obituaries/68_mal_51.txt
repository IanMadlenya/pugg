
        Reginald L. Auchincloss Jr., a former investment counselor and a trustee of the Fifth Avenue Presbyterian Church, died on Tuesday at his home in Manhattan. He was 72 years old.
        Mr. Auchincloss, who had been ill for years, died of Parkinson's disease, said his wife, Frances.
        A native of Manhattan, he graduated from the Groton School in 1936 and Yale University in 1940. He enlisted in the Navy after graduation from Yale and served in the Asian and Pacific theaters during World War II.
        After the war he was a partner in the Wall Street investment firms of Courts & Company and Inverness Investment Counseling. From 1970 to 1980 he served several terms as a trustee of the Fifth Avenue Presbyterian Church.  In addition to his wife, he is survived by two daughters, Eve A. Lilley of Washington and Ruth A. O'Neill of Manhattan; a son, Reginald L. 3d, of Manhattan; three sisters, Ruth Collard of the Isle of Man, Britain, and Lisa Ever and Hope Whipple, both of Manhattan; a brother, Bayard, of Santa Barbara, Calif., and four grandchildren. A daughter from Mr. Auchincloss's first marriage, Sandra A. Wadsworth, died in 1991.


        Joseph G. Blum, a New York lawyer for more than six decades and longtime benefactor of people with disabilities, died on Sunday at his home in Larchmont, N.Y. He was 86 years old.
        His family said he died of congestive heart failure.
        A 1927 graduate of the Columbia Law School, Mr. Blum was senior partner in the law firm of Blum, Gersen & Stream, where he specialized in international law and estate practice.
        He was a past president and board chairman of the New York Society for the Deaf and played an important role in the construction of Tanya Towers, on East 13th Street, and Blum House, on East 14th Street, housing projects for the deaf and the blind. He was also a former treasurer of the Parkinson's Disease Foundation and president of the John W. and Laura S. Stewart Foundation, which supports charitable causes.
        Mr. Blum is survived by his wife, Ray; a son, Robert P., of Montclair, N.J.; a daughter, Judy Blum of Manhattan, and three grandchildren.


        Francis R. Schanck, a former partner with the investment banking firm of Bacon, Whipple & Company of Chicago, died Nov. 1. He was 84 years old and lived in Hinsdale, a suburb of Chicago.
        The cause of death was prostate cancer, said his son, J. Thomas Schanck.
        A graduate of Stanford University, Mr. Schanck worked for First Boston Corporation from 1930 to 1948, except for a five-year period during World War II, when he was a Navy commander. He joined Bacon, Whipple & Company in 1948 and became a managing partner in 1967. He retired in 1987.
        He was president of the Investment Bankers Association in 1967 and 1968.
        An expert on municipal financing, Mr. Schanck testified often before Congressional committees, including those considering a Federal bailout of New York City during its fiscal crisis in the mid-1970's.
        Mr. Schanck, whose first wife, Kathryn Short Schanck, died in 1989, is survived by his second wife, Katharine Maley Schanck; his son, Thomas, of Oak Brook, Ill.; his son, Peter, of Lawrence, Kan.; his daughter, Susan Fawcett, of Casper, Wyo.; six grandchildren and four great-grandchildren.


        Kenton Kilmer, a writer and editor and the son of the poet Joyce Kilmer, died on Feb. 10 at his home in Vienna, Va. He was 85.
        The cause was cancer, his family said.
        Mr. Kilmer, who was 9 when his father was killed in France in World War I, was born in Morristown, N.J. He was a member of the legislative reference division of the Library of Congress from 1939 to 1967 and was poetry editor of The Washington Post from 1940 to 1947. He was the editor of "This Is My America," a volume of poems about World War II, and the author of a 1993 book, "Memories of My Father, Joyce Kilmer." He also was a member of the Poetry Society of America.
        He is survived by his wife, Frances; a sister, Sister Michael Kilmer of the St. Scholastica Convent in St. Cloud, Minn.; 10 children, 36 grandchildren and 4 great-grandchildren.

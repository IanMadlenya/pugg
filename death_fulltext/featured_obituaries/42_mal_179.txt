        James Randall Creel, a retired judge of the New York City Criminal Court, died on Sunday at North Shore University Hospital in Manhasset, L.I. He was 85 years old and lived in Mill Neck, L.I.
        The family said he died of congestive heart failure.
        Judge Creel served on the Court of Special Sessions in Manhattan and later the Criminal Court before he retired in 1968. He had previously been an assistant United States attorney and a New York City magistrate.
        He was born in El Dorado Springs, Mo., and was a graduate of Harvard College and the Harvard Law School.
        Judge Creel's wife, the former Alexandra Gardiner, is co-owner with her brother, Robert, of Gardiners Island in Long Island Sound.
        In addition to his wife, Judge Creel is survived by a daughter, Alexandra Goelet of Manhattan, and four grandchildren.

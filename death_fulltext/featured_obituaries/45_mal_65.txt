        Frank P. Camastro, publisher of Motor Magazine, died on Tuesday. He was 50 years old and lived in Bayside, Queens. He apparently had a heart attack and was pronounced dead at Bellevue Hospital, a spokesman for the family said.
        He was the publisher of Motor Magazine, a Hearst publication, since 1983. He had been a national advertising manager for Popular Mechanics, also published by Hearst, and the East Coast advertising manager for the magazine.
        In 1966, he joined Hearst's the advertising staff for Harper's Bazaar. Mr. Camastro, who graduated from Pace University, had also worked as editor of the house publication of RCA Institute and been on the advertising staff of Popular Science magazine.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Claire Cuff; a daughter, Ericka, and a son, Charles.

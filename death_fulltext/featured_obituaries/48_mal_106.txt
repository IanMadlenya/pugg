
        Seymour Lakritz, a New York State Supreme Court Justice in Queens, died on Dec. 27 at Long Island Jewish Hospital. He was 69 years old and lived in Laurelton, Queens.
        He died of cancer, court officials said.
        Justice Lakritz served from 1970 to 1979 on the Civil Court bench before he was elected to the Supreme Court in 1980. He was a graduate of City College and Brooklyn Law School and served in the Army in World War II.
        His survivors include two sons, Mark, of Oceanside, L.I., and Jonathan, of Baldwin, L.I., and a brother, Hyman, of Miami.

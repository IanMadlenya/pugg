        James F. White, a retired vice president and treasurer of the National Broadcasting Company, died on Monday at his home in Wilton, Conn., at the age of 69. The cause of death was congestive heart failure, his son Bryan said.
        He was born in Brooklyn and graduated from St. John's University, where he was captain of the 1941-42 basketball team. In World War II he served as a Navy lieutenant, commanding a landing craft. At NBC, from which he retired in 1987, he was also vice president of finance and administration for NBC News for several years.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Elizabeth Kelly; three sons, Brian, of Newtown, Conn., James P., of Ho-Ho-Kus, N.J., and William, of Pacific Palisades, Calif.; a daughter, Betty Wilson of Wilton; a brother, John, of Douglaston, Queens, and four grandchildren.

        John J. O'Neill, a former New York City police inspector who supervised the computerization of the Police Department's fingerprint records in the 1960's, died of heart failure April 15 at Bellevue Hospital Center in Manhattan. He was 71 years old and lived in Bayside, Queens.
        Mr. O'Neill had been commanding officer of the department's Bureau of Criminal Identification. He was named the city employee of the year in 1964.
        Mr. O'Neill, who joined the department as a patrolman in 1941, graduated from Fordham University in 1936. He received a master's degree from New York University in 1956.
        After he retired from the Police Department in 1971, he was named executive director of security at the Mount Sinai Medical Center in Manhattan.
        He is survived by his wife, Lorraine; a son, the Rev. John F. X. Norton of Washington; a daughter, Jean M. Schroeder of Queens; a sister, Peg Haggerty of Westbury, L.I., and three granddaughters.


        Daniel Frankel, an importer and distributor of films who helped introduce the work of a number of prominent directors to American audiences, died on Saturday at the Applewood Health Care Center in Winchester, N.H. He was 91 and lived in Keene, N.H.
        As the president of Zenith International Films, Mr. Frankel was instrumental in introducing many important films to American moviegoers, among them Francois Truffaut's "400 Blows," Louis Malle's "Lovers" and Alain Resnais's "Hiroshima, Mon Amour." Earlier, he was a vice president and director of Four Continent Films and served as general manager for United Artists in Belgium and Luxembourg.
        Mr. Frankel was born in Brooklyn in 1903. He attended the University of Berlin in Germany, and the University of Michigan. While living in Paris he managed the Theatre des Champs-Elysees. In 1930 he joined the French movie production company Pathe.
        He is survived by his wife of 58 years, Janet; a daughter, Ann Robinson of Swanzey Center, N.H.; a sister, Margaret Schoonover of Santa Fe, N.M., and three granddaughters.

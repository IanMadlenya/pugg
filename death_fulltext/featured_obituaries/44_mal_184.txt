        David H. McConnell 3d, an investment banker, died on Sunday at the Manhattan home of his mother, the Baroness Hubert von Pantz. He was 64 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        He died of throat cancer, his family said.
        Mr. McConnell, who was born in Suffern, N.Y., served in the United States Marines in World War II and the Korean War. He was a partner in the New York investment banking firm of McConnell Wetenhall & Company.
        In addition to his mother, who also has a home in Paris, he is survived by his wife, the former Joy Monroe; five daughters, Geraldine Platt of Mystic, Conn., Constance Rush of Westbrook, Me., Stephanie, of Somerville, Mass., and Garret and Julie, both of Manhattan; a brother, Neil, of Manhattan, and two grandchildren.


        Eugene J. Wyser-Pratte, a retired investment banker, died on Sunday at his home in La Jolla, Calif. He was 89 years old.
        He died of cancer, his son Guy, of Manhattan, said.
        Mr. Wyser-Pratte was a senior vice president at Prudential-Bache Securities from 1967 until he retired in 1971 as manager of its risk arbitrage department. Before that, he had headed his own arbitrage firm, Wyser-Pratte & Company, for 20 years. He was born in Budapest, studied in Europe and was a banker in Paris before coming to the United States in 1947. He established his firm on Wall Street in 1947 and merged it into Bache & Company in 1967.
        In addition to his son Guy, he is survived by his wife, Marguerite; two other sons, John and Christian, both of Manhattan, and eight grandchildren.

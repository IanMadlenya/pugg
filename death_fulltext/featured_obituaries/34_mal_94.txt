        The Rev. Donald I. MacLean, president of St. Joseph's University in Philadelphia from 1976 to 1986, died of cancer Monday in Weston, Mass. He was 59 years old.
        During his presidency, St. Joseph's received university status from the state, developed a college of business and administration, established a college to administer night classes and expanded graduate offerings.
        Father MacLean received bachelor's and master's degrees at Boston College and a doctorate in physical chemistry at Catholic University. He was ordained in 1961 in Innsbruck, Austria, He came to St. Joseph's from Creighton University in Omaha. After leaving Philadelphia, he went to Loyola University of Chicago to serve as vice president for campus ministry. In August he moved to Mobile, Ala., to serve as president of Spring Hill College but became ill three weeks later.
        He is survived by two sisters.

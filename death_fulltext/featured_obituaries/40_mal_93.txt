        Gary Keeper, a senior programming executive at the Showtime cable television network, died of AIDS on Sunday at the North Hollywood Medical Center. He was 39 years old and lived in Los Angeles.
        As senior vice president of original programming, Mr. Keeper was responsible for Showtime series that included ''It's Garry Shandling's Show,'' as well as ''The Neon Empire,'' a movie about the creation of Las Vegas that was commissioned by the cable network and shown this season.
        Mr. Keeper is survived by his parents, Cele and Sam Keeper; a sister, Laurie Keeper, and his grandmother, Rose Keeper, all of Houston.

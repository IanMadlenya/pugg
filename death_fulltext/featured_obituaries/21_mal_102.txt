        Dr. Angelo Carmine Ferrara, a pediatrician and neonatologist, died of cancer Saturday at his home in Manhattan. He was 57 years old.
        Dr. Ferrara was a professor of pediatrics at the New York University School of Medicine, the director of the Infant Transport System of New York City, which takes sick newborns to special facilities, and the director of neonatology at New York University-Bellevue Hospital.
        He was the author of many articles on pediatrics and on respiratory disorders in infants. He was president of the Faculty Council at N.Y.U. and was affiliated with a number of professional medical societies.
        He received his medical degree from the University of Rome, Italy, and held a Ph.D. in public administration from N.Y.U.
        He is survived by his wife, Joyce Polvere of Manhattan; three daughters, Lisa Ciardi of Montclair, N.J.; Angela and Christine, both of Manhattan, and two sons, Joseph and Carl, both of Manhattan.

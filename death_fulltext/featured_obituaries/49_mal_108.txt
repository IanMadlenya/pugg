
        Alexander Kosloff, a lawyer in Manhattan who specialized in regulations and insurance for the taxicab industry, died on Thursday at his home in Hollywood Beach, Fla. He was 88 years old and formerly lived in Dobbs Ferry, N.Y., and Boca Raton, Fla.
        He died of congestive heart failure, his son, George, said.
        Mr. Kosloff was a graduate of Columbia University Law School. He helped form the League of Mutual Taxicab Owners and was a director of the Manhattan Casualty Company. He retired in 1975.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Rose Newmark; his son, George, of Delray Beach, Fla., and Bushkill, Pa.; two sisters, Claire Levy of Bradley Beach, N.J., and Jean Edelman of North Miami Beach, Fla.; and two brothers, Samuel, of North Miami Beach and Mike, of Teaneck, N.J., and Pompano Beach, Fla.

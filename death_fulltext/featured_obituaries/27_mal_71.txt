        William Henry White, an author, editor and publisher, died of cancer yesterday at the White Plains (N.Y.) Hospital Medical Center. He was 64 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. White, a 1949 graduate of the Graduate School of Journalism at Columbia University, was the owner and publisher of the Scarsdale Inquirer and the Hastings Enterprise, newspapers he bought in 1980 and 1984 respectively. He had been an editor or in management at numerous publications, including Family Health, Medical World News and Look.
        He is survived by two daughters, Victoria Vinton of Manhattan and Deborah, of Warwick, N.Y.

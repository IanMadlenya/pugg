        Sherman T. van Esselstyn, an oil executive who retired in 1986 as chairman of Moore & Munger, a marketer of waxes and other petroleum and synthetic products, died of liver cancer Friday at the Lahey Clinic in Burlington, Mass. He was 63 years old and lived in Sanibel, Fla., and previously was a longtime resident of New Canaan and Weston, Conn.
        Mr. van Esselstyn was born in Montclair, N.J., and attended the Brooklyn Polytechnic Institute. He joined Moore & Munger in 1945 as a laboratory assistant and remained with the company for 41 years.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Mary Jane Padgett; a son, Peter, of Orleans, Mass.; a daughter, Mary Ellen, of Upper Montclair, N.J.; two brothers, Douglas, of Fairfield, Conn., and Richard, of Rolling Hills, Calif.; a sister, Mary Louise Peterkin of Upper Montclair, and three grandchildren.

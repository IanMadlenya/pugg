
        Walter J. Mahoney, a retired Wall Street executive, died on Thursday at his home in Jupiter, Fla. He was 76 years old.
        He died of heart failure, his family said.
        Mr. Mahoney was a member of the New York Stock Exchange and worked at Adler, Coleman & Company, trading specialists on the exchange. He retired from the firm as a senior partner 11 years ago.
        He was a trustee of St. Agnes Hospital in White Plains, a member of the Cardinal's Committee of the Laity of the Archdiocese of New York and a member of the Friendly Sons of St. Patrick.
        Mr. Mahoney was born in Manhattan and graduated from Manhattan College in 1937. During World War II he served four years in the Army Intelligence Corps.
        He is survived by his wife of 49 years, the former Shirley Ann Velsor; three sons, Walter, of Arlington, Va., Jay, of Armonk, N.Y., and Daniel, of Rye, N.Y.; a daughter, Kerry Brown of Rye, and 13 grandchildren.

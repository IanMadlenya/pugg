        Randolph H. Guthrie, a lawyer, died of a heart attack Monday at his home in Hilton Head, S.C. He was 83 years old and was a longtime resident of New York City.
        Mr. Guthrie was a senior partner at Mudge, Rose, Guthrie, Alexander & Ferdon, a law firm in New York. He was chairman of the boards of the Studebaker-Packard Corporation from 1963 to 1971 and UMC Industries Inc., a money-changing equipment manufacturer, from 1969 to 1977. He was chairman of the executive committee of both companies until 1980.
        Mr. Guthrie was born in Richmond and graduated from the Citadel and the Harvard Law School, magna cum laude. He joined Mudge, Rose in 1931. He was a former member of the board of The Boys Club of New York.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Mabel Welton; two sons, Randolph and George, and a daughter, Jo, all of New York City; two sisters, Tomasia Phillips, of Hilton Head, and Cary Bonner, of Camden, S.C., and seven grandchildren.
        Correction:  September 15, 1989, Friday, Late Edition - Final
        An obituary yesterday about Randolph H. Guthrie, a Manhattan lawyer, misidentified a sister who survived him. She is Thomasia Phillips of Hilton Head, S.C.


        Seymour Osborne, a theatrical voice coach who worked with Broadway stars for four decades, died on Tuesday at New York Hospital-Cornell Medical Center in Manhattan. He was 93 and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Osborne began giving voice lessons in the 1950's and was associated with the New York College of Music for several years. His students included George Rose, William Daniels, Fred Gwynne and John Cullum.
        He was born in Odessa, Russia, in 1903 and immigrated to the United States in 1923. He had begun his training as a bass-baritone in Russia and continued his studies in New York City at the Metropolitan Opera School. He was a soloist in the Radio City Music Hall Glee Club and also sang with the New York City Opera.
        He is survived by his wife, Ann, and a son, Steven L., of Manhattan.

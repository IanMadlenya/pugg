        Harry F. Bliss Jr., a former president of the international division of the American Cyanamid Company, died last Saturday at his home in Naples, Fla. He was 75 years old. His family said he died from bone cancer.
        Mr. Bliss joined Cyanamid in 1951 and became president of Cyanamid International in 1965. In 1974 he became president of the Europe-Mideast-Africa division and in 1975 was named assistant to the chairman. He retired in 1976.
        Mr. Bliss was born in Newark. He was a Phi Beta Kappa alumnus of Princeton and had a degree with high honors from the Harvard Law School. From 1939 to 1951 he ws associated with the law firm of Milbank, Tweed, Hope & Hadley in Manhattan. In World War II Mr. Bliss commanded a unit of PT boats in the Pacific and was awarded the Bronze Star.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Jeannette Hunter, and a son, Harry 3d, of Carlsbad, N.M.

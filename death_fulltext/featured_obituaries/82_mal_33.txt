
        Charles Feit, a former executive of Weight Watchers International, died on Wednesday at the Princeton Medical Center. He was 68.
        The cause of death was a heart attack, according to his wife, Hedwig Feit.
        The Weight Watchers organization was started in 1963 and Mr. Feit (pronounced fight) joined it four years later an an auditor. He rose to become the company's executive vice-president and a member of its board of directors, serving for 13 years until Weight Watchers was acquired by the H. J. Heinz Company of Pittsburgh.
        A graduate of City College and the New York Law School, Mr. Feit was an agent of the United States Treasury Department before entering the private practice of law and accountancy in New York in 1955. He served three years as an Army paratrooper in the Pacific during World War II.
        In addition to his wife, Mr. Feit is survived by two sons, Paul-Andre, also of Princeton, and Anthony E. of Hartford; a daughter, Jane A. Berman of South Bend, Ind., and three grandchildren.

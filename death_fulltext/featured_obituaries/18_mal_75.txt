        James Gordon Shanklin, a lawyer and former agent of the Federal Bureau of Investigation who directed an exhaustive inquiry into the assassination of President Kennedy, died of cancer Monday at Presbyterian Hospital in Dallas. He was 78 years old and lived in Dallas.
        Mr. Shanklin, was named special agent in charge of the F.B.I.'s Dallas office in April 1963, seven months before the shooting of the President on Nov. 22. Before this assignment he headed the F.B.I.'s offices in Pittsburgh, El Paso and Honolulu.
        Mr. Shanklin was born in Elkton, Ky. and held a law degree from Vanderbilt University in Nashville, Tenn. He was with the F.B.I. for 32 years, retiring in 1975 to practice law in Dallas.
        He is survived by his wife, Emily Shacklett Shanklin, of Dallas; a daughter, Elizabeth S. Cavanaugh, of the Riverdale section of the Bronx; and a granddaughter.

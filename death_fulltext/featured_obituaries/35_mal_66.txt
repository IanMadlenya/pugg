        Israel Unterman, a professor of management at San Diego State University, died Dec. 1 from congestive heart failure at his home in La Jolla, Calif. He was 68 years old.
        Dr. Unterman was born in Gdansk, and came to the United States when he was 2 years old. He worked in business and finance in New York City after his return from the Army after World War II.
        He retired from business to attend Harvard University, where he earned a Ph.D. in 1968. He then taught at Harvard and several other universities before joining the faculty at San Diego in 1976.
        He is survived by his wife, Ruth; two sons, Robert and Lee, both of Manhattan; his father, Max, of Brooklyn; a brother, David of Palo Alto, Calif.; a sister, Rebecca Hoberman of Tamarac, Fla., and one grandson.

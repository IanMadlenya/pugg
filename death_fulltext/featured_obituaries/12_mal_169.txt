        William F. Larkin, a lawyer and former member of the New York State Assembly, died of lung cancer Sunday at Cabrini Hospice of Cabrini Medical Center. He was 67 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Larkin, a Republican, was active in city and state politics and was a member of the New York City Alcoholic Beverage Control Board at his death. He represented the 62d Assembly District on Manhattan's East Side from 1966 to 1968, when he was defeated by Andrew J. Stein.
        He was a member of the District 2 Community School Board from June 1970 to June 1971 and served on the Board of Elections from 1971 to 1973. He was elected its president in 1972, the first Republican to hold that post in 30 years.
        He was in private practice in recent years.
        Early in his career, Mr. Larkin had been a New York City police officer. From 1959 to 1961 he was an assistant attorney general for New York State.

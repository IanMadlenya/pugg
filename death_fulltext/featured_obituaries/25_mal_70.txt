
       Cliff Buck, president of the United States Olympic Committee from 1969 to 1973, died Wednesday night at the Heritage Park Care Center in Carbondale, Calif. He was 88 years old.  Mr. Buck was an administrator in amateur sports for more than 30 years. He worked for the Rocky Mountain Amateur Athletic Union and the national A.A.U.
        Cliff Buck, president of the United States Olympic Committee from 1969 to 1973, died Wednesday night at the Heritage Park Care Center in Carbondale, Calif. He was 88 years old.  Mr. Buck was an administrator in amateur sports for more than 30 years. He worked for the Rocky Mountain Amateur Athletic Union and the national A.A.U. as well as the United States Olympic Committee.
        He is survived by a daughter, Nancy Brown of Burnsville, Minn., and a son, John of Denver.

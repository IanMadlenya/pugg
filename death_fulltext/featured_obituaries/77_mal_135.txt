
        Hugh V. Cochrane, a retired retailing executive who was the chief financial officer of Saks Fifth Avenue and Gimbels, died on Wednesday at his home in Norwalk, Conn. He was 60.
        Mr. Cochrane began his business career at the Touche, Ross accounting firm, then entered retailing as the controller of Stewart's department store in Baltimore. Later he became the treasurer of the Joseph Horne Company in Pittsburgh, then in 1974 joined Saks.
        The Batus Retail Group promoted him to senior vice president and chief financial officer over Saks, Gimbels and Marshall Field. Later he was the chief financial officer of Seaman's furniture stores, which he retired from in December.
        Mr. Cochrane was born in Baltimore. He served in the United States Army and graduated from the University of Pennsylvania's Wharton School.
        Surviving are his wife of more than 30 years, the former Alice E. Haggerty; three sons, Allan of Darien, Conn., and Laurance and Hugh, both of Norwalk; and three brothers, L. Stewart of Glenmore, Pa., N. Blair of Baltimore and Richard of Santa Barbara, Calif.

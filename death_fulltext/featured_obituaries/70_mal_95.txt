
        Dr. Syed Abdus Salam Shahidi, a Pakistani-American leader and researcher in New York City's Department of Health, died on Oct. 28 at University Hospital in New Brunswick, N.J. He was 59 years old and lived in North Brunswick.
        He died of a heart attack, said his wife, Dr. Azra Shahidi.
        Dr. Shahidi was vice chairman of the Pakistan League of America and commissioner for its national elections, which have been postponed from Nov. 15 until Nov. 22 because of his death. He was also president of the National Association of Pakistani Americans, a cultural organization.
        Dr. Shahidi joined New York's Health Department in 1967 and was a research scientist in the Bureau of Laboratories.
        Dr. Shahidi was born in Nagpur, India, and immigrated to Pakistan in 1950. He obtained a master's degree in microbiology from Karachi University and, after coming to the United States in 1960, received a master's degree from the University of Wisconsin and a Ph.D. in microbiology from the University of Missouri in 1967.
        In addition to his wife, he is survived by a son, Nadeem, of North Brunswick, and two daughters, Samina, of North Brunswick and Dr. Rubina Shahidi of Danbury, Conn.

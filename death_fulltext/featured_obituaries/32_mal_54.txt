        Warren S. Lockwood, a foreign trade consultant, died of a stroke and heart attack Friday at the Chesterfield Nursing Home in Chester, Conn. He was 81 years old and lived in Essex, Conn.
        In 1954, Mr. Lockwood founded Warren S. Lockwood Associates, a firm specializing in the foreign trade of rubber. He retired in 1978. He also served as president of the Natural Rubber Bureau in Washington.
        Born in New York City, he graduated from Dartmouth College. Surviving are his wife, the former Lucille Peterson Monahan; two daughters, Molly, of New York City, and Helshl Hewson of Darien, Conn.; three stepdaughters, Kerry Tanner of East Haddam, Conn., Lynn Clark of New York City and Gayla Wright of Englewood, Colo.; a stepson, Charles Monahan of Essex, and 13 grandchildren.

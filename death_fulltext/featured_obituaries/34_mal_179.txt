        Harvey Hart, a Canadian film and television director, died of a heart attack on Nov. 21 in Toronto. He was 61 years old.
        Mr. Hart, who had been a director with the Canadian Broadcasting Corporation since the early 1950's, won a Golden Globe Award for ''East of Eden,'' a mini-series adapted from the John Steinbeck novel. He also directed episodes of ''Columbo'' and pilots for the series ''Judd for the Defense'' and ''The Young Lawyers.'' Films that Mr. Hart directed include ''Bus Riley's Back in Town'' (1965), ''Fortune and Men's Eyes'' (1977) and ''Shoot'' (1977).

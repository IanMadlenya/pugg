        Gordon K. Smith, a retired insurance executive, died of leukemia on Wednesday at his home in Manhasset, L.I. He was 88 years old.
        Mr. Smith joined the Equitable Life Assurance Society of the United States in 1923 after graduating cum laude from Colgate University. He held several executive positions before being appointed secretary in 1953, a position he held until his retirement in 1966.
        He was a founder of the New York Chapter of the Chartered Life Underwriters and served on committees of the College of Insurance, the American College of Life Underwriters and the American Society of Chartered Life Underwriters.
        He is survived by his wife, Gwendolyn; a daughter, Charlotte Harrison of Roslyn, L.I., and four grandchildren.

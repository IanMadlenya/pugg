        George Sands Hastings, a retired vice president of the American Machine and Foundry Company, died of congestive heart failure on Wednesday at his home in Hamden, Conn. He was 90 years old.
        Mr. Hastings joined American Machine in 1928 as a lawyer and rose to become head of the patent department, a director and a vice president in charge of patents. He retired in 1964. He also worked as a senior patent lawyer for Dorr-Oliver Inc., of Stamford, Conn., a machine manufacturing company.
        He was born in Chicago, graduated from Washington University and received a law degree from the Washington University Law School.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Jane Hope; a son from a former marriage, George S. Jr., of Albany; a brother, Atherton, of Florence, Ala.; eight grandchildren, and six great-grandchildren.

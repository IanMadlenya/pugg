        Patrick T. Finnegan, a retired executive vice president of the James Gordon Bennett Foundation and a former newspaper industry executive, died of a pulmonary embolism on Thursday at a nursing home in Martin, Tenn. He was 74 years old and until recently had lived in Carlisle, Pa.
        Mr. Finnegan was a budget director for Scripps-Howard Newspapers and had been with the organization in New York and Cincinnatti for 38 years before retiring in 1980. As an executive with the James Gordon Bennett Foundation and the Scripps-Howard Foundation, he was responsible for distributing scholarships to thousands of children of newspaper employees.
        He is survived by his son, John, of Martin; six granddaughters, and one great-grandchild.

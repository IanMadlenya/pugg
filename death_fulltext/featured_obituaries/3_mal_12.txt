        Ray Miller, a former featherweight boxer and later a referee with the New York State Boxing Commission, died Tuesday. He had been suffering from lung cancer for some time, his wife said. He was 82 years old.
        Mr. Miller fought in the 1920's and early 1930's in New York, Chicago and Detroit. He refereed many championship fights before he retired in the mid-1950's. Mr. Miller is survived by his wife, Mary, and a sister, Sydelle Goldstein, of Torrance, Calif.

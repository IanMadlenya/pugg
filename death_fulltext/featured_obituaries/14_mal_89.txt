        Joseph Sylvester, who rode seven winning horses on an eight-race card at Ravenna (Ohio) Race Track on Oct. 18, 1930, died of heart failure Saturday at New York University Medical Center in Manhattan. He was 77 years old.
        Mr. Sylvester, who rode approximately 1,500 winners from 1930 to 1939, had the string at Ravenna three weeks after accepting his first mount.
        He is survived by his wife, Concetta; two sons, Frank of Newburgh, N.Y., and Anthony of New York; and two daughters, Joann Brophy of New Windsor, N.Y., and Maria McKeel of Chapel Hill, N.C.

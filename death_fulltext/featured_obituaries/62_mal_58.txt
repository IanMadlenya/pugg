
        Irving Dimson, a real estate developer in New York City and the Northeast, died on March 5 at Memorial Sloan-Kettering Cancer Center in Manhattan. He was 81 years old and lived in Manhattan and Tamarac, Fla.
        He died of lung cancer, his family said.
        Mr. Dimson began as a developer in the 1940's and was a pioneer in getting federally guaranteed mortgages for high-rise luxury apartments. By the 1960's, his company, Carlyle Construction, was the city's fifth largest developer. Among its buildings were the Regency Towers and the Wellesley.
        In Boston, he developed Harbor Towers on the waterfront. In Philadelphia, he developed 4 Penn Center and the Rittenhouse Regency. He also developed homes in Georgia and Ohio. His most recent project was Journey's End Hotel in Manhattan.
        He was a benefactor of Sloan-Kettering, the Federation of Jewish Philanthropies, the Anti-Defamation League, the University of Miami Medical School and the Vineyard Theater's Gertrude and Irving Dimson Theater in Zeckendorf Towers at Union Square.
        He was born in Brooklyn and graduated from City College and Brooklyn Law School.
        Surviving are his wife of 57 years, Gertrude; a daughter, Barbara Zinn Krieger, and two sons, Barry and Norman, all of Manhattan; a brother, Samuel, of Boca Raton, Fla.; a sister, Rose, of Deerfield, Fla., and six grandchildren.

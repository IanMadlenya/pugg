
        Robert Smith, a prolific author of books about sports, died on Aug. 4 while on a fishing trip in Coburn Gore, Me. He was 91 and lived in Lenox, Mass., and Irvington, N.Y.
        Mr. Smith's many books, among them six novels, included ''Baseball,'' ''Heroes of Baseball,'' ''Baseball in America'' and ''Babe Ruth's America.'' He also collaborated on Gen. Matthew B. Ridgway's memoir, ''The Korean War,'' published in 1967.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Jean Kelly; four children, Anvil, of Mount Airy, N.C.; Pamela and Robert S., of Manhattan, and Nora, of Irvington, and three grandchildren.


        A. Oakley Brooks, a retired investment banker, died on Tuesday at a hospital in Falmouth, Mass. He was 81 and lived in Manhattan and Edgartown, Mass.
        The cause was bone cancer, his family said.
        Mr. Brooks was a former chairman of Wood Struthers & Winthrop, where he worked for nearly 30 years. He also served as a director of the Medusa Portland Cement Company, Deltec International and Emigrant Savings Bank.
        He served as president of the Union Club in New York for five years and also was the president of the Mental Health Association of New York and Bronx Counties and the treasurer of the State Charities Aid Association.
        Mr. Brooks was born in Milton, Mass. After graduating from Harvard College, he worked for Chase National Bank and Manufacturer's Trust Company in the 1930's.
        He was married for 50 years to the former Barbara Joan Connick, who died last year.
        His survivors include two daughters, Pamela B. Speir and Candace B. Widmer, both of Martha's Vineyard, Mass.; two sons, A. Oakley Jr., of Washington, and Peter C., of Carlsbad, Calif., and five grandchildren.

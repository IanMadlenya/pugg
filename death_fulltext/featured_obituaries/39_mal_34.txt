        William T. Slater, senior vice presi-dent of the College Retirement Equities Fund of the Teachers Insurance and Annuity Association, died of heart disease on Sunday at the Robert Wood Johnson Medical Center in New Brunswick, N.J. He was 56 years old and lived in Matawan, N.J.
        Mr. Slater joined the $84 billion college teachers' pension organization in 1956 after graduating from Colgate University. In 1967 he was named head of the fund's educational research division and in 1977 he became senior vice president.
        In 1971 he helped develop the Common Fund, an investment arrangement for collegiate endowment funds. It began with 70 institutions but now includes more than 1,000 colleges and universities, with $10 billion in assets.
        He is survived by his wife, Barbara; two sons, William Jr., of Hamburg, N.J., and Jeffrey, of Matawan; two daughters, Barbara, of Plainsboro, N.J., and Willa Hudanich of Sayreville, N.J.; a brother, Joseph, of Rocky Mount, N.C., and two grandsons.

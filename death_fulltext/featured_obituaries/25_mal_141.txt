        Walter A. Shields, an inventor, designer and manufacturer of pharmaceutical machinery, died of cancer Sunday at Palms of Pasadena Hospital in St. Petersburg Beach, Fla., where he was vacationing with his wife. He was 82 years old and lived in Floral Park, L.I.
        Mr. Shields founded the Ampoule Machine Company of Long Island City in 1939 and was its president until his retirement in 1984, when the company was disbanded. He held more than 80 patents for medical and dental packaging machines, including devices that produced disposable needles and syringe assemblies. A native of Lorain, Ohio, he moved to Brooklyn in his youth and received a degree in mechanical engineering from Cornell University.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Helen Trautmann; five daughters, Virginia Walker of Shelter Island, L.I., Eileen West of Washington, Dr. Helen Shields of Boston, Joan E., of Arlington, Mass., and Loraine E., of Beverly Hills, Calif., and three granchildren.

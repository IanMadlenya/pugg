
        John Chaffe Wrightson, a retired textile industry executive, died Thursday at the Dartmouth-Hitchcock Medical Center in Hanover, N.H. He was 67 years old and had moved to Walpole, N.H., from Darien, Conn.
        He died of cardiac arrest, a family spokeswoman said.
        Mr. Wrightson attended Yale University but left to serve with the Army Air Forces in World War II.
        He worked for Burlington Mills and in 1958 founded Heritage Quilts Inc., a textile manufacturer with a sales office in Manhattan, headquarters in Chattanooga, Tenn., and four plants in North Carolina.
        He retired in 1986 and served as a volunteer with the International Executive Service Corps.
        He is survived by his wife, Mary; two daughters, Margaret Lane of Darien and Elizabeth Laurence Wrightson of Atlanta; a brother, George of Norwich, Vt.; and two granddaughters.

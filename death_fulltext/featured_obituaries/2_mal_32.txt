        Donald J. Grout, professor emeritus of musicology at Cornell University, died yesterday at his home in Skaneateles, N.Y., after a long illness. He was 84 years old.
        Dr. Grout was the author of ''A History of Western Music,'' published in 1960, which has become the standard one-volume history of music in English. He also wrote ''A Short History of Opera.''
        He was chairman of Cornell's music department from 1945 to 1970. He was president of the American Musicological Society from 1961 to 1963.
        Dr. Grout was born in Rock Rapids, Iowa. He was a graduate of Syracuse University, and he received his doctorate from Harvard University.
        He is survived by his wife, Margaret Dunn; a daughter, Martha M. Swerdfeger, of Skaneateles; a brother, Arthur, of Easton, Conn., and three grandchildren.

        Brian Roberts, a newspaper editor who was tried and acquited for championing press freedom over a Government report on the Nigerian civil war, died Thursday, his family said. He was 81 years old. The cause of death was not given.  Mr. Roberts spent 37 years with the Telegraph newspapers in London and was the editor of The Sunday Telegraph from 1966 to 1976.
        In 1970, he published a confidential diplomatic report on Nigeria's prospects to prevent the secession of the territory of Biafra. Mr. Roberts and two others were charged under the Official Secrets Act, under which any information can be classified secret and cannot be legally published unless authorized by an official.
        He is survived by his wife, Dora, and a son.

        Morgan Farley, a stage and film actor whose first major role was the protagonist in Booth Tarkington's ''Seventeen'' in 1923, died Tuesday in a San Pedro, Calif., hospital. He was 87 years old and lived in San Pedro.
        Among Mr. Farley's Broadway roles were the adolescent suitor of Peggy Wood in a 1925 revival of Bernard Shaw's ''Candida,'' the hero in a 1926 production of Theodore Dreiser's ''American Tragedy,'' the co-star of Eva Le Gallienne in ''Camille'' in 1931, Raskolnikov in ''Crime and Punishment'' in 1935 and the bartender in a 1938 revival of Sutton Vane's ''Outward Bound.''
        Mr. Farley, a native of Mamaroneck, N.Y., had supporting roles in such films as ''Gentleman's Agreement'' (1947), ''Macbeth'' (1950), ''Goodbye, My Fancy'' (1951) and ''Julius Caesar'' (1953).
        He is survived by a nephew and two nieces.

        Walter E. Harris Jr., a former head of the American subsidiary of Alfred Dunhill, the London tobacco merchant, and earlier an executive at Abraham & Straus in Brooklyn, died on Tuesday at Memorial Sloan-Kettering Cancer Center. He was 65 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        His family said he died of lung cancer.
        As president and later chairman of Alfred Dunhill of London, Inc., based in New York, from 1963 to 1984, Mr. Harris led the company's diversification into luxury goods and men's clothing.
        He was born in Manhattan and was a graduate of Yale. In World War II he served as an ensign in the United States Navy in the Pacific.
        He joined A & S in 1946 and rose to become head of operations before leaving to head Dunhill. Six years ago he co-founded Nature's Best, a manufacturer and distributor of vitamins and food supplements.
        He is survived by his wife, Barbara; a son, Walter 3d, of Chipita, Colo.,; a daughter, Patricia, of Manhattan; a brother, Michael, of Manhattan and a grandson.

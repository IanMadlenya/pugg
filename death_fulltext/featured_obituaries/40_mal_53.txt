        Sidney Kalikow, a Queens real-estate developer, died on Friday in Long Island Jewish Hospital after a 36-year battle with Parkinson's disease. He was 70 years old.
        Mr. Kalikow was born and reared in Brooklyn. A graduate of New York University, Mr. Kalikow had planned on becoming a dentist but instead joined the family building business. He built his first home when he was 17.
        Mr. Kalikow, who was one of three brothers involved in New York real estate, was the founder of the Kaled Management Corporation, which has developed condominium projects in Queens.
        He is survived by his wife, Pearl Bernice; a daughter, Laurie Margaret Platt of Hewlett Harbor, L.I.; a son, Edward Mark, of Old Westbury, L.I., and four grandchildren.


        Harry Whitfield Howze, retired vice president of Esso International, died on Saturday at his home in Newtown Square, Pa. He was 86 years old.
        He died of a heart attack, a family spokesman said.
        Mr. Howze began his career as a pilot for the Humble Oil Company. He became vice president of Esso International Inc., in 1957, with responsibility for international aviation fuel sales. He retired in 1964.
        He was a native of Grenada, Miss., and a graduate of the University of Missouri. In World War II he served in the Army Air Corps, where he developed policies to insure adequate supplies of aviation petroleum products. He was awarded the Legion of Merit and made Commander of the Order of the British Empire.
        He is survived by a son, Bruce, of Springfield, Pa.; a sister, Minna Shofner of Corpus Christi, Tex., and a grandson.

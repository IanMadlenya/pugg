        Lester B. Knight, founder of a consulting engineering firm, died Thursday at his home in Golf, Fla., after a long illness. He was 81 years old.
        Mr. Knight founded Lester B. Knight & Associates Inc. in Chicago in 1945. Born in Albany, he graduated from Cornell University. He endowed a scholarship there and established the Knight National Research Facility for Submicron Structures.
        He was a former president and director of the Travelers Aid Society of Metropolitan Chicago and a former director of the Chicago Association of Commerce and Industry. He retired in 1985.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Frances Edens; a son, Charles, of St. Louis; a daughter, Leslie Abbott of Weston, Mass.; four sisters, Louise Weedon and Althea Bell, both of Salem, Ark., Emilie MacRae of Horseshoe Bend, Ark. and Marjorie Ollayos of Agnos, Ark.; seven grandchildren and two great-grandchildren.

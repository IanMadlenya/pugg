        Dr. Norbert Freinkel, an educator and an international expert on endocrinology and diabetes, died of a heart attack on Tuesday while attending a concert on a visit to Leningrad. He was 63 years old and lived in Evanston, Ill.
        Dr. Freinkel, a former president of the American Diabetes Assocation, held several positions at Northwestern University Medical School. He taught medicine and molecular biology, headed several clinics and directed the Center for Endocrinology, Metabolism and Nutrition.
        A 1949 graduate of New York University Medical College, Dr. Freinkel joined Northwestern in 1966 after 10 years on the medical faculty at Harvard University.
        He is survived by his wife, Dr. Ruth Freinkel, two daughters, Susan, of San Francisco, and Lisa, of Berkeley, Calif; and a son, Dr. Andrew, of Charlottesville, Va.

        Dr. Julius Lane Wilson, first director of medical education of the American Thoracic Society, the medical section of the American Lung Association, died of an aneurysm Tuesday at his home in Tryon, N.C. He was 90 years old.
        Dr. Wilson, a pulmonary specialist, held the post from 1953 until 1963.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Elizabeth M. Paterson; a sister, Elizabeth Copeland of Sacremento, Calif.; two daughters, Mary Louise, of Manhattan, and Helen Maginnis of New Orleans; a son Hugh, of Manhattan; three grandchildren, and one great-grandchild.

        Henry G. Hansburg, a psychologist and author, died of lymphoma Friday at Maimonides Hospital in Brooklyn. He was 79 years old and lived in Brooklyn.
        A 1939 graduate of Columbia University with a Ph.D. in psychology, Dr. Hansburg was senior psychologist with the Jewish Child Care Association from 1955 to 1976. He was chief psychologist at the education clinic at Brooklyn College from 1949 to 1955 and worked for the Bureau of Child Guidance of the New York City Board of Education from 1943 to 1949.
        He was a founder of the Brooklyn Psychological Association and the author of a three-volume work, ''Separation Anxiety,'' published by the Robert E. Krieger Publishing Company of Malabar, Fla.
        He is survived by his wife, Rose; a daughter, Norma, of Pomona, Calif; a son, Matthew, of New Paltz, N.Y.; a brother, Lawrence, and a sister, Edith Sage, both of Miami.

        Robert Burritt Hiatt, a surgeon and teacher, died of cancer on Saturday at Kennebec Valley Medical Center in Gardiner, Me. He was 72 years old and lived in Dresden, Me.
        Dr. Hiatt joined the staff at Columbia Presbyterian Medical Center as an intern in 1942, shortly after receiving his medical training at the University of Cincinnati. He was associated with the hospital for 40 years and his specialty was internal physiology. He also taught surgery at Columbia University College of Physicians and Surgeons.
        In 1954, he helped start a medical school in Shiraz, Iran, and was director of surgery at the Shiraz Medical Center from 1954 to 1956. In 1980 he started the Choerin Research Foundation in Dresden.
        He is survived by his wife, Nancy Eliot; two daughters, Margaret, of Greenwich, Conn., and Betsy Ratcliff, of Hartford, N.Y.; a son, Andrew, of San Diego; three brothers, Edwin and Richard, both of Wilmington, Ohio, and Harold, of Cincinnati; five step-grandchildren, and 11 grandchildren.

        Morris A. Kravitz, one of first developers of suburban shopping centers, died Sunday in Lankenau Hospital, Ardmore, Pa., after a long illness. He was 88 years old and lived in Ardmore.
        Mr. Kravitz was founder and chairman of Morris Kravitz Development, Inc., and was also a founding partner of Kravco, Inc.
        Originally a builder of family housing on Long Island. Mr. Kravitz, in a 65-year career, was responsible for building 15 million square feet of shopping centers. The first was built in Haverstown, Md., and the best-known and largest is the King of Prussia Mall in King of Prussia, Pa.
        In 1957 he and other center builders formed the International Council of Shopping Centers; Mr. Kravitz served as its president in 1968 and 1969.
        He is survived by his wife, Esther; two daughters, Valerie Hyman of Englewood, N.J., and Maxine Kostoff of Villanova, Pa., and five grandchildren.

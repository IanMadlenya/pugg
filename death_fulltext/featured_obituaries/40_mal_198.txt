        Burt J. Siris, a manufacturer of handbags, accessories and luggage, died of emphysema Wednesday at his home in Palm Beach, Fla. He was 71 years old and also lived in Manhattan.
        He was founder and chief executive officer of Sirco International of Mount Vernon, N.Y., an importer and maker of handbags, accessories and luggage. He was a board member for the United Jewish Appeal-Federation and former chairman of its division for solicitations from companies making handbags, belts and fashion accessories.
        Mr. Siris, who was born in Brooklyn, attended the University of Virgina. During World War II he was a deep-sea diver with the Navy.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Linda Petrillo; a daughter, Helin, of Manhattan; two daughters and a son from an earlier marriage, Margo Helphand of Eugene, Ore., Penny Goldsmith of Harrison, N.Y., and Peter Siris of Manhattan, and six grandchildren.

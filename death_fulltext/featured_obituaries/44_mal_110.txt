        Ernest L. Sommer, a partner in the New York City accounting firm of Sommer, Abraham, died on Sunday at his home in Hillsdale, N.J. He was 64 years old.
        Mr. Sommer died of congestive heart failure, said his son, Robert, of Ridgewood, N.J.
        Mr. Sommer founded Sommer, Abraham & Company in Manhattan in 1959. In 1988, it merged with Edward Isaacs & Company to become the 11th largest accounting firm in the city.
        He was born in Kassel, Germany, and came to the United States in 1938. He was a graduate of Pace College and received a master's degree in business from Columbia University.
        Besides his son Robert, Mr. Sommer is survived by his wife, the former Donna Anne Lapin; two other sons, Jeffrey, of Simsbury, Conn., and Benjamin, of Cambridge, Mass.; a brother, Fred, of Manhattan, and a grandchild.

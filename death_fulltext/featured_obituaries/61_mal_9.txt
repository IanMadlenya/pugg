
        James H. Halpin, a former leader of the New York City Bar Association, died on Monday at his home in Bronxville, N.Y. He was 89 years old.
        He died of cancer, his family said.
        For 30 years he was active in the bar association, serving as its vice president, chairman of the executive and judiciary comittees and a member of 20 other committees.
        He was a longtime volunteer for needy children, starting in 1941 on the board of the Tuberculosis Preventorium for Children in Farmingdale, N.J. Later he helped merge it with two other groupsto become the Graham-Windham Services to Families and Children in Hastings-on-Hudson, N.Y.
        He was a director of the Society of Architectural Historians and a founder of the Friends of the American Wing at the Metropolitan Museum of Art. He wrote and lectured on American silver.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Frances Round; a son, James R., of Newburgh, N.Y.; a daughter, Harriet Delihas of Pound Ridge, N.Y., and four grandchildren.

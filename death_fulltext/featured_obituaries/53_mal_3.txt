
        Murray Woodrow, a lawyer, died on Thursday at his home in Rye Brook, N.Y. He was 66 years old.
        Mr. Woodrow died of bone cancer, his son, Robert, said.
        From 1953 to 1980 he was a senior partner in the law firm of Woodrow & Burrows in Yonkers. He then moved to White Plains, where he opened a private practice in general law. He retired in the spring of 1990.
        During World War II, he served in the Army in Europe and was a recipient of the Bronze Star.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Rosalie Levine; two sons, Robert, of Greenwich, Conn., and Michael, of Rye Brook; a daughter, Wendy Weinreb of Purchase, N.Y.; three brothers, Dr. Jack, of Mamaroneck, N.Y., Harold, of Briarcliff Manor, N.Y., and Dr. Louis, of Yonkers; a sister, Marjorie Penchev of Sarasota, Fla., and three grandsons.

        T. Coleman Andrews Jr., a former Virginia legislator who headed the American Independent Party, died of a heart attack Sunday at his home in Richmond, Va. He was 64 years old.
        The American Independent Party, conservative and segregationist, was founded in 1969 as a vehicle to back the Presidential candidacy of George C. Wallace of Alabama. Mr. Andrews had served from 1960 to 1968 in the Virginia House of Delegates.
        Born in Richmond, he graduated from Dartmouth College and earned a master's degree from the Wharton School at the University of Pennsylvania.
        Surviving are his wife, Bryan; a daughter, Deborah Dunlap of Richmond; three sons, T. Coleman 3d and Alan Scott, both of McLean, Va., and Christopher, of Charlottesville, Va.; a stepson, David al-Attiyah of Richmond; a brother, Wilson, of Richmond, and five grandchildren.

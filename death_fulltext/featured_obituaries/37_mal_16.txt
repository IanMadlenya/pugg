        The Rev. Harold E. Fey, an author, educator and former editor of religious journals, died of congestive heart failure on Tuesday at Pomona Valley Hospital in California. He was 91 years old and lived in Claremont, Calif.
        Mr. Fey, a 1927 graduate of Yale University, was an ordained minister in the Disciples of Christ. He was associated with The Christian Century, a nondenominational Protestant weekly published in Chicago.
        He was a correspondent, field editor and executive editor of the journal until 1956, when he was named editor in chief. He left in 1964 to become a professor.
        Mr. Fey (pronounced FYE) joined the publication in 1940 after serving d as editor of World Call magazine from 1932 to 1935 and Fellowship Magazine from 1935 to 1940. Before that, he taught at Union Theological Seminary in Manila and was the minister at First Christian Church in Hastings, Neb.
        After retiring from publishing, Mr. Fey taught social ethics at Christian Theological Seminary in Indianapolis. He was named professor emeritus in 1968 and continued teaching until 1975.
        He was the author of six books, including ''The Lord's Supper: Seven Meanings,'' ''Life - New Style,'' ''With Solemn Reverence'' and an autobiography, ''How I Read the Riddle.''
        He is survived by a son, Russell of Fresno, Calif.; a daughter, Constance Thullen of Bellevue, Wash.; a sister, Evelyn Slater of Hagerstown, Md., seven grandchildren and one great-grandchild.


        Harold D. Klipstein, a lawyer in Manhattan, died on Tuesday at White Plains Hospital. He was 82 years old and lived in Scarsdale, N.Y.
        He died of a stroke, his son Robert said.
        Mr. Klipstein, who specialized in estates, trust and wills, was a partner in what is now Bangser, Kelin & Rocca and its predecessor firms.
        He was a member of the City Bar Association in New York and chairman of its committee on trusts, estates and surrogate's court.
        In addition to his son Robert A., of Manhattan, Mr. Klipstein is survived by his wife, the former Frances G. Horowitz; another son, Dr. Arnold L., of Manchester, Conn.; two stepsons, Lawrence Horowitz of Manhattan and Daniel Horowitz of Larchmont, N.Y.; a sister, Thelma Kraus of Brooklyn, and five grandchildren.

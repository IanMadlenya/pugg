        Frank DiLuzio, a civil engineer who worked on nuclear projects and became an Assistant Secretary of the Interior in the Johnson Administration, died of a heart attack on Sunday at his home in Santa Fe. He was 77 years old.
        Mr. DiLuzio was assigned in 1943 to the Manhattan Project, which built the atomic bomb. He later became a manager for the Atomic Energy Commission.
        In the 1960's he was staff director for the Senate Aeronautical and Space Science Committee, and in 1965 President Lyndon B. Johnson named him Assistant Secretary of the Interior for water pollution control.
        Mr. DiLuzio is survived by his wife, Phyllis; a daughter, a son, six grandchildren and four great-grandchildren.

        Jack Rechtzeit, an actor in the Yiddish theater for more than 60 years, died Sunday at Beth Israel Hospital in Manhattan. He was 85 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Rechtzeit, who was president of the Hebrew Actors Union, made his last New York stage appearance in the 1985-86 season at the Folksbiene Theater in ''Broome Street, America.'' He appeared in productions at the Folksbiene for more than 10 years.
        He was born near Warsaw, and came to the United States in 1923. He acted in the Yiddish theater in Poland, Rumania, France, Belgium, London, Israel and South America, as well as the United States, and was featured in such Yiddish musicals as ''Yoshe Kalb'' and ''Hard to Be a Jew.'' Mr. Rechtzeit was also vice president of the Associated Actors and Artists of America, and an executive member of the Yiddish Theatrical Alliance.
        He is survived by his wife, Frankie; a brother, Seymour, of Manhattan; two sisters, Ruth Balman and Helen Friedman, both of Brooklyn; two daughters, Susan Solomons of Manhattan and Rochelle Deitsch of Albany; three grandchildren, and one great-grandson.
        Funeral services are to be held tomorrow at noon at the Riverside Memorial Chapel, Amsterdam Avenue and 76th Street in Manhattan.

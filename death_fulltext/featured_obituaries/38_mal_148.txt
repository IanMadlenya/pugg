        John Denison Niles, a partner in the law firm of Debevoise & Plimpton in Manhattan , died of pancreatic failure, a complication of AIDS, yesterday at New York University Hospital. He was 55 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Niles joined Debevoise & Plimpton in 1963, after having been a law clerk for one year for Chief Justice Earl Warren. Mr. Niles became a partner in the firm in 1971.
        He was a member of the American Bar Association, the City Bar Association in New York and the American College of Investment Counsel. He specialized in corporate and international finance.
        Mr. Niles graduated in 1962 from the Boalt Hall Law School at the University of California at Berkeley.
        Surviving are two children from a previous marriage, Catherine and Andrew, both of Manhattan, and his father, Russell, of San Francisco.

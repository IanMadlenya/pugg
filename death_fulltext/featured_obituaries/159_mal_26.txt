
        George T. Piercy, a former senior vice president of Exxon Corporation who, in the 1980's, was chairman of the Educational Broadcasting Corporation of New York, now known as Channel 13/WNET, died March 30 at his home in Ponte Vedra Beach, Fla. He was 84.
        He had been in declining health for two years, his family said.
        Mr. Piercy was the chairman of the Educational Broadcasting Corporation from 1981 to 1983. He joined the board in 1967, became vice chairman in 1973 and remained on the board for a time after stepping down as its chairman.
        Mr. Piercy was a senior vice president at Exxon from 1970 until he retired from that corporation in 1980. He was also a trustee of Princeton Theological Seminary and on the boards of other nonprofit organizations.
        He was born in Eau Claire, Wis., and received a bachelor's degree in chemical engineering in 1938 from the University of Minnesota.
        His wife of three decades, the former Virginia Mae Budrow, died in 1979.
        He is survived by his wife, Janet R. Piercy, whom he married in 1982; two daughters, Nancy P. Murray of Elkton, Md., and Susan P. McCarthy of Arden Hills, Minn.; and five grandchildren.

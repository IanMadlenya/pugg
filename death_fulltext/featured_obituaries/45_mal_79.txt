        Scott Bartlett, an educator and independent film maker, died Sept. 29 at Pacific Presbyterian Medical Center in San Francisco. He was 47 years old and lived in San Francisco.
        His former wife, Freude Bartlett, said he died of complications from a kidney and liver transplant.
        In the 1970's Mr. Bartlett, whose works were shown at the Film Forum and the Whitney Museum, produced experimental and erotic movies including ''Medina'' and ''Off-On.'' His films won numerous international awards.
        A graduate of the Illinois Institute of Technology, he also taught film making at various universities, including the University of California at Santa Cruz and the University of Maine. He received fellowships from the Guggenheim Foundation and the American Film Institute.
        He is survived by a son, Adam, of Santa Cruz.


        Frank Tarloff, an Academy Award-winning screenwriter who was blacklisted during the McCarthy era, died on June 25 at his home in Beverly Hills, Calif. He was 83.
        Mr. Tarloff, along with S. H. Barnett and Peter Stone, won an Oscar in 1964 for the screenplay of ''Father Goose,'' a comedy drama set in the South Pacific during World War II and starring Cary Grant. He was also the author of several books, including ''A Guide for the Married Man,'' which he adapted for the screen.
        His many television credits included ''Make Room for Daddy,'' ''I Married Joan,'' ''The Andy Griffith Show'' and ''The Jeffersons.''
        During the years he was blacklisted, Mr. Tarloff lived in London.
        He is survived by his wife, Lee Barrie; a daughter, Julie Owen, and a son, Erik, both of Berkeley, Calif., and three grandchildren.

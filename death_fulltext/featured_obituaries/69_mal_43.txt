
        Dr. Benjamin B. Greenberg, an orthopedic surgeon, died on Oct. 1 while vacationing in La Baule, France. He was 85 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Dr. Greenberg died of a heart attack, said his son, Dr. Robert A. Greenberg.
        Dr. Greenberg, a native of Providence, R.I., earned bachelor's and medical degrees at the University of Michigan in 1927 and 1931.
        From 1937 to 1958, Dr. Greenberg was on the staff of Montefiore Hospital in the Bronx, becoming director of orthopedic surgery there and playing a major role in the development of its residency training program. Later Dr. Greenberg was appointed chairman of the department of orthopedic surgery at Albert Einstein College of Medicine.
        In addition to his son, of Manhattan, he is survived by his wife of 53 years, Madeline; a daughter, Linda Raskin, of the Bronx; a brother, Burleigh, of Providence, and five grandchildren.

        Maj. Gen. Harry M. Arthur, retired who spent two years as the ranking American officer with the Chinese Army in World War II, died Tuesday. He was 88.
        A 1921 graduate of the Citadel, General Arthur was called to active duty in World War II. He served in the Chinese Combat Command, rising to the rank of colonel.
        He retired as a major general in 1959. He became president and chairman of Arthur State Bank, which he and his family founded in 1933.

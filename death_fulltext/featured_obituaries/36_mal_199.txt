        Dr. Hugh Eustis Potts, a biologist and an educator, died of heart disease on Jan. 16 at New Milford (Conn.) Hospital. He was 87 years old and lived in Washington, Conn.
        Dr. Potts became a research biologist with the Biochemical Research Foundation in Newark, Del., shortly after graduating from New York University with a Ph.D. in zoology in 1940. He taught science and biology at the St. Andrew's School in Middletown, Del., from 1944 to 1947, when he joined the faculty at the Pennsylvania College for Women - now Chatham College - in Pittsburgh. Dr. Potts worked at Finch College in Manhattan from 1952 until retiring in 1967.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Alexandra Soundstrom; a daughter, Alexandra Pool of Pittsburgh; two sisters, Laurette Murdock of Boston and Ethel Bernard of Cambridge, Mass.; two grandchildren, and two great-grandchildren.

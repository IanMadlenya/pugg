        William F. Cassin Jr., a real-estate developer, died of pancreatic cancer Monday at his home in Rye, N.Y. He was 49 years old.
        Mr. Cassin graduated from Villanova University and New York Law School. He was the founder and president of the W. C. Realty Corporation, which develops and manages properties in lower Westchester County.
        He was a member of the Rye City Council from 1973 to 1976 and a special agent for the Federal Bureau of Investigation from 1964 to 1967.
        Surviving are his wife, Marilyn; three sons, William 3d, Daniel and Brian; two daughters, Michelle and Laura; his mother, Anne, of Larchmont, N.Y.; four brothers, John, of Luxembourg; Joseph, of Rye, Michael, of San Francisco, and Richard, of Bronxville, N.Y.


        John A. Corbett, a longtime radio and television broadcaster in Buffalo, died on Monday at Sarasota Memorial Hospital in Sarasota, Fla., where he lived. He was 69.
        He died of cancer, his family said.
        On WBEN-TV, Mr. Corbett led Buffalo's ratings as the anchor of the noon and 6 P.M. news reports. He also worked as an editor, writer and reporter and as host of "By the People," an investigative program. On WBEN radio he was the host of the live-audience "Breakfast Program" for 20 years and "Contact," a daily interview program, for 15 years.
        He moved to Sarasota in 1978, and did radio and television programs there.
        Mr. Corbett narrated medical, military and industrial films and also taught communications at Canisius College in Buffalo.
        In Buffalo he was chairman of the local Easter Seals, finance chairman of the Amherst Community Church and on the boards of the Girl Scouts and Y.M.C.A. He also served on the State Veterans Commission.
        His first two marriages ended in divorce.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Susan McCoy; his father, Alan J. of Fayetteville, N.C.; two sons, John D. of Phoenix and Jeffrey of Greenbrae, Calif.; two daughters, Rebecca Neville of Tucson, Ariz., and Patricia Ann Tatro of Newark, N.Y., and several grandchildren.

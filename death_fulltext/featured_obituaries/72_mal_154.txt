
        Harry Thomashevsky, a producer and director in Yiddish theater, died on Thursday in Los Angeles. He was 97.
        Mr. Thomashevsky, the eldest son of Boris and Bessie Thomashevsky, founding members of the Yiddish theater in the United States, made his theatrical debut in New York City at 13 in "The Pintele Yid." Later he was a director of the Federal Theater's Yiddish Theater Project. He also staged productions of the classics, including "Macbeth" and "She Stoops to Conquer," for young audiences.
        He is survived by his wife, Vera, and a daughter, Judith.

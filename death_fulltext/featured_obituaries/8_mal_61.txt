        John Lenehan, a pianist who performed in New York nightclubs, died of complications brought on by AIDS Wednesday at Lenox Hill Hospital. He was 36 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Lenehan, who was born in Manchester, N.H., played and sang in various New York cabarets and nightclubs such as Don't Tell Mama, Brandy's and the Duplex. He appeared in the Broadway production of ''Pump Boys and Dinettes'' and toured with a number of other musicals.
        Earlier in his career, he had been a pianist at the Improvisation, a comedy club in Manhattan.
        He is survived by his mother, Beatrice Lenehan of Manchester, N.H., and three sisters, Maureen McCafferty and Patricia Fitzpatrick, both of Manchester, and Kathleen Roust of Milton, Mass.

        J. Huber Wetenhall, former chairman and chief executive of the National Dairy Products Corporation, died Friday at his home in Franconia, N.H. He was 85 years and a former resident of the Westchester Country Club in Rye, N.Y.
        Mr. Wetenhall was associated with National Dairy, a predecessor of Kraft, for more than 40 years, serving in a variety of managerial positions in Milwaukee, Chicago and New York.
        He was elected president of the company in 1959 and became its chief executive in 1961. He was elected chairman in 1965 and he retired from the company in 1966.
        Mr. Wetenhall continued to work as a director of National Dairy, retiring in 1972 after having served in that capacity for 17 years.
        During his career, he also was president of a National Dairy subsidiary, the Luick Milk and Ice Cream Company in Milwaukee. And he was in charge of the Sealtest Division's milk and ice cream business in Chicago.
        Mr. Wetenhall was also a former director of Boise Cascade, the Manufacturers Hanover Trust Company and Sears, Roebuck & Company.
        He is survived by two sons, John H. Wetenhall Jr., of Franconia, and Palm Beach, Fla., and Robert C. Wetenhall of Rye, and six grandchildren.

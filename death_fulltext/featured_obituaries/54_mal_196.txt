
        John K. Hanson, a retired New York advertising executive, died on Monday at his home in Rumson, N.J. He was 66 years old.
        Mr. Hanson died of cancer, a spokeswoman for the family said.
        He was director of advertising for Burlington Industries in Manhattan from 1961 until his retirement in 1983. He joined the textile company in 1949 and remained with it throughout his career, except for three years with American Enka Corporation.
        Mr. Hanson was born in Newark and graduated from Georgetown University. He served as a lieutenant in the Navy in the South Pacific during World War II.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Joan Black; three sons, John K. Jr., of Camden, Me., Mark, of Medford, N.J., and Timothy, of Hoboken, N.J.; a daughter, Kate, of Kittery, Me.; a sister, Helen, of South Orange, N.J.; a brother, Joseph E. Jr. of Summit, N.J.; and three grandchildren.


        Meyer F. Wiles, an engineer and lawyer who was acting commissioner of public works in New York City in the 1960's, died on Friday at Montefiore Medical Center in the Bronx. He was 83 years old and lived in New Rochelle, N.Y. He died of heart disease, said his daughter, Judith Cohen.
        Besides his daughter, of Commack, L.I., he is survived by his second wife, Betty; a son, Harold, of Miller Place, L.I.; three sisters, Sylvia Schiffenbauer of Rockville, Md., Rose Schectman of Santa Monica, Calif., and Lee Dauber of Los Angeles; four grandchildren, and a great-granddaughter.

        Dr. Erich J. Kahn, retired director of pediatrics at Harlem Hospital and professor emeritus of pediatrics at the Columbia University College of Physicians and Surgeons, died of a stroke on Wednesday at Englewood (N.J.) Hospital. He was 77 years old and lived in Englewood.
        Dr. Kahn, who was born in Paderborn, Germany, immigrated to South Africa during the Nazi era and completed his medical education at the University of Witwatersrand in Johannesburg. After World War II he specialized in pediatrics in London and Edinburgh, where he was a Fellow of the Royal College of Physicians.
        From 1950 to 1962 Dr. Kahn headed the pediatric department at Baragwanath Hospital, serving the population of Soweto, the black township outside Johannesburg. He came to the United States in 1962 and became director of pediatrics at Harlem Hospital. From 1963 until 1979 he taught at the College of Physicians and Surgeons.
        Dr. Kahn is survived by his wife, the former Eva Jourdan, and two sons, David, of Hackensack, N.J., and Colin, of Parsippany, N.J.

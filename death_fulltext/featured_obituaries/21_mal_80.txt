        Shailer L. Bass, a former board chairman and chief executive officer of the Dow Corning Corporation, died Sunday at Provincial House, a nursing home in Midland, after a long illness. He was 82 years old.
        Mr. Bass joined the Dow Chemical Company in 1936 and served as research director on cellulose ethers and other compounds. He is credited with developing the first commercial silicone product, a substance used to protect aircraft ignition systems.
        Mr. Bass was active in the founding of Dow Corning, a joint venture of Corning Glass and Dow Chemical, in 1943, and served as its director of research. In 1967 he became president, chief executive officer and board chairman. He retired in 1971.
        He is survived by his wife, Elizabeth, and seven children.

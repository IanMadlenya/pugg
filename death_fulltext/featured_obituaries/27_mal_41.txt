        Edmund F. Wagner, a longtime real-estate executive and former president of the Seamen's Bank for Savings, died yesterday at Lohman Village in the Wartburg Home complex in Mount Vernon, N.Y. He was 91 years old and, until this year, had lived in Scarsdale, N.Y., for 40 years.
        Mr. Wagner was born and grew up in the Bronx, where he completed his education at Morris High School and went on to become president of the General Realty and Utilities Corporation, a major development company, from 1944 to 1958.
        He worked in various real-estate companies for 41 years, until he joined the Seamen's Bank for Savings, where he was president, board chairman or chief executive officer from 1958 to 1968, when he retired from business. He then served as president of the American Bible Society for 18 years.
        Mr. Wagner, a genial man with a commanding presence, was a former president of the Interchurch Center in Manhattan, the Downtown-Lower Manhattan Association, the Commerce and Industry Association of New York and the Bankers Club of America.
        He had been the treasurer of the United Lutheran Church in America, the Lutheran Church in America and the National Association of Mutual Savings Banks, a vice president of the Citizens Budget Commission and a trustee of Wagner College, which awarded him an honorary doctorate in law in 1949.
        Other organizations that honored Mr. Wagner included the National Conference of Christians and Jews, the Lutheran Church in America, the Lutheran Society, the Boy Scouts of America and the New York Board of Trade.
        Surviving are his wife, Mildred; a son, John W., of Chappaqua, N.Y., and two grandchildren.

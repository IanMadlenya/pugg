        John A. Reardon, a retired overseas oil executive, died of pneumonia June 10 at the Rutland Medical Center in Rutland, Vt. He was 70 years old and lived in Castleton, Vt.
        Mr. Reardon was managing director and chairman of Esso Standard Thailand, a subsidiary of what is now the Exxon Corporation, from 1963 until 1973. He had been with Esso units in China, the Philippines and Africa before that.
        Mr. Reardon graduated from Holy Cross College. He served in the Marine Corps in World War II, emerging as a captain.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Jean Richon; three daughters, Eileen Robbins of Buffalo, Sheila Reardon of San Francisco and Kathleen Murphy of Brooklyn; three sons, John Jr. of Los Angeles, Daniel of Boston and Peter of Brooklyn; four sisters, Elizabeth Rogers of Briarcliff Manor, N.Y., Therese Hart of Yorktown, N.Y., Ruth Castelli of Arlington Heights, Mass., and Eileen Fitzpatrick of Westford, Mass., and two grandchildren.


        Adolfo M. Vargas, a United Nations official, died Nov. 10 at Bellevue Hospital in Manhattan. He was 47 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        He died of a gunshot wound a day after he was attacked in the elevator of his apartment house. The police said yesterday that they did not know the motive and had no suspects.
        Mr. Vargas, who was born in Peru, joined the United Nations staff in 1975 and was most recently chief of the energy resources branch of the Department for Technical Cooperation for Development. A graduate of the National University of Engineering in Peru and New York University, Mr. Vargas was formerly an energy adviser to the Peruvian Government.
        He is survived by his wife, Hilda, two sons, Adolfo and Jorge, both of Manhattan; and two brothers and six sisters in Peru.

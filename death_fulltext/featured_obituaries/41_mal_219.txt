        Peter W. Bove, a retired telephone company supervisor who was active in community affairs, died on Tuesday at his home in Upper Montclair, N.J. He was 82 years old.
        He died of a heart condition, a funeral home representative said.
        He worked for the New Jersey Bell Telephone Company for 39 years and had been supervisor of training and public relations. He retired in 1968. He was an Army captain in World War II.
        Mr. Bove, a graduate of Harvard University, served as an officer of many civic organizations, including the Montclair Public Library, the Rutgers University Foundation, the Family and Children's Society of Montclair, the Community Nursing Service and the Montclair Historical Society.
        He is survived by his wife, Beryl; a daughter, Gertrude Parton of Rumson, N.J.; a son, Roger, of West Chester, Pa.; three sisters and five grandchildren.


        Sol E. Flick, a New York business executive who spent 50 years with companies that made watches, died on Saturday at his home in Roslyn Heights, L.I. He was 78.
        The cause of death was cancer, said his son, Dr. Jonathan A. Flick.
        Mr. Flick retired two years ago as the general counsel of the North American Watch Company in New York, which he had joined in 1981, and as the president of its international subsidiary. For 38 years previously he had been with the Bulova Watch Company.
        Mr. Flick graduated from Brooklyn College, from which he also received a law degree in 1938.
        In addition to his son, Jonathan, of Bryn Mawr, Pa., Mr. Flick is survived by his wife, Stella; a daughter, Susan Falb of Glen Cove, L.I., and a sister, Claire Stein of Roslyn, L.I.

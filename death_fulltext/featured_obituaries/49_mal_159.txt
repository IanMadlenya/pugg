
        Charles W. McCullom, a vice president of Nabisco Brands, died on Thursday at his vacation home in Chatham, Mass. He was 57 years old and lived in Sparta, N.J.
        He died of cancer, the company said.
        Mr. McCullom, a native of Andover, Mass., joined Nabisco in 1954 and rose to direct customer relations for the food manufacturing and marketing company. He also staged the annual Nabisco Dinah Shore Golf Tournament.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Diane Mazur; a daughter, Dr. Susan McCullom-Barry of Portsmouth, N.H.; a son, Douglas, of Kennebunk, Me.; his mother, Jackie, MacLellan of Largo, Fla.; two brothers, William, of Andover, and Thomas, of South Berwick, Me., and two sisters, Joanne Anderson of Portsmouth and Sally Hudgins of Andover.

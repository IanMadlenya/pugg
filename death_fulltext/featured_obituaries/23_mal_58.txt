        Donald E. Schwartz, a lawyer, author and professor of corporate and securities law at Georgetown University, died of cancer Saturday at George Washington University Hospital in Washington. He was 58 years old and lived in Bethesda, Md.
        A graduate of Harvard Law School, Mr. Schwartz was co-director of the joint degree program in business administration and law at Georgetown. For the last 10 years he was also counsel to the Washington law firm of Williams & Connolly.
        Before joining the Georgetown faculty in 1966, Mr. Schwartz was a lawyer with the Securities and Exchange Commission and was in private practice in Manhattan.
        He wrote numerous books and articles and was a leading advocate of corporate social responsibility.
        He is survived by his wife, Ann, and two daughters, Pamela and Abigail, all of Bethesda; his mother, Esther, and a brother, Arthur, both of Manhattan.

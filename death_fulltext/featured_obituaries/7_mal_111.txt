        William A. Rose, a civil engineer and author, died of cancer Monday at Memorial Sloan-Kettering Cancer Center. He was 80 years old and lived in Briarcliff Manor, N.Y.
        Mr. Rose had his own architectural and engineering firm, Rose, Beaton & Rose in White Plains, founded by his father in 1911. He wrote numerous technical articles on engineering and was co-author with Harold Wessman of a book, ''Aerial Bombardment Protection,'' published in 1942.
        He was born in Minneapolis and was a graduate of New York University, where he also earned two master's degrees, one in science and mathematics, the other in engineering.
        Mr. Rose is survived by his wife, the former Josephine Grohe; three sons, William A. Jr., of White Plains, Donald W., of Armonk, N.Y., and Alan D., of Wellesley, Mass.; a daughter, Nancy Aktas of Fairfax, Calif.; two sisters, Mary Rose of Stewart Manor, L.I. and Alexenia Elder of Orlando, Fla., and 10 grandchildren.

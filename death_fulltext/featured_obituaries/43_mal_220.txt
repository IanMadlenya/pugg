        Dr. Paul P. Casamassimo, a retired dentist who was on the staff of the North Central Bronx Hospital for almost 50 years, died on Monday at Waterbury (Conn.) Hospital. He was 77 years old and lived in Southbury, Conn.
        He died of congestive heart failure, his daughter Marie Faynor said.
        Dr. Casamassimo was a teacher and attending dentist at the hospital and also maintained a private practice in the Bronx. He retired in 1989.
        He was born in Manhattan and graduated from Fordham University in 1936 and from the Georgetown University College of Dentisry in 1940.
        A widower, he is survived by two daughters, Marie Faynor of Southbury and Emily Pogers of Brookfield, Conn.; a son, Paul, of Columbus, Ohio., and five grandchildren.

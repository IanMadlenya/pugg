        Charles (Red) Barrett, who holds the major league record for the fewest pitches in a nine-inning game, died at his home Saturday after a long illness. He was 75 years old.  While pitching for the Boston Braves in 1944, he faced 27 batters and threw only 58 pitches in a 2-0 victory over the Cincinnati Reds.
        He was named to the National League All-Star team in 1945. He pitched in the World Series for the Braves in 1948.
        Mr. Barrett, who also pitched for Cincinnati, compiled a career record of 69-69 with an earned run average of 3.53.

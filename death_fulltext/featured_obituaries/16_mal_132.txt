        Sammy Davis Sr., a vaudeville performer and the father of the entertainer Sammy Davis Jr., died Saturday at his home in Beverly Hills, Calif. He was 87 years old.
        Mr. Davis spent more than 40 years in show business, performing in vaudeville acts, on stage and in nightclubs like the Copacabana in New York.
        Mr. Davis was born in Wilmington, N.C. He began his career as a dancer and soon joined the Will Mastin Trio, a song-and-dance group formed after World War I. He played Fletcher Henderson in the 1955 film ''The Benny Goodman Story,'' and, after his son's rise to fame, he appeared on the ''Colgate Comedy Hour'' and many other television specials from the 1950's to the 1970's.
        In addition to his son, Mr. Davis is survived by two daughters, Ramona James of New York and Suzette Davis of Portland, Ore.; six grandchildren, and one great-grandchild.

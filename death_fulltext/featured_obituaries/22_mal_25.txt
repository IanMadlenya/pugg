        George Folsey, a cinematographer who was nominated for 13 Academy Awards, died at a hospital here Tuesday after a stroke and long illness. He was 90 years old.
        Mr. Folsey's career spanned 50 years, and he was best known for his collaborations with the directors Vincente Minelli, George Cukor, Frank Capra, Ernst Lubitsch, Rouben Mamoulian and Busby Berkeley.
        Among the films that Mr. Folsey shot were ''Animal Crackers,'' ''State of the Union'' and ''Meet Me in St. Louis'' and the television series ''The Fugitive.''
        In 1976, he returned to motion pictures to film special footage of Fred Astaire and Gene Kelly for ''That's Entertainment, Part 2.''
        His Oscar nominations included ''Executive Suite,'' ''Seven Brides for Seven Brothers'' and ''The Balcony.'' Mr. Folsey also served as president of the American Society of Cinematographers in the late 1950's.
        He is survived by his wife of 61 years, Angele; a son, George Jr.; and two grandchildren.

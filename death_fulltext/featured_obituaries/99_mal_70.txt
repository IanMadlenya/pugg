
        John Joseph Crowley Jr., a career Foreign Service officer and former deputy representative at the Organization of American States, died on March 28 at Sibley Memorial Hospital in Washington. He was 67 and lived in Bethesda, Md.
        The cause was non-Hodgkins lymphoma, his family said.
        Mr. Crowley, a former Ambassador to Suriname, retired from his O.A.S. post in 1986. He had risen through the ranks in Peru, Belgium, Ecuador, the Dominican Republic and Venezuela, interspersed with assignments in Washington. There he directed the Venezuela desk in the mid-1960's and the Bureau of Northern European Affairs in the mid-1970's.
        Mr. Crowley is survived by his wife of 42 years, Ileana Rivera Cintron Crowley; two daughters, Gail-Marie C. Brenny of Annapolis, Md., and Nita C. Sayre of Buenos Aires; and one grandson.

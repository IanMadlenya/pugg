
        Robert J. Davis, former executive vice president of the American Optical Company in New York, later owned by Warner Lambert Inc., died on Tuesday at a nursing home in Montclair, N.J. He was 94 years old and lived in Montclair.
        Mr. Davis died of natural causes, a spokesman for the family said.
        He joined American Optical Company in Manhattan in 1954, as executive vice president of international operations and later of domestic operations. He retired in 1960.
        From 1948 to 1954, he was executive vice president of Warner Lambert Inc., in Manhattan.
        He served as a Naval aviator in World War I.
        He is survived by a son, Robert J. of Bay Head, N.J.; a daughter, Leora Krayer of Glen Ridge, N.J.; five grandchildren, and seven great-grandchildren.


       Nancy Sutton, a retired advertising executive, died on Friday at her home in Manhattan. She was 67 years old.
        Ms. Sutton, who spent 30 years in advertising, became a vice president in 1962 at Grey Advertising Inc. in Manhattan. Canada Dry, Timex, Revlon and B. F. Goodrich were among her accounts. She retired in 1987.
        She received many Clio awards and Andy Awards, the Advertising Club of New York's highest honor. She also received a Cannes International Advertising Festival award.
        Ms. Sutton was active in civic affairs, including the Joffrey Ballet, the JOBS program and the Phoenix House drug program.
        She graduated from Allegheny College in 1944.
        There are no immediate survivors.

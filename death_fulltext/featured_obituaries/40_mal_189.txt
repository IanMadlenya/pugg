        Leonard Friedlander, an award-winning television producer, died of a heart attack on May 15 at his home in Marina del Rey, Calif. He was 48 years old.
        Mr. Friedlander produced the series ''Santa Barbara,'' for which he won two Emmy awards. His other credits include ''TV's Bloopers and Practical Jokes,'' ''The Explorers'' and ''A Conversation With . . . Starring Gloria Steinem.'' He also directed Worldwide Videotape Production at 20th Century Fox and co-produced shows in England with the British Broadcasting Corporation. He began his career as an associate producer with ''The Dick Cavett Show.''
        He is survived by his wife, Jennie Blackton; his son, James, of Marina del Rey; his father, Benjamin, of Fort Lauderdale, Fla., and his sister, Elaine Fishman, of New York City.

        Irving Edison, former president of Edison Brothers Stores and a longtime member of the National Jewish Welfare Board, died Tuesday at his home in St. Louis. He was 89 years old.
        Mr. Edison, with his four brothers, was a co-founder in 1922 of Edison Brothers Stores, a chain that grew to more than 300 outlets selling women's shoes, hosiery, handbags and accessories when he was president from 1957 until 1968.
        He was served on the welfare board for several decades and was president from 1950 to 1954. He was also a past president of the World Federation of Young Men's Hebrew Associations and Community Centers.
        Mr. Edison's brothers, Harry, Mark, Sam and Simon, predeceased him, as did his wife, the former Beatrice Chanin, who died last October. He is survived by a son, Bernard, of St. Louis; a daughter, Ruth Tilsley of Santa Monica, Calif.; seven grandchldren and one great-grandchild.

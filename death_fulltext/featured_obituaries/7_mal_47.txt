        Dr. William G. MacDonald, a surgeon and former house doctor for the Metropolitan Opera, died of a heart attack Monday at his home in the Riverdale section of the Bronx. He was 75 years old.
        Dr. MacDonald served as medical director at the Metropolitan Opera House and as its physician on Monday night duty for 24 years until he retired in 1978. In those volunteer posts, he tended to such medical emergencies as the ballet dancer Rudolf Nureyev's injured knee.
        He also served as medical director for the Lord & Taylor Fifth Avenue store from 1950 to 1978.
        He was born in Wilmington, Vt., and earned his medical degree at the University of Vermont. In 1953, he began a private practice in Manhattan and was on the staff of St. Clare's Hospital until 1978.
        He is survived by his wife, Margaret Sweeney MacDonald; a son, Dr. Douglas G. MacDonald of Weare, N.H.; a daughter, Sandra MacDonald Devaney of Cambridge, Mass., and two grandchildren.

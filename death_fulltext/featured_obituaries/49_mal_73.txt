
        Charles W. Beck, president of the investment banking concern of Charles W. Beck & Company, died on Feb. 4 at his home in Manhattan. He was 79 years old and also lived in Palm Beach, Fla.
        He died of heart failure, his family said.
        Mr. Beck, a native of Kansas City, Mo., worked there in the stock brokerage business before moving to Chicago and then to New York, where he founded the banking concern. It engaged in several ventures in real-estate development, gold mining and oil exploration, including the organization of the Carlton Oil Company, a drilling operation in Ohio, in the early 1980's.
        Mr. Beck was a lieutenant in the Navy in World War II. He was semi-retired at his death.
        He is survived by a brother, Leon, of Alamogordo, N.M.

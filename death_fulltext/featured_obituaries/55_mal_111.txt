
        Alexander Weinstein, a teacher in the New York City school system for more than 30 years and a championship tennis coach, died on Saturday at his home in Mount Vernon, N.Y. He was 76 years old.
        He died of brain cancer, his grandson, Allan, said.
        From 1972 to 1985, Mr. Weinstein coached the tennis team at the Bronx High School of Science, where he also taught health education. In 1982, 1983 and 1985 his teams won the city championship. Before that he had taught at a number of New York City high schools and had been an administrator in Brooklyn.
        He earned bachelor's and master's degrees at New York University.
        Before and during World War II he worked at the U.S.O., and from 1948 to 1954, he was an administrator at the Anti-Defamation League of B'nai B'rith.
        Mr. Weinstein is survived by his wife, Mildred; two daughters, Eve Weinstein of Ardley, N.Y., and Shelley Leichman of Tarrytown, N.Y.; a brother, George Wile of Cincinnati, and four grandchildren.

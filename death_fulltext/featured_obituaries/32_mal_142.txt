        Dr. Gabriel Maria Giannini, a physicist and aerospace equipment manufacturer, died of a stroke Wednesday at the Eisenhower Medical Clinic in Rancho Mirage, Calif. He was 83 years old and lived in Indio, Calif.
        In 1945 Mr. Giannini founded his own company for making aircraft and missile guidance systems. After the concern, Giannini Controls Corporation, went public and expanded, Mr. Giannini left in 1961 to establish other companies. He earned the latest of more than 50 patents this year, for a fiberoptic connector.
        Born in Rome, Mr. Giannini received a doctorate from the University of Rome and immigrated to the United States in 1931. He worked as an acoustical engineer for the Curtis Institute and Radio Corporation of America.
        He was a former member of the New York Yacht Club and a well-known competitive sailor. Surviving are his wife, the former Olga Harrington; a son, Valerio of Newport Beach, Calif.; two daughters, Maria Giannini Madigan of Newport Beach and Gabriella Giannini of San Diego; a stepdaughter, Olga Porteous, and a stepson, John Porteous, both of San Diego; a sister, Valeria Tessiore of Rome; five grandchildren, and two great-grandchildren.

        Julius J. Schaefer, a transportation broker, died of a heart attack on March 22 at the Miami Heart Institute in Miami Beach. He was 72 years old and had homes in Englewood Cliffs, N.J., and North Miami Beach, Fla.
        Mr. Schaefer was president of Crossroads Forwarding, a company that seeks the cheapest and fastest routes to ship freight. He was formerly president of Julius J. Schaefer Associates, another forwarder, and was a founder of the Transportation Brokers Conference Association.
        He was a veteran of World War II and a past commander of the Bergen County chapter of the Jewish War Veterans.
        Surviving are his wife, Goldie; a daughter, Barbara Engber of Englewood Cliffs; a son, Barry, of Hollywood, Fla., and a sister, Mina Stein of Hollywood, Calif.

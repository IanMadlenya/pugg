        Arthur M. Kreidmann, a partner in the New York law firm of Weil, Gotshal & Manges, died of complications from a heart attack Saturday at Mount Sinai Medical Center. He was 80 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Kreidmann had been with the firm for about 45 years, specializing in corporate law.
        He was born in Manhattan and graduated from New York University in 1930 and from Columbia Law School in 1933. He was an officer and trustee of the American Museum of Immigration on Liberty Island.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Leona Benjamin, and a son, Kenneth, of Manhattan.

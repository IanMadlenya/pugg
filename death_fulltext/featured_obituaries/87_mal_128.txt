
        Franklin B. Dryden, a leading lobbyist and public relations executive for the tobacco industry in Washington for 14 years, died on Thursday at the Manor Care Nursing Center in Arlington, Va. He was 78.
        The cause was respiratory failure, said his son, Steven Dryden.
        From 1966 to 1980 Mr. Dryden was the executive vice president of the Tobacco Institute, the industry's trade association. He had spent 10 years as a tobacco marketing specialist in the Department of Agriculture. From 1964 to 1966 he was Deputy Director of the Federal Office of Emergency Planning in Washington.
        Mr. Dryden graduated from the University of Kentucky and was a major in the Army in World War II.
        In addition to his son, of Bethesda, Md., Mr. Dryden is survived by his wife, Dorothy; a daughter, Susannah, of Arlington, and a sister, Katherine Dryden of Paris, Ky.


        Pierre Braunberger, who produced the first films of Jean Renoir and Luis Bunuel during a 67-year career in cinema, died on Friday, his family said today. He was 85 years old.
        The cause of death was not disclosed.
        Mr. Braunberger entered the film industry in 1922 and became a producer of silent movies two years later.
        He produced Renoir's first film, "Catherine," and Bunuel's first film, "The Andalusian Dog," as well as works by renowned directors like Alain Resnais, Jean-Luc Godard and Claude Berri. His most recent production was "Knights of the Round Table" in 1989.
        In 1980 he received an honorary Cesar award, the French equivalent of the Oscars given in Hollywood.
        Mr. Braunberger married in 1964 and had two sons.

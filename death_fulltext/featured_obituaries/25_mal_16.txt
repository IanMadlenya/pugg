        Herbert Ross, an industrial designer who specialized in supermarket interiors, died of cancer Sunday at the Tisch Hospital of New York University. He was 61 years old and lived in the Riverdale section of the Bronx.
        Mr. Ross, a graduate of Pratt Institute, began designing food stores in the early 1950's and most recently was president of Herb Ross Design Associates, a Manhattan concern specializing in retail planning and design.
        Some of his clients included D'Agostino's in Manhattan; Shop Rite in New Jersey; Winn Dixie in the South and supermarket chains in Europe and South America.
        He is survived by his wife, Helen, and two children, Ivy and Neil, both of Manhattan.

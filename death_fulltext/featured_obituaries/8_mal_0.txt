        Dr. Samuel Forsheit, a former high school principal in Brooklyn, died of a heart attack Thursday at his home in Brooklyn. He was 73 years old.
        Dr. Forsheit was associated with the New York City school system for 39 years, first, in 1937, as a teacher and later as principal of Bushwick High School and James Madison High School. He retired from the school system in 1967 and taught at Pace University in Manhattan.
        Born in Odessa, the Ukraine, Dr. Forsheit came to the United States as a child. He was a graduate of the City College of New York, where he later earned a master's degree. He also earned a doctorate in education at Yeshiva University.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Nettie Mendelson; a sister, Sylvia Krevisky of the Bronx; a son, Lewis of Los Angeles; three daughters, Beth Kochen of Princeton, N.J., Carrie Regenstein of Ithaca, N.Y., and Ann Cohen of West Hartford, Conn., and 10 grandchildren.


        Rabbi Isaac Swift, a leader in the Orthodox community in Bergen County, N.J., for 30 years, died on Friday at his home in Englewood. He was 80 years old.
        Rabbi Swift was the spiritual leader of Congregation Ahavath Torah from 1960 until he retired in 1984. He continued to serve the congregation as its rabbi emeritus. He played a leading role in the founding of the Rabbinical Council of Bergen County and in establishing Orthodox Jewish education in northern New Jersey.
        He founded the Moriah School of Engelwood and the Frisch Yeshiva High School. Chairs in Judaic studies were established in his name at the University of North Carolina and at the Jewish Community Center in Teaneck. Rabbi Swift was born in England, and educated there and in Israel, where he was ordained in 1938. He led congregations in England and Australia before coming to the United States in 1954.
        He is survived by his wife, Ruth; two daughters, Valerie Sharfman of West Orange, N.J., and Eleanor Batya Yasgur of Teaneck, N.J.; two sisters, Gertrude Lederman of Melbourne, Australia, and Dvora Bender of London, and seven grandchildren.

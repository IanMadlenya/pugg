        Konstantin A. Andrianov, a member of the International Olympic Committee and the first Soviet representative to that organization, has died, the Soviet press agency Tass reported today. He was 78 years old.
        The report did not say when he died or give the cause of death.
        Mr. Andrianov was first vice president of the International Olympic Committee from 1962 to 1974. He was the Soviet Union's first representative to the committee in 1951, and was the head of the Soviet Olympic Committee until 1975.

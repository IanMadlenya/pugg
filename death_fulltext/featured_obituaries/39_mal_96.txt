        Frederick K. Daniel, a chemist who founded Daniel Products, a paint manufacturing company in Jersey City, died of complications of Parkinson's disease on March 27 at his home in Princeton, N.J. He was 79 years old.
        Mr. Daniel, a native of Hamburg, Germany, studied aircraft engineering in Berlin and chemistry in Zurich and London before coming to the United States. He founded his paint company in 1957, sold it to a Dutch concern in 1978 and remained active as a consultant until his retirement in 1982.
        He is survived by his wife, Ruth; two daughters, Marjorie Van Name of Boulder, Colo., and Kathie Johnson of Chicago; a son, Thomas, of Norwalk, Conn., and six grandchildren.


        Allen C. Holmes, a lawyer and former managing partner of Jones Day Reavis & Pogue, one of the nation's largest law firms, died Thursday at his home in Bratenahl, Ohio. He was 70 years old.
        He died of an infectious nerve disease known as Guillain-Barre, a family spokesman said.
        Mr. Holmes was a member of Jones Day for more than 40 years. He helped to build the firm, which has a Cleveland heaquarters, into a national organization. It now has more than 1,200 lawyers and offices in 19 cities around the world.
        Mr. Holmes joined the firm in 1944, shortly after graduating from the University of Michigan Law School. He served as managing partner of the Cleveland office, was Ohio regional managing partner and from 1980 to 1985 was national managing partner. He retired in 1986.
        He served on dozens of boards, including the Greater Cleveland Growth Association, United Way, the Cleveland Institute of Art, Cleveland Scholarship Program and the Greater Cleveland Domed Stadium Corporation.
        He is survived by his wife, Louise; four sons, Peter of Washington, Douglas of Chicago, and William and Thomas, both of Cleveland; a brother, Charles, of Cincinnati; and six grandchildren.

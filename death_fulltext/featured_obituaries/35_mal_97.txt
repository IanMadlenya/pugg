        Louis A. Breglio, a former dean of students at Brooklyn College, died yesterday at his home in Wantagh, L.I., after a long illness. He was 74 years old.
        Dr. Breglio, a retired English professor, was appointed dean of students in 1968 and remained in the post until 1970. He retired in 1975.
        He joined Brooklyn College in 1951 and became an English professor and faculty adviser to student publications.
        Dr. Breglio, a native New Yorker, had also taught at Columbia University and at Marymount College. He earned his Ph.D. in English from Columbia in 1941.
        He was a founder of the Wantagh Public Library and a former president of the Wantagh Board of Education.
        A widower, he is survived by a son, Anthony P., of Amityville, L.I.; two daughters, Mary Lou Coronios of Manhattan and Elizabeth L., of Wantagh; two sisters, Irene Buttner of Bethpage, L.I., and Florence DeLuca of Levittown, L.I., and four grandchildren.

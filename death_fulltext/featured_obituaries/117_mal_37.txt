
        Harry Glickman, assistant concertmaster of the NBC Orchestra under Arturo Toscanini and first violinist of the WQXR String Quartet for 25 years, died Oct. 6 at the Jewish Home and Hospital for the Aged in Manhattan. He was 86.
        Mr. Glickman was born in Russia and was brought to the United States by his family when he was a boy. At 14 he won a scholarship to the Paris Conservatory of Music and was awarded first prize for music in 1924. He was also a graduate of the Juilliard School.
        In addition to his work with the WQXR String Quartet, he was concertmaster of the Brooklyn Philharmonic and was a member of the Hofstra Quartet for more than 15 years. He worked in television for Sid Caesar's ''Your Show of Shows.''
        He is survived by his wife, Beatrice; a daughter, Marj Brown of Manhattan; two sons, Arthur, of Berkeley, Calif., and Norman, of Princeton, N.J., and four grandchildren.

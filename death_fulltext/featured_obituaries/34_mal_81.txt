        Walter I. Garms, a retired dean of the University of Rochester's Graduate School of Education and Human Development and a professor emeritus of education, died Thursday, apparently of a heart attack, at his home in Berkeley, Calif. He was 64 years old.
        Professor Garms taught at the University of Rochester from 1972 to 1987, when he retired. Previously, he taught at Teachers College, Columbia University. Professor Garms was born in Hayden, Ariz. He graduated from Stanford University where he earned master's and doctorate degrees.
        Surviving are his wife, George Ann; a son, Walter 3d, of Palo Alto, Calif.; a daughter, Margaret, of Chicago; a sister, Mary Alice Ramsden of Orinda, Calif., and one grandson.

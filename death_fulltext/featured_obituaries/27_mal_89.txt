        Denys W. Knoll, a retired Navy rear admiral who helped organize the first military staff for the United Nations, died Wednesday at St. Vincent Health Center. He was 82 years old and lived in Erie.
        He served as a commander of the naval staff in the Philippines when the Japanese bombed Pearl Harbor. He later was a member of the United States military mission to the Soviet Union in Moscow. He retired in 1967.
        Survivors include his wife, Jean Shaw Knoll, three brothers and four sisters.


        Hugh McPhillips, a cast member of the CBS daytime television drama "The Young and the Restless," died yesterday at Sherman Oaks (Calif.) Community Hospital. He was 70 years old and lived in North Hollywood.
        He died of injuries from an automobile accident in August, said his former wife, Rosemary Q. McPhillips.
        Mr. McPhillips played Andre, the restaurant host, in "The Young and the Restless." In 1980 he won an Emmy for outstanding cameo appearance in a daytime drama for "Days of Our Lives." He was also in "Murder, She Wrote," "All in the Family," "The Guiding Light" and "Santa Barbara."
        He was appearing in Shakespeare's "Cymbeline" at the Chandler Studio in Los Angeles at the time of the accident.
        Surviving are two sons, Brian, of Portsmouth, R.I., and Michael, of Columbus, Ohio; two daughters, Maureen M. Allen of New York City, and Anne-Marie, of Bethesda, Md., and a brother, Charles, of Whiting, N.J.

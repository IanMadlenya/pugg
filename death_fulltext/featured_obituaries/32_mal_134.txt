        Henricus A. F. Heidweiller, a former Foreign Minister of Suriname, died Tuesday at his home in Paramaribo, the South American's country's capital. He was 60 years old.
        Mr. Heidweiller was a member of Parliament from 1958 to 1967 and later was Suriname's representative at the United Nations. In 1984 he was named Ambassador to the Netherlands, Suriname's former colonial master, and in 1987 he became Foreign Minister. He retired in 1988.
        He is survived by his wife, Rosario, and seven children.

        Dr. Hugh C. Banks, assistant chancellor at New York University, died on Saturday at New York University Hospital. He was 61 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Dr. Banks died of cancer, his daughter, Sonia, said.
        A psychologist, Dr. Banks joined the N.Y.U. faculty in 1965 as an associate professor and became special assistant to the chancellor in 1968. He was instrumental in founding the Association of Black Faculty at the university and was appointed assistant chancellor in 1969. He went on sick leave in February.
        From 1962 to 1964, he was a clinical psychologist for the Veterans Adminstration, and he continued in that role as a consultant until February.
        Dr. Banks was born in Lynchburg, Va., and graduated from Howard University in 1950, where he received an master's degree in 1952. In 1962 he received a doctorate from N.Y.U.
        He was chairman of the university's faculty council and a member of its senate. He was also a member of the the university governance committee.
        He belonged to a number of professional groups, including the American Psychological Association, the New York Psychological Association and the Association of Black Psychologists.
        Dr. Banks is survived by his wife, the former Olivia Brown; a daughter, Sonia, of Queens, and two stepsons, Mulbah Coleman of Queens and John Coleman of East Providence, R.I.
        A memorial service will be held on Thursday at 7 P.M. at St. Albans Congregational Church, 172-17 Lyndon Boulevard, St. Albans, Queens.

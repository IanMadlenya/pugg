        Dr. George J. Becker, an author of books on writers and literary criticism and a retired chairman of the English department at Swarthmore College, died on Friday in a nursing home in Bellingham, Wash., after a long illness. He was 81 years old.  Dr. Becker was on Swarthmore's faculty for 25 years, heading its English department from 1953 to 1970, when he transferred to Western Washington University. He retired in 1974.
        His books included ''John Dos Passos'' (1974), ''Shakespeare's Histories'' (1977), ''Realism in Modern Literature'' (1980), ''D. H. Lawrence'' (1980), ''Master European Realists of the Nineteenth Century'' (1982) and ''James A. Michener'' (1983).
        Dr. Becker, who grew up in Yakima, Wash., was elected to Phi Beta Kappa at the University of Washington, where he earned bachelor's, master's and doctoral degrees. Competent in six languages, he translated messages for the War Department in World War II.
        Among his translated volumes were ''Jean-Paul Sartre: Anti-Semite and Jew'' (1948) and ''Documents of Modern Literary Realism'' (a widely used reference that he also edited, 1963).
        Surviving are his wife of 57 years, Marion; three sons, John, of Columbus, Ohio, Dennis, of Silver Spring, Md., and Michael, of Carbondale, Ill.; a sister, Marian Becker, of Los Angeles; nine grandchildren and two great-grandchildren.

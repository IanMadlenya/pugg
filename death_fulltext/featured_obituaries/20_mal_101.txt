        Michael Francis Powers, executive vice president of the Betsy Nolan Literary Agency, died Monday at St. Vincent's Hospital in Manhattan. He was 51 years old and lived in Greenwich Village.
        Mr. Powers died of pneumonia, according to his family.
        A native of Milford, Mass., Mr. Powers joined the agency in August 1984. Before that, he had worked for the reference division of Macmillan and for the Encyclopaedia Britannica. Mr. Powers had been an assistant agent at International Creative Management and was managing director of Personal Investor magazine.
        He is survived by his parents, Francis J. and Eva Piccinotti Powers, and a sister, Donna Julian, all of Milford.

        Dr. Thomas B. McConnell, professor emeritus at the University of California Graduate School of Education at Berkeley, died of heart failure Jan. 16 at his home in Oakland, Calif. He was 87 years old and suffered from Parkinson's disease.
        In 1954, after four years as chancellor of the University of Buffalo, Dr. McConnell resigned to resume writing and teaching. Previously he was dean of the College of Science, Literature and Arts of the University of Minnesota.
        From 1965 to 1968, Professor McConnell organized and directed the Center for Research and Development in Higher Education in Berkeley, one of five such centers jointly founded by the United States Office of Education and universities. He retired in 1968.
        He received a number of awards and served on four Presidential commissions on public education.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Ruth Kegley; a son, Robert of Mansanita, Ore.; a daughter, Carol of Gurnee, Ill.; a sister, Josephine McConnell of Des Moines, Iowa; five grandchildren and two great-grandchildren.

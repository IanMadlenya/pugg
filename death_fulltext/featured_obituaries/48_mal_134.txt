
        Ross McLeod, former vice president and general counsel for the New York Life Insurance Company in Manhattan, died on Saturday in a nursing home in Stamford, Conn. He was 91 years old and lived in Old Greenwich, Conn.
        He joined the company in 1927 as assistant counsel and was named general counsel in 1956. When he retired in 1965, he was in charge of legal matters involving the company's investments.
        Mr. McLeod was born in Newfoundland and graduated from Dalhousie University in Nova Scotia. He received a law degree from Harvard in 1924 and practiced law in Nova Scotia until 1927.
        During World War II he served with the Air Force in China and was awarded a Bronze Star.
        He is survived by his daughter, Sheila Ainslie of Manhattan.

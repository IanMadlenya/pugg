        Roy Hanson, a writer and editor at Newsday, died of lung cancer Tuesday at North Shore University Hospital in Manhasset, L.I. He was 54 and lived in Copiague, L.I.
        Mr. Hanson began at Newsday in 1962 and worked in several jobs, including editor of Part II, a daily feature section. After his cancer was diagnosed, Mr. Hanson wrote ''Night Thoughts,'' describing his feelings about the illness. The Sunday Magazine Editors Association named it the best article of 1987.
        Mr. Hanson was born in Waterbury Conn. He graduated from Marquette University.
        Surviving are his wife, Christine Lord; his stepson, Alexander Hoffman-Lord; his father, Rodney, of Naugatuck, Conn. and a brother, Paul.

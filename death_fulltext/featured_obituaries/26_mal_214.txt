        Nathan B. Fogelson, a bankruptcy lawyer, died of cancer Tuesday at New York University Hospital. He was 86 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        From 1983 to 1985, Mr. Fogelson was president of the Bankruptcy Lawyers Bar Association and editor of its Bankruptcy Bulletin from 1972 to the present. He practiced law for 66 years and was with the firm of Barst & Mukamul.
        Mr. Fogelson was born in Russia. He graduated from New York University Law School, where he was an adjunct professor from 1972 to 1975.
        Surviving are his wife, Gussie; three sons, Robert, of Cambridge, Mass., David, of Los Angeles and Jeffrey, of Scarsdale, N.Y.; a sister, Anna Cheifetz of Palisades, N.Y.; two brothers, Jacob, of Manhattan, and Harold, of Sayerville, N.J., and five grandchildren.

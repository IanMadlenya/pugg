
        Edward Ginsberg, a Cleveland corporate and real-estate lawyer who was a prominent national fund-raiser for Jewish causes and Israel, died Jan. 3 at Scottsdale Memorial Hospital in Scottsdale, Ariz., where he was spending the winter. He was 79.
        He suffered a respiratory illness, his family said.
        In the 1960's and 1970's, Mr. Ginsberg was general chairman and, subsequently, president of the National United Jewish Appeal, and president of the American Joint Distribution Committee. He sponsored many educational programs at Hebrew University of Jerusalem, which made him an honorary founder.
        Born in Brooklyn, Mr. Ginsberg grew up in Cleveland and graduated from the University of Michigan in 1938. He received his law degree at Harvard University in 1941.
        He was a past director of El Al Airlines and First Israel Bank and Trust Company. He was associated with several law firms over the years, and at his death was counsel to the firm Dinn, Hochman & Potter in Cleveland.
        Mr. Ginsberg is survived by his wife, Rosalie; two sons, William, of London, and Robert S., of Cleveland; a sister, Mildred Sindell, of Baltimore, and two granddaughters.

        Dr. Henry G. Russell, a professor and college administrator, died Monday at Doctor's Hospital, after a long illness. He was 77 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        He was a professor of religion at Bowdoin College for 11 years and also taught at Columbia University and Swarthmore College. He did administrative work at the American University at Cairo in the 1960's and was associated with the Foundation Center in Manhattan when he retired in 1977.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Sylvia Ceasar Solomon; two daughters, Margaret Stenstrom of Summit, N.J., and Emily Russell of Newton, Mass.; a son, Stephen, of Wausau, Wis.; a sister, Mary Thomas of Chapel Hill, N.C., and five grandchildren.

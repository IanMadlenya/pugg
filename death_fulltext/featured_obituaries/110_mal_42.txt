
        Leon Finley, a Manhattan lawyer whose lifelong support of St. John's University put his name on Finley Hall at the School of Law, died on March 2 at  New York Hospital. He was 88 and lived on the Upper East Side.
        Mr. Finley, a 1929 graduate of the law school, was a member of the university's board of trustees. He helped make improvements to St. John's library, classrooms, faculty offices and alumni services.
        In addition to his work on behalf of the law school, he served as chairman of the Hunter College Opera Guild and sat on the boards of the Anti-Defamation League and the Hebrew University of Jerusalem and was active as a member of the  Metropolitan Opera Club.
        A founding senior partner of the defunct law firm of Finley, Kumble, Wagner,  Heine, Underberg, Manley, Myerson & Casey, he was of counsel to the Manhattan firm of Baer Marks & Upham at his death.

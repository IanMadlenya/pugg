        George J. Aspland, a Suffolk County prosecutor who became a State Supreme Court justice, died after a heart attack on Sunday at his retirement home in Delray Beach, Fla. He was 73 years old and also had a home in Babylon, L.I.
        As District Attorney from 1966 to 1973, Mr. Aspland directed the prosecution of land speculators in Islip and on the East End. He also brought cases of bookmaking and usury against organized-crime figures. One, Julius Klein, was convicted in the slaying of a 20-year-old dancer who worked at a discotheque he owned in Sayville.
        In 1973 Mr. Aspland, a Republican, was elected to Supreme Court, where he served until his retirement in 1982.
        Justice Aspland was born in Herkimer, N.Y. He was a graduate of Fordham College and the Fordham Law School and practiced law in Babylon until being elected District Attorney. In 1983, he was elected president of the Suffolk Bar Association.
        Surviving are his wife, Margaret; 5 sons, George and Robert, both of Babylon, Bryan, of Danville, Calif., John, of West Fort Ann, N.Y., and Dennis, of Manhattan; a daughter, Margaret Swanson of Littleton, Colo.; 2 sisters, Helen Day of Boynton Beach, Fla., and Katherine Diggans of Delray Beach; a brother, Michael, of Lake George, N.Y., and 12 grandchildren.

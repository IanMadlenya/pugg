        Henderson G. Riggs, a lawyer and former assistant attorney general for New York State, died of a heart attack Saturday at his home in Albany. He was 64 years old.
        Mr. Riggs began his legal career in Elmira, N.Y., by going into practice with his father, Gaylord Riggs, and was appointed assistant attorney general by Attorney General Louis J. Lefkowitz in 1962. He retired from the post in 1982.
        He was born in Elmira, graduated from Cornell University in 1944 and received a law degree there in 1947.
        Mr. Riggs is survived by his wife, the former Sarah Corey; a son, David, of Tokyo; two daughters, Katherine Van Wie of Houston and Barbara, of Washington; a brother, John, of Tequesta, Fla.; a sister, Ann Teetor of Elmira, and three grandchildren.

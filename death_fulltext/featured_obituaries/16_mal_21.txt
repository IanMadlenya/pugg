        William R. Laidlaw 4th, a writer, died of throat cancer last Friday at Calvary Hospital in the Bronx. He was 80 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        He was a co-author of the screenplay for the 1949 film ''Command Decision,'' which starred Clark Gable, Walter Pidgeon and Van Johnson. He later assisted Gen. Henry (Hap) Arnold of the Air Force with his autobiography, ''Global Mission.''
        Mr. Laidlaw is survived by his wife, Helen Herold Laidlaw of Manhattan, and a sister, Caroline H. Laidlaw of Marathon, Fla.

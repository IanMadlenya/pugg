
        Dr. Walter F. Sloan, a dentist in Ridgewood, N.J., for 40 years, died on Wednesday at Valley Hospital in Ridgewood. He was 66 years old and lived in Ridgewood.
        He died of a ruptured aortic aneurysm, his wife, Ruth, said.
        Dr. Sloan, a graduate of the Tufts University School of Dentistry, served in the Navy in World War II. He was a past president of the New Jersey State Board of Dentistry and a member of the Northeast Regional Board of Dental Examiners in Washington. He was a past president of the Bergen County Dental Society and a former delegate to the American Dental Society.
        In addition to his wife, he is survived by three sons, Dr. Bruce, of Ridgewood, and Philip and the Rev. Richard, both of Glen Rock, N.J.; two daughters, Catherine Sloan of Ridgewood and Marion Stallings of Marshfield, Mass., and six grandchildren.

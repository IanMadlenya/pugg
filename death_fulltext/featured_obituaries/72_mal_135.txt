
        Dr. Samuel S. Cohen, an orthopedic surgeon for more than 50 years, died on Friday at Memorial Sloan-Kettering Cancer Center in Manhattan. He was 79 and lived in Manhattan and Southold, L.I.
        He died of leukemia, his family said.
        Dr. Cohen practiced in Manhattan and for many years was the orthopedist to the New York City Ballet. His patients also included Louis Armstrong, George Balanchine and other celebrities from the arts and sports.
        He was an attending surgeon at Metropolitan Hospital Center, and had been an attending surgeon at Flower Fifth Avenue Hospital and on the orthopedic staff of Beth Israel Medical Center, all in Manhattan. He was also an associate clinical professor at New York Medical College in Valhalla, N.Y.
        Dr. Cohen was born in Brooklyn. He was a graduate of the University of Michigan and New York Medical College. During World War II, he served as a captain in the Army Medical Corps.
        He is survived by his wife of 54 years, the former Edith Sevin; a daughter, Ann Riva of Edgewater, N.J.; a son, Matthew, of Manhattan; a brother, Herbert G., and a sister, Bess Greisman, both of Manhattan, and two grandchildren.

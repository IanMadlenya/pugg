        Richard Kendrick, a retired actor, died at the Bellevue Hospital Center last Tuesday after suffering a stroke. He was in his early 80's and lived in Manhattan.
        A native of South Dakota, Mr. Kendrick began to act in campus production as a student at the University of Michigan. He made his Broadway debut in 1933 when he played the juvenile lead in ''Double Door.'' Mr. Kendrick appeared in comedies and other plays, as well as on television in the Kraft Theater and the Goodyear Television Playhouse.
        There are no immediate survivors.


        Stanley Newhouse, an insurance executive and running advocate, died on Thursday at White Plains Hospital Center. He was 81 years old and lived in Scarsdale, N.Y.
        He died of pneumonia, his family said.
        For each of the last 40 years Mr. Newhouse ranked among the top 10 in sales at the Massachusetts Mutual Life Insurance Company. He was also a co-founder of the Newhouse Denton Corporation in White Plains, which specializes in employee benefits and estate planning.
        He was born in Kansas City, Mo., and graduated from the Wharton School at the University of Pennsylvania. During World War II, he was a commander in naval intelligence.
        Mr. Newhouse was chairman of the Greater New York Y.M.C.A. Fitness Council and organized jogging groups. A founder of the Scarsdale Antiques runners' club, he completed six New York City Marathons while in his 60's.
        He is survived by his wife, Eda; two sons, Douglas, of Weston, Conn., and James, of South Salem, N.Y.; a sister, Virginia Hardie of Kansas City, Mo., and a granddaughter.

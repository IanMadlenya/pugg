
        Lorenzo Alvary, a bass who was heard regularly in character roles at the Metropolitan Opera for more than three decades, died on Dec. 13 at Lenox Hill Hospital in Manhattan. He was 87 and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Alvary was born in Debrecen, Hungary, in 1909 and studied in Milan and Berlin. He made his professional debut in Vienna in 1937 and his American debut at the San Francisco Opera two years later. He became an American citizen in 1944.
        Mr. Alvary's first performance at the Met, in November 1942, was as Zuniga in Bizet's ''Carmen,'' a role he sang 51 times with the company. All told, he sang 983 performances of 53 roles at the Met. He appeared most frequently as Antonio in Mozart's ''Nozze di Figaro,'' which he sang 104 times, and Alcindoro in Puccini's ''Boheme,'' which he performed 93 times.
        He was also heard regularly as Grenvil in ''La Traviata,'' Angelotti and the Sacristan in Puccini's ''Tosca,'' Masetto in ''Don Giovanni,'' Foltz in ''Die Meistersinger'' and Mathieu in ''Andrea Chenier.'' He retired from the Met in 1978.
        Mr. Alvary was also the host of a radio show, ''Opera Topics,'' which was broadcast on WKCR in New York from 1964 to 1991.
        He is survived by his wife, Hallie; two brothers, Dr. George Alvary of McHenry, Ill., and Lewis Alvary of Miami; three grandchildren and five great-grandchildren.

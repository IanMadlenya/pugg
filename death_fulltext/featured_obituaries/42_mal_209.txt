
       Bertram Thorp Blake Jr., a retired paper company executive, died yesterday at his home in Darien, Conn. He was 71 years old. Mr. Blake died of lung cancer, his wife, Margaret, said. Mr. Blake was executive vice president of the Paper Corporation of the United States, based in Manhattan, from 1966 to 1981, when he retired.
        Bertram Thorp Blake Jr., a retired paper company executive, died yesterday at his home in Darien, Conn. He was 71 years old. Mr. Blake died of lung cancer, his wife, Margaret, said. Mr. Blake was executive vice president of the Paper Corporation of the United States, based in Manhattan, from 1966 to 1981, when he retired. Earlier he had been president of the Paper Corporation of New York and an executive with Bermingham & Prosser, a Manhattan paper company.
        Mr. Blake was born in Brooklyn and graduated from Dartmouth College in 1940.
        Besides his wife, he is survived by four daughters, Margaret Mills of New Canaan, Conn., Catherine McLean of New Canaan, Nancy Matheny of Ridgefield, Conn., Elizabeth Goff of Rowayton, Conn.; a son, Bertram 3d, of Darien; a sister, Sally McMannis also of Darien, and 18 grandchildren.

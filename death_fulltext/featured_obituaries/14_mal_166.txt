        Fred Jacobson, a former United States Foreign Service officer and a lyricist, died of a heart attack at Greenwich Hospital today. He was 73 years old and lived in Greenwich, Manhattan and Florence.
        Mr. Jacobson, born in Austria, received his Doctor of Laws degree at the University of Vienna. He left Vienna in 1939 because of the Nazis and came to New York. During World War II he was with the Office of War Information. Following the war he joined the Voice of America, where he headed the German desk. From 1963 until 1973 he was a Foreign Service officer in Munich and Berlin. Mr. Jacobson then became a successful lyricist in West Germany.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Mary Wulman, and a son, Dr. Michael Jacobson of New York.

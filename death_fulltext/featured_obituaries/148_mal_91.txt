
        Lembit Oll, an Estonian chess grandmaster who was preparing to take part in a qualifying competition for the International Chess Federation's world championship match this summer in Las Vegas, Nev., died on Sunday in a plunge from a window of his fourth-floor apartment in Tallinn, the Estonian capital, press reports there said. He was 33.
        Mr. Oll was ranked No. 42 in recent world standings.
        The reports from Tallinn said he was survived by two sons.


        George H. Newi, a former president of the ABC Television Network, died on Tuesday while on vacation in Norway. Mr. Newi, a resident of Bonita Springs, Fla., was 62.
        The cause of death was a heart attack, said his wife, Sally Garnett Newi.
        Mr. Newi joined the ABC sales department in 1963 and remained with the company until he retired as senior executive vice president of affiliate relations in 1995. During that time, he headed the daytime and sports sales divisions and rose to  vice president/general manager of the network in 1978 and president of it in 1985. After Capital Cities bought ABC in 1986, Mr. Newi was appointed head of affiliate relations.
        Mr. Newi served in the Army for three years. Before joining ABC, he worked for two advertising agencies, Compton and McCann-Erickson.
        Besides his wife, he is survived by one daughter, Lisa Long of Pleasantville, N.Y., and two sons, George Jr. of Atlanta and Alec of Chicago.

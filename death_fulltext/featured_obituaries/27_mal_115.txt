        Enzo Gaspari, a former New York State senator and assemblyman and the managing legislative lawyer for Shea & Gould, a Manhattan law firm, died of a stroke Thursday at St. Peter's Hospital in Albany. He was 74 years old and lived in Albany.
        Mr. Gaspari was born in the Bronx. He graduated from St. John's University and its law school. A Democrat, Mr. Gaspari was a state senator for the Bronx in 1950 and an assemblyman in 1954. In 1956, he worked as an assistant counsel in the governor's office.
        In 1958, he was appointed counsel to the New York State Lottery Control Commission. In 1963, he became an assistant legislative representative for New York City's legislative lobbying office in Albany, where he served for 20 years. He joined Shea & Gould in 1983.
        Surviving are his wife, Kathryn, and two sisters, Mabina Gaspari and Jeanette Yablonsky, both of the Bronx.

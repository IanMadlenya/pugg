        Bill Pace, the head football coach at Vanderbilt University from 1966 until 1973, died of a heart attack Monday in Nashville. He was 58 years old.
        Mr. Pace, who was also the school's athletic director for two years, had a 22-38-2 record as coach.
        Born in Pitcher, Okla., Mr. Pace was a quarterback at Wichita State, then served as an assistant coach at Arkansas, Kansas and Arkansas again before going to Vanderbilt. He also coached professionally as an assistant with the New England Patriots of the National Football League in 1973.

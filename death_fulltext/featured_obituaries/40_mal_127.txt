        Dr. Philip Clark, a psychologist and an educational administrator, died of heart failure on Monday at his home in Scarsdale, N.Y. He was 73 years old.
        From 1965 until his retirement in 1978, Dr. Clark was an educational psychologist with Harcourt Brace Jovanovich Inc. He previously held administrative and counseling positions at the City College of New York and Cornell University. He was also with the California Testing Bureau from 1960 to 1965, and from 1946 to 1957 he taught at Teachers College of Connecticut, now known as Central Connecticut State University.
        A graduate of the Rhode Island School of Design, Dr. Clark received a master's degree from Harvard and a Ph.D. from Boston University. After his retirement, Dr. Clark was an active supporter of Alcoholics Anonymous, urging corporate leaders to create support groups for recovering alcoholics within their organizations.
        He is survived by his wife, Frances Palmer Clark; three daughters, Sally Collins of LaCrosse, Wis., Janet Morse and Wendy Wescott, both of Barrington, R.I.; six grandchildren, and two great-grandchildren.

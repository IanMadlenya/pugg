        Alan F. Conwill, a lawyer and former director of the division of corporate regulation of the Securities and Exchange Commission, died of heart failure in his sleep Monday during a trip to San Antonio, Tex. He was 67 years old and lived in Wyckoff, N.J.
        Mr. Conwill joined the law firm of Willkie Farr & Gallagher in Manhattan in 1949 and was named a partner in 1956. In 1961 he was appointed general counsel to the S.E.C. and later director of the division of corporate regulation.
        He returned to Willkie Farr & Gallagher in 1964 and devoted himself to securities and corporate law until his retirement in 1986. He also taught at New York Law School from 1951 to 1956 and at Columbia from 1964 to 1967.
        Mr. Conwill was born in Hutchinson, Kan., and was a graduate of Northwestern University and its law school.
        He served as a lieutenant in the Navy in World War II and was awarded a Bronze Star.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Arolyn Hodgkiss; three sons, Joseph D., of Rangley, Me., Stephen H., of Seattle, and Michael F., of Acton, Mass., and one granddaughter.

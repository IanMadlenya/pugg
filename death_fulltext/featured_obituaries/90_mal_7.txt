
        Dr. Herbert Harris, an obstetrician and gynecologist who practiced in Brooklyn for more than 30 years, died on Tuesday at Long Island College Hospital in Brooklyn. He was 68 and lived in Neponsit, Queens. He died of heart failure,  said a spokeswoman for the hospital, where he was an attending physician in the  Mary Polak Oenslager department of obstetrics and gynecology.
        Dr. Harris had been associated with the hospital since he began his residency there in 1952. At his death he also was a clinical associate professor at the State University Health Science Center in Brooklyn.
        He started the infertility service at Long Island College Hospital in 1957 and was a past chief of infertility and endoscopy. He also directed the hospital teaching program of endoscopic and microsurgical techniques.
        Surviving are his wife, Portia Rinaldi Harris; three sons, Steven, of Orangeburg, N.Y.; Dr. Michael Harris of Cleveland, and Jeffrey, of Maplewood, N.J., and three grandchildren.

        Chadbourne Gilpatric, a retired official of the Rockefeller Foundation, died Wednesday after undergoing surgery at the Eastern Maine Medical Center in Bangor. He was 74 years old and lived in Mount Desert, Me.
        Mr. Gilpatric, a graduate of Harvard University and a Rhodes Scholar at Balliol College, Oxford, joined the Rockefeller Foundation in 1949 and served as associate director of humanities and social sciences. He retired in 1972 while on the field staff of the foundation in India.
        In World War II, he was on the Board of Economic Warfare and was an Army captain with the Office of Strategic Services in London and Germany. From 1947 to 1949, he was with the Central Intelligence Agency in Washington.
        He is survived by a son, David, of Winchester, Mass.; a daughter, Elisabeth Converse of Santa Rosa, Calif.; a brother, Roswell, of Manhattan, and six grandchildren.

        Leo Gottlieb, a lawyer and former president of the New York County Lawyers Association, died yesterday at his home in Manhattan. He was 93 years old.
        Mr. Gottlieb, a 1920 graduate of the Harvard University Law School, was since 1946 a partner in the Manhattan firm of Cleary Gottlieb Steen & Hamilton and its predecessor. He retired in 1974 but maintained ties with the firm. From 1920 to 1946, he was with the firm of Root, Clark, Buckner & Howland.
        He was president of the New York Lawyers Association in the early 1960's and was on many professional and civic boards, including those of Bennington College and the Mount Sinai Medical Center. He was president of the Harvard Law School Association of New York City.
        Mr. Gottlieb is survived by a daughter, Elinor Gottlieb Mannucci, and a brother, Morris, both of Manhattan, and two grandchildren.

        Merrill P. Jackson, an official in the Federal Department of Health and Human Services, died of cancer Tuesday at Columbia Presbyterian Medical Center. He was 57 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        At the time of his death, he was Equal Employment Opportunity officer for the department's regional office that covers New York, New Jersey and Puerto Rico.
        Educated as a sociologist and social worker, Mr. Jackson had a master's degree from Columbia University.
        He is survived by a brother, Bernard, a New York State Supreme Court Justice, who lives in Manhattan.

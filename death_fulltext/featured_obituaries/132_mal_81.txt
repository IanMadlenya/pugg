
        John Paul Monahan 3d, a lawyer and NBC News legal analyst, died yesterday of cancer at Lenox Hill Hospital in Manhattan. The husband of Katie Couric, the NBC ''Today'' host, Mr. Monahan was 42 and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Monahan, who went by the name Jay, had provided legal analysis for NBC News, MSNBC and CNBC since 1995. The cases he covered included the O.J. Simpson criminal and civil trials, the Timothy McVeigh trial and the Unabomber trial.
        Robert Wright, president of NBC, called Mr. Monahan a fine lawyer, a perceptive legal analyst and a clever wit. ''He was very special to those of us who were fortunate enough to call him 'friend and colleague,' '' he said.
        Diagnosed with cancer in early 1997, Mr. Monahan continued to work, covering the Unabomber trial until last month. He underwent surgery for cancer of the colon in June, The Associated Press reported.
        Mr. Monahan was born in Manhasset on Long Island and graduated with honors in 1977 from Washington and Lee University. He graduated with honors from Georgetown Law School in 1985.
        Mr. Monahan worked as a clerk for Judge Thomas Hogan in United States District Court in the District of Columbia. From 1986 to 1993, Mr. Monahan litigated criminal and civil law for Williams & Connolly in Washington.
        In 1989, he married Ms. Couric, then a reporter in Washington. From 1993 to 1994, he worked in the New York office of Hunton & Williams.
        In addition to Ms. Couric, Mr. Monahan is survived by the couple's two daughters, Elinor, 6, and Caroline, 2. He is also survived by his parents, Carol and John P. Monahan Jr. of Rehoboth Beach, Del.; his brothers and sisters, Barbara Monahan, Clare Myers, Chris Monahan, Kathryn Monahan, Henry Monahan and Sally Monahan.

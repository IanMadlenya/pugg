        Sinclair Hatch, a retired partner in the law firm of Milbank, Tweed, Hadley & McCloy, died after a heart attack yesterday at his home in Oyster Bay, L.I. He was 82 years old and also had a home in Setauket, L.I.
        Mr. Hatch, who was regional director of the Office of Civilian Defense in 1943, had previously served with the Federal Trade Commission and the Securities and Exchange Commission. In 1944 and 1945 he was with the Navy in Washington as a lieutenant commander.
        Mr. Hatch, who was born in Chicago and raised in Minneapolis, was a graduate of Princeton and of the Harvard Law School. He began his legal career with Davis, Polk, Wardwell, Gardiner & Reed in 1931.
        Before he joined Milbank, Tweed in 1960, he was a partner in Graustein, Hatch & Kormendi and successor firms.
        Mr. Hatch is survived by his wife, the former Wendy Miller; three sons, Robertson, of Stony Brook, L.I., George, of Manhattan, and Sinclair Jr., of Leucadia, Calif., and eight grandchildren.

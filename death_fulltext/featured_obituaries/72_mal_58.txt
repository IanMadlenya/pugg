
        Charles E. Lord, an investment banker and a former vice chairman of the Export-Import Bank of the United States, died Monday in Bridgeport Hospital in Connecticut. He was 64 and lived in Hobe Sound, Fla.
        Mr. Lord died of pulmonary disease, said his wife, Margaret. He was in Connecticut to visit relatives.
        In 1982 and 1983 Mr. Lord was first vice president, vice chairman and a director of the Export-Import Bank of the United States. From 1979 to 1982 he held several senior posts in the Federal Office of the Comptroller of the Currency. He was Acting Comptroller for most of 1981.
        After leaving Federal office, Mr. Lord was chairman and chief executive of the Prudential Bank and Trust Company, a subsidiary of the Prudential Insurance Company of America, and a senior adviser with Dillon Read & Company. Since 1989 he had been a principal of Lord & Associates and vice chairman of the Madison Financial Group.
        Mr. Lord, a native of New York City, earned a bachelor's degree from Yale University in 1949.
        In addition to his wife, Mr. Lord is survived by four children: Catharine Lord Brown of Evergreen, Colo.; Sarah, of Manhattan; Charles E. Jr., of Flagstaff, Ariz., and William P., of Cincinnati; his mother, Frances G. Lord of East Hampton, N.Y.; a brother, Sheridan N., of Sagaponack, N.Y.; and eight grandchildren.

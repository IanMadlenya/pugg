        John R. DeLuca, vice president of health care business development for the Primerica Corporation, died of bone marrow cancer on July 18 at Memorial-Sloan Kettering Cancer Center. He was 43 years old and lived in Weston, Conn.
        Mr. DeLuca, director of the New York State Division of Alcoholism and Alcohol Abuse under Gov. Hugh L. Carey, joined Primerica, then the American Can Company, in 1984 as managing director, health care business development. From 1979 to 1981, he was director of the National Institute on Alcohol Abuse and Alcoholism in the United States Department of Heath and Human Services. Earlier he had been a vice president at Equitable Life Assurance Society of the United States and director of its medical department.
        He was born in Manhattan and was graduated from the University of Vermont in 1965. He also earned a master's degree from the Woodrow Wilson School of Public and International Affairs, Princeton University, in 1975.
        Mr. DeLuca is survived by his wife, the former Louise Roumagoux; two sons, Shawn and Todd, and a daughter, Kirsten all of Weston.

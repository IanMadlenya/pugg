        Dr. Beverly Chew Smith, a general surgeon and a founder of Doctors Hospital, died of a heart ailment Sunday at his home in Manhattan. He was 92 years old.
        Dr. Chew was born in Franklin, La., and was a graduate of the University of Virginia and its medical school. From 1919 to 1979, he practiced medicine in Manhattan. He was on the surgical staff of Columbia Presbyterian Medical Center and Greenwich (Conn.) Hospital. He also consulted at Englewood (N.J.) Hospital.
        He was a founding member of the American Board of Surgeons, the New York Diabetes Association, the American Diabetes Association and the New York Society of Cardiovascular Surgery.
        Dr. Smith was married to the late Ruth Shepley. He is survived by his sister, Daisy Belle Smith Baldwin of New Orleans.

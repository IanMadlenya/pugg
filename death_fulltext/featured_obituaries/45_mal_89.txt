        Frank E. McCreery, a former chief executive of Rohr Industries, a manufacturer of aircraft components, died Sunday at Alvarado Hospital in San Diego. He was 72 years old and lived in Poway, Calif.
        He died of a heart attack, a company spokesman said, Mr. McCreery joined Rohr in 1940, shortly after graduating from the California Institute of Technology. He began as assistant chief engineer and rose to senior vice president and executive vice president.
        He was president from 1970 to 1974 and vice chairman of the board from 1973 to 1976. He was named a corporate director in 1953 and had been on the board from 1965 to 1976, when he retired.
        He was an officer of the Aerospace Industries Association and of the Society of Automotive Engineers.
        He is survived by his wife, Ruth; three sons, James and Douglas, both of Escondido, Calif., and Michael of San Diego; a daughter, Maureen Nicks of San Diego; a brother, Jack of San Luis Obispo, Calif.; four grandchildren and one great-grandchild.

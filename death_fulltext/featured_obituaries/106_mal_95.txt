
        Dr. Harold Kirshner, an ophthalmologist who was chief of clinic at the New York Eye and Ear Infirmary in Manhattan for some 50 years, died on Thursday at his home in West Hempstead, L.I. He was 82.
        The cause was prostate cancer, his family said.
        Dr. Kirshner was a member of the faculty of the Mount Sinai School of Medicine and the medical school of the State University of New York at Stony Brook. He was an attending surgeon at the Beth Israel Hospital of New York for 25 years.
        Dr. Kirshner was a widely recognized expert in problems of the nasal passages and tear glands and on surgery to alleviate those problems. In his honor, the New York Eye and Ear Infirmary established a special library section dealing with diseases of the tear glands.
        Dr. Kirshner, who was born in New York City, graduated from New York University and attended medical school in Bern, Switzerland.
        He was immediate past president of the World Fellowship of the Israeli Medical Association, an avid Hebraic scholar and a violinist.
        He is survived by his wife of 56 years, Ruth; a son, Bruce, of Bayside, Queens, and a daughter, Susan, of Nanuet, N.Y.

        Dimitri M. Yassukovich, a retired partner in the investment banking concern of White, Weld & Company, died of complications from pneumonia Tuesday at his summer home in Locust Valley, L.I. He was 91 years old and lived in Hobe Sound, Fla.
        Mr. Yassukovich was born in Russia and came to the United States in 1918. Two years later he joined the brokerage firm of Prince & Whitely, which was a casualty of the stock market crash of 1929. The following year he joined White, Weld and became a partner in 1937. He retired in 1963 but remained active in investment banking for many years.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Denise Henry; a daughter, Ariane Wellin of Locust Valley; a son, Stanislaus, of London; seven grandchildren and three great-grandchildren.


        Nathan Skolnick, a former executive of the United Service Organizations, died on Sunday at his home in Miami Beach. He was 71 years old.
        He died of cardiac arrest, said his wife, Theodora Fire Skolnick.
        Born in Poland, Mr. Skolnick came to this country as a child. His family settled in St. Paul. He received graduate degrees from the University of Minnesota and Columbia University.
        From 1951 to 1954, while in the armed services division of the National Jewish Welfare Board, Mr. Skolnick served as executive director of U.S.O. operations in New York City. From 1963 to 1972, he was the executive director of the United Jewish Council of Greater Bridgeport, Conn. He retired in 1982 as director of the planning and budgeting department of the Greater Miami Jewish Federation.
        In addition to his wife, he is survived by two brothers, Irving, of Chicago, and Samuel, of Minneapolis; a son, Mark, of Portland, Ore.; a daughter, Amy Ravish, of Salem, Mass., and two granddaughters.

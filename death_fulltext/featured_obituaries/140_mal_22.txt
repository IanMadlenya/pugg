
        Albert W. Johnson, a former Republican Congressman from Pennsylvania, died last Tuesday at Boca Raton Community Hospital in Florida. He was 92 and lived in Boca Raton.
        The cause was complications from pneumonia, his family said.
        Mr. Johnson, the son of Swedish immigrants, grew up in Smethport, Pa. He attended the Wharton School of Finance of the University of Pennsylvania and Stetson University Law School in St. Petersburg, Fla.
        He was elected to the Pennsylvania House of Representatives in 1946 and served until 1963. He served in Congress from 1963 until 1976.
        Mr. Johnson is survived by three sons, Richmond of Warren, Pa., David of Ocean Isle Beach, N.C., and Ronald of Ormond Beach, Fla.; a daughter, Karen Johnson of New York City and Boca Raton; a brother, Emil of Stow, Ohio; a sister, Linda Norman of Smethport; seven grandchildren, and seven great-grandchildren.


        Jack B. Collins, whose career was spent in public service, died on Thursday at his home in Southbury, Conn. He was 74 years old.
        He died of cancer, said John Tebbel, a neighbor and close friend of the family.
        From 1953 to 1957 Mr. Collins was a senior financial adviser at the Pentagon, specializing in school systems within the Army establishment. He was then appointed executive director of President Dwight D. Eisenhower's Committee on Education Beyond High School.
        Later, as assistant vice president and director of training for the New York Life Insurance Company, he designed its first executivwe development program. In 1961, he was named executive vice president of the Sleepy Hollow Restoration in Tarrytown, N.Y., and, in 1966, became a vice president of the Center for Inter-American Relations in Manhattan.
        During his New York State career, Mr. Collins was associate director of the Public Executive Project for the State University of New York in Albany, and directed part of the state's Social Security Disability Program.
        In New York City, he was a special assistant for legislation in the Health and Hospitals Corporation.
        He is survived by his wife, Beatrice, and a brother, Leo, of Irvine, Calif.

        Luis Federico Leloir, the Argentine who won the Nobel Prize in Chemistry in 1970, died Wednesday night, a family spokesman said. He was 81 years old.
        Dr. Leloir received the award for his discovery of the nucleotides-sugars and their role in the biosynthesis of carbohydrates.
        Manuel Sadosky, Argentina's Secretary of Science and Technology, said Dr. Leloir had played an important role in forming a large school of dedicated scientists.


        Leo Katcher, a reporter, screenwriter, novelist and biographer, died Wednesday at the Tri-City Medical Center in Oceanside, Calif. He was 79 years old and lived in Oceanside.
        He died of a heart attack, his brother, Edward, said.
        Mr. Katcher's books included "The Big Bankroll: The Life and Times of Arnold Rothstein," the biography of a gambler, which was made into a movie, "King of the Roaring Twenties" (1961). He also wrote "Earl Warren: A Political Biography" and "Post Mortem," about Jews living in postwar Germany.
        His novels included "The Hard Man" and "The Blind Cave" and his screenplays included "The Eddy Duchin Story" with Tyrone Power in the title role (1956).
        Mr. Katcher was the city editor of The New York Post in the 1930's and later a West Coast correspondent for the paper.
        He was born in Bayonne, N.J., and attended the University of Pennsylvania and New York University.
        Surviving in addition to his brother are two sisters, Ruth Rosenberg and Gladys Bletter, all of New York City.


        Everett C. Lattimore, a former Mayor and Superintendent of Schools in Plainfield, N.J., died on Tuesday at the Muhlenberg Regional Medical Center in Plainfield. He was 63 years old and lived in Plainfield.
        Mr. Lattimore died of complications after suffering a heart attack last Thursday, a son said.
        Mr. Lattimore, who was elected mayor in 1981, was the first black to be elected to that post and the first Democratic mayor in 68 years. He resigned in 1983 to become the Superintendent of Schools.
        He was a member of the Plainfield City Council from 1961 to 1971.
        Mr. Lattimore was born in Winston Salem, N.C., and graduated from Shaw University in Raleigh, N.C., in 1949.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Rosetta Norwood; two daughters, Karen Goffin of Columbia, Md., and Dawn Edwards of Plainfield; five sons, Keith, of Plainfield, Derek, of Houston, Karl, of San Diego, Kraig, of Adelphi, Md., and Kirk, of Louisville, Ky.; three brothers; three sisters, and 11 grandchildren.

        Worth Blanding Fowler, a former chairman of the American President Lines, died of heart failure Sunday at his home in Sonoma, Calif. He was 78 years old.
        Mr. Fowler graduated from Princeton University in 1933 and joined the States Steamship Company of Portland, Ore. He left in 1940 to join the American Mail Line.
        In World War II he was a naval officer and served with the Alaska-War Shipping Administration and the United States Maritime Commission. He returned to American Mail in 1947 and became its president in 1959.
        In 1968, he was also named president of American President Lines, a West Coast shipping concern that is the parent company to American Mail. In 1973 Mr Fowler became chairman of the company and retired shortly thereafter. He was a director of the National Conference of Christians and Jews and an official in many shipping organizations.
        There were no survivors.

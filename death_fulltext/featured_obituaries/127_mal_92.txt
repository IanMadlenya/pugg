
        A. Gerdes Kuhbach, a corporate lawyer who had a long career with the New Haven Railroad followed by three years as executive director of the Port Authority of New York and New Jersey, died on Tuesday at Overlook Hospital in Summit, N.J. He was 80 and lived in Chatham, N.J.
        His family said he died of heart failure after the hospitalization of his wife of 55 years, Muriel Dinger Kuhbach. Mrs. Kuhbach, 78, died on Wednesday, also of heart failure.
        Mr. Kuhbach joined the New Haven Railroad, now operated by the Metropolitan Transportation Authority, in 1941 as an assistant counsel. He became chief financial officer and finally executive vice president.
        In 1962 he moved to the Port Authority. As director of finance, he oversaw outlays for major projects like the World Trade Center and airport expansions.
        He was acting director for a year and served as executive director from 1974 to 1977. He was a senior financial adviser until 1982.
        Mr. Kuhbach was born in the Bronx and graduated from Columbia College in 1938 and from Columbia University Law School in 1940.
        The Kuhbachs are survived by a daughter, Ellen K. Lucas of Summit; two sons, Robert G. of Manhattan and Peter D. of Alexandria, Va., and five grandchildren. Mr. Kuhbach is also survived by a brother, Charles M. of Poughkeepsie, N.Y.

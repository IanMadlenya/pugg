
        Charles J. Muss, a real-estate developer, died on Thursday at New York Hospital. He was 89 years old and lived in Flushing, Queens.
        He died of a stroke, said his daughter, Adele Feinerman of Roslyn Heights, L.I.
        Mr. Muss was a partner, with his brother, Hyman, in the Central Management Corporation in Jackson Heights, Queens. Founded by his father, Isaac, in 1907, the company built residential and commercial projects.
        He was active in philanthropic affairs and was a founder of the Albert Einstein Medical Center and Yeshiva University, and a board member of Long Island Jewish Hospital.
        Besides his daughter and brother, Mr. Muss is survived by three sisters: Lilian November of Boynton Beach, Fla.; Bess Glaster of Delray Beach, Fla.; and Ida Bobrow of Queens; six grandchildren and three great-grandchildren.

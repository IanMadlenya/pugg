        The Rev. Dr. John Oliver Patterson, an Episcopal priest and educator, died Saturday at Children's Hospital in San Francisco, after a short illness. He was 80 years old and lived in San Francisco.
        Dr. Patterson was headmaster of the Kent School in Connecticut from 1949 to 1963. During his tenure the school opened a campus for girls in 1960.
        In 1962 he moved to Rome to found St. Stephen's School, a coeducational preparatory school with students from numerous countries, along with two of his former colleagues from Kent. From 1966 to 1968 he was director of the European Council of International Schools. He retired in 1970.
        Dr. Patterson studied at the University of Chicago, the University of Illinois and at the school of architecture of the Massachusetts Institute of Technology. After two years in architecture, he entered Seabury Western Seminary in Evanston, Ill., and was ordained in 1934. In 1979 he returned to the United States, where he served as associate at St. James Parish in San Francisco.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Elizabeth Andrews; a brother, Oliver Macrae of Chicago; four children, Mary Elizabeth, of Minneapolis, John Michael, of Cincinnati, Stephen Martin, of Wilmington, Mass., and Anne Shepard Harmon of Walnut Creek, Calif.; nine grandchildren and two great-grandchildren.

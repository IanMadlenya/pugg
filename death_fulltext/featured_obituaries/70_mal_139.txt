
        W. David Streeter, an expert in hospital financial management, died on Monday at his home in Cuddebackville, N.Y. He was 75 years old.
        He died of cancer and emphysema, said his wife, Harriette.
        A native of Harrisburg, Pa., Mr. Streeter attended the City College of New York. He worked for Women's Hospital, St. Luke's Medical Center, Jewish Hospital of Brooklyn, Maimonides Medical Center and Jamaica Hospital.
        He was a founding member and past president of the New York City Metropolitan Chapter of the Hospital Financial Management Association. In 1979 he was honored by the National Hospital Financial Management Association for his service to the profession.
        He was also a founder of the United Hospital Fund Statistical Report, the forerunner of the report used today by the Federal Medicare program for all hospitals.
        He retired from his post at Jamaica Hospital in 1981.
        Besides his wife, he is survived by a daughter, Imelda, of Los Angeles; two granddaughters, a grandson, and a great-granddaughter.

        Edward Lender Woodyard, a retired rear admiral in the Navy, died of congestive heart failure on Sunday at his home in Hamden, Conn. He was 86 years old.
        Admiral Woodyard retired from the Navy in 1954 after 30 years of service and later became a special assistant to the president of AVCO/Lycoming, a Connecticut-based military contractor now known as Textron. He retired in 1967. A 1924 graduate of Annapolis, he was named captain in 1944 and during the Korean War commanded the Rochester, the flagship of the Seventh Fleet.
        He is survived by two sons, Edward, of Armonk, N.Y., and John, of Las Vegas, Nev.; a sister, Helen Townsend of Coronado, Calif.; and two grandchildren.

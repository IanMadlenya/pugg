
        Walter T. Shirley Jr., who helped his father develop the Long Island community that bears his family name, died on Tuesday at his home in Manhattan. He was 60 years old.
        Mr. Shirley died of a heart attack, a family spokesman said.
        During the suburban boom that followed World War II, the family's company, Shirley Long Island Inc., developed the middle-class community of Shirley on the South Shore of Brookhaven Town in Suffolk County. The company built 4,000 homes, many of them from prefabricated sections.
        Mr. Shirley's father served as New York City's Commerce Commissioner in the early 1950'sunder Mayor Vincent R. Impellitteri. When his father died in 1963, Mr. Shirley succeeded him as president of the family real-estate company.
        Mr. Shirley was born in Manhattan and attended New York University.
        He was an avid book collector and a benefactor of the libraries at College of William and Mary and the University of Maryland and other charities.
        His marriages to Barbara Joan Schmid, Juliette Verwey and Annabelle Teves ended in divorce.
        He is survived by his mother, Rose Shirley Guy of Williamsburg, Va.


       Samuel Perlman, a businessman and philanthropist, died of cardiac arrest on Tuesday at his home in Manhattan. He was 82 years old.         Samuel Perlman, a businessman and philanthropist, died of cardiac arrest on Tuesday at his home in Manhattan. He was 82 years old. Mr. Perlman, a Brooklyn native, was a graduate of St. John's University and the New York Law School. He served in the Army in World War II and with the American military government in Germany after the war.
        He was chief executive officer of L. M. Rabinowitz & Company, manufacturers of hook-and-eye fasteners, before his retirement in 1973. He was active in causes supporting the state of Israel.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Lucille Rabinowitz; two sons, Bill, of Manhattan, and Lee, of Portland, Ore.; a brother, Martin, of Los Angeles; two sisters, Ceil Lipman of Miami Beach, and Rose, of Manhattan, and a granddaughter.

        James H. Walker, a former vice president of the Bethlehem Steel Corporation, died of cancer Dec. 31 in Hobe Sound, Fla. He was 80 years old and lived in Hawley, Pa.
        Mr. Walker, who was born in Salina, Kan., graduated from the University of Kansas and earned a master's degree at the Harvard University Graduate School of Business Administration. He joined Bethlehem Steel in 1932 and rose to become its vice president of finance. He retired in 1972. He served on the boards of the Equitable Life Assurance Company, Transatlantic Corporation, Smith Barney Equity Fund, and Elfun Trust. He was a trustee of Lehigh University.
        He was a member of the University Club of New York.
        Mr. Walker is survived by his wife, Louise; three sons, Graham, of Stroudsburg, Pa., James, of Sacramento, Calif., and Douglas, of Malvern, Pa., and five grandchildren.

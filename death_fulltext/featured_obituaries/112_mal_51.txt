
        Richard J. Scheuer Jr., a general partner in a Manhattan arbitrage firm who supported education and the arts as a trustee of his family's charitable foundation, died on May 5 at Columbia-Presbyterian Medical Center. He was 48 and lived in Larchmont, N.Y.
        The cause was a brain hemorrhage, his family said.
        At his death, he was a partner in R. J. Scheuer & Company, managing real estate and other investments.
        He was also a trustee of the Scheuer Family Foundation, which aids Jewish causes, higher education and cultural programs. Its beneficiaries have included  the Jewish Museum, UJA-Federation of New York, Lincoln Center for the Performing
        Arts and the United States Holocaust Memorial Council in Washington.
        He was a trustee of the Hoff-Barthelson Music School in Scarsdale, N.Y.
        Mr. Scheuer graduated from Harvard College in 1969 and Columbia University Law School in 1972.
        He is survived by his wife, Sylvia Bezem Scheuer; three sons, Benjamin, Adam  and Simon; his parents, Joan and Richard J. Scheuer of Larchmont; a sister, Marian S. Sofaer of Palo Alto, Calif., and two brothers, Daniel and Jonathan, both of New York City.

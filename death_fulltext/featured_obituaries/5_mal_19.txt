        Everett J. Penny, a former vice chancellor of the New York State Board of Regents, died of respiratory failure Tuesday at the King Street Nursing Home in Port Chester, N.Y. He was 83 years old and lived in Scarsdale, N.Y.
        Mr. Penny, a certified public accountant, was elected to the Board of Regents in 1957 and named vice chancellor in 1967. He retired from the board in 1974.
        He was a trustee of Pace University and a former partner in the accounting firm of Ernst & Whinney.
        He is survived by his wife, Helen; three sons, Dr. John, of Franklin Lakes, N.J., Dr. William, of Scarsdale, and Ralph, of Eastchester, N.Y.; a daughter, Bonnie Verses of Stamford, Conn.; two brothers, Wallace, of Miami and Dr. Kenneth, of Syracuse, and eight grandchildren.

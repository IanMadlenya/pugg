        Theodore Henry Marshall, a retired chemical engineer and retired brigadier general in the Army Reserve, died of heart failure Tuesday at his home in Island Park, L.I. He was 86 years old.
        Mr. Marshall, a 1936 graduate of Iowa State University, joined the chemical engineering department at the University of Kansas in 1935 and was head of the department from 1939 to 1941. He was with the Army Chemical Corps from 1941 to 1945. He was with Lederle Laboratories from 1945 to 1957, when he retired as chief engineer for overseas operations. He was later a consultant for the Agency for International Development in India.
        He is survived by his wife, Edith; two sons, Charles, of White Hall, Md., and Theodore, of Highland, Md.; two daughters, Jacqueline Anson, of Island Park, and Judith Rogers, of Red Bank, N.J.; eight grandchildren, and six great-grandchildren.

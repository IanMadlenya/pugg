        Daniel Peterkin Jr., former chairman of Morton-Norwich Products Inc., now Morton Thiokol Inc., died from complications of heart disease Monday at Northwestern Memorial Hospital in Chicago. He was 82 years old and lived in Chicago.
        A graduate of Princeton University, Mr. Peterkin joined the Morton Salt Company in 1928 and became its president in 1941. In 1965, he founded Morton International Inc., which became Morton-Norwich, makers of Pepto-Bismol and Morton Salt. He retired as chairman in 1972.
        Surviving are his wife, Dorothy; three daughters, Joan Foxwell, of Lincolnville, Me., Ann Snyder, of Villanova, Pa., and Joy Rasin, of Hinsdale, Ill.; a sister, Jeanette Hunt, of Chicago, 11 grandchildren, and two great-grandchildren.


        Kenneth T. Downs, a war correspondent who became a public relations executive, died on Feb. 19 in Champrobert-Larochemillay, a village in central France where he lived in retirement. He was 81 years old.
        He died of Alzheimer's disease, his family said.
        Mr. Downs was Paris bureau chief of the International News Service in 1939 when World War II began. After Paris fell in 1940, he reported from Lisbon, making trips into unoccupied France. He served in the United States Army from 1942 to 1946, rising to lieutenant colonel and winning the Silver Star and the Croix de Guerre.
        After the war Mr. Downs served with the Americn military government in Germany until 1951, when he joined Time Inc. From 1958 to 1961 he was assistant director of the Office of Civil Defense and Mobilization in Washington.
        In 1961 he became a vice president of the public relations firm of Selvage & Lee and in 1964 he opened his own firm, Downs & Roosevelt, of which he was president.
        Mr. Downs is survived by his wife, the former Marie Therese Laudet, and two sons, Terry and Lawrence, both of Champrobert-Larochemillay.

        Eleanor Upton Brown-Shev, medical director for disability at A.T.&T. Communications Systems, died of a heart attack Sept. 16 while on a tour in Vienna. She was 64 years old and had residences in Chatham, Mass., and Sausalito, Calif.
        Dr. Brown, a native of Dedham, Mass., joined the American Telephone and Telegraph Company as Western regional medical director in 1973. In 1987 she became medical director for disability. Earlier she had been an associate medical director of the American Tel and Tel Communications Company and the Pacific Telephone and Telegraph Company.
        She graduated from Wellesley College and received her medical degree from Columbia Presbyterian's College of Physicians and Surgeons.
        Dr. Brown leaves her husband, Dr. Edward E. Shev, a neurologist; a daughter, Ann Elizabeth Shev-Dearborn of Tilton, N.H.; two stepchildren, Randall Shev of San Jose, Calif., and Karen Dunn of Ithaca, N.Y., and two sisters, Kay Cunningham of Phoenix and Barbara Holekamp of St. Louis.

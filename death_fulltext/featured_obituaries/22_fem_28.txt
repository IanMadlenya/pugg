        Mary D. Costa, the former deputy general manager of the New York City Housing Authority, died of a heart attack yesterday at New York Hospital in Manhattan. She was 69 years old and lived in Brooklyn.
        Mrs. Costa's career at the Housing Authority began 48 years ago, when she was hired as a typist. She became the second-ranking executive in the agency, the nation's largest housing authority with a staff of 15,000. She retired in April.
        She is survived by a son, George, of Honolulu; a brother, Vincent Demm of Gilboa, N.Y., and two sisters, Therese Sbano and Margaret Meade, both of Brooklyn.

        Lon Tuck, a music critic for The Washington Post, was found dead at his home here Thursday. He was 49 years old and had been suffering from epilepsy and a heart ailment.
        Mr. Tuck was born in Sherman, Tex., and graduated from Princeton University in 1959. He joined The Post in 1961 as a copy boy, became a reporter in 1962 and was one of the first editors of the paper's Style section. He also worked as an editorial-page writer and assistant editor before becoming a music critic.
        He is survived by his mother, Grace Tuck, of Sherman.

        Harold S. Anderson, who worked for Thomas A. Edison and later managed the Edison Museum, died of heart failure Oct. 5 at the Muhlenberg Regional Medical Center in Plainfield, N.J. He was 92 years old and lived in Westfield.
        Mr. Anderson was born in Thompsonville, Mich., and graduated cum laude from Harvard University in 1917.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Dorothy Holland; three sons, Henry, of Westfield, Richard, of Vashon, Wash., and Steven, of Putney, Vt.; 16 grandchildren, and 14 great-grandchildren.
        Correction:  October 26, 1987, Monday, Late City Final Edition

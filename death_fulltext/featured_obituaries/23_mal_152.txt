        John Newton Thurber, a labor economist, died of cardio-respiratory arrest December 18 at Providence Hospital in Washington. He was 81 years old and lived in Washington.
        Dr. Thurber had been associated with the Department of Labor since the 1950's. When he retired in 1977, he was with the Department's International Manpower Institute.
        Dr. Thurber was assistant research director of the United Rubber Workers International Union, in Akron, Ohio, from 1954 to 1955. From 1962 to 1973, he was president of the American Federation of Government Employees, Local 12.
        He developed a model collective bargaining agreement that later became the basis for an executive order on Federal collective bargaining issued by President Kennedy in 1963. He was a member of the Greater Washington Central Labor Council A.F.L.-C.I.O..
        In 1951 to 1954, Dr. Thurber was an economist with the Deparment of Labor's Bureau of Labor Statistics.
        From 1946 to 1951, he was an assistant professor of industrial relations at Cornell University, where he was co-author of ''Out of Work,'' a guide to unemployment in New York State. Earlier he had taught at Syracuse University.
        Dr. Thurber was born in Sherburne, N.Y., graduated from Iowa State University in 1928, and earned a master's degree from the University of Iowa in 1929 and a Ph.D from the University of California at Berkeley in 1943.
        A widower, he is survived by a brother, the Rev. L. Newton Thurber of Upper Montclair, N.J.; a son, John Rogers Thurber, and three daughters, Pamela Jo, Michele Lee and Sandra Newton, all of Washington, D.C.; and two grandchildren.

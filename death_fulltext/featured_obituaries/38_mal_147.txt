        Harry T. Morris, a magazine executive, died on Friday at St. Joseph's Medical Center in Stamford, Conn., after a long illness. He was 75 years old and lived in Darien, Conn.
        Mr. Morris, a native of Salisbury, N.C., had been publisher of Signature magazine and was an advertising director for The Saturday Review and Scientific American magazines. He also worked for Cosmopolitan and Look magazines in advertising sales and marketing. He retired in 1982.
        He was a graduate of Davidson College, with an M.B.A. from Harvard University.
        Mr. Morris is survived by his wife, the former Anne Marie Walsh; three sons, Richard, of Atlanta, Thomas, of New Orleans, and Robert, of Stamford; two daughters, Nancy, of New Haven, and Melinda Jennings of Gardiner, Me.; a brother, Mac G., of Princeton, N.J., and four grandchildren.

        Dr. Shepard B. Clough, who retired after 42 years as a history professor at Columbia University, died on Thursday at Northeast Vermont Hospital in St. Johnsbury. He was 88 years old and lived in East Peacham, Vt. His son Peter said Dr. Clough (pronounced cluff) had died after a stroke.
        Dr. Clough, a specialist in European economic history, wrote ''The Rise and Fall of Civilization,'' (1951), ''Basic Values in Western Civilization'' (1960), ''The Economic History of Modern Italy'' (1964) and ''The Economic Basis of American Civilization'' (1968).
        He was born in Bloomington, Ind. After earning a bachelor's at Colgate University, he did postgraduate work at the Sorbonne and Heidelberg. He received a doctorate from Columbia, where he began teaching in 1928. He retired in 1970.
        In World War II he was in the economic studies division of the State Department, lectured at the School of Military Government in Charlottesville, Va., and worked in the Office of Foreign Relief and Rehabilitation. After the war he became a knight of the Order of Merit of the Italian Republic and a chevalier of the French Legion of Honor.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Marion McGill; two sons, Peter, of Pound Ridge, N.Y., and Shepard, of Lexington, Mass; two sisters, Dorothy Howard of Lebanon, N.H., and Barbara of Wellesley, Mass.; a brother, Reginald, of Old Saybrook, Conn.; eight grandchildren, and three great-grandchildren.

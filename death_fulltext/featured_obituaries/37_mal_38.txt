        Abraham Schnapf, a mechanical engineer who was project manager for the Tiros and other weather satellites, died of renal failure Friday at Care Inn of Voorhees in New Jersey. He was 68 years old and lived in Willingboro, N.J.
        Mr. Schnapf joined the Radio Corporation of America, now RCA, in 1950, after several years at the Goodyear Aircraft Corporation. At RCA he was project manager for the Tiros system and subsequent generations of weather satellites. He also worked on communications satellites. He retired in 1982.
        A graduate of City College of New York and Drexel University, he received several awards, including a public service award from the National Aeronautics and Space Administration for developing the first operational satellite system for meteorology.
        He is survived by his wife, Edna; two sons, Donald of Willow Street, Pa., and Bruce of San Antonio; two brothers, Nathan of Levittown, L.I., and Larry of Matawan, N.J.; three sisters, Margie Koenig of the Bronx, Shirley Eichler and Toby Goldstein, both of Roosevelt. N.J.; and seven grandchildren.


        Dr. Benjamin Post, a retired professor of physics and chemistry at New York Polytechnic University in Brooklyn, died on Wednesday at his home in Brookline, Mass. He was 82.
        The cause was cancer, said his wife, Janet.
        Dr. Post was an authority on X-ray diffraction and its use in analyzing the structures of crystals. He was the author of more than 100 scientific papers and was an officer of the American Crystallographic Association and the National Academy of Science and was a member of the National Research Council.
        Dr. Post graduated from City College of New York and received his doctorate from Polytechnic University. He retired in 1982.
        In addition to his wife, he is survived by two daughters, Laura Eisen of Bethesda, Md., and Diana Churchill of Brookline; a son, David, of Washington, and a sister, Rena Inger of Boca Raton, Fla.

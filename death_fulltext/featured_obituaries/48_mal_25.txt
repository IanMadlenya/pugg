
        Dr. Leon Russell McKinney, a psychiatrist, died Dec. 21 at St. Luke's-Roosevelt Hospital Center in Manhattan. He was 66 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        He died of liver cancer, a family spokesman said.
        Dr. McKinney was a 1947 graduate of Howard University Medical School. He was an intern at Harlem Hospital, did his psychiatric residency at Kings County Hospital and trained at the State University of New York Downstate Medical Center, where he later taught. He also taught at the Mount Sinai School of Medicine.
        In 1971, he joined the staff of Harlem Interfaith Counseling Services as clinical director, and was also a staff psychiatrist with the Brooklyn Bureau of Community Services. He returned to private practice several years ago.
        He is survived by a sister, Jean Phillips of Manhattan.

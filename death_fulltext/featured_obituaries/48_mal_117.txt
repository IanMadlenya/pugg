
        Ralph Delahaye Paine Jr., a former publisher of Fortune magazine and other Time Inc. periodicals, died on Saturday at Tisch Hospital in Manhattan. He was 84 years old and lived in Barton, Vt.
        He died of heart failure, his family said.
        Mr. Paine, who was known as Del, was editor and managing editor of Fortune from 1941 to 1953 and publisher from 1953 to 1967. During that time the magazine created its Fortune 500 list of the nation's largest businesses. He also served as publisher of Architectural Forum from 1954 to 1963 and of House and Home in 1962 and 1963.
        He was born in Newark and graduated from Yale. He was a securities analyst for Edward B. Smith & Company on Wall Street before joining Time as a business editor in 1933.
        In 1938 he became a personal assistant to Henry R. Luce, Time's founder, and in World War II he was managing director of "The March of Time," the newsreel series, in London and in charge of European operations for Time publications before becoming a war correspondent in the Pacific.
        At his death Mr. Paine was president and treasurer of Barton Mountain Corporation, a real-estate and landholding company in Vermont.
        Mr. Paine's companion was Sheila White of Manhattan.
        He is survived by a daughter, Katharine, of Durham, N.H.

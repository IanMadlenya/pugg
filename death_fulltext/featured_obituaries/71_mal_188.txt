
        M. Roy Fisher, an art historian and a dealer in Old Masters, died on Dec. 22 at the New York University Medical Center. He was 61 and lived in Manhattan.
        He died of complications of leukemia, said his brother Jules.
        Mr. Fisher was director of research at Wildenstein & Company, the New York art dealers, from 1964 to 1981. Before that he was research editor for the Frick Collection.
        After he left Wildenstein, Mr. Fisher joined the European paintings department of the J. Paul Getty Museum in Malibu, Calif., in 1984. He remained in New York while he worked for the museum.
        In 1990, he opened his own gallery, M. Roy Fisher Fine Arts, in SoHo, specializing in Old Masters.
        He was a native of Norristown, Pa., and graduated from Temple University. He received a master's degree and a doctorate at Harvard.
        His marriage to Martha Beck ended in divorce.
        Besides his brother Jules, he is survived by his companion, Elizabeth F. Hamilton, and her daughter, Cary, both of Manhattan, and another brother, David, of Baltimore.

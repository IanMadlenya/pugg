
        Paul R. Miller Jr., a vice president of the Council for Aid to Education and a former academic and corporate public-relations official, died on Sunday at his home in Amagansett, L.I. He was 65 years old.
        He died of complications from leukemia, his family said.
        Mr. Miller joined the council's predecessor, the Council for Corporate Support of Private Universities, in 1979. He had previously been vice president for public affairs and development at Simmons College in Boston. From 1967 to 1970 he was director of public relations for the Celanese Corporation.
        He was with the McGraw-Hill Publishing Company from 1949 to 1967, serving at various times as an assistant managing editor of Business Week, chief editor of International Management magazine and director of public affairs and communications.
        Mr. Miller, who was born in Glen Ridge, N.J., graduated from Princeton. He served in the Navy in World War II.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Susan Alling; three sons, Frederic, of Port Washington, L.I., Daniel, of Manhattan, and Paul 3d, of Baltimore; a daughter, Darcy Donaldson of Manhattan; a sister, Polly Spanding of Ridgefield, Conn., and two grandchildren.

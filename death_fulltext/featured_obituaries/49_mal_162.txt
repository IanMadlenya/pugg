
        Hendricks Hallett Whitman, president of Clark Tyler Associates, financial advisors, died on Feb. 14 at his home in Oyster Bay, L.I. He was 70 years old.
        He died of a heart attack, his daughter Meg said.
        Mr. Whitman founded the firm, based in Oyster Bay, in 1977, after working for Meinhard Commercial and John P. Maquire Inc., businesses that made commercial loans.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Margaret Goodhue; a son, Dr. Hendricks Hallett Whitman 3d, of New Vernon, N.J., an internist; two daughters, Anne, of Cambridge, Mass., the founder of Bright Horizons, a chain of day care centers, and Meg, of Brentwood, Calif., vice president of strategic planning and development for the Walt Disney Company, and five grandchildren.

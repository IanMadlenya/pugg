
        Charles F. McElwain, an executive who retired in 1965 after 42 years with the International Business Machines Corporation, died on Wednesday at his home in Fort Lauderdale, Fla. He was 87 years old and had lived in Bronxville, N.Y., for many years.
        Mr. McElwain, a native of Duquesne, Pa., went to work for I.B.M. in Pittsburgh in 1923. During World War II he was in charge of defense manufacturing at I.B.M.'s plant in Endicott, N.Y., and in 1955 he became general manager of the company's military products division. From 1958 to 1965 he directed all overseas manufacturing for I.B.M. World Trade.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Margaret Crimmins; a sister, Dorothy Allen of Pittsburgh, and a brother, A. Leroy McElwain of Cleveland.

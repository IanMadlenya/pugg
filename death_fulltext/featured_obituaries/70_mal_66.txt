
        Francis R. Curry, a retired lawyer, business executive and lay leader in the Roman Catholic Church, died on Sunday at Lenox Hill Hospital in Manhattan. He was 81 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        He died of complications from bone fractures, his family said.
        For several years Mr. Curry was the chairman and chief executive of Treadwell, an industrial design and contracting company. He was also a lawyer in the firms of Stuart & Scherer and Reilly; Curry & Gibbons, and a counsel to Sherry & O'Neill. He retired several years ago.
        He was active as a lay leader and capital fund-raiser in the Saint Francis de Sales and Saint Ignatius Loyola parishes in Manhattan. He headed the committee that created Loyola's first parish council and was also president of Saint Joseph's Day Nursery in Manhattan. In his retirement, he was a volunteer tutor in the city schools.
        Mr. Curry, who was born in Manhattan, was a son of John F. Curry, the former Democratic leader of Tammany Hall. After graduating from Holy Cross College, Mr. Curry earned his law degree at Harvard.
        His wife of 54 years, the former Eileen McCarty, died this year.
        Survivors include two brothers, Robert, of Manhattan and Bernard, of Palm Beach, Fla.; a son, Frank, of Nashville; two daughters, Ann Marcato of Rye, N.Y., and Barbara Curry Walsh of Manhattan, and two grandchildren.

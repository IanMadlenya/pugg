        Alexander T. Colt, an advertising and publishing executive for more than 35 years, died of leukemia Friday at Queen of the Valley Hospital in Napa, Calif. He was 75 years old and lived in Napa.
        Mr. Colt, a graduate of Princeton University, served in the field artillery in the South Pacific in World War II and rose to the rank of lieutenant colonel.
        After the war he joined Time Inc. in Cleveland, moving in 1954 to New York, where he was on the original staff of Sports Illustrated. He later became managing director of Newsweek's European edition and headed the London office of the International Herald Tribune before retiring in the 1980's to Napa.
        Mr. Colt is survived by his wife, the former Toby Pearcey; two daughters, Jennifer Johnson of Indianapolis and Susan Roberts of London; a son, Oliver, of San Francisco, and three grandchildren.

        Edward Owen Curran, former secretary and general counsel of the Nestle Foods Company, died after a long illness at a nursing home in Waterville, Me. He was 87 years old and lived in Waterville.
        Mr. Curran began his career with the law firm of Cravath, Swaine & Moore in Manhattan. In 1940 he was named secretary and general counsel of the American subsidiary of Nestle, a Swiss company with large operations in the United States. He held the posts at the subsidiary's headquarters in Manhattan and White Plains until he retired in 1968.
        He was born in Lathrop, Mich., and graduated from the University of Michigan in 1930. He graduated from its law school in 1932. He was elected to Phi Beta Kappa, and was editor of the Michigan Law Review.
        Mr. Curran had belonged to several organizations including the board of governors of the White Plains Hospital Association, and the Washington Irving Council of the Boy Scouts of America in White Plains.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Laura Meyer, and a daughter, Eileen, of Waterville.

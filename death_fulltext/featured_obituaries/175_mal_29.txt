
        Dr. Josef H. Weissberg, a psychoanalyst and a former president of the American Academy of Psychoanalysis, died on Aug. 6 at Columbia-Presbyterian Center in New York. He was 72 and lived in Manhattan.
        The cause was cancer, his family said.
        Dr. Weissberg had a private practice in New York for more than 40 years. After attending Union College, he received his M.D. at Albany Medical College and completed his psychiatry training at Columbia Presbyterian.
        Dr. Weissberg's first wife, Ann, died in 1997. He is survived by his wife, Carol Wheeler; his mother, Anna Weissberg; and a brother, Norbert Weissberg, all of Manhattan; a son, Ted, of Brooklyn; two daughters, Dr. Laura Weissberg of Newton, Mass., and Claire Weissberg of Brooklyn; a stepson, Nicholas Wheeler of Tokyo; and six grandchildren.


        Dr. David Rauch, a lifelong New York educator and specialist in adult education, died on May 9 at White Plains Hospital Center. A former resident of Fresh Meadows, N.Y., he was 79 and had been living in a nursing home in Rye Brook, N.Y.
        He suffered a long illness, according to Ronna Telsey of the Great Neck Public Schools District on Long Island, where he had taught for 32 years.
        He was a member of a Federal task force that evaluated adult education programs statewide and led national seminars for the United States Bureau of Business and Technology.
        In 1976, he was program chairman of the Bicentennial Congress on Adult Education.
        Dr. Rauch, who retired in 1984, also taught at Teachers College of Columbia University, New York University and Hofstra University.
        He graduated in 1940 from the University of Minnesota with degrees in psychology and journalism and received a master's degree in social work and psychology from the University of Minnesota in 1942. In 1955, he received a doctorate in adult education from Teachers College.
        He is survived by a brother, Stephen, of Berkeley, Calif.


        William R. Reid Jr., a former chief executive of the Torrington Company, a Connecticut manufacturer, died on Sunday at his home in Litchfield, Conn. He was 74 years old.
        Mr. Reid, who also lived in Key Largo, Fla., died of cancer, said Bradford Rowe, director of the Rowe Funeral Home, in Litchfield.
        A native of Springfield, Mass., Mr. Reid graduated from Dartmouth College in 1940 and began working for the Torrington Company, a manufacturer of ball bearings in Torrington, Conn. He spent his entire professional career there.
        He served in the Navy during World War II and was discharged in 1946 with the rank of lieutenant. He returned to the Torrington Company and worked in a variety of middle-level and senior executive posts, becoming a vice president in 1958, a director of the company in 1959, and executive vice president in 1964. He was appointed president in 1967 and chief executive in 1974, before retiring in 1976.
        Mr. Reid was a member of the Board of Governors of Charlotte Hungerford Hospital, in Torrington, and a director of several companies and organizations, including the Hartford Steam Boiler Company and the Hartford Electric Light Company.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Mary Lou Evanson; a son, William R. Reid 3d of Reading, Pa.; a daughter, Jane R. Schick of Bloomsburg, Pa.; and five grandchildren.

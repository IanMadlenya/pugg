        Allen H. Morgan, an environmentalist, died of cancer on Sunday at the Lahey Clinic in Burlington, Mass. He was 64 years old and lived in Wayland, Mass.
        In 1953 Mr. Morgan was a founder of the Sudbury Valley Trustees Inc., a land conservation group that protects waterways and open space in the Concord River basin. The organization has grown to more than 2,400 members and has helped preserve thousands of acres of land. He was its executive director since 1981.
        Before that, from 1957 to 1980, he was executive vice president of the Massachusetts Audubon Society. He worked previously as an insurance salesman.
        A 1947 graduate of Bowdoin College, Mr. Morgan served with the Marines in both World War II and the Korean War. He was a director of many environmental organizations.
        He is survived by his wife, Alice; two sons, Charles of Clark, N.J., and David of Vineyard Haven, Mass.; a daughter, Cindy Frothingham of Wayland; a sister, Jerry Nugent of Virginia Beach, Va., and eight grandchildren.

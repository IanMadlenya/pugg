        Arthur N. Levien, a builder and co-developer of the 52-story Olympic Tower on Fifth Avenue, died of complications from amyotrophic lateral sclerosis, or Lou Gehrig's Disease, Saturday at Long Island Jewish Medical Center in Queens. He was 69 years old and lived in Aventura, Fla.
        Mr. Levien founded the Arlen Realty and Development Corporation in 1959 with Arthur Cohen. The company co-developed the Olympic Tower, at 51st Street, with a company controlled by the late Aristotle Onassis.
        Mr. Levien is survived by his wife, Jane; four sons, Barry of Locust Valley, L.I., Philip of Kings Point, L.I., Thomas of Rockville, Md., and George of Aventura; three brothers, Henry of Manhattan, Robert of Roslyn, L.I., and Edward of Miami, and seven grandchildren.


        Dumond Peck Hill, a specialist in international law, died Saturday at his home in Washington. He was 68 years old.
        He died of a heart ailment, a family spokeswoman said.
        Mr. Hill was a Government counsel from 1950 to 1959, serving with the Atomic Energy Commission, the Departments of Defense, Army and Justice and the House Committee on Foreign Affairs.
        For the last 32 years, he was a managing partner with three Washington law firms, most recently with Kirkpatrick & Lockhart.
        Mr. Hill was a native of Fayetteville, N.C., and a graduate of Davidson College and the University of Virginia Law School. In World War II, he was an Army sergeant in Europe.
        His survivors include his wife, Elizabeth, a book publisher; three daughters, Emma, a securities analyst of Bronxville, N.Y., Julia, a culinary instructor of Rhinebeck, N.Y., and Alice, an assistant United States Attorney of Los Angeles; a brother, Tom, a retired philosophy professor of Chapel Hill, N.C.; a sister, Sarah Brown, a piano instructor of Richmond, and five grandchildren.

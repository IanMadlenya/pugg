
        Seymour B. Feller, a housing official under Mayor John V. Lindsay and a leader in Democratic Reform politics in New York City, died on Monday at St. Joseph's Hospital in Yonkers. He was 64 years old and lived in the Riverdale section of the Bronx.
        He died of cancer, his wife said.
        Mr. Feller was appointed director of demolition in the Housing Development Administration in 1964 and remained in the post until 1977. He then joined National Contracting Risks Ltd. in Manhattan, which specializes in the sale of surety bonds.
        Mr. Feller was a founding member of the John F. Kennedy Reform Democratic Club and one of its earliest candidates for the State Assembly. He later became a member and officer of the Benjamin Franklin Reform Democratic Club in Riverdale.
        He was born in the Bronx and graduated from Cornell University.
        Besides his wife, the former Sheila Spiro, he is survived by two daughters, Marcy, of Manhattan, and Andrea, of Los Angeles; a brother, Milton, of Yonkers, and a grandson.

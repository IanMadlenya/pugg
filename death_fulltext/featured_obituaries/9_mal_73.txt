        George Archinal, a former New York State Assemblyman and Queens County Republican leader, died of heart failure Monday at his home in Glendale, Queens. He was 87 years old.
        Mr. Archinal served as an Assemblyman from 1941 to 1948, when he became deputy clerk of the Queens County Surrogate Court. He was chief clerk of the court from 1956 to 1964, and from 1965 until 1971, he was a member of the New York State Board of Standards and Appeals. From 1965 to 1968, he was leader of the Queens Republicans.
        Mr. Archinal's political career included unsuccessful campaigns for Queens Borough President, in 1945, and for a Congressional seat from Queens, which he lost narrowly in 1960 to a Democrat, Joseph P. Addabbo, who remained a Representative until his death last year.
        Born in Brooklyn, the son of a brewery driver, Mr. Archinal graduated from Commercial High School in Brooklyn, and from the New York Stock Exchange Institute of Finance. He was a stockbroker with a number of companies.
        Mr. Archinal's wife, the former Dorothy Koelling, died in 1983. He is survived by a daughter, Dolores Wieck of Glendale and Miami Beach; a sister, Mildred A. Herts of Georgia, and a grandson.

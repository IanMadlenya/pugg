        Edwin Scalabrino, who managed several Manhattan hotels before retiring as general manager of the Milford Plaza earlier this year, died Tuesday after coronary bypass surgery at St. Vincent's Hospital and Medical Center. He was 61 years old and lived in Southampton, L.I.
        Mr. Scalabrino began his hotel career as a front-office manager at the Park Sheraton in 1946. He also managed the Manhattan, Taft and Penn Garden Hotels before being named general manager of the Warwick in Philadelphia in 1969. He returned to New York three years later to manage the City Squire Inn, and in 1973 was named managing director of the Americana. He served as general manager of the Roosevelt and the Biltmore before joining the Milford Plaza as manager in 1981.
        He is survived by a sister, Gloria Raiteri, of Howell, N.J.

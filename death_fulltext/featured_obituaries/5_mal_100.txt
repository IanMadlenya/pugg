        Lester Eisner Jr., a lawyer who was a former regional administrator of the Federal Department of Housing and Urban Development, died Tuesday at his Manhattan apartment after a long illness. He was 73 years old and also had a home in Saxtons River, Vt.
        He was named an assistant commissioner of the New York State Division of Housing in the late 1950's and in 1960 became regional administrator of the Federal Housing and Home Finance Agency, which later became the Department of Housing and Urban Development. In 1966, Mr. Eisner resigned the Federal post to enter private business.
        In 1973, he rejoined the State Division of Housing as the first deputy commissioner but resigned in 1976.
        A native of Red Bank, N.J., Mr. Eisner was a graduate of Princeton University and Harvard Law School. He was a captain in the Army Air Corps in World War II.
        He is survived by his wife, Margaret; a daughter, Margot Freedman of Mamaroneck, N.Y.; a son, Michael, of Los Angeles; two brothers, Gerald Steiner and Erwin Steiner, both of New York City, and five grandchildren.

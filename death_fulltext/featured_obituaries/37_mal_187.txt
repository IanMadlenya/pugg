        Donald S. Hutter, a book editor and publisher for more than 30 years who had his own imprint at Henry Holt, died of cancer at Norwalk (Conn.) Hospital on Thursday. He was 57 years old and lived in New Canaan, Conn.
        Mr. Hutter edited a wide range of works, including fiction, cartoon books, science fiction and books about sports and the outdoors as well as about business. Over the years, he worked with writers and cartoonists including Charles Schulz, Gary Trudeau, J. G. Ballard and Hugh Nissenson.
        He was an editor at Charles Scribner's Sons from 1957 to 1967, then joined Dial Press, where he was a senior editor, then editor in chief.
        He became the executive editor of Holt, Rinehart & Winston (Holt's precursor) in 1972 and was its editor in chief from 1981 to 1982.
        From 1982 to 1985 he was a vice president and senior editor at Simon & Schuster.
        Mr. Hutter also wrote a children's book, ''Upright Hilda,'' and contributed to magazines, including Esquire, and to ''Best American Short Stories, 1965.''
        He was born in London, attended the Middlesex School in Massachusetts and graduated from Princeton University in 1954.
        Surviving are his wife, Martha; three daughters, Vickie, Stephanie and Sarah, and his mother, Catherine.


        Aubrey Mallach, a former executive of social service organizations in New York City, died on Monday at White Plains Hospital. He was 85 years old and lived in White Plains.
        He died of complications of bone marrow cancer, family members said.
        Mr. Mallach was executive director of the Jewish Guild for the Blind from 1970 to 1977 and oversaw the building of the guild's headquarters in Manhattan.
        His previous positions included assistant director of the Federation of Social Agencies in Pittsburgh, executive director of the Health Council of Greater New York and acting director of the Welfare and Health Council of New York City.
        From 1953 to 1959, he was deputy director general in Israel for Malben, a program of the American Joint Distribution Committee, which offers medical and social services to Jews.
        Mr. Mallach was born in England. He studied at the Jewish Theological Seminary in Manhattan, graduated from New York University and received a master's degree from the Columbia University School of Social Work.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Esther Dingol; three sons, Efrem, of Needham, Mass., Alan, of Trenton, and David, of Maplewood, N.J., and six granchildren.

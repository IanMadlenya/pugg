        Isidore Levine, a former Family Court judge appointed by Mayor John V. Lindsay, died of a heart attack Friday at Long Island Jewish Hospital in Queens. He lived in Flushing, Queens, and was 75 years old.
        Mr. Levine, a partner in the Manhattan law firm of Levine & Greenberg for more than 50 years, also had served as an assistant district attorney in Queens and as chairman of the Queens County Liberal Party.
        In 1969, he was appointed to the New York City Family Court by Mayor Lindsay. He served on the Family Court until 1981, when he retired, and since then as a judicial hearing officer for State Supreme Court in Queens and Nassau County.
        He is survived by his wife, Annette; a son, William, of Forest Hills, Queens; a brother, Jacob, of Hallandale, Fla., and a grandson.

        Mary Hill Fried, a writer, an artist and a founder of the American Carousel Association, died of cancer of the liver and colon Thursday at Doctors Hospital. She was 74 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mrs. Fried, who was born in Baltimore, was a descendant of Daniel Boone. With her husband, Frederick, she wrote several books, including ''America's Forgotten Folk Art.'' The carousel association, founded in 1972, is a national organization dedicated to preserving carousels.
        She is survived by her husband; a daughter, Rachel, of Atlanta; a son, Robert, and a grandson.


        Jack Roche, a writer and producer for radio and television who specialized in comedy series, died yesterday at Lawrence Hospital in Bronxville, N.Y. He was 86 years old and lived in Scarsdale, N.Y.
        He died of complications from a broken hip, his family said.
        Mr. Roche produced the long-running comedy "Duffy's Tavern" on radio and co-wrote the "Bob and Ray" show on both radio and television. He also wrote for such television series as "The Danny Thomas Show," "Pete and Gladys" and "Highway Patrol."
        On Broadway, he co-wrote sketches for a 1950 revue, "Tickets, Please!," starring Grace and Paul Hartman.
        John Francis Roche grew up in Brooklyn and in Monroe, N.Y., where he graduated from parochial schools.  Early in his career, he was an associate editor of the journalism trade journal Editor and Publisher, a "Talk of the Town" reporter for The New Yorker and an assistant director of news and special events for CBS Radio.
        He is survived by his wife, Joan; a son, Terence, of Chevy Chase, Md., and two grandsons.

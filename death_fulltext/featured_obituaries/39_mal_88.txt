        Guy P. Simoni, a founder and executive vice president of MacLean Grove & Company, a construction concern based in Greenwich, Conn., died of lung cancer yesterday in St. Luke's Hospital in Manhattan. He was 62 years old and lived in Tenafly, N.J., and Vero Beach, Fla.
        Mr. Simoni started in the construction business in 1953 after graduating from the Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute. He worked on various projects including a portion of the Lincoln Tunnel and the West Delaware Tunnel in Roscoe, N.Y. In 1966, he formed MacLean Grove & Company with Norman Nadel of Greenwich. It handled tunnel construction work, including subway tunnels and stations on the Bay Area Rapid Transit system in San Francisco and the Metro in Washington.
        Mr. Simoni was a member of the executive committee of the General Contractors Association of New York.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Barbara Grove; two daughters, Jane Forsythe of Brooklyn Heights and Kate Fralin of Washington; a son, David G., of Santa Monica, Calif.; two sisters, Thelma Nolin of New Rochelle, N.Y., and Sandra Williams of Weston, Conn., and two brothers, Philip, of Huntington, L.I., and Peter, of Orlando, Fla.

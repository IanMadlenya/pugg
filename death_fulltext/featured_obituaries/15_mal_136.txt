        James C. Evans, a retired Defense Department aide and former professor at Howard University, died of Alzheimer's disease April 14 at his home in Washington, D.C. He was 87 years old.
        Mr. Evans worked on efforts to integrate the armed forces in the 1940s and was an aide to the Secretary of the Army. Later, he was an assistant in the Defense Department's Office for Manpower.
        From 1946 to 1970, Mr. Evans taught electrical engineering at Howard University. He was born in Gallatin, Tenn., and received bachelor's and master's degrees at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology.
        Survivors are a daughter, Evangeline E. Wells, of Washington, and a son, James, of Guilford, Conn.; a brother, George, of Greensboro, N.C., and four grandchildren.

        Harold V. Webb, former executive director of the National School Boards Association, died of cancer Monday at the Evanston Hospital in Evanston, Ill. He was 68 years old. Mr. Webb, who lived in Wilmette, Ill., served from 1961 to 1977 as executive director of the association, which has its headquarters in Alexandria, Va., and represents 15,000 of the nation's local school boards. He later founded a firm that helped school boards find new superintendents. He was executive director of the Wyoming School Boards Association from 1953 to 1958.
        He is survived by his wife, Beulah; three sons, David, of Schaumburg, Ill., Jon, of Acton, Mass., and Jeffrey, of Tulsa; a brother, Roland, of Peel, Ark., and four grandchildren.

        Max Block, a nationally recognized accountant and former managing editor of The CPA Journal, died of heart failure Monday at the Del Rey Beach Community Hospital in Florida. He was 86 years old and lived in White Plains.
        A graduate of City College of New York, Mr. Block was a founder of Anchin, Block & Anchin in 1923. He was a member of the New York State Society of C.P.A's for more than 60 years and was managing editor of its publication, The CPA Journal, from 1961 to 1974.
        The annual Max Block Distinguished Article Award was established 12 years ago to honor the author of the best article in the journal. Mr. Block had been active with community organizations in Mount Vernon, where he had lived for many years.
        He is survived by his wife, Shirley; two daughters, Ruth Harris of Roslyn, L.I., and Nancy Sherman of White Plains; five grandchildren, and one great-grandchild.

        Charles Prentice Thompson, a Manhattan architect, died of pneumonia Dec. 16 at New York University Hospital in Manhattan. He was 67 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        He had been associated with the architect Victor Proetz, working on residences for Oveta Culp Hobby in Houston and Aristotle Onassis in Manhattan. In 1953 and 1954, he was the architect and designer for the United States ambassador's residence, the Villa Taverna, in Rome. He also worked on other consulates and embassies in Italy and the Middle East.
        Mr. Thompson was a partner in Thompson Robinson Toraby, a Manhattan architectural firm. Previously, he worked at McKim, Mead & White.
        He is survived by a sister, Virginia Fishback of Jacksonville, Ill.

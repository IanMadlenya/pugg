        Walter J. Ludovico, the city's longest-serving Councilman, died Monday at Crouse Memorial Hospital after suffering a heart attack. He was 65 years old.
        Mr. Ludovico was a Golden Gloves boxer and attended Manhattan College on a boxing scholarship. He served in the Army Air Corps in World War II and later coached football at Bishop Ludden High School in Syracuse.
        He was first elected to the City Council in 1957 and served two terms before serving two years on the Onondaga County Board of Supervisors from 1961 to 1963. In 1963, he was elected again to the Council and then re-elected every two years after that.
        He is survived by a daughter, Lynn Madden of Syracuse; four brothers, Leo, Michael, Vincent and Frank, all of Syracuse, and four sisters, Veda Donath, Lousie Kankowski, Clara Raymond and Helen Fodaro, all of Syracuse.

        Ned L. Pines, a New York publisher, died yesterday at the American Hospital in Paris after a brief illness. He was 84 years old and lived in Paris, Manhattan and East Hampton, L.I.
        Mr. Pines, a native of Malden, Mass., became president of Pines Publications, magazine publishers in Manhattan, in 1928 and continued in that post until 1961. He was also president of Popular Library from 1942 to 1966 and chairman from 1966 to 1968. He retired in 1971 but continued to work as a consultant.
        Mr. Pines was chairman of the board of Eastern Life Insurance from 1949 to 1971 and a member of the coordinating committee of the Columbia University Institute of Research from 1945 to 1947. He was made a life trustee of the Federation of Jewish Philanthropies in 1968, was on the advisory board of Commentary Magazine and was a member of the board of directors of the Merce Cunningham Dance Federation from 1970 to 1974.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Maxine Firestone; two daughters from a previous marriage, Judith Ann Bernard and Susan, both of Manhattan; two stepsons, Anthony and Kenneth Michaelman, both of Los Angeles, and a granddaughter.

        Dr. Carl R. Oman, a dentist and retired professor of dentistry at the Columbia University School of Dental and Oral Surgery, died of cardiac arrest Wednesday at North Carolina Memorial Hospital in Chapel Hill. He was 96 years old and lived in Chapel Hill.
        Dr. Oman, a graduate of the University of Minnesota, joined Columbia in 1936 and retired in 1960. He helped develop an ultrasonic dental drill.
        He is survived by a daughter, Jane Sargent of Wellesley, Mass.; two sons, Carl, of Meriden, Conn., and Frederick, of Dallas; 13 grandchildren, and 7 great-grandchildren.

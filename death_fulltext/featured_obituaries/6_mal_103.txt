        Dr. Thomas C. McGinnis, a psychotherapist, writer and radio commentator, died of lung cancer Monday at his home in Fair Lawn, N.J. He was 62 years old.
        In 1959, Dr. McGinnis founded the Counseling and Psychotherapy Center in Fair Lawn, one of the country's first private psychological service centers. He was also co-director of the New York University doctoral program in marriage, family and human sexuality.
        He was the author of five books on human relations and appeared on radio and television. He was a consultant to 12 major league baseball teams and, in 1982, founded the Institute for Professional Sports Psychology in Fair Lawn.
        Dr. McGinnis is survived by his wife, the former Mary Yorke; two sons, Thomas Jr. of Woodcliff Lake, N.J., and John, of Holliston, Mass.; a daughter, Karen Mercantino of Fair Lawn; a sister, Rose King of Petersburg, Va.; a brother, the Rev. James W. McGinnis of Cary, N.C., and three grandchildren.

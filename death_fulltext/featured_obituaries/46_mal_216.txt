
        Richard Stotesbury Jessup, a certified public accountant and a tax specialist, died on Sunday at his home in Hightstown, N.J. He was 83 years old.
        He died of cancer, a family spokesman said.
        Mr. Jessup had been the head of his own accounting firm based in Smithtown, L.I. He retired in 1979.
        He was born in Manhattan and graduated from Princeton University in 1929. In the 1930's, he was associated with the Mercer-Allied Corporation, investment managers. He later became a tax consultant for Scovill Inc.
        In World War II he was a captain in the Army Air Forces in Europe, earning four battle stars. After the war he worked for the Marshall Plan in Paris and was financial director of CARE, the international assistance program.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Anne Mills Long.

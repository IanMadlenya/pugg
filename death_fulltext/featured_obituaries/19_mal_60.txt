        James T. McDonald, a former assistant chief inspector of the New York City Police Department, died Wednesday following a stroke in Silver Spring, Md., where he lived with his daughter. He was 85 years old and had earlier been a resident of Ridge, L.I., and of Queens Village.
        He retired in 1965 after 38 years on the police force.
        He is survived by his daughter, Joan Castellan; four sons, James Jr., of Shoreham, L.I., Dr. Kenneth, of Allentown, Pa., Dr. Bruce, of Southport, Conn., and Col. Lawrence, who is stationed with the Marine Corps on Okinawa, and 17 grandchildren and six great-grandchildren.

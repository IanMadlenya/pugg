
        Samuel C. Brightman, the Democratic National Committee's deputy chairman and spokesman during the Truman, Kennedy and Johnson Administrations, died on Jan. 29 at Sibley Memorial Hospital in Washington. He was 80 years old and lived in Bethesda, Md.
        Mr. Brightman died of emphysema, his family said.
        Early in his career he worked for the Cincinnati Post, St. Louis Star-Times, KSD radio in St. Louis and the Louisville Courier-Journal.
        In World War II he enlisted in the Army and helped run press operations. He coordinated coverage of the liberation of Paris and was the executive officer of the press camp at the post-war Potsdam conference of Allied leaders. He won two Bronze Stars.
        In 1947 the Democrats hired him as associate director of publicity. He continued working for the party under eight chairmen, rising to director and deputy chairman for public affairs and serving as editor of the Democratic Digest.
        His first marriage, to the former Ruth Christian, ended in divorce. He is survived by his wife, the former Lucy Kirk Cleaver; a daughter, Elizabeth Stedman of Silver Spring, Md.; three sons, Samuel C. Jr., of Gaithersburg, Md., George, of Manhattan, and David, of San Francisco, and one grandchild.

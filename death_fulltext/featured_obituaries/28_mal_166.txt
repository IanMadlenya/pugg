        Rabbi Zelo Schussheim, a professor of Talmud for more than 40 years, died of a heart attack Sunday at Booth Memorial Medical Center in Flushing, Queens. He was 62 years old and lived in Kew Gardens.
        Rabbi Schussheim was born in Brooklyn and was educated at Brooklyn College, the Rabbi Isaac Elchanan Theological Seminary and Yeshiva University. He was among the first group of American-born graduates of the Elchanan Seminary to join its rabbinic faculty. He was appointed as a professor of Talmud at the seminary, an affiliate of Yeshiva University, in 1969. He had taught at Talmudic Academy-Yeshiva University High School.
        He is survived by his wife, Sheilah; a son, Michael, of Cedarhurst, L.I.; a daughter, Barbara Grossman of Hillcrest, Queens; a sister, Gloria Goldstein of Hollis, Queens, who is a State Supreme Court Justice, and four grandchildren.

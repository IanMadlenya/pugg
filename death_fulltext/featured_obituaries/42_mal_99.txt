        Marvin S. Pittler, vice president of manufacturing research for I.B.M. Research, died on Wednesday at his office in Yorktown Heights, N.Y. He was 61 years old and lived in Poughkeepsie, N.Y. The cause of death apparently was a heart attack, a spokesman for the company said.
        Mr. Pittler joined International Business Machines in 1963 and was named vice president of manufacturing research last year.
        He was born in Brooklyn. Mr. Pittler graduated from Polytechnic University, of which he was named a fellow in 1986. Last year he was elected a fellow of the Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Ruth Cowen, and two daughters, Samantha and Marisa, both of Poughkeepsie.

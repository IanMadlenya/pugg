
        Richard Salter Storrs, a retired New York securities lawyer and senior partner in the international law firm of Sullivan & Cromwell, died on Sunday at Hilaire Farm in Huntington, L.I. A resident of Oyster Bay, L.I., he was 84.
        He died of pneumonia, his family said.
        Mr. Storrs, a Phi Beta Kappa graduate of Yale University, class of 1932, received his law degree from Harvard University in 1935. He practiced law with Sullivan & Cromwell from 1935 to 1980. He became a partner in 1945 and continued to work in general practice and the securities field as a senior partner through the 1970's.
        He dealt with securities firms like Kidder Peabody & Company in underwriting equity and debt offerings. He was a director of several mutual funds as well as of the Panhandle Eastern Pipe Line Company, which he represented as outside counsel.
        A native of Orange, N.J., he served in the Coast Guard Auxiliary in World War II. He was a founder and past board president of the East Woods School in Oyster Bay. He also served as a trustee of Lloyd Harbor, L.I., and as a mayor of Cove Neck, L.I. He also was a senior vestryman of St. John's Episcopal Church in Cold Spring Harbor, L.I.
        Mr. Storrs is survived by his wife of 56 years, Frances Rousmaniere Storrs; three sons, Richard S. of Lunenburg, Mass., David K. of Fairfield, Conn., and Cleveland M. of Yarmouthport, Mass., and three daughters, Ayer Bellerman of Oyster Bay, Ginny Akabane of Lenox, Mass., and Nancy H. of St. Catharines, Ont.


        Dr. William J. Sanders, a retired educator and the former Commissioner of Education in Connecticut for 17 years, died on Sunday at the Fowler Nursing Home in Guilford, Conn. A resident of West Hartford, he was 87.
        He suffered a long illness, his family said.
        Dr. Sanders was first appointed Commissioner in 1956 by Gov. Abraham A. Ribicoff and kept the post under Govs. Thomas J. Meskill and Ella T. Grasso. He retired in 1973.
        Before going into government work, he was a professor of education at DePaul University, the University of Michigan and New Haven State Teachers College. During World War II, he served as personnel director of the National War Labor Board in Washington. He was also a former president of Fitchburg State Teachers College in Massachusetts.
        He was born in New Haven, and graduated from Yale University, from which he also received his doctorate in education.
        Dr. Sanders is survived by three daughters, Patricia Behan of Madison, Conn., Eleanor Sheehy of Woodbridge, Conn., and Roberta Roepke of Seattle; a son, William J. Jr., of West Hartford; a brother, the Rev. Robert E. Sanders of New Milford, Conn., and 11 grandchildren.

        Lawrence M. Rogin, a labor educator for more than 50 years and national director of education of the A.F.L.-C.I.O. in the 1960's, died of cancer Jan. 26 at his home in Washington. He was 79 years old.
        A native of New York City, Mr. Rogin trained thousands of future officers of local unions in a career that began in 1933.
        Mr. Rogin is survived by his wife, the former Hilda Zack Sexton; three children from a previous marriage, Michael, of Berkeley, Calif., Andrea Stanger of Monroeville, Pa., and Edward, of Piedmont, Calif., and six grandchildren.

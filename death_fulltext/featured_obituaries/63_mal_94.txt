
        Dr. Bernard Jacobs, an orthopedic surgeon and clinical professor at Cornell University Medical College who was an authority on the spine, died on Sunday at his home in New Rochelle, N.Y. He was 68 years old.
        Dr. Jacobs died of heart failure, said his wife, Ingrid.
        An expert on lower back pain, Dr. Jacobs was for 31 years an attending orthopedic surgeon at the Hospital for Special Surgery in Manhattan. He was a vocal advocate for changing the workers' compensation system and the liability insurance industry to provide better health care. He also conducted extensive research on the effects of steroids and alcoholism on bone disease.
        He was president of the New York State Society of Orthopedic Surgeons in 1977, chairman of the board of councilors of the American Academy of Orthopedic Surgeons in 1979 and chairman of the orthopedic section of the New York State Medical Society in 1973.
        He was born in London and graduated from the University College Hospital Medical School there in 1948. He received his early surgical training in England, Montreal and New York, where he became an American citizen in 1950. He served as a captain in the Army Medical Corps from 1953 to 1956.
        Besides his wife, Dr. Jacobs is survived by a son, Mark, of Washington, and a daughter, Karen Lauder of New York, both from a previous marriage, and a granddaughter.

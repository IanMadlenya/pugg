        Richard Brittain Tuggle, a retired rear admiral who participated in several major battles in World War II, died Sunday at the age of 92. No cause of death was given.
        Admiral Tuggle, who was naval attache to Turkey at the outbreak of the war, commanded the blockade of Manila Bay in the Philippines in the early weeks of the war with Japan.
        In 1943, he commanded 20 ships in the capture of Sicily. He was naval chief of staff at the landings at Anzio, Italy.
        He later commanded the Pasadena, which provided supporting gunfire in Marine landings at Leyte, in the Philippines, and at Iwo Jima and Okinawa.
        His last naval assignment, before retirement in 1946, was to arrange the evacuation of the Bikini Islands in preparation for atomic bomb tests.
        He taught mathematics at the University of Miami before moving to Jupiter Island in 1952.
        Survivors include his wife, Elizabeth, of Hobe Sound, Fla.; a son, Richard, of Los Angeles, and a brother, Allan, of Charlotte, N.C.

        Dr. Allan Garfield Gruchy, professor of economics at the University of Maryland for 50 years, died of cancer Monday at Washington Adventist Hospital. He was 83 years old and lived in College Park, Md.
        Dr. Gruchy joined the faculty at Maryland in 1937, after having taught at Ohio University and at the College of St. Thomas in Minnesota. He became a professor emeritus in 1977 and taught part time until 1986.
        He received his Ph.D. in 1931 from the University of Virginia.
        Dr. Gruchy was the author of several books, including ''Contemporary Economic Thought: The American Contribution,'' published in 1947, ''Comparative Economic Systems,'' published in 1966, and ''The Reconstruction of Economics: An Analysis of the Fundamentals of Institutional Economics,'' published in 1987.
        He was a founder and former president of the Association for Evolutionary Economics.
        A widower, he is survived by a son, Allan Jr. of Somerset, N.J.; a daughter, Katherine Gilliams of Columbia, Md.; a brother, John de Gruchy of Vancouver, British Columbia, and two grandchildren.


        William G. Kay Jr., a management consultant and retired business executive, died last Saturday at St. Luke's Hospital in New Bedford, Mass. He was 63 and lived in South Dartmouth, Mass.
        He died of cancer, said his wife, Marcia.
        From 1976 to 1982, Mr. Kay was an executive vice president of the Sun Company, formerly the Sun Refining and Marketing Company, in Philadelphia. He had previously been an executive of the Rival dog food division of Associated Products and the Pepperidge Farms division of the Campbell Soup Company. In recent years he was a management consultant.
        Besides his wife he is survived by two sons, Peyton S., of Manhattan and William G. Jr., of Philadelphia, and a daughter, Caroline W., , also of Philadelphia.

        Norman E. Cash, a former president of the Television Bureau of Advertising, died of a heart attack on Saturday at his home in Palm Beach, Fla. He was 71 years old.
        Mr. Cash, who was known in the televison industry as Pete, was a founder of the bureau in 1955. He was named its president the next year and later its vice chairman. He retired in 1980.
        Under his leadership, the organization grew to include more than 300 television stations and the three leading networks. He began his career in broadcasting in 1939, and after serving in World War II, worked at the National Broadcasting Company, where he was named director of station relations in 1949. He later became eastern sales manager for ABC-TV.
        He was a member of many professional organizations, including the Broadcast Pioneers, the Broadcast Rating Council, and Televison Pioneers.
        He is survived by his wife, Jane; two daughters, Deborah McIntosh of New Canaan, Conn., and Camilla Hanson of Dallas; a son, Jeffrey, of Columbus, Ohio, and seven grandchildren.

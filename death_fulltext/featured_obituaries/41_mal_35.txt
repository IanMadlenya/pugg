        Norman E. Richardson, chairman of the philosophy department at Gettysburg College from 1945 until he retired in 1979, died of heart failure on Saturday at Gettysburg Hospital. He was 80 years old and lived in Gettysburg.
        Dr. Richardson, a native of Cambridge, Mass., was a graduate of Amherst College and held a bachelor of divinity degree and a doctorate in philosophy from Yale. He taught at Amherst and at Whitman College before becoming the William Bittinger Professor at Gettysburg in 1945.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Anne Gordon; three sons, Gordon of Winchester, Mass., N. Cristopher of St. Paul and Bruce of Freesoil, Mich.; a daughter, Jean Frick of Ludington, Mich.; a brother, Channing of Clinton, N.Y.; a sister, Margaret Philbrick of Richmond, and five grandsons.

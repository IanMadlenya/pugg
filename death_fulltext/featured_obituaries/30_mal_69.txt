        H. Mason Hicks, a physician and former chief of medicine at Doctors Hospital, died there of cardiac arrest Thursday. He was 85 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Dr. Hicks practiced medicine in Manhattan for more than 50 years until he retired in 1985. An award for excellence in medicine is named in his honor at Doctors Hospital, where he was a trustee.
        Born in Rockwood, Tenn., Dr. Hicks graduated from the University of Tennessee and Columbia University College of Physicians and Surgeons. He was chief resident at Bellevue Hospital and a former president of the Bellevue Alumni Society and the medical board of Doctors Hospital. He was also a consulting physician at the Hospital for Special Surgery.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Rebecca Tarwater; three sons, H. Mason Jr. and James, both of Manhattan, and Alan, of Mountain Lakes, N.J.; a sister, Della Sue Newman of Princeton, N.J., and six grandchildren.


        Robert W. Bechtel, a retired Dun & Bradstreet executive, died on April 15 at Columbia Presbyterian Medical Center in Manhattan. He was 68 years old and lived in Ridgewood, N.J.
        He died of complications after surgery, his physician said.
        In his 40-year career at the company, Mr. Bechtel rose to president of Dun & Bradstreet International from 1971 to 1978 and executive vice president of the parent Dun & Bradstreet Corporation from 1978 to 1983, when he retired.
        He was born in Reading, Pa., graduated from Muhlenberg College in Allentown, Pa., and did postgraduate study at Temple University and Harvard. In World War II, he was a special agent in the United States Counterintelligence Corps, operating domestically and in Asia.
        Starting at Dun & Bradstreet in 1944 as a credit reporter in Philadelphia, he moved to management posts in Allentown and Baltimore before moving to the Manhattan headquarters in 1955.
        In 1964 he was named a vice president, overseeing marketing services and computer services. He later took over the business information division.
        He was a past board member of the Institute for Inter-Industry Data and the Center for International Management Studies and was on the International Committee of the Greater New York City Y.M.C.A.
        He is survived by his wife of 44 years, the former Jean Frances Botbyl; two daughters, Barbara Jean Bechtel Green of Denver and Leslie Noel Bechtel Caruso of Darien, Conn.; a son, David R. of Manhattan; a sister, Dorothy Kessler Slaugh of Lancaster, Pa., and three grandchildren.

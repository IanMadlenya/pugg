        Joseph Lester Hankerson, a longtime participant in civil rights activities in the South, died of a heart attack Tuesday at Georgia Baptist Medical Center in Atlanta. He was 63 years old.
        Mr. Hankerson, known as Big Lester, started out as the leader of a street gang in Savannah. He later renounced violence to join the civil rights movement and marched with the Rev. Dr. Martin Luther King Jr. in nonviolent demonstrations of the 1960's.
        Mr. Hankerson is survived by his wife, the former Violet Johnson; three sons and a daughter.

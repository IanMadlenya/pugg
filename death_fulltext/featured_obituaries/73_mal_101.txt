
        Robert B. Lisle, a corporate and labor lawyer, died on Tuesday at his home in Greenvale, L.I. He was 80.
        The cause was cancer, said his wife, Elizabeth.
        At his death, Mr. Lisle was partner emeritus of Cullen & Dykman, a Brooklyn law firm with which he was affiliated for 57 years, first as an associate, then partner. He represented such diverse clients as Brooklyn Union Gas and the Episcopal Diocese of Long Island.
        Mr. Lisle was the Mayor of the Village of Roslyn Harbor, L.I., for 18 years until 1976. Before that he was a village trustee.
        Vice chancellor of the Episcopal Diocese of Long Island from 1940 to 1952 and a church advocate, handling the diocese's legal matters for many years, Mr. Lisle served as vestryman and later warden at Trinity Church in Roslyn.
        Born in Troy, N.Y., he graduated from Harvard College and Harvard Law School.
        Besides his wife, Mr. Lisle is survived by a son, Dr. Robert W. Lisle of Timonium, Md.; a daughter, Judith Ann Irons of Arlington, Va.; six grandchildren, and one great-grandchild.

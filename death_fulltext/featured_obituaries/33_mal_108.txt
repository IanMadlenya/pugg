        Robert L. Myers, a chemist involved in industrial research and development, died of renal failure and other complications last Thursday at Stamford (Conn.) Hospital. He was 66 years old and lived in Darien, Conn.
        Dr. Myers, a 1947 graduate of Kent State University with a Ph.D. in chemistry, was formerly director of research at Borg-Warner, American Cyanamid, GAF and the St. Regis Paper Company. In 1975 he was named vice president for technology at St. Regis, which was later acquired by Champion International. He retired in 1987. Dr. Myers also served on the National Academy of Sciences Research Council and on professional boards.
        He is survived by his wife, Alice; two sons, Douglas, of Hong Kong, and Jeffrey, of Miami; two daughters, Susan, of Fairfield, Conn., and Laurie, of Boston; two sisters, Doris Rucker of Akron, Ohio, and Barbara Stefany, of Tampa, Fla., and three grandchildren.

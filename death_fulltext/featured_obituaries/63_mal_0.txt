
        Gosta Oldenburg, the Swedish Consul General and Dean of the Consular Corps in Chicago until his retirement in 1959, died last Tuesday at New York Hospital. He was 98 years old and lived in Manhattan in recent years.
        The cause of death was not immediately available.
        Mr. Oldenburg, a graduate of Uppsala University in Sweden, entered the Swedish diplomatic service in 1920. He served in London, Berlin, Oslo and New York before being posted to Chicago in 1936.
        Mr. Oldenburg is survived by two sons, Claes, the artist known for his large public sculptures, and Richard, the director of the Museum of Modern Art, both of Manhattan.

        Dr. Irving D. Buchin, an orthodontist and professor at the University of Pennsylvania School of Dental Medicine, died of prostate cancer yesterday at Calvary Hospital in the Bronx. He was 69 years old and lived in Forest Hills, Queens.
        Dr. Buchin, who had taught at the dental school since 1969, practiced in Manhattan. He invented the Buchin cephalometric template, used to take facial measurements.
        Dr. Buchin was born in Manhattan and graduated from New York University and its dental school. He was a former president of the Northeastern Society of Orthodontists and an editor of the American Journal of Orthodontics.
        Surviving are his wife, Jean; two sons, Peter and John, both of Manhattan, and a brother, Sidney, of Lake Worth, Fla.

        Heley Howell Rhys, an educator and art historian, died Wednesday at the nursing center at the retirement community of Crosslands in Kennet Square, Pa., where he lived. He was 80 years old and died of comlications of Parkinson's disease, his wife, Jean, said.
        Dr. Rhys joined the faculty at Swarthmore College in 1948, and from 1970 until his retirement in 1976 was chairman of the art department there. He had been director of the Works Project Administration art programs in the West Virginia from 1935 to 1938.
        Dr. Rhys was an authority on Maurice Brazil Prendergast, the American Impressionist, and in 1960 wrote a biography ofhim. He was also an authority on 19th-century French artists and on Chinese and Japanese art.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Jean Orton.

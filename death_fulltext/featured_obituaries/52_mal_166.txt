
        Dr. John Benjamin Schmoker, a longtime official in international student exchange, died on Sunday at the Highland Farms nursing home in Black Mountain, N.C., where he lived. He was 88 years old.
        He died after a stroke, his family said.
        Dr. Schmoker, who was born and raised in Iowa, was a graduate of the University of Minnesota and also received a doctorate there. He was executive director of the University Young Men's Christian Association in Minneapolis from 1931 to 1945.
        From 1946 to 1969 Dr. Schmoker was executive secretary of the Committee on Friendly Relations Among Foreign Students, and later the International Student Service. From 1969 to 1973 he was executive director of the Institute of World Affairs.
        Dr. Schmoker is survived by his wife, the former Blanche Churchill; a daughter, Marilyn Jeanne Anderson of Highland Farms; two grandsons, and six great-grandchildren.

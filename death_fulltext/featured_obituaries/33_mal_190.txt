        Gerald Sussman, a humorist and advertising copywriter, died early Sunday in Alford, Mass., after a bronchial asthmatic attack while visiting friends. He was 56 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        At his death, Mr. Sussman was an associate creative director in the sales promotion department at Saatchi & Saatchi, the advertising concern.
        From 1973 to 1984, he was a member of the editorial board at The National Lampoon, becoming editor in chief of the magazine in 1983. During that time Mr. Sussman was also a co-author of scripts for two Lampoon feature films.
        He wrote seven humor books, including ''The Not Quite TV Guide'' (Crown, 1983), a parody of TV Guide, and ''The Official Sex Manual'' (G.P. Putnam, 1965), a parody of sexual terms and problems.
        Mr. Sussman was born in Manhattan on April 2, 1933, and graduated from Brooklyn College.
        He is survived by his wife, Elaine Louie; a daughter, Anna Louie, and a brother, Harvey, of Austin, Tex.
        A memorial service will be held Saturday at 4 P.M. at St. Luke in the Fields Church, 487 Hudson Street, at Grove Street.

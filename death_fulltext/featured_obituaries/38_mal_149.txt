        Morris Abrams, an industrialist and philanthropist, died on Sunday at his home in Neponsit, Queens. He was 99 years old.
        Mr. Abrams, who was born in Russia and came to the United States in 1898, founded Morris Abrams Inc., a Manhattan-based supplier of machinery and factory tools. He founded the company in 1922 and retired about 15 years ago. For many years, he was a director of Temple Ahavath Sholom and of the Hebrew Educational Society, both in Brooklyn.
        He is survived by a daughter, Dr. Evelyn Mauss of Neponsit; two sons, the actor Mason Adams of Westport, Conn., and Dr. Herbert Abrams of Stanford, Calif.; a sister, Mabel, of Manhattan; seven grandchildren, and four great-grandchildren.

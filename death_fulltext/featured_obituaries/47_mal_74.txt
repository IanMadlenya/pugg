
        William A. Epstein, a retired obstetrician and gynecologist at Mount Sinai Medical Center, died yesterday at Mount Sinai. He was 81 years old and lived in Scarsdale, N.Y.
        Dr. Epstein died of heart failure, his daughter, Karin, of Bronxville, N.Y., said.
        He joined the staff of Mount Sinai in 1935 as an associate and attending doctor. He did research in fertility, and was also a staff member of Flower Fifth Avenue and Morrisania Hospitals and a member of the faculty of the New York Medical College. He retired in 1979, but remained in private practice.
        Dr. Epstein was a graduate of City College and the Cornell University Medical College.
        Besides his daughter, he is survived by his wife, the former Ruth Meyer; a son, Hal, of Hastings-on-Hudson, N.Y.; a sister, Florence, and a brother, Tom, both of Manhattan, and a grandson.


        Matthew P. Deane, a retired investment banker and stockbroker, died on Friday at his home in Todt Hill, S.I. He was 60 years old.
        He died of cancer, his wife said.
        He was a former vice chairman of L. F. Rothschild, an investment banking and stock brokerage firm in Manhattan. He joined the firm in 1950, was named a partner in 1966, senior managing partner in 1981 and vice chairman in 1986. He retired in 1987.
        In the 1960's he was a member of several regulatory committees for the securities industry.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Christina Wogas; four sons, Matthew Jr., and Edmond, both of Manalapan, N.J., Paul, of Englishtown, N.J., and John, of Hazlet, N.J.; five daughters, Dorothy, of Jersey City, Virginia Murphy of Atlantic Highlands, N.J., Audrey, of San Francisco, Martha, of Woodbridge, N.J., and Bridget, of Clove Lakes, S.I.; his mother, Loretta Deane of Clove Lakes; two brothers, James, of Brooklyn, and Patrick, of Annandale, S.I., and seven grandchildren.

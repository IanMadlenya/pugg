        Charles C. Bassine, a businessman who was a benefactor and trustee of number of New York City hospitals, died on Friday at St. Mary's Hospital in West Palm Beach, Fla. He was 81 years old and lived in Palm Beach. He died of cancer, his daughter said.  Mr. Bassine was the former chairman of Spartan Industries, a sportswear company that merged with E.J. Korvette in 1966. Mr. Bassine was an overseer at the Albert Einstein College of Medicine, a fellow of New York University and a trustee of Montefiore Medical Center, Rockefeller University and the Federation of Jewish Philanthropies. He helped found Brandeis University and North Shore University Hospital.
        Mr. Bassine was born in Brooklyn and was a graduate of New York University and New York Law School.
        He is survived by his second wife, Susan H. Bassine; a son, David; two daughters, Karen B. Cohen and Ellen B. Davis; two stepchildren, Ronnie Storch and David Halpern; four sisters; nine grandchildren, and four great-grandchildren.

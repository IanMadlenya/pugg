
        Sydney L. Whitman, the owner of a large kosher catering house in Brooklyn, died on Monday at Englewood Hospital in Englewood, N.J. He was 84 and lived in Fort Lee, N.J.
        He had bone cancer, said Stephen B. Wilson, his son-in-law.
        Mr. Whitman was born and raised on the Lower East Side of Manhattan. He served in the Army during World War II and then opened Menora Temple, a kosher catering house at 5000 14th Avenue in Borough Park.
        He is survived by his wife, Anne Rogoff Whitman; two sisters, Sylvia Stein of Pompano Beach, Fla., Dorothy Lieberman of Hallandale, Fla., a brother, Julius Whitman of Pembroke Pines, Fla.; two sons, Alan, of Manhattan, Stephen, of Hackensack, N.J.; a daughter, Judith Whitman-Wilson of Manhattan, and two grandchildren.

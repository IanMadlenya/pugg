        Craig Starbuck Atkins, a retired judge of the United States Tax Court, died of Alzheimer's disease on Saturday at a nursing home in Fort Myers, Fla. He was 86 years old and lived in Fort Myers.
        Judge Atkins, a graduate of George Washington University Law School, served on the Tax Court from 1955 to 1976. Before that, he was assistant chief counsel at the Bureau of Internal Revenue.
        He also spent two years as a tax adviser to the Economic Cooperation Administration in Greece and was a lawyer with the United States Board of Tax Appeals from 1927 to 1937.
        He is survived by his wife, Lucille; a son, Craig Jr., and a daughter, Constance McShulskis, both of Potomac, Md.; a stepson, Dennis Foreman of Binghamton, N.Y.; a stepdaughter, Arlene Foreman of Fort Myers; three sisters, Claire Bastable of Chevy Chase, Md., Kathleen Carter of Bala-Cynwyd, Pa., and Grace Stewart of Tampa, Fla.; six grandchildren and five great-grandchildren.

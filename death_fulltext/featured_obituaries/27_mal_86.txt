        William H. Brett, director of the United States Mint under President Eisenhower, died of pancreatic cancer Monday at his home in Palm Desert, Calif. He was 95 years old.
        Mr. Brett served the Mint from 1954 to 1961, supervising plants in Philadelphia, Denver and San Francisco.
        A native of Cleveland and graduate of Dartmouth College, he served in the Army in World War I. Before his appointment in Washington he was in business in Ohio. After his retirement he served as a financial consultant.
        His wife of 58 years, Ruth C. Brett, died in 1975. He is survived by his second wife, the former Mary Lynn Marburg; two sons by his first marriage, Peter, of Falls Church, Va., and William 3d of Manhattan; five grandchildren and seven great-grandchildren.

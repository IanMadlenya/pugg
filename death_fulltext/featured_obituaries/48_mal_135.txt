
        Vasco Pratolini, an Italian novelist, playwright and poet, died on Saturday while putting the finishing touches on his latest book. He was 78 years old.
        He died of a heart attack, the police said.
        Mr. Pratolini, whose works include "A Tale of Poor Lovers," a novel about urban poverty and the growth of Fascism in his native Florence, wrote 19 novels, 2 plays, a book of poetry, and many radio plays and film scripts.
        In the late 1930's and early 40's, Mr. Pratolini edited an avant-garde literary journal, Field of Mars. He was active in the Partisans' resistance to Fascism in World War II. Among his other works are "The Naked Streets," "Metello" and "Bruno Santini."

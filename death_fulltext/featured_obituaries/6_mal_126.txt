        Van R. Halsey, a former senior partner of a Wall Street brokerage firm, died July 17 at his home in Rumson, N.J. He was 90 years old.
        Mr. Halsey was a stockbroker from 1922 until 1966, when he retired as senior partner of Carlisle & Jacquelin.
        Born in Manhattan, he graduated from Princeton University in 1918. He was an Army lieutenant in World War I and served with the Office of Strategic Services in World War II. Active in local politics, he was Mayor of Rumson in the 1930's.
        He was a former vice president and treasurer of the board of trustees of St. Luke's Hospital, now known as St. Luke's-Roosevelt Hospital Center.
        He is survived by two sons, Stephen of Manhattan and C. Van R. Halsey Jr. of Pawcatuck, Conn.; a stepson, Michael Sherman of Princeton, N. J., seven grandchildren and three great grandchildren.

        Gladys Collins, a photographer who organized exhibitions of artworks by the elderly, died from a stroke yesterday at St. Vincent's Hospital and Medical Center. She was 73 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Miss Collins was a photographer for the Works Progress Administration. As a freelance photographer, her works were published in Life and Vogue magazines. From 1978 to 1986, she worked as a liaison between the Department for the Aging and the Grey Art Gallery and Study Center.
        She is survived by a sister, Blanche, of Manhattan.

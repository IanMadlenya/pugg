        John E. Bowman, a former executive director of the Council on International Educational Exchange, died of cancer yesterday at Nyack (N.Y.) Hospital. He was 67 years old and lived in Pomona, N.Y.
        From 1952 to 1979, Mr. Bowman headed the council, a nonprofit organization for international student exchanges. He founded the Consortia for Study Abroad.
        He served in Europe as an administrator for war relief programs from 1946 to 1948. Mr. Bowman, who was born in Bridgewater, Va., received a bachelor's degree from Bridgewater College and a master's degree from the University of Pennsylvania.
        Surviving are his wife, Virginia; three daughters, Katherine, of Ione, Wash., Barbara, of Seattle, and Carol, of Managua, Nicaragua; his mother, Flora, of Bridgewater; a brother, Paul, of Kansas City, Kan.; two sisters, Grace Koons of Gettysburg, Pa., and Gene Johnson of Durham, N.C., and two grandchildren.

        Dr. James F. Gilliam, a professor at Princeton's School of Historical Studies for two decades before his retirement in 1985, died on Friday after a long illness, at the Merwick Nursing Home in Princeton, N.J. He was 75 years old and lived in Princeton.
        Dr. Gilliam was born in Seattle. He received a bachelor's degree from San Jose State College, a master's from Stanford and a doctorate from Yale.
        A specialist in papyrology and in Roman studies, he taught at Yale, Wells College, the Universities of Iowa and Oregon and Columbia before joining the Princeton faculty in 1965. He was a member of the Institute for Advanced Study in 1958-59 and 1963-64.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Elizabeth Holzworth; a daughter, Elizabeth Brown of Princeton; a son, John, of Newport, Ky., and two grandchildren.

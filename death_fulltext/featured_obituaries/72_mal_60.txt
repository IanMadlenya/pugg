
        Herman Odell, a retired lawyer, died on Saturday at his home in Manhattan.
        He had suffered from Alzheimer's disease and died of heart failure, his family said. He was 81.
        Mr. Odell, who specialized in corporate law, practiced in Manhattan for more than 40 years. For many years he was a partner in Becker, Berman & Odell, a firm he joined after returning from military service in World War II. Later he went into individual practice. He retired in the mid-1980's.
        Mr. Odell was born in Chicago. After graduating from the University of Chicago and its law school, he worked as a staff lawyer for the Securities and Exchange Commission in Washington.
        In the war, he enlisted in the Army, where he reached the rank of lieutenant colonel and won a Bronze Star.
        Surviving are his wife of 44 years, the former Laura Green; two sons, James C. of Millington, N.J., and Robert G. of Los Angeles; a brother, Harold of Dayton, Ohio, and two grandsons.

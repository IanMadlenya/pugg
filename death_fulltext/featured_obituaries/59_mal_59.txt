
        Claude Hill, a biographer of Bertolt Brecht who was a retired Rutgers University professor, died on Tuesday at Princeton Medical Center in New Jersey. He was 80 years old and lived in Princeton.
        He had stomach cancer, said a colleague, Ralph Ley.
        Mr. Hill was born in Berlin and studied at the University of Jena in Germany. He joined the German department at Rutgers in 1946, became director of graduate studies in German and retired in 1979.
        His biography and literary appraisal, "Bertolt Brecht," was published in 1974. He wrote eight other books.
        He is survived by his wife, Helen; a son, Thomas, of Washington, and three granddaughters.

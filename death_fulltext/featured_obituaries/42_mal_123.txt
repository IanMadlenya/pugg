        Max R. Spencer, vice president for operations of the world grain division of the Continental Grain Company, died on Saturday at Morristown Memorial Hospital in New Jersey. He was 68 years old and lived in Convent Station, N.J.
        He died of cancer, according to the Lanterman Funeral Home in Morristown.
        Mr. Spencer joined Continental Grain in 1962 as manager of its plant in Westwego, La., after having served 15 years with the Central Soya Company in Fort Wayne, Ind. From 1965 he served in Continental Grain's New York headquarters, becoming vice president in 1985.
        He was born in Fort Wayne and received a chemical engineering degree from Purdue University.
        Mr. Spencer is survived by his wife, the former Billie Mauk; a daughter, Julia Fischer of Pittsfield, Vt.; a brother, Roland, of La Grange, Ill., and a sister, Rosalind Bainter of Trotwood, Ohio.

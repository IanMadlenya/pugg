
        Clarence E. Bracebridge, director of Seventh-day Adventist Chaplaincy Ministries, died Saturday at Shady Grove Adventist Hospital in Rockville, Md. He was 55 years old and lived in Highland, Md.
        Mr. Bracebridge died of leukemia, a spokeswoman for the family said.
        Mr. Bracebridge, director of the ministries since 1987, was an officer of the National Conference on Ministry to the Armed Forces.
        Before working for the church he was for 21 years civilian chaplain at military installations in the South. Earlier he had been pastor to Adventist churches in the South and Middle West.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Arline Hendrick; a son, Ronald Lee Bracebridge of Lexington, S.C.; two daughters, Rhonda Kae, also of Lexington, and Rachelle Lynn Bracebridge-Chaney of Avon Park, Fla.; and four grandchildren.

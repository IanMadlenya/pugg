        Dr. Paul A. Paroski Jr., medical director for the department of ambulatory care at the Woodhull Medical and Mental Health Center in Brooklyn, died of complications from AIDS on Saturday at St. Vincent's Hospital and Medical Center in Manhattan. He was 37 years old and lived in Brooklyn.
        Dr. Paroski, who joined the Woodhull center five years ago, also served as secretary of Medical Associates of Woodhull, a health-care association. He was formerly the senior public-health physician of the New Jersey Department of Health's sexually transmitted diseases program.
        Dr. Paroski was a founder of the National Lesbian and Gay Health Education Foundation, the Lesbian and Gay People in Medicine, and the Gay and Lesbian Alcohol Services. He was chairman of ambulatory care services for the New York State AIDS Institute and also chairman of the institute's Ambulatory Care Medical Criteria Committee.
        Dr. Paroski was born in Buffalo and graduated from Canisius College and the School of Medicine of the State University of New York at Buffalo.
        He is survived by his parents, Dr. Paul A. and Dr. Jacqueline Paroski, and a sister, Dr. Elizabeth Barlog, all of Buffalo.
        His companion was William D. Fortune.

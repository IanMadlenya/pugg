        Charles H. Gross, a New York stockbroker for four decades, died of lung cancer Tuesday at Long Island Jewish Hospital. He was 67 years old and lived in Roslyn Estates, L.I.
        Mr. Gross, a private investor at his death, was managing partner of Newburger, Loeb & Company in the 1960's. Earlier he had been senior partner of his own concern, Gross & Company.
        Mr. Gross was a past president of the Jewish Institute for Geriatric Care, a former chairman of the Nassau-Suffolk Health Systems Agency and a member of the Long Island Health and Hospitals Planning Corporation.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Norma Sherry; three daughters, Laurie Goldstein of Bethesda, Md., Deborah, of San Francisco, and Amy, of Manhattan; a sister, Jeanne Donoghue of Melbourne, Australia, and three grandchildren.

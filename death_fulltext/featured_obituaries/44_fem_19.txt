        Fannie Gelb, a children's dress designer, died on Wednesday at Mount Sinai Medical Center in Manhattan. She was 94 years old and was a resident of the Jewish Home and Hospital in the Bronx.
        She died of cardiac arrest, her daughter-in-law, Barbara Gelb, said.
        She had several dress shops, the last one at 67th Street and Madison Avenue. Her husband, Daniel, died in 1960. She is survived by two sons, Harold, of Scarsdale, N.Y., and Arthur, of Manhattan, president of The New York Times Foundation; four grandchildren, and seven great-grandchildren.


        Sidney Sokol, a former United States Treasury Department official, died on Sunday at New York University Medical Center. He was 77 years old and lived in Manhatan.
        He died of cancer, his daughter Marilyn said.
        Mr. Sokol, a certified public accountant, was an assistant secretary of the Treasury Department in Washington from 1971 to 1973. From 1965 to 1971, he was commissioner of the Bureau of Accounts for the Treasury Department.
        His joined the Treasury Department in 1935. During World War II, he was a civilian employee with the Army.
        He received a Master of Arts from City College.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Evelyn Gold; two daughters, Marilyn Sokol and Bernice Kramer, both of Manhattan, and two grandchildren.

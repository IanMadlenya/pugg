        Arthur J. Lumsden, a former head of the Greater Hartford Chamber of Commerce and chairman of the publishing house of Dodd, Mead & Company, died of lung cancer Sunday at Bethesda Memorial Hospital in Boynton Beach, Fla. He was 68 years old and lived in Delray Beach, Fla., and West Hartford, Conn.
        From 1956 to 1981, he headed the Greater Hartford Chamber of Commerce, one of the nation's largest. He was also a founder of the Community Renewal Team, an antipoverty agency.
        After retiring from the chamber, he formed Arthur Lumsden Associates Inc., which coordinated the financing of private businesses in the Caribbean and Central America through the United States Agency for International Development and Caribbean/Central American Action Inc.
        He became chairman of Dodd, Mead in 1986.
        Mr. Lumsden was born in Lorain, Ohio, and graduated from the Case Institute of Technology in Cleveland.
        He is survived by his wife, Ruth; his daughter, Lynne, of Manhattan; a brother, Alexander, of Lancaster, Pa., and two sisters, Margaret L. Lorence and Florence L. Gay, both of Lorain.
        A funeral service will be held Friday at 11 A.M. at St. James Episcopal Church, 1018 Farmington Avenue, West Hartford.

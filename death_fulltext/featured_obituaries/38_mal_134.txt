        Winslow Martin, a retired management consultant for Arthur D. Little Inc. in Cambridge, Mass., died of cancer on Tuesday at his home in Lincoln, Mass. He was 68 years old and also lived in Grantham, N.H.
        Mr. Martin joined the consulting concern in 1960. From 1963 to 1970 he was managing director of its Zurich and London offices. He retired in 1986. He was a graduate of Dartmouth College and Harvard Business School.
        Mr. Martin served with the field artillery in the Pacific in World War II and won the Bronze Star.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Ann Baker; a daughter, Sandra Ripa of Bethlehem, Pa.; a son, Winslow Martin Jr., of Watertown, Mass., and two grandsons. Two sisters, Nancy Keeney of Sturbridge, Mass., and Anita White of Darien, Conn., and a brother, William B. of St. Petersburg, Fla., also survive.

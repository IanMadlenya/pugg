
        Henry A. Brachtl, a Manhattan securities lawyer and a former assistant United States Attorney, died on Monday at his home in Old Greenwich, Conn. He was 50.
        The cause of death was cancer, his wife, Martis, said.
        He taught at the New York University School of Law from 1974 to 1979. He was an assistant United States Attorney in Brooklyn from 1971 to 1976, the last two years as the deputy chief of the office's Civil Division.
        In addition to his wife, he is survived by his mother, Leona Y. Brachtl of Berwyn, Ill., and a brother, James R. Brachtl of Glen Ellyn, Ill.

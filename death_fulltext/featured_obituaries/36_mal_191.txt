        Howard A. Golieb, a stockbroker and account executive, died of pneumonia on Sunday at New York Hospital. He was 77 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Golieb was an account executive with Gruntal & Company Inc., a stockbrokerage firm in Manhattan. He had worked for the firm for about 15 years. He had previously been with the Ira Haupt Company, a stockbrokerage firm that has since gone out of business.
        Mr. Golieb was born in the Bronx and was a graduate of Yale University and New York University Law School.
        In World War II, he was a lawyer with the rank of lieutenant on the staff of the Army Adjutant General's office.
        His first wife, the former Nancy Judell, died in 1984.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Regina Lorch, and a stepdaughter, Jeanne Kassler of Manhattan.

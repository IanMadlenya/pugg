
        Frederick M. Glass, a former transportation industry executive, died yesterday at his home in Manhattan. He was 79.
        He died of cancer, said a family friend, Margie Gewirtz.
        From 1947 to 1949, Mr. Glass was president of Air Cargo Inc. From 1949 to 1955, he was director of aviation for the Port Authority of New York and New Jersey. In 1961 President John F. Kennedy named him chairman of his Task Force on National Aviation Goals.
        Mr. Glass was an executive vice president of the Hertz Corporation from 1960 to 1962 and president and chief executive of National Car Rental Systems Inc. from 1962 to 1965.
        He was with the Empire State Building Corporation from 1955 to 1960 and became vice chairman and chief executive. Later he was an investment banker, president of Prudential Funds Inc. and a financial management consultant.
        He was a former president of the Airport Operators Council and of the Wings Club, in mid-Manhattan, many of whose members are fliers or active in the aviation industry.
        Frederick Marion Glass 3d was born in Vaiden, Miss., and graduated from the University of Mississippi in 1934 and from Northwestern University law school. In World War II he became a colonel in the Air Transport Command and won the Bronze Star and the Legion of Merit.
        The boards he served on included those of International Banknote, Wackenhut, the Flight Safety Foundation and the College of Aviation.
        Mr. Glass was married in 1941 to Betsy Keller, who died in 1979.
        He is survived by his second wife, the former Marcia Hubbard, whom he married in 1980; two sons, Frederick M. Glass 4th of Atlanta and William Keller Glass of Washington Grove, Md.; a daughter, Barbara Richardson Glass of Atlanta, and seven grandchildren.
        Correction:  June 4, 1993, Friday
        An obituary yesterday about Frederick Glass, 79, a former transportation industry exective who died on Wednesday in Manhattan, misidentified a college on whose board he served. It is the College of Aeronautics in Flushing, Queens.


        Walter Crawford Bachman, a ship designer and marine engineer, died on Friday at Overlook Hospital in Summit, N.J. He was 79 years old and lived in Short Hills, N.J.
        He died of complications of respiratory insufficiency, a funeral home representative said.
        He was a former vice president and chief engineer of Gibbs & Cox Inc., a naval architectural firm, in Manhattan. He joined the firm in 1936, shortly after graduating from Lehigh University, and retired in 1970. He specialized in the design of ship-propulsion systems. He was a member of the American Society of Naval Engineers and the National Academy of Engineering.
        He is survived by a son, Van Cleaf Bachman of Lunenberg, Nova Scotia; a daughter, Elizabeth Ramjoue of Oberhaching, Germany; six grandchildren, and three great-grandchildren.

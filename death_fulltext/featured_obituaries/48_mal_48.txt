
        Frank Faso, a Daily News reporter for 33 years who investigated organized crime, died yesterday at John F. Kennedy Hospital in Lake Worth, Fla.. He was 54 years old and lived in Lake Worth.
        He died of a heart ailment that had disabled him, a family spokesman said.
        Mr. Faso was the newspaper's bureau chief at the Manhattan Criminal Courthouse in the 1980's. He was a native of Manhattan, a graduate of parochial schools here and had served in the Army in the Korean War.
        Surviving is his wife, Alicia; a son, Anthony, of Whitestone, Queens; a daughter, Leanna, of Lake Worth, and his mother, Frances, of Whitestone.

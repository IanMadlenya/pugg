
        E. Garrett Cleary, a lawyer in Rochester who had been a New York State prosecutor, a special investigator and a member of the Commission on Judicial Conduct, died on Aug. 24 at his family's summer home in Canandaigua, N.Y. He was 68 and lived in Pittsford, N.Y.
        The cause was cancer, said Martin Birmingham, a lifelong friend.
        Mr. Cleary was named to the judicial conduct commission, which polices the court system, in 1981. At his death, he was a senior partner in Harris Beach & Wilcox in Albany. He was a graduate of Albany Law School.
        Mr. Cleary is survived by his wife of 40 years, the former Patricia Coleman; three sons, Mark and James, both of Rochester, and Peter, of Pittsford; four daughters, Colleen, of Providence, R.I., Mary P. Cleary of Boston, Amy Mealey of Pittsfield, Mass., and Kathleen Bannan of Chicago, and six grandchildren.

        Jason Boe, a former President of the Oregon Senate who dominated the Legislature in the 1970's, died of a heart attack on Tuesday at University Hospital here. He was 61 years old and lived in Portland.
        Mr. Boe, a Democrat, served in the Legislature from 1965 to 1981. He presided over the Senate four times, in the sessions of 1973, 1975, 1977 and 1979. He ran unsuccessfully for the United States Senate in 1974, and gave up his State Senate seat in 1980 to run unsuccessfully for state treasurer.
        Originally an optometrist, he later owned an international consulting concern on government affairs, Jason Boe & Associates Inc., with offices in Taiwan and Korea.
        He is survived by his wife, Kathryn; three sons, two grandsons and a brother.


        Dr. James M. McDonough, an orthopedic surgeon in Morris Plains, N.J., for 25 years, died on Thursday at Morristown (N.J.) Memorial Hospital. He was 57 years old and lived in Morris Plains.
        He died of heart failure, a funeral home representative said.
        Dr. McDonough, a 1960 graduate of the St. Louis University School of Medicine, trained at St. Vincent's Hospital, New York Orthopedic Hospital and at Columbia-Presbyternian Hospital, all in Manhattan. He was a member of several professional organizations, including the American Academy of Orthopedic Surgeons.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Mary Ellen Fischer; two sons, Paul, of Winthrop, Mass., and Brian, of Morris Plains; two daughters, Laura E. Wilkie of Clinton, N.J., and Christine A. McDonough of Morris Plains, and a sister, Patricia Soldavini of Naples, Fla.

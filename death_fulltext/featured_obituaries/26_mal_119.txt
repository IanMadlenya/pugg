        Rupert Harris Johnson, the founder of R. H. Johnson & Company, a general securities firm and investment banking house, died of a heart attack Sunday at his home in Bal Harbour, Fla. He was 89 years old. Mr. Johnson founded the Manhattan-based company in 1927. In 1947 he founded and managed The Franklin Group of Mutual Funds, now one of the country's largest such organizations. He retired in 1956.  He is survived by his wife, the former Amybelle Siler; four daughters, Mary F. Furlong of Montclair, N.J., Florence M. Jacobson of Verona, N.J., Bonnie L. Sacerdote of Greenwich, Conn., and Suzanne O. Razor of Salt Lake City; three sons, Charles B., of Hillsborough, Calif., Andrew R. and Rupert H. Jr., both of San Mateo, Calif.; 33 grandchildren and 18 great-grandchildren.

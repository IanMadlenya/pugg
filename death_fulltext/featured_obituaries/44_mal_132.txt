        Walter Scott McPherson, a retired steamship executive, died on Monday at his home in Manhattan. He was six days short of his 106th birthday.
        Mr. McPherson died from a stroke, said his son-in-law, Adrian Farley.
        Mr. McPherson, a native of Oak Park, Ill., was senior vice president of the American Hawaiian Steamship Line when he retired in 1953. He had been with the company since he graduated from Williams College in 1907. He was a past president of the New York Shipping Association.
        During World War II he was a member of the War Shipping Board in Washington, which controlled all wartime shipping.
        A widower, he is survived by two daughters, Harriet Farley and Marjorie Adams, both of Greenwich, Conn.; four grandchildren, and eight great-grandchildren.

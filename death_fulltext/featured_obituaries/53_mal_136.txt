
        Horace P. Moulton, former vice president and general counsel of A.T.& T., died on Sunday at the Health Care Center in Cokesbury Village, a retirement center in Hockessin, Del. He was 83 years old and also had a home in Delray Beach, Fla.
        He died of cancer, his daughter, Anne Anderson, said.
        He began his career wth the Bell System as a lawyer for New England Telephone & Telegraph Company in Boston in 1944. In 1951 he moved to New York as a lawyer for A. T.&T. He was named vice president and general counsel of the company in 1955, a post he held until he retired in 1972.
        He then became a partner with the law firm of Cadwalader, Wickersham & Taft in Manhattan, where he remained until 1978.
        Mr. Moulton, who was born in Burlington, Vt., graduated from Dartmouth College in 1928 and from Harvard Law School in 1931.
        Besides his daughter, of Summit, N.J., he is survived by his wife, the former Elizabeth H. Munson; a son, Sherman R. 2d, of Fairfield, Conn., and five grandchildren.

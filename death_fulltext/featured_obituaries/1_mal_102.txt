        Knut Olav Frydenlund, Foreign Minister for four Labor Party Governments since 1973, died today after suffering a cerebral hemorrhage. He was 59 years old.
        Mr. Frydenlund was stricken Wednesday at Oslo's Fornebu Airport after returning from a Nordic Council meeting in Helsinki, Finland. He died at Ullevaal Hospital today.
        Mr. Frydenlund, born in Drammen, received a law degree from Oslo University in 1950. He was appointed to the Ministry of Foreign Affairs in 1952, passed his foreign service examination the next year and then served in the Norwegian Embassy in Bonn.
        He returned to the Foreign Ministry, serving as executive officer from 1956 to 1962. For several years, he was personal secretary to the late Foreign Minister Halvard Lange.
        Mr. Frydenlund was a Labor Party representative in Parliament, beginning in 1969. He first was appointed Foreign Minister in 1973, and was reappointed in 1976, 1981 and 1986.
        Trade Minister Kurt Mosbakk is temporarily filling in as acting Foreign Minister. Culture Minister Hallvard Bakke is acting Prime Minister.

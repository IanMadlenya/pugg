        Jack Chernus, a retired psychiatrist and medical writer, died of colon cancer on Oct. 18 at his home in Sarasota, Fla. He was 77 years old.
        Dr. Chernus was chief of psychiatry at St. Barnabas Hospital in Livingston, N.J., from 1962 to 1976.
        He was a graduate of New York University and Glasgow Medical School in Scotland. He also trained at the New York Psychoanalytic Institute.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Dorothy Morrison; a son, Roy, and a daughter, Margot, both of Manhattan; and a sister, Ida Schein of Long Branch, N.J.

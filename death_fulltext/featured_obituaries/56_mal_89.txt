
        Alfred E. Moon, a former budget director of Nassau County and budget director of New York City's Housing and Development Administration, died on Sunday at his home in Silver Spring, Md. He was 73 years old.
        He died of lung cancer, his family said.
        Mr. Moon, who was born in St. Paul, worked in several Federal agencies and in 1957 became a deputy state budget director in New York. He later was a deputy Nassau County executive and budget director and for five years was vice president for business and financial affairs at Adelphi University in Garden City, L.I. He was named budget director for the New York City housing agency in 1972 and retired in the late 1970's.
        Surviving are his wife, Doris Pender Moon; a son, Peter, of Hartford; three daughters, Mary Jo, of Kensington, Md., and Linda Prince and Susan Moon, both of Gaithersburg, Md., and two grandchildren.

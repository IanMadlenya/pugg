
        William S. Borden, a retired vice president of Trans World Airlines, died on Tuesday at St. Peter's Medical Center in New Brunswick, N.J. He was 65 years old and lived in Somerset, N.J.
        He died of a heart attack, a family spokeswoman said.
        Mr. Borden worked for T.W.A. for 37 years and was vice president of in-flight services when he retired in 1988. He then joined the faculty at Rutgers University's Institute of Management and Labor Relations as an adjunct professor.
        He is survived by his wife, Joan; a daughter, Barbara Mack of San Diego; two sons, William, of Charlotte, N.C., and Brian, of New Brunswick; two brothers, Benjamin, of Orlando, Fla., and Robert, of Marysville, Ohio; a sister, Rebecca Schalip of Marysville, and one granddaughter.

        Cordell D. Meeks Sr., the first black district judge in Kansas and the first black in state history to be elected to a countywide office, died Monday. He was 73 years old.
        Judge Meeks was a Wyandotte County commissioner from 1950 to 1973. He served eight years as a Wyandotte County District Court judge, retiring in 1981.
        He also helped organize the first black-owned bank in Kansas, the Douglass State Bank, serving as a director, vice president and vice chairman.

        James J. Donovan, a lawyer who was president of the Maritime Law Association of the United States from 1978 to 1980, died of cancer Saturday at his home in Harrison, N.Y. He was 73 years old.
        Mr. Donovan, a native of Manhattan, received bachelor's and law degrees from Fordham University. He was a founder of the law firm of Donovan, Maloof, Walsh & Repetto, and was associated with it until his death.
        He is survived by his wife, Virginia; two sons, James Jr., of Los Angeles, and Michael, of Worcester, Mass.; a daughter, Patricia Duff of Hastings-on-Hudson, N.Y., and five grandchildren.

        Mahlon Apgar 3d, a real-estate broker who specialized in large-scale land acquisitions, died of cancer Monday at Mease Hospital-Dunedin in Clearwater, Fla. He was 85 years old and lived in Clearwater.
        Mr. Apgar, who was born in Jersey City and was educated at Newark Academy and New York University, was instrumental in arranging the sale of headquarters tracts in New Jersey to such major corporations as I.B.M., A.T.& T. and American Cyanamid.
        He is survived by a son, Mahlon 4th, of Baltimore, and three grandchildren.

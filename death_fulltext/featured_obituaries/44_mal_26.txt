
       Brewster H. Morris, a career Foreign Service officer who was Ambassador to Chad from 1963 to 1967, died on Monday at his home in Tiburon, Calif. He was 81 years old. His brother, Theodore, said he died of Parkinson's disease.
        His brother, Theodore, said he died of Parkinson's disease.
        Mr. Morris, a member of a prominent Philadelphia family, was a 12th-generation descendant of Elder William Brewster, the minister on the Mayflower when it landed at Plymouth, Mass., in 1620.
        Mr. Morris, who was born in Bryn Mawr, Pa., was a Phi Beta Kappa graduate of Haverford College and received a degree in business literature from New College at Oxford. After traveling around the world for a year he joined the Provident Trust Company in Philadelphia in 1934 as an investment adviser but soon decided to apply for the Foreign Service.
        His first post was in Montreal in 1937, and he went on to serve in Vienna, Berlin, Stockholm, Moscow, London and Washington before retiring in 1968.
        His wife, the former Margaret Downes, a fellow Foreign Service officer whom he married in 1948, died in 1985. His only immediate survivor is his brother, who lives in Villanova, Pa.

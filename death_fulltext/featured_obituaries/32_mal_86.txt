        Joseph P. Carucci, a stockbroker, died of cardiac arrest Monday at North Shore University Hospital in Manhasset, L.I. He was 76 years old and lived in Kings Point, L.I.
        Mr. Carucci was president of the Carr Securities Corporation, an over-the-counter securities concern on Wall Street. A 1938 graduate of New York University, he began his career as a messenger with Western Union on Wall Street.
        He later became a partner in the over-the-counter securities firm of J. K. Rice. In 1956, he founded Carr and was president and chief executive at his death. He was also a member of the board of governors of the Human Resources Center, in Albertson, L.I. He served in the Army in World War II.
        He is survived by his wife, Clara; two sons, Walter, of Plandome, L.I., and Mitchell, of Kings Point; a sister, Anna Bosco, of Valley Stream, L.I., and a grandson.

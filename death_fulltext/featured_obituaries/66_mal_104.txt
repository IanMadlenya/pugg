
        Walter J. Bayer, a former judge of the Criminal Court of New York City, died on Monday at the Lawrenceville Nursing Home in Lawrenceville, N.J. He was 87 years old.
        Judge Bayer died of natural causes, said his son, Walter J. Bayer 3d.
        Judge Bayer was born in Manhattan, where he was a lifelong resident until he moved to Lawrenceville three years ago. He graduated from Stuyvesant High School in 1923. He received his law degree at St. John's in 1928.
        From 1928 to 1952 Mr. Bayer had a general law practice in Manhattan. He was also active in Democratic Party politics and Polish-American organizations. He was appointed to the bench in the Criminal Court in 1952 and served until 1972, when he retired.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Helen Koszalka; his son, Walter, of Lawrenceville, and two grandchildren.

        David Siegal, a labor leader in the culinary trades, died of complications of Parkinson's disease on Monday at his home in Brooklyn. He was 83 years old.
        Mr. Siegal, a native of Poland, was president for over 40 years of Local 1 of the Dining Room Employees Union and a predecessor waiters' union. He led about 11,000 waitresses, waiters, busboys, cashiers and coat checkers in the union in strikes in 1965 and 1972.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Charlotte Meyers; four sons, Harvey, of Dayton, Ohio, Barry, of Greensboro, N.C., Lester, of Brooklyn, and Joel, of San Francisco, and three grandchildren.

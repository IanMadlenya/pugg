
        Norman F. Washburne, a professor of sociology at the Newark campus of Rutgers University, died on Saturday at Reed Memorial Hospital in Richmond, Ind. He was 64 years old and lived in West Windsor, N.J.
        He died of complications of lymphoma, his family said.
        Dr. Washburne, a native of Manhattan, was a graduate of the University of Missouri and held a master's degree from the New School and a doctorate from Washington University in St. Louis. He joined the Rutgers faculty in 1970 and was in his third one-year term as president of the Rutgers council of the American Association of University Professors.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Bernice Johnson; two sons, Theodore, of Mesa, Ariz., and Christopher, of Manhattan; a daughter, Shelley Masar of Urbana, Ill.; a stepson, Daniel Hough of Hopewell, N.J.; a stepdaughter, Sharon Martynowski of Cleveland; his mother, Vera Megroth of West Windsor; a sister, Antonia Flint of Princeton, N.J., and 13 grandchildren.

        Norman Applezweig, a consulting biochemist and the founder of Progenics Inc., a health care research and development company, died of a heart attack Monday at Columbia-Presbyterian Medical Center. He was 70 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Applezweig was a graduate of New York University and founded Progenics in 1982.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Lenore Sanders; a brother, Mortimer, of Cambridge, Mass.; two daughters, Erica, of Manhattan, and Barbara Hendrickson of Berkeley, Calif.; a stepson, David Rule of Manhattan, and two grandchildren.

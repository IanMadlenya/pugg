
        Dr. John H. Hubbard, a neurosurgeon and professor from Ridgewood, N.J., died on Thursday in Cooper Hospital-University Medical Center in Camden, N.J. He was 56 years old.
        Dr. Hubbard died of head injuries after he fell while vacationing at the New Jersey shore, his family said.
        He had been chief of neurosurgery at Pascack Valley Hospital in Westwood, N.J., and at Holy Name Hospital in Teaneck, N.J. More recently, he had practiced at Hackensack Medical Center and at Montefiore Medical Center in the Bronx. He also taught neurosurgery at the Albert Einstein College of Medicine and had previously taught at the University of Pennsylvania.
        Born in Detroit, he was a graduate of Duke University and Tulane University Medical School.
        He is survived by his wife, Irene Matis Hubbard; a daughter, Kimberly, and two sons, Michael and Christopher, all of Ridgewood, and his mother, Betha, and sister, Janice, both of Acworth, Ga.

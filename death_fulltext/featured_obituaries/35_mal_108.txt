        Leland H. Watson, a lighting designer for many years, died of leukemia on Dec. 8 at his home in Lafayette, Ind. He was 63 years old.
        Mr. Watson worked on 40 Broadway productions, including ''The Diary of Anne Frank,'' ''A View From the Bridge'' and ''Suddenly Last Summer.'' In addition, he lighted productions at regional theaters, industrial shows and more than 60 operas. He was on the staff of CBS Television for 12 years.
        Mr. Watson was on the faculty of Purdue University and was the author of ''The Handbook of Lighting Design,'' which is scheduled to be published in March.
        He was born in Charleston, Ill., and was a graduate of the University of Iowa. He received a Master of Fine Arts degree from Yale University in 1952.
        He is survived by his parents, Dallas and Hazel Watson of Charleston.

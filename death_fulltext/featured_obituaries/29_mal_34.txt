        Kurt A. Solmssen, a former vice president of Smith, Kline & French Laboratories, died of heart failure Saturday at his home in Philadelphia. He was 84 years old.
        Mr. Solmssen joined the pharmaceutical concern in 1948 and later became head of its international division. After his retirement in 1967 he was associated with the securities firm of Butcher and Singer in Philadelphia.
        Mr. Solmssen, a native of Berlin and the son of the chairman of the Deutsche Bank, came to the United States in 1936. In World War II he was an analyst with the Securities and Exchange Commission.
        He was active in several civic activities in Philadelphia and served on the City Planning Commission.
        Mr. Solmssen is survived by his wife of 62 years, the former Marguerite Warschauer; two sons, Arthur and Peter Solmssen, both of Philadelphia; a brother, Max, of Summit, N.J., and five grandchildren.

        John K. Schemmer, a New York lawyer, died of cancer Monday at his home in Pawling, N.Y. He was 70 years old and also lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Schemmer was a partner in the law firm of Carter, Ledyard & Milburn and was appointed by a Federal judge as permanent receiver of two Bahamas-based companies that were involved in the tangled affairs of Robert L. Vesco, the financier who is a fugitive from justice.
        Mr. Schemmer was also a former president of the Coca-Cola Bottling Company of New York and a former partner in the private investment firm of J. H. Whitney & Company.
        He was born in Colby, Wis., and was a graduate of the University of Notre Dame and of Harvard Law School.
        Mr. Schemmer is survived by his wife, the former Rose Gallagher; a son, John, of Boston, and two grandchildren.

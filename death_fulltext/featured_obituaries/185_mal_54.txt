
        Guilford Dudley Jr., a Tennessee insurance executive who was an ambassador to Denmark in the Nixon administration and a Nixon aide, died on Thursday at his home in Nashville. He was 94.
        Mr. Dudley, a Republican, was president of the Life and Casualty Insurance Company of Tennessee before becoming ambassador in 1969.
        In 1971 he resigned as ambassador, becoming a business consultant to the Nixon White House for a time. He then returned to his insurance company and became its chairman.
        He was later a trustee of the body that liquidated Nixon's campaign funds after his resignation as president.
        Mr. Dudley and his wife, Jane Anderson Dudley, entertained politicians and celebrities at many events in the Reagan years in Washington, notably parties after Ronald Reagan's first and second inaugurations.
        Mr. Dudley was born in Nashville, grew up there and received a bachelor's degree from Vanderbilt University. He was a Navy officer in the Pacific during World War II.
        In addition to his wife, he is survived by two sons from an earlier marriage that ended in divorce, Guilford III of Abiquiu, N.M., and Robert, of Nashville; a daughter from his second marriage, Trevania Dudley Henderson of Boston; four grandchildren; and four great-grandchildren.

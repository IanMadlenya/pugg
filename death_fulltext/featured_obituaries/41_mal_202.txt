        Mike Jones, a boxing manager since the mid-1970's, died Tuesday at his home in West Hempstead, L.I. He was 55 years old.
        Mr. Jones was conferring by telephone with a lawyer about a forthcoming fight between one of his boxers, Alex Stewart, and Mike Tyson, when he suffered a heart attack, Mr. Stewart's co-manager, Jim Fennell, said.
        Mr. Jones was also manager of the world-ranked welterweight Glenwood Brown.
        Mr. Jones entered boxing in 1976 as co-manager with Dennis Rappaport of Gerry Cooney, Ronnie Harris and Howard Davis. He later managed Billy Costello, who won the World Boxing Council superlightweight title in 1984.
        Mr. Jones is survived by his wife, Stella, two daughters, Rita and Gwen, and a son, Adam.

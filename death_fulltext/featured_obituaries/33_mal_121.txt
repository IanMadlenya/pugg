        Dr. Peter Dineen, a professor and vice chairman of the department of surgery at New York Hospital-Cornell Medical Center in Manhattan, died of cancer there Tuesday. He was 64 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Dr. Dineen, a graduate of Cornell University Medical College, began his medical career in 1947 as an intern at New York Hospital. He was an authority on infections and held many positions at the hospital over the years, including director of affiliations, chairman of the operating room committee and of ambulatory surgery, director of the surgeons assistant program and chairman of the committee on infections.
        He was also an attending surgeon at New York Hospital and at North Shore University Hospital. He was a member of numerous professional organizations and wrote more than 200 papers, including many about infections.
        He is survived by his wife, Anne, and a son, Michael, of Manhattan.


        Jeff Walker, vice president of creative services for Polo/Ralph Lauren, died on Monday at Lenox Hill Hospital. He was 41 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        He died of pulmonary hypertension, said Dr. James R. Wilentz.
        He joined the company in 1983 as director of creative services and was named vice president in 1988. His work included showroom design as well as creating detailed displays for the Madison Avenue store and other stores owned by the corporation. In 1988, he received the National Association of Display Industries award for visual display in the Madison Avenue store.
        Mr. Walker was art director for Wilkes-Bashford, a men's clothing store in San Francisco for five years before he joined Polo/Ralph Lauren.
        He is survived by two brothers and a sister, all of Seattle.

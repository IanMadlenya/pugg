        J. W. Todd Ferretti, a self-employed international commodities trader, died of complications from brain surgery Sunday at Montefiore Hospital in the Bronx. He was 63 years old and lived in Bronxville, N.Y.
        Mr. Ferretti was a graduate of Brown University. Before becoming a commodities trader, he was president of Smoke-X Inc., a New York manufacturer of equipment to treat and control air and water pollution. He held several patents for devices to control water pollution.
        He is survived by his wife, Patricia; four daughters, Carolyn, of Eastchester, N.Y., Susan Lapham of Bronxville, Patricia Symonds of Crompond, N.Y., and Elizabeth Roberts of Tuckahoe, N.Y.; a sister, Roma Fanton of Fairfield, Conn., and four granddaughters.

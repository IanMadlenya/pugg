        Percy Bugbee, former chief executive of the National Fire Protection Association, died Tuesday at his home in Plymouth, Mass. He was 90 years old.
        Mr. Bugbee, a graduate of the Massachusetts Institute of Technology, was known internationally for his efforts to reduce the loss of life and property caused by fires. He was honored by fire service organizations in the United States Britain, France, Canada and Australia.
        He devoted 48 years to fire prevention and helped create ''Sparky, the fire dog,'' a component of fire safety education for children. He was a member of many fire prevention organizations.
        He is survived by his wife, Wilhelmina; a daughter, Margery Atherton, of Plymouth; two sons, Richard, of Rutherford, N.J., and Alan, of West Simsbury, Conn.; a brother, Harold, of Woburn, Mass., and nine grandchildren.

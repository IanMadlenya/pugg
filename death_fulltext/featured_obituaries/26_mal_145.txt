        David Shirra Meiklejohn, a retired financial executive, died of a heart attack Saturday at Greenwich (Conn.) Hospital. He was 80 years old and lived in Greenwich.
        A 1931 graduate of Williams College, Mr. Meiklejohn held several banking positions before becoming director of the New York City Department of Commerce in 1940. He was executive secretary to Mayor Fiorello H. La Guardia from 1945 to 1946.
        He was treasurer and executive vice president of the American Machine and Foundry Company and in 1970 joined Integrated Resources Inc., of Manhattan, as executive vice president and chief financial officer. He retired in 1982. He was a director of several companies, including the Putnam Trust Company and the W. J. Voit Rubber Corporation. During World War II, he was a Lieutenant Commander in the Naval Reserve.
        He is survived by a daughter, Shirra Wilson of Guilford, Conn.; a brother, Robert, of Greenwich, and four grandchildren.

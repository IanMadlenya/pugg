        Lester D. Egbert, a former chairman of Brown, Crosby & Company, a Manhattan-based insurance brokerage firm, and a former trustee of Columbia University, died Sunday at Montclair (N.J.) Community Hospital. He was 95 years old and lived in Montclair.
        Mr. Egbert, who was born in Jersey City, was the son of Dr. James Chidester Egbert, a professor of classics for many years at Columbia. Lester Egbert graduated from Columbia College in 1914.
        After joining Brown, Crosby, he became a director in 1924 and rose to be president and chairman before retiring from the firm, which was merged with Frank B. Hall & Company.
        In 1921 Mr. Egbert was married to Beatrice Valerie Cook. She died in 1982. He is survived by two sons, John Pennington Egbert of Nutley, N.J., and Richard Cook Egbert of Greenwich, Conn.; five grandchildren, and two great-grandchildren.

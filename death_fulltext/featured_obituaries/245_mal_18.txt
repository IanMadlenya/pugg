
        D. Nelson Adams, a former managing partner of Davis Polk & Wardwell, one of the nation's most prominent law firms, died on Thursday at his home in Barneveld, N.Y. He was 97 and also had a home in Manhattan.
        The death was confirmed by his son, Daniel N. Adams Jr.
        A specialist in corporate tax law, Mr. Adams spent his entire career at Davis Polk, becoming its managing partner in 1970. At his death he was a senior counsel there. Davis Polk now has offices in New York and nine other cities, with more than 600 lawyers.
        Daniel Nelson Adams, known as Nelson, was born in Manhattan on Dec. 30, 1909. He earned bachelor's degrees in history and English from Yale in 1932 and a law degree from Harvard in 1935. From 1935 to 1936, he was a clerk for Judge Learned Hand of the United States Court of Appeals for the Second Circuit. In World War II he served in the Army Air Corps.
        Mr. Adams joined Davis Polk in 1936, becoming a partner in 1949. He became head of the firm's tax department the next year and 20 years later was named managing partner. He retired in 1977.
        Mr. Adams's clients included J. P. Morgan, Morgan Stanley and Brown Brothers Harriman. He was also known for helping expand the firm's diversity in hiring.
        Mr. Adams's wife, the former Diana Gerli, died in 1995. Besides his son, of Barneveld, he is survived by two grandsons.


        Myron A. Ellis, a lawyer for almost 60 years, died on Tuesday at New York Hospital in Manhattan. He was 82 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        He died of heart failure, his daughter, Patricia, said.
        Mr. Ellis, a graduate of St. John's University Law School, formed the firm Ellis, Ellis & Ellis with his brothers Emil and Jonas. The firm specialized in entertainment law, and one of its major clients was the Associated Booking Corporation, a talent booking agency. Mr. Ellis retired in 1987.
        He is survived by his wife, Rita Kaye, an interior designer; a son, Richard, a lawyer, of Manhattan; his daughter, Patricia, a television news producer in Washington; two sisters, Charlotte Ellis and Millicent Selsam, both of Manhattan, and two grandchildren.

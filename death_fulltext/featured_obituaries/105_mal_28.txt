
        Dick Jurgens, a composer and big-band leader, died on Thursday at Sutter General Hospital here. He was 85.
        During World War II, he had three tunes simultaneously on the "Lucky Strike  Hit Parade." He was a co-writer of "Elmer's Tune," "One Dozen Roses" and "Careless."
        Jurgens served in the Marines during World War II, organizing and leading a  band that performed in the Pacific islands.
        He is survived by a son, a daughter and a sister.
        His marriage tot he former Marian Davidson ended in divorce.


        George William Staempfli, founder of Staempfli Gallery in Manhattan, died on Thursday at the Anne Arundel Medical Center in Annapolis, Md. He was 89.
        Mr. Staempfli was born in Bern, Switzerland, in 1910 and moved to the United States in 1935, taking a job at M. Knoedler & Company in Manhattan. He was curator of paintings at the Museum of Fine Arts in Houston from 1954 to 1956.
        In 1959 he founded the Staempfli Gallery and showed the work of a variety of European and American artists, including Salvador Dali, Paul Delvaux, David Park, Elmer Bischoff, Joan Brown and George Rickey. He retired to Annapolis in 1992.  He was a painter and showed his work in Europe and the United States.
        He is survived by two daughters, Candida S. Steel of Annapolis and  Fordyce S. Williams of Worcester, Mass., and a son, G. Gamble Staempfli of Brooklyn; three brothers, Edward, of Berlin, Robert, of Homburg, Germany, and Beat Hirt of Basel, Switzerland; a sister, Eleonore Hirt of Paris, and a grandchild.

        William N. Johnston, a marine engineer and business executive, died of cancer Thursday at his home in Short Hills, N.J. He was 67 years old.
        Mr. Johnston, a graduate of Auburn University and the Massachusetts Institute of Technology, was the retired president of the American Bureau of Shipping, a 127-year-old international ship classification concern that sets construction standards for ships and offshore-drilling rigs.
        He was also the retired chairman of the ABS Group of Companies, which is affiliated with the shipping bureau. A mechanical and marine engineer who spent 36 years with the company, he joined it in 1951 as a surveyor, became president in 1976 and chairman in 1979. He retired in 1987. Mr. Johnston belonged to many organizations, including the Maritime Association of the Port of New York.
        He is survived by his wife Kathryn; a daughter, Kathryn, of Miami; three sons, William, of Detroit, Gregory, of Jacksonville, Fla., and Paul, of New Orleans, and five grandchildren.

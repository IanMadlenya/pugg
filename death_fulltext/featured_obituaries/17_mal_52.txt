        Edward Kramer, a former vice president and general manager of the Eastern region of the International Ladies Garment Workers Union, died Wednesday at Englewood (N.J.) Hospital of injuries sustained in an automobile accident and what was apparently a stroke. He was 75 years old and lived in Fort Lee, N.J.
        Mr. Kramer, who was a graduate of City College and New York Law School, retired as vice president in the New Jersey region of the garment workers union. He was a former vice president of the New Jersey state A.F.L.-C.I.O and had served on the board of trustees of Rutgers University. He was also a director of the National Conference of Christians and Jews and participated in civil rights marches in Washington and Selma, Ala.
        He is survived by his wife, Pearl; a son, Arthur, of Palisades Park, N.J.; a daughter, Susan Romoff of Plano, Tex., and four grandchildren.

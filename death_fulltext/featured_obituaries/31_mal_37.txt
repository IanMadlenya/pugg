        Raphael G. Scobey, a Manhattan lawyer who specialized in the entertainment business, died of lymphoma yesterday at the White Plains Hospital Medical Center. He was 72 years old and lived in Scarsdale, N.Y.
        At his death Mr. Scobey was a partner in the firm of Battle Fowler, which he joined in 1981 to found its entertainment department. From 1955 to 1981 he was a partner at Marshall, Bratter, Greene, Allison & Tucker and taught legal studies at Manhattanville College.
        He was a founder, first president and board member of the Creative Arts Rehabilitation Center, a Manhattan agency that offers arts therapy to adults and children with emotional illneses. He was a former board member of Circle in the Square.
        He is survived by his wife, Joan; two sons, Richard G., of Washington, and David M., of Ann Arbor, Mich.; a sister, Dena Justin of Berkeley, Calif., and a grandson.

        Richard Harland Norwood, a former executive with Rockefeller Center Inc., died Tuesday at Monmouth Medical Center in Long Branch, N.J., after suffering a heart embolism. He was 73 years old and lived in Bay Head, N.J.
        Mr. Norwood, a 1941 graduate of Columbia University Law School, joined the law firm of Milbank, Tweed, Hadley & McCloy in 1946 after serving in the Army in World War II. In 1959, he joined Rockefeller Center Inc., now known as the Rockefeller Group, and was later named vice president and general counsel. He retired in 1982.
        He was a director of several subsidiaries, including Radio City Music Hall Productions Inc. and the Rockefeller Center Management Corporation. He was also a former trustee of the Hospital Center at Orange (N.J.).
        He is survived by a son, Richard, of Little Silver, N.J.; two daughters, Brooks Hallock of Pound Ridge, N.Y., and Elizabeth Callahan of Chester, N.J., and five grandchildren.

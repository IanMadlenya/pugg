        A. Donald MacKinnon, a lawyer with Milbank, Tweed, Hadley, & McCloy for nearly 50 years, died on June 21 at the Seabrook Health Center in Hilton Head Island, S.C. He was 94 years old and moved to Hilton Head recently from Summit, N.J.
        The cause of death was septicemia, an infection of the blood, a spokeswoman for the firm said.
        Mr. MacKinnon graduated from Columbia Law School in 1922 and in 1923 became associated with a firm that merged to become Milbank, Tweed, Hope & Webb. He later became a partner.
        Mr. MacKinnon was president of the James Foundation from 1941 until its dissolution in 1964 and president of the Columbia Law School Alumni Association from 1958 to 1960. He was on the executive committee of the City Bar Association from 1952 to 1956.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Cornelia Evans; two daughters, Nancy London of Greenbrae, Calif., and Donna Flom of Lake Wylie, S.C.; three grandchildren, and a great-grandchild.

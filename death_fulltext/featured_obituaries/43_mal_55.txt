        Eugene F. Black, a former Michigan Supreme Court justice, died Saturday at his home in Port Huron, Mich. He was 87 years old. The cause of death was not announced. Justice Black was a member of the State Supreme Court from 1955 until 1972, when he returned to private practice. He was Attorney General of Michigan from 1946 to 1948. He was a partner in the law firm of Black & Black with his son Douglas and later his grandsons Bryan and David from 1972 until his death.
        Justice Black was born in Marine City, Mich., and attended the Detroit College of Law. He was in private practice with the firm of Steward & Black in Port Huron from 1922 to 1942, when he left to serve in the Navy.
        He returned to practice law with the firm of Black, Bush & Bradt in Port Huron in 1944. In 1954 and 1955, he served on the St. Clair County Circuit Court.
        A widower, he is survived by a daughter, Susan Land of Port Huron; two sons, Douglas, also of Port Huron, and Garry of Marysville, Mich.; and 14 grandchildren.

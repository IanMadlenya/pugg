
        David Lubart, a retired senior partner in the Manhattan law firm Stroock, Stroock & Lavan, died on Thursday at Mount Sinai Medical Center. He was 76 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        He died of multiple myeloma, said his son, Thomas.
        Mr. Lubart retired about 10 years ago when he was stricken with cancer but remained a counsel with the law firm. He joined Stroock, Stroock & Lavan in 1942 and became a senior partner in 1954. After graduating from St. Johns University Law School in 1938, he became an associate in the law firm Paskus, Gordon & Hyman.
        He served on the boards of numerous companies, including Browarn, Ltd., the Baldwin Securities Corporation, the Lamda Electronics Corporation, the Malt-Diastase Company, Oriniala Inc., Temco Service Industries and Veeco Instruments Inc.
        He was also a vice president and trustee of American Friends of Alliance Israelite Universelle, president and trustee of the Tenmain Foundation, secretary of the Nerken Foundation and a trustee of Maimonides Medical Center in Brooklyn.
        In addition to his son, Thomas, of Manhattan, he is survived by his wife, the former Muriel Alpert; a sister, Belle Goldstein of Miami, and two granddaughters.

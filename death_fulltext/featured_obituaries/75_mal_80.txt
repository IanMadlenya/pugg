
        Leo H. Arnstein, a Chicago trial lawyer and partner in the law firm of Arnstein & Lehr, died on April 15 at his home in Glencoe, Ill. Mr. Arnstein was 86 years old.
        He died in his sleep, a spokeswoman for the law firm said.
        Mr. Arnstein, though retired, remained active in the firm until his death. Among its clients were Sears Roebuck & Company, Whirlpool Corporation, Kroehler Manufacturing Compna. and The Kellstadt Foundation. Mr. Arnstein served on the board of directors of Sears.
        Born in Chicago, Mr. Arnstein received both undergraduate and law degrees from the University of Chicago.  He joined the law firm, then named Adler, Lederer & Kahn, in 1928. Mr. Arnstein served as the director of the Chicago Bar Foundation from 1972 to 1980.
        He is survived by three daughters, Julie Ellison, Marcia Franklin and Nicki Wilken, five grandchildren, and one great-grandchild.


        David Fallek, a retired lawyer and New York City Police Inspector, died on Wednesday at North Broward Medical Center in Pompano Beach, Fla. He was 77 years old and lived in Pompano Beach.
        He died of cancer, his family said.
        Mr. Fallek served 30 years on the police force, then worked 15 years as a private lawyer in Brooklyn representing police officers and organizations before retiring five years ago.
        Among his police duties, he was responsible for keeping order in front of the Soviet Mission to the United Nations, the site of many protests.
        He earned his law degree studying nights at Brooklyn Law School while on the police force.
        Surviving are his wife of 41 years, the former Mickey Mendelson; a son, Scott, of Huntington Beach, Fla.; two daughters, Sheri Goldman of Dix Hills, L.I., and Lori Falleck of Pompano Beach; a brother, Joseph, of Roslyn, L.I.; a sister, Bess Storch of Maryland, and two grandchildren.

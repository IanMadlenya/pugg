
        Dr. Sidney H. Silverman, a specialist in obstetrics and gynecology who practiced for 45 years in Brooklyn and Queens and on Long Island, died on Sept. 27 at his home in Boca Raton, Fla. He was 75 and had lived in Florida since retiring in 1990.
        The cause of death was not immediately disclosed.
        Born in Brooklyn, Dr. Silverman was a longtime member of the staff of Maimonides Medical Center in Brooklyn. He was affiliated with other hospitals in the region, including North Shore, Long Island Jewish, Peninsula and Franklin General.
        During World War II, he was a medic in the infantry in Europe. He was awarded the Purple Heart twice, his family said, and won the Bronze Star for bravery under fire in France.
        After graduating from Lafayette College in Easton, Pa., he received his medical degree in 1951 from the University of Rochester.
        He is survived by his wife, Donna; two sons, Dr. Matthew Silverman of Manhasset Hills, N.Y., and Andrew Silverman of Burlingame, Calif.; a daughter, Debby Kessler of Manhattan; a sister, Beverly Sills Greenough of Manhattan, who is chairwoman of Lincoln Center for the Performing Arts; a brother, Stanley S. of Boca Raton, and seven grandchildren.

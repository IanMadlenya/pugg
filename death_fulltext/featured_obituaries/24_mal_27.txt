        Lyman C. Thunfors, a retired business executive, died of cancer Monday at his home in Bridgeport, Conn. He was 85 years old.
        For 18 years Mr. Thunfors was executive vice president and a director of Jenkins Brothers, a valve manufacturer in Beacon Falls, Conn. He retired in 1968. Previously he had been president of the Rensselaer Valve Company in Troy, N.Y.
        After Mr. Thunfors retired, he worked in the International Executive Service Corps, which sends retired executives to developing countries. He also worked in Score, a program of the Federal Small Business Administration that helps people start businesses.
        Surviving are his wife, Frances; a son, C. Randolph, of West Stockbridge, Mass.; three daughters, Jacquelyn, of Wilton, Conn., Patricia Dann of Hammondsport, N.Y., and Marilyn Bremser of Litchfield, Conn., 13 grandchildren and 14 great-grandchildren.

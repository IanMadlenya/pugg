        E. Robert Livernash, professor emeritus at Harvard Business School, died of cancer Monday at the Brigham and Women's Hospital in Boston. He was 77 years old.
        Dr. Livernash, an authority on collective bargaining and labor relations, was a member of the business school's faculty from 1953 to 1976. He directed the first study of collective bargaining in the steel industry at the behest of President Eisenhower in 1959.
        Dr. Livernash was named as Harvard's first Alfred J. Weatherhead Jr. Professor of Business Administration in 1965. He was also the first president of the Iran Center for Management Studies in Teheran from 1972 to 1975.
        Dr. Livernash was born in Fort Collins, Colo., and was graduated from the University of Colorado in 1932. He taught economics at Tufts University from 1932 to 1934. He earned his doctorate from Harvard in 1941.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Virginia Hall, and two sons, Stephen E. Livernash of Arlington, Mass., and Robert T. Livernash of Washington.

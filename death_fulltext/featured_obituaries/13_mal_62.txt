        William E. Woodman, a retired insurance executive, died of heart failure Saturday at his home in Fort Myers, Fla. He was 68 years old and also lived in Short Hills, N.J.
        Born in Cortland, N.Y., Mr. Woodman graduated from Wesleyan University. For more than 30 years, he worked for Frank B. Hall & Company, where he was chairman of the international aviation division. He was a member of the Union League Club of New York.
        He is survived by his wife, Phyllis; three sons, William Jr. and David, both of Denver, and Stephen, of Glen Gardner, N.J.; a brother, Wesley, of Hendersonville, N.C.; a sister, Mary Barnes of Millington, N.J., and six grandchildren.


        Leon Rosenbluth, an advertising executive, died on Saturday at the Veterans Administration Medical Center in Gainesville, Fla. He was 65 years old and lived in Gainesville.
        He died of complications of a stroke, said his former wife, Jacqueline Rosalagon.
        In the 1960's and 70's Mr. Rosenbluth was research director of three advertising agencies in Manhattan, Young & Rubicam, J. Walter Thompson and D'Arcy. He then worked as research director at the Public Broadcasting Corporation in Washington until 1978, when he retired.
        He is survived by three sons, John, of Accord, N.Y., Charles, of Boston, and Eric, of Clarks Summit, Pa.

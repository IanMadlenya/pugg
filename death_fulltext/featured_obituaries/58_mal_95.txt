
        Andrew Varick Stout Jr. a former governor of the New York Stock Exchange and retired chairman of the investment banking firm of Dominick & Dominick, died on Friday at his son's home in Norwich, Vt. He was 89 years old and a resident of Hobe Sound, Fla.
        He died of pneumonia, his son, Andrew, said.
        Mr. Stout was appointed a governor of the New York Stock Exchange in 1932 at the age of 30.
        After graduating from Yale in 1926, he entered the family investment firm of Dominick & Dominick on Wall Street, where he remained until retiring in 1965. As senior partner and chairman, he served on many boards.
        A collector of American furniture and Impressionist art, he served on the Fine Arts Committee of the State Department. He gave American antiques to the White House, State Department and museums. As president of St. Luke's Hospital for many years, he raised funds for it through "Impressionist Painting Treasures" shows.
        A native of New York City, he served as a Navy commander during World War II.
        In addition to his son, Mr. Stout is survived by his wife, Elizabeth S. of Burlington, Vt.; a daughter, Juliet C. Perkins of Arlington, Mass.; a brother, Bayard D. of Rumson, N.J.; four stepchildren; eight grandchildren and five great-grandchildren.

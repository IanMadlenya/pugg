
        Albert Steven Pergam, a partner with the international law firm of Cleary, Gottlieb, Steen & Hamilton in New York, died on Sunday at Tisch Hospital of New York University Medical Center in Manhattan. He was 54 and lived in Manhattan.
        The cause was complications relating to cancer, his family said.
        Mr. Pergam was a native of Manhattan. He graduated summa cum laude from Yale College and magna cum laude from Harvard Law School.
        In the mid-1960's he served as a special assistant to the Assistant Attorney General in charge of the Civil Rights Division in the Department of Justice.
        He joined the New York office of Cleary, Gottlieb in 1966 and became a partner in 1973. He worked out of the London office from 1980 to 1984. His entire career dealt with the Eurobond market and other international legal matters.
        He served on the Committee on Professional Ethics of both the New York City and state bar associations. Active in Yale alumni affairs, he was on the board of directors of the Yale Alumni Fund.
        Mr. Pergam is survived by his wife, Natalie Chaliff Pergam; two daughters, Ilana and Elizabeth, and a brother, Carl of Omaha.


        Dr. Marvin G. Freid, who was a dentist for more than 50 years, mostly in Manhattan, died on Friday at a hospital in Cortlandt Manor, N.Y. He was 89.
        Dr. Freid practiced dentistry from 1933 to 1984, including three years in the Army during World War II. Marvin George Freid, who lived in Putnam Valley, N.Y., was born in the Bronx on Jan. 1, 1910. He graduated from New York University School of Dentistry and was the first dental intern at Mount Sinai Medical Center in Manhattan.
        In 1992, Dr. Freid was given an award from Mount Sinai Medical Center honoring his more than 60 years of service. He was an associate clinical professor emeritus at the medical school.
        He is survived by his wife, Eunice; a daughter, Maxine Haigh of Putnam Valley; a son, Marvin G. Freid Jr. of Belle Mead, N.J., four grandchildren, and four great-grandchildren.

        Gerald Arthur Fitzgerald, a pioneer in the frozen food industry, died Sunday of congestive heart failure at Sharon Hospital in Sharon, Conn. He was 88 years old and lived in Lakeville.
        Mr. Fitzgerald, who was a colleague of Clarence Birdseye, a founder of the frozen food industry, was a food consultant to industry and the Government. He developed a canned chicken ration for the Army in World War II and helped develop a dried fruit and canning industry in Iran. He was also a former director of the Frozen Food Foundation in Syracuse and until 1970 a professor of food engineering at the University of Massachusetts.
        He is survived by his wife, Regina Ford Fitzgerald, of Lakeville; three sons, Gerald Jr., of Washington, David, of Edina, Minn., and Richard, of Lakeville; two sisters, Kathleen and Margaret Saulnier, of Hampton, N.H., and seven grandchildren.

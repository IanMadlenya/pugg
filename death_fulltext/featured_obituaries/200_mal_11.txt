
        Raymond Walters Jr., a retired editor for The New York Times Book Review who established and wrote the Paperback Talk column, died on Aug. 30 in Columbus, Ohio, where he moved in 2001. He was 91 and a longtime resident of Manhattan.
        The column appeared weekly in the 1970's and early 1980's and chronicled the revolution brought about in the publishing industry, and in American culture, by paperback books. A compilation of the columns, ''Paperback Talk'' (Academy Chicago), was published in 1985.
        Mr. Walters, who held a doctorate in American history from Columbia University, also wrote biographies of two early treasury secretaries, Alexander James Dallas and Albert Gallatin.
        Mr. Walters was born on Aug. 23, 1912, in Bethlehem, Pa., to Elsie and Raymond Walters. The senior Mr. Walters was president emeritus of the University of Cincinnati.
        He graduated from Swarthmore College in 1933 and did postgraduate work at Princeton as well as at Columbia. He served with the historical office of the United States Army Air Forces during World War II.
        Mr. Walters, who was on the Book Review staff from 1958 until his retirement in 1982, had worked previously at Current History magazine, Saturday Review and Encore.

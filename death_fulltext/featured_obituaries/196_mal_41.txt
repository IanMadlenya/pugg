
        William D. Witter, an investment executive who founded two firms named William D. Witter Inc., died on Sunday at his home in Manhattan. He was 73. The cause of death was not announced.
        His father was Dean Witter, a founder of the investment firm Dean Witter & Company, which has become part of Morgan Stanley.
        Born in San Francisco, William David Witter received a bachelor's degree from Yale and a master's degree in business administration from Stanford.
        In 1966 he created the first firm bearing his name. It provided institutional research and asset management and was bought in 1976 by Drexel Burnham Lambert.
        In 1977, he set up his second firm in the Citicorp Building.
        He is survived by his wife, Inger Kullberg Witter, whom he married in 1972; one son from his second marriage, Michael D., of Manhattan; one daughter from his second marriage, Anna Christina Witter, also of Manhattan; three daughters from his first marriage, Virginia Woods, of Altamont, N.Y.; Elizabeth Tacy Mayer, of Los Altos, Calif., and Sidney Daire, of Manhattan; a son from the first marriage, William P., of Boulder, Colo.; nine grandchildren; and a sister, Ann Witter, of San Francisco.

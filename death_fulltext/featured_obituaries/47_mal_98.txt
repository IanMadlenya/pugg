
        Sidney S. Dell, an economist at the United Nations and an author of six books on international trade, died Monday at Memorial Sloan-Kettering Cancer Center in Manhattan. He was 71 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        He died of cancer, his son, Michael, said.
        At his death Mr. Dell was a senior fellow of the United Nations Institute for Training and Research and an adviser to the Intergovernmental Commission on Transnational Corporations. Born in England and educated at Oxford University, he served with the Royal Navy in World War II.
        He joined the United Nations staff in 1947 as an economist and his later positions included service as director of the United Nations Conference on Trade and Development and as assistant administrator of the United Nations Development Program.
        In addition to his son, he is survived by his wife, Ethel; two daughters, Linette Whitehead of Bladon, England, and Gillian Dell of Manhattan; a sister, Evelyn Rothfield of Melbourne, Australia; a brother, Edmund of London; and six grandchildren.

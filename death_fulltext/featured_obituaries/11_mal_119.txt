        Bernward U. Kurtz, an architect and former partner and director in the Eggers Group Architects & Planners in Manhattan, died of a lung ailment Monday in Tampa, Fla., where he lived. He was 51 years old. Mr. Kurtz, a native of Neustadt, West Germany, came to the United States in 1965 after earning degrees in architecture and engineering in Mannheim. He joined Eggers & Higgins, the predecessor firm to the Eggers Group, in 1966 and was responsible for the design of several buildings, including corporate headquarters for the St. Regis Corporation in New York, American Hoechst in Somerville, N.J., and Air Products and Chemicals in Allentown, Pa.
        He also designed office buildings for the Metropolitan Life Insurance Company, the Prudential Insurance Company of America and Crum and Forster.
        Surviving are his wife, Nancy; two daughters, Christina and Caroline, and a sister, Margo Hirth of Neustadt.

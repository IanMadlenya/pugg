
        Irwin C. Watson, a comedian who appeared frequently on television in the 1950's and 60's and in nightclubs throughout the country, died on Feb. 1 at the Winter Park Memorial Hospital in Orlando, Fla. He was 70 and lived in Orlando.
        The cause was a heart attack, said his wife, Edlyne O. Watson.
        Mr. Watson belonged to the generation of black comedians that included Redd Foxx and Slappy White, with whom he performed.
        He was a familiar presence on the television shows of Johnny Carson, Ed Sullivan, Mike Douglas and many others. He also performed with singers, including Sammy Davis Jr., Bobby Darin, Dakota Staton, Shirley Bassey, Gladys Knight, Aretha Franklin and, most recently, Natalie Cole.
        He also appeared at most of the major resort hotels in the Catskills and Poconos.
        Mr. Watson toured West Africa in 1981. He performed on Norwegian American Cruise Line ships up to the time of his death.
        In addition to his wife, he is survived by two children, Rodwin and Regina, of Brooklyn, and two sisters, Bernice O'Neil of Brooklyn and Cynthia Thorp of Barbados.

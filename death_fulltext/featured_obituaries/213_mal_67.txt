
        James A. Rousmaniere, a socially prominent yachtsman and professional fund-raiser, died Oct. 22 in Southbury, Conn. He was 86. A longtime resident of Oyster Bay, N.Y., he lived in Southbury.
        Mr. Rousmaniere (pronounced roo-man-EER) died six days after having a stroke, his family said.
        Mr. Rousmaniere helped organize development campaigns for the United Negro College Fund, the Museum of Modern Art, the Episcopal Church, Harvard and other organizations.
        James Ayer Rousmaniere was born Aug. 2, 1918, in Manhattan. His father, John, was a successful textile manufacturer and, later, the mayor of Oyster Bay. With his Harvard roommate John F. Kennedy, James Rousmaniere was part of the team that won the national intercollegiate sailing championship in 1938. He graduated from Harvard in 1940 and from Harvard Law School in 1947.
        A leader of local Democratic Party organizations, he was commissioner of commerce and industry for Nassau County in the early 1970's.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Jessie Pierce; a sister, Frances Storrs; eight children, John, James, Peter, David, Joseph, Edmund, Arthur and Kate; 15 grandchildren; and one great-grandchild.

        G. Douglas Hofe Jr., a former Deputy State Attorney General for New Jersey, died of cancer yesterday at his home in Morris Township, N.J. He was 59 years old and also lived in Orleans, Mass.
        Mr. Hofe was Deputy Attorney General from 1958 to 1962. From 1962 to 1969, he was with the law firm of Meyner & Wiley in Newark.
        In 1969, he was named director of the United States Department of the Interior's Bureau of Outdoor Recreation. He had been vice president of the Essex County (N.J.) Park Commission and more recently was on the Cape Cod National Seashore Advisory Commission.
        He was a graduate of Wesleyan University, Harvard Law School and Harvard's Graduate School of Education. He received a Ph.D. from Columbia University Teachers College in 1958.
        He is survived by his mother, Alice M. Hofe of Morris Township, and a brother, Dean, of Morristown, N.J.

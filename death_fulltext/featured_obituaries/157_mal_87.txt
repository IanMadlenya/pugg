
        John W. Hannon Jr., a former president of Bankers Trust, died last Monday at Carolina House, an assisted living facility in Pinehurst, N.C. Mr. Hannon, a resident of West End, N.C., was 77.
        The cause of death was cardiac arrest, said his son, Bruce.
        Mr. Hannon worked for Bankers Trust, which was acquired by Deutsche Bank in 1999, for 32 years. He was appointed executive vice president in charge of operations in 1971 before being named president in 1975. He also served as chairman of the executive committee, part of the corporate board, starting in 1973.
        Mr. Hannon was part of the Bankers Trust management team in 1978 when it reorganized, selling branch offices and switching its focus from consumer banking to investment banking.
        Mr. Hannon's career in banking started in 1946 at Commercial National Bank and Trust, after he served in the Army Air Corps in World War II and a short pitching career on a New York Yankees farm team. He graduated from St. Lawrence University in 1946. Bankers Trust bought Commercial National in 1951, and Mr. Hannon became an assistant treasurer before being named an executive vice president.
        In addition to his son, who lives in London, Mr. Hannon is survived by his wife, Vivien; two daughters, Elizabeth Reichard of North Andover, Mass., and Christine Hannon, of Northampton, Mass.; and five grandchildren.

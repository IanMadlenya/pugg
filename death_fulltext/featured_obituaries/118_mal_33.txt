
        Dr. Earl O. Butcher, a former chairman of the department of anatomy at New York University's College of Dentistry, died on Nov. 2 at his home in the Meadow Lakes retirement community in Hightstown, N.J. He was 93.
        A noted researcher, Dr. Butcher taught at N.Y.U. from 1943 until his retirement in 1972. He also served as associate dean of the dental college and taught biological sciences at the Graduate School of Arts and Sciences. In 1969 he received the university's Great Teacher Award.
        As an anatomist he contributed new data on the growth and differentiation of hair, on skin transplantation and on cosmetic ingredients that improve skin quality. He was an early practitioner of tooth transplants and implants.
        A Phi Beta Kappa graduate of DePauw University, he received his master's degree and doctorate at Cornell University. He began his career as an instructor at Cornell College and spent 15 years on the biology faculty of Hamilton College in Clinton, N.Y., before joining N.Y.U.
        His research produced about 160 articles and several textbooks. He also lectured at Stanford University and the University of California at Los Angeles, as well as colleges from Canada to Venezuela to Norway.
        Dr. Butcher is survived by two sons, Dr. Barry M., of Albuquerque, N.M., and Robert M., of East Aurora, N.Y.; a daughter, Elizabeth B. Spraker of Winter Park, Fla.; nine grandchildren, and five great-grandchildren. His wife, Helen, died four years ago.

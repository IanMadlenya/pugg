
        Frank Joseph Kenny, who led one of New York City's oldest trucking companies, died on Saturday at Riverview Medical Center in Red Bank, N.J. He was 88 and lived in Little Silver, N.J.
        Mr. Kenny, a native of Brooklyn, retired in 1974 as president of the Monroe Express Company, founded by his father, Thomas. He was with the company for 48 years.
        He is survived by his wife of 64 years, Mary Margaret; two daughters, Mary Ann Kenny Devine of Little Silver and Carol Kenny LiVolsi of Danbury, Conn.; a son, Frank J. Jr., of Princeton, N.J., and 12 grandchildren.

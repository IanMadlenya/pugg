        Otto M. Buerger, who served as a lawyer for the Long Island Rail Road for 28 years until his retirement in 1963, died in his sleep Dec. 29 at his home in Sands Point, L.I. He was 88 years old.
        Mr. Buerger, a native of Buffalo, was a graduate of Cornell University and received a law degree from the University of Buffalo. He was an assistant district attorney in Erie County before coming to New York in 1934. After retiring from his railroad post, he was an associate professor of political science at C.W. Post College.
        Mr. Buerger was a veteran of the two World Wars, serving in the United States Army as a second lieutenant in World War I and as a major in World War II.
        Surviving are his wife, Johanna; two daughters, Helene Peck of Sherman Oaks, Calif., and Anne Luise Buerger of Manhattan; a son, Otto M. Buerger 3d of Sands Point; five grandchildren and two great-grandchildren.

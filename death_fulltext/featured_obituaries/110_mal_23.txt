
        Peter B. Smith, a senior executive at J. P. Morgan & Company, died on Saturday at the Sharon Hospital in Sharon, Conn. He was 61.
        Mr. Smith, who lived in Sharon, died of heart failure, the company said.
        A former Marine Corps captain, Mr. Smith joined J. P. Morgan in 1958. After  several assignments in the United States, Mr. Smith represented the banking company in Buenos Aires and in the Netherlands from 1969 to 1975. In 1981, he was chosen to head J. P. Morgan's new worldwide banking group, a responsibility he held through the 1980's.
        At the time of his death, Mr. Smith was the chairman of J. P. Morgan's credit policy committee and co-head of its corporate risk management group.
        Born in New York City, Mr. Smith attended the Canterbury School in New Milford, Conn., and graduated from Yale in 1956. He was a longtime director of the Canterbury School and served on the board of the New York City Ballet.
        He is survived by his wife, Betsy; two sons, Peter Jr., of Houston and Michael, of Greenwich, Conn.; five daughters, Dr. Marjorie Smith of Sacramento,  Calif., Alison Marcinek of Essex, Conn., Hannah and Elizabeth, both of New York  and Sharon, and Madeleine Cooke of Perth, Australia, and seven grandchildren.

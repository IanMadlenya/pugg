
        Abram Joseph Abeloff, a surgeon with Lenox Hill Hospital for more than 50 years and a former associate clinical professor of surgery at New York University, died on Aug. 15 at the hospital. He was 97 and lived on the Upper East Side of Manhattan.
        Dr. Abeloff was an honorary board member of the American Jewish Joint Distribution Committee and former chairman of its medical advisory board.
        Dr. Abeloff was a Manhattan native and received his medical degree from Columbia College. He joined the Lenox Hill staff in 1930. He served in World War II, rising to colonel in the Army Medical Corps.
        He is survived by his wife of 44 years, Gertrude Kopsch Abeloff, and their son, Tobias, also of Manhattan.

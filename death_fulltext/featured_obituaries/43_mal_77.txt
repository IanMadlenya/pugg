        James L. Howard, a lawyer, certified public accountant and former insurance executive, died on July 29 at Engelwood Hospital in New Jersey. He was 71 years old and lived in Teaneck, N.J.
        He died of respiratory failure, his wife, Gloria, said.
        Mr. Howard retired in 1985 as president and chief executive of the United Mutual Life Insurance Company. Before that, he had been vice president and mortgage officer of the Carver Federal Savings Bank in Harlem.
        He was a member of 100 Black Men, the Harlem Business Alliance and the Harlem Lawyers Association. He was also treasurer of the Metropolitan Association of the New York Conference of the United Church of Christ.
        He graduated from the Brooklyn Law School and the New York University Accounting School.
        Also surviving are two sons, James, of Julian, Calif., and Kyland, of Washington, and a daughter, Dawn, of Boston.

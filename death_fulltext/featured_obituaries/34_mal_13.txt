        James A. Attwood, a former chairman and chief executive of the Mutual Life Insurance Company of New York, died of acute anemia Tuesday at New York Hospital. He was 62 years old and lived in Bronxville, N.Y.
        Mr. Attwood joined the company in 1983 and retired in July. Previously, he was senior executive vice president and chief investment officer of The Equitable Life Assurance Society, where he worked for 35 years.
        Mr. Attwood, who was born in Detroit, graduated from the University of Michigan. He was a former vice president of the Society of Actuaries. He was also a member of the board of the Committee for Economic Development and a former board member of the New York City Partnership, Fisk University and the New York Theological Seminary.
        Surviving are his wife, Pauline; a son, James Jr., of Bronxville; three daughters, Terry Beckmann of Bronxville, Dorothy, of Swarthmore, Pa., and Katherine, of Philadelphia; two sisters, Evelyn Hansen of Southfield, Mich., and Beatrice Smith of Inkster, Mich., and one grandchild.

        William B. Burke, executive secretary of the St. Patrick's Day Parade Committee in New York City from 1978 to 1984 and grand marshal of the 1980 parade, died Friday at the Frances Schervier Home and Hospital in the Bronx, where he had lived since May. He was 84 years old and had resided in the Inwood section of Manhattan before moving to Yonkers in 1977.
        Mr. Burke, a native of Tuam, County Galway, came to this country in 1923. He retired from the payroll division of the New York City Transit Authority in 1967 after 35 years in city service.
        He became active in Irish-American organizations the year he arrived in the United States, holding positions on the city, state and national levels. He had been a member of the Parade Committee since 1928. He served on Francis Cardinal Spellman's Committee of the Laity and became a friend of Terence Cardinal Cooke. He was a past president of the Galway Men's Association of New York.
        He is survived by 41 nieces and nephews. Mass will be offered at 11 A.M. tomorrow at the Church of the Good Shepherd, at Broadway and Isham Street in Inwood.

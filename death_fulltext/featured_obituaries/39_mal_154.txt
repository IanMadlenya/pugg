        Lawrence R. Chenault, emeritus professor of economics at Hunter College, died Monday at Centre Community Hospital in State College, Pa. He was 92 years old and lived in State College.
        Professor Chenault, a certified public accountant, joined Hunter in 1936 and was chairman of the economics department from 1956 to 1965. He retired in 1967. He also taught at Columbia University, New York University, the University of Puerto Rico and the University of Rome.
        He was a former president of the Metropolitan Economics Association of New York.
        Professor Chenault was born in Hico, Tex., and was a graduate of the University of Texas. He received a master's degree from Wayne State University and a Ph.D. from Columbia University. From 1922 to 1926 he was an auditor for the Treasury Department. He served in the Navy in World War I. He is survived by his wife, the former Minetta Mittleton; two daughters, Laurel Buhrman of Upper Saddle River, N.J., and Jeanne Porter of Lemont, Pa., and four grandchildren.

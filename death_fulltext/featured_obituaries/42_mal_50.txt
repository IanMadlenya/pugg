        Jack Welsch, a wine distribution executive, died Wednesday. He was 77 years old and lived in San Francisco.
        The Wine Institute, a San Francisco-based lobbying body for the California wine industry, said Mr. Welsch died of heart failure.
        Mr. Welsch served as president and vice chairman of Fromm & Sichel, the distributor of Christian Brothers wine and brandy, from 1964 to 1983. He had also been a vice president of the Joseph E. Seagram Company and the Schenley Company.
        Surviving are his wife, Marjorie, of San Francisco; two daughters, Toni, of Columbus, Ohio, and Jackie, who lives in Denmark, and six grandchildren.

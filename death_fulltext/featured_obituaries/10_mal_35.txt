        The Rev. Orlando Enrique Costas, dean of the Andover Newton Theological School and a prominent Hispanic Protestant theologian, died of stomach cancer Thursday night at his home in Newton Centre, Mass. He was 45 years old.
        Dr. Costas had been academic dean since 1984 at the 175-year-old theological school, which is affiliated with the American Baptist Churches, U.S.A., and the United Church of Christ. From 1980 to 1984, he was the Thornley B. Wood Professor of Missiolog and director of Hispanic Studies and Ministries at Eastern Baptist Theological Seminary in Philadelphia.
        He wrote 13 books, the last of which, ''Liberating News! A Contextual Theology of Evangelization,'' is scheduled to be published in the spring by the William B. Eerdmans Publishing Company.
        Dr. Costas, who was born in Ponce, P.R., served as a missionary in Costa Rica and was a visiting professor at Texas Christian University, and at three theological seminaries, Fuller in Pasadena, Calif., Gordon-Conwell in Hamilton, Mass., and Union in Richmond. He also served as pastor at several churches in Puerto Rico and elsewhere in the United States.
        He is survived by his wife, Rosa Lydia Feliciano Costas; two daughters, Annette Michelle Costas Feliciano and Dannette Gisele Costas Feliciano, all of Newtown Centre; his parents, Rosalina Rivera and Ventura Enrique Costas, of Ocoee, Fla., and four sisters, Edmee Flores, Milca Costas, Mirta Sussman, all of Milford, Conn., and Marlene Costas, of Ocoee.

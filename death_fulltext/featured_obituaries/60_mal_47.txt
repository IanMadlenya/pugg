
        Francis M. Pipkin, a former associate dean and physics department chairman at Harvard University, died Sunday at Mount Auburn Hospital in Cambridge, Mass. He was 66 years old and lived in Belmont, Mass.
        The cause of death was a heart ailment, his family said.
        Mr. Pipkin, a native of in Marianna, Ark., served with the Army in Germany during World War II and earned a bachelor's degree from the University of Iowa in 1950 and a doctorate from Princeton in 1954. He became a Harvard fellow that year, joined the faculty in 1957 and was a professor at his death.
        He was chairman of the physics department from 1985 to 1988 and was the associate dean of faculty for Harvard and Radcliffe colleges from 1974 to 1977. He helped plan and oversee the Cambridge Electron Accelerator and worked on particle experiments at the Fermi National Accelerator Laboratory in Batavia, Ill., and the Synchrotron Laboratory at Cornell.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Phyllis Burr; two daughters, Augusta Pipkin Heywood of Medford, Mass., and Jane Pipkin of Wilmington, N.Y., and a brother, Larry Pipkin of Cedar Rapids, Iowa.

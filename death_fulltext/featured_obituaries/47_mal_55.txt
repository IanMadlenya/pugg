
        Jonathan Miles Read, a corporate lawyer who specialized in mergers and acquisitions, died on Thursday at the University of Connecticut Health Center in Farmington. He was 32 years old and lived in Barkhamsted, Conn.
        He died of leukemia, a spokesman for the family said.
        He was a member of the Hartford law firm of Day, Berry & Howard, which he joined in 1988. Before that he was associated with the firm of Sullivan & Cromwell, in its offices in Manhattan and Melbourne, Australia.
        Mr. Read was a graduate of Amherst College and the New York University School of Law.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Molly Sexton; two sons, Benjamin and Samuel, both of Barkhamsted; his parents, Donald and Jean Read of Riverside, Conn., and two brothers, Bartow, of Ann Arbor, Mich., and Mark, of Brooklyn.

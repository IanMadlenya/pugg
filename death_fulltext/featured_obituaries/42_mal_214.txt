        Robert S. Forman, a lawyer from Long Island, died on Wednesday at his home in Sands Point. He was 71 years old and also lived in Palm Beach, Fla.
        He died of cancer, a spokesman for the family said.
        Mr. Forman was senior partner in Forman, Arweiler & Schaefer in Mineola and was the Sands Point Village Attorney for more than 30 years. He had also been a member of the Town of North Hempstead Board of Zoning and Appeals.
        Mr. Forman was active in Republican politics as a Port Washington committeeman from 1947 to 1984 and as executive leader of the committee from 1960 to 1973.
        He was born in Woodmere, L.I., and graduated from Princeton University and the Columbia Law School. He was in the Army from 1942 to 1946 and served in the Reserve until 1979, retiring as a lieutenant colonel.
        Surviving are his second wife, the former Marcia Willis; four sons, Robert Jr., of Oldsmar, Fla., Michael, of Orlando, Fla., Guy, of Flower Hill, L.I., and Christopher, of Cape Coral, Fla.; two stepdaughers, Patricia Willis and Cynthia Willis, both of Manhattan; a sister, Kathryn, of Sands Point; a brother, Alexander 3d, of Mill Neck, L.I., and five grandchildren.


        Harry Ellerbe, an actor and director who worked on Broadway, in regional theater and in films, died on Dec. 3 at the A. G. Rhodes Nursing Home in Marietta, Ga. He was 91 years old.
        Mr. Ellerbe appeared on Broadway in Ibsen's "Ghosts" and "Hedda Gabler," with the Russian actress Alla Nazimova. He also appeared on Broadway in "Man on Stilts," "Junior Miss,"
        As a director, Mr. Ellerbe worked for many regional theaters, including Gregory Peck's Actors Playhouse in La Jolla, Calif., and the Westport Country Playhouse in Connecticut. His film credits included "So Red the Rose" (1935), with Margaret Sullavan, and "The Man Who Understood" (1959), with Henry Fonda. He also appeared on television in "Hallmark Hall of Fame," "Perry Mason" and other programs.
        He is survived by a sister, Marie Hancock of Atlanta.

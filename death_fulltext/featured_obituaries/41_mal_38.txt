        Vinjamuri E. Devadutt, a retired professor of ecumenical theology and world Christianity at Colgate Rochester Divinity School, died of a heart attack May 26 at Strong Memorial Hospital in Rochester. He was 81 years old and lived in Rochester.
        Mr. Devadutt received four university degrees in India before earning a doctorate in theology at Victoria University in Toronto in 1949. He came to the United States in 1953 to teach at Princeton University and joined the faculty at the University of Rochester and at Colgate Rochester Divinity School in 1956. He retired in 1974 as a professor emeritus.
        He is survived by his wife, Sushila; a son, Dave Vinjamuri of Glastonbury, Conn.; two daughters, Chelli Devadutt of Manhattan and V. Sumati Devadutt of Rochester, and six grandchildren.

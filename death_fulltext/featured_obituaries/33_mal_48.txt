        Ralph Ernest Henderson, the creator and first editor in chief of The Reader's Digest Condensed Books, died yesterday in Northern Westchester Hospital Center in Mt. Kisco, N.Y., after a long illness. He was 90 years old and lived in Pleasantville, N.Y., and South Kent, Conn.
        Mr. Henderson was The Reader's Digest's first business manager when he joined the three-year-old magazine in 1925. He was a war correspondent for the magazine in 1944 and became editor of Condensed Books in 1950. He was a vice president of The Readers Digest Association Inc. when he retired in 1965.
        Born in Mongai, Burma, he graduated from Harvard College and taught for the American Baptist Foreign Mission Society in Burma from 1921 to 1924.
        He is survived by a daughter, Fay Porter of Rockville, Md., and two sons, Lee, of Lake Forest, Ill., and Ralph, of Geneva.

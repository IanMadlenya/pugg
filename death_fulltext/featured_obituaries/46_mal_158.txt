
        William Kemsley Sr., a labor relations specialist, died Monday at Medical Center Hospital of Vermont in Burlington. He was 82 years old and lived in Burlington.
        Mr. Kemsley died of kidney failure, a spokesman for the family said.
        He began his career in Detroit during the Depression as an organizer for the United Auto Workers, and after World War II went to Berlin as a specialist helping to rebuild labor relations machinery.
        In 1950 he became education director for the 650,000 members of the Michigan C.I.O. Council. From 1951 to 1954 he served with the Marshall Plan in Paris as head of the labor training section.
        He resigned to become representative at the United Nations of the International Confederation of Free Trade Unions. He then became director of the New York office of the International Confederation of Free Trade Unions in Manhattan,
        After retiring to Vermont in 1961 Mr. Kemsley was a member of the Vermont Labor Relations Board for 18 years.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Ann Terech; three sons, Michael of San Francisco, William Jr. of Greenwich, Conn., and Brian of Sterling, Alaska; two daughters, Joan Kemsley of Burlington and June Heffington of Santa Barbara, Calif.; a sister, Edith Knowles of Bloomfield Hills, Mich.; and 14 grandchildren.

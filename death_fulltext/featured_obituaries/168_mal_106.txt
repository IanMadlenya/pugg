
        Charles F. Kiley Sr., a former editor in chief of The New York Law Journal, died on Saturday at his home in Westfield, N.J. He was 87.
        Mr. Kiley oversaw The Law Journal's news coverage from 1967 until he retired in 1989.
        He was born in Jersey City. Early in his career, he spent two decades with The New York Herald Tribune and worked for other newspapers, including Stars and Stripes during his Army service in World War II. As a pool reporter, he covered the German surrenders in Rheims, France, and in Berlin.
        Mr. Kiley is survived by his wife, Billee; five sons, Charles, of Mountainside, N.J.; Edward, of Leesburg, Va.; Paul, of Clifton, N.J.; John, of Laurel, Md.; and David, of Ann Arbor, Mich.; a daughter, Anne Kiley of Hammondsport, N.Y.; two sisters, Eleanor Haines of Spring Lake, N.J., and Betty Phillips of Lyndhurst, N.J.; 12 grandchildren; and one great-grandchild.

        Morrison Parker, a stockbroker and a Republican district leader, died Saturday of a heart attack at his home in Manhattan. He was 81 years old.
        Mr. Parker, who was born in Manhattan and graduated from City College, worked for the securities firm of Drexel Burnham Lambert.
        At his death, he was leader of the 67th Assembly District on the West Side.
        He is survived by his wife, Gertrude, executive secretary of the New York Republican County Committee; a sister, Anne Needler of Denver, and a brother, Herman, of White Plains.

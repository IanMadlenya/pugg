
        Donald Carswell, an executive at NBC television and a community leader, died on March 25 at the Brooklyn Hospital Center, where he served as vice chairman. He was 75.
        The cause was leukemia, his family said.
        Mr. Carswell spent 36 years with NBC and was senior vice president for finance from 1979 to 1992, managing the network's budgets as it introduced hit shows like ''Cheers'' (1982), ''The Cosby Show'' (1984) and ''Seinfeld'' (1990). In 1990, Mr. Carswell also assumed the new title of chief financial officer.
        In Brooklyn, his lifelong home, he was a leader in starting Brooklyn Community Access Television, for which he also wrote and developed the public service program ''HealthWatch.''
        Mr. Carswell was board president of the Caledonian Hospital in East Flatbush from 1975 to 1982, when he helped it merge with the Brooklyn Hospital Center. He then became a board member of the center and chairman of the Brooklyn Hospital Foundation.
        He was also chairman of the board of trustees of Poly Prep Country Day School, his alma mater.
        Mr. Carswell was born Oct. 25, 1929, in Brooklyn. He received a bachelor's degree and a business degree from Harvard and served in the Navy as a supply officer. He joined NBC in 1956 as a financial trainee.
        He is survived by his wife of 48 years, Lois; a daughter, Anne Carswell Tang, of San Carlos, Calif.; two sons, Alexander, of Pasadena, Calif., and Robert Ian, of New York; a brother, Robert, of New York; and four grandchildren.

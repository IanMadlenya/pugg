        Frederic M. Roberts, an investor, died of a cerebral hemorrhage Tuesday in New York Hospital. He was 59 years old and lived in Tuxedo Park, N.Y., and Lawrence, L.I.
        Mr. Roberts owned or invested primarily in apparel and real-estate businesses. He was president of the James Reed Roberts Foundation, which gives art scholarships to young people.
        Mr. Roberts was born in Manhattan. He was an alumnus of Yale University.  Surviving are his wife, the former Joan Brown; a son, Curtis, of Manhattan, and his father, Jacob Rosenblatt of Lawrence.

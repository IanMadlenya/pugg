        Reagan Cook, a theater set designer, died of brain cancer on Monday at his home in Manhattan. He was 49 years old.
        Mr. Cook designed sets for Off Broadway productions at Manhattan Punchline Theater, Theater for the New City, Circle Repertory Company, Actors Playhouse, the Puerto Rican Traveling Theater and other companies. He began his career in Texas, and spent some years as resident designer with the Arizona Theater Company in Tucson before settling in New York in 1978.
        Mr. Cook also designed sets for many regional theaters and for the Kennedy Center for the Performing Arts in Washington. He taught designing at Lehman College in New York City. He is survived by his wife, Melissa Davis, of Manhattan; a son, Anthony Brigance Cook, of Sitka, Alaska; his parents, Walter and Doris Cook, of Athens, Tex., and a brother, Sean Cook, of San Antonio. A memorial service will be held at 11 A.M. Sunday at the Theater for the New City, 155 First Avenue, at Ninth Street.


        Hyman Kavett, a retired college professor and writer, died on Saturday at the Elizabeth Medical Center in New Jersey. He was 70 years old and lived in Union, N.J.
        He died of a circulatory ailment, a family spokesman said.
        In 1967 Dr. Kavett joined the faculty of Richmond College, which later became the College of Staten Island, part of the City University of New York. He remained there as a professor of education until his retirement in 1985.
        At the same time, he was editor of Social Education, the journal of the National Council of Social Studies.
        A native of Brooklyn, he graduated from Brooklyn College. He received his doctorate in education from Rutgers University in 1967.
        During World War II, he served as an Army staff sergeant in the South Pacific and was among the first Allied servicemen to enter Japan.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Phyllis Flyer; two sons, Henry C. of Union and Joshua A. of Howell, N.J.; a daughter, Diana C. Kirsch of Paramus, N.J., and six grandchildren.


        W. L. Hadley Griffin, retired chairman and chief executive of a national footwear company and a former chairman of the Federal Reserve Bank of St. Louis, died on Nov. 9 at his home in St. Louis. He was 79.
        The cause of death was cancer, said a son, Dustin H. Griffin 2d.
        Mr. Griffin was named chief executive of the Brown Group, a footwear retailer that owns several chains in the United States and Canada, including Famous Footwear and Naturalizer, in 1969. He became chairman three years later and remained in that position until his retirement in 1985.
        He was named director and deputy chairman of the St. Louis Fed in 1982 and chairman the next year. He stepped down in 1987.
        Mr. Griffin graduated from Williams College in Williamstown, Mass. in 1940 and received a law degree from Washington University in St. Louis in 1947.
        He served in the Navy during World War II and again in the Korean War. In 1947, he went to work for the Wahl Shoe Company, which merged with Brown four years later.
        Mr. Griffin is survived by his wife, Phoebe, of St. Louis; three sons, Dustin H. of Englewood, N.J., L. Perry of Carbondale, Colo., and Peter B. of St. Louis; his brother, Dustin H. Griffin of St. Louis, and four grandchildren.

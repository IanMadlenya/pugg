
        John P. Killilea, a college scout and director of player personnel for the Houston Rockets, died last Saturday in Denver. He was 67.
        Mr. Killilea died after suffering a heart attack at the Denver airport while  boarding a flight for Kansas City, Mo.
        Mr. Killilea worked as an assistant coach for three National Basketball Association teams, beginning with the Boston Celtics in 1971. During his time with the team, it won five division titles and two league championships.
        Mr. Killilea served as an assistant coach under Don Nelson in Milwaukee and under Stan Albeck with the Nets. He joined the Rockets in 1988.
        Mr. Killilea is survived by his wife, Eli.

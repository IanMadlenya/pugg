        James D. Heslin, an executive with the Tishman Construction Corporation for 23 years, died of a heart attack on Thursday at St. Clare's Hospital in New York City. He was 60 years old and lived in the Riverdale section of the Bronx.
        Mr. Heslin, a first vice president of Tishman, oversaw the construction of several large midtown buildings recently, including the offices of the Swiss Bank Corporation and of U.S. Trust. He also headed the company's tenant-development group, which handles interior construction and design, and helped develop the interiors of the World Trade Center in the 1970's.
        He is survived by a sister, Anne, of the Bronx; and two brothers, John, of Smithtown, N.Y. and Daniel, of Manhattan Beach, Calif.

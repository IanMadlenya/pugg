        Alfred C. Clapp, a former New Jersey judge who managed Governor Kean's first successful gubernatorial campaign, died of heart disease Monday at his home in Montclair, N.J. He was 84 years old.
        Judge Clapp, a former presiding judge of the Appellate Division of the Superior Court, was a member of the State Senate from 1947 to 1953.
        He was dean of the Rutgers University School of Law from 1951 to 1953. At the time he was also president of the Essex County Bar Association and editor of The New Jersey Law Journal, a post he held from 1944 to 1953 and again from 1970 to 1984.
        He was legal counsel or campaign manager for a number of Republican candidates for office, including Mr. Kean, who was the Assembly minority leader when he ran for governor. Counsel to Legislature
        Judge Clapp was the author of a seven-volume work, ''Wills and Administration in New Jersey,'' and the chairman of the New Jersey Supreme Court Civil Practice Committee from 1947 to 1987.
        He served as counsel to the New Jersey Legislature for the drafting of the 1944 State Constitution and a delegate to the state constitutional conventions in 1947 and 1966.
        He was a professor of law at the Mercer Beasley School of Law and the Rutgers Law School intermittently from 1929 until 1971 and founder and chairman of the New Jersey Institute for Continuing Legal Education from 1962 to 1986.
        He was born in East Orange, N.J., and graduated from the University of Vermont in 1923 and from Harvard Law School in 1927.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Catharine Shotwell; four sons, Alfred Jr., of Clinton, N.Y.; Edward, of Vermont, Ill.; John, of Spring Lake Heights, N.J., and Roger, of Pottersville, N.J., and seven grandchildren.
        A memorial service is to be held on Friday at 2 P.M. at the Central Presbyterian Church, at Park Street and Claremont Avenue in Montclair.

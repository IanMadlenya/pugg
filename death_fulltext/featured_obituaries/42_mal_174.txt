        Wolf Weinreb, an owner and manager of apartment buildings in Manhattan and the Bronx, died on Tuesday at Mount Sinai Hospital. He was 93 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        His family said he died of heart failure.
        In the last four decades Mr. Weinreb acquired many residential properties on Riverside Drive, West End Avenue and in the 80's and 90's on the West Side. He remained active in his business until his death.
        He was born in Nizhnev, in what was then Poland, in 1896. He once owned a tin factory in Lvov, Poland. During World War II, fleeing from the Nazis, he and his family hid in forests in Hungary and Czechoslovakia before reaching France. The family came to New York in 1950.
        Mr. Weinreb is survived by his wife, the former Sarah Heller, whom he married in 1921; a son, Leon, of Manhattan; a daughter, Irene Scharf of Manhattan; six grandchildren, and 29 great-grandchilldren.

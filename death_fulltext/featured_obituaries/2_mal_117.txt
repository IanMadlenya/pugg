        F. Edgar Davis, a former senior vice president of Newsweek, died of pneumonia Wednesday at a Branford, Conn., hospice. He was 70 years old and lived in Plainfield, N.J.
        Mr. Davis, who retired in 1978 after 33 years in executive positions at Newsweek, was a 1937 graduate of Dartmouth College. He served in the Navy from 1941 to 1945, attaining the rank of lieutenant commander.
        He is survived by his wife, Dorothy Campbell Davis; two sons, F. Edgar Jr., of Plainfield, and Geoffrey, of Geneva, N.Y., and two grandchildren.


        Erwin A. Stuebner, former national partner and president of Kidder, Peabody & Company, died on Tuesday at Evanston Hospital, Evanston, Ill. Mr. Stuebner, who lived in Wilmette, Ill., was 88.
        The cause was complications of Parkinson's disease, said his son James Stuebner.
        Mr. Stuebner joined Kidder, Peabody in 1934 and helped start the investment banking concern's Philadelphia office. He then became a vice president of Fidelity Philadelphia Bank & Trust, and later served as a lieutenant colonel in the United States Army. He rejoined Kidder, Peabody in 1947.
        Mr. Stuebner moved to Chicago in 1949 to manage the Kidder, Peabody office there. He became president of the firm in 1964 and vice chairman in 1977. He retired in 1983.
        Mr. Stuebner is survived by his wife of 63 years, Frances, of Wilmette; two sons, James of Minneapolis and Erwin A. Jr. of Williamstown, Mass.; two daughters, Anne Damon of Newport, R.I., and Mary Coonen of Dusseldorf, Germany; 13 grandchildren, and a brother, Roland of Seattle.

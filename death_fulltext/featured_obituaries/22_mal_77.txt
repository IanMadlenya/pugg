        Dr. Rafael Tavares, a psychiatrist who developed programs to prevent the spread of AIDS, particularly among Hispanic people, died of cardiopulmonary arrest Tuesday at St. Luke's Hospital, his doctor said. He was 43 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Dr. Tavares was director of community psychiatry at Presbyterian Hospital and was an assistant professor of clinical psychiatry at the College of Physicians and Surgeons. At the Columbia-Presbyterian Medical Center, he led in developing an educational program to combat AIDS that has been widely copied.
        A specialist on mental-health problems of Hispanic people, Dr. Tavares was a co-founder and president of the Hispanic AIDS Forum and a director of the Gay Men's Health Crisis.
        He was a major contributor to a monograph in 1984 on the psychiatric implications of AIDS. Dr. Tavares was co-chairman of the State Health Systems Agency committee that developed psychiatric standards of care for people with AIDS.
        Surviving are his mother, Luz, of Manhattan; two sisters, Flora, of Manhattan, and Judy, of Las Vegas, Nev., and two brothers, Luis, of Manhattan, and Hector, of Nyack, N.Y.

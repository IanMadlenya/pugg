        Gerald T. Broderick, president and chief executive officer of Arnold & Company, a Boston advertising agency, died yesterday, apparently of a heart attack, at Massachusetts General Hospital in Boston. He was 52 years old.
        A graduate of the University of Notre Dame, Mr. Broderick began his career in public relations with the Bell Telephone system in Manhattan and Hill & Knowlton, a subsidiary of the J. Walter Thompson Company.
        In 1965, he entered advertising with J. Walter Thompson. In 1984 he went to Arnold & Company, and a year later he was named president. Last July he and several partners bought the agency and he became chief executive officer.
        Mr. Broderick, who lived in Weston, Mass., leaves a wife, Alexandra; a daughter, Caroline, and a son, Gerald Jr., all of Weston, and three brothers.

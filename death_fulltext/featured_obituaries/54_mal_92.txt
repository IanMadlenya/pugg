
        Thomas W. Zito, who retired earlier this year as executive director of the New Jersey Association of Housing and Redevelopment Authorities, died on Wednesday at Bayonne Hospital in Bayonne, N.J. He was 77 years old and lived in Bayonne.
        He died of heart failure, his family said.
        Mr. Zito served with the association for more than 20 years and earlier held similar posts with the Bayonne Housing Authority. From 1957 to 1959 he was president of the Bayonne Board of Education.
        He was a native of Bayonne and held bachelor's and master's degrees from New York University.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Rose Marie Scarito; a daughter, Mary Ann Feldstein; two sons, Thomas Jr., and Robert; a sister, Rita Manzo; a half brother, Dr. Nicholas Videtti, and four grandchldren.

        George B. Vest, general counsel of the Federal Reserve Board from 1945 until his retirement in 1957, died of liver cancer on Sunday at his home in Washington. He was 94 years old.
        Mr. Vest, a native of Charlottesville, Va., was a Phi Beta Kappa graduate of the University of Virginia and of its Law School.
        He joined the legal staff of the Federal Reserve Board in 1924. In World War II he was instrumental in formulating a program that extended loan guarantees to contractors for the Government.
        Mr. Vest is survived by two sons, Steuart, of Vienna, Va., and George Jr. of Hartwell, Va., four grandchildren and a great-granddaughter.

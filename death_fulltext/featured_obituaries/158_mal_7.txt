
        James J. O'Leary, an economist and a former vice chairman of the U.S. Trust Corporation, died Monday at his home in Westport, Conn. He was 85.
        Dr. O'Leary was vice chairman of U.S. Trust, a money management company based in New York, from 1970 until 1980.
        He also served on the board of the National Bureau of Economic Research and was its chairman from 1976 to 1980.
        Born in Manchester, Conn., on May 7, 1914, Dr. O'Leary graduated from Wesleyan University, where he also earned a master's degree. He received his doctorate from Duke University.
        During World War II, he taught at Wesleyan and at the Air Navigation Flight Preparatory School in Middletown, Conn.
        After the war, Dr. O'Leary became a consultant for the Connecticut General Life Insurance Company, taught briefly at Duke and served in various positions with the Life Insurance Association of America.
        He was also chairman and chief economist at Lionel D. Edie & Company from 1967 to 1969, the year he joined U.S. Trust as an executive vice president and economist. After his 10 years with U.S. trust, he was an economic consultant.
        In 1992, Dr. O'Leary published the book, ''Stagnation or Healthy Growth?: The Challenge to the United States in the 90's'' (University Press of America).
        His wife, Rita, died in 1997. He is survived by three sons, James P., of Goshen, Conn.; Paul H., of Fairport, N.Y.; and Mark E., of Montauk, N.Y.; a daughter, Martha E. O'Leary of Garden City, N.Y.; and five grandchildren.

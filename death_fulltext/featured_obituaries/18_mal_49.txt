        Abraham S. Jacobson, editor in chief of Medical Tribune and a professor of clinical medicine and pathology at Cornell Medical School, died of pancreatic cancer yesterday at New York Hospital, where he was an attending physician. He was 75 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Dr. Jacobson began writing for Medical Tribune, a professional journal dealing in worldwide medical topics, at its founding in 1960 and became its editor in chief in 1978.
        A native of New York, he received his bachelor's degree from Columbia College and his medical degree from the New York University School of Medicine. In World War II he was commanding officer of the 302d Medical Battalion, 77th Infantry Division, serving during its three years of combat experience in the Pacific.
        From 1946 to 1954 Dr. Jacobson was chief of medicine at the Bronx Veterans Hospital.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Paula Mann; two sons, David of Alaska and Carl of Manhattan; a sister, Anna Schwartz, and a brother, Meyer, both of Manhattan.

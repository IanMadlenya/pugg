
        The Rev. Dr. Edwin O. Kennedy, who had been a professor and administrator at Union Theological Seminary and the pastor of Presbyterian churches in New Jersey, died on Oct. 3 at Heath Village, a retirement community in Washington Township, N.J., where he lived. He was 97.
        Dr. Kennedy was professor of practical theology at Union Theological Seminary, in Manhattan, from 1952 until he retired in 1966.
        Concurrently, he was the seminary's secretary. In that post, his duties included supervision of the program for the seminary's alumni, enlistment of students for the ministry and coordination of the activities of the seminary's board of directors, faculty and student body.
        He had been a member of the seminary's board of directors and president of its alumni association.
        He was born in New York City, and graduated in 1921 from Union College in Schenectady, N.Y., which awarded him an honorary doctorate in divinity years later, as did Carroll College in Wisconsin. In 1924, he received a bachelor of divinity degree from Union Theological Seminary.
        He was pastor of Ridgeview Community Presbyterian Church in West Orange, N.J., from 1924 to 1934 and of the First Presbyterian Church in Englewood, N.J., from 1944 to 1952. Over the years, he was also pastor or interim pastor of other churches in New Jersey, New York and Wisconsin. He retired from the ministry in 1984.
        He is survived by his wife of 63 years, the former Ann Swain; two daughters, Margaret Kennedy of Medford, Mass., and Jean Holloway of Edinburgh, Scotland; four grandchildren, and two great-grandchildren.

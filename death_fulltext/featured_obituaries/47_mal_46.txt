
        Harrington Putnam Jr., a retired designer of wallpaper, textiles and furniture, died on Tuesday at his home in Charlotte, N.C. He was 57 years old.
        He died of AIDS after a long illness, his stepfather, Arthur Crocker, said.
        Mr. Putnam designed products for manufacturers of home furnishings. He was born in Manhattan and graduated from Princeton University and the Parsons School of Design. He moved to Charlotte from Manhattan in 1986.
        During the Korean War he served with Army counterintelligence.
        Besides his stepfather, Mr. Putnam is survived by his wife, the former Gail Wilkins; his mother, Barbara Crocker of Glen Cove, L.I.,; a brother, Tarrant, of Alexandria, Va.; and a stepdaughter, Elizabeth Hardison of North Augusta, S.C.

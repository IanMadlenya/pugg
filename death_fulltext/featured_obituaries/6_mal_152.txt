        Henry Hirsch, a founder and chairman emeritus of the Welbilt Corporation, died yesterday at the New York University Medical Center. A resident of Manhattan, he was 84 years old.
        In 1929, Mr. Hirsch and his brother Alexander founded the Welbilt Stove Company of Queens, maker of ranges for the home. The renamed company manufactures equipment for the food service and bakery industries.
        Mr. Hirsch was a founder of the Fifth Avenue Synagogue and of the Yeshiva Torah Vodaath & Mesivta of Brooklyn, which he also served as president.
        He is survived by his wife, Myrtle; two sons, Richard and David, of Manhattan; a daughter, Carole Friedman of Manhattan, nine grandchildren and two great-grandchildren.

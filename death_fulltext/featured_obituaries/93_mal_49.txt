
        Dr. Arnold Gottlieb, who practiced dentistry on Fifth Avenue for more than four decades, died on Oct. 6 at the New Canaan Racquet Club. He was 68 and a resident of New Canaan.
        The cause was cardiac arrest, his family said.
        Born in Brooklyn, Dr. Gottlieb graduated from City College and received his dental degree from the University of Pittsburgh in 1949.
        He was also a financial consultant and corporate officer, including president of the Henry Hudson Investment Group of Riverdale.
        Dr. Gottlieb is survived by his wife of 47 years, Joan Rigler Gottlieb; two daughters, Susan Shilleh of Armonk, N.Y., and Jane Gottlieb of Manhattan; a son, Jeffrey, of Glastonbury, Conn., and five grandchildren.

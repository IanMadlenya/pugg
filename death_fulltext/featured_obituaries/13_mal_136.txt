        Raymond W. Miller, a consultant to the Food and Agricultural Organization of the United Nations and a lecturer and writer on public relations, died of Parkinson's disease Friday at the Althea Woodland Nursing Home in Silver Spring, Md., where he lived. He was 93 years old.
        Mr. Miller was a founder of a public relations association that evolved into the Public Relations Society of America.
        From 1945 to 1947, Mr. Miller was president of the American Institute of Cooperation, a national educational organization for farmer cooperatives. He also wrote seven books.
        He is survived by a daughter, Ruth Powell of St. Helena, Calif.; a son, Robert, of Valley Forge, Pa., four grandchildren and eight great-grandchildren.

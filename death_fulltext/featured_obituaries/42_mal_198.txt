        James Carey, a retired vice president of the Bank of New York, died on Tuesday at the Princeton (N.J.) Medical Center. He was 84 years old and lived in Princeton.
        His family said he died of congestive heart failure.
        Mr. Carey was born in Mineola, L.I., and grew up in Short Hills, N.J. He was a graduate of the Hotchkiss School and of Princeton. He joined the bank in 1938 and retired in 1969. In 1957, he was elected president and chairman of The Seeing Eye Inc., of Morristown, N.J., an organization that provides guide dogs to blind people.
        His first wife, the former Elizabeth Lees Smith, died in 1977. He is survived by his second wife, the former Deborah Locke Livingston; two sons, George, of Amherst, Mass., and James Jr., of Dedham, Mass.; a daughter, Nancy Bicknell of Amherst, and six grandchildren.


        Dr. Kenneth W. Vaughn, a management consultant and a specialist in educational testing, died on May 9 at Good Samaritan Hospital in Portland, Ore. He was 80 years old and lived in Albany, Ore.
        He died of heart failure, his family said.
        Dr. Vaughn was a graduate of Western Illinois State College and held a doctorate from the University of Iowa. With the Carnegie Foundation for the Advancement of Education in the 1940's he contributed to the development of the Scholastic Aptitude Test and the General Equivalency Diploma.
        In management consulting he was with the firm of Booz Allen & Hamilton and with Rohrer, Hibler & Repogle, of which he became a partner.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Estel Boyd; two sons, Kenneth Jr., of Cleveland, and Paul, of Kent, Wash.; a daughter, Lucy Fleming of Montclair, N.J., and five grandchildren.


        Jacob Wendroff, rabbi of the Queens Jewish Center of Queens Village for the last 32 years, died on Sunday at New York Hospital-Cornell Medical Center. He was 68 years old and lived in Queens.
        Rabbi Wendroff died of complications from diabetes, his daughter, Mara, said.
        He was ordained in Brooklyn almost 50 years ago. Active in the religious and political life of Queens, he served on the board of governors of the New York Board of Rabbis, was a vice president of the Queens Jewish Community Council and was a member of the executive board of the Long Island Commission of Rabbis. He was a member of Queens Community Board No. 13.
        He was also involved in the United Jewish Appeal, the Federation of Jewish Philanthropies and the Zionist Organization of America. He was a chaplain at the New York Hospital and at the Hospital for Special Surgery.
        Earlier in his career he was rabbi of Congregation Emmanuel in Bronx Gardens and rabbi and principal of the Yeshiva Torah M'Zion in Brooklyn. He also served congregations in Colchester, Conn., and Hartford.
        Besides his daughter, Mara Baror of Bedford Hills, N.Y., he is survived by two sons, Michael, of Westport, Conn., and Joseph, of Queens, and five grandchildren.

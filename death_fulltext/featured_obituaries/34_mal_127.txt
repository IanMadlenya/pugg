        Carl D. Chambers, a former research director for the New York State Narcotic Addiction Control Commission, died of complications of a heart attack on Friday at West Pennsylvania Hospital in Pittburgh. He was 55 years old and lived in Athens, Ohio.
        In 1971, Dr. Chambers completed one of the first statewide studies of increases in drug use, a work that became a model for future drug studies. He was also the author of more than a dozen books on drugs and on problems of the elderly.
        From 1971 to 1973, he was co-director of the addiction sciences division at the University of Miami's medical school. He then became executive vice president of the Resource Planning Corporation, a social-science research concern in Washington.
        In 1980, he became professor and coordinator in the health services administration program at the College of Health and Human Services, at Ohio University in Athens.
        Dr. Chambers, who was born in Miami, Okla., graduated from Oklahoma State University. He received a Ph.D. from the University of Colorado and did postdoctoral work with the National Institute of Mental Health.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Kathryn Keplar; his mother, Clella Stephens of Athens; a daughter, Christine, also of Athens, and a brother, Lowell, of Perry, Fla.

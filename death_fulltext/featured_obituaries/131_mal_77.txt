
        David Howard Harris, a retired New Jersey lawyer who was a prosecutor and administrative judge in state and Federal government, died last Sunday at the Hospice of West Palm Beach in Florida. He was 89 and had lived in Morris Plains, N.J.
        Mr. Harris was an assistant prosecutor for Bergen County after World War II. His effectiveness in organized crime cases led to his appointment as a special assistant United States district attorney.
        He was an assistant prosecutor in Newark under Attorneys General Herbert Brownell and Robert F. Kennedy. President Richard M. Nixon named him an administrative law judge for the Food and Drug Administration in 1968. He later was Chief Administrative Judge for the Occupational Safety and Health Review Commission. He was in solo practice in East Orange from 1979 until his retirement 10 years ago.
        Mr. Harris was born in Newark. He fought in the infantry in Europe during World War II.
        He is survived by his wife, Rita Holover Harris; two sons, Evan, of Bridgewater, N.J., and Loren, of Manhattan, and five grandchildren.

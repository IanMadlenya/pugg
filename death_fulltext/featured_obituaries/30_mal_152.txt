        Dr. Max Hamburgh, a biologist and educator, died of congestive heart failure Wednesday at Columbia-Presbyterian Medical Center in Manhattan. He was 67 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Dr. Hamburgh taught in the biology department at City College of the City University of New York for 30 years. He joined the faculty in 1958, became a professor in 1970 and became head of the Sophie Davis Center for Biomedical Education at the college in 1973. He was named professor emeritus at his retirement in 1988.
        He is survived by his wife, Gertrude; a son, Mark, of Manhattan; a daughter, Annette Sebba of Fair Lawn, N.J., and three grandchildren.

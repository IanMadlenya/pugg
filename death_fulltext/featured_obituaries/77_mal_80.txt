
        Frank C. Campbell, a former executive with the New York Public Library for the Performing Arts at Lincoln Center, died on Saturday at St. Luke's-Roosevelt Hospital in Manhattan. He was 76.
        The cause was heart failure, the hospital said.
        Mr. Campbell was chief of the library's music division when he retired in 1985. Before joining the library in 1959, he was a music librarian for 16 years in the Library of Congress.
        Mr. Campbell was born in Winston-Salem, N.C. He attended Salem College there and received a master's degree in music in 1942 at the Eastman School of Music in Rochester.
        From 1950 to 1953, he was professor of music history at the American University in Washington. From 1953 to 1959, he was the music critic for The Washington Evening Star.
        He was president of the Music Library Association from 1967 to 1969.
        He is survived by his companion, Robert Heller, and his sister, Mary Marvel Shore of Greensboro, N.C.

        F. William Harder, a retired investment banker, died Wednesday at the Miami Heart Institute in Miami Beach. He was 84 years old and lived in Bal Harbour, Fla.
        Mr. Harder began his career in Syracuse in 1922 and joined Allen & Company, the New York investment concern, in 1938. He was president of the company and, later, chairman of its executive committee, until 1975.
        Mr. Harder owned the Elcee H. Stables and was a breeder of thoroughbred horses in Florida. He was also a trustee of Skidmore College.
        Surviving are his wife, Lois; three daughters, Betty McClellan of Williamsville, N.Y., Phyllis Reininger of Bellevue, Wash., and Joanne Woodward of Rancho Santa Fe, Calif.; 10 grandchildren, and 9 great-grandchildren.

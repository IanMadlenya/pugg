        Yevgeny Mravinsky, who became the chief conductor of the Leningrad Philharmonic Orchestra in 1938, has died of a heart attack, the Soviet press agency Tass reported. He was 81 years old. Tass did not say when or where he died.
        Mr. Mravinsky befriended the Soviet composer Dmitri Shostakovich 50 years ago while they were preparing the premiere of the composer's Fifth Symphony.

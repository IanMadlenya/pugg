
        Jefferson F. Armstrong, a former chairman of the Westchester County Board of Supervisors, died on March 3 at his home in Bronxville, N.Y.
        He was 95 years old and died of natural causes, his family said.
        Mr. Armstrong practiced law for 60 years. He served 19 years on the County Board representing the City of Yonkers, became the majority leader of the Republican board members and was board chairman in 1950 and 1951.
        He was also a former chairman of the Bronxville District Committee of Boy Scouts, commander of a local American Legion post and secretary of the Community Welfare Fund.
        Born in Denver, he grew up there and in Jamestown, N.Y. During World War I he served in the Navy. He was educated at the University of Utah, Purdue University and Fordham Law School.
        He began practicing law in 1924 at a New York City law firm. In 1931, he opened a private practice in Bronxville, specializing in estates. He retired from full-time practice several years ago.
        His wife, the former Dorothy Hall, died in 1982. A daughter, Cornelia Lee, is also deceased. He is survived by a son, Jefferson Jr. of Lancaster, Pa., and three grandchildren.

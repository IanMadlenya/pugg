        Roland Maycock, a retired senior vice president and general counsel of the Metropolitan Life Insurance Company, died Sunday at his home in North Tarrytown, N.Y., after a long illness. He was 74 years old. Born in New York City, Mr. Maycock attended DeWitt Clinton High School in the Bronx, Harvard College and Harvard Law School.
        He joined Metropolitan as an attorney in 1936, specializing in the area of company investments. He was named assistant general counsel in 1948 and senior vice president and general counsel in 1969. In 1971, he became a member of the company's executive committee. He retired in 1978.
        He is survived by his wife, Helen; two children, Heather Maycock Ogilvy and Dr. Thomas Maycock; and three grandchildren.

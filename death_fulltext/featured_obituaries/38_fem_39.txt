        Dr. Rita Wallsh, a psychiatrist in Manhattan, died of pancreatic cancer yesterday at the Mount Sinai Medical Center. She was 56 years old.
        Dr. Wallsh's clients included people in the fashion and theatrical industries. She was affiliated with Mount Sinai and was on the faculty of its medical school.
        While attending Lafayette High School in Brooklyn, she was also an award-winning concert pianist.
        Dr. Wallsh graduated from Brooklyn College and was a speech therapist before returning to school to study medicine. She studied at Columbia University and graduated from the New York University School of Medicine in 1964. After residency at Mount Sinai, she studied at the New York Psychoanalytic Institute.
        Surviving is her sister, Miriam Mechanic of Spring Valley, N.Y.

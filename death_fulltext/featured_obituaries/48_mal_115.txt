
        Dr. Henry W. Kumm, a Rockefeller Foundation specialist in tropical diseases and later the foundation's fellowships director, died Thursday at his home in Pennswood Village in Newtown, Pa. He was 89 years old.
        His death resulted from heart disease, a family spokesman said.
        Dr. Kumm conducted medical research, particularly in the prevention and control of malaria and yellow fever, in West Africa and in Central and South America for 23 years. His honors included the Order of the Southern Cross, the highest award granted by Brazil. From 1953 to 1959, he was research director of the National Foundation for Infantile Paralysis.
        Henry William Kumm was born in Wiesbaden, Germany, came to this country at 14 and was a graduate of Haverford College. He received doctorates in medicine and public health at John Hopkins University and interned at the Beijing Union Medical College Hospital in China.
        His wife of 62 years, Joyce, died two months ago.
        Surviving are two sons, William, a physicist and engineer of Severna Park, Md., and Frederick, a marketing specialist of Irvine, Calif.; a daughter, Joceline Alexander, a public health administrator of Austin, Tex.; a half brother, John of Quensland, Australia, eight grandchildren and three great-grandsons.

        Claude Arpels, who headed the American operations of the Van Cleef & Arpels jewelers, died on Monday in Switzerland, his family said today. He was 79 and lived in Manhattan.
        No details were disclosed about the cause of death.
        Mr. Arpels and his two brothers, Pierre and Jacques, were the heirs of the business founded in Paris by their parents and in-laws. Pierre Arpels died 10 years ago; Jacques Arpels is the chief executive.
        Claude Arpels spent virtually his entire career with Van Cleef & Arpels in the United States. He opened the New York store on Fifth Avenue in 1939, opened a second in Palm Beach, Fla., the next year, and in 1969 added a branch in Beverly Hills, Calif.
        He is survived by his wife, Malou; two sons, four daughters and four granchildren.

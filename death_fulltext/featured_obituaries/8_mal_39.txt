        Irve Tunick, a longtime scriptwriter and former president of the Eastern Region of the Television Writers of America, died of a cerebral hemorrhage Saturday at Putnam Hospital Center in Carmel, N.Y. He was 75 years old and a resident of Somers, N.Y.
        Mr. Tunick, who was born on July 27, 1912, in New York City, attended Georgetown University in Washington and New York University. After beginning his career writing radio scripts, he moved to Washington during World War II, where he wrote films used by the Federal Government to promote the war effort.
        Returning to New York after the war, Mr. Tunick became a scriptwriter in the developing television industry, and was a founder of the Eastern Region of the Television Writers of America. He wrote scripts for a wide range of television shows and series, including ''Studio One,'' ''The Armstrong Circle Theater,'' ''The Bold Ones,'' ''Bonanza,'' ''Ironside,'' ''Witness'' and ''The F.B.I.'' He also wrote one feature film, ''Murder Inc.,'' released in 1960.
        Mr. Tunick received the Robert E. Sherwood and George Foster Peabody Awards for achievements in the scriptwriting field.
        Mr. Tunick's first wife, Adele Lehnstul, died in 1981. He is survived by his second wife, Bea Greenberg; a son, Richard, of Scarsdale, N.Y.; two daughters: Carole Maxfield of Manhattan and Lisa Sarasohn of Portland, Ore., and eight grandchildren.

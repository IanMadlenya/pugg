        Robert F. Shea, a Red Cross executive who specialized in disaster preparedness, died Tuesday at the Fairfax Nursing Center in Fairfax, Va., after a long illness. He was 72 years old and lived in Fairfax.
        Mr. Shea, who was educated at St. Thomas College, was associated with the Red Cross for 43 years. He was chief executive of the American Red Cross of Greater New York from 1973 until his retirement in 1984. From 1955 to 1973 he served as vice president of the national organization in Washington and had served as national director for fund raising, executive assistant to the president and area manager of offices across the country.
        He is survived by his wife, Mary; two daughters, Maureen Cohen, of Phoenix, Ariz., and Sheila Langmaid, of Oakland, Calif.; a son, Robert, of Alexandria, Va; two stepsons, Eric Farrar, of Brooklyn, N.Y., and Andrew Farrar, of Huntington station, L.I.; a sister, Kathleen Ryan, and a brother, Dr. Andrew, both of Minneapolis; and six grandchildren.

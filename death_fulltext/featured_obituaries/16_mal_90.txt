        David L. Guyer, a former diplomat and president of Save the Children Federation from 1977 until last year, died of lymphoma of the brain Saturday in Honolulu. He was 62 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Guyer joined Save the Children as executive director in 1971. Six years later, he was elected president. The federation is active in the United States and 43 other countries. He retired last year because of failing health.
        Working for the United Nations from 1950 until 1960, Mr. Guyer held technical-assistance posts in Pakistan, India and Nepal. He was also a recruitment officer for North America.
        From 1960 to 1965, Mr. Guyer was a member of the United States mission to the United Nations, under Henry Cabot Lodge and Adlai E. Stevenson. In 1965, he was named vice president of the Institute for International Education, with responsibility for development and public affairs.
        Mr. Guyer was born in Pasadena, Calif. He was a 1948 graduate of Stanford University and had a master's from New York University. Mr. Guyer was in the Army Air Corps in World War II.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Carol Penney, a daughter of James Cash Penney; four daughters, Cynthia, of Portland, Ore., Alissa, of Honolulu, Shelly, of Berkeley, Calif., and Marion, of Albany, Calif.; two sons, Grant, of London, and Leigh, of New Orleans; a sister, Loraine Percy of Chicago; a brother, John, of Colombo, Sri Lanka, and two grandchildren.


        Arthur W. Glick, a former head of dermatology at Mount Sinai Medical Center in Manhattan, died at the hospital on Wednesday. He was 85 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        He died of a heart attack, his son, John, said.
        He joined Mount Sinai as a resident in 1938 and retired in 1981. He was a specialist in occupational skin diseases and had a private practice in Manhattan.
        He was the director of the department of dermatology from 1971 to 1974 and was an attending physician from 1962 to 1981. He was a former clinical professor of dermatology at the Mount Sinai School of Medicine.
        He received the hospital's distinguished service award at his retirement and was a consultant until 1985.
        Dr. Glick, a native of Toronto, graduated from the University of Pittsburgh and the University of Buffalo School of Medicine. He did postgraduate training in dermatology at what is now the Harris Skin and Cancer Unit of the New York University Medical Center.
        A widower, he is survived by a son, Dr. John H. Glick of Swarthmore, Pa., and two granddaughters. His companion in recent years was Dr. Mary Jane Cutler of Manhattan.

        Susumu Yamashita, a retired director and corporate secretary of Mitsubishi International Corporation, based in Manhattan, died of cardiac arrest Tuesday at Morristown (N.J.) Memorial Hospital. He was 84 years old and lived in Mendham, N.J.
        Mr. Yamashita was born in Oakland, Calif., but spent most of his youth in Tokyo. He was a graduate of the University of California at Berkeley.
        Mr. Yamashita joined the Mitsubishi Corporation in San Francisco in 1931. From 1942 to 1945 he was held in an internment camp in Topaz, Utah, for Japanese-Americans. In 1951 he opened the Mitsubishi offices in Manhattan, becoming director and corporate secretary. He was executive vice president and chief executive of Yusen Air and Sea Shipping Service from 1968 to 1979. He was a director of Tokyomyo Air Cargo Ltd. until 1987, when he retired.
        Mr. Yamashita is survived by his wife, the former Kiyoko Kitano; two daughters, Susan Bower of Los Angeles, and Evelynn Cauwell of Paris; three sons, Kenneth, of Stockton, Calif., Michael, of Mendham, and Alan, of Tokyo, and five grandchildren.

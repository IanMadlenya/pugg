        Paul Manfred Meyer, a concert pianist and vocal coach, died of complications resulting from pneumonia, Wednesday in Paramus, N.J. He was 96 years old.
        Mr. Meyer was a voice coach for the Metropolitan Opera from the 1940's to the 1960's. His students included Robert Merrill, Marian Anderson and Jan Peerce. He accompanied many of his pupils in recitals, and played the piano for many chamber-music concerts in New York. Mr. Meyer, who was born in Germany and came to the United States in 1938, continued to teach voice into the 1970's.
        There are no immediate survivors.

        Thomas J. Conlin, a retired colonel in the Army, died Wednesday at the Brookhaven Memorial Hospital in East Patchogue, L.I., after a long illness. He was 81 years old and lived in Bellport, L.I.
        Colonel Conlin, a native of Stamford, Conn., was an Army officer for more than 30 years, retiring in 1960. He was a regimental tank commander in the Battle of the Bulge in World War II and was in command of an armored unit in the Korean War. He took part in peace talks related to the Arab-Israeli war in 1947-48.
        He is survived by a son, Thomas G., of Bellport and a grandson.

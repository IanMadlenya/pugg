
       John Jay Corson 3d, a former Government official, educator and management consultant, died Sept. 2 at Arlington Hospital in Virginia. He was 84 years old and lived in Fairfax, Va.
        He died of complications from a stroke, said his son, John Jay 4th.
        Educated at the University of Virginia, Mr. Corson was assistant to the editor of The Richmond News Leader before joining the National Recovery Administration in 1933. He later served with the Social Security Administration as director of the Bureau of Old Age and Survivors Insurance.
        From 1943 to 1945, he was deputy director general of the United Nations Relief and Rehabilitation Administration. After World War II, he was circulation director for The Washington Post before becoming a managing partner in McKinsey & Company Inc., an international management consultant company. In addition, he was a professor at the Woodrow Wilson School at Princeton University. He retired from both positions in 1966.
        After retirement, Mr. Corson served as a special adviser to several secretaries of Health, Education and Welfare. He also served as president of the American Blood Commission and was a consultant to foreign governments.
        Mr. Corson was the author of a dozen books on government, public administration and economics, including ''Men Near the Top,'' ''Business in the Humane Society'' and ''Public Administration in Modern Society.'' He had also been a visiting professor at a variety of colleges and was a trustee at many educational institutions, including the Chicago Medical College, Sweet Briar College and George Mason University.
        In addition to his son, of Fairfax, Va., he is survived by a daughter, Nancy Gibbes, of Columbia, S.C., and six grandchildren.

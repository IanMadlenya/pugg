
        Stuart Johnston Northrop, a former chairman and chief executive of the Huffy Corporation, died on Saturday at a nursing home in Scottsdale, Ariz. Mr. Northrop, a resident of Naples, Fla., was 72.
        The cause of death was amyotrophic lateral sclerosis, which is known as Lou Gehrig's disease, Mr. Northrop's son, Richard, said.
        Mr. Northrop joined Huffy, which makes bicycles, garden tools and sports equipment and which provides business services to the retail industry, in 1972. He became chief executive in 1976 and chairman three years later. Mr. Northrop stepped down as chief executive in 1982, but remained chairman until his retirement in 1994.
        Mr. Northrop graduated from Yale University in 1948 with a bachelor's degree in physics. He was a vice president at the Singer Company before joining Huffy.
        Mr. Northrop is survived by his wife, Judith Stone Northrop of Naples; a daughter, Christine Daniell Northrop of Haverford, Pa.; his son, Richard R. S. Northrop of Greenwich, Conn.; a brother, Johnston Filmer Northrop of Greenwich, and three grandchildren.

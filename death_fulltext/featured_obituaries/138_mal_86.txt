
        Dr. Charles L. Wisseman, an expert on insect-borne diseases who headed the microbiology department at the University of Maryland School of Medicine for 32 years, died on July 12 at the University of Maryland Hospital in Baltimore. He was 78 and lived in nearby Cockeysville, Md.
        The cause was lung cancer, said his wife, Jane Wisseman.
        Dr. Wisseman was known for his research on typhus, Rocky Mountain spotted fever and related illnesses, known collectively as rickettsial diseases. He was an adviser to the World Health Organization and to the National Institutes of Halth and a number of other government agencies in the United States and abroad.
        He was born in Seguin, Tex., near San Antonio, and received a B.A. from Southern Methodist University,  an M.S. in parasitology from Kansas State College and, in 1945, his M.D. from Southwestern Medical School at the University of Texas at Dallas.
        In addition to his wife of 57 years, he is survived by a sister, Lola Johnson of Great Barrington, Mass.; two daughters, Dr. Jan Christie of Hull, England, and Mary Donahue of Cockeysville; two sons, Dr. Charles Wisseman 3d of Champaign, Ill., and Bob Wisseman of Corvallis, Ore., and six grandchildren.


        James E. Gerstner Sr., an executive at Bristol-Myers Squibb, died on Wednesday at Memorial Sloan-Kettering Cancer Center in Manhattan. He was 48 years old and lived in East Amwell, N.J.
        He died of complications from stomach cancer, his family said.
        Mr. Gerstner was a vice president of Convatec International, which manufactures medical devices. It is a division of Bristol-Myers Squibb, where he spent his career.
        Mr. Gerstner was born in Mineola, L.I. After graduating from Villanova University in 1964, he earned a master's degree in business administration from Columbia University in 1966.
        He is survived by his wife, Patricia; two daughters, Cynthia and Carolyn, and two sons, James and Gregory, all of East Amwell; his parents, Luis V. and Marjorie of Southampton, L.I., and three brothers, Richard T. of Stamford, Conn., Louis V. Jr. of Greenwich, Conn., and Joseph P. of Manhattan.

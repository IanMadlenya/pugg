        Philip Donham, a management consultant who was the co-author of a book on the inner workings of Congress, died Monday at Brigham and Women's Hospital in Boston. He was 82 years old and lived in North Weymouth, Mass.
        His son, Michael, said he died of complications of pneumonia.
        Mr. Donham was educated at Harvard University and graduated with a master's degree in business administration in 1933 and a doctorate in commercial science in 1936. He worked in Nashua, N.H., and Buffalo before becoming a senior management consultant with Booz Allen & Hamilton in Manhattan in 1947. In 1956, he joined Arthur D. Little in Cambridge and in 1967, along with Robert J. Fahey, was the co-author of ''Congress Needs Help,'' published by Random House. He left in 1971 to form his own company and retired in 1978. He then became a volunteer with Score, the Service Corps of Retired Executives.
        In addition to his son, Michael of Watertown, Mass., he is survived by his wife, Eleanor; another son, Parker of Sidney, Nova Scotia; two daughters, Ann Molison of Ames, Iowa, and Gay Quereau of Cleveland Heights, Ohio; a brother, Paul of Orleans, Mass., and 11 grandchildren.

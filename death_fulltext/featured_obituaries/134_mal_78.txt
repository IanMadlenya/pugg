
        Greg MacGregor, a foreign correspondent who reported on the Korean War as well as economic and political developments in Southeast Asia during a 17-year career at The New York Times, died on Sunday in Pompano Beach, Fla. He was 85.
        The cause of death was cancer, according to his family.
        A former marine who served in World War II, Mr. MacGregor joined The Times in 1948 and became a correspondent in Korea in 1952 and in Singapore in 1956. He returned to New York in 1960 as a city reporter.
        Mr. MacGregor, a native of San Francisco, studied journalism at the University of California at Berkeley. He left The Times in 1965 to be editor of The Latin American Times, an English-language newspaper, now defunct, that focused on Latin politics. He also worked in public relations before retiring in 1988.
        Mr. MacGregor is survived by his wife of 16 years, Anita, of Pompano Beach; three sons, Fletcher, of Detroit, and Ronald and Philip, both of New York; a daughter, Jean MacGregor Brown of Jamestown, R.I.; a stepson, David Randolph of Boulder, Colo.; a stepdaughter; Tracey Ballesteros of Albany, Calif; three granddaughters; a grandson, and a step-grandson.

        Leone J. Peters, the former chairman and chief executive of Cushman & Wakefield, a real-estate concern in Manhattan, died of heart failure Saturday after open-heart surgery at the University of Pennsylvania Hospital in Philadelphia. He was 76 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Peters, who joined Cushman & Wakefield in 1929, is credited with transforming the company from a family-owned building management concern to a leading national real-estate services organization. An avid horseman, he was a co-owner of the 1982 Kentucky Derby winner, Gato del Sol.
        He is survived by his wife, Betty Martone; two children, Paul, of Manhasset, L.I., and Gail Peters Beitz of Manhattan; three brothers, William, of Boca Raton, Fla.; Anthony, of Longboat Key, Fla., and Vincent, of Manhattan; a sister, Dina Boylan of Manhattan, and four grandchildren.


        Abe Newborn, a longtime theatrical agent, died on Aug. 12 at Mount Sinai Hospital in Manhattan. He was 77 and lived in Manhattan.
        The cause was congestive heart failure, said his son, Sam.
        After founding the Baum-Newborn  agency with Marty Baum in 1948, Mr. Newborn represented scores of actors, composers and lyricists. His client list included Robert Alda, Vivian Blaine, Jose Ferrer, Hume Cronyn, Joel Grey, Tony Roberts and Betty Buckley. He also represented Cy Coleman, Meredith Willson, Dorothy Fields and Martin Charnin.
        In addition to his son, of New Rochelle, N.Y., he is survived by his wife, Joyce; a daughter, Marian Newborn Tenenbein of Manhattan, and five grandchildren.

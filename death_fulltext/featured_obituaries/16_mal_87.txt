        Milton U. Copland, for many years a top officer of the Uris Buildings Corporation, a major developer of Manhattan office towers, died of cancer of the colon yesterday at his home in Manhattan. He was 79 years old.
        He was born in Brooklyn and received a law degree from Harvard in 1932. Mr. Copland was a first lieutenant in the United States Army Transportation Corps in World War II.
        He joined Uris in 1953 and was named corporate secretary. He was president of the Uris Organization, which manages office properties, and director of the Uris Brothers Foundation, a philanthropic organization.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Helen Weiner; two daughters, Nancy Stein of Chappaqua, N.Y., and Ronda Copland of Manhattan, and three grandchildren.

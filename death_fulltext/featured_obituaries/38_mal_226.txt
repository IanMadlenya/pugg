        George A. McKenna, a retired battalion chief in the New York City Fire Department and a former manager of security at the Metropolitan Museum of Art, died on Sunday at North Shore Hospital in Manhasset, L.I., after a long illness. He was 79 years old and lived in Holliswood, Queens.
        Mr. McKenna retired from the Fire Department in 1956 and joined the Metropolitan Museum. He retired from the museum in 1976. He also was a security consultant to the Museum of the City of New York, the New York Botanical Gardens and other centers. For many years, he was a member of the National Fire Prevention Association, helping to draft standards for fire safety in museums.
        He is survived by his wife, Theresa; a daughter, Linda Roxe of Chicago; a sister, Irene Brosnan of Port Washington, L.I.; a brother, the Rev. Joseph McKenna of the Bronx, and a grandson.

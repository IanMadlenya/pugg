        Seymour B. Wyler, owner of S. Wyler Inc., an East Side antiques store specializing in English silver and porcelain, died yesterday at Lenox Hill Hospital after a long illness. He was 79 years old and lived in Manhattan and Palm Beach, Fla.
        Mr. Wyler, who was born and raised in New York, was president of the company founded by his father, Sigmund Wyler, in 1890. After 58 years on Madison Avenue near 63d Street, the store was moved last year to Lexington Avenue near 69th Street. Mr. Wyler traveled extensively in his work and was the author of two books on silver, ''The Book of Old Silver'' (1937, Crown Publishers) and ''The Book of Old Sheffield Plate'' (1949, Crown Publishers).
        He is survived by his wife, the former Felice Margulies; a daughter, Wendy Weinstein of White Plains; a son, Richard, of Chappaqua, N.Y.; a brother, Wilfred, of Manhattan, and five grandchildren.

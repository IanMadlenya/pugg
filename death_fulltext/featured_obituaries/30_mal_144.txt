        Louis A. Siegel, a corporate lawyer and business executive, died of congenital heart disease Tuesday at Berkshire Medical Center in Pittsfield, Mass. He was 75 years old and had homes in Manhattan and Stockbridge, Mass.
        A graduate of Brooklyn Law School, Mr. Siegel was general counsel and executive vice president of Lee National Corporation, a subsidiary of the ADCO Group, at his retirement earlier this year. Previously, he was president of the Franchard Corporation, a forerunner of Lee National; president of the real estate concern, the Glickman Corporation, and was with Hearn department stores for 19 years as a vice president and counsel directing its real estate activities.
        He is survived by his wife, Beatrice; two daughters, Cynthia A. Lepouper and Helene Goodner, both of Miami; a son, Dr. Richard, of Honolulu, and six grandchildren.

        Philip Adler, a theater manager and producer, died yesterday in his home in Pasadena, Calif. He was 84 years old.
        Mr. Adler was general manager for scores of Broadway and road shows from the 1930's to the late 1960's, including ''Tobacco Road,'' ''Call Me Mister,'' ''Gentlemen Prefer Blondes,'' ''My Fair Lady,'' ''Applause,'' ''Seesaw,'' ''No Exit'' and ''The Great White Hope.'' He managed shows for such producers as Herman Levin and Alexander Cohen, and produced several plays himself.
        Mr. Adler is survived by his wife, Polly, of Pasadena, and two sons, Jerry, of Burbank, Calif., and Michael, of Manhattan.

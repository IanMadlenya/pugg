        Spear Knebel, a former Episcopal priest and youth counselor in New York City, died of heart disease Monday at Berkshire Medical Center in Pittsfield, Mass. He was 87 years old and lived in Canaan, N.Y.
        Mr. Knebel, a 1928 graduate of Union Theological Seminary, was ordained a priest in 1932. He was rector of St. Paul's Church in Woodside, Queens, became a counselor with delinquent youth and later worked in state government. He retired in 1973.
        He is survived by a son, Theodore, of Berne, N.Y.; two daughters, Dr. Ruth Deuel, of Clayton, Mo., and Marguerite Frongillo, of Brooktondale, N.Y, and two brothers, Lewis, of College Park, Md., and Fletcher, of Honolulu.


        Thomas R. Dash, a longtime drama critic for Women's Wear Daily, the garment-industry trade paper, died on Tuesday at St. Mary's Hospital in West Palm Beach, Fla. He was 93 years old and lived in West Palm Beach.
        He died of a heart attack, his wife, Ruby, said.
        Mr. Dash joined Fairchild Publications, publisher of Women's Wear Daily, in 1919, shortly after graduating from Columbia University, where he majored in journalism.
        He held editorial positions before becoming a critic in 1947. He retired as critic and theater editor in 1962, then was a drama critic and editorial adviser at Show Business.
        Mr. Dash was a former president of the New York Drama Critics Circle and the Drama Desk.
        Surviving besides his wife are a daughter, Naomi Newman of New York City and Woodstock, N.Y., three grandchildren and three great-grandchildren.

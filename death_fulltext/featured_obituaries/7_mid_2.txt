        Mary Cover Jones, most remembered for the Oakland Growth Study of youths at the Institute of Human Development at the University of California at Berkeley, has died at the age of 91.
        Dr. Jones, a professor of education and a researcher at the university, died July 22 of a brief illness. The study involved following the lives of a group of people who were preadolescents when Dr. Jones began studying them in 1931. She continued her research until last year, for a total of 55 years.
        Dr. Jones's husband, Dr. Harold E. Jones, was the director of the school's Institute of Child Welfare from 1935 until his death in 1960.

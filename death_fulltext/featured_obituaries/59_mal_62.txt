
        Willoughby F. Brazeau, a retired business executive who promoted foreign-trade groups and was decorated by five nations for his service in World War II, died on Friday at Calvary Hospital in the Bronx. He was 87 years old and lived in Garden City, L.I.
        He died of cancer, his family said.
        Mr. Brazeau was a former president of the National Association of Export Managers, chairman of the World Trade Committee of the New York Chamber of Commerce and Industry, a director of the French Chamber of Commerce and a member of the New York Regional Export Expansion Council.
        He retired in 1987, after 27 years as president of the American Steel Export Company in Manhattan. He had been president of the International Selling and Intsel Metals Corporations in New York. Intsel managed ores and metals for W. R. Grace & Co. Mr. Brazeau was also vice president of the Mercantile Metal and Ore Corporation, also in New York.
        He was born in Port Leyden, N.Y. Mr. Brazeau graduated from Hamilton College and did postgraduate study at Columbia and Hofstra Universities. In World War II he was a lieutenant colonel in military intelligence for the Army General Staff and was chief of the French and Air sections of the foreign liaison office. He was awarded the French Legion of Honor Chevalier as well as decorations from Norway, the Netherlands, China and Poland.
        Surviving are two daughters, Ann, of Falls Church, Va., and Elizabeth, of Roslyn Heights, L.I.; a son, Thomas, of Scott Depot, W. Va.; two granddaughters and two sisters, Ruth Dorn and Marion Brazeau, both of Utica, N.Y.

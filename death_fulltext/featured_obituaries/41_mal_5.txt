        Kipp Alan Rogers, a lawyer who represented entertainment figures and organizations, died of AIDS on Saturday at his home in Los Angeles. He was 32 years old.
        Before moving to Los Angeles last year to become the director of business affairs for Home Box Office, Mr. Rogers was a lawyer at the New York firm of Paul, Weiss, Rifkind, Wharton & Garrison. While there he worked with theater, film and television clients and did legal work on plays, including ''The Phantom of the Opera,'' ''Starlight Express'' and ''Lend Me a Tenor.'' He was also a pro bono counsel for a 1987 benefit at Lincoln Center that raised about $1.3 million for AIDS care, research and education.
        Mr. Rogers was born and grew up in Wellington, Kan., and graduated with honors in economics from Harvard College in 1979. He worked for a year for the producer Richard Horner in New York, then returned to Cambridge and graduated with honors from the Harvard Law School in 1983.
        He was a corporate lawyer on Wall Street for a year, at Cravath, Swaine & Moore, before joining Paul, Weiss, Rifkind, Wharton & Garrison.
        Mr. Rogers's companion was Harold Gunn of Los Angeles.
        Surviving are Mr. Rogers's parents, Mr. and Mrs. Gerald Rogers, of Wellington; two brothers, Jeffrey, of Wellington, and John, of New Haven, and a sister, Anne, of Belchertown, Mass.

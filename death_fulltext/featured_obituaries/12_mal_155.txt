        Milton H. Adler, a former assistant attorney general in New York, died of a heart attack Thursday at his home in Manhattan. He was 82 years old.
        Mr. Adler was born in the Bronx, attended public schools and received his law degree from St. John's Law School.
        After law school, he entered private practice in Manhattan with his brother, Alfred H. Adler, and became an active member of the West Side RepublicanClub. In 1956 and 1958 he was an unsuccessful candidate for the House of Representatives from Manhattan.
        In 1958, he was named an assistant attorney general, a position he held for two decades.
        He is survived by a daughter, Zita Gulitz of Yorktown Heights, N.Y., and two grandchildren.

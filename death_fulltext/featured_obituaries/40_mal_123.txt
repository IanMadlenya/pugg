        Henry P. Hayes, a retired executive of the National Broadcasting Company, died of complications from a series of strokes Monday at Meadow Lakes, a retirement community in Hightstown, N.J., where he lived. He was 83 years old and had been a longtime resident of Manhattan.
        Mr. Hayes was born in Jersey City and was a graduate of Brown. He was with NBC from 1930 to 1960 and served as business manager and later as manager of radio recording. He left to join the Television Bureau of Advertising, where he became a vice president before he retired in 1976.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Amy Nell Walker.

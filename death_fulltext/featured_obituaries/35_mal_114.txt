        Franklin P. Darmory, president of the Alcide Corporation of Norwalk, Conn., died of a heart attack on Dec. 7 while in London on a business trip. He was 46 years old and lived in Westport, Conn.
        Before joining Alcide, which makes health products for people and animals, Mr. Darmory was a vice president of the United States affiliate of Rhone-Poulenc, the French chemical company, and lived for many years in Westfield, N.J.
        Mr. Darmory was born in New York City and received his bachelor's degree from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology and a Ph.D. from Columbia University.
        He is survived by his wife, Marsha, and two children, Suzanne and Paul, both of Westport.

        Charles A. Bucher, a professor at New York University for 35 years, died of cancer Thursday at the Nathan Adelson Hospice in Las Vegas, Nev. He was 75 years old and lived in Las Vegas.
        He taught in the school of education at N.Y.U. before he retired in 1979 and became a professor at the University of Nevada. He was president and executive director of the National Fitness Leaders Assocation from 1982 until his death, and he was a delegate to President Eisenhower's White House Conference on Physical Fitness.
        Professor Bucher received a bachelor's degree from Ohio Wesleyan University, a master's degree from Columbia University and a doctorate from N.Y.U.
        He is survived by his wife, Jacqueline; two daughters, Diana Grantz of Lafayette, Calif., and Nancy MacCarric of Ithaca, N.Y.; two sons, Richard of Eldersburg, Md., and Jerry of Merrick, N.Y.; three brothers, Glenn of Alfred, N.Y., Morris of New Fane, N.Y., and Quentin of Hamburg, N.Y.; a sister, Virginia Johnston of Amherst, N.Y., and nine grandchildren.

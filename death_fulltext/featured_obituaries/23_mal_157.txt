        Norman W. Elson, a manager of New York movie theaters for more than 50 years, died Monday at Lenox Hill Hospital in Manhattan. He was 81 years old.
        Mr. Elson began operating movie theaters in Times Square in 1932. He ran Guild Enterprises, a theater concern, from the 1950's until his death. The chain included the Embassy 1, 2, 3 and 4 theaters, the Guild 50th Street theater and the Embassy 72d Street theater, which recently closed.
        He is survived by his wife, Peggie, and two sons, Peter and Paul, all of Manhattan; a daughter, Patricia Altorfer, of Springfield, Ill., and two grandchildren.

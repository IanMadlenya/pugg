        Stanley Butcher Kirk, a retired vice president of Union Carbide, died Thursday at New Rochelle (N.Y.) Medical Center. He was 97 years old and lived in New Rochelle.
        Mr. Kirk, a 1913 graduate of the University of Wisconsin, joined Union Carbide in 1915 as a salesman. He was named a vice president of Union Carbide & Carbon Company in 1945. He retired in 1959.
        He is survived by two daughters, Marion Marshall and Jane Duffy, both of Greenwich, Conn.; a brother, William, of Minneapolis; six grandchildren, and 11 great-grandchildren.


        Edward J. Grenier, a lawyer who practiced in New York City for more than 50 years, died on Sept. 11 at his home. He was 87 years old.
        His son, Edward J. Grenier Jr., gave no specific cause of death.
        Mr. Grenier, a Manhattan resident since 1927, was in private practice for many years. In the early 1950's, he was a co-founder of the firm of Grenier, Humes & Nolan, from which he retired in 1990. Mr. Grenier, a graduate of St. Lawrence University, specialized in estate, trust and probate law.
        A native of Worcester, Mass., Mr. Grenier, who was of French-Canadian descent, was a member of the Canadian Society of New York for more than 50 years. He was made a Chevalier of the National Legion of Honor by the French Government and was a Knight of Malta and a Knight of the Holy Sepulcher.
        Mr. Grenier is survived by his wife of nearly 60 years, Jane; his son, Edward Jr., of Potomac, Md.; a sister, Florence Cornwell, of Worcester, and three grandchildren.

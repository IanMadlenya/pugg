        William B. Bergen, a retired aerospace executive, died Friday at his home in St. Michaels, Md., of cancer of the lung and esophagus. He was 72 years old.
        Mr. Bergen, a graduate of Massachusetts Institute of Technology, was president of the space division of North American Aviation Inc., the prime contractor on the Apollo space program, from 1967 to 1970. He left the company, whose name by then was Rockwell International, in 1978.
        He is survived by his wife, Eleanor; a son, William Jr. of Rodgers Forge, Md.; a daughter, Lynn Bergen of Phoenix, Md., and two brothers, Richard of Winter Park, Fla., and Wilbur of Jupiter, Fla.

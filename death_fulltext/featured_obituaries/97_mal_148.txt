
        Dr. Joseph D. Brenna, former president of the medical staff at St. Francis Medical Center in Trenton, died on Thursday at the hospital. He was 74.
        The cause was bladder cancer, family members said.
        Dr. Brenna, who lived in Trenton, was a general surgeon at St. Francis from 1948 until his retirement in 1989. He was the hospital's director of surgery from 1968 to 1970 and was president of the medical staff from 1987 to 1989.
        He was surgical consultant to the New Jersey Department of Corrections from 1963 to 1978. After his retirement from active practice, he was medical director of Aetna Health Plans for New Jersey, based in New Brunswick.
        Born in Trenton, Dr. Brenna graduated in 1941 from Duke University, from which he received his medical degree in 1944.
        Dr. Brenna is survived by his wife, Rosalie; three daughters, Diana and Susan, of New York, and Amy, of Philadelphia; a son, Joseph, of Phillipsburg, N.J.; a brother, Anthony, of Morrisville, Pa.; two sisters, Maria Sheridan of Yardley, Pa., and Mina Palidar of Bordentown, N.J., and two grandsons.

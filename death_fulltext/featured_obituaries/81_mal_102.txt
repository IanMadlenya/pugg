
        Herbert J. Osborne, an executive for more than 40 years with the bank that grew into the Manufacturers Hanover Trust Company, died on Wednesday at a nursing home in Mountainside, N.J. He was 96 and had lived in Summit, N.J., most of his life.
        He died of natural causes, his family said.
        Mr. Osborne was born in Brooklyn and worked during high school to support his family. After serving on the Kansas during World War I, he started his career as a trainee at the Central Union Trust Company, which after a series of mergers became Manufacturers Hanover Trust Company. The bank is now known as the Chemical Banking Corporation after a 1991 merger with Chemical Bank.
        He served for several years as senior lending officer for the Hanover Bank and as head of the bank's metropolitan division, working in commercial finance and with factoring companies. In 1936 he was appointed vice president and in 1950 he was appointed senior vice president of the bank. He retired in 1962 as senior vice president of Manufacturers Hanover Trust Company.
        Mr. Osborne also served on the boards of several corporations as director or as chairman of a finance committee. The companies he served included the Boorum & Pease Company, Mills Factors Corporation and Hygrade Food Products Corporation, now part of the Sara Lee Corporation.
        He is survived by two sons, Robert E., of Sydney, Australia, and Dr. Alan W., of Westfield, N.J.; 14 grandchildren, and four great-grandchildren. His wife, the former Helen Concagh, died in 1983, and a son, Donald, died in 1985.

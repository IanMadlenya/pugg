        Alfred Kalfus, a mathematics teacher who was awarded a Presidential citation for teaching excellence in 1983, died of a heart attack Wednesday at North Shore Hospital in Manhasset, L.I. He was 67 years old and lived in Roslyn, L.I.
        Mr. Kalfus, who earned master's degrees from New York University and Adelphi University, was a mathematics professor at Hofstra University and had taught at many colleges in Nassau and Suffolk Counties. He had also taught at Babylon and Amityville high schools.
        He was a founder of several mathematics organizations, including the Long Island Mathematics Fair, the Senior High School Interscholastic Mathematics League of Suffolk County and the United States Mathematics Olympiad. He was also founder and president for many years of the New York State and the American Region mathematics leagues.
        He is survived by his wife, Marlene; a daughter, Suzanne, of Washington; a son, Daniel, of Oakland, Calif., and two sisters, Hilda Briner and Muriel Smith, both of Miami.

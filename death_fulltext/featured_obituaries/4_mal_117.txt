        John J. Schieffelin, a World War I naval aviator and former official of the English-Speaking Union of the United States, died of cancer Sunday at his Manhattan apartment. He was 89 years old.
        Mr. Schieffelin, a descendant of John Jay, the first Chief Justice of the United States, was born in Scarborough-on-Hudson, N.Y. As an ensign in World War I, he won the Navy Cross for his actions as a pilot in damaging an enemy submarine in the North Sea.
        After the war, he completed his education at Yale and joined an insurance concern, Chubb & Son. He re-entered the Navy during World War II, and helped train recruits.
        From 1963 to 1966, he was executive director of the English-Speaking Union, which seeks to improve ties among English-speaking people of the world.
        He is survived by his wife, Lois; two sons, John Jr., of Florence, Mass., and Joseph, of Lakewood, Colo.; a daughter, Eleanor, of Immigrant, Mont.; two brothers, Elliot, of Palo Verde, Calif., and Bayard, of Short Hills, N.J.; a sister, Barbara Bosanquet of Northumberland, England, and 11 grandchildren.

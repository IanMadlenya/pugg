        Antonie Stolper, an author who was active in refugee affairs during World War II, died Tuesday at her home in Alexandria, Va. She was 97 years old.
        Mrs. Stolper, who came to New York from Germany in 1933, wrote several books in German on economics and worked with Self Help and the American Council for Emigres in the Professions, two resettlement agencies.
        She is survived by a son, Max, of Alexandria; a daughter, Joan Campbell of Toronto; two stepsons, Wolfgang, of Ann Arbor, Mich., and Ernst, of Vincentown, N.J.; nine grandchildren, and five great-grandchildren.

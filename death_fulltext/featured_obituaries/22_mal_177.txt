        Ranald Hugh Macdonald, a former stockbroker and investment banker, died of heart failure Sunday at the Hilaire Farm Nursing Home in Huntington, L.I. He was 97 years old and was a former resident of Manhattan.
        Mr. Macdonald, a 1915 graduate of Yale University, was a former partner in the brokerage firm of Dominick & Dominick and was a governor of the New York Stock Exchange from 1944 to 1950. He was a trustee of the Franklin Savings Bank from 1931 to 1961 and a trustee of the State Executive Committee of the Y.M.C.A.
        He was also a trustee of St. Paul's School in Concord, N.H., and served as chairman of the Yale Alumni Fund from 1943 to 1945.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Anne Thompson; a son, Ranald Jr., of Denver; a daughter, Anne M. Thomas of Locust Valley, L.I.; 10 grandchildren, and 2 great-grandchildren.

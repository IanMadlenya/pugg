
        Stewart Warner Pach, who ran the Gillette Company's razor business in the 1960's, died April 14 at New England Baptist Hospital in Boston following a heart attack, according to his son, Peter B. Pach. He was 79.
        Mr. Pach was born in Worcester, Mass., and reared in Bronxville, N.Y. He received a bachelor's degree in mechanical engineering in 1942 from Princeton University.
        During World War II, he served in the Army Air Corps. After the war, Mr. Pach, who was also a certified public accountant, joined Lybrand, Ross Bros. & Montgomery in New York, now PricewaterhouseCoopers.
        Mr. Pach joined the Gillette Company in Boston in 1951. He was named president of the Papermate pen division in 1960. Four years later, he was appointed president of the Gillette Safety Razor Company.
        In 1969 he became president and chief executive of the American Optical Company in Southbridge, Mass., then a division of the Warner-Lambert Company. Mr. Pach left the company in 1974 to become an independent general business consultant. He was also a painter, showing his work in Boston-area galleries.
        Mr. Pach is survived by his wife, Constance Barnard Pach of Dover, Mass.; his son, Peter, of Middle Haddam, Conn.; two daughters, Sandra Zebal of Westminster, Md., and Nicolette M., of Huntington Bay, N.Y.; a brother, G. Vincent, of Hopedale, Mass., and six grandchildren.

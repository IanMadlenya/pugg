
        Carl Hartdegen 2d, an official of the Palisades Geophysical Institute, died on Feb. 18 at the Cape Canaveral (Fla.) Hospital. He was 72 years old and lived in Cape Canaveral.
        He died of pneumonia and a heart attack, his family said.
        Mr. Hartdegen was director of the Cape Canaveral division of the institute, which has its corporate headquarters in West Nyack, N.Y. The institute has done research in underwater acoustics for the Navy since 1949.
        Mr. Hartdegan was born in Philadelphia and was a graduate of Lehigh University. In World War II he served in the Navy submarine service and was awarded a bronze star.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Mary Payson Greene; two daughters, Ann, of Chicago, and Cynthia, of Westfield, Mass.; a brother, Carl, also of Westfield, and two grandchildren.
        Correction:  March 5, 1991, Tuesday
        An obituary on Thursday about an official of the Palisades Geophysical Institute rendered his name incorrectly. He was Carl Hartdegen 3d, not 2d. The obituary also misidentified a survivor. Carl E. Hartdegen is his son, not his brother.


        Jay Blackton, a music director for movies and Broadway shows, died on Saturday at Granada Hills Community Hospital in Granada Hills, Calif. He was 84 and lived in Los Angeles.
        The cause was heart failure, said his wife, Louise.
        Mr. Blackton won an Academy Award in 1955 for the music direction of the film "Oklahoma!" He had also been nominated for his work on "Guys and Dolls."
        Mr. Blackton, who changed his name from Jay Schwartzdorf in 1943, became the assistant conductor of the New York Opera Comique shortly after his graduation from the Juilliard School. From 1937 to 1942, he conducted the St. Louis Municipal Opera.
        In 1943, Richard Rodgers and Oscar Hammerstein 2d hired him to conduct "Oklahoma!" on Broadway. He later conducted "Annie Get Your gun," "Call Me Madam," "Wish You Were Here," "New Faces of 1956" and other hit musicals. He conducted "The Magic of Cole Porter" at Lincoln Center in 1967 and the 1969 revival of "Oklahoma!"
        Mr. Blackton also directed the Los Angeles Civic Opera and occasionally conducted the Beverly Hills Symphony.
        In addition to his wife, he is survived by a daughter, Jennie Lou Blackton Philhower of Santa Monica, Calif.; a son, William, of Arlington, Va., and a grandson.

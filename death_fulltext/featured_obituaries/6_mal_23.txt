        George E. Spargo, former general manager of the Triborough Bridge and Tunnel Authority in New York City, died Saturday at the Miami Heart Institute. He was 84 years old.
        Mr. Spargo, a protege of Robert Moses, the urban builder, supervised construction of the Throgs Neck Bridge, the Verrazano-Narrows Bridge and the New York Coliseum.
        He is survived by his wife, Madeline, of Bal Harbour, Fla.; a daughter, Patricia Bryan, of Elkin, N.C., and three grandchildren.


        Randolph Linsly Simpson, a voice teacher and collector of black Americana, died on Jan. 6 at Veterans Hospital in West Haven, Conn. He was 64 years old and lived in Northford, Conn.
        He died of lung cancer, said his brother, Josiah, of South Salem, N.Y.
        Mr. Simpson was born in Rochester, N.Y. He served in the Navy during World War II and later studied voice in Europe. He taught voice for many years and was on the board of the Marcella Sembrich Memorial Association, which assists opera students.
        In 25 years, he built a collection of 6,000 items relating to black history in America. The collection, including letters, early prints, photographs, film clips and other memorabilia, is housed in the Wadsworth Atheneum in Hartford.
        Mr. Simpson is survived by his brother.

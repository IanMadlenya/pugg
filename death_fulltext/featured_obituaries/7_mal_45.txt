        The Rev. G. Paul Musselman, an Episcopal clergyman who was co-author of ''The Twelve Step Recovery Program of Alcoholics Anonymous,'' died Friday at his home in Daytona Beach, Fla. He was 92 years old.
        Mr. Musselman had been the minister at St. James Episcopal Church, in Ormond Beach, Fla., for the past nine years. He was a former executive director of the Department of Evangelism for the National Council of Churches in the United States, and director of his denomination's Division of Urban-Industrial Church Work for the executive council in Detroit.
        He wrote several books, including ''The Church on the Urban Frontier'' and ''The Twelve Step Recovery Program of Alcoholics Anonymous,'' which detailed the principles of the organization's program for recovery.
        George Paul Musselman was born in Lancaster, Pa., and graduated from the University of Pennsylvania in 1932. From 1937 to 1941 he was assistant rector of the Calvary Episcopal Church in Manhattan.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Dorothea Manna; two daughters, Polly Musselman of Maumee, Ohio, and Dorothy Ogelby of Sun City, Fla.; two stepsons, John and Mario Stemberger, and three stepdaughters Valeri, Rosemary and Judy Stemberger, all of Daytona Beach, and eight grandchildren.

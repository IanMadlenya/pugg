        Michael Mok, a journalist who won a half dozen awards for his reporting, died of a heart attack Friday at the Veterans Administration Hospital in Manhattan. He was 58 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Among the honors Mr. Mok won was the 1961 Albert Lasker Medical Journalism Award when he was with The New York World Telegram. At his death, he was doing freelance work.
        He is survived by his wife, Sylvia; two daughters, Jennifer Johnson of Santa Fe, N.M., and Rose Mary Magdelena of San Francisco; a son, Michel, of Manhattan; a sister, Susan Einarson of Manhattan, and a brother, Paul, of Dallas.

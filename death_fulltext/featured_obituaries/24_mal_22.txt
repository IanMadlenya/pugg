        Oliver L. Austin Jr., an ornithologist who wrote a definitive study, ''Birds of the World,'' in 1961 and who was curator emeritus of ornithology at the Florida Museum of Natural History, died Saturday at his retirement home in Gainesville, Fla. He was 85 years old and had lived for many years in Tuckahoe, N.Y., and in Wellfleet. Mass.
        In 1929, Dr. Austin and his father, a surgeon, founded the Austin Ornithological Research Station in Wellfleet, which he directed for a quarter-century before moving to Florida. In 1931, at the age of 28, he was elected to membership in the American Ornithological Union. He was also a member of the Explorers Club.
        A graduate of Wesleyan University, he held a doctorate from Harvard. After World War II service in the Navy he went to Japan, where he served in the Allied Military Goverment. He later wrote studies of birds in Japan and Korea.
        Dr. Austin is survived by his wife, Edythe; two sons, Anthony, of Harwichport, Mass., and Timothy, of Gainesville; a daughter, Valerie, of Charleston, W.Va.; a sister, Dorothy Houck of Medford, N.J., and three grandchildren.

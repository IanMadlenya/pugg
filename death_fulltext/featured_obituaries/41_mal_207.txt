        Charles S. Jensen, a retired executive of the CIT Corporation, died on Tuesday at St. Francis Hospital in Roslyn, L.I. He was 76 years old and lived in Manhasset, L.I.
        His family said he died of cancer.
        Mr. Jensen worked his entire career with CIT except for four years with the Federal Bureau of Investigation in World War II. At his retirement in 1978 he headed two subsidiaries of the parent corporation, now the CIT Group.
        He was born in Reno, Nev. and was an alumnus of the University of Nevada.
        He joined CIT in 1936 and became head of the New York division of the Universal CIT Credit Corporation in 1952. Later he was president of the Canadian Acceptance Corporation.
        In 1962, Mr. Jensen became president of the CIT Corporation, an industrial-finance company, and the CIT Leasing Corporation. In 1969 he became chairman of both. He was also a vice president and director of the parent company.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Eva Mae Beemer; two daughters, Karen Mount of Canandaigua, N.Y., and Linda Sendar of Croton-on-Hudson, N.Y.; a son, James, of Evanston, Ill.; a sister, Elna Robison of San Jose, Calif.; two brothers, Dyer, of Reno, and James, of Salt Lake City, and three grandchildren.

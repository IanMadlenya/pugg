        Dwight T. Myers, a onetime deputy commissioner and chief engineer for what was formerly the New York City Department of Traffic, died Sept. 9 at the Nyack Manor Nursing Home in Valley Cottage, N.Y. He was 84 years old and previously lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Myers was deputy commissioner from 1953 until 1956, when he became president of the Veon Chemical Corporation. The department later became a bureau in the City Department of Transportation.
        He studied engineering at the University of Pittsburgh and also studied at Yale University.
        He is survived by a son, Roger T. Myers of West Nyack, N.Y.; a sister, Helen M. Gillespie of Johnstown, Pa., and a brother, Robert T. Myers of Pittsburgh.

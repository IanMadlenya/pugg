
        Walter F. O'Connell, a retired executive of the Olin Corporation, died on Friday at his home in Aiken, S.C. He was 78 years old.
        He died of cancer, the funeral home said.
        Mr. O'Connell was a partner in the accounting firm of A. M. Pullen & Company, and headed his own tax and financial consulting concern before joining Olin Industries. In the 1950's he was the company's vice president for finance and a senior vice president in charge of Olin's alumninum division as well as head of an Olin subsidiary, the United Nuclear Corporation. He was elected a director in 1964 and served until his retirement in 1986.  An Honorary Degree
        Mr. O'Connell was a director of several other corporations, and a trustee of the John M. Olin Foundation, of New York City, the Tax Foundation, of Washington, and the Aiken Day School, in Aiken, S.C. The University of South Carolina-Aiken named two buildings after Mr. O'Connell and his wife, Pauline, and awarded Mr. O'Connell an honorary degree of Doctor of Business Administration in 1990.
        Besides his wife, Mr. O'Connell is survived by three sons, Michael K., of Los Angeles, Walter F. Jr, of Waterbury, Conn., and Edward T., of Beaufort, S.C.; four daughters, Frances Rust of Briarcliff Manor, N.Y., Roberta Gibson, of Ponte Vedra Beach, Fla., Martha Ann O'Connell of Burlington, Vt., and Pauline Blair, of Glenville, N.C.; two stepchildren, Edward Forney Morrison, of Salt Lake City, and Ann-Whitney Morrison, of Fairfax, Va.; a sister, Myra Rose of Sandwich, Mass.; two brothers, Michael, of Rockville, Md., and Leo, of Redondo Beach, Calif., and twenty-three grandchildren.

        Prof. Peter H. von Blanckenhagen, a scholar of Greek and Roman art who was the Robert Lehman Professor Emeritus of Fine Arts at the New York University Institute of Fine Arts, died of complications from a respiratory ailment yesterday at Lenox Hill Hospital. He was 80 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Professor Blanckenhagen was known for his studies of architecture, sculpture and painting in the ancient world. He received the Gold Medal of the Archeological Institute of America in 1982.
        He was born in Riga, Latvia and received a doctorate from the University of Munich. He taught at the University of Hamburg before coming here after World War II to teach at the University of Chicago and Harvard. In 1959 he joined the Institute of Fine Arts, remaining there until 1979.
        There are no immediate survivors.

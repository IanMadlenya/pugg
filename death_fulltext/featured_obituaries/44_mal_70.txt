        Daniel M. Feins, a teacher and an administrator in the New York City public schools for almost 40 years and the former principal of Walton High School in the Bronx, died on Monday at Lawrence Hospital in Bronxville, N.Y. He was 73 years old and lived in Yonkers.
        He died of cardiac arrest, a daughter, Judith D. Feins of Belmont, Mass., said.
        Mr. Feins began teaching in 1939 after graduating with a master's degree in history from Columbia University. During World War II, he served in the Army Medical Corps as a first lieutenant. He returned to teaching at Columbus High School in the Bronx and later was chairman of the social studies department at Prospect Heights High School in Brooklyn. He became principal of Walton High School in 1966 and retired in 1979.
        He was a John Hay Fellow and served as president of the John Hay Association of New York. Along with Gerald Leinwand, he was the co-author of a book on the teaching of history and social studies in the secondary schools.
        Besides his daughter Judith, he is survived by his wife, Claire; another daughter, Roberta P. Feins of Seattle, and two grandchildren.

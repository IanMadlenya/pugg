        Jordan Olivar, former head football coach at Yale, Villanova and Loyola Marymount, died of lung cancer Wednesday at his home in Inglewood, Calif. He was 75 years old.
        Mr. Olivar coached at Yale from 1952 to 1962, compiling a 61-32-6 record in his 11 seasons. His 1956 and 1960 teams won the Ivy League championship. The 1960 squad finished 9-0, earning a share of the Lambert Trophy, symbolizing the East's top team.
        Mr. Olivar, a native of Brooklyn, graduated from high school at age 15. He graduated from Villanova College in 1938 with highest academic honors on a football scholarship.
        After several years of high school coaching and teaching, Mr. Olivar returned to his alma mater in 1942 as head football coach, serving for six seasons. His teams compiled a 33-20 record from 1943 to 1948.
        After Villanova won the Harbor Bowl in San Diego, Mr. Olivar accepted the head coaching position at Loyola Marymount, where he coached from 1949 to 1951.
        Mr. Olivar is survived by his wife, Stella; twin children, Harriet Pilmanis, of Boulder, Colo., and Harry Olivar, of Los Angeles, and nine grandchildren.

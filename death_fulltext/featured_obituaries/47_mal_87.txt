
        Stephen P. Berner, the former head of educational publishing at Random House, died Dec. 2 at his home in Manhattan. He was 56 years old.
        He died of a heart attack, his wife, Sherry, said.
        Mr. Berner was president of Contemporary Perspectives Inc., a company he started in 1976 to prepare textbooks for educational publishers. Before that, he was an executive vice president of Random House beginning in 1965, and from 1963 to 1965, he was a vice president with Encyclopedia Brittanica.
        Mr. Berner received a master's degree from New York University in 1957. He was a science consultant in the Manhasset School District on Long Island and entered publishing in 1958 through Learning Materials Inc., a company that developed experiment kits for elementary school children. It was eventually sold to Encyclopedia Brittanica.
        In addition to his wife, he is survived by a son, Scott, of Queens; a daughter, Rickie Black of Orlando, Fla.; three stepdaughters, Brett Finley of Manhattan, Dru Finley of Beijing and Erin Finley of East Quogue, L.I.; a sister, Barbara Berner of Manhattan, and two granddaughters.

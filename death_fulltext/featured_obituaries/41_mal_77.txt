        John E. Stambaugh, a professor of classics at Williams College, died on Wednesday at his home in Williamstown, Mass., after a long illness. He was 50 years old.
        Mr. Stambaugh taught at the school since 1965 and served several terms as the chairman and acting chairman of the classics department. He specialized in the history and sociology of Greek and Roman religions and of early Christianity.
        He was born in Harrisburg, Pa., graduated summa cum laude from Trinity College in Hartford and received a Ph.D. from Princeton University.
        He is survived by his wife, Paula, and two sons, John, of San Diego, and Thomas, of Bryn Mawr, Pa.

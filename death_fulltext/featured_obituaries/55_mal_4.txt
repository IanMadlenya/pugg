
        Jon Tuttle, a television reporter, commentator and documentary producer in Portland, Ore.,, died Sunday at Emanuel Hospital there.
        He was 49 years old and died of complications from leukemia, The Associated Press reported.
        Mr. Tuttle was a member of the KGW-TV news staff in Portland from 1966 to 1989 and was a documentary producer for Oregon Public Broadcasting since September 1989.
        In 1984 Mr. Tuttle won a Peabody award for "Rajneesh: Update", a documentary on the Oregon cult leader Bhagwan Shree Rajneesh and his followers. Earlier this year he won the gold plaque at the Chicago Film Festival for "Anatomy of an Oil Spill," an analysis of the Exxon Valdez oil spill.
        He is survived by a son, Jonathan Tuttle; a daughter, Kelen Tuttle; two sisters, Patricia Budzinski and Fran Leahy, and a brother, Greg Tuttle.

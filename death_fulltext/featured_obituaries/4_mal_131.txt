        Daniel Stern Schwartz, a real-estate investor who was active in New York charities, died of pnuemonia Thursday at Beth Israel Hospital. He was 94 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Schwartz owned and managed commercial and residential property in Manhattan and Brooklyn. He was chairman emeritus of the Brooklyn Philharmonic Orchestra and was a member of the national board of the Union of American Hebrew Congregations and of Hebrew Union College. He was a contributor to the Jewish Guild for the Blind, the United Hospital Fund and the National Association for the Advancement of Colored People, among other charities.
        Mr. Schwartz was born in Brooklyn and was a graduate of New York University.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Tillie Levinthal; and three nephews.

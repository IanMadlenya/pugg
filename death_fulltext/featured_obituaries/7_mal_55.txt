        Eugene Bordinat Jr., a former chief designer for the Ford Motor Company, died Tuesday at Henry Ford hospital here, the company said today. He was 67 years old.
        Mr. Bordinat, who retired as Ford's vice president for design in 1980, oversaw the styling of cars ranging from the Mustang and Falcon to the Lincoln Town Car. Before becoming chief designer in 1961, he headed styling operations in various Ford divisions. He joined Ford in 1947.
        Mr. Bordinat, a native of Toledo, Ohio, joined the General Motors Corporation in 1939 and became a tank production supervisor in World War II.

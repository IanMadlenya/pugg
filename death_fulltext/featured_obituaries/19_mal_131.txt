        Edward W. Duffy, retired chief executive of the USG Corporation, died of heart failure Monday at Evanston (Ill.) Hospital. He was 68 years old and lived in Glenview, Ill.
        Mr. Duffy graduated from LaSalle-Peru Junior College and attended Loyola University of Chicago. He joined what was then U.S. Gypsum in 1941 as a research chemist and rose to be president of the building materials company from 1971 to 1981. He was chairman and chief executive from 1983 until he retired in 1985.
        He is survived by his wife, Rosemary; a daughter, Jill Plaza of Chicago, and two grandchildren.


        The Rev. Arthur C. McGiffert Jr., a retired Congregationalist minister who was president of two seminaries, died on Friday in a retirement community in Claremont, Calif. He was 100.
        Mr. McGiffert served as president of the Chicago Theological Seminary from 1946 to 1958 and of the Pacific School of Religion in Berkeley, Calif., from 1939 to 1945. He was also a professor, pastor and author of religious articles and books.
        Born in Cincinnati, he grew up in Pelham Manor, N.Y. His father was a historian of Christian thought.
        After graduating from Harvard University in 1913, he won fellowships to Greece and later to Switzerland. He earned a bachelor of divinity degree from Union Theological Seminary and a master's at Columbia University, both in 1917.
        His early pastorates were in Rensselaerville, N.Y.; Roxbury, Vt.; New York City and Lowell, Mass. From 1926 to 1939, he was the director of studies and a professor at Chicago Theological. Aided Japanese-Americans
        At Berkeley he organized ecumenical training of pastors and lay leaders for postwar rehabilitation in Europe and China and helped form the Committee on American Principles and Fair Play to help interned Japanese-Americans. In Chicago he was the local chairman of the American Civil Liberties Union.
        After formally retiring in 1959, he remained active in academic, church and community affairs. A summer resident of Mount Desert Island, Me., he served on local church and hospital boards, was a founding trustee of the College of the Atlantic and taught at Bangor Theological Seminary.
        His wife of 74 years, the former Elisabeth Eliot, a granddaughter of Charles William Eliot, a Harvard president, died in 1991.
        Mr. McGiffert's survivors include two sons, David E. of Washington and Michael of Williamsburg, Va.; a daughter, Ellen M. Brokaw of Santa Paula, Calif.; seven grandchildren, and three great-grandchildren.

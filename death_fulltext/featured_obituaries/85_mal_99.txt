
        Maurice Mound, a founding partner of the law firm of Mound, Cotton & Wollan, died in Manhattan last Saturday. He was 86.
        Mr. Mound was graduated from Columbia College in 1928 and from Columbia Law School in 1930. He was admitted to the bar the following year and in 1942 joined the law firm of Rein & Cotton. It became Rein, Mound & Cotton and in 1987 became Mound, Cotton & Wollan. The firm now has 55 lawyers with offices in New York City, New Jersey, San Francisco and London.
        Mr. Mound specialized in insurance regulation, commodities law, securities law, trusts and estates and corporate law. He served as outside counsel to the New York Cotton Exchange, the New York Mercantile Exchange and the Commodity Clearing Corporation.
        He retired after a stroke in 1985.
        He is survived by his wife, Hortense, of New York City; a son, Peter, of Santee, Calif., and a granddaughter.

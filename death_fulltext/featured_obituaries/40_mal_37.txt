        George H. Armacost, who was president of the University of Redlands in California from 1945 to 1970, died of a heart attack on Wednesday at Bay Front Medical Center in St. Petersburg, Fla. He was 84 years old and lived in St. Petersburg.
        Before going to Redlands he was a dean at William and Mary College. He was a former president of the Western College Association and the Council of Protestant Colleges and Universities. He had also been president of the Southern California Baptist Convention.
        He is survived by three sons, Michael, the United States Ambassador to Japan; Peter, of St. Petersburg, and Samuel, of Hillsborough, Calif.; a daughter, the Rev. Mary Armacost Hulst, of Denver; 11 grandchildren and 3 great-grandchildren.

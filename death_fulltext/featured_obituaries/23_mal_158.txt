        Tom William Scott, a retired Air Force brigadier general who oversaw long-range bombing of Communist forces in the Korean War, died of natural causes Thursday at his home in La Jolla. He was 86 years old.
        General Scott was a base commander in England in World War II. As deputy commander of the 20th Air Force during the Korean War, he oversaw American long-range bombing. He retired in February 1957.
        He is survived by his wife, Marian, and two sons, David of Manhattan Beach, who was an Apollo and Gemini astronaut, and Tom Jr. of Birmingham, Ala.

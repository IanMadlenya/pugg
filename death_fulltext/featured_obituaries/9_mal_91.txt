        Frank H. Tuohy, a former Controller of Customs for the Port of New York, died of a heart attack Thursday at Riverview Medical Center in Red Bank, N.J. He was 75 years old and lived in Little Silver, N.J.
        Mr. Tuohy, a native of Jersey City, was appointed Controller of Customs by President Kennedy in 1963. His office, at that time, had jurisdiction over 27 New York counties and 8 New Jersey counties.
        Mr. Tuohy is survived by his wife, the former Veronica McBride; a son, Kevin, of Little Silver; two sisters, Catherine Murphy of Jersey City and Josephine Shaughnessy of Avon, N.J., and two grandchildren.

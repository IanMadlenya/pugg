        David Schaps, a retired men's clothing manufacturer, died of lung cancer Wednesday at Lenox Hill Hospital in Manhattan. He was 76 years old and lived in East Rockaway, L.I.
        In the mid-1940's Mr. Schaps founded Brookfield Industries, a manufacturer of men's clothing. He sold the company to the Phillips-Van Heusen Corporation in 1969. He then served as a volunteer with the International Executive Service Corps, an organization of retired executives that helps fledgling companies, in Greece and Turkey. He worked with youth through the Workshop for Business Opportunities and the Open Doors program at the New York City Board of Education.
        He is survived by his wife, Toby; a son, Richard, of Manhattan; a daughter, Jane Rascoff of Beverly Hills, Calif.; four brothers, and four grandchildren.

        Vernon Grant Crudge, an aerospace industry consultant, died of cancer Tuesday at his home in Manhattan. He was 76 years old.
        Born in Shanghai, Mr. Crudge was general manager for North America for the British Overseas Airways Corporation from 1942 to 1950. In 1950, he became executive director of the Munitalp Foundation Inc., which did meteorological research. He also founded his own consulting concern, Vernon Crudge Associates, with clients including the Boeing Company and Rolls-Royce Ltd.
        Mr. Crudge was a member and former president of the Wings Club, a member of the American Institute of Aeronautics and Astronautics and a former member of the board of the Flight Safety Foundation.
        He is survived by his wife, Anne.

        Richard Kendrick Parsell, a retired patent attorney, died Oct. 14 at Church Homes, a retirement community in Hartford. He was 89 years old and until two years ago, lived in Manhasset, L.I., and in Center Harbor, N.H.
        Mr. Parsell was a partner in the law firm of Kenyon & Kenyon in Manhattan. He joined the concern as a patent attorney in the 1920's and spent his entire career there, retiring in the mid-1970's.
        He was born in Ithaca, N.Y., and graduated from Cornell and Cornell University Law School, where he edited the law review.
        A widower, Mr. Parsell is survived by a son, Kendrick, of Chicago; two daughters, Gail Beckett of Annapolis, Md., and Lynn Maley of Burlington, Vt.; six grandchildren, and three great-grandchildren.


        Herbert Beckwith, an architect and longtime professor at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology, died on June 3 at his home in Kingston, Mass. He was 94.
        Mr. Beckwith helped to design 11 buildings at M.I.T., including the Dorrance Food Laboratory and the Van de Graaf Generator building,  which was recently demolished. In 1953-54, he was an associate of Eero Saarinen in the development of the Kresge Auditorium and the M.I.T. Chapel. He also helped design buildings at Rutgers University, Fisk University in Nashville, the University of Rochester and the University of Virginia.
        He designed the United States Embassy in Taiwan and various buildings in Tokyo; Yangon (then Rangoon), Myanmar; Manila, and Bermuda.
        Mr. Beckwith was born in Midland, Mich. He began teaching at M.I.T. in 1926. Except for a brief leave of absence during World War II, he  taught there until his retirement in 1968.
        He is survived by his wife, Elizabeth; a daughter, Suzanne B. Bynum of Cambridge, Mass.; three grandchildren, and three great-grandchildren.

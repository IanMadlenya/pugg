
        Carl W. Stursberg Jr., an executive in the food industry and a specialist in venture-capital financing, died on Monday at Lenox Hill Hospital in Manhattan. He was 71 years old and lived in Roslyn Harbor, L.I.
        He died of a heart attack, his family said.
        Mr. Stursberg held high posts with Warner-Lambert Company and Squibb Beech-Nut in the 1960's. In the 1970's he was president of the STP Corporation and at his death he was chairman and president of the Deer Park Baking Company of Hammonton, N.J.
        He was born in Newark, graduated from New York University and attended the Harvard Business School. After serving in naval intelligence in World War II he became a research analyst with McCann-Erickson and a vice president of Foote, Cone & Belding, both advertising agencies, before joining Colgate-Palmolive Company in 1953 as an assistant advertising manager.
        From 1955 to 1958 he was sales and advertising manager for General Foods in White Plains and from 1958 to 1960 he was assistant to the president of the Street & Finney advertising agency. He was head of the consumer products division of Warner-Lambert from 1960 to 1965, president of the Squibb Products Company from 1965 to 1967 and president of Beech-Nut from 1967 to 1970.
        In 1970 he wrote "A Guide to Venture Capital Financing" and, after four years with STP, he became chairman of the Decision Data Computer Corporation of Horsham, Pa., in 1975.
        His wife, the former Muriel Fine, died in 1989. He is survived by two sons, Walter, of Manhattan and Henry, of Philadelphia; a daughter, Amy, of Manhattan, and four grandchildren.

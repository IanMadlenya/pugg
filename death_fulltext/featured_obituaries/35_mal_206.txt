        Sam Morgenstern, a composer, conductor and musicologist, died on Dec. 22 at Roosevelt Hospital in Manhattan. He was 83 years old.
        Mr. Morgenstern wrote two short operas as well as several songs and piano pieces. His books included ''The Dictionary of Vocal Themes,'' ''The Dictionary of Musical Themes'' and ''Composers on Music.'' He conducted a group in Lower Manhattan called the Lemonade Opera Company, which presented the American premiere of Sergei Prokofiev's ''Duenna'' in 1948, and other ensembles in New York and Philadelphia. Mr. Morgenstern taught at Mannes College of Music, and was a private vocal teacher and coach.
        There are no known survivors.

        William Charles Palmer, an artist and teacher and director emeritus of the Munson-Williams-Proctor Institute School of Art in Utica, N.Y., died of a heart attack Monday at his home in Clinton, N.Y. He was 81 years old.
        Mr. Palmer founded the Utica school in 1941 and served as its director until he retired in 1971. He was also a member of the Hamilton College art faculty and a former vice president of the National Society of Mural Painters. Examples of his work may be seen at the Metropolitan Museum of Art, the Whitney Museum and the National Gallery in Washington.
        He was born in Des Moines, Iowa. From 1924 to 1930 he attended the Art Students League of New York, where he later became an instructor.
        In recent years, Mr. Palmer had worked on displays for the Metropolitan Museum and the Whitney.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Catherine Wechsler.

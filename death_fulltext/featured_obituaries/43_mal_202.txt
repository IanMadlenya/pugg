        John J. O'Hara, a former Internal Revenue Service intelligence agent and a member of the Justice Department's Organized Crime Strike Force, died on Tuesday at the Community Medical Center in Toms River, N.J. He was 71 years old and lived in Berkeley Township, N.J.
        His family said that he died after a short illness and that the cause of death had not yet been determined.
        Mr. O'Hara joined the I.R.S. in 1946, serving in the Newark and New York City districts. In 1962 he was named to the newly organized strike force and in 1965 became chief of the intelligence division of the I.R.S.
        After retiring in 1975, Mr. O'Hara became a supervisor in the New Jersey Division of Criminal Justice, dealing with antitrust matters and Medicaid fraud. He retired again in 1985.
        He was born in Jersey City and held an accounting degree from Fordham University. In World War II he served in the Army, later rising to major in the Army Reserves.
        Mr. O'Hara is survived by his wife, the former Helen Quinn; three sons, the Rev. John J. Jr., of Staten Island, Terrence, of Pine Bush, N.Y., and Michael, of Rockaway, N.J., and four grandchildren.

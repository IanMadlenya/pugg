
        Marian Searchinger, a New York theatrical agent, died on Monday at a convalescent home in Santa Barbara, Calif. She was 81.
        Ms. Searchinger's clients included such actors as Ossie Davis, Ruby Dee and Jane Alexander, the television writer Ernest Kinoy and the directors Franklin Schaffner and Fielder Cook. She also represented the poet Langston Hughes, the lyricist Hal David and the playwright Roger Hirson. She worked as an agent at Creative Management Associates (now International Creative Management) from the 1950's to the early 80's, and then joined NBC, where she worked on literary acquisitions and daytime programming.
        She is survived by her husband, Gene; a daughter, Jill; two sons, Brian and Tim, and a sister, Renee Cohen.


        D. William Hanway, a retired newspaper executive, died on Saturday at his home in Hypoluxo, Fla. He was 67 years old.
        He died of a heart attack, his family said.
        In 1962 he joined the International Edition of The New York Times in Paris as an executive assistant to the general manager and later became assistant general manager. In 1965, he transferred to The Times's executive staff at the home office in Manhattan.
        From 1965 to 1972, he worked at The Daily News as an assistant city editor. For the next several years he was the publisher of the Exchange magazine on Wall Street, and then was an editor at The National Enquirer.
        Surviving are his wife of 37 years, the former Tomoko Tanaka; a son, William S., of Manhattan, and two sisters, Elizabeth Morrill, of Hendersonville, N.C., and Maude Jacopozzi, of Florence, Italy.

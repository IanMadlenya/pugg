        Paul Grotz, an architect and former art director and managing editor of the magazine Architectural Forum, died on Sept. 15 at Cape Cod Hospital in Hyannis, Mass. He was 88 years old and lived in Wellfleet, Mass. He died of a stroke, his son, Stefan, said. Mr. Grotz was educated in Stuttgart, Germany, and received his advanced training at the University of Munich. He immigrated to the United States in 1931, and in 1934 joined the staff of Architectural Forum. He collaborated with Frank Lloyd Wright in designing and publishing issues of the Forum devoted to Mr. Wright's work.
        In 1961, he helped organize an exhibition of Mr. Wright's work in Milan for the United States Government. In 1972, he was a co-founder of the magazine Architecture Plus and was editor at large until his retirement in 1975. He was also an accomplished photographer.
        In addition to his son, who lives in Monterey, Mass., he is survived by his wife, Dorothy, and two granddaughters.

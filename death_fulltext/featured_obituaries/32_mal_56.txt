        Dr. Maurice J. Saklad, a longtime clinical professor at New York University's College of Dentistry and a university trustee for whom a dental center building was named, died of a heart attack Saturday at Desert Hospital in Palm Springs, Calif., where he had lived since he retired five years ago. He was 74 years old.
        Dr. Saklad, a prosthodontist, was a native of Brooklyn and a graduate of N.Y.U. and its dental school. He had also been an overseer at Harvard University, a governor of the University of Southern California and a consultant for the United States Navy.
        Survivors include his wife, Jean, of Manhattan, and two sisters, Sally Tabenken of Fort Lauderdale, Fla., and Rosalind Pap of Atlanta.

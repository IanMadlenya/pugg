        Felix Joachim Freeman Jr., an investment banker in New York, died of brain cancer Saturday at his home in Narragansett, R.I. He was 73 years old.
        Mr. Freeman, who was a graduate of Brown University and Harvard Law School, was a commodities and precious metals trader for more than 40 years. He retired in 1987 as a vice president of Goldman Sachs & Company.
        He is survived by his wife, Phoebe Ann Davis Freeman; two sons, William, of South Kingstown, R.I., and John, of Burlington, Vt.; a daughter, Ellen, of Santa Cruz, Calif., and two grandchildren.

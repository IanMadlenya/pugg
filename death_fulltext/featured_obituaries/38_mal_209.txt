        Theodore S. Cart, founder of two companies in Trenton, died on Sunday at his home in Lake Wales, Fla. He was 91 years old. Mr. Cart founded the Pocono Rubber Cloth Company, manufacturer of material for automobile tops, in 1920. Five years later he organized Atlantic Products, which manufactured luggage and sporting goods, as a division of Pocono. Atlantic Products was sold to Cluett, Peabody & Company Inc. in the early 1970's. Pocono Rubber Cloth went out of business.
        Mr. Cart had been president of the National Luggage and Leather Goods Manufacturers Association and of the National Association of Golf Bag Manufacturers.
        In World War II he was on advisory committees to the War Production Board and the Office of Price Administration in Washington.
        Mr. Cart was born in Charleston, S.C., and attended Dartmouth College before enlisting in the Navy in World War I. He is survived by his wife, the former Dorothy Jones Wilson; a daughter, Barbara Macauley of Chapel Hill, N.C.; a son, Theodore W., of Pamplico, S.C.; three grandchildren and seven great-grandchildren.

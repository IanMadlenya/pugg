
        Edward A. Manfield, a world champion bridge player, died on Monday at his home in Philadelphia. He was 55.
        The cause was a heart attack, said his wife, Melanie.
        In 1986, Mr. Manfield was a member of an American team that won the Rosenblum Cup for the World Team Championship. In 1982, he won a silver medal in the same event. He also won 11 other national victories.
        Mr. Manfield was born in the Bronx and graduated from Harvard University in 1965. He served in the Army and received a master's degree from the University of Virginia in 1972. He worked at the Federal Trade Commission and later became the head of Binary Traders Inc.
        His marriages to Lynn Johannesen of Sunnyvale, Calif., and JoAnn Sprung of Philadelphia ended in divorce. In addition to his wife, he is survived by their two children, Seth and Sabrina Manfield; another daughter, Karen Manfield of Manhattan; his mother, Roslyn Manfield of Fort Lauderdale, Fla., and two brothers, Philip, of Oakland, Calif., and David, of Lake Oswego, Ore.

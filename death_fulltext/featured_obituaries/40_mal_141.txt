        Casper J. Schoener, a reporter for the Associated Press for more than 40 years who was better known by his nickname, Joe Schroeder, when he covered City Hall from 1966 to 1981, died of complications from anemia on Wednesday at the Medical Center of Ocean County in Point Pleasant, N.J. He was 74 years old and lived in Lakewood, N.J.
        Mr. Schoener, who was born in Brooklyn, was with the City News Association when it was absorbed by the Associated Press in 1942. Before his assignment to City Hall he covered crime, labor and politics.
        In World War II he served for three years on anti-submarine patrol in the North Atlantic.
        He is survived by his wife, Peggy; two sons, William, of Maplewood, N.J., and John, of Brooklyn; two daughters, Marie Enny of Brooklyn and Margaret Schoener of Wilton, Conn., and four grandchildren.

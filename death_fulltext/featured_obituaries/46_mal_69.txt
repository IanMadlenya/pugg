
        Joseph Owen Zurhellen Jr., a former Assistant Secretary of State for East Asian Affairs, died on Monday at Montefiore Hospital in the Bronx. He was 70 years old and lived in Putnam Valley, N.Y.
        Mr. Zurhellen died of cancer, a spokesman for the family said.
        A graduate of Columbia University, he entered the Foreign Service in 1946 after service in the Marine Corps. He served in various posts in Japan and was also assigned to Germany and Israel.
        Mr. Zurhellen was the first United States Ambassador to Suriname after it gained independence in 1975. He retired from the Foreign Service in 1978 and later taught political science at Manhattanville College.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Helen Millar; four sons, J. Owen 3d of Yonkers, Willam and David of Putnam Valley, and Robert of Placentia, Calif.; a daughter, Stephanie Hepburn of Urbana, Ill.; and 11 grandchildren. Two brothers, Edward of Norwalk, Conn., and Henry of Memphis, also survive.

        Morton Pepper, a Manhattan lawyer for more than half a century and former president of the Jewish Guild for the Blind, died of a heart attack Saturday at Lenox Hill Hospital. He was 81 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Pepper, a graduate of Harvard College and Harvard Law School, practiced law in Manhattan from 1930 until his death.
        For many years he was the chairman of the Committee on Housing and Urban Development of the City Bar Association and he held similar posts with the Community Service Society. From 1965 to 1967 he was president of the Jewish Guild for the Blind.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Emmy Lou Lilienthal; two daughters, Alice Cooper of Jerusalem and Carol Cooper of Lyonsville, N.Y.; a sister, Eleanor Pepper of Manhattan, and four grandchildren.

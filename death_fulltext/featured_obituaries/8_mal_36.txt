        John Denis Pollis, an authority on art preservation, died of cancer Sunday at Memorial Sloan-Kettering Hospital. He was 65 years old and lived in Manhattan and Cephalonia, Greece.
        Mr. Pollis was a founding trustee of the recently established Friends of the Benaki Museum in America, a group organized to raise funds for the Benaki Museum in Athens and to encourage Greek-American cultural exchanges.
        He was born in New York City and attended Fordham University and the University of Wisconsin.
        At the time of his death, he was co-owner of the art restoration company of Christine Roussel Inc. and president of Museum Marketing Associates, a consulting company.
        He is survived by his wife, Christine Roussel, and three stepchildren, Elizabeth Roussel of Athens and Dianne Roussel and Marc-Christian Roussel of Manhattan.

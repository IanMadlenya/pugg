
        Donald Burros, a business executive who was also a sculptor, died yesterday at his home in Bethesda, Md. He was 64 years old.
        He died of pancreatic cancer, said his wife, Marian.
        Mr. Burros became president of the White Conveyor Corporation of Kenilworth, N.J., in 1985. The company manufactures conveyor systems for dry-cleaning establishments and other industrial uses.
        As an artist, he did abstract sculptures and sculptures of animals. His work was shown in three exhibitions in the last decade in Manhattan.
        Mr. Burros was born in Brooklyn, served as a gunner on Navy warplanes during World War II and earned a bachelor's degree from Alfred University in Alfred, N.Y.
        In addition to his wife, the former Marian Fox, a food columnist and reporter for The New York Times, he is survived by two sisters, Elaine Breitstein of Delray Beach, Fla., and Roberta Selznick of Staten Island; a son, Michael, of Baltimore, and a daughter, Ann, of Katonah, N.Y.

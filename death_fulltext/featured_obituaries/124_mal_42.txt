
        H. Myles Jacob, a former chief executive of the Inspiration Consolidated Copper Company, died yesterday at his home in Pittstown, N.J. He was 84.
        Mr. Jacob joined Inspiration, an Arizona mining concern with headquarters in New York, in 1936. He was named president in 1960 and became chairman and chief executive in 1973.
        He retired as chief executive in 1978 and continued on the board. The company is now part of Cyprus Amax, an international mining and coal producer.
        Mr. Jacob, who received a business degree from New York University in 1935, started his career as a certified public accountant at Pogson, Peloubet & Company in New York, and joined Inspiration a year later.
        He is survived by his wife, Peggie, of Pittstown; his daughter, Jere, of Greenport, N.Y.; his son, Reid, of Fort Collins, Colo.; two grandchildren, and four great-grandchildren.

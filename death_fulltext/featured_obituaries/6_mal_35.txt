        Robert Pilpel, who was active in Jewish and nonsectarian humanitarian organizations for many years, died of arteriosclerosis Tuesday at Mount Sinai Hospital. A resident of Manhattan, he was 82 years old.
        Since the mid-1970's, Mr. Pilpel had been assistant secretary of the board of the National Association on Drug Abuse Problems, an organization that helps former addicts obtain jobs. A volunteer, his tasks included editing the organization's bimonthly newsletter.
        A graduate of Harvard College and New York Law School, he had worked in the past on the staffs of the American Jewish Joint Distribution Committee and The Hudson Guild, the Chelsea district settlement house.
        He is survived by his wife, Harriet, an attorney; a son, Robert H., of Manhattan; a daughter, Judith Appelbaum of Manhattan, and two grandchildren.

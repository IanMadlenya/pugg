        Dr. Theodore Norman, former managing director of the Baron de Hirsch Fund, which assists Jewish farmers, died of cancer Wednesday at his apartment in Manhattan. He was 77 years old.
        Dr. Norman, who had a doctorate in economics from Harvard University, wrote four books, including ''An Outstretched Arm,'' a history of the Jewish Colonialization Association. He retired from the fund in 1983, but continued to write and lecture.
        Surviving are his wife, Jane; two daughters, Lucy Friedman of Manhattan and Alice Mandel of Philadelphia; a sister, Ruth White of Newton, Mass., and six grandchildren.

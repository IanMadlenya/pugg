
        Lester A. Mount, a leading neurosurgeon associated with Columbia University College of Physicians and Surgeons and New York Presbyterian Hospital, died March 28 at Lawrence Hospital in Bronxville, N.Y. He was 89 and lived in Bronxville.
        Dr. Mount was a teacher and practitioner. A native of Lebanon, Ohio, he earned his M.D. from the University of Cincinnati in 1935. In 1941 he was appointed an assistant in neurosurgery at Columbia and rose to become professor of clinical neurosurgery, retiring from the university in 1983. He won the 1989 Harvey Cushing Medal from the American Association of Neurological Surgeons.
        Dr. Mount is survived by two sons, Philip, of Glen Arm, Ohio, and David, of Closter, N.J.; a daughter, Melinda Mount of Alexandria, Va., and two grandchildren.

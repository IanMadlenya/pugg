
        Winston S. Williams, a former reporter for The New York Times, died on Wednesday in Norfolk, Va. He was 51 and lived in Manhattan.
        He died after being hospitalized for pneumonia, said his brother Miles Williams. He was on leave from his job as a senior reporter for Knight-Ridder Financial.
        Mr. Williams was born in Norfolk. He received a bachelor's degree in economics and a master's in journalism, both from Columbia University. In 1977, he joined The Times as a business reporter, and left in 1989.
        Mr. Williams is survived by his mother, Florence Williams of Norfolk; five brothers, Harry and Miles of New York City, Jerome, of Los Angeles, Crosby, of Norfolk, and Stanley, of Washington; and four sisters, Barbara and Constance of  Norfolk; Carolyn, of Los Angeles, and Denise, of Chicago.

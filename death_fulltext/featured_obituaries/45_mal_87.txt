        Donald Royce, a retired rear admiral of the United States Navy who held high posts in aeronautical engineering, died Sunday at the Powhatan Nursing Home in Falls Church, Va. He was 98 years old and lived in McLean, Va.
        Family members said he died of cardiopulmonary arrest.
        Admiral Royce, a native of Marquette, Mich., was a graduate of the United States Naval Academy and held a master's degree in naval architecture from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology.
        In 1942, after various inspection and engineering duties in Navy construction, he became director of production in the Navy's Bureau of Aeronautics. In 1944 he was given command of the Naval Air Material Center in Philadelphia, a post he held until 1948. He retired in 1951.
        A widower. he is survived by three daughters, Priscilla Fothergill of Washington, Phoebe Gilchrist of Rockville, Md., and Margaret Larson of Sandia Park, N.M., and eight grandchildren.


        Harry B. Warner, a retired president of the B. F. Goodrich Company, died Friday in Naples, Fla. He was 74 years old.
        A chemist and a chemical engineeer, Mr. Warner was a graduate of Ohio State University who, at the age of 25, became the youngest plant manager at B. F. Goodrich, based in Akron.
        In 1960 he was named president of B. F. Goodrich Chemical Company. He was president of the parent company from 1967 to 1974, when he retired.
        He is survived by his wife, Vesta; a daughter, Wendy Good of Groton, Mass.; two sons, Tim of Exton, Pa., and Tom of Columbus, Ohio, and two grandchildren.

        Dr. Max J. Routtenberg, former rabbi of Temple B'nai Sholom of Rockville Centre, L.I., died of a heart attack Sunday at his home in Yorktown Heights, N.Y. He was 77 years old.
        Dr. Routtenberg led Temple B'nai Sholom, a Conservative congregation, for 18 years until he retired in 1972. Earlier in his career he had been executive vice president of the Jewish Theological Seminary of America.
        He was born in Montreal, studied at McGill University and graduated with a B.S. from New York University. In addition to a rabbinical degree, he held the degree of Doctor of Hebrew Letters from the seminary.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Lilly Soloway; two children, Ruth Seldin of Pleasantville, N.Y., and Dr. Aryeh Routtenberg of Evanston, Ill.; four grandchildren; a brother, Rabbi Hyman Routtenberg of Jerusalem, and two sisters, Hadassah Levin of Philadelphia, Pa., and Constance Chodos of Montreal.

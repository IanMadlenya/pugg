        Ernest O. Melby, a former dean at New York University and a former president of Montana State University, died Sunday in Boca Raton, Fla., where he had lived for the last six years. He was 95 years old.
        Throughout his 70-year career, Dean Melby was known for his emphasis on teacher training and his work in human relations.
        During his 11 years as dean of the School of Education at N.Y.U. in the 1940's and 50's, he modernized its curriculum and developed programs in community service, research and human relations studies.
        Dean Melby was born in Lake Park, Minn. He earned his bachelor's degree in 1913 from St. Olaf's College in Northfield, Minn., his master's degree in 1926 and his doctorate in 1928, both from the University of Minnesota. Taught at Northwestern
        He began his career as a high school teacher and principal in Minnesota before serving for nine years as the superintendent of schools in Brewster, Black Duck and Long Prairie, Minn.
        In 1926, he joined the faculty of the School of Education at the University of Minnesota as the assistant director of the Bureau of Educational Research.
        He moved to Northwestern University in 1928 as an assistant professor in the School of Education. Six years later, he was named dean, a post he held until 1941.
        He was appointed president of Montana State University in 1941 and served as chancellor of the University of Montana during the 1943-44 academic year. He left Montana for N.Y.U. in 1945.
        After he retired from N.Y.U. in 1956, he taught for 22 years as a distinguished professor at Michigan State University.
        He is survived by his wife, Aurora; a son, Stanley, of Port St. Lucie, Fla., and three grandchildren.

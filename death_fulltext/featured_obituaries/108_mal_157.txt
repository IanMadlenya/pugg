
        Thruston Johnson, a violinist and director of phe International Festival Series, a program of music of many nations, died on Thursday at St. Luke's Hospital. He was 81 and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Johnson, a native of Louisville, Ky., was a familiar soloist in the concert halls of New York beginning in the mid-1950's. He was also a violinist with the Chicago Symphony, the Kansas City Philharmonic, the Pittsburgh Symphony and other orchestras and concertmaster of the Naumberg Symphony.
        He is survived by his wife, Evelyn.

        Harold A. Rousselot, a retired investment banker and commodity trader, died of cancer Saturday at his home in Manhattan. He was 80 years old.
        Mr. Rousselot was a vice president at Drexel Burnham Lambert, where he worked for 14 years before retiring in 1986.
        He was born in Manhattan and graduated from Columbia College. He rowed on the American championship lightweight crew, which won the Henley Regatta in 1929.
        Mr. Rousselot was a member of the Boston Stock Exchange, president of the Commodity Exchange, a vice president of the New York Cotton Exchange, a governor of the American Stock Exchange and chairman of the Association of Stock Exchange Firms.
        He was a life trustee, then trustee emeritus, of Columbia University and president of the Columbia University Alumni Federation.
        He is survived by his wife, Elsie; a son, Anthony, of Santa Fe, N.M., and two grandchildren.

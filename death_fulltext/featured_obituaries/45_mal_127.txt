        Albert J. Harris, an education professor and a leader in the teaching of reading, died on Monday at White Plains Hospital. He was 82 years old and lived in West Palm Beach, Fla., and New Rochelle, N.Y.
        He died of heart failure, said his daughter Rona Conrad of Mount Vernon, N.Y.
        Dr. Harris received Master of Arts and Ph.D. degrees from Harvard University in 1930. He joined Queens College in 1949 as an associate professor after teaching in Indiana, Maryland and New York.
        He was a professor of education and head of the Psycho-Educational Clinic at Queens College from 1951 to 1964. He was then named director of the office of research and evaluation in the division of teacher education at the City University of New York. He retired in 1968.
        He wrote several books, including ''How to Increase Reading Ability'' and ''Effective Teaching of Reading,'' first published in 1940 and 1962, respectively, by McKay Press. He was a founder of the International Reading Association and served on many professional organizations.
        In addition to his daughter Rona, he is survived by his wife, Edith; another daughter, Charlotte Weiner of Manhattan, and six grandchildren.

        Robert S. Ferguson, the former head of marketing for Columbia Pictures, with which he was associated for nearly a half century, died of heart disease last Friday in Westchester Medical Center. He was 73 years old and lived in Atlantic Beach, L.I.
        Mr. Ferguson was responsible for marketing such pictures as ''Lawrence of Arabia,'' ''Guess Who's Coming to Dinner,'' ''Bob and Carol and Ted and Alice,'' ''Easy Rider,'' ''Oliver'' and ''Funny Girl.'' Since 1940 he had held several posts at Columbia in advertising, publicity and marketing. He was a consultant to Columbia's president from 1979 to 1983 and to its chairman of the board until 1987.
        Mr. Ferguson is survived by his wife, Helene; two daughters, Carole Brodsky of Setauket, L.I., and Sandy Doft of Pittsburgh; a brother, Richard; a sister, Vera Mandel, and five grandchildren.

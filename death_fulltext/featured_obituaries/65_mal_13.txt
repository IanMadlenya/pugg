
        Roger Manton Keefe, a retired banker who worked for nearly four decades at the Chase Manhattan Bank in New York and later with the Connecticut Bank and Trust Company, died on Saturday at St. Joseph's Medical Center in Stamford, Conn. He was 73 years old and lived in Norwalk, Conn.
        His family said he died after a long illness.
        Mr. Keefe, a native of New London, Conn., graduated from Yale University in 1941 and then served with the Army in Europe. He won a Silver Star and a Bronze Star with cluster before being demobilized with the rank of major.
        He joined the Chase in 1945 and was a senior vice president 26 years later when he left to join Connecticut Bank and Trust as executive vice president in charge of its operations in Fairfield County. He retired in 1983 as chairman of the bank's executive committee.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Ann Hunter; three sons, Christopher and Matthew, both of Fairfield; three daughters, Susan Keefe of Durham, N.C., Robin Lynch of Riverdale, the Bronx, and Victoria Morrill of Manhattan; a brother, Arthur, of Chestertown, Md., and six grandchildren.

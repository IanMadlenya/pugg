        Zeva E. Spiegler, director of college guidance and head of the science program at the Lenox School, a private school on East 70th Street, died of cancer Thursday at her home in Manhattan. She was 54 years old.
        Mrs. Spiegler introduced programs that brought third and fourth graders together with high school students in the science laboratory. She had also taught science in the gifted children program at C. W. Post College, now the C. W. Post campus of Long Island University in Brookville, L.I.
        She is survived by her husband, William; a son, Louis, of Manhattan, and two sisters, Dr. Gily Bard Starobin of Stamford, Conn., and Nuri Relis of Manhattan.


        Charles M. Powell, an executive of the Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences and a longtime marketing executive in the film industry, died Friday at his office in Los Angeles. He was 57 years old.
        Mr. Powell died of a heart attack, said a business partner, Buddy Young.
        Mr. Powell was born in Brooklyn and retained a home in Manahattan, but since 1969 he had lived and worked primarily in Los Angeles.
        At the time of his death, he was first vice president of the academy, which presents the annual Oscar awards. He was also director of the Peter Stark Motion Picture Producing Program at the University of Southern California.
        In his career, Mr. Powell served as senior vice president of marketing at Columbia, Metro-Goldwyn-Mayer and MCA/Universal, and held a top marketing post at Paramount as well. He got his start in the film business by joining an executive training program at Columbia Pictures after his graduation from New York University's School of Journalism.
        Among the films he helped market were "Bob and Carol and Ted and Alice," "Butterflies Are Free," "Cactus Flower," "That's Entertainment," "Jaws 2," "Animal House," "The Deer Hunter," "Rocky III" and "Annie." He also supervised publicity for the opening of the MGM Grand Hotel in Las Vegas and formed two consulting firms, Pegasus Entertainment and Powell & Young.
        Mr. Powell was a founder and president of both the Shofar Temple and the Synagogue for the Performing Arts in Los Angeles.
        Survivors include his wife, Jane Burman Powell; a son, Mel, and his mother, Sue Lehrer, all of Los Angeles. Memorial services are sheduled for 10 A.M. today at the Hillside Memorial Park Chapel in Los Angeles, and at 11 A.M. Tuesday at the Riverside Memorial Chapel at 180 W. 76th St., Manhattan.

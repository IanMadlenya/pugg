        Nicholas M. Pette, a former justice of the Appellate Division of the State Supreme Court, Second Department, died of a bleeding ulcer Monday in St. Vincent's Hospital in Manhattan. He was 97 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Justice Pette served in Municipal Court in Queens for 20 years until 1950, when he was appointed to the State Supreme Court in Queens. In 1959, he was appointed to the Appellate Division. He retired in 1967. Since then, he was of counsel to Lipsig, Liapakis & Sullivan, a Manhattan law firm, until last year.
        Surviving are his wife, Ruth; a daughter, Janet Kujan of Forest Hills, Queens; a stepdaughter, Nancy Tormey of Lloyd Harbor, L.I.; a stepson, Dr. Stephen Carney of Albany; a sister, Amelia Dempsey of Short Hills, N.J.; nine grandchildren, and two great-grandchildren.

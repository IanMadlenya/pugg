        John Denton Brundage, a retired insurance executive and former chairman of the American Heart Association, died of liver failure yesterday at his home in Winnetka, Ill. He was 70 years old.
        A 1941 graduate of Princeton University, Mr. Brundage was president of Bankers National Life Insurance Company of Montclair, N.J. and later served as president of Georgia International Life in Atlanta and Globe Life Insurance Company in Chicago, now part of Aon Corporation. He was chairman of the American Heart Association in the 1960's and was later affiliated with heart associations in Georgia and Chicago. He retired in 1985.
        He is survived by his wife, Ann; three daughters, Elizabeth Bush of Montclair, Patricia Copley and Susan Thorpe, both of Manhattan; a son, John, of Winnetka; a brother, Howard, of Old Lyme, Conn., and five grandchildren.

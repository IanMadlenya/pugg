        Carl Frederick Farbach, a retired lawyer and former Federal counsel, died of cardiopulmonary arrest Sept. 15 at Sibley Memorial Hospital in Washington. He was 87 years old and lived in Washington.
        A graduate of Havard Law School, Mr. Farbach held a variety of posts in the New Deal Administration of Franklin D. Roosevelt. He was counsel to the Reconstruction Finance Corporation and chief counsel to the Public works Administration and, later, of the United States Maritime Commission.
        From 1943 to 1964 he was in private practice in Manhattan. He returned to Washington as counsel to the International Finance Corporation of the World Bank. From 1967 until his retirement in 1976, he was in private practice in Washington.
        He is survived by his wife, Florence Whitehead Farbach of Washington; a daughter, Karen McCombs, of Bethesda, Md,, a son, Peter, of Mijas, Spain, and two grandsons.

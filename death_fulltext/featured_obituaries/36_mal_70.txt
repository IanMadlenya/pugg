        C. Robert Devine, a retired executive of the Readers Digest Association Inc., died of prostate cancer on Saturday at Calvary Hospital in the Bronx. He was 72 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Devine became vice president and director of corporate and public affairs of the company in 1970 and retired in 1983. He joined the magazine in 1955 and became advertising director of Readers Digest International Editions in 1958. He had been promotion and research director of the U.S. News Publishing Company.
        Mr. Devine was born in Clarksburg, W. Va., and graduated from Princeton in 1938. He became a major in the Army in World War II and received the Bronze Star.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Gisele Edenbourgh Lichine; three children from an earlier marriage, Rodney and Ian, both of Manhattan, and Mallory Norris of Manchester, Conn.; two brothers, Joseph, of Wilton, Conn., and Richard, of Annapolis, Md.; two sisters, Catherine Hayden, widow of the actor Sterling Hayden, of Wilton, and Elizabeth Smith of Portland, Me., and two grandchildren.

        Dr. Milton Gabel, a dentist who practiced in Manhattan for more than 50 years, died on Wednesday at his home in Manhattan at the age of 83. His daughter, Sukhreet, said he died of complications from prostate cancer.
        Dr. Gabel was the husband of Hortense Gabel, a former State Supreme Court justice who was acquitted in 1988 of Federal charges that she had secured a job for their daughter in 1983 with Bess Myerson, then New York City's Cultural Affairs Commissioner, by making rulings favorable to Carl A. Capasso, Ms. Myerson's companion, in a divorce case.
        Dr. Gabel was born in Manhattan. A graduate of City College, he received his dental degree from Tufts College. From 1932 until he retired in 1977 he was on the attending staff at New York Hospital. He was an Army captain in World War II.
        He is survived by his wife and daughter.

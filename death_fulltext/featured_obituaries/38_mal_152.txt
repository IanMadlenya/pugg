        Norman L. Spelke, a retired Connecticut banker, died of Alzheimer's disease on Thursday at the John Douglas French Center in Laguna Hills, Calif. He was 79 years old and lived in Laguna Hills.
        Mr. Spelke had been president of the Security Bank and Trust Company in Stamford, Conn., for 14 years when, in a 1959 merger, the bank became part of the Connecticut Bank and Trust Company. He later worked as an executive vice president and a director of Universal American Corporation, and as a vice president and director of the Lee National Corporation. He retired in 1979.
        Mr. Spelke was born in Stamford, and graduated from the University of Michigan and the University of Virginia Law School. He was a Navy lieutenant commander in World War II.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Miriam Lerner; a son, Richard, of Pasadena, Calif.; a daughter, Karen Sorensen of Berkeley, Calif.; a brother, Harold, of Stamford; a sister, Mildred Weil of Washington, and a grandaughter.

        Dr. Gordon Kenneth Moe, retired director of the Masonic Medical Research Library in Utica, N.Y., died Oct. 27 at St. Luke's Hospital in Utica after a long illness. He was 74 years old and lived in Barneveld, N.Y.
        Dr. Moe joined the laboratory in 1959 as director of research and was also professor of physiology at the State University of New York College of Medicine. He retired in 1984.
        Dr. Moe and his colleagues studied cardiac arrhythmias - abnormal heart rhythms associated with heart attacks. These studies helped lead to the development of the pacemaker.
        Dr. Moe, a native of Fairchild, Wis., received bachelor's, master's and doctoral degrees at the University of Minnesota and a medical degree from Harvard University. He taught at the Universities of Minnesota and Michigan and the Harvard Medical School.
        From 1950 to 1960, he was professor and chairman of the department of physiology at what was then the Upstate Medical Center's College of Medicine.
        Dr. Moe is survived by his wife, the former Janet Foster; two daughters, Melanie Rich of Syracuse, and Sally Glasgow of Tallahassee, Fla.; four sons, Eric, of Holland Patent, N.Y., Chris and Bruce, of Santa Barbara, Calif., and Jonathan, of Scranton, Pa., and eight grandchildren.

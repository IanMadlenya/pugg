        Ira J. Hertan, a lawyer who was active in the Long Island Jewish community, died of lung cancer Wednesday at Memorial Sloan-Kettering Medical Center. He was 58 years old and lived in Dix Hills, L.I.
        Mr. Hertan, a senior partner in the firm of Fishbach Hertan & Reis in Manhattan, was a member of the executive committee and the board of trustees of the United Jewish Appeal-Federation of Jewish Philanthropies and was past president of the Commack Jewish Center.
        He was a trustee of the United Jewish Y's of Long Island, a founder and past president of the Suffolk County Y.M.-Y.W.H.A., and was instrumental in the acquisition and development of its 47-acre campus.
        He was born in Brooklyn and graduated from Indiana University and Yale Law School.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Muriel Tucker; three daughters, Karen Goldstein of Melville, L.I., Amy, of Morristown, N.J., and Roni, of Falls Church, Va.; a son, David, of London; his mother, Eva P., of Manhattan; two brothers, Peter, of Los Gatos, Calif., and Victor, of Palm Beach, Fla., and a granddaughter.

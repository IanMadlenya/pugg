
        Dr. Raymond Smith Willis, emeritus professor of Spanish at Princeton University, died on Sunday at his home in Jamesburg, N.J. He was 84 years old.
        Dr. Willis died of heart failure, the university said.
        Dr. Willis, born an American citizen in Mexico, retired in 1973 after 43 years on the faculty. He was a Phi Beta Kappa graduate of Princeton and earned his Ph.D. there in 1933.
        An expert in medieval and modern Spanish literature, Dr. Willis was the author or co-author of nine books, including three Spanish and Portuguese grammars. He also was known for his scholarly articles on Spanish and Portuguese literature.
        He is survived by his wife, Margaret; a son, John, of Peterborough, N.H., and two step-grandchildren.

        Paterson Bond, former managing partner of Orvis Brothers & Company, a former Wall Street brokerage house, died of cancer on Sunday at Sibley Memorial Hospital in Bethesda, Md. He was 80 years old and lived in Montclair, N.J.
        Mr. Bond, the son of the late Representative Charles G. Bond of Brooklyn, joined Orvis in 1930 after he graduated from Hamilton College. He worked for many years as a branch manager in Plainfield, N.J., before becoming a managing partner in 1967.
        When he retired in 1989, he was a vice president of Prudential-Bache Securities.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Gwen Stenehjem; three daughters, Dr. Elinor Martin of Chevy Chase, Md.; Geraldine Laybourne of Montclair and Deborah Bond-Upson of Kentfield, Calif.; a son, Charles G. 3d, of Madera, Calif., and 12 grandchildren.

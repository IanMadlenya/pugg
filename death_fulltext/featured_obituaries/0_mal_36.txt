        Phil Seghi, who hired Frank Robinson as baseball's first black manager with Cleveland in 1975 and signed Pete Rose for the Cincinnati Reds in 1960, died today in Thousand Oaks, Calif., after a long illness with cancer. He was 68 years old.
        Mr. Seghi, who was the senior player-personnel adviser with the Cleveland Indians, had served as vice president and general manager with the club after joining the organization in 1971. He also spent 12 years in the Cincinnati organization, and worked for the Pittsburgh Pirates and the Oakland A's.
        He is survived by his wife, Ella; a daughter, Sandra, and a son, Michael, traveling secretary for the Indians.

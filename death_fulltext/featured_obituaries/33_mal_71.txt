        J. Rupert Picott, an educator and early civil rights leader, died of prostate cancer Wednesday at Georgetown University Hospital in Washington. He was 77 years old and lived in Washington.
        Mr. Picott, a graduate of Virginia Union University, was for 22 years executive secretary of the Virginia Teachers Association and fought for the equalization of pay for black and white teachers. He was also a former vice president of the National Education Association and former executive director of the Association for the Study of Afro-American Life and History.
        In the 1970's he lobbied for the designation of February as Black History Month.
        He is survived by his wife, Lorraine; two sons, J. Rupert Jr. of Richmond, Va., and Hodges, of Pittsburg, Calif.; a brother, Randolph, of Newport News, Va.; a sister, Marian Tyler, of Columbus, Ohio; and two grandchildren.

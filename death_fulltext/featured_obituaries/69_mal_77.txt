
        John T. McNutt, who retired in 1979 as manager and associate editor of The New York Times News Service, died at his home in Monroe, Wash., on Oct. 2. He was 78 years old.
        The cause of death was pulmonary fibrosis, his family said.
        He worked for The Stars and Stripes in Paris before coming to United Press International in New York in 1947 to work as a foreign news editor and United Nations correspondent. He joined The Times News Service in 1963.
        Mr. McNutt is survived by his wife, the former June Statler; a daughter, Sherry Lyn McNutt of Montreal; a son, Mark S. McNutt of Deadwood, Ore.; a daughter by a previous marriage, the Rev. Elizabeth Moulton of Stroudsburg, Pa.; a sister, Katherine Floyd of Montgomery, Ala., seven grandchildren and two great-grandchildren.

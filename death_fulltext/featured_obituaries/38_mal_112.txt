        John Root, a Broadway set designer, died of heart failure on Tuesday at Doylestown (Pa.) Hospital. He was 85 years old.
        Mr. Root designed sets for 53 Broadway shows from the 1930's to the 1950's, frequently working with the producer George Abbott. His credits included ''Harvey,'' ''Kiss the Boys Goodbye,'' ''Red Harvest'' and ''George Washington Slept Here.'' He also designed sets for television shows like ''The Perry Como Show'' and ''Armstrong Circle Theater,'' as well as for commercials.
        In the 1960's, Mr. Root opened a real estate business in Bucks County, Pa.
        Mr. Root is survived by his wife, Margaret, of Solebury, Pa.; a daughter, Linda Kenyon of Washington; a son, Evan Root of Brookline, Mass.; two sisters, Betty Root and Marian Olson, both of Downers Grove, Ill., and eight grandchildren.

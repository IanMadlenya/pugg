
        Sheldon Gitelman, a banker in New York and Washington for 20 years, died on Tuesday at his home in Washington. He was 64.
        The cause was heart failure, said his wife, Natalie.
        Mr. Gitelman was an executive vice president of Marine Midland Bank in New York for 12 years before his retirement in 1986. Previously he served in Washington as an official of the Agency for International Development and the Overseas Private Investment Corporation.
        Mr. Gitelman received a law degree from the University of Minnesota.
        He is survived by his wife; two daughters, Heidi of Boston and Joan of Port Townsend, Wash.; a son, Steven of Alexandria, Va., and a sister, Dolores Basuk of Charles City, Iowa.

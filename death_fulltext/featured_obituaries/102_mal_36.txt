
        Grant S. Lewis, a partner in the law firm of LeBoeuf, Lamb, Greene & MacRae, died on Wednesday at his home in Manhattan. He was 53.
        He died after a prolonged illness, said Samuel M. Sugden, the firm's chairman.
        Mr. Lewis was born in Manhattan. He graduated Phi Beta Kappa from Bates College, and received his law degree from Harvard Law School in 1965, the year he started his legal career at LeBeouf.
        He became a partner in 1973 and thereafter served as chairman of the litigation department and a member of the administrative committee. Mr. Lewis is survived by his wife, Shari Gruhn Lewis; a daughter, Carrie Ann, of Manhattan; his mother, Gladys, and stepfather, Samuel Colin, of Livingston Manor, N.Y.,; a brother, Alan, of Easton, Conn., and two stepsisters, Sandra Dubowsky of Verona, N.J., and Gail Liebovich, of Ithaca, N.Y.

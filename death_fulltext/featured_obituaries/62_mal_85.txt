
        Paul Gavert, a singer and voice teacher, died on March 11 at his home in Manhattan. He was 76 years old.
        He died of cardiac arrest, said his daughter, Gail, of Manhattan.
        Mr. Gavert, a baritone, began his career in 1945, appearing in the London production of the musical "Song of Norway." He had a successful career as a lieder singer, touring Europe and the United States and making recordings.
        He was born in Sioux City, Iowa, and studied at Northwestern University and the Manhattan School of Music. He was a well-known vocal teacher and coach to dozens of performers, including Betty Buckley, Len Cariou, Jane Seymour, Ron Perlman and Treat Williams. He also headed the music department at the University of Colorado in Boulder.
        In addition to his daughter, he is survived by two sisters, Carma A. Gruman of Scotch Plains, N.J., and Dagmar Olsson of Winter Park, Fla.

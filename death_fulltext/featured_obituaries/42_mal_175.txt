        Edgard Feder, a music critic, cellist and conductor, died at his home in Manhattan on Sunday. He was 81 years old.
        He Feder died of complications from a stroke, Riverside Memorial Chapel said.
        Mr. Feder was born in Bucharest, Romania, in 1909. At the age of 21 he earned degrees in law, economics and philosophy in Paris while also studying music. In 1939 he immigrated to the United States, where he founded Feder Industries, a cosmetics packaging concern.
        In 1941 Mr. Feder began writing for the French-language publication France-Amerique, which published his weekly music reviews and features until his death. He was a director of the Violoncello Society of New York, an active coach for chamber music and conductor of the Gotham Chamber Players.
        He is survived by his wife, Geraldine; a son, Yves Albert Feder of Killingworth, Conn., and a granddaughter, Sarah Feder of Centerbrook, Conn.

        Alan Colby Green, the executive vice president of the Cooper Union for the Advancement of Science and Art, died of a heart ailment on Friday at the Maho Bay campground in St. John, V.I., which he was visiting with his family. He was 54 years old and lived in Pelham, N.Y.
        Mr. Green was previously a teacher of architecture at the Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute and an administrator at the Academy for Educational Development and its Educational Facilities Laboratory.
        He was a native of Walkerville, Ont., and a graduate of the University of Florida and Rensselaer.
        He was a trustee of the New York School for the Deaf, an editorial board member of the Society for College and University Planning, a founder and former chairman of Partners for Livable Places, a neighborhood renewal group, and a former board member of many professional organizations.
        Surviving are his wife, Sherry; two daughters, Jennifer, of Manhattan, and Sarah, of Port-au-Prince, Haiti; two sons, Mark, of Stony Brook, L.I., and Joshua, of Williamsburg, Va.; a sister, Esther Bierbaum of St. Petersburg, Fla., and two stepbrothers, John Dugan of Louisville, Ky., and Caldwell Dugan of Alexandria, Va.

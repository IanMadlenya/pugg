        Dr. Fellowes Morgan Pruyn, a physician and founder of one of the nation's first group medical practices, died Monday at his home in Chappaqua, N.Y., after a long illness. He was 79 years old.
        He was the co-founder in the mid-1940's of the Mount Kisco (N.Y.) Medical Group, which today consists of 27 doctors and a support staff of more than 130.
        Dr. Pruyn attended Harvard College before receiving his medical degree from the Columbia University College of Physicians and Surgeons in 1935.
        He is survived by a sister, Beatrice Thibault of Clayton, N.Y.

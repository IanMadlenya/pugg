        Dr. Dong Sun Chu, a specialist in rehabilitation medicine for 23 years at the New York University Medical Center, died Tuesday at White Plains Hospital after suffering a cerebral hemmorhage on Nov. 9 at his home in Scarsdale, N.Y. He was 63 years old.
        Dr. Chu was born in South Korea and received two medical degrees from Seoul National University. After serving as chief resident in neurosurgery at University Hospital in Seoul, he came to the United States in 1965 and joined the staff at the Howard A. Rusk Institute of Rehabilitation Medicine at N.Y.U.
        For the last 17 years Dr. Chu was also on the faculty at the N.Y.U. Medical School, holding the rank of associate professor since 1976. He was also an attending physician at Bellevue Hospital Center.
        He is survived by his wife, Dr. Ke Won Park Chu; two daughters, Margaret and Julie; a son, David, all of Scarsdale; three brothers, Dong Yung Chu of Scarsdale, and Dong Ju Chu and Dong Myung Chu, both of Seoul, and a sister, Dong Il Choi of Scarsdale.

        Jerome H. Teitelbaum, a New York real-estate executive, died Monday at Lenox Hill Hospital. He was 73 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Teitelbaum, who headed three corporations that owned real estate on the Upper East Side, was the son of Joseph Teitelbaum, a Democratic National Committee member and business executive. In 1933, with his father and several local businessmen, Jerome Teitelbaum founded the Yorkville chapter of B'nai B'rith.
        He was a graduate of New York University in 1933 and of Duke University in 1944. He was a former member of the board of directors of the Riverdale Temple.
        Mr. Teitelbaum is survived by his wife, the former Marian Berlin; two sons, Andrew, of Manhattan, and Steven, of Albany, and a granddaughter.

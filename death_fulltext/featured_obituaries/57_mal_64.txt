
        John Lewis Cobbs, a former editor of Business Week magazine, died on Sunday at Memorial Hospital in Easton, Md. He was 74 years old.
        He died of complications from cancer and congestive heart failure, his family said.
        Mr. Cobbs joined Business Week in 1942 as finance editor. In 1943 he became a Washington correspondent and in 1945 he was named business editor. He later became managing editor and was named editor in 1966, a post second only to editor in chief. He retired in 1982.
        Mr. Cobbs won several awards for the magazine, including one from the Overseas Press Club in 1972 and, in 1959, one from Sigma Delta Chi, an organization of journalists, which named a special report on education by Mr. Cobbs as the best magazine reporting in the United States in 1958.
        Mr. Cobbs was born in Washington and lived for 33 years in Chappaqua, N.Y., where he was also president of the local school board. He moved to St. Michaels, Md., in 1982.
        He is survived by his wife, Phyllis; two sons, John Lewis of Collegeville, Pa., and Nicholas Hammer of Washington; a brother, James W., of Greenwich, Conn., a sister, Betty Cobbs Brown, also of Greenwich, and three grandchildren.

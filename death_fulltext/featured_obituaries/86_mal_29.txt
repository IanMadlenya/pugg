
        John E. Corette, an official with the Montana Power Company for more than 40 years, died on Saturday at a retirement home in Rancho Palos Verdes, Calif. He was 85 and had spent most his life in Butte, Mont.
        He died of natural causes, said a spokesman for Montana Power.
        Mr. Corette joined the company in 1934 as legal counsel. He was chief executive from 1952 to 1973 and board chairman from 1967 to 1977. He retired in 1980.
        For more than 20 years, he also served in various capacities with the Edison Electric Institute, a trade group of private utilities.
        Mr. Corette's first wife, the former Elsie Paul, died in 1971.
        He is survived by his second wife, the former Sallie Murphy Sheridan, and two sons and two daughters from his first marriage, John E., of Chevy Chase, Md., William P., of Rolling Hills, Calif., Joan Hanley of Rancho Palos Verdes and Diane Simperman of Mercer Island, Wash. He is also survived by three stepsons, James Sheridan of Winnetka, Ill., William Sheridan of Los Angeles and Brian Sheridan of Missoula; 13 grandchildren and 11 great grandchildren.

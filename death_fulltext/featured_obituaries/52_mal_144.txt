
        Robert S. Heaney, a former British diplomat in China and a retired businessman in Manhattan, died on Monday at Greenwich Hospital in Connecticut. He was 88 years old and lived in Greenwich.
        He died of cancer, a family spokesman said.
        Mr. Heaney was born in Wimbledon, England, and joined the British China Service at the legation in Beijing in 1926. He was consul at the Consulate General office in Shanghai from 1931 to 1941. During the Japanese invasion in August 1937, he supervised the evacuation of British subjects. For that work, King George VI conferred on the Order of the British Empire. In 1950, he became managing director of the New York office of Scottish Woolen Mills Ltd., a consortium of Scottish textile companies.
        He is suvived by his wife, Margaret; a daughter, Margaret Greene of Bethesda, Md.; a sister, Gladys Callow of Crowhurst, England, and three grandchildren.

        Leonard H. Steibel, a founding partner and senior member of the New York City law firm Smith, Steibel, Alexander & Saskor, died of cordoma, a rare form of cancer, Saturday at his home in Sands Point, L.I.. He was 72 years old.
        Mr. Steibel, who was born in Manhattan, was a graduate of City College and St. John's University Law School, where he received his law degree in 1937. For many years he specialized in international corporate law, representing English and Irish companies and agencies of their Governments. He was also a member of the board of directors of many corporations.
        In 1978 the British Government honored him with the title Commander of the Order of the British Empire for services in facilitating international trade.
        He is survived by his wife, Virginia; a son, Bruce, of Manhattan and Wassaic, N.Y.; three daughters, Pamela, of Philadelphia, Karen Doner of Sands Point and Linda Kennedy of Manhattan; two brothers, Victor, of Manhasset and Dr. William, of Dix Hills, L.I., and two grandchildren.

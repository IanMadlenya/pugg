        Roland F. Simons, a retired health-care industry executive, died on Saturday at the Naples Community Hospital in Florida. He was 85 years old and lived in Naples.
        He died of heart failure resulting from complications of a perforated ulcer, his wife said.
        At his retirement in 1968, Mr. Simons was president and chief executive of C. R. Bard Inc., a manufacturer of cardiovascular and urological supplies in Murray Hill, N.J. He joined Bard in 1962 and was named president in 1966.
        Before that he was with Johnson & Johnson for 27 years and served as executive vice president of its Ethicon Sutures Inc. division. He had also been president of the Hospital Industries Association, a national trade group.
        Mr. Simon was a graduate of the Wharton School of Finance and Commerce at the University of Pennsylvania.
        In addition to his wife, the former Majorie Merwin, he is survived by a daughter, Majorie Harvey of Baltimore and Naples; a son, Bruce, of Devon, Pa., and six grandchildren and six great-grandchildren.

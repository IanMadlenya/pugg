
        Frederic C. Decker, a publisher of business magazines, died on Sunday at his home in Elizabethtown, Pa. He was 74 years old.
        He died in his sleep, his family said.
        Mr. Decker, a native of South Bend, Ind., was managing director of Guideposts and associate publisher of Christian Herald before becoming publisher of Printers' Ink, which was founded in 1888 to report on advertising.
        In 1965 he led a group of employees who bought Printers' Ink, which became Marketing/Communications. His firm, Decker Communications Inc., also published Business Advertising and Consumer Advertising. He retired in 1982 and worked as a consultant.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Millicent Neill; four sons, William, of Washington, Craig, of Cincinnati, Keith, of Bowie, Md., and Michael, of Danbury, Conn., and six granddaughters.

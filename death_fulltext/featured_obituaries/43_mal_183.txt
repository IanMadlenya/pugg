        Dr. R. Caird Watson, a radiologist and chairman of the medical-imaging department at Memorial Sloan-Kettering Cancer Center, died there on Thursday. He was 61 years old.
        The hospital said he died of colon cancer.
        Dr. Watson pioneered the development of angiography of kidney tumors. He also developed methods for using X-rays to assist in biopsies and to bypass blockages in the urinary tract.
        Dr. Watson received his medical degree from St. Thomas Hospital School of Medicine in London in 1955. After completing his residency in diagnostic radiology at the New York Hospital-Cornell Medical Center, he joined the faculty of Cornell University Medical College.
        He is survived by a sister, Dr. Elizabeth Whalen, of Shrewsbury, N.J.

        Donald C. Blaisdell, a former professor of political science at City College of the City University of New York, died of pneumonia Saturday at the Lorien Nursing Home in Columbia, Md. He was 88 years old and lived in Skaggsville, Md.
        Professor Blaisdell, who was the political science department chairman for part of his tenure, taught at City College from 1955 to 1967, when he retired. Earlier, he worked for the State Department as a delegate to the United Nations and, from 1952 to 1954, was the United States representative to United Nations agencies headquartered in Geneva.
        Professor Blaisdell graduated from Pennsylvania State College in 1920 and received a doctorate from Columbia University.
        A widower, he is survived by a son, Nesbitt, of Manhattan; a daughter, Ann Irvine of Annapolis, Md.; two brothers, Thomas, of Berkeley, Calif., and William, of Houston, and seven grandchildren.

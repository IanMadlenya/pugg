
        Russell O'Neil, a novelist and playwright, died on Wednesday at the Veteran's Hospital in Manhattan. He was 64 years old.
        He died of respiratory failure, said his brother, John O'Neil, of Norristown, Pa.
        Among Mr. O'Neil's novels were "A Climate of Violence" (1961), "The Devil's Profession" (1964), "The Most Beautiful Girls in the World" (1970) and "The Alcatraz Incident" (1971). A play by him, "Don't Call Back," was produced on Broadway in 1975 and starred Arlene Francis.
        Mr. O'Neil was born in Conshohocken, Pa., on March 6, 1927, and grew up in New Jersey. He was a paratrooper late in World War II.
        He is survived by his brother.

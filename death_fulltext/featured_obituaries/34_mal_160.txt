        Louis Achitoff, a leading expert in aeronautical planning and flight operations, died yesterday at Columbia-Presbyterian Medical Center after a long illness. He was 73 years old.
        Mr. Achitoff, a United States Navy pilot in World War II, spent 20 years with the Port Authority of New York and New Jersey, the last 10 as head of the aviation technical services division. In 1979, he moved to the Federal Aviation Administration, where he ultimately became head of the planning staff of its eastern region.
        A graduate of City College of New York, Mr. Achitoff received a master's degree in aeronautical engineering from New York University.
        He is survived by his wife, Sandy; a son, Paul; a daughter, Lesley Achitoff-Gray; a sister, Anne Brown, and two grandchildren.

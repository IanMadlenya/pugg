        Joseph Sittler, a Lutheran theologian known for his work on religion and ecology, died of cancer Dec. 28 at his home in Chicago. He was 83 years old.
        Mr. Sittler, who was born in Ohio, was a professor of theology at the University of Chicago Divinity School from 1957 to 1973, when he became Distinguished Professor in Residence at the Lutheran School of Theology in Chicago. He continued teaching until a year ago.
        Mr. Sittler wrote two books about the church's responsibility for the earth's ecology: ''The Ecology of Faith,'' published in 1961, and ''The Care of the Earth,'' in 1964.
        He was also active in the worldwide ecumenical movement and worked for the World Council of Churches and the National Council of Churches.
        Mr. Sittler is survived by his wife, Jeanne; six children, Stephen, Joseph Jr., Barbara, Philip, Bay, all of Chicago, and Hans, of Brooklyn; and five grandchildren.

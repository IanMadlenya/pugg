
        William Myer, a harness horse trainer and driver for almost 50 years. died of congestive heart failure at Milford Memorial Hospital on Tuesday. He was 74 years old.
        Mr. Myer, who retired in 1980, compiled 1,840 victories and had $6.8 million in earnings, the United States Trotting Association said.
        Mr. Myer began his career at age 16. He raced on the Delmarva Peninsula circuit before moving to New York in 1940 to work at Roosevelt Raceway on Long Island.

        Leo Jiranek, a furniture and interior designer, died of cancer Saturday at Greenwich (Conn.) Hospital. He was 89 years old and lived in Old Greenwich.
        Mr. Jiranek was a consultant to Ethan Allen Inc. in Danbury, Conn., at his death. He had been in the furniture business for 62 years and had contributed designs to many furniture companies.
        He was born in Grand Rapids, Mich., and graduated from Princeton University. He founded and served as first president of the American Designers Institute, now the Industrial Designers Society of America. In the 1960's he founded and was president of the Jiranek School of Furniture Design and Technology in Manhattan. It closed in the early 1980's.
        Mr. Jiranek is survived by his wife, the former Elaine Jennings Hoffman; four sons, Robert, of Danville, Va.; James, of Wellsville, Pa.; David, of Old Greenwich, and Henry Heald of Coral Gables, Fla.; two stepsons, Theodore Hoffman of Middletown, Md., and R. Todd Hoffman of East Lebanon, Me.; 10 grandchildren and one great-grandchild.

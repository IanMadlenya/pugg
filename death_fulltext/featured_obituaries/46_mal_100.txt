
        Frank D. Rich Sr., a retired construction company official who helped redevelop the city of Stamford, Conn., died on Saturday at his home in Stamford. He was 96 years old.
        He died of respiratory failure, his son, Frank Jr., said.
        Mr. Rich was chairman emeritus of F. D. Rich Company, which he founded in 1920. The construction company was a major builder of military airplane hangars and barracks.
        He was born in Italy and came to the United States in 1903. He had been involvd in redevelopment planning for Stamford since the late 1920's. He later served on the planning board there and and on many organizations, including the State National Bank and the Kiwanis Club.
        In the 1960's the Stamford Redevelopment Commission selected the F. D. Rich Company as the sole developer of the city's downtown area and helped change the face of the city by constructing dozens of office towers, department stores and apartment buildings.
        In addition to his son, Frank, of Darien, he is survived by his wife, Mary; another son, Robert, of Stamford; 10 grandchildren and 18 great-grandchildren.

        Harrison L. Winter, former chief judge of the United States Court of Appeals for the Fourth Circuit, died of cancer yesterday at Johns Hopkins Hospital in Baltimore. He was 69 years old and lived in Baltimore.
        Judge Winter joined the Fourth Circuit in 1966. He had been a judge on the Federal District Court for Maryland, appointed in 1961 by President John F. Kennedy. He became chief judge of the Fourth Circuit in 1981, but stepped down from that post last year because of illness. He continued to sit with the court until several weeks ago.
        Judge Winter was born in Baltimore, graduated from Johns Hopkins University in 1942 and from the University of Maryland Law School in 1944. He practiced law with the Baltimore firm of Miles & Stockbridge. He was an assistant attorney general of Maryland in 1954-55 and City Solicitor for Baltimore in 1959-61.
        Judge Winter was a trustee of Johns Hopkins University, the Peabody Institute and the Evergreen House Foundation.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Gladys Woolford; two daughters, Barbara Glauber of Washington, and Anne West of Baltimore; and three grandchildren.

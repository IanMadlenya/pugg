        Wilbur E. Kelley, a retired engineer who worked in the procurement and processing of uranium and other elements for construction of the first atomic bomb, died of colon cancer March 16 at Deepdale Hospital in New Hyde Park, L.I. He was 80 years old and lived in New Hyde Park.
        A 1940 graduate of the University of Louisville, Mr. Kelley was vice president of Stone and Webster Engineering Corporation in Garden City, L.I., where he was responsible for design and construction of nuclear power plants around the world.
        During World War II he was area engineer for the Manhattan Project and afterward was manager of the New York office of the United States Atomic Energy Commission. He was also responsible for construction of the electro-magnetic plant at Oak Ridge, Tenn.
        He is survived by his wife, Elizabeth; two sons, Wilbur, of Greensboro, N.C., and William, of Freeport, L.I., and seven grandchildren.

        Harold E. Vick, a tenor saxophone player, composer and arranger, died last Friday of a heart attack at his home in Manhattan. He was 51 years old.
        Mr. Vick, who played with many different studio and jazz bands, appeared with a long list of well-known performers, including Nat Adderley, Aretha Franklin, Dizzy Gillespie, Mercer Ellington, Sarah Vaughan, Billy Taylor, Shirley Scott, Donald Byrd, Jimmy Smith and Jack McDuff.
        He performed in several movies, including ''Stardust Memories'' and ''Cotton Club,'' in which he appeared as a musician. His film credits included a featured role in the upcoming Spike Lee film ''School Days.'' He was also heard on the soundtrack for Mr. Lee's ''She's Gotta Have It.''
        Mr. Vick played for many stage productions, including ''Lena Horne: The Lady and Her Music,'' ''Sophisticated Ladies'' and ''Uptown It's Hot.''
        Mr. Vick, who was born in Rocky Mount, N.C., attended Howard University in Washington. He is survived by his mother and stepfather, Alice and Herbert Boddie of the Bronx, and his daughter, Cymantha Guest of Manhattan.

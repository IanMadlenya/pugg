        Ernest Bartley Gorin, a retired vice president of the RCA Corporation, died of congestive heart failure Sunday at Greenwich (Conn.) Hospital. He was 77 years old and lived in Riverside, Conn.
        Mr. Gorin, a native of Louisiana, Mo., joined the company in 1944, when it was called the Radio Corporation of America, a name it changed in 1969. He was elected treasurer in 1949, a vice president in 1951 and retired in 1974. Earlier he had been affiliated with Montgomery Ward & Company.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Mildred Sweeney; two sons, Walter, of Dallas, and Thomas, of Old Greenwich, and six grandchildren.


        Dr. Henry M. Goldman, the founding dean of the Goldman School of Graduate Dentistry at Boston University, died on Tuesday at Massachusetts General Hospital in Boston. He was 79 years old and lived in Boston.
        The cause of death was not disclosed.
        Dr. Goldman was a specialist in periodontology, or diseases of the gums. He developed the school, the country's first dental school that offered postdoctoral study, in 1963 and served as its dean until 1977, when he became dean emeritus. In 1978 the university's board of trustees named the school in his honor.
        A native of Boston, he was a graduate of Brown University and the Harvard School of Dental Medicine. He received honorary degrees from six universities, including Boston University, Brown and the University of Pennsylvania.
        Dr. Goldman was a former president of the American Society of Periodontists and the American Academy of Oral Pathology. He held many government consultancies, wrote many textbooks and edited the Journal of Periodontics and Periodontics.
        He is survived by his wife, Dorothy; two sons, Richard of Rochester and Gerald of Washington, and six grandchildren.


        Joseph M. Baird, a retired New York businessman and White House adviser on trade issues during the Kennedy Administration, died on Nov. 1 at Holy Cross Hospital in Fort Lauderdale, Fla. He was 82. A former resident of Merrick, L.I., he retired to Fort Lauderdale in 1973.
        Mr. Baird was the founder, in 1946, of the Baird Chemical Corporation, with headquarters in Manhattan. He sold the company to Ciba-Geigy of Switzerland in 1970.
        Early in his career, he became involved in Democratic politics, sitting on advisory panels of the State Democratic Committee and acting as treasurer. He also raised money and managed finances in New York for the Presidential campaigns of Adlai E. Stevenson, John F. Kennedy and Hubert H. Humphrey.
        Mr. Baird later advised President Kennedy on foreign trade during negotiations for the General Agreement on Tariffs and Trade. He also  went on several missions to Africa for the Department of Commerce.
        From 1961 to 1968, he was a member of the board of the National Urban League.
        A Manhattan native, he is survived by his wife of 55 years, Dolce Levine Baird; two daughters, Merrily Baird of McLean, Va., and Benita Baird of Atlanta; a son, Dr. Robert Baird of Hartford, and five grandchildren.

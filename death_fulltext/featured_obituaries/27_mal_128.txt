        John B. Poole, a lawyer and broadcasting executive, died Friday at his winter home in Miami Beach, Fla., after a long illness. He was 76 years old.  Mr. Poole was a graduate of the University of Chicago and of the Detroit College of Law. He lived in suburban Detroit for more than 50 years.
        From 1945 to 1955 he was secretary, vice president and general counsel of the Storer Broadcasting Company. He also served from 1960 to 1964 as a director and chairman of the executive committee of Capital Cities Communications in New York.
        In 1964, Mr. Poole founded Poole Broadcasting Company, which owned WJRT-TV in Flint, Mich., WPRI-TV in Providence, R.I., and WTEN-TV in Albany, N.Y. He sold the company to Knight-Ridder Newspapers Inc. in 1978.
        Mr. Poole is survived by his wife, Evelyn, a son, Michael; a daughter, Leah Boyle Vartanian; three stepchildren; a sister; five grandchildren and three great-granchildren.

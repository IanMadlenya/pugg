
        Jack Crawford, who won three Grand Slam tennis tournaments in one year, died here today. He was 83 years old. Mr. Crawford, who won Wimbledon, the French and Australian Opens in 1933 and lost the United States final to Fred Perry of Britain, died in a nursing home after a long illness.
        Mr. Crawford won an epic Wimbledon singles title against Ellsworth Vines in 1933, and was runner-up to Perry the next year. Mr. Crawford won four Australian titles and was a member of Australia's winning Davis Cup team in 1939.

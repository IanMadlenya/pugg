        Lincoln H. Clark, co-founder of the Cooperative for American Relief Everywhere, died of cancer Friday in his home in Falmouth, Me. He was 77 years old.
        Mr. Clark was also a professor of marketing at several colleges. He helped found the relief organization in 1945, to assist the recovery of postwar Europe. Mr. Clark delivered the first CARE package to France in 1946 and served as the group's assistant executive director until 1947.

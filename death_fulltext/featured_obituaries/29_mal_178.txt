        Nathan Zucker, an independent film producer, died Friday of kidney failure at Princeton (N.J.) Medical Center. He was 74 years old and lived in Cranbury, N.J.
        Mr. Zucker was born in Manhattan and graduated from Yale University. In 1948, he founded Dynamic Films, which specialized in nontheatrical and educational films. He experimented in television by transmitting a live Broadway performance to a Rochester theater's television screen and was at one time the president of the Film Producers Association of New York. He retired in 1975.
        Surviving are his wife, Helen; two sons, Paul, of Glen Ridge, N.J., and William, of Newton, Mass; two daughters, Ruth Montone of Sydney, Australia, and Laura Zucker of Los Angeles; a brother, Harry, of Bridgeport, Conn., and seven grandchildren.

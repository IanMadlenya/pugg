
        Adam Young, who helped found one of the nation's 25 largest television station groups, died Monday in Palm Beach, Fla. He was 91.
        He had a stroke, Young Broadcasting Inc. said.
        Mr. Young joined NBC as a page at 17, and moved up to the research and promotion department before leaving in 1936. He shifted into advertising with his own business representing radio and later television stations before starting Young Broadcasting Inc. with his son Vincent in 1986. The company owns 10 television stations.
        In addition to Vincent Young of Bedford, N.Y., he is survived by his wife, Margaret Kelly Young; another son, Richard, of Greenwich, Conn.; a daughter, Sharon Conroy of Palm Beach, Fla.; and nine grandchildren. Another daughter, Susan Quinn, died before him.

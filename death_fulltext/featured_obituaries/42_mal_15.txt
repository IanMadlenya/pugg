        Kenn Stryker-Rodda, a teacher, editor and lecturer, died Thursday at Heritage House, a care center in Wilkes-Barre, Pa., where he lived. He was 86 years old.
        His son, Paul, said Dr. Stryker-Rodda died of complications from Parkinson's disease.
        After he retired in 1960 from a career in education, Dr. Stryker-Rodda began a second career in genealogy. He specialized in Dutch and English Colonial history, especially in the New York area, and founded Flagon and Trencher, a society of descendants of Colonial tavern-keepers.
        From 1966 until 1986 he edited the New York Genealogical and Biographical Society Record, a quarterly publication. He was a past president of the American Society of Genealogists, of which he was a fellow, and of the National Society of Genealogists. He was also a fellow of the Holland Society.
        Dr. Stryker-Rodda was born in Arlington, N.J. He held a bachelor's degree from Princeton, a master's from New York University and a doctorate in literature from Webster University. He taught at the Pennington School in New Jersey, the Massahutten Military Academy in Virginia and Colby Academy in Brooklyn.
        In addition to his son, who lives in Dallas, Pa., he is survived by his wife, the former Harriet Mott; a daughter, Andrea, of Arlington; four grandchldren and two great-grandchildren.

        Herbert P. Lansdale Jr., a retired executive of the National Council of Young Men's Christian Associations, died of heart failure a week ago yesterday at his home in Palo Alto, Calif. He was 90 years old.
        Mr. Lansdale, a native of Baltimore, began his career with the Y.M.C.A. as educational director in Worcester, Mass., after graduating from Oberlin College with bachelor's and master's degrees.
        From 1925 to 1939 he served as general secretary of the Y.M.C.A. in Thessalonika, Greece, as part of World Service, a program that helps Y.M.C.A.'s overseas. He returned to the United States and headed the organization in Rochester, N.Y. In 1957, he was elected general secretary of the National Council of Y.M.C.A's, based in New York, the organization's highest staff position. He retired in 1964.
        He is survived by his wife, Jessie Lansdale of Palo Alto; two sons, H. Parker of Sarasota, Fla. and Bruce of Thessalonika; two daughters, Ann Hartley Montchiloff of Boston and Jacqueline Peterson of Palo Alto; 20 grandchildren and 12 great-grandchildren.

        Larry Shay, a composer and musical coach who worked with such stars as Al Jolson and Jimmy Durante and whose best-known song was ''When You're Smiling,'' died of lung cancer on Monday at Hoag Memorial Hospital. He was 90 years old.
        Mr. Shay's work was recorded by Judy Garland, Bing Crosby, Perry Como, Andy Williams, Louis Armstrong, Nat (King) Cole, Dean Martin, Doris Day and Guy Lombardo.
        His career started in World War I, when he put together a band and show group for soldiers.
        In the 1920's he lived in New York, coaching such stars as Durante, Jolson and Sophie Tucker. He was Metro-Goldwyn-Mayer's movie musical director in the early 1930's and was program director for NBC radio in the late 30's.
        Among his other compositions are ''That's Georgia,'' ''Kentucky, Sure as You're Born,'' ''Knee Deep in Daisies'' and ''Tie Me to Your Apron Strings Again.''
        He is survived by his wife, Doris, a daughter, a son, seven granchildren and three great-grandchildren.

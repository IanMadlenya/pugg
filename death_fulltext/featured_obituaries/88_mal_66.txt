
        Stephen C. Smith, a lobbyist for gay rights, died on Tuesday at the Hospice of Washington. Mr. Smith, who lived in Washington, was 37.
        He died of AIDS, said Gregory King, a friend.
        Mr. Smith, who received a law degree from Harvard in 1980, was a lobbyist from 1987 to 1993 for the Human Rights Campaign Fund, a national political organization in Washington that campaigns for gay rights. He was president of the Gay and Lesbian Activist Alliance of Washington from 1983 to 1985.
        He is survived by his mother, Margaret Smith of Geneseo, N.Y.; two brothers, David, of Geneseo, and Edward, of Raleigh, N.C.; four sisters, Katherine Smith of Henrietta, N.Y., Anne Williams and Susan Smith, both of Geneseo, and Barbara Sylvester of Ithaca, N.Y., and his companion, Greg Gibbs of Washington.

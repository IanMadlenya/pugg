        John David House, a founder of the 110,000-member United Rubber Workers union, died Sunday. He was 83 years old.
        A native of Cleveland, Ga., Mr. House moved to Akron after he was graduated from high school and subsequently worked for B. F. Goodrich, Firestone Tire and Rubber Company and Goodyear Tire and Rubber Company.
        Mr. House was at the Portage Hotel in Akron when the rubber workers' union was founded there on Sept. 12, 1935. He was the first president of Local 2 in Akron, serving from 1935 to 1940, and held several other positions with the union before he retired Dec. 31, 1969.
        Mr. House, a widower, is survived by his son, John Jr. of Naples, Fla., and a daughter, Eloise Hall, of Akron.

        William H. Lang, a retired steel company executive, died Wednesday at a nursing home in Palm Harbor, Fla. He was 78 years old and lived in Belleair, Fla.
        Mr. Lang was executive vice president for realty and finance for the United States Steel Corporation, now the USX Corporation, at his retirement in 1976.
        He had been with the company for 42 years, having joined it in 1934 as a statistician.
        A 1933 graduate of Dartmouth College, Mr. Lang received a master's degree from the University of Pittsburgh.
        He served as an overseer of the Amos Tuck School of Business Administration at Dartmouth and as a trustee of the College for Medicine and Dentistry of New Jersey. He was also a former treasurer of the New York Deafness Research Foundation and was a municipal official of Millburn, N.J.
        He is survived by his wife, Lida; three daughters, Marjorie Smith of Princeton, N.J., Janet Strife of Cincinnati, and Jean Rexford of Westport, Conn., and 10 grandchildren.

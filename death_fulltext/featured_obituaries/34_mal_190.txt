        Julius Kass, a prominent labor relations lawyer in Manhattan for many years and a former senior partner of Bandler & Kass, died of a heart ailment Tuesday at his home in Bal Harbour, Fla. He was 84 years old.
        Mr. Kass was counsel to the New York City Division of Labor from 1946 to 1948 and helped negotiate contracts with unions representing city employees. He returned to private practice in 1948 and continued in labor relations. He moved to Florida in 1970 and served as chairman of the Pan American Aluminum Corporation. He retired in 1980.
        During World War II he served as chief of labor relations for the Army Air Forces and later headed the strikes section of the National War Labor Board. He was a 1929 graduate of the New Jersey Law School.
        He is survived by his wife, Eleanor; a daughter, Emme Pedinielli of Miami Shores, Fla.; a son, Stephen, of White Plains, and seven grandchildren.

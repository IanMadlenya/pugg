
        Dr. Alexander Gralnick, the founder and psychiatrist in chief of High Point Hospital in Port Chester, N.Y., died on Thursday at Columbia-Presbyterian Medical Center in Manhattan. He was 80 and lived in Port Chester and West Palm Beach, Fla.
        The cause was complications of heart-bypass surgery, his family said.
        Dr. Gralnick specialized in the study of schizophrenia and suicide. In 1951 he founded High Point, a private psychiatric hospital that has 43 beds. He also created the Gralnick Foundation to further the study of mental illness and its treatment.
        He was vice president of the Society of Medical Psychoanalysts and president of the Central Neuropsychiatric Hospital Association and of the National Association of Private Psychiatric Hospitals. He was also active in the Central Westchester Planning Commission, American Psychiatric Association, International Congress of Social Psychiatry and International Society of Adolescent Psychiatry. Author of 8 Books
        He taught at New York Medical College for about 15 years, wrote 105 scientific papers and 8 books and received several professional awards. He was active in peace and anti-nuclear causes.
        Dr. Gralnick was born in New York City. After graduating from Columbia University, he received his medical degree at Rush College and studied psychoanalysis at New York Medical College.
        He worked six years at Central Islip State Hospital, advancing to supervising psychiatrist, then had a private practice in Manhattan for five years before founding High Point.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Dr. June Gantz, a psychologist; his children by two previous marriages, William, of Stamford, Conn., Daniel, of Fort Walton Beach, Fla., Michael, of Ryebrook, N.Y., and Nancy Norris of Boxboro, Mass.; two stepsons, Michael Gantz of Westport, Conn., and Robert Gantz of Los Angeles, and six grandchildren.

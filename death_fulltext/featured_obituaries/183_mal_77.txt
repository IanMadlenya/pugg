
        Leonard Marx, a longtime developer and manager of commercial real estate properties throughout the United States and Canada, died on April 12 at his home in Scarsdale, N.Y. He was 97.
        When he died, he was president of the Marx Realty and Improvement Company, based in Manhattan, which he founded in the late 1920's.
        He had also been chairman and chief executive of Merchants National Properties Inc. since the 1930's.
        Over the years, he was also active in philanthropy.
        He and his wife endowed two professorships at Teachers College of Columbia University and built the Virginia Marx Children's Center at Westchester Community College.
        His wife, Virginia Wise Marx, died in 1999.
        His survivors include a son, Leonard Jr., of Greenwich, Conn.; two daughters, Mary Lynn Bianco of Los Angeles and Jennifer Gruenberg of Scarsdale; six grandchildren; and seven great-grandchildren.

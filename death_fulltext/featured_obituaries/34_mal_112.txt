        Frederick Katz, a lawyer and philanthropist, died Wednesday at his home in Manhattan. He was 92 years old and had been active on the Committee on Character and Fitness of the Appellate Division of State Supreme Court until he became ill in June.
        Mr. Katz, a 1921 graduate of New York Law School, had been active for many years in Democratic Party campaigns in the Bronx, especially on behalf of candidates for judicial positions. He was president of the Hebrew Institute of University Heights for 21 years and had supported many civic and charitable organizations.
        He is survived by three daughters, Mickie Wohl of West Orange, N.J., Lucille Sorkow of Cliffside Park, N.J., and Marian Vatske of the Bronx; two sisters, Augusta Marmorstein of Fort Lee, N.J., and Clara Amster of Tucson, Ariz.; six grandchildren, and two great-grandchildren.

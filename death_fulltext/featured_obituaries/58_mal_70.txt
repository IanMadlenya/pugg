
        Kenneth M. Nelson, who headed the United States operations of a Dutch publishing company, Elsevier N.V., died on Sunday at his home in Mendham, N.J. He was 47 years old.
        He died of liver cancer, said his secretary, Candy Thompson.
        A Chicagoan, Mr. Nelson became chief executive officer of Elsevier U.S. Holdings, a division of the Dutch company, earlier this year, and worked until shortly before his death.
        Mr. Nelson was on the executive board of the Dutch company. Earlier he was president of Elsevier Business Press, a division of Elsevier U.S. Holdings.
        He is survived by his wife, Barbara, and two sons, Chris and Brian.

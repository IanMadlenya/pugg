
        John Scanlan, a character actor who appeared on and Off Broadway, died on Sunday at his home in Closter, N.J. He was 73.
        The cause was congestive heart failure, said his nephew, Cornelius Geary.
        In a career spanning more than 40 years, Mr. Scanlan appeared in some 200 roles. His Broadway credits include "Mastergate," "The Best Man" and "Captain Brassbound's Conversion." Off Broadway, he was seen in "The Plough and the Stars," "The Master Builder" and "No Exit." His final stage appearance was in the New York Shakespeare Festival's 1992 production of "As You Like It" in Central Park.
        Mr. Scanlan, who was born in Milwaukee, attended the American Academy of Dramatic Arts in New York. He appeared with many regional theaters and gave 1,000 performances of a one-man show, "Richard Mansfield, Curtain Call."
        He was seen in several films, including "Green Card" and "The Hudsucker Proxy," and on television in "The Goodyear Playhouse," "Alcoa Hour" and on soap operas. He was also the author of "Davis," a novel published in 1969.
        He is survived by his wife, Kathleen, of Closter; two sisters, Sara Geary of Random Lake, Wis., and Mary Brigden of Wauwatosa, Wis.; three sons: John Jr., of South Bend, Ind.; Stephen, of Rowland Heights, Calif., and Peter, of Ridgefield Park, N.J., and a daughter, Brigid Grode of Camp Hill, Pa.

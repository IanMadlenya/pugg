
        Bernard Raymond Bell, an economist and a former deputy director of the World Bank, died on March 29 at his home in Washington. He was 82.
        He died of cancer, his family said.
        Mr. Bell began his career as an economist with the Department of Agriculture in 1935. He later became an analyst for the Office of Inter-American Affairs and chief economist of the United States Export-Import Bank.
        He was also a consultant for the Governments of Indonesia and Israel. At the World Bank, he was deputy director from 1966 to 1968, and was its vice president for East Africa and then for East Asia in the 1970's.
        Mr. Bell later served as a consulting economist for the International Finance Corporation and for the World Bank, and was a director of the Housing Development Corporation in Bombay until 1989.
        He is survived by his wife, Judith Tumbelaka Bell; two daughters from his first marriage to the late Jackie Hunter Bell, Carolyn Pancoast of Gaithersburg, Md., and Wendy Lane of Manasquan, N.J.; a sister, Harriet Segal of Philadelphia, and five stepchildren.

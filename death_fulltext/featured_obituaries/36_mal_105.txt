        Donald F. Bowler, an investigator best known for helping send the gangster Mickey Cohen to prison for tax evasion, was found dead on Tuesday at his home in Canoga Park, Calif. The cause of death was not made known. He was 69 years old.  Mr. Bowler, as intelligence chief of the Internal Revenue Service for Southern California, directed the grand jury investigation in 1960 and 1961 that led to Mr. Cohen's imprisonment for hiding about $400,000 from tax collectors.
        From 1976 to 1981 Mr. Bowler was chief of the Bureau of Investigation for the Los Angeles County district attorney.
        He is survived by his wife, Betty, and five children.

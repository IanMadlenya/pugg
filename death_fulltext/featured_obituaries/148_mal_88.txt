
        Stephen S. Weisglass, the executive managing director of Conifer Securities, a San Francisco investment banking firm, died on Sunday at his home in Miami. He was 70.
        The cause of death was lung cancer, according to a statement from the Owen Cheatham Foundation, a nonprofit organization headed by his wife, Celeste, who survives him. Mr. Weisglass served as vice president and treasurer of the foundation.
        A native of New York, he became president of Ladenburg Thalmann & Company, a New York brokerage firm, in 1979. In 1986 he was named Ladenburg's vice chairman. He left the firm seven years later, when he went into semiretirement and moved to Miami. Prior to joining Ladenburg, Mr. Weisglass worked for Bear, Stearns & Company.
        In addition to his wife, he is survived by two stepsons, Kenneth R. Kennerly of Jupiter, Fla., and Owen C. Kennerly of San Francisco, as well as two step-grandchildren.

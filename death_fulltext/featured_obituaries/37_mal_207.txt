        Bernard G. Nemeroff, a lawyer and owner of the Dellwood Country Club in New City, N.Y., died of lung cancer on Sunday at Mount Sinai Hospital in Miami Beach. He was 82 years old and had lived in retirement in Miami Beach since 1979.
        Mr. Nemeroff, who was a native of Brooklyn, attended Pennsylvania State University and was a graduate of the New York University Law School. He practiced with Nemeroff, Jelline, Danzig, Paley & Kaufman law firm in Manhattan.
        In 1948 Mr. Nemeroff bought Dellwood, which is still owned by his family, and managed it until 1963.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Frances Gottlieb; three daughters, Gladys Agell of West Topsham, Vt., Susan Cohen of Hanover, N.H., and Karen Cohn of Naples, Fla.; a son, Michael, of Bethesda, Md.; a brother, Herbert, of Rancho Palos Verdes, Calif., and 11 grandchildren.

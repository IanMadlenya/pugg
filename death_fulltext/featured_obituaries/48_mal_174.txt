
        Benjamin Mendelsund, an architect and sculptor, died on Sunday at Memorial Sloan-Kettering Cancer Center in Manhattan. He was 48 years old and lived in Scarborough, N.Y.
        He died of a brain tumor, his family said.
        Mr. Mendelsund, who was born in the Bronx, graduated from Columbia College and the Columbia School of Architecture. His sculpture was exhibited at shows in Manhattan and in Brattleboro, Vt.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Judith Gimbel; his parents, Shifrah and Henoch, of the Riverdale section of the Bronx; a daughter, Lisa, and a son, Peter, both of Manhattan, and a sister, Effie Goldwasser of Manhattan.

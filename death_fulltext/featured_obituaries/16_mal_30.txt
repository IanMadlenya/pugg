        Walter N. Reilly, a motion-picture and theater executive, died of cancer on Sunday at Northern Westchester Hospital in White Plains. He was 73 years old.
        Mr. Reilly served as chief executive assistant to Dore Schary, the producer and writer, for nearly 20 years, first at Metro-Goldwyn-Mayer and later with Dore Schary Productions, a New York theatrical production company. The son of James F. Reilly, an executive director of the League of New York Theaters, Walter Reilly was involved with several of Mr. Schary's Broadway productions, including ''Sunrise at Campobello'' (1958) and ''The Unsinkable Molly Brown'' (1960), for which he served as associate producer.
        Mr. Reilly is survived by two sisters, Genevieve Ford of Somers, N.Y., and Mary Glacken of Alexandria, Va.

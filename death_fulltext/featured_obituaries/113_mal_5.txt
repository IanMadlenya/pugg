
        Ebrahim Eshaghian, a dealer in carpets, a real estate executive and a philanthropist who was a benefactor of Yeshiva University, died on Tuesday at the Bencor Hospital in Los Angeles. Mr. Eshaghian, who maintained homes in Flushing, Queens, and Los Angeles, was 85.
        In 1990, Mr. Eshaghian pledged $2 million to Yeshiva. His contribution established the Ebrahim Ben Davood Eliahu Eshaghian Transgenic Facility for Biomedical Research at the university's Albert Einstein College of Medicine. It  also created scholarships at Yeshiva College, Stern College for Women and the Rabbi Isaac Elchanan Theological Seminary, a school for Sephardic students, descendants of the Jews who fled the Spanish Inquisition.
        Mr. Eshaghian also endowed a nursery school at the Hillcrest Jewish Center in Flushing, and an auditorium at the Eretz Cultural Center in Resada, Calif.
        Mr. Eshaghian was born in Teheran, Iran, in 1910. He became the president of  the Societe Maghroub, importers of most of Iran's textiles from France, and an owner and developer of real estate..
        He came to the United States in 1960. He founded and was former president of  E. D. Eshaghian Rug Corporation in Manhattan and joined with his family in real  estate enterprises.
        Mr. Eshaghian is survived by his wife, Nelly; four sons, David and E. (Ike) Eshaghian, both of Forest Hills, Queens; Massoud Jack and Joseph Eshaghian, both of Los Angeles; a daughter, Helen Iloulian of Beverly Hills, Calif.; 12 grandchildren, and 7 great-grandchildren.


        Dr. Douglas Stanton Damrosch, a physician and medical administrator, died on Sunday at a health center in Ellsworth, Me. He was 76 years old and lived in Blue Hill, Me.
        He died of colon cancer, his daughter Barbara said.
        In 1941, Dr. Damrosch joined Columbia-Presbyterian Medical Center as an assistant resident. He was associate professor of pediatrics and associate dean in the College of Physicians and Surgeons. From 1965 to 1966 he was assistant vicepresident of Presbyterian Hospital, and from 1970 to 1971 he was director of the medical center.
        He was a consultant to the Federal Department of Health, Education and Welfare from 1965 to 1966. He also served as medical director of Mary Imogene Bassett Hospital in Cooperstown, N.Y., a teaching hospital of Columbia University, from 1966 to 1967, and director of the Associated Medical Schools of New York and New Jersey in the early 1970's. He retired in 1975.
        He graduated from Columbia College and Columbia University's College of Physicians and Surgeons in 1940. During World War II, he served in the Navy on destroyer duty and later as a lieutenant commander in charge of an epidemiology unit.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Eleanor Southern; three daughters, Anne Williams of Burlington, Vt., Barbara Damrosch of Washington, Conn., and Eloise MacMurray of Portland, Ore.; a sister, the Rev. Mary Damrosch Sleeper of Orono, Me., and four grandchildren.

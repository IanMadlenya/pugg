        Panos Georgopulo, the president of Georgopulo & Company, Inc., makers and importers of cigarettes and tobacco products, died of amyotrophic lateral sclerosis on Saturday, his 69th birthday, at his home in Westport, Conn.
        The company makes Rameses II, Andron and Turkish Special cigarettes from blends of imported and domestic tobacco at its factory at 48 Stone Street in Manhattan.
        Mr. Georgopulo was a native of Manhattan and a graduate of the Trinity School and Lehigh University. In World War II, he was a staff sergeant in the Signal Corps in the South Pacific.
        Surviving are his wife Elinor; a daughter Katina, and a brother George, all of Westport.

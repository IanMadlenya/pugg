        David C. Anchin, a retired managing partner of Anchin, Block & Anchin, a New York accounting firm, died on Tuesday at the Cleveland Clinic in Ohio. He was 86 years old and lived in Sarasota, Fla.
        Mr. Anchin underwent heart surgery two weeks ago, but the exact cause of death was not known, said Norman Berlant, a spokesman for the firm.
        Mr. Anchin, a certified public accountant, founded the firm with Max Block and Max Anchin in Manhattan in 1923. He retired in 1971.
        Mr. Anchin was a graduate of City College and of Fordham Law School.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Anne Sternlight; two daughters, Ruth Hochman of Sarasota and Isabel Becker of Rye, N.Y.; and eight grandchildren. Also surviving are a brother, Howard, of Scarsdale, N.Y., and a sister, Mildred Schindler of Deefield Beach, Fla.

        Charles M. Wiltse, a historian and an editor of the papers of Daniel Webster, died on Tuesday at Dartmouth-Hitchcock Medical Center in Hanover, N.H. He was 83 years old and lived in Hanover.
        Mr. Wiltse was a professor of history emeritus at Dartmouth College, where he was on the faculty from 1966 to 1972. In this period he edited the Webster papers, a 16-volume work that was published by the University Press of New England from 1974 to 1989.
        Professor Wiltse also edited a three-volume collection of the papers of John C. Calhoun, published by Bobbs-Merrill from 1944 to 1951. He was the author of ''The Jeffersonian Tradition in American Democracy'' (University of North Carolina Press, 1935).
        He was a graduate of West Virginia University and received a doctorate from Cornell University.
        A widower, he is survived a sister, Clara E. Wiltse of Muncie, Ind.

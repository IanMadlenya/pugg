        Lloyd Marx, a composer and conductor who was musical director of ''The Original Amateur Hour,'' died at his home in Manhattan yesterday. He was 72 years old and had had complications from a heart attack in November.
        Mr. Marx, who composed and conducted for film, television, radio and recordings, was a partner, director and musical conductor for ''The Original Amateur Hour'' from its inception with Maj. Edward Bowes at the Capitol Theater in 1935 through its days on radio and during the television program with Ted Mack.
        Mr. Marx was also a founding member of the New York Chapter of the National Academy of Television Arts and Sciences and was a staff conductor for CBS for 11 years.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Barbara Prentice, and a daughter, Judy Marx Kelley of Dumas, Tex.

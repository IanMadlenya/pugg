        John J. Ryan, who emigrated from Ireland as a penniless 19-year-old and eventually owned several businesses in Queens, died of a stroke Saturday at the Ozanam nursing home in Bayside. He was 83 years old and lived in Astoria, Queens.
        He is survived by his wife, Mary; three sons, the Rev. John Ryan of Middletown, N.Y.; Patrick, of Circleville, N.Y., and Michael, of Forest Hills, a senior vice president of The New York Times Company; two daughters, Sister Mary Ellen of Manhattan and Margaret Admirand of Port Washington, N.Y., and nine grandchildren.

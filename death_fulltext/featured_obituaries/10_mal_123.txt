        Gregory Muhlin, a research scientist at the New York State Psychiatric Institute at the Presbyterian Hospital in Manhattan, died at the hospital Tuesday after suffering a cerebral hemorrhage. He was 42 years old and lived in Brooklyn.
        Mr. Muhlin was a specialist in the epidemiology of mental disorders. He had published studies on why criminals become recidivists, and on the neighborhoods that were looted after the 1977 blackout in New York City.
        He is survived by three children, Jay, Karen and Jessica.

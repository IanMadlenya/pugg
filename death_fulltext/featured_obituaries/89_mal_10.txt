
        Dominic V. Kane, who in the 1940's was the president and part owner of what is now the Brooklyn Union Gas Company, died on Monday at the Shady Grove Adventist Hospital in Rockville, Md. Mr. Kane, who lived in Silver Spring, Md., was 85.
        The cause was congestive heart failure, said his son, Paul Kane.
        In the 1930's Mr. Kane was president of the Brooklyn Union Coal Company, a subsidiary of the Lehigh Coal and Navigation Company, which was a major supplier of fuel to New York City homes. In 1944 he organized a management buyout, and after World War II sold the company.
        He became an investigator for the General Accounting Office in Washington, and in the 1960's was a financial officer for the National Aeronautics and Space Administration in Washington.
        He is survived by two sons: Michael of White Oak, Md., and Paul of McLean, Va.; two daughters, Margaret Kane and Patricia Curtiss, both of Wheaton, Md.; a brother, Edward of Hudson, N.Y., and a sister Virginia Neumann of Huntington, L.I.

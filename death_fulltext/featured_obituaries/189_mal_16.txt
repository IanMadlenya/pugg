
        Justice Richard A. Goldberg, an active justice on the New York State Supreme Court in Brooklyn, died on Monday at a hospital in Warwick, N.Y. He was 71.
        The cause was pancreatic cancer, his family said.
        Richard Alan Goldberg was born in Brooklyn. He graduated from City College with a major in business administration in 1951 and from Columbia Law School in 1954. He was a partner in the firm Goldberg & Morganstern and a hearing examiner for the city's Parking Violations Bureau when first elected to the New York City Civil Court in 1977.
        A Democrat, he was elected to the Supreme Court in 1990. He wrote the operations manual for the court's Kings County Civil Term in 1997 and was a former president of the City College Alumni Association.
        Justice Goldberg is survived by his wife, Lola Morales Goldberg; two sons, Glenn J. of Medford, N.J., and Evan, of New Rochelle, N.Y.; a sister, Sheila Benson of Brooklyn; three stepsons, Paul, Ronald and Robert Gallo; and 10 grandchildren.

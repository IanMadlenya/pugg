
        John A. Miles, a former director of the New York office of the United Nations Relief and Works Agency for Palestine Refugees, died on Friday at the Sacred Heart Hospice in Sydney, Australia. He was 67 years old and had homes in Manhattan and Shelter Island, L.I.
        He died of a brain tumor, said William F. Lee, who succeeded Mr. Miles as director.
        Mr. Miles, an Australian, retired in 1991 after 12 years as head of the office, which is at 2 United Nations Plaza. The agency has offices in Vienna and Amman, Jordan.
        His 1951 marriage to Audrey Gibbins ended in divorce. He is survived by his second wife, the former Margaret Lackey, to whom he was married in 1976; five children from his first marriage, Deborah Birnbaum, Philip Miles and Andrew Miles, all of Sydney, and Catherine Jesiolowski and Gregory Miles, both of Coff's Harbour, New South Wales, and eight grandchildren.

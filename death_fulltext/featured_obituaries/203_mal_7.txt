
        Edward W. Scudder, former owner and president of The Newark News and the Newark Broadcasting Corporation, died on Nov. 27 at his home in Lake Placid, N.Y. He was 91.
        His principal residence was in Delray Beach, Fla., his son Edward said.
        Edward Wallace Scudder Jr. was born on Dec. 8, 1911, in Newark. He graduated from Lawrenceville School and Princeton University, and joined the Evening News Publishing Company, which published The Newark News, in 1935.
        Mr. Scudder was a naval aviator in World War II and became a lieutenant commander.
        He then went to work in the mailroom of The News, which was founded by his grandfather, Wallace M. Scudder, in 1883, and eventually became president. His brother, Richard, was publisher and part owner. They sold the paper in 1970 to Media General; after a strike in 1971, it closed in 1972.
        Mr. Scudder also helped found and operate the Newark Broadcasting Company, which owned WVNJ, an AM-FM radio station. He was president until 1978, when he and his brother sold the company.
        Mr. Scudder was vice president of another family company, the Garden State Paper Company, which recycled newsprint.
        His wife, Louise Fry, died in 1998. He is survived by his brother, Richard, of Atlantic Highlands, N.J.; his daughters, Katherine Tiballi, of Shelburne, Vt., and Gale Scudder, of Hawaii; his sons Edward, of San Diego, and Robert, of Yardley, Pa.; eight grandchildren and three great-grandchildren.

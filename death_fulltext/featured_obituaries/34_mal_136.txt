        Sherman Plunkett, an offensive tackle for the champion Baltimore Colts in 1958 and 1959 who starred for the Jets later in his career, died of cancer Saturday at Sinai Hospital. He was 56 years old.
        Mr. Plunkett played 10 seasons with the Colts, the San Diego Chargers and the Jets. As a Jet, he was named to the American Football League all-star game in 1964 and 1966.
        He was born in Oklahoma City and attended Maryland State College, later renamed the University of Maryland-Eastern Shore. He was signed in 1956 by the Cleveland Browns but was with the team only a few months before being drafted into the Army. Mr. Plunkett joined the Colts in 1958.
        He is survived by his wife, Betty.

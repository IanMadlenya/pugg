        Edward J. Quinn, a retired banking executive, died of cancer Sunday at his home in Huntington, L.I. He was 77 years old.
        Mr. Quinn, who was born in Manhattan, joined the Bank of Farmingdale in 1928. He was a senior vice president of the Franklin National Bank and senior vice president of the European American Bank, from which he retired in 1976.
        He was a former treasurer of the Farmingdale School District.
        Surviving are his wife, Margaret; two daughters, Maryanne Quinn Brown of Bedford, N.Y., and Sheila Quinn Lucey of Islip, L.I.; two sons, James, of McLean, Va., and Patrick, of Huntington; a sister, Helen Kinike of Belmore, L.I., and six grandchildren.

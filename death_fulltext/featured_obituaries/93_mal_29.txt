
        Dr. Daniel John Melia, a retired pediatrician and longtime Police Commissioner of Hastings-on-Hudson, N.Y., died on Sunday at Columbia-Presbyterian Medical Center in Manhattan. He was 75 and had lived in Irvington, N.Y., since 1990.
        The cause of death was cancer, said his son, John.
        Dr. Melia, a graduate of Phillips Exeter Academy, received a B.A. from Harvard in 1941 and entered the Coast Guard Academy. Commissioned as a lieutenant, junior grade, he served in the North Atlantic on the U.S.S. Bibb, a cutter, from 1942 until 1944, when he retired with a service-related illness, tuberculosis.
        After graduating from the Columbia University College of Physicians and Surgeons, he interned at Bellevue Hospital from 1949 to 1950. He had a private practice in pediatrics in Yonkers from 1952 to 1975.
        Dr. Melia was the director of medical education and director of pediatrics at Yonkers General Hospital from 1975 to 1987, when he retired from the practice of medicine. He was also an assistant clinical professor of pediatrics at the Columbia College of Physicians and Surgeons from 1953 to 1987.
        He was a fellow of the American Academy of Pediatrics.
        Dr. Melia was appointed Police Commissioner of Hastings in 1962 and held the job until 1991.
        His father, Francis A. J. Melia, was the manager of the Harvard Club in New York from 1919 to 1945.
        In addition to his son, of Brooklyn, Dr. Melia is survived by his wife of 52 years, the former Rita Lough; another son, Daniel, of San Francisco; a daughter, Deborah Ann Clark of Irvington; a sister, Mary Louise Friedman of Garrett Park, Md., and six grandchildren.


        Claude S. Vanden Broeck, an investment banker, died on Saturday at Columbia-Presbyterian Medical Center. He was 74 and lived in Ridgewood, N.J.
        The cause was cancer of the esophagus, his family said.
        At his death, Mr. Vanden Broeck was senior vice president for investment management at C. J. Lawrence/Deutsche Bank Securities Corporation.
        A native of Belgium, he served with United States Army counterintelligence during the liberation of Europe. He was a 1946 physics graduate of St. Lawrence University in Canton, N.Y.
        In 1955 he joined his father, Alfred, in founding a securities firm, which later merged with Prescott, Merrill Turben of Cleveland. As managing partner he led the new company through a series of acquisitions to build its presence in both Ohio and New York.
        Mr. Vanden Broeck is survived by his wife, May Lee Vanden Broeck; three daughters, Leslie Dale of Portland, Ore., Barbara Fernie of Boulder, Colo., and Claire Vanden Broeck of Napa Valley, Calif.; a son, Peter, also of Ridgewood; a brother, Andre, of South Otselic, N.Y.; two stepdaughters, Tina Starke of Houston and Cathy Lee of Washington, and five grandchildren.

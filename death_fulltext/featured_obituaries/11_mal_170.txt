        Lewis C. Coffin, a former law librarian of the Library of Congress, died of cancer Monday at his home in Washington. He was 78 years old.
        Born in Ashland, Me., he graduated from Bowdoin College and George Washington University Law School. He began working for the Library of Congress in 1931 and, except for four years in the Navy, the library was his career. In 1964 he became law librarian and general counsel. He retired in 1971.
        Mr. Coffin is survived by his wife, Inger Warmind Coffin; a daughter, Elizabeth L. Wallace of Washington, and a stepson, Morten Warmind of Copenhagen.


        Minton M. Anderson, former director and executive vice president of the Aluminum Company of America, died Friday at his home in Pittsburgh. He was 92 years old.
        Mr. Anderson died of natural causes, a spokeswoman for the family said.
        He joined the company in Pittsburgh as personnel manager in 1930, was elected a director in 1957 and executive vice president in 1959. He retired in 1963, but continued as a director until 1968.
        He was a founder of the Aluminum Association in 1933, and was its president in 1959 and 1960 and chairman in 1961 and 1962. He was a former director of the National Association of Manufacturers and of the Dollar Savings Bank of Pittsburgh.
        Mr. Anderson was born in Cannon Falls, Minn., and graduated from the University of Minnesota in 1920. He also received a master's degree in chemical engineering from the university, which, in 1954 presented him its outstanding achievement award.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Florence Johnson; two daughters, Marilyn Gilmore and Janet Kennedy, both of Pittsburgh; five grandchildren and seven great-grandchildren.

        Leonard Elliott, an actor and comedian who performed on the Broadway stage and in Manhattan nightclubs, died of cardiac arrest on Sunday in St. Vincent's Hospital. He was 84 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Elliott's acting experience included light opera in summer theaters. He made his debut on Broadway in 1938 in ''Right This Way'' with Joe E. Lewis and Joey Ray, portrayed Judas in ''Family Portrait,'' which starred Judith Anderson, in 1939, and was in ''The Would-Be Gentleman'' with Bobby Clark in 1946. His last professional appearance was in 1961 in ''The Gay Life.''

        Dr. Joseph D. Bachelder, a public-opinion researcher, died yesterday at his home in Jamesburg, N.J. He was 78 years old.
        Dr. Bachelder was a former president of the Center for Marketing Communications in Princeton, N.J. He retired in 1976, after 22 years there.
        In his career he conducted more than 700 polls, many of them for Republican office-seekers, including former Presidents Dwight D. Eisenhower and Richard M. Nixon and former Gov. Nelson A. Rockefeller. In 1948, Dr. Bachelder correctly predicted Harry S. Truman's defeat of Thomas E. Dewey.
        He was born in Beloit, Wis. Dr. Bachelder was an alumnus of Westminster College in Fulton, Mo., and had a doctorate from Yale. He had taught at the University of New Hampshire, Washington State and the University of Illinois. He was also a lieutenant commander in the Navy in World War II.
        Surviving are two sons, Joseph, of Princeton, and Stephen, of Portland, Me.; a daughter, Jane Johnson of Bridgewater, N.J.; eight grandchildren, and two great-grandchildren.

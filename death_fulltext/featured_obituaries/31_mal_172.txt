        Jan Stacy, a writer and artist, died of AIDS on Aug. 20 at Cape Cod Hospital in Hyannis, Mass. He was 41 years old.
        Mr. Stacy wrote several books about movies, including ''The Great Book of Movie Monsters,'' and several paperback adventure novels, including a series called ''Body Smasher.''
        In the mid-1960's, Mr. Stacy co-founded a drug-rehabilitation program for teen-agers in New York called Encounter. He also opened a short-lived gallery in SoHo called Fear of Art in the 1970's.
        Mr. Stacy is survived by his mother and stepfather, Sybil and Robert Claiborne of New York City; a stepsister, Amanda Claiborne of Cambridge, Mass., and a stepbrother, Sam Claiborne of New York City.

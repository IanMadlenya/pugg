        Dorothy Elizabeth Bliss, a retired curator of the American Museum of Natural History, died of cancer last Saturday at Rhode Island Hospital in Providence. She was 71 years old and lived in Wakefield, R.I.
        Miss Bliss was named the museum's curator of invertebrates in 1967, and from 1974 to 1977 she served as chairman of the its department of fossils and living invertebrates.
        She was a research assistant professor at the Albert Einstein College of Medicine in the Bronx and adjunct professor of biology at CUNY. Since 1980, she had been adjunct professor of zoology at the University of Rhode Island.
        From 1957 to 1978, Dr. Bliss's research on land crabs was supported by the National Science Foundation. She was editor in chief of the 10-volume work ''The Biology of Crustaceans.''
        Miss Bliss was born in Cranston, R.I., and graduated from Brown University, where she also earned a master's degree. In 1952, she earned a doctorate from Harvard University, where she was a research fellow in biology.
        She joined the Museum of Natural History in 1956 and was appointed curator emeritus in 1980.
        She was president of the American Society of Zoologists and a fellow of the American Association for the Advancement of Science.
        She is survived by a brother, Frank, of Cranston.

        Thomas C. Simons, an insurance and financial executive who was chairman of the Capital Holding Corporation, died of cancer of the esophagus on Wednesday in Louisville, Ky., where he lived. He was 59 years old.
        Mr. Simons, a graduate of Harvard, began his business career with the Connecticut General Life Insurance Company, eventually become an executive vice president. He was named chairman, president and chief executive officer of Capital Holding in 1978, presiding over the Louisville concern as its assets increased from $2.3 billion to $11 billion.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Jean Gardiner; two sons; Thomas S. Simons of Andover, Mass., and John C. Simons of Manhattan; two daughters, Sarah Rivers of Los Angeles and Martha Dale of Bloomfield, Conn.; two sisters, Grace Torrey of Rancho Santa Fe, Calif. and Anne Cobb of Dayton, Ohio, and six grandchildren.

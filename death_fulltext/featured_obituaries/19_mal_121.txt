        Dr. Gordon Pollock, an early advocate of prophylactic dentistry and a founder of the New York Institute of Clinical Oral Pathology, died of a heart attack Saturday at the Norwalk Hospital in Connecticut. He was 91 years old and lived in New Canaan.
        For more than 50 years, Dr. Pollock practiced in Manhattan. He was born in Cincinnati and graduated from the University of Pennsylvania School of Dentistry.
        He is survived by his wife, Tatiana; a son, Nicholas, of Fairfield, Conn.; a daughter, Elizabeth Harrison of Chappaqua, N.Y., and a grandson.

        Gordon Crawford Kinney, a retired senior vice president of the Advertising Council Inc., died of a heart attack Saturday at his home in White Plains. He was 70 years old.
        A 1941 graduate of Marshall College, which is now Marshall University, in Huntington, W. Va., Mr. Kinney was a radio announcer in West Virginia and deputy of the Domestic Bureau of War Information in Washington before joining the Advertising Council in 1946. He was involved in the council's public service campaigns to promote the sale of United States Savings Bonds, to combat paralytic polio and drug abuse, and to spearhead campaigns against drunk driving and to ''Keep America Beautiful.''
        He is survived by his wife, Mary; five daughters, Sheila, of Hoboken, N.J., Nancy, of Northampton, Mass., Lynn Sunderland of Austin, Tex., Lesley Logan of Forest Hills, Queens and Sarah, of White Plains, and two granddaughters.

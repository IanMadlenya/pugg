
        Woodbridge Strong, a former law partner in the family practice of Strong & Strong in New Brunswick, N.J., died on June 21 at the Francis E. Parker Memorial Home in Piscataway, N.J. He was 87 and had previously lived in Highland Park, N.J., and Lenox, Mass.
        Mr. Strong was a son of State Senator Theodore Strong and Cornelia L. Van Rensselaer Strong. A 1932 graduate of Harvard University, he was admitted to the New Jersey bar in 1940 and served during World War II with the 11th Armored Division in the Battle of the Bulge and other campaigns in Europe.
        At one time, he was one of six brothers practicing as Strong & Strong, which was founded in 1852. He retired in 1988, but the firm is living on in New Brunswick as John VR. Strong Jr., Esq., with a nephew as the sole practitioner.
        Mr. Strong was a member of the Descendants of the Signers of the Constitution and other societies rooted in American and New Jersey history. He was a founding trustee of the Rutgers Preparatory School.
        He is survived by his wife, Julia DeViry Strong, also of Piscataway; four sons, Alan and Harmon, of Lenox, Timothy, of Marlboro, Mass., and Benjamin, of Montgomery Township, N.J.; two brothers, Robert, of Westwood, Mass., and Philip, of Cranbury, N.J.; five grandchildren, and three great-grandchildren.

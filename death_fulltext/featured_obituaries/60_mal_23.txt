
        John L. Schimel, a noted psychoanalyst, died on Tuesday at New York Hospital in Manhattan. He lived in Manhattan and had a second home in Truro, Mass.
        Dr. Schimel, who was 75 years old, died of cancer, his family said.
        Dr. Schimel, who practiced for 50 years, was a past president of the American Academy of Psychoanalysis and also of the American Society of Adolescent Psychiatry.
        He held several pivotal posts at the William Alanson White Institute of Psychiatry, Psychoanalysis and Psychology, a leading institute in its field. He was its associate director for the past 25 years and also served variously as director of clinical services, newsletter editor and president of the affiliated White Society.
        He was also a clinical professor in the Psychiatry Department at New York University's Medical School, where he specialized in adolescent psychiatry. He wrote numerous articles and books, including a recent series of articles on the use of humor in treating adolescents.
        Born in East Orange, N.J., he grew up in Jersey City. He earned his bachelor's degree from St. Peter's College and medical degree from Georgetown University Medical School. He was certified in psychiatry, child psychiatry, neurology and psychoanalysis. During World War II, he served in the United States Army as a captain in the Medical Corps.
        Suriving are his wife, the former Phyllis Leffler; two sons, David, of Boulder, Colo., and Joshua, of Fairbanks, Alaska; a daughter, Elizabeth, of Manhattan, and a granddaughter.

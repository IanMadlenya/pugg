        Arthur Fredrick Jones Jr., a writer and editor, died of pneumonia Sunday at the Hillcrest (N.Y.) Nursing Home. He was 85 years old and lived in Pomona, N.Y.
        He was born in Brooklyn and began writing about dogs in the 1930's. He was editor of the American Kennel Club's publication Pure-Bred Dogs from 1942 to 1965, when he retired. He was a consulting editor for the National Georgraphic Society's ''Book of Dogs.'' He also wrote and edited other books.
        He is survived by his son, Arthur 3d., of Pomona; a daughter, Margaret Downing of Point Lookout, L.I., and five grandchildren.

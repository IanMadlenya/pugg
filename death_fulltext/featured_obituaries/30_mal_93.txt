        Alfred Parker, retired executive director of the Tax Foundation, a research and education group in Washington, died of a heart attack on Saturday at his home in Larchmont, N.Y. He was 81 years old.
        Mr. Parker was a founder of the organization in 1937 and became executive director in 1962. He retired in 1974, but continued as an adviser until his death. From 1965 to his death, he was on the board of the Philharmonic Symphony of Westchester.
        Mr. Parker was born in Manhattan. He graduated from DeWitt Clinton High School and Columbia University.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Lydia Ayars; a sister, Ethel Pushkoff of Manhattan; a daughter, Salli, of South Lake Tahoe, Calif.; a son, Ted, of Chevy Chase, Md., and three grandchildren.

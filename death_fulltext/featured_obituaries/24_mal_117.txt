        James Wayne Cooper, a retired lawyer and former president of the Connecticut Bar Association, died of cardiac arrest Monday at his home in Woodbridge, Conn. He was 84 years old.
        Mr. Cooper graduated from Yale Law School in 1929 and served as clerk to Judges Learned Hand and Thomas W. Swan of the United States Court of Appeals for the Second Circuit. He retired in 1974 as senior partner of Tyler, Cooper & Alcorn of New Haven.
        He was president of the Connecticut Bar in 1957 and 1958 and of the Connecticut Bar Foundation from 1973 to 1975. He was active in many community organizations, serving as counsel to the Anna Fuller Fund, a cancer research foundation.
        He is survived by his wife, Louise; a daughter, Field McIntyre of London; two sons, James, of Cambridge, Mass., and Peter, of Bethany, Conn.; eight grandchildren, and five great-grandchildren.

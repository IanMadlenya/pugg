        Abner W. Berry, a former editor of the Harlem edition of The Daily Worker, died of complications of a stroke Wednesday at the Nash General Hospital in Rocky Mount, N.C. He was 85 years old and lived in Rocky Mount.
        Mr. Berry, originally from Texas, became an organizer for the Communist Party in Harlem in 1934. He joined The Daily Worker in 1942 and left in the late 1950's.
        Mr. Berry served in the United States Army during World War II.
        Mr. Berry was a former resident of West Orange, N.J.
        He is survived by a son, Peter, of Manhattan; a daughter, Melinda, of Orange, N.J.; a brother, Warren, of New York City, and a grandchild.

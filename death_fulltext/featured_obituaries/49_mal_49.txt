
        James N. Lanzetti, a haberdashery buyer and a vice president of merchandising and design at Bidermann Industries in Manhattan, died on Tuesday at his home in Manhattan. He was 45 years old.
        He died of pancreatolysis, his companion, Albert Ciccarelli, said.
        After graduating from Quinnipiac College in Hamden, Conn., Mr. Lanzetti began as a sportswear buyer in Boston in 1973 and later moved to New York and was a buyer for Gimbels and for the May Merchandising Corporation. In 1977 he became a sportswear stylist for the Van Heusen Company and in 1982 he became a vice president at Daniel Hechter Menswear, a Bidermann Industries subsidiary. In 1985 he was named a vice president and was in charge of designer shirts and ties.
        He is survived by his mother, Alice Lanzetti of New Haven.

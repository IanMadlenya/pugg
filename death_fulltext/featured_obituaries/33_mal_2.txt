        Stephen P. King, an architect, died of cancer Friday at his home n East Hampton, L.I. He was 44 years old and also lived in Manhattan.
        At his death, Mr. King was in charge of architectural design for the firm of Swanke, Hayden, Connell Architects in Manhattan. Previously, Mr. King headed the Boston office of Skidmore, Owings & Merrill. He had also worked for I. M. Pei & Partners in Manhattan.
        Born in Rochester, he earned his architecture degree from North Carolina State University.
        He is survived by his wife, Catherine; a daughter, Valerie, and a son, Justin; and his parents, Lily and Richard King of Washington, N.C.

        Mary Ground, an elder of the Blackfeet Indian tribe, died Wednesday. She was 107 years old.
        In her teens, she was matched to marry a stranger, John Ground. They were married on Aug. 12, 1899; he died in 1951.
        The Grounds were among the Blackfeet who worked for the Great Northern Railway and greeted tourists at Glacier National Park in the summers.
        Mrs. Ground also was a midwife on the Blackfeet reservation.
        Survivors include two sons and two daughters, all of Browning.

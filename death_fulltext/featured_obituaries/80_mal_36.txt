
        H. Bart McHugh 3d, an expert on national radio and television programming, died on Wednesday at his home in Tinton Falls, N.J. He was 60.
        His family said he had had a brief illness.
        At his death Mr. McHugh was senior vice president and associate director of the National TV-Radio group at the DDB Needham Worldwide advertising agency.
        He was a graduate of Georgetown University and served as a lieutenant in the United States Army.
        He began his business career as a talent agent in 1957 with the General Artists Corporation, where he represented such stars as Perry Como and Carol Burnett. He joined the Doyle Dane Berbach advertising agency in 1965 and left there in 1972 to produce and syndicate television programs.
        He then joined the Wells Rich Greene advertising agency as senior vice president for communications and returned later to Doyle Dane Bernbach, where he was responsible for cable television. The agency later became DDB Needham Worldwide.
        He was a member of the Broadcast Network and Programming Committee of the American Association of Advertising Agencies and the National Academy of Cable Programming.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Joan Daugherty; two sons, H. Bart McHugh 4th and James; four daughters, Marianne Tiley, Diane Davis, Barbara Swintik and Patricia McHugh, and eight grandchildren.

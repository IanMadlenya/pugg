        John G. Benedict, an editor and director of W. W. Norton & Company, died on Monday at his home in Brooklyn. He was 57 years old.
        His family said he died of cancer of the esophagus.
        Mr. Benedict served as poetry editor at Norton, which he joined in 1960. He edited works by A. R. Ammons and Adrienne Rich, both of whom won National Book Awards. He was also the principal editor of the Norton anthologies, literary collections that are widely used in colleges and universities. His first anthology, ''The Norton Anthology of English Literature,'' became the most successful book in Norton's 67-year history; now in its fifth edition, it has sold more than four million copies.
        Mr. Benedict subsequently edited Norton anthologies in modern poetry, American literature and women's literature. At his death he was developing an anthology of black American literature.
        Mr. Benedict was born in Cambridge, Mass. He graduated from Harvard University and did graduate work at Stanford University.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Lois Ann Sembower, of Brooklyn; his mother, Katherine Henry Benedict of Concord, Mass.; a sister, Joan B. Annese of Boston; two sons, Sam John Benedict of Brooklyn and Peter Benedict of New York City, and a daughter, Miranda Benedict of New York City.

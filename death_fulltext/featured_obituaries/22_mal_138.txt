        Dr. Harold W. Katz, a business executive and electrical engineer, died of cancer yesterday at United Hospital in Port Chester, N.Y. He was 65 years old and lived in Rye, N.Y.
        Dr. Katz was vice president of Stern Telecommunications in New York City from 1981 to 1986. Previously, he held several positions in Ann Arbor, Mich., as market developer for Interactive Systems; president of Vicom Industries Inc., for whom he invented one of the first interactive cable television systems; director of electronic products for KMS Industries, and director of research for the Tecumseh Products Company. From 1952 to 1964, he worked in the General Electric electronics laboratory in Syracuse. He received a Ph.D. in electrical engineering from the University of Illinois in 1952.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Lee Pankler; a son, Stuart, of Manhattan; a daughter, Rhonda Barnat of Ann Arbor; two sisters, Leah Waitz of Tappan, N.Y., and Eileen Kutikoff of Brooklyn and Rhinebeck, N.Y., and four grandchildren.

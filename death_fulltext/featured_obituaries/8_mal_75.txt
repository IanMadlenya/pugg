        Harold J. Reilly, a physical therapist, died in his sleep Sunday at his home in Oak Ridge, N.J. He was 92 years old.
        Mr. Reilly, who was born in Manhattan, founded the Reilly Institute, a center for physical therapy at Rockefeller Center, in 1935. He closed the center in 1965, when he retired.
        Mr. Reilly was the author of three books, including ''The Edgar Cayce Handbook for Health Through Drugless Therapy,'' published in 1975.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Betty G. Billings; a daughter, Lucille Kornylak of Hamilton, Ohio; a son, William, of Westwood, N.J.; four grandchildren and seven great-grandchildren.

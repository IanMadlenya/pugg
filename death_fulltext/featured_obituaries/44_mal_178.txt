        Prescott D. Schutz, an art dealer who specialized in 19th- and 20th-century American art, died yesterday at Columbia-Presbyterian Medical Center. He was 42 years old and lived in Manhattan. His family said he died of complications from AIDS. Mr. Schutz and his father, Herbert D. Schutz, were partners in Schutz & Co., which they founded in 1983. He had previously been a director of the Hirschl & Adler Gallery. He was a graduate of Columbia College and the Sorbonne. He is survived by his father and his stepmother, Suzanne, of Greenwich, Conn.; a sister, Elizabeth Lydon of Manhattan; two brothers, Peter, of Dillon, Colo., and Jeffrey, of Denver, and two half-sisters, Leslie and Suzanne, of Greenwich.

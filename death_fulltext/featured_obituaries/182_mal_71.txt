
        Dr. John M. Eisenberg, a former medical educator who led the federal agency that monitors quality and safety in health care and research, died on March 10 at his home in Potomac, Md. He was 55.
        The cause was a brain tumor, said Tommy G. Thompson, secretary of health and human services.
        Dr. Eisenberg was appointed director of the Agency for Healthcare Research and Quality in 1997. He had been chairman of the department of medicine and physician in chief at Georgetown University.
        Born in Atlanta, John Meyer Eisenberg graduated magna cum laude from Princeton University in 1968. He received his medical degree in 1972 at the Washington University School of Medicine in St. Louis and an M.B.A. from the Wharton School four years later.
        He was a founder and former chairman of the Congressional Physician Payment Review Commission. Before moving to Georgetown in 1990, he was associated with the University of Pennsylvania, where he founded the department of general internal medicine.
        Dr. Eisenberg is survived by his wife of 32 years, DD Rudner Eisenberg; two sons, William R., of Philadelphia, and Michael R., of Potomac; his mother, Roslyn E. Karesh of Charleston, S.C.; and three brothers, Richard M., of Arlington, Ill., William C., of Memphis, and Jeff D., of Bedford, N.H.

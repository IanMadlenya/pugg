        Leonard F. Shepard, an engineer and entrepreneur who helped develop space suits and other technology used in the United States space program, died of cancer Saturday at University Hospital in Stony Brook, L.I. He was 61 years old and lived in Lake Grove, L.I.
        Mr. Shepard was head of Time System Technology, a company he founded in 1980 to develop precision instruments. Before that, he spent 29 years with the International Latex Corporation and its successor, ILC Industries, where he was director of research and technology.
        In 1970 Mr. Shepard managed a group that won the development contract from the National Aeronautics and Space Administration for the suit to be worn by the Apollo astronauts during space walks and while on the moon.
        Mr. Shepard was born in New York City and received a degree in electrical engineering from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology. He also studied music and became a concert pianist, appearing with the New York City Symphony while in his early 20's.
        Survivors include his wife, Doris; three daughters, Barbara Ridgely of Camden, Del., Lisa Shepard of Nutley, N.J., and Ann Shepard of Lake Grove; a brother, Martin, of Westlake Village, Calif., and two grandsons.

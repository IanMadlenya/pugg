        Dr. George Theodore Golding, a urologist in Queens for almost 50 years, died of an aneurism on Tuesday at his home in Boca Raton, Fla. He was 83 years old.
        Dr. Golding was a founding partner of Parkway Hospital in Forest Hills, Queens, and was in private practice in Forest Hills and Kew Gardens until he retired to Florida in 1981. He was a 1932 graduate of Rush College of Medicine at the University of Chicago and was among the pioneers in the use of renal dialysis. He was also an attending physician at La Guardia Hospital in Forest Hills and was a member of many medical societies.
        He is survived by his wife, Roslyn; two sons, Andrew, of Billings, Mont., and Mark, of Portland, Ore., and two grandchildren.

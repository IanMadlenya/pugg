
        John Kupchun, the president of the Nashua Industrial Machine Company in Nashua, N.H., died on Thursday in the USAir plane crash. He was 52 and lived in Merrimack, N.H.
        Mr. Kupchun got a job as a draftsman at Nashua Industrial after graduating from Nashua High School, and he had been working his way up ever since. He was named president of the company in July.
        Last week, he went to Chicago for a machine tool show with Larry Grondin, who also worked for Nashua Industrial.
        Mr. Kupchun is survived by his wife, Phyllis; two daughters, Paula and Marianne, and a son, Stephen, of Dania, Fla.

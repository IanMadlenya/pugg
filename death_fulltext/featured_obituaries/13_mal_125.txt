        Clinton B. F. Brill, a civil engineer, architect and former chairman of the New York State Thruway Authority, died of a heart attack Feb. 9 at his home in Tallahassee, Fla. He was 94 years old.
        Mr. Brill was chairman of the authority from 1957 to 1960, and also served in a broader capacity overseeing state construction projects for Governors W. Averell Harriman and Nelson A. Rockefeller. Before and after his state service he established, alone or with partners, architectural and engineering companies that engaged in a variety of design work on public and private projects, including the Triborough Bridge, the 1939-40 New York World's Fair and Idlewild Airport, now Kennedy International Airport.
        Mr. Brill, who was born in Mount Union, Pa., attended Bloomsburg State Teachers College, Trinity College and the Massachusetts Institute of Technology, where he was trained in architecture and engineering.
        During World War II, he managed construction projects in France, Belgium, the Netherlands and Germany. He was discharged with the rank of colonel.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Elizabeth Klapp; two stepchildren, Margaret Olwell of Sausalito, Calif., and Oliver Scholle of Hillsborough, Calif.; six step-grandchildren and one step-great-grandchild.

        Andrew E. Ullmann, a lawyer and retired chairman of the Stony Brook Council, a policy-making body of the State University of New York at Stony Brook, died of cancer Tuesday at Huntington (L.I.) Hospital. He was 63 years old and lived in Cold Spring Harbor, L.I.
        Mr. Ullmann, a 1950 graduate of Brooklyn Law School, was counsel to the Anchor Savings Bank and had been active in Democratic politics in Northport, L.I. He was appointed to the Stony Brook Council in 1976 by Gov. Hugh L. Carey. He resigned in September. The council oversees student and faculty activities at the university.
        He is survived by his wife, Dolores; three children, Richard, of Washington, and Susan and Roger, both of Hilton Head, S.C.; three stepchildren, David Murolo of East Northport, L.I., Joseph Murolo of Bayshore, L.I., and Cara Murolo of Huntington, L.I.; a sister, Jane Goldblatt of East Northport; a brother, Anthony, of Hastings-on-Hudson, N.Y., and five stepgrandchildren.

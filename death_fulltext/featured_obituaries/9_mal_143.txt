        Edward F. Blettner, a retired vice chairman and director of the First National Bank of Chicago and its parent, the First Chicago Corporation, died of heart failure last Friday at his home in Chicago. He was 79 years old.
        Mr. Blettner retired in 1973 as the bank's vice chairman and deputy chief executive officer after a career of 43 years, but remained an honorary director until 1978.
        He was born in Chicago and graduated from Harvard University, where he also earned a master's degree in business administration. He later earned a degree from John Marshall Law School in Chicago.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Beverly Dowis; two daughters, Margaret Jean Angell of New York and Elizabeth Blettner of Annapolis, Md., and three grandchildren.

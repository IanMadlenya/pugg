        Don Bessent, a pitcher who was one of the Boys of Summer of the Brooklyn Dodgers, died of alcohol poisoning on Saturday. He was 59 years old.
        Mr. Bessent was drafted by the Yankees in 1949. He played for the Dodgers from 1955 to 1958, moving with them to Los Angeles in his last year in the major leagues. He finished his career with a 14-7 record, 3.33 earned-run average and 12 saves.
        Mr. Bessent was best remembered for his World Series performances, pitching against the Yankees in five series games in 1955 and 1956. He was 1-0 with a 1.35 e.r.a. in 13Y innings.

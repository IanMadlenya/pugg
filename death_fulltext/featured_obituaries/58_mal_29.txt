
        Harold E. Leaf, a retired investment banker, died Friday at the Ruth Taylor Institute, a geriatric center in Hawthorne, N.Y. A resident of Scarsdale, he was 82 years old.
        A native of New Rochelle, N.Y., Mr. Leaf went to work in 1929 as a telephone clerk on the floor of the New York Stock Exchange. He left in 1937 to become a founding partner in R. D. White & Company, investment bankers in New York. He retired in 1975 as the company's managing partner.
        He was a past president of St. Andrews Golf Club in Hastings-on-Hudson, N.Y.
        Mr. Leaf is survived by four sons, Aurell of Palm Beach, Fla.; Robert of Scotts Valley, Calif.; John of Scarsdale, and Roger of Manhattan; a daughter, Mary Gillette of Mound, Minn., and six grandchildren.

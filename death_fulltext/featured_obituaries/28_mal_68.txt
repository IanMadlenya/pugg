        Herman W. Bevis, who was a senior partner and chief executive officer of Price Waterhouse, the accounting firm, when he retired in 1969, died of a stroke last Friday at his home in Colorado Springs. He was 80 years old and had lived for many years in Greenwich, Conn., before moving to Colorado last November.
        Mr. Bevis was born in Waterloo, Ala., and raised in Memphis. He was a graduate of Southwestern College, in Memphis, and of the Harvard Business School. He spent his entire business career with Price Waterhouse, which he joined in 1933.
        From 1970 to 1975 Mr. Bevis was executive director of the Banking and Securities Industries Committee, a private panel of executives from different companies which sought to solve paperwork problems in the industry. He was an advocate of doing away with stock certificates held by individual shareholders.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Jennie Wade; two sons, Richard, of Vancouver, B.C., and William, of Missoula, Mont; a daughter, Ginger Littleton of Colorado Springs, and four granddaughters.

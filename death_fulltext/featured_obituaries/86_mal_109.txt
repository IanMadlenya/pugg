
        Richard L. Harris, an amateur sportsman and retired shipping executive, died last Saturday at his home in Southampton, L.I. He was 74.
        The cause was heart failure, said his wife, Jean.
        Mr. Harris was with United States Lines for 27 years, retiring as vice president in 1972. He was a past president of the Shinnecock Hills Golf Club and the Downtown Athletic Club, as well as chairman of its Heisman Award Committee.
        He graduated from Georgetown University. During World War II, he was a major in the Army and was awarded the Distinguished Service Cross and the Silver Star for bravery in the campaigns in North Africa and Sicily.
        Mr. Harris is survived by his wife; a son, Richard Jr., of San Francisco; a daughter, Megan Davis, of Bedford, N.Y., and five grandchildren.


        Gregory J. Andrews, the president and chief executive of Carson Inc., which makes hair and beauty products for African-Americans, died on Sunday in Johannesburg during a business trip. Mr. Andrews, a resident of Savannah, Ga., was 47.
        The cause of death has not been determined, said Leonea Andrews, his mother.
        Before joining Carson in July, Mr. Andrews worked for several health care and consumer products companies.
        In 1992, Mr. Andrews was named marketing director for Colgate-Palmolive Puerto Rico. He was promoted to general manager, the top position at the subsidiary, in 1994, and in 1997, he was named general manager of Colgate-Palmolive Argentina in Buenos Aires.
        Mr. Andrews graduated from the University of Connecticut with a bachelor's degree in industrial management in 1974, and he received an M.B.A. from the University of Wisconsin in 1975.
        In addition to his mother, who lives in Washington Parish, La., Mr. Andrews is survived by a daughter, Azania, of Palo Alto, Calif., and two sons, Jovar, of New York, and Gregory Jr., of Miami. He and his wife, Denise, of Miami, were divorced in 1996.


        Stephen F. Keating, the president of Honeywell from 1965 to 1974, died on Monday at his home in Wayzata, Minn. He was 83.
        The cause was a heart ailment, The Associated Press reported.
        Mr. Keating was the sixth president of Honeywell, a computer business, which was then based in Minneapolis. Mr. Keating was its chairman from 1974 to 1978 and vice chairman from 1978 to 1981, when he retired after three decades with Honeywell. The company, now Honeywell International, is based in New Jersey.
        Born in Graceville, Minn., Mr. Keating received bachelor's and law degrees from the University of Minnesota. After serving briefly as an agent with the Federal Bureau of Investigation and practicing law in St. Paul, he joined Honeywell.
        Mr. Keating is survived by his wife, Mary; two daughters, Elizabeth Schroeder, of Baltimore and Mary Keating, of Wayzata; two sons, Stephen D., of Long Lake, Minn., and Tom, of Manhattan; and seven grandchildren.

        Gordon E. Hyde, an advertising executive for half a century and a former president of the Grand Jury Association of New York County, died of pneumonia on Saturday at his home in North Salem, N.Y. He was 92 years old.
        Mr. Hyde, who was born in St. Paul and was a graduate of the University of Minnesota, was an Army pilot in World War I. He entered the advertising business in 1923 as a solicitor for Curtis Publishing and later worked with N. W. Ayer and McCann-Erickson before becoming president of Federal Advertising in 1945. He was named a vice president of D'Arcy Advertising in 1952 when Federal was merged into D'Arcy. He retired in 1973. In 1963 Mr. Hyde was elected to head the Grand Jury Association, an advocacy group composed of people who served on grand juries.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Louise Brand; two daughters, Barbara Crafford of Santa Cruz, Calif., and Happy Mauck of Farmington, Conn.; a son, Donald, of Devon, Pa.; nine grandchildren, and eight great-grandchildren.


        Richard P. Saunders, an educator and sociologist who headed two colleges and three welfare organizatons, died on Tuesday at a nursing home in Chittenango, N.Y. He was 89 years old and lived in Fayetteville, N.Y.
        He died of congestive heart failure, said his daughter, Faith Ball of Manlius, N.Y.
        Dr. Saunders was active in organizations involved in improving the quality of children's lives, particularly in American Indian and Latin American communities.
        He was the founder and first president of Mitchell College in New London, Conn., in 1938; of Palos Verdes College in California, in 1946; of Futures for Children, in Washington, in 1960, and of its sister organization, Futuro para la Ninez in Medellin, Colombia, in 1962. In 1974, Dr. Saunders moved the national headquarters of Futures for Children to Albuquerque, N.M., where he and his wife lived until he retired in 1981.  Bush Honors Group
        This year, Futures for Children was identified by President Bush as one of his "Points of Light" programs, in recognition of the voluntary aspects of its work. In 1986, Dr. Saunders was given Colombia's highest award, the Cross of Boyaca.
        He was a former president of the Save the Children Federation, an international child welfare organization, and from 1961 to 1967 he was associate executive secretary of the Association for Higher Education, an affiliate of the National Education Association.
        Dr. Saunders was born in Somerville, Mass. He received a B.S., an M.A. and a Ph.D. in higher education from New York University, where he began his career as an administrative assistant.
        His wife, the former Dorothy Matthew Olton, died in 1988.
        In addition to his daughter, he is survived by two sisters, Marion Freeman of Natick, Mass., and Helen Churchill of Wellesley, Mass.; and two grandchildren.

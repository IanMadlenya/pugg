
        Neal Steinman, a lawyer and expert in public finance, died on Thursday in a Philadelphia hospital. He was 50 and lived in Philadelphia.
        David F. Girard-diCarlo, a colleague, said the cause of death was cancer.
        Mr. Steinman was a partner in the Philadelphia law firm of Blank, Rome, Comisky & McCauley, and was a nationally known authority on issues of public finance.
        Mr. Steinman served on the National Finance Council of the Clinton-Gore campaign. He was recently elected president of the Yale Law School Association, a position he was to have assumed in October 1993.
        In his 20 years of work for Blank, Rome, Mr. Steinman structured numerous bond issues, advising state and local governments in Pennsylvania, New Jersey, and Delaware on public finance transactions.
        He also served as a special consultant to the Anthony Commission on Public Finance for the Ways and Mean Committee of the United States House of Representatives. He advised several New Jersey and Pennsylvania legislative committees on public finance.
        Born in Philadelphia in 1942, Mr. Steinman graduated from the Pennsylvania State University in 1964. He earned a master's degree from Temple University in 1968 and a law degree from Yale Law School in 1971.
        He was a member of numerous legal associations, and was an active member of the American Bar Association.
        He is survived by his wife, Barbara; a daughter, Jordana; his mother, Beatrice Steinman of Philadelphia, and two sisters, Shirley Schwartz, who lives in the Washington area, and Judith Steinman-Smith of Cherry Hill, N.J.

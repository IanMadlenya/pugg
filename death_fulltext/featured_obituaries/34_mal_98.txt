        Julian D. Rosenberg, a retired lawyer and a former officer in the Central Intelligence Agency, died of heart failure Monday at Middlesex Memorial Hospital in Middletown, Conn. He was 92 years old and lived in East Haddam, Conn.
        Born in New York City, Mr. Rosenberg graduated from the Columbia University Law School and was in private practice in Manhattan. He served in the Navy in World War I and World War II and was a lieutenant commander in naval intelligence. He worked for the C.I.A. and the State Department from 1946 to 1953.
        Mr. Rosenberg was the founder of the Connecticut Valley Action Committee Inc., which challenged Federal agencies planning development in the valley. He was also a founder of the Connecticut River Gateway Commission.
        He is survived by his daughter, Leone Mendenhall of Washington, four granddaughters and three great-grandchildren.

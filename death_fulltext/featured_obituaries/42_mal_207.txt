        Max Kamiel, a retired certified public accountant, died yesterday at Mount Sinai Medical Center. He was 85 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        His family said he died of heart failure.
        Mr. Kamiel was born in Panama and was a graduate of Pace University and the University of Santiago in Chile. He was with the accounting firm of Kandel, Schaeffer & Company in New York City and served for many years on the New York State Advisory Council on Mental Health.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Claire Laub; four daughters, Rosalind Rosenblum of Miami, Sylvia Rolnick of Great Neck, L.I., Harriet Levine of Miami Beach, and Lillian Fabrikant of Manhattan; nine grandchildren, and six great-grandchildren.

        Victor Thane Norton, retired chief executive and chairman of the board of the Amerace Corporation, died of congestive heart failure Saturday at his summer home in Riverside, Conn. He was 86 years old and also lived in Lighthouse Point, Fla.
        Mr. Norton joined Amerace, manufacturers of hard rubber products, in 1957 and retired in 1968. He had been vice president of NBC from 1950 to 1953, and was president of American Home Foods Inc. from 1946 to 1950. He was a merchandising consultant to Kenyon & Eckhardt advertising agency in Manhattan and a member of the agency's plans board.
        He was born in Ridgway, Pa., and graduated from Northwestern Univesity with a degree in civil engineering in 1926.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Elizabeth Ravenscroft Smith; a son, Victor Thane Jr., of Bowling Green, Ohio; two daughters, Nina Smith of Stony Creek, Conn., and Elizabeth Patterson of St. Louis; a brother, Ralph, of Wooster, Ohio; six grandchildren, and a great-grandchild.


        Bernard P. Stengren, a retired public information official of the Consolidated Edison Company in New York, died yesterday at Central General Hospital, Plainview, L.I. He was 68.
        The cause was cancer, said his wife, Elizabeth.
        Mr. Stengren, who was known as Bud, became a public relations officer for the utility in 1965. He served as director of public information from 1978 to 1980 and retired in 1989 as an assistant to the senior vice president for public affairs.
        For many years Mr. Stengren was the secretary and guiding force of the Silurians, a society of newspaper reporters and editors in New York.
        Mr. Stengren was discharged from the United States Army in 1946. Before going to Con Ed, he had spent 14 years as a reporter for The New York Times.
        He is survived by his wife, Elizabeth, of Huntington Station, L.I.; five daughters, Mary Giardina of Hicksville, L.I., Patricia D'Ambrisi of Simi Valley, Calif., Kathy Ellis of Bayport, L.I., Maureen Iacono of Syracuse, Peggy Graeve of Hampton Bays, L.I.; a son, Bernard Stengren Jr., of Babylon, L.I.,; a sister, Virginia Grealish of Lafayette, Calif., a brother, George Stengren of Mount Pleasant, Mich., and nine grandchildren.

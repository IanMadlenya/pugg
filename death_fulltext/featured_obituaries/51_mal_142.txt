
        Philip H. Steckler Jr., a New York stockbroker, died on Wednesday at New York Hospital. He was 72 years old and lived in Purchase, N.Y., and Wilmington, Vt.
        Mr. Steckler died after a series of operations for cancer, a spokesman for the family said.
        He was a senior partner in the brokerage firm of Neuberger & Berman and managed its partners fund for many years. He was a member of the board of the Purchase College Foundation at the State University of New York. A former nationally ranked tennis player, he was instrumental in promoting tennis for New York City youth.
        A native New Yorker, he was a graduate of Yale University and served as a lieutenant in the Army Air Forces in World War II.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Lois Riegelman; three sons, Philip H. 3d, of Brattleboro, Vt., and Donald and Alan, both of Old Greenwich, Conn.

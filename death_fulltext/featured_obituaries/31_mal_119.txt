        Irving E. Hankin, a commodities economist, died of lung cancer Saturday at his home in Fort Lauderdale, Fla. He was 79 years old and was a longtime resident of Syosset, L.I.
        Mr. Hankin served as assistant to the commissioner of the Commodity Futures Trading Commission in Washington from 1977 to 1981, when he retired. Before that, he managed the commodity research department at Merrill Lynch, Pierce, Fenner & Smith for 27 years.
        Born in Philadelphia, Mr. Hankin graduated from Brooklyn College. He is a former president of the Commodity Club of New York and a former Democratic county committeeman from Brooklyn.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Florence Kramer; a daughter, Barbara Pashe of Fort Lauderdale; a son, Alan of Dartmouth, Mass.; two sisters, Lillian Bleifer and Annette Welles, and a brother, Nathan, all of Los Angeles; four grandchildren and two great-grandchildren.

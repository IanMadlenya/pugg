
        I. Donald Terner is a former director of Housing and Community Development for the State of California and a developer of low-income housing in San Francisco. He is 56 and lives in San Francisco.
        Mr. Terner is president and founder of the Bridge Housing Corporation, a 12-year-old not-for profit organization. Operating in the housing market of the  San Francisco Bay area, Bridge has developed more than 6,000 new homes.
        A native of New Jersey, he had also been active in the 1970's in New York City's low-income housing market, where he founded and served as executive director of the Urban Homesteading Assistance Board, a group that helps low income rental tenants convert their buildings to cooperatives.

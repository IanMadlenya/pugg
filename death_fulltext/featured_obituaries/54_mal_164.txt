
        William Bryant Rawls, a retired New York internist who was active in many medical organizations, including the forerunner of Empire Blue Cross and Blue Shield, died on Wednesday at St. Francis Hospital in Poughkeepsie, N.Y. He was 93 years old and lived in Pleasant Valley, N.Y.
        He died of cancer, a spokesman for the family said.
        Dr. Rawls, who specialized in rheumatism, maintained a private practice in Manhattan from 1926 to 1979, when he retired. He was president of the medical staff and chairman of the executive committees of Polyclinic and St. Clare's Hospitals.  Received Many Awards
        He was a former president and chairman of the board of trustees of the New York County Medical Society, of the American Therapeutic Society and of the Physicians Equity Association. He was also a former president of the New York Rheumatism Society.
        He was a founder of the United Medical Service in 1944, now Empire Blue Cross and Blue Shield and a founding member of the Arthritis Foundation in 1948. He was a member of the house of delegates of the American Medical Association for 18 years.
        He received a number of awards, including the distinguished service award of the American Academy of Compensation Medicine and of the Arthritis Foundation.
        He was born in Concord, Ga., and graduated from Mercer University in Macon. He received a medical degree from Emory University in Atlanta in 1922.
        He was founder in 1959 and president of Bilmar Nurseries, Inc., in Pleasant Valley, and founder in 1972 and president of William B. Rawls & Sons Developers Inc., of Pleasant Valley.
        His wife, the former Mary Palmer, died in 1987.
        He is survived by a son, William Bryant Jr. of Pleasant Valley, a daughter, Jan-Elizabeth Arnold of Haverhill, Mass.; a brother, Roger S. of Wiilliamson, Ga.; six grandchildren, and a great-granddaughter.

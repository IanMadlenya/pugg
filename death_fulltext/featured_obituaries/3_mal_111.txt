        Mathias Polakowitz, who under the name Paul Mathias was a foreign correspondent for Paris-Match magazine and who covered the 1956 revolt in his native country, Hungary, died of cancer on April 9 in Paris. He was 65 years old.
        Mr. Polakowitz emigrated to France with his family in 1948. He soon began covering special assignments for Paris-Match. In 1956, he returned to Budapest to cover the Hungarian uprising and the invasion of Soviet tanks to crush the revolt.
        Later, while stationed in New York City, he covered the funeral of President John F. Kennedy in 1963 and was on the train carrying the body of the President's brother, Robert, for burial in Arlington National Cemetery in 1968.
        Mr. Polakowitz left no survivors. A memorial service is being planned.

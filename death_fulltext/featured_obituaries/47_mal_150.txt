
        Hortenius Chenault, a dentist, died on Monday at his home in Hempstead, L.I. He was 80 years old.
        He died after a stroke, said his son Kenneth.
        Dr. Chenault practiced in Hempstead from 1939 until his retirement in 1987.
        He was born in Richmond, Ky., and graduated from Morehouse College and from Howard University's College of Dentistry. He received a Guggenheim Award and did postgraduate work in children's dentistry.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Anne Quick; three sons, Kenneth, of New Rochelle, Arthur, of Great Neck, L.I., and Stephan, of Brooklyn; a daughter, Patricia, of Hempstead; a brother, Arthur Chenault Jr. of Mount Healthy, Ohio, and a sister, Lydia Martin of Washington.

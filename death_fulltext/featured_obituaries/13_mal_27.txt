        Robert S. Sturtevant, co-founder and chairman of a Manhattan advertising agency, Sturtevant and Hildt, died Saturday at the Norwalk (Conn.) Hospital. He was 56 years old and lived in New Canaan, Conn.
        Mr. Sturtevant, who had previously worked as a writer for the J. Walter Thompson, Ogilvy & Mather and Doremous advertising agencies, founded his firm in 1986 with Robert E. Hildt.
        He is survived by his wife, Elizabeth; three sons, Matthew and Timothy, of Manhattan and David, of Reston, Va., and one grandchild.

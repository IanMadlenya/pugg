
        Jacob Fox, who founded the Newark law firm of Fox & Fox in 1920, died yesterday at St. Barnabas Medical Center in Livingston, N.J. He was 94 years old and lived in Verona, N.J.
        He died after a brief illness, his family said.
        At the time of his death, Mr. Fox was of counsel to the firm. A native of Newark, he graduated from New York University and the New York University School of Law.
        For many years, he was a liaison for Newark with the State Legislature in Trenton. He was also counsel to the Newark Board of Education from 1940 to 1968.
        Mr. Fox is survived by four sons, Martin S. of Milburn, N.J., Dr. Arthur C. of New York City, Richard H. of Livingston, and David L. of Roseland, N.J.; nine grandchildren, and nine great-grandchildren.

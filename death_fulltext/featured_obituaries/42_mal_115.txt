        Joseph P. Dougherty, a retired executive vice president of Capital Cities/ ABC, died on Saturday in Point Pleasant Hospital in Point Pleasant, N.J. He was 66 years old.
        He apparently died of a heart attack, a spokeswoman for the company said.
        Mr. Dougherty, who retired two years ago, was born in Philadelphia and lived in New York before moving to Sea Girt, N.J., in 1980. He graduated from St. Joseph's University in Philadelphia in 1948 and worked for Capital Cities for 29 years becoming president of its broadcasting division.
        Survivors include his wife, Mary, of Sea Girt; two sons, Peter, of Boston, and David, of Newtown, Conn., and five daughters, Geraldine Dempsey of New York City, Jody, of Boston, Elizabeth, of Los Angeles, Aimee, of Bryn Mawr, Pa., and Brighde Margaret, of Sea Girt.
        He also is survived by a stepson, Robert J. Debbs of Philadelphia; three stepdaughters, Tyler Debbs, Melissa Debbs and Hilary Debbs, all of Sea Girt; a brother, James, of Yardley, Mass., and two grandchildren.

        William J. McCarthy, president of Kroch's & Brentano, the oldest and largest bookstore chain in Chicago, died Tuesday of complications resulting from a liver transplant at the University of Chicago Medical Center. He was 61 years old and lived in Evanston.
        Mr. McCarthy, a native of Chicago, joined Kroch's as a salesman in 1952 and rose to the presidency in 1986.
        He is survived by his wife, Virginia, of Evanston; a daughter, Mary, of Chicago, and two sons, Patrick of Philadelphia, and William Jr. of Evanston.

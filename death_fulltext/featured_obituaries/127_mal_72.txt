
        Harris Gilbert, a New Jersey business executive and a longtime national leader with the Union of American Hebrew Congregations, died on Aug. 8 at Overlook Hospital in Summit, N.J. He was 74 and lived in Westfield, N.J.
        At his death, Mr. Gilbert was chairman of the National Outreach Commission of Reform Judaism. He was a member of the executive committee of the Union of American Hebrew Congregations and a past chairman of the Commission on Social Action of Reform Judaism.
        He was born in Newark and graduated from Pennsylvania State University in 1948. He spent much of his career with Alfax Industries of Manhattan, a distributor of school and office furniture. In 1982 he founded the Computech Financial Management Corporation of Westfield, an investment management firm. He was its president at his death.
        Mr. Gilbert is survived by his wife of 48 years, Sally Klein Gilbert; their son, Bruce, of Manhattan; a daughter, Carol Singer of Hastings-on-Hudson, N.Y., and five grandchildren.


        Finley P. Dunne Jr., a writer and editor who later became director of the Temple of Understanding , died Monday at George Washington Hospital in Washington. He was 87 years old and lived in Washington.
        He died of prostate cancer and pneumonia, his family said.
        After graduating from Harvard in 1925, Mr. Dunne joined The Boston Globe as a reporter, He later worked as an assistant drama editor for The World newspaper in New York and as an assistant managing editor for Vanity Fair magazine.
        In 1933 he moved to Hollywood, where he worked as a screenwriter on several films, including "Cleopatra," "Magnificent Obsession" and "Imitation of Life."
        During World War II he served in the Army Air Corps and received a Legion of Merit.
        He was a co-founder of the American Graduate School of International Management, also known as Thunderbird, in Glendale, Ariz., and for about 15 years, until his retirement in 1973, he served as executive director and then board chairman of the Temple of Understanding, an interfaith organization.
        A book of his verse, "Song of the Outer Banks," was published in 1989.
        His father was a political commentator who created the character of "Mr. Dooley."
        He is survived by his wife, the former Evelyn Johnson; a brother, Philip I. of Malibu, Calif., a screenwriter and director; a son, Dr. Peter B. Dunne of Tampa, Fla.; a daughter, Patricia Rosenblad of North Brunswick, N.J., and four grandchildren.

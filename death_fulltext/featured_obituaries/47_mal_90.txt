
        Dr. Claudio Gerbi, a physician who had a general practice in Manhattan for 37 years, died on Tuesday at his home in Manhattan. He was 82 years old.
        He died of cardiac arrest, his family said.
        Dr. Gerbi was born in Florence and practiced in Milan before coming to the United States in 1938. He then conducted research in hypertension at Case Western Reserve University in Cleveland, served as a district physician in Boston and moved to New York in 1942.
        Dr. Gerbi taught internal medicine at the College of Physicians and Surgeons of Columbia University. He retired in 1979.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Jeanette Klein; two daughters, Susan McIlwain of Providence, R.I., and Stephanie Harris of Princeton, N.J., and two grandchildren.

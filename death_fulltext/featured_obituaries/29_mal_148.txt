        Otis Bantum, a jail warden in New York City, died of a heart attack Monday while on vacation in Vancouver, British Columbia. He was 50 years old and lived in Westbury, L.I.
        Mr. Bantum's most recent assignment was warden of the North Facility, one of nine jails on Rikers Island. He began his career in 1967 as an officer in the Men's House of Detention on Rikers and was promoted to warden in 1981. He was also president of the Wardens' Association. Mr. Bantum was born in Salem, N.J.
        Surviving are his wife, Dorothy; two daughters, Marian, of Westbury, and Barbara Lathan of Roselle, N.J., and and a son, Otis Jr., of Westbury.

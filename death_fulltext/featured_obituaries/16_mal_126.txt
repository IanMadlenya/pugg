        The Rev. W. Seavey Joyce, a Jesuit priest and president of Boston College from 1968 to 1972, died of cancer of the pancreas on Thursday at the Campion Center Jesuit Residence in Weston, Mass., where he lived. He was 75 years old.
        Father Joyce, who was ordained in 1943, joined the faculty of the college in 1949, after receiving a Ph.D. from Harvard University. He held several administrative posts, and as vice president for community relations, he was active in helping to improve inner city Boston, He helped form the Boston Citizens Seminars, which became a moving force in revitalizing the city's transit system and the waterfront. He was also a member of the Metropolitan Area Planning Council.
        After he resigned as college president, Father Joyce served as executive director of the Boston branch of the National Alliance of Businessmen.
        Until recently, he was pastor of two churches, St. Ann's in Frankford, Mich., and St. Joseph's in East Tawas, Mich.
        There are no survivors.

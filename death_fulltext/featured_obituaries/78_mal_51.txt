
        John Ferris, a retired reporter and freelance writer, died July 3 at the Mary Manning Walsh nursing home in Manhattan. He was 93 and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Ferris reported Bruno Hauptmann's trial for the kidnapping of the Lindbergh baby in the 1930's for The Associated Press. He also was a reporter for The Philadelphia North American, The New York Post and The New York World-Telegram and The Sun.
        As a magazine writer, he contributed to The New Yorker, Life, Opera News, Horticulture, The Saturday Review and the Travel and Book Review sections of The New York Times.
        His first wife, the former Edna Booth, died in 1964.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Rita Rissetto; three sons, Dudley, of Phoenix, Stephen, of Turlock, Calif., and Gregory, of Appleton, Wis.; five grandchildren, and two great-grandchildren.

        Dr. Leslie P. Barker, a physician who founded the dermatological service at St. Luke's Hospital in Manhattan in 1942, died of colon cancer on Sunday at his home in Manhattan. He was 88 years old.
        Dr. Barker, who was director of the dermatology department at the merged St. Luke's-Roosevelt Hospital Center until he retired in 1967, was born in Idaho and graduated from the University of Utah. He received his medical degree from the College of Physicians and Surgeons of Columbia University, where he served on the faculty from 1943 until his retirement.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Katherine Buckhout, and a stepdaugher, Mary Bonnell.

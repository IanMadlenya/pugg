        Benjamin F. Leventhal, a businessman and economic analyst, died Friday at Norwalk (Conn.) Hospital, where he had been since suffering a heart attack July 4. He was 62 years old and lived in Westport, Conn.
        In 1977 Mr. Leventhal became vice chairman of the D & L Venture Corporation, a holding company in New Britain, Conn. He held the title at his death.
        Mr. Leventhal was born in Hartford and earned a bachelor's degree at Brown University in 1946. He served in the Navy during World War II. He then became a partner at L. F. Rothschild Holdings Inc. in New York City and headed its research department.
        He is survived by his wife, Susan; a son, Eric, of Wakefield, Mass.; a daughter, Eve, of Manhattan; his mother, Mary D. Leventhal of New Britain; a brother, H. David Leventhal of Hartford, and a sister, Selma L. Bernstingle of Providence, R.I.

        Thomas R. Nunan Jr., an account executive with the CBS Network sales division, died on Saturday at Greenwich Hospital in Connecticut. He was 67 years old and lived in Riverside, Conn. He died of lung cancer, his family said.
        He graduated from Loomis School and Harvard College. He was awarded a Bronze Star in World War II while serving as a tank corps lieutenant in the Philippines. He entered broadcasting sales after the war and was with the Steinman stations in Pennsylvania and with Crosley Broadcasting, later Avco. He joined CBS in 1971, working there until early this year.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Pat Schinzing; two sons, Thomas 3d, of New Canaan, Conn., and Michael, of Potsdam, N.Y., and three daughters, Caroline Hill of Annapolis, Md., Louise Taylor of Norwich, Vt., and Mary Patricia Nunan of Riverside.


        Robert Edwin Kenyon Jr., a former president of the Magazine Publishers Association, died on Monday at Greenwich Hospital in Connecticut. He was 87 and  most recently resided in Old Greenwich.
        Mr. Kenyon was a former publisher of Printers' Ink magazine, the weekly trade publication of advertising and marketing. In 1956 he was named president of the  publishers association, which at the time represented some 100 publishers producing 377 magazines with total circulation of more than 146 million copies per issue.
        He assumed the title of executive vice president in 1961 and held it until he retired in 1974. He then served as a visiting professor and assistant dean at the Medill School of Journalism at Northwestern University and lectured at various other journalism schools.
        Between 1978 and 1990, Mr. Kenyon served as executive director and secretary  of the American Society of Magazine Editors. He was particularly concerned with  the society's National Magazine Awards and internship projects.
        Born in Chicago, he graduated Phi Beta Kappa from Kenyon College in Ohio. He  joined Printers' Ink in 1945.
        Mr. Kenyon is survived by a son, Robert E. 3d, of Darien, Conn.; a daughter,  Pamela K. Force of Norwich, Vt.; a brother, Bruce W., of St. Petersburg, Fla.; eight grandchildren, and three great-grandchildren. His wife of 50 years, Doris  Bokum Kenyon, died in 1988.

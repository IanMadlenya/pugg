        Loren R. Kirkwood, a former division vice president for RCA Consumer Electronics and a key figure in the development of color television, died Saturday. He was 77 years old.
        Mr. Kirkwood joined RCA in 1930. He retired in 1974 as vice president of television electronics for the Consumer Electronics division, but served as chief technical consultant until 1983.
        Mr. Kirkwood held 36 patents in radio and television and helped to develop the first color television set, which went on sale in 1954 for $1,000.

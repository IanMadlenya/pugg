        Thomas P. Robinson, a television executive who was involved in the industry since its early days, died on Friday at George Washington University Hospital in Washington. He was 85 years old and lived in retirement in Tequesta, Fla.
        Mr. Robinson was an investment banker and worked in the Office of Price Administration in World War II before he entered broadcasting with the Blue Network, the divested portion of the NBC Network that later became ABC. He later served as an administrative vice president of WPIX, Channel 11, in New York.
        Surviving him are two sons, Thomas Jr. of San Antonio and Davis, of Washington, and three grandchildren.

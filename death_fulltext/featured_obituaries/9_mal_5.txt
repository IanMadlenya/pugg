        Jacob H. Singer, a cantor, died Sunday at the New York University Medical Center after a long illness. Mr. Singer was 70 years old and lived in Queens.
        Born in Poland in 1916, Mr. Singer came to the United States at the age of 12.
        Mr. Singer attended Los Angeles City College briefly, but did not graduate. Instead, pursuing a career as a cantor, he studied with several famous voice teachers and cantors, including Louis Lipitz.
        During the 1930's and 40's, he gave solo cantorial concerts at Carnegie Hall. During the 1950's, he led a 30-voice choir and performed at various hotels, including the Concord Hotel in Kiamesha Lake, N.Y.
        He was the cantor at Temple Beth-El in Rockaway Park, Queens, for 28 years.
        Mr. Singer is survived by his wife, Miriam, and a daughter, Rose Flax of Manhattan.

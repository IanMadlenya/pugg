        James Kahn, the president of the Pension Planning Company of Manhattan, a division of Republic Hogg Robinson, was killed in an automobile accident en route to the airport in West Palm Beach, Fla., on Monday. He was 64 years old and lived in White Plains and Palm Beach.
        He was associated with the company founded by his father-in-law, Meyer Goldstein, for 38 years and was named its president 29 years ago. The company merged in 1983 with Republic Hogg Robinson, a subsidiary of Hogg Robinson Gardner Mountain, insurance brokers in London.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Joyce Goldstein Ulrich; a son, Jeffrey, of Manhattan; a daughter, Susan Gell of Charleston, Md.; a sister, Barbara St. Lifer of West Orange, N.J., and two grandchildren.

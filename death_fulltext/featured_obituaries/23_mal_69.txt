        Charles H. Sallee, the secretary-treasurer of the Amalgamated Clothing and Textile Workers Union, died Monday at Barnes Hospital in St. Louis, Mo., after a brief illness. He was 63 years old and lived in Manhattan and St. Louis.
        Mr. Sallee was elected secretary-treasurer in June 1987. He had served as executive vice president since 1985.
        A native of Springfield, Ky., he began his career as a member of Local 630 of the Textile Workers Union of America with the Johnson & Johnson Company in New Brunswick, N.J. In 1954 he was elected president of the Local. In 1963 he joined the union's staff as an international representative.
        He was elected to the textile workers' executive council in 1972 and became a vice president in 1976, when the union merged with the Amalgamated Clothing Workers of America. The union is affiliated with the A.F.L.-C.I.O.
        Since 1985, Mr. Sallee had served as secretary of the Textile Workers Pension Fund and chairman of the A.C.T.W.U. Textile Workers Health Plan. He was secretary-treasurer of the Amalgamated Life Insurance Company and a member of the executive committee of the board of directors of the Amalgamated Bank of New York.
        He is survived by his mother, Gertrude Gibson, of Diamond, Ohio; two daughters, Sue Ann Moran of Ellisville, Mo., and Peggy Williams of Dunedin, Fla., and eight grandchildren.

        Thomas A. Wilson, administrator of the Federal milk-marketing program in the New York-New Jersey area, died last Wednesday at the Hoboken (N.J.) Medical Center after a heart attack. He was 60 years old and lived in Scarsdale, N.Y.
        Mr. Wilson was born in Twinsburg, Ohio. He was a graduate of Ohio State University and pursued graduate studies at Cornell. In the Department of Agriculture's Milk Marketing Administration, he was in charge of regulating the price paid to dairy farmers for milk delivered in the New York metropolitan area.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Marian Walker; a daughter, Jane Wilson, and a son, Thomas, both of Yonkers; a brother, John, of Reno, and a sister, June Smith of Columbus, Ohio.

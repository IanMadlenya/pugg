
        Leonard W. Arentsen, president of Batus, Inc., the United States subsidiary of the British conglomerate B.A.T. Industries, died on Friday at the Scottsdale Memorial Hospital in Arizona. He was 53 years old and lived in Anchorage, Ky.
        The cause of death was not disclosed.
        Mr. Arentsen joined Batus in 1978 and was controller and chief financial officer before becoming president last year. He represented Batus on the board of The Farmers Group, Inc., its insurance subsidiary.
        Born in Chicago, he received a degree in accounting from the University of Illinois. He was an audit partner at the accounting firm of Arthur Andersen & Company from 1971 to 1978.
        Mr. Arentsen is survived by his wife, the former Janet Beard; five sons, Jeffrey and Paul, both of Louisville, Ky., John, of Fort Wayne, Ind., Scott, of Cincinnati, and Steven, of Chicago; a brother, Albert, of Elmhurst, Ill,, and a grandson.

        Dr. Charles R. Marshall Jr., a psychiatrist who was director of rehabilitation medicine at St. Joseph Medical Center in Stamford, Conn., died on Thursday at his home in Greenwich, Conn., at the age of 39.
        He died of complications from pneumonia, his family said.
        Dr. Marshall was born in Pontiac, Mich., and was a graduate of the University of Michigan Medical School. He was named to head the rehabilitation program at St. Joseph in 1982. He founded and served as medical director of Norwalk (Conn.) Rehabilitation Services. In 1984 and 1985 he was president of the Connecticut Society of Physical Medicine and Rehabilitation.
        Dr. Marshall is survived by his parents, Charles Sr. and Margaret, of Norwalk; a brother, David, of Manhattan, and a sister, Cynthia Knoble of Long Beach, Calif.
        Correction:  October 4, 1990, Thursday, Late Edition - Final
        An obituary on Monday about Dr. Charles R. Marshall Jr. misstated his profession. He was a physiatrist, a specialist in physical medicine, not a psychiatrist.

        Leo Schwartz, a retired New York surgeon who was an ear, nose and throat specialist, died of a heart attack on Wednesday at his home in Sarasota, Fla. He was 87 years old and had lived in Florida for 17 years.
        Dr. Schwartz practiced medicine in Manhattan for more than 40 years. He had been president of the medical board of the Manhattan Eye, Ear and Throat Hospital and at various times chief of staff at Doctors Hospital and Roosevelt Hospital, among others.
        Dr. Schwartz was born in Nashville and was a graduate of the University of Pennsylvania and Vanderbilt Medical School in Nashville.
        He is survived by a son, Roger, of Oradell, N.J., and four grandchildren.

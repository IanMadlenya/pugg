
        Lester D. Supiro, a metallurgical engineer and inventor who founded M & R Refractory Metals Inc. of Springfield, N.J., died on Tuesday at Englewood Hospital in New Jersey. He was 69 years old and lived in Fort Lee, N.J.
        He died of heart disease, a family spokeswoman said.
        Mr. Supiro was the chief executive officer of his metals processing concern from 1953 to 1969, when he sold it to the Whittaker Corporation and became a consultant. He held 49 patents for metal processes, including the hexamine process for manufacturing tungsten and molybdenum.
        Lester Daniel Supiro was a native of Little Falls, N.Y., and the valedictorian of his high school class there. He earned a bachelor of science degree from the New York University School of Engineering.
        In World War II, he joined the Army Air Forces, rose to captain, piloted more than 175 bombing missions over Europe and then served in the Far East, where he headed Air Force engineering projects. His awards included the Legion of Merit, a bronze star and the Army Commendation Ribbon.
        Survivors include his wife, Francoise, and a sister, Ruth Cess of Manhattan.

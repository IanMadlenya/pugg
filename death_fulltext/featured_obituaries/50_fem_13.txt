
        Elizabeth Ulman Rowe, former chairwoman of the National Capital Planning Commission and a leader in urban beautification, died yesterday at George Washington University Hospital in Washington. She was 79 years old and lived in Washington.
        Mrs. Rowe died of pnuemonia, a spokesman for the family said.
        Active in Democratic Party campaigns, she was named to the National Planning Commission by President John F. Kennedy. She headed the commission from 1961 to 1968. She also headed the Parks and History Association, a private group supporting the National Parks Service.
        Her husband, James Henry Rowe, an adviser to Presidents Kennedy and Lyndon B. Johnson, died in 1984.
        She is survived by a son, James H. 3d of Washington; two daughters, Elizabeth Costle of Woodstock, Vt., and Clarissa Batzell of Arlington, Mass.; a sister, Jean Friendly of Washington; and six grandchildren.

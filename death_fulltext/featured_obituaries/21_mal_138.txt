        Barry Campbell Good, a managing director and energy industry analyst at the brokerage house of Morgan Stanley & Company Inc., died of lung cancer Saturday at his Manhattan home. He was 56 years old and also lived in Watermill, L.I.
        Mr. Good, a native of Manhattan, was a graduate of Phillips Andover Academy and attended Yale College. He had been an employee of Dean Witter Reynolds Inc. and a partner of Laird & Company.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Stormy Byorum; two daughters, Hilary Noubar Stone of West Hartford, Conn., and Ashley Good of Manhattan; a son, Brian, of Manhattan; a half-brother, Edward A. Daley, of New Canaan, Conn., and a stepsister, Eugenie Voorhees of Manhattan.

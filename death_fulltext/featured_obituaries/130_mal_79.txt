
        Lester David, who wrote 14 books and hundreds of magazine articles, died on Nov. 10 at South Nassau Communities Hospital in Oceanside, N.Y. He was 83 and lived in Woodmere, N.Y.
        Mr. David began his career at The Brooklyn Eagle in 1935, working as a reporter, copy editor and assistant city editor. He joined the Army in World War II and became the managing editor of the Paris edition of Stars and Stripes, the Army newspaper.
        After the war he began working as a freelance writer, and his byline appeared regularly in magazines like Good Housekeeping, Coronet, Ladies Home Journal, Redbook and Parade.
        Mr. David's books included ''Ted Kennedy, Triumphs and Tragedies'' (1972); ''The Lonely Lady of San Clemente: The Story of Pat Nixon'' (1978); ''Second Sight'' (1979) and ''Ike and Mamie'' (1981).
        He is survived by two daughters, Margery Rosen and Susan Lewin, both of Manhattan, and four grandchildren.

        George R. Morrison, a professor of economics at the Rutgers University School of Management, died of a heart attack Dec. 15 at his home in Morristown, N.J. He was 57 years old.
        Before coming to Rutgers in 1971, Mr. Morrison had been a senior economist at the Federal Reserve Bank of St. Louis and a manager and chief economist for the RCA Corporation in New York City.
        He is survived by his wife, Rita, and a daughter, Cynthia Friedlander, of Sterling, Va.

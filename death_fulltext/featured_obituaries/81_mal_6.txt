
        Gustavo E. Brillembourg, a tax lawyer at the New York firm of Davis Polk & Wardwell, died on Tuesday night after he fell 120 feet from a mountain ledge he was climbing in the Yosemite Valley in California. He was 35 and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Brillembourg took part in a number of large corporate mergers and acquisitions in the 1980's, including the formation of the drug company SmithKline Beecham.
        A member of the American Alpine Club, he was an avid mountain climber and had accomplished a number of first ascents.
        Mr. Brillembourg was born and raised in Caracas, Venezuela, where his father, Arturo, is a lawyer and business leader. He graduated from Harvard College and the Georgetown University Law School. He was a writer on legal issues and a poet. He was an editor of The Georgetown Law Review and The Tax Lawyer. His poems appeared in the Harvard Advocate and Magus Mabus.
        He is survived by his wife, Fredrika Rhodie, the mezzo-soprano; a son, Gustavo Jose; his parents, Arturo and Clara Brillembourg of Caracas; a sister, Elisabeth, of London, and three brothers: Arturo of Washington, Carlos of Manhattan and Alfredo of Caracas.

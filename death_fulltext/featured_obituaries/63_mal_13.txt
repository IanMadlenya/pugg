
        Dr. Henry Greenbaum, a retired psychoanalyst affiliated with New York City hospitals and colleges for nearly 40 years, died March 25 at Beth Israel Hospital in Boston. He was 85 years old and lived in Boston, having moved there from Forest Hills, Queens after his retirement at the end of 1990.
        His family said he died after a brief illness.
        Dr. Greenbaum, born in Poland, received his medical degree from the University of Paris in 1935 and his New York medical license seven years later. He served in the United States Army Medical Corps in World War II, then continued his training in psychiatry and psychoanalysis in this country.
        Over the years, he was affiliated with New York Medical College, Mount Sinai Hospital and Manhattan Veterans Administration Hospital. Most recently, he was a clinical professor of psychiatry at New York University Medical Center and an associate attending physician at Bellevue Hospital's department of psychiatry. He also was in private practice and contributed to professional journals.
        Dr. Greenbaum is survived by his daughter, Anita Greenbaum-Brush of Newton, Mass.; a sister, Rose Dinerman of Forest Hills, and two grandchildren.

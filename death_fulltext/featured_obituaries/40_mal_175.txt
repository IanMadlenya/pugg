        Dr. Charles Polk, a physician in New Jersey for more than 60 years, died of pneumonia on Friday at St. Elizabeth Hospital, Elizabeth, N.J. He was 98 years old and lived in Roselle, N.J.
        Dr. Polk, past president and secretary of the North Jersey Medical Society, graduated from Howard University and its medical school. He maintained a private practice in Roselle and was associated with the Rahway Medical Center. He founded a well-baby clinic in 1934 and a venereal-disease clinic in 1939 in Roselle.
        He is survived by four daughters, Carolyn Pryor of Cranford, N.J.; Dr. Gene Ann Polk-Horne of Englewood, N.J., Barbara Riley of Roselle, and Josephine Polk-Matthews of Inglewood, Calif.; a brother, George, and a sister, Rebecca Scott, of Lawnside, N.J.; 11 grandchildren, and one great-grandchild.

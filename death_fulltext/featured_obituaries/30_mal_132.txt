        James J. O'Connor, an electrical engineer who was editor in chief of Power Magazine when he retired in 1980 after 37 years with the publication, died of heart failure Saturday at his home on Hilton Head Island, S.C. He was 71 years old.
        Mr. O'Connor wrote The Standard Handbook of Lubrication Engineering in 1968. He was a fellow of the Institute of Electrical and Electronic Engineers.
        Mr. O'Connor was born in the Bronx and received an engineering degree from New York University.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Mary O'Brien; three daughters, Maureen Kilar of Boca Raton, Fla., Carol O'Connor of Lindenwold, N.J., and Colleen Coco of Grafton, Mass.; a son, Brian, of Birdsboro, Pa.; two sisters, Helen O'Connor of Monsey, N.Y., and Mary Condon of Blauvelt, N.Y.; 13 grandchildren, and one great-grandchild.

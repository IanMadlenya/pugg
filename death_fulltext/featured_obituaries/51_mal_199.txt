
        Paul Cortissoz, a professor of English, died on Wednesday at Westchester County Medical Center in Valhalla, N.Y. He was 66 years old and lived in Briarcliff Manor, N.Y.
        He died of a tumor, his daughter Marie said.
        He joined the English and world literature department of Manhattan College in 1947 and was the department chairman from 1963 to 1978 before retiring in 1990.
        Professor Cortissoz, a native of Manhattan, received a B.A. from Manhattan College in 1947, an M.A. from Columbia University in 1949 and a Ph.D. from New York University in 1955.
        He was a cryptographer in the Army Air Corps in World War II.
        Besides his daughter Marie, of Eastchester, N.Y., he is survived by his wife, the former Geraldine Smith; two other daughters, Anne Richard of Scarsdale, N.Y., and Celia Lionetta of Acton, Mass., and three granddaughters.

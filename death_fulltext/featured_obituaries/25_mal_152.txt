        The Rev. William A. Fagal, founder of a religious television program that has been broadcast since 1950, died Thursday at the Pleasant Valley Hospital in Camarillo, Calif., from complications of a stroke. He was 70 years old and lived in Camarillo.
        The program was first broadcast on WABC-TV as ''Faith for Today.'' Mr. Fagal was pastor at a Seventh-day Adventist church in New York at the time. It changed its title to ''Westbrook Hospital'' and is now called ''Christian Lifestyle Magazine,'' based in Newbury Park, Calif,, and is shown on cable stations. Mr. Fagal stopped appearing on the program in 1981.
        Born in Albany, Mr. Fagal earned a bachelor's degree from Atlantic Union College and a doctor of divinity degree from Andrews University.
        Surviving are his wife, Virginia; a daughter, Kathryn Prall; a son, William; a brother, Harold, and three grandchildren.

        Robert W. Schleck, a former foreign service officer, teacher and research analyst who conducted studies on economic issues in New York City, died of cardiac arrest on Nov. 29 at Leland Memorial Hospital in Riverdale, Md. He was 64 years old and lived in Mount Rainier, Md.. After graduating from Princeton
        University, Mr. Schleck began his career as an intelligence analyst at the State Department and was second secretary at the United States Embassy in Tel Aviv during the Suez crisis in 1956.
        He returned to the United States to become a research analyst, first for the Public Affairs Research Council of Louisiana and then in New York at the Tax Foundation and the Economic Development Council.
        After returning to Washington he worked as a senior research analyst with the Congressional Research Service of the Library of Congress.
        In his later years, Mr. Schleck, who received a Ph.D. in public administration and public finance from New York University, taught at the Wakefield Country Day School in Huntley, Va. Mr. Schleck, a native of Elizabeth, N.J., is survived by his wife, Anne Louise, and his two sons, Paul William and Peter Michael, all of Mount Rainier.

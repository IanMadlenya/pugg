        Charles Simone, a founder of the Children's Blood Foundation at New York Hospital, died yesterday in Memorial Hospital after a long illness. He was 86 years old and lived in Cold Spring Harbor, L.I.
        Mr. Simone, a former director of Technicolor Inc., the film company, was born and educated in Paris and served in the United States Army in World War II, rising to captain. In 1973 he was designated a commander of the Legion of Honor for his efforts to rebuild postwar France.
        Surviving are his wife, Marthadrew; a daughter, Sondra Jaques, of Cold Spring Harbor; a son, Winston, of Manhattan; a brother, Pierre, of Manhattan, and three grandchildren.

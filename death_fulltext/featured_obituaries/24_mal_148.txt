        George Case, who led the American League in stolen bases six times, died at a hospital here today. He was 73 years old. Mr. Case, who lived in Morrisville, Pa., had suffered from emphysema.
        He played from 1937 to 1945 for the Washington Senators and was traded to the Cleveland Indians in 1946 before finishing his career with the Senators the following year. He led the American League in steals from 1939 to 1943 and in 1946, with a career-record 61 in 1943.
        In 11 major league seasons, Case hit .282 and stole 349 bases. He was a four-time All-Star and led the league in runs scored in 1943 with 102.
        His best season was 1942, when he hit .320 with 43 runs batted in and 101 runs scored.
        After his retirement as a player, Case went on to coach in the major leagues for Washington and Minnesota. He also served as a minor league instructor for the Yankees and was the Rutgers baseball coach for 10 years. His last baseball job was as a minor league instructor for the Seattle Mariners in 1985.
        He is survived by his wife; a son, George W. Case, a daughter, Robin Davis, and five grandchildren.

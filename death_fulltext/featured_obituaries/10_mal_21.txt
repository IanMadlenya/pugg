        Maj. Gen. Vernon Evans, United States Army, retired, a former chief of staff of the China-India-Burma theater, died of kidney failure Wednesday at the Carriage Hill nursing home in Bethesda, Md. He lived in Washington and was 94 years old.
        General Evans, who was born in Detroit, graduated from the United States Military Academy in 1915. Eisenhower. He commanded a machine gun company in France in World War I. In World War II he was the chief of staff to Maj. Gen. Joseph W. Stilwell, commander of American forces in China, Burma and India.
        From 1948 to 1951 he was chief of the United States military mission to Iran. He retired from the army in 1954. He is survived by a son, Vernon Jr. of Butler, N.J., four grandchildren and one great-grandchild.


        Hillier Krieghbaum, a journalism professor at New York University for 25 years, died on Sunday in Mishawaka, Ind., where he was living in retirement. He was 90 and was a former resident of Mamaroneck, N.Y.
        The Association for Education in Journalism and Mass Communication in Columbia, S.C., said his death followed a fall a week earlier.
        Professor Krieghbaum taught journalism at N.Y.U. until 1973, when he reached emeritus status. He served as chairman of the journalism department from 1957 to 1963. After leaving the university, he taught as a visiting professor at Manhattanville College and Temple University until the late 1970's.
        Professor Krieghbaum, a native of South Bend, Ind., graduated from the University of Wisconsin and earned a Master of Science degree at Northwestern University in 1939 after working for United Press in New York City. He served in the Navy in World War II, reaching the rank of commander.
        His wife, the former Katherine Lancaster, died in 1959, and his only daughter, also named Katherine, died in 1969.

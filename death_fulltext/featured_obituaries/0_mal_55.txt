        Dr. H. R. Wei, an educator, author, nuclear physicist and former adviser to the Republic of China Mission to the United Nations, died Jan. 2 in Honolulu.
        Dr. Wei served at the United Nations from 1946 to 1963 as an observer, chairman and representative of the Chinese delegation to many conferences, including the First and Second Conferences on the Peaceful Uses of Atomic Energy and the First National Conference on the Peaceful Uses of Outer Pace.
        On leaving the United Nations in 1963 with the rank of Minister Plenipotentiary, he joined the staff of Bethany College, W. Va., as chairman of the department of physics. He remained at Bethany until his retirement in 1972, when he moved to Hawaii and made his home in Honolulu.
        During World War II, he developed the use of audio-visual education in China.
        He is survived by his wife, Ying-bao; two sons, Robert Wei and Donald Wei; four daughters, Betty Liu, Beatrice Kam, Barbara Lau and Dorothy Lee, and 11 grandchildren.

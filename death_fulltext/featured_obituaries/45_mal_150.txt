        Lloyd H. Bailer, an economist and arbitrator, died at his home in Brentwood on Tuesday after suffering a series of strokes. He was 76 years old.
        In the 1940's Mr. Bailer, who held a doctorate in economics from the University of Michigan, formed a labor arbitration practice and was appointed to the War Labor Board and the War Shipping Panel.
        He was later appointed to the New York State Board of Higher Education and was director of industrial relations at the Urban League of Greater New York.
        Mr. Bailer moved to Los Angeles in 1967. In 1968 he became the first chairman of the Los Angeles County Employee Relations Commission. He taught at Columbia, Howard, Rutgers and New York universities.
        He is survived by his wife, Marvelyne, four daughters, two sons and nine grandchildren.

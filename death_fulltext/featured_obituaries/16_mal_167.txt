        Dr. Harold Rusch, the first director of the University of Wisconsin McArdle Laboratory for Cancer Research, died of prostate cancer Thursday at his home in Madison. He was 79 years old.
        He was director of the laboratory from 1946 until 1972, when he became head of the school's new Clinical Cancer Center.
        Dr. Rusch received a bachelor's degree from the University of Wisconsin in 1931 and was graduated from its medical school in 1933.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Louise Turner Van Wart; a daughter, Caroline Schlotthauer, of Crossplains, Wis.; a brother, William, of Menasha, Wis., and two grandchildren.


        Dr. Fred Schulman, a former chief of nuclear space power programs at the National Aeronautics and Space Administration, died on Dec. 9 at Suburban Hospital in Bethesda, Md. He was 77.
        The cause was a heart attack, his family said.
        He began his career as a chemist with the Office of Naval Research. He later served as the propulsion and power systems development coordinator for the chief of naval operations.
        At NASA, he worked on the Apollo 13 mission and was a member of  energy research teams that worked with West Germany and France. He retired in 1973.
        A native of Brooklyn, he graduated from Brooklyn College. He received  master's and doctoral degrees in chemistry from Georgetown University.
        He is survived by his wife of 54 years, Madelynne, of Silver Spring, Md; two sons, Richard, of Rockville, Md., and David, of Lancaster, Pa.; two daughters, Susan Press, of Morristown, N.J., and Gail Schulman of Columbia, Md.; two brothers, Harold, of North Miami Beach, Fla., and Seymour, of Boynton Beach, Fla., a sister, Evelyn Stopak, of Baltimore,  and six grandchildren.

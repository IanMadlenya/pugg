        Franc M. Ricciardi, the founder and chief executive of the Richton International Corporation, died of cancer Monday at Valley Hospital in Ridgewood, N.J. He was 65 years old and lived in Ridgewood.
        Mr. Ricciardi founded Richton International, a Manhattan-based conglomerate, in 1969. Before then he was president and a director of Walter Kidde Inc., of Clifton, N.J.
        He had also been president of the Kimball Company in Orange, N.J. and vice president of Monroe International Division of Litton Industries, whose headquarters at the time were in Orange. He had also served with American Management Association, the New York Stock Exchange Institute and on the faculty of the University of Vermont. He was a 1947 graduate of Rutgers University.
        He is survived by his wife, Rosemarie; two daughters, April Garschina of Garden City, L.I., and Francesca, of Boston; and five grandchildren.

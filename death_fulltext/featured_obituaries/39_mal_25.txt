        Edgar G. E. Moss, a dog breeder and former publisher of The New Brunswick Spokesman, a weekly newspaper in New Jersey, died of cancer on Tuesday at the Hunterdon Medical Center in Flemington, N.J. He was 83 years old and lived in Lebanon, N.J.
        Mr. Moss, along with his late wife, Mildred, was the former owner of the Romed Bulldog Kennel in Flemington and was a breeder of champion English bulldogs. He was the advertsing manager for several newspapers before buying The New Brunswick Spokesman in 1972.
        He is survived by his second wife, Alberta; his son, Ronald, of Westfield, N.J., and three grandchildren.

        Maj. Gen. Pete C. Sianis, retired, an Air Force officer who planned devastating B-52 bombing raids on North Vietnam, died Saturday after a long illness. He was 68 years old and lived in Bellevue.
        As the Strategic Air Command's deputy chief of staff for operations, General Sianis was the architect of an operation in which B-52's bombed North Vietnam's capital, Hanoi, and its principal port, Haiphong, from Dec. 18 to Dec. 29, 1972, after negotiations to end the Vietnam War had broken off.


        Guy T. O. Hollyday, a retired Baltimore businessman who was commissioner of the Federal Housing Administration in 1953 and 1954, died Friday at Mercy Medical Center in Baltimore. He was 98 years old and lived in Sparks, Md.
        Mr. Hollyday died of pnuemonia, a spokesman for the family said.
        Mr. Hollyday was president of the Mortgage Bankers Association in the 1940's before his appointment as housing commissioner by President Dwight D. Eisenhower. He resigned following accusations abuse and fraud in the agency.
        He was a past division chairman of the National Association of Real Estate Boards and had been a member of the Baltimore Board of Trade.
        Mr. Hollyday was a past president and chairman of the Title Guarantee Company, which later merged with the Maryland Title Comany and other companies.
        A native of Baltimore, he graduated from Johns Hopkins University in 1914.
        His wife, the former Louise Este Fisher, died in 1988. He is survived by two sons, Guy T. of Baltimore and Este Fisher of Nashville, Tenn.; a daughter Louise E. Hollyday 2d of Sparks, six grandchildren and two great-grandchildren.

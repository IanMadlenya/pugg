        James Ortman, president of J. Ortman Inc., a Manhattan jewelry concern, died on Tuesday at New York University Medical Center, after a long illness. He was 78 years old and lived in Manhattan and Quogue, L.I.
        Mr. Ortman, who was known as Jack, headed the company that was founded by his father, Julius Ortman, in 1899. Mr. Ortman took over the business at age 21 when his father retired because of illness.
        He was also a consultant on gems, and was a sponsor of rare gemstones exhibitions at the Smithsonian Institution and other museums.
        He was a trustee of Child Development Research, a nonprofit organization that provides professional training and conducts research on early childhood development.
        He is survived by a daughter, Elizabeth, of Quogue; five sisters, Judith Spero of Manhattan, Selma Cooper of Fort Lee, N.J., Ruth Philip of Manhattan, Lucille Margolin of Brooklyn and Naomi Friedman of Mountainside, N.J., and three grandchildren.

        Roderic Burwell Swenson, a former vice president of Citibank in Manhattan, died of kidney disease on Thursday at Danbury (Conn.) Hospital. He was 71 years old and lived in Ridgefield, Conn.
        Mr. Swenson was associated with Citibank from the late 1940's until 1974, when he retired. He was named a vice president in 1958.
        He was a former director of Swen R. Swenson Land & Cattle Company of Stamford, Tex. His grandfather, Eric P. Swenson, who had been chairman of Citibank in the 1930's, built one of the last of the great ''camps'' on Upper Saranac Lake in New York, where his grandson and his family spent much of their leisure time.
        Mr. Swenson was born in Washington. He was a graduate of the Lawrenceville School and attended Yale University.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Hope Hollister; a daughter, Laura, of Ridgefield; two sons, Roderic B. Jr., of Manhattan, and Winthrop, of Takoma Park, Md.; two brothers, Bruce, of Dallas, and Randolph, of Key Largo, Fla., and three grandchildren.

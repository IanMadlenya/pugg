        Andrew Lawrence Murphy, a retired realtor and business executive, died of a brain tumor Thursday at New York University Medical Center in Manhattan. He was 74 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Murphy, a former senior vice president of the Allied Stores Corporation and president of its Alstores Realty Corporation, was a pioneer in the creation of suburban shopping malls. He began working for Allied in 1946, after his military service on the staff of Gen. Douglas MacArthur.
        Mr. Murphy was was a member of the Cardinal's Committee of the Laity, the Sovereign Order of the Knights of Malta and was a Knight of the Equestrian Order of the Holy Sepulcher of Jerusalem, all Roman Catholic organizations.
        He is survived by his wife, Mary; two daughters, Andrea and Jill Hall, both of Atlanta; a sister, Madeline Carrigan of Santa Rosa, Calif.; a brother, Robert J., of Northvale, N.J., and four grandchildren.

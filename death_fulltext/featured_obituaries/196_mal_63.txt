
        Chauncey William Wallace Cook, a former chairman and chief executive of the General Foods Corporation, died last Monday at his home in Austin, Tex. He was 93.
        After becoming General Foods' chief engineer in 1942, he rose in a succession of marketing posts. He became president in 1962 and chief executive in 1965 of what was then the nation's second-largest food processing company after the National Dairy Products Company.
        He went on to become General Foods' chairman, as well as its chief executive. He retired from both posts in 1974.
        Born in Hugo, Okla., he received a bachelor's degree in electrical engineering from the University of Texas. In 1937, he became chief engineer of the Procter & Gamble Company's Cincinnati plant.
        He was a trustee of the Lady Bird Johnson Wildflower Research Foundation.
        He is survived by his wife, Ethel Frances Crain; a son, David W., and a daughter, Ann Cook Cole, both of Arlington, Va.; three grandchildren; and a great-granddaughter.

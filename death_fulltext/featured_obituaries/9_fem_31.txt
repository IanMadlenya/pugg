        Naomi Henry Sebner, a former concert pianist who helped develop day-care programs, died of breast cancer Tuesday at White Plains Hospital. She was 76 years old and lived in White Plains.
        Mrs. Sebner, who was born in Philadelphia, began her career as a pianist and arranger for the Miami Symphony Orchestra in the 1930's.
        In 1965, she was appointed by the Council of Churches of Elmira, N.Y., to serve as one of the first directors of the federally sponsored Head Start program for youngsters. Mrs. Sebner was later hired to organize day-care centers for the Y.W.C.A.-Y.M.C.A. in Manhattan. She retired in 1980.
        She is survived by her husband, Emanuel, and a sister, Constance Connors of Bethel Island, Calif.
        Correction:  November 12, 1987, Thursday, Late City Final Edition

        Paul Derringer, who won 161 games during 10 years with the Cincinnati Reds, died today in Sarasota, Fla., the team announced. He was 81 years old.
        Mr. Derringer pitched from 1933 to 1942 with the Reds. During that time, he was a 20-game winner four times. His 161 victories are the second-highest total in team history. He began his career with the St. Louis Cardinals.
        In the Reds' pennant-winning years of 1939 and 1940, Mr. Derringer went 25-7 and 20-12, respectively. He was the winning pitcher in Game 7 of the 1940 World Series against the Detroit Tigers.
        During his 15-year major-league career, Mr. Derringer had a record of 223-212.

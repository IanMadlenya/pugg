        Howard F. Corcoran, a retired judge of the Federal District Court for the District of Columbia, died of complications of cancer Thursday at his home in Washington. He was 83 years old.
        Judge Corcoran, a former United States Attorney in the Southern District of New York, served on the Federal court for 22 years. Before his appointment to the court by President Lyndon B. Johnson in 1965, he worked for a number of Government agencies, including the Securities and Exchange Commission, the Tennessee Valley Authority and the Department of Agriculture. He retired from the bench in 1987.  He became United States Attorney in New York in 1943, after working in the office five years.
        He was in private practice with the firms of Corcoran, Kostelanetz & Gladstone in Manhattan and Corcoran, Foley, Youngman & Rowe in Washington.
        A native of Pawtucket, R.I., he was a graduate of Phillips Exeter Academy, Princeton University and Harvard Law School.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Esther Pierce; a brother, David M. Corcoran of Waterville Valley, N.H., and 10 nieces and nephews.

        Clifford L. Alexander Sr., for many years resident manager of the Metropolitan Life Insurance Company's Riverton housing development in Manhattan, died Tuesday at St. Luke's Hospital after a brief illness. He was 91 years old and lived in Riverton.
        Mr. Alexander, a native of Kingston, Jamaica, was named manager of the six-block Riverton complex in 1946. It is bounded by 135th and 138th Streets, Fifth Avenue and the Harlem River. He left that post in 1963 to manage the 135th Street branch of the New York Bank for Savings, and he retired in 1966.
        In 1945 he was named manager of the Urban Housing Management Association Inc., a nonprofit corporation sponsored by the New York Urban League. Earlier in his career, he was business manager of the Harlem branch of the Y.M.C.A., a director and founding member of the Carver Federal Savings and Loan Association and vice chairman of Community Planning Board 11.
        His first wife, Edith McAllister Alexander, died in 1965.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Minette Delph; a son, Clifford L. Jr., of Washington, who was Secretary of the Army from 1977 to 1981, and two grandchildren.


        Carl Allensworth, a playwright whose work was presented on Broadway and on television, died on Tuesday in the United Hospital Medical Center in Port Chester, N.Y. He was 83 years old and lived in Mamaroneck, N.Y.
        He died after a heart attack, said a spokesman for the Cox & Graziano Funeral Home in Mamaroneck.
        Mr. Allensworth was born in Oberlin, Ohio, and was a graduate of Oberlin College. He was the author of "Village Green," a comedy that was produced on Broadway in 1941. He also wrote many documentary films for business and industry and training films for branches of the armed forces.
        He is survived by his wife, Dorothy; two sons, Stephen, of Mamaroneck, and Robert, of New York City; a brother, John, of Canton, Ohio, and two grandchildren.

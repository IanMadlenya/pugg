        William Lehigh Rees, a retired Navy vice admiral who commanded the aircraft carrier Enterprise after World War II, died of a heart attack last Thursday at George Washington University Hospital in Washington. He was 88 years old and lived in Washington.
        Admiral Rees, a 1920 graduate of the United States Naval Academy, served on a battleship in World War I. He became a Navy pilot and was later flight commander of the carrier Yorktown. In 1943 he was given command of the escort carrier Mission Bay. He became a rear admiral in 1944 and was assigned to naval headquarters in Washington. He took command of Enterprise just after the war ended.
        In 1956 he was made a vice admiral and commander of all air forces of the Atlantic Fleet. He retired in 1960.
        There are no immediate survivors.

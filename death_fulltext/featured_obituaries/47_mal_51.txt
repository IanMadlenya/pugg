
        Charles E. Carples, a clothier and a founder of Casual Corner, a chain of women's clothing stores, died on Wednesday at his home in Boca Raton, Fla. He was 68 years old.
        He died of cancer, his wife, Florence, said.
        Mr. Carples founded Casual Corner with Stanley W. Vogel in West Hartford in 1950. The business grew into a chain of 800 stores nationwide, specializing in women's casual clothing. In 1970, the chain merged with the United States Shoe Corporation and Mr. Carples continued as executive vice president of its specialty retailing division. He also served on the corporation's board and on the board of Designs Inc.
        He was a 1943 graduate of the Wharton School of Finance at the University of Pennsylvania and served in the Army Air Corps during World War II. Afterward, he was a merchandise manager of a department store in Hartford. In 1986 he left United States Shoe and he and a son, David, of Boca Raton, founded Charles Carples Associates, a retail consulting firm. He retired in 1989.
        Besides his wife and son David, he is survived by two other sons, Jeffrey, of Manhattan, and Steven, of Flanders, N.J., and two grandchildren.

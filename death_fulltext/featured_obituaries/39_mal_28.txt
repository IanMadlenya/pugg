        Julius Granirer, a lawyer and the president of the Peninsula Hospital Center in Far Rockaway, Queens, died of a heart attack on Tuesday at Huntington (L.I.) Hospital. He was 86 years old and lived in Rockaway Park, Queens.
        He was first associated with Peninisula Hospital in 1930, when he became a board member of Rockaway Hospital, as it was then known. He was named president in 1965 and guided its growth into a 472-bed hospital center.
        Mr. Granirer, a graduate of New York University Law School, was in private practice in Queens at the time of his death. He was a member of the board of the Richmond Hill Savings Bank for 27 years and was a past president of the Queensboro Public Library.
        He is survived by his wife, Grace; two sons, Martus Granirer of New City, N.Y., and Joseph Friedman of Neponsit, Queens, and four grandchildren.

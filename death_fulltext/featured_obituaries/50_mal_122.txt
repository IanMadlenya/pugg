
        Harry B. Hollins, founder of WorldPaper, a newspaper supplement aimed at promoting cooperation among nations to form a new world order, died on Monday at New York Hospital. He was 82 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        He died of a lung ailment, his family said.
        Mr. Hollins, who was active for years in organizations devoted to world government, founded WorldPaper, based in Boston, in 1978. It is now printed monthly in six languages in 27 countries and has an international reporting staff.
        A native of Manhattan and a graduate of Harvard College, Mr. Hollins was with Bankers Trust and was a partner in H. N. Whitney & Sons before serving as an air-combat intelligence officer in World War II.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Elizabeth Etnier; four daughters, Elizabeth Aldrich of Germantown, N.Y., Lilias Outerbridge of Belfast, Me., Evelina Kats of Guilford, Conn., and Angelica Braestrup of Washington; a son, Harry Jr. of Chico, Calif.; two stepdaughters, Stephanie Doane of Bath, Me., and Elizabeth Villamil of Chestnut Hill, Pa., 19 grandchildren and five great-grandchildren.

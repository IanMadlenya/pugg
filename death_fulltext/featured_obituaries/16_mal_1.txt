        Pieter van den Berg, chairman of North American Philips Corporation from 1948 to 1975, died of emphysema Friday at his home in New Rochelle, N.Y. He was 84 years old.
        Born in Rotterdam, the Netherlands, Mr. van den Berg joined N.V. Philips's Gloeilampenfabrieken, North American Philips's parent company, in 1926. He came to the United States in 1940 and helped build the North American company into one of the 100 largest industrial concerns in the country. The company manufactures electrical products under such brand names as Philips, Norelco, Magnavox and Philco.
        He is survived by a daughter, Johanna Engel, of Wilton, Conn.; two sons, Jan, of New Canaan, Conn., and Willem, of Aaronsburg, Pa., and nine grandchildren.

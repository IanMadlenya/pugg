
        Arthur L. Richards, a former United States Ambassador to Ethiopia, died on Saturday at his home in Bethesda, Md. He was 83 years old.
        He died of a heart ailment and Parkinson's disease, his family said.
        Mr. Richards joined the Foreign Service in 1930 and served in Mexico, Iran, Palestine and South Africa before becoming assistant chief of the State Department's British Commonwealth division after World War II.
        He was named Ambassador to Ethiopia in 1960. He served with the Arms Control and Disarmarment Agency in 1963 and 1964 before retiring in 1966. He then became executive director of the National Council for Community Services to International Visitors, a private foundation.
        Mr. Richards is survived by his wife, the former Ida Miles; two sons, Herbert, of Laurel, Md., and John, of Gaithersburg, Md.; a daughter by a previous marriage, Sarah Nurminen of Acme, Pa., four grandchildren and a great-grandchild.

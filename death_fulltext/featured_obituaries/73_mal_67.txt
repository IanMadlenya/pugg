
        Dr. Joseph P. Donnelly, a former medical director of the Margaret Hague Maternity Hospital in Jersey City and past president of New Jersey Blue Shield, died on Tuesday at Christ Hospital in Jersey City. He was 86.
        His family said his death followed a long illness.
        He was born in Jersey City and lived there all his life. He graduated from Catholic University of America and Yale University School of Medicine. For more than three decades he was associated with Margaret Hague Hospital, starting his residency when it had just opened. He was named medical director in 1954 and left in 1968 to become president of New Jersey Blue Shield. He retired 16 years ago.
        Dr. Donnelly was on the consulting staff of a number of hospitals, including Christ, and taught at Seton Hall University Medical School and the College of Physicians and Surgeons, Columbia University. Margaret Hague Maternity Hospital was absorbed by Jersey City Medical Center in 1970.
        He was a past trustee of the Medical Society of New Jersey and president of the Hudson County Medical Society. He was a founder of the James F. Norton Scholarship Fund that provides tuition aid for Hudson County students.
        He is survived by his wife of 53 years, the former Monica Treacy; three sons, Peter, and Paul, of Jersey City, and James, of Arlington, Mass.; five daughters, Ann Malinowski of Rutherford, N.J., Pat Donnelly Martinez of Hoboken, N.J., Mary Donnelly of West Newton, Mass., and Monica Moffitt and Kate Donnelly of Jersey City, and 13 grandchildren.

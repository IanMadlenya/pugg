
        William E. Herndon, a director and actor on stage and television, died Monday at his home in Manhattan. He was 54 years old. He died of AIDS, a spokesman for the family said.
        Mr. Herndon appeared in various daytime television serials, including "The Guiding Light" and "Love of Life." His stage credits include the New York Shakespeare Festival production of "A Midsummer Night's Dream."
        Mr. Herndon directed various Off Broadway and Equity Library Theater productions. He also taught at the American Academy of Dramatic Arts and at the Actors Studio in Manhattan.
        He is survived by his father, William O. Herndon of Santa Barbara, Calif.; four sisters, Gladys Bond, of Chesterfield, Conn.; Olive Johnson, of Santa Barbara; Rita Beatrice, of Torrington, Conn., and Marie Herndon, of East Hartford, Conn.; and a brother, John, of New London, Conn.

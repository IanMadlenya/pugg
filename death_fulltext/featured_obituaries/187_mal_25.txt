
        John M. Allen, a retired Reader's Digest executive and former official of the philanthropy set up by the magazine's founders, died on July 31 at his home in Pawling, N.Y. He was 75.
        The cause was cancer, his family said.
        Mr. Allen joined Reader's Digest in 1956 and was a senior editor, assistant managing editor and vice president for corporate and public affairs. He retired in 1986. He was a vice president of the Reader's Digest Foundation, the philanthropy, which was founded by DeWitt and Lila Acheson Wallace.
        John Milton Allen was born in Waukesha, Wis., and grew up in Scarsdale, N.Y. He served with the Marines in World War II and graduated from Yale in 1950.
        He is survived by his wife, Elizabeth Peale Allen; three children from an earlier marriage, which ended in divorce, John M. Allen III of Sherman, Conn.; Cynthia Allen of Silver Spring, Md.; and Jennifer Allen Feiffer of New York City; and three children from his second marriage, Rebecca Allen Ehrhardt of Boston; Katheryn Allen Berlandi of Rye, N.Y.; and Andrew Allen of Pawling.

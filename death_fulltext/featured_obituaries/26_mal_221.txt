        Harry Leichter, who ran a news service for newspapers and news agencies in New York City for 36 years, died of Alzheimer's disease Monday at the Grotta Rehabilitation Center in West Orange, N.J. Mr. Leichter was 72 years old and lived in Cedar Grove, N.J.
        He founded the Tower News Service in 1946 in Manhattan and moved it to Brooklyn in the 1960's. He retired in 1982. He specialized in reporting developments in Federal and local courts.
        He is survived by his wife, Sylvia; a son, Donald, of Melville, L.I.; a daughter, Barbara Rosen, of North Woodmere, L.I., and four granchildren.

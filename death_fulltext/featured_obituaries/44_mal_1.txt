        Dr. Lot B. Page, a physician associated with the National Institute on Aging who also served as chief of medicine at the Veterans Affairs Medical Center in Manchester, N.H., died on Wednesday at his home in Lincoln, Mass. He was 67 years old.
        His family said he died of prostate cancer.
        Dr. Page, who specialized in treatment of hypertension and kidney disease, headed the program of cardiovascular aging at the institute in Bethesda, Md., from 1985 until last year, when he joined the veterans hospital.
        He was born in Tarrytown, N.Y., attended Middlebury College in Vermont and received his medical degree from Harvard. He was chief of the hypertension clinic at Massachusetts General Hospital in Boston before serving as chief of medicine at Newton-Wellesley Hospital in Massachusetts from 1966 to 1985.
        Dr. Page is survived by his wife, the former Patricia Howe; a daughter, Deborah Peterson of Falmouth, Mass.; two sons, Jesse, of Syracuse and John of Cambridge, and a brother, Bryant, of Acton, Mass.

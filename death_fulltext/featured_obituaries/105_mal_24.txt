
        Hamilton Shea, a retired NBC executive and the former owner of radio and television stations in Harrisonburg, Va., died on Wednesday at his home here. He was 80 and had lived in Midlothian since 1993.
        Mr. Shea joined the National Broadcasting Company in New York in 1948 as comptroller of the division that owned and operated stations. He became the division's director of operations in 1950, and in 1951 he became general manager of NBC's Cleveland stations, WTAM-AM and WNBK-TV. He returned to New York in 1953 as a vice president and the general manager of WNBC radio and television.
        In 1956 he became the co-owner, with The Washington Evening Star Publishing  Company, of WSVA radio and television stations in Harrisonburg. Nine years later, the stations were sold to the Gilmore Group of Kalamazoo, Mich., and Mr.  Shea became executive vice president and chief operating officer. He retired in  1979.
        A native of Brooklyn, Mr. Shea graduated from Middlebury College in 1936.
        His wife, the former Dorothy R. Hill, died in 1994. He is survived by four daughters, Gail S. Nardi and Deborah S. O'Toole of Richmond, Dorothy S. Weinstein of St. Johnsbury, Vt., and Mira Shea of Colchester, Vt., and 12 grandchildren.

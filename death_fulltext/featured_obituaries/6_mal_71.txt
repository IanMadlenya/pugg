        Jim Hawthorne, a theater and television actor, died Monday of lung cancer in Glen Cove, L.I. He was 63 years old.
        Mr. Hawthorne was born in Lancastershire, England. He came to New York in 1945 and appeared on Broadway in ''South Pacific,'' ''Sweethearts'' and other shows during the late 1940's. He was a member of the New York City Opera company in the 1950's and was the leading tenor in ''Carmen,'' ''Rigoletto'' and other productions.  Mr. Hawthorne, who used the stage name James, appeared in many Off Broadway and regional theater productions. His television credits included ''All My Children,'' ''One Life to Live'' and ''Edge of Night'' in the early 1980's.
        He is survived by his second wife, Marcia, of Port Washington, L.I.; his daughter, Candace Adelaide, of Miami, and his mother, Jessie Gracie Hawthorne, who lives in England. A memorial service will be held Monday at St. John's of Lattingtown Episcopal Church in Lattingtown, L.I.

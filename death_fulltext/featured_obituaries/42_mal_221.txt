        Bobby Day, a singer and band leader who recorded ''Rockin' Robin'' ''Little Bitty Pretty One'' and other doo-wop hits of the 1950's, died on Friday. He was 60 years old.
        Mr. Day, whose real name was Robert James Byrd Sr., died of cancer and had been hospitalized since July 15, said a family friend, Karen Castillo.
        Mr. Day, who was born in Texas, formed a group called the Hollywood Flames in the 1950's. The band's songs ''Buzz-Buzz-Buzz'' and ''Crazy'' were hit singles in 1957.

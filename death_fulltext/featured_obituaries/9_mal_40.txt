        Larry Spector, one of the founders of Calet, Hirsch & Spector Inc., the New York advertising agency, died Wednesday at the North Shore University Hospital in Manhasset, L.I. He was 58 years old and lived in Kings Point, L.I.
        Mr. Spector was a tax lawyer and certified public accountant who entered the advertising business in the late 1950's. He was president of the agency from 1970, when it was known as DKG Inc., until last February. At that time he was named chairman of its management committee.
        Born and raised in Brooklyn, he was a graduate of City College and Brooklyn Law School.
        Surviving are his wife, Shirley; a son, David, of Mountain View, Calif.; a daughter, Dr. Nancy Spector of Stamford, Conn., and a granddaughter.

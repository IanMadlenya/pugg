        Wade Carroll Stephens, the director of studies at the Lawrenceville School, a coeducational New Jersey boarding school, died of a heart attack Sunday while vacationing in Ogunquit, Me. He was 55 years old and lived on the Lawrenceville campus.
        Dr. Stephens had been a faculty member since 1957. He had been chairman of the classics department and of the department of foreign languages.
        He was a graduate of Lawrenceville and of Princeton University, and earned a master's degree from Cornell University and a Ph.D. from Princeton.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Jean Roper Samuels; his mother, Louise Stephens of Ogunquit; three children, Carroll Havran of Durham, N.C.; David, of Katonah, N.Y.; Elizabeth Hebb of Baltimore; a brother, Horace, of Houston, and a grandson.

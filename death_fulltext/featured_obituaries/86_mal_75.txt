
        Dr. Martin Panzer, a family doctor who practiced medicine in Massapequa, L.I., for 35 years, died on Wednesday at North Shore University Hospital in Manhasset, L.I. Until last year, when he moved to Palm Beach, Fla., Dr. Panzer lived in Massapequa Park. He was 68.
        The cause was a coronary occulusion, said his daughter, Susan Panzer.
        A graduate of Cornell University and the Northwestern University School of Medicine, Dr. Panzer was in private practice in Nassau County from 1956 until his retirement in 1990.
        He was president of the New York State Academy of Family Physicians in 1992 and president of the Nassau Academy of Medicine in 1993. He was also a clinical assistant professor of family medicine at the State University of New York at Stony Brook.
        Dr. Panzer is survived by his wife, Marion; three daughters, Susan of Manhattan, Margaret Pemberton of Massapequa, and Dorothy Accordino of Massapequa Park, and two brothers, Dr. Stewart Panzer of Massapequa and Dr. Gilbert Panzer of Boynton Beach, Fla.

        Dr. Irwin Lubowe, a dermatologist specializing in disorders of the hair and scalp, died Monday at Doctors Hospital in Manhattan after a long illness. He was 83 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Dr. Lubowe was clinical professor of dermatology at New York Medical College for 30 years and maintained a private practice. He retired in 1983. He was the author of several books on skin and hair care.
        He was born in Manhattan and received his medical degree at New York Medical College in 1930. He was a fellow of a number of organizations, including the American Academy of Dermatology and the American College of Allergists. He was also a member of the Royal Society of Health.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Ruth Katz; a sister Syde Kremer of Manhattan; a son, Mark, of Eureka, Calif., and a granddaughter.

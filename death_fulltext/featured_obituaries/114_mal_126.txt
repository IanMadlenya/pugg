
        Howard Fried, a tenor in supporting roles with several major American opera companies, died on July 10 at a hospital in San Diego. He was 75 and lived in East San Diego.
        Mr. Fried, who was born in New York City, received a bachelor's degree in physics from the City University of New York and worked on the Manhattan Project to build the atomic bomb in World War II. After the war, he received a master's  from the Manhattan School of Music, where he later taught singing.
        In the 1960's and 70's, Mr. Fried was a regular with the New York City Opera, a frequent soloist with the Little Orchestra Society in New York and a frequent guest with the San Francisco Opera.
        In 1967, Mr. Fried and his wife, Evelyn, moved to San Diego, where he became  a regular with the San Diego Opera. He taught voice at San Diego Community College.
        In addition to his wife, he is survived by a daughter, Cora, of San Diego; a  sister, Sally Fox, of Bloomfield Hills, Mich., and a brother, David, of Queens.

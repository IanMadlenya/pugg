        Dr. Louis J. Gilbert, a psychoanalyst who was a trustee of the William Alanson White Psychoanalytic Institute, died of renal disease Sunday at New York Hospital. He was 75 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Dr. Gilbert was born in South Bend, Ind. He received his medical degree from Indiana University and trained at the White Institute. He was a consultant to the Ethical Culture Schools and to the Bureau of Child Guidance of the New York City Board of Education. For many years he was associated with Presbyterian Hospital and the College of Physicians and Surgeons.
        He is survived by his wife, Dr. Ruth Moulton; a daughter, Karen, of Brentwood, Calif.; two stepsons, Dr. David Flory of Manhattan and Dr. Christopher Flory of Northampton, Mass.; a brother, Stanley, of South Bend, and four grandchildren.

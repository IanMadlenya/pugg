        Walter J. Brownstone, the former president of a chain of clothing stores, died Thursday at his home in Manhattan after a long illness. He was 89 years old.
        Mr. Brownstone was president of the Day Company, a chain featuring ready-to-wear clothes. He joined the family-owned business in 1930 and retired in the mid-1960's. The business has since been sold.
        He was also a trustee of the Educational Alliance, a Lower East Side settlement house.
        He was born in Manhattan and graduated from Dartmouth College in 1928.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Sylvia Krohnberg; two sons, Clyde R., of Manhattan, and Gilbert, of Paris, and four grandchildren.

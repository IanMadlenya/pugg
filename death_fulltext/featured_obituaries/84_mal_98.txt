
        William J. McRoberts of Rumson, N.J., a retired corporate security executive and private detective, died on Wednesday at Monmouth Medical Center in Long Branch, N.J. He was 72.
        The cause was heart disease, said his wife, June McRoberts.
        A graduate of Princeton University, Mr. McRoberts served in Europe with the Army military police during World War II. In 1946 he joined the McRoberts Protective Agency, which was founded in 1876. The family company, based in Manhattan, supplies international security services to maritime companies and other businesses. He remained with the company for 47 years, retiring as chairman last year.
        He is survived by his wife, of Rumson; three daughters, Susan Minford of Rumson, Melissa Heus of Locust Valley, L.I., and Meredith Olt of Oyster Bay, L.I., and a stepson, Jeffrey Bolton of Rumson.

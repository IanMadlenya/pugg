        Dr. Joseph Barnett, a psychoanalyst who specialized in the field of personality development, died of heart failure Sunday at the Sarasota (Fla.) Memorial Hospital. He was 62 years old and lived in Great Neck, L.I., and Sarasota.
        Dr. Barnett joined the faculty at the William Alanson White Institute of Psychiatry in 1962 and the New York Medical College in 1972.
        He is survived by his wife, Dr. Tess Forrest Barnett; a daughter, Dr. Roanne Gildesgame of Manhattan; a son, Jonathan, of Purchase, N.Y., a sister, Sylvia Verrilli of Lawrence, L.I., and two grandchildren.

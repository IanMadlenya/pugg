        Martin H. Newman, executive director of the Will Rogers Memorial Fund and former president of Century Theaters, died of cancer Monday in New York Hospital. He was 76 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Newman worked with the theater chain for more than 40 years before retiring in 1974. He then joined the Will Rogers foundation, which provides health services to motion picture industry workers.
        Mr. Newman was also a president of the Metropolitan Motion Picture Theater Association. As director of membership services and chairman of the Pay TV Committee of the National Association of Theater Owners, he spoke for the industry on the impact of pay television on the theatrical exhibition of films.
        Surviving are his wife, Jeanne, and two daughters, Margaret Newborg and Betty Newman, all of New York City.
        Correction:  January 13, 1990, Saturday, Late Edition - Final
        An obituary yesterday about Martin Newman, a theater executive, misidentified one of his positions. He was a former executive vice president of Century Theaters, not a former president.

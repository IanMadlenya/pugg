        William Frankel, an editor who retired from Time-Life Books in 1983, died Thursday after a stroke at Capitol Hill Hospital in Washington. He was 68 years old and lived in Alexandria, Va.
        Mr. Frankel, a native of New York City, graduated from Townsend Harris High School and City College. He taught at the New School for Social Research and served in the Army Signal Corps in World War II. He was a book editor at Random House and Harcourt, Brace before joining Time-Life Books.
        He is survived by his wife, Betsy; a son, David, of Manhattan, and a daughter, Madeleine Lynn of Beijing.

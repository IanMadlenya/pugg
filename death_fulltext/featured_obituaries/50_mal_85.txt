
        Lionel O. Lindsay, an accountant and former president of the Boston chapter of the National Association for the Advancement of Colored People, died Wednesday at Martha's Vineyard Hospital in Oak Bluffs. He was 87 years old and lived in Oak Bluffs.
        He had been a trustee of Operation Exodus, a program to bus black children from Boston to suburban schools that was the precursor to the Metropolitan Council for Educational Opportunity program.
        Mr. Lindsay, a graduate of the Bentley School of Accounting, now Bentley College, worked as a comptroller for a furniture company. Later he opened an accounting and insurance agency in Boston. He retired in 1968.
        Survivors include his wife, Edna; a son, Lionel Jr. of Boston, and a brother, Alfred of Mount Vernon, N.Y.

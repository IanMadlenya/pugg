        Robert A. Wesselmann, a longtime officer of the Agency for International Development, died of lung cancer on April 19 at his home in Fort Plain, N.Y. He was 68 years old. He joined the State Department in 1953 and was assigned to the Point Four program, which became the A.I.D. before his retirement in 1973. His work took him to 69 countries to disseminate information about agricultural innovations.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Anita Ziegler; two daughters, Janine, of West Redding, Conn., and Nancy Maxwell of Chesiers, Switzerland; a sister, Ellen Spalding of Buffalo, and a brother, Paul, of Dolgeville, N.Y.

        Benjamin Bogin, vice president and director of the executive committee of Conde Nast publications, died last Thursday at Stamford (Conn.) Hospital. He was 86 years old and lived in Stamford.
        Mr. Bogin, who was a member of the executive committee of the company, had worked for Conde Nast since 1923. He was also active in civic affairs and was honored by the American Jewish Committee last May, when he was given its Human Relations Award for ''his enduring devotion to the community at large.''
        He was born in Russia and emigrated to the United States as a youth. He had been a director of the Stamford Savings Bank since 1951. Mr. Bogin was chairman of the Stamford chapter of the American Red Cross, president of the Stamford Community Chest and general chairman of the Stamford United Jewish Appeal.
        Surviving are a daughter, Jacqueline Levine of Savannah, Ga.; a son, Bruce, of Orlando, Fla.; seven grandchildren, and five great-grandchildren.

        Vincent H. Herrmann, a retired government bond dealer on Wall Street, died Tuesday at the North Shore University Hospital in Manhasset, L.I. He was 80 years old and lived in Mattituck, L.I.
        In the 1930's, Mr. Herrmann was a founding partner of Christopher J. Devine & Company, which became one of the largest dealers in government bonds. In 1964, after Mr. Devine's death, the firm merged with Merrill Lynch, Pierce, Fenner & Smith and Mr. Herrmann became a senior vice president in Merrill Lynch's government bond department. He retired in 1972.
        In World War II, Mr. Herrmann was a lieutenant colonel in the Air Force.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Marie Byrne, and two sisters, Helen E. Fink of New Hyde Park, L.I., and Anne Famulare of Bellerose, Queens.

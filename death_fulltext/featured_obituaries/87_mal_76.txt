
        Solomon Hoberman, who served as New York City's director of personnel and chairman of its Civil Service Commission from 1967 to 1970, died on Thursday at Calvary Hospital in the Bronx. Mr. Hoberman, who lived in Manhattan, was 79.
        The cause was cancer, said his wife, Dorothy.
        Mr. Hoberman was a city administrator for 30 years, starting with the Civil Service Commission as an examiner. He worked his way up through the ranks until his appointment to the twin positions by Mayor John V. Lindsay.
        Mr. Hoberman was a graduate of New York University. Since his retirement in 1970 Mr. Hoberman had been a consultant for the National Child Labor Committee in New York. He was a graduate of New York University.
        He is survived by his wife; a daughter, Jane Hoberman of Berkeley, Calif.; a son, James, of Manhattan; two sisters, Jeanne Arcus of Hudson, N.Y., and Marilyn Tierman of North Adams, Mass., and five grandchildren.

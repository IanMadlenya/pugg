
        John G. Von der Heide, a former official of the New York Stock Exchange, died on Sunday at the Sprain Brook Manor convalescent home in Scarsdale, N.Y. He was 88 years old and lived in Scarsdale.
        He died of congestive heart failure, his wife, Louise, said.
        Mr. Von der Heide joined the stock exchange in 1914 as a runner, and when he left in 1948 he was a vice president. He then became a partner in Georgeson & Company, a firm that specializes in proxy solicitation and investor relations. He retired in 1976.
        Besides his wife, he is survived by two sons, John Jr., of Madison, N.J., and Ronald, of Rochester; a daughter, Ruth O'Hara of Brookfield, Conn.; a stepson, Robert H. Wubbenhorst of Wappingers Falls, N.Y.; a stepdaughter, Karen Sybrandt of Athens, Ga.; 15 grandchildren, and three great-grandchildren.


        Duncan Stalker, senior editor of Vanity Fair magazine, died on Monday at his home in Manhattan. He was 36 years old.
        He died of complications from AIDS, his family said.
        Mr. Stalker was a native of Bridgewater, N.J., and a graduate of Princeton University. He was managing editor of Manhattan, inc. and executive editor of Conde Nast's Traveler before joining Vanity Fair.
        He is survived by his parents, Carol and Walter, of Bridgewater, and two sisters, Susan, of Bridgewater, and Nancy Brian of Middlesex, N.J.

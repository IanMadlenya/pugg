
        Bernard Frawley, an actor, died last Thursday at St. Vincent's Hospital. He was 63 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        He died of throat cancer, said his wife, Maureen.
        Mr. Frawley appeared in a number of Broadway productions, including "Mass Appeal" with Milo O'Shea. His Off Broadway credits include Brian Friel's "Philadelphia, Here I Come!" and Michael Neville's "Ballymurphy."
        A native of Ireland, Mr. Frawley began his acting career in Seattle, where he also taught acting and produced and directed plays. He appeared in many East Coast regional theaters, including the Arena Stage in Washington and the Hartford Stage Company, where he was seen as Cornelius in Shakespeare's "Cymbeline," Boniface in George Farquhar's "Beaux' Stratagem" and Leonid Gayev in Chekhov's "Cherry Orchard."
        In addition to his wife, he is survived by a daughter, Oona.

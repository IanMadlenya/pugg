
        Irving Lerner, president of the Mary Lerner chain of women's clothing stores, died on Saturday at his home in Harrison, N.Y.. He was 77 years old.
        He died of cancer, a family spokesman said.
        He inherited the family clothing store in the Bronx and built it into a chain of women's apparel stores in Westchester, Connecticut and on Long Island.
        An advocate of disarmament, Mr. Lerner was chairman of Sane/Freeze of Westchester. He was also president of the board of Abbott House in Irvington, a home for neglected and dependent children.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Judy Keller; two daughters MaryBeth Lerner of Washington and Dorothy Freker of Bruhl, Germany; two sons, Martin, of Manhattan, and David, of New Rochelle, N.Y., and six grandchildren.

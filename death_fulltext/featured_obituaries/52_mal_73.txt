
        Paul J. Stapleton, a special agent for the Federal Bureau of Investigation for 26 years and later vice president and director of security for Paine Webber, died on Saturday at Memorial Sloan-Kettering Cancer Center. He was 60 years old and lived in Ridgewood, N.J.
        He died of prostate cancer, his family said.
        Mr. Stapleton was with the F.B.I. from 1955 until he retired in 1981. He was an executive for security at the Irving Trust Company before joining Paine Webber.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Virginia Smith; three sons, Paul Jr., of Ridgewood, Jeffrey, of Elberon, N.J., and Peter, of Gaithersburg, Md.; two daughters, Susan, of Boston, and Ann Ewell, of Chicago; four brothers, Robert, of Bronxville, N.Y., William, of Reston, Va., John, of Yonkers, and George, of Tuckahoe, N.Y.; and two sisters, Marion, of Tampa, Fla., and Virginia Bree of the Bronx.

        George Lynn, a composer and the former music director of Westminster Choir College in Princeton, N.J., died of heart failure on March 16 at Penrose Hospital in Colorado Springs, Colo. He was 73 years old.
        Dr. Lynn composed more than 200 choral and orchestral works, including a choral setting of Lincoln's Gettysburg Address, and many of his pieces were performed by ensembles like the Philadelphia Orchestra and the Denver Symphony. He was Westminster's music director from 1963 to 1969 and also taught at the University of Colorado, at Rice University and at several colleges. Dr. Lynn directed many church and community choirs and was a pianist, organist, singer and longtime member of Ascap.
        Dr. Lynn is survived by his wife, Lucile, of Colorado Springs; a son, Eric, of Pryor, Okla.; two daughters, Christina, of Denver, and Lorna, of Philadelphia, and one grandchild.

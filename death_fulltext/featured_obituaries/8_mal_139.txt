        Gerald Adler, a State Supreme Court Justice in Brooklyn, died of a heart attack Thursday at Maimonides Hospital in Brooklyn. He was 69 years old and lived in Brooklyn.
        A former assistant district attorney under Edward F. Silver in Brooklyn, Justice Adler served as a civil court judge in Brooklyn until 1979, when he was elected to the bench.
        Justice Adler graduated from St. John's University Law School.
        In 1982 he ordered Brooklyn District Attorney Elizabeth Holtzman, in an unusual action, to personally appear before him about possible prosecutorial misconduct by her office in an arson-for-profit case.
        He is survived by his wife, Helen; a daughter, Jane and a son, Bruce, both of Brooklyn; and two brothers, David, of Manhattan, and Martin, of Rumsen, N.J.
        Correction:  October 9, 1987, Friday, Late City Final Edition

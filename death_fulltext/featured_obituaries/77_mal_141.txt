
        William F. Prigge, a travel executive and a leader in his industry, died on Wednesday at his home in Greenwich, Conn. He was 77.
        The cause was a stroke, his family said.
        Mr. Prigge was a past president of the Travel and Tourism Research Association, a former chairman of the Pacific Asia Travel Association's Marketing Authority and a director of the Hotel Sales and Marketing Association.
        He had been an executive at American Airlines and a vice president at National Airlines before becoming the vice president for marketing at Hilton International, where he worked for 19 years. He also formed a consulting firm, B.P. Marketing.
        He taught hotel marketing and travel courses at Cornell University and the New School, wrote articles on travel, lectured internationally and conducted training courses in China when it was expanding its tourism industry.
        Mr. Prigge was born in New York City. He was a graduate of the University of Pennsylvania's Wharton School, where he majored in transportation and became interested in the fledgling airline industry.
        Surviving are his wife, Louise; two sons, Peter F., of Folkestone, England, and Marietta, Ohio, and Paul J., of Denver; a daughter, Stephanie L. Prigge of Greenwich, Conn.; a sister, Rita G. Peake of Stratford, Conn.; a brother, John H., of Palm Bay, Fla., and three granddaughters.

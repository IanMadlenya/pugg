        Dr. Stanley Eisenberg, an optometrist on Long Island for 32 years, died Monday at his home in Woodbury, L.I. His wife, Sondra, said he died of cancer. He was 59 years old.
        Dr. Eisenberg began in private practice in Seaford, L.I. in 1956, after graduating from Columbia University, earning a master's degree in optometry there, and serving two years in the Army.
        He was a founding member of the State College of Optometry in Manhattan in 1971. A member of the first class of the school, which is part of the State University of New York, he was awarded a Doctor of Optometry degree in 1973. He taught practice development at the college and was director of career guidance. He was also a trustee of the Optometric Center Foundation at the college and was a member of the Civic Association of Seaford.
        Besides his wife, he is survived by two sons, Andrew, of Acton, Mass., and Eric, of Needham, Mass.; a daughter, Lisa Eisenberg of Seattle; his mother, Anne, of Long Beach, L.I.; two brothers, Robert, of Dix Hills, L.I., and Alan, of Brooklyn, and two grandchildren.

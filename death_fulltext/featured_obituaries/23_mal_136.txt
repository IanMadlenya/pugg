        Willie Kamm, who made baseball history when his minor league contract was purchased in 1922 by the Chicago White Sox for the record amount of $100,000, died of Parkinson's disease Wednesday at Belmont Convalescent Hospital. He was 88 years old.
        Kamm batted .281 over a 13-year career in the major leagues. He was a third baseman for the San Francisco Seals when his contract was purchased by Charles A. Comiskey, the owner of the White Sox, for the highest price paid for a minor league player at the time.

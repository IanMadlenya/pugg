        Juan Orol, a leading director during the golden age of Mexican cinema, died Thursday of complications from diabetes. He was 90 years old.
        Among his best known movies were ''Mother Dearest,'' his first, in 1935; ''The Right and the Duty'' in 1937, and ''The Reign of the Gangsters'' and ''Tania, the Beautiful Savage,'' both in 1947.
        Mr. Orol was born in Spain and emigrated to Mexico in 1917. He served as producer, director, screenwriter and editor on almost all his movies.

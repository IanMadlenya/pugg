
        George Taylor Conklin Jr., a former chief executive of the Guardian Life Insurance Company of America, died on Nov. 4 at his home in Port Washington, N.Y. He was 90.
        His death was announced by Guardian Life, one of the country's oldest and largest life insurance companies, which is based in Manhattan. He retired as chief executive in 1980 and retired from the board of directors at the end of 1995, after nearly 57 years with the company.
        Mr. Conklin was born in Merrick, N.Y., and graduated in 1936 from Dartmouth College, where he received a master's degree in business a year later. He held a Ph.D. in economics from New York University and one in finance from Columbia University.
        He joined Guardian Life in 1939 and worked his way up to president in 1969. He added the posts of chief executive in 1971 and chairman in 1977. He was credited with strengthening the company's economic research and leading Guardian Life into the computer era.
        Mr. Conklin is survived by his wife, Julie Pokorny Conklin; three daughters, Sandra Conklin-Wright of Denver, Heather C. Raduazzo of South Easton, Mass., and Holly C. Graham of London; a son, George T. III of Houston; a sister, Joan Huston of DeLand, Fla.; nine grandchildren, and one great-grandson.

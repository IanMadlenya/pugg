        Harold Leland Goodwin, an author who wrote science-adventure books for children and books about space exploration, died of cardiac arrest at his home on Sunday. He was 75 years old and lived in Bethesda, Md.
        Mr. Goodwin, who sometimes wrote under the pseudonym John Blaine, worked at various times for the National Aeronautics and Space Administration and the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration.
        His largest work was the ''Rick Brant Science-Adventure Series,'' a 26-volume series for children. The first book, ''Rocket's Shadow,'' was published in 1947 and the most recent, ''The Magic Talisman,'' this year.
        He is survived by his wife, Elizabeth; three sons, Alan, of Plymouth, Minn.; Christopher, of New Market, Md., and Derek, of Scottsdale, Ariz., and three grandchildren.

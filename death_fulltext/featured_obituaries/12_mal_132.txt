        Chauncey Eskridge, once legal counsel for the Rev. Dr. Martin Luther King Jr. and who was with Dr. King when he was assassinated, died Monday. Mr. Eskridge was 70 years old.
        In the late 1960's he was legal counsel for Dr. King's Southern Christian Leadership Conference and represented the civil rights leader. In 1968 he was at the Memphis, Tenn., motel where Dr. King was slain. Mr. Eskridge subsequenty was an attorney for Dr. King's estate. In the early 1970's he represented Muhammad Ali when the boxer was charged with evading the draft.
        He was an associate judge of the Cook County Circuit Court from 1981 to 1986, when he retired.
        He is survived by his wife, Yvonne, his son, Victor, and his daughter, Victoria.

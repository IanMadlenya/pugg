
       Louis F. Thompson, former chairman of the English department at Bloomsburg University in Pennsylvania, died Monday at the Geisinger Medical Center in Danville, Pa. He was 66 years old and lived in Bloomsburg.
        He was 66 years old and lived in Bloomsburg.
        Dr. Thompson, who held a doctoral degree from Lehigh University, headed the English department at Bloomsburg from 1963 to January of this year. Before that, he taught at the College of William and Mary in Virginia. He was a member of the Pennsylvania Council of Teachers of English.
        He is survived by two daughters, Marjorie Duck of Lewisburg, Pa., and Virginia Thompson of Ridley Park, Pa., and one grandson.

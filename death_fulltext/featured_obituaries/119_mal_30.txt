
        Robert C. Rosenberg, a senior partner in the law firm of Hawkins, Delafield & Wood who counseled state and municipal agencies, public authorities and bond underwriters throughout the country and overseas, died on Thursday at Lenox Hill Hospital. Mr. Rosenberg, who lived on the Upper East Side of Manhattan, was 54.
        The cause was a heart attack, according to his son, David.
        Mr. Rosenberg, who joined the law firm in 1967, participated in the development of financing programs for economic development, housing, transportation, infrastructure and other public projects and the issuance of millions of dollars of bonds to finance them.
        He was bond counsel to the New York City Industrial Development Agency from its inception in 1973, and at various times served as bond counsel to the states of Maine, West Virginia and New Jersey and the Government of the Virgin Islands as well as many investment banking firms and public authorities in New York and New Jersey.
        In addition to his son, David G., of Manhattan, he is survived by his daughter, Keli Ann, of Brooklyn, and his brother, Richard H., of Manhattan.

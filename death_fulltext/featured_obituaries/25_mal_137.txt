        Sidney G. Albert, a philanthropist and former board chairman and president of the Albert Pipe Supply Company, died of cancer Friday at his home in Clearwater, Fla. He was 80 years old.
        Mr. Albert lived in Manhasset, L.I., until he retired from the family company in 1981. He was a native of Richmond Hill, L.I., and a graduate of the Polytechnic Institute of New York and the Massachusetts Institute of Technology.
        Mr. Albert had been a founder of the Albert Einstein College of Medicine, a member of M.I.T.'s education council, a trustee of the Polytechnic Institute and a board member of the Mease Hospital Foundation in Safety Harbor, Fla.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Jeanette Robins; two sons, Arthur, of Campton, N.H., and Stephen, of Newton, Mass.; three sisters, Fay Greenbaum, of Great Neck, L.I.; Sylvia Fuchs of Bal Harbour, Fla., and Harriette Bookheim 3d, of La Jolla, Calif., and five grandchildren.

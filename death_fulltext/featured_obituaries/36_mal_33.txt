        Richard Z. Steinhaus, a lawyer and lobbyist, died of a heart attack on Dec. 25 at St. Thomas Hospital while on vacation in the United States Virgin Islands. He was 61 years old and lived in Loudonville, a suburb of Albany.
        He was senior partner of Richard Z. Steinhaus Associates, legislative counsel to clients that included the New York League of Savings Institutions, the Motion Picture Association of America and Kraft General Foods. He maintained law practices in Tarrytown and Albany from 1978 to 1982.
        Mr. Steinhaus was a native New Yorker and a graduate of New York University and the Brooklyn Law School.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Mary Kopley; two sons, Peter M., of Syracuse, and Richard Z. Jr., of Queens; two brothers, Henry, of Kansas City, Mo., and Stanley, of Manhattan; two sisters, Dr. Florence Lipsig of Manhattan and Pearl Sullivan of Hackensack, N.J.

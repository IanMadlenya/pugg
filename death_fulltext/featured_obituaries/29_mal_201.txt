        Wallace F. Traendly, a retired executive of McGraw-Hill Publishing Company and McGraw-Hill Information Systems Company, died of cancer yesterday at his home in Naples, Fla. He was 79 years old and also lived in Essex Fells, N.J.
        Mr. Traendly, a native of Brooklyn, began his career with McGraw-Hill in 1949. In 1960, he was appointed senior vice president of McGraw-Hill Publications Company. Three years later, he was named president and management-board chairman of F. W. Dodge, a business group within McGraw-Hill.
        He was a founder of the Construction Action Council of the United States Chamber of Commerce, a member of the board of governors of the New York Building Congress and chairman of the advisory council of Pratt Institute, from which he graduated in 1934.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Edith Healy; a daughter, Joy, of Gualala, Calif.; a son, W. Blake, of Strafford, Vt.; a sister, Beatrice S. James of Margate, Fla., and four grandchildren.

        C. George Dandrow, a retired sales executive of the Johns-Manville Corporation and a past president of the New York Building Congress, died of a heart ailment Tuesday in Jacksonville Beach, Fla., where he lived in retirement. He was 89 years old and had been a longtime resident of Bronxville, N.Y.
        Mr. Dandrow, a native of Boston and a graduate of the Massachusetts Institute of Technology, was the intercollegiate hammer-throwing champion in 1920 and a member of that year's United States Olympic team.
        He spent 42 years with Johns-Manville, working mostly in sales of industrial products, before retiring in 1964 as vice president and general manager.
        Mr. Dandrow is survived by a daughter, Carol Fletcher of Boca Raton, Fla.; a son, George, of West Lafayette, Ind.; a stepson, Thomas F. Maguire of Atlantic Beach, Fla.; a sister, Harriette Doherty of Vero Beach, Fla.; five grandchildren, and two great-grandchildren.

        Alfred A. Gross, a former ethics professor and founder of the George W. Henry Foundation, which provided job training and legal assistance to the indigent, died of a heart attack Thursday at St. Luke's Hospital. He was 91 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Dr. Gross was executive director of the George W. Henry Foundation from its inception in 1937 to 1974, when he retired. Earlier in his careeer he had taught ethics at Harvard College and at Oxford University.
        He graduated from Amherst College in 1915 and from Yale Divinity School in 1917. He received a doctorate from the University of Edinburgh in 1921. He was an ordained priest in the Church of England.
        In 1936 he wrote ''The Strangers in Our Midst,'' which discussed issues surrounding homosexuality and the church, and in 1974 he wrote ''The Primer of Ethics.''
        There are no immediate survivors.

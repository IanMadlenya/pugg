
        Philip D. Reed, a legal expert on environmental law, died Sunday at his home in Darien, Conn. He was 47.
        He died after a heart attack, his family said.
        Mr. Reed was a partner in the law firm of Skadden, Arps, Slate, Meagher & Flom and headed its environmental practice in New York, advising clients on Federal regulations like the Clean Air Act.
        A graduate of Princeton University and Yale Law School, he joined Skadden, Arps in 1986 after working at the Environmental Law Institute, a research and educational organization in Washington, and at other Federal and state agencies in the environmental field.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Elizabeth Jackson; two sons, Jackson and Philip; his mother, Elizabeth Reed of Silver City, N.M., and two sisters, Patricia Reed of Silver City, and Katherine Porter of Durham, N.C.

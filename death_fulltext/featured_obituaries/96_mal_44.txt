
        John P. Mackin, a senior vice president and director of the government division of the Segal Company, a New York-based employee benefits consulting firm, died on Friday at his home in Croton-on-Hudson. He was 54.
        The cause was cancer, said Martin E. Segal, the company's chairman emeritus.
        An economist by training, Mr. Mackin joined the Segal Company in 1965 and for many years was the editor of its annual survey of state employee health benefit plans that is published and circulated among state legislators and government officials.
        He received an undergraduate degree from the University of Miami and a master's degree and Ph.D. in economics from the University of Wisconsin in Madison.
        He is survived by his wife, Patricia; his mother, Rosetta Mackin of Coral Gables, Fla.; two sons, John D., of Manhattan and Michael A., of Denver; two daughters, Deborah E., of Croton-on-Hudson and Stephanie L., of New Orleans, and four sisters, Dixie Parkin of Islamorada, Fla., Mollie Fleshman of Atlanta and Sara Sanderson and Kathleen Aissen, both of Miami.

        Charles King Jr., a corporate executive and management consultant, died of cancer Wednesday in Norwalk Hospital, Norwalk, Conn. A resident of Wilton, Conn., he was 67 years old.
        Mr. King, a descendant of Rufus King, a framer and signer of the United States Constitution, was born in New York City. He received a degree in engineering from the Stevens Institute of Technology.
        He began his career as a sales engineer for Gulf Oil Corporation in 1949 and over the years held top posts in several major companies. He was a vice president and director of planning for Litton Industries, a vice president of corporate development of Lone Star Industries and president and director of the Coplay Cement Manufacturing Company, among other positions. He began his own consulting business in 1975.
        He is survived by a son, Charles 3d, of Boston; two daughters, Noelle O'Connor of Oyster Bay, L.I., and Damaris Ceitlin of North Andover, Mass.; a sister, Dorothy Trimingham of Paget, Bermuda, and two grandsons.

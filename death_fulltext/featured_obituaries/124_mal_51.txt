
        Frederick E. M. Ballon, a practitioner of bankruptcy and insolvency law and a founder of the Manhattan law firm of Ballon Stoll Bader & Nadler, died on April 25 at a hospital in Palm Beach, Fla., where he had a winter house. He was 88 and lived on the Upper East Side of Manhattan.
        Mr. Ballon was especially knowledgeable about the textile industry and often represented textile and apparel concerns.
        He was born in Manhattan and  graduated from Columbia College in 1928 and from Columbia Law School in 1930. Mr. Ballon is survived by his wife, Sydell; a daughter, Lois Ballon, and a stepdaughter, Geraldine Lipshie, both of Manhattan; a sister, Rhita Berhrman of Florida; six grandchildren, and six great-grandchildren.

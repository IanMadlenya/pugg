        John B. Rogan, developer of the Boar's Head Inn and other properties in Charlottesville, Va., died of gastrointestinal cancer on Saturday at the University of Virginia Hospital in Charlottesville. He was 71 years old and lived at Oakencroft Farm in Charlottesville.
        Mr. Rogan, a native of Glendale, Ohio, attended the University of Virginia. In World War II he served in the United States Army Air Corps.
        He was president of Boar's Head Enterprises and the Oakencroft Land Corporation, and was vice president of the Piedmont Tractor Company of Charlottesville. He was a co-developer of the Barracks Road Shopping Center and the Thomas Jefferson Motor Inn.
        Mr. Rogan's first wife, the former Rosemary Rinehart, died in 1981. He is survived by his second wife, the former Felicia Warburg; three daughters from his first marriage, Rosemary Thompson of Carmel Valley, Calif., Lindsay Rogan-Gilligan of Columbia, Md., and Margo Benton of Darien, Conn.; a son from the first marriage, Roger, of Charlottesville; two stepdaughters, Serena Benenson of Darien and Claudia Parrot of South Strafford, Vt., and 12 grandchldren. -30-

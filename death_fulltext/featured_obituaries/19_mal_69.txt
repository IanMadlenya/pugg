        Marvin Small, an advertising executive and author, died Tuesday after a heart attack at his summer home in East Hampton, L.I. He was 89 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Small entered the advertising business in 1923, specializing in testing for the introduction of new products. Among his books were, ''The Low Calorie Diet'' and ''The World's Best Recipes.''
        He is survived by his wife, the fomer Dorothy Neithardt; three sons, Peter, of Poughkeepsie, N.Y., Roger, of Woodbridge, Conn., and John, of New Canaan, Conn.; two daughters, Claire Javna of Englewood, N.J., and Nina Salamon of New Haven; a brother, Alan, of Sherman Oaks, Calif., and 13 grandchildren.

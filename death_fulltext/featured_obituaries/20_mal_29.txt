        Schuyler Van Vechten Jr., an advertising executive, died in his sleep Saturday at his home in Highlands, N.J. He was 65 years old.
        Mr. Van Vechten was with Young & Rubicam for 16 years and left in 1966 as vice president and associate creative director. He joined Abercrombie & Fitch as an executive and later became executive vice president and director of advertising for the McCall Pattern Company. He retired in 1973 and published a newsletter for the advertising field.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Margaret Hill of Highlands; three daughters, Stevens V.V. Thompson of Locust, N.J., Charlotte V. McGee of Cambridge, Mass., and Mary Jane Yoder of Stafford, Va; a stepdaughter, Barbara Duddy of Stoneham, Mass.; three stepsons, Craig and Mark Bell of Milan, Italy, and Frederigo Magherini, of Highlands.

        Martin Victor, a former Mayor of Lattingtown, L.I., and a former vice president of Babcock & Wilcox, an engineering concern, died on Friday in his home in Glen Cove, L.I. He was 76 years old.
        He died of bone cancer, members of his family said.
        As Mayor through the 1960's, Mr. Victor played a leading role in the fight to defeat the proposed 10-mile Oyster Bay-Rye Bridge over Long Island Sound.
        Mr. Victor graduated from Harvard and Yale Law School. During World War II, he served with the Navy in the Atlantic and Pacific, attaining the rank of lieutenant commander.
        In 1952, he joined Babcock & Wilcox and remained with the company for 25 years.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Helene Peters; three daughters, Amy Robinson of Landgrove, Vt., Helen Remmel of New York City and Martine McGrail of Cove Neck, L.I.; a sister, Nancy Millard of Pebble Beach, Calif.; a brother, Royall Victor of Hopkinton, N.H., and six grandchildren.

        Bernard M. Barenholtz, co-founder of the Creative Playthings children's toy company, died of a heart attack Saturday at his home in Marlborough, N.H. He was 75 years old.
        Creative Playthings, which Mr. Barenholtz founded with Frank Kaplan in 1950 in Princeton, N.J., was sold to CBS in 1966. Mr. Barenholtz was also the founder of Pyne Press, Princeton book publishers, in 1970. At his death, he was chairman of the Whitney Brothers Company, which makes learning materials for pre-school children.
        He was a collector of antique toys and folk art, and parts of his collections had been shown at more than 40 museums. He was also the author of ''Antique Toys 1830-1900.''
        Mr. Barenholtz, a native of St. Louis, received bachelor's and master's degrees from Washington University. He continued his studies in early childhood education at Teachers College at Columbia University.
        Mr. Barenholtz began his career with the New York Federation of Jewish Philanthropies and the National Refugee Service Federation of Jewish Charities. From 1942 to 1944, he was field director and supervisor of the American Red Cross in Italy.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Betty Willis; two daughters, Susan Smith of Minneapolis, and Joan, of Manhattan; a stepdaughter, Nancy Mcgee of Marlborough, and three grandchildren.

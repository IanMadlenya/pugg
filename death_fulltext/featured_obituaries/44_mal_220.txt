        George Walter Harrington, a former New York State assemblyman and New York City Criminal Court judge, died on Thursday at New Hanover Memorial Hospital in Wilmington, N.C. He was 75 years old.
        Mr. Harrington died of a heart attack, said a family friend, Sylvia Kaim.
        Born in the Bronx in 1914, Judge Harrington graduated from Brooklyn Law School in 1936. During World War II, he served as an enlisted man in the Army.
        In March 1969, he was appointed law secretary to a State Supreme Court justice in the Bronx. And then, in 1974, Mayor Abraham D. Beame appointed him as a judge of the New York City Criminal Court in the Bronx, where he served until 1979.
        Mr. Harrington is survived by his wife, Edith; two sons, George Jr., of Wilmington, and Kenneth, of Tampa, Fla.; a brother, James, of Novato, Cal.; a sister, Florence Koenig of Orwell, N.Y., and two grandchildren.

        Dr. Lehan K. Tunks, a former dean of law at Rutgers University and the University of Washington, died of lymphoma and acute respiratory distress in a Seattle nursing home Monday. He was 72 years old and lived in Seattle.
        Dr. Tunks, who received his doctorate in judicial science from Yale University, was the dean of the Rutgers School of Law in Newark from 1953 to 1962. From 1963 to 1968, he was dean of the University of Washington School of Law, where he continued to teach until his retirement in 1984.
        He is survived by his wife, Margaret; two sons, Lehan 2d, of Hollywood, and Timothy, of Santa Monica, Calif., and two grandchildren.

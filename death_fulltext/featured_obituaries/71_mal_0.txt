
        Allan Hunter, an investment banker and philanthropist in New York, died on Thursday at his home in Pound Ridge, N.Y. He was 77 years old.
        Mr. Hunter, who also lived in Manhattan, died of cancer, said his son, Andre Hunter.
        Mr. Hunter, a native of New York City, began his career on Wall Street as a runner for Lehman Brothers. He rose to become a partner of the firm and president of its One Williams Street mutual fund. He resigned in 1974 to join the investment firm of Neuberger & Berman as a partner.
        Mr. Hunter was chairman of the Hertz Foundation, an educational philanthropy.
        He was a former trustee of Montefiore Hospital in the Bronx and a former investment adviser to both New York Hospital and the United Jewish Appeal.
        Mr. Hunter is survived by his wife, Renate; his son, Andre, of Bedford, N.Y.; a sister, Sylvia Alin, of Mount Vernon, N.Y., and a grandson.

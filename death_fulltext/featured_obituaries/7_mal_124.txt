        Gunnar H. Berg, a former national director of the Boy Scouts of America, died yesterday at Dover General Hospital, Dover, N.J., after a brief illness. He was 90 years old and lived in Lake Telemark, N.J.
        Mr. Berg was associated with the Boy Scouts for 42 years, retiring in 1962. He was the founder of the Schiff Scout Reservation, a professional training center in Mendham, N.J.
        He was born in Oslo and came to the United States at the age of 13. He was a graduate of Columbia University, where he received a doctorate in education in 1946.
        Mr. Berg is survived by his wife, the former Rose Arias; two sisters, Jennie Werner of Seattle, and Ella Myr of Kalamath, Wash.; two sons, Gunnar M., of Glen, Rock N.J., and Raymond C., of Bethesda, Md., and four grandsons.

        Prescott B. Huntington, a retired lawyer and New York State Assemblyman, died Thursday at his home in St. James, L.I., after a short illness. He was 82 years old.
        Mr. Huntington, who was born in St. James, graduated from Harvard College and Harvard University Law School. He was a partner in the law firm of Choate, Reynolds, Huntington & Hollister in Manhattan from 1929 until he retired in 1970. From 1957 to 1970, he served as an assemblyman from Suffolk County. He was a Republican county committee member for 25 years before his election.
        He is survived by his wife, Sarah; 3 sons, Francis and Lawrence, both of Manhattan, and Samuel, of Boston; 2 daughters, Sarah H. Fletcher of Locust Valley, L.I., and Susan H. Fisher of Greenwich, Conn.; 2 brothers, William and Christopher, both of Norwich, Vt.; 12 grandchildren; 2 step-grandchildren, and 3 great-grandchildren.

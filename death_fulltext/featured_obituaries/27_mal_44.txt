        Msgr. Philip P. Shannon, pastor of the Immaculate Heart of Mary Roman Catholic Church in Scarsdale, N.Y., and a Navy chaplain in two wars, died of bone cancer Wednesday at St. Vincent's Hospital in Manhattan. He was 73 years old and lived in Scarsdale.
        Monsignor Shannon was ordained in 1940 after graduating from St. Joseph's Seminary. He was an assistant at Ascension Church in Manhattan before joining the Navy in 1943, where he was chaplain to a guerrilla unit known as the ''Rice Paddy Navy'' behind Japanese lines in China.
        He was released from active duty in 1946 and was assigned to St. Andrew's Church in Manhattan. Monsignor Shannon was recalled to active duty in the Korean War and served on the carrier Franklin D. Roosevelt.
        He worked at St. Angela Merici and St. Philip Neri churches in the Bronx before becoming pastor at Immaculate Heart in 1982.
        Surviving are a brother, the Rev. Daniel Shannon of Summit, N.J., and a sister, Mary McNulty of Yonkers.

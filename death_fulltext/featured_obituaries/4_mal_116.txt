        Julius B. Raynes, a New York realtor and developer for more than 60 years, died Saturday at his home in Manhattan after a long illness. He was 84 years old.
        The Raynes family's group of companies owns extensive residential and commercial properties in New York City and Westchester.
        Mr. Raynes specialized in assembling sites for commercial development. He remained active until his health forced him to retire from business two years ago.
        He was a past president of the Midtown Realty Association and trustee of New York University Medical Center. He also served as chairman of the Daughters of Jacob Geriatric Center.
        He is survived by his wife, Beatrice; a son, Martin, of Manhattan; a sister, Libby Adelman of Bay Shore, L.I., and a grandson.

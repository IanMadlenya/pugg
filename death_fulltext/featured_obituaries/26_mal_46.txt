        Robert Miller Lunny, the former director of the New Jersey Historical Society, died Saturday at Morristown (N.J.) Memorial Hospital after a long illness. He was 81 years old and lived in Oldwick, N.J.
        Mr. Lunny, a graduate of the University of Pennsylvania, headed the society from 1956 until his retirement in 1977. He was also associate director of the Truxtun Decatur Naval Museum in Washington and was a former commander in the Naval Reserve. During World War II, he worked at the Office of Naval History. He was a trustee of the Gil-St. Bernard's School in Gladstone, N.J.
        He is survived by his wife, Liane, and two daughters, Liane Neuhauser of Manhattan and Frederica Kerlinger of New Hampton, N.J.

        Heminway Merriman, a lawyer and former executive of Scovill Inc., a manufacturing concern in Waterbury, Conn., died of a heart attack Friday at King Edward VII Memorial Hospital in Bermuda. He was 74 years old and lived in Watertown, Conn.
        Mr. Merriman joined Scovill as counsel in 1948, becoming secretary-treasurer and general counsel in 1959. A decade later, he was named vice president, retaining the title of secretary and general counsel. He retired in 1982.
        He was born in Manhattan and was a graduate of Yale College in 1934 and of Yale Law School in 1937.
        Mr. Merriman is survived by his wife, Natalie Smith Rowbottom; a brother, William, of Watertown; a son, M. Heminway Merriman 2d, also of Watertown; two daughters, Mallory Constantine, of Rockville, Md., and Natalie-Smith Merriman, of Watertown, and five grandchildren.
        Services are to be held at 2 P.M. tomorrow at Christ Episcopal Church on the Green in Watertown.

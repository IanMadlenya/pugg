
        David M. Bick, president of Manhattan Capital Partners Inc. and an expert in real-estate investment and restructuring, died on Monday in Stamford Hospital in Connecticut. He was 51 and lived in Stamford.
        His office said the cause was a heart attack.
        A founder and former manager of the Salomon Brothers Real Estate Transaction Group, Mr. Bick was involved in a number of major real-estate deals, including the $275 million refinancing of the Chrysler Building. He founded Capital Partners in 1988 and has been an adviser to regulatory agencies, insurance companies, pension funds and others involved in real-estate investment and liquidations.
        He was also a board member of Children of Chernobyl, an international foundation that assists victims of the nuclear disaster in the former Soviet Union.
        Mr. Bick graduated from Temple University and received a master's degree in city planning from the University of Pennsylvania. He is survived by his wife, Marcia; two sons, Ross and Jordan; his parents, Samuel and Ann Bick, of Philadelphia, and a sister, Evelyn Bernhardt, of Philadelphia.


        William Cattell Trimble, a retired Foreign Service officer, died last Monday  in his home in Brooklandville, Md. He was 89.
        Mr. Trimble began his career as a vice consul in Seville, Spain, in 1931 and  finished it in 1968 as Deputy Assistant Secretary of State for African Affairs.  In 1958, he became United States Ambassador to Cambodia, where he remained until 1962.
        Mr. Trimble is survived by his wife of 62 years, Nancy Gordon Carroll Trimble; a daughter, Carroll L. Cabot of Manchester, Mass.; two sons, T. Ridgeway and William C. Jr., both of Baltimore; a brother, the Rev. Canon David  C. Trimble of Boonsboro, Md.; five grandchildren, and three great-grandchildren.

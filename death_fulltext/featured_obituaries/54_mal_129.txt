
        Albert J. Bazin, former senior vice president of Albert B. Ashforth Real Estate in Manhattan, died on Tuesday in Greenwich Hospital in Greenwich, Conn. He was 86 years old and lived in Rye, N.Y., and Jupiter, Fla.
        Mr. Bazin died of lymphoma, a spokesman for the family said.
        Mr. Bazin was born in Caracas, Venezuela, and later became a United States citizen. He joined Albert B. Ashforth in 1935 after graduating from New York University with a degree in accounting. In 1942 he was elected director and treasurer of the company. Soon after he became senior vice president, a post he held until he retired in 1975.
        He was a member of the Real Estate Board of New York for many years.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Margaret Chalmers; a son, James, of Sudbury, Mass.; two sisters, Margo Connell and Corina Bush, both of Fort Lauderdale, Fla.; a brother, Robert, of Thomaston, Conn., and a grandson.

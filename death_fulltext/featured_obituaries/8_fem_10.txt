        Florence V. Lucas, a former deputy commissioner of the New York State Division of Human Rights, died of cancer Sunday at her home in Rosedale, Queens. She was 71 years old.
        Miss Lucas was born in Manhattan and graduated from Hunter College and Brooklyn Law School. She was president of the Queens branch of the National Association for the Advancement of Colored People in the late 1950's and early 1960's.
        She was appointed a special assistant to the Human Rights Division in 1966, became assistant commissioner and was made deputy commissioner in 1972. She retired in 1975.
        In the division, she helped revise the state's human rights law and helped draft the procedures for enforcing it.
        Miss Lucas was also a vice president of the Judicial Council, a high-ranking body in the United Methodist Church.
        Miss Lucas's married name was Edwards, but she used her maiden name professionally.
        She is survived by her husband, D. Rex Edwards; two sisters, Marjorie Smith of Rosedale and Bernice Patterson of Jamaica, Queens; an adopted son, Ralph Barkley of Virginia Beach, Va.; an adopted daughter, Freda Muldoon of Newburyport, Mass., and four grandchildren.

        Dr. Maurice S. Segal, professor emeritus at Tufts University School of Medicine who was a prominent researcher in lung disease, died Tuesday of a heart attack at his home in Palm Beach, Fla. He was 80 years old.
        Dr. Segal established the Tufts Lung Station in 1946, one of the nation's first centers for respiratory care and research.
        He taught at the medical school for more than 40 years and lectured at Harvard University School of Medicine from 1968 to 1974.
        He leaves his wife, Sylvia; a son, Peter of Washington: a grandson, and two sisters, May Spigel of Lexington, Mass., and Frances Segal, of Pembroke, Mass. .

        Norman Marcus, an architect and a vice president of Citicorp, died on Sunday at his home in Manhattan. He was 53 years old.
        He died of complications of AIDS, his brother, Dr. Alan Marcus, said.
        As senior project manager in the real properties services unit at Citicorp, Mr. Marcus completed many projects at the bank's corporate headquarters, including offices for senior executives and dining rooms, an official of the bank said. Before joining Citicorp in 1974, he worked for the architectural firm of Ulrich Franzen & Associates in Manhattan.
        In addition to his brother, Alan, of Toronto, he is survived by his parents, Sol and Lillian Marcus, also of Toronto.

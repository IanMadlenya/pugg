
        Michael A. Scully, a neoconservative writer, former managing editor of The Public Interest quarterly in Washington and, at his death, a corporate communications executive in New York City, died on Dec. 17 at St. Clare's Hospital in Manhattan. He was 47 and lived on Manhattan's Upper East Side.
        The cause was a heart attack, said a friend, Tony Dolan, the former chief speech writer for President Ronald Reagan.
        Mr. Scully was the director of policy communications for Pfizer Inc. at the pharmaceutical firm's Manhattan headquarters since 1992.
        Mr. Scully, who had been a contributor to a number of publications, including National Review and The American Spectator, joined the staff of The Public Interest, a journal on public and social policy, as an assistant editor in 1977. He was the managing editor from 1978 to 1981, then continued as a consultant to its founders, Irving Kristol and Nathan Glazer, until 1987.
        In 1981, Mr. Scully became a founder of This World, a journal of religion and public life in New York, and was its editor until 1986.
        Mr. Scully was born in Bridgeport, Conn., and graduated from Colgate University in 1971. He received a master's degree in American government from Boston College in 1977.
        He is survived by his wife, Mary Tortora Scully, and a young daughter, Grace Mary; his mother, Mary Louise Scully of Branford, Conn.; a brother, Geoffrey L., of Trumbull, Conn., and three sisters, Elizabeth M. Scullywest of Bothell, Wash., Dr. Mary Louise Scully of Madison, Conn., and Agnes Theresa Corigliano of Stamford, Conn.

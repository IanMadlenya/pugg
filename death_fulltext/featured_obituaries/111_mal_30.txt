
        Lee Jackson was the United States representative on the board of the European Bank for Reconstruction and Development,
        He was 37 and was based in London.
        Mr. Jackson, who was born in Glen Ridge, N.J., and raised in White Plains, N.Y., spent his early career as an investment banker in New York and San Francisco. He became treasurer of the City of Boston in 1988 and joined the bank in 1993.  President Clinton named him United States Executive Director in 1995.  The bank promotes free-market economies in formerly Communist countries.
        According to Treasury officials, he worked to increase the investment activity of the bank, especially with small and medium-sized businesses in the former Soviet Union and elsewhere in eastern Europe, and to broaden its efforts  on environmental infrastructure.

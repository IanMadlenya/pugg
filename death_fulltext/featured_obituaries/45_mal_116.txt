        William R. Knapp, a retired senior editor of The New Yorker magazine and a former town official in Westport, Conn., died on Friday at his home in Westport at the age of 70.
        He died of kidney failure, his family said.
        Mr. Knapp was a writer and editor at The New Yorker for 30 years until retiring in 1989, after which he held the position of consulting editor. He had previously been with Life magazine, Life International and The Reporter.
        He was born in Manhattan, was a graduate of Harvard College and served with the Eighth Air Force in Europe in World War II, rising from private to major in intelligence.
        In Westport he served as a Justice of the Peace, a member of the Zoning Board of Appeals and chairman of the Democratic Town Committee.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Peggy Meeske; three daughters, Margaret Talevi of Earlton, N.Y., Kathrine DeGaramo of Alford, Mass. and Dorothea Baker of Great Barrington, Mass.; a brother, Robert, of Redding, Conn., and three grandchildren.

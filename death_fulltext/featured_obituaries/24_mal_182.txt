        Robert W. Burmester, national director of accounting and auditing for the Arthur Young accounting firm, died of a heart attack Monday night at his home in Darien, Conn. He was 58 years old.
        Mr. Burmester, a senior partner at Arthur Young, was an authority on technical accounting issues and policies. He was active in a number of professional organizations, including the American Institute of Certified Public Accountants, the National Association of Accountants and the Financial Accounting Standards Board.
        He received a bachelor's degree from New York University and a master's degree from Columbia University. He joined Arthur Young in 1954, became a partner in 1966 and was named director of accounting and auditng in 1981.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Florence Hosek; three daughters, Susan, Lynn and Gwenn, all of Darien; his parents, William and Florence Burmester of New Rochelle, N.Y., and a sister, Doris Garvey of Santa Fe, N.M.
        Correction:  January 28, 1989, Saturday, Late City Final Edition
        An obituary yesterday about Robert W. Burmester, an accounting executive who died on Monday, misstated his age and misidentified his wife. He was 55 years old; his wife was the former Barbara Hosek.

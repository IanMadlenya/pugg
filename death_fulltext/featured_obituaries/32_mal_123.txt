        Lawrence I. Marks, former chief financial officer of the Culbro Corporation, a manufacturer of tobacco, candy, health and beauty aids and other products, died of amyotrophic lateral sclerosis, also known as Lou Gehrig's disease, on Saturday at his summer home in New London, N.H. He was 64 years old and lived in Forest Hills, Queens.
        Mr. Marks, a certified public accountant, joined the corporation, then the General Cigar Company, in 1965. In 1971 he became senior vice president for finance. He retired as executive vice president for finance in 1988. Earlier he had been vice president of the Amecom Division of Litton Industries.
        He was born in Brooklyn, graduated from Harvard College in 1946 and received a master's degree from the Harvard Graduate School of Business Administration in 1948. He was a member of the management advisory board of the Polytechnic Institute of Brooklyn.
        Mr. Marks is survived by his wife, the former Barbara Katzman; two daughters, Nancy, of Glen Ridge, N.J., and Sheila, of East Hampton, Mass.; a brother, Martin, of Manhattan; a sister, Natalie Emil of Towaco, N.J., and three grandchildren.

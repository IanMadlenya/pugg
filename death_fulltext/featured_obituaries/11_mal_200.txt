        Dr. Peter Fousek, executive vice president and director of research at the Federal Reserve Bank of New York, died Monday at White Plains Hospital Medical Center after a brief illness. He was 64 years old and lived in Larchmont, N.Y.
        Dr. Fousek, a native of Prague, joined the bank in 1950 as an economist in the research department. He was named executive vice president in 1983.
        He was a graduate of Cambridge University, with a master's degree and a doctorate from Columbia University.
        Dr. Fousek is survived by his wife, the former Adrienne Albee; two sons, John, of Ithaca, N.Y., and Christopher, of Somerville, Mass., and a daughter, Kendall, of New Rochelle, N.Y.


        James Alexander Henderson, whose 36-year-career at American Express took him to the company's top echelon, died on Sunday at his home in St. James, L.I. He was 72.
        The cause of death was a heart attack, his family said.
        He was born in Throgs Neck in the Bronx, the son of a prominent New York builder of the 1920's and 1930's, also named James A. Henderson. He graduated from Hamilton College with a philsophy major.
        In World War II, he joined the United States Army and took part in the African-Middle Eastern campaign, winning a Bronze Star. He later served as assistant attorney general in the military government of occupied Austria. Top Posts in Subsidiaries
        At American Express, he held leading positions in subsidiaries and the corporate parent alike. He was president and chairman of the American Express Publishing Corporation; president of Wells Fargo & Company, the armored-car and money-handling arm of American Express; president of American Express Canada, and senior vice president and later executive vice president and treasurer of the Traveler's Check division. In 1968, he became executive vice president and treasurer of American Express itself.
        After retiring from the company in 1983, he moved to Suffolk County and raised thoroughbreds. He was also an avid golfer.
        Active in community affairs, he served as a trustee of Hamilton College, the Madison Square Boys Club in the Bronx and the Knox School in St. James. At his death, he was chairman of the governing board of the Nissequogue Golf Club in St. James.
        He is survived by his wife of 42 years, the former Jean Conway; his daughters, Elizabeth Barrera of St. James and Hilary Stephens of Washington; his sons, James A. of Wayland, Mass., and John G. of Washington; three sisters, Grace Rich and Ruth Erichsen, both of St. James, and Molly Greene of Ventura, Calif., and six grandchildren.

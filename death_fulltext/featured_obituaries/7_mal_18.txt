        Thomas A. Benjamin, a professor of science education at Lehman College, died of bone cancer Monday at his home in Irvington, N.Y. He was 73 years old.
        He was a project consultant for the Ford Foundation in Kenya from 1961 to 1964 and was instrumental in the construction of the Science Teacher Training Center in Nairobi, the first institution devoted to training science teachers for the African nation.
        Besides teaching at Lehman College, Mr. Benjamin worked as a consultant for the State Department from 1966 to 1977 in West Africa. He also wrote textbooks on science education.
        Surviving are his wife, Rose Ellen; two sons, Dr. Robert Benjamin of Oakland, Calif., and Dr. Richard Benjamin of Berkeley, Calif.; two daughters, Georgia Sassen of Worcester, Mass., and Joanne Bauman of Mesa, Ariz., and seven grandchildren.
        Correction:  August 6, 1987, Thursday, Late City Final Edition

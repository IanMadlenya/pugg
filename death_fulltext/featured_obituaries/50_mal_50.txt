
        Patrick M. Murphy Jr., a lawyer and partner in the Murphy & Barthol law firm on Long Island, died on Feb. 27 at the Memorial Sloan-Kettering Cancer Center in Manhattan. He was 52 years old and lived in East Williston, L.I.
        He died of complications after surgery for lung cancer, his Hempstead law firm said.
        Mr. Murphy was born in Brooklyn and held a bachelor's degree and law degree from St. John's University. From 1968 to 1973, he was counsel to State Senator John Caemmerer and the Senate Committee on Motor Vehicles and Transportation.
        From 1978 to 1985, he served as village attorney of Mineola, L.I. He was chairman of the Historical Landmarks Preservation Commission in North Hempstead for the last three years.
        Mr. Murphy is survived by his wife, the former Rita Solomita; a son, Thomas, of Mineola, L.I.; two daughters, Kerry Vecchione, also of Mineola, and Tara, of East Williston, and his mother and father, Catherine and Patrick M. Murphy Sr. of Flushing, Queens.

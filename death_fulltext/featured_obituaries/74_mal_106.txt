
        Henry Gellerman, a former president of the Overseas Press Club of America, died on Wednesday at the New York Hospital-Cornell Medical Center. He was 80.
        The club reported his death and said it came after a brief illness.
        From 1964 to 1977, he was vice president of public relations at Bache & Company and later worked as a New York consultant for The International Herald Tribune. He became a member of the Overseas Press Club in 1941 and served two terms as president from 1978 to 1982. He headed the Overseas Press Club Foundation until 1991.
        Mr. Gellerman is survived by his wife of 32 years, the former Sylvia Solal.

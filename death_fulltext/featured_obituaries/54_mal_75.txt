
        Ernest F. Zaloom, president of George B. Zaloom & Company Inc., importers of Oriental rugs in Manhattan, died on Tuesday at Overlook Hospital in Summit, N.J. He was 78 years old and lived in Short Hills, N.J.
        Mr. Zaloom died of kidney failure, his son, George, said.
        Mr. Zaloom was associated with the firm -- founded by his father on Fifth Avenue in 1893 -- from 1945 to 1985, when he retired. He was among the first to import Oriental rugs from mainland China in the 1970's.
        He was a member of the executive board of the Oriental Rug Association of New York City and a past president of the Oriental Rug Importers Association.
        In World War II, he served in the Pacific with the Army Amphibious Engineers, attaining the rank of major.
        He was the owner and past president of The Pike County Dispatch in Milford, Pa., from the early 1970's to 1990, when the newspaper was sold.
        Besides his son, of Pacific Palisades, Calif., Mr. Zaloom is survived by his wife, the former Madeleine Reilly; a sister, Jean Zrike of Greenwich, Conn., and a granddaughter.

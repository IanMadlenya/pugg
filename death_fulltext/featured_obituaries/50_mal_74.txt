
        Rabbi Simcha Kling, an author, teacher and translator of Hebrew literature, died Feb. 26 at his home in Louisville, Ky. He was 69 years old.
        He died of heart disease and cancer, his family said.
        Dr. Kling was rabbi emeritus of Congregation Adath Jeshurun in Louisville. He wrote "Embracing Judaism," a publication of the Rabbinical Assembly, the umbrella organization of Conservative rabbis, dealing with conversion, and several biographies.
        A native of Fort Thomas, Ky., Dr. Kling was a graduate of the University of Cincinnati and held a master's degree from Columbia and master's and doctoral degrees from the Jewish Theological Seminary. He was ordained in 1948.
        Dr. Kling is survived by his wife, the former Edith Leeman; three daughters, Adina, of Manhattan, Reena, of Boston and Elana Perkins of Teaneck, N.J.; a brother, Harold, of Cincinnati and two grandchildren.

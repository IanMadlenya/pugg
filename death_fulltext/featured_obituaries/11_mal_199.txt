        Monroe M. Stearns, a teacher, writer and editor, died Dec. 17, apparently of a respiratory ailment, at St. Luke's-Roosevelt Hospital Center. He was 74 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Stearns began his career as a teacher of English, working for several New England preparatory schools and later as a visiting lecturer at New York, Indiana and Brown Universities.
        In 1951 he entered book publishing as an editor and was associated with Prentice-Hall, Bobbs-Merrill, and Hawthorn Books. From 1962 to 1964 he was affiliated with UNICEF. He also wrote a number of children's books, biographies aimed at juvenile readers and historical works.
        His wife, the former Katherine Hartt, died in 1961.
        He is survived by a son, Michael, of Manhattan.

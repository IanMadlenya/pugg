        William H. Fetridge, an editor, business executive and Republican Party official, died of pneumonia Thursday at Northwestern Memorial Hospital in Chicago. He was 82 years old and lived in Chicago.
        Mr. Fetridge was executive vice president of Popular Mechanics magazine from 1945 to 1959, after which he became executive vice president of the Diamond T. Motor Truck Company, a post he held until 1965.
        He served in the Navy in World War II as a lieutenant commander on a destroyer. He also edited two volumes of anecdotes about naval life, ''The Navy Reader'' and ''The Second Navy Reader,'' both published by Bobbs-Merrill.
        Mr. Fetridge was chairman of the Midwest Volunteers for Nixon-Lodge, supporting the Presidential candidacy of Vice President Richmond M. Nixon in 1960, and was finance chairman of the Illinois Republican Party from 1968 to 1973.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Bonnie Jean Clark; a daughter, Blakely Bundy of Winnetka, Ill.; a son, Clark, of Chicago; a sister, Mrs. Jerome Thor of Los Angeles, and six grandchildren.

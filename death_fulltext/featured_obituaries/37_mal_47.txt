        Robert Claiborne, a writer, editor and musician, died of a heart attack on Saturday at his home in Manhattan. He was 70 years old.
        Mr. Claiborne wrote more than a dozen nonfiction books, many of them about the English language. They include ''Our Marvelous Native Tongue'' (1983), ''Loose Cannons and Red Herrings: The Book of Lost Metaphors'' (1988) and ''The Roots of English'' (1989).
        In the 1940's and 50's, he sang folk songs and taught guitar, music theory and songwriting. With Pete Seeger, Lee Hays and others, he founded People's Songs, an organization of folk singers and composers that published songs and produced concerts.
        In the mid-1950's, he began a second career as an editor of publications that included Scientific American, Time-Life Books and Hospital Practice.
        Mr. Claiborne grew up in the New York City area and graduated from New York University. He was a founding member of the National Writers Union.
        He is survived by his wife, Sybil; a daughter, Amanda, of Cambridge, Mass.; a son, Sam, of Brooklyn, and three grandchildren.

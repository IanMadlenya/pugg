        Theodore Kaghan, a former Foreign Service officer and journalist, died of heart failure Wednesday at Brattleboro (Vt.) Memorial Hospital. He was 77 years old and lived in Brattleboro.
        Mr. Kaghan served from 1950 to 1953 as deputy director of public affairs for the United States High Commission in In 1953, he testified before Joseph R. McCarthy's Senate Permanent Investigations Subcommittee. While he denied any Communist sympathies, his resignation was demanded by the State Department, which eventually cleared him of the allegations.
        He joined The New York Post, where he was a United Nations correspondent and a foreign affairs columnist. From 1967 to 1973, he was director of public information in Rome for the Food and Agriculture Organization of the United Nations. He worked for a Manhattan public relations firm until he retired in 1975.
        Mr. Kaghan, who was born in Boston, was graduated from the University of Michigan.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Nancy Mainland, of Valley Cottage, N.Y.; a son, Benjamin, of Putney, Vt.; a daughter, Susan Sharko of Valley Cottage, and two grandchildren.


        James A. Cathcart Jr., retired president and chairman of the General Reinsurance Corporation, died last Sunday at his home in Hilton Head, S.C. He was 82 years old.
        His family said he had been in failing health and had died of natural causes.
        Mr. Cathcart started at General Reinsurance, a subsidiary of the General Re Corporation, in New York City in 1930 and left in 1942 to work for the Peerless Casualty Company, where he became a vice president. He returned to General Reinsurance in 1950 and was appointed president in 1952. He became chairman in 1960 and retired from full-time service in 1973, although he remained on the board until 1979.
        Mr. Cathcart, who was born in Columbia, S.C., attended the University of South Carolina and later earned a law degree at New York Law School.
        He is survived by his wife, Ann; six sons, James Cathcart 3d and Charles Cathcart of Tuxedo Park, N.Y., George Cathcart of Phoenix, John Cathcart of Columbia, Sanders Cathcart of New Providence, N.J., and William Cathcart of Catalina, Ariz.; two brothers, John and William Cathcart of Hilton Head; three sisters, Beverly Todd and Katharine McCaskill of Columbia and Ann Fersner of Orangeburg, S.C., 10 grandchildren and a great-grandchild.

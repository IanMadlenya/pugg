        Ziona Tagger, an Israeli artist known for her depictions of the old port city of Jaffa, died Thursday in Tel Aviv. She was 88 years old.
        Ms. Tagger, one of the founders of the Israeli Artists and Sculptors Association, studied painting at the Bezalel School of Art in Jerusalem and at the Andre Lot Academy in Paris. Her work has been displayed in shows in Israel, France and Egypt and in group exhibitions in Italy, South Africa and the United States.
        She is survived by a son, Avraham Katz-Oz, who is Israel's Deputy Agriculture Minister.

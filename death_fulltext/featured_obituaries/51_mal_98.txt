
        John H. Humpstone, a real estate executive and former Foreign Service official, died yesterday at Georgetown University Hospital in Washington. He was 86 years old.
        He died of cardiorespiratory failure, said his son Charles.
        Mr. Humpstone, who lived in Washington, was assistant to the president of Previews Inc., an international real estate clearing house, from 1956 until he retired in 1968.
        He joined the company in 1954 after serving with the Economic Cooperation Administration in Europe. Earlier, after receiving a degree from Williams College in 1926, Mr. Humpstone worked in banking and construction in Manhattan before joining the Glen L. Martin Aircraft company in Baltimore as project administrator in 1940.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Maribel Cheney; two sons, Charles, a lawyer, of South Woodstock, Vt., and John Jr., a retired business executive of Locust Valley, L.I.; a sister, Nancy Gardiner of Hightstown, N.J., five grandchildren and two great-grandchildren.


        Bruce G. Fiigen, the chief entertainment lawyer for Warner Brothers Inc., died on April 29 at his home in the Bel Air section of Los Angeles at the age of 39.
        The cause was Kaposi's sarcoma and other complications of AIDS, said David Hebert, his companion.
        After specializing in trademark and entertainment law in Manhattan, Mr. Fiigen became the senior intellectual-property counsel at Warner, a subsidiary of Time-Warner Inc., in Burbank, Calif., in 1990.
        He was in charge of licensing and protecting the rights to Warner cartoon characters like Bugs Bunny, Porky Pig and Daffy Duck, DC Comics characters like Superman, Batman and Wonder Woman and Lorimar Television programs, including "The Jenny Jones Show," "Gabriel's Fire" and "Bodies of Evidence."
        He previously worked for 10 years at Carro Spanbach Kaster & Cuiffo, a law firm of which he was a partner, and two years at Nims Howe Collision & Isner. He also worked briefly for Cab levision Systems in Woodbury, L.I.
        Mr. Fiigen was born in Springfield, Mass. He was a graduate of the University of New Hampshire and the Georgetown University law school.
        Besides Mr. Hebert, his survivors include his parents, William and Mildred Fiigen of Portsmouth, N.H., and a brother, Richard, of North Canton, Ohio.

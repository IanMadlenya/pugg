        Harry G. Shad, a former executive vice president of the Atlantic Refining Company, died of Alzheimer's disease Thursday in the Beneva Nursing Pavilion in Sarasota, Fla., where he lived. He was 85 years old.
        Mr. Schad joined Atlantic in 1925 and retired in 1967. He was appointed to the Permanent International Association of Navigation Congresses, an international body that exchanges information on navigation.
        He ws a former director of the Delaware Port Authority, the Philadelphia Maritime Exchange and the Tabor Home for Children. He served as chairman of the joint executive committee for the improvement and development of the Philadelphia port area. Mr. Schad was a trustee of Bucknell University.
        Surviving are a daughter, Marilyn Killian, of Gaithersburg, Md.; two sons, Richard, of Manhattan, and Rovert, of Huntingdon Valley, Pa. and fivegrandchildren.

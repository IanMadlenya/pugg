
        Walter Thompson Ash, an actor and stage manager who was a member of Orson Welles's Mercury Theater, died on Feb. 1 at the Cohler Memorial Hospital on Roosevelt Island. He was 78 years old and lived on Roosevelt Island.
        He died of pneumonia, said his sister, Doris Malvicini of Patchogue, L.I.
        Mr. Ash first worked with Orson Welles at the Henry Street Settlement in 1937, and joined the Mercury Theater the following year.
        He was the lighting designer and stage manager for Cheryl Crawford's 1942 production of "Porgy and Bess" prior to its Broadway run, was the assistant director of "Anna Lucasta" on Broadway, and directed the London and West Coast productions of that show. From 1949 to 1952 he taught directing in the drama department of Columbia University. His last show was "Twanger," an off-Broadway musical he directed in 1973.

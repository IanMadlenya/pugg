
        Murray Koenig, a retired judge of Bronx Criminal Court and later acting justice of New York State Supreme Court, died on Saturday in Miami Beach. A former resident of the Bronx, he was 85 and had lived in Aventura, Fla., since retiring from the bench in 1986.
        Judge Koenig attended City College and St. John's University and graduated from Brooklyn Law School. He was in private practice and held a number of borough and city posts before Mayor John V. Lindsay appointed him to Criminal Court in 1969.
        He was a founding member of the New York State Liberal Party and ran as its candidate several times.
        Judge Koenig is survived by his wife, Ruth; two sons, Joshua, of Albany, and Zachary, of Berkeley, Calif.; a daughter, Neva, of Woodbourne, N.Y.; a sister, Dorothy K. Bleefeld; and two granddaughters.

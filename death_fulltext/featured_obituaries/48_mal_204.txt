
        Dr. Frederick Dorian, a lecturer, author and professor of music, died on Thursday at Montefiore Hospital in Pittsburgh. He was 89 years old.
        He died of a stroke, his wife, Judith, said.
        Dr. Dorian was a professor emeritus at Carnegie Mellon University in Pittsburgh, where he taught music for more than 40 years. He founded the school's opera department and conducted orchestral and choral concerts there for 18 years. He also taught at the Curtis Institute in Philadelphia, served as a faculty member and program coordinator at the Marlboro Festival in Vermont and taught musicology as a visiting professor at the Hebrew University in Jerusalem.
        Dr. Dorian, born in Vienna, studied theory and composition with Anton Webern and piano with Eduard Steurmann. He was the author of several books, including "The Musical Workshop."
        He is survived by his wife.

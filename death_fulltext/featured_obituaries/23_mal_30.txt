        Charles P. Buckley Jr., a lawyer who was active in Long Island affairs, died Monday at his home in Baldwin, L.I., after a long illness. He was 86 years old.
        Mr. Buckley was senior partner in the firm of Rivkin, Radler, Dunne & Bayh in Uniondale, L.I., at his death. He was president of the Nassau County Bar Association from 1961 to 1962.
        Mr. Buckley was a director of the Long Island Trust Company, the Beacon Federal Savings Bank and the South Shore Federal Savings and Loan. He had been on the board of South Nassau Communities Hospital in Oceanside, L.I., since 1960 and was its president from 1967 to 1970.
        Mr. Buckley was also on the board of Adelphi University and the State University of New York at Farmingdale, and he was chairman of the board of overseers of the New York Institute of Technology in Old Westbury, L.I. He had been a vice president and member of the executive committee of the Boy Scouts of America and a trustee of the American Heart Association and of the American Cancer Society.
        Mr. Buckley was born in Brooklyn. He graduated from Union College in Schenectady in 1926 and received a law degree from Fordham University in 1930.
        Mr. Buckley's wife, the former Marion Munroe, died in 1981. He established the Marion A. Buckley School of Nursing at Adelphi in her memory.
        He is survived by a sister, Margaret Hurley, and a brother, David Buckley, both of Baldwin.

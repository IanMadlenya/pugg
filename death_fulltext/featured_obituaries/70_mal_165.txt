
        Peter Geelan, an international publishing agent, died Wednesday at his home in Las Vegas, Nev. He was 63 years old.
        Mr. Geelan died of cancer, according to a friend, Robert Hagelstein, president of the Greenwood Publishing Group. He reported Mr. Geelan's death yesterday.
        A native of London, Mr. Geelan spent his entire career in book publishing. He oversaw the establishment of European operations for Prentice Hall from 1958 to 1960 and for Holt, Rinehart & Winston from 1961 to 1970. In 1970 he formed his own company in London, European Ltd., and represented American book publishers, including Rand McNally, Houghton Mifflin, Greenwood and many university presses.
        Mr. Geelan is survived by his wife, Majorie; three sons, Michael, Jeremy and Christopher Geelan, all of London; a daughter, Jane Geelan of Las Vegas; a brother, Patrick Geelan of London, and seven grandchildren.

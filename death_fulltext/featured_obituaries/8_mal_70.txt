        David Fromson, a lawyer who lectured at Pace University, Georgetown Law School and Southern Methodist University, died Friday at St. Mary's Hospital in Palm Beach, Fla. He was 57 years old and lived in Palm Beach and Oakland, Me.
        Mr. Fromson, who retired in 1984, had served as general counsel to the Long Island Association, a business group, and organized the Long Island Industrial Job Development Corporation.
        He was vice chairman and executive counsel of C.W. Post College of Long Island University, served on the board of directors of the Nassau County Bar Association and was a director of the Nassau County Red Cross.
        He attended Fordham College, graduated from Fordham Law School and received a Master of Law degree from St. John's Law School. During World War II he served in the Army and during the Korean War he was a major in the Air Force.
        He is survived by his wife, Marjorie; two sons, Dr. John, of Newton, Mass., and Dr. Thomas, of West Hartford, Conn., and a sister, Alice Moskowitz of Queens.

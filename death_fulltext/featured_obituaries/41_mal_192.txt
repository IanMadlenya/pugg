        Robert Bower, founder of the Washington-based Bureau of Social Science Research, died on Friday at his home in Washington. He was 71 years old.
        Mr. Bower died of a heart attack, said his wife, Jean.
        Mr. Bower founded the bureau, a private nonprofit group that conducted social science research projects for government and industry, in 1950. He retired in 1981.
        He was the author of three books, most recently ''The Changing Television Audience in America'' (Columbia University Press, 1985).
        Mr. Bower graduated from Yale College and received a doctorate from Columbia University.
        In addition to his wife, he is survived by a son, Stephen, of Manhattan; two stepdaughters, Jennifer Just of Easthampton, Mass., and Julia Just of Manhattan, and a grandson.

        Harry A. Grossman, a lawyer who was a founder of Daytop Village, the narcotics treatment program, died of a heart attack on Sunday in Quogue, L.I., where he was on a family visit. He was 77 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Grossman, who specialized in Social Security law, was a deputy collector with the Internal Revenue Service for the Third District in New York from 1937 to 1940. He was in private law practice for many years and at his death was of counsel to the firm of Blum, Gersen & Stream.
        A native of Manhattan, Mr. Grossman was a grduate of New York University and Columbia Law School. During World War II he served in the Army, rising to the rank of major.
        In 1964, he joined with Msgr. Willlam B. O'Brien, who became the president of Daytop Village, to found the anti-drug program that has served 53,000 young people in the last 25 years.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Gloria Goldstein; three daughters, Patricia Gutman of Manhattan, Sandra Schindler of Plainview, L.I., and Ilene Grossman of San Francisco; a brother, Louis, of Manhattan, and four granchildren.

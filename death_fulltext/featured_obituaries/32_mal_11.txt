        Marvin Gordon, a choreographer, teacher and dancer, died Thursday at St. Vincent's Hospital in New York City. The cause of death was a heart attack, his companion, William H. Hooks, said. Mr. Gordon was 59.
        Mr. Gordon was born in New York and trained in ballet with Antony Tudor and Margaret Craske, and in modern dance with Charles Weidman and Doris Humphrey. He performed on Broadway in ''Damn Yankees,'' ''Candide'' and ''First Impressions.'' He also choreographed and directed Off Broadway shows, industrial shows and for television and summer stock. Reviewers noted the fast pace of his direction.
        Mr. Gordon founded Ballet Concepts, a dance company, in 1965. He taught ballet and musical theater at his Manhattan studio, Dance Concepts.
        He is survived by his sister, Dorothy Rubin, of Forest Hills.

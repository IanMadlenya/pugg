
        Vincent P. Brennan, a retired executive of Bloomingdale's, died on Oct. 4 at his home in Bronxville, N.Y. He was 84.
        In nearly 40 years at the department store, Mr. Brennan held a variety of management positions in what is now commonly called human resources. In the 1950's he was director of personnel and labor relations, and in 1978 he was named senior executive vice president for operations and personnel. He retired in 1984.
        A native New Yorker, he received a degree from Fordham, served in the Army for five years and was discharged as a major.
        From 1985 to 1990 he was a vice president of Pace University.
        He is survived by his wife of 59 years, Mary McMorrow Brennan; two daughters, Mary Gerster, of Rye, N.Y. and Joan Hearst, of Montclair, N.J.; two sons, Vincent, of Vancouver, British Columbia, and David, of New York City; eight grandchildren; three great-grandchildren; and a brother, Donald, of Hazlet, N.J.

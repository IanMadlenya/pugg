        Dr. Arthur J. Antenucci, an internist, died of Hodgkin's disease Monday at St. Luke's-Roosevelt Hospital Center. He was 81 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Dr. Antenucci joined Roosevelt Hospital in 1934 and was chief of medicine there from 1957 to 1970.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Anna Ahlin; a son, Arthur, of Manhattan; three daughters, Karin Lovett of Durango, Colo., Nina Schoeller of Dallas and Frances Johnston of Lyme, N.H.; a brother, Edward, of Rochester; a sister, Margaret Ballard of Queens; 11 grandchildren, and a great-grandchild.

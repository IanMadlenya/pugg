
        Charles D. Miller, a retired executive of the hobby and modeling industry, died on Thursday at his home in Litchfield, Conn. He was 78.
        The cause of death was heart failure, according to Dick Ebersol, Mr. Miller's son-in-law, who is the president of NBC Sports.
        Mr. Miller was a president of the Hobby Industry Association from 1955 to 1957 and was the president and chief executive officer of the Testor Corporation in Rockford, Ill., a major manufacturer of model kits and toys and the materials to build them, from 1960 to 1974.
        Mr. Miller is survived by his wife, Constance Miller of Litchfield; two sons, Charles Jr. and David, both of Rockford; two daughters, the actress Susan Saint James Ebersol of Litchfield and Mary Mercedes Dewey of Roscoe, Ill., and 14 grandchildren.

        Lev N. Tolkunov, who was twice chief editor of the Government newspaper Izvestia, has died, the Soviet press agency Tass reported today. He was 70 years old.
        From 1984 to 1988, Mr. Tolkunov was chairman of the Soviet of the Union, one of two houses in the Soviet Parliament, which was reconstituted this year.
        After holding senior staff posts in the Central Committee, he became editor of Izvestia in 1965. From 1976 to 1983, he was chairman of the Novosti press features syndicate, then went back to the Izvestia post for a year before going to the Soviet of the Union.
        A prominent figure in the Soviet Communist Party for three decades, Mr. Tolkunov graduated from the Higher Party School of the Communist Party Central Committee. He began his career in 1938 working for the newspaper Pravda and became the paper's deputy chief editor. He was elected to the Central Committee in 1966.

        Louis Reshin Frumer, a former editor in chief of Matthew Bender & Company, a publisher of legal books, died of a heart attack Friday at his home in Manhattan. He was 69 years old.
        An expert on product liability and personal injury, Mr. Frumer was also a former professor at the law schools of Southern Methodist, Syracuse and New York Universities.
        Mr. Frumer was a native of Shreveport, La., and a graduate of the University of Texas, the University of Texas Law School and Harvard Law School. In World War II, he served in the Army Air Force in England and was awarded a Bronze Star.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Elaine Doret; a daughter, Nancy, of Dracut, Mass.; a son, John, of Boston, and a sister, Minnie Goodman of St. Louis.

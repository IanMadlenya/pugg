        Dean Chamberlin, a retired Time Inc. executive, died of cancer on Tuesday in a nursing home in Sarasota, Fla. He was 82 years old and lived in Sarasota.
        Mr. Chamberlin, a certified public accountant, joined Life magazine in 1937 as an assistant to the general manager. After service in World War II, he became business manager for Time-Life International. In 1948 he became business manager for the circulation department, a post he held until he retired in 1954. He moved to Sarasota from New York 12 years ago.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Edith Juell; a daughter, Martha Hunter, of Chevy Chase, Md.; two stepdaughters, Jennifer Griffith, of San Franciso, and Nancy Griffith, of Manhattan; two grandchildren, and four sisters.

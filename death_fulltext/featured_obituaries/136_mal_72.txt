
        Don Wharton, an editor and a prolific writer of magazine articles, died on May 6 at his home in Manhattan. He was 92.
        Mr. Wharton's byline was familiar to readers of Look, The Saturday Evening Post, Collier's, Cosmopolitan, Reader's Digest and other magazines for nearly 50 years. He was also the author of ''The Roosevelt Omnibus'' (1934), an illustrated work about Franklin D. Roosevelt.
        Mr. Wharton, whose full name was Lacy Donnell Wharton Jr., was born in Smithfield, N.C., and went to work for The Greensboro (N.C.) Daily News after completing college. In 1929, he joined the staff of The New York Herald Tribune and from 1932 to 1935 worked for The New Yorker.
        He also served as literary editor and staff writer for Today magazine, associate editor of Scribner's magazine and roving editor for Reader's Digest, a position he held from 1958 until his retirement in 1976.
        He is survived by two daughters, Margaret W. Segalas and Julia W. Douglas, five grandchildren and three great-grandchildren.

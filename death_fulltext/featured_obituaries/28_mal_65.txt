        Dan Kahn, an advertising writer and columnist for Newsday, died of cancer Sunday at his home in Rockville Centre, L.I. He was 56 years old.
        Mr. Kahn covered marketing and advertising for about six years and had written his column for the Monday business section for the last two years. He was a graduate of City College and joined Newsday in 1961.
        He is survived by his wife, Dora Potter; three daughters from a previous marriage, Deborah, of Rego Park, Queens; Dianna, of San Francisco, and Dannette, of Mineola, L.I.; two stepsons, Van Potter and Steven Potter of Sea Cliff, L.I.; his parents, Ralph and Tillie Kahn of San Jose, Calif.; a brother, Joel, of Centerport, L.I., and a sister, Judith Sadoff of Palo Alto, Calif.

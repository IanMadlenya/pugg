        Albert Kreindler, a real estate lawyer and former member of the New York City Taxi and Limousine Commission, died Thursday at Mount Sinai Hospital after a long illness. He was 59 years old and lived in the Riverdale section of the Bronx.
        Mr. Kreindler, a graduate of the Brooklyn Law School, began to practice real estate law in 1957 and served on the Taxi and Limousine Commission from 1975 to 1979. For the past year, he also served as an administrative judge for the city's Parking Violations Bureau.
        He is survived by two sons, William of Philadelphia, and Richard of Manhattan; his father, David of Miami; and two sisters, Lois Greene of Floral Park, N.J., and Edith Abramowitz of East Norwich, L.I.

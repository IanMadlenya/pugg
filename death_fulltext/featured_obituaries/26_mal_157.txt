        Waldo Hutchins Jr., a lawyer who practiced in Manhattan for more than 30 years died Monday at his home in Glen Head, L.I. He was 93 years old.
        A graduate of Yale University in 1917 and of Columbia Law School in 1922, Mr. Hutchins joined his father's law firm, A. S. & W. Hutchins. He closed the firm in the mid-1950's and retired soon after. He was a former trustee of the Manhattan Savings Bank, a former director of Crum & Foster, the U.S. Fire Insurance Company, the North River Insurance Company, and the Community Hospital at Glen Cove.
        He was also a member of the Mayflower Society and a former member of the governing committee of the Brooklyn Museum. During World War I, he served as a lieutenant with the American forces in France.
        He is survived by two sons, Waldo 3d, of Glen Head, and John, of Fruita, Colo.; eight grandchildren and five great-grandchildren.

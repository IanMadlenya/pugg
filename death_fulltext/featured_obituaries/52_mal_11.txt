
        Robert K. Pfister, former vice chairman of Sterling Drug Inc., died last Wednesday at his home in Delray Beach, Fla. He was 72 years old and also lived in Short Hills, N.J.
        He died of liver failure, a son, Greg, said.
        Mr. Pfister, a certified public accountant, joined the company in 1947 as a tax accountant after working for Price Waterhouse. He was named manager of the tax department in 1952 and rose to become a corporate vice president and director in 1966. In 1971, he was elected executive vice president for finance and became vice chairman in 1974. He retired in 1978 after 31 years with the company but continued as a member of the board until 1983.
        He was a director of American States Insurance Company and Dentsply International, a maker of dental supplies. He was a past president of the Drug, Chemical and Allied Trades Association and a former director and treasurer of the American Foundation for Pharmaceutical Education.
        He was born in Newark and graduated from New York University.
        He is survive by his wife, the former Doris Cane; three sons, Greg and Richard, both of Short Hills, and Jeffrey, of New Providence, N.J.; two daughters, Carol Hindel of St. Thomas, V.I., and Nancy Pfister of Delray Beach, and seven grandchildren.

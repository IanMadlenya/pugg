        Esmond Austin Van Name, who was in charge of planning, design and building operations for many years at the United Nations headquarters, died Friday at his home in Garden City, L.I. He was 79 years old.
        He was chief of building management services for the United Nations in the 1950's and 60's, overseeing the development of the Dag Hammarskjold Library, among other things. The architects were Harrison & Abramowitz. He was also involved as a consultant in the development of United Nations buildings in Geneva, Rome and other foreign cities. He retired from the U.N. in 1974.
        Mr. Van Name, who was born in Mount Vernon, N.Y., received a Bachelor of Science degree in architectural engineering from Pratt University in Brooklyn in 1932.
        He is survived by his wife, Nancy; two sons, Barry, of Queens, and Kerry, of Shoreham. L.I.; a daughter, Dorothy Lapine of Riverside, Conn.; a stepdaughter, Susan Mulhern of Los Angeles; six grandchildren and two stepgrandchildren.

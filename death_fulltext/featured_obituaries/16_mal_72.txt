        Gordon D. Sterling, chairman of the Formica Corporation, died after a heart attack Wednesday at Walkill (N.J.) Borough Hospital. He was 53 years old and lived in Vernon, N.J.
        Mr. Sterling began his career with the American Cyanamid Company in 1963, where he became president of Formica. He became chairman and chief executive of Formica in 1985, when it became an independent company. He relinquished the post of chief executive in February.
        Mr. Sterling was born in White Plains. He graduated from Hamilton College.
        Surviving are his wife, Nancy; a son, Jeffrey, of Vernon; two daughters, Pamela Hallak, of Clinton, N.Y., and Sandra Vesey, of Erial, N.J.; his father, Daniel, of Westwood, N.J., and a sister, Dori Bell, of Rochester.

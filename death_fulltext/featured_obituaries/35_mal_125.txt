        Simon Stein, a doctor who specialized in the treatment of ear, nose and throat ailments, died of a stroke on Saturday at the Ridge Terrace Health Care Center in Lantana, Fla. He was 93 years old and until recently lived in Boca Raton.
        Dr. Simon, a 1917 graduate of the Long Island College of Medicine, served in the Army Medical Corps before joining the staff of Beth Israel Hospital. He was the former chief of otolaryngology at Beth Israel and was active in educational charities. He was a founder of Friends of Hebrew University and a member of the New York University Institute of Hebrew Studies Scholarship Program.
        He is survived by a daughter, Ellen S. Ostreich, of Boca Raton; a son, Dr. Myron, of Amherst, Mass.; a brother, Dr. Nathan, of Miami; seven grandchildren, and seven great-grandchildren.

        Theodore Israel, a violist and music teacher, died of a ruptured aorta Thursday at New York Hospitaly. He was 60 years old and was a resident of Manhattan and Port Washington, L.I.
        A freelance and recording artist, he was a graduate of the Curtis Institute of Music in Philadelphia. His career included playing first viola with the Detroit and Washington Symphony Orchestras. He also toured the Soviet Union with the New York Philharmonic. He is survived by his wife, Dora; two daughters, Vivian Israel, a professional cellist, and Karen Israel, both of Port Washington, and two granddaughters.

        George A. Cornish, a former managing editor and executive editor of The New York Herald Tribune, died of a heart ailment yesterday at Saint Anthony's Hospital in St. Petersburg, Fla., where he lived. He was 87 years old.
        Mr. Cornish worked at The Herald Tribune for 37 years in several jobs, including Sunday editor, assistant managing editor, managing editor and executive editor until 1960. He also worked at Grolier Inc., a publishing house, where he was editor in chief of the Encyclopedia International.
        Mr. Cornish was a native of Demopolis, Ala. and a graduate of the University of Alabama, where he was elected to Phi Beta Kappa. He began his career as a reporter for The Birmingham (Ala.) Age-Herald.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Constance Brown; two sons, Edward S., of Bethesda, Md., and Robert M., of Southport, N.C.; a stepson, Douglas G. B. McGill of Rochester, Minn.; a sister, Elizabeth George of Demopolis; five grandchildren, and a great-grandson.

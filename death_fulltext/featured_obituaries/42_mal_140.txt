        Norbert A. Witt, former president of the Noxell Corporation, makers of Noxzema skin cream and other cosmetic products, died on Saturday in Palm Beach Gardens Hospital, Palm Beach, Fla. He was 81 years old and lived in Palm Beach.
        Mr. Witt died of the effects of a stroke he suffered on July 4, his daughter Carol said.
        Mr. Witt moved with his family to Baltimore in 1955 to join what was then called the Noxzema Chemical Company. He was named president in 1963. Under his leadership the company changed its name to Noxell and moved its headquarters to a building complex in Cockeysville, Md. He retired in 1973 and became vice chairman of the board, a post he held until 1985.
        He was also a director of the United States Fidelity and Guaranty Company, Maryland National Bank and Providence Savings Bank.
        Mr. Witt was born in Muskegon, Mich., and began his career in 1928 as a salesman for Procter & Gamble. He later moved to Lever Brothers where he worked in marketing jobs for 22 years.
        A widower, he is survived by two daughters, Carol MacPhail of Hunt Valley, Md., and Barbara Brown of Fort Myers, Fla.; a brother, Walter Witt of Muskegon; a sister, Geraldine Berry of Muskegon; five grandchildren, and one great-grandchild.

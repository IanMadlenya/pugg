        John H. Stamler, the Prosecutor in Union County, N.J., for 13 years, died of cancer on Sunday at his home in Scotch Plains, N.J. He was 51 years old.
        He received a heart transplant in 1988 and later developed cancer.
        Mr. Stamler started in the Prosecutor's office in 1967. He was director of the Narcotics Strike Force from 1972 to 1975 and after two years in private practice, was appointed Prosecutor by Gov. Brendan T. Byrne in 1977. Gov. Thomas H. Kean reappointed him in 1982 and again in 1988.
        Mr. Stamler was an alumnus of the Vanderbilt University Law School and was president of the County Prosecutors Association of New Jersey.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Jeanne Martin; a daughter, Elizabeth; a son, Dennis; three sons from a prior marriage, Stephen, of Berkeley Heights, N.J., Richard, of Scotch Plains, and Gary, of Edison, N.J., and two sisters, Nancy, of Middletown, Conn., and Jane Pallenberg of Clinton, Conn.

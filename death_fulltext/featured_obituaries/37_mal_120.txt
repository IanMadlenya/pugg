        Dr. Sanford S. Hammer, provost and dean of faculties at Hofstra University, died yesterday at North Shore University Hospital in Manhasset, L.I., after a long illness. He was 54 years old and lived in Melville, L.I.
        Dr. Hammer, a native of Brooklyn, was part of the Hofstra administration that helped advance the university's growth in the mid-1970's. He joined Hofstra as an assistant professor of engineering in 1966 and became a professor in 1975.
        From 1972 to 1974, he was chairman of the school's engineering and computer sciences department. He later became dean of continuing education, and in 1975 he was appointed executive director of student recruitment and financial aid.
        He was named executive dean for student services in 1976, acting provost and dean of faculties in 1981 and provost and dean of faculties in 1983.
        Earlier, Dr. Hammer was a consultant to various aerospace companies, including the Aerojet General Corporation and the Curtiss Wright Corporation. He also worked on projects sponsored by the National Aeronautics and Space Administration.
        He received bachelor's, master's and Ph.D. degrees from the Polytechnic Institute of Brooklyn.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Marcia Rainer; his mother, Gertrude, of Fort Lauderdale, Fla.; a sister, Carole, of Brooklyn; a son, Kenneth, of Cary, N.C., and a daughter, Michelle Latt of Westbury, L.I.

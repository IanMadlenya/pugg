        Leonard Zahn, a stockbroker and philanthropist, died of a heart attack on Tuesday at Good Samaritan Hospital in Palm Beach, Fla. He was 73 years old and had homes in Palm Beach and Kings Point, L.I.
        Mr. Zahn was a partner in Brukenfeld Mitchell & Company. At his death he was a vice president in the brokerage firm of Josephthal & Company. He was active in philanthropic causes, including the United Jewish Appeal-Federation of Jewish Philanthropies. He was also a member of the board at the Long Island Jewish Medical Center and was founder and chairman of the Beth Abraham Hospital Foundation.
        He is survived by his wife, Millicent; a son, Steven, of Tallahassee, Fla.; a daughter, Sandra Zahn-Oreck of Washington, and six grandchildren.


        Dr. Lawrence H. Clayman, a retired dentist who had taught at several universities, died on Thursday in Boynton Beach, Fla. He was 82 years old and lived in Great Neck, L.I.
        He drowned, his son-in-law, Dr. Jonathan Ferencz, said.
        From 1935 until 1983, Dr. Clayman had a practice in midtown Manhattan. He specialized in prosthodontics, the replacing of teeth.
        He was an attending dentist at the Montefiore Hospital and Medical Center from 1935 until the late 70's and was on the visiting faculty of the New York University College of Dentistry. Dr. Clayman had also taught at the University of Pennsylvania and Tufts and Emory Universities.
        He was born in Manhattan. Dr. Clayman and graduated from the Tufts Dental School in 1934. He was married in 1938 to Edna Levine, who died in 1979.
        Surviving are his companion, Joan Antonoff; a son, Stuart, of Brewster, N.Y.; two daughters, Maxine Ferencz of Chappaqua, N.Y., and Shari Clayman-Kerr of Brooklyn; five grandchildren, and three sisters, Sarah Claymore and Marjorie Cole of Palm Beach, Fla., and Jessie Rinsky of North Miami, Fla.

        Byron Francis West, a banker and securities broker, died of leukemia on March 2 at Memorial Sloan-Kettering Cancer Center in Manhattan. He was 61 years old and lived in Wycombe, Pa.
        Mr. West spent 26 years in commercial banking in the New York metropolitan region before founding the Manhattan investment firm of Diversified Financial Services in 1980.
        He is survived by his wife, Rosemary; three sons, Stephen, of Great Falls, Va., and David and Thomas, both of Sterling, Va.; a daughter, Anne, of Sterling; a sister, Dr. Jane W. Magill of Woodstock, N.Y., and two grandchildren.

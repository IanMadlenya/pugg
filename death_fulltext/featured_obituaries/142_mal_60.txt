
        Robert H. Whitstock, a retired vice president of the Seeing Eye organization in Morristown, N.J., which trains guide dogs and those who use them, died on Oct. 16 at the home of a daughter, Holly Seeger, in Palisades, N.Y. He was 68 and lived in Cortland, N.Y.
        He had a brain tumor, said his wife, Mary Jane.
        A native New Yorker, he was struck by a car and blinded when he was 7. He attended the New York Institute for the Education of the Blind and graduated in 1952 from Hamilton College in Clinton, N.Y., and from Harvard Law School in 1955.
        He joined the Seeing Eye's staff in Morristown in 1957 and became the organization's principal field representative, traveling widely in the United States and Canada and addressing many audiences.
        He went on to be vice president of field services in 1967 and vice president for programs in 1977. He retired in 1992.
        Besides his daughter Holly, and his wife of 44 years, Mary Jane Noonan Whitstock, he is survived by two other daughters, Ivy Bryan of Yardley, Pa., and Laurel Carfaro of Morristown; two sons, Robert, of Palisades, and Paul, of Bethesda, Md.; four sisters, Rita Milano of Manorville, N.Y., Edith Carthay of White Springs, Fla., Joan Cortes of Irvine, Calif., and Edna Murphy of Naples, Fla., and eight grandchildren.

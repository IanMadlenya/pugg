        C. Lincoln Crane Jr., a marine engineer and chief of stability and control for the Davidson Laboratory at the Stevens Institute of Technology in Hoboken, N.J., died at Southern Ocean County (N.J.) Hospital on Sept. 10 after a swimming accident. He was 58 years old and lived in Basking Ridge, N.J.
        Mr. Crane spent more than 30 years in research on ship design, operations and hydrodynamics for Exxon and for naval architectural concerns. He invented a special ice-breaking bow for oil tankers and developed the first computer mathematics model for early ship-handling simulators.
        He is survived by a daughter, Elizabeth, of Berkeley, Calif.; two sons, Toby, of Jersey City, and Thomas, of Madison, N.J., and his father, C. Lincoln, of Toms River, N.J.

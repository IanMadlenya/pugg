
        John K. Hutchens, an author, book critic and longtime judge of the Book of the Month Club, died on Saturday at the Westchester County Medical Center in Valhalla, N.Y. He was 89 and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Hutchens was a book reviewer at The New York Herald Tribune from 1948 to 1963, and was the editor of The New York Times Book Review from 1944 to 1946.
        As a critic and an editor of anthologies, Mr. Hutchens was a champion of modern literature. He was on committees for the Pulitzer Prize in fiction and was a judge for the Book of the Month Club from 1963 to 1988.
        In 1964, Mr. Hutchens wrote "One Man's Montana: An Informal Portrait of a State," a memoir about growing up. He was born in Chicago in 1905. He graduated from Hamilton College in 1926, and began his journalism career as a reporter on The Daily Missoulian and Sentinel in Missoula, Mont.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Ruth Brine, of Manhattan; a daughter, Anne McCormick, also of Manhattan; a son, Timothy, of Washington; two grandchildren, and five step-children, Janet Billingsley, Brigid Gollogly and Kevin Brine of Manhattan, Peter Brophy of Chappaqua, N.Y., and Duncan Brine of Pawling, N.Y.

        Raymond Dier, a retired professor of finance at Rutgers University and a retired Methodist minister, died of a heart attack Sunday at Overlook Hospital in Summit, N.J. He was 71 years old and lived in Summit.
        Dr. Dier, who earned a Ph.D. and a law degree from New York University and was a graduate of Union Theological Seminary, taught an investments course at the Graduate School of Management at Rutgers for almost 30 years. He had been pastor of the Janes United Methodist Church and the Ocean Parkway Methodist Church in Brooklyn.
        He is survived by his wife, Dorothea, and a brother, Robert, of Southold, L.I.

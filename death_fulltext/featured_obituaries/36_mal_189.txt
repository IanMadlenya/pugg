        Dr. Francis Place Coombs, an educator and dermatologist, died of cancer Sunday at Community Hospital in Glen Cove, L.I. He was 74 years old and lived in Old Westbury, L.I.
        He was with the New York Hospital-Cornell Medical Center as a physician and teacher for more than 25 years. He was also with Community Hospital in Glen Cove and with the Memorial Sloan-Kettering Cancer Center in Manhattan. Previously, he worked for several hospitals in Brooklyn.
        Dr. Coombs, who was trained as a physician at Cornell Medical College, served in the Army in World War II. In 1948, he attended the University of Pennsylvania, specializing in dermatology.
        He is survived by his wife, Amelia; a son, Jeff, of Golden, Colo.; a stepdaughter, Deborah Kuhnel of West Palm Beach, Fla.; two brothers, Merle, of Las Vegas, Nev., and Fred, of New London, N.H.; two sisters, Dorothy Mattia of Brewster, Mass., and Elizabeth Woodbury of Woodstock, Vt.; a granddaughter, and a stepgrandson.

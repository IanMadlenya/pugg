        Fernando Lay, First Counselor of the Italian Mission to the United Nations, died Sunday at Montefiore Medical Center in the Bronx, after a long illness. He was 51 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Lay, whose area of expertise was European Community problems and disarmament, became First Counselor in 1986.
        Mr. Lay was a member of the political department of Italy's Foreign Ministry from 1979 to 1986. From 1975 to 1979, he was part of the United Nations mission in Manhattan, and he was attached to the Italian Embassy in Ottawa from 1972 to 1975. From 1970 to 1972, he was a member of the economic department of the Foreign Ministry.
        He was born in Sassari, Sardinia, and received a law degree from the University of Sassari.
        Mr. Lay is survived by his wife, the former Maria Gabriella Cinellu, and a daughter, Genziana Lay of Manhattan.

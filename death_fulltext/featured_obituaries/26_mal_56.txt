        Alexander Bookstaver, a retired banker and financial consultant to unions, died of a liver ailment Tuesday at his home in Miami Beach. He was 77 years old.
        Mr. Bookstaver, a graduate of the American Institute of Banking, was controller and director of investments for the International Ladies Garment Workers Union from 1956 to 1961 and was director of the investment department of the American Federation of Labor and Congress of Industrial Organziations in Washington from 1961 to 1968. Previously he was with the trust departments of Hanover Bank and Schroder Trust Company of New York City.
        From 1963 to 1968 he was also an adviser on housing and urban development for the Agency for International Development. In the early 1970's he headed a mortgage company in Miami.
        He is survived by a son, Richard, of Manhattan; three sisters, Gertrude Clair and Viola, both of Queens, and Louise Gilson, of Fort Lauderdale, Fla.' and one grandson .

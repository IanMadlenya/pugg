        Virginia Haviland, an author and retired director of the Children's Literature Center at the Library of Congress, died of a stroke at her home in Washington on Wednesday. She was 76 years old.
        As the center's first director, Miss Haviland helped organize it in 1963 and served as its head until she retired in 1981. Her life was devoted to raising 1981. Her life was devoted to raising awareness of high-quality children's literature, on which she lectured and She was the author or editor of more than two dozen books. She also wrote book reviews, and served on juries that judged children's books.
        Miss Haviland was born in Rochester. She graduated from Cornell University and worked at the Boston Public Library from 1934 to 1963. She was a past chairman of the Children's Library Association, and a member of the National Society of Women Geographers, PEN and the Authors Guild.
        She leaves no immediate survivors.

        John Miller Dickey, an architect who restored historic buildings in Pennsylvania and New Jersey, died yesterday at his home in Westtown, Pa. He was 79 years old. He died of liver cancer, his family said.  Mr. Dickey began his restoration work in the late 1960's, after he and a partner, William Price Jr., based in Media, Pa., had designed many buildings for the Pennsylvania educational system.
        An interest in historical research and genealogy led Mr. Dickey to restoration projects that included the Walnut Street Theater, the Athenaeum and the City Tavern in Philadelphia, the National Memorial Arch in Valley Forge (Pa.) National Historical Park, the Frelinghuysen House in Raritan, N.J., and the Morris Arboretum in Chestnut Hill, N.J.
        Mr. Dickey, a native of Chicago, was a graduate of Princeton University. He was a fellow of the American Institute of Architects and a founding member of the Philadelphia chapter of the Society of Architectural Historians.
        He is survived by two sons, Samuel, of Westtown, and John Jr., of Minneapolis; a daughter, Alice White, of Chapel Hill, N.C.; two brothers, Thomas, of Westport, Conn., and Parke, of Owasso, Okla,; a sister, Louise Davison, of Washington, and four grandsons.

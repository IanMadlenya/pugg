        Simon Gluckman, an accountant and an executive, died of cardiac arrest Friday at Benedectine Hospital in Kingston, N.Y. He was 81 years old and lived in Palm Beach, Fla., and Woodstock, N.Y.
        Mr. Gluckman, a founder of the accounting firm of S. P. Cooper & Company in New York, was also a founder of the Horizon Corporation, a developer in Arizona.
        He was born in Providence, R.I. Mr. Gluckman received his accounting degree from Columbia and a law degree from Fordham.
        Surviving are his wife, Evelyn; two daughters, Francine and Susan, both of Manhattan; a son, Harvey, of Montauk, L.I.; a sister, Lillian Myers of Providence; a brother, Murray, of Delray Beach, Fla., and two grandsons.

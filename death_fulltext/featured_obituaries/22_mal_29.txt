        Lawrence Matloff, executive vice president of Selfhelp Community Services, a New York City geriatric and home care agency, died of a heart attack Wednesday at his home in Flushing, Queens. He was 62 years old.
        He had worked for Selfhelp in a variety of positions since 1969. Previously, he had been executive director of the Y.M.-Y.W.H.A. of Greater Flushing.
        Mr. Matloff earned a bachelor's degree from New York University and a master's from Columbia University. He taught part-time at N.Y.U. Surviving are his wife, Hildegard; two daughters, Judith, of London and Susan, of Manhattan; his mother, Ida, of Flushing, and three brothers, Simon, of Whitestone, Queens, Maurice, of Bethesda, Md., and William, of Phoenix.

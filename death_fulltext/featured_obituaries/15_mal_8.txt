        Henry S. White, a longtime television and film executive, died of a heart attack Thursday at New York University Hospital. He was 80 years old and lived in Manhattan and Westhampton Beach, L.I.
        Mr. White was born in London, attended Columbia University and served in the Army Air Corps during World War II. In 1948, he helped found World Video, an early television production company that was later awarded the first Peabody Award for television drama.
        In the 1950's, Mr. White worked as an executive at CBS and Screen Gems Inc., and in 1960, he was appointed vice president and general manager of WNTA-TV in New York City, a commercial predecessor of WNET-TV, Channel 13. That year he was also elected president of the New York Chapter of the Academy of Television Arts and Sciences, succeeding Walter Cronkite.
        In 1967, he become president of Sagittarius Productions, one of the first companies to produce made-for-television movies, and in 1972, he won the Golden Palm award at the Cannes Film Festival as executive producer of the film ''Joe Hill.''
        Mr. White's wife, Elizabeth Fryar, died in 1973. He is survived by three daughters, Robin White Owen, Heather White and Hilary White Johnstone, all of Manhattan.
        A memorial service will be held Wednesday at 5 P.M. at the Frank E. Campbell Funeral Chapel, 1076 Madison Avenue, near 81st Street, in Manhattan.

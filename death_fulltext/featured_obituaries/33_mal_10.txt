        Amos J. Peaslee Jr., a lawyer and the Mayor of East Greenwich, N.J., died Friday at his home in Clarksboro, N.J. He was 66 years old and had suffered a heart attack in April that left him in a coma.
        Mr. Peaslee, a 1951 graduate of the University of Michigan law school, practiced law in Manhattan before and after serving as an assistant United States Attorney in the Southern District of Manhattan. Most recently, he was with Cresse, Carr, Peaslee & Thompson in Gloucester County, N.J. He was mayor of East Greenwich in 1975 and was elected again in 1988.
        He is survived by his wife, Barbara; a son, Amos 3d, of San Francisco; three daughters, Daphne, of Ocean City, N.J., Sharon, of Fairfield, Iowa, and Roxane Gardner, of Williamstown, N.J.; two sisters, Dorothy Xydis of Athens, and Lucy Dougall of Seattle; a brother, Richard, of Manhattan, and two grandchildren.

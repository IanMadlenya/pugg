
        The Rev. Kenneth S. Dannenhauer, a retired Baptist minister, died suddenly on Friday in Manhattan. He was 73 and lived in Manhattan.
        His family said he died of arteriosclerotic cardiovascular disease while out for a walk.
        From 1978 until his retirement in 1984, Mr. Dannenhauer was pastor of the Madison Avenue Baptist Church on 31st Street, where he developed a number of community services. In cooperation with the First Moravian Church he established a homeless shelter and a meals program for the elderly, particularly residents of single-room-occupancy hotels. He was also a volunteer for the Gay Men's Health Crisis.
        A graduate of Bucknell University and Colgate Rochester Divinity School, he was ordained into the ministry in 1944 in Rochester. Before New York City, he had pastorates in Cranston, R.I., Warwick, R.I., and from 1958 until 1978 at Christ Congregation in Princeton, N.J. There he also served as Baptist chaplain of Princeton University.
        Mr. Dannenhauer is survived by his wife, Waka; his son, Mark of Boston, and one grandson.

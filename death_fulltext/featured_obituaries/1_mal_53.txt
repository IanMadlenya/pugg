        Dr. Leonardo Santamarina, professor emeritus of Spanish language and literature at Douglass College in New Brunswick, N.J., died Thursday at the Robert Wood Johnson University Hospital in New Brunswick. He was 74 years old and lived in Franklin Township, N.J.
        Dr. Santamarina taught at Douglass College, a division of Rutgers Unversity, from 1945 to 1981, when he retired. He was chairman of the Romance language department from 1961 to 1976.
        He was born in Fonsagrada in northwest Spain. During the Spanish Civil War he was an artillery captain in the Republican Army from 1937 to 1939. His studies at the University of Madrid, interrupted by the war, were completed in Cuba at the University of Havana, where he earned a doctorate. He came to the United States in 1945. Dr. Santamarina is survived by his wife, the former Alice Corrie, and four brothers, Enrique, of East Brunswick, N.J.; Mario, who lives in Venezuela, and Antonio and Benjamin, both of Spain.

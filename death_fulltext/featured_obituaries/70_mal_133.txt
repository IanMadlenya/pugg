
        Henry B. Keiser, a lawyer and businessman in Washington, died Monday at Suburban Hospital in Bethesda, Md. He was 65 years old and lived in Bethesda, Md.
        Mr. Keiser died of cancer, said his son, Matthew R. Keiser, of Washington.
        With his wife, Jessie W. Keiser, Mr. Keiser was founder and co-chairman of Federal Publications Inc., a publisher of newsletters, textbooks, reports and other information on government procurement and public contract law.
        In 1985 they sold the 25-year-old company to the Longman Group, P.L.C., of London and founded Keiser Enterprises. Its businesses include a construction management company, a limousine company and a marketing management firm.
        Mr. Keiser was born in New York City. He graduated from the University of Michigan in 1947 and the Harvard Law School in 1950. He served in the Air Force after law school and was a major in the Air Force Reserves.
        In addition to his son and his wife of 39 years, Mr. Keiser is survived by a daughter, Betsy Keiser Smith of Palm Beach Gardens, Fla., and a grandchild.

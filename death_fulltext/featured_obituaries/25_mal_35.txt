        Mark Silverman, a producer of independent films, died of complications from acquired immune deficiency syndrome on Friday at the Beth Israel Medical Center. He was 36 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Silverman helped produce several features that were both commercial and critical successes, including ''Raising Arizona'' and ''Blood Simple,'' which was shown at the 1984 New York Film Festival. He was also a production consultant for ''Crocodile Dundee.''
        Mr. Silverman was born in Detroit in 1952. Independent feature films provided Mr. Silverman with access to the motion picture industry after he earned a master's degree from the New York University Institute of Film and Television. At the time of his death, he was co-producing a Hollywood film for 20th Century-Fox. The movie, ''Miller's Crossing,'' is in production in New Orleans, directed by Joel and Ethan Coen.
        He is survived by his mother, Hilda Silverman of Detroit, and two sisters, Eileen Kleiman of Ann Arbor, Mich., and Pola Friedman of Detroit.

        Malcolm A. Love, who was president of San Diego State College for 19 years and oversaw its growth into a university, died on Saturday at Hillside Hospital after a long illness. He was 86 years old.
        In Mr. Love's tenure, enrollment increased by more than 20,000 students and research financing grew from zero to more than $6 million.
        In 1950 Mr. Love was named president at the University of Nevada, where he was responsible for establishing the Las Vegas campus.
        He came to San Diego in 1952. San Diego State College was granted university status in 1971, the year Mr. Love retired.
        He is survived by his wife, Maude; a daughter, Joan Maher of San Diego, two grandchildren and two great-grandchildren.

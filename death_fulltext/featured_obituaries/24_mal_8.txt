        Delmar Karlen, a professor emeritus of the New York University School of Law and a former director of its Institute of Judicial Administration, died of a heart attack Friday at his home in Williamsburg, Va. He was 76 years old.
        Professor Karlen was a faculty member of the N.Y.U. Law School from 1953 to 1977 and the Tazewell-Taylor Visiting Professor of Law at the Marshall-Wyte School of Law at the College of William & Mary from 1977 to 1981. He had previously taught law at the University of Wisconsin.
        The N.Y.U. institute he headed was founded in 1952 to improve the administration of court systems and offer classes to trial and appellate judges.
        He was a native of Chicago and earned his B.A. degree at the University of Wisconsin and his law degree at Columbia University. In World War II, he served in the Pacific and later rose to captain in the Judge Advocate General's Corps.
        Professor Karlen's writings included ''Cases and Materials on Trials and Appeals'' (1961), ''Appellate Courts in the United States and England'' (1963) and ''Anglo-American Criminal Justice'' (1967).
        His first wife, Alice, died in 1984. Surviving are his second wife, Adele; a son, Delmar Jr., of Staten Island; a daughter, Karen Dunn, of Cold Spring, N.Y., and a granddaughter.

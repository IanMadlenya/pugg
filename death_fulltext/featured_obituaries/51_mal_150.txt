
        Dr. Herbert I. Calman, an educator and dentist in Manhattan for more than 45 years, died yesterday at the Westchester Medical Center in Valhalla, N.Y. He was 77 years old and lived in Mamaroneck, N.Y.
        He died of heart failure, said his son, Michael.
        Dr. Calman graduated from the New York University College of Dentistry in 1939. After serving in the Navy in World War II, he opened his practice in Manhattan. He retired in 1990.
        He taught oral and maxilla facial surgery at the Albert Einstein College of Medicine-Bronx Municipal Hospital from 1955 to 1984. He was a member of numerous professional societies including the Midtown Dental society, of which he was a past president.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Louise L. Levey, his son, Michael, of Mamaroneck; a daughter, Wendy Lynne Calman of Bloomington, Ind.; a sister, Adele Bogard of Delray Beach, Fla., and one grandchild.


        Frank D. Visceglia, a commercial real-estate developer and an executive vice president of Federal Business Centers of Edison, N.J., died on Sunday at his home in Short Hills, N.J. He was 49 years old.
        He died of a heart attack, a family spokesman said.
        After graduating from the University of Notre Dame in 1964, he joined Federal Business Centers, a development company founded by his father in 1938. The company owns and manages more than 5 million square feet of office and industrial space at Raritan Center in Edison, as well as other industrial complexes in New Jersey.  Led Development Organization
        He was immediate past president of the National Association of Industrial and Office Parks, an organization with 7,400 members that seeks to promote responsible development initiatives, and was a founding director of the Building Owners and Managers Association of New Jersey. He was also secretary-treasurer of the Office Developers Association.
        Mr. Visceglia, who was active in civic affairs, was treasurer of the Raritan Bay Medical Center in Perth Amboy and served on the boards of the Perth Amboy and Old Bridge General Hospitals. He was also a trustee of the Delbarton School in Morristown, N.J.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Linda L. Jerzykowski; a son, Frank Jr., of Hoboken, N.J.; a daughter, Nicole Visceglia of Short Hills; his father, Frank, of Mantoloking, N.J.; two sisters, Rosemarie Sery of Greenwich, Conn., and Christine Markel of Villanova, Pa., and a brother, Peter, of Red Bank. N.J.

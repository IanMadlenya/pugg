
        Frederick S. Gilbert Jr., a banker and division executive of the Citibank Private Bank, died yesterday at Bellevue Hospital Center in Manhattan. He was 51 years old and lived in Darien, Conn.
        He died after suffering a heart attack while commuting to Manhattan from his home, a Citibank spokesman said.
        Mr. Gilbert joined Citibank in 1961 as a management trainee after graduating from Williams College. He held a variety of positions at theretail and corporate banking levels and later managed Citibank's middle-market lending activities.
        From 1978 to 1988 he headed Citibank's leveraged finance department before moving to its private capital division.
        He is survived by his wife, Margaret; three sons, Malcolm, of Cleveland, F. Christopher, and Douglas, both of Manhattan; two brothers, Timothy, of Lambertville, N.J., and J. Malcolm, of Wayland, Mass; a sister, Anne Kukulan of Piedmont, Calif.; a stepbrother, John Watling of Greenwich, Conn., and a stepsister, Christine Watling Paddock of San Diego.

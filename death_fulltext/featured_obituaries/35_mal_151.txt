        Ronald D. Pacchiana, a construction executive in Westchester County, died last Friday in the crash of a twin-engine Piper Navajo near Cairo, N.Y. He was 56 years old and lived in Pound Ridge, N.Y.
        Mr. Pacchiana, a 1954 graduate of Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute, received an M.B.A. from Iona College in 1972. He, the pilot and four other passengers were killed while returning to the Orange County Airport from a business meeting in the Glens Falls area.
        He was the founder and president of the Briar Construction Corporation in Peekskill, which builds highways, bridges, tunnels and sewage treatment plants. He was also the owner of Unicorn Industries, a Peekskill construction company that specializes in residential and commercial buildings.
        He was a member of the New York State Society of Professional Engineers and a former president of its Westchester County chapter.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Jeanne Pitta, and a daughter, Katherine, both of Pound Ridge; two sons, Douglas, of Laguna Niguel, Calif., and David, of Peekskill; his parents, Anselm and Marie Pacchiana of Briarcliff Manor, N.Y.; two brothers, Ernest, of Briarcliff Manor, and Paul, of Waccabuc, N.Y., and one grandchild.

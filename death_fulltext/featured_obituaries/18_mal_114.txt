        Dr. Ely Perlman, an allergist who issued daily pollen counts, died Wednesday of respiratory failure at North Shore University Hospital in Manhasset, L.I. He was 74 years old and lived in Roslyn, L.I.
        As physician-in-charge of allergies at Long Island Jewish Medical Center, Dr. Perlman helped make public the hospital's daily pollen readings and offered advice on how to remain more comfortable during summer days when the pollen count peaked. His information was published in newspapers and broadcast on radio and television in the New York metropolitan region.
        Born in Manhattan, he graduated from Columbia College and New York University. He had a private practice in allergies and immunology in Roslyn.
        He is survived by his wife, Norma; a daughter, Janice of Manhattan; a son, Andrew, of Palo Alto, Calif.; a brother, Samuel of Marlboro, N.J.; a sister, Joan Petteys of Gansevoort, N.Y., and one grandchild.

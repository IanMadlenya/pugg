
        L'Huillier S. Sheaff, a former chairman of Cushman & Wakefield, a real estate services firm, died on Tuesday at his home in Riverside, Conn. He was 93.
        During Mr. Sheaff's career as an executive, Cushman & Wakefield grew from a New York City property management specialist to a national real estate firm. He  started at Cushman & Wakefield as an appraiser in 1923. In the 1950's and 1960's, he was a leasing representative for many of the city's newest commercial office buildings, including, in 1956, the 38-story Seagram Building at 375 Park  Avenue, between 52d and 53d Streets.
        After serving as president of Cushman & Wakefield for three years, Mr. Sheaff was elected chairman in 1960. He retired in 1970 in his 47th year at the  company.
        Born in Beacon, N.Y., he was a member of the Union League of New York City, the Riverside Yacht Club and the Greenwich Country Club of Greenwich, Conn.
        Mr. Sheaff is survived by a sister, Harriet Eckel of Queens Village, Queens;  two daughters, Barbara Bedell of Palm City, Fla., and Virginia Liddel of Riverside, Conn.; 6 grandchildren, and 11 great-grandchildren. His wife, Mabelle E. Trolliet, died in 1990.

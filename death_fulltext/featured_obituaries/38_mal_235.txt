        Dr. Arthur Lee Wooten Jr., a retired research chemist and educator, died of cancer on Thursday at his home in Durham, N.C. He was 67 years old.
        Dr. Wooten retired from the Reichhold Chemical Company in 1980 after 37 years. His most recent position was director of its corporate research center in Sterling Forest, N.Y. He had also been general manager of its New England division in Andover, Mass., and a research chemist and technical manager in Detroit.
        After retiring from Reichhold, Dr. Wooten became a professor at Mississippi State University. He retired from there in 1987 and was a consultant to the resin, plastics and chemical industries.
        He graduated from the University of Alabama in 1944 and received a Ph.D. in chemistry from the University of Michigan in 1948.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Gertrude Reid; a daughter, Janet, of Manhattan; three sons, John and David, both of Durham, and Arthur Lee 3d, of Manhattan, and one grandchild.

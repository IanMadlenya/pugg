
        Sidney S. Greenberg, a New York internist for nearly 50 years, died on Monday at Mount Sinai Medical Center. He was 78 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        He died of cancer, said his son, Peter.
        Dr. Greenberg was a pioneer in the treatment of alcoholism and drug addiction. He was a member of the New York State Medical Society Committee on Alcoholism and a trustee of the Alcoholism Council of Greater New York.
        He was on the staff at Bellevue Hospital and New York University Hospital. At his death, Dr. Greenberg was on the staff at Mount Sinai Hospital and was an assistant professor of clinical medicine at the New York University School of Medicine, where he joined the faculty in 1953.
        He graduated from Columbia University and received a degree from Downstate Medical Center in 1937. He trained in internal medicine at Montefiore Hospital in the Bronx and in psychiatry at Brooklyn State Hospital.
        Besides his son, a syndicated travel columnist who lives in Los Angeles, Dr. Greenberg is survived by his wife, Jill Warren, and a daughter, Karen Greenberg Rudman of Manhattan.

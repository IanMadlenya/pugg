        The Rev. Dr. John Oliver Nelson, an ordained Presbyterian minister who founded the Kirkridge Retreat and Study Center in Bangor, Pa., died of congestive heart failure on April 9 at a convalescent center in State College, Pa. He was 80 years old.
        Dr. Nelson, who lived at Kirkridge, founded the ecumenical retreat center in 1964 and was its director for 10 years. He also briefly operated a boarding school and alcholic rehabilitation center there.
        From 1950 to 1964 he was a professor of Christian vocation and director of field work at Yale Divinity School in New Haven.
        Before that he was director of the commission on ministry for the Federal Council of Churches of Christ and director of the department of life work of the Presbyterian Board of Christian Education.
        Pastor of Pittsburgh Church
        After graduating from Princeton University in 1930, he received a divinity degree from McCormick Theological Seminary in 1933 and a Ph.D. from Yale. Dr. Nelson was pastor of the Brentwood Church in Pittsburgh from 1935, when he was ordained, to 1940.
        He was a former chairman of the national council of the Fellowship of Reconciliation and of a study commission on vocations for the World Council of Churches. For many years he was a national director of the Young Men's Chrisitian Association.
        Dr. Nelson was the author or editor of more than a dozen books, including ''America Inherits Religion,'' ''Young Layman, Young Church,'' ''Work and Vocation'' and ''The Student Prayerbook.''
        He is survived by a son, Richard Nelson, of Honolulu; a sister, Margaret Hanson, of Rochester, and a brother, Wenley, of Gladwyn, Pa.

        Eric Bernard, chairman and chief executive officer of Ground Round Inc., a national restaurant chain, and a former president of the Holiday Inn chain, died suddenly Monday in a hotel in Delray, Fla., while traveling. He was 57 years old and lived in Boston and Palm Beach, Fla.
        An autopsy is being performed to determine the cause of death, said a spokesman for Ground Round.
        Mr. Bernard worked for more than 30 years experience in the hotel and restaurant industries.
        Before joining Ground Round Inc., a subsidiary of Hanson Industries in May 1987, he was the head of his own consulting firm, Eric Bernard Associates, in Palm Beach. Before that he was president of Holiday Inn for six years.
        Earlier he worked nine years as chief executive of the Grand Metropolitan Hotels in London, overseeing the operations of 200 hotel and 2,500 restaurants around the world.
        He was a graduate of The University of Paris, and had done graduate work in hotel administration in Paris.
        He is survived by his wife, Mei Ling.


        Jerome Francis Cantwell, an aerospace and military industry analyst, died on July 5 at his home in East Quogue, N.Y. He was 52.
        The cause was a brain tumor, said his wife, Josephine Cantwell.
        Mr. Cantwell, who spent his entire career in the aerospace and military industry, founded Lionheart Research, an investment analysis firm in Manhattan, in 1993. Before forming Lionheart, where he was president, he was a senior analyst at the First Manhattan Company beginning in 1984, and served as a vice president for equity research at Schroder & Company starting in 1987.
        Mr. Cantwell graduated from Virginia Polytechnic Institute in 1967 with a bachelor of science degree in physics, and earned a master's degree in business from Washington University, St. Louis, in 1983. After graduation, he was hired as a program manager/engineer at the Navy's missile center in Point Mugu, Calif. He spent six years with the General Dynamics Corporation in St. Louis, first as a divisional marketing director, starting in 1976, and then as director of business planning for the corporation, beginning in 1978.
        Mr. Cantwell is survived by his wife; his father, Fredrick, of East Rockaway, N.Y.; four brothers, Fredrick, Edward, Raymond and Christopher; seven nieces, and six nephews.


        Herbert R. Jordan, founder and president of the General Tire Company of New Jersey, died Wednesday at his home in Short Hills, N.J. He was 61 years old.
        He died of a heart attack, a spokesman for the family said.
        Mr. Jordan started the East Rutherford tire distributor in 1959 and remained its president until his death. Earlier he had been eastern regional manager for the General Tire and Rubber Company of Akron, Ohio.
        He was a trustee of St. Peter's College in Jersey City, from which he graduated.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Margaret Graf; a daughter, Rita Coffey of Short Hills; three sons, Herbert R. Jr., of South Orange, N.J.; John G., of Upper Montclair, N.J., and Justin, of Manhattan; two brothers, Raymond, of Simsbury, Conn., and Vincent, of Jersey City, and a granddaughter.

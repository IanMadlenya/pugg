        Joseph P. C. Mei, a former president of the Chinese Consolidated Benevolent Society, died of a heart attack on March 3 at his home in Chinatown. He was 76 years old.
        Mr. Mei was twice elected to two-year terms, in 1981 and 1985, as head of the society, the Chinese community's most venerable institution. The society serves as an umbrella organization for 60 Chinese groups in New York City. It also operates a school that trains American-born generations in the ways of their forebears.
        Shortly before his death, Mr. Mei received a distinguished service award from the Government of Taiwan for his work with Chinese in the United States.
        Mr. Mei, who was born in Canton and was educated in China, Japan and the United States, fought against the Japanese from 1937 to 1945. He served as a liaison between the American military and the Chinese Nationalist Army in World War II.
        When the Chinese Communists won control of mainland China in 1949, Mr. Mei went to Hong Kong where was in in the export-import business until coming to the United States in 1967.
        He is survived by his wife, Fung Mei; a son, Joseph, of Morgantown, W. Va.; four daughters, Vera Chan of San Francisco, Sarah Loh of New Rochelle, N.Y., May Chen of St. Louis, Evelyn Huang of Manhattan, and eight grandchildren.
        The funeral is to be held Monday at noon at the Ng Fook Funeral Home, 36 Mulberry Street.


        Rabbi Samuel Horowitz, retired spiritual leader of Temple Zion in Long Beach, L.I., died on Sunday at his home in Manhattan. He was 72 years old.
        He died of a heart attack, his son, Rabbi Harold Horowitz of Brooklyn, said.
        Rabbi Samuel Horowitz was associated with the Orthodox synagogue for over 30 years. He retired in 1982.
        He was born in Kassel, Germany, and grew up in Romania. After imprisonment in Siberia during World War II, Rabbi Horowitz came to the United States in 1949.
        He was a founder of the Hebrew Academy of Long Beach, a co-educational day school, and of the Mesivta of Long Beach, a Jewish high school for boys.
        In addition to his son, he is survived by his wife, the former Miriam Brum, and six grandchildren.


        Lewis Cass Ledyard 3d, a lawyer and horse breeder, died on Sunday at his home in West Grove, Pa. He was 79 years old.
        He died of heart failure, his son Michael said.
        Mr. Ledyard practiced law in Manhattan after graduating from Yale Law School. He served with the Royal Canadian Air Force during World War II and afterward became a lawyer with the Legal Aid Society of New York. In 1952, he moved to West Grove where he raised thoroughbreds and devoted time to painting.
        He is survived by his wife, Eve; three sons, Lewis 4th and Michael, both of Kennett Square, Pa., and John, of New London, Pa.; three daughters, Evelyn Cochran of Glyndon, Md., Wendy Jones of West Grove, Pa., and Eve Pell of Mill Valley, Calif., and 20 grandchildren.

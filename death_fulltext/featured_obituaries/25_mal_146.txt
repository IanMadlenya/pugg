        H. Lyman Stebbins, a retired stockbroker and a prominent Roman Catholic layman, died of emphysema Sunday at his home in New Rochelle, N.Y. He was 77 years old.
        Mr. Stebbins was born in Manhattan and graduated from Yale University. He became a managing partner in the securities firm of DeCoppet & Doremus. He retired in the early 1960's and devoted his time to Catholic lay activities.
        In 1951 Mr. Stebbins was a founder of Mount Savior Monastery, a Benedictine monastery in Elmira, N.Y. He was a founder and president of Catholics United for the Faith, an international organization of Catholic lay people dedicated to advancing the church's teachings. He was a former member of the board of Morality in Media and a former trustee of Magdalen College in Manchester, N.H.
        Surviving are his wife, Madeleine; a daughter, Victoria Wallop of Needham, Mass.; two sons, Timothy, of Delray Beach, Fla., and John, of Ventura, Calif.; a sister, Marion Heydt of Manhattan, and eight grandchildren.

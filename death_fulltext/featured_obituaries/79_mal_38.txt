
        Dr. Edward Harold Bergofsky, an expert in lung diseases and a founding member of the Department of Medicine at the State University of New York at Stony Brook, L.I., died on Friday at New York University Hospital. He was 66 and lived in Stony Brook.
        He died after a brief illness, his family said.
        Dr. Bergofsky was chief of pulmonary and critical-care medicine at Stony Brook as well as at the Northport Veterans Affairs Medical Center. He was appointed to both positions in 1974.
        A Baltimore native, he graduated from the University of Maryland in 1948. He also received his medical degree there in 1952. Before coming to Stony Brook in 1974, Dr. Bergofsky held teaching appointments at the College of Physicians and Surgeons, Columbia University, and at New York University School of Medicine, where he became a physiology professor in 1973.
        Dr. Bergofsky is survived by his wife, Rebecca, and a daughter, Lucy Bergofsky of Setauket, L.I.

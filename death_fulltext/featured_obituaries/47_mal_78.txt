
        Dr. Donald A. Buyske, founder and chairman of Somerset Pharmaceuticals of Denville, N.J., died on Saturday at his country home in Cuddebackville, N.Y. He was 63 years old and lived in West Palm Beach, Fla.
        He died of a heart attack, his family said.
        He founded Somerset in 1985 to market Eldepryl, a drug made in Hungary that is intended to slow the progression of Parkinson's disease. He had worked for several drug companies, including Johnson & Johnson, Warner-Lambert, Ayerst and Lederle.
        Dr. Buyske, who was born in Milwaukee, was a graduate of Drury College and held a doctorate in biochemistry from the University of Wisconsin. He taught at Duke University before entering the pharmaceutical industry.
        He is survived by three daughters, Donna Cattell-Gordon of Waynesboro, Va., Gail, of Manhattan, and Jo, of Cambridge, Mass.; a son, Steven, of Flemington, N.J.; three sisters, Betty Crabbe, Mary Jane Gregg and Lucille Seaton, all of Springfield, Mo.; two brothers, Richard, of Decatur, Ala., and William, of Troy, Mont., and a grandson.

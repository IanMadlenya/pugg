        Jay Doyle, a character actor who worked primarily in regional theaters, died June 2 in St. Vincent's Hospital. He was 59 years old.
        A hospital spokesman said Mr. Doyle died of liver failure.
        Mr. Doyle had worked with the McCarter Theater Company in Princeton, N.J., for the last 11 years. Until 1976, he was a member of the original American Theater Conservatory company in San Francisco. He also acted in Joshua Logan's production of ''Rip Van Winkle'' at the Kennedy Center. In New York, Mr. Doyle performed in ''Beyond the Horizon,'' ''My Kinsman, Major Molineux,'' ''Hamlet'' and, on Broadway in 1969, ''The Three Sisters.''
        There are no survivors.


        Frank G. Drummond, a retired New York real-estate executive, died on Monday at his home in Dumont, N.J. He was 81 years old.
        He died of Parkinson's disease, his family said.
        Mr. Drummond was vice president and treasurer of Brown, Harris, Stevens Inc., before he retired in 1985. Earlier he was treasurer of the Empire State Building Corporation.
        He was born in West New York, N.J., and graduate from Case Institute.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Kathryn Hangley; two sons, Jerry, of Dumont, and Greg, of Oakland, N.J.; a daughter, Marilyn Williamson of Manalapan, N.J.; two sisters, Gertrude Dwyer of West Palm Beach, Fla., and Jean Miller of Fort Lee, N.J., and 10 grandchldren.

        James V. Delaney, an assistant acquisitions director for New York City's Division of Real Property, died of cancer Wednesday at Cabrini Medical Center. He was 28 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Delaney bought several properties for the city over three years. He had been an intern with Justice Thomas B. Galligan of State Supreme Court and an aide in the city's consumer affairs and budget agencies.
        Mr. Delaney, a Manhattan native, earned a bachelor's degree at Fordham University and a law degree at St. John's University.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Amy Shaw, and his father, James J., also of Manhattan.

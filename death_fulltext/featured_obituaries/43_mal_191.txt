        Dr. Selvan Davison, a rheumatologist who was chief of the arthritis clinic at Mount Sinai Hospital and clinical professor of medicine emeritus at the Mount Sinai School of Medicine, died on Sunday at the hospital. He was 78 years old and lived in Bayside, Queens. He died of leukemia, his family said. Dr. Davison, who practiced for 45 years in Manhattan until shortly before his death, was a founder of the first major group health insurance plan in the East in 1946. He was a Bronx native, and began his medical career as an intern at Mt. Sinai in 1938 after graduating from the medical school of the University of Edinburgh. In World War II he was a major in the Army Medical Corps.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Mary Smykla; a daughter, Ann Rubin of Chappaqua, N.Y.; a son, David, of Merion, Pa.; a sister, Lillian Bluth of Brooklyn, and four grandchildren.

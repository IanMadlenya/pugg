
        Arthur R. Kreutz, a composer and professor of music, died on Monday at the Golden Years Retirement Home in Oxford, Miss. He was 84 years old.
        He died of cancer, his family said.
        Mr. Kreutz's compositions included the "Paul Bunyan" Suite, "Music for Symphony Orchestra" and "Dixieland Concerto" and were played by the New York Philharmonic, with which he appeared as a guest conductor.
        Mr. Kreutz was born in La Crosse, Wis., and received a Master of Arts from Columbia University. He also attended the Royal Conservatory in Ghent, Belgium. In 1940, he won the Prix de Roma from the American Academy in Rome and in 1944 was awarded a Guggenheim fellowship.
        The composer became a professor of music at the University of Mississippi in Oxford in 1951 and taught there until his retirement in 1965.
        He is survived by his wife, Zoe, of Oxford; three daughters, Prescilla Tietje of Seattle; Nancy Puckett of Wayne, Pa., and Mary Catherine Marrow of Philadelphia; a son, Joseph J. Schiller of Oxford; 12 grandchilden, and 6 great-grandchildren.

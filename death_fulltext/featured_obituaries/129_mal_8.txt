
        Frederick P. Haas, a lawyer who became general counsel for the Liggett Group, died after a long illness last Sunday at the Watrous Health Center in Madison, Conn. He was 85.
        Mr. Haas, a graduate of Yale Law School in 1938, served in the Navy from 1943 to 1945 as a lieutenant commander. He joined the law firm of Webster, Sheffield in New York City in 1946 and became a partner. In 1965 he joined the Liggett & Myers Tobacco Company, as it was known then, as general counsel. He was elected to the board and remained with the company until 1976.
        Mr. Haas lived in New York City and later moved to Darien, Conn., and Durham, N.C. From 1983 on he lived in Madison, Conn. He leaves his wife, Anne, of Madison, and three daughters: Suzanne Harned of Toledo, Ohio, Margaret, of Yarmouth, Me., and Harriet Levesque of Madison, and six grandchildren.


        John A. Baker, a vice president of the Atlantic Council and a retired Foreign Service officer who specialized in East European affairs, died on Tuesday at his home in Washington. He was 66.
        The cause was prostate cancer, his wife, Dr. Katherine Baker, said.
        A career Foreign Service officer, Mr. Baker served in the State Department from 1951 until his retirement in 1986. He served at posts in Belgrade, Moscow, Prague and Rome, was director of East European affairs, deputy assistant secretary for international organizations, and deputy assistant secretary for intelligence and research.
        From 1980 until his retirement, Mr. Baker taught at the National War College. He then joined the Atlantic Council of the United States, which promotes understanding of international issues and was its vice president for education and civil-military programs.
        Mr. Baker graduated from Yale University in 1949, and also received a doctorate in political science from American University. Before joining the State Department he was a second lieutenant in the army.
        Mr. Baker is survived by his wife of Washington; four sons, John A. 3d, of East Lansing, Mich.; Kendall, of Manhattan; Andrew, of Shelburne Falls, Mass., and Malcolm, of Brookline, Mass.; a sister, Carol Baker of Racine, Wisc., a brother, Patrick, of Keene, N.H., and five grandchildren.

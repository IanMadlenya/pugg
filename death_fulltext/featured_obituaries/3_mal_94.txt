        Alan S. Hays Sr., a prominent lawyer who specialized in international trade law for many years, died Sunday in Paris, where he had lived for several decades. He was 84 years old.
        A graduate of Harvard College and Harvard Law School, he practiced law in New York until after World War II, when he established his own firm, Cabinet Hays, in Paris. He was also a partner in the New York firm of Hays & Busby and was associated with the Washington firm of Busby, Rohm and Leonard.
        Surviving are his wife, Augusta, of Lausanne, Switzerland; a son, Alan S. 2d, of Washington; a daughter, Florence Erb of Atlanta, and four grandchildren.

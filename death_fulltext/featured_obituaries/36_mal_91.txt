        Richard P. Hayes, a theater arts teacher and drama critic, died after a heart attack on Monday in his Manhattan home. He was 60 years old.
        Mr. Hayes taught drama at Fordham, Hofstra and New York Universities and at Brooklyn and Hunter Colleges. He edited several theater anthologies, including ''Port Royal and Other Plays.'' Earlier, as a result of his work as a drama critic for Commonweal magazine, he received a Fulbright Fellowship for study in England.
        Surviving are his father, John A. Hayes, and his brother, Donald F. Hayes, both of New Rochelle, N.Y.

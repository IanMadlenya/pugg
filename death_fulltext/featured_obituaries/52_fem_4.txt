
        Pam Martin Sarnoff, an author and a volunteer in charitable causes, died on Saturday at her home in Manhattan. She was 61 years old.
        She died of colon cancer, a spokeswoman for the family said.
        Mrs. Sarnoff wrote "The Ultimate Spa Book" (Warner Books, 1990), as well as articles for travel magazines.
        She was a co-chairwoman of the Winter Antiques Show, held at the Seventh Regiment Armory at Park Avenue and 67th Street, from the mid-1970's until her death. She was a board member of the East Side Settlement House and the New York City Opera Guild and chairwoman of the benefit for the American Foundation for AIDS Research. She was also a literacy volunteer.
        She is survived by her husband, William, chairman of Time Warner Trade Publishing; two sons, Jeffrey and Richard, both of Manhattan, and a daughter, Nancy, also of Manhattan.

        Gerald L. Norman, a retired administrative judge for the Workmen's Compensation Board, died on Sunday at Downstate Medical Center in Brooklyn. He was 78 years old and lived in Brooklyn. He died of lung cancer, his sister, Winifred L. Norman, said. Mr. Norman served as a judge for the Workmen's Compensation Board for several years before he retired in 1980. In the 1970's, he was assistant deputy comptroller for New York City under Mayor Abraham D. Beame. He also maintained a private law practice in Brooklyn.
        He was born in Queens, graduated from Virginia State College and received a law degree from New York University. In recent years, he was part of a committee to save the Latimer House in Queens, the home of Lewis H. Latimer, an inventor and a scientist who was the only black member of Thomas Edison's staff and was also Mr. Norman's grandfather.
        A widower, he is survived by his sister, who lives in Manhattan.

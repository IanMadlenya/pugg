        Hattie Clark Rosenbaum, a longtime vice president at Columbia Artists Management and one of the first women to attain a leading role in the concert-management profession, died of a brain tumor Dec. 24 at the Columbia Presbyterian Medical Center in New York. She was 82 years old.
        Mrs. Rosenbaum was born and educated in New York. She went to work for the antecedent of the Columbia company, the Arthur Judson concert-management concern, as a secretary in the late 1920's and eventually shifted to publicity as an assistant to Dorle Soria.
        Survivors include a sister, Amy Blocher, of Springfield, Va.; a brother, Louis, of Westfield, N.J.; two sons, Arnold, of Marlboro, N.J., and Paul, of Wayland, N.J., and three grandchildren.
        Correction:  January 9, 1989, Monday, Late City Final Edition
        An obituary headline in some editions on Friday about one of the first women to become a leader in concert management misidentified her. She was Hattie Clark Rosenbaum.

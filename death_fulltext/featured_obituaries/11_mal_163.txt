        Oscar Dane, a lawyer who was prominent in the Jewish community, died Tuesday at his Manhattan home after a long illness. He was 84 years old.
        Born in Russia, he came to the United States in 1917. He graduated from New York University and the New York University Law School.
        After practicing law in Manhattan, Mr. Dane founded the Templetone Radio Manufacturing Corporation in Mystic, Conn., which manufactured radio communication equipment. In 1949, he founded Inland Credit Corporation, a finance company of which he was chairman. He retired from the company in 1983.
        He was a founder of two synagogues, Beth-El in New London, Conn., and the Conservative Synagogue of Riverdale, now Adath Israel of New York.
        He served on the boards of the Jewish Theological Seminary, Beth Israel Hospital, the Hebrew Home for the Aged in Riverdale and the Riverdale YW-YMHA.
        He is survived by his wife, Goody; three daughters, Gail Propp, Sharon Dane and Wilma Kule, all of Manhattan, and 11 grandchildren.

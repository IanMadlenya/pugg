
        Alexander W. Allport, a founder of the Green Guerillas, died on Oct. 15 in a Mount Vernon, N.Y., hospital. He was 78 and lived in Denver, N.Y.
        The cause was a heart attack, said a daughter, Susan Allport Howell.
        The Green Guerillas, founded in 1973, is a nonprofit group that helps preserve and enhance community gardens in New York City.
        Mr. Allport was active in a score of nonprofit organizations over the years. At different times, he was executive director of the National Association of Olmsted Parks, the Central Park Community Fund and the Horticultural Society of New York.
        He was also deputy parks commissioner for a time and a vice president of the Foreign Policy Association.
        He was born in Vienna, grew up in Europe and Bronxville, N.Y., and earned degrees from Harvard and New York University.
        Three marriages ended in divorce. He married Mary Lou Daniels in 1957 and she died in 1987.
        Besides his daughter Susan, of Katonah, N.Y., he is survived by a son, Chris, of Santa Monica, Calif.; two other daughters, Catherine Allport, of Santa Fe., N.M., and Faye Pettus, of Anniston, Ala.; and seven grandchildren.

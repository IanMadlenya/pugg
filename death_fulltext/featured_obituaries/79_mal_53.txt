
        Dr. Michel Robert Grimaud, professor of French at Wellesley College, died on Wednesday at his home in Wellesley, Mass. He was 47 years old.
        The cause was cancer, his family said.
        A native of Edinburgh, he was raised in Toulon, France. He attended the Universite de Provence in Aix before earning his master's and Ph.D. degrees at the University of Wisconsin.
        Dr. Grimaud moved to Wellesley 17 years ago. He served as director of Wellesley College's junior-year Wellesley-in-Aix program and, from 1988 until lat year, as chairman of the Department of French.
        He was the author of more than 80 articles and books and the co-founder and editor of Empirical Studies of the Arts. He also founded and edited Revue des Lettres Modernes: Serie Victor Hugo.
        Dr. Grimaud is survived by his wife of 20 years, Lisa Ayman Grimaud; a daughter, Sarah Grimaud, and a sister, Christiane Bain, a resident of France.

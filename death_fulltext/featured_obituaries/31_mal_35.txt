        George F. Mason Jr., a lawyer and president of Gunston Hall Inc., a California home building corporation, died of a heart attack Tuesday at St Joseph's Hospital in Orange, Calif. He was 65 years old and lived in Laguna Hills, Calif.
        Mr. Mason, a native of Brooklyn, had been corporate vice president and associate general counsel of W. R. Grace from 1959 to 1979. He acquired a real-estate company to be called Gunston Hall Inc., from W. R. Grace in a management buy-out. Earlier he had been a partner in the law firm of Coudert Brothers. He was a former trustee of Hanover Square Realty Investors, a Manhattan real-estate investment trust, and a consultant on real-estate development.
        During World War II, he studied physics and meteorology at Bowdoin College, Brunswick, Me. As a member of the Army Corps of Engineers he was assigned to the Manhattan Project where he acted as a radar specialist for the first atomic bomb test. He graduated magna cum laude from St. John's University Law School in 1948.
        A widower, he is survived by a son, Stephen of Laguna Hills; three daughters, Carol Harbison and Margaret Mason, also of Laguna Hills, Janet Mason of Irvine, Calif., and five grandchildren.

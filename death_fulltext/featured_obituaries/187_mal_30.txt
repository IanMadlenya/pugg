
        William R. Crawford, a retired American career diplomat and expert on the Middle East and Cyprus, died on Sunday at his home in Greensboro, Vt. He was 74 and also lived in Washington, D.C.
        He had cancer, his family said.
        In the 1970's, he was ambassador to Yemen and then to Cyprus and later became principal deputy assistant secretary of state for Near East and South Asian affairs.
        He became ambassador to Cyprus in 1974 after his predecessor, Rodger P. Davies, was assassinated there.
        From 1959 to 1964, Mr. Crawford was officer in charge of Arab-Israeli affairs at the State Department.
        William Rex Crawford Jr. was born in Philadelphia and held degrees from Harvard and the University of Pennsylvania.
        He married Virginia Vollrath Lowry in 1950. She died in 1987.
        He is survived by his wife, Celia Faulkner Crawford; a daughter, Sarah Crawford-Najafi; a grandchild; a brother, Dr. John Crawford; and two stepsons, Winthrop T. Clevenger and Peter R. Clevenger.

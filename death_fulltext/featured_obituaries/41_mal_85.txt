        Horace Havemeyer Jr., a former president of the National Sugar Refining Company, died yesterday at his home in Dix Hills, L.I. He was 75 years old. Mr. Havemeyer died of lung cancer, his son William said.
        Mr. Havemeyer was the fifth generation of his family to work in the sugar business. National Sugar, of which he was president from 1948 to 1968, became the second largest refiner in the country. The New York company was sold to private buyers in 1968.
        Mr. Havemeyer was a former board member of the New York Trust Company and a director of Amalgamated Sugar, a beet-sugar company in Ogden, Utah. He was also a board member of Huntington (L.I.) Hospital, of the East Woods School in Oyster Bay, L.I., and of Drew University in Madison, N.J.
        He was born in Islip, L.I., and graduated from Yale University in 1936.
        In recent years Mr. Havemeyer had been involved in grain farming and historic preservation in Chestertown, Md.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Rosalind Everdell; four children, Rosalind Roosevelt of Armonk, N.Y., William and Horace 3d, both of Manhattan, and Christian, of Chestertown; a sister, Adaline Rand, and a brother, Harry Havemeyer, both of Manhattan, and four grandchildren.

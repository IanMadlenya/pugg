        Hubert Weldon Lamb, professor emeritus of music at Wellesley College, died of a heart attack July 27 in Blue Hill (Me.) Memorial Hospital. He was 78 years old and lived in Santa Fe, N.M., and Little Deer Isle, Me.
        Mr. Lamb, who studied in Paris with Nadia Boulanger after graduating from Harvard University in 1930, began his association with Wellesley College in Massachusetts in 1935. He held the Hamilton C. MacDougal Chair of Music at Wellesley for many years before retiring in 1974 as professor emeritus.
        He is survived by his wife, Lydia Cazneau Lamb; two daughters, Margaret Weldon Lamb of Questa, N.M., and Lucy Lamb Letton of Charlestown, Mass.; a son, William Palfrey Lamb of Charlestown; a brother, Paul Chadwick Lamb of Englewood, N.J., and two grandchildren.

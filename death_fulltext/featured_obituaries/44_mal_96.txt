        Percy Buchanan, a retired investment banker and the former director of the research department at Morgan Stanley & Company died on Sept. 8 at St. Barnabas Medical Center in Livingston, N.J. He was 95 years old and lived in Maplewood, N.J.
        Mr. Buchanan's doctors said he died of pneumonia, said a spokeswoman for Morgan Stanley.
        Mr. Buchanan, a 1917 graduate of Princeton University, was affiliated with Morgan Stanley for more than 15 years and retired in 1961. He served with the Army in World War I and with the Army Air Corps in World War II.
        He was a member of the board of trustees of the Janet Memorial Home in Elizabeth, N.J.; the Pingry School, and the Eastern Union County Red Cross.
        He is survived by a son, Peter, of Madison, N.J., and two grandsons.

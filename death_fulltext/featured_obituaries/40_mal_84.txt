        Frank Bernarducci Sr., a retired advertising executive, died of cancer on Monday at the Columbia Presbyterian Medical Center in Manhattan. He was 65 years old and lived in Rego Park, Queens.
        Mr. Bernarducci, who attended the City College of New York and New York University, was with Doyle Dane Bernbach Inc., a Manhattan advertising agency, from 1960 to 1986. He served as a vice president and international media supervisor. The company is now known as DDB Needham Worldwide Advertising.
        Before that he was media director for the National Export Advertising Service and a space buyer for the Ted Bates agency. He was also a past president of the International Media Buyers Association.
        He is survived by his wife, Catherine; two sons, Frank Jr., of Manhattan and Gregory, of Elmhurst, Queens, and two sisters, Gloria Tedesco of Lauderhill, Fla., and Dea Riso of Brighton, Mich.

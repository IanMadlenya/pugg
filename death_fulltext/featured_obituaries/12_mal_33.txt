        Mark J. Rozance, deputy director of the Metropolitan Transportation Authority, died of leukemia Tuesday at the New York University Medical Center in Manhattan. He was 36 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Rozance was a marketing executive with the Port Authority of New York and New Jersey before joining the M.T.A. in 1982.
        He is survived by his wife, Ellyn; his parents, Vida and John Rozance, and a sister, Mary Beth Kolding, all of El Cerrito, Calif., and a brother, Jack, of Sacramento, Calif.

        Dr. Marvin M. Fein, an organic chemist and research executive, died on Tuesday at Overlook Hospital in Summit, N.J. He was 66 years old and lived in Westfield, N.J.
        His daughter, Carrie, said he died of cancer.
        In 1988, Dr. Fein founded the Corporation for the Application of Rutgers Research, with its headquarters in East Brunswick. The corporation, of which he was president, assists in the development and marketing of new technolgy.
        From 1970 to 1988, he was senior vice president of business development for Dart & Kraft Inc., formerly Dart Industries. From 1964 to 1970, he was technical director for dyestuffs and chemical products for the GAF Corporation.
        He was an adviser to the President's Committee on Science during the Nixon Administration and was active in the New York Academy of Sciences and other professional groups.
        Dr. Fein, who was born in Brooklyn, graduated from Brooklyn College and received a Ph.D. in organic chemistry from Purdue University.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Bernice Lederman; his daughter, Carrie, of Manhattan; a son, David, of Livingston, N.J., and two grandchildren.

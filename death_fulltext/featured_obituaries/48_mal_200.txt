
        Sali Vongkhamsao, a Deputy Prime Minister of Laos and a senior member of its governing Communist Party, died on Wednesday, the state radio has reported. He was 66 years old.
        Sali Vongkhamsao died at Hospital No. 1 in the Laotian capital, Vientiane, after several months of illness, the broadcast said, quoting a statement from the party's Central Committee. The cause of death was not specified.
        Sali Vongkhamsao was a member of the Politburo, the most powerful body of the Communist Party that has governed Laos since 1975. He was also the Minister of Economy, Planning and Finance and a member of the National Assembly.
        He was named Deputy Prime Minister in 1982, when he also became chairman of the State Planning Committee, an economic board.

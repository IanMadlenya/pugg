        Martin D. Jacobs, a retired United States magistrate, died Sunday at New York Hospital after a brief illness. He was 82 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Jacobs left private practice as an attorney in 1966 to become a pretrial examiner for the United States District Court for the Southern District of New York. He was appointed on May 17, 1971, and retired on May 17, 1979.
        He was born in 1904 in Manhattan and was graduated from Columbia College in 1925 and the Columbia Law School in 1928. He was editor of The Columbia Law Review.
        Mr. Jacobs is survived by his wife, Ada Eynon, and a son, Stephen, of Manhattan.

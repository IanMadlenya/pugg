
        Edward L. Schuman, a film and theater executive, died on Saturday at his daughter's home in Detroit. He was 79 and lived in Seattle.
        In the early 1950's, Mr. Schuman was director of the Film Society at the Detroit Institute of Arts. He was also part owner and chief operating officer of the Art Theater Guild, which operated movie theaters throughout the Middle West. From 1963 to 1974, he was senior vice president of the Walter Reade Theater Organization and served on its board from 1973 to 1983.
        Mr. Schuman either invested in or was a producer of a number of Broadway shows, including "Comedians," "Sleuth" and "Same Time, Next year." He was also a member of the film panel of the New York State Council on the Arts. From 1979 to 1985, as part owner and vice president of Quartet Films in New York City, he imported the Australian film "Breaker Morant" and other foreign movies.
        He is survived by his wife, Alix Cassaday; two daughters, Dr. Paula Schuman-Vielot of Detroit and Gwen Schuman Aten of Boulder, Colo., and six grandchildren.

        George W. Barton, founder of an international transportation planning firm, died Nov. 12 at his home in Sarasota, Fla., of a stroke. He was 81 years old.
        He founded Barton-Aschman Associates Inc. of Evanston, Ill., consultants to municipal and national governments, in 1958. In the late 1960's, Mr. Barton was transportation adviser to the White House.
        Born in Waverly, Ill., he earned a bachelor's and master's degree from Northwestern University.

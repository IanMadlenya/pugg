
        Simon R. Bregman, a partner from 1945 to 1990 in Bregman & Stolz, a firm that built shopping centers and buildings in the Bronx and Manhattan, died on Friday at Presbyterian Hospital in Manhattan. He was 94 and lived at the Hebrew Home for the Aged in the Bronx.
        The cause of death was pneumonia, said his wife, Ruth.
        Mr. Bregman's philanthropies included the Hebrew Home, of which he was a past president, and the Albert Einstein College of Medicine.
        In addition to his wife, he is survived by two sons, Myron of Wayne, N.J. and Arthur of Rye Brook, N.Y.; three daughters, Phyllis Olin of New Rochelle, N.Y., Susan Neumann of Boca Raton, Fla. and Myrna Back of Old Bridge, N.J.; 10 grandchildren, and four great-grandchildren.

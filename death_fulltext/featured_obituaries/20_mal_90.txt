        Bernard A. Confer, a former executive director of Lutheran World Relief, died of leukemia Saturday at his home in Teaneck, N.J. He was 74 years old.
        Mr. Confer joined the organization in 1946, after his discharge from the Army. He was named administrative secretary in 1948 and was executive director from 1953 until his retirement in 1981. He was honorary chairman of the American Council of Voluntary Agencies for Foreign Service.
        Mr. Confer was also president of the Teaneck Board of Education from 1962 to 1968.
        He graduated from Pennsylvania State University in 1934.
        Surviving are his wife, Hallie Baker Confer; a son, the Rev. David Confer of Ewing, N.J.; three daughters, Ruth Confer and Miriam Lynn, both of Teaneck, and Joyce Liberto of Manhattan, and five grandchildren.

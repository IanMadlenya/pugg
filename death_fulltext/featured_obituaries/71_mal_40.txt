
        Harold Kreiger, a lawyer in Jersey City for 60 years, died on Saturday at his home in Manhattan. He was 86.
        Mr. Kreiger died of a heart attack, said his wife, Stephanie.
        Mr. Kreiger, a senior member of the Jersey City law firm of Kreiger & Browde, was a Democratic Party stalwart in New Jersey for more than 50 years. In 1949 Mr. Kreiger was a chief adviser to John V. Kenny, whose election as Mayor of Jersey City destroyed Frank (Boss) Hague as a power in Hudson County and state Democratic politics.
        Mr. Kreiger was a municipal court judge in Jersey City, counsel to the Jersey City redevelopment agency and the parking authority and counsel to the Hudson County Democratic Committee.
        A graduate of Rutgers University and Rutgers Law School, he was a member of the Hudson County and New Jersey State bar associations, and of the Association of Trial Lawyers of America.
        In addition to his wife, Mr. Kreiger is survived by a daughter, Elaine, of San Diego, Calif.


        Dr. Carl Levenson, a physician who specialized in rehabilitation, died Tuesday at his home in Wyncote, Pa. He was 85 years old.
        He died of cancer, a funeral home representative said.
        Dr. Levenson was chief of rehabilitation medicine at the Veterans Administration Medical Center in Coatsville, Pa., from 1971 to 1983 and from 1979 to 1983 was also a clinical professor of rehabilitative medicine, psychiatry and human behavior at Jefferson Medical College in Phailadelphia.
        Before that, he taught at Temple University and at the Albert Einstein Medical Center in Philadelphia, where he was an attending physician and chairman of the department of physical medicine.
        A graduate of the State University of New York Medical School in Brooklyn, Dr. Levenson had also been medical director of the Moss Rehabilitation Hospital in Philadelphia. He was a founder of the American Board of Physical Medicine and Rehabilitation.
        He is survived by his wife, Beatrice; a son, Donald of Cherry Hill, N.J.; a stepdaughter, Margaret Levy Shapin of Highland Park, Ill.; a brother Milton of Manhattan; a sister, Lillian Moore Reavin of Kerhonkson, N.Y., and seven grandchildren.

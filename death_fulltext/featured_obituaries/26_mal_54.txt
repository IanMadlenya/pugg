        Robert L. Weiner, who began his career as a theatrical and television producer and film executive at age 21, died Saturday at Memorial Sloan-Kettering Cancer Center. He was 53 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Weiner died of acute kidney failure, said a friend, Carol Matheson Connell.
        While a Columbia College student in the 1950's, Mr. Weiner organized a syndicate that invested in plays. During the 1960's, he was the general manager and production supervisor for the Broadway productions of ''Spoon River,'' ''Joe Egg,'' ''Gantry'' ''Her First Roman'' and ''What Makes Sammy Run?''
        Mr. Weiner turned to television in the 1970's. He served as the producer for a number of television specials, including ''Spoon River'' and ''A Child's Christmas in Wales'' and ''Ailey Celebrates Ellington.'' In 1986 he was associate producer and talent coordinator for ''Robert Klein Time'' on the USA Network.
        Mr. Weiner wrote for many New York and national publications. For six years, under the byline Bob Weiner, he wrote a column for The SoHo Weekly News.
        There are no survivors.

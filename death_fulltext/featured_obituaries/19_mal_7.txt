        Joseph Costa, a veteran newspaper photographer, died of pnuemonia Monday at a convalescent home in Carmel, Calif. He was 84 years old and lived in Rockville Centre, L.I..
        He was a founder and the first president of the National Press Photographers Association.
        His career spanned 60 years and began on the New York Morning World. He also lectured and was a staff member of The Daily News and of King Features Syndicate.
        He is survived by his wife, Margaret; a daughter, Frances Peters of Rockville Centre, and four grandchildren.

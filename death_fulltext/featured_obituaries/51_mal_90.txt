
        A. Goodwin Cooke, a retired lawyer and yachtsman who served as president of the Destroyer Escort Captains Organization, died on Thursday at Whitney Center, a retirement community in Hamden, Conn. He was 86 years old and had moved to Whitney Center from Branford, Conn., about five years ago
        He died of Alzheimer's disease, said his son Goodwin, of Syracuse.
        Mr. Cooke joined the Manhattan law firm of Root, Clark, Buckner & Ballantyne in the early 1930's after graduating from Columbia University Law School. During World War II he was captain of the destroyer escort Cronin.
        In 1948, he joined International Telephone and Telegraph as European general counsel, and he retired in 1967 as director of Far Eastern operations.
        In addition to his son Goodwin, a former United States Ambassador to the Central African Republic who is vice president for international affairs at Syracuse University, he is survived by another son, Nathaniel, of Cleveland, director of manufacturing for the Loctite Company; a daughter, Sarah Burke of Manhattan, a social worker; seven grandchildren, and one great-granddaughter.

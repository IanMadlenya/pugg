        Maitland Lee Griggs, a retired carpet company executive, died of heart failure Thursday at Phelps Memorial Hospital in North Tarrytown, N.Y. He was 86 years old and lived in Ardsley-on-Hudson.
        Mr. Griggs was, in the 1940's, a vice president of Alexander Smith & Sons, a carpet manufacturer then in the Bronx. He retired in the early 1950's and devoted himself to civic causes, including the Hudson River Museum in Yonkers. He had attended the Christ Church college at Oxford University.
        He is survived by his wife, Margaret Dunham Griggs; two daughters, Faith G. Knox, of Cranford, N.J., and Hope G. Turner, of Weston, Conn.; a son, Maitland Jr., of Ardsley-on-Hudson; a stepdaughter, Margaret M. Wille, of Harpswell, Me.; two stepsons, Eugene A. McCain Jr., of Hawaii, Hawaii, and T. Ward McCain, of Kauai, Hawaii; 10 grandchildren, and six great-grandchildren.


        Franklin B. Pollock, a self-made businessman who rose to vice chairman of Dart Industries, died June 26 at his home in Holmby Hills, Calif. He was 95.
        Born in Brooklyn in 1903, Mr. Pollock began his career as a salesman for the Imperial Cork Company and later became chairman of the board. He moved to the Thatcher Glass Manufacturing Company, where he became president and chief executive. In 1966, Mr. Pollock merged Thatcher with the Rexall Drug and Chemical Company, which was led by Justin Dart, creating Dart Industries.
        A chairman of the Glass Container Association, Mr. Pollock was inducted into the Packaging Hall of Fame, and was a member of the board of several companies, including Western Union, Ward Foods, the Dorsey Corporation and the Kennedy Valve Company.
        A friend and supporter of Ronald Reagan, Mr. Pollock was a founding member of the Ground Floor Committee, which helped the former Governor Reagan to his Presidential election victory in 1980.
        Mr. Pollock is survived by his wife,  Elsie; a son, Jack, of Chattanooga, Tenn., and Jupiter, Fla.; a daughter, Penny, of Dallas; seven grandchildren, and eight great-grandchildren. Another son, Robert, died in 1995.

        Gregory R. McNab, who retired from General Motors in 1974 as director of distribution outside the United States, died of cancer on Tuesday at his home in Summit, N.J. He was 79 years old.
        Mr. McNab, a native of Summit, was a graduate of Franklin & Marshall College and the Rutgers Law School. In his 35-year career with General Motors, he held several posts abroad, beginning in 1949 when he became director of distribution in Chile. Subsequently he served in the Caribbean and in the Middle East, where he supervised the building of the G.M. plant in Iran in the early 1960's. He became director of worldwide distribution in 1965.
        He was also a former commissioner of the Summit Housing Authority.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Jane Poten; a son, Gregory Jr., of Exeter, R.I.; five daughters, Claire, of Summit, Teddy Taranto and Maggie Marshall of Westfield, N.J., Jeani Anderson of El Toro, Calif., and Ann Saretsky of Lexington, Ky.; a sister, Josephine Gifford of Summit, and 11 grandchildren.

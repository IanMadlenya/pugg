
        Benjamin Siegel, a novelist and short story writer, died Wednesday at his home. He was 76 years old and lived in Tuxedo, N.Y.
        He died of cancer, said his wife, Brook Lark Ing.
        Mr. Siegel's works included "The Principal," "Witch of Salem," "The Sword and the Promise," "A Kind of Justice," "Strangers, Healers" and "Adventures of Richard O'Boy."
        In addition to his wife, he is survived by a son, Matthew, of Boston; a stepson, Edward, of Ramsey, N.J., and a daughter, Joanna, of New York City.

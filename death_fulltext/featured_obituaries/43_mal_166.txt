        Thomas W. McMahon Jr., a former executive at the Chase Manhattan Bank, died yesterday at Union (N.J.) Hospital. He was 69 years old and lived in Union.
        He died of a heart attack, his wife, Margaret, said.
        Mr. McMahon was associated with the bank from 1947 until 1972, when he retired as executive vice president and chairman of the credit policy committee. In recent years, he was a self-employed financial consultant.
        Earlier in his career, he had been president and chief executive officer of the Sterling National Bank of New York and vice chairman and chief administrative officer of Security National Bank in Melville, L.I. From 1975 to 1977, he was chairman of United Utilities in Payson, Ariz.
        He was born in Newark and graduated from Rutgers University. He received a master's degree in finance from the Columbia University Graduate School of Business and was a doctoral candidate at the New York University Graduate School of Business Administration.
        During World War II he served in the Army as a technical sergeant in the Pacific.
        Mr. McMahon is survived by his wife, the former Margaret Strebinger.

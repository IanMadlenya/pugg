        James J. Reilly, a former personnel director of the New York City Housing Authority, died of kidney failure Saturday at New York Hospital. He was 78 years old and lived in Forest Hills Gardens, Queens.
        Mr. Reilly was a native of Brooklyn, a graduate of St. James Academy and St. John's University and a former teacher of mathematics and physics at the Bishop Loughlin Memorial High School. He also was a former president and director of the Forest Hills Gardens Corporation.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Kathleen Stapleton; three sons, James S., of Birmingham, Ala.; Richard M., of Boston, and Robert J., of Manhattan; a daughter, Karlene, of Forest Hills, and four grandchildren.

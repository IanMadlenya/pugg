
        Charles Morris, an executive of Lever Brothers for 35 years before he retired in 1971, died on Monday at the Nathan Miller Center for Nursing Care in White Plains. He was 80 years old and lived in Eastchester, N.Y.
        He died of congestive heart failure, his family said.
        Mr. Morris was born in Boston and was a graduate of Harvard College. He was coordinator of national sales accounts at Lever Brothers when he retired.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Alice White; two daughters, Jeanne Faup of Simsbury, Conn., and Carole Martin of Albany, Ga.; a brother, William, of Old Greenwich, Conn.; eight grandchildren, and eight great-grandchildren.

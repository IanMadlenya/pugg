
        Syl Veitch, a trainer whose horses won two Belmont Stakes, died on Wednesday  at Winthrop Hospital in Mineola, L.I., after a brief illness. He was 85.
        Mr. Veitch, who won the Belmont in 1947 with Phalanx and in 1951 with Counterpoint, was inducted into racing's Hall of Fame in 1971 after a career that spanned 37 qears. Among the top horses he trained, for the stables of C. V. Whitney and George D. Widener, were First Flight, Career Boy, What a Treat, Brave Lad and Cornish Prince.
        He is survived by a son, John, who is also a trainer.

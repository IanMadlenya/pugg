        Harry E. Mattin, a chemist, businessman and philanthropist, died of cancer yesterday at his home in Briarcliff Manor, N.Y. He was 92 years old.
        Mr. Mattin, who graduated from Cornell University, was the founder and chief executive of the Mearl Corporation, a manufacturer of pearlescent pigments used in automobile finishes and nail polish. Mearl is based in Ossining, N.Y.
        Mr. Mattin was a major contributor to Cornell. He financed a room at the university's Center of Performing Arts in his daughter's honor and established the Harry L. Mattin Fellowship in the School of Chemical Engineering in memory of his son.
        He is survived by his daughter, Helen, of Manhattan, a granddaughter and a great-granddaughter.

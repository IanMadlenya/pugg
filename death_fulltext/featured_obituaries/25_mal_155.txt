        Dr. Morris C. Finkel, a former assistant superintendent of the New York City public schools, died of pneumonia Monday at the Meridian nursing home in Silver Spring, Md. He was 82 years old and lived in Silver Spring.
        Dr. Finkel, who earned a master's degree in education from Columbia University and a doctorate in law from St. Lawrence University, joined the New York City school system in 1927.
        He was an elementary and high school teacher before becoming principal of Public School 33 in Manhattan, where he started one of the first all-day neighborhood schools. In 1957 he was named an assistant superintendent of schools. He retired in 1966.
        He is survived by his wife, Jan; a daughter, Robin Goodfriend of Silver Spring; four sisters, Anna Goldman, Gertrude Finkel, Tillie Frauenglass and Lilly Straus, all of Manhattan, and two grandchildren.

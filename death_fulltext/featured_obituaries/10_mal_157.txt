        Jerry Carlson, conductor of the Gay Men's Chorus of Los Angeles, died of complications from AIDS on Monday at his home here. He was 31 years old.
        Mr. Carlson became the group's principal conductor in 1981 after serving as assistant conductor. He was also its musical director. In 1980 he moved from Chicago, where he had been a co-founder of the Windy City Chorus and a charter member of the Chicago Gay Pride Band. He was also a co-founder of the Gay-Lesbian Association of Choruses.
        Mr. Carlson is survived by his parents, Elmer and Maxine Carlson.

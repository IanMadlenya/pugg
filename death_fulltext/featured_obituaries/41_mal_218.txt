        Julius Leventhal, founder and chairman of Leventhal Brothers & Company, clothing manufacturers in Manhasset, L.I., died on Wednesday at his home in Manhasset. He was 91 years old.
        Mr. Leventhal died of a heart condition, his wife said.
        Mr. Leventhal, founded the company with his two brothers in the late 1920's. He retired in 1986.  He was a past president of the East New York Young Men's Hebrew Association, and active in the Young Women's Hebrew Association, the Federation of Jewish Philanthropies and United Jewish Appeal.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Frances Rosenberg; a son, Robert, of Great Neck, L.I.; a brother, Harry, of Palm Beach, Fla.; four grandchildren, and two great-grandchildren.

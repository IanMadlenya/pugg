        James F. Corrigan 2d, an aviator, died of cancer Monday at Jess Parish Memorial Hospital in Titusville, Fla. He was 84 years old and lived in Titusville.
        Mr. Corrigan, a past president of the Long Island Early Fliers Club, began flying in 1931. He was a pilot with Pan American World Airways, the Air Transport Command, Consolidated Vultee Corporation and Aramco before retiring in 1970.
        Surviving are his wife, Ellen; a son, James 3d, of Smyrna, Tenn.; a daughter, Ruth Stanat, and two sisters, Majorie Miggins and Helen Fitzgerald, all of Manhattan, and eight grandchildren.

        Dr. Daniel C. Leicht, senior assistant vice president of the New York City Health and Hospitals Corporation, died after a long illness Sunday at his country home in Ellenville, N.Y. He was 40 years old and also lived in Manhattan.
        Dr. Leicht joined the Health and Hospitals Corporation in 1983 and developed the agency's AIDS initiative, which helps organize interdisciplinary teams of health professionals to provide medical and mental health care services to people with the disease.
        Before joining the city agency, Dr. Leicht had spent three years with the Postal Service, reorganizing its occupational health services for the Eastern region.
        After completing his training in internal medicine at Montefiore Medical Center in the Bronx in 1974, where he was one of the founding members of the center's residency program in social medicine, Dr. Leicht spent six years with the Valentine Lane Family Practice in Yonkers.
        Dr. Leicht was born in Racine, Wis., and graduated from the University of Wisconsin and its School of Medicine.
        He is survived by his parents, Margaret and Raymond Leicht of Racine and a brother, John, of Milwaukee.

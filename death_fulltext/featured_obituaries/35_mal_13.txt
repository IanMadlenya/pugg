        Ransom Harris Skeen, a retired executive of the Chase Manhattan Bank and Chase International Corporation, died of an aneurysm Thursday at Winter Park (Fla.) Memorial Hospital. He was 88 years old and had lived in Bronxville, N.Y., for many years before moving to Winter Park in 1987.
        Mr. Skeen joined Chase in 1926 and retired in 1968. He had been a director of the New York Association for the Blind, the Rockefeller Brothers Fund for Theological Education, World Brotherhood Inc., and the New York Citizens Budget Commission.
        He was a graduate of the University of Georgia and the Harvard School of Business Administration.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Elizabeth Towle, and four sisters, Rebekah Candler, Virginia Norton, Martha Gould and Elizabeth Dawsey, all of Winter Park.

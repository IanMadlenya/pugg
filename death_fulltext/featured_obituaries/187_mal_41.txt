
        Gary L. Klott, a journalist, syndicated tax columnist and founder of the Web site TaxPlanet.com, died Aug. 10 at his home in San Luis Obispo, Calif. He was 52.
        The cause was heart disease, said a brother, David L. Klott.
        Mr. Klott wrote widely syndicated columns on personal finance, real estate and tax laws. In 2000, he founded the Web site, which offers information about personal taxes.
        Mr. Klott was born on Oct. 4, 1949. He graduated from the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign and served in the Navy in Vietnam.
        He was a business writer for United Press International and then for The New York Times, covering the tax law changes of 1986 and writing the Tax Watch column. He also wrote three books, including ''The New York Times Complete Guide to Personal Investing'' (Times Books, 1987).
        He was president of the Society of American Business Editors and Writers in 1994-95.
        Besides his wife, Sandra J. Duerr, who is the executive editor of The San Luis Obispo Tribune, his survivors include a son, Alexander, and a daughter, Jacqueline.

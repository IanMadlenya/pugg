        Richard Corbin, an English teacher, editor and writer of textbooks, died of heart disease Wednesday at Peekskill (N.Y.) Community Hospital. He was 76 years old and lived in Peekskill.
        Mr. Corbin was chairman of the English department at Hunter College High School in Manhattan, where he taught from 1960 until his retirement in 1972. From 1937 to 1960, he taught at Peekskill High School, where he also headed the English department. He was president of the National Council of Teachers of English in 1965.
        He was born in Schenectady, N.Y. Mr. Corbin was a graduate of Colgate University and received a master's degree from Columbia. He wrote ''Guide to Modern English,'' a text published by Scott Foresman in 1955. He edited other standard works, including ''12 American Plays, 1920-1960,'' published by Scribner's, and ''Currents in Poetry,'' published by Macmillan.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Marjorie Kennedy; three sons, Jonathan, of Chestnut Ridge, N.Y., Geoffrey, of Chelsea, N.Y., and Christopher, of San Antonio, and four grandchildren.

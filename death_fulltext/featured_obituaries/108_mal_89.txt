
        Johnny Johnston, a popular big-band singer in the 1940's and 50's, died on Jan. 6 at his home in Cape Coral, Fla. He was 80.
        Mr. Johnston, who was born in St. Louis, began his career in small nightclubs and soon became a singer with Art Kassel and his band, Castles in the Air. He also appeared in many radio shows in the late 1930's.
        In 1942, he signed with Capitol Records, for which he recorded "That Old Black Magic," "I Don't Want to Walk Without You," "Dearly Beloved" and other songs. His recording of "Laura" sold more than a million copies.
        Mr. Johnston also appeared in several movie musicals, including "Star Spangled Rhythm" (1942), with Bing Crosby, Bob Hope and Veronica Lake, and "This Time for Keeps" (1947), which starred Esther Williams and Xavier Cugat. In 1951  he played a lead in a Broadway musical, "A Tree Grows in Brooklyn," opposite Shirley Booth.
        He came out of retirement in 1982 to sing at the Capitol Records 40th-anniversary party. He had been one of the first four artists signed by the  label, and his version of "That Old Black Magic" was one of the company's first  hits.
        Mr. Johnston was married six times, including a marriage to the actress and singer Kathryn Grayson from 1947 to 1951.
        He is survived by his wife of 20 years, Beverley; four daughters, Julie, of Los Angeles; Patti Towers of Woodland Hills, Calif.; Jamie, of Venice, Calif., and Jennifer, of New Orleans; a son, Denny, of La Quinta, Calif,, and six grandchildren.

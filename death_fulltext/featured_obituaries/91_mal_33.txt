
        Victor Pasche, the first secretary-treasurer of the American Newspaper Guild, died on July 28 in Seattle. He was 90.
        Mr. Pasche, who was a reporter for The New York Times from October 1929 until he took the full-time post with the newspaper union in 1934, served under Heywood Broun, the columnist who founded the union in 1933. Mr. Pasche was defeated for re-election on Oct. 15, 1941.
        He also worked for the United Electrical, Radio and Machine Workers Union and Meharry Medical College in Nashville before he retired in 1976.
        Mr. Pasche, who was born in Geneva, graduated from Yale in 1927.
        His wife, Bernice, died in 1984.
        Mr. Pasche is survived by a son, Nicholas, and a daughter, Jean, both of Seattle.


        Samuel L. Ornstein, executive director of the National Children's Center in Washington, died on Jan. 19. He was 69 and lived in Silver Spring, Md.
        He had a heart attack at the center, his family said.
        The center provides services to developmentally disabled infants, children and adults in the District of Columbia metropolitan area.
        He received an undergraduate degree from Ohio State University in 1949 and a Ph.D. in clinical psychology from the University of Florida at Gainesville in 1955. He began his career as a clinical psychologist at the Illinois Institute of Technology in Chicago.
        From 1965 to 1967, Dr. Ornstein was the chief of the Bureau of Mental Retardation in the Nevada Department of Health.
        He was born in Cleveland and served in the Army Air Forces in the South Pacific and Japan in World War II.
        Dr. Ornstein is survived by two sons, Seth, of Silver Spring, and Joel, of New York City; two sisters, Frances Fierman of Atlanta and Sally Glick of Columbus, Ohio, and two grandchildren.

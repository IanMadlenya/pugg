
        David E. Diener, for decades a major figure in film industry advertising, died of kidney failure on Monday at his home in Greenwich Village. He was 78.
        Mr. Diener served as president of the Diener Hauser advertising agency from 1964 to 1975.
        Known as the Monroe Greenthal Company before its purchase by Mr. Diener and a partner in 1964, the agency was a leading creator of advertising campaigns for Hollywood studios including Paramount Pictures, 20th Century Fox and United Artists.
        Mr. Diener also helped create advertising for the Rugoff and Walter Reade theater chains, which in the 1950's and 60's played key roles in introducing New York audiences to independent and foreign movies.
        He was also a founding member of Cinema 16, a New York film society that operated from 1947-63 and had a membership of almost 7,000.
        Mr. Diener was born in Queens and graduated from City College in 1940. He served as a captain in the United States Army during World War II and entered the advertising industry in 1947 as a copywriter for Columbia Pictures.
        Mr. Diener is survived by his wife, Esther Diener; three sons, Bart of Los Angeles, Jonathan of Albuquerque, N.M., and Mick of San Francisco; a brother, Edward, of Delray Beach, Fla.; a sister, Marcia Vogel, of New York, and two grandchildren.

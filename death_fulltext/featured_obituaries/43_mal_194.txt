        James E. Snyder, a professor and art historian, died Thursday at Mercy Hospital in Port Jervis, N.Y. He was 61 years old and lived in Sunrise Lake, Pa.
        He died of liver disease, said his daughter, Kim Snyder of Manhattan.
        A graduate of the University of Colorado and Princeton University, Dr. Snyder taught art history at the University of Michigan before joining the faculty at Bryn Mawr College in 1964. He was a professor of art history there until he retired in 1989, and was curator of Hobson Pittman House at Bryn Mawr from 1975 to 1988. He wrote numerous art history books, including ''Northern Renaissance Art'' (1985) and ''Medieval Art'' (1989), both published by Prentice Hall-Abrams.
        In addition to his daughter, he is survived by his mother, Martha Ritter Snyder of Peoria, Ill.


        Charles A. Willard, a Broadway theatrical producer and manager, died on Sunday in Lake Placid, N.Y., where he had a summer home. He was 48 years old.
        He died in a boating accident, said George Pappastavrou, a friend and coexecutor of the estate.
        In recent years, Mr. Willard managed "Lucifer's Child," "The Belle of Amherst" and "Driving Miss Daisy," all starring Julie Harris. He was the manager for several of Joseph Papp's New York Shakespeare Festival productions, including "The Pirates of Penzance" in New York and the road tours of "Two Gentlemen of Verona" and "A Chorus Line."
        Mr. Willard was born in New York City and received a Master of Fine Arts from Columbia University in 1968. Hi s professional apprenticeship began in 1970 with the Broadway mounting of Paul Sills's "Story Theater." He also did apprentice work with the Harold Prince Organization on its productions of "Fiddler on the Roof" and "Company." Supervising Plays
        In 1978, he joined the office of the producer Alexander H. Cohen, where he became the director of creative affairs. While there, he supervised and managed such plays as "Anna Christie" and "I Remember Mama," both starring Liv Ullmann, "Comedians" with Jonathan Pryce and "A Day in Hollywood/A Night in the Ukraine" with Priscilla Lopez and directed by Tommy Tune.
        In 1982, he became an assistant professor in the drama department at Carnegie-Mellon University, where he helped to establish its theater management program. He was also an adjunct professor of musical comedy at New York University from 1984 to 1988, and lectured at various colleges around the country. He was a member of the Association of Theatrical Press Agents and Managers and served as a three-term co-director of its apprentice program.
        He is survived by his mother, Eugenia Willard, and a sister, Margaret Willard Bailey, both of Manhattan.

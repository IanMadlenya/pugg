        Norman W. Lee, a retired shipping executive, died of leukemia last Wednesday at Fishermen's Memorial Hospital in Marathon, Fla. He was 76 years old and lived in Ramrod Key, Fla.
        Mr. Lee worked for the Farrell Lines of Manhattan for 43 years, retiring in 1977 as a vice president. He was a 1934 graduate of the New York State Merchant Marine Academy, now the Maritime College of the State University. He was a lieutenant commander in the Naval Reserve.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Lorna Jean Crider, a son, Norman, of Anchorage; a daughter, Caroline Rickard of Ridgewood, N.J.; a brother, Alan, of Wichita, Kan.; a sister, Alberta Feynman of Saratoga Springs, N.Y.; two stepsons, Leon Shal of Summerland Key, Fla., and George Shal of Blue Bell, Pa.; a stepdaughter, Lorna Wooten of Naples, Fla., and four grandchildren.

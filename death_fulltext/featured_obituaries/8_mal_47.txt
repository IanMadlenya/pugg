        Bennett Rose, a real-estate developer and a veteran of the Jewish Legion in Palestine during World War I, died of cancer Tuesday at the Connecticut Hospice in Branford. He was 89 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Rose, a native New Yorker and a longtime Zionist, served as a Jewish Legion member with the 39th Royal Fusiliers of British Army during the Middle East campaign.
        In the mid-1920's, he founded the National Tile and Marble Corporation, a contracting concern based in New York. During World War II, he built Liberty and Victory ships in association with Henry J. Kaiser. After the war, he became a builder and developer in New York and Houston.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Pauline Raden; a daughter, Judith R. Margulies of Purchase, N.Y., and a son, F. Peter, of Manhattan.

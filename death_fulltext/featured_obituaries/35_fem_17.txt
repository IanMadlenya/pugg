        Mary Rogers Brooks, the daughter of the humorist Will Rogers, died Wednesday after a brief illness. She was 76 years old.  Mrs. Brooks died at St. John's Hospital and Health Center from complications associated with cancer, a hospital spokeswoman, Paulette Weir, said.
        Mrs. Brooks performed in the 1933 movie ''My Weakness'' under the name Mary Howard and later in the 1930's under the name Mary Rogers in the Broadway plays ''On to Fortune'' and ''Crime Marches On.''
        She is survived by two brothers, Will Rogers Jr. of Tubac, Ariz., and James B. Rogers of Bakersfield, Calif.

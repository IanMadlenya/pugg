        Frank X. Gargiulo, a former member of the New York City Civil Service Commission, died Thursday of a brain tumor at the St. Rose of Lima Home in Manhattan. He was 60 years old and lived in the Carroll Gardens section of Brooklyn.
        Mr. Gargiulo gave up his post as a Republican district leader in Brooklyn when he was appointed to the commission, a post he held from 1982 until 1989. Earlier, he had been on the city's Board of Elections.
        At the time of his death, Mr. Gargiulo was president of Abstract Enterprise, a title-insurance agency, and headed his own firm, Valiant Brokerage.
        He is survived by his father, Joseph Ryder Gargiulo; his wife, Christina Bartolomeo; a son, Frank Joseph, and a daughter, Christina Marie, all of Brooklyn; a brother, Joseph, of Ozone Park, Queens; three sisters, Marie Brandt of Belrose, L.I., and Theresa Conwell and Louise Benenati of Deer Park, L.I.

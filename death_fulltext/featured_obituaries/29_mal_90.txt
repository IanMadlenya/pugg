        Anson James McArthur, a New York architect who oversaw the development of the Madison Square Garden-Pennsylvania Station complex, died of lung cancer Sunday at his home in Westport, Conn. He was 65 years old.
        Mr. McArthur was associated with Charles Luckman Associates from 1951 until 1973. In the early 1960's, he became the architect in charge of the Madison Square Garden development. For the last 10 years he was director of design and construction for the GTE Corporation in Stamford, Conn.
        He was born in Los Angeles and served in the Navy for six years during World War II. He studied architecture at the University of California at Los Angeles and the University of Southern California.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Beverly Woodcock; his father, Anson McArthur of Los Angeles; a daughter, Carrie Moody of Darien, Conn.; a son, Gordon, of Vineyard Haven, Mass.; a brother, Charles, of Los Angeles, and two grandchildren.

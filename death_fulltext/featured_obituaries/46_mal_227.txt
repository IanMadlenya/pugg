
        Roger C. Harvey, an advertising executive and producer of television commercials, including the Timex "takes a licking and keeps on ticking" ads, died on Sunday in Plantation, Fla., where he lived. He was 61 years old.
        He died of multiple myeloma, his family said.
        In the 1960's Mr. Harvey produced "The Bell Telephone Hour," a Sunday night variety show. He won several awards for advertising campaigns, including the Timex series narrated by John Cameron Swayze.
        Mr. Harvey, who was born in Asbury Park, N.J., and was a graduate of the University of Virginia, began his advertising career with N. W. Ayer & Sons in New York. In 1970 he moved to Florida and became head of a television production facility there.
        He is survived by his mother, Evelyn, of Pompano Beach, Fla.; his wife, the former Tyler Schaefer; a daughter, Hattie, of Plantation, and a son, Roger Jr., of Richmond.


        Mark Gault, a longtime executive at McCann-Erickson Worldwide Advertising, part of the Interpublic Group of Companies, died on May 24 at his home in Woodbury, Conn. He was 45.
        The cause was a heart attack, the agency said.
        Mr. Gault, a native of England, started in 1980 as a management trainee in the agency's London office, becoming head of the H. K. McCann unit in London and later director for worldwide accounts. He moved to Tokyo in 1996 to join McCann-Erickson Japan, the largest Western advertising agency in the country, and two years later was named chairman there and director of the Asia-Pacific region.
        Mr. Gault came to New York in 2000 as worldwide executive vice president and director for worldwide accounts. In 2001 he was appointed executive vice president and regional director for North America.
        Mark William Gault was born on Sept. 9, 1957, in Stockton-on-Tees, England. He earned a Master of Arts from Cambridge University.
        He is survived by his wife, Susie; two sons, Harry and Jack; his parents, Phyllis and Jim; and his brothers, Philip and Hugh.

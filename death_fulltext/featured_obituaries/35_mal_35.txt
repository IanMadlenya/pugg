        Richard R. Salzmann, the retired editor in chief of the Research Institute of America, a publisher of tax guides, died of a heart ailment on Saturday at his home in Santa Fe, N.M. He was 69 years old and had lived for many years in Pound Ridge, N.Y.
        Mr. Salzmann was a director and former senior vice president of the International Rescue Committee for more than 40 years and had aided many refugees.
        Surviving are his wife, Karin; two daughters, Katharine Thelen of Portland, Ore., and Margaret Tyler of East Dubuque, Iowa; three sons, Michael, of Fairbanks, Alaska, Richard, of Dubuque, and David, of Tampa, Fla.; his mother, Hedwig, of Dubuque; two sisters, Margaret, of Dubuque and Hildegarde Surbacher of Scranton, Pa., and three grandchildren.

        Lionel M. Gelber, a historian and philanthropist, died of a heart attack on Aug. 10 at Mount Sinai Hospital in Toronto, where he lived. He was 82 years old.
        Mr. Gelber wrote seven books on international relations. He taught history at the University of Toronto from 1941 to 1943. Born in Toronto, he graduated from the University of Toronto and was a Rhodes scholar at Balliol College, Oxford.
        In the 1940's he served as an adviser to the Jewish Agency for Palestine, which advocated the formation of Israel. He was a special assistant to Prime Minister John Diefenbaker.
        Surviving are three brothers, Arthur and Marvin, both of Toronto, and S. Michael of New York City, and a sister, Sylva, of Ottawa.

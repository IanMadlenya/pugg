
        James Stewart, a former chairman and chief executive officer of Frank B. Hall & Company, an international insurance brokerage company, died Sept. 15 at New York Hospital. He was 78 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        He died after a heart attack, his family said.
        Mr. Stewart was born in Germany, the son of Cecil Parker Stewart, who had also been chief executive officer of Frank B. Hall & Company. James Stewart, who had worked as an insurance underwriter in the Far East as a young man, joined the company after his father's death in 1940.
        At the time he had been a member of the American Volunteer Ambulance Corps in France, where he had attended school as a boy.
        For a time he was a front-line war correspondent for the newspaper Paris-Soir. Assignments in China
        From 1942 to 1945, he served in the Army and the Navy. During his Navy service, he met and formed a lifelong friendship with former President Richard M. Nixon.
        On loan from the Navy to the Office of Strategic Services, Mr. Stewart used his knowledge of French and Chinese to carry out several clandestine assignments in China. On one occasion he was parachuted into Indochina to report on Japanese troop movements and organize resistance groups.
        He returned to Hall & Company after the war and in 1969 was named chairman. Under him, the company went public and was listed on the New York Stock Exchange. He served as chairman of the executive committee from 1972 to 1978.
        Mr. Stewart is survived by his wife, Martha Terry Dwight Stewart; a son, James, of Los Angeles; four daughters, Reine Libby and Tracy McKenna, both of Maine; Barry Grace, of Locust Valley, L.I., and China Meir of Sacramento; a half-brother, Aymon de Roussy de Sales of Manhattan; nine grandchildren, two-stepchildren and three step-grandchildren.

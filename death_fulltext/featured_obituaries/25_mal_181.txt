        Dr. Harry Gorman, a veterinarian who trained animals that the United States sent into space, died Friday. He was 72 years old.
        Dr. Gorman, who retired in 1982 from the faculty of the Colorado State University College of Veterinary Medicine, designed the first artificial hip joint for dogs. He later adapted it for use by humans.
        A past president of the American Veterinary Medical Association, he spent 22 years in the military, serving with the Army and the Air Force, In 1956, as head of the Air Force's Veterinary Corps, he raised and trained the nation's first animals in space at Brooks Air Force Base in San Antonio. He retired in 1960 as a colonel.
        In World War II he led veterinarians who rescued more than a million cattle from drowning in the Netherlands after dikes were destroyed by bombs and farmland was flooded.

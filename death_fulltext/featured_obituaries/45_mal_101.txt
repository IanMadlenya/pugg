        Dr. Barnet Halperin, a physician who practiced in the Bushwick section of Brooklyn for more than half a century, died on Wednesday at his home in Forest Hills, Queens. He was 83 years old.
        He died of colon cancer, his family said.
        Dr. Halperin was born in Manhattan and was a graduate of New York University and the New York Medical College. He served in the Army in New Guinea for three years in World War II and retired as a colonel. He was affiliated with Wyckoff Heights Hospital in Brooklyn.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Ramona Vazquez; two daughters, Doralee, of Washington and Linda Matthews of Holden, Mass.; a son, S. Rafael, of Bartonsville, Md.; two sisters, Henrietta Kushner of Coconut Creek, Fla., and Rose Stein of Brooklyn; a brother, Stanley, of Sedona, Ariz., and a granddaughter.

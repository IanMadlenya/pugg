        Murray A. Blaivas, a leader in the use of computers in clinical laboratory testing, died of heart failure Tuesday at Columbia-Presbyterian Medical Center. He was 71 years old and lived in Hollywood, Fla.
        He is survived by his wife, Marilyn; a son, Dr. Jerry G. Blaivas, of Briarcliff Manor, N.Y.; two daughters, Terri Finley of Dunwoody, Ga., and Cathy Blaivas of Manhattan; two sisters, Shirley Ackerman of Lido Beach, L.I., and Doris Foreman, of North Miami Beach, Fla., and five grandchildren.

        Nathan C. Leites, an expert on the Soviet Union who did detailed studies of leaders and policies for the United States Government, died Friday in France, where he was living in retirement. He was 75 years old.
        Mr. Leites had been an associate since 1947 at the Rand Corporation, a research institution here. He gained an international reputation by applying principles of psychoanalysis to the study of world figures, including Stalin.
        He wrote several books on politics and Soviet affairs, including ''The Language of Politics'' in 1949, ''The Operational Code of the Politburo'' in 1951, ''A Study of Bolshevism'' in 1954 and ''On the Game of Politics in France'' in 1959.
        Mr. Leites had also served on the faculty of the University of Chicago.

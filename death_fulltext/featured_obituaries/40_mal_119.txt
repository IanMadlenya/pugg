        Roberto Ornelas, a former national president of the League of United Latin American Citizens, died of cancer on Friday in a Dallas hospital. He was 58 years old.
        Mr. Ornelas, who was the Dallas regional director of the Department of Labor's Office of Federal Contract Compliance Programs, served as national president of the league in 1967 and 1968. After his term as president, he was a director of the organization until 1985.
        In 1967 he was assigned to the White House to coordinate the first White House Conference on Hispanics for President Lyndon B. Johnson.
        He is survived by his wife, Norma, a daughter, Cynthia Brown, and a son, Bobby, all of Dallas, as well as a sister.

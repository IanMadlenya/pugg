        Stanley Givirtz, a biblical scholar who taught at Hebrew Union College-Jewish Institute of Religion in Los Angeles, died of cancer of the liver on Friday at his home in Santa Monica. He was 59 years old.
        Dr. Givirtz, who was born in Brooklyn, received a bachelor's degree from Brooklyn College and a doctoral degree from the University of Chicago, where he taught from 1956 until 1972. Since 1972 he was professor of biblical studies and Near Eastern civilizations at Hebrew Union.
        Surviving are his wife, Marianne; a son, Elihu; two daughters, Sarah and Aliza, all of Los Angeles; and a sister, Frieda, of New York.

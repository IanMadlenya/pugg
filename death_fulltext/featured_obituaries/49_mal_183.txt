
        Edgar M. Reilly, the curator of zoology at the New York State Museum in Albany for 30 years, died on Wednesday at his home in Old Chatham, N.Y. He was 75 years old.
        He died of cancer, said his daughter, Kathleen Curtis of Stephentown, N.Y.
        Dr. Reilly, a Ph.D graduate of Cornell University, was an ardent birdwatcher and wrote several books on birding, including the "Audubon Illustrated Handbook on American Birds" (McGraw Hill, 1968) and "Birdwatchers Diary" (Harper & Row, 1987). He was a member of the American Ornithological Society, the Alan Devoe Bird Club and the New York State Federation of Bird Clubs.
        In addition to his daughter, he is survived by his wife, Alice; two sons, Thomas, of Elmira, N.Y., and Brian, of Phoenix; a brother, Richard, of Lexington, Ky., and five grandchildren.

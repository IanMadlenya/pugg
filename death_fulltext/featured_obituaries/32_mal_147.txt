        Leonard E. Kust, a tax lawyer, died of spongiform encephalopathy, a neurological disorder, Thursday at his home in Manhattan. He was 72 years old.
        He was a partner in the Manhattan law firm of Cadwalader, Wickersham & Taft from 1970 to 1988, when he became of counsel. He was a vice president and counsel for the Westinghouse Electric Corporation from 1955 to 1970, and had been a lawyer with Cravath, Swaine & Moore in Manhattan.
        Mr. Kust was born in Luxemburg, Wis., and graduated from the University of Wisconsin and the Harvard Law School.
        He was a former director of the United States Chamber of Commerce. He was chairman of the Governor of Pennsylvania's Commission on Tax Administration and a member of an advisory committee to the Commissioner of the Internal Revenue Service. He was also a former president of the Tax Executives Institute and the National Tax Association.
        Surviving are his wife, Henrietta; two daughters, Alice Harding of Crofton, Md., and Andrea Logan Kust of New York City, and a sister, Mabel Schraufnagel of Madison, Wis.

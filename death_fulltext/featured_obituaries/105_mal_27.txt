
        Alvin E. Coleman, the former senior partner of Coleman & Company, died on Thursday at his home in Manhattan. He was 97.
        Coleman & Company, which advanced cash to manufacturers in exchange for buying their credit accounts at a discount, was sold to Bankers Trust Company in 1968.
        Mr. Coleman was the former president of Temple Emanu-El on Fifth Avenue and 65th Street, a 150-year-old institution that is regarded as the largest reform congregation in the world. He was elected in 1967 and served until 1974, when he was made honorary president.
        Mr. Coleman was also a former president of Hillside Hospital in Glen Oaks, Queens, a member of the national finance committee of the National Conference of Christians and Jews and a trustee of the Federation of Jewish Philanthropies. He was a graduate of the University of Pennsylvania.
        His wife, Kathryn Coleman, died in 1985.
        Mr. Coleman is survived by a daughter, Doris C. Kempner, and a son, John A. Coleman, both of White Plains; seven grandchildren, and three great-grandchildren.

        Jack London, a New York lawyer specializing in entertainment law, died of cancer Friday at the Memorial-Sloan Kettering Cancer Center. He was 66 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. London, a native New Yorker, was a founding member of the law firm of Becker & London in 1957. Among his clients were Frank Sinatra, Dinah Shore, Steve Lawrence, Eydie Gorme, George C. Scott and other stage, television and film personalities.
        The firm of Becker & London is national counsel to the American Federation of Television and Radio Artists; the American Guild of Musical Artists and the Associated Actors and Artistes of America.
        Mr. London was an alumnus of City College and the Yale Law School.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Charlotte Lord; a son, David, of New Haven; three daughters, Jacqueline, of Medford, Mass., Marjorie, of Manhattan, and Barbara Medina of Spokane, Wash., and two grandchildren.


        Don Irwin, a newspaperman who covered Washington for 30 years for The New York Herald Tribune and The Los Angeles Times, died Monday at his home in Chevy Chase, Md. He was 74 years old.
        He died of lung cancer, said his brother, Wallace Irwin Jr., of Larchmont, N.Y.
        Mr. Irwin, who retired in 1984, began his career in 1941 with The Louisville Courier-Journal. In 1942 he joined The Herald Tribune, moving to its Washington bureau in 1950. He joined The Los Angeles Times in Washington in 1963.
        Donald McDonald Irwin was a graduate of Princeton University. He was an aide to Nelson A. Rockefeller when Mr. Rockefeller was a special assistant to President Dwight D. Eisenhower.
        In addition to his brother, Mr. Irwin is survived by his wife, Sarah Paulding Ray; a son, Donald, of Richmond; two daughters, Edith Greene of Edinburgh, Scotland, and Sarah Taylor of Millboro, Va.; and five grandchildren.


        Harry Sternberg, an accountant and an expert on the renegotiation of military contracts, died on Monday at his home in Scarsdale, N.Y. He was 80 years old.
        He died of a heart attack, his wife, Judith, said.
        Mr. Sternberg, who grew up in Manhattan, headed Harry Sternberg Inc., an accounting firm in Manhattan, and was the author of "Renegotiation of Government Contracts," which is used in accounting courses. He was a former president of the Jewish Community Center in White Plains and was a former chairman of the United Jewish Appeal chapter in Scarsdale and White Plains.
        In addition to his wife, he is survived by two sons, Richard, of Flemington, N.J., and Gerald, of Madison, Wis., and three grandchildren.

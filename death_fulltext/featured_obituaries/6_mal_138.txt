        John C. Sacco, a composer and conductor, died of a cerebral hemorrhage Wednesday at Roosevelt Hospital. He was 82 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Sacco composed hundreds of songs, including ''Johnny the One,'' ''Six Doves,'' ''High Flight,'' ''You Can't Take It With You,'' ''Rapunzel'' and ''With This Ring I Thee Wed.''
        He was a former associate musical director of the St. Louis Municipal Orchestra and executive director of the Starlight Musical Theater in Indianapolis.
        Born in Manhattan, Mr. Sacco graduated from Columbia College and Teachers College of Columbia University. He served in the Army Air Forces in World War II.
        Surviving are his wife, Frances Pole; a son, Peter, of Gales Ferry, Conn., and a granddaughter.

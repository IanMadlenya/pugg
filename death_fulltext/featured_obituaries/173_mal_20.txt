
        Howard M. Packard, former president of S. C. Johnson & Son Inc. of Racine, Wis., the producer of Johnson's Wax and other home products, died on Sunday at a retirement community in Sister Bay, Wis. He was 90.
        He became the company's treasurer in 1946 and was its president from 1958 to 1966.
        While he was president, the company expanded its overseas operations. It also acquired a large collection of contemporary American art for exhibition in the United States and abroad. After stepping down as president, he served as chairman for about a year and was later vice chairman until he retired from the company in 1975.
        Mr. Packard was born in Pasadena, Calif., and earned a business degree from Northwestern University and a law degree from George Washington University. He was a Navy officer, concerned with financial matters, in Washington during World War II.
        His wife, Josephine, died in 1985. He is survived by two daughters, Joan Lewis, of Ephraim, Wis., and Barbara Ziegler, of Nauvoo, Ill.; a son, Richard, of Stevens Point, Wis.; seven grandchildren; and a great-grandchild.

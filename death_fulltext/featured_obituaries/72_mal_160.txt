
        Imam Ali Rashid, the leader of the Malcolm Shabazz Mosque in Harlem, died on Wednesday at St. Vincent's Hospital in Manhattan. He was 62 and lived in New Rochelle, N.Y.
        Imam Rashid died of cancer, said Imam Nurridin of the mosque.
        Born in Los Angeles, Imam Rashid attended college at the University of Oregon and graduated from the California State University system in the late 1940's. After moving to New York in 1975 to become leader of the Harlem mosque, he earned a master's degree in education from the City University of New York in 1983.
        Imam Rashid served in the Army in the late 40's. He converted to Islam in 1952 and joined the Nation of Islam.
        He is survived by his wife of 45 years, Jameela; two daughters, Khadijah Muhammed, and Deanne, and a son, Sharriff, all of New York City, and four grandchildren.

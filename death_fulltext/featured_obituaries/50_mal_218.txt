
        James Patrick Erdman, a New York fund-raiser for philanthropic causes, died on Thursday, at St. Francis Hospital in Roslyn, L.I. He was 71 years old and lived in Old Brookville, L.I.
        He died of complications after heart surgery, a spokesman for the family said.
        Mr. Erdman raised funds for Cancer Care, of which he was a member from 1971 to 1980 and president from 1975 to 1980; the Lighthouse for the Blind, and Lenox Hill Hospital, where he was a member of the board. At his death he was chairman of the April benefit for the Shaare Zedek Hospital in Jerusalem and co-chairman of the June Benefit Auction for Old Westbury Gardens.
        He was a native of Rice Lake, Wis., and worked for General Motors in the late 1930's. During World War II he served in the Army, attaining the rank of captain.
        After the war, he studied interior design at the School of Fine Arts in Fontainebleau, France, for two years and in 1950 became coordinator of design for the Furniture Guild in Grand Rapids, Mich. He worked in the design field as a freelancer here and in Japan and had his own studio for several years in La Jolla, Calif. He also produced a televison series on design.
        His wife, Jean Boyd Copley, died in 1983.
        He is survived by a brother, Robert John, of Fort Myers, Fla., and a sister, Margaret Abernathy of Denver.

        Joseph F. Kern, publisher of Popular Mechanics magazine for 17 years until his retirement in 1980, died of heart failure Friday at his home in Garden City, L.I. He was 73 years old.
        Mr. Kern, a native of Boston, joined the Hearst Corporation in 1936 and, except for four years in the Navy in World War II, remained with it until he retired. He was eastern advertising manager for Good Housekeeping from 1950 unti 1953, when he was named vice president and publisher of Popular Mechnics.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Mary Griffin; four sons, Joseph Jr., Peter and Christopher, all of Manhattan, and Thomas, of Short Hills, N.J.; a daughter, Mary Elizabeth, of Manhattan, and two grandchildren.

        Dr. Irving Sarot, a retired New York thoracic surgeon, died on Monday, apparently of a heart attack, at the Lotus Club in Manhattan. He was 78 years old and lived in West Hampton Beach, L.I.
        Dr. Sarot's specialty was a procedure used to treat tuberculosis, known as extra pleural pnuemonectomy, that was widely used before the advent of anti-tubercular drugs.
        His hospital affilations included Mount Sinai Medical Center, Beth Israel Medical Center and Seaview Sanitarium in Brooklyn. He also had a private practice in Manhattan. He retired in 1978.
        He graduated from Columbia College and Columbia University's College of Physicians and Surgeons.
        In World War II he headed an Army field hospital, rising to major, and took part in the Normandy invasion and the liberation of the Buchenwald concentration camp.
        Dr. Sarot is survived by his wife, the former Hortense Schachtel; a son, Daniel, of Forest Hills, Queens; a daughter, Ellen, of Boston; a stepson, James Kramon of Baltimore; two stepdaughters, Elizabeth Harlan of Cranbury, N.J., and Patricia Pincus of Manhattan; a brother, Dr. Eden, of Maplewood, N.J., and nine grandchildren.
        Correction:  April 27, 1990, Friday, Late Edition - Final
        An obituary on Wednesday about Dr. Irving Sarot, a retired New York thoracic surgeon, referred incorrectly to his daughter. She is Ellin Sarot.

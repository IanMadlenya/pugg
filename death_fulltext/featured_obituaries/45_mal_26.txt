        David Blumstein, a mathematics teacher at James Madison High School in Brooklyn for 30 years and the former owner of summer camps in the Poconos, died on Monday at Jefferson University Hospital in Philadelphia. He was 88 years old and lived in Philadelphia.
        He died of complications of Parkinson's disease, his son, Prof. James F. Blumstein, said.
        Mr. Blumstein, a graduate of City College of New York and Fordham University, joined the faculty at James Madison High School when the school opened in 1927 and remained there until his retirement in 1957. He served as assistant principal at the school. Members of the class of 1935 honored him as their ''beloved teacher'' at their 50th reunion in 1985.
        From 1937 to 1981, Mr. Blumstein owned and operated Camp Swago in Wayne County, Pa., and from 1958 to 1981 he also operated Camp Swatonah. He received many awards and honors from the Association of Independent Camps.
        In addition to his son, who lives in Nashville, Tenn., Mr. Blumstein is survived by his wife, the former Rita Sondheim; a daughter, Judge Anita Brody of Bala Cynwyd, Pa., and three grandchildren.

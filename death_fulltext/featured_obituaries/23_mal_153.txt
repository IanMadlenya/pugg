        Leon Freedman, a lawyer who specialized in workers' compensation cases, died Saturday at his home in Maitland, Fla. He was 83 years old and had lived for many years in Nanuet and Nyack, N.Y.
        Mr. Freedman was active in Democratic politics in the 30's. He retired as a lawyer in 1977.
        He was a graduate of Columbia College and the Columbia Law School.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Lillian Sorin; two daughters, Patricia Paikin of Congers, N.Y., and Claire Koshar of Havre de Grace, Md.; a sister, Martha Dorfman of Fort Lee, N.J.; seven grandchildren and seven great-grandchildren.

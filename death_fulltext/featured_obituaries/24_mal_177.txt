        Chester Hammond, a retired industrialist who served as a millitary aide to Franklin D. Roosevelt, died of cardiac arrest Tuesday at the Maplewood Manse Rest Home in Peterborough, N.H. He was 77 years old and lived in Peterborough.
        Mr. Hammond, a 1932 graduate of the United States Military Academy at West Point, was associated with the H. F. Livermore Corporation, a Boston-based manufacturer of textile looms, from 1947 to 1975, serving as president and chairman of the board.
        From 1938 to 1945, rising to the rank of lieutenant colonel, he served as a military aide to President Roosevelt.
        There are no immediate survivors.

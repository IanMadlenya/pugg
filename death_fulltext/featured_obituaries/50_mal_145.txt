
        Paul E. Fran, the executive director of the Bloomingdale House of Music, died on Thursday at St. Luke's Hospital. He was 56 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        He died of a cerebral hemorrhage, his family said.
        Mr. Fran became the director of the Bloomingdale House of Music, an Upper West Side community music school, in 1988. He led the school's Orff Program of music education for public school children as well as its private music instruction and concert programs.
        Mr. Fran, who was born in Brooklyn, graduated from the Manhattan School of Music. Before joining the Bloomingdale House, he served as the director of the Cathedral Concert Service in Newark, the Conductor's Guild Inc. in Manhattan, and the Roosa School of Music in Brooklyn. He also had been the assistant director of the music program for the National Endowment for the Arts and an associate director of Young Audiences in New York.
        A composer and choral singer, Mr. Fran was a member of the American Federation of Musicians and the American Society of Composers, Authors and Publishers.
        He is survived by his wife, Mary, and two sons, Ethan, of New York, and Adam, of Salt Lake City.

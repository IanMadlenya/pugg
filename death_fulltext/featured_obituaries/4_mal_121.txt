        Siegmund Klein, a renowned strongman in the 1920's and 1930's who operated a gymnasium on Seventh Avenue for 45 years, died of cancer at St. Luke's Hospital Sunday. He was 85 years old.
        Born in Germany, Mr. Klein moved with his family to Cleveland as a child. He came to New York in 1925 and opened his gymnasium on Seventh Avenue at 48th Street. Many well-known actors, among others, trained there.
        Mr. Klein is survived by his wife, Grace; a daughter, Jeanne Bacols, and a granddaughter.

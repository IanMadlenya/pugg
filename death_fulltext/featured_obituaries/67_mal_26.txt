
        Robert Peak, an award-winning illustrator known for his many movie posters, national magazine covers and postage-stamp designs, died on Friday at Scottsdale Memorial North Hospital in Arizona. He was 64 years old and lived in nearby Carefree.
        He suffered a brain hemorrhage in a fall, said his agent, Harvey Kahn.
        Mr. Peak won praise for injecting vitality and vibrant colors into movie posters. Among the more than 130 films for which he did posters were "West Side Story," "Camelot," "My Fair Lady," "Apocalypse Now" and five "Star Trek" features.
        He received a lifetime achievement award from The Hollywood Reporter in June. His other honors included prizes from the Artists Guild of New York, the Art Directors Club and the Society of Illustrators. Covers and Commemorative Stamps
        Magazines featuring his illustrations included Ladies Home Journal, McCall's, Good Housekeeping, Parents, TV Guide, Esquire and Time, for which he did more than 40 covers.
        The United States Postal Service commissioned him to create a series of 30 Olympics commemorative stamps in 1983-84. He designed a stamp on automobile racing in 1978 and one on football in 1969.
        He was born in Denver and grew up in Wichita, Kan. He displayed a flair for art as early as age 7. After graduating from Wichita State University, he served in the Navy, then studied at the Art Center College of Design in Los Angeles.
        During most of his career, he lived and worked in New York City and in Greenwich and Westport, Conn.
        His wife, the former Lucille Tedesco, died in 1977.
        He is survived by three sons, Robert Jr., of Orlando, Fla.; Thomas, of Los Angeles, and Matthew, of San Francisco; a daughter, Cathi Rogers of Barrington, R.I.; three sisters, Geraldine Regen of Kennewick, Wash., and Virginia Zimmerman and Rosemary Malone, both of Wichita; a brother, William, of Wichita, and five grandchildren.

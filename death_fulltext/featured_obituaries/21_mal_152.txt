        William L. Grey, an attorney and former chief executive of Anaconda Wire and Cable Co., died in his sleep yesterday at his home in Somers, N.Y. He was 72 years old and had moved to Somers from Hartsdale, N.Y., in 1986.
        Mr. Grey, a graduate of New York University in 1937 and Brooklyn Law School in 1950, was president of Anaconda from 1967 to 1974. He became a partner in the Manhattan patent and trademark law firm of Penne & Edmonds. He retired in 1981 but remained a consultant. He had been a director of Handy & Harman, a precious-metals concern based in Manhattan, since 1971.
        He is survived by his wife, Marguerite Kefer; a son, Richard, of Rockford, Ill.; a daughter, Carol Grey Romaine, of Riverside, Conn., and five grandchildren.

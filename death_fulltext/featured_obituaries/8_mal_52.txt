        Brian F. Newton, who retired last year as manager of the sulfur division of Exxon Chemicals, died of cancer of the gall bladder Monday at his home in Quechee, Vt. He was 61 years old.
        Mr. Newton, graduated from University College, London, in 1947. He was the division's manager from 1974 to 1986, after which he moved to Vermont. Before that, he had lived in New Canaan, Conn., and Houston.
        He is survived by his wife, Rosetta; two sons, Simon, of Bedford, N.Y., and Anthony, of Cornish, N.H.; three daughters, Monica Murray of South Salem, N.Y., Rosemary Newton of Yonkers and Nicola Newton-Craig of Canaan, N.H., and seven grandchildren.

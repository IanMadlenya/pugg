
        James A. Jacobson, a retired senior vice president of Chase Manhattan Bank, died on May 3 at the Bryn Mawr Terrace nursing home in Bryn Mawr, Pa. He was 84 years old.
        He died of congestive heart failure, said his stepdaughter Patricia Onderdonk Pruett of Bryn Mawr.
        Mr. Jacobson's career at Chase Manhattan spanned 40 years. He joined the bank's predecessor, Chase National Bank, in 1931 after graduating from St. Olaf College in Northfield, Minn., and the Harvard Business School. During his career, Mr. Jacobson helped establish most of Chase Manhattan's operations in the Far East and, at the time he retired, was in charge of all the bank's operations outside the United States.
        Mr. Jacobson took leave from the bank during World War II to serve as special assistant to the director of the War Production Board. His assignments took him to many Allied countries, including Great Britain, Mexico, China, India and the Soviet Union.
        Mr. Jacobson, who was born in North Dakota and reared in Montana, received an honorary doctorate from St. Olaf College. He was a director of numerous organizations, including the Japan Society and the Asia Society.
        Mr. Jacobson's first wife died in the late 1970's.
        In addition to his stepdaughter, he is survived by his wife, Marion; another stepdaughter, Adrienne Onderdonk Dudden of Bryn Mawr, and four step-grandchildren.

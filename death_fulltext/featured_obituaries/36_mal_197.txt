        Charley Eugene Johns, who was Acting Governor of Florida for 15 months in the mid-1950's, died at Alachua County Hospital in Gainesville, Fla., on Tuesday after a long illness. He was 84 years old.
        Mr. Johns, then president of the State Senate, became Acting Governor in 1953 after the death of Gov. Daniel Thomas McCarty.
        In 1954 he was defeated for the Democratic gubernatorial nomination by Leroy Collins. He served 15 terms in the Legislature.
        Mr. Johns was also the founder and former president of the Community State Bank in his hometown, Starke, where he also operated an insurance agency.
        He is survived by his wife, Thelma; a son, Charley, of Starke; a daughter, Markleyann Cash of Orlando, five grandchildren and five great-grandchildren.

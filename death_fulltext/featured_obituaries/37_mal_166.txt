        Dr. Carl A. Smith, a retired surgeon and professor emeritus of surgery at the New York University Medical School, died of a heart attack on Tuesday at his home in Manhattan. He was 69 years old.
        Dr. Smith maintained a private practice and taught medicine at N.Y.U. from 1949 until he retired in 1987. He was also a staff doctor with The New York Times from 1955 to 1957. Dr. Smith worked with underdeveloped countries and taught medicine in Guatemala in 1968 and Ethiopia in 1971. He volunteered as a surgeon in the Israeli-Egyptian war in 1973.
        Dr. Smith was born in Brooklyn and graduated from City College in 1939. He received a master's degree in biochemistry from the University of Michigan in 1940 and graduated from the N.Y.U. Medical School in 1944. He was president of the N.Y.U. Medical Alumni Association in 1961 and 1962.
        Surviving are three daughters, Tamar, of Boston, Julia and Laura, both of Manhattan, and two sisters, Sophie Hollander of Sacramento, Calif., and Roena Spotkov of Los Angeles.

        Samuel Zachary Gelsey, a retired New York stockbroker, died yesterday at the Miami Heart Institute. He was 89 years old and lived in Miami Beach.
        He became a stockbroker before the 1929 crash. In the 1930's, he worked with Ernst & Company. After World War II, he became a partner in the firm of Batchker, Eaton & Company. When it went out of business in 1973, Mr. Gelsey rejoined Ernst. He retired in 1980.
        He is survived by his wife, Billie; a son, Daniel, of Miami; a daughter, Helen Zirinsky of Manhattan, and six grandchildren.

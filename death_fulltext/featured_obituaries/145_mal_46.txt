
        Stanley C. Gillette, a former clothing industry executive, died on Feb. 4 at his home in New York City. He was 81 years old.
        The cause was prostate cancer, said his wife, Joan.
        Mr. Gillette joined Phillips-Jones, the manufacturer of Van Heusen brand shirts and other men's wear, in 1947 as a regional sales manager. He rose to the position of vice president in charge of sales in 1954 and was named a director the next year.
        In 1960, Mr. Gillette was promoted to vice president for marketing at the company, a family-held corporation that changed its name to Phillips-Van Heusen in 1957. As vice president for marketing, he was responsible for sales, advertising and marketing. Mr. Gillette was named executive vice president of Phillips and president of the Van Heusen Company, a subsidiary, in 1963, positions he held for 12 years.
        Mr. Gillette left Phillips in 1975 to become chairman and president of McGregor-Doniger, a men's sportswear manufacturer. In 1979, he joined Ship 'n Shore, part of the General Mills Fashion Group, as chairman and president, where he was also a vice president of General Mills.
        In 1987, Mr. Gillette left General Mills to start a management consulting company. He retired in 1990.
        Mr. Gillette attended City College, Columbia University and New York University. He worked in the apparel industry in New York after serving in World War II in the Army medical corps before he joined Phillips-Jones.
        In addition to his wife, he is survived by a daughter, Patty Gillette of Sleepy Hollow, N.Y.; a son, Richard, of Forestville, Calif.; four grandchildren, and one great-grandchild.

        Ewing Everett, a retired Federal tax lawyer, died on Sunday at Danbury (Conn.) Hospital after a brief illness. He was 92 years old and lived in Ridgefield, Conn.
        Mr. Everett helped form the Manhattan law firm of Everett & Johnson in 1942. He retired in 1973.
        He was born in Pulaski, Tenn., served in the Navy in World War I and graduated from Yale University in 1920 and from its law school in 1922.
        Mr. Everett wrote several books on taxation, including ''Organization of Corporations in Sales Assets'' (Ronald Press, 1931).
        Mr. Everett is survived by a daughter, Marietta Williams of Elverson, Pa.; a son, Robert A., of New Haven; a sister, Ellen Douglas Carruthers of Birmingham, Ala.; and four grandchildren.

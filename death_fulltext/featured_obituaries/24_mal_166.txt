        Dr. Carlos Alfredo Yorio, a professor of linguistics at Lehman College and at the City University Graduate Center, died Monday at New Rochelle (N.Y.) Hospital. He was 48 years old and lived in the Riverdale section of the Bronx.
        Dr. Yorio died of pneumonia complicated by lymphoma, Leonard Lief, the president of Lehman, said.
        Dr. Yorio also directed the Lehman College program in English as a second language. He taught at the University of Toronto before joining the Lehman faculty in 1983.
        At Lehman he instituted a rigorous sequence of English courses integrated with bilingual teaching of undergraduate core courses.
        Dr. Yorio, who was born in Cordoba, Argentina, was a graduate of the University of Cordoba. He came to the United States in 1963 on a Fulbright-Hays scholarship. He received master's and doctoral degrees in linguistics from the University of Michigan.
        Dr. Yorio's longtime companion was Gilbert Watson of Riverdale.

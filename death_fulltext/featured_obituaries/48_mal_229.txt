
        Robert F. Bonner Jr., a professional engineer and an officer with Malcolm Pirnie, Inc., one of the nation's largest environmental engineering companies, died Sunday at his home in Hampton, Va. He was 55 years old.
        He died of cancer, a funeral home representative said.
        Mr. Bonner, who directed the design and construction of some of the nation's largest water treatment plants, began his career with Malcolm Pirnie in 1965. He was named a vice president in 1973 and a corporate director in 1979. He became senior corporate officer for the company's regional headquarters in Newport News, Va., in 1984.
        Mr. Bonner, a graduate of Villanova University and the Newark College of Engineering, had previously worked on environmental projects in Iran and Nigeria with the firm of Sidney Bowne of Manhattan.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Linda Hanifin, head of a public relations firm; a daughter, Christina Bonner of Orlando, Fla.; a son, Tyrus of White Plains; a sister, Anne Bonner of Manhattan; two brothers, Frederick of Baltimore and Edward of Westbury, L.I.; and a grandson.

        Joseph D. Johnson Sr., a retired executive of Western Union International, died on Sunday at North Shore Hospital in Manhasset, L.I. He was 83 years old and lived in Huntington Station, L.I.
        A spokesman for the family said Mr. Johnson died of a heart attack.
        Mr. Johnson joined the New York Western Telegraph Company in 1927 in Chicago as a branch manager. He retired in 1964 as vice president of the metropolitan division of the Western Union Telegraph Company in Manhattan. He went on to become special assistant to the president of Western Union International for another decade.
        He was a lifetime member and a former president of the Rotary Club of New York. He was also a past president of the West Side Association of Commerce and a former vice president of the New York Board of Trade and of the Broadway Association, a group of businesses organized for philanthropic purposes.
        Mr. Johnson was born in Columbus, Ohio, and attended the University of Chicago.
        A widower, he is survived by a son, Joseph D. Jr., of Huntington Station; a brother, John, of Chicago, and two grandchildren.

        Nathan B. Kogan, a lawyer and a member of the board of directors of the Jewish Theological Seminary, died yesterday at Mount Sinai Hospital of complications following a stroke. He was 80 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        He served on the boards of directors of the Jewish Museum and of the New York County Lawyers Association Foundation and was a trustee of the Park Avenue Synagogue. He was president of the Martha and Regina Brand Foundation, which provides funds for a school in pastoral psychiatry at the Jewish Theological Seminary.
        Mr. Kogan, who was born in Catasauqua, Pa., graduated from St. John's University and Brooklyn Law School. He was a former police commissioner and associate judge in Atlantic Beach, L.I., where his family once had a summer home.
        He is survived by his wife, Marjorie; two sons, Dr. Michael, of Little Falls, N.J., and Barton, of Los Angeles; a brother, William, of Westbury, L.I., and five sisters, Ruth Harris of White Plains, Regina Gershater of Laguna Beach, Calif., Sarah Banet of Queens, and Belle Watman and Leah Carmelli, both of Petah Tikva, Israel.

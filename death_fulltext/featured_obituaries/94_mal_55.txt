
        Kenneth H. Ross, the chairman of a family-owned clothes-making company based in Milwaukee and a philanthropist, died on Wednesday at St. Luke's Hospital in Manhattan. He was 66 and lived in Milwaukee.
        He was awaiting a heart transplant at the time, a company spokeswoman said in New York.
        Mr. Ross, a graduate of the University of Wisconsin, headed JH Collectibles, which manufactures women's clothes. He joined the business in 1954 and had been chairman, president and chief executive since 1977.
        Mr. Ross is survived by his wife, Audrey; two sons, Bruce and Richard; a daughter, Karen Ross-Serotte; a brother, Donald, and three grandchildren.

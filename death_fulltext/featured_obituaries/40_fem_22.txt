        Susan Oliver, an actress who appeared in numerous television series, died of cancer May 10 at the Motion Picture and Television Hospital in Calabasas, Calif. She was 61 years old and lived in Hollywood.
        Ms. Oliver, whose name was originally Charlotte Gercke, was seen on Broadway in ''Small War on Murray Hill'' and ''Look Back in Anger.'' Her films included ''The Gene Krupa Story,'' ''Butterfield 8,'' ''Change of Mind'' and ''The Disorderly Orderly.''
        Ms. Oliver played Ann Howard in ''Peyton Place,'' a prime-time television series in the 1960's. Her televison credits also included ''Star Trek,'' ''The Defenders,'' ''The Nurses,'' ''Murder, She Wrote'' and ''Magnum P.I.'' She also directed episodes of ''M*A*S*H'' and ''Trapper John, M.D.''
        Ms. Oliver was also a pilot. In 1967 she flew a single engine Aero Commander 200 across the Atlantic Ocean alone from New York to Denmark.
        There are no survivors.

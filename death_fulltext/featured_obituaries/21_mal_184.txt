        Paul S. Graziano, a retired professor at St. John's University Law School, died of a heart attack Friday at his home in Flushing, Queens. He was 70 years old.
        Mr. Graziano, who was a native of Manhattan, was a graduate of City College, Fordham University Law School and New York University Law School.
        He was also a former president of the Queens County Bar Association, a former acting chief law assistant in State Supreme Court in Queens and a former lieutenant colonel in the Army Judge Advocate General's Corps.
        Surviving are his wife, Mary; three sons, Paul, of Norwalk, Conn., Robert, of Flushing, and Stephen, of Anaheim, Calif.; a daughter, Mary Flanagan, of Lindenhurst, L.I.; two brothers, Benedict, of Philadelphia, and Eugene, of Miller Place, L.I., and five grandchildren.

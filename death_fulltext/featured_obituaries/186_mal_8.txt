
        Harold L. Oshry, an early proponent of automobile leasing in the United States, died on June 29 at his home in Tamarac, Fla. He was 84.
        In 1955, Mr. Oshry set up the All-State Auto Leasing Corporation and developed it into one of the earliest car-leasing companies in the country. The company subsequently developed several other business lines, changed its name to Sandgate Corporation and went public in 1962.
        Mr. Oshry, a longtime resident of Hewlett Harbor, N.Y., also raised money and contributed to a variety of Jewish charities.
        He was born in Chelsea, Mass., and graduated from Bowdoin College in 1940. He was a captain in the Eighth Army Air Force in World War II.
        He is survived by his wife, Claire; a brother, George, and a sister, Sally Adelson; two daughters, Meryl Evens and Suzanne Oshry; a son, Michael, and seven grandchildren.


        Albert J. Marino, a retired judge of the New York City Civil Court, died on Sunday at the Applegarth Care Center in Hightstown, N.J. He was 90 and a longtime resident of Brooklyn.
        His death came after a long illness, his family said.
        Mr. Marino graduated from the Fordham University School of Law. While in private practice in Brooklyn, he held a number of positions with the New York State Legislature.
        He was a Republican district leader in Bay Ridge, Brooklyn, for 20 years.
        Mr. Marino's wife of 54 years, Mary Melone Marino, died in 1989.
        He is survived by three daughters, Doris Corrao of Olney, Md., Lorraine O'Brien of West Windsor, N.J., and Alice Conlon of East Windsor, N.J.; a brother, Anthony, of Moriches, L.I.; a sister, Alvira Salatino of Ponte Vedra, Fla., and six grandchildren.

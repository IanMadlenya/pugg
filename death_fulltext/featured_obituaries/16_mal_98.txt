        Joseph J. Tomlin, the founder of the Pop Warner football program for youths, died Monday after a brief illness. He was 85.
        Mr. Tomlin guided the Pop Warner program as it grew from a local junior football league in Philadelphia in 1929 to a far-reaching organization in 40 states, Mexico and the United Kingdom with 200,000 participants annually.
        He is survived by his wife, Regina; a daughter, Francine; a son, David, and two grandchildren.

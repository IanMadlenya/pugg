        Robert Oel Briggs, a lamp and accessories designer, died on July 9 at Lenox Hill Hospital. He was 60 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Briggs died of kidney and liver failure after a long illness, said a friend of many years, Irvin Sandson.
        Mr. Briggs, known professionally as Briggs, was a designer for Paul Hanson Company in Manhattan for 19 years, starting in the late 1950's. He then joined Ethan Allen Industries. From 1985 to 1987, he was design director and a consultant to the Ainsley Lamps Company in Brooklyn.
        He was born in Lansing, Mich., attended Michigan State University and graduated from the University of Michigan. He also completed a master's program in retailing at New York University.
        He is survived by his parents, Samuel Oel and Beulah L. Briggs of Mt. Bethel, Pa.


        Dr. Bruce Ruddick, a psychoanalyst, psychiatrist and poet, died on Friday in White Plains Hospital. He was 77 years old and lived in Manhattan and Amagansett, L.I.
        He died of pulmonary fibrosis, his family said.
        Dr. Ruddick was a clinical professor of psychiatry at Albert Einstein College of Medicine and adjunct professor of psychology at Columbia University.
        Dr. Ruddick, who was born in Montreal, was the son, grandson and great-grandson of medical doctors. He earned his undergraduate and medical degrees at McGill University and received his psychoanalytic training at the New York Psychoanalytic Institute between 1948 and 1952.
        Afterward, Dr. Ruddick returned to Montreal, where he was a founder of the Canadian Psychoanalytic Society. He moved back to New York City in 1957.
        He was a founder of Preview, a Canadian literary journal published between 1942 and 1946. One of his poems, "Bestiary," was published in Exile, a Toronto literary quarterly, in 1991.
        Dr. Ruddick is survived by his wife of 41 years, Dorothy Cole Ruddick; three daughters, Abby Cole Ruddick of Bali, Indonesia, Lisa Cole Ruddick of Chicago and Margaret Emily Ruddick of New York City; a sister, Eleanor Jean Close of Markham, Ontario; a brother, Donald Ruddick, of Montreal, and a granddaughter.

        Rabbi Samuel Umen, a writer and teacher who held pulpits in Michigan and Maryland and at Temple Adath Yeshurun in Manchester, N.H., died Saturday at Elliott Hospital in Manchester, where he lived. He was 73 years old.
        His death resulted from liver cancer, his wife, Lenore, said.
        Rabbi Umen's books included ''Jewish Concepts and Reflections,'' ''Links Between Judaism and Christianity'' and ''The Nature of Judaism.'' He taught philosophy at Nathaniel Hawthorne College in Antrim, N.H., and at New Hampshire College in Manchester.
        Surviving, besides his wife, are two sons, Andrew, of Minneapolis, and Harry, of Henniker, N.H., and two grandchildren.


        William F. Michelfelder, a journalist and novelist, died on Sunday at Hospice by the Sea in Boca Raton, Fla. He was 82 years old and lived in Gulf Stream, Fla.
        He died of congestive heart failure and diabetes, said his wife, Phyllis.
        Mr. Michelfelder wrote of two novels, "A Seed Upon the Wind" (1954) and "Be Not Angry" (1960). He also wrote several nonfiction books, including "It's Cheaper to Die" (1960), about the high cost of medicine in the United States, and "Medical Nightmares" (1964), a collection of medical articles written for magazines.
        Mr. Michelfelder received a bachelor's degree from Fordham University and a master's from the Columbia School of Journalism. He had a long career as a journalist. He was a reporter and editor at many newspapers, including The New York Journal-American, The Boston Record-American and The New York World-Telegram & Sun, where he won the 1955 Silurian Award and the A.F.L.-C.I.O. Award for Public Service, both for his medical reporting. He also worked for The National Enquirer and as a freelance writer.
        In the 1960's he was director of public relations and development at Knickerbocker Hospital in New York and was executive director of the public affairs office at Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute in Troy, N.Y.
        He is survived by his wife.

        Abraham W. Rosenthal, the former executive director of the Y.M.-Y.W.H.A. and the former chairman of the Council of Social Agencies, both in the Bronx, died Thursday at his home in Santa Monica, Calif., after a short illness. He was 91 years old.
        He was born in New York City and graduated from Columbia College. He was executive director of the Bronx Y's for 20 years and was a planner for the Federation of Jewish Philanthropies.
        He is survived by his daughter, Lee Grant of Manhattan, and a granddaughter.

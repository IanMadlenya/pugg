        J. David Greenstone, a professor of political science at the University of Chicago and an author, died of cancer on Wednesday at Bernard Mitchell Hosiptal in Chicago. He was 52 years old and lived in Chicago.
        Dr. Greenstone, a specialist on American government and political theory, was the William Benton Distinguished Service Professor at the university.
        His books included ''Labor and American Politics,'' published in 1968, and ''Race and Authority in Urban Politics: A Study of the War on Poverty,'' published in 1973.
        A graduate of Harvard College, he received his doctorate in 1963 at the University of Chicago. He joined the faculty there in 1964 and was chairman of the political science department from 1972 to 1975.
        He later served as master of the social sciences collegiate division. In 1988 he received the Quantrell Award for excellence in undergraduate teaching.
        He is survived by his wife, Joan, and two sons, Michael and Daniel, all of chicago; and his mother, Helen Greenstone of Rochester, N.Y.

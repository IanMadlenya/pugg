
        William Maidman, a plumbing and fittings manufacturer who was also a major real estate owner and manager in New York, died on Saturday at his home in Hollywood, Fla. He was 95.
        Mr. Maidman was vice president of the Central Foundry Company, which had offices or plants in New York, Newark and Forest Hills, Queens.
        He was also senior partner of Wm. Maidman & Company, which at one time managed and owned some 90 buildings, many of them garages in the Garment District. Today the company owns 25 buildings, notably the Remington Building at 113 West 42nd Street.
        Mr. Maidman owned several of the buildings with his older brother, Irving, who died in 1978.
        William Maidman's first wife, Aida, died in 1986. He is survived by his second wife, Ada; two sons, Richard, of Manhattan and Sands Point, N.Y., and David, of Manhattan; a daughter, Bonnie, of Hollywood, Fla.; six grandchildren; and four great-grandchildren.

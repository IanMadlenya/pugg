        Anatole Chelnov, a retired vice president of the international division of Encyclopedia Britannica, died July 1 at his home in San Francisco. He was 71 years old.
        He died of cardiopulmonary arrest, said his wife, Jean Potter Chelnov.
        Born in Moscow and educated in Belgium, Mr. Chelnov came to the United States in 1937. He served in the United States Air Force in World War II and worked in Berlin and Paris before joining Encyclopedia Britannica in 1960.
        He helped to open marketing offices for the company in Europe and Asia and in 1969 was appointed as international franchise sales director. He became a vice president in 1978 and retired in 1983.
        In addition to his wife, he is survived by a son, Michael of Fairfield, Iowa; a daughter, Sandra Chelnov of Oakland, Calif., and one granddaughter.

        Dr. Morris Neiman, an educator and author, died from complications of cancer surgery Thursday at the Mount Sinai Medical Center. He was 79 years old and lived in Rego Park, Queens.
        A graduate of Columbia University with a Ph.D. in Hebrew, Dr. Neiman taught Hebrew at the Ramaz School on the Upper East Side for 26 years and at Brooklyn College for 22 years. He was also a lecturer in Hebrew at Yeshiva University. His book, ''Modern Hebrew Literary Criticism - 1788 to 1888,'' was published in 1985 by Ktav Publishers, of Manhattan. He also wrote numerous articles for Hebrew publications. He retired in 1980.
        He is survived by two daughters, Hadassah Gurfein of Englewood, N.J., and Shirah of Manhattan; a brother, David, of Newton, Mass.; three sisters, Mary Sanders of Jerusalem, Sarah Kouffman of Boca Raton, Fla., and Deborah Schwartz, of Little Neck, Queens, and three grandchildren.

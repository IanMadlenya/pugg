        Ralph J. Mills, a former president and chairman of the Yonkers Savings Bank, died on Sunday at his home in Southbury, Conn. He was 88 years old. He died of pneumonia, his son said.  Mr. Mills went to work at the Yonkers Savings Bank in 1918 as a high school student. He retired as chairman in 1967. The bank is now the Manhattan Savings Bank.
        From 1967 through 1970, Mr. Mills was president of the Andrus Pavillion of St. John's Hospital in Yonkers. He also was a former president and a trustee of the Yonkers Y.M.C.A., a former director of the Yonkers Chamber of Commerce and of the Savings Bank Trust Company of New York, and former chairman of the examining committee of the Institutional Securities Corporation of New York City.
        Mr. Mills, who was born in Yonkers, was an alumnus of Rutgers University and its Graduate School of Banking.
        A widower, he is survived by his son, R. Jeffries Mills Jr. of Chappaqua, N.Y., and a granddaughter.

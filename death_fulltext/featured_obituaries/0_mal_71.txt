        Robert Swanton Driscoll, former managing partner of Lord, Abbett & Company, an investment management firm, died of a heart attack Tuesday at his home in Chappaqua, N.Y. He was 75 years old.
        Mr. Driscoll joined the firm in 1941 and was named a partner in 1949. From 1964 to 1980, when he retired, he was a managing partner. He wrote a number of articles and books on financial subjects including ''The Growing Demand for Common Stocks.''
        He was born in Manhattan and graduated with honors in economics from Columbia College in 1933.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Jane Word; three sons, Robert Swanton Driscoll 4th of Washington, Steven of Tokyo, and David of Coral Springs, Fla., and seven grandchildren.

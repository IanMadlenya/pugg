
        Harold Schwartz, a retired publishing executive, died on Sunday at his home in South Portland, Me. He was 70 years old.
        Mr. Schwartz died of complications after heart surgery, said his daughter Hilary.
        He had a long career in publishing, first as vice president for Parents Magazine Press in Manhattan, where he helped create Humpty Dumpty magazine and Children's Digest. Later, he was co-founder and president of an academic reprint publishing company, Greenwood Press, in Westport, Conn.
        Mr. Schwartz moved to Penobscot, Me., in 1971 and in 1981 was a founder of the Winslow Investment Company, a brokerage concern later acquired by the Maine Savings Bank. At his death he was vice president of F. L. Putnam/ Winslow, an investment management firm he helped to found in Portland, Me.
        He was born in Manhattan and graduated from New York University. He served with the Army Air Corps in China during World War II.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Penelope Carnahan; two daughters, Hilary and Margaret, both of South Portland; two sons, Peter, of Washington, and William, of Manhattan, and a sister, Doris Kalisman of Wantaugh, L.I.

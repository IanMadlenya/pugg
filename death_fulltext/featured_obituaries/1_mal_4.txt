        Christian Broda, a former Austrian Justice Minister who fought long and successfully for abolishing the death penalty, died today at his home, apparently after a heart attack, according to relatives. He was 70 years old.
        Mr. Broda, a member of the Socialist Party, was Justice Minister from 1960 to 1966 and from 1970 to 1983. He was responsible for abolition of the death sentence in Austria and for other wide-reaching judicial and penal changes.
        Mr. Broda and President Raul Alfonsin of Argentina shared the European Human Rights Award given by 21-Nation Council of Europe this year, and Mr. Broda attended presentation ceremonies in Strasbourg, France, on Wednesday.
        Born in Vienna on March 12, 1916, Mr. Broda studied law after finishing secondary school. Nazi sentiment grew in the 1930's, and he was briefly imprisoned in 1934 because of his Socialist leanings.
        After the Nazi takeover of Austria in 1938, he was drafted into service with the German Army and served until the end of World War II. In 1943, during his army service, he was he was again imprisoned briefly because of his political beliefs.
        After the war, Mr. Broda practiced law until 1957, when he was elected to Parliament.


        Herbert Klynn, a pioneer in film and television animation, died on Feb. 3 at a hospital in Tarzana, Calif. He was 81.
        Mr. Klynn helped create prize-winning cartoon shorts, including ''Mr. Magoo,'' ''Gerald McBoing-Boing'' and ''Madeline,'' at the United Productions of America Studios, where he worked from 1944 to 1959. He  became a vice president and production chief at the studio.
        In 1959 he founded Format Productions, where he created such cartoons as ''The Alvin Show,'' ''The Lone Ranger'' and ''The Curiosity Shop.'' He was president of the company until 1982. Mr. Klynn also created the opening graphic titles for the television series ''I Spy.''
        He is survived by his wife, Selma; a brother, Marvin, of Encino, Calif.; a daughter, Gail Spindler of Los Angeles, and two sons, Lee, of Los Altos, Calif., and Richard, of Upper Nyack, N.Y..

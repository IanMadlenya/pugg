        Mark J. Appleman, a consultant, died of heart failure Monday at his home in Manhattan. He was 72 years old.
        After a career in advertising and investor relations, Mr. Appleman established a consulting firm specializing in investor relations and corporate communications.
        He wrote two books and contributed articles to many business publications. He also published a newsletter, ''The Corporate Shareholder.''
        Mr. Appleman, who was born in Columbus, Ohio, wrote a film script and a novel before becoming an executive with the J. Walter Thompson advertising agency. He later moved to Wall Street, where he worked in investor relations at Francis I. duPont & Company and Oppenheimer & Company.
        There are no survivors.

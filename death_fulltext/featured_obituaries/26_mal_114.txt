        Joseph Francis Follmann Jr., a retired health insurance executive and author, died of a heart attack Tuesday at Lenox Hill Hospital in Manhattan. He was 81 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Follman, a graduate of the University of Pennsylvania, began his career with the Pennsylvania Insurance Department in 1930. In 1945 he became manager of the Bureau of Accident and Health Underwriters in Manhattan, and from 1956 until his retirement in 1974 he was with the Health Insurance Association of America, first as director of information and later as vice president.
        He wrote six books, including ''Alcoholics and Business'' in 1976 and ''Helping the Troubled Employee'' in 1979, both published by the American Management Association. He was also an accomplished musician, composer and conductor.
        He is survived by his wife, Judith; a son, Jeffrey Brooks of St. Paul, Minn.; a daughter, Amy Ujifusa of Chappaqua, N.Y., and four grandchildren.

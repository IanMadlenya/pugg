        Christopher James Carven, an executive of the Exxon Corporation, died of leukemia Saturday at his home on Kiawah Island, S.C. He was 59 years old.
        Mr. Carven, who joined Exxon 40 years ago, was manager of the transportation division of the company's petroleum products department when he retired in 1986.
        Surviving are his wife, Mary; two sons, John of Garwood, N.J., and Coleman of Summit, N.J., and four daughters, Mary Beth de Rose of Bernardsville, N.J., Christine Faenza of Kiawah Island, Claire Schauseil of Lutz, Fla., and Catherine Frank of Jersey City.

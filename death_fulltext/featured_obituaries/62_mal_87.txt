
        Jack Washburn, an actor and nightclub singer, died on Sunday at his home in New Hope, Pa. He was 64 years old.
        He died after a long illness, said his wife, Diane.
        Mr. Washburn was born in San Francisco, where his career as a nightclub performer began. He appeared in the 1954 Broadway musical "Fanny" and toured extensively with that show. In 1963 he appeared on Broadway in Irving Berlin's "Mr. President."
        He also acted in many regional productions at such theaters as the Bucks County Playhouse in New Hope and the Music Circus in Lambertville, N.J. Productions in which he appeared included "Annie Get Your Gun," "Sweet Charity" and "Dial M for Murder." He also appeared in the 1959 film "Black Orchid" with Sophia Loren.
        In addition to his wife, he is survived by a daughter, Wendy Rice of Yardley, Pa.; a son, Kevin, of Long Beach, Calif., and a grandson.

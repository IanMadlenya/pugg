
        Whitner (Whit) Bissell, a character actor who appeared on Broadway and in scores of films, died on Tuesday at the Motion Picture and Television Hospital in Woodland Hills, Calif. He was 86.
        Mr. Bissell began his career in the theater. He appeared on Broadway in a 1936 production of "Hamlet" that starred John Gielgud and Judith Anderson and in Maxwell Anderson's "Star-Wagon" (1937) with Lillian Gish and Burgess Meredith. He also had roles in "The American Way" (1939), "Cue for Passion" (1940), "Cafe  Crown" (1942) and "Winged Victory" (1943).
        In Hollywood, working as Whit Bissell, he acted in thrillers, Westerns and science-fiction dramas. His film credits included "Another Part of the Forest" (1948), "The Great Missouri Raid" (1951), "The Creature From the Black Lagoon" (1954) and "Birdman of Alcatraz" (1962).
        He is survived by three daughters, Kathy Marden, Victoria Brown and Amanda Whiteley; a son, Brian Forster, and six grandchildren.


        Carl Green Sr., a community leader in Brooklyn, died Thursday at his home in Crown Heights, Brooklyn. He was 70.
        He died of lung cancer, said a son, Roger L. Green, a State Assemblyman.
        Carl Green, who was born in Wilmington, N.C., served in World War II as a sergeant with the Army's 184th Combat Engineers, one of the all-black units. He became a New York City building inspector in 1963 and was a founder of the Progressive Inspectors Fraternity, an association of black city inspectors. He was a member of Community Board 9 in Brooklyn and served on many other community organizations.
        In addition to his son, Roger, of Brooklyn, Mr. Green is survived by his wife, Theopia; another son, Carl Jr., of Brooklyn; four sisters, two brothers and 11 grandchildren.

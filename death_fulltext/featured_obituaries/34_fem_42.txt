        Teresa S. Reardon, an administrative law judge in the New York City Parking Violations Bureau, died of cancer Thursday at Memorial Sloan-Kettering Cancer Center. She was 60 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Judge Reardon graduated from the College of Mount St. Vincent and received a master's degree from Columbia University and a law degree from St. John's University. She was an assistant United States attorney and a hearing examiner in Family Court before joining the Parking Violations Bureau.
        She is survived by her husband, Roy; three daughters, Abigail and Letitia, both of Manhattan, and Cordelia Laverack of Darien, Conn.; a son, Roy Jr., of Manhattan, and a grandchild.

        Morton S. Rogers, executive director of Louise Wise Services, a welfare organization for children and families, died Friday at his home in Mount Vernon, N.Y. He was 62 years old.
        Mr. Rogers joined Louise Wise in 1980. He had previously been associate director of the Children's Aid Society and an assistant deputy human resources administrator for New York City.
        He was born in Houston. Mr. Rogers graduated from Fisk University and earned a master's from Columbia University. He was a former vice president and board member of the Columbia University School of Social Work Alumni Association.
        Surviving are two brothers, Charles Jr., of Dallas, and William, of Los Angeles, and a sister, Lillian Randolph of Los Angeles.

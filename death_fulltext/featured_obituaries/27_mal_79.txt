        Capt. Burton O. Leeb, a retired United States Navy pathologist, died of cancer Tuesday at his home in McLean, Va. He was 53 years old.
        Dr. Leeb received the Meritorious Service Medal for his work in connection with the mass suicides and murder of more than 900 followers of the Rev. Jim Jones in Guyana in 1978.  Born in Brooklyn, he was a graduate of Davis and Elkins College and the University of Louvain in Belgium. He joined the Navy in 1970. Before he retired in 1988, he was chief of laboratory services in several hospitals and served at the Naval Medical Command in Washington.
        Surviving are his wife, Marilyn; a daughter, Rita, both of McLean; a son, Steven, of Boston, and a sister, Gloria Berkowitz, of North Woodmere, L.I.

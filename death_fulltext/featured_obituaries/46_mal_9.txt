
        Clayton H. Banzhaf, retired vice president and treasurer of Sears Roebuck & Company, died yesterday at his home in Blowing Rock, N.C. He was 72 years old and also lived in Longboat Key, Fla.
        Mr. Banzhaf died of a heart attack, a spokesman for the company said.
        Mr. Banzhaf joined Sears as a trainee in 1936 in Buffalo. After working in Sears offices in Washington and Pittsburgh, he moved to the company's national headquarters in Chicago in 1950. He was elected treasurer in 1974 and vice president and treasurer in 1976. He retired in 1981.
        In addition to his duties as treasurer, he was president, chiefexecutive officer and a director of Sears, Roebuck Acceptance Corporation, a subsidiary finance company, from 1963 to 1973.
        Mr. Banzhaf was also a director of Barclays American Corporation in Charlotte, N.C.
        He was born in Buffalo and received an M.B.A. degree from the University of Chicago.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Dolores Gapinski; two daughters, Debra York of Springfield, Va., and Barbara Grimmett of Fort Lauderdale, Fla.; a son, Dr. William C. of Evanston, Ill., and six grandchildren.

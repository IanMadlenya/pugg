
        Robert B. Miller, a psychiatrist, neurologist and educator died on Tuesday at his home in Weston, Conn. He was 74 years old.
        Dr. Miller died of lung cancer, a spokesman for the family said.
        Dr. Miller, who joined the faculty of Columbia University College of Physicians & Surgeons in the late 1940's was a Fellow of the New York Academy of Medicine and also maintained a private practice.
        In 1964 he moved to Connecticut to become chief of professional services at Connecticut Valley Hospital in Middletown and then superintendent of Fairfield Hills Hospital at Newtown.
        In 1981 Dr. Miller left state service to move to California because of illness. There he lectured in psychiatry at the University of California College of Medicine in San Francisco.
        In 1985 he returned to Connecticut and became a consultant to St. Vincent's Hospital in Bridgeport and to Norwalk Hospital in Norwalk. He continued to practice until quite recently.
        He was elected a Life Fellow of the American Psychiatric Association, and lectured at Yale Medical School.
        A native New Yorker, he was a graduate of Princeton University and Loyola College of Medicine. He served in the Navy and Marine Corps in World War II.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Shirley Kraft; a son, Nicholas of Manhattan, and a daughter, Andrea Wrensen of Byron, N.Y.; a step-son, John B. Charnay of Northridge, Calif., and a step-daughter, Dr. Joyce LeBeau of Tiburon, Calif.; a sister, Amy Shapiro of Stamford, Conn., and six grandchildren.

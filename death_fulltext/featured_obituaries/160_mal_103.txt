
        Gerald M. Kramer, a retired professor and former chairman of periodontology in the Boston University School of Dental Medicine, died on May 18 at a hospital in Boston after a long illness. He was 77 and lived in Swampscott, Mass.
        Dr. Kramer practiced periodontics, treating the bone and tissue surrounding the teeth, in Swamp scott and Lynn, Mass., for more than  50 years. He lectured widely at universities and dental societies.
        A native of Gloucester, Mass., he graduated from Tufts University, where he also received a doctor of dental medicine degree in 1944.
        He became a full professor and department chairman at Boston University in 1968 and retired in 1980.
        Dr. Kramer's survivors include his wife of 55 years, Sylvia Sher Kramer; a son, Lloyd, of New York City; a daughter, Leslie Kramer of San Francisco; and a brother, Hilton, of New York City.

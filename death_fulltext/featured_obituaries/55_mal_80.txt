
        John Wilkie, a former president and chairman of Central Hudson Gas and Electric Corporation, died on Saturday at Northern Westchester Hospital in Mount Kisco, N.Y. He was 87 years old and lived in Katonah, N.Y.
        He died of complications after surgery, his wife said.
        Mr. Wilkie also served as chairman of the Vassar College Board of Trustees from 1961 to 1969 and was a trustee emeritus at the time of his death. While he was chairman, Vassar opened its admissions to men as well as women.
        Mr. Wilkie, who worked for 62 years as a director of Central Hudson Gas and Electric, was active in several regional organizations. He served for many years as a director of the Regional Plan Association and was a past chairman of the Hudson River Conservation Society. He had also been a director of the Marine Midland Bank.
        He graduated from Harvard College in 1923 and from Harvard Business School in 1925. He was a member of the Century Club, the Harvard Club, the Down Town Association, the Edgartown Yacht Club in Massachusetts and the Amrita Club of Poughkeepsie. His first marriage, to Dorothea Jones, ended in divorce.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Margot Loines; two daughters, Rennie Lieber of New Haven and Edith, of Washington; two sons, John McNeil of Darien, Conn., and Peter L. of Poughkeepsie; two stepdaughters, Faith Morrow Williams of Washington and Constance Murrow Fulenwider of Weston, Mass.; seven grandchildren, and eight grandchildren.

        Wilfred Wilson, a lawyer and retired senior vice president of a national teachers' pension fund, died on Aug. 26 at Sydney City Hospital in Sydney, Nova Scotia. He was 68 years old and lived in Port Washington, L.I.
        He died of congestive heart failure, his wife, Emily, said.
        Mr. Wilson retired in 1986 from the Teachers Insurance and Annuity Association-College Retirement Equities Fund, a nationwide pension system for higher education. He had joined the company in 1956 and was elected an executive officer in 1963. Before joining the fund, he was in private practice in Toronto.
        He graduated from the Osgoode Hall Law School in Toronto.
        Besides his wife, he is survived by three daughters, Susan, of Naugatuck, Conn., Dr. Mary Wilson of Iowa City, and Helen Wilson of Birmingham, Ala., and his mother, Edythe Snyder of Fergus, Ontario.

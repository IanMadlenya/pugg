        Edward J. Connolly, a lawyer in Brooklyn for almost half a century, died of cancer yesterday at his home in Brightwater, L.I. He was 74 years old.
        Mr. Connolly, a graduate of the Columbia University Law School, was a founding partner in the firm of Connolly, Frey, Eschmann & La Pasta and was active until several months ago. He was also a former president of the Brooklyn Bar Association and a member of the House of Delegates of the New York State Bar Association.
        He is survived by a son, Edward, of Phoenix; a daughter, Jane Schwarz of Manhattan; six stepchildren, William Sheehan of Mount Vernon, N.Y.; Kathleen Burke of Stamford, Conn.; Jane Murphy of Princeton, N.J.; Clare and Kevin Sheehan, both of Brooklyn, and Brian Sheehan of Tokyo, and two sisters, Grace Belford of Bay Shore, L.I., and Jean Young of Brooklyn.

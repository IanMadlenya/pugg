        Dr. John W. Bromley, an orthopedist who was doing extensive research on use of the chemical collagen to avert surgery for debilitating back pain, died yesterday battling heart disease and cancer at St. Joseph's Hospital in Paterson, N.J. He was 62 years old and lived in North Haven, L.I.
        Dr. Bromley had a practice and a home in Paterson for more than 25 years and taught at New York University Medical School for 20 years. He was a graduate of Cornell College and the Cornell University-New York Hospital School of Medicine and interned at Lenox Hill Hospital.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Jeanne Trent; his mother, Louise Oswell of North Haven; a brother, Leo, of Nutley, N.J.; two daughters, Carol, of Orange, N.J., and Beth, of Los Angeles; two sons, Trent, of Manhattan, and Christopher, of Orange.

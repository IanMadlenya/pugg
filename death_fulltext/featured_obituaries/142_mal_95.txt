
        Emil G. Reynolds, a globe-trotting press photographer who covered World War II and other events, died last Sunday at Monmouth Medical Center in Long Branch, N.J. He was 93 and lived in Long Branch.
        Mr. Reynolds, who was born in Vienna but grew up in Newark, supplied his work to United Press International, The Associated Press and various newspapers. In World War II he covered nearly all fronts, and used his fluent German at the Potsdam peace conference and Nuremberg war trials .
        His photographs appeared on the front pages of Newsweek, Time and Life magazines, among others.
        Mr. Reynolds is survived by a son, Paul, of Tempe, Ariz., and two grandsons. His wife, Clara Silverman Reynolds, died in 1991.

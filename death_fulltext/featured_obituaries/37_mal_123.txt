        Ethelbert Herrick Low, a banker who worked in New York and in San Francisco, died of a stroke on Wednesday at Mills Memorial Hospital in San Mateo, Calif. He was 83 years old and lived in Hillsborough, Calif.
        Mr. Low joined the First National Bank of New York in 1925. In 1929, he became vice president of the Corn Exchange Bank and Trust Company of New York. When it merged with the Chemical Bank in 1954, he was named executive vice president.
        In 1956, Mr. Low moved to San Francisco, where he joined the First Western Bank and Trust Company. He became a senior vice president of the United California Bank in 1961, when it merged with First Western. He became executive vice president in 1963 and vice chairman in 1968. Mr. Low retired in 1972.
        He was an alumnus of the St. Paul's School in Concord, N.H.
        Surviving are his wife, Adelaide; a son, E. Holland, of Longmeadow, Mass.; a daughter, Elizabeth Stewart of Bloomfield, Conn., and six grandchildren.

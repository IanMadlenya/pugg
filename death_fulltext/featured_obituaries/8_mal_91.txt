        Dr. Donald D. Ritchie, a former chairman of the biological sciences department at Barnard College and an expert on marine fungi, died of pancreatic cancer Thursday at St. Luke's Hospital Center in Manhattan. He was 73 years old.
        Dr. Ritchie, who was born in Atlanta, received a bachelor's in English from Furman University in Greenville, S.C., in 1933. A year later, after undertaking the study of botany, he received another bachelor's, in science, from Furman. In 1947, he received a doctorate in botany from the University of North Carolina.
        During World War II, Dr. Ritchie served in the Navy.
        From 1938 until 1948, he was on the faculty of the University of West Virginia. He was later appointed an assistant professor at Barnard. From 1952 until his retirement in 1979, he served as chairman of Barnard's department of biological sciences.
        In 1955, under a grant from the National Academy of Sciences, he went to Panama to study the effect of marine fungi on tropical diseases.
        Dr. Ritchie's wife, Nancy, died in 1980. He is survived by two daughters, Nancy Ritchie-Noakes of Cheshire, England, and Alice Joy of Darby, England; a son, Douglas, of Six Mile, S.C.; a sister, Virginia Jones of Wilkesboro, N.C.; four grandchildren, and his companion, Ellen Kendrick.

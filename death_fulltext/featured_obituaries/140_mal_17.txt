
        Stanley Seiden, an executive of the Nederlander theater organization, died on Aug. 27 at Cedars-Sinai Medical Center in Los Angeles. He was 76 and lived in Beverly Hills, Calif.
        The cause was Lou Gehrig's disease, company officials said.
        Mr. Seiden was president of the James M. Nederlander Companies West since 1975. He was resposible for all operations of the Greek, Pantages, Wilshire and Henry Fonda theaters in Los Angeles as well as the Los Angeles Civic Light Opera and all other Nederlander enterprises in Southern California.
        Born in New York, Mr. Seiden began his career as a Broadway press agent, representing the Shubert organization and the vaudeville team of Olsen and Johnson, among other clients, and eventually becoming a producer and presenter of shows. After moving to California, he originated the San Diego Playgoers Series and was instrumental in the building of the Pacific Amphitheater in Costa Mesa, Calif.
        He is survived by his wife, Rivka; two sons, Darren and Steve, both of Beverly Hills, and two sisters, Marilyn Lehrbaum and Ruth Gale, both of New York City.

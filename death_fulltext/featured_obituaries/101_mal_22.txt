
        Keith R. Garrity, chairman and chief executive of Fansteel Inc., a specialty  metals producer in North Chicago, died on Thursday at Rush-Presbyterian-St. Luke's Medical Center in Chicago. He was 63 and lived in Libertyville, Ill.
        The cause was cancer, said a spokesman for the company, which produces specialty metals used in aircraft, aerospace and weapon system components.
        Mr. Garrity was born in Portage, Me., and graduated from Northwestern University. He earned an M.B.A. from the University of Chicago. He began his business career at Fansteel in 1954.
        He rose through various executive positions and was elected president, chief  operating officer and a director in 1977. He became chairman and chief executive officer in 1982. In the 1980's he was president and later chairman of what was then Fansteel's parent company, H. K. Porter Company Inc. of Pittsburgh.
        In addition to other business affiliations and directorships, Mr. Garrity was a past chairman of the Greater Pittsburgh Chamber of Commerce and the Boy Scouts of America Allegheny Trails Council in Pittsburgh.
        He is survived by his wife, Millie Johnson Garrity; two daughters, Susan Shamrock of Gurnee, Ill., and Michele Neville of Wadsworth, Ill.; a son, Daniel, also of Gurnee; five grandchildren and a great-grandson.


        Dr. T. William Brockbank, a psychiatrist who was medical director of the Guidance Institute of Catholic Charities of New York City for 35 years, died on Tuesday in Watervliet, N.Y., where he was visiting. He was 97 years old and lived in Del Ray Beach, Fla., and for many years had been a resident of Scarsdale, N.Y.
        In 1931 Dr. Brockbank organized the psychiatric clinic that became the Guidance Institute, and directed it until he retired in 1966. He also maintained a private practice for many years in White Plains.
        He was born in DuBois, Pa., was a graduate of St. Bonaventure College and held a doctorate in psychology from Catholic University and a medical degree from Georgetown University.
        He was a founder of the American Psychological Association in 1925.
        His wife, Dr. Margert Halleran Brockbank, died in 1987. He is survived by four daughters, Margaret Lowright of Selinsgrove, Pa.; Eileen Hodgson of Manhattan, Priscilla Griffin of Latham, N.Y., and Susan McCullough of Montgomery, Ala., and two granddaughters.

        Richard M. Goldwater, a Manhattan lawyer and former New York State Assemblyman, died of complications of pneumonia Friday at Mount Sinai Medical Center in Manhattan. He was 70 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Born in the Bronx, Mr. Goldwater was managing partner, until 1981, of Goldwater & Flynn, a Manhattan law firm. He graduated from Yale College and Yale Law School.
        A Democrat, he was the State Assemblyman from the 2d District, in the Bronx, from 1947 to 1953. A specialist in housing and urban development, Mr. Goldwater served as counsel to the New York State Joint Legislative Committee on Housing and Urban Development in 1965 and 1966.
        He is survived by his wife, Maria Udenburgh Goldwater; three sons, David, of Denver, Daniel, of Rosemont, Pa., and Harry, of Silver Spring, Md.; a brother, James, of Pleasantville, N.Y. and Manhattan; a sister, Mary Abrons of Manhattan, and six grandchildren.

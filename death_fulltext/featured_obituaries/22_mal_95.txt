        Gerry LaFurn, a jazz trumpeter who performed with Stan Kenton, Woody Herman, Buddy Rich and others, died of liver cancer Wednesday at St. Rose's Hospice in Manhattan. He was 59 years old.
        Mr. LaFurn recorded with Frank Sinatra, Tony Bennett, Barbra Streisand and others, and was the lead trumpeter with many big bands. He also performed and did arranging for Broadway shows, and led a 17-piece group called Superband in the 1970's and 1980's.
        Mr. LaFurn is survived by his wife, Ellen, of Manhattan; his daughter, Gina, of Manhattan; his brothers, Edmund LaFurno, of Dunedin, Fla., and Guy LaFurno, of Kissimmee, Fla.; and his sisters, Marie Del Prete, of Sun Lakes, Ariz., and Lila Moray, of Dunedin.


        Edward P. Hutchinson, professor emeritus of sociology at the University of Pennsylvania, died Sunday at Bryn Mawr Hospital in Pennsylvania. He was 84 years old and lived in Strafford, Pa.
        He died of heart failure, said his daughter, Joan, of St. Paul.
        Dr. Hutchinson joined the faculty at Penn in 1943 and taught until his retirement in 1976. A demographer, his research focused on immigration. He wrote several books on the subject.
        He was a trustee emeritus of the Balch Institute of Ethnic Studies, an organization he helped found in 1982.
        Besides his daughter, he is survived by a son, John, of Seattle; a sister, Dorothy McCabe of Concord, N.H., and two grandchildren.

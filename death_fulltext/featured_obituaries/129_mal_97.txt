
        Arthur Stephen Lane, a lawyer and a former United States District judge, and a former chairman of the New Jersey State Commission of Investigation, died on Thursday at his home in Princeton. He was 86.
        Most recently, he was a partner in the Trenton firm of Smith, Stratton, Wise, Heher & Brennan. He became counsel in 1986 and retired last year.
        Mr. Lane began his career in private practice in Trenton and served as an assistant Mercer County prosecutor. After an unsuccessful run for the State Senate as a Republican, he was named to the Mercer County bench in 1956.
        After President Dwight D. Eisenhower appointed him to the District Court, he sat as the sole Federal judge in Trenton, hearing civil suits and criminal cases from Fort Dix. He resigned in 1967.
        Later that year, he was offered the post of general counsel at the pharmaceutical company Johnson & Johnson, and a vice presidency and board membership followed. He left in 1976, the year he joined Smith Stratton as a partner and expert in alternative dispute resolution.
        He was named to the State Commission of Investigation in 1977, reappointed in 1979 and became its  chairman. He retired in 1985.
        A native of Arlington, Mass., Mr. Lane graduated from Princeton University in 1934 and finished Harvard Law School in 1937.
        He is survived by his wife of 50 years, Sally Kuser Lane; their seven children, Sally, of Trenton, A. Stephen Jr., of Groton, Mass., Mark, of Verona, N.J., Catherine S., of Colorado Springs, Henry W., of San Luis Obispo, Calif., Mary L. McNamara of Concord, Mass., and Teresa D., of Greenwich, Conn.; a sister, Louise Parker of Revere, Mass.; two brothers, Philip, of Lexington, Mass., and Robert R., of Phoenix,; 12 grandchildren and 3 great-grandchildren.

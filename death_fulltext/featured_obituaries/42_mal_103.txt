        Earl Stewart, who won two events on the PGA Tour and coached golf for 13 years at Southern Methodist University, died Wednesday after a long illness. He was 68 years old.
        As a player at Louisiana State, Mr. Stewart won the National Collegiate Athletic Association individual title in 1941 and led the Tigers to the N.C.A.A. team title in 1942. As a professional, he won the 1953 Greensboro Open and the 1961 Dallas Open. As a coach at S.M.U. from 1975 to 1987, he won a national championship with the women's team in 1979 and coached a men's team that included Payne Stewart, no relation, now a star on the professional tour.
        Mr. Stewart is survived by his wife, Dot, and two sons, Chip and Mark.

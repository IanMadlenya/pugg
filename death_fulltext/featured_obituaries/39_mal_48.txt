        Dr. Norman T. Crane, a New Jersey pediatrician for more than four decades, died of a blood clot on the brain on Thursday at a nursing home in Green Brook, N.J. He was 88 years old and lived in Plainfield, N.J.
        Dr. Crane, who had a private practice in Plainfield, was elected to the board of governors of the Muhlenberg Regional Medical Center in 1957. Before retiring from the center in 1980 he served as chief of staff, chief of pediatrics and chief of the ambulatory health care center.
        He was a graduate of Dartmouth College and received his medical degree in 1925 from the College of Physicians and Surgeons at Columbia University.
        He was a lieutenant colonel in the Army and was awarded the Legion of Merit.
        His wife, the former Marian Bradley, died of nephrosclerosis yesterday at the same nursing home.
        Survivors include two daughters, Shirley Reynolds of Hamilton, N.Y., and Joan Freeman of Long Valley, N.J.; a sister, Ruth Agor of Mahopac, N.Y.; six grandchildren, and eight great-grandchildren survive.

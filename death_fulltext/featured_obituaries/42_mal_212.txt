        Gordon A. Ewing, who directed United States radio programming in occupied Germany after World War II, died Tuesday at Thomas Hospital in Fairhope, Ala. He was 77 years old and lived in Fairhope. His family said he died of pneumonia.
        Mr. Ewing was political director and later head of RIAS, the German-language station that began broadcasting in 1946 from the American sector of Berlin. From 1957 to 1959, he was deputy director of Voice of America.
        Mr. Ewing, who was born in Newfane, N.Y., received bachelor's and master's degrees from Wayne State University and taught English literature there from 1935 to 1938.
        In World War II, he was an intelligence officer in the Seventh Army in Europe, rising to the rank of major and receiving the Bronze Star and the French Croix de Guerre.
        After serving with RIAS and the Voice of America, he became a public affairs officer at American embassies in Vienna, Rome and Bonn. He retired in 1972.
        Mr. Ewing is survived by his wife, the former Marjorie Edwards, and a son, John, of Delano, Calif.

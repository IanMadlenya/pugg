
        William J. Kridel, a partner in a Manhattan law firm, died at Mount Sinai Medical Center on Thursday. He was 76 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Kridel died of complications after heart surgery, his family said.
        For nearly two decades he served as a trustee of the Collegiate preparatory school and of Mount Sinai Medical Center. He was also a trustee of Mount Sinai's School of Medicine and president of the Mount Sinai School of Nursing and the Ruth Turner Fund, a medical foundation. His grandfather, Isaac Wallach, was the founding president of Mount Sinai hospital.
        Born in Manhattan, Mr. Kridel earned his undergraduate and law degrees at Columbia University. He worked for the State Labor Relations Board for several years and then joined the law firm now known as Dewey Ballantine Bushby Palmer & Wood and became head of its labor law division.
        In 1956 he formed his own firm, now known as Kridel & Neuwirth, and continued practicing until his illness.
        He is survived by his wife, Carol H.; a son, William J. Kridel Jr. of London, managing director of Barclays DeZoete Wedd, and a daughter, Linda K. Smith Ferrall of Beloit, Wis., a lawyer.

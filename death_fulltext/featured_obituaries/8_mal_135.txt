        Ned E. Jaffa, a noted chemical engineer, died of a heart attack Tuesday at his home in Linden, N.J. He was 86 years old.
        For 35 years, Mr. Jaffa was employed as a research engineer by the Food Machinery and Chemical Corporation of Carteret, N.J. In that capacity, he was instrumental in developing processes for the use of phosphates in detergents.
        Mr. Jaffa, a Brooklyn native, was a member of the first class of chemical engineering to graduate from New York University, in 1921. In 1927, he earned a master's degree in chemical engineering from the Brooklyn Polytechnic Institute.
        After his retirement in 1965, Mr. Jaffa became a professor of chemistry at Rutgers University.
        He is survived by his wife, Mae; two daughters, Faith Goldstein of Rumson, N.J., and Marcella Low of Ridgewood, N.J., and three grandchildren.

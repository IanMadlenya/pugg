
        Harold J. Treanor, a lawyer who served as counsel to the Real Estate Board of New York for almost 50 years, died on Tuesday at New Rochelle Hospital. He was 91 years old and lived in Pelham, N.Y.
        He died of cardiac arrest, his family said.
        Mr. Treanor was born in Yonkers and was a graduate of Fordham and the Fordham Law School. He was a member of the Manhattan law firm of Cox, Treanor & Schaughnessy. He was counsel to the realty group from 1927 until 1975. He retired in 1989.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Edith Kelly; two sons, William, of Manhattan and John, of Ridgewood, N.J., and a granddaughter.

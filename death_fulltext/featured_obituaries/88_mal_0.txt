
        Dr. Edward P. Perley, a retired ophthalmologist who practiced in Manhattan for more than 30 years and who also was a professor at the Columbia-Presbyterian Medical Center, died on Thursday while playing golf at the Westchester Country Club in Rye. Dr. Perley, who lived in Rye Brook, was 72.
        The cause was cardiac arrest, said his wife, Elizabeth.
        Dr. Perley was an attending ophthalmic surgeon at the Manhattan Eye, Ear and Throat Hospital. He retired in 1987. He was a graduate of Manhattan College, the New York Medical College and the New York University School of Medicine.
        Dr. Perley is survived by his wife, of Rye Brook; two daughters, Rosemary Kwauk of Manhattan and Susan Kennedy of Darien, Conn., and two sons, Edward P. Jr. of Port Chester, and William E. of Manhattan.

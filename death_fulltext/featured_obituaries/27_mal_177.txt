        Anthony M. Yelencsics, the Mayor of Edison Township, N.J., died, apparently of a heart attack, yesterday at his home there. He was 67 years old and nearing the end of his sixth four-year term.
        Mr. Yelencsics was Mayor of the Middlesex County community from 1958 to 1969 and again from 1978 to the present. He planned to seek a seventh term in the November elections. He was a founder and chairman of the John F. Kennedy Medical Center in Edison and was also the founder and president of Boro Motors, a Ford automobile dealership in Metuchen, N.J.. Mr. Yelencsics was a graduate of Rider College in Lawrenceville, N.J. He was a member of many community and veteran organizations, including the Kiwanis, Elks, American Legion and Disabled Veterans. During World War II, he served as a Marine Corps captain on Okinawa.
        He is survived by his wife, Norma; a son, Anton, of Edison; a daughter, Mary Ann Karabinchak, of New Brunswick, N.J.; three brothers, Julius and Stephen, both of Edison, and Ernest, of Lavalette, N.J.; two sisters, Mary Swales, of Edison, and Helen Toth, of Metuchen, and one grandson.

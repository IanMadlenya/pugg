        Dr. Carl Reich, a physician and hematologist, died of heart failure Thursday at Lenox Hill Hospital. He was 88 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Dr. Reich was on the Lenox Hill staff 60 years, retiring in 1984 as chief of hematology. He was a former professor at the New York Medical College and was the first American doctor to perform bone-marrow examination by sternum puncture to diagnose malignancies.
        Surviving are two sons, Charles, of San Francisco, and Peter, of Boston.

        James N. Black, a jazz drummer who played with such musicians as Lionel Hampton, Cannonball Adderley, John Coltrane and Dr. John, died Tuesday. He was 48 years old. The cause of death was not announced, pending the result of an autopsy.
        Mr. Black attended Southern University in Baton Rouge, La., before leaving college to play with Ellis Marsalis at the Playboy Club in New Orleans.
        His work with Mr. Marsalis and others resulted in the 1963 album, ''Monkey Puzzle.'' Mr. Black also performed on the ''Adderley Brothers'' album with Cannonball and Nat Adderley in 1963.
        In the 1960's, he toured with musicians such as Mr. Hampton, Dr. John, Freddie Hubbard and the Dukes of Dixieland.
        The National Endowment for the Arts gave Mr. Black two fellowships for his work as a composer.

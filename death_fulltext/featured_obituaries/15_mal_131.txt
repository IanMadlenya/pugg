        Theodore R. Urban, an upholsterer who did work for the White House, died of lung cancer Wednesday at St. Francis Hospital in Roslyn, L.I. He was 58 years old and lived in Middle Village, Queens.
        Mr. Urban's company, Baron Upholsterers in Manhattan, did extensive work for museums across the country, including the Metropolitan Museum of Art, and made drapes and did wall upholstering for the White House during the Nixon Administration.
        Survivors include his wife, Ingeborg; a daughter, Vienna Campbell of Boston, and two sons, Stephen, of Roslyn, and Paul, of Queens.

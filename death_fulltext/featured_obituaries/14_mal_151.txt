        Dr. Leon Ginzburg, a surgeon, teacher and expert in gastrointestinal diseases, died of cardiac and renal failure Saturday at the Beth Israel Medical Center. He was 89 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        In collaboration with Dr. Burrill Bernard Crohn and Dr. Gordon D. Oppenheimer, Dr. Ginzburg wrote the first description of ileitis in 1932, for the Journal of the American Medical Association. Ileitis is an inflammation of the ileum, the lower part of the small intestine.
        From 1947 to 1967, Dr. Ginzburg was director of surgery of Beth Israel Hospital, as the medical center was then known. In the 1950's, he was president of Beth Israel's medical board.
        Dr. Ginzburg also was associated for 40 years with the Mount Sinai Medical Center. He was clinical professor of surgery for many years at the Mount Sinai School of Medicine. He retired from Mount Sinai in 1963.
        Between 1930 and 1939, Dr. Ginzburg also helped reorganize the surgical services at Harlem Hospital.
        There are no survivors.

        Clifford Jackson, a baritone and coach, died Monday at his home in Manhattan. He was 71 years old.
        Mr. Jackson was a member of the faculty of the American Academy of Dramatic Arts from 1954 to 1971, where his students included the actor Robert Redford and the soprano Joan Morris. With Ms. Morris and her husband, William Bolcom, Mr. Jackson made two albums for Nonesuch Records, devoted to the songs of Henry Clay Work and Henry Russell. He also appeared throughout the United States with opera companies, experimental workshops and in concert.

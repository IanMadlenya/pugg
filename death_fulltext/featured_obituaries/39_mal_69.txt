        George Strawbridge Sr., a stockbroker, suffered a heart attack on April 3 at a business meeting at the Union League in Philadelphia and was pronounced dead at Thomas Jefferson Hospital there. He was 80 years old.
        Mr. Strawbridge was a senior vice president and director of Janney Montgomery Scott Inc., a Philadelphia stockbrokerage. He had been a partner with Montgomery Scott since 1969 and when the firm merged with Janney, Battles & E. W. Clark in 1971, he was made a senior vice president. From 1979 to 1984, he was also a director of the IVB Financial Corporation.
        In Army Quartermaster Corps
        Mr. Strawbridge, who lived in Malvern, Pa., was president of the National Bank of Malvern from 1961 to 1967 and president of the Aero Service Corporation, an aerial surveying company, from 1959 to 1961.
        After serving as a major in the Army Quartermaster Corps in World War II, he joined the Campbell Soup Company in 1945 and served as assistant to its treasurer until 1957.
        Mr. Strawbridge was a 1933 graduate of Princeton University and an amateur steeplechase rider.
        With Steeplechase Association
        In the 1930's, he won several races and was awarded the Harston Cup in 1936. He joined the board of the National Steeplechase and Hunt Association in 1969 and served as its secretary in 1971 and president in 1975.
        He was a trustee and president of the Margaret Dorrance Strawbridge Foundation, a philanthropy named for his late first wife, who died in 1953, and a trustee of the Steeplechase Fund for Injured Jockeys.
        Mr. Strawbridge is survived by his wife, Brunner; a son, George, of Unionville, Del.; a daughter, Diana Norris, of Palm Beach, Fla.; a stepson, Edgar R. Owen, of Malvern, Pa.; a sister, Louise Hunsicker of Plymouth Meeting, Pa., and six grandchildren.

        Arthur Schoenhaut, a retired Federal official, died of a heart attack Saturday at Peninsula General Hospital in Salisbury, Md. He was 64 years old and lived in Bethesda, Md.
        A graduate of City College of New York, Mr. Schoenhaut worked in finance in Washington from 1950 to 1980. He retired in 1982 as budget director of the International Atomic Energy Agency.
        He is survived by his wife, Betty; a son, Robert, of Manhattan; a daughter, Carrie Bristol, of Fort Lauderdale, Fla.; a sister, Irene Males, of Houston; and a stepbrother, Alex Meshbane, of Syosset, L.I.

        Coleman J. Graham, a former official with the private investigation agency, Pinkerton's Inc., died of pneumonia on Tuesday at Palms of Pasadena Hospital in St. Petersburg. Fla. He was 84 years old and lived in North Reddington Beach, Fla.
        Mr. Graham joined Pinkerton's in 1935 as an investigator in New York. He was later transferred to the West Coast but returned to New York in 1960 to help develop the security program for the New York World's Fair. In 1964, he was named a vice president and assistant general manager and three years later was elected executive vice president. He retired in 1971. He also served on the board of directors of the investigation agency and was a trustee of the Pinkerton Foundation.
        He is survived by two sisters, Elizabeth McCormack and Florence Conroy, both of Lynbrook, L.I.

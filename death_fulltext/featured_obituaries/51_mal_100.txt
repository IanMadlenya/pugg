
        Richard M. Rosen, a New York lawyer who was assistant for cultural affairs in John V. Lindsay's first administration, died on Thursday at his home in Manhattan. He was 55 years old.
        He died of pneumonia, his brother, Hilton, said.
        At his death, Mr. Rosen was counsel to the Minskoff Organization, a real-estate concern.
        He was admitted to the New York State Bar in 1961 and appointed assistant counsel to the New York State Assembly in 1962, after serving as legislative assistant to Congresswoman Katherine St. George in Washington and Albany. A year later, he was named executive assistant to Attorney General Louis J. Lefkowitz.
        Mr. Rosen, a Republican, served as an aide to Mr. Lindsay, working on special projects like bringing filmmaking to the city. He was also a member of the State Council on Narcotic Addiction and the New York State Council of Law Enforcement Officers.
        He was born in South Fallsburg, N.Y., and graduated from Dartmouth College in 1956, received an M.A. from the Amos Tuck School of Business Administration in 1957 and the New York University School of Law in 1960.
        Besides his brother, he is survived by his parents, Philip and Beatrice Rosen of South Fallsburg.

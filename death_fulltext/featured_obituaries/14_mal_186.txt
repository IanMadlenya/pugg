        Rabbi Samuel J. Gedalowitz of the Civic Center Synagogue in lower Manhattan died of cancer today at Booth Memorial Hospital in Queens. He was 59 years old and lived in Kew Gardens Hills, Queens.
        Rabbi Gedalowitz had headed the synagogue, near City Hall, for five years.
        He is survived by his wife, Sandra; four sons, Yitzchak, of Silver Spring, Md., Israel, of Oakland, Calif., Yehudah, of Brooklyn and David, of Kew Gardens Hills; a sister, Sylvia Zuckerman of Israel and Miami Beach, Fla.; two brothers, Joseph, of Brooklyn, and Moshe, of Israel, and one grandson.

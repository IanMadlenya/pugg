
        Zane Klein, general counsel of Guardsmark Inc., a Memphis-based national security and investigative service, died on June 27 after collapsing in his office in Memphis. He was 60 and had lived in Memphis since moving from Manhattan in 1993.
        The company said the cause was thought to be a pulmonary embolism.
        He joined the company after more than 30 years as a lawyer and partner in law firms in Manhattan, specializing in corporate, financial and securities matters.
        He graduated from Princeton University in 1958 and from Yale Law School in 1961. While in New York, he was a representative of the city's Board of Estimate on the Municipal Assistance Corporation in the late 1970's.
        He is survived by his wife of 26 years, Barbara Schreiber Klein; two daughters, Amanda Trilling Klein of Manhattan and Bettina Sheppard Duffy of Bethesda, Md.; a son, Andrew Todd Sheppard of Malvern, Pa., and a sister, Gilda Becker of Cranbury, N.J.

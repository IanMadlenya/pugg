        Henry J. Mali, chief executive officer of the nation's oldest and largest manufacturer of billiard table cloth, died yesterday at his home in Manhattan. He was 87 years old.
        Mr. Mali was the chief executive officer of Henry W. T. Mali and Company, a concern of his family founded in 1826. He served as the company's president from 1940 to 1965.
        Born in Plainfield, N.J., in 1899, Mr. Mali graduated from Yale University in 1921 and joined his family's company two years later. His wife of 50 years, Katherine Lord Mali, died in 1980.
        Mr. Mali is survived by a sister, Eva Noyes of Oyster Bay, L.I.; three sons, Frederick J., H. Allen, and Derek L., all of Manhattan, and five grandchildren.
        A memorial service will be held tomorrow at 1 P.M. at the Madison Avenue Presbyterian Church, at East 73d Street.

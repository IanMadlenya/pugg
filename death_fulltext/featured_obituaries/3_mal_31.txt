        Chogyam Trungpa Rinpoche, the Buddhist leader who founded the Naropa Institute in Boulder, Colo., died Saturday at a hospital in Halifax, Nova Scotia, of cardiac arrest and respiratory failure, officials said. He was 47 years old.
        A Tibetan Buddhist who began his training at the age of 5, he went to Boulder in 1970 and four years later founded the Naropa Institute. Naropa is an accredited liberal arts college combining Eastern and Western fields of study.
        He introduced thousands of people to Buddhism. About 3,000 followers practiced his teachings at centers he founded in the United States, Canada and Europe.
        Survivors include his wife and five sons.

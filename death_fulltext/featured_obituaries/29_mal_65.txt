        Donald L. Katz, a retired educator, chemical engineer and internationally known expert on oil engineering, died May 29 at his home in Ann Arbor, Mich., after a long illness. He was 81 years old.
        Educated at the University of Michigan, Dr. Katz earned a Ph.D. in chemical engineering there in 1933 and joined the faculty in 1936. He was chairman of the department from 1951 to 1962. He received numerous awards and was named professor emeritus in 1977.
        Dr. Katz wrote 10 books, including an industry standard, ''The Handbook of Natural Gas Engineering,'' published by McGraw Hill in 1959.
        He leaves his wife, Elizabeth; five children, Marvin, of Dallas; Linda Cantrell, of Ann Arbor; Richard Correll, of Ann Arbor; Steven Correll, of Chelsea, Mich., and Jonathan Correll, of San Jose, Calif.; a sister, Lola Parks, of Jackson, Mich.; 10 grandchildren, and two great-grandchildren.

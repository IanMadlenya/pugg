
        Ben Benjamin, an agent who represented Burt Lancaster, Sir John Gielgud and Ingrid Bergman, among others, in a longtime Hollywood career, died of cancer on Thursday. He was 80 years old.
        He died at his home in the Brentwood section of Los Angeles, said his spokesman, Dick Winters.
        Mr. Benjamin was born in Butte, Mont., and was a graduate of the University of California at Berkeley. After a stint with the United States Navy during World War II, he joined an agency whose clients included Greer Garson, Paul Muni and Merle Oberon. In 1950 he joined Famous Artists, which later became International Creative Management. There his client list included Albert Finney and Richard Widmark, as well as the writers A. B. Guthrie and William Faulkner.
        He is survived by his wife, Carlyn; a daughter, Lisa; a son, Jeff; a sister, Rosalie, and a granddaughter.

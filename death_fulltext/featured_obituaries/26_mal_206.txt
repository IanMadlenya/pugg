        Reginald Miller Sutton, who in 60 years with the Union Pacific Railroad rose from office boy to executive vice president, died of heart failure Monday at Bishop Clarkson Memorial Hospital in Omaha. He was 90 years old and lived in Omaha.
        Mr. Sutton, who served in the Navy in World War I, joined Union Pacific in 1915 and by 1955 was a vice president and general auditor. In 1960 he was named vice president-controller and was elected to the board of directors. He became executive vice president in 1960 and retired in 1975.
        He served on finance committees of the Episcopal Church and was a trustee of Bishop Clarkson Memorial Hospital.
        He is survived by three daughters, Betty Jane McVicar, of Winchester, Mass.; Alice Nancy Gilmore, of Omaha, and Beverly Ann Ward, of Lincoln, Neb.; 10 grandchildren and 5 great-grandchildren.


        Leland Locke Tolman, a lawyer and former New York State court administrator, died on Friday at his home in Castine, Me. He was 82 years old.
        He died of cancer, said his stepson, Clergue Jones of Lanham, Md.
        Mr. Tolman served with the Department of Justice for 20 years as a special attorney and in other legal posts before joining the New York State Judicial Conference in 1955 as deputy administrator of the First Judicial Department. When he retired in 1972, he was director of administration of the courts in the First Department.
        Mr. Tolman, a 1933 graduate of the University of Chicago Law School, served as a consultant to the New York State Legislative Commission on Court Reorganization. In the 1950's he was a consultant on court reorganization for the United States High Commissioner for Germany. He was a lieutenant commander in the Navy during World War II.
        In addition to his stepson, he is survived by his wife, Sally; a stepdaughter, Clarissa Parker of Washington, and three grandchildren.


        Jeff Morrow, a stage, film and television actor, died on Sunday at a nursing home in Canoga Park, Calif., after a long illness. He was 86.
        Mr. Morrow was born in New York City. After serving in the Army during World War II, he played the title role in the "Dick Tracy" radio series. He appeared in many Broadway productions, notably "Billy Budd," the Maurice Evans production of "Macbeth" and Katharine Cornell's production of "Romeo and Juliet."
        In the 1950's, he went to Hollywood to act in films. He appeared in "The Robe," "Captain Lightfoot," the Jerry Lewis-Dean Martin movie "Pardners" and the science-fiction films "This Island Earth" and "The Creature Walks Among Us."
        In addition to making guest appearances on television in "Bonanza" and "The Twilight Zone," he starred in the series "Union Pacific."
        He is survived by his wife, Anna Karen, and a daughter, Lissa Morrow-Christian of New York.

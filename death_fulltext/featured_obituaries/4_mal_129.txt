        Alvin D. Chandler, former president of the College of William and Mary, died of a heart ailment Tuesday at the Virginia Beach General Hospital. He was 84 years old.
        Mr. Chandler was a rear admiral in charge of planning naval logistics when the college's board of visitors selected him in 1951 to head William and Mary, the nation's second oldest second oldest institution of higher learning after Harvard.
        He was president of the college, whose campus is in Williamsburg, Va., until 1960, when he was appointed chancellor of the Colleges of William and Mary, consisting of five colleges in the state. He held that post until 1962.
        Mr. Chandler's predecessor had resigned over a scandal involving grades being given to athletes, and one of his first decisions was to pledge a single admission standard for all students.
        College officials credit Mr. Chandler with expanding the faculty, overseeing the construction of five major campus buildings and introducing a series of curriculum changes.
        Mr. Chandler's father, Dr. J.A.C. Chandler, was president of the college from 1919 until his death in 1934.
        Mr. Chandler was born in Richmond and was graduated from the United States Naval Academy in 1923. He commanded destroyer squadrons in the South Pacific in World War II.
        He is survived by his wife, Louise.

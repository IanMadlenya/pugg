        Robert C. Allen, vice president of Walt Disney World, died Sunday, apparently after a heart attack. He was 55 years old.
        Mr. Allen had been with the Disney organization for 32 years. He started in the Disneyland cast in 1955, worked his way up to manager of the Golden Horseshoe attraction and later was manager of guest relations at the California park. He came to Florida when Walt Disney World opened in 1972 and was named vice president in 1977.

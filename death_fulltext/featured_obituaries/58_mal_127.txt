
        Harold M. Tract, a co-founder of an international law firm specializing in insurance, died on Monday at Memorial Sloan-Kettering Cancer Center in Manhattan. He was 65 years old and lived in East Hills, L.I..
        He died of cancer, his family said.
        Mr. Tract was born in Manhattan. He was a graduate of the University of Wisconsin and Harvard Law School.
        He worked for 33 years for the Manhattan law firm of Rein Mound & Cotton, becoming a senior partner. In 1985 he formed a new firm, Kroll & Tract, which grew to have 100 lawyers in its Manhattan headquarters and at branches in London, Paris and nine cities across the United States.
        He was on the board of directors of 17 companies, most of them in the insurance business. He was an underwriting member of Lloyd's of London, a past chairman for the New York County Lawyers Association Committee on Insurance, an officer in the insurance section of the American Bar Association and an adviser to the National Association of Insurance Commissioners. He wrote numerous articles for business and professioanl journals.
        He is survived by his wife, Lisa; two sons, Marc of Roslyn, L.I., and Laurence of Manhattan, and a brother, Dr. Albert Trachtenberg of Old Field, L.I.


        Dr. Ambrose P. Merrill, a former president of the Hospital Association of New York State, died on Monday in Guilderland Center Nursing Home in Guilderland, outside Albany. He was 80 years old and a longtime resident of nearby Delmar.
        He died of abdominal cancer, said a funeral home spokesman.
        Dr. Merrill was superintendent and later executive director of St. Barnabas Hospital in the Bronx from 1945 to 1967. In 1953 and 1954, he headed the Greater New York Hospital Association, which then represented 93 voluntary nonprofit and 30 municipal hospitals, and in 1956 and 1957, he headed the state association, which then represented 305 voluntary and nonprofit hospitals.
        He is survived by his wife, Elizabeth, and three sisters, Ruth Grix of Ogden, Utah, Emily M. Hammond Richardson of Logan, Utah, and Marjorie McDaniel of Waskom, Tex.

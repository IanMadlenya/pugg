        Peter von Wiesenthal, an engineer and a manufacturing executive, died of cancer on Tuesday at the Mount Sinai Medical Center in Manhattan. He was 70 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. von Wiesenthal, a 1943 graduate of the Massachusetts Institute of Technology, was involved in high-temperature heat transfer technology and in designing and manufacturing parts for the oil refining industry. Most recently, he was president of the Heat Research Corporation of Manhattan, from which he retired in 1976. He also worked as a consultant and held many patents.
        He was president and later chairman of the Alcorn Combustion Company in Manhattan, a designer and manufacturer of cracking furnaces and incinerators. Before that he was a vice president with the Foster-Wheeler Corporation and helped to develop specialized high-temperature furnaces.
        He began his career with the Petro-Chem Development Company in Manhattan in 1948.
        Mr. von Wiesenthal owned Snowberry Farm in Hudson, N.Y., where he bred thoroughbred racehorses.
        He is survived by his wife, Ruth; a daughter, Andrea; a son, Christian, and one granddaughter.

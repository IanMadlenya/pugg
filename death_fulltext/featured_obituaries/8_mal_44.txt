        Robert C. Johnson, a former president of the Dollar Savings Bank, died of cancer Tuesday at Lenox Hill Hospital. He lived in Manhattan and Vale do Lobo, Portugal.
        Mr. Johnson, who was born in Boston, was a 1929 graduate of Boston University.
        He was president of the Dollar Savings Bank, now the Dollar Dry Dock Savings Bank, from 1962 to 1965. Before joining the bank, he was a senior partner of Kidder Peabody & Company, an investment banking concern.
        He is survived by his wife, Claire.

        Thomas Victor, a photographer of authors and performing artists, died Tuesday at his sister's home in Detroit. He was 51 years old. He died of leukemia, said his longtime assistant, Benjamin Ford.
        Mr. Victor did book-jacket and publicity photographs for hundreds of writers and poets, including Ann Beattie, Susan Sontag, V. S. Naipaul, Saul Bellow, Eudora Welty, John Updike, John Cheever and John Irving. Mr. Victor was also the resident photographer for the poetry series at the 92d Street Y, and exhibited his work there.
        Mr. Victor published two books of photographs of dancers - ''Quintet'' and ''The Making of a Dance: Mikhail Baryshnikov and Carla Fracci in 'Medea.' '' He also took photographs for ''Preferences,'' a 1974 poetry anthology edited by Richard Howard, and was working on an anthology of his literary photographs at the time of his death.
        Mr. Victor was born in Akron, Ohio, and studied at the University of Michigan and at Yale University. He briefly edited The New York Quarterly, a poetry magazine, before turning to photography.
        He is survived by his sister, Harriet Spurlin, of Detroit.

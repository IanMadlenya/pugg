
        Dr. Sanford M. Farrer, the chairman of the pathology department of the Brooklyn Hospital Center, died at the hospital on Saturday. He was 61 years old and lived in Manhattan and Pawling, N.Y.
        He died of pancreatic cancer, a hospital spokesman said.
        Dr. Farrer had headed the department since 1971 and founded and directed the hospital's residency program in pathology. From 1966 to 1974 he was a pathologist and the blood bank director at Roosevelt Hospital in Manhattan, where he was also on the medical board and executive committee. He served as the director of laboratories in 1965 and 1966 at Columbus Hospital in Manhattan, now part of Cabrini Medical Center, and as an assistant pathologist at Long Island College Hospital in Brooklyn from 1962-65.
        He was an assistant clinical professor of pathology at the State University of New York Health Science Center at Brooklyn and previously taught at Columbia University and Harvard Medical School.
        Dr. Farrer graduated magna cum laude from Princeton in 1952 and received his medical degree from Columbia in 1956. His postgraduate training was at Mount Sinai Medical Center in Manhattan, Brigham and Women's Hospital and Children's Hospital in Boston and Memorial Sloan-Kettering Cancer Center in Manhattan.
        There are no immediate survivors.


        Edmund Demaitre, a journalist and retired political analyst with the Voice of America, has died of respiratory failure. He was 85 years old.
        Mr. Demaitre, who was born in Hungary, died Friday at Georgetown University Hospital.
        He covered the German occupations of Austria and Czechoslovakia and the Japanese invasion of China for various newspapers before becoming Stockholm correspondent for the Daily Express of London in 1940. Mr. Demaitre joined the Voice of America in 1949, specializing in European and Soviet affairs. He retired in 1976.
        Survivors include a daughter and two grandchildren.

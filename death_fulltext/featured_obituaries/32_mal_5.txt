        Robert Schakne, a writer and correspondent for CBS News for 33 years before he retired in 1988, died of cancer yesterday at Sibley Hospital in Washington. He was 63 years old and lived in Washington.
        As an investigative correspondent since 1976, Mr. Schakne worked on the Iran-contra arms scandal and the investigation into the explosion of the space shuttle Challenger. He covered the nuclear power industry on a regular basis.
        Mr. Schakne was born in Manhattan and attended the University of Chicago. He was with the International News Service from 1949 to 1955, for most of the time as a correspondent in the Far East and as chief of the service's Tokyo bureau.
        After joining CBS in 1955 he reported extensively from Latin America. From 1966 to 1976 he was based in New York. For his coverage of the uprising at the New York state correctional facility at Attica in 1971, he received the Distinguished Service Award of Sigma Delta Chi, the Society of Professional Journalists.
        Mr. Schakne is survived by his wife, Toshiye; a daughter, Miye, of Anchorage; a sister, Ann Harris of New York, and a grandchild.

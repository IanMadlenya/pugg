
        Dr. Dennis D. Kelly, a physiological psychologist serving a one-year term as president of the New York Academy of Sciences, died on Thursday at his home in Manhattan. He was 52 years old.
        He died of cancer of the bile duct, said his wife, Dr. Ann-Judith Silverman.
        Dr. Kelly had been a research scientist at the New York State Psychiatric Institute since 1968. He had been on the faculty of the Columbia University College of Physicians and Surgeons since 1971.
        His research focused on the effects of stress on the central nervous and endocrine systems, stress-related disorders, pain perception and its relief and the biology of emotional behavior,
        He was born in New York City, was a graduate of Fordham University and received his doctorate at Columbia University. He was a captain in the Army Medical Corps from 1960 to 1967.
        Dr. Kelly was a founding member of the International Association for the Study of Pain, a charter member of the American Pain Society and a member of the Society for Neuroscience.
        In addition to his wife, he is survived by his father, Charles D., of Middletown, N.Y., and a brother, Kevin M., of Bayville, L.I.

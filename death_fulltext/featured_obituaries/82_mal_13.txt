
        James Brenner, who served as a bureaucrat and a lobbyist when Edward I. Koch was Mayor of New York, died on Saturday at his home in Little River, S.C. He was 63.
        The cause was prostate cancer, said his son William M. Brenner.
        James Brenner was an assistant director of New York City's Office of Management and Budget during the 1970's and early 1980's. In 1985 he became the Koch administration's chief lobbyist in Albany.
        A native of Cincinnati, Mr. Brenner was a graduate of Wabash College. After earning a degree from the Columbia University Law School in 1957, he served in Korea as a Marine Corps officer, rising to the rank of colonel in the reserve.
        Mr. Brenner entered public service in 1964 after working as a Wall Street lawyer. He first became a consultant with the Office of Economic Development in Washington, D.C., then as an assistant attorney general in New York State. He served a stint in city government in Buffalo, then went to work for New York City's Addiction Services Administration. A variety of administrative positions with city government followed.
        Until recently he lived in Kinderhook, N.Y.
        He is survived by his wife, Loretta Dilger Brenner; five sons, William M. of Columbus, Ohio, James C. of Weston, Mass., Thomas G. of Dublin, John C. of Stony Point, N.Y., and Mark A. of Brooklyn; a daughter, Anne M. Brenner of Manhattan; his mother, Jean M. Brenner and a brother, John M., both of Cincinnati, and four grandchildren.

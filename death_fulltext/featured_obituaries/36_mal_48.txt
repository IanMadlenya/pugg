        Duane C. Bogie, a television producer who helped create the ''Hallmark Hall of Fame'' drama series, died of brain cancer Tuesday in Parkland Hospital in Dallas. He was 67 years old and had homes in Dallas and Litchfield, Conn.
        From 1950 to 1975 Mr. Bogie worked with the Hallmark series as director, producer, executive producer or production supervisor. In that era advertising agencies produced television programs and he worked on the Hallmark series for Foote, Cone & Bending, eventually becoming an executive vice president and president of the agency's subsidary, FCB Productions. Since 1976 Mr. Bogie has been a professor of film at the Meadows School of the Arts at Southern Methodist University.
        He is survived by three sons: Jim, of Darien, Conn.; Peter, of Chicago, and Scott, of Dallas, and a daughter, Susan, of Phoenix.

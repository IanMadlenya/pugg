        Kevin Charles Shank, director of horticulture for the Cathedral of St. John the Divine, died of AIDS Saturday at St. Luke's-Roosevelt Hospital Center. He was 37 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Shank joined the cathedral staff in April 1988. From 1985 to 1988, he was director of the Open Space Greening Program of the Council on the Environment of New York City. From 1981 to 1985 he was an economic botanist and founding horticulturalist of Glie Farms in the South Bronx, a national model for urban agriculture. He was also environmental education director at the Madison Square Boys and Girls Club in Manhattan.
        Mr. Shank was born in Teaneck, N.J., and graduated from SUNY at Delhi and Cornell University.
        He is survived by his mother and father, Marie and William Shank of Delhi, N.Y.; six sisters, and three brothers.

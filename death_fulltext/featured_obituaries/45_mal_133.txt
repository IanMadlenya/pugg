        John Adams Bross Sr., a lawyer and former Federal aide, died yesterday at his home in McLean, Va. He was 79 years old and also had a home in Peterborough, N.H.
        He died of cancer, a spokeswoman for the family said.
        A graduate of Harvard College and Harvard Law School, Mr. Bross practiced law in Manhattan from 1936 to 1942. After serving in Europe with the United States Air Force and with the Office of Strategic Services, he joined the staff of the Hoover Commission, which made recommendations to the reorganize the Federal Government, in 1948.
        From 1949 to 1951 he was assistant general counsel to the United States High Commissioner to West Germany and from 1951 to 1971 he was with the Central Intelligence Agency, where he was deputy to the director of program evaluation from 1963 to 1971, when he retired.
        He is survived by his wife, Joanne; two sons, Dr. Peter Bross of San Francisco and John Jr. of Chicago; two daughters, Wendy Frazier of Keene, N.H., and Justine Yildiz of Cambridge, Mass.; two stepchildren, Joanne Field of Peterborough, and Marshall Field 5th of Chicago and Hobe Sound, Fla., and 15 grandchildren.

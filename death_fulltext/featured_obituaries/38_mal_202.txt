        Dr. James Eliab Perkins, an epidemiologist and former managing director of the National Tuberculosis Association, now known as the American Lung Association, died of complications from Parkinson's disease on Sunday at Saddleback Memorial Hospital in Laguna Hills, Calif. He was 84 years old and lived in Laguna Hills.
        Dr. Perkins, a former resident of Bronxville, N.Y., received a medical degree from the University of Minnesota in 1930 and advanced degrees from Johns Hopkins University in 1931 and 1933.
        Before becoming managing director of the National Tuberculosis Association in 1948, he worked for the New York State Health Department as commissioner and director of communicable diseases. In World War II, he served as senior surgeon with the Public Health Service Reserve with the rank of lieutenant colonel.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Anna Diehl; two daughters, Adele Perkins of Watertown, N.Y., and Melinda LeRoy of Portland, Ore.; a son, William, of Pacific Palisades, Calif., and a granddaughter.

        Richard M. Lubell, a former deputy superintendent of the New York City schools, died of a heart attack on Thursday at his home in Stamford, Conn. He was 84 years old.
        Mr. Lubell was with the New York City school system for 46 years, as a teacher, principal and deputy superintendent. He began as a teacher in the elementary schools in 1924 and moved to the junior high schools in 1931.
        A graduate of Teachers College of Columbia University, he was named assistant principal of Junior High School 210 in Brooklyn in 1933. He returned to the elementary schools and from 1946 to 1950 he was principal of P.S. 130 and from 1950 to 1957 was principal of P.S. 92, both in Brooklyn. In 1960, he was named acting assistant superintendent of schools for special education and pupil personnel services. He later advanced to associate superintendent and in the late 1960's was named deputy superintendent in charge of special services. He retired in 1970.
        He is survived by a son, Michael, of Westport, Conn.; a sister, Rose Martin of West Palm Beach, Fla.; a brother, Ben, of Brooklyn, and one grandchild.

        Charles S. Peckham, a retired political consultant, died of a brain tumor Friday at his home in Stonington, Conn. He was 56 years old. In 1975 and 1976, Mr. Peckham was director of field operations for the Republican National Committee. He was a consultant to Mayor John V. Lindsay and President Ronald Reagan. After he left politics, Mr. Peckham founded the American Musical Theater in New London, Conn., in 1984.
        Mr. Peckham was born in Manhattan and graduated from George Washington University. With him when he died was his longtime companion, Landine Manigault. He is survived by his brother, Rufus, of Washington.

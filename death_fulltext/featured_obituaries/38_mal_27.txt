        Leslie Winship 2d, a former vice president of Dartmouth College, died of a heart attack Friday at the Dartmouth-Hitchcock Medical Center in Hanover, N.H. He was 68 years old and lived in Hanover.
        Mr. Winship, a 1942 Dartmouth graduate, joined the college in 1959 as director of development. At his retirement in 1984 he was vice president for alumni affairs and development.
        Mr. Winship was credited with the success of a capital campaign in 1982 that raised more than $204 million.
        He leaves his wife, the former Christine Hill; his mother, Ruth Crance of Hanover; two daughters, Sarah Samaha of Franconia, N.H., and Leslie Bodeur of Hanover; a son, Peter of Bow, N.H.; two sisters, Jacqueline Kimball of Hanover, and Patricia Crance of Atlanta, and five grandchildren.

        Donn Beach, a restaurateur credited with creating many popular bar drinks, died of liver cancer here Wednesday. He was 81 years old.
        Mr. Beach was an Army colonel in World War II and was in charge of recreation and food for soldiers.
        He went on to own Don the Beachcomber restaurants in Hollywood, Chicago, Las Vegas and Honolulu, where patrons were entertained with dances and food of Polynesia.
        Among the 84 bar drinks he is said to have concocted are the mai tai, the zombie and the missionary's downfall.

        Paul Groesse, who won three Academy Awards for art direction in the 1940's, died of pneumonia Monday. He was 80 years old.
        Mr. Groesse was nominated 11 times for an Academy Award, and with Cedric Gibbons, another art director at Metro-Goldwyn-Mayer, shared Oscars for work on ''Pride and Prejudice'' in 1940, ''The Yearling'' in 1946 and ''Little Women'' in 1949. Mr. Groesse also worked for RKO and Walt Disney studios before retiring in 1971.

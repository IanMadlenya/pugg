
        Davidson Sommers, a former chairman of the Equitable Life Assurance Society and World Bank official who had a keen interest in philanthropy, died on Dec. 17 at his home in Washington. He was 95.
        A graduate of Harvard Law School, Mr. Sommers joined the World Bank's legal department in 1946 and became vice president of an affiliate, the International Finance Corporation, before retiring in 1960. He then became a senior vice president and general counsel at Equitable Life, where he rose to chairman.
        Mr. Sommers also helped establish and served as president of the Eugene and Agnes E. Meyer Foundation, a charity founded in 1951 by Eugene Meyer, then the owner and publisher of The Washington Post.
        Mr. Sommers was born in St. Paul. During World War II, he served in the Army Air Forces as special assistant to the secretary of war in 1945.
        He was married 41 years to Alice Decker, who died in 1979. He is survived by his wife, Katharine G.; a sister, Eleanor Otto of St. Paul; a daughter, Elizabeth Spaulding of Nova Scotia; four stepchildren, Stephen Clark of Boston, Rosalind Parkinson of Columbus, Ohio, William Clark of Baltimore and Anna Stone of Los Angeles; and seven grandchildren.

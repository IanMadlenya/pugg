        Leonard Natman, director of operations at the Brooklyn Academy of Music, died Tuesday at New England Deaconess Hospital in Boston from complications from acquired immune deficiency syndrome. He was 34 years old.
        Mr. Natman, born in Brooklyn and a graduate of Brooklyn College, joined the academy as an usher in 1974, becoming director of operations and of the performing arts program for young people in 1984. In the latter position, he developed programs in music, dance and theater for New York City schoolchildren that eventually reached more than 120,000 children.
        Mr. Natman is survived by his mother, Harriet Lessman of Brooklyn; his father, Saul Natman of Miami; and a sister, Diana Vertuccio, and a step-brother, Howard Lessman, both of Brooklyn.

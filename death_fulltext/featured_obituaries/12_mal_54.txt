        Dr. Randolph K. Byers, a Milton, Mass., pediatrician and child neurologist, died of cardiac arrest Tuesday. He was 91 years old.
        Dr. Byers was a member of the Harvard Medical School faculty and the Children's Hospital staff from 1925 until he retired in 1971. He published more than 40 medical papers, including a 1943 study with Elizabeth Lord demonstrating that lead poisoning in children could cause neurological disorders.
        Surviving are his wife, Margaret Cooke Byers, three daughters and a son.

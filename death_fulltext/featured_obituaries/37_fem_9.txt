        Barbara Lewis Golub, a fund-raiser for the New York Philharmonic, died of a heart attack on Monday at her home in Manhattan. She was 76 years old.
        Mrs. Golub was born in Chicago, where she studied piano as a child. She earned a bachelor's degree in music from Northwestern University in Evanston, Ill. After graduating, she moved to New York and later married William Golub, a lawyer.
        Since the early 1950's, Mrs. Golub devoted herself to volunteer work for the New York Philharmonic. She was a member of the Auxiliary Board, which in 1980 became the Volunteer Council. She served on numerous fund-raising and special events committees.
        She is survived by her husband and her daughter, Joan, both of Manhattan; a sister, Monica Lewis Lang, and a brother, Marlo Lewis.

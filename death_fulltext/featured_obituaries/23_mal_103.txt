        Dr. Carl Soule, former executive secretary of the United Methodist Office for the United Nations, died of cancer of the prostate Friday at his daughter's home in Fairfax Station, Va. He was 82 years old and lived in Lady Lake, Fla.
        Dr. Soule came to the United Nations from Chicago in 1960. He was one of those responsible for building the organization's church center, which opened in 1963. He retired in 1972.
        In addition to his daughter, Mary Smythe, he is survived by his wife, Mildred, and two grandchildren.

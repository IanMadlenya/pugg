
        Rufus E. Miles Jr., an author and Federal administrator who served as an assistant secretary under three presidents, died on Tuesday at Ithaca Hospital.  He was 85.
        Mr. Miles was born in Columbus, Ohio, in 1910. He began his Federal service in 1935 with the Civilian Conservation Corps. In 1941, he joined the Bureau of the Budget, the predecessor of the Office of Management and Budget.
        In the early 1950's, Mr. Miles was named an assistant administrator of the Federal Security Agency, the predecessor of the Department of Health, Education  and Welfare. In 1953, he was appointed the agency's director of administration,  a position later renamed assistant secretary for administration.
        Before retiring in 1965, Mr. Miles was an assistant secretary under Presidents Dwight D. Eisenhower, John F. Kennedy and Lyndon B. Johnson and six H.E.W. secretaries.
        In the 1970's, Mr. Miles wrote a book on H.E.W. and was a leading proponent of creating a separate Department of Education. His book, "Awakening From the American Dream: The Social and Political Limits to Growth," was a runner-up for  a National Book Award in 1977.
        He moved to Ithaca in 1987.
        Mr. Miles's first wife, Nell, died in 1988. He is survived by two daughters from his first marriage, Barbara Miles and Mary Patten, and his second wife, Elizabeth Klaer Miles of Ithaca.

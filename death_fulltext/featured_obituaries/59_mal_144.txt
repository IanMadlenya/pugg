
        David Greenspan, who spent 40 years as a narrator for film projects ranging from Olympic documentaries to the television program "Lifestyles of the Rich and Famous," died Saturday at St. Vincent's Hospital in Manhattan. He was 68 years old. His family said he had been suffering from lung cancer.
        Using the professional name David Perry, Mr. Greenspan became known to many as the voice of the Olympics after narrating movies on the Games made by his brother, Bud Greenspan.
        Mr. Greenspan also narrated sports and news specials for television and radio. He was also a lawyer.
        In addition to his brother, he is survived by two sisters and three children.

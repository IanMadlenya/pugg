
        Walter E. Maloney, a nationally known admiralty lawyer for many years, died on Monday at a nursing home in Chatham, N.J. He was 88 years old and lived in Convent Station, N.J.
        Mr. Maloney, whose specialty involved the law of the sea, served as chief attorney for the American Merchant Marine Institute from 1942 to 1952 and was its president and chairman of the board from 1952 to 1955. He also was a member of the Maritime Law Association.
        He was born in Phillipsburg, N.J., and graduated from Lafayette College and Columbia Law School. In 1961, he joined the admiralty law firm of Bigham, Englar, Jones & Houston in New York and worked there as a partner and senior partner until his retirement in 1981.
        Mr. Maloney's first wife, the former Mary Reynolds, died in 1955. He is survived by his second wife, Hilda; eight sons, James M. Maloney of Chatham, Edgar H. Donohoe of Harding Township, N.J., Thomas R. Maloney of Houston, John F. Donohoe of Short Hills, N.J., William R. Donohoe of Cherry Hill, N.J., Michael J. Donohoe of Deland, Fla., Walter E. Maloney Jr. of Cranford, N.J., and Peter B. Maloney of Bozeman, Mont., and three daughters, Claire D. O'Mara of Harding Township, N.J., Katheryn D. Schuessler of Doylestown, Pa., and Anne D. Goldsmith of Morristown, N.J.; one sister, Mary M. Sargent of Bronxville, N.Y.; 43 grandchildren and 19 great-grandchildren.

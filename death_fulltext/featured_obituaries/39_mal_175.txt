        Lloyd I. Miller, an industrialist and horseman who was a former United States Ambassador to Trinidad and Tobago, died on Sunday at his home in Naples, Fla. He was 66 years old.  Mr. Miller was ambassador to Trinidad and Tobago from 1973 to 1975. He was a former chairman of Vulcan International, whose Vulcan Corporation manufactures shoes, sports equipment and plastics products.
        He was a former part owner of the Cincinnati Reds and a director of several corporations in the Cincinnati area.
        One of Mr. Miller's horses, Dark Mirage, was the champion 3-year-old filly in 1968.
        He is survived by his wife, Catherine.

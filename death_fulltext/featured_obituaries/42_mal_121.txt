        Joseph V. Stolper, an antiques dealer, died on Friday in Orlando, Fla. He was 89 years old.
        Mr. Stolper died of heart failure, his grandson, Bruce Rennert, said.
        Mr. Stolper was born in Austria and came to the United States in 1909. After working various jobs, he opened an antiques store in Manhattan in 1941. He later opened two other stores in Manhattan and a store in Dania, Fla., where he retired in the late 1970's. The last of Mr. Stolper's stores closed in 1983.
        He is survived by a daughter, Corinne Wildman of Orlando, Fla.; three grandchildren, and four great-grandchildren.

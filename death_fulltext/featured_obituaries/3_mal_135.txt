        Pierre Bourdain, an executive in the classical-music recording industry, died of a heart attack Tuesday at his home in Manhattan. He was 57 years old.
        Born in Manhattan, Mr. Bourdain attended Yale University and served in the United States Army from 1951 to 1953. He worked in sales promotion for London Records and was classical merchandising manager for CBS Records in the 1960's and 70's. After several years as a private consultant to several record companies, he became co-manager of Orpheus Compact Disks on Manhattan's Upper East Side.
        Mr. Bourdain is survived by two sons, Anthony and Christopher, both of New York City.

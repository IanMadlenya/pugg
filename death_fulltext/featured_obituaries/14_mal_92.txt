        Martin H. Smith, a pianist and the dean of faculty and students at the Manhattan School of Music, died of a cerebral hemorrhage Friday at Harlem Hospital. He was 50 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        A native of West Virginia, Mr. Smith appeared as a piano soloist with orchestras in the early 1950's, and began a career as an accompanist in 1957. Among the singers for whom he played were Birgit Nilsson, Eleanor Steber, Anna Moffo and Robert Merrill. He was a graduate of Columbia University and the University of Vienna.
        He is survived by a brother, Benton Smith of West Chester, Pa., and a sister, Jeanette Sipple of Livonia, Mich. A memorial service is scheduled for May 1 at 3 P.M. at the Manhattan School of Music, 120 Claremont Avenue.


        Lloyd B. Gottlieb, a specialist in corporate-commercial law and real-estate law, died on Nov. 24 at his home in Manhattan. He was 58.
        His family said he had had a brief flu-like illness.
        Mr. Gottlieb was a founding partner of the law firm of Opton Handler Gottlieb Feiler Landau & Hirsch. He represented many European and American companies.
        Mr. Gottlieb was born in New York City. He graduated from Cornell University and from New York Law School.
        He is survived by his wife, Gaile; two daughters, Sharon and Alisa, and two sons, Philip and William.

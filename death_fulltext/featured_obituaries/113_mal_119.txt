
        George W. Rothschild, a Chicago lawyer and Federal official who retired last  year as judge of the Circuit Court of Cook County, died on Tuesday at Evanston Hospital. He was 80 and lived in Evanston.
        A graduate of Harvard College and the University of Chicago Law School, he went to Washington in 1949 as a counsel in the Marshall Plan administration and  successor agencies, practicing international law and performing legislative work.
        He returned to Chicago in 1955 to become general counsel and a vice president of the GATX Corporation. After retiring in 1978 he was a counsel for the Commerce Department in Washington and subsequently for the law firm Coin, Crowley & Nord in Chicago. He became a circuit court judge in 1983.
        He was a trustee of Hull House Association and a member of the Harvard College Center of Jewish Studies, the University of Chicago Law School Association and the Evanston Youth Commission.
        He is survived by his wife, Valerie Myers; a daughter, Jane Jacobsen; a son,  John, and three grandchildren, all of Evanston.

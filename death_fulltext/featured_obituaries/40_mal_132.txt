        George Bernard Burrus, retired head of Peoples Drug Stores, died of a heart attack on Monday at Sibley Memorial Hospital in Washington. He was 84 years old and lived in Washington.
        Mr. Burrus was with Peoples for almost 50 years, beginning as a soda fountain clerk in 1923. He became president in 1950 and chairman in 1958. The chain now has 490 stores in the Middle Atlantic States.
        Mr. Burrus was a 1925 graduate of the Max-Morris School of Pharmacy in Washington. At various times during his long career, he was president of both the National Association of Chain Drug Stores and the Associated Chain Drug Stores, and a director of the American Security and Trust Company, Washington Children's Hospital, the United Givers Fund and the Interracial Council for Business Opportunity, all in Washington.
        He is survived by his wife, Beulah; a daughter, Margaret Drerup of Union City, Tenn.; five grandchildren, and three great-grandchildren.

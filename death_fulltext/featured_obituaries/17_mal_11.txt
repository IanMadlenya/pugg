        David Loth, the author of almost 50 books, died Sunday in Boulder, Colo. He was 88 years old.
        Mr. Loth's books ranged from ''The Erotic in Literature'' to ''Public Plunder,'' a history of graft in America. He also wrote or was the co-author of many biographies, including ones of Lafayette, Woodrow Wilson, Alexander Hamilton, John Marshall and Philip II of Spain. He was also the co-author of ''Peter Freuchen's Book of the Seven Seas.''
        Mr. Loth was born in St. Louis on Dec. 7, 1899. He was a journalist for 20 years in the United States, Europe and Australia. Among the many newspapers he worked on were The New York Times and the New York World.
        Mr. Loth is survived by a nephew, Nicholas Helburn, of Longmont, Colo.

        Alvin R. Jennings, a former managing partner of Coopers & Lybrand and a past president of the American Institute of Certified Public Accountants, died of a stroke on March 14 in Miami, where he lived in retirement. He was 85 years old.
        Mr. Jennings spent his entire professional career with the firm, which was known as Lybrand, Ross Brothers and Montgomery when he joined it. He became a partner in 1940 and in 1954 managing partner, a position he held until he retired in 1962.
        Under Mr. Jennings' leadership the international firm of Coopers & Lybrand was formed when Cooper Brothers of Britain and McDonald, Currie & Company of Canada merged with the American firm. He was also responsible for making Coopers & Lybrand the first of the ''Big Eight'' accounting firms to offer actuarial services to clients.
        Mr. Jennings was instrumental in establishing the Accounting Principles Board and was one of its early chairmen.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Nell Foster Montgomery: a daughter, Susan, of Davis, Calif.; two sons, Peter, of Corvallis, Ore., and Stuart, and a sister, Mrs. Charles Van Buskirk of Hendersonville, N.C.


        Walter Krementz Jr., the longtime head of a major jewelry company, died yesterday at the Morristown Memorial Hospital in Morristown, N.J. He was 81 years old and lived in New Vernon, N.J. He also had a home in Bermuda.
        He died of heart failure, his family said.
        Mr. Krementz was the retired board chairman of Krementz & Company in Newark, one of the oldest and largest manufacturers of men's and women's jewelry in the United States. He remained active in the firm's sales and promotion work.
        He modernized and expanded the company's product lines during his stewardship. The firm was founded by his grandfather, George Krementz, in 1866.
        Mr. Krementz, who was born in East Orange, N.J., grew up in Morristown. After graduating from Yale in 1935, he went into the family business.
        For many years he served as president of the Essex Club in Newark.
        Mr. Krementz is survived by his wife, the former Virginia Hyde; two daughters, Christie Krementz Graham of New Vernon and the photographer Jill Krementz Vonnegut, of Manhattan; a son, Frederick, of Santa Fe, N.M.; a brother, Robert, of Hilton Head, S.C., a sister Letitia, Lady Strathalmond, of Palo Alto, Calif., and eight grandchildren.

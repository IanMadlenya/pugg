        Dr. Edward Frederick Landau, a retired research chemist and holder of six patents, died on Monday at St. Barnabas Medical Center in Livingston, N.J. He was 74 years old and lived in West Orange.
        He died of lung disease, a funeral home spokesman said.
        Dr. Landau retired in 1982 as assistant to the vice president for research of the Allied Chemical Company, now known as the Allied Signal Company. He joined the company in 1957 and later served as director of planning and administration at the corporate research center.
        He had taught at Brooklyn Polytech and at Farleigh Dickinson University. At his death he was a board member of Prospect House, a psychiatric rehabilitation center in East Orange.
        He was a 1945 Ph.D. graduate of the Polytechnic Institute of Brooklyn and the holder of six patents in synthetic resins, plastics and fibers.
        He is survived by his wife, Veda; a daughter, Carolyn of Maplewood, N.J.; a son, Jeremy, of San Francisco; a stepson, Henry Goodfriend of South Orange, and a sister, Muriel Perman of Brooklyn.

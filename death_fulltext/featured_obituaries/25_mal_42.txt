        Ondrej Nepela, the only Czechoslovak to have won an Olympic gold medal in figure skating, died in West Germany last Thursday, the West German Ice Skating Association announced. He was 38 years old and had been suffering from throat cancer.
        Mr. Nepela captured the men's title at the 1972 Games in Sapporo, Japan. He was the three-time world champion from 1971 to 1973, when he won the last of his five straight European titles. At the age of 23, he retired.
        In recent years he had been coaching, a role he had to give up because of his illness.

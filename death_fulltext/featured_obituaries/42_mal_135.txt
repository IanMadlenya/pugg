        Charles Travers Davies, who was corporate secretary and assistant to the president of the Hanover Insurance Company when it was in New York City, died on Tuesday at his home in Stratford, Conn. He was 80 years old Mr. Davies died of anemia, his daughter, Patricia Haas, said.
        Mr. Davies, a native New Yorker, had been with the company for 20 years when he retired in 1975. The company has since moved to Worcester, Mass.
        He was a graduate of New York University and a volunteer and former member of the board of Meals on Wheels in Stratford.
        In addition to his daughter, of Killingworth, Conn., Mr. Davies is survived by his wife, the former Alyce Murray; a sister, Ethel Harris of Naples, Fla., and a granddaughter.

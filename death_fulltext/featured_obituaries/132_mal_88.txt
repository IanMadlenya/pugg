
        Randolph Symonette, a baritone who sang at the New York City Opera and the Metropolitan Opera, died on Jan. 1 at Willow Place, a nursing home in Windsor, N.Y. He was 88.
        Mr. Symonette was born in Inagua, the Bahamas, and studied voice at the New School in Manhattan. He made his New York City Opera debut as the Commendatore in Mozart's ''Don Giovanni'' in 1952 and sang Telramund in Wagner's ''Lohengrin'' at the Met in 1961.
        Mr. Symonette also sang in Broadway productions of Kurt Weill's ''Firebrand of Florence'' and Gian Carlo Menotti's ''Consul.''
        In Europe, he performed with the Vienna State Opera, the Paris Opera and the Deutsche Oper am Rhein. After his retirement from the stage in 1972, Mr. Symonette became a professor at Florida State University in Tallahassee, where he was on the faculty until 1983.
        He is survived by his wife, Lys Symonette, vice president of the Kurt Weill Foundation, and a son, Victor, a conductor, both of New York City.

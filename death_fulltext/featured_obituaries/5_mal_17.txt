        William Feinberg, a founder of City Center who served for 15 years as an official of Local 802 of the American Federation of Musicians, died Saturday at Mount Sinai Hospital. He was 87 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        In 1943, with the support of former Mayor Fiorello H. La Guardia, he helped found the New York City Center of Music and Drama at the city-owned Mecca Temple on West 55th Street.
        Mr. Feinberg, a violinist, was born in Manhattan in 1899, He was president and secretary of the musicians' union, retiring in 1946 and becoming a consultant on labor relations in the entertainment field.
        He served on the boards of directors of the High School of Performing Arts and the American Theatre Wing, which sponsored Stage Door Canteens throughout the country during World War II.
        In 1965, he began yet another career. He organized the Senior Concert Orchestra of New York with financial assistance from Local 802's Senior Musicians Association.
        All of the orchestra's 70 members were retired professional musicians, with their ages ranging from 65 to 88.
        He is survived by his wife, Anne; a brother, David, of Manhattan, and two sisters, Eve Stillman, also of Manhattan, and Bert Freeman of Miami.

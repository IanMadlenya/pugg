        Michael Lepiner, president of Telecom Entertainment, producers of programs for commercial television, died of pancreatic cancer Sunday at Mt. Sinai Medical Center. He was 46 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Lepiner formed Telecom, a subsidiary of the D'Arcy Masius Benton & Bowles advertising agency, in 1981. Most recently he was executive producer of ''The Attic: The Hiding of Anne Frank,'' and ''In the Line of Duty: The FBI Murders,'' both of which appeared on network television.
        In 1983 he received the Film Advisory Board's award of excellence for ''The Gift of Love: A Christmas Story,'' which starred Lee Remick and Angela Lansbury. In 1980 he received the Christopher Award for the production of ''A Private Battle,'' the story of the writer Cornelius Ryan's battle against cancer.
        Mr. Lepiner joined D'Arcy Masius Benton & Bowles in 1971 and became senior vice president and director of programming. Before joining the agency he worked at Battan Barton Durstine & Osborn Inc., NBC-TV and the A. C. Nielsen Company. He graduated from the University of Buffalo in 1963.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Rhyna Lacayo; two daughters, Karina and Danielle; his parents, Benjamin and Kay Lepiner of Queens, and two sisters, Evelyn Fisher and Marion Grimm of New City, N.Y.

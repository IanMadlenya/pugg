        William C. Schraft Jr., a pathologist and president of the Westchester County Board of Health, died on Thursday while vacationing with his family in Stonington, Conn. He was 69 years old and lived in New Rochelle.
        Dr. Schraft died of a heart attack, his daughter Constance said.
        He was director of the laboratories at New Rochelle Hospital Medical Center from 1950 until his death, and had recently been appointed president of the Board of Health. Dr. Schraft was also a consulting pathologist at Lawrence Hospital in Bronxville.
        He was a captain in the Army Medical Corps from 1945 to 1947.
        His wife, the former Mary Masalski, died in July. He is survived by three daughters, Susan, of Brooklyn, and Constance and Barbara, both of Manhattan; a sister, Grace Lauter of Spring Lake Heights, N.J., and three grandchildren.

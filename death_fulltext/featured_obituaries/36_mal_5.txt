        John Frank Wood, a retired partner in the Manhattan law firm of Thacher Proffitt & Wood, died of a heart attack Thursday at his home in Buck Hill Falls, Pa. He also lived in Manhattan and was 81 years old.
        Mr. Wood, who practiced banking law, was a member of the New York State Pension Commission and chairman of the Advisory Council on Pensions for the state civil service department.
        Mr. Wood was born in Chester, Pa., and graduated from Harvard College and Harvard Law School. He had been a trustee of the Franklin Savings Bank of New York.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Ann Budd; five children, John, of Manchester, N. H., Lydia Sargent of Boston, Susannah Tavernier of Oakland, Calif., Sarah Lewis of Vancouver, B.C., William H., of Wayne, Pa., and 13 grandchildren.

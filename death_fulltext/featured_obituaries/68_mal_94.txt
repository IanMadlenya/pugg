
        Robert Briggs, a bass who won the Luciano Pavarotti International Vocal Competition in 1985, died on Tuesday at Roosevelt Hospital in Manhattan. He was 39 years old and lived in New York City.
        He died of AIDS, his family said.
        Mr. Briggs took part in Mr. Pavarotti's nationally televised series of master classes on public television in 1979.  He performed with opera companies in various parts of the United States, and his concert work included a "Messiah" with the Oratorio Society at Carnegie Hall.
        Mr. Briggs received a bachelor of arts degree and a master's degree from the Juilliard School.
        He is survived by his mother, Margaret Briere Briggs of Middletown, Conn.

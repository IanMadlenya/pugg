        J. H. Tyler McConnell, a retired Delaware banker and lawyer who was a member of the board of governors of the United States Postal Service, died of a stroke Monday at St. Mary's Hospital in Palm Beach, Fla. He was 74 years old and lived in Palm Beach.
        Mr. McConnell, who earned a law degree at the University of Virginia, was a prominent Democrat in Delaware and was an unsuccessful candidate for Governor in 1958. He was former chairman of the Delaware River and Bay Authority and a founder of the Greater Wilmington Development Council.
        He retired in 1984 as chief executive of the Delaware Trust Company. Previously, he was counsel and corporate secretary of Hercules Inc. President Reagan named him to the Postal Service in 1985.
        He is survived by his wife, Margaret; two daughters, Marion Lassen, of Wilmington Del., and Susan Rhinehart, of Charlottesville, Va.; a son, J. H. Tyler Jr., of Charlottesville; and three grandchildren.

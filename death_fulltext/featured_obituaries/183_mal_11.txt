
        Franklin S. Forsberg, a publishing executive and former ambassador to Sweden, died last Friday in Greenwich, Conn. He was 96 and lived in Greenwich.
        After holding executive positions with Street & Smith Publications, Mr. Forsberg became a United States Army colonel in World War II and played a role in founding Yank magazine, an Army weekly that was published in 21 editions and printed in 17 countries worldwide. He also oversaw other American military publications.
        He later became executive vice president and publisher of the publishing house Holt, Rinehart and Winston, directing its magazine division.
        Mr. Forsberg, who was of Swedish descent, was ambassador to Sweden from 1981 to 1985, during the Reagan administration.
        Born in Salt Lake City and raised in Utah, he received a bachelor's degree from the University of Utah and a master's in business administration from New York University.
        He is survived by his wife of 58 years, Ann Rountree Forsberg; a daughter, Kristin Forsberg Williams of Washington; two sons, Lars and Erik, both of Greenwich; and five grandchildren.

        David Schwartz, a former judge in the United States Court of Claims and a former Justice Department lawyer, died of pancreatic cancer yesterday at the Washington Hospice. He was 73 years old and lived in Washington.
        He served on the Federal bench from 1968 to 1982, when he left to return to private practice. From 1959 to 1968, he was a partner in the Manhattan law firm of Stroock & Stroock & Lavan and from 1957 to 1959 he was general counsel of the Development and Resources Corporation.
        A graduate of Harvard Law School, he served from 1945 to 1957 as a special assistant with the Justice Department. Just after World War II he handled the Government's seizure of enemy property, including the United States holdings of I. G. Farben and General Aniline. He also taught at several law schools.
        He is survived by his wife, Louisa; two children, Jonathan, of Santa Monica, Calif., and Joanna Kyd, of Manhattan; three stepchildren, Anthony Stone of Atlanta, Katherine Stone of Brooklyn and Daniel Stone of Kensington, Md., and three grandchildren.


        Yale M. Fendell, a Manhattan sporting goods executive, died on Sunday at Columbia-Presbyterian Medical Center. He was 67 years old and lived in Watchung, N.J.
        He died of cancer, a spokesman for the family said.
        Mr. Fendell was executive vice president of the Ralph Libonati Company, which distributes Bata Shoes.
        From 1986 until 1989 he was executive vice president of Pony Sport & Leisure, which distributes sports shoes. In the late 1950's he helped found the Libonati Company.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Sylvia Rechtschaeffer; a daughter, Alyson Brier of Manhattan; a son, Bruce, of Short Hills, N.J.; a brother, Edward, of Houston, and three grandchildren.

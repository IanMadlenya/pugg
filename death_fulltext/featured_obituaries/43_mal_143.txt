        Albert E. Whitaker, a retired special agent of the Secret Service who was in charge of the New York district for many years, died on Monday in Mercy Hospital in Rockville Centre, L.I. He was 82 years old and lived in Wantaugh, L.I.
        Mr. Whitaker died of stomach cancer, a spokesman for the family said.
        He was associated with the Secret Service for 47 years and retired in 1977. From 1947 to 1977, he provided security for President Dwight D. Eisenhower, President Harry S. Truman and other dignitaries. He also tracked down counterfeit bills and fake food stamps circulated in the New York area.
        Mr. Whitaker was born in Providence, R.I., and attended Georgetown University in Washington. He joined the Army Intelligence Division in 1942 and remained until 1946, when he was discharged as a captain. He worked on security for the Manhattan Project, which developed the atomic bomb, and for this he received the Legion of Merit.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Margaret Warren; a daughter, T. Denise Bowers of Rogersville, Tenn., and five grandchildren.

        Robert Ward Blauvelt, former president of Porcella Vicini & Company, a Manhattan shipping concern, died on Wednesday at his home in Franklin Lakes, N.J., after a long illness. He was 71 years old.
        Mr. Blauvelt joined the company in 1965 and retired as president in 1989. Earlier, he had been an executive with the Alcoa Steamship Company in Manhattan, Puerto Rico and in South America. From 1946 to 1947, Mr. Blauvelt was an investigator for the Atomic Energy Commission in Santa Fe, N.M.
        Mr. Blauvelt was born in Paterson, N.J., and graduated from Williams College.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Elizabeth Anderson; a brother, William, of Franklin Lakes; a son, David, of Fort Valley, Va.; two daughters, Ardath, of Hollis, N.H., and Gillian Capparelli of Honduras, and five grandchildren.

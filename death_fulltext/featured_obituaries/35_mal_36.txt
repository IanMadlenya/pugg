        Steven S. Schwarzchild, professor of philosophy and Judaic studies at Washington University, St. Louis, died of an aneurysm Friday at the Jewish Hospital in St. Louis, where he lived. He was 65 years old.
        Before joining the faculty at Washington in 1965 he had been a visiting professor at Brown University and held professorships at Hebrew University in Jerusalem and the University of Notre Dame.
        Dr. Schwarzchild was ordained a rabbi at Hebrew Union College in Cincinnati in 1948. He served congregations in Fargo, N.D., and Lynn, Mass., in the 1950's and 60's.
        He was born in Frankfurt, Germany, and, with his family, came to the United States in 1939.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Lily Rose; a son, Maimon of San Diego, and a brother, Henry of White Plains.

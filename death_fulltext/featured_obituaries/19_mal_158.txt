        Dr. Max Black, an author, educator and an authority on the philosophy of language, died of heart complications Saturday at Tompkins Community Hospital in Ithaca, N.Y. He was 79 years old and lived in Ithaca.
        Dr. Black, who was born in Russia and educated in England, taught at the University of London and at the University of Illinois before joining Cornell University in 1946. He retired in 1977.
        Dr. Black wrote numerous books and articles, including ''Philosophy in America,'' (Cornell University Press, 1965). In 1981, he became the second American to be elected president of the International Institute of Philosophy since it was established in 1937.
        He is survived by his daughter, Naomi, of Toronto; a son, Jonathan, of King of Prussia, Pa.; five grandchildren, and two great-grandchildren.

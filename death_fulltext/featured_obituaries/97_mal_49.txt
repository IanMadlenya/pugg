
        Antonio Aris de Castilla, the Consul General of Guatemala and dean of the consular corps in New York, died on Jan. 23 at New York Hospital-Cornell University Medical Center. He was 81 and lived in Riverdale, N.Y.
        The cause was pneumonia, said his daughter, Lilly Rose Marshall-Carr.
        Born in Guatemala City, Mr. Aris de Castilla began a 57-year career in his country's service in 1937 as first secretary of the Consulate General in New York. He headed the mission as Consul General since 1946, concurrently serving as Consul General in Montreal.
        In addition to his daughter, a resident of Pensacola, Fla., Mr. Aris de Castilla is survived by a sister, Blanca Aris de Capuano, and a brother, Ivan Aris, both of Guatemala City. His wife of many years, Bette Ann Griffith, died in 1984.

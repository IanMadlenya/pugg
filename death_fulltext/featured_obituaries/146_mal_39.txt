
        Charles J. Elia, 72, a retired business reporter and columnist, died Sunday after a heart attack at his home in Brightwaters, N.Y., said his son, Ricardo Elia of Medford, Mass.
        In the 1960's, he worked for The New York World-Telegram and The Sun, The Wall Street Journal and The Daily News in New York, where he was business editor. His syndicated column, ''Make Your Money Work,'' appeared in 120 newspapers. He returned to The Journal in 1972 and wrote the ''Heard on the Street'' stock market column until 1981, when he became a financial adviser.
        Mr. Elia was born in Clearfield, Pa., and grew up in Lebanon, Pa. After serving in the Navy during World War II, he worked for The Lebanon Daily News.
        Other survivors include his wife, Mary, of Brightwaters; his sons, Anthony, of Mission Viejo, Calif.; Christopher, of Columbus, Ohio, and Michael, of Lower Gwynedd, Pa.; a daughter, Rosanne, of Brightwaters; his brothers, Reece, of Arcadia, Calif., and Anthony, of Lebanon, Pa.; his sisters, Catherine Simone and Carrie Catalano, both of Lebanon, and nine grandchildren.

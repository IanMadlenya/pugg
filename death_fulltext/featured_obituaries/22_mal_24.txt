        Seymour Posner, legislative director of the New York City Central Labor Council and a former Bronx Assemblyman, died of a heart attack Tuesday at his home in Brooklyn. He was 63 years old.
        Mr. Posner was public relations director for the marches on Washington led by the Rev. Dr. Martin Luther King Jr. in 1958, 1959 and 1963. At that time, he was public relations director for the Urban League.
        In 1965, he was elected to the Assembly from the South Bronx, and was a member 13 years. Mr. Posner was influential in passing the first law in the United States to grant bargaining rights to 40,000 employees of nonprofit, charitable, religious and educational institutions.
        Other laws he sponsored included workers' compensation for farm workers and minimum-wage protection and bargaining rights for household and home-care workers. From 1984 to this year, he was vice chairman of the State Workers Compensation Board. At his death, he was legislative director of the Central Labor Council.
        Surviving are his wife, Ann; a sister, Miriam Katz of Deerfield Beach, Fla., and a daughter, Naomi, of Brooklyn.

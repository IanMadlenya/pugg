        John E. Carton, president of the Patrolmen's Benevolent Association in New York City from 1947 to 1958, died of pneumonia Wednesday at the Westchester Medical Center in Valhalla, N.Y. He was 84 years old and lived in the Bronx.
        Mr. Carton, who joined the New York City Police Department in 1928, was an official of the P.B.A. for two decades, during which time it evolved from a fraternal group into a municipal labor union.
        He is survived by four daughters, Joan Donahue of Nanuet, N.Y.; Kay Masterson of Boca Raton, Fla., and Mary Biaggi and Anne Marie O'Brien, both of Bayside, Queens; a brother, Joseph, of Teaneck, N.J.; a sister, Sister Marie Amy, of Blauvelt, N.Y.; 19 grandchildren, and 13 great-grandchildren.

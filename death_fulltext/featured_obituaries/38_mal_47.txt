        Peter Glenn, a publisher of talent and modeling guides and directories, died of complications from AIDS on Monday at his home in Manhattan. He was 69 years old.
        Mr. Glenn published the Madison Avenue Handbook, the International Directory of Talent and Model Agencies and the New York Casting and Survival Guide.
        He was born in Clarksdale, Miss., and was an honors graduate of Dartmouth. In World War II, as a member of the American Field Service attached to British forces in North Africa, he was captured by Axis forces and held prisoner for 10 months. He was later sent to the China-Burma-India theater to return to combat.
        In the 1950's Mr. Glenn became a theatrical producer, serving as an associate to Kermit Bloomgarten in a revival of ''The Children's Hour'' and other vehicles. He began a career as a model with the Hartford, Ford and Wilhelmina agencies and in 1961 founded Peter Glenn Publications.
        There are no immediate survivors.

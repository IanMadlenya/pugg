        John C. Lobb, retired chairman of Northern Telecom Ltd., the Toronto-based communications concern, died of cancer on Wednesday at his home in Ligonier, Pa. He was 76 years old.
        Mr. Lobb joined the Northern Electric Company, the Montreal-based predecessor of Northern Telecom, as president in 1971. He became chairman and chief executive in 1974.
        Two years later he relinquished the post of chief executive of Northern Telecom Ltd, to become chairman of Northern Telecom Inc., the United States subsidiary, to devote full time to developing the United States market for telecommunications equipment. He retired in 1977.
        Mr. Lobb was born in Minneapolis and was a graduate of Phillips Exeter and of the University of Minnesota. He received a law degree from the University of Wisconsin. Before he joined Northern Telecom he practiced law and held senior positions with International Telephone and Telegraph, A. O. Smith and other major corporations. He was also president of the Marine Bank in Milwaukee and of Crucible Steel of Pittsburgh.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Mary Montgomery; two daughters, Josephine Adamson of Virginia Beach, Va., and Carol Kimball of St. Louis, and a sister, Isabel Jones of Fairmont, W.Va.

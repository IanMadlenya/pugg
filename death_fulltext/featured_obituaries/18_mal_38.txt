        Evert Clark, a reporter who specialized in aviation, science and space, died Thursday after surgery for an aneurism at Montgomery General Hospital in Olney, Md. He was 62 years old.
        Mr. Clark was a senior staff writer for the Washington bureau of Business Week magazine at his death. He wrote for a number of publications in his 40-year career, including The New York Times and Newsweek magazine.
        He is survived by his wife, the former June Hennesy; a son, David, of Kill Devil Hills, N.C.; two daughters, Kristin Dougherty of Sumpter, S.C., and Amy Bockhaus of Mount Airy, Md., and five grandchildren.

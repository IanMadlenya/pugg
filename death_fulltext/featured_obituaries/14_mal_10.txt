        Henry Simon Bloch, managing director of E. M. Warburg Pincus & Company, investment bankers, and adjunct professor emeritus at Columbia University, died Sunday at his home in Manhattan after a long illness. He was 72 years old.
        Born in Kehl, Germany, he earned a doctorate from the Universite de Nancy. He emigrated to the United States in 1937 and taught economics at the University of Chicago.
        In 1945 he joined the Treasury Department, and the following year became deputy director of the financial branch of the United Nations.
        In 1962 he became president of Zinder International Ltd., an investment banking firm. He joined E. M. Warburg and Company, the predecessor to E. M. Warburg Pincus and Company, where he was managing director until his death.
        He taught in the Law School and the School of International Affairs at Columbia from 1953 to 1986. He was an honorary associate fellow of Berkeley College of Yale University, and had an honorary doctorate from the Universite Libre de Bruxelles. He was a member of the Council on Foreign Relations and the Cosmos Club.
        He is survived by his daughter, Mimi B. Feuerstein of Brookline, Mass., and five grandchildren.

        Harry Kraf, who as chairman of the Joint Legislative Committee on Mental Retardation sponsored bills to increase staff members in New York State institutions for the retarded, died of cancer on Friday in Montefiore Hospital in the Bronx. He was 82 years old and lived in the Riverdale section of the Bronx.
        As a member of the State Senate and Assembly, representing the west Bronx from 1956 to 1972, Mr. Kraf, a Democrat, strongly backed rent controls, deplored New York City hotels that charged exorbitant fees for welfare clients and fought a Westchester County ban on the use of its parks by residents from outside the county.
        Mr. Kraf was an officer of many political and Jewish organizations in the Bronx, and in later years was a civil court judge in the Bronx and a member of the city's Taxi and Limousine Commission. He was a native of the Lower East Side of Manhattan and a graduate of Morris High School and Fordham University Law School. He had maintained a private law practice until recently.
        Surviving are his wife, Lena; a daughter, Elaine Altman of Jackson Heights, Queens; a sister, Ruth Jacobson of Newburgh, N.Y., and a granddaughter.

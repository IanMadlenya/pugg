        Willard Gustav Triest, founder of the Triest Construction Company in New York City, died of heart failure Monday at his home in Annapolis, Md. He was 83 years old and also lived in Southampton, L.I.
        Under Mr. Triest, Triest Construction built several miles of subway tunnel on the IND line, part of the Queens-Midtown Tunnel and the cutoff wall of the Merriman Dam in Wawarsing, N.Y., part of New York City's water supply system.
        More recently he was involved with engineering projects in the Annapolis area, including construction of the Severn River Bridge. He was the former vice president of the Hydrasearch Company, a mechanical engineering firm. He retired in 1986.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Laura Wolcott Tuckerman, and a daughter, Laura Wolcott Triest of Annapolis.

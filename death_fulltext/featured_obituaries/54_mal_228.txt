
        Maj. Gen. Bryan L. Milburn, who served in the Army in three wars and helped to govern Berlin after World War II, died Saturday in his home in Laverock, Pa. He was 95 years old.
        He died after a brief illness, said his daughter, Ann Beal of Philadelphia.
        He enlisted in the Army in World War I. His service in World War II included planning for the military government of Germany, and he commanded the Berlin Command, Office of Military Government for Germany.
        In the Korean War, General Milburn commanded the Headquarters and Service Group of the Far East Commands in Tokyo. His 39-year military career ended in 1956 after he served as the commanding officer of Fort Devens, Mass.
        In retirement, General Milburn was president of Arms for Friendship, an organization of veterans, retired members of the armed forces and civilians who sought to make it easier for American and Soviet veterans to meet.
        In addition to his daughter, he is survived by his wife of 74 years, Frances; five grandchildren and a great grandson.

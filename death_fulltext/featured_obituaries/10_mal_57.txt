        Allan C. Bernstein, who invented a series of office communications systems, died of a heart attack Sunday at the inpatient unit of Hospice Inc. in Tamarac, Fla. He was 76 years old and lived in Tamarac.
        Mr. Bernstein, an electronics engineer, was the founder and president of Adams Laboratories, the manufacturing arm of what later became Executone.
        He was born in Brooklyn and graduated from the Stevens Institute of Technology in Hoboken, N.J., in 1936.
        As a young man, he invented one of the first intercom systems, followed by the patient-to-nurse call station. He held a number of patents in the electronics field.
        Mr. Bernstein was active in civic affairs and had served as a Councilman in Tamarac.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Frances Orange; two daughters, Barbara Friedman of Potomac, Md., and Carol Nimetz of Bethesda, Md., and four grandchildren.

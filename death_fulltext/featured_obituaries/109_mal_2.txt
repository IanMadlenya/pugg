
        Charlie Jewtraw, who won the first event at the first Winter Olympic Games, died last Friday. He was 95.
        Mr. Jewtraw won the 500 meters in speed skating at Chamonix, France, in 1924. A renowned sprinter, he also won the United States championship in 1921 and 1923. His Olympic gold medal is now at the Smithsonian Institution in Washington.
        He later worked with the Spaulding sports equipment company and was a bank security guard in New York City before retiring to Florida.

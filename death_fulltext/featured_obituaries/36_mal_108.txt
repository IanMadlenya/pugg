        Earl D. Johnson, who was an Under Secretary of the Army and a top executive at General Dynamics and Delta Airlines, died in his sleep on Thursday at a nursing home in Greenwich, Conn. He was 84 years old and lived in Greenwich.
        Mr. Johnson was appointed Assistant Secretary of the Army for manpower and reserve forces by President Harry S. Truman in 1950 and was named Under Secretary in 1952. He held the post through 1954. Among other duties, he was a senior Defense Department representative at the signing of the peace treaty and security pact with Japan.
        Role in Panama Canal Company
        He was also chairman of the Panama Canal Company from 1952 to 1954.
        From 1954 to 1955 he was president of the American Transport Association and Air Cargo Inc., Washington-based trade association groups.
        In 1959 he joined the General Dynamics Corporation as executive vice president and later became its president. He became executive vice president and a director of Delta Airlines in 1963 and retired in 1964.
        In Air Corps in 1932
        Mr. Johnson was born in Hamilton, Ohio. He graduated from the University of Wisconsin.
        In 1932 he was commissioned a second lieutenant in the Army Air Corps and served a year on active duty. He then joined Loomis, Sayles & Company of Boston, investment counselors. In 1942 he reported for duty with the Army's Air Transport Command. He left the service as a colonel and returned to Loomis, Sayles.
        He is survived by a son, Raud of New Canaan, Conn.; two daughters, Susan Maloney of Riverside, Conn., and Cynthia Johnson of Greenwich; a sister, Marion Rushton of Falls Church, Va., and six grandchildren.

        William J. Fischer, a New York auctioneer of furnishings, decorations and art, died yesterday in a nursing home in Burbank, Calif. He was 91 years and lived in Manhattan until he moved to California 18 months ago.
        Mr. Fischer, a Manhattan native whose education ended in the eighth grade, opened his own auction business in 1926.
        He is survived by two daughters, Eileen Cohen of Burbank and Billie Bartelstone of Manhattan; a brother, Arthur, of Brooklyn; a sister, Olga Solomon of Manhattan; five grandchildren, and four great-grandchildren.

        Alexander Smalley, a retired executive and former labor leader, died Jan. 24 at Overlook Hospital in Summit, N.J., after a long illness. He was 79 years old and lived in Boca Raton, Fla.
        Mr. Smalley retired in 1974 as senior vice president of the St. Regis Corporation, a paper-manufacturing concern. He joined the company in 1945 as director of labor relations in New York.
        He was on the board of several organizations, including the American Paper Institute, the Pulp and Paper Manufacturing Association, the Urban League and St. Michael's Hospital in Newark.
        Mr. Smalley was a labor leader from 1936 to 1941, serving on the executive boards of the International Brotherhood of Electrical Workers and of the Electrical Workers Union in New York.
        He is survived by two daughters, Maureen Lombardi of Short Hills, N.J., and Patricia Gulbenkian of Pound Ridge, N.Y., and five grandchildren.

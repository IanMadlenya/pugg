        Michael Singer, an industrialist and a former chairman of the board of the Albert Einstein College of Medicine of Yeshiva University, died of cancer on Saturday at his winter home in Palm Beach, Fla. He was 85 years old and also lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Singer, who was born in Manhattan and was a graduate of Columbia College, was the longtime president of the Federal Oil Company and the Federal Credit Corporation, both with headquarters in Newark. He was a founder of the Albert Einstein College of Medicine.
        Surviving are his wife, Sydel; a daughter, Barbara Wolfson of Manhattan; four sisters, Sylvia Thal and Ann Gottlieb of Manhattan, Ruth Gould of Los Angeles and Lillian Winn of Far Rockaway, Queens; a stepdaughter, Georganne Heller of Beverly Hills, Fla., and two grandchildren.


        Michael Ference Jr., a physicist who was in charge of scientific research and development at the Ford Motor Company for almost 20 years, died yesterday in San Antonio. He was 84.
        A graduate of the University of Chicago, where he earned bachelor's, master's and doctoral degrees, Mr. Ference began his career teaching physics at his alma  mater.
        A specialist in atmospherics, from 1946 until 1948 he was the chief meteorologist for the Army Signal Corps and later chief scientist at its Evans Signal Laboratory.
        He joined Ford in 1953 as an associate director in its science laboratory. Four years later, he was named executive director, and in 1962 he became vice president of research. He retired in 1970.
        A former director of the Rand Corporation and the Carnegie Institution, Mr. Ference was also a former chairman of Wayne State University.
        Mr. Ference is survived by his wife, the former Margaret W. Wilfinger, of San Antonio; five children, Lois F. Nissly of Ann Arbor, Mich., Carole Ference of Los Angeles, Michele F. Klosson of Greenwich, Conn., Michael Ference 3d of San Antonio, and Richard H. Ference of Greenwich; nine grandchildren, and three great-grandchildren.

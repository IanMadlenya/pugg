
        Harlan Foss, a longtime New York City Opera baritone, died on Saturday at his in-laws' home in North Myrtle Beach, S.C. He was 50 years old.
        He died of colon cancer, said his agent, Martha Munro.
        Mr. Foss was born in Beverly, Mass., and graduated from the University of South Florida. For many years he sang with the New York City Opera and the San Diego Opera, as well as many other regional opera companies.
        He was especially noted for his work in contemporary American operas. He portrayed Abraham Lincoln in Robert Wilson's "Civil Wars."
        He is survived by his wife, Catherine Stapleton of Manhattan; his mother, Frances McDaniel of Miami; his stepfather, Ivey McDaniel of Miami; two sisters, Susan F. Reed of Salem, Mass., and Sandra F. Falstreau of Bay City, Tex., and two brothers, Richard A. Foss of Fort Lauderdale, Fla., and Marshall J. Foss of Boston.


        David DuVivier, who practiced law in New York and Paris for 30 years, died on May 7 at the Medical Center of Princeton, N.J. Mr. DuVivier, who lived in Princeton, was 83.
        The cause was congestive heart failure, said his wife, Georgine DuVivier.
        After receiving a law degree from Columbia University in 1937, Mr. DuVivier was an assistant corporation counsel for New York City, then an assistant district attorney in Manhattan.
        He served as a captain in the Army Air Force during World War II, returning to the District Attorney's office in 1945.
        In 1949 Mr. DuVivier became a partner in the Manhattan law firm of Reynolds, Richards, Ely & LaVenture. He was attorney in chief of the Legal Aid Society in Manhattan in 1956, and in 1961 joined the law firm of Coudert Brothers in Paris, where he remained until 1968, when he retired to study art.
        He graduated from Princeton University in 1932 and received a master's degree in fine arts from Harvard University.
        Mr. DuVivier is survived by his wife; a daughter, Tracy Gary of San Francisco; a son, Jerome Gary of Los Angeles, and two brothers, Paul, of Baltimore, and Edward, of Alton, Ill.


        Alfred Levitt, a wedding jewelry maker, died on Tuesday at Winthrop-University Hospital in Mineola, L.I. He was 74 years old and lived in Roslyn, L.I.
        He died of complications of liver cancer, his son, Jeffrey, of Manhattan, said.
        At his death Mr. Levitt was treasurer of Alfred Levitt & Son, which he founded in 1950 after several years in the trade. His specialty was working in platinum.
        Mr. Levitt was born in Toronto, and moved to the Bronx when he was 2 years old.
        Besides his son, he is survived by his wife, the former Pearl Haber; a daughter, Laurie Koen of Glenview, Ill., and two grandchildren.

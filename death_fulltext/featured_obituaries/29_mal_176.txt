        An obituary on Sunday about Matthew Schutz, a Manhattan antiques dealer, misstated the day of his death.
        It was Saturday, not Friday.
        Matthew Schutz, an antiques dealer, died Friday at Doctors Hospital. He was 55 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        He died after a long illness, his family said.
        Mr. Schutz established Matthew Schutz Ltd., at 1025 Park Avenue, more than 30 years ago. Born in Sheldon, Iowa, he graduated from Creighton University. He was a founding member of the Cooper-Hewitt Museum's decorative arts committee.
        Surviving are two sisters, Mary Jo Wilder of Manhattan and Bette DeVolder of Macomb, Ill.

        Max Benjamin Schreiber, a former director of design and chief architect for the New York City Housing Authority, died of a brain tumor yesterday at the Calvary Hospice in the Bronx. He was 79 years old and lived in the Bronx.
        A 1932 graduate of Cornell University School of Architecture, Mr. Schreiber joined the Housing Authority in 1946. He became chief architect in 1964 and was promoted to director of design in 1970. He was responsible for the design of hundreds of Housing Authority properties, including the 30-story Polo Grounds Houses in upper Manhattan. He retired in 1978.
        He is survived by his wife, Beatrice; two sons, Dr. Paul, of North Easton, Mass., and Dr. David, of Westwood, Mass.; a sister, Florence Schreiber of the Bronx; a brother, William, of Plantation, Fla., and five grandchildren.

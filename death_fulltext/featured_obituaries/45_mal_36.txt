He died of leukemia, said his son, Harry Clay Blaney 3d. Mr. Blaney, who came from a theatrical family that dated to the 1880's, ended his career as a Broadway producer in 1968 with ''Portrait of a Queen,'' a documentary-style play about Queen Victoria that starred Dorothy Tutin and that ran at the Henry Miller Theater for three months.
        Harry Clay Blaney, a theater producer who was active in New York for more than three decades, died on Wednesday at St. Vincent's Hospital. He was 86 years old and lived in Manhattan. He died of leukemia, said his son, Harry Clay Blaney 3d. Mr. Blaney, who came from a theatrical family that dated to the 1880's, ended his career as a Broadway producer in 1968 with ''Portrait of a Queen,'' a documentary-style play about Queen Victoria that starred Dorothy Tutin and that ran at the Henry Miller Theater for three months. The play was performed in London before coming to New York.
        Mr. Blaney began his theatrical career as a ticket seller in family-owned theaters and stock companies around the country. By the 1920's, Mr. Blaney had become a manager and producer.
        Mr. Blaney's first wife, Mae Agnes Planagan, was a production assistant and stage manager for her husband and the mother of Harry Clay Blaney 3d. She died in 1966. In 1981, Mr. Blaney married Holly Brook, who survives him.

        Solomon Telushkin, an accountant, died of a stroke Dec. 24 in his home in Brooklyn. He was 81 years old.
        Born near Minsk, in the Soviet Union, he emigrated to New York in 1924. He was certified as an accountant in 1933.
        He was the accountant for the Lubavitch movement and the United Lubavitcher Yeshiva, for Religious Zionists of America Mizrachi-Hapoel Hamizrachi, and for AMIT Women, a Zionist women's organization a formerly known as the Mizrachi Women's Organization of America.
        He is survived by his wife, Helen; a son, Joseph, of Brooklyn; a daughter, Shalva Siegel of Brookline, Mass., and three grandchildren.

        Marvin S. Machson, a lawyer and arbitrator, died of prostate cancer Tuesday at Northern Westchester Hospital in Mt. Kisco, N.Y. He was 74 years old and lived in Somers, N.Y.
        Mr. Machson, who was born in Monticello, N.Y., held bachelor's and law degrees from Harvard. He began practicing law in Manhattan in 1939. During World War II he served in the Army in Europe, and after the war he was a prosecutor at the war crimes trials in Nuremberg. He retired from law practice in 1981 but continued as an arbitrator for the American Arbitration Association until this fall.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Joan Golding; a son, Robert, of Croton-on-Hudson, N.Y.; a daughter, Sandra Machson-Carter of Medford, Mass., and two grandchildren.

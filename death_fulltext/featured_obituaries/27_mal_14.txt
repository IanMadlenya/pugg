        Franklin A. Korn, a Bell Telephone Laboratories researcher who helped create touch-tone telephones and the direct-dial long-distance system, died of kidney failure Friday. He was 83 years old.
        Mr. Korn, who at his retirement was director of telephone laboratories at Western Electric, now owned by A. T. & T., held more than a dozen telephone-related patents. He was a fellow of the Institute of Electronic and Electrical Engineers, that organization's highest honor.
        He is survived by his wife, a daughter, two grandchildren and a great-grandchild.

        Bob Dunn, a cartoonist and writer for the syndicated comic strip ''They'll Do It Every Time,'' died of complications of diabetes Tuesday at Overlook Hospital in Summit, N.J. He was 80 years old and lived in the Short Hills section of Millburn, N.J.
        Mr. Dunn took over direction of the strip with Al Scaduto after the death of its creator, Jimmy Hatlo, in 1963. It is distributed by King Features Syndicate to 250 newspapers.
        He is survived by his wife, Marie; two sons, Robert, of Rye. N.Y., and William, of Chevy Chase, Md.; a daughter, Elizabeth Murphy of Brooklyn; four grandchildren, and two sisters.

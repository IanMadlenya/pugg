
        Samuel D. Magavern, a lawyer in Buffalo for more than 50 years and the former chairman of the State Board of Social Welfare, died on May 28 at his home in Buffalo. He was 85 years old.
        He died of pneumonia, said his son, William.
        Mr. Magavern graduated from the University of Buffalo in 1929 and began practicing law with his father. He retired last year as senior partner in the firm of Magavern & Magavern.
        He was appointed to the Board of Social Welfare by Gov. Thomas E. Dewey in 1947. He was also active in local civic affairs, including as director of the Albright-Knox Art Gallery and as trustee of the Buffalo General Hospital.
        He is survived by his wife, Gertrude; two sons, James and William, both of Buffalo; three daughters, Margaret Hargraves of Huntington, L.I., Mary Worrell of Providence, R.I., and Jane Beebe of Portland, Ore; 17 grandchildren, and six great-grandchildren.

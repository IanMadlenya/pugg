        Lawrence Milberg, a lawyer and senior partner in the Manhattan-based firm of Milberg Weiss Bershad Specthrie & Lerach, died on Wednesday after suffering a heart attack while playing tennis near his home in Great Neck, L.I. He was 76 years old.
        A member of the New York bar since 1937, Mr. Milberg was a specialist in cases involving shareholder rights and class actions.
        He earned his law degree from Harvard University in 1936. During World War II he served with an Army intelligence group.
        He became a partner in Blumberg, Singer & Ross in 1959. Two years later he was with Milberg & Levy and from there became a founding partner of his present firm.
        He is survived by his wife, Madeline; a daughter, Susan, of Manhattan; a son, Frederic, of San Diego; a brother, Alter, of Great Neck, and two grandchildren.


        Dr. Joseph Zubin, a research psychologist whose career spanned more than 60 years, died on Tuesday at the home of his son in Buffalo, where he was visiting. He was 90 years old and lived in Leonia, N.J., and Pittsburgh.
        He died of congestive heart failure, his family said.
        At his death Dr. Zubin, a professor emeritus of psychology at Columbia University, was research career scientist at the Highland Drive Veterans Administration Medical Center in Pittsburgh, where he established a biometrics laboratory in 1976. He was Distinguished Research Professor at the Western Psychiatric Institute of the University of Pittsburgh, and was for many years the Gregory Razran Psychology Professor at Queens College.
        In the last year Dr. Zubin, an authority on the diagnosis of schizophrenia and other diseases, conducted several symposiums in this country and abroad, the most recent last month in Cleveland.
        He was born in Raseiniai, Lithuania, and grew up in Baltimore. He was a graduate of Johns Hopkins and received a doctorate from Columbia, where he taught from 1939 until 1969.  Honored for His Work
        From 1932 to 1956 he was also a research psychologist at the New York State Psychiatric Institutea. From 1956 to 1976 he was chief of psychiatric research in biometrics at the New York State Department of Mental Hygiene.
        He was a former president of the American Psychopathological Association and of the American College of Neuropsychopharmacology. This year he was awarded the Gold Medal of the American Psychological Foundation.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Winifred Anderson, whom he married in 1934; a son, Jonathan, of Baltimore; a daughter, Winifred Riley of Manhattan; three sisters, Blanche Becker of Worcester, Mass., and Doris Goldstein and Shirley Levin, both of Baltimore, and seven grandchildren.

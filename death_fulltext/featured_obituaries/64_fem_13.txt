
        Dr. Elizabeth Gougam Estrin, a physician in New Rochelle, N.Y., for more than 35 years and a member of the faculty at Columbia-Presbyterian Medical Center in Manhattan, died on May 16 in Paris during a visit. She was 79 years old.
        Dr. Estrin died of a heart attack, said her son, James.
        A native of New York City, Dr. Estrin attended Smith College and began medical studies at the University of Austria, in Vienna. She received her medical degree from the Women's Medical College, in Philadelphia.
        Dr. Estrin, a pediatrician, centered her practice on the care of underprivileged children. Dr. Estrin retired from her practice in 1988 and moved to Los Angeles.
        Dr. Estrin's husband, Dr. Irving Jonathan Estrin, died several years ago.
        She is survived by two sons, Jonathan, of Los Angeles, and James, of Yonkers; two daughters, Jennifer, of Los Angeles, and Judy, of San Luis Obispo, Calif., and two sisters, Gertrude Drachmanad, of New Rochelle, and Margaret Galgau, of Paris.

        John Michael Kingsland, an investment banker, died of a heart attack on Monday at Massachusetts General Hospital in Boston. He was 58 years old and lived in Boston.
        Mr. Kingsland, a graduate of Dartmouth College and the Amos Tuck School of Business Administration at Dartmouth, was a director of the Sealed Air Corporation, Saddle Brook, N.J., and the Central Hudson Gas and Electric Corporation, Poughkeepsie, N.Y. He was also a private investment banker.
        From 1978 to 1985 he was a vice president with Kidder Peabody & Company and for 20 years before that had been with White, Weld & Company, where he became a vice president. He served in the Navy from 1954 to 1957.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Elena de la Ossa, and two children, Susan and Samuel, all of Boston.

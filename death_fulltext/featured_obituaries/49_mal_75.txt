
        Louis Shapiro, a retired associate justice of the Connecticut Supreme Court and a former majority leader in the State House of Representatives, died on Friday at Hartford Hospital. He was 86 years old and lived in Farmington.
        Mr. Shapiro, a Republican, was elected to the Connecticut House of Representatives in 1941 and served until 1953. From 1951 to 1953, he was the House majority leader.
        In 1953, Gov. John Lodge appointed him to the Superior Court. He served from 1953 to 1970 and was a chief judge from 1966 to 1970. He was then appointed an associate justice of the Supreme Court by Gov. John Dempsey, a position he held until 1975, when he reached retirement age.
        After retiring, Mr. Shapiro became a state referee, negotiating settlements in legal cases. He also was chairman of the State Board of Education and Services for the Blind from 1970 to 1980.
        Mr. Shapiro was born in New York City. He graduated from the University of Connecticut School of Law in 1933.
        Survivors include his wife, Betty, and a son, Andrew.

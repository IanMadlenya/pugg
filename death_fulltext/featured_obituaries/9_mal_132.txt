        Stuart Buchan, a novelist and short story writer, died on Oct. 15 in the Berkshire Medical Center in Pittsfield, Mass. He was 45 years old and lived in Craryville, N.Y.
        Mr. Buchan died of AIDS-related pneumonia, according to a spokesman at the Pittsfield City Hall.
        Mr. Buchan was born in Sydney, Australia, on Aug. 30, 1942, and raised in Singapore, Scotland and Vancouver, British Columbia. A Canadian citizen, he graduated from the University of British Columbia in 1966 and won the Macmillan of Canada short story prize that year.
        Mr. Buchan wrote several books under his own name, including ''Fleeced,'' a mystery published by Putnam's (1975), and two novels, ''When We Lived With Pete'' (Scribner's, 1978) and ''Guys Like Us'' (Delacorte, 1986).
        He is survived by his father, Reginald, of Vancouver, and two sisters, Sheena Wyllie Muir Bienvenue of Vancouver and Kerry Erskine Muir Fife of Australia.

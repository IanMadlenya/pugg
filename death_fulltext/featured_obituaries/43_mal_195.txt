        Paul Russell Fitchen, a former banking executive, died on Saturday at his home in Brewster, N.Y. He was 88 years old.
        Mr. Fitchen died after cancer surgery, his wife, Eleanor, said.
        From 1957 until his retirment in 1967, Mr. Fitchen was executive director of the New York Clearing House Association at 100 Broad Street, which clears five million checks daily for its members. Earlier, he served for 23 years with the Federal Reserve Bank of New York, first as an examiner and in banking relations, and then with the bank's cash- and check-handling operations.
        He was born in Ithaca, N.Y., and graduated from Williams College and the Harvard School of Business Administration.
        He was active in civic affairs and was chairman of Brewster's first Conservation Commission; president of Save Open Spaces, a nonprofit Putnam County land trust he helped to organize, and president of the Brewster Public Library.
        Besides his wife, the former Eleanor Beach, he is survived by a son, Douglas, of Ithaca; two daughters, Ellen Tappan of Allendale, N.J., and Anne Burton of London; a sister, Ellen Cooper of Bedford, Mass., and 11 grandchildren.


        Shirley F. Bowden, an early advocate of middle-income housing in New York, died Tuesday at a nursing home in Valhalla, N.Y. He was 88.
        Mr. Bowden was founder and president of the Association for Middle Income Housing, which became the Metropolitan Mutual Housing Association. He was responsible for building large racially integrated housing co-ops in Manhattan, the Bronx and Brooklyn in the 1960's and 70's.
        One project, Morningside Gardens, changed the landscape north of Columbia University with 1,200 units of middle-income housing. At the time, it was one of the largest cooperative housing ventures in the nation.
        Mr. Bowden, a Methodist, worked  with religious and educational groups. He helped the Archdiocese of New York build the Christopher Court project in the Bronx.
        Shirley Fenwick Bowden was born in Rochester on April 14, 1911. A conscientious objector, he worked in a psychiatric hospital during World War II. After the war, he joined the United Housing Foundation, then founded the Association for Middle Income Housing in 1958. He also founded the Cooperative Housing Foundation in Washington.
        He is survived by his wife, Cynthia, a sister, Janet Thayer of Rochester, two daughters, Phyllis Thompson of Houston and Joyce Hundley of Washington, and four grandchildren.
        Correction:  September 14, 1999, Tuesday  An obituary on Thursday about an early advocate of middle-income housing in New York City misspelled his surname. He was Shirley F. Boden, not Bowden.

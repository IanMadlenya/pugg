
        Jarlath J. Graham, a former editor of Advertising Age, died on Jan. 24 at Wagner Health Center in Evanston, Ill. Mr. Graham, a resident of Chicago, was 78.
        The cause of death was cancer, said Carol Graham, his daughter.
        Mr. Graham, who was known as Jack, joined Advertising Age, a weekly trade publication, as an associate editor in 1950. He rose to the position of editor in 1969.
        In 1975, Mr. Graham became the director of editorial development for Crain Communications, the publishing company that owned Advertising Age and Crain Books. He was named vice president of communications and editorial development two years later, a position he retained until he retired in 1985. He was also a marketing columnist for Crain's Chicago Business.
        He is survived by his daughter, Carol, of Brattleboro, Vt. His wife, Elizabeth Carlson Graham, died in 1993, and a daughter, Karen Christie, died in 1987.

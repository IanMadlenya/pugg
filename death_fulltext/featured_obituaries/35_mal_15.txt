        William P. Hoffman, a stockbroker who from 1917 to 1965 was a member of the New York Curb Exchange and its successor, the American Stock Exchange, died on Nov. 18, 10 days before his 101st birthday, at his home in Menlo Park, Calif.
        Mr. Hoffman headed his own brokerage firm, William P. Hoffman Company, which acted as a clearing agent for specialists who traded on the exchange. He helped plan and arrange financing of the present exchange building on Trinity Place, which opened in 1921. The New York Curb Exchange became the American Stock Exchange in 1953.
        After retiring from active participation in his firm in 1942, he operated a cattle ranch in Colorado, which he sold in 1952 when he moved to Menlo Park.
        Mr. Hoffman is survived by a daughter, Ruth Fitzpatrick, of Mill Creek, Wash., and by 11 grandchildren, 31 great-grandchildren and 7 great-great-grandchildren.

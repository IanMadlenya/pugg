
        Dr. John Wilson Boylan, an internist who specialized in experimental kidney medicine, died on Tuesday at the University of Connecticut Health Center in Farmington. He was 76 years old.
        His family gave the cause of death as heart failure and complications from a yearlong illness.
        Dr. Boylan, who lived in Guilford, Conn., was born in Plattsburgh, N.Y.  He graduated from the Long Island College of Medicine, and later became associated with the University of Buffalo, where he was a professor of medicine and physiology. He was also president of the Buffalo Academy of Medicine.
        He retired in 1986 as chief of staff of the Veterans Administration Hospital in Newington, Conn. Until his retirement Dr. Boylan also did renal research during the summers at the Marine Biological Laboratory on Mount Desert Island in Maine.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Jean Curtin; four daughters, Nuala, Anne and Molly, all of New York City, and Alma of Wenham, Mass.; two sons, John, of Los Angeles, and Terence, of Rhinebeck, N.Y.; a brother, Alfred, of Rochester, and seven grandchildren.

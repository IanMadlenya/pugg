        Harry F. Nees, an environmentalist and insurance company official, died Monday at the Somers (N.Y.) Manor Nursing Home after a long illness. He was 87 years old and had lived in Belleville, N.J.
        Mr. Nees was associated with the Equitable Life Assurance Society of New York from 1922 until 1963, when he retired. He was a founder of the Scenic Hudson Preservation Conference, an environmental group that led the successful fight in the 1960's against construction by Consolidated Edison of a pump storage electric generating center along the Hudson River at Cornwall, N.Y. He was also a former president of the New York-New Jersey Trail Conference and was affiliated with the Sierra Club for many years.
        He is survived by two daughters, Claire Marrow of Spring Lake, N.J., and Ruth Bartlett of Armonk, N.Y.; five grandchildren, and two great-grandchildren.

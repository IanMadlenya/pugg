        Ruth Jackendoff, retired director of economics and statistics of the Wool Bureau, died of leukemia on Aug. 28 at her home in Manhattan. She was 80 years old.
        Miss Jackendoff, one of the first economists for the International Wool Secretariat, joined the Wool Bureau in 1948. Previously, she was director of business analysis of the Industrial Commodity Corporation in New York. After retiring in 1974, Miss Jackendorff taught English to the foreign-born in the School Volunteer Program.
        She was born in Manhattan and graduated from Cornell University.
        Surviving are three brothers, Sol and David, both of New York, and Nathaniel, of Philadelphia, and two sisters, Leda Engel of Palo Alto, Calif., and Helen Stickney of Rockville, Md.

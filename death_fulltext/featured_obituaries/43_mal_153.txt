        Mitchell R. Cutler, a dealer in rare art books, died on Monday at St. Vincent's Hospital. He was 39 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Cutler died of complications from AIDS, his mother, Lillian, said.
        After graduating from college, Mr. Cutler worked as an intern at the Metropolitan Museum of Art for a year before working for five years with several art dealers in Manhattan. In 1980, working out of his apartment, he began selling books about art and design ranging in price from $25 to $20,000.
        Mr. Cutler graduated from Syracuse University in 1973 and earned a master's degree from the Institute of Fine Arts in Manhattan.
        In addition to his mother, he is survived by his father, George, both of Rockville Centre, L.I.; his grandmother, Zelda Scheer of Stuart, Fla., and two brothers, Fred B., of Long Valley, N.J., and Dr. Adam, of Queens.

        Donald M. Bachmann, a retired certified public accountant and a former New York official, died, apparently of a heart attack, on Wednesday at his home in Sherman, Conn. He was 55 years old and also had a home in Manhattan.
        Mr. Bachmann was appointed by Gov. Mario M. Cuomo to head the Housing Finance Agency, the Medical Care Facilities Finance Agency, the Municipal Bond Bank Agency, the Project Finance Agency and the Affordable Housing Coprporation from 1984 to 1987.
        Gov. Hugh L. Carey had appointed Mr. Bachmann vice chairman of the State Facilities Development Corporation. He held the post from 1979 to 1984.
        Mr. Bachmann was a founding partner of the accounting firm of Bachmann, Schwartz & Abramson in Manhattan in 1973. He retired in 1987. A graduate of City College, he became a C.P.A. in 1959.
        He had been chairman of Applied D.N.A. Systems Inc. and was a board member of the Shield Institute for Retarded Children.
        Surviving are his wife, Harriet Bunny; two daughters, Jill Cheriff and Penny, and a son, Richard, all of Manhattan, and a brother, Steven, of Merrick, L.I.

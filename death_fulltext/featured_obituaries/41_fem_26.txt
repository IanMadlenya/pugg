        Isabelle Silverman, a former public relations consultant for the Cooper-Hewitt Museum, died on Thursday at the Jewish Home & Hospital for the Aged in Manhattan. She was 81 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mrs. Silverman died of anemia after a long illness, a hospital spokesman said.
        Mrs. Silverman was associated with Cooper-Hewitt from 1974 to 1986.
        She was born in Ashland, Ky., and studied music at the University of Cincinnati. Earlier in her career she was a business manager for several symphony orchestras including that of the Brooklyn Academy of Music.
        Mrs. Silverman is survived by her husband, Selig J.; three sisters, Violet Straus and Louise Kline of Portsmouth, Ohio, and Eva Sofer of Falls Church, Va.; and a brother, Harold Strauss of Huntington, W.Va.

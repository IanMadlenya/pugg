
        Edward A. Blatt, a theatrical and film producer and director, died on Tuesday on his 88th birthday. He lived in Manhattan.
        A family spokesman, Paul B. Berkowsky, said Mr. Blatt died after a heart attack on a mid-Manhattan street shortly after a celebration dinner.
        Mr. Blatt began his career in 1929 as the producer of two plays, "Harlem" and "Subway Express." He also wrote two plays, "Great Scott," on which he collaborated with Howard Koch, and "Young Man With a Horn," with Dorothy Baker.
        In Hollywood, Mr. Blatt directed "Between Two Worlds" (1943), "Escape in the Desert" (1944) and "Smart Woman" (1947).
        He is survived by his wife, Jane.

        Geoffrey Leon, an actor and lyricist, died Saturday at his home in Manhattan. He was 44 years old. He died of complications of AIDS, said a friend, Robert Poterelli. Mr. Leon graduated from the University of California at Santa Barbara in 1967 and moved to New York the same year. He appeared on Broadway in ''Georgy'' and was a founder of a vocal group, Twentieth Century. He was also co-author of the 1986 Off Broadway show ''A Battle of Nerves'' at the Village Gate.
        He is survived by his parents, Barbara and Ralph Leon of Lake Elsinore, Calif., and a brother, Alan, of Salem, Ore. His companion was John D. Tsikatis of Manhattan.

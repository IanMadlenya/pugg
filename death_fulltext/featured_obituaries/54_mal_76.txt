
        Frank Whitson Fetter, an economist and educator, died on Sunday at the Hanover Terrace Healthcare, a nursing home in Hanover, N.H. He was 92 years old and lived in Hanover.
        Dr. Fetter died of cancer, said a spokesman for the family.
        Dr. Fetter taught economics at Princeton University, Haverford College and Northwestern University and lectured at the Johns Hopkins University School for Advanced International Studies.
        From 1950 to 1973 he was a director of the National Bureau of Economic Research. He was a former president of the Midwest Economic Association.
        In the 1920's Dr. Fetter served with the Kemmerer Commission of Financial Advisers to Government, working in Chile, Bolivia, Ecuador, Poland and China.
        He graduated from Swarthmore College and received master's degrees from Princeton and Harvard universities and a Ph.D. in economics from Princeton.
        In World War II he served with the Lend Lease Administration and with the State Department.
        He is survived by two sons, Robert P. of Roanoke, Va., and Thomas W. of Thetford Center, Vt.; a brother, Theodore 2d of New York City ; a daughter, Ellen Gille of Boulder, Colo.; and six grandchildren.

        John W. Miller, president of Miller-Rogaska Inc., an importer of crystal, died of heart failure Friday in John F. Kennedy Memorial Hospital in Atlantis, Fla. He was 65 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Miller established the American subsidiary of Waterford Crystal in 1960. Three years ago, he formed he formed Miller-Rogaska, an American distributor of Yugoslav crystal.
        He was born in Monmouth Beach, N.J.
        Surviving are his wife, Barbara; six daughters, Judith Jacubecy and Lisa Roberto, both of Oceanport, N.J., Evelene Arliss of Navesink, N.J., Patricia Amburg of Spring Lake, N.J., Joanne Timolat of Fair Haven, N.J., and Evangeline, of Manhattan; a son, Richard, of Manhattan; a stepdaughter, Lisa Lewin of Manhattan; a stepson, Joshua Lewin of West New York, N.J.; two brothers, Ira, of Rumson, N.J., and Carlton, of Tenants Harbor, Me., and six grandchildren.

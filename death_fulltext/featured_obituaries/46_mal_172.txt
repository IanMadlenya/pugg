
        Elmer M. Bloch, a retired New York stockbroker, died on Tuesday at his home in Manhattan. He was 87 years old.
        Mr. Bloch died of pneumonia, his son-in-law, David Lupton, said.
        Mr. Bloch became a member of the New York Stock Exchange in 1928. He served two terms as a member of the board of governors, from 1954 to 1960. He retired from the firm of Henry Goldberg & Company in 1984.
        Mr. Bloch was a founder of the Buttonwood Club, a charitable organization for stock exchange members.
        He was a native of Newport, R.I., and a graduate of Lehigh University in 1923. A violinist, he was a member of the Bay String Quartet until his death.
        He is survived by a daughter, Claudette Lupton, of Locust, N.J.

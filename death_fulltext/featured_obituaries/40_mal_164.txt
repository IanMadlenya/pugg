        Dr. Antoni B. Wojcicki, a retired social-affairs officer at the United Nations and a former official of the Polish government in exile during World War II, died on May 14 at his home in Menlo Park, Calif. He was 83 years old and had recently suffered a heart attack.
        Dr. Wojcicki, a graduate of Columbia University with a Ph.D. in adult education, joined the United Nations in 1946 after emigrating from London to the United States. He served with the World Health Organization and the Food and Agriculture Organization before retiring in 1966. He remained a consultant until 1975.
        While in Poland, he was regional director of the YMCA and served in the Polish Army and later with Polish units in the French Army during World War II. After being evacuated to London, he later served as secretary of the Polish government in exile.
        He is survived by his wife, Halina of Cambridge, Mass.; a daughter, Jagna Sharff of Manhattan; a son, Andrei of Palo Alto, Calif.; a step-daughter, Christine Gaynor of New Haven; seven grandchildren and one great-grandson.

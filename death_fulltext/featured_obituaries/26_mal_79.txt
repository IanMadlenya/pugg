        James Satterwhite, a social worker and educator, died of cancer Wednesday at Memorial Sloan-Kettering Cancer Center in Manhattan. He was 52 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Satterwhite, a graduate of the Columbia University School of Social Work, was director of the Child Protective Services Training Academy of the New York City Human Resources Administration. Previously, he directed training for the Department of Welfare and headed the Mayor's Committee on Foster Care. He had also taught social work at Columbia University and Hunter College.
        He is survived by two sisters, Severia Park of Oxford, N.C., and Ruby Mitchell of Fayetteville, N.C., and four brothers, Fred, of Richmond, and James Davis, Edward Davis and Albert Holloway, all of Oxford.

        Stefan Korbonski, a Polish statesman, author and a leader of the underground resistance against the Germans in World War II, died of pneumonia Sunday at George Washington University Hospital in Washington D.C. He was 86 years old and lived in Washington.
        A graduate of Poznan University with a degree in law, Mr. Korbonski was chairman of the Polish Peasant Party and a member of the underground government of Poland. He was chief of general resistance from 1941 to 1944 and was later a member of the Polish Parliament. In 1947, when the Communists gained firm control of Poland, he escaped to Sweden and later moved to the United States.
        Mr. Korbonski was the author of many books and articles in Polish, including ''Fighting Warsaw'' and ''Warsaw in Chains,'' published by Macmillan in 1956 and 1959.
        He is survived by his wife, Zofia; and two sisters.


        Lawrence G. Hines, professor of economics emeritus at Dartmouth College, died Saturday at the Dartmouth-Hitchcock Medical Center in Hanover, N.H. He was 75 years old and lived in Hanover.
        Professor Hines died of cancer, a family spokesman said.
        He joined the Dartmouth faculty in 1947 and became a full professor in 1953. He was chairman of the social sciences division from 1960 to 1963 and of the economics department from 1963 to 1965. He retired in 1986.
        He wrote several books on economics and was a consultant to the Department of the Interior, the Department of Transportation and the Sierra Club, among others.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Ann Philips ; a son, Terence Hines of Ossining, N.Y., and a granddaughter.

        Richard Kaufman, a managing director of the Wall Street firm of Donaldson, Lufkin & Jenrette, died yesterday at Lenox Hill Hospital. He was 54 years old and lived in Stamford, Conn.
        He died of a heart attack, family members said.
        Mr. Kaufman, who joined the firm in 1984, was head of its retail brokerage operations. He had previously been with Paine Webber as an executive vice president.
        He was born in Worcester, Mass., and was a gradute of Brandeis University. He served in the Army for six years.
        Mr. Kaufman is survived by his wife, the former Joanne Ippolito; his mother, Anne Kaplan of Malden, Mass.; a son, Kenneth, of Rome, and a daughter, Shari Levins of Manhattan.

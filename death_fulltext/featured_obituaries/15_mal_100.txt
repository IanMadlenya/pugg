        Kanzaburo Nakamura, a leading kabuki actor and recipient of cultural awards from the Japanese Government, died following a heart attack today, people in the kabuki world reported. He was 78 years old.
        Mr. Nakamura, whose real name was Seiji Namino, was active in promoting kabuki overseas and led a troupe to New York in 1960. He also performed in Europe.
        Kabuki, a highly stylized form of drama, originated in 1603 in Kyoto and is performed solely by men.
        Mr. Nakamura began on the stage in 1916. In 1980, he received an Order of Culture award from the Japanese Government for his contribution to the art form, and was designated a ''holder of important intangible cultural properties.''
        He is survived by his wife, Hisae, and three children.

        Sol Orlinsky, president of S. O. Textiles, which manufactures plastic hangers and supplies notions to the garment industry, died on Wednesday at Lenox Hill Hospital. He was 73 years old and lived in Woodmere, L.I.
        Mr. Orlinsky died of complications from diabetes, his wife said.
        A native New Yorker, he founded the company in Manhattan 42 years ago and was later joined by a partner, Julian Bernstein.
        Mr. Orlinsky was past president and chairman of the Pride of Judea, an organization that treats emotionally disturbed children and young adults. He was also a founder of Albert Einstein College of Medicine.
        Besides his wife, the former Sarah Goldstein, he is survived by a daughter, Myra Brumberger of Escondido, Calif.; three brothers, Max, of Queens, Phil, of Manhattan and Ben, of Tamarack, Fla., and two grandchildren.

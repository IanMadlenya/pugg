
        Wade H. Nichols, former editor in chief of Good Housekeeping and editor in chief and publisher of Redbook, died at Calvary Hospital in the Bronx on March 15. He was 80 and lived in Bronxville, N.Y.
        The cause was cancer, said his son, Wade H. Nichols 3d of Bronxville.
        Mr. Nichols was also a vice president and director of the McCall Corporation, which at the time owned Redbook. He worked there from 1949 to 1958. He joined Good Housekeeping in 1958 and became a vice president of the Hearst Corporation, its parent.
        In addition to his son, he is survived by his daughter, Nancy O'Donnell of Brewster, N.Y., five grandchildren and a great-grandchild. His wife, Edith, died in 1976.

        Paul Cioffi, a physicist at Bell Laboratories, now A.T. & T. Bell Laboratories, for more than 40 years, died of liver cancer Sunday at his home in Summit, N.J. He was 94 years old.
        Mr. Cioffi, a specialist in magnetics, held several patents and had many technical papers published. After his retirement in 1961, he continued as a private consultant until 1983.
        He was born in Cervinara, Italy, and was brought to the United States as a child. He received an electrical engineering degree from Cooper Union and a master's degree in physics from Columbia University. He was a fellow of the American Physical Society and a member of the Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers.
        Mr. Cioffi is survived by his wife, the former Anne Rossano; a daughter, Lucille McLeod of Holmdel, N.J.; two sisters, Mary Mignani and Florence Cioffi, both of Wading River, L.I., and a grandchild.

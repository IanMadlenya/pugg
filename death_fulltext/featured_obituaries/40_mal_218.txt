        Howard D. Hosbach, a former president of the Standard & Poor's Corporation and former executive vice president of operations for McGraw-Hill, died of cancer on Friday at his home in Allendale, N.J. He was 59 years old.
        Mr. Hosbach joined McGraw-Hill in 1955 and worked for the company or its subsidiaries until his retirement in 1988. He occupied several management positions with the McGraw-Hill Book Company from 1958 to 1969 and switched to Standard & Poor's, the financial analysis service, in 1970. He was president from 1981 to 1984. In 1985, he became executive vice president of operations for McGraw-Hill, the parent company.
        Born in North Bergen, N.J., Mr. Hosbach was a graduate of Fairleigh Dickinson University, where he received a master's degree in business administration in 1967.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Eugenia Paracka of Allendale, N.J.; four daughters, Susan Murray and Cynthia Miezeiewski, both of Nutley, N.J., Beth Ann Lowry of Midland Park, N.J., and Alyssa Hosbach of Allendale, and five grandchildren.

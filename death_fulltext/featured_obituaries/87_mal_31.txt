
        Barnard Townsend, a retired publishing executive and banker, died on March 24 at his Manhattan home. He was 85.
        The cause was a heart attack, said his daughter, Diana Townsend-Butterworth.
        Mr. Townsend was an administrator of the E. W. Scripps Company of Cincinnati from 1955 until his retirement in 1979 as financial vice president. Before that he had been financial vice president of the Mohawk Carpet Mills and president of the Title Guarantee and Trust Company of New York.
        He was a 1932 graduate of Yale University.
        Mr. Townsend is survived by his wife of 61 years, Marjorie, and his daughter, both of New York, and two grandchildren.

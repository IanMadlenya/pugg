
        Fred Fortess, an authority on the use of fabrics in clothing who was a retired professor at the Philadelphia College of Textiles and Science, died on Saturday at Graduate Hospital in Philadelphia. He was 77 years old and lived in Philadelphia and in Ventnor, N.J.
        He died of heart disease, his family said.
        Mr. Fortess joined the college in 1973 as director of apparel research and head of its School of Textiles.
        Previously he was with the Celanese Corporation for 31 years. At Celanese he worked on the development of synthetic rubber in World War II and received 40 patents on new dyeing and finishing processes textiles and helped to develop the synthetics Arnel and Fortrel.
        Mr. Fortess, a native of Antwerp, Belgium, was a graduate of the University of Chicago and did graduate work there in physical organic chemistry.
        He was a past president of the American Association of Textile Chemists and Colorists. He received the Medal of Honor of the Southern Burn Institute for contributions to fire safety and the Harold DeWitt Smith Award of the American Society of Testing and Materials.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Ethel Miller; two daughters, Janet, of Ithaca, N.Y., and Jane Gerber of Manhattan; two brothers, Karl, of Woodstock, N.Y., and Leo, of Oahu, Hawaii, and two grandchildren.
        Correction:  January 11, 1991, Friday
        An obituary on Tuesday about Fred Fortess, a retired professor who was an authority on fabrics, misidentified a surviving daughter. She is June Gerber of Manhattan.

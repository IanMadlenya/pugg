
        Bernard J. Minetti, a former executive with the Central Railroad of New Jersey, died on Monday at the Indian River Memorial Hospital in Vero Beach, Fla. He was 86 years old and had recently moved to Vero Beach from Pompano Beach, Fla.
        Mr. Minetti died three days after suffering a major stroke, his son Richard said.
        For 29 years, Mr. Minetti was the chief engineer of the Central Railroad of New Jersey and vice president of several of its subsidiaries. After retiring from the Central, he became chief of the Bureau of Rail and Motor Carrier Division of the New Jersey Public Utilities Commission. He retired from that position in 1977.
        Graduating from Polytechnic Institute of New York with a master's degree in civil engineering, Mr. Minetti began his long career in the railroad industry in 1926 at the New York Central Railroad.
        Besides his son Richard of Vero Beach, Mr. Minetti is survived by his wife of 62 years, Ettorina; another son, Bernard Jr. of Tucson, Ariz.; a daughter, Marilyn Barletta of San Francisco; a brother, Joseph of Washington, and four grandchildren.

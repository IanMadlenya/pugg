        Albert Loewentheil, a retired Westchester County real-estate developer, died Wednesday at Lawrence Hospital in Bronxville. He was 88 years old and lived in Eastchester.
        Mr. Loewentheil, a graduate of City College of New York, was a vice president of the Colonial Sand and Stone Company and in 1947 opened Aetna Builders Materials Inc. In 1964, he founded Loewen Associates and built more than 1,000 houses and apartments.
        He is survived by his wife, Florence; three sons, Howard, of Mamaroneck, N.Y., Leon, of Monticello, N.Y., and Kenneth, of White Plains; five grandchildren, and six great-grandchildren.

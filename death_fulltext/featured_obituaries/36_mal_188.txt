        Francis Higginson Philp, a senior vice president of the Fiduciary Trust Company International, died of a heart attack on Thursday at Presbyterian Hospital. He was 59 years old and lived in Larchmont, N.Y.
        Mr. Philp joined Fiduciary Trust in 1972, after having been an officer of the Empire Trust Company and of the Bank of New York.
        Mr. Philp was born in New York. He graduated cum laude from Princeton University in 1952 and attended the Harvard Law School. He was in the Army from 1953 to 1954. Before beginning his career in banking, he was associated with Dunnington, Bartholow & Miller, a law firm in Manhattan.
        He was a director of Visions-Services for the Visually Impaired and chairman of the Fund for the Blind. Mr. Philp had been a trustee of the New York Infirmary-Beekman Downtown Hospital; the Westchester Council of the Arts; Clear Pool Camp, which operated programs in Carmel, N.Y., for needy urban children, and the Princeton Library.
        Surviving is a brother, Sanborn, of Pittsfield, Mass.

        Ben-Fleming Sessel, a retired executive vice president of the Irving Trust Company, a New York commercial bank, died of Alzheimer's disease Jan. 13 in the Hotel Seteais in Sintra, Portugal. He was 86 years old and lived in New Canaan, Conn.
        Mr. Sessel retired in 1967. He had been with the bank since the early 1940's.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Esther Ford; a son, Palmer, of San Francisco; two daughters, Linda Curtiss of Newton, Mass., and Amanda Legare of North Montpelier, Vt.; two stepsons, George Snowden of Manhattan, and Richard Wechsler of New Canaan, and six grandchildren.

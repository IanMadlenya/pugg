        Thomas J. Davis, a founder of the Mayfield Fund, a venture-capital investment firm on the West Coast, died Tuesday at the University Convalescent Hospital in Menlo Park, Calif. He was 77 years old.
        A spokesman for the Mayfield Fund said he died after a stroke.
        Mr. Davis founded the fund, which helped start more than 125 companies in high-technology fields, in 1969. Eight years earlier he and Arthur Rock founded Davis & Rock, one of the nation's first venture-capital firms.
        Mr. Davis, a native of Cincinnati, was a graduate of Harvard, where he was captain of the polo team, and of the Harvard Law School. In World War II he served on the War Production Board, the Lend-Lease Administration and in the Office of Strategic Services as an Army captain.
        After the war he was with the National Labor Relations Board in San Francisco before becoming a vice president of the Kern County Land Company.
        Mr. Davis, who lived in Woodside, Calif., is survived by his wife, the former Shirley Ross; six daughters, Nancy Holter of Ross, Calif., Diana Michener of London, Virginia Nivola of Somerville, Mass., Cynthia Norgeot of Kentfield, Calif., Christiana Jones of Tahoe City, Calif., and Jane Davis of Manhattan; a sister, Virginia Loomis of Oyster Bay, L.I.; a brother, Laurence, of Homosassa, Fla., and 11 grandchildren.

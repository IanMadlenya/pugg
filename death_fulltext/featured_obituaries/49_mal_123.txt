
        Wallace Wesley Elton, a former advertising executive and a retired senior vice president of the International Executive Services Corporation, died on Thursday of injuries suffered in an automobile accident in Coral Gables, Fla.
        He was 83 years old and had homes in Key Biscayne, Fla. and Rye, N.Y.
        Mr. Elton served from 1940 to 1966 with the J. Walter Thompson Company in Manhattan and was an executive vice president when he left. He then joined the International Executive Services, a private, nonprofit organization intended to help businesses in developing countries, as a vice president for United States operations and then as vice president for development. He retired in 1987.
        He served as a lieutenant commander in the Navy during World War II.
        He is survived by his wife, Jane, who was also severely injured in the accident; two brothers, Roger, of Pinehurst, N.C. and Richard, of Phoenix, and two sisters, Elizabeth Hardy, also of Phoenix and Jean Moran, of Springfield, Va.

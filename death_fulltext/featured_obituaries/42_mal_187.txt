        Donald Meston Lovejoy, a former general partner at Bache & Company, died on Monday at Greenwich (Conn.) Hospital in Connecticut. He was 93 years old and lived in Greenwich and in Gulf Stream, Fla.
        Mr. Lovejoy died of heart failure, his son Thomas said.
        Mr. Lovejoy began working on Wall Street in 1928 and became a member of the New York Stock Exchange in 1932. He joined Bache & Company, now Prudential-Bache Securities, in 1936 and became a senior floor partner. He retired as an officer in 1971, but continued as a floor broker until 1978.
        For more than 30 years he was president of Minent Ltd., which he co-founded in 1954. Minent was an original shareholder in the Liberia Mining Company, which formed a joint venture with the Republic Steel Company to develop an iron-ore mine in Liberia.
        Mr. Lovejoy was born in New Rochelle, N.Y. He attended Dartmouth College, leaving in 1917 to enlist in the Navy.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Winifred Walker; three sons, Thomas of Greenwich, Donald, of West Palm Beach, Fla., and Robert, of Binghamton, N.Y., and three grandchildren.


        Robert H. Mulreaney, a retired senior law partner in Manhattan and a former chairman of the Community Service Society of New York, died on April 12 at the King James Care Center in Chatham Township, N.J. He was 82 and a former resident of Summit and Westfield, N.J.
        Mr. Mulreaney headed the trustees of the Community Service Society, a private welfare agency, from 1959 to 1972. He was also a past chairman of the New York School of Social Work at Columbia University and the Mayor's Advisory Committee on Public Welfare, as well as a former mayor and school board president of Westfield, among many other public commitments.
        He was born in Brooklyn, studied architecture at Brooklyn Technical High School and worked his way through college and law school at New York University. He began a 57-year career at what is now the firm of DeForest & Duer in 1933 as an office boy.
        Rising to senior partner, he specialized in tax-exempt foundations dealing with social problems, better management techniques, medical discoveries and developments in leadership. He also had long associations with Columbia Presbyterian Hospital and Overlook Hospital in Summit.
        Mr. Mulreaney is survived by his wife of 57 years, Dorothy Muens Mulreaney of Summit; two daughters, Doreen O'Brien of Springfield, Ill., and Carol Henwood of San Francisco; a sister, Virginia Worn of Brandon, Vt., and four grandchildren.

        Frank A. Gulotta, a former presiding justice of the appellate division of the New York State Supreme Court and the father of the Nassau County Executive, Thomas S. Gulotta, died of complications of diabetes on Sunday at the Franklin Hospital Medical Center in Valley Stream, L.I. He was 82 years old and lived in Lynbrook, L.I.
        Justice Gulotta was first elected to the Supreme Court in 1958. He joined the appellate division in 1971 and was presiding justice of the Second Department, which covers Brooklyn, Queens, Staten Island, Long Island and several upstate counties, from 1974 through 1977, when he retired. He was a senior associate justice until 1983.
        A graduate of St. John's University Law School, Justice Gulotta was admitted to the bar in 1932. He was on the Lynbrook Zoning Board of Appeals and was the Lynbrook Village Counsel. He was an assistant district attorney from 1938 to 1949, when Gov. Thomas E. Dewey appointed him district attorney for Nassau County. He held that post until 1958.
        In World War II, Mr. Gulotta was a major in the Army.
        In addition to his son, surviving are his wife, Josephine; another son, Frank Jr., of Woodbury, L.I.; a daughter, Franca Rizzo of North Merrick, L.I.; a brother, Charles, of East Williston, L.I., and six grandchildren.

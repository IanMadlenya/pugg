
        Richard Segrin, an executive in the pattern industry, died on Thursday at Hospice of Palm Beach County, in Palm Beach, Fla.
        He was 44 years old and died of complications from AIDS, said his companion, Geordi Humphreys.
        Mr. Segrin was born in Springfield, Mass. and graduated from Pratt Institute in New York. He was design director of major pattern companies, starting with Butterick in 1969. He also worked for Vogue Patterns and became a vice president of the McCall's Pattern Company and Simplicity.
        In 1988 he became chairman of the Palm Beach County Food Pantry, which provides meals for people with AIDS.
        He is survived by his mother, Claire Segrin of Springfield.

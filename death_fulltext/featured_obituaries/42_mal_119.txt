        John M. Musser, an executive of the Weyerhaeuser Company and its affiliated businesses from 1934 until his retirement in 1978, died Saturday at his home in St. Paul. He was 82 years old.
        His family said he died after a brief illness.
        Mr. Musser, born in Muscatine, Iowa, was a graduate of Yale College and received a master's degree from the Harvard School of Business Administration.
        He began with Weyerhaeuser as an auditor in a subsidiary company. He rose to president of the Northwest Paper Company and of the Wayerhaeuser Sales Company before joining board of the parent company and being elected to its executive committee.
        He was active in a number of organizations in agriculture, forestry and education. He was a founder and chairman of the Nitrogen Fixing Tree Assocaition in Hawaii, a trustee of Carleton College, president of the Yale University Council and chairman of the General Service Foundation and of the St. Paul Chamber Orchestra.
        Mr. Musser is survived by his wife, the former Elizabeth Willett; two daughters, Susan Halby of Concord, Mass., and Lee Dominick of Sheridan, Wyo.; a son, Robert, of Boulder, Colo.; a sister, Marion Lloyd of Libertyville, Ill., and seven grandchildren.

        Richard Siegel, a retired English teacher, died of a heart attack yesterday at his home in Manhattan. He was 70 years old.
        He was born in Boston, graduated from Harvard and earned a master's degree there. He was an associate professor at Queensborough Community College for six years and taught for more than two decades in New York City's public high schools. He retired from John Bowne High School in Flushing, Queens, in 1983.
        Surviving are his wife, Pauline, who is known professionally as Bobbe Siegel, a literary agent; two sons, Peter, of Merrick, L.I., and Robert, of Rockville Centre, L.I.; five sisters, Elinor Sawyer of Jaffrey Center, N.H., Phyllis A. Ehrlich of Norwalk, Conn., Louise Ladd of Lawrence, Mass., Denise M. Sabal of Manhattan, and Althea N. Fiske of Hopedale, Mass.; two brothers, James, of Sunrise, Fla., and Nathan, of Jacksonville, Fla., and two grandchildren.

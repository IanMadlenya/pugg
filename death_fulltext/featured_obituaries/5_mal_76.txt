        Paul D. O'Brien, a New York lawyer who had many famous clients from the entertainment industry, died after a long illness on May 29 at his home in Longboat Key, Fla. He was 82 years old.
        He represented, among others, the silent screen stars Mary Pickford and Douglas Fairbanks; the composers Irving Berlin and George M. Cohan; the producer Michael Todd, and the former heavyweight boxing champion Jack Dempsey.
        He retired in 1970 as a senior partner of O'Brien, Driscoll & Raftery, successor to the firm of O'Brien, Malevinsky Driscoll & O'Brien, which was founded by his father, Dennis F. O'Brien, in 1913. The firm, now known as Raftery, Grainger, Rosenbloom & Drew, was one of the founders of United Artists.
        He was born in Narragansett Pier, R.I., and graduated from Exeter Academy, Brown University in 1927 and Harvard Law School in 1930.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Kathryn Walsh; three sons, Dennis, of Hoffman Estates, Ill., Peter, of Lafayette, Calif., and Barry, of Essex, Mass.; a daughter, Sharon, of Agoura Hills, Calif.; a sister, Denise Shay of Misquamicut, R.I.; a brother, Robert, of Quonachon, R.I., and eight grandchildren.

        Joseph James Nazzaro, a retired four-star general of the Air Force, died of cancer Monday at the Tucson Medical Center in Arizona. He was 76 years old and lived in Tucson.
        General Nazzaro, who retired in 1971 after 35 years in the military, was a former commander in chief of both the Strategic Air Command and the Pacific Air Force.
        A native of Brooklyn, he was a 1936 graduate of the United States Military Academy, attending flight school after graduation. In World War II he directed the Army Air Forces in England and a bombardment unit in Okinawa.
        He later commanded SAC units in North Africa, California and Nebraska. He became commander in chief of SAC in 1967 and was also director of joint strategic target planning. In 1968 he became head of the Pacific Air Force. His awards included the Distinguished Service Medal, the Croix de Guerre and the Air Medal.
        He is survived by his wife, Helen; two daughters, Janet Corey of Laguna Beach, Calif., and Barbara Mitchell of Scottsdale, Ariz.; a brother, John of Melbourne, Fla.; a sister, Rose Perrotta of Rego Park, Queens, and one granddaughter.
        Correction:  February 9, 1990, Friday, Late Edition - Final
        An obituary on Wednesday about Joseph James Nazzaro, a retired Air Force general, referred incorrectly to one of his World War II assignments. He commanded a bombardment group of the Army Air Forces in England.


        Wesley C. Clark, a former dean of the School of Journalism at Syracuse University, died on Tuesday at his home in Skaneateles, N.Y. He was 83 years old.
        He joined the faculty at Syracuse in 1941 and was dean from 1952 to 1971, when he retired and the journalism school became part of the S. I. Newhouse School of Public Communications.
        Mr. Clark had been a reporter and columnist for The Philadelphia Evening Bulletin for almost 10 years. From 1937 to 1941 he was also an instructor in public opinion and American government at the Wharton School of the University of Pennsylvania.
        Survivors include a son, William, of Ellsworth, Me.; a daughter, Sally Judd of Indianapolis; a stepdaughter, Mary Kate Shane of Skaneateles; a stepson, Peter Eckel of Bellevue, Wash., and four grandchildren.

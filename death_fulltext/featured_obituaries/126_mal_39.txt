
        Guy Murchie, an author, artist and aviator, died on Tuesday at a convalescent hospital in Fullerton, Calif. He was 90.
        Mr. Murchie was a Chicago Tribune photographer, staff artist and reporter, serving as a war correspondent in England and Iceland  from 1940 to 1942. He was also a flight instructor. His books included ''Men on the Horizon'' (1932), ''Song of the Sky'' (1954), ''Music of the Spheres'' (1961) and ''The Seven Mysteries of Life'' (1978).
        He is survived by a daughter, Gretel Goldsmith; a son, Barnaby Porter; three grandchildren, and one great-grandchild.

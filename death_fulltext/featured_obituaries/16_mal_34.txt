        Jean P. Hesse, a retired United Nations administrator and officer with the Free French during World War II, died Monday in Montreal. He was 85.
        Born in Neuilly-sur-Seine, France, Mr. Hesse was the science editor of the Dictionnaires Larousse and author of a book on the Curie family.
        In 1946, he was sent to the United Nations, where he became head of terminology. He returned to Paris in 1964 and retired to Montreal in 1968.
        Surviving are his wife, Eleanor Sparks; a son, Roland, of Paris; a daughter, Patricia Treves of Manhattan, and four grandchildren.

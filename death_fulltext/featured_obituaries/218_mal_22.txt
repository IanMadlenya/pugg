
        William G. Craig, who led college systems in California and Vermont and directed the Monterey Institute of International Studies, died on Monday at his home in San Diego. He was 90.
        The death was confirmed by his son Gregory B. Craig, the Washington lawyer.
        The elder Mr. Craig was chancellor of the California Community College system from 1977 to 1980 and of the Vermont State Colleges from 1973 to 1976.
        From 1980 to 1988 he was president of the Monterey Institute.
        At Stanford University, he was associate dean of students and associate professor of education from 1954 to 1962. He was previously dean of students at Kansas State University and Washington State University.
        He directed training for the Peace Corps in the early 1960's. In 1966 he became deputy to John Gardner and Harold Howe II at the Department of Health, Education and Welfare.
        William Gregory Craig was born in West Hebron, N.Y., on Oct. 2, 1914. He graduated from Middlebury College, where he was captain of the undefeated 1936 football team.
        After serving in the Navy during World War II, he received a master's degree in psychology from the University of Minnesota and a doctorate in education from Harvard.
        In addition to his son Gregory B., of Washington, he is survived by his wife, Lorenza; 3 other sons, James B., of Irvine, Calif.; Thomas G., of Charlottesville, Va.; and Dr. William A.P., of Montpelier, Vt.; a stepson, Dr. Ron Schmidt of Portland, Me.; and 10 grandchildren. His marriage to Lois B. Craig ended in divorce.

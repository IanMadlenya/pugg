        Jack Clark, the announcer for NBC's popular television game show ''Wheel of Fortune,'' died of bone cancer at St. Joseph's Medical Center in Burbank last Thursday. He was 62 years old.
        Mr. Clark was born in St. Joseph, Mo., and brought up in California. He began his career in broadcasting more than 40 years ago, and received his first professional job as a substitute radio announcer at KROW in Oakland. After graduating, he worked as a staff radio announcer for CBS.
        By 1952, Mr. Clark had moved to New York, where he became the program announcer for the television game shows ''Password,'' ''To Tell the Truth,'' ''Split Second'' and ''The $25,000 Pyramid.''
        He is survived by his wife, Barbara, and four children.

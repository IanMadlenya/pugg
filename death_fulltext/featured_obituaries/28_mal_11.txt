        Philip Spina, the founder and retired president of Standard Tile, one of the country's largest tile distributors, died of complications from a fall on Sunday at Mercy Hospital in Rockville Centre, L.I. He was 93 years old and lived in Rockville Centre.
        Mr. Spina, who was born in Italy, came to the United States in 1907. In 1920, he founded the Standard Tile Company, with its headquarters in Brooklyn. He retired in 1970.
        He was a member of the council of St. John's University, a lifetime member of Casa Italiana at Columbia University and a former president of the Frank J. Becker Educational Foundation, in Lynbrook, L.I.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Mary Amari; two daughters, Josephine Stigliano of Garden City, L.I., and Catherine Gerard of Glen Ridge, N.J.; a brother, John, of Cape Coral, Fla.; 10 grandchildren, and three great-grandchildren.

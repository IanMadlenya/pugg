        William P. Palmer, a senior partner in the Manhattan law firm of Dewey, Ballantine, Bushby, Palmer & Wood, died Monday at Stamford (Conn.) Hospital. He was 92 years old and lived in Darien, Conn.
        Mr. Palmer, a native of Fall River, Mass., and a 12th-generation Mayflower descendant, joined a predecessor law firm in 1925 after having served in the Navy in World War I and having graduated from Harvard College and Harvard Law School. He was a member of Phi Beta Kappa as an undergraduate and was editor of The Harvard Law Review.
        As a lawyer he handled a wide variety of corporate, financing and litigation matters. He was counsel to the Reconstruction Finance Corporation and to the American Can Company for many years. He represented claimants in several railroad reorganizations, including that of the New York New Haven & Hartford, the Wabash and the Missouri Pacific.
        Mr. Palmer is survived by his wife, Alice; three sons from a former marriage, William Jr., of Ridgefield, Conn., John, of Riverside, Conn., and David, of Bristol, Conn.; eight grandchildren, and six great-grandchildren.

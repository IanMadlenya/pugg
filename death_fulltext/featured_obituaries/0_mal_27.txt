        Edwin J. Thomas, retired chairman of the Goodyear Tire and Rubber Company, died last Tuesday at the Akron General Medical Center in Ohio. He was 87 years old and lived in Akron.
        Mr. Thomas rose from stenographer to chairman in his 55-year career at Goodyear. He joined the company in 1916 while a senior at high school. He was superintendent of the Goodyear plant in Los Angeles and in charge of the company's operations in Britain before his election as president in 1941.
        He was named chief executive officer in 1956 and chairman in 1958. He stepped down from the chairmanship in 1964, and from the board of directors in 1971.
        He was a director of the University of Akron, where the Edwin J. Thomas Performing Arts Hall was named for him.
        He leaves his wife, the former Mildred Vaughan; a daughter, Jean Mercer, of Akron, and two grandchildren.

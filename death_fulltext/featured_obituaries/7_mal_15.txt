        Frederick B. Silliman, chairman of the board of the Hydraulic Company of Bridgeport, Conn., parent company of Bridgeport Hydraulic, a water utility, died Monday at the Carolton Chronic Convalescent Hospital in Fairfield, Conn. He was 72 years old and lived in Fairfield.
        Mr. Silliman, who retired from the day-to-day operation of the company in 1982 but remained chairman, played a major role in the formation of the Hydraulic Company, a holding company, out of Bridgeport Hydraulic.
        He was born in Bridgeport and graduated from the Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute. He was president of Bridgeport Hydraulic from 1956 to 1973. He was named chairman of the holding company in 1979.
        He is survived by his wife, Isabel; four daughters, Blair MacLeod of Bridgeport, Margaret Hawley of Glastonbury, Conn., Elizabeth Clarke of Westport, Conn., and Wendy Dolce of Rye, N.Y.; a stepson, Douglas Low of Nashua, N.H.; two stepdaughters, Alice Ellsworth of London and Marian Horton of Greenwich, Conn.; a sister, Mrs. Henry Callahan of Westfield, N.J., and 11 grandchildren.

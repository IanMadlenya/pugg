        Bernard Striar, a textile manufacturer, died at his home in Miami Beach on Tuesday after a long illness. He was 76 years old and also had a home in South Orange, N.J.
        Mr. Striar was president of the Eastland Woolen Mills Corporation, a company founded by his father in 1912, with operations in Manhattan and Corinna, Me.
        Mr. Striar was a graduate of Boston University. He was active in many Jewish philanthropies and was a member of the board of Yeshiva University Graduate Schools.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Carol Finkelhoffe; two sons, Alan, of New Rochelle, N.Y., and David, of Portland, Ore.; two daughters, Patricia Rohner of Georgetown, Mass., and Robin Rollnick of Miami; a brother, Max, of Bangor, Me.; two sisters, Sylvia Menaker of Baltimore, and Mildred Kominsky of Bangor, and nine grandchildren.

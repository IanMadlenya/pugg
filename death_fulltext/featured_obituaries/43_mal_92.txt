        William E. Foley, the Federal judiciary's chief administrative officer from 1977 to 1985, died Saturday at Sibley Memorial Hospital in Washington. He was 79 years old and lived in Chevy Chase, Md.
        He died of heart disease, a family spokesman said.
        Dr. Foley, a native of Danbury, Conn., earned bachelor's, master's and doctoral degrees from Harvard University and was an executive with the Justice Department and the Federal court system for 37 years. In World War II, he served in naval intelligence in Europe, rising to lieutenant commander.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Marguerite Pratt; a daughter, Anne, of Charlottesville, Va.; six sons, the Rev. William, of Olney, Md.; Christopher and Richard, both of Washington; Jonathan, of Baltimore; David, of Concord, N.H.; and Carl Procario-Foley, of Mount Vernon, N.Y.; and a grandson.

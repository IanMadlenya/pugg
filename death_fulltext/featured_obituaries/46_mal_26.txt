
        Ira A. McCown, a surgeon who served for 24 years as medical director of the New York State Athletic Commission, died of heart failure associated with Parkinson's disease on Tuesday. He was 92 years old and died at the Althea Woodland Nursing Home in Silver Spring, Md.
        Dr. McCown was one of the first black doctors to serve internship and residency at Harlem Hospital and was associated with the hospital for more than 50 years. He was appointed medical director of the state athletic commission by Gov. Thomas E. Dewey in 1951 and served until 1975. He retired from private practice in 1985.
        He is survived by his wife, Frances; his son, Ira A. McCown Jr. of New York, and a brother, James J. McCown of Columbus, Ohio.

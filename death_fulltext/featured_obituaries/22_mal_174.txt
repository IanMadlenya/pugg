        Benjamin Heffner, a lawyer and a former New York State assistant attorney general, died of heart failure Monday at Lenox Hill Hospital. He was 79 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        A graduate of New York University and Yale Law School, he served for nine years in the Attorney General's office before resigning in 1942 to go into private law practice in Manhattan.
        He is survived by his wife, Ruth; a son, John, of Alexandria, Va.; two daughters, Lenore Briskman of Bethesda, Md., and Rhoda Becker of Manhasset, L.I., and eight grandchildren.

        Henry Ciccarone, who coached the Johns Hopkins University lacrosse team to three consecutive national championships from 1978 through 1980, died of a heart attack Wednesday night at his home in Cockeysville, Md. He was 50 years old.
        Mr. Ciccarone coached the Blue Jays for nine seasons and his teams compiled a record of 105-16. He was also the head basketball coach at Johns Hopkins for six seasons. He left the university five years ago to go into business.
        Mr. Ciccarone is survived by his wife and four sons.

        Erik Hamrin, a public-relations representative who represented many well-known classical musicians, died of cardiac arrest on Sunday in Mount Sinai Hospital in Manhattan. He was 57 years old.
        Mr. Hamrin was born in Stockholm and educated at Uppsala University and the Sorbonne in Paris. He came to the United States in 1962 and worked nine years as associate producer of the Texaco-Metropolitan Opera radio broadcasts. He then became chief publicist for Peter Gravina Associates, representing such artists and ensembles as Benita Valente, Tatiana Troyanos, Steve Reich, Judith Blegen and the St. Louis Symphony.
        He is survived by his mother and two brothers in Sweden.

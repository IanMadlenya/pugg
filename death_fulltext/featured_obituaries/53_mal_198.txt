
        Robert O'Byrne, a theater producer and writer, died on June 18 at the U.C.L.A. Medical Center in Los Angeles. He was 74 years old and lived in Bel Air and Rancho Mirage, Calif.
        He died of a heart attack, said his wife, the former Gloria Monty.
        Mr. O'Byrne and his wife were the founding producers, in 1946, of the Abbe Practical Workshop, an experimental Off Broadway group that introduced new playwrights. He also produced and directed summer stock productions on Long Island.
        From 1966 to 1976 Mr. O'Byrne was an editor and writer for Sports Afield. He wrote two books in the 1970's, "Senior Golf" and "Golfer's Guide to Travel."
        Mrs. O'Byrne, the only survivor, is the executive producer of the daytime television series "General Hospital."

        James Vincent Ryan, a former assistant vice president of public relations for the American Telephone and Telegraph Company, died of congestive heart failure on Thursday at Overlook Hospital in Summit, N.J. He was 74 years old and lived in Summit.
        Mr. Ryan held the post of assistant vice president for A.T. & T. from 1957 to 1978, when he retired. He was responsible for the Bell System pavilions at World's Fairs at Seattle in 1962, New York in 1964 and at Montreal in 1967. He was also instrumental in the production of the Bell Telephone Hour, a popular show for many years.
        From 1950 to 1957, Mr. Ryan was a member of the public relations staff at the New Jersey Bell Telephone Company, where he supervised the production of a series of educational science films.
        He was born in Macon, Ga., and graduated from Southwest Missouri State Teachers College in 1937. He served in the Navy from 1944 to 1946, leaving with the rank of lieutenant.
        Mr. Ryan is survived by his wife, the former Anne Buhr; two sons, William, of Weston, Mass., and James, of Wayland, Mass.; two daughters, Nancy and Anne, both of Manhattan; two sisters, Marguerite Ryan of Dennis, Mass., and Mary McClernon of Springfield, Mo., and six grandchildren.


        Samuel E. Thorne, a retired legal historian and professor at the Harvard Law School, died on Thursday at the Brookhaven Nursing Center in Lexington, Mass. Mr. Thorne, who lived in Cambridge, Mass., was 87.
        The cause was a stroke, said a son, Samuel L. Thorne.
        Mr. Thorne joined the Yale Law School faculty in 1945, becoming a professor of legal history. He joined the faculty of Harvard Law School in 1956 and retired as a professor of legal history in 1978.
        Mr. Thorne was an authority on English legal history and common law dating to the 12th century, wrote extensively on the subject and edited many collections of articles and monographs.
        He graduated from City College of New York in 1927 and received a law degree from Harvard in 1932. During World War II he was a Navy cryptanalyst in the Pacific, attaining the rank of lieutenant commander.
        In addition to his son Samuel, of Concord, Mass., he is survived by his wife of 50 years, Margaret Thorne of Cambridge, and another son, Stuart, also of Cambridge.

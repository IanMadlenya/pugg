        David Carew Huntington, an author and professor of art history, died Saturday at his home in Ann Arbor, Mich. He was 67 years old and died of a heart attack, his son Caleb said.
        A graduate of Princeton University and Yale University with degrees in art history, Dr. Huntington was teaching at the University of Michigan, where he had been chairman of the art history department. Before joining the Michigan faculty in 1966, he was a professor at Smith College for 11 years. Dr. Huntington wrote several books on art history, including one on Frederic Edwin Church.
        In addition to his son, of Ann Arbor, he is survived by his wife, the former Gertrude Enders; a daughter, Abigail Mueller of Philadephia; another son, Daniel of Portland, Ore.; a brother, Thomas of Princeton; a sister, Alice Allen of Amherst, Mass., and four grandchildren.


       Dr. Edward M. Sewell, a Philadelphia pediatrician who specialized in tuberculosis in children, died last Wednesday at the University of Pennsylvania Hospital in Philadelphia. He was 67 years old and lived in Philadelphia. His family said he died of pneumonia resulting from lymphoma.
        His family said he died of pneumonia resulting from lymphoma.
        Dr. Sewell, who was on the staff of Children's Hospital of Philadelphia for three decades, was a professor of pediatrics at the Thomas Jefferson University Medical College. He was co-author with Edith Lincoln of ''Tuberculosis in Children,'' a standard textbook on the subject.
        Dr. Sewell is survived by his wife of 46 years, the former Jean Webb; his mother, Mary, of Philadelphia; two daughters, Vivian Sewell and Nancy Johnson, both of Philadelphia, and a son, Richard, of Manhattan.

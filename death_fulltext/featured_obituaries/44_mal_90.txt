        David Tufts, a New York shipping consultant, died on Thursday at a nursing home in Stamford, Conn. He was 75 years old and lived in Old Greenwich.
        Mr. Tufts was operations manager for the Manhattan-based shipping company of Garcia & Diaz, before becoming an independent consultant in 1970. He retired in 1985.  He was born in Medford, Mass., where his family had been influential in establishing Tufts College.
        He served in the Navy during World War II in the North Atlantic as commander of a destroyer escort and was twice awarded the Legion of Merit for capturing submarines.
        Mr. Tufts is survived by his wife, the former Florence Watters; two sons, Peter, of Rye, N.Y. and David Jr., Of Old Greenwich; two sisters, Molly Shultis of Hingham, Mass., and Jeanne Cassidy of Washington, and four grandchildren.

        Fred W. Carlson, who patented a 3-inch grocery store pie and was president of a baking company, died Dec. 10. He was 91 years old.
        He began his business in 1926 as Carlson Pies, which became the Paramount Baking Corporation of St. Paul in the early 1930's. His trademark ''nickel pies'' were sold throughout the Middle West, said his son, Kenneth.

        George B. Pidot, a lawyer and former village official on Long Island, died Saturday at North Shore University Hospital in Manhasset, L.I., after a long illness. He was 82 years old and lived in Hobe Sound, Fla., and during the summer in Locust Valley, L.I.
        A 1930 graduate of the University of Chicago Law School, Mr. Pidot was a lawyer with Consolidated Electric in Chicago and the Stone & Webster Service Corporation in New York before joining the Manhattan law firm of Shearman & Sterling in 1945. He was made a partner in 1948 and was a senior partner when he retired in 1977. In the 1970's, he served as mayor of the Village of Matinecock and as president of the Locust Valley Library.
        He is survived by three sons, George Jr., of Port Washington; Whitney, of Locust Valley, and Jeffrey, of Hallowell, Maine; a daughter, Diane, of Dover, N.J., and eight grandchildren.

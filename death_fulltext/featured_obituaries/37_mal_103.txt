        Willem C. Schilthuis, a grain industry executive and former president of the New York Produce Exchange, died on Saturday at his home in Westport, Conn. He was 95 years old.
        Mr. Schilthuis was with the Continental Grain Company for 38 years, serving as executive vice president from 1943 until he retired in 1960. He was president of the Produce Exchange from 1948 to 1950.
        He was born in Groningen, the Netherlands, and graduated from the University of Cologne. He came to the United States in 1915 as a representative of Schilthuis & Company, a family grain business.
        After World War II Mr. Schilthuis was a member of the Department of Agriculture's Advisory Committee for Grain Export. Later he was chairman of the North American Export Grain Association.
        He is survived by three daughters, Sally Johnson of Greenwich, Conn., and Carol Schilthuis and Pamela Rossbach, both of Manhattan; a sister, Maria Priesman-Schilthuis of Wageningen, the Netherlands; seven grandchildren, and 12 great-grandchildren.

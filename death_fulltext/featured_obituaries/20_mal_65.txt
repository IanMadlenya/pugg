        William L. Mitchell, a former vice president for design at the General Motors Corporation, died of heart failure Monday at William Beaumont Hospital in Royal Oak, Mich. He was 76 years old.
        Mr. Mitchell, who lived in Bloomfield Hills, Mich., retired in 1977 after 42 years with General Motors. He supervised the design, among other cars, of the Corvette Sting Ray, the Buick Riviera and the Cadillac Eldorado.
        He is survived by his wife, Marian Mitchell, of Bloomfield Hills; three daughters, Wendelin L. Campbell of San Diego; Lynne Werner of Carlsbad, Calif., and Penelope Mitchell of Bethesda, Md., and four grandchildren.

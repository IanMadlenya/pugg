        Ben Hill Brown Jr., a former Ambassador to Liberia and a former Deputy Assistant Secretary of State, died of cancer on Thursday at Georgetown University Hospital in Washington. He was 75 years old and lived in Alexandria, Va.
        A graduate of George Washington University Law School, Mr. Brown joined the State Department in 1946 after serving in the Army. He was a legal assistant until 1949, when he was named deputy assistant secretary for Congressional relations. He headed aid missions to Iraq and Liberia, was consul general in Liberia and Ambassador from 1964 to 1969. He retired in 1970.
        He is survived by his wife, Naomi; three daughters, Barbara Mitchell of Palo Alto, Calif., Hardee Klitzman of Durham, N.C., and Clara Hopkins of Weatherford, Tex.; two brothers, William of Spartanburg, S.C., and Hutson of Greensboro, N.C., and six grandchildren.

        Harmon L. Remmel, a retired partner in White Weld & Company Inc., an investment banking concern, died of leukemia Tuesday at the Memorial Sloan-Kettering Cancer Center. He was 78 years old and lived in Southampton, L.I., and Manhattan.
        Mr. Remmel worked at Chase Manhattan and Chemical Bank before becoming a partner of White Weld in 1950. He was also a consultant with the Merrill Lynch Corporate Finance Group.
        He was born in Little Rock, Ark., attended Princeton University and graduated from the University of Virginia. He served on several boards, including Walmart and West Florida Natural Gas. He was treasurer of the Southampton Nursing Home, assistant treasurer of Southampton Hospital and a director of the Manhattan Eye, Ear and Throat Hospital.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Jean Purrington; a daughter, Alexandra Swoope of Manhattan; a son, H. Lawrence, of Manhattan; two sisters, Gertrude Butler and Carrie Dickinson, and three brothers, Augustus, Pratt and Roland, all of Little Rock, and two grandsons.

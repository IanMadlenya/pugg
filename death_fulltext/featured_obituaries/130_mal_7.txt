
        James G. Gantsoudes, global head of the equity capital markets department at Morgan Stanley, Dean Witter, Discover & Company, died Oct. 17 at his home in Redding, Conn. He was 55.
        His family said the cause of death was cancer.
        Mr. Gantsoudes (pronounced GAN-sou-des) graduated in 1964 from the United States Military Academy at West Point, N.Y. He served as a captain in the Army in Vietnam for two years and received a Bronze Star for distinquished service.
        He received a Master of Business Adminstration degree from Harvard University in 1971.
        In the 1970's and 1980's, Mr. Gantsoudes worked as an executive for several Wall Street firms, including Morgan Stanley and Dillon, Read & Company. He rejoined Morgan Stanley in 1995.
        Mr. Gantsoudes is survived by his wife, Martha; three sons, Spencer, Nicholas and George; two daughters, Alexis and Sarah; his mother, Helen Gantsoudes of Danville, Va.; a sister, Betsy Robeson of Santa Fe, N.M., and a brother, Peter of Atlanta.

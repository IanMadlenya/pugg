        Dicron Aram Berberian, an abstract expressionist painter and a leader in international aid for Armenians, ia, died of cancer Monday cancer at St. Peter's Hospice in Albany. He was 46 years old and lived in Easton, Md.
        Mr. Berberian was a board member and former assistant director of the Central Committee of the Armenian General Benevolent Union, an international group for Armenian aid. In the Carter Administration, he was national program manager for the Refugee Resettlement Program, which dealt with relocating Southeast Asians.
        He was born in Beirut, Lebanon, and was a graduate of Columbia University and of the California School of Fine Arts, now the San Francisco Art Institute.
        He is survived by his parents, Dr. Dicron Abraham Berberian and Armine Berberian of Loudonville, N.Y.; a sister, Dr. Cynthia B. Hale of Katmandu, Nepal; a brother, Raffi R. Berberian of Boston, and two nephews.

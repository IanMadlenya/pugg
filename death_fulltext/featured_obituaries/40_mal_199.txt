        Charles J. Mason, a retired vice president of the Manufacturers Hanover Trust Company, died on Monday at his home in Stonington, Conn. He was 90 years old.
        Mr. Mason joined the Brooklyn Trust Company in 1939. He was an assistant vice president of the Brooklyn Trust Company when it merged with Manufacturers Hanover in 1950. He became an assistant vice president in 1953 and vice president in 1955, retiring in 1964.
        Mr. Mason was born in Orange, N.J. He graduated from the Groton School and Harvard University. He was a direct descendant of Capt. John Mason, the Pequot Indian fighter and first Lieutenant Governor of Connecticut.
        Surviving are Mr. Mason's wife, the former Katharine Post Palmer; two daughters, Patricia Scoll of Manhattan and Jane Flicker of Ridgewood, N.J., and two grandchildren.

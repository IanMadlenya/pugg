        Paul M. Bernstein, senior partner of the Manhattan law firm of Bernstein Litowitz Berger & Grossman, died on Wednesday at Lenox Hill Hospital in Manhattan. He was 60 years old and lived in Belmore, L.I.
        Mr. Bernstein had been suffering from leukemia for five years, a spokesman for the famiy said.
        As a lawyer, he specialized in class-action litigation involving securities. He was chairman of the executive committee of counsel for the plaintiffs in one of the nation's largest security suits - against the Washington Public Power Supply System. Settlements totaling $757 million have been approved.
        Mr. Bernstein wrote and lectured extensively. For 13 years, he was the author of a monthly column, ''Securities - Class Actions,'' in The New York Law Journal.
        He was a native New Yorker and graduated in 1953 from the Columbia Law School, where he was a Harlan Fiske Stone Scholar.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Mae Piccirilli; a daughter, Jane Shapiro, and a son, Richard, both of Coral Springs, Fla., and six grandchildren.

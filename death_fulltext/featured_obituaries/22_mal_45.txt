        Arthur E. Pettit, a former senior partner of the Manhattan law firm Winthrop, Stimson, Putnam & Roberts, died of heart disease Thursday at his home in Rumson, N.J. He was 91 years old.
        Mr. Pettit was a former director of the Singer Manufacturing Company and the General Aniline and Film Corporation, now known as GAF Corporation, and a former director and treasurer of the New York Law Institute. He was a native of Stuttgart, Ark., a graduate of Columbia College and the Columbia University Law School and a Navy aviator in World War I. His wife, the former Mary Mabel Long, died on Sept. 22. Surviving are two sons, Jeffrey and A. Gerald, both of Manhattan; a brother, Louis L., of Landrum, S.C., and two granddaughters.

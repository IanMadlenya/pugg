        Irvin Husin, a Manhattan lawyer and former president of the New York Federation of Reform Synagogues, an influential Jewish congregational group, died on Thursday at his home in Queens. He was 84 years old.
        Mr. Husin died of congestive heart failure, said his daughter, Margo Call of Manhattan.
        Mr. Husin was a founder and senior partner in general practice with the law firm of Husin, Miller & Levy, with which he was associated for 50 years. He was active with the New York Federation of Reform Synagoges for many years and was its president from 1965 to 1968.
        He was born in Philadelphia and graduated from New York Law School in Manhattan in 1927. At his death, he was a trustee of the law school.
        In addition to his daughter, Mr. Husin is survived by his wife, the former Bernice Spitalny; a second daughter, Carol Adler of Smithtown, L.I., and two grandchildren.

        F. George du Pont, a retired executive vice president of the Remington Arms Company, died Saturday at his home in Fairfield, Conn. He was 74 years old.
        Mr. du Pont joined Remington, a division in Bridgeport, Conn., of the E. I. du Pont de Nemours & Company, in 1938 and retired in 1970. He was an avid yachtsman, having skippered his own sailboat in four Newport-to-Bermuda races.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Beatrice Churchman; a daughter, Jean Foulke Blair of New Orleans; four sons, F. George Jr. of Rowayton, Conn., David Bayard of Bronxville, N.Y., Henry Geyelin of Block Island, R.I., and William Churchman of Tempe, Ariz., and four brothers, Stephen of Fairfield, Conn., Benjamin of Guilford, Conn., Jacques, of Conowingo, Md., and Alexis, of Wilmington, Del.

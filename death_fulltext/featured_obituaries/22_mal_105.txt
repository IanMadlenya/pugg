        Thomas G. Spates, a professor emeritus at Yale University's School of Engineering, and a former vice president of the General Foods Corporation, died of a lung tumor yesterday in Hamden, Conn., where he lived. He was 98 years old.
        Before joining Yale in 1950, he had worked for 14 years for General Foods. He retired from Yale in 1955. Mr. Spates was the author of ''Human Values Where People Work'' and ''Man and Management,'' both published by Harper & Row.
        Surviving are his wife, Lorraine; a daughter, Jean Samilson of Tarrytown, N.Y., three grandchildren and six great-grandchildren.

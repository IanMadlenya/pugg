        J. Roy Carroll Jr., a Philadelphia architect who was a former president of the American Institute of Architects, died on Tuesday at Crozer Hospital in Chester, Pa. He was 85 years old and lived in Swarthmore, Pa. He died after a short illness, his family said.  Among Mr. Carroll's best-known designs were two Philadelphia landmarks, Prentiss House of the Presbyterian Board of Pensions and the headquarters of the American Society for Testing and Materials. He also designed the Philadelphia Airport terminal buildings and the Lister Hill National Center for Biomedical Communications in Bethesda, Md.
        Mr. Carroll was born in Philadelphia and received bachelor's and master's degrees in architecture from the University of Pennsylvania. He was elected a Fellow of the American Institute of Architects in 1954 and served as its president in 1963 and 1964. He retired from practice in 1985.
        His first wife, the former Doris Packard, died in 1980. Surviving are his second wife, the former Ann Haggerty; three daughters, Spencer Dowd of Eugene, Ore., Mary Margaret Morrison of Swarthmore and Patricia Croach of Boston; three grandchildren, and one great-grandchild.

        Sander Simon, a former construction and manufacturing executive who also published books on sports figures, died Friday at his home in Sarasota, Fla. He was 84 years old.
        Mr. Simon was a building contractor on public works projects in New York in the 1930's, including the Lincoln and Queens Midtown Tunnels. Later, as head of Simon Adhesives, he manufactured pressure-sensitive labels. He also founded Simon & Flynn Communications, which published books and videotapes on Vince Lombardi, the football coach, and other sports figures.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Jeanne Stanley; a stepson, Jay Kirchheimer, of East Hampton, L.I.; a stepdaughter, Jane Mertens, of San Clemente, Calif., and one grandchild.

        Jerome M. Warshaw, a first vice president of Shearson Lehman Hutton Inc., died of a heart attack Monday outside his home in Brooklyn Heights. He was 60 years old.
        Before joining Shearson two years ago, Mr. Warshaw worked for Merrill Lynch Pierce Fenner & Smith Inc., where he was a vice president. Previously, he held a seat on the New York Stock Exchange. Born in Brooklyn, Mr. Warshaw graduated from Bethany College.
        Surviving are two sons, Stephen, of Cleveland, and Henry, of St. Louis; a daughter, Andrea Deitch of Scarsdale, N.Y.; his mother, Mae, of Miami Beach; two brothers, Stanley, of Lawrence, L.I., and Howard, of Roslyn Heights, L.I., and two grandchildren.

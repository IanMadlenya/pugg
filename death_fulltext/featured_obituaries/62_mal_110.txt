
        James C. Crumlish Jr., a former Pennsylvania state judge and district attorney of Philadelphia, died on March 17 at his home in Philadelphia. He was 72 years old.
        His family said he died after a brief illness.
        Mr. Crumlish retired two years ago as president judge of the Commonwealth Court in Philadelphia, an intermediate appellate court. He joined the bench after serving as district attorney from 1961 to 1965.
        Mr. Crumlish is survived by his wife, the former Rosemary McCarthy; two sons, James C. 3d and T. Sean, both of Philadelphia; three daughters, Rosemary Lord and Patricia Murphy of Philadelphia and Frances Keating of Haddenfield, N.J.; five brothers; a sister and 11 grandchildren.

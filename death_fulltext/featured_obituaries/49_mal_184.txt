
        Gary Mihalic, a landscape architect who was chief of design for the New York City Parks and Recreation Department, died on Feb. 13 at Cabrini Hospital. He was 40 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Mihalic died of complications after a splenectomy, a spokesman for the department said.
        He received a degree in ornamental horticulture from the State University at Farmingdale, L.I., and had bachelor's and master's degrees in landscape architecture from the University of Georgia.
        Mr. Mihalic, employed by the Parks Department since 1979, was appointed chief of design in 1989.
        He is survived by his mother, Wanda, of Flushing, Queens, and three brothers, Robert, of Plainville, Conn., John, of Bayside, Queens, and Dennis, of Glen Oaks, Queens. His companion was James DeMeaux.

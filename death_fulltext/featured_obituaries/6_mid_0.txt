Mr. Yeager was a World War II hero and a test pilot who became the first man to break the sound barrier.
        Susie M. Yeager, mother of the aviator Charles E. (Chuck) Yeager, died Sunday after a long illness. She was 89 years old. Mrs. Yeager died in Putnam General Hospital at Hurricane, officials at Koontz Funeral Home in Hamlin said today. Mr. Yeager was a World War II hero and a test pilot who became the first man to break the sound barrier. He now lives in California. Mrs. Yeager is also survived by another son, Hal Yeager Jr.; a daughter, Tansy Cummings; two brothers, two sisters, seven grandchildren and 16 great-grandchildren.

        James P. McDonald, a retired public relations executive, died on Dec. 27 at Englewood (N.J.) Hospital. He was 73 years old and lived in Tenafly, N.J.
        Mr. McDonald had been public relations director for the Maritime Association of New York, a Manhattan-based agency that promoted maritime and related commerce, from 1971 to 1982, when he retired. He was one of the organizers of Operation Sail, the international maritime salute to the United States Bicentennial in 1976.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Christine Pinkava, and two sisters, Kathleen Paterno of Rumson, N.J., and Mary French of Berkeley Heights, N.J.

        Capt. James O. Houghton, commanding officer of the Naval Aeorspace Medical Research Laboratory, has died in an automobile accident,the police said. He was 45 years old.
        Captain Houghton ws killed Thursday night when his car collided head-on with a speeding car that had veered into his lane, said Officer Venus North of the Florida Highway Patrol.
        Captain Houghton had commanded the laboratory at Pensacola Naval Air Station since June 1985. On July 1, he was to begin overseeing medical research and development at the Naval Medical Command in Washington.
        Born in Littlefield, Tex., he graduated from Union College in Nebraska and earned his medical degree at Loma Linda University in Caifornia. He was designated a naval flight surgeon in 1970.
        He served with the First and Third Marine Air Wings in Santa Ana, Calif. Da Nang, South Vietnam, and Futenma, Okinawa.
        Captain Houghton is survived by three brothers and three sisters.

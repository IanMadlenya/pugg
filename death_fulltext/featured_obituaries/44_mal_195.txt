        Dr. C. Douglas Hoyt, a dentist who was the first medical officer to go ashore in the invasion of Guadalcanal in World War II, died on Friday at Monmouth Medical Center in Long Branch, N.J. He was 74 years old and lived in Fair Haven, N.J.
        He died of a heart attack, his family said.
        Dr. Hoyt, an orthodontist, was the casualty evacuation officer with the First Marine Division when it spearheaded the invasion of Guadalcanal in August 1942. During the Korean War he served on the medical staff of the Brooklyn Navy Yard.
        He practiced in Fair Haven for 44 years before retiring this year.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Dicksie Medina; a son, Douglas, of Readington, N.J.; three daughters, Dorothy, of Rumson, N.J., Diane Karper of London and Dicksie Ann Callen of Concord, Mass.; a brother, Dr. Anson Hoyt of Fair Haven, and six grandchildren.

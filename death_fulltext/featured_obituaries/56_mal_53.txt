
        Ray Berry Burton Jr., a founding principal of the New York investment-management firm of Trevor Stewart Burton & Jacobsen Inc., died on Tuesday at Lawrence & Memorial Hospitals in New London, Conn. He was 52 years old and lived in Niantic, Conn., and Manhattan.
        He died of complications from liver disease, a company spokesman said.
        Mr. Burton, a native of Knoxville, Tenn., graduated from Vanderbilt University and from the Wharton School. He started his career in 1966 with a securities firm in Nashville and held positions at other firms in Greensboro, N.C., and New York before Trevor Stewart was founded in 1976. He resigned his positions of executive vice president and director in March for health reasons.
        Mr. Burton is survived by his wife, Karen; two sons, Ray 3d and David; his mother, Catherine R. Burton of Knoxville; and a sister, Betsy, of Chicago.

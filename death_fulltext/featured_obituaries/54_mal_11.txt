
        David Halper, a lawyer and leading bridge player, died on Saturday in New York Hospital. He was 78 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        He had suffered a stroke, said his daughter Vicki, of Seattle.
        Mr. Halper was chairman of the American Contract Bridge League's governors from 1959 to 1962, and president of the Greater New York Bridge Association in 1963.
        He graduated from Columbia University in 1931 and from its law school in 1934. A specialist in labor law, he headed the firm of Halper, Passick, Prince & Weinstein before practicing independently in Manhattan.
        Also surviving are his wife, Lee, another daughter, Judith Philpot of Knoxville, Tenn., and three grandchildren.

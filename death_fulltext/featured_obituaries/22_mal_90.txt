        Gerald F. Meltzer, the founder and chairman of the American Hepatitis Association, died Saturday of chronic hepatitis B at Cabrini Medical Center. He was 58 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Meltzer contracted the infectious disease in 1980 and took early retirement to establish the association. He had been a vice president at Cantor Fitzgerald Inc., the Manhattan investment banking company. The association raises research money and dispenses information about the disease.
        Survivors include a brother, Marvin, of Orlando, Fla., and a sister, Gertrude Ratner, of Boca Raton, Fla.

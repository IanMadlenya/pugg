        Leo Henry Rosenberg, a retired advertising executive who was one of radio's first announcers, died in his sleep Monday at a nursing home in Dunn Loring, Va. He was 92 years old.
        Mr. Rosenberg made broadcasting history in 1920, announcing the Harding-Cox Presidential election returns. In 1925 he left radio to join Lord & Thomas, a Chicago advertising agency that later became Foote, Cone & Belding. Mr. Rosenberg retired in 1961, but continued with the agency as a consultant until 1976.
        He is survived by a son, Warren, of Manhattan; a daughter, Judy Flander, of Vienna, Va.; three grandsons and a great-grandson.

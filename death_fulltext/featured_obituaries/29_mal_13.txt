        Donald K. Ballman, retired senior vice president of the Dow Chemical Company, Midland, Mich., died in his sleep Wednesday at his daughter's home in Hinsdale, Ill. He was 79 years old and lived in La Jolla, Calif.
        Mr. Ballman, a graduate of Indiana University, joined Dow in 1935. His specialty in his career with the company was marketing, including the introduction of Saran Wrap in 1953.
        In 1968 he became the company's first senior vice president. In the same year he was named president and chief executive of Dow's Ventures Investment and Finance Company, a division of Dow Chemical. He retired from the company in 1973.
        After moving to La Jolla, Mr. Ballman became trustee of the La Jolla Museum of Contemporary Art in 1974 and served as president from 1982 to 1984. In 1982 he became the first chairman of the Synbiotics Corporation, a bio-technological concern based in San Diego. He was chairman emeritus at his death.
        A widower, Mr. Ballman is survived by a daughter, Brenda Keyser of Hinsdale, and two granddaughters.

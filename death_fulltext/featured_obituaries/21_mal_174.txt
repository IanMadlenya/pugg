        Albert Asbury Edwards, a former educator and social worker, died last Saturday at his home in Manhattan. He was 92 years old.
        Mr. Edwards, who graduated from Atlanta University and Columbia University, was the director of the Bedford-Stuyvesant Community Center in Brooklyn for 15 years, and until 1985 was director of the St. Philips Community Center in Manhattan.
        He was a former teacher and administrator in the public schools in New Orleans and Gary, Ind. Mr. Edwards was a member of the board of the New York City Mission Society Cadet Corps and was on the advisory committee of the Fresh Air Fund.
        His wife, Ruby Harrison White Edwards, predeceased him.
        A memorial service will be held at St. Philip's Church in Harlem on Nov. 16.

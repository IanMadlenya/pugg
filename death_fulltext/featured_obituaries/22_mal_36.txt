        Man Bloom, a retired lawyer who specialized in workers' compensation, died of Alzheimer's disease Thursday at a nursing home in Williamsville, N.Y. He was 86 years old and had lived in Brooklyn until three years ago.
        Mr. Bloom was associated with the firm of Popish & Pasternack in Manhattan when he retired in 1978. For 30 years, he was workers' compensation lawyer for District 1199 of the National Union of Hospital and Health Care Employees.
        Surviving are two sons, Clifford, of Williamsville, and Stephen, of Ithaca, N.Y.; four grandchildren, and a great-grandson.

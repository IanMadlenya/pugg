        John George Murphy, a retired Army colonel and a former professor at Fordham University, died of a stroke Saturday at his home in Washington. He 97 years old.
        Colonel Murphy taught military science and tactics at Fordham from 1936 to 1940.
        A graduate of Manhattan College, he was commisioned in the Army in 1917 and served in World War I. He was a graduate of the Command and General Staff School in Fort Leavenworth, Kan.
        In World War II he served as chief logistics officer on the staff of the 9th Army in Europe and in China as part of a military advisory group. He was chief staff officer for logistics of the 2d Army, with headquarters at Fort Meade, Md., when he retired in 1953.
        Colonel Murphy's decorations include the Distinguished Service Medal, the Legion of Merit and the Bronze Star.
        His wife, the former Helene Barnes, died in 1985. He is survived by three daughters and a son: Marjorie Blanc of Kelseyville, Calif., Nancy Adams of Lewes, Del., and Patricia Carter and John George Jr., both of Washington; 15 grandchildren and 13 great-grandchildren.

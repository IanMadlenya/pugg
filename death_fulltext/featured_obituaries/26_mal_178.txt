        The Rev. Robert C. Dodds, a former official of the National Council of Churches who was also a psychologist and marriage counselor, died last Saturday at Central Vermont Hospital in Barre, Vt., after a long illness. He was 70 years old and lived in Montpelier, Vt.
        Mr. Dodds, a graduate of Columbia College and the Harvard Divinity School, served as director of planning and of ecumenical affairs for the National Council of Churches in the 1960's. He was a minister of the United Church of Christ and served congregations in Dennis, Mass., Waterbury, Conn., and Wantagh, L.I.
        He was a founding member of the Couples Institute of New England and was president of the Northeast Seminar for Transactional Analysis.
        He is survived by his wife, Elisabeth; a daughter, Margaret Bulow, of Adamant, Vt.; two sons, Paul, of Cambridge, Mass., and Andrew, of Seattle; a brother, Joseph, of San Diego; a sister, Alice, of Cambridge, and two grandchildren.


        Henry W. McGee Sr., the first black postmaster in Chicago, died on March 18 at his home in Chicago. He was 90.
        Mr. McGee became postmaster in 1966 and retired in 1973. He began his career at the post office as a temporary substitute clerk in 1929 and became a full-time clerk in 1935.
        Henry Wadsworth McGee Sr. was born on Feb. 7, 1910, in Hillsboro, Tex. He attended the Illinois Institute of Technology and received a master's degree in public administration from the University of Chicago.
        In the 1960's, he was president of the Chicago chapter of the National Association for the Advancement of Colored People. In the 1970's, he served on the Chicago Board of Education.
        Mr. McGee is survived by a son, Henry W. Jr., of Seattle; two daughters, Sylvia E. Morrison and Marguerite Porterfield, both of Chicago; eight grandchildren and six great-grandchildren.

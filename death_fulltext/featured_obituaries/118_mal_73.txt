
        Erwin Savelson, the founding editor of The Metropolitan Star, the magazine of District 1 of B'nai B'rith, and a longtime volunteer in  Jewish community affairs, died on Monday at the Sound Shore Medical Center in New Rochelle, N.Y. He was 89 and lived in Mount Vernon, N.Y.
        Mr. Savelson was long associated with the Anti-Defamation League of B'nai B'rith, and in particular with The Star, from the magazine's inception in 1945. He continued to work for the quarterly, which is published in Manhattan for B'nai B'rith's New York district, as a volunteer until 1985.
        He was born in Manhattan. His first career was as a reporter, for The Daily Mirror, The World Telegram & Sun and the World Journal Tribune, from which he retired in 1967, the year of its demise.
        He is survived by his wife of 64 years, Elinor Skaletsky Savelson; two sons, Robert, of Scarsdale, N.Y., and Donald, of Manhattan, and 11 grandchildren.

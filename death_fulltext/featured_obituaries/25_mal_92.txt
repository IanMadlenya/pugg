        Dr. Robert C. Swain, a research chemist and former president of Cyanamid International, died of a heart attack Sunday at his home in Riverside, Conn. He was 81 years old.
        Dr. Swain was vice president of research and development, executive vice president and president of Cyanamid International. He was a director of the corporation from 1946 until his retirement in 1972.
        He had been a drector of the Perkin-Elmer Corporation, an officer and a director of the Herbert Hoover Foundation in Manhattan and a member of the board of overseers of the Hoover Institution on War, Revolution and Peace in Stanford, Calif.
        Dr. Swain was awarded the Castner Memorial Medal in London in 1953 and the James Turner Morehead Medal in New York in 1958 for his contributions to chemistry.
        Dr. Swain was born in Palo Alto, Calif. He graduated from Stanford University. Dr. Swain attended the University of Heidelberg and the University of Berlin, from which he received a Ph.D. in 1932.
        Surviving are a daughter, Mary Cole of New Canaan, Conn.; a son, Robert, of Tulsa, Okla.; a sister, Dorothy Begien of Richmond; five grandchildren, and eight great-grandchildren.

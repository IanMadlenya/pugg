        Leif Andreas Larsen, a World War II resistance fighter and one of the most highly decorated naval heroes in Norway, died from a stroke on Friday, news reports here said. He was 84 years old.
        Mr. Larsen died at a hospital in Bergen. A sea captain, he crossed the North Sea 52 times in fishing boats to bring arms, supplies and Allied agents past Nazi warships into occupied Norway.
        Mr. Larsen's adventures have been recounted in numerous Norwegian films and books, and his 11 medals for bravery made him among the war's most decorated naval officers. He is survived by his wife and three daughters.

        Dr. Otto Brandman, an expert on diabetes and a former president of the New Jersey State Diabetic Association, died of pneumonia Wednesday at Bay Shore Community Hospital in Holmdel, N.J. He was 88 years old and lived in Locust, N.J. Previously, he lived in Maplewood.
        Dr. Brandman, a 1926 medicial-school graduate in Poland, had been chief of metabolic services at St. Michael's Medical Center in Newark, where he was on the staff for 44 years.
        He was an associate professor of medicine at the New Jersey College of Medicine, now known as the University of Medicine and Dentistry of New Jersey, and chief of staff at the old Newark City Hospital. He was also a consultant on metabolic diseases at Columbus Hospital in Newark.
        He is survived by two stepchildren, Susan Cohen of Manhattan and John Simon of Los Angeles.

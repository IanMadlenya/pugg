
        Marvin S. Gilman, a builder and developer for nearly 50 years in New York, Maryland and Delaware, died April 8 in the Christiana Hospital in Wilmington, Del., where he had undergone surgery for a staphylococcus infection. He was 76 years old and had been living in Wilmington.
        Mr. Gilman received undergraduate and law degrees from Brooklyn College, specialized in housing for low-  and moderate-income families. During the Carter Administration he was a director of the Federal National Mortgage Association.
        Mr. Gilman is survived by his wife, Muriel; a son, Peter, of Wynnewood, Pa.; a daughter, Martha, of Yorklyn, Del.; a brother, Bertrand, of Manhattan, and six grandsons.

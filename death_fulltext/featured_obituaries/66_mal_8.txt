
        Bernard J. Reilly, former president of both the Suffolk County and New York State Bar Associations, died on Tuesday at his home in Brightwaters, L.I. He was 68 years old.
        He died of congestive heart failure, a son, Matthew, said.
        He was a partner in the Babylon, L.I., law firm of Reilly, Like & Tenety & Ambrosino since its founding in 1955. Mr. Reilly, a real-estate and banking specialist, was president of the Suffolk County Bar Association in 1969 and president of the New York State Bar Association in 1982. He served in the American Bar Association's House of Delegates and as a fellow of the American College of Real Estate Lawyers.
        Mr. Reilly, who was born in Brooklyn, enlisted in the Army during World War II and received a Purple Heart and two battle stars during his service in both the European and Pacific theaters. After the war he graduated from Manhattan College and earned his law degree from Fordham Law School in 1950. He was a director of the Fordham Law School Alumni Association and in 1990 received its most distinguished alumnus award.
        He is survived by his wife, Lillian; three daughters, Kathleen Stahl of East Islip, L.I., Margaret Killen of Brightwaters and Jane Farnan of Arlington, Va.; two sons, Bernard Jr., of Bay Shore, L.I., and Matthew, of Brightwaters, and three grandchildren.

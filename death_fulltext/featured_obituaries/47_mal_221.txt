
        Edgar A. Harcourt, a lawyer who specialized in international finance and commodities, died on Thursday at Mount Sinai Hospital. He was 63 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        He died of a heart attack, a spokesman for his law firm said.
        Mr. Harcourt, a native of Paris, held bachelor's and law degrees from the University of Chicago.
        He was resident counsel of the New York branch of the French banking concern Societe Generale from 1962 to 1965 and was assistant general counsel to the Continental Grain Company from 1965 to 1968, when he became a partner in his Manhattan firm, Pavia & Harcourt.
        He was secretary of the French Institute/Alliance Francaise here.
        Mr. Harcourt is survived by his wife, the former Renee Barnoud; a daughter, Caroline, and a son, Bernard, both of Manhattan, and a sister, Alix Deguise of New London, Conn.

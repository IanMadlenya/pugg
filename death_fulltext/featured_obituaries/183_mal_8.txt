
        Horace A. Shepard, a former chief executive and chairman of TRW, died on March 5 at his home in Leary, Ga. He was 89.
        Mr. Shepard worked for TRW or a predecessor company for 26 years. TRW makes automotive, aerospace and military products. He joined Thompson Products, which made aircraft engine parts, in 1951. It merged with Ramo Wooldridge, a military contractor, in 1958 to form Thompson Ramo Wooldridge.
        Mr. Shepard was named vice president and general manager in 1961 and president a year later. He served as chief executive and chairman of TRW from 1969 through 1977, when he retired. During his tenure the company expanded its automotive and military divisions and developed aerospace and information systems products.
        After he received a bachelor's degree in aeronautical engineering from Auburn University in 1934, Mr. Shepard joined the Army Air Corps. In 1947, he was promoted to brigadier general in the Air Force. He retired in 1951 to join Thompson Products.
        Mr. Shepard is survived by three daughters, Lucy Milligan of Cincinnati, Katherine Boone of Wilmington, N.C., and Anne Nuckols of New Canaan, Conn.; eight grandchildren and seven great-grandchildren.

        Oscar J. Youngberg, a retired certified public accountant, died on Sunday at a nursing home in Huntington, L.I. He was 94 years old and lived in Dix Hills, L.I.
        Mr. Youngberg, who retired in 1979, was one of the last survivors of the Army's 69th Rainbow Division, whose members fought in France in World War I and were known as the ''Fighting Irish.''
        He was a graduate of Pace Institute, where he taught accounting from 1927 to 1932. He later became a partner in Ward, Weber & Company, a Manhattan-based public accounting firm, and then a vice president and treasurer of Waite, Bond & Company.
        He is survived by three daughters, Marie Blass of Dix Hills, Anne Dolan of Largo, Fla., and Joan Schwartz of East Northport, L.I.; two sons, Charles, of Madison, N.J., and Oscar Jr., of Smoke Rise, N.J.; 29 grandchildren, and 30 great-grandchildren.

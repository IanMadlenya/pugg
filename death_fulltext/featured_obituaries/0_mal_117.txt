        Howard J. Lichtenstein, a specialist in labor-relations law and a partner of the Manhattan law firm of Proskauer Rose Goetz & Mendelsohn, died Sunday at the Mount Sinai Medical Center. He was 80 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Lichtenstein joined the firm in 1945 and became a partner in 1949. He headed the labor-relations department, with a staff of 60 lawyers.
        From 1942 to 1944, Mr. Lichtenstein was assistant general counsel of the National Labor Relations Board, and from 1944 to 1945 was vice chairman of the New York Regional War Labor Board.
        He was born in Manhattan. Mr. Lichtenstein was an alumnus of New York University and its law school.
        Surviving are a daughter, Susan L. Hunsdon of Cincinnati; a son, Daniel, of Mount Kisco, N.Y., and two grandchildren.

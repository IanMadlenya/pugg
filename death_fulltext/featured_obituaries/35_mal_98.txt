        Pedro Toledo Ugarte, co-president of Spain's largest commercial bank, Banco Bilbao Vizcaya, died of a liver ailment on Tuesday at the Mayo Clinic in Rochester, Minn. He was 54 years old.
        Mr. Toledo, considered one of the most influential and innovative executives in the Spanish financial world, played a significant role in the 1988 merger of Banco Vizcaya, which he directed, with Banco de Bilbao, creating Spain's largest private bank, with control over the equivalent of more than $36 billion.

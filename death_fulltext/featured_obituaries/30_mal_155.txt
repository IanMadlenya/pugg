        Albert H. Corbin, an actor and puppeteer for more than 35 years, died of prostate cancer Thursday at Beth Israel Medical Center. He was 63 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Corbin, who has performed in numerous Off Broadway and regional theaters, made his Broadway acting debut in ''The Wayward Saint'' with Paul Lukas in 1955. On television he performed as the lion in the ''Omnibus'' production of ''Androcles and the Lion'' with Bert Lahr. He also worked with the Suzari Marionettes and the puppeteer Bil Baird.
        He is survived by his mother, Olive, of New Britain, Conn.

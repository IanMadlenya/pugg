
        John Hilder Loeb, former vice president of Brillo Manufacturing Corporation of New York, died on Wednesday at New York Hospital. He was 72 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mr. Loeb died of cancer, his son, David, said.
        He joined the Brillo Corporation, founded by his father, Milton B. Loeb, in 1913, in the late 1940's. He remained with it for 26 years as treasurer and executive vice president, and as a member of the board of directors of both Brillo British and Purex, which took over in 1964 as parent corporation, until 1982.
        In 1967 Mr. Loeb joined Grossman Publishers, which later merged with Viking Press and Penguin Books. At his death, he was manager of inventory control for Penguin USA.
        He was a member of UJA/Federation of Jewish Philanthropies, where he was a founding member of the management assistance program. He was a member of the board of directors of the United Way of New York and a member of the board of the National Assembly of Jewish Federations.
        His first wife, the former Diana Marks, died in 1987.
        He is survived by his wife, Virginia B. Slaughter; two children from his first marriage, a son, David Loeb of Berkeley, Calif.; and a daughter, Ginny Loeb of Cambridge, Mass; four stepsons, Bill Slaughter of Philadelphia, Pa., Tom Slaughter of Manhattan, Charles Slaughter of Fairfield, Conn., and Jamie Slaughter of Manhattan, and eight grandchildren.

        Dr. Eugene M. Kleban, an educator and former executive director of the Polish Institute of Arts and Sciences of America, died of a cerebral hemorrhage Oct. 9 in his Manhattan home. He was 78 years old.
        Born in Poland, Dr. Kleban was educated there in electrical engineering, political and military science and became an Army officer.
        He immigrated to this country in 1952 and worked as a design engineer. He received a master's degree and doctorate in sociology from New York University and taught sociology at N.Y.U., Brooklyn College and Wagner College. At Wagner, he headed the department of sociology and anthropology.
        From 1973 to 1976, Dr. Kleban was executive director of the Polish Institute of Arts and Sciences, a nonprofit educational and academic organization. He was the Polish representative to the Mayor's Ethnic Advisory Council from 1979 to 1984.
        He is survived by his wife, Jacqueline of Manhattan; and a daughter, Dr. Barbara Zwolinska; a sister, Maria Pazdzior, and a grandson, all of Poland.

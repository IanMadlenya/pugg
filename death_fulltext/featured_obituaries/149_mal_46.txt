
        Robert J. Lapham, who served as president of Conde Nast Publications from 1974 through 1986, died June 4  at a nursing home in Newport Beach, Calif. He was 77 years old and lived in Corona del Mar, Calif.
        He died of lung cancer, his wife, Edith, said.
        During his years as president of the company, Mr. Lapham introduced new magazines including Self and the West German edition of Vogue. He also served as the vice chairman of the Magazine Publishers Association and was chairman of the Publishers Information Bureau.
        Mr. Lapham joined the magazine publishing company in 1954 as the West Coast advertising manager of Vogue. He later became manager of the Conde Nast office in Los Angeles.
        He moved to the company's New York office in 1961 as advertising director and later became general manager. In 1969, he became a vice president of Conde Nast and was named president five years later.
        Mr. Lapham was born on Dec. 12, 1921, in Kansas City, Mo. He attended the University of Southern California and served in the Coast Guard during World War II. Before joining Conde Nast, he worked for Esquire and Seventeen magazines.
        Mr. Lapham is also survived by a son, Scott Lapham of Shoreview, Minn., and two grandchildren.

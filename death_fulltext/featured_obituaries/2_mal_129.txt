        Patrick George Troughton, a British actor who portrayed ''Doctor Who'' on television in the 1960's, died of a heart attack Saturday while in Columbus to appear at a science-fiction convention. He was 67 years old. Mr. Troughton's long acting career also included co-starring roles with Sir Laurence Olivier in the film version of ''Hamlet'' and with Gregory Peck in ''The Omen.''
        He was an attraction here as the second of seven actors who have portrayed ''Doctor Who'' in the long-running British television program, which has a cult following in the United States. He starred in the show from 1966-68.
        He was pronounced dead at the Medical Center in Columbus after having a heart attack in his hotel room.
        Mr. Troughton had recently been appearing in ''The Two of Us,'' a comedy on an independent London television station.

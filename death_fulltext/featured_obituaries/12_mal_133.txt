        Irston Roberts Barnes, an economist and naturalist, died after a short illness Tuesday at his home in Groton, Conn. He was 83 years old. Mr. Barnes was an assistant professor of economics at Yale University from 1932 to 1941 and a professor of political economy at the graduate school of business administration at Columbia University from 1960 to 1963.
        In between, he held a number of economic consultancy and Federal Government positions that included service with the Justice Department, the Civil Aeronautics Board and the Federal Trade Commission.
        Mr. Barnes wrote a weekly column titled ''The Naturalist'' for The Washington Post from 1951 to 1976 and was chairman of the Audubon Naturalist Society of the Central Atlantic States from 1962 to 1968.
        From 1963 to 1981, when he retired, he was an economic consultant to corporations and law firms in the United States and abroad.
        Mr. Barnes is survived by a son, Chaplin Bradford Barnes, of Stonington, Conn., and two granddaughters.


        Charles R. Speers, a former senior vice president of sales for American Airlines, died on Saturday at his home in Glen Cove, L.I. He was 83 years old and also lived in Naples, Fla.
        He died of cancer, a family spokesman said.
        In 36 years with American Airlines, Mr. Speers had also been the district traffic manager in Detroit, Chicago and New York, the general sales manager and vice president for industry affairs. In 1934 he pioneered the Universal Air Travel Plan, a charge account system.
        Surviving is his third wife, Barbara; a son, Christopher, of Wilton, Conn.; four stepsons, William Smyth of Boston, Whitney Smyth of South Salem, N.Y., John Mayo of Tallahassee, Fla., and Geoffrey Mayo of Manhattan; three stepdaughters, Theodora Page of Barrington, R.I., Deborah Rockwell of Huntington, L.I. and Linda Smyth-Kelly of Altamont, N.Y., and 16 grandchildren.

        Dr. Abner H. Rosenthal, a physician who practiced family medicine in Queens for nearly 50 years before joining Phoenix House, a drug-abuse services agency, as a staff physician, died of complications Tuesday following a stroke at his home in Flushing, Queens. He was 81 years old.  Dr. Rosenthal, a graduate of City College, received his medical degree from the University of Maryland in 1930. He began his general practice in 1931 with offices in Richmond Hill, Queens, and in Flushing. In World War II he was a captain in the Army medical corps.
        Dr. Rosenthal closed his practice in 1980 at the age of 74 to become a staff physician at Phoenix House, which operates drug-abuse treatment and prevention programs in the New York Metropolitan region. He retired from Phoenix House earlier this year.
        He is survived by his wife, Adele; a son, Dr. Mitchell S. Rosenthal of Manhattan; a daughter, Jane Rosenthal of Manhattan; a brother, Victor Rosenthal of Merrick, L.I.; a sister, Eleanor Cohen of Miami, and three grandchildren.

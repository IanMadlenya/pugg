
        Thomas Stewart Brush, a former newspaper executive and a patron of the arts, died on Thrusday at his home in Lakeville, Conn. He was 70 years old and also kept a residence in Manhattan and a summer home in the Bay of Islands, Ontario.
        He died of a heart attack in his sleep, his family said.
        A native of Salem, Ohio, Mr. Brush was the son of the author Katharine Brush. He attended the Loomis School and graduated from Yale University with B.A. and M.A. degrees.
        He was a grandson of one of three founders of the Brush-Moore newspaper chain, based in Canton, Ohio. In 1967, as a vice president of the company, he played a major role in the sale of its 12 dailies and 4 weeklies to Thomson Newspapers. The papers circulated in Ohio, California, Maryland, Pennsylvania and West Virginia.
        Mr. Brush was a managing director of the Metropolitan Opera Association from 1978 until his death. Beginning in 1970 he was a trustee of the Loomis Institute and was board chairman from 1980 to 1988. His other associations included Sharon (Conn.) Hospital, Harlem School of the Arts, Brooklyn Museum, Glyndebourne Association America and Yale Library Associates.
        Mr. Brush is survived by his wife, the former Katherine Ford; a daughter, Lucy L. Jay, of Manhattan, and a granddaughter.

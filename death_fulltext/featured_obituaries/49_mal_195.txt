
        Edward Pennell Brooks, founding dean of the Massachusetts Institute of Technology's Sloan School of Management, died Friday at his farm in Millboro Spring, Va. He was 95 years old.
        He died of a heart attack, a spokesman for the family said.
        Dean Brooks was a vice president and director of Sears, Roebuck & Company in Chicago in 1951 when he was chosen to head the new school of industrial management at M.I.T. He retired in 1960 as dean emeritus. From 1941 to 1946 he was a member of the M.I.T. Corporation, the school's governing body.
        He was born in Westbrook, Me., and graduated from M.I.T. In World War I he served as an Army officer in the American Expeditionary Force in France, and was awarded the Distinguished Service Cross for heroism in action. In World War II he was awarded the Medal of Freedom for service as a consultant.
        Dean Brooks was a former director of the American Management Association and a Fellow of the American Academy of Arts and Sciences.
        His wife, the former Carol Wright, died in 1971.
        He is survived by two daughters, Beverly Floe of Belmont, Mass., and Carol Pihl of Hinsdale, Ill.; a son, Robert Wright Brooks of Manakin Sabot, Va., seven grandchildren and nine great-grandchildren.

        Brig. Gen. Harry Disston of the New York National Guard, a retired executive of the American Telephone and Telegraph Company in Manhattan, died Tuesday at his home in Keswick, Va. He was 91 years old.
        General Disston was a member of the National Guard for 39 years and served as an officer in both World Wars. He had been executive commander of the 107th Infantry, formerly the Seventh Regiment of New York, in Manhattan and had earned scores of medals and commendations. He was an executive with A.T.&T. for 42 years.
        He was born in Red Bank, N.J., and graduated from Amherst College, Phi Beta Kappa, in 1921.
        He is survived by his wife, Catherine; a son, Robin J., of Greenwich, Conn.; a sister, Charlotte D. Gambel of Clearwater, Fla.; six grandchildren, and eight great-grandchildren.

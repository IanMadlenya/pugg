        Myron L. Chase, president of the Witte Chase Corporation and past president of the Institute of Scrap Iron and Steel, died of a heart attack Friday en route to St. Clare's Hospital in Manhattan. He was 68 years old and lived in Mamaroneck, N.Y.
        Mr. Chase was born in Boston and was a graduate of Harvard College, where he majored in economics and played varsity football. He founded the Witte Chase Corporation, a leading exporter of steel scrap, in 1967.
        He is survived by his wife, Sylvia; a daughter, Carol Oppenheimer of Santa Fe, N.M.; a son, Stephen, of Scarsdale, N.Y.; a sister, Doris Levi of Boston, and five grandchildren.

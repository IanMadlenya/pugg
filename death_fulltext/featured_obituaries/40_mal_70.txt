        Robert Watchorn Drummond, a Philadelphia investment banker, died of cancer on Friday at his vacation home in West Palm Beach, Fla. He was 82 years old.
        Mr. Drummond joined the brokerage firm of E. H. Rollins & Son after graduating from Wesleyan University in 1929. He later joined the Francis I. DuPont Securities Brokerage Company, and retired in 1970. He subsequently served on several corporate boards, including those of Connelly Containers Inc. and Crown Cork & Seal Inc. He was president of Kenelm Inc., an investment firm he founded in 1961.
        Mr. Drummond, who lived in Stone Harbor, N.J., is survived by his wife, Anne; a son, Robert Jr., of Audubon, Pa.; two daughters, Beverly Mowbray of Phoenix and Bonnie Sargent of Gladwyne, Pa., two brothers and six grandchildren.

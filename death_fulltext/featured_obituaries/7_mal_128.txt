        Richard M. Montgomery, a retired lieutenant general of the Air Force who was a chief of staff of the Strategic Air Command in the 1950's, died at the Heritage Park Skilled Care Center in Bradenton, Fla., Thursday, of complications after neurosurgery. He was 75 years old.
        General Montgomery served as executive vice president of the Freedoms Foundation of Valley Forge, Pa., an educational organization, for a year after his retirement from the Air Force in 1966. He continued to serve on the foundation's board of directors until his death.
        He was born in Hollidsaysburg, Pa., reared in nearby Altoona, and was graduated from the United States Military Academy at West Point in 1933.
        He served as chief of staff of the Air Force's Strategic Air Command under Gen. Curtis E. LeMay from 1952 to 1956. From 1962 until his retirement he was vice commander in chief of the United States Air Forces in Europe.
        The general, who lived in Longboat Key, Fla., is survived by two sons, Richard M. Jr. of San Francisco, and Thomas C. of Fort Myers, Fla.; a daughter, Nancy Hunter, of Rio, Ill.; a brother, Charles W. Jr. of Bowling Green, Ohio; four grandchildren, and six great-grandchildren.

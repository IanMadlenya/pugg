
        Jack G. Thayer, chief operating officer and executive vice president of Gear Broadcasting International and former president of NBC radio, died on Jan. 1 at St. Joseph's Hospital in Providence, R.I. He was 72 and lived in Providence and Manhattan.
        He died after a brief illness, said Ed Robalisky, president of Gear Broadcasting, a subsidiary of Gear Telecommunications, a wireless cable operator in New England.
        Mr. Thayer was born in Chicago and started his career as a disk jockey in Minneapolis. He managed stations in Minneapolis, Cleveland and Sacramento, Calif.
        In 1974, Mr. Thayer was named president of NBC Radio and its network stations. He became general manager of WNEW-AM in 1980, when it was the flagship station of Metromedia in New York. He joined Gear Broadcasting in the mid-1980's.
        He is survived by two sons, Tim, of Bakersfield, Calif., and Terry, of Palm Springs, Calif.; a daughter, Tracie Ireland of Sylman City, Calif., and five grandchildren.

        Stephen A. McCarthy, a research librarian who was former director of university libraries at Cornell University, died of cancer Sunday at Anne Arundel Medical Center in Maryland. He was 81 years old and lived in Annapolis, Md.
        Dr. McCarthy was director at Cornell from 1946 to 1967, a period in which he created a unified library system, directed an extensive reclassification project and oversaw the planning, construction and development of collections for the John M. Olin Research Library.
        In 1967 Dr. McCarthy left Cornell to become executive director of the Association of Research Libraries in Washington.
        In 1953 he was a Fulbright lecturer and consultant in Egypt, and in 1967 was a Fulbright lecturer at the University of London. He retired in 1975.
        He received bachelor's and master's degrees from Gonzaga University, a degree in library science from McGill University and a Ph.D. from the University of Chicago.
        Dr. McCarthy is survived by a son, Stephen G. of Pacific Palisades, Calif.; two daughters, Louise Mehta of Baltimore and Kathleen M. of Manhattan, and three grandchildren. Two sisters, Mary Alice McCarthy of St. Paul and Esther Koetting of Milwaukee, also survive.

        Peniamin Noorigian, an Armenian-American translator and writer, died Feb. 6 after a long illness in Hollywood. He was 93 years old.
        Mr. Noorigian, who was born in Armenia, came to the United States in 1913. He translated Mark Twain's tale ''The Prince and the Pauper'' into Armenian and, for 15 years, edited an Armenian literary journal in the New York area. In addition to his many translations, he wrote short stories based on Armenian village life that were published in several anthologies.
        Mr. Noorigian is survived by four sons, all of New Jersey: Lazarus, of Spring Lake Heights, George, of Wall Township, Jack, of Spring Lake, and Harry, of Cresskill, and by eight grandchildren and 13 great-grandchildren.

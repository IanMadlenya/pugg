        Peter R. Knauss, an expert on Africa who was an associate professor of political science at the University of Illinois at Chicago, died of AIDS on Sunday at St. Francis Hospital in Evanston, Ill. He was 52 years old and lived in Evanston. Dr. Knauss's area of expertise was African politics. He joined the faculty in 1968; in 1988 he was promoted to associate professor.
        He was the author of several books, among them ''The Persistence of Patriarchy: Class, Gender and Ideology in Twentieth Century Algeria'' (Prager Publishing; 1987).
        He graduated from Rutgers University in 1959 and received an M.A. from the Fletcher School of Law and Diplomacy, Tufts University, in 1961 and a Ph.D. from Northwestern University in 1968. He studied and taught in Africa for four years on a Fulbright Fellowship, one of two he received.
        Dr. Knauss is survived by his father and stepmother, Richard and Mary Jane Knauss of Allentown, Pa.; a son, Orlando, of Manhattan, and a daughter, Olivia, of Boulder, Colo.

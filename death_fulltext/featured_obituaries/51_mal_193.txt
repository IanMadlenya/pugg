
        Herman Miles Somers, professor emeritus of politics and public affairs at Princeton University, died Tuesday at his home in Pennswood Village in Newton, Pa. He was 80 years old.
        He died of complications of a stroke, said a spokesman for Princeton.
        Dr. Somers retired in 1979 from the Woodrow Wilson School of Politics and International affairs at Princeton. He had held a joint appointment in that school and in the university's department of politics since 1963.
        From 1948 to 1963, he was a professor and chairman of the department of political science at Haverford College. Before World War II he was a senior economist in Washington with the National Resources Planning Board, then served as a lieutenant colonel in the Army and returned to Washington in 1946 to work at the Office of War Mobilization and Reconversion.  An Adviser to Presidents
        From 1934 to 1939, he was a statistician for the State Public Welfare Department in Wisconsin. He was also a visiting professor at universities in New York, California, Massachusetts and Wisconsin.
        Dr. Somers, who was born in Brooklyn on April 11, 1911, studied at the University of Wisconsin before earning master's and doctoral degrees at Harvard University.
        He was a consultant to numerous government and state agencies, foundations and private organizations. He was a counselor to the International Labor Organization and a member of advisory councils on Social Security and health insurance that developed recommendations for Presidents Dwight D. Eisenhower, John F. Kennedy and Lyndon B. Johnson.
        He was the author or co-author of several books, including "Presidential Agency," a study of economic mobilization during World War II, published in 1950 by the Harvard University Press.
        He is survived by his wife, Anne, and two daughters, Sara, of Oakland, Calif., and Margaret, of Princeton, N.J.


        Robert Ridgely, a film and television actor, died on Feb. 8 at his home in Toluca Lake, Calif. He was 65.
        The cause was cancer, his wife, Patricia, said.
        Mr. Ridgely was a familiar face in Jonathan Demme and Mel Brooks films. He was a game show host in Mr. Demme's ''Melvin and Howard'' and a homophobic lawyer in ''Philadelphia.'' He played Boris the Hangman in Mr. Brooks's ''Blazing Saddles'' and did a reprise in ''Robin Hood: Men in Tights.''
        His other film credits include ''Beverly Hills Cop II,'' in which he played the mayor; ''The Ref,'' and ''Fire Down Below.''
        Mr. Ridgely, a native of Teaneck, N.J., began as a cabaret entertainer. He appeared in television shows like ''Get Smart,'' ''Sea Hunt'' and ''Coach.'' He did voice-over work in many television commercials and cartoon shows.
        In addition to his wife, he is survived by a brother, Dr. William Ritterbusch of Germany.

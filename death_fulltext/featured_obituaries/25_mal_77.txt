        Robert J. Lynch, a retired executive in the foreign trade industry, died of pneumonia Thursday at Palm Beach Gardens Hospital in North Palm Beach, Fla. He was 87 years old and lived in Bronxville, N.Y.
        Mr. Lynch founded American and Foreign Enterprises Inc. in 1947 and served as its president until he retired in 1975. Previously, he served as an executive assistant in the State Department and the Lend-Lease Administration.
        Mr. Lynch was born in Manhattan and graduated from City College.
        Surviving are his wife, Mary Louise; seven sons, Robert Jr. and Richard, both of Manhattan, Ronald and Roger, both of Greenwich, Conn., Ralph, of Glenview, Ill., Russell, of Scarsdale, N.Y., and Raymond, of North Palm Beach; two daughters, Rosemary Whitmer of Greenwich and Rita Ann Gow of Rochester; a sister, Winifred Lynch of Pawling, N.Y.; 24 grandchildren, and four great-grandchildren.

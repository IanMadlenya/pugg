
        Felix J. Colangelo, the retired president of the Wool Bureau in Manhattan, died on Jan. 29 at his home in Jericho, L.I. He was 72.
        The cause was cardiac arrest, said a daughter, Emily.
        Mr. Colangelo spent 27 years with the Wool Bureau, a wool promotion and technical services branch of the International Wool Secretariat. In 1962 he became head of the American branch of the global organization, retiring in 1980.
        Mr. Colangelo was a graduate of City College of New York. He served in the Army during World War II.
        He is survived by his wife of 46 years, Frances; one son, Philip, of Reston, Va., and two daughters, Emily of Arlington, Va., and Alison of McLean, Va.

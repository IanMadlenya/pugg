        Paul E. O'Neil, a writer and reporter with Time Inc. for more than 40 years, died Sunday at the United Hospital of Port Chester, N.Y., after complications arose from surgery for the removal of a brain tumor. He was 79 years old.
        Mr. O'Neil, a native of the Pacific Northwest, was a reporter for three Seattle newspapers, as well as a short story writer for the Saturday Evening Post and Collier's. In the 1930's he became Time's first stringer from Seattle, moving with his family to New York in 1944 to become a staff writer for Time magazine. He joined Sports Illustrated at its inception, in 1954, and became a staff writer for Life in 1957. He also wrote several volumes of Time-Life Books.
        He is survived by his wife, Jane Walker O'Neil of Rye, N.Y.; a son, J. Michael O'Neil of Vernon, Conn., and a daughter, Sally C. Oberbeck of Fairborn, Ohio.

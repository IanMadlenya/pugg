        James T. Moock, a retired first international vice president of the International Longshoremen's Association, died of complications resulting from pnuemonia Saturday at the Graduate Hospital in Philadelphia. He was 79 years old and lived in West Deptford Township, N.J.
        Mr. Moock, a native of Philadelphia, went to work as a longshoreman in 1925, joining the I.L.A. Deepsea Local 1116, which later became Local 1291. In 1945 he was elected the Philadelphia local's assistant financial secretary. He became I.L.A. Atlantic Coast district vice president in 1951 and in 1955 was elected 26th international vice president. He became first vice president in 1975 and retired in 1985.
        Mr. Moock is survived by his wife, the former Marie Gannon; a son, James Jr. of Yardley, Pa.; three daughters, Loretta Sweeney of Bellmawr, N.J., Regina Cunningham of Mt. Laurel, N.J., Patricia Minges of Horsham, Pa.; and 12 grandchildren.
        A funeral mass will be celebrated at 8 P.M. Wednesday at Saint Matthew's Roman Catholic Church, at Third Street and Wesley Avenue, National Park, N.J.

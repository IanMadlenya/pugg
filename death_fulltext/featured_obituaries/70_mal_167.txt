
        Robert D. Huntoon, a former official of the National Bureau of Standards, now the National Institute of Standards and Technology, died Saturday at Prince George's Hospital Center in Cheverly, Md. He was 83 years old and lived in Mitchellville, Md.
        Mr. Huntoon died of congestive heart failure, a stepdaughter, Harriett Underwood said.
        Mr. Huntoon joined the bureau in 1941 and served during World War II as a consultant to Secretary of War Henry L. Stimson, participating in the research that significantly improved the accuracy and dependability of military weapons.
        Mr. Huntoon later held several posts at the agency, including director of the bureau's regional laboratories in Corona, Calif., acting director of its laboratories in Boulder, Colo., associate director for physics and deputy director for the technical program. He retired from the Institute in 1969 and became a consultant to Congress on the metric system.
        Mr. Huntoon's first wife, Olive Sinclair Huntoon, died in 1968.
        He is survived by his wife, Eleanor Alexander Huntoon; two daughters, Dorothy H. McCoy of Lewisburg, Pa., and Rebecca H. Chao of Portland, Ore.; a stepson, Michael N. Alexander of Lexington, Mass; two stepdaughters, Harriett A. Underwood of Upper Marlboro and Diane A. Meyer of Alexandria; seven grandchildren, and five great-grandchildren.

        Dr. John F. Marchand, a physician, educator and researcher, died on Sunday at New York Hospital-Cornell Medical Center in Manhattan. He was 78 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        A spokesman for the family said that he died of cancer.
        Dr. Marchand graduated from Yale University Medical School in 1940. After his internship and residency in hospitals in Philadelphia and Boston, he was appointed an instructor at Cornell Medical College. He was also a clinical attending physician at Bellevue Hospital and at New York Hospital.
        He was noted for his research into the influence of potassium in cardiac arrest. He also helped to control an outbreak of meningitis among Indians in the Yukon Territory in Canada during his service as medical officer with the United States Public Roads Administration on the Alaskan Highway. In 1953, he was director of the University of Lousiville Respirator Center and helped organize field tests of the Salk polio vaccine in eight Southern states. He was a family physician in Manhattan for many years.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Keiko Yahagi, and a brother, Eric, of Rochester.

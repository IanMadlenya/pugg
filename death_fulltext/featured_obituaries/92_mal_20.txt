
        The Most Rev. Bill Burnett, who headed the Anglican Church in southern Africa from 1974 to 1981, died on Aug. 23 in Grahamstown in the Cape Province of South Africa. He was 77 and lived in Grahamstown.
        From 1974 to 1981, he was concurrently Archbishop of Cape Town, South Africa.
        Archbishop Burnett, who grew up speaking Afrikaans as well as English, called racial segregation morally indefensible. In 1977, speaking as head of his church in South Africa, South-West Africa, Lesotho, Swaziland and Mozambique, he reaffirmed opposition to racial segregation.
        He is survived by his wife of 49 years, the former Sheila Fulton Trollip, two sons and a daughter.

        John Collett Goodbody, retired director of communications for the Episcopal Church, died on Sunday at Roper Hospital in Charleston, S.C. He was 75 years old and lived on Seabrook Island, S.C.
        Mr. Goodbody died of congestive heart failure after a stroke, his wife said.
        Mr. Goodbody was director of communications of the Episcopal Church, which has its headquarters on the East Side of Manhattan, from 1975 to 1980, when he retired. He was president of The Seabury Press in Manhattan from 1961 to 1971. From 1946 to 1961 he was with the Colonial Willamsburg restoration.
        In 1980, he and his wife, the former Harriet Linen, retired to Seabrook Island where they were co-editors of the Jubilate Deo, a monthly diocesan newspaper.
        Mr. Goodbody was a graduate of Williams College. In World War II he was in naval intelligence in the Pacific, rising to lieutenant commander.
        Besides his wife, Mr. Goodbody is survived by two daughters, Margretta Niles of Darien, Conn., and Joan Goodbody of Seabrook Island; a son, David L., of Jackson, Wyo.; a brother, Maurice F., of Hope, N.Y.; a sister, Margaret McNeil of Kennett Square, Pa., and two grandchildren.

        Fred Cramer, a former United States and international chess official who was Bobby Fischer's manager in a 1972 match with Boris Spassky of the Soviet Union, died of cancer Tuesday. He was 76 years old and lived in Mequon.
        Mr. Cramer was president of the United States Chess Federation from 1960 to 1963. He later served as vice president of the International Chess Federation.
        He is survived by his wife, Ramona; a daughter, Jennifer, of Mequon; two sons, Fred Jr., of Mequon, and Eric, of San Diego, and a sister, Catherine C. Engle of Oro Valley, Ariz.

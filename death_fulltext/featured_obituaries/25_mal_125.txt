        Edward Zang, an actor who appeared in many Off Broadway and regional-theater productions, was found dead on Tuesday in his Manhattan apartment. He was 54 years old.
        Mr. Zang died of a massive hemorrhage, according to his sister, Marie Forhan Woolley of Oxted, England.
        After attending City College and Boston University, Mr. Zang helped found the Charles Street Theater in Boston and worked with companies like Playhouse in the Park in Cincinnati and McCarter Theater in Princeton, N.J. He also acted in ''Amadeus'' and ''St. Joan'' on Broadway. His Off Broadway credits included ''The Boys in the Band,'' ''Early Morning,'' ''Polly,'' ''The New York Idea'' and ''The Banana Box.''
        Mr. Zang also taught acting and directing at New York University, and acted in many Shakespeare plays produced by the Great Lakes Shakespeare Festival, the Guthrie Theater in Minneapolis and the New York Shakespeare Festival.
        He is survived by his sister.

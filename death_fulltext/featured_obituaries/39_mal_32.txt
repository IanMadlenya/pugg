        Richard Aaron Levin, a former Federal prosecutor and an investment fund manager, died of stomach cancer on Tuesday at the Mount Sinai Medical Center in Manhattan. He was 57 years old and lived in South Orange, N.J.
        Mr. Levin practiced law for 24 years before he changed careers in 1983 and founded Orion Inc., an investment concern specializing in futures funds.
        He was an assistant United States attorney in New Jersey from 1961 to 1965 and was a lead prosecutor in the trial of the organized crime figure Anthony (Tony Pro) Provenzano. Mr. Provenzano was convicted of racketeering.
        In 1972, as a special deputy state attorney general in New Jersey, Mr. Levin helped convict a former New Jersey Secretary of State, Paul J. Sherwin, of bid-rigging and kickback charges.
        A 1959 graduate of Harvard Law School, Mr. Levin had been in private practice with Amster & Levin in Millburn, N.J. He had also been chairman of Odyssey House, the drug rehabiliation organization.
        He is survived by a sister, Barbara Amster of Florham Park, N.J.

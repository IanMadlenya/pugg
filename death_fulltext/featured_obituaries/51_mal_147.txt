
        Sharon Clay Risk, a lawyer who was a former associate director of corporate finance for the Federal Communications Commission, died Wednesday at Bellevue Hospital Center in Manhattan. He was 71 years old and lived in Chevy Chase, Md.
        He suffered a heart attack in Manhattan while planning the 50th reunion of Princeton University's class of 1943 and died of cardiac arrest, a spokesman for the family said.
        Most recently Mr. Risk was with Johnston, Lemon & Company, a Washington law firm he joined in 1967. He retired in 1989. Mr. Risk was a graduate of Yale Law School and was with the Manhattan firm of Shearman & Sterling before joining the F. C. C. in 1957. He was a lieutenant in the Army Air Force in World War II.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Louise G. Brown; twin daughters, Nancy Rollings of Tucson, Ariz,, and Barbara de Boinville of Chevy Chase, Md; a son, Clay of Boston, and five grandchildren.

        Wolfgang W. Zuelzer, former director of the division of blood diseases and resources at the National Heart, Lung and Blood Institute of the National Institutes of Health, died today of acute leukemia. He was 77 years old.
        Dr. Zuelzer, who completed his medical studies at the University of Prague in 1935 after fleeing his native Germany, served for many years at the Child Research Center and Children's Hospital in Detroit. He joined the National Institutes of Health in 1975, and retired in 1980.
        He was the first doctor to treat children's leukemia with chemotherapy. His work in hematology and pediatrics earned him the Howland Award from the Pediatric Research Society in 1985.
        Dr. Zuelzer is survived by his wife, Ruby, two daughters from a previous marriage and three grandchildren.

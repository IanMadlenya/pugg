        Adrian S. Lamb, an artist whose portraits hang in the White House, the Smithsonian Institution, Harvard Law School, the Pentagon and the United States Naval Academy, died of a heart attack in Norwalk (Conn.) Hospital on Saturday. He was 87 years old and had homes in New Canaan, Conn., and Chappaquiddick, Mass.
        Mr. Lamb studied at the Art Students League, Cooper Union and, in Paris, at the Julien Academy. His portrait of Daniel Webster hangs in the United States Senate's Hall of Fame.
        He is survived by his wife, Mabel; a son, Dana; a daughter, Frances, and three grandchildren.

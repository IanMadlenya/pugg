        Joe Collins, a first baseman for 10 years with the New York Yankees, died today at his home here. He was 66 years old.
        He played for the Yankees from 1948 to 1957 and was a member of six World Series champion teams.
        Mr. Collins, who was born in Scranton, Pa., had a career batting average of .256 with 86 home runs and 404 runs batted in. His best season was in 1952 when he hit 18 homers with 59 runs batted in. He also played the outfield.
        Mr. Collins is survived by his wife and a son, Joe Jr.

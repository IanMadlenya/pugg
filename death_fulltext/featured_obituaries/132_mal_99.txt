
        Cameron Clark, a partner in the Manhattan-based law firm of Paul, Weiss, Rifkind, Wharton & Garrison for more than two decades, died on Jan. 15 at the Mount Sinai Medical Center. He was 53 and had homes on the Upper East Side and in Tuxedo Park, N.Y., and Seal Harbor, Maine.
        The cause was a ruptured aorta, said his father, Blair Clark.
        Cameron Clark, a litigator, handled a variety of cases, many of them in the intellectual property, antitrust and environmental areas. He played a leading role in Paul Weiss's pro bono practice and he served on a task force of the Association of the Bar of the City of New York formed to increase the number of minority associates at New York firms.
        A Boston native, he graduated from Harvard in 1966 and from the University of Minnesota Law School in 1969.
        Besides his father, he is survived by his wife, Sheila; a daughter, Eliza Clark of Cambridge, Mass.; a son, Simon, of Manhattan, and two brothers, Timothy, of Bethesda, Md., and Ian, of Los Angeles.

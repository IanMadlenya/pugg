        The Rev. Chester L. Marcus, a retired regional secretary of the United Church Board for World Ministries, died of cardiac arrest Wednesday at his home in Teaneck, N.J. He was 72 years old.
        Dr. Marcus worked for the church organization's overseas mission for 20 years, serving as the regional secretary for Africa before retiring in 1982. Previously he had taught school in Ho, Ghana, and had been the administrator of the Evangelical Hospital in Adidome, Ghana.
        Mr. Marcus joned the United Church Board in 1962. He had been secretary for racial and cultural relations for the Evangelical and Reformed Church and for the United Church of Christ from 1954 to 1962.
        Born in Swiftown, Miss., Mr. Marcus graduated from Alcorn Agricultural and Mechanical College. He was ordained by the Philadelphia Presbytery of the Presbyterian Church. From 1943 to 1954 he was pastor of the Washington Street Presbyterian Church in Reading, Pa.
        Surviving are his wife, Dorothy; a daughter, Kathryn, and a son, Chester Jr., both of Teaneck; two sisters, Priscilla Jefferson of Reading, and Rebecca Cox of Cleveland, Miss., and a brother, Kenneth, of Cleveland.

        Prof. John F. Middlemiss, a lawyer in Brooklyn and a retired law professor, died on Thursday at the home of a daughter, Nancy Palmer, in Fairfield, Conn., where he celebrated his 91st birthday on Sunday.
        Professor Middlemiss died of a heart attack, his grandson, Warren Palmer, said.
        Professor Middlemiss, a former law associate of Gov. Hugh L. Carey, had law offices on Montague Street in Brooklyn Heights for more than 40 years. He also taught business law to two generations of students at St. John's University.
        Mr. Middlemiss was born in Brooklyn. He graduated from St. Francis Xavier College and the Fordham University Law School.
        Also surviving are another daughter, Martha McCormack of Brooklyn; 2 sons, John Jr., of Blue Point, L.I., and Thomas, of Queens; 15 grandchildren, and 11 great-grandchildren.

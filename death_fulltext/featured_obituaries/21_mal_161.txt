        Abraham Schlesinger, a retired labor lawyer, died of lung cancer yesterday at his home in Wilton, Conn. He was 82 years old.
        A graduate of New York Law School, Mr. Schlesinger practiced law for 52 years and was the lawyer-negotiator for many locals and employee benefit funds of the International Ladies Garment Workers Union. He is credited with helping to establish the right of workers to severance pay in a collective-bargaining agreement and with setting the norms for bargaining in the garment industry.
        He is survived by his wife, Marie; two daughters, Ronnie J. Levin of Williamstown, Mass., and Barbara J. Paul of Westport, Conn.; a son, Benjamin, of Bethesda, Md.; a sister, Belle Scolnick of West Palm Beach, Fla.; a brother, Emil, of Fort Lauderdale, Fla., and seven grandchildren.

        Jack Lipson, the chief of operations of the Federal defender services unit of the Legal Aid Society for the last decade, died, apparently of a heart attack, on Saturday at St. Francis Hospital in Port Washington, L.I. He was 45 years old and lived in Port Washington.
        Mr. Lipson was a native of Brooklyn and a graduate of Columbia College, Columbia University Law School and Cambridge University.
        Surviving are his wife, Robin Charlow; a brother, Lawrence, of the Bronx, and a sister, Janet Lieber of Brooklyn.

        Joseph Venditto, deputy chief of the New York City Fire Department, died of cancer yesterday at the Westchester Medical Center in Valhalla, N.Y. He was 52 years old and lived in Hopewell Junction, N.Y.
        Chief Venditto joined the Fire Department in 1970. He was in charge of the explosives unit and also dealt with motion picture and television production companies that filmed scenes of fires and explosions in the city.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Marlene D'Elia; his mother, Mary Aranzullo of Palm Harbor, Fla.; a daughter, Donna Messinger of Carmel, N.Y., and a son, Louis, of Yorktown Heights, N.Y.


        Bertram A. Levine, a retired partner in the Manhattan law firm of Kramer & Kaprow, died on Wednesday at his home in White Plains. He was 84 years old.
        He died of cardiac arrest, said his daughter, Dinah L. Moche of Mamaroneck, a professor of physics at the City University of New York.
        Mr. Levine, a 1929 graduate of Columbia University law School, specialized in commercial law. He retired in the early 1970's. Active in civic affairs, he was a former president of the Hebrew Institute of White Plains. He was also a charter member of the New York chapter of B'nai B'rith and a founder of the Westchester Day School in Mamaroneck.
        In addition to his daughter, he is survived by his wife, the former Mollie Last; a son, Joel, of Tel Aviv, a businessman; eight grandchildren, and two great-grandchildren.

        Frank M. Smith, an advertising executive for nearly half a century who retired in 1969 as a senior vice president and partner in Benton & Bowles, now D'Arcy Masius Benton & Bowles, died Aug. 11 of a heart attack at his home in Scarsdale, N.Y. He was 88 years old.
        Mr. Smith, who was born in Mount Vernon, Ind., was a graduate of the University of Michigan. He worked for several advertising agencies and was with General Foods for eight years before joining Benton & Bowles in 1941. In the early days of television, he initiated commercials for, among other sponsors, Procter & Gamble.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Frieda Boes; a son, Frank Jr., of Scarscale; two daughters, Polly Holder of Glastonbury, Conn., and Deborah Jones of Riverside, Calif.; nine grandchildren and one great-grandchild.


        Dr. Morton A. Schiffer, who was chairman of the obstetric advisory committee to New York City's health commissioner from 1972 to 1982, died on Friday at his home in West Norwalk, Conn. He was 88.
        Dr. Schiffer was a clinical professor of obstetrics and gynecology at Yale's School of Medicine beginning in 1993 and then retired.
        Earlier, he was director of the obstetrics and gynecology department at Jewish Hospital and Medical Center in Brooklyn, where he worked for 41 years. He was also obstetrician and gynecologist in chief at Stamford Hospital in Stamford, Conn., among other positions.
        A native of New York City, he received a bachelor's degree from Alfred University and his medical degree from Long Island College of Medicine. He was a decorated Navy doctor in World War II.
        His wife, Sara Jane Troy Schiffer, died in 1971.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Ruth Heller; two daughters, Peggy Noland and Holly Zucker; a stepdaughter, Ruth Andrea Levinson; two stepsons, Peter Levinson and James Heller Levinson; two grandchildren; and six stepgrandchildren.

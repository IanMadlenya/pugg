        Baier Lustgarten, founder of the Lustgarten Nurseries, which had outlets at Shelter Island, L.I., Middle Island, L.I., Cream Ridge, N.J., and Miami, died of cancer on Wednesday at his home in Quogue, L.I. He was 82 years old.
        Mr. Lustgarten began his career as a florist in Queens in 1927. In 1939, he bought property on Middle Island and by 1950 had started the expansion that led to the current nurseries.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Elizabeth Odman; two children from a former marriage, Suzanne Martin of Belmont, Mass., and David, of Poona, India, and four stepchildren, Kenneth Lustgarten of Cream Ridge, Garry Lustgarten of Wrightstown, N.J., Howard Lustgarten of Binghamton, N.Y., and J. Paul Hoffman of Newport, Me., and 12 grandchildren.

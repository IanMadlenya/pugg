        Thomas F. X. Gorman, an advocate for the mentally ill, died of septicemia April 1 at George Washington University Hospital in Washington. He was 75 years old and lived in Washington.
        A graduate of New York University, Mr. Gorman was a former executive director of the National Committee Against Mental Illness. He served on many government commissions on mental illness and worked for the Albert D. Lasker Foundation, from which he retired in 1988.
        He is survived by two children, Michael, of Charlottesville, Va., and Breon, of Manhattan; a brother, Arthur, of Rochester, N.Y., and one grandson.

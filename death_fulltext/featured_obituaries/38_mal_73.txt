        Frederick Evoy Kneip, a retired executive in the paper products industry, died of cancer on Tuesday at the Lakeland (Fla.) Regional Medical Center. He was 75 years old and lived in Lakeland.
        Mr. Kneip was with the Union-Camp Corporation for 24 years, having joined the company in 1941. In 1957, he was made general manager of its Lakeland, operations and the following year became general manager in New York City. In 1965, he became a senior vice president of the Stone Container Corporation, a corrugated box manufacturer based in Chicago. He retired in 1968.
        He is survived by his wife Loraine; two sons, Frederick, of Brooklyn and Edward, of Mobile, Ala.; a daughter, Ronde Bradley of Huntington Bay, L.I.; two sisters, Louise Hall of St. James, L.I., and Joan Adams of Madison, Conn., and seven grandchildren.

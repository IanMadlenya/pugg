
        Clarence A. Barnes Jr., an author and advertising agency art director, died at his home in Manhattan on Feb. 2. He was 85 years old and also had a home in Edgartown, Mass.
        He died of emphysema, his family said.
        During his 40-year career, Mr. Barnes worked for several advertising agencies in New York City, including Ted Bates and Benton & Bowles.
        In the 1950's he wrote "White Collar Zoo" and successive "Zoo" books, a series that featured animal photographs with humorous captions. He also wrote and illustrated "The Secret of Cooking for Dogs" and "The Secret of Cooking for Cats."
        Mr. Barnes, who was born in Boston, graduated from Yale University in 1929.
        He is survived by his wife, Elizabeth Betts Barnes; a daughter, Susan Walker of New York City; a son, Clarence, of Vineyard Haven, Mass.; four brothers, three sisters and five grandchildren.

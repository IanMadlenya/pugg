        Frederick Schang, a founder and former president of Columbia Artists Management Inc., the world's largest classical-music management firm, died Sunday at Bethesda Memorial Hospital in Delray Beach, Fla. He was 96 years old, said his granddaughter, Gabrielle Schang of Manhattan.
        Mr. Schang was with Columbia Artists from its inception in 1930 until he retired in 1963. Among the many artists he managed were the tenor Enrico Caruso, the violinist David Oistrakh and the pianist Sviatoslav Richter.
        Mr. Schang was born in Manhattan. For a time after his graduation from the Columbia Graduate School of Journalism, he was a reporter for The New York Tribune.
        In addition to Ms. Schang, he is survived by a daughter, Rosalind Swanson of Woodstock, Vt.; seven grandchildren, and seven great-grandchildren.

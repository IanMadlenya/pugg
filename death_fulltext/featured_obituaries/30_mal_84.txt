        Elmer F. Bennett, a retired Washington lawyer who was a former Under Secretary of the Interior, died of congestive heart failure July 10 at St. Francis Hospital in Roslyn, L.I. He was 71 years old and lived in East Hills, L.I.
        Mr. Bennett was an official in the Interior Department in both Administrations of President Dwight D. Eisenhower, serving as Under Secretary from 1958 to 1961, After leaving the department, he practiced law until 1970, when he joined the Office of Emergency Preparedness as special assistant and general counsel. From 1973 to 1988 he was counsel to the law firm of Loomis, Owen, Fellman & Howe in Washington.
        Born in Longmont, Colo., Mr. Bennett graduated from Colorado State University and Stanford University Law School. A longtime resident of Washington, he moved to East Hills in 1983.
        Surviving are his wife, Jewell; a son, John, of Washington; a daughter, Kathryn Bennett of Santa Fe, N.M., and two grandchildren.

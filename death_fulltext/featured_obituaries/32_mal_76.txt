        Philip Sterling, a writer and public relations representative, died of lung cancer Monday at his home in Wellfleet, Mass. He was 82 years old.
        Mr. Sterling joined the Columbia Broadcasting System in 1945 and later became chief of press information for CBS Radio. He also worked in public relations for CBS Television. He retired in 1965.
        He was the author of eight books, the most recent of which was ''The Real Teachers'' (Random House, 1972). His biography of Rachel Carson, ''Sea and Earth,'' (Thomas Crowell & Sons, 1970) won a Christopher Award.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Dorothy Dannenberg; a son, Peter, of Philadelphia; a daughter, Anne Fausto Sterling of Providence, R.I., and two grandchildren.

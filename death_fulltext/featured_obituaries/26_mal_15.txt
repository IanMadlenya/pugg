        Lee E. Cooper, a retired real-estate editor of The New York Times, died Tuesday at the Lake Wales (Fla.) Hospital. He was 89 years old and lived in Lake Wales.
        A native of Oxford, N.C., he joined the news staff of The Times in 1928 after working on The Asheville (N.C.) Times. He was real-estate editor from 1934 to 1954, when he retired.
        He is survived by his wife, Alma; a daughter, Kay Ingley of Lake Wales; a son, Robert W., of Jacksonville, Fla.; a sister, Mary Van Story of Fayetteville, N.C., and four grandchildren.

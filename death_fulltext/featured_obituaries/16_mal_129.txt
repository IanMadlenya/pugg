        Dale McConathy, chairman of the Department of Art and Art Education of the New York University School of Education, died of a heart attack Sunday at his home in Manhattan, an N.Y.U. spokesman, Randi Wershba, said. He was 48 years old.
        Mr. McConathy began working at N.Y.U. in 1980 and had been an associate professor since 1985. He was founding executive director and consultant of Artpark, an arts center in Lewiston, N.Y. He was also an editorial consultant for magazines, including Time, Vogue, Working Woman and Harper's Bazaar.
        He was born in McAlester, Okla., and received a bachelor's degree from Central State College in Oklahoma and a master's degree from the University of Oklahoma.
        His companion was Grant Taylor, Mr. McConathy's family said. He is survived by his parents, Edward and Irene, of Oklahoma City; a brother, Walter, also of Oklahoma City, and a sister, Kay, of San Francisco.

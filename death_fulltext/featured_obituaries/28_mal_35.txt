        Lord Martonmere, the former Governor General of Bermuda and a member of the British House of Commons for 33 years, died on Wednesday at his home in Lyford Cay, Nassau, the Bahamas, after a brief illness. He was 82 years old.
        Born John Roland Robinson, he became Lord Martonmere when he was created a baron in 1964. He studied law at Trinity Hall, Cambridge, and became a member of Parliament, where he served from 1931 to 1964.
        He was then appointed Governor of Bermuda and served until 1972. He was a wing commander with the Royal Air Force in World War II and was liaison to the American Eagle Squadron attached to the R.A.F.
        He was keenly interested in the tourist industry and for 27 years was president of the Association of Health and Pleasure Resorts of Great Britain.
        Lord Martonmere, who spent the winters at Lyford Cay and summers at Romay House, Tucker's Town, Bermuda, is survived by his wife, Lady Martonmere, the former Maysie Gasque; a daughter, Loretta Rogers, of Toronto; and seven grandchildren.

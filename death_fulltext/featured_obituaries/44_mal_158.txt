        John E. (Jackie) Moran, who was a child actor in films in the 1930's, died on Thursday. He was 67 years old.
        Mr. Moran died of cancer at Franklin Medical Center, his attorney, Robert Doyle, said.
        As a child, Mr. Moran was discovered by Mary Pickford and appeared in several Hollywood films, including ''The Adventures of Tom Sawyer'' in 1938 and ''Destination Moon,'' a Buck Rogers serial, in 1939.
        Later in life, he became a screenwriter and worked in public relations for the Roman Catholic Diocese of Chicago, Mr. Doyle said.

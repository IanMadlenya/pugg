
        Lawrence Terry, the headmaster of the Middlesex School in Concord, Mass., for a quarter-century, died Friday at the Apple Valley Continuing Care Center in Ayer, Mass. He was 91 years old and lived in Harvard, Mass.
        He died of prostate cancer, the center said.
        Mr. Terry held the Middlesex post from 1938 until he retired in 1964. Earlier he had taught and been assistant headmaster at the Noble & Greenough School in Dedham, Mass.
        Mr. Terry is survived by his wife, the former Katharine Johnson; two daughters, Julia Terry and Elise Brown; two sons, Charles and Lawrence Jr., and four grandchildren.

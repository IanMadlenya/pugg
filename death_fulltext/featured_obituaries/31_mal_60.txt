        Harold Barger, professor emeritus of economics at Columbia University, died of pneumonia Wednesday at his home in Kinderhook, N.Y. He was 82 years old.
        Professor Barger taught at Columbia from 1937 until his retirement in 1975. He was chairman of the economics department from 1961 to 1964. He served as the first director of Reid Hall, the university's center in Paris, in 1965.
        Born in London, he graduated from King's College, Cambridge University and earned a doctorate from the London School of Economics.
        Surviving is his wife, Gwyneth; two nieces, and a nephew.

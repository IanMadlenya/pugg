        Jean P. Baltzell, a retired New York lawyer who was active in Democratic politics in Manhattan in the 1950's and 60's, died of cancer Saturday in Palm Beach, Fla., where he lived. He was 71 years old.
        Mr. Baltzell, a graduate of Princeton University and the University of Pennsylvania Law School, had been associated with the law firm of Milbank Tweed Hadley & McCloy in New York and had been a partner in Evarts, Baltzell, Wainwright & Baker.
        An anti-Tammany Democrat, he was president of the Lexington Democratic Club, which he helped found, and Democratic leader in the Ninth Assembly District on the East Side. He also founded Vision, a Spanish and Portuguese magazine. Mr. Baltzell retired from the law in 1976.
        Surviving are a son, Francis Drexel, of New York; two brothers, William and E. Digby, both of Philadelphia, and a stepdaughter, J. Reeve Bright of Palm Beach.

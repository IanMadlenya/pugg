
        Eugene H. Zagat, a retired New York real-estate executive, died on Monday at his home in Manhattan. He was 82 years old.
        Mr. Zagat died of throat cancer, said his son, Eugene H. Zagat Jr., a publisher of the Zagat New York City Restaurant Survey.
        Mr. Zagat, a native of Manhattan, earned a bachelor's degree at Dartmouth College in 1930. He worked on the advertising and circulation business staff of The New York Times from 1932 to 1935, leaving to join his father, Paul H. Zagat, in the family real-estate business, Paul H. Zagat & Company, which controlled properties throughout the metropolitan New York area and in other cities. Mr. Zagat was president of the firm from the early 1950's until his retirement in the early 1980's.
        During his career, Mr. Zagat was a director of the Broadway Association, the American Council of Nationalities Services and the New York City Real Estate Board. He was also a vice president of the Educational Alliance.
        Besides his son, Mr. Zagat is survived by his second wife, the former Elizabeth McNichol; a daughter, Cornelia Z. Eland; a stepdaughter, Lori McNichol, and four grandchildren, all of Manhattan.

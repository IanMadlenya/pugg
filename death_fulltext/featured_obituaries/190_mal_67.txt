
        Edward Bliss Jr., a retired CBS News executive whose writing and editing set standards at the network in the 1940's, 50's and 60's, died on Monday in Washington. He was 90.
        During a 25-year career with CBS, Mr. Bliss wrote and edited the news summary for Edward R. Murrow's broadcasts, worked with Fred Friendly on ''CBS Reports,'' was Walter Cronkite's news editor and an executive assistant to Richard S. Salant, then president of CBS News.
        The son of missionaries, Mr. Bliss was born in Fuzhou, China, on July 30, 1912. He started his career in the 30's, working on newspapers in Ohio.
        In 1943 he joined CBS Radio News as a writer and in 1947 began preparing Murrow's broadcasts. In 1963 he became Mr. Cronkite's news editor.
        In 1968 Mr. Bliss moved from the newsroom to the classroom, starting the broadcast journalism program at the School of Communications at American University in Washington. He retired in 1997.
        He wrote ''Writing News for Broadcast,'' a journalism textbook first published in 1971. In 1991 he published ''Now the News: The Story of Broadcast Journalism'' (Columbia University Press).
        Mr. Bliss is survived by a daughter, Anne Mascolino of Washington.

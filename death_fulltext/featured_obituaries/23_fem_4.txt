        Kathleen M. Kane, the first female judge of the Nassau County District Court, died of complications Tuesday after undergoing surgery at St. Joseph's Hospital in Tampa, Fla. She was 87 years old and lived in Tampa.
        She was born in Manhattan and graduated from St. John's University Law School. After practicing law in Mineola, L.I., for 20 years, she was elected a judge of the Nassau County District Court, where she served from 1953 to 1970, when she retired.
        Miss Kane prepared the charter for the Nassau County Women's Bar Association and served as its president. She was among the first female members of the Nassau County Bar Association.
        Surviving are two nieces, Agnes Rutledge Hanford and Grail S. Hanford, both of Tampa.

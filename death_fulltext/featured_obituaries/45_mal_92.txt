        Malcolm Smith, a financier and an investment banker, died on Tuesday at his home in Greenwich, Conn., at the age of 86.
        He died of pneumonia, his family said.
        Mr. Smith was a partner in several Wall Street firms, including Glore, Forgan & Company and J. H. Whitney & Company, before becoming a vice president of the Hughes Tool Company and assistant to Howard R. Hughes, its chairman, in 1948.
        From 1954 to 1957 he was a partner in Dean Witter & Company. During that time he organized the Southern Nitrogen Company, based in Savannah, Ga., and was its chairman until 1966. He was also chairman of the executive committee of Congoleum-Nairn Inc., from 1960 to 1968.
        Mr. Smith, a native of Rowley, Mass., was a graduate of Brown University and of the Harvard Business School.
        His wife, the former Sarah Paschall Davis, whom he married in 1943, died in 1983. Surviving are a son, Malcolm Jr., of Washington; two daughters, Sarah, of Santa Fe, N.M., and Sandra Mongendre, who lives in France; a stepson, David Getchell of Issaquah, Wash., and four grandchildren.

        Alphonse Ouimet, a former president of the Canadian Broadcasting Corporation who helped introduce television to Canada 40 years ago, died of heart failure on Tuesday. He was 80 years old.
        Mr. Ouimet went to work for the CBC as an engineer when it was founded 52 years ago. In the 1940's he studied the operation of television in the United States and Europe and played a major role in introducing it to Canadian audiences. In 1958, he became president of the CBC and held that post until 1966. He was president of Telesat Canada from 1969 to 1980.

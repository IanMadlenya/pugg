
       Adele Lewis Corwin, who founded the Career Blazers employment agency in 1949 and headed it until she retired in 1975, died on Saturday at Beth Israel Hospital. Mrs. Corwin, who was 67 years old and lived in Hawley, Pa., died of complications from lung cancer, her son, William Lewis, said yesterday.
        Adele Lewis Corwin, who founded the Career Blazers employment agency in 1949 and headed it until she retired in 1975, died on Saturday at Beth Israel Hospital. Mrs. Corwin, who was 67 years old and lived in Hawley, Pa., died of complications from lung cancer, her son, William Lewis, said yesterday.
        Mrs. Corwin was the author or co-author of 11 books related to careers, including ''How to Write a Better Resume,'' published in 1975, and ''Fast Track Careers for the Nineties'' (1989).
        She was born in Yonkers, and received bachelor's and master's degrees in chemistry from Columbia University. She was a chemist with the Manhattan Project, which developed the atomic bomb, during World War II.
        Mrs. Corwin is survived, in addition to her son, who lives in Manhattan, by her husband, Eugene; a sister, Charlotte Kassel of Washington, and a grandson.

        Julian Bingham Abbott, an interior designer based, died on Aug. 20 at Southampton (L.I.) Hospital. He was 64 years old and lived in East Hampton, L.I.
        He died of complications from AIDS, family members said.
        Mr. Abbott, who was born in Cleveland, was a graduate of Phillips Academy in Andover, Mass., and of Yale. He served with the Army in Japan after World War II.
        From 1950 to 1957 Mr. Abbott was associated with Rorrimer-Brooks, a Cleveland decorating concern. He then joined Theodore Hofstatter Inc., in New York, which he left in 1975 to establish his own company in East Hampton, where he had maintained a weekend home for many years. His business partner and companion was W. Scott Jackson of East Hampton.
        Mr. Abbott is survived by his brother, Gardner Jr., of Madison, Conn.

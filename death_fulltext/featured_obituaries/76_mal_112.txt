
        Walter Rolf Warner, a retired New York cosmetics executive, died on Thursday at Indian River Hospital in Vero Beach, Fla. He was 75 and had moved from Rye, N.Y., to Vero Beach after his retirement in 1984.
        He had a lengthy illness, his family said.
        Mr. Warner worked for 30 years at a number of companies in the cosmetics industry. His final position was with Orlane of Paris and New York, a large French concern. He was president of Orlane Inc., its American subsidiary in New York. Before that he was a vice president at Revlon.
        Mr. Warner, who was born in Nashville, Tenn., grew up in Des Moines and attended Grinnell College and the University of Iowa. In World War II, he was commissioned a second lieutenant in the Army Air Corps and served as a navigator.
        Mr. Warner is survived by his wife, Carol Ratsey Warner; a daughter, Bette Warner of Wellfleet, Mass.; three sons, Michael of Freeport, Fla., and Jeff and Doug, both of Mendocino, Calif.; four stepchildren, Jane Williams and Colin Ratsey, both of Greenport, L.I., and Cynthia Young of Rye, and Scott Ratsey of Vero Beach; a brother, Meredith of Mesa, Ariz., and nine grandchildren.

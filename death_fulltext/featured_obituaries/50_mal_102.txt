
        Dr. John L. Bergstresser, an educator who was dean of students at City College of New York in the 1940's, died on Feb. 24 at his home in San Francisco. He was 87 years old.
        He died of a heart attack, his family said.
        Dr. Bergstresser held bachelor's and doctoral degrees from the University of Wisconsin, where he was assistant dean of students before joining the Progressive Education Study to evaluate American colleges and universities.
        At City College he was dean of men from 1942 to 1945 and dean of students from 1945 to 1947, when he left to join the University of Chicago. He was later dean of students at San Francisco State College and Chico State College in California. He retired in 1973.
        His wife, the former Idaleen Velde, died in January. He is survived by two sons, Kenneth, of Berkeley, Calif., and Richard, of Greenwich, Conn.; a daughter, Ann Graves of Tiburon, Calif.; a sister, Virginia Knott of Iowa City, and seven grandchildren.

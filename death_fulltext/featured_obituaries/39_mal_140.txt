        The Rev. Charles E. Sheedy, former dean of the College of Arts and Letters at the University of Notre Dame, died of chronic lung disease on Saturday. He was 77 years old and lived in South Bend.
        Father Sheedy, a professor emeritus of theology and a former chairman of the theology department, was dean of the college from 1952 to 1968.
        Father Sheedy was a specialist in moral theology and Christian ethics. In 1957 he condemned the use of nuclear weapons, even for defensive purposes. In 1964 he testified before a legislative committee in Massachusetts to advocate abolishing capital punishment.

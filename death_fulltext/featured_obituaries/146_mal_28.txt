
        Richard Goldberg, a longtime executive of the American Contract Bridge League, died yesterday at his home in Nashville, Tenn. He was 76.
        He died after suffering a heart attack, said his daughter, Joan Conroy, also of Nashville.
        Mr. Goldberg was executive secretary and general manager of the American Contract Bridge League from 1971 to 1984. From 1963 to 1971, he supervised the league's tournament division.
        After he retired, he served the World Bridge Federation as a delegate from the American league, then as secretary, treasurer and financial director.
        He was educated at Vanderbilt University, and he served in the Navy at the close of World War II.
        In addition to his daughter, he is survived by his wife, Frederica; a son, Richard Jr., of Plano, Tex.; another daughter, Geraldine Goldberg of Arcata, Calif., and six grandchildren.

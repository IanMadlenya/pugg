        Lincoln H. Clark, co-founder of the relief organization CARE and a professor of marketing at several colleges during his career, died of cancer Friday in his home Falmouth, Me. He was 77 years old.
        Mr. Clark helped found the relief organization in 1945 as a way of assisting post-war Europe. Mr. Clark delivered the first CARE package to France in 1946 and served as the group's assistant executive director until 1947.

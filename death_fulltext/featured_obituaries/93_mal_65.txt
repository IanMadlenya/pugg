
        E. Wyatte Hicks, an executive in the advertising industry, died on Sunday at the Sylvester Cancer Center in Miami. He was 68 and had moved to Stuart, Fla., from New Canaan, Conn., in 1992.
        He died of cancer, said his son Mark of New Canaan.
        In 30 years in advertising, Mr. Hicks was an executive at several New York agencies, rising to executive vice president and corporate director of client services at the J. Walter Thompson Company.
        Mr. Hicks began his career at McCann Ericson in New York in 1947. He moved to Benton & Bowles in 1953, where he was vice president and account supervisor.
        In 1961 he joined J. Walter Thompson, and 10 years later was named managing director of the New York office and then executive vice president. In 1976 he resigned to become chairman of Barickman Advertising in Kansas City, Mo.
        In 1978, Mr. Hicks founded Wyatte Hicks Partners Inc., a management consulting firm. He retired in 1988.
        He was born in El Dorado, Ark., and served in the Army in World War II, attaining the rank of captain. After the war he received a B.A. from the University of Missouri.
        Besides his son Mark, Mr. Hicks is survived by two daughters, Connie, of Miami, and Victoria, of Rowayton, Conn.; another son, David, of Denver; two brothers, John, of Bloomington, Ind., and Robert, of St. Louis, and three grandchildren.

        John Lydenberg, a retired professor of American literature at Hobart and William Smith Colleges in Geneva, N.Y., died of cancer Wednesday at his home in Princeton, N.J. He was 74 years old.
        Mr. Lydenberg was educated at Oberlin College and was one of the first recipients of a doctorate in American civilization from Harvard University.
        He is survived by his wife, Mary Jane; a son, Steven, of Brookline, Mass.; a daughter, Ann Tweed of Sebastopol, Calif.; a sister, Mary Davis of Westville, Ohio, two grandchildren and eight stepchildren.

        Israel L. Crausman, an architect and engineer, died of heart disease on Sunday at his home in Spring Valley, N.Y. He was 90 years old. Mr. Crausman was born in Bessarabia, now part of the Soviet Union, and came here with his family at the age of 13. He graduated from Cooper Union six years later with architectural and engineering degrees.
        Mr. Crausman designed apartment buildings, schools, armories, bridges and other structures, mostly in the Bronx, Manhattan and Queens and Westchester and Rockland Counties.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Sybil Lipman; a son, Dr. William, of Warwick, R.I.; three grandchildren, and a great-grandson.

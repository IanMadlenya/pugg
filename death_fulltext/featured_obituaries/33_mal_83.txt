        Hyman Solniker, a retired New York City Criminal Court judge, died Thursday at Beth Israel Hospital after a long illness. He was 82 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Judge Solniker was appointed to the bench in 1965 by Mayor Robert F. Wagner. He had been a Democratic district leader on the Lower East Side for 12 years and for eight of those years was law secretary to Justice Charles Marks of State Supreme Court.
        Judge Solniker, who retired in 1976, was a native of Manhattan and a graduate of Brooklyn Law School.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Dorothy Greenblatt.

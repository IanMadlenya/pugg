
       Peter Whittenhall Phelps, a sportsman, painter and fishing-tackle specialist who was a partner in the Bedford Sportsman store in Bedford Hills, N.Y., died of cancer Monday at the Northern Westchester Hospital in Mount Kisco, N.Y. He was 54 years old and lived in Katonah, N.Y.
        Before joining the Sportsman in 1979, Mr. Phelps taught English at the Harvey School in Katonah, edited college textbooks for the W. W. Norton publishing company in New York and served as an acquisitions editor for St. Martin's Press there.
        Mr. Phelps, who was born in Bronxville, N.Y., was graduated from Hamilton College in 1957 and later studied at the Arts Students League in Manhattan.
        In addition to his wife, the former Kathryn Humphreys, and their two daughters, Haydee and Elizabeth, he is survived by a son and a daughter from a previous marriage, Peter Jr. and Suzanne Phelps; a brother, Charles, of Bethel, Conn., and three sisters, Leslie Shohet, of Chester, Vt.; Frances Faulks, of Hamilton, N.Y., and Marie Anne Seabury, of Berkeley, Calif.

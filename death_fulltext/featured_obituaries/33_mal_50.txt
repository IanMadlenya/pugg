        Alexander Mauro, a professor of biophysics at Rockefeller University, died of cancer Friday at New York Hospital. He was 68 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Professor Mauro was a co-inventor of the radio-frequency cardiac pacemakers, one of the first pacemakers. Born in New Haven, Professor Mauro graduated from Yale University, where he also earned a doctorate. From 1951 to 1958, when he joined Rockefeller, he taught at the Yale University School of Medicine.
        Surviving are his wife, the former Jean Gilpatrick; two brothers, John, of Hamden, Conn., and Albert, of Kansas City, Mo., and a sister, Philomena Leindecker of West Haven, Conn.

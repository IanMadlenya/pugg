        Richard C. Sheldon, a retired Ford Foundation executive, drowned in a boating accident Wednesday off Mount Desert Island, Me. He was 70 years old.
        Mr. Sheldon was former director of the foundation's office of the arts. He retired to Maine in 1985. Earlier, he had been director of marketing and research for The New York Herald Tribune.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Nancy Hadzinoff; three sons, John, of South Paris, Me., Richard, of Tokyo, and Frederick, of Bryn Mawr, Pa.; a sister, Arabella Levorsen, of Navato, Calif., and four grandchildren.

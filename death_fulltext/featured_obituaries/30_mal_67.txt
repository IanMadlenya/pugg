        Dwight P. Robinson Jr., retired chairman of the Massachusetts Investors Trust, the country's oldest mutual fund, died of a heart attack Wednesday in Wareham, Mass., while traveling to his home in Brookline. He was 89 years old.
        Mr. Robinson, who was born in Seattle and raised in Boston, was a graduate of Harvard College and the Harvard Business School. In 1926 he entered the financial field with the Lee Higginson Corporation and six years later joined Massachusetts Investors Trust as director of research.
        He was named a trustee of the fund in 1937, vice chairman in 1950 and chairman in 1954. He was credited with popularizing the use of mutual funds as investment vehicles for the general public. He retired in 1965.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Mary Gass.

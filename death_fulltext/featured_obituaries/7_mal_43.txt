        Joseph H. Aronson, a rabbinical scholar who served as a vice president of the Union of Orthodox Rabbis of the United States and Canada in the 1940's and 50's, died of a stroke Saturday at Good Samaritan Hospital in Suffern, N.Y. He was 96 years old and lived in Monsey, N.Y.
        Mr. Aronson was best known in scholary circles for his commentaries on the Palestinian Talmud, which were published in Jerusalem in 1964 and 1972.
        He is survived by his wife, Ida; five children, Tillie Malin of Jerusalem, Dr. Seymour Aronson of West Hempstead, L.I., Rabbi Leonard Aronson of New Hyde Park, L.I., Edith Steier of Monsey, N.Y. and Dr. Irving Aronson of Brooklyn; 17 grandchildren and 49 great-grandchildren.


        Howard C. Brown Jr., a former assistant general manager of the Atomic Energy Commission, died on Sunday at Georgetown University Hospital in Washington. He was 75 and lived in Kensington, Md.
        Mr. Brown died of cardiac arrest after a long illness with non-Hodgkins lymphoma, his wife, Janet L. Brown, said.
        Mr. Brown, a lawyer, joined the Atomic Energy Commission in 1948. The agency was the forerunner of the Energy Department and the Nuclear Regulatory Commission. In 1965 he helped investigate the loss of 200 pounds of bomb-grade uranium and pointed out security shortcomings.
        In 1972, Mr. Brown became president of Electro Nucleonics Laboratories Inc., of Bethesda, a company engaged in research on cancer and medical diagnostic tools. He was also executive director of the National Association of Life Sciences Inc., a trade association for medical research laboratories, from 1977 until he retired in 1985.
        Mr. Brown's first wife, Vivian Stroster Brown, died in 1967. In addition to his wife of 24 years, Janet, he is survived by three children from his second marriage, Stephen C., Matthew W. and Vincent A., all of Kensington; six children from his first marriage, Howard C. III, of Laytonsville, Md., D. Barrett, of New Market, Md., and Mary-Michael, Mary-Carolyn and John Christopher, all of Washington, and Mary-Patrice, of McLean, Va.; a sister, Beverly Parent of San Francisco, and three grandchildren.

        Erich O. Grunebaum, an investment banker who was chairman of the board of the New York Hanseatic Corporation, died Monday at the Mt. Kisco (N.Y.) Medical Center. He was 86 years old and lived in Dobbs Ferry, N.Y.
        Mr. Grunebaum was born in Essen, Germany, and was a banker there until he and his family fled the Nazis and came to the United States in 1941. He was with New York Hanseatic and its successor firm, Stuart Brothers, until his retirement in 1981.
        He was president of Congregation Habonim, a Reform synagogue in Manhattan, from 1965 to 1974 and a longtime board member of United Help and Selfhelp Community Services, philanthropic agencies.
        He is survived by his wife, the former Gabriele Neumann; two sons, Ernest of Chappaqua, N.Y., and David of Sudbury, Mass.; a daughter, Irene Koppel of Springfield, N.J., and eight grandchildren.

        Jerome Metzner, who taught biology in New York City for more than 30 years, died of cancer on Thursday at his home in Lido Beach, L.I. He was 79 years old.
        Dr. Metzner was a professor of biology at John Jay College of Criminal Justice for 11 years before retiring in 1978. From 1950 to 1967, he was chairman of the biology departments at the Bronx High School of Science and at Francis Lewis High School and Jamaica High School in Queens.
        He served as the administrative officer of the United States Army education mission in Korea in 1948. He was a 1944 graduate of Columbia University, where he received a Ph.D. in botany.
        He is survived by his wife, Jeanette; a daughter, Joan Behrmann of Palm Springs, Calif.; two sons, Jeffrey, of Manhattan and Chatham, N.Y., and James, of Yorktown Heights, N.Y.; a brother, Irving, of Hollywood, Fla., and 10 grandchildren.

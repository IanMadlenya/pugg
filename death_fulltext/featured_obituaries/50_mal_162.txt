
        Brendan O Hehir, a scholar who specialized in the works of James Joyce and William Butler Yeats, died of a brain tumor Sunday at his home in Berkeley. He was 63 years old.
        Mr. O Hehir was co-founder of the Celtic studies program at the University of California at Berkeley, whose faculty he joined in 1958.
        At his death he was working on a book on Joyce's "Finnegan's Wake," translations of 24 Irish tales and other works.
        He is survived by his wife, Laura Morland; a son and a sister.

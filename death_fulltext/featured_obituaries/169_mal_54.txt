
        Martin LeBoutillier, a former president of Paine, Webber, Jackson & Curtis, the brokerage house, died Tuesday at a hospital in Old Lyme, Conn. He was 79.
        The cause was chronic obstructive pulmonary disease and pneumonia, said his wife, Janet.
        Mr. LeBoutillier was elected president of Paine, Webber, Jackson & Curtis in 1971, at 49. He joined the company's investment banking division as a general partner in 1968.
        In addition to his wife, he is survived by a daughter, Clelia Zacharias, of Manhattan; two sons, Martin, of Fogelsville, Pa., and John, of Larchmont, N.Y.; a stepson, Duncan McNeill, of McKinleyville, Calif.; a stepdaughter, Sarah Treffry, of Fowey, England; and eight grandchildren.

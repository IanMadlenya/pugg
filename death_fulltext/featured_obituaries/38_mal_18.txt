        Louis Landerson, the general counsel for the Rodgers & Hammerstein Organization, died on Wednesday at Lenox Hill Hospital in Manhattan. He was 40 years old.
        The cause of death was pulmonary failure, said a friend, Richard Christel.
        Mr. Landerson was an expert in legal and business issues related to the musical theater. He joined Rodgers & Hammerstein, which administers the works of Richard Rodgers, Lorenz Hart, Oscar Hammerstein 2d and others, in 1983 as general counsel and director of business affairs.
        Mr. Landerson was also special counsel for Leonard Bernstein since 1980 and a board member of both the Jose Limon Dance Company and Video Music Education, which was created to distribute videos of the Young People's Concert series. After graduating from the Northeastern School of Law, he worked for the New York law firm of Sage, Gray, Todd & Sims.
        Mr. Landerson is survived by two sisters, Joan Sommers, of Mount Laurel, N.J., and Susan Epstein, of Denver.

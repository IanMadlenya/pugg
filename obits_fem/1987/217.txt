        Dr. Carol Nunis Lazarus, chief psychologist at the Southeast Nassau Guidance Center, died of breast cancer Aug. 9 in the Janker Klinik in Bonn, West Germany. She was 57 years old and lived in Garden City, L.I.
        Dr. Lazarus became associated with the Guidance Center, which among its activities treats alcohol and drug-related problems, in 1977. The center has offices in Seaford, L.I., and Wantagh, L.I.
        Earlier she worked at South Shore Center for Psychotherapy, in Wantagh, and at the South Oaks Hospital, in Amityville, L.I.
        She was born in Bethlehem, Pa., graduated from the University of Pittsburgh in 1950 and received a master's degree from its graduate school of business in 1951. She also earned two psychology degrees at Adelphi University, a master's in 1975 and a doctorate in 1978, Dr. Lazarus was an adjunct professor of psychology at the Florida Institute of Technology and an instructor and lecturer at Hofstra University.
        She is survived by her husband, Dr. Harold Lazarus; her mother, Louise Comens of Bethlehem; a sister, Dr. Iris Rotberg of Potomac, Md., and two sons, Eric, of Garden City and Mark, of Jackson, Miss.

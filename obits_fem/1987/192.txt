        Natalie Priest, a stage, television and film actress, died of cancer Tuesday at her home in Manhattan. She was 68 years old.
        Ms. Priest appeared in a number of daytime television serials over the years, including ''The Edge of Night,'' ''As the World Turns'' and ''Ryan's Hope,'' in which she played Sister Mary Joel. Her screen credits included ''The Wrong Man'' and ''Paternity.'' On Broadway, she was the mother of the bride in ''Yentl,'' and also played in ''The Respectful Prostitute'' and ''Venus Is.'' Her most recent stage role was her own one-woman show, ''Looking Forward.''
        She was a longtime officer of the New York local of the American Federation of Television and Radio Artists.
        Ms. Priest is survived by a son, Jamie Priest, and a daughter, Esther Blue, both of Manhattan; a sister, Hinda Neufeld of Ithaca, N.Y., and two brothers, Elias Cohen of Philadelphia and Sam Crane of Manhattan.

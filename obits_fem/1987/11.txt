        Dr. Helen B. Lewis, a psychoanalyst and professor emeritus of psychology at Yale University, died of cancer Sunday at her home in Cambridge, Mass. She was 73 years old and also had a home in Croydon, N.H.
        For 40 years, Dr. Lewis had a private practice in Manhattan and the New Haven area. From 1984 to 1985, she was president of the psychoanalysis division of the American Psychological Association. She was founding editor of the Psychoanalytic Psychology journal published by the association's psychological division.
        Dr. Lewis was the author of ''Shame and Guilt in Neurosis,'' published in 1971, and now a standard text; ''Psychic War in Men and Women,'' and the two-volume ''Freud and Modern Psychology.'' Two books completed before her death are to be published soon by Lawrence Erlbaum Associates of Hillsdale, N.J.: ''Sex and the Superego,'' and ''The Role of Shame in Symptom Formation,'' which Dr. Lewis contributed to and edited.
        Dr. Lewis was born in Manhattan. She was a graduate of Barnard College and earned a doctorate at Columbia University.
        She is survived by her husband, Naphtali Lewis; a daughter, Dr. Judith L. Herman, of Cambridge; a son, John B., of Cambridge, and two grandchildren.

        Janet Hart Sylvester, the first woman to head a division of the Federal Reserve System, died of cancer Sunday at her home in Washington. She was 70 years old.
        Mrs. Sylvester, who used her maiden name of Hart professionally, was named director of the Federal Reserve's Office of Saver and Consumer Affairs in 1976.
        She is survived by her husband, Harry A. Sylvester; a sister, Barbara Hart Fogle of Marietta, Ohio, and three children by a previous marriage, Barbara D. Wick of Northfield, Ill., Beatrice O. Hart of London, and John B. Diebold of Nyack, N.Y.

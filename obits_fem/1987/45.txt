        Mildred Smith Huser, a Brooklyn lawyer who was a delegate to the 1920 Republican state convention, died Monday at the New Rochelle Medical Center. She was 94 years old and lived in Scarsdale, N.Y.
        Mrs. Huser was born in Brooklyn and graduated from Smith College and St. Lawrence University Law School. She was a teacher, magazine editor and judge's assistant before she became a lawyer.
        Her husband, Thomas, died in 1948. She is survived by a son, Thomas E. Huser Jr. of North Palm Beach, Fla.; a daughter, Eleanor H. Kirk of Bronxville, N.Y.; seven grandchildren, and three great-grandchildren.

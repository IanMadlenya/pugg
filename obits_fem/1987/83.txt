        Eleanor B. Stevenson, who, with her husband, Willaim E. Stevenson, received the Bronze Star for their work with the American Red Cross in World War II in Europe, died Sunday at her home in Shell Point Village in Fort Myers, Fla. She was 84 years old Mrs. Stevenson went with her husband when he volunteered to established wartime Red cross units in European theaters of war. She was director of Red Cross Clubmobiles in the Mediterranean theater and after the war she wrote a book, ''I Knew Your Soldier.'' Mr. Stevenson died in 1985.
        Mrs. Stevenson leaves two daughters, Priscilla S. Hunt, of Cambridge, Mass., and Helen S. Meyner, a former member of Congress, who lives in Princeton, N.J., and three grandchildren.

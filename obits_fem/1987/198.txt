        Helen Dowling, a violinist and music teacher, died of lung cancer at her home in London on July 12. She was 71 years old.
        Mrs. Dowling, who was born in New York City, performed and taught violin in this country and in London, Tokyo and New York. She played in London with Albert Coates and in this country with Georges Enesco, and was to assume the Mischa Elman Chair at the Manhattan School of Music next year.
        Her husband, Allan, died in 1983. She is survived by a sister, Maia Gregory of Manhattan, and a stepdaughter, Anne de Beixedon of Santa Barbara, Calif.

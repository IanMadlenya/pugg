        Elizabeth Corday, the longtime executive producer of the daytime drama ''Days of Our Lives,'' died of respiratory failure Monday at Cedars-Sinai Medical Center here. She was 75 years old.
        Mrs. Corday, who won an Emmy for her work on ''Days of Our Lives'' in 1978, created the daytime television series - the first color soap opera - in 1965 with her husband, Ted. He died three years later.
        Mrs. Corday is survived by two sons: Kenneth, who is co-producer of the series, and Christopher.

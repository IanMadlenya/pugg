        Rose Kiesler Lieberman, a former editor who was the widow of Dr. Elias Lieberman, an associate superintendent of schools in New York City, died Saturday at her home in South Orange, N.J. She was 93 years old.
        Mrs. Lieberman worked closely with her husband, a poet, and compiled a book of his poetry after his death in 1969. The book, ''Notation in Haste,'' was published in 1976 by Profile Press.
        A volunteer nurse at Queens General Hospital during World War II, Mrs. Lieberman also studied interior design at Cooper Union.
        She is survived by two children, Dr. Amy Lowenstein of South Orange and Dr. James Lieberman of Westport, Conn; four grandchildren, and six greatgrandchildren.

        Vera Allen, an actress who performed in leading roles on Broadway, in Hollywood and on radio and television, died of heart failure Monday in a retirement home in Croton-on-Hudson, N. Y. Miss Allen, who was active in Actors Equity and was a president of the American Theater Wing, was 89 years old.
        After graduating from Barnard College, Miss Allen began her stage career in the early 1920's at the Neighborhood Playhouse in New York, where she appeared in ''The Dybbuk,'' ''The Critic'' and ''Grand Street Follies.'' In the 1930's, she toured in 10 Shakespearian roles and performed with the Chicago Civic Repertory.
        On Broadway, she appeared in ''The Silver Cord,'' ''Susan and God,'' ''The Phiadelphia Story,'' ''At Home Abroad,'' ''The Show Is On,'' ''The Two Mrs. Carrolls'' and ''Ladies of the Corridor.'' On the screen, she played opposite Will Rogers in ''Doctor Bull.''
        Miss Allen was married for 60 years to Malcolm Schloss, who died in 1981. She is survived by a son, John Schloss Jr., and two grandchildren.

        Ellen Frankfort, the feminist author, died early yesterday at Long Island's Southampton Hospital. She was 50 years old and lived in Sag Harbor, L.I.
        A supervisor at the hospital, Margaret Saladino, said Ms. Frankfort died at 5:15 A.M. in the hospital's intensive care unit. Mrs. Saladino said she was unable to disclose the cause of death.
        A close friend of Ms. Frankfort said that she had been depressed for some time and had left a suicide note.
        Ms. Frankfort, a former columnist for The Village Voice, was the author of five books, including ''Vaginal Politics,'' ''Rosie: The Investigation of a Wrongful Death,'' and ''The Voice: Life at The Village Voice.''

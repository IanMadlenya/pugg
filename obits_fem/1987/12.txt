        Helen Tubbs, a cafe singer of the 1940's and 50's and the director of the Soldier's, Sailor's and Airmen's Club, a charitable organization, died on Dec. 23 at her home in Manhattan. She was 77 years old.
        She was born in Minneapolis on March 14, 1909. She studied singing in Minneapolis and then moved to Hollywood, where she worked as an actress. She was the Rome correspondent for Variety from 1949 to 1954. With her husband, the late actor William Tubbs, she was an important part of a postwar film colony in Rome that included Ingrid Bergman, Roberto Rossellini, Vittorio De Sica and others.
        There are no survivors.

        Roslyn Lindheim, a professor of architecture at the University of California at Berkeley who was known for her work in hospital design, died of cancer Tuesday at her home in Berkeley. She was 65 years old.
        Professor Lindheim was born in New York City and graduated from Radcliffe College and the Columbia University School of Architecture. She moved to California in the 1950's and practiced in both California and New York. She joined the Berkeley faculty in 1963.
        She was an expert on health and environmental factors in hospital design and had directed a design team to remodel Montefiore Hospital in the Bronx.
        She is survived by her husband, Richard; a son, Daniel, of Washington; a daughter, Susan Lindheim, of El Cerrito, Calif.; a stepbrother, Ralph Colp Jr. of New York City, and three grandchildren.

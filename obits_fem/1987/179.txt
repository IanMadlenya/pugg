        Mollie Golub, former president of the Mizrachi Women's Organization of America, a Zionist organiztion now known as AMIT Women, died of pneumonia Tuesday in Maimonides Hospital. She was 85 years old and lived in Brooklyn.
        Mrs. Golub, an alumna of the Brooklyn Law School, was president of the group from 1949 to 1951 and again in 1956.
        Surviving are a son, Robert, of Syosset, L.I.; a daughter, Judith Singer of Jacksonville, Fla.; two brothers, Dudley Feit of Brooklyn and Herbert Feit of Jamaica Estates, Queens; a sister, Rose Blau of Brooklyn, and six grandchildren.

        Slava H. Levi, a volunteer reader for the visually impaired, died of cancer of the pancreas Saturday at her home in Manhattan. She was 67 years old.
        Mrs. Levi came to the United States from Yugoslavia in 1953 and was a member of the United Nations Women's Auxiliary from 1953 to 1954. She worked at the International Rescue Committee and later at Recording for the Blind.
        In recent years she has been a volunteer at In Touch Networks, a radio reading service for the visually and physically handicapped, of which her husband, Jasha, is executive director.
        In addition to her husband, Mrs. Levi is survived by a daughter, Tamara, of Manhattan.

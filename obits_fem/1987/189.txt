        Alice Kavookjian, a philanthropist and a prominent member of the Armenian community, died of heart failure Friday at her home in Manhattan. She was 86 years old.
        Mrs. Kavookjian, a patron of Armenian culture, was instrumental in the construction of the St. Vartan Armenian Cathedral in Manhattan. In May she became the first woman to be honored by the Diocese of the Armenian Church of America as ''Armenian of the Year'' for her contributions to the Armenian church and community.
        Mrs. Kavookjian was born in Istanbul, Turkey, to the novelist Zarouhi Kalemkerian. She came to the United States in 1920.
        She is survived by a son, Samuel M. Melkon Jr., of Paramus, N.J.; four stepchildren, Helen Barnes and Kathleen Mendes of West Palm Beach, Fla., Dr. Haik Kavookjian Jr. of Darien, Conn., and Sarkis Kavookjian of Rumson, N.J., and several grandchildren and great-grandchildren.

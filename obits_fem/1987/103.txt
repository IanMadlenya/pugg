        Carolin Babcock Stark, a women's amateur tennis star in the 1930's, died Wednesday, March 25, at Southampton (L.I.) Hospital, two days after suffering a stroke at her home in the North Haven section of Sag Harbor. She was 74 years old.
        A memorial service will be held at 11 A.M., April 7, at St. Thomas Episcopal Church at Fifth Avenue and 53d Street in Manhattan. Mrs. Stark is survived by her son, Morgan Babcock Stark of Manhattan and North Haven and three grandchildren.
        Mrs. Stark won the United States Lawn Tennis Association national doubles championship with Marjorie (Midge) Van Ryan in 1936. In 1932 she was the runner-up in women's singles to Helen Jacobs, who defeated her in the final, 6-2, 6-2, at the West Side Tennis Club in Forest Hills.
        Mrs. Stark was born in Billings, Mont., and was graduated from the Marlborough School in Los Angeles in 1934. She was highly ranked nationally in singles and doubles from 1932 to 1936 and played on four Wightman Cup teams from 1933 through 1936.
        She also played regularly on the East Coast invitation tournament circuit, winning the singles championship in 1934 and the doubles in 1935 at the Seabright (N.J.) Lawn and Cricket Club. She also won the Wooden Gold Cup, emblematic of the doubles championship, in 1934 at the Maidstone Club in East Hampton, L.I. She was married for 10 years to Richard S. Stark and she resided from the mid-1940's until 1980 in Malibu, Calif., before making her home in North Haven.

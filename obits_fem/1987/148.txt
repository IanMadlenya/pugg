        Faith Whitney Ziesing, a former trustee of Sarah Lawrence College, died of cancer last Friday at her home in Princeton, N.J. She was 75 years old.
        Mrs. Ziesing was president of the New York Junior League from 1938 to 1940. She was a Sarah Lawrence trustee from 1942 to 1957. In 1944, Mrs. Ziesing was the first alumna appointed to the faculty, teaching about the social aspects of economics.
        She was an officer on the boards of the Association of the New Jersey Neuropsychiatric Institute and Princeton Hospital. In 1928, Mrs. Ziesing helped start the Women's National Republican Club.
        She was born in Durham, N.H., the daughter of Caspar Whitney, a sportswriter, explorer and architect, and Florence Canfield, a founder of the League of Women Voters.
        Mrs. Ziesing graduated from Sarah Lawrence in 1932. In 1934, she married Morgan Wing and was divorced in 1947. In 1949, she married Hibben Ziesing, a chemical consultant.
        In addition to her husband, surviving are three daughters, Whitney Wing Oppersdorf of Lincolnville, Me.; Faith Wing Beiler of Greenwich, Conn., and Lucinda, of Manhattan, and five grandchildren.

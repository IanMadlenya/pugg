        Robina Gallup, the wife of a co-chairman of the Gallup Poll, died Saturday when a car she was driving crashed into a train at a crossing near Halkirk, Scotland. She was 45 years old and lived in Skillman, N.J.
        Mrs. Gallup's husband, Alec Gallup, is a co-chairman of the Gallup Organization, which conducts public opinion polls on politicians, trends and current events.
        Mrs. Gallup was traveling to her mother's home in Cirencester, Gloucestershire, when she died. Mrs. Gallup, her husband and several other Gallup officials had been in London to celebrate the 50th anniversary of the Gallup affiliate there. Mr. Gallup returned home and she went to Scotland.
        Mrs. Gallup attended Kings College and the London School of Economics.
        In the early 1960's she joined the Institute for Advanced Study in Princeton, N.J., as a research assistant. Her father, Peter W. Mould, a pilot in the Royal Air Force, was killed in World War II.
        She managed the Gallup farm in Montgomery Township, N.J., and was a former coach of the boys' junior varsity tennis team at the Princeton Day School. J. She is survived by her mother, Phyllis Grainger; her stepfather, Thomas H. Grainger; and three children from a previous marriage, Peter, Brian and Jennifer, all of Princeton, N.J.

        Isabel Sayer Cox, who was active for many years in civic and social affairs in Montclair, N.J., died Sunday of pneumonia at a retirement village in Bedford, Mass. She was 92 years old.
        She was the widow of Dr. William Wheeler Cox, a physician and surgeon in Montclair from 1919 until his death in 1971.
        Mrs. Cox was a past president of the women's auxiliary of St. Luke's Episcopal Church and of the Questers Group of the Montclair Women's Club. She was a volunteer for the Red Cross and Mountainside Hospital.
        Mrs. Cox is survived by her son, Dr. Denton Sayer Cox, of Manhattan; a daughter, Patricia Cox Mansfield, of Weston, Mass., and four grandchildren. A memorial service, as yet unscheduled will be held at St. Luke's Church.

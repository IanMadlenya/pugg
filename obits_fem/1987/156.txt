        Evelyn Levitt, a champion bridge player, died of a heart attack Tuesday in Wilmington (Del.) Hospital. She was 67 years old and lived in Wilmington.
        Mrs. Levitt was a life master with 7,800 master points and was the top-ranked female player in Delaware. She was a world master of the World Bridge Federation and won North American championships in various events in 1978, 1981, 1983, 1985 and 1986.
        She was to represent the United States in the Venice Cup competition in Jamaica in October.
        She is survived by her husband, Harold; three daughters, Andrea Baum of Brooklyn, Ilene, of Sunderland, Mass., and Donna, of San Francisco, and a grandson.

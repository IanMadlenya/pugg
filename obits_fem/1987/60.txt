        Josephine Scacciaferro, who taught Italian in New York City high schools for 22 years, died Tuesday at her home in Nutley, N.J. She was 88 years old.
        She was born in Castelbuono, Italy, and obtained a bachelor's degree from Barnard College and a master's degree in education from Teachers College at Columbia University. She retired from teaching in 1960.  She is survived by a daughter, Rosary Gilheany of Nutley.

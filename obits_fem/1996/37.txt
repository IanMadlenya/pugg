
        Natalie Holland, who supported a number of philanthropic and other institutions, died on Monday at her winter home in Palm Beach, Fla. She was 89 and lived on Manhattan's Upper East Side.
        She was a prominent supporter of Bar-Ilan University in Israel. Her other principal beneficiaries included the United Jewish Appeal-Federation of Jewish Philanthropies, the Kingsbrook Jewish Medical Center and Yeshiva University's Albert Einstein College of Medicine, where she belonged to the Society of Founders.
        She is survived by her husband of 67 years, Jack C. Holland; two sons, Stephen of Manhattan, and Richard of Woodmere, L.I.; four grandchildren, and one great-granddaughter.


        Natalie Lieberman, the founder and president of Learning Through Art/The Guggenheim Museum Children's Program and a member of the museum's board, died on Wednesday at Mount Sinai Medical Center. She was 86 and lived on the Upper East  Side of Manhattan.
        Mrs. Lieberman was born in Brooklyn. She studied piano at the Juilliard School and acting at the Theater Guild. She was a lead in Eugene O'Neill's "Marco's Millions" on Broadway and in Yiddish Theater.
        In the 1950's, she owned a millinery store, James Joel, on Madison Avenue.
        Her first husband, Sidney J. Kovner, died in 1963. She is survived by her second husband, Dr. Joseph Lieberman, two sons, Anthony R. Kovner of Manhattan and Joel W. Kovner of Santa Monica, Calif., and six grandchildren.

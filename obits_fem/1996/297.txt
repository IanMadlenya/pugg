
        Kay Strozzi, an actress who appeared on Broadway in the 1920's and 30's and who also performed on radio and television, died on Thursday at the Bayberry Nursing Home in New Rochelle, N.Y. She was 96.
        Ms. Strozzi's Broadway credits included "The Crown Prince" (1927), "Heavy Traffic" (1928), "The Silent Witness" (1931) and "St. Helena" (1936), in which Maurice Evans starred as Napoleon. She also toured with Ethel Barrymore in "The  Constant Wife."
        Among the many radio shows on which she appeared were "Our Gal Sunday," "Stella Dallas," "Young Dr. Malone," "Lights Out" and "Cavalcade of America." On television, she had roles in "Robert Montgomery Presents" and "Kraft Theater."


        Barna Ostertag, a theatrical agent and actress, died on Monday at Beth Israel North Hospital in Manhattan. She was 91 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        She died in her sleep of natural causes, said Rusty Berkower, her guardian and executor.
        Miss Ostertag was born in Piqua, Ohio, in 1902. She received her early training as an actress at the Goodman Repertory Theater in Chicago. She later moved to New York. During World War II, she worked with the American Red Cross in the Southwest Pacific.  She was a lifetime member of Actors' Equity and was also a member of the National Association of Talent Representatives.
        There are no immediate survivors.

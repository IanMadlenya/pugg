
        Elizabeth Sullivan Ridder, a philanthropist who was formerly the national secretary of the Girl Scouts of America, died on Thursday in West Palm Beach, Fla. She was 93 and formerly lived on the East Side of Manhattan.
        She had been in declining health for almost a year, said Maureen Ridder, a great-granddaughter.
        Mrs. Ridder was on the national board of the Girl Scouts from 1946 to 1958 and from 1961 to 1970 and was national secretary from 1950 to 1968. She was treasurer of the Girl Scouts' western hemisphere committee from 1963 to 1973.
        Other beneficiaries of her philanthropy included Casita Maria, a settlement house in the Hunts Point section of the Bronx. She was a co-founder of Casita Maria in 1934 in Harlem. 1942 Award From Pope
        She was a former member of the boards of directors of the Welfare Council of New York City and the National Conference of Catholic Charities.
        Born in Amsterdam, N.Y., she grew up in Manhattan, graduated from Trinity College in Washington and did graduate work at Columbia.
        She received the Pro Ecclesia et Pontifice award from Pope Pius XII in 1942 and the Catholic Youth Organization award in 1958 for "outstanding service and inspiration to youth" among numerous other awards.
        Mrs. Ridder's husband of 20 years, Charles H. Ridder, publisher of The Catholic News, the weekly newspaper for the Roman Catholic Archdiocese of New york, died in 1964.
        She is survived by two stepsons, Henry, of Pearl River, N.Y., and Thomas, of Maui, Hawaii; a stepdaughter, Katherine Wetzel of Belford, N.J.; grandchildren and great-grandchildren.

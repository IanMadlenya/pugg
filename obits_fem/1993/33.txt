
        Joy Garrett, an actress who performed in soap operas and on the New York stage, died on Thursday at the U.C.L.A. Medical Center in the Westwood section of Los Angeles. She was 47.
        Rich Elbaum, a spokesman for the medical center, said the cause was liver failure.
        For the past seven years, Ms. Garrett played the role of the mother figure Jo Johnson on the NBC daytime soap opera "Days of Our Lives." Previously she had performed for a year on another daytime soap opera, "The Young and the Restless," on CBS.
        In 1969, she appeared in "Gertrude Stein's First Reader," a musical revue based on works of Gertrude Stein, at the Astor Place Theater on Lafayette Street in the East Village. Ms. Garrett appeared in the 1970 production, at the Edison Theater, of "The Candyapple," playing the mistress of a lapsed Roman Catholic priest.
        She also performed in "Silver Queen Saloon" at La Mama E.T.C. in 1978.
        She also appeared in movies and stock and touring productions.
        She was born in Fort Worth and attended Texas Wesleyan University and the American Academy of Dramatic Arts.
        She is survived by her parents, Clarence and Kathleen Garrett of Fort Worth.

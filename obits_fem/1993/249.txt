
        Eda Helen Brooks, an actress and voice teacher, died yesterday at her home in Manhattan. She was 94.
        Mrs. Brooks was in the original cast of "Chicago," which was the first show to appear at the Music Box Theater in the 1920's. She also toured on the Keith and Pantages theater circuits and appeared in Jerome Kern's "Cat and the Fiddle."
        She retired from teaching in March.
        Her daughter Patricia Brooks Mann, a soprano with the New York City Opera, died in January. She is survived by her other daughter, Joan Palermo of Manhattan; a stepson, Allan Brooks of Great Neck, L.I., and three grandsons. A service is to take place on Sunday at 2 P.M. at the John Kitril Funeral Home, 1297 First Avenue, at 70th Street.

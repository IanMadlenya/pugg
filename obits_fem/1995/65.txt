
        Joan L. Hafey, a retired vice president for the New York advertising agency Young & Rubicam who was active in public-service advertising campaigns, died July 24 at Tisch Hospital of New York University Medical Center. She was 67 and lived in Manhattan and Southbury, Conn.
        The cause was pulmonary fibrosis, a spokesman for Young & Rubicam said.
        After beginning her career at Ladies Home Journal, Ms. Hafey managed press relations for Magnavox. She moved to Young & Rubicam in 1969. Ms. Hafey represented the agency and was involved in the Ad Council's public-service campaigns for the United Negro College Fund and the National Urban League. She also did volunteer public relations work for Covenant House and the Y.W.C.A. in New York, of which she was a board member.
        After retiring in 1992, Ms. Hafey served as vice president for the agency's foundation and as the editor of its alumni magazine.
        Ms. Hafey was a native of Holyoke, Mass., and graduated from Mount Holyoke College in 1949.

        Polly Cronyn Parrott, a former editor at The New York Herald Tribune died at her home in Plandome, L.I., on Thursday. She was 79 years old.
        Mrs. Parrott, a longtime resident of Plandome, graduated in 1931 from Cornell University and worked for 18 years as an editor on the society desk of the newspaper. She retired in 1950.
        She was active in her community, serving on its election board, singing alto in the Port Washington Singers choir in Port Washington, L.I., and working as a Friend of the Library in Manhasset.
        She is survived by her husband, Thomas; a son, John, of Brookfield, Wis.; a brother, Thomas Cronyn of Plandome; a sister, Nancy C. Knox of Darien, Conn., and one grandson.

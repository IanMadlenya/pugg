        Martha Redfield Wallace, a management consultant who was a director of many corporations, died of a heart attack Friday at her home in Manhattan. She was 61 years old.  She was the founder in 1983 of a consulting business, Redfield Associates, which helped to raise money for many causes. Its headquarters were in River House at 435 East 52d Street.
        From 1967 to 1982, she was executive director of the Henry Luce Foundation and a member of the foundation's board. For four years before joining the foundation, she worked for Time Inc. as a researcher and as assistant director of corporate development.
        Mrs. Wallace had been a director of Bristol-Myers until it agreed to merge with Squibb this year. She had also been a director of the Chemical Bank and continued to serve on the board of American Express and the surveillance committee of the New York Stock Exchange. She was a director of the exchange from 1977 until 1983. On Rhodes Scholar Panel
        She served on the Williams College board from 1974 to 1986, then becoming a trustee emeritus. She was a director of the American Judicature Society and its board chairman from 1981 to 1983. She was a trustee of the New York Racing Association and a former director of the Legal Aid Society.
        She was chairman of the Rhodes Scholar selection committee from 1983 to 1986 and a director of the Citizens Crime Commission in Manhattan. From 1972 to 1982, she was a director of the Council on Foreign Relations and since 1980 she had been a member of the American Council on Germany, a private group concerned with United States-German relations.
        Mrs. Wallace, who was born in Omaha, graduated Phi Beta Kappa from Wellesley College. She received a master's degree from the Fletcher School of Law and Diplomacy at Tufts University, where she was an instructor and assistant to the dean in the School of Economics.
        She was an economist with the State Department from 1951 to 1953, and before joining Time, she worked for RCA and International Business Machines.
        Her marriage to Donald L. Wallace, a lawyer, ended in divorce in the late 1970's.
        She is survived by a sister, Janice Redfield Roach of Omaha.

        Dr. Leoni Neumann Claman, a physician and allergist, died of cancer Sunday at Mount Sinai Hospital in Manhattan. She was 87 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Dr. Claman, a graduate of Columbia University, was in private practice for 40 years. She served as chairwoman of the Allergy Foundation of America and as president of the Women's Medical Association of New York City and of the New York Allergy Association.
        She is survived by a daughter, Betsy Magnus of Stamford, Conn.; two sons, Henry, of Denver, and Victor, of Weston, Mass.; 12 grandchildren, and 2 great-grandchildren.

        Elinor G. Black, a teacher and volunteer in community organizations, died of heart failure yesterday in New Rochelle (N.Y.) Hospital. She was 83 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mrs. Black was a former English teacher in the City College of New York's Adult Education Program. She was a former secretary of the Harlem Hospital Auxiliary and had been active in the Women's City Club of New York.
        Surviving are her husband, Algernon; three sons, David, of Manhattan, Peter, of Syracuse, and Jonathan, of Chicago; a sister, Maxine Aaron of Pittsburgh; eight grandchildren and two great-grandchildren.

        Bessie Stolzenberg, a social studies teacher in Manhattan for almost 40 years and an early advocate of women's suffrage, died of heart failure Thursday at her home in Rego Park, Queens. She was 95 years old.
        Miss Stolzenberg was born in Zbaraz, Austria, and immigrated to the United States as a child. She became a social studies teacher at Seward Park High School on the Lower East Side in 1924 and was later acting chairman of the department. She retired in 1962.
        She was a leader in the Woman Suffrage Party and marched with the suffragists demanding the right of women to vote.
        There are no immediate survivors.

        Leontine Sharaga, one of the first female dentists in New York City, died of colon cancer Saturday at Montefiore Hospital. She was 95 years old and lived in the Bronx.
        Dr. Sharaga, who began practicing during World War I, was born in Rumania. She came to New York with her family as a child and lived on the Lower East Side of Manhattan. She was a graduate of the Columbia School of Oral Surgery and for many years was a dental surgeon at the New York Eye and Ear Infirmary.
        There are no immediate survivors.

        E. Gail Macandrew, a producer for public television, died of complications from diabetes on Friday at Winthrop-University Hospital in Mineola, L.I. She was 45 years old and lived in Manhattan and in Garden City, L.I.
        Ms. Macandrew, whose full name was Elizabeth Gail Macandrew, was a graduate of Mount Holyoke College and in 1965 joined WNET-TV, where she produced programs on news and public affairs, including ''Newsfront'' and ''Roundtable.'' In the early 1980's, she became a freelance producer. In 1987, the New York chapter of the National Academy of Television Arts and Sciences awarded her a regional Emmy for ''First City,'' a news-magazine program for WNYC-TV.
        Her father was James F. Macandrew, who helped create New York City's public radio and television systems. He died in January 1988.
        She is survived by her mother, Catharine Schenck Macandrew; a sister, Bonnie Rosenblatt of Baltimore, and a brother, Peter F. Macandrew of Garden City.

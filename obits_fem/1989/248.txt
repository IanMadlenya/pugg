        Rowena Wyant, the first woman to be named a vice president of Dun & Bradstreet, died Aug. 23 at Huntington (L.I.) Hospital of injuries received in an auto accident a few days earlier. She was 72 years old and lived in Centerport, L.I.
        Miss Wyant, a 1938 graduate of Bennington College, joined Dun & Bradstreet in 1942 as a researcher and was vice president for business economics from 1975 until her retirement in 1984.
        She is survived by a brother, Robert, of Potsdam, N.Y.

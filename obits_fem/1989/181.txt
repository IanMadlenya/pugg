        Edith Begner, a novelist whose work focused on the medical profession, died of lymphatic leukemia on Wednesday at Emerson Hospital in Concord, Mass. She was 71 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mrs. Begner, a native New Yorker, wrote five novels, including ''Just Off Fifth'' (1959), ''Son and Heir'' (1960) and ''Accident of Birth'' (1977).
        She is survived by a son, Thomas, of Concord; a sister, Theodora Baron, of Miami, and two grandchildren.

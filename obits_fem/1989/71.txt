        Mary Elizabeth Johnston, the chief of reporters for Fortune magazine, died of emphysema Friday at her home in Manhattan. She was 71 years old.
        Miss Johnston worked at Fortune for more than 30 years before retiring at the end of 1987. A graduate of Rice University, she was a reporter for The Houston Post and The Christian Science Monitor. She joined Time magazine in 1949 and moved to Fortune two years later.
        She was a former member of the board of governors of Rice University. Surviving are her niece, Sydney Freeman of Atlanta, and a nephew, Harry Johnston of Manhattan.

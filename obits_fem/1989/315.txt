        June Guicharnaud, a translator and editor, died of cancer on Sunday at Yale-New Haven Hospital. She was 67 years old and lived in New Haven.
        Mrs. Guicharnaud translated about 15 books and many articles. Among her translations are works by Andre Malraux and the letters of Andre Gide and Paul Valery. She was nominated for a National Book Award in 1979 for her translation of ''The Horse of Pride'' by Pierre-Jakez Helias.
        She was co-author with her husband, Jacques, the Benjamin F. Barge Professor of French at Yale University, of ''The Modern French Theater from Giraudoux to Genet,'' published by Yale University Press.
        She was born in Elizabeth, N.J., studied at the Sorbonne and was graduated from New York University. She is survived by her husband and a brother, Robert Beckelman of Yardley, Pa.

        Electra B. McDowell, former board chairman of the New York City School Volunteers Program, died Friday at New York Hospital after a brief illness. She was 55 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mrs. McDowell was a volunteer in the city's schools for the last 15 years, tutoring at Public School 9 in Manhattan. She was also president of the Shelburne Museum in Vermont.
        She is survived by her husband, Dr. Fletcher McDowell, a neurologist; her father, Dunbar W. Bostwick of Shelburne, Vt.; two sisters, Dundeen Catlin of Concord, Mass., and Elliot Wood of Novato, Calif.; three daughters, Electra and Linn of Manhattan and Laura of Shelburne.

        Rachel Bernstein Wischnitzer, a professor and art historian who specialized in synagogue architecture, died Monday at her home in Manhattan. She was 104 years old.
        Mrs. Wischnitzer, who taught at Stern College of Yeshiva University from 1956 until she retired in 1968, wrote four books and many articles on art history and architecture. Her books included ''Synagogue Architecture in the United States,'' ''Architecture of the European Synagogue'' and ''Messianic Themes in the Paintings of the Dura Synagogue.''
        Mrs. Wischnitzer was born in Minsk, Czarist Russia, and came to the United States in 1940. She studied at Heidelberg University and graduated from the Ecole Speciale d'Architecture in Paris in 1907 as one of the first women to receive a degree in architecture. She received a master's degree from New York University in 1944.
        She was a fellow of the American Academy for Jewish Research.
        She is survived by a son, Leonard Winchester, of Terrace Park, Ohio, and a granddaughter.

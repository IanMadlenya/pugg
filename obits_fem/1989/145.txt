        Joanna Roos, an actress, died of a ruptured aorta on May 13 at the Medical Center at Princeton (N.J.) She was 88 years old and lived in Hightstown, N.J.
        Miss Roos, who was born in Brooklyn, attended Syracuse University. She appeared on Broadway and in summer stock from 1921 until the late 1960's, working with such actors as Humphrey Bogart and Ingrid Bergman. She acted in the San Diego and New York Shakespeare Festivals, as well as in films and television. She retired in 1979 from the soap opera ''Love of Life.''
        She was a founding member of the New Dramatists Committee. Several of the plays she wrote for the group won awards.
        Miss Roos is survived by her son, Peter Rickett, of Greenville, S.C., and by four grandchildren and two great-grandchildren.

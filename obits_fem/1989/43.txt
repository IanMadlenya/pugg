        Catherine M. Conroy, who helped to organize the National Organization for Women, died of cancer Saturday. She was 69 years old.
        Ms. Conroy, a telephone operator, worked for the Communications Workers of America in Chicago and Milwaukee from 1960 until her retirement in 1982.
        In 1965, as a member of a Wisconsin commission for women, she helped to organize NOW at a meeting in Washington. In 1980 she was named by President Carter to his Advisory Commission for Women.

        Gertrude Tonkonogy Friedberg, a playwright and an author, died of cancer on Sunday at her home in Manhattan. She was 81 years old.
        She wrote two Broadway plays, called ''Three-Cornered Moon,'' which starred Ruth Gordon and Brian Donlevy and which was made into a movie, and ''Town House,'' based on stories by John Cheever. She also published humorous short stories and a science-fiction novel, ''Revolving Boy.''
        She is survived by a son, Richard; a daughter, Barbara; a sister, Letty Osserman, and her brothers, Eugene and Andrew, all of Manhattan.

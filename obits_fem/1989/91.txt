        Sister Mary Assisium Byrne, a former president of the Staten Island Mental Health Council who was long associated with the St. Vincent's Medical Center of Richmond, died of colon cancer Wednesday at the Sisters of Charity Home in Yonkers. She was 79 years old.
        Sister Mary Assisium was director of family and child-care services at New York Foundling Hospital from 1944 to 1955 and later directed social and psychiatric services at the St. Agatha Home in Nanuet, N.Y.
        She was born in Lancaster, Pa. Sister Mary Assisium was an alumna of the College of Mount St. Vincent and received a master's from Catholic University. She joined the St. Vincent's outpatient department as chief psychiatric social worker in 1964.
        From 1969 to 1971, she served in Vietnam with Catholic Relief Services, assisting in the placement of homeless and orphaned children.

        Ruth Moore Garbe, a writer of books about science, died of cancer Monday at her brother's home in San Francisco. She was 80 years old lived in San Francisco.
        Under the name of Ruth Moore, Mrs. Garbe was the author of books that included ''Man, Time and Fossils'' and ''The Coil of Life: The Story of the Great Discoveries in the Life of Sciences'' published in 1953 and 1961 by Alfred Knopf & Co. She also wrote a biography of Nils Bohr, published by Knopf in 1966.
        She was a graduate of Washington University, St. Louis. From 1943 to 1970 she was a reporter for The Chicago Sun-Times.
        In 1986, Mrs. Garbe and her brother, Norman Moore, gave $1 million to Washington University to endow a professorship in architecture. In 1987 she and her husband, Raymond W. Garbe, since deceased, donated $1 million to Harvard University to endow a professorship in urban design.
        She is survived by her brother.

        Elizabeth V. Murrell-Fax, a social worker and former director of the Forest Neighborhood House in the South Bronx, died of cancer Tuesday in La Guardia Hospital in Queens. She was 78 years old and lived in Queens.
        Ms. Murrell, a graduate of the New York Schol of Social Work, became director of the South Bronx settlement house in 1954. In the 1960's she was an adviser to African students for the Institute for International Education. She was a member of Women for Racial and Economic Equality and a delegate to the Women's Peace Congress in Moscow in 1977, the World Peace Conference in Prague in 1983, and the World Congress of Women in 1987.
        Surviving are her husband, Elton Fax, an artist, and her brother, Edward Murrell, of Fayette, Me. uf30>

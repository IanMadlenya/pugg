        L. Janette Richardson, a professor of literature who specialized in Chaucer, died of cancer Jan. 28 at the home of a friend in Piedmont, Calif. She was 63 years old and lived in Oakdale, Calif.
        Dr. Richardson received a Ph.D. from the University of California at Berkeley in 1962 and joined the faculty at Berkeley a year later. She became a professor in 1974 and served as chairman of the department of comparative literature from 1974 to 1979 and the department of rhetoric from 1981 to 1986.
        She is survived by a brother, Lawrence, of Salem, Ore.

        Marion Oppenheimer, director of public education of the New York City Sanitation Department, died of cancer on Friday at the New York University Medical Center. She was 57 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mrs. Oppenheimer joined the city's Environmental Protection Department in 1974. In 1978, she moved to the Sanitation Department, where she developed a student volunteer program to help clean streets.
        She was an alumna of Adelphi College.
        Surviving are her husband, Myron; two daughters, Toni and Jane, of Manhattan, and her parents, Herman and Alice Levine of Bayside, Queens.

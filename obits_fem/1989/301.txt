        Betty B. Linker, vice chairman of the commissioners of Montgomery County, Pa., died of breast cancer Saturday at Chestnut Hill Hospital in Philadelphia. She was 67 years old and lived in Flourtown, Pa.
        Mrs. Linker, the first Republican woman to serve as a Montgomery County Commissioner, had been the county's register of wills, clerk of the Orphans Court and director of the county's Consumer Affairs Department.
        She is survived by her husband, Erich G. Linker; two sons, Erich G. Linker Jr., of Garden City, L.I., and Stephen K. Linker of Flourtown; a sister, Phyllis Bortz, and two grandchildren.

        Lucetta Harkness, who taught English at high schools in Queens for more than a quarter-century and then became a Methodist missionary teacher in Japan, died of an intestinal hemorrhage on July 18 at North Shore Hospital in Manhasset, L.I. She was 92 years old and lived in Whitestone, Queens.
        She taught at Flushing and Bayside High Schools before her retirement in 1960, after which she went to Japan to teach English in church-sponsored junior colleges for seven years.
        Mrs. Harkness, a native of Manhattan, was a graduate of Barnard College. After receiving a master's degree from Columbia Teachers College, she set up a one-room school in Newtown, Conn.
        In the early 1930's, after she had lived in New Zealand for several years, she worked with the anthropologists Margaret Mead and Franz Boas on exhibits of Polynesian culture.
        She is survived by two sons, Peter, of Port Washington, L.I., and Donald, of Huntington, L.I.; a daughter, J. Marion Nentwig of Chattanooga, Tenn.; a sister, Grace Koster-Chase of Park Forest, Ill; eight grandchildren, and three great-grandchildren.

        Margery Madden Porter, one of the first women appointed as a corporate officer of a worldwide company, died of cancer Tuesday at Columbia-Presbyterian Medical Center in Manhattan. She was 85 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mrs. Porter was named an assistant secretary of the Standard Oil Company (New Jersey), now known as Exxon, in September 1945 and shared in the corporate administration of the company until her retirement in 1963. She had joined the company in 1930 as a stenographer. She had also served on the board of governors of the Women's National Republican Clubs.
        There are no immediate survivors.

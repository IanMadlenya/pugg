        Jean Holloway Tobin, a scriptwriter for radio, film and television, died of a stroke Saturday. She was 72 years old.
        Mrs. Tobin began writing for radio with ''The Kate Smith Show'' in the late 1930's. She also wrote for such shows as ''The Hallmark Radio Hall of Fame,'' ''Mr. President'' and ''Mayor of the Town.''
        In the 1940's, she wrote the film musicals ''Till the Clouds Roll By,'' ''Words and Music'' and ''Summer Holiday.'' Her television credits included ''Wagon Train,'' ''Dr. Kildare'' and ''Marcus Welby, M.D.''

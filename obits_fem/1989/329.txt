        Ann Hatfield Rothschild, who helped design the interiors of several well-known New York City buildings, died Thursday at a nursing home in Newton, Mass. She was 86 years old and had moved to North Easton, Mass., last year, from Piermont, N.Y., where she lived for many years.
        Ms. Rothschild, a native of Weston, Mass., founded Ann Hatfield Associates with Elsa Voelker in the 1930's. In 1942, her furniture designs took first prize at a contest sponsored by the Museum of Modern Art.
        She helped design the interiors of the International Arrivals Building at Kennedy International Airport, the Museum of Modern Art, the Rockefeller Resort Hotels in the Virgin Islands and the Mellon Bank in Pittsburgh. She retired in 1979.  Ms. Rothschild is survived by two sons, Thomas, of Newton, Mass., and V. Henry 3d, of Santa Fe, N.M.; a sister, Margaret Mackusick of Southport, Me.; nine grandchildren and four great grandchildren.

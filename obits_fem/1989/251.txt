        Dorothy Bigelow Melville, long active in philanthropy and civic affairs, died Tuesday at her home in Stony Brook, L.I. She was 95 years old.
        Mrs. Melville had been president of the Stony Brook Community Fund, chairwoman of the Museums at Stony Brook and a trustee of Bennett College in Greensboro, N.C.
        She was the wife of Ward Melville, originator of the Thom McAn brand of shoes. He died in 1977.
        Surviving are two daughters, Margaret Blackwell of Old Field, L.I., and Ruth Berlin of Southport, Conn.; two sons, Frank, of Norfolk, Conn., and David, of Rum Cay, the Bahamas; seven grandchildren, and a great-grandson.

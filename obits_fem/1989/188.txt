        Marion C. Heffernan, who was principal of Walton High School, in the Bronx, from 1937 to 1965, died Friday at Dobbs Ferry (N.Y.) Hospital after a stroke two days earlier. She was 88 years old and had lived in retirement in Ardsley-on-Hudson for 13 years.
        Mrs. Heffernan was one of the first women to be appointed principal of a New York City high school. Previously, she was chairman of the economics department at Erasmus Hall High School, in Brooklyn. She was a graduate of Vassar College and received a doctorate in economics from Columbia University.
        Mrs. Heffernan is survived by her son, William, of Manhattan.

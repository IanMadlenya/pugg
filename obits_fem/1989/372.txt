        Virginia Xanthos Faggi, coordinator for special projects at Columbia University, died of a heart attack Wednesday at St. Luke's-Roosevelt Hospital Center. She was 60 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mrs. Faggi, who received a bachelor's degree from Columbia, began her career at Columbia University Press in 1947. From 1955 to 1975 she was an assistant to Jacques Barzun, the author and historian. Mrs. Faggi, the widow of John A. B. Faggi, a Columbia professor and dean, became special events coordinator in 1982.
        She is survived by two daughters, Christina White and Alexandra Faggi, both of Manhattan; her mother, Mary Xanthos of Manhattan; a brother, Robert Xanthos of Akron, Ohio, and one grandchild.

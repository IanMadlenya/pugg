        Rose Schulman, an acting teacher at the nation's oldest repertory company, the Hedgerow Theater of Rose Valley, Pa., died after a heart attack on Sunday at the Crozer Chester (Pa.) Medical Center. She was 79 years old.
        For 57 years Miss Schulman worked with Hedgerow as actress, director and teacher. She and Jasper Deeter, the actor who founded the company, established the Hedgerow Theater School. Her students included Libby Holman, Richard Basehart, Alfre Woodard and Margo Skinner.
        She is survived by a brother, Albert, of Philadelphia, and a sister, Dorothy Bordock of Brooklyn.

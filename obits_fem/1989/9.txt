        Olga Averino, a former lyric soprano and a voice teacher for more than 50 years, died in her sleep Tuesday at her home in Cambridge, Mass. She was 93 years old.
        Miss Averino emigrated from Russia to the United States in 1918, and sang recitals and concerts for many years.
        She taught at Wellesley College and at the Longy School and the New School of Music in Cambridge, Mass.
        Miss Averino's husband, Paul Fedorovosky, who died in 1958, was a violinist with the Boston Symphony Orchestra. She is survived by a daughter, Irina Lasoff of St. Paul, and three grandchildren.

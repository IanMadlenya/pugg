        Olive Booth, the last of the surviving granddaughters of Gen. William Booth, founder of the Salvation Army, died on Wednesday. She was 98 years old.
        Miss Booth, who held the rank of lieutenant colonel in the evangelical and charitable organization, died in a home for the elderly.
        After World War II, she directed the Salvation Army's European relief staff, 150 people who worked with other church and relief organizations helping repatriate children displaced by war.
        She officially retired from Salvation Army work in 1951.

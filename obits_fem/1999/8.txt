
        Joan Engel Stern, a ballet dancer, died on Jan. 18 at her home in Manhattan. She was 78.
        The cause was cancer, the family said.
        Born Shirley Engel in Baltimore, she was known as Joan Engel when she became a professional dancer. In 1945, she became a founding member of a touring troupe formed by the British stars, Alicia Markova and Anton Dolin.
        Ms. Engel also appeared in Broadway shows and taught at the American Academy of Dramatic Art.
        She is survived by her husband, Irving; a son, Anthony Marc Benjamin, and a daughter, Lisa Stern Lasday, all of New York.


        Mathilda Terner, who engaged in philanthropy with her industrialist husband, Emmanuel, died on Wednesday at her home in Palm Beach, Fla. She was 88 and also maintained a home in Oakhurst, N.J.
        Together Mrs. Terner and her late husband made possible the Mathilda and Emmanuel M. Terner wing of St. Mary's Hospital in West Palm Beach, which opened in 1989.
        They also donated money to Lincoln Center, the Holocaust Museum, the United Jewish Appeal, the American Jewish Committee, New York Service for the Handicapped and Temple Emanuel-El in New York City.
        She is survived by three daughters, Nancy Behrman, Elaine Cooper and Carol Lederman; five grandchildren, and two great-grandchildren.

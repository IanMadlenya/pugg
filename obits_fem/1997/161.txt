
        Cathleen Cordell, an actress who appeared on Broadway, in Britain and in films and television, died on Aug. 19 at Cedars-Sinai Medical Center in Los Angeles. She was 81.
        Miss Cordell was born in Brooklyn and educated in England and France. She trained at the Royal Academy of Dramatic Art. She made her British stage debut in 1933 and joined the Hull Repertory Company as a juvenile lead.
        She made her Broadway debut in 1937 at the St. James Theater in ''Richard II'' and took her first film role the next year. She spent most of World War II in Britain, where she was first heard in radio dramas. She also appeared on screen in the original British version of ''Gaslight.''
        After the war, returning to the United States, she had roles in radio soap operas, on stage and in films, including ''M*A*S*H'' and ''Airport.'' She also played guest parts in many successful television series, including ''Perry Mason,'' ''Marcus Welby, M.D.,'' ''Dragnet'' and ''I Dream of Jeannie.''


        Beatrice Brown, the longtime music director and conductor of the Ridgefield Symphony Orchestra, died on Feb. 11 at Connecticut Hospice in Branford, Conn. She was 79,  and lived in Norwalk, Conn.
        Ms. Brown began her career as a violist and then became a student of the German conductor Hermann Scherchen. She worked in Europe for six years, conducting concerts in Naples, Berlin, Geneva and Brussels. She also studied with Leopold Stokowski and Serge Koussevitzky.
        Before coming to Ridgefield, Ms. Brown was music director and conductor of the Scranton Philharmonic (now the Northeastern Pennsylvania Philharmonic) for 10 years. She led the Ridgefield orchestra from 1970 to 1995.
        She is survived by her husband, Morris Rothenberg.


        Miriam Phillips, an actress on Broadway and in regional theaters for more than four decades, died on Oct. 24 at the Actors' Home in Englewood, N.J. She was 98.
        Ms. Phillips, a native of Philadelphia, began her career at the Hedgerow Theater in Rose Valley, Pa., in 1924. There she performed more than 120 roles in classic and modern plays, appearing as Lady Macbeth, as Mrs. Alving in Ibsen's ''Ghosts'' and as Shaw's Candida.
        She left the Hedgerow in 1956 and went on to act at the Alley Theater in Houston, Arena Stage in Washington and on Broadway, where she appeared in ''Waltz of the Toreadors'' and ''Filumena.'' Her television credits included the ''U.S. Steel Hour'' and ''As the World Turns.'' She also appeared in films, including ''Naked City,'' ''The Way We Live Now'' and ''Crossing Delancey.'' Her last role was in the Off Broadway play ''Daughters'' in 1986.
        She is survived by her companion, Paul Modes.

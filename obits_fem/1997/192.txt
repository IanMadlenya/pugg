
        Eleanor Doermann Larrabee, an architect who specialized in the construction and layout of libraries, died on Saturday at St. Vincent's Hospital and Medical Center in Manhattan. She was 74 and lived in Manhattan.
        She died of complications from injuries suffered when struck by a car two weeks ago, her family said.
        A Radcliffe College graduate, she received a master's degree in architecture from Columbia University. In 1955, she joined the architecture practice of Charles H. Warner Jr. of New York, and in 1963, she became an associate in WBTL Architects in Manhattan. She retired in 1992.
        Her husband, Eric Larrabee, an author and educator, died in 1990. She is survived by two brothers, John H. Doermann of Salem, Mass., and Humphrey Doermann of St. Paul.

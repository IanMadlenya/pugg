
        Murial Williams Hart, a Broadway actress of the 1930's and a soap-opera heroine of the 1950's and 60's, died on Thursday at York Hospital in York, Me. She was 80.
        The cause was cancer, said a stepdaughter, Frederica Hart.
        Miss Williams, as she was known professionally, appeared first on Broadway in 1934 in the Kaufman-Hart drama "Merrily We Roll Along." She also appeared in Jacques Deval's drama "Lorelei" in 1938.
        Later she had parts in Off Broadway productions and road-company tours. Her television credits include "The Brighter Day," "Another World" and "As the World Turns."
        In addition to her stepdaughter, of Ogunquit, Me., she is survived by a brother, G. Robert Williams Jr. of Melville, L.I.; a sister, Harriet Gilcreast of Bedford, Mass., and a stepson, Francis R. Hart 3d of Hingham, Mass.


        Amelia Opdyke Jones, an artist who made the "Subway Sun" posters for the New York City Transit Authority for 20 years, died on Thursday at her home in Sea Girt, N.J. She was 80.
        The cause was respiratory complications, said her son, William.
        Mrs. Jones's drawings, which she signed "Oppy," were first published in Life magazine in the 1930's. She also drew a syndicated comic strip, "The Young Idear.
        The "Subway Sun" posters, which appeared in subway cars and buses in New York City in the 1940's and 50's, portrayed places of interest and promoted good behavior by users of the transit system.
        Mrs. Jones's husband, William J. Jones, died in 1965.
        In addition to her son, of Sea Girt, she is survived by a daughter, Margaretta J. Friday of Weston, Conn., four grandchildren and a great-grandchild.

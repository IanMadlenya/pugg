
        Linda L. Ellenbogen, who was the first woman lawyer in the law department of the New York State Supreme Court in Westchester County, died on Thursday at Rosary Hill Nursing Home in Hawthorne, N.Y. She was 43 years old and a resident of White Plains.
        She died of a brain tumor, her family said.
        Born in the Bronx, Ms. Ellenbogen grew up in Queens and graduated from Queens Colege and St. John's Law School. She began working for the State Appellate Division Court in Brooklyn in 1976, researching and drafting opinions, before moving to the state court in White Plains in 1982. While there, she headed the department for motion hearings. She also served as an officer and director of the Westchester Women's Bar Association.
        She worked despite beign afflicted with multiple sclerosis in 1985 and continued part time after her brain tumor was diagnosed two years ago.
        Surviving are her husband, Milton; a son, Michael, and a daughter, Eve, both of White Plains; her father, Abraham Letich of Queens; two sisters, Davida Luttenberg of New Hyde Park, L.I., and Judith Kaplan of Ocala, Fla., and a brother, Larry Letich of Verona, N.J.

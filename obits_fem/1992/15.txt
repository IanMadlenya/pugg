
        Dr. Eileen Halsey Pike, a professor emeritus of microbiology at New York Medical College in Valhalla, N.Y., died on Jan. 7 at the Ruth Taylor Institute, a nursing home in Mount Pleasant, N.Y. She was 73 years old and lived in White Plains.
        The cause of death was listed as emphysema, said Penelope W. Morgan, her executor.
        Dr. Pike, a native of London, was a graduate of Hunter College and Tulane University with a Ph.D. from Columbia University. She joined the faculty of New York Medical College in 1964 as an associate professor of parasitology and became a full professor of microbiology in 1976. She reached emeritus status in 1981 but remained as director of the interdisciplinary program in the college's Graduate School of Basic Medical Sciences until 1988.
        She is survived by a nephew, Richard K. B. Halsey of London.

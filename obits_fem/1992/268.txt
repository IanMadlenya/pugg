
        Patricia Phipps Buckley, a psychotherapist practicing in Manhattan and Queens, died in an accident on Aug. 30 in Southington, Conn. She was 61 years old and lived in Forest Hills, Queens.
        A police report said she died of multiple injuries received in a fall while on a hiking trip.
        Ms. Buckley, a native of Rockville Center, L.I., graduated in nursing and psychology from Adelphi University. She held an M.S. degree in counseling and psychology from Miami University and did postgraduate work in vocational rehabilitation and psychology at Columbia University.
        She was a former director of the Manhattan Center for the Living, a voluntary nonprofit organization for people with chronic illnesses. She was also a member of the board of directors for Unicef in New York.
        In addition to her practice, Ms. Buckley was involved with Catholic Charities, making home visits to care for patients terminally ill with AIDS-related diseases. She was also an active member of the Daughters of the American Revolution.
        Ms. Buckley is survived by her son, Thomas L. Thron of San Francisco, and her fiance, Herbert Boyer of Manhattan.

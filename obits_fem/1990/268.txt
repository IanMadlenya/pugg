        Marjorie M. Bitker, a writer and former trustee of Barnard College, died on Tuesday at Columbia Hospital in Milwaukee. She was 89 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mrs. Bitker died of congestive heart failure, a spokeswoman for Barnard said.
        Mrs. Bitker was a Barnard trustee from 1964 to 1968.
        Under her maiden name, Marjorie Marks, she published short stories and poetry in The New Yorker, The American Mercury, Parents Magazine and other publications. She had been an editor from the mid-1940's to the mid-1950's with Farrar, Straus & Company, G. P. Putnam's Sons and the David McKay Company.
        Mrs. Bitker, a native New Yorker, graduated in 1921 from Barnard, where she was elected to Phi Beta Kappa.
        Her husband, Bruno V. Bitker, a lawyer, died in 1984.
        She is survived by three daughters, Emilie J. Jacobi of Manhattan, Margaret Jacobson of Mountain View, Calif., and Elizabeth Reiss of Orange, Conn.; nine grandchildren, and four great-grandchildren.

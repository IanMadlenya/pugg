        Marcia R. Richman, a lawyer who specialized in juvenile justice, died of cancer on Monday at her home in Short Hills, N.J. She was 55 years old.
        Mrs. Richman began her legal career in the New Jersey Public Defender's Office in 1967 after graduating from Seton Hall Law School. She became head of the first statewide Juvenile Defender's Office in 1969 and helped draft the state's juvenile justice code in the early 1970's.
        In 1977, she became assistant commissioner of the New Jersey Department of the Public Advocate and later supervised its juvenile program. Most recently she represented youngsters in child-abuse and child-advocacy cases.
        She is survived by her husband, Stanley M. Richman; two daughters, Nancy, of Port Washington, L.I., and Deborah, of Morristown, N.J.; and her mother, Belle Rosenberg of South Orange, N.J.

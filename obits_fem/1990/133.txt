        M. Steffy Browne, professor emeritus of economics at Brooklyn College, died of heart failure Friday at New York Hospital. She was 91 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Dr. Browne, a native of Vienna, came to the United States in 1940 and established as a specialist in economic theory. She was the author of ''Governmental Economic Theory,'' a textbook published in Vienna in 1929.
        In 1947 she joined the faculty at Brooklyn College and received tenure in 1962. She retired in 1969 but continued to teach at New York University's School of Continuing Education until 1981.
        She is survived by a daughter, Dorothy Shaffer of Stamford, Conn.; a son, Clement, of Toronto and Lewiston, N.Y.; a sister, Kitty C. Margulies of Manhattan, five grandchildren and two great-grandchildren.

        Pauline Lichtenstein, former director of research and planning at Hofstra University, died August 4 at home in Manhattan. She was 65 years old.
        Mrs. Lichtenstein died of cancer, a spokesman for the family said.
        She joined the staff at the university in 1966 as a research associate in the Center for the Study of Higher Education. She retired in 1986. Earlier in her career, she had been a statistical consultant, and a statistician with the Industrial Commodity Corporation in Manhattan.
        A native New Yorker, Mrs. Lichtenstein graduated with a bachelor of business administration degree cum laude, from the City College of New York. She also studied mathematical statistics at Columbia University. She received a master's degree in psychology from Hofstra in 1966.
        She is survived by her husband, Dr. Hyman Lichtenstein; two daughters, Carolyn and Diane; her mother; a sister, and two grandchildren.

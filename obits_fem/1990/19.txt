        Dr. Isabel Beck, a gynecologist and a pioneer in the field of family planning, died Tuesday at her home in New Rochelle, N.Y. She was 91 years old.
        She was a 1923 graduate of the Columbia University College of Physicians and Surgeons and was a member of its second class to admit women. When she interned at Mount Sinai Hospital, she was its only female intern. She was a founder of Planned Parenthood of Southern Westchester in the early 1930's and worked closely in Manhattan with Margaret Sanger, primary founder of Planned Parenthood.
        Dr. Beck was medical director of Planned Parenthood's clinic in Mount Vernon, N.Y., from 1935 to 1969. She also maintained a private practice in New York City and New Rochelle. She retired in 1986.
        She is survived by a daughter, Barbara Neumann of Paramus, N.J.; two grandsons and one great-grandson.

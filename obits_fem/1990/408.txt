
        Dr. Margaret W. Poe, a retired dentist who also held a medical degree, died on Saturday at Humana Bennett Hospital in Plantation, Fla. She was 93 years old and lived in Plantation.
        She died of congestive heart failure, said Martha Karlin, a friend from Sunrise, Fla.
        Dr. Poe came to the United States from Vienna in 1927. She was a graduate of the University of Vienna, with degrees in medicine and dentistry. She started a practice in Manhattan as a dental surgeon, sharing offices with her husband, the late Dr. David L. Poe, an otolaryngologist. Among her clients were celebrities like Lillian Gish, Eugene Ormandy and Van Johnson,
        Her husband died in 1960. Dr. Poe retired in 1972 and moved to Florida from Manhattan in 1979.
        She is survived by a sister, Louise Pollak of Manhattan.

        Janet Hill Gordon, a lawyer and former Republican State Senator from Chenango County, died on Monday at the Upstate Medical Center in Syracuse. She was 75 years old and lived in Norwich, N.Y.
        She died of a heart attack, her daughter, Gail, said.
        Mrs. Gordon began her legislative career in 1946 as an Assemblywoman, one of the youngest ever to be elected to the New York State Assembly. She ran for the State Senate in 1958 and began serving the first of two terms in 1959. She was defeated in her bid for a third term.
        She was active in a wide range of legislative efforts in family law and adoption as chairman of the Joint Legislative Committee on Matrimonial and Family Law from 1955 to 1962. She was also a member of the temporary State Commission to Recodify the Family Court Act from 1979 to 1984.
        In 1950, she became the first woman to give birth to a child while serving in the State Legislature.
        Held Conciliation Post
        Mrs. Gordon was a delegate to the New York State Constitutional Convention in 1967 and was conciliation commissioner for the Sixth Judicial District of the State Conciliation Bureau from 1967 to 1973. In 1970, she was a delegate to the White House Conference on Children.
        Mrs. Gordon maintained a private law practice in Norwich for 50 years and was semiretired at her death.
        She was born in Manhattan and graduated from Syracuse University and Brooklyn Law School, magna cum laude.
        Her husband, William J. Gordon, a Chenango County judge and surrogate, died in 1975.
        Besides her daughter, Gail Hill Gordon McCale of Slingerlands, N.Y., Mrs. Gordon is survived by a grandson.

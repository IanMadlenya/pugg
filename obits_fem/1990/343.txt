        Denise Carty-Bennia, a lawyer and visiting professor at the City University of New York Law School at Queens College, died on Sept. 11 at her home in Detroit. She was 43 years old.
        The Detroit police said Mrs. Carty-Bennia committed suicide.
        She had been on leave from Northeastern University Law School in Boston for the last two years.
        Mrs. Carty-Bennia was co-founder of the National Center for Fair and Open Testing in Cambridge, Mass., in 1985.
        She was a graduate of Barnard College and received a law degree from Columbia University Law School in 1973. From 1979 to 1980, she was a Charles E. Revson Fellow at City College.
        She is survived by her husband, Warren Bennia; her mother, Sylvia Carty of St. Paul, Minn., and two sisters, Celeste Carty of St. Paul, Minn., and Jacqueline Carty of New York City.

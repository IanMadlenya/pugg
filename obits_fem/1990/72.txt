        Julie V. Marsteller, dean of disabled students at Barnard College, died of complications resulting from muscular dystrophy yesterday at the St. Luke's-Roosevelt Hospital Center in Manhattan. She was 46 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Dean Marsteller was discovered to have muscular dystropy at the age of 11. She graduated from Barnard College in 1969 and served as college archivist before becoming a dean in 1979.
        She was the recipient of the 1980 Ruth Kurzon Group for Handicapped Children Humanitarian Award and the Handicapped Professional Woman of the Year Award from Pilot International, a women's service organization. In 1986 she was inducted into the National Hall of Fame for Persons With Disabilities and this year was selected to receive Barnard's Medal of Distinction.
        She is survived by her mother, Gloria Marsteller of Palm Beach, Fla., and a sister, Elizabeth A. Gordon of San Francisco.

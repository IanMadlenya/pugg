        Mary Funnye Goldson, an associate professor at the Columbia University School of Social Work and a specialist in developing programs for families and children, died of cancer on Friday at St. Luke's-Roosevelt Hospital Center. She was 55 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mrs. Goldson joined the faculty at Columbia in 1969. From 1975 to 1979, she directed the Clark Foundation Project on Permanency, which trained social workers to deal with hard-to-place children. From 1979 to 1982, she was principal investigator of the Adoption Resource Center and Child Welfare Center for Region 2, covering New York, New Jersey, Puerto Rico and the Virgin Islands.
        She was born in Birmingham, Ala., and received a master's degree from the Western Reserve University School of Applied Social Science and an undergraduate degree from Bennett College. She was a vice president of the board of Louise Wise Services and a board member of Edwin Gould Services for Children.
        Mrs. Goldson is survived by two sons from an earlier marriage, Clarence and Bradley Funnye of Manhattan; her mother, Gladys Ensley of Birmingham; two brothers, John and Charles Ensley of Manhattan, and a sister, Jean Ensley, also of Manhattan.

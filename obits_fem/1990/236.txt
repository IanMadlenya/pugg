        Neta Lohnes Frazier, an award-winning writer, died Saturday. She was 100 years old.
        Of her 14 books for young readers published between 1947 and 1973, four received awards from the Junior Literary Guild.
        Most of her works were based on Pacific Northwest history. They included ''Stout-Hearted Seven,'' ''The Magic Ring,'' ''Secret Friend,'' ''My Love Is a Gypsy'' and ''Little Rhody.''

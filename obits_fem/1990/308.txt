        Alice Pentlarge Loeb, a writer and producer, died Friday at her home in Baltimore. She was 95 years old.
        She died of natural causes, said her daughter, Frances Kleeman of Baltimore.
        Under her maiden name, she wrote a biography, ''The Reminiscences of a Spanish Diplomat'' (1933).
        In 1934, she joined the staff of the radio station WQXR, where she produced two programs, a review of New York cultural events and a program for inventors. From 1952 to 1956, when she retired, she was radio and television director of ''America's Town Meeting of the Air.''
        In addition to Ms. Kleeman, she is survived by another daughter, Elizabeth Frank of Baltimore; a son, Richard P. Kleeman of Washington; eight grandchildren, and 12 great-grandchildren.

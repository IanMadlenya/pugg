
       Mary Ellen McAdam Byrn, a senior banker in the Institutional Recovery Management Unit of Citibank, died on Monday at her home in Red Hook, N.Y. She was 45 years old and also lived in Manhattan.
        Before joining Citibank in 1982, she was an economist with the First National Bank of Boston, now the Bank of Boston.
        Ms. Byrn graduated from Wellesley College and received a degree in 1974 from the Harvard Business School, where she was a Loeb Rhodes Fellow.
        Her companion for many years was Joseph A. DeBellis.
        She is survived by her parents, Robert and Jane Weeks McAdam of Hockeffin, Del.; and two sisters, Barbara Muller of Columbus, Ohio, and Nancy Fleming of Weston, Mass.

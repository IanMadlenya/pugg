        Dr. Eleanor Alexander Jackson, a research scientist in medical bacteriology, died on Tuesday at Lenox Hill Hospital after a short illness. She was 85 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        She was awarded the A. Cressy Morrison Prize by the New York Academy of Sciences for her discovery of certain forms of the tubercle bacillus, using a staining technique she developed.
        Dr. Jackson, who was born in Manhattan, graduated from Wellesley College and received a master's degree from Columbia and a Ph.D. from New York University.
        She is survived by a son, Dr. T. A. Jackson of Saskatoon, Saskatchewan, and two grandsons.

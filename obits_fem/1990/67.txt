        Elizabeth Bryan Patten, a Chattanooga philanthropist and civic leader, died Thursday at Memorial Hospital in Chattanooga. She was 82 years old.
        Mrs. Patten started the Tennessee Conference on Historic Preservation. In 1967 she was elected to the board of the National Trust for Historic Preservation, a group established by Congress to preserve historic houses. She was a board member for 10 years.
        Survivors include two daughters, Sarah P. Gwynn of Atlanta and Emmy P. Casey of Chattanooga, two sons, D. Carter Patten 3d and W. A Bryan Patten of Chattanooga, a sister and 11 grandchildren.

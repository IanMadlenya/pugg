        Esther V. Foley, the director of the women's athletic program at New York University from 1951 to 1971, died on Monday at her home in Katonah, N.Y., after a long illness. She was 85 years old.
        Miss Foley, who won the women's national amateur platform diving championship as an undergraduate in 1927, was a coach of many women's sports teams at N.Y.U. from 1929 until her retirement in 1971. They included field hockey, swimming and diving, basketball, volleyball and tennis.
        In 1976 she was inducted into the N.Y.U. Women's Athletic Hall of Fame.
        She was born in Sanborn, Iowa, and studied at Northwestern University before earning a bachelor's degree from N.Y.U. in 1928.
        She left no survivors.

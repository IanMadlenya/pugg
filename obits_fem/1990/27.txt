        S. Fannie Simon, a retired teacher and labor leader, died of a ruptured appendix Wednesday at St. Luke's-Roosevelt Hospital Center in Manhattan. She was 88 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Miss Simon, who came to the United States from Poland in 1913, taught economics at James Monroe High School in the Bronx for 30 years before retiring in 1962.
        She was a founder of the Teachers Guild, the United Federation of Teachers and the Retired Teachers Chapter of the federation.
        Miss Simon, a 1923 graduate of Cornell University, received a master's from Columbia University.
        She is survived by her sister, Diana S. Granof of Bethesda, Md.

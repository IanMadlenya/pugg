        Helen Steele Reece, founder and director of the Reece School, a private elementary school for children with emotional and learning disabilities, died in her sleep on Monday at her home in Manhattan. She was 99 years old.
        She founded the school in 1948 in a brownstone at 180 East 93d Street, using small classes with more teachers than usual to create a supportive setting. The school was also a training center for teachers in the field.
        Mrs. Reece, a native of Hillsboro, Ohio, graduated from the Teachers College of Columbia University. She began as a teacher at the City & Country School in Manhattan, later becoming director of Rosemary Hall, a private school in Greenwich, Conn. She was a former director of the Downtown Community School in Manhattan.
        There are no immediate survivors.
        Correction:  January 17, 1990, Wednesday, Late Edition - Final
        An obituary on Thursday about the founder of the Reece School in Manhattan, a private school for children with emotional and learning disabilities, misidentified her. She was Ellen Steele Reece.

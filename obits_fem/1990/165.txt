        Margaret T. Hance, a supporter of business who presided over eight years of explosive growth as Phoenix's first female Mayor, died of cancer on Sunday. She was 66 years old.
        Mrs. Hance, a Republican, was elected in November 1975 and served four two-year terms before retiring in 1983.
        She was a national co-chairwoman of President Ronald Reagan's 1984 re-election campaign. In 1988 she directed George Bush's Presidential campaign in Arizona.
        Mr. Reagan appointed Mrs. Hance to the Advisory Commission on Intergovernmental Relations and the Presidential Federalism Advisory Committee. She also was a trustee of the United Conference of Mayors, a director of the National League of Cities and a former president of the National Conference of Republican Mayors.
        Born in Spirit Lake, Iowa, Mrs. Hance had lived in Arizona since 1926. She was a graduate of Scripps College in Claremont, Calif.
        Survivors include three sons, Richard, Galen and Ted.

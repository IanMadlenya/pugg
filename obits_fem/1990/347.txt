
       Elaine Penn, an opera coach and accompanist who worked with many principal singers at the Metropolitan Opera and the New York City Opera, died at St. Luke's-Roosevelt Hospital on Aug. 28. She was 73 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Her companion, Ellen Alexander, said she died of a brain hemorrhage.
        Born in Brookhaven, Miss., Miss Penn studied at Louisiana State University in Baton Rouge and at Columbia University. She also studied with the pianists Mieczyslaw Munz at the Juilliard School and Harold Bauer at the Manhattan School of Music.
        Miss Penn was principal opera coach for the American Institute of Musical Studies in Graz, Austria; the International Institute of Vocal Arts in Tampa, Fla., and the Musicians' Club of America's summer workshops in Franklin, N.C. She also did private coaching in her home.
        Among the singers Miss Penn coached and accompanied in recitals are the mezzo-sopranos Regina Resnick and Mignon Dunn, the soprano Barbara Daniels and the baritone Robert Hale.
        There are no immediate survivors.

        Sister Carol Brody, director of the Weekend College at Marymount College in Tarrytown, N.Y., died on Tuesday at the Montefiore Medical Center in the Bronx after a brief illness. She was 58 years old and lived at the college.
        Sister Brody, a native New Yorker, entered the religious order of Sacred Heart of Mary in 1947. Before going to Tarrytown, she worked in schools in California for nearly 30 years, the last nine as academic dean at Marymount College in Palos Verdes, Calif. She was a graduate of Marymount College in Los Angeles and received a master's degree in history from the University of Southern California.
        She is survived by four sisters, Marian Pagano and Kathleen Brody, both of Clifton, N.J., Janice Di Giacomo of Freeport, L.I., and Peggy Grembowiec of Saddle Brook, N.J., and two brothers, Gerald, of Brooklyn, and Edwin, of Lindenhurst, L.I.

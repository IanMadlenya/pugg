        Anne R. Izard, a retired children's librarian in Westchester County, died of complications from cancer on Monday at her home in Gwynedd, Pa. She was 74 years old.
        Miss Izard was a consultant for the Westchester Library System, which has more than 40 branches, for nearly 20 years. She led classes, workshops and monthly meetings for librarians in book reviewing, storytelling and book collections.
        She headed the children's services division of the American Library Association and was former chairwoman of its Newbery-Caldecott committee.
        She began her career at the New York Public Library and also worked in Greenwich, Conn., and Mount Vernon, N.Y., before going to Westchester in 1959. Miss Izard retired in 1976. The Board of Regents gave her the James E. Allen Jr. Memorial Award for distinguished service to education.
        She graduated from Duke University in 1937 and received an M.A. from Simmons College in 1940.
        There are no immediate survivors.

        Dr. Helen L. Miller, an obstetrician and a gynecologist in New York and New Jersey, died on June 18 at her home in Kinnelon, N.J. She was 93 years old.
        Dr. Miller had a practice in Manhattan from 1927 to 1978. Later she started a practice in Kinnelon, in which she worked until 1987. She was also associated with the New York Infirmary and Doctors' Hospital.
        She was a past president of the Women's Medical Society of New York State which named her Woman of the Year in 1965.
        In 1931, Dr. Miller bought a former schoolhouse in Kinnelon for a retreat. In 1965, she built a medical center on the site to attract young doctors.
        Dr. Miller was born in Corvallis, Ore. She graduated from Oregon State University in 1917 and the Oregon Medical School in 1926. In the early 30's, the New York City Health Department selected her to establish one of the first prenatal clinics in a settlement house.
        There are no immediate survivors.

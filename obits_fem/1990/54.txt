        Vera Hahn, an interior-design editor for several magazines, died of lymphoma on Tuesday at New York Hospital. She was 67 years old and lived on Roosevelt Island.
        Mrs. Hahn had been interior-design editor of Home Funishings Daily, American Home Magazine, Women's World and Parents Magazine. She also worked for European periodicals like Architecture and Women and Maison Francaise. Her consulting clients included the American and European divisions of the Du Pont Company and the Century Furniture Company. She retired in 1987.
        She was also president of the Roosevelt Island Historical Society and was on the boards of the Good Shepherd Corporation, Goldwater Hospital and the Roosevelt Island Youth Center. She helped establish a shelter for the homeless at the Good Shepherd Chapel.
        Mrs. Hahn was born in Vienna. She was an alumna of the Parsons School of Design.
        A widow, she is survived by a stepson, Thomas, of New London, Conn.; a stepdaughter, Katherine Way of Stonington, Conn., and a granddaughter.

        Marcia Dawkins Nauckhoff, a Peace Corps official and a former executive with Chase Manhattan Bank, died of breast cancer on Monday at her home in Washington. She was 49 years old.
        With the Peace Corps, Ms. Nauckhoff was director of the office of private sector relations. She helped develop the agency's Leadership for Peace campaign, which sought to encourage more Americans to be involved in the work of the Peace Corps.
        From 1982 to 1985 she was second vice president and corporate responsibilities officer for Chase Manhattan in Manhattan. From 1980 to 1982 she was director of the foreign investment development program for the New York Governor's Council on International Business. Earlier she was a member of the New York City Commission for the United Nations and Consular Corps, and an aide to Mayor John V. Lindsay.
        She was born in Bayonne, N.J., and graduated from Rosemont College.
        There are no immediate survivors.

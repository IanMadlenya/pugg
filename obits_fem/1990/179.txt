        Mae Sien Hong, a former press agent for the Ringling Brothers and Barnum & Bailey Circus and the Harlem Globe Trotters, died of cancer on May 4 at Memorial Sloan-Kettering Cancer Center. She was 75 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mrs. Hong, a New Yorker, began her career in 1952 with the Mills Brothers Circus in Ohio. She was the first woman to represent Ringling Brothers, and later worked with the Clyde Beatty Circus.
        From 1963 until 1978, Mrs. Hong represented touring Broadway shows, including productions of ''Carnival,'' ''My Fair Lady,'' ''Sweet Charity'' and ''Chicago.''
        She is survived by her husband, Edward.

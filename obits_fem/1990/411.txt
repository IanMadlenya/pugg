
        Jo-Ann M. Cox, a partner in the New York accounting firm Ernst & Young, died at her home in Manhattan on Thursday. She was 45 years old and also lived in Sea Bright, N.J.
        Ms. Cox died of cancer, a sister, Cynthia Schmidl, said.
        Ms. Cox was born in Newark and raised in Union, N.J., where she attended grammar and high schools. She received a bachelor's degree in accounting at Fairleigh Dickinson University in 1967 and later earned a master's degree in international finance from Pace University.
        She joined Ernst & Young about 20 years ago and spent her entire career with the company, specializing in auditing. She became a partner in 1985. Ms. Cox was also a director of Inwood House, a home for unwed mothers in New York City.
        Ms. Cox is survived by her mother, Olga Wagner, of Union, and two sisters, Sonia Wagner, of Brigantine, N.J., and Mrs. Schmidl, of Middletown, N.J.

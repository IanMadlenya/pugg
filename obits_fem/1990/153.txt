        Alice Lake, a writer whose specialty was health and education issues, died Wednesday at New York Hospital from complications following surgery for lung cancer. She was 73 years old and lived in Harrison, N.Y.
        Ms. Lake was the author of ''Our Own Years: What Women over 35 Should Know About Themselves'' (Random House, 1979) and was also a contributor to McCall's, Ladies' Home Journal and Redbook magazines.
        She was a graduate of Vassar College and a member of Phi Beta Kappa.
        Ms. Lake is survived by her husband, Leonard Lake; a daughter, Ellen, of Oakland, Calif.; a sister, Dorothy Sterling of Wellfleet, Mass., and two grandchildren.

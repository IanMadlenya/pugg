        Helen Drees Ruttencutter, a musician and a writer for The New Yorker magazine, died on Friday at her home in Manhattan at the age of 63. Her family said she died of a brain tumor.
        Mrs. Ruttencutter, a native of Carroll, Iowa, held a degree in music from the University of Iowa. A violinist, she toured with a string orchestra before joining The New Yorker in 1950. Articles she wrote on the Guarneri Quartet, the conductor Andre Previn and a young pianist, Robin McCabe, were expanded into books.
        She was married to the illustrator James Ruttencutter, who died in 1986. She is survived by a sister, Betty Fife of Salt Lake City; a brother, Joseph Drees of Saratoga, Calif., and a stepdaughter, Eva Brock of Tacoma, Wash.

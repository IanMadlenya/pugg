        Mildred Webber, a talent scout who helped to begin the careers of Gregory Peck, Carol Channing, Butterfly McQueen, Arthur Kennedy and many other screen and stage stars, died of heart disease on Tuesday at New York Hospital. She was 86 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Between 1928 and 1945, Miss Webber worked for Warner Brothers and the William Morris Agency. She later started her own agency. A friend, Claire Lane, said Miss Webber's discoveries also included Sidney Lumet, Betty Hutton, Lee J. Cobb, Celeste Holm, Garson Kanin, Karl Malden, Gary Merrill, Edmund O'Brien and Ruth Hussey.
        There are no survivors.

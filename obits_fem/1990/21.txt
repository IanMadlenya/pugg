        Marie Vale, a Republican fund raiser and a former government aide, died of leukemia on Tuesday at the New York University Medical Center. She was 46 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Ms. Vale was deputy public affairs director of the National Aeronautics and Space Administration and public affairs director and Congressional liaison aide of the President's Commission on the Environment from 1982 to 1987.
        She had been a fund raiser for the National Republican Party and for New York State Republicans. Ms. Vale had also run unsuccessfully for the New York State Assembly.
        She graduated from Florida State University in 1966.
        At her death, Ms. Vale led Vale Consultants, a public relations business.
        Surviving are two aunts, Gertrude Boyle of New Britain, Conn., and Alice D. Villers of Orlando, Fla.; and an uncle, William P. Downey of New Hampton, N.Y.

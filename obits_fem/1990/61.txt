        Marion K. Morgan, an interior designer who specialized in the decorative arts of the 18th century, died Jan. 21 at her home in Manhattan after a long illness. She was 84 years old.
        For 40 years, Mrs. Morgan was associated with McMillan Inc., one of New York City's oldest decorating concerns. Her clients included Marjorie Merriweather Post, Henry Ford 2d, Claus von Bulow and Salomon Brothers. She retired in 1981.
        Mrs. Morgan was born in Englewood, N.J., and graduated from the New York School of Fine and Applied Art, now the Parsons School of Design in Manhattan. She also studied at the school's Paris branch.
        Her husband, Urban Morgan, also an interior designer, died in the early 1980's. There are no immediate survivors.
        Correction:  February 12, 1990, Monday, Late Edition - Final
        An obituary on Wednesday about Marion K. Morgan, an interior designer, misidentified the Manhattan concern that employed her. It is McMillen Inc.

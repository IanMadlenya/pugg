        Dorothy Gillam Baker, a scholar and educator who researched patterns of development in world history, died on Aug. 3 at Albert Einstein Hospital in the Bronx. She was 84 years old and lived in the Bronx.
        She died of congestive heart failure, her niece, Mary Jane Schaffer, said.
        Dr. Baker taught at City College, at Long Island University and at the graduate division of Hunter College. She traveled extensively and was a speaker at the World Citizens Assembly in Innsbruck, Austria, in 1976 and in Paris in 1977.
        She was born in Saratoga, N.Y., and graduated from Hunter College. She received a master's degree from the University of California at Los Angeles and a Ph.D. from New York University.
        A widow, she is survived by her sister, Margaret Brinckerhoff of Old Greenwich, Conn.

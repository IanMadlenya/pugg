        Lee Grape, who was active in educational and recreational programs in Fairfield, Conn., died on Wednesday at her home there. She was 73 years old.
        Mrs. Grape died of lymphoma, a son said.
        In 1972 Mrs. Grape began the ''Arts for Youth'' program in the Fairfield schools, where she also headed the ''Save Our Sports'' campaign. She was a former chairwoman of the Fairfield Board of Recreation.
        She is survived by her husband, Dudley H. Grape; two sons, Peter, of Wellesley, Mass., and Thomas, of Boston; two daughters, Judy Barnett of Darien, Conn., and Dee Karlen of Fairfield; a brother, George A. Oldham Jr. of Peoria, Ariz.; a sister, Lorraine Garger of Golden, Colo., and eight granchildren.

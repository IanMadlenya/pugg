
        Elinor Remick Warren, who composed more than 200 works for orchestra and voice, died at her home on Saturday. She was 91 years old.
        Miss Warren was born in Los Angeles, attended Mills College, and studied with musicians in New York and Paris. Her first choral-orchestral work, "The Harp Weaver," was introduced at Carnegie Hall in 1936. Her "Legend of King Arthur" was first performed by the Los Angeles Philharmonic in 1940.
        Miss Warren's husband, Z. Wayne Griffin, a film producer, died in 1981. She is survived by a daughter, two sons and five grandchildren.

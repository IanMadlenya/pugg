
        Marion Vannett Ridgway, who served as an agent for artists and writers for more than 40 years, died on Saturday at her home in Southbury, Conn. She was 88 years old.
        She died of pneumonia and vascular disease, said Elizabeth Park, a friend of more than 20 years.
        Ms. Ridgway graduated from the Halstead School in Yonkers and studied art and design in Manhattan. For 40 years she operated Marion Ridgway, artist representative, at 299 Madison Avenue in Manhattan and represented commerical artists, photographers and writers for publishers and advertising concerns.
        Early in her career, she wrote 11 books for children.
        There are no immediate survivors.

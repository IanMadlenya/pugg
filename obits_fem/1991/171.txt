
        Irene Samuel, an authority on the poet John Milton and a professor emeritus of English at Hunter College, died on Thursday at her home in Chapel Hill, N.C. She was 76 years old.
        She died of lung cancer, a family spokesman said.
        Dr. Samuel was educated at Cornell University, earning a B.A. in 1935, an M.A. in 1936 and a Ph.D. in 1940. She was an English instructor at Cornell University and at Queens College before joining the faculty of Hunter College in 1944.
        She became an assistant professor in 1949, associate professor in 1962 and professor in 1965. She was named professor emeritus at her retirement in 1971 and continued as a visiting professor at universities across the country and in Canada until 1976. She was the author of several books, including "Plato and Milton," and "Dante and Milton: 'The Commedia' and 'Paradise Lost,' " both published by Cornell University Press.
        She was a former president of the Milton Society of America and in 1972 was named an honorary scholar by the group. She was also a member of the Modern Language Association of America and the Academy of Literary Studies.
        There are no known survivors.

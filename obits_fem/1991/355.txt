
        Madeleine Martin, an artist and founder of the Peter and Madeleine Martin Foundation for the Creative Arts, died on Saturday in San Francisco. She was 69 years old and had homes in San Francisco and Fair Harbor, L.I.
        She died of liver disease, her brother, Nelson Doubleday, said.
        Mrs. Martin, a daughter of Ellen Doubleday and Atwood Violett, was born in New York City. She and her husband, Peter, who died in 1988, operated the New Yorker Bookstore, an Upper West Side institution that closed in 1982.
        Mrs. Martin became an artist after serving in the Waves in World War II and after attending the California School of Fine Arts, where she studied under Hassell Smith. In 1990 she founded the arts foundation, which recognizes and rewards artists.
        In addition to Mr. Doubleday, who lives in Hobe Sound, Fla., and Locust Valley, L.I., she is survived by a daughter, Lavinia Dimond of Ranchos de Taos, N.M.; two sisters, Ellen Violett of Manhattan, and Neltje, of Banner, Wyo.; two granddaughters, and two great-grandchildren.


        Ruth Frankenthaler Feldon, the first woman to become a trustee of Congregation Emanu-El in Manhattan, died on Tuesday at her home in Manhattan. She was 73 years old.
        She died of cancer, her daughter said.
        Mrs. Feldon was named a trustee of Congregation Emanu-El in May 1957 and remained in the post until 1967.
        She was born in Manhattan, the daughter of George Frankenthaler, a State Supreme Court justice, and Marion Hendricks Frankenthaler. She graduated from Vassar College in 1939.
        Her first husband, Richard Lewisohn Jr., died in 1976, and her second husband, Richard Feldon, died in 1988.
        She is survived by three children from her first marriage: a daughter, Aileen Godsick of Manhattan, and two sons, Richard Lewisohn 3d of Manhattan and Clifford Lewisohn of Teaneck, N.J.; a sister, Anne F. Kohn of Manhattan, and seven grandchildren.

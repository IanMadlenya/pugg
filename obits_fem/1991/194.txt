
        Dr. Marjorie Harley, a psychoanalyst who specialized in treating children and adolescents, died Wednesday at her home in Baltimore. She was 77 years old.
        She died of a brain tumor, an associate said.
        Dr. Harley, who was born in New York City, received a bachelor's degree at Barnard College and master's and doctoral degrees at Columbia University. After moving to Baltimore in 1965 she took part in training programs at the Baltimore Institute of Psychoanalysis. She taught psychology, pediatrics and psychoanalytic psychology at Johns Hopkins University.
        She was the widow of Dr. Russell Anderson, a psychoanalyst. There are no immediate survivors.

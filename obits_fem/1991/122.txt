
        Sadie H. Zaidens, a dermatologist and psychiatrist, died on April 1 at St. Clare's Hospital and Medical Center. She was 80 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        She died of intestinal bleeding and complications of diabetes, her daughter, Dr. Debra Brand, said.
        Dr. Zaidens was first a dermatologist and then became a psychiatrist. She wrote articles on the psychosomatic aspects of dermatology.
        She was a native of Manhattan and a graduate of George Washington University and the university's medical school. She studied and practiced with her husband, Morris A. Brand, the medical director of the Sidney Hillman Health Center, who died in 1968.
        Besides her daughter, who lives in Farmington, Conn., Dr. Zaidens is survived by a sister, Isabel Levin of Manhattan.


        Margaret Kalil, a soprano who sang with the Metropolitan Opera, died on Thursday at the Royal Victoria Hospital in Montreal. She was 66 years old and lived in Montreal.
        She died of pulmonary disease, her niece, Barbara Wright, said.
        Miss Kalil was a soloist with the Metropolitan Opera from 1965 to 1973. She also sang with the New York Philharmonic and other orchestras.
        She was a voice teacher at McGill University in Montreal.
        Miss Kalil was born in Monroe, La. She graduated from Columbia University, received a master's degree from the Juilliard School in 1960 and continued her studies at the University of Milan in Italy.
        She is survived by three sisters: Frances Shashy of Ocala, Fla., and Dolores Nomey and Gloria Harrison of Monroe, La.

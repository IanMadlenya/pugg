
        Dorothy M. Dunbar, creative director of Time-Life Home Video, died on Saturday at her home in Manhattan. She was 58 years old.
        She died of cancer, her husband, Ernest, said.
        Mrs. Dunbar, in 15 years at Time Inc., was also advertising director of Time-Life Video and Time-Life Multimedia. She previously worked for McGraw-Hill, Macmillan Films and Doubleday.
        She was a native of Philadelphia and a journalism graduate of Temple University.
        In addition to her husband, she is survived by two daughters, Dr. Adrienne Dunbar of Coral Gables, Fla., and Gail, of New York; a son, Dean, of New York, and a granddaughter.

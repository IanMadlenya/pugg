
        Laura Mondadori, a member of a prominent Italian publishing family, died on Wednesday at her home in Milan, Italy. She was 67 years old.
        The cause of death was not available.
        Mrs. Mondadori, who was known as Mimma, was the daughter of the late Arnoldo Mondadori, who founded a publishing empire in Italy 80 years ago. She was a significant shareholder in the holding company that controls Arnoldo Mondadori Editore S.p.A., Italy's largest publisher of books, magazines and newspapers, including the influential daily La Repubblica. She was also president of the Arnoldo and Alberto Mondadori Foundation, which supports writers and intellectuals.
        She is survived by her son, Leonardo, of Milan; a sister, Cristina, also of Milan, and three grandchildren.

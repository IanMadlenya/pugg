
        Viola Hymes, a former president of the National Council of Jewish Women, died Friday at Methodist Hospital in St. Louis Park, Minn. She was 84 years old and lived in Minneapolis.
        Mrs. Hymes died of complications after suffering a stroke, her grandson Michael said.
        Mrs. Hymes was national president of the organization from 1959 to 1963. In 1975 she received the council's Hannah G. Solomon Award for distinguished service.
        Mrs. Hymes was appointed to the Commission on the Status of Women by President John F. Kennedy, serving from 1961 to 1963, and to the Council on the Status of Women by President Lyndon B. Johnson, serving from 1963 to 1968.
        She is survived by two sons, Richard, of Minneapolis, and Alan, of Seattle; seven grandchildren, and two great-grandchildren.

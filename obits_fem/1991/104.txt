
        Gloria Holden, an actress whose film career spanned three decades, died Friday after a heart attack. She was 73 years old.
        The London-born actress played the wife of Paul Muni in the Oscar-winning 1937 film "The Life of Emile Zola."
        She appeared with Spencer Tracy, Myrna Loy and Clark Gable in "Test Pilot" in 1938, with Rosalind Russell and Melvyn Douglas in "This Thing Called Love" in 1941 and with Tyrone Power in "The Eddie Duchin Story" in 1956. Her credits also included "The Corsican Brothers," "Miss Annie Rooney" and "Dream Wife."

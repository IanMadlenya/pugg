
        Elizabeth Youngblood Cobb, a writer on home improvement, died on Sunday at Middlesex Hospital in Middletown, Conn. She was 76 years old and lived in Chester, Conn.
        Mrs. Cobb died of complications from pneumonia, a spokeswoman for the family said.
        With her husband, Hubbard, Betsy Cobb, as she was known professionally, wrote about ways to acquire, maintain and enhance living space.
        Their books included "City People's Guide to Country Living" (Macmillan, 1973), "Vacation Houses" (Dial, 1973) and "Your Barn House," to be published this spring by Henry Holt & Company.
        In addition to her husband, she is survived by a son, Thomas Youngblood Simon of Los Angeles, and two granddaughters.

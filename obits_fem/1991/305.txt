
        Carolyn Elsinger, executive vice president of CKDC Steel Inc., died on Tuesday in New York. She was 46 years old and had homes on Shelter Island, L.I., and in Avon-by-the-Sea, N.J.
        She died of pancreatic cancer, family members said.
        She was with CKDC Steel, a Manhattan company that trades in carbon wire and wire rod, from 1981 until she became ill earlier this year. Before that she was an executive assistant to the chairman of Genimar Inc., manufacturers of fabricated masonry wall reinforcements, also in New York.
        Her companion was Frank Hopler of Cliffside Park, N.J.
        She is survived by a son, Brett Smith of Miami; two brothers, Bernard, of Leonia, N.J., and Drew, of Miami; a sister, Marjorie Smith of Miami, and a niece and two nephews.

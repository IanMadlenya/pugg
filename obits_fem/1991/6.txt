
        Elsie Frum, a survivor of the Johnstown flood of 1889, died in her sleep early Wednesday. She was 108 years old.
        Mrs. Frum was 6 years old when she and her family fled to a hilltop and watched a wall of water roar through Johnstown on May 31, 1889, killing 2,209 people.
        Mrs. Frum is survived by two daughters, a son, eight grandchildren, 14 great-grandchildren and seven great-great-grandchildren.

Elayne Hanley Varian, an art curator and exhibition organizer, died of cardiopulmonary arrest on Nov. 4 at her home in Orlando, Fla. She was 74 years old.
For three years, she served as curator of contemporary art at the John and Mabel Ringling Museum in Sarasota.
After leaving there in 1978, she did art appraisal work and mounted several shows as a freelance curator.
She taught art history for 37 years at the Spence School, where she inspired many future scholars, including Joan R. Mertens, curator of Greek and Roman art at the Metropolitan Museum.

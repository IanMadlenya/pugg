        Nell Blaine, a widely respected New York landscape painter and watercolorist, died yesterday at Mount Sinai Hospital.
Just as Ms. Blaine was becoming known as a promising abstract painter, and gaining the admiration of such critics as Clement Greenberg, she started to shift back to representation.
She is survived by her companion, Carolyn Harris, a painter.
      

        Elaine Anthony, a painter who specialized in lush semi-abstract landscapes, died on Oct. 26 at her home in West Redding, Conn. She was 53 years old.
Her father, Erskine Nicol, was a prolific and fairly successful landscape painter, as was his father before him.
While she was still a child, the family moved to  Milan, where at 17 she had her first exhibition as a painter.

        Helena Leigh-Hunt, a realist still-life painter, died on Dec. 28 at Southampton Hospital in Long Island.

        Lady Caroline Blackwood, a writer of wry, macabre novels and essays, and a beguiling Anglo-Irish aristocrat who married the painter Lucian Freud and the poet Robert Lowell, died yesterday in the Mayfair Hotel in Manhattan, where she  stayed the last few weeks while she was ill. She was 64.
She recalled meeting Mr. Freud at that time at a party memorable because the painter Francis  Bacon caused a row when he hooted down Princess Margaret, who had just started singing "Let's Do It.

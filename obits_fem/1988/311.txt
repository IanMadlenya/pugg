        Shirley Burke, a literary agent who represented several prominent authors, died of a heart attack Monday at Memorial Sloan-Kettering Cancer Center. She was 81 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mrs. Burke, a native of Brooklyn, was a casting director for the Theater Guild before becoming a literary agent. She represented, among others, Stephen Longstreet, Dalton Trumbo, Willy Brandt, Herbert Kupferberg and Lucy Freeman.
        She is survived by her husband, Harry Levine; their son, Stephen, of Austin, Tex.; three sisters, Renee Feld and Helen Libles, both of Fort Lauderdale, Fla., and Martha Smith of Oakdale, L.I., and two grandchildren.

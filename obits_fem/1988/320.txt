        Annette Daum, an educator and leader in interfaith programs, died of cancer Monday at her home in Syosset, L.I. She was 62 years old.
        Mrs. Daum, a graduate of Hunter College, was director of the department of interreligious affairs at the Union of American Hebrew Congregations. She was also the organization's representative to the World Council of Churches and to the Vatican Commission for Religious Relations.
        She is survived by her husband, Alfred; two sons, Russell, of Holtsville, L.I., and Elliot, of Bayside, Queens, and a daughter, Sandra, of Washington.

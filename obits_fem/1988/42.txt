        Margaret M. Heyman, a social worker and retired professor at Hunter College who researched industrial programs on alcoholism, died of a cerebral hemorrhage last Sunday at New York Hospital. She was 77 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        She was born in Central Islip, L.I., and was a supervisor in the New York City Department of Welfare.
        She joined the faculty at Hunter in 1964, retiring in 1972 to serve as principal investigator and project director for a national study on alcohol programs in industry.
        There are no immediate survivors.

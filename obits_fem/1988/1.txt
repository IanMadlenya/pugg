        Betty Rhodes, a cabaret singer who played in the original production of ''Jacques Brel Is Alive and Well and Living in Paris,'' died of cancer Tuesday at Lenox Hill Hospital in Manhattan. She was 52 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Born in Tacoma, Wash., she graduated from the University of Southern California in Los Angeles. Her new album, ''No Regrets,'' was released recently.
        She is survived by her daughter, Jan Norman, of Ithaca, N.Y., and five grandchildren.

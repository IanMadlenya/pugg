        Judith Epstein, a former national president of Hadassah, the Women's Zionist Organization of America, died of a heart attack yesterday at St. Luke's-Roosevelt Hospital Center in Manhattan. She was 92 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mrs. Epstein graduated from Hunter College. She was a member of Hadassah for 72 years, becoming a national secretary of the organization and serving as president from 1937 to 1939 and 1942 to 1947.
        She is survived by her husband, Moses; a daughter, Naomi Cohen of Manhattan; five grandchildren, and nine great-grandchildren.

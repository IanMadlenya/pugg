        Rita Develin, a retired New York City teacher, died of cancer Wednesday at her home in Sedona, Ariz. She was 74 years old.
        Mrs. Develin was the founding president of Equal Retirement for New York City Teachers, a lobby for improved retirement conditions. She had been president of the 67th Precinct Youth Council in Brooklyn and had fought to ban such weapons as switch-blade knives and zip guns.
        Mrs. Develin was born in Brooklyn. She graduated from Brooklyn College and became an elementary school teacher in the Flatbush area. Mrs. Develin retired in 1975, after teaching for 30 years.
        Surviving are a daughter, Joy, of Sedona; a son, Hugh, of Gaithersburg, Md., and two grandchildren.

        Edna Baer, a retired New York City social worker and the widow of State Supreme Court Justice Harold Baer, died of heart disease Sunday at her home in Manhattan. She was 82 years old.
        Mrs. Baer, a native New Yorker, developed housing programs and other services for the aged. She retired in the mid-1970's.
        From 1961 to 1968, she was active in neighborhood conservation for New York City, and for 16 years before that, she was executive director of the New York Jewish Child Care Council.
        She received several honors, including the Naomi Lehman Award for contributions to the welfare of Jewish children in the city.
        She was a graduate of New York University and of its law school in 1928. She earned a master's degree in social work from Columbia University and served as president of the board of trustees of the School of Social Work.
        She is survived by a son, State Supreme Court Justice Harold Baer Jr. of Manhattan, and two granddaughters.

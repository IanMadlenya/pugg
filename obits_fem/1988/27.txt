        Patricia Powers, a short-story writer and the wife of Bertram A. Powers, president of New York Typographical Union No. 6, died of a heart attack Monday at St. Vincent's Hospital and Medical Center. She was 62 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mrs. Powers was born in Athlone, Ireland, and came here as a teen-ager. Her stories have appeared in such publications as The Kenyon Review, The Massachusetts Review, Ascent and Confrontation.
        Also surviving are her mother, Anagusta Colville of New Haven; two brothers, George Colville of Madison, Conn., and Paul Colville of Middleton, Nova Scotia; two sisters, Meta Wyss and Pamela Reycraft, both of Manhattan; two sons, Kevin, of Buffalo, and Brian, of Washington; two daughters, Patricia Inciardi of Novato, Calif., and Moya Keating of Chatham, N.J., and eight grandchildren.

        Betty Oakes, an actress in plays and musicals on and Off Broadway, died of cancer Wednesday at the Memorial-Sloan Kettering Cancer Center. She was 60 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Ms. Oakes appeared as Rosabella opposite Robert Weede on Broadway and on the national tour in ''The Most Happy Fella,'' taking over the role created by Jo Sullivan. She starred opposite Jack Carson in the 1952 Broadway revival of ''Of Thee I Sing'' and was Barbara Bel Geddes's standby in ''The Moon Is Blue.''
        Off Broadway she appeared in the 1960 revival of ''Epitaph for George Dillon'' at the Actors' Playhouse and opposite Jack Cassidy in ''Sandhog'' at the Phoenix Theater. Ms. Oakes also appeared in many plays at musical theaters around the country.
        She is survived by a daughter, Jennifer Greenberg of Manhattan.

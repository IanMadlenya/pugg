        Camille Davied Rose, former executive editor of McCall's magazine, died of heart failure July 20 at Medford Leas, a retirement home in Medford, N.J., where she lived. She was 95 years old.
        Mrs. Rose, who was born in Chattanooga, Tenn., was editor of McCall's from 1945 to 1958 and consulting editor until 1962. She taught magazine writing at Hunter College and the New School for Social Research in Manhattan.
        She was predeceased by her husband, Marc A. Rose. Surviving are her daughter, Patricia Cummings of Westport, Mass., and two grandchildren.

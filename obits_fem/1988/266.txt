        Gertrude Baltimore, a former psychology instructor at Sarah Lawrence College in Bronxville, N.Y., died of cancer yesterday at her home in Manhattan. She was 79 years old.
        Mrs. Baltimore taught at the New School for Social Research from 1958 to 1966. From 1965 to 1977, she taught experimental psychology at Sarah Lawrence. She was a former dean of the college's Center for Continuing Education and a former dean of graduate studies.
        Surviving are two sons, David, of Cambridge, Mass., who shared the Nobel Prize in Medicine in 1975, and Robert, of Orange, Conn., and three grandchildren.

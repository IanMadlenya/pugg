        Eleanor Gottheil Kubie, an author and artist, died of acute leukemia Sunday at her home in Westport, Conn. She was 89 years old.
        A 1920 graduate of Barnard College, Mrs. Kubie wrote 12 fiction and nonfiction books for juveniles, and ''Road to Nineveh,'' a biography of the archeologist Austin Henry Layard. As an amateur archeologist, Mrs. Kubie participated in digs in Israel.
        Mrs. Kubie was a member of the Corporation of the MacDowell Colony in Peterborough, N.H.
        She is survived by a stepdaughter, Ann Kubie Rabinowitz of Nutley, N.J.; a stepson, Robert H. Kubie of St. Louis, and a grandson.

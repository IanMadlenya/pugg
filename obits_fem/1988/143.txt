        Carroll Kowal, a social worker and longtime advocate of adequate housing for the homeless and disabled, died of intestinal cancer Saturday at the Mount Sinai Medical Center in Manhattan. She was 59 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Ms. Kowal coordinated special housing under Gov. Hugh L. Carey and was a housing official in the administration of Mayor Abraham D. Beame. She was president-elect of the New York chapter of the National Association of Social Workers and past president of the Alumni Association of the Columbia University School of Social Work.
        Surviving are her husband, John; a brother, Lynfield Ott, of Myrtle Beach, S.C., and three stepchildren, Elizabeth Guide, of Hackensack, N.J., Elizabeth, of Berkeley, Calif., and Robert, of Manhattan.

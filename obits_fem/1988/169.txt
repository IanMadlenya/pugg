        Leonie Adams, who served as a consultant in poetry to the Library of Congress in 1948 and 1949, died of heart disease Monday in the Candlewood Valley Care Center, a nursing home, in New Milford, Conn., where she made her home. She was 88 years old.
        Among the honors that Miss Adams received for her poetry were the Harriet Monroe Award and the Shelley Memorial Award in 1954, the Bollingen Prize in 1955 and the Brandeis Medal in 1969. She was a fellow of the American Academy of Poets and was elected a member of the National Institute of Arts and Letters in 1951.
        Miss Adams was the author of ''Those Not Elect,'' ''High Falcon,'' ''Poems: A Selection,'' ''This Measure,'' and editor of ''Lyrics of Francois Villon.'' She described her work as ''lyric poetry in largely traditional forms.''
        The poet, who was born in Brooklyn, taught writing and poetry at New York University, Sarah Lawrence, Columbia University, the New Jersey College for Women, the University of Washington and Purdue University.
        Miss Adams is survived by a sister, Louise Adams Holland, of Philadelphia.

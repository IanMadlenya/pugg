        Margaret Cunningham Braun, a member of the United Nations international staff for 28 years, died of cancer Wednesday at her home in Manhattan. She was 74 years old. In the early 1940's, she was a staff member of the United Nations Information Office, an intergovernmental agency that helped prepare for the founding conference of the United Nations in San Francisco in 1945. During her career, she helped organize the United Nations' documentation service and then became a writer-researcher.
        When she retired in 1973, she joined the Metropolitan Museum of Art, first as a volunteer working to increase membership and then as a staff member involved in assisting visitors to the museum.
        She is survived by her husband, Alan; a daughter, Eleanor Lohn of Yorktown Heights, N.Y., and two grandchildren.

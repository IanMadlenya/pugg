        Gertrude Hill, director of training for the New York City Department for the Aging, died of a heart attack Saturday at St. Luke's-Roosevelt Hospital Center in Manhattan. She was 58 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mrs. Hill, who was born in Manhattan, earned a bachelor's degree from Adelphi College and a master's from Columbia University. Mrs. Hill joined the Department for the Aging in 1972, training people who work with the elderly.
        She is survived by her mother, Florencemae Jose of Manhattan, and three sons, Joshua, Daniel and Thomas, all of Manhattan.

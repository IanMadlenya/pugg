        Dr. Rhetta M. Arter, a social and cultural researcher and former director of field studies for the Center for Human Relations Studies at New York University, died of breast cancer in New York Hospital last Sunday. She was 79 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        In 1959, Dr. Arter founded and became president of Research and Action Inc., a social research company in Manhattan. She created and administered projects serving women and girls for the National Board of the Y.W.C.A. and the National Council of Negro Women.
        She is survived by a brother, Carl N., of Pittsburgh.


       Charlotte Rosen, a musician and teacher, died of Alzheimer's disease Saturday at her home in Manhattan. She was 95 years old. Born in Berlin, she was trained as a violinist. After immigrating to New York in 1939, she switched to the viola, and performed at New York Public Library branches and the New-York Historical Society.
        Charlotte Rosen, a musician and teacher, died of Alzheimer's disease Saturday at her home in Manhattan. She was 95 years old. Born in Berlin, she was trained as a violinist. After immigrating to New York in 1939, she switched to the viola, and performed at New York Public Library branches and the New-York Historical Society.
        From 1939 to 1960, she taught music at the Henry Street Settlement on the Lower East Side.
        She is survived by her husband, Dr. Michael Heidelberger.

        Mae Gurevich, vice chairman of the New York State Democratic Party for many years and more recently an aide to Senator Daniel Patrick Moynihan, died of cancer Monday at her home in Manhattan. She was 78 years old.
        Mrs. Gurevich, who entered politics at the grass-roots level before World War II, worked her way up in the Bronx organization and went on to state party office, serving in the No. 2 post from 1960 until 1974. She was a delegate to three Democratic National Conventions, in 1960, 1964 and 1968, and was a member of the Electoral College in 1961, 1965 and 1969.
        During World War II, Mrs. Gurevich was assistant director of civil defense in the Bronx.
        After retiring from her state party post, she was project director for the New York City Bicentennial Corporation. Joining Senator Moynihan's staff in 1977, she became a special assistant, handling liaison to Federal agencies and to state and local officials. She retired last year.
        Mrs. Gurevich's husband, Abraham, died in 1977. Surviving are three sisters, Lillian Fisher of Riverside, Calif., Aida Winkler of Nanuet, N.Y., and Tillie Berson of White Plains; a brother, Harry Altman of North Miami Beach, Fla., and two sons, Carl, of Manhattan and Ned, of Mount Kisco, N.Y.

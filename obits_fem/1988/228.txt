        Annette Overby, a retired psychologist and a former executive director of the National Psychological Association for Psychoanalysis, died of Alzheimer's disease yesterday at her home in Manhattan. She was 86 years old.
        Mrs. Overby was born in Princeton, Wis., and was a graduate of the University of Minnesota and of Smith College, where she received her master's degree. She was a practicing psychologist for several decades until her retirement in 1985.
        Mrs. Overby's husband, Andrew N. Overby, an international banker who had been Assistant Secretary of the Treasury during the Eisenhower Adminstration, died in 1984. Surviving are two sisters, Pauline Reynard of Manhattan and Ruth Pedersen of Owen, Wis. -

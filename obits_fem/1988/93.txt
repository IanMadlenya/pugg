        Dr. Marion C. Morris, a bacteriologist and immunologist who held posts with several public and private health agencies, died of heart failure April 1 at New York Hospital. She was 85 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Dr. Morris, who was born in Holyoke, Mass., was a Phi Beta Kappa graduate of Smith College and received a Ph.D. from Washington University Medical School in St. Louis. She taught at Vassar College before joining the Public Health Research Institute, a nonprofit agency in New York.
        From 1952 until 1962 she was with the National Foundation for Infantile Paralysis, allocating scholarships and fellowships in the health professions. In 1963 she became assistant scientific director for the Health Research Council of New York. She retired in 1971.
        Dr. Morris is survived by a sister, Lillian O'Connell of Holyoke.

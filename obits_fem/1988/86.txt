        Anne Dunlap Winkler, an interior designer, died Sunday in her Manhattan apartment. She was 91 years old.
        Mrs. Winkler was a reporter for The New York American from 1919 to 1926 before leaving for Paris, where she studied interior decorating at the Parsons School of Design. In Paris she met William C. Pahlmann, a leading decorator, and on her return here, Mrs. Winkler joined his New York firm, William Pahlmann Associates.
        She was born in Nashville. Mrs. Winkler was a former president of the Decorators of New York Club and a member of the American Institute of Decorators.
        She retired 10 years ago, but continued to work for a few clients.
        There are no immediate survivors.

        Dr. Grace K. Baruch, associate director of the Wellesley Center for Research on Women, died yesterday of cancer at her home in Newton Center, Mass. She was 52 years old.
        A specialist in the changing role of women in society, Dr. Baruch was the author of several books, including ''Gender and Stress,'' ''The Competent Woman'' and ''Lifeprints.'' She was also co-author of a study that concluded that today's mature women feel increased self-esteem and confidence.
        Dr. Baruch, who earned her doctorate at Bryn Mawr College, is survived by her husband, Franklin; a daughter, Susannah, and her mother, Ruth Kestenman of East Providence, R.I.

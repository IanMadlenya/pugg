        Suria Saint-Denis, consultant director of the drama division of the Juilliard School, died Dec. 29 at St. Luke's-Roosevelt Hospital Center. She was 85 years old and lived in Manhattan.
        Mrs. Saint-Denis, who was born in Russia, assisted such directors as John Gielgud, John Dexter and David Lean. She had been affiliated with the Juilliard School since the creation of its drama division in 1968 by her husband, Michel Saint-Denis. He died in 1971, and Mrs. Saint-Denis was was named consultant director to the drama division faculty that year.
        Mrs. Saint-Denis is survived by a stepdaughter, Christine De La Potterie, of Paris.
        A memorial tribute is to be held at 5 P.M. on Jan. 20 in the Drama Theater at the Juilliard School, 144 West 66th Street.

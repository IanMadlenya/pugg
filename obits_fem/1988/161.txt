        Dr. Marion Armbruster Pierce, an educator and research scientist, died of a stroke Thursday at the Wilmington (Del.) Medical Center. She was 79 years old and lived in Wilmington.
        Dr. Pierce earned a doctorate in physical chemistry from Bryn Mawr College in 1934. She then worked at the United States Steel Corporation in Kearny, N.J., and did research at the DuPont Laboratory in Arlington, N.J.. She was an associate professor at Barnard College in Manhattan until 1947.
        Surviving are her husband, Dr. Robert H. H. Pierce, and two daughters, Dr. Dorothy H. Pierce of Wilmington, and Dr. Roberta M. Pierce of Narberth, Pa.

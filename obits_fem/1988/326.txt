        Cornelia Goldsmith, a former director of the New York City Health Department's division of day care, day camps and institutions, died of cardiac arrest on Nov. 23 in Clinton, N.Y., where she lived. She was 96 years old.
        From 1943 to 1963, when she retired, she was the division's director. She then served as executive secretary of the National Association for the Education of Young Children, in Washington, until 1966.
        Surviving are two daughters, Hertha Slauson Klugman of Boston and Gerda Slauson Cooke of Clinton; six grandaughters, and two great-granddaughters.

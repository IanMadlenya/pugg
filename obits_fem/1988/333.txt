        Dr. Julia R. Vane, a clinical psychologist and a professor at Hofstra University, died Thursday at Mercy Hospital in Rockville Centre, L.I., after a long illness. She was 69 years old and lived in Garden City, L.I.
        Dr. Vane was chairman of the psychology department at Hofstra, where she spent 26 years developing a doctoral program. She created the Vane Kindergarten Test, used to measure children's readiness for school.
        She earned a bachelor's degree from Brooklyn College, a master's from Columbia University and a doctorate from New York University. Before joining Hofstra, she was chief psychologist for the Hempstead, L.I. schools.
        Surviving are her husband, Salvatore Cannizzaro; two stepsons, John Cannizzaro of Syracuse, and Joseph Cannizzaro of Commack, L.I.; a stepdaughter, Lynn Hargraves of Babylon, L.I., and three grandchildren.

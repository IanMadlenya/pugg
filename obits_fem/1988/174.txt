        Gertrude Joy Grimm, an assistant director of the Waves in World War II, died of cancer Thursday at her home in Sarasota, Fla. She was 83 years old.
        A native of Plainfield, N.J., and a graduate of Wellesley College, Mrs. Grimm was a retailing manager in New York City and Detroit before the war. In 1942, when the women's branch of the Navy, the Women Accepted for Volunteer Emergency Service, was organized she became assistant director for personnel. She retired in 1946 with the rank of commander.
        Mrs. Grimm is survived by a son, Peter G. of Larchmont, N.Y. and a grandson. Stephen, of New York City.

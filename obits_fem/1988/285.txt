        Dr. Ruth Ellen Steinman Bloustein, a pediatrician and clinical assistant professor at the Robert Wood Johnson Medical School of the University of Medicine and Dentistry of New Jersey, died yesterday at her home in Piscataway, N.J. She was 65 years old.
        Dr. Bloustein, the wife of Edward J. Bloustein, president of Rutgers University, was born in Brooklyn and graduated from the University of Vermont and the Long Island College of Medicine. In the 1970's, she co-founded a night clinic for infants of indigent families at the Willets Health Center at Rutgers University's Douglass College.
        Also surviving are two daughters, Elise, of Manhattan, and Lori, of Brooklyn; a sister, Georgianna Sheldon of Bethesda, Md., and two brothers, Dr. Robert Steinman of Lancaster, Pa., and Richard Steinman of Portland, Me.

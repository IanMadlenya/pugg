        Blanche Lasker, a retired principal in School District 10 in the Bronx, died of cancer Sunday in the Tally Ho Manor in Boonton, N.J., where she lived. She was 79 years old.
        Born in the Bronx, she graduated from Hunter College and taught in the New York City school system for 40 years until she retired in 1973.
        She is survived by four nephews, Dr. Monroe Mufson of Denville, N.J., Dr. Alan Mufson of Boonton, Donald Mufson of Closter, N.J., and Dr. Bernard Abrams of St. Louis; and a niece, Dr. Joan Abrams of Washington.

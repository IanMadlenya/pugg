        Marjorie Draper, a leader in New Jersey civic and community affairs, died Sunday at her home in Montclair, N.J., after a brief illness. She was 60 years old.
        Mrs. Draper, a native of Milengavie, Scotland, was involved in a number of volunteer activities. In 1984 she became president of the United Way of North Essex. She was a member of the New Jersey State Child Placement Advisory Council and the Civil Rights Commission for Essex County. Earlier this year, she became president of the Volunteer Center of Essex County, which acts as a central clearinghouse for corporate volunteers.
        She is survived by her husband, Daniel C.; a son, John W., of Manhattan; two daughters, Louise Gaumond of Manhattan and Elizabeth, of Jersey City.

        Alice Dresel Beal Van Santvoord, a patron of artists and a contributor to theatrical and educational institutions, died of complications following surgery Friday at St. Luke's Hospital in Newburgh, N.Y. She was 95 years old and lived in Newburgh.
        Mrs. Van Santvoord befriended and served as benefactor for such well-known artists as Childe Hassam, Walt Kuhn and Timothy Cole, the woodcarver. She contributed to a wide variety of projects, among them the Museum for Experimental Theater at Vassar and the Thaddeus Reynolds Beal Fund of Yale University.
        Mrs. Van Santvoord had been married to Thaddeus Reynolds Beal, the first president of the Hudson Gas and Electric Company. After his death in 1933, she married George Van Santvoord, the late headmaster of the Hotchkiss School, a private preparatory school in Lakeville, Conn.
        She is survived by three children, John Wilkie Beal of Newburgh, Alger Dresel Beal of Bennington, Vt., and Alice Louise Curten of Hyde Park, N.Y.; 14 grandchildren and 12 great-grandchildren.
